# 1 Corinthians 1:21 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The verse describes the limited understanding of God's wisdom by both the Jews and Gentiles. Despite their intellectual pursuits, they failed to truly know God. However, God, in His wisdom, chose to save those who believe through the seemingly foolish act of preaching. This choice of salvation through preaching is seen as a benevolent and divine plan that goes beyond human comprehension.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "After all the experience of the world it was ascertained that human beings would never by their own wisdom come to the true knowledge of God."
2. "It was desirable that the powers of man should be fully tried before the new plan was introduced, in order to show that it was not dependent on human wisdom."
3. "The preaching of the cross which is thus esteemed foolishness, is made the means of saving them, because it sets forth God’s only plan of mercy."

### Archibald T Robertson:

The mystery-religions all offered salvation by initiation and ritual as the Pharisees did by ceremonialism. Christianity reaches the heart directly by trust in Christ as the Saviour. It is God's wisdom."
