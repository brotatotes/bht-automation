## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The use of δύναμις and σοφία here as applied to Him who was the greatest example of both, would not justify the absolute use of σοφία in this sense in 1 Corinthians 1:21."
2. "The repetition of χριστόν gives solemnity, at the same time that it concentrates the δύναμις and σοφία in the Person of Christ; q. d. ‘Christ even in His humiliation unto death, the power of God and wisdom of God.’"
3. "to these, viz. the elect, the αὐτοῖς serving to identify them with the σωζόμενοι of 1 Corinthians 1:18. There it was ἡμῖν, here αὐτοῖς, because by the mention of preaching joined with ἡμεῖς, he has now separated off the hearers."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The same class as the 'us which are (being) saved' (1 Corinthians 1:18); the elect, who have obeyed the call; called effectually." 
2. "When the offense of the cross is overcome, 'Christ' is received in all His relations, not only in His cross, but in His life and His future kingdom." 
3. "The cross (the death of a slave), which to the Jews was a 'stumbling-block,' is really 'the power of God' to the salvation of all who believe."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Christ appears to them as the power of God; or it is through him that the power of salvation is communicated to them."
2. "They see that there is a beauty in his character; an excellency in his doctrines; and an efficacy in his atonement, to secure their salvation."
3. "All Christians have similar views of the Saviour... They see him to be the power and the wisdom of God. They are united in him, and therefore united to each other."

### John Calvin:
1. "The fact that Christ was so unfavorably received, was not owing to any fault on his part, nor to the natural disposition of mankind generally, but arose from the depravity of those who were not enlightened by God."
2. "The elect of God, whether Jews or Gentiles, are not hindered by any stumblingblock from coming to Christ, that they may find in him a sure salvation."
3. "He is, notwithstanding, to all the elect, of whatever nation they may be, at once the power of God unto salvation for surmounting these stumblingblocks, and the wisdom of God for throwing off that mask."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Christ crucified is God's answer to both Jew and Greek and the answer is understood by those with open minds."
2. "Christ, the power of God and the wisdom of God."
3. "Christ crucified is the demonstration of God's power and wisdom to those who are called."

### John Gill:
1. "Being called out of darkness, and savingly illuminated by the Spirit of God, they see wisdom, beauty, glory, excellency, and suitableness in Christ, and in his Gospel."
2. "Christ, to them is, the power of God, and the wisdom of God: he is 'the power of God'; this is opposed to the Jews who stumbled at his weakness, his sufferings and death, even the death of the cross."
3. "In redemption and salvation by a crucified Christ, God hath abounded towards us in all wisdom and prudence: there is in this article a high display of the wisdom of God."

### John Wesley:
1. "But to them that are called — And obey the heavenly calling." This quote emphasizes the importance of not only being called by God but also obeying His call. It highlights the active response required from believers to fully experience the blessings of God's calling.

2. "Christ — With his cross, his death, his life, his kingdom." This quote encapsulates the multifaceted nature of Christ's work. It reminds readers that through His sacrifice, resurrection, and establishment of His kingdom, Christ brings salvation and eternal life to believers.

3. "And they experience, first, that he is the power, then, that he is the wisdom, of God." This quote highlights the progressive revelation of Christ's attributes to believers. It suggests that through their relationship with Him, believers come to understand and experience His power and wisdom in their lives.
