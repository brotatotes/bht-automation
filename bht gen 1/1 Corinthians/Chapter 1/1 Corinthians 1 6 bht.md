# 1 Corinthians 1:6 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The verse being described is a powerful testament to the impact of the gospel in the lives of believers. It speaks of the confirmation and establishment of the truth of Christ's message among the Corinthians. This confirmation is not merely external, through miracles, but also internal, through faith and the transformative power of the Holy Spirit. The verse emphasizes that the witness of Christ goes beyond words, extending to the lives of believers who boldly proclaim His truth and demonstrate His love.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The gospel of Christ was at first established among you by means of the miraculous endowments of the Holy Spirit."

3. "The message of the gospel consists in bearing witness to Christ and his work; see 1 Corinthians 15:1-4; 2 Timothy 1:8."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Witness of Christ" refers to the testimony or evidence that speaks about Christ and His teachings. It is a powerful declaration of His life, death, and resurrection, revealing His divine nature and purpose.

2. This witness is not limited to words alone but is also demonstrated through the lives of believers who follow Christ's example and share His love with others.

3. Just as the early disciples were empowered by the Holy Spirit to be witnesses of Christ, believers today are called to boldly proclaim the truth of Jesus and His redemptive work in the world. It is a responsibility and privilege that should not be taken lightly.

### John Calvin:
1. "God has sealed the truth of his gospel among the Corinthians, for the purpose of confirming it."
2. "The gospel is properly confirmed in our experience by faith, because it is only when we receive it by faith that we 'set to our seal that God is true.'"
3. "The Spirit of God is the earnest and seal, giving efficacy to the gospel through the internal influence of the Spirit and the excellence and variety of gifts, miracles, and all other helps."

2. 3. "By its marvellous transformation" - This phrase underscores the visible and tangible evidence of the gospel's work in the Corinthian believers' lives. It suggests that the transformation was so remarkable that it served as a powerful testimony to the truth and power of Christ's message.
