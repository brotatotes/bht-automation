# 1 Corinthians 10:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, believers partake of the same bread which symbolizes the body of Christ. As they partake, they become united as one body with Christ and with one another. The act of sharing one loaf signifies their oneness and unity in faith. This communion represents the spiritual bond between Christians and exemplifies how they are joined together as one through their participation in the body of Christ. Through this shared meal, they demonstrate their unity, friendship, and common oneness with Christ and with each other.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "By partaking of that bread, we become, not figuratively but literally, one bread: it passes into the substance of our bodies, and there is in every one who partakes, a portion of himself which is that bread."
2. "The bread is the Body of Christ; we partake of the bread: therefore we partake of the Body of Christ."
3. "The argument is a very simple and direct one; the bread is the Body of Christ; we partake of the bread: therefore we partake of the Body of Christ."

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "One loaf alone seems to have been used in each celebration."
2. "We, the many (namely, believers assembled; so the Greek), are one bread."
3. "We become one body with Christ, and so with one another."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "This idea is not, as our translation would seem to indicate, that Christians were numerous, but that 'all' were united, and constituted one society." 

2. "We are all one society; united as one, and for the same object. Our partaking of the same bread is an emblem of the fact that we are one." 

3. "In almost all nations the act of eating together has been regarded as a symbol of unity or friendship."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "By each and all receiving a piece of the one loaf, which represents Christ's body, they signify that they are all bound in one spiritual body, united to Christ and therefore to each other."
2. "Take care to keep one eucharistic feast only; for there is one flesh of our Lord Jesus Christ, and one cup unto unity of His blood;" i.e., that all may be one by partaking of His blood."
3. "That which all eat is taken from the one loaf, and they eat of it mutually, in common, sharing it among them."

Note: The commentary is about 1 Corinthians 10:17 - "Because there is one bread, we who are many are one body, for we all partake of the one bread."

### John Calvin:
1. "we must, even by external profession, maintain that unity which subsists between us and Christ"
2. "the breaking of bread...describes the entire Supper"
3. "lest any less experienced person should be put off his guard by the foolish cavil that is brought forward by certain sycophants"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "In all ancient times friends were made one over a common meal; much more is this oneness manifested when, on some festal occasion, great principles are represented and celebrated by those whose principles they are."
2. "How much more when Christians, as 'one body,' 'eat of that bread' in which their Lord would have them see 'His body broken for them,' and 'drink of that cup' in which they were to see 'His blood of the new covenant shed for them.'"
3. "Thus was their common oneness with Him, in the first instance, and in virtue of this, their oneness among themselves, visibly set forth and palpably expressed."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "One bread (εις αρτος). One loaf."
2. "We all (ο παντες). We the all, the whole number."
3. "One body (εν σωμα). Here the mystical spiritual body of Christ."

### John Gill:
1. "Believers, though they are many, yet are one body, of which Christ is the head; one in union with him and one another, and one in their communion together at the Lord's table."
2. "By partaking of the same bread, believers appear to be the same body, and of the same mass and lump with one another."
3. "We are much more associated together into one body, by eating one and the same bread, appointed us by our Savior."

### John Wesley:
1. "For it is this communion which makes us all one."
2. "We being many are yet, as it were, but different parts of one and the same broken bread."
3. "We receive to unite us in one body."
