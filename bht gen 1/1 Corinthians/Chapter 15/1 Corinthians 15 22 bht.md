## BHT:
The Bible verse being described speaks of the contrast between Adam and Christ as bringers of death and life. It emphasizes that just as Adam's sin brought physical death to all, Christ's act of salvation offers life to everyone. This verse highlights the inclusive nature of God's love and salvation, as it applies to all individuals without distinction or exclusion. It stresses that while Adam's deed brought temporal death, Christ's sacrifice offers the opportunity for eternal life and resurrection to all who believe in Him.

### Henry Alford:
1. "In that He is to us the bringer-in of LIFE, as Adam was the bringer-in of DEATH."
2. "The antithesis is merely between the bringing in of death by Adam, and of life (its opposite) by Christ."
3. "No consequence, whether on the side of death or of life, is brought into consideration. That death physical involved death eternal that life eternal (in its only worthy sense) involves bliss eternal, is not so much as thought of, while the two great opposites, Death and Life, are under consideration."

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "In Adam all—in union of nature with Adam, as representative head of mankind in their fall." 
2. "In Christ . . . all—in union of nature with Christ, the representative head of mankind in their recovery." 
3. "The life brought in by Christ is co-extensive with the death brought in by Adam."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "His deed was the procuring cause, or the reason, why all are subjected to temporal death."
2. "The argument of the apostle here is ample to prove that all people are subjected to temporal death by the sin of Adam."
3. "As the death of all was occasioned by the sin of one, even so, in like manner, the resurrection of all shall be produced by one."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The word 'all' in this verse signifies that there is no distinction or exclusion. It encompasses every individual, without exception."
2. "This verse reminds us that God's love and grace are not limited to a select few, but are available to all people. It is a powerful reminder of the inclusive nature of God's salvation."
3. "The use of 'all' in this verse emphasizes the universal scope of God's love and the equal opportunity for salvation that is offered to every person, regardless of their background or status."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "It has been the Divine plan from the first, and will be to the last, that mankind shall be dealt with under two heads Adam and Christ hence called 'the first man' and 'the second man;' as if there never had been, nor ever will be more than those two men."
2. "Though the life meant here is indeed co-extensive with the death spoken of, it does not mean the life actually conferred upon any one, but the life procured and made available for all on condition of their believing."
3. "It is Humanity as actually lost in Adam and as actually recovered in Christ, that the apostle is here treating of the whole ruin expressed by the all-comprehensive word 'death,' and the whole recovery expressed by the equally comprehensive word 'life.'"

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Shall be made alive (ζωοποιηθησοντα). First future passive indicative of ζωοποιεω, late verb (Aristotle) to give life, to restore to life as here."
2. "He means resurrection (restoration) by the verb here, but not necessarily eternal life or salvation."
3. "All who die die in Adam, all who will be made alive will be made alive (restored to life) in Christ."

### John Gill:
1. "All men were in Adam seminally, as the common parent of human nature, in such sense as Levi was in the loins of Abraham when Melchizedek met him, and in him paid tithes unto him."
2. "They became subject to a corporeal death, which has ever since reigned over mankind, even over infants, such who have not sinned after the similitude of his transgression."
3. "As all that were in Adam, all that belonged to him, all his natural seed and posterity, all to whom he was a federal head, died in him, became mortal, and subject to death through him; so all that are in Christ, that belong to him, who are his spiritual seed and offspring, to whom he is a covenant head, and representative, shall be raised to an immortal life by him."

### John Wesley:
1. "Through Christ all these shall be made alive - He does not say, 'shall revive,' (as naturally as they die,) but shall be made alive, by a power not their own."
2. "Through Adam all, even the righteous, die."
3. "Through Christ all these shall be made alive."
