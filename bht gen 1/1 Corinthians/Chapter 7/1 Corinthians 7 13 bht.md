# 1 Corinthians 7:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, the Apostle addresses the dynamics of mixed marriages within the Christian community. He emphasizes that the instruction applies to both parties involved, acknowledging the reality of believers being married to unbelievers. The passage urges the wife, if she is a believer, not to divorce her husband, but rather to continue to dwell with him and show him the excellence of her faith through her love, kindness, and fidelity. The Apostle recognizes the challenges faced by these marriages and advises the wife to persevere, bearing much until the circumstances become unbearable or life-threatening.

### Henry Alford:
1. "The Apostle uses the vox media ἀφιέναι here, of both parties, the husband and wife, not ἀπολύειν (as Matthew 5:31, &c.), which would apply only to the husband."

3. "Paul puts both alternatives because he regards the Christian party as the superior one in the marriage."

3. "The wife had the power of effecting a divorce." This statement provides historical context, explaining that in Greek and Roman law, the wife had the ability to initiate a divorce. This sheds light on the cultural context in which the instruction was given.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The wife did not have power to 'put away' the husband, and expel him from his own home; but she might think it her duty to be separated from him."
2. "She should still love her husband and seek his welfare; she should be still a kind, affectionate, and faithful wife; and all the more so that she may show him the excellence of religion, and win him to love it."
3. "She should even bear much, and bear it long; nor should she leave him unless her life is rendered miserable, or in danger; or unless he wholly neglects to make provision for her, and leaves her to suffering, to want, and to tears."
