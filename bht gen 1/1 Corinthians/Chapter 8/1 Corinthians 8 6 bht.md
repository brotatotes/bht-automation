# 1 Corinthians 8:6 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, the commentators emphasize the belief in the unity of God and the exclusive worship of Him. They highlight that Christians acknowledge one God, who is the source and purpose of all things. The commentators also emphasize the distinction between God the Father and the Lord Jesus Christ, acknowledging Jesus as the one Lord who is worthy of worship. They further note that through Jesus, believers have access to the Father and receive spiritual blessings. The verse underscores the fundamental Christian belief in the oneness of God and the exaltation of Jesus as Lord.

### Henry Alford:
1. "Yet to us, there is one God, the Father, of whom are all things, and we unto Him." 
2. "One Lord Jesus Christ, by whom are all things, and we by Him."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "God the Father is the end for whom and for whose glory believers live."
2. "In Colossians 1:16, all things are said to be created not only 'by' Christ, but also 'for Him.'"
3. "One Lord, contrasted with the 'many lords' of heathendom."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "We acknowledge but one God; and he alone has a right to rule over us."
2. "The word 'Father' here is not used as applicable to the first person of the Trinity, as distinguished from the second, but is applied to God as God; not as the Father in contradistinction from the Son, but to the divine nature as such."
3. "The leading idea here is, probably, that to God Christians owe their hopes and happiness."

### John Calvin:
1. "Whatever has its origin from what is foreign to itself, is not eternal, and, consequently, is not God. All things have their origin from one Being: he alone, therefore, is God."
2. "He is assuredly God who gives existence to all, and from whom all things flow, as from the supreme source; but there is only One, from whom all things flow, and hence there is but one God."
3. "For this reason our Lord is spoken of as one. But in respect of dominion being ascribed to him alone, this is not to be taken as meaning that worldly distinctions are abolished."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "To us Christians there is no divinity, and no object of worship, save the one living and true God."
2. "From this state of mind sprang the conception...that there exist intermediate and subordinate divinities, or emanations from the supreme Divinity through whom the two extremities meet."
3. "What Christianity does is not to extinguish this conviction and this emotion, out of which the universe came to be thus ignorantly peopled, but to disclose the sublime Reality that underlies all these dreamings."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Yet to us there is one God, the Father." - This verse emphasizes the belief in the unity of God, specifically the Father, as the sole deity.
2. "Of whom...our goal is God." - The Father is not only the source of the universe but also the ultimate destination or purpose for believers.
3. "Here Paul calls Jesus Lord and not God." - While Paul acknowledges Jesus as Lord, he distinguishes him from God, using the term "Lord" to honor Jesus' authority and sovereignty.
