# 1 John 2:25 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this Bible verse, the commentators highlight the significance of the promise of eternal life. They emphasize that this promise is made by God the Father, who cannot lie and is trustworthy. The promise is closely connected to the work of the Holy Spirit and is linked to the knowledge of Christ. By abiding in him and believing in him, we enter into a covenant relationship where we can find hope, assurance, and everlasting life. This promise should be a sufficient inducement for us to hold fast to the truth and trust in the faithfulness and commitment of Christ.

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "Eternal life shall be the permanent consummation of thus abiding in the Son and in the Father." 
2. "He," referring to Christ, "the Son." 
3. "Promised" in various other verses in the Gospel of John.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The 'promise' of heaven to people is limited to those who repent of their sins, who believe in the Lord Jesus Christ, and who lead a holy life; and if anyone will base his hope of heaven on a 'promise,' it must be limited to these things."
2. "How does anyone know that he can be saved, unless he has some assurance from God that it may and shall be so? Is not heaven his home? How does anyone know that he may dwell there, without some assurance from Him that he may?"
3. "However people may reason, or conjecture, or hope, the only 'promise' of eternal life is found in the Bible; and the fact that we have such promise should surely be a sufficient inducement to us to hold fast the truth."

### John Calvin:
1. "The sum of what is said is, that we cannot live otherwise than by nourishing to the end the seed of life sown in our hearts."
2. "John insists much on this point, that not only the beginning of a blessed life is to be found in the knowledge of Christ, but also its perfection."
3. "But no repetition of it can be too much, since it is well known that it has ever been a cause of ruin to men, that being not content with Christ, they have had a hankering to wander beyond the simple doctrine of the gospel."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "This is the promise" - The promise referred to here is "the life eternal," as explained in 1 John 1:2. This highlights the significance of eternal life as a central theme in the Johannine writings.

2. "The promise" - The term "the promise" is also used in Acts 1:4 to describe the coming of the Holy Spirit. This connection suggests that the promise mentioned here is closely related to the work of the Holy Spirit in the lives of believers.

3. "He promised" - The use of the phrase "He promised" emphasizes that it is Christ Himself who made this promise. This highlights the trustworthiness and authority of Christ's words, reinforcing the importance of placing our faith in Him.

### John Gill:
1. "God the Father, who is that God that cannot lie, who in the covenant of his grace, before the world began, made this promise unto his people, [even] eternal life." 
2. "The promise, with all others, was put into the hands of Christ, where, with them, it is yea and amen."
3. "This is the sum of the Gospel declaration, that whoever believes in him shall have everlasting life."

### John Wesley:
1. "The promise of the Son is not just a mere statement, but a guarantee of his faithfulness and commitment to those who remain in him."
2. "By abiding in him, we enter into a covenant relationship where we can trust in his promises and rely on his unfailing love and grace."
3. "The assurance of his promise gives us hope and confidence, knowing that he will never leave us nor forsake us."
