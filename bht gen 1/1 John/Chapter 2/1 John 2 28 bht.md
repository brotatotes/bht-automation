# 1 John 2:28 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this passage, the author emphasizes the importance of abiding in Christ, maintaining a strong connection through faith, hope, and love. The mention of Christ's coming highlights the significance of this steadfastness, as it determines whether believers will confidently face His judgment or shrink back in shame. The commentators emphasize the need to hold fast to Christ's teachings, with a hopeful expectation of being made pure and righteous in His presence. The overall message encourages believers to cultivate unwavering faith, aspiring to resemble Christ and eagerly awaiting His return.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Little children, abide in Him...in order that if He should be manifested, we may have confidence and may not shrink with shame from Him at His coming." 
2. "The doing of righteousness, the sign of new birth from God: the opposite, the sign of not being of God." 
3. "Hoping as we do to be entirely like Christ at His appearing, each one of us, in pursuance of this hope, is even now approximating to this perfect likeness by purifying himself even as He is pure."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Little children—Greek, 'little sons,' as in 1 John 2:1; believers of every stage and age."
2. "John repeats his monition with a loving appellation, as a father addressing dear children."
3. "Ashamed before him—literally, 'from Him'; shrink back from Him ashamed."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Abide in him; that, when he shall appear - In the end of the world, to receive his people to himself." 
2. "We may have confidence - Greek, boldness - παῤῥησίαν parrēsian. This word is commonly used to denote openness, plainness, or boldness in speaking."
3. "It means that they would not be overwhelmed and confounded at the coming of the Saviour, by its being then found that all their hopes were fallacious."

### John Calvin:
1. "For faith is not a naked and frigid apprehension of Christ, but a lively and real sense of his power, which produces confidence."
2. "Indeed, faith cannot stand, while tossed daily by so many waves, except it looks to the coming of Christ, and, supported by his power, brings tranquillity to the conscience."
3. "But a godly confidence delights to look on God. Hence it is, that the godly calmly wait for Christ, nor do they dread his coming."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Throughout this Epistle the human side is never forgotten, while all is referred finally to the indwelling of the Son."
2. "The ‘coming’ of the Lord is His coming to judgment; but St. John here uses, and here only, a gracious word that signifies His presence."
3. "We are exhorted to ‘abide in Him;’ and whether we meet Him or are brought with Him, the confidence will be the same."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "John tenderly repeats the exhortation, 'keep on abiding in him.'" 
2. "A clear reference to the second coming of Christ which may be at any time." 
3. "Purpose clause with ινα and the ingressive second aorist active subjunctive of εχω, 'that we may get boldness.'"

### John Gill:
1. "Abide in Christ, that is, in the exercise of faith on him, of hope in him, and love to him."
2. "Hold fast his word and Gospel, and abide by his truth and ordinances."
3. "They will have on the wedding garment, the righteousness of Christ, and will stand before the throne without fault, spot, or blemish."

### John Wesley:
1. "Abide in him, that we — A modest expression."
2. "May not be ashamed before him at his coming — O how will ye, Jews, Socinians, nominal Christians, be ashamed in that day!"
3. "Having finished his address to each, he now returns to all in general."
