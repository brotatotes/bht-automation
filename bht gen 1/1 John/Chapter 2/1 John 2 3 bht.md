# 1 John 2:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, it is evident that true knowledge of God goes beyond intellectual understanding. It is characterized by an experiential and heartfelt knowledge of Him as the advocate, the righteous one, and the propitiation. This knowledge is demonstrated through obedience to God's commandments, specifically those related to faith and love. As we keep His commandments in faith and with a desire to bring Him glory, it becomes a tangible evidence of our genuine knowledge of Him and His will.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "It is herein," and herein only, that we know (present tense) that we have knowledge of (perfect tense, once-for-all obtained and continuing knowledge of) Him" (1 John 2:4; 1 John 2:13; 1 John 2:14).

### Albert Barnes:
1. "And hereby we do know that we know him - To wit, by that which follows, we have evidence that we are truly acquainted with him, and with the requirements of his religion; that is, that we are truly his friends."
2. "The apostle had stated in the previous part of this Epistle some of the leading points revealed by the Christian religion, and he here enters on the consideration of the nature of the evidence required to show that we are personally interested in it, or that we are true Christians."
3. "The first, the grand evidence - that without which all others would be vain - he says is, that we keep his commandments."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The expression points to what follows, 'if we keep His commandments,' yet with a covert reference to that idea as generally implied in the previous words concerning fellowship with God and walking in the light."
2. "We know [γινωσκομεν] . Or, perceive. By experience, from day to day; distinguished from oidamen we know, expressing absolute, immediate knowledge of a fact once for all."
3. "We keep His commandments [τας εντολας αυτου τηρωμεν] . A phrase peculiar to John and occurring elsewhere only Matthew 19:17; 1 Timothy 6:14."

### John Calvin:
1. "The knowledge of God, derived from the gospel, is not ineffectual, but that obedience proceeds from it."
2. "No evil has been more common in all ages than vainly to profess God’s name."
3. "The doctrine of the gospel is a lively mirror in which we contemplate the image of God, and are transformed into the same."
