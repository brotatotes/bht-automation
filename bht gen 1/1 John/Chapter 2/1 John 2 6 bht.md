# 1 John 2:6 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The verse being described is a call for believers to abide in Christ, to maintain a lasting and intimate relationship with Him. This relationship is not just a mere profession of faith, but one that requires consistent action and imitation of Christ's example. In this way, believers are called to live as Christ lived, walking in love and holiness, and reflecting His character in the world.

1. "The state of being in Him is carried forward a step further by the expression 'abide in Him'".
2. "The man saying that He abideth in Him ought... even as He walked".
3. "Not any one particular of Christ's walk upon earth is here pointed at, but the whole of his life of holiness and purity and love".

1. "Abideth—implying a condition lasting, without intermission, and without end."

1. "He that saith, he abideth in him... abides or remains in the belief of his doctrines, and in the comfort and practice of religion."
2. "The expression is one of those which refer to the intimate union between Christ and his people."
3. "Ought to live and act as he did... imitate him in all things."

### Marvin Vincent:

### John Calvin:

### John Gill:
1. "Once in Christ, and always in Christ; they are set as a seal on his arm and heart, which can never be removed." 
2. "They are in his arms, and can never be plucked from thence; and are members of him, and can never be disunited from him."
3. "He that saith I am in him...ought himself also to walk even as he walked; as Christ walked, lived, and acted, so ought he; that is, to imitate him and follow him, as he has him for an example."

These quotes emphasize the unbreakable bond between believers and Christ, highlighting the eternal security and unity found in Him. They also emphasize the call for believers to imitate Christ's actions and character, particularly in the expression of love towards others.

### John Wesley:
1. "He that saith he abideth in him — which implies a durable state; a constant, lasting knowledge of, and communion with, him."
2. "Ought himself — Otherwise they are vain words."
3. "So to walk, even as he walked — In the world."
