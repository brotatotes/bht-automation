# 1 John 3:23 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This verse emphasizes the interconnection between faith in Christ and love for one another. It urges believers to have faith in the name of Jesus, not just as a mere acknowledgment, but as a deep trust in His character and finished work. This faith is a continuous commitment and leads to a personal relationship with Him. Additionally, the verse highlights the importance of love, which flows from faith in Christ and is a commandment from God Himself. It is through faith and love that believers can truly fulfill God's greatest commandment.

1. "Summing up of all these commandments in one: faith in Christ, and brotherly love according to Christ’s command." 
2. "His commandment is this, that we should believe the name of His Son Jesus Christ, and love one another." 
3. "To believe the Gospel message concerning Him, and Him as living in it, in all His fulness."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Faith and love are not separate commandments, but are indissolubly united."
2. "We cannot truly love one another without faith in Christ, nor can we truly believe in Him without love."
3. "Believe - once for all; Greek aorist."
4. "On the name of his Son - on all that is revealed in the Gospel concerning Him, and on Himself in respect to His person, offices, and atoning work."
5. "As he - as Jesus gave us commandment."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "His commandment, by way of eminence; the leading, principal thing which he enjoins on us; the commandment which lies at the foundation of all true obedience."
2. "That we should believe on the name of his Son Jesus Christ."
3. "And love one another ... This follows from the other, and hence they are mentioned as together constituting his commandment."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Believing on the name of Jesus is not simply acknowledging His existence or even affirming His identity as the Son of God. It is a deep, personal trust and reliance on His character, His promises, and His finished work on the cross."

2. "To believe on the name of Jesus is to surrender our own self-sufficiency and place our full confidence in Him as our Savior and Lord. It is an act of faith that transforms our lives and brings us into a personal relationship with Him."

3. "Believing on the name of Jesus is not a one-time event, but a continuous, ongoing commitment to trust and follow Him. It is a daily choice to rely on His grace, seek His guidance, and walk in obedience to His Word."

### John Calvin:
1. "The meaning is, that such is the discord between us and God, that we are kept off from an access to him, except we are united by love to one another."
2. "John only shews, that God favors none with the honor and privilege of intercourse with himself but his own children, even those who have been regenerated by his Spirit."
3. "He defines briefly as well as lucidly in what the whole perfection of a holy life consists. There is then no reason that we should allege any difficulty, since God does by no means lead us about through long labyrinths, but simply and shortly sets before us what is right and what he approves."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The sum of faith is in the name of Jesus, and the sum of duty is love." 
2. "The Father’s will is that we should believe on the name of his Son Jesus Christ." 
3. "The commandment of Jesus to love is born out of the Father’s command to believe."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "We should believe either in a crisis or the continuous tenor of our lives." 
2. "The 'name' of Jesus Christ here stands for all that he is, 'a compressed creed'." 
3. "We should love one another, as he has already urged and as he will repeat."

### John Gill:
1. "Faith in Christ and love for one another are inseparable; where one is, the other is; faith works by love."
2. "To believe in [Jesus] is not merely to believe facts about him, but to go forth in special and spiritual acts upon him, such as looking at him, coming to him, venturing on him, trusting in him for life and salvation."
3. "Faith in Christ Jesus is a fruit of electing grace, a blessing of the covenant of grace, and is peculiar to the elect of God and sheep of Christ; it cannot belong to the law of works."

### John Wesley:
1. "And this is his commandment — All his commandments in one word." 
2. "That we should believe and love — in the manner and degree which he hath taught." 
3. "This is the greatest and most important command that ever issued from the throne of glory."
