# 1 John 4:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, the act of confessing Jesus as the Son of God is emphasized as a proof of genuine faith and a condition for experiencing the life of God. The confession is not merely lip service, but a heartfelt acknowledgment of Jesus' divinity. This confession is significant, as it signifies a surrender of one's life to Christ and is accompanied by a belief in Him, obedience to His commands, and love for one another. Through this confession and belief, God dwells in the individual, establishing a deep connection and transforming their life.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Whosoever shall confess that Jesus is the Son of God - In the true sense, and from the heart." 
2. "This will always prove that a man is a Christian." 
3. "But the passage cannot mean that if he merely says so in words, or if he does it insincerely, or without any proper sense of the truth, it will prove that he is a Christian."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Whosoever [ος εαν] . Lit., who if there be any." - This phrase emphasizes the inclusiveness of the statement, indicating that anyone who meets the condition can benefit from it.

2. "Shall confess." - This suggests that the act of confessing is essential for the fulfillment of the promise. It implies an acknowledgment and declaration of belief in the Son of God.

3. "Son of God." - This title refers to the divinity of Jesus and highlights the significance of believing in him. It signifies a recognition of Jesus' unique relationship with God and the implications of that relationship for salvation.

### John Calvin:
1. "Faith and confession are used indiscriminately in the same sense; for though hypocrites may wisely boast of faith, yet the apostle here acknowledges none of those who ordinarily confess, but such as truly and from the heart believe."
2. "After having said in general, that men are so united to Christ by faith, that Christ unites them to God, he subjoined what they themselves had seen so that he accommodated a general truth to those to whom he was writing."
3. "Then follows the exhortation, to love one another as they were loved by God. Therefore the order and connection of his discourse is this, — Faith in Christ, makes God to dwell in men, and we are partakers of this grace; but as God is love, no one dwells in him except he loves his brethren."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "This confession of the deity of Jesus Christ implies surrender and obedience also, not mere lip service."
2. "This confession is proof (if genuine) of the fellowship with God."
3. "The confession of Jesus Christ as Lord is not just a verbal declaration, but a surrendering of one's life to Him."

### John Gill:
1. "Whosoever shall confess that Jesus is the Son of God... which was the test of faith in those times, and the grand article of belief."
2. "This confession is such as is attended with a believing in Christ from the heart unto righteousness, life, and salvation, and a cheerful obedience to his ordinances and commands."
3. "God dwelleth in him, and he in God... this should encourage to an open and hearty confession of Christ as the Son of God, and Saviour of sinners."

### John Wesley:

2. "...that Jesus is the Son of God..." - This quote highlights the specific confession that is required - acknowledging Jesus as the Son of God, affirming His divine nature.

3. "...God abideth in him." - This quote reveals the profound result of such confession - the indwelling presence of God in the one who openly confesses their faith in Jesus.
