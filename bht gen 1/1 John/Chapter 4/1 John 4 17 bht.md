# 1 John 4:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This verse speaks of the fulfillment and perfection of love through our relationship with God. It highlights the confidence and fearlessness we will have when facing judgment, and the assurance of our status as children of God. The presence of genuine love for our brethren serves as evidence of our spiritual rebirth and inheritance in Christ. The verse emphasizes the communion with God that allows our love to be perfected and our fear to be transformed into boldness.

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "Love dwelling in us advances to its consummation 'with us' that is, as it is concerned with us."
2. "Herein is our love perfected, namely, in God dwelling in us, and our dwelling in God, involving as its result 'that we can have confidence (or boldness) in the day of judgment.'"
3. "The ground of our 'confidence' is, 'because even as He (Christ) is, we also are in this world' (and He will not, in that day, condemn those who are like Himself), that is, we are righteous as He is righteous, especially in respect to that which is the sum of righteousness, love."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The love that is within us, or in us, is made perfect."
2. "Love is rendered complete or entire in the manner in which the apostle specifies."
3. "He who has true love to God will have nothing to fear in the day of judgment."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Love has its fulfillment in freeing us from fear, and inspiring us with boldness even in view of the final judgment." 
2. "The fellowship of God with man and of man with God, carries with it the consummation of love." 
3. "The love of God is perfected with us, in communion with us, through our abiding in Him and He in us."

### John Calvin:
1. "He shews to what purpose God has in love embraced us, and how we enjoy that grace manifested to us in Christ."
2. "It is an invaluable benefit, that we can dare boldly to stand before God."
3. "The image of God, when it appears in us, is as it were the seal of his adoption."

These quotes highlight the purpose of God's love, the confidence we have in His love, and the importance of reflecting His image as evidence of our adoption. They emphasize the gratuitous nature of salvation and the need to have faith in God's grace alone.

### Archibald T Robertson:
