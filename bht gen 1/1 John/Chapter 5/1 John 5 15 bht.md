# 1 John 5:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, commentators emphasize the assurance and confidence that believers have when they pray to God. They highlight that God hears and answers all prayers, even if the answer is not immediate. The commentators emphasize that prayers offered in faith, according to God's will, are never lost or disregarded, but are granted in God's perfect timing and way. They also stress the unlimited scope of our prayers, encouraging believers to bring any request before God. Ultimately, this verse teaches that believers can have assurance that their prayers are heard and answered by a faithful and caring God.

### Henry Alford:
1. "If we know that He heareth us whatsoever we ask, we know that we have the petitions which we have asked from Him." 
2. "The perf. reaches through all our past prayers to this moment. All these we ἔχομεν: not one of them is lost: He has heard, He has answered them all: we know that we have them in the truest sense, in possession." 
3. "The παρ ʼ αὐτοῦ belongs, by the arrangement of the words, to ᾐτήκαμεν, not to ἔχομεν, as Huther."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "We have, as present possessions, everything whatsoever we desired (asked) from Him."
2. "Not one of our past prayers offered in faith, according to His will, is lost."
3. "Like Hannah, we can rejoice over them as granted even before the event; and can recognize the event when it comes to pass, as not from chance, but obtained by our past prayers."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Even though we may not 'see' immediately that the prayer is answered, we may have the utmost confidence that it is not disregarded, and that it will be answered in the way best adapted to promote our good."
2. "The 'argument' here is derived from the faithfulness of God; from the assurance which we feel that when he has promised to hear us, there will be, sooner or later, a real answer to the prayer."
3. "The answer to prayer is sometimes delayed, though ultimately granted. There may be reasons why the answer should be deferred, and the promise is not that it shall be immediate."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Whatsoever we ask." - This phrase emphasizes the unlimited scope of our prayers, indicating that we can bring any request before God.
2. "The whole phrase is governed by the verb hear." - This highlights that God not only hears our prayers but also responds to them, showing His attentive and caring nature.
3. "If we know that He heareth our every petition." - This statement encourages us to have confidence in God's faithfulness, assuring us that He listens to every prayer we offer.

### John Calvin:
1. "The godly pray or ask for nothing from God but what they obtain."
2. "The faithful do not give loose reins to their desires, nor indulge in anything that may please them, but always regard in their prayers what God commands."
3. "This ease and security ought not, however, to abate in them their earnestness in prayer, for he who is certain of a happy event ought not to abstain from praying to God."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "An amplification of the second limitation" (D. Smith).
2. "We know that we have (οιδαμεν οτ εχομεν). Repetition of οιδαμεν, the confidence of possession by anticipation."
3. "We have the answer already as in Mark 11:24."

### John Gill:
1. "As it may be assured he does hear and answer all such persons that ask according to his will."
2. "For as it is the nature of that holy confidence, which believers have in God, to believe whatever they ask according to his will, in general, shall be grappled."
3. "They are as sure of having it in God's own time and way, as if they now had it in hand and fact."

### John Wesley:
1. "Faith anticipates the blessings."
2. "The petitions which we asked of him — Even before the event."
3. "And when the event comes, we know it comes in answer to our prayer."
