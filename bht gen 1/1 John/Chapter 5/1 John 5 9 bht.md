### Henry Alford:
1. "If we receive the testimony of men, the testimony of God is greater."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "We do accept (and rightly so) the witness of veracious men, fallible though they be, much more ought we to accept the infallible witness of God (the Father)."
2. "The testimony of the Father is, as it were, the basis of the testimony of the Word and of the Holy Spirit; just as the testimony of the Spirit is, as it were, the basis of the testimony of the water and the blood."
3. "What that testimony is we find above in 1 John 5:1, 'Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God'; and below in 1 John 5:10; 1 John 5:11."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "We are constantly acting on the belief that what others say is true; that what the members of our families, and our neighbors say, is true; that what is reported by travelers is true; that what we read in books, and what is sworn to in courts of justice, is true."
2. "The mind is so made that it must credit the testimony borne by others; and if this should cease even for a single day, the affairs of the world would come to a pause."
3. "The only question on which the mind ever hesitates is, whether we actually have his testimony, or certainly know what he bears witness to; when that is ascertained, the human mind is so made that it cannot believe that God would deliberately deceive a world."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "If we receive, as we do."
2. "The witness of God is greater."
3. "This is the witness of God, even the fact that, etc."

### John Calvin:
1. "How ungrateful men are when they reject Christ, who has been approved, as he has related, by God."
2. "If in worldly affairs we stand to the words of men, who may lie and deceive, how unreasonable it is that God should have less credit given to him."
3. "Our own corruption alone prevents us from receiving Christ, since he gives us full proof for believing in his power."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The apostle supposes as a general truth that we receive the testimony of credible witnesses."
2. "The divine being ‘greater’ as accompanying and rendering infallible the human witness to the Saviour’s Messiahship and salvation."
3. "The entire series of attestations borne in the Old Testament and in the New by evangelists and apostles is no other than one grand attestation of God Himself, who witnesseth one thing only, that all His witness by man’s agency is concerning His Son."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "The conditions for a legally valid witness are laid down in Deuteronomy 19:15." 
2. "God is always true." 
3. "The Son comes, the Spirit witnesses, the Father has witnessed."

### John Gill:
1. "The witness of a sufficient number of credible men, of men of good character and report, is always admitted in any case, and in any court of judicature."
2. "If an human testimony may be, and is received, the testimony of God is greater; more valuable, surer, and to be more firmly depended on, since it must be infallible."
3. "The witness of the Father, the Word, and the Spirit, must be the testimony of God, since, though three persons, they are one God."

### John Wesley:
1. "The testimony of God is greater - Of higher authority, and much more worthy to be received."
2. "This very testimony which God the Father, together with the Word and the Spirit, hath testified of the Son, as the Saviour of the world."
3. "As we do continually, and must do in a thousand instances."
