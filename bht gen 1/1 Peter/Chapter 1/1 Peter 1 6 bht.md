# 1 Peter 1:6 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this beautiful verse, commentators describe the deep joy and exultation that believers experience despite their present sufferings. The faithful find solace in their hope of future salvation, enabling them to rejoice even in the midst of afflictions and trials. Their confidence in God's promises sustains them, allowing sorrow to fade away and making room for the overwhelming joy that comes from knowing their inheritance is secure. The trials believers face serve as a test of character and can be endured with calmness and patience, for they serve a greater purpose in God's plan.

### Henry Alford:
1. "The present ἀγαλλιᾶσθε in both places must be a categorical present, used of a future time."
2. "I prefer the former interpretation, and the quasi-future sense of ἀγαλλιᾶσθε in both places."
3. "Looking back from the time of which exultation, the grief is regarded as passed away and gone."

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "Salvation is realized by faith as a thing so actually present as to cause exulting joy in spite of existing afflictions."
2. "The realized joy of the coming salvation makes the present grief seem as a thing of the past."
3. "The temptations or trials that believers face are meant to test their faith."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The prospect which they had of the future inheritance was to them a source of the highest joy, even in the midst of their many sufferings and trials."
2. "The hope which they had of their future inheritance enabled them to rejoice even in the midst of persecutions and trials. It not only sustained them, but it made them happy."
3. "There was some good to be accomplished by their afflictions, which made it desirable and proper that they should be thus afflicted."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The word is always employed in the New Testament for great or lively joy." 
2. "The word gives a vivid picture of the diversity of the trials, emphasizing this idea rather than that of their number, which is left to be inferred."
3. "It embraces all that goes to furnish a test of character."

### John Calvin:
1. "But the faithful know by experience, how these things can exist together, much better than can be expressed in words."
2. "Thus sorrow does not prevent their joy, but, on the contrary, give place to it."
3. "For all who raise hither their minds, find it an easy thing calmly to bear all evils."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "In all which blessings into which God begat you, ye rejoice."
2. "Peter connects one section with another by carrying over the closing word or idea."
3. "The 'temptations' will refer here... to the threatenings and slanders which these Christians had to endure from heathen neighbors."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Either makes sense, though possibly καιρω is correct." 
2. "Present middle indicative (rather than imperative) of αγαλλιαομα, late verb from αγαλλομα, to rejoice."
3. "Accusative case of time (ολιγον) probably as in Mark 6:31, though it can be used of space (to a small extent) as in Luke 5:3."
4. "Present active neuter singular participle of δε (it is necessary). Some MSS. have εστιν after δεον (periphrastic construction)."
5. "First aorist passive participle (concessive circumstantial use) of λυπεω, to make sorrowful (from λυπη, sorrow), old and common verb."

### John Gill:
1. "Regenerated persons rejoice, in that they are the elect of God, according to his everlasting love towards them, and free grace, and good will."
2. "The cause of this heaviness is not only indwelling corruptions, the hidings of God's face, and the temptations of Satan, but afflictions and persecutions."
3. "Afflictive dispensations, in whatsoever form, are necessary, by the will of God, who has appointed them, and therefore must be, and ought to be, quietly submitted to, and patiently borne, on that consideration."

### John Wesley:
1. "Ye even now greatly rejoice, though now for a little while - Such is our whole life, compared to eternity."
2. "If need be — For it is not always needful. If God sees it to be the best means for your spiritual profit."
3. "Ye are in heaviness — Or sorrow; but not in darkness; for they still retained both faith, hope, and love; yea, at this very time were rejoicing with joy unspeakable."
