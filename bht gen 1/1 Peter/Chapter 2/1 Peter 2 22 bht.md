### John Calvin:
1. "He, at the same time, shews how far we come short of what Christ was, when he says, that there was no guile found in his mouth; for he who offends not by his tongue, says James, is a perfect man." 

2. "He then declares that there was in Christ the highest perfection of innocency, such as no one of us can dare claim for himself." 

3. "It hence appears more fully how unjustly he suffered beyond all others. There is, therefore, no reason why any one of us should refuse to suffer after his example, since no one is so conscious of having acted rightly, as not to know that he is imperfect."

### Philip Schaff:

3. "The statement is given...with the direct and positive force of simple historical tenses, which may imply...that in no instance did He ever do the wrong deed, or say the guileful word."
