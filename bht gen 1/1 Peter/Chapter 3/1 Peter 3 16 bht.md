# 1 Peter 3:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this Bible verse, the importance of maintaining a good conscience is emphasized. It is described as a conscience that does not accuse one of wrongdoing, and that results in a life and behavior aligned with the teachings of the Gospel. The righteous actions of believers can serve as a rebuke to those who speak against them, causing them to feel ashamed of their words. By joining meekness and fear, believers can confidently and humbly defend and represent the doctrines of the Gospel, reflecting their faith in Christ through their conduct.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Having a good conscience—the secret spring of readiness to give account of our hope."
2. "Profession without practice has no weight."
3. "Those who have a good conscience can afford to give an account of their hope 'with meekness.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Having a good conscience - That is, a conscience that does not accuse you of having done wrong."
2. "No man, indeed, should act against the dictates of his conscience; but there may have been a previous wrong in not using proper means to ascertain what is right."
3. "There is no penalty that will more certainly be inflicted, sooner or later, than that incurred by a guilty conscience."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Conscience is, essentially, determining of the self-consciousness by the spirit as the essential principle of life."
2. "Conscience does not only offer itself to show us the way we should walk in, but it likewise carries its own authority with it, that it is our natural guide, the guide assigned us by the Author of our nature."
3. "The conscience, in short, is a different faculty of the mind from the mere understanding. We must hold it to be simple and unresolvable till we fall in with a successful decomposition of it into its elements."

### John Calvin:
1. "Unless our minds are endued with meekness, contentions will immediately break forth."
2. "Meekness is set in opposition to pride and vain ostentation, and also to excessive zeal."
3. "The integrity of conscience alone is that which gives us confidence in speaking as we ought."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The term conscience seems to make a nearer approach in this passage than in the previous...to the modern philosophical definitions of it as the ‘principle of reflection in men by which they distinguish between, approve and disapprove, their own actions’."
2. "The moral quality of a man’s actions is attested to him...by the heart, specially as that is aided and enlightened by the revelation of God’s law...In the New Testament it is by a light within the man, or by this inner witness, termed conscience in the Epistles."
3. "It is essential that it be given not only in meekness and fear, but in the calm, clear strength of a mind conscious of nothing in the walk to give the lie to the apology."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Having a good conscience" - This phrase emphasizes the importance of maintaining a clear and guilt-free conscience in one's actions and decisions.
2. "That they may be put to shame" - This suggests that the righteous behavior of believers can serve as a rebuke to those who speak against them, causing them to feel ashamed of their actions.
3. "In Christ" - This phrase highlights the significance of being united with Christ and the transformative power it has on one's life and conduct. It implies that our actions should reflect our identity in Christ.

### John Gill:
1. "Having a good conscience... a life and conversation according to the dictates of such a conscience, in the uprightness and sincerity of it, and by the grace of God, and according to the Gospel." 
2. "A conscientious discharge of duty, both to God and men, which is one way of defending and recommending the doctrines of the Gospel."
3. "When they shall see your modest and humble deportment in the world, and before them, and with what reverence and esteem you treat them."

### John Wesley:
1. "Beware of anger, to which the very consciousness of your innocence may betray you."
2. "Join with a good conscience meekness and fear, and you obtain a complete victory."
3. "Your good conversation in Christ — That is, which flows from faith in him."
