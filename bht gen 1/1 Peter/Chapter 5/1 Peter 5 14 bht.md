1. "Salute one another in a kiss of love."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Kiss of charity": the token of love to God and the brethren. Love and holiness are inseparable.
2. "Peace" flowing from salvation, was Christ's own salutation after the resurrection, and from Him Peter derives it.
3. "Be with you all that are in Christ Jesus": The oldest manuscripts omit "Jesus." Contrast, "Be with you all," Romans 16:24; 1 Corinthians 16:23.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Greet ye one another with a kiss of charity - A kiss of love; a common method of affectionate salutation in the times of the apostles."
2. "Peace be with you all that are in Christ Jesus - That are true Christians."
3. "See the notes at Romans 16:16; Ephesians 6:23 note; Philippians 4:7 note."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The 'kiss of charity' mentioned here is not merely a physical act, but a symbol of genuine love and affection among believers." 

2. "This verse reminds us of the importance of expressing our love and unity within the body of Christ, as it serves as a testimony to the world of our faith." 

3. "The kiss of charity signifies a deep bond and mutual care among believers, reflecting the love that Christ has shown us and the love we should have for one another."

### John Calvin:
1. "The kiss of love should be a genuine expression of sincerity from the heart."
2. "The external act of the kiss should reflect the inner sincerity of the heart."
3. "The kiss of love is a symbol of genuine affection and unity among believers."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The Christian practice of saluting with a kiss was a token of brotherly love and had 'the specific character of Christian consecration.'"
2. "The ancienter, the better ceremonies of religion are; howbeit, not absolutely true and without exception; but true only so far forth as those different ages do agree in the state of those things, for which at the first those rites, orders, and ceremonies were instituted."
3. "The closing words 'in Christ' are peculiarly Pauline in tone, although Paul himself is not in the habit of defining the subjects of his benedictions by that phrase."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "With a kiss of love (εν φιληματ αγαπης). As in 1 Corinthians 16:20." 
2. "The abuse of this custom led to its confinement to men with men and women with women and to its final abandonment (Apost. Const. ii. 57, 12)."
3. "That are in Christ (τοις εν Χριστω). This is the greatest of all secret orders and ties, one that is open to all who take Christ as Lord and Saviour."

### John Gill:
1. "The kiss of charity is not only opposite to everything that is lascivious and impure, but is expressive of true love and affection, and is hearty and sincere."
2. "A kiss and love differ, the one may be without the other, a mere compliment, a show of friendship, and not arise from sincere love."
3. "To those who are in Christ Jesus, the apostle wishes peace, temporal, spiritual, and eternal."
