# 1 Thessalonians 5:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This verse encourages believers to find joy in their relationship with God, rejoicing in Him regardless of their circumstances. It teaches the importance of prayer in maintaining a mindset of joy and gratitude, and embracing a spirit of continuous communication with God. It also emphasizes the significance of giving thanks in all situations as a reflection of Christian maturity. Rejoicing, praying, and giving thanks form the basis of a life marked by unwavering joy and contentment in God.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Rejoice evermore" reminds us that joy is not dependent on our circumstances, but on our relationship with God. It is a constant state of contentment and gratitude, regardless of what we may be facing.

2. This verse calls us to cultivate a mindset of joy, choosing to focus on the goodness of God and the blessings in our lives. It is a reminder that joy is not just a fleeting emotion, but a deep-rooted attitude that can sustain us through the ups and downs of life.

3. "Rejoice evermore" is an invitation to find joy in the Lord, even in the midst of trials and challenges. It is a call to trust in God's sovereignty and to find comfort in His presence, knowing that He is working all things together for our good.

### John Calvin:
1. "I refer this to moderation of spirit, when the mind keeps itself in calmness under adversity, and does not give indulgence to grief."
2. "For when he recommends constant praying, he points out the way of rejoicing perpetually, for by this means we ask from God alleviation in connection with all our distresses."
3. "But lest we should be borne down by grief, sorrow, anxiety, and fear, he bids us repose in the providence of God."

These quotes highlight the importance of maintaining a calm and composed mind, finding joy in prayer, and relying on God's providence to overcome adversity.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "It is a scandalous misprision, vulgarly admitted, concerning religion, that it is altogether sullen and sour, requiring a dull, lumpish, morose kind of life, barring all delight, all mirth, all good humour."
2. "It alone is the never-failing source of true, pure, steady joy; such as is deeply rooted in the heart, immoveably founded in the reason of things, permanent like the immortal spirit wherein it dwelleth, and like the eternal objects whereon it is fixed."

### John Gill:

3. "Rejoice...in things spiritual, that our names are written in heaven, and we are redeemed by the blood of Christ, and called by his grace, and shall be glorified together with him."

### John Wesley:
1. "Rejoice evermore — In uninterrupted happiness in God."
2. "Pray without ceasing — Which is the fruit of always rejoicing in the Lord."
3. "In everything give thanks — Which is the fruit of both the former. This is Christian perfection."
