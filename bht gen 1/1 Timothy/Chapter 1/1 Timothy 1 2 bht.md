# 1 Timothy 1:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This verse, rich in blessings, describes the deep affection between Paul and Timothy. Paul acknowledges Timothy as his true son in faith, adopting him with a father's love and considering him a genuine disciple. As they mature, Paul adds the element of mercy to his usual greeting of grace and peace, highlighting the importance of this tender grace in the ministry. Through this verse, we learn that experiencing mercy and extending it equips believers to serve and spread the gospel.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "My true child in faith."

### John Calvin:
1. "Paul means by it, that he owns Timothy to be a true and not a bastard son, and wishes that others should acknowledge him to be such; and he even applauds Timothy in the same manner as if he were another Paul."
2. "While Paul claims for himself the appellation of father, he does it in such a manner as not to take away or diminish the smallest portion of the honor which is due to God."
3. "Thus God, and God alone, strictly speaking, was Timothy’s spiritual Father; but Paul, who was God’s minister in begetting Timothy, lays claim to this title, by what may be called a subordinate right."

3. "‘Mercy’ and ‘peace’ are found together in Galatians 6:16."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Legitimate, not spurious."

### John Gill:
1. "Timothy was a real Christian, a true believer, and a hearty and upright professor and preacher of the faith of Christ, as well as truly regenerated by the Spirit of God."
2. "When he wishes 'grace' to Timothy, he may mean a fresh discovery of the love and free favour of God unto him, and an increase of grace in him, and of the gifts of the Spirit upon him."
3. ### John Wesley:
1. "Grace, mercy, peace" - St. Paul's heartfelt wishes for the churches, encompassing the full spectrum of divine blessings and compassion.
2. "Mercy, the most tender grace" - A profound understanding of mercy, the gentlest form of grace, is essential for those who minister the gospel.
3. "The experience of this prepares a man" - The personal experience of receiving and extending mercy equips individuals to serve as ministers of the gospel.
