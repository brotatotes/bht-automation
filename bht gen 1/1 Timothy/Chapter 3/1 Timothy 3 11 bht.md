# 1 Timothy 3:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this passage, commentators express their views on the qualities and roles of women associated with the deacons in the Church. They suggest that these women may be deaconesses who support and assist the deacons in their ministry. It is emphasized that these women should possess integrity, a good reputation, and faithful behavior. They are expected to be reliable and trustworthy, avoiding slander and negative influences. Their faithfulness should extend beyond religious matters, encompassing their relationships and responsibilities towards others. It is crucial for these women to exemplify moral character and demonstrate commitment and loyalty in all areas of life.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "For there is no reason that special rules should be laid down as to the wives of the deacons, and not also as to the wives of the bishops or overseers."
2. "Thus it appears he requires the same qualifications in female deacons as in deacons, only with such modifications as the difference of sex suggested."
3. "Faithful in all things—of life as well as faith. Trustworthy in respect to the alms committed to them and their other functions."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "No one can doubt that the character of a woman may be such, that it is not desirable that her husband should be an officer in the church. A bad woman ought not to be entrusted with any additional power or influence." 

2. "They should not be women who were in the habit of calumniating others, or aspersing their character. Mingling as they would with the church, and having an opportunity to claim acquaintance with many, it would be in their power, if they chose, to do great injury to the character of others."

3. "Faithful in all things - To their husbands, to their families, to the church, to the Saviour."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "A Deacon whose wife is wanting in the qualities required in him, is not to be chosen."
2. "She would sustain an active relation to his office, and by her ministries would increase his efficiency."
3. "By frivolity, slander, or intemperance, would bring him and his office into disrepute."

### John Calvin:
1. "The wives must be aids to their husbands in their office."
2. "Their behavior must excel that of others."
3. "They must be supportive and exemplary in their conduct."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The mention of women in this parenthetic way is, in any case, remarkable, seeing that the writer returns to the deacons in the next verse."
2. "The absence alike of the article and the pronoun in the Greek, and the obvious parallelism with 1 Timothy 3:8 make it far more probable that St. Paul is speaking of the women who had a like work, the deaconesses of the Apostolic Church."
3. "The functions of these deaconesses were probably analogous to those of their male colleagues, such as the distribution of alms to their own sex, caring for the sick, nursing orphan children, instructing female converts, and helping in the administration of their baptism."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Apparently 'women as deacons' (Romans 16:1 about Phoebe) and not women in general or just 'wives of deacons.'" 
2. "Not slanderers... 'She-devils' in reality (Titus 2:3). 'While men are more prone to be διλογους, double-tongued, women are more prone than men to be slanderers' (White)." 
3. "Faithful in all things... the deaconesses had special temptations."

### John Gill:
1. "Their wives must be grave, of an honest report, a good behaviour, and chaste conversation; which will reflect honour and credit to their husbands."
2. "Excessive drinking is very scandalous in the female sex."
3. "The wives of deacons may be sometimes intrusted with the church's stock in their husband's absence, to impart to the poor."

### John Wesley:
1. "Faithful in all things" highlights the importance of unwavering commitment and loyalty, not just to God, but also to one's spouse and those in need. 

2. This phrase emphasizes the need for trustworthiness and reliability in all aspects of life, reflecting a deep sense of integrity and moral character.

3. It reminds us that faithfulness is not limited to religious devotion, but extends to our relationships and responsibilities towards others, demonstrating a holistic approach to living out our faith.
