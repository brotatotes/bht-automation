# 2 Corinthians 10:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Paul, in this Bible verse, humbly addresses the Corinthians, contrasting his approach to boasting with that of false apostles. He emphasizes that his "measure" of boasting is limited to the area where God has assigned him to minister. The commentators highlight the idea that Paul acknowledges the extent of God's plans and authority, while also recognizing the boundaries of his own ministry. His dedication to fulfilling his proper calling and reaching the Corinthians exemplifies his commitment to spreading the Gospel message.

### Henry Alford:
1. "We will not boast without measure, as they do who compare themselves with themselves and measure themselves by themselves, for there is no standard for, no limit to, a man’s good opinion of himself."
2. "We adopt no such vague standard for our boasting."
3. "We reach as far as to you, according to the measure of the rule which God apportioned to us as a measure."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "There is no limit to a man's high opinion of himself, so long as he measures himself by himself and his fellows, and does not compare himself with his superiors."
2. "Undeterred by the charge of vanity, he felt he must vindicate his apostolic authority by facts."
3. "Paul's 'measure' is the apportionment of his sphere of Gospel labors ruled for him by God."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Paul says that he had not boldness as they had to boast of things wholly beyond his proper rule and his actual attainments and influence."
2. "He made no boast of having done anything where he had not been himself on the ground and labored assiduously to secure the object."
3. "Paul means to say that God had appropriated a certain line or boundary as the proper limit of his sphere of action; that his appropriate sphere extended to them; that in going to them, though they were far distant from the field of his early labors, he had confined himself within the proper limits assigned him by God."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Rev., correctly, glory beyond our measure."
2. "The measure is given by God's measuring-line: 'Which God hath apportioned to us as a measure;' and his boasting extends only to this limit."
3. "Corinth being thus far the extreme limit of the field measured out for him."

### John Calvin:
1. "This he calls the measure of his rule. For everyone's rule, according to which he ought to regulate himself is this — God's gift and calling."
2. "It is not lawful for us to glow in God's gift and calling on our own account, but merely in so far as it is expedient for the glory of him, who is so liberal to us with this view — that we may acknowledge ourselves indebted to him for everything."
3. "He modestly reproves, however, their ingratitude, in overlooking, in a manner, his apostleship, which ought to have been especially in estimation among them, on the ground of God's commendation of it."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "We go simply by the line of action divinely assigned to us, and not beyond it."
2. "As the apostleship of the circumcision was specially committed to Peter, while Paul was sent to the Gentiles."
3. "He says he was only fulfilling it in his ministry there."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Beyond our measure" - This phrase suggests that there are aspects of God's work and plans that are beyond our understanding and comprehension. It highlights the vastness and limitless nature of God's ways.

2. "Of the province" - The word "province" here refers to the boundaries or limits of Paul's authority and influence. It implies that Paul's ministry extends beyond what is expected or defined, reaching even to the Corinthians.

3. "To reach even unto you" - This phrase emphasizes the extent of Paul's ministry, indicating that it goes as far as reaching the Corinthians. It demonstrates Paul's dedication and commitment to spreading the message of the Gospel to all people, regardless of their location or circumstances.

### John Gill:

2. "The apostle and his fellow labourer, desired only to speak of those things which were done of them, and of their successful labours."
3. "The line of their ministration was drawn, or the bounds of their preaching were carried from Judea, and through all the intermediate places to Corinth, so that the Corinthians were properly under the jurisdiction of the apostles."

### John Wesley:
1. "We will not, like them, boastingly extend ourselves beyond our measure."
