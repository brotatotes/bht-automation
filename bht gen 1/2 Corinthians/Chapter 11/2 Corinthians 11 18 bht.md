# 2 Corinthians 11:18 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, the speaker confronts the practice of boasting according to worldly standards. They acknowledge that many individuals partake in this fleshly boasting, highlighting the prevalent nature of this behavior. The commentators emphasize that while there is an element of foolishness in such boasting, the speaker also recognizes their own ability to boast in worldly achievements. However, the verse brings to attention the contradiction between earthly status and the values of the spirit, urging readers to shift their focus from the external and superficial aspects of life to the matters of the heart and the realm of the spirit.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Many—including the 'false teachers'." 
2. "After the flesh—as fleshly men are wont to boast, namely, of external advantages, as their birth, doings, c."
3. "I will glory also—that is, I also will boast of such fleshly advantages, to show you that even in these I am not their inferiors, and therefore ought not to be supplanted by them in your esteem though these are not what I desire to glory in."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The false teachers in Corinth boasted of their birth, rank, natural endowments, eloquence, etc."
2. "I also will boast of my endowments, which though somewhat different yet pertain in the main to the 'flesh' also."
3. "His endowments 'in the flesh' related not so much to birth and rank, but to what the flesh had endured - to stripes and imprisonments, and hunger and peril."

### John Calvin:

2. "Am I alone, or am I the first, in glorying according to the flesh? Why should that be reckoned praiseworthy in them, that is imputed to me as a fault?" - Paul questions the double standard of praising others for the same actions he is criticized for, challenging the notion of what is considered praiseworthy.

3. "To glory after the flesh, is to boast one’s self, rather in what has a tendency towards show, than in a good conscience." - This quote explains the concept of "glorying after the flesh," which refers to seeking praise and recognition based on outward appearances rather than having a genuine and righteous conscience.

2. "But he knows that it is a bit of foolishness and not like Christ." - This quote suggests that following the desires of the flesh is seen as foolishness and contrary to the example set by Christ. It implies that true wisdom lies in living in accordance with Christ's teachings.

3. "καγω (kago)." - This quote indicates the speaker's acknowledgement of their own tendency to sometimes fall into the trap of living according to the flesh. It suggests a sense of self-awareness and a desire to overcome this inclination and strive towards a more Christ-like way of living.

### John Gill:
1. "Many glory after the flesh...such as their high birth and parentage, carnal descent, circumcision, learned education, and the like."
2. "He might be allowed to boast also of such like things, so far as he could with truth and a good conscience, and in order to secure some valuable ends."
3. "He gloried of these, and of others besides them, which the false apostles could not, and thereby proved himself to be superior to them, even in external things, of which they bragged so much."

### John Wesley:
