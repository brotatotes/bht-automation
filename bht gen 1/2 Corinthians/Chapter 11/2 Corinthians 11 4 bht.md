1. "There can be no new Gospel, there is but the one which I first preached; therefore it ought not to be 'borne' by you, that the false teachers should attempt to supersede me."
2. "Another implies a distinct individual of the same kind; different implies one quite distinct in kind."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "If the false teacher could recommend a better Saviour than I have done, or a Spirit better able to sanctify and save, then there would be a propriety in your receiving him and tolerating his doctrines."
2. "The main idea doubtless is, that the gospel which he preached was as perfect as it could be, and that any change would be for the worse."
3. "Let us never forsake the gospel which we have until we are sure we can get a better. Let us adhere to the simple doctrines of the New Testament until some one can furnish better and clearer doctrines."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Another denies the identity; a different denies the similarity of nature."
2. "Ye might well bear. The expression is ironical. You gladly endure these false teachers, why do you not endure me?"
3. "You gladly endure these false teachers, why do you not endure me?"

### John Calvin:
1. "An absurd reverence of this nature he condemns, because in the mean time they showed no discrimination or judgment."
2. "Had they brought you another Christ, or another gospel, or another Spirit, different from what you received through my hands, I would assuredly approve of your regard for them, for they would be deserving of such honor."
3. "But as they have conferred upon you nothing, that I had not given you previously, what sort of gratitude do you show in all but adoring those, to whom you are indebted for nothing, while you despise me, through whom God has bestowed upon you so many and so distinguished benefits?"

### Philip Schaff:

3. "The suppressed irony is very keen."
