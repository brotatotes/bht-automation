# 2 Corinthians 3:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, the commentators emphasize the hope and confidence that comes from spreading the Gospel. They highlight the importance of speaking openly and honestly, without reservation or fear. The hope mentioned is not wishful thinking, but a confident expectation based on the promises of God. The Gospel is described as a transformative message that brings forth future glory and boldly proclaims truth in contrast to the deceptive behavior of others. This hope in Christ gives believers the strength to persevere through trials and difficulties.

3. "without reserve" - This short but powerful quote underscores the need to share the Gospel without any hesitation or reservation, fully embracing the message and its transformative power.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Hope properly is a compound emotion, made up of a desire for an object, and an expectation of obtaining it."
2. "It refers to the prospect, confidence, persuasion, anticipation which he had as the effect of what he had just said."
3. "They should use expressions which other people use; and express themselves as far as possible in the language of common life."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Plainness [παρρησια, ] . Rev., boldness."
2. "The contrast is with the dissembling with which his adversaries charged him."
3. "The plainness or boldness of Jesus' words and actions stood in stark contrast to the deceptive behavior of his opponents."

### John Calvin:
1. "The Jews, therefore, acknowledged by this, that they had not eyes to behold the law, except when veiled. This veil, he adds, is not taken away, except by Christ." 
2. "In the first place, he draws this distinction between the law and the Gospel — that the brightness of the former rather dazzled men’s eyes, than enlightened them, while in the latter, Christ’s glorious face is clearly beheld."
3. "He now triumphantly exults, on the ground that the majesty of the Gospel is not terrific, but amiable — is not hid, but is manifested familiarly to all."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Having therefore such a hope in the exercise of such a ministry, we use great boldness (or ‘openness’) of speech." 
2. "The great truths now openly proclaimed in the Gospel lay concealed under the figures of the legal economy."
3. "But now that the substance has come, the wonders of grace in Christ Jesus may with all openness and boldness of speech be proclaimed."

### John Gill:
1. "Having this confidence, and being fully persuaded that God has made us able and sufficient ministers of the Gospel, has called and qualified us for such service."
2. "We use great plainness of speech; plain and intelligible words, not ambiguous ones."
3. "We speak out all our mind, which is the mind of Christ; we declare the whole counsel of God, hide and conceal nothing that may be profitable to the churches."

### John Wesley:
1. "Hope is not wishful thinking or mere optimism, but a confident expectation based on the promises of God."
2. "This hope is not based on our own abilities or circumstances, but on the unchanging character and faithfulness of God."
3. "It is this hope that gives us the strength and endurance to persevere through trials and difficulties, knowing that our future is secure in Christ."
