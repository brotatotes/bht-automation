# 2 Corinthians 4:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this profound Bible verse, we witness the depth of human experience. We find ourselves pressed and reduced to inextricable straits, persecuted and struck down. Yet, within these tribulations, we discover strength and resilience. Though perplexed and at times in despair, we hold onto our faith, finding inner resources to endure trials and engage in our calling. This passage holds a powerful truth - that even in our darkest moments, we are not crushed, not utterly perplexed, and not destroyed.

### John Calvin:

3. "You see, how he turns to his own advantage every charge that the wicked bring against him."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Pressed (θλιβομενο). From θλιβω, to press as grapes, to contract, to squeeze."
2. "Yet not straitened (αλλ' ου στενοχωρουμενο). Each time the exception is stated by αλλ' ου."
3. "Yet not unto despair (αλλ' ουκ εξαπορουμενο). A very effective play on words here, lost, but not lost out."

### John Gill:
1. "Wherever we be, we are in the world, in which we must expect tribulation."
2. "Notwithstanding all our troubles, we have freedom at the throne of grace, and in our ministry."
3. "We are sometimes at the utmost loss about things temporal, how we shall be provided for with food and raiment; nor are we without our perplexing thoughts, doubts, and fears about spiritual affairs."

### John Wesley:
1. "Not crushed - Not swallowed up in care and anxiety."
2. "Perplexed - What course to take, but never despairing of his power and love to carry us through."
3. "The four articles in this verse respect inward, the four in the next outward, afflictions."
