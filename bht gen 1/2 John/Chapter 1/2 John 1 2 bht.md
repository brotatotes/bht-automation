1. "For the truth's sake" emphasizes the importance of truth in love, highlighting that genuine love is rooted in truth.
2. "They who love in the truth, also love on account of the truth" suggests that true love is not only based on personal feelings but also on the truth and principles that guide it.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Those who love the gospel itself will rejoice in all the effects which it produces in society, on individuals, families, neighborhoods, and their hearts will be drawn with warm affection to the places where its influence is most fully seen."
2. "Truth may be said to have taken up a permanent abode in the hearts of all who love religion."
3. "Its abode with us is not for a night or a day; not for a month or a year; not for the few years that make up mortal life; it has come to us to make our hearts its permanent home, and it is to be with us in all worlds, and while ceaseless ages shall roll away."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Enlarging on the idea of the truth: that which abideth." 
2. "Shall be with us. With us has the emphatic position in the sentence: and with us it shall be."
3. "Note the change from abideth in to shall be with."

Note: The commentary is discussing the concept of truth and its enduring nature, emphasizing that it will be with us. It also highlights the change in wording from "abideth" to "shall be with."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "the truth, as revealed by the Christ, and gradually unfolded by the Spirit, who is truth" (Brooke).
2. "abiding in Christ, and so it includes all who are in Christ."
3. "Confident assertion, not a mere wish. Note the order of the words, 'With us it shall be'."

### John Gill:
1. "Not for her high birth, nobility, or riches; but either for Christ's sake, who is the truth, and who dwells in the hearts of believers by faith."
2. "Unity of mind and judgment produces unity of affection."
3. "Where Christ enters and takes up his abode, from thence he never finally and totally departs."

### John Wesley:
1. "The truth's sake" refers to the enduring power and presence of truth within us, serving as a guiding force in our lives.

2. "Which abideth in us" highlights the ongoing nature of truth, emphasizing that it is not a fleeting or temporary concept but a constant and unwavering presence.

3. "As a living principle of faith and holiness" emphasizes that truth is not merely an intellectual belief, but a dynamic force that shapes our actions and character, leading us towards faith and holiness.
