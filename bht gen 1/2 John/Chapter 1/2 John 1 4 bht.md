# 2 John 4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, the author expresses great joy upon discovering that certain individuals from the recipient's family are steadfastly following the teachings of the Christian faith. The commentators emphasize the significance of walking in the truth, attributing it to the commandment received from God the Father. It is noted that this news brings immense happiness not only to the writer but also to godly parents and ministers who witness individuals faithfully living out their beliefs. The exact source of this information remains unknown, but it is clear that this faithfulness is deeply valued and celebrated within the community.

1. "The apostle does not say how he had learned this. It may have been that he had become personally acquainted with them when they were away from their home, or that he had learned it from others."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "I rejoiced greatly that I have found of thy children walking in truth." - St. John expresses his deep joy at finding certain children of the recipient walking in the full truth of the Christian religion.

2. "Even as we received commandment from the Father." - The commandment to believe in the name of Jesus Christ and love one another is not only from St. John but also from the Father.

3. "This great preliminary commandment omits the name of the Son because the reception of Him is its substance." - The commandment to believe in the name of Jesus Christ and love one another is so significant that it encompasses the essence of the Son's presence.
