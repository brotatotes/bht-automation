# 2 John 9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, it is explained that those who try to set themselves up as teachers without abiding in the teachings of Christ do not have God. On the other hand, those who remain in the teachings of Christ have both the Father and the Son. The verse emphasizes the importance of following Christ's teachings and highlights the exclusive role of Christ in providing access to God. The truth must be held respecting the precepts, character, and work of the Savior, and there is an intimate union between the Father and the Son.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The essential idea is, that the truth must be held respecting the precepts, the character, and the work of the Saviour."
2. "If anyone did not hold the true doctrine respecting the Saviour, he had no real knowledge of God."
3. "There is such an intimate union between the Father and the Son, that he who has just views of the one has also of the other."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The meaning is, whosoever advances beyond the limits of Christian doctrine."
2. "Such false progress is contrasted with abiding in the teaching."
3. "Not the teaching concerning Christ, but the teaching of Christ Himself and of His apostles."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "To abide in the doctrine of Christ is to remain content with His teaching or what He teaches; to go beyond it is to follow an imaginary development, and affect to be wiser than the Master Himself."
2. "The penalty is an awful one: one step beyond the commandment received in the beginning leads to the loss of God."
3. "But he that abideth in the doctrine, the same hath both the Father and the Son: the change is in St. John’s manner, from God generally to the Father and the Son."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Every one who goes ahead. Προαγω literally means to go on before... but here the bad sense comes out by the parallel clause."
2. "Not the teaching about Christ, but that of Christ which is the standard of Christian teaching as the walk of Christ is the standard for the Christian's walk."
3. "Progress we all desire, but progress toward Christ, not away from him. Jesus Christ is still ahead of us all calling us to come on to him."

### John Gill:

3. "He that abideth in the doctrine of Christ... hath both the Father and the Son; he has an interest in them both, and has knowledge of each of them, and fellowship with them."
