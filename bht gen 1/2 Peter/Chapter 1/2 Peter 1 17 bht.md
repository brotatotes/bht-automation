# 2 Peter 1:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, the voice from the excellent glory affirms the divine nature and authority of Jesus. It highlights his unique relationship with God the Father, expressing divine honor and inexpressible glory. The radiant splendor shines from above, surpassing earthly brightness. The voice testifies that Jesus is the beloved Son of God, bearing divine approval and a special mission. This event is distinct from his baptism, as the voice is heard by the apostles on a mount, enveloped in a cloud.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He was honored by God in being thus addressed."
2. "The magnificent splendor; the bright cloud which overshadowed them."

2. "Lit., having been born. Compare come (Rev., ver. 18); moved (ver. 21); and rushing wind, lit., a wind born along (Acts 2:2)."
3. "From [υπο] . Lit., by. Excellent [μεγαλοπρεπους] . Or sublime. Only here in New Testament."

### John Calvin:
1. "And though Peter does not relate all the circumstances, yet he sufficiently designates them when he says, that a voice came from the magnificent glory." 
2. "For when Christ is acknowledged by us to be him whom the Father has sent, this is our highest wisdom." 
3. "In the other part of the sentence, he announces Christ as his own Son, in whom his whole love dwells and centres."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The title ‘Father’ is appropriately introduced here, as the testimony which Christ received from God was one to His own Sonship." 
2. "The thing dwelt on is not the splendour of Christ’s own appearance on the occasion, but the tribute which came by the voice." 
3. "The testimony uttered by the voice differs very slightly from the form in which it is reported in Matthew’s Gospel."

### John Gill:
1. "Not as an inferior from a superior, for he was equal in glory with the Father, and was, and is, the brightness of his Father's glory."
2. "God the Father testifying of his glory, declaring the honour that belonged to him, as the Son of God, at the same time that an external glory was put on him, and received by him, as the son of man."
