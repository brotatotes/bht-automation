### Henry Alford:
1. "For it were better for them not to have known the way of righteousness than, having known it, to turn back from the holy commandment delivered to them." 
2. "The holy commandment, the moral law of the gospel, is designated as such because it is of moral corruption that the Apostle is treating." 
3. "The emphasis is on the commandment being delivered to them, highlighting the importance and responsibility that comes with knowing and receiving it."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "the way of righteousness—"the way of truth" (2 Peter 2:2)." 
2. "from the holy commandment—the Gospel which enjoins holiness; in opposition to their corruption." 
3. "delivered—once for all; admitting no turning back."

### Albert Barnes:
1) "It would have been better for them, for: (1)Then they would not have dishonored the cause of religion as they have now done"
2) "They would not have sunk so deep in profligacy as they now have"
3) "If people are resolved on being wicked, they had better never pretend to be good"

### John Calvin:
1. "By saying that having forsaken the commandment delivered unto them, they returned to their own pollutions, he intimates first, how inexcusable they were."
2. "The doctrine of a holy and virtuous life, though common to all and indiscriminately belonging to all, is yet peculiarly taught to those whom God favors with the light of his gospel."
3. "The faithful also do indeed sin; but as they allow not dominion to sin, they do not fall away from the grace of God, nor do they renounce the profession of sound doctrine which they have once embraced."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The ‘better’ here, as in 1 Peter 3:17 (see note there), means more to their advantage."
2. "The ‘way of righteousness’ is not quite the same as ‘the Gospel’ or ‘the way of salvation.’ It is a term for Christianity specifically on its ethical side, as a new moral life."
3. "The ‘holy commandment’ is not to be limited either to the commandment known as the ‘new commandment’ (John 13:34), or to the Sermon on the Mount. It is the ethical requirement of the Gospel as a whole, the law of life which Christ has left."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "It were better not to have known the way of righteousness." 
2. "After knowing it, to turn back."
3. "II Peter strikes a high ethical note."

### John Gill:
1. "Not that ignorance is good, or to be excused; but it would have been a lesser evil, and not so much aggravated."
2. "The Gospel, which points out the way and method of a sinner's justification before God, which is not by the works of the law, but by the righteousness of Christ imputed to them, and received by faith."
3. "Wherefore, it would have been better for them to have been in their former ignorance, either in Judaism, or in Gentilism, since proportionate to a man's light is his guilt, and so his punishment."

### John Wesley:
1. "The commandment refers to the entire law of God, which was not only communicated to the people through oral teaching but also inscribed in their hearts."
2. "This verse highlights the importance of internalizing God's law, allowing it to guide and shape our thoughts, attitudes, and actions."
3. "The commandment serves as a reminder that obedience to God's law is not merely an external act but a matter of the heart."
