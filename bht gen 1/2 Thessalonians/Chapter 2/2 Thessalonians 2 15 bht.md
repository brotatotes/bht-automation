# 2 Thessalonians 2:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This verse encourages believers to stand firm in the teachings of the Gospel and to hold fast to the truths that have been passed down to them. The commentator highlights the importance of both oral and written traditions in conveying these teachings, and emphasizes the connection between preaching and writing in delivering the message. Additionally, there is reference to a previous letter written by the apostle to further reinforce the significance of the traditions being upheld.

1. "No human reasoning powers can connect the two, God’s sovereignty and man’s freewill: all we know of them is, that the one is as certain a truth as the other."

3. "The great Apostle never fails to pursue this course."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "God's sovereign choice of believers, so far from being a ground for inaction on their part, is the strongest incentive to action and perseverance in it."
2. "The Thessalonians had not held fast his oral instructions but had suffered themselves to be imposed upon by pretended spirit-revelations, and words and letters pretending to be from Paul."
3. "Inspired tradition, in Paul's sense, is not a supplementary oral tradition completing our written Word, but it is identical with the written Word now complete."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Stand fast - Amidst all the temptations which surround you."
2. "Hold the traditions which ye have been taught... It means properly things delivered over from one to another; then anything orally delivered - any precept, doctrine, or law."
3. "This passage can furnish no authority for holding the 'traditions' which have come down from ancient times... There is not the least evidence that the apostle here meant to refer to any such thing."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Tradition, in the scriptural sense, may be either written or oral."
2. "It implies on the part of a teacher that he is not expressing his own ideas, but is delivering or handing over a message received from someone else."
3. "The prominent idea of tradition is therefore that of an authority external to the teacher."

### John Calvin:
1. "Our steadfastness and power of perseverance rest on nothing else than assurance of divine grace."
2. "The calling of God ought to fortify us against all occasions of offense in such a manner, that not even the entire ruin of the world shall shake, much less overthrow, our stability."
3. "He includes all doctrine under this term, as though he had said that they have ground on which they may stand firm, provided they persevere in sound doctrine, according as they had been instructed by him."

### Philip Schaff:

3. "Do not listen to what everyone says, but keep clearly in mind what I have communicated to you, and maintain your faith in that."

### Archibald T. Robertson:

### John Gill:

3. "Hold the traditions which ye have been taught...the truths of the Gospel...the ordinances of the Gospel...rules of conduct and behaviour...all the commandments of Christ."
