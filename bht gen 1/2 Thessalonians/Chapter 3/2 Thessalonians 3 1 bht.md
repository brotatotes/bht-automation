### Albert Barnes:
1. "Finally, brethren, pray for us - That is, for Paul, Silas, and Timothy, then engaged in arduous labors at Corinth."
2. "The idea is, that it might meet with no obstruction, but that it might be carried abroad with the rapidity of a racer out of whose way every hindrance was removed."
3. "The gospel would spread rapidly in the earth if all the obstructions which men have put in its way were removed; and that they may be removed should be one of the constant subjects of prayer."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "May have free course [τρεχη]. More literally, simply, and better, may run. Have swift progress through the world." 
2. "Be glorified [δοξαζηται]. Acknowledged in its true power and glory. Comp. John 12:28."
3. "The phrase the word of the Lord - be glorified, only here."

### John Calvin:
1. "Though the Lord powerfully aided him, and though he surpassed all others in earnestness of prayer, he nevertheless does not despise the prayers of believers, by which the Lord would have us aided."
2. "He does not desire, therefore, so much that regard should be had to himself individually, as to the glory of God and the common welfare of the Church."
3. "Hence, holiness of life and uprightness on the part of Christians is the glory of the gospel; as, on the other hand, those defame the gospel who make profession of it with the mouth, while in the meantime they live in wickedness and baseness."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Paul not only prays for his ‘children’ in Christ, but begs them to pray for him."
2. "Paul felt his need of courage to face those who opposed the preaching of the Gospel, of constancy to avail himself of every opportunity to introduce it into new audiences."
3. "Paul desires that the word of God may be glorified, attaining its rightful position in men’s minds."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Keep on praying."
