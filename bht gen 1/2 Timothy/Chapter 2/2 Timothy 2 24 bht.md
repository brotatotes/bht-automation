The verse being described emphasizes the qualities that a servant of the Lord should possess. It highlights the importance of avoiding contentions and foolish debates, instead promoting peace and gentleness towards all. The verse encourages patience in the face of adversity and the need to endure evil with grace. It also emphasizes the role of teaching, with an emphasis on being patient, forbearing, and apt to instruct. ### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The servant of the Lord must imitate his master in not striving contentiously, though uncompromising in earnestly contending for the faith."
2. "Gentle unto all men, patient in respect to adversaries. He is to be gentle so that he may occasion no evils; patient so that he may endure evils."
3. "Apt to teach, implying not only solid teaching and ease in teaching, but patience and assiduity in it."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The servant of the Lord...Must not strive...The meaning is, that the servant of Christ should be a man of peace."
2. "He should not struggle for mere victory, even when endeavoring to maintain truth; but should do this, in all cases, with a kind spirit, and a mild temper."
3. "The Christian minister is to be meek and mild toward all, not disputatious and quarrelsome."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The servant of the Lord [δουλον κυριου] . The teacher or other special worker in the church."
2. "The phrase is often applied to the Old Testament prophets as a body."
3. "Apt to teach, patient [διδακτικον, ανεξικακον] . Didaktikov apt to teach, only here and 1 Timothy 3:2 (note). Anexikakia forbearing."

### John Calvin:
1. "The servant of God must stand aloof from contentions; but foolish questions are contentions; therefore whoever desires to be a 'servant of God,' and to be accounted such, ought to shun them."
2. "When he says, that we must be 'gentle towards all,' he means that we ought to be easy and affable in receiving all who come to be taught in the gospel, for if we do not give them access it is like shutting the door against them, so that they shall never have it in their power to approach to God."
3. "He who wishes to be a good teacher must conduct himself with civility, and must have some way of drawing those who come to him, so as to gain their affections; and that cannot be, unless he have that 'gentleness' of which Paul speaks."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Patient under, or putting up with, evil."
2. "This verse reminds us that as followers of Christ, we are called to endure and persevere in the face of evil."
3. "It is not enough to simply avoid evil or turn a blind eye to it; we are to actively endure it, showing patience and grace."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Must not strive (ου δε μαχεσθα). Rather, 'it is not necessary for him to fight' (in such verbal quibbles)." 
2. "Gentle (ηπιον). Old word (from επος, speech), affable, mild, in N.T. only here (and 1 Thessalonians 2:7 in some MSS.; W. H. have νηπιος)." 
3. "Forbearing (ανεξικακον). Late compound (from future of ανεχω, ανεξω, and κακον, putting up with evil). Here only in N.T."

### John Gill:
1. "The servant of the Lord must not strive...about mere words, and in a litigious, quarrelsome manner, and for mastery and not truth."
2. "A minister of the word...ought to strive for the faith of the Gospel; this is praiseworthy in him."
3. "Be gentle unto all men...even to those who contradict the truth, and themselves, by mild and kind instructions."
4. "Apt to teach, showing a willingness to instruct the ignorant and obstinate, and making use of abilities given for that purpose, notwithstanding all discouragements."
5. "Patient, or 'bearing evil'...so as not to be irritated and provoked, or to be discouraged, and desist from the defence of the Gospel."

### John Wesley:
1. "A servant of the Lord must not eagerly or passionately strive." 
2. "Strive as do the vain wranglers spoken of." 
3. "A servant of the Lord must be apt to teach, chiefly by patience and unwearied assiduity."
