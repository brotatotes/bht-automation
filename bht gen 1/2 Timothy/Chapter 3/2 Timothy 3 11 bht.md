## BHT:
In this passage, the verse is described as the Apostle Paul reflecting on the persecutions and afflictions he endured in places like Antioch, Iconium, and Lystra. These sufferings were severe, but the Lord delivered him from them all, serving as an encouragement to Timothy to not fear persecutions. The description highlights the consoling aspect of afflictions having a joyful end and emphasizes the presence and deliverance of God throughout Paul's journey. Additionally, it mentions the significance of Paul's experiences for Timothy, urging him to follow Paul's example.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Persecutions and afflictions are not uncommon in the life of a believer, as seen in the example of Antioch, Iconium, and Lystra (Acts 13:50; Acts 14:2 ff.; Acts 14:19)."
2. "The word 'persecutions' can also refer to the sinful passions that believers must overcome (Romans 7:5; Galatians 5:24)."
3. "The term 'delivered' (ερυσατο) carries the idea of drawing out from peril, which is what Christ does for believers (Romans 11:26)."

### John Calvin:
1. "It is a consolation which mitigates the bitterness of afflictions, that they always have a happy and joyful end."
2. "When God sometimes delivers us, he testifies, in this manner, that he is present with us, and will always be present."
3. "Thou hast known by experience that God hath never forsaken me, so that thou hast no right to hesitate to follow my example."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "There is something at once natural and touching in the way in which the aged apostle goes back to the memories of the first missionary journey in which Timothy had known him."
2. "Persecution and then deliverance, that had been the course of his life then."
3. "He is confident that it will be so to the end."

### Archibald T. Robertson:

3. "What persecutions I endured (οιους διωγμους υπηνεγκα)."

### John Gill:
1. "Persecutions, afflictions, which came unto me at Antioch...and also at Iconium...and likewise at Lystra." 
2. "And these instances are the rather mentioned because they were done in those parts, where Timothy had lived...and perhaps he might have been a witness to some of them himself."
3. "What persecutions I endured...see a detail of them in 2 Corinthians 11:23."
4. "But out of them all the Lord delivered me...this he says to the glory of the grace and power of God, to whom he ascribes all his deliverances."
