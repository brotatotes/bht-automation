### Philip Schaff:
1. "Heady. Better, 'headlong.'"
2. "High-minded. The same word as 1 Timothy 3:6, 1 Timothy 6:4, 'fevered' or 'delirious with pride.'"
3. "Lovers of pleasures. Better, as contrasting more pointedly the two objects of love, Movers of pleasure' in the singular."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Traitors (προδοτα). Old word (from προδιδωμ), in N.T. only here, Luke 6:16; Acts 7:52." 
2. "Headstrong (προπετεις). Old word (from προ and πιπτω), falling forward, in N.T. only here and Acts 19:36." 
3. "Lovers of pleasure (φιληδονο). Literary Koine word (φιλοσ, ηδονη), only here in N.T."

### John Gill:
1. "Traitors,.... To their princes and sovereigns, whose deaths they have contrived and compassed, and whom they have assassinated and murdered."
2. "Heady; rash, daring, bold, and impudent, fit to say and do anything, though ever so vile and wicked."
3. "High-minded; puffed and swelled up with a vain conceit of themselves, and speaking great swelling words of vanity; lovers of pleasure more than lovers of God."

### John Wesley:
1. "Lovers of sensual pleasure" refers to those who prioritize and indulge in physical pleasures, which can distract them from experiencing a deep love for God.
2. Such indulgence "naturally extinguishes all love and sense of God," implying that excessive focus on sensual pleasure can lead to a loss of spiritual connection and understanding.
3. This commentary highlights the potential conflict between pursuing sensual pleasure and cultivating a genuine love for God, suggesting that one must be mindful of their priorities and choices in order to maintain a strong spiritual connection.
