1. "You know the history as a matter of universal rumor: and we are witnesses of the facts."

### John Calvin:
1. "That he may make his words to be believed, he saith, that he and his fellows in office saw with their eyes all those things which they spake concerning Christ."
2. "They were appointed witnesses by God, and they are brought forth, as it were, by God, that they might by their preaching bring men unto the faith of Christ."
3. "Peter calleth himself only an historical witness, because he beheld the things which were done. And here he toucheth his death briefly, because it was openly known; he standeth longer upon his resurrection, which was more doubtful, and the knowledge whereof was more available unto faith."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "There is an emphatic stress in this sentence on the word 'we'." 
2. "Peter at once takes away the ground from the exaggerated reverence for himself individually, shown by Cornelius."
3. "St. Peter does not shrink from setting forth strongly the humiliating circumstances of the death of Christ."

These quotes highlight the emphasis on the collective witness of the apostles, the humility of Peter, and the intention to lead Cornelius to the understanding of the significance of Christ's death.

### Archibald T. Robertson:

3. "They 'also' slew him, went that far, 'this crowning atrocity'."

### John Gill:
