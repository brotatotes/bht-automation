# Acts 10:41 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This verse describes the significance of Jesus' post-resurrection appearances to a select group of witnesses. It highlights the reality of his risen body and the credibility of his resurrection. The chosen witnesses, appointed by God himself, were privileged to eat and drink with Jesus, providing tangible proof of his physical presence. These intimate encounters demonstrate Jesus' care for his disciples and the depth of their relationship, affirming the authenticity of his resurrection and the importance of their testimony.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The fact of their having eaten and drunk with Him after His Resurrection gives most important testimony to the reality and identity of His risen Body." 
2. "Luke 24:41 and John 21:12 give us instances [of eating and drinking with Jesus after His Resurrection]." 
3. "Had not Peter in his mind the Lord's own solemn words, 'οὓς δέδωκάς μοι ἐκ τοῦ κόσμου,' Joh 17:6?"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Not to all the people - Not to the nation at large, for this was not necessary in order to establish the truth of his resurrection."
2. "Chosen of God - Appointed by God, or set apart by his authority through Jesus Christ."
3. "Who did eat and drink ... - And by doing this he furnished the clearest possible proof that he was truly risen; that they were not deceived by an illusion of the imagination or by a phantom."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Chosen before [προκεχειροτονημενοις]. Only here in New Testament."
2. "The simple verb ceirotonew, to appoint, occurs Acts 14:23; 2 Corinthians 8:19; and originally means to stretch out the hand for the purpose of giving a vote."
3. "The compound pronoun marks them more strongly as belonging to the class of eye-witnesses."

### John Calvin:
1. "Although there could no reason be showed, yet ought the counsel of God alone to suffice sober and modest men, that they may assure themselves without all doubt that that is best which God hath thought meet."
2. "For the certainty of the resurrection was proved by many and firm testimonies, and this was profitable to exercise the faith of the godly, to believe the gospel rather than their eyes."
3. "And here it appeareth what great regard Christ had of our rudeness and ignorance, who did abase himself so far for our sake, that when he was now endued with heavenly glory, he did yet, notwithstanding, eat and drink as a mortal man."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "To commit the testimony to select eye-witnesses was 'more in keeping with the dignity and glory of the risen Saviour, which would now have been degraded by the same promiscuous and unreserved association with men, that was necessary to His previous ministry.'" 
2. "The very fact that no such public recognition of His person is recorded, though at first it might have seemed to detract from the evidence of His resurrection, but serves to enhance it, by showing how free the witnesses of this event were from a disposition to exaggerate or make their case stronger than it was in fact."
3. "Witnesses chosen before of God. 'Witnesses, namely those who had been previously appointed by God.' Again there is reference to the Divine regulation of everything that related to the first proclamation of the gospel."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Peter is evidently stating the thing as it happened and not trying to make a convincing story by saying that both friends and foes saw him after his resurrection." 

2. "The sceptical Jews would not have believed and Jesus was kept from open contact with the world of sin after his Passion." 

3. "Peter makes it clear that it was no hallucination or ghost, but Jesus himself whom they saw after he rose from the dead."

### John Gill:
1. "Not to all the people,.... Of the Jews, who crucified him; nor to the whole body of the Christians, though at one time to a large number, even five hundred brethren at once." 
2. "But unto witnesses chosen before of God; by Christ himself, who is God."
3. "Even to us, who did eat and drink with him after he rose from the dead."

### John Wesley:
1. "Not now to all the people" - This phrase suggests that there was a shift in Jesus' availability and accessibility to the masses. It implies a more exclusive and intimate interaction with a select group of individuals.
2. "To us who did eat and drink with him" - This statement highlights the close relationship and fellowship shared between Jesus and his disciples. It signifies a deeper level of connection and intimacy, emphasizing the privilege and special bond they had with him.
3. "Conversed familiarly and continually with him" - This phrase emphasizes the ongoing and personal nature of the disciples' relationship with Jesus. It implies a deep level of trust, friendship, and understanding that comes from spending significant time together.
