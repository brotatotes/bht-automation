# Acts 10:47 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The commentators affirm that the unexpected effusion of the Holy Spirit removed any doubts about the necessity of circumcision before baptism. The reception of the Holy Ghost on both Jews and Gentiles brought them to the same level of spiritual standing, highlighting the unifying factor of the Holy Spirit. The commentators argue that the water used in baptism is co-existent with the Spirit, emphasizing the significance of water as the recognized and customary element. Furthermore, they assert that those who have received the Spirit are entitled to the privilege of Christian baptism.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The highest blessing of all, the Holy Spirit, had been received: hence the minor gift, which was emblematic of the other, and which procured admission into the Church of Christ, could not be refused."
2. 3. "The fact that in this instance, and in this instance only, the Holy Ghost was received previously to Baptism, has been the subject of many notes by commentators."

### Archibald T. Robertson:

### John Gill:
1. "Who can forbid these persons the ordinance of baptism, or deny the administration of it to them? For such who have the Spirit of Christ, are openly Christ's, and therefore have a right to his ordinances."

3. ### John Wesley:
