### Philip Schaff:
1. "Words, whereby thou and all thy house shall be saved." - This verse emphasizes the power of words in bringing salvation to both Cornelius and his household.
2. "‘All thy house’ is a special addition here." - The inclusion of Cornelius' entire household in the promise of salvation highlights the significance of family and community in the context of faith.
3. "The promise is in harmony with the preparation made for the Gospel in the house of Cornelius." - The promise of salvation to Cornelius and his household aligns with the divine preparation and openness to the Gospel that was evident in their lives.

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Clearly Cornelius was unsaved in spite of his interest in Jewish worship."
2. "Clearly also the household of Cornelius would likewise be won to Christ by the words of Simon Peter."
3. "This is household conversion before the household baptism."

### John Gill:
1. "Preach doctrines, such as relate to the person and office of Christ, to peace and pardon, and righteousness and salvation by him." 
2. "The Gospel only points out the way of salvation, and is only effectual to it, when it is accompanied with the power of God."
3. "Only such who believe in Christ with the heart, who is the sum and substance of the Gospel, are saved."

### John Wesley:
1. "Saved" - This word signifies the deliverance from sin and its consequences, as well as the restoration of a relationship with God. It encompasses both the present reality of being set free from the power of sin and the future hope of eternal life.

2. "Full Christian salvation" - This emphasizes that salvation is not just a partial or temporary deliverance, but a complete and comprehensive transformation. It encompasses forgiveness of sins, reconciliation with God, empowerment by the Holy Spirit, and the promise of eternal life.

3. "In this world and the world to come" - Salvation is not limited to the present life, but extends into eternity. It is a continuous process of experiencing God's grace and transformation in this world, and culminates in the eternal bliss and fellowship with God in the world to come.
