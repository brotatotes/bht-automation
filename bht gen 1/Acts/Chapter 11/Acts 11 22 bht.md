Despite persecutions and dispersals, the Jerusalem church remained steadfast. Thus, they chose Barnabas, known for his generosity and championing of Saul, to investigate the work of the preachers and confirm the Greek-speaking believers in Antioch. ### Henry Alford:
1. "His mission does not seem exactly to have been correspondent to that of Peter and John to Samaria."
2. "He can in any distinctive sense, be said to have been an Apostle, as they were."
3. "The choice of such a man, one by birth with the agents, and of a liberal spirit, shows sufficiently that they wished to deal, not harshly, but gently and cautiously, whatever their reason was."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Sent . . . Barnabas . . . as far as Antioch—implying that even on the way to Antioch he found churches to visit." This quote highlights the proactive nature of Barnabas' mission, suggesting that he was not only focused on reaching Antioch but also took the opportunity to visit other churches along the way.

2. "It was in the first instance, no doubt, a mission of inquiry." This quote emphasizes that Barnabas' mission was primarily aimed at gathering information and understanding the proceedings of the Cyprians and Cyrenians in Antioch.

3. "No one could be more suitable to inquire into the proceedings... than one who was himself a 'Grecian' of Cyprus and 'a son of consolation.'" This quote highlights Barnabas' unique qualifications for the mission, being both a native of Cyprus and known for his ability to bring comfort and encouragement to others.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It was natural that so remarkable an occurrence as the conversion of the Gentiles, and the extraordinary success of the gospel in a splendid and mighty city, should be reported at Jerusalem, and excite deep interest there."
2. "To aid the disciples there, and to give them their sanction."
3. "He was a native of Cyprus, and was probably well acquainted with Antioch. He was, therefore, especially qualified for the work on which they sent him."

### John Calvin:
1. "God had by evident signs declared that no nation ought to be counted profane."
2. "The apostles did at that time bear all the burden of the kingdom of Christ; therefore, it was their duty to frame and set in order Churches every where."
3. "The greater gifts every Church hath, the more careful ought it to be, lest Satan mix or trouble any thing amongst the ignorant, and those who are not as yet established in the right faith."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The Church in Jerusalem is here spoken of collectively, as a local ἰκκλησία: and the oriental phrase ‘came into the ears of the Church’ tends almost to personify it."
2. "There is great beauty in the description of his character which follows. This mission was alike creditable to him and to them."
3. "They may have sent Barnabas simply to inquire into the facts and to seek explanations. But at all events they sent the man who was best known among them for large-heartedness and generosity of character."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "The term 'church' was not yet applied to the group of disciples in Antioch as it is in Acts 11:26; Acts 13:1." 
2. "The choice of Barnabas was eminently wise. He already had a position of leadership in Jerusalem because of his generosity and his championship of Saul after his conversion." 
3. "He was to investigate the work of the travelling preachers all the way to Antioch."

### John Gill:
1. "Tidings of the spread of the Gospel in several parts, and the success of it in the conversion of sinners, especially at Antioch, came unto the ears of the church which was in Jerusalem." 
2. "Notwithstanding the persecution raised against the church of Jerusalem at the death of Stephen, and the havoc that was made of the members of it, and the dispersion of others, yet it still continued a church."
3. "Barnabas, who was himself an Hellenist, and of the country of Cyprus, and so very fit to be sent to the Grecians or Hellenists at Antioch, who had received the Gospel to confirm them in it."
