# Acts 11:24 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This verse describes a man who was noted for his generosity, open-mindedness, and fervent belief in God. With a large-hearted and liberal-minded disposition, he rose above narrow sectarianism and embraced the conversion of souls through humble piety. Filled with the Holy Spirit and faith, he rejoiced in the success of others and attributed it to the work of the Divine Spirit. Through his ministry, he drew a large multitude to the Lord, embodying the goodness and grace of God in his character and actions.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "For he was a good man—The sense of 'good' here is plainly 'large-hearted,' 'liberal-minded,' rising above narrow Jewish sectarianism, and that because, as the historian adds, he was 'full of the Holy Ghost and of faith.'" 

2. "and much people were added unto the Lord—This proceeding of Barnabas, so full of wisdom, love, and zeal, was blessed to the great increase of the Christian community in that important city." 

3. "BARNABAS, FINDING THE WORK IN ANTIOCH TOO MUCH FOR HIM, GOES TO TARSUS FOR SAUL—THEY LABOR THERE TOGETHER FOR A WHOLE YEAR WITH MUCH SUCCESS, AND ANTIOCH BECOMES THE HONORED BIRTHPLACE OF THE TERM CHRISTIAN."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Humble piety will often do more in the conversion of souls than the most splendid talents."
2. "The real power of a minister is concentrated in [humble piety], and without this his ministry will be barrenness and a curse."
3. "There is nothing on the earth so mighty as goodness."
4. "He was filled with the influences of the sacred Spirit, producing zeal, love, peace, joy, etc."
5. "A man’s ministry will usually be successful only as he seeks to possess those endowments which distinguished Barnabas - a kind, tender, benevolent heart; devoted piety; the fulness of the Spirit’s influence; and strong, unwavering confidence in the promises and power of God."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Good [αγαθος]. More than strictly upright."
2. "His benevolence effectually prevented him censuring anything that might be new or strange in these preachers to the Gentiles."
3. "Caused him to rejoice in their success."

### John Calvin:
1. "For all those are condemned of ungodliness and malice who envy other men’s labors, and are grieved when they see the same have good success."
2. "That, abandoning the affections of the flesh, he did, with all his heart, embrace godliness, having the Spirit to be his guide."
3. "Thus doth the building of the Church go forward when one doth help another with mutual consent, and one doth gently allow that which another hath begun."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The word ‘good’ does not mean merely that Barnabas was a man of earnest religious character. Rather it denotes that he was a man of a genial, generous, charitable, and candid disposition."
2. "The reason is given why he unfeignedly rejoiced in what he saw at Antioch. There may have been misgivings and suspicions at Jerusalem. But in his heart there were none."
3. "He frankly acknowledged the work of the Divine Spirit, and was glad because Pagans had received the fall grace of God."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "He rose above such racial narrowness."

3. "These people were added to the Lord Jesus before they were added to the church."

### John Gill:
1. "He had the grace of God wrought in his soul, and did good works; he was very kind, and generous, and charitable." 
2. "He was full of the several graces of the Spirit, and particularly of faith; and he was full of the extraordinary gifts of the Spirit, and of the faith of miracles." 
3. "By the means of Barnabas, through his ministry, and the exercise of those gifts he was full of; so the Arabic version, 'and he drew a large multitude to the Lord'."
