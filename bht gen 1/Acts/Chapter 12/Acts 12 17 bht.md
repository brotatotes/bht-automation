In the midst of a miraculous escape, the haste of Peter is evident as he quickly departs and keeps his place of retreat a secret. The account of Peter's deliverance evokes a range of emotions, from calm and solemn reflection to joyful and wondering interrogatories. ### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "In the hubbub of joyful and wondering interrogatories there might mingle reflections, thrown out by one against another, for holding out so long against the testimony of Rhoda."
2. "The emotion of the apostle's own spirit would be too deep and solemn to take part in such demonstrations or utter a word till, with his hand, he had signified his wish for perfect silence."
3. "James is singled out, because he had probably begun to take the oversight of the Church in Jerusalem, which we afterwards find him exercising."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Religion is calm and peaceful; and its great scenes and surprising deliverances are rather suited to awe the soul to produce calm, sober, and grateful contemplation, than the noise of rejoicing, and the shoutings of exultation."
2. "The consciousness of the presence of God, and of his mighty power, does not produce rapturous disorder and tumult, but holy, solemn, calm, grateful emotion."
3. "Go, show these things... Acquaint them that their prayer is heard, and that they may rejoice also at the mercy of God."
