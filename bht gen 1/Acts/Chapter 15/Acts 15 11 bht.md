# Acts 15:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This verse speaks of the universal salvation brought about by the grace of Christ. It emphasizes that salvation is not obtained through keeping the law or relying on rites and ceremonies, but through the mercy of God in the Redeemer. The verse further explains that the ground of trust for both Jews and Gentiles is the same: the grace of the Lord Jesus. It highlights the all-encompassing power of grace, which reconciles and justifies believers, irrespective of their background or works.

### Henry Alford:
1. "The grace of Christ is the sufficient, and only cause of our salvation."
2. "It can neither be reasonable nor according to God's will, to fetter that grace with superfluous and vexatious conditions."
3. "Their ground of trust is the same as ours: ours, no more than theirs."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Through the grace of the Lord Jesus—that is, by that only." 
2. "Circumcision in our case being no advantage, and in their case uncircumcision no loss." 
3. "Grace doing all for both, and the same for each."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "We apostles, who have been with them, and have seen the evidences of their acceptance with God."
2. "By the grace or mercy of Christ alone, without any of the rites and ceremonies of the Jews."
3. "We are to be saved, not by these ceremonies, but by the mere mercy of God in the Redeemer."

### John Calvin:
1. "Those are justified by faith who, being free and quit from the yoke of the law, seek for salvation in the grace of Jesus Christ." (116)
2. "Salvation is not placed in keeping the law, neither are those which believe in Christ subject to the curse of the law." (117)
3. "We must define the grace of Christ as free reconciliation gotten by the sacrifice of his death, or free forgiveness of sins." (119)

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The believing Jew, who has tried to keep the law and failed, will be saved like the Gentile through the power of the blood of Jesus."
2. "'Their ground of trust is the same as ours, ours as theirs' (Alford; and see Galatians 2:15 and following verses, where this train of thought is more fully carried out by St. Paul)."
3. "Through the grace of our Lord Jesus Christ, we shall be saved, even as they."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "We believe that we are saved through the grace of the Lord Jesus in like manner as they also."
2. "This thoroughly Pauline note shows that whatever hopes the Judaizers had about Peter were false."
3. "His doctrine of grace is as clear as a bell."

### John Gill:
1. "Salvation is by Christ; Jehovah the Father appointed him to be his salvation; he sent him, and he came to save sinners; and he has obtained salvation for them; and it is in him, and in no other." 

2. "The only way of salvation to both, and under all dispensations, is the Lord Jesus Christ; through whose sacrifice sin is atoned for, through whose blood it is pardoned, through whose righteousness men are justified before God, and are accepted with him."

3. "Salvation, in all its parts and branches, is owing to grace; and so it is with respect to all persons that are saved; some are not saved by their works, and others by the grace of Christ, but they are all saved by grace; and none have any room to boast of themselves against others."

### John Wesley:
1. "The Lord Jesus — He does not here say our Lord; because in this solemn place he means the Lord of all."
2. "we - Jews, shall be saved even as they - Gentiles, namely, through the grace of the Lord Jesus."
3. "not by our observance of the ceremonial law."
