### John Gill:
1. "Simeon hath declared how God at the first did visit the Gentiles" - James confirms Peter's speech, acknowledging that God showed favor to the Gentiles by sending the Gospel to them and making it effectual through His Spirit. This visitation was a gracious act, where God looked upon them, quickened them, and bestowed special favors upon them.

2. "How God first promised" - James refers to the promises concerning the calling of the Gentiles, affirming that God had already foretold this visitation. This highlights the faithfulness of God in fulfilling His promises.

3. "To take out of them a people for his name" - God's purpose in visiting the Gentiles was to choose a people for Himself, a people who would bear His name, call upon His name, and support His Gospel. This demonstrates the distinguishing grace of God in selecting and separating a special and peculiar people for His glory.

### John Wesley:
1. "Simon hath declared" - James, the apostle of the Hebrews, refers to Peter by his Hebrew name, emphasizing his Jewish heritage and connection to the Hebrew people.

2. "To take out of them a people for his name" - This phrase highlights the purpose of belief in Jesus, which is to be called by his name and become part of a chosen people, chosen by God.

3. "That is to believe in him" - The act of believing in Jesus is the means by which individuals are included in the people called by his name, emphasizing the importance of faith in salvation.
