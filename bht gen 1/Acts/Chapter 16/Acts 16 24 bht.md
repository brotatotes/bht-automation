# Acts 16:24 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this Bible verse, a group of prisoners is thrown into a dark, inner prison, locked away in an isolated and secure part of the prison. They are further subjected to a form of torture and confinement called "the stocks," which keep their legs in extended and painful positions. The prison itself is described as a damp and lightless cell, emphasizing the harsh conditions endured by the inmates. Despite their suffering, these prisoners choose to sing praises to God, showcasing their unwavering faith even in the darkest of circumstances.
