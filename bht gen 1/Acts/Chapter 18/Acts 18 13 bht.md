# Acts 18:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, the commentators highlight the contrast between the old Mosaic law and the new way of righteousness through faith in Christ. They emphasize that the Mosaic law was a temporary measure and was never intended to be the means of salvation. The inclusion of the Gentiles challenges traditional Jewish exclusivity and highlights the unity and equality among all believers. Furthermore, the verse portrays the Apostle Paul accused of promulgating a religion that went against both the laws of the Roman Empire and the Jewish law.

### Henry Alford:
1. "Against the Mosaic law: the exercise of which, as a ‘religio licita,’ was allowed to the Jews." 
2. "The phrase 'paranomon' emphasizes the contrast between the new way of righteousness through faith in Christ and the old way of righteousness through the observance of the Mosaic law." 
3. "The Mosaic law served as a temporary measure to reveal sin and point people to their need for a Savior, but it was never intended to be the means of salvation."

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "Contrary to the Jewish law, this verse suggests that the Gentiles were not required to be circumcised." 

2. "The inclusion of the Gentiles in the new covenant challenges the traditional understanding of Jewish exclusivity." 

3. "This verse highlights the radical shift in the early Christian community's approach towards Gentile believers, emphasizing the equality and unity among all believers, regardless of their cultural or religious background."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Contrary to the law - Evidently intending contrary to all law - the laws of the Romans and of the Jews."
2. "It was easy for them to maintain that he taught contrary to the laws of the Romans and their acknowledged religion."
3. "Their design seems to have been to accuse him of teaching people to worship God in an unlawful and irregular way, a way unknown to any of the laws of the empire."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The ‘law’ here alluded to was the law not of the Jews, but of the Empire; it was the Roman, not the Mosaic law, which the stranger Jew, Paul, was accused of violating." 

2. "The offence consisted in the attempt to promulgate a religion which was not sanctioned by the imperial government." 

3. "The apostle was charged now before the proconsul’s court with preaching in Corinth a new and unlawful religion."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "They bring the same charge here that the owners of the slave-girl brought in Philippi (Acts 16:21) Perhaps they fear to go too far with Gallio, for they are dealing with a Roman proconsul, not with the politarchs of Thessalonica."
2. "The Jewish religion was a religio licita and they were allowed to make proselytes, but not among Roman citizens."
3. "To prove that Paul was acting contrary to Roman law (for Jewish law had no standing with Gallio though the phrase has a double meaning) these Jews had to show that Paul was making converts in ways that violated the Roman regulations on that subject."

### John Gill:
1. "This fellow persuadeth men to worship God contrary to the law."
2. "Meaning either to the law of the Romans, which forbade the bringing in of any new gods, without the leave of the senate."
3. "Or rather to the law of Moses: the Arabic version reads, 'our law'; though this was false, for Moses in his law wrote of Christ, and ordered the children of Israel to hearken to him."
