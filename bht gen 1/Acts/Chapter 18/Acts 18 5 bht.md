# Acts 18:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this Bible verse, we see how Paul, upon the arrival of Silas and Timotheus, was filled with a deep sense of urgency to continue preaching the word. His spirit was pressed and his conviction to share the message of Jesus as the Messiah was intensified. The presence of his friends provided him with renewed energy and support, allowing him to devote himself wholeheartedly to his mission. Paul's dedication to proclaiming Christ's message to both Jews and Gentiles is reflected in his unwavering resolve and constant use of Scripture to support his teachings.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Paul was pressed in the spirit—rather (according to what is certainly the true reading) 'was pressed with the word'; expressing not only his zeal and assiduity in preaching it, but some inward pressure which at this time he experienced in the work."
2. "His early ministry at Corinth was colored by these feelings. Himself deeply humbled, his power as a preacher was more than ever felt to lie in demonstration of the Spirit."
3. "Such emotions are known only to the ministers of Christ, and, even of them, only to such as 'travail in birth until Christ be formed in' their hearers."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Paul was pressed - Was urged; was borne away by an unusual impulse. It was deeply impressed on him as his duty."
2. "In spirit - In his mind; in his feelings. His love to Christ was so great, and his conviction of the truth so strong, that he labored to make known to them the truth that Jesus Was the Messiah."
3. "The presence of Silas and Timothy animated him; and the certainty of aid in his work urged him to zeal in making known the Saviour."

### Marvin Vincent:
