1. "After these things were ended—completed, implying something like a natural finish to his long period of labor at Ephesus."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "After the gospel was firmly established at Ephesus, so that his presence there was no longer necessary."
2. "Resolved in his mind."
3. "To bear the contribution of the Gentile churches to the poor and oppressed Christians in Judea."

### John Calvin:
1. "Paul purposed to take his journey through the instinct and motion of the Spirit; that we may know that all his whole life was framed according to God’s will and pleasure." 
2. "God doth oftentimes govern and rule his faithful servants, suffering them to be ignorant of the end." 
3. "He was wholly addicted to profit the churches, omitting and neglecting his own commodity, in that he had rather deprive himself of Timotheus, a most excellent to him of all, most faithful, most dear, finally, a most fit companion, than not to provide for the Macedonians."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "His own prolonged stay seems to have been suggested by the events which have been related as just having taken place at Ephesus. A new opening, on the one hand, seems to have presented itself among the Heathen population, and there was also a grave necessity for consolidating and strengthening his work among many of the professing believers." 
2. "It was probably, however, owing to a secret impulse of the Spirit that he formed the purpose of this long and hazardous journey." 
3. "He had a twofold object in purposing to visit these congregations. The first was to stir up their faith, and correct and set in order any disorders which might be disturbing their progress and development, such as we know were at that time distracting the peace of the Church of Corinth."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "A new stage in Paul's career begins here, a new division of the Acts."
2. "Paul had long desired to go to Rome, but had been frequently hindered."
3. "Rome had a fascination for Paul as the home of Aquila and Priscilla and numerous other friends, but chiefly as the capital of the Roman Empire and a necessary goal in Paul's ambition to win it to Jesus Christ."

### John Gill:
1. "After many souls had been converted at Ephesus, and were formed into a church state, and were established in the faith."
2. "The apostle had already been [to Corinth], and preached the Gospel with success, and had laid the foundation of a Gospel church state in each place."
3. "The apostle's desire was not so much to see the magnificence of the city [Rome], as the saints in it; and that he might impart some spiritual gift unto them, and have some fruit among them."

### John Wesley:
1. "Paul sought not to rest, but pressed on, as if he had yet done nothing." 
2. "Faith and love to God and man had enlarged his heart, even as the sand of the sea." 
3. "No Cesar, no Alexander the Great, no other hero, comes up to the magnanimity of this little Benjamite."
