## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "These words would seem as if they should precede the last." 
2. "μεγαλεῖα" refers to "גְּדֹלוֹת," as referenced in the Psalms and Luke. 
3. "μεγαλεῖα" can be translated as "great things" or "mighty deeds."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The Cretans among the Greeks were famous for deceit and falsehood."
2. "The languages spoken by the apostles could not have been less than seven or eight, besides different dialects of the same languages."
3. "The gospel would be carried by those who were converted to all these places, and the way would be prepared for the labors of the apostles there."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Arabians. Whose country bordered on Judaea, and must have contained many Jews."
2. "Speak [λαλουντων]. Rev., rightly, gives the force of the participle, speaking."
3. "Wonderful works [μεγαλεια]. See on majesty, 2 Peter 1:16. From megav, great. Rev., mighty works. Used by Luke only."

### John Calvin:
1. "The apostles being before ignorant and private persons, born in a base corner, did, notwithstanding, intreat profoundly of divine matters, and of heavenly wisdom."
2. "They have new tongues given them suddenly."
3. "We must so wonder at the works of God, that there must be also a consideration, and a desire to understand."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "No sufficient reason, however, can be assigned for these two names occurring at the end of the list." 
2. "The wonderful works of God... described the grand mission of Israel, which was, to keep the torch of the knowledge of God ever burning through long centuries in a great heathen world."
3. "Surely we may assume that in some of these Pentecostal utterances, at least, the outlines of the arguments of the great epistles were first sketched out."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Crete is an island to itself and Arabia was separate also though near Judea and full of Jews."
2. "The point is not that each one of these groups of Jews spoke a different language, but that wherever there was a local tongue they heard men speaking in it."
3. "The mighty works (τα μεγαλεια). Old adjective for magnificent. In LXX, but only here (not genuine in Luke 1:49) in the N.T. Cf. 2 Peter 1:16 for μεγαλειοτης (majesty)."

### John Gill:
1. "These Jews, of different nations, declared concerning the apostles, saying, we do hear them speak in our tongues the wonderful works of God." 
2. "Things which struck them with amazement, and the more, that such illiterate persons should have such knowledge of them, and should be able to speak of them in such a clear, distinct, and powerful manner." 
3. "And still the more, that they should speak of them in their several tongues in which they were born, and to which they were used, and which the apostles had never learned."

### John Wesley:
1. "Cretans - One island seems to be mentioned for all." 
2. "The wonderful works of God - Probably those which related to the miracles, death, resurrection, and ascension of Christ."
3. "The effusion of his Spirit, as a fulfillment of his promises, and the glorious dispensations of Gospel grace."
