# Acts 2:26 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, the power and significance of speech is highlighted, emphasizing the value of using our tongues to express gratitude and worship. The verse also speaks of our confident hope and trust in God, which brings joy and gladness to our souls. The metaphor of our bodies finding rest in the grave symbolizes the quietness and security we experience through God's protection. This passage beautifully captures the assurance of future resurrection and the peace that comes from resting in God's care.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Thus, Paul says Hebrews 12:2 that 'Jesus ...'for the joy that was set before him,' endured the cross, despising the shame,' etc."
2. "The prospect of future glory and triumph should sustain us amidst all afflictions, and make us ready, like him, to lie down in even the corruptions of the grave."
3. "The passage means, 'My body will I commit to the grave, with a confident expectation of the future, that is, with a firm belief that it will not see corruption, but will be raised up.'"

### John Calvin:
1. "Joy of the soul, gladness of the tongue, and quietness of all the whole body, do ensue upon sure hope and confidence."

3. "The rest of the flesh doth signify the quietness of the whole man, which we have through the protection of God."


### Philip Schaff:
1. "These words describe Messiah’s glad consciousness on earth of His oneness with the Father."
2. "The Hebrews recognized the pre-eminent dignity of the soul by paraphrasing 'my glory' as 'my tongue.'"
3. "Christ expresses His confidence that His very flesh would rest in the grave in sure and confident hope."
