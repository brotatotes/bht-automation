1. "The Galileans were often looked down upon by the Judeans, who considered themselves more educated and sophisticated. This comment highlights the surprise and skepticism of the Judeans towards the Galileans speaking with such authority."

2. "The fact that the Galileans were speaking in the name of Jesus, who was also from Galilee, adds another layer of irony. It challenges the preconceived notions and prejudices of the Judeans, showing that God can use anyone, regardless of their social status or background."

3. "This statement also emphasizes the power and impact of Jesus' ministry in Galilee.
