### Albert Barnes:
1. "In Herod's judgment hall - Greek: in the praetorium of Herod."
2. "The word used here denoted formerly 'the tent of the Roman praetor'; and since that was the place where justice was administered, it came to be applied to 'halls (courts) of justice.'"
3. "This had been raised probably by Herod the Great as his palace, or as a place for administering justice."

### Marvin Vincent:

### John Gill:

3. "Here he was kept by a guard of soldiers, but not in close confinement; he had much liberty, and his friends and acquaintance had leave to come to him."

### John Wesley:
1. "Herod's palace served as a symbol of power and opulence, reflecting the grandeur of the ruling elite. It was within these walls that decisions were made, lives were changed, and destinies were determined."

2. "The palace, with its luxurious architecture and lavish decorations, stood as a stark contrast to the humble surroundings of Jesus' birth. It highlights the stark difference between worldly power and the humble beginnings of the Savior."

3. "The mention of a state prison within the palace suggests the potential for injustice and abuse of power. It serves as a reminder of the oppressive nature of Herod's rule and the need for divine intervention to bring justice and liberation to the oppressed."
