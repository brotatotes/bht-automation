# Acts 25:10 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, the apostle Paul confidently declares that he stands before the proper tribunal of Caesar, acknowledging that he cannot expect justice from the prejudiced and hostile Jews. He emphasizes that he has done no wrong to the Jews and appeals to the knowledge of the Roman governor, Festus. Paul resolutely seeks impartial justice and protection of the laws, recognizing that his innocence grants him the right to boldly make his case in this Roman court.
