### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The flight of the crew, therefore, might well be regarded as certain destruction to all who remained."

3. "Paul speaks and acts throughout this whole scene in the exercise of a sound judgment as to the indispensable HUMAN conditions of safety."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The certainty of an event does not render it improper to use means to obtain it."
2. "The doctrine of the divine purposes or decrees...does not make the use of man's agency unnecessary or improper."

### John Gill:
1. "God had determined to save the whole ship's crew, and that in the same way and manner; they were all to be shipwrecked; some were not to leave the ship before hand, and save themselves in the boat, but they were all to be exposed to equal danger, and then be saved." 
2. "Thus spiritual and eternal salvation is a certain thing; it is the appointment of God, which is absolute and unconditional, immutable and unfrustrable."
3. "None can be saved without regeneration; without this there is no meetness for heaven; nor does it appear without it that any have a right unto it; nor can an unregenerate man have any true hope of it."

### John Wesley:
1. "Unless these mariners abide in the ship — Without them ye know not how to manage her, ye cannot be saved." 
2. "We may learn hence, to use the most proper means for security and success, even while we depend on Divine Providence, and wait for the accomplishment of God's own promise." 
3. "He never designed any promise should encourage rational creatures to act in an irrational manner; or to remain inactive, when he has given them natural capacities of doing something, at least, for their own benefit."
