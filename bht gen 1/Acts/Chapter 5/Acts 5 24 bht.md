### Henry Alford:
1. "If the ἱερεύς of the rec. be genuine, it must designate the High Priest; not that the word itself can bear the meaning, but that the context points out the priest thus designated to be the H. P." 
2. "On ὁ στρατ. τ. ἱερ., see note, ch. Acts 4:1. He appears to have been summoned to meet the Sanhedrim, perhaps as the offence had taken place within his jurisdiction."

4. "τί ἂν γέν. τοὺτο To what this would come; 'whereunto this would grow,' E. V.: not 'quomodo factum sit,' as Kuin., nor 'quid hoc esset rei' (τί ὂν εἴη, as ch. Act 10:17), as Grot. and others."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The disciples' doubt and perplexity in this moment is a reminder that even those closest to Jesus struggled to fully comprehend his power and authority."
2. "Their doubt serves as a reminder that faith is not always easy or immediate, but requires ongoing trust and surrender to God's plan."
3. "In their doubt, the disciples were given an opportunity to witness Jesus' miraculous power and to grow in their understanding of who he truly was."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The word rendered 'doubted' denotes 'that state of anxiety which arises when a person has lost his way, or when he does not know what to do to escape from a difficulty.'" 
2. "What this 'would be'; or, what would be the result or end of these events."
3. "They need 'not' have doubted; but sinners are not disposed to be convinced of the truth of religion."

### Archibald T Robertson:

### John Gill:
1. "The high priest...is certainly designed, since he is distinguished from the chief priests after mentioned."
2. "They doubted of them, whereunto this would grow; they did not doubt of the truth of the things their officers told them, but they were amazed at them, and hesitated in their minds about them."
3. "They were in suspense and anxiety of mind, what to impute it to; whether to a divine and supernatural power, or to magic art; and were uneasy in their minds what would be the issue of so strange and surprising an event."

3. "The world, in persecuting the children of God, entangle themselves in numberless difficulties" - This statement suggests that those who oppose and persecute believers often find themselves caught up in countless complications and challenges as a result.
