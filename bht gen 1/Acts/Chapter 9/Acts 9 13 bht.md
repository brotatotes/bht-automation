## BHT:
In this verse, believers are referred to as "holy ones" and "saints," highlighting their set-apartness and belonging to God. The astonishment and hesitation expressed towards showing mercy to Saul reveal the extent of his notorious reputation as a persecutor of Christians. The term "saints" marks a shift in how believers are identified, suggesting a higher moral life imparted by Christ. Despite our natural tendency to reason against God, the verse encourages us to trust in His wisdom and respond with obedience, even in the face of fear or disbelief.
