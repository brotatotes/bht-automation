# Acts 9:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In an incredible moment, Saul's eyes were opened, revealing that he had closed them when the glorious vision appeared. Overwhelmed by the intense brightness of the light, he was temporarily blinded and saw no one. This blindness, not as a punishment but as a consequence of encountering the divine light, caused Saul to rely on others to guide him, shining a light on the transformation occurring within him.

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "He saw no man—after beholding the Lord, since he 'could not see for the glory of that light'": This quote highlights the overwhelming brightness of the light that Saul encountered, which temporarily blinded him and prevented him from seeing anything else.

2. "He had involuntarily closed his eyes to protect them from the glare": This quote explains that Saul's instinctual response to the intense light was to close his eyes, indicating his natural inclination to shield himself from harm.

3. "It is not said, however, that he was blind, for it was no punishment": This quote clarifies that Saul's loss of vision was not a punishment but a temporary consequence of his encounter with the divine light. It emphasizes that his blindness was not a result of wrongdoing or sin.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The intense, sudden light had so affected the optic nerve of the eye as to cause a temporary blindness."
2. "A disease of this kind is often caused by excessive light."
3. "A total loss of sight has been the consequence of looking at the sun during an eclipse, or of watching it as it sets in the west."

### John Calvin:

3. "They led him by the hand" - This quote illustrates the care and support provided by Saul's companions, demonstrating their willingness to assist him in his journey.
