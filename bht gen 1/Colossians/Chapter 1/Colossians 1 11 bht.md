# Colossians 1:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this Bible verse, believers are empowered with the strength of God's glorious power. They are strengthened to face and overcome trials with patience and endurance, not just persevering but doing so with a joyful attitude and thankfulness towards God. By relying on the strength bestowed upon them by God, believers are able to navigate challenges, fulfilling His will and finding ultimate fulfillment in their journey of faith.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The attribute of His glorious majesty here brought out is its κράτος, the power which it has thus to strengthen." 
2. "Endurance is the result of the union of outward and inward strength."
3. "Joyfulness in suffering expresses itself in thankfulness to God."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Being made mighty with all might."
2. "His power is inseparable from His glory."
3. "Patience, or endurance, is exercised in respect to those whom one cannot repel."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He desired that they might be strengthened for the performance of duty; to meet temptations; and to bear up under the various trials of life."
2. "There is a manifestation of power in the spirit with which Christians are enabled to bear up under trials, which shows that it is not of human origin."
3. "It is the power which God gives them in the day of trial. This power is 'glorious,' or, as it is in the Greek, it is the 'power of his glory.'"

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The compound (ejndunamow to make strong) is frequent in Paul, Romans 4:20; Ephesians 6:10; Philippians 4:13; 1 Timothy 1:12." 
2. "Power - might [δυναμει - κρατος] . See on 2 Peter 2:11; John 1:12. Glory. See on Romans 3:23."
3. "The idea of joy is contained in thanksgiving, which would make the emphatic position of with joy inexplicable; besides which we lose thus the idea of joyful endurance (ver. 24) and of joyful suffering expressing itself in thanksgiving."

### John Calvin:
1. "He says, that they will not be strong otherwise than by the Lord’s help; and not only so, but with the view of magnifying this exercise of grace the more, he adds, according to his glorious power."
2. "So far from any one being able to stand, through dependence on his own strength, the power of God shews itself illustriously in helping our infirmity."
3. "They must, therefore, arm themselves with an admirable patience, that what Isaiah says may be accomplished, In hope and in silence shall be your strength."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "With all strength; by means of every form of strength imparted by God." 
2. "According to the might of his glory; not, ‘glorious power’ (E. V.). The word rendered ‘might’ occurs in Ephesians 1:19, referring to power which manifests itself; here it is that might, ‘which is the peculiar characteristic of His glory’ (Ellicott)." 
3. "To endure this, Divine strength is needed, especially to endure with joy, a characteristic of Christian patience."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Empowered with all power." 
2. "His might is accompanied by glory (Shekinah)." 
3. "Hυπομονη is remaining under difficulties without succumbing, while μακροθυμια is the long endurance that does not retaliate."

### John Gill:
1. "Believers are weak in themselves, and insufficient to do or bear anything of themselves, but stand in need of strength from above, even of 'all might'." 
2. "Power belongs to God, is a perfection of his nature, and has been, and is gloriously displayed in many things."
3. "From this glorious power of God, saints may hope to be supplied with all might, or a sufficient supply of strength for every service, and for every difficulty."

### John Wesley:
1. "Strengthened unto all patience and longsuffering with joyfulness" - This verse emphasizes the importance of enduring hardships and challenges with not just patience, but also with a joyful attitude.
2. "Not only to know, to do, to suffer, the whole will of God" - The commentary highlights the significance of not only understanding and obeying God's will, but also being willing to endure the difficulties that may come with it.
3. "To suffer it to the end, not barely with patience, but with thankful joy" - The commentary encourages believers to not only endure trials until the end, but to do so with a spirit of gratitude and joy, recognizing that God's plan is ultimately for their good.
