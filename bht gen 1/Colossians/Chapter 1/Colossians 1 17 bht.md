# Colossians 1:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, the eternal and divine nature of Christ is emphasized as the preexistent creator of all things, existing before time itself. Not only is Christ the creator, but he also upholds and sustains all things through his infinite power and wisdom. He is the central figure, the living center and controlling force that holds the entire universe together, ensuring its stability and harmonious existence. From the majestic expanse of the heavens to the intricate details of nature and existence, Christ's authority and preeminence are showcased, reminding us of his infinite power and divine nature.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "And He Himself (the great HE) is (implying divine essential being) before all things," in time, as well as in dignity. Since He is before all things, He is before even time, that is, from eternity."
2. "by him—Greek, 'IN Him' (as the conditional element of existence)"
3. "The Son of God is the Conserver, as well as the Creator of all things"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The fair and proper meaning of the word 'before' is, that he was before all things in the order of existence."
2. "It is equivalent to saying that he was eternal - for he that had an existence before anything was created, must be eternal."
3. "If this be the proper interpretation, then it is the ascription to Christ of infinite power - for nothing less could be sufficient to uphold the universe; and of infinite wisdom - for this is needed to preserve the harmonious action of the suns and systems of which it is composed."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "He emphasizes the personality, is the preexistence" (Lightfoot).
2. "Christ not only creates, but maintains in continuous stability and productiveness."
3. "He, the Wisdom of God, holds the universe in tune together."

### John Calvin:
1. "All things were created by him, and for him." - This highlights the authority and purpose of Christ as the creator of all things, emphasizing his divine power and sovereignty.

2. "He places angels in subjection to Christ, that they may not obscure his glory." - This reveals the role of angels in acknowledging and submitting to the supremacy of Christ, ensuring that his glory remains unobscured.

3. "He sustains them by his power, and upholds them in their condition." - This emphasizes Christ's ongoing involvement in the existence and well-being of angels, showcasing his sustaining power and care for his creation.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He (emphatic) is before all things." - This emphasizes the preexistence of Christ, not just before all beings, but before all things in time. 

2. "In him all things subsist." - This highlights the organic permanence of creation in Christ. He not only created all things, but also sustains and holds them together. 

3. "Christ is the Living Centre, to which all things in creation converge, the Divine Keystone in the arch of the universe, on which the whole fabric leans." - This vivid imagery portrays Christ as the central figure in creation, the one in whom everything finds its purpose and unity.

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The precedence of Christ in time and the preeminence as Creator are both stated sharply." 
2. "Christ calls himself the Alpha and the Omega, the Beginning and the End." 
3. "Christ is the controlling and unifying force in nature."

### John Gill:
1. "He is before all things, not only in dignity, but in existence, as he must needs be, since they are all made by him."
2. "He upholds all things by the word of his power; the heavens have their stability and continuance from him."
3. "The whole frame of nature would burst asunder and break in pieces, was it not held together by him."

### John Wesley:
1. "And he is before all things — It is not said, he was: he is from everlasting to everlasting." 
2. "And by him all things consist — The original expression not only implies, that he sustains all things in being, but more directly, All things were and are compacted in him into one system." 
3. "He is the cement, as well as support, of the universe. And is he less than the supreme God?"
