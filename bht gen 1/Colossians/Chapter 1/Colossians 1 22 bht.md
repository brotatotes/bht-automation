### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Now hath he reconciled." - This emphasizes the present order of things in the new dispensation of grace in Christ.
2. "In the body of his flesh." - Paul combines both "σωμα" and "σαρξ" to emphasize the actual humanity of Jesus against those who denied it.
3. "Through death." - The reconciliation was accomplished by means of Christ's death on the cross, not just by the Incarnation.

These quotes highlight the significance of Christ's work of reconciliation, his humanity, and the role of his death in accomplishing this reconciliation.

### John Gill:
1. "These words express the means by which that reconciliation was made, which in the virtue and efficacy of it was applied particularly to these Colossians at their conversion whereby their minds were actually reconciled to God."
2. "It is so called either to distinguish it from his mystical and spiritual body the church, of which he is the head before spoken of; or from his glorious and immortal body, as now raised and exalted at God's right hand."
3. "This presentation of the saints by Christ is either in his own sight, 'before himself', as the Arabic version reads it; and is here in this present state, they being considered by him both as sanctified and as justified."

### John Wesley:
