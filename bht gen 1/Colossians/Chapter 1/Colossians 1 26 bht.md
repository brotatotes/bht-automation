The verse being described is a profound revelation in which the gospel is unveiled to both Jews and Gentiles. This mystery, concealed throughout the ages, has been made manifest to the saints through the preaching of the apostles. ### Henry Alford:
1. "The mystery which has been hidden from the ages and the generations."

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "The mystery, once hidden, now revealed, is redemption for the whole Gentile world, as well as for the Jews, 'Christ in you (Gentiles) the hope of glory'." 
2. "The 'ages' are the vast successive periods marked by successive orders of beings and stages of creation."
3. "The Spirit by Paul presciently, in opposition to Gnostic error already beginning, teaches, that the mystery of redemption was hidden in God's purposes in Christ, alike from the angelic beings of the pre-Adamic 'ages,' and from the subsequent human 'generations'."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Even the mystery - To make that mystery fully known."
2. "The great doctrine that salvation was to be proclaimed to all mankind, Paul says, had been concealed for many generations."
3. "But now is made manifest to his saints - It was communicated especially to the apostles who were appointed to proclaim it, and through them to all the saints."

### John Calvin:
1. "Here we have a commendation of the gospel — that it is a wonderful secret of God." 
2. "For the first is — that whereas God had, previously to the advent of Christ, governed his Church under dark coverings, both of words and of ceremonies, he has suddenly shone forth in full brightness by means of the doctrine of the gospel." 
3. "Lest any one should turn aside to another meaning the term mystery, as though he were speaking of a thing that was still secret and unknown, he adds, that it has now at length been published, that it might be known by mankind."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Redemption in Christ as belonging to the eternal plan of God."
2. "The concealment from the beginning of human history is marked."
3. "The entire historical manifestation, which took place in different ways, partly by revelation, partly by preaching and exposition, and partly by all combined."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Paul takes their very word and uses it for the gospel." 
2. "The Gnostics talked much of 'mysteries.'" 
3. "The construction is suddenly changed (anacoluthon) from the participle to the finite verb."

### John Gill:
1. "The Gospel, called 'the mystery', as it often is; because it contains things, which, though revealed, are mysteries to a natural man."
2. "The whole may be applied to the Gospel mystery in general which was first hid in the heart of God, in his thoughts and purposes, in his counsel and covenant, and in his Son, in whom are hid all the treasures of wisdom and knowledge."
3. "Now under the Gospel dispensation, since the coming of Christ; there is an external revelation of the Gospel by him, more clearly, by whom grace and truth came, called the revelation of Christ; and an internal revelation of it by his Spirit, who is the spirit of wisdom and revelation, in the knowledge of him."

### John Wesley:

3. "The mystery... hath been comparatively hid." This quote underscores the contrast between the past generations who were unaware of this mystery and the present generation who has the privilege of knowing and experiencing it.
