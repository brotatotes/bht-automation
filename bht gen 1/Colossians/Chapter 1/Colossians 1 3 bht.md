### John Calvin:
1. "We must always take notice that he makes use of thanksgiving in place of congratulation, by which he teaches us, that in all our joys we must readily call to remembrance the goodness of God."
2. "He admonishes us, by his example, to acknowledge with gratitude not merely those things which the Lord confers upon us, but also those things which he confers upon others."
3. "By this he intimates, that the condition of believers is never in this world perfect, so as not to have, invariably, something wanting. For even the man who has begun admirably well, may fall short in a hundred instances every day; and we must ever be making progress while we are as yet on the way."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The plural (‘we’) is probably occasioned by the mention of Timothy... but in 1 Corinthians 1:4, Philippians 1:3, the singular occurs after others have been named in the address." 
2. "The plural does not stand for the singular, but is used when the Apostle, in thought, associates others with himself."
3. "The force of the participle is better expressed by supplying ‘when."
