# Colossians 2:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This Bible verse emphasizes the significance of holding onto Christ as the head of the church and the source of growth and nourishment. It emphasizes the need for personal, individual connection to Christ rather than relying solely on others. Through love and faith, believers are united and strengthened, forming a harmonious body under Christ's guidance. Trusting in Christ alone and cultivating a strong bond with Him are essential for a flourishing spiritual life.

1. "Each must hold fast the Head for himself, not merely be attached to the other members, however high or eminent in the Body."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:

2. "Each must hold fast the Head for himself, not merely be attached to the other members, however high in the body."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Not holding the Head - Not holding the true doctrine respecting the Great Head of the church, the Lord Jesus Christ." 
2. "This is regarded here as essential to the maintenance of all the other doctrines of religion."
3. "He who has just views of the Redeemer will not be in much danger of erring respecting the other points of religious belief."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Holding the head, Christ as contrasted with the angelic mediators."
2. "The actual connection is expressed by bands or ligaments."
3. "The discoveries of modern physiology have invested the apostle's language with far greater distinctness and force than it can have worn to his own contemporaries."

### John Calvin:
1. "He condemns in the use of one word whatever does not bear a relation to Christ."
2. "The Church does not stand otherwise than in the event of all things being furnished to her by Christ, the Head."
3. "The constitution of the body will be in a right state, if simply the Head, which furnishes the several members with everything that they have, is allowed, without any hinderance, to have the pre-eminence."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Not to hold Christ as Head is to let go of Him altogether."
2. "All the body; including every member of it, not ‘the whole body,’ since the false teachers did not deny the unity of the Church, but slighted the fact that each member for himself must hold fast to the Head."
3. "Increased with the increase of God; effected by Him. 'God being the first cause of life to the whole, and carrying on this growth in subordination to and union with the Head, Jesus Christ' (Afford)."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "The headship of Christ is the keynote of this Epistle to the Colossians and the heart of Paul's Christology."
2. "Both words picture well the wonderful unity in the body by cells, muscles, arteries, veins, nerves, skin, glands, etc. It is a marvellous machine working together under the direction of the head."
3. "Increaseth with the increase of God."

### John Gill:
1. "By making use of angels as mediators and intercessors, Christ the only Mediator, the Lord and head of angels, and of the church, was dropped and laid aside."
2. "The grace of Christian charity, or love, is that in Christ's mystical body, as joints and bands are in a human body; for by this the members of Christ are joined, united, and knit together."
3. "It is from him [Christ] that the saints 'are knit together': both to one another in him the cornerstone, and also to him, being made one body and one spirit with him."

These quotes highlight the importance of Christ as the head of the church, the role of love in uniting believers, and the nourishment and growth that comes from being connected to Christ.
