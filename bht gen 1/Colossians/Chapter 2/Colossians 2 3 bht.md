### Albert Barnes:
1. "The more correct translation is 'in whom.' The reference is doubtless to Christ, as his name is the immediate antecedent, and as what is affirmed here properly appertains to him."
2. "Christ, as Mediator, was the great treasure-house where were to be found all the wisdom and knowledge needful for people."
3. "Christ is able to instruct us in all that it is desirable for us to know, so that it is not necessary for us to apply to philosophy, or to the teachings of human beings."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "In whom are all the treasures, etc., hidden." - This emphasizes that all the treasures of wisdom and knowledge are found in Christ, highlighting his importance and superiority.
2. "Hidden may convey an allusion to the Apocrypha or secret writings of the Essenes." - This suggests that the term "hidden" may refer to the secret teachings of certain groups, contrasting them with the accessible wisdom and knowledge found in Christ.
3. "The treasures of Christian wisdom which are accessible to all in Christ." - This highlights the inclusive nature of the wisdom and knowledge found in Christ, available to all believers regardless of their background or status.
