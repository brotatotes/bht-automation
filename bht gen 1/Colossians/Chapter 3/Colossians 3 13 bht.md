1. "Forbearing - as to present offenses." This quote highlights the importance of patience and restraint when dealing with current wrongdoings, emphasizing the need to exercise self-control and not react impulsively.

2. "Forgiving - as to past offenses." This quote emphasizes the significance of letting go of past grievances and choosing to forgive, showing the transformative power of forgiveness in healing relationships and moving forward.

3. "Christ - who had so infinitely greater cause of complaint against us." This quote reminds us of the immense grace and mercy shown by Jesus, who, despite our countless faults and sins, chose to forgive and reconcile us to Himself, setting the ultimate example of forgiveness.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "We are to harbor no malice against him; we are to be ready to do him good as if he had not given us occasion of complaint."
2. "We are to be willing to declare that we forgive him when he asks it."
3. "We are always afterward to treat him as kindly as if he had not injured us - as God treats us when he forgives us."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The latter pronoun emphasizes the fact that they are all members of Christ's body - everyone members one of another - so that, in forgiving each other they forgive themselves."
2. "Quarrel [μομφην]. Only here in the New Testament. Cause of blame. Rev., complaint."
3. "The A.V. uses quarrel in its earlier sense of cause of complaint. So Shakespeare: 'The lady Beatrice hath a quarrel to you.'"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The pronouns are different, as in Ephesians 4:32; the latter marking more strongly the relation of Christians as members of Christ."
2. "This state of things renders it most probable that ‘the Lord’ was the original form, especially since the parallel passage (Ephesians 4:23) has ‘God in Christ.’"
3. "The mode of forgiveness is here spoken of (‘even as,’ ‘so’); the ground of Christ’s forgiveness is left out of view."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Forbearing one another (ανεχομενο αλληλων). Present middle (direct) participle of ανεχω with the ablative case (αλληλων), 'holding yourselves back from one another.'"

2. "Forgiving each other (χαριζομενο εαυτοις). Present middle participle also of χαριζομα with the dative case of the reflexive pronoun (εαυτοις) instead of the reciprocal just before (αλληλων)."

3. "But Christ's forgiveness of us is here made the reason for our forgiveness of others. See Matthew 6:12; Matthew 6:14 where our forgiveness of others is made by Jesus a prerequisite to our obtaining forgiveness from God."

### John Gill:
1. "Forbearing one another... forbearing to render evil for evil, or railing for railing, or to seek revenge for affronts given." 
2. "Forgiving one another... all trespasses and offences, so far as committed against themselves, and praying to God to forgive them, as committed against him."
3. "Even as Christ forgave you, so also do ye... as Mediator, he has procured the remission of sins by the shedding of his blood; and as God he forgives sins freely, fully, forgetting the injuries done."

### John Wesley:
1. "Forbearing one another" emphasizes the need for patience and tolerance in dealing with the faults and shortcomings of others in the present moment.
2. "Forgiving one another" highlights the importance of letting go of past offenses and grievances, extending grace and mercy to those who have wronged us.
3. This verse reminds us to practice both forbearance and forgiveness, recognizing that we all fall short and make mistakes, and that extending grace and understanding is essential in maintaining healthy relationships.
