# Colossians 3:25 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, we are encouraged to regard Christ as our Master and serve Him, leaving our wrongs in His hands to be righted. We are reminded that at His tribunal, every person will receive just consequences for their actions, regardless of rank or wealth. God's impartiality is highlighted, as He does not show favoritism and will avenge all wrongs, ensuring fairness and justice for both masters and servants alike.

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Shall receive again for the wrong that he hath done" - This verse highlights the principle of retribution, where the one who does wrong will face consequences for their actions. It applies to both the master and the slave, emphasizing the fairness and justice of God's law.

2. "This is a general law of life and of God" - The commentary emphasizes that this principle of retribution is not limited to the context of slavery but applies universally to all aspects of life. It is a fundamental law established by God.

3. "There is no respect of persons" - Unlike humans who often show favoritism, God does not show partiality. This quote emphasizes the impartiality and fairness of God's judgment, where everyone is treated equally before Him.
