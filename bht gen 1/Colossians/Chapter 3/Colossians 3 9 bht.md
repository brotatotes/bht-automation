# Colossians 3:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, believers are urged to renounce and completely discard the old self, symbolized as "the old man," along with its deceitful habits and sinful actions. The commentators emphasize the importance of truthfulness, highlighting lying as a fruit of sin that should be put away. The act of putting off the old self is depicted as intentionally removing a useless garment and signifies a complete transformation and renewal of one's inner being. This new self, renewed by the Spirit of Christ, is characterized by truthfulness and obedience to righteousness.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The reason for putting away lying, stated in Ephesians 4:25, is, that we 'are members one of another' - or are brethren."
2. "The sense is, that lying is one of the fruits of sin. It is that which the corrupt nature of man naturally produces; and when that is put off, then all that that nature produces should be also put off with it."
3. "The vice of lying is a universal fruit of sin, and seems to exist everywhere where the gospel does not prevail."

### Marvin Vincent:

2. "The old man." - This term refers to the sinful nature or the old self that believers have left behind. It signifies a complete transformation and renewal of the inner being, leaving behind the old patterns of sin and embracing a new life in Christ.

3. "See on Romans 6:6." - This reference points to Romans 6:6, which further explains the concept of putting off the old man. It highlights the crucifixion of the old self, symbolizing the death of the sinful nature and the believer's identification with Christ's death and resurrection.

### John Calvin:
1. "When he forbids lying, he condemns every sort of cunning, and all base artifices of deception."
2. "The old man denotes — whatever we bring from our mother’s womb, and whatever we are by nature."
3. "The new man is that which is renewed by the Spirit of Christ to the obedience of righteousness, or it is nature restored to its true integrity by the same Spirit."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Lying (ψευδος) could have been included in the preceding list where it belongs in reality."

3. "Here Paul brings in another metaphor (mixes his metaphors as he often does), that of the old life of sin regarded as 'the ancient man' of sin already crucified and dropped now once and for all as a mode of life."

### John Gill:
1. "Lie not one to another... since the saints are members one of another, and of the same body, which makes the sin the more unnatural."
