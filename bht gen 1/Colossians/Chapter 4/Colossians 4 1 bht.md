### Henry Alford:
1. "Seeing that such exhortations occur in Ephesians also in terms so very similar, we are not justified, with Chrys., al., in assuming that there was any thing in the peculiar circumstances of the Colossian church, which required more than common exhortation of this kind."
2. "It has been said, that it is only in Epistles addressed to the Asiatic churches, that such exhortations are found: but in this remark the entirely general character of the Epistle to the Ephesians is forgotten."
3. "Besides, the exhortations of the Epistle to Titus cannot be so completely severed from these as to be set down in another category, as Eadie has endeavoured to do."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They intended to instruct the slaves to embrace the earliest opportunity to be free which was presented."
2. "They meant to suggest such considerations, and to lay down such principles as would lead masters to emancipate their slaves, and thus ultimately abolish it."
3. "These principles actually effected the freedom of slaves in the Roman empire in a few centuries after Christianity was introduced, and they are destined to effect it yet all over the world."

### John Gill:
1. "The apostle having observed the duty of servants to their masters, proceeds to direct masters to the discharge of their duty to their servants, by giving them that which is just and equal."

3. "Carrying it with an even hand to all, not preferring or indulging one before another, without any reason."

### John Wesley:
