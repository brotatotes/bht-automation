## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The raising of Christ is not only an earnest of our bodies being hereafter raised, but has a spiritual power in it involving the resurrection, spiritually of the believer's soul now, and, consequently, of his body hereafter."
2. "The Son, too, as God (though not as man), had a share in raising His own human body."
3. "Where He remains till all His enemies have been put under His feet. Being appointed to 'rule in the midst of His enemies' during their rebellion, He shall resign His commission after their subjection."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The 'power' which was then exerted was as great as that of creation."
2. "It is impossible to conceive of a more direct exertion of 'power' than in raising up the dead."
3. "Great power was displayed by this, and that a similar exhibition is made when man is renewed and exalted to the high honor of being made an heir of God."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Christ's dead body was the point on which this working of divine power was exhibited."
2. "In that He raised."
3. "In the heavenly places."

### John Calvin:
1. "Christ alone, therefore, is the mirror in which we can contemplate that which the weakness of the cross hinders from being clearly seen in ourselves."
2. "We shall find our advantage in directing our views to Christ, that in him, as in a mirror, we may see the glorious treasures of Divine grace, and the unmeasurable greatness of that power, which has not yet been manifested in ourselves."
3. "The expression does not refer to any bodily posture, but denotes the highest royal power with which Christ has been invested."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Which he wrought, or, ‘hath wrought.’ Good authorities support the latter reading, which presents the matter as an accomplished fact with permanent results."
2. "In Christ; both as the first-fruits and as the Head of the Church."
3. "Christ’s resurrection is not merely a pledge of our bodily resurrection, but involves the resurrection of the church in both senses."

### Archibald T Robertson:

### John Wesley:
