# Ephesians 2:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The verse being described is about the powerful concept of salvation through grace and faith. Commentators emphasize that salvation is a gift from God, not based on our own merit or efforts. The salvation received is not merely a future state, but a present reality, and it is experienced through faith. The emphasis is on the abundance of God's grace, and the humbling recognition that salvation is wholly sourced in Him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "For by grace...ye are saved, through [your] faith...and this...not of yourselves, GOD'S is the gift." 
2. "The abstract, 'through faith,' must be the rendering if the article be omitted."
3. "There is no occasion...to parenthesize these words: they form a contrast to οὐκ ἐξ ὑμ ., and a quasi-parallel clause to ἵνα μή τις καυχήσ."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Ye are in a saved state" - illustrating the exceeding riches of His grace in kindness.
2. "The instrument or mean of salvation on the part of the person saved; Christ alone is the meritorious agent."
3. "God justifies the believing man, not for the worthiness of his belief, but for the worthiness of Him in whom he believes."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "By mere favor. It is not by your Own merit; it is not because you have any claim."
2. "Salvation does not proceed from yourselves."
3. "Faith is the gift of God. It exists in the mind only when the Holy Spirit produces it there."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "This may truly be called exceeding riches of grace."
2. "Grace has the article, the grace of God."
3. "Not faith, but the salvation. Of God. Emphatic."

### John Calvin:
1. "First, he asserts, that the salvation of the Ephesians was entirely the work, the gracious work of God. But then they had obtained this grace by faith." 
2. "If, on the part of God, it is grace alone, and if we bring nothing but faith, which strips us of all commendation, it follows that salvation does not come from us." 
3. "Faith, then, brings a man empty to God, that he may be filled with the blessings of Christ."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Ye have been saved, and ye are now in a state of salvation."
2. "Salvation by grace is not arbitrarily attached to faith by the mere sovereign dictate of the Most High, for man’s willing acceptance of salvation is essential to his possession of it."
3. "The gift of salvation comes from God, by whose grace we have been and are saved."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "By the grace" already mentioned in verse Ephesians 2:5 and so with the article.
2. "Grace" is God's part, "faith" ours.
3. Paul shows that salvation does not have its source in men, but from God.

### John Gill:
1. "This is to be understood, not of temporal salvation, nor of preservation in Christ, nor of providential salvation in order to calling, and much less of being put in a way of salvation, or only in a salvable state; but of spiritual salvation, and that actual."
2. "Salvation is through faith, not as a cause or condition of salvation, or as what adds anything to the blessing itself; but it is the way, or means, or instrument, which God has appointed, for the receiving and enjoying it, that so it might appear to be all of grace."
3. "Salvation is not of ourselves; it is not of our desiring nor of our deserving, nor of our performing, but is of the free grace of God."

### John Wesley:
1. "Grace, without any respect to human worthiness, confers the glorious gift."
2. "Faith, with an empty hand, and without any pretence to personal desert, receives the heavenly blessing."
3. "That ye are saved through faith, is the gift of God."
