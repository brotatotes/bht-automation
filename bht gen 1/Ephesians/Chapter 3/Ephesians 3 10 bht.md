## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "For this sublime cause the humble Paul was raised up, to bring about, he, the least worthy of the saints, that to the heavenly powers themselves should be made known, by means of those whom he was empowered to enlighten."
2. "That the holy angels are capable of a specific increase of knowledge, and of a deepening insight into God’s wisdom, seems from this passage clear and incontrovertible."
3. "The Church is a mirror of God’s wisdom, chromatic, so to speak, with the rainbow colours of that light which in itself is one and undivided."

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "The design of God in giving Paul grace to proclaim to the Gentiles the mystery of salvation heretofore hidden."
2. "God's wisdom in redemption is made known to evil angels, who dwell 'in heavenly places' in a lower sense, namely, the air."
3. "The Church is the 'theater' for the display of God's manifold wisdom, a spectacle to angels."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "One grand purpose in the creation of the universe was, that the wisdom of God might be clearly shown by the church."
2. "There were views of the divine character which could be obtained only in connection with the redemption of the world."
3. "In the redemption of the church, there is not merely one form or one phase of wisdom. It is wisdom, ever-varying, ever-beautiful."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Grace was given me to preach Christ and to enlighten men as to the long-hidden mystery of the admission of the Gentiles." 
2. "By means of the Church, God's wisdom in its infinite variety is to be displayed - the many-tinted wisdom of God." 
3. "Through the Church, God's wisdom is manifested in different modes of power, different characters, methods of training, providences, forms of organization, etc."

### John Calvin:
1. "The preaching of the gospel exhibits the manifold grace of God, with which, till now, the heavenly angels themselves were unacquainted."
2. "The wisdom of God, therefore, which was manifested by uniting Jews and Gentiles in the fellowship of the gospel, ought to be regarded by men with the highest admiration."
3. "The church, composed both of Jews and Gentiles, is a mirror, in which angels behold the astonishing wisdom of God displayed in a manner unknown to them before."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "In this extension of the gospel to the Gentiles God’s manifold wisdom has been most fully made known on earth."
2. "By this full designation of the angelic hosts Paul gives prominence to their power and dignity, and thus exalts the church."
3. "We are not indeed the professors at whose feet the angels must sit as scholars, but it is God who leads them onward in the knowledge of His wisdom; we are but the means of instruction."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "The mystery was made known to Paul and now he wants it blazoned forth to all powers."
2. "The church is described as the wonderful body of Christ."
3. "The manifold wisdom of God is described as much-variegated, with many colors."

### John Gill:
1. "The Gospel, which is the pure produce of the wisdom of God; which is gloriously displayed in the several doctrines of it."
2. "In redemption, which is seen in the person of the Redeemer, who is both God and man; and in the manner in which it is effected, being both for the glory of God's grace and mercy, and for the honour of his justice and holiness."
3. "And it may be called 'manifold', because of its various doctrines and promises and because of the various instances of wisdom in them, and the various persons to whom it is made known, and the various times in which it is displayed."

### John Wesley:
1. "The church serves as a platform for the display of God's multifaceted wisdom, showcasing the depth and breadth of His divine understanding."
2. "Through the workings of the church, God's wisdom is unveiled, allowing believers to witness the intricate and diverse ways in which He operates."
3. "The church acts as a stage upon which God's wisdom is performed, revealing His profound insight and understanding to the world."
