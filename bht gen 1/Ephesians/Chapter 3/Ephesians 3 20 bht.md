# Ephesians 3:20 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This verse describes the incomprehensible power of God, who is capable of doing far beyond what we can even ask or imagine. Commentators emphasize the limitless nature of God's abilities, his provision for our needs before we even ask, and the importance of strengthening our faith. They remind us to have unwavering trust in God, as he can surpass our expectations and grant us blessings that go beyond our imaginations.

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "That is able above all things to do exceeding abundantly above what we ask or (even) think": thought takes a wider range than prayers.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Now unto him - It is not uncommon for Paul to utter an ascription of praise in the midst of an argument...he desires that praise should be given to him."
2. "Exceeding abundantly - The compound word used here occurs only in this place...It means, to an extent which we cannot express."
3. "According to the power that worketh in us - The exertion of that same power can accomplish for us more than we can now conceive."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Exceeding abundantly [υπερεκπερισσου]. Superabundantly."

### John Calvin:

### Philip Schaff:

### Archibald T Robertson:

### John Gill:

3. "He can do more than we can think, imagine, or conceive in our minds."
