### Albert Barnes:
1. "If you have listened attentively to his instructions, and learned the true nature of his religion."
2. "By his Spirit, or by the ministers whom he had appointed."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "If ye heard as ye did." - The indicative mood implies the truth of the supposition: if you truly heard Him.
2. "In Him." - Rev., correctly, in Him. In fellowship with.
3. "As truth is in Jesus." - Render, as Rev., as truth is in Jesus. The life of Christianity consists in believing fellowship with the historic Jesus, who is the Christ of prophecy.

### John Calvin:
1. "This doctrine had not been slightly pointed out, but faithfully delivered and explained."
2. "A reproof of that superficial knowledge of the gospel, by which many are elated, who are wholly unacquainted with newness of life."
3. "Any knowledge of Christ, which is not accompanied by mortification of the flesh, is not true and sincere."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Nothing is truly heard through the gospel message, until He is heard."
2. "This fellowship was not only the result but the essential condition of the instruction; for saving knowledge is referred to."
3. "If you were taught so that what you received was according to what is true (true and real) as permanently embodied in the personal Saviour."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "If indeed." Condition of first class with aorist indicatives here, assumed to be true.
2. "Even as truth is in Jesus." It is not clear what Paul's precise idea is here. The Cerinthian Gnostics did distinguish between the man Jesus and the aeon Christ.
3. "He flatly affirms that there is 'truth in Jesus' which is in direct opposition to the heathen manner of life."

### John Gill:
1. "Not heard him preach, but heard him preached; and that not merely externally, with the outward hearing of the ear; though oftentimes spiritual conviction and illumination, true faith in Christ, real comfort from him, and establishment and assurance of interest in him, come this way."
2. "But internally, so as to know him, understand his word, and distinguish his voice; so as to approve of him and love him, and believe in him; feel the power of his Gospel, relish his truths, and obey his ordinances, and so bring forth fruit to his glory."
3. "And have been taught by him: not personally, but by his Spirit and ministers; for Christ is not only the subject of the ministry of the word, and whom the Spirit of God teaches and directs souls to for righteousness, pardon, cleansing, and for every supply of grace; but he is the efficient cause of teaching."

### John Wesley:
1. "Teaching you inwardly by his Spirit" - This highlights the personal and transformative nature of God's teaching. It emphasizes the role of the Holy Spirit in guiding and enlightening believers in understanding the truth.

2. "The truth is in Jesus" - This phrase emphasizes that Jesus himself is the embodiment of truth. It reminds us that our understanding of truth should be rooted in Christ and his teachings.

3. "According to his own gospel" - This phrase emphasizes the authority and reliability of the gospel message. It reminds us that our understanding of truth should align with the teachings of Jesus as revealed in the gospel.
