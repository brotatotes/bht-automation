# Ephesians 5:6 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, the commentators warn against being deceived by empty and deceitful words that justify sinful actions. They emphasize that the true nature of these actions contradicts God's commandments, even though they may be considered innocent by others. The consequences of indulging in these sinful behaviors include the wrath of God, which can manifest in various forms. It is crucial for believers to uphold God's standards of righteousness and holiness and avoid being ensnared by deceptive teachings.

### John Calvin:
1. "Let no man deceive you. There have always been ungodly dogs, by whom the threatenings of the prophets were made the subject of merriment and ridicule."
2. "Paul, on the contrary, exclaims that we must guard against that sophistry by which consciences are ensnared to their ruin."
