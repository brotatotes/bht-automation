# Ephesians 6:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The verse being described is Ephesians 6:11: "Put on the whole armor of God, that you may be able to stand against the wiles of the devil."

Description: Believers are urged to fully equip themselves with the spiritual armor provided by God against the crafty schemes of the devil. This armor is not of our own making, but a divine provision to withstand the strength and cunning of our adversary. It is the complete set of armor, emphasizing the necessity of each piece for our protection and victory in the spiritual warfare we face.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "No opening at the head, the feet, the heart, the belly, the eye, the ear, or the tongue, is to be given to Satan." 
2. "Believers have once for all overcome him; but on the ground of this fundamental victory gained over him, they are ever again to fight against and overcome him."
3. "The armor of God is furnished by God; not our own, else it would not stand. It is spiritual, therefore, and mighty through God, not carnal."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The armor of God is not that which God wears, but that which he has provided for the Christian soldier." 
2. "We are to put on the 'whole armor.' We are not to go armed partly with what God has appointed, and partly with such weapons as people use; nor are we to put on 'a part' of the armor only, but the 'whole' of it."
3. "The wiles 'of the devil' are the various arts and stratagems which he employs to drag souls down to perdition. We can more easily encounter open force than we can cunning; and we need the weapons of Christian armor to meet the attempts to draw us into a snare, as much as to meet open force."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The armor is a defense against strategy as well as assault."
2. "The devil...used as the equivalent of Satan, of a single person, the enemy of mankind."
3. "The Septuagint usage implies enmity in general, without accusation either true or false."

### John Calvin:
1. "God has furnished us with various defensive weapons, provided we do not indolently refuse what is offered."
2. "We ought to be prepared on all sides, so as to want nothing. The Lord offers to us arms for repelling every kind of attack."
3. "To quicken our vigilance, he reminds us that we must not only engage in open warfare, but that we have a crafty and insidious foe to encounter."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Put on the whole armour, a term then applied to the entire equipment, offensive and defensive, of heavy armed infantry."
2. "Supplied by God, 'altogether of a Divine kind, in contrast to the arms of the opponent.'"
3. "Against the wiles of the devil, in contrast with 'the whole armor of God.' 'Wiles,' or, stratagems, usually in a bad sense; the plural marking the variety of the attacks."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Put on (ενδυσασθε). Like Ephesians 3:12. See also Ephesians 4:24." 
2. "The whole armour (την πανοπλιαν). Old word from πανοπλος (wholly armed, from παν, οπλον)."
3. "The wiles of the devil (τας μεθοδιας του διαβολου). See already Ephesians 4:14 for this word. He is a crafty foe and knows the weak spots in the Christian's armour."

### John Gill:
1. "Not that which God himself is sometimes clothed with, and uses against his enemies; but what he has provided for his people, and furnishes them with." 
2. "It is called 'the armour of God', because it is prepared by him for his people, and is bestowed on them by him." 
3. "The whole armour of God should be put on, which is proof against all his might and craft, in order to stand against him, oppose him, and fight, and get the victory over him."

### John Wesley:
1. "Put on the whole armour of God" - This phrase emphasizes the importance of fully equipping oneself with the spiritual armor provided by God. It implies that partial armor is insufficient in facing the challenges and temptations of life.

2. "The whole armour" - This repetition highlights the necessity of having the complete set of armor, indicating that each piece serves a specific purpose in protecting and empowering believers.

3. "Because of the strength and subtilty of our adversaries" - This statement acknowledges the formidable nature of the spiritual opposition believers face. It reminds us that our adversaries are not to be underestimated and that we need to be fully prepared to withstand their attacks.
