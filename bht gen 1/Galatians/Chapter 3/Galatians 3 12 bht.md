### Albert Barnes:
1. "The Law is not a matter of faith; it does not relate to faith; it does not require faith; it deals in other matters, and it pertains to another system than to faith."

### John Calvin:
1. "The law is not of faith; that is, it has a method of justifying a man which is wholly at variance with faith."
2. "The difference lies in this, that man, when he fulfils the law, is reckoned righteous by a legal righteousness."
3. "The reply to this objection is easy. There are none righteous by the works of the law, because there are none who do those works."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "The law demands complete obedience and rests not on mercy, faith, grace."
2. "The law is not of faith."
3. "Law and faith are fundamentally different in their nature and purpose."

### John Gill:
1. "The law does not consist of faith in Christ, nor does it require it, and that a man should live by it upon his righteousness."
2. "The law does not take any notice of a man's faith; nor has it anything to do with a man as a believer, but as a doer, in the point of justification."
3. "Since eternal life is a promise made before the world began, is provided for in an everlasting covenant, is revealed in the Gospel, and is the pure gift of God's grace through Christ, it seems that it never was the will of God that it should be obtained by the works of the law."

### John Wesley:

2. "It does not say, Believe; but, Do" - The law emphasizes obedience and actions, not simply belief. It requires people to follow specific rules and regulations, rather than relying solely on faith.
3. "Leviticus 18:5" - This verse serves as an example of the law's emphasis on doing. It highlights the commandment to keep the statutes and ordinances, rather than emphasizing belief or faith.
