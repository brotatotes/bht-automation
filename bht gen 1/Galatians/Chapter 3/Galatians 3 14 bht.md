# Galatians 3:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This verse speaks of the inclusive nature of God's salvation plan, as the blessing promised to Abraham extends to both Jews and Gentiles. It emphasizes the sacrificial love of Christ, who became a curse for us and removed the curse through his death. Through faith, all believers, regardless of their background, can receive the promise of the Holy Spirit. This verse reminds us that salvation and the fulfillment of God's promises are obtained through faith and trust in His grace, not through our own efforts.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The deliverance from the curse results not only in extending to the Gentiles the blessing promised to Abraham, but in the impartation of the Spirit to both Jews and Gentiles through faith."
2. "The eujlogia blessing is not God's gift of justification as the opposite of the curse; for in vv. 10, 11, justification is not represented as the opposite of the curse, but as that by which the curse is removed and the blessing realized."
3. "The subject of the promise is the life which comes through the Spirit."

### Philip Schaff:

### John Wesley:
1. "The blessing of Abraham...might come on the gentiles" - This verse reveals that the blessing promised to Abraham is not exclusive to the Jewish people but is also available to the gentiles. It emphasizes the inclusive nature of God's grace and salvation.

2. "We...might receive the promise of the Spirit" - The promise of the Spirit is not limited to a specific group but is open to all who believe, regardless of their background. It highlights the universal nature of the gift of the Holy Spirit and the indwelling presence of God in the lives of believers.

3. "Through faith...faith looks wholly to the promise" - Salvation and the fulfillment of God's promises are not achieved through human effort or works, but through faith. It emphasizes the importance of trusting in God's promises and relying on His grace rather than relying on our own abilities or achievements.
