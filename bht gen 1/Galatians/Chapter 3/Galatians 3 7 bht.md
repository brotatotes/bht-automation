# Galatians 3:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This passage emphasizes that those who have faith, like Abraham, are the true heirs of his promises. It clarifies that only those who have faith in God can be considered as the true children of Abraham. Through this faith, believers become heirs of the promises made to Abraham. This highlights the importance of faith in establishing a connection to the promises and blessings of God.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The imperative is livelier, and the statement in the verse is one of the points which the writer is trying to prove."

### John Calvin:
1. "He says that those 'are of faith,' who have relinquished all confidence in works, and rely on the promise of God alone."
2. "To be of faith, therefore, is to rest their righteousness and hope of salvation on the mercy of God."
3. "The scope of the passage shews that γινώσκετε is not the Indicative, but the Imperative. Paul does not presuppose that the Galatians acknowledge this principle; he is exerting himself to convince them of it."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The Greek may be the indicative or the imperative. The former agrees better with the argumentative character of the sentence and with the particle ‘therefore’ (άρα)."
2. "Those of faith, emphatic, no others, in opposition to the self-righteous men of the law."
3. "They whose starting-point, whose fundamental principle is faith."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The real sons of Abraham are those who believe as he did, 'they which be of faith'." 
2. "Those whose spiritual sonship springs out of faith, not out of blood." 
3. "Jesus termed the Pharisees children of the devil and not spiritual children of Abraham."

### John Gill:
1. "It ought not to be contradicted or disputed, it is so plain a case, and so clear a point."
2. "His spiritual seed, though they may not be his natural offspring; for he is the father of all that believe."
3. "None are such, but they that are of faith, or seek for righteousness by faith; not they that are of the law, or seek for justification by the works of it."

These quotes emphasize the clarity and certainty of the truth that those who have faith are the true children of Abraham, regardless of their lineage. It highlights the importance of faith in seeking righteousness and justification, rather than relying on works or legalistic practices.

### John Wesley:
1. "Partakers of his faith" - Those who share in Abraham's faith are the true heirs of his promises.
2. "These, and these only" - Only those who have faith in God can be considered as the true sons of Abraham.
3. "Heirs of the promises" - Through faith, believers become heirs of the same promises that were made to Abraham.
