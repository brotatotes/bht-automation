# Galatians 4:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This verse speaks to the deep love and concern the writer has for his audience, comparing his relationship to that of a father to his children. The commentator describes the writer's anguish and longing for the spiritual growth of his readers, even comparing it to the pain of a mother giving birth. The ultimate desire is for the readers to be fully transformed and shaped by Christ, embracing His teachings and embodying His character. The goal is that they would be conformed to the image of Christ and live in complete devotion to Him.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "My little children - The language of tender affection, such as a parent would use toward his own offspring."
2. "Of whom I travail in birth again - For whose welfare I am deeply anxious: and for whom I endure deep anguish."
3. "Until Christ be formed in you - until you wholly and entirely embrace his doctrines; and until you become wholly imbued with his spirit."

### John Gill:
1. "My little children,.... A soft and tender way of speaking, used by Christ to his disciples, and frequently by that affectionate and beloved disciple, John."
2. "He compares himself to a woman with child, as the church in bringing forth souls to Christ sometimes is; and all his pains and labours in the ministry of the word to the sorrows of a woman during the time of childbearing."
3. "It is also the same as to be conformed to the image of Christ, which in regeneration is stamped upon the saints, and is gradually increased, and will be perfected in heaven."

### John Wesley:
1. "My little children - He speaks as a parent, both with authority, and the most tender sympathy, toward weak and sickly children."
2. "Of whom I travail in birth again - As I did before, Galatians 4:13, in vehement pain, sorrow, desire, prayer."
3. "Till Christ be formed in you - Till there be in you all the mind that was in him."
