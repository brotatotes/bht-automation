# Galatians 4:28 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Believers, both Jews and Gentiles, are described as children of the promise, resembling the birth of Isaac who was born through supernatural power. As Christians, we share in the inheritance of the promises given to Abraham, symbolizing our identity as part of God's spiritual seed. Through our faith, we are united as the fortunate recipients of God's covenant, having access to the abundance of His blessings.

1. "We" better accords with "mother of us." 
2. Children of promise - not children after the flesh, but through the promise.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "We who are Christians." - This phrase highlights the identity of the intended audience of the commentary, emphasizing that the message is directed towards believers in Christ.

2. "We so far resemble Isaac." - This comparison draws a parallel between Christians and Isaac, emphasizing that just as Isaac received great promises from God, believers also have access to precious promises.

3. "We are not in the condition of Ishmael." - This statement contrasts the situation of Christians with that of Ishmael, highlighting the distinction between those who have received promises from God and those who have not.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Christian believers are born, like Isaac, of the unfruitful Sarah, contrary to the ordinary course of nature." 
2. "By the supernatural power of the divine promise." 
3. "They are therefore children of the heavenly Jerusalem."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Paul means that Christians (Jews and Gentiles) are children of the promise as Isaac was." 
2. "Christians are children of the promise, just like Isaac." 
3. "Paul is emphasizing that believers, regardless of their background, are heirs of God's promises, just as Isaac was."

### John Gill:
1. "Isaac signified, and was a type and figure of Abraham's spiritual seed, whether Jews or Gentiles, under the Gospel dispensation."
2. "As Isaac was born by virtue, and in consequence of a promise made to Abraham of God's free good will and pleasure, and his generation and conception were beyond the strength and course of nature, were the effects of a divine power, and were something supernatural; so this spiritual seed are born again, by virtue, and in consequence of a promise."
3. "Moreover, to these children, or spiritual seed of Abraham, signified by Isaac, do all the promises belong, as that of God, as a covenant God gave unto them; of Christ, as a Saviour and Redeemer; of the Holy Spirit, as a sanctifier and comforter; and of all good things, of temporal mercies, so far as are for their real good; and of all spiritual blessings, as righteousness, peace, pardon, comfort, all supplies of grace, and eternal life."

### John Wesley:
1. "We are children of the promise - Not born in a natural way, but by the supernatural power of God."
2. "As such we are heirs of the promise made to believing Abraham."
3. "Whether Jews or Gentiles, we who believe are united as children of the promise."
