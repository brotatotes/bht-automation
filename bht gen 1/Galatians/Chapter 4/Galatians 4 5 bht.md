### Henry Alford:
1. "Christ, being born under the law, a Jewish child, subject to its ordinances, by His perfect fulfilment of it, and by enduring, as the Head and in the root of our nature, its curse on the tree, bought off those who were under the law."
2. "In buying off those under the law, He effected that all men should receive the adoption of sons."
3. "The adoption of sons is not a recovery of the state before the fall, but a far more glorious thing, the bestowal of an adoption which Adam never had."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "That He might redeem them...under the law" - The redemption mentioned here is not limited to the Jews, but also includes the Gentiles, as they are representative of the world. (Galatians 3:13)

2. "Receive as something destined or due" - The Greek word used here implies that the redemption was predestined by God and is suitable for all. (Luke 23:41; 2 John 1:8)

3. "God makes of sons of men sons of God" - Through this redemption, God transforms humanity, making them sons of God, just as He made the Son of God the Son of man. (Augustine on Psalm 52)

### Albert Barnes:
1. "To redeem them - By his death as an atoning sacrifice."
2. "Them that were under the law - Sinners, who had violated the Law, and who were exposed to its dread penalty."
3. "That we might receive the adoption of sons - Be adopted as the sons or the children of God."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "To redeem from the dominion and curse of the law."

### John Calvin:
1. "The fathers, under the Old Testament, were certain of their adoption, but did not so fully as yet enjoy their privilege."
2. "Adoption, like the phrase, 'the redemption of our body,' is here put for actual possession."
3. "Those who now burden the church with an excess of ceremonies, defraud her of the just right of adoption."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "To redeem, to buy off from the curse and the slavery of the law."
2. "The redemption by Christ infinitely transcends the original child-like innocence lost by Adam."
3. "The sonship, through and for the sake of Jesus, the only begotten Son."

### John Gill:
1. "The law was given to Adam as a covenant of works, and not to him as a single person, but as a federal head to all his posterity; hence he sinning, and they in him, they all came under its sentence of condemnation and death, God's elect not excepted."
2. "The redemption of them, which is the end of Christ's being sent, intends not only a deliverance of them from sin and Satan, and the world, to whom they were in bondage, but from the law under which they were; from the bondage of the ceremonial, and from the curse and condemnation of the moral law."
3. "Adoption, as a blessing of grace, exists before it is received; nor does the reception of it add anything to the thing itself; it was in God's designation from all eternity, who predestinated his chosen ones unto it by Christ, according to the good pleasure of his will."
