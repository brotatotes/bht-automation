# Galatians 4:6 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The indwelling of the Spirit enables believers to have an intimate and personal relationship with God, as they are able to cry out to Him as their loving Father. Through the Spirit, believers have the confidence and attitude of dutiful children when approaching God in prayer. This verse also highlights the unity of believers, both Jews and Gentiles, as they join together in their cry to God. The Spirit's work of adoption and witness enables believers to experience the profound grace of becoming children of God.

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "The gift of the Spirit of prayer is the consequence of our adoption."
2. "The Spirit of God's only Begotten Son in your hearts, sent from, and leading you to cry to, the Father, attests your sonship by adoption."
3. "The believers' prayer is His prayer: hence arises its acceptability with God."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The communication of the Spirit is not confined to a single act, but goes on increasing with the spiritual growth of the children of God."
2. "The Holy Spirit so deeply sinks into the spirit of believers and so closely identifies himself with them that He prays in them and through them as their advocate."
3. "Abba, Father. 'Abba' is the Aramaic word for 'Father,' so childlike in its very sound, and sanctioned by the beginning of the Lord's Prayer, as originally uttered, also by His prayer in Gethsemane."

### John Gill:
1. "It is more to be a child of God, than to be redeemed, pardoned, and justified; it is great grace to redeem from slavery, to pardon criminals, and justify the ungodly; but it is another and an higher act of grace to make them sons."
2. "The Spirit of God not only regenerates them, which is an evidence of their sonship, but as a spirit of adoption manifests it to them, works faith in them to receive it, and frequently witnesses to the truth of it."
3. "The work he does there is various, and consists of divers parts; as convincing of sin, and righteousness, working faith, and acting the part of a comforter; but what is here referred to, is the discharge of his office as a spirit of adoption, 'crying Abba, Father'."

### John Wesley:

3. "The Hebrew and Greek word are joined together, to express the joint cry of the Jews and gentiles" - This statement highlights the unity of believers, both Jews and Gentiles, in their cry to God.
