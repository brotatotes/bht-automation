# Galatians 5:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Bible verse being described emphasizes the concept of freedom within the Christian faith. Commentators highlight the idea that believers have been called to this freedom, which is not to be misused or turned into an opportunity for indulgence in sinful desires. Rather, it should be used as an occasion to serve one another in love, as this demonstrates the true liberation that Christ has brought. The verse cautions against distortion and abuse of liberty, urging believers to use it in a way that upholds the principles of the faith and avoids giving ammunition for criticism.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "They were striking at the very root of their Christian calling, which was for freedom." 
2. "Make not your liberty into an occasion for the flesh, but by means of love, be in bondage to one another." 
3. "The mother of heresies is the desire for power."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The state or condition in which ye have been called to salvation, is one of liberty."
2. "Gospel liberty consists in three things, freedom from the Mosaic yoke, from sin, and from slavish fear."
3. "Do not give the flesh the handle or pretext for its indulgence which it eagerly seeks for; do not let it make Christian 'liberty' its pretext for indulgence."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The meaning here is, that Paul wished the false teachers removed because true Christians had been called unto liberty, and they were abridging and destroying that liberty."
2. "It is liberty from the servitude of sin, and free from subjection to expensive and burdensome rites and customs."
3. "Paul is, therefore, at great pains to show that the doctrines which he had maintained did not lead to licentiousness, and did not allow the indulgence of sinful and corrupt passions."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Well may I speak thus strongly of those who thus overthrow your whole polity and enslave you, for ye are called for freedom." 
2. "Christian freedom is not to be abused for selfish ends."
3. "Individual liberty is subject to the law of love and mutual service."

### John Calvin:
1. "Liberty lies in the conscience, and looks to God; the use of it lies in outward matters, and deals not with God only, but with men."
2. "Liberty is not granted to the flesh, which ought rather to be held captive under the yoke, but is a spiritual benefit, which none but pious minds are capable of enjoying."
3. "The method here explained of restraining liberty from breaking out into wide and licentious abuse is, to have it regulated by love."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "True freedom is self-government and inseparable from law; it is a law to itself."
2. "How often has the word freedom been abused and perverted into its diabolical caricature!"
3. "By faith we are lords, by love we are servants of all."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Ye were called for freedom" - emphasizes the purpose of the call, highlighting the importance of freedom in the Christian faith.
2. "Turn not your liberty into an occasion for the flesh" - warns against using freedom as an opportunity for indulging in sinful desires.
3. "Liberty so easily turns to license" - highlights the potential danger of misusing freedom and falling into a state of moral laxity.

### John Gill:
1. "He asserts that they were 'called' not merely externally, but internally, by the effectual grace of God, out of bondage to sin, Satan, and the law, unto the liberty of the Gospel and of the grace of God." 
2. "Christ's free men should not [misuse their Christian liberty by indulging the flesh and the lusts of it], for that is to disgrace the doctrine of Christian liberty, to enthral themselves in bondage instead of using their liberty aright, and to give the enemy occasion to blaspheme."
3. "Gospel liberty and the service of the saints are not at all inconsistent; as it becomes them to love one another, as the new command of Christ, their profession of religion, and their relation to each other, require, so they should show their love by their service."

### John Wesley:
1. "Ye have been called to liberty — From sin and misery, as well as from the ceremonial law." 
2. "Only use not liberty for an occasion to the flesh — Take not occasion from hence to gratify corrupt nature." 
3. "But by love serve one another — And hereby show that Christ has made you free."
