# Galatians 6:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This description focuses on the motives and intentions of those advocating for circumcision. It highlights that their actions were not driven by a genuine commitment to the law, but rather by personal gain and the desire for recognition and boasting. The overall message conveyed is that these individuals were insincere in their zeal for circumcision, using it as a means to achieve their own self-interest and to gain favor with others.

### Henry Alford:
1. "For not even they who are being circumcised themselves keep the law, but wish you to be circumcised." 
2. "That in your flesh they may make their boast." 
3. "In this way they escaped the scandal of the Cross at the hands of the Jews, by making in fact their Christian converts into Jewish proselytes."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "They arbitrarily selected circumcision out of the whole law, as though observing it would stand instead of their non-observance of the rest of the law."
2. "That they may glory in your flesh - namely, in the outward change which they have effected in bringing you over to their own Jewish-Christian party."
3. "The outward change (opposed to an inward change wrought by the SPIRIT)."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It was true in general that the Jews did not wholly and entirely obey the Law of Moses."
2. "Paul insists on it that if they were circumcised, and depended on that for salvation, they were under obligation to keep the whole law. But they did not."
3. "They thus show their insincerity and hypocrisy."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "They are in the same category with all who are circumcised, who do not and cannot fully observe the law."
2. "Hence, if circumcision develops no justifying results, it is apparent that their insistence on circumcision proceeds not from moral, but from fleshly motives."
3. "They desire only that you, like them, should make a fair show in the flesh."

### John Calvin:
1. "It is not from a strong attachment to the law that they bind you with the yoke of ceremonies; for, even with their own circumcision, they do not keep the law."
2. "He rather insinuates against them a charge of insincerity, because, except when it suited their own designs, they found themselves at liberty to despise the law."
3. "Paul therefore declares that they are not acting the part of honest men, and that they have no other object in enjoining circumcision than to boast to the Jews of the converts they have made."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "For not even they who are circumcised keep the law (in all its details, comp. Galatians 5:3), but they desire to have you circumcised, that they may glory in your flesh."
2. "The advocates of circumcision are not sincere in their zeal, but want to gratify their vanity in making proselytes."
3. "(Comp. Matthew 23:15.)"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They who receive circumcision" - This phrase refers to those who actively choose to undergo circumcision, indicating a deliberate action on their part.
2. "Those who are having themselves circumcised" - This wording emphasizes the personal agency of the individuals in the act of circumcision, suggesting that it is a conscious decision they make for themselves.
3. "They who have been circumcised" - This alternative reading suggests that circumcision is something that has already been done to them, implying a passive role in the process.

### John Gill:
1. "It means those that were of the circumcision, on that side of the question, whether heartily or not."
2. "This might be done, not out of regard unto it, and zeal for it, but for some other end."
3. "Their view in bringing persons to submit to circumcision was, that they might have occasion to glory among the Jews, and boast to them of the numbers of proselytes they brought."

These quotes highlight the motives behind the proponents of circumcision, emphasizing that their actions were not driven by genuine devotion to the law, but rather by personal gain and recognition.

### John Wesley:
1. "So far are they from a real zeal for it." 
2. "But yet they desire to have you circumcised, that they may glory in your flesh." 
3. "That they may boast of you as their proselytes, and make a merit of this with the other Jews."
