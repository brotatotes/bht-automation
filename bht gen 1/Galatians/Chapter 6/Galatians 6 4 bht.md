### Albert Barnes:
1. "Let him compare himself with the word of God, and the infallible rule which he has given, and by which we are to be judged in the last great day."
2. "The man who forms an improper estimate of his own character will be sure to be disappointed."
3. "He will find in himself a source of pure joy. He will not be dependent on the applause of others for happiness."

These quotes emphasize the importance of self-examination, forming a fair estimate of one's character, and finding true happiness within oneself rather than relying on external validation.

### John Calvin:
1. "Let no man, he says, measure himself by the standard of another, or please himself with the thought, that others appear to him less worthy of approbation."
2. "It is not what we gain by detracting from others, but what we have without any comparison, that can be regarded as true praise."
3. "With respect to thyself alone, and not by comparison with others, thou wilt have praise."
4. "When a man is valued for his own worth, and not for the baseness of others, the praise is just and substantial."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "If a man desires to find cause for boasting, let him test and examine his own actions, and not contrast his fancied virtues with the faults of his neighbor." 
2. "But every sincere self-examination results in humiliation." 
3. "His own work, collective and emphatic: the aggregate of his actions."

### John Gill:
1. "Let every man prove his own work... let him examine, try, and prove his whole conduct in life by the rule of God's word." 
2. "He may rejoice 'in himself', in his own works, as the fruits of grace, but not as the effects of his own power and strength."
3. "His glorying will be on account of his own actions, and not through a comparison of other men's."

### John Wesley:
1. "Let every man try his own work." This encourages individuals to closely examine themselves and their actions, taking personal responsibility for their work.
2. "He shall have rejoicing in himself." If one's works are righteous before God, they will find inner joy and satisfaction, not relying on the approval or recognition of others.
3. "Not in another." True rejoicing does not come from comparing oneself to others or seeking superiority over them, but from a genuine evaluation of one's own actions.
