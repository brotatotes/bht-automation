# Hebrews 10:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The verse in question speaks of the eternal efficacy and completeness of Christ's sacrifice for sins. It contrasts the repeated offerings of earthly priests with Christ's one-time sacrifice, highlighting the finished work of redemption. The verse also emphasizes the unchanging and eternal priesthood of Christ, as He intercedes for believers. Through His sacrifice, Christ has brought about the forgiveness of sins and exalted Himself to a position of authority and honor, forever seated in majesty as the Son of God.

### Henry Alford:
1. "having offered one sacrifice for sins for ever" - This emphasizes the eternal efficacy of Christ's sacrifice, highlighting its completeness and sufficiency for the forgiveness of sins. 

2. "we preserve the contrast between ἕστηκεν καθʼ ἡμέραν and εἰς τὸ διηνεκὲς ἐκάθισεν" - This points out the contrast between the repeated standing of earthly priests to offer sacrifices and the permanent sitting of Christ, indicating the finished work of redemption.

3. "we are in full accordance with the ἱερεὺς εἰς τὸν αἰῶνα so often insisted on" - This highlights the continuous and unchanging priesthood of Christ, emphasizing His eternal role as the High Priest who intercedes for believers.

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "The offering of Christ, once for all made, will continue the one and only oblation for ever; no other will supersede it."
2. "The mass, which professes to be the frequent repetition of one and the same sacrifice of Christ's body, is hence disproved."
3. "A repetition would imply that the former once-for-all offering of the one sacrifice was imperfect, and so would be dishonoring to it."

Note: The commentary provides insights into the concept of Christ's sacrifice being a one-time event that has eternal efficacy, refuting the idea of repeated sacrifices in the mass.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The apostle does not state here whether he was a man, or a being of a higher order. He merely mentions him as a priest in contradistinction from the Jewish priests."
2. "This he did but once; this could not be repeated; and need not be repeated, for it was sufficient for the sins of the world."
3. "He will no more submit himself to scenes of suffering and death to expiate human guilt."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Forever [εις το διηνεκες]. Const. with offered." - This phrase emphasizes the eternal nature of the sacrifice, contrasting it with the repeated sacrifices of the past.

2. "One sacrifice forever is contrasted with the same sacrifices often." - The author highlights the significance of the single, eternal sacrifice in contrast to the repeated offerings under the Old Covenant.

3. "He offered one sacrifice forever, and then sat down, awaiting its eternal result." - This statement underscores the completion and efficacy of Christ's sacrifice, as He now awaits the eternal outcome of His redemptive work.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "An evidence of the completeness of His work, which left no room for another sacrifice or for the repetition of His own."
2. "His priesthood indeed continues, and the presentation of His sacrifice 'the perpetual oblation;' but His atoning work is over."
3. "'For ever,' in perpetuity, uninterruptedly, may be connected with 'took His seat,' but the usage of this Epistle is to connect it with the words that precede."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "The one sacrifice does the work that the many failed to do."
2. "One wonders how priests who claim that the 'mass' is the sacrifice of Christ's body repeated explain this verse."
3. "For ever. Can be construed either with 'one sacrifice' or with 'sat down'."

### John Gill:
1. "Jesus Christ, for he is a man, though not a mere man."
2. "After he had offered one sacrifice for sins; the sacrifice of himself, body and soul, and this but once."
3. "He sits enjoying rest, ease, and pleasure, and that for ever; all which is opposed to the priests under the law."

### John Wesley:
1. "The virtue of whose one sacrifice remains for ever" - This quote highlights the eternal significance and power of Christ's sacrifice on the cross, emphasizing that it is sufficient for all time and for all people.

2. "Sat down" - This phrase signifies Christ's completion of his work of redemption and his exaltation to a position of authority and honor, indicating his victory over sin and death.

3. "As a son, in majesty and honour" - This quote emphasizes the divine nature of Christ and his rightful place as the Son of God, seated in a position of majesty and honor, ruling over all creation.
