# Hebrews 11:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, we see Abraham's unwavering faith in God's power to overcome even death itself. Despite there being no previous instance of resurrection, Abraham believed that God was able to raise Isaac from the dead. Through his obedience and trust, Abraham witnessed the triumph over death as he received Isaac back alive, foreshadowing the ultimate victory over death through Jesus' resurrection. This verse highlights the incredible power and faithfulness of God, reassuring us that He is capable of fulfilling His promises.

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "Faith answered the objections which reason brought against God's command to Abraham to offer Isaac, by suggesting that what God had promised He both could and would perform, however impossible the performance might seem."
2. "The quickening of Sarah's dead womb suggested the thought of God's power to raise even the dead, though no instance of it had as yet occurred."
3. "I prefer understanding the figure to be the representation which the whole scene gave to Abraham of Christ in His death (typified by Isaac's offering in intention, and the ram's actual substitution answering to Christ's vicarious death), and in His resurrection (typified by Abraham's receiving him back alive from the jaws of death)."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "To fulfil these promises, therefore, there was no other way possible but for him to be raised up from the dead, and Abraham fully believed that it would be done."
2. "The obvious interpretation is that he then received him by his being raised up from the altar as if from the dead."
3. "The strength of his faith was seen in the fact that in obedience to the simple command of God, all the strong feelings of a father were overcome."

### John Calvin:
1. "Nor did hope disappoint Abraham, for it was a sort of resurrection, when his son was so suddenly delivered from the midst of death."
2. "I take it simply as meaning likeness; for though Isaac did not really rise from the dead, yet he seemed to have in a manner risen, when he was suddenly and wonderfully rescued through the unexpected favor of God."
3. "Abraham did not receive his Son otherwise than if he had been restored from death to new life."

### Archibald T Robertson:

### John Gill:
