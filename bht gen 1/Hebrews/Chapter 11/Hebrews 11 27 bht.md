# Hebrews 11:27 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, we see the remarkable faith of Moses as he courageously led the Israelites out of Egypt. His actions were fueled by an unwavering trust in God's guidance and protection, allowing him to overcome his fear of the king's wrath. This faith was not a one-time occurrence but a steady and enduring force throughout Moses' journey, as evidenced by his previous encounters with the king's anger. Moses' story serves as a powerful reminder of the transformative power of faith and its ability to embolden us in the face of adversity.

### Henry Alford:
1. "By faith, he left Egypt, not fearing the wrath of the king... for he endured as seeing the invisible One." 
2. "The fact of his flight was the effect of his fear, but the same flight itself, the dereliction of Egypt and reserving himself for further action, showed that that fear did not possess nor bear him away." 
3. "He so left Egypt, as intending to return to it, but avoiding the thrusting of himself into danger at the moment."

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "He did not fear the king so as to neglect his duty and not return when God called him."
2. "His resolution to take his portion with oppressed Israel, which he could not have done had he stayed, was the motive of his flight, and constituted the 'faith' of this act."
3. "He had fled, not so much from fear of Pharaoh, as from a revulsion of feeling in finding God's people insensible to their high destiny, and from disappointment at not having been able to inspire them with those hopes for which he had sacrificed all his earthly prospects."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He went forth, indeed, at the head of his people when all the power of the king was excited to destroy them, but he went confiding in God: and this is the faith referred to here."
2. "He persevered, amidst all the trials and difficulties connected with his leading forth the people from bondage."
3. "He had no more doubt that God had called him to this work, and that he would sustain him, than if he saw him with his physical eyes."

### John Calvin:
1. "It was doubtless an instance of the wonderful strength of faith, that he brought out a multitude untrained for war and burdened with many incumbrances, and yet hoped that a way would be opened to him by God’s hand through innumerable difficulties."
2. "He saw a most powerful king in a furious rage, and he knew that he would not cease till he had tried his utmost. But as he knew that God had commanded him to depart, he committed the event to him, nor did he doubt but that he would in due time restrain all the assaults of the Egyptians."
3. "We hence learn, that the true character of faith is to set God always before our eyes; secondly, that faith beholds higher and more hidden things in God than what our senses can perceive; and thirdly, that a view of God alone is sufficient to strengthen our weakness, so that we may become firmer than rocks to withstand all the assaults of Satan."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Not fearing... Moses did flee from Egypt after slaying the Egyptian, but the author omits that slaughter and ignores it as the dominant motive in the flight of Moses." 
2. "He endured... Moses had made his choice before slaying the Egyptian. He stuck to it resolutely."
3. "As seeing him who is invisible... This is the secret of his choice and of his loyalty to God and to God's people."

### John Gill:
1. "His fleeing was consistent with courage, and was a point of prudence, and in obedience to the will of God."
2. "He endured affliction and meanness in Midian, and waited patiently, till God should call him again to service."
3. "He expected divine help, and persevered; and so do such, who are called by the grace of God."
