# Hebrews 11:33 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Through faith, believers have achieved extraordinary feats like subduing kingdoms and executing acts of righteousness. Their faith not only allowed them to obtain promises, but also empowered them to face and conquer intimidating challenges, such as stopping the mouths of lions. This chapter describes the monumental impact of faith on the lives of biblical figures who trusted in God's promises and overcame seemingly insurmountable obstacles. The power of faith is showcased in the achievements and triumphs of these faithful individuals throughout history.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "through faith...subdued kingdoms" - This verse highlights the power of faith in conquering and overcoming challenges, even on a national scale. 
2. "wrought righteousness" - The commentary emphasizes that righteousness is not limited to specific individuals, but can be seen in the actions of various biblical figures. 
3. "obtained promises" - The commentary suggests that this phrase can refer to both receiving spoken promises from God and experiencing the fulfillment of those promises. It highlights the faith of those who trusted in God's promises, even when they were not yet fully realized.

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "subdued kingdoms—as David did so also Gideon subdued Midian."
2. "wrought righteousness—as Samuel did; and David."
3. "obtained the fulfilment of promises, which had been previously the object of their faith."
4. "stopped the mouths of lions—because he believed in his God."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Who through faith subdued kingdoms" - Some of them subdued kingdoms, demonstrating their faith by conquering nations and establishing God's rule.
2. "Wrought righteousness" - They executed God's justice by punishing the wicked and carrying out His purposes.
3. "Obtained promises" - They received blessings and promises from God as a result of their faith, securing blessings for future generations.
4. "Stopped the mouths of lions" - They demonstrated their faith by facing and overcoming dangerous situations, such as Samson, David, and Daniel, who triumphed over lions.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Through faith, the expression for the last time in this chapter, and specially appropriate as describing the instrument by which those great works were accomplished."
2. "Subdued kingdoms, true of all the judges named, as it is of Samuel and David."
3. "Obtained promises, i.e obtained the fulfilment of them, not indeed of the great promise of all, but of the lesser promises which God fulfilled to the prophets themselves."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Through faith" - Change thus from the routine πιστε used so far.
2. "Subdued kingdoms" - First aorist middle indicative of καταγωνιζομα, Koine verb to struggle against, to overcome, here alone in the N.T.
3. "Stopped the mouths of lions" - First aorist active indicative of φρασσω, old verb to fence in, to block up. See Daniel 6:18-23.

### John Gill:
1. "Believers have no reason to be afraid of kings, or kingdoms; and this should encourage the saints, in their combats with the powers of darkness."
2. "To do works of righteousness in faith, and by it, is something very considerable; it is reckoned here among actions of the greatest fame."
3. "Their faith was not the cause of promises being made, nor of their being fulfilled; but was the grace by which these believers received them, believed them, and waited for the accomplishment of them."
