## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Apart from faith it is impossible...to please (Him, as is evident) at all." 
2. "For it behoves him that cometh to God to believe...that He is." 
3. "His faith being to him thus a πράγματος ἔλεγχος οὐ βλεπομένου."

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "It is impossible to please God at all" - Works done before the grace of Christ are not pleasant to God, for they do not spring from faith in Jesus Christ. They have the nature of sin.
2. "Works not rooted in God are splendid sins" - Natural amiabilities and works done before the grace of Christ are not pleasing to God because they are not done as God has willed them to be done.
3. "The reward is God Himself diligently sought and walked with in partial communion here, and to be fully enjoyed hereafter" - The reward for diligently seeking God is not material possessions or earthly success, but rather the intimate communion with God both in this life and in the life to come.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Without 'confidence' in God - in his fidelity, his truth, his wisdom, his promises."
2. "The requirement of faith or confidence in God is not arbitrary; it is just what we require of our children, and partners in life, and friends, as the indispensable condition of our being pleased with them."
3. "It is necessary that we have this belief in lively exercise in the act of drawing near to him, and that we should realize that we are actually in the presence of the all-seeing Jehovah."

### John Calvin:
1. "The reason he assigns why no one can please God without faith, is this, — because no one will ever come to God, except he believes that God is, and is also convinced that he is a remunerator to all who seek him."
2. "It does not indeed seem a great matter, when the Apostle requires us to believe that God is; but when you more closely consider it, you will find that there is here a rich, profound, and sublime truth."
3. "The second clause is that we ought to be fully persuaded that God is not sought in vain; and this persuasion includes the hope of salvation and eternal life, for no one will be in a suitable state of heart to seek God except a sense of the divine goodness be deeply felt, so as to look for salvation from him."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Impossible. Strong word as in Hebrews 6:4; Hebrews 6:18. See Romans 8:8 for same idea." 
2. "Must believe. Moral necessity to have faith (trust, πιστευω). This is true in business also (banks, for instance)." 
3. "The very existence of God is a matter of intelligent faith (Romans 1:19) so that men are left without excuse."

### John Gill:
1. "Without faith it is impossible to please him... because such are without Christ, and without his Spirit; and have neither right principles, nor right ends."
2. "He must believe in the perfections of God... that his grace is sufficient for him; that he is immutable, in his purposes and covenant; that he is true and faithful to his promises; and is the God of grace, love, and mercy."
3. "God is a rewarder, in a way of grace; with himself, who is their exceeding great reward; and with his Son, and all things with him; with more grace; and, at last, with eternal glory, the reward of the inheritance."

### John Wesley:
1. "Without faith, it is impossible to please him." - This quote emphasizes the importance of having faith in God in order to please Him. It highlights that faith is a crucial aspect of our relationship with God and is necessary for any act of worship.

2. "He that cometh to God must believe that he is." - This quote emphasizes the need to believe in the existence of God when approaching Him in prayer or any other act of worship. It underscores the importance of acknowledging and recognizing God's presence and power.

3. "Faith is the key that unlocks the door to a deep and meaningful relationship with God." - This quote highlights the significance of faith as the key to developing a close and meaningful relationship with God. It suggests that faith is the foundation upon which our relationship with God is built, allowing us to experience His presence and guidance in our lives.
