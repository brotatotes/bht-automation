1. "Believers being like the angels, 'sons of God,' are so their 'equals'."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The design is to 'contrast' the Christian dispensation with the Jewish... It had more to win the affections; more to elevate the soul; more to inspire with hope."
2. "They were citizens of the heavenly Jerusalem... and were entitled to its privileges."
3. "The apostle does not represent them as if they were expecting that it would be visibly set up on the earth, but as being now actually dwellers in that city, and bound to live and act as if they were amidst its splendors."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The heavenly Jerusalem. The spiritual mountain and city where God dwells and reigns."
2. "To an innumerable company of angels."
3. "Rend. 'to an innumerable multitude,' placing a comma after muriasin, and connecting of angels with the next clause."

### John Calvin:
1. "He alludes to those prophecies in which God had formerly promised that his Gospel should thence go forth."
2. "He expressly calls it heavenly, that the Jews might not cleave to that which was earthly."
3. "He means that we are associated with angels, chosen into the ranks of patriarchs, and placed in heaven among all the spirits of the blessed."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Sharp contrast to verse Hebrews 12:18 with same form προσεληλυθατε."
2. "In contrast to Mount Sinai (verses Hebrews 12:18-21)."
3. "Heaven is termed thus a spiritual mountain and city."

### John Gill:
1. "By 'Mount Sion', and the other names here given, is meant the church of God, under the Gospel dispensation, to which the believing Hebrews were come; in distinction from the legal dispensation, signified by Mount Sinai, from which they were delivered."
2. "The Gospel church is a city, built on Christ, the foundation; and is full of inhabitants, true believers... it is well guarded by watchmen, which he has set upon the walls of it; and it is endowed with many privileges, as access to God, freedom from the arrests of justice, and from condemnation, adoption, and a right to the heavenly inheritance."
3. "The church of God goes by the name of Jerusalem often... it agrees in its name, which signifies the vision of peace, or they shall see peace... it is well fortified, God himself, and his power, being all around it, and having salvation, for walls and bulwarks, and being encamped about by angels."

### John Wesley:
1. "But ye — Who believe in Christ. Are come — The apostle does not here speak of their coming to the church militant, but of that glorious privilege of New Testament believers, their communion with the church triumphant."
2. "St. Paul here shows an excellent knowledge of the heavenly economy, worthy of him who had been caught up into the third heaven."
3. "To mount Sion — A spiritual mountain. To the city of the living God, the heavenly Jerusalem — All these glorious titles belong to the New Testament church."
