# Hebrews 13:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Bible verse being described is a reminder to believers that this world is not their permanent home. The commentators emphasize the temporary nature of earthly things, highlighting the impending destruction of Jerusalem as an example. They encourage believers to not become attached to the things of this world, but to set their hopes on the eternal city that is to come. It is a call to remember that our true home is in heaven, not on earth.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "For we have not here an abiding city, but we seek for that which is to come."
2. "The heavenly Jerusalem, in all her glory, is not yet existing, nor shall be until the number of the elect is accomplished."
3. "This verse certainly comes with a solemn tone on the reader, considering how short a time the μένουσα πόλις did actually remain, and how soon the destruction of Jerusalem put an end to the Jewish polity which was supposed to be so enduring."

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "The earthly Jerusalem proved to be no 'abiding city,' having been destroyed shortly after this Epistle was written, and with it fell the Jewish civil and religious polity; a type of the whole of our present earthly order of things soon to perish."
2. "Those Hebrews who clung to the earthly sanctuary are representatives of all who cling to this earth."
3. "The one to come" is referenced in multiple passages of the Bible, emphasizing the anticipation of a future city and kingdom beyond the earthly realm.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "We do not regard this as our final home, or our fixed abode, and we should be willing to bear reproaches during the little time that we are to remain here."
2. "If, therefore, in consequence of our professed attachment to the Saviour, we should be driven away from our habitations, and compelled to wander, we should be willing to submit to it, for our permanent home is not here, but in heaven."
3. "He tells them that their Lord was led from that city to be put to death, and they should be willing to go forth also; that their permanent home was not Jerusalem, but heaven, and they should be willing in view of that blessed abode to be exiled from the city where they dwelt, and made wanderers in the earth."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The city itself is not abiding. As a holy city, it is the center and representative of a system of shadows and figures, which is to be shaken and removed, even as is the city itself."
2. "Be not carried away with divers and strange teachings, for example, those concerning meats and drinks and sacrificial feasts."
3. "It is the sacrifice of Christ through which you become partakers of grace. It is impossible to obtain grace through meats; for meats represent the economy which denies Christ."

### John Calvin:
1. "As strangers and wanderers in this world we should consider that we have no fixed residence but in heaven."
2. "Whenever we are driven from place to place, or whenever any change happens to us, let us think of what the Apostle teaches us here, that we have no certain shade on earth, for heaven is our inheritance."
3. "It is hence profitable for us, who are prone to this kind of sloth, to be often tossed here and there, that we may learn to turn our eyes up to heaven."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Israel still claimed to be the people of God, and Jerusalem was outwardly His dwelling-place. But God had already quitted it."
2. "Here, therefore, we have no continuing city, no material temple, no imperfect sacrifice."
3. "But the cross and Christ and heaven the antitype of them all."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Jerusalem has lost its charm for followers of Christ."
2. "The Epistle must have been written before the destruction of Jerusalem else a reference to that event could hardly have been avoided here."
3. "We are now where Abraham was once."

### John Gill:
1. "For here have we no continuing city... the common instability of this world, and of the state of the saints in it."
2. "The saints have no settlement and abiding here; they are not of it, though in it; and though they are in it, it is but for a time."
3. "And it is their mercy, that they are not to continue here; since, while in it, they are exposed to a great many sorrows and afflictions."

These quotes highlight the transient nature of the world and the temporary dwelling of the saints in it. They emphasize the instability of worldly things and the challenges faced by believers.

### John Wesley:
1. "No continuing city - All things here are but for a moment."

3. "On earth, we have no permanent dwelling place."
