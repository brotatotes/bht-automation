# Hebrews 13:21 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This verse speaks of a desire for perfection and the accomplishment of good works according to the will of God. The commentators emphasize the need for divine intervention in order to fulfill God's commands and be pleasing in His sight. They highlight the importance of unity, mutual adjustment, and the role of God's grace in enabling believers to do His will. Ultimately, the verse emphasizes that true goodness and sanctification come from God alone, as He works in the hearts of His people.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The praise of having made a beginning is not necessarily involved in the wish that they may be perfected." 
2. "The expression here is in the same final sense as there, as the aor. shews: it is not εἰς τὸ ποιεῖν, ‘to the habit of doing,’ but εἰς τὸ ποιῆσαι, ‘to the having done,’ i.e. ‘to the accomplishing.’"
3. "It is a pregnant construction, involving τὸ ἀγαθὸν ἐνώπιον αὐτοῦ, καὶ διὰ τοῦτο εὐάρεστον αὐτῷ."

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "Make you perfect—properly said of healing a rent; join you together in perfect harmony." 
2. "Whatever good we do, God does in us." 
3. "He closes as he began, with giving glory to Christ."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The meaning here is, that Paul prayed that God would fully endow them with whatever grace was necessary to do his will and to keep his commandments."
2. "The only hope that they would do the will of God was, that he would, by his own agency, cause them to do what was well-pleasing in his sight."
3. "God does not directly, and by his own immediate agency, convert and sanctify the heart, but it is through the gospel of Christ, and all good influences on the soul must be expected through the Saviour."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The prayer is for the complete mutual adjustment of all the members of the flock into a perfected whole, fitted to do the perfect will of God."
2. "In every good thing."
3. "Make you perfect to do his will, he doing in us what is well-pleasing in his sight."

### John Calvin:
1. "No works are to be deemed good, but such as are agreeable to the will of God." 
2. "In vain is obtruded on God what he has not commanded; for he values the decrees of his own will far more than all the inventions of the world."
3. "Our works, performed by the odor of Christ's grace, emit a sweet fragrance in God's presence, while otherwise they would have a fetid smell."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Make you perfect (καταρτισα). A wish for the future." 
2. "Working in us (ποιων εν εμιν). 'Doing in us.' Some MSS. read 'in you.'"
3. "Well-pleasing (ευαρεστον). Usually with the dative (Romans 12:2), here with ενωπιον αυτου more like the Hebrew."

### John Gill:
1. "A good work is what is done in obedience to the command of God, and in faith, and from a principle of love, in the name and strength of Christ, and with a view to the glory of God."
2. "Saints need to be strengthened with spiritual strength, to perform good works; for they are weak and feeble, and unable of themselves to do anything spiritually good."
3. "Grace is a work not of man, but of God; it is an internal work, something in a man's heart, and not anything without him, or done by him; and it is a gradual and progressive work; it is carried on by degrees, and is not yet perfect, though it will be."
