### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Messiah declares the name of the Father, not known fully as Christ's Father, and therefore their Father, till after His crucifixion."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The whole of that Psalm has been commonly referred to the Messiah...there can be no reasonable doubt that it had an original reference to him."
2. "The meaning is, that it would be a part of the Messiah’s work to make known to his disciples the character and perfections of God."
3. "He spoke of them as 'brethren.' Paul is showing that he was not ashamed to call them such."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "This acknowledgment as brethren the writer represents as prophetically announced by Messiah in Psalms 22:22."
2. "The Psalmist declares that God has answered his prayer, and that he will give public thanks therefore."
3. "Christ is represented as identifying himself with them in thanksgiving."

### John Calvin:
1. "The proclamation of God's praises is always promoted by the teaching of the gospel."
2. "Christ encourages us by his own example publicly to celebrate [God's praises], so that they may be heard by as many as possible."
3. "It is a truth...when we hear that Christ leads our songs, and is the chief composer of our hymns."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The church... best represents the sense, the exact meaning of the original and the force of the argument."
2. "The Old Testament name is the congregation."
3. "In modern usage the congregation is one thing, and the church is another."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "To prove his point the writer quotes Psalms 22:22 when the Messiah is presented as speaking 'unto my brethren.'" This quote highlights the writer's use of scripture to support their argument and emphasizes the connection between the Messiah and his brethren.

2. "The word came to mean the local church and also the general church or kingdom." This quote explains the different meanings of the word "congregation" and suggests that it can refer to both a specific group of believers and the broader community of believers.

3. "Here we have the picture of public worship and the Messiah sharing it with others as we know Jesus often did." This quote portrays the Messiah as actively participating in public worship and highlights Jesus' own engagement in communal worship, providing insight into his character and actions.

### John Gill:
1. "And these are the words of Christ addressed to his Father; whose name he promises to declare to his brethren; meaning not the Jews, in general, his brethren according to the flesh; but his disciples and followers, particularly the twelve apostles, and the five hundred brethren to whom he appeared after his resurrection; and indeed all the saints and people of God may be included."
2. "By his name he would declare to them, is not meant any particular name of his, as Elohim, El-shaddai, Jehovah, or the like; but rather he himself, and the perfections of his nature, which he, the only begotten Son, lying in his bosom, has declared."
3. "This is expressive of Christ's prophetic office, of his preaching of the Gospel, both in his own person, and by his ministers."

### John Wesley:
1. "Christ declares the name of God, gracious and merciful, plenteous in goodness and truth, to all who believe, that they also may praise him."
2. "In the midst of the church will I sing praise unto thee — As the precentor of the choir. This he did literally, in the midst of his apostles, on the night before his passion."
3. "He has done it in the church by his word and his Spirit; he still does, and will do it throughout all generations."
