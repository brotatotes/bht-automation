# Hebrews 2:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this Bible verse, we learn that God has bestowed great honor and dignity upon man, raising him to a position just beneath the angels and close to God Himself. The verse also alludes to the incarnation of Christ, highlighting how He willingly humbled Himself to be made lower than the angels during His suffering and death. This astonishing act of lowering Himself led to the crown of glory and honor that He now wears, fulfilling God's purpose and bringing awe and wonder to those who contemplate the greatness of His sacrifice.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Thou lettest him be little inferior to God." 
2. "Man, who was left not far behind the divine attributes themselves, was also invested with kingly majesty on earth, put into the place of God Himself in sovereignty over the world." 
3. "The angelic nature, being the lowest of that which is divine and heavenly, marks well the terminus just beneath which man is set."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Man, in his original creation, was set next beneath them." 
2. "The man Jesus, though Lord of angels, when He emptied Himself of the externals of His Divinity, was in His human nature 'a little lower than the angels'." 
3. "Crownedst him with glory and honour as the appointed kingly vicegerent of God over this earth."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The meaning is, that God had made man but little inferior to the angels in rank. He was inferior, but still God had exalted him almost to their rank."
2. "The wonder of the Psalmist is, that God had given to human nature so much honor - a wonder that is not at all diminished when we think of the honor done to man by his connection with the divine nature in the person of the Lord Jesus."
3. "That such honor should be put on 'man' is what is suited to excite amazement, and well may one continue to ask why it has been done?"

### John Calvin:
1. "I have already shown that the passage is fitly applicable to the Son of God."
2. "The meaning of David is this, — 'O Lord, thou hast raised man to such a dignity, that it differs but little from divine or angelic honor; for he is set a ruler over the whole world.'"
3. "He only bids us to consider the abasement of Christ, which appeared for a short time, and then the glory with which he is perpetually crowned."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Thou madest him a little lower...to lessen, to decrease, to make less." 
2. "Than the angels...beside angels." 
3. "Thou crownedst...to crown...God's purpose in creating man with such a destiny as mastery over nature."

### John Gill:
1. "Christ was made a little lower than the angels, through the assumption of the human nature, which is inferior to angels, especially the corporeal part of it."
2. "He was made lower than they, when he was deprived of the gracious presence of God, and in the time of his sufferings and death."
3. "Thou crownest him with glory and honour; with that glory he had with the Father before the world was, and which followed upon his sufferings and death."

### John Wesley:
1. "Thou hast made him — Adam.A little lower than the angels — The Hebrew is, a little lower than (that is, next to) God. Such was man as he came out of the hands of his Creator: it seems, the highest of all created beings."
2. "But these words are also in a farther sense, as the apostle here shows, applicable to the Son of God."
3. "It should be remembered that the apostles constantly cited the Septuagint translation, very frequently without any variation."
