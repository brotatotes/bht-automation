# Hebrews 6:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In the pursuit of strengthening our hope until the end, we are urged to show diligence and cultivate faith and love. It is important to remain steadfast, eagerly awaiting the fulfillment of our hope in the coming of the Lord. This assurance of hope is not a one-time event, but rather a continuous process of nurturing our trust in God. Through holy living and a life of consistent piety, we can attain the full assurance of hope, firmly rooted in faith and love, which becomes our safeguard against apostasy.

### Henry Alford:
1. "But we earnestly desire...that every one of you do show the same diligence...with regard to the full assurance of your hope until the end." 
2. "The full assurance of hope is not a one-time event, but an ongoing process of diligently cultivating and strengthening our hope in God." 
3. "The end refers to the coming of the Lord, which believers eagerly anticipate and should remain steadfast in their hope until its fulfillment."

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "He cares alike for great and small, and overlooks none."
2. "Every one of them," even those diligent in acts of LOVE, needed to be stimulated to persevere in the same diligence with a view to the full assurance of HOPE unto the end.
3. They needed, besides love, patient perseverance, resting on hope and faith.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "We wish that every member of the church should exhibit the same endeavor to do good until they attain to the full assurance of hope."
2. "The full assurance of hope exists where there is the highest desire of heaven, and such corresponding evidence of personal piety as to leave no doubt that it will be ours."
3. "Full assurance of hope is to be obtained only by holy living, and it is usually only as the result of a life of consistent piety that such an assurance is to be obtained."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The manifestations just mentioned make the writer desire that they may exhibit more of the spirit which animates their beneficent works."
2. "He is concerned, not only for the body of believers, but for each member."
3. "We desire that each of you exhibit the same diligence to develop your hope, which is in danger of failing, into full assurance, unto the end of the present season of trial with its happy consummation."

### John Calvin:
1. "The life of a Christian is not complete in all its parts, unless we attend to faith as well as to love."
2. "He bids them to be so fixed in firm faith, as no longer to vacillate nor be driven here and there, suspended between alternate winds of doubts."
3. "Let us then know that true faith is ever connected with hope."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The stress is on ‘the full assurance of your hope,’ and ‘unto the end.’" 
2. "The warnings of the Gospel are solemn, and yet Christians should live in the sunshine of an assured hope as the true safeguard against apostasy."
3. "A hope, however, which it is difficult to maintain."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "They have not given up Christ, but many of them are still babes and not adults, and others are in peril of becoming so." 
2. "Unto the fulness of hope." 
3. "To the end."

### John Gill:
1. "In the constant exercise of the graces of faith and love, and in ministering to the necessities of the saints: there is a spiritual labour, diligence, and industry to be used by the saints."
2. "And the persons addressed being 'everyone', this shows the apostle's impartiality in his exhortations, and served to remove all groundless suspicions in particular persons."
3. "And what he had in view in the whole was, that they might arrive to the full assurance of hope unto the end; not only that they might keep up an hope, but attain to the full assurance of it."

### John Wesley:
1. "The full assurance of faith relates to present pardon; the full assurance of hope, to future glory."
2. "The former is the highest degree of divine evidence that God is reconciled to me in the Son of his love; the latter is the same degree of divine evidence of persevering grace, and of eternal glory."
3. "But this assurance of faith and hope is not an opinion, not a bare construction of scripture, but is given immediately by the power of the Holy Ghost; and what none can have for another, but for himself only."
