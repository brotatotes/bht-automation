### John Gill:

2. "Grace makes a man a good man, not gifts" - The commentator emphasizes that while gifts may be impressive, it is the presence of grace that truly transforms a person. Grace is lasting, fruitful, and ultimately leads to salvation, whereas gifts may fade away or lack the transformative power of grace.

3. "Things that accompany salvation" - The commentator highlights the various aspects that are intertwined with salvation, such as faith and love. These elements are not separate from salvation but are integral parts of it. They form a chain of events that lead to predestination, justification, calling, adoption, sanctification, and ultimately, glorification.
