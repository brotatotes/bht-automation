# Hebrews 8:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, God is described as establishing a new covenant, superseding the old one. The commentators emphasize that this new covenant is characterized by forgiveness and grace. The old covenant, with its limitations and emphasis on the remembrance of sins, has become obsolete. In the new covenant, God's mercy prevails, and sinners are justified and pardoned, leading to a deeper knowledge of God and a transformation of the heart. Through this new covenant, God's laws are engraved on the believer's heart, establishing a personal and intimate relationship between God and the individual.
