# James 4:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, the commentators emphasize the importance of refraining from speaking against others and the harm it can cause to relationships and peace. They warn against judging and condemning others, as it goes against the true spirit of religion and disrespects the law. The commentators highlight that when we engage in evil speaking and judgment, we not only speak against our brothers and sisters but also undermine the authority and principles of God's law. Ultimately, the verse urges humility, love, and adherence to God's law in our interactions with others.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Do not speak against one another...depreciation of character and motive." 
2. "He that speaketh against a brother...speaketh against the law."
3. "If thou judgest the law, thou art not a doer of the law, but a judge."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Speak not evil—literally, 'Speak not against' one another." 
2. "Those who superciliously condemn the acts and words of others which do not please themselves, thus aiming at the reputation of sanctity, put their own moroseness in the place of the law, and claim to themselves a power of censuring above the law of God, condemning what the law permits."
3. "This is the last mention of the law in the New Testament. ALFORD rightly takes the 'law' to be the old moral law applied in its comprehensive spiritual fulness by Christ: 'the law of liberty.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The evil here referred to is that of talking against others - against their actions, their motives, their manner of living, their families, etc. Few things are more common in the world; nothing is more decidedly against the true spirit of religion."
2. "Religion forbids both, and would restrain us from all evil speaking against any human being."
3. "Not a few of the harsh judgments which one class of religionists pronounce on others, are in fact judgments on the laws of Christ."

### John Calvin:
1. "For hypocrisy is always presumptuous, and we are by nature hypocrites, fondly exalting ourselves by calumniating others."
2. "He intimates, that so much is taken away from the law as one claims of authority over his brethren."
3. "Let us then learn that we are not to judge but according to God’s law. Thou art not a doer of the law, but a judge."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Humility in the sight of God will show itself in humility with reference to our fellow-men: we will think humbly of ourselves, and so will not be so apt to undervalue others."
2. "The evil speaking which St. James here condemns, is sinful censuring; judging the motives and character of men; pretending to see into their hearts, and discerning the motives of their actions; condemning them without good reason from prejudice and envy, and thus usurping the judicial authority of God."
3. "He who in a censorious spirit judges his brother, sets at nought this law of love, and thus speaks evil of it, or undervalues it."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Prohibition against such a habit or a command to quit doing it."
2. "Often harsh words about the absent."
3. "This tone of superiority to law is here sharply condemned."

### John Gill:
1. "Speaking evil one of another; which is done either by raising false reports, and bringing false charges; or by aggravating failings and infirmities; or by lessening and depreciating characters, and endeavouring to bring others into discredit and disesteem among men." 
2. "For the saints to speak evil one of another, to sit and speak against a brother, and slander an own mother's son, is barbarous and unnatural."
3. "He that is a talebearer and backbites his brother, his fellow member, and detracts from his good name and character, and takes upon him to judge his heart, and his state, as well as, to condemn his actions, he speaks evil of the law; and judges and condemns that."

### John Wesley:
1. "Speak not evil one of another — This is a grand hinderance of peace."
2. "He that speaketh evil of another does in effect speak evil of the law, which so strongly prohibits it."
3. "Thou art not a doer of the law, but a judge — Of it; thou settest thyself above, and as it were condemnest, it."
