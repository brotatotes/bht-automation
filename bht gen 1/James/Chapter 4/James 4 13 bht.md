### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Go to now—'Come now'; said to excite attention."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The phrase 'go to now' is a phrase designed to arrest attention, as if there were something that demanded their notice, and especially, as in this case, with the implied thought that that to which the attention is called is wrong."
2. "All plans are wrong that are formed in the same spirit."
3. "It is not improbable that there is an allusion here to the commercial habits of the Jews at the time when the apostle wrote."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Go to now. Go to is an obsolete phrase, though retained in Rev. It is a formula for calling attention: come now."
2. "Such a city. More accurately, as Rev., this city."
3. "Better, as Rev., spend a year there. The word poihsomen implies more than mere continuance; rather, a doing something with the year."

### John Calvin:
1. "James does not reprove the form of speaking, but rather the arrogance of mind, that men should forget their own weakness, and speak thus presumptuously."
2. "It is indeed right and proper, when we promise anything as to future time, to accustom ourselves to such words as these, 'If it shall please the Lord,' 'If the Lord will permit.'"
3. "But James roused the stupidity of those who disregarded God’s providence, and claimed for themselves a whole year, though they had not a single moment in their own power."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The address 'Go to,' again repeated (chap. James 5:1), seems to indicate that the words in the two apostrophes are addressed to those without the Church."
2. "Those addressed are not designated as 'brethren,' as is the usual custom of St. James, nor are any marks given to indicate that they are Christians."
3. "The sin consisted in a presumptuous confidence in themselves, and in a want of realization of their dependence on God."

These quotes highlight the debate about who the passage is addressed to, the significance of the repeated phrase "Go to," and the underlying sin of presumptuous confidence.

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Today or tomorrow" - Correct text (Aleph B), not κα (and).
2. "Into this city" - Old demonstrative οδε, rare in N.T. (Luke 10:39) save in neuter plural ταδε (these things Acts 21:11).
3. "And get gain" - Future (Ionic form) active of κερδαινω, old verb from κερδος (gain, Philippians 1:21), as in Matthew 16:26.

### John Gill:
1. "The apostle passes from exposing the sin of detraction, and rash judgment, to inveigh against those of presumption and self-confidence."
2. "The phrase, 'go to now', is a note of transition, as well as of attention, and contains the form of a solemn and grave address to persons."
3. "The apostle does not condemn merchandise, and the lawful practice of buying and selling, and getting gain; but that men should not resolve upon those things without consulting God, and attending to his will."

### John Wesley:
1. "As peremptorily as if your life were in your own hands."
2. "The invitation is urgent and immediate, calling for immediate action."
3. "It challenges the readers to examine their own lives and motivations."
