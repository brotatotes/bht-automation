# John 1:20 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Commentators describe the humble confession of John the Baptist, who openly declared that he was not the long-awaited Messiah. His testimony carried significant weight and was delivered publicly and officially, depreciating himself in comparison to Jesus. John's firmness in his identity and purpose showed that he did not entertain the expectations and desires of the people, firmly directing the attention to the true Messiah, Jesus Christ. This confession proved John's authenticity as a genuine forerunner of Christ, showcasing the unwavering faithfulness and selflessness of his mission.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "He openly and formally confessed."
2. "The importance of his testimony, which was so publicly and officially delivered."
3. "The way in which he depreciated himself in comparison with Him who came after him."

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "While many were ready to hail him as the Christ, he neither gave the slightest ground for such views, nor the least entertainment to them."
2. "He did not confess to being the Christ, despite the expectations and desires of the people."
3. "Jesus remained firm in his identity and purpose, refusing to be swayed by the opinions and pressures of others."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "This confession proves that John was not an impostor."
2. "The fact that he did not do it is full proof that he did not intend to impose on people, but came only as the forerunner of Christ."
3. "His example shows that all Christians, and especially all Christian ministers, however much they may be honored and blessed, should be willing to lay all their honors at the feet of Jesus."

### John Calvin:
1. "He confessed openly, and without any ambiguity or hypocrisy."
2. "The word confess... means generally, that he stated the fact as it really was."
3. "He replied expressly, that he was not the Christ."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The effect of the double statement, ‘he confessed and denied not’ is to give peculiar impressiveness to the words: St. John thus brings into relief the single-minded faithfulness of the Baptist." 

2. "The first word is strongly emphatic, ‘It is not I who am the Christ.’ The Baptist thus prepares the way for the further statements which he is to make with the view of guiding his hearers to that Christ who is come."

3. "The Baptist...corrects mistaken opinions as to the character of his mission...he is to proclaim."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "The continued paratactic use of κα (and) and the first aorist active indicative of ομολογεω, old verb from ομολογος (ομον, λεγω, to say the same thing), to confess, in the Synoptics (Matthew 10:32) as here." 
2. "Negative statement of same thing in Johannine fashion, first aorist middle indicative of αρνεομα, another Synoptic and Pauline word (Matthew 10:33; 2 Timothy 2:12). He did not contradict or refuse to say who he was."
3. "Direct quotation again with recitative οτ before it like our modern quotation marks. 'I am not the Messiah,' he means by ο Χριστος (the Anointed One)."

### John Gill:
1. "He freely, and without any reserve, declared... that he was not the Messiah."
2. "He stood to it, and insisted on it, that he was not that illustrious person."
3. "They might assure themselves he was not Christ."

### John Wesley:
1. "I am not the Christ" - This statement by John the Baptist clarifies the misconception that many had about his identity. It emphasizes his humility and redirects the focus to the true Messiah, Jesus Christ.

2. "For many supposed he was" - This phrase highlights the confusion and speculation surrounding John's identity. It underscores the need for clarity and discernment in recognizing the true messengers of God.

3. "John's role was not to draw attention to himself but to point others to the one who could truly save them." - This quote emphasizes the selfless nature of John's mission. It reminds us that our purpose is not to seek personal glory, but to direct others to the ultimate source of salvation.
