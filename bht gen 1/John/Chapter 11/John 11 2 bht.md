# John 11:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this passage, we encounter a profound demonstration of love and devotion to Jesus by Mary, the sister of Martha and Lazarus. Through the act of anointing Jesus' feet with expensive perfume, Mary symbolizes her recognition of his divinity and foretells his impending death. This intimate gesture signifies her humility and willingness to honor and serve him, emphasizing the close relationship between Jesus and Mary. The anointing serves as a timeless reminder of the sacrificial love that fills the room, foreshadowing the redemptive power of Jesus' ultimate sacrifice for humanity.

### John Calvin:
1. "This mistake was occasioned by the anointing; as if it were not evident enough that Christ was anointed on various occasions, and even at different places."
2. "The woman who was a sinner, of whom Luke gives an account, anointed Christ at Jerusalem, where he dwelt; but Mary afterwards anointed him at Bethany, which was her own village."
3. "The past tense employed by the Evangelist, who anointed, must be referred, not to the time of the occurrence which he is now relating, but to the time when he wrote."

### Philip Schaff:

2. "There are particulars in which this narrative closely resembles that of chap. John 2:1-11: as there we have the closest tie of kindred, so here we read of the most intimate friendship."
3. "But the one tie as well as the other must yield to the voice of God."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
