# John 11:27 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, there is a woman named Martha who expresses her unwavering belief in Jesus as the promised Messiah and the Son of God. Through her confession, Martha showcases her full confidence and understanding of Jesus' teachings, particularly his role as the resurrection and the life. Despite her brother being dead for four days, Martha's faith remains steadfast, believing that Jesus has the power to raise the dead and grant eternal life to those who believe in him. Her confession exemplifies the heroic faith she holds, even in the face of difficult circumstances.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "And having such faith in Thee, I can believe all which that comprehends."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "This was a noble confession."
2. "It showed her full confidence in him as the Messiah."
3. "Her full belief that all that he said was true."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "πεπιστευκα. Literally, I have believed. The perfect tense." 
2. "Rev. Martha goes back to her previous belief, which consists in the recognition of Christ as her Lord." 
3. "Whatever faith she has in this new revelation of Christ rests upon the truth that He is the Anointed, the Son of God, even He that cometh into the world."

### John Calvin:
1. "To prove that she believes what she had heard Christ say about himself, that he is the resurrection and the life, Martha replies, that she believes that he is the Christ, and the Son of God."
2. "Now when Martha confesses that it was he who was to come into the world, she strengthens her faith by the predictions of the prophets."
3. "Hence it follows, that we ought to expect from him the full restoration of all things and perfect happiness; and, in short, that he was sent to erect and prepare the true and perfect state of the kingdom of God."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The substitution of ‘I have believed’ for ‘I believe’ is striking."
2. "His last words have been in some measure new and unfamiliar, and in her present state of mind she is incapable of comparing the old and the new."
3. "But that which she has believed and still believes contains the fullest recognition of her Lord."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Martha had heroic faith and makes now her own confession of faith in words that outrank those of Peter in Matthew 16:16 because she makes hers with her brother dead now four days and with the hope that Jesus will raise him up now."
2. "It is my settled and firm faith."
3. "No one said it under more trying circumstances than Martha."

### John Gill:
1. "She firmly believed all that he said concerning himself, and the happiness of those that believed in him."
2. "I have believed...that thou art the Christ the Son of God, which should come into the world."
3. "She must believe that he was able to raise the dead to life, and to secure those that believe in him from dying eternally."
