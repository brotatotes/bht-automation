He expresses gratitude to the Father, confident that His desire to showcase His power and give glory to God will be fulfilled. ### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Jesus lifted up his eyes—an expression marking His calm solemnity."
2. "He prayed not for what He wanted, but for the manifestation of what He had."
3. "Having the bright consciousness of the answer in the felt liberty to ask it, and the assurance that it was at hand, He gives thanks for this with a grand simplicity before performing the act."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Lifted up his eyes - In an attitude of prayer."
2. "I thank thee that thou hast heard me - The thanks which Jesus renders here are evidently in view of the fact that power had been committed to him to raise up Lazarus."
3. "In all his actions, he recognized his union to the Father, and his dependence upon him as Mediator."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The phrase 'from the place where the dead was laid' emphasizes the physical location of the tomb, highlighting the reality of death and the finality of the burial. It serves as a reminder of the power and significance of Jesus' miracle in raising Lazarus from the dead."
2. "By omitting the specific details of the tomb, the focus shifts from the physical location to the spiritual significance of the miracle. It invites readers to reflect on the greater meaning behind Jesus' ability to conquer death and bring forth new life."
3. "The omission of the specific location also serves to highlight the universality of Jesus' power over death. It suggests that Jesus' ability to raise the dead is not limited to a particular place or circumstance, but extends to all who believe in him."

### John Calvin:
1. "This was the token of a mind truly prepared for prayer; for before any one calls on God aright, he must be brought into communication with him, and this can only be done when, raised above the earth, he ascends even to heaven."
2. "Certainly, we cannot doubt that, when Christ raised his eyes to heaven, he was carried towards it with extraordinary vehemence. Besides, as all his thoughts were with the Father, so he also wished to bring others to the Father along with him."
3. "By saying that he has received this power from the Father, and by not ascribing it to himself, he does nothing more than acknowledge that he is the servant of the Father... that men may know that he was sent by the Father, that is, that they may acknowledge him to be the Son of God."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "No pomp of incantation, no wrestling in prayer even; but simple words of thanksgiving, as if already Lazarus was restored" (Dods).
2. "Jesus well knew the issues involved on this occasion. If he failed, his own claims to be the Son of God (the Messiah), would be hopelessly discredited with all."
3. "If he succeeded, the rulers would be so embittered as to compass his own death."

### John Gill:
1. "From the door of the sepulchre" - The stone was taken away from the place where the dead was laid, emphasizing the physical act of removing the barrier to the tomb.
2. "Jesus lift up his eyes" - Jesus looked up to heaven, demonstrating a posture of prayer and connection with the Father.
3. "Father, I thank thee that thou hast heard me" - Jesus expresses gratitude to the Father for already hearing him, indicating a deep trust and belief in the Father's continued support and intervention.

### John Wesley:
1. "Jesus lifted up his eyes — Not as if he applied to his Father for assistance. There is not the least show of this."
2. "He wrought the miracle with an air of absolute sovereignty, as the Lord of life and death."
3. "But it was as if he had said, I thank thee, that by the disposal of thy providence, thou hast granted my desire, in this remarkable opportunity of exerting my power, and showing forth thy praise."
