### John Calvin:
1. "He testifies that he came into the world to be a light, by which men might be delivered from darkness and errors."
2. "The higher the excellence of this benefit, of being called from darkness to light, the less excusable are they who, through their indolence or carelessness, quench the light that had been kindled in them."
3. "The term, whosoever, appears to have been added on purpose, partly, that all believers, without exception, may enjoy this benefit in common, and partly, to show that the reason why unbelievers perish in darkness is, that, of their own accord, they forsake the light."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Here we have the substance of the Saviour’s last words to the multitude...but nowhere has it been as clearly taught that all are ‘in the darkness’ until by faith in Jesus they receive light." 

2. "It is easy to trace a certain connection of thought in these verses...From beholding to light is a natural transition; from this point each verse directly leads the way to that which follows it."

3. "The thought is at first expressed in the language of figure, then with studious plainness and simplicity."
