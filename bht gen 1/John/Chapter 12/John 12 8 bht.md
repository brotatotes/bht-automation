# John 12:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, a beautiful act of pouring perfume on Jesus is commended as a good work done for Him. The love and devotion behind this act of service erects an eternal monument, surpassing any mere mechanical performance. Although there may be differing opinions on outward displays of worship, it is clear that the duty of giving to the poor is recognized and should never be forgotten. The act of pouring perfume serves as a symbol of faith and devotion, appreciated by Christ even when misunderstood by others.

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "In the act of love done to Him she had erected to herself an eternal monument, as lasting as the Gospel, the eternal word of God."

### John Calvin:
1. "Those persons, therefore, are apes, and not imitators, who are desirous to serve Christ by costly and splendid display; as if Christ approved of what was done once, and did not rather forbid that it should be done afterwards."
2. "When he says, that he will not always be with his disciples, this ought to be referred to that kind of presence to which carnal worship and costly honors are suitable."
3. "Of all the pompous ceremonies which the Papists have contrived for the worship of Christ, in vain do they tell us, that they have bestowed them upon him, for he openly rejects them."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The duty of giving to the poor is fully recognised: it must never be forgotten." 
2. "But there are moments when what may seem lavish waste upon objects visible only to the eye of faith are to be commended for the faith that is present in them."
3. "The very charity that cares for the poor whom we see has been kept alive by faith in, and devotion to, the crucified Redeemer whom we cannot see."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Jesus does not discredit gifts to the poor at all. But there is relativity in one's duties."

3. "John does not mention the precious promise of praise for Mary preserved in Mark 14:9; Matthew 26:13, but he does show her keen sympathetic insight and Christ's genuine appreciation of her noble deed."
