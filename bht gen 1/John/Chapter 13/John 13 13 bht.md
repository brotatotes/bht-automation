## BHT:
In this verse, Jesus is acknowledged as both a "Master" and a "Lord". These terms were not given flippantly, but rather out of a deep reverence and respect for his authority and divinity. Jesus himself affirms this acknowledgement, emphasizing the correctness of calling him Master and Lord. This dual role represents his position as both a teacher and instructor, guiding his followers, as well as their sovereign and king, ruling over them. Jesus' conscious dignity and surprising contrast throughout his life further underscore the extraordinary nature of his teachings and actions.
