# John 14:30 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, Jesus warns His disciples that the time of His departure is near and that Satan, the prince of this world, is coming to make his final assault. Jesus reassures them that although the enemy is approaching, he has no grounds or power over Him. Jesus is free from sin and corruption, making Him impervious to the temptations of the devil. This verse emphasizes the spiritual battle between good and evil and the victorious position of Christ over the forces of darkness.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "I have a little more to say, but My work hastens apace, and the approach of the adversary will cut it short."
2. "The prince of this world cometh with hostile intent, for a last grand attack, having failed in His first formidable assault."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The prince of this world - See the notes at John 12:31."
2. "Cometh - Satan is represented as approaching him to try him in his sufferings."
3. "Hath nothing in me - There is in me no principle or feeling that accords with his, and nothing, therefore, by which he can prevail."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Hereafter I will not talk [ουκ επι λαλησω]. Rev., more correctly, I will no more speak." - This quote highlights the shift in communication from Jesus, indicating that He will no longer engage in verbal discourse.
2. "The prince of this world. The best texts read, 'of the world.'" - This quote clarifies that the reference is to the ruler or authority of the world, emphasizing the contrast between Christ and the worldly power.
3. "Hath nothing in me. No right nor power over Christ which sin in Him could give." - This quote emphasizes that sin has no hold over Jesus, indicating His purity and righteousness.

### John Calvin:
1. "By this word he intended to fix the attention of the disciples on himself, and to impress his doctrine more deeply on their minds."
2. "For the prince of this world cometh...he makes use of a circumlocution, to fortify their minds beforehand, lest, terrified by a kind of death so hideous and detestable, they should faint."
3. "It is in consequence of the sin of Adam that Satan holds the dominion of death, and, therefore, he could not touch Christ, who is pure from all the pollution of sin, if he had not voluntarily subjected himself."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Here it is particularly to be noted that ‘the prince of this world’ is equivalent to the world in its essence." 
2. "He embodies the spirit of the world, so that what is said of it may be said of him, what is said of him may be said of it."
3. "Observe the ‘cometh,’ the contrast of the ‘coming’ of Jesus."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The prince of the world" refers to Satan, as mentioned in John 12:31. This highlights the spiritual battle between good and evil that exists in the world.

2. This phrase emphasizes Satan's authority and influence over the fallen world, symbolizing the power of sin and temptation.

3. By acknowledging Satan as the prince of the world, the verse reminds believers of the need to be vigilant and resist the temptations and deceptions of the enemy.

### John Gill:
1. "The design of this was, to observe to them that his time of departure was near at hand, and to quicken their attention to what he did say to them; since they could not expect to hear him long, or much more from him; he would be otherwise engaged."
2. "By 'the prince of this world', is meant the devil... to point out what a powerful adversary he had, and was about to engage with, and partly to observe to his disciples, what they must expect from the world, even hatred and persecution."
3. "Christ had no sin in him, which can be said of none but him... though he had sin upon him, he had none in him; the sins of his people were imputed to him, but he had no sin inherent in him."

### John Wesley:
1. "The prince of this world is coming — To make his grand assault."
2. "But he hath nothing in me — No right, no claim, or power."
3. "There is no guilt in me, to give him power over me; no corruption to take part with his temptation."
