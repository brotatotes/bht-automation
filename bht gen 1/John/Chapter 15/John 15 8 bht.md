## BHT:
The Bible verse describes the importance of bearing much fruit as a disciple of Christ. Commentators emphasize how this fruitfulness brings joy to God, glorifies His name, and demonstrates genuine discipleship. They highlight that the fruit refers to the production of good works, a holy life, and progress in Christian growth. Being fruitful showcases unity with Christ, communion with the Father, and a reflection of God's power and grace. Ultimately, being a disciple means embodying the teachings of Jesus and living a life that glorifies God.

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "Glorified that ye bear much fruit" - This phrase highlights the joy and satisfaction that God experiences when believers produce abundant spiritual fruit. It emphasizes the divine delight in the growth and productivity of His followers.

2. "Not only from His delight in it for its own sake" - This quote suggests that God's pleasure in the fruit-bearing of His disciples goes beyond mere enjoyment. It implies that the production of spiritual fruit has a greater purpose and significance in God's plan.

3. "So shall ye be my disciples" - This statement underscores the connection between bearing much fruit and being true disciples of Jesus. It implies that the evidence of genuine discipleship lies in the fruitful lives of believers, indicating their commitment to following Christ and living out His teachings.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Bear much fruit - Are fruitful in good works; are faithful, zealous, humble, devoted, always abounding in the work of the Lord. This honors God." 
2. "It is not by profession, but it is by a holy life, that the character is tried. This is a test which it is easy to apply, and one which decides the case." 
3. "The design and tendency of his religion is to excite men to do much good, and to call forth all their strength, and time, and talents in the work for which the Saviour laid down his life."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "In the perfect unity of will between the Son and the disciple, which results in the disciple's obtaining whatever he asks, the Father is glorified." 
2. "The design of this glorification is that you may bear much fruit." 
3. "Christian discipleship implies progress and growth."

### John Calvin:
1. "We ought not to doubt that God will listen to the prayers of his people, when they desire to be rendered fruitful; for this contributes very greatly to his glory."
2. "There is nothing which we ought to value more highly than that the name of God may be glorified by us."
3. "He declares that he has no one in his flock who does not bear fruit to the glory of God."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The purpose is union, communion, fellowship; and out of these flows an ever-increasing bearing of fruit ('much fruit'), and an ever-growing conformity of the believer with his Lord, alike in privilege and in life."
2. "Herein was my Father glorified belongs, therefore, to the previous verse, to that abiding in Jesus, and that asking and receiving in Him, which expressed the purpose of the Father."
3. "At the point we have reached this is supposed to be accomplished, and as a consequence of such abiding fellowship with the Father and the Son comes the growing fruitfulness, the deepening discipleship, of those who are true branches of the fruitful vine."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "In the vital union and the much fruit bearing."
2. "Keep on bearing much fruit."
3. "Become my disciples in the fullest sense of rich fruit-bearing."

### John Gill:

3. "Their fruitfulness...made them appear to be [disciples of Christ], or made them honourable ones...as by continuing in his word, abiding by his Gospel they appeared to be 'disciples indeed', really and truly such."

### John Wesley:
