# John 16:24 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Prayer in the name of Jesus is not a mere formula, but a profound understanding of our relationship with Him as the Mediator and the basis upon which we approach the Father. It is a recognition of our dependence upon Him, an alignment of our requests with His will, and a submission to His authority. Through this approach, we express our trust in His character and promises, knowing that He is able and willing to answer our prayers according to His perfect wisdom and love.

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "Prayer in the name of Christ, and prayer to Christ, presuppose His glorification." 

2. "Ask when I am gone, 'in My name.'" 

3. "Prayer in the name of Christ is not a magical formula, but rather a recognition of our dependence on Him and a submission to His authority."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "This great truth that we must approach God in the name of the Mediator was reserved for the last that the Saviour was to communicate to them."
2. "Then they were prepared in some degree to understand it; and then, amid trials, and wants, and a sense of their weakness and unworthiness, they would see its preciousness, and rejoice in the privilege of being thus permitted to draw near to God."
3. "Now they had the assurance that they might approach God in his name; and, amid all their trials, they, as well as all Christians since, might draw near to God, knowing that he would hear and answer their prayers."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Ask [αιτειτε]. The present imperative, implying continuous asking. Be asking."
2. "May be full [η πεπληρωμενη]. Very literally, may be having been fulfilled."
3. "Rev., more correctly, fulfilled. Compare John 14:11."

### John Calvin:
1. "It was, therefore, one of the principles of faith, that prayers offered to God, when there was no Mediator, were rash and useless."
2. "We need not wonder, therefore, if he was not acknowledged to be the Mediator as he is, now that he appears for us in heaven before the Father, reconciling Him to us by his sacrifice."
3. "This teaches us that it is a wicked profanation of the name of God, when any one, leaving Christ out of view, ventures to present himself before the judgment-seat of God."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "After the resurrection of Jesus, they are to use his name in prayer to the Father, seeking parity and worship like the Father." 
2. "The purpose of using Christ's name in prayer is for the fulfillment of joy, emphasizing its abiding permanence." 
3. "The disciples had not previously used Christ's name in prayer, but now they are instructed to do so, expressing their desire for unity with the Father."

### John Gill:
1. "Not that they had never prayed as yet; for they had desired him to teach them to pray, which he did: they had prayed to him particularly for an increase of faith, and for many other things."
2. "They had made no mention of his blood, righteousness, and sacrifice, nor any use of his mediation; things they had not as yet such clear knowledge of."
3. "Ask, and ye shall receive; that is, in my name, and whatever ye ask for, ye shall have it, to fit you for your work, to carry you through it, and to give you success in it."

### John Wesley:
1. "Asking in Jesus' name is not simply tacking on the phrase 'in Jesus' name' at the end of our prayers. It is a recognition that we are approaching the Father on the basis of our relationship with Jesus, and that our requests are aligned with his will and purposes."

2. "By asking in Jesus' name, we are acknowledging our dependence on him and his authority. It is a humble recognition that we cannot accomplish anything apart from him and that our prayers are ultimately for the glory of God."

3. "Asking in Jesus' name also implies a deep trust in his character and promises. It is a declaration of faith that he is able and willing to answer our prayers according to his perfect wisdom and love."
