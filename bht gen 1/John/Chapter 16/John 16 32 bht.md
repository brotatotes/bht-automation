# John 16:32 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, Jesus speaks to his disciples, telling them of an impending event that will lead to their scattering and his apparent abandonment. Commentators explain that Jesus is assuring his disciples that even though they will desert him, he will not lose any of his dignity or divine power. This moment marks the fulfillment of prophecy and the presence of God with Jesus, even in his darkest hour. The disciples are urged to leave Jesus to his own home, symbolizing the journey they must take while Jesus faces his impending crucifixion alone.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "You shall see me die, and suppose that my work is defeated, and return to your own dwellings." 
2. "Leave me alone - Leave me to die without human sympathy or compassion."
3. "Without his favor then, death will be full of horrors, though we be surrounded by weeping relatives, and by all the honor, and splendor, and wealth which the world can bestow."

### John Calvin:
1. "When Christ is forsaken by men, he loses nothing of his dignity."
2. "If it happens that he is forsaken by the whole world, still he is in no degree impaired, because he is God."
3. "God will be on his side, so that he will have no need to borrow anything from men."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "The long-looked-for hour is so close that it has virtually begun."
2. "Ye shall be scattered, used in John 10:12 of sheep scampering from the wolf."
3. "To his own home" as in John 1:11; John 19:27.

### John Gill:
1. "The time is at hand, yea, it may, in a sense, be said to be already come." 
2. "And so was fulfilled the prophecy in Zechariah 13:7." 
3. "He was not alone at this time; and his meaning is, that he should not be alone then when they should be scattered from him: because the Father is with me."
