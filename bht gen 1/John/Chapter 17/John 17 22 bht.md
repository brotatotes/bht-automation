# John 17:22 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The given glory mentioned in this verse encompasses the present indwelling Spirit of Christ for believers, emphasizing acceptance, holiness, and grace. It aims to unite believers into a unity resembling the unity between God the Father and God the Son. This glory restores the image of God in believers, engraving the likeness of the glory of the Father. It is not found in worldly honor or station but in self-sacrificing love and the ability to love and sacrifice for the good of others. Christians, filled with the fullness of grace and truth, reflect the glory of the only begotten Son.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The glory which thou gavest—hast given." - This quote highlights the idea that the glory given to believers is not just a future heavenly state, but the present indwelling Spirit of Christ. It emphasizes the acceptance, holiness, and grace that comes with this glory.

2. "I have given them, that they may be one, even as we are one." - This quote reveals that the purpose of this given glory is to bring believers into a unity similar to the unity between God the Father and God the Son. It emphasizes the importance of unity among believers.

3. "The glory, therefore, of the indwelling Spirit of Christ; the glory of an accepted state, of a holy character, of every grace." - This quote summarizes the nature of the given glory, highlighting its association with the indwelling Spirit of Christ and its manifestation in an accepted state, holy character, and every grace. It emphasizes the comprehensive and transformative nature of this glory.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The honor which thou hast conferred on me by admitting me to union with thee, the same honor I have conferred on them by admitting them to like union with me."
2. "Not in nature, or in the mode of existence - for this was not the subject of discourse, and would be impossible - but in feeling, in principle, in purpose."
3. "Always, in the creation, preservation, and redemption of the world, the Father and the Son have sought the same object, and this is to be the model on which Christians should act."

### John Calvin:
1. "Our happiness lies in having the image of God restored and formed anew in us, which was defaced by sin."
2. "Christ is not only the lively image of God, in so far as he is the eternal Word of God, but even on his human nature, which he has in common with us, the likeness of the glory of the Father has been engraved."
3. "No one ought to be reckoned among the disciples of Christ, unless we perceive the glory of God impressed on him, as with a seal, by the likeness of Christ."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Jesus had prayed that all believers might be one as He and the Father were one."
2. "The ‘glory’ referred to is that of self-sacrificing love, brought out from amidst the taunts with which men met it when displayed in Jesus, and owned by the Father as the only true glory."
3. "Not worldly honour or station, the favour of kings, the patronage of statesmen, or the wealth of nations, was their glory; but the gift to love, and to sacrifice themselves for the world’s good."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "And I the glory" - emphasizes the glory of the Incarnate Word, not the Eternal Word mentioned in John 17:24.
2. "The glory of the Incarnate Word" - refers to the divine glory manifested in Jesus Christ.
3. "Quanta majestas Christianorum!" - Bengel's exclamation highlights the great majesty of Christians.

### John Gill:
1. "Not the glory of his deity; this is the same with his Father, what he has in right of nature, and not by gift; nor can it be communicated to creatures; this would be to make them one in the Godhead, as the three are one, which is not the design of the expression in the close of the verse."
2. "Rather the Gospel is meant, which is glorious in its author, matter and subject, in its doctrines, in the blessing: grace it reveals, and promises it contains, and in the efficacy and usefulness of it to the souls of men."
3. "This is one considerable branch of the glory of Christ, as Mediator, to be full of grace and truth; this was given him by the Father, and is what he communicates to his; even the Spirit, and all sorts of grace, and every supply of it; and which greatly contributes to the union of the saints among themselves."

### John Wesley:
1. "The glory of the only begotten shines in all the sons of God." 
2. "How great is the majesty of Christians." 
3. "The glory which thou hast given me, I have given them."
