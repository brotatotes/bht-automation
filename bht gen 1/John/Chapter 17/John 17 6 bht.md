# John 17:6 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, Jesus prays to the Father, acknowledging His name and the believers He has been given. Commentators emphasize the Father's role as the source of divine authority and the intimate relationship between Jesus and the disciples. They highlight how Jesus revealed God's character and will to them, prepared them to receive Him, and prayed for their preservation and fidelity. The verse alludes to the disciples' election, their separation from the world, and their faithful adherence to the message of the Father.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Thy Name of FATHER, which was so constantly on the lips of our Lord; and which derived its living meaning and power from His teaching."
2. "The Father gave them to Christ, by leading them to Christ."
3. "Thine (σοί, from σός) they were Israelites Thy people, before: not only outwardly, but Israelites indeed, and thus prepared to receive Christ."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "From praying for Himself He now comes to pray for His disciples." 
2. "I have manifested—I manifested. thy name—His whole character towards mankind." 
3. "To the men thou gavest me out of the world."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Jesus had made known his character, his law, his will, his plan of mercy - or, in other words, he had revealed God to them."
2. "The word 'name' is often used to designate the person."
3. "God has a right to the services of all; and he has a right to appoint us to any labor, however humble, or hazardous, or wearisome, where we may promote his glory and honor his name."

### John Calvin:
1. "The first argument which he employs on their behalf is, that they have embraced the doctrine which makes men actually children of God."
2. "Let us therefore infer that not all to whom the doctrine is exhibited are truly and efficaciously taught, but only those whose minds are enlightened."
3. "The certainty of that election by free grace lies in this, that he commits to the guardianship of his son all whom he has elected, that they may not perish."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "His work was over: theirs was to begin; and it involved a struggle and needed strength, similar to His own."
2. "In tenderest pity and love, therefore, He now prays for them, that they may be preserved as He has been."
3. "They had been given to the Son by the Father 'out of the world;' that is, they were no longer in the world as the element of their existence."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Jesus regards the apostles as the Father's gift to him."
2. "Jesus claims loyalty and fidelity in these men with the one exception of Judas."
3. "He does not claim perfection for them, but they have at least held on to the message of the Father in spite of doubt and wavering."

### John Gill:
1. "The name of twelve letters, Galatinus pretends, is Father, Son, and Holy Ghost, the words for which in the Hebrew language consist of twelve letters."
2. "It is none of these names, nor any other scriptural ones, that are here meant; but either God himself, or the perfections of his nature, or his will of command, or rather his Gospel."
3. "The persons given were not the Father's merely by creation; for so others are his also; nor would they be peculiarly his, for they are the son's likewise in this sense; but they are his by electing grace."

### John Wesley:
1. "I have manifested thy name — All thy attributes; and in particular thy paternal relation to believers."
2. "They were thine — By creation, and by descent from Abraham."
3. "And thou hast given them me — By giving them faith in what I have spoken."
