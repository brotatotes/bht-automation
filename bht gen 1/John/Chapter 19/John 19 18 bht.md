### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The placement of the two criminals on either side of Jesus is significant. It symbolizes the choice that each person has to make - to either accept or reject Jesus as the Savior. It also highlights the contrast between the two criminals, one who mocks Jesus and the other who recognizes his innocence and asks for his mercy." 

2. "The fact that the two criminals were crucified with Jesus emphasizes the depth of his suffering and humiliation. It shows that Jesus willingly took on the punishment that should have been ours, even to the point of being associated with criminals. This demonstrates his love and sacrifice for humanity."

3. "The different descriptions of the two criminals in the Gospel accounts highlight the diversity of responses to Jesus. While one criminal remains hardened in his heart and mocks Jesus, the other recognizes his own guilt and acknowledges Jesus as the righteous one. This reminds us that even in the darkest moments, there is still the opportunity for repentance and redemption."
