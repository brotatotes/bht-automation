# John 2:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this Bible verse, Jesus enters the temple and openly proclaims His Messiahship. He warns against the profaning of His Father's house with merchandise, emphasizing the importance of worship and spiritual devotion over material gain. Jesus not only takes action to restore the honor and holiness of the temple, but also reveals His divine authority and Messianic claim. This act serves as a fulfillment of prophecy and invites believers to consider the sacredness of God's dwelling place.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Only, when but a youth, with no authority, He was simply 'a SON IN His own house'; now He was 'a SON OVER His own house' (Hebrews 3:6), the proper Representative, and in flesh 'the Heir,' of his Father's rights."
2. "There was nothing wrong in the merchandise; but to bring it, for their own and others' convenience, into that most sacred place, was a high-handed profanation which the eye of Jesus could not endure."
3. "The same consciousness of intrinsic relation to the temple—as the seat of His Father's most august worship, and so the symbol of all that is due to Him on earth—dictating both speeches."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The people had made God's house their own."
2. "Merchandise [εμποριου]. Only here in the New Testament."
3. "The Synoptists say a den of robbers."

### John Calvin:
1. "At present, he merely warns them not to profane the Temple of God by applying it to improper uses."
2. "Why, then, does he drive the buyers and sellers out of the Temple? It is that he may bring back to its original purity the worship of God, which had been corrupted by the wickedness of men, and in this way may restore and maintain the holiness of the Temple."
3. "We ought always, therefore, to keep before our eyes the majesty of God, which dwells in the Church, that it may not be defiled by any pollutions; and the only way in which its holiness can remain unimpaired is, that nothing shall be admitted into it that is at variance with the word of God."
