# John 2:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In the beginning of his ministry, Jesus distinguishes himself from the asceticism of John. He invites his disciples, forming a close circle of trust and authority. This invitation is an honor and signifies the special relationship and esteem Jesus had for his disciples, as they were called to continue his work. Jesus attends a wedding, showing his humility and honoring the institution of marriage. He also sanctifies human society, allowing for joy and celebration, drawing his disciples through his affable and courteous nature.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Our Lord at once opens His ministry with the character which He gives of himself as distinguished from the asceticism of John."
2. "He also...gives us his own testimony against the tendency...of giving up those things and occasions to the world and the devil, which we have not Christian boldness to mingle in and purify."
3. "The especial honour conferred upon marriage by the Lord should also be noticed. 'He here adorned and beautified it with his presence, and first miracle that He wrought.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The miracle performed here was doubtless to convince them more fully that he was the Christ."
2. "His disciples - Those that he had made when in Judea."
3. "They were not yet called to be apostles, but they believed that he was the Messiah."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "He was called by God, chosen for a specific purpose and mission. This emphasizes the divine calling and appointment of Jesus, highlighting his unique role in God's plan for salvation." 

2. "The disciples were not just followers or students, but they were personally invited by Jesus to be in his close circle. This signifies the special relationship and intimacy Jesus had with his disciples, as well as the trust and authority he bestowed upon them." 

3. "The act of being called by Jesus was an honor and privilege. It speaks to the recognition and esteem Jesus had for his disciples, valuing them as individuals and entrusting them with the task of spreading his message and continuing his work."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The form of the sentence shows that our chief attention is to be fixed on Jesus, not on the disciples." 
2. "They were invited as His disciples." 
3. "Those who came were probably the five or six mentioned in chap. 1, viz. Andrew, Simon Peter, Philip, Nathanael, and John himself (and probably James)."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Jesus was also invited, as well as his mother, possibly at her suggestion." 
2. "His disciples were included in the invitation, forming the nucleus of the great host of 'learners' who will follow Jesus as Teacher, Lord, and Savior." 
3. "The term 'disciples' is sometimes restricted to the twelve apostles, but often has a wider circle in view."

### John Gill:
1. "To attend a wedding, was reckoned, with the Jews, an act of beneficence and kindness."
2. "Our Lord, being at this wedding, was acting like himself, and his general character, of being free, affable, and courteous."
3. "It shows his humility in not disdaining his poor relations, but giving them his company at such a time; as also it was bearing a testimony to the institution of marriage, as honourable."

### John Wesley:
1. "Christ does not take away human society, but sanctifies it." 
2. "Water might have quenched thirst; yet our Lord allows wine; especially at a festival solemnity." 
3. "Such was his facility in drawing his disciples at first, who were afterward to go through rougher ways."
