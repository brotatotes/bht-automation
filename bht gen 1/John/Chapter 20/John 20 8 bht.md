## BHT:
In the moments following the discovery of the empty tomb, the disciple John experienced a profound realization and conviction. As he observed that the body of Jesus was not there, he embraced the truth of the resurrection. This belief was not merely based on his own observations, but also on the testimony of Mary Magdalene, whom he trusted. In this pivotal moment, doubt transformed into faith, solidifying John's understanding of the miraculous resurrection of Jesus.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The repetition of this, in connection with his not having gone in till after Peter, seems to show that at the moment of penning these words the advantage which each of these loving disciples had of the other was present to his mind."
2. "Probably he means, though he does not say, that he believed in his Lord's resurrection more immediately and certainly than Peter."
3. "The order of their going in, and the impression produced on each, were according to the order of their faith."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "He believed (at length) that Jesus was risen; for up to this time [ουδεπω] he, with his fellow-disciple (plural, hdeisan) knew not, etc." 

2. "The singular number, he believed, as Meyer profoundly remarks, 'only satisfies the never-to-be-forgotten personal experience of that moment, though it does not exclude the contemporaneous faith of Peter also.'"

### John Calvin:
1. "It is a poor exposition which some give of these words, that John believed what he had heard Mary say, namely, that Christ’s body had been carried away."
2. "There is no passage in which the word believe bears this meaning, especially when it is used simply and without any addition."
3. "Nor is this inconsistent with the fact, that Peter and John return home, while they are still in doubt and perplexity."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "As John stood gazing on the signs which bore their silent witness that the body of Jesus had not been taken away by violent hands, the truth revealed itself to him, that Jesus had of Himself left the tomb."
2. "To receive the truth of the Resurrection was to be led to a deeper and more real faith in Jesus Himself."
3. "The uncertainties, doubts, and difficulties occasioned by the events of the days just passed disappeared from John’s mind. He ‘believed’ in Jesus as being what He truly was, the Son of God, the Saviour of man."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "John saw into the meaning of it all better than Peter."
2. "John was the first to believe that Jesus was risen from the tomb even before he saw him."
3. "John was evidently proud to be able to record this great moment when he believed without seeing in contrast to Thomas."

### John Gill:
1. "John, being animated by the example of Peter, went down into the sepulchre likewise."
2. "The first was last, and the last first."
3. "He saw the linen clothes lie in one place, and the napkin folded up in order, lying by itself in another."

These quotes highlight John's actions, the reversal of roles between Peter and John, and the specific details of what John saw in the sepulchre.

### John Wesley:
1. "He saw...and believed" - This phrase highlights the moment of realization and conviction in the disciple's heart. It signifies a shift from doubt to faith, as he comprehends the truth of Jesus' resurrection.

2. "The body was not there" - This statement emphasizes the physical absence of Jesus' body, which serves as evidence for the disciple's belief in the resurrection. It underscores the miraculous nature of Jesus' resurrection and challenges any alternative explanations.

3. "Taken it away as Mary said" - This quote refers to Mary Magdalene's earlier claim that the body of Jesus had been taken. It shows the disciple's willingness to consider and accept the testimony of others, further strengthening his belief in the resurrection. It also highlights the importance of communal witness in affirming the truth of Jesus' resurrection.
