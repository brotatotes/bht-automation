This passage, commonly believed to be written by the disciple John, is a powerful testimony filled with truth and reliability. His firsthand account of the events carries significant weight and credibility. The author's identification as an eyewitness emphasizes the authenticity and value of what is being conveyed. Furthermore, the endorsement and authentication of a group of disciples add to the trustworthiness and recognition of this Gospel.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "This proves that the beloved disciple was John."
2. "It was so decidedly his character that he always declared the truth, that it had become known and was unquestioned."
3. "In this case, therefore, we have the testimony of a man whose character for nearly a century was that of a man of truth - so much so that it had become, in a manner, proverbial, and was put beyond a doubt."

### John Calvin:
1. "John now declares that it is himself; that greater weight may be attached to the statements of one who was an eye-witness." 

2. "Having hitherto mentioned himself in the third person, John...had fully known all that he relates." 

3. "John's declaration emphasizes the credibility and reliability of his account as an eyewitness."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The writer means more than that the things stated by him are true; he is uttering a Divine testimony to their inner reality and value."
2. "By his witnessing he claims to be more than a historian: he proclaims himself a prophet of God, commissioned to announce great verities to men."
3. "They could not fail to notice how different this Gospel was from its predecessors. It might seem to them that hesitation would be felt in receiving it, and they stamp it with their authenticating seal."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "The one just mentioned in verse John 21:20, 'the disciple whom Jesus loved.'"
2. "Here there is a definite statement that the Beloved Disciple wrote this book."
3. "The plural here seems intentional as the identification and endorsement of a group of disciples who know the author and wish to vouch for his identity and for the truthfulness of his witness."

### John Gill:
1. "The testimony of one that was an eye and ear-witness, as John was, of all that he testified and wrote, must be known, owned, and allowed by all to be true, firm, and unquestionable."
2. "The disciple John wrote these things; all that is contained in this book, as well as the particulars relating to this conversation of Christ with Peter."
3. "The attestation of the Ephesian church, or of the bishops of the Asiatic churches, who put John upon writing this Gospel; of which they give their judgment and testimony, as believing it to be a true and faithful narrative."

### John Wesley:
1. "This is the disciple who testifieth — Being still alive after he had wrote." 
2. "And we know that his testimony is true — The Church added these words to St. John's Gospel." 
3. "Tertius did those to St. Paul's Epistle to the Romans, Romans 16:22."
