# John 3:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This verse speaks of the unique authority of Jesus, for no one can understand heavenly things unless they have come down from heaven like He has. Jesus, who is both divine and human, has the knowledge of heavenly things and has the ability to reveal them to us. This verse also highlights the omnipresence of Jesus, as He is both in heaven and on earth at the same time. The communication of properties between Jesus' divine and human natures is demonstrated in His ability to know and teach about heavenly truths.

### Henry Alford:
1. "The Son of Man, the Lord Jesus, the Word made Flesh, was in, came down from, heaven, and was in heaven while here, and ascended up into heaven when He left this earth."
2. "He regards therefore throughout the passage, the great facts of redemption as accomplished, and makes announcements which could not be literally acted upon till they had been so accomplished."
3. "To explain such expressions as ἀναβαίνειν εἰς τ . οὐρ ., &c., as mere Hebrew metaphors is no more than saying that Hebrew metaphors were founded on deep insight into divine truth."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "No one has gone up but He that came down, even He who is at once both up and down." 
2. "The perfect knowledge of God is not obtained by any man's going up from earth to heaven to receive it—no man hath so ascended." 
3. "He whose proper habitation, in His essential and eternal nature, is heaven, hath, by taking human flesh, descended as the Son of man to disclose the Father."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "No man hath ascended into heavens...To speak of those things requires intimate acquaintance with them." 
2. "But he that came down...The Lord Jesus...He is represented as coming down, because, being equal with God, he took upon himself our nature."
3. "Which is in Heaven...This can be understood only as referring to the fact that he had two natures...his 'divine nature' was in heaven, and his 'human nature' on earth."

These quotes emphasize that no one is qualified to speak of heavenly things except for Jesus, who came down from heaven and has both divine and human nature. They highlight the uniqueness and authority of Jesus in revealing the truth about heavenly matters.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Jesus says that no one has been in heaven except the Son of man who came down out of heaven; because no man could be in heaven without having ascended thither."
2. "No man could be in heaven without having ascended thither."
3. "Many authorities omit [which is in heaven]."

### John Calvin:
1. "No mortal man can, by his own unaided powers, enter into heaven, but only he who goes thither under the guidance of the Son of God."
2. "Christ quickly proposes a remedy, when he adds, that what was denied to all others is granted to the Son of God."
3. "Christ, therefore, who is in heaven, hath clothed himself with our flesh, that, by stretching out his brotherly hand to us, he may raise us to heaven along with him."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "No one can tell the heavenly things unless he has been in heaven, and no one has been in heaven and come down to earth save myself."
2. "He came forth from the Father, and came into the world, that He might declare the Father and speak unto the world what He had heard from Him."
3. "The special thought is not the having gone up into heaven, but the having been in heaven. This was the qualification for revealing the truths which are here spoken of as heavenly things."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "The Incarnation of the Pre-existent Son of God who was in heaven before he came down and so knows what he is telling about 'the heavenly things.'"
2. "This high conception of Christ runs all through the Gospel and is often in Christ's own words as here."
3. "He is the one who has come down out of heaven."

### John Gill:
1. "No man hath ascended into heaven...that no man had, or could go up to heaven, to bring from thence the knowledge of divine and heavenly things." 
2. "He was the only teacher of heavenly things, as being the only person that had been in heaven, and in the bosom of the Father." 
3. "There never had been, nor was, nor could be, any mere man that could go up to heaven, and learn the mysteries of God, and of the kingdom of heaven, and return and instruct men in them."

### John Wesley:
1. "For no one... hath gone up to heaven, but he that came down from heaven." 
2. "Who is in heaven... he is omnipresent; else he could not be in heaven and on earth at once." 
3. "This is a plain instance of what is usually termed the communication of properties between the Divine and human nature."
