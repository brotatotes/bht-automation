# John 3:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This verse, often interpreted as a dialogue between Jesus and Nicodemus, explores the theme of spiritual rebirth and the limitations of human understanding. Commentators emphasize Nicodemus' initial skepticism and his struggle to comprehend the concept of being "born again." They highlight the divine nature of this transformation and caution against the hindrance of pride and preconceived notions that prevent true understanding. Ultimately, this verse invites us to embrace humility and acknowledge God's power in the workings of our salvation.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The new birth is not something that can be achieved through human effort or willpower, but it is a divine work that comes to pass in us."
2. "The phrase 'come to pass' emphasizes the supernatural nature of the new birth, highlighting that it is a transformation that is brought about by God Himself."
3. "This verse reminds us that our salvation is not dependent on our own abilities or actions, but on the grace and power of God working in us."

### John Calvin:
1. "Every thing that he hears appears monstrous, because he does not understand the manner of it; so that there is no greater obstacle to us than our own pride."
2. "We always wish to be wise beyond what is proper, and therefore we reject with diabolical pride every thing that is not explained to our reason."
3. "As if it were proper to limit the infinite power of God to our poor capacity."

These quotes highlight the obstacle of pride and our tendency to reject what we cannot fully comprehend. They remind us of the importance of humility and reverence in our pursuit of understanding God's works.
