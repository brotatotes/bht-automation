### Albert Barnes:
1. "It shows how ready sinners are to misunderstand the words of Christ, and to pervert the doctrines of religion."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The interrogative particle indicates that a negative answer is expected: Surely thou art not." 
2. "The su, thou, first in the sentence, is emphatic, and possibly with a shade of contempt." 
3. "The mention of the herds completes the picture of their nomadic progenitor."

### John Calvin:
1. "How faulty this comparison is, appears plainly enough from this consideration, that she compares the servant to the master, and a dead man to the living God."
2. "The more cautious ought we to be not to extol the persons of men so as to obscure the glory of God."
3. "It is, therefore, proper that we should honor men who are eminent in piety, or endued with other uncommon gifts; but it ought to be in such a manner that God may always remain eminent above all."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The well was his; he drank of it himself." 
2. "The Samaritan woman had sought this well partly on account of its connection with the fathers of her people." 
3. "The first part of her answer showed how limited the range of the woman’s thoughts still was: in the words of this verse we see her dawning conviction of the Stranger’s greatness, and the impression made upon her by His manner and His words."
