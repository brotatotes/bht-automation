In this Bible verse, the commentators discuss the significance of the disciples' mission and the role of God in their work. The commentators also highlight the disciples' past involvement in baptism, underscoring their previous experience and readiness for their mission. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Here, as often, our Lord speaks of the office and its work as accomplished, which is but beginning."
2. "By ἄλλοι here He cannot mean the O.T. Prophets, for then His own place would be altogether left out."
3. "The plural is I believe merely inserted as the correspondent word to ὑμεῖς in the explanation, as it was ἄλλος ἄλλος, in the proverb."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "I, the Lord of the whole harvest: 'sent you,' points to their past appointment to the apostleship, though it has reference only to their future discharge of it."
2. "Ye bestowed no labour—meaning that much of their future success would arise from the preparation already made for them."
3. "Referring to the Old Testament laborers, the Baptist, and by implication Himself, though He studiously keeps this in the background, that the line of distinction between Himself and all His servants might not be lost sight of."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The man who is crowned with eminent success has no cause of 'boasting' over others, any more than the man who reaps a field of grain should boast over the man who sowed it." 
2. "We should not be discouraged if we do not meet with immediate success. The man that sows is not disheartened because he does not see the harvest 'immediately' spring up."
3. "Every part of the work of the ministry and of teaching men is needful, and we should rejoice that we are permitted to bear any part, however humble, in bringing sinners to the knowledge of our Lord and Saviour Jesus Christ."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The I is emphatic." 
2. "The aorist tense points back to the mission of the disciples as involved in their original call." 
3. "Their labor showing its effects in the present case."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The disciples are the reapers of this harvest; their commission including, however, that of the disciples of Jesus throughout all time was to reap a harvest which had not been prepared by their own toil."
2. "The surprise and gladness with which they would shortly witness the faith of the men of Sychar was an emblem of what should repeat itself continually in the history of the Church."
3. "The words point to Jesus Himself. From beginning to end of the narrative His ‘word,’ first in the conversation with the woman, and then as spoken to the Samaritans, is the instrument by which the joyful result is gained."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "The disciples had done no sowing here in Sychar, only Jesus and the woman."
2. "Others (αλλο: Jesus, the Baptist, the prophets)."
3. "Have entered (εισεληλυθατε). Perfect active indicative of εισερχομα. Into their labour (εις τον κοπον αυτων). Into the fruit and blessed results of their toil (κοπος)."

### John Gill:
1. "I sent you to reap,.... To preach the Gospel, and gather in souls by your ministry." 
2. "They had nothing more to do, than to declare to those persons who were cultivated by the prophets, and were like to ground tilled and manured, that the Messiah was come, and the kingdom of heaven was at hand."
3. "To finish the work they had begun, and which was almost done to their hands."

### John Wesley:
1. "The Lord of the whole harvest" - This phrase emphasizes God's authority and control over the entire harvest, highlighting His sovereignty and power.
2. "I have sent you" - This statement implies that the disciples were not acting on their own accord, but were sent by God Himself. It underscores the divine commission and purpose behind their work.
3. "He had employed them already in baptizing" - This reference to John 4:2 highlights the disciples' previous involvement in baptizing, indicating their prior experience and preparation for the task at hand.
