# John 5:23 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The verse being described emphasizes the equal honor and divine authority of Jesus Christ, the Son, in relation to God the Father. It reveals that the purpose of Jesus' mission and the giving of all judgment to him is to bring honor to the Father through him. The commentators highlight the intimate relationship between the Father and the Son, stating that to dishonor the Son is to dishonor the Father. They emphasize the importance of rightly worshiping and acknowledging Jesus as equal to God, and the necessity of honoring both the Son and the Father as one.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The end of all is the honour of the Father in and by the Son." 
2. "He (the Son) is the Lord of life and the Judge of the world." 
3. "Whosoever does not honor the Son with equal honor to that of the Father does not honor God at all."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "As he who believes that Christ in the foregoing verses has given a true account of His relation to the Father must of necessity hold Him entitled to the same honor as the Father."
2. "It was the Father's express intention in making over all judgment to the Son, that men should thus honor Him."
3. "He who does not honor the Son, does not honor the Father in fact, whatever he may imagine, and will be held as not doing it by the Father Himself."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "We honor God when we obey him and worship him aright."
2. "We honor the Son when we regard him as possessed of wisdom, goodness, power, eternity, omniscience - equal with God."
3. "Those who withhold proper homage from Jesus Christ, who do not honor him even as they honor the Father, cannot be Christians."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Which sent Him." - This phrase emphasizes the divine authority behind Jesus' mission, highlighting the Father's role in sending Jesus into the world.
2. "A phrase peculiar to John." - This commentary points out that this phrase is unique to the Gospel of John, indicating the distinctiveness of John's theological perspective.
3. "Used only by the Lord, of the Father." - This quote highlights that Jesus exclusively uses this phrase to refer to the Father, underscoring the intimate relationship between Jesus and God the Father.

### John Calvin:
1. "For what else is the meaning of these words, that all men may honor the Son, but that the Father wishes to be acknowledged and worshipped in the Son?"
2. "Our duty, therefore, is to seek God the Father in Christ, to behold his power in Christ, and to worship him in Christ."
3. "We shall never, therefore, find the true God but in Christ, nor shall we ever worship Him aright but by kissing the Son."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "These words express the purpose of the Father in giving all judgment to the Son."
2. "It is true, indeed, that the ‘judgment’ of John 5:22 implies condemnation, and that, by consequence, this verse might seem to relate to foes only and not obedient subjects in the kingdom of God. But the ‘all’ is rightly introduced, for when judgment has compelled the honour of unwilling adoration, much more may it be expected that willing hearts will see the unity of the Father and the Son, and will honour the Son even as they honour the Father."
3. "But so truly one are the Father and the Son, that all who dishonour the Son dishonour the Father."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Jesus claims here the same right to worship from men that the Father has."
2. "Dishonouring Jesus is dishonouring the Father who sent him."
3. "There is small comfort here for those who praise Jesus as teacher and yet deny his claims to worship."

### John Gill:
1. "This is the end of all judgment, and the exercise of all authority, and power being committed to him; namely, that he might have the honour given him by men that is due unto him."
2. "And Christians, who are favoured with a clearer revelation still of the Father of Christ, are much wanting in giving him his due glory; but in common he is honoured, though in an imperfect manner."
3. "He that honoureth not the Son; that denies his divine sonship, or his proper deity; that detracts from the dignity of his person or office; that shows no regard to him in point of salvation, or of obedience: honoureth not the Father which hath sent him."

### John Wesley:
1. "This demonstrates the EQUALITY of the Son with the Father."
2. "If our Lord were God only by office or investiture, and not in the unity of the Divine essence, and in all respects equal in Godhead with the Father, he could not be honoured even as, that is, with the same honour that they honoured the Father."
3. "He that honoureth not the Son — With the same equal honour, greatly dishonoureth the Father that sent him."
