# John 5:31 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this passage, the speaker acknowledges that their testimony alone is not sufficient and requires confirmation from other sources. They concede that if their witness is solely based on their own words, it may be regarded as invalid or untrustworthy. The emphasis is placed on the need for external validation, recognizing the limitations of self-testimony and the importance of corroborating evidence to establish the credibility of their claims. This humble acknowledgment reveals a deeper truth about the value of humility and the need for objective verification in matters of testimony and belief.
