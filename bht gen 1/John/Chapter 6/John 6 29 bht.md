# John 6:29 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The verse being described emphasizes the importance of the work of God, which is defined by the act of believing in Jesus Christ. This belief is not passive, but an active duty that requires effort and determination on the part of the believer. It is the central work that encompasses all other works and is pleasing to God. Faith in Christ is highlighted as the essential foundation for salvation and obedience to God's will.

### Henry Alford:
1. "The truest way of working the work of God is to believe on Him whom He hath sent."
2. "This is a most important saying of our Lord, as containing the germ of that teaching afterwards so fully expanded in the writings of Paul."
3. "All eternal life in men proceeds from nothing else than faith in Christ."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The work of God lies at the threshold of all acceptable obedience."
2. "It is not only the prerequisite to obedience, but also the proper spring of it."
3. "It is emphatically 'the work of God', the work of works."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "This is the work of God" - Jesus emphasizes that there is a specific work that is acceptable to God, implying that it is not enough to simply sit back and wait for salvation to come. 

2. "There was a work to perform" - Jesus makes it clear that there is an active duty that sinners must fulfill in order to be saved. This implies that salvation requires effort and action on the part of the believer. 

3. "It was to believe on the Messiah" - The work that Jesus refers to is the act of believing in Him as the Messiah. This highlights the importance of faith in salvation and underscores the centrality of Christ in the process.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Believe. Faith is put as a moral act or work."
2. "The work of God is to believe. Faith includes all the works which God requires."
3. "Jesus' answer directs them to one work. Canon Westcott justly observes that 'this simple formula contains the complete solution of the relation of faith and works.'"

### John Calvin:
1. "Men toil to no purpose, when they endeavor to please God without faith, because, by running, as it were, out of the course, they do not advance towards the goal."
2. "Faith does not exclude either the love of our neighbor or any other good work, because it contains them all within itself."
3. "It is idle sophistry, under the pretext of this passage, to maintain that we are justified by works, if faith justifies, because it is likewise called a work."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The one work which God would have them do is believing in Him whom He sent."
2. "This faith leads to union with Him and participation of His Spirit, and thus includes in itself all works that are pleasing to God."
3. "The act of believing in Jesus, the soul’s casting itself on Him with perfect trust, is here spoken of as a work, as something which requires the exercise of man’s will and calls forth determination and effort."
