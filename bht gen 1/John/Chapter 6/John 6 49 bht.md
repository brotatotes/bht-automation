# John 6:49 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The verse being described talks about how the bread from heaven, the manna, could not save the people from death. It emphasizes the finality and consequences of their physical and spiritual death. The commentators highlight that this bread, given out of heaven, did not bestow eternal life like the new manna that Jesus offers. The verse also contrasts the disbelief and death of the fathers who ate the manna with the eternal life granted to believers who partake in the new bread.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "That bread from heaven had no power to keep off death, and that, death owing to unbelief."
2. "Death is regarded as being swallowed up in the glory of the resurrection, and the second death which was hidden in the former ἀπέθανον has over him who eats this Bread of Life, no power."
3. "He is brought, even here, into a resurrection state from sin and death."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Your fathers, ye say, ate manna in the wilderness; and ye say well, for so they did, but they are dead." 
2. "The Bread whereof I speak cometh down from heaven, which the manna never did." 
3. "Men, eating of it, may live forever."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "There was a perpetual interposition of God which showed that they were his chosen people."
2. "The bread which they ate could not save them from death."
3. "Though God interfered in their behalf, yet they died."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The aorist points, not to their present condition but to the historical fact; they died." 
2. "The use of the word 'dead' emphasizes the finality and permanence of their state."
3. "The mention of their death serves as a reminder of the consequences of sin and the need for redemption."

### John Calvin:
1. "Souls do not find anywhere else than in him that food by which they are fed to spiritual life."
2. "Christ here accommodates his discourse to the hearers, who, caring only about feeding the belly, looked for nothing higher in the manna."
3. "He invites them to eat, when he says that he has come, that any man may eat; for this mode of expression has the same meaning as if he said, that he is ready to give himself to all, provided that they are only willing to believe."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "No other bread has given life eternal."
2. "Even the manna, the bread given out of heaven, did not bestow life on their fathers."
3. "The fathers, who 'did not believe the Lord' and died, are contrasted with the believer who 'hath eternal life'."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "And they died (κα απεθανον). Physical death." 
2. "The manna did not prevent death." 
3. "But this new manna will prevent spiritual death."

### John Gill:
1. "It is observable, that Christ says, not 'our fathers', but 'your fathers'; for though Christ, as concerning the flesh, came of these fathers, yet in every sense they were rather theirs than his."
2. "Because regard may be had to such of them more especially who ate the manna as common food, and not as spiritual meat, as typical of the Messiah, as others did; and whom these, their offspring, did very much resemble."
3. "This food, though it supported them in life for a while, could not preserve them from a corporeal death, and still less from an eternal one: for some of them not only died the first, but the second death."
