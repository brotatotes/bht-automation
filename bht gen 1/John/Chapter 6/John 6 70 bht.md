# John 6:70 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, Jesus addresses his chosen disciples, highlighting the contrast between His selection of them and the imminent betrayal by one among them. Commentators emphasize that Jesus choosing the twelve does not prevent the possibility of one falling away. The mention of Judas as a "devil" emphasizes the malicious and treacherous nature of his actions. The verse cautions against overconfidence and highlights the importance of genuine repentance in the face of temptation and potential apostasy.

### Henry Alford:
1. "His selecting, and the Father’s giving and drawing, do not exclude final falling away."
2. "The solemn addition, τοὺς δώδεκα after ὑμᾶς, heightens the contrast to the opposite result which follows."
3. "Certainly, in the dark act here prophesied, Judas was under the immediate instigation of and yielded himself up to Satan."

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "Though I have chosen you but twelve, one even of these is a 'devil'." 

2. "The 'we' embraces not so wide a circle as in the simplicity of thine heart thou thinkest." 

3. "The temple, the tool of that wicked one."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Have not I - I, the Saviour, the Messiah, chosen you in mercy and in love, and therefore it will be a greater sin to betray me?"
2. "Chosen. Chosen to the apostolic office; conferred on you marks of special favor, and treason is therefore the greater sin."
3. "Is a devil - Has the spirit, the envy, the malice, and the treasonable designs of a devil."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The word is an adjective, meaning slanderous, but is almost invariably used in the New Testament as a noun, and with the definite article." 
2. "The article is wanting only in 1 Peter 5:8; Acts 13:10; Revelation 12:9; and perhaps Revelation 20:2."
3. "It is of the very essence of the devilish nature to oppose Christ. Compare Matthew 16:23."

### John Calvin:
1. "Since Christ replies to all, we infer from it that all spake by the mouth of Peter."
2. "It was a powerful instrument of Satan for shaking their faith, when they were reduced to so small a number, but the fall of Judas might take away all their courage."
3. "He used the word chosen, therefore, to denote those who were eminent and distinguished from the ordinary rank."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Alas! even in this small circle there is an element that the light attracts not but repels."
2. "Amongst the disciples He knew who would desert Him: in this inner circle He knew who would show himself a traitor ‘a devil.’"
3. "The traitor will do his work at the instigation of the Evil One, and animated by his spirit: his work will be the work of the devil: he himself in doing it will be the associate of Satan."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Jesus does not say that Judas was a devil when he chose him, but that he is one now."
2. "In John 13:2; John 13:27 John speaks of the devil entering Judas."
3. "How soon the plan to betray Jesus first entered the heart of Judas we do not know (John 12:4)."

### John Gill:
1. "Have not I chosen you twelve; not to grace and glory, to holiness and happiness; though this was true of eleven of them, but to be apostles." 
2. "One of you is a devil? or like to one, is a deceiver, a liar, and a murderer, as the devil is from the beginning; all which Judas was, and appeared to be, in the betraying of his master."
3. "And this our Lord said, partly that they might not too much presume upon their faith and love, and steady attachment, and be over confident of their standing; and partly, to prepare them for the apostasy of one from among them."

### John Wesley:
1. "Even ye have not all acted suitable to this knowledge." 
2. "But they might fall even from that election." 
3. "On this gracious warning, Judas ought to have repented; is a devil."
