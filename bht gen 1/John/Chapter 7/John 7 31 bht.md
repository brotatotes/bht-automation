### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The phrase 'surely he will not at all do' emphasizes the certainty that God will not act in a certain way."
2. "This phrase highlights the absolute trustworthiness of God's character and his faithfulness to his promises."
3. "The use of the negative particle 'not at all' intensifies the assurance that God will not act contrary to his nature and his word."

### John Calvin:
1. "And, therefore, though some may murmur, and others scorn, and others slander, and though many differences of opinion may arise, still the preaching of the Gospel will not be without effect; so that we must sow the seed, and wait with patience until, in process of time, the fruit appear."
2. "The word believe is here used inaccurately, for they depended more on miracles than they relied on doctrine, and were not convinced that Jesus was the Christ; but as they were prepared to listen to him, and showed themselves willing to receive instruction from him as their Teacher, such a preparation for faith is called faith."
3. "When the Holy Spirit bestows so honorable a designation on a small spark of good disposition, it ought to encourage us, so as not to doubt that faith, however small it may be, is acceptable to God."
