### Albert Barnes:
1. "If ye continue in my word - If you continue to obey my commandments and to receive my doctrines." 
2. "This is the true test of Christian character." 
3. "Jesus assures them that if their faith should abide the test, if it should produce obedience to his commandments and a truly life, it would be proof that their faith was genuine."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Believed on Him. Note the different phrase, distinguishing the Jews from the mixed company in ver. 30. Rev., rightly, believed Him." 
2. "If ye continue. The emphasis is on the ye, addressed to those whose faith was rudimentary; who believed Him, but did not yet believe on Him. Rev., abide." 
3. "In my word. Literally, in the word which is mine: peculiarly mine, characteristic of me. The expression is intentionally stronger than my word."

### John Calvin:
1. "It is not enough for anyone to have begun well, if their progress to the end do not correspond to it."
2. "He distinguishes his followers from hypocrites by this mark, that they who falsely boasted of faith give way as soon as they have entered into the course, or at least in the middle of it; but believers persevere constantly to the end."
3. "If we wish that Christ should reckon us to be his disciples, we must endeavor to persevere."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The former denotes a true faith in Jesus, such an acceptance of Him as includes a surrender of the heart, the ‘self,’ to Him; the latter, an acceptance of His words as true." 
2. "The impression produced by the last words spoken by Jesus appears to have been very great, bringing many to the position of full discipleship."
3. "They believed His word; if they abide in this word of His, clinging to it, continuing under its influence, the word will be to them a revelation of Jesus, and will assert its power."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "They believed him as to his claims to being the Messiah with their own interpretation, but they did not commit themselves to him." 
2. "If you abide in my word, your future loyalty to my teaching will prove the reality of your present profession." 
3. "Continuance in the word (teaching) proves the sincerity or insincerity of the profession. It is the acid test of life."

### John Gill:
1. "To continue in my word...is having cordially received it, to abide by it, and hold it fast, and not to be moved from it." 
2. "When men continue thus steadfast in it, and faithful to it, it is an evidence that it has come with power, and has a place in their hearts." 
3. "The genuine disciples of Christ continue in his Gospel, hold fast to him, the head, and remain with his people; which to do to the end, is an evidence of their being disciples indeed."
