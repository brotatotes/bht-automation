The people fail to recognize Jesus' divine nature and the power and authority he possesses through God.

### John Calvin:
1. "We know not whence he is." - The people's statement reflects their lack of understanding and recognition of Jesus' prophetic calling. They fail to acknowledge his divine authority and question his legitimacy as a messenger from God.

2. "They refer not to his country or the place of his birth." - The people's confusion is not about Jesus' earthly origins but rather about his spiritual calling and mission. They are ignorant of the fact that Jesus is not just a man from a particular place, but a chosen vessel of God's revelation.

3. "They have no knowledge of his calling, so as to receive him as having proceeded from God." - The people's rejection of Jesus stems from their ignorance of his divine commission. They are unable to recognize him as a true prophet sent by God because they lack knowledge and understanding of his calling.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "In holding by the law of Moses, then, they are safe and are assured that they are doing the will of God."
2. "If they do not know the origin of ‘this man,’ he can be worthy of no regard, certainly he cannot be from God!"
3. "We know that God hath spoken unto Moses; but as for this man we know not from whence he is."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "God hath spoken unto Moses" - This highlights the divine authority behind Moses' teachings and the significance of his role as a messenger of God (Exodus 33:11).
2. "They are proud to be disciples of Moses" - This suggests that the people held Moses in high regard and took pride in following his teachings.
3. "We do not know whence he is" - This statement reflects the ignorance and indifference of the Pharisees towards Jesus, indicating their lack of understanding and recognition of his true identity.
