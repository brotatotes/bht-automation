## BHT:
In this verse, the commentator emphasizes the power of persistence and shamelessness in prayer. They highlight the importance of not being deterred by apparent unanswered prayers, but instead continuing to knock and ask with unwavering determination. The commentators also draw attention to biblical examples of importunate prayer, showcasing the remarkable results that can be obtained through constant and earnest supplication. Overall, the verse encourages believers to persevere in prayer and not be discouraged by initial denials, seeking to nourish a deeper understanding of the value of steadfastness in seeking God's blessings.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Rather than be disturbed he would rise and give what was asked. This is to be applied to God in no other sense than that he often hears prayers and grants blessings even 'long after' they appear to be unanswered or withheld."

3. "Let us first ask aright; let us see that our minds are in a proper state; let us feel our need of the blessing; let us inquire whether God has 'promised such' a blessing, and 'then' let us persevere until God gives it."
