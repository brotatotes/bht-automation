### John Calvin:
1. "Here Christ opens up the source of the evil, and, as it were, applies the lancet to the ulcer."

1. "A further reproach for want of knowledge of personal duty, which involved great want of prudence."
2. "Even of yourselves. Either independently of their teachers, or independently of the plain signs of the times."
3. "What is right, namely repentance, as appears from the figure which follows. They ought not only to have discerned the coming of the Messiah, but thus prepared for it. Want of discernment in regard to God’s dealings (‘this time’) usually involves ignorance and neglect of personal duty."

### John Gill:
1. "From their own observation, as in discerning the signs of the weather; in a rational way, by the light of reason, and according to the dictates of their own consciences; by what they themselves saw and heard; by the signs and wonders which were done, they might have concluded, that now was the time of the Messiah's coming; and that he was come, and that Jesus of Nazareth was he."
2. "This was as easy, by observation, to be discerned, as the face of the sky was; even of themselves, without any hints or directions from others."
