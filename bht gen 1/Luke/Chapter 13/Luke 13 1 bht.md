# Luke 13:1 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, commentators explain that Jesus responds to the news of the Galileans' tragic fate by challenging the crowd's tendency to hastily judge others. While some suggest that the Galileans were followers of a rebellious figure, others emphasize that their slaughter was not an isolated event. Jesus uses this incident as a cautionary tale, urging his listeners to reflect on their own lives and the unpredictability of the future. Overall, the commentators highlight Jesus' redirection of the conversation from condemnation of Pilate to a deeper reflection on personal repentance and the transient nature of life.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The answer of Jesus is a reproof of their habit of hastily judging the character of others."
2. "The Galileans, in the time of Christ, were very wicked."
3. "It is likely that the Galileans excited a tumult in the temple, and that Pilate took occasion to come suddenly upon them, and show his opposition to them and Herod by slaying them."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "At that very season. Probably, but not necessarily, at that very time."
2. "Some that told him. Apparently they spoke, because exasperated by the intelligence, not in consequence of the preceding discourse."
3. "Whose blood Pilate mingled with their sacrifices. His soldiers probably fell on them and slew them while engaged in the temple-sacrifices."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "At that very season." Luke's frequent idiom, "at the season itself."
2. "These people had a piece of news for Jesus."
3. "Jesus comments on the incident, but not as the reporters had expected. Instead of denunciation of Pilate he turned it into a parable for their own conduct in the uncertainty of life."

### John Gill:
1. "These Galileans were very likely some of the followers of Judas Gaulonitis, or Judas of Galilee, who endeavored to draw off the Jews from the Roman government." 
2. "The phrase of mingling blood with blood is Jewish; it is said of one Trogianus the wicked that he slaughtered the Jews and mingled their blood with their blood." 
3. "The Jews have a notion that 'in the age in which the son of David comes, Galilee shall be destroyed.' Here was a great slaughter of the Galileans now, but there was a greater afterwards by the Romans."
