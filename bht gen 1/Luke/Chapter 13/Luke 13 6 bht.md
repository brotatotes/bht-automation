# Luke 13:6 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this parable, the fig tree symbolizes the people of Israel, who were chosen by God and planted in His vineyard, representing the land of promise. The owner of the vineyard, representing God, expects to find fruit on the fig tree, symbolizing the spiritual fruitfulness and obedience that He desires. This parable serves as a warning to the people of Israel, reminding them of the patience and opportunities they have been given by God to bear fruit. The parable highlights God's long-suffering and mercy, but also warns of the consequences if they continue to be unfruitful.

### John Calvin:
1. "The wicked flattery, by which hypocrites are hardened, and become more obstinate, arises from this cause, that they do not think of their sins till they are compelled."
2. "Thus they indulge themselves more freely, as if... they had made a covenant with death, and were in friendship with the grave."
3. "When the Lord does not immediately take vengeance on the reprobate, but delays to punish them, there are the best reasons for his forbearance."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The fig tree symbolizes the people of Israel, who were chosen by God and planted in his vineyard, representing the land of promise." 

2. "The owner of the vineyard represents God, who expects to find fruit on the fig tree, which symbolizes the spiritual fruitfulness and obedience that God desires from his people." 

3. "The parable serves as a warning to the people of Israel, reminding them that they have been given ample time and opportunity to bear fruit, but if they continue to be unfruitful, they will face judgment and the possibility of being cut down."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Planted (πεφυτευμενην). Perfect passive participle of φυτευω, to plant, an old verb, from φυτον, a plant, and that from φυω, to grow."
2. "But this participle with ειχεν (imperfect active of εχω) does not make a periphrastic past perfect like our English 'had planted.'"
3. "It means rather, he had a fig tree, one already planted in his vineyard."

### John Gill:
1. "Setting forth the patience of God towards the Jewish nation, their unfruitfulness, and the danger of their being destroyed, in case of non-amendment."
2. "By the 'certain man' may be meant, either God the Father, who is sometimes called an husbandman or rather the Lord Jesus Christ, who is truly man, as well as properly God."
3. "And by 'the fig tree planted' in it, may be principally meant the Scribes and Pharisees, and the generality of the Jewish people; who were plants, but not of Christ's Father's planting, and therefore to be cut down, or rooted up."

### John Wesley:
1. "Either we may understand God the Father by him that had the vineyard, and Christ by him that kept it: or Christ himself is he that hath it, and his ministers they that keep it." 

2. "The fig tree is a symbol of Israel, and the owner's expectation of finding fruit on it represents God's expectation of finding righteousness and obedience among His people." 

3. "The patience of the owner in giving the tree another chance to bear fruit reflects God's long-suffering and mercy towards His people, even when they fall short."
