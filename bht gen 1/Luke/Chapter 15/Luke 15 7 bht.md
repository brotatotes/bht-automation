# Luke 15:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this Bible verse, the Lord reveals the unseen world of glory. It speaks of the subjectively righteous who view themselves as such. It also highlights the shepherd who seeks and joyfully retrieves the bewildered sinner. These commentators emphasize the joy in heaven over the repentance of a sinner and the value placed on recovering the lost. They underscore the transformative power of repentance, God's mercy, and the pursuit of the lost. The parables illustrate the immense joy in heaven over the salvation of the lost and the significance God places on each individual.

### Henry Alford:
1. "In these words the Lord often introduces His revelations of the unseen world of glory."
2. "They are the subjectively righteous, and this saying respects their own view of themselves."
3. "This one is the stupid and bewildered sinner, erring and straying away in ignorance and self-will from his Shepherd, but sought by the Shepherd, and fetched back with joy."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "ninety-nine just . . . needing no repentance—not angels, whose place in these parables is very different from this; but those represented by the prodigal's well-behaved brother, who have 'served their Father' many years and not at any time transgressed His commandment." 
2. "such as have grown up from childhood in the fear of God and as the sheep of His pasture." 
3. "He leaves them to fill up the place of the other class, if they could."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It is a principle of human nature that the 'recovery' of an object in danger of being lost, affords much more intense joy than the quiet 'possession' of many that are safe."
2. "We rejoice most in our health when we recover from a dangerous disease; we rejoice over a child rescued from danger or disease more than over those who are in health or safety."
3. "They rejoice at his repentance because it recovers him back to the love of God, and because it will save him from eternal death."

These quotes highlight the concept of joy in heaven over the repentance of a sinner and the value placed on the recovery of those who were lost. They emphasize the intense joy experienced when something or someone is saved from danger or sin.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Repenteth" - This word emphasizes the theme of repentance, highlighting the transformative power of turning away from sin and returning to God. (Matthew 3:2)
2. "The Parables of the Lost Coin and of the Prodigal Son" - These parables, unique to Luke, convey the message of God's relentless pursuit of the lost and His boundless mercy and forgiveness. (Luke 15:8-32)
3. "The Lost Coin" - This parable illustrates the value God places on each individual, emphasizing that no one is insignificant or beyond His reach. (Luke 15:8-10)

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The variation in the amount deducted is without any special meaning." 
2. "Still we may find in it a proof of the steward’s prudence. He knew the men with whom he had to deal and acted accordingly."
3. "This parable condemns putting a premium on ignorance."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Repenting is what these sinners were doing, these lost sheep brought to the fold."
2. "The joy in heaven is in contrast with the grumbling Pharisees and scribes."
3. "Jesus does not mean to say that the Pharisees and the scribes do not need repentance or are perfect. He for the sake of argument accepts their claims about themselves and by their own words condemns them for their criticism of his efforts to save the lost sheep."

### John Gill:
1. "The reason why there is joy in heaven over such persons is, because, without such a repentance, they must perish; and by this they appear to be openly in a state of grace; and become proper subjects of the ordinances of Christ; and this repentance is unto life and salvation; or these are inseparably connected with it."
2. "The more joy over repenting sinners, than over [righteous] is, because the salvation of the one is before certain to them, and the other is unexpected."
3. "Learn that a self-righteous person is an impenitent one; that a repenting sinner is more regarded in heaven than a self-righteous man."

### John Wesley:
1. "Joy shall be...in heaven - First, in our blessed Lord himself, and then among the angels and spirits of just men."
2. "Over one sinner...thoroughly changed in heart and life; more than over ninety and nine just persons."
3. "The sum is, as a father peculiarly rejoices when an extravagant child...so do the angels in heaven peculiarly rejoice in the conversion of the most abandoned sinners."
