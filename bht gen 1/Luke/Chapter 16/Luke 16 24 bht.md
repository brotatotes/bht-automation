# Luke 16:24 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, a rich man, tormented in the afterlife, cries out to Abraham for mercy. Though he once enjoyed privilege, he now endures anguish with no relief, longing even for a drop of water to quench his agony. The scene depicts the consequences of hardened sin and serves as a cautionary tale, urging readers to seek righteousness and avoid the fate of the unrepentant.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Father Abraham—a well-founded, but unavailing, claim of natural descent."
2. "mercy on me—who never showed any."
3. "send Lazarus—the pining victim of his merciless neglect."
4. "that he may—take me hence? No; that he dares not to ask."
5. "dip . . . tongue—that is the least conceivable and the most momentary abatement of his torment; that is all. But even this he is told is unreasonable."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The Jews considered it a signal honor that Abraham was their 'father' - that is, that they were 'descendants' from him."
2. "The mercy of God will be at an end when the soul is lost."
3. "The rich will no longer despise such people; they would 'then' be glad of their friendship, and would beg for the slightest favor at their hands."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Compare the exquisite passage in Dante, where Messer Adamo, the false coiner, horribly mutilated, and in the lowest circle of Malebolge, says: 'I had, while living, much of what I wished; And now, alas! a drop of water crave.'"
2. "The rivulets that from the verdant hills Of Cassentin descend down into Arno, Making their channels to be soft and cold, Ever before me stand, and not in vain: For far more doth their image dry me up Than the disease which strips my face of flesh."
3. "Tormented is too strong. The word is used of the sorrow of Joseph and Mary when the child Jesus was missing; and of the grief of the Ephesian elders on parting with Paul. Rev., I am in anguish."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Even there the man does not forget that he is a Jew."
2. "It is possible, but not probable, that he still fancies he has some right to the services of one who was his inferior on earth."
3. "The souls of the impenitent after death suffer as terribly as though fire were tormenting their bodies."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "That he may dip (ινα βαψη). First aorist active subjunctive of βαπτω, common verb, to dip." 
2. "In water (υδατος). Genitive, the specifying case, water and not something else." 
3. "A small service that will be welcome."

### John Gill:
1. "And here the Jews, in their distress, are represented as applying to him, saying, have mercy on me, and send Lazarus which seems to have respect to the mercy promised to Abraham, the covenant made with him, and the oath swore unto him, to send the Messiah." 

2. "That he may dip the tip of his finger in water in allusion to the washings and purifications among the Jews, and the sprinkling of blood by the finger of the high priest; which were typical of cleansing, pardon, comfort, and refreshment, by the grace and blood of Christ."

3. "This man could not so much as get a drop of water to cool his tongue, not the least refreshment, nor mitigation of the anguish of his conscience, for the sins of his tongue."

### John Wesley:
1. "Here is one precedent in Scripture of praying to departed saints."
2. "Who is it that prays, and with what success?"
3. "Will any, who considers this, be fond of copying after him?"
