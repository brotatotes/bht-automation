Jesus was on a journey, passing through the important city of Jericho. The mention of Zacchaeus, a chief tax collector, highlights the significance of this encounter in a prosperous region. Despite Zacchaeus' wealth, his eagerness to climb a tree and see Jesus demonstrates his intense desire to connect with Him. This encounter with Jesus in the midst of His ministry reveals the divine inspiration behind Zacchaeus' preparation for faith.

### John Calvin:
1. "For it was an evidence of intense desire that he climbed up a tree; since rich men are, for the most part, haughty, and plume themselves on affected gravity."

3. "In this manner, before revealing himself to men, the Lord frequently communicates to them a secret desire, by which they are led to Him, while he is still concealed and unknown."
