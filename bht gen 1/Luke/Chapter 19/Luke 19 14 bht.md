# Luke 19:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this Bible verse, a nobleman, who represents the Lord Jesus, is appointed as the ruler over his citizens, symbolizing the Jews. However, these citizens, displaying deep opposition, hatred, and even sending ambassadors to protest against his authority, reject him as their king. The rejection of the nobleman by his own subjects parallels the rejection of Jesus by the Jews, reflecting the resistance towards accepting his divine rule. This parable reveals the importance of maintaining a positive relationship with those under one's authority and the consequences of rejecting the authority of Christ.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "His citizens—His proper subjects; meaning the Jews, who expressly repudiating our Lord's claims said, 'We have no king but Cæsar'." 
2. "In Christendom, these correspond to infidel rejecters of Christianity, as distinguished from professed Christians." 
3. "These citizens represent those who reject Jesus as their king and instead align themselves with worldly powers."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "His discontented subjects, fearing what would be the character of his reign, sent an embassy to remonstrate against his being appointed as the ruler."
2. "The Jews, knowing his character, sent an embassy of 50 men to Rome, to prevail on Augustus 'not' to confer the title on him, but they could not succeed."
3. "By this part of it Christ designed to denote that the Jews would reject 'him' - the Messiah, and would say that they did not desire him to reign over them."

### Philip Schaff:
