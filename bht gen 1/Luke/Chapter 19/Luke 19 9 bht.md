### Albert Barnes:
1. "Salvation 'commences' when people truly receive Christ and their sins are pardoned; it is 'completed' when the soul is sanctified and received up into heaven."
2. "For he has given 'evidence' that he is a new man, and is disposed to forsake his sins and receive the gospel."
3. "Now, by repentance, and by receiving the Christ whose day Abraham saw and was glad, he has shown himself to be worthy to be called his son."

### John Calvin:
1. "Today is salvation come to this house." - Christ's declaration affirms that Zaccheus' conversion is a true sign of divine adoption and salvation. It emphasizes that good works alone do not cause salvation, but rather faith in God's undeserved mercy and redemption of the Church.

2. "In Zaccheus faith is chiefly commended, on account of which his good works were acceptable to God." - The commendation of Zaccheus' faith highlights the importance of imitating Abraham's faith to be counted among the children of Abraham. Faith distinguishes genuine children of Abraham from strangers.

3. "There is nothing in this change at which any man ought to take offense, since he was sent by the Father to save those who were lost." - Christ's role as the Savior of the lost justifies Zaccheus' salvation and assures that his past life does not hinder his access to salvation. This emphasizes the inclusive nature of God's grace and redemption.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The reason salvation had come was, that he also, as well as the other Jews, who despised him as a sinner (Luke 19:7), was a son of Abraham, having now availed himself of his rights as a Jew in thus receiving the Lord."
2. "The promised restitution did not bring salvation."
3. "Nor was he a Gentile who became by repentance 'a son of Abraham;' had he been a Gentile, mention would have been made of it in the hostile murmurs (Luke 19:7)."

### John Gill:
1. "The Gospel publishes and proclaims [salvation]; the ministers of it show unto men the way of salvation, and direct them, and encourage to go to Christ for it."
2. "The Gospel came to [Zacchaeus] to purpose, and was effectual... it wrought effectually in him."
3. "[Zacchaeus] was not only made sensible that he stood in need of salvation, but this was brought near unto him, and set before him, and applied to him; he had not only hopes of it, but faith of interest in it."
