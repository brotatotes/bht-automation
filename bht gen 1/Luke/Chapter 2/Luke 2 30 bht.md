## BHT:
In this verse, commentators describe a profound encounter with Christ, emphasizing the significance of salvation and the person of Jesus. The commentators highlight how seeing Christ, whether as a child or in His glory, evokes a deep sense of faith rewarded by sight. ### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Many saw this child, nay, the full-grown 'man, Christ Jesus,' who never saw in Him 'God's Salvation.' This estimate of an object of sight, an unconscious, helpless babe, was pure faith."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Thy salvation" refers to the one who will bring about salvation for his people, the Savior himself.
2. The phrase implies that salvation is not something that can be achieved by human effort alone, but is dependent on the work of the Savior.
3. This commentary highlights the significance of the Savior in the process of salvation, emphasizing the need for divine intervention.

### John Calvin:
1. "By saving I understand the matter of salvation: for in Christ are hid all the parts of salvation and of a happy life."
2. "How much more abundant materials of lasting peace are now furnished to us, who have the opportunity of beholding our salvation altogether completed in Christ?"
3. "If such a sight does not bring peace to our minds, and make us go cheerfully to death, we are highly ungrateful to God, and hold the honor, which he has bestowed upon us, in little estimation."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He sees the world’s salvation, while beholding the form of a helpless child."
2. "The prominence given by Simeon to ‘salvation’ rather than to the person of the child, confirms the early date of the song."
3. "All peoples, both Jews and Gentiles, as the next verse shows."

### John Gill:
1. "He is called 'God's salvation' because he is a Saviour of his choosing, calling, and constituting; whom he promised under the Old Testament dispensation and in the fulness of time sent."
2. "They see him in the glory of his person, the fulness of his grace, the suitableness and excellency of his righteousness, the efficacy of his blood, and the perfection of his sacrifice; and as an able, willing, complete, and only Saviour."
3. "Such a sight of him, puts them out of conceit with themselves, and their own works of righteousness, as saviours; makes the creature, and all it has and does, look mean and empty; fills the soul with love to Christ, and a high esteem of him, and with joy unspeakable, and full of glory."

### John Wesley:
1. "Thy salvation" refers to the ultimate deliverance and redemption that comes through Christ, who is the Savior of all mankind.
2. The phrase "Thy salvation" emphasizes the personal and intimate nature of the relationship between the individual and their Savior.
3. Through Christ, believers can experience the fullness of salvation, which includes forgiveness of sins, reconciliation with God, and eternal life.
