### John Calvin:
1. "A light for the revelation of the Gentiles...that he might enlighten the Gentiles, who had been formerly in darkness."

### John Gill:
1. "Light", is one of the names of the Messiah in the Old Testament, as in Psalms 43:3 Daniel 2:22, which passages are by the Jews themselves interpreted of Christ.
2. The Gentiles enjoy this benefit of light by him; who were, and as this supposes they were, in darkness, as they had been some hundreds of years before the Messiah's coming.
3. The Gospel was first preached to them, even after the commission was enlarged to carry it among the Gentiles; and many of them were converted, and the first Gospel church was planted among them.

### John Wesley:
1. "The glory of thy people Israel" refers to the ultimate fulfillment of God's promises to the nation of Israel. It signifies the restoration and redemption of Israel as a chosen people, reflecting the glory of God's faithfulness and grace.

2. "After the Gentiles are enlightened" highlights the sequence of events in God's plan of salvation. It emphasizes that the Gentiles, who were once outside of God's covenant, will be brought into the fold through the light of the gospel.

3. "All Israel shall be saved" points to the future salvation of the entire nation of Israel. This signifies that God's redemptive work is not limited to a select few, but extends to all who belong to the people of Israel, demonstrating God's inclusive and merciful nature.
