### Albert Barnes:
1. "The tribe of Aser, or Asher, dwelt in the northern part of the land of Canaan."
2. "Why Anna was called a prophetess is not known."
3. "It might be because she had been the wife of a prophet, or because she was employed in celebrating the praises of God, or because she herself had foretold future events, being inspired."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "A prophetess [προφητις] . Only here and Revelation 2:20." 
2. "Asher. That tribe was celebrated in tradition for the beauty of its women, and their fitness to be wedded to high priests or kings." 
3. "Of great age [προβεβηκυια εν ημεραις πολλαις] . Lit., advanced in many days."

### John Calvin:
1. "Whatever belongs to God, however small it may be, ought to be preferred by us to the whole world."
2. "The Spirit of God, whose presence was not at all enjoyed by those rulers, dwelt in Simeon and Anna, those two persons are entitled to greater reverence than an immense multitude of those whose pride is swelled by nothing but empty titles."
3. "The Spirit of prophecy still shone in a very few, who served as tokens to attest the doctrine of the Law and the Jewish religion, till the coming of Christ."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "One Anna. ‘One’ is supplied to relieve the English construction."
2. "A prophetess, so called previous to this time."
3. "She was of a great age. From this point to ‘fourscore and four years’ (Luke 2:37), the description is parenthetical, referring to the particulars of her great age."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "The word προφητις occurs in the N.T. only here and Revelation 2:20. In old Greek writers it means a woman who interprets oracles." 

2. "The long parenthesis into verse Luke 2:37 tells of her great age. Montefiore makes it 106 as she was 15 when married, married 7 years, a widow 84."

3. "One Anna a prophetess (Hαννα προφητις)."

### John Gill:
1. "One that had the grace of God herself, and was a publisher of the glad tidings of grace and redemption by Christ, to others."
2. "This woman was the daughter of Phanuel... and now beheld his face in the flesh, who is God over all, blessed for ever."
3. "This tribe had its seat in Galilee; so that though the Jews denied that any prophet came from thence, yet it seems a prophetess did."
