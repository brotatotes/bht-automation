### Henry Alford:
1. "Women, according to the maxims of the school of Hillel, were bound to go up once in the year to the Passover."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The mention of 'his parents' highlights the significance of the occasion and the importance of family in Jewish culture."
2. "The fact that women were not required to be present at this particular event emphasizes the unique role of Mary as the mother of Jesus."
3. "This detail also underscores the obedience and devotion of Mary and Joseph, who faithfully followed the customs and traditions of their faith."

### John Calvin:
1. "It is mentioned in commendation of the piety of Mary and Joseph, that they gave diligent attendance to the outward worship of God."
2. "The law enjoins the males 'only to appear before the Lord,' (Exodus 23:17.) This arrangement does not entirely exclude females, but spares them by an exercise of kindness."
3. "The worship of the temple was, no doubt, infected with many corruptions, the priesthood was sold for money, and doctrine was involved in many errors. Yet, as legal ceremonies were still in force, and the outward rite of sacrifice was observed as it is laid down in the law, believers were bound to perform such exercises in testimony of their faith."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The male Israelites were required to appear at the three yearly feasts." 
2. "Women, according to the teachings of a prominent Rabbi (Hillel), were bound to attend the Passover feast." 
3. "Mary probably went from pious motives, rather than Rabbinical rules."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Every male was originally expected to appear at the passover, pentecost, and tabernacles." 
2. "The Dispersion rendered that impossible." 
3. "Mary went with Joseph as a pious habit, though not required by law to go."

### John Gill:
1. "Joseph was obliged to go three times a year, as were all the males in Israel, at the feasts of the passover, pentecost, and tabernacles." 
2. "The passover of women is voluntary, or in their own power; they might go up to the feast, or not, as they pleased."
3. "This was a voluntary thing in Mary; which discovers her piety and religion, and her great regard to the ordinances and appointments of God."
