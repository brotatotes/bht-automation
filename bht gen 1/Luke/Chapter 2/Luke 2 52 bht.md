As Jesus grew, His soul opened to the wisdom and knowledge of both natural and spiritual things, gaining favor with God through grace and gifts bestowed upon Him. In His innocence, He developed into a life of complete holiness, fulfilling the law for others. ### Albert Barnes:
1. "In favour with God - That is, in proportion to his advance in wisdom." 
2. "This does not imply that he ever lacked the favor of God, but that God regarded him with favor in proportion as he showed an understanding and spirit like his own." 
3. "Happy are those children who imitate the example of Jesus - who are obedient to parents who increase in wisdom - who are sober, temperate, and industrious, and who thus increase in favor with God and people."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The word may be rendered age, which would be superfluous here."

3. "Compare Matthew 3:1-12; Mark 1:1-8."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "In favor (or, 'grace') with God and men. The favor with God found expression at His baptism, and that expression implies sinless perfection."
2. "The innocence of childhood, free in this case from all childish faults, developed into complete holiness of life, in the way of positive obedience."
3. "During this time of youthful obedience and subjection, was performed a large part of that work which the second Adam must do as fulfilling the law for others."

### John Gill:
1. "As he grew in body, the faculties of his soul opened, and received gradually large measures of wisdom and knowledge, in things natural and spiritual."
2. "He increased in the wisdom and knowledge of his human soul, so he likewise increased in the stature of his body."
3. "He appeared by the grace that was in him, and the gifts bestowed on him, to be high in the love and favour of God; and had a large share in the esteem and affections of all good men."

### John Wesley:
1. "Jesus increased in wisdom - As to his human nature." 
2. "Though a man were pure, even as Christ was pure, still he would have room to increase in holiness." 
3. "In consequence thereof to increase in the favour, as well as in the love of God."
