# Luke 2:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this Bible verse, the shepherds are visited by an angel of the Lord, potentially Gabriel, who brings them the news of the birth of the Messiah. As the angel appears, a glorious and extraordinary light surrounds them, illuminating the presence of the angel. Overcome with fear, the shepherds are in awe of the majestic and holy nature of the divine manifestation, realizing their own sinfulness and recognizing the symbolism of the light as a representation of God's glory.

### Marvin Vincent:

### Philip Schaff:
1. "An angel, not 'the angel.'"
2. "Stood by. This indicates a sudden but actual appearance; not a vision."
3. "The glory of the Lord. The Shekinah, the brightness of God's presence, so often spoken of in the Old Testament."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Stood by them (επεστη αυτοις). Ingressive aorist active indicative. Stepped by their side." 
2. "The same word in Acts 12:7 of the angel there."
3. "It is a common old Greek word, εφιστημ."
4. "Were sore afraid (εφοβηθησαν φοβον μεγαν). First aorist passive indicative with cognate accusative (the passive sense gone), they feared a great fear."

### John Gill:
1. "And lo, the angel of the Lord,.... It may be Gabriel, who had brought the tidings of the conception of the Messiah to the virgin, and now the birth of him to the shepherds." 
2. "and the glory of the Lord shone round about them; or a very glorious and extraordinary light shone with surprising lustre and brightness all around them; by which light, they could discern the illustrious form of the angel that was over them."
3. "and they were sore afraid; at the sight of such a personage, and at such unusual light and glory about them: they were not used to such appearances, and were awed with the majesty of God, of which these were symbols, and were conscious to themselves of their own sinfulness and frailty."
