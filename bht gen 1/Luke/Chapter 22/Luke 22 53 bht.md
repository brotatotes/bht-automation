In this passage, Jesus acknowledges that the appointed hour has come, a significant moment in God's plan. He willingly submits himself to the power of darkness, symbolizing the spiritual battle against evil. Despite the temporary authority of Satan, God's sovereignty prevails, highlighting the ultimate victory of light over darkness. This verse underscores the divine timing and purpose behind Jesus' sacrifice, emphasizing the profound significance of this hour in God's redemptive plan.

### Henry Alford:
1. "Our Lord here distinguishes between the power exercised over Him by men, and that by the Evil One: but so as to make the ἐξουσία which rules over them to be that of darkness while His own assertion of this shews that all was by the determinate counsel and foreknowledge of God."
2. "In the word σκότος there is also an allusion to the time midnight."
3. "Compare with this declaration of the power of darkness over Him, the declaration, in ch. Luke 4:13, that the devil left Him ἄχρι καιροῦ."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Darkness was appropriate to such a deed, hence it was the hour which suited them." 
2. "‘Power of darkness’ therefore points to the kingdom of darkness. They were doing the work of the Evil One, and the power over Him was the power of darkness." 
3. "This clause suggests mysterious, and as yet unexplained, facts in regard to the relation of God’s purpose, man’s agency, and Satanic power."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The moral value of his atoning sacrifice on the Cross consists in the voluntariness of his death."
2. "He makes it clear that they have taken undue advantage of him in this hour of secret prayer."
3. "But 'the power of darkness,' had its turn. A better day will come."

### John Gill:
1. "The reason was, because his time was not come, and they had no licence or permission to hurt him, or any power given them against him from above."

3. "The power of the prince of darkness is here meant; that power which he usurped, and was now permitted him to exercise against Christ."
