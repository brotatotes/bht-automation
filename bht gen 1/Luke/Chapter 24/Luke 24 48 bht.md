### Henry Alford:
1. "From what follows, Acts 1:22, if these words are to be taken in their strict sense, they must have been spoken only to the Apostles."
2. "They may however have been more general, and said to all present."
3. "These words imply that the Apostles were to be witnesses of the resurrection, and were to be chosen from among those who had been with Jesus from the beginning."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The role of witnesses is crucial in the spread of the Gospel, as they provide firsthand accounts of the life, death, and resurrection of Jesus Christ." 

2. "By being witnesses, believers are called to testify to the truth and power of the Gospel, sharing their personal experiences and encounters with Jesus." 

3. "Just as the disciples were empowered by the Holy Spirit to be witnesses, Christians today are also given the same power to boldly proclaim the message of salvation."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "How solemn was their office - to 'testify' these things to the world, and, in the face of suffering and death, to go and proclaim them to all nations!"
2. "In like manner, 'all' Christians are witnesses for Christ. They are the 'evidences' of his mercy and his love."
3. "They should so live that others may be brought to see and love the Saviour."

### John Calvin:
1. "He does not yet commission them to preach the gospel, but only reminds them to what service he has appointed them, that they may prepare themselves for it in due time."
2. "Conscious of their recent departure from their Master, they must have been in a state of dejection and here, contrary to all expectation, Christ bestows on them incredible honor, enjoining them to publish to the whole world the message of eternal salvation."
3. "In this manner he not only restores them to their former condition, but by the extent of this new favor he utterly obliterates the recollection of their heinous crimes; but at the same time, as I have said, he stimulates them, that they may not be so slow and dilatory in reference to the faith of which they were appointed to be preachers."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The Apostles, but others may have been present Acts 1:22 hints that others saw Him ascend."
2. "As such they were to proclaim the facts (Luke 24:46), and the repentance and remission based upon them; and thus be the fulfillers of the prophecies summed up in Luke 24:47."
3. "The Gospel facts respecting Christ, centering in His Death and Resurrection, and including His Ascension. The fulfilment of prophecy and the commission to preach remission and repentance, are not excluded."

### John Gill:
1. "As they were witnesses of the truth of his humanity, having seen, and heard, and handled him, both before, and after his resurrection from the dead."
2. "Their business was also to testify the doctrine of repentance, and remission of sins, in Jerusalem, in all Judea, and Samaria, and to the uttermost parts of the earth."
3. "Their testimony was not based on mere hearsay or speculation, but on firsthand experiences of seeing Jesus, hearing His teachings, witnessing His miracles, and even touching His resurrected body."
