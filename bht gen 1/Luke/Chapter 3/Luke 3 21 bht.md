### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "When all the people were baptized—that He might not seem to be merely one of the crowd." 
2. "Thus, as He rode into Jerusalem upon an ass, 'whereon yet never man sat' and lay in a sepulchre 'wherein was never man yet laid,' so in His baptism He would be 'separate from sinners.'" 
3. "In His baptism, Jesus was making a statement about His uniqueness and separation from sinners."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The opening of the heavens signifies a divine revelation, a breaking through of the spiritual realm into the earthly realm. It symbolizes the communication between God and humanity, and the unveiling of divine truths." 

2. "The tearing of the heavens in Mark's account emphasizes the dramatic nature of this event, suggesting a forceful and powerful action. It highlights the significance of Jesus' baptism as a pivotal moment in his ministry, marking the beginning of his public proclamation and mission."

3. "The imagery of the heavens being opened also echoes the prophecy in Isaiah 64:1, where the prophet cries out for God to rend the heavens and come down. This connection reinforces the idea that Jesus' baptism is a fulfillment of Old Testament prophecies and a manifestation of God's presence and power."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Luke’s account of the baptism of Jesus is concise, but we have some new details."
2. "Luke alone mentions the latter [prayer]."
3. "The heaven was opened... This variety and agreement show that some actual external phenomenon occurred."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Luke does not say that all the people were baptized before Jesus came or were baptized at the same time. It is merely a general statement that Jesus was baptized in connexion with or at the time of the baptizing of the people as a whole." 
2. "For the narrative of the baptism see Mark 1:9; Matthew 3:13-16."
3. "The heaven was opened... while Jesus was praying though not necessarily in answer to his prayer."

### John Gill:
1. "Now when all the people were baptized,.... That came from several parts to John for this purpose, even as many as he judged to be proper subjects of that ordinance." 
2. "It came to pass that Jesus also being baptized of John in Jordan, he coming from Galilee thither on that account: and praying; after he was baptized, for the coming down of the Spirit upon him, as man, to anoint, and qualify him for his office he was now about to enter on publicly."
3. "And for success in it, and for a testimony from heaven, that he was the Son of God, and true Messiah: the heaven was opened."

### John Wesley:
1. "Jesus praying, the heaven was opened" - This signifies the divine connection and communication between Jesus and the Father. It highlights the importance of prayer in Jesus' life and ministry.

2. "The three voices from heaven...by which the Father bore witness to Christ" - These voices serve as a confirmation of Jesus' identity and mission. They demonstrate the Father's approval and endorsement of Jesus as the Son of God.

3. "The voices were pronounced either while he was praying, or quickly after it" - This emphasizes the significance of prayer as a catalyst for divine intervention and revelation. It suggests that prayer opens the door for God to speak and act in our lives.
