### John Calvin:
1. "The eyes of all who were in the synagogue God touched their hearts, I doubt not, with astonishment, which made them more attentive, and induced them to listen to Christ, while he was speaking."
2. "For they must have been withheld from opposing this discourse at the commencement, or breaking it off in the midst, when they were sufficiently disposed, as we shall see, to treat Christ with contempt."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "How much he read is not known; the usual lesson from the prophets is said to have comprised twenty-one verses."
2. "And sat down, to explain what He had read, that being the usual position of those making such expositions."
3. "The man brought up among them was about to address them for the first time; the report from other places had preceded this visit; the passage read was remarkable, and doubtless there was something in the appearance of our Lord, especially under these circumstances, which would command unusual attention."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "He closed the book... Rolled up the roll and gave it back to the attendant" - This action signifies the completion of reading and the return of the sacred text, showing reverence and respect for the Scriptures.

2. "Sat down... Took his seat there as a sign that he was going to speak" - Sitting down was a customary posture for teaching and public speaking in Jewish culture, indicating that Jesus was about to deliver a message of importance and authority.

3. "The eyes of all in the synagogue were gazing fixedly upon him" - Jesus captivated the attention of the people with his presence and demeanor, creating a sense of anticipation and awe. His gaze held them spellbound, even before he began to speak.

### John Gill:
1. "And he closed the book,.... 'Or rolled it up'; and so the high priest did, after he read in the book of the law, on the day of atonement, all that was necessary to be read." 
2. "And sat down; as was the manner of the Jews, when they taught, or preached." 
3. "The eyes of all them that were in the synagogue were fastened on him: they looked very wistly at him, and were very attentive to what he should say, upon such a passage of Scripture, which they knew referred to the Messiah."
