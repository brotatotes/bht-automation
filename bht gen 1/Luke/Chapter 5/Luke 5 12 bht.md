# Luke 5:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this Bible verse, a person afflicted with leprosy, a severe and sore disease, approaches Jesus, recognizing his authority and power to cleanse. The commentators highlight the accurate medical description and the severity of the condition. Jesus, moved with compassion, goes beyond societal norms by reaching out and physically touching the leper, not only healing but also restoring his dignity. This remarkable act highlights Jesus' mission to restore and bring wholeness to those who are marginalized and rejected. It emphasizes his willingness to extend grace and mercy to all, symbolizing his power to heal both physical and spiritual afflictions.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The expression, full of leprosy, seems to be used here with professional accuracy."
2. "Luke means to describe an aggravated case."
3. "All three evangelists say cleanse instead of heal, because of the notion of uncleanness which specially attached to this malady."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Evidently a bad case full of sores and far advanced as Luke the physician notes."
2. "The law (Leviticus 13:12) curiously treated advanced cases as less unclean than the earlier stages."
3. "His condition of the third class turned on the 'will' (θεληις) of Jesus who at once asserts his will (θηλω) and cleanses him."

### John Gill:
1. "Between this disease and sin, there is a very great likeness... all mankind, by reason of sin, are by the Lord pronounced filthy; and by their evil actions, not only defile themselves, but others."
2. "Sin has spread itself over all mankind, and over all the powers and faculties of the soul, and members of the body; there is no place free of it."
3. "Christ only, by his blood and stripes, heals the disease of sin, and cleanses from it."
4. "If a man acknowledges that there is no soundness in his flesh, that in him, that is, in his flesh, dwells no good thing, but that his salvation is alone, by the grace and mercy of God, such a man is justified by faith in Christ Jesus."
5. "The cleansing of a leper, two birds were to be taken clean and alive, which were both typical of Christ, and pointed at the meekness of his human nature, his innocence, harmlessness, and purity, and that he had a life to lay down."

### John Wesley:
1. "The leper's approach to Jesus is a powerful demonstration of faith and humility. Despite being an outcast and considered unclean, he boldly comes to Jesus and kneels before him, acknowledging Jesus' authority and power to heal."
2. "Jesus' response to the leper's plea is filled with compassion and love. He reaches out and touches the leper, breaking the social and religious norms of the time. This act of physical contact not only demonstrates Jesus' willingness to heal, but also his desire to restore the leper's dignity and humanity."
3. "The healing of the leper serves as a powerful reminder of Jesus' mission to bring restoration and wholeness to those who are broken and marginalized. It reveals his heart for the outcasts and his willingness to extend grace and mercy to those society has rejected."
