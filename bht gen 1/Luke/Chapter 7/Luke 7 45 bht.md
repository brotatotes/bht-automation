## BHT:
In this verse, the absence of a kiss signifies a lack of love or affection, emphasizing the significance of genuine care and deep emotional connection. While the host neglected to offer a customary welcome, this unbidden woman continued to shower the Savior's feet with tender and heartfelt kisses, expressing her continuous display of love and devotion. The act of kissing becomes a powerful symbol of intimacy and heartfelt expression, in contrast to the lack of affection shown by others in the story.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The kiss was a token of affection or a common mode of salutation, and Simon had even neglected this mark of welcoming him to his house."
2. "How striking the difference between the conduct of Simon and this woman! He, with all the richness of a splendid preparation, had omitted the common marks of regard and affection."
3. "The most splendid entertainments do not always express the greatest welcome. There may be in such entertainments much insincerity - much seeking of popularity or some other motive; but no such motive could have operated in inducing a broken-hearted sinner to wash the Saviour’s 'feet' with tears."

### John Gill:
