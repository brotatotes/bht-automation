# Luke 8:45 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this Bible verse, Jesus asks the question "Who touched me?" not out of ignorance, but to bring attention to the faith and healing of the woman who reached out to Him. Despite the denial from those around Him, the woman came forward and confessed what she had done. The quote highlights the crowded and pressing nature of the multitude, emphasizing the significance of the woman's deliberate touch amidst the chaos.

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "Askest Thou, Lord, who touched Thee? Rather ask who touched Thee not in such a throng." This quote highlights the significance of the question asked by Jesus. It suggests that in a crowded place, it is not surprising that someone would touch Him, but what is remarkable is the faith and intention behind the touch.

2. "The question 'Who touched me?' was not asked out of ignorance, but to bring the woman's faith and healing into the open." This quote emphasizes that Jesus already knew who touched Him, but He wanted the woman to publicly acknowledge her faith and receive recognition for her healing.

3. "The touch of faith draws from Jesus a response of power." This quote reveals the connection between faith and healing. It suggests that the woman's touch was not merely physical but carried the power of her belief, which prompted Jesus to release healing power in response.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Who touched?": This question highlights the curiosity and surprise of the disciples, as they were surrounded by a crowd and yet Jesus sensed a specific touch. It emphasizes the significance of the interaction that took place.
2. "Throng and press": This phrase describes the intense and crowded environment in which Jesus and his disciples found themselves. It conveys the idea of a tightly packed crowd, making it even more remarkable that Jesus was able to discern a single touch.
3. "Crush": This word suggests the force and pressure exerted by the crowd, likening it to squeezing out wine from grapes. It adds depth to the understanding of the physical environment and the challenges Jesus and his disciples faced in navigating through the crowd.
