In this verse, Moses is seen to have given permission for divorce through the use of a bill, emphasizing the legal nature of the act. Jesus' repetition of this teaching underscores the significance of upholding the original institution of marriage and correcting misinterpretations. By citing the creation of man and woman as one flesh, Jesus argues against the easy dissolution of marriage. ### Philip Schaff:
1. "The repetition of this precept in different parts of the Gospel shows the importance of it, and the necessity of attending to it; and that it was not only what Christ delivered in one place, and on one occasion, but what he frequently inculcated, and which he would have carefully observed and regarded by his disciples."

2. "The design of Christ in this, as in other cases, was to correct the false glosses and interpretations of the Scribes and Pharisees, who allowed of divorces upon very slight occasions, and to bring men back to the original institution of marriage, as it was from the beginning."

3. "Christ's argument is taken from the original formation of man and woman, and the union that subsisted between them; which was such, that they were one flesh, and therefore should not be separated."

4. "From hence it appears, that marriage is an ordinance of God, of his appointing, and not man's invention; and that it is a sacred and inviolable one, and not to be dissolved at pleasure, or on any account, but that of adultery."
