### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "He that was one of the twelve" - Note the article here, "the one of the twelve," Matthew has only εις, "one." 

2. "Some have held that Mark here calls Judas the primate among the twelve." - Some have held that Mark is suggesting Judas was the leader among the twelve.

3. "Rather he means to call attention to the idea that he was the one of the twelve who did this deed." - Mark is emphasizing that Judas was the one among the twelve who committed this act.

### John Wesley:
1. "Judas went to the chief priests" - This action by Judas reveals his true character, as he chooses to betray Jesus for personal gain. It highlights the contrast between his outward appearance as a disciple and his inward motivations of greed and self-interest.

2. "Immediately after this reproof" - Judas' decision to betray Jesus comes right after Jesus confronts him about his sinful intentions. This shows that Judas was not willing to repent or change his ways, but instead hardened his heart and proceeded with his treacherous plan.

3. "Having anger now added to his covetousness" - Judas' anger towards Jesus for exposing his true intentions further fuels his desire to betray him. This combination of covetousness and anger reveals the depths of Judas' betrayal and highlights the destructive power of unchecked sin.
