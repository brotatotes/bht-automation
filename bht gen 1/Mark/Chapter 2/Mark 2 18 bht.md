# Mark 2:18 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The disciples of John the Baptist questioned Jesus about fasting, revealing a contrast in their mindset. Jesus responds by using the analogy of a wedding feast to explain that his disciples should not fast while he is with them, emphasizing the joy and celebration of his presence. However, he acknowledges that there will be a time for fasting when he is no longer with them, foreshadowing his future departure. These verses remind us to approach spiritual disciplines with discernment and to seek a genuine connection with Jesus, allowing him to guide us in our practices.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The A. V. refers to the fact as a custom; but Mark means that they were observing a fast at that time." 
2. "Hence the use of the participle with the finite verb. Rev., correctly, were fasting."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "This explanatory remark, peculiar to Mark, may point to some particular fast, which these classes were then observing."
2. "The form of the question in Matthew and Luke indicates the habits of these classes."

### John Wesley:
1. "The disciples of John the Baptist came to Jesus with a question about fasting. This question reveals a fundamental difference in approach between the disciples of John and the disciples of Jesus." 

2. "Jesus responds to their question by using the metaphor of a wedding feast. He explains that while the bridegroom is with them, it is not appropriate for his disciples to fast. This statement highlights the joy and celebration that comes with the presence of Jesus."

3. "Jesus goes on to say that there will come a time when the bridegroom will be taken away from them, and then they will fast. This foreshadows his impending death and departure from the disciples, and the subsequent period of mourning and fasting that will follow."

4. "In these verses, Jesus is not dismissing the practice of fasting altogether, but rather emphasizing the need for discernment and understanding of the appropriate time and context for fasting. It is a reminder that our spiritual practices should be guided by the presence and leading of Jesus."

5. "Ultimately, these verses invite us to reflect on our own approach to spiritual disciplines such as fasting. Are we simply going through the motions, or are we seeking a deeper connection with Jesus? Are we willing to let go of our own agendas and expectations, and allow Jesus to guide us in our spiritual practices?"
