# Mark 4:24 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Be attentive to the knowledge you receive, for as you diligently heed and understand it, you will be rewarded with even greater understanding. Take care to listen and make good use of what you hear, for the measure of your attention will determine the measure of your spiritual growth. The more you diligently study and apply the teachings, the more wisdom and enlightenment you will be granted.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "More shall be added, i.e. more knowledge." 
2. "As diligence increases such knowledge, so laziness destroys it." 
3. "For whoever has attentiveness, knowledge will be given to them, and whoever does not have it, even what they have will be taken away from them."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Take heed what ye hear—In Luke ( :-) it is, 'Take heed how ye hear.' The one implies the other, but both precepts are very weighty."
2. "With what measure ye mete, it shall be measured to you—See on :-."
3. "And unto you that hear—that is, thankfully, teachably, profitably. Shall more be given."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Take heed what ye hear - Or, consider well what you hear. Make a good improvement of it."
2. "With what measure ye mete ... - You shall be treated according to the use you make of your opportunities of learning."
3. "If you consider it well, and make a good improvement of what you hear, you shall be well rewarded. If not, your reward shall be small."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The reference is to a proper improvement of the opportunities now graciously afforded them, as appears from what follows."
2. "Giving and receiving are reciprocal. As you treat me as your Instructor (giving attention), you will be treated (in receiving profit)."
3. "The promise of increased knowledge is certainly given to those who faithfully teach in God’s kingdom; but here the other application is the primary one, as appears from the more immediate connection."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Some things should not be heard at all for they besmirch the mind and heart."
2. "What is worth hearing should be heard rightly and heeded."
3. "With what measure."

### John Gill:
1. "Take heed what you hear: diligently attend to it, seek to understand it, and lay it up in your minds and memories, that it may be of use to you in time to come, and you may be useful in communicating it to others."
2. "If the disciples carefully hearkened to what they heard from Christ, and studiously laboured to understand it, and faithfully dispensed it to others, in return, a larger measure, and greater degree of spiritual knowledge, would be bestowed upon them."
3. "Those who hear so as to understand, keep, and make a good use of what they hear, more shall be communicated to them; they shall have an increase of knowledge in the doctrines of grace, and mysteries of the Gospel."

### John Wesley:
1. "Take heed what ye hear — That is, attend to what you hear, that it may have its due influence upon you." 
2. "With what measure you mete — That is, according to the improvement you make of what you have heard, still farther assistance shall be given." 
3. "And to you that hear — That is, with improvement."
