# Mark 5:34 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, Jesus addresses a woman who had touched his garment to be healed. The commentators highlight Jesus' compassionate response, referring to her as "daughter" to show both tender affection and her spiritual relationship as a child of God. They emphasize that her healing came through faith in Christ and not her own merit, underscoring the power of Jesus' healing. Additionally, they point out that the peace Jesus offers extends beyond absence of conflict, representing true spiritual peace found through faith in Christ, justification, and salvation.

### John Calvin:
1. "Go in peace, and be delivered from thy scourge." - This exhortation from Jesus confirms that the woman's healing was not only physical but also spiritual. It emphasizes the importance of finding peace and freedom from the burdens that afflict us.
2. "The benefit which she had obtained was fully ratified." - This statement highlights the significance of Jesus' words in affirming and validating the woman's healing. It shows that the true enjoyment of God's blessings comes from recognizing and accepting them through the promises of God.
3. "We do not truly, or with a safe conscience, enjoy God's benefits in any other way than by possessing them as contained in the treasury of his promises." - This quote emphasizes the importance of trusting in God's promises and relying on them to experience the fullness of His blessings. It reminds us that our faith and confidence in God's word are essential for truly enjoying His benefits.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Be healed. Not the same word as in the previous clause." 
2. "These words were a gracious and solemn ratification of the healing, which had been stolen, as it were."
3. "Go in peace. Lit, ‘into peace.’ The state in which she could now live in contrast with her previous suffering and her unquiet up to this moment."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Go in peace (Hυπαγε εις ειρηνην)." - The woman found sympathy, healing, and pardon for her sins. This phrase suggests that peace here may encompass the idea of both physical and spiritual well-being.

2. "Peace here may have more the idea of the Hebrew shalom, health of body and soul." - The concept of peace in this context goes beyond mere absence of conflict and includes the wholeness and flourishing of both the body and the soul.

3. "Be whole of thy plague (ισθ υγιης απο της μαστιγος σου)." - Jesus instructs the woman to continue in a state of wholeness and well-being, not just temporarily healed from her affliction but completely free from it. This emphasizes the restoration of her physical health and the eradication of her ailment.

### John Gill:
1. "Instead of reproving her, or showing any anger, or resentment at her, as she feared, he speaks to her in a very soft, kind, and tender manner, and called her 'daughter', which was not only an expression of affection and civility, this being an affable, courteous way of speaking used by the Jews; but might signify her spiritual relation through him, being a child of God by adopting grace."
2. "Through faith in Christ she received the cure from him; for it was not her act of faith that either merited, or procured it, but his power, and he himself the object of her faith that effected it."
3. "Peace is the effect of faith in Christ, of pardon through his blood, and salvation in him; true, spiritual, solid peace is enjoyed in a way of believing; it is the fruit of a view of interest in justification by faith in Christ's righteousness."
