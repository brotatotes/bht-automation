In this verse, a desperate plea is made to God, recognizing our dependence on His help. The demons acknowledge Jesus as the Son of the Most High God and implore Him to spare them from imminent torment. Their admission of His authority and power highlights His divine nature. The demon's adjuration, invoking the name of God, reflects the urgent and solemn plea, signifying Jesus' supernatural power. ### Philip Schaff:
1. "I adjure thee by God." - This strong expression used by the demon shows the desperate plea for mercy, asking Jesus to deal with them as God does. It is not just a blasphemy, but a cunning argument to prolong their respite from final doom.
2. "We implore thee to deal with us as God Himself does." - The demon's plea is for Jesus to extend the respite they currently enjoy, mirroring how God grants mercy and delays judgment. This acknowledgment comes from the most virulent demon, highlighting the power and authority of Jesus.
3. "The highest acknowledgment comes from the most virulent demon." - Despite their evil nature, even the most powerful demons recognize and acknowledge Jesus' authority. This highlights the unmatched power and divinity of Jesus, as even the forces of darkness cannot deny His supremacy.

3. "Torment me not" - The demoniac's plea reveals his fear of being subjected to torment or torture. This demonstrates the recognition of Jesus' power to bring about judgment and punishment, highlighting the demons' awareness of their impending defeat in the presence of Jesus.
