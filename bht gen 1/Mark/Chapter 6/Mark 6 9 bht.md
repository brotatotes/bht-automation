# Mark 6:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, the disciples are instructed to go out on their mission without delay or unnecessary preparations. The commentators highlight the urgency and readiness required, emphasizing the promptness of their departure and their reliance on God's provision. The disciples are reminded to focus on their ministry without being weighed down by worldly concerns. This mission marks the beginning of their apostolic work and serves as a reminder of God's faithfulness throughout their lives. Sandals are mentioned as a symbol of leaving worldly comforts behind and embarking on this important journey.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "With sandals, i.e., such as they had on at the time, without waiting for shoes especially adapted for the journey." 
2. "The construction changes into a direct command in the last clause, as if the memory of one present had supplied it." 
3. "The disciples were to go out in a state of readiness and urgency, not delaying for unnecessary preparations."

### John Gill:
1. "A minister of the Gospel ought not to be a worldly minded man, that minds earth and earthly things, and seeks to amass wealth and riches to himself."
2. "He ought to have his mind free from all solicitude and anxious concern, about a subsistence for himself and his, that so he may with greater and more close application attend to his ministry."
3. "It is highly necessary that the people to whom he ministers should take care, that a sufficient provision be made for him; that he may live without any anxious care and thought about such things, and his mind be more intent about the work he is called unto."

### John Wesley:
1. "Sandals were pieces of strong leather or wood, tied under the sole of the foot by thongs, something resembling modern clogs."
2. "Our Lord intended by this mission to initiate them into their apostolic work."
3. "It was doubtless an encouragement to them all their life after, to recollect the care which God took of them, when they had left all they had, and went out quite unfurnished for such an expedition."
