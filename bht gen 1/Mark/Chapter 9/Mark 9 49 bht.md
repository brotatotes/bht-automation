### Henry Alford:
1. "It connects it with the solemn assertions in Mark 9:43-48, καλόν ἐστίν σε … and furnishes a reason why it is better for us to cut off and cast away, &c."
2. "Fire is the symbol of the divine purity and presence: our God is a consuming fire, not only to his foes, but to his people."
3. "This very fire shall be to them as a preserving salt."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Every one" probably means "Every follower of mine"; and the "fire" with which he "must be salted" probably means "a fiery trial" to season him.
2. The reference to salting the sacrifice is of course to that maxim of the Levitical law, that every acceptable sacrifice must be sprinkled with salt, to express symbolically its soundness, sweetness, wholesomeness, acceptability.
3. "Every disciple of Mine shall have a fiery trial to undergo, and everyone who would be found an odor of a sweet smell, a sacrifice acceptable and well-pleasing to God, must have such a salting, like the Levitical sacrifices."

### John Calvin:
1. "To be salted with fire is an incorrect phrase; but as salt and fire possess the same quality of purifying and refining, Christ applied the same term to both." 
2. "Believers are salted by the word of the Gospel, that they may be sanctified."
3. "When men have lost, by their carelessness, that savor which they obtained by the grace of God, there is no farther remedy."

### John Gill:
1. "That is every one of those that transgress the law of God, offend any that believe in Christ, retain their sins, and sinful companions; every one of them that are cast into hell, where the worm of conscience is always gnawing, and the fire of divine wrath is always burning, with that fire every one of them shall be salted."
2. "So that these words are a reason of the former, showing and proving, that the soul in torment shall never die, or lose any of its powers and faculties; and particularly, not its gnawing, torturing conscience; and that the fire of hell is inextinguishable."
3. "Now our Lord in this has either respect to the same persons, as before; and signifies hereby, that the wicked in hell shall be victims to divine justice, and sacrifices to his wrath and vengeance; and that as the sacrifices under the law were salted with salt, these shall be salted with the fire of hell, and shall never be utterly destroyed; but shall ever remain the objects of God's sore displeasure; and fiery indignation."

### John Wesley:
1. "Who does not cut off the offending member, and consequently is cast into hell, shall be, as it were, salted with fire, preserved, not consumed thereby." 
2. "Every acceptable sacrifice shall be salted with another kind of salt, even that of Divine grace, which purifies the soul." 
3. "Divine grace... preserves [the soul] from corruption."
