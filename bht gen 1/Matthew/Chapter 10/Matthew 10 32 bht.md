# Matthew 10:32 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This verse speaks of the importance of openly and boldly confessing faith in Christ, even in the face of opposition or shame. It emphasizes the reciprocal nature of this confession, with Jesus promising to acknowledge and affirm those who openly acknowledge and confess Him. The verse also highlights the deep unity and connection between Christ and believers, demonstrating God's love for them. It calls for a practical and enduring confession of faith that goes beyond mere words, but is demonstrated through a life aligned with Christ's teachings.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Whosoever therefore shall confess me before men—despising the shame." - This quote emphasizes the importance of boldly proclaiming one's faith in Jesus, even in the face of shame or opposition.

2. "Him will I confess also before my Father which is in heaven." - This quote highlights the promise that Jesus will acknowledge and affirm those who openly acknowledge and confess Him, assuring believers of their acceptance and recognition by God.

3. "I will not be ashamed of him, but will own him before the most august of all assemblies." - This quote underscores Jesus' commitment to stand with and publicly acknowledge those who are unashamedly devoted to Him, even in the presence of the highest authority.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Confess in me." The idea is that of confessing Christ out of a state of oneness with him. It implies identification of the confessor with the confessed, and thus takes confession out of the category of mere formal or verbal acknowledgment.

2. The true confessor of Christ is one whose faith rests in him. It gives great force to the corresponding clause, in which Christ places himself in a similar relation with those whom he confesses.

3. "I in them and thou in me, that they may be perfected into one, and that the world may know that thou hast sent me, and hast loved them as thou hast loved me" (John 17:23). This verse highlights the profound unity between Christ and his followers, demonstrating the love of God for them.

### John Calvin:
1. "For this purpose the disciples of Christ must be bold and courageous, that they may be always ready for martyrdom."
2. "Now confession of Christ, though it is regarded by the greater part of men as a trifling matter, is here represented to be a main part of divine worship, and a distinguished exercise of godliness."
3. "A more public confession of faith, no doubt, is demanded from teachers than from persons in a private station. Besides, all are not endued with an equal measure of faith, and in proportion as any one excels in the gifts of the Spirit, he ought to go before others by his example."

### Archibald T. Robertson:

3. "Confess in me" - The use of this Aramaic idiom in Greek further emphasizes the deep connection and unity between Christ and the individual who boldly proclaims their allegiance to him.
