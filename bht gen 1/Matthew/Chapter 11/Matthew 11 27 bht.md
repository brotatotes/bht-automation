# Matthew 11:27 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this Bible verse, Jesus speaks to his disciples about the authority and knowledge that he possesses. He explains that all things, including the administration of the kingdom of grace, have been delivered to him by the Father, emphasizing his role as the mediator. The verse also highlights the mutual and exclusive knowledge between the Father and the Son, illustrating the deep connection and equality they share. Understanding the full extent of Christ's nature and work requires divine revelation and goes beyond human comprehension.

### John Gill:
1. "All things are delivered unto me of my Father" - Christ, as mediator, has been entrusted with all things by the Father. This includes all persons, both angels and men, who are under his authority and command. (Quote 1)

2. "No man knoweth the Son, but the Father" - The full extent of Christ's nature as the Son of God and his work as the mediator is known only by the Father. His divine glories and the depth of his sacrifice for his people are beyond human comprehension. (Quote 2)

3. "Neither knoweth any man the Father, save the Son" - The Son alone has perfect knowledge of the Father's essence, will, purposes, and love for his chosen people. Only through the Son's revelation can we come to know the Father and experience his grace and glory. (Quote 3)
