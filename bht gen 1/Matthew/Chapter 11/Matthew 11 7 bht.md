# Matthew 11:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this passage, Jesus responds to the departing disciples of John the Baptist who may have undervalued John's ministry. Jesus takes the opportunity to affirm John's character and office, reminding the people of his deep influence and unwavering commitment to the truth. He warns against judging John as a reed shaken by the wind, emphasizing that John's preaching was consistent and firm. This conversation serves as a confirmation of Jesus' own Messiahship and a testimony to John's unwavering faithfulness to his ministry.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "They are not spoken till after the departure of the disciples of John, probably because they were not meant for them or John to hear, but for the people, who on account of the question which they had heard might go away with a mistaken depreciation of John." 
2. "And our Lord, as usual, takes occasion, from reminding them of the impression made on them by John’s preaching of repentance, to set forth to them deep truths regarding His own Kingdom and Office." 
3. "Ὁ τοσαῦτα μαρτυρήσας μετεπείσθη νῦν, καὶ ἀμφιβάλλει εἴτε οὗτος εἴτε ἕτερος εἴη ὁ ἐρχόμενος?" (Translation: "He who bore witness to so much is now doubting whether this is the one who is to come or whether there is another?")

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Jesus took occasion, from the inquiries made by John’s disciples, to instruct the people respecting the true character of John."
2. "He took occasion, therefore, by some striking questions, to examine the motives by which they had been drawn to his ministry."
3. "Jesus, by asking this question, declared that, notwithstanding this appearance, this was not the character of John."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "As these went their way; or while they, John's disciples, were departing: thus giving the simultaneousness of Jesus' words with the act of departure."
2. "The calm, continuous contemplation of an object which remains before the spectator."
3. "The more earnest expression suits the first question."

### John Calvin:
1. "Christ praises John before the people, in order that they may state from recollection what they have heard from him, and may give credit to his testimony."
2. "Christ reminds them, that those who went out to see him in the wilderness lost their pains, if they did not devoutly apply their minds and faculties to his doctrine."
3. "If therefore you would reap advantage from your undertaking, it is necessary that what he spoke should remain fixed in your memory."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The comment follows at once, to uphold the character of John, which might have been undervalued in consequence of his message."
2. "The great influence of John appears from the fact that our Lord thus appeals to a mixed crowd."
3. "Popularity is very often due to curiosity, even in the case of an earnest and faithful preacher."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "It may almost be called the funeral oration of the Baptist, for not long afterwards Herodias compassed his death" (Plummer).
2. "A reed shaken by the wind" - Used of the reeds that grew in plenty in the Jordan Valley where John preached, of a staff made of a reed (Matthew 27:29), as a measuring rod (Revelation 11:1), of a writer's pen (3 John 1:13).
3. "The reeds by the Jordan bent with the wind, but not so John."

### John Gill:
1. "Christ, by giving an account of his character and office, confirms his own Messiahship."
2. "What did they expect to see there? A reed shaken with the wind?"
3. "If you did, you were mistaken; he was firm and stable in his sentiments and ministry, his preaching was not yea and nay, his doctrine was all of a piece."

### John Wesley:
1. "Of whom probably he would not have said so much when they were present."
2. "Nothing could ever shake John in the testimony he gave to the truth."
3. "The expression is proverbial."
