### Henry Alford:
1. "The repetition of this question, and the order of the suggestive answers, are remarkable."
2. "The first sets before them the scene of their desert pilgrimage the banks of Jordan with its reeds."
3. "The second reminds them that it was a man but not one in soft clothing, for such are not found in deserts."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Clothed in soft raiment - The kind of raiment here denoted was the light, thin clothing worn by effeminate persons."
2. "This kind of clothing was an emblem of riches, splendor, effeminacy, feebleness of character."
3. "He meant to say that John was a man of a different stamp - coarse in his exterior, hardy in his character, firm in his virtue, suited to endure trials and privations."

### John Calvin:
1. "Those who think that Christ here condemns the extravagance of a court are mistaken."
2. "There are many other passages in which luxury of dress, and excessive attention to outward appearance, are censured."
3. "But this passage simply means, that there was nothing in the wilderness to attract the people from every quarter; that every thing there was rude and unpolished, and fitted only to inspire disgust; and that such elegance of dress as delights the eyes is rather to be looked for in the courts of kings."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "A man clothed in soft raiment? An allusion to the coarseness of John's clothing."
2. "Behold. This is equivalent to, 'oh no, such are not found in the wilderness.'"
3. "In kings' houses; not in kings' prisons. An allusion to the courtiers about Herod Antipas."

### John Gill:
1. "John's raiment was of camel's hair, undressed, and he had a leathern girdle about his loins; and as for his diet, it was locusts and wild honey." 
2. "They that wear soft clothing, or are gorgeously apparelled, and live delicately, are in kings' houses, not in a desert or a prison." 
3. "From his very garb and diet, his character is vindicated from the charge of levity and change."

### John Wesley:
1. "An effeminate courtier, accustomed to fawning and flattery?"
2. "You may expect to find persons of such a character in palaces; not in a wilderness."
3. "A man clothed in soft, delicate raiment."
