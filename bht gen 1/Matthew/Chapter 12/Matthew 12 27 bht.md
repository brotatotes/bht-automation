# Matthew 12:27 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, Jesus questions the Pharisees about the work of casting out demons, making them confront their own contradiction. The commentators note that the Pharisees' disciples also practiced such exorcisms, emphasizing the Pharisees' self-condemnation. Furthermore, the commentators argue that the Pharisees' unjust judgment would lead to their own condemnation. Through this verse, Jesus highlights the fallacy in the Pharisees' reasoning and asserts that the kingdom of God has unexpectedly arrived.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "If casting out devils is an evidence of a league with Satan, then this holds good against your scholars who profess to do it also."
2. "Therefore they shall be your judges. They shall convict you of partiality."
3. "The existence of 'demoniacs' in those days is proof that supernatural power, of itself, is no test of truth."

### John Gill:
1. "Christ asks them, by whom they cast out devils? Whether by the Spirit of God, or by Beelzebub? They would doubtless say by the former, and not the latter, which would show their great partiality."
2. "Their children cast out devils, or pretended to do so, and it was by the help of Satan; and yet they ascribed it to a divine power, even though they made use of the name of Satan, under that of Beelzebub."
3. "Therefore they shall be your judges; who will rise up against you, and condemn you one day, for this unequal judgment you now pass; and which was just the reverse of the true state of the case."

### John Wesley:
1. "Ask them, if Satan will cast out Satan: let even them be judges in this matter."
2. "And they shall convict you of obstinacy and partiality, who impute that in me to Beelzebub, which in them you impute to God."
3. "The kingdom of God is come upon you - Unawares; before you expected: so the word implies."
