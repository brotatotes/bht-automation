### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "Not surely to abolish it—that surely were a strange lordship, especially just after saying that it was made or instituted for MAN—but to own it, to interpret it, to preside over it, and to ennoble it, by merging it in the 'Lord's Day'."

3. "The Son of man is Lord even of the sabbath day, not to destroy it, but to fulfill it; to bring out its true meaning, and to show that it was intended to be a blessing and not a burden."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The Son of man is Lord even of the Sabbath day" - This statement by Christ asserts his authority over the Sabbath, indicating his divinity and his right to determine how it should be observed.

2. "He had a right to direct the manner of its observance" - This highlights that Jesus not only had the authority to determine how the Sabbath should be kept, but also the responsibility to guide and teach others in its proper observance.

3. "Undoubted proof that he is divine" - By claiming to be the Lord of the Sabbath, Jesus provides undeniable evidence of his divinity, reinforcing his unique and divine nature.

### Philip Schaff:

### John Gill:
1. "No mere man is lord of any law, moral or ritual, natural or positive; or has a power of disposing of it, and dispensing with it at pleasure; but Christ himself."
