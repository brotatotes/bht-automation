# Matthew 12:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, Jesus declares himself as Lord of the Sabbath, demonstrating his divine authority over it. He reveals his power to interpret, enforce, and even relax the requirements of the Sabbath. Christ's claim as the Son of Man signifies his Messiahship and highlights his role as the representative and head of renewed humanity. By asserting his authority over the Sabbath, Jesus shows that it is an institution of utmost significance, with the potential to be a type and foreshadowing of heavenly communion.

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "Not surely to abolish it—that surely were a strange lordship, especially just after saying that it was made or instituted for MAN—but to own it, to interpret it, to preside over it, and to ennoble it, by merging it in the 'Lord's Day'."

3. "The Son of man is Lord even of the sabbath day."

2. "He had a right to direct the manner of its observance" - This quote highlights Christ's authority to establish the guidelines for Sabbath observance, indicating his role as the ultimate authority in matters of faith and practice.

3. "Undoubted proof that he is divine" - This statement underscores the significance of Christ's claim to be Lord of the Sabbath, serving as evidence of his divinity and reinforcing his unique position as the Son of God.

### John Calvin:
1. "Christ, by an allusion to the temple, affirmed that whatever was connected with his personal holiness was not a transgression of the Law."
2. "He declares that he has received authority to exempt his followers from the necessity of observing the Sabbath."
3. "The Son of man, in the exercise of his authority, can relax the Sabbath in the same manner as other legal ceremonies."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The term ‘Son of man’ implies His Messiahship."
2. "As Son of man, Head and Representative of renewed humanity, our Lord is Lord of the Sabbath."
3. "It becomes a type and foretaste of heaven, a day when we get nearest our Lord, when we rise most with Him, when our truest humanity is furthered, because we are truly made like the ‘Son of man.’"

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Lord of the Sabbath (κυριος του σαββατου). This claim that he as the Son of Man is master of the Sabbath and so above the Pharisaic regulations angered them extremely."
2. "By the phrase 'the Son of man' here Jesus involves the claim of Messiahship."
3. "As the Representative Man he affirms his solidarity with mankind, 'standing for the human interest' (Bruce) on this subject."

### John Gill:
1. "No mere man is lord of any law, moral or ritual, natural or positive; or has a power of disposing of it, and dispensing with it at pleasure; but Christ himself."
2. "Christ is greater than the temple, and has all the perfections of the divine nature in him, is equal to the Father in power and glory; and even as mediator, has all power in heaven and earth given him."
3. ### John Wesley:
