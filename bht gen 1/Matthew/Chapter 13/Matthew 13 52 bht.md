# Matthew 13:52 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The verse suggests that a well-instructed teacher, belonging to the kingdom of heaven, possesses a treasure of divine knowledge. This treasure enables the teacher to bring forth a variety of instructions that combine old truths with new insights, harmonizing different aspects of faith. The teacher's duty is to share this treasure, not hoard it, and through their zealous communication, they enrich the understanding of both familiar and fresh perspectives on spiritual matters.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Every scribe...hath been made a disciple." - The scribes, as official expounders of the Scriptures, are called to be disciples of Jesus, not just knowledgeable teachers.
2. "The 'treasure' is a constantly increasing knowledge of God's word." - The scribe's duty is to bring forth from their treasure of knowledge and understanding, not selfishly hoard it.
3. "Things new and old." - The scribe must be able to present a combination of old truths and new insights, bringing harmony between the old and the new.

### John Wesley:
1. "Every scribe instructed unto the kingdom of heaven has a treasure of Divine knowledge." 
2. "Such a scribe is able to bring forth all sorts of instructions from this treasure." 
3. "The word treasure signifies any collection of things whatsoever, and the places where such collections are kept."
