In this verse, we see Jesus showing His compassion and power as He feeds a multitude with a few loaves of bread and fish. The commentators bring out the significance of the three days mentioned, symbolizing a period of testing and preparation for the disciples. This event showcases Jesus' ability to provide beyond what seems possible and underscores the importance of spending time in His presence for spiritual growth. Ultimately, the three days also point to the resurrection of Jesus, offering hope and assurance for believers.

### John Calvin:
1. "Let us learn from this, that the power of God is not restricted to means or outward assistance, and that it is all one with Him whether there be much or little."
2. "As the blessing of God can make one loaf suffice as well as twenty for satisfying a great multitude, so, if that be wanting, a hundred loaves will not be a sufficient meal for ten men."
3. "The three days' fasting, of which Christ speaks, must not be understood to mean that they had eaten nothing for three days; but that in desert places they had few conveniences, and must have wanted their ordinary food."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Our Lord Himself takes the first step."

3. "The Lord's compassion was called out by their physical want, which, however, resulted from their desire to be near Him."

### John Gill:
1. "Christ, our high priest, is a merciful one, and is touched with the feeling of the infirmities of men, of every sort, both of soul and body." 
2. "The diligence and indefatigableness of Christ, as well as the attachment of the people to him." 
3. "He could not bear the thoughts of dismissing them in such a condition; having had but very little sustenance all this while, and so might be said to be in a manner fasting during this time, at least now."

### John Wesley:
1. "The three days mentioned here symbolize a period of testing and preparation. Just as Jesus was tested in the wilderness for forty days, the disciples were being tested and prepared for what was to come. This reminds us that God often uses times of waiting and testing to shape and mold us for His purposes."

2. "The disciples had been with Jesus for three days, witnessing His miracles and teachings. This extended time with Him allowed them to develop a deeper understanding of His power and authority. It serves as a reminder that spending time in the presence of Jesus is essential for our spiritual growth and transformation."

3. "The three days also foreshadow the resurrection of Jesus. Just as Jesus rose from the dead on the third day, the disciples would soon witness His ultimate victory over sin and death. This points to the hope and assurance we have as believers, knowing that Jesus conquered death and offers us eternal life."
