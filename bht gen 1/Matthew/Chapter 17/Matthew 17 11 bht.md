# Matthew 17:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, commentators highlight the connection between Elijah and John the Baptist, emphasizing the fulfillment of prophecy. They explain that Elijah had already come in the person of John the Baptist, who played a significant role in restoring the fallen condition of the Church. The verse speaks of restoration and preparation, with the understanding that Elijah's coming preceded the coming of the Messiah. It is a reminder to regulate all things in our lives in anticipation of Christ's return, allowing Him to transform us and prepare us for His glorious second coming.

### John Calvin:
1. "As John the Baptist was to resemble Elijah by restoring the fallen condition of the Church, the prophet Malachi had even given to him the name of Elijah."
2. "Christ now declares that every thing which Malachi uttered was true, but that his prediction had been misunderstood and distorted from its true meaning."
3. "Christ reminds his disciples that the impostures of such men ought not to give them uneasiness, and that it ought not to be reckoned strange, if, after having rejected the servant, they should, with equal disdain, reject his Master."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Elijah indeed cometh. Our Lord confirms the view, that Elijah should come." 
2. "Shall restore or ‘establish anew,’ all things." 
3. "The actual work of restoration was however the work of the Messiah, for which Elijah should prepare the way."

### John Gill:
1. "The truest sense of the phrase is to be learned out of Malachi 4:6. 'He shall restore, he shall turn all things, the heart of the fathers to the children, and the heart of the children to their fathers'." 

2. "He shall turn the disobedient to the wisdom of the just, and make ready a people prepared for the Lord."

3. "The whole of the Gospel being to be reduced to these two heads, repentance towards God, and faith in Christ; both which were taught by the true Elias."

### John Wesley:
1. "Regulate all things" implies the need for order and structure in our lives, aligning our thoughts, actions, and desires with the teachings of Christ. It reminds us to prioritize our relationship with Him and live in a way that reflects His love and grace.

2. The phrase "in order to the coming of Christ" highlights the anticipation and preparation for Christ's return. It encourages us to live with a sense of urgency, constantly seeking to grow in faith and holiness, and to be ready for His second coming.

3. This commentary reminds us that regulating all things in our lives is not a burden, but rather a means of drawing closer to Christ. It is an invitation to surrender our will to His, allowing Him to transform us and mold us into His likeness, preparing us for His glorious return.
