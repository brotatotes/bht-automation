# Matthew 17:20 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this Bible verse, commentators discuss the significance of having even the smallest amount of genuine faith. They emphasize that faith, no matter how feeble or little, has the power to accomplish great things and overcome doubt and unbelief. The commentators highlight the importance of maintaining strong faith and not allowing doubts to hinder our relationship with God. They also mention that the phrase "to remove mountains" was a proverbial expression used to describe a difficult or seemingly impossible task.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Albert Barnes:
1. "If you have the smallest or feeblest faith that is genuine, you can do all things."
2. "If you have increasing, expanding, enlarged faith, growing and strengthening from small beginnings, you can perform the most difficult undertaking."
3. "There is a principle of vitality in the grain of seed stretching forward to great results, which illustrates the nature of faith."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Unbelief [απιστιαν] . But the better reading is ojligopistian, littleness of faith." 
2. "Hence Rev., Because of your little faith." 
3. "This verse highlights the importance of having strong faith in God and not allowing doubt or unbelief to hinder our relationship with Him."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The attempt showed some faith, the failure ‘little faith.’ The revelation of our Lord’s death may have caused despondency and doubt."
2. "Small, yet living (chap. Matthew 13:33), and capable of rapid increase, while their faith had decreased."
3. "This promise of power to remove the most formidable obstacles, is misunderstood, only when power over material things is deemed greater than spiritual power."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Little faith (ολιγοπιστιαν). A good translation."
2. "They had no miracle faith."
3. "Our trouble is always with 'this mountain' which confronts our path."

### John Gill:
1. "Christ says, not because of the unbelief of the parent of the child, and those that were with him, though that also was a reason; but because of their unbelief, being willing to convince them of their unbelief, as he had done the father of the child, who had confessed it, and desired it might be removed from him."

2. "And it is used in like sense in other eastern nations; and by Mahomet in his Alcoran, who says, 'We will appoint just balances in the day of resurrection, neither shall any soul be injured at all, although the merit or guilt of an action be of the weight of 'a grain of mustard seed'.'"

3. "So that it does not follow, because the apostles did not do it in a literal sense, therefore they could not, as the Jew insultingly says; since it was meant that they should, and besides, have done, things equally as great as this, and which is the sense of the words."

### John Wesley:
1. "Because of your unbelief - Because in this particular they had not faith."
2. "If ye have faith as a grain of mustard seed - That is, the least measure of it."
3. "To remove mountains was a proverbial phrase among the Jews, and is still retained in their writings, to express a thing which is very difficult, and to appearance impossible."
