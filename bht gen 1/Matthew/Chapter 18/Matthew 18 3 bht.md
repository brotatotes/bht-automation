# Matthew 18:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, commentators emphasize the concept of conversion and the need for a change in direction. Descriptions include turning back from a previous course, turning around and facing a different way, and returning from a path of ambition to childlike humility. The commentators emphasize that without this conversion, one cannot enter or even be admitted into the kingdom of heaven. The verse also highlights the importance of humility, an inward change, and letting go of ambitious rivalry.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "στραφῆτε = μετανοῆτε: it also conveys the idea of turning back from the course previously begun." 
2. "Without this they should not only not be pre-eminent in, but not even admitted into, the Christian state the Kingdom of Heaven."
3. "Turning away from ambitious rivalry is necessary for admission into the Kingdom of Heaven."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The word converted has acquired a conventional religious sense which is fundamentally truthful."
2. "The picture is that of turning round in a road and facing the other way."
3. "So far from being greatest in the kingdom of heaven, ye shall not so much as enter."

### John Calvin:
1. "To the example of little children must be referred the conversion of which he now speaks."
2. "If they would gain their object, they must pursue a totally different course."
3. "Christ does not allot even the lowest place to any man who does not lose sight of distinctions and humble himself."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Return from this path of ambition to childlike humility; not implying that they had never been converted."
2. "The wider application is to the absolute necessity of conversion (turning ourselves to God) in entering the kingdom of heaven."
3. "'Instead of discussing who shall be greater, you need to inquire whether you have entered it.' This is not denied, but the ambitious question, opposed to the humility which is essential, should raise a doubt."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They were headed in the wrong direction with their selfish ambition."
2. "His tone at this time is markedly severe, as much as when He denounces the Pharisaism in the bud He had to deal with" (Bruce).
3. "The strong double negative ου μη εισελθητε means that they will otherwise not get into the kingdom of heaven at all, let alone have big places in it."

### John Gill:
1. "You may take it for a certain truth, and what may be depended upon, that except ye be converted or turned."
2. "Unless ye learn to entertain an humble, and modest opinion of yourselves, are not envious at one another, and drop all contentions about primacy and pre-eminence."
3. "Ye shall be so far from being one greater than another in it, that you shall not enter into it at all."

### John Wesley:
1. "The first step toward entering into the kingdom of grace, is to become as little children: lowly in heart, knowing yourselves utterly ignorant and helpless, and hanging wholly on your Father who is in heaven, for a supply of all your wants."
2. "Except ye be turned from darkness to light, and from the power of Satan to God: except ye be entirely, inwardly changed, renewed in the image of God, ye cannot enter into the kingdom of glory."
3. "Thus must every man be converted in this life, or he can never enter into life eternal."
