# Matthew 18:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, Jesus uses children as a metaphor for humility and the honor they hold in His heavenly kingdom. Acting in Jesus' name means serving with Christian love, representing Him and His interests. When believers humble themselves and align their actions with His will, they are esteemed highly and received into spiritual fellowship. This passage emphasizes the importance of humility, extending kindness to those in need, and recognizing the value of both literal and spiritual children in the context of the Christian faith.

### Henry Alford:
1. "Having shown the child as the pattern of humility, the Lord proceeds to show the honor in which children are held in His heavenly kingdom."
2. "The receiving in My name is the serving with Christian love, and as belonging to Christ."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "In my name" signifies acting in accordance with the authority and character of Jesus, representing Him and His interests.
2. The phrase implies a close relationship and identification with Jesus, as if the person acting in His name is an extension of Him.
3. Acting in Jesus' name means acting in alignment with His will and purposes, seeking to bring glory to Him and advance His kingdom.

### John Calvin:
1. "The term children is now applied metaphorically by Christ to those who have laid aside lofty looks, and who conduct themselves with modesty and humility."
2. "And thus believers are taught in what way they ought to esteem each other: it is by every one humbling himself."
3. "Christ enjoins that the more a man abases himself, the more highly shall he be honored."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The primary reference is to children in years, but the context extends it to children in spirit."
2. "The general application is to those apparently small, those needing and receiving instruction, forbidding pride and a hierarchical spirit on the part of Christ's disciples."
3. "'Shall receive,' i.e., into spiritual fellowship. This implies that little children can be Christians and members of Christ's Church."
