# Matthew 19:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this biblical verse, individuals are depicted bringing their children to Jesus, signifying their desire to dedicate both themselves and their children to him. It was a customary practice for the Jews to lay hands on the person being prayed for, symbolizing consecration. These parents had confidence in the prayers of pious individuals, believing that blessings bestowed by a saint or prophet would bring happiness. This narrative reveals that Christ welcomes not only those who come to him with faith, but also those who come on behalf of others, demonstrating the power and grace of the Holy Spirit.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Probably these were brought by some of his followers, who desired not only to devote themselves to Jesus, but all that they had - their children as well as themselves."
2. "It was customary among the Jews, when blessings were sought for others in prayer, to lay the hands on the head of the person prayed for, implying a kind of consecration to God."
3. "They had also much confidence in the prayers of pious men, believing that those blessed by a saint or a prophet would be happy."

### John Calvin:
1. "This narrative is highly useful; for it shows that Christ receives not only those who, moved by holy desire and faith, freely approach to him, but those who are not yet of age to know how much they need his grace."
2. "We must observe the intention of those who present the children; for if there had not been a deep-rooted conviction in their minds, that the power of the Spirit was at his disposal, that he might pour it out on the people of God, it would have been unreasonable to present their children."
3. "But hence we learn, that they who judge of Christ according to the feeling of their flesh are unfair judges; for they constantly deprive him of his peculiar excellencies, and, on the other hand, ascribe, under the appearance of honor, what does not at all belong to him."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "An encouragement to parents to bring even ‘infants’ to Christ, since, according to Luke, such were among the little children."
2. "A recognition of Christ’s power to bless, since He healed by laying on His hands."
3. "Abstract theories about household relations should not stand between the Lord and little children."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "How little they understood children and Jesus."
2. "It is a tragedy to make children feel that they are in the way at home and at church."
3. "The new child world of today is due directly to Jesus."

### John Gill:
1. "The end for which they were brought is expressed, that he should put his hands on them, and pray; not that he should baptize them, nor did he."
2. "It was common with them to bring their children to venerable persons, men of note for religion and piety, to have their blessing and prayers."
3. "From this rebuke and prohibition of the disciples, it looks plainly as if it had never been the practice of the Jews, nor of John the Baptist, nor of Christ and his disciples, to baptize infants."

### John Wesley:
1. "That he should lay his hands on them — This was a rite which was very early used, in praying for a blessing on young persons."
2. "The disciples rebuked them — That is, them that brought them: probably thinking such an employ beneath the dignity of their Master."
3. "See Genesis 48:14,20; Mark 10:13; Luke 18:15."
