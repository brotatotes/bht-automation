# Matthew 19:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, Jesus emphasizes the significance of childlike qualities and attitudes for those seeking entrance into the kingdom of heaven. The commentators assert that the kingdom of heaven refers to the church, composed of individuals humble, unambitious, and docile. They stress the importance of not hindering children's participation in spiritual matters, as they serve as emblems of proper subjects of the church. Jesus' words highlight the necessity of innocence, trust, and humility, emphasizing the significance of childlike faith for those aspiring to enter the kingdom of heaven.

### Albert Barnes:

### John Calvin:
1. "To exclude from the grace of redemption those who are of that age would be too cruel; and therefore it is not without reason that we employ this passage as a shield against the Anabaptists."
2. "Infants are renewed by the Spirit of God, according to the capacity of their age, till that power which was concealed within them grows by degrees, and becomes fully manifest at the proper time."
3. "But it is presumption and sacrilege to drive far from the fold of Christ those whom he cherishes in his bosom, and to shut the door, and exclude as strangers those whom he does not wish to be forbidden to come to him."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Suffer the little children, etc. The natural impulse would be to bring children to Him, do not check it."
2. "To such belongeth the kingdom of heaven."
3. "Since they are to come (or be brought) to Christ, who is a Saviour, the doctrine of universal depravity is not denied here."

### John Gill:

### John Wesley:
