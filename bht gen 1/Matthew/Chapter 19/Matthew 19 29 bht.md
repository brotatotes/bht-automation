# Matthew 19:29 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Believers are called to forsake worldly attachments, not just the apostles, throughout history and in every country. This decision should be made with a clear conscience, ensuring that it aligns with God's will. While the reward may not be material wealth, those who forsake worldly possessions will receive abundant spiritual blessings and a deepened relationship with God, both in the present world and in the eternal life to come.

### John Calvin:
1. "Those who shall willingly lose all for the sake of Christ, will be more happy even in this life than if they had retained the full possession of them; but the chief reward is laid up for them in heaven."
2. "If any man estimate aright the immediate grace of God, by which he relieves the sorrows of his people, he will acknowledge that it is justly preferred to all the riches of the world."
3. "Though persecutions always await the godly in this world, and though the cross, as it were, is attached to their back, yet so sweet is the seasoning of the grace of God, which gladdens them, that their condition is more desirable than the luxuries of kings."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The promise is of general application."
2. "‘Homes,’ household ties, rather than ‘possessions,’ which are mentioned afterwards."
3. "The motive is everything; self-denial to buy God’s favor is no self-denial."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "A hundredfold (εκατονπλασιονα)." 
2. "But Westcott and Hort read πολλαπλασιονα, manifold." 
3. "Eternal life is the real reward."

### John Gill:
1. "Believers are always to be ready to part with them for his sake, when persecution arises, because of the word."
2. "The love and presence of God... should be more and better to them than an hundred houses, fields, and friends, without them."
3. "They then have the best of it, who being called, in providence, to quit all terrene enjoyments for Christ's sake, are favored with his presence here, and shall enjoy eternal glory and happiness with him in another world."

### John Wesley:

2. "Either by giving any of them up, when they could not be retained with a clear conscience" - This quote highlights the importance of maintaining a clear conscience when making decisions about worldly attachments, indicating that forsaking them should be done with a righteous motive.
3. "Shall receive a hundred-fold - In value, though not in kind, even in the present world" - The commentary suggests that those who forsake worldly possessions will be rewarded abundantly, not necessarily in material possessions, but in spiritual blessings and a deeper relationship with God.
