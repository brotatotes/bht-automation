# Matthew 2:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In a treacherous and divided kingdom, a resolute Joseph is guided by the angel of the Lord to flee to Egypt with Mary and the infant Jesus for safety. Joseph's obedience is justified as Herod's demise is plagued by a gruesome illness. As Joseph and his family seek refuge, the watchful providence of God and the ministry of angels prove crucial in the preservation of the Messiah, ensuring His safety amidst the dangers of the world.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "Miserable Herod! Thou thoughtest thyself safe from a dreaded Rival; but it was He only that was safe from thee; and thou hast not long enjoyed even this fancied security."
2. "Our translators, somewhat capriciously, render the same expression 'the angel of the Lord,' Matthew 1:20; Matthew 2:13; and 'an angel of the Lord,' as here."
3. "As the same angel appears to have been employed on all these high occasions—and most likely he to whom in Luke is given the name of 'Gabriel'—perhaps it should in every instance except the first, be rendered 'the angel'."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Herod left three sons, and the kingdom was at his death divided between them." 
2. "Each of these was also called Herod, and these are the individuals who are so frequently referred to in the New Testament during the ministry of the Saviour and the labors of the apostles."
3. "The above table will show at a glance the chief connections of this family, as far as they are mentioned in the sacred history."

### John Calvin:
1. "We see, at the same time, that the Lord never disappoints his own people, but renders them seasonable aid."
2. "Joseph complied with the suggestion of the angel, because, under a prince who had no delight in shedding blood, and who treated his subjects with mildness, there was less danger."
3. "His danger was our safety: his fear was our confidence."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The length of the stay in Egypt has however been variously reckoned from a few weeks to three years in accordance with the various dates assigned to the Nativity."
2. "The Evangelist adds no comment on Herod's character, no terms of reproach. He can learn little, who will not of himself make proper inferences."
3. "A similar expression is used (Exodus 4:19) in a revelation made to Moses, with which Joseph was certainly acquainted."

### John Gill:
1. "The account which Josephus gives of his miserable death, is as follows; a burning fever seized him, with an intolerable itching all over his body, and continual pains of the colic; his feet swelled with a dropsy; he had an inflammation in the lower part of his belly: a putrefaction in his privy parts, which bred worms; a frequency and difficulty of breathing, and convulsions in all his members; he had a voracious appetite, a stinking breath, and his intestines abounded with ulcers."
2. "Now some time after his death, behold an angel of the Lord appeareth in a dream to Joseph in Egypt."
3. "Which shows the watchful providence of God, and the useful ministry of angels, concerned in the preservation of the infant Jesus."
