# Matthew 21:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this Bible verse, Jesus demonstrates his authority and compassion by healing the blind and lame in the temple. By doing so, he proclaims himself as the Messiah and fulfills the prophecies of the prophets. Jesus transforms the temple from a place of religious rituals to a house of mercy, offering healing to the marginalized. Through these miracles, Jesus confirms the truth of his teachings and displays his power to restore and bring about restoration.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Blind and lame." This phrase highlights the presence of those who were physically disabled and in need of healing. It emphasizes Jesus' compassion and willingness to bring restoration to those who were marginalized and suffering.

2. "‘A house of prayer’ becomes a house of mercy." This quote emphasizes the transformation that took place in the temple. Instead of being solely focused on religious rituals, Jesus turned it into a place of compassion and mercy, where people could find healing and restoration.

3. "The making it ‘a den of robbers’ was unmerciful." This statement highlights the contrast between the actions of the religious leaders who exploited the people for their own gain and Jesus' merciful nature. It emphasizes that true worship involves caring for the needs of others rather than exploiting them.
