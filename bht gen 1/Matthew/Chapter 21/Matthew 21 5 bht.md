## BHT:
In this verse, the humble state of Jesus is emphasized as he enters Jerusalem. The commentators describe him as choosing to ride on a common beast-of-burden, emphasizing his meekness and humility. They point out that this choice of transportation is in contrast to the pride associated with a horse. The verse holds significance for believers, as it is seen as the fulfillment of a prophecy and a source of complete joy, as the Redeemer enters Jerusalem in a prominent yet gentle manner.
