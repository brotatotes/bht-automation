### Philip Schaff:
1. "The Jews referred it to the Messiah, since no objection was raised at this point."
2. "'My lord' implies superiority, not only to David himself, but to his own royal race and the people of Israel."

### John Gill:
1. "By 'my Lord', the person spoken to, the Messiah is designed, who was David's 'Adon', or Lord, by right both of creation and redemption."
2. "The words 'sit thou on my right hand'...expressive of the exaltation, dignity, power, and authority of the Messiah."
3. "That these words were spoken of the Messiah, and therefore pertinently cited, and properly applied to him, by Jesus, is evident from the silence of the Pharisees."

### John Wesley:
