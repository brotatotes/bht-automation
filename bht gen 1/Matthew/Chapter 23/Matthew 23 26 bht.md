# Matthew 23:26 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, Jesus rebukes the Pharisees for their hypocrisy and external focus. He urges them to prioritize the cleanliness of their hearts and minds, highlighting that true purity comes from within. Jesus also emphasizes the importance of generosity and acts of kindness in purifying oneself. The commentators emphasize the need for inner purity and the significance of embodying the teachings of the prophets, rather than just paying lip service to them. This verse calls for a genuine transformation of the heart and a sincere commitment to living out the principles of faith.

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "Thou blind Pharisee, cleanse first that which is within the cup and platter, that the outside of them may be clean also." - Jesus challenges the Pharisees to focus on the condition of their hearts and minds, recognizing that true cleanliness comes from within.

2. "He to whom belongs the outer life, and of right demands its subjection to Himself, is the inner man less His?" - Jesus emphasizes that the inner life is just as important as the outer life, and that God desires to have authority over both.

3. "But rather give alms of such things as ye have, and behold, all things are clean unto you." - Jesus teaches that acts of generosity and kindness can purify and cleanse a person, highlighting the importance of selflessness and compassion in the eyes of God.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Thou blind Pharisee. 'Blind,' failing to see that the great matter should come first." 
2. "Cleanse first. Begin with inward purity." 
3. "Outward morality is very important, but it naturally follows purity of heart. The former without the latter is not real morality."

### John Gill:
1. "Well might Christ call such an one a blind Pharisee, who was so scrupulously careful to cleanse his cup and platter; and yet made no conscience of filling them with what was gotten in an unjust way, and so defiled himself and them."
2. "Cleanse first that which is within the cup and platter, that the outside of them may be clean also: get food and drink in an honest way, remove all extortion and oppression out of thine hands, and luxury and intemperance from thy table."
3. "The great concern of all men should be inward purity; that their hearts be purified by faith in the blood of Christ, and sprinkled from an evil conscience by the same; that principles of grace and holiness be formed in them by the Spirit of God."

### John Wesley:
1. "Ye build the tombs of the prophets" - This quote highlights the superficiality of the people's actions. They honor the prophets by constructing tombs for them, but fail to truly understand and follow their teachings.

2. "And that is all, for ye neither observe their sayings" - This quote emphasizes the lack of obedience and adherence to the words of the prophets. The people may show outward respect, but they do not truly internalize or live out the teachings they claim to honor.

3. "nor imitate their actions" - This quote points out the hypocrisy of the people. They may honor the prophets with physical memorials, but they fail to embody the same righteousness and integrity that the prophets exemplified in their lives.
