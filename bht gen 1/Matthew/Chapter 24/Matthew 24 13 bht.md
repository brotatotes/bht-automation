### Henry Alford:
1. "The primary meaning of this seems to be, that whosoever remained faithful till the destruction of Jerusalem, should be preserved from it."
2. "No Christian, that we know of, perished in the siege or after it."
3. "It has ulterior meanings, according to which τέλος will signify, to an individual, the day of his death, his martyrdom, as in the case of some of those here addressed, to the Church, endurance in the faith to the end of all things."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He that perseveres in bearing these persecutions to the end of the wars shall be safe. God will protect his people from harm, so that not a hair of the head shall perish."
2. "He that bears afflictions and persecutions faithfully, that constantly adheres to his religion, and does not shrink until death shall be saved, or shall enter heaven."
3. "In your patience possess ye your souls" - that is, keep your souls "patient;" keep proper possession of patience as your own. It is a part of religion to teach it, and in these trying times let it not depart from you.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The Christians were saved from the horrors attending the destruction of Jerusalem. But the principle is a general one."
2. "For the individual, ‘the end’ is the day of his death; for the Church, it is the Advent of Christ, the end of all things."
3. "Over against the apostasy of ‘the many’ we have the faithfulness of the few, in spite of false teaching, in spite of prevailing wickedness, an endurance in love."

### John Gill:
1. "But he that shall endure to the end...shall be saved." 
2. "In the profession of faith in Christ...and in holiness of life and conversation."
3. "Those that believed in Christ...were preserved from the general calamity."

### John Wesley:
1. "The endurance required of the disciples is not simply a matter of physical stamina, but of remaining faithful to Jesus and his teachings in the face of opposition and persecution." 

2. "These verses remind us that following Jesus is not always easy or popular. It requires a steadfast commitment to the truth, even when it means facing rejection and hostility from others." 

3. "The promise of Jesus is that those who endure to the end will be saved. This is a reminder that our salvation is not based on our own efforts or accomplishments, but on our faithfulness to Jesus and our willingness to persevere in following him."
