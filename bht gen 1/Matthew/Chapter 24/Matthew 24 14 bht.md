### Henry Alford:
1. "The Gospel had been preached through the whole ‘orbis terrarum,’ and every nation had received its testimony, before the destruction of Jerusalem."
2. "This was necessary not only as regarded the Gentiles, but to give to God’s people the Jews, who were scattered among all these nations, the opportunity of receiving or rejecting the preaching of Christ."
3. "The apostasy of the latter days, and the universal dispersion of missions, are the two great signs of the end drawing near."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The evidence that this was done is to be chiefly derived from the New Testament, and there it is clear."
2. "This preaching the gospel indiscriminately to 'all' the Gentiles shall be a proof to them, or a witness, that the division between the Jews and Gentiles was about to be broken down."
3. "When, therefore, God sent the gospel to all other people, it was proof, or 'a witness unto them,' that the special Jewish economy was at an end."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The term 'world' in this verse refers to the entire inhabited globe, emphasizing the universal scope of God's love and salvation."

2. "By using the word 'world,' the author highlights the fact that God's love extends to all people, regardless of their nationality, race, or social status."

3. "The phrase 'inhabited earth' reminds us that God's plan of redemption encompasses every corner of the globe, leaving no one outside of His reach."

### John Calvin:
1. "Whatever may be the contrivances of Satan, and how numerous soever may be the multitudes which he carries away, yet the gospel will maintain its ground till it be spread through the whole world."
2. "He describes this to be the end of preaching; for although God has never left himself without witness, yet it was a testimony remarkable beyond all others when he revealed himself in Christ."
3. "Let us, therefore, learn that, wherever the gospel is preached, it is as if God himself came into the midst of us, and solemnly and expressly besought us, that we may not wander in darkness."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The preaching of the gospel throughout the Roman world preceded the end of the Jewish state; the promulgation of the gospel throughout the whole world will be the sign of the end of this world."
2. "To them, if they accept; against them, if they reject it. It is not revealed here, which result will preponderate."
3. "The universal extension of missions, no less than the great apostasy, is a sign of the approach of our Redeemer."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Shall be preached (κερυχθησετα). Heralded in all the inhabited world." 
2. "It is not here said that all will be saved nor must this language be given too literal and detailed an application to every individual." 
3. "It is not a promise that every person will hear the gospel, but rather a statement that the gospel will be proclaimed throughout the world."

### John Gill:
1. "And this Gospel of the kingdom... gives the best account of the glories of it." 
2. "Shall be preached in all the world... and churches were planted in most places, through the ministry of it."
3. "For a witness unto all nations... as a testimony of Christ and salvation, unto all such as should believe in him."

### John Wesley:
1. "This Gospel shall be preached in all the world" - Not universally: this is not done yet: but in general through the several parts of the world, and not only in Judea.
2. "And this was done by St. Paul and the other apostles, before Jerusalem was destroyed."
3. "Josephus's History of the Jewish War is the best commentary on this chapter. It is a wonderful instance of God's providence, that he, an eyewitness, and one who lived and died a Jew, should, especially in so extraordinary a manner, be preserved, to transmit to us a collection of important facts, which so exactly illustrate this glorious prophecy, in almost every circumstance."
