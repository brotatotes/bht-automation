# Matthew 24:31 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this Bible verse, we find a description of a gathering of God's people, symbolized by the sound of a trumpet. Commentators highlight that this gathering is different from the final judgment and refers to the assembly of the elect, called forth by angels and messengers sent by God and Christ. The trumpet, historically used to signal the march of Israel's army, now represents the summons of believers before their King. Ultimately, this gathering signifies the hope of the faithful, emphasizing the importance of endurance in faith and the transformative power of love in their lives.

### Henry Alford:
1. "The voice of the Archangel and the trump of God are distinguished from one another."
2. "This is not the great Trumpet of the general Resurrection, except in so far as that may be spoken of as including also the first resurrection."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The blowing of trumpets was anciently the signal for the host of Israel on their march through the desert." 
2. "Hence the symbolism of the New Testament. Jehovah's people shall be summoned before their king by sound of trumpet." 
3. "Compare the proclamation of Christ as king at the trumpet of the seventh angel, Revelation 11:15."

### John Calvin:
1. "He describes the effect of his power, that he will send his angels to gather his elect from the most distant parts of the world."
2. "Christ speaks hyperbolically, in order to show that the elect, even though they were carried away from the earth and scattered in the air, will again be gathered."
3. "Whenever, therefore, we perceive the Church scattered... let us learn to turn our eyes to this gathering of the elect."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The trumpet, used to call assemblies together, refers to some means employed in connection with the actual 'angels' to gather Christ's people together." 

2. "This sound of the trumpet is to be distinguished from the great Trumpet of the Judgment day, since both this verse and Matthew 24:40-41, point to a gathering out from the world, while at the great Judgment all are collected." 

3. "A gathering, either of living and raised believers into one place, or of the saints hitherto scattered among the nations into one organization."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "With a great sound of a trumpet" - The trumpet was the signal employed to call the hosts of Israel to march as to war and is common in prophetic imagery (Isaiah 27:13).
2. "The coming of the son of man is not to be identified with the judgment of Jerusalem but rather forms its preternatural background" - Bruce.
3. "Clearly 'the coming of the son of man is not to be identified with the judgment of Jerusalem'" - Bruce.

### John Gill:
1. "Not the angels, i.e. ministering spirits, so called, not from their nature, but their office, as being sent forth by God and Christ; but men angels, or messengers, the ministers and preachers of the Gospel, whom Christ would call, qualify, and send forth into all the world of the Gentiles, to preach his Gospel, and plant churches there still more." 

2. "The Gospel being rich and precious, all of a piece, useful for gathering souls to Christ, and to his churches; to direct saints in their journey to Canaan's land; to encourage them to fight the Lord's battles; and is a joyful sound, being a sound of love, grace, and mercy, peace, pardon, righteousness, life and salvation, by Christ."

3. "By the ministration of the Gospel; the Spirit of God accompanying it with his power, and grace, the ministers of the word should gather out of the world unto Christ, and to his churches, such persons as God had, before the foundation of the world, chosen in Christ, unto salvation, through sanctification of the Spirit, and belief of the truth."

### John Wesley:
1. "His elect" refers to those who have remained faithful to their belief in God and have shown their love through their actions.
2. The phrase "endured to the end" emphasizes the importance of perseverance in maintaining one's faith.
3. The faith that "worketh by love" highlights the idea that true faith is not just a belief, but also a commitment to love and serve others.
