# Matthew 24:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Believers will experience affliction and hatred from all nations, facing unfair treatment and blame for societal troubles. This opposition, which may even lead to imprisonment and martyrdom, is a consequence of their commitment to the gospel and their identification as Christians. Despite the promise of persecution, believers must endure it for the sake of Christ's name, recognizing that this universal hatred by the world is a testament to their faithfulness.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Albert Barnes:
1. "To be afflicted - By persecution, imprisonment, scourging, etc."
2. "Ye shall be hated of all nations - This was fulfilled then, and has been in all ages."
3. "For my name’s sake - On account of attachment to me, or because you bear my 'name as Christians.'"

### John Calvin:
1. "It is painful and distressing enough in itself that the children of God should be afflicted in such a manner as not to be distinguished from the reprobate and the despisers of God." 
2. "But Christ treats here strictly of the afflictions which the disciples had to endure on account of the gospel."
3. "He intended to warn his disciples that the doctrine of the gospel, of which they were to be witnesses and messengers, would never be pleasant or agreeable to the world."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "They shall deliver you up, etc. Soon literally fulfilled. But it may now be referred to the spirit of persecution, always latent in the world and to break out in the last times."
2. "Hated of all the nations. The Roman historian Tacitus speaks of the early Christians as a hated race of men."
3. "But to be universally abhorred is not a proof of being a Christian. It must be for my name’s sake. This hatred has not ceased; it will probably manifest itself anew in startling form."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Ye shall be hated...to emphasize the continuous process of the linear action."
2. "For tribulation...the oppression (pressure) that the Christians received."
3. "For my name's sake...an honour to be dishonoured for the Name's sake."

### John Gill:
1. "Our Lord proceeds to acquaint his disciples, what should befall them in this interval; and quite contrary to their expectations, who were looking for a temporal kingdom, and worldly grandeur, assures them of afflictions, persecutions, and death."
2. "The Jews continuing in their obstinacy and unbelief, would deliver them up to the civil magistrates, to be scourged and imprisoned by them."
3. "And shall kill you; as the two James', Peter, Paul, and even all the apostles, excepting John, who suffered martyrdom, and that before the destruction of Jerusalem."

### John Wesley:
1. "Then shall they deliver you up to affliction — As if ye were the cause of all these evils." - This quote highlights the unfair treatment and persecution that believers may face, being blamed for the troubles and challenges in society.

2. "And ye shall be hated of all nations — Even of those who tolerate all other sects and parties." - This quote emphasizes the universal nature of the hatred and opposition that believers may encounter, even in societies that claim to be tolerant of diverse beliefs.

3. "But in no nation will the children of the devil tolerate the children of God." - This quote underscores the stark contrast between the followers of God and those who oppose them, suggesting that there will always be a fundamental conflict between the two.
