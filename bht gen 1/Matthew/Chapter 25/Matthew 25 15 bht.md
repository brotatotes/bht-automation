# Matthew 25:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this Bible verse, commentators emphasize the importance of recognizing and utilizing our unique God-given abilities. Each person has been entrusted with gifts that vary in capacity and character. The verse clarifies that these talents are not just natural or external in nature, but also include spiritual gifts and ministerial abilities. The parable highlights the necessity of using our talents rather than allowing them to go to waste. Ultimately, the verse points to the future return of the master, symbolizing Christ's eventual return to judge and reward accordingly.

1. "The same fidelity is required of all, and equally rewarded. And thus there is perfect equity."

### Marvin Vincent:

2. "Each individual possesses their own distinct set of skills and talents, enabling them to contribute in their own unique way."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The greater value of the talent suggests the superiority of spiritual endowments to merely official ones."
2. "Even spiritual gifts are regulated by personal susceptibility and capacity."
3. "The order of the parable is that demanded by its form; but the Ascension (the departure) preceded the day of Pentecost (the distribution of gifts)."

### John Gill:
1. "By these talents, special grace is not meant; for the parable speaks not of what was wrought in these servants, but of what was committed to their trust, and of what might lie useless by them, and be taken away from them."
2. "Outward gifts are designed by the talents; and these not merely the gifts of natural knowledge and riches, the gifts of nature and of providence; nor the external ministry of the word, Gospel ordinances, and opportunities of enjoying them; but ministerial gifts, such as fit and qualify men to be preachers of the Gospel."
3. "For the sense is, not that he gave these talents, or gifts, according to the different capacities, abilities, stations, and employments of these men; but according to that power and authority which he, as Mediator, had, to dispense these gifts to each as he would; to some more, others less, as he knew would best serve his interest and kingdom."

### John Wesley:
1. "And who knows whether (all circumstances considered) there be a greater disproportion than this, in the talents of those who have received the most, and those who have received the fewest?"
2. "According to his own ability - The words may be translated more literally, according to his own mighty power."
3. "And immediately took his journey - To heaven."
