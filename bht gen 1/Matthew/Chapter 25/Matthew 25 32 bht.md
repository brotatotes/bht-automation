# Matthew 25:32 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, the judgment day is described, where all nations of the world will be gathered. The righteous believers in Christ will be separated from the wicked. The judgment will include all individuals, spanning across every nation, tribe, and family. The gathering will be carried out by the powerful angels who have come with Christ, enabling them to accomplish this profound task. The judge, with His discerning understanding, will distinguish between those who have genuine inner faith and those who only profess outwardly, highlighting the importance of true grace in the hearts of believers.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The expression πάντα τὰ ἔθνη implies all the nations of the world, as distinguished from the ἐκλεκτοί already gathered to Him." 
2. "Among these are 'the other sheep which He has, not of this fold,' John 10:16."
3. "The sheep are those referred to in Romans 2:7; Romans 2:10; the goats in ib. Romans 2:8-9, where this same judgment according to works is spoken of."

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "That this should be understood to mean the heathen nations, or all except believers in Christ, will seem amazing to any simple reader."
2. "Their chief argument is, the impossibility of any that ever knew the Lord Jesus wondering, at the Judgment Day, that they should be thought to have done—or left undone—anything 'unto Christ.'"
3. "If it be said, But how can this be the general judgment, if only those within the Christian pale be embraced by it?—we answer, What is here described, as it certainly does not meet the case of all the family of Adam, is of course so far not general."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "At his coming to judgment the world will be burned up."
2. "The dead in Christ...will be raised up from their graves."
3. "All the wicked will rise and come forth to judgment."

These quotes highlight the key elements of the commentary, emphasizing the future judgment and separation of the righteous and the wicked.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The whole human race; though the word is generally employed in the New Testament to denote Gentiles as distinguished from Jews."
2. "Nations are regarded as gathered collectively; but in contemplating the act of separation the Lord regards the individuals."
3. "Goats are an appropriate figure, because the goat was regarded as a comparatively worthless animal."

### John Calvin:
1. "He employs large and splendid titles for extolling his kingdom, that the disciples may learn to expect a different kind of happiness from what they had imagined."
2. "Christ extends much farther the benefit of the redemption brought by him, for he will be the Judge of the whole world."
3. "In short, he declares that his kingdom will be fully established, when the righteous shall have obtained a crown of glory, and when the wicked shall have received the reward which they deserved."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "All the nations, all mankind."
2. "Christ is really the Shepherd of all mankind."
3. "Together in the pasture, they are now divided."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "A 'reduced' Christ has trouble with all the Gospels, not merely with the Fourth Gospel, and no less with Q and Mark than with Matthew and Luke."
2. "This is a majestic picture with which to close the series of parables about readiness for the second coming. Here is the program when he does come."
3. "The shepherd stands at the gate and taps the sheep to go to the right and the goats to the left."

### John Gill:
1. "That is, all that have professed the Christian religion in all the nations of the world...though it is also true of every individual of mankind of every nation, tribe and family, of every sex, age, and state, that ever has been, is, or will be."
2. "This collection of them before Christ, the righteous judge, will be made by the holy angels, who will come with him for this purpose; and being mighty, as they are, will be able to accomplish great a work."
3. "But then the judge, who is of quick understanding, will easily discern the one from the other; such as have the oil of grace in the vessels of their hearts, together with their lamps, from such as have only the outward visible lamp of a profession, but destitute of the grace of God."
