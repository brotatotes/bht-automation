## BHT:
In this verse, the solemn mention of the sacrifices serves as a fixed point in understanding the chronological arrangement of the Passion. Jesus speaks of his betrayal as if it has already occurred, emphasizing its certain and determined nature according to God's purpose and prophecy. The verse also hints at the approaching Passover, which was a significant Jewish festival involving the sacrificial feast and unleavened bread. Furthermore, it provides insights into the manner in which the Passover was celebrated, including the consumption of unleavened bread, bitter herbs, and the sharing of a broken loaf and cup of thanksgiving.

### Henry Alford:
1. "The solemn mention of them in this connexion is equivalent to a declaration from Himself, if it were needed, of the identity, both of time and meaning, of the two sacrifices; and serves as the fixed point in the difficult chronological arrangement of the history of the Passion."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The 'opportunity' which he sought, therefore, was one in which the multitude would not see him, or could not rescue the Saviour."
2. "To betray him - The word 'betray' commonly means to deliver into the hands of an enemy by treachery or breach of trust; to do it while friendship or faithfulness is 'professed.' All this took place in the case of Judas."
3. "But the word in the original does not necessarily imply this. It means simply to 'deliver up,' or to give into their hands."

### Marvin Vincent:

2. "The Son of Man is betrayed according to the divine decree."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "After two days. This means, ‘the day after tomorrow,’ according to Jewish usage."
2. "The passover cometh. It was the greatest Jewish festival; a sacrificial feast and a memorial feast of thanksgiving."
3. "The word ‘passover’ is used in a threefold sense in the New Testament: (1.) The paschal lamb itself; (2.) The sacrificial lamb and the supper; (3.) The whole feast of unleavened bread."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Cometh (γινετα). Futuristic use of the present middle indicative."
2. "After two days (μετα δυο ημερας) is just the familiar popular mode of speech."
3. "Thus Jesus sets a definite date for the coming crucifixion which he has been predicting for six months."

### John Gill:
1. "This was said on Tuesday, and on the Thursday following, the passover began. Christ speaks of this as a thing well known to the disciples, as it must be, since it always began on a certain day, the fourteenth of the month Nisan."
2. "Christ speaks of his being betrayed, as if it was already done; not only because it was so near being done, there being but two days before it would be done; but because it was a sure and certain thing, being determined in the purpose of God, and foretold in prophecy that it should be."
3. "The crucifixion and death of Christ were not casual and contingent events, but were determined by the counsel of God, with all circumstances attending. The betraying and delivery of him were by the determinate counsel and foreknowledge of God; and not only his death, but the manner of it by crucifixion, was pointed out in prophecy, and was a certain thing."

### John Wesley:
1. "The manner wherein this was celebrated gives much light to several circumstances that follow."

3. "After supper, he took a thin loaf or cake, which he broke and divided to all at the table, and likewise the cup, usually called the cup of thanksgiving, of which he drank first, and then all the guests."
