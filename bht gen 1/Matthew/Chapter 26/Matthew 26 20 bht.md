### John Calvin:
1. "Not to eat the passover, which they were bound to do standing, as travelers, when they are in haste, are wont to take food hastily, with shoes on their feet, and a staff in their hand."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Even. Luke: 'the hour.' Both point to the regular time."
2. "He was sitting at meat, or, 'reclining at table.'"
3. "The original requirement was, to eat the Passover standing (Exodus 12:11). The Jews altered this when they came to the land of promise and rest."

### John Gill:
1. "Judas was now returned again, and took his place among the disciples, as if he was as innocent, and as friendly, as any of them."
2. "The posture of the Jews, at the passover table especially, was not properly sitting, but reclining, or lying along on coaches, not on their backs, nor on their right side, but on their left."
3. "This custom was so constantly and uniformly observed at the passover, that it is taken particular notice of in the declaration, or showing forth of the passover by the master of the family."

### John Wesley:
1. "Jesus knew that the time had come for him to fulfill his purpose, and he chose this moment to share a final meal with his disciples. This act of communion not only symbolized the unity and fellowship among them, but also foreshadowed the ultimate sacrifice Jesus was about to make for humanity." 

2. "In breaking bread and sharing the cup, Jesus was establishing a new covenant, one that would be sealed with his own blood. This act of self-giving love would forever change the course of history and provide a way for all people to be reconciled with God."

3. "By participating in this sacred meal, the disciples were not only remembering the past, but also anticipating the future. They were being invited to partake in the ongoing work of God's kingdom, to continue the mission of Jesus in the world."

4. "This meal was a powerful reminder that Jesus was not just a teacher or a prophet, but the Son of God who came to offer his life as a ransom for many. It was a moment of profound significance, where the divine and the human intersected in a profound and mysterious way."
