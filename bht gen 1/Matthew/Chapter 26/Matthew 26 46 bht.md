### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "He is at hand (ηγγικεν). The same verb and tense used of the hour above, present perfect active of εγγιζω, to draw near, the very form used by John the Baptist of the coming of the kingdom of heaven (Matthew 3:2)."

### John Gill:
1. "This was said, partly to arouse his sleepy disciples; and partly to show his love to his Father, and his submission to his will."

3. "A 'behold' is prefixed to this, partly to awaken the attention of his disciples; and partly to express what a horrid, insolent, and unparalleled action that was, Judas was now about to be guilty of."
