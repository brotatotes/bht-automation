# Matthew 26:54 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, Jesus, with a clear understanding of his destiny, willingly refrains from seeking divine intervention or using worldly means to avoid his impending suffering and death. Through his submission and acceptance, Jesus fulfills the prophecies of the Scriptures regarding his sacrificial role as the Messiah. The commentators highlight the necessity and inevitability of Jesus' sufferings, which were predetermined by God's eternal and unchangeable purposes. This verse showcases Jesus' unwavering commitment to fulfilling his mission, even in the face of great trial and the fulfillment of ancient prophecies.

### John Calvin:
1. "By this expression Christ means, that he ought not to attempt any method of escaping death, to which he knew that he was called by the Father."
2. "Christ, with a view to his disciples, properly refers to the testimony which God gave of his will."

### Philip Schaff:

3. "Our Lord's death could not be incidental or accidental. He 'must' suffer."
