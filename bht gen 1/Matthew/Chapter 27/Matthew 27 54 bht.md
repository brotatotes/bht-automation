The centurion, overwhelmed by the crucifixion and convinced of a divine presence, proclaimed, "Truly this was the Son of God." Though his words may have been influenced by the pagan sense of a hero or demigod, it is clear that he recognized Jesus' claim to be the Son of God. ### Marvin Vincent:

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The heathen officer may have used these words in the heathen sense: hero or demigod; but this is not probable."

3. "Only the centurion thus spoke, but as the soldiers 'feared, some decided spiritual effect may have been produced on them also."

### John Gill:

3. "What he said he was, and the Jews charged him with blasphemy, on account of, and condemned him for, as these soldiers might have seen and heard."
