# Matthew 28:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
On a mountain where Jesus had appointed them, the disciples gathered in response to His divine plan. This chosen location held previous significance, having been the site of Jesus' transfiguration. It is remarkable that over five hundred brethren witnessed His appearance, highlighting the widespread impact of Jesus' ministry and the multitude of eyewitnesses to His resurrection. This post-resurrection gathering reflects the intentional and glorious nature of Jesus' presence among His disciples.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "What it was can only be conjectured; but of the two between which opinions are divided—the Mount of the Beatitudes or Mount Tabor—the former is much the more probable."
2. "That the interview here recorded was the same as that referred to in one place only—1 Corinthians 15:6—when 'He was seen of above five hundred brethren at once of whom the greater part remained unto that day, though some were fallen asleep,' is now the opinion of the ablest students of the evangelical history."
3. "Nothing can account for such a number as five hundred assembling at one spot but the expectation of some promised manifestation of their risen Lord: and the promise before His resurrection, twice repeated after it, best explains this immense gathering."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Judas was dead, leaving but eleven of the original number of the apostles." 
2. "This 'appointment' is recorded in Matthew 26:32."
3. "Matthew has omitted many appearances which Jesus made to his disciples which have been recorded by Luke, John, and Paul."

### John Calvin:
1. "Matthew, passing by those occurrences which we have taken out of the other three Evangelists, mentions only in what place the eleven disciples were appointed to the apostolic office."
2. "Matthew has therefore selected what was of the greatest importance to us, namely, that when Christ appeared to the disciples, he likewise commissioned them to be apostles, to convey into every part of the world the message of eternal life."
3. "Though the mountain is not mentioned any where else, yet we conclude that this spot in Galilee was known to Mary."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "It was probably the eighth appearance, preceded by that at the Sea of Galilee." 
2. "An apocryphal tradition of a late date (13th century) says that the mountain was the northern peak of the Mount of Olives, which it is asserted, was called Galilea. But this is a useless attempt at harmonizing the accounts."
3. "There was a good reason for the withdrawal into the district of Galilee, remote from Jerusalem."

### John Gill:
1. "For Judas was not only gone from them, but was dead; so that there were now but eleven of them."
2. "This is generally thought to be Mount Tabor; but of this there is no proof, nor certainty."
3. "A mountain was appointed for this meeting, both for solitariness and for sight; for here it was he was seen by above five hundred brethren at once."

3. "He appeared to above five hundred brethren at once" - This statement reveals the magnitude of Jesus' post-resurrection appearances. It underscores the widespread nature of his ministry and the eyewitness testimony of many who encountered the risen Christ.
