## BHT:
In this Bible verse, an earthquake occurs as the women arrive at the tomb, signifying the dramatic and powerful nature of the events that unfolded. The stone is rolled away, not to let Jesus out, but to allow the women to enter and witness the empty tomb. The appearance of angels, one or two, serves as a divine affirmation of the resurrection and underscores the significant role of heavenly beings in proclaiming this miraculous moment. The entire scene is a representation of the extraordinary supernatural event of Christ's resurrection, conveying the immense power and glory of God.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It does not mean that this was while they were there, or while they were going, but that there 'had been' so violent a commotion as to remove the stone."
2. "The word rendered here as 'earthquake' does not of necessity mean that the convulsion extended to the earth, but only that there had been such a concussion as to remove the stone."
3. "Sat upon it when the keepers saw him, Matthew 28:4. It is not said that he was sitting when he appeared to the women."

### John Calvin:
1. "By many signs the Lord showed the presence of his glory, that he might more fully prepare the hearts of the holy women to reverence the mystery."
2. "It was necessary to remove all doubts, that the divine majesty might be openly and manifestly presented to the eyes of the women."
3. "The raiment and the countenance of the angel...were rays by which the splendor of Godhead was diffused, so as to enable them to perceive that it was not a mortal man that stood near them."

These quotes highlight the purpose of the signs and wonders surrounding the resurrection of Jesus, emphasizing the importance of removing doubts and revealing the divine presence to the women. They also describe the angel's appearance as a manifestation of God's glory, helping the women recognize the supernatural nature of the event.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The earthquake is connected with the sudden rolling away of the great stone by the angel."
2. "The tomb was opened for the sake of the women and the disciples, not to allow the Lord to pass out."
3. "This is to be distinguished from all the other angelic appearances."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "The Resurrection of Jesus is one of the great supernatural events of all time."
2. "The earth, which trembled with sorrow at the Death of Christ as it were leaped for joy at His Resurrection."
3. "The stone was rolled away not to let the Lord out, but to let the women in to prove the fact of the empty tomb."

### John Gill:
1. "It was an emblem of the shaking of the earth by the preaching of the Gospel, the sound of which was now to go after Christ's resurrection to the ends of the world."
2. "This might be done, that way might be made for the risen body of Christ to pass out of the sepulchre."
3. "Moreover, this might be done, that the women might have access to the sepulchre, and enter into it, which was the thing they were concerned about by the way, who should roll away the stone for them."

### John Wesley:

2. "St. Luke and St. John speak of two angels that appeared" - The presence of multiple angels emphasizes the significance of the resurrection event and highlights the divine intervention in the empty tomb.
3. "It seems as if only one of them had appeared sitting on the stone without the sepulchre" - This detail suggests that the angel's presence was not limited to the physical space of the tomb, but extended beyond it, signifying the transcendence of Christ's resurrection.
