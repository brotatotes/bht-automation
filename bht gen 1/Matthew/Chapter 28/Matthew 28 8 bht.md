### Albert Barnes:
1. "Joyful at the 'news,' and wishing to impart it to all, they fled to find the disciples, and to tell them that the Lord was risen."
2. "Fear because of: 1. The wonderful scenes which they had witnessed the stone rolled away, and the presence of an angel; 2. A confused state of mind, apprehensive, perhaps, that it might not, after all, be true."
3. "The news was too good to be credited at once, yet they had sufficient faith in it to fill them with great and unexpected joy."

### John Calvin:
1. "By these words Matthew means that they were indeed gladdened by what the angel told them, but, at the same the were struck with fear, so that they were held in suspense between joy and perplexity."
2. "For there are sometimes opposite feelings in the hearts of the godly, which move them alternately in opposite directions, until at length the peace of the Spirit brings them into a settled condition."
3. "Yet there is some diversity in the words of Mark, that they fled, seized with trembling and amazement, so that through fear they were dismayed."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The word 'quickly' is not against this, for the events must have taken place in rapid succession."
2. "With fear and great joy. A natural state of mingled feeling, in view of what they had seen and heard."
3. "Fear at what they had seen, joy at what they had heard, and both mingled because the latter seemed too good to be true."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "A touch of life was this as the excited women ran quickly." 
2. "They had the greatest piece of news that it was possible to have." 
3. "Mark calls it fear and ecstasy. Anything seemed possible now."

### John Gill:
1. "They went into the sepulchre upon the invitation of the angel, and saw the place where the Lord lay."
2. "With fear and dread, because of the vision they had seen, and with joy at the news of Christ's resurrection."
3. "Their fright, as well as their joy, and their regard to the angel's order, might cause them to run, and make the quicker dispatch."
