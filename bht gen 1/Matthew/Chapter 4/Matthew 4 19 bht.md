# Matthew 4:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This Bible verse entails an invitation from Jesus to his disciples to leave behind their old lives and join him as his followers. By committing to Jesus, the disciples would develop an intimate relationship with him as they walked alongside him and learned directly from him. They would be transformed into fishers of men, tasked with spreading the gospel and bringing people into a relationship with Jesus. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "Follow me" - This invitation from Jesus to his disciples is a call to leave behind their old way of life and follow him as their teacher and leader. It implies a personal and intimate relationship with Jesus, as they would be walking alongside him and learning from him directly.

2. "Come ye after Me" - The phrase "come ye after me" emphasizes the disciples' active participation in following Jesus. It implies a sense of urgency and a willingness to leave everything behind and pursue Jesus wholeheartedly. It also suggests that following Jesus is a journey, a continuous process of growth and transformation.

3. "I will make you fishers of men" - This metaphor used by Jesus signifies the disciples' new mission and purpose. Just as fishermen catch fish to sustain and provide for others, the disciples would be called to "catch" people, leading them to salvation and spiritual nourishment. It highlights the disciples' role in spreading the message of Jesus and bringing others into a relationship with him.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Fishers of men" refers to the role of ministers and preachers in spreading the gospel and bringing people to Christ.
2. The phrase implies that just as fishermen cast their nets wide to catch fish, ministers must cast the net of the gospel wide to reach as many souls as possible.
3. The duty of a fisherman is to patiently wait, persevere, and work diligently to catch fish. Similarly, ministers must be patient, persistent, and dedicated in their efforts to bring people to Christ.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The call of Jesus to follow Him, 1. A call to faith; 2. A call to labor; 3. A call to suffering and cross-bearing; 4. A call to our blessed home." (Lange.)
2. "His power, not their ability, made them what they became." 
3. "The main points of resemblance cannot be mistaken, such as the value of the object, the necessity of skill as well as strength, of vigilance as well as labor, with an implication, if not an explicit promise, of abundance and success in their new fishery." (J. A. Alexander.)

### John Gill:
1. "And he saith unto them, follow me... be a disciple of mine." 
2. "You shall be fishers still, but in a higher sense; and in a far more noble employment, and to much better purpose." 
3. "The fish they were to catch were the souls of men, both among Jews and Gentiles; of whose conversion and faith they were to be the happy instruments."
