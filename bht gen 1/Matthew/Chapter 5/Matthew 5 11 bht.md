## BHT:
In this verse, the actions described are the expansion of past persecution. It speaks of enduring persecution for righteousness' sake, not reviling in return, and blessing those who revile. The verse comforts believers, reminding them that they are united with Christ and that they should not lose courage even when faced with slander and persecution. It encourages patience, meekness, and a willingness to do good to those who mistreat them. The verse implies that the suffering is for Christ's sake and promises a reward in heaven for those who endure.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "He identifies Himself and His cause with that of righteousness, binding up the cause of righteousness in the world with the reception of Himself."
2. "Would Moses, or David, or Isaiah, or Paul have so expressed themselves? Never. Doubtless they suffered for righteousness' sake. But to have called this 'their sake,' would, as every one feels, have been very unbecoming."
3. "He that speaks, being Righteousness incarnate, when He so speaks, speaks only like Himself."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "But, being reviled, he reviled not again... and thus being reviled, we should bless." 
2. "An emphasis should be laid on the word falsely in this passage. It is not blessed to have evil spoken of us if we deserve it; but if we deserve it not, then we should not consider it as a calamity."
3. "When thus reviled or persecuted, we are to be meek, patient, humble; not angry; not reviling again; but endeavoring to do good to our persecutors and slanderers."

### John Calvin:
1. "Christ intended to comfort those who believe in him; that they may not lose courage, even though they see themselves to be detestable in the eyes of the world."
2. "Christ knew that there is no class of men more envenomed than hypocrites, and foresaw with what furious madness the enemies of the Gospel would attack his small and despised flock."
3. "It was therefore his will to furnish them with a sure defense, that they might not give way, though an immense mass of reproaches were ready to overwhelm them."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The personal application; a prophecy also, since when men shall revile you, etc., implies that this will happen."
2. "The first revilers and persecutors were the unbelieving Jews, here referred to indefinitely."
3. "Those only suffer for righteousness’ sake, who suffer for Christ’s sake; elsewhere we learn more distinctly; those only are blessed with righteousness who are blessed for Christ’s sake."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "The bad things said of Christ's followers shall be untrue and that they are slandered for Christ's sake."
2. "Both things must be true before one can wear a martyr's crown and receive the great reward in heaven."
3. "No prize awaits one there who deserves all the evil said of him and done to him here."

### John Gill:
1. "Men should 'revile' them, speak very reproachfully of them, brand them with infamy, and load them with disgrace."
2. "The treatment they meet with is on his account, and the same that he himself met with; the like reproaches fell on him."
3. "The curse causeless returns to him that curseth."
4. "They that suffer injury, and do it not; who hear reproach, and do not return it; who act from love, and rejoice in chastisements, of them the Scripture says, 'let them that love him', &c. Judges 5:31.'"
