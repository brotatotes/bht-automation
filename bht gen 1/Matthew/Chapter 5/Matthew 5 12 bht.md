## BHT:
In this verse, commentators highlight the joyful and transcendent nature of the reward mentioned. They emphasize that it is not a mere earthly or temporary reward, but something that surpasses human imagination. It is a gift from God, given through His grace, to those who diligently seek after Him and strive to live a holy life. The reward serves as a reminder of God's love and faithfulness, both in the present and in eternity, and brings a deep sense of fulfillment and happiness to those who receive it.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Regard it as a great privilege thus to be persecuted and to suffer - a thing not to be mourned over, but as among the chief blessings of life."
2. "To those who suffer most, God imparts the highest rewards."
3. "The flame, therefore, kindled to consume the martyr, has also been a bright light, showing the truth and power of the gospel of Jesus."

### John Calvin:
1. "Rejoice ye, and leap for joy...we there behold vast grounds of joy, which dispel sadness."
2. "Christ pronounces those only to be happy who are employed in defending a righteous cause."
3. "Christ had good reason for warning them, that, as soon as they succeeded to the place of the prophets, they must sustain the same contests in which the prophets were formerly engaged."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Rejoice, etc. An exhortation based on the declaration of blessedness in Matthew 5:11, and confirming it. Needful, because the prospect of persecution is far from awakening joy."
2. "For great is your reward in heaven. The reason both for rejoicing and for the blessedness. 'Reward,' i.e., recompense; but of grace, not of debt."
3. "For so persecuted they, i.e., the unbelieving Jews, the prophets who were before you. Not an express assertion that the disciples were prophets. It, however, puts them on the same level, establishes the connection between the Old and the New Testaments, showing that the old antagonism remains."

### John Gill:
1. "Rejoice and be exceeding glad, because of the honor put upon them, the glory they bring to Christ and his cause, by cheerfully suffering for it."
2. "Great is your reward in heaven; not of debt, but of grace; for there is no proportion or comparison between what the saints suffer for Christ, and the glory that shall be revealed in them by him."
3. "Saints must not expect their reward here, but hereafter, when God himself will be their reward; he will be all in all; Christ and all his glory, glory and all the riches of it will be the reward of the inheritance, and which must needs be a 'great' one."

### John Wesley:
1. "The reward mentioned here is not just a temporal or earthly reward, but something that goes beyond what we can experience in this life. It is a reward that is eternal and transcendent, bringing a joy and fulfillment that surpasses anything we can imagine."

2. "Holiness itself brings its own inherent happiness and satisfaction, but the reward mentioned here is an added bonus, a divine gift that is bestowed upon those who diligently seek after God and strive to live a holy life."

3. "This reward is not earned through our own efforts or merits, but is given to us by God's grace. It is a testament to His love and faithfulness towards His people, and a reminder that He is a God who rewards those who earnestly seek Him."

4. "The reward of holiness is not just a future hope, but something that can be experienced even in the present. It is a taste of the joy and blessings that await us in eternity, a foretaste of the abundant life that God has prepared for those who love Him."
