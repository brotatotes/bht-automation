# Matthew 5:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, Jesus emphasizes the significance of observing and teaching the commandments. Those who dismiss or distort the importance of God's laws will be considered insignificant in the kingdom of heaven. Conversely, those who diligently follow and teach the commandments will be esteemed as great. Jesus prioritizes both faithful practice and the responsibility to pass on God's teachings. Those who neglect this duty will be excluded from the rewards of the kingdom, highlighting the necessity of actively living out and sharing the truth of God's word.

1. "Whosoever therefore shall break...one of these least commandments...he shall be called the least in the kingdom of heaven."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Christ teaches that in his kingdom they who make this distinction, or who taught that any laws of God might be violated with impunity, should be called least; while they should be held in high regard who observed all the laws of God without distinction."
2. "The meaning of this passage seems to be this: in the kingdom of heaven, that is, in the kingdom of the Messiah, or in the church which he is about to establish, he that breaks the least of these commandments shall be in no esteem, or shall not be regarded as a proper religious teacher."
3. "He that teaches that all the law of God is binding, and that the whole of it should be obeyed, without attempting to specify what is most important, shall be a teacher worthy of his office, and shall be called great."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Whosoever, therefore, because of this permanent character of the law."
2. "Shall break, or at any time may break, one of these least commandments, the smallest part of this law, or, in the wider sense, of this revelation which God has made, and shall teach men so, by example or precept, shall be called, recognized as, least in the kingdom of heaven, in the new dispensation He was proclaiming."
3. "He shall be called great. 'He' is emphatic here."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Jesus puts practice before preaching." 
2. "The teacher must apply the doctrine to himself before he is qualified to teach others."
3. "This is Christ's test of greatness."

### John Wesley:
1. "One of the least" - This phrase refers to someone who is considered insignificant or unimportant in the eyes of society. It highlights the paradoxical nature of God's kingdom, where those who are often overlooked or undervalued are given great significance.

2. "Shall teach" - This phrase emphasizes the responsibility of believers to share their knowledge and understanding of God's teachings with others. It implies that true discipleship involves not only personal growth but also the sharing of wisdom and guidance with others.

3. "Shall be the least" - This phrase suggests that those who fail to fulfill their duty of teaching and sharing God's word will be excluded from the rewards and blessings of the kingdom. It serves as a reminder of the importance of actively living out one's faith and fulfilling the call to spread the message of God's love and truth.
