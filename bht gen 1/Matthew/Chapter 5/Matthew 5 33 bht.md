# Matthew 5:33 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, Jesus addresses the issue of swearing and oaths. He condemns the false and light use of oaths, emphasizing that every oath carries an appeal to God. The commentators highlight the misconception by the Jews that only false swearing and swearing by the name of God were forbidden. Jesus clarifies that the commandment goes beyond that, prohibiting swearing by any creature and in everyday conversation. This teaching emphasizes the importance of truthfulness and reverence for God's name in our words and actions.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "These are not the precise words of [the Old Testament], but they express all that it was currently understood to condemn, namely, false swearing."
2. "For even Jehovah is said once and again to have sworn by Himself and our Lord certainly answered upon oath to a question put to Him by the high priest; and the apostle several times, and in the most solemn language, takes God to witness that he spoke and wrote the truth."
3. "Against all this our Lord now speaks decisively; teaching His audience that every oath carries an appeal to God, whether named or not."

### John Calvin:
1. "God condemned in the law not only acts of perjury, but lightness in swearing, which lessens the reverence for his name."
2. "The man who perjures himself is not the only person who takes the name of God in vain. He does so, who idly and contemptuously pronounces the name of God on trivial occasions, or in ordinary conversation."
3. "We are, no doubt, strictly enjoined to perform to the Lord what we have sworn: for he who, after employing the name of God, cheats and deceives his neighbors, does an injury to God as well as to man."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "A summary of the Mosaic precepts in regard to swearing; negatively, Thou shalt not swear falsely; positively, but shalt perform to the Lord thine oaths." 

2. "The twofold mistake of the Jews, answered by our Lord: that only false swearing, and swearing by the name of God, were forbidden." 

3. "They probably considered no oaths binding, save those in which the name of God occurred; this error, though not mentioned, is necessarily opposed."

### John Gill:
1. "The Jews were chiefly, if not only concerned about; little regarding the vanity, only the truth of an oath."
2. "They took swearing vainly, to be the same as swearing falsely; wherefore so long as what they swore was truth, they were not careful whether it was of any importance or not."
3. "These men sinned, in that they swore by the creatures, which they thought they might do, and not sin; and when they had so done, were not under obligation to perform."

### John Wesley:
1. "Our Lord here refers to the promise made to the pure in heart of seeing God in all things, and points out a false doctrine of the scribes, which arose from their not thus seeing God."
2. "What he forbids is, the swearing at all, 1, by any creature, 2, in our ordinary conversation: both of which the scribes and Pharisees taught to be perfectly innocent."
