# Matthew 6:10 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, commentators emphasize the prayer for God's kingdom to come, which signifies the fulfillment of prophetic Scripture and the establishment of the Messianic kingdom. The prayer includes the removal of hindrances, the submission of all people to God's dominion, and the enlightenment of the world through His Word and Spirit. It also expresses the desire for the success of the Gospel, the conversion of God's elect, and the destruction of evil kingdoms. Ultimately, the prayer seeks God's will to be done on earth as it is in heaven, aligning human will with His divine plan.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Thy kingdom here is the fulness of the accomplishment of the kingdom of God, so often spoken of in prophetic Scripture; and by implication, all that process of events which lead to that accomplishment." 
2. "The one for which he contends exclusively, the Messianic kingdom, does in fact include or imply them all." 
3. "Let thy will be done, as in heaven, (so) also on earth."

### John Calvin:
1. "By this prayer we ask, that he may remove all hindrances, and may bring all men under his dominion, and may lead them to meditate on the heavenly life."
2. "We therefore pray that God would exert his power, both by the Word and by the Spirit, that the whole world may willingly submit to him."
3. "The substance of this prayer is, that God would enlighten the world by the light of his Word, — would form the hearts of men, by the influences of his Spirit, to obey his justice, and would restore to order, by the gracious exercise of his power, all the disorder that exists in the world."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The Messiah’s kingdom, which in organized form had not yet come, but was proclaimed by the Lord Himself, as at hand."
2. "For this coming we now pray and the prayer is answered, in part by every success of the gospel, and will be answered entirely when the King comes again."
3. "The idea of human doing is prominent, our will subordinate to God’s will 'As' expresses similarity in kind and completeness."

### John Gill:
1. "Christ alters the expression, leaves out the word 'Messiah', and puts it thus, 'thy kingdom come', to let them know that the Messiah was come; and that it was the kingdom of the Father, in the power of his grace, upon the souls of men, they must pray for and expect."
2. "In this petition the disciples were taught to pray for the success of the Gospel, both among Jews and Gentiles; for the conversion of God's elect, in which the kingdom of God would greatly appear, to the destruction of the kingdom of Satan, and the abolition of the kingdom of the beast, in the latter day."
3. "Thy will be done in earth, as it is in heaven. Christ says 'thy will'; not the will of wicked men, nor the will of Satan, nor a man's own will, but the will of God: by which is meant either his secret will, which is the rule of all his proceedings both in providence and grace; is unknown to us, till facts make it appear; is always fulfilled in heaven and in earth; and sometimes is fulfilled by those who have no regard to his revealed will."
