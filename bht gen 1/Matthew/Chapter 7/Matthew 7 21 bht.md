# Matthew 7:21 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this Bible verse, the passage warns against the hypocrisy of those who claim to follow Christ but do not truly live according to His teachings. It emphasizes that simply professing faith or using religious language is not enough to enter the Kingdom of heaven. True discipleship requires genuine devotion and obedience to God's will, demonstrated through both words and actions. This passage highlights the importance of sincere, heartfelt confession of Jesus as Lord, accompanied by a life that aligns with His teachings.

### Henry Alford:
1. "The doom of the hypocritical false prophets introduces the doom of all hypocrites, and brings on the solemn close of the whole."
2. "Here the Lord sets Himself forth as the Judge in the great day, and at the same time speaks not of τὸ θέλ . μου, but τὸ θέλ . τοῦ πατρός μου: an important and invaluable doctrinal landmark."

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "Not every one that saith unto me, Lord, Lord—the reduplication of the title 'Lord' denoting zeal in according it to Christ." 
2. "Yet our Lord claims and expects this of all His disciples, as when He washed their feet: 'Ye call me Master and Lord: and ye say well; for so I am.'" 
3. "But now He rises higher still—not formally announcing Himself as the Judge, but intimating what men will say to Him, and He to them, when He sits as their final judge."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The power of working miracles had no necessary connection with piety."
2. "God may as well, if he chooses, give the power of raising the dead to a wicked man, as the skill of healing to a wicked physician."
3. "Saving power on the mind is the work of God, and he may convey it by any agency which he chooses."

### John Calvin:
1. "He not only directs his discourse to them, to rouse them from the indifference, in which they lie asleep like drunk people, but also warns believers, not to estimate such masks beyond their proper value."

3. "To do the will of the Father not only means, to regulate their life and manners, but also to believe in Christ, according to that saying, 'This is the will of him that sent me, that every one which seeth the Son, and believeth on him, may have everlasting life.'"

3. "Of all who thus confess, only those doing the will of God shall enter into the Kingdom of heaven."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Sharp contrast between the mere talker and the doer of God's will."
2. "The emphasis is on action rather than words."
3. "It is not enough to simply speak about God's will; one must actively live it out."

3. "He that seeth the Son, looks unto him, ventures on him, commits himself to him, trusts in him, relies on him, and believes on him for righteousness, salvation, and eternal life, he it is that does the will of the Father, and he only."
