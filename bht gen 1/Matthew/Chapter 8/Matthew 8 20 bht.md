### Philip Schaff:
1. "The Son of Man. A term applied to no one else, and often applied by our Lord to himself; used in Daniel 7:13, in reference to the Messiah seen in a vision. The prominent idea is that of the second Adam, but it also implies that Jesus was the Messiah."
2. "Hath not where to lay his head. He did not own a dwelling, as even the foxes and birds do; but we have no reason to believe that He ever suffered from want of a lodging."

### John Wesley:
1. "The Son of man - The expression is borrowed from Daniel 7:13, and is the appellation which Christ generally gives himself: which he seems to do out of humility, as having some relation to his mean appearance in this world."
2. "Hath not where to lay his head - Therefore do not follow me from any view of temporal advantage."
3. "Christ's identification as the Son of man reflects his humility and his association with the humble conditions of his earthly existence."
