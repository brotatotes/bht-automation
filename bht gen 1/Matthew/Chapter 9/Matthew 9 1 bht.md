# Matthew 9:1 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, we see Jesus returning to Capernaum, a city where he performed miracles and taught profound truths, and which held great significance to his ministry. The familiarity and connection Jesus had with Capernaum is evident, as it is referred to as "his own city." This highlights the personal relationship Jesus had with the people there, emphasizing his investment in their lives. Despite their lack of gratitude, Jesus returns to this city, demonstrating his compassion and desire to continue ministering to its inhabitants.

### John Calvin:
1. "This passage shows, that Capernaum was generally believed to be the birth-place of Christ, because his visits to it were frequent."
2. "Luke says that scribes had come from various parts of Judea, who were spectators when Christ healed the paralytic; and at the same time states indirectly, that there were others who also received healing through the grace of Christ."
3. "Another special reason why the Evangelists dwell more on this miracle than on others is, that the scribes were offended at Christ for claiming power and authority to forgive sins; while Christ intended to confirm and seal that authority by a visible sign."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The events of this day are recorded more fully than those of any other during the ministry in Galilee."
2. "These variations of order show the independence of the Evangelist."
3. "The whole section is a vivid sketch of the various forms of weakness and opposition our Lord always encounters."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "His own city" refers to Capernaum, where Jesus spent a significant amount of time during his ministry. This highlights the familiarity and connection Jesus had with the people in this city.

2. Capernaum was not only a physical location for Jesus but also a symbolic representation of his mission. It was a place where Jesus performed many miracles and taught profound truths, making it a central hub for his ministry.

3. The fact that Capernaum was referred to as "his own city" emphasizes the personal relationship Jesus had with the people there. It shows that Jesus was not just a distant figure but someone who was deeply invested in the lives of the people in this community.
