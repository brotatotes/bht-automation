# Matthew 9:27 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Two blind men, with unwavering faith and belief in Jesus as the Messiah, followed him, passionately pleading for his mercy. Their persistent cry of "son of David" revealed their deep conviction that Jesus was the long-awaited deliverer. Drawing from the Messianic prophecy in Isaiah, they hoped and believed that Jesus had the power to restore their sight. Their example teaches us to approach Jesus with confidence, recognizing his identity and placing our trust in his transformative power.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "It is remarkable that in the only other recorded case in which the blind applied to Jesus for their sight, and obtained it, they addressed Him, over and over again, by this one Messianic title, so well known—'Son of David' (Matthew 20:30)."
2. "Can there be a doubt that their faith fastened on such great Messianic promises as this, 'Then the eyes of the blind shall be opened,' &c. (Isaiah 35:5)?"
3. "This appeal to Him, as the Consolation of Israel, to do His predicted office, would fall with great weight upon the ears of Jesus."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The blind men probably followed him with their cry for aid immediately on his leaving the house of Jairus."
2. "By the Son of David the Jews meant the Messiah."
3. "This was therefore a profession of belief, on the part of these blind men, of the Messiahship of Jesus, and, at the same time, the expression of a belief that, being the Messiah, he could heal them."

### John Calvin:
1. "Now Matthew relates that sight was restored to two blind men, but not so speedily as Christ was wont, on many other occasions, to grant relief to the wretched."
2. "Both by action and by words he intended to make trial of their faith; for he holds them in suspense, — nay, passes by as if he did not hear them, — tries their patience, and what root faith had in their heads."
3. "But it may be asked, if a man is convinced of the power of God and of Christ, is that enough to make him a believer?"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Blindness was common in the East, and it was natural that the sufferers consorted."
2. "To follow Him, they need only let the crowd take them along."
3. "The title, 'Son of David,' applied to Christ by all the blind men whose recovery is mentioned by Matthew, certainly implied His Messiahship."

### John Gill:
1. Their persistence and faith serve as an example for us to approach Jesus with confidence and trust in his power to bring about transformation in our lives.

2. "Whence it appears, that they firmly believed, and were fully persuaded, that he was the true Messiah." - The blind men's use of the title "son of David" reveals their deep conviction that Jesus was indeed the long-awaited Messiah. Their unwavering belief in his identity as the promised deliverer shows the importance of recognizing Jesus for who he truly is and placing our faith in him.

3. "Since it had been prophesied of the Messiah, that he should 'open the eyes of the blind'... they might be greatly encouraged to hope and believe they should obtain mercy from him in this respect." - The blind men's knowledge of the Messianic prophecy in Isaiah 35:5 gave them hope and confidence that Jesus had the power to restore their sight. This reminds us of the significance of God's promises and prophecies, which can serve as a source of encouragement and assurance in our own lives.
