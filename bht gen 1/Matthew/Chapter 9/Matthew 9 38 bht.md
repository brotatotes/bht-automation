# Matthew 9:38 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this Bible verse, Jesus instructs his disciples to pray to the Lord of the harvest, emphasizing the urgency and importance of their request. The commentators note that this prayer is necessary because God is the one who raises up and equips faithful ministers. The verse highlights the need to send forth laborers forcefully, expressing the immediate demand to remove obstacles and fulfill the mission. It is acknowledged that becoming a laborer in Christ's harvest is a challenging task that requires divine intervention and a deep commitment to serving God's purposes.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "He [Jesus] asks them to pray for laborers, not because He is unable to find them Himself, but in order to make them more fervent in prayer." (Chrysost. Hom. xxxii. 2, 3, p. 367)

2. "He [Jesus] shows how great the gift is when He says, 'Pray ye therefore the Lord of the harvest,' and by this very expression, though He does not openly declare Himself to be the Lord, He yet makes it manifest that He is the Lord of the harvest." (Chrysost. Hom. xxxii. 2, 3, p. 367)

3. "Hence it is clear that He Himself is the husbandman, Himself the Lord of the harvest, Himself the master of the prophets." (Chrysost. Hom. xxxii. 2, 3, p. 367)

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Pray ye therefore the Lord of the harvest—the great Lord and Proprietor of all."
2. "Compare: 'I am the true vine, and My Father is the husbandman.'"
3. "That he will send forth labourers into his harvest—The word properly means 'thrust forth'; but this emphatic sense disappears in some places, as in Matthew 9:25; John 10:4—'When He putteth forth His own sheep.'"

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Send forth [εκβαλη]...the word is stronger: thrust out, force them out, as from urgent necessity."
2. "This verse emphasizes the urgency and necessity of sending forth or thrusting out. It implies that there is an immediate need to remove or expel something or someone."
3. "The use of the word 'force' suggests that there may be resistance or opposition to the act of sending forth, but it must be done regardless of the obstacles."

### John Calvin:
1. "As no man will of himself become a sincere and faithful minister of the gospel, and as none discharge in a proper manner the office of teacher but those whom the Lord raises up and endows with the gifts of his Spirit..."
2. "Whenever we observe a scarcity of pastors, we must raise our eyes to him to afford the remedy."
3. "There never was greater necessity for offering this prayer than during the fearful desolation of the church which we now see every where around us."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Beseech ye. A strong word."
2. "The harvest included the Gentile nations, for the laborers sent forth at this time afterwards preached to them also."
3. "Real laborers are needed, but only such as God sends forth. This prayer to the Lord of the harvest was first answered in the sending forth of laborers (the Twelve) by Christ."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Jesus turns from the figure of the shepherdless sheep to the harvest field ripe and ready for the reapers."
2. "The verb εκβαλλω really means to drive out, to push out, to draw out with violence or without."
3. "Prayer is the remedy offered by Jesus in this crisis for a larger ministerial supply."

### John Gill:
1. "By 'the Lord of the harvest' is either meant God the Father, whose are all the elect, who has a hearty concern for them, and will have them all gathered in, not one of them shall be left; or the Lord Jesus Christ himself, who has the care and charge of the whole election of grace."
2. "He only is able to furnish with ministerial gifts, to work upon, and powerfully incline the hearts of men to this service, to call and send them forth into it, and to assist and succeed them in it."
3. "The request the disciples are directed to make, concerning these persons for this work, is, that the Lord of the harvest would 'send', or 'thrust' them 'forth'; implying power and efficacy, and authority, on the part of the sender; and backwardness on the part of those that are sent, through modesty."

### John Wesley:
1. "The Lord of the harvest...who alone is able to do it."
2. "It is an employ not pleasing to flesh and blood."
3. "Those who never felt this, never yet knew what it is to be labourers in Christ's harvest."
