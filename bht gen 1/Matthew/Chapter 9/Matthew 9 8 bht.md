# Matthew 9:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, we witness a remarkable display of wonder-working power granted by God to mankind. The people are struck with astonishment and acknowledge the authority and praise of God for this extraordinary event. While the scribes view it as an opportunity to accuse Jesus of blasphemy, the people are incited to glorify God, recognizing His power at work through Jesus. This event serves as a dividing point, revealing the contrasting responses of disbelief and reverence towards Jesus' divine nature.

### Henry Alford:
1. "They regarded this wonder-working as something by God granted to mankind."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "To 'glorify' God, here, means to 'praise him,' or to acknowledge his power."
2. "The expression, 'which had given such power to people,' was a part of 'their' praise."
3. "It expresses no sentiment of the evangelist about the nature of Christ, but is a record of their feelings and their praise."

### John Calvin:
1. "The design of all the Evangelists is to show, that the power of God was not merely acknowledged, but that all were struck with astonishment, and compelled to give glory to God."
2. "The fear, which followed the astonishment, had the effect of preventing them from opposing Christ, and of making them submit to him with reverence as a Prophet of God."
3. "They are no doubt right in saying, that the nature of man received great honor in Christ for the general advantage of the human race: but as they do not perceive him to be God manifested in the flesh, their confession is involved in some error."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Either a religious awe, awakened by the higher character in which Jesus had presented Himself, or a spiritual conflict echoing that between Christ and the scribes."
2. "The result was they glorified God, who had given such power, or ‘authority.’ Power to forgive sins as well as to heal; the two were indissolubly united in the demonstration."
3. "The pardon of the paralytic was a foreshadowing of the rending of the vail of the temple, promising direct intercourse between God and the sinner, yet through the Son of man."

### John Gill:
1. "They marvelled, and glorified God: they were struck with amazement and astonishment at the sight, it being what was strange and unusual."
2. "Concluding it to be more than human; they ascribed it to God; they praised, and adored the divine goodness."
3. "Still looking upon Christ as a mere man, by whom God did these things; not knowing yet the mystery of the incarnation, God manifest in the flesh."

### John Wesley:
1. "What was to the scribes an occasion of blaspheming" - The scribes, who were experts in the law, saw this event as an opportunity to accuse Jesus of blasphemy. They were quick to criticize and condemn. 

2. "Was to the people an incitement to praise God" - On the other hand, the people saw this miraculous event as a reason to praise and glorify God. They recognized the power and authority of Jesus and were filled with awe and wonder.

3. "An occasion of blaspheming...an incitement to praise God" - This stark contrast between the reactions of the scribes and the people highlights the division caused by Jesus' actions. While some chose to blaspheme and reject him, others were moved to worship and acknowledge his divine nature.
