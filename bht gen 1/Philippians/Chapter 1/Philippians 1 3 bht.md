# Philippians 1:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, we find a deep and lasting remembrance of the Philippians, evoking gratitude from the apostle Paul. Their impact on him is comprehensive and continuous, creating a strong bond of affection. The Philippians' piety is seen as a sign of God's favor towards both them and Paul, leading to rejoicing and thanksgiving. The message is inclusive, emphasizing that this gratitude applies to all and is rooted in the distinct relationship between Paul and his God. The foundation of thankfulness lies in the remembrance of the Philippians and their shared fellowship in the gospel.
