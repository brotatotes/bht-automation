# Philippians 2:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, the commentators highlight the importance of unity and harmony among believers. The desire is for complete joy that can only be fulfilled through perfect unity and mutual agreement. The emphasis is on both unity of sentiment and unity of affection, where Christians are of the same mind and have the same love for God and one another. This unity is not just intellectual, but also heart-felt and sincere, resulting in a shared purpose and fervor in serving Christ.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "He already had joy in them, but it was not complete, because they did not walk in perfect unity." (πληρώσατε)
2. "The unanimity of the Philippians is the far greater and more important result, to which the πληροῦν μου τὴν χ. is but accessory." (ἵνα)
3. "This expression (be of the same mind) is more general than τὸ ἓ φρονοῦντες (‘being of one mind’) below." (τὸ αὐτὸ φρονῆτε)

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Fulfil—that is, Make full."
2. "likeminded—literally, 'that ye be of the same mind'; more general than the following 'of one mind.'"
3. "having the same love—equally disposed to love and be loved."
4. "being of one accord—literally, 'with united souls.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Fill up my joy so that nothing shall be wanting to complete it."
2. "Perfect unity of sentiment, opinion, and plan would be desirable if it could be attained."
3. "The object which he aimed at was union of heart, of feeling, of plan, of purpose."

These quotes emphasize the importance of unity and harmony among Christians, highlighting the desire for complete joy and the need for like-mindedness and love. The commentary emphasizes that if Christians were united in their purpose and love for one another, they could have a powerful impact on the world and the salvation of souls.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Fulfill [πληρωσατε]. Or complete." 
2. "The expression is a general one for concord, and is defined in the two following clauses: unity of affection, the same love; unity of sentiment, of one accord." 
3. "The general expression is then repeated in a stronger form, thinking the one thing."

### John Calvin:
1. "Here again we may see how little anxiety he had as to himself, provided only it went well with the Church of Christ."
2. "The chief indication of a prosperous condition of the Church is when mutual agreement prevails in it, and brotherly harmony."
3. "The beginning of love is harmony of views, but that is not sufficient, unless men’s hearts are at the same time joined together in mutual affection."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The apostle’s joy will not be complete till this is attained."
2. "An expression indicative of most complete unity, the high ideal after which the Christian must strive."
3. "All striving alike after this highest Christian grace, and not being content to be surpassed by any in this struggle."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Paul's cup of joy will be full if the Philippians will only keep on having unity of thought and feeling."
2. "Being of one accord, harmonious in soul, souls that beat together, in tune with Christ and with each other."
3. "Perfect intellectual telepathy. Identity of ideas and harmony of feelings."

### John Gill:
1. "He had much joy in them, on account of the good work being begun, and carrying on in their souls; and because of their steadfastness in the faith, notwithstanding the persecutions they met with."
2. "Their unity in affection, judgment, and practice would crown his joy and fill it brimful."
3. "Having the same love, both for quality, being hearty, sincere, and unfeigned; and for quantity, returning the same that is measured to them; and with respect to objects, loving the same Christ, the same doctrines of Christ, the same ministers of the Gospel, and all the saints, rich and poor, high and low, weak or strong believers, without making any difference, by which means unity is preserved."

### John Wesley:
1. "Seeing Christ is your common Head."
2. "Having the same love — To God, your common Father."
3. "Being of one soul — Animated with the same affections and tempers, as ye have all drank ill to one spirit."
