# Philippians 3:21 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, the commentators highlight the remarkable transformation that awaits believers in the resurrection. They emphasize that our current bodies, characterized by frailty, weakness, and sin, will be completely changed. God will take our lowly "body of humiliation" and conform it to the glorious body of Christ. Described as pure, bright, and beautiful, this transformed body will shine like the sun in the heavenly kingdom, reflecting God's own magnificent glory. It is a powerful display of God's transformative power and the hope believers have of being made perfect and united with Christ.

### Henry Alford:
1. "The change from the dust of death in the resurrection, however we may accommodate the expression to it, was not originally contemplated by it; witness the ἀπεκδεχόμεθα, and the σῶμα τῆς ταπεινώσεως ἡμῶν."

3. "He once had such a ταπείνωσις, and has passed through it to His glory and He shall change us so as to be like Him."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "He shall transfigure the body of our humiliation, that it may be conformed unto the body of His glory." 
2. "Not only shall He come as our 'Saviour,' but also as our Glorifier."
3. "Our spiritual resurrection now is the pledge of our bodily resurrection to glory hereafter."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The word thus indicates a change in what is outward and shifting - the body."
2. "The A. V. seems to countenance the stoic contempt of the body."
3. "The body in which He appears in His present glorified state."

### John Calvin:
1. "This body which we carry about with us is not an everlasting abode, but a frail tabernacle, which will in a short time be reduced to nothing."
2. "There is no part of us that ought not to aspire after heaven with undivided affection."
3. "Paul places before our eyes the boundless power of God, that it may entirely remove all doubt."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He shall change our body in all those points specified by the apostle (1 Corinthians 15:42-44). Its corruption shall become incorruption; its dishonour, glory; its weakness, power, and from natural it shall become spiritual." 

2. "The body is called ‘the body of our humiliation,’ because while in it we have so much to humiliate us, so much to mourn over, from which we cannot get free, till we be clothed upon with our house which is from heaven."

3. "The fleeting fashion of the body shall be done away, its essential form shall remain, and be like unto Christ in His glory."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "The body of our state of humiliation will be made suitable to associate with the body of Christ's glory."
2. "If any one doubts the power of Christ to do this transformation, Paul replies that he has power 'even to subject all things unto himself.'"
3. "According to the working... 'According to the energy.'"

### John Gill:
1. "This vile body, in the resurrection morn, shall be stripped of all its vileness, baseness, and meanness; and be changed, not as to its substance, nor as to its form and figure, but as to its qualities."
2. "The bodies of the saints in the resurrection shall be like to Christ's, though not equal to it, and shall shine like the sun in the kingdom of their Father."
3. "And now who would but follow such persons, who are citizens of heaven, have their conversation there, look for Christ the Saviour from thence, who when he comes will raise the dead in Christ first, put such a glory on their bodies as is on his own, and take them to himself, that where he is they may be also?"
