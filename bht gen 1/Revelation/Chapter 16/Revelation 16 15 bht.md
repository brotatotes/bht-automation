# Revelation 16:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, the sudden and unexpected nature of Christ's coming is emphasized, likening it to that of a thief. It is a call to be watchful and prepared, as those who are vigilant will be blessed and ready to partake in the joy of the Lamb. Believers are urged to maintain their spiritual garments and hold fast to righteousness, to avoid being exposed to shame and the consequences of neglecting their faith.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Behold, I come as a thief - That is, suddenly and unexpectedly...whenever it would occur, it would be at a time when people were not expecting him."
2. "Blessed is he that watcheth...he who watches for these events...is in a better and happier state of mind than he on whom they come suddenly and unexpectedly."

4. "As Christians are clothed with the garments of righteousness, they should not lay them aside, so that their spiritual nakedness should be seen."
5. "They are to be always clothed with the robes of salvation; always ready for any event, however soon or suddenly it may come upon them."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The words are those of Matthew 24:1; Matthew 25:1, Mark 13:34, Luke 12:37, and they embrace the thought both of the suddenness of Christ’s coming, and of the destruction which it brings with it to the wicked." 
2. "In the remaining words of the verse the Seer seems to take up the strain, as he pronounces blessedness upon him who is ready for the events of the day so rapidly approaching."
3. "Similar parentheses occur at chaps. Revelation 13:9 and Revelation 14:12."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Behold, I come as a thief." - The voice of Christ breaks in with the same metaphor as in Revelation 3:3, emphasizing the unexpected and sudden nature of His coming.

2. "There comes one of seven beatitudes in Rev." - This verse is one of the seven beatitudes found in the book of Revelation, highlighting the blessedness of those who heed its message.

3. "Lest he walk naked." - This negative purpose clause warns against the consequence of not being watchful, emphasizing the need to be spiritually prepared and clothed.

4. "And they see his shame." - The final clause highlights the potential shame and disgrace that can be exposed if one is not vigilant and faithful, underscoring the importance of living a life worthy of Christ's return.

### John Gill:
1. "Behold I come as a thief": Christ's coming is sudden and surprising, emphasizing the urgency for believers to be watchful and prepared.
2. "Blessed is he that watcheth": Those who are vigilant against sin, temptation, and false teachings will be blessed and ready to partake in the marriage chamber and the supper of the Lamb.
3. "Keepeth his garments": Believers must maintain their conversation garments, unspotted from the world, and hold fast to the robe of Christ's righteousness to avoid being exposed to shame and everlasting ruin.

### John Wesley:
1. "Behold, I come as a thief — Suddenly, unexpectedly. Observe the beautiful abruptness."
2. "Happy is he that watcheth. — Looking continually for him that 'cometh quickly.'"
3. "Lest he walk naked, and they see his shame — Lest he lose the graces which he takes no care to keep, and others see his sin and punishment."
