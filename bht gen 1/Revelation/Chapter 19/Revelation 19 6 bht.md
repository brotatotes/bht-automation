# Revelation 19:6 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, a multitude of voices rise up like the roaring of mighty waters and thunder, praising God for His reign as the Almighty. The voices of this vast assembly, representing the redeemed from all corners of the world, joyfully declare the establishment of God's kingdom. Their unified worship signifies the grandeur and power of God, prompting all of His servants to join in the chorus of praise.

1. "Many waters—Contrast the 'many waters' on which the whore sitteth."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "That is, in the former case he heard a shout that he at once recognized as the voice of a great multitude of persons; here he says that he heard a sound not distinctly recognized at first as such, but which resembled such a shout of a multitude."
2. "The comparison of the voices of a host of people with the roar of mighty waters is not uncommon in the Scriptures."
3. "The loud, deep, heavy voice of thunder. The distant shouts of a multitude may properly be represented by the sound of heavy thunder."

### Philip Schaff:

2. "Hallelujah, for the Lord hath taken to him his kingdom, even our God, the Almighty." - This proclamation highlights the joy and exultation in recognizing that God has established His kingdom and reigns as the Almighty.

3. "The song is new, celebrating something greater and higher than the last." - This statement suggests that the song of praise in this verse represents a new level of worship, surpassing previous expressions, and signifies the significance of God's kingdom being fully realized.

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The voice of the redeemed is described as the voice of many waters, symbolizing the vastness and power of their praise." 

2. "The fourth 'Hallelujah' comes from this vast multitude, emphasizing the unity and collective worship of the redeemed." 

3. "The designation of God as 'the Lord our God, the Almighty' highlights His sovereignty and all-encompassing power."

### John Gill:
1. "A vast multitude of converted Jews and Gentiles, in the several parts of the world, who in answer to the voice out of the throne, which came with great power and energy, lift up their voices in praise to God, both for their own conversion, and for the downfall of Babylon."
2. "The united voice of so great a multitude of converts as will be gathered together everywhere at this time."
3. "They will call upon one another to celebrate the praises of God, on account of the above things, in the same manner, and using the same word the people in heaven, and the four and twenty elders and four living creatures, do."

### John Wesley:

3. "So all his servants did praise him."
