# Revelation 2:10 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this Bible verse, the commentators emphasize the upcoming sufferings and trials the believers will face. The verse warns that the devil will cast some into prison, and that they will undergo tribulation for a limited time. However, the faithful are encouraged to remain steadfast unto death, as their faithfulness will be rewarded with the crown of life. The commentators highlight the certainty of suffering, the distinction between God's testing and Satan's temptations, and the importance of remaining faithful, even in the face of death.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Fear not the things which thou art about to suffer...behold, for certain, the devil is about to cast (some) of you into prison." 
2. "That ye may be tried...and ye shall have tribulation ten days." 
3. "Be thou faithful unto death, and I will give thee the crown of life."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The Captain of our salvation never keeps back what those who faithfully witness for Him may have to bear for His name's sake." 
2. "The conflict of [Christ's] people was not with mere flesh and blood, but with the rulers of the darkness of this world." 
3. "The same event is often both a temptation from the devil, and a trial from God."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Fear none of those things which thou shalt suffer... We need not dread suffering if we can hear the voice of the Redeemer encouraging us."
2. "Behold, the devil shall cast some of you into prison... It was common to cast those who were persecuted into prison."
3. "That ye may be tried... It was common in the early periods of the church to suffer religion to be subjected to trial amidst persecutions, in order to show that it was of heavenly origin."
4. "And ye shall have tribulation ten days... the assurance was that it would not be long, and they were to allow this thought to cheer and sustain them in their sorrows."
5. "Be thou faithful unto death... if he is faithful unto death, a crown of life awaits him."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The persecution of the Christians is thus traced to the direct agency of Satan, and not to the offended passions or prejudices of men."
2. "There is to be a succession of trials demanding an increase in the power and a variety in the direction of faith."
3. "Faithfulness is to be not only existent but becoming, developing with new strength and into new applications."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Strength and comfort are to be found in other thoughts."
2. "God proves His people. Satan tries them."
3. "Be thou faithful unto death, that is, not merely during the whole of life, but even to the extremity, if necessary, of meeting death."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Worse things are about to come than poverty and blasphemy, perhaps prison and death, for the devil 'is about to cast.'" 
2. "John himself is in exile. Peter and John had often been in prison together. James the brother of John, Paul, and Peter had all suffered martyrdom." 
3. "'Keep on becoming faithful'...as the martyrs have done (Jesus most of all)."

### John Gill:
1. "God's people undergo sufferings of various sorts... and these are certain, they shall suffer them; they are all known beforehand to Christ, and he sometimes gives his people previous notice of them, nor should they indulge a slavish fear about them."
2. "Suffering times are trying times, whether men are real Christians or not; whether they have the true grace of God or not; and whether the principles they hold are right and true, and are worth, and will bear suffering for."
3. "Be thou faithful unto death... to continue believing in Christ, professing his name, striving for his Gospel, attending on his ordinances, and following him whithersoever he went; though this should expose them to sufferings, even unto death, which it became them cheerfully to undergo."

### John Wesley:
1. "Fear none of those things which thou art about to suffer — Probably by means of the false Jews."
2. "The devil — Who sets all persecutors to work; and these more particularly."
3. "Be thou faithful — Our Lord does not say, 'till I come,' as in the other letters, but unto death - Signifying that the angel of this church should quickly after seal his testimony with his blood; fifty years before the martyrdom of Polycarp, for whom some have mistaken him."
