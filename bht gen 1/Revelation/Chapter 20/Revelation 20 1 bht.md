### Albert Barnes:
1. "The whole character of the composition of the book leads us to suppose that different angels were employed to make these communications to John."

3. "The fact that he has the key of that underworld is designed to denote here, that he can fasten it on Satan so that it shall become his prison."

3. "A great chain" - The use of a great chain implies that the binding of the forces of evil will be secure and unbreakable. It signifies the complete and total restraint of these forces, ensuring that they will not be able to cause harm or wreak havoc.

These quotes highlight the divine authority, power, and control over evil that the one holding the key and chain possesses. They emphasize the significance of this figure in bringing about the restraint and defeat of evil forces.

### John Gill:
