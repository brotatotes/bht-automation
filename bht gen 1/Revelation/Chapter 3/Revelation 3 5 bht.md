# Revelation 3:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this Bible verse, those who overcome sin and temptation are promised a glorious reward. They will be clothed in radiant white garments, symbolizing victory, joy, and holiness. Their names will be written and preserved in the book of life, ensuring eternal life and fellowship with God. Furthermore, the Lord Himself will openly acknowledge and commend their faithful devotion. This verse emphasizes the triumph of the faithful and the everlasting significance of their confession and perseverance.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The body transfigured into the likeness of Christ's body, and emitting beams of light reflected from Him, is probably the 'white raiment' promised here."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Whosoever he may be that shall overcome sin and the temptations of this world, shall be admitted to this glorious reward."
2. "The hope that is thus held out before us, is that of appearing with the Redeemer in his kingdom, clad in robes expressive of holiness and joy."
3. "The names of his people are thus represented as enrolled in a book which he keeps - a register of those who are to live forever."

3. "What we are told is, not that some names shall be blotted out, but that certain names shall in no wise be so."

### Archibald T Robertson:

2. "I will in no wise blot out (ου μη εξαλειψω). Strong double negative ου μη and the first aorist active (or future) of εξαλειφω, old word, to wipe out."
3. "His name (το ονομα αυτου). The name of the one who overcomes (ο νικων). Clear reminiscence of the words of Christ about confessing to the Father those who confess him here."

### John Gill:
