# Revelation 5:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this vision, there is a breathtaking display of heavenly worship, with an immense multitude of angels and a vast number that cannot even be quantified. They join in unison to praise the Lamb of God, symbolizing the universal harmony in heaven. This celestial gathering highlights the infinite love, wisdom, and power of God, as well as the profound interest and involvement of angels in the redemption of humanity. Through this vision, we are reminded of the immeasurable greatness of God's creation and the central role of mankind in His eternal plan.

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "The heavenly hosts ranged around gaze with intense love and adoration at this crowning manifestation of God's love, wisdom, and power." 

2. "The angels form the outer circle, while the Church, the object of redemption, forms the inner circle nearest the throne." 

3. "Ten thousand times ten thousand" refers to the countless number of angels present, emphasizing the magnitude of the heavenly host's worship.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The design is to show that there is universal sympathy and harmony in heaven, and that all worlds will unite in ascribing honor to the Lamb of God."
2. "The angels are represented here, as they are everywhere in the Scriptures, as taking a deep interest in all that pertains to the redemption of people."
3. "Implying that the number before specified was not large enough to comprehend all. Besides the 'ten thousand times ten thousand,' there was a vast uncounted host which one could not attempt to enumerate."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Ten thousand times ten thousand [μυριαδες μυριαδων]. Lit., ten thousands of ten thousands." 
2. "Muriav, whence the English myriad, means the number ten thousand."
3. "In the plural used for an unlimited number."

These quotes highlight the significance of the phrase "ten thousand times ten thousand" and its use in the Bible. It emphasizes a vast and limitless number, symbolizing the multitude of saints or an unlimited quantity.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The Son of God, in carrying out the process of redemption, took on Him the nature of man, that man might be elevated to a participation in His Divine nature, and it is this process of redemption that is here the main topic of praise."
2. "Although, however, angels are not themselves partakers of the redemption spoken of, they have the deepest interest in its glorious results."
3. "It is remarkable that the smaller number seems to be given last, and various explanations have been offered, that 'in enormous numbers distinctions vanish,' 'that the larger number preceding, large as it is, is not enough,' that 'the same idea is conveyed whether by climax or anticlimax.' No one of these explanations is satisfactory."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "A new feature introduced by the outer and vaster circle of angels who catch up the new song of redemption in antiphonal singing." 

2. "Ten thousand times ten thousand, literally 'myriads of myriads and thousands of thousands,' a mild husteron-proteron." 

3. "They are all efforts to express the innumerable hosts of the angels."

### John Gill:
1. "Immaterial spirits, made by Christ, and worshippers of him, and ministering spirits to him and his, the holy and elect angels."
2. "The angels are near unto, and encompass the ministers of the Gospel; they are about them, and give them intimations and discoveries of the mind and will of God."
3. "The saints are nearer the Lamb and the throne than the angels be; according to this account, there was a throne, and one that sat on it, who is the living God; nearest to the throne stood the Lamb."

### John Wesley:
1. "Men are represented through this whole vision as nearer to God than any of the angels." 
2. "The number of them was at least two hundred millions, and two millions over."
3. "These were but a part of the holy angels."
