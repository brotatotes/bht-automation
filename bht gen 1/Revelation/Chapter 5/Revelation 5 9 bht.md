# Revelation 5:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, the commentators highlight the concept of a "new song" that symbolizes the unique and unprecedented redemption accomplished through the blood of the Lamb. The Lamb's worthiness is established by His sacrificial death, granting Him authority and knowledge. The redemption achieved by Christ extends to individuals from every nation, tribe, and language, emphasizing the universal nature of salvation. This verse captures the continuous worship of heaven and the inclusion of both Jews and Gentiles in God's redemptive plan.

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "The theme of redemption is ever new, ever suggesting fresh thoughts of praise, embodied in the 'new song.'" 
2. "The present election-church gathered out of the world, as distinguished from the peoples gathered to Christ as the subjects, not of an election, but of a general and world-wide conversion of all nations."
3. "Thus there is here marked the election-Church gathered from Jews and Gentiles."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "New in the sense that it is a song consequent on redemption, and distinguished therefore from the songs sung in heaven before the work of redemption was consummated."
2. "The fact that he was thus slain constituted the ground of his worthiness to open the book."
3. "This is the true nature of redemption, that by the price paid we are rescued from the servitude of Satan, and are henceforth to regard ourselves as belonging unto God."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Present tense, denoting the continuous, unceasing worship of heaven."
2. "The preposition in is used Hebraistically of the price; the value of the thing purchased being contained in the price."
3. "Kindred [φυλης]. Rev., tribe. Often in the New Testament of the twelve tribes of Israel."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "A new song...called καινην because a fresh song for new mercies...here in praise of redemption to Christ." 
2. "The blood of Christ as the price of our redemption runs all through the Apocalypse." 
3. "Men of every...representatives of all races and nations."

### John Gill:
1. "And they sung a new song... redemption by the blood of the Lamb" - This new song represents the unique and unparalleled redemption brought about by the sacrifice of Jesus, emphasizing the worthiness of the Lamb to open the sealed book.

2. "Thou art worthy to take the book, and open the seals thereof" - The Lamb's worthiness is established through His sacrificial death, which not only qualifies Him to take away sins but also grants Him authority and knowledge of future events.

3. "Hast redeemed us to God by thy blood, out of every kindred, and tongue, and people, and nation" - The redemption accomplished by Christ extends beyond the Jewish people, encompassing individuals from every nation and language. This highlights the universality of salvation and the inclusion of both Jews and Gentiles in God's redemptive plan.

### John Wesley:
1. "And they sing a new song — One which neither they nor any other had sung before."
2. "Thou hast redeemed us — So the living creatures also were of the number of the redeemed."
3. "This does not so much refer to the act of redemption, which was long before, as to the fruit of it; and so more directly to those who had finished their course, 'who were redeemed from the earth,' Revelation 14:1, out of every tribe, and tongue, and people, and nation - That is, out of all mankind."
