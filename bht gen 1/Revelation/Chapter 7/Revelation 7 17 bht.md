# Revelation 7:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this profound verse, the Lamb, who is the Messiah, takes on the role of a caring and guiding Shepherd, leading and nurturing his flock with everlasting peace and joy. Not only does he provide sustenance, but he also leads them to the life-giving fountains of the Holy Spirit, quenching their spiritual thirst. In this blessed state, they will no longer experience suffering or grief, as God tenderly wipes away all their tears, promising a future of eternal happiness and comfort.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "In the midst of the throne" refers to the middle point in front of the throne, indicating a position of prominence and authority.
2. The word "feed" in Greek can also be translated as "tend as a shepherd," suggesting a caring and nurturing role.
3. The phrase "living fountains of water" can be understood as "eternal life's fountains of waters," emphasizing the everlasting nature of these life-giving sources.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He is still the great agent in promoting the happiness of the redeemed in heaven."
2. "Shall exercise over them the office of a shepherd - ποιμανεῖ poimainō. This includes much more than mere feeding. It embraces all the care which a shepherd takes of his flock."
3. "The word 'living' here must refer to the fact that that happiness will be perennial. These fountains will always bubble; these streams will never dry up. The thirst for salvation will always be gratified; the soul will always be made happy."
4. "And God shall wipe away all tears from their eyes - This is a new image of happiness taken from another place in Isaiah Isaiah 25:8, 'The Lord God will wipe away tears from off all faces.' The expression is one of exquisite tenderness and beauty."
5. "To that blessed world, when our eyes run down with tears, are we permitted to look forward; and the prospect of such a world should contribute to wipe away our tears here - for all our sorrows will soon be over."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Shall feed...shall be shepherd of" - This emphasizes the role of the Messiah as a caring and guiding figure, similar to a shepherd who tends to and leads his flock.
2. "Shall lead" - This highlights the Messiah's role as a guide, leading his followers in the right path and providing direction and guidance in their lives.
3. "Living fountains of waters of life" - This phrase suggests that the Messiah is the source of eternal life and spiritual nourishment, providing a continuous flow of life-giving water to those who believe in him.

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Shall shepherd them" - Jesus is still the Good Shepherd of his sheep (John 10:11; John 10:14).
2. "Shall guide them" - Used of God's guidance of Israel, individual lives, and the Holy Spirit (Exodus 15:13; Psalms 5:9; John 16:13).
3. "Unto fountains of waters of life" - Emphasizes the idea of life's water springs, similar to other biblical references (Isaiah 49:10; Jeremiah 2:13; John 4:12; John 4:14; John 7:38; Revelation 21:6; Revelation 22:1; Revelation 22:17).

### John Gill:
1. "For this Lamb is a Shepherd, and this great multitude are his flock; whom he will feed in this state, not by his ministers, word, and ordinances, as now; but in person, and with the rich discoveries of himself, and of his love." 
2. "Christ will now be visibly King of saints, and King over all the earth, and will reign before his ancients gloriously; and, in these days of his, Judah shall be saved, and Israel shall dwell safely under his power and protection."
3. "And hither will Christ lead his people, which is one branch of his office as a Shepherd; and which shows his care of them, and affection for them."

### John Wesley:
1. "The Lamb will feed them — With eternal peace and joy; so that they shall hunger no more." 
2. "And will lead them to living fountains of water — The comforts of the Holy Ghost; so that they shall thirst no more." 
3. "Neither shall they suffer or grieve any more; for God 'will wipe away all tears from their eyes.'"
