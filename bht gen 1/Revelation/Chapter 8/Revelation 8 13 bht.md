# Revelation 8:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, a powerful symbol of judgment and vengeance, represented by an eagle, is mentioned. The woe proclaimed is directed towards the ungodly world outside the church, signifying impending calamities. These calamities are different from the previous ones, symbolizing a higher potency of judgment. The repetition of the woe emphasizes its intensity, indicating that there would be an interval before further destruction upon the earth. This verse marks a turning point, preparing for more imminent and grievous woes that will bring judgment upon the whole world.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "An eagle: the symbol of judgment descending fatally from on high; the king of birds pouncing on the prey." 
2. "John is compared in the cherubim (according to the primitive interpretation) to a flying eagle: Christ's divine majesty in this similitude is set forth in the Gospel according to John, His judicial visitations in the Revelation of John." 
3. "The ungodly, the 'men of the world,' whose 'portion is in this life,' upon whom the martyrs had prayed that their blood might be avenged. Not that they sought personal revenge, but their zeal was for the honor of God against the foes of God and His Church."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The repetition of the word is intensive, and the idea is, that the sounding of the three remaining trumpets would indicate great and fearful calamities."
2. "These three are grouped together as if they pertained to a similar series of events, as the first four had been."
3. "The two classes are separated from each other by this interval and by this proclamation - implying that the first series had been completed, and that there would be some interval, either of space or time, before the other series would come upon the world."

### Marvin Vincent:

2. "Mid heaven means, properly, the meridian, the highest point in the heavens which the sun occupies at noon."
3. "It means, not the space between heaven and earth."
