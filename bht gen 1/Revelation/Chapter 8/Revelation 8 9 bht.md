In this verse, commentators shed light on the symbolic meaning behind the imagery used. The ships represent churches, highlighting the importance of faith communities in guiding believers. The sea symbolizes the journey of life, emphasizing the need for God's guidance and direction. The destruction depicted signifies the downfall and desolation of a powerful nation or empire, impacting all levels of society and causing great loss. Ultimately, this verse serves as a reminder of the spiritual well-being that comes from nurturing our souls and sharing the message of God's love and faith.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The symbolical interpreters take the ships here to be churches."
2. "The first churches were in the shape of an inverted ship."
3. "The Greek for destroyed is also used of heretical corruptings."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The effect was as if one-third of all the fish in the sea were cut off. Of course this is not to be taken literally. It is designed to describe an effect, pertaining to the maritime portion of the world, as if a third portion of all that was in the sea should perish."
2. "The destruction of the 'ships' was produced, in some way, by casting the mountain into the sea - either by their being consumed by the contact with the burning mass, or by being sunk by the agitation of the waters."
3. "The symbol of a blazing or burning mountain, torn from its foundation, and precipitated into the ocean, would well represent this mighty nation moved from its ancient seat, and borne along toward the maritime parts of the empire, and its desolations there."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Life [ψυχας]": This term refers to the soul or the inner self, highlighting the importance of spiritual well-being. It reminds us that true fulfillment comes from nurturing our souls and seeking a deeper connection with God.

2. "Ships [πλοιων]": This word symbolizes the journey of life and the need for guidance and direction. Just as ships rely on a captain to navigate them through treacherous waters, we too must trust in God to lead us through the challenges and uncertainties of life.

3. "See on Luke 5:2": This reference directs us to the story of Jesus teaching from a boat, illustrating the significance of using vessels as platforms for sharing the Word of God. It reminds us that our lives can be vessels for spreading the message of love, hope, and faith to others.

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Of the creatures (των κτισματων). See Revelation 5:13 for this word κτισμα." 
2. "Even they that had life (τα εχοντα ψυχας). Here the nominative articular participle is in apposition with the genitive κτισματων."
3. "Was destroyed (διεφθαρησαν). Second aorist passive indicative of διαφθειρω, old compound, to corrupt, to consume, to destroy (perfective use of δια), also Revelation 11:18."

### John Gill:

### John Wesley:
1. "And the third part of the creatures that were in the sea — That is, of all sorts of men, of every station and degree." 
2. "It is a frequent thing to resemble a state or republic to a ship, wherein many people are embarked together, and share in the same dangers." 
3. "How often was the sea tinged with blood! How many of those who dwelt mostly upon it were killed! And what number of ships destroyed!"
