# Revelation 9:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this vivid and symbolic verse, fearsome horsemen are described, their power depicted both in their mouths and in their tails. The commentators offer various interpretations, linking the horses to Turkish cavalry armed with gunpowder and artillery, and even associating their tails with the doctrines of a false prophet. The imagery of the tails, likened to serpents, symbolizes the destructive and inescapable nature of the horsemen's power, inflicting both physical and spiritual harm. Overall, this verse captures the apocalyptic clash of powers and the devastating consequences they bring upon humanity.

"Many interpreters understand the horsemen to refer to the myriads of Turkish cavalry arrayed in scarlet, blue, and yellow, the lion-headed horses denoting their invincible courage, and the fire and brimstone out of their mouths, the gunpowder and artillery introduced into Europe about this time, and employed by the Turks."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "What struck the seer as remarkable on looking on the symbol was, that this immense destruction seemed to proceed out of their mouths."
2. "It was not that they trampled down their enemies; nor that they destroyed them with the sword, the bow, or the spear: it was some new and remarkable power in warfare."
3. "The power of a fish, or a scorpion, or a wasp, may be said to be in their tails, for their strength or their means of defense or of injury are there; but we never think of speaking in this way of horses."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The power of the horses, represented by their long, smooth, subtle tails, is described as being like serpents, capable of clasping their victims in an inescapable embrace." (Milligan)
2. "The horses' tails symbolize the authority of the Turkish pashas, according to the imaginative interpretation of Elliott." (Apocalyptic exposition)
3. "The imagery of the horses and their tails serves as one of the many fantastical elements found in Apocalyptic literature." (Elliott)

### Philip Schaff:
1. "That power is also in their tails, for their tails are like unto serpents, having heads, and with them they do hurt." 
2. "Three characteristics of the tails are specially mentioned; first, they are ‘like unto serpents,’ long, smooth, subtle, clasping their victim in an embrace from which he cannot escape."
3. "The tail of a horse is for its own protection: these tails devastate. Yet they are not so fatal as the mouths. The former ‘hurt,’ the latter ‘kill.’"

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "The power of the horses is both in their mouths and in their tails." 
2. "Their tails are like unto serpents." 
3. "Surely dreadful monsters."

### John Gill:
1. "Their power is in their mouth... in their guns, and what came out of them." 
2. "Their tails may intend the doctrine of Mahomet, the false prophet, who is the tail." 
3. "With their guns, the power in their mouth, they did hurt to the bodies of men; and with their false doctrines, their tails, they did hurt to the souls of men."
