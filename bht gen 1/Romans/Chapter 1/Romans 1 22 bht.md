# Romans 1:22 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The verse being described is about the folly of those who profess themselves to be wise, but in reality, become fools. It addresses the irony of individuals who boast about their wisdom and knowledge, yet end up demonstrating foolishness. These commentators emphasize the tendency of people to take credit for their misguided beliefs and idolatry, which ultimately stems from a falling away from the true knowledge of God. The verse speaks to the human inclination to elevate one's own understanding while disregarding the wisdom of God.

3. "So the heathen" (1 Corinthians 1:21) - This reference to 1 Corinthians 1:21 highlights that even in the ancient world, the same pattern of professing wisdom while being fools was observed. It suggests that this is not a new phenomenon but a timeless aspect of human nature.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Professing themselves to be wise." - This verse refers to the conceit of wisdom that underlies heathenism, not just the claims of heathen philosophers.
2. "Their folly was manifested in their idolatry." - The foolishness of those who rejected the knowledge of the true God is evident in their worship of idols.
3. "Heathenism is not the primeval religion, but the result of a falling away from the known original revelation of the true God." - Heathenism is not the original religion of humanity, but a deviation from the initial knowledge of the true God revealed through His works.

3. "...they showed themselves to be such in those very things they prided themselves with the knowledge of..."

These quotes highlight how the learned men among the Gentiles initially called themselves wise, but their arrogance and pride led them to become fools in the very areas they boasted about.
