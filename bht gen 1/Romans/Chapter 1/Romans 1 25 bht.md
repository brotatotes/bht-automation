# Romans 1:25 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The verse describes the degradation of worship and the perversion of the truth of God. Commentators point out that the truth of God is turned into a lie through idolatry, where the worship of the Creator is replaced by the worship of created beings. This act is seen as disgraceful and sacrilegious, as religious honor given to creatures takes away from God. The verse emphasizes the contrast between the superior excellency and glory of the one living God and the degrading idolatry of the pagans.

### Henry Alford:
1. "The truth of God (the true notion of Him as the Creator) which they professed, they changed into a lie, thus counteracting its legitimate agency and depriving it of all power for good." 
2. "Both verbs belong to τῇ κτίσει; though σεβάζομαι would require an accusative, λατρεύω, the nearest, takes the government." 
3. "The doxology expresses the horror of the Apostle at this dishonour, and puts their sin in a more striking light."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The truth concerning God into idol falsehood." 
2. "Professing merely to worship the Creator by means of the creature, they soon came to lose sight of the Creator in the creature." 
3. "How aggravated is the guilt of the Church of Rome, which, under the same flimsy pretext, does shamelessly what the heathen are here condemned for doing, and with light which the heathen never had!"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "God is called 'the true God' in opposition to idols, which are called false gods. There is but one real or true God, and all others are false."
2. "Idols are not infrequently called falsehood and lies, because they are not true representations of God."
3. "The mention of the degrading idolatry of the pagans was strongly calculated to impress on his mind the superior excellency and glory of the one living God."

### John Calvin:
1. "When the truth of God is turned to a lie, his glory is obliterated."
2. "Religious honor cannot be given to a creature, without taking it away, in a disgraceful and sacrilegious manner, from God."
3. "He is one whom they ought alone to have honored and worshipped, and from whom it was not right to take away anything, no, not even the least."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Being such as, or, ‘since they were such as.’ Here the Apostle reverts to the reason for the punishment."
2. "The truth of God. The truth or reality of God, the true Divine essence, practically the same as ‘the true God.’"
3. "The doxology is the natural outburst of piety, aroused into holy indignation at the sin of idolatry, which is by the contrast portrayed in its darkest colors."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "What a bargain they made, 'the truth of God for the lie.'" 
2. "The price of mythology." 
3. "They preferred the creature over the Creator."

### John Gill:
1. "They changed the truth of God into a lie" - by ascribing false deities to what was known of God, they distorted the truth and embraced falsehoods.
2. "They worshipped and served the creature more than the Creator" - their worship was directed towards created beings instead of the one who created them, neglecting the Creator Himself.
3. "Who is blessed for ever, Amen" - God is the source of all blessedness, and it is a truth that should be acknowledged and affirmed with an "Amen."

### John Wesley:
1. "The true worship of God" was perverted and distorted, being changed into "false, abominable idolatries."
2. The act of worship was not just an outward display, but also an inward devotion, as they "worshipped" in their hearts.
3. The people not only worshipped falsely, but they also "served" these false gods, indicating a complete surrender and dedication to these idols.
