# Romans 10:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this Bible verse, it is explained that there is no distinction or difference between Jew and Greek, or Gentile. The same Lord of all, Christ, is rich in grace and mercy towards those who call upon Him. This universal offer of God's mercy in Christ applies to all, regardless of their nationality or background. Faith in the Lord Jesus Christ is the means by which all people, Jews and Gentiles alike, are justified and saved. The invitation to call upon the name of the Lord Jesus signifies the expression of true worship and the heartfelt acknowledgment of His lordship.

### John Gill:
1. "For there is no distinction of nations, no superiority on account of carnal descent, or fleshly privileges, no preeminence on the score of the laws and ordinances of the former dispensation, all which are now abolished."
2. "He is rich unto all that call upon him; he is not only rich as God, being possessed of all divine perfections and glory, but as Mediator, having the riches of grace and glory in him."

### John Wesley:
