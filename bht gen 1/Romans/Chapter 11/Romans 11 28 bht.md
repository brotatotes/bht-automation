# Romans 11:28 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, we see that the unbelieving Jews are described as enemies for the sake of the Gentiles, not enemies of God or the gospel. Their rejection of the gospel has had a purpose in allowing it to be preached to the Gentiles. Despite their current state of enmity, God still regards them with interest and intends to show them mercy. This passage underscores the centrality of the gospel and its role in the relationship between God, the Jews, and the Gentiles.
