The use of the infinitive form emphasizes the continuous and ongoing nature of this command. ### Albert Barnes:
1. "Rejoice with them ...": The church is one; it has one interest; and therefore, there should be common sympathy in its joys and sorrows. (Romans 12:4-5)

2. "In this way happiness diffuses and multiplies itself": The union of the Christian body tends to enlarge the sphere of happiness and prolong the joy conferred by religion.

3. "Weep ...": The design of this direction is to produce mutual kindness and affection, and to divide our sorrows by the sympathies of friends. It is the special duty of Christians to sympathize in each other's griefs.

3. "Let there be such a sympathy among us as may at the same time adapt us to all kinds of feelings."
