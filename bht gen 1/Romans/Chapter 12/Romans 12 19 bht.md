# Romans 12:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, the apostle Paul addresses believers with deep affection, emphasizing the need to refrain from seeking personal revenge and to trust in God's justice instead. Various commentators emphasize the importance of leaving room for God's wrath and not attempting to execute vengeance ourselves. The divine nature of wrath and the assurance that God will bring about justice are highlighted. The verse encourages believers to rely on God's guidance and not to fear the rage of the wicked, confident that God will ultimately avenge and bring about justice.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The more difficult this duty, the more affectionately does the Apostle address his readers, with this word." 
2. "Allow space, i.e. 'interpose delay,' to anger." 
3. "Make not the wrath your own, but leave it for God."

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "But give room or space for wrath to spend itself."
2. "The injunction is to leave vengeance to God."
3. "Wrath here seems to mean, not the offense, which we are tempted to avenge, but the avenging wrath of God."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "To take such satisfaction for injuries done to society, is lawful and proper for a magistrate... But the apostle here is addressing private individual Christians. And the command is, to avoid a spirit and purpose of revenge." 
2. "The command here 'not to avenge ourselves' means, that we are not to take it out of the hands of God, or the hands of the law, and to inflict it ourselves."
3. "To 'give place,' then, is to leave it for God to come in and execute wrath or vengeance on the enemy. Do not execute wrath; leave it to God; commit all to him; leave yourself and your enemy in his hands, assured that he will vindicate you and punish him."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Give place unto wrath... Do not get in its way, as you will do by taking vengeance into your own hands."
2. "Vengeance belongeth unto Me."
3. "The antithesis between vengeance by God and by men is not found in Deuteronomy."

These quotes highlight the importance of not seeking personal vengeance and leaving it to God. They emphasize the divine nature of wrath and the need to trust in God's justice rather than taking matters into our own hands.

### John Calvin:
1. "He commands here, that however grievously we may be injured, we are not to seek revenge, but to commit it to the Lord."
2. "To give place to wrath, is to commit to the Lord the right of judging, which they take away from him who attempt revenge."
3. "He intimates, that we are not to fear that the wicked will more furiously rage when they see us bearing patiently; for God does not in vain take upon himself the office of executing vengeance."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Let God’s wrath take its course, do not attempt to execute it yourself; comp. our Lord’s conduct, as described in 1 Peter 2:23."
2. "The morality of this precept is based on the holiness of God; hence so far as love and wrath are the two poles of holiness, it does not exclude the blessing of our adversaries and intercession for them."
3. "Vengeance is mine, I will recompense again, saith the Lord."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Give room for the wrath of God instead of taking vengeance in your own hands."
2. "Paul quotes Deuteronomy 32:35 (the Hebrew rather than the LXX)."
3. "Socrates and Epictetus condemned personal vindictiveness as Paul does here."

### John Gill:
1. "This affectionate appellation the apostle makes use of, expressing his great love to them, the rather to work upon then, and move them to an attention to what he is about to say."
2. "Moreover, he may hereby suggest to them, not only that they were dear to him, but that they were greatly beloved of God, that they were high in his favour and affection."
3. "Vengeance belongs to God, and to him only; it is proper and peculiar to him, not to Heathen deities, one of which they call δικη, 'vengeance'; nor to Satan, who is of a revengeful spirit, and is styled the enemy and the avenger."

### John Wesley:
1. "Dearly beloved — So he softens the rugged spirit."
2. "Revenge not yourselves, but leave that to God."
3. "Perhaps it might more properly be rendered, leave room for wrath; that is, the wrath of God, to whom vengeance properly belongs."
