### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "Let every soul...be subject unto the higher powers." This verse emphasizes the importance of submitting to the authorities that are above us. It reminds us that the powers that exist have been ordained by God, highlighting the divine authority behind earthly authorities.

2. "For there is no power but of God." This quote reinforces the idea that all authority comes from God. It reminds us that even though earthly authorities may not always be perfect, they are still part of God's overall plan and should be respected.

3. "The powers that be are ordained of God." This statement further emphasizes the divine ordination of earthly authorities. It reminds us that God is ultimately in control and has a purpose for allowing certain authorities to be in power. This encourages us to trust in God's sovereignty and to respect and obey those in positions of authority.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Let every soul...submit himself." - This suggests that obedience to authority is not a blind subjection, but a voluntary and rational act.

2. "Political rulers are undoubtedly meant, and most probably all such, of every rank." - The verse refers to all political rulers, not just those in higher positions, emphasizing the importance of obedience to all levels of authority.

3. "Civil government is necessary, and of divine appointment." - The Apostle asserts that civil government is ordained by God, highlighting the importance of respecting and obeying the established authorities.

4. "Obedience is not demanded in matters contrary to God's appointment." - While obedience is encouraged, it is not required when it goes against God's will, emphasizing the importance of discernment and aligning one's actions with God's principles.

5. "To the duty of obedience are added those of political knowledge and prudence." - Christian citizens have a responsibility to not only obey but also to be informed and wise in their political engagement, recognizing that their faith should inform their participation in governance.

### John Wesley:
1. "Let every soul be subject to the supreme powers" - An admonition peculiarly needful for the Jews.
2. "The powers that be are appointed by God" - Implying that they are God's deputies or vicegerents.
3. "Their authority being, in effect, his, demands our conscientious obedience." - Highlighting the importance of obediently submitting to the authorities appointed by God.
