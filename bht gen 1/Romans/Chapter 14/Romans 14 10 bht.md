### John Gill:
1. "The emphasis lies upon the word 'brother', in both branches of the expostulation; and the force of the apostle's reasoning is that they should not judge or despise one another, because they were brethren, stood in the same relation to God and Christ, belonged to the same family, were partakers of the same grace, and had no pre-eminence one over another."
2. "For we shall all stand before the judgment seat of Christ; at the last day, when he shall sit on his throne of glory, and all nations shall be gathered before him, and he shall pronounce and execute the decisive sentence on each of them."
3. "Here the saints are particularly designed, 'we shall all stand'; whether ministers or private Christians, weak or strong believers; they that are apt to judge, and others that are too ready to despise; they shall all stand before the tribunal of Christ, who is sole Judge, and shall render to every man according to his works, and from whom they shall all receive their sentence."

### John Wesley:
1. "Despising one's brother is a serious offense in the eyes of God, as it goes against the very essence of Christian love and unity."
2. "The stronger brother is reminded that it is not his place to judge or look down upon the weaker brother, but rather to show compassion and understanding."
3. "By despising his brother, the stronger believer not only undermines the unity of the body of Christ, but also fails to recognize the value and worth of every individual in God's eyes."
