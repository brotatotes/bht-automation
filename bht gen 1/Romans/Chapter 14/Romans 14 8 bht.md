## BHT:
In this verse, believers are reminded that they belong wholly to Christ, both in life and in death. Every aspect of their existence is dedicated to serving and glorifying Him. ### John Calvin:
1. "We are not only forbidden rashly to attempt this or that without God's command, but we are also commanded to be patient under all troubles and losses."
2. "If at any time the flesh draws back in adversities, let it come to our minds, that he who is not free nor has authority over himself, perverts right and order if he depends not on the will of his lord."
3. "Thus also is taught us the rule by which we are to live and to die, so that if he extends our life in continual sorrows and miseries, we are not yet to seek to depart before our time; but if he should suddenly call us hence in the flower of our age, we ought ever to be ready for our departure."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Whether we live, we live unto the Lord, i.e., Christ." This verse reminds us that our entire lives should be dedicated to serving and honoring Christ. Our actions, thoughts, and decisions should all be guided by our commitment to Him.

2. "We die unto the Lord; even our dying is an act of consecration to Christ." Even in death, we continue to belong to Christ. Our passing from this life is not the end, but rather a continuation of our devotion to Him.

3. "The whole course of our existence here being unto Christ, it follows that in all we belong to Christ." Every aspect of our lives, from the mundane to the significant, should be lived with the understanding that we belong to Christ. He is the center of our existence, and we should strive to honor Him in all that we do.

### John Gill:
1. "Believers live by faith upon him, and according to his will revealed in the word; find to his honour and glory."
2. "Dying to be with him, to be raised again by him, and live with him for evermore; in the faith and hope of this, the believer both lives and dies, and so glorifies Christ both in life and death."
3. "They do all they do for his glory; whether they observe, or not observe a day, it is to the Lord; whether they eat, or not eat things formerly forbidden, it is to him; and whether they live or die, it is to the Lord, whose they are."
