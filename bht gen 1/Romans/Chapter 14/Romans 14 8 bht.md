# Romans 14:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, believers are reminded that they belong to Christ and are under His authority. Their entire life, including their actions and choices, is dedicated to Him. Whether they live or die, believers are called to honor and glorify the Lord. Even in death, they continue to belong to Him, and their dying is seen as an act of consecration to Christ. In all that they do, believers strive to live according to His will and bring glory to His name.

### Henry Alford:
1. "We are, under all circumstances, living or dying, CHRIST'S: His property." 
2. "The inference that we are His property applies to all aspects of our lives, including eating or abstaining, observing days or not observing them." 
3. "No matter what we do or don't do, we belong to Christ and are under His authority."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "For Christ is here—in the most emphatic terms, and yet in the most unimpassioned tone—held up as the supreme Object of the Christian's life, and of his death too."
2. "Nor does Paul teach this here, but rather appeals to it as a known and recognized fact, of which he had only to remind his readers."
3. "And since the apostle, when he wrote these words, had never been at Rome, he could only know that the Roman Christians would assent to this view of Christ, because it was the common teaching of all the accredited preachers of Christianity, and the common faith of all Christians."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "We live unto the Lord - We live to do his will, and to promote his glory. This is the grand purpose of the life of the Christian."
2. "The truth taught here is, that it is the leading and grand purpose of the Christian to do honor to the Saviour."
3. "In all conditions we are 'his,' and bound to do his will."
4. "Since we belong to another in every state, and are bound to do his will, we have no right to assume the prerogative of sitting in judgment on another."
5. "The passage proves also that the soul does not cease to be conscious at death. We are still the Lord’s; his even when the body is in the grave; and his in all the future world."

### John Calvin:
1. "We are to conform to his will and pleasure, and design all things to his glory."
2. "Our death as well as our life is to be referred to his will."
3. "Whether we live or die, we are his: and it hence follows, that he has full authority over our life and our death."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Whether we live, we live unto the Lord, i.e., Christ." This verse highlights the idea that our entire existence is dedicated to Christ. It emphasizes the importance of living a life that is centered around Him and His teachings.

2. "We die unto the Lord; even our dying is an act of consecration to Christ." This quote reminds us that even in death, we continue to belong to Christ. It emphasizes the eternal nature of our relationship with Him and the significance of surrendering our lives to Him completely.

3. "The whole course of our existence here being unto Christ, it follows that in all we belong to Christ." This statement emphasizes that every aspect of our lives, from the smallest details to the grandest achievements, is meant to be lived for Christ. It reminds us that we are not our own, but rather we belong to Him and should strive to honor Him in everything we do.

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Both if--and if" (εαν τε--εαν τε). 
2. Both living and dying are "to the Lord."
3. "We are the Lord's" (του κυριου εσμεν).

### John Gill:
1. "Believers live by faith upon him, and according to his will revealed in the word; find to his honour and glory."
2. "Dying to be with him, to be raised again by him, and live with him for evermore."
3. "They do all they do for his glory; whether they observe, or not observe a day, it is to the Lord."
