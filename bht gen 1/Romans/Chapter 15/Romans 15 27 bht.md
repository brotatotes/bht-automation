# Romans 15:27 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The verse under consideration expresses the reciprocal nature of giving and receiving within the Christian community. Commentators point to the duty of the Gentiles to contribute to the material needs of the Jews who had provided them with spiritual blessings. This duty is likened to a sacrificial service and an act of worship. The obligation to support those who have ministered to others spiritually is emphasized, highlighting the value of the gospel in this exchange. The verse also highlights the voluntary and cheerful nature of giving and the gratitude owed to those who have shared the gospel.

### John Calvin:
1. "He adds the ground of this obligation, — that they had received the gospel from them: and he takes his argument from the comparison of the less with the greater."
