# Romans 3:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Bible verse describes the dangerous and corrupting nature of the words that come out of the wicked's mouth. Their speech is compared to an open sepulchre, symbolizing their insatiable hunger to devour others. Their deceitful tongues are like poison, causing harm and destruction. This malicious and deadly speech is as destructive as the poisonous breath of an adder, leaving a trail of ruin in its wake. The verse also highlights the persistence of their wickedness, emphasizing their continuous and unrepentant behavior.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "From the sole of the foot even to the head there is no soundness in us."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "As the grave is ever open to receive all into it, that is, into destruction, so the mouth or the throat of the slanderer is ever open to swallow up the peace and happiness of all." 
2. "From the mouths of slanderous persons there proceed noisome and ruinous words." 
3. "The words of the slanderer are deadly, pestiferous, quickly destroying the reputation and happiness of man. They are as subtle, as insinuating, and as deadly to the reputation, as the poison of the adder is to the body."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Open sepulchre [ταφος ανεωγμενος] . Lit., a sepulchre opened or standing open."
2. "Some explain the figure by the noisome exhalations from a tomb."
3. "The imperfect tense denotes perseverance in their hypocritical professions."

### John Calvin:
1. "It is more than if he had said, that they were devourers (ἀνθρωποφάγους — men-eaters;) for it is an intimation of extreme barbarity, when the throat is said to be so great a gulf, that it is sufficient to swallow down and devour men whole and entire."
2. "Their tongues are deceitful, and the poison of asps is under their lips, import the same thing."
3. "As from the sepulchre, issues forth an offensive and pestilential vapor; so from the mouths of slanderous persons issue noisome and pestilential words. Their words are like poison, they utter the poisonous breath of slander."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Their throat is an open sepulchre...the reference is to the corrupting character of the speech."
2. "They have used deceit...the reference is to the habitual, continued action of deceitful speech."
3. "The poison of asps...the reference is to the malice which is behind the cunning of their tongues."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Their mouth (words) like the odor of a newly opened grave." 
2. "Some portions of Greek and Roman literature stink like a newly opened grave."
3. "The poison of the asp lies in a bag under the lips."

### John Gill:
1. "The throat is said to be an open sepulchre, as in Psalms 5:9, so called, for its voracity and insatiableness."
2. "The words of the wicked are devouring ones."
3. "The poison of asps is under their lips, and as that stupefies and kills insensibly, so an evil tongue does, and that in a deadly and incurable manner."

### John Wesley:
1. "Their throat — Is noisome and dangerous as an open sepulchre." 
2. "Observe the progress of evil discourse, proceeding out of the heart, through the throat, tongue, lips, till the whole mouth is filled therewith."
3. "The poison of asps — Infectious, deadly backbiting, tale-bearing, evil-speaking, is under (for honey is on) their lips."
