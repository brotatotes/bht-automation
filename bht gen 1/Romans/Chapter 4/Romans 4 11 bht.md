# Romans 4:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, the commentators highlight the significance of circumcision as a sign and seal of the covenant between Abraham and God. They emphasize that circumcision did not convey righteousness itself, but rather confirmed the righteousness of faith. The commentators also draw parallels between circumcision and baptism, underscoring the importance of faith preceding baptism. Overall, this verse teaches that the faithful are not limited to the circumcised but also include those who believe in uncircumcision, emphasizing the universality of faith.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "And he received (from God) the sign (token, or symbol) of circumcision, a seal of the righteousness of the faith." 
2. "The Apostle’s point is, that the righteousness was reckoned, and the promise made, to Abraham, not in circumcision, but in uncircumcision."
3. "Abraham is the father of the faithful. But the triumph and recognition of that faith whereby he was constituted so, was not during his circumcision, but during his uncircumcision: therefore the faithful, his descendants, must not be confined to the circumcised, but must take in the uncircumcised also."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Circumcision thus showed that there was a covenant between Abraham and God."
2. "Circumcision is called a seal of the covenant which God had made with Abraham."
3. "All this was done that Abraham might be held up as an example, or a model, of the very doctrine which the apostle was defending."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The sign - a seal. Sign refers to the material token; seal to its religious import."
2. "That he might be. Not so that he became, but expressing the divinely appointed aim of his receiving the sign."
3. "See on to seal, Revelation 22:10."

### John Calvin:
1. "According to the testimony of Paul, they are seals by which the promises of God are in a manner imprinted on our hearts, and the certainty of grace confirmed."
2. "Hence it remains a fixed principle, that sacred symbols are testimonies, by which God seals his grace on our hearts."
3. "Mark how the circumcision of Abraham confirms our faith with regard to gratuitous righteousness; for it was the sealing of the righteousness of faith, that righteousness might also be imputed to us who believe."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The ‘sign’ was ‘circumcision,’ which is described as ‘a seal,’ etc."
2. "The true idea of a sacrament is here suggested; it is a sign, seal, and means of grace, but not the grace itself."
3. "The sign and seal is not itself the ground of confidence, but it testifies and openly ratifies a Divine covenant or blessing."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "The circumcision did not convey the righteousness, but only gave outward confirmation."
2. "Whatever parallel exists between baptism and circumcision as here stated by Paul argues for faith before baptism and for baptism as the sign and seal of the faith already had before baptism."
3. "This idiom may be God's purpose (contemplated result) as in εις το λογισθηνα below, or even actual result (so that he was) as in Romans 1:20."

### John Gill:
1. "Circumcision was a distinctive sign or badge, which distinguished the posterity of Abraham from other people, and was also a typical one; not of baptism, for circumcision was peculiar to Abraham's natural seed, whereas baptism is not, but was administered to Gentiles as well as Jews."
2. "Circumcision was a seal, not for secrecy, but for certainty; it being a confirmation, not merely of the sincerity of Abraham's faith, but of his justifying righteousness, which was not his faith, but that which his faith looked to."
3. "The blood of Christ is the seal, and the only seal of the covenant of grace, by which its promises and blessings are ratified and confirmed; and the Holy Spirit is the only earnest, pledge, seal, and sealer of the saints, until the day of redemption."

### John Wesley:
1. "Circumcision, which was a sign or token of his being in covenant with God."
2. "An assurance on God's part, that he accounted him righteous, upon his believing, before he was circumcised."
3. "Who believe in uncircumcision — That is, though they are not circumcised."
