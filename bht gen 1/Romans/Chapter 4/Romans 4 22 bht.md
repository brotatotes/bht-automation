# Romans 4:22 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The verse being described is about Abraham's faith, specifically how it was accounted to him as righteousness. Commentators highlight that Abraham's faith was a simple and unwavering trust in God's promises. They note that his belief in God's promises demonstrated his friendship with God and his obedience to Him. The verse emphasizes that faith, not works, is what makes one righteous before God, and that it is through faith that Abraham obeyed. Abraham's faith was not a mere intellectual agreement, but a deep reliance on and trust in God's promises.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The nature of this faith...was a simple unconditional credence of God and His promise." 
2. "It was reckoned to him for righteousness." 
3. "Besides being thus great and admirable, it was reckoned to him for righteousness."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Let all then take notice that this was not because of anything meritorious in Abraham, but merely because he so believed."
2. "It is not our works that make us righteous before God, but our faith in Him."
3. "Abraham's belief in God's promises was counted as righteousness, showing us that faith is the key to our relationship with God."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "His faith was so implicit, and so unwavering, that it was a demonstration that he was the firm friend of God."
2. "He had such confidence in God that he showed that he was supremely attached to him, and would obey and serve him."
3. "This was reckoned as a full proof of friendship; and he was recognised and treated as righteous; that is, as the friend of God."

### John Calvin:
1. "Faith can bring us nothing more than what it receives from the word."
2. "He does not become immediately just, who is imbued only with a general and confused idea that God is true, except he reposes on the promise of his favor."
3. "It is most true, as Paul says to the Romans, that by faith Abraham was justified, and not by obedience: but it is just as true what he says to the Hebrews, that it was by faith that Abraham obeyed."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The whole discussion is here summed up, the last clause of Romans 4:3 being repeated."
2. "The immediate connection is with Romans 4:18-21; because Abraham had believed God in the way there described."
3. "Abraham's faith was not a mere intellectual assent to a set of doctrines, but a deep trust and reliance on God's promises."

### John Gill:
1. "Not because his faith was strong, and he had a full assurance of it, but because it was right, resting on the promise of God, and relying upon his power and faithfulness to perform it."
2. "For though the righteousness of faith is not imputed to any sort of believers, not to mere nominal ones, yet to all such as have true faith, though it may be but weak."
3. "Faith, as to nature, kind, and object, though not as to degree, is the same in all true believers, and the same righteousness is imputed to one as to another."
