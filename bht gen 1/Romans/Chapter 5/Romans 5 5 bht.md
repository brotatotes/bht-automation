# Romans 5:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This verse speaks of the unshakable nature of hope, as it fills us with a glorious assurance that does not disappoint. The pouring out of God's abundant love into our hearts is a cause for rejoicing, as it stirs within us a deep conviction of His love for us. This love of God, experienced through the work of the Holy Spirit, becomes the foundation of our relationship with Him and the beginning of an eternal communion with Him.

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "And hope maketh not ashamed—putteth not to shame, as empty hopes do."
2. "because the love of God—that is, not 'our love to God,' but clearly 'God's love to us'—as most expositors agree."
3. "is shed abroad—literally, 'poured forth,' that is, copiously diffused."
4. "by the Holy Ghost which is—rather, 'was' given unto us—that is, at the great Pentecostal effusion."
5. "how can this hope of glory, which as believers we cherish, put us to shame, when we feel God Himself, by His Spirit given to us, drenching our hearts in sweet, all-subduing sensations of His wondrous love to us in Christ Jesus?"

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Putteth not to shame, thus giving better the strong sense of the word, to disgrace or dishonor." 
2. "Hath been shed abroad. Lit., poured out." 
3. "Compare Titus 3:6; Acts 2:33; Acts 10:45."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "It will not disappoint or mock us; it even now gives triumphant certainty."
2. "We are assured that hope shall not put us to shame, not by anything in ourselves, but because of the love of God."
3. "The love of God did not descend upon us as dew in drops, but as a stream which spreads itself through the whole soul, filling it with a consciousness of His love and favor."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Hath been shed abroad" - This phrase emphasizes the active and intentional pouring out of God's love into our hearts. It suggests that God's love is not limited or confined, but rather abundantly poured out for all to receive.

2. "Perfect passive indicative" - This grammatical explanation highlights the completeness and ongoing nature of God's love being poured out. It implies that God's love has been fully and permanently poured out into our hearts, leaving a lasting impact.

3. "In our hearts" - This phrase signifies that the pouring out of God's love is not merely an external act, but a deeply personal and intimate experience. It suggests that God's love is meant to dwell within us, transforming our hearts and shaping our lives.

### John Gill:
1. "The grace of hope is of such a nature, as that it never fails, deceives, or disappoints."
2. "The love of God is shed abroad in our hearts, which denotes the plenty and abundance of it, and the full and comfortable sensation which believers have of it."
3. "The love which the Spirit sheds abroad in the heart is the source and spring, both of justification itself and of all the effects of it."

These quotes highlight the unwavering nature of hope, the abundance of God's love, and the role of the Holy Spirit in applying and experiencing that love.

### John Wesley:
1. "Hope shameth us not — That is, gives us the highest glorying."
2. "We glory in this our hope, because the love of God is shed abroad in our hearts."
3. "The divine conviction of God's love to us, and that love to God which is both the earnest and the beginning of heaven."
