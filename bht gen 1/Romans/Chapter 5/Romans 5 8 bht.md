# Romans 5:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This verse beautifully showcases the profound love of God towards humanity, as commentators emphasize. It is revealed that God demonstrates His own love by sacrificing His Son for us, even when we were still sinners. The expression of His love remains continuous, extending beyond our worthiness or goodness, as He extends His favor towards us. The act of Christ dying for sinners provides undeniable proof of God's unwavering love and willingness to save us.

### Henry Alford:
1. "He gives proof of His own love towards us."
2. "While we were yet sinners, Christ died for us."
3. "Christ died for us."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "But God commendeth his love toward us, in that, while we were yet sinners." 
2. "Christ died for us." 
3. "Now comes the overpowering inference, emphatically redoubled."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "God commendeth ... His love - His kind feeling; his beneficence; his willingness to submit to sacrifice to do good to others."
2. "While we were yet sinners - And of course his enemies. In this, his love surpasses all that has ever been manifested among people."
3. "Christ died for us - In our stead; to save us from death. He took our place; and by dying himself on the cross, saved us from dying eternally in hell."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "God continuously establishes His love in that the death of Christ remains as its most striking manifestation."
2. "His love [εαυτου] . Rev., more literally, His own. Not in contrast with human love, but as demonstrated by Christ's act of love."
3. "Note the present tense. God continuously establishes His love."

### John Calvin:
1. "He then confirms, that is, exhibits his love to us as most certain and complete, inasmuch as for the sake of the ungodly he spared not Christ his own Son."
2. "Since Christ has attained righteousness for sinners by his death, much more shall he protect them, being now justified, from destruction."
3. "Our condition is such, that he purposes more efficaciously to put forth and daily to increase his favor towards us."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The proof is of such a character as to render the love conspicuous, and thus to ‘commend’ it."
2. "His own love... suggesting it was God’s love (of benevolence) which led to the Atonement."
3. "His death was the ground of our justification; God’s love provided this ground, while we were yet sinners."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "His own love (την εαυτου αγαπην). See John 3:16 as the best comment here." 

2. "Not because we were Jews or Greeks, rich or poor, righteous or good, but plain sinners." 

3. "Cf. Luke 18:13, the plea of the publican, 'μο τω αμαρτωλω.'"

### John Gill:
1. "God commendeth his love towards us...he has given clear evidence of it, a full proof and demonstration of it."
2. "God's elect were sinners...and such they were considered when Christ died for them."
3. "And that before conversion is particularly mentioned here, to illustrate the love of God to them, notwithstanding this their character and condition."

These quotes highlight the manifestation of God's love, the fact that Christ died for sinners, and the emphasis on God's love preceding conversion and being independent of human merit.

### John Wesley:
1. "God recommendeth - A most elegant expression. Those are wont to be recommended to us, who were before either unknown to, or alienated from, us."
2. "While we were sinners - So far from being good, that we were not even just."
3. "God's love is not based on our worthiness or goodness, but on His own gracious nature."
