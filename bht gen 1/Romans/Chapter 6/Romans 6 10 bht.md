# Romans 6:10 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this passage, the commentators provide insightful perspectives on the significance of Jesus' death and resurrection. Jesus' death is described as a sacrifice to atone for and abolish sin, settled in the divine plan and typified by sacrifices in the Old Testament. His resurrection is portrayed as the beginning of a glorious eternal life, triumphant over death. Through faith in Jesus, believers can share in this eternal life, offering hope for the future. The verses emphasize the unique nature of Jesus' death, the exclusivity and finality of his sacrifice, and the eternal victory over sin and death that he achieved.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The death which He died, unto sin He died." 
2. "Our Lord at death passed into a state in which He had 'no more to do with sin'." 
3. "The life which He liveth, He liveth unto God."

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "For in that he died, he died unto—that is, in obedience to the claims of sin once—for all."
2. "But in that he liveth, he liveth unto—in obedience to the claims of God."
3. "Now that He has 'put away sin by the sacrifice of Himself,' He 'liveth unto God,' the acquitted and accepted Surety, unchallenged and unclouded by the claims of sin."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "His death was 'unto sin,' it could have nothing more to do with Him, hence death could have power over Him no more."
2. "In both cases the idea of separation is expressed; but in the case of the believer, it is separation from personal, indwelling sin; in that of Christ, it is separation from the burden of His people's sin, which He bore upon the cross."
3. "Christ's life on earth was also a life 'unto God', but in conflict with sin and death; having triumphed over these at His resurrection, He now lives unto God in the fullest sense."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "The death that he died" - This phrase emphasizes the significance of Jesus' death, highlighting its unique and singular nature.
2. "Once and once only" - This phrase emphasizes the exclusivity and finality of Jesus' sacrifice, contrasting it with any other sacrifices or offerings.
3. "The life that he liveth" - This phrase highlights the eternal and ongoing nature of Jesus' life, emphasizing his resurrection and victory over death.

### John Gill:
1. "The death of Christ was settled and agreed to in the covenant and council of peace; it was spoken of by the prophets, and typified by sacrifices."
2. "Christ came into the world in order to die, and actually did die the death of the cross; in which the great love of God and Christ is expressed to us."
3. "He died not for any sin of his own, or of angels, nor for the sins of every man, but for the sins of his people."

### John Wesley:
1. "He died to sin — To atone for and abolish it." This quote highlights the purpose of Jesus' death on the cross, emphasizing that it was to pay the price for sin and ultimately eliminate its power over humanity.

2. "He liveth unto God — A glorious eternal life." This quote emphasizes the resurrection of Jesus and the eternal life that he now lives, highlighting the victory over death and the promise of eternal life for believers.

3. "Such as we shall live also." This quote reminds readers that through faith in Jesus, believers can also experience the same kind of eternal life that Jesus now lives, offering hope and assurance for the future.
