# Romans 6:22 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Freedom from sin and serving God go hand in hand, leading to the fruitful outcome of holiness. Holiness, which is initiated by the work of the Spirit, is not only a result of being set free from the bondage of sin but also a valuable quality that prepares believers for glory. This verse encourages a deep longing for righteousness and a detestation of sin, while affirming the ultimate aim of eternal life.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "being made free from Sin, and become servants to God—in the absolute sense intended throughout all this passage."
2. "your fruit unto holiness—"sanctification," as in :-; meaning that permanently holy state and character which is built up out of the whole 'fruits of righteousness,' which believers successively bring forth."
3. "and the end everlasting life—as the final state of the justified believer; the beatific experience not only of complete exemption from the fall with all its effects, but of the perfect life of acceptance with God, and conformity to His likeness, of unveiled access to Him, and ineffable fellowship with Him through all duration."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Being made free from sin - Being delivered from its dominion, and from bondage; in the same manner as before conversion they were free from righteousness." 

2. "This service produces holiness, as the other did sin. It is implied here, though not expressly affirmed, that in this service which leads to holiness, they received important benefits, as in the service of sin they had experienced many evils."

3. "And the end - The final result - the ultimate consequence will be. At present this service produces holiness; hereafter it will terminate in everlasting life."

### John Calvin:
1. "Sin in this life brings the torments of an accusing conscience, and in the next eternal death."
2. "We now gather the fruit of righteousness, even holiness; we hope in future to gain eternal life."
3. "These things, unless we are beyond measure stupid, ought to generate in our minds a hatred and horror of sin, and also a love and desire for righteousness."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "But now, as opposed to 'then,' being made free." 
2. "God Himself here takes the place of 'righteousness,' for their relation is now one of personal love." 
3. "They are having fruit now, in contrast either with their having no fruit 'then,' or with the evil fruit in their previous condition. This fruit is of such a kind as at once results in 'sanctification' the progressive state, the ultimate issue being eternal life."
