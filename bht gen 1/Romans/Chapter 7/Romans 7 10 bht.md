# Romans 7:10 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Bible verse discusses a commandment that was originally intended to bring life and spiritual prosperity. However, due to the fallen nature of humanity and the presence of sin, this commandment became an occasion for sin to lead to spiritual death. It reveals the paradoxical nature of sin and the need for salvation through the gospel to rescue humanity from the consequences of breaking the law. The verse highlights the intention behind the commandment and the ultimate result it produced in the context of human nature and God's plan for salvation.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The commandment, which was designed to give life through the keeping of it, I found to be unto death through breaking it." 
2. "Sin, taking occasion by the commandment, deceived me—drew me aside into the very thing which the commandment forbade." 
3. "By it, sin slew me—discovered me to myself to be a condemned and gone man."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Which was ordained to life - Which was intended to produce life, or happiness. Life here stands opposed to death, and means felicity, peace, eternal bliss." 
2. "The law of a parent may be good, and may be appointed to promote the happiness of his children; it may be admirably suited to it if all were obedient; yet in the family there may be one obstinate, self-willed, and stubborn child, resolved to indulge his evil passions, and the results to him would be woe and despair." 
3. "The commandment, which was ordained for the good of the family, and which would be adapted to promote their welfare, he alone, of all the number, would find to be unto death."

### John Calvin:
1. "The commandment shows to us a way of life in the righteousness of God."
2. "It was given in order that we by keeping the law of the Lord might obtain eternal life."
3. "It is incidental that the law inflicts on us a deadly wound, as when an incurable disease is more exasperated by a healing remedy."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The promise of the law, covering its every 'commandment,' was 'do this and 'live;' its aim was 'life.'" 
2. "The latter is perhaps preferable, giving a tragical force to the expression: 'this very commandment.'" 
3. "The aim was 'life;' as a matter of personal human experience the result was 'death.'"

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "The commandment the one for (meant for) life, this was found for me unto death."
2. "It turned out so for me."
3. "First aorist (effective) passive indicative of ευρισκω, to find, not active as the English has it."

### John Gill:
1. "The law which promised a continuance of an immortal life to Adam, in case of perfect obedience to it."
2. "The law...was appointed to the Israelites, that by the observation of it they might live in the land of Canaan."
3. "This same law...was an occasion, through the vitiosity of nature, of stirring up sin in him, which brought forth fruit unto death."

### John Wesley:
1. "The commandment which was intended for life" - This highlights the original purpose of the commandment, which was to preserve and increase spiritual life.

2. "Doubtless it was originally intended by God" - This emphasizes the certainty that God had a specific intention behind giving the commandment.

3. "A grand means of preserving and increasing spiritual life" - This phrase underscores the significance of the commandment in nurturing and growing one's spiritual life.
