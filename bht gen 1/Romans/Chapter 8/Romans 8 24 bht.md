This Bible verse highlights the significant role of hope in our salvation journey. We are saved by hope, as it becomes both the means and the goal of our salvation. Our salvation is not yet fully realized, but it sustains us in the midst of trials and upheavals. ### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "For in hope we are saved"; that is, it is more a salvation in hope than as yet in actual possession.
2. "For the very meaning of hope is, the expectation that something now future will become present."
3. "The latter ending when the other comes."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Our trials are so great that nothing but the prospect of future deliverance would uphold us; and the prospect is sufficient to enable us to bear them with patience."
2. "Hope thus sustains the soul in the midst of trials, and enables it to bear them without a complaint."
3. "What a man already possesses, he cannot be said to hope for."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "We are saved by faith." 
2. "Hope - not hope. Here the word is used of the object of hope."
3. "See Colossians 1:5; 1 Timothy 1:1; Hebrews 6:18."

### John Calvin:
1. "Since hope extends to things not yet obtained, and represents to our minds the form of things hidden and far remote, whatever is either openly seen or really possessed, is not an object of hope."
2. "If then it be grievous to any to groan, they necessarily subvert the order laid down by God, who does not call his people to victory before he exercises them in the warfare of patience."
3. "When we are said to be saved by hope, the meaning is that we are not fully or perfectly saved now, and that this is what we hope for."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The fact of salvation placed us in a condition of which hope was a characteristic." 
2. "Faith apprehends the object, in so far as it is present; hope, in so far as it is still future." 
3. "Why does he still hope, when there is no more ground for it?"

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "We were saved in hope, by hope, for hope" - This phrase highlights the central role of hope in the process of salvation, emphasizing its significance in the life of a believer.

2. "We were saved in hope" - This phrase suggests that salvation is not a one-time event but an ongoing process that is sustained and fueled by hope.

3. "For hope" - This phrase implies that hope is not only the means by which we are saved but also the goal or outcome of our salvation, specifically the hope of the redemption of the body.

### John Gill:
1. "We are saved by hope... salvation, or glory, is the object of it." 
2. "The hope of it is of great use to the saints, whilst in this world of trouble." 
3. "Good reason there is to hope for it, since the Scriptures of truth so clearly express it; and the person, blood, and righteousness of Christ, lay such a solid foundation for hope of it."

### John Wesley:
1. "Our salvation is now only in hope."
2. "We do not yet possess this full salvation."
3. "Hope is the anchor of our souls, keeping us steadfast in the midst of trials and tribulations."
