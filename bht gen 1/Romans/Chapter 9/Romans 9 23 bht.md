# Romans 9:23 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this Bible verse, God's abundant mercy is highlighted as the vessels of mercy, despite not deserving it, are chosen for salvation and displayed as a glorious testament to God's goodness and power. The deliberate and predestined nature of God's plan for redemption is emphasized, as well as the contrast between those who will receive God's mercy and those who will face His wrath. Ultimately, God's longsuffering and enduring love are showcased, as He prepares the vessels of mercy for eternal glory.

### Jamieson Fausset Brown:
1. "The riches of his glory on the vessels of mercy" refers to the abundant and glorious display of God's mercy towards those who are chosen for salvation.
2. The phrase "glorious exuberance of Divine mercy" emphasizes the overwhelming and abundant nature of God's mercy towards sinners.
3. The choosing and eternal arrangement for the salvation of sinners highlights the deliberate and predestined nature of God's plan for the redemption of humanity.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Besides His great long-suffering toward the vessels of wrath, He had another purpose in the endurance, one with reference to 'vessels of mercy.'" 
2. "The riches of his glory...refers to the fulness of the divine glory, in its beneficence, in its bestowal of blessing."
3. "The vessels are the object of divine mercy in every age, but especially in the gospel dispensation."
4. "The verb does not mean 'predestined,' nor is it to be explained as 'prepared by providence and grace,' since the latter involves a process, while the tense here used points to a single act."
5. "The end of the preparation is the possession of the full and eternal glory of the kingdom of heaven."

### John Gill:
1. "That is, his glorious riches, the perfections of his nature, his love, grace, and mercy, his wisdom, power, faithfulness, justice, and holiness; all which are most evidently displayed in the salvation of his people." 
2. "They are not called so, because deserving of mercy more than others, they are in no wise better than others, and are by nature children of wrath, even as others; but because God of his infinite goodness fills them with his mercy." 
3. "The whole of his conduct is free from blame and censure; the vessels of wrath he shows his wrath upon, are such as fit themselves for destruction, and whom he endures with much longsuffering and patience."

### John Wesley:
1. "By showing such longsuffering even to 'the vessels of wrath,' he did the more abundantly show the greatness of his glorious goodness, wisdom, and power on the vessels of mercy."
2. "Is this any injustice?"
3. "He had himself, by his grace, prepared [the vessels of mercy] for glory."
