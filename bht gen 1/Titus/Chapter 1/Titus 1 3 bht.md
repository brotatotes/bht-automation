# Titus 1:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In due seasons, at the proper time appointed by God, Christ's glory and the promise of eternal life are fully manifested through the preaching of the Gospel. This divine commission, entrusted by God our Savior, reveals the hidden depths of God's eternal purpose. As Christ is publicly displayed, the word of God is proclaimed, shedding light on the truth and the path to godliness. It is in these extraordinary and appointed times that God's work unfolds, carrying the authority of the Highest.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Manifested in its own seasons, fixed by Him for the manifestation."
2. "His word, not to be taken in apposition with ἥν, i.e. the Gospel."
3. "The proclamation with which I was entrusted according to the command of our Saviour God."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "in its own seasons," the seasons appropriate to it, and fixed by God for it
2. the "promise," had lain hidden in His eternal purpose heretofore
3. "eternal life" through preaching

### Albert Barnes:
1. "But hath in due times - At the proper time; the time which he had intended; the best time."
2. "Manifested his word through preaching - ...he has made known his eternal purpose through the preaching of the gospel."
3. "According to the commandment of God our Saviour - Paul always claimed to be divinely commissioned, and affirmed that he was engaged in the work of preaching by the authority of God."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "In due times [καιροις ιδιοις] . Better, in his (or its) own seasons."
2. "Through preaching [εν κηρυγματι] . Rather, in a proclamation."
3. "Which is committed unto me [ο επιστευθην εγω] . Betters wherewith I was intrusted."

### John Calvin:
1. "Christ publicly displayed by his coming those things which the prophets had obscurely predicted."
2. "The 'times' are placed in the hand, and at the disposal, of God, in such a manner that we ought to think that he does everything in the proper order and at the most seasonable time."
3. "God hath manifested the word concerning the life by the preaching of the gospel."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The Gospel is the final revelation of life eternal."
2. "Things promised are still in part concealed; performance alone is full manifestation."
3. "Due times denotes either an epoch in history when all things fitted, or at God's own time."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "In his own seasons (καιροις ιδιοις). Locative case."
2. "The human proclamation (preaching) of God's word."
3. "Of God our Saviour (του σωτηρος ημων θεου). In verse Titus 1:4 he applies the words 'του σωτηρος ημων' to Christ."

### John Gill:
1. "Christ is revealed in the glory of his person, and the fulness of his grace, and in the efficacy of his blood, righteousness, and sacrifice."
2. "The promise of eternal life, which lay in God's heart, in the covenant of grace, and in the hands of Christ, is clearly made known."
3. "The preaching of the Gospel was appointed by God, agreed between the Father and the Son, and suitable to the state, case, and condition of men."

### John Wesley:
1. "His own times are fittest for his own work."
2. "Manifested his word - Containing that promise, and the whole 'truth which is after godliness.'"
3. "And who dares exercise this office on any less authority?"
