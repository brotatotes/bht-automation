# Titus 1:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this verse, we are urged to hold fast to the faithful word, which is the reliable and true doctrine of the Gospel. This teaching, passed down by the apostles, should be firmly grasped and unwaveringly adhered to. By embracing and preserving this sound doctrine, we equip ourselves to exhort and edify others in the truth. It also empowers us to effectively respond to any opposition or contradiction that may arise. This verse emphasizes the importance of staying grounded in the teachings of the apostles as the authoritative source of instruction for the Christian faith.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He must be a man who is firm in his belief of the doctrines of the Christian faith, and a man who can be relied on to maintain and defend those doctrines in all circumstances."
2. "He was to hold the faith as it was delivered by those whom the Saviour had appointed to make it known to mankind."
3. "He was not to dictate, or to denounce; but to seek to convince by the statement of the truth."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The faithful word. The trustworthy, reliable word." 
2. "Agreeing with the apostolic teaching." 
3. "May be able both to exhort in the sound teaching."

### John Calvin:
1. "The faithful word" is the appellation which he gives to that doctrine which is pure, and which has proceeded from the mouth of God.
2. Paul therefore enjoins that those persons shall be chosen who, having cordially embraced the truth of God, and holding it firmly, never allow it to be wrested from them, or can be torn from it.
3. The first thing required in a pastor is, that he be well instructed in the knowledge of sound doctrine; the second is, that, with unwavering firmness of courage, he hold by the confession of it to the last; and the third is, that he make his manner of teaching tend to edification.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The elder is to be one who holds fast by the word, or doctrine, 'which agrees with the teaching' of the apostles, as a word worthy of credit." 
2. "The necessity for this qualification lies in another department of the presbyterial office." 
3. "The elder has first to exhort (address for edification) the congregation of believers in that instruction, which being 'wholesome' (‘sound,’ another word of the Pastoral Epistles), tends to holiness, and next to convict or confute the opponents."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Holding to the faithful word" - The author emphasizes the importance of clinging to the truth and not wavering in one's beliefs (1 Thessalonians 5:14).

2. "The faithful word" - This phrase suggests that the word being referred to is reliable, trustworthy, and worthy of adherence (1 Timothy 1:15; 1 Timothy 6:3; Romans 16:17).

3. "That he may be able" - The purpose of holding to the faithful word is to equip oneself with the ability to effectively respond to those who oppose or contradict it (2 Timothy 1:12; Romans 4:21; Romans 11:23).

### John Gill:
1. "The doctrine of the Gospel, so called because it is true, and to be believed...contains nothing but truth."
2. "This is not only to be held forth by the elder, but to be held fast, and tenaciously abode by; in opposition to all wavering about it, departure from it, dropping or concealing any part of it."
3. "Sound doctrine is the faithful word, the wholesome words of our Lord Jesus Christ, which being retained, qualify an elder to discharge the following branches of his office; to 'exhort' the members of churches to their duty...and to 'convince gainsayers'."

### John Wesley:
1. "As he hath been taught" refers to the teaching or doctrine of the apostles, as seen in Acts 2:42.
2. This phrase emphasizes the importance of following the teachings of the apostles, indicating that they were the authoritative source of instruction for the early Christians.
3. By adhering to the apostles' teaching, believers can ensure that they are following the correct and authentic interpretation of the Scriptures.
