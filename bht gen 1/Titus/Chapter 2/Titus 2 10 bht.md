1. "Not to appropriate to themselves what belongs to their masters."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The fundamental idea of the word is to put far away from another; to set apart for one's self; hence to purloin and appropriate to one's own use." 

2. "Shewing all good fidelity." 

3. "Adorn the doctrine."

### John Calvin:
1. "He censures two vices that are common among servants, petulant replies, and a propensity to steal."
2. "By faith he means fidelity to their masters; and therefore, to show all faith is to act faithfully, without using fraud or doing injury, in transacting the affairs of their masters."
3. "This ought to be a very sharp spur of exhortation to us, when we learn that our becoming conduct adorns the doctrine of God, which, at the same time, is a mirror of his glory."

### Archibald T Robertson:
1. "Not purloining (μη νοσφιζομενους). Present middle participle of νοσφιζω, old verb (from νοσφ, apart), in middle to set apart for oneself, to embezzle, in N.T. only here and Acts 5:2." 
2. "Fidelity (πιστιν). See Galatians 5:22; 1 Timothy 5:12 for πιστις in the sense of faithfulness." 
3. "That they may adorn (ινα κοσμωσιν). Final clause with ινα and present active subjunctive. See 1 Timothy 2:9 for κοσμεω. Paul shows slaves how they may 'adorn' the teaching of God."

### John Gill:
1. "Not purloining..... Or stealing, embezzling their master's substance, taking away, and making use of what is their property."
2. "Showing all good fidelity; approving themselves to be faithful servants in everything they are intrusted with."
3. "To adorn the Gospel, is first to believe and receive it, then to profess it, and hold fast that profession, and walk worthy of it."

### John Wesley:

2. "Soft, obliging faithfulness" - This description portrays fidelity as not only being steadfast, but also gentle and accommodating. It suggests that true faithfulness involves a willingness to go above and beyond, to be kind and considerate in our interactions with others.

3. "That they may adorn the doctrine of God our Saviour" - This statement reminds us that our actions should reflect and enhance the teachings of our faith. It encourages believers to live in a way that brings honor and glory to God, serving as a positive example to others.
