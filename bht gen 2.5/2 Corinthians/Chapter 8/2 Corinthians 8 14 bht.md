# 2 Corinthians 8:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The principle of equality in giving and receiving is emphasized, urging believers to use their abundance to meet the needs of others. This fosters fairness and mutual support within the community. Christians are encouraged to contribute generously while they have the means, as circumstances can change, and they may find themselves in need. This mutual contribution creates a sense of symmetry and unity among believers.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The passage has been curiously misunderstood to mean, ‘that their (the Jewish Christians’) abundance in spiritual things may be imparted to you to supply your deficiency.’"
2. "The modern Romanists introducing the monstrous perversion of the attribution of the merits of the saints to others in the next world."
3. "Notanda virtus eleemosynæ, quæ facit hominem participem meritorum ejus in quem confertur."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "by the rule of equality"
2. "at the present juncture"
3. "in order that, at another season, when your relative circumstances may be reversed"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "That at the present time your abundance may be a supply for their needs, so that at some future time, if there should be occasion for it, their abundance may be a supply for your needs."
2. "The support of the objects of benevolence should be on equal principles... The rich should bear an equal and fair proportion."
3. "Christians should contribute liberally while they have the means. In the vicissitudes of life no one can tell how soon he may be unable to contribute, or may even be dependent on the charity of others himself."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "By an equality [εξ ισοτητος]...I speak on the principle that your abundance should go to equalize the difference created by their want."


### John Calvin:
1. "If it is irksome to you to help your brethren with riches that are of no value, consider how many blessings you are destitute of, and these too, far more precious, with which you may be enriched by those who are poor as to worldly substance."
2. "This participation, which Christ has established among the members of his body, should animate you to be more forward, and more active in doing good."
3. "For the system of proportional right in the Church is this — that while they communicate to each other mutually according to the measure of gifts and of necessity, this mutual contribution produces a befitting symmetry, though some have more, and some less, and gifts are distributed unequally."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "By equality (εξ ισοτητος). Old word from ισος, fair, equal."
2. "Abundancy (περισσευμα). Late word from περισσευω like περισσεια."
3. "Want (υστερημα). Late word from υστερεω, to be in want."

### John Gill:
1. "that now at this time; which was a very necessitous time at Jerusalem, there being a famine there, and their common stock exhausted." 
2. "and so should it ever be their case, as it might be, that they should be reduced, and these other churches increased, and enjoy a large abundance, they might hereafter in their turn supply what would be wanting in them" 
3. "so that the argument is taken from the hope of retribution, in case of such vicissitudes"

### John Wesley:
1. "That their abundance — If need should so require. May be — At another time. A supply to your want: that there may be an equality." 
2. "No want on one side, no superfluity on the other."
3. "It may likewise have a further meaning:-that as the temporal bounty of the Corinthians did now supply the temporal wants of their poor brethren in Judea, so the prayers of these might be a means of bringing down many spiritual blessings on their benefactors: so that all the spiritual wants of the one might be amply supplied; all the temporal of the other."
