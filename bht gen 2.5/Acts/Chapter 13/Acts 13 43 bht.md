# Acts 13:43 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
After the breaking up of the synagogue, Paul and Barnabas spoke to the Jews and proselytes, persuading them to continue in the grace of God. The gospel is called the grace of God, and they were exhorted to persevere in their attachment to it. This success was remarkable and showed the power of the gospel when preached faithfully. Many Jews and proselytes followed Paul and Barnabas, embracing the Lord Jesus and cordially embracing the doctrines of the Gospel. They were encouraged to persist in the grace they had received, as trials were at hand.

### Henry Alford:
1. "After the breaking up of the synagogue."
2. "Paul and Barnabas; and to the Jews and proselytes."
3. "The work of the Gospel begun in the hearts of the converts."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Speaking to them—following up the discourse in the synagogue by some further words of encouragement."
2. "Persuaded them to continue in the grace of God—which they had experienced through the Gospel."
3. "(Compare Acts 11:23)."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The 'gospel' is called the grace (favor) of God and they were exhorted to persevere in their attachment to it." (Acts 13:43)
2. "This success was remarkable, and shows the power of the gospel when it is preached faithfully to people." (Acts 13:43)
3. "It would appear from this that they professedly received the truth and embraced the Lord Jesus." (Acts 13:43)

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Religious [σεβομενων] . Lit., worshipping."
2. "Proselytes [προσηλυτων] . Originally, one who arrives at a place; a stranger; thence of one who comes over to another faith."
3. ""Proselytes [προσηλυτων] . Originally, one who arrives at a place; a stranger; thence of one who comes over to another faith."

### John Calvin:
1. "They exhorted Paul and Barnabas that they should not faint, but stand stoutly in the grace of God."
2. "They saw combats prepared for them; and that therefore they had need of invincible constancy to suffer and abide the brunts of the contrary faction."
3. "They entertained them courteously and gently, and confirmed and strengthened them, that they might persist in the grace which they had received."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "To continue in the grace of God."
2. "It was a very favourite expression of his."
3. "No doubt the very words of Paul used to these Jews and proselytes who followed the missionaries."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "In the N.T. the word occurs only in Matthew 23:15; Acts 2:10; Acts 6:5; Acts 13:43."
2. "Many (both Jews and proselytes) followed (ηκολουθησαν, ingressive aorist active indicative of ακολουθεω) Paul and Barnabas to hear more without waiting till the next Sabbath."
3. "Paul had great powers of persuasion...These Jews 'were beginning to understand for the first time the true meaning of their national history' (Furneaux), 'the grace of God' to them."

### John Gill:
1. "Many of the Jews and religious proselytes followed Paul and Barnabas; who were converted by this sermon, and believed in Christ, and cordially embraced the doctrines of the Gospel, that had been preached."
2. "They spoke a word of exhortation to them, beseeching them to continue in [the doctrine of the grace of God], it springing from the grace of God, and being a declaration of it."
3. "For it is more likely, and was more necessary, that these [the Jews and proselytes] should be exhorted by the apostles, and not the apostles by them."

### John Wesley:
1. "Persuaded them to continue - For trials were at hand." 
2. "In the grace of God - That is, to adhere to the Gospel or Christian faith."
