# Ephesians 3:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Paul humbly acknowledges his unworthiness and insignificance in comparison to the saints and the grace given to him to preach to the Gentiles. The unsearchable riches of Christ are described as inexhaustible and incomprehensible, encompassing wisdom, righteousness, sanctification, and redemption. Paul's humility is genuine, not feigned, and he recognizes that God's grace can transform even the greatest sinner. The unsearchable riches of Christ are the astonishing and boundless treasures of grace bestowed on the Gentiles, which believers will continue to explore for eternity.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "To me, who am less than the least of all saints, was given this grace to preach to the Gentiles..."
2. "The unsearchable riches of Christ."
3. "The fulness of wisdom, righteousness, sanctification, and redemption all centred and summed up in Him."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Not merely was I in times past, but I still am the least worthy of so high an office."
2. "Least of all saints—not merely 'of all apostles.'"
3. "Unsearchable as a mine inexhaustible, whose treasures can never be fully explored."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Unto me, who am less than the least of all saints [...] who am incomparably the least of all the saints; or who am not worthy to be reckoned among the saints."
2. "The unsearchable riches of Christ [...] there was a 'sufficiency' in Christ which could not be traced out or explored. It was wholly incomprehensible."
3. "There is no more emphatic expression in the New Testament than this. It shows that the heart of the apostle was full of admiration of the sufficiency and glory that was in the Saviour."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Less than the least [τω ελαχιστοτερω]. Only here in the New Testament, and very characteristic."
2. "A comparative is formed upon a superlative: more least than all the saints."
3. "Unsearchable [ανεξιχνιαστον]. Only here and Romans 11:33 (note). Which cannot be tracked out."

### John Calvin:
1. "He labors to exhibit himself, and everything that belongs to him, in as humiliating a light as possible, in order that the grace of God may be the more highly exalted."
2. "Most men are ready enough to make professions of feigned humility, while their minds are swelled with pride, and in words to acknowledge themselves inferior to every one else."
3. "By the unsearchable riches of Christ are meant the astonishing and boundless treasures of grace, which God had suddenly and unexpectedly bestowed on the Gentiles."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The great Apostle, however, so truly, so earnestly, felt his own weakness and nothingness... that the mention of God’s grace towards him awakens within... his own sinful nature and unworthiness for so high an office."
2. "He had been a persecutor, and since God’s grace had helped him, there is no one whom it may not help."
3. "'The fulness of wisdom, righteousness, sanctification, and redemption all centred and summed up in Him'... Because Christ is what He is, the riches are unsearchable, exhaustless; because He, through His redeeming work, becomes ours the riches are ours."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Unto me who am less than the least of all saints."
2. "This was not mock humility."
3. "Paul undertook to track out the untrackable in Christ."

### John Gill:
1. "The greatest saints are generally speaking, the most humble souls."
2. "The reasons of their great humility are, because they have the largest discoveries of the love and grace of God and Christ."
3. "The riches of Christ...are unsearchable to the natural man, and cannot be fully investigated by believers themselves; they will be telling over to all eternity."

### John Wesley:
1. "Unto me, who am less than the least of all saints, is this grace given."
2. "Here are the noblest strains of eloquence to paint the exceeding low opinion the apostle had of himself."
3. "The fulness of unfathomable blessings which are treasured up in Christ."
