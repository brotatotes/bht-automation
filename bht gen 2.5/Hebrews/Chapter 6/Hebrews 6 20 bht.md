# Hebrews 6:20 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus is our forerunner, representing and introducing us to God. His ascension is our promotion, and where He has gone, we can follow. He functions as our great high priest, better than Aaron, and has entered into the presence of God on our behalf. True piety is based on knowledge and does not go beyond that. Christ is the most excellent runner, guiding and setting an example for us to follow. He has entered into heaven as our high priest, representing us and interceding for us. Jesus, our glorious forerunner, is now appearing in the presence of God for us.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The 'where' of the Greek implies being IN a place: 'whither' is understood to 'entered,' taken out of 'where'." 
2. "The 'for us' implies that it was not for Himself, as God, He needed to enter there, but as our High Priest, representing and introducing us." 
3. "Christ's ascension is our promotion: and whither the glory of the Head has preceded, thither the hope of the body, too, is called."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "No man can be a Christian who does not do this, or who does not desire to be perfect as God is perfect." 
2. "If a man does not intend to be an eminent Christian, he may be certain he never will be." 
3. "True piety is 'principle.' It is not fancy, or dreaming, or visions, or enthusiasm. It is based on knowledge, and does not go 'beyond' that."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The peculiarity of the new economy is that Christ as high priest goes nowhere where his people cannot follow him. He introduces man into full fellowship with God." 
2. "Become, because his office must be inaugurated by his suffering human life and his death."
3. "The sin in the case supposed is the relinquishment of the spiritual gifts and powers accompanying faith in Christ, and rejecting Christ himself."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The Levitical high priest entered the Holy of Holies on behalf of the people, as Christ also entered into the Holiest of all."
2. "He is now gone to prepare a place for us; we are to follow and to share His glory and His throne."
3. "The 'priest for ever' of the Psalm is now changed into 'high priest,' a title made appropriate by the fact that it is not into the holy place simply, but into the immediate presence of God, He is gone."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Jesus has shown us the way, has gone on ahead, and is the surety and guarantor of our own entrance later."
2. "Our anchor of hope with its two chains of God's promise and oath has laid hold of Jesus within the veil. It will hold fast."
3. "There he functions as our great high priest, better than Aaron for he is 'after the order of Melchizedek,' the point that now calls for elucidation."

### John Gill:
1. "Christ is the most excellent runner; there is none like him; there is none that can come up to him; he has out ran and exceeded all others; he has performed in the best manner; he has run out his race first, and has entered into heaven first by his own blood." 
2. "He is a guide and pattern for them to follow." 
3. "He is entered into heaven for them, as man and Mediator, and as their high priest; where he represents their persons, appears and intercedes for them, takes care of their affairs, and presents their services; prepares mansions of glory for them, and takes possession of heaven in their name, and opens the way for them to follow him thither."

### John Wesley:
1. "Christ who is gone before us is infinitely superior to us."
2. "What an honour is it to believers, to have so glorious a forerunner."
3. "Now appearing in the presence of God for them."
