# John 16:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Holy Spirit's role is to glorify Christ by revealing the truth about Him and His work. The Spirit's teaching focuses on Christ and His teachings, not on anything new or beyond Him. The Spirit's guidance leads believers to a deeper understanding of Christ's character and the sufficiency of His work. The Spirit's influence exalts Christ and His work, leading believers to a greater appreciation of His glory. The Spirit's work in the Church is to glorify Christ and lead believers to Him as the source of their faith. The Spirit enriches believers with the riches of Christ, displaying His glory in all things.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The Spirit guiding into truth is in fact the Son declaring the truth, for He shall shew forth the glory of Christ, by revealing the matters of Christ, the riches of the Father’s love in Him."
2. "This verse is decisive against all additions and pretended revelations subsequent to and besides Christ; it being the work of the Spirit to testify to and declare THE THINGS OF CHRIST; not any thing new and beyond Him."
3. "And this declaration is coincident with inward advance in the likeness and image of Christ, not with a mere external development."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Thus the whole design of the Spirit's office is to glorify Christ."
2. "The subjective nature of the Spirit's teaching—the discovery to the souls of men of what is Christ outwardly—is here very clearly expressed."
3. "The vanity of looking for revelations of the Spirit which shall do anything beyond throwing light in the soul upon what Christ Himself is, and taught, and did upon earth."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The nature of his influence shall be such as to exalt my character and work in view of the mind."
2. "He shall receive his commission and instructions as an ambassador from me, to do my will and complete my work."
3. "All serious impressions produced by him lead to the Lord Jesus...to reveal to us the loveliness of his character, and the fitness of his work to our needs."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Shall receive [ληψεται] . Rev., take. See on 3 32.


### John Calvin:
1. "The Spirit will not come to erect any new kingdom, but rather to confirm the glory which has been given to him by the Father."
2. "If Scripture is quoted against the Pope, he maintains that we ought not to confine ourselves to it, because the Spirit is come, and has carried us above Scripture by many additions."
3. "The Spirit enriches us with no other than the riches of Christ, that he may display his glory in all things."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He shall glorify me, because of that which is mine will he receive and will declare it unto you."
2. "This glory will be given Him by the powerful working of the Holy Spirit in the Church."
3. "To Him as the Alpha and Omega of our faith, and never beyond Him, the Spirit leads us."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He shall glorify me (εκεινος εμε δοξασε). This is the glory of the Holy Spirit, to glorify Jesus Christ."
2. "For he shall take of mine (οτ εκ του εμου λημψετα). Future middle of λαμβανω and a definite promise of the Spirit's guidance in interpreting Christ."
3. "One need only refer to Peter's sermon at pentecost after the coming of the Holy Spirit, to Peter's Epistles, to Paul's Epistles, to Hebrews, to John's Epistles, to see how under the tutelage of the Holy Spirit the disciples grew into the fulness of the knowledge of God in the face of Christ (2 Corinthians 6:4)."

### John Gill:
1. "It declares him to be the only Saviour and Redeemer of lost sinners, that justification is solely by his righteousness, pardon by his blood, and atonement by his sacrifice."
2. "The Spirit of God glorifies Christ in the experience of the saints, by leading them to him for righteousness, peace, and pardon, for grace, and fresh supplies of it, for wisdom and strength, for food and rest, for life and happiness."
3. "The Spirit from Christ takes and shows to the saints...the blessings of grace held forth in the Gospel, such as justification, pardon, adoption, c. and so comforts them, and glorifies Christ."
