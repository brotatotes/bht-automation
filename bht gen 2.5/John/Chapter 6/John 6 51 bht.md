# John 6:51 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus declares Himself as the living bread, His flesh, which He will give for the life of the world. This signifies His sacrifice for sin, offering eternal life to sinners. The use of the word world emphasizes the universal nature of His sacrifice. By partaking in this bread through faith, one can receive eternal life. This discourse highlights Jesus' atoning death for the spiritual life of all people.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The Bread of Life is Himself: and, strictly treated, when we come to enquire what, of that body, soul, and spirit, which constituted Himself, this Bread specifically is, we have His answer that it is His Flesh which He will give."
2. "The flesh here, and the eating of the flesh, are distinct from the blood, and the drinking of the blood. It is then only through or after the Death of the Lord, that by any propriety of language, His Flesh could be said to be eaten."
3. "But the spiritual verity which underlies the ordinance is one and the same with that here insisted on; and so considered, the discourse is, as generally treated, most important towards a right understanding of the ordinance."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "THE BREAD WHICH I WILL GIVE IS MY FLESH, WHICH I WILL GIVE FOR THE LIFE OF THE WORLD."
2. "From this time we hear no more (in this discourse) of "Bread"; this figure is dropped, and the reality takes its place."
3. "The words 'I will give' may be compared with the words of institution at the Supper, 'This is My body which is given for you'."

3. "The use of the word world here shows that the sacrifice of Christ was full free ample, and designed for all men."
