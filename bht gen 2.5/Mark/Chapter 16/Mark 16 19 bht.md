# Mark 16:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
After the Lord's glorious Ascension, He was received up into heaven and sat on the right hand of God, signifying His exaltation and proper condition in glory. The Ascension took place on the Mount of Olives, and Jesus was exalted to honor and power in the heavens. This event marked the completion of the Resurrection and the beginning of Christ's reign on His throne and in His kingdom. His presence at the right hand of God signifies His highest honor and exalted position.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "So then after the Lord... when He comes to His glorious Ascension and its subsequent fruits."
2. "He was received up into heaven."
3. "And sat on the right hand of God... His proper condition in glory."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He was received up into heaven - In a cloud from the Mount of Olives." 
2. "The right hand of God - We are not to suppose that God has hands, or that Jesus sits in any particular direction from God. This phrase is taken from the manner of speaking among men, and means that he was exalted to honor and power in the heavens." 
3. "So, to be seated at the right hand of God, means that Jesus is exalted to the highest honor of the universe."

### John Calvin:
1. "Mark, too, takes no notice of the place and the manner, both of which are described by Luke."
2. "He intended that this mystery of faith should be known by the preaching of the gospel rather than beheld by the eyes."
3. "He did not, after his resurrection, appear indiscriminately to all, so he did not permit all to be the witnesses of his ascension to heaven."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "After such a glorious triumph over death and hell, Christ could not die again, but only return to His former glory and take possession of His throne and kingdom..." 
2. "The Ascension is the natural completion of the Resurrection."
3. "On Christ’s presence there [at the right hand of God], see John 19:3; Acts 2:33; Acts 7:56; Ephesians 1:20; Colossians 3:1."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The Ascension in Mark took place after Jesus spoke to the disciples, not in Galilee (Mark 16:15-18), nor on the first or second Sunday evening in Jerusalem."
2. "We should not know when it took place nor where but for Luke who locates it on Olivet (Luke 24:50) at the close of the forty days (Acts 1:3) and so after the return from Galilee (Matthew 28:16)."
3. "Sat down at the right hand of God (εκαθισεν εκ δεξιων του θεου). Swete notes that the author 'passes beyond the field of history into that of theology,' an early and most cherished belief."

### John Gill:
1. "he was received up into heaven; in a cloud, angels attending him, and devils led captive by him, and with a welcome into his Father's presence."
2. "is an honour never conferred on angels, or any mere creature; and is a peculiar dignity conferred on the human nature of Christ, in union with his divine person."
3. "here he will remain, till his second coming."

### John Wesley:
1. The Lord — How seasonable is he called by this title! After he had spoken to them - For forty days. Luke 24:50.
