# Revelation 3:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Lord commends a few individuals in the church of Sardis who have remained faithful and pure amidst a larger group of unfaithful believers. These individuals have not allowed themselves to be defiled by sin and have kept their Christian profession untarnished. They will be rewarded by walking with the Lord in white, symbolizing their purity and worthiness. While they do not claim heaven on their own merit, it is fitting that they should be counted among the glorified, sharing in the glory of the Redeemer.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The gracious Lord does not overlook any exceptional cases of real saints in the midst of unreal professors."
2. "Meanwhile she is not to sully her Christian profession with any defilement of flesh or spirit, but to 'keep her garments.' For no defilement shall enter the heavenly city."
3. "The promised reward accords with the character of those to be rewarded: keeping their garments undefiled and white through the blood of the Lamb now, they shall walk with Him in white hereafter."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "In most cases, when error and sin prevail, there may be found a few who are worthy of the divine commendation; a few who show that true religion may exist even when the mass are evil."
2. "They were like persons clothed in white walking in the midst of the defiled, yet keeping their raiment from being soiled."
3. "The declaration is not that they have any claim to heaven on the ground of their own merit, or that it will be in virtue of their own works that they will be received there; but that there is a fitness or propriety that they should thus appear in heaven."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Names is equivalent to persons, a few who may be rightly named as exceptions to the general conception."
2. "The meaning is, have not sullied the purity of their Christian life."
3. "The verb denotes a solemn investiture, and means literally to throw or put around."

3. "Their glory shall be the very glory of their Lord, for there is force in the preposition 'along with;' they shall be sharers in what the glorified Redeemer is."
