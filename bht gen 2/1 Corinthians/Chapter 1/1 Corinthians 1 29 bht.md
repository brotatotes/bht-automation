# 1 Corinthians 1:29 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
That all mankind may recognize that they have nothing to boast about in the presence of God. Our human accomplishments and wisdom are temporary and fleeting, much like the beauty of flowers. Our true glory should come from placing our trust and confidence in God, recognizing that our salvation is solely dependent on His grace and not on anything we can achieve on our own.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "That all flesh may have no ground of boasting before God." 
2. "The negative in these clauses goes with the verb, not with the adjective; so that each word retains its proper meaning."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "They who try to glory (boast) because of human greatness and wisdom, are 'confounded' or put to shame." 
2. "Flesh, like 'the flower of the field,' is beautiful, but frail."
3. "We are to glory not before Him, but in Him."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "That man should really have nothing of which to boast; but that the whole scheme should be adapted to humble and subdue him."
2. "It may be observed also, that true virtue and excellence; sincerity and amiableness; honesty and sincerity, are usually found [among those of humble life]."
3. "The gospel is designed to produce humility, and to place all people on a level in regard to salvation."

### John Calvin:
1. "That no flesh should glory...there is nothing left us in which we may justly glory." 
2. "In the presence of the world many delight themselves for the moment in a false glorying, which, however, quickly vanishes like smoke."
3. "Let every thing, therefore, that is at all deserving of praise, be recognized as proceeding from God."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "That no flesh should glory before God. This has been all along the design of God in the erection and growth of His kingdom of grace."
2. "But had the early converts been chiefly drawn from such influential classes, would not the triumphs of Christianity have been set down rather to the rank, power, and culture which it had contrived to draw within its pale than to the Divine power residing in and going along with the message itself?"
3. "Now it was to preclude all such surmises that, by a Divine ordination, the bulk of the converts in every church and for a long time consisted of the despised classes, that none might have even a pretext for glorying before God."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "That no flesh should glory before God."
2. "The first aorist middle of the old verb καυχαομα, to boast, brings out sharply that not a single boast is to be made."
3. "One should turn to 2 Corinthians 4:7 for Paul's further statement about our having this treasure in earthen vessels that the excellency of the power may be of God and not of us."

### John Gill:
1. "That no flesh should glory in his presence." 
2. "He has taken a method in choosing and calling the reverse of these, to stain the glory of all flesh." 
3. "No man may attribute his salvation to anything of the creature, but wholly to the sovereign grace and good pleasure of God."

### John Wesley:
1. That no flesh — A fit appellation. Flesh is fair, but withering as grass.May glory before God — In God we ought to glory.
