# 1 Corinthians 1:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The believers in this passage are commended for their abundance of spiritual gifts and are reassured that they lack nothing in this regard. These gifts include various manifestations of God's kindness towards them and the exercise of faith, hope, and love. Additionally, they are encouraged to patiently and eagerly wait for the day when Jesus will be fully revealed and manifested. This waiting demonstrates their high level of piety and faith. The focus of their anticipation is on the Lord's second coming, which will bring about the fulfillment of salvation for those who eagerly long for Him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "So that ye are behind (others) in no gift of grace; not, lack no gift of grace, which would be genitive." 
2. "ἀπεκδεχομένους as parallel with and giving the result of μὴ ὑστ . κ . τ . λ ."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "ye come behind—are inferior to other Christians elsewhere"
2. "different persons among them had different gifts"
3. "The crowning proof of their 'coming behind in no gift.' Faith, hope, and love, are all exercised herein"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Ye come behind -... they abounded in everything." 
2. "In no gift - ...includes also all the kindnesses of God toward them in producing peace of mind, constancy, humility, etc." 
3. "Waiting for - ...an evidence of a high state of piety. It demands strong faith, and it will do much to elevate the feelings above the world, and to keep the mind in a state of peace."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Its prevailing sense in this epistle is that of special spiritual endowments, such as tongues, prophecy, etc."
2. "Here of spiritual blessings generally."
3. "Denoting assiduous waiting."

### John Calvin:
1. "The Lord has not merely honored you with the light of the gospel, but has eminently endowed you with all those graces that may be of service to the saints for helping them forward in the way of salvation."
2. "For he gives the name of gifts to those spiritual graces that are, as it were, means of salvation to the saints."
3. "Thus the meaning will be, 'So that ye are in want of no gift in the meantime while you are waiting for the day of perfected revelation, by which Christ our wisdom will be fully manifested.'"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The faith of His first coming, to put away sin by the sacrifice of Himself, and the hope of His second appearing without sin unto salvation to them that look for Him." 
2. "These were the two wings on which Christians were taught to mount up as eagles in their spiritual life."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Come behind is to be late... It is a wonderful record here recorded." 
2. "But in 2 Corinthians 8:7-11; 2 Corinthians 9:1-7 Paul will have to complain that they have not paid their pledges for the collection, pledges made over a year before, a very modern complaint." 
3. "Waiting for the revelation... It is an eager expectancy of the second coming of Christ here termed revelation... 'As if that attitude of expectation were the highest posture that can be attained here by the Christian' (F.W. Robertson)."

### John Gill:
1. "So that ye come behind in no gift....by which it appears that they were not inferior in gifts to any of the churches."
2. "Waiting for the coming...when gifts will cease and be of no more use, and when they must all be accounted for."
3. "Who will surely come again, and call his servants and churches to an account for the talents he has intrusted them with."

### John Wesley:
1. Waiting — With earnest desire. For the glorious revelation of our Lord Jesus Christ - A sure mark of a true or false Christian, to long for, or dread, this revelation.
