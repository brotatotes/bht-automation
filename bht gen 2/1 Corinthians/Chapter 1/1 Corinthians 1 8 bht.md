# 1 Corinthians 1:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
God's promise to confirm believers until the end signifies His continuous work in strengthening and upholding them in their faith. This assurance ensures that believers will stand firm and unwavering in their commitment to Christ. Although we currently live in a challenging and laborious day, the day of Christ's return will be marked by His glorious presence, radiating through His transformed saints.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "ὅς] viz. θεός, 1 Corinthians 1:4, not Ἰησοῦς χριστός, in which case we should have ἐν τῇ ἡμέρᾳ αὐτοῦ."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Who—God...unto the end—the coming of Christ."
2. "Blameless in the day of...Christ."
3. "Now is our day to work, and the day of our enemies to try us: then will be the day of Christ, and of His glory in the saints [BENGEL]."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He shall make you 'firm' amidst all your trials, and all the efforts which may be made to shake your faith."
2. "They should be kept, and should not be suffered to fall away and perish."
3. "It is the design of God so to keep his people, and so to justify and sanctify them through the Lord Jesus, that the church may be presented 'a glorious church, without spot or wrinkle' on the Day of Judgment."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Confirm. Compare ver. 6. Unto the end. Of the present aeon or period. See on end of the world, Matthew 28:20."
2. "Blameless [ανεγκλητους] . Used by Paul only. In apposition with you. Rev., unreprovable."
3. "The word here points to appearance at God's bar."

### John Calvin:
1. "Though the expectation of a salvation to come keeps you still in suspense, you ought nevertheless to feel assured that the Lord will never forsake you, but will on the contrary increase what he has begun in you."
2. "It is as if he had said — Though the expectation of a salvation to come keeps you still in suspense, you ought nevertheless to feel assured that the Lord will never forsake you, but will on the contrary increase what he has begun in you."
3. "Blameless...this is the end of our calling — that we may appear pure and unreproachable in the presence of Christ."

### Philip Schaff:
1. 1 Corinthians 1:8.  who shall also confirm you unto the end. . . unreproveable in the day of our Lord  Jesus Christ,  the decisive day of His second coming (Romans 2:16; 2 Corinthians 5:10).
	 

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The relative ος (who) points to Christ." 
2. "End of the age till Jesus comes, final preservation of the saints."
3. "Unimpeachable, for none will have the right to impeach."

### John Gill:
1. "The author of this blessing of confirmation is...God the Father."
2. "Their confirmation in grace by God, the author and giver of all grace."
3. "They [the saints] are...blameless in Christ their head, being justified by his righteousness, and washed in his blood."

### John Wesley:
1. "Confirm you to the end."
2. "Now it is our day, wherein we are to work out our salvation; then it will be eminently the day of Christ, and of his glory in the saints."
