# 1 Corinthians 10:10 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Do not murmur against the faithful ministers and providences of God, for the people of Israel were prone to this sin and paid the consequences. Remember that the destroyer mentioned here refers to the destroying angel sent by God. Be cautious with your words and actions, for rebellion against God's chosen leaders will not go unpunished.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The warning is substantially the same as the last, but regards more the spirit, and its index the tongue."
2. "The destruction referred to must be that related Numbers 16:41 ff. when the pestilence took off 14,700 of the people."
3. "The punishment of the unbelieving congregation in Numbers 14:0 does not seem to answer to the expression ἀπώλοντο ὑπὸ τ . ὀλοθρευτοῦ."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Their murmurs against Moses and Aaron were virtually murmurs against God."
2. "Paul herein glances at the Corinthian murmurs against himself, the apostle of Christ."
3. "The destroyer—THE same destroying angel sent by God as in Exodus 12:23; 2 Samuel 24:16."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Do not repine at the allotments of Providence, or complain of His dealings."
2. "The ground of their complaining was, that they had been disappointed...and that instead of being conducted at once to the land of promise, they were left to perish in the desert."
3. "The simple idea here, however, is, that they died for their sin, and were not permitted to enter the promised land."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Murmur [γογγυζετε] . See on John 6:41. The destroyer [του ολοθρευτου] . The destroying angel, who is called oJ ojloqreuwn, Exodus 12:23.

### John Calvin:
1. "It is a striking and memorable token of God’s wrath against rebels and seditious persons, that murmur against him."
2. "Let us, accordingly, bear in mind that we have to do with God, and not with men, if we rise up against the faithful ministers of God."
3. "By the destroyer you may understand the Angel, who executed the judgment of God."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Neither murmur ye, as some of them murmured, and perished by the destroyer."
2. "The reference here is...to that rebellion which broke out on the return of the spies, when the Lord 'sware that they should not enter into His rest.'"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Implying that some of them were murmuring." 
2. "The reference seems to be to Numbers 16:41 after the punishment of Korah." 
3. "The reference is to the destroying angel of Exodus 12:23 (ο ολοθρευων)."

### John Gill:
1. "Neither murmur ye,.... Against the true apostles of Christ, and faithful ministers of the word; nor against the laws and ordinances of Christ, or providences of God; so some of the members of this church did, or were inclined to do."
2. "The people of Israel were very prone unto, and often guilty of this sin; but what the apostle here has respect unto, is either their murmuring upon the report the spies made of the good land, in Numbers 14:1, or that of Korah and his company against Moses and Aaron, as principal officers."
3. "but it is not necessary, on account of the character given him, to understand an evil angel; it is true indeed, that Satan is by the Jews a called המשחית, 'the destroyer.'"

### John Wesley:
1. The destroyer — The destroying angel. Numbers 14:1,36
