# 1 Corinthians 10:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
I urge you to listen and consider my words with wisdom and discernment. I trust in your ability to understand and judge the truth of what I am saying. Take the time to evaluate and analyze what I have presented to you, and you will see that it aligns with what is right and fitting.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "An appeal to their own sense of what is congruous and possible, as introducing what is to follow."
2. "ὡς expresses an assumption on the Apostle’s part, that they are φρόνιμοι."
3. "ὑμεῖς is emphatic, be YE the judges of what I am saying."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "We cannot divest ourselves of the responsibility of 'judging' for ourselves. The weakness of private judgment is not an argument against its use, but its abuse."
2. "If Paul, an inspired apostle, not only permits, but urges, men to judge his sayings by Scripture, much more should the fallible ministers of the present visible Church do so."
3. "To wise men—refers with a mixture of irony to the Corinthian boast of 'wisdom.' Here you have an opportunity of exercising your 'wisdom' in judging 'what I say.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "I speak as to wise men ... - I speak to people qualified to understand the subject"


### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The warning against the sacrificial feasts and the allusion in ver. 3 suggest the eucharistic feast."
2. "An act of worship is sacramental, as bringing the worshipper into communion with the unseen."
3. "In citing the Eucharist he appeals to them as intelligent (wise) men, concerning a familiar practice."

### John Calvin:
1. "I speak as to wise men."
2. "You understand the efficacy of the sacred Supper in it we are ingrafted into the Lord’s body."
3. "How unseemly a thing is it then, that you should enter into fellowship with the wicked, so as to be united in one body."

### Philip Schaff:
1. 1 Corinthians 10:15.  I speak as to  wise men; judge ye what I say: ‘Apostolic authority I have no need to urge; to your own judgment as wise men I appeal.’
	 

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  As to wise men  (ως φρονιμοις). No sarcasm as in 2 Corinthians 11:19, but plea that they make proper use of the mind (φρεν) given them. 

### John Gill:
1. "I speak as to wise men...judge what I say; consider and weigh the matter well, and you will discern and judge that what I say is proper and pertinent, just and right."
