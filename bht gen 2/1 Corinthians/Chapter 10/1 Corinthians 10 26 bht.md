# 1 Corinthians 10:26 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
For God, the Creator and Owner of the earth and everything in it, has given its produce to humanity. Therefore, we can freely partake of all things without any doubts or concerns.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The principle on which such an eating ought to rest: that all is GOD'S, and for our use."
2. "Where no subjective scruple is cast in, all to be freely partaken of."
3. "See 1 Timothy 4:4."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The ground on which such eating without questioning is justified is, the earth and all its contents... including all meats, belong to the Lord, and are appointed for our use."
2. "Where conscience suggests no scruple, all [meats] are to be eaten."
3. "All are to be eaten."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "For the earth is the Lord’s...It all belongs to the Lord...It may, therefore, be partaken of as his gift, and should be received with gratitude."
2. "And the fulness thereof - All that the earth produces belongs to Him...still the gift of God; still the production of His hand; still the fruit of His goodness and love."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. The earth is the Lord 's, etc. The common form of Jewish thanksgiving before the meal. For fullness, see on Romans 11:12.

### John Calvin:
1. "If the fullness of the earth is the Lord’s, there is nothing in the world that is not sacred and pure."
2. "The fullness of the earth is an expression which is made use of by the Prophet to denote the abundance of blessings, with which the earth is furnished and adorned by the Lord."
3. "Paul is speaking of believers, to whom all things are sanctified through Christ."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "for the earth is the Lord’s, and the fulness thereof"
	 
2. "its contents, therefore, created for use, are free to all who gratefully own Him in it" 
3. (1 Timothy 4:4-5)

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "This verse gives the reason for Paul's advice."
2. "It is a quotation from Psalms 24:1 and was a common form of grace before meals."
3. "Fulness (πληρωμα). Old word from πληροω, to fill, here that with which a thing is filled, whatever fills the earth."

### John Gill:
1. "For the earth is the Lord's, and the fulness thereof.... to be understood of Christ, who by creation and preservation is Lord of the whole earth."
2. "Having broken down the middle wall of partition, [Christ] gives his people the free use of all creatures."
3. "The saints have a full right from Christ, to make use of all the creatures in a moderate way, and with thankfulness."

### John Wesley:
1. "For God, who is the Creator, Proprietor, and Disposer of the earth and all that is therein..."
2. "God hath given the produce of it to the children of men..."
3. "...to be used without scruple."
