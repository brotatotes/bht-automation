# 1 Corinthians 10:31 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In every aspect of life, whether it be in religious or secular matters, it is crucial to keep the glory of God in mind. We should seek to honor Him in all of our actions, recognizing His goodness and acknowledging that everything we have is because of Him. The ultimate goal should be to promote the knowledge and love of God, first within ourselves and then to others. By directing our focus towards these pursuits, we align ourselves with God's purpose and bring honor to His name.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "All such hindrances to God’s glory they are to avoid."
2. "In all things, eating or drinking, or any other particular of conduct, the glory of God is to be the aim, self-regard being set aside."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The godly may 'eat and drink,' and it shall be well with him." (Jeremiah 22:15; Jeremiah 22:16)
2. "To the glory of God" (Colossians 3:17; 1 Peter 4:11), which involves our having regard to the edification of our neighbor.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Whether therefore ye eat or drink...let all be done to the glory of God." 
2. "We live to the glory of God when we honor him in all the relations which he sustains to us...and when we so live as to lead those around us to cherish elevated conceptions of his goodness, and mercy, and holiness." 
3. "The rule is universal. It extends to everything...assuredly we should in all other things."

### John Calvin:
1. "There is no part of our life, and no action so minute, that it ought not to be directed to the glory of God."
2. "We must take care that, even in eating and drinking, we may aim at the advancement of [the glory of God]."
3. "If we are eagerly desirous of the glory of God, as it becomes us to be, we will never allow, so far as we can prevent it, his benefits to lie under reproach."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "To give specific directions for every supposable case is neither needful nor possible, for what is proper in one case may in another be the reverse..." 
2. "If only guided by the great principle of what is and what is not for the glory of God, you will be at no loss how to act."
3. "Whether therefore ye eat, or drink, or whatsoever ye do, do all to the glory of God."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "To the glory of God." This is the ruling motive in the Christian's life, not just having his own way about whims and preferences.

### John Gill:
1. "in doing of which he directs them to have the glory of God in view, and so to conduct, that that end may be answered"
2. "when every mercy is considered and owned as coming from him; and when we confess ourselves unworthy of any; and when we ascribe all we have to the free and unmerited goodness of God"
3. "God's glory is the end of all his works and actions; in creation, providence, and grace; in election, in the covenant, in the blessings and promises of it, in redemption, in the effectual calling, and in bringing many sons to glory"

### John Wesley:
1. "Whatsoever ye do - In all things whatsoever, whether of a religious or civil nature, in all the common, as well as sacred, actions of life, keep the glory of God in view."
2. "Steadily pursue in all this one end of your being, the planting or advancing the vital knowledge and love of God, first in your own soul, then in all mankind."
