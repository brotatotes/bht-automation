# 1 Corinthians 10:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
With the majority of them, God was not pleased, despite the numerous signs of His presence among them. They were condemned and violently overthrown, showcasing the severity of God's wrath upon their disobedience and lack of faith. This serves as a cautionary tale for us to be mindful and learn from their mistakes, for God will not allow our transgressions to go unpunished.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "With the more part of them (in fact the exceptions were Joshua and Caleb only), God was not well pleased."
2. "The very words of the LXX, see ref."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "But—though they had so many tokens of God's presence."
2. "the majority of them"; "the whole part."
3. "Not, with the more part of them was...God...for...the event showed, they had not pleased God."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The design of the apostle here is, to remind them that although they enjoyed so many privileges, yet they were destroyed; and thus to admonish the Corinthians that their privileges did not constitute an absolute security from danger."
2. "The phrase rendered here 'with many'... should have been rendered 'with most of them,' literally 'with the many; and it means that with the greater part of them God was not well pleased."
3. "They were overthrown... by the pestilence, by wars, or died by natural and usual diseases, so that they did not reach the land of Canaan."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "All perished save Caleb and Joshua."
2. "The word [overthrown] belongs mostly to later Greek, though found in Herodotos in the general sense of slaying."
3. "It is used of spreading a couch."

### John Calvin:
1. "Let us, therefore, take heed, lest the same thing should happen to us, being admonished by so many examples, for God will not suffer that to go unpunished in us, which he punished so severely in them."
2. "Hence the manna, in relation to God, was spiritual meat even to unbelievers, but because the mouth of unbelievers was but carnal, they did not eat what was given them."
3. "Proof is here furnished, by adducing a token, that they did not please God — inasmuch as he exercised his wrath upon them with severity, and took vengeance on their ingratitude."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "With most of them God was not well pleased...for they were overthrown in the wilderness."
2. "Caleb and Joshua, because they 'had another spirit with them, and followed the Lord fully.'"
3. "They were overthrown in the wilderness (as the issue shewed)."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "A mournful understatement," for only two (Caleb and Joshua) actually reached the Promised Land (Numbers 14:30-32).
2. All the rest were rejected or αδοκιμο (1 Corinthians 9:27).
3. Powerful picture of the desolation wrought by the years of disobedience and wanderings in the desert by this verb quoted from Numbers 14:16.

### John Gill:
1. "As he is with none but those that are in Christ; and with none of the services of men, but what are done in faith, which become acceptable to him through Jesus Christ."
2. "However, they were not all, excepting Joshua and Caleb, as some interpreters understand it; for not all that died in the wilderness were out of the special grace and favour of God."
3. "He does not say merely that they died there, for many with whom God was well pleased died there; but these, their carcasses fell in the wilderness, being stricken, thrown down, and overthrown by the immediate hand of God."

### John Wesley:
1. Yet — Although they had so many tokens of the divine presence.They were overthrown — With the most terrible marks of his displeasure.
