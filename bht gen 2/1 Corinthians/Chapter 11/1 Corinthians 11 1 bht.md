# 1 Corinthians 11:1 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Let us imitate the example of Paul, who imitated Christ by seeking the glory of God rather than his own, and by living a blameless life. He encouraged the Corinthians to not focus on worldly status, but instead to humbly serve others in order to win them over to the faith. Therefore, let us strive to follow this example and live in a way that brings honor to God.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  31 11:1. ]  General conclusion of this part of the Epistle, enforced by the example of himself .

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Rather belonging to the end of the tenth chapter, than to this chapter."
2. "Followers—Greek, 'imitators.'"
3. "We are to follow Christ first, and earthly teachers only so far as they follow Christ."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Be ye followers of me - Imitate my example in the matter now under discussion."
2. "As I deny myself; as I seek to give no offence to anyone; as I endeavor not to alarm the prejudices of others, but in all things to seek their salvation, so do you."
3. "Even as I also am of Christ - I make Christ my example."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Followers [μιμηται] . Lit., imitators, as Rev. This verse belongs to the closing section of ch. 10.

### John Calvin:
1. "Paul had there brought forward his own example in confirmation of his doctrine. Now, in order that the Corinthians may understand that this would be becoming in them, he exhorts them to imitate what he had done, even as he had imitated Christ."
2. "For while it is the part of a good teacher to enjoin nothing in words but what he is prepared to practice in action, he must not, at the same time, be so austere, as straightway to require from others everything that he does himself, as is the manner of the superstitious."
3. "Let us, therefore, maintain so much the more carefully this doctrine of Paul — that we are to follow men, provided they take Christ as their grand model, (πρωτότυπον,) that the examples of the saints may not tend to lead us away from Christ, but rather to direct us to him."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "When the first love of the converts began to cool, and, as a natural consequence, they drew closer to their heathen acquaintances and fellow-citizens."
2. "Invitations would be given them, in the first instance, to the private houses of their friends, and, by and by, to the religious festivals of their townsmen."
3. "For accepting such invitations plausible reasons would easily occur."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "In the principle of considerate love as so clearly shown"
2. "The preacher is a leader and is bound to set an example or pattern"
3. "This verse clearly belongs to the preceding chapter and not to chapter 11."

### John Gill:
1. "He imitated Christ, who sought not his own glory, but the glory of him that sent him."
2. "He was desirous that they should likewise be blameless, harmless, and without offence until the day of Christ."
3. "He would have them not to mind high things, but condescend to men of low estates, and become all things to all, that they might gain some as he did."
