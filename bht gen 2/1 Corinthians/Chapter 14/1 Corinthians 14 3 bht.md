# 1 Corinthians 14:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
He who prophesies speaks to men for their edification, to build them up in Christ. His words serve as an exhortation, encouraging them to exercise their grace with greater vigor and to fulfill their duties with a cheerful and fervent spirit. Additionally, his message brings comfort to those whose minds are distressed, whether it be due to the temptations of Satan, divine desertions, inward corruptions, or outward afflictions.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He that speaks under the influence of inspiration in the common language of his hearers."
2. "Both were under the influence of the Holy Spirit; both might speak the same truths; both might occupy an equally important and necessary place in the church; but the language of the one was intelligible to the church, the other not."
3. "Speaks so as to enlighten and strengthen the church."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "For edification see on build up, Acts 20:32."
2. "Exhortation, so American Rev. Rev., comfort. See on Luke 6:24."
3. "Paramuqia comfort, Rev., consolation, occurs only here in the New Testament."

### John Calvin:
1. "Prophecy is profitable to all, while a foreign language is a treasure hid in the earth."
2. "What great folly, then, it is to spend all one’s time in what is useless, and, on the other hand, to neglect what appears to be most useful!"
3. "To speak to edification, is to speak what contains doctrine fitted to edify."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Edification (οικοδομην). Building up."
2. "Comfort (παρακλησιν). Encouragement, calling to one's side."
3. "Consolation (παραμυθιαν). Old word...a stimulating word...Edification, cheer, incentive in these words."

### John Gill:
1. "speaketh unto men to edification; to the building of them up in Christ"
2. "exhortation; to the more vigorous exercise of grace, and the more cheerful and fervent discharge of duty"
3. "comfort; of distressed minds, either through the temptations of Satan, or divine desertions, or inward corruptions, or outward afflictions"
