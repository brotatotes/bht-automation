# 1 Corinthians 15:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
If Christ has not risen, then our preaching is empty, lacking any true basis. This statement highlights the vital role of the resurrection in Christian preaching and belief. Without the resurrection, our preaching would be meaningless and our faith would lack substance. The truth and power of Christianity hinges on the reality of Christ's resurrection.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "If Christ’s Resurrection be gone, then also our faith is gone."
2. "Idle also is our preaching, idle also is your faith."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The principal argument of the first preachers in support of Christianity was that God had raised Christ from the dead."
2. "If this fact were false, the faith built on it must be false too."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "If Christ is not risen, then is our preaching vain - Another consequence which must follow if it be held that there was no resurrection, and consequently that Christ was not risen. It would be vain and useless to preach."
2. "If that were not true, the whole system was false, and Christianity was an imposition... It would be 'false' to affirm that the Christian system was from heaven; it would be useless to proclaim such a system, since it could save no one."
3. "Were they, could they be prepared to admit a doctrine which involved the consequence that all the evidences which they had that the apostles preached the truth were delusive, and that all the evidences of the truth of Christianity which had affected their minds and won their hearts were false and deceptive?"

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Vain [κενον] . Empty, a mere chimaera

### John Calvin:
1. "Then is our preaching vain — not simply as having some mixture of falsehood, but as being altogether an empty fallacy."
2. "For what remains if Christ has been swallowed up by death — if he has become extinct — if he has been overwhelmed by the curse of sin — if, in fine, he has been overcome by Satan?"
3. "In short, if that fundamental article is subverted, all that remains will be of no moment. For the same reason he adds, that their faith will be vain, for what solidity of faith will there be, where no hope of life is to be seen?"

### Philip Schaff:
1. 1 Corinthians 15:14.  and . . .  then is our preaching vain, your faith  also is vain  all Christianity, as a historical fact, is subverted.
	 

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Both Paul's preaching and their faith are empty if Christ has not been raised."
2. "If the sceptics refuse to believe the fact of Christ's resurrection, they have nothing to stand on."

### John Gill:
1. "If Christ be not risen, then is our preaching vain; false, empty, delusory, unprofitable, and useless..."
2. "...the preaching of his incarnation, obedience, sufferings, and death, is of no use and avail, if he has not abolished death, and brought life and immortality to light..."
3. "...that faith which is one, uniform, harmonious, and consistent, which was once delivered to the saints; which they are to stand fast in, to strive, contend, and fight for, and not part with at any rate, upon any account whatever; and yet this, and the preaching and belief of it, are useless and insignificant things, if Christ is not risen..."

### John Wesley:
1. Then is our preaching — From a commission supposed to be given after the resurrection.Vain — Without any real foundation.
