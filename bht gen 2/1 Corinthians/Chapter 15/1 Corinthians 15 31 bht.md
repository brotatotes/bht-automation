# 1 Corinthians 15:31 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The apostle Paul, in 1 Corinthians 15:31, solemnly declares by all his reasons for rejoicing in the believers, that he faces the constant and imminent risk of death. He endures daily sufferings and persecutions that can be compared to a daily dying. Paul's willingness to lay down his life for Christ and the Gospel is a testament to his strong belief in the resurrection of the dead. His words reflect a deep devotion and sacrifice in his journey of faith.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "To die daily is a strong expression for to be daily in sight of death and expecting it." (31.)
2. "He strengthens [his statement] by an asseveration, grounded on his boast of them as his work in Christ." (31.)
3. "He would not think of deceiving those of whom he boasted before God in connexion with Christ." (31.)

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "by your rejoicing—by the glorying which I have concerning you, as the fruit of my labors in the Lord."
2. "I die daily—This ought to stand first in the sentence, as it is so put prominently forward in the Greek. I am day by day in sight of death, exposed to it, and expecting it."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "I protest, or solemnly declare by the glorying or exultation which I have on your account; by all my ground of glorying in you; by all the confident boasting and expectation which I have of your salvation."
2. "So certainly as I confidently expect your salvation, and so certainly as we look to eternal life, so certain is it that I am constantly exposed to die, and suffer that which may he called a daily death."
3. "I endure so many sufferings and persecutions, that it may be said to be a daily dying. I am constantly in danger of my life; and my sufferings each day are equal to the pains of death."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "You Corinthian Christians are the fruit of my apostolic labor which has been at a daily risk to life; and as truly as I can point to you as such fruit, so truly can I say, 'I die daily."'
2. "I am in constant peril of my life."
3. "And each day, nay, each hour, I die beforehand, enduring many deaths instead of one, the last."

### John Calvin:
1. "I am every day, incessantly beset with death."
2. "I die daily... because death in a manner was impending over me."
3. "Believe, therefore, a man who is thoroughly experienced."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "I protest by that glorying in you... I die daily." (1 Corinthians 15:31)

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Paul takes solemn oath by the use of νη (common in Attic) with the accusative." 
2. "I am in daily peril of death."

### John Gill:
1. "He always bore in his body the dying of the Lord Jesus, and was continually delivered to death for Jesus' sake."
2. "he did not count his life dear unto himself, but was very willing to lay it down for the sake of Christ and his Gospel."
3. "which he would never have done, if he had not good reason to believe the doctrine of the resurrection of the dead."

### John Wesley:
1. "I protest by your rejoicing, which I have — Which love makes my own."
2. "I die daily — I am daily in the very jaws of death."
3. "Beside that I live, as it were, in a daily martyrdom."
