# 1 Corinthians 15:57 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this passage, we are reminded of our victory over sin, death, and hades. It is not something we have achieved on our own, but a gift from God. Through the work of our Lord Jesus Christ, who conquered sin and death, we have obtained this victory. It is through Him that we are able to experience the blessings of this triumph. The mention of God's full name adds a sense of reverence and grandeur to the thanksgiving, emphasizing that our victory is a result of His grace and power.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "For this blessed consummation of victory over death."
2. "He breaks out in thanks to God, who gives it to us (present, as being certain) through our Lord Jesus Christ."
3. "The Name in full, as befits the solemnity and majesty of the thanksgiving."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The victory was in no way due to ourselves."
2. "The victory, which death and Hades had aimed at, but which, notwithstanding the opposition of them, as well as of the law and sin, we have gained."
3. "The repetition of the word is appropriate to the triumph gained."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "God alone is the author of this victory."
2. "He formed the plan; he executed it in the gift of his Son; and he gives it to us personally when we come to die."
3. "By his death, thus destroying the power of death; by his resurrection and triumph over the grave; and by his grace imparted to us to enable us to sustain the pains of death, and giving to us the hope of a glorious resurrection."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "It is therefore Death alone that can make any man suddenly know himself."
2. "He holds a glass before the eyes of the most beautiful, and makes them see therein their deformity and rottenness; and they acknowledge it."
3. "O eloquent, just and mighty Death! whom none could advise, thou hast persuaded; what none hath dared, thou hast done; and whom all the world hath flattered, thou only hast cast out of the world and despised."

### John Calvin:
1. "Death has no sting with which to wound except sin, and the law imparts to this sting a deadly power."
2. "We may already with confidence glory in [the benefits of Christ's victory], because it is necessary that what has been accomplished in the Head should be accomplished, also, in the members."
3. "Though we still carry about with us the remains of sin, it, nevertheless, does not reign in us: though it still stings us, it does not do so fatally, because its edge is blunted."

### Philip Schaff:
