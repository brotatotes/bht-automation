# 1 Corinthians 15:58 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Be firm and strong in the faith, knowing that your work and effort in the Lord will not be in vain. The hope of the resurrection and future glory should motivate us to persevere and abound in good works, for our labor in the Lord will be rewarded. Let us continue to be steadfast and unwavering, always progressing in our faith and dedication to the Lord's work, as we eagerly anticipate the day when death will be overcome and our hope will be fully realized.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Seeing that the victory is sure."
2. "Knowing that your labor is not vain."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Beloved—Sound doctrine kindles Christian love."
2. "Steadfast—not turning aside from the faith of the resurrection of yourselves."
3. "In the Lord—applying to the whole sentence and its several clauses: Ye, as being in the Lord by faith, know that your labor in the Lord (that is, labor according to His will) is not to be without its reward in the Lord (through His merits and according to His gracious appointment)."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Be firm, strong, confident in the faith, in view of the truth that you will be raised up."
2. "The hope of the resurrection and of future glory should stimulate us to great and self-denying efforts in honor of Him who has revealed that doctrine, and who purposes graciously to reward us there."
3. "All this is in the way to immortality, and is closely and indissolubly connected with immortality."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Steadfast, unmovable. The former refers to their firm establishment in the faith; the latter to that establishment as related to assault from temptation or persecution."
2. "Fixedness is a condition of abounding in work."
3. "All activity has its center in rest."

### John Calvin:
1. "Since your labor...is not in vain in the Lord, be steadfast, and abound in good works...the hope of a resurrection makes us not be weary in well doing." 
2. "...if the hope of reward is taken away and extinguished, alacrity in running will not merely grow cold, but will be altogether destroyed."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The way not to go back is to go forward, the way to be 'unmoveable' is to be 'always abounding.' The secret of stability is progress."
2. "Not to advance is to recede, forasmuch as ye know that your labour is not in vain in the Lord."
3. "Thus, with beautiful calmness and ease, does the apostle come down, in this closing verse, from the height to which he had risen in the verses immediately preceding, to the everyday work and warfare of life."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Be ye steadfast, unmovable. Let the sceptics howl and rage."
2. "Paul has given rational grounds for faith and hope in Christ the Risen Lord and Saviour."
3. "The best answer to doubt is work."

### John Gill:
1. "Be ye steadfast, unmoveable; in all the doctrines of the Gospel, and particularly in this of the resurrection of the dead..."
2. "Always abounding in the work of the Lord; going on in it, being more and more in the practice of it; either in the work of the ministry... or any other work, even all good works..."
3. "Forasmuch as ye know that your labour is not in vain in the Lord; the labour of such who were in the ministry was not in vain, but was by the Lord made useful for the conversion of sinners, and the edification of saints..."

### John Wesley:
1. "Be ye steadfast — In yourselves.Unmovable — By others; continually increasing in the work of faith and labour of love."
2. "Knowing your labour is not in vain in the Lord — Whatever ye do for his sake shall have its full reward in that day."
3. "Let us also endeavour, by cultivating holiness in all its branches, to maintain this hope in its full energy; longing for that glorious day, when, in the utmost extent of the expression, death shall be swallowed up for ever, and millions of voices, after the long silence of the grave, shall burst out at once into that triumphant song, O death, where is thy sting? O hades, where is thy victory?"
