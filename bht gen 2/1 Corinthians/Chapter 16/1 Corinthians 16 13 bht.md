Watch ye, for you must be vigilant not only against external threats but also against the temptations within yourselves. Stand fast in the faith, unwavering in your belief and trust in God, even in the face of uncertainty and doubt. ### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Watch ye": for ye are slumbering. "Stand": for ye are like men tottering. "Quit you like men; be strong": for ye are effeminate ( :-). 
2. "Let all your things be done with charity": not with strifes as at present.
3. "In the faith" which was assailed by some.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They were to watch lest their souls should be ruined, and their salvation endangered; lest the enemies of the truth and of holiness should steal silently upon them, and surprise them."
2. "Be firm in maintaining what you believe to be true, and in holding on to your personal confidence in God, notwithstanding all the arts, insinuations, and teachings of seducers and the friends of false doctrine."
3. "Be strong - Be firm, fixed, steadfast."

### John Calvin:
1. "He exhorts them to watch, in order that Satan may not oppress them, finding them off their guard."
2. "Now watchfulness of spirit is this — when, free and disentangled from earthly cares, we meditate on the things of God."
3. "He stirs them up to manly fortitude...exhorts them fourthly to strengthen themselves, or gather strength."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Watch ye, stand fast in the faith, quit you like men." 
2. "Be strong."
3. "In the Greek of the Old Testament we find these words more than once together."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Watch ye (γρηγορειτε). Stay awake."
2. "Quit you like men (ανδριζεσθε). Play the man."

### John Gill:
1. "Watchfulness, not for the coming of Apollos, and a convenient season for that; but over themselves, over their hearts, thoughts, affections, words, actions, and their whole conversations."
2. "Unwatchfulness exposes to many evils, temptations, and snares."
3. "Quit yourselves like men; like men of wisdom and understanding; be not like children for non-proficiency, instability, and weakness."

### John Wesley:
1. "Watch ye — Against all your seen and unseen enemies."
2. "Stand fast in the faith — Seeing and trusting him that is invisible."
3. "Acquit yourselves like men — With courage and patience."
