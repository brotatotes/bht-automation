# 1 Corinthians 4:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Timothy, being a faithful and beloved son in the Lord, has been sent to the Corinthians as a reminder of Paul's teachings and way of life. Paul emphasizes that Timothy is trustworthy and faithful, and therefore able to faithfully represent him. Through Timothy, Paul hopes to bring to remembrance his ways, which are centered on Christ and his gospel, and to encourage the Corinthians to follow his example in all things.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "ἐν κυρίῳ points out the spiritual nature of the relationship."
2. "Timothy, by being himself a close imitator of the Christian virtues and teaching of his and their spiritual father, would bring to their minds his well-known character, and way of teaching."
3. "To shew the importance of this his manner of teaching, he reminds them of his unvarying practice of it: and as he was guided by the Spirit, by inference, of its universal necessity in the churches."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "For this came—that ye may the better 'be followers of me', through his admonitions." 
2. "Timothy, from his spiritual connection with Paul, as converted by him, was best suited to remind them of the apostle's walk and teaching, which they in some respects, though not altogether, had forgotten."
3. "An argument implying that what the Spirit directed Paul to teach 'everywhere' else, must be necessary at Corinth also."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "In order to remind you of my doctrines and my manner of life."
2. "He sent Timothy and Erastus into Macedonia, probably with instruction to go to Corinth if convenient."
3. "He wished them simply to conform to the common rules of the churches, and to be like their Christian brethren everywhere."

### John Calvin:
1. "That you may know what my manner of life is, and whether I am worthy to be imitated, listen to what Timothy has to say, who will be prepared to be a faithful witness of these things."
2. "In calling him his dearly beloved son, he intimates that he knew him intimately, and was acquainted with all his affairs; and farther, he speaks of him as faithful in the Lord."
3. "It is then the part of a prudent minister so to regulate his procedure, and to observe such a method of instruction, that no such objection may be brought against him, but he shall be prepared to answer on the same ground as Paul does."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "For this cause have I sent unto you Timothy, who is my beloved and faithful son in the Lord."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Timothy had already gone as seems clear from 1 Corinthians 16:10."
2. "Paul expects his teachings and practices to be followed in every church."
3. "Timothy as Paul's spokesman will remind the Corinthians of Paul's teachings."

### John Gill:
1. "For this cause have I sent unto you Timotheus....in all things trusty and faithful." 
2. "And faithful in the Lord; a faithful steward of the mysteries of grace." 
3. "Who shall bring you into remembrance of my ways...which are recommended by their being such ways,which be in Christ; the doctrines he had preached among them, the sum and substance of them were Christ, and him crucified."

### John Wesley:
1. My beloved son — Elsewhere he styles him "brother," 2 Corinthians 1:1; but here paternal affection takes place.As I teach — No less by example than precept.
