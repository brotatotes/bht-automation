# 1 Corinthians 9:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
If these supposed apostles claim the right to be maintained, shouldn't we, who have labored even more for you, have an even greater right? Yet, we have chosen not to assert this right, so as not to hinder the spread of the gospel or give any opportunity for criticism.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The second ἀλλά is not in apposition with the first, but in opposition to the idea implied in ἐχρ . τῇ ἐξ . ταύτῃ ."
2. "στέγομεν...of enduring or bearing up against."
3. "The ἐγκοπαί (hindrances)...would arise from his being charged with covetousness and self-seeking, which his independence of them would entirely prevent."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "we rather—considering our greater labors for you"
2. "We desire to conceal (literally, 'hold as a water-tight vessel') any distress we suffer from straitened circumstances."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "We have chosen to forego this right, and to labor for our own support."
2. "We subject ourselves to poverty, want, hunger, thirst, nakedness, rather than urge a 'claim' on you."
3. "His conduct therefore in this was just one illustration of the principle on which he said 1 Corinthians 8:13 he would always act; a readiness to deny himself of things lawful, if by that he could promote the welfare of others."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The right to claim maintenance."
2. "Hence to protect by covering, as with a tight ship or roof."
3. "In the sense of cutting into one's way, it gets the meaning of hindrance."

### John Calvin:
1. "He does not, however, make mention of what he has done, but of what he would have done in accordance with his right, if he had of his own accord refrained from using it."
2. "He returns now to the point on which the matter hinges — that he had of his own accord given up that power which no one could refuse him, and that he was prepared rather to suffer all things, than by the use of his liberty throw any impediment in the way of the progress of the gospel."
3. "He wishes, therefore, that the Corinthians should, after his example, keep this end in view — to do nothing that would hinder or retard the progress of the gospel."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Nevertheless we did not use this right." 
2. "That we may cause no hindrance to the gospel of Christ."
3. "To cut off the pretext of mercenary motives, slanderously insinuated against him by opponents."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "But we bear all things (αλλα παντα στεγομεν). Old verb to cover (στεγη, roof) and so to cover up, to conceal, to endure (1 Corinthians 13:7 of love)."
2. "That we may cause no hindrance (ινα μη τινα ενκοπην δωμεν). Late word ενκοπη, a cutting in (cf. radio or telephone) or hindrance from ενκοπτω, to cut in, rare word (like εκκοπη) here only in N.T. and once in Vettius Valens."
3. "How considerate Paul is to avoid 'a hindrance to the gospel of Christ' (τω ευαγγελιω του Χριστου, dative case and genitive) rather than insist on his personal rights and liberties, an eloquent example for all modern men."

### John Gill:
1. "If others are partakers of this power over you, are not we rather?"

### John Wesley:
1. "If others — Whether true or false apostles. Partake of this power — Have a right to be maintained." 
2. "Do not we rather — On account of our having laboured so much more?" 
3. "Lest we should give any hinderance to the gospel - By giving an occasion of cavil or reproach."
