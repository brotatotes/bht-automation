# 1 Corinthians 9:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Paul acknowledges that his ability to preach the Gospel without charge and with great success is solely from God. He realizes that he has nothing to boast about because his preaching is only possible through the power and Spirit of God. Paul considers his duty to preach the Gospel as an expression of his love and gratitude towards God. He solemnly affirms that if he were to neglect this calling, woe would be upon him, for he is under an obligation to fulfill it.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The essential idea here is, therefore, that there was such a necessity laid on him in his call to preach the gospel, that his compliance with that call could not be regarded as appropriately connected with reward."
2. "My preaching is in a manner inevitable, and cannot therefore be regarded as that in which I especially glory."

### John Calvin:
1. "Let not the man, then, who has been once called to it, imagine that he is any longer at liberty to withdraw when he chooses... for he is devoted to the Lord and to the Church, and bound by a sacred tie, which it were criminal to break asunder."
2. "He says that a curse was ready to fall upon him if he did not preach the gospel. Why? Because he has been called to it, and therefore is constrained by necessity."
3. "What sort of successors, then, have the Apostles in the Pope and the other mitred bishops, who think that there is nothing that is more unbecoming their station than the duty of teaching!"
