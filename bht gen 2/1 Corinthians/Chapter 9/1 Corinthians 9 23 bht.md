# 1 Corinthians 9:23 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Paul's primary motivation for becoming all things to all people was not for his own benefit, but for the sake of spreading and advancing the Gospel. He desired to share in the blessings and joys of the Gospel with all, including those from various backgrounds and cultures. The conversion and salvation of sinners were sources of great joy for Paul, and he longed to experience the fruits of the Gospel alongside fellow believers, regardless of their differences. His goal was for the Gospel to be useful and impactful among all people, surpassing any personal gain or comfort.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Not only this of which I have spoken, but all things I do on account of the gospel."
2. "That I may be a fellow-partaker of it (of the blessings promised in the gospel to be brought by the Lord at His coming)."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Fellow partaker": of the Gospel blessings promised at Christ's coming.
2. "With them, namely, with those thus 'gained' by me to the Gospel."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "For the gospel's sake - That it may be advanced, and may be successful."
2. "That I might be partaker thereof with you - You hope to be saved. You regard yourselves as Christians; and I wish to give evidence also that 'I' am a Christian, and that I shall be admitted to heaven to partake of the happiness of the redeemed."
3. "This he did, by so denying himself as to give evidence that he was truly actuated by Christian principles."

### John Calvin:
1. "For when he declares, that his aim had been, that he might become a partaker of the gospel, he indirectly intimates, that all who do not act the same part with him are unworthy of the fellowship of the gospel."
2. "To become a partaker of the gospel is to receive the fruit of it."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "And I do all things for the gospel’s sake, that I may be a joint partaker thereof joint partaker of the Gospel’s blessed fruits and final joys with all who are saved by it."
2. "Hitherto the apostle has dwelt on the duty of self-denial for the good of others."
3. "Now, however, he rises higher to the absolute necessity of it to final salvation even in himself, as an indispensable feature of the Christian character."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "That I may become co-partner with others in the gospel."
2. "The point is that he may be able to share the gospel with others, his evangelistic passion."
3. "Paul does not wish to enjoy the gospel just by himself."

### John Gill:
1. "He became all things to all men...not for his own sake, but for the spread of the Gospel and its greater usefulness among men."
2. "The conversion and salvation of sinners...would be a matter of joy both to him and them."
3. "He desired to enjoy the blessings of grace and eternal life in common with others, not only with the Corinthians, but with Jews and Gentiles; with men of all sorts, who may be gained over to Christ, and saved by him, through the ministry of the word."
