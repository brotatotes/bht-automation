# 1 John 1:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The purpose of the apostle's declaration is to confirm and illustrate what had been said before, as well as to lead into what follows. Through their testimony, they declare what they have seen and heard from Jesus, in order to bring others into fellowship with them. This fellowship not only involves a close relationship with each other but also with the Father and the Son, through the work of the Holy Spirit.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Our fellowship is with the Father and with His Son." Faith realizes what we have not seen as spiritually visible; not till by faith we too have seen, do we know all the excellency of the true Solomon.
2. He Himself is ours; He in us and we in Him. We are "partakers of the divine nature."
3. We know God only by having fellowship with Him; He may thus be known, but not comprehended.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The object of the apostle in what he wrote was, that they might have the same views of the Saviour which he had, and partake of the same hope and joy. This is the true notion of fellowship in religion."
2. "And truly our fellowship is with the Father - With God the Father...there was a union in feeling, and affection, and desire, and plan, and this was to him a source of joy."
3. "The Christian will have fellowship with his God and Saviour in the triumphs of the latter day...when the Redeemer shall appear, that he may be admired and adored by assembled worlds." (Total: 67 words)

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The true life in man, which comes through the acceptance of Jesus as the Son of God, consists in fellowship with God and with man."
2. "Our fellowship... indicates fellowship as a distinguishing mark of Christians rather than as merely something enjoyed by them."
3. "The fellowship with both [the Father and the Son] contemplates both as united in the Godhead."

### John Calvin:
1. "That which we have seen...the real certainty of his doctrine."
2. "He also testifies...their salvation; which served not a little to induce them to believe."
3. "He also sets forth the fruit received from the Gospel...that they might be all one in him."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Emphasis by repetition is a thoroughly Johannine trait." 
2. "Purpose clause with ινα and present active subjunctive of εχω (may keep on having)."
3. "This Epistle often uses εχω with a substantive rather than a verb."

### John Gill:
1. "This is repeated, both to confirm and illustrate what had been before said, and to carry on the discourse to what follows." 
2. "The person and offices of Christ being the sum and substance of the Gospel ministration, that declares him to be the true God and eternal life, God over all, blessed for ever."
3. "And this declaration of him is made in the Gospel, for the following ends and purposes,that ye also may have fellowship with us; in hearing, seeing, and handling of Christ in a spiritual sense."

### John Wesley:
1. "That which we have seen and heard - Of him and from him. Declare we to you - For this end."
2. "That ye also may have fellowship with us - May enjoy the same fellowship which we enjoy."
3. "And truly our fellowship - Whereby he is in us and we in him. Is with the Father and with the son - Of the Holy Ghost he speaks afterwards."
