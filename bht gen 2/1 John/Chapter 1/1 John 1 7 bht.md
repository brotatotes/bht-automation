# 1 John 1:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Walking in the light as God is in the light is more than just imitation. It is an essential part of our daily walk with God. Through the blood of Jesus Christ, we are cleansed from all sin. Enjoying the comfort and joy of religion comes from our fellowship with God and adherence to His truth. The blood of Christ washes away all stains of sin and brings us into fellowship with God. ### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "WE WALK"; "God is (essentially in His very nature as 'the light,' 1 John 1:5) in the light." WALKING in the light, the element in which God Himself is, constitutes the test of fellowship with Him.
2. Walking in the light as He is in the light, is no mere imitation of God, but an identity in the essential element of our daily walk with the essential element of God's eternal being.
3. Without having fellowship with God there can be no true and Christian fellowship one with another.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Walking in the truth; that is, embracing the truth in opposition to all error of paganism and infidelity, and having clear, spiritual views of truth, such as the unrenewed never have."
2. "Enjoying the comforts of religion; that is, having the joy which religion is fitted to impart, and which it does impart to its true friends."
3. "The true Christian in his character and feelings resembles God."
4. "There is no stain made by sin so deep that the blood of Christ cannot take it entirely away from the soul."
5. "No system of religion intended for man could be adapted to his condition which did not contain provision for cleansing the soul from sin and making it like God."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "We walk, advancing in the light and by means of the light to more light." 
2. "Fellowship with God exhibits and proves itself by fellowship with Christians."
3. "The whole phrase, His Son Jesus Christ, includes the two elements of the confessions which John makes prominent. 1. Jesus is the Son of God. 2. Jesus is the Christ."

### John Calvin:
1. "Wherever God comes, all things are so imbued with his holiness, that he washes away all filth."
2. "He walks in darkness who is not ruled by the fear of God, and who does not, with a pure conscience, devote himself wholly to God, and seek to promote his glory."
3. "The faithful know of a certainty, that they are accepted by God, because he has been reconciled to them through the sacrifice of the death of Christ. And sacrifice includes cleansing and satisfaction. Hence the power and efficiency of these belong to the blood of Christ alone."

### John Gill:
1. "Are persons enlightened by the Spirit of God, so as to have a true sight and sense of sin, to know Christ, and the way of salvation by him."
2. "This 'as' denotes not equality, but likeness: when this is the case, then it is a clear point, that we have fellowship one with another; not with the saints, with the apostles, and other Christians, but with God."
3. "Christ's blood, being applied by the Spirit of God, has been always cleansing from sin; it had this virtue in it, and was of this use, even before it was actually shed, to the Old Testament saints... and it has the same efficacy now as when first shed, and will have to the end of the world."

### John Wesley:
1. "If we walk in the light...then we may truly say, we have fellowship one with another."
2. "The imitation of God being the only sure proof of our having fellowship with him."
3. "And the blood of Jesus Christ his Son cleanseth us from all sin."
