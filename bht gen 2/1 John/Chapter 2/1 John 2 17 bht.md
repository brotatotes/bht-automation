# 1 John 2:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The allure of the world and its temporary desires are fleeting and will eventually fade away. However, those who prioritize doing God's will and loving Him instead of being overtaken by worldly desires will find eternal fulfillment. By abiding in God's love and remaining committed to Him, they will enjoy everlasting blessings.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "And the world is passing away, and the lust of it... summing up in one the three which are there mentioned. παράγεται as in 1 John 2:8... it is even now in act so to pass."
2. "he that doeth the will of God abideth for ever... eternal fixity and duration belongs only to that order of things... in entire accordance with the will of God."
3. "And among these is he that doeth that will, which is... the true proof and following out of love towards Him. As God Himself is eternal, so is all that is in communion with Him."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The world—with all who are of the world worldly."
2. "The doer of God's will has three abiding goods, 'riches, honor, and life'."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "And the world passeth away - Everything properly constituting this world where religion is excluded."
2. "These things are passing away like the shifting scenes of the stage."
3. "But he that doeth the will of God abideth forever - This cannot mean that he will never die; but it means that he has built his happiness on a basis which is secure, and which can never pass away."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Forever [εις τον αιωνα]. The only form in which aijwn age, life, occurs in the Gospel and Epistles of John, except ejk tou aijwnov since the world began (John 9:32)."
2. "Some old versions add, 'as God abideth forever.'"

### John Calvin:
1. "The true happiness which God offers to his children, is eternal; it is then a shameful thing for us to be entangled with the world, which with all its benefits will soon vanish away."
2. "By saying that they who do the will of God shall abide forever, or perpetually, he means that they who seek God shall be perpetually blessed."
3. "The will of God is first made known to us in the law; but as no one satisfies the law, no happiness can be hoped from it. But Christ comes to meet the despairing with new aid, who not only regenerates us by his Spirit that we may obey God, but makes also that our endeavor, such as it is, should obtain the praise of perfect righteousness."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "There is consolation in this view of the transitoriness of the conflict with the world."
2. "Even the lust which belongs to the world passes also."
3. "The one who keeps on doing the will of God 'abides for ever' amid the flux of transitory things."

### John Gill:
1. "The riches and honours of the world are fading, perishing, and transitory things; everything is upon the flux, nothing is permanent."
2. "The pleasures of sin are but for a season, and a very short one; and are indeed but imaginary, and leave a real bitterness and sorrow behind them."
3. "He that doeth the will of God... abideth for ever; in the love of God, which will never depart from him, nor shall he be separated from that; and in the hands and arms of Christ, out of which none can pluck him."

### John Wesley:
1. "The world passeth away, and the desire thereof — That is, all that can gratify those desires passeth away with it."
2. "But he that doeth the will of God — That loves God, not the world."
3. "Abideth — In the enjoyment of what he loves, for ever."
