# 1 John 2:24 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Let the truth about the Father and the Son, which you have heard from the start, remain firmly planted in you. By doing so, you will also remain in a blessed relationship with the Son and the Father, experiencing a deep and lasting communion with them.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Let that—truth respecting the Father and the Son, regarded as a seed not merely dropped in, but having taken root (1 John 3:9)."
2. "Ye, therefore, acknowledge the Son, and so shall ye have the Father also (1 John 2:23)."
3. "Having taken into us the living seed of the truth concerning the Father and the Son, we become transformed into the likeness of Him whose seed we have taken into us."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Adhere steadfastly to it; let the truth obtain a permanent lodgement in the soul."
2. "The same doctrines which you have always been taught..."
3. "Truly united to the Son and to the Father; or having evidence of the favor and friendship of the Son and the Father."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The force of the emphatic you at the beginning of the sentence is utterly lost in the A. V., which takes the pronoun simply as nominative to ye have heard."
2. "From the beginning. See on 1 1. Notice the change in the order of the repeated sentence, that which ye heard from the beginning : o hjkousate ajp' ajrchv, that which ye heard; the emphasis being on their reception of the message."
3. "O ajp ajrchv hjkousate, that which ye heard from the beginning; emphasizing the time of the reception as coincident."

### John Calvin:
1. "He then exhorts them to perseverance in the faith, so that they might retain fixed in their hearts what they had learnt."
2. "We hence learn what we are to seek in every truth pertaining to religion. He therefore makes the greatest proficiency, who makes such progress as wholly to cleave to God."
3. "Moreover, this is the highest commendation of sound doctrine, that it unites us to God, and that in it is found whatever pertains to the real fruition of God."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "As for you (υμεις). Emphatic proleptic position before the relative ο and subject of ηκουσατε, a familiar idiom in John 8:45; John 10:29, etc."
2. "Let abide in you (εν υμιν μενετω). Present active imperative of μενω, to remain."
3. "Do not be carried away by the new-fangled Gnostic teaching."

### John Gill:
1. "Let that therefore abide in you,.... Meaning the word of God...the apostle exhorts them to perseverance in it..."
2. "the doctrine concerning the Father and the Son, and this their relation to each other, which is the foundation of the doctrine of the Trinity..."
3. "Such are in union with Christ, and at times enjoy sensible communion with him, and shall never be finally and totally removed from it..."

### John Wesley:
1. If that truth concerning the Father and the Son, which ye have heard from the beginning, abide fixed and rooted in you, ye also shall abide in that happy communion with the Son and the Father.
