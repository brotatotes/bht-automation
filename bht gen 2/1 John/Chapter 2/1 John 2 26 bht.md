# 1 John 2:26 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
St. John, in his customary fashion, begins and ends his letter by expressing that he has written to the readers. He resumes his previous thoughts on those who are attempting to lead them astray. St. John acts as a caring father, a faithful shepherd, and a vigilant monitor by warning the little children, who are especially vulnerable to deception.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 26. These things— ( :-). 
	have I written—resumed
	from 1 John 2:21; 1 John 2:14.
	seduce you—that is, are
	trying to seduce or lead you into error.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "These things have I written unto you concerning them that seduce you - Respecting their character, and in order to guard you against their arts."
2. "The word “seduce” means to lead astray; and it here refers to those who would seduce them “from the truth,” or lead them into dangerous error."
3. "The apostle does not mean that they had actually seduced them, for he states in the following verse that they were yet safe; but he refers to the fact that there was danger that they might be led into error."

### John Calvin:
1. "I indeed do my part, but still it is necessary that the Spirit of God should direct you in all things; for in vain shall I, by the sound of my voice, beat your ears, or rather the air, unless he speaks within you."
2. "When we hear that he wrote concerning seducers, we ought always to bear in mind, that it is the duty of a good and diligent pastor not only to gather a flock, but also to drive away wolves; for what will it avail to proclaim the pure gospel, if we connive at the impostures of Satan?"
3. "No one, then, can faithfully teach the Church, except he is diligent in banishing errors whenever he finds them spread by seducers."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Concerning those that are trying to lead you astray."
2. "John is doing his part to rescue the sheep from the wolves, as Paul did."

### John Gill:
1. "The little children, who were most likely to be imposed upon by antichrists and deceivers."
2. "They lay in wait to deceive, and attempted to deceive them, by walking in craftiness, and handling the word of God deceitfully."
3. "He acted the part of a tender Father, a faithful shepherd, and a careful monitor."

### John Wesley:
1. "I have written to you" - St. John, according to his custom, begins and ends with the same form.
2. "Having finished a kind of parenthesis" - St. John continues what he said in the twentieth verse, concerning them that would seduce you.
3. "St. John, according to his custom" - St. John begins and ends with the same form.
