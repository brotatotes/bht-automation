# 1 John 2:28 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Believers are lovingly exhorted to continue to have a close and steadfast relationship with Christ, relying on faith, hope, and love. This means remaining faithful to His word and truth, steadfast in His cause, and unwavering in their commitment to Him. By doing so, they will not be moved or shaken when Christ appears in His glory. Instead, they will be filled with confidence, experiencing the fulfillment of their hopes and desires without shame or disappointment. It is a gentle reminder that those who turn away from Christ will be left ashamed and regretful at His imminent return.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "And now, little children, abide in Him, in order that if He should be manifested, we may have confidence, and may not shrink with shame from Him at His coming." 
2. "The doing of righteousness, the sign of new birth from God: the opposite, the sign of not being of God."
3. "Hoping as we do to be entirely like Christ at His appearing, each one of us, in pursuance of this hope, is even now approximating to this perfect likeness by purifying himself even as He is pure."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "John repeats his monition with a loving appellation, as a father addressing dear children."
2. "When we are manifested, we must not shrink back from Him ashamed."
3. "Contrast 'boldness in the day of judgment,' compare 1 John 3:21, 1 John 5:14."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Abide in him; that, when he shall appear - In the end of the world, to receive his people to himself."
2. "It means that they would not be overwhelmed and confounded at the coming of the Saviour, by its being then found that all their hopes were fallacious."
3. "Many will thus be ashamed in the last day, but it is one of the promises made to those who truly believe on the Saviour, that they shall never be ashamed or confounded."

### John Calvin:
1. "He at the same time shews, that the children of God are for no other end illuminated by the Spirit, but that they may know Christ."
2. "Faith is not a naked and frigid apprehension of Christ, but a lively and real sense of his power, which produces confidence."
3. "A godly confidence delights to look on God. Hence it is, that the godly calmly wait for Christ, nor do they dread his coming."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "But throughout this Epistle the human side is never forgotten, while all is referred finally to the indwelling of the Son."
2. "We are exhorted to 'abide in Him;' and whether we meet Him or are brought with Him, the confidence will be the same."


### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Keep on abiding in him."
2. "A clear reference to the second coming of Christ which may be at any time."
3. "As if shrinking away from Christ in guilty surprise."

### John Gill:
1. "And whom he exhorts to abide in Christ, that is, in the exercise of faith on him, of hope in him, and love to him; and to hold to him the head, and to hold fast his word and Gospel, and abide by his truth and ordinances, and adhere to his cause and interest, and not to be moved away on any consideration." 
2. "That when he shall appear; that is, Christ...his appearance will be a glorious one, and his saints shall appear in glory with him, and shall be like him, and see him as he is."
3. "And not be ashamed before him at his coming; they will not be put to shame by him; nor will they be ashamed of their confidence, faith, hope, and expectation; their hope will not make them ashamed, for they will now enjoy what they hoped for."

### John Wesley:
1. "Abide in him, that we — A modest expression."
2. "May not be ashamed before him at his coming — O how will ye, Jews, Socinians, nominal Christians, be ashamed in that day!"
