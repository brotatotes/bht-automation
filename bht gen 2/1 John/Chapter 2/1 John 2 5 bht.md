# 1 John 2:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Those who diligently keep God's commandments demonstrate their genuine love for Him, which is fully realized in their obedience. Through Christ, we are reconciled to God, and in Him, our understanding of His love is perfected. By faithfully adhering to His word, we can confidently affirm our union with Him. Truly knowing God, abiding in Him, and maintaining a consistent relationship with Him are intertwined concepts that reveal our true connection to Him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "but whosoever keepeth His word...of a truth in him is the love of God perfected." 
2. "true knowledge and love increase together, and the former is the measure of the latter, just as keeping God's commandments is the test and measure of true knowledge of Him."
3. "So that there is no need again to depart from the meaning of τετελείωται, as has been done by Beza...In this we know that we are in Him."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "In Him, verily (not as a matter of vain boasting) is the love of (that is towards) God perfected."
2. "Love here answers to knowledge... In proportion as we love God, in that same proportion we know Him, and vice versa, until our love and knowledge shall attain their full maturity of perfection."
3. "His word—His word is one... and comprises His 'commandments,' which are many."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The term 'word' here will include all that he has made known to us as his will in regard to our conduct."
2. "Love to the Saviour would be defective without that, for it is never complete without obedience."
3. "To be in him, is to be united to him; to be his friends."

### John Calvin:
1. "To love God in sincerity of heart is to keep his commandments."
2. "The perfect love of God is the complete keeping of his law."
3. "No one makes a real progress in faith except he who cleaves from the heart to God."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The ‘keeping’ is more interior than the ‘doing,’ including that sacred reverence for the principle of obedience which is its permanent or abiding safeguard in the soul."
2. "The Divine love in the mission and atoning work of the Son has been exhibited as effecting the forgiveness and sanctification of the soul; but that does not constitute the full knowledge of God in Christ: His love in us attains its perfect operation only when it becomes the full power of a simple and pure obedience to His word; that is its finished work in us."
3. "Being abidingly in Christ absolutely involves a Divine necessity of righteous obedience; and the profession of it binds the professor to do his own part to imitate Him."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "This prize is open to all, not confined to a few initiated Gnostic intellectuals or pneumatics." 
2. "Hath the love of God been perfected... Probably objective genitive, our love for God, which is realized in absolute obedience."
3. "Hereby... by continuous keeping of Christ's commandments, not by loud talk and loose living."

### John Gill:
1. "In him verily is the love of God perfected: not the love wherewith God loves him, but the love wherewith God is loved by his people."
2. "By keeping the word of God, both his truths and his ordinances, it is clearly seen that their love to him is without dissimulation, and is not in tongue only, but in deed and in truth."
3. "The latter being a consequent and an effect of the former, is the evidence of it, of the truth and sincerity of it."

### John Wesley:
1. "Whoso keepeth his word — His commandments.Verily in him the love of God — Reconciled to us through Christ.Is perfected — Is perfectly known."
2. "Hereby — By our keeping his word.We know that we are in him — So is the tree known by its fruits."
3. "To 'know him,' to be 'in him,' to 'abide in him,' are nearly synonymous terms; only with a gradation,-knowledge, communion, constancy."
