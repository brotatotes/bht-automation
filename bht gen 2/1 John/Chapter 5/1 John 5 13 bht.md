# 1 John 5:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The purpose of writing these things is to give believers a deep and unwavering assurance that they have eternal life. By understanding the truth of eternal life in Christ and believing in His name, believers can have the confidence in their salvation. This knowledge is not based on flippant superficiality, but on a genuine and growing faith that comes through the study of Scripture and the work of the Holy Spirit.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "These things wrote I to you that ye may know that ye have eternal life, (to you) that believe in the name of the Son of God."
2. "The two readings come, in the sense, to much the same."
3. "The internal character of the text agrees with the external."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "These things have I written unto you... that ye may know that ye have eternal life."
2. "To 'know that we have eternal life' is the sure way to 'joy in God.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "To believe on his name, is to believe on himself - the word 'name' often being used to denote the person."
2. "That you may see the evidence that eternal life has been provided, and that you may be able, by self-examination, to determine whether you possess it."
3. "It is often one of the most important duties of ministers of the gospel, to present to real Christians such views of the nature, the claims, the evidences, and the hopes of religion, as shall be adapted to secure their perseverance in the faith."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "John speaks as looking back over his Epistle and recalling the aim with which he wrote."
2. "Know with settled and absolute knowledge."
3. "The adjective eternal is added as an after-thought. So Westcott: 'that ye have life - yes, eternal life.'"

### John Calvin:
1. "There ought to be a daily progress in faith."
2. "It therefore becomes us assiduously to attend to the duty of learning, that our faith may increase through the whole course of our life."
3. "The Apostle teaches further in this passage, that Christ is the peculiar object of faith, and that to the faith which we have in his name is annexed the hope of salvation."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "St. John returns now to his one great design, the fulfilling of the Joy of those who believe." 
2. "It was not his purpose to establish their assurance, and on that to superinduce a challenge to faith, or to a higher faith, as the reading of our present translation might suggest."
3. "'That ye may know:' this is one of the watchwords of the Epistle; and it is here finally introduced in such a way as to show that, while it is the gift of God’s Spirit, it is the bounden duty and privilege of every Christian to live in the enjoyment of it."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He wishes them to have eternal life in Christ (John 20:31) and to know that they have it, but not with flippant superficiality (1 John 2:3)."
2. "Unto you that believe on (τοις πιστευουσιν εις). Dative of the articular present active participle of πιστευω and εις as in verse 1 John 5:10."
3. "For this use of ονομα (name) with πιστευω see 1 John 3:23; John 2:23."

### John Gill:
1. "The end of writing these things to them was, that ye may know that ye have eternal life; that there is such a thing as eternal life; that this is in Christ...the knowledge of which is had by faith, under the testimony of the Spirit of God."
2. "The above things were written to them concerning the Son of God, that they might be encouraged to continue believing in him...that their faith in him might be increased; for faith is imperfect and is capable of increasing, and growing exceedingly."
3. "Nothing more tends unto, or is a more proper means of it, than the sacred writings...especially that part of them which respects the person, office, and grace of Christ." (104 words)

### John Wesley:
1. "These things have I written... that ye may know - With a fuller and stronger assurance, that ye have eternal life."
