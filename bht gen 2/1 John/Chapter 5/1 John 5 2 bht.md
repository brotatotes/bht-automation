# 1 John 5:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Our love to God is demonstrated by our love for His children, the believers. This reciprocal relationship reveals the authenticity of our love. When we love God and keep His commandments, we can be confident that our love extends to our brothers and sisters in Christ. Our love for the brethren flows from our love for God, as we recognize that they are His children, bearing His image and experiencing His grace.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Our love to God, ascertained by our keeping His commandments, is itself the measure of our love to the children of God."
2. "In this we know that we love the children of God when we love God and do His commandments."
3. "All the devices which have been used to extract from this verse a sense different from that which it really conveys are wholly unneeded, nay, out of place."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Our love to the brethren is the sign and test of our love to God."
2. "Our love to God (tested by our 'keeping his commandments') is, conversely, the ground and only true basis of love to our brother."
3. "John shows what true love is, namely, that which is referred to God as its first object."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "As it is universally true that if we love Him who has begotten us, we shall also love His children, or our Christian brethren, so it is true also that if we love His children it will follow that we love Him."
2. "We may have undoubted evidence that we love God, and from that, as the basis of an argument, we may infer that we have true love to His children."
3. "When we are conscious that an attachment does exist toward Christians, we may ascertain that it is genuine, or that it does not proceed from any improper motive, by the fact that we love God."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "By this [εν τουτω] . Not by this or from this, as an inference, but in the very exercise of the sentiment toward God, we perceive."
2. "Our perception of the existence of love to our brethren is developed on every occasion when we exercise love and obedience toward God."
3. "Keep [τηρωμεν] . Read poiwmen do. So Rev. The exact phrase poiein tav ejntolav to do the commandments, occurs only here."

### John Calvin:
1. "He now teaches us that men are rightly and duly loved, when God holds the primacy."
2. "For his purpose is to show that mutual love ought to be in such a way cultivated that God may be honored."
3. "The first thing is, that our hearts should be devoted to God in willing reverence, and then, that our life should be formed according to the rule of the law."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "We know that we love God by the token that we love the brethren."
2. "We also know that we love God's children by the very fact of our loving Him."
3. "The consciousness of loving God is guarantee that we have in us all that brotherly love means; especially as that love feels in itself the energy of all obedience."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The love of God and the love of the brethren do in fact include each other."
2. "Each is a test of the other."
3. "Whenever we keep on loving God ... and whenever we keep on doing his commandments."

### John Gill:
1. "By this we know that we are the children God,...when we love God, and keep his commandments."
2. "True love to the brethren springs from love to God..."
3. "They love them because they belong to God, and are the objects of his love; because his grace is wrought in them, and his image stamped upon them."

### John Wesley:
1. Hereby we know — This is a plain proof.That we love the children of God — As his children.
