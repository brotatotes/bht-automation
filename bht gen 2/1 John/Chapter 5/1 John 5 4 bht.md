# 1 John 5:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Those who are born of God conquer everything that the world presents to them, whether it be the temptations and seductions of the enemy, false teachings, or the wickedness of people. This victory is made possible through their unwavering faith in Christ. This faith allows them to overcome any obstacle and accomplish great things, for nothing is impossible for the one who believes. The power of God within them enables them to triumph over the world in its entirety, proving that their faith is a force to be reckoned with.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "the commandments of God are not grievous: for, although in keeping them there is ever a conflict, yet that conflict issues in universal victory"
2. "the victory which ( hath ) conquered the world is this, our faith"
3. "wherever there is faith there is victory"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The reason why 'His commandments are not grievous.' Though there is a conflict in keeping them, the sue for the whole body of the regenerate is victory over every opposing influence; meanwhile there is a present joy to each believer in keeping them which makes them 'not grievous.'"
2. "The Father has given, as it were, the whole mass to the Son, that all whom He gave may be one whole: that universal whole the Son singly evolves, in the execution of the divine plan."
3. "This is the victory that overcometh—Greek aorist, '. . . that hath (already) overcome the world': the victory (where faith is) hereby is implied as having been already obtained."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The world, in its maxims, and precepts, and customs, does not rule him, but he is a freeman."
2. "There is a conflict between religion and the world, and in the heart of every true Christian, religion secures the victory, or triumphs."
3. "It is one of the settled maxims of religion that every man who is a true Christian gains a victory over the world; and consequently a maxim as settled, that where the spirit of the world reigns supremely in the heart, there is no true religion."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The victory over the world was, potentially, won when we believed in Jesus as the Christ, the Son of God."
2. "We overcome the world by being brought into union with Christ."
3. "Our faith is embraced in the confession that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God."

### John Calvin:
1. "This passage is remarkable, for though Satan continually repeats his dreadful and horrible onsets, yet the Spirit of God, declaring that we are beyond the reach of danger, removes fear, and animates us to fight with courage."
2. "The term world has here a wide meaning, for it includes whatever is adverse to the Spirit of God: thus, the corruption of our nature is a part of the world; all lusts, all the crafts of Satan, in short, whatever leads us away from God."
3. "But as this promise secures to us perpetually the invincible power of God, so, on the other hand, it annihilates all the strength of men."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "For the reason why God's commandments are not heavy is the power that comes with the new birth from God."
2. "The victory over the world is a continuous victory because it is a continuous struggle."
3. "Jesus won the victory over the world and God in us gives us the victory."

### John Gill:
1. "this overcometh the world; the god of the world, Satan; the lusts which are in the world; false prophets gone forth into the world; and the wicked men of the world."
2. "but such are more than conquerors over all these, through Christ that has loved them."
3. "great things, heroic actions, and wonderful victories, are ascribed to faith; which must not be understood of the grace itself, as separately considered, but of Christ the object of it, as supported, strengthened, assisted, and animated by him."

### John Wesley:
1. "For whatsoever - This expression implies the most unlimited universality."
2. "Is born of God overcometh the world - Conquers whatever it can lay in the way, either to allure or fright the children of God from keeping his commandments."
3. "And this is the victory - The grand means of overcoming. Even our faith - Seeing all things are possible to him that believeth."
