The call to holiness in Leviticus 11:44 serves as a strong foundation for the command to be holy. Being addressed to the Israelites, who were scattered among the Gentiles, it held great weight and value for them. This declaration from God's own words reinforces the argument for a holy life, as it aligns with His divine nature and will.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Ye shall be holy because I am holy."
2. "The duty of assimilation to Him in His Holiness."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Scripture is the true source of all authority in questions of doctrine and practice."
2. "As I am the fountain of holiness, being holy in My essence, be ye therefore zealous to be partakers of holiness, that ye may be as I also am."
3. "God is essentially holy: the creature is holy in so far as it is sanctified by God."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The foundation of the command is, that they professed to be his people, and that as his people they ought to be like their God."
2. "It is a great truth, that people everywhere will imitate the God whom they worship. They will form their character in accordance with his."
3. "And hence, also, we may see that the tendency of true religion is to make people pure. As the worship of the impure gods of the pagan moulds the character of the worshippers into their image, so the worship of Yahweh moulds the character of His professed friends into His image, and they become like him."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The future, 'ye shall be,' is better supported than the imperative, 'be ye.' The sense, however, remains substantially the same."
2. "Peter appends a reason for his counsel, and this he expresses in words which he takes from God's charge to Israel."
3. "They are used by Peter because they mean that the relation which results from God's call, being a covenant relation, conveys obligations on two sides."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Because it is written." 
2. "The quotation is from Leviticus 11:44; Leviticus 19:2; Leviticus 20:7." 
3. "Reenforced by Jesus in Matthew 5:48."

### John Gill:
1. "An argument the apostle knew must have weight with these persons, who were chiefly Jews, scattered abroad among the Gentiles, and had a value for the Scriptures of truth."
2. "The argument for holiness of life, from the nature and perfections of God, is strong, it must receive additional strength from this being the declared will of God."
3. "Though holiness, equal to God, is never to be attained to by a creature, yet so far as it is capable of it, it is desirable, because agreeable both to the nature and will of God."
