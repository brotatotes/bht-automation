# 1 Peter 3:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Let him turn away from evil and do good, avoiding all kinds of evil and having no fellowship with it. Seek peace, pursue it, and strive to live peaceably with all men, even when peace seems to flee from you. Do acts of kindness and beneficence to all, including enemies, and especially to those who are of the household of faith. In this troubled world, where peace can be hard to attain, cultivate a peaceful spirit and actively pursue peace with all people, even in the face of difficulties and hindrances.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Moreover, let him turn away from evil, and do good."
2. "Let him seek peace, and pursue it."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Moreover (besides his words, in acts), let him."
2. "Eschew - 'turn from.'"
3. "Ensue - pursue as a thing hard to attain, and that flees from one in this troublesome world."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Let him avoid all evil."
2. "Let him seek peace, and ensue it."
3. "The meaning is, that a peaceful spirit will contribute to length of days."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The Greek word here occurs only twice elsewhere (Romans 3:12; Romans 16:17), where Rev. renders turn aside and turn away."
2. "The picture in the word is of one bending aside from his course at the approach of evil."

### John Calvin:
1. "It is not enough to embrace peace when offered to us, but it ought to be followed when it seems to flee from us."
2. "When we seek peace as much as we can, others may not grant it to us."
3. "On account of these difficulties and hindrances, he bids us to seek and pursue peace."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The 'eschew' of the A. V. (comp. Shakespeare’s ‘What cannot be eschewed, must be embraced,’ Mer. Wives, v. 5, 251), connected with the old French eschever, German scheuen, English shy, means to shun, and sufficiently expresses the idea, which is that of turning away from something which comes in one’s way."
2. "This blamelessness and kindliness of life, at once in word and in deed, should take the still more definite form of a determination to secure peace."
3. "They [those who suffer from slander or other kinds of wrong] are to meet evil by doing positive good, and cultivating all that makes for peace. This last is represented as something worth straining every effort for. It is to be sought, nay, it is to be pursued, with the expenditure of strenuous and unflagging endeavor which the hunter devotes to the chase."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Let him turn away" (εκκλινατω). First aorist active imperative third person singular of εκκλινω.
2. "So as to ποιησατω (let him do) for ποιησον (do thou), ζητησατω (let him seek) for ζητησον (do thou seek), διωξατω (let him pursue) for διωξον (do thou pursue), all first aorist active imperatives."
3. "See Hebrews 12:14 for 'pursuing peace.' If men only did!"

### John Gill:
1. "Avoid all kinds of evil, hate it, abstain from the appearance of it, and have no fellowship with it..."
2. "Do good to all men, acts of kindness and beneficence, even to enemies, and especially to them that are of the household of faith."
3. "Let him seek after [peace], in the world, and with all men, as much as possible, yea, with his very enemies; and live a peaceable and quiet life..."

### John Wesley:
1. Let him seek — To live peaceably with all men.And pursue it — Even when it seems to flee from him.
