# 1 Peter 3:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
You are called to inherit a blessing, so the evil and reproach of others cannot harm you. By choosing not to retaliate but to bless them instead, you are imitating God, who blesses you.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "not giving back [to others] evil for evil, or reproach for reproach"
2. "on the contrary, blessing...because to this...ye were called"
3. "our portion is, blessing"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "No railing can injure you. Imitate God who 'blesses' you."
2. "It is not in order to inherit a blessing that we must bless, but because our portion is blessing."
3. "The first fruits of His blessing for eternity are enjoyed by the righteous even now."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Knowing that you were called to be Christians in order that you should obtain a blessing infinite and eternal in the heavens. Expecting such a blessing yourselves, you should be ready to scatter blessings on all others."
2. "The hope of eternal life should make your minds calm; and the prospect that you are to be so exalted in heaven should fill your hearts with benignity and love."
3. "There is nothing which is better suited to cause our hearts to overflow with benignity, to make us ready to forgive all others when they injure us, than the hope of salvation."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Rendering evil, etc. See Romans 12:17. Blessing [ευλογουντες] . Not a noun governed by rendering, but a participle. Be not rendering evil, but be blessing.

### John Calvin:
1. "In order to preserve love, we must bear with many things."
2. "Peter requires...that we should pray for those who reproach us...evils are to be overcome by acts of kindness."
3. "He shows how much would be the gain of patience; for if we submissively bear injuries, the Lord will bestow on us his blessing."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "An undue esteem of ourselves is inconsistent either with the oneness of mind and feeling which makes genuine brotherliness, or with the Christian law of overcoming evil with good."
2. "The return which we are to render for injury done us, whether in the form of the evil deed or the reviling word, is to desire and pray for the good of the injurers."
3. "The duty which was formerly enjoined on slaves by an appeal to Christ’s example, is now repeated as a duty applicable to all Christians, and as involved in the Divine call which first makes us Christians."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Not rendering evil for evil... an approximation to Christ's repeal of the λεξ ταλιονις (Matthew 5:38) which Plato first opposed among the Greeks." 
2. "But contrariwise blessing... 'on the contrary.'"
3. "That ye should inherit a blessing... Christians are the new Israel (both Gentiles and Jews) and are the spiritual descendants of Isaac (Galatians 4:22)."

### John Gill:
1. "Not rendering evil for evil... is contrary to what is before advised to, and which is taking God's prerogative and work out of his hands."
2. "Instead of returning injuries, blows, or words, [we are called] to turn to him that smites on the right cheek, to bless them that curse, and to do good to them that hate."
3. "For if we are to pray for a blessing on our enemies, then much more should we pray for our fellow Christians who may do us an injury or speak evil of us."

### John Wesley:
1. Ye are called to inherit a blessing — Therefore their railing cannot hurt you; and, by blessing them, you imitate God, who blesses you.
