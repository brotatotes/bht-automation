# 1 Thessalonians 1:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The church in Thessalonica was not only a follower of Christ, but also a pattern for other believers to look to as examples of faith, holiness, courage, meekness, and patience. They were even ensamples for others to imitate, showing that sometimes those who came later can be a model for those who came before. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "τύπον, of the whole church as one: see Bernhardy, p. 60."
2. "οἱ πιστεύοντες, like ὁ πειράζων, designates the kind."
3. "But it was not so: for the only church in Europe which was in Christ before the Thessalonian, was the Philippian (Act 16:12 to Acts 17:1, see ch. 1Th 2:2)."
4. "ἔθνη μέγιστα κ . ἐπὶ σοφίᾳ θαυμαζόμενα, and so their praise being the greater: these are mentioned simply because the Apostle had been, since their conversion, in Macedonia, and had left Silvanus and Timotheus there, and was now in Achaia."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The Macedonian Church of Philippi was the only one in Europe converted before the Thessalonians."
2. "Therefore he means their past conduct is an ensample to all believers now."
3. "He specifies those 'in Macedonia' because he had been there since the conversion of the Thessalonians, and had left Silvanus and Timotheus there; and those in 'Achaia,' because he was now at Corinth in Achaia."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Examples in reference to the firmness with which you embraced the gospel, the fidelity with which you adhered to it in trials, and the zeal which you showed in spreading it abroad."
2. "They became, as it were, a model or pattern after which the piety of others should be moulded, or showed what the piety of others ought to be."
3. "A revival of religion in such a place will extend its influence afar to other places, and appropriate zeal among the friends of the Redeemer there may have an important effect on sea-ports, and towns, and lands far remote."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "An ensample [τυπον] . See on 1 Peter 5:3."

### John Calvin:
1. "It is a great thing to get so decidedly the start of those who had entered upon the course before us, as to furnish assistance to them for prosecuting their course."
2. "He says, then, that the courage of the Thessalonians had been so illustrious, that other believers had borrowed from them a rule of constancy."
3. "The plural number expresses, in my opinion, something more than if he had said that that Church as a body had been set forward for imitation, for the meaning is, that there were as many patterns as there were individuals."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The true followers became themselves in turn patterns for others."
2. "All the believers, whether their conversion preceded or followed that of the Thessalonians."
3. "These two provinces at this time included the whole of Greece, the former being the northern, and the latter the southern portion of it."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The singular looks at the church as a whole, the plural as individuals like υμας." 
2. "Τυπος is an old word from τυπτω, to strike, and so the mark of a blow, print as in John 1 Thessalonians 20:25." 
3. "It was a great compliment for the church in Thessalonica to be already a model for believers in Macedonia and Achaia."

### John Gill:
1. "They were not only followers of Christ and his apostles, whom they took for examples of faith, holiness, courage, meekness, and patience; but they were patterns of good works; and of suffering afflictions to other believers..." 
2. "They were even ensamples to them to copy after; so the first are sometimes last, and the last first."
3. "These saints at Thessalonica set them an example in many things, and particularly in acts of beneficence and liberality..."
