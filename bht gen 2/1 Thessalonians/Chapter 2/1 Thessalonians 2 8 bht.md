# 1 Thessalonians 2:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The deep and selfless love that Paul and his companions had for the Thessalonians is shown in their willingness to not only share the gospel message but also to sacrifice their own lives for their sake.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The comparison is exceedingly tender and beautiful: as the nursing-mother, cherishing her children, joys to give not only her milk, but her life, for them, so we, bringing up you as spiritual children, delighted in giving, not only the milk of the word, but even (and here it was matter of fact) our own lives, for your nourishment in Christ."
2. "ἐγενήθητε must not be pressed to a passive meaning, as in my earlier editions: see on ch. 1Th 1:5)  very dear to us."
3. "μεταδοῦναι will not strictly apply to τὰς ἑαυ . ψυχ ., but we must borrow from the compound verb the idea of giving, or offering."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "So...we were willing." 
2. "As a nursing mother...so we not only imparted gladly...but risked our own lives." 
3. "Ye were become, dear."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The sense here is, that Paul was so strongly attached to them that he would have been willing to lay down his life for them."
2. "To be willing to communicate the knowledge of the gospel was in itself a strong proof of love, even if it were attended with no self-denial or hazard in doing it."
3. "There is no method in which we can show so strong an interest in our fellow-men, and so much true benevolence for them, as to go to them and tell them of the way by which they may be rescued from everlasting ruin."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "She is not satisfied with nursing the child, but interests herself affectionately in all that concerns it."
2. "We continued to entertain and manifest our affectionate solicitude."
3. "Better lives."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "So genuine and cordial was the love of Paul and his companions for the Thessalonians, that they did not merely deliver their message as officials seeking to discharge a responsibility laid upon them, but they were willing to sacrifice their lives for them, if need were."
2. "This willingness manifested itself in the self-denying and excessive toil of which Paul proceeds to speak."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The verb ομειρομα [...] occurs nowhere else except [...] and the Lycaonian sepulchral inscription [...] about the sorrowing parents [...] greatly desiring their son." 
2. "Moulton suggests that it comes from a root σμερ, remember, and that ο- is a derelict preposition [...] like ο-δυρομαι, ο-κελλω, ω-κεανος."
3. "A beautiful picture of the growth of Paul's affection for them as should be true with every pastor."

### John Gill:
1. "Were as fond of them as a nursing mother is of her children, who, when absent from them but ever so little a while, longs to see them."
2. "The highest expression and strongest proof of love that can be given; and respect is still had to the similitude of the nursing mother, who may be said to impart her blood, for such it her milk diversified, for the nourishment of her children."
3. "Their lives were not dear unto them, so that these ends might he answered: because ye were dear unto us."

### John Wesley:
1. To impart our own souls — To lay down our lives for your sake.
