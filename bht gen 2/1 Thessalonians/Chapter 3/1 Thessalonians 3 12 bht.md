# 1 Thessalonians 3:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The prayer is that the Lord would cause the believers to increase and grow in various graces, like faith, hope, holiness, and more. Additionally, the desire is for the believers to abound in love towards one another. While they had already shown love, there was room for growth in this area. It is also worth noting that the believers were to extend their love even to those outside the church, treating them with kindness and praying for their well-being.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "But what concerns 'YOU,' whether we come or not, 'may the Lord make you to increase and abound in love.'"
2. "'Increase' has a more positive force that 'abound' a more comparative force, 'make you full and even abound.'"
3. "'Love' is the fruit of the Spirit, and His office is 'to stablish in holiness'."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "And the Lord make you to increase and abound in love."
2. "The word 'Lord' here probably refers to the Lord Jesus, as this is the name by which he is commonly designated in the New Testament."
3. "If this be so, then this is a petition to the Lord Jesus as the fountain of all grace and goodness."

### John Calvin:
1. "And from this again we learn in what the perfection of the Christian life consists — in love and pure holiness of heart, flowing from faith."
2. "He recommends love mutually cherished towards each other, and afterwards towards all...our love ought to go forth to the whole human race."
3. "He prays that the love of the Thessalonians may be perfected by God, intimating that its increase, no less than its commencement, was from God alone."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Whatever becomes of us and of our prayer, whether we are allowed to come to you or not, may you be tended by the Lord." 
2. "As the beginnings, so the increase of love is from the Lord." 
3. "Love is the soil out of which all Christian grace can grow, it contains in itself the germ of all other grace, it is greater than even faith or hope."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Paul prays to Christ." 
2. "Make you to increase... to superabound."
3. "And abound... to be over."

### John Gill:
1. "The request is, that he would cause these saints to increase in number...and in his graces, as in faith, in hope, in holiness, in humility, in knowledge, in spiritual joy and strength..." 
2. "...and abound in love one towards another; for though they were taught of God to love one another, and did do so, and the apostle had had good tidings of their love; yet it was not perfect, there was room for a further exercise of it..."
3. "...the men of the world, who were without, were not members of the church, nor professors of the Christian religion, but enemies to that, and to Christ, and to them; and yet they were to love them as men, and pray for them, and do them all the good that lay in their power..."
