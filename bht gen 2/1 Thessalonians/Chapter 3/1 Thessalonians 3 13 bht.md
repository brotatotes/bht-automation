# 1 Thessalonians 3:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Christ will come with His holy angels and His redeemed people, both angels and men, to establish His saints in love and holiness. As believers, we are called to be blameless before God and men, living in a way that reflects the principles of holiness. Though we may still struggle with sin in this life, through God's grace, our hearts can be established and our actions can bring glory to Him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "in order to confirm (i.e. εἰς τὸ τὸν κύριον στηρίξαι ‘in order that He may confirm’) your hearts unblameable in holiness"
2. "This ensures the genuineness of this absence of blame in holiness: that it should be not only before men, but also before God"
3. "Certainly He will be accompanied with the angels: but also with the spirits of the just"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "May your holiness be such as will stand His searching scrutiny."
2. "We must have 'holiness' if we are to be numbered with His holy ones or 'saints.'"
3. "This verse shows that 'love' is the spring of true 'holiness'."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "May the Lord cause you to increase in love...so that you may be established, and be without blame in the day of judgment."
2. "The meaning is, so that there could be no 'charge' or 'accusation' against them."
3. "We should be fitted to be welcomed to the 'goodly fellowship' of the angels, and to be regarded as worthy to be numbered with the redeemed who 'have washed their robes and have made them pure in the blood of the Lamb.'"

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Saints is often explained as angels; but the meaning is the holy and glorified people of God."
2. "It is doubtful if oiJ agioi is used of angels in LXX Zechariah 14:5, which is confidently cited as an instance, is quoted at the conclusion of the Didache (xvi. 7), clearly with the sense of glorified believers."
3. "This does not exclude the attendance of angels on the Lord's coming, but when Paul speaks of such attendance, he says, with the angels of his power."

### John Calvin:
1. "He means that a man is acceptable to God only when he brings holiness of heart; that is, not merely external, but also internal." 
2. "Paul does not exclude remission of sins, through which it comes that our holiness, which is otherwise mixed up with many pollutions, bears God’s eye."
3. "Paul employed the term saints for the purpose of admonishing us that we are called by Christ for this end—that we may be gathered with all his saints."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "This Paul looks forward to as the time when those who are holy shall be acknowledged and manifested as such."
2. "Christ is to appear with all His holy angels, and at His appearing His saints are at once to join Him."
3. "Probably both angels and men are included in the present expression."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "To the end he may stablish." 
2. "Another example of εις and the articular infinitive of purpose."
3. "Same idiom in 1 Thessalonians 3:2."
4. "From στηριζω, from στηριγξ, a support."
5. "Unblameable."
6. "Old compound adjective (α privative and verbal of μεμφομα, to blame)."
7. "Rare in N.T."
8. "Predicate position here."
9. "Second coming of Christ again."

### John Gill:
1. "The saints need to be established in the love of God, against the fears of men, the frowns of the world, the temptations of Satan, and in, and with the doctrines of grace." 
2. "No man is perfectly holy in this life; no man is without sin in himself, or lives without the commission of it; holiness in the best is imperfect."
3. "But through the grace of God their hearts may be so established with principles of holiness, and they may be so assisted in the acts of it daily, as to give no just cause of blame to men, and so to behave as to approve themselves 'before God', who sees the heart, and knows from what principles all actions flow."

### John Wesley:
1. With all his, Christ's, saints - Both angels and men.
