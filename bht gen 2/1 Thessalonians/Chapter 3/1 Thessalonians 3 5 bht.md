# 1 Thessalonians 3:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The hardships the Thessalonians faced had prompted Paul's concern. He wanted to know if the enemy had seized the opportunity to tempt them into turning away. Paul worried that their labor, in sharing the Gospel, could be in vain, if Satan succeeded in swaying them from their hope and faith. Their well-being was of utmost importance to him, praying that their efforts would not be wasted.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "because tribulation had verily begun among you (καθὼς καὶ ἐγένετο)."
2. "lest perchance the tempter (ref.) have tempted (not, as Whitby, al., ‘seduced’) you (indicative betokening the fact absolute), and our labour might be (subjunctive, betokening the fact conditional) to no purpose (reff.)."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "To know . . . whether haply the tempter have tempted you (the indicative implying that he supposed such was the case), and lest (in that case) our labor may prove to be in vain" (compare Galatians 4:11).
2. "Our labor in preaching would in that case be vain, so far as ye are concerned, but not as concerns us in so far as we have sincerely labored" (Isaiah 49:4; 1 Corinthians 3:8).
3. "Because I know of your 'tribulation' having actually begun... when I [could] no longer contain myself [endure the suspense]."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "For this cause - Since I knew that you were so liable to be persecuted, and since I feared that some might be turned from the truth by this opposition."
2. "The tempter - Satan; for though the Jews were the immediate actors in those transactions, yet the apostle regarded them as being under the direction of Satan, and as accomplishing his purposes."
3. "In all times of affliction, as well as in prosperity, we may be sure that 'the Tempter' is not far off, and should be on our guard against his wiles."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The tempter [ο πειραζων] . Only here and Matthew 4:3. LXX See on Matthew 6:13."
2. "In vain [εις κενον] . The phrase only in Paul. See 2 Corinthians 6:1; Galatians 2:2; Philippians 2:16."
3. "The force of the preposition is fairly represented by to in the phrase to no purpose."

### John Calvin:
1. "Temptations are always to be dreaded, because it is the proper office of Satan to tempt."
2. "As, however, he never ceases to place ambushes for us on all sides, and to lay snares for us all around, so we must be on our watch, eagerly taking heed."
3. "He says openly what in the outset he had avoided saying, as being too harsh — that he had felt concerned lest his labors should be vain, if, peradventure, Satan should prevail."

### Philip Schaff:
1. 1 Thessalonians 3:5.  For this cause,  i.e.  on account of these afflictions which had fallen upon you.
	

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Paul's common idiom (verse 1 Thessalonians 3:2), εις το and the infinitive of purpose (second aorist ingressive active of γινωσκω, come to know)."
2. "It is a fear that the thing may turn out to be so about the past."
3. "Here the usual construction appears (aorist subjunctive with μη πως) about the future."

### John Gill:
1. "Whether it was weak or strong, what was wanting in it; and whether it grew and increased." 
2. "Lest by some means the tempter... have tempted you with success, and got an advantage over them, improving these afflictions to such a purpose as to move them from the hope of the Gospel, and relinquish the profession of it." 
3. "And our labour be in vain... with respect to the Thessalonians, to whom, should Satan gain his point, it would be of no use and service, for which the concern was."
