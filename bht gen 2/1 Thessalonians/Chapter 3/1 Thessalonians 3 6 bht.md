Upon Timothy's arrival, the apostle Paul swiftly penned this letter, eager to share the fresh news of his joy and deep affection for the recipients.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "But Timotheus having just now come &c."
2. "First their Christian state comforted him, then, their constant remembrance of himself."
3. "So that direction, not intensity is the force of the preposition."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Paul is writing from Corinth."
2. "Faith was the solid foundation: charity the cement which held together the superstructure of their practice on that foundation."
3. "The desires of loving friends for one another's presence are reciprocal."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "And brought us good tidings - A cheerful or favorable account."
2. "Of your faith - Of your faithfulness or fidelity. Amidst all their trials they evinced fidelity to the Christian cause."
3. "Desiring greatly to see us, as we also to see you - There was no disposition to blame him for having left them, or because he did not return to them. They would have welcomed him again as their teacher and friend."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Now [αρτι] . See on John 13:33. Const. with we were comforted (ver. 7), not with came."
2. "Good remembrance [μνειαν αγαθην] . Better kindly remembrance."
3. "Comp. Romans 5:7 (see note); Romans 7:12; Titus 2:6; 1 Peter 2:18. See on ch. 1 Thessalonians 1:3."

### John Calvin:
1. "He shews here, by another argument, by what an extraordinary affection he was actuated towards them, inasmuch as he was transported almost out of his senses by the joyful intelligence of their being in a prosperous condition."
2. "And, assuredly, this must have been a most powerful excitement, when they learned that the holy Apostle felt so great consolation and joy from the advancement of their piety."
3. "This form of expression should be the more carefully observed by us in proportion to the frequency with which it is made use of by Paul, for in these two words he comprehends briefly the entire sum of true piety."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "But Timothy having just now come." 
2. "Good remembrance, a remembrance which was hearty and spoke well for their spiritual condition."
3. "For had they not continued to relish the teaching inaugurated by Paul, they would not have thought frequently about him nor have longed to see mm again."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Even now" (αρτ). Just now, Timothy having come (ελθοντος Τιμοθεου, genitive absolute).
2. "Good remembrance" (μνειαν). Same word used by Paul 1 Thessalonians 1:2.
3. "Longing to see us" (επιποθουντες ημας ιδειν). Old and strong verb, επι-, directive, to long after.

### John Gill:
1. "Brought us good tidings of your faith and charity; of their faith, the grace of faith, that it was of the right kind...an unfeigned one, strong and lively, operative and growing."
2. "Timothy also brought an account of their 'charity', or love, which faith works by; these two graces are always found together...and this was not the whole of the report."
3. "And by this grace is meant love to God, to Christ, to his truths, ordinances, ways, and worship, and to one another, and even to all men; and which was without dissimulation, in sincerity, in deed, and in truth, and was constant and fervent."

### John Wesley:
1. "Immediately after his return, St. Paul wrote; while his joy was fresh, and his tenderness at the height."
