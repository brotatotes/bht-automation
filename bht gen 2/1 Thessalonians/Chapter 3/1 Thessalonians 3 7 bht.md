# 1 Thessalonians 3:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The report of the Thessalonians' faith and steadfastness during times of affliction and distress was a great source of comfort for Paul. Their unwavering trust in God and their commitment to living in the truth brought him relief and solace in his own trials. Hearing that their faith continued to grow exceedingly uplifted his spirits and served as a reminder of the power of the gospel to sustain and encourage believers in the midst of challenges.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "τοῦτο combining the whole of the good news in one."
2. "You were the object of our consolation: the faith which you shewed was the means whereby that object was applied to our minds."
3. "in (reff., i.e. 'in the midst of,' 'in spite of') all our necessity and tribulation: what necessity and tribulation does not appear; but clearly some external trouble, not, as De W., care and anxiety for you, for this would be removed by the message of Timotheus."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "over you—in respect to you."
2. "in—in the midst of"
3. "notwithstanding 'all our distress (Greek, 'necessity') and affliction,' namely, external trials at Corinth, whence Paul writes"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Their steadfastness was a great source of comfort to him in his trials."
2. "The holy lives and the fidelity of a people did much, as will always be the case, to lighten the burdens and cheer the heart of a minister of the gospel."
3. "In the inevitable trials of the ministerial office there is no source of comfort more rich and pure than this."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Affliction [αναγκη] . Rev. distress. The derivation from agxein to press tightly, to choke (Lightfoot, Ellicott) is doubtful."
2. "In the sense of urgency, distress, seldom in Class."
3. "See 1 Corinthians 7:26; 2 Corinthians 6:4; 2 Corinthians 12:10; Luke 21:23."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The tidings which Paul received of the stedfastness of his Thessalonians under trial, enabled him to endure his own troubles with greater equanimity."
2. "Their continued faith showed him that his former labours and trials had not been in vain, their affection and sympathy cheered him."
3. "Their uncomplaining endurance was a fresh stimulus to his own patience."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Over you (εφ' υμιν). Επ with the locative, the basis on which the 'comfort' rests."
2. "Distress (αναγκη). Physical necessity, common sense in late Greek, choking (αγχω, ανγορ), and crushing trouble (θλιψισ, θλιβω)."

### John Gill:
1. "This gave abundant consolation in all our affliction and distress."
2. "The news of the good estate and condition the Thessalonians were in, was a great relief and comfort to him, particularly their faith."
3. "By the report of it, that it grew exceedingly, and that they walked in the truth."
