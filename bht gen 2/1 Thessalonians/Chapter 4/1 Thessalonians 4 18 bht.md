# 1 Thessalonians 4:18 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In these words, we find comfort that the saints, even in death, are not gone but merely asleep in Jesus, with their souls resting in His care. The assurance that the righteous will rise first in the resurrection morning, joining with the living saints, brings hope and joy. Ultimately, all the believers will be forever with Christ, providing a source of true and lasting comfort in the midst of life's trials.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "These words, which I have by inspiration delivered to you."
2. "It will be manifest to the plain, as well as to the scholar-like reader, that attempts like that of Prof. Jowett, to interpret such a passage as this by the rules of mere figurative language, are entirely beside the purpose."
3. "Either these details must be received by us as a matter of practical expectation, or we must set aside the Apostle as one divinely empowered to teach the Church."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 18. comfort one another—in
	your mourning for the dead ( :-).

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The death of the Christian is a calm and gentle slumber... Why, then, should the Christian be afraid to die?"
2. "There are magnificent scenes before us... Great events are brought together here, any one of which is more grand than all the pomp of courts, and all the sublimity of battle, and all the grandeur of a triumphal civic procession."
3. "The saints will be forever with the Lord... To be with Christ will be, in itself, the perfection of bliss; for it will be a security that they will sin no more, that they will suffer no more, and that they will be shielded from danger and death."

### John Calvin:
1. "In the faith of the resurrection we have good ground of consolation, provided we are members of Christ."
2. "The Apostle would not have each one to seek for himself assuagement of grief, but also to administer it to others."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "There being no ground for your supposing that your dead friends will suffer any disadvantage from dying before Christ comes."
2. "Paul scarcely expects that mourners will themselves remember in their grief that which should alleviate it; but he calls upon their fellow-Christians to assume the office of comforter."
3. "And that no one may excuse himself on the score of having no consolation to offer, he gives them wherewithal they may mitigate the bitterness of bereavement."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  With these words  (εν τοις λογοις τουτοις). In these words. They were a comfort to the Thessalonians as they still comfort the people of God. 

### John Gill:
1. "The saints, when they die, do not cease to be, but are asleep, and asleep in Jesus; that their souls are with him, and their bodies sleep in his arms, and are his care."
2. "The righteous will rise first in the morning of the resurrection, and before the living saints are changed, and are with Christ; that they will both be taken up together to meet him."
3. "They shall all be with him, and that for ever, and never part more; than which nothing can yield more true and solid comfort, under all the trials and troubles of this life."
