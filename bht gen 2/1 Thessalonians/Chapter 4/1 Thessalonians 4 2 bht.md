# 1 Thessalonians 4:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
You are familiar with the commandments I previously gave you, which include instructions about faith, love, worship, discipline, and behavior. These commandments were not of human origin, but were given to you by the authority of the Lord Jesus. It is essential to recognize the obligation we have to obey these commandments, as they encompass all that Christ has commanded us.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "takes up the καθὼς παρελάβετε of the former verse, and appeals to their memory in its confirmation."
2. "The stress is on τίνας, to which τοῦτο answers, 1 Thessalonians 4:3."
3. "by, i.e. coming from, παραγγελθείσας διὰ."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "by the Lord Jesus—by His authority and direction"
2. "He uses the strong term, 'commandments,' in writing to this Church not long founded, knowing that they would take it in a right spirit, and feeling it desirable that they should understand he spake with divine authority."
3. "The Lord marks His paramount authority, requiring implicit obedience."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "For ye know what commandments - It was but a short time since Paul was with them, and they could not but recollect the rules of living which he had laid down."
2. "By the Lord Jesus - By the authority of the Lord Jesus."
3. "Some of those rules, or commandments, the apostle refers to, probably, in the following verses."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Commandments [παραγγελιας] . Better, charges."
2. "The verb paraggellein to command or charge is frequent, and is often used in Class of military orders."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "I give you no new code of morals, but beseech you to live up to the instructions I formerly gave you."
2. "I refer you to my original teaching, 'for ye know,' etc."
3. "It was the Lord Jesus who moved the apostle to deliver these commandments. Christ was the agent in the matter."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  What charge  (τινας παραγγελιας). Plural, charges or precepts, command (Acts 16:24), prohibition (Acts 5:28), right living (1 Timothy 1:5). Military term in Xenophon and Polybius. 

### John Gill:
1. "Such as those of faith and love, the ordinances of the Gospel, baptism, and the Lord's supper, and all such as relate to the worship and service of God, to the discipline of Christ's house, to their behaviour one towards another, and their conduct in the world."
2. "Which were delivered to them, not as from themselves, and by their own authority, but by the Lord Jesus; in his name, and by his authority, and as ordered by him."
3. "For their commission ran to teach men all things, whatsoever Christ commanded: now since they knew what these commandments were, and whose they were, and the obligation they lay under to regard them."
