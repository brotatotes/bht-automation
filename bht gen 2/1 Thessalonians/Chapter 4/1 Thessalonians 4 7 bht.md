# 1 Thessalonians 4:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
God's call is not for impurity but for sanctification. It is important to understand that God's calling involves leaving behind unclean practices and embracing a life of holiness. This call is not just about external behavior, but also about purity in our thoughts, words, and actions. The purpose of this calling is to live a life that is marked by sobriety, righteousness, and devotion to God.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "There is no mention here of avarice: nor is it possible to understand ἀκαθαρσία, when 1Th 4:3 has gone before, of anything but carnal impurity."
2. "Chap. 1 Thessalonians 2:3, which is adduced to show that it may here represent covetousness, is a very doubtful example: see there."
3. "ἐπί, for the purpose of, on condition of: ἐν, in, ‘in the element of,’ not = εἰς, the aim: but ἁγιασμός is the whole sphere of our Christian life."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Our calling has place in a sphere of holiness."
2. "Saint is another name for Christian."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "For God hath not called us unto uncleanness - When he called us to be his followers, it was not that we should lead lives of impurity, but of holiness."
2. "We should, therefore, fulfil the purposes for which we were called into his kingdom."
3. "The word “uncleanness” (ἀκαθαρσία akatharsia), means, properly, 'impurity, filth;' and then, in a moral sense, 'pollution, lewdness,' as opposed to chastity."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The intention is viewed as the basis of the act."
2. "Sanctification is the characteristic life-element of the Christian, in which he is to live."

### John Calvin:
1. "For after having discoursed as to the correcting of the vices of the flesh, he proves, from the end of our calling, that God desires this."
2. "He concludes, that all that reject this doctrine reject not men, but God, the Author of this calling."
3. "Such persons must not be taught, but must be beaten with severe reproofs as with the stroke of a hammer."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "For God called us not onto uncleanness. Paul returns to the idea of the third verse, the idea that such sins were antagonistic to God’s purpose and work in Christians."
2. "If we profess to be responding to God’s call, let us clearly understand what it is; what we must abandon, and what we must seek."
3. "It is a call from one moral condition to another."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Not for uncleanness, but in sanctification." 
2. "God has 'called' us all for a decent sex life consonant with his aims and purposes."

### John Gill:
1. "This is another reason to enforce the above exhortations, and to caution them against the above unclean practices, taken from the end of the effectual calling by the efficacious grace of God, which is not unto uncleanness of any sort."
2. "But this call is unto holiness of life and conversation in general, and to chastity in thought, look, word, and actions in particular; for God that calls is holy, and therefore those who are called ought to be so."
3. "The calling with which they are called is a holy calling, principles of grace and holiness are wrought in their souls, when they are called; and the end of their calling is to live soberly, righteously, and godly."
