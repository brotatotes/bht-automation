# 1 Timothy 1:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
I am grateful to Christ, who has empowered me and considered me trustworthy by entrusting me with the ministry. It is a great honor to be a preacher of the Gospel and all the abilities and strengths I possess for this calling are not of my own doing, but are a result of the grace and favor of God.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The honor done him in having the Gospel ministry committed to him suggests the digression to what he once was, no better than those lawless ones described above..."
2. "I thank—Greek, 'I have (that is, feel) gratitude'... enabled me, namely, for the ministry."
3. "He foreordained and foresaw that I would be faithful to the trust committed to me. Paul's thanking God for this shows that the merit of his faithfulness was due solely to God's grace, not to his own natural strength."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "For all this, though it had required him to give up his favorite projects in life, and all the flattering schemes of ambition, he now felt that praise was due to the Redeemer."
2. "A minister, when he comes to die, feels that the highest favor which Heaven has conferred on him has been in turning his feet away from the paths of ambition, and the pursuits of ease or gain, and leading him to that holy work to which he has been enabled to consecrate his life."
3. "The apostle traced to the Lord Jesus the fact that he was in the ministry at all, and all the ability which he had to perform the duties of that holy office."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "He counted me faithful in that he appointed, etc."
2. "The term is general, covering every mode of service, either to God or to men."
3. "Diakonoi ministers is used of the secular ruler."

### John Calvin:
1. "Great is the dignity — of the apostleship, which Paul has claimed for himself; and he could not, looking at his former life, be accounted at all worthy of so high an honor."
2. "But he goes further, and turns to his own advantage what appeared to lessen his authority, declaring that the grace of God shines in him so much the more brightly."
3. "Paul says that he is satisfied with having the authority and warrant of Christ. As if he had said, 'Men may reject me, but it is enough that I am declared to be faithful by him who has all authority in himself, and who, being the heavenly Judge, hath pronounced it.'"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "I give thanks to Him who gave me power, to Christ Jesus our Lord." 
2. "Christ in His pity saw, through the rage and fury of the persecutor, the germ of that thoroughness in action, and loyalty to conscience, which was capable of being developed into the higher faithfulness."
3. "Putting. Better, when used of a Divine act, 'appointing,' as in 1 Thessalonians 5:9."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "I have gratitude to."
2. "Late verb, but regular Pauline idiom."
3. "Pauline phrase and atmosphere."

### John Gill:
1. "He esteemed it a high honour and special favour bestowed upon him, that he was a preacher of the Gospel and that all his gifts and abilities for it were not of himself, nor from men, but were owing to the free grace of God, and favour of Christ."
2. "Who gave him all his abilities for the preaching of the Gospel, and all that strength to perform the various parts of labour and service he was called unto, and all that firmness, resolution, and fortitude of mind he was endued with, to bear and suffer what he did for the sake of Christ and his Gospel."
3. "He counted him faithful, having made him so by his grace, and thus he kept him; faithfulness being a necessary requisite and qualification for a Gospel minister, he having a great trust committed to him."

### John Wesley:
1. "I thank Christ, who hath enabled me, in that he accounted me faithful, having put me into the ministry."
