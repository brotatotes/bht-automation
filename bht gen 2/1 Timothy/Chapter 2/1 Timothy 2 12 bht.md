# 1 Timothy 2:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
To usurp authority over the man, in this context, refers to women taking on the role of public teaching. While women are not prohibited from teaching in private settings, Paul's instruction is clear that they should not assume authority in public teaching. Instead, Paul highlights the importance of women learning quietly and silently, being receptive to instruction rather than taking on the role of teacher. This instruction reflects the belief that men are entrusted with the responsibility of public teaching, a role that should not be overshadowed by women's authority.

### Albert Barnes:
1. But I suffer not a woman to teach - see the notes on 1 Corinthians 14:34.Nor to usurp authority over the man - notes, 1 Corinthians 11:3.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Suffer [επιτρεπω] . Lit. turn over to; thence, permit." 
2. "The verb means to do a thing one's self; hence, to exercise authority." 
3. "Rend. to have or exercise dominion over."

### John Calvin:
1. "Not that he takes from them the charge of instructing their family, but only excludes them from the office of teaching, which God has committed to men only." 
2. "Extraordinary acts done by God do not overturn the ordinary rules of government, by which he intended that we should be bound."
3. "Accordingly, he bids them be 'quiet,' that is, keep within their own rank."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The question meets us whether the precept is of permanent obligation."
2. "The appeal is to a standard of what is ‘becoming,’ and this may vary with the habits of society."
3. "It was perhaps with a consciousness that something more was needed that St. Paul fell back upon the argument that follows."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Paul speaks authoritatively."
2. "One feels somehow that something is not expressed here to make it all clear."
3. "The word αυθεντεω is now cleared up by Kretschmer... It comes from αυτ-εντες, a self-doer, a master, autocrat."

### John Gill:
1. "They may teach in private, in their own houses and families they are to be teachers of good things."
2. "Timothy, no doubt, received much advantage from the private teachings and instructions of his mother Eunice, and grandmother Lois."
3. "To sit and hear quietly and silently, and learn, and not teach."

### John Wesley:
1. To usurp authority over the man — By public teaching.
