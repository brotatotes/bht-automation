# 1 Timothy 2:6 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The concept conveyed in these commentaries highlights the monumental sacrifice made by Christ for all of humanity. He gave Himself as a ransom, an offering more valuable than all people combined. This selfless act was not limited to a select few but was intended for all kinds of individuals. The timing of this testimony was carefully chosen by God, revealing His redemptive plan in due season.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Not only the Father gave Him for us...but the Son gave Himself" 
2. "The Son of God, therefore, became man in order that, being made like unto us in all things, sin only excepted, as our elder brother He should redeem us"
3. "The Greek implies not merely ransom, but a substituted or equivalent ransom: the Greek preposition, 'anti,' implying reciprocity and vicarious substitution"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The argument is, that as Christ died for all, it is proper to pray for all, and that the fact that he died for all is proof that God desired the salvation of all."
2. "Paul affirms it as a great and important truth that Christ gave himself a ransom for all mankind - for Jews and Gentiles; for all classes and conditions of people alike."
3. "It was a doctrine of revelation that redemption was to be provided for all people, and that it was intended that the testimony to this should be afforded at the proper time."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The gift of Christ as a ransom was to be the substance or import of the testimony which was to be set forth in its proper seasons."
2. "Marturion testimony is in apposition with the whole preceding sentence, and not with ransom only."
3. "The apostles are said, marturein to bear witness, as eye or ear witnesses of the sayings, deeds, and sufferings of Jesus."

### John Calvin:
1. "There is a necessary connection between the two things, the sacrifice of the death of Christ, and his continual intercession."
2. "The intercession by which God is reconciled to us is founded on the sacrifice."
3. "He cuts off all ground for that question, by referring to the purpose of God the season for revealing his grace."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "A ransom for all. The words at once repeat and interpret those which St. Matthew (Matthew 20:28) records as spoken by our Lord Himself."
2. "There a ‘ransom’ simply, here ‘a ransom paid as in exchange;’ there ‘instead of many,’ here ‘on behalf of all.’"
3. "To be testified. Better ‘the testimony,’ i.e. the witness which was needed, and in its own special season was given (comp. Galatians 4:4), to make known to men the saving will of God."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "A ransom for all (αντιλυτρον υπερ παντων)."
2. "Paul may have coined αντιλυτρον with the saying of Christ in mind."
3. "The testimony (το μαρτυριον). Either the nominative absolute or the accusative absolute in apposition to the preceding clause"

### John Gill:
1. "What the Mediator gave as a ransom for men is 'himself', his body and his soul, which were both made an offering for sin..."  
2. "This ransom was given for 'all'; not for every individual of mankind, for then all would be delivered, freed, and saved, whereas they are not..."  
3. "But the meaning is, either that he gave himself a ransom for many... or rather it intends that Christ gave himself a ransom for all sorts of men..."

### John Wesley:
1. "Such a ransom, the word signifies, wherein a like or equal is given; as an eye for an eye, or life for life: and this ransom, from the dignity of the person redeeming, was more than equivalent to all mankind."
2. "To be testified of in due season — Literally, in his own seasons; those chosen by his own wisdom."
