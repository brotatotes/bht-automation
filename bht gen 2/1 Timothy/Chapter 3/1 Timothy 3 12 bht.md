# 1 Timothy 3:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The qualifications for deacons are similar to those for bishops or elders. They should be faithful husbands, ruling their households well, including their children. These requirements emphasize the importance of setting a good example in the family and maintaining a strong reputation.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Let the deacons be husbands of one wife, ruling well over children."
2. "The having children to rule is to be considered as a qualification."
3. "Chrys. gives a curious and characteristic reason for the precept: πανταχοῦ τίθησι τὴν τῶν τέκνων προστασίαν, ἵνα μὴ ἀπὸ τούτου οἱ λοιποὶ σκανδαλίζωνται."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "There is no article in the Greek, 'ruling children'; implying that he regarded the having children to rule as a qualification."
2. "In the case of the deacons, as in that of the bishops, he mentions the first condition of receiving office, rather than the special qualifications for its discharge."
3. "The practical side of Christianity is the one most dwelt on in the Pastoral Epistles, in opposition to the heretical teachers."

### Albert Barnes:
1. Let the deacons be the husbands of one wife - notes, 1 Timothy 3:2.Ruling their children and their own houses well - notes, 1 Timothy 3:4-5.

### John Calvin:
1. "Let the deacons be... each of them — satisfied within having but one wife — shall set an example of a chaste and honorable father of a family, and shall keep his children and his whole house under holy discipline."
2. "And this refutes the error of those who understand this passage as referring to domestic servants."

### Philip Schaff:
1. 1 Timothy 3:12. After the parenthetic digression, the list of qualifications for the deacons is continued, the conditions of good reputation being identical with those for the bishops.
