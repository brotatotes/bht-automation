# 1 Timothy 4:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Command and teach these crucial truths, rejecting false and futile teachings and emphasizing the importance of godliness. Remind believers of the promises of salvation and encourage them to labor and endure suffering for the sake of Christ and the Gospel. Teach them to place their trust in the God who gives life and offers salvation to all.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  11 .]  Command (see ch. 1Ti 1:3 )  these things (viz. those insisted on since ver.7)  and teach them .

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 11. These truths, to the
	exclusion of those useless and even injurious teachings ( :-), while weighing well thyself, charge also upon others.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "A minister of the gospel is solemnly bound to teach that there is a sense in which God is the Saviour of all people."
2. "It is a glorious truth - and it is a thing for which a man should unceasingly give thanks to God that he may go and proclaim that He has provided salvation for all, and is willing that all should come and live."

### John Calvin:
1. "He means that the doctrine is of such a kind, that men ought not to be weary of it, though they heard it every day."
2. "There is emphasis in the demonstrative 'these'; for it means that they are not things of small importance, of which it is enough to take a passing and brief notice."
3. "A prudent pastor ought, therefore, to consider what things are chiefly necessary, that he may dwell on them."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The exhortation becomes more personal, as if the writer called to mind all that he had observed of the strength and weakness of his young disciple, and felt for and with him in the work and responsibility to which he had been, it may be, so unexpectedly called."
2. "Command and teach. The first word points to single precepts and counsels, the latter to more systematic instruction."
3. "These things, i.e. the precepts of this chapter specially."

### John Gill:
1. "Command to refuse and reject all profane and fabulous doctrines, and exhort to the exercise of true godliness, and teach the profitableness of that, and declare the promises made unto it, and assert the truth and acceptableness of them."
2. "Command, order, and encourage believers to labour and suffer reproach for the sake of Christ, and his Gospel, in hope of enjoying the said promises."
3. "Teach them to trust in the God of their lives, and the Saviour of all men."
