# 1 Timothy 5:10 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
A woman who is well reported of is someone who has brought up children in a proper manner, showing kindness and care. She has also practiced hospitality, welcoming strangers into her home with generosity and warmth. In addition, she has diligently followed every good work, showing a sincere devotion to promoting goodness and helping others. These qualities, encompassing a wide range of good deeds, are important for anyone seeking to serve the Lord and reflect His love in their actions.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "if—if, in addition to being 'well reported of.'"
2. "lodged strangers—given to hospitality"
3. "washed . . . saints' feet—after the example of the Lord"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "If she have brought up children - Either her own or others. The idea is, if she has done this in a proper manner."
2. "If she have lodged strangers - If she has been characterized by hospitality - a virtue greatly commended in the Scriptures."
3. "If she have diligently followed every good work - This is one of the characteristics of true piety. A sincere Christian will, like God, be the friend of all that is good, and will be ready to promote every good object according to his ability."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The phrase includes good deeds of all kinds, and not merely special works of beneficence."
2. "Washed the feet. A mark of Oriental hospitality bestowed on the stranger arriving from a journey, and therefore closely associated with lodged strangers."
3. "Common in Class. Originally, to suffice for, to be strong enough for, as in Homer, where it is always used in connection with danger or injury."

### John Calvin:
1. "Paul does not wish that any should be admitted into [the assemblies of widows], but those who had excellent attestations of the whole of their past life."
2. "[Paul] wishes [widows] to have had long practice and experience in all the duties which belong to [their office]; such as — labor and diligence in bringing up children, hospitality, ministering to the poor, and other charitable works."
3. "By a figure of speech, in which a part is taken for the whole, [Paul] means by the washing of the feet all the services which are commonly rendered to the saints; for at that time it was customary to 'wash the feet.'"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Including, as in the parallel of 1 Timothy 3:7, the testimony of those outside the Church."
2. "The isolated position of a small Christian community in an Asiatic town, the utter loneliness of a Christian traveller arriving in such a town, gave a prominence to the exercise of hospitality which made it incumbent on poor as well as rich."
3. "As in John 13:14, the typical instance of extremest humility in ministration, analogous to the test of kissing a leper’s flesh which Francis of Assisi imposed on his disciples."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Condition of first class. Late and rare word...Qualification for her work as leader." 
2. "Hospitality another qualification for such leadership."
3. "Proof of her hospitality, not of its being a church ordinance."

### John Gill:
1. "Training them up in the paths of religion and virtue, from which they will not so easily depart when grown up." 
2. "This may be understood of strangers in common, but especially of the brethren, ministers, and others, who came from distant parts, and traveled about to spread the Gospel of Christ." 
3. "If she has condescended to do the meanest office for the saints."

### John Wesley:
1. If she hath washed the feet of the saints — Has been ready to do the meanest offices for them.
