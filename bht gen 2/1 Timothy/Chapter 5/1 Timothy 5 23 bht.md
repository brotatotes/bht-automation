# 1 Timothy 5:23 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In order to carry out his important work, Timothy was given the advice to drink wine occasionally, not as a means of indulgence, but for the sake of his health. This recommendation was not meant to encourage excessive drinking, but rather to maintain a balanced approach. It was important for Timothy to take care of his physical well-being so that he could effectively serve the Lord. This instruction served as a reminder for Timothy to prioritize his health and not go to extremes in his pursuit of purity.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "This injunction to drink wine occasionally is a modification of the preceding 'keep thyself pure.'"
2. "Paul therefore modifies the preceding words, 'keep thyself pure,' virtually saying, 'Not that I mean to enjoin that kind of purity which consists in asceticism, nay, be no longer a water-drinker,' that is, no longer drink only water, but use a little wine, as much as is needed for thy health."
3. "God hereby commands believers to use all due means for preserving health, and condemns by anticipation the human traditions which among various sects have denied the use of wine to the faithful."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He was giving counsels in regard to an office which required a great amount of labor, care, and anxiety."
2. "Thus considered, this direction is as worthy to be given by an inspired teacher as it is to counsel a man to pay a proper regard to his health, and not needlessly to throw away his life."
3. "The exhortation extended only to a very moderate use of wine. It was not to drink it freely; it was not to drink it at the tables of the rich and the great, or in the social circle; it was not even to drink it by itself; it was to use 'a little,' mingled with water - for this was the usual method."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Timothy is not enjoined to abstain from water, but is bidden not to be a water-drinker, entirely abstaining from wine."
2. "This use of often as an adjective appears in earlier English...Puknov often, very common in Class."
3. "In this sense 3 Macc. 4 10, tw puknw sanidwmati the close planking of a ship 's deck."

### John Calvin:
1. "The meaning of the Greek wordὑδροποτεῖν is that Timothy should accustom himself to drink a little wine, for the sake of preserving his health; for he does not absolutely forbid him to 'drink water,' but to use it as his ordinary beverage."
2. "In order to meet jeers of [wicked] kind, [Paul] declares that he provides only for a case of necessity; and at the same time he recommends moderation."
3. "While we ought to be temperate in eating and drinking, every person should attend to his own health, not for the sake of prolonging life, but that, as long as he lives, he may serve God, and be of use to his neighbors."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "All experience shows that it is the weakened bloodless brain that can least control its thoughts, and is most open to the assaults of impure imaginations."
2. "St. Paul...saw...that what Timothy needed for that equilibrium was a moderate use of the stimulant which he had hitherto denied himself."
3. "The special reason given, 'for thy stomach's sake,' savours of the medical adviser...lest the disciple should draw a wrong inference from the previous words and plunge into more rigorous austerities."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Not complete asceticism, but only the need of some wine urged in Timothy's peculiar physical condition."
2. "The emphasis is on 'a little'."
3. "Timothy was clearly a semi-invalid."

### John Gill:
1. "But it was proper that he should take care of his health, that it was not impaired by too much severity, and so he be incapable of doing the work of the Lord."
2. "Not as though there was any danger of Timothy's running into an excess of drinking; but for the sake of others, lest they should abuse such a direction, to indulge themselves in an excessive way."
3. "This advice to the use of wine, was not for pleasure, and for the satisfying of the flesh, but for health, for thy stomach's sake; to help digestion, and to remove the disorders which might attend it."
