### Albert Barnes:
1. "The character of many wicked people is now clearly known. No one has any doubt of it. Their deeds have gone before them, and are recorded in the books that will be open at the judgment."
2. "The character of many wicked people is concealed. They hide their plans. They are practicing secret iniquity. They do not mean that the world shall know what they are. More than half the real depravity of the world is thus concealed from human view, and in regard to more than half the race who are going up to the judgment there is an entire mistake as to their real character."
3. "The modest, the retiring, the humble, and those here unknown, will not be overlooked in the last great day. There is much good, as there is much evil in the world, that is now concealed."

### John Calvin:
1. "Sometimes piety and other virtues obtain early and speedily their applause among men; so that great men are held in estimation."
2. "The Lord will not suffer innocence and uprightness to be always oppressed; for it is often obscured by calumnies, or by clouds."
3. "We have need of a calm spirit to endure; and therefore we must always consider what is the limit of our knowledge, that we may not go beyond it; for that would be to assume to ourselves the prerogative of God."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "In some cases a man’s good deeds are clear and patent, in others meliora latent. 'Better than the seen lies hid,' but that, too, cannot be hid for long."
2. "Enquiry will bring it to the light of day in spite even of the wish or humility of the doer."
3. "If we inquire carefully, and have the gift of insight, we shall find out before long what men are and what they have been doing."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Those (deeds, εργα) which have it otherwise." That is good deeds not clearly manifest.

3. "See Matthew 5:14-16."

### John Gill:
1. "The characters of some men, on the other hand, are so fair and bright...that they prevent any formal inquiry, examination, and judgment..."
2. "...there can be no difficulty in admitting them at once..."
3. "If they are men of bad principles or practices, they will be discovered in time...their errors and sins will break out, and be exposed..."
