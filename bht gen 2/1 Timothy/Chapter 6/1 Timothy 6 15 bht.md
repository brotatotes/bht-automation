# 1 Timothy 6:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
God reveals His plans and purposes for His kingdom at the time He sees fit. The manifestation and unfolding of His kingdom happen in successive stages, each with its own appropriate time according to His timing. We must trust in His wisdom and sovereignty, knowing that He alone has the power and knowledge to bring about His purposes.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The plural implies successive stages in the manifestation of the kingdom of God, each having its own appropriate time, the regulating principle and knowledge of which rests with the Father."
2. "He shall show—'display': an expression appropriate in reference to His 'APPEARING,' which is stronger than His 'coming,' and implies its visibility."
3. "Blessed in Himself: so about to be the source of blessing to His people at Christ appearing, whence flows their 'blessed hope'."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Which God will reveal at such times as he shall deem best."
2. "God, who is the ruler over all."
3. "The idea here is, that all the sovereigns of the earth are under his sway; that none of them can prevent the accomplishment of his purposes; and that he can direct the winding up of human affairs when he pleases."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "In his times [καιροις ιδιοις] . Better, his own seasons, or its own seasons. wither the seasons proper to the appearing, or the seasons which God shall see fit to select."
2. "Potentate [δυναστης] . Only here of God. Very often in LXX See Sir. 46 5; q 2 Macc. 12 15, etc."
3. "Of kings [των βασιλευοντων] . Lit. of those who rule as kings. Only here for the noun, basilewn. Basileuv bsilewn king of kings, Revelation 17:14; Revelation 19:16. Of lords [κυριευοντων] . Lit. of those who Lord it. Only here for the noun kuriwn. See kuriov kuriwn Lord of lords, Revelation 19:16; comp. LXX, Deuteronomy 10:17; Psalms 135:3. Probably liturgical."

### John Calvin:
1. "We are commonly hasty in our wishes, and not far from prescribing a day and hour to God, as if we should say, that he must not delay to perform anything that he has promised; and for that reason the Apostle takes an early opportunity of restraining excessive haste, by expecting the coming of Christ."
2. "Those splendid titles are here employed in exalting the princely authority of God, in order that the brilliancy of the princes of this world may not dazzle our eyes."
3. "The sum of it is, that all the governments of the world are subject to his dominion, depend upon him, and stand or fall at his bidding, but that the authority of God is beyond all comparison, because all the rest are nothing as compared with his glory, and while they fade and quickly perish, his authority will endure for ever."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He leaves the times and the seasons in the hands of the Great Ruler."
2. "The 'only' need not be explained as referring to any Gnostic scheme of dualism. It was the word which in the mouth of every true Israelite connected itself more than any other with the Divine Name."
3. "Few facts could illustrate more clearly the strength of the belief of St. John that all the attributes of the one Divine Person are shared by the other."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "In its own times. Clearly not for us to figure out." 
2. "The happy and alone Potentate." 
3. "The King of those who rule as kings."

### John Gill:
1. "God will bring it about, and make it manifest in his own time"
2. "He is the Governor among the nations, and over all the nations of the earth; his kingdom rules over all other kingdoms"
3. "For he sets up kings, and removes kings at his pleasure, and to him they must be accountable for all their administrations another day"

### John Wesley:
1. Which — Appearing.In his own times — The power, the knowledge, and the revelation of which, remain in his eternal mind.
