# 1 Timothy 6:21 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Some individuals have veered off the true path of faith, distorting the doctrine and causing confusion. Their misguided pursuit of false knowledge has led them astray, resulting in a complete abandonment of the faith. To protect against such dangers of empty discussions and deceptive teachings, the apostle urges Timothy to remain grounded in the truth, avoiding the traps that often lead to apostasy. The apostle extends his desire for God's grace to be with Timothy, enabling him to fulfill his responsibilities and navigate away from all evil and falsehood as he journeys toward the kingdom and glory of God.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "which some professing erred concerning the faith." 
2. "The grace be with thee."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "True sagacity is inseparable from faith."
2. "He restricts the salutation to Timothy, as the Epistle was not to be read in public."
3. "The 'thee' may be a transcriber's alteration to harmonize with 2 Timothy 4:22; Titus 3:15."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Evidently some who professed to be true Christians. They were attracted by false philosophy, and soon, as a consequence, were led to deny the doctrines of Christianity."
2. "This result has not been uncommon in the world."
3. "Grace be with thee - see the notes, Romans 1:7."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "In proclaiming 'ye shall live; through Christ,' is an apostle according to the promise." 
2. "The phrase promise of life only here and 1 Timothy 4:8." 
3. "Life in Christ is a Pauline thought."

### John Calvin:
1. "The way in which God punishes the haughtiness of those who, through the desire of obtaining reputation, corrupt and disfigure the doctrine of godliness, is, that he allows them to fall away from soundness of understanding, so that they involve themselves in many absurd errors."
2. "Faith is here taken, as in some former passages, for the summary of religion and sound doctrine."
3. "Warned by such examples, if we abhor revolt from “the faith,” let us adhere to the pure word of God, and let us detest sophistry and all useless subtleties, because they are abominable corruptions of religion."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Once again we have the indefinite mention of those who were known though unnamed."
2. "There were some who, boasting of their knowledge, had as concerning the faith missed their mark."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  Have erred  (ηστοχησαν). First aorist active indicative of αστοχεω. See 1 Timothy 1:6 for this word. 

### John Gill:
1. "Have erred concerning the faith: have wandered from the way of truth, and gone into the path of error; have fallen from the doctrine of faith, and made shipwreck of it, and become entire apostates." 
2. "From the danger attending vain jangling, the use of new words, the profession of a false science, and making objections from it against the truth, does the apostle dissuade Timothy from them, since they generally issue in apostasy."
3. "This the apostle wishes to him, that he might be enabled to discharge every branch of his duty he had pointed to him in this epistle, and to keep him from all evil, and every false way, and preserve him safe to the kingdom and glory of God."
