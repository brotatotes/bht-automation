# 2 Corinthians 1:20 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In Christ, all of God's promises are affirmed and fulfilled. This includes promises of forgiveness, sanctification, guidance, and eternal glory. God's faithfulness to his word is unwavering, and the fulfillment of his promises brings glory to Him. Through Christ, the gospel is confirmed, and the assurance of God's promises is assured. By embracing these promises, we glorify God's veracity, faithfulness, power, and grace. Faith in God's promises brings about the fulfillment of them, both for us and for those who hear the message of Christ.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "For how many soever be the promises of God, in Him is the yea (the affirmation and fulfilment of them all)."
2. "δι ʼ ἡμῶν must signify ‘by means of us Apostles, by our work in the Lord.’"
3. "Thus ἀμήν will be merely a strengthening of ναί the affirmation and completion of God’s promises."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Rather, How many soever be the promises of God, in Him is the 'yea'... inconstancy as to one's word."
2. "In Him is faithfulness ('yea') to His word... the immutable verification of it ('Amen')."
3. "The whole range of Old Testament and New Testament promises are secure in their fulfilment for us in Christ."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The promises of God which are made through Christ, relate to the pardon of sin to the penitent; the sanctification of his people: support in temptation and trial; guidance in perplexity; peace in death, and eternal glory beyond the grave."
2. "There shall be no vacillation on the part of God; no fickleness; no abandoning of his gracious intention."
3. "The fact that He has made such promises is an act that tends to His own glory - since it was of His mere grace that they were made; and the fulfillment of these promises in and through the church, shall also tend to produce elevated views of His fidelity and goodness."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "How many soever be the promises of God, in Him is the yea." 
2. "In giving this answer in His person and life, Christ puts the emphatic confirmation upon God's promises."
3. "Christ, in and through whom are the yea and the amen, is so proclaimed by us as to beget assurance of God's promises, and so to glorify Him."

### John Calvin:
1. "For it is not simply of the gospel in general that he treats, but he honors more especially his own gospel with this distinction. 'If the promises of God are sure and well-founded, my preaching also must of necessity be sure, inasmuch as it contains nothing but Christ, in whom they are all established.'" 
2. "It depends, however, on another principle — that it is only in Christ that God the Father is propitious to us. Now the promises are testimonies of his fatherly kindness towards us. Hence it follows, that it is in him alone that they are fulfilled."
3. "In fine, if any one considers what is the fruit of Christ’s death and resurrection, he will easily gather from this, in what respect the promises of God have been sealed and ratified in him, which would otherwise have had no sure accomplishment."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "For how many soever be the promises of God, in him is the yea." 
2. "Through him is the 'Amen, unto the glory of God through us."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "In him was the Yea come true." This applies to all God's promises.
2. "The Amen. In public worship."

### John Gill:
1. "God has made many promises to his people...relating to God himself, that he will be their God, which includes his everlasting love, his gracious presence, and divine protection."
2. "These promises are all 'in' Christ...where could they be safe and secure but in him, in whose hands are the persons, grace, and glory of his people?"
3. "These promises serve to illustrate and advance the glory of God...faith by laying hold on, and embracing the promises, glorifies the veracity, faithfulness, power, and grace of God."

### John Wesley:
1. "For all the promises of God are yea and amen in him — Are surely established in and through him."
2. "They are yea with respect to God promising; amen, with respect to men believing; yea, with respect to the apostles; amen, with respect to their hearers."
