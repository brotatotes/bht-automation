# 2 Corinthians 11:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
These false apostles were not truly called and commissioned by Christ, lacking the genuine grace and gifts of apostleship. Instead, they were deceitful workers who only sought to benefit themselves and indulge their own desires. They cunningly manipulated the teachings of God's word, using craftiness and deception to ensnare others. These impostors were skilled in the art of deceit, presenting themselves as servants of Christ while serving their own selfish intentions.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "For such men are false Apostles, dishonest workmen, changing themselves into Apostles of Christ."
2. "By a fair comparison between us, this mask will be stript off; by the abundance of my sufferings, and distinctions vouchsafed by the Lord, my Apostolicity will be fully proved, and their Pseudapostolism shewn."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "False apostles are no apostles at all."
2. "Deceitful workers, pretending to be 'workmen' for the Lord, and really seeking their own gain."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "People who had conducted thus; who attempted to impose on the people; who had brought another gospel, whatever pretences they might have - were really impostors and the enemies of Christ."
2. "It is probable that they were people who saw that great advantage might be taken of the new religion; people who saw the power which it had over the people, and who saw the confidence which the new converts were inclined to repose in their teachers."
3. "Thence they were 'deceitful (δόλιοι dolioi) workers,' full of trick, and cunning, and of plausible arts to impose on others."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Transforming themselves [μετασχηματιζομενοι] . Rev., better, fashioning, thus preserving the distinctive force of schma outward fashion, which forms part of the compound verb. See on Matthew 17:2; 1 Corinthians 4:6.

### John Calvin:
1. "They are deceitful workers, says he, that is — they do not discover their wickedness at first view, but artfully insinuate themselves under some fair pretext."
2. "Hence they require to be carefully and thoroughly sifted, lest we should receive persons as servants of Christ, as soon as any appearance of excellence is discovered."
3. "Because there was a dangerous profanation of virtue in pretending to burn with greater zeal than all the servants of Christ."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "For such men are false apostles, deceitful workers, fashioning themselves into apostles of Christ." 
2. "He now tears off the mask, giving his opponents their true name."
3. "They but copy their real master."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "False apostles (ψευδαποστολο). From ψευδης, false, and αποστολος."
2. "Fashioning themselves (μετασχηματιζομενο). Present middle (direct) participle of the old verb μετασχηματιζω for which see on 1 Corinthians 4:6."


### John Gill:
1. "They were not called and sent forth by Christ; they had not the grace of apostleship, or gifts qualifying them for that high office."
2. "They pretended to work, but did not; and to work for Christ, when they only served themselves, and their own bellies."
3. "They walked in craftiness, and handled the word of God deceitfully, and lay in wait to deceive men; and were masters of so much art and cunning."
