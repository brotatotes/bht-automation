# 2 Corinthians 12:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Despite the accusations of his adversaries, who claimed that he used cunning tactics and other people to secretly obtain support from the Corinthians, the apostle Paul asserts that he did not burden them himself. Instead, he acknowledges that there may have been a perception of craftiness, as he employed indirect means to achieve his financial goals. However, he emphasizes that he did not act deceitfully or maliciously, but rather sought to spread the gospel freely and sincerely.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Let the fact be granted, that I myself did not burden you." 
2. "Nevertheless, being crafty, I caught you with guile." 
3. "Caught you, in order to practice upon you for my own ends."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "I did not burden you—The 'I' in the Greek is emphatic." 
2. "A possible insinuation of the Corinthians is hereby anticipated and refuted."
3. "But, you may say, granted that I did not burden you myself; nevertheless, being crafty, I caught you (in my net) with guile; namely, made a gain of you by means of others."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "We admit that you did not burden us. You did not exact a support from us. But all this was mere trick. You accomplished the same thing in another way. You professed when with us not to seek our property but our souls. But in various ways you contrived to get our money, and to secure your object."
2. "Being crafty - Being cunning. That is, by sending persons to obtain money on different pretences."
3. "I caught you with guile - I took you by deceit or fraud. That is, making use of fraud in pretending that the money was for poor and afflicted saints, when in reality it was for my own use."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. With guile. Alluding to a charge that he availed himself of the collection for the poor to secure money for himself. He uses his adversaries ' words.

### John Calvin:
1. "They insinuated, that he was a sly, crafty man, going about preaching, persuading, and catching people with guile."
2. "Hence, our choice of them must not be made lightly, or as a matter of mere form, but with the utmost possible care."
3. "‘What end,’ says Paul, ‘could I have in view, in persuading you to embrace the gospel? Have I gained any thing by you? When I was with you, was I burdensome to you? No: nor, as things are, will I be burdensome. Yet being crafty, forsooth, I caught you with guile.’”

### Philip Schaff:
1. "But me being crafty, I caught you with guile."
2. "True (ye say), you took no money from us yourself, but you were crafty enough to get it through others."
3. "The answer to this base insinuation, which the apostle deems his detractors capable of, now follows."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "I did not myself burden you."
2. "Crafty... skilful... cunning."
3. "The enemies of Paul said that he was raising this big collection for himself."

### John Gill:
1. "They insinuated to the Corinthians, and objected to the apostle, that though he did not receive anything from them with his own hands, yet he craftily and cunningly made use of others to drain their purses." 
2. "These words are not spoken by the apostle in his own person of himself, but in the person of his adversaries, and contain a concession and an objection of theirs."
3. "They are spoken in the person of the false apostles, charging him with a wicked and criminal craftiness, by making use of other persons in a sly underhanded way, to get this church's money, when he pretended to preach the Gospel freely."

### John Wesley:
1. "Being crafty I caught you with guile - I did secretly by my messengers what I would not do openly, or in person."
