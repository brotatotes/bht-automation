# 2 Corinthians 12:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Paul strongly denies the false accusation that he had deceived or defrauded the Corinthians. He challenges them to point out a single person whom he had used in such a dishonest manner or had received money from on his behalf. By asking them to provide specific examples, Paul exposes the slander as baseless and emphasizes his integrity in his dealings with the Corinthians.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "You know well I did not."
2. "My associates were as disinterested as myself."
3. "An important rule to all who would influence others for good."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Same the man," says he, "who has thus defrauded you under my instructions."
2. "If the charge is well-founded, let him be specified, and let the mode in which it was done be distinctly stated."
3. "Here Paul asks whether he had defrauded them by means of anyone whom he had sent to them."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "By any one of them (τινα--δι' αυτου). An anacoluthon for τινα is left in the accusative without a verb and δι' αυτου takes up the idea, 'as to any one by him.'".
2. "Whom (ων). The genitive relative is attracted from the accusative ους into the case of the unexpressed antecedent τουτον)."

### John Gill:
1. "He appeals to the Corinthians against such calumnies and false insinuations, whether ever he had circumvented them in such a manner, or had ever used such artful methods to pillage them."
2. "He desires them to name one single person of the many who came to them from him, that had received any money from them for him; or that they had any reason to believe he had employed for such purposes; and if they could not pitch on a single instance, they ought therefore to look upon this as a downright slander and calumny."

### John Wesley:
1. "I answer this lying accusation by appealing to plain fact."
2. "It should be carefully observed, that St. Paul does not allow, but absolutely denies, that he had caught them with guile."
