# 2 Corinthians 13:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The apostles acknowledged that their power was not meant to be used against the truth, but rather to promote and defend it. They were not seeking personal gain or reputation, but were solely focused on doing what was right and furthering the cause of truth. They understood that their authority was to be used for the benefit of those who embraced the truth and were walking in accordance with it.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "For we have no power against the truth."
2. "We shall be at one with you and so have no opportunity of shewing our power."
3. "On behalf of the truth."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Our apostolic power is given us that we may use it not against, but for the furtherance of, the truth."
2. "Where you are free from fault, there is no scope for its exercise: and this I desire."
3. "Far be it from me to use it against the innocent, merely in order to increase my own power."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "We who are under the influence of the Spirit of God; who have been commissioned by him as apostles, can do nothing that shall be against the great system of truth which we are appointed to promulgate and defend."
2. "Our aim is to promote the truth, and to do what is right; and we cannot, therefore, by any regard to our own reputation, or to any personal advantage, do what is wrong, or countenance, or desire what is wrong in others."
3. "It was a fixed principle with him to act only in accordance with truth; to do what was right."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "For we can do nothing against the truth."
2. "Your well-doing is what we truly aim at."
3. "In that case, we should be unapproved before God."

### John Calvin:
1. "I do not seek, or desire any other power, than what the Lord has conferred upon me, that I may promote the truth."
2. "To false Apostles it is all one, provided they have power; and they feel no concern to make use of their power for the promotion of what is good."
3. "This passage must be carefully observed, because it limits the power, which the Pastors of the Church should have, and fixes its proper bounds — that they be ministers of the truth."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "For we can do nothing against the truth, but for the truth." 
2. "Our one object is to promote and establish the truth; and that end attained, if with the exercise of discipline, all the better." 
3. "Not to display our apostolic authority do we live, but to see the truth triumphant everywhere."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  Against the truth  (κατα της αληθειας). He means in the long run. We can hinder and hold down the truth by evil deeds (Romans 1:18), but in the end the truth wins. 

### John Gill:
1. "For we can do nothing against the truth,.... The apostles had no power, nor could they, nor did they desire to exercise any against such who received the truth of the Gospel in the love of it."
2. "The authority the apostles had received from Christ was not to be exercised upon such."
3. "For the truth: for the sake of defending the truth against those that dropped, denied, and opposed it; and for the honour of it, by chastising, correcting, reproving, censuring, and punishing such, who either contradicted it, or caused it to be blasphemed and spoken evil of."

### John Wesley:
1. For we can do nothing against the truth — Neither against that which is just and right, nor against those who walk according to the truth of the gospel.
