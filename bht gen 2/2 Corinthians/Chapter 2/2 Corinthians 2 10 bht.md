# 2 Corinthians 2:10 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Whenever I forgive an offender, I do it for your sake and in full assurance of your forgiveness. In the presence of Christ, I act sincerely and wholeheartedly. I forgive this man on your behalf, and by the authority given to me. Therefore, you are also encouraged to extend your forgiveness.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Your forgiveness is mine: not said generally (as Meyer), but definitely, pointing at the one person here spoken of and no other."
2. "Then he substantiates this assurance, by further assuring them, that his forgiveness of any fault in this case, if it takes place, takes place on their account."
3. "In the person of Christ, acting as Christ, in the same way as he had commanded the punishment ἐν τῷ ὀνόματι τοῦ κυρίου ἡμῶν Ἰησοῦ, 1 Corinthians 5:4."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "They may be assured of Paul's apostolic sanction to their doing so."
2. "It is for your sakes I have forgiven, and do forgive, that the Church may suffer no hurt by the loss of a soul, and that ye may learn leniency as well as faithfulness."
3. "In the person of Christ—representing Christ, and acting by His authority."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "I have confidence in you as a Christian society and such confidence, that if you forgive an offence in one of your members, I shall approve the act, and shall also be ready to forgive."
2. "It is not on account of the offender alone; it is in order to promote the happiness and purity of the church."
3. "In the presence of Christ; with his eye upon me, and conscious that I am acting before him, and must give account to him. It implies, undoubtedly, that Paul acted by his authority, and felt that he was doing that which Christ would approve."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. In the person [εν προσωπω] . Better, as Rev., in margin, presence; or face, as if Christ were looking on. See on ch. 2 Corinthians 1:11.

### John Calvin:
1. "Do not hesitate to forgive: I promise that I shall confirm whatever you may have done, and I already subscribe your sentence of forgiveness."
2. "Instead of the expression in the sight of Christ, some prefer person, because Paul in that reconciliation was in the room of Christ, and did in a manner represent his person."
3. "For he is accustomed to employ this phrase to express pure and undisguised rectitude."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "As your excommunication, in my absence, yet by my instructions, was mine, so will your restoration be." 
2. "For what I also have forgiven, if I have forgiven any thing, for your sakes have I forgiven it in the person (Gr. 'the presence') of Christ." 
3. "No stronger claim to apostolic authority in the highest sense could be advanced."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  In the person of Christ  (εν προσωπω Χριστου). More exactly, "in the presence of Christ," before Christ, in the face of Christ. Cf. ενωπιον του θεου (2 Corinthians 4:2) in the eye of God, ενωπιον Κυριου (2 Corinthians 8:21). 

### John Gill:
1. "Whenever I have forgiven any offender, as I now do forgive this man, I always do it for your sake; either at your request, or in confidence of this, that you yourselves have forgiven."
2. "What I do of this kind, I do it in the person of Christ; in his sight and presence, who knows that what I do this way, I do it sincerely, and with my whole heart and soul."
3. "Since I forgive this man for your sakes, and in the name of Christ, you ought to do so likewise."

### John Wesley:
1. "He makes no question of their complying with his direction."
2. "So mildly does he speak even of that heinous sin, after it was repented of."
3. "By the authority wherewith he has invested me."
