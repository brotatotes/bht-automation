# 2 Corinthians 2:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Rest eluded me as I anxiously waited to hear about the reception of my letter. My spirit was unsettled, longing for news from Titus. When he did not arrive as expected, I felt a deep sense of unease. In search of answers, I decided to travel to Macedonia, where I hoped to gather information about you and your well-being.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "I had not rest for my spirit...He could not with any tranquillity pursue the spiritual duties opened to him at Troas."
2. "My colleague in the Apostleship."
3. "...the disciples there: understood from the context."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "no rest for my spirit" ( :-).
2. "spirit" under the Holy Spirit
3. "taking . . . leave of
them"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "I had no rest in my spirit - I was disappointed, sad, deeply anxious."
2. "But taking my leave of them - Though so many considerations urged me to stay; though there was such a promising field of labor, yet such was my anxiety to hear from you, that I left them."
3. "It shows that there may be cases where it is proper for ministers to leave a field of great and inviting usefulness, to go to another field and to engage in another part of the great vineyard."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Rest [ανεσιν] . Rev., relief. See on liberty, Acts 24:23."
2. "Taking my leave [αποταξαμενος] . The verb means, primarily, to set apart or separate; hence to separate one's self, withdraw, and so to take leave of."
3. "The A. V. gives this sense in every case, except Mark 6:46, where it wrongly renders sent away."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "I had no relief for my spirit, because I found not Titus my brother as if this seemed to bode ill news."
2. "Before parting they had probably arranged their respective routes, so as to give reasonable hope of their meeting at Troas, and failing this in Macedonia."
3. "I went forth into Macedonia and there meeting with Titus, he got from him such tidings as caused him to break forth into the transport expressed in the next verse."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "I had no relief" 
2. "For my spirit" 
3. "Because I found not Titus" 
4. "Taking my leave of them"

### John Gill:
1. "Though there was such a door opened to preach the Gospel, and such an opportunity of doing good, yet he was greatly distressed in his mind; very restless and uneasy in his spirit."
2. "Not finding him as he expected, he grew uneasy: not that he wanted him as an interpreter for him, or his assistance in preaching the Gospel at Troas, but because he was exceedingly desirous of knowing from him the state of affairs in the church at Corinth."
3. "He goes on with, saying, 'but taking my leave of them; the disciples at Troas, and ordering and prescribing, as the word here used signifies, how things should be managed for the best after his departure...'"

### John Wesley:
1. "I had no rest in my spirit — From an earnest desire to know how my letter had been received."
2. "Because I did not find Titus — In his return from you."
3. "So I went forth into Macedonia — Where being much nearer, I might more easily be informed concerning you."
