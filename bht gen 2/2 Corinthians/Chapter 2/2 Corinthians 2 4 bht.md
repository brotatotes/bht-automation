# 2 Corinthians 2:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
From a place of great trouble and anguish of heart, I wrote to you not with levity, but with sincere and intense love. My purpose was not to cause you sorrow, but to demonstrate the depth of my affection for you. It was my desire for you to understand how much I cared for you, even more than others.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "that he did not write in levity of purpose, but under great trouble of mind, not to grieve them, but to testify his love."
2. "he wrote, out of much tribulation and anguish of heart, with many tears."
3. "ἀγάπην, before the conjunction ἵνα, for special emphasis."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "So far from my change of purpose being due to 'lightness,' I wrote my letter to you 'out of much affliction and anguish of heart, and with many tears.'"
2. "My ultimate and main object was, 'not that ye might be made sorry,' but that through sorrow you might be led to repentance, and so to joy, redounding both to you and me."
3. "'Love' is the source from which sincere reproof springs; that the Corinthians might ultimately recognize this as his motive, was the apostle's aim."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The evil conduct of others gives pain to a good man; and the necessity of administering reproof and discipline is often as painful to him who does it, as it is to those who are the subjects of it."
2. "With all his strength of mind, and all His courage and readiness to face danger, Paul was not ashamed to weep; and especially if he had any occasion of censuring his Christian brethren, or administering discipline."
3. "Admonition would always be done right if it was done with tears. Discipline would always be right, and would be effectual, if it were administered with tears."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Anguish, from the Latin, angere to choke: anger, which, in earlier English, means affliction, mental torture." 
2. "The Latin anguis a snake, marking the serpent by his throttling." 
3. "In Sanskrit, anhas, from the same root, was the name for sin, the throttler."

### John Calvin:
1. "Intensity of grief has extorted from me everything that I have written."
2. "For there are many noisy reprovers, who...display a surprising ardour of zeal, while in the meantime they are at ease in their mind...It is, however, the part of a pious pastor, to weep within himself, before he calls upon others to weep."
3. "The more tender the affections of love are, they are so much the more praiseworthy."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Anguish (συνοχης). Ablative case after εκ (out of). Old word from συνεχω, to hold together. So contraction of heart (Cicero, contractio animi), a spiritual angina pectoris." 
2. "He dictated that letter 'through tears' (accompanied by tears). Paul was a man of heart." 
3. "He twice mentions his tears in his speech at Miletus (Acts 20:19-31)."

### John Gill:
1. "Being greatly pressed in his spirit, and grieved at his heart, for the abominable iniquities among them..."
2. "Which was written, not that you should be grieved; that is, not merely for the sake of grieving of them, in which he took no pleasure..."
3. "As his love was very vehement towards them, he was desirous they should know it, and how exceeding abundant it was; and that it was even greater towards them, than to others..."

### John Wesley:
1. From much anguish I wrote to you, not so much that ye might be grieved, as that ye might know by my faithful admonition my abundant love toward you.
