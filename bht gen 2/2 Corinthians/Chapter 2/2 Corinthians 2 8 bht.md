# 2 Corinthians 2:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
By publicly restoring him to fellowship and treating him with love and kindness, you can confirm your deep affection for him. Embrace him as a brother and extend warm gestures of acceptance and friendship, leaving no doubt about your renewed love for him. This act should be done collectively as a church, with the whole community giving their full consent and unanimous support.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "[To ratify by a public decree of the church] his exclusion was not by such a decree, but only by the abstinence of individuals from his society."
2. "The ratifying their love to him would consist in the majority making it evident to him that he was again recognized as a brother."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Confirm your love toward him—by giving effect in act, and showing in deeds your love."
2. "By restoring him to your fellowship and praying for his recovering from the sickness penally inflicted on him."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Wherefore I beseech you that ye would confirm your love toward him..."
2. "...they should give strong expressions and assurances of their love to him; that they should pursue such a course as would leave no room for doubt in regard to it."
3. "Paul referred, doubtless, here to some public act of the church by which the sentence of excommunication might be removed, and by which the offender might have a public assurance of their favor."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Confirm your love." (κυρωσαι αγαπην)
2. The verb is found only here and Galatians 3:15.
3. From kurov supreme power, authority.
4. Hence to take judicial resolution to treat the offender with brotherly love.

### Philip Schaff:
1. 2 Corinthians 2:8.  Wherefore I beseech you to confirm   your   love towards him by as public a restoration to fellowship as publicly he had been banished from it.
	 

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "To confirm (κυρωσα). First aorist active infinitive of old verb κυροω, to make valid, to ratify, from κυρος (head, authority)."
2. "In N.T. only here and Galatians 3:15."

### John Gill:
1. "Express your love to him in the most kind and tender manner, show the same, and as strong love to him as you did before, and as if he had never offended."
2. "Receive him as a brother in the most affectionate manner, and embrace him with the most endearing expressions of respect and friendship."
3. "Let your reception of him in this kind and friendly way be with the full consent, and by the joint vote and suffrage of the whole church."
