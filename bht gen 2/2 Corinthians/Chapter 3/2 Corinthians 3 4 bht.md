# 2 Corinthians 3:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
And such trust have we in God, that we are fully confident in the truth and effectiveness of our ministry. The Corinthians themselves serve as living testimonies to the power and impact of our work. We rely not on human achievements or self-conceit, but our confidence is rooted in Christ and the grace He imparts. Through Christ, we trust in God and His work in us and through us.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "He had just spoken of his consciousness of apostolic success among them (which assertion would be true also of other churches which he had founded) being his worldwide recommendation."
2. "It is this confidence of which he here speaks."
3. "Such confidence however we possess through Christ towards God: i.e. ‘it is no vain boast, but rests on power imparted to us through Christ in regard to God’s work and our own account to be given to Him.’"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "But such confidence, however, we have through Christ toward God."
2. "Confidence toward God is solid and real, as looking to Him for the strength needed now, and also for the reward of grace to be given hereafter."
3. "Human confidence is unreal in that it looks to man for its help and its reward."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "And such trust have we - Such confidence have we that we are appointed by God, and that he accepts our work."
2. "Such evidence have we in the success of our labors; such irrefragable proof that God blesses us; that we have trust, or confidence, that we are sent by God, and are owned by him in our ministry."
3. "His confidence did not rest on letters of introduction from people, but in the evidence of the divine presence, and the divine acceptance of his work."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Confidence. In the fact that he may appeal to them, notwithstanding their weaknesses and errors."
2. "Through Christ to God - ward [δια του χριστου προς τον θεον]. Through Christ who engenders the confidence, toward God, with reference to God who gives us success, and to whom we must account for our work."

### John Calvin:
1. "By this boasting, I extol God rather than myself, by whose grace I am what I am."


### Philip Schaff:
1. "And such confidence have we through Christ."
2. "... our sufficiency is from God."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  Through Christ  (δια του Χριστου). It is not self-conceit on Paul's part, but through Christ. 

### John Gill:
1. "They made manifest the savoury knowledge of God and Christ everywhere."
2. "The Corinthians were so many living epistles of commendations of the power and efficacy of their ministry."
3. "Such confidence and firm persuasion of the truth of grace on your souls, and of our being the happy instruments of it, we have through Christ, the grace of Christ, to God-ward."

### John Wesley:
1. Such trust have we in God — That is, we trust in God that this is so.
