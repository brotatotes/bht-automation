# 2 Corinthians 4:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Though our bodies may weaken and decay, our souls are continually being renewed. The trials and suffering we endure in this earthly life do not cause us to lose heart. Instead, they serve as reminders to redirect our focus towards the eternal and to strengthen our spiritual beings. It is through these challenges that we are sustained and encouraged to carry out the work of God, knowing that our inner selves are constantly being refreshed by His grace.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "though even (not ‘even if,’)" 
2. "our outward man is [being] wasted away"
3. "our inner (man) is [being] renewed day by day"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "We faint not—notwithstanding our sufferings."
2. "The inward man—our spiritual and true being, the 'life' which even in our mortal bodies 'manifests the life of Jesus.'"
3. "Is renewed—'is being renewed,' namely, with fresh 'grace,' 'faith,' and hope."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The purpose of trying to save as many as possible would make toil easy, privations welcome, and would be so accompanied by the grace of God, as to gird the soul with strength, and fill it with abundant consolations."
2. "We are sustained, encouraged, emboldened by having such an object in view."
3. "In an effort to do good, and to promote the salvation of man, the soul will be sustained in trials, and will be comforted and invigorated even when the body is weary, grows old, decays, and dies."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The more the marble wastes the more the statue grows."
2. "Time does not depress your spirit, but it grows young again: your body, however, is weak."
3. "A Hebrew form of expression."

### John Calvin:
1. "There is no cause why we should lose heart, or sink down under the burden of the cross."
2. "The outward man is the maintenance of the earthly life, which consists not merely in the flower of one’s age and in good health, but also in riches, honors, friendships, and other resources."
3. "For as we are too much taken up with the present life, so long as everything goes on to our mind, the Lord, on that account, by taking away from us, by little and little, the things that we are engrossed with, calls us back to meditate on a better life."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Wherefore on all the foregoing grounds we faint not."
2. "But though our outward man is decaying, yet the inward man is renewed day by day."
3. "Under a growing experience of 'the power' of His resurrection."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Here the decay of the bodily organism is set over against the growth in grace of the man himself" (Bernard).
2. "Repeats from verse 2 Corinthians 4:1."
3. "In Romans 7:22; Colossians 3:9; Ephesians 4:22, we have the inward man and the outward for the higher and the lower natures (the spirit and the flesh)."

### John Gill:
1. "Our spirits do not sink under the weight of them; we do not give out from the work of the ministry because of them, but go on cheerfully therein."
2. "Our bodies are almost worn out with fatigue, labour, and sorrow; our earthly tabernacles are tottering, and just ready to fall in pieces."
3. "The internal hidden man of the heart, the new man is in a prosperous condition; our souls are in good health; the work of God is comfortably carried on in us."

### John Wesley:
1. Therefore — Because of this grace, we faint not.The outward man — The body.The inward man — The soul.
