# 2 Corinthians 4:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
We often find ourselves facing troubles from every direction, experiencing difficulties inwardly and outwardly. These challenges can leave us feeling restricted, overwhelmed, and uncertain of the way forward. However, we can take comfort in the fact that although we may be pressed, we are not completely crushed by our circumstances. We may be perplexed and unsure of what steps to take, but we need not despair, for we have the assurance that God's power and love will guide us and see us through every trial.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Pressed, but not crushed."
2. "Perplexed, but not in despair."
3. "Persecuted, but not deserted."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "BEING hard pressed, yet not inextricably straitened; reduced to inextricable straits."
2. "Perplexed, but not in despair."
3. "The first clause in each member of the series of contrasted participles, implies the earthiness of the vessels; the second clause, the excellency of the power."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The whole passage is one of the most pathetic and beautiful to be found in the New Testament."
2. "He refers to the fact that he was called to endure a great number of trials and afflictions."
3. "Their needs were provided for; their embarrassments were removed; their grounds of perplexity were taken away; and unexpected strength and resources were imparted to them."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "We are pressed closely, yet not cramped."
2. "We are pressed on every side, yet not straitened."
3. "We are perplexed, but not utterly perplexed."

### John Calvin:
1. "We are reduced...to straits, but the Lord at length opens up for us an outlet...under God’s protection we are safe."
2. "Though we are brought low...still we do not perish."
3. "You see, how he turns to his own advantage every charge that the wicked bring against him."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Pressed [...] that vividly picture Paul's ministerial career." 
2. "Yet not straitened [...] to keep in a tight place." 
3. "Yet not unto despair [...] lost, but not lost out."

### John Gill:
1. "We are troubled on every side, wherever we are, we are sure to meet with trouble, of one sort or another."
2. "We are never free from one trial or another."
3. "We are sometimes at the utmost loss about things temporal, how we shall be provided for with food and raiment."

### John Wesley:
1. "The four articles in this verse respect inward, the four in the next outward, afflictions."
2. "Not crushed - Not swallowed up in care and anxiety."
3. "Perplexed — What course to take, but never despairing of his power and love to carry us through."
