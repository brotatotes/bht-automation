# 2 Corinthians 5:20 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
We are ambassadors for Christ, called to publish and proclaim His message. Consider the incredible condescension and tender mercies of God, who not only offers us reconciliation, but invites us and entreats us to accept it. Let us submit our wills to His and be reconciled to all that He has done for us.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "On Christ's behalf" (verse 20). 
2. "Such 'beseeching' is uncommon in the case of 'ambassadors,' who generally stand on their dignity." 
3. "Be reconciled to God, that is, let God reconcile you to Himself."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "We are the ambassadors whom Christ has sent forth to negotiate with people in regard to their reconciliation to God."
2. "He is sent to do what the sovereign would himself do were he present."
3. "The gospel message is God speaking to people through the ministry, and entreating them to be reconciled."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. We are ambassadors [πρεσβευομεν] . Only here and Ephesians 6:10.

### John Calvin:
1. "It is a matter of too much importance, to allow of our resting contented with the promise of men, without feeling assured that they are ordained by God, and that God speaks to us by them."
2. "This is the design of those commendations, with which Christ himself signalizes his Apostles: He that heareth you, heareth me, etc."
3. "For this is a continued embassy, which must be assiduously sounded forth in the Church until the end of the world; and the gospel cannot be preached, unless remission of sins is promised."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "We are ambassadors therefore on behalf of Christ, as though God were intreating by us (as His mouth), we beseech you on behalf of Christ, be ye reconciled to God." 
2. "Calvin would extend this reconciliation to the daily forgiveness which believers themselves stand in need of, because the Epistle is addressed to believers."
3. "But besides that the opening verse of the next chapter is a word to them, the call is evidently to all unreconciled ones whom his words might reach, no longer to withhold their seal to that great act of God in the death of His Son in which lay their only hope of reconciliation to Him."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "We are ambassadors therefore on behalf of Christ...Paul was Christ's Legate to act in his behalf and in his stead." 
2. "The ambassador has to be persona grata with both countries...Paul has a natural pride in using this dignified term for himself and all ministers."
3. "Be ye reconciled to God...This is the ambassador's message as he bears it to men from God."

### John Gill:
1. "We are ambassadors for Christ...to publish and proclaim it as made by him." 
2. "Consider from whence it takes its rise, what methods have been used to effect it, and how it is accomplished." 
3. "Be reconciled to all the dispensations of divine Providence towards you; let your wills bow, and be resigned to his, since he is the God of peace to you."

### John Wesley:
1. "In general he uses a more calm, sedate kind of exhortation, as in the beginning of the next chapter."
2. "What unparalleled condescension and divinely tender mercies are displayed in this verse!"
3. "Yet our almighty Lord, and our eternal Judge, not only vouchsafes to offer these blessings, but invites us, entreats us, and, with the most tender importunity, solicits us, not to reject them."
