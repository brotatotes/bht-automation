# 2 Corinthians 6:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
O beloved Corinthians, our dear friends, we speak to you openly and freely with the utmost honesty. Our hearts overflow with love and deep affection for you, desiring nothing but your well-being. It is from the abundance of our hearts, from our genuine care for you, that our lips pour forth unreservedly. We address you by name, emphasizing the significance of our words. May you receive our exhortation with the same openness and love with which it is given.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Our mouth is open towards you, Corinthians, our heart has become enlarged." 
2. "The heart is considered as a space, wherein its thoughts and feelings are contained."
3. "He assures them of their place in his heart, which is wide enough for, and does contain them."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "I use all freedom and openness of speech to you as to beloved friends."
2. "The enlargement of his heart towards them produced his openness of mouth, that is, his unreserved expression of his inmost feelings."
3. "The apostle's heart is enlarged by love, so as to take in his converts at Corinth, not only with their graces, but with their many shortcomings."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "We speak freely, and fully... words would flow freely, and... he spoke to them with the utmost freedom of what he had thought, and purposed, and done."
2. "We have deep feelings, which naturally vent themselves in fervent and glowing language... he had a strong affection for them; a heart which embraced and loved them all, and which expressed itself in the language of deep emotion."
3. "True attachment will find means of expressing itself... if a minister loves the souls of his people he will pour out the affections of his heart in strong and glowing language."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Ye Corinthians. The readers are addressed by name in only two other epistles, Galatians 3:1; Philippians 4:15." 
2. "Is enlarged [πεπλατυνται] . Only here, ver. 13, and Matthew 23:5, where it is used of widening the phylacteries. From platuv broad."
3. "The idea of enlargement of heart in the sense of increased breadth of sympathy and understanding, as here, is also expressed in the Old Testament by other words, as concerning Solomon, to whom God gave largeness of heart, Sept., cuma outpouring."

### John Calvin:
1. "I have ample ground of glorying, and an upright conscience opens my mouth."
2. "It is quite a common figure, to speak of a narrow and contracted heart as denoting either grief, or disgust, while, on the other hand, an enlarged heart is employed to denote dispositions of an opposite kind."
3. "Hence it is, that the tongue, also, is free and unfettered, does not falter, does not with difficulty draw up from the bottom of the throat broken syllables, as usually happens when the mind is influenced by a less joyful affection."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He has kept back nothing in his portrayal of the glory of the ministry as the picture of the open mouth shows."
2. "Hence his freedom of speech for 'out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaks'."

### John Gill:
1. "We shall hide and conceal nothing from you, we shall deal with you with all plainness and faithfulness."
2. "Our heart is enlarged: with love to you, and eager desires after your good."
3. "It is from the abundance of our hearts, and hearty affection for you, that our mouth is open so freely to communicate to you."

### John Wesley:
1. "From the praise of the Christian ministry, which he began, he now draws his affectionate exhortation."
2. "O ye Corinthians — He seldom uses this appellation. But it has here a peculiar force."
3. "Our mouth is opened toward you — With uncommon freedom, because our heart is enlarged - In tenderness."
