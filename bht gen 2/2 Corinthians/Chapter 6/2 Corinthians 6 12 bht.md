# 2 Corinthians 6:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
You are not limited in our affections for you - our hearts are open and welcoming. But it is you who are constricted in your own affections. Your hearts are shut, preventing you from experiencing the full blessings and love that could be poured out upon you.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Any constraint ye feel towards me, or narrowness of heart, is not from want of largeness of heart on my part towards you, but from want of it on your part towards me."
2. "Bowels - that is, affections."
3. "Not straitened in us - that is, for want of room in our hearts to take you in."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Ye are not straitened in us - That is, you do not possess a narrow or contracted place in our affections."
2. "There is no lack of room in our affections toward you. It is not narrow, confined, pent up. It is ample and free."
3. "The idea here is, that they were straitened, or were confined in their affections for him. It is the language of reproof, meaning that he had not received from them the demonstrations of attachment which he had a right to expect, and which was a fair and proportionate return for the love bestowed on them."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Not straitened in us. It is not that our hearts are too narrow to take you in."
2. "Straitened in antithesis with enlarged."
3. "In your own bowels [τοις οπλαγχνοις υμων]. See on 1 Peter 3:8; James 5:11. Rev., affections. It is your love that is contracted."

### John Calvin:
1. "Ye are not straitened in us... My very heart would willingly pour itself forth, but you shut up your bowels." 
2. "It is owing to your own fault that you are not able to share in this feeling of cheerfulness... owhing to their corrupt judgment, that the things that he utters are not relished by them."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Ye are not straitened in us."  
2. "There is no restraint in me (my heart)."  
3. "My adversaries may have caused some of you to tighten up your affections."

### John Gill:
1. "Ye are not straitened in us.... but ye are straitened in your own bowels."
2. "You have room enough in our hearts... an expression, setting forth the exceeding great love and strong affection the apostle bore to the Corinthians."
3. "He had a heart to hold them all without being straitened for room; and among all them they could scarce find room enough in their hearts and affections for him."

### John Wesley:
1. "Ye are not straitened in us — Our heart is wide enough to receive you all."
2. "But ye are straitened in your own bowels — Your hearts are shut up, and so not capable of the blessings ye might enjoy."
