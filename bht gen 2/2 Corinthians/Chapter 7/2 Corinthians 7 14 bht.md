# 2 Corinthians 7:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Apostle Paul had expressed his confidence in the Corinthians to Titus, believing that they would respond positively to his visit and implement the Apostle's instructions. Through Titus' report, Paul was pleased to find that he was not put to shame. The Corinthians had proven to be receptive and true to Paul's expectations. Paul's confidence in them was justified, as they demonstrated sincerity, kindness, and a willingness to do what is right. Paul's honest praise of their character and disposition had been proven true.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "I was not put to shame, viz. by being shewn, on Titus’s coming to you, to have boasted in vain."
2. "'But truthfulness was shewn to be my constant rule of speech, to whomsoever I spoke.' But as we spoke (generally, not merely in our teaching, as Theodoret, al.) all things in truth (truthfully) to you, so also our boasting concerning you before Titus was (was proved to be: was, as shewn by proof) truth."
3. "De W. suggests that the Apostle had described (by anticipation) to Titus in glowing terms the affection and probable prompt obedience of the Corinthians, as an encouragement to his somewhat unwelcome journey."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "I am not ashamed - 'I am not put to shame,' namely, by learning from Titus that you did not realize the high character I gave him of you."
2. "As our speaking in general to you was true, so our particular boasting to Titus concerning you is now, by his report, proved to be truth."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He had probably expressed his belief that he would be kindly received; that they would be disposed to listen to him, and to comply with the directions of the apostle..." 
2. "All my expectations are realized; and you have been as kind, and hospitable, and benevolent as I assured him you would be."
3. "My boasting of your character, and of your disposition to do right, which I made before Titus has turned out to be true."

### John Calvin:
1. "He shows indirectly, how friendly a disposition he had always exercised towards the Corinthians, and with what sincerity and kindness he had judged of them."
2. "Here truly we have a signal evidence of a rightly constituted and candid mind — reproving to their face those that you love, and yet hoping well, and giving others good hopes respecting them."
3. "Such sincerity ought to have induced them not to take amiss any thing that proceeded from him."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "For if in any thing I have gloried to him on your behalf, I was not put to shame." 
2. "The event has justified to himself the high character I gave him of you."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Paul had assured Titus, who hesitated to go after the failure of Timothy, that the Corinthians were sound at bottom and would come round all right if handled properly." 
2. "He had not hesitated to speak plainly of their sins."

### John Gill:
1. "I am not ashamed; since these all appeared to be true; as he must have been had they been otherwise."
2. "Our preaching among you was true; all the doctrines we delivered to you were truth; our word was not yea and nay, but uniform, and all of a piece."
3. "Even so our boasting, which I made before Titus, is found a truth."
