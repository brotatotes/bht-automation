### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "his inward affection—literally, 'bowels' (compare 2 Corinthians 6:12; Philippians 1:8; Philippians 2:1; Colossians 3:12)." 
2. "obedience— (Colossians 3:12- :)." 
3. "fear and trembling—with trembling anxiety to obey my wishes, and fearful lest there should be aught in yourselves to offend him and me (Colossians 3:12- :; compare 1 Corinthians 2:3)."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "His affectionate regard for you has been greatly increased by his visit." 
2. "It denotes here: deep, tender attachment, or love."
3. "With fear of offending, and with deep apprehension of the consequences of remaining in sin."

### John Calvin:
1. "He wished...to express emphatically the idea, that while Titus had loved the Corinthians previously, he had been, at that time, more vehemently stirred up to love them; and that, from the innermost affections of his heart." 
2. "He at the same time encourages them to go on well, that they may render themselves beloved by all the good." 
3. "Whichever exposition you may choose, this passage teaches, what is a right reception for the ministers of Christ. Assuredly, it is not sumptuous banquets, it is not splendid apparel, it is not courteous and honorable salutations, it is not the plaudits of the multitude, that gratify the upright and faithful pastor... when the people give themselves up to his direction, to be regulated by his ministry under Christ’s banners."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "And his inward affection is more abundantly toward you, whilst he remembereth the obedience of you all, how with fear and trembling ye received him." 
2. "'How, instead of receiving him with cold suspicion, with a haughty disposition to justify yourselves and resent his interference, ye received him with a trembling anxiety to yield implicit obedience to whatever might be deemed necessary to repair the injury that had been done.'" 
3. "No wonder, then, that he adds, as a closing word,"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "A remarkable statement of the complete victory of Titus in spite of a stubborn minority still opposing Paul." 
2. "He had brought a stern message and they had trembled at the words of Titus." 
3. "Paul had himself come to the Corinthians at first with a nervous dread."

### John Gill:
1. "his inward affection is more abundant toward you"
2. "his bowels"
3. "denoting the tenderness of his heart, the strength of his affections"
4. "whilst he remembereth the obedience of you all"
5. "readily, cheerfully, and universally complied with"
6. "with fear and trembling you received him"
7. "with great humility and respect, with much deference to him"
8. "embraced him with great marks of honour and esteem"
