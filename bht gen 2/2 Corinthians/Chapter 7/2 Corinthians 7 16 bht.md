## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "I rejoice that in every thing I am (re)-assured by you." 
2. "The ordinary rendering, 'I can have confidence in you,' is wrong in not giving the indic. θαῤῥῶ, and still more, in making θαῤῥεῖν ἐν mean 'to have confidence in,' which is unexampled."
3. "The strongest reason against it is not mere want of usage, but the psychological meaning of θαῤῥεῖν, which is not like πεποιθέναι, descriptive of a relative, but of an absolute state of mind, to be of good courage."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The conclusion is more emphatical without it."
2. "That in everything I am of good courage concerning you."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Christians are bound by every solemn and sacred consideration to endeavor to purify themselves."
2. "Every true Christian will aim at perfection."
3. "No man can be a Christian who voluntarily indulges in sin, or in what he knows to be wrong."


### Marvin Vincent:
1. I have confidence in you [θαρρω εν υμιν] . Wrong. Rev., correctly, I am of good courage. In you expresses the ground of his encouragement as lying in them. 

### Philip Schaff:
1. 2 Corinthians 7:16.  I rejoice that in everything I am of good courage concerning you.
	

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  I am of good courage  (θαρρω). The outcome has brought joy, courage, and hope to Paul. 

### John Gill:
1. "I rejoice therefore that I have confidence in you in all things."
2. "That I can speak freely and boldly to you, reprove, admonish, and advise you, since you take it all in good part, as I design it."
3. "That I can confidently speak in your favour, boast of your love and obedience, which is found upon trial and by experience to be truth."
