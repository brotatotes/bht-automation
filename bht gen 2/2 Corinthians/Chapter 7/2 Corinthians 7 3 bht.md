# 2 Corinthians 7:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The writer expresses that their words are not intended to condemn, but rather to address false apostles. They emphasize that the Corinthians are important to them, engraved on their hearts as a cherished letter. The writer desires to live and die with them, showing a deep affection and willingness to sacrifice for their sake.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "I do not say it for condemnation...I do not say it in any but a loving spirit." 
2. "I have said before that you are in our hearts, to die together and live together." 
3. "If our lot is to die, in death, and if our lot is to live, in life, never to be torn from our hearts."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "I do not accuse you, as though you suspected me of such things."
2. "I speak only of the false apostles."
3. "I am ready to die and live with you and for you."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "I speak not this to condemn you - I do not speak this with any desire to reproach you."
2. "It is not because I have no love for you, and wish to have occasion to use words implying complaint and condemnation."
3. "That ye are in our hearts - That is, we are so much attached to you; or you have such a place in our affections."

### John Calvin:
1. "The Corinthians, it is true, were unkind, and they deserved that, on Paul's being acquitted from blame, they should be substituted in his place as the guilty party; nay more, that they should be held guilty in two respects - in respect of ingratitude, and on the ground of their having calumniated the innocent."
2. "Such, however, is the Apostle's moderation, that he refrains from recrimination, contenting himself with standing simply on the defensive."
3. "He undoubtedly in a manner condemns them, while he says that he does not do so."


### Philip Schaff:
1. "I say it not to condemn you... against yourselves I have nothing to say."
2. "I must repel it as a cruel wrong."
3. "Ye are in our hearts to die together and live together."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Not for condemnation."
2. "For the dying together and living together."
3. "You are in our hearts to share death and life."

### John Gill:
1. "My view is only to the false apostles, who have done these things, when we have not, and therefore we have the best claim to your affections."
2. "You are inscribed on our hearts, engraved there, 'ye are our epistle written in our hearts'."
3. "There is nothing we more desire than to live with you; and should there be any occasion for it, could freely die with you, and for you."

### John Wesley:
1. "I speak not to condemn you — Not as if I accused you of laying this to my charge."
2. "I am so far from thinking so unkindly of you, that ye are in our hearts, to live and die with you."
3. "That is, I could rejoice to spend all my days with you."
