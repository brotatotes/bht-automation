# 2 Corinthians 7:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
I am filled with comfort, not only from the consolations of God, but also from the news brought to me by Titus about the state of this church. This news greatly contributes to my inexpressible joy, despite the tribulations we encounter in preaching the Gospel of Christ.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "παρρησία, as in reff., confidence, which leads to and justifies καύχησις: not here liberty of speech,' as Chrys., al."
2. "τῇ παρ., the consolation (which I have received), viz. that furnished by the intelligence from you."
3. "ὑπερπ., I am made exceedingly to abound, see Matthew 13:12. The pres. indicates the abiding of the effect."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "boldness of speech—(compare 2 Corinthians 6:11)."
2. "filled with comfort—at the report of Titus."
3. "exceeding joyful—Greek, I overabound with joy."
4. "our tribulation—described in 2 Corinthians 7:5; also in 2 Corinthians 4:7; 2 Corinthians 4:8; 2 Corinthians 6:4; 2 Corinthians 6:5."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He speaks freely of their faults, and he speaks freely of his love to them; and he as frankly commends them and praises them. It is the open, undisguised language of a friend, when he throws open his whole soul and conceals nothing."
2. "He refers here to the fact that he had boasted of their liberality in regard to the proposed collection for the poor saints of Judea; that he had formerly boasted much of them to Titus, and of their readiness to obey his commands."
3. "I am filled with comfort... I am overjoyed... I have joy which cannot be expressed."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "My boldness. Note the change for the first time to the first person singular."
2. "The Greek has the comfort, the article apparently pointing to the special comfort he had received through the coming of Titus (ver. 6)."
3. "I am exceeding joyful [υπερπερισσευμαι τη χαρα] . Lit., I superabound with the joy."

### John Calvin:
1. "For the things that Titus has reported to me respecting you are not merely sufficient for quieting my mind, but afford me also ground of glorying confidently on your account."
2. "Nay more, they have effectually dispelled the grief, which many great and heavy afflictions had occasioned me."
3. "What a sudden change! what a wonderful transformation! Formerly we saw him like a soldier, wounded, weak, disabled, dispirited, fallen to the ground; now he is lifted up, victorious, and borne on the triumphant car."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Great is my boldness of speech toward you, great is my glorying on your behalf."
2. "I am filled with comfort, I overflow with joy in all our tribulations."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "I overflow with joy in all our affliction."
2. "A thoroughly Pauline sentiment."
3. "Hυπερ-περισσευω is to have a regular flood."

### John Gill:
1. "I very freely, and without any disguise, open my mind to you."
2. "I am filled with comfort, says he; not only with divine and spiritual consolations from God, but with the news Titus brought of the state of this church."
3. "Yea, it makes me inexpressibly joyful in all our tribulation; which is not small we meet with, wherever we go, in preaching the Gospel of Christ."

### John Wesley:
1. I am filled with comfort — Of this he treats, 2 Corinthians 7:6, etc.; of his joy, 2 Corinthians 7:7, etc.; of both, 2 Corinthians 7:13.
