# 2 Corinthians 7:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
It is evident that the Corinthians treated Titus with kindness and respect, satisfying him with their desire to make amends for their wrongdoings. Their faith and devotion to God brought joy and comfort not only to Titus but also to Paul. The joy and encouragement they provided overshadowed the troubles and afflictions that Paul was facing, extinguishing his sorrows. Their fervent love and sorrow for their actions demonstrated their strong commitment to rectifying their mistakes and honoring God.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "not only ... but also with the comfort with which he was comforted concerning you: i.e. ‘we shared in the comfort which Titus felt in recording to us your desire,’ &c."
2. "ἐπιπόθησιν, either longing to see me, or longing to fulfil my wishes. The former is the more simple."
3. "ζῆλον ὑπὲρ ἐμοῦ... cohere in the sense, and form as it were but one..."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "We shared in the comfort which Titus felt in
	recording your desire."
2. "He rejoiced in telling the news; we in hearing them."
3. "They in Paul's behalf showed the zeal against the sin which Paul would have shown had he been present."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He had been kindly treated, and he had seen all the effect produced by the letter which he had desired."
2. "The joy of one friend will diffuse itself through the heart of another. Joy is diffusive, and one Christian cannot well be happy without making others happy also."
3. "Your fervent mind toward me - It denotes that they evinced great ardor of attachment to him, and an earnest desire to comply with his wishes."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Comfort. The manner in which Paul, so to speak, fondles this word, is most beautiful."
2. "Mourning [οδυρμον] . Only here and Matthew 2:18. It implies a verbal expression of grief."
3. "Cebes, a disciple of Socrates, in his Pinax 147 represents Luph Lupe, Sorrow, as a woman, with her head bowed upon her breast; Odunh Odune, consuming Grief, follows, tearing her hair."

### John Calvin:
1. "It was not so much the presence of one individual, as the prosperous condition of the Corinthians, that had cheered him."
2. "Their desire originated in the circumstance, that they held Paul’s doctrine in high estimation. Their tears were a token of respect; because, being affected with his reproof, they mourned over their sins. Their zeal was an evidence of good will."
3. "Hence we see, not merely with what fervor of mind he desired the public good of the Church, but also how mild and gentle a disposition he possessed, as being one that could suddenly bury in oblivion offenses of so serious a nature."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The manner in which Paul, so to speak, fondles this word (παρακαλεω) is most beautiful."
2. "Wherewith"
3. "Result expressed by ωστε and the second aorist passive infinitive of χαιρω with accusative of general reference."

### John Gill:
1. "The church at Corinth received Titus with all respect and reverence; treated him in the most kind and tender manner; satisfied him in the affair of the incestuous person; greatly refreshed his spirits with an account of their faith in Christ, experience of the grace of God, and their regard and close attachment to the honour of religion, and the interest of the Redeemer."
2. "The joys and comforts of one believer yield a considerable pleasure, and are matter of joy and comfort, to another."
3. "His joy on this narrative of things abundantly exceeded his troubles and afflictions, which surrounded him on every side, and overcame and extinguished that sorrow, which had possessed him on their account."

### John Wesley:
1. Your earnest desire — To rectify what had been amiss.Your grief — For what had offended God, and troubled me.
