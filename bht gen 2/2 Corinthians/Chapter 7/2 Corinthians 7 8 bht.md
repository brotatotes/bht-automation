# 2 Corinthians 7:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Paul initially felt remorse for his previous letter that had caused sorrow among the Corinthians. However, upon observing the positive outcome and genuine sorrow it evoked, he no longer regrets it. He recognizes that the temporary sorrow he caused served a greater purpose and ultimately led to their spiritual growth.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "But there is no difficulty even on the strictest view of inspiration, in conceiving that the Apostle may have afterwards regretted the severity which he was guided to use."
2. "It appears then that he would render 'if even for a season,' = 'scarcely for any time.'"
3. "The best sense, as well as the most legitimate rendering, is to regard βλέπω .… ὑμᾶς as the epexegesis of ἐλύπησα, as above."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "I do not regret it, though I did regret it."
2. "Paul was almost regretting, through parental tenderness, his having used rebukes calculated to grieve the Corinthians; but now that he has learned from Titus the salutary effect produced on them, he no longer regrets it."
3. "for I perceive that that Epistle did make you sorry, though it was but for a season."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "I have seen such happy effects produced by it; it has so completely answered the end which I had in view; it was so kindly received, that I do not regret now that I wrote it."
2. "It gives me no pain in the recollection, but I have occasion to rejoice that it was done."
3. "I perceive that it produced the kind of sorrow in you which I desired. I see that it has produced permanent good results. The sorrow which it caused in you is only for a season; the good effects will be abiding."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Repent [μεταμελομαι]. See on Matthew 21:29. Rev., regret it."
2. "Punctuate as Am. Rev., I do not regret it: though (even if) I did regret it (for I see that that epistle made you sorry, though but for a season) I now rejoice."


### John Calvin:
1. "Your welfare is so much an object of desire to me, that I am delighted to see that I have done you good." 
2. "I am grieved no longer on that account when I see that it has been of advantage to you."
3. "He preferred duty to inclination."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "I do not regret it, though I did regret it, a strong expression of the pain it gave him." 
2. "Haunted with the impression that he had expressed himself too strongly."
3. "He was now glad to find was not the case."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Paul is now glad that he made them sorry."
2. "I was in a regretful mood at first."
3. "A parenthetical explanation of his present joy in their sorrow."

### John Gill:
1. "I do not repent, though I did repent; not of writing the letter, which was wrote by divine inspiration; but of the sorrow occasioned by it, though now he did not repent of that."
2. "For I perceive that the same epistle made you sorry, though it were but for a season; inasmuch as the sorrow was true, hearty, and genuine, though it was but for a time, the apostle was entirely satisfied, and the more pleased, because of its brevity, since it was sincere."

### John Wesley:
1. I did repent — That is, I felt a tender sorrow for having grieved you, till I saw the happy effect of it.
