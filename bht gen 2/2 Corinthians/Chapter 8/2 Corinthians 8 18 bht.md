# 2 Corinthians 8:18 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In 2 Corinthians 8:18, along with the apostle and Timothy, they sent a brother who is widely recognized and highly praised by all the churches. This unnamed brother, who may be Luke, has earned a commendable reputation for faithfully proclaiming the gospel. His commitment to the ministry has gained him the admiration and approval of believers in various congregations.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The delicate nature of the mission would require that there should be at least no family connexion between those sent to fulfil it."
2. "Who this was, we know not."
3. "It may have been Trophimus, who accompanied Paul into Asia and to Jerusalem."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "whose praise is known in connection with the Gospel: Luke may be meant."
2. "Luke was the most prominent, having been his companion in preaching the Gospel at his first entrance into Europe."
3. "He who is faithful in the Gospel will be faithful also in matters of inferior importance."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Whoever it was, it was someone well known, in whom the church at Corinth could have entire confidence."
2. "It is remarkable that though Paul mentions him again 2 Corinthians 12:18, he does it also in the same manner, without specifying his name."
3. "Whose praise - Who is well known and highly esteemed. Is in the gospel - Either for writing the gospel, or for preaching the gospel. The Greek will bear either construction. In some way he was celebrated for making known the truths of the gospel."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The person referred to has been variously identified with Titus' brother, Barnabas, Mark, Luke, and Epaenetus, mentioned in Romans 16:5."
2. "The reference to Epaenetus has been urged on the ground of a supposed play upon the word praise, epainos; Epaenetus meaning praiseworthy."
3. "The parallel is cited in the case of Onesimus profitable, of whom Paul says that he will henceforth be useful, Philippians 1:11."

### John Calvin:
1. "The circumstance that three persons are sent is an evidence that great expectations were entertained, and it became them to be so much the more attentive to duty, that they might not disappoint the hopes of the Churches."
2. "He honors [the second person] with a signal commendation, that he had conducted himself as to the gospel in a praiseworthy manner, that is, he had earned applause by promoting the gospel."
3. "He had received praise, not from one individual, or even from one Church merely, but from all the Churches."
