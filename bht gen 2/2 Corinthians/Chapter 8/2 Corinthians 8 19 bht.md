# 2 Corinthians 8:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The appointed brother, esteemed for his preaching and writing, was chosen by the churches to convey the collection out of love and for the glory of the Lord. This appointment aimed to promote the influence of religion in producing true benevolence and to provide an opportunity to showcase their readiness to do good and promote the welfare of others. It was a joint decision by the churches to entrust this brother as a faithful messenger of their generous bounty.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "parenthetical (see on 2Co 8:20), adding to his general commendation a particular qualification for this office."
2. "and not only so (i.e. praised in all the churches), but who was also appointed (‘suffragiis designatus,’ see ref. and note; and Stanley here) by the churches (of Macedonia? see 2Co 8:1) as our fellow-traveller (to Jerusalem, from what follows) in (the matter of) this charity which is being ministered by us, in order to subserve the glory of the Lord and our readiness (this clause refers not to διακον. ὑφʼ ἡμ. as usually interpreted, but to the fact related, the union of this brother with Paul in the matter of the alms, which was done to avoid suspicions detrimental to Christ’s glory, and to the zeal of the Apostle)."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "These companions of Paul are called 'messengers of the churches' (2 Corinthians 8:23)." 
2. "The union of the brother with Paul in this affair of the collection was done to guard against suspicions injurious 'to the glory' of the Lord." 
3. "It was also done in order to produce a 'readiness' on the part of Paul and the brother to undertake the office which each, by himself, would have been less ready to undertake."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Not only is he esteemed on account of other services which he has rendered by his preaching and writings; but he has had a new mark of the confidence of the churches in being appointed to convey the collection to Jerusalem." 
2. "The design was to promote the glory of the Lord by showing the influence of religion in producing true benevolence."
3. "To afford you an opportunity of evincing your readiness to do good to others, and to promote their welfare."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The reference is, of course, to the contribution as a work of love; cariv being used in the sense of benefaction or bounty."
2. "Paul says that the brother was appointed as his fellow - traveller in the matter of this bounty; in the prosecution of this kindly act."
3. "For appointed, see on Acts 14:23; Acts 10:41."

### John Calvin:
1. "He says, that it tends to promote the glory of God, and their kindness of disposition."
2. "Hence it comes, that these two things are conjoined — the glory of God and their liberality."
3. "There is, in addition to this, the labor of those distinguished men, which it were very inconsistent to reject, or allow to pass unimproved."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Who was also appointed by the churches to travel...in the matter of this grace." 
2. "Ministered by us to the glory of the Lord." 
3. "To show our readiness."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The first aorist passive participle χειροτονηθεις is from χειροτονεω, old verb to stretch out the hands (χειρ τεινω) and so to vote in public."
2. "The idea is that this brother was chosen by the churches, not by Paul."
3. "Late word for travelling companion. So in the inscriptions (συν, together with, εκδημος, away from home)."

### John Gill:
1. "Not only famous for preaching the Gospel, but he was also appointed by the joint suffrages of the churches."
2. "This brother had the honour to be chosen by these churches, to travel...with this grace; bounty or beneficence of the churches, what they had freely and liberally contributed for the supply of the poor, from a principle of grace, and by the assistance of it."
3. "And also to the declaration of your ready mind; or to stir up their ready mind to increase it and promote it in them, as well to make it manifest, and that it might appear to others, how readily and freely they came into this service."

### John Wesley:
1. "He was appointed by the churches - Of Macedonia."
2. "With this gift - Which they were carrying from Macedonia to Jerusalem."
3. "For the declaration of our ready mind - That of Paul and his fellow-traveller, ready to be the servants of all."
