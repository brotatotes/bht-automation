# 2 Corinthians 8:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The believers in question were truly blessed, excelling in various areas of the Christian faith. They possessed an abundance of knowledge, zeal, and gratitude towards God. Furthermore, their faith was evident and strong, as they continued to grow in it and consistently exercised it. Because of these spiritual riches, the apostle encouraged them to extend their generosity towards the needy, urging them to also excel in the grace of giving.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The love which, arising from you, has us for its object."
2. "The sense is imperative, κελεύω, or βούλομαι, being omitted."
3. "'This grace also; other graces having been enumerated.'"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "in faith—"
2. "utterance—"
3. "knowledge—"
4. "diligence—"
5. "your love to us—"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Paul never hesitated to commend Christians where it could be done with truth; and the fact that they were eminent in some of the Christian duties and graces, he makes the ground of the exhortation that they would abound in all."
2. "From those who had so many eminent characteristics of true religion he had a right to expect much; and he therefore exhorts them to manifest a symmetry of Christian character."
3. "The idea here is, that eminence in spiritual endowments of any kind, or in any of the traits of the Christian character should lead to great benevolence, and that the character is not complete unless benevolence be manifested toward every good object that may be presented."

### John Calvin:
1. "You ought not merely to associate yourselves as partners with the Macedonians, who require that; but surpass them in this respect, too, as you do in others." 
2. "He makes a distinction between utterance and faith, because it. is impossible that any one should have faith, and that, too, in an eminent degree, without being at the same time much exercised in the word of God."
3. "He makes mention of their love to himself, that he may encourage them also from regard to himself personally, and in the mean time he gives up, with a view to the public advantage of the brethren, the personal affection with which they regarded him."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "As ye abound in every thing,...and in all earnestness...see that ye abound in this grace also."
2. "Faith, utterance, and knowledge...and in your love to us."
3. "Carrying out his directions in the painful case of the incestuous person...was specially in view."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  In this grace also  (κα εν ταυτη τη χαριτ). This gifted church (2 Corinthians 8:1) had fallen behind in the grace of giving. Kindly irony in this allusion. 

### John Gill:
1. "They were enriched in everything, and wanted nothing... they abounded in the knowledge of it, zeal for it, and thankfulness to God on account of it." 
2. "They abounded in the grace of faith... inasmuch as they had a large measure of it, grew in it, were full of it, and were much in the exercise thereof."
3. "And since they were so eminent for these graces, and the exercise of them, the apostle exhorts them to liberality to the poor saints; see that ye abound in this grace also."
