# 2 Corinthians 9:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Despite the Corinthians' poor performance, Paul still takes pride in their readiness. Their cheerful disposition and eagerness to start the collection has stirred up a zeal in others and has caused Paul to boast about them to the Macedonians.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "I am in the habit of boasting concerning you to Macedonians." 
2. "Achaia has been ready from last year."
3. "The zeal which proceeds from you stirred up the greater number of them."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Your zeal—Greek, 'the zeal from you,' that is, on your part; propagated from you to others."
2. "Provoked—that is, stimulated."
3. "Very many—Greek, 'the greater number,' namely, of the Macedonians."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "I know your promptitude, or your readiness to do it."
2. "So well assured was he that the church at Corinth would make the collection as it had proposed, that he boasted of it to the churches of Macedonia as if it were already done."
3. "The readiness with which you entered into this subject, and your desire to relieve the needs of others."

### John Calvin:
1. "He shows the good opinion that he had of them from this, that he had, in a manner, stood forward as their surety by asserting their readiness."
2. "His words are not to be understood as though Paul had declared, that what they were to give was already laid aside in the chest, but he simply mentioned what had been resolved upon among them."
3. "It was, then, of this promise that Paul spoke."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "I know your readiness, of which I glory on your behalf..."
2. "...that Achaia the whole province, but especially Corinth, the central church, hath been prepared for a year past..."
3. "...and your zeal has stirred up very many of them."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "I still am glorying, in spite of the poor performance of the Corinthians."
2. "Stands prepared."
3. "To excite in a good sense here."

### John Gill:
1. "How readily they came into it; what a cheerful disposition of mind they showed towards it; and how forward they were to begin the collection."
2. "They had shown a readiness of mind, as to every good work, so to this of communicating to the saints a year ago, when they made a beginning, though as yet had not finished."
3. "That emulation which was stirred up in some of the leading persons among the Macedonians, upon hearing how ready they at Corinth were to minister to the necessitous brethren; and the zeal which appeared in these principal men, which was very warm, and yet prudent and seasonable, wrought very much on the minds of others..."

### John Wesley:
1. I boast to them of Macedonia — With whom he then was.
