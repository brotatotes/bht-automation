The apostle writes to a person of influence, warning about deceivers who seek to lead her and her family astray. Christian love is grounded in Christian truth, and all believers are part of one fellowship, rejoicing in each other's spiritual growth. The apostle commends the elect lady as chosen by God, emphasizing the importance of both her and her children. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Being a person of influence, 'deceivers' were insinuating themselves into her family to seduce her and her children from the faith."
2. "Christian love rests on the Christian truth."
3. "All Christians form one fellowship, rejoicing in the spiritual prosperity of one another."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He addresses her as one chosen of God to salvation, in the use of a term often applied to Christians in the New Testament."
2. "The word here rendered 'children' (τέκνοις teknois) would include in itself both sons and daughters."
3. "The commendation of the apostle here shows that it is possible that a family shall be extensively known as one of order, peace, and religion, so that all who know it or hear of it shall regard it with interest, respect, and love."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The word ['elder'] is used originally of seniority in age." 
2. "It is supposed by some that the title describes a person, by others, a society."
3. "The explanation turns on the meaning of ['elect lady']."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "embracing the mother and the children of both sexes" (Vincent).
2. "in truth, in the highest sphere" (Brooke).
3. "those that have come to know and still know." (γινωσκω)

### John Gill:
1. "The elect lady is the person he writes unto; by whom is designed not the church of Christ, since such a way of speaking is unusual; and besides, he speaks of coming to see her face to face, and of the children of her elect sister."
2. "she is called a 'lady', because she was a person of distinction and substance, which shows that God sometimes calls by his grace some that are rich and noble; and also that titles of respect and honour, where flattery is avoided, may be lawfully given to persons of dignity and wealth."
3. "the apostle loved this lady and her children sincerely and heartily, without dissimulation; not in word and in tongue, but in deed and in truth."

### John Wesley:
1. "The elder - An appellation suited to a familiar letter, but upon a weighty subject."
2. "Whom - Both her and her children."
3. "I love in the truth - With unfeigned and holy love."
