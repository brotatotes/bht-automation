# 2 John 6 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This is the essence of love, that we live in accordance with God's commandments. It is through our obedience to these commandments that we demonstrate our love for Him. The commandment, which we have heard from the beginning, is that we should walk in love. This means that our lives should be characterized by love, following the example of Christ who loved us sacrificially.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "And this is love, that we walk after his commandments - This is the proper expression or evidence of love to God." 
2. "This is the commandment - That is, this is his great and special commandment; the one by which his disciples are to be especially characterized, and by which they are to be distinguished in the world."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Love [η αγαπη]. The love just mentioned in the verb we love."
2. "After His commandments [κατα τας εντολας αυτου]."
3. "From the beginning [απ αρχης]."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Here we have once more St. John’s familiar tribute to the ethical supremacy of love..."
2. "The verse is remarkable for its circular argument: love is the walking in all the commandments, the strength to keep them all being in love, and love being their compendium..."
3. "...the one commandment heard from the beginning is ‘that ye should walk in it,’ that is, in love."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The love just mentioned."
2. "That we should keep on walking."
3. "Either to truth, love, or the commandment."

### John Gill:
1. "And this is love, that we walk after his commandments,.... By observing them as a rule of conversation, in so doing love is shown to God; and such may expect the fresh discoveries of the love of God to them;"
2. "This is the commandment, that as ye have heard from the beginning, ye should walk in it; that is, this is the will of God, that his people should walk in the truth of the Gospel, and abide by it, as they heard and received it at their first conversion, from the mouths of the apostles, or other faithful ministers of the word;"

### John Wesley:
1. And this is the proof of true love, universal obedience built on the love of God.This — Love. Is the great commandment which ye have heard from the beginning - Of our preaching.
