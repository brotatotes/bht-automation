# 2 John 8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Beware of deception and false doctrines, as they can cause us to lose the fruits of our labor. It is important to be vigilant and ensure that our works are built on a strong foundation, so that even though we may be saved, our works will not be destroyed. The reward for our faithfulness is a gift of grace from God, and we must fully utilize our talents to receive the full reward that He has promised. Let us follow the Apostle's example in humility and devotion.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Look to yourselves...that ye lose not the things which we wrought."
2. "Ye, our converts, are our μισθός in the day of the Lord."
3. "The reward laid up for faithfulness, and for everything done in His name, must be understood, which is reckoned of grace, and not of debt."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Look to yourselves amidst the widespread prevalence of deception so many being led astray." 
2. "Look that ye lose not the believing state of 'truth and love,' which we were the instruments of working in you." 
3. "There are degrees of heavenly reward proportioned to the degrees of capability of receiving heavenly blessedness."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "When error abounds in the world, our first duty is not to attack it and make war upon it; it is to look to the citadel of our own souls, and see that all is well guarded there."
2. "The first thing for a Christian to do is to take care of his own heart.That we lose not those things which we have wrought."
3. "The truth which is taught here is one of interest to all Christians - that it is possible for even genuine Christians, by suffering themselves to be led into error, or by failure in duty, to lose a part of the reward which they might have obtained."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Ina in order that, marks the intent of the caution." 
2. "The compounded preposition ajpo, has the force of back: receive back from God." 
3. "Reward [μισθον]."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Look to yourselves: a rare expression, intimating the deep earnestness of the warning."
2. "The reward of Christian labour is a familiar idea in the New Testament."
3. "There is a beautiful contrast in the original words: ‘See that ye let not slip all the fruits of our teaching, and all the benefits of your Christian discipline, in the present world; see that hereafter ye be found worthy of the completed rewards of Christian fidelity."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Look to yourselves (βλεπετε εαυτους). The verb often used absolutely (Philippians 3:2) like our 'look out.'"
2. "John does not wish his labour to be lost."
3. "The 'full reward' (μισθον πληρη) is the full day's wages which each worker will get (1 Corinthians 3:8)."

### John Gill:
1. "Beware of these deceivers, and their doctrines."
2. "Take heed what they build upon it, since, though they themselves may be saved, their works may be destroyed, and so they suffer loss."
3. "This is a reward of grace, God has of his own grace promised it to those who love and serve him."

### John Wesley:
1. "That we lose not the things which we have wrought — Which every apostate does."
2. "But receive a full reward — Having fully employed all our talents to the glory of him that gave them."
3. "Here again the apostle modestly transfers it to himself."
