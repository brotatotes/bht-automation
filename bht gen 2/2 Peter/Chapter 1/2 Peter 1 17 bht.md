# 2 Peter 1:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
He received divine honor and inexpressible glory. Shining from heaven above the brightness of the sun, a voice came to testify of his greatness. This voice, heard on the mount of transfiguration, proclaimed the words, This is my beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased. This declaration came from God the Father, revealing the significance and approval of Christ as the Son of God.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "For (justification of the above assertion that we were admitted witnesses of His majesty) having received...from God the Father...honor and glory."
2. "This is my beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased."
3. "εἰς ὅν is a pregnant construction, 'on whom my pleasure has lighted and abides.' εὐδόκησα, aor., but only to be given in Eng. by the present. If an account is to be given of the aoristic sense, it must be 'my pleasure rested from eternity'."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "received . . . honour—in the voice that spake to Him. glory—in the light which shone around Him."
2. "in whom—Greek, 'in regard to whom' (accusative case); but Psalms 21:5- :, 'in whom' (dative case) centers and rests My good pleasure."
3. "I am—Greek aorist, past time, 'My good pleasure rested from eternity.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He was honored by God in being thus addressed."
2. "The magnificent splendor; the bright cloud which overshadowed them."
3. "This demonstrated that he was the Messiah."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "When there came [ενεχθεισης] . Lit., having been born. Compare come (Rev., ver. 18); moved (ver. 21); and rushing wind, lit., a wind born along (Acts 2:2)."
2. "From [υπο] . Lit., by."
3. "Excellent [μεγαλοπρεπους] . Or sublime. Only here in New Testament. In Septuagint (Deuteronomy 33:26), as an epithet of God, excellency. The phrase excellent glory refers to the bright cloud which overshadowed the company on the transfiguration mount, like the shekinah above the mercy - seat."

### John Calvin:
1. "For he received from God the Father...a celestial majesty shone on every side." 
2. "...the authority of that voice which came might be more awful and solemn."
3. "When Christ is acknowledged by us to be him whom the Father has sent, this is our highest wisdom."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The thing dwelt on is not the splendour of Christ’s own appearance on the occasion, but the tribute which came by the voice."
2. "The voice is called ‘such a voice,’ that is to say, ‘such as I am now to record,’ or perhaps ‘a voice so wonderful in kind.’"
3. "Many of the best recent interpreters regard the words as a designation of God, and translate them ‘by the sublime majesty.’"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "For he received..."
2. "There came such a voice to him..."
3. "From the excellent glory..."

### John Gill:
1. "God the Father testifying of his glory, declaring the honour that belonged to him, as the Son of God, at the same time that an external glory was put on him, and received by him, as the son of man."
2. "This voice was not that at his baptism...nor that of which mention is made in John 12:28...but that voice which came from the cloud, when Christ was transfigured on the mount, and which was heard by his three disciples...": John 12:28-.
3. "[The voice] proclaimed the following words... 'this is my beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased'": John 12:28-.

### John Wesley:
1. "He received divine honour and inexpressible glory."
2. "Shining from heaven above the brightness of the sun."
3. "When there came such a voice from the excellent glory — That is, from God the Father."
