# 2 Peter 2:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Righteous Lot, who was justified by the righteousness of Christ, lived a godly life amidst the ungodliness of the inhabitants of Sodom. Their filthy and lawless behavior was a heavy burden to him. The sins of others, whether believers or unbelievers, weighed on his soul and caused him great distress. Despite this, Lot remained faithful to God, demonstrating the challenge and the importance of living righteously in a sinful world.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The deliverance of the righteous is here brought out at more length."
2. "The righteous man, dwelling among them, day by day tormented his righteous soul with their lawless deeds."
3. "It was by his own sight and hearing of what went on around him, that he distressed himself."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Just—righteous. filthy conversation—behavior in licentiousness" 
2. "The wicked—lawless: who set at defiance the laws of nature, as well as man and God."
3. "The Lord reminds us of Lot's faithfulness, but not of his sin in the cave: so in Rahab's case."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "God makes a distinction between the righteous and the wicked; and while the latter will be destroyed, the former will be saved." (Genesis 19:16)
2. "Lot is called 'just,' because he preserved himself uncontaminated amidst the surrounding wickedness." (Genesis 19:16)
3. "The corruption of Sodom was open and shameless; and as Lot was compelled to see much of it, his heart was pained." (Genesis 19:16)

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Vexed [καταπονουμενον] . Only here and Acts 7:24. Kata gives the force of worn down. So Rev., sore distressed." 
2. "With the filthy conversation of the wicked [υπο της των αθεσμων εν ασελγεια αναστροφης] . Lit., by the behavior of the lawless in wantonness. Rev., the lascivious life of the wicked."
3. "Wicked [αθεσμων] , lit., lawless. Only here and ch. 3 17. Wantonness [ασελγεια] , see on Mark 7:22."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The verb...has the stronger sense of 'sore distressed' (as the R. V. puts it), or 'worn down,' occurs only once again..."
2. "The adjective...has the more definite sense of 'lawless,' occurs only once again..."
3. "Jude omits this notice of the deliverance of Lot, which in Peter serves to throw into still stronger relief the unerring penal judgment of God, but also to prepare the way for the assertion of God’s knowledge of how to 'deliver the godly out of temptation.'"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Righteous Lot" (δικαιον Λοτ). 
2. "Sore distressed" (καταπονουμενον). 
3. "By the lascivious life of the wicked" (υπο της των αθεσμων εν ασελγεια αναστροφης).

### John Gill:
1. "Who was a just man, being justified by the righteousness of Christ imputed to him; and having the new man formed in him, which is created in righteousness and true holiness; and living soberly, righteously, and godly, though not without sin."
2. "The inhabitants of Sodom, who had no regard to the laws of God or man, or to the law and light of nature; but as worse than brute beasts, lived daily in the commission of unnatural lusts; and therefore their conversation is rightly said to be filthy, and was a grievous burden to righteous Lot."
3. "For to a good man, not only his own sins, but the sins of others, whether professors or profane, are a burden, and make him groan under them, being grievously fatigued with them, as this good man was, and weary of life because of them."
