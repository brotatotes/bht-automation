# 2 Peter 2:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Lord's knowledge surpasses human comprehension as He is able to deliver the godly from all temptations. He provides His presence, support, sanctification, and eventual deliverance. In His justice, He reserves the souls of the ungodly until the day of judgment, where He will punish them according to their deeds.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "the Lord knoweth how to rescue godly (men) out of temptation"
2. "to reserve unrighteous (men) under punishment to the day of judgment"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "He is at no loss for means, even when men see no escape."
2. "Sin is already its own penalty; hell will be its full development."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The Lord knoweth... He sees a way to do it when we cannot, though it is often a way which we should not have thought of."
2. "He can send an angel to take his tempted people by the hand; he can interpose and destroy the power of the tempter; he can raise up earthly friends; he can deliver his people completely and forever from temptation, by their removal to heaven."
3. "The case of the angels shows that God can keep wicked men, as if under bonds, reserved for their final trial at his bar."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Godly. Used by Peter only."
2. "The reading at Acts 22:12, is eujlabhv, devout."
3. "The participle here is, lit., being punished, and therefore the A. V. is wrong."

### John Calvin:
1. "he testifies that the Lord knows when it is expedient to deliver the godly from temptation."
2. "But when God claims to himself the office of helping, and protecting his own, that they may not fail in the contest, we gather courage to fight more strenuously."
3. "Peter reminds us that a day of judgment has been appointed by the Lord; and that, therefore, the wicked shall by no means escape punishment, though it be not immediately inflicted."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The knowledge which is here in view is the Divine type of knowledge, which means both the perception of the way and the possession of the ability."
2. "The unrighteous are sustaining now a certain measure of punishment, in the state in which they are held in reserve for the final judgment of the great day."
3. "All that is meant, however, may be a general mention of authority as such, and of the contempt of that, in all its forms, human, angelic, and Divine, as a characteristic mark of the class dealt with."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "God can deliver his servants as shown by Noah and Lot and he will deliver you." (οιδεν κυριος)
2. "The idiomatic use of οιδα and the infinitive (ρυεσθα present middle and see verse 2 Peter 2:7) for knowing how as in Matthew 7:11; James 4:17." (οιδεν κυριος)
3. "To keep (τηρειν). Present active infinitive of τηρεω after οιδεν." (τηρειν)

### John Gill:
1. "The Lord knoweth how to deliver the godly" 
2. "He grants his presence in [afflictions]; he supports under them; he sanctifies them to them, and in his own time delivers out of them" 
3. "The Lord...knows how to reserve 'in prison'...the souls of those in hell...untou the day of judgment"

### John Wesley:
1. "The Lord knoweth, hath both wisdom and power and will."
2. "Deliver the godly out of all temptations."
3. "Punish the ungodly."
