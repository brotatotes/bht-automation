# 2 Peter 3:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The importance of remembering the words spoken by the holy prophets is emphasized, as these predictions provided insight into the character and work of the Savior before His advent. The Old Testament contains significant teachings about the kingdom of the Messiah, further emphasizing the relevance of both the prophets and the apostles in delivering these doctrines. The commandment of the apostles encompasses the entirety of their instruction, emphasizing the ethical aspects of the Gospel. This reminder is given to combat false teachings and to encourage the faithful to hold steadfastly to the teachings of the prophets and the apostles.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "that ye should remember...the words spoken before by the holy prophets" 
2. "the commandment of the Lord and Saviour given by your apostles" 
3. "τῶν ἀποστόλων ὑμῶν meaning 'your Apostles' as we call St. Paul ἀπόστολον ἐθνῶν"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "And of the commandment of the Lord and Saviour (declared) by YOUR apostles."
2. "The apostles who live among you in the present time, in contrast to the Old Testament 'prophets.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The predictions of the prophets before the advent of the Saviour, respecting his character and work."
2. "Many of the most important doctrines respecting the kingdom of the Messiah are stated as clearly in the Old Testament as in the New Testament."
3. "No man would have used this language who did not regard himself and his fellow apostles as inspired, and as on a level with the prophets."

### John Calvin:
1. "We have enough in the writings of the prophets, and in the gospel, to stir us up, provided we be as diligent as it behoves us, in meditating on them."
2. "When we have the prophets and apostles agreeing with us, nay, as the ministers of our faith, and God as the author, and angels as approvers, there is no reason that the ungodly, all united, should move us from our position."
3. "By the commandment of the apostles he means the whole doctrine in which they had instructed the faithful."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The importance of the testimony of prophecy is again pressed...specially those sections of it which spoke of the Advent of Messiah." 
2. "We thus get a sentence which is variously translated... 'your commandment, which the apostles, nay, the Lord Himself, gave.'"
3. "The 'commandment' means here neither the Gospel generally...far less the preaching of the prophecies as a charge committed to the apostles. It has substantially the sense which it had in Revelation 2:21, the new evangelical law of life, or the Gospel on its ethical side."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Purpose (indirect command) is here expressed by this infinitive."
2. "Spoken before (προειρημενων). Perfect passive participle of προειπον (defective verb)."
3. "The meaning is that they should remember the teaching of their apostles and not follow the Gnostic libertines."

### John Gill:
1. "The prophets of the Old Testament, who were holy men of God, and therefore their words are to be regarded, and retained in memory."
2. "The Gospel itself was spoken by them, and so was Christ, and the things relating to his person and offices."
3. "Particularly many things were said by them concerning the second coming of Christ to judge the world, and destroy it, and to prepare new heavens and a new earth for his people."
