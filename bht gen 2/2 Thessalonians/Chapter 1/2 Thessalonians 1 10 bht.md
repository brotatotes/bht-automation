# 2 Thessalonians 1:10 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
When Christ comes again, his glory will be revealed in his saints. The redeemed believers who have been saved by his grace will shine with the radiance of Christ's glory and reflect his holiness. This will be a testament to the power and transforming work of God in their lives. Through their faith in Christ, they will be a living testimony of his grace and mercy, bringing glory to his name. Their lives will display the wonders of his salvation and cause others to marvel at the greatness of God.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Glorified in His saints" reminds us that holiness is glory in the bud; glory is holiness manifested.
2. "Once they believed, now they see: they had taken His word on trust. Now His word is made good and they need faith no longer."
3. "The early preaching of the Gospel was not abstract discussions, but a testimony to facts and truths experimentally known. Faith is defined as "an assent unto truths, credible upon the testimony of God, delivered unto us by the apostles and prophets."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "His chief glory as seen in that day will be connected with the fact that he has redeemed his people."
2. "He will be 'glorified' then by the numbers that shall have been redeemed; by their patience in the trials through which they have passed; by the triumphs which religion shall have made on the earth; by their praises and songs, and by their ascent with him to the realms of blessedness."
3. "The honors of this world have been withheld from them. The great have regarded it as no honor to be identified with the church, and the proud have been ashamed to be enrolled among the followers of the Lamb. In the last day all this will be changed, and the assembled church will show to admiring worlds how great and glorious is its Redeemer, and how glorious was the work of redemption."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. To be glorified [ενδοξασθηναι] . Only here and ver. 12 in N. T. Repeatedly in LXX See Exodus 14:4, Exodus 14:17; Isaiah 45:26. oClass.

### John Calvin:
1. "Christ will come, that he may be glorified in them; that is, that he may irradiate them with his glory, and that they may be partakers of it."
2. "The crowning and choice consolation of the pious, that when the Son of God will be manifested in the glory of his kingdom, he will gather them into the same fellowship with himself."
3. "Let us learn, therefore, that the promises of God are ratified in us, when they gain credit with us."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Christ will be glorified in His saints when His power and goodness become apparent through them."
2. "Christ will be admired or wondered at, because of those who have believed in Him."
3. "The person who has been able to evoke from characters so various a faith which no worldly force has been able to subdue, cannot but be an object of amazement to all who witness this faith."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "When he shall come...to be glorified...in his saints." 
2. "To be marvelled at...that believed." 
3. "The day of Christ's coming."

### John Gill:
1. "He will appear glorious to them; he will come in his own glory both as God and man; and in his Father's glory, authority, and majesty, conferred on him as the Judge of the whole earth; and with the glory of his angels, who will accompany him."
2. "Their souls will be endowed with perfect knowledge and holiness; and their bodies will be made like to his glorious body; and both will shine like the sun in the kingdom of their Father."
3. "He is admired by them now; he is with them the chiefest among ten thousands, and altogether lovely."

### John Wesley:
1. To be glorified in his saints — For the wonderful glory of Christ shall shine in them.
