# 2 Thessalonians 1:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The name of our Lord Jesus Christ is more than just a title; it encompasses His whole person and His role as the God-man. Our actions and the way we live our lives should bring honor and glory to Jesus. As believers, our graces and virtues reflect Christ's glory, and in turn, His glory shines upon us. There is an inseparable unity between God and Jesus, and it is through this unity that we can experience the glory of His name.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "the name of our Lord Jesus—Our Lord Jesus in His manifested personality as the God-man."
2. "The believer's graces redound to Christ's glory, and His glory, as their Head, reflects glory on them as the members."
3. "There is but one Greek article to both, implying the inseparable unity of God and the Lord Jesus."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "That the name of our Lord Jesus Christ - That is, that the Lord Jesus himself may be honored among you; the name often denoting the person."
2. "It will greatly aggravate the punishment of the wicked that it will be 'a righteous thing' for God thus to punish them."
3. "If it be a 'righteous thing' that the wicked shall be punished, then they never can be saved by mere justice."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Paul follows O. T. usage, according to which the name of the Lord is often used for all that the name covers; so that the name of the Lord = the Lord himself."
2. "The deepest significance of the life beyond time lies, not in endlessness, but in the moral quality of the eon into which the life passes."
3. "In this passage the word destruction is qualified. It is 'destruction from the presence of the Lord and from the glory of his power,' at his second coming, in the new eon."

### John Calvin:
1. "He calls us back to the chief end of our whole life — that we may promote the Lord’s glory."
2. "This, however, is a twofold miracle, that he afterwards irradiates us with his glory, as though he would do the same to us in return."
3. "For there is nothing here that is ours either in the action itself, or in the effect or fruit, for it is solely by the guidance of the Holy Spirit that our life is made to contribute to the glory of God."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The name of our Lord Jesus Christ may be glorified in you."
2. "Christ may be glorified in us by obedience and patience."
3. "He is glorified now in all who manifest how firmly they believe His word, how highly they esteem the holiness He exemplified, how profoundly they love Him."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The Name of our Lord Jesus means the Messiahship and Lordship of Jesus."
2. "This reciprocal glorying is Pauline, but it is also like Christ's figure of the vine and the branches in John 15:1-11."
3. "Here strict syntax requires, since there is only one article with θεου and κυριου that one person be meant, Jesus Christ."

### John Gill:
1. "Christ and his Gospel are dishonoured...so they are glorified in and by the saints" 
2. "Christ will be glorified in them hereafter...for should not these be completed, Christ would lose the glory of redemption and salvation" 
3. "the saints are now glorified in him as their head and representative...and when the work of grace is finished upon their souls, they will be glorified together with him and by him"

### John Wesley:
1. That the name — The love and power of our Lord may be glorified - Gloriously displayed in you.
