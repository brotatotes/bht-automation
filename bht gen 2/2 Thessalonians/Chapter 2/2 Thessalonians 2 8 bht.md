# 2 Thessalonians 2:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The lawless one, who disregards both divine and human laws, will be exposed when the restraining force is removed, allowing him to carry out his wicked schemes. Christ, in His powerful presence, will swiftly bring about the downfall of this enemy, obliterating him with just a breath. This ultimate destruction of the lawless one signifies the triumph of true doctrine and the invincible victory of Christ over the forces of evil.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Shall be revealed the lawless one ... whom the Lord Jesus will destroy by the breath of His mouth." 
2. "The mere outburst of His presence shall bring the adversary to naught."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "How awful that He whose very name means God-Saviour, should appear as the Destroyer."
2. "The salvation of the Church requires the destruction of her foe."
3. "The first outburst of His advent—the first gleam of His presence—is enough to abolish utterly all traces of Antichrist, as darkness disappears before the dawning day."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The methods by which this will be done are immediately specified - and it is of much importance to understand them..." 
2. "The reference in the passage before us is to one of the methods which would be employed to 'destroy' the man of sin; and the sense is, that it would be by what is spoken by the Redeemer."
3. "The words would naturally harmonize with the idea that there would be a somewhat gradual process under the operation of truth toward the destruction of the man of sin, but that the complete annihilation of his power would be by some more manifest exhibition of the personal glory of the Saviour."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Spirit [πνευματι] . Better, breath. Pneuma, almost always translated spirit, is from pnein to breathe or blow."
2. "Shall destroy [καταργησει] . See on cumbereth, Luke 13:7 and make without effect, Romans 3:3."
3. "Coming [παρουσιας] . Or presence, which is the original meaning. In N. T. with a few exceptions, of the second coming of Christ."

### John Calvin:
1. "He afterwards added, that he was already hatching a secret work of impiety."
2. "That Antichrist would be wholly and in every respect destroyed when that final day of the restoration of all things shall arrive."
3. "This is a signal commendation of true and sound doctrine — that it is represented as sufficient for putting an end to all impiety, and as destined to be invariably victorious, in opposition to all the machinations of Satan."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "As soon as the restraining influence is removed."
2. "The breath of his mouth...denoting the ease with which omnipotence accomplishes its object."
3. "His mere appearing will be sufficient to destroy His enemies."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "And then when the restraining one is taken out of the way, then the man of sin will be revealed."
2. "Whom the Lord [Jesus] shall slay, the breath of the Lord will destroy this arch-enemy."
3. "The advent of the man of sin will be a grand fiasco, the mere appearance of Christ destroys the adversary."

### John Gill:
1. "That lawless one, who sets himself above the laws of God and man, and dispenses with them at pleasure..."
2. "Christ will by his Spirit blow a blast upon antichrist and his kingdom, which he shall never recover again..."
3. "This Armillus, the Jews say, is the head of all idolatry, the tenth king who shall reign at Rome, the city of Satan..."

### John Wesley:
1. "When every prince and power that restrains is taken away."
2. "That wicked one - Emphatically so called, be revealed."
3. "Whom the Lord will soon consume with the spirit of his mouth - His immediate power."
