# 2 Thessalonians 3:1 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The word of the Lord is powerful and can bring salvation to believers. It can spread rapidly and be free from any obstructions or hindrances. By praying for the word of God to have swift progress, we can help remove any obstacles that may prevent its widespread dissemination. It is important that the word of God is acknowledged in its true power and glory, and that it is embraced and obeyed by those who hear it. Ultimately, when the word of the Lord is glorified, it can bring forth much fruit and change lives for the better.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The word of the Lord is then glorified when it becomes the power of God to salvation to the believer." 
2. "On τὸ λ. (= λοιπόν), see 1 Thessalonians 4:1."
3. "Contrast to 'being bound:' see 2Ti 2:9 may spread rapidly."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "may have free course—literally, 'may run'; spread rapidly without a drag on the wheels of its course."
2. "The opposite is the word of God being 'bound'."
3. "glorified—by sinners accepting it."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The gospel would spread rapidly in the earth if all the obstructions which men have put in its way were removed; and that they may be removed should be one of the constant subjects of prayer."
2. "The idea is, that it might meet with no obstruction, but that it might be carried abroad with the rapidity of a racer out of whose way every hindrance was removed."
3. "It is evident from this that Paul met with some obstructions in preaching the gospel where he was then laboring."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "May have swift progress through the world... An O. T. idea." 
2. "Acknowledged in its true power and glory. Comp. John 12:28."
3. "The phrase the word of the Lord - be glorified, only here."

### John Calvin:
1. "Though the Lord powerfully aided him, and though he surpassed all others in earnestness of prayer, he nevertheless does not despise the prayers of believers, by which the Lord would have us aided."
2. "When, however, he adds — that the word of God may have its course, he shows that he has not so much concern and regard for himself personally, as for the entire Church."
3. "Holiness of life and uprightness on the part of Christians is the glory of the gospel; as, on the other hand, those defame the gospel who make profession of it with the mouth, while in the meantime they live in wickedness and baseness."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Paul not only prays for his 'children' in Christ, but begs them to pray for him."
2. "Paul felt his need of courage to face those who opposed the preaching of the Gospel, of constancy to avail himself of every opportunity to introduce it into new audiences, and again and again appealed to the churches to pray for him in connection with this matter."
3. "Paul desires that the word may have free circulation, may not be checked in its onward race, may extend everywhere and be glorified, attaining its rightful position in men’s minds."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Pray (προσευχεσθε). Present middle, keep on praying."
2. "That the word of the Lord may run and be glorified (ινα ο λογος του κυριου τρεχη κα δοξαζητα)."
3. "Paul recognizes the close relation between himself and the readers. He needs their prayers and sympathy and he rejoices in their reception of the word of the Lord already,even as also it is with you (καθως κα προς υμας)."

### John Gill:
1. "It becomes all the churches, and the several members of them, to pray for their ministers: with respect to their private studies, that they might be directed to suitable subjects; that their understandings might be opened to understand the Scriptures; that their gifts might be increased, and they be more and more fitted for public service."
2. "The ministry of the word is a course or race, and ministers are runners in it...which is the message they are sent with, and the errand they run upon."
3. "The Gospel is glorified when it is attended upon by large numbers, and is heard with a becoming reverence; when it is received in the love of it, is greatly prized and highly esteemed; when it is cordially embraced, and cheerfully obeyed."

### John Wesley:
1. May run — Go on swiftly, without any interruption.And be glorified — Acknowledged as divine, and bring forth much fruit.
