# 2 Thessalonians 3:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Lord of peace, who is peaceable Himself and the author of peace, can alone give true and lasting peace to the heart and to the world. The way to have this peace and prosperity in churches is by maintaining the discipline of God's house. The apostle earnestly prays for a constant and perpetual peace, desiring it to be obtained in every possible way through prayer, preaching, administering the ordinances, and maintaining Christian fellowship and conversation.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "May the Lord of Peace give you (that) Peace always in every way." 
2. "ἡ εἰρήνη must not be understood only of peace with one another: but of peace in general, outward and inward, here and hereafter."
3. "μετὰ πάντων ὑ. ] therefore with the ἀτάκτως περιπατοῦντες also (Lün.): not as Jowett, pleonastic."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "An appropriate title in the prayer here, where the harmony of the Christian community was liable to interruption from the 'disorderly.'"
2. "The Greek article requires the translation, 'Give you the peace' which it is 'His to give.'"
3. "May He bless you not only with peace, but also with His presence."

### Albert Barnes:
1. Now the Lord of peace - The Lord who alone can impart peace; see the Romans 15:33 note; 1 Corinthians 14:33 note; Hebrews 13:20 note; John 14:27 note.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The Lord of peace [ο κυριος της ειρηνης]. The only instance of the formula."
2. "By all means [εν παντι τροπω], or in every way."
3. "The alternative reading topw place is rejected by the principal texts."

### John Calvin:
1. "He had forbidden them to treat even the contumacious as enemies, but rather with a view to their being brought back to a sound mind by brotherly admonitions."
2. "He could appropriately, after this, subjoin an injunction as to the cultivation of peace; but as this is a work that is truly Divine, he betakes himself to prayer, which, nevertheless, has also the force of a precept."
3. "At the same time, he may also have another thing in view — that God may restrain unruly persons, that they may not disturb the peace of the Church."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The Lord of peace signifies not only that He can bestow peace, but also and primarily that it is His own attribute. He has peace, because He sees the end from the beginning, and is unassailable in His righteousness and sovereignty."
2. "He gives His own peace by enabling men to rely upon Him, to accept His will, that will which shall certainly be accomplished, and by thus lifting them up above anxiety into His own security."
3. "He leaving them, leaves the Lord with them. It is the ‘farewell’ which rises naturally to the lip of him who must now lose sight of those he loves, but would fain leave them with all and far more than all the protection and blessing which he would himself have striven to provide them with."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The Lord of peace himself."
2. "Give you peace."
3. "The Lord Jesus whose characteristic is peace, can alone give real peace to the heart and to the world."

### John Gill:
1. "The Prince of peace, who is peaceable himself, and the author of peace in all his churches."
2. "The way to have true peace and prosperity in churches is to keep up the discipline of God's house."
3. "The apostle prays for constant and perpetual peace, which is greatly to be desired; and that it might be had by all means, and in every way through praying, preaching, administering the ordinances, laying on censures, when necessary, and Christian conversation."

### John Wesley:
1. The Lord of peace — Christ.Give you peace by all means — In every way and manner.
