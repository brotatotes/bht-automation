### Henry Alford:
1. "The love of man to God...must be subjective, the love of man to God."
2. "The patient waiting for Christ...will not bear this meaning. It occurs thirty-four times in the N. T., and always in the sense of endurance, patience."
3. "It must be...the patience of Christ, which Christ showed."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "If 'the Lord' be here the Holy Ghost (2 Corinthians 3:17), the three Persons of the Trinity will occur in this verse."
2. "The grace, 'patience,' or persevering endurance, is connected with the 'hope' of Christ's coming."
3. "We are to endure, as looking for the hope to be realized at His coming."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The prayer of the apostle was, that they might have the love of God in their hearts, and 'the patience of Christ;' that is, the same patience which Christ evinced in his trials."
2. "They needed patience, that they might endure their trials in a proper manner."
3. "It was natural for the apostle to refer them to the Saviour, the great example of patience, and to pray that they might have the same which he had."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Hearts... patient waiting for Christ" - The prayer is that their hearts may be directed to love God and to exhibit the patience of Christ.
2. "The patience of Christ" - The prayer is that their hearts may be directed to love God and to exhibit the patience of Christ.

### John Calvin:
1. "Let every one make it his endeavor to make proficiency in these two things, in so far as he desires to make progress towards perfection."
2. "The love of God cannot reign in us unless brotherly love is also exercised."
3. "This is the only thing that sustains us in the warfare of the present life, that we wait for the Redeemer; and farther, this waiting requires patient endurance amidst the continual exercises of the cross."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "To love God is to have in the heart the root of all activity and endurance, the spring of duty, and the fountain of all virtue."
2. "The apostle desires they may be enabled to exhibit under trial the same patient endurance which Christ Himself exhibited."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Bold figure for making smooth and direct road."
2. "Either subjective or objective genitive makes sense and Lightfoot pleads for both."
3. "Most scholars take it here as subjective, the characteristic of God."

### John Gill:
1. "By a directing of the heart into it, that is, to a lively exercise of it."
2. "To have the heart directed into this, is to be led into it directly; or by a straight line."
3. "To be rooted and grounded in it, and firmly persuaded of interest in it; and that nothing shall separate from it."

### John Wesley:
