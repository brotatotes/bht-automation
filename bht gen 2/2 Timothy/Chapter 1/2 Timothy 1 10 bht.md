# 2 Timothy 1:10 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The grace of God, which brings salvation to the elect, has been made known through the coming of our Savior Jesus Christ. He abolished the power of death by His own death and resurrection, transforming it from an enemy to be feared into a friend to be welcomed. Through the gospel, Jesus has brought to light the true life and immortality that were previously hidden, clarifying the reality of eternal life. His appearance on earth and His sacrifice have unveiled the glorious truth of salvation through Him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The carrying out of the abolition of death into full effect is to be at the resurrection."
2. "The death of the body meanwhile is but temporary, and is made no account of by Christ and the apostles."
3. "Before the Gospel revelation from God, man, by the light of nature, under the most favorable circumstances, had but a glimmering idea of the possibility of a future being of the soul, but not the faintest idea of the resurrection of the body."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The purpose to save us was long concealed in the divine mind, but the Saviour came that he might make it known."
2. "These things were before obscure or unknown, and that they have been disclosed to us by the gospel."
3. "The gospel thus has truths not found in any other system, and contains what man never would have discovered of himself."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "There is an implication that the divine counsel was hidden until the fitting time."
2. "Better, since he made of none effect."
3. "Only here in Pastorals. In Paul, 1 Corinthians 4:5; Ephesians 1:18; Ephesians 3:9."

### John Calvin:
1. "God has now called us by the gospel, not because he has suddenly taken counsel about our salvation, but because he had so determined from all eternity."
2. "The knowledge of those things is revealed to us by faith; and so the Apostle judiciously connects the gospel with the most ancient promises of God, that novelty may not render it contemptible."
3. "It is a high and remarkable commendation of the gospel, that it 'bringeth life to light.' To life he adds immortality; as if he had said, 'a true and immortal life.'"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Remarkable as the only passage in the New Testament in which the word ἐπιφανεία (= manifestation) is applied to the Incarnation of our Lord."
2. "Christ has deprived death of its power... It is no longer an enemy to be dreaded, but a friend to be welcomed."
3. "He has illumined the true, higher life of man, which was previously clouded by the lower."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "But hath now been manifested...the contrast made." 
2. "By the appearing...for the second coming." 
3. "Who abolished death...Hebrews 2:14."

### John Gill:
1. "The grace according to which the elect of God are saved and called... is now made manifest by the appearing of our Saviour Jesus Christ." 
2. "He has abolished corporeal death with regard to his people... by dying, and rising again." 
3. "Christ was the first that rose again from the dead to an immortal life... and through the Gospel, [eternal life] has been brought to light in a more clear manner than ever before."

### John Wesley:
1. "By the appearing of our Saviour - This implies his whole abode upon earth."
2. "Who hath abolished death - Taken away its sting, and turned it into a blessing."
3. "And hath brought life and immortality to light - Hath clearly revealed by the gospel that immortal life which he hath purchased for us."
