# 2 Timothy 1:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Paul longed to see Timothy and desired a sight of him. He remembered the tears that Timothy shed, possibly shed multiple times, including at their last parting. Paul wanted to see Timothy to be filled with joy and expressed a heartfelt and strong affection for him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "With yearning as for one much missed."
2. "Mindful of thy tears, not only at our parting, but also often when under pious feelings."
3. "That I may be filled with joy."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Greatly desiring to see thee...He wished to see him...because he would be useful to him in his present circumstances." 
2. "Being mindful of thy tears...nothing is more probable than that Timothy would weep when separated from such a father and friend. It is not wrong thus to weep, for religion is not intended to make us stoics or savages." 
3. "That I may be filled with joy...It is easy to imagine what joy it would give Paul...to see a friend whom he loved as he did this young man."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Greatly desiring [επιποθων] . Better, longing."
2. "The words give the reason for the longing to see Timothy. The allusion is probably to the tears shed by Timothy at his parting from Paul."
3. "He bids the overseers take heed to the flock, for false teachers will arise in the bosom of the church (Acts 20:29, Acts 20:30); these letters contain directions for the guidance of the flock, and denunciations of heretical teachers."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Remembering thy tears. The words clearly refer to their last parting, probably that referred to in 1 Timothy 1:3." 
2. "Now that absence had increased the yearning desire to see him once again, and so ‘be filled with joy,’ it was as natural to dwell on it."
3. "It is characteristic of Timothy’s sensitive, emotional nature that the tears were shed by him."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Night and day" - Genitive of time, "by night and by day."
2. "Longing" - Present active participle of επιποθεω, old word, eight times in Paul.
3. "Remembering thy tears" - Perfect middle participle of μιμνησκω, old and common verb with the genitive, only here in the Pastorals and elsewhere by Paul only in 1 Corinthians 11:2.

### John Gill:
1. "Greatly desiring to see thee.... he longed for a sight of him." 
2. "being mindful of thy tears; shed... wept with those that wept." 
3. "that I may be filled with joy; at the sight of him... an instance of hearty, sincere, and strong affection."

### John Wesley:
1. Being mindful of thy tears — Perhaps frequently shed, as well as at the apostle's last parting with him.
