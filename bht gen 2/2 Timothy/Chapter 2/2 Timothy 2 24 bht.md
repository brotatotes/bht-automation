# 2 Timothy 2:24 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The servant of the Lord is expected to avoid engaging in arguments and contentions. Instead, they should possess a gentle and patient spirit. Additionally, they should be skilled in teaching and demonstrate a willingness to instruct others, using their abilities with patience and perseverance.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The servant of the Lord" must imitate his master in not striving contentiously, though uncompromising in earnestly contending for the faith.
2. He is to be gentle so that he may occasion no evils; patient so that he may endure evils.
3. Apt to teach - implying not only solid teaching and ease in teaching, but patience and assiduity in it.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The servant of Christ should be a man of peace."
2. "He should not struggle for mere victory, even when endeavoring to maintain truth; but should do this, in all cases, with a kind spirit, and a mild temper."
3. "The Christian minister is to be meek and mild toward all, not disputatious and quarrelsome."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The servant of the Lord [δουλον κυριου] . The teacher or other special worker in the church."
2. "Apt to teach, patient [διδακτικον, ανεξικακον] ."
3. "Anexikakia forbearing, N. T. o. Anexikakia forbearance Wisd. 2 19. Rend. Forbearing."

### John Calvin:
1. "The servant of God must stand aloof from contentions; but foolish questions are contentions; therefore whoever desires to be a 'servant of God,' and to be accounted such, ought to shun them."
2. "When he bids the servant of Christ be 'gentle,' he demands a virtue which is opposite to the disease of contentions."
3. "A godly teacher ought even to try whether it be possible for him to bring back to the right path obstinate and rebellious persons, which cannot be done without the exercise of gentleness."

### Philip Schaff:
1. 2 Timothy 2:24.  Patient. The Greek is more expressive, ‘  patient under, or  putting up with, evil.’
	

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Must not strive (ου δε μαχεσθα). Rather, 'it is not necessary for him to fight' (in such verbal quibbles)."
2. "Gentle (ηπιον). Old word (from επος, speech), affable, mild."
3. "Forbearing (ανεξικακον). Late compound (from future of ανεχω, ανεξω, and κακον, putting up with evil)."

### John Gill:
1. "The servant of the Lord must not strive, but be gentle unto all men."
2. "Apt to teach, showing a willingness to instruct the ignorant and obstinate, and making use of abilities given for that purpose."
3. "Patient, or 'bearing evil', not only the infirmities of weak brethren in the church, but also the contradictions and oppositions of the adversaries of truth."

### John Wesley:
1. "A servant of the Lord must not...strive eagerly or passionately."
2. "Strive...not as do the vain wranglers spoken of."
3. "But be apt to teach...chiefly by patience and unwearied assiduity."
