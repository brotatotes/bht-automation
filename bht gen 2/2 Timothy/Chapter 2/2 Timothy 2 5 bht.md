# 2 Timothy 2:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
No Christian can expect the crown of life unless they run the race set before them in the right way, striving lawfully as a faithful minister. They must look to Christ as the mark, pressing through all difficulties and holding on and out unto the end to receive the prize of the high calling of God.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The soldier must serve on condition of not dividing his service: now we have another instance of the same requirement."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Strive for masteries - strive in the games"
2. "Yet is he not crowned, except even though he gains the victory."
3. "Strive lawfully - observing all the conditions of both the contest and the preparation for it."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "No one could obtain the prize unless he had complied with all the laws of the games, and had thus given to those with whom he contended, a fair opportunity to succeed."
2. "In those contests, he who transgressed the rules in the least matter, not only failed of the prize, even though the apparent victor, but was sometimes disgraced and punished."
3. "The apostle here represents the Christian minister as engaged in a struggle or conflict for the crown. He says that he could not hope to win it unless he should comply with all the laws by which it is conferred; unless he should subdue every improper propensity, and make an effort like that evinced by the combatants at the Olympic games."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The verb [strive for masteries] may mean to contend in battle; and the preceding reference to the soldier would seem to suggest that meaning here." 
2. "Among the Rom. crowns were the highest distinction for service in war."
3. "The law of the ministerial office requires that the minister shall not entangle himself with secular pursuits."

### John Calvin:
1. "He borrows a comparison from wrestlers, not one of whom obtains the prize till he has been victorious in the end."
2. "If any man, therefore, wearied with the conflict, immediately withdraw from the arena to enjoy repose, he will be condemned for indolence instead of being crowned."
3. "To strive lawfully is to pursue the contest in such a manner and to such an extent as the law requires, that none may leave off before the time appointed."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Strive in the games would perhaps answer the purpose, as bringing out more distinctly the new comparison." 
2. "The phrase... implied the observance of all rules of life prior to the contest as well as during it."
3. "Failure to keep to the appointed diet and discipline, no less than taking an unfair advantage at the time, excluded the competitor from his reward."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Not 'except he have contended,' but simply 'unless he contend' (in any given case) 'lawfully' (νομιμως)."
2. "The victor in the athletic contests was crowned with a garland."

### John Gill:
1. "No man that calls himself a Christian, minister, or any other, can expect the crown of life, the prize of the high calling of God, except he runs the race set before him, in the right way."
2. "Looking to Christ, the mark, pressing through all difficulties, towards the prize, and holds on and out unto the end."
