# 2 Timothy 2:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Of all the genealogies, it is important to focus on the fact that Jesus Christ is of the seed of David. This lineage is significant because it emphasizes his humanity, confirming that he truly came in the flesh and was a genuine human being. In considering this lineage, we are reminded of the incarnational nature of Christ and his true embodiment in human form.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Remember Jesus Christ, raised from the dead."
2. "Christ's resurrection is put prominently forward as being the truth now assailed and the one best calculated to stimulate Timothy to steadfastness in sharing Paul's sufferings for the Gospel's sake."
3. "In His death He assured us of His humanity; by His resurrection, of His divinity."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Remember Jesus Christ; him who was raised from the dead...Think of the Saviour, now raised up from the dead after all the sorrows of this life, and let this encourage you to bear your trials."
2. "There is nothing better fitted to enable us to endure the labors and trials of this life, than to think of the Saviour."
3. "According to my gospel - The gospel which I preach."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Remember Jesus Christ raised from the dead. The perfect marks the permanent condition - raised and still living."
2. "Not referring to Christ's human descent as a humiliation in contrast with his victory over death, but only marking his human, visible nature along with his glorified nature."
3. "According to my gospel. Comp. Romans 2:16; Romans 16:25, and see 1 Corinthians 14:1; 1 Corinthians 2:0 Gal 11:7; Galatians 1:11; Galatians 2:2; 1 Timothy 1:11."

### John Calvin:
1. "How necessary this admonition of Paul was, the ancient histories show; for Satan put forth all his strength, in order to destroy this article of our faith."
2. "In the resurrection of Christ we all have a sure pledge of our own resurrection."
3. "This clause not only asserts the reality of human nature in Christ, but also claims for him the honor and name of the Messiah."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Quoting words which were probably part of some formulated confession of faith, St. Paul calls on Timothy to remember the two great truths of the Resurrection and the Incarnation."
2. "If that remembrance were clear and strong, he could not fail to be ready to take his share of hardships; he would be ready also to take a firm stand against the false teachers."
3. "Special stress is laid on 'the seed of David' as indicating that Jesus was a historical, not an ideal Messiah (comp. Romans 1:3)."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Remember Jesus Christ as risen from the dead." This is the cardinal fact about Christ that proves his claim to be the Messiah, the Son of God.
2. Christ is central for Paul here as in Philippians 2:5-11.
3. The humanity of Christ as in Romans 1:3; Philippians 2:7.

### John Gill:
1. "Christ being of the seed of David, according to the flesh, or human nature, is expressive of his incarnation; shows that he was really come in the flesh, and was truly man."
2. "And now the apostle puts Timothy in mind, that he was raised from the dead; which implies that he died; and so includes all the doctrines relating to his death."
3. "The doctrine of the Gospel [...] because it was committed to him, and he was intrusted with it, and fully and faithfully preached it."

### John Wesley:
1. Of the seed of David — This one genealogy attend to.
