# 2 Timothy 3:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
All scripture is inspired of God, continuously guiding and assisting those who read it with prayer. It is profitable for doctrine, convicting and correcting those in error or sin, and instructing the children of God in righteousness. The Scriptures are the breath of God, attested and confirmed by divine authority, and are beneficial for teaching and spiritual growth.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The immense value to Timotheus of this early instruction is shewn by a declaration of the profit of Scripture in furthering the spiritual life."
2. "The object of the present verse plainly is to set before Timotheus the value of his early instruction as a motive to his remaining faithful to it."
3. "The Scripture is given by inspiration of God and is profitable for teaching, for conviction, for correction, for discipline in righteousness, that the man of God may be perfect, thoroughly made ready for every good work."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Every Scripture," that is, Scripture in its every part.
2. "All Scripture is God-inspired and therefore useful"; because we see no utility in any words or portion of it, it does not follow it is not God-inspired. It is useful because God-inspired; not God-inspired because useful.
3. Verbal inspiration does not mean mechanical dictation, but all "Scripture is (so) inspired by God," that everything in it, its narratives, prophecies, citations, the whole—ideas, phrases, and words—are such as He saw fit to be there.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "If now it can be proved that Paul himself was an inspired man, this settles the question as to the inspiration of the Old Testament."
2. "The idea seems to have been, that the life was in the breath, and that an intelligent spirit was communicated with the breath."
3. "The Scriptures are a powerful means of reformation, or of putting men into the proper condition in regard to morals."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Every divinely-inspired Scripture is, besides being so inspired and for that reason, also profitable, etc."
2. "Reproof: conviction."
3. "Correction: restoring to an upright state; setting right."

### John Calvin:
1. "In order to uphold the authority of the Scripture, he declares that it is divinely inspired; for, if it be so, it is beyond all controversy that men ought to receive it with reverence."
2. "This is a principle which distinguishes our religion from all others, that we know that God hath spoken to us, and are fully convinced that the prophets did not speak at their own suggestion, but that, being organs of the Holy Spirit, they only uttered what they had been commissioned from heaven to declare."
3. "And thus he indirectly censures those unprincipled men who fed the people with vain speculations, as with wind. For this reason we may in the present day, condemn all who, disregarding edification, agitate questions which, though they are ingenious, are also useless."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "All Scripture is God-inspired, and is profitable, etc."
2. "Every Scripture, so far as it is inspired, works for the completeness of 'the man of God,' of the minister of Christ, and of his work."
3. "It is of small use acknowledging the inspiration of Scripture as a dogma unless you use it for its appointed work."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "All scripture (or every scripture) is inspired of God and profitable."
2. "All scripture (or every scripture), inspired of God, is also profitable."
3. "God-breathed."

### John Gill:
1. "The Scriptures are the breath of God, the word of God and not men; they are 'written by the Spirit.'"
2. "The Scriptures are commended, from the divine authority of them; and which is attested and confirmed by various arguments."
3. "The Scriptures are profitable for doctrine; for the discovering, illustrating, and confirming any doctrine concerning God and many others."

### John Wesley:
1. "All scripture is inspired of God — The Spirit of God not only once inspired those who wrote it, but continually inspires, supernaturally assists, those that read it with earnest prayer."
2. "Hence it is so profitable for doctrine, for instruction of the ignorant, for the reproof or conviction of them that are in error or sin, for the correction or amendment of whatever is amiss,"
3. "and for instructing or training up the children of God in all righteousness."
