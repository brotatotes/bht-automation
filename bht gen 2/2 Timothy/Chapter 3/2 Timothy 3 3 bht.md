# 2 Timothy 3:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Despisers of those that are good — Of good men, and all that is good.One who cannot be brought to terms, or to make or keep a truce with others.An accuser of others; a calumniator; one who falsely charges others with crime.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Truce-breakers" - rather as the Greek is translated "implacable."
2. "False accusers" - slanderers.
3. "Incontinent, fierce" - at once both soft and hard: incontinently indulging themselves and inhuman to others.
4. "Despisers, c." - "no lovers of good", the opposite of "a lover of good".

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Without natural affection - see the notes at Romans 1:31."
2. "It may thus refer to those who are unwilling to enter into any agreement; that is, either those who are unwilling to be reconciled to others when there is a variance - implacable; or those who disregard treaties or agreements. In either case, this marks a very corrupt condition of society."
3. "Religion produces gentleness; the want of it makes men rough, harsh, cruel."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Without natural affection [αστοργοι] . Only here and Romans 1:31. o LXX See on ajgaph love, Galatians 5:22, under stergein to love with a natural affection."
2. "Truce - breakers [ασπονδοι] . N. T. o.  o LXX Rend. implacable. From aj not, and spondai a treaty or truce. The meaning is, refusing to enter into treaty, irreconcilable."
3. "Incontinent [ακρατεις] . Or intemperate, without self - control. N. T. o. Once in LXX, Proverbs 27:20. Akrasia incontinence, Matthew 23:25; 1 Corinthians 7:5; 1 Corinthians 1:0 Macc. 6 26; Ps. of Song of Solomon 4:3."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Truce-breakers. Better, ‘implacable,’ not the persons who break a truce, but those with whom no truce can be made." (2 Timothy 3:3)
2. "False accusers. Better, ‘slanderers,’ as in 1 Timothy 3:11." (2 Timothy 3:3)
3. "Despisers of them that are good. Better, ‘having no love of good.’" (2 Timothy 3:3)

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Implacable (ασπονδο). Truce-breakers."
2. "Slanderers (διαβολο)."
3. "Without self-control (ακρατεις)."

### John Gill:
1. "Without natural affection,.... thrusting their children into religious houses, cloisters, c. against their wills children leaving their parents without their knowledge or consent; married bishops and priests being obliged to quit their wives, and declare their children spurious; with many other such unnatural actions." 
2. "Trucebreakers; or covenant breakers; stirring up princes to break through their treaties and covenants with one another; dissolving the allegiance of subjects to their sovereigns, and moving them to rebellion against them; loosing the marriage bond between husband and wife; making void all oaths, contracts, and agreements, among men, which stand in the way of their designs."
3. "Despisers of those that are good; or without love to good; both to good works, to which they are reprobate, notwithstanding all their pretensions to them, and bluster about them; and to good men, whom they hate."

### John Wesley:
1. Without natural affection — To their own children.Intemperate, fierce — Both too soft, and too hard.
