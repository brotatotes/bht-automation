# 2 Timothy 3:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Some people are constantly seeking to gain knowledge, always eager to learn new things. However, their pursuit often leads them astray, keeping them from truly grasping the knowledge of God's truth. While they may accumulate various teachings and ideas, they fail to arrive at the understanding that comes from embracing the truth of the gospel.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Ever learning—some new point, for mere curiosity, to the disparagement of what they seemed to know before."
2. "The knowledge—the perfect knowledge; the only safeguard against further novelties."
3. "Gnosticism laid hold especially of the female sex."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They may learn many things, but the true nature of religion they do not learn."
2. "Many obtain much speculative acquaintance with the 'doctrines' of Christianity, but never become savingly acquainted with the system."
3. "Nothing is more common than for persons to be very busy and active in religion, and even to 'learn' many things about it, who still remain strangers to the saving power of the gospel."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "It is a graphic picture of a large class, by no means extinct, who are caught and led by the instructions of itinerant religious quacks."
2. "Because they have not the right motive, and because they apply to false teachers."
3. "Ellicott thinks that there is in dunamena a hint of an unsuccessful endeavor, in better moments, to attain to the truth."

### John Calvin:
1. "Always learning, while yet they never can come to the knowledge of the truth."
2. "That fluctuation between various desires, of which he now speaks, is when, having nothing solid in themselves, they are tossed about in all directions."
3. "It is ill-conducted study, and widely different from knowledge."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The words describe vividly the fruitless wanderings of those who pass from system to system and teacher to teacher."
2. "The knowledge of the truth, i.e., as in 2 Timothy 2:25, the full clear knowledge that shuts out uncertainty."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Never able to come to the knowledge of the truth."
2. "Pathetic picture of these hypnotized women without intellectual power."
3. "They never come into the full knowledge (επιγνωσιν) of the truth in Christ."
4. "And yet they even pride themselves on belonging to the intelligentsia!"

### John Gill:
1. "Ever learning,.... Some new notion and practice or another."
2. "Never able to come to the knowledge of the truth."
3. "The sins they are laden, and the lusts they are led away with, which hinder them from coming to the knowledge of the truth."

### John Wesley:
1. Ever learning — New things. But not the truth of God.
