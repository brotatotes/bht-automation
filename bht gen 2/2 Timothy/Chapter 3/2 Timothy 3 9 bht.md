# 2 Timothy 3:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
They may continue to advance in ungodliness and error, but they will not be able to make further progress in converting others to their way of thinking. Their folly and deceitfulness will be fully revealed, making it clear to all that they are false teachers.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Notwithstanding they shall not advance further... for their folly shall be thoroughly manifested to all..."
2. "It is in vain to deny that there is an apparent and literal inconsistency between the two assertions."
3. "While there the Apostle is speaking of an immediate spread of error, here he is looking to its ultimate defeat and extinction."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "they shall advance or proceed unto more ungodliness"
2. "they shall at last be forever prevented from seducing others"
3. "Often malice proceeds deeper down, when it cannot extend itself"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "There is a certain point beyond which they will not be allowed to go. Their folly will become manifest, and the world will understand it."
2. "Error will advance only to a certain point, when it will be 'seen' to be falsehood and folly, and when the world will arise and cast it off."
3. "By smooth sophistry, and cunning arts, they will not be able always to deceive mankind."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Folly [ανοια] . Only here and Luke 6:11 (note). The senselessness of their teaching, with an implication of its immoral character."
2. "Manifest [εκδηλος] . N. T. o. LXX, 3 Macc. 3 19; 6 5."


### John Calvin:
1. "He encourages Timothy for the contest, by the confident hope of victory; for, although false teachers give him annoyance, he promises that they shall be, within a short time, disgracefully ruined."
2. "It is more correct to say, that he looked at them in various aspects; for the affirmation, that they will not make progress, is not universal; but he only means, that the Lord will discover their madness to many whom they had, at first, deceived by their enchantments."
3. "That godly teachers may not despair, as if it were in vain for them to make war against error, they must be instructed about the prosperous success which the Lord will give to his doctrine."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The context shows that the earlier passage refers to the development of evil tendencies from their germ, and the later to their ultimate failure and decay."
2. "Heresies spread rapidly but are short-lived."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They shall proceed no further" (ου προκοψουσιν επ πλειον).
2. "Folly" (ανοια). 
3. "Theirs" (εκεινων).

### John Gill:
1. "They may proceed to more ungodliness, and wax worse and worse in error; but they shall proceed no further than the magicians of Egypt, who did lying wonders, hardened Pharaoh's heart, and deceived him and the Egyptians; but could not destroy the Israelites, nor hinder their departure out of Egypt, when their time was come." 
2. "These wicked men do false miracles, harden the popes of Rome, and deceive the nations subject to them; but they cannot deceive the elect of God, nor destroy the church of God, against which the gates of hell cannot prevail; nor could they hinder the reformation, or the departure of the Lord's people out of Babylon." 
3. "The frauds, impostures, tricks, and villanies of the church of Rome, and its votaries, have been detected and exposed; which have been the means of hindering them from proceeding any further than they have."

### John Wesley:
1. They shall proceed no farther — In gaining proselytes.
