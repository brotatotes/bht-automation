# 2 Timothy 4:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Be diligent in your faith, unwavering in your hope, and fervent in your love, continually seeking God's guidance and relying on His strength. Endure difficulties and trials, both external and internal, with patience and reliance on God's grace. Proclaim the message of peace, forgiveness, righteousness, and salvation through Jesus Christ, faithfully fulfilling the role of a bearer of good news and spreading the Gospel to all. Watch diligently in all aspects of your life and ministry, remaining steadfast and vigilant in your commitment to God's calling.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Do thou be sober...that thou be not entrapped into forgetfulness, but discern and use every opportunity of speaking and acting for the truth."
2. "Suffer hardship, do the work of an Evangelist...including all that belongs to a preacher and teacher of the Gospel."
3. "Fill up, in every point; leaving nothing undone in...thy ministry."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Follow my steps, inherit their result, and the honor of their end."
2. "Watch thou - literally, 'with the wakefulness of one sober.'"
3. "Make full proof of - fulfil in all its requirements, leaving nothing undone."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Be vigilant against error and against sin, and faithful in the performance of duty."
2. "Do the work of preaching the gospel, or of one appointed to proclaim the glad tidings of salvation."
3. "Each one should resolve, by the blessing of God, that the ministry, in his hands, shall be allowed, 'by a fair trial,' to show to the utmost what it is adapted to do for the welfare of mankind."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Watch thou... Endure afflictions... Make full proof of thy ministry." 
2. "An apostle, as such, was an evangelist, but every evangelist was not an apostle."
3. "A traveling minister whose work was not confined to a particular church."

### John Calvin:
1. "The more grievous the diseases are, the more earnestly Timothy may labor to cure them." 
2. "The ministers of Christ, when they faithfully discharge their office, are immediately called to engage in combats." 
3. "Timothy, by watching, and by patiently enduring afflictions, and by constant teaching, will succeed in having the truth of his ministry established, because from such marks all will acknowledge him to be a good and faithful minister of Christ."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Thou, whatever others may do, shouldst watch."
2. "Do the work of an evangelist."
3. "Make full proof of thy ministry."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Be sober in thy head."
2. "Suffer hardship."
3. "Do the work of an evangelist."

### John Gill:
1. "Relating to himself, his doctrine, and conversation; and to others, to feed the flock of God under his care, to know the state of them, and care for them; to give the time of night, and notice of approaching danger, and see the laws of Christ put in execution." 
2. "Endure afflictions... meaning reproaches and persecutions for the sake of the Gospel, from without, and all trials and exercises from within, through the infirmities and ill conduct of the saints themselves." 
3. "But to preach peace, pardon, righteousness, life, and salvation alone by Jesus Christ, and through the free grace of God."

### John Wesley:
1. "Watch — An earnest, constant, persevering exercise. The scripture watching, or waiting, implies steadfast faith, patient hope, labouring love, unceasing prayer; yea, the mighty exertion of all the affections of the soul that a man is capable of."
2. "In all things — Whatever you are doing, yet in that, and in all things, watch."
3. "Do the work of an evangelist - Which was next to that of an apostle."
