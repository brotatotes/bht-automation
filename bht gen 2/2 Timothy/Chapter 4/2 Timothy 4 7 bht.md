# 2 Timothy 4:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
I have fought a good fight reflects the imagery of an athletic contest where Paul portrays his struggle for Christ. Similarly, “I have finished my course” compares the Christian life to a race that Paul has successfully completed. Lastly, I have kept the faith signifies Paul's unwavering dedication to upholding and living in accordance with the gospel teachings. These statements highlight Paul's strong commitment to his faith and the sacrifices he made for the sake of Christ.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "I have striven the good strife... to any contest."
2. "I have finished my race... the image belongs peculiarly to St. Paul."
3. "I have kept the faith... the constant use of ἡ πίστις in these Epistles in the objective technical sense."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "I have striven the good strife" (...), that of the racecourse (1 Timothy 6:12 [ALFORD]; 1 Corinthians 9:24-27; Hebrews 12:1; Hebrews 12:2).
2. "kept the faith" - the Christian faith committed to me as a believer and an apostle (compare 2 Timothy 1:14; Revelation 2:10; Revelation 3:10).

### Albert Barnes:
1. "I have fought a good fight - The Christian life is often represented as a conflict, or warfare; see the notes on 1 Timothy 6:12."
2. "I have finished my course - The Christian life, too, is often represented as a 'race' to be run; compare the notes at 1 Corinthians 9:24-26."
3. "I have kept the faith - I have steadfastly maintained the faith of the gospel; or, have lived a life of fidelity to my Master."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "I have fought a good fight."
2. "For a good fight rend. the good fight."
3. "I have kept the faith."

### John Calvin:
1. "He even congratulates himself on his death, because it may be regarded as the goal or termination of his course."
2. "He affirms that to Christ's combatants death is desirable, because it puts an end to their labors."
3. "He alludes to the solemn oath taken by soldiers; as if he had said that he was a good and faithful soldier to his captain."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "I have striven a good strife" - The Greek is wider in its range and takes in every kind of contest.
2. "I have kept the faith" - So far he has not failed; he has kept the faith which had been committed to him.

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The 'fight' is the athletic contest of his struggle for Christ." 
2. "He had used this metaphor also of himself to the elders at Ephesus. Then the 'course' was ahead of him. Now it is behind him."
3. "Paul has not deserted. He has kept faith with Christ."

### John Gill:
1. "I have fought a good fight,"
2. "I have finished my course, or race;"
3. "I have kept the faith;"
