## BHT:
Gaius is commended for his faithfulness and dedication to the truth. He is praised for his kindness and hospitality towards the brethren. Despite being surrounded by false teachers, he continues to walk in truth and uphold the Gospel message. Gaius' commitment to the truth is evident in both his beliefs and his actions, and he consistently uses his talents for God's glory.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They may have been members of the same church with Gaius... or they may have been the 'brethren' who had gone from him with a letter of commendation to the church... and had been rejected by the church through the influence of Diotrephes."
2. "They would of course bear honorable testimony to the kindness which they had received from Gaius, and to his Christian character."
3. "That you adhere steadfastly to the truth, notwithstanding the fact that errors abound, and that there are many false teachers in the world."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Rejoiced greatly." See on 2 John 1:4.
2. "Thou walkest in truth." See on 1 John 1:8. for the phrase walk in, see on 2 John 1:6.
3. "Thou is emphatic, suggesting a contrast with less faithful ones, as Diotrephes, ver. 9."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "I rejoiced greatly... at the good tidings about Gaius." 
2. "Present participle here denotes repetition, from time to time."
3. "As always in the Johannine writings, 'truth' covers every sphere of life, moral, intellectual, spiritual."

### John Gill:
1. "These brethren seem to be preachers of the word, who travelled from place to place to spread the Gospel"

### John Wesley:
