# 3 John 4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
As a faithful pastor, there is no greater joy than to witness the spiritual growth and steadfastness of those under my care. The joy that comes from knowing that my spiritual children, whom I have nurtured and guided in their faith, are walking in the truth is unparalleled. It is a testament to the transformative power of God's grace and the fulfillment of my pastoral calling.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "I have no greater joy than this."
2. "That I hear of my children walking in the truth."


### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. my
	children
	— members of the Church: confirming the view that the “elect
	lady” is a Church.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "I have no greater joy than to hear that my children walk in truth - That they adhere steadfastly to the truth, and that they live in accordance with it."
2. "John, the aged apostle, says that he had no higher happiness than to learn, respecting those who regarded him as their spiritual father, that they were steadfast in their adherence to the doctrines of religion."
3. "The highest joy that a Christian parent can have is to know that his children, whether at home or abroad, adhere to the truths of religion, and live in accordance with the requirements of the gospel of Christ."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Joy. The texts vary; some reading carin grace or favor from God, on which see 2 John 1:3."
2. "Note the Greek order: greater joy than this have I not."
3. "Walk. Rev., rightly, walking. The participle expresses something habitual."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Greater (μειζοτεραν). A double comparative with -τερος added to μειζων, like our 'lesser' and like μαλλον κρεισσον (more better) in Philippians 1:23."
2. "Like forms occur in the vernacular papyri and even in Homer (χειροτερος, more worse) as also in Shakespeare."
3. "Walking in truth (εν αληθεια περιπατουντα). As in 2 John 1:4, which see. By the use of τεκνα John may mean that Gaius is one of his converts (1 Timothy 1:1)."

### John Gill:
1. "I have no greater joy.... Nothing that causes greater joy."
2. "...he had nothing to be more thankful for:than to hear that my children walk in truth."
3. "meaning his spiritual children, those whose conversion he had been the instrument of; and among these it seems Gaius was one."

### John Wesley:
1. "I have no greater joy than this."
2. "Such is the spirit of every true Christian pastor."
3. "To hear that my children walk in the truth."
