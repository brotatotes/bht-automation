# 3 John 6 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In order to encourage Gaius to continue showing kindness to the same individuals, the apostle highlights how they had spoken highly of his previous acts of love in front of the church. As a gesture of honor and friendship, Gaius is urged to provide support to these individuals as they embark on their journey. This act, done in a manner that reflects the character of God, is fitting for those who serve Him and demonstrates the apostle's tender concern.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "To stimulate others by the good example."
2. "Honors God’s missionary servants ( :-), honors God."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Which have borne witness of thy charity before the church."
2. "Whom if thou bring forward on their journey."
3. "After a godly sort - As becomes those who serve God."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The Church [εκκλησιας]. See on Matthew 16:18."
2. "Thou shalt do well [καλως ποιησεις]. For the phrase, see Acts 10:33; Philippians 4:14; James 2:8, James 2:19; 2 Peter 1:19."
3. "Rev., renders the whole: whom thou wilt do well to set forward on their journey worthily of God."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Thou wilt do well. Future active of ποιεω with adverb καλως, a common polite phrase in letters (papyri) like our 'please.'" 
2. "To set forward on their journey. First aorist active participle (simultaneous action) of προπεμπω, to send forward, 'sending forward,' old word." 
3. "Worthily of God. Precisely this phrase in 1 Thessalonians 2:12 and the genitive with αξιως also in Romans 16:2; Philippians 1:27; Colossians 1:10; Ephesians 4:1. 'Since they are God's representatives, treat them as you would God' (Holtzmann)."

### John Gill:
1. "And this the apostle mentions to encourage Gaius to go on, and continue in his kindness to the same persons, since they retained such a grateful sense of past favours."
2. "The word here used signifies, to send on before...dismiss them, take his leave of them in a friendly and honorable way."
3. "And this he would have him do, after a godly sort...in imitation of God, who is merciful, kind, and beneficent...fit and proper such servants of God...should be used."

### John Wesley:
1. "Who have testified of thy love before the church — The congregation with whom I now reside."
2. "Whom if thou send forward on their journey — Supplied with what is needful."
3. "Thou shalt do well — How tenderly does the apostle enjoin this!"
