# Acts 1:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In Acts 1:3, Luke summarizes the instructions that Christ gave to his apostles during the forty days after his resurrection. Then, in Acts 1:4 and the following verses, Luke specifically records what Jesus said on the day of his ascension. This marks the beginning of the Acts of the Apostles, as Luke transitions from his previous account to the events that unfolded from that day onward.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "His last commands were given in the power of the Holy Ghost." 
2. "The use of the verb in this abbreviated form, without the εἰς τ . οὐρ ., testifies to the familiarity of the apostolic church with the Ascension as a formal and recognized event in our Lord’s course."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "It is worthy of notice that nowhere else are such communications of the risen Redeemer said to have been given 'through the Holy Ghost.'"
2. "But after His resurrection, as if to signify the new relation in which He now stood to the Church, He signalized His first meeting with the assembled disciples by breathing on them and saying, 'Receive ye the Holy Ghost,' thus anticipating the donation of the Spirit from His hands."
3. "His parting charges are here said to have been given 'through the Holy Ghost,' as if to mark that He was now all redolent with the Spirit."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "After that ... - This passage has been variously rendered. The Syriac translates it, 'After he had given commandment unto the apostles whom he had chosen by the Holy Spirit.' So also the Ethiopic version."
2. "To understand this, it is necessary to call to mind the promise that Jesus made before his death, that after his departure, the Holy Spirit would descend to be a guide to his apostles."
3. "The instructions were given by divine authority, by infallible guidance, and as a part of the work which the Holy Spirit was sent down to accomplish."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Had given commandment [εντειλαμενος] . Special injunctions or charges."
2. "Through the Holy Ghost. Construe with had given commandment: by means of the Holy Spirit, which inspired him."

### John Calvin:
1. "Our redemption was fully complete and finished then when Christ did ascend unto his Father."
2. "Luke doth show unto us, that Christ did no sooner depart hence, but straightway he provided for the government of his Church; whence we may gather that he is careful for our salvation."
3. "In the preaching of the gospel there is nothing which issueth from man's brain, but that it is the divine ordinance of the Spirit, whereunto the whole world must be subject."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The ascension of Jesus is the turning-point at which the gospel history of the work and teaching of Jesus on earth ends, and where the Acts, the story of His work and teaching from His throne in heaven, commences."
2. "Jesus, who was anointed with the Holy Ghost, in the power of the Holy Ghost gave commandment to the apostles to be His witnesses, and to wait in Jerusalem till they were endued with power from on high."
3. "The last commandments of Jesus in reference to the sequel of the Acts were in St. Luke’s mind a point of great importance, while the choice of the apostles had already fallen within the range of gospel history."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Until the day in which Was received up... with or without 'into heaven'".
2. "This special commandment refers directly to what we call the commission given the apostles before Christ ascended on high".
3. "In his human life Jesus was under the guidance of the Holy Spirit".

### John Gill:
1. "Our Lord having chosen twelve of his own free grace and goodness, and not according to their worth and merit, to be his apostles, a little before his ascension to heaven, gave them more express and explicit commands and orders where they should go, into all the world, to all nations."
2. "The commands which Christ gave to his apostles... were from him as God, and so carried a divine authority with them."
3. "Just before his being taken up to heaven, he gave some special orders and directions to his apostles, whom he had chosen to that office through the Holy Ghost, and not through human affection in him, or according to any desert of theirs; but as under the influence of the Holy Spirit."

### John Wesley:
1. "After having given commandment" - In the3d verse Acts 1:3 St. Luke expresses in general terms what Christ said to his apostles during those forty days.
2. "But in the4th Acts 1:4 and following verses he declares what he said on the day of his ascension."
3. "He had brought his former account down to that day; and from that day begins the Acts of the Apostles."
