# Acts 1:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The promise of being baptized with the Holy Ghost applies to all true believers throughout history. It encompasses not only the indwelling of the Spirit but also the extraordinary gifts bestowed by the Holy Ghost.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The Lord cites these words from the mouth of John himself, reff. Matt.; and thus announces to them that, as John’s mission was accomplished in baptizing with water, so now the great end of His own mission, the Baptism with the Holy Ghost, was on the point of being accomplished."
2. "Calvin remarks, that He speaks of the Pentecostal effusion as being the Baptism with the Holy Ghost, because it was a great representation on the whole Church of the subsequent continued work of regeneration on individuals: ‘Quasi totius Ecclesiæ communis baptismus.’"
3. "I may add, also because it was the beginning of a new period of spiritual influence, totally unlike any which had preceded."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "ye shall be baptized with the Holy Ghost not many days hence—ten days hence, as appears from Leviticus 23:15; Leviticus 23:16"
2. "but it was expressed thus indefinitely to exercise their faith."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "John truly baptized ... with water, but the Messiah who was to come, as baptizing with the Holy Spirit and with fire."
2. "This promise was now about to be fulfilled in a remarkable manner."
3. "Not many days hence - This was probably spoken not long before his ascension, and of course not many days before the day of Pentecost."

### John Calvin:
1. "Christ did not then only baptize with the Holy Ghost, when as he sent him under the form of fiery tongues; for he had baptized his apostles before this; and he baptizeth all the elect thus daily."
2. "The sending of the Holy Ghost after so glorious a sort was a token of the hidden grace wherewith he doth daily inspire his elect."
3. "We must always hold fast this distinction, lest, whilst we deck man too much, we take from Christ."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "His words which you all remember respecting a future baptism with the Holy Ghost and fire, you will soon yourselves be able to test the truth of."
2. "That fiery baptism of Pentecost with which they were so soon to be baptized."
3. "Calvin well calls the common baptism of the Church ecclesiae communis baptismus, 'because it was a great representation on the whole Church of the subsequent continued work of regeneration on individuals' (Alford)."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "John's own words (Matthew 3:11) to which Jesus apparently refers use εν (in) both with water and Spirit."
2. "This baptism of the Holy Spirit was predicted by John (Matthew 3:11) as the characteristic of the Messiah's work."
3. "A neat Greek idiom difficult to render smoothly into English: 'Not after many days these.'"

### John Gill:
1. "John's baptism was water baptism, an immersion of persons in water."
2. "It is Christ's prerogative to baptize with the Spirit, as John foretold of him."
3. "It designs such an extraordinary and plentiful donation of the gifts of the Spirit, as may be expressed by a baptism."

### John Wesley:
1. Ye shall be baptized with the Holy Ghost — And so are all true believers to the end of the world. But the extraordinary gifts of the Holy Ghost also are here promised.
