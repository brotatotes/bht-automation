# Acts 10:20 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Arise and go with them, not doubting anything, for the Spirit of God has sent them. Put aside any reservations about the Jewish customs and laws, as the message they carry is from God. It is important to note that the Holy Spirit takes responsibility for those messengers and connects their mission with Peter's unsettling vision. In this act, we see the truth of the personality and guidance of the Holy Spirit in leading the Church into all truth.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Make no question as to who or what they are, but"
2. "The Holy Spirit, shed down upon the Church to lead it into all the truth, had in His divine arrangements brought about, by the angel sent to Cornelius, their coming."

### John Calvin:
1. "The Scripture useth this word often, when it will express of what sort the obedience of faith ought to be."
2. "But we must not follow God with a doubtful and wavering, but with a quiet and constant mind."
3. "And hereby are we also admonished that men’s consciences shall by no other means be quiet, that they safely do that which they do, than when being taught by the Word of God, they determine that they do nothing without his commandment and conduct."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "In other words, we have before us here the truth of the personality of the Holy Spirit."
2. "A further point is here reached, of the highest doctrinal importance."
3. "This is similar to the general method of other Divine communications recorded in the Acts of the Apostles."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Now then, get thee down." 
2. "Go on, nothing doubting."
3. "The Holy Spirit assumes responsibility for the messengers from Cornelius and thus connects their mission with the vision which was still troubling Peter."

### John Gill:
1. "Arise, therefore, and get thee down,.... From the top of the house where he was:and go with them; the three men, to Caesareadoubting nothing." 
2. "Whether it is right or wrong, lawful or unlawful, to go with them, because not Jews, but uncircumcised Gentiles, laying aside all such Jewish scruples."
3. "For I have sent them: the Spirit of God is said to do what Cornelius did at his instigation and direction, signified by an angel he sent to him, Acts 10:5."
