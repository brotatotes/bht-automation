# Acts 10:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Cornelius recognized the divine nature of the angel's message, filling him with awe and fear. His prayers and acts of love were like sacrifices that pleased God and obtained His approval, as they have come up to God as a memorial. Although Cornelius did not yet have faith in Christ, his earnest seeking through prayer and good deeds was a precursor to a deeper and more perfect faith. It is clear that when one seeks faith, it should be done through prayer and acts of kindness, recognizing that God's favor and indulgence are needed along this journey.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "for a memorial, 'so as to be a memorial.'" 
2. "His faith was all that he could then attain to, and brought forth its fruits abundantly in his life."
3. "the earnest seeking by prayer for a better and more perfect faith."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "What is it, Lord?—language which, tremulously though it was uttered, betokened childlike reverence and humility."
2. "Thy prayers and thine alms—The way in which both are specified is emphatic. The one denotes the spiritual outgoing of his soul to God, the other its practical outgoing to men."
3. "are come up for a memorial before God—that is, as a sacrifice well-pleasing unto God, as an odor of a sweet smell."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Are come up for a memorial - Are remembered before God."
2. "God prefers the offering of the heart to external forms; the expressions of love to sacrifice without it."
3. "Cornelius was disposed to do the will of God as far as it was made known to him. Where this exists there is religion. The moral man is not."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. When he looked [ατενισας] . Rev., more accurately, fastening his eyes. Compare ch. 8 55; and see on Luke 4:20.

### John Calvin:
1. "The fear wherewith he was taken proceeded from the perceiving of the majesty of God; for so soon as men conceive the presence of God, they must needs be afraid and cast down with fear."
2. "What is it, Lord? It appeareth plainly by this answer, that Cornelius’ mind was touched with religion; that he knew that he had to deal with God."
3. "Good works do indeed purchase for us the increase of grace, but not by their own desert. For they cannot be acceptable to God without pardon, which they obtain by the benefit of faith."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "There is no part of the description of Peter’s trance corresponding with what we read here of the ‘fear’ of Cornelius."
2. "He was now manifestly to be 'remembered.' It was no longer to appear as if he was forgotten."
3. "The 'prayers and alms' of Cornelius expressed what a Hebrew sacrifice expressed; and they were registered in heaven accordingly."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Cornelius recognizes the angel of God (verse Acts 10:3) as God's messenger." 
2. "Gone up like the smoke of incense in sacrifices." 
3. "His prayers and his alms proved his sincerity and won the ear of God."

### John Gill:
1. "He perceived there was something uncommon and divine in this vision, and therefore was filled with awe and reverence, yea, with something of a panic fear."
2. "The prayers which he had put up in faith, for himself and family, and the charitable actions he had performed from a principle of love, were like sacrifices upon the altar, which ascended to God with acceptance."
3. "These were taken notice of, approved by God, and remembered by him, and the fruits and effects he was shortly to enjoy."

### John Wesley:
1. "Thy prayers and thine alms are come up for a memorial before God" - Dare any man say, These were only splendid sins? Or that they were an abomination before God? 
2. And yet it is certain, in the Christian sense Cornelius was then an unbeliever. He had not then faith in Christ. 
3. So certain it is, that every one who seeks faith in Christ, should seek it in prayer, and doing good to all men: though in strictness what is not exactly according to the Divine rule must stand in need of Divine favour and indulgence.
