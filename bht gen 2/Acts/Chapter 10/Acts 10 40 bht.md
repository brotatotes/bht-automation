# Acts 10:40 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The resurrection of Christ, though attributed to God the Father, is a clear demonstration of its truth. Through this resurrection, He was revealed in a glorious body, yet still the same as He was before His suffering. This manifestation is a complete proof of His victory over death and the grave.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Not the less certain, therefore, was the fact of His resurrection, though withholding Himself from general gaze in His risen body."
2. "He had before proclaimed Him 'Lord of all,' for the dispensing of 'peace' to all alike now he announces Him in the same supreme lordship, for the exercise of judgment upon all alike."
3. "Thus we have here all Gospel truth in brief. But, forgiveness through this exalted One is the closing note of Peter's beautifully simple discourse."

### Albert Barnes:
1. Showed him openly - Manifestly; so that there could be no deception, no doubt of his resurrection.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Shewed him openly [εδωκεν αυτον εμφανη γενεσθαι] . Lit., gave him to become manifest. Compare, for the construction, ch. 2 27.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Here, as everywhere in the Acts of the Apostles, the Resurrection is the culminating point of the apostolic testimony concerning Jesus Christ."
2. "Showed him openly. Literally, ‘gave Him to become visibly manifest.’"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Gave him to be made manifest."
2. "Peculiar phrase, here only in the N.T and in Romans 10:20."
3. "Predicate accusative after infinitive γενεσθα agreeing with αυτον object of εδωκεν."

### John Gill:
1. "The resurrection, of Christ, though it is sometimes ascribed to himself, as God, whereby he was declared to be the Son of God, yet generally to God the Father" 
2. "showed him openly; in a glorious body, and yet numerically the same he before lived and suffered in" 
3. "by which full proof was given of the truth of his resurrection, in which he appeared to be the conqueror over death and the grave"
