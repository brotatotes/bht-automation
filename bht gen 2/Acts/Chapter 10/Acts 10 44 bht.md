# Acts 10:44 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Holy Ghost fell on all that were hearing the word in a manifestation of supernatural and divine power. This event of bestowing the Spirit before baptism was unique and important, indicating that the doctrine of the gospel is for both the Jews and the Gentiles. The interruption and suddenness of this occurrence had a profound effect on the hearers and served as a confirmation of the conversion and acceptance of the Gentiles into the same privileges and blessings of salvation as the Jews.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The fire of the Lord fell, approving the sacrifice of the Gentiles, conferring on them the substance before the symbol, the baptism with the Holy Ghost before the baptism with water." 
2. "As the Holy Spirit dispensed once and for all with the necessity of circumcision in the flesh, so can He also, when it pleases him, with the necessity of water baptism." 
3. "The outpouring of the Spirit on the Gentiles was strictly analogous to that in the day of Pentecost."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 44, 45. While Peter yet spake . . .
	the Holy Ghost fell—by visible and audible manifestation ( :-).

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The Holy Ghost fell ... - Endowing them with the power of speaking with other tongues, Acts 10:46."
2. "Of this the apostle Peter makes much in his argument in Acts 11:17."
3. "By this, God showed that the Gentiles were to be admitted to the same privileges with the Jews, and to the blessings of salvation in the same manner."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. The Holy Ghost fell. The only example of the bestowment of the Spirit before baptism.

### John Calvin:
1. "God declareth now by a new miracle, that the doctrine of the gospel is common as well to the Gentiles as to the Jews."
2. "This visible sign representeth unto us, as it were in a table, what an effectual instrument of God’s power the preaching of the gospel is."
3. "But it doth not, nor shall not, always so fall out, that all those which hear the word with their outward ears, do or shall also receive the Spirit."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "What is of the utmost importance to us to mark is, that an occurrence took place on this occasion which is recorded on no other occasion of the same kind."
2. "The sudden interruption was far more forcible in its effect on the hearers than any additional words from Peter would have been."
3. "The Holy Ghost fell on all them which heard the word. The same verb, fell (ἐπέπεσε), is used in St. Peter’s account. The new impulse came from above. It was manifestly supernatural and Divine."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The Holy Ghost fell... Used of the Holy Spirit in Acts 8:16; Acts 10:44; Acts 11:15."
2. "It appears that Peter was interrupted in his sermon by this remarkable event."
3. "Here it was unexpected by Peter and by Cornelius and was indubitable proof of the conversion of these Gentiles who had accepted Peter's message and had believed on Jesus Christ as Saviour."

### John Gill:
1. "the Holy Ghost fell on all them which heard the word."
2. "the grace of the Spirit...falls down from above...the suddenness of it...comes at once, unthought of, and undesired...the power and efficacy of it...melts the heart, and converts the sinner...the plenty of it...falls in abundance...the permanency of it...continues...a well of living water springing up unto everlasting life."
3. "these extraordinary gifts of the Spirit were necessary at the first preaching of the Gospel to the Gentiles, for the confirmation of it...for the further confirmation of Peter...as well as for the sake of the believing Jews he brought along with him."

### John Wesley:
1. "The Holy Ghost fell on all that were hearing the word." 
2. "Thus were they consecrated to God, as the first fruits of the Gentiles."
3. "And thus did God give a clear and satisfactory evidence, that he had accepted them as well as the Jews."
