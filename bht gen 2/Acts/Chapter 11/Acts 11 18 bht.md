# Acts 11:18 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The critics ceased their wrangling and recognized that God had granted repentance unto life to the Gentiles. This demonstrates that the covenant of grace extends to all people, fulfilling the purpose of the Gospel being preached to them. It encourages sinners of the Gentiles to have hope and turn to Christ, who is able to grant this grace. Through true repentance, which brings about spiritual life, one can find everlasting life. This is a cause for glorifying God.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Whatever may be the consequences, or however mysterious the proceeding to us, this at least is plain, that God...has given unto the G. also repentance, that they may attain unto life."
2. "The involved position of the words in the present text is quite in St. Luke's manner."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Then hath God also granted to the Gentiles, &c.—rather, 'granted to the Gentiles also.' ... To 'grant repentance unto life'—that is, 'such as issues in life'—is more than to be willing to pardon upon repentance."
2. "The case of Cornelius is so manifestly one of grace reigning in every stage of his religious history, that we can hardly doubt that this was just the feature of it which they meant here to express."
3. "This is the grace that reigns in every conversion."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They were convinced, as Peter had been, by the manifest indications of the will of God."
2. "The great truth in this manner established that the doors of the church are opened To the entire Gentile world."
3. "To this truth we owe our hopes; for this, we should thank the God of heaven; and, impressed with it, we should seek to invite the entire world to partake with us of the rich provisions of the gospel of the blessed God."

### John Calvin:
1. "This is an evident sign of godliness, in that being thoroughly instructed touching the will of God, they cease forthwith to contend."
2. "Those are not to be despised who, being offended through unadvised zeal, reprove anything wrongfully; but that their consciences must be appeased by the Word of God."
3. "The gospel...tendeth, to wit, that God may reconcile men to himself, being renewed by his Spirit...the forgiveness of sins shall, notwithstanding, be no cause of sluggish security."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "They held their peace, and glorified God. The climax of this history is most beautiful...they broke out into praise and thanksgiving." 
2. "It was a noble example of candour, generosity, and charity."
3. "When the grace of repentance is given, spiritual life is the result."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The wrangling (verse Acts 11:2) ceased. The critics even 'glorified God'." 
2. "Reluctant acquiescence in the undoubted fact that God had 'granted repentance unto life' to these Gentiles in Caesarea." 
3. "Peter will see in this incident (Acts 15:8) the same principle for which Paul contends at the Jerusalem Conference."

### John Gill:
1. "They held their peace; and ceased contending and disputing with Peter, or blaming him for his conduct."
2. "And this being given to Gentiles, shows that the covenant of grace belongs to them, as well as to the Jews; and answers the design of the Gospel being sent among them, whereby the doctrines both of repentance and remission are preached unto them."
3. "It may encourage sinners of the Gentiles to hope for this grace, and apply to Christ for it, who is exalted to give it."

### John Wesley:
1. They glorified God — Being thoroughly satisfied.Repentance unto life — True repentance is a change from spiritual death to spiritual life, and leads to life everlasting.
