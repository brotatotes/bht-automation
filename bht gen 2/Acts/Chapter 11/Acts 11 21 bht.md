# Acts 11:21 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Lord's hand, symbolizing his love and grace, was upon them, enabling them with the gifts and graces needed for their ministry. The power of God accompanied their preaching, allowing the Gospel to be proclaimed not just in words but with divine power that led to salvation. The work of faith is not a human achievement, but a demonstration of God's almighty power and grace, resulting in multitudes believing in Christ for righteousness and eternal life.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "By visible manifestations not to be doubted, the Lord shewed it to be His pleasure that they should go on with such preaching."
2. "αὐτῶν being, the preachers to the Gentiles, whose work the narrative now follows."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The accession of Cornelius and his party was not the first admission of uncircumcised Gentiles into the Church."
2. "The only great object served by the case of Cornelius was the formal recognition of the principles which that case afterwards secured."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "And the hand of the Lord - See the notes on Luke 1:66."

### John Calvin:
1. "The hand of the Lord...testify by his present aid that the Gentiles were called together with the Jews, through his direction, to be made partakers of the grace of Christ."
2. "This place did also teach us, that what pains soever the ministers of God take in teaching, it shall be all vain and void, unless God bless their labors from heaven."
3. "Faith...is not idle and cold, but such as restoreth men (who were before turned away from God) unto his government, and bindeth them unto his righteousness."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The hand of the Lord was with them, indicating the manifestation of miraculous powers."
2. "A great number believed. All such terms are relative. At all events a considerable Christian community was formed rapidly at Antioch."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The hand of the Lord was with them... proof of God's approval of their course in preaching the Lord Jesus to Greeks." 
2. "Turned unto the Lord... the usual expression for Gentiles turning to the true God." 
3. "Here 'Lord' refers to 'the Lord Jesus'... the early disciples put Jesus on a par with Jehovah."

### John Gill:
1. "His hand of love, grace, and mercy, which was upon them, and supplied them with gifts and grace, and everything necessary for them."
2. "The power of God attended their ministry; so that the Gospel preached by them came not in word only, but in power, and it was the power of God unto salvation."
3. "The work of faith is not a work of man's will, but of God's almighty power and grace; and when that is displayed, multitudes believe in Christ for righteousness and life."

### John Wesley:
1. And the hand of the Lord — That is, the power of his Spirit.
