# Acts 11:23 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The amazing work of God's grace in bringing about the conversion of many souls is a cause for great rejoicing and giving glory to Him. It is heartening to see genuine believers like Barnabas who solely seek the honor and glory of Christ in the growth of the gospel. Their exhortation to the new converts is to determine in their hearts to stay devoted to the Lord, persisting in their faith, and remaining true to His teachings and commands until the very end.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  23.   παρεκάλει ] in accordance with his name, which (ch. Act 4:36 ) was interpreted υἱὸς παρακλήσεως .

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "When he . . . had seen the grace of God—in the new converts."
2. "Was glad—owned and rejoiced in it at once as divine, though they were uncircumcised."
3. "Exhorted them all that with purpose of heart—as opposed to a hasty and fickle discipleship. they would cleave unto the Lord—the Lord Jesus."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The effect of a revival is to produce joy in the hearts of all those who love the Saviour."
2. "With a firm mind; with a fixed, settled resolution that they would make this their settled plan of life, their main object."
3. "It is the leading principle; the strongest affection; the guiding purpose of the will to adhere to God, and, unless this is the prevalent, governing desire of the heart, there can be no evidence of conversion."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Purpose [προθεσει] ... a purpose."
2. "Something set before one as an object of attainment."
3. "Originally, placing in public; setting before."
4. "Hence of the shew-bread, the loaves set forth before the Lord (see on Mark 2:26)."

### John Calvin:
1. "By these words Luke teacheth, first, that the gospel which they had received was true; secondly, that Barnabas sought nothing else but the glory of Christ."
2. "The faithful servants of Christ must rejoice (as did Barnabas) when they see the gospel increase, by whomsoever God shall make his name known."
3. "We must note Barnabas’ exhortation. We have already said that Barnabas did subscribe to the former doctrine which they had embraced; but lest doctrine fall away, it is most requisite that it be thoroughly imprinted in the minds of the faithful by continual exhortations." 

### Philip Schaff:
1. "We see in this rejoicing, and in his attributing all this blessing to the free goodness of God, the marks of a true Christian heart."
2. "Barnabas was clearly the right man to have sent to Antioch; and all generations of Christians since have had in his mission grounds for praise and for 'glorifying God in him.'"
3. "His exhortation was simply to perseverance, heartiness, consistency, and progress."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Grace brings gladness."
2. "A smaller man would have raised difficulties as to circumcision or baptism."
3. "Cleave unto the Lord... Persistence was needed in such a pagan city."

### John Gill:
1. "The great goodness, love, and favour of God in enlightening, quickening, and converting so many souls."
2. "Rejoiced at heart, and gave glory to God, as every good man will, at the success of the gospel in the conversion of sinners."
3. "With a fixed resolution in the grace and strength of Christ, they would hold to his person, exercising grace upon him, abide by his truths and ordinances, keep close to his people, adhere to his cause and interest, and hold on and out unto the end."
