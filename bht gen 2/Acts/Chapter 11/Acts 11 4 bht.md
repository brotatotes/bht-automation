# Acts 11:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Peter carefully recounted the entire sequence of events, starting from the very beginning, in a deliberate and organized manner. Rather than relying on his authority as an apostle, he chose to present a simple and logical explanation, allowing his story to speak for itself. By approaching the matter in a methodical and orderly fashion, he demonstrated his willingness to have his case evaluated and judged by the collective body of believers, emphasizing the importance of this particular instance in shaping the early Christian community.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "A simple and unvarnished statement of facts is usually the best way of disarming prejudice and silencing opposition."
2. "In most cases prejudice will thus be disarmed, and opposition will die away."
3. "He thus showed that his own mind had been as much biased as theirs, and stated in what manner his prejudices had been removed."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Graphically indicating the solemn purport of the speech."
2. "His beginning with the first circumstances and going through the whole list of incidents."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "A simple and careful statement of the facts from the outset was more likely to be persuasive than anything else."
2. "He did not argue. The mere telling of the story was a proof of the Divine teaching in this case, which was far beyond any argument."
3. "The course which St. Peter followed was utterly different from that which he would have adopted if the privileges of infallibility and supremacy belonged to him."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Peter began at the beginning and gave the full story of God's dealings with him in Joppa and Caesarea."
2. "A deliberate and detailed narrative 'in order'."
3. "The case of Cornelius was a test case of primary importance."

### John Gill:
1. "Peter rehearsed the matter from the beginning...in a very methodical and orderly manner."
2. "He did not insist upon his authority as an apostle...but submitted to have his case heard, examined, and judged of by the body of the brethren."
