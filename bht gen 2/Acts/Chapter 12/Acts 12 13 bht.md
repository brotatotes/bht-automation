# Acts 12:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The gate, which was a distance from the house, was kept shut out of fear of the Jews, so when someone knocked, a young woman came to listen attentively to see who it was before opening the door. This young woman, named Rhoda, had a Greek name that meant a rose.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 13. came to hearken—not to
	open; for neither was it a time nor an hour of night for that, but to
	listen who was there.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "At the door of the gate - Rather the door of the vestibule, or principal entrance into the house."
2. "Named Rhoda - This is a Greek name signifying a rose."
3. "It was not unusual for the Hebrews to give the names of flowers, etc., to their daughters."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The narrative implies that she was more than a mere menial, if a servant at all."
2. "Her prompt recognition of Peter's voice, and her joyful haste, as well as the record of her name, indicate that she was one of the disciples gathered for prayer."
3. "God, who leaves in oblivion names of mighty conquerors, treasures up that of a poor girl, for his church in all ages."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Peter’s knock startled and alarmed the anxious, troubled assembly. It suggested fresh arrests and new cares and anxieties."
2. "The door was most probably that small outer door by which one entered through the large gate from the street into the court or area where the house was."
3. "The names of plants and flowers were favourite names for the daughters of Israel. So Susannah signifies ‘a lily,’ Esther ‘a myrtle,’ Tamar ‘a palm.’"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "When he knocked at the door of the gate... to knock or knock at." 
2. "To answer... to obey, to hearken."

### John Gill:
1. "The door was kept shut, for fear of the Jews, lest they should be disturbed."
2. "A damsel came to hearken; hearing a knocking, she came out to the door, and listened, to try if she could know who it was, a friend or a foe, before she opened."
3. "And the damsel was named Rhoda, which signifies a rose in the Greek language."

### John Wesley:
1. The gate — At some distance from the house; to hearken - If any knocked.
