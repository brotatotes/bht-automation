# Acts 13:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
John Mark, one of Paul's companions, withdrew from the group and returned, possibly due to weariness or fear of the dangers they were facing. This departure caused tension between Paul and Barnabas and would later lead to a disagreement between the two.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Henceforward Paul is the principal person, and Barnabas is thrown into the background."
2. "It may have been Paul's desire to extend the Gospel of Christ to this neighboring people."
3. "John probably took the opportunity of some ship sailing from Perga."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Perga was the metropolis of Pamphylia, on the river Cestrus, and about seven miles inland from Attalia."
2. "As Paul afterwards peremptorily refused to take Mark with him on his second missionary journey, because he 'had departed [or 'fallen off'] from them and had not gone with them to the work,' there can be no doubt that he had either wearied of it or been deterred by the prospect of the dangers which lay before him."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Paul and his company - Those with him - Barnabas and John - and perhaps others who had been converted at Paphos."
2. "Pamphylia was a province of Asia Minor, lying over against Cyprus, having Cilicia east, Lycia west, Pisidia north, and the Mediterranean south."
3. "But it is plain from Acts 15:38, that it was from some cause which was deemed blameworthy, and that his conduct now was such as to make Paul unwilling again to have him as a companion."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Loosed [αναχθεντες] . See on Luke 8:22."
2. "Paul and his company [οι περι τον παυλον] . Lit., those around Paul."
3. "Paul, and not Barnabas, now appears as the principal person."

### John Calvin:
1. "He made there a worthy sermon, which Luke will recite, together with the success."
2. "By noting his softness, he praiseth others which followed Paul with great constancy."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The ungrudging spirit of Barnabas seems at once to have conceded the first place to his more gifted fellow-worker."
2. "Paul, whose home was in the not distant Cilician Tarsus, and who had recently spent two or more years there, was of course acquainted with these mixed Jewish and Gentile congregations, and considered that among them the preaching of Jesus as Messiah would receive a welcome."
3. "But the more probable reason for his desertion was, that he shrank from the dangers and hardships of the mission."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "In nothing is the greatness of Barnabas more manifest than in his recognition of the superiority of Paul and acceptance of a secondary position for himself."
2. "Luke is silent on John's reasons for leaving Paul and Barnabas. He was the cousin of Barnabas and may not have relished the change in leadership. There may have been change in plans also now that Paul is in command."
3. "There were perils of many sorts around them and ahead, perils to which John Mark was unwilling to be exposed."

### John Gill:
1. "Paphos was 'urbs maritima', a city on the sea coast of the island of Cyprus; it was on the western part of the island."
2. "Perga in Pamphylia... where was a famous temple of Diana, whence she was sometimes called Pergea."
3. "John Mark...departed...from Perga in Pamphylia...his departure was resented by Paul; and it laid a foundation for a sharp contention between him and Barnabas."

### John Wesley:
1. John withdrawing from them returned — Tired with the fatigue, or shrinking from danger.
