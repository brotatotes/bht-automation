# Acts 13:24 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Before the coming of Christ, John the Baptist preached and prepared the way for his entrance into the world. He was the forerunner of Christ, who bore testimony to him and gave notice of his coming. John not only administered baptism but also preached about it, explaining its nature and purpose, and requiring repentance and fruits of righteousness. The preaching and testimony of John were well known and acknowledged by many, including the apostle Paul and the apostle Peter. The ministry of John created a stir in the Jewish nation and was heard of even in foreign countries.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Before his coming [προ προσωπου της εισοδου αυτου] . Lit., before the face of his entrance. A Hebrew form of expression.

### John Calvin:
1. "Therefore Paul bringeth in his testimony, that he may prove to the Jews that he preached no false Christ, but the true Christ of God, whom that most famous forerunner had before commended."
2. "Not that man’s testimony is sufficient to prove so weighty a matter; but there was another respect to be had to John whom all men almost did think to be a prophet of God."
3. "And we must note the phrase, that he preached baptism. Whereby we are taught that the sacraments are then rightly administered, when doctrine is joined with the visible figure; for the mouth of the baptizer must not be dumb, because the sign is vain without doctrine."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Paul mentions this preaching and testimony of the Baptist to Jesus as a thing well known."
2. "A vast number of the Jews seem to have acknowledged John’s authority as a prophet."
3. "His mission created a great stir in the Holy Land; and later we read of his disciples at Ephesus, some twenty-five years after the death of the Baptist (Acts 19:3)."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Literally, before the face of his entering in (here act of entrance as 1 Thessalonians 1:9, not the gate as in Hebrews 10:19)." 
2. "Baptism marked by, characterized by (genitive case, case of kind or species) repentance (change of mind and life)."
3. "It is clear therefore that Paul understood John's ministry and message as did Peter (Acts 2:38; Acts 10:37)."

### John Gill:
1. "John did not preach before the coming of Christ in the flesh, being born but half a year before him; but before he came forth and was manifest to Israel; before he entered upon his work and office, as a prophet and teacher of the people..."
2. "This points out the business of John the Baptist, who was the forerunner of Christ, and went before his face to prepare his way, who gave notice of his coming, and bore testimony to him..."
3. "He not only administered the ordinance of baptism, but he preached the doctrine of it, opened the nature and design of it, and required repentance and fruits meet for it, in those who came to have it administered to them..."

### John Wesley:
1. "John having first preached — He mentions this, as a thing already known to them."
2. "It gave so loud an alarm to the whole Jewish nation, as could not but be heard of in foreign countries."
