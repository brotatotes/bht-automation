# Acts 13:28 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Though they searched for a reason to condemn Jesus to death, they were unable to find any charge that warranted a punishment of such magnitude. Despite their attempts to produce false witnesses and false accusations, their efforts were in vain. They pressed Pilate relentlessly, pleading for Jesus' execution, as they knew that only the Romans had the authority to carry out capital punishments. However, their failure to find any valid grounds for sentencing Jesus ultimately highlights his innocence and the unjust nature of his crucifixion.

### Henry Alford:
1.  28 .] Not, ‘  though ,’ but rather  because they found no cause:  when they found no cause of death in him, they besought , &c.: see Luke 23:22-23 .

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 28. found no cause of death—though
	they sought it (Matthew 26:59;
	Matthew 26:60).

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They found no crime which deserved death."
2. "The Sanhedrin condemned him for blasphemy; and yet they knew that they could not substantiate the charge before Pilate."
3. "They therefore endeavored to procure his condemnation on the ground of sedition."

### John Calvin:
1. "It was very pertinent to the matter that they should know that Christ was put to death guiltless, for we could not have been justified by his death, if he had suffered death for his own evil deeds..."
2. "...that his death might be a satisfaction for the sins of the world."
3. "Paul did plainly declare that Pilate condemned Christ, not according to the office of a judge, but that he consented that he should be put to death after that he was overcome with the ungodly requests of the people..."

### Philip Schaff:
1. Acts 13:28.  And though they found no cause of death in him.  They accused Jesus of blasphemy and sedition, but were utterly unable to prove either charge.

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The Sanhedrin did charge Jesus with blasphemy, but could not prove it."
2. "Paul knew that Jesus was innocent."
3. "He uses this same idiom about his own innocence."

### John Gill:
1. "And though they found no cause of death in him,.... That is, no crime that deserved death." 
2. "They sought for such, but could find none; they suborned false witnesses, who brought charges against him, but could not support them."
3. "They were urgent and importunate with him, that he would order him to be put to death; the power of life and death being then in the hands of the Romans."
