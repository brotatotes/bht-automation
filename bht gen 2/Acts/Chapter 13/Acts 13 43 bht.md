# Acts 13:43 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The missionaries continued to speak to the Jews and proselytes, encouraging them to hold firm in their faith in Christ and to persevere in the grace of God. This was important because trials and challenges were coming their way, and they needed to be steadfast in their belief. The message of the Gospel had brought about a profound transformation in their lives, and they were urged to remain committed to it. By continuing in the grace of God, they would experience the favor, guidance, and strength that comes from a relationship with Him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "After the breaking up of the synagogue."
2. "To the Jews and proselytes."
3. "The grace of God similarly used of the work of the Gospel begun in the hearts of the converts."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "who, speaking to them—following up the discourse in the synagogue by some further words of encouragement."
2. "persuaded them to continue in the grace of God—which they had experienced through the Gospel."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It would appear from this that they professedly received the truth and embraced the Lord Jesus. This success was remarkable, and shows the power of the gospel when it is preached faithfully to people."
2. "In the grace of God - In his favor - in the faith, and prayer, and obedience which would be connected with his favor."
3. "The 'gospel' is called the grace (favor) of God and they were exhorted to persevere in their attachment to it."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Proselytes [προσηλυτων] . Originally, one who arrives at a place; a stranger; thence of one who comes over to another faith."
2. "Religious [σεβομενων] . Lit., worshipping."
3. "Compare ver. 50 and ch. 16 14."

### John Calvin:
1. "They saw combats prepared for them; and that therefore they had need of invincible constancy to suffer and abide the brunts of the contrary faction."
2. "They sought to encourage Paul and Barnabas to hold on."
3. "They entertained them courteously and gently, and confirmed and strengthened them, that they might persist in the grace which they had received."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "To continue in the grace of God." - No doubt the very words of Paul used to these Jews and proselytes who followed the missionaries to their temporary home in the city.
2. "It was a very favourite expression of his." - It was a very favourite expression of his (see Act 20:24 ; 1 Corinthians 15:10; 2 Corinthians 6:1; Galatians 2:21).

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Apparently Paul and Barnabas had gone out before the synagogue was formally dismissed."
2. "So we are to picture Paul and Barnabas speaking to eager groups."
3. "Paul had great powers of persuasion."

### John Gill:
1. "Many of the Jews and religious proselytes followed Paul and Barnabas; who were converted by this sermon, and believed in Christ, and cordially embraced the doctrines of the Gospel."
2. "they spoke a word of exhortation to them, beseeching them to continue in it, it springing from the grace of God, and being a declaration of it."
3. "particularly in those special doctrines of grace, which had been insisted on, namely, remission of sins, and free justification by the blood of Christ."

### John Wesley:
1. "Persuaded them to continue - For trials were at hand"
2. "In the grace of God - That is, to adhere to the Gospel or Christian faith."
