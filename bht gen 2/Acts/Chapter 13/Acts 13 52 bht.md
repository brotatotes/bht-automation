### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "the disciples... were filled with joy and with the Holy Ghost"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The disciples in Antioch were filled with joy."
2. "This happened even in the midst of persecution."
3. "The gospel is able to fill the soul with joy even in the severest trials."

### John Calvin:
1. "With joy of the Spirit, or (which is all one) with spiritual joy; because there is no quietness, peace, or joy of conscience, but it cometh of the Spirit of God."
2. "The grace of the Holy Spirit reigned in them, which alone doth so make us glad, truly and perfectly, that we are carried up above the whole world."
3. "If our faith shall be well grounded in God, and shall be thoroughly rooted in his word; and, finally, if’ it shall be well fortified with the aid of the Spirit as it ought, it shall nourish peace and joy spiritual in our minds, though all the world be in an uproar."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The members of the Antioch Church, instead of being depressed and disheartened by the enforced departure of their teachers, Paul and Barnabas, conscious of the intense happiness which had now become their inheritance as Christians, were ‘filled with joy and with the Holy Ghost.’"
2. "Chrysostom tells us how the sufferings of a Master, far from discouraging the disciple, gives fresh ardour to his purpose."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Persecution had precisely the opposite effect to the intention of the Jews for they 'were filled with joy and the Holy Spirit'."
2. "The blood of the martyrs is still the seed of the church."

### John Gill:
1. "The apostles rejoiced, both at the success they had met with, and because they were counted worthy to suffer reproach and persecution for the sake of Christ and his Gospel."
2. "The disciples... were filled with joy at the Gospel being preached unto them, and at the constancy and courage of the apostles in suffering for it."
3. "...the Holy Ghost; which... designs the same thing as spiritual joy, or joy in the Holy Ghost; or else the gifts and graces of the Spirit, which they had both for their own comfort, and the advantage of others."
