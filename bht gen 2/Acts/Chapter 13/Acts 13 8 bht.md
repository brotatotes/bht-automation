# Acts 13:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Elymas, whose name means God's counsel, persisted in his opposition to Saul and Barnabas, just as the magicians of Egypt opposed Moses. He was determined to turn the deputy away from the faith, seeking to distort and pervert the doctrine of faith. His motives were driven by self-interest and the fear of losing his privileged position as a soothsayer.

### Henry Alford:
1.  8.   Ἐλύμας ] See above on Acts 13:6 .  διαστρέψαι  …   ἀπό ] A pregnant construction, as ἀπέστησεν ὀπίσω , ch. Acts 5:37 .

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "But Elymas—or 'the wise.' for so is his name by interpretation—the word is from the Arabic."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Elymas is the interpretation, not of the name Bar-jesus, but of the word rendered 'the sorcerer.' It is an Arabic word, and means the same as Magus."
2. "He was sensible that if the influence of Saul and Barnabas should be extended over the proconsul, that he would be seen to be an impostor, and his power be at an end."
3. "The love of popularity and power, the desire of retaining some political influence, is often a strong reason why people oppose the gospel."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The position of soothsayer to a Roman proconsul, even though it could only last a year, was too distinguished and too lucrative to abandon without a struggle."


### John Calvin:
1. "No marvel if the seducer seek to put away the light, whereby he saw his own darkness driven away."
2. "We must wrestle with such lets; for as there be [not] always and everywhere magicians present, which procure us some business, Satan thrusteth in lets enough, which possess our minds to drive away Christ, which the flesh is too ready to receive."
3. "Finally, both the enticements of the world and the wicked affections of our flesh are as many charms, whereby Satan ceaseth not to overthrow the faith."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Elymas is an Arabic word which means the 'wise,' the 'Magian,' or 'magician.'" 
2. "It was evidently self-interest which induced the vagrant Jew to depreciate the stranger missionaries in the eyes of the proconsul."
3. "They, he saw, were in earnest; and he feared with reason, if his patron listened to them, his chances of further gain in Cyprus were gone."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He persisted in his opposition and was unwilling to lose his great prize."
2. "There may have been a public discussion between Elymas and Saul."
3. "To turn aside, first aorist active infinitive of διαστρεφω, old verb to turn or twist in two, to distort, to pervert."

### John Gill:
1. "Elymas is in Hebrew, אלמעץ, 'Elmahatz'; the interpretation of which is, God's counsel, or the counsel of God."
2. "He withstood them...just as Jannes and Jambres, the magicians of Egypt, withstood Moses."
3. "Seeking to turn away the deputy from the faith; the doctrine of faith, from hearing and receiving it."
