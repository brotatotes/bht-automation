# Acts 14:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The unbelieving Jews were responsible for stirring up the Gentiles against the brethren. They influenced the Gentiles' minds and caused them to have negative feelings towards the believers. The Gentiles, who were unaware and lacked faith in this matter, were easily swayed by the Jews' false portrayal of the believers as seditious individuals with malicious intentions.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The past part. indicates who believed not, viz. when Paul preached."  
2. "ἐκάκωσαν, ‘male affecerunt,’ κακούργως διέθηκαν, Chrys. So Jos. Antt. xvi. 1. 2, κακοῦν,.… καὶ τῆς εὐνοίας ἧς εἶχεν εἰς τοὺς παῖδας ἀφαιρεῖν ."  
3. "Act 14:3 gives the sequel of Acts 14:1, Acts 14:4, of Acts 14:2. The μὲν οὖν, as usual (see ch. Act 11:19 ), takes up the narrative which had been interrupted."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "And made their minds evil-affected - Irritated, or exasperated them." 
2. "Against the brethren - One of the common appellations by which Christians were known."

### John Calvin:
1. "They were like firebrands to inflame the minds of the Gentiles"
2. "The godly were vexed and troubled whosoever embraced the gospel, as if some pernicious sect had risen"
3. "They were vexed... to spread discord, to trouble the peace of the city, to shake the public state"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Of the numerous persecutions recorded in the ‘Acts,’ there were only two which were not occasioned by the Jews."
2. "The Jews saw that all those privileges which belonged to the covenant people, and of which they were so jealously proud, would cease altogether to be their peculiar heritage if the Gentiles were admitted on the same terms into the kingdom of God."
3. "To these unhappy men, the thought that ‘believing Jews’ and ‘believing Gentiles’ should constitute one holy brotherhood, was strangely hateful."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "To disbelieve the word of God is to disobey God."
2. "Evidently Paul preached the same message as in Antioch for it won both Jews and Gentiles, and displeased the rabbis."
3. "Certainly the picture of Paul herein drawn cannot be considered authentic though a true tradition may underlie it: 'bald, bowlegged, strongly built, small in stature, with large eyes and meeting eyebrows and longish nose; full of grace; sometimes looking like a man, sometimes having the face of an angel.'"

### John Gill:
1. "But the unbelieving Jews stirred up the Gentiles...and made their minds evil affected against the brethren."
2. "These stirred up the Gentiles, who had no knowledge of, nor faith in this matter."
3. "By representing them as seditious persons, and of bad designs."
