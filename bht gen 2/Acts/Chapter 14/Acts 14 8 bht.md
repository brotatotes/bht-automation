# Acts 14:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This man was born crippled, unable to walk or even stand on his feet. His condition was so weak that he had been that way since birth, making his case seemingly incurable by any human means. The purpose of mentioning these details is to emphasize the impossibility of his situation, thus setting the stage for the remarkable miracle that was about to take place.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "ἐκάθητο] Not ‘dwelt,’ as Kuin., but sat, probably in the forum or some place of resort."
2. "περιεπάτησεν is the historic past: who never walked."
3. "The pluperfect seeming more apt, it has been altered in the later MSS. accordingly."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 8-10. there sat there a certain man
	. . . a cripple from his mother's womb . . . The same heard Paul
	speak—in the open air and ( :-) to a crowd of people.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The word 'sat,' however, indicates his usual posture, his helpless condition."
2. "Such persons commonly sat by the wayside, or in some public place, to ask for alms."
3. "The miracle, therefore, would be more remarkable, as the man would be well known."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The almost universal meaning of the word in the New Testament is impossible."
2. "The sense of weak or impotent occurs only here and Romans 14:1."

### John Calvin:
1. "Luke reckoneth up the circumstances, which do more plainly set forth the power of God, when he saith that the man did never walk, and that he was a cripple even from his mother’s womb, and that he was suddenly healed by the voice of Paul alone before the eyes of all men, and that his legs, which were dead, were made nimble, so that he leapt up without making any stop." 
2. "Luke reciteth one miracle which we may think was one of many; but there was mention made of it alone by reason of the famous event. For we shall see by and by what happened."
3. "− “Probabile est,” it is probable. (15) − “Sine difficultate,” without difficulty."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The incident here related was evidently no very unusual one in the life of these first great missionaries of the faith."
2. "The scene of the healing, no uncommon one, reminds us 'of the manner in which those who carry the message of salvation to the heathen in the present day collect around them groups of listeners in Burmah and Hindostan."
3. "It was on one of these occasions, as Paul was preaching in some thoroughfare of the city, that the lame man heard him: his friends had placed him there perhaps to solicit alms."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The incident narrated here... shows how they got a real hearing among these rude heathen." 
2. "Possibly outside the gate or some public place." 
3. "Common in medical writers in the sense of 'impotent.'"

### John Gill:
1. "This man was impotent in his feet; so weak, as not to be able to walk, and even to stand on them..."
2. "Being a cripple from his mother's womb; he was born lame..."
3. "These circumstances are mentioned, to show that his case was incurable by any human art, and to illustrate the following miracle."
