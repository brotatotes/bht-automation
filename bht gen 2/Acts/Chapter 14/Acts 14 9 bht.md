# Acts 14:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This man, who had been a diligent listener to Paul's preaching, caught the attention of the apostle. Through the gift of spiritual discernment, Paul saw a flicker of faith in his countenance, signaling his readiness to receive healing. The image of this cripple reminded Paul of the miracles performed by the Savior and the present power of God. With the assurance that the man's faith was genuine, Paul, guided by the Holy Spirit, commanded him to rise and walk, witnessing the instantaneous effect of his faith.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "He was listening to Paul’s preaching, and, while listening, his countenance, read by the Apostle’s gift of spiritual discernment, gave token of faith to be healed."
2. "His countenance, read by the Apostle’s gift of spiritual discernment, gave token of faith to be healed."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Paul may have been led by the sight of this cripple to dwell on the Saviour's miracles of healing, and His present power."
2. "Perceiving from the eagerness with which the patient drank in his words, that he was prepared to put his own case into the Redeemer's hands, the Spirit of the glorified Physician came all upon Paul."
3. "The effect was instantaneous - he sprang to his feet 'and walked'."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Fixing his eyes intently on him."
2. "Perhaps it was indicated by the ardor, humility, and strong desire depicted in his countenance."
3. "Had faith to be healed."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Heard [ηκουε] . The force of the imperfect should be given here. He was hearing while Paul preached.

### John Calvin:
1. "But forasmuch as God was determined to show a token of his grace in the cripple, he prepared his mind before, and made him capable of this that should come upon him." 
2. "And this kind of faith is likewise particular which giveth place to miracles, which many of God’s children do want, who are, notwithstanding, indued with the Spirit of adoption."
3. "We know how doubtful and how deceitful a thing the countenance of man is, therefore there could no sure judgment be given thereby of faith, which hath God alone to be witness thereof; but, as I have already said, the cripple’s faith was revealed to Paul by the secret inspiration of the Spirit, as he was to the apostles their only guide and master to work miracles."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Something in the sufferer's face moved him to utter the commanding words which possessed such strange awful power."
2. "The poor helpless man had heard, no doubt, the apostles' public teaching, and was convinced of the reality of the great redemption worked by the Master."
3. "This conviction the apostle read in the upturned face of the afflicted one who lay helpless at his feet."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "This man was 'no mendicant pretender,' for his history was known from his birth." 
2. "Paul saw a new hope in the man's eyes and face." 
3. "He had faith to be made whole."

### John Gill:
1. "He was one of his hearers, and faith came to him by hearing."
2. "Paul beholding the lame man, as the Syriac and Ethiopic versions both express it."
3. "Perceiving that he had faith to be healed; as he might by his looks, his gestures, his attention in hearing, and it may be by somewhat that he said, as well as by revelation, or a spirit of discerning, which he had."

### John Wesley:
1. He had faith to be healed — He felt the power of God in his soul; and thence knew it was sufficient to heal his body also.
