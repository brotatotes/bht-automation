# Acts 15:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
When the Lord had destroyed the sinful kingdom and the Jewish dispensation expired, He promised to return and show favor to them. He declared that He would rebuild the fallen tabernacle of David, which represents the spiritual kingdom or church of Christ. This rebuilding signifies the reviving of true religion and the enlargement of the church of God, bringing forth a spiritual and eternal kingdom through the seed of David, who is Christ.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Simeon—a Hebrew variation of Simon, as in 2 Peter 1:1; (Greek), the Jewish and family name of Peter."
2. "hath declared how God at the first—answering to Peter's own expression 'a good while ago' (Acts 15:7)."
3. "did visit the Gentiles to take out of them—in the exercise of His adorable sovereignty. a people for his name—the honor of his name, or for His glory."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The main point of the quotation, as made by James, was to show that, according to the prophets, it was contemplated that the Gentiles should be introduced to the privileges of the children of God."
2. "This implied that the city of Jerusalem, the temple, and the walls of the city would be destroyed. But after that he would restore them to their former privileges - would rebuild their temple, their city, and their walls."
3. "The tabernacle of David... means the house or royal residence of David and the kings of Israel. That is, he would restore them to their former glory and splendor as his people."

### John Calvin:
1. "After that God hath promised the restoring of the tabernacle of David, he saith also, that he will bring to pass that the Jews shall possess the remnants of Edom...For God placeth the Gentiles in like degree of honor with the Jews, when he will have them to call upon his name."
2. "For if the kingdom of Christ cannot be otherwise established, unless God be called upon everywhere throughout the whole world, and the Gentiles grow together to be one with his holy people, it is an absurd thing that they should be driven from hope of salvation, and the middle wall must fall to the ground."
3. "So Isaiah saith, that there shall a branch arise out of the contemptible and base stock...that God doth observe this wonderful way in restoring the Church, that he doth build it up, when it is decayed."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "This Amos prophecy speaks first of the fall of the Jewish Church, and the abolition of its temple service."
2. "God will build a new church on the ruins of the old, and gather together in it all the Gentiles."
3. "Amos declares in these words ‘that the true restoration of the tabernacle of David is to be found in the reception of the residue of the human family, and in the flowing in of all nations, whether Jew or Gentile, into the Church of Christ."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Compound (ανα, up or again) of οικοδομεω, the verb used by Jesus in Matthew 16:18 of the general church or kingdom as here which see."
2. "The tabernacle of David, a poetical figure of the throne of David, now 'the fallen tent' (την πεπτωκυιαν), perfect active participle of πιπτω, state of completion."
3. "It is a desolate picture of the fallen, torn down tent of David."

### John Gill:
1. "After the Lord had destroyed the sinful kingdom from off the face of the earth...he promises to return and show favor to them..."
2. "The tabernacle of David designs the spiritual kingdom or church of Christ...in a fallen ruinous condition when he came on earth..."
3. "Christ's kingdom is not a worldly one; the raising up and rebuilding of this tabernacle must design the reviving of true religion, the doctrine and practice of it, the enlargement of the church of God..."

### John Wesley:
1. "After the Jewish dispensation expires."
2. "I will build again the fallen tabernacle of David."
3. "By raising from his seed the Christ, who shall build on the ruins of his fallen tabernacle a spiritual and eternal kingdom."
