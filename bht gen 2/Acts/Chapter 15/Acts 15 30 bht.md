The church at Antioch sent Paul and Barnabas, so it was fitting for them to convene and provide an update on their mission. They delivered the epistle, entrusting it to a member of the congregation for reading. The entire church, the gathering of believers, was involved in this process.

### John Calvin:
1. "For if there fall out any controversy in the doctrine of faith, it is meet that the judgment be referred over unto the learned and godly, and to such as are exercised in the Scripture." 
2. "Notwithstanding, because it belongeth to all alike to know for a surety what they must hold, the godly and learned teachers must make known to the whole Church what they have set down out of the word of God."
3. "There must be a mean observed, that lawful governments may continue; and that, on the other side, the people may have that liberty which unto them belongeth, lest they be oppressed like slaves."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "So when they were dismissed. These words probably imply a formal and solemn leave-taking on the part of the Jerusalem Church, accompanied with certain religious services."


### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "When they were dismissed (απολυθεντες). First aorist passive participle of απολυω, common verb to loosen, to dismiss."
2. "Possibly (Hackett) religious services were held as in verse Acts 15:33 (cf. Acts 13:3) and perhaps an escort for part of the way as in verse Acts 15:3."
3. "The multitude (το πληθος). Public meeting of the church as in verses Acts 15:1-3."

### John Gill:
1. "Paul and Barnabas were sent by the church at Antioch...it was highly reasonable, and necessary, that they should be called together, and an account of the business they were sent upon be given them..."
2. "They delivered the epistle...this they put into the hands of some one or other of the brethren of the church, in order to be read."
3. "The whole church...the company, or congregation of the faithful."
