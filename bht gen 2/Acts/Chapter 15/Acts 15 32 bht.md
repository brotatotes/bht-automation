# Acts 15:32 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Judas and Silas, who were also prophets themselves, had the ability to explain the prophecies of the Old Testament and provide the true understanding of the Scriptures for the edification of others. They exhorted the brethren with extensive and meaningful teachings, comforting them and providing them with ample instructions and exhortations. Furthermore, they confirmed the believers in the doctrines of the Gospel, specifically reinforcing their Christian liberty and freedom from the law of Moses, which had been undermined by false teachers.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  32. ]  προφ  .   ὄντ  . gives the reason for their superadding to the appointed business of their mission the work of exhorting and edifying.
	On προφ ., see ch. Acts 11:27 ; Acts 13:1 ; Ephesians 2:20 , and notes.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Judas and Silas, being prophets themselves—that is, inspired teachers."
2. "exhorted the brethren with many words—'much discourse.'"
3. "and confirmed them—opening up, no doubt, the great principle involved in the controversy now settled, of gratuitous salvation, or the purification of the heart by faith alone."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Being prophets - See the notes on Acts 11:27. This evidently implies that they had been preachers before they went to Antioch."
2. "What was the precise nature of the office of a prophet in the Christian church it is not easy to ascertain."
3. "Confirmed them - Strengthened them; that is, by their instructions and exhortations."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Many words... adding the spoken to the written consolation."
2. "Exhorted... the latter agrees better with consolation there."
3. "Confirmed."

### John Calvin:
1. "Judas and Silas were endued with excellent knowledge and understanding of the mysteries of God, that they might be good interpreters of God."
2. "Luke's meaning is, that Judas and Silas were prophets, not in the sense of foretelling the future, but in their ability to edify the Church through teaching, exhortation, and consolation."
3. "We must beware, lest any man pass his bounds; as Paul teacheth...that every one keep himself within the measure of grace received."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "In that age, when the foundation-stones of the mighty temple of Christianity were being laid in so many lands, that hundreds, it may possibly have been thousands of inspired men were helping forward the Master’s work."
2. "The prophets possessed a strange, winning power of words, which had a weighty effect on their hearers. They were, then, earnest, impassioned preachers, who possessed a supernatural insight into the hearts of men."
3. "Among the influences at work in those first years of care and anxiety, when Christianity, struggling against the opposition of the whole world, still advanced and ever advanced with strange, resistless power, unaided by any human help, must be reckoned the Divine gift of prophecy in this extended sense."


### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Being themselves also prophets."
2. "Confirmed."
3. "It was a glorious time with no Judaizers to disturb their fellowship."

### John Gill:
1. "And Judas and Silas being also prophets themselves... such who were able to explain the prophecies of the Old Testament, give the true sense of the Scriptures, and open them to the edification of others." 
2. "Beza's ancient copy adds, 'full of the Holy Ghost': and exhorted the brethren with many words... with copiousness, fulness, and abundance of Gospel truths, with which they comforted the brethren, giving them many useful instructions and exhortations."
3. "and confirmed them; in the doctrines of the Gospel, and particularly in their Christian liberty, and freedom from the law of Moses, in which the false teachers had endeavored to stagger them."
