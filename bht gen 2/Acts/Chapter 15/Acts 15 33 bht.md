After completing their mission, Judas and Silas were let go in peace. The members of the church expressed their heartfelt gratitude for the service they had provided and wished them well. The departure of Judas and Silas marked a time of tranquility and unity within the church. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "On every account it is probable that the words forming this verse in rec. (see var. readd.) are an interpolation."
2. "The αὐτοῦ is αὐ  τούς in C and D, and αὐτοῖς and αὐτόθι in some cursives; and D and the Vulg. add μόνος δὲ Ἰούδ . ἐπορεύθη."
3. "We learn from Galatians 2:10, that a condition was attached to the cordiality with which the Gentile mission of Paul and Barnabas was recognized by the chief Apostles: that they should remember the poor, i.e. the poor at Jerusalem."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 33. were let go in peace—with
	peace, as the customary parting salutation.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They were let go in peace - An expression implying that they departed with the affectionate regard of the Christians to whom they had ministered, and with their highest wishes for their prosperity."
2. "A space - For some time."
3. "Many mss., however, instead of 'unto the apostles,' read 'unto those who had sent them.' The sense is not materially different."

### John Calvin:
1. "They were let go in peace...the brethren...did wish them well, as friends use to do." 
2. "The Church was quiet before they thought upon any returning." 
3. "They had all one and the same desire without grudging, so that they joined hand in hand to do good."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "They were dismissed with peace."
2. "Judas and Silas both returned to Jerusalem to give account of their mission at Antioch."
3. "Silas returned soon to Antioch to be with Paul; won over, no doubt, to a deep admiration of the single-hearted apostle by his earnestness and fervour."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  Some time  (χρονον). Accusative after ποιησαντες, "having done time." How long we do not know. 

### John Gill:
1. "They were dismissed, and went away with the leave and consent of the church; who returned them thanks for the service and good offices they had done them, and wished them all peace, prosperity, and happiness."
2. "As they were dismissed in peace, they left the church in perfect peace; an end being put to the controversy about circumcision, which was raised among them; and the members of the church being agreed in their sentiments, and heartily united in their affections to one another."
3. "And thus Judas and Silas were honourably dismissed from the brethren, at Antioch, to the Apostles, at Jerusalem; [...] it was proper that they should return to them, and give them an account of the success of it, how it was received, and what good it had done."
