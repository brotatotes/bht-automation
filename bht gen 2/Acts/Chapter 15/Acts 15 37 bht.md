### Albert Barnes:
1. "And Barnabas determined - Greek: willed, or was disposed to."
2. "He had been with them before as a traveling companion."
3. "Barnabas’ affection for his nephew was the main reason for inducing him to wish to take him with him in the journey."

### John Calvin:
1. "They had, by many experiences, tried [felt] the excellent favor of God, yea, that wonderful success mentioned heretofore by Luke was a manifest blessing of God."
2. "Surely he ought to have borne with such a holy and courageous woman, lest immoderate rigor should alienate her."
3. "For if, in a matter of no weight or edification, a man vaunt of his constancy, prepare himself for the conflict, and cease not to defend that until the end, wherein he did once take delight it shall be but foolish and perverse obstinacy."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The old kindness of heart which prompted him in old days to seek out Saul, the former persecutor of the followers of Jesus, and to plead his cause with the Jewish Christian leaders at Jerusalem, now induced him to forget Mark’s former faint-heartedness, and to welcome him again as a fellow-labourer in the Master’s cause."
2. "In the all-seeing wisdom of God, the stern severity of Paul and the gentle love of Barnabas, on the one side seem to have deeply humbled, and on the other to have preserved from despondency, the hitherto weak and vacillating spirit of the young disciple, who became, under the tutelage of Barnabas, subsequently one of the brave Christian leaders of the first days."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Barnabas willed, wished and stuck to it."
2. "Συνπαραλαβειν is second aorist active infinitive of the double compound συνπαραλαμβανω, old verb to take along together with, used already about John Mark in Acts 12:25 and by Paul in Galatians 2:1 about Titus."
3. "Nowhere else in the N.T. Barnabas used the ingressive aorist in his suggestion."

### John Gill:
1. "And Barnabas determined,.... Or consulted, and contrived in his own mind, and purposed within himself..."
2. "he had a mind, a very great desire, to take with them John, whose surname was Mark..."
3. "him they brought with them to Antioch, when they returned from Jerusalem thither, where they had been sent by the church at Antioch, with money for the relief of the poor saints..."

### John Wesley:
1. Barnabas counselled to take John — His kinsman.
