# Acts 15:39 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Sometimes even the strongest of believers can have disagreements, as was the case with Paul and Barnabas. Despite their sharp contention, they were able to reconcile and move forward in their ministry. This incident reminds us to be careful in our judgments and to not let anger and heated disagreements hinder our work for God. In the end, both Paul and Barnabas continued to serve the Lord in different ways, leaving a remarkable impact on the spread of the Gospel.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Barnabas appears to have gone with his cousin without any special sympathy or approval; whereas Paul was commended to the grace of God by the assembled church."
2. "We find Mark afterwards received into favour by Paul."
3. "In the former of those places it would seem as if he was dependent for his reception on Paul’s special commendation."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "that John Mark did retrieve his character in these respects, and a reconciliation took place between Paul and him, so cordial that the apostle expresses more than once the confidence he had in him and the value he set upon his services"
2. "each of these great servants of Christ had something to say for himself, in defense of the position which they respectively took up"
3. "how watchful does all this teach Christians, and especially Christian ministers and missionaries, to be against giving way to rash judgment and hot temper towards each other"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The word used here παροξυσμός paroxusmos is that from which our word 'paroxysm' is derived."
2. "It may denote 'any excitement of mind,' and is used in a good sense in Hebrews 10:24."
3. "It here means, however, 'a violent altercation' that resulted in their separation for a time, and in their engaging in different spheres of labor."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The contention was so sharp [εγενετο παροξυσμος] . More correctly, there arose a sharp contention." 
2. "An angry dispute is indicated." 
3. "Barnabas. The last mention of him in the Acts."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "However, if the two old friends and devoted servants of God parted in anger, they soon forgot all bitterness" 
2. "We know Mark became once more the loved and trusted companion of Paul" 
3. "Subsequently, church historians relate how Mark founded (probably organized) the Church of Alexandria, and became its bishop, and there endured a martyr’s death."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "It is often so that the little irritations of life give occasion to violent explosions."
2. "But there is no way to settle differences about men and we can only agree to disagree as Paul and Barnabas did."
3. "One's judgment may go with Paul, but one's heart goes with Barnabas."

### John Gill:
1. "And the contention was so sharp between them...and were so warmed and heated on both sides..." 
2. "as soon almost as peace was made in the church, a difference arises among the ministers of the word, who are men of like passions with others..." 
3. "this affair was overruled by the providence of God, for the spread of his Gospel, and the enlargement of his interest..."

### John Wesley:
1. "St. Paul, who had the right on his side, as he undoubtedly had, maintained it with love."
2. "Forsaking the work in which he was engaged, [Barnabas] went away to his own country."
