# Acts 15:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
On their arrival, they received an official reception, being welcomed cordially into the community of believers in Jerusalem. Although unsure of the reception they would receive, they were warmly received, with the church acknowledging them as Christian brethren. In private meetings with the chief apostles, they were able to share their experiences and explain the remarkable conversion of the Gentiles, thus paving the way for their support and unity in spreading the Gospel. They were grateful for the opportunity to declare all that God had done through their ministry, to the glory of His name.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "On their arrival at Jerusalem, there seems to have taken place an official reception of them and their message, in public."
2. "Paul did not lay before the whole assembly the Gospel which he preached among the Gentiles, viz. the indifference of the Mosaic law to their salvation, for fear of its being hastily disparaged or repudiated, and so his work being hindered."
3. "In private interviews with the chief Apostles, James, Peter, and John, he did unfold the whole freeness of this Gospel, and so effectually, as to prepare the way for their full and public accordance with him at the council."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "This was Paul's THIRD VISIT TO JERUSALEM after his conversion..."
2. "...the deputation being one so influential, and from a church of such note."
3. "...they declared all things that God had done with them."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They were received of the church - By the church, in a hospitable and friendly manner."
2. "They were acknowledged as Christian brethren, and received with Christian kindness."
3. "And they declared - Paul and Barnabas, and those with them. That is, they stated the case; the remarkable conversion of the Gentiles, the evidence of their piety, and the origin of the present dispute."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Were received [προπεμφθεντες] . The word implies a cordial welcome, which they were not altogether sure of receiving.

### John Calvin:
1. "Furthermore, because the apostles had no certain place of abode at Jerusalem, but went ever now and then sometimes to one place and sometimes to another, whithersoever occasion did call them, that church had elders to whom the ordinary government of the Church was committed."
2. "And hereby it appeareth what brotherly courtesy there was in the apostles and elders, because they do not only courteously receive Paul and Barnabas, but so soon as they hear what success they had with their pains they took, they magnify the grace of God."
3. "Luke repeateth again that form of speech which we had before in the chapter next going before, when he saith, that they declared whatsoever things God had done with them."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The word translated here 'were received,' implies a cordial reception on the part of the apostles and elders of the Jerusalem community."
2. "They welcomed with affection Barnabas and Paul as the great missionaries of the faith."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "This first public meeting is referred to by Paul in Galatians 2:2 'I set before them (αυτοις) the gospel, etc.'"
2. "At [the reception], an opportunity was given to hear the story of Paul and Barnabas about God's dealings with them among the Gentiles."
3. "It was a public reception for Paul and Barnabas provided by the whole church including the apostles and elders."

### John Gill:
1. "They were received into their houses, and accommodated with everything convenient for them." 
2. "James, and Cephas, and John, who gave to Paul and Barnabas the right hand of fellowship, in token of their approbation of them, affection for them, and agreement with them."
3. "They declared all things that God had done with them."

### John Wesley:
1. They were received — That is solemnly welcomed.
