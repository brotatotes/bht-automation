# Acts 16:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The churches were strengthened and established in the faith, as the wise and conciliatory measures of Paul, Silas, and Timothy had a tremendous impact on the growth and stability of these communities. This strengthening was not only external, but it also had a profound effect on the belief and conviction of the believers. The establishment in the faith was a result of the continuous work and blessing of God, as the churches increased in number daily and experienced growth in both gifts and grace.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  5. ] This general notice, with μὲν οὖν , like those at ch. Acts 9:31 , Acts 12:24 , marks the opening of a new section.

### Albert Barnes:
1. Established in the faith - Confirmed in the belief of the gospel The effect of the wise and conciliatory measure was to increase and strengthen the churches.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Were established [εστερεουντο] . Rather, were strengthened. Another word is used for established. See ch. Acts 14:22; Acts 14:32, Acts 14:41; Acts 18:23. There is a difference, moreover, between being strengthened and established. See 1 Peter verse 10.

### John Calvin:
1. "Paul did so order external things, that he was principally careful for the kingdom of God, which consisteth in the doctrine of the gospel, and doth far surpass and surmount external order."
2. "Religion and godliness hath the former place, whose sole foundation is faith; which again doth stay itself upon the pure Word of God, and doth not depend upon men’s laws."
3. "The more those profit in faith who are first called, the more do they bring unto Christ; as if faith did creep abroad unto others by branches."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "A great bar to the acceptance of the gospel by the mass of Gentiles had been permanently removed."
2. "Established in the faith, and increased in number daily."
3. "Rarum incrementum, numero simul et gradu."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Were strengthened (εστερεουντο). Imperfect passive of στερεοω, old verb to make firm and solid like the muscles (Acts 3:7; Acts 3:16), these three the only examples in the N.T."
2. "Increased (επερισσευον). Imperfect active of the old and common verb περισσευω from περισσος (overplus)."
3. "The blessing of God was on the work of Paul, Silas, and Timothy in the form of a continuous revival."

### John Gill:
1. "And so were the churches established in the faith."
2. "Both in the grace and doctrine of faith."
3. "Increased in number daily, as well as in gifts and grace."
