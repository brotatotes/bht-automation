## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The two enemies it has ever had to contend with are the two ruling principles of the Epicureans and Stoics—Pleasure and Pride."
2. "What will this babbler say?—The word, which means 'a picker-up of seeds,' bird-like, is applied to a gatherer and retailer of scraps of knowledge, a prater; a general term of contempt for any pretended teacher."
3. "because he preached Jesus and the resurrection—Not as if they thought he made these to be two divinities: the strange gods were Jehovah and the Risen Saviour, ordained to judge the world."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Certain strange things - Literally, something pertaining to a foreign country or people."
2. "Here it means something unusual or remarkable - something different from what they had been accustomed to hear from their philosophers."
3. "What these things mean - We would understand more clearly what is affirmed respecting Jesus and the resurrection."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Strange [ξενιζοντα] . A participle : surprising. Compare 1 Peter 4:4, 1 Peter 4:12.

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "For thou bringest certain strange things (ξενιζοντα γαρ τινα εισφερεις). The very verb used by Xenophon (Mem. I) about Socrates."
2. "Ξενιζοντα is present active neuter plural participle of ξενιζω and from ξενος (verse Acts 17:18), 'things surprising or shocking us.'"
3. "We would know therefore (βουλομεθα ουν γνωνα). Very polite still, we wish or desire, and repeating γνωνα (the essential point)."

### John Gill:
1. "Strange doctrines and strange deities, such as they had never heard of before."
2. "They desire he would explain these things to them, and let them know the rise, and ground, and nature, and end, and design of them."
