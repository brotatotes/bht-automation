# Acts 17:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
After receiving the necessary reassurances and explanations from Jason and the others, the magistrates were satisfied and released them. It became clear to the authorities that the teachings of Paul and Silas were not meant to incite rebellion or disrupt the existing order. Hence, no further action was taken against them, and they were free to resume their daily lives without any penalties or imprisonment.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "They bound over Jason and the rest (τινας ἀδελφούς, Act 17:6) to take care that no more trouble was given by these men."
2. "In accordance with which security they sent them away; and by night, to avoid the notice of the ὄχλος."
3. "Satisdatione accepta; either by sureties, or by a sum of money, or both."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "And when they had taken security of Jason and of the other, making them deposit a money pledge that the preachers should not again endanger the public peace."


### Albert Barnes:
1. "This is an expression taken from courts, and means that Jason and the other gave satisfaction to the magistrates for the good conduct of Paul and Silas, or became responsible for it."
2. "The sense is, that they did it in accordance with the Roman usages, and gave sufficient security for the good conduct of Paul and Silas."
3. "Heuman supposes that the pledge given was that they should leave the city. Michaelis thinks that they gave a pledge that they would no more harbor them; but if they returned again to them, they would deliver them to the magistrates."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Security [το ικανον] . See on Luke 7:6. Bail, either personal or by a deposit of money."
2. "A law term."
3. "They engaged that the public peace should not be violated, and that the authors of the disturbance should leave the city."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The ‘security’ was most probably a sum of money deposited by Jason, who appears to have been a person of substance, as were very likely some of the others."
2. "These converts among the early Christians in these great Grecian cities were by no means all drawn from the poorer classes."
3. "The purpose of this security was to assure the magistrates that there should be nothing done by these eastern strangers contrary to the decrees of Cæsar."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "A Greek idiom=Latin satis accipere, to receive the sufficient (bond), usually money for the fulfilment of the judgment."
2. "The idiom λαμβανειν το ικανον now is found in two inscriptions of the second century A.D."
3. "The charge was serious but the proof slim so that the politarchs were glad to be rid of the case."

### John Gill:
1. "Received satisfaction from them, by the defence which they made for themselves, and the apostles, by the account that they gave of them and their doctrines."
2. "Whereby it plainly appeared to the full satisfaction of the magistrates, that their principles had no tendency to move sedition or to alter the form of their government."
3. "They let them go; about their business, to their own houses, and company, and did not inflict any punishment upon them, or commit them to prison."
