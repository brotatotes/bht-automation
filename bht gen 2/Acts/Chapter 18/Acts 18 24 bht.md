## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "An intelligent Jew educated in that city could hardly fail to manifest all these elements in his mental character." 
2. "His eloquence enabling him to express clearly and enforce skillfully what, as a Jew, he had gathered from a diligent study of the Old Testament Scriptures." 
3. "On what errand is not known."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Apollos afterward became a distinguished and successful preacher of the gospel, 1Co 1:12; 1 Corinthians 3:5-6; 1 Corinthians 4:6; Titus 3:13."
2. "An eloquent man... it is probable that Apollos, in addition to his natural endowments, had enjoyed the benefit of these schools."
3. "Mighty in the scriptures - Well instructed, or able in the Old Testament. The foundation was thus laid for future usefulness in the Christian church."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The word [eloquent] is used in Greek literature in several senses. As logov means either reason or speech, so this derivative may signify either one who has thought much, and has much to say, or one who can say it well."
2. "Hence it is used: 1. Of one skilled in history. 2. Of an eloquent person. 3. Of a learned person generally."
3. "There seems hardly sufficient reason for changing the rendering of the A. V. (Rev., learned), especially as the scripture-learning of Apollos is specified in the words mighty in the scriptures, and his superior eloquence appears to have been the reason why some of the Corinthians preferred him to Paul."

### John Calvin:
1. "Luke giveth him first two titles of commendation, that he was eloquent and mighty in the Scriptures; afterward he will add his zeal, faith, and constancy."
2. "But as the distribution of the gifts of the Spirit is divers and manifold, Paul’s infancy, that I may so call it, did no whit let but that the Lord might choose to himself eloquent ministers."
3. "And this (in my judgment) is rather the praise of the Scripture than of man, that it hath sufficient force both to defend the truth, and also to refute the subtilty of Satan."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The writer of the 'Acts,' under the direction of the Holy Ghost, no doubt selected this little episode to show how the disciples of John the Baptist, widely scattered evidently, and perhaps fairly numerous, were won to that broad, worldwide school of teaching."
2. "What Priscilla and Aquila took upon themselves to do in their master’s absence, no doubt many another of the apostle’s pupils undertook, and with like success."
3. "Apollos, a native of Alexandria and a disciple of the Baptist or one of his followers, had been no doubt a hearer, possibly a pupil, of the great Alexandrian teacher Philo, and had come some time in Paul’s Third Missionary Journey to Ephesus."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Apollos was undoubtedly a man of the schools and a man of parts."
2. "Apollos was doubtless both learned (mighty in the Scriptures) and eloquent, though eloquence varies greatly in people's ideas."
3. "Being powerful (δυνατος verbal of δυναμα and same root as δυναμις, dynamite, dynamo) in the Scriptures (in the knowledge and the use of the Scriptures), as should be true of every preacher."

### John Gill:
1. "And a certain Jew named Apollos... an eloquent man; in speech, as well as learned, wise, and 'prudent'..."
2. "...and mighty in the Scriptures; of the Old Testament, particularly in the prophecies of them concerning the Messiah..."
3. "...he had thoroughly read them, and carefully examined them, and could readily cite them; as well as had great knowledge of them, and was capable of explaining them..."

### John Wesley:
1. An eloquent man, mighty in the Scriptures — Of the Old Testament. Every talent may be of use in the kingdom of God, if joined with the knowledge of the Scriptures and fervour of spirit.
