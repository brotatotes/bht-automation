# Acts 19:22 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Paul sent Timothy and Erastus to Macedonia, specifically to Philippi, Thessalonica, or Berea, in order to inform the believers there about his upcoming visit and to make preparations. Timothy, a faithful disciple of Paul, was chosen to accompany him on this mission, as he had done in the past. Meanwhile, Paul remained in Asia, particularly in Ephesus, for a period of time, continuing his work and ministry there.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "He intended himself to follow after Pentecost." 
2. "The object of it was to bring these churches in Macedonia and Achaia into remembrance of the ways and teaching of Paul." 
3. "Asia is named by way of contrast with Macedonia, just before mentioned."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "So he sent into Macedonia . . .
	Timotheus and Erastus—as his pioneers, in part to bring 'them
	into remembrance of his ways which were in Christ', partly to convey his
	mind on various matters." (1 Corinthians 4:17; 1 Corinthians 16:10)
2. "It is very unlikely that this Erastus was 'the
	chamberlain of the city' of Corinth, of that name." (1 Corinthians 16:10)
3. "he himself stayed in—the
	province of 
	Asia for a season—that
	is, at Ephesus, its chief city. (Asia is mentioned in contrast with
	Macedonia in the previous clause)."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He was a proper person to send there to visit the churches."
2. "He was, therefore, a very proper person to be sent with Timothy for the purpose of making the collection for the poor at Jerusalem."
3. "Paul had wisdom enough to employ a man accustomed to monied transactions in making a collection."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "From a passage in that epistle, we learn some of the reasons why one of these two friends of Paul was sent over into Europe before his master."
2. "Erastus was most likely the same as the person alluded to in Romans 16:23 as the chamberlain of Corinth, and was not improbably chosen as the companion of Timothy on this difficult and delicate mission."
3. "The tumult evidently cut short this renewed period of Paul’s activity, and he seems to have left Ephesus and his work there with some precipitancy."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Paul had sent Timothy to Corinth and had requested kindly treatment of this young minister in his difficult task of placating the divided church."
2. "Timothy and Erastus go on to Macedonia to prepare the way for Paul who will come on later."
3. "The reason for Paul's delay is given by him in 1 Corinthians 16:8, the great door wide open in Ephesus."

### John Gill:
1. "So he sent into Macedonia,.... To Philippi, or Thessalonica, or Berea, or some of the places there, to let them know of his coming, and to prepare for him."
2. "Timotheus and Erastus; the former of these was a disciple he found at Lystra, and took along with him, and to whom he afterwards wrote two epistles."
3. "But he himself stayed in Asia for a season; that is, at Ephesus, as the following narration shows."
