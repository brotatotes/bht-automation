# Acts 2:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Peter, along with the eleven other apostles, stepped forward boldly to address their accusers and defend themselves against the false charges. With utmost sobriety and courage, Peter, as their spokesman, fearlessly proclaimed the truth, lifting up his voice and addressing the crowd in their own language. By speaking in Hebrew, he ensured that his message was clear to all the native dwellers of Jerusalem, reminding them of the undeniable presence and power of the Holy Spirit in their midst.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Peter and the eleven come forward from the great body of believers."
2. "He distinguishes... himself and the eleven from the rest."
3. "The Jews, properly so called: native dwellers in Jerus."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 14-21. Peter, standing up with the
	eleven—in advance, perhaps, of the rest.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "No ridicule should deter Christians from an honest avowal of their opinions, and a defense of the operations of the Holy Spirit."
2. "When the enemies of religion deride us or the gospel, we should answer them kindly and respectfully, yet firmly. We should reason with them coolly, and convince them of their error."
3. "The design of Peter was to vindicate the conduct of the apostles from the reproach of intoxication; to show that this could be no other than the work of God; and to make an application of the truth to his hearers."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "This most solemn, earnest, yet sober speech" (Bengel).
2. "Hearken [ενωτισασθε]. Only here in New Testament. From ejn, in; and ouv, the ear. Rev., give ear."
3. "Words [ρηματα]."

### John Calvin:
1. "he gathereth that Christ is already revealed and given by the gift of the Holy Ghost, which they saw."
2. "Peter saith, that it is no likely thing. Those which have but even small skill in antiquity do know that the civil day, from the rising of the sun until the going down thereof, was divided into twelve hours"
3. "when he saith that that is now come to pass which was foretold, he toucheth briefly their unthankfulness, because they do not acknowledge such an excellent benefit promised unto them in times past which they now see with their eyes."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "More eagerly than the rest, he rushes forth to bear witness of Christ, and to confound his adversaries with the doctrine of the resurrection." 
2. "The same Peter, for whom we had wept when denying Christ, is seen and admired preaching Him."
3. "That tongue, which at the sound of one was driven to denial, now inspires many thousand enemies to confess Christ. This was the work of the Holy Spirit."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Took his stand with the eleven including Matthias, who also rose up with them, and spoke as their spokesman, a formal and impressive beginning."
2. "The large crowd and the confusion of tongues demanded loud speaking. 'This most solemn, earnest, yet sober speech' (Bengel)."
3. "People's ears differ greatly, but in public speech they have to be reached through the ear. That puts an obligation on the speaker and also on the auditors who should sit where they can hear with the ears which they have, an obligation often overlooked."

### John Gill:
1. "But Peter standing up with the eleven... Their number being now complete... these all at once rose up, as abhorring the fact they were charged with, and to show the falsehood of it, and to vindicate themselves..."
2. "Peter, as their mouth, stood 'in the midst' of them, with great courage, boldness, and intrepidity of mind..."
3. "Being endued with the Spirit from on high, he was fearless of men, who but a little while ago was frightened by a servant maid."

### John Wesley:
1. "Then Peter standing up — All the gestures, all the words of Peter, show the utmost sobriety."
2. "lifted up his voice - With cheerfulness and boldness; and said to them."
3. "Men of Judea — That is, ye that are born in Judea. St. Peter spoke in Hebrew, which they all understood."
