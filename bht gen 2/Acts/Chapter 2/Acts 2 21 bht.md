# Acts 2:21 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Whosoever shall call on the name of the Lord shall be saved. This promise signifies the establishment of the economy of salvation, which follows the dissolution of the Jewish state. Calling upon the Lord is a universal invitation, open to all who believe and have the faith to reach out to Him in prayer. Salvation is not an arduous task, but rather a simple act of calling upon God in trust and surrender. Through this act, we are delivered from the many perils and traps of life, experiencing a spiritual and everlasting salvation by the grace of Jesus Christ.

### John Calvin:
1. "For we must diligently note this circumstance. If God should promise salvation simply, it were a great matter; but it is a far greater when as he promiseth the same amidst manifold dungeons of death."
