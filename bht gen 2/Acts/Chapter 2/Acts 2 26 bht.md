# Acts 2:26 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Psalmist found immense joy and gladness in his heart and tongue because of his unwavering trust in God's truth, faithfulness, and power. His flesh, representing his entire being, would find rest in the confident hope of eternal life. This hope provided a secure and peaceful resting place, shielding him from the corruption of the grave. The Psalmist's words remind us of the profound joy that comes from having a steadfast faith in God's presence and protection.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "my glory:"
2. "I will give praise with the best member that I have."
3. "Cf. also Psalms 57:8."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The prospect of future glory and triumph should sustain us amidst all afflictions, and make us ready, like him, to lie down in even the corruptions of the grave."
2. "Because God is near me in time of calamity, and will support and deliver me, I will not be agitated or fear, but will exult in the prospect of the future, in view of the 'joy that is set before me.'"
3. "The death of Christians is also in the New Testament represented as a sleep, and as repose... They will lie in the grave under the assurance of a happy resurrection."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "My flesh shall encamp on hope; pitch its tent there to rest through the night of death, until the morning of resurrection."
2. "Lit., on hope: resting on the hope of resurrection; his body being poetically conceived as hoping."

### John Calvin:
1. "Joy of the soul, gladness of the tongue, and quietness of all the whole body, do ensue upon sure hope and confidence."
2. "That sure trust which we repose in God doth not only deliver us from carefulness, but doth also replenish our hearts with wonderful joy and gladness."
3. "The rest of the flesh doth signify the quietness of the whole man, which we have through the protection of God."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Therefore did my heart rejoice."
2. "And my tongue was glad."
3. "Moreover also my flesh shall rest in hope."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Was glad...rejoiced." 
2. "Shall dwell...shall tabernacle, pitch a tent, make one's abode."
3. "In hope...on hope, the hope of the resurrection."

### John Gill:
1. "Because that he had always the truth, faithfulness, and power of God in his view, and the presence and protection of God with him."
2. "My tongue was glad...because it is both the glory of man, for that being endowed with the faculty of speaking, gives him a glory above the brute creatures."
3. "Moreover also my flesh shall rest in hope, or 'safely'; meaning, that his body should lie quietly in the grave, as in its resting place from all toil and labour, pains and sorrows, and be secure from worms, or any corruption."
