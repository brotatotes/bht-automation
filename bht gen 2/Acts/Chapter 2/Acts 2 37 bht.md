# Acts 2:37 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The repentance of those who were pricked in their heart was genuine and sincere. They recognized their guilt and were filled with remorse for their past actions. They saw the need to do something to make amends for their sins and to seek forgiveness and favor with God. The word of God had a powerful impact on them, piercing their hearts and revealing the depth of their wrongdoing. They acknowledged the apostles as their brethren, signifying a newfound unity and reconciliation with the believers.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The compunction arose from the thought that they had rejected and crucified Him who was now so powerful, and under whose feet they, as enemies, would be crushed."
2. "Concionis fructum Lucas refert, ut sciamus non modo in linguarum varietate exsertam fuisse Spiritus Sancti virtutem, sed in eorum etiam cordibus qui audiebant." Calvin.
3. "ποιήσωμεν, the deliberative subjunctive, cf. Winer, edn. 6, § 41, a. 4, b. What most we do?"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "pricked in their hearts—the begun fulfilment of Zechariah 12:10, whose full accomplishment is reserved for the day when 'all Israel shall be saved' "
2. "what shall we do?—This is that beautiful spirit of genuine compunction and childlike docility, which, discovering its whole past career to have been one frightful mistake, seeks only to be set right for the future, be the change involved and the sacrifices required what they may."
3. "So Saul of Tarsus (Acts 9:6)."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "There was no fanaticism in his discourse; it was cool, close, pungent reasoning."
2. "They were suddenly and deeply affected with anguish and alarm at what Peter had said."
3. "The state of mind in this case - the case of a convicted sinner - consists in: (3) An earnest wish, amounting sometimes to agony, to be delivered from this sense of condemnation and this apprehension of the future."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The word does not occur in profane Greek."
2. "The radical idea of the word is given in the simple verb nussw, to prick with a sharp point."
3. "Here, therefore, of the sharp, painful emotion, the sting produced by Peter's words."

### John Calvin:
1. "This is the beginning of repentance, this is the entrance unto godliness, to be sorry for our sins, and to be wounded with the feeling of our miseries."
2. "For so long as men are careless, they cannot take such heed unto doctrine as they ought."
3. "We must take a good heart to us, and lift up our mind with this hope of salvation, that we may be ready to addict and give over ourselves unto God, and to follow whatsoever he shall command."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "St. Luke here relates what was the fruit of the sermons, that we may know that the Holy Spirit was displayed not merely in the variety of tongues, but in the hearts too of those who heard." 
2. "They [the hearers] were pricked in their heart...a vast number of the hearers were affected."
3. "In the bitter sorrow and deep regret of these men for what they had done or allowed to be done, the words of Zechariah 12:10 seem to have received a partial fulfilment: ‘And I will pour upon the house of David, and upon the inhabitants of Jerusalem, the spirit of grace and of supplications: and they shall look upon me whom they have pierced, and they shall mourn for him, as one mourneth for his only son, and shall be in bitterness for him, as one that is in bitterness for his first-born.’"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They were pricked in their heart." 
2. "The sermon went home, they felt the sting of Peter's words, compunction."

### John Gill:
1. "The word of God entered into them, and was as a sharp sword in them, which cut and laid open their hearts, and the sin and wickedness of them."
2. "They saw themselves guilty of the crime laid to their charge, and were filled with remorse of conscience for it."
3. "Convinced, awakened sinners, are generally at first upon a covenant of works; are for doing something to atone for their past crimes, to set themselves right in the sight of God, to ingratiate themselves into his favour, and procure the pardon of their sins, and the inheritance of eternal life."

### John Wesley:
1. They said to the apostles, Brethren — They did not style them so before.
