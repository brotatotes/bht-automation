### Henry Alford:
1.  1. ] παρακαλέσας has probably been omitted on account of the two participles coming together: or perhaps on account of the same word occurring again in Acts 20:2 .

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "From his Epistles we learn...that he would have remained longer there but for his uneasiness at the non-arrival of Titus...that he might bring him word what effect his first Epistle to that church had produced."
2. "At length Titus arrived, to the joy of the apostle, the bearer of better tidings from Corinth than he had dared to expect."
3. "Under the mixed feelings which this produced, he wrote...his SECOND EPISTLE TO THE CORINTHIANS"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Embraced them - Saluted them; gave them parting expressions of kindness." 
2. "The Syriac translates this, 'Paul caned the disciples, and consoled them, and kissed them.'"
3. "To go into Macedonia - On his way to Jerusalem, agreeably to his purpose, as recorded in Acts 19:21."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Embraced [ασπασαμενος] . Better, as Rev., took leave. The word is used for a salutation either at meeting or parting. See ch. Acts 21:6, Acts 21:7.

### John Calvin:
1. "It appeareth that the Church was preserved in safety by the wonderful power of God amidst those troublesome tumults."
2. "We need not doubt that Paul did with much ado depart from them; yet because greater necessity doth draw him unto another place, he is enforced to leave his sons who were lately begotten, and had as yet scarce escaped shipwreck in the midst of the raging sea."
3. "We must diligently note these examples, that one of us may study to help another in this miserable dispersing; but if it so fall out at any time that we be bereft of profitable helps, let us not doubt nor waver, knowing that God doth hold the helm of our ship."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He had already... determined to leave Ephesus, and the words of the writer of the ‘Acts’ here simply tell us that he waited until quiet was restored in the city, and then set out on the journey which he had previously resolved to make."
2. "From Ephesus, St. Paul seems to have gone by land direct to Alexandria Troas; there he waited anxiously... for the arrival of Titus, whom he had sent to Corinth on a mission."
3. "At Philippi, the old scene of his labours, then a flourishing and devoted Christian community, it is most probable... he met at length his trusted disciple, and received much comfort from the news which Titus brought him from Corinth and its church."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Pictures the whole incident as bustle and confusion." 
2. "Salutation in Acts 21:7; Acts 21:19."
3. "Luke here condenses what was probably a whole year of Paul's life and work."

### John Gill:
1. "Paul called unto him the disciples...and embraced them."
2. "exhorted them to continue steadfast in the faith...comforted them under all their tribulations...with the exceeding great and precious promises of the Gospel."
3. "he did not choose to leave Ephesus till the tumult was over...and now it was over, he judged it proper to take his leave of them, and visit other churches."
