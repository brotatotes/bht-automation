# Acts 20:22 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Bound by the Spirit, Paul was troubled within himself, knowing that afflictions and bonds awaited him in Jerusalem. But driven by a strong impulse of the Spirit of God, he was compelled to go despite the hardships that lay ahead. Though he only had a general knowledge of the trials that awaited him, he remained resolute in his course, guided by the conviction that duty must be done at all costs.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The dative, as here, is found Romans 12:11, τῷ πν. ζέοντες, 1 Corinthians 5:3, παρὼν τῷ πνεύμ., 2 Corinthians 2:13, οὐκ ἔσχηκα ἄνεσιν τῷ πν. μου, and al., see also ch. Acts 19:21."
2. "How he was bound in the spirit is manifest, by comparing other passages, where the Holy Spirit of God is related to have shaped his apostolic course. He was bound, by the Spirit of God leading captive, constraining, his own spirit."
3. "He had no detailed knowledge of futurity, nothing but what the Holy Spirit, in general forewarning, repeated at every point of his journey, announced, viz., imprisonment and tribulations."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "And now, behold, I—"I" - is emphatic here."
2. "Bound in the spirit—compare Acts 19:21. This internal pressure, unattended with any knowledge of 'what was to befall him there,' was the result of that higher guidance which shaped all his movements."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Bound in the spirit" - Strongly urged or constrained by the influences of the Holy Spirit on my mind.
2. "The expression...is one of great strength and emphasis."
3. "When we are strongly urged by the convictions of duty...we should not shrink from danger or from death."
4. "Duty is to be done at all hazards."
5. "It is ours to follow the directions of God; results we may safely and confidently leave with him."
6. "Not knowing the things that shall befall me there."
7. "He knew that calamities and trials of some kind awaited him, but he did not know."
8. "We should commit our way unto God, not knowing what trials may be before us...knowing that...we have nothing to fear in the result."

### John Calvin:
1. "It was very expedient that the pattern which was set before them by God of them to be followed should be always before their eyes..."
2. "He speaketh doubtfully at the first of set purpose, that he may soften that which was about to be more (hard and) bitter..."
3. "For to the end he may excuse himself of rashness, he saith that the Spirit is the author and guide of his journey."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Urged by an intense sense of duty, Paul was going up to the Holy City."
2. "He was so persuaded that this was right, that no prospect of danger deterred him, no urgent affectionate entreaties moved him from his purpose."
3. "He was ignorant what the dangers were which too surely would meet him. He only knew that some terrible trial certainly awaited him in that city."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Bound in my spirit" he means, as in Acts 19:21, from a high sense of duty.
2. His own spirit was under the control of the Holy Spirit (Romans 8:16) and the sense does not differ greatly.
3. One of the rare instances of the future participle in the N.T.

### John Gill:
1. "Not in his own spirit, though the Ethiopic version reads, "in my spirit"; as if he was pressed and straitened, and troubled within himself, at what afflictions and bonds he was to endure at Jerusalem"
2. "he was under such a strong impulse of the Spirit of God, by which he was moved to such a vehement desire to go thither, that the bonds and afflictions he saw waited for him there, could not deter him, and all the entreaties of his friends could not dissuade him from it"
3. "he only in general knew, that bonds and afflictions would be his lot and portion, and which therefore he excepts in the next verse"

### John Wesley:
1. Bound by the Spirit — Strongly impelled by him.
