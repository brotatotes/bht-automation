# Acts 20:23 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Holy Ghost, through prophets in various cities, foretold to the apostle the afflictions and bonds that awaited him. These prophetic testimonies were given to Paul as he traveled, and served as a confirmation of the hardships he would face in Jerusalem. It was through the ministry of these prophets that the Holy Spirit communicated His will to Paul, demonstrating both God's providence and care for His servants.

### Henry Alford:

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Save that the Holy Ghost witnesseth in every city, c."
2. "by prophetic utterances from city to city"
3. "Analogous premonitions of coming events are not unknown to the general method of God's providence."
4. "They would tend to season the apostle's spirit."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "This was all that he knew, that bonds and afflictions were to be his portion."
2. "Such had been his experience in every city where he had preached the gospel by the direction of the Holy Spirit, that he regarded it as his certain portion that he was thus to be afflicted."
3. "When Paul was called to the apostleship it was predicted that he would suffer much."

### John Calvin:
1. "For by this means the word of God hath his [its] authority, when we confess that the Spirit of God is the author thereof, though the ministers be men."
2. "Now, forasmuch as the same Spirit, which foretelleth Paul of bonds and tribulations, doth also hold him fast bound that he cannot refuse to submit himself unto him, by this we learn, that what dangers soever hang over our heads, we are not thereby acquitted, but that we must obey the commandments of God, and follow his calling."
3. "In vain, therefore, do those men flatter themselves, who will do good so long as they be free from molestation, and may make discommodities, damages, and dangers of death, sufficient excuses."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Save that the Holy Ghost witnesseth in every city, saying that bonds and afflictions abide me."
2. "The gift of prophecy' appears to have been no uncommon possession in the days of the apostles."
3. "Such a sorrowful foreboding was probably written down in Corinth after one of those prophetic intimations here referred to: ‘The Holy Ghost witnesseth in every city.’"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Save that (πλην οτ). The οτ clause is really in the ablative case after πλην, here a preposition as in Philippians 1:18, this idiom πλην οτ occasionally in ancient Greek."
2. "Bonds and afflictions (δεσμα κα θλιψεις). Both together as in Philippians 1:17; 2 Corinthians 1:8. Literal bonds and actual pressures."
3. "Abide me (με μενουσιν). With the accusative as in verse Acts 20:5 (εμενον ημας) and nowhere else in the N.T."

### John Gill:
1. "In the churches of those times there were prophets who foretold things to come, and by these the Holy Ghost testified to the apostle, as he travelled along, and called upon the churches in every city, what would befall him when he came to Jerusalem."
2. "This in general was only made known to him by the Spirit of God, that wherever he came, affliction and persecution would attend him, and he must expect bonds and imprisonment."
3. "Since the Holy Ghost testified beforehand of the afflictions and bonds of the apostle, whether in every city or in Jerusalem, or both; it is no inconsiderable proof of the proper deity of the Spirit of God, and is an instance of his affectionate regard to the apostle, to give him previous notice of these things."

### John Wesley:
1. "Save that — Only this I know in general."
2. "the Holy Ghost witnesseth - By other persons."
3. "Such was God's good pleasure to reveal these things to him, not immediately, but by the ministry of others."
