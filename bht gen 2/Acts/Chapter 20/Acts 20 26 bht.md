# Acts 20:26 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
His appeal for testimony from his listener's own conscience seems peculiar to Paul, similar to his use of the word testify in other letters. This day is significant, as it marks their parting after Paul's diligent efforts in teaching and warning them. Paul claims to be blameless for the harm that may befall any individual, emphasizing the importance of faithfully fulfilling one's responsibilities in spreading the gospel.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "I appeal to your own selves to testify that I have been faithful."
2. "Ministers who preach the gospel with fidelity may thus appeal to their hearers; and in the day of judgment may call on themselves to witness that the fault of the ruin of the soul is not to be charged to them."
3. "He had discharged his duty in faithfully warning and teaching them; and now, if they were lost, the fault would be their own, not his."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. This day [τη σημερον ημερα] . Very forcible. Lit., on today's day; this, our parting day.

### John Calvin:
1. "Let them leave that to God alone to be ordered at his pleasure...mortal man shall not be so bold as to mangle the Scripture and to pull it in pieces, that he may diminish this or that at his pleasure, that he may obscure something and suppress many things."
2. "Therefore, those do set forth the will of God who interpret the Scriptures faithfully, and out of them instruct the people in the faith, in the fear of God, and in all exercises of godliness."
3. "If anything perish through their negligence, an account may be required at their hands; yea, that unless they show the way of salvation without guile and crooks, the destruction of those who go astray may be imputed unto them."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "I am pure from the blood of all men."
2. "His ceaseless, self-denying labours among the people at Ephesus would at least free him from that blood-guiltiness."
3. "If any man perishes, I am not myself guilty."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "I testify (μαρτυρομα). Elsewhere in the N.T. only in Paul's Epistles (Galatians 5:3; Ephesians 4:17; 1 Thessalonians 2:12). It means 'I call to witness' while μαρτυρεω means 'I bear witness.'" 
2. "This day (εν τη σημερον ημερα). The today day, the last day with you, our parting day." 
3. "I am pure from the blood of all men (καθαρος ειμ απο του αιματος παντων). Paul was sensitive on this point as in Corinth (Acts 18:6). It is much for any preacher to claim and it ought to be true of all. The papyri also give this use of απο with the ablative rather than the mere ablative after καθαρος."

### John Gill:
1. "I am pure from the blood of all men."
2. "They could only bear a testimony for him with respect to themselves, and the people at Ephesus."
3. "The ruin and destruction of no one of them could be laid to his charge."
