# Acts 20:35 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In all things, I have shown you an example of how to live and labor, supporting the weak just as I have done. Remember the words of the Lord Jesus, though not recorded by the Evangelists, that it is truly more blessed and happier to give than to receive. Let us imitate His example and find joy in helping the disabled and needy, knowing that in doing so, we imitate God Himself.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "In all things: so Paul (only), see reff."
2. "κοπιῶντας: A word used by Paul fourteen times, by Luke once only."
3. "ἀσθενούντων: Not here the weak in faith, but the poor."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "that so labouring—as I have done for others as well as myself. ye ought to support the weak" 
2. "to remember the words of the Lord Jesus, how he said, It is more blessed to give than to receive"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "I have showed you - I have taught you by instruction and example. I have not merely discoursed about it, but have showed you how to do it."
2. "It is more blessed to give - It is a higher privilege; it tends more to the happiness of the individual and of the world. The giver is more blessed or happy than the receiver."
3. "Imitating his great example, and complying with his command, the church shall yet learn more and more to give its wealth to bless the poor and needy; its sons and daughters to bear the gospel to the benighted pagan; its undivided and constant efforts to save a lost world."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "In all things I gave you an example."
2. "This saying of Jesus is not recorded by the Evangelists, and was received by Paul from oral tradition."
3. "The speech of Paul to the Ephesian elders 'bears impressed on it the mark of Paul's mind: its ideas, its idioms, and even its very words are Pauline.'"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Not only have I told you in words what is the duty of a Christ-loving man, but I tried to live the life before you which I told you of."
2. "It is a beautiful and touching reminder not only to these elders of Ephesus, but to all who say they love the Lord Jesus, to exercise self-denial in various ways, that they may possess some means wherewith to help those poorer, weaker, more helpless than themselves."
3. "They possess a far deeper meaning, for they assert as an eternal truth, the higher blessedness of giving than receiving."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "I gave you an example. First aorist active indicative of υποδεικνυμ, old verb to show under one's eyes, to give object lesson, by deed as well as by word (Luke 6:47)."
2. "So labouring ye ought to help. So, as I did. Necessity (δε). Toiling (κοπιωντας) not just for ourselves, but to help (αντιλαμβανεσθα), to take hold yourselves (middle voice) at the other end."
3. "He himself said. Not in the Gospels, one of the sayings of Jesus in current use that Paul had received and treasured. Various other Agrapha of Jesus have been preserved in ancient writers and some in recently discovered papyri which may be genuine or not."

### John Gill:
1. "They should labor with their hands as he did, and so support the weak..."
2. "...the weak in faith, and take nothing of them, lest they should think the preachers of the word sought only their own worldly advantage..."
3. "It is more blessed to give than to receive: it is more comfortable, honorable, pleasant, and profitable..."

### John Wesley:
1. "Ye ought to help the weak - Those who are disabled by sickness, or any bodily infirmity, from maintaining themselves by their own labour."
2. "The word which he himself said - Without doubt his disciples remembered many of his words which are not recorded."
3. "It is happier to give — To imitate God, and have him, as it were, indebted to us."
