# Acts 20:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The room where they were gathered was brightly lit with many lamps, not only to enable them to see and read the word of God, but also to dispel any suspicion or reproach that might arise as if they were engaging in wicked practices.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "This may be noticed, as Meyer observes, to shew that the fall of the young man could be well observed: or, perhaps, because many lights are apt to increase drowsiness at such times."
2. "Calvin and Bengel suppose, in order that all suspicion might be removed from the assembly ('ut omnis abesset suspicio scandali,' Beng.)."
3. "Kuin. and partly Meyer, that the lights were used for solemnity’s sake, for that both Jews and Gentiles celebrated their festal days by abundance of lights."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "there were many lights in the upper chamber—not a mere piece of graphic detail by an eye-witness, but mentioned, probably, as increasing the heat and contributing to drowsiness."
2. "mentioned, probably, as increasing the heat and contributing to drowsiness."
3. "as the next clause seems to show."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Why this circumstance is mentioned is not apparent. It, however, meets one of the slanders of the early enemies of Christianity, that the Christians in their assemblies were accustomed to extinguish all the lights, and to commit every kind of abomination."
2. "Perhaps the mention of many lights here is designed to intimate that it was a place of public worship, as not only the Jews, but the Gentiles were accustomed to have many lights burning in such places."
3. "In the upper chamber - See the notes on Acts 1:13."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Many lights. A detail showing the vivid impression of the scene upon an eye-witness."
2. "It has been remarked that the abundance of lights shows how little of secrecy or disorder attached to these meetings."
3. "The upper chamber. See on ch. Acts 1:13."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "All the little details of this memorable scene are carefully recorded; the very appearance of the brilliantly-lighted upper chamber; the lateness of the hour; the length of Paul’s sermon."
2. "The fact is probably mentioned to account for the sleep of Eutychus, which, no doubt after the fatigue of a long working day, was induced by the heat of the crowded, lit-up room."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Many lights...These lamps were probably filled with oil and had wicks that flickered and smoked."
2. "It was dark at night since the full moon (passover) was three weeks behind."
3. "In the upper room...As in Acts 1:13 which see."

### John Gill:
1. "Which were lighted up, both for necessary uses, to see by, to read the word, and to administer the ordinance of the supper."
2. "As well as to remove all ground of suspicion, or occasion of reproach, as if it was a midnight society met for wicked practices."
3. "This no ways countenances the use of lamps and wax candies in the daytime at divine worship, since this was in the night."

### John Wesley:
1. There were many lamps in the room where they were assembled — To prevent any possible scandal.
