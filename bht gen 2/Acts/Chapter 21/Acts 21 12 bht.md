# Acts 21:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
For the safety of the Church, they pleaded with Paul, tears streaming down their faces, to not go to Jerusalem. Despite their deep affection for the apostle, their plea was seen as a weakness, a reflection of their human nature. Unbeknownst to them, their request was actually a command from God, delivered through the powerful prophecy of Agabus.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "This suggests the case of Luther when on his journey to the Diet of Worms..."
2. "...and the story of Regulus the Roman, who...insisted on fulfilling his promise to the Carthaginians to return in the event of the failure of negotiations..."
3. "...and went back to certain torture and death."

### John Calvin:
1. "Their desire is worthy [of] praise, in that they desired to provide for the common safety of the Church by keeping back Paul."
2. "Paul’s constancy deserveth so much the more praise, when as he continueth so steadfast in the calling of God."
3. "Therefore, we must use such courtesy toward our brethren, that the beck or will of God have always the upper hand."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The prediction of Agabus, set forth in so striking and impressive a manner, moved even the fearless companions of Paul, men like Luke."
2. "They, and Philip and his daughters, and others, joined their entreaties to the great leader not to risk a life so precious to the Master’s cause, but to give up the journey."
3. "Commentators strikingly call attention here to the parallel between Paul and Paul’s Master, who had to listen to His disciple Peter endeavoring to persuade Him to turn aside from the way of suffering on which He had entered."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Both we and they of that place." 
2. "We tried to persuade him from going up." 
3. "This dramatic warning of Agabus came on top of that in Tyre and Paul's own confession in Miletus."

### John Gill:
1. "These prophecies, concerning the binding of the apostle by the Jews, and the delivery of him to the Romans, and saw the symbolical representations of these things."
2. "an instance of weakness in them, though an expression of their affection to the apostle;"
3. "this request was made with tears, as is evident from what follows."

### John Wesley:
1. "Both we, (his fellow travellers,) and they of the place, besought him not to go up to Jerusalem." 
2. "St. Paul knew that this prediction had the force of a command." 
3. "They did not know this."
