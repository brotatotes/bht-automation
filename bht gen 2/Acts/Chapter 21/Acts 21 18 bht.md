# Acts 21:18 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Paul, accompanied by his companions, went to see James, who was known as the brother of the Lord and the leader of the churches in Judea. This visit was significant because it showed the unity of purpose among the believers. The presence of all the elders further emphasized the importance of this meeting, as they came together to receive Paul and have a meaningful conversation with him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  18.   Ἰάκωβον ] James, ‘the brother of the Lord:’ the president of the church at Jerusalem: see ch. Acts 12:17 ; Acts 15:13 , Galatians 2:12 , and notes, and Prolegg. to the Epistle of James, vol. iv. pt. 1, § i. 24 37.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Paul went in with us unto James; and all the elders were present—to 'report himself' formally to the acknowledged head of the church at Jerusalem, and his associates in office."
2. "Had any other of the apostles been in Jerusalem on that occasion, it could hardly fail to have been noted."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It is not improbable that he was the only one of the apostles then at Jerusalem; and there is reason to believe that the church at Jerusalem was left under his particular care."
2. "James was the cousin of our Lord, and in Galatians 1:19 he is called the Lord’s brother."
3. "On all accounts, therefore, he was entitled to, and would receive, particular respect from the early disciples."

### John Calvin:
1. "All the elders were present. We may gather that out of this place which we had already in the fifteenth chapter."
2. "So often as any weighty business was to be handled, the elders were wont to come together, to the end the consultation might be more quiet without the multitude."
3. "We shall see anon, that the people were likewise admitted in their order, yet after that the elders had had their secret consultation."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "This James was the so-called brother of the Lord, not one of the Twelve, but who...took his place at once among the more prominent members of the Jerusalem Church, of which community after some little time he became the ‘bishop’ or presiding elder."
2. "There are in the New Testament story three men bearing the name of James... This is the James who received Paul when he came up to the Holy City to keep this feast of Pentecost, A.D. 58."
3. "The mention of James and 'all the elders,' and the omission of any allusion to the apostles, is a clear proof that none of these were at this time resident in the Holy City."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Together with us to James (συν ημιν προς Ιακωβον). So then Luke is present."
2. "There are many details that read like an eye witness's story."
3. "Clearly James is the leading elder and the others are his guests in a formal reception to Paul."

### John Gill:
1. "Paul took the first opportunity of paying a visit to James, very likely at his own house, to give him an account of his success among the Gentiles, and to know the state of the church at Jerusalem, and confer with him about what might be most proper and serviceable to promote the interest of Christ."
2. "Paul took with him those who had been companions with him in his travels, partly to show respect to James, and partly to be witnesses of what he should relate unto him."
3. "All the elders were present, who hearing of the coming of the apostle, and of his visit to James, assembled together to see him, and converse with him."

### John Wesley:
1. "Paul went in with us — That it might appear we are all of one mind..."
2. "to James - Commonly called the Lord's brother; the only apostle then presiding over the Churches in Judea."
