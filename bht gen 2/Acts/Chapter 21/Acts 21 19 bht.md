### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "he declared particularly—in detail. what God had wrought among the Gentiles by his ministry" 
2. "no doubt referring to the insidious and systematic efforts of the Judaizing party in a number of places to shrivel the Church of Christ into a Jewish sect" 
3. "and his own counter-procedure."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He declared particularly ... - As an evidence that God had been with him."
2. "It is not improbable that there might have been some suspicion in regard to Paul among the disciples at Jerusalem, and he might have heard that they were prejudiced against him."
3. "This prejudice would be removed by his stating what had actually occurred under his ministry."

### John Calvin:
1. "As we must grant, that whatsoever thing is excellent and worthy of praise, it is not done by our own power, but forasmuch as God doth work in us; and especially touching the edifying of the Church."
2. "Undoubtedly, that zeal of the law, which was in them, was corrupt, and assuredly even the very elders declare sufficiently that they like it not...they both signify that they are of another mind, and also that they do not allow [approve] the superstition of the people."
3. "Therefore, seeing they understand the matter very well, their meaning is, to have this alone made known to the rude and unskillful, that Paul meant nothing less than to persuade the Jews to contemn the law."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Both from the private and public reception of Paul and his companions by the presiding elders and bishop of the Jerusalem Church, it is quite clear that the governing body among the resident Jerusalem Christians sympathized with Paul's work, and thoroughly endorsed his teaching and practice."
2. "Before this venerable assembly of the elders of the mother church of Christianity, presided over by one who had been with the Lord from the days of the sacred childhood, Paul rehearsed the story of the past three years, including what is generally called the Third Missionary Journey."
3. "On this occasion, he, no doubt, presented the costly presents and alms contributed as a token of love and sympathy by these foreign Gentile congregations to their poor Jewish brethren in Palestine."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He rehearsed...for he had a great story to tell of what had happened since they saw him last."
2. "God had wrought...and he takes pains to give God the glory."
3. "The most that can be said is that the Judaizers referred to by James do not appear actively against him. The collection and the plan proposed by James accomplished that much at any rate. It stopped the mouths of those lions."

### John Gill:
1. "He declared particularly what things God had wrought among the Gentiles by his ministry."
2. "He declared, what multitudes of souls were converted, and what numbers of churches were planted; and this he ascribes not to himself, but to the power and grace of God."
3. "He was only an instrument, God was the efficient, and ought to have the glory."
