# Acts 21:22 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
What is the course of action that must be taken in this situation? The large number of individuals who hold negative opinions about the apostle Paul will undoubtedly gather together in a hostile and chaotic manner. The pressing question is how to address these objections and reconcile the hearts and minds of the people in order to prevent a tumultuous situation from arising.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "a multitude (of these Judaizers) will certainly come together: 'they will meet and discuss your proceeding in a hostile manner.'"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 20-25. they glorified the Lord,
	&c.—constrained to justify his course, notwithstanding the
	Jewish complexion of the Christianity of Jerusalem.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "What is it therefore? - What is to be done? What is it proper to do to avoid the effects of the evil report which has been circulated?"
2. "The multitude - The multitude of Jews. Must needs come together - There will be inevitably a tumultuous assemblage."
3. "It was, therefore, the part of friendship to him and to the cause to devise some proper plan to prevent, if possible, the anticipated excitement."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "What is it therefore? How does the matter lie?"
2. "The multitude must needs come together."
3. "Some texts omit. So Rev. If retained, we should read a multitude."

### John Calvin:
1. "The multitude must needs come together. The verb is a verb neuter, as if they should say, the multitude must of necessity come together."
2. "For if he had eschewed the light and sight of people, the sinister suspicion might have been increased."
3. "But this moderation must be kept in the Church, that the pastors be in great authority, and yet that they rule not proudly as lords, neither despise the rest of the body."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Seeing, now, this is the state of things, that you, Paul, are looked upon by a large number of our countrymen with jealous suspicion and dislike, let us consider what is best under the circumstances for you to do." 
2. "For it is certain that out of all these multitudes of foreign Jews come up to keep Pentecost in Jerusalem, a great number will always be watching you and your actions, to see whether what they have heard alleged against you be just."
3. "For the news of your arrival will be soon noised abroad."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "What is it therefore?" (Τ ουν εστιν?). See this form of question by Paul (1 Corinthians 14:15; 1 Corinthians 14:26).
2. "Clearly James and the elders do not believe these misrepresentations of Paul's teaching, but many do."
3. "There was no way to conceal Paul's arrival nor was it wise to do so."

### John Gill:
1. "The multitude must needs come together; either the whole church at Jerusalem, or the great confluence of people, even of believing Jews, that were come from all parts thither."
2. "There is danger in the case, the consequence may be bad; and therefore something must be done, to remove the opinion they had formed of the apostle, and the prejudice they had entertained against him."
3. "What must be done in this case? What method must be taken to remove these objections, and reconcile the minds of the people?"

### John Wesley:
1. "The multitude must needs come together - They will certainly gather together in a tumultuous manner."
2. "What is to be done? The multitude must needs come together."
3. "They will certainly gather together in a tumultuous manner, unless they be some way pacified."
