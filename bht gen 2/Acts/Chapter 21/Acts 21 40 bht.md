# Acts 21:40 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Paul, standing on the stairs, beckoned with his hand to gain the attention of the people. In the midst of the hushed silence, he spoke to them in the Hebrew tongue, the language commonly spoken in Jerusalem.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The Syro-Chaldaic, the mother-tongue of the Jews in Judæa at this time."
2. "His motive is implied (ch. Act 22:2) to be, that they might be the more disposed to listen to him."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "What nobler spectacle than that of Paul at this moment!"
2. "Yet in the midst of so many dangers, how self-possessed is he, how tranquil!"
3. "The Syro-Chaldaic, the vernacular tongue of the Palestine Jews since the captivity."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "On the stairs - See the notes on Acts 21:35."
2. "Beckoned with the hand - Waving the hand as a sign that he was about to address them, and to produce silence and attention."
3. "In the Hebrew tongue - The language which was spoken by the Jews, which was then a mixture of the Chaldee and Syriac, called Syro-Chaldaic. This language he doubtless used on this occasion in preference to the Greek, because it was understood better by the multitude, and would tend to conciliate them if they heard him address them in their own tongue."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Beckoned with the hand. Compare ch. 26 1. Tongue [διαλεκτω] . Lit., dialect : the language spoken by the Palestinian Jews - a mixture of Syriac and Chaldaic. 

### Philip Schaff:
1. "We have in these ‘Acts’ several marked instances of this strange power Paul gained so quickly over those with whom he was brought into contact."
2. "It was a strange scene for that feast of Pentecost... accused of the self-same crimes, making his defence before a crowd as wild and frenzied as that of which he had then been the leader."
3. "No doubt ‘the great silence,’ the hush which fell on this angry, vociferating crowd, was produced by the sound of the loved Hebrew words."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Paul had faced many audiences and crowds, but never one quite like this... He will speak about himself only as it gives him a chance to put Christ before this angry Jewish mob who look on Paul as a renegade Jew, a turncoat, a deserter."
2. "Paul knows only too well what this mob thinks of him... Paul's stature and words reach heroic proportions on this occasion."
3. "Self-possessed with majestic poise Paul faces the outraged mob beneath the stairs."

### John Gill:
1. "Paul stood on the stairs...and beckoned with the hand unto the people." 
2. "And when there was made a great silence...he spake unto them in the Hebrew tongue."

### John Wesley:
1. In the Hebrew tongue — That dialect of it, which was then commonly spoken at Jerusalem.
