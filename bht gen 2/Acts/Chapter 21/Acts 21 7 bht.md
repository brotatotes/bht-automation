Having completed our voyage, we arrived at the renowned city of Ptolemais, which was once known as Accos and now lies in ruins like many other great cities of the past.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Having ended our voyage, viz. the whole voyage, from Neapolis to Syria."
2. "With their landing at Ptolemais their voyage ended: the rest of the journey was made by land."
3. "It is now called St. Jean d’Acre, and is the best harbour on the Syrian coast, though small."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "When we had finished our course—completing the voyage from Tyre, we came—to Ptolemais." 
2. "Anciently called Accho, now St. Jean d'Acre, or Acre." 
3. "And saluted the brethren, and abode, &c.—disciples gathered probably as at Tyre, on the occasion mentioned."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "We came to Ptolemais - This was a city situated on the coast of the Mediterranean, on the north angle of a bay which extends, in a semicircle of three leagues, as far as the point of Mount Carmel."
2. "The Christian crusaders gave it the name of Acre, or John of Acre, from a magnificent church which was built in it, and which was dedicated to the apostle John."
3. "And saluted the brethren - Embraced them; gave them expressions of affection and regard."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Finished [διανυσαντες] . Only here in New Testament. Saluted. The word rendered take leave in verse 6. See on ch. Acts 20:1.

### John Calvin:
1. "By this we may easily gather, that that deaconship was an office which continued but for a time."
2. "The evangelists, in my judgment, were in the midst between apostles and doctors."
3. "For it was a function next to the apostles to preach the gospel in all places, and not to have any certain place of abode."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The arrival of the apostle and his companions at Ptolemais completed the sea portion of their journey..."
2. "Ptolemais is one of the oldest cities in the world... It was situate in the portion of Asher, and seems to have been ever considered as a Phoenician city."
3. "On the partition of the Macedonian Empire, Accho fell to the lot of the Ptolemais. It was rebuilt and renamed Ptolemais by Ptolemy Soter."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "And we, having (thereby) finished the voyage, arrived from Tyre at Ptolemais."
2. "The harbour is the best on the coast of Palestine and is surrounded by mountains."
3. "Possibly the scattered brethren (Acts 11:19) founded the church here or Philip may have done it."

### John Gill:
1. "Ptolemais, a maritime city in Judea, near Mount Carmel, which was formerly called so from one Ptolomy."
2. "the Gospel had been preached here with success; some had believed and professed it, and very likely were in a church state."
3. "these brethren Paul and his company visited, and saluted them; and abode with them one day; conferring together about spiritual things, and employing their time, no doubt, in religious exercises."

### John Wesley:
1. "Having finished our voyage, we came to Ptolemais - A celebrated city on the sea coast."
2. "Anciently called Accos, it is now, like many other once noble cities, only a heap of ruins."
