# Acts 22:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
God has a plan and purpose for each of us. His will is to reveal the salvation of mankind through Jesus Christ, the righteous and faithful one. As we seek to know His will, we have the opportunity to see and experience the righteousness of Christ in our lives. Through His voice, both in the words spoken to us directly and in the proclamation of the Gospel, we receive love, grace, mercy, peace, pardon, righteousness, and salvation. May we be attentive to His voice and obedient to His commands.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "So Peter, ch. Acts 3:13; Acts 5:30. In ch. Acts 9:17, ὁ κύριος is the word: this title is given for the Jews." 
2. "So Stephen, ch. Acts 7:52. How forcibly must the whole scene have recalled him, whom presently (Act 22:20) he mentions by name."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Just One—compare Acts 3:14; Acts 7:52."
2. "In order to place him on a level with the other apostles, who had 'seen the [risen] Lord.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "His will in the plan of salvation, and in regard to your future life."
2. "It was necessary that he should see him, that thus he might be a competent witness of his resurrection."
3. "Shouldst hear and obey his commands."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The God of our fathers - Just One. A conciliatory touch in Paul's speech, mentioning both God and Christ by their Jewish names."
2. "Hath chosen [προεχειρισατο]. See on ch. Acts 3:20. Better, as Rev., appointed."

### John Calvin:
1. "In calling him the God of the fathers, he reneweth the remembrance of the promises, that the Jews may know that the new calling of Paul is joined with them, and that those fall not away from the law who pass over unto Christ."
2. "Therefore, he which rejecteth the Son hath not the Father, who cannot be separated from him."
3. "Ananias setteth down the cause why God did vouchsafe Paul of so great honor, to wit, that he might be to his Son a public witness; and he doth so prepare him, that he may learn not only for himself alone, but that he may have so much the more care to profit, because he shall be the teacher of all the whole Church."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The whole sentence of Stephen, which was probably reproduced in its entirety by Paul (Luke no doubt abbreviates it), ran thus: ‘The God of our fathers, the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob.’"
2. "We are here distinctly told by Ananias what hardly appears from Luke’s account of the vision, or from either of Paul’s own recitals, how in the blinding glory Paul gazed on the Divine form of Jesus Christ."
3. "He who had been condemned as a malefactor was emphatically, above all the sons of men, the ‘Righteous,’ the ‘Just One.’"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "To plan, propose, determine." 
2. "God's purpose about Paul: to know his will, to see the Righteous One, to hear a voice from his mouth."

### John Gill:
1. "The ends of this choice or separation were, that thou shouldest know his will; his revealed will, concerning the salvation of men by Jesus Christ, which is no other than the Gospel."
2. "And see that just One: Jesus Christ the righteous, who is both as he is God, and as he is man, and also as he is Mediator, having faithfully discharged his office, and performed his engagements."
3. "And shouldest hear the voice of his mouth; both his human voice in articulate sounds, when he spoke to him in the Hebrew tongue, and the voice of his Gospel, of which he appeared to make him a minister; which is a voice of love, grace, and mercy, of peace, pardon, righteousness, and salvation."
