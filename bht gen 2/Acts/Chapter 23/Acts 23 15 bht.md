### Philip Schaff:
1. "This seems at first sight a strange story, that so monstrous a design should have been conceived and communicated to the chief priests and elders, to the leading members, in fact, of the august council of the Sanhedrim." 
2. "Still, strange as it may seem, it was in perfect accordance with the practice of the leading members of the Jewish state in these unhappy days." 
3. "'It is melancholy,' writes Professor Plumptre, 'to remember how often the casuistry of Christian theologians has run in the same groove. In this respect the Jesuit teaching absolves subjects from their allegiance to heretical rulers, and the practical issue of that teaching in the history of the Gunpowder Plot and of the murders perpetrated by Clement (Henry 111.) and by Ravaillac (Henry IV.) presents only too painful a parallel.'"
