# Acts 23:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Paul, despite receiving a divine promise of safety from Christ, recognized the importance of not neglecting any appropriate measures for his own preservation. He understood that divine assurance did not exempt him from fulfilling his own responsibilities and taking necessary actions to ensure his well-being. Paul's example teaches us the importance of actively participating in God's divine plan while trusting in His promises.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Paul had the most positive divine assurance that his life would be spared, and that he would yet see Rome..."
2. "He always understood the divine promises and purposes as being consistent with his own efforts, and with all proper measures of prudence and diligence in securing his own safety..."
3. "He did not rest merely on the divine promises without any effort of his own, but he took encouragement from those promises to put forth his own exertions for security and for salvation."

### John Calvin:
1. "Paul was not so desirous of life, but he would have made haste to die, if the Lord had thought it good so to be."
2. "Thus must all the ministers of Christ deal, that being furnished with invincible constancy, so far as their calling requireth, they fear not danger, and yet that they cast not away themselves through rashness."
3. "Let them call upon the name of the Lord cheerfully, even amidst the pikes; and yet let them not contemn those helps which are offered; otherwise they shall be injurious to God."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Attention has been justly called here to the fact that, although Paul had just received the Divine promise of protection in all these present dangers, yet he neglected none of the ordinary means of safety which were presented to him, evidently looking on them as the saving hand of the Lord stretched out to him."
