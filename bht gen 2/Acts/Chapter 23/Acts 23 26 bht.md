# Acts 23:26 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Claudius Lysias addresses the most excellent Governor Felix, a title of honor that belongs to him as a governor. The Greek term for greeting used here is similar to the one used in Luke 1:3, where Theophilus is greeted and wishes for all health and prosperity.

### Henry Alford:
1. "This letter seems to be given (translated from the Latin) as written, not merely according to its general import."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Unto the most excellent governor Felix - The most honored, etc."
2. "This was a mere title of office."
3. "Greeting - A term of salutation in an epistle wishing health, joy, and prosperity."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. To the most excellent [τω κρατιστω] . "His excellency" : an official title. Compare ch. Acts 24:3; Acts 26:25. Greeting [χαιρειν] . See on ch. Acts 14:23.

### Archibald T. Robertson:

### John Gill:
1. "Claudius Lysias, unto the most excellent Governor Felix...."

3. "The same is given to Theophilus, Luke 1:3 sendeth greeting; or wishes all health and prosperity."
