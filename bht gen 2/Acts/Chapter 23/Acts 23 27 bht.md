### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Lysias wishes to make the impression that Paul's citizenship was the cause of his rescuing him."
2. "He did not know of this until afterward."
3. "He says nothing about the proposed scourging."

### John Calvin:
1. "A man, being a Roman, was by them sore beaten, and almost slain; also, he commendeth him for the right and privilege of his freedom, that he may be the more courteously handled."
2. "Furthermore, this commendation was not purchased by prayer or flattery, neither was it bought with money."
3. "How came it to pass, then, that the chief captain did show himself so courteous freely to an obscure man, and whom all men did hate, save only because the Lord had appointed him to be his servant’s patron?"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The commander in Antonia wished his superior, Felix, to think that he had interfered on the prisoner’s behalf because he found Paul was a Roman citizen; but, in truth, he did not interpose until after Paul had been chained up to be scourged by his own orders."
2. "A desire to exhibit his zeal in the public service induced him to write this distorted view of the facts as they occurred."
3. "He evidently wished to throw a veil over the grave fault he had committed in ordering a Roman citizen to be scourged."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Lysias did rescue Paul and he did learn that he was a Roman, but in this order."
2. "He did not first learn that he was a Roman and then rescue him as his letter states."
3. "Lysias simply reversed the order of the facts and omitted the order for scourging Paul to put himself in proper light with Felix his superior officer and actually poses as the protector of a fellow Roman citizen."

### John Gill:
1. "He was nigh unto death."
2. "With these he came upon the Jews on a sudden, as they were beating Paul, and took him out of their hands, and saved him."
3. "He suggests that it was his great concern for the Roman name, and for a Roman citizen, which put him upon this enterprise."

### John Wesley:
1. Having learned that he was a Roman — True; but not before he rescued him. Here he uses art.
