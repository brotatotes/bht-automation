# Acts 23:30 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
When the chief captain found out about the conspiracy against Paul's life, he decided to send him to Felix, the governor, as it was under his jurisdiction to judge this matter. It is revealed that the Jews had conspired to kill Paul, and they sought to keep it a secret. Therefore, the chief captain wanted this matter to remain concealed and bid farewell to the recipient of the letter.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  30. ] Two constructions are combined here: (1) μηνυθείσης ἐπιβουλῆς τῆς ἐσομένης , and (2) μηνυθέντος , ἐπιβουλὴν ἔσεσθαι .

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 30. gave commandment to his accusers
	. . . to say before thee—This was not done when he wrote, but
	would be before the letter reached its destination.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. When it was told [μηνυθεισης] . Lit., pointed out, or shown, as Rev. See on Luke 20:37. Farewell. The best texts omit. See on ch. Acts 14:29.

### John Calvin:
1. "The chief captain doth bring the adversaries into contempt...because they went about to kill Paul treacherously." (560)
2. "They trouble Paul unjustly...sought so sore against his life without any cause." (560)
3. "When as they seek to kill him, it appeareth that they have no reason." (560)

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Though I thought him innocent, yet, hearing of this further plot against the life of a Roman citizen, I judged it wise to refer the whole matter to you, the chief authority in Judæa, as the affair may be more serious than I have deemed it to be." 


### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "When it was shown to me that there would be a plot."
2. "Charging his accusers also."
3. "Before thee."

### John Gill:
1. "The Jews laid wait for the man; had formed a conspiracy to take away his life."
2. "He sent him to Felix, as being governor, to whom the judgment of this affair properly belonged."
3. "He desired [the affair] might be concealed: farewell."
