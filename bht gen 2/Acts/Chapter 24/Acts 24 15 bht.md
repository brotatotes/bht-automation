# Acts 24:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In a public court, it is important to acknowledge and address the concerns of both the just and the unjust. By emphasizing that both groups will be impacted by the resurrection, it ensures fairness and impartiality in the judgment. This approach was particularly appropriate in this context and speaks to the need for justice and equity in all aspects of society.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Paul might regard them as representing the whole Jewish people, and speak generally."
2. "The juxtaposition of those words, which excited mockery even when the Gospel was being directly preached, would hardly have been hazarded in this defence, where every expression is so carefully weighed."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "This appeal to the faith of his accusers shows that they were chiefly of the Pharisees, and that the favor of that party, to which he owed in some measure his safety at the recent council (Acts 23:6-9), had been quite momentary."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Having a hope of the resurrection of the dead, which arises from the promises of God."
2. "He held nothing in this great cardinal point which they did not also hold."
3. "Of the righteous and the wicked; that is, of all the race."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Allow [προσδεχονται] . Or, as Rev., look for. The word admits of either sense.

### John Calvin:
1. "Thus doth the reverence of the Scripture go before, that it may hold us fast bound, and it is the beginning of faith."
2. "After that the knowledge of those things which God hath revealed there doth follow, being coupled and linked with sure hope."
3. "But as Paul doth in this place say that all men shall rise again, so by the plain voice of Christ all are cited; some unto judgment, some unto life."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "His belief in the Law and the Prophets gave him a hope founded on God, because His word and the promises contained in the Law and the Prophets furnish the only grounds for such a hope."
2. "The devout hope of the resurrection was, after all, entertained in the hearts of the majority of the Jewish people."
3. "That hope was a source of comfort to Job; Isaiah foretold it; Daniel bore witness to it."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "That there shall be a resurrection." 
2. "Both of the just and the unjust." 
3. "The Talmud teaches the resurrection of Israelites only, but Paul was more than a Pharisee."

### John Gill:
1. "In this article the Pharisees of those times were sounder than the modern Jews."
2. "It is certain the Jews are divided in their sentiments about this matter; some of them utterly deny that any other shall rise but the just."
3. "Indeed there will be a difference between the resurrection of the just and of the unjust, both in the time of their rising, the dead in Christ will rise first at the beginning of the thousand years, the wicked not until they are ended; and in the means and manner of their rising; they will be both raised by Christ, but the one by virtue of union to him, the other merely by his power."

### John Wesley:
1. Both of the just and of the unjust — In a public court this was peculiarly proper to be observed.
