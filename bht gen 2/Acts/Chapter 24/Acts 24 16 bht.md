# Acts 24:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
With the hope of the resurrection of the dead in mind, the apostle Paul applies himself diligently to living a godly and upright life. He strives to keep his conscience clear and unblamable before both God and man. Paul's determination to lead a life free from offense is not only an individual pursuit but a shared one, as he encourages others to do the same.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  16.   ἐν τούτῳ ]  Accordingly , i.e. ‘having and cherishing this hope;’ see reff.
	 καί ]  also , ‘  as well as they .’

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "And herein—On this account, accordingly; that is, looking forward to that awful day (compare 2 Corinthians 5:10)."
2. "I exercise myself—The 'I' here is emphatic; 'Whatever they do, this is my study.'"
3. "To have always a conscience void of offence, c.—See Acts 23:1, 2 Corinthians 1:12, 2 Corinthians 2:17, c. that is, 'These are the great principles of my life and conduct—how different from turbulence and sectarianism!'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Paul often appeals to his conscientiousness as the leading habit of his life."
2. "To have always a conscience...to do what is right, so that my conscience shall never reproach me."
3. "The great and noble aim of Paul was to be pure; and no slander of his enemies, no trials, persecutions, perils, or pains of dying could take away the approving voice of conscience."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Exercise myself [ασκω]... to practice, exercise, discipline; and so, in ecclesiastical language, to mortify the body." 
2. "Void of offense [αποσκοπον] . Lit., without stumbling; unshaken." 
3. "The word is used thus in a passive sense here, as in Philippians 1:10."

### John Calvin:
1. "There is no sharper prick to prick men forward, with all desire to lead a godly and holy life, than the hope of the last resurrection..."
2. "An evil conscience is as good as a thousand witnesses to accuse men of blockishness, that they may gather for a certainty that they do not earnestly and thoroughly believe eternal life..."
3. "...those do indeed hope for the last resurrection who are never weary of well-doing."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The rule of his life was to struggle to keep himself from sin."
2. "Paul's belief this is above all things most noteworthy was anything but a merely speculative, it was a real and living faith."
3. "The Greek word rendered 'do I exercise myself' (α ̓σκω ͂), tells us of the restless, ceaseless warfare within waged by this true gallant soldier of Christ to keep his conscience, not only in the sight of men, but before the unseen majesty of God, white and pure."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Do I also myself take exercise, take pains, labour, strive."
2. "Void of offence... 'free from hurt or harm'"
3. "Certainly his conscience acquitted him of having caused any offence to his countrymen"

### John Gill:
1. "On account of this doctrine of the resurrection of the dead, which is a doctrine according to godliness, and promotes and engages to a holy life and conversation..."
2. "The apostle studied, and laboured, and bent himself, and employed his thought, care, and time,to have always a conscience void of offence toward God and toward man..."
3. "By a 'conscience void of offence', is meant a good conversation; which as it respects God, lies in a carefulness not to offend him, but to do his will; and as it respects men, a shunning what may give offence, or be a stumbling to them..."

### John Wesley:
1. For this cause — With a view to this, I also exercise myself - As well as they.
