Festus, having arrived in the province of Judea, specifically in Caesarea, began his rule by familiarizing himself with the important city. Caesarea, being a secure and convenient port, served as the ideal landing spot. After his arrival, Festus took three days to rest from his journey before continuing with his governance.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The term ἐπαρχία is properly used of a province, whether imperial or senatorial."
2. "The term ἐπαρχία is here loosely applied to Judæa, which was only a procuratorship."
3. "Josephus calls Festus ἔπαρχος, Antt. xx. 8. 11; as also Albinus, ib. 9. 1."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 1-3. Festus . . . after three days .
	. . ascended . . . to Jerusalem—to make himself acquainted with
	the great central city of his government without delay.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Judea at that time was a Roman province."
2. "This was the usual language in describing a journey to Jerusalem."
3. "As Jerusalem had been the former capital; as it was still the seat of the religious solemnities; as the Sanhedrin held its meetings there; and as the great, and rich, and learned men, and the priests resided there, it is evident that a full knowledge of the state of the province could be obtained only there."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Was come into the province [επιβας τη επαρχια] . Lit., having entered upon the province..

### John Calvin:
1. "The grace of God doth so much the more plainly show itself in delivering him, because, contrary to all hope, he is delivered out of the jaws of death."
2. "The Lord, in stretching forth his hand, and breaking such a strong conspiracy, did show an eternal example of his power in defending his."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The term we find here was perhaps used in consequence of the importance of Judæa at that time, although it was only reckoned as a part of the imperial province of Syria."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Having come into the province..."
2. "Judea was not strictly a province, but a department..."
3. "After three days... That is on the third day..."

### John Gill:
1. "he now being landed in some part of the province, namely, at Caesarea, and so might be said to have entered upon the government of it"
2. "Caesarea was the safest and most commodious port"
3. "here Festus stayed three days after his landing, to rest himself after the fatigue of the voyage"
