# Acts 25:24 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Festus spoke to King Agrippa, addressing him first due to his position and knowledge. He mentioned Paul, the prisoner, and how the Jews had fervently pleaded with him to rule against Paul, both in Jerusalem and in Caesarea. The Jews were loud and clamorous, insisting that Paul should not be allowed to live, just as they had done before with Lysias.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  24.  ἅπαν τὸ πλ .] At Jerusalem ( Act 25:1 ) literally, by the popular voice (probably) of some tumultuous outcry: here, by their deputation.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "I would also hear—"should like to hear.""
2. "Paul was right when he said, 'The king knoweth of these things... for I am persuaded that none of these things are hidden from him; for this thing was not done in a corner' (Acts 26:26)."
3. "Hence his curiosity to see and hear the man who had raised such commotion and was remodelling to such an extent the whole Jewish life."

### Albert Barnes:
1. Have dealt with me - Have appeared before me, desiring me to try him. They have urged me to condemn him.Crying ... - Compare Acts 22:22. They had sought that he should be put to death.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The expression here, 'all the multitude,' is a strange one; but Festus believed, and with some reason, that the feeling against Paul among the Jews was a very general one."
2. "Certainly it existed to a great extent among the influential men who guided the destinies of the unhappy people in these last years of their existence as a nation."
3. "'And all here' speaks for the unanimity of the Cæsarean Jews in this matter with their Jerusalem brethren."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Present articular participle of συνπαρειμ (only here in N.T.) with associative instrumental case ημιν." 
2. "Second aorist active indicative of εντυγχανω, old verb to fall in with a person, to go to meet for consultation or supplication as here."
3. "Some MSS. have plural here ενετυχον rather than the singular ενετυχεν.Crying (βοωντες). Yelling and demanding with loud voices."

### John Gill:
1. "And Festus said, King Agrippa,.... He addressed himself to him in the first place, as being the principal person, and of great dignity, as well as knowledge."
2. "ye see this man the prisoner at the bar, meaning Paul:about whom all the multitude of the Jews have dealt with me: applied unto him, interceded with him, and very importunately pressed and desired him to give judgment against him:both at Jerusalem and also here; at Caesarea, whither they came from Jerusalem to accuse him"
3. "crying: in a very noisy and clamorous way:that he ought not to live any longer; as they did before Lysias the chief captain, Acts 22:22 and so in the hearing of Festus; for it was his death they sought, and nothing else would satisfy them."
