# Acts 27:24 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Through his fervent prayers, Paul not only sought his own deliverance, but also interceded for the lives of all his shipmates. God graciously answered his prayers, sparing not only their lives, but possibly even their souls. The providence of God is truly remarkable, as He preserves many wicked men for the sake of a few righteous ones. In the eyes of God, Paul assumed the role of master and pilot of the ship, steering it through the storm with divine guidance and wisdom.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Etiam centurio, subserviens providentiæ divinæ, Paulo condonavit captivos" (Bengel). 
2. "Non erat tam periculoso alioqui tempore periculum, ne videretur Paulus, quæ necessario dicebat, gloriose dicere" (Bengel).
3. "Paulus, in conspectu Dei, princeps navis, et consiliis gubernator" (Bengel).

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Paul was wrestling in prayer, not for himself only and the cause in which he was going a prisoner to Rome, but with true magnanimity of soul for all his shipmates."
2. "God heard him, 'giving him' (remarkabl expression!) all that sailed with him."
3. "What a contrast to this is the speech of Cæsar in similar circumstances to his pilot, bidding him keep up his spirit because he carried Cæsar and Cæsar's fortune!"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Fear not, Paul - Do not be alarmed with the danger of the loss of life."
2. "God hath given thee all ... - That is, they shall all be preserved with thee. None of their lives shall be lost."
3. "The lives of the wicked are often spared because God interposes to save the righteous."

### John Calvin:
1. "He is very desirous to bring to pass that they may give God alone the praise for their deliverance, lest these superstitious men do falsely translate it unto their idols; and by this means he inviteth them unto the true faith."
2. "But by this it appeareth how great the men’s wickedness is, in that they shut their ears against sound and wholesome counsel, and do forthwith forget the grace of God, though it were familiarly known to them."
3. "This is a singular pledge of God’s love toward us, in that he maketh certain drops of his goodness distill from us unto others."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Fear not, Paul. It is a natural inference from these words that St. Paul himself had been experiencing fear."
2. "Nor is he ashamed to own this in his letters."
3. "God hath given thee all them that sail with thee. This seems to show that he had been engaged not only in prayer, but in intercessory prayer."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Thou must stand before Caesar." 
2. "The lives of those that sailed with Paul God had spared as a gift (χαρις) to Paul."

### John Gill:
1. "For though the apostle knew and believed he should go to Rome, and appear before Caesar, to whom he had appealed, and where he should bear a testimony for Christ; and though he had previous notice of this storm, and of the loss and damage which should be sustained, and which he expected; yet the flesh was weak, and he might be under some fears and misgivings of heart..." 
2. "Thou must be brought before Caesar; as has been declared, and therefore cannot be lost in this storm; it is the will and decree of God, which cannot be frustrated, it must be..."
3. "God had determined to save the whole ship's company for his sake, and in answer to his prayers, which he had been putting up for them; the Lord had heard him, and granted his request, and would save them all on his account..."

### John Wesley:
1. "God hath given — Paul had prayed for them. And God gave him their lives; perhaps their souls also."
2. "How wonderfully does his providence reign in the most contingent things! And rather will many bad men be preserved with a few good, (so it frequently happens,) than one good man perish with many bad."
3. "So that Paul, in the sight of God, was the master and pilot of the ship."
