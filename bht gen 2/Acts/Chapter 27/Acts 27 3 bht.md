# Acts 27:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Julius, the centurion, showed great kindness and respect towards Paul. His courteous treatment towards Paul indicated a favorable disposition towards him, perhaps influenced by Paul's defense.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The Sidonians were renowned for their manufactures of glass, linen, and silversmith's work."
2. "The Sidonians furnished the best ships in Xerxes' navy."
3. "The φίλοι here mentioned were probably Christian brethren, but it is usual in that case for ἀδελφοί or μαθηταί to be specified."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "To reach this ancient and celebrated Mediterranean port, about seventy miles north from Cæsarea, in one day, they must have had a fair wind."
2. "Julius courteously—(See on Acts 27:2)."
3. "Such small personal details are in this case extremely interesting."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "And Julius courteously entreated Paul - Treated him kindly or humanely."
2. "And gave him liberty ... - The same thing had been done by Felix, Acts 24:23."
3. "To refresh himself - To enjoy the benefit of their care; to make his present situation and his voyage as comfortable as possible."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Touched is an inferential rendering. Landed would be quite as good."
2. "Courteously. . . expresses rather polish of manners than real kindness."
3. "To refresh himself, lit., to receive care or attention."

### John Calvin:
1. "He suffered him to go to his friends, that they might dress and refresh him, whom he might have left in the stinking ship." 
2. "He could not withdraw himself from the calling of God."
3. "Their sailing was dangerous and tempestuous... until a cruel storm arose, whose end was shipwreck."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Sidon lay due north of Cæsarea on the Phoenician coast, and the course to ‘the neighbourhood of Asia’ was west-north-west."
2. "Julius courteously entreated (i.e. treated) Paul. Already we have a strong indication of the centurion’s friendly disposition, and of the influence gained by the apostle over him."
3. "To refresh himself. More literally, to obtain friendly care. Two particulars here naturally suggest themselves."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Using Paul kindly, he was kindly to Paul throughout the voyage, taking a personal interest in his welfare." 
2. "Whether it was mere hospitality we do not know. It may have been medical attention required because of Paul's long confinement."
3. "This is Paul's first visit to Sidon, but Christians were already in Phoenicia (Acts 11:19) and so Paul had 'friends' here."

### John Gill:
1. "This was a famous city in Phoenicia, upon the northern border of the land of Israel; it was a maritime place, and noted for trade and navigation."
2. "the centurion into whose hands the apostle was delivered, used him with great humanity and civility; he found grace in his sight"
3. "the apostle was allowed to go ashore, and visit his friends, and be refreshed by them, both in body and spirit"

### John Wesley:
1. Julius treating Paul courteously — Perhaps he had heard him make his defence.
