# Acts 27:32 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Paul's influence on the ship is evident as the soldiers take immediate action, cutting the ropes of the boat and letting it fall off. They prioritize Paul's authority over the shipmen, recognizing the credibility of his words over theirs. This decision prevents the sailors from abandoning the ship, a choice that would have resulted in disastrous consequences. The soldiers' action showcases the power of faith and demonstrates the importance of heeding the guidance of those with spiritual wisdom.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Cut off the ropes ... - It is evident that the mariners had not yet got on board the boat."
2. "They had let it down into the sea Acts 27:30, and were about to go on board."
3. "By thus cutting the ropes which fastened the boat to the ship, and letting it go, all possibility of their fleeing from the ship was taken away, and they were compelled to remain on board."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "With military promptitude and without any argument, the soldiers settled the question."
2. "The ropes were cut; and the boat drifted off into the darkness, and was dashed to pieces on the rocks."
3. "A very good religious moral, full of very varied instruction, might be connected with the story of this boat."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Paul is now saviour of the ship." 
2. "The soldiers quickly cut loose the skiff and 'let her fall off' rather than be the means of the escape of the sailors who were needed."
3. "This dastardly scheme of the sailors would have brought frightful loss of life."

### John Gill:
1. "The soldiers cut off the ropes of the boat... and let her fall off." 
2. "They prevented the shipmen quitting the ship; for now they gave more credit to Paul than to them."
