# Acts 27:34 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
For this is for your safety and preservation, as it was necessary for them to regain their strength in order to endure the hardships and challenges that lie ahead. They needed to be in a better position to help themselves and one another during this difficult time. They were assured of their complete safety and protection, as not even a single hair would be harmed, signifying their utter well-being. It is interesting to note that shaving hair and cutting nails were considered ill omens for sailors, highlighting the significance of this promise in the midst of their impending shipwreck.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "I pray you to take some meat, for this is for your health, for there shall not a hair fall from . . . any of you." 
2. "On this beautiful union of confidence in the divine pledge and care for the whole ship's health and safety."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Not a hair fall from the head ... - A proverbial expression, denoting that they would be preserved safe."
2. "None of them would be lost."
3. "In their persons they should not experience the least damage."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "This is for your health. The correct meaning is: ‘This is essential for your safety.’ Their imminent danger was not death from starvation, but from drowning in the waves. They had laborious and difficult work before them; and it was necessary that they should recruit their strength."
2. "There shall not a hair fall from the head of any of you. This was a proverb denoting entire safety and exemption from the slightest harm."


### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "This is for your safety."
2. "Perish, to destroy."

### John Gill:
1. "For this is for your health; the Alexandrian copy reads, 'for our health'; it was for the health of them all, that they might be better able to bear the shock and fatigue of the shipwreck, and be in better spirits, and in a better capacity to help themselves, and one another."
2. "For there shall not an hair fall from the head of any of you; a proverbial phrase, expressing the utmost safety of their lives, and therefore might cheerfully eat their food, and rest themselves, and be satisfied."
3. "To dream of shaving the hair, portended shipwreck to sailors; nor was it lawful for any to pare his nails, or cut off his hair, but in a storm; to which custom, some think, the apostle here alludes."

### John Wesley:
1. This is for your preservation — That ye may be the better able to swim to shore.
