## BHT:
The centurion recognized Paul as someone of great importance and was likely influenced by a divine power guiding him to preserve Paul's life and fulfill God's will. Despite the initial intention to kill the prisoners, the centurion's command prevented them from carrying out this plan. He even ordered them to cast themselves first into the sea, ensuring the safety of the entire group, including the mariners, soldiers, and other prisoners or passengers on board. This narrative highlights the remarkable nature of Paul's presence and the protective hand of God in their midst.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Great must have been the influence of Paul over the centurion's mind to produce such an effect."
2. "According to the divine pledge and Paul's confident assurance given them, every soul got safe to land—yet without miracle."
3. "The graphic minuteness of this narrative of the shipwreck puts it beyond doubt that the narrator was himself on board."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "For the sake of this one righteous man, the lives of all were spared."
2. "It is possible for a pious man, like Paul, so to conduct in the various trying scenes of life...as to conciliate the favor of the people of this world."
3. "God often confers important blessings on the wicked for the sake of the pious relatives, friends, and neighbors with whom they are connected."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "We have here a new indication of the charm and power which St. Paul exercised over the minds of those with whom he came in contact."
2. "Thus the other prisoners owed their lives to the apostle."
3. "This military order may have been of great importance at this moment of hesitation, when many may have feared to go through the breakers, and try to 'get to land.'"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "To save Paul. And no wonder for the centurion knew now how much they all owed to Paul." 
2. "Stayed them from their purpose. Ablative case of βουλημα after εκωλευσεν. 
3. "And get first to land. This classic verb εξειμ occurs four times in Acts and nowhere else in the N.T. It was a wise command."

### John Gill:
1. "...perceived he was some extraordinary person;... moved thereunto by a superior influence... that the will of God might be fulfilled..."
2. "Kept them from their purpose... laid his commands upon them to the contrary."
3. "Commanded that they... should cast themselves first into the sea... though it seems rather to have respect to the whole company... the mariners, who generally can swim, and the soldiers, as many of them as could, and the rest of the prisoners or passengers..."
