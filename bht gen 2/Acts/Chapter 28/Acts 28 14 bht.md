# Acts 28:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The brethren in Puteoli, a port city bustling with people from various countries, eagerly requested that Paul and his companions stay with them for a week. This would not only allow them to fellowship together and worship, but also to engage in enriching conversations about their faith. It is of great significance that Paul eventually arrived in Rome, known as the grand center of the empire and the world. However, it is noteworthy that this arrival did not occur as expected, marking a turning point in his journey.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  14. ] These Christians were perhaps Alexandrines, as the commerce was so considerable between the two places.
	 ουτως ]  after this stay with them : implying that the request was complied with.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "If this request came from Julius, it may have proceeded partly from a wish to receive instructions from Rome and make arrangements for his journey thither, partly from a wish to gratify Paul, as he seems studiously and increasingly to have done to the last."
2. "One can hardly doubt that he was influenced by both considerations."
3. "The apostle had thus an opportunity of spending a Sabbath with the Christians of the place, all the more refreshing from his long privation in this respect, and as a seasoning for the unknown future that lay before him at the metropolis."

### Albert Barnes:
1. Brethren - Christian brethren. But by whom the gospel had been preached there is unknown.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "We know from Romans 16:0, and indeed from the mere existence of the Epistle to the Romans, that the Christian ‘brethren’ were at this time numerous in the metropolis."
2. "We seem here to have a clear indication of the observance of the Lord’s day, as in Acts 20:6-7 at Troas, and in Acts 21:4 at Tyre."
3. "From this bare statement of the fact of their arrival at the end of their journey, St. Luke turns aside in the next sentence to mention most interesting circumstances connected with their route from Puteoli."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Paul and his party remained so long at the urgent request of the brethren."
2. "Luke is exultant... It is the climax of the book of Acts."
3. "The great event is that Paul reached Rome, but not as he had once hoped."

### John Gill:
1. "Which is not to be wondered at, since it was a port much frequented, and where many came and went, of different countries and nations..."
2. "...the Christians at Puteoli desired the apostle, and those that were with him, to stay a week with them, that they might have the advantage of a day of public worship together, and might enjoy much of their Christian conversation..."
3. "Rome was the metropolis of Italy, the seat of the empire, and mistress of the whole world; it is so well known, as not to need describing..."
