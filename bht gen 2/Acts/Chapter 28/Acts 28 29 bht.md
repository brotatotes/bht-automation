### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "This verse is wanting in many manuscripts [and omitted by several recent editors], but certainly without reason."
2. "Probably the words were regarded as superfluous, as they seem to tell us what we were told before, that Paul 'departed'."
3. "But in Acts 28:25 it is the breaking off of the discourse that is meant, here the final departure from the house" [OLSHAUSEN].

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Great reasoning - Great discussion or debates."
2. "The part which believed that Jesus was the Messiah Acts 28:24 discussed the subject warmly with those who did not believe."
3. "This whole verse is missing in the Syriac version, and in some Greek mss., and is supposed by Mill and Griesbach to be spurious."

### John Calvin:
1. "The wicked were more nettled because he cited the prophecy against them; for they are so far from waxing meek when they are reproved, that they are more inflamed with fury."
2. "But seeing there was such disputing, it appeareth that some did so embrace those things which Paul had spoken, that they doubted not to defend and stoutly to avouch that which they believed."
3. "Assuredly, if we will have peace with God, we must strive against those which contemn him."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The whole of this verse is wanting in the more ancient MSS. and versions, and must be considered spurious."
2. "It was, however, probably added in early times by some scribe, to soften down the apparent abruptness of the close of the account of the interview between Paul and the leading Jews of Rome."

### John Gill:
1. "Cited the prophecy of Isaiah, and declared the mission of the Gospel to the Gentiles, and their calling by it; both which must greatly gravel and disturb the unbelieving part of his audience."
2. "And had great reasoning among themselves; not only with them that believed, but with others, that seemed to incline towards the apostle, and who espoused and undertook to defend some principles of his, against the rest."
3. "Things which must be very grating to them."
