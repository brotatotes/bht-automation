## BHT:
The healed lame man clung to his benefactors, not wanting to be separated from them. The man's actions drew a crowd, providing an opportunity for the preaching of the gospel. This scene took place in a magnificent porch, a famous gathering place for celebrating feasts to God. The people marveled at the man's healing, the method used, and the ones who performed it.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "holding, physically: not spoken of mental adhesion, but of actual holding by the hand or arm, that he might not be separated from them in the crowd, but might testify to all, who his benefactors were."
2. "στοᾷ τῇ κ. Σολομ .": See John 10:23, note."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "This is human nature."
2. "How vividly do these graphic details bring the whole scene before us!"
3. "Thus was Peter again furnished with a vast audience, whose wonder at the spectacle of the healed beggar clinging to his benefactors prepared them to listen with reverence to his words."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He clung to his benefactors, and would not be separated from them" (Prof. Hackett).
2. "The fact of the cure and the conduct of the man would soon draw together a crowd, and thus furnish a favorable opportunity for preaching to them the gospel."
3. "This 'porch' was a covered way or passage on the east side of the temple. It was distinguished for its magnificence."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The lame man which was healed."
2. "He held them firmly, took fast hold."
3. "Greatly wondering, wondering out of measure."

### John Calvin:
1. "The old temple was pulled down, but Zerubbabel and Ezra, in the re-edifying and new building of the same temple, had imitated the same, so nigh as they could possibly devise."
2. "That vain cost which Herod had bestowed had not yet blotted out the remembrance of Solomon in the hearts of the people."
3. "Luke nameth the same [porch] as a most famous place, whereunto the people ran together to celebrate their feasts unto God at the times appointed."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Perhaps, as Alford suggests, in the ardour of his gratitude, that he might testify to all who his benefactors were."
2. "It was built on an artificial embankment which filled up a deep valley. The embankment was the work of King Solomon, hence, perhaps, the name, and the cloister was restored on the original plan."
3. "According to Lightfoot, the whole court of the Gentiles was spoken of popularly, at times, as Solomon’s Porch."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Perhaps out of gratitude and partly from fear."
2. "It was on the east side of the court of the Gentiles and was so called because it was built on a remnant of the foundations of the ancient temple."
3. "Wondering out of measure, already filled with wonder."

### John Gill:
1. "And as the lame man which was healed..."
2. "...hung about them, and was loath to part with them..."
3. "...greatly wondering; at the man that was cured; at the cure that was wrought upon him; and still more at the persons who did it, and the manner in which it was done."
