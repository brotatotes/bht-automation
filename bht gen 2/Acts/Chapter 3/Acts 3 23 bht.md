# Acts 3:23 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Every soul that refuses to listen and heed the words of Jesus, the prophet sent by God, will be utterly destroyed and cut off. The punishment for rejecting Jesus as the Messiah is exclusion from the kingdom of God, which carries the sentence of eternal death. This warning, echoing the words of Moses, serves as a powerful reminder to the Jewish people of the dire consequences of their unbelief and rejection of Jesus as the true prophet.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "This word, only known to later Greek, is often found in the LXX."
2. "In most places where it occurs, the readings vary between - ολοθρ - and - ολεθρ -; see var. readd."


### Albert Barnes:
1. "In this verse Peter has not quoted the passage in Deuteronomy literally, but he has given the sense."
2. "Soul is often put for the whole man by the Hebrews."
3. "The sense is, that he should be punished in the usual manner; that is, by excision, or by being destroyed from among the people."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Shall be destroyed [εξολοθρευθησεται] . Only here in New Testament. Rev., "utterly destroyed," giving the force of ejx out.

### John Calvin:
1. "By a most grievous punishment against the rebellious, the authority of all the prophets, but most of all of Christ, is established."
2. "God had adopted the stock and kindred of Abraham unto himself, upon this condition, that this might be sufficient for them unto the chiefest felicity to be reckoned in that number."
3. "He is not worthy to be accounted one of the Church, whosoever he be that refuseth to have him to be his Master, by whom alone God doth teach us, and by whom he will have us to hear himself."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Every soul which, through hardness of heart, self-will, hatred of goodness and purity, refuses to listen to the voice of Jesus the Messiah."
2. "The only difference is, while the original words of the passage in Deuteronomy affirm the purpose of God to exact vengeance, the well-known formula employed by the apostle defines the nature of the punishment reserved for that stubborn soul which refuses to hear the Lord Jesus."
3. "This punishment is exclusion from the kingdom of God, from life in its highest sense; and this exclusion from life carries with it the sentence of eternal death."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They had refused to 'hearken' to Moses and now, alas, many had refused to 'hearken' to Christ."
2. "Shall be utterly destroyed... to destroy utterly (εξ), only here in the N.T., common in the LXX."

### John Gill:
1. "he that hears not him, hearkens not to God, in whose name he speaks, and whose word he delivers"
2. "requiring often intends punishment, or a cutting off"
3. "which was remarkably fulfilled in the Jewish nation, for their rejection of Jesus as the true Messiah, and that prophet"

### John Wesley:
1. "Every soul who will not hear that prophet, shall be destroyed from among the people" - One cannot imagine a more masterly address than this, to warn the Jews of the dreadful consequence of their infidelity.
