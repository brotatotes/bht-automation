# Acts 4:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The rejected stone, Jesus, is the true Messiah and the cornerstone of the spiritual building of the church. Despite being rejected and put to death, He was raised from the dead and exalted at the right hand of God. Jesus is the chief cornerstone that adorns, strengthens, knits, and holds together the entire building of the church, fulfilling the prophecy from Psalm 118:22.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "This is the stone which was set at naught of you builders, &c."
2. "This application of Psalm 118:22, already made by our Lord Himself before some of the same 'builders' (Matthew 21:42), is here repeated with peculiar propriety after the deed of rejection had been consummated."
3. "The rejected One had, by His exaltation to the right hand of the Majesty on high, become 'the head of the corner'."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The ancient Jews applied this to David...The New Testament writers, however, apply it without any doubt to the Messiah." 
2. "And from this passage we may learn that God will overrule the devices and plans of wicked men to accomplish his own purposes." 
3. "What people despise and set at naught, he esteems of inestimable value in his kingdom...what they have rejected God has made the very basis of his schemes."

### John Calvin:
1. "Therefore, this is David’s comfort, that howsoever the captains have rejected him, yet did not their wicked and ungodly endeavors hinder him from being extolled by God unto the highest degree of honor."
2. "But that was shadowed in David which God would have perfectly expressed in the Messias."
3. "Furthermore, this happeneth not once only, but it must be fulfilled daily; at least it must seem no new thing if the chief builders do even now reject Christ."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The rulers of the Jews were the builders to whom the charge of the house of God was given."
2. "They should have been the first to acknowledge the long-looked-for Messiah, and to have worked for the glory of His kingdom, but they had rejected Him and cast Him aside."
3. "God, by raising Him from the dead, had shown that He was the corner-stone on which the whole fabric of the spiritual temple of God on earth must rest."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The experts, the architects, had rejected Jesus for their building... This very Rejected Stone God had made the head of the corner."
2. "as Jesus himself had pointed out... as Peter here declares and repeats later."
3. "either the highest corner stone right under the roof or the corner stone under the building."

### John Gill:
1. "this Jesus of Nazareth, by whose name the lame man was made whole, is that stone spoken of in Psalms 118:22 by whom is meant the true Messiah, comparable to a stone, for his strength and duration, and usefulness, as a foundation and corner stone, in the spiritual building of the church"
2. "Christ was rejected by them, both in person and in doctrine, and was ignominiously treated, and at last put to death, yet he was raised from the dead, and exalted at the right hand of God"
3. "He is the stone, which is become the head of the corner; or the chief corner stone, that adorns, strengthens, knits, and keeps together, the whole building"

### John Wesley:
1.  Psalm 118:22.
