### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "What shall we do?" (Τ ποιησωμεν). Deliberative aorist active subjunctive (ingressive and urgent aorist).
2. "Notable miracle" (γνωστον σημειον). Or sign. It was useless to deny it with the man there.
3. "We cannot deny it" (ου δυναμεθα αρνεισθα). That is, it will do no good.

### John Gill:
1. "For that indeed a notable miracle hath been done by them...a clear case, a well known thing, of which there was no room to doubt."
2. "It is manifest to all them that dwell in Jerusalem...his cure being wrought so openly, and in such a miraculous way, it was the common talk of the city."
3. "The fact is so certain and evident; nor hide it, as the Ethiopic version renders it, it being so notorious and public."
