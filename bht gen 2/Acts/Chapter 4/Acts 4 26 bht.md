# Acts 4:26 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Those who stood in opposition, set themselves against the chosen one, Jesus Christ, the Messiah. Their actions were not just against a man, but against the Lord Himself. Jesus was anointed to fulfill important roles as a prophet, priest, and King. This demonstrates the significance of what was done to Him, as it was an attack on God's purposes and glory.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 23-30. being let go, they went to
	their own company—Observe the two opposite classes,
	representing the two interests which were about to come into deadly
	conflict.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "This passage implies that opposition to the Messiah is opposition to Yahweh. And this is uniformly supposed in the sacred Scriptures. He that is opposed to Christ is opposed to God. He that neglects him neglects God."
2. "To despise him, or to oppose him, is to despise and oppose him who appointed him to this work, to contemn his counsels, and to set him at naught."
3. "His work is dear to God. It has engaged his thoughts. It has been approved by him. His mission has been confirmed by the miraculous power of the Father, and by every possible manifestation of his approbation and love."

### John Calvin:
1. "The Spirit teacheth by this word, that all those do make war against God which refuse to submit themselves to Christ..."
2. "Because God will reign in the person of his Son alone, we refuse to obey him so often as we rebel against Christ..."
3. "Let the hypocrites profess a thousand times that they mean nothing less than to make war against God, yet shall they find this true, that God is their open enemy, unless they embrace Christ with his gospel."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The true king, who to the prophet’s mind is to fulfill all his largest hopes, has taken the place of the visible and earthly king."
2. "The nations are not merely those who are now mustering for the battle, but whatsoever opposeth and exalteth itself against Jehovah and against His Anointed."
3. "There is an exact correspondence between the leading enemies mentioned in the Psalm, who arose against the Lord and His Anointed, and those who were present at the scenes of the condemnation and death of Jesus."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  Set themselves in array  (παρεστησαν). Literally, stood by.Against his Anointed  (κατα του Χριστου αυτου). Against his Messiah, his Christ. 

### John Gill:
1. "These stood, or rose up in an hostile manner, and set themselves against, and opposed themselves to the Messiah, Jesus of Nazareth."
2. "What was done against Christ, was done against the Lord, their views and designs, their interest and glory, being the same."
3. "He [Jesus] was anointed... to be prophet, priest, and King."
