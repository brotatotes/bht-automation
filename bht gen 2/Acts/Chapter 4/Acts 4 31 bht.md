# Acts 4:31 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The believers gathered together were filled with the Holy Spirit, an outward manifestation of God's presence. This filling of the Spirit not only empowered them to speak the word of God with boldness, but it also served as a testimony that their prayer had been answered. Their boldness in proclaiming the word of God without fear served as an example for all believers.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "As the first outpouring of the Spirit, so this special one in answer to prayer, was testified by an outward and visible sign" 
2. "It was on every ground probable that the token of the especial presence of God would be some phænomenon which would be recognized by those present as such" 
3. "ἐπλήσθησαν, with a fresh and renewed outpouring."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Place was shaken—glorious token of the commotion which the Gospel was to make" 
2. "The Spirit rested upon the entire community, first, in the very way they had asked, so that they 'spoke the word with boldness.'"
3. "The community of goods was but an outward expression of this, and natural in such circumstances."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The event which followed was regarded by them as an evidence that God heard their prayer."
2. "The place was shaken... The language here is suited to express the idea of an earthquake."
3. "Their being filled with the Holy Spirit here rather denotes their being inspired with confidence or boldness than being endowed with new powers."

### John Calvin:
1. "Luke declareth now that God did not only hear this prayer, but did also testify the same by a visible sign from heaven."
2. "The shaking of the place should, of itself, have done them small good; but it tendeth to another end, that the faithful may know that God is present with them."
3. "This is a perpetual profit of prayer, which is also set before us for an example."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The place was shaken... a token of God's presence and power." 
2. "They spake... began to speak, after being filled with the Holy Spirit." 
3. "Luke uses the very words of the prayer in verse Acts 4:29 to describe their conduct."

### John Gill:
1. "the place was shaken where they were assembled together... a symbol of the divine presence"
2. "they were all filled with the Holy Ghost... speaking with divers tongues"
3. "they spoke the word of God with all boldness... without fear"

### John Wesley:
1. They were all filled — Afresh; and spake the word with boldness - So their petition was granted.
