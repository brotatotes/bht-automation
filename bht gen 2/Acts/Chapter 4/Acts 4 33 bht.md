The apostleswere able to bear witness to the resurrection of Jesus with great power, revealing the efficacy and ability given to them by God. They preached the gospel with zeal, relying on the resurrection of Jesus as the foundation of their strong patient faith. Through their witness, they provoked many to bear them goodwill and drew people to the Savior. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The Apostles were the specially appointed witnesses of the Resurrection, and this their testimony they gave with power."
2. "That the Spirit did not inspire them with unbroken uniformity in matters of fact, our present Gospels, the remnants to us of this very testimony, sufficiently witness."
3. "It was in the manner of delivering this report that the great power of the Spirit was shown."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 33. with great power—effect on
	men's minds. 
	great grace was upon them
	all—The grace of God copiously rested on the whole community.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The word 'power' here denotes 'efficacy,' and means that they had 'ability' given them to bear witness of the resurrection of the Saviour."
2. "Their union, their benevolence, their liberality in supplying the needs of the needy, was a means of opening the hearts of the people, and of winning them to the Saviour."
3. "If we wish to incline others to our opinions, nothing is better adapted to it than to show them kindness, and even to minister to their temporal needs."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Gave [απεδιδουν] . Lit., gave back [απο] ; as something which they were in duty bound to give.

### John Calvin:
1. "The zeal which the apostles had to preach the gospel was so far from being diminished, that they were rather endowed with new power."
2. "The resurrection [...] is, as it were, the furnishing or fulfilling of the gospel."
3. "Their honesty, and temperance, and modesty, and patience, and other virtues, did provoke many to bear them good-will."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The powerful and effective eloquence of the apostolic preaching...was the solemn answer to the congregation's solemn (and, we believe, often repeated) prayer."
2. "The resurrection of the Lord Jesus...was the column upon which their strong patient faith rested; they knew they had seen their Master after He had risen; they told it out to others that men might grasp the mighty issues which this victory of Jesus over death involved for every man and woman."
3. "The fact of their standing high in popular estimation was an important one: it showed under whose protection and by whose favour their public preaching and work went on after the threats of the supreme council."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They kept on giving their witness with power after the answer to their prayer."
2. "Of the resurrection. It was on this issue that the Sadducees had arrested them."

### John Gill:
1. "With great power...gave the apostles witness of the resurrection of the Lord Jesus."
2. "Great grace was upon them all...the gracious protection of God over them, preserving them from the rage and malice of men."
3. "That grace and favour which they had among the people in common...charity, liberality and beneficence, which were among them."

### John Wesley:
1. And great grace — A large measure of the inward power of the Holy Ghost, was upon them all - Directing all their thoughts, words, and actions.
