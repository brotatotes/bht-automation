# Acts 4:35 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The believers entrusted the money from the sale of their property to the apostles, demonstrating their reverence for the apostles and their willingness to give up their worldly possessions. This act of commitment signified that they completely entrusted their resources to the apostles' authority and were subject to their decision on how to distribute them. The appointed individuals carried out the distribution, ensuring that each individual received what they needed according to the judgment of the apostles.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "παρὰ τοὺς πόδας, not a Hebraism for the whole person but literal." 
2. "The Apostles, like the Prætor, probably sat upon a raised seat, on the step of which, at their feet, the money was laid, in token of reverence."


### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 35. laid . . . at the apostles'
	feet—sitting, it may be, above the rest. But the expression may
	be merely derived from that practice, and here meant figuratively.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They committed the money received for their property to the disposal of the apostles, to distribute it as was necessary among the poor."
2. "This soon became a burdensome and inconvenient office."
3. "They therefore appointed men who had special charge of it."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Distribution was made (διεδιδετο)." 
2. "According as anyone had need (καθοτ αν τις χρειαν ειχεν)."

### John Gill:
1. "Showing great veneration and respect to the apostles, and a sort of neglect and contempt of their worldly substance."
2. "Signifying that they entirely delivered them to the apostles, and subjected them to their disposal."
3. "The distribution was made by them, to every man, to the original proprietors, as well as to others, and that not as much as a man would have, or he might crave; but as much as he needed, for the present, of which the apostles were the judges."

### John Wesley:
1. And distribution was made — At first by the apostles themselves, afterward by them whom they appointed.
