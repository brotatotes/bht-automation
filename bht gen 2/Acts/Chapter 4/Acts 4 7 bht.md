# Acts 4:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In the midst of the assembly, the Sanhedrin placed them in the middle, seeking to catch them with their words. They asked by what power or by what name they had done this, attempting to intimidate them and challenge the divine authority behind their actions. Their ambiguous questioning revealed their intention to trap the apostles and undermine their credibility.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "in what (manner of) power; of what kind was the enabling cause, the element in which, as its condition, the deed was wrought?"
2. "by (‘in,’ see above) what (manner of) name, spoken"
3. "the miracle: and that only."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 7. By what power or . . . name have
	ye done this—thus admitting the reality of the miracle, which
	afterwards they confess themselves unable to deny ( :-).

### Albert Barnes:
1. "By what power ... - A similar question was put to Christ in the temple."
2. "By what name - That is, by whose authority. It is very probable that they expected to intimidate the apostles by this question."
3. "It has been usual with the enemies of religion to attempt to intimidate its friends, and when argument fails, to attempt to silence Christians by appealing to their fears."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. What power - what name. Lit., what sort of power; what kind of name. Have ye done. The ye closes the sentence in the Greek with a contemptuous emphasis : you people.

### John Calvin:
1. "They feign that they are careful that the honor due unto God may not be given to any other."
2. "In the mean season, their importunateness is wonderful, in that they go about to drive the apostles to make denial, by asking many questions concerning a manifest matter, and to wring out by fear some other thing than they had confessed."
3. "But God doth bring their crafty wiliness to nought, and maketh them hear that which they would not."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The judges well knew the name, but they wanted to convict Peter and John of sorcery, by having worked a miracle not in the name of God, but of a crucified malefactor."
2. "They hoped to bring the apostles under the awful death-sentence pronounced in the law, which especially provides for the case when the sign or the wonder comes to pass."
3. "Maimonides, commenting on the words of Deuteronomy 13:0, speaks of one endeavoring to turn away the people from the Lord their God, and tells them that the sign such an one had performed was done by enchantment and witchcraft, and that, therefore, he must be strangled."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "In the midst (εν τω μεσω). The Sanhedrin sat in a semicircle."
2. "They inquired (επυνθανοντο). Imperfect middle, began to inquire."
3. "Or in what name (η εν ποιω ονοματ). As if by some magical formula such as exorcists practised (Acts 19:13) as if to catch them by (Deuteronomy 13:1)."

### John Gill:
1. "And when they had set them in the midst,.... Of the assembly, as the Ethiopic version adds; in the middle of the sanhedrim, which sat in a semicircular form...so that those that were set before them, were placed in the middle of them..."
2. "they asked, by what power, or by what name have ye done this? they inquired of them, whether it was by a natural, or by a divine, or by a diabolical power, that they had wrought the cure upon the lame man?"
3. "or whether they pretended to a divine and supernatural aid? and also what name they had made use of, and by whose authority they acted?"

### John Wesley:
1. By what name — By what authority, have ye done this? - They seem to speak ambiguously on purpose.
