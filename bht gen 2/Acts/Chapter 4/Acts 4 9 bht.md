# Acts 4:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
If we are being questioned today about the good deed, let it be known that it was done in faith, for the glory of God, and for the benefit of the man. This suggests that they were treated unfairly for being detained and interrogated for performing an act of kindness. The question posed is how the man was healed, and the answer is readily available, pointing to the power and name by which it was done.

### Henry Alford:
1. "If, with an implication of the fact being so."
2. "Wherein, in what, as the conditional element."
3. "On the other hand, ἐν τούτῳ, may very well be masculine, as referring to Ἰησοῦς χρ. Himself, included in the previous words τῷ ὀν. Ἰ. χρ."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The captain of the Levitical guard and the Sadducees are annoyed at the disturbance created around the temple and the apostles preaching through Jesus the resurrection from the dead." (1-12)
2. "The resurrection of Christ, if a fact, effectually overthrew the Sadducean doctrine." (1-12)

### Albert Barnes:
1. "If we this day - If as is the fact; or since we are thus examined."
2. "The good deed - The act of benevolence; the benefit conferred on an infirm man."
3. "By what means - This was the real point of the inquiry."

### John Calvin:
1. "Peter layeth tyranny to the charge of the priests and the scribes, because they examine them unjustly concerning a benefit which deserveth praise."
2. "Peter hath in this place more respect unto the wicked affection of the mind than unto the very order of the question."
3. "Peter, being supported with this confidence, doth at the first gird them wittily with a taunting preface, because they sit as judges to condemn good deeds."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Concerning a good deed done to an impotent man."
2. "Skilful turn made by Peter."
3. "Is made whole."

### John Gill:
1. "If we this day be examined of the good deed... it being done in faith, and to the glory of God, and for the good of the man."
2. "and hereby tacitly suggests, that they were dealt very hardly with, to be seized and kept in custody, and be called in question, for doing an action so beneficent and kind."
3. "by what means he is made whole; restored to perfect health, and the proper use of his limbs; that is, by what power, and in what name this was done; the answer is ready, and it is as follows."
