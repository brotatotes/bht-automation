# Acts 5:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
They kept back part of the price for themselves, pretending that they had given everything. They brought only a certain part and laid it at the apostles' feet, trying to deceive others into thinking they had given the whole amount. Their deceptive actions aggravated their guilt and showed their desire for praise and recognition.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The coolness with which they planned the deception aggravated the guilt of this couple."
2. "They brought a certain part, pretending it to be the whole proceeds of the sale."


### Albert Barnes:
1. "They 'secretly' kept back a part, while 'professedly' devoting all to God." 
2. "His wife 'knowing it,' and evidently concurring in it." 
3. "This was evidently an act professedly of devoting all to God."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Kept back [ενοσφισατο]. Only here, ver. 3, and Titus 2:10, where it is rendered purloining."
2. "The verb means to set apart for one's self; hence to appropriate wrongfully."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The praise of Joseph was too much for Ananias, but he was not willing to turn over all."
2. "He wanted praise for giving all and yet he took care of himself by keeping some."
3. "Thus he started the Ananias Club that gave a new meaning to his lovely name (God is gracious)."

### John Gill:
1. "And kept back part of the price... he reserved it for his own use, after he had given out that he sold it for the service of the church" 
2. "and brought a certain part; whether the greater part, or an equal part, half of it, or a lesser part; some little part of it, so the phrase seems to signify, is not certain"
3. "and laid it at the apostles' feet; as the rest did, thereby to make a show of charity, and cover the deceit."

### John Wesley:
1. And bringing a certain part — As if it had been the whole: perhaps saying it was so.
