### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Hardened ecclesiastics, all unawed by the miraculous tokens of God's presence with the apostles, and the fear of the mob only before their eyes!"
2. "Being dismissed, they depart rejoicing, and continue their preaching."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The command of the Sanhedrin was sufficient to secure their presence, as they did not intend to refuse to answer for any alleged violation of the laws."
2. "Christians, when charged with a violation of the laws of the land, should not refuse to answer... It is a part of our religion to yield obedience to all the just laws of the land."
3. "Opposition by them would have excited commotion; and though 'they' would have been rescued, yet they resolved to show that they were not obstinate, contumacious, or rebellious, but were disposed, as far as it could be done with a clear conscience, to yield obedience to the laws of the land."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The many sick who had been lately healed had predisposed a vast number of the inhabitants of Jerusalem and the neighbourhood to listen with interest and kindness to the earnest preaching."
2. "Then the spirit of love which reigned among them, the self-denying eagerness of the richer members, their devotion to the poor and sick, the number of widows and other unemployed women, who before had been leading purposeless lives, for whom the growing Church had found congenial occupation, all these things weighed with the fickle populace."
3. "The tide, however, soon turned, and a few months later we shall see a bitter persecution raging against the Church, the populace apparently careless of what might happen to those men whose words they had listened to so gladly."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They handled the apostles gently for fear of being stoned themselves by the people."
2. "Brought (ηγεν). Imperfect active of αγω, was bringing (leading), slowly no doubt, and solemnly."
3. "For they feared (εφοβουντο γαρ). Imperfect middle, still feared, kept on fearing."

### John Gill:
1. "They did not lay hold upon them, and drag them away in a violent manner; but gave them good words, and allured them, and entreated them to go along with them."
2. "They used no forcible, but gentle methods; they did not seize them in a violent way, and bind them, and carry them away by force."
3. "Because of the miracles done by them, and the benefit they received from them, both for their souls and bodies, [the officers] feared the people."
