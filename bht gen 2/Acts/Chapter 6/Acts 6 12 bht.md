## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "τὸν λαόν, first, that by means of the popular feeling they might act upon the πρεσβ. κ. γρ., the members of the Sanhedrim."
2. "ἐπιστάντες] The same persons, acting now by the authority of the Sanhedrim; Saul, among οἱ ἀπὸ Κιλικίας, being, as is afterwards (ch. Act 7:58) implied, among the foremost, came upon him."
3. "and seized him."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They 'excited' the people, or alarmed their fears"
2. "The members of the Sanhedrin, or Great Council"
3. "To the Sanhedrin, or the Great Council of the nation, which claimed jurisdiction in the matters of religion."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "They stirred up the people...imply[ing] to stir up as a mass, to move them together." 
2. "This is the first record of the hostility of the people toward the disciples."
3. "Caught...better as Rev., seized."

### John Calvin:
1. "What can be more unmeet than in their cause to lean unto lies?"
2. "If you ask these Rabbins, whether it be lawful to slander a man or no, they will deny that it is lawful generally; but when they come unto us, good zeal doth excuse them."
3. "We are specially taught by this example, how dangerous the color of good zeal is, unless it be governed by the Spirit of God; for it breaketh out always into furious madness, and, in the mean season, it is a marvelous visor to cover all manner of wickedness."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "It was above all things necessary for the enemies of these Nazarenes to have public opinion on their side."
2. "The people were now won over to the side of the persecutors of the followers of Jesus by an appeal to their patriotism."
3. "The teaching of Stephen arrayed both these two great parties against him and his cause."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They shook the people together like an earthquake."
2. "The elders and the scribes (Pharisees) are reached, but no word about the Sadducees."
3. "Rushed at him."

### John Gill:
1. "The common people, who were easily wrought upon, and soon incensed and provoked..."
2. "And the elders and the Scribes; who belonged to the sanhedrim, to whom they reported these things, as persons, under whose cognizance they properly came..."
3. "And brought him to the council; the great sanhedrim, then sitting at Jerusalem, to whom it belonged to judge of blasphemy."
