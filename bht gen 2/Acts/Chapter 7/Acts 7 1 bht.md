# Acts 7:1 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Stephen responds to the accusations made against him by the high priest and the council. Instead of directly addressing the charges, he chooses to explain his beliefs through a historical perspective, examining God's dealings with the people of Israel and the Gentiles. Although his speech is cut short, it is clear that Stephen's message revolves around the worldwide mission of the gospel of Christ, aligning with the commission given by Jesus himself. Through his defense, Stephen sought to challenge the narrow understanding of the law and the temple, emphasizing the transformative power of Jesus of Nazareth.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  1 .] On the H. P.’s question, see Chrys. just quoted. It is parallel with Matthew 26:62 , but singularly distinguished from that question by its mildness: see above.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The high priest seems to have presided in the council."
2. "Are these things so? - To wit, the charge alleged against him of blasphemy against Moses and the temple."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The glorified countenance of Stephen has caused a pause of surprise and admiration." 
2. "The high-priest interrupts by calling upon the accused for his defense."

### John Calvin:
1. "Stephen speaketh very appertinently, as the matter requireth."
2. "He wringeth from them this false boasting."
3. "He affirmeth plainly that their fathers were elected of God to be a peculiar people before there was any temple."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "A hush seems to have fallen on the council as they watched that strange unearthly brightness light up the countenance of the accused, and in silence all gazed on the rapt expression of that face which seemed to his enemies the face of an angel."
2. "The high priest breaks the silence, but his gentle question betrays his emotion, very different from the rougher address of Caiaphas to our Lord (Matthew 26:62), or to the harsh command of the high priest Ananias when he bade his officers smite the prisoner Paul on the mouth when he was examined before the council (Acts 23:2)."
3. "He simply asks him, Are you really guilty of impious blasphemies against the Temple and the law?"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Stephen undertakes to explain his real position by the historical method, that is to say, by a rapid survey of God's dealing with the people of Israel and the Gentiles."
2. "The speech was interrupted at the end and may not include all that Stephen meant to say. But enough is given to give us a good idea of how Stephen met the first charge."
3. "It is a great and masterful exposition of the worldwide mission of the gospel of Christ in full harmony with the Great Commission of Christ."

### John Gill:
1. "Is it true what they say, that thou hast spoken blasphemous words against the temple, and the law, and hast said that Jesus of Nazareth will destroy the one, and change the other?"
2. "What hast thou to say for thyself, and in thine own defence?"
