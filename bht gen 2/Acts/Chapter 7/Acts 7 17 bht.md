1. "When the time of the promise came, the people grew and multiplied in Egypt." 
2. "For more than two hundred years they amounted to no more than seventy-five souls; how prodigious, then, must have been their multiplication during the latter two centuries."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. When [καθως] . Rev., more correctly, as; the word being not a particle of time, but meaning in proportion as.

### John Calvin:
1. "Stephen passeth over unto the deliverance of the people, before which went that innumerable issue which had increased beyond the ordinary manner."
2. "God seemeth to take from the Jews all hope, because Pharaoh doth tyrannously afflict them, and their bondage groweth greater daily."
3. "The sum is this; that God, being mindful of his promise, did increase the people in time, that he might perform that which he had sworn to Abraham; but the Jews (as they were unthankful and froward) did refuse the grace of God, so that they did what they could to shut up the way before themselves."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "They increased so rapidly in power as well as in numbers..."
2. "The marvellous increase of the little shepherd family... was well calculated to alarm the advisers of a Pharaoh..."


### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  Drew nigh  (ηγγιζεν). Imperfect active, was drawing nigh. 

### John Gill:
1. "But when the time of the promise drew nigh...the time of the four hundred years...which God had sworn to Abraham..."
2. "The people grew and multiplied in Egypt...their number was increased to six hundred thousand, and three thousand and five hundred and fifty men..."
3. "And it seems, that they multiplied the more towards the time when the promise of deliverance drew nigh to be accomplished, and even when they were the most afflicted..."
