# Acts 7:21 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Pharaoh's daughter took him up out of the water, adopting him as her own son and treating him with favor. This act of adoption meant that Moses would receive all the advantages that the land could furnish for his education. According to Jewish tradition, Moses was even chosen as Pharaoh's successor. Miriam, Moses' sister, seized the opportunity to offer a Hebrew nurse to care for the child, ensuring his proper upbringing. Moses, being designated for a kingdom, had all these advantages of education had he not been exposed in the first place.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Albert Barnes:
1. "Was cast out - When he was exposed on the banks of the Nile..."
2. "And nourished him - Adopted him, and treated him as her own son..."
3. "An adopted son in the family of Pharaoh would be favored with all the advantages which the land could furnish for an education."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Took up [ανειλετο] . Used among Greek writers of taking up exposed children; also of owning new-born children."
2. "So Aristophanes: 'I exposed (the child) and some other woman, having taken it, adopted [ανειλετο] it' ('Clouds,' 531)."
3. "There is no reason why the meaning should be limited to took him up from the water (as Gloag)."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Took him up surely signifies, ‘lifted him up out of the water.’" 
2. "Far her own son. There is a Jewish tradition that, after his adoption by the daughter of the sovereign, Moses was chosen as Pharaoh’s successor."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The word was used of old for picking up exposed children as here."
2. "After the birth of a child the father took it up to his bosom, if he meant to rear it; otherwise it was doomed to perish."
3. "This use of εις=as occurs in the old Greek, but is very common in the LXX as a translation of the Hebrew le."

### John Gill:
1. "Miriam the sister of Moses, observing what was done, and perceiving the inclination of Pharaoh's daughter to take care of the child, offered to call an Hebrew nurse, to nurse the child for her..."
2. "According to Philo the Jew, this princess was the king's only daughter, who had been a long time married, but had had no children, of which she was very desirous..."
3. "Other Jewish writers say that he took the crown from off the king's head, and put it on his own; upon which, the magicians that were present, and particularly Balaam, addressed the king, and put him in mind of a dream and prophecy concerning the kingdom being taken from him, and moved that the child might be put to death..."

### John Wesley:
1. "Pharaoh's daughter took him up" - By which means, he had all those advantages of education.
2. "Being designed for a kingdom" - He had all those advantages of education if he had not been exposed.
