# Acts 7:46 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
David, a chosen man after God's heart, sought to find a fixed habitation for the God of Jacob. Although his petition was not granted, God, who remains without a temple, allowed Solomon to build Him a house. It is important to note the wisdom in the choice of words used in this passage.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "asked permission, see 2 Samuel 7:2 ff., in which this request is made through Nathan the prophet, and at first conceded by Nathan, though afterwards, on a revelation made from God, denied"
2. "The vow (a species of prayer) here referred to, is defined by the words εὑρεῖν σκήνωμα, to be that mentioned Psalms 131:1-3 (LXX)."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "gave them up—judicially."
2. "the passage is from Amos 5:25."
3. "neither did ye offer to Me only, nor always, nor with a perfect and willing heart."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "God granted him great prosperity, and delivered him from his enemies."
2. "To prepare a permanent dwelling-place for the 'ark' and for the visible symbols of the divine presence."
3. "David sought to build a house that would be permanent, where the ark might be deposited."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Desired [ητησατο] . More correctly, asked : through Nathan."
2. "Tabernacle [σκηνωμα] . It was not a tabernacle or tent which David proposed to build, but a house."
3. "Rev., rightly, habitation."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Asked for himself (as a favour to himself)."  (ηιτησατο). 
2. "A more permanent abode (οικον, house, in verse Acts 7:47), though from the same root as σκηνη." 
3. Like Psalms 132:5, but it was a house that David proposed to build (2 Samuel 7:2), not a tent (σκηνη) which already existed.

### John Gill:
1. "David, who had an interest in the free favour and love of God, was chosen of God, a man after his own heart, and raised up to do his will."
2. "David having a deep sense of the love of God to him, and the grace of God wrought in his heart, was exceeding desirous of finding a place for the building of an house, or fixed habitation for God."
3. "David determines not to go to his house, nor up to his bed, nor give sleep, to his eyes, nor slumber to his eyelids, till he had found out a place for the habitation of the God of Jacob."

### John Wesley:
1. "Who petitioned to find a habitation for the God of Jacob — But he did not obtain his petition: for God remained without any temple till Solomon built him a house."
2. "Observe how wisely the word is chosen with respect to what follows."
