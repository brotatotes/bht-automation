## BHT:
When the audience heard these words, it caused a fierce reaction within them. They were cut deep to the heart, filled with anger and indignation. Their response was not one of remorse or reflection, but rather of uncontrollable rage. They gnashed their teeth at him, just like a pack of furious wolves. 1. 54-56. When they heard these things

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They were cut to the heart - They were exceedingly enraged and indignant."
2. "The whole course of the speech had been such as to excite their anger, and now they could restrain themselves no longer."
3. "They gnashed on him ... - Expressive of the bitterness and malignity of their feeling."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "They were cut. See on ch. Acts 5:33. In both instances, of anger."
2. "A different word is used to express remorse, ch. 2 37."
3. "Gnashed [εβρυχον] . Originally to eat greedily, with a noise, as wild beasts : hence to gnash or grind the teeth."

### John Calvin:
1. "The reprobate, who are at Satan’s commandment, must needs be thus moved with the hearing of the word of God; and this is the state of the gospel, it driveth hypocrites into madness."
2. "Yet, notwithstanding, this ought not to be ascribed to the doctrine of salvation, whose end is rather this, to tame men’s minds to obey God after that it hath subdued them."
3. "And it is certain that those which will flatter the wicked do not respect the fruit, but are faint-hearted through fear of danger."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Bitterly as the Sanhedrim felt the sting of Stephen’s reproachful words, as yet they had not proceeded to open violence; this was not used until the open adoration of the Crucified, occasioned by the vision of glory, moved them to an irrepressible fury..." 
2. "The expression ‘to gnash with the teeth’ is frequently used in the Old Testament to signify furious rage..."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They were cut to the heart...Stephen had sent a saw through the hearts of the Pharisees that rasped them to the bone." 
2. "They gnashed on him with their teeth...Literally, They began to gnash their teeth at him (just like a pack of hungry, snarling wolves)." 
3. "Stephen knew that it meant death for him."

### John Gill:
1. "They were cut to the heart; as if they had been sawn asunder; they were filled with anguish, with great pain and uneasiness; they were full of wrath and madness." 
2. "They gnashed on him with their teeth: being enraged at him, and full of fury and indignation against him."
