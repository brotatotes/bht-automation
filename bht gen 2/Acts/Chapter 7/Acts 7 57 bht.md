## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "To men of their mould and in their temper, Stephen's last seraphic words could but bring matters to extremities."
2. "That only revealed the diabolical spirit which they breathed."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They had charged him with blasphemy; and they regarded what he had now said as full proof of it."
2. "And stopped their ears - That they might hear no more blasphemy."
3. "With one accord - In a tumult; unitedly."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Stopped [συνεσχον] . Lit., held together.

### John Calvin:
1. "The preaching of the glory of Christ was unto them such a torment, that they must needs burst through madness."
2. "They were carried violently, as those men which have no hold of themselves use to leap out immoderately."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "When they heard Stephen in his awful joy saying that he beheld ‘the Crucified’ encircled with the visible glory, thus boldly confessing that the Shekinah belonged to Jesus of Nazareth."
2. "The purport of their cries no doubt was identical with the memorable expression of the high priest, recorded by St. Matthew, who, when Jesus claimed as belonging to Him the Majesty of heaven, ‘rent his clothes, saying, He hath spoken blasphemy."
3. "What think ye? They answered and said, He is guilty of death.'"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They held their ears together with their hands and affected to believe Stephen guilty of blasphemy."
2. "Stoning was the Jewish punishment for blasphemy."
3. "Our first introduction to the man who became the greatest of all followers of Jesus Christ."

### John Gill:
1. "They cried out, either to God to avenge the blasphemy, or rather to the sanhedrim to pass a sentence on him."
2. "Stopped their ears; with their fingers, pretending they could not bear the blasphemy that was uttered."
3. "And ran upon him with one accord; without any leave of the sanhedrim, or waiting for their determination."

### John Wesley:
1. They rushed upon him — Before any sentence passed.
