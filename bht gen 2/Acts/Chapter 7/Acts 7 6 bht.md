The quote from Genesis 15:13 speaks about the future experiences of Abraham's descendants. It mentions that his seed will be a stranger in a foreign land, experiencing a sojourn that is not their own. Furthermore, it states that during this time, the people of this land will treat Abraham's descendants with evil intent. The period of their sojourn and mistreatment is not limited to Egypt but extends to other lands as well. ### Albert Barnes:
1. "His seed - His posterity; his descendants."
2. "The word ['sojourn'] is used in opposition to a fixed, permanent home, and is applied to travelers, or foreigners."
3. "The only satisfactory way is by supposing that the 430 years includes the whole time from the calling of Abraham to the departure from Egypt."

### John Calvin:
1. "This history ought to have been of great force, partly to tame their lofty courages, and to teach them modesty; partly to set forth the grace of God, because God had always had a care of that nation."
2. "Bondage is of itself hard and bitter; but when cruelty of masters is added thereunto, it seemeth to be intolerable."
3. "But faith doth know that his words do agree well together amongst themselves, and with his works. And this is the purpose of God, to the end he may extend the sight of our faith the farther, to show his promises afar off, as it were, a long place [space] being put between."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Stephen here quotes the passage to which he had been previously alluding, with a very slight variation, from the LXX. of Genesis 15:14-15, the very words spoken by the Eternal to Abraham His friend, containing the promise, and also an intimation that its fulfilment must not be expected for a long period of years."
2. "It was a touching reminder to his hearers, how mistaken they were to set so superstitious a value on ground of which their great ancestors the friends and specially-protected ones of God had no tenure."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Shall be a sojourner, one dwelling near one's home, but not of it, so a stranger, foreigner, old word..."
2. "In a land not one's own, that belongs to another, alien..."
3. "But in Galatians 3:17 Paul, following the LXX in Exodus 12:40, takes the 430 years to cover the period in Canaan and the stay in Egypt, cutting the sojourn in Egypt to about half."

### John Gill:
1. "That his seed should sojourn in a strange land; or 'be a stranger in a land not theirs';...the natives of them" 
2. "and entreat them evil four hundred years; which must be reckoned not from the time of their going down into Egypt, but from the birth of Isaac"
3. "for the sojourning and evil entreating of Abraham's seed are not to be confined to the land of Egypt, but belong to other lands, where they were within this time"
