# Acts 8:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
For a long time, he had amazed and bewitched them with his sorceries. His magical arts and pranks had captivated their attention and caused them to regard him highly. This demonstrates the power of deception and how easily people can be deluded, even those of high standing. It also serves as an example of Philip's unwavering courage and commitment to the Lord's work, despite not knowing how it would be accomplished. We are reminded that our own wisdom and reasoning are insufficient to protect us from the cunning schemes of Satan.

### John Calvin:
1. "For seeing all men, of what estate soever they were, were deluded, what entrance could the gospel have, especially since it was no mean seducing?"
2. "And besides that we see thereby how mighty the truth is, there is also set before us an example of constancy in Philip, who, though he saw no way, yet doth he set hand to the Lord’s work with a valiant courage, waiting for the success which God should give."
3. "Furthermore, when Luke saith that they were all seduced one with another, we are taught that neither wit, neither all that reason and wisdom which we have, are sufficient to avoid the craftiness of Satan withal."

### Philip Schaff:
