# Acts 8:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Simon, after witnessing the powerful works performed by Philip, became convinced of their authenticity. He believed in the reality of these miracles and the power behind them.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "He was convinced, from the works which Philip did, that he was in league with some powerful spirit."
2. "He viewed baptism as the initiation into communion with that spirit, and expected that he should be able to make use of the higher power thus gained for his own purposes, and unite this new magical power to his own."
3. "All were baptized who professed belief in Jesus as the Messiah: there was therefore no reason for rejecting Simon..."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Left without followers, he thinks it best to join the man who had fairly outstripped him, not without a touch of real conviction."
2. "What a light does this throw on what is called Baptismal Regeneration!"
3. "He continued with Philip - 'was in constant attendance upon' him."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "All this he could believe in entire consistency with his own notions of the power of magic."
2. "People often profess religion because, if they do not, they fear that they will lose their influence, and be left with the ungodly."
3. "He was amazed that Philip could 'really' perform so much greater miracles than 'he' had even pretended to."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Continued with. See on ch. Acts 1:14."
2. "Miracles and signs [σημεια και δυναμεις] . Lit., signs and powers."
3. "He was amazed. After having amazed the people by his tricks. See ver. 9. The same word is employed."

### John Calvin:
1. "He which had boasted himself to be the principal power of God submitteth himself to God... that offense might be taken out of the way which might have hindered the unskillful."
2. "There is some mean between faith and mere dissimulation... they receive doctrine; for they conceive that God must be heard; that he is both the author and also the judge of the world."
3. "It appeareth plainly, by this example of Simon that all men have not that grace given them in baptism, which grace is there figured... the Lord offereth to us by the sacraments, whatsoever... they are not offered in vain, so that... we set [seek] from him whatsoever the sacraments do promise."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "It is not necessary to assume that the unhappy man was simply moved by the persuasion that Philip was a greater magician than himself, though no doubt this thought influenced him."
2. "He seems to have accepted the fact that Philip’s Master was in some way or other the long-looked-for Messiah."
3. "His heart all the while, though receiving the historic fact, remaining utterly unchanged and hardened."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He wanted this power that Philip had to add to his own pretensions."
2. "He was determined to get this new 'power,' but had no sense of personal need of Jesus as Saviour for his sins."
3. "The more he watched the more the wonder grew."

### John Gill:
1. "With an historical and temporary faith, as that Jesus was the Messiah..."
2. "He so artfully made [his profession of faith], that Philip could not discover his hypocrisy..."
3. "He was as much amazed at the miraculous performances of Philip, as the inhabitants of Samaria had been at his, which he could observe were real things..."

### John Wesley:
1. And Simon believed — That is, was convinced of the truth.
