# Acts 8:28 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The eunuch, while traveling in his chariot, read the book of Isaiah. This is a commendable practice, as it allows one to seek knowledge and information even during a journey. The prophet Isaiah contains many prophecies about the Messiah, making it an evangelical and valuable book to read. It is important for us to remember God and His ways by engaging in the reading of Scripture, as it can greatly enrich our understanding and faith. Hence, we should strive to make good use of our time and opportunities for reading and studying the Word of God.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "He probably read in the LXX, the use of which was almost universal in Egypt."
2. "The word περιοχή below... is not decisive against this (as if there were περιοχαί only in the Hebrew, not in the LXX)."
3. "Besides, must there not have been περιοχαί in the copies of the LXX read in the synagogues?"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "It says much for his fidelity and value to his royal mistress that he had such liberty."
2. "But the faith in Jehovah and love of His worship and word, with which he was imbued, sufficiently explain this."
3. "But this is not all; for as Philip 'heard him read the prophet Esaias,' he must have been reading aloud and not so as merely to be audible, but in a louder voice than he would naturally have used."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It is well to be in the habit of reading the Scriptures when we are on a journey. It may serve to keep the heart from worldly objects, and secure the affections for God."
2. "It is well at all times to read the Bible. It is one of the means of grace. And it is when we are searching his will that we obtain light and comfort."
3. "The sinner should examine with a candid mind the sacred volume. It may be the means of conducting him in the true path of salvation."

### John Calvin:
1. "The reading of the prophet showeth that the eunuch did not worship a God unadvisedly, according to the understanding of his own head, whom he had reigned to himself, but whom he knew by the doctrine of the law."
2. "Therefore, those which are God’s scholars do worship aright only, that is, those who are taught in his school."
3. "Thus must we also read the Scriptures. We must greedily, and with a prompt mind, receive those things which are plain, and wherein God openeth his mind."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He was returning home, deeply impressed with the sanctuary, the wonders of which he had just been beholding, and whose strange, glorious history had so deeply interested him..." 
2. "Probably the passage he was meditating on was one of those to which his attention had been just called in Jerusalem as referring to the sufferings of Messiah..."
3. "The scriptures he was reading were the Greek version of the LXX., well known throughout Egypt and the adjacent countries."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He was reading aloud as Philip 'heard him reading,' a common practice among orientals."
2. "He had probably purchased this roll of Isaiah in Jerusalem and was reading the LXX Greek text."
3. "See imperfect again in verse Acts 8:32."

### John Gill:
1. "This prophet is a very evangelical one, and very delightful and profitable to read: many things are prophesied by him concerning the Messiah."
2. "However, the eunuch improved his time, as he was travelling in a religious way, which was very commendable."
3. "It may serve greatly to recommend the reading of the Scriptures."

### John Wesley:
1. "Sitting in his chariot, he read the Prophet Isaiah — God meeteth those that remember him in his ways."
2. "It is good to read, hear, seek information even in a journey."
3. "Why should we not redeem all our time?"
