# Acts 8:29 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Spirit spoke to Philip, prompting him internally, and it was the Holy Spirit who communicated with him in this way. He was moved by a secret impulse upon him. Philip was directed to get closer to the chariot he saw on the road and follow it attentively. He was instructed not to leave until an opportunity to converse with the person inside was presented.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "This is the first mention of that inner prompting of the Spirit referred to again, probably ch. Acts 13:2, but certainly ch. Acts 10:19; Acts 16:6-7."
2. "Chrysostom understands the words of the appearance of an angel, but the text hardly allows it."
3. "κολλ.] no stress attach thyself to."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "the Spirit said—by an unmistakable voice within"
2. "go near and join this chariot—This would reveal to Philip the hitherto unknown object of his journey, and encourage him to expect something."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The Holy Spirit is evidently intended here."
2. "All good thoughts and designs have the same origin."
3. "Join him in his chariot. Go and sit with him."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Join thyself [κολληθητι] . See on Luke 14:15; Luke 10:11; Acts 5:12.

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Join thyself (κολληθητ). See this vivid word (be glued to, first aorist passive imperative) already in Acts 5:13; Luke 10:11; Luke 15:15."


### John Gill:
1. "The Spirit said unto Philip... the Holy Spirit... who spoke unto him... by a secret impulse upon him." 
2. "He bid him make up to the chariot, he saw driving on the road at such a distance, and follow it, and attend it closely."
3. "Do not leave it, till an opportunity of conversing with the person in it offered."
