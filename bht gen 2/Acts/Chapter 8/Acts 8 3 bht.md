# Acts 8:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Saul's actions can be likened to a wild beast ravaging its prey, as he unleashed his fury upon the Church. His persecution spared no one, as he targeted both men and women without regard for age or gender. No one was safe from his destructive pursuit.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "ἐλυμαίνετο" - Properly used of wild beasts, or of hostile armies, devastating and ravaging.
2. "κατὰ τοὺς οἴκους, entering (the houses) from house to house, a pregnant construction."
3. "σύρων" - So Philo, in Flacc. 9, vol. ii. p. 526, συρόμενοι κ. πατούμενοι διὰ τῆς πόλεως ἁπάσης ἐξαναλώθησαν.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Saul . . . entering into every house—like as inquisitor."
2. "Haling men and women."
3. "See his own affecting confessions afterwards."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Saul raged against the church like a wild beast - a strong expression, denoting the zeal and fury with which he engaged in persecution."
2. "It may be remarked here that there never was a persecution commenced with more flattering prospects to the persecutors."
3. "That the church was not destroyed was owing to the protection of God, a protection which not only secured its existence, but which extended its influence and power by means of this very persecution far abroad on the earth."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The part which he played at this time in the horrid work of persecution has, I fear, been always underrated."
2. "It is only when we collect the separate passages - they are no less than eight in number - in which allusion is made to this sad period, it is only when we weigh the terrible significance of the expressions used that we feel the load of remorse which must have lain upon him, and the taunts to which he was liable from malignant enemies."
3. "Note the imperfect, of continued action."

### John Calvin:
1. "We must note two things in this place, how great the cruelty of the adversaries was, and how wonderful the goodness of God was, who vouchsafed to make Paul a pastor of so cruel a wolf."
2. "For that desire to lay waste the Church wherewith he was incensed did seem to cut away all hope. Therefore his conversion was so much the more excellent afterward."
3. "And it is not to be doubted but that this punishment was laid upon him by God... that he should be the ringleader of cruelty."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "As for Saul, he made havoc of the church." 
2. "Every possible effort he used to make them blaspheme that holy Name whereby they were called."
3. "His fame as an inquisitor was notorious far and wide; even in Damascus, Ananias had heard how much evil he had done to the saints of Christ at Jerusalem."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Picturesque description of the havoc carried on by Saul now the leader in the persecution."
2. "Plainly not just an 'assembly,' but an organized body that was still 'the church' when scattered in their own homes."
3. "A new feature of the persecution that includes the women. They met it bravely as through all the ages since."

### John Gill:
1. "He made havoc of the church...did evil to the saints, destroyed them that called upon the name of Christ." (Acts 9:13)
2. "Now did Benjamin, of which tribe Saul was, ravine as a wolf." (Genesis 49:27)
3. "Entering into every house; where the saints dwelt: and haling men and women...committed them to prison." (Acts 22:4)

### John Wesley:
1. Saul made havoc of the Church — Like some furious beast of prey. So the Greek word properly signifies.Men and women — Regarding neither age nor sex.
