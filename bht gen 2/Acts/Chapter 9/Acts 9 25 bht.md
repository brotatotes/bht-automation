# Acts 9:25 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
When Saul faced the threat of danger, the disciples and followers of Christ took him in the night season and let him down by the wall in a basket. This act, guided by the providence of God, allowed Saul to escape from those who sought to harm him. It serves as a reminder that it is permissible to employ wise and appropriate measures to thwart the plans of the wicked and protect one's well-being. In doing so, we see the grace of God at work, preserving the life of the apostle and demonstrating the sanctity of life.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The only escape from this inference is by supposing an unusual government of a gen. by λαβόντες, such as we sometimes find in Homer..."
2. "[The wall with windows] is further particularized by the addition of διὰ θυρίδος."
3. "σπυρίδι σαργάνη, 2 Corinthians 11:33."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Such overhanging windows in the walls of Eastern cities were common, and are to be seen in Damascus to this day."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Took him by night ... - This was done through a window in the wall."
2. "In a basket - This word is used to denote commonly 'the basket in which food was carried.'"
3. "This conduct of Saul was in accordance with the direction of the Lord Jesus... Saul was certain of death if he remained; and as he could secure his life by flight without abandoning any principle of religion, or denying his Lord, it was his duty to do so."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "By the wall [δια του τειχους] . Rev., more accurately, through the wall, as is explained by 2 Corinthians 11:33."
2. "Either through the window of a house overhanging the wall, or through a window in the wall itself opening to houses on its inner side."
3. "In Paul 's account of this adventure he uses sarganh, a plaited or braided basket of wicker - work; or, as some think, of ropes."

### John Calvin:
1. "Therefore, it was no less lawful for the faithful to be let down in a basket, than it shall be lawful for any private person to leap over a wall, that he may avoid the sudden invasion of the enemy."
2. "Nevertheless, we see how the Lord useth to humble those that be his, seeing that Paul is enforced to steal his life from the watchmen of the city if he will save himself."
3. "He was acquainted betime with the cross with this first exercise."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Paul in 2 Corinthians 11:33 explains δια του τειχους as being δια θυριδος (through a window) which opened into the house on the inside of the wall."
2. "Same verb used by Paul of this experience (2 Corinthians 11:33)."
3. "This escape by night by the help of the men whom he had come to destroy was a shameful memory to Paul (2 Corinthians 11:33)."

### John Gill:
1. "The disciples and followers of Christ... took Saul in the night season and let him down by the wall in a basket." 
2. "The disciples were directed by the overruling providence of God, in order to preserve the life of the apostle... it shows that it is lawful to make use of all prudent means and proper methods to prevent the designs of wicked men, and escape out of their hands, and preserve life when in danger."
