## BHT:
Saul, later known as Paul, was allowed to be in the company of the disciples in Jerusalem, affirming his acceptance and unity with them. He boldly proclaimed the Gospel, demonstrating his unwavering courage and commitment to the spreading of God's message. Even amidst challenges and opposition, he remained resolute, steadfastly following his faith. His presence and actions were not met with resistance, but rather, he was embraced and supported by the other apostles.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 28, 29. And he was with them, coming
	in and going out at Jerusalem—for fifteen days, lodging with
	Peter (Galatians 1:18).

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He was admitted to their friendship, and recognized as a Christian and an apostle."
2. "The time during which he then remained at Jerusalem was, however, only fifteen days."

### John Calvin:
1. "Luke saith again that he dealt boldly in the name of the Lord, by which words he commendeth his (stoutness and) courage in professing the gospel."
2. "For he durst never have whispered amidst so many lets, unless he had been endowed with rare constancy."
3. "Nevertheless, all men are taught what they ought to do; to wit, every man according to the measure of his faith."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  Going in and going out  (εισπορευμενος κα εκπορευομενος). Barnabas and Peter and James opened all the doors for Saul and the fear of the disciples vanished. 

### John Gill:
1. "He lived with them, conversed with them, and joined with them in all religious exercises..."
2. "...signified by his coming in and going out at Jerusalem..."
3. "...no one of the apostles or disciples forbidding or hindering him."
