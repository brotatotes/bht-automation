# Acts 9:29 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
He fearlessly proclaimed the Gospel of Christ boldly, openly, and faithfully, not intimidated by opposition. Engaging in debates with the Hellenistical Jews, those born or raised in Greece who were well-versed in the Greek language and scriptures. In response to being challenged and unable to counter his arguments, they resorted to plotting his murder, filled with indignation at his intellectual prowess.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "This he did, partly, we may infer, to avoid the extreme and violent opposition which he would immediately encounter from the Jews themselves."
2. "But partly also, it may well be believed, because he himself in the synagogues of the Hellenists had opposed Stephen formerly."
3. "See ch. Act 6:1 and note."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "29. disputed against the Grecians—(See on Acts 6:1); addressing himself specially to them, perhaps, as being of his own class, and that against which he had in the days of his ignorance been the fiercest." 
2. "they went about to slay him—Thus was he made to feel, throughout his whole course, what he himself had made others so cruelly to feel, the cost of discipleship."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He openly defended the doctrine that Jesus was the Messiah."
2. "By the authority of the Lord Jesus."
3. "They sought to slay him; or they formed a purpose to put him to death as an apostate."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Grecians. Rev., correctly, Grecian Jews. See on ch. Acts 6:1. Went about [επεχειρουν] . Better, attempted : lit., took in hand.

### John Calvin:
1. "The godly must be incensed with an holy wrath, when they see the pure truth of God corrupted with false and wicked opinions; yet, so that they moderate their zeal, that they set down nothing until they have thoroughly weighed the cause..."
2. "But hypocrites are always ready to shed blood before they know the matter; so that superstition is bloody, through blind and headlong fury."
3. "And yet this estate was far better for him than if he should have reigned in peace and quietness, driving the godly everywhere out of their places."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "These Grecians or Hellenists were Jews who, in the ordinary intercourse of life, used the Greek language."
2. "It has been suggested that these disputes were probably held in the same Cilician synagogue at Jerusalem, of which Saul in old days had been so distinguished a Rabbi."
3. "Saul held his famous disputation with Stephen, the martyr deacon."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Preaching boldly (παρρησιαζομενος)."
2. "Disputed (συνεζητε). Imperfect active of συνζητεω, the very verb used in Acts 6:9 of the disputes with Stephen."
3. "But they went about to kill him (Hο δε επεχειρουν ανελειν αυτον)."

### John Gill:
1. "He spoke the Gospel of Christ boldly, as it ought to be spoken; he spoke it openly, publicly, freely, and faithfully, not fearing the faces or revilings of any."
2. "He disputed against the Grecians; or Hellenists, that is, the Hellenistical Jews; by whom are meant, not Greeks proselyted to the Jewish religion, but Jews who had been born and brought up in Greece, or at least had learned the Greek language, and used the Greek Bible."
3. "But they went about to slay him; he being an over match for them; and they not being able to resist him, but being confuted, and confounded, and put to silence, they were filled with indignation."
