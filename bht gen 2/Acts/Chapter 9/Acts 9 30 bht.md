## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "He was praying in the temple, and saw the Lord in a vision, who commanded him to depart, for they would not receive his testimony: and sent him from thence to the Gentiles." 
2. "From the whole cast of the sentence, the κατήγαγον and ἐξαπέστειλαν, we should infer this to be Cæsarea Stratonis, even if this were not determined by the word Καισάρεια used absolutely, which always applies to this city, and not to Cæsarea Philippi."
3. "He was not idle there, but certainly preached the Gospel, and in all probability was the founder of the churches alluded to."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "While he was praying in the temple, he was in a trance," and received express injunctions to this effect.
2. "It probably was now that he became the instrument of gathering into the fold of Christ those "kinsmen," that "sister," and perhaps her "son."
3. "FLOURISHING STATE OF THE CHURCH IN PALESTINE AT THIS TIME."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "To Cesarea - See the notes on Acts 8:40."
2. "And sent him forth to Tarsus - This was his native city. See the notes on Acts 9:11."
3. "It was in Cilicia, where Paul doubtless preached the gospel, Galatians 1:21, 'Afterward I came into the regions of Syria and Cilicia.'"

### John Calvin:
1. "In that he went to Tarsus, he did it undoubtedly to this end, that he might carry the doctrine of the gospel thither."
2. "He hoped that he should have some favor and authority in his country, where he was famous."
3. "Yet was he brought thither by the brethren, that they might deliver him from the lying in wait."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The writer of the ‘Acts’ tells us, it was in consequence of the enmity of the Jews, who feared the able and powerful arguments of their former associate, that Paul departed from Jerusalem."
2. "In spite of the dangerous hostility of the Jews which threatened his life, Saul in his enthusiasm would have remained in the city had he not received, as he tells us, a direct warning from heaven."
3. "In his own family one may well imagine that some of those Christian kinsmen whose names are handed down to us, possibly his sister, the playmate of his childhood, and his sister’s son, who afterwards saved his life, were by his exertions gathered into the fold of Christ."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The disciples saw it clearly, so they conducted."
2. "Sent him out and off to Tarsus."
3. "But it takes little imagination to picture the scene at home when this brilliant young rabbi, the pride of Gamaliel, returns home a preacher of the despised Jesus of Nazareth whose disciples he had so relentlessly persecuted."

### John Gill:
1. "When the brethren knew... they brought him down to Caesarea."
2. "His own native place, where he might be more safe, and also useful among his friends and acquaintance."
