As Peter embarked on his journey to visit the new churches, his purpose was to strengthen the Gospel through his presence and miraculous works. Among the places he visited was Lydda, a renowned city with Jewish scholars. The mention of Peter's visit to Lydda highlights the remarkable miracle that he performed there, reflecting the power of his ministry.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "These words are aptly introduced by the notice in Acts 9:31, which shews that Peter’s journey was not an escape from persecution, but undertaken at a time of peace, and for the purpose of visiting the churches."
2. "It seems most likely that it was before his arrival [of Saul in Jerusalem]."
3. "A large village near Joppa (Act 9:38), on the Mediterranean, just one day’s journey from Jerusalem."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Peter, not fleeing from persecution, but peacefully visiting the churches."
2. "Lydda, about five miles east of Joppa, where the saints dwelt."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "To the saints - To the Christians."
2. "This town was situated on the road from Jerusalem to Caesarea Philippi."
3. "It is now so entirely ruined as to be a miserable village."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Lydda. The Lod of the Old Testament (Ezra 2:33); about a day's journey from Jerusalem.

### John Calvin:
1. "And it was meet that the apostles, who had no certain place of abode, should wander hither and thither as occasion was offered."
2. "Wherefore, whilst they are all occupied in divers parts, Peter took upon him this charge, whereby the foolishness of the Papists is refuted."
3. "But would to God the bishop of Rome, who will be counted Peter’s successor, would travel as he did to animate the brethren, and would every where prove indeed that he is the apostle of Christ."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The 'society,' which now numbered in its ranks many thousand converts from Judaism, in the Holy Land, Syria, and perhaps even in more distant countries, was to be freed for ever from the trammels with which the Mosaic laws, and the traditionary customs and rites which had grown up in the course of ages round it, had hitherto shackled it."
2. "The human instrument of this startling change of policy in the 'society' was Peter, hitherto the acknowledged head of the Church of Jerusalem."
3. "The circumstances which happened at Lydda and Joppa, places which he visited in the course of this circuit, may be looked upon as examples of many similar unrelated instances in the great apostle’s early career."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  Lydda  (Λυδδα). In O.T. Lod (1 Chronicles 8:12) and near Joppa. Later Diospolis. 

### John Gill:
1. "Peter took a tour, in order to visit the new churches... and confirm the Gospel by miracles."
2. "Lydda... a city... famous for Jewish doctors."
3. "Peter, among the rest, visited the saints [at Lydda]... and which is mentioned for the sake of the miracle he there wrought."
