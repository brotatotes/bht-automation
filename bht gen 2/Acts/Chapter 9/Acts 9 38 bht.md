# Acts 9:38 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The disciples sent for him, indicating that the disciples at Joppa did not possess miraculous gifts. It is also uncertain whether they had any particular expectation of a miracle from him.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 38. the disciples sent unto
	Peter—showing that the disciples generally did not possess
	miraculous gifts [BENGEL].

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They sent unto him ... - why they sent is not affirmed."
2. "It is probable that they desired his presence to comfort and sustain them in their affliction."
3. "We have no authority for assuming, or supposing, that they had formed any such expectation."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Delay not."
2. "To come."
3. "Come on."

### John Calvin:
1. "The washing of the corpse showeth that the disciples knew not what would come to pass, for by this means they make the corpse ready to be buried."
2. "Yet this is some token of hope, that they lay her in an upper chamber, and send to Peter."
3. "Furthermore, they murmur not against God, neither do they cry out that it is an unmeet thing; but they humbly crave God’s help, not that they will make Tabitha immortal, but their only desire is to have her life prolonged for a time, that she may yet profit the Church."

### Philip Schaff:
1. Acts 9:38.  Lydda was nigh to Joppa.  About nine or ten miles was the distance of Lydda from Joopa.
	

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Delay not." (μη οκνησηις). Ingressive aorist active subjunctive in prohibition.
2. "Possibly the two messengers started before Dorcas was quite dead, though we do not know."
3. "Peter had recently healed Aeneas and the disciples may have had faith enough to believe that he could raise the dead by the power of Christ."

### John Gill:
1. "And forasmuch as Lydda was nigh to Joppa,.... Some say it was six miles distant, but it must be eight..."
2. "they sent unto him two men; very likely of their own company or church..."
3. "desiring him, that he would not delay to come to them; they entreated he would not refuse to come..."

### John Wesley:
1. The disciples sent to him — Probably none of those at Joppa had the gift of miracles. Nor is it certain that they expected a miracle from him.
