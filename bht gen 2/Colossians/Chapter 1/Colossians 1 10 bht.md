# Colossians 1:10 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
To truly walk worthy of the Lord, we must live in accordance with His will and strive to please Him in all aspects of our lives. This means seeking to know Him deeply and intimately, growing in our understanding and experience of who He is and what He desires for us. Through faith in Christ, we are able to please God and perform good works that are acceptable to Him. By walking in this way, we can bring glory to His name and fulfill our calling as His followers.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "True knowledge of God's will is inseparable from walking conformably to it."
2. "Being fruitful is the first manifestation of their 'walking worthy of the Lord.'"
3. "The full knowledge of God is the real instrument of enlargement in soul and life of the believer."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "That ye might walk worthy of the Lord - That you may live as becomes the followers of the Lord."
2. "Being fruitful in every good work - This is one way in which we are to walk worthy of the Lord, and so as to please him."
3. "And increasing in the knowledge of God - This is another way in which we may walk worthy of the Lord, and so as to please him."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Walk worthy...worthily...so as to please God in all ways."
2. "In the knowledge...by means of the knowledge."
3. "The phrase occurs...gives the correct adverbial rendering."

### John Calvin:
1. "Whoever they may be that do not direct their endeavors towards this object, may possibly toil and labor much, but they do nothing better than wander about in endless windings, without making any progress."
2. "If we would walk worthy of God, we must above all things take heed that we regulate our whole course of life according to the will of God, renouncing our own understanding, and bidding farewell to all the inclinations of our flesh."
3. "He again repeats, that they have not arrived at such perfection as not to stand in need of farther increase; by which admonition he prepares them, and as it were leads them by the hand, to an eagerness for proficiency, that they may show themselves ready to listen, and teachable."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Worthy of the torn, i.e., Christ, since Paul seems always to use the term with this reference, except in citations from the Old Testament. Christians belong to Christ, and hence their conduct should correspond."
2. "Unto all pleasing; to please Christ in all things is the true end (‘unto’) as well as the manner of the Christian walk."
3. "Being fruitful, etc. As the phrase ‘in every good work’ comes first, some have joined it with ‘unto all pleasing;’ others join it with both of the participles. But the view indicated by the E. V. is preferable, since it preserves the symmetry of the construction."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The end of all knowledge is conduct" (Lightfoot).
2. "This is the way for fruit-bearing and growth to come."
3. "In order to please God in all things."

### John Gill:
1. "To walk worthy of him, is to walk by faith in him; to walk after his Spirit, and according to his word, and in his ordinances; to have the conversation as becomes his Gospel, and worthy of that calling wherewith the saints are called by grace to the obtaining of his kingdom and glory."
2. "An unregenerate man cannot please God in anything [...] nor is there anything they can do that is pleasing to God but through Christ."
3. "Good works being done in faith, and from a principle of love, and with a view to the glory of God, are acceptable unto him through Christ."

### John Wesley:
1. "Knowing his whole will, ye may walk worthy of the Lord, unto all pleasing - So as actually to please him in all things"
2. "daily increasing in the living, experimental knowledge of God, our Father, Saviour, Sanctifier"
