# Colossians 1:25 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Paul asserts that he was made a minister by God and entrusted with a stewardship to dispense the gospel to the Church. He emphasizes that his role as a minister is not to save or redeem the Church, but to serve it and minister to it in holy things. As a steward, he is responsible for fulfilling the word of God and faithfully preaching the Gospel to all, leaving nothing profitable untold. This highlights the divine arrangement and authority behind his ministry, underscoring the power and universal reach of the gospel message.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "I was made a minister": resuming, "whereof I Paul was made a minister."
2. "The stewardship committed to me to dispense in the house of God, the Church, to the whole family of believers, the goods of my Master."
3. "To bring it fully to all: the end of his stewardship: 'fully preached'."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "According to the dispensation of God - The arrangement which God has made."
2. "That is, he designed that the gospel should be preached to the Gentiles, and, in accordance with that arrangement, he has called me to be a minister."
3. "To fulfil the word of God - Margin, 'fully to preach.' The Greek is, 'to fill up the word of God;' the meaning is, 'fully to teach and promulgate the gospel.'"

### John Calvin:
1. "Mark under what character he suffers for the Church — as being a minister, not to give the price of redemption, but to proclaim it."
2. "For this is the excellence of the gospel, that it is the power of God unto salvation to every one that believeth."
3. "God, therefore, gives efficacy and influence to his word through means of the Apostles."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "In the Church there is no true ministry apart from the ministry of the gospel." 
2. "His Apostolic office is thus described as of God, belonging to God. In the Church, the house of God, he exercises the function of a steward." 
3. "The reference seems to be to carrying the word of God to the Gentiles as a whole, thus filling out the full measure of its universal destination. Thus the duties of Paul’s stewardship would be discharged."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "According to the economy of God."
2. "It was by God's stewardship that Paul was made a minister of Christ."
3. "To fulfil the word of God."

### John Gill:
1. "He was not a saviour of the body, nor a redeemer of the church, nor Lord of it; but a minister, a servant of it, that ministered to it in holy things."
2. "God is the householder or master of the family; Christ is the Son over his own house; ministers are stewards in it, and their work is to give to everyone their portion of meat in due season."
3. "To preach fully and faithfully the Gospel, keeping back nothing that was profitable, but declaring the whole counsel of God, continuing faithful to it to the end."

### John Wesley:
1. According to the dispensation of God which is given me — Or, the stewardship with which I am intrusted.
