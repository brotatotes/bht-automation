## BHT:
The Gospel is a profound and wondrous secret plan of God's redemption for all mankind, both Jews and Gentiles. It was concealed throughout the generations of the past, but now, in these times, it has been made known to His saints. This revelation was communicated to the apostles and prophets, who were chosen to proclaim this truth. The Gospel, once hidden, is now revealed to all who are willing to receive it. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "the mystery (see on Eph 1:9) which has been hidden from the ages and the generations" 
2. "but now (in these times) was manifested to His saints"
3. "the Apostles and prophets, as some of the Commentators have explained it"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The mystery, once hidden, now revealed, is redemption for the whole Gentile world, as well as for the Jews, 'Christ in you (Gentiles) the hope of glory'."
2. "The 'ages' are the vast successive periods marked by successive orders of beings and stages of creation."
3. "The Spirit by Paul teaches, that the mystery of redemption was hidden in God's purposes in Christ, alike from the angelic beings of the pre-Adamic 'ages,' and from the subsequent human 'generations'."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The great doctrine that salvation was to be proclaimed to all mankind, Paul says, had been concealed for many generations. Hence, it was called a mystery, or a hidden truth."
2. "But now is made manifest to his saints - It was communicated especially to the apostles who were appointed to proclaim it, and through them to all the saints."
3. "Paul says that he regarded himself as specially called to make this truth known, as far as possible, to mankind."

### John Calvin:
1. "Here we have a commendation of the gospel â€” that it is a wonderful secret of God."
2. "An attentive consideration of these things constrains us to reverence and adore this mystery which Paul proclaims, however it may be held in contempt by the world, or even in derision."
3. "Hence, there is no reason why its obscurity should alarm us, after the revelation that God has made of it."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Even the mystery, etc...the reference is not merely to the salvation of the Gentiles, but to Redemption in Christ as belonging to the eternal plan of God."
2. "This particular phrase is peculiar...the concealment from the beginning of human history is marked."
3. "Now...it was made manifest...The tense points to the single past act, but our English idiom requires 'hath been.'...the change of construction sharpens the contrast...It is properly referred to the entire historical manifestation."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Paul takes their very word (already in common use, Matthew 13:11) and uses it for the gospel." 
2. "The Gnostics talked much of 'mysteries.'"
3. "The construction is suddenly changed (anacoluthon) from the participle to the finite verb."

### John Gill:
1. "The Gospel, called 'the mystery', as it often is; because it contains things, which, though revealed, are mysteries to a natural man."
2. "The Gospel mystery...was first hid in the heart of God, in his thoughts and purposes, in his counsel and covenant, and in his Son, in whom are hid all the treasures of wisdom and knowledge."
3. "Now under the Gospel dispensation, since the coming of Christ; there is an external revelation of the Gospel by him, more clearly, by whom grace and truth came, called the revelation of Christ; and an internal revelation of it by his Spirit, who is the spirit of wisdom and revelation, in the knowledge of him; which is made to saints."
