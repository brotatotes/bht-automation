# Colossians 1:29 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In his ministry, he labors to present every person complete in Christ through preaching, warning, teaching, and guidance. He longs to bring a multitude of them before Christ as proof of his service and the grace bestowed upon them. This striving is not in his own strength, but by the power of Christ working mightily within him, enabling him to effectively preach the Gospel and make a lasting impact on countless souls.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "His own personal part in this general work."
2. "I also toil in conflict."
3. "According to His working which worketh in me in power."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "I labor also. striving in 'conflict' of spirit...literally, 'agonizing,' 'striving as in the agony of a contest.'"
2. "Paul avows that he has power to 'strive' in spirit for his converts, so far only as Christ works in him and by him."
3. "Mightily - literally, 'in power.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "We should rejoice in the piety of others; Colossians 1:2-8. It should be to us a subject of unfeigned gratitude to God; when others are faithful to their high calling, and when they so live as to adorn the blessed gospel."
2. "In all our wants let us go to Christ; Colossians 1:19, 'It pleased the Father that in him should all fulness dwell.' We do not have a need which he cannot supply; there is not a sorrow of our lives in which he cannot comfort us; not a temptation from which he cannot deliver us."
3. "It is a privilege to suffer for the welfare of the church; Colossians 1:24. Paul regarded it as such and rejoiced in the trials which came upon him in the cause of religion. The Saviour so regarded it, and shrank not from the great sorrows involved in the work of saving his people."

### John Calvin:
1. "For which thing. He enhances, by two circumstances, the glory of his apostleship and of his doctrine."
2. "Those things are for the most part the most excellent that are the most difficult."
3. "He does not speak, however, merely of the success of his preaching, but also of the efficacy of the Spirit, in which God manifestly showed himself."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "I labor also. Besides preaching, he labors in every way."
2. "The minister of the Word labors with the Eternal on the Eternal 'for eternity,' more than the artist."
3. "Often the truest success springs from the severest conflicts and from apparent defeat."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "To present every man perfect in Christ."
2. "Striving... to contend in athletic games, to agonize."
3. "Paul was conscious of God's 'energy' at work in him 'mightily', 'in power' like dynamite."

### John Gill:
1. "In the word and doctrine, by preaching Christ, warning sinners of their danger, teaching them the way of salvation, and their duty."
2. "That, in the great day of account, he might bring a large number of them, and set them before Christ as the seals of his ministry, as instances of the grace of Christ, and as perfect in him."
3. "Striving according to his working, which worketh in me mightily; meaning...through the power of Christ; which enabled him to preach the Gospel far and near, in season and out of season; which supported his outward man, and strengthened his inward man for that service, and made it effectual to the good of the souls of many."
