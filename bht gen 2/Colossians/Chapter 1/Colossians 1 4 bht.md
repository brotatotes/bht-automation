# Colossians 1:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Having heard of their faith in Christ, their sincere and constant profession of it is a testimony to their soundness and steadfastness. The grace of love, which is always present where true faith in Christ is, is evident in how they serve one another, relieve those in need, sympathize in distress, and pray together. It is encouraging to hear of the effects of their love, not merely in words but in their actions.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Having heard."
2. "The language implies that he had only heard of, and not seen, them."
3. "Compare Romans 1:8, where like language is used of a Church which he had not at the time visited."
4. "Love...to all."
5. "The absent, as well as those present."
6. "[BENGEL]."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "This does not prove that Paul had never been at Colossae, or that he did not establish the church there, for he uses a similar expression respecting the church at Ephesus Ephesians 1:15, of which he was undoubtedly the founder."
2. "The meaning is, that he had heard of their faith at that time, or of their perseverance in faith and love."
3. "Nothing could be more acceptable information respecting them to one who himself so ardently loved the church; and nothing could have furnished better evidence that they were influenced by the true spirit of religion."

### John Calvin:
1. "Having heard of your faith...that they were distinguished by faith and love."
2. "Gifts of God...ought to have such an effect upon us as to stir us up to love them wherever they appear."
3. "True love...will extend to mankind universally, but in respect of degrees, it will begin with those who are of the household of faith."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Having heard. 'After hearing,' rather than, 'since we heard,' or 'because we heard.'"
2. "Your faith in Christ Jesus. He is the sphere and object of the faith; comp. Ephesians 1:15."
3. "The love which ye have to all the saints. The Italics of the E. V. are unnecessary, since the better-supported reading gives 'which ye have,' thus emphasizing the tact that they possess it."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Having heard of (ακουσαντες). Literary plural unless Timothy is included."
2. "Aorist active participle of ακουω of antecedent action to ευχαριστουμεν. Epaphras (verse Colossians 1:8) had told Paul."
3. "There is a real distinction here between εν (sphere or basis) and εις (direction towards), though they are often identical in idea."

### John Gill:
1. "Their faith in Christ; by which is not only meant their hearty assent to the whole doctrine of faith, concerning the person, offices, and grace of Christ, their soundness and steadfastness in it, and their sincere and constant profession of it..."
2. "The grace of love itself, which is a fruit of the Spirit implanted in regeneration, and is an evidence of the new birth, and always is where true faith in Christ is, for faith works by it..."
3. "The effects of love, which lies not in bare words, in expressions of spiritual affection and friendship, but in deeds, by serving one another in love, by relieving in necessity, sympathizing in distress, praying with and for one another, and the like; all which these saints were famous for."
