# Colossians 2:22 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The actions of touching, tasting, and handling have no lasting impact or significance on a person's true worship of God or their holiness. These transient and perishable things cannot defile or contaminate a person in any meaningful or lasting way.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Which—things, namely, the three things handled, touched, and tasted, are to perish...Therefore they cannot really and lastingly defile a man" (Matthew 15:17; 1 Corinthians 6:13).
2. "All these 'ordinances' are according to human, not divine, injunction" (referring to Colossians 2:20; Colossians 2:21).
3. "Doctrines—Greek, teachings...translates, '(doctrinal) systems'" (ALFORD).

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They were all to perish with the use of them. Nothing was permanent about them. It could really then be of no great importance what was eaten, or what was drunk, provided it was not in itself injurious."
2. "These ordinances had a value among the Hebrews when it was designed to keep them as a distinct people; but they had no value in themselves, so as to make them binding on all mankind."
3. "Many of the ordinances on which the Jews insisted were those which were handed down by tradition. They depended on human authority only, and of course, should not bind the conscience."

### John Calvin:
1. "The worship of God, true piety, and the holiness of Christians, do not consist in drink, and food, and clothing, which are things that are transient and liable to corruption, and perish by abuse."  
2. "Enactments are of no value in reference to those things which tend to excite scruples of conscience."  
3. "Those who bring consciences into bondage do injury to Christ, and make void his death. For whatever is of human invention does not bind conscience."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "This verse is parenthetical, and describes the character of the objects prohibited: which all are to perish with the using: all of them appointed to destruction by being used up."
2. "The view here advocated is accepted by the best recent commentators, and agrees with our Lord’s own words in Matthew 15:17; Matthew 7:18-19; comp. 1 Corinthians 6:13."
3. "'Doctrines’ is a term of wider signification than ‘precepts;’ but both are united here and described as ‘of men,’ in contrast ‘with God’s law and word in Christ, indeed with the law of Moses, beyond which they have gone’ (Braune)."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Are for perishing in the using." 
2. "These material things all perish in the use of them."

### John Gill:
1. "The ordinances concerning touching, tasting, and handling, which bring destruction and death on them that use them."
2. "The meats not to be touched, tasted, or handled; these are in their own nature perishing things, and perish by being used."
3. "The ordinances concerning them are not of God, but are after the commandments and doctrines of men."

### John Wesley:
1. Perish in the using — Have no farther use, no influence on the mind.
