# Colossians 2:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Though I am not physically present with you, my spirit is there, rejoicing in the unity and steadfastness of your faith. I see your orderly arrangement, like a well-trained army, standing firm against any attacks or temptations. It fills me with joy and admiration to witness a community of believers who walk together in Gospel order, remaining grounded in their faith in Christ and faithfully upholding the teachings of the Word.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "I am also absent in the flesh, yet in my spirit I am with you, rejoicing."
2. "Seeing your order and the solid basis of your faith on Christ."
3. "The solid basis of your faith on Christ."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "the same Greek as that for knit together, is used of the body" of the Church compacted"
2. "steadfastness expresses the inner basis on which their Church rested"
3. "their 'faith' here is the solid thing which constituted the basis of their Church"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "I seem to see you; I feel as if I were there, and were looking upon you."
2. "I have the same solicitude as if I were there, and saw all the danger which exists that your beautiful order and harmony should be disturbed."
3. "I rejoice as if I saw your order."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Orderly array. A military metaphor, quite possibly suggested by Paul's intercourse with the soldiers in his confinement."
2. "Steadfastness. Only here in the New Testament. The military metaphor is continued. Faith is represented as a host solidly drawn up: your solid front, close phalanx."
3. "The firmament. In Esther 9:22, of the confirmation of a letter."

### John Calvin:
1. "For though I am absent in body. [...] his affection towards them made him be present with them in spirit, and judge of what is expedient for them, as though he were present."
2. "By praising, also, their present condition, he admonishes them not to fall back from it, or turn aside. Rejoicing, says he, And seeing, that is — 'Because I see.' For and means for, as is customary among the Latins and Greeks."
3. "Order and steadfastness. He mentions two things, in which the perfection of the Church consists — order among themselves, and faith in Christ. [...] He commends their faith, in respect of its constancy and steadfastness, meaning that it is an empty shadow of faith, when the mind wavers and vacillates between different opinions." (150 words)

### Philip Schaff:
1. "External bodily presence is contrasted with spiritual presence; with you in the spirit."
2. "The contemplation of their orderly array, although it might have been first the cause, was afterwards the consequence of the Apostle’s rejoicing. He looked, because it gave him satisfaction to look."
3. "A military figure, readily suggested to a Roman prisoner, and referring to the external department of the Church."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "A few stragglers had gone over to the Gnostics, but there had been no panic, no breach in the line."
2. "The solid part of the line which can and does stand the attack of the Gnostics."
3. "In 2 Thessalonians 3:6; 2 Thessalonians 3:8; 2 Thessalonians 3:11 Paul speaks of his own ταξις (orderly conduct)."

### John Gill:
1. "Though I be absent in the flesh... yet am I with you in the spirit." 
2. "I rejoice that I can see your order."
3. "To see a set of Christians, a church of Christ walking together in Gospel order, steady in their faith on Christ, abiding by the doctrine of faith, and maintaining an honorable profession, how beautiful and delightful is it!"
