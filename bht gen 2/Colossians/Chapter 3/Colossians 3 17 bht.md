# Colossians 3:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In everything we say and do, let it be done in the authority and guidance of the Lord Jesus, with a heart full of gratitude towards God the Father. The name of Christ should motivate and shape our actions, as Jesus is the channel through which we receive grace and through whom we express our thanksgiving to God. All things, at all times, are to be done in the name of Jesus, with thankful hearts towards God the Father.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "And every thing whatsoever ye do in word or work...all things (do) in the name of the lord Jesus...giving thanks to God the Father through Him."
2. "The name of Christ is the element in which all is done which furnishes a motive and gives a character to the whole."
3. "Through Him (Jesus) as the one channel of all communication between God and ourselves, whether of grace coming to us, or of thanks coming from us."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "And everything whatsoever ye do . . . do all" - this includes words as well as deeds.
2. "in the name of the Lord Jesus" - as disciples called by His name, seeking His guidance and help, and desiring to act so as to gain His approval.
3. "by him" - Greek, "through Him" as the channel of His grace to us, and of our thanksgiving to Him.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Do all in the name of the Lord Jesus - Do it all because he requires and commands it, and with a desire to honor him."
2. "His authority should be the warrant; his glory the aim of all our actions and words."
3. "We are to engage in every duty, not only in the name of Christ, but with thankfulness for strength and reason; for the privilege of acting so that we may honor him; and with a grateful remembrance of the mercy of God that gave us such a Saviour to be an example and guide."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. In the name. See on Matthew 28:19. Giving thanks. Notice the emphasis on the duty of thanksgiving placed at the close of the exhortations. See ch. Colossians 1:12; Colossians 2:7; Colossians 3:15; Colossians 4:2.

### John Calvin:
1. "Life must be regulated in such a manner, that whatever we say or do may be wholly governed by the authority of Christ, and may have an eye to his glory as the mark."
2. "We must give thanks to the Father through Christ, as we obtain through him every good thing that God confers upon us."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "let your whole conduct find in Him its sphere and motive."
2. "by making Him the centre of our lives, so that His name stands for the source of our strength, the guide in our duty, the keynote of our words, the end of our effort."
3. "living a life of constant gratitude to God the Father...gives us a Mediator for the expression of our thanksgiving."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "This is a sort of Golden Rule for Christians 'in the name of the Lord Jesus', in the spirit of the Lord Jesus."
2. "Sociological problems have always existed. Paul puts his finger on the sore spot in each group with unerring skill like a true diagnostician."

### John Gill:
1. "Whether in preaching the word of Christ, in hearing the Gospel, in singing psalms, hymns, and spiritual songs, and in conference and conversation with each other; or in whatsoever action, civil or religious throughout the whole life and conversation..."
2. "Do all in the name of the Lord Jesus; both in the strength of Christ, without whom nothing can be well said or done; and according to the mind and will of Christ declared in the Gospel..."
3. "The things for which thanks are to be given, are 'all things'; and the time when, always... The person to whom they are to be given is God the Father, the Father of our spirits, and of our mercies..."

### John Wesley:
1. In the name — In the power and Spirit of the Lord Jesus. Giving thanks unto God - The Holy Ghost.And the Father through him — Christ.
