# Colossians 3:24 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
By speaking of the reward of the inheritance, the apostle refers to the heavenly glory that awaits believers. This reward can be likened to an inheritance, an everlasting possession that is kept in the heavens and will never fade or diminish. These Christian servants, aware of this glorious inheritance, can find solace and encouragement in their present servitude, knowing that their true reward comes from the Lord. With the assurance of their future inheritance, they are motivated to faithfully fulfil their duties, finding joy and contentment in serving their Master, the Lord Christ.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "You must look to Him, not to men, as the source of all Christian reward."
2. "Here the sense would appear to be, with a marked reference to their present state of slavery, the compensation."
3. "The very word κληρονομία should have kept the Roman Catholic expositors from introducing the merit of good works here."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Knowing that it is from the Lord (the ultimate source of reward), ye shall receive the compensation (or recompense, which will make ample amends for your having no earthly possession as slaves now) consisting of the inheritance."
2. "Serve ye the Lord Christ."
3. "To the Lord and not unto men."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Compare the similar construction, ch Colossians 1:12."
2. "See Matthew 21:35-38, where the doulov bond - servant and the klhronomov heir are contrasted; and Romans 8:15-17; Galatians 4:1-7."
3. "Some take the verb as imperative, serve ye; but the indicative is better as explaining from the Lord."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "That from the Lord, i.e., from Christ, the true Master of the Christian."
2. "Ye shall receive the recompense. Not pay or reward, but that which is a compensation for the present privations."
3. "The Lord Christ, i.e., the Master Christ."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Ye shall receive...to get back, to recover."
2. "The full recompense."
3. "Ye serve the Lord Christ...as his slaves and gladly so."

### John Gill:
1. "By 'the reward of the inheritance' is meant the heavenly glory, called a reward, because the apostle is speaking to servants and therefore uses language agreeable to them."
2. "The heavenly glory is such a reward as that it is an 'inheritance'; or, as the Syriac version renders it, 'in an inheritance'; it lies in an inheritance, an inheritance incorruptible and undefiled, that fades not away, reserved in the heavens."
3. "Now these Christian servants 'knowing' all this, having a lively hope of this inheritance, a full persuasion, and a firm faith of right unto it, and meetness for it, and having the Spirit of God as an earnest and pledge of it; the consideration of it must greatly tend to make them quiet and easy in their present servitude, and to encourage them, to the discharge of their duty with diligence, faithfulness, and cheerfulness."
