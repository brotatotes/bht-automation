# Galatians 3:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
You have endured great suffering, both from the Jews and the pagans, because of your commitment to the gospel. But I implore you, do not let these sufferings be in vain. Do not abandon the truth that you have fought so hard for. I have hope in you, that you will remain steadfast and not lose the rewards that await you.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Did ye undergo all those sufferings in vain?"
2. "Let not such grace have been received in vain."
3. "If it be only in vain (and not something worse)."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Have ye suffered so many things—namely, persecution from Jews and from unbelieving fellow countrymen, incited by the Jews, at the time of your conversion."
2. "Or, shall ye, by falling from grace, lose the reward promised for all your sufferings, so that they shall be 'in vain'?"
3. "I hope better things of you, for I trust you will return from legalism to grace; if so, as I confidently expect, you will not have 'suffered so many things in vain.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He assures them, that if the opinions on account of which they had suffered were false, then their sufferings had been in vain."
2. "I hope you have not so far abandoned the gospel, that all your sufferings in its behalf have been of no avail."
3. "I trust, that although your principles have been shaken, yet they have not been wholly overthrown, and that you will not reap the reward of your having suffered so much on account of the gospel."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The evidence on the whole makes very strongly for the meaning suffer; in which case the reference is, probably, to the annoyances suffered from Judaising Christians."
2. "In vain. So that ye have fallen from the faith and missed the inheritance of suffering and the rich fruitage of your spiritual gifts."
3. "If, that is, it be really in vain."

### John Calvin:
1. "Having suffered so many things in behalf of the gospel, would they now, in an instant, lose it all?"
2. "If the true faith had not been delivered to them by Paul, it was rash to suffer anything in defense of a bad cause."
3. "Accordingly, he charges the false apostles with ill-will in depriving the Galatians of such valuable ornaments."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The usual rendering 'suffer' would refer to persecutions which the Galatians had to endure... but as we know nothing of them, it seems preferable to take the Greek verb... in the neutral and wider sense, embracing all spiritual experiences."
2. "This leaves room for doubt; the Apostle cannot believe that the Galatians will lose all the benefit of their spiritual experiences and continue in their folly."
3. "Others take the words in the sense: 'if it be only in vain,' and not much worse; since spiritual experiences increase the responsibility and risk."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Did ye suffer?... often means to suffer ill." 
2. "But alone, as here, it often means to suffer ill (τοσαυτα, so many things)." 
3. "Paul clings to hope about them with alternative fears."

### John Gill:
1. "These Galatians had suffered great reproach, many afflictions and persecutions for the sake of the Gospel..."
2. "...if such who have made a profession, and have suffered for it, should after all relinquish it, their sufferings for it are in vain..."
3. "...the apostle does, as it were, correct himself, and expresses his hope of them, that they would see their mistake, revoke their error, and abide by the truth of the Gospel."

### John Wesley:
1. "Have ye suffered — Both from the zealous Jews and from the heathens."
2. "So many things — For adhering to the gospel."
3. "In vain — So as to lose all the blessings which ye might have obtained, by enduring to the end."
4. "If it be yet in vain — As if he had said, I hope better things, even that ye will endure to the end."
