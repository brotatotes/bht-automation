# Galatians 3:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Scripture, by its divine inspiration, foretold and settled even the most disputed matters. God, in His wisdom, saw that both Jews and Gentiles would be justified by faith, not by works. This promise was made to Abraham, the father of the Messiah, declaring that through him all nations would be blessed. The Scripture's anticipation of these events affirms the greatness and completeness of its message.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "the Scripture (as we say, Nature: meaning, the Author of the Scripture; see reff.) foreseeing...that of faith (emphatic, 'and not of works') God justifieth..."
2. "the question is between those who were ἐκ πίστεως, and those who wanted to return to the ἔργα νόμου, whether Jews or Gentiles."
3. "In thee (not, 'in thy seed,' which is a point not here raised; but strictly in thee, as followers of thy faith, it having first shewn the way to justification before God."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "One great excellence of Scripture is, that in it all points liable ever to be controverted, are, with prescient wisdom, decided in the most appropriate language."
2. "God justifieth the Jews, too, 'by faith, not by works.' But he specifies the Gentiles in particular here, as it was their case that was in question."
3. "The blessing of justification was to Abraham by faith in the promise, not by works."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The idea is, that he by whom the scriptures were inspired, foresaw that."
2. "The fact that the pagan, or the Gentiles would be admitted to the privileges of the true religion, and be interested in the benefits of the coming of the Messiah, is a fact which is everywhere abundantly predicted in the Old Testament."
3. "The apostle here affirms that the gospel, that is, the way of justification through Christ, in opposition to the legal system he had been condemning - was, in few words, preached to Abraham, being contained in that promise, 'in thee shall all nations be blessed.'"

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The particular passage cited below." 
2. "The time foreseen was the Christian present." 
3. "The blessing is the messianic blessing of which the Gentiles are to partake - the imparting of the Spirit as the new life principle and the pledge of future blessedness in Christ."

### John Calvin:
1. "The calling of the Gentiles was a new and extraordinary occurrence."
2. "Paul shows, on the other hand, that by faith they arrive at the blessing, and by faith they must be 'in grafted' into the family of Abraham."
3. "He obtained the blessing by faith, and in the same manner must it be obtained by all."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The promise to Abraham was an anticipation of the gospel, i.e., the good tidings of salvation by Christ."
2. "The blessing promised includes the whole Christian salvation, which implies justification, i.e., the remission of sins and imputation of Christ’s merit."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The Scripture is here personified."
2. "This Scripture announced beforehand the gospel on this point of justification by faith."
3. "It is a crucial passage for Paul's point, showing that the promise to Abraham included all the nations of the earth."

### John Gill:
1. "The Scripture here, by a 'prosopopeia', is represented as foreseeing an event that would come to pass, and accordingly spoke of it before hand."
2. "the wisdom of God, the eternal Logos, having such a certain foresight, both as God and as Mediator, concerned in the covenant of grace for his people, that God would justify the Heathen through faith"
3. "Christ was the first preacher of the Gospel that ever was; he first preached it to Adam and Eve in the garden, and afterwards to Abraham: it was Gospel, it was good news to him, that the Messiah should spring from him, and all nations be blessed in him; he rejoiced at it, and by faith saw Christ's day and was glad"

### John Wesley:
1. "So great is the excellency and fulness of the scripture, that all the things which can ever be controverted are therein both foreseen and determined."
2. "Foreseeing that God would justify the gentiles also by faith, declared before."
3. "In or through thee, as the father of the Messiah, shall all the nations be blessed."
