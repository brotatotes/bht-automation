# Galatians 4:18 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
A zealous affection, when directed towards a good cause, is highly praiseworthy. It should be expressed in actions aligned with God's will and the things of Christ. Additionally, this zeal should be constant and unwavering, continually directed towards God, Christ, and those entrusted with teaching the truth.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "It is good to be zealously affected in a good cause, and not only during my presence with you."
2. "I do not mean to blame them in the abstract for the zeal to teach you good; any teacher who did this well, preaching Christ, would be a cause of joy to me."
3. "I have no wish, in thus writing, to set up an exclusive claim to zealously court you; whoever will really teach you good, at any time, let him do it and welcome."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "I do not find fault with them for zealously courting you, nor with you for being zealously courted: provided it be 'in a good cause'" (Galatians 4:18). 
2. "Their zealous courting of you is not in a good cause" (Galatians 4:18).
3. "I do not desire that I exclusively should have the privilege of zealously courting you. Others may do so in my absence with my full approval, if only it be in a good cause, and if Christ be faithfully preached" (Galatians 1:14).

### Albert Barnes:
1. "In itself, it is good; and their zeal would be good if it were in a good cause."
2. "You were exceedingly zealous in a good cause when I was with you. You loved the truth; you loved me."
3. "The doctrine is, that true zeal or love will live alike when the object is near and when it is removed."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "It is good for you Galatians to be zealously sought."
2. "In a good matter - the interest of the gospel."
3. "There is no harm in seeking to interest and enlist you, provided it is in a good cause."

### John Calvin:
1. "Good ministers are exhorted to cherish holy jealousy in watching over the churches, 'that they may present them as a chaste virgin to Christ.'"
2. "They indeed attempt to withdraw your affections from me, that, when you are thrown destitute, you may go over to them; but do you, who loved me while I was present, continue to cherish the same regard for me when I am absent."
3. "By condemning improper jealousy, he now exhorts the Galatians to engage in a different sort of competition, and that, too, while he was absent."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "It is good to be zealously courted in a good cause at all times, and not only when I am present with you." 
2. "I do not object to kind attentions and zealous devotion, provided it be from pure motives and in an honorable cause."
3. "I myself received your warmest affection during my personal presence; I only wish you would not grow cold and indifferent during my absence."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "To be zealously sought in a good matter." 
2. "It is only in an evil matter that it is bad." 
3. "When I am present, in the being present as to me."

### John Gill:
1. "A zealous affection when right is very commendable..."
2. "But then it must be expressed in a good thing; in a good cause, for God, and the things of Christ..."
3. "...it should be constant, and always continue; it should be ever the same towards God, Christ, and his ministers..."

### John Wesley:
1. In a good thing — In what is really worthy our zeal. True zeal is only fervent love.
