# Galatians 4:23 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The birth of Ishmael, Abraham's son with Hagar, followed the customary laws of nature. On the other hand, Isaac's birth, as a child of the free woman, was a result of God's promise and intervention. Isaac's conception and birth were a testament to God's power and favor towards Abraham, surpassing the limitations of human understanding.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "κατὰσάρκα, according to nature, in her usual course."
2. "διʼ ἐπαγγελίας, by virtue of (the) promise, as the efficient cause of Sara’s becoming pregnant contrary to nature."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Abraham was to lay aside all confidence in the flesh..."
2. "...and to live by faith alone in the promise..."
3. "...Isaac was miraculously born, contrary to all calculations of flesh and blood."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "But he who was of the bondwoman was born after the flesh - In the ordinary course of nature, without any special promise, or any unusual divine interposition, as in the case of Isaac." 
2. "The idea here of Paul is, that the son of the slave was in a humble and inferior condition from his very birth... He was born into a state of inferiority and servitude which attended him through his whole life." 
3. "The object of Paul is, to state the truth in regard to a condition of servitude and slavery. It is attended with evils from beginning to end; from the birth to the grave."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Born...after the flesh: According to the regular course of nature."
2. "By promise: In virtue of the promise; for according to natural conditions he would not have been born."

### John Calvin:
1. "In Isaac there was the election of God, signified in part by the manner of his birth, which was not in the ordinary course, but miraculous."
2. "Yet there is an indirect reference to the calling of the Gentiles, and the rejection of the Jews: for the latter boast of their ancestry, while the former, without any human interference, are become the spiritual offspring of Abraham."


### Philip Schaff:
1. "The son from the bondmaid was born after the flesh, in the regular course of nature."
2. "The son of the freewoman is through the promise, by virtue of supernatural influence, by the Spirit of God working through the word of promise."
3. "As in the conception of our Lord."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Is born (γεγεννητα). Perfect passive indicative of γενναω, stand on record so."
2. "Through promise (δι' επαγγελιας)."
3. "In addition to being 'after the flesh' (κατα σαρκα)."

### John Gill:
1. "Ishmael, who was begotten and born of Hagar, was born after the flesh; after the common order and course of nature."
2. "But he of the free woman was by promise; by a previous promise made by God to Abraham, that he should have a son in his old age."
3. "Isaac was born out of the common order and course of nature; his conception and birth were owing to the promise and power of God, and to his free grace and favor to Abraham."

### John Wesley:
1. Was born after the flesh — In a natural way.By promise — Through that supernatural strength which was given Abraham in consequence of the promise.
