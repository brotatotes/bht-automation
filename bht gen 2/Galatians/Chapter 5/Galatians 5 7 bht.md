# Galatians 5:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Once, you were faithfully running the race of faith, making great progress and remaining steadfast in your commitment to the truth. But now, something or someone has come along and hindered you, causing you to veer off course and stray from the path of obedience. Who could have possibly interfered in such a detrimental way, causing you to abandon the truth?

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Ye were running well" in the Gospel race. 
2. "Hinder: The Greek means, literally, 'hinder by breaking up a road.'"
3. "Not obey the truth: not submit yourselves to the true Gospel way of justification."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The Christian life is often represented as a race."
2. "Paul means here, that they began the Christian life with ardour and zeal."
3. "Paul asks, with emphasis, who it could have been that retarded them in their Christian course, implying that it could have been done only by their own consent."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Ye were running. You were on the right road, and were making good progress when this interruption occurred."
2. "Well. Bravely, becomingly, honorably to yourselves and to the church."
3. "Did hinder. Obey the truth. The exact phrase N. T. o. Disobey the truth."

### John Calvin:
1. "The censure which the apostle administers for their present departure from the truth is mingled with approbation of their former course..."
2. "...that, by being brought to a sense of shame, they may return more speedily to the right path."
3. "The astonishment conveyed in the question, who hindered you? was intended to produce a blush."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The martial and heroic spirit of Paul often compares the course of Christian life with the running of a race in the stadium."
2. "Comp. Galatians 2:2; Php 3:14; 1 Corinthians 9:24-27; 2 Timothy 4:7."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Who did hinder you? (τις υμας ενεκοψεν?). First aorist active indicative of ενκοπτω, to cut in on one, for all the world like our use of one cutting in on us at the telephone." 
2. "There was some ringleader in the business. Some one 'cut in' on the Galatians as they were running the Christian race and tried to trip them or to turn them."

### John Gill:
1. "In the Christian race; when they first set out in a profession of religion, they embraced and held fast, and were zealously attached to the truths of the Gospel...they made great proficiency in the knowledge of divine things, and ran with cheerfulness and without weariness in the ways of Christ." 
2. "It was the false apostles that hindered them, who did all they could to remove them to another Gospel, and turn them aside out of the right way."
3. "He says not by way of inquiry, but of complaint and concern; and with some indignation against the persons who had been the means of hindering their Christian progress."

### John Wesley:
1. Ye did run well — In the race of faith. Who hath hindered you in your course, that ye should not still obey the truth?
