# Galatians 5:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This belief that you have been persuaded to, does not come from God who has called you to his kingdom and glory. It is not of divine origin and should not be attributed to God, but rather it is a deceptive persuasion from another source.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The persuasion (to which you are yielding active; not your persuasion, passive) is not from Him who calleth you." 
2. "A little false doctrine corrupts the whole mass (of Christians)."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "This persuasion which ye have obeyed comes not from Him, but from an enemy."
2. "The calling is the rule of the whole race."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "This persuasion - This belief that it is necessary to obey the laws of Moses, and to intermingle the observance of Jewish rites with the belief of the Christian doctrines in order to be saved."
2. "Not of him that calleth you - That is, of God, who had called them into his kingdom."
3. "They knew well enough that Paul had not persuaded them to it, and it was important now to show them that it could not be traced to God, though they who taught it pretended to be commissioned by him."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "This persuasion [η πεισμονη]...the persuasion which the Judaising teachers exert over you."
2. "Obedience to the truth is the result of the persuasive power of the truth."
3. "The persuasion to subject yourselves to the Jewish law does not proceed from him who called you to freedom in Christ."

### John Calvin:
1. "Having formerly combated them by arguments, he at length pronounces, with a voice of authority, that their persuasion came not from God."
2. "God is never inconsistent with himself, and he it is who by my preaching called you to salvation. This new persuasion then has come from some other quarter; and if you wish to have it thought that your calling is from God, beware of lending an ear to those who thrust upon you their new inventions."
3. "The apostle’s statement seems to be, ‘This persuasion to which you have yielded is not from Christ. It comes from a very different quarter. The men who have employed it are not moved by his spirit. They have no divine authority; and you ought not to yield to them, no, not for an hour.’"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Of him that calleth you, God."
2. "The Father draws to the Son by the Spirit through the gospel."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  This persuasion  (η πεισμονη). "The art of persuasion," the effort of the Judaizers to persuade you. Only here and in ecclesiastical writers. 

### John Gill:
1. "This persuasion cometh not of him that calleth you."
2. "This is not of God, who had called them into the grace of Christ; nor of Christ, who had called them to the knowledge of himself, and communion with him; nor of the Spirit of Christ, who had called them with an holy calling, and who still continued to call them to repentance; nor of any faithful minister of the Gospel, who had been concerned as an instrument in their effectual calling."
3. "Such a conceit as this could never be of God, nor any evangelical minister, but must be of Satan or his emissaries, the false apostles."

### John Wesley:
1. This your present persuasion cometh not from God, who called you - to his kingdom and glory.
