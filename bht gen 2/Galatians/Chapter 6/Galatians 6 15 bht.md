# Galatians 6:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
For neither external rituals, like circumcision, nor the lack thereof, like uncircumcision, have any real significance. What truly matters is being transformed into a new creation by God. This inner renewal is what truly counts and brings about a complete change within us.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Confirmation of last verse: so far are such things from me as a ground of boasting, that they are nothing: the new birth by the Spirit is all in all."
2. "κτίσις (see note on 2Co 5:17), creation: and therefore the result, as regards an individual, is, that he is a new creature: so that the word comes to be used in both significations."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Not only are they of no avail, but they are nothing. So far are they from being matter for 'glorying,' that they are 'nothing.'"
2. "But Christ's cross is 'all in all,' as a subject for glorying, in 'the new creature.'"
3. "A transformation by the renewal of the mind."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "In his religion;... a new creature - The fact that a man is created anew, or born again, constitutes the real difference between him and other people."
2. "It is not by conformity to certain rites and customs that a man is to be accepted... but the grand inquiry is, whether a man is born again, and is in fact a new creature in Christ Jesus."
3. "The distinction which he [Christ] designs to make... is whether a man is born again, and is in fact a new creature in Christ Jesus."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "A new creature [καινη κτισις]. Comp. 2 Corinthians 5:17. For kainh new see on Matthew 26:29. For ktisiv on Romans 8:19; 2 Corinthians 5:17." 
2. "Here of the thing created, not of the act of creating."
3. "The phrase was common in Jewish writers for one brought to the knowledge of the true God. Comp. Ephesians 2:10, Ephesians 2:15."

### John Calvin:
1. "The reason why he is crucified to the world, and the world to him, is, that in Christ, to whom he is spiritually united, nothing but a new creature is of any avail."
2. "If any man wishes to be considered as belonging to the kingdom of Christ, let him be created anew by the Spirit of God; let him not live any longer to himself or to the world, but let him be raised up to 'newness of life.'"
3. "The truth of the gospel swallows up, and brings to nought, all the shadows of the law."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "All external distinctions are lost in Christ, and the new creature is everything."
2. "The phrase 'new creature' was common among Jewish writers to designate a moral change or conversion to Judaism (= proselyte); but in Paul it has a far deeper spiritual meaning."
3. "In Christ Jesus, says he, neither circumcision, nor uncircumcision, neither single life nor marriage, neither meat nor fasting, do any whit avail."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  A new creature  (καινη κτισις). For this phrase see on 2 Corinthians 5:17. 

### John Gill:
1. "they contain a reason why the apostle gloried in a crucified Christ, and looked upon the world as dead to him, and he to that, in every state of it."
2. "But to apply these words to baptism and non-baptism is a wretched perversion, and making a very ill use of them, whereby the minds of men are worked up to an indifference to a Christian institution."
3. "The phrase is Rabbinical; by which is meant, not a new creation of a man, as a man, of the members of his body, or of the faculties of his soul; nor of an external reformation, or a walking in newness of life, which is the fruit and effect of this new creation work; but an internal principle of grace, a good work of God begun in the soul."

### John Wesley:
1. For neither circumcision is anything, nor uncircumcision — Neither of these is of any account.But a new creation — Whereby all things in us become new.
