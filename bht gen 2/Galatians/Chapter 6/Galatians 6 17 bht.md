# Galatians 6:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
From now on, don't bother me anymore with arguments and disagreements. I have endured enough hardships and sufferings for the sake of Christ, and they are evident in the scars and bruises on my body. These wounds are not just physical, but they symbolize my unwavering dedication and loyalty to the Lord Jesus.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "τοῦ λοιποῦ, as E. V., henceforth: scil., χρόνου...τὸ λοιπόν continuum et perpetuum tempus significat"
2. "ἐγὼ γάρ...who carry (perhaps as in Galatians 6:5, and ch. Galatians 5:10, bear, as a burden"
3. "Ἰησοῦ is the genitive of possession, answering to the possessive βασιλήϊα...the scars of his wounds received in the service of his Master"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Let no man trouble me—by opposing my apostolic authority, seeing that it is stamped by a sure seal, namely, 'I (in contrast to the Judaizing teachers who gloried in the flesh) bear (as a high mark of honor from the King of kings).'" 
2. "The marks—properly, marks branded on slaves to indicate their owners. So Paul's scars of wounds received for Christ's sake, indicate to whom he belongs, and in whose free and glorious service he is."
3. "The Judaizing teachers gloried in the circumcision mark in the flesh of their followers: Paul glories in the marks of suffering for Christ on his own body."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "From henceforth - For the remaining time; that is, during the remainder of my life."
2. "He had felt it necessary to go into a vindication of it. His instructions had been departed from on the ground that he was not one of the original apostles, and that he differed from others."
3. "He had received them in his cause; and they were the proofs that he belonged to the Lord Jesus. He had suffered for him, and had suffered much. Having thus suffered, and having thus the evidence that he belonged to the Saviour, and having by his sufferings given ample proof of that to others, he asks to be freed from further molestation."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The wounds, scars, and other outward signs of persecutions and sufferings in the service of Christ."
2. "The scars on the apostle's body marked him as the bondservant of Jesus Christ."
3. "The passage naturally recalls the legend of Francis of Assisi."

### John Calvin:
1. "Let them cease to throw hinderances in the course of my preaching."
2. "This one thing is enough for me. Other matters are of no importance, and give me no concern."
3. "These marks, however, differ from the other in one important respect, that they partake of the nature of the cross, and in the sight of the world are disgraceful."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "For I bear in my body the marks of Jesus."
2. "Paul means the wounds and scars of persecution and suffering which he endured in the service of his Master, and which proved him to be a faithful bondman of Christ."
3. "They were his credentials and his trophies."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The genitive case (as here and Ephesians 6:10) means 'in respect of the remaining time.'"
2. "The marks of Jesus...probably actual scars from the scourgings (thirty-nine lashes at a time)."
3. "If for no other reason, listen to me by reason of these scars for Christ and 'let no one keep on furnishing trouble to me.'"

### John Gill:
1. "Having so clearly stated and explained the doctrine of justification... he requires, that they give him no further trouble on that head."
2. "For I bear in my body the marks of the Lord Jesus... the reproachful characters... the real scars... his sufferings and persecutions in general, which he endured for the sake of Christ and his Gospel."
3. "The apostle was known to be a firm and faithful servant, and a good soldier of Christ... the reproaches and afflictions... marks of honour."

### John Wesley:
1. "From henceforth let none trouble me — By quarrels and disputes."
2. "For I bear — And afflictions should not be added to the afflicted."
3. "In my body the marks of the Lord Jesus — The scars, marks, and brands of my sufferings for Him."
