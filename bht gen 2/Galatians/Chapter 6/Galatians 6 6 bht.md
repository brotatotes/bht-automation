# Galatians 6:6 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Let those who receive instruction give freely to those who teach, providing for their needs and sharing with them all that is necessary for their well-being.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "He who shares in the necessities of the saints can only do so by making that necessity partly his own, i.e., by depriving himself to that extent and communicating to them." 
2. "The word of life is the Gospel, the accusative of reference or of second government, after κατηχούμενος."
3. "In all good things: the things of this life mainly, as the context shows."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "From the mention of bearing
one another's burdens, he passes to one way in which those burdens
may be borne—by ministering out of their earthly goods to their
spiritual teachers."
2. "The 'but' in the Greek,
beginning of this verse, expresses this: I said, Each shall bear his
own burden; BUT I do not
intend that he should not think of others, and especially of the
wants of his ministers."
3. "communicate unto him—"impart
a share unto his teacher": literally, 'him that teacheth
catechetically."
4. "in all good things—in
every kind of the good things of this life, according as the
case may require."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Let him that is taught in the word... communicate unto him."
2. "Let there be a common participation of all good things."
3. "In everything that is needful for their comfortable subsistence."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Christian fellowship in all morally good things is to be maintained between the teacher and the taught."
2. "The exhortation therefore is, that the disciple should make common cause with the teacher in everything that is morally good and that promotes salvation."
3. "Communicate [κοινωνειτω]. Hold fellowship with; partake with."

### John Calvin:
1. "How disgraceful is it to defraud of their temporal support those by whom our souls are fed!"
2. "He saw that the ministers of the word were neglected, because the word itself was despised; for if the word be truly esteemed, its ministers will always receive kind and honorable treatment."
3. "To supply their real necessities, let believers cheerfully devote any part of their property that may be required for the services of devout and holy teachers."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Let him that is taught (or, orally instructed) in the word (of God) share with him that teacheth, in all good things (temporal possessions of every kind)." 
2. "Their relation is a partnership, a communion of interests. They are mutually dependent and helpful, and should share each other’s blessings and burdens." 
3. "Whatsoever a man soweth, that shall he also reap. A proverbial expression, found also among classical writers, but here spiritualized and applied to the future reward and punishment."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "For this late and rare verb κατηχεω, see on Luke 1:4; Acts 18:25; 1 Corinthians 14:19."
2. "The active (τω κατηχουντ) joined with the passive is interesting as showing how early we find paid teachers in the churches."
3. "Those who receive instruction are called on to 'contribute' (better than 'communicate' for κοινωνειτω) for the time of the teacher."

### John Gill:
1. "Instructed in the knowledge of the word...the written word, particularly the Gospel..."
2. "He that is taught in this [word], is...one that has been taught to know himself, and his lost state by nature; to know Christ, and salvation by him..."
3. "It is the will and ordinance of Christ, that they that preach the Gospel should live of it."

### John Wesley:
1. Let him that is taught impart to him that teacheth all such temporal good things as he stands in need of.
