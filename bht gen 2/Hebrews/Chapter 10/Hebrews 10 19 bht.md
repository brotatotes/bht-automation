The writer concludes his teaching and transitions to offering practical exhortations for the readers. He begins by summarizing the main points he has discussed thus far. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Having therefore, brethren, confidence as regards the entering into the holy places in the blood of Jesus." 
2. "He having once entered in with His blood as our High Priest, and thereby all atonement and propitiation having been for ever accomplished, it is in that blood that our boldness to enter in is grounded."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "boldness—'free confidence,' grounded on the consciousness that our sins have been forgiven."
2. "It is in the blood of Jesus that our boldness to enter is grounded. It is His having once for all entered as our Forerunner and High Priest, making atonement for us with His blood, which is continually there before God, that gives us confident access."
3. "No priestly caste now mediates between the sinner and his Judge. We may come boldly with loving confidence, not with slavish fear, directly through Christ, the only mediating Priest. Each must come for himself, and all are accepted when they come by the new and living way opened by Christ."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The word rendered 'boldness' - παῤῥησίαν parrēsian - properly means 'boldness of speech,' or freedom where one speaks all that he thinks."
2. "Here the idea is, that before Christ died and entered into heaven, there was no such access to the throne of grace as man needed."
3. "The blood of Jesus is the means by which this access to heaven is procured."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Eisodov in N. T. habitually of the act of entering."
2. "By the blood [εν τω αιματι] . Lit. 'in the blood' : in the power or virtue of."
3. "Parrhsia with peri concerning, John 16:25; with provwith reference to, 2 Corinthians 7:4; 1 John 3:21; 1 John 5:14."

### John Calvin:
1. "He states the conclusion or the sum of his previous doctrine, to which he then fitly subjoins a serious exhortation, and denounces a severe threatening on those who had renounced the grace of Christ."
2. "Allegories do indeed sometimes obscure rather than illustrate a subject; but when the Apostle transfers to Christ the ancient figures of the Law, there is no small elegance in what he says, and no small light is attained; and he did this, that we may recognize as now really exhibited in him whatever the Law shadowed forth."
3. "He says first, that we have boldness to enter into the holiest... But now the case is very different, for not only symbolically, but in reality an entrance into heaven is made open to us through the favor of Christ."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Boldness (courage) is the need of the hour."
2. "That is, the heavenly sanctuary where Jesus is."
3. "This is the better sacrifice just discussed."

### John Gill:
1. "The place saints have boldness to enter into is heaven, called 'the holiest', in reference to the holy of holies, in the tabernacle; which was a type of it, for the sacredness and invisibility of it, and for what was in it, went into it, or was brought thither."
2. "Christ has in person entered into it by his blood, and opened the way for his people; and believers in him may 'enter' now, and they do, when they exercise grace on him, who is there, and when they come and present their prayers and praises to God by him."
3. "The way of entrance is 'by the blood of Jesus'; and which gives both entrance and boldness; for hereby sin is removed both from the sight of God, and the conscience of the believer; peace is made with God, and spoken to him; pardon is procured, law and justice satisfied."

### John Wesley:
1. "Having finished the doctrinal part of his epistle, the apostle now proceeds to exhortation deduced from what has been treated." 
2. "He begins by a brief recapitulation."
