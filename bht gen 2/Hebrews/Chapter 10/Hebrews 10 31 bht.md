# Hebrews 10:31 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
It is a fearful thing to fall into the hands of the living God. For the faithful, falling into God's hands is a blessed experience, as they trust in His love and mercy. However, for the unfaithful, it is a dreadful thing, as they face His just judgment and wrath. The living God's zeal and power ensure that He will consume His adversaries. It is of paramount importance to embrace God's offer of salvation to avoid falling into His hands as a righteous, avenging judge.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "It is a fearful thing to fall into the hands of the living God."
2. "For the faithful, in their chastisement, it is a blessed thing to fall into God's hands: for the unfaithful, in their doom, a dreadful one."
3. "The idea of life and energy, attached to the name of God, brings vividly out the zeal with which He will consume His adversaries."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "It is good like David to fall into the hands of God, rather than man, when one does so with filial faith in his father's love."
2. "It is fearful to fall into His hands as a reprobate and presumptuous sinner, doomed to His just vengeance as Judge."
3. "He is the living God, therefore able to punish forever."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "To fall into the hands of the Lord, after having despised his mercy and rejected his salvation, would be terrific; and the fear of this should deter from the commission of the dreadful crime."
2. "It is the more fearful to fall into his hands because he will live 'forever.'"
3. "How unspeakably important it is then now to embrace his offers of salvation, that we may not fall into his hands as a righteous, avenging judge, and sink beneath his uplifted arm forever!"

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The living God, revealed in the living Christ, will not suffer his sacrificial gift and his covenant to be slighted and insulted with impunity."
2. "To fall, etc. Comp. LXX, 2 Samuel 24:14; Sir. 2 18."
3. "See on ch. Hebrews 3:12."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "It is a fearful thing to fall into the hands of the living God."
2. "His hands represent His power for work, whether in love or in wrath."
3. "To fall into His hands in faith is to have peace; but to fall into His hands in punishment is dreadful."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "A fearful thing (φοβερον). The wrath of God faces wrongdoers."
2. "To fall (το εμπεσειν). We are not dealing with a dead or an absentee God, but one who is alive and alert."

### John Gill:
1. "Believers...choose to fall into the hands of him as a chastising Father, rather than into the hands of men."
2. "It is a dreadful thing to fall into the hands of God, in the way of his judgments in this world."
3. "To stand before the Judge, charged with sin, naked, and without righteousness, speechless, and no one to speak in favour of them; to hear the dreadful sentence pronounced, and feel the wrath of God to the uttermost, how horrible must this be!"

### John Wesley:
1. To fall into the hands — Of his avenging justice.
