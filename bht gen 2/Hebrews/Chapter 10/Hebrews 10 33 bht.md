# Hebrews 10:33 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The believers were not only brought upon the stage and made a spectacle to the world, angels, and men, but they also suffered in their characters, reputations, persons, and substance. However, despite these hardships, they remained in communion with their fellow persecuted brethren, providing relief in times of distress and demonstrating true sympathy.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "being made a spectacle...the theatre being the place where conspicuous punishments were inflicted"
2. "partly in reproaches and tribulations"
3. "having become partakers with them who were thus living"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The persecutions here referred to seem to have been endured by the Hebrew Christians at their first conversion, not only in Palestine, but also in Rome and elsewhere, the Jews in every city inciting the populace and the Roman authorities against Christians."
2. "Gazing-stock—as in a theater (so the Greek): often used as the place of punishment in the presence of the assembled multitudes."
3. "Ye became—of your own accord: attesting your Christian sympathy with your suffering brethren."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Whilst ye were made a gazing-stock - you were made a public spectacle, as if in a theater; you were held up to public view, or exposed to public scorn."
2. "It was not an uncommon thing, however, for the early Christians to be held up to reproach and scorn, and probably this refers to some time when it was done by rulers or magistrates."
3. "They were not merely reviled in words, but they were made to endure positive sufferings of various kinds."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Whilst ye were made a gazing-stock."
2. "By becoming partakers."
3. "Imply Christian fellowship as the basis of the liberality."

### John Calvin:
1. "Let no man therefore deceive himself by self-flattery as though he had reached the goal, or had no need of incentives from others."
2. "But we ought especially to notice the latter clause, when he says that they became companions, or associates of the godly in their persecutions; for as it is Christ’s cause for which all the godly contend, and as it is what their contend for in common, whatever one of them suffers, all the rest ought to transfer, as it were, to themselves; and this is what ought by all means to be done by us, unless we would separate ourselves from Christ himself."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Partly in that ye became a spectacle of shame, a theatrical spectacle, in the scornful taunts and in active persecution."
2. "The word ‘living’ is not passive, but is repeatedly found in the Epistles to describe the actual condition of a man’s life."
3. "Such ‘reproach and affliction’ is recorded in Acts and in the history of Paul himself."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Being made a gazing-stock, to bring upon the stage, to hold up to derision."
2. "By reproaches and afflictions."
3. "Partners with those."

### John Gill:
1. "Brought upon the stage or theatre, and made a spectacle to the world, angels, and men..."
2. "Suffering both in their characters and reputations, and in their persons and substance..."
3. "They maintained their communion with them, relieved them in distress, and sympathized with them."
