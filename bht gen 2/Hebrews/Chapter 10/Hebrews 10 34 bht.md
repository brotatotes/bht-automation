# Hebrews 10:34 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The believers showed genuine compassion for those who were in bonds, as well as a remarkable willingness to endure the plundering of their own goods. They understood that their possessions on earth were temporary and fleeting, but they possessed a better and enduring substance in heaven. They recognized that the treasures of heaven far outweighed the temporary riches of this world. Thus, they were able to find joy in the midst of their hardships and losses, knowing that their true and lasting reward awaited them in the eternal kingdom of God.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "[The believers] sympathized with them who were in bonds"
2. "[The believers] took with joy the plundering of your goods"
3. "[The believers] knowing that ye have for yourselves a better possession and abiding"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Ye both sympathized with those in bonds and accepted with joy the plundering of your own goods."
2. "Knowing that ye have for yourselves a better, heavenly enduring possession."
3. "Substance: peculiarly our own, if we will not cast away our birthright."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They, in common with all the early Christians, counted it a privilege and honor to suffer in the cause of their Master."
2. "Men may be brought to such a state of mind as to part with their property with joy. It is not usually the case; but religion will enable a man to do it."
3. "The treasures of heaven are worth more...it is more enduring. Property here soon vanishes. Riches take to themselves wings and fly away...in heaven all is permanent and secure."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Ye had compassion on the prisoners."
2. "Took joyfully."
3. "Knowing that ye yourselves have a better, etc."

### John Calvin:
1. "As poverty is deemed an evil, the plunder of their goods considered in itself touched them with grief; but as they looked higher, they found a cause for joy, which allayed whatever grief they felt."
2. "It is indeed thus necessary that our thoughts should be drawn away from the world, by looking at the heavenly recompense; nor do I say any other thing but what all the godly find to be the case by experience."
3. "If then we wish to bear anything for Christ with patience and resigned minds, let us accustom ourselves to a frequent meditation on that felicity, in comparison with which all the good things of the world are nothing but refuse."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "For ye had compassion upon those who were in bonds, and ye also took joyfully the spoiling (the plundering) of your goods."
2. "Knowing that ye have yourselves or for yourselves the alternate reading ('in yourselves') is certainly wrong, and 'in heaven' is probably wrong, though it makes a good sense, and is implied in the shorter reading a better and an abiding substance (possession."
3. "Compare Acts 4:32; Luke 12:15, where a form of the same word is used."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Ye had compassion on them that were in bonds." 
2. "Took joyfully...ye received to yourselves with joy."
3. "In place of their plundered property they have treasures in heaven."

### John Gill:
1. "The reason of which joy was, knowing in yourselves that ye have in heaven a better and an enduring substance."
2. "That which is laid up for the saints in heaven is 'substance'; it is signified by an house, a city, a kingdom; and so it is rendered here in the Ethiopic version; and by riches, true, glorious, and durable; and by a treasure and an inheritance."
3. "And this is an 'enduring' substance; it cannot be wasted by the saints themselves; nor taken away from them by others; nor can it decay in its own nature; and the saints will always endure to enjoy it."

### John Wesley:
1. For ye sympathized with all your suffering brethren, and with me in particular; and received joyfully the loss of your own goods.
