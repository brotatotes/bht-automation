# Hebrews 10:35 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The apostle exhorts the believers to not cast away the confidence they have in their faith and hope. This confidence is something that can only be lost by their own actions. It is a reminder that they should hold on firmly to their beliefs and not give in to doubt or fear.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Cast not away therefore your confidence..." (35)
2. "...the which hath great recompense of reward." (35)
3. "...hath great recompense of reward." (35)

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Cast not away—implying that they now have 'confidence,' and that it will not withdraw of itself, unless they 'cast it away' wilfully."
2. "It hath in reversion recompense of reward—of grace not of debt: a reward of a kind which no mercenary self-seeker would seek: holiness will be its own reward; self-devoting unselfishness for Christ's sake will be its own rich recompense."
3. "Consequent exhortation to confidence and endurance, as Christ is soon coming."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They were to bear up manfully under all their trials, and to maintain a steadfast adherence to God and to his cause."
2. "Nothing could take it from them if they trusted in God, and it could be lost only by their own neglect or imprudence."
3. "The Christian soldier if he should cast away his shield of faith; compare the notes, Ephesians 6:16."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Confidence [την παρρησιαν] . Rend. boldness. The boldness and courage which you manifested under persecution.

### John Calvin:
1. "He shows what especially makes us strong to persevere, even the retaining of confidence."
2. "It hence appears that confidence is the foundation of a godly and holy life."
3. "By mentioning reward, he diminishes nothing from the gratuitous promise of Salvation; for the faithful know that their labor is not vain in the Lord in such a way that they still rest on God’s mercy alone."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Cast not away, therefore, your confidence..."
2. "(the faith and hope and boldness with which you confessed Christ, and)"
3. "which hath a great recompense of reward."


### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Cast not away therefore your boldness." 
2. "The Jewish Christians in question were in peril of a panic and of stampeding away from Christ."

### John Gill:
1. "Prayer itself should not be left off, nor should freedom, boldness, and confidence in it be slackened, or laid aside."
2. "The grace of faith in its full assurance, with respect to interest in God, as a covenant God and Father, and in his love; and with respect to interest in Christ, and in his grace, and a right to the glorious inheritance, the better and enduring substance."
3. "The reward of the inheritance follows upon it; and this reward is the recompense of God's own grace: and it is a very great one; it is the fruit of great love and grace."

### John Wesley:
1. Cast not away therefore this your confidence - Your faith and hope; which none can deprive you of but yourselves.
