# Hebrews 10:37 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
It is a reminder that Christ will come again to judge and reward everyone according to their deeds. This coming of Christ is certain and should be eagerly anticipated because it will bring about the fulfillment of God's justice and redemption for his people. So, let us be diligent in our actions, knowing that we will one day give an account before Him who is coming.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "For yet a little little while...ὅσον is often joined to adjectives and nouns, &c., which denote size, to give a certain definiteness to the idea...It gives the sense of very small, 'aliquantillum' as Hermann expresses it..." 
2. "He that is coming (the solemn prophetical ὁ ἐρχόμενος, 'He that is to come: see reff)...both are naturally referred to someone indicated by the ὅρασις; and ἐρχόμενος ἥξει, 'coming it will come,' is paraphrased into ὁ ἐρχόμενος ἥξει, 'He that is coming shall come."
3. "Shall not tarry."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Encouragement to patient endurance by consideration of the shortness of the time till Christ shall come, and God's rejection of him that draws back."
2. "Christ, being the grand and ultimate subject of all prophetical vision, is here made by Paul, under inspiration, the subject of the Spirit's prophecy by Habakkuk, in its final and exhaustive fulfilment."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The idea which the apostle means to convey evidently is, that the time of their deliverance from their trials was not far remote."
2. "The Christians in Palestine were oppressed, reviled, and persecuted by the Jews. The destruction of the city and the temple would put an end to that power, and would be in fact the time of deliverance for those who had been persecuted."
3. "To all the afflicted it may be said that 'he that shall come will come' soon. The time of affliction is not long. Soon the Redeemer will appear to deliver his afflicted people from all their sorrow."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "A little while [μικρον οσον οσον] . Strictly, a very little while."
2. "He that shall come will come [ο ερχομενος ηξει] . Rend. "he that cometh will come.""
3. "Similarly he refers hxei shall come to the final coming of Messiah to judge the world."

### John Calvin:
1. "That it may not be grievous to us to endure, he reminds us that the time will not be long."
2. "As a general holds forth to his soldiers the prospect that the war will soon end, provided they hold out a little longer; so the Apostle reminds us that the Lord will shortly come to deliver us from all evils, provided our minds faint not through want of firmness."
3. "By these words he intimates that the ungodly, however they may be fortified by defenses, should not be able to stand, for there is no life of security but by faith."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "For yet a very little while...for a little time, how little." 
2. "He that cometh...the coming One...the name of Christ under both economies."
3. "Will come though it tarry, wait for it...the author of the Epistle to the Hebrews makes the person the Messiah."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "A very little while."
2. "He that cometh."

### John Gill:
1. "For yet a little while, and he that shall come will come... but of his coming in his kingdom and power to destroy Jerusalem, and take vengeance on the Jews, for their rejection of him."
2. "It may be applied to his coming to help his people in time of need; the afflictions of the saints are many; and Christ has promised to come, and visit them; and which he does often, and speedily, and seasonably."
3. "And it may likewise be suitably improved, with respect to Christ's coming to judgment; that he will come is certain... and this coming of his is desirable, because it will be the marriage of the Lamb, and the redemption of the saints... and this will be quickly, in a little time."

### John Wesley:
1. He that cometh — To reward every man according to his works.
