# Hebrews 10:38 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The righteous person will experience true life by placing their continued confidence in God. This involves living a life of trust and steadfast obedience to Him. While the possibility of falling away exists, it is crucial to remain steadfast in faith. God does not take pleasure in those who turn away from Him, so it is important to persevere and hold firm to the Christian faith. Only by trusting and relying on God's grace can we truly live a life that is pleasing to Him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The just shall live by his faith."
2. "The possibility of such a fall is, as he observes, among the principal things taught us by this Epistle."
3. "My soul hath not pleasure in him."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The just shall live by my faith": answering to the Hebrew, "the just shall live by the faith of Him," namely, Christ.
2. The faith meant here is that fully developed living trust in the unseen Savior, which can keep men steadfast amidst persecutions and temptations.
3. "Behold, his soul which is lifted up, is not upright in him." Habakkuk states the cause of drawing back: a soul lifted up, and in self-inflated unbelief setting itself up against God.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The righteous should live by 'continued confidence' in God."
2. "They should pass their lives not in doubt, and fear, and trembling apprehension, but in the exercise of a calm trust in God."
3. "The apostle urges on them the importance of perseverance, and the guilt and danger of apostasy from the Christian faith."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Through his steadfast obedience to God he shall be kept alive in the time of confusion and destruction."
2. "The possibility of the lapse of even the just is assumed."
3. "Shall have no pleasure."

### John Calvin:
1. "He means that patience is born of faith; and this is true, for we shall never be able to carry on our contests unless we are sustained by faith."
2. "And thus by this expression is intimated the power of faith no less than the character of impiety; for pride is impiety, because it renders not to God the honor due to him, by rendering man obedient to him."
3. "The Bible is full of this mode of addressing Christians, and yet the Bible assures us that the sheep of Christ shall never perish. Warnings and admonitions are the very means which God employs to secure the final salvation of his people."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "But if he (A. V. ‘any man’) Owen and Gill, Winer and De Wette, prefer ‘he,’ which is simpler and in harmony with the context; the same person is described in the two clauses." 
2. "The apostasy of a professed Christian is always possible, or warnings would be needless: not necessarily the apostasy of a true Christian."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "If he shrink back condition of third class with εαν and the first aorist middle subjunctive of υποστελλω, old verb to draw oneself under or back, to withdraw."
2. "The just shall live by faith."
3. "See Romans 1:17 for the quotation also of 'the just shall live by faith.'"

### John Gill:
1. "The 'just' man is one not in appearance only, but in reality; not by his obedience to the law, but by the obedience of Christ; and he is one, who lives soberly and righteously..."
2. "And the manner of this just man's living is 'by faith'; not upon his faith, but upon Christ, the object of it; and by 'his faith', as in Habakkuk 2:4 his own, and not another's; or by the faith of Christ..."
3. "But if [any man] draw back, my soul shall have no pleasure in him... it may intend one, who finally and totally apostatizes from the doctrine of faith, and the profession of it: and in such persons God has no pleasure..."

### John Wesley:
1. "The justified person shall live in God's favor, a spiritual and holy life."
2. "By faith, as long as he retains that gift of God."
3. "If he draw back, if he make shipwreck of his faith, my soul hath no pleasure in him."
