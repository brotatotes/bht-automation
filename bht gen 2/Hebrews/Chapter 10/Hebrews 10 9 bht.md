# Hebrews 10:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In that very instant, he declared, I come to do Your will. This signifies the offering of a more pleasing sacrifice. By doing so, he takes away the legal dispensation and establishes the evangelical one.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "He (Christ again) taketh away the first, that he may set up the second."
2. "The contrast is between that which God wills not, and that which He wills."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The rejection of the legal sacrifices involves, as its concomitant, the voluntary offer of Jesus to make the self-sacrifice with which God is well pleased."
2. "I am come."
3. "Sets aside the first, namely, the legal system of sacrifices which God wills not."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He taketh away the first - The word 'first' here refers to sacrifices and offerings. He takes them away; that is, he shows that they are of no value in removing sin. He states their inefficacy, and declares his purpose to abolish them."
2. "That he may establish the second - To wit, the doing of the will of God. The two stand in contrast with each other, and he shows the inefficacy of the former, in order that the necessity for his coming to do the will of God may be fully seen."
3. "If they had been efficacious, there would have been no need of his coming to make an atonement."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. He taketh away the first that he may establish the second. Removes that which God does not will, the animal sacrifice, that he may establish that which God does will, the offering of an obedient will.

### John Calvin:
1. "the full and perfect righteousness under the kingdom of Christ stands in no need of the sacrifices of the Law"
2. "the sacrifices of beasts were to be removed by the priesthood of Christ, as they had nothing in common with it"
3. "he denies that the usual sacrifices are required of a pious man rightly instructed, and he testifies that without sacrifices God is fully and perfectly obeyed."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Legal sacrifices are abolished that there may be substituted for them, the will the good pleasure of God, which Christ came to do by the one sacrifice of Himself." 
2. "He (Christ) taketh away the first, that he may establish the second."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The which (αιτινες). 'Which very things' (θυσια)." 
2. "He taketh away the first (αναιρε το πρωτον). Present active indicative of αναιρεω, to take up, to abolish, of a man to kill (Matthew 2:16)." 
3. "That he may establish the second (ινα το δευτερον στηση). Purpose clause with ινα and the first aorist active (transitive) subjunctive of ιστημ, to place."

### John Gill:
1. "God has taken away, and abolished the law, that he might establish the Gospel." 
2. "He has removed and rejected [the sacrifices and offerings] as insignificant and useless, that he might establish what is mentioned in the second place; namely, the will of God, which is no other than the sacrifice of Christ, offered up according to the will of God, and by which his will is done."

### John Wesley:
1. "Then said he — in that very instant he subjoined."
2. "Lo, I come to do Thy will — To offer a more acceptable sacrifice."
3. "By this very act he taketh away the legal, that he may establish the evangelical, dispensation."
