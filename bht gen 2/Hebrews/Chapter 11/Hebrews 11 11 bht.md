# Hebrews 11:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Sarah, who initially laughed at the promise, eventually received the fulfillment of God's word. Despite her doubts, Sarah's faith in God's faithfulness prevailed as she became a witness of His power and grace.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Example of SARAH, whose faith worked with that of Abraham to produce Isaac."
2. "By faith Sarah herself also received power for the deposition of seed, power to fructify seed deposed."
3. "Beyond the time of age, seeing that she esteemed Him faithful who had promised."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Though being the weaker vessel, and though at first she doubted."
2. "And that when she was past age, she judged him faithful who had promised."
3. "After she had ceased to doubt, being instructed by the angel that it was no jest, but a matter in serious earnest."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The word 'herself' here implies that there was something remarkable in the fact that 'she' should manifest this faith."
2. "Even 'her' strong incredulity was overcome, and though everything seemed to render what was announced impossible, and though she was so much disposed to laugh at the very suggestion at first, yet her unbelief was overcome, and she ultimately credited the divine promise."
3. "She had no other ground of confidence or expectation. All human probability was against the supposition that at her time of life she would be a mother."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Both Abraham and Sarah doubted at first...but both became persuaded of the truthfulness of the promise." 
2. "Herself [αυτη]: She who at first doubted."
3. "Originally it means throwing down; hence, the depositing of the male seed in the womb."

### John Calvin:
1. ".but as she cast aside her unbelief when reproved, her faith is acknowledged by God and commended."
2. "that when our faith in some things wavers or halts, it ceases not to be approved of God, provided we indulge not the spirit of unbelief."
3. "True faith then is that which hears God speaking and rests on his promise."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Sarah herself, not ‘who had so long doubted’... The expression is nothing but an extension of the lesson of the previous verse to a new and connected instance: Sarah likewise."
2. "And when she was past age... and yet, in spite of her barrenness, her age, her former incredulity... she believed, and therein found a large reward."
3. "Deeming... him faithful."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "To conceive seed (εις καταβολην σπερματος). For deposit of seed." 
2. "Since she counted him faithful who had promised (επε πιστον ηγησατο τον επαγγειλαμενον). Sarah herself (αυτη--Σαρρα)."
3. "Even Sarah, old as she was, believed God who had promised. Hence she received power."

### John Gill:
1. "Sarah, being a believer, as well as Abraham, received strength to conceive seed...which Sarah, in her circumstances, without the interposition of the almighty power, could never have done."
2. "Here was a particular promise respecting this matter; and there were great difficulties in nature attending it, and such as to reason were insuperable; but these were got over, through the power and grace of God."
3. "Because she judged him faithful who had promised."

### John Wesley:
1. Sarah also herself — Though at first she laughed at the promise, Genesis 18:12. Genesis 21:2.
