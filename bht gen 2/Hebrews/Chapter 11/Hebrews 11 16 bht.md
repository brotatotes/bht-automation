# Hebrews 11:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The patriarchs lived with a longing for a heavenly home, a country far superior to the earthly one they resided in. Their desire for this better country is evidence of the promise of eternal glory in heaven that they received from God. As a result, God is not ashamed to be called their God. In fact, He has prepared a city for them, a divine dwelling place that is truly worthy of Him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "They desire a better (home), that is, a heavenly one...wherefore God is not ashamed of them to be called their God." 
2. "For He prepared for them a city, the home for which they yearned...He did not deceive their hopes, but acted as their God by verifying those hopes." 
3. "In the preparation of the way of Christ, and bringing in salvation by Him, of which salvation they in their anticipation of faith were partakers."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Not merely once did God call himself their God, but He is NOW not ashamed to have Himself called so, they being alive and abiding with Him where He is."
2. "He who is Lord and Maker of heaven and earth, and all things therein, when asked, What is Thy name? said, omitting all His other titles, 'I am the God of Abraham, and the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob.' Not only is He not ashamed, but glories in the name and relation to His people."
3. "For—proof of His being 'their God,' namely, 'He hath prepared for them a city,' the city in which He Himself reigns, so that their yearning desires shall not be disappointed."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They lived as if they had no expectation of a permanent residence on earth, and were looking to another world."
2. "Since they had such an elevated aim, he was willing to speak of himself as their God and Friend."
3. "They did not expect a permanent home on earth. They made no efforts to enter into the possession of the promised land themselves."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Is not ashamed... because they have commended themselves to God by their faith, so that he acknowledges them as his own." 
2. "To be called their God... God was called the God of Abraham, of Isaac, and of Jacob."
3. "City is significant, as showing that the fulfillment of God's promise lies in introducing them into the perfection of social life."

### John Calvin:
1. "It is a singular honor when God makes men illustrious, by attaching his name to them; and designs thus to have himself distinguished from idols."
2. "This privilege, as the Apostle teaches us, depends also on faith..."
3. "We are hence to conclude, that there is no place for us among God’s children, except we renounce the world, and that there will be for us no inheritance in heaven, except we become pilgrims on earth."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "They desire a better, that is, a heavenly (home); wherefore God is not ashamed of them, to be called their God."
2. "He prepared for them a permanent home above not a lent but a city of His and welcomed them there."
3. "Their desire was for the presence and blessing of Him who was their trust, and with whom they hoped to be when their earthly pilgrimage was ended."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They desire...stretching out after, yearning after." 
2. "Their God...to be called...is not ashamed."

### John Gill:
1. "Who, as the patriarchs desired a better country than Chaldea, or even Canaan itself, so they desire a better country than this world; and such is heaven: it is on high; here are no noxious and pestilential vapours, no mists or fogs beclouding, no storms and tempests, but it is full of light and glory."
2. "The word denotes a vehement desire; and it is such, that the saints desire to depart from this world, and go unto it; which shows that they are weaned from this, and have seen something glorious in another."
3. "This proves that he is not ashamed of the relation he stands in to them, since he has made a provision for them to dwell with him to all eternity."

### John Wesley:
1. "But they desire a better country, that is, an heavenly — This is a full convincing proof that the patriarchs had a revelation and a promise of eternal glory in heaven."
2. "Therefore God is not ashamed to be called their God: seeing he hath prepared for them a city - Worthy of God to give."
