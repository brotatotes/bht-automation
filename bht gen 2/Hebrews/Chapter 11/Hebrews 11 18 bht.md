# Hebrews 11:18 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The phrase In Isaac shall thy seed be called indicates that the blessed lineage would originate from Isaac, as stated in Genesis 21:12. This promise refers to both the physical descendants of Isaac and the spiritual significance of the chosen seed, the Messiah. The assurance given here is that the faithful line of God's people would be established through Isaac, ensuring that his descendants would be the ones to inherit the land and fulfill God's promises.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "This verse gives a definition of the 'only-begotten Son'"
2. "The posterity of Isaac alone shall be accounted as the seed of Abraham, which is the heir of the promises."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The phrase 'thy seed be called' means, that his posterity was to be named after Isaac, or was to descend only from him."
2. "To 'name' or 'call' a thing was the same as to say that it was, or that it existed."
3. "It does not mean here that his 'spiritual' children were to be called or selected from among the posterity of Isaac, but that the posterity promised to Abraham would descend neither from Ishmael nor the sons of Keturah, but in the line of Isaac."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Even he to whom ... it was said, In Isaac shall there be named to thee a seed - only his descendants shall be Abraham's seed." 
2. "To be called, is generally used in Scripture with one of two senses, 'to have the name,' or really to be." 
3. "Sometimes, as here, the two senses are combined."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
