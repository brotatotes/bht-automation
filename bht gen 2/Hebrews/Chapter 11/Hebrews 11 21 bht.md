# Hebrews 11:21 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jacob, in the final moments of his life, displayed great faith as he blessed each of Joseph's sons. He distinguished them not by sight, but with the conviction of faith, intentionally giving the greater blessing to the younger son, Ephraim. Leaning on the top of his staff, Jacob expressed his deep reverence and gratitude to God for His covenant faithfulness. These acts demonstrated his strong confidence in God, even in his dying moments. Genesis 48:15; Genesis 47:31; Hebrews 11:22

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "By faith Jacob, when dying, blessed each of the sons of Joseph...and he worshipped on the top of his staff." 
2. "What Jerome thought of such an idea, is plainly seen...Sanctus quippe et Deo deditus vir, oppressus senectute, sic habebat lectulum positum, ut ipse jacentis habitus absque difficultate ulla ad orationem esset paratus."
3. "If the 'staff' is to be taken, then it must be his own, not Joseph’s staff, which is indicated, and the gesture might have had a meaning correspondent to the thought in Genesis 32:10."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "He knew not Joseph's sons, and could not distinguish them by sight, yet he did distinguish them by faith, transposing his hands intentionally, so as to lay his right hand on the younger, Ephraim, whose posterity was to be greater than that of Manasseh."
2. "His faith, as Joseph's (Hebrews 11:22), consisted in his so confidentially anticipating the fulfilment of God's promise of Canaan to his descendants, as to desire to be buried there as his proper possession."
3. "The staff, too, was the emblem of his pilgrim state here on his way to his heavenly city, wherein God had so wonderfully supported him."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The word rendered in Genesis 47:31 as 'bowed'... is bowing, or prostration for the purpose of worship or homage." 
2. "Jacob was tenderly affected in view of the goodness of God... nothing was more natural than that the old man should lean reverently forward and incline his head upon the top of his staff, and adore the covenant faithfulness of his God." 
3. "Both the acts here referred to were expressive of strong confidence in God."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The meaning is simply that these events took place in Jacob's last hours."

### John Calvin:
1. "If, then, the Jews wish to be anything, they should glory in nothing else, but in faith."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Jacob showed the energy of his faith by the energy of his thankfulness."
2. "Though dying, he rose in his bed, leaned on his staff, and bowed in worship."
3. "The writer adopts the version of the Septuagint."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Leaning upon the top of his staff (επ το ακρον της ραβδου αυτου). From Genesis 47:31, but no word for 'leaning.'"
2. "The quotation is from the LXX, the Hebrew having 'the head of the bed,' but the Hebrew word allows either meaning with different vowel points."

### John Gill:
1. "By faith Jacob, when he was a dying,...this shows the truth of what the apostle says in Hebrews 11:1."
2. "The form of blessing them is recorded in Genesis 48:15 and which was done in faith, and under the direction and inspiration of the Spirit of God."
3. "The sense is, that Jacob, having blessed the two sons of Joseph, being sat upon his bed, and weak, he leaned upon the top of his staff, and worshipped God, and gave praise and glory to him."

### John Wesley:
1. Jacob when dying — That is, when near death. Bowing down on the top of his staff - As he sat on the side of his bed. Genesis 48:16; Genesis 47:31
