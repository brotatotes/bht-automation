## BHT:
When the trumpet sounded, it added to the terror of the moment and served as a prelude to the words that were about to be spoken. These words, known as the Ten Commandments, were not merely spoken quietly, but were uttered with a loud and authoritative voice, emphasizing their importance and significance.

### Henry Alford:
1. "Hence simply to deprecate, to refuse, or forbid."
2. "Calvin explains the sense, 'Cæterum quod dicit populum excusasse, non ita debet accipi quasi populus renuerit audire Dei verba, sed deprecatus est, ne Deum ipsum loquentem audire cogeretur.'"
3. "Persona enim Mosis interposita horrorem nonnihil mitigabat."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "trumpet—to rouse attention, and herald God's approach"
2. "they wished that God should not Himself speak, but employ Moses as His mediating spokesman"
3. "The voice of words was the Decalogue, spoken by God Himself, a voice issuing forth, without any form being seen: after which 'He added no more'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The sound of the trumpet amidst the tempest was suited to increase the terror of the scene." 
2. "It is easy to conceive what must have been the awe produced by a voice uttered from the midst of the tempest so distinct as to be heard by the hundreds of thousands of Israel, when the speaker was invisible." 
3. "It was so fearful and overpowering that the people earnestly prayed that if they must be addressed, it might be by the familiar voice of Moses and not by the awful voice of the Deity."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Sound of a trumpet" (σαλπιγγος ηχω) - See Exodus 19:16, Exodus 19:19; Exodus 20:18. Hcov a noise, almost entirely in Luke and Acts. See Luke 4:37; Acts 2:2; comp. LXX, 1 Samuel 14:19.
2. "Voice of words" (φωνη ρηματων) - See Exodus 19:19; Deuteronomy 4:12; Deuteronomy 5:22, Deuteronomy 5:24, Deuteronomy 5:26.
3. "Be spoken to them any more" (προστεθηναι αυτοις) - Lit. be added. See on Luke 3:19; Luke 20:11; Acts 12:3. To them refers to the hearers, not to the things heard. Rend. "that no word more should be spoken unto them." Comp. Exodus 20:19; Deuteronomy 5:25; Deuteronomy 18:16.

### John Calvin:
1. "For when it [the Law] was promulgated there was nothing but terrors on every side."
2. "The Gospel contains nothing but love, provided it be received by faith."
3. "Moses spoke thus in the name of the people, whose requests as their delegate he brought to God."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Unto blackness (γνοφω). Dative case of γνοφος (late form for earlier δνοφος and kin to νεφος, cloud), here only in N.T."
2. "The sound of a trumpet (σαλπιγγος ηχω). From Exodus 19:16. Εχος is an old word (our εχο) as in Luke 21:25; Acts 2:2."
3. "The voice of words (φωνη ρηματων). From Exodus 19:19; Deuteronomy 4:12."

### John Gill:
1. "And the sound of a trumpet....made it still more awful, as the sound of the trumpet will at the last day."
2. "The voice of words; of the ten words, or decalogue; which was as an articulate voice, formed by angels."
3. "Fearing that they should die; wherefore they desired Moses to be their mediator, and draw nigh to God, and hear his words, and speak them to them, from him."

### John Wesley:
1. "The sound of a trumpet [...] preparatory to the words, that is, the Ten Commandments, which were uttered with a loud voice."
