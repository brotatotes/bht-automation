# Hebrews 12:22 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Believers have come to the heavenly Jerusalem, the dwelling place of God Himself. In contrast to the earthly Mount Sinai, this heavenly city signifies the church of God under the Gospel dispensation. It is a city built by God, where He dwells and safeguards His people. This city is not only inhabited by God but also by innumerable hosts of angels. What a privilege it is for New Testament believers to be part of this glorious community, sharing in the blessings and wonders of the heavenly city.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Mount Sion, the abode of God which He loved and where He will abide continually, is used to signify, not its mere representative, which men know by that name, but the reality, God’s own abode in heaven."
2. "And so Thl., ἀντὶ τοῦ Σινᾶ ἔχομεν Σιὼν ὄρος νοητὸν καὶ πόλιν νοητὴν Ἱερουσαλήμ, τουτέστιν αὐτὸν τὸν οὐρανόν."
3. "He is its maker and builder, ch. Hebrews 11:10; nor only so, but also evermore dwells in it with the light of His presence, cf. Rev 21:22-24."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Believers being like the angels... are so their 'equals'..."
2. "For the full completion of this we pray."
3. "Angels and saints together constitute the ten thousands."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It had more to win the affections; more to elevate the soul; more to inspire with hope."
2. "They were citizens of the heavenly Jerusalem and were entitled to its privileges."
3. "Christians in their feelings and worship become united to this vast host of holy angelic beings."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The heavenly Jerusalem." 
2. "The spiritual mountain and city where God dwells and reigns." 
3. "To an innumerable company of angels."

### John Calvin:
1. "He contrasts mount Sion with mount Sinai...that the Jews might not cleave to that which was earthly, and which had flourished under the Law."
2. "By the heavenly Jerusalem he understood that which was to be built throughout the whole world."
3. "He means that we are associated with angels, chosen into the ranks of patriarchs, and placed in heaven among all the spirits of the blessed, when Christ by the Gospel calls us to himself."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Unto Mount Zion...in contrast to Mount Sinai...Heaven is termed thus a spiritual mountain and city." 
2. "The heavenly Jerusalem...see Hebrews 11:10; Hebrews 11:16; Isaiah 60:14."
3. "Innumerable hosts of angels... 'Myriads of angels.'"

### John Gill:
1. "By 'Mount Sion', and the other names here given, is meant the church of God, under the Gospel dispensation, to which the believing Hebrews were come; in distinction from the legal dispensation, signified by Mount Sinai, from which they were delivered."
2. "And this may be called 'the city of God', because it is of his building, and here he dwells, and protects, and defends it; and who is styled 'the living God', to distinguish him from the idols of the Gentiles, which are lifeless and inanimate, no other than sticks and stones."
3. "And to an innumerable company of angels; which are created spirits, immaterial and immortal; very knowing, and very powerful; and swift to do the will of God; they are holy, and immutably so, being the elect of God, and confirmed by Christ."

### John Wesley:
1. "The apostle does not here speak of their coming to the church militant, but of that glorious privilege of New Testament believers, their communion with the church triumphant."
2. "St. Paul here shows an excellent knowledge of the heavenly economy, worthy of him who had been caught up into the third heaven."
3. "All these glorious titles belong to the New Testament church."
