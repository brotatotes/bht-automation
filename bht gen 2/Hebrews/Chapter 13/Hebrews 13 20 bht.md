# Hebrews 13:20 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The reference to the blood of the eternal covenant in this passage emphasizes the lasting and profound impact of the Christian covenant. In contrast to the temporary nature of the Old Testament covenant, the covenant established through Christ's blood is intended to be everlasting. Through the application of this blood, believers are transformed and sanctified in all areas of their being, both internally and externally.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The word 'peace' in the New Testament is used to denote every kind of blessing or happiness."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The phrase signifies God who is the author and giver of peace."
2. "The connection of the blood of the covenant with Christ's pastoral office gives a thoroughly scriptural sense, and one which exactly fits into the context."
3. "Christ becomes the great shepherd solely through the blood of the covenant."

### John Calvin:
1. "We hence conclude, that we are by no means fit to do good until we are made or formed for the purpose by God, and that we shall not continue long in doing good unless he strengthens us."

### Archibald T. Robertson:

### John Gill:
1. "The God of peace, who is so called, because of his concern in the peace and reconciliation of his people."
2. 3. "That great Shepherd, being the man, God's fellow, equal to him, the great God and our Saviour."
