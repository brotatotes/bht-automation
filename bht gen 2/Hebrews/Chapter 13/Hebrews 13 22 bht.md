# Hebrews 13:22 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Receive and heed the exhortation given to you in this epistle, which, though shorter than what could have been said, is filled with consolatory messages for those who may be suffering and in need of comfort. Reflect on the abundance of the subject matter and be open to receive the encouragement it provides, even in its brevity.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "But I beseech you, brethren, endure the word of my exhortation." 
2. "For also in few words I have written to you."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Few words—compared with what might be said on so important a subject."
2. "On the seeming inconsistency with Galatians 6:11, compare Note, see on Galatians 6:11- :."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Suffer the word of exhortation - Referring to the arguments and counsels in this whole Epistle, which is in fact a practical exhortation to perseverance in adhering to the Christian religion amidst all the temptations which existed to apostasy."
2. "For I have written a letter unto you in few words - But it means, that it was brief compared with the importance and difficulty of the subjects of which he had treated."
3. "None can doubt, also, that the author of this Epistle could have himself greatly expanded the thoughts and the Illustrations if he had chosen."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Do not become impatient at my counsels in this letter."
2. "The word of exhortation refers to the entire epistle which he regards as hortatory rather than didactic or consolatory."
3. "Do not grow impatient. The letter is short."

### John Calvin:
1. "Let us hence learn that the Scripture has not been committed to us in order to silence the voice of pastors, and that we are not to be fastidious when the same exhortations often sound in our ears."
2. "The holy Spirit has so regulated the writings which he has dictated to the Prophets and the Apostles, that he detracts nothing from the order instituted by himself; and the order is, that constant exhortations should be heard in the Church from the mouth of pastors."
3. "Though men are by nature anxious to learn, they yet prefer to hear something new rather than to be reminded of things known and often heard before."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Now I exhort you, brethren, bear with (in the sense of giving a patient, willing audience to; see Acts 18:14; 2 Corinthians 11:4) the word of exhortation." 
2. "The language is partly apologetic, on the ground that the writer stands in no close relation to his readers, and yet had not spared them in his warnings (cp. 6 and 10)."
3. "For (with deeper reasons for such forbearance, there is also the brevity of the letter itself) I have written a letter (which is implied in the word used) in few words."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Bear with... 'hold yourselves back from' as in Colossians 3:13."
2. "The word of exhortation... a powerful appeal in fact."
3. "I have written... to send a letter... as in Acts 15:20."

### John Gill:
1. "The subject matter of his entreaty is, that they would suffer the word of exhortation...this shows that the children of God are sometimes heavy and sluggish..." 
2. "the word may be rendered 'consolation', or 'solace', as it is by the Vulgate Latin, Syriac, and Arabic versions; and may refer to the whole of this epistle, which is of a consolatory nature." 
3. "this may suggest that these Hebrews were under afflictions, and needed comfort; and yet through unbelief might be tempted to refuse the comfort administered to them; which is too often the case with God's people."

### John Wesley:
1. "Suffer the word of exhortation — Addressed to you in this letter..."
2. "which, though longer than my usual letters, is yet contained in few words..."
3. "considering the copiousness of the subject."
