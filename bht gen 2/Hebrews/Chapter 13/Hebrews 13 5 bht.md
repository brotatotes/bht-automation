# Hebrews 13:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
He has made this promise, not just to specific individuals in the past, but to all believers. It is a reminder that God will never abandon or forsake His people, no matter what they may face or experience. This assurance provides comfort and encouragement, especially during times of difficulty or uncertainty. It is a promise that holds true for all who trust in Him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Let your manner of life be void of avarice: contented with things present."
2. "For He hath said, I will not leave thee, no nor will I forsake thee."
3. "This is certainly singular, and cannot be mere coincidence."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The love of filthy lust and the love of filthy lucre follow one another as closely akin, both alienating the heart from the Creator to the creature." 
2. "I will never leave thee, nor forsake thee—A promise tantamount to this was given to Jacob, to Israel, to Joshua, to Solomon. It is therefore like a divine adage. He will neither withdraw His presence nor His help."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Let your conversation - Your 'conduct' - for so the word 'conversation' is used in the Scriptures; notes, Philippians 1:27."
2. "And be content with such things as ye have - see the Philippians 4:11-12 notes; Matthew 6:25-34 notes."
3. "For he hath said - That is, God has said. I will never leave thee nor forsake thee - see Deuteronomy 31:6; Joshua 1:5; 1 Chronicles 28:20."

### John Calvin:
1. "It is the true contempt of money, or at least a true greatness of mind in the right and moderate use of it, when we are content with what the Lord has given us, whether it be much or little."
2. "He who has set limits to his desire so as to acquiesce resignedly in his lot, has expelled from his heart the love of money."
3. "The source of covetousness is mistrust... When therefore the Apostle is seeking to cure us of the disease of covetousness, he wisely calls our attention to God’s promises, in which he testifies that he will ever be present with us."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Be ye free from the love of money... The N.T. is full of the peril of money on the character as modern life is also." 
2. "Content with such things as ye have... 'Contented with the present things' (τοις παρουσιν)." 
3. "For himself hath said... A noble promise in times of depression."

### John Gill:
1. "Let your conversation be without covetousness... a believer's conversation should be without it; in his family... and in the world... and in the church... and such a conversation is becoming the Gospel." 
2. "Be content with such things as ye have; or with present things; with present riches, or with present poverty... contentment with these things shows itself by thankfulness for every mercy, and by submission to the will and providence of God in every state of life."
3. "For he hath said, I will never leave thee, nor forsake thee; which is a promise made to Joshua, and belongs to all believers... God will not leave his people in the hands of their enemies, nor forsake them in distress, nor withhold any good thing from them needful for them, but will supply them with the necessaries of life."

### John Wesley:
1. He — God.Hath said — To all believers, in saying it to Jacob, Joshua, and Solomon. Genesis 28:15; Joshua 1:5; 1 Chronicles 28:20.
