# Hebrews 13:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Remember those who have gone before us, who spoke the word of God and led us in the faith. Reflect on their example, how they lived their lives in devotion to God, even to the end. Let their teachings and their faithfulness inspire us to walk in their footsteps and continue the work they started.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Remember your leaders, the ones who spoke to you the word of God." 
2. "Surveying the termination of their conversation, imitate the faith."
3. "It is remembering with a view to imitation that is enjoined."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Remember—so as to imitate: not to invoke in prayer, as Rome teaches."
2. "Considering how they manifested the soundness of their faith by their holy walk, which they maintained even to the end of that walk (their death by martyrdom)."
3. "Looking up to," "diligently contemplating all over," as an artist would a model, the termination, at death, of their "manner of life."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The duty here enjoined is that of remembering them; that is, remembering their counsel; their instructions; their example."
2. "Respect is to be shown to the ministerial office, by whomsoever it is borne."
3. "The proper remembrance of those now deceased who were once our spiritual instructors and guides, should be allowed to have an important influence in inducing us to lead a holy life."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Remember them which have the rule over you...with a view to observing their admonitions...Used of both civil and ecclesiastical rulers." 
2. "Imitate their example...considering their outcome...their life in intercourse with men."
3. "The reference is to the end of their life; what kind of an end they made; possibly, but not necessarily, with an allusion to cases of martyrdom."

### John Calvin:
1. "He first sets before the Jews the example of those by whom they had been taught; and he seems especially to speak of those who had sealed the doctrine delivered by them by their own blood."
2. "...though still there is no reason why we should not understand this generally of those who had persevered in the true faith to the end, and had rendered a faithful testimony to sound doctrine through their whole life as well as in death."
3. "It was a matter of no small importance, that he set before them their teachers for imitation; for they who have begotten us in Christ ought to be to us in the place as it were of fathers."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Remember them who are your leaders...Leadership is the prominent thought with so much of ruling as is essential to lead."
2. "The which have spoken to you the word of God...whose faith copy, thoroughly considering what a blessed end their life had."
3. "A course of Christian conduct, which even to the end is the outcome of a holy noble faith, is well worthy of the contemplation and imitation of all who observe it."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Remember Them that had the rule over you. 'Keep in mind.' Few lessons are harder for the average Christian to learn, viz., good following."

### John Gill:
1. "Remember them which have the rule over you...for such point out the way of peace, life, and salvation to men, and direct them to Christ; and guide them into the understanding of the Scriptures, and the truths of the Gospel; and lead them in the paths of faith and holiness, and are examples to them."
2. "To 'remember' them is to know, own, acknowledge, and respect them as their governors; to obey them, and submit to them; to treasure up in memory their doctrines and exhortations; to be mindful of them at the throne of grace, to pray for them; and to take care of their maintenance and outward supply of life..."
3. "Consider the end of their conversation; which may intend the whole of their conduct in the discharge of the several duties of their office...and this is to be considered for imitation and encouragement."

### John Wesley:
1. Remember them — Who are now with God, considering the happy end of their conversation on earth.
