# Hebrews 3:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Exhort one another to hold fast to Christ, his Gospel, and the faith and confidence in it, because as Scripture repeatedly reminds us, today is the day to hear God's voice and obey. These instructions and exhortations are timeless and remain important for us in the present times of the Gospel, providing us with great concern and matter of moment.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Thus the context goes on smoothly, and the purpose of the whole is to show, as is summed up in Hebrews 3:12, that it is the καρδία πονηρὰ ἀπιστίας which they have above all things to avoid."
2. "This argument is now carried forward by taking up the word παραπικρασμῷ, and asking, in a double question, who they were that provoked, and with whom it was that He was offended."
3. "The faithful exceptions are overlooked, and the whole of Israel is included in the παραπικρασμός, to make the exhortation fall more forcibly on the readers."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "It entirely depends on yourselves that the invitation of the ninety-fifth Psalm be not a mere invitation, but also an actual enjoyment."
2. "ALFORD translates, 'Since (that is, 'for') it is said,' regarding Hebrews 3:15 as a proof that we must 'hold . . . confidence . . . unto the end,' in order to be 'partakers of Christ.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Persevere as long as life lasts, or as long as it can be said 'today,' and by persevering in this manner you will have evidence that you are the friends of the Redeemer."
2. "Paul means, undoubtedly, to make use of this language himself as a direct exhortation to the Christians to whom he was writing."
3. "He entreats them, therefore, as long as it could be said 'today,' or as long as life lasted, to take care lest they should harden their hearts as had been done in the temptation in the wilderness."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The exhortation of ver. 12 answered to Psalms 95:0; so the condition of fulfillment in ver. 14 is declared to rest on the same Scripture."
2. "Only on the ground of what is said in that Psalm does the holding fast come to pass."
3. "We are fellows of Christ if we hold the beginning of our confidence steadfast unto the end, seeing it is said," etc.

### John Calvin:
1. "The reason for making progress never ceases as long as we live, because God calls us daily."
2. "It is not enough for us readily to receive his doctrine, except we exhibit the same teachableness and obedience tomorrow and every following day."
3. "We ought to continue growing in faith."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "We must hold fast if we would be partakers of Christ."
2. "Today if ye hear his voice, etc."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "While it is said (εν τω λεγεσθα)."
2. "Locative case with εν of the articular present passive infinitive of λεγω, 'in the being said.'"
3. "Thus the author (cf. same phrase in Psalms 42:4) introduces the repeated quotation from verses Hebrews 3:7; Hebrews 3:8."
4. "Probably it is to be connected with κατασχωμεν, though it can be joined with παρακαλειτε in verse Hebrews 3:13 (treating Hebrews 3:14 as a parenthesis)."

### John Gill:
1. "Exhort one another, and hold fast Christ and his Gospel, and faith and confidence therein."
2. "The words belong to the present times of the Gospel, and contain in them matter of moment, and great concern."
3. "Scripture instructions and exhortations are of perpetual use."
