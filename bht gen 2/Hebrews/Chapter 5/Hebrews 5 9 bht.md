# Hebrews 5:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Christ's sufferings were necessary for him to fulfill his purpose of bringing salvation. He became the author and initiator of eternal salvation through his obedience and death. This salvation is not temporary, but everlasting, surpassing the limitations of time. Those who willingly listen to his Gospel and obey his commands will be recipients of this salvation. Thus, Christ's obedience unto death sets the example for us and is the foundation of our hope for salvation.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "made perfect—completed, brought to His goal of learning and suffering through death"
2. "eternal salvation—obtained for us in the short 'days of Jesus' flesh'"
3. "unto all... that obey him—As Christ obeyed the Father, so must we obey Him by faith."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Sufferings were necessary to the 'completeness' or the 'finish' of his character as a Saviour."
2. "He became the author... of salvation. It is to be traced wholly to his sufferings and death."
3. "Only those who 'obey' him have any evidence that they will be saved."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The fundamental idea in teleioun is the bringing of a person or thing to the goal fixed by God."
2. "The consummation was attained in his death, Philippians 2:8: his obedience extended even unto death."
3. "Not everlasting salvation, but a salvation of which all the conditions, attainments, privileges, and rewards transcend the conditions and limitations of time."

### John Calvin:
1. "Here is the ultimate or the remoter end, as they call it, why it was necessary for Christ to suffer: it was that he might thus become initiated into his priesthood..."
2. "If then we desire that Christ’s obedience should be profitable to us, we must imitate him; for the Apostle means that its benefit shall come to none but to those who obey."
3. "He seems at the same time to have adopted a universal term, all, for this end, that he might show that no one is precluded from salvation who is but teachable and becomes obedient to the Gospel of Christ."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Being made perfect, not only brought to the end, the completion of His learning and suffering, but having acquired all the necessary merit, power, and sympathy needed in His office after His obedience unto death."
2. "He became the author of eternal salvation. A salvation not partial or temporal, like the atonements of the law, but a complete and ever-enduring deliverance from evil in all its forms and in every degree."
3. "His obedience unto death is the ground of our hope, and His obedience unto death is the model to which our life is to be conformed."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Having been made perfect [...] the completion of the process of training mentioned by this same verb in Hebrews 2:10 'by means of sufferings' as stated again here in verse Hebrews 5:8."
2. "The author of eternal salvation [...] causing, often in Greek with σωτηριας, in N.T. only here, Luke 23:4; Luke 23:14; Luke 23:22; Acts 19:40."
3. "See same idea in Hebrews 2:10 (αρχηγον). See Isaiah 45:17."

### John Gill:
1. "The salvation Christ is the author of is 'eternal'; it was resolved upon from eternity, and contrived in it; it was secured in the everlasting covenant, in which not only a Saviour was provided, but blessings both of grace and glory."
2. "Christ is the cause or author of this salvation, by his obedience and sufferings; by obeying the precept, and bearing the penalty of the law; by the price of his blood, and by the power of his arm; by his death and by his life; by his sacrifice on the cross, and by his intercession in heaven; by bestowing grace here, and glory hereafter."
3. "Those to whom he is the author of salvation, are such as hearken to the voice of his Gospel, and obey him in his ordinances."

### John Wesley:
1. "And being perfected by sufferings, he became the author of eternal salvation to all that obey him."
2. "By doing and suffering his whole will, he became the author of eternal salvation to all that obey him."
