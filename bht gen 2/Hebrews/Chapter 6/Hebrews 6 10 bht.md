# Hebrews 6:10 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
God is righteous and just, and He will not forget the good work and love that you have shown for His name's sake. Your labors of faith and acts of kindness towards others are not in vain, for God sees and will reward them. Even though we are undeserving, God has promised to reward the good works of His people out of His own grace. So continue to show love and perform good deeds, knowing that God is faithful to remember and reward your efforts.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "For God is not unjust, (so as) to forget... your work and the love which ye shewed towards His name." 
2. "The whole forgetfulness would be one act of oblivion, which the aorist expresses."
3. "Beneficence towards them was in fact shown towards His name."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Not that we have any inherent right to claim reward; for (1) a servant has no merit, as he only does that which is his bounden duty; (2) our best performances bear no proportion to what we leave undone; (3) all strength comes from God; but God has promised of His own grace to reward the good works of His people." 
2. "God will be no man's debtor."
3. "By their love he sharpens their hope and faith."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "God will do no wrong. He will not forget or fail to reward the endeavors of his people to promote his glory, and to do good."
2. "By their kindness in ministering to the wants of the saints, they had given full evidence of true piety."
3. "To show kindness to Christians, because they are Christians, is an important evidence of piety. It will in no case be unrewarded."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Your work and labor of love."
2. "The love which ye shewed ... toward his name."
3. "They have shown both love and work toward God's name."

### John Calvin:
1. "We hence conclude, that God does not pay us a debt, but performs what he has of himself freely promised, and thus performs it, inasmuch as he pardons us and our works; nay, he looks not so much on our works as on his own grace in our works."
2. "What can God find in us to induce him to love us, except what he has first conferred on us?"
3. "Thus let him who would engage in the duties of love, prepare himself for a life of labor."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Their ‘work’ was their whole Christian life of active obedience."
2. "Their love shown to God’s name is not the love with regard to or for the sake of His name, but the love towards it."
3. "Love towards God, and towards all that bear His name, holy love, is the divinest grace and likest God, and the Holy God would cease to be holy if He did not approve and bless it."

### John Gill:
1. "For God is not unrighteous,.... He is just and true, righteous in all his ways and works; there is no unrighteousness nor unfaithfulness in him..."
2. "But this is to be understood of the work of God upon them, called in Scripture a good work, and the work of faith..."
3. "And love to them being laborious, and appearing in many instances, and this shown for the Lord's sake, for his name's sake, might lead the apostle more strongly to conclude better things of them, even things of a saving nature..."

### John Wesley:
1. For — Ye give plain proof of your faith and love, which the righteous God will surely reward.
