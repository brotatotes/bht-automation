# Hebrews 6:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The writer desires that each person diligently pursues the full assurance of hope until the end. This assurance is obtained by leading a holy life and manifesting faith and love towards others. It is important to remember that the life of a Christian is not complete without attending to both faith and love. The goal is to be steadfast in firm faith, not wavering or being driven by doubts. This assurance of hope is the highest degree of divine evidence, encompassing present pardon and future glory. Ultimately, it is a personal experience given by the power of the Holy Spirit.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "But we earnestly desire that every one of you do show the same diligence with regard to the full assurance of your hope until the end." 
2. "The full assurance of your hope until the end."


### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "He cares alike for great and small, and overlooks none."
2. "They needed, besides love, patient perseverance, resting on hope and faith."
3. "Compare 'the full assurance of faith.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The full assurance of hope is to be obtained by a persevering effort to lead a holy life."
2. "Hope is a compound emotion, made up of an earnest 'desire' for an object, and a corresponding 'expectation' of obtaining it."
3. "(4) It is usually only as the result of a life of consistent piety that such assurance is to be obtained."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The manifestations just mentioned make the writer desire that they may exhibit more of the spirit which animates their beneficent works."
2. "He is concerned, not only for the body of believers, but for each member."
3. "We desire that each of you exhibit the same diligence to develop your hope, which is in danger of failing, into full assurance, unto the end of the present season of trial with its happy consummation."

### John Calvin:
1. "You have sedulously labored not to be wanting in your duties to men; but with no less earnestness it behooves you to make progress in faith, so as to manifest before God its unwavering and full certainty."
2. "Let us then remember, that the life of a Christian is not complete in all its parts, unless we attend to faith as well as to love."
3. "He bids them to be so fixed in firm faith, as no longer to vacillate nor be driven here and there, suspended between alternate winds of doubts."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "But (though persuaded of better things and recognising your work and love) we desire... that every one of you do show the same diligence with respect to the full assurance of your hope unto the end."
2. "The stress is on 'the full assurance of your hope,' and 'unto the end.' 'Full assurance of hope' is no doubt the meaning... And we desire that you show this quality and persevere in it even to the end."
3. "The warnings of the Gospel are solemn, and yet Christians should live in the sunshine of an assured hope as the true safeguard against apostasy."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They have not given up Christ, but many of them are still babes and not adults."
2. "Others are in peril of becoming so."
3. "To the fulness of hope."

### John Gill:
1. "In the constant exercise of the graces of faith and love, and in ministering to the necessities of the saints."
2. "And the persons addressed being 'everyone', this shows the apostle's impartiality in his exhortations, and served to remove all groundless suspicions in particular persons."
3. "And what he had in view in the whole was, that they might arrive to the full assurance of hope unto the end."

### John Wesley:
1. "The full assurance of faith relates to present pardon; the full assurance of hope, to future glory."
2. "The former is the highest degree of divine evidence that God is reconciled to me in the Son of his love; the latter is the same degree of divine evidence (wrought in the soul by the same immediate inspiration of the Holy Ghost) of persevering grace, and of eternal glory."
3. "But this assurance of faith and hope is not an opinion, not a bare construction of scripture, but is given immediately by the power of the Holy Ghost; and what none can have for another, but for himself only."
