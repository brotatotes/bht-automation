# Hebrews 6:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
God's desire to confirm and assure us of His unchanging will is demonstrated in His use of an oath, an act of condescension to our limited understanding. The immutability of His purpose is beyond question, for all of His plans are steadfast and unwavering. This divine oath reaffirms His role as both the giver of the promise and the mediator who faithfully intercedes on our behalf. Therefore, as recipients of His promises, let us respond with unwavering faith and belief in His faithfulness.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "God, willing to show more abundantly... to the heirs of the promise."
2. "The unchangeableness of His counsel, interposed with an oath."
3. "By means of the oath, He exercised the office of mediator."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Which being the case among men, God, in accommodation to their manner of confirming covenants, superadded to His sure word His oath: the 'TWO immutable things.'" (Hebrews 6:18)
2. "The utmost benignity. more abundantly than had He not sworn."
3. "He acted as Mediator, coming between Himself and us; as if He were less, while He swears, than Himself by whom He swears."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It does not mean more abundantly than in the case of Abraham, but that he was willing to give the most ample assurance possible." 
2. "The plans of God never change; and all the hope which we can have of heaven is founded on the fact that his purpose is immutable."
3. "No one could honor a God that had not such an immutability of purpose; and all the hope which man can have of heaven is in the fact that He is unchanging."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Wherein [εν ω] . Referring to the whole previous clause." 
2. "The immutability [το αμεταθετον] . The adjective used substantively."

### John Calvin:
1. "See how kindly God as a gracious Father accommodates himself to our slowness to believe; as he sees that we rest not on his simple word, that he might more fully impress it on our hearts he adds an oath."
2. "The certainty of salvation is then a necessary thing; for he who forbids to swear without reason has been pleased to swear for the sake of rendering it certain."
3. "Believers ought therefore to be fully persuaded that whenever they hear the voice of the Gospel, the secret counsel of God, which lay hid in him, is proclaimed to them, and that hence is made known to them what he has decreed respecting our salvation before the creation of the world."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "God, willing to show more abundantly to the heirs of the promise the immutability of his will."
2. "He Himself came as pledge and surety, not for us but for Himself."
3. "The same loving purpose that provided the blessings He promised prompted Him to do everything that could be done to win our trust and establish our faith."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "To show in addition to his promise 'more abundantly'." 
2. "The immutability of his counsel, the unchangeableness of his will." 
3. "Interposed with an oath."

### John Gill:
1. "God, in condescension to the weakness of men, made use of [an oath]; being very desirous and determined, more abundantly to show the immutability of his counsel."
2. "And the immutability of this, God was willing to show 'more abundantly' than in other purposes, though all God's purposes are unchangeable."
3. "[God] confirmed [his purpose and promise] by an oath; his counsel and purpose; he not only determined in his mind that he would save them, and promised it in his covenant; but he also, to confirm it the more to the persons concerned in it, if possible, annexed his oath to it; or 'he interposed or acted the part of a Mediator by an oath.'"

### John Wesley:
1. "God interposed by an oath — Amazing condescension!"
2. "He who is greatest of all acts as if he were a middle person."
3. "Thou that hearest the promise, dost thou not yet believe?"
