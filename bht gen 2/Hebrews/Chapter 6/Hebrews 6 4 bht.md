# Hebrews 6:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
For those who have experienced the illuminating grace of God and tasted the sweet blessings of forgiveness and the presence of the Holy Spirit, it would be impossible for them to fall away and lose the glorious salvation they have received. These are not mere external experiences, but rather intimate encounters with the love of God in Christ, that have transformed their lives and made them true partakers of God's Spirit.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "It is impossible, in the case of those who have been once enlightened, tasted of the heavenly gift, and have been made partakers of the Holy Spirit."
2. "An historic interest belongs to the occurrence of this word here, as having in all probability given rise to a meaning of φωτίζειν and φωτισμός, as denoting baptism, which was current throughout the Church down to the Reformation."
3. "This heavenly gift the persons supposed have tasted for themselves."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "We must 'go on toward perfection'; for if we fall away, after having received enlightenment, it will be impossible to renew us again to repentance."
2. "The heavenly gift"; Christ given by the Father and revealed by the enlightening word preached and written: as conferring peace in the remission of sins; and as the Bestower of the gift of the Holy Spirit."
3. "Made partakers of the Holy Ghost—specified as distinct from, though so inseparably connected with, 'enlightened,' and 'tasted of the heavenly gift,' Christ, as answering to 'laying on of hands' after baptism, which was then generally accompanied with the impartation of the Holy Ghost in miraculous gifts."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It seems to me that it refers to true Christians; that the object is to keep them from apostasy, and that it teaches that if they should apostatize, it would be impossible to renew them again or to save them."
2. "The apostle was addressing Christians. He was endeavoring to keep them from apostasy."
3. "An examination of those phrases will show that he refers to those who are sincere believers."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "It is impossible to dilute this word into difficult."
2. "Indicating that the enlightenment ought to have sufficed to prevent them from falling away; not that it does not admit of repetition."
3. "The heavenly gift is the Holy Spirit."

### John Calvin:
1. "He who has in anything, or in any ways offended, has fallen away from his state as a Christian; therefore all sins are so many fallings."
2. "He falls away who forsakes the word of God, who extinguishes its light, who deprives himself of the taste of the heavens or gift, who relinquishes the participation of the Spirit. Now this is wholly to renounce God."
3. "Let us then know, that the Gospel cannot be otherwise rightly known than by the illumination of the Spirit, and that being thus drawn away from the world, we are raised up to heaven, and that knowing the goodness of God we rely on his word."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The metaphorical sense here occurs in Polybius and Epictetus." 
2. "The accusative case is due to ανακαινιζειν in verse Hebrews 6:6." 
3. "These are all given as actual spiritual experiences."

### John Gill:
1. "For it is impossible for those who were once enlightened...to stand in need of a new work upon them, which would be impossible to be done."
2. "Now there are some who have a saving spiritual taste of this gift... and therefore cannot be the persons here spoken of."
3. "But then such can never so fall away as to perish...the glory of the Father, and of the Son, would be lost likewise."

### John Wesley:
1. "It is impossible for those who were once enlightened — With the light of the glorious love of God in Christ."
2. "And have tasted the heavenly gift — Remission of sins, sweeter than honey and the honeycomb."
3. "And been made partakers of the Holy Ghost — Of the witness and the fruit of the Spirit."
