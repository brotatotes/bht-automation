## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The art. is used, because it is not merely ‘better things,’ of some sort, that he is persuaded, but, of two opposite courses, that one which is the more excellent."
2. "and (things) akin to salvation"
3. "the formula ἔχεσθαί τινος, ‘to be next to,’ ‘bordering on,’ has occasioned the participle ἐχόμενος to be used in the sense of akin to, partaking of the nature of."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "LOVE to you prompts me in the strong warnings I have just given"
2. "I anticipate better things of you; that ye are not thorn-bearing, or nigh unto cursing, and doomed unto burning, but heirs of salvation"
3. "It is better to make you afraid with words, that ye may not suffer in fact"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "We confidently hope for better things respecting you."
2. "We trust that you are true Christians; that you will produce the proper fruits of holiness; that you will be saved."
3. "Is not this verse a clear proof that Paul felt that it was certain that true Christians would never fall away and be lost?"

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Beloved. It often suggests an argument."
2. "We are firmly convinced. The verb indicates a past hesitation overcome."
3. "He is persuaded that they will give heed to all things which attend the work of salvation and will enjoy all that attaches to a saved condition."

### John Calvin:
1. "Whosoever wishes to do good by teaching, ought so to treat his disciples as ever to add encouragement to them rather than to diminish it, for there is nothing that can alienate us more from attending to the truth than to see that we are deemed to be past hope."
2. "The Apostle then testifies that he thus warned the Jews, because he had a good hope of them, and was anxious to lead them to salvation."
3. "We hence conclude, that not only the reprobate ought to be reproved severely and with sharp earnestness, but also the elect themselves, even those whom we deem to be the children of God."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "But we are persuaded...Better things...That accompany salvation." 
2. "Though we thus speak...Not unrighteous to forget...But even God cannot remember what they did not do." 
3. "In that ye ministered and still do minister."

### John Gill:
1. "And the apostle also so calls them, because they were beloved by him, not merely as being his countrymen, but as saints."
2. "He was persuaded they had the true grace of God; whereas the above mentioned persons had only gifts, when in the height of their profession; and the least degree of grace is better than the greatest gifts men can be possessed of."
3. "Grace makes a man a good man, not gifts; a man may have great gifts and not be a good man; grace is useful to a man's self, gifts are chiefly useful to others."

### John Wesley:
1. "We are persuaded of you things that accompany salvation"
2. "We are persuaded you are now saved from your sins; and that ye have that faith, love, and holiness, which lead to final salvation."
3. "To warn you, lest you should fall from your present steadfastness."
