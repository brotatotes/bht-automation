# James 1:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
To ascribe evil rather than good to God is a grievous error. This fundamental misconception strikes at the very nature and perfection of God's holiness and denies His true being. It is a destructive heresy that should be rejected and abhorred by all who profess to honor God and His glory.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Do not err...my beloved brethren." 
2. "This earnest address...shew how important the Writer feels this to be."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 16. Do not err in attributing to
	God temptation to evil; nay (as he proceeds to show), "every
	good," all that is good on earth, comes from God.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Do not err, my beloved brethren - This is said as if there were great danger of error in the point under consideration."
2. "There was great danger that they would embrace that opinion, for experience has shown that it is a danger into which men are always prone to fall."
3. "To meet the danger he says that, so far is it from being true that God is the source of evil, he is in fact the author of all that is good: every good gift, and every perfect gift is from him."

### John Calvin:
1. "For as God is the author of all good, it is absurd to suppose him to be the author of evil."
2. "To do good is what properly belongs to him, and according to his nature; and from him all good things come to us."
3. "But if God is in all things and always like himself, it hence follows that well-doing is his perpetual work."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Do not err, a common Pauline expression, elsewhere always translated, 'Be not deceived.' Here it refers rather to what precedes than to what follows." 
2. "Be not deceived in this matter, in supposing that temptation to evil comes from God." 
3. "My beloved brethren, strengthening the exhortation."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Be not deceived. This is the way of sin to deceive and to kill."
2. "The devil is a pastmaster at blinding men's eyes about sin."

### John Gill:
1. "To make God the author of sin, or to charge him with being concerned in temptation to sin, is a very great error, a fundamental one, which strikes at the nature and being of God, and at the perfection of his holiness."
2. "It is a denying of him, and is one of those damnable errors and heresies, which bring upon men swift destruction."
3. "Therefore to be guarded against, rejected, and abhorred by all that profess any regard unto him, his name and glory."

### John Wesley:
1. Do not err — It is a grievous error to ascribe the evil and not the good which we receive to God.
