# James 1:21 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Remove all wickedness and unnecessary evil from your life, as it is both vile and contemptible. Embrace the gospel word with humility and openness, allowing it to take root in your heart. This word has the power to bring salvation and nourish a meek and gentle spirit within you.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Putting off all filthiness and abundance of malignity in mildness, receive the implanted word, which is able to save your souls."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Filthiness" is cleansed away by hearing the word.
2. Meekness...includes also a childlike, docile, humble spirit.
3. The Gospel word, whose proper attribute is to be engrafted by the Holy Spirit, so as to be livingly incorporated with the believer.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "In view of the fact that excited feeling tends only to wrong, let us lay aside all that is evil, and submit ourselves wholly to the influence of truth."
2. "Sin may be contemplated as a wrong thing; as a violation of law; as evil in its nature and tendency, and therefore to be avoided; or it may be contemplated as disgusting, offensive, loathsome... sin in any form is more loathsome than the most offensive object can be to any of the senses."
3. "The thing referred to had the essence of evil in it; but it was not merely evil, it was evil that was aggravated, that was overflowing, that was eminent in degree... a corrupt mind, a mind full of sensuality and wickedness, is not favorable to the reception of the truth." (128 words)

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Filthiness [ρυπαριαν] . Only here in New Testament, but James uses the kindred adjective (ch. 2 2), 'vile raiment.'"
2. "Perisseia is an unclassical word, and occurs in three other New - Testament passages - Romans 5:17; 2 Corinthians 8:2; 2 Corinthians 10:15. In all these it is rendered abundance, both by A. V. and Rev. There seems to be no need of departing from this meaning here, as Rev., overflowing. The sense is abounding or abundant wickedness."
3. "Naughtiness has acquired a petty sense in popular usage, as of the mischievous pranks of children, which renders it out of the question here."

### John Calvin:
1. "He requires that it should be a living implanting, by which the word becomes as it were united with our heart."
2. "By [meekness] he means humility and the readiness of a mind disposed to learn."
3. "It is a high eulogy on heavenly truth, that we obtain through it a sure salvation."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Lay apart, divest yourself of, all filthiness, pollution... putting away all the defilement and rank growth of malice which like weeds encumber the ground, and prevent the growth of the ingrafted word."
2. "Receive with meekness... a gentle and loving disposition toward our fellow-men."
3. "The implanted word... the word of truth or the Gospel of Christ as received into the heart... able to save your souls."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Putting away, metaphor of removing clothing as in Romans 13:12; Colossians 3:8; Ephesians 4:22; Ephesians 4:25; 1 Peter 2:1."
2. "The implanted word...sown in the heart as the soil or garden of God (Matthew 13:3-23; Matthew 15:13; 1 Corinthians 3:6)."
3. "Able to save...Ultimate salvation."

### John Gill:
1. "All manner of filthiness, both of flesh and spirit; all pride, vanity, wrath, malice, and evil speaking, under hearing the word."
2. "The ingrafted word... when it is put into the heart by the Spirit of God, and is mixed with faith by them that hear it; so that it is, as it were, incorporated into them, and becomes natural to them."
3. "The word... being a declaration of salvation by Christ, or what shows unto men the way of salvation by him; and is the power of God unto salvation to them, when it is attended with the energy of the Spirit, and the efficacy of divine grace."

### John Wesley:
1. "All the filthiness and superfluity of wickedness - all wickedness is both vile, hateful, contemptible, and really superfluous."
2. "Receive - Into your ears, your heart, your life. The word - Of the gospel."
3. "The hope of salvation nourishes meekness."
