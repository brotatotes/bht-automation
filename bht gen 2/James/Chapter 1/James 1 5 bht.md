# James 1:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The need for wisdom is evident in the challenges and trials that Christians face. There is a danger of losing direction and succumbing to complaints and rebellion when afflicted. However, God is ever ready to give wisdom and guidance if we seek it with sincere hearts. His giving is pure and gracious, without any mix of evil or bitterness. It is through asking and seeking His wisdom that we can navigate through life's difficulties and receive the blessings He freely offers.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "if any of you is deficient in wisdom, let him ask from God who giveth to all men simply and upbraideth not"
2. "asking and giving are put forward as belonging to us and God in the abstract, and we do not want any Object"
3. "in what sense is rather doubtful...the real meaning here is just as in Sir 20:15 above, and in Sir 41:22, viz. upbraiding with any kind of reproaches, as God might well do"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "If any of you want wisdom, namely, the wisdom whereby ye may 'count it all joy when ye fall into divers temptations,' and 'let patience have her perfect work.'"
2. "God gives without adding aught which may take off from the graciousness of the gift."
3. "God hears every true prayer and grants either the thing asked, or else something better than it."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "There is nothing in which Christians more feel the need of heavenly wisdom than in regard to the manner in which they should bear trials, and what they should do in the perplexities, and disappointments, and bereavements that come upon them."
2. "We are in great danger of going wrong when we are afflicted; of complaining and murmuring; of evincing a spirit of rebellion, and of losing the benefits which we might have obtained if we had submitted to the trial in a proper manner."
3. "The promise of divine aid is always limited to the desire. No blessing is promised to man that is not sought; no man can feel that he has a right to hope for the favor of God, who does not value it enough to pray for it."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "In pursuing this perfection you will find yourselves lacking in wisdom."
2. "Ask of the giving God," or of "God the giver."
3. "It is pure, simple giving of good, without admixture of evil or bitterness."

### John Calvin:
1. "He bids us to ask of the Lord to give us wisdom... we must necessarily be overcome with impatience, except we be sustained by this kind of comfort."
2. "Since we see that the Lord does not so require from us what is above our strength, but that he is ready to help us, provided we ask, let us, therefore, learn, whenever he commands anything, to ask from him the power to perform it."
3. "God is so inclined and ready to give, that he rejects none, or haughtily puts them off... there is nothing like this in God; for he is ready ever to add new blessings to former ones, without any end or limitation."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "By wisdom here may be primarily meant wisdom or prudence in the present trying circumstances of the Jewish Christians; wisdom to bear their afflictions well."
2. "Wisdom was especially necessary to Christians in their temptations, to convert them from being incitements to sin to be occasions of Christian perfection."
3. "God does not give as man does, grudgingly and restricting His gifts, but simply, that is, freely and graciously."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "If any one falls short of wisdom," 
2. "Let him keep on asking" 
3. "Not only 'wisdom,' but all good gifts, including the Holy Spirit."

### John Gill:
1. "This shows that the perfection before spoken of is not to be understood as in this life, since the apostle immediately supposes lack of wisdom in them"
2. "Saints often want wisdom to consider God as the author of them, and not look upon them as matters of chance, or impute them merely to second causes"
3. "Moreover, this may be applied to all other cases, in which wisdom is wanted; men want wisdom to conduct them in the common affairs of life, and especially the people of God"

### John Wesley:
1. "The connexion between the first and following verses, both here and in the fourth chapter, will be easily discerned by him who reads them, while he is suffering wrongfully."
2. "The sum of wisdom, both in the temptation of poverty and of riches, is described in the ninth and tenth verses."
3. "Who giveth to all — That ask aright.And upbraideth not — Either with their past wickedness, or present unworthiness."
