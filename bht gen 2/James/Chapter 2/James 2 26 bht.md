## BHT:
Faith is a spiritual essence, while works represent the external and material aspect. However, some argue that works should be viewed as the body and faith as the spirit. Genuine faith is always accompanied by good works, which serve as evidence of its validity. Just as a body without a spirit is lifeless, so is faith without works. It is this lifeless faith that fails to justify or save. This passage highlights the importance of a living faith that produces good works. Without these works, faith becomes sterile and loses its significance.
