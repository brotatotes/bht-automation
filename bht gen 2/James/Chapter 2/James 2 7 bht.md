# James 2:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Do they not blaspheme that worthy name of God and Christ, by which you are called? The apostle is highlighting the behavior of the rich heathens who blasphemed the name of Christ. However, he also suggests that Christians should examine their own actions and attitudes, as they should not be any different from the unbelievers when it comes to honoring and respecting the name of Christ.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Is it not they that blaspheme the goodly name which was called on you?"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Rich heathen must here chiefly be meant for none others would directly blaspheme the name of Christ."  
2. "Only indirectly rich Christians can be meant, who, by their inconsistency, caused His name to be blasphemed."   
3. "They who dishonor God's name by wilful and habitual sin, 'take (or bear) the Lord's name in vain.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The 'worthy name' here referred to is, doubtless, the name of the Saviour."
2. "It is no improbable supposition that many of those who were not disposed to engage in open persecution, would revile the name of Christ, by speaking contemptuously of him and his religion."
3. "Many who profess to be Christians have been more disposed to treat even such persons with respect and attention than they have their own brethren, if they were poor."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Is it not they who blaspheme?" (αυτοι . Emphatic)
2. "The disgracefulness of the blasphemy is emphasized." (Worthy [καλον])
3. "The name of Christ, invoked in baptism." (The phrase is an Old-Testament one.)

### John Calvin:
1. "The good name in which ye glory, or which ye deem it an honor to be called by; but if they proudly calumniate the glory of God, how unworthy are they of being honored by Christians!”

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Is it not they who blaspheme."
2. "The allusion may be to the attempts of the unbelieving Jews to compel believers to blaspheme the name of Christ."
3. "So the name of Christ was put upon believers to signify that they belonged to Him."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Blaspheme (βλασφημουσιν). Present active indicative of common verb βλασφημεω (from βλασφημος, speaking evil, βλαξ or βλαπτω and φημη), as in Luke 22:65."
2. "The honourable name (το καλον ονομα). 'The beautiful name.'"
3. "By the which ye were called (το επικληθεν εφ' υμας). 'The one called upon you' (first aorist passive articular participle of επικαλεω, to put a name upon, to give a surname to, as Acts 10:18)."

### John Gill:
1. "Do they not blaspheme that worthy name...by the which ye are called?" 
2. "Unbelieving rich men...blasphemed and cursed the name of Christ, and compelled others to do so likewise."
3. "Such who professed the Christian religion, by their supercilious and disdainful treatment of their poor brethren...caused the name of Christ...to be blasphemed and evil spoken of, among the Gentiles."

### John Wesley:
1. "Do not they blaspheme that worthy name — Of God and of Christ."
2. "The apostle speaks chiefly of rich heathens: but are Christians, so called, a whit behind them?"
3. "Are Christians, so called, a whit behind them?"
