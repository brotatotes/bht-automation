# James 3:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This heavenly wisdom is pure, free from all earthly and carnal influences. It produces purity of heart and a righteous way of life. It is not only gentle and moderate, but it also promotes peace and harmony. It is full of compassion and good fruits, leading to acts of kindness and generosity towards others. This wisdom does not tolerate doubt or insincerity.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "the wisdom from above is first of all pure"
2. "peaceable, forbearing, easily persuaded, full of compassion and good fruits"
3. "without doubting, without feigning"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "This is put, 'first of all,' before 'peaceable' because there is an unholy peace with the world which makes no distinction between clean and unclean."
2. "Purity or sanctity is put first because it has respect both to God and to ourselves; the six that follow regard our fellow men."
3. "Our first concern is to have in ourselves sanctity; our second, to be at peace with men."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The first thing which [religion] produces is to make the man himself pure and good; then follows the train of blessings which the apostle enumerates as flowing from that."
2. "A pure heart will never relinquish its hold on God’s truth for the sake of a peace that at such a price would be too dearly purchased."
3. "The effect of true religion - the wisdom which is from above - will be to dispose a man to live in peace with all others."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The idea is not first numerically, but first essentially." 
2. "The other qualities are secondary as outgrowths of this primary quality." 
3. "Without partiality [αδιακριτος] . Only here in New Testament and very rare in classical Greek."

### John Calvin:
1. "He says first that [celestial wisdom] is pure; by which term he excludes hypocrisy and ambition."
2. "[Celestial wisdom] is peaceable, to intimate that it is not contentious."
3. "By good fruits he generally refers to all those duties which benevolent men perform towards their brethren; as though he had said, it is full of benevolence."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The heavenly wisdom is described by seven qualities which, as has been well said, are ‘nothing but the seven colours of the one ray of light of heavenly truth which has appeared and been revealed in Christ Himself the Wisdom of God.’"
2. "Purity is its primary quality; all other qualities of heavenly wisdom are subservient to this."
3. "The word is to be taken in its widest sense, as all sin is impurity."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Purity (righteousness) comes before peace and peace at any price is not worth the having."
2. "Equitable, compliant, approachable."
3. "This wisdom does not put a premium on doubt."

### John Gill:
1. "This wisdom is first pure; it is pure in itself, it is free from everything that is earthly, carnal, or sensual, or devilish; it produces purity of heart, of life, and conversation."
2. "It is also 'gentle'; or makes men gentle, moderate, and humane, so as that they bear, and forbear; they bear with the infirmities of the weak; readily forgive injuries done them; do not rigidly exact what is their due, but recede from their just right for the sake of peace and love."
3. "Full of mercy and good fruits; of compassion and beneficence to the poor; feeding the hungry, clothing the naked, visiting the widows and fatherless in their affliction; and doing all other good works and duties, both with respect to God and man, as fruits of grace, and of the Spirit."

### John Wesley:
1. "But the wisdom from above is first pure — From all that is earthly, natural, devilish."
2. "Then peaceable — True peace attending purity, it is quiet, inoffensive."
3. "Gentle — Soft, mild, yielding, not rigid."
