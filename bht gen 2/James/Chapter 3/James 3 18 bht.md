# James 3:18 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The seed of righteousness, which produces true religion, is sown in the peace of a believer's mind. It grows and brings forth a harvest of happiness, which is the proper fruit of righteousness. This happiness is promised to those who work towards and strive to promote peace among all people.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Fruit of righteousness is sown in peace."
2. "The Apostle uses a prolepsis, as if a husbandman should this autumn be said to sow next year's bread."
3. "The peace-workers themselves are the sowers of the fruit."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The peaceable fruit of righteousness" - Because it is itself the true wisdom. 
2. "The seed whose 'fruit,' namely, 'righteousness,' shall be ultimately reaped, is now 'sown in peace.'" 
3. "Those truly wise towards God, while peaceable and tolerant towards their neighbors, yet make it their chief concern to sow righteousness."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Righteousness or true religion produces certain results on the life like the effects of seed sown in good ground."
2. "The whole effect, therefore, of religion, is to produce peace. It is all peace - peace in its origin and in its results; in the heart of the individual, and in society; on earth, and in heaven."
3. "Wherever and however it is manifested, it is like seed sown, not amid the storms of war and the contentions of battle, but in the fields of quiet husbandry, producing in rich abundance a harvest of peace."

### John Calvin:
1. "Those who are wise according to God’s will, are so kind, meek, and merciful, as yet not to cover vices nor favor them; but on the contrary in such a way as to strive to correct them, and yet in a peaceable manner, that is, in moderation, so that union is preserved."
2. "Those who study peace, are nevertheless careful to sow righteousness; nor are they slothful or negligent in promoting and encouraging good works; but they moderate their zeal with the condiment of peace, while hypocrites throw all things into confusion by a blind and furious violence."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "As bitter emulation and party-strife are the fruits of earthly wisdom, so righteousness is the fruit of heavenly wisdom."
2. "The sower is not God; but, as is evident from the context, the peacemakers."
3. "The seed of righteousness is sown by the peacemakers in a spirit of peace. Only those who are actuated by the spirit of peace are the true sowers of righteousness."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Only in peace is the fruit of righteousness found." 
2. "Only those who act peaceably are entitled to peace."

### John Gill:
1. "And the fruit of righteousness....is sown in peace of them that make peace." 
2. "That peace which they make, pursue after, exercise, and maintain, is a seed, which, being sown by them, will, in the issue, be followed with eternal happiness and felicity." 
3. "Righteousness and peace promise a large and comfortable harvest both here and hereafter."

### John Wesley:
1. "The principle productive of this righteousness is sown, like good seed, in the peace of a believer's mind."
2. "[The principle] brings forth a plentiful harvest of happiness, (which is the proper fruit of righteousness)."
3. "[Happiness is produced] for them that make peace - That labour to promote this pure and holy peace among all men."
