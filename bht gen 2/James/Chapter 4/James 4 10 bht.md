# James 4:10 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Humbly submit to the Lord, recognizing your own unworthiness and sinfulness, and seek His mercy and grace. Walk humbly with God, acknowledging your dependence on Him and attributing all you have to His grace. In His usual way, God will lift up the humble and exalt those who humble themselves, giving them a place and a name in His house. This is the true path to exaltation and the key to receiving His abundant grace and blessings.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Be humbled before the Lord and He shall exalt you." 
2. "This latter gives more the realization in the soul of the presence of God, as drawing near to Him in humility: that, the subjection to Him in recognition of His providence and His judgments." 
3. "Both here and hereafter: by His grace and counsel here to the hidden glory of His waiting children, and by His fruition and presence hereafter to the ineffable glory of His manifested children."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "In the sight of the Lord—as continually in the presence of Him who alone is worthy to be exalted: recognizing His presence in all your ways, the truest incentive to humility."
2. "The tree, to grow upwards, must strike its roots deep downwards; so man, to be exalted, must have his mind deep-rooted in humility."
3. "lift you up—in part in this world, fully in the world to come."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Be willing to take your appropriate place in the dust on account of your transgressions."
2. "Our sins have been committed against him; and their principal aggravation... arises from that consideration."
3. "He will exalt you from the condition of a broken-hearted penitent to that of a forgiven child."

### John Calvin:
1. "The conclusion of what is gone before is, that the grace of God then be ready to raise us up when he sees that our proud spirits are laid aside."
2. "Whosoever, then, seeks a firm elevation, let him be cast down under a sense of his own infirmity, and think humbly of himself."
3. "As a tree must strike deep roots downwards, that it may grow upwards, so every one who has not his soul fixed deep in humility, exalts himself to his own ruin."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The true way to exaltation is through humility."
2. "Humility is one of the rarest and one of the most lovely of all graces."
3. "Humility is the true spirit of all obedience; submission is the perfection of virtue; and resignation to the Divine will is just another term for universal holiness."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The passive here has almost the middle or reflexive sense."
2. "See 1 Peter 5:6 for this same form with the same promise of exaltation."
3. "Used by Jesus in contrast with ταπεινοω as here."

### John Gill:
1. "Humble yourselves in the sight of the Lord... acknowledge their meanness and unworthiness, their vileness, sinfulness, and wretchedness, and implore the grace and mercy of God in Christ."
2. "When they walk humbly with God, acknowledging they can do nothing without him; owning their dependence on his grace, and ascribing all they have, and are, unto it."
3. "God's usual way to lift up the meek, and exalt those that humble themselves; he lifts them from the dunghill, to set them among princes; he gives them a place, and a name in his house, better than sons and daughters; he adorns them with his grace."
