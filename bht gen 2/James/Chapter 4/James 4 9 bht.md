# James 4:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Be afflicted, as a response to your past unfaithfulness to God, and mourn over the consequences of your sins. It is important to recognize the depth of your wrongdoing and the suffering it has caused.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Be wretched in your minds, from a sense of your sinfulness."
2. "Let your laughter be turned into mourning and your joy into humiliation."
3. "Deep repentance, leading them through deep sorrow."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Endure misery," that is, mourn over your wretchedness through sin. Repent with deep sorrow instead of your present laughter.
2. A blessed mourning.
3. "falling of the countenance," casting down of the eyes.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Be afflicted, and mourn, and weep... they were to afflict themselves - that is, they were to feel distressed and sad on account of their transgressions."
2. "Sorrow, deep and unfeigned, was appropriate on account of their sins, and the sound of laughter and of revelry should be changed to notes of lamentation."
3. "It is not gloom, melancholy, or moroseness, but it is sorrow on account of sin. God has so made us that we should feel sorrow when we are conscious that we have done wrong, and it is appropriate that we should do so."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Be afflicted...mourn...heaviness." 
2. "A casting down of the eyes."

### John Calvin:
1. "Christ denounces mourning on those who laugh, as a curse."
2. "He addresses those who, being inebriated in their minds, did not perceive God's judgment."
3. "That he might shake off from them this deadly torpor, he admonishes them to learn to mourn."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Be afflicted, and mourn, and weep…over your envy and hatred, your strifes and contentions, and the miseries occasioned by them."
2. "The epithets 'sinners' and 'double-minded' imply the necessity of repentance; and true repentance must ever be accompanied with godly sorrow."
3. "Let your laughter be turned to mourning, and your joy to heaviness: feelings which are more appropriate for the occasion."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Be afflicted (ταλαιπωρησατε). First aorist active imperative ταλαιπωρεω, old verb from ταλαιπωρος (Romans 7:24), to endure toils, here only in N.T."
2. "Mourn (πενθησατε). First aorist active imperative of πενθεω, old verb from πενθος (mourning, James 4:9), as in Matthew 5:4. Often in N.T. joined as here with κλαιω, to weep (Mark 16:10; Luke 6:25). A call to the godly sorrow spoken of in 2 Corinthians 7:10 (Mayor), like an O.T. prophet."
3. "Heaviness (κατηφειαν). Old word from κατηφης (of a downcast look, from κατα, φαη eyes), hanging down of the eyes like the publican in Luke 18:13, here only in N.T."

### John Gill:
1. "Afflicting the soul is meant, an inward mourning and weeping over the plague of the heart, the impurity of nature, and the various sins of life; after a godly sort, and because contrary to a God of infinite love and grace."
2. "Let your laughter be turned to mourning, and your joy to heaviness; meaning their carnal joy, on account of their friendship with the world, and their enjoyment of the things of it, since they consumed them on their lusts, and which betrayed enmity to God."

### John Wesley:
1. Be afflicted — For your past unfaithfulness to God.
