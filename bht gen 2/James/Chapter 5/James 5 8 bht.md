# James 5:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Be patient and strengthen your hearts, just as a farmer waits expectantly for the rain to come. The apostle emphasizes the nearness of the Lord's coming, which was expected to occur soon. In the meantime, we are encouraged to endure with constancy and not become weary, for the time of deliverance will surely come. The realization of the Lord's imminent return serves as a source of comfort and stability amidst present troubles. Let us remain steadfast in hope, knowing that the Lord's coming is drawing near and will bring both judgment and reward.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Establish (confirm, strengthen, both which are required for patience) your hearts, because the coming of the Lord is nigh."
2. "Colligendum robur ad durandum: colligi autem melius non potest, quam ex spe et quasi intuitu propinqui adventus Domini."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "We are to live in a continued state of expectancy of the Lord's coming, as an event always nigh."
2. "Nothing can more 'stablish the heart' amidst present troubles than the realized expectation of His speedy coming."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Be ye also patient - As the farmer is. In due time, as he expects the return of the rain, so you may anticipate deliverance from your trials."
2. "Do not become weary and fretful; but bear with constancy all that is laid upon you, until the time of your deliverance shall come."
3. "It is clear, I think, from this place, that the apostle expected that that which he understood by 'the coming of the Lord' was soon to occur."

### John Calvin:
1. "In the meantime, he bids us to correct the softness of the heart, which weakens us, so as not to persevere in hope."
2. "And doubtless the time appears long, because we are too tender and delicate."
3. "We ought, then, to gather strength that we may become hardened and this cannot be better attained than by hope, and as it were by a realizing view of the near approach of our Lord."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "We need strength of mind to be patient; endurance is an evidence of strength."
2. "The Lord is near; His coming to execute vengeance on your oppressors, and to reward your patience, is close at hand."
3. "The longing of the apostolic Church 'hasted unto' the coming of the Lord. All Christian time appeared only as the point of transition to the eternal, and thus as something passing quickly away."
