# John 1:30 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
John the Baptist affirms that Jesus is the one who was anticipated, the one who existed before him, and the one who surpasses him in greatness. John's previous testimonies about Jesus reveal his significance, and his statement about Jesus coming after him emphasizes Jesus' divine nature. This expression is not meant to imply Jesus' maturity, but rather emphasizes the certainty and uniqueness of the one who would follow John.

### John Calvin:
1. "He comprehends every thing in a few words, when he declares that Christ is the person who, he said, was to be preferred to him." 
2. "Three things are here stated; for when he says that a man cometh after him, he means that he himself was before him in the order of time, to prepare the way for Christ." 
3. "That honor, therefore, which the Father bestowed upon him was not accidental, but was due to his eternal majesty."
