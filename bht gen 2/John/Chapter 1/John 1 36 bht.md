# John 1:36 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
John, recognizing the nearing end of his ministry, directed his attention solely towards Jesus, contemplating Him as the promised Messiah and Deliverer. In this moment, with deep fascination, John singled out Christ and fervently gazed upon Him. This act served as a gentle hint to his disciples to follow after Jesus. Through such unwavering focus on Christ, John exemplified the way all ministers should direct their gaze upon the Son of God and guide others towards Him. In fixing his eyes intently on Jesus, John delighted in the Lamb of God, who takes away the sin of the world.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "having fixed his eyes, with significant gaze, on Jesus."
2. "The repetition of that wonderful proclamation, in identical terms and without another word, could only have been meant as a gentle hint to go after Him."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Singling him out and regarding him with special attention."
2. "Contemplating him as the long-expected Messiah and Deliverer of the world."
3. "In this way all ministers should fix the eye upon the Son of God, and direct all others to him."

### John Calvin:
1. "When John perceived that he was approaching the end of his course, he labored incessantly to resign his office to Christ."
2. "Here we see also how small and low the beginning of the Church was. John, indeed, prepared disciples for Christ, but it is only now that Christ begins to collect a Church."
3. "We ought not therefore to fear that Christ will withdraw from us or refuse to us easy access, provided that he sees us desirous to come to him; but, on the contrary, he will stretch out his hand to assist our endeavors."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He looked (εμβλεψας). First aorist active participle of εμβλεπω, antecedent action before λεγε (says)."
2. "As he walked (περιπατουντ). Present active participle in dative case after εμβλεψας and like ερχομενον in verse John 1:29 vividly pictures the rapture of John in this vision of Jesus, so far as we know the third and last glimpse of Jesus by John (the baptism, verse John 1:29, and here)."
3. "Saith (λεγε). Historical present, change from ιστηκε before. He repeats part of the tribute in verse John 1:29."

### John Gill:
1. "John fixed his eyes intently on Christ, with great pleasure and delight..."
2. "...behold the Lamb of God..."
3. "...which taketh away the sin of the world."
