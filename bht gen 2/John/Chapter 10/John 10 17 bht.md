# John 10:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Christ willingly laid down his life so that he may have the power to take it up again for the salvation and justification of humanity. In doing so, he displayed the immense love of the Father towards him. The purpose behind his sacrificial death was not only to atone for the sins of mankind but also to conquer death through his resurrection, ensuring the ultimate victory over sin and eternal life for all who believe in him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "In this wonderful verse lies the mystery of the love of the Father for the Son."
2. "The Son has condescended to the work of humiliation, and to earn the crown through the cross."
3. "Without this purpose in view, the Death of Christ would neither be lawful nor possible."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Therefore doth my Father love me, because I lay down my life, &c.—As the highest act of the Son's love to the Father was the laying down of His life for the sheep at His 'commandment,' so the Father's love to Him as His incarnate Son reaches its consummation, and finds its highest justification, in that sublimest and most affecting of all acts."
2. "That I might take it again—His resurrection-life being indispensable to the accomplishment of the fruit of His death."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "I lay down my life - I give myself to die for my people, in Jewish and pagan lands."
2. "I offer myself a sacrifice to show the willingness of my Father to save them; to provide an atonement, and thus to open the way for their salvation."
3. "That I might take it again - Be raised up from the dead, and glorified, and still carry on the work of redemption."

### John Calvin:
1. "We need not wonder if he declares it to be the reason why the Father loveth him, that our salvation is dearer to him than his own life. This is a wonderful commendation of the goodness of God to us, and ought justly to arouse our whole souls into rapturous admiration."
2. "And indeed there was no necessity that Christ should take upon him our flesh, in which he was beloved, but that it might be the pledge of the mercy of his Father in redeeming us."
3. "And even at the present day, we ought to contemplate the death of Christ, so as to remember, at the same time, the glory of his resurrection. Thus, we know that he is life, because, in his contest with death, he obtained a splendid victory, and achieved a noble triumph."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "In John 10:15 we have read of the Father’s recognition of the Good Shepherd, who gives the highest proof of His devotion to the shepherd’s work and possession of the shepherd’s character in laying down His life for the sheep."
2. "The perfect conformity to the Father’s will is shown not in laying down the life only, but also in taking it again. The duty of the Shepherd, as set forth in John 10:15-16, can only in this way be accomplished."
3. "He died that His own may ever live in His life. But, if the Father’s love can rest on the Son who is obedient even unto death, and unto life through death, it is essential that the obedience be entirely free. Hence the words of the next verse."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The Father's love for the Son is drawn out by the voluntary offering of the Son for the sin of the world."
2. "The value of the atoning death of Christ lies in the fact that he is the Son of God, the Son of Man, free of sin, and that he makes the offering voluntarily."
3. "The purpose of the Passion was not merely to exhibit his unselfish love; it was in order that He might resume His life, now enriched with quickening power as never before."

### John Gill:
1. "Christ was the object of his Father's love from all eternity, and was loved by him on various accounts."
2. "The laying down his life on this account, was not only well pleasing to his Father, but likewise was done, with the following view."
3. "And which life he took up again, by his divine power, and as the surety of his people, to use it for their good."

### John Wesley:
1. "I lay down my life that I may take it again."
2. "I cheerfully die to expiate the sins of men, to the end I may rise again for their justification."
