Many good works have I showed you, such as healing the sick, casting out devils, and giving sight to the blind. These were not only great and miraculous works, but also acts of mercy and benevolence. ### Henry Alford:
2. "ἐκ τοῦ πατρός μου, because (cf. Joh 10:37-38) He Himself proceeded forth from the Father, and the Father wrought in Him."
  12. "ἔδειξα, because they were part of the manifestation of Himself as the Son of God."
  21. "λιθάζετε, are ye stoning (preparing to stone) Me?"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Many good works have I showed you—that is, works of pure benevolence."
2. "This He says to meet the imputation of unwarrantable assumption of the divine prerogatives."
3. "For which of those works do ye stone me?"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "His miracles were good works, as they tended to promote the happiness of men, and were proofs of his benevolence."
2. "He had performed no other works than those of benevolence; he knew that they could charge him with no other, and he confidently appealed to them as witnesses of that."
3. "Happy would it be if all, when they are opposed and persecuted, could appeal even to their persecutors in proof of their own innocence."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Good works [καλα]. Beautiful, noble works, adapted to call forth admiration and respect."
2. "Jesus does not mean, as the A. V. and Rev. imply, 'for which one of these works,' but 'what is the character of that particular work among all these for which you stone me?'
3. "The me, closing the sentence, is emphatic."

### John Calvin:
1. "...he accuses them of ingratitude, in making so unjust a requital for God’s favors."
2. "He upbraids them with being ungrateful, not only to himself, but rather to God..."
3. "Banish me as much as you please, I have done nothing that does not deserve praise and good-will."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Many good works have I showed you from the Father; for which of these works do ye stone me?"
2. "Every work He has shown them has borne the perfect stamp of a work noble and perfect in its kind, for He has shown it 'from the Father,' who sent Him and ever works with and in Him."
3. "The works and the words are essentially one, alike manifestations of Himself."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Proceeding out of the Father as in John 6:65; John 16:28 rather than as in John 1:14; John 6:46; John 7:29; John 17:7." 
2. "Noble and beautiful deeds Jesus had done in Jerusalem like healing the impotent man and the blind man." 
3. "They had the stones in their hands stretched back to fling at him, a threatening attitude."

### John Gill:
1. "Jesus answered them, many good works,.... Such as healing the sick, and all manner of diseases; dispossessing devils, cleansing lepers, giving sight to the blind, causing the dumb to speak, the deaf to hear, and the lame to walk."
2. "which were not only works of power, but of mercy and beneficence; and therefore are called good works, as well as they were great and miraculous ones: have I showed you from my Father."
3. "suggesting, that his public life had been a continued series of such kind actions to the sons of men, and it could be for nothing else surely, that they took up stones to stone him; wherefore the part they acted, was a most ungrateful, cruel, and barbarous one."
