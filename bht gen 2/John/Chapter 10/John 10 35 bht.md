# John 10:35 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Scripture cannot be broken is a significant declaration affirming the authority and infallibility of God's Word. It assures us that the words spoken by God hold true and cannot be refuted or nullified. This statement emphasizes the power and reliability of Scripture, emphasizing that it is the ultimate authority that cannot be challenged or contradicted. Thus, when God called certain individuals gods, it signifies that they received a divine message or assignment, underscoring the divine origin and importance of the word of God.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "We can hardly build on this passage, as Luthardt has done, a theory as to the distinction between those to whom ὁ λόγος τοῦ θεοῦ came merely in utterance, and those to whom He came in Person." 
2. "The expression, καὶ οὐ δύν . λυθ . ἡ γρ . implies, ‘and if you cannot explain this expression away, if it cannot mean nothing, for it rests on the testimony of God’s word,’ ..."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The whole force of this reasoning, which has been but in part seized by the commentators, lies in what is said of the two parties compared."
2. "The contrast is between those 'to whom the word of God came'—men of the earth, earthy, who were merely privileged to get a divine message to utter or a divine office to discharge—and 'Him whom the Father sanctified and sent into the world,' an expression never used of any merely human messenger of God, and used only of Himself."
3. "Our Lord considers all this as just saying of Himself, 'I am the Son of God'—one nature with Him, yet mysteriously of Him."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The word is characteristic of John."
2. "He uses it of the destruction of the temple, the breaking of the Sabbath, the violation of the law, the destruction of Satan's works."
3. "The Scripture. The passage of scripture."

### John Calvin:
1. "Hence we infer that empires did not spring up at random, nor by the mistakes of men, but that they were appointed by the will of God, because he wishes that political order should exist among men, and that we should be governed by usages and laws."
2. "For this reason Paul says, that all who resist the power are rebels against God, because there is no power but what is ordained by God."
3. "Under the term Law, Christ includes the whole doctrine by which God governed his ancient Church; for since the prophets were only expounders of the Law, the Psalms are justly regarded as an appendage to the Law."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "To be sure, it is in Psalms 82:6 a lower use of the term θεος, but Jesus did not call himself 'Son of Jahweh,' but 'υιος θεου' which can mean only 'Son of Elohim.'"
2. "He is simply stopping the mouths of the rabbis from the charge of blasphemy and he does it effectually."
3. "And the scripture cannot be broken (κα ου δυνατα λυθηνα η γραφη). A parenthesis that drives home the pertinency of the appeal, one that the Pharisees had to accept."

### John Gill:
1. "The Scripture cannot be broken; or be made null and void; whatever that says is true, there is no contradicting it, or objecting to it."
2. "It is a Jewish way of speaking, much used in the Talmud, when one doctor has produced an argument, or instance, in any point of debate, another says, 'it may be broken'; or objected to, in such and such a manner, and be refuted."
3. "But the Scripture cannot be broken, that is not to be objected to, there can be no confutation of that."

### John Wesley:
1. "If he (God) called them gods unto whom the word of God came, (that is, to whom God was then speaking,)"
2. "The Scripture cannot be broken"
