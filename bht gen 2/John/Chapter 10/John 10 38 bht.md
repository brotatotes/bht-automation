The works of Jesus, which none but God can do, serve as irrefutable evidence of His divine nature and authority. Through these works, one can come to a deep understanding and belief in the truth and reality of His teachings. It is through this knowledge and belief that the inseparable union between Jesus and the Father is revealed, affirming their oneness. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Marvin Vincent:
1. In Him. The best texts read ejn tw patri, in the Father.

### John Calvin:
1. "Though I allow you to doubt of my doctrine, you cannot deny, at least, that the miracles which I have performed are from God." 
2. "You therefore openly reject God, and not a man."
3. "The Father is in me; that is, Divine power is manifested in me. And I am in my Father; that is, I do nothing but by the command of God, so that there is a mutual connection between me and my Father."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "If He does the works of His Father, then, even although they might be unwilling to accept His witness respecting Himself, the works bear a testimony they are bound to receive."
2. "Receiving this testimony and thus learning that the works of Jesus are the Father’s works, men will know that He and the Father are one, the Father abiding in Him, and He in the Father."
3. "Their eyes once opened, they will ever see in the works tokens of the Father’s presence."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "These stand irrefutable. The claims, character, words, and works of Jesus challenge the world today as then." 
2. "Purpose clause with ινα and the same verb γινωσκω repeated in different tenses (first γνωτε, the second ingressive aorist active subjunctive, that ye may come to know; then the present active subjunctive, 'that ye may keep on knowing')." 
3. "Thus he repeats (verse John 1:30) sharply his real claim to oneness with the Father as his Son, to actual deity."

### John Gill:
1. "Works, which none but God can do"
2. "Believe the works; not only that they are true and real, and not imaginary and delusory"
3. "That ye may know and believe, that the Father is in me, and I in him"

### John Wesley:
1. "That ye may know and believe — In some a more exact knowledge precedes, in others it follows faith."
2. "I am in the Father and the Father in me."
3. "I and the Father are one — These two sentences illustrate each other."
