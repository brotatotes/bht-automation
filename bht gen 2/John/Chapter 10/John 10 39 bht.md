# John 10:39 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Their attempts to seize and stone Him were futile, for in their rage and hatred, they failed to understand that He was claiming equality with God. Although they sought to bring charges against Him, their efforts were in vain, as He miraculously escaped their clutches. Despite their determination to carry out their evil intentions, the moment was not right, and He eluded their grasp, leaving them astonished and puzzled.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The attempt to stone Him seems to have been abandoned."
2. "They tried again to take Him into custody: and, as before, He (miraculously?) withdrew Himself from them."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "They saw perfectly well that He meant to 'make Himself God' throughout all this dialogue."
2. "He escaped out of their hand."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They evidently understood him as still claiming equality with God."
2. "Jesus left them...and if so, then he is divine."
3. "He escaped."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Escaped out of... literally, went forth out of."

### John Calvin:
1. "Their rage was not at all abated by the words of Christ."
2. "He escaped out of their hands, this could not be accomplished in any other way than by a wonderful exertion of Divine power."
3. "We are not exposed to the lawless passions of wicked men, which God restrains by his bridle, whenever he thinks fit."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "‘Again’ seems to point back to chap. 7, where the same word ‘seize’ is found three times."
2. "We cannot suppose that the Jews had laid aside their design of stoning Him in consequence of the words just spoken"
3. "For some reason not mentioned they now seek not to stone Him on the spot, but to seize Him and carry Him away."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They kept on seeking to seize...as they had tried repeatedly, but in vain." 
2. "They gave up the effort to stone him." 
3. "Overawed, but still angry, the stones fell to the ground, and Jesus walked out."

### John Gill:
1. "He being so far from clearing himself from the charge of blasphemy, they had brought against him, that in their opinion he had greatly strengthened it." 
2. "They thought they had now sufficient proof and evidence to convict him as a blasphemer, in their high court of judicature." 
3. "He escaped out of their hands... because his time was not yet come, and he had other work to do."
