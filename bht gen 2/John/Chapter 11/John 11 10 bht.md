After losing divine guidance and protection, a person walks in the darkness of uncertainty and vulnerability. Without the light from God's providence, he becomes prone to stumbling and being hindered by the obstacles that lie in his path.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 7-10. Let us go into Judea again—He
	was now in Perea, "beyond Jordan."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "It is spiritual darkness that Jesus here pictures, but the result is the same."
2. "The ancients had poor illumination at night...Pedestrians actually used to have little lamps fastened on the feet to light the path."
3. "Man has the capacity for light, but is not the source of light."

### John Gill:
1. "After the sun is set, and there is no light in the air and heavens to direct him:he stumbleth; at everything that lies in the way,because there is no light in him."
2. "So our Lord suggests, that when the time of his death was come, he should then fall a prey into the hands of his enemies."
3. "Nor had he anything to fear from them, and therefore could go into Judea again, with intrepidity and unconcern."

### John Wesley:
1. "If a man walk in the night — If he have not light from God; if his providence does no longer protect him."
