## BHT:
Amongst those who came to them was a group of mourners, offering their condolences and seeking to comfort the sisters concerning their deceased brother. In Jewish customs, it was customary for mourners to gather at the grave, with the comforters standing on the left side. One by one, the comforters would go to the mourners and say, “may you be comforted from heaven.” This act of consoling mourners was regarded as a pious and merciful act, demonstrating the importance of offering comfort in times of grief.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Lazarus' friendship with Jesus had not caused him to be regarded as an apostate, at whose burial every indignity would have been shown."

### John Calvin:
1. "To comfort them concerning their brother."
2. "It is evident from what is here mentioned, that the house of Lazarus and his sisters was greatly respected and honored."
3. "This duty, which the Evangelist mentions, ought not to be blamed, unless on this ground, that sinful excess, which prevails in this and in other departments of life, corrupts what is not in itself sinful."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Amongst those who came to pay to the bereaved sisters the visits of condolence during the seven days of mourning, were many of the leaders of the people, many who were also leaders in hostility to Jesus."
2. "But this is not the only contrast which the mention of the Jews calls forth. As leaders of the people, ruling in 'the city of their solemnities,' they were the representatives of their Church and religion; and the 'comfort' they can offer in the presence of death is no inapt symbol of all that Judaism could do for the mourner."
3. "Thus on the one side we have human sorrow and the vanity of human comfort in the presence of death; on the other side we have Him who is the Life."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "These Jews were probably not hostile to Jesus."
2. "The presence of so many indicates the prominence of the family."
3. "To console them... to offer consolation."

### John Gill:

"The mourners gather together, and stand on the side of the grave; and all that accompany the dead stand round about them, one row within another... the mourners stand on the left hand of the comforters; and all the comforters go to the mourners, one by one, and say to them, תנוחמו מן השמים, 'may ye be comforted from heaven'."
3. "This business of comforting mourners was reckoned an act of great piety and mercy."
