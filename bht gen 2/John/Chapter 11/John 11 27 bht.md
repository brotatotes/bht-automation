# John 11:27 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Martha's confession shows her unwavering confidence in Jesus and her steadfast belief in his words. Though she may not have fully comprehended the depth of his teachings on resurrection and eternal life, she firmly believes in the power of Christ to raise the dead and bring about everlasting life. Her faith is rooted in the understanding that Jesus is the long-awaited Messiah, the Son of God, sent into the world to fulfill God's plan of salvation. Through her confession, Martha acknowledges Jesus' divine nature and his role as the true Savior of humanity.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Her confession, though embracing the great central point of the truth in the last verse, does not enter fully into it."
2. "Nor does she seem to have adequately apprehended its meaning."
3. "[She] have convinced myself, and firmly believe."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "And having such faith in Thee, I can believe all which that comprehends."
2. "While she had a glimmering perception that Resurrection, in every sense of the word, belonged to the Messianic office and Sonship of Jesus."
3. "she means, by this way of expressing herself, to cover much that she felt her ignorance of—as no doubt belonging to Him."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "This was a noble confession. It showed her full confidence in him as the Messiah, and her full belief that all that he said was true."


### Marvin Vincent:
1. "I believe [πεπιστευκα]. Literally, I have believed. The perfect tense."
2. "Rev. Martha goes back to her previous belief, which consists in the recognition of Christ as her Lord."
3. "Whatever faith she has in this new revelation of Christ rests upon the truth that He is the Anointed, the Son of God, even He that cometh into the world."

### John Calvin:
1. "To prove that she believes what she had heard Christ say about himself, that he is the resurrection and the life, Martha replies, that she believes that he is the Christ, and the Son of God."
2. "Now when Martha confesses that it was he who was to come into the world, she strengthens her faith by the predictions of the prophets."
3. "Hence it follows, that we ought to expect from him the full restoration of all things and perfect happiness; and, in short, that he was sent to erect and prepare the true and perfect state of the kingdom of God."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The substitution of ‘I have believed’ for ‘I believe’ is striking."
2. "His last words have been in some measure new and unfamiliar."
3. "She has received Him as the fulfilment of Messianic hope, the revelation of the Divine to man, the long-expected Redeemer of the world."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Martha probably did not understand all that Jesus said and meant, but she did believe in the future resurrection, in eternal life for believers in Christ, in the power of Christ to raise even the dead here and now."
2. "She had heroic faith and makes now her own confession of faith in words that outrank those of Peter in Matthew 16:16 because she makes hers with her brother dead now four days and with the hope that Jesus will raise him up now."
3. "That thou art the Son of God... as John stated that it was his purpose to prove in his Gospel."

### John Gill:
1. "She saith unto him, yea, Lord....she firmly believed all that he said concerning himself, and the happiness of those that believed in him."
2. "I believe...that thou art the Christ the Son of God, which should come into the world; that he was the true Messiah, and the proper and natural Son of God."
3. "He was now come into the world by the assumption of human nature, to work out salvation for his people."
