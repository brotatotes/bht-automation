## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Yet...I said it, that they might believe that thou hast sent me." 
2. "His doing even this, in the audience of the people, was not from any doubt of the prevalency of His prayers in any case, but to show the people that He did nothing without His Father, but all by direct communication with Him."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "I had no anxiety, no doubt as to myself, that I should always be heard."
2. "Jesus never prayed in vain."
3. "In all his miracles the ground of his joy was, not that he was to be praised or honored, but that others were to be benefited and God glorified."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "In view of the distinction which John habitually makes between the Jews and the multitude, the use of the latter term here is noticeable."
2. "It would seem to indicate that a miscellaneous crowd had gathered."
3. "Rev., the multitude."

### John Calvin:
1. "He means, therefore, that there is so great an agreement between him and the Father, that the Father refuses him nothing."
2. "He had no need to pray, because he only executed what he knew that the Father had enjoined."
3. "A certain fixed limit was assigned to miracles by the purpose of God, so far as he knew to be sufficient for confirming the Gospel."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "This confident knowledge is no new experience with Jesus. It has 'always' been so."
2. "It was a picturesque and perilous scene."
3. "Here was a supreme opportunity and Jesus opens his heart about it."

### John Gill:
1. "I knew that thou hearest me always...a support to the faith of Christ...whose advocate, intercessor, and mediator he is." 
2. "He was heard, and always heard by God; and, therefore must have great interest in his affection, and knowledge of his will."
3. "This was a full proof, and clear evidence of his divine mission."
