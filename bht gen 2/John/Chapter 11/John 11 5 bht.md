# John 11:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus had a deep and unwavering love for Martha, her sister, and Lazarus. This love was not only everlasting and unchanging but also unique and special to this noble family. Jesus had a close relationship with them, frequently visiting their home. When the sisters sent word to Jesus about Lazarus's illness, they expected him to come and heal him immediately. This expression of love between Jesus and this beloved family shows us the tender affection and deep friendship that Jesus had with those he loved.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Jesus loved Martha and her sister and Lazarus—what a picture!—one that in every age has attracted the admiration of the whole Christian Church."
2. "No wonder that those miserable skeptics who have carped at the ethical system of the Gospel, as not embracing private friendships in the list of its virtues, have been referred to the Saviour's peculiar regard for this family as a triumphant refutation, if such were needed."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Loved [ηγαπα]. Notice the verb here: not fileiv, as ver. 3."
2. "Lazarus is not mentioned in Luke 10:38 sqq."

### John Calvin:
1. "As Christ is the only mirror of the grace of God, we are taught by this delay on his part, that we ought not to judge of the love of God from the condition which we see before our eyes."
2. "When we have prayed to him, he often delays his assistance, either that he may increase still more our ardor in prayer, or that he may exercise our patience, and, at the same time, accustom us to obedience."
3. "Let believers then implore the assistance of God, but let them also learn to suspend their desires, if he does not stretch out his hand for their assistance as soon as they may think that necessity requires; for, whatever may be his delay, he never sleeps, and never forgets his people."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "This simple record of His love for this family...connects itself both with John 11:4 and also with the statement of John 11:5-6...The object of the Evangelist is to set before us the mind of Jesus."
2. "In John 11:4 we see the first principle of all, supreme regard to the glory of God; here His love for those on whom the affliction must fall, and whom He cannot help save at the hour appointed by His Father."
3. "[Jesus'] obedience to His Father’s will and His love for His sorrowing friends unite in leading Him to Bethany (John 11:7)."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Jesus loved this noble family where he had his home so often."
2. "The sisters expected him to come at once and to heal Lazarus."

### John Gill:
1. "Not only with an everlasting love, a love of complacency and delight, an unchangeable one, and which never varies, nor will ever end, with which he loves all his people alike."
2. "But with a very great human affection, and which was very singular and peculiar to them."
3. "They were the intimate friends, and familiar acquaintance of Christ, whom he often visited, at whose house he frequently was when in those parts."
