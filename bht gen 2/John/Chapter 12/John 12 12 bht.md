# John 12:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
A multitude of people, including many Galileans who had come for the feast, gathered the next day, which was a Sunday. This was a crowded Passover, with people from all over the nation assembling in Jerusalem. So when the word spread that Jesus was coming into Jerusalem, it caused great excitement and drew a large crowd.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The order of the narrative seems to require that these people should have visited Bethany late on the Sabbath, after sunset, and the anointing."
2. "ἀκούσ., from the multitude who had returned from Bethany, John 12:9."
3. "τῇ ἐπ., i.e. on the Sunday: see on John 12:1."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Even so, from the day of this solemn entry into Jerusalem, 'Christ our Passover' was virtually set apart to be 'sacrificed for us'."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. A great multitude [οχλος πολυς] . Some editors add the article and render, the common people.

### John Calvin:
1. "In the first place, we ought to remember Christ’s design, which was, that he came to Jerusalem of his own accord, to offer himself to die; for it was necessary that his death should be voluntary, because the wrath of God could be appeased only by a sacrifice of obedience."
2. "And, indeed, he well knew what would be the result; but before he is dragged to the cross, he wishes to be solemnly acknowledged by the people as their King; nay, he openly declares that he commences his reign by advancing to death."
3. "Thus strangers were more ready to discharge the duty of paying respect to the Son of God than the citizens of Jerusalem, who ought rather to have been all example to all others."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The day, it may be observed, fixed in the tradition of the Church for the triumphal entry, tradition thus confirming the exegesis of the text, and finding in the latter support for its own correctness."
2. "This first day of the Jewish week was the 10th Nisan, the day on which the typical Paschal lamb was selected and set apart for sacrifice."
3. "'The common people' here spoken of are not 'the Jews' (John 12:9), but the multitude that had assembled at Jerusalem at the time in order to celebrate the Passover."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "That is on our Sunday, Palm Sunday." 
2. "A great multitude...should be so translated here."
3. "What they heard was: 'Jesus is coming into Jerusalem.' He is defying the Sanhedrin with all their public advertisement for him."

### John Gill:
1. "They were much people indeed, that came yearly to this feast, from all parts of the nation."
2. "Multitudes of them came, and the fame of Jesus might bring the more."
3. "This might be called indeed, 'a crowded passover'."

### John Wesley:
1. "The next day - On Sunday."
2. "Who were come to the feast - So that this multitude consisted chiefly of Galileans, not men of Jerusalem."
3. "Matthew 21:8."
