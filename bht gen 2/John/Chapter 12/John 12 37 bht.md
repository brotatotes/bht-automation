# John 12:37 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
He had done so many miracles before them, undeniable proofs of his divine power and authority. These miracles were not performed in secret, but openly witnessed by the people. The evidence was right in front of them, undeniable and impossible to ignore.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "So many, not so great." 
2. "The generality did not [have faith]."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Here, having arrived at what was virtually the close of our Lord's public ministry, he casts an affecting glance over the fruitlessness of His whole ministry on the bulk of the now doomed people."
2. "Though he had done so many miracles—the word used suggests their nature as well as number."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "This does not refer to any miracles performed on this occasion, but to all his miracles performed in view of the nation, in healing the sick, opening the eyes of the blind, raising the dead, etc."
2. "John here gives the summary or the result of all his work - undeniable proof of his being the Messiah, yet the nation did not believe on him."
3. "The Jewish nation did not believe as a nation, but rejected him."

### John Calvin:
1. "No man may be disturbed or perplexed at seeing that Christ was despised by the Jews, the Evangelist removes this offense, by showing that he was supported by clear and undoubted testimonies."
2. "First, therefore, we ought to believe that it was not owing to Christ that the Jews did not place confidence in him, because by many miracles he abundantly testified who he was."
3. "John proceeds further, and shows that faith does not proceed from the ordinary faculties of men, but is an uncommon and extraordinary gift of God, and that this was anciently predicted concerning Christ, that very few would believe the Gospel."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "But though he had done so many signs before them, they believed not in him."
2. "One so full of power and grace, so divine in majesty, so human in tenderness."
3. "These signs, too, had been 'so many'... for it is number, not greatness, that in our Gospel is always referred to in this word."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Though he had done so many signs before them, yet they believed not on him." - Genitive absolute with perfect active participle in concessive sense of ποιεω.
2. "No 'yet' in the Greek." - Negative imperfect active of πιστευω, "they kept on not believing on him," stubborn refusal in the face of the light.

### John Gill:
1. "meaning those miracles which were done at Jerusalem, as those which brought Nicodemus to him, and to an acknowledgment of him as a teacher sent from God"
2. "the miracles done by Christ before their eyes, which they could not deny, nor disprove, and were so many, and so great, were aggravations of their unbelief"
3. "such indeed is the nature of that sin, and so deeply rooted is it, that the most powerful means, and mighty works, will not bring a person to believe in Christ, without the powerful and efficacious grace of God."

### John Wesley:
1. "He had done so many miracles before them." 
2. "So that they could not but see them."
