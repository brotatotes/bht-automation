# John 12:41 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Isaiah's vision of God's glory and his subsequent prophetic words were directed towards Christ. The glory that Isaiah saw referred to Christ's divine nature and power, making it evident that Isaiah's prophecy was meant for the revelation of Christ. Those who have truly experienced the sight of Christ's glory cannot help but speak of him, testifying to others about his person and the abundance of his grace.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "This apocalyptic vision was the occasion of that prophecy."
2. "The Evangelist is giving his judgment, having... had his understanding opened to understand the Scriptures, that the passage in Isaiah is spoken of Christ."
3. "The glory which Isaiah saw could only be that of the Son, Who is the ἀπαύγασμα τῆς δόξης of the Father, Whom no eye hath seen."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "These things said Esaias, when he saw his glory, and spake of him—a key of immense importance to the opening of Isaiah's vision." 
2. "THE SON is the King Jehovah who rules in the Old Testament and appears to the elect, as in the New Testament THE SPIRIT, the invisible Minister of the Son, is the Director of the Church and the Revealer in the sanctuary of the heart."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "By his glory is meant the manifestation of him - the Shechinah, or visible cloud that was a representation of God, and that rested over the mercy-seat."
2. "The whole discourse has respect to the Lord Jesus, and the natural construction of the passage requires us to refer it to him."
3. "This passage is therefore conclusive proof that Christ is equal with the Father."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "His glory. In the vision in the temple, Isaiah 6:1, Isaiah 6:3, Isaiah 6:5."
2. "Of Him. Christ."

### John Calvin:
1. "Lest readers should think that this prediction was inappropriately quoted, John expressly states, that the prophet was not sent as a teacher to a single age, but, on the contrary, that the glory of Christ was exhibited to him, that he might be a witness of those things which should take place under his reign."


### Philip Schaff:
1. "It is clear, therefore, that John intentionally unites that Jesus who is the 'I' in 'I shall heal them' with 'the Lord' spoken of in Isaiah 6:1, etc. unites, in short, the Incarnate Word as Messiah and Prophet and the Divine Word in His glory."
2. "If the Jews were thus doomed to unbelief, where was their guilt? The answer is, that they are supposed to have willfully rejected the revelation and grace of God before that point of their history is reached which is now in the eye both of prophet and Evangelist."
3. "Almost at the very instant when the Almighty appeals to me by the presentation of Jesus, He may be appealing to me by His providence, His grace, the general working of His Spirit, so as to make me ready to receive Jesus; these dealings I may so use that the bent of my character may at once appear."


### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Isaiah with spiritual vision saw the glory of the Messiah and spoke of him, John says, whatever modern critics may think or say."
2. "So Jesus said that Abraham saw his day."
3. "Cf. Hebrews 11:13."

### John Gill:
1. "when he saw his glory, and spake of him"
2. "he saw his glory, the glory of his majesty, the glory of his divine nature"
3. "such persons who have a true, spiritual, and saving sight of Christ, of the glory of his person, and the fulness of his grace, cannot but be speaking of him to others"
