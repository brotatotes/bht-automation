# John 12:50 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
By obeying His commandment, we not only receive the promise of eternal life, but we also experience the beginnings of that life. Following His command is the path that leads us to everlasting life and is the very essence of our new spiritual existence.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "[Jesus' command] results in, not as a means merely, but in its accomplishment and expansion, eternal life."
2. "Thus all who do not believe are without excuse; because Jesus is not come, and speaks not, of Himself, but of the Father, Whose will and commandment respecting Him is, that He should be, and give, Life to all."
3. "They who reject Him, reject Life, and [in John 3:19] prefer darkness to Light."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He that obeys the commandment of God shall obtain everlasting life."
2. "The reason of the earnestness and fidelity of the Lord Jesus was because he saw that eternal life depended on the faithful preaching of the message of God."
3. "It is such a close as all his ministers should desire to make a solemn, deliberate, firm exhibition of the truth of God, under a belief that on it was depending the eternal salvation of his hearers, and uttering without fear the solemn message of the Most High to a lost world."

### John Calvin:
1. "He again applauds the fruit of his doctrine, that all may more willingly yield to it."
2. "It is reasonable that wicked men should feel the vengeance of God, whom they now refuse to have as the Author of life."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The substance of the Divine commandment is contained in the word of Jesus, and His word gives life eternal, His word is life."
2. "His commandment is eternal life."
3. "Whatsoever I speak therefore, even as the Father hath said unto me, so I speak."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Thou hast the words of eternal life." Jesus had just said (John 6:63) that his words were spirit and life.
2. The secret lies in the source, "as the Father hath said to me" (ειρηκεν).

### John Gill:
1. "By 'his commandment' is not meant the law... more than this it did not promise to Adam, in innocence."
2. "But the Gospel is here meant... it is a means of quickening sinners with a spiritual life, which issues in an eternal one."
3. "This is to be understood not of what Christ spoke in common conversation, but in the ministry of the word, even of the doctrines of the Gospel."

### John Wesley:
1. His commandment — Kept, is life everlasting - That is the way to it, and the beginning of it.
