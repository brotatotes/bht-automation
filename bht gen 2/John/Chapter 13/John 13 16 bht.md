# John 13:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus taught, The servant is not greater than his lord, emphasizing the humility and submission expected of his followers. This saying, frequently repeated throughout the scriptures, underscores the importance of recognizing and accepting one's role in relation to Jesus. It is essential to understand that even in Christianity, where knowledge of the truth is valued, true happiness lies not in knowing, but in doing. Thus, our actions should align with our beliefs for a complete and fulfilling Christian life.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The servant is not greater than his lord, &c.—an oft-repeated saying." 
2. "If ye know these things, happy are ye if ye do them—a hint that even among real Christians the doing of such things would come lamentably short of the knowing."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Verily, verily. See on John 1:51; John 10:1."
2. "Better a servant, as Rev., a bond-servant."
3. "He that is sent [αποστολος]. Literally, an apostle. See on Matthew 10:2."

### John Calvin:
1. "Verily, verily, I tell you. These are indeed proverbial sayings, which admit of a far more extensive application."
2. "In my opinion, therefore, they are mistaken who suppose them to have a general acceptation, as if Christ were now exhorting his disciples to bear the cross."
3. "It is more correct to say that he employed them to serve his purpose."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "No servant is greater than his lord; neither one that is sent greater than he that sent him."
2. "How often does Jesus speak of Himself in this gospel as the ‘Sent’ of God!"
3. "The disciples are the ‘sent,’ taking the place of Him who was first ‘sent’ but is now gone to the Father."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Jesus here enforces the dignity of service."
2. "He clearly repeated this λογιον often."

### John Gill:
1. "Verily, verily, I say unto you,.... This is a strong way of asseveration, and is used when anything of moment and importance, and worthy of attention and observation is delivered."
2. "The servant is not greater than his Lord; it is enough that he be as his Lord, which was a common phrase among the Jews."
3. "Neither he that is sent, is greater than he that sent him. This is also a way of speaking in use among the Jews... 'he that sends, is greater than he that is sent'."

### John Wesley:
1. "The servant is not greater than his lord"
2. "Nor therefore ought to think much of either doing or suffering the same things."
