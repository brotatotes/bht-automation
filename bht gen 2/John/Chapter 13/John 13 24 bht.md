Simon Peter, positioned behind Jesus, motioned to him using nods and signs. The rest of the disciples, surprised, observed their exchange. Peter's intention was not mere curiosity, but rather a genuine and zealous desire to understand. Through their gestures, they aimed to demonstrate their disapproval of the person mentioned, prevent any harm he might cause, and protect the innocent from suspicion.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Peter, as usual the first to act, is the spokesman of the rest."
2. "Nothing is said to explain why either he or any other of the apostolic band should have supposed that John would know what they themselves were ignorant of."
3. "It may have arisen simply from their having witnessed many tokens of love and confidence on the part of Jesus towards him."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They were all looking in surprise at each other."
2. "Peter was cautious, but could not contain his curiosity."
3. "This is the moment represented in Leonardo da Vinci's 'Last Supper,' only he shows the figures like the monks for whom he painted it."

### John Gill:
1. "Peter perhaps lay at a distance from Christ...he beckoned to him...they made signs, by nodding to one another...such a method Peter took, signifying his desire..." 
2. "...not out of mere curiosity, but from an honest intention and pure zeal..." 
3. "...that he, with the rest, might show their abhorrence of such a person, and avoid him; and do all that lay in their power to hinder him from putting his designs into execution, and that the innocent might be free from all suspicion."

### John Wesley:
1. Simon Peter — Behind Jesus, who lay between them.
