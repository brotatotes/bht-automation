# John 13:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Christ was fully aware of his own greatness and dignity as Mediator, yet he willingly humbled himself to serve others. He understood that the Father had given him all authority and power, and he had complete control over all things. This remarkable act of washing his disciples' feet demonstrated his incredible humility in the midst of his divine authority.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Præfatio gloriæ est instar protestationis, ne quid indignum fecisse existimetur Dominus pedes suorum lavans." Bengel.
2. "The perfect, δέδωκεν, and present, ὑπάγει, are used indefinitely: of things fixed in the counsel of God: or perhaps, rather, as consistent with the historical presents, ἐγείρεται, τίθησιν, to give life and presence to the whole scene."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Jesus knowing that the Father had given all things into his hands." 
2. "An unclouded perception of His relation to the Father, the commission He held from Him, and His approaching return to Him, possessed His soul."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "With the full understanding of his dignity and elevation of character, he yet condescended to wash their feet."
2. "Had he been a mere human teacher or a prophet, it would have been remarkable; but when we remember the dignity of his nature, it shows how low he would stoop to teach and save his people."
3. "Was about to return to heaven."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The aorist marking Jesus' commission as given once for all."
2. "The aorist points to His coming as a historic fact, not as related to its result."
3. "Note the original order: that it was from God He came forth, and unto God He is going."

### John Calvin:
1. "Jesus, knoweth that the Father had given all things into his hands. I am of opinion that this was added for the purpose of informing us whence Christ obtained such a well-regulated composure of mind."
2. "It usually happens, that men seized with fear are greatly agitated. The Evangelist means, that no agitation of this sort was to be found in Christ, because, though he was to be immediately betrayed by Judas, still he knew that the Father had given all things into his hand."
3. "It may be asked, How then was he reduced to such a degree of sadness that he sweat blood? I reply, both were necessary. It was necessary that he should have a dread of death, and it was necessary that, notwithstanding this, he should fearlessly discharge everything that belonged to the office of the Mediator."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Jesus knowing that the Father had given him all things into his hands." 
2. "That he came forth from God." 
3. "That he goeth unto God."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Repeated from verse John 13:1, accenting the full consciousness of Jesus."
2. "A similar statement with εν instead of εις."
3. "Jesus is fully conscious of his deity and Messianic dignity when he performs this humble act."

### John Gill:
1. "These words express the sense Christ had of his own greatness and dignity as Mediator."
2. "He had given all things into his hands; all the persons of the elect, all blessings both of grace and glory for them, and power and authority over all other persons and things, to make them subservient to his purposes."
3. "It greatly illustrates his wonderful humility, that in the view and under a sense and consideration of all this, he should condescend to wash the feet of his disciples."

### John Wesley:
1. Jesus knowing — Though conscious of his own greatness, thus humbled himself.
