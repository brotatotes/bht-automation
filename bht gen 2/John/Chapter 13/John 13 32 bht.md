# John 13:32 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Christ's sufferings and death unquestionably display all of God's perfections, bringing glory to Him. In return for this ultimate act of service, God will also glorify Christ alongside Him, fulfilling the promise of glory bestowed upon Him from before the world began. God will raise Him from the dead on the third day, giving Him the honor He deserves. This sequence of events emphasizes that after the Cross comes the Ascension, without any delay.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "If God be glorified in him, God shall also - in return and reward of this highest of all services ever rendered to Him, or capable of being rendered - glorify him in himself."
2. "Straightway glorify him - referring now to the Resurrection and Exaltation of Christ after this service was over, including all the honor and glory then put upon Him, and that will for ever encircle Him as Head of the new creation."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "If the life and death of the Messiah be such as to lead to the honor of God, such as shall manifest its perfections, and show his goodness, truth, and justice, then he will show that he thus approves his work."
2. "He will not suffer him to go without a proper attestation of his acceptance, and of the honor that God puts on him."
3. "God will always, in the proper time and way, manifest his approbation of those who live so as to promote the honor of his name."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "His glory will be contained in and identified with the divine glory."
2. "En in, indicates unity of being; para with, unity of position."

### John Calvin:
1. "Christ concludes that he will obtain a glorious triumph by his death; because his sole design in it is, to glorify his Father."
2. "The death of the cross, which Christ suffered, is so far from obscuring his high rank, that in that death his high rank is chiefly displayed."
3. "Though the whole world should conspire to cover us with infamy, yet if we sincerely and honestly endeavor to promote the glory of God, we ought not to doubt that God will also glorify us."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "God is the source of the glory and is the glory succeeding the Cross."
2. "No postponement now. First and quickly the Cross, then the Ascension."

### John Gill:
1. "Seeing this is a certain truth, is indisputably matter of fact, that all the perfections of God are glorified in Christ, by his sufferings and death."
2. "God shall also glorify him in himself; either 'with himself'; with his own glory, which was promised to Christ, and which he had before the world was, and for which he prays."
3. "He will raise him again the third day, and give him glory."
