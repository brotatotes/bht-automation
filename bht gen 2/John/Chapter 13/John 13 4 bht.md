# John 13:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
He takes off his outer garments, not wanting anything to impede his action. He is setting aside any distinction or superiority that may have been associated with his clothing, humbling himself to take on the role of a servant. It is a powerful symbol of his humility and love, underscoring the contrast between the disciples' desire for greatness among themselves and Jesus' example of selflessness and service.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "He put Himself into the ordinary dress of a servant."
2. "Did He not humble Himself so far as literally to divest Himself, and gird Himself merely, as the basest of slaves?"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "He riseth from supper, and laid aside his garments—outer garments which would have impeded the operation of washing."
2. "And took a towel and girded himself—assuming a servant's dress."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He riseth from supper - Evidently while they were eating."
2. "Laid aside his garments - His outer garment... customary to lay this aside when they worked or ran, or in the heat of summer."
3. "Took a towel and girded himself - This was the manner of a servant or slave."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "From the supper [εκ του δειπνου]. Out of the group gathered at the table."
2. "Garments [ιματια]. See on Matthew 5:40. Upper garments."
3. "Towel [λεντιον]. A Latin word, linteum. A linen cloth. Only here and ver. 5."

### John Calvin:
1. "The meaning is, that he laid aside his upper garment, not his coat."
2. "For we know that the inhabitants of Eastern countries wore long garments."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "How wonderful the act when compared with the circumstances (mentioned in the previous verse) by which it is introduced!"
2. "In the fullest consciousness of the glory of that work of redeeming love which He had undertaken, He who was in the ‘form of God’ assumed the ‘form,’ and did the work, of ‘a servant,’ a slave, nay, felt that to do this was glory."
3. "What He does, too, is rendered all the more striking by the fact that the remarkable scene described in Luke 22:24, the strife among the disciples which should be the greatest, may have just occurred. In contrast with that eager desire among His servants for superior station in the world, the Master ‘riseth,’ ‘layeth down’ His outer garments, and ‘girdeth’ Himself, becomes as ‘he that serveth (Luke 22:27)."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Riseth from supper... Layeth aside his garments... He had already rebuked the apostles for their strife for precedence at the beginning of the meal."
2. "A towel... Only in this passage in the N.T."
3. "Girded himself... Did Peter not recall this incident when in 1 Peter 5:5 he exhorts all to 'gird yourselves with humility'?"

### John Gill:
1. "his mind being intent on something else; and it being his meat and drink to do his Father's will, he rises and leaves his disciples sitting to finish their meal"
2. "amazing patience and humility!"
3. "an emblem of his laying aside, as it were for a while, his glory and dignity as the Son of God, and of his appearing in the form of a servant"

### John Wesley:
1. Layeth aside his garments — That part of them which would have hindered him.
