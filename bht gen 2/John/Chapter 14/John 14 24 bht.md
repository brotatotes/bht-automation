Those who do not love Jesus do not keep his sayings and fail to properly adhere to his words or commands. However, it is not only Jesus' concern, but also the concern of his Father. The Father regards those who hear, receive, and keep Jesus' words. Jesus emphasizes the importance of his words, truths, and ordinances to his disciples, urging them to pay greater attention and regard to them. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Albert Barnes:
1. The word which ye hear is not mine - See the notes at John 5:19; John 7:16.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. My sayings [λογους] . Rev., words. Compare word, ver. 23. The constituent parts of the one word.

### John Calvin:
1. "Do not look upon the world so as to depend on it; for there will always be some who despise me and my doctrine; but as for you, preserve constantly to the end the grace which you have once received."
2. "Yet he likewise intimates that the world is justly punished for its ingratitude when it perishes in its blindness since, by despising true righteousness, it manifests a wicked hatred towards Christ."
3. "And, indeed, the strength of our faith consists in our knowing that God is our leader, and that we are founded on nothing else than his eternal truth."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "A fuller explanation than before why the world cannot receive the manifestation of the Father and the Son, but given now from the negative rather than the positive side."
2. "To him who receives in faith the 'words' of Jesus are one; he sees their unity; they are a 'word:' to him who receives not in faith they are scattered and unconnected, 'words' not a 'word.'"
3. "Our Lord... shows with equal distinctness that there is no class favored in an arbitrary manner. All make themselves what they are."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He that loveth me not (ο μη αγαπων με). Present active articular participle of αγαπαω with negative μη, 'the one who keeps on not loving me.'"
2. "Is not mine, but the Father's (ουκ εστιν εμοσ, αλλα του πατρος). Predicative possessive pronoun εμος and the predicate genitive of possession πατρος."

### John Gill:
1. "He that loveth me not, keepeth not my sayings... not to keep his words or commands, because they do not keep them aright."
2. "It is not only mine, but my Father's also... my Father has a concern in it, and therefore regards such who hear, receive, and keep it."
3. "This is said by him partly to engage a greater regard and attention to his word, his truths, and ordinances, by his disciples; and to expose and aggravate the sin of those who despised and rejected them."
