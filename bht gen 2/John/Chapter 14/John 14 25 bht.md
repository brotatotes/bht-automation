# John 14:25 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
These words were spoken by Jesus to his disciples as he was preparing to depart from them. He spoke about various topics, such as his relationship with the Father, the way to the Father's house, and the importance of keeping his commandments. Jesus wanted to convey these teachings to his disciples while he was still with them, implying that his time with them was limited. He desired to share these truths in a concise manner that they could understand and apply in the future.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "λελάληκα is proleptic, referring, as εἶπον ( Joh 14:26 ), to the futures, διδάξει and ὑπομνήσει." 
2. "Meyer supposes that a pause took place here, and the Lord looks back on what He had said to them." 
3. "But this does not seem so natural."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "As the Son came in the Father's name, so the Father shall send the Spirit in My name, says Jesus, that is, with like divine power and authority to reproduce in their souls what Christ taught them."
2. "Bringing to living consciousness what lay like slumbering germs in their minds."
3. "The whole of what is here said of THE SPIRIT is decisive of His divine personality."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "For your consolation and guidance."
2. "He had said so many things to console them."
3. "The Spirit would be given also as their Comforter and Guide."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Being yet present [μενων] . Rev., stronger and more literally, while yet abiding.

### John Calvin:
1. "He testifies that in the doctrine which they had heard they have abundant ground of consolation, and that they ought not to seek it anywhere else."
2. "If we do not immediately understand what Christ teaches, we begin to grow weary, and grudge to bestow unprofitable labor on what is obscure. But we must bring an eager desire to receive instruction; we must lend our ears and give attention if we desire to make due proficiency in the school of God."
3. "By this single word we may refute all the inventions which Satan has brought into the Church from the beginning, under the pretense of the Spirit. The spirit that introduces any doctrine or invention apart from the Gospel is a deceiving spirit, and not the Spirit of Christ."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "These things have I spoken unto you while abiding with you."
2. "We now enter upon a new part of the discourse, in which the leading idea is the strength to be afforded to the disciples after the departure of their Lord."
3. "It is important to notice that this is bestowed upon them not merely as disciples, but as disciples about to be sent forth to occupy their Master’s place, and to do His work."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Have I spoken. Perfect active indicative of λαλεω, for permanent keeping (τηρεω verse John 14:23)." 
2. "While yet abiding with you. Present active participle, no 'yet' (ετ) in the Greek, 'while remaining beside (παρ') you' before departing for the coming of the other Paraclete."

### John Gill:
1. "These things have I spoken unto you,.... Concerning his Father, and his Father's house, and the way to both; concerning his being in the father, and the Father in him; concerning keeping his commandments, and the advantages and benefits following upon it..."
2. "...being, says he,[yet] present with you; which is a strong intimation that in a little time he should not be present with them..."
3. "...and that whilst he was present with them, he was desirous of saying such things to them in a brief compendious manner, as they were able to bear; which might be of future use and instruction to them."
