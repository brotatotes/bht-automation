# John 14:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The disciples had knowledge of the way to the Father, as Jesus had spoken of it before. This way encompasses faith, holiness, and even suffering, as they would follow Jesus in his path to his Father's house. They were to imitate his example and be willing to endure whatever challenges come their way, knowing that the way to glory and the fulfillment of their hope lies through these experiences.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "They might have known, and doubtless did know in some sense; but, as Lampe remarks, 'interdum quis laudatur ut officii sui moneatur.' We use thus 'you know,' leaving to be supplied, 'if you would give the matter thought.'"
2. "ὅπου, to the Father; τὴν ὁδόν (in our Lord’s own case, of which this verse treats), His death."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "I am the way, c.—By saying this, He meant rather to draw out their inquiries and reply to them."
2. "Christ is 'THE WAY' to the Father—'no man cometh unto the Father but by Me.'"
3. "He is 'THE TRUTH' of all we find in the Father when we get to Him, 'For in Him dwelleth all the fulness of the Godhead bodily,' and He is all 'THE LIFE' that shall ever flow to us and bless us from the Godhead thus approached and thus manifested in Him—'this is the true God and eternal life'."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He had so often told them that he was to die, and rise, and ascend to heaven, that they could not but understand it."
2. "The way that leads to the dwelling-place to which he was going."
3. "The way which they were to tread was to obey his precepts, imitate his example, and follow him."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "I go [υπαγω]. Withdraw from you." 
2. "See on 8 21." 
3. "Whither I go ye know the way."

### John Calvin:
1. "The disciples know that his death is not a destruction, but a passage to the Father."
2. "We must see Christ, by the eyes of faith, in the heavenly glory and a blessed immortality."
3. "We ought to know that he is the first-fruits of our life, and that the way which was closed against us has been opened by him."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "These words convey to the disciples the assurance that they already had the pledge and earnest of all that Jesus had spoken of." 
2. "To 'know' is not merely to know of; it is to have inward experience of... They 'know the way.'"
3. "They might have feared that it was not so, that they had still much to be taught before they could anticipate with confidence the possession of their hope; and who was to teach them now? But Jesus says, 'Ye know me; and, in knowing me, ye know the way; it is already yours.'"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Ye know the way." - Definite allusion to the puzzle of Peter in John 13:36.
2. "The path to the Father's house is now plain."

### John Gill:
1. "They might have known, at least, whither he was going, since he had spoke of his Father's house, and of his going to prepare a place for them there, and doubtless had some knowledge thereof, though very confused and imperfect."
2. "The way to his Father's house lay through sufferings and death, in which way they also were to follow him to his kingdom and glory."
3. "This also they might have known from some expressions of his, that the way to his Father's house lay through sufferings and death, in which way they also were to follow him to his kingdom and glory."

### John Wesley:
1. The way — Of faith, holiness, sufferings.
