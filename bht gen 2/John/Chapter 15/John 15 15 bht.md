# John 15:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
He has disclosed to them everything that he has heard from the Father, leaving them with no need to search for further knowledge or hidden truths.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Henceforth I call you not servants—that is, in the sense explained in the next words; for servants He still calls them, and they delight to call themselves so, in the sense of being 'under law to Christ.'"
2. "The servant knoweth not what his lord doeth—knows nothing of his master's plans and reasons, but simply receives and executes his orders."
3. "But... friends, for all things that I have heard of my Father I have made known unto you—admitted you to free, unrestrained fellowship, keeping back nothing from you which I have received to communicate."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He here means that the ordinary title by which he would hence forth address them would be that of friends."
2. "It is one of the conditions of slavery not to be let into the counsels and plans of the master. It is the privilege of friendship to be made acquainted with the plans wishes, and wants of the friend."
3. "I have given you the name of friends...that he should give them the name."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "No longer servants, as you were under the dispensation of the law." 
2. "Has no instinctive perception. See on 2:24." 
3. "The position of the pronoun in the Greek is emphatic : 'You I have called friends.'"

### John Calvin:
1. "I have condescended, far more to you than a mortal man is wont to condescend to his servants."
2. "It is indeed a noble commendation of the Gospel, that we have the heart of Christ opened (so to speak) in it."
3. "Let us be satisfied with this testimony of his love toward us which is contained in the Gospel, for it will never deceive us."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "In one sense, indeed, they would be always ‘servants’...but that is not their only relationship to their Lord."
2. "The livelier our sense of the privilege of friendship the deeper will be our humility."
3. "The evidence of this their state (or privilege) is given in the remainder of the verse."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "No longer (ουκετ). As he had done in John 13:16. He was their Rabbi (John 1:38; John 13:13) and Lord (John 13:13)."
2. "I have called you friends (υμας ειρηκα φιλους). Perfect active indicative, permanent state of new dignity."
3. "They will prove worthy of it by continued obedience to Christ as Lord, by being good δουλο."

### John Gill:
1. "Henceforward he would not use, treat, or account them as servants, for the servant knoweth not what his Lord doeth."
2. "I have called you friends, that is, accounted, reckoned of them, used them as his friends and familiar acquaintance, whom he told all his mind unto."
3. "For all things I have heard of my Father, I have made known unto you, all things which he had delivered to him as man and Mediator, by his Father, respecting the salvation of men."

### John Wesley:
1. All things — Which might be of service to you.
