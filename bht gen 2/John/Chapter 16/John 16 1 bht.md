# John 16:1 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
These things were spoken by Christ to prepare and strengthen his disciples, so that they would not be caught off guard or stumble when faced with challenges. Rather than discouraging them, Jesus wanted to prevent them from being tripped up or falling away. It shows his deep care and concern for his followers, actively removing any stumbling blocks or potential sources of offense.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. ταῦτα, scil. ch. John 15:18-27, not only the warning of the hatred of the world, but the promise of the testifying Spirit (Stier).

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "These things have I spoken unto you, that ye should not be offended—both the warnings and the encouragements just given."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "These things - The things spoken in the two previous chapters, promising them divine aid and directing them in the path of duty."
2. "If they had not been apprised of this, if they had not known why Jesus was about to die, and if they had not been encouraged with the promised aid of the Holy Spirit, they would have sunk under these trials, and forsaken him and his cause."
3. "And, in like manner, we should be persuaded that 'he is able to keep that which we commit to him against that day,' 2 Timothy 1:12."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Be offended [σκανδαλισθητε] Rev., made to stumble. In this Gospel only here and John 6:61."
2. "See on Matthew 5:29."
3. "Wyc, be not slandered."

### John Calvin:
1. "He again states that none of those things which he has spoken are superfluous; for, since wars and contests await them, it is necessary that they should be provided beforehand with the necessary arms."
2. "Let us remember that what he then said to the disciples is also spoken to us... we ought to understand that Christ does not send his followers into the field unarmed."
3. "But how few there are that learn this doctrine in a proper manner, is evident from this fact, that they who think that they know it by heart when they are beyond arrow-shot, are no sooner obliged to enter into actual combat than they give way, as if they were utterly ignorant, and had never received any instruction."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "These things have I spoken unto you, that ye may not be made to stumble."
2. "The difference between this passage and the earlier is simply one climax."
3. "It points to the danger of having faith and constancy shaken by trial instead of standing firm in allegiance to Jesus, whatever might be the difficulties encountered in His service."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "the stumblingblocks which trip up a disciple" (Bernard).
2. "the σκανδαλα of faith" (Bernard).
3. "That ye should not be made to stumble" (ινα μη σκανδαλισθητε).

### John Gill:
1. "These things Christ thought, proper to give them notice of beforehand, that expecting them, they might be prepared for them, and be fortified against them."
2. "His view in speaking of them was not to discourage them but to prevent their stumbling at them, and falling by them."
3. "This shows the tender concern of Christ for his disciples, how careful he was to remove every occasion of stumbling, or what might be matter of offense to them."
