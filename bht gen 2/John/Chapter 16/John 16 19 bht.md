# John 16:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus, in his divine omniscience, anticipated their unspoken inquiry and addressed it directly. He knew their hearts and desires even before they articulated them. This not only serves as a demonstration of his deity but also reveals his willingness to explain and clarify his teachings to his disciples. As he perceived their hesitation and curiosity, he took the initiative to offer further insight and understanding. His ability to preempt their question showcases his boundless knowledge and his compassionate desire to guide and instruct his followers.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Knew [εγνω] . Better, Rev., perceived. See on 2 24.

### John Calvin:
1. "Though sometimes the Lord appears to speak to the deaf, he, at length, cures the ignorance of his disciples, that his instruction may not be useless."
2. "Our duty is to endeavor that our slowness of apprehension may not be accompanied by either pride or indolence, but that, on the contrary, we show ourselves to be humble and desirous to learn."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He entered at once into their difficulties, and proceeded to explain more fully what he meant." 
2. "Not indeed dwelling most upon the ‘little while,’ but upon the great and sudden contrasts of mind to be experienced by them."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Jesus perceived... that they were desirous to ask him." 
2. "Their embarrassment was manifest after four inquiries already."
3. "So Jesus takes the initiative."

### John Gill:
1. "This is a proof of Christ's deity, that he is the omniscient God who knows all things, what is in man, even the secrets of the heart."
2. "He not only knew the whisperings of the disciples, and their inquiries among themselves about the sense of his words, but also their secret desires to ask him concerning it."
3. "Which he said before they could put the question to him, they being bashful, and backward, through fear or shame; and which not only confirms what is before observed of his omniscience, but also shows his readiness to open his mind and meaning, and explain himself to his disciples."

### John Wesley:
1. Jesus said to them — Preventing their question.
