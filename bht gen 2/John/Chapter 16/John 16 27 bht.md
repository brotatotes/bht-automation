# John 16:27 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The disciples' love for Christ is a response to the eternal love of God, who gave His Son for them. This love, reflected in their hearts, is a warm and friendly love that binds them to Christ. Their love for Christ is evidence of the Father's love for them, for the cause is known by its effect. Their faith in Christ and their love for Him are intertwined, as faith works by love.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "This love of theirs is that which is called forth by God's eternal love in the gift of His Son..." 
2. "...mirrored in the hearts of those who believe..." 
3. "...and resting on His dear Son."

### Albert Barnes:
1. See John 14:21, John 14:23.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Loveth, as sons, with the love of natural affection."
2. "The love of the disciples for Christ."
3. "From God, from the Father, from beside."

### John Calvin:
1. "The only bond of our union with God is to be united to Christ."
2. "By this word [love], he has well expressed the power and nature of faith."
3. "God loves men in a secret way, before they are called, if they are among the elect; for he loves his own before they are created."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "φιλε, the word for warm and friendly love, here used of God's love for the disciples, while in John 3:16 αγαπαω occurs of God's love for the world." 
2. "πεφιληκατε. Perfect active indicative of φιλεω, 'loved and still love me warmly.'" 
3. "πεπιστευκατε. Perfect active indicative again. Recall the exhortation in John 14:1."

### John Gill:
1. "The Father loved them from everlasting, and had given proofs of it in time, in the gift of his Son to them, and for them."
2. "The Father's love to them was the cause of their love to Christ; and therefore as the cause is known by its effect, they might be assured of the Father's love to them by their love to Christ."
3. "Faith in Christ, and love to him, go together; where the one is, there is the other; faith works by love."
