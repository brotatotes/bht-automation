## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "'Thou hast spoken so clearly of our feeling towards Thee, and of Thyself, that we have no occasion to ask Thee any thing."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It is evident that they had not mentioned their difficulty to him, and that he had not even heard their conversation among themselves."
2. "When, therefore, by his answers to them... he showed that he clearly understood their doubts."
3. "They were convinced that there was 'no need that any man should ask him,' or propose his difficulties to him, since he knew them all and could answer them."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "We are sure. By this." 
2. "Literally, in this."
3. "Compare 1 John 2:3, 1 John 2:5; 1 John 3:16, 1 John 3:19, 1 John 3:24; 1 John 4:9, 1 John 4:10, 1 John 4:11, 1 John 4:17; 1 John 5:2."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They had failed to understand the plain words of Jesus... but Jesus read their very thoughts... and this fact seemed to open their minds to grasp his idea." 
2. "Present active subjunctive with ινα in original sense of asking a question."
3. "Compare παρα του πατρος (verse John 16:27) and εκ του πατρος (verse John 16:28), απο, εκ, παρα all with the ablative of source or origin."

### John Gill:
1. "Not only all men, but all things, even the secrets of men's hearts..."
2. "...they were fully assured that he must know all things..."
3. "By this we believe that thou camest forth from God; was the true Messiah, and had his mission and commission from God, as such..."

### John Wesley:
1. "Thou knowest all things - Even our hearts. Although no question is asked thee, yet thou answerest the thoughts of every one."
2. "By this we believe that thou camest forth from God - They, as it were, echo back the words which he had spoken in John 16:27, implying, We believe in God; we believe also in thee."
