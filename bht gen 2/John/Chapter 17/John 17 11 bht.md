# John 17:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus, in his prayer, expresses his concern for his disciples as he prepares to leave them. He asks his Father to protect and preserve them from the challenges and influences of the world they will continue to live in. The unity Jesus desires for his disciples is not just superficial, but one that mirrors the unity within the Trinity. This unity is not based on equality, but on likeness. Ultimately, Jesus' request for the Father to keep his disciples reflects his deep care and longing for their well-being and harmony as they continue to carry out his mission.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:

### Albert Barnes:
1. "I am no more in the world - I have finished my work among men, and am about to leave the world." 
2. "Keep - Preserve, defend, sustain them in trials, and save them from apostasy."
3. "That they may be one - That they may be united. As we are - This refers not to a union of nature, but of feeling, plan, purpose."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "I come. I am coming. Spoken of His departure to the Father."
2. "Holy. See on saints, Acts 26:10; also on 1 Peter 1:15. Compare 1 John 2:20, and righteous Father."
3. "Those whom. The correct reading is w=, referring to name. Thy name which Thou hast given me. So in ver. 12. Compare Philippians 2:9, Philippians 2:10; Revelation 2:17; Revelation 19:12; Revelation 22:4."

### John Calvin:
1. "So long as he dwelt with them, he cherished them, as a hen gathereth her chickens under her wings... but now that he is about to depart, he asks that the Father will guard them by his protection."
2. "It yields no small consolation to us when we learn that the Son of God becomes so much the more earnest about the salvation of his people when he leaves them as to his bodily presence."
3. "Then will our unity be truly happy, when it shall bear the image of God the Father and of Christ, as the wax takes the form of the seal which is impressed upon it."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The very greatness of His joy in the thought of His own glorious return to His Father rouses His tenderest sympathy for those who have so much to do and to suffer before they can share His joy."
2. "He who is holy is entirely separated from all that is carnal and outward in this present world, so that pure spirituality and heavenliness alone rule in Him."
3. "It is the Divine unity of love that is referred to, all wills bowing in the same direction, all affections burning with the same flame, all aims directed to the same end one blessed harmony of love."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Christ will no longer be visibly present to the world, but he will be with the believers through the Holy Spirit."
2. "Jesus offers the unity in the Trinity (three persons, but one God) as the model for believers."
3. "The witness of the disciples will fail without harmony."

### John Gill:
1. "Christ was not so intent on his own glory, as to neglect the good of his people, and to be unconcerned for them."
2. "His request is, that his Father would keep them from the evil of the world; from sinking under temptations and afflictions; faithful to him and to his Gospel, and in unity among themselves."
3. "That they may be one as we are; in nature, will, affection and understanding; which must be understood not of equality, but of likeness."

### John Wesley:
1. "Keep them through thy name — Thy power, mercy, wisdom, that they may be one - with us and with each other." 
2. "One body, separate from the world: as we are - By resemblance to us, though not equality."
