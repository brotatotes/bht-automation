# John 17:20 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Christ's prayer is a plea for the well-being of all believers, both present and future. It is a request for their faith to be strengthened and protected. The power of the apostolic word is emphasized, as it is through their preaching and teaching that people would come to believe in Christ. This prayer serves as a reminder of the importance of the Church's tradition, as it is the authoritative guide for what believers should believe. Ultimately, Christ's prayer is a source of comfort and assurance, assuring believers that they are under his care and protection.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The present part. expresses the state of faith in which all believers are found: the future (of the re) would refer more to the act of belief by which that state is begun." 
2. "But perhaps it is best to take the pres. as proleptic."
3. "It is strikingly set forth here that all subsequent belief on Christ would take place through the apostolic word: see Romans 10:16-17."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "This very important explanation, uttered in condescension to the hearers and readers of this prayer in all time, is meant not merely of what follows, but of the whole prayer."
2. "The majority of the best manuscripts read 'which believe,' all future time being viewed as present, while the present is viewed as past and gone."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Shall believe [πιστευσοντων] . The future body of believers is conceived as actually existing.
2. On me through their word. "Believe through their word" forms a compound idea.

### John Calvin:
1. "This prayer of Christ is a safe harbour, and whoever retreats into it is safe from all danger of shipwreck; for it is as if Christ had solemnly sworn that he will devote his care and diligence to our salvation."
2. "Let the world then condemn us a thousand times, this alone ought to satisfy us, that Christ acknowledges us to be his heritage and pleads with the Father for us."
3. "The tradition of the Church is therefore their only authoritative guide to what they shall believe. But let us remember that the Son of God, who alone is competent to judge, does not approve of any other faith than that which is drawn from the doctrine of the apostles."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "But not concerning these only do I ask, but also concerning them which believe in me through their word."
2. "From the thought of the disciples whom He was sending forth to carry on His work, Jesus now turns, in the third and last section of His prayer, to the thought of all who through their word shall be brought to faith, to the thought of believers in every country and in every age."
3. "The ‘word’ spoken of is that of John 17:14, the special word which is the revelation of the Father, and which brings man to recognize the love of the Father as it appears in the Son, and in the Son to them."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Through their word. Through the agency of conversation and preaching, blessed privilege open to all believers thus to win men to Christ." 
2. "But an agency sadly limited by the lives of those who speak in Christ's name."

### John Gill:
1. "Christ is the object of true faith; which faith is not a mere assent of the mind to any truth concerning Christ, but it is a spiritual sight of him, of the necessity, fitness, and suitableness of him as a Saviour, a going forth unto him, laying hold on him, and depending upon him for life and salvation."
2. "The preaching of the Gospel is the instrumental means through which faith is obtained, and it is indeed a gift of God, and a fruit of electing grace."
3. "Our Lord knew that there would be a number, in all successive generations, that would believe in him, through the ministry of the word; and for these persons, and their conversion, and the success of the Gospel, to the good of their souls, he prays."

### John Wesley:
1. For them who will believe — In all ages.
