# John 17:22 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The glory that radiates from the only begotten Son is reflected in all those who are called children of God. This truth reveals the magnificence and significance of every believer, highlighting the grandeur of being united with Christ. Indeed, the majesty of Christians should not be underestimated, for it is a testament to the transformative power of God's grace and the immeasurable riches of His glory.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The last clause shows the meaning of the first. It is not the future glory of the heavenly state, but the secret of that present unity just before spoken of."
2. "The glory, therefore, of the indwelling Spirit of Christ; the glory of an accepted state, of a holy character, of every grace."
3. "The glory which thou gavest—hast given me I have given them, that they may be one, even as we are one."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The honor which thou hast conferred on me by admitting me to union with thee, the same honor I have conferred on them by admitting them to like union with me."
2. "Not in nature, or in the mode of existence - for this was not the subject of discourse, and would be impossible - but in feeling, in principle, in purpose."
3. "This is the union between the Father and the Son. Always, in the creation, preservation, and redemption of the world, the Father and the Son have sought the same object, and this is to be the model on which Christians should act."

### John Calvin:
1. "While a pattern of perfect happiness was exhibited in Christ, he had nothing that belonged peculiarly to himself, but rather was rich, in order to enrich those who believed in him."
2. "Our happiness lies in having the image of God restored and formed anew in us, which was defaced by sin."
3. "No one ought to be reckoned among the disciples of Christ, unless we perceive the glory of God impressed on him, as with a seal, by the likeness of Christ."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Jesus had prayed that all believers might be one as He and the Father were one."
2. "The 'glory' referred to is that of self-sacrificing love, brought out from amidst the taunts with which men met it when displayed in Jesus, and owned by the Father as the only true glory."
3. "Not worldly honour or station, the favour of kings, the patronage of statesmen, or the wealth of nations, was their glory; but the gift to love, and to sacrifice themselves for the world’s good."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "And the glory (καγω την δοξαν). Literally, 'And I the glory,' with emphasis on 'I.' It is the glory of the Incarnate Word (Bernard), cf. John 1:14; John 2:11, not the glory of the Eternal Word mentioned in John 17:24."
2. "Quanta majestas Christianorum!"
3. "Then verse John 17:22 repeats the unity prayed for in verse John 17:21."

### John Gill:
1. "Not the glory of his deity; this is the same with his Father, what he has in right of nature, and not by gift; nor can it be communicated to creatures."
2. "Rather the Gospel is meant, which is glorious in its author, matter and subject, in its doctrines, in the blessing: grace it reveals, and promises it contains, and in the efficacy and usefulness of it to the souls of men."
3. "This is one considerable branch of the glory of Christ, as Mediator, to be full of grace and truth; this was given him by the Father, and is what he communicates to his; even the Spirit, and all sorts of grace, and every supply of it; and which greatly contributes to the union of the saints among themselves."

### John Wesley:
1. "The glory of the only begotten shines in all the sons of God."
2. "How great is the majesty of Christians."
