# John 17:6 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus Christ, in his prayer to the Father, reveals that he has made known the character, attributes, and saving will of God to his disciples. These disciples, who belong to God by creation and through their lineage as Israelites, have been given to Jesus by the Father through their faith in his teachings. By keeping God's word, the Gospel, in their hearts and defending it against its enemies, they have demonstrated their loyalty and commitment to the Father and his Son.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Thy Name of FATHER, which was so constantly on the lips of our Lord; and which derived its living meaning and power from His teaching." 
2. "The Father gave them to Christ, by leading them to Christ, see ch. John 6:37; John 6:44-45." 
3. "Thine (σοί, from σός) they were Israelites Thy people, before: not only outwardly, but Israelites indeed, see ch. John 1:48, and thus prepared to receive Christ."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "From praying for Himself He now comes to pray for His disciples."
2. "I have manifested—I manifested thy name—His whole character towards mankind."
3. "To the men thou gavest me out of the world."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Jesus had made known his character, his law, his will, his plan of mercy - or, in other words, he had revealed God to them."
2. "The word 'name' is often used to designate the person."
3. "God has a right to the services of all; and he has a right to appoint us to any labor, however humble, or hazardous, or wearisome, where we may promote his glory and honor his name."

### John Calvin:
1. "There was no want of faith or diligence on the part of Christ, to call all men to God, but among the elect only was his labor profitable and efficacious."
2. "Let us therefore infer that not all to whom the doctrine is exhibited are truly and efficaciously taught, but only those whose minds are enlightened."
3. "The certainty of that election by free grace lies in this, that he commits to the guardianship of his son all whom he has elected, that they may not perish; and this is the point to which we should turn our eyes, that we may be fully certain that we belong to the rank of the children of God."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "That 'word' had been especially the 'name' of God, His name as 'Father,' including His character, His attributes, His saving will as revealed in Jesus."
2. "The position is exactly His own (John 17:14), so that even already we see how closely they are identified with Him, and are fitted, as taking His place, to lift men up into their own higher sphere."
3. "They, on their part, had answered the purpose of the Father: they had kept the 'word' of God; not the general revelation of His will, but, if we may so speak, the revelation of the Logos, of the 'Word,' in the soul."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Jesus regards the apostles as the Father's gift to him." 
2. "Jesus claims loyalty and fidelity in these men with the one exception of Judas."

### John Gill:
1. "The name of twelve letters, Galatinus pretends, is Father, Son, and Holy Ghost, the words for which in the Hebrew language consist of twelve letters."
2. "It is none of these names, nor any other scriptural ones, that are here meant; but either God himself, or the perfections of his nature, or his will of command, or rather his Gospel."
3. "They have kept thy word; the Gospel, not only in their memories, but in their hearts; and having publicly professed it, they defended it valiantly against the enemies of it, and kept it pure and incorrupt."

### John Wesley:
1. "I have manifested thy name — All thy attributes; and in particular thy paternal relation to believers."
2. "They were thine — By creation, and by descent from Abraham. And thou hast given them me — By giving them faith in what I have spoken."
3. "And so John 17:12.They were thine — By creation, and by descent from Abraham. And thou hast given them me — By giving them faith in what I have spoken. So John 17:9."
