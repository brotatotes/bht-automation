# John 17:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Through his words, Jesus declares that his disciples now understand that all of the teachings and miraculous works he has done are from God. This knowledge not only confirms the divine origin of Jesus' mission but also deepens his disciples' appreciation for him, as they recognize the grace of God at work in his actions. The disciples have come to see that everything Jesus has done and spoken is not only right and true but also a reflection of the Father's will.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "My whole words and works: are all from Thee."
2. "On this their conviction, which however had not reached its ripeness yet, see ch. John 16:30."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They have known - They have been taught that and have believed it."
2. "Hast given me - This refers, doubtless, to the doctrine of Christ."
3. "They are assured that all my instructions are of God."

### John Calvin:
1. "Here our Lord expresses what is the chief part in faith, which consists in our believing in Christ in such a manner, that faith does not rest satisfied with beholding the flesh, but perceives his Divine power."
2. "For when he says, They have known that all things which thou hast given me are from thee, he means, that believers feel that all that they possess is heavenly and divine."
3. "And, indeed, if we do not perceive God in Christ, we must remain continually in a state of hesitation."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "These words do more than state that the disciples knew this fact. They include a far deeper meaning, intended to bring out more fully the position of the disciples as the representatives of Jesus."
2. "For what was it that He knew? What was the element of relation to the Father in which he lived? It was that all He had was from the Father; that all He was the reflex of the Father; that His words, His works, His whole activity, were the Father’s; that He came forth from the Father, and was sent by Him into the world."
3. "This was the consciousness which especially distinguished Him in the fulfilling of His mission; and now that consciousness has passed over into them."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They have come to know, not as fully as they felt (John 16:30), and yet in a real sense."

### John Gill:
1. "Now they have known, that all things whatsoever thou hast given me...are of thee." 
2. "Everything relating to his mediatorial office and character, power to perform miracles, knowledge and wisdom to preach the Gospel, strength to procure the salvation of his people."
3. "The knowledge of this by his disciples, must greatly confirm the mission of Christ, render him very suitable to them, cause them to entertain a greater esteem for him, lead them into some admiring views of the grace of God."

### John Wesley:
1. "Now they know that all things...are of thee." 
2. "Which I have done and spoken...right and true."
