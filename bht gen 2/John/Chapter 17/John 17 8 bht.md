# John 17:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The disciples have received the words of Christ and recognized them truly, believing in His mission from the Father and being fully persuaded that He is the true Messiah. These words are not mere commands, but doctrines of God and of Christ, in accordance with the Scriptures. Christ, as the great prophet, faithfully delivered these words from His Father, imparting them to His disciples who received them through faith.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The imparting to them of these ῥήματα was the efficient cause of their faith."
2. "In the first, the ἔγνωσαν ἀληθῶς stamps our Lord’s approval on their knowledge, and distinguishes it from such knowledge as the bare οἴδαμεν [ch. Joh 3:2] of Nicodemus and his colleagues."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 8. they . . . have known surely that
	I came out from thee—(See on  :-; John 17:1).

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Christ often represented himself as instructed and sent to teach certain great truths to men."
2. "Those he taught, and no others."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The words [τα ρηματα] . Compare thy word [λογον] , ver. 6. That signified the gospel message in its entirety."
2. "This, the message considered in its constituent parts."
3. "See on Luke 1:37. Compare John 5:38, John 5:47; John 6:60, John 6:63, John 6:68; John 8:43, John 8:47, John 8:51; John 12:47, John 12:48; John 14:3, John 14:7."

### John Calvin:
1. "The receiving, of which he speaks, arose from his having efficaciously manifested to them the name of his Father by the Holy Spirit."
2. "The meaning amounts to this, that faith ought to cast its eyes direct on Christ, yet so as to form no conception of him that is earthly or mean, but to be carried upwards to his Divine power, so as to believe firmly that he has perfectly in himself God, and all that belongs to God."
3. "Let it be observed, also, that in the former clause he employs the verb know and now he employs the verb believe; for thus he shows that nothing which relates to God can be known aright but by faith, but that in faith there is such certainty that it is justly called knowledge."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The disciples had received a consciousness similar to that of Jesus, because He, on His part, had implanted His words in them."
2. "They, on their part, had responded, receiving what He gave."
3. "He has transferred His own mind to His disciples, that He has taught them His own truths and thoughts, and that they, while retaining their own proper individuality, have fully made them their own."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Each word of God, as in John 3:34, and of Christ."
2. "They definitely 'received and recognized truly' (αληθως). There was comfort to Christ in this fact."
3. "The disciples believed in Christ's mission from the Father (John 6:69; Matthew 16:16)."

### John Gill:
1. "Not commands, but doctrines; and these not the doctrines of men; nor any of the divers and strange doctrines; but what are called in Scripture the doctrine of God, of Christ, of the apostles, are agreeably to the Scriptures, and according to godliness; and are styled the word of truth, of faith, of righteousness, of peace and reconciliation, of life and salvation, and of the Gospel."
2. "...for the words and doctrines which Christ, as the great prophet in Israel spoke, were not his own, but his Father's; and these were given him to speak and deliver to others, and in which he was faithful..."
3. "...being fully persuaded he was the true Messiah, and no impostor."

### John Wesley:
1. They have received them — By faith.
