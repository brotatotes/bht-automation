# John 18:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
She effortlessly identifies John as a disciple of Jesus and wonders if Peter is also one of them. While she refers to Jesus as this man in a derogatory manner, it is unlikely that she meant to harm Peter with her question.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "This first denial was to all appearance rashly and almost inadvertently made, from a mere feeling of shame."
2. "Lücke suggests that Peter may have set himself among the servants of the High Priest to bear out his denial."
3. "The μὴ καὶ σύ (Joh 18:25), as Luthardt remarks, implies that the other disciple had already been recognized as a follower of Jesus, and had escaped annoyance."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "His demeanor and timidity, which must have vividly showed themselves, as it so generally happens, leading to the recognition of him."
2. "He denied before them all, saying, I know not what thou sayest."
3. "This was THE FIRST DENIAL."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The question is put in a negative form, as if expecting a negative answer: thou art not, art thou?"
2. "Showing that she recognized John as a disciple."

### John Calvin:
1. "He had boasted that he would prove to be a valiant champion, and able to meet death with firmness; and now, at the voice of a single maid, and that voice unaccompanied by threatening, he is confounded and throws down his arms."
2. "Certainly, all the strength that appears to be in men is smoke, which a breath immediately drives away."
3. "Let us therefore learn not to be brave in any other than the Lord."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The maid knew that John was one of the disciples of Jesus, and the interest taken by him in Peter leads her to suppose that the latter must also be one of them."
2. "A little incident is now mentioned which, slight as it seems, must be carefully attended to."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Art thou also?" (μη κα συ ει?): Expecting the negative answer, though she really believed he was.
2. "This man's" (του ανθρωπου τουτου): Contemptuous use of ουτος with a gesture toward Jesus. 


### John Gill:
1. "She speaks of Christ in the vulgar dialect of the Jews, calling him 'this man'; not only esteeming him a mere man, but a worthless man."
2. "He never denied that Christ was God or the Son of God, or that he was come in the flesh, or that he was the Messiah and Saviour of sinners."
3. "Many are the aggravations of his fall; which came to pass as soon as ever he was entered almost; and that by the means of a maid, a servant maid, a very inferior one."

### John Wesley:
1. "Art thou also — As well as the others, one of this man's disciples"
2. "She does not appear to have asked with any design to hurt him."
