# John 18:20 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
I spake openly, Jesus confidently declares, emphasizing His fearless and straightforward communication of truth to the world. His teachings were not hidden in private apartments, but delivered with clarity and boldness. Throughout His ministry, He tirelessly taught in synagogues, the public places of worship where people gathered. There were no concealed doctrines or hidden messages; everything He taught in secret was in perfect alignment with what He taught openly. Jesus' words demonstrate His unwavering commitment to proclaiming the truth to all who would listen.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "I am one, who..."
2. "plainly (subjective): not openly"
3. "the distinction is made between synagogues... and τὸ ἱερόν"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "I ever taught in the synagogues and in the temple, whither the Jews always resort—courting publicity, though with sublime noiselessness."
2. "Nothing—that is, nothing of any different nature; all His private communications with the Twelve being but explanations and developments of His public teaching."
3. "In secret have I said—spake I."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "If his doctrine had tended to excite sedition and tumult, if he had aimed to overthrow the government, he would have trained his friends in secret...Instead of that, he had proclaimed his views to all."
2. "He here speaks the language of one conscious of innocence and determined to insist on his rights."
3. "He had taught no private or concealed doctrine. He had taught nothing to his disciples which he had not himself taught in public and commanded them to do."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Render, in synagogue: when the people were assembled."
2. "Always resort: For always, read all."
3. "Sunercontai is rather come together, assemble."

### John Calvin:
1. "It is a childish error into which some have fallen, who think that this reply of Christ condemns those who expound the word of God in private apartments."
2. "Christ does not argue as to what is lawful and what is not lawful, but his intention was to put down the insolent malice of Caiaphas."
3. "He could, therefore, testify with a good conscience that he had openly declared and honestly proclaimed the substance of his doctrine."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Openly (παρρησια). As already shown... See John 7:4 for same contrast between εν παρρησια and εν κρυπτω."
2. "I ever taught (εγω παντοτε εδιδαξα). Constative aorist active indicative. For the temple teaching see John 2:19; John 7:14; John 7:28; John 8:20; John 19:23; Mark 14:49 and John 6:59 for the synagogue teaching (often in the Synoptics)."
3. "Examples of private teaching are Nicodemus (John 18:3) and the woman of Samaria (John 18:4). Jesus ignores the sneer at his disciples, but challenges the inquiry about his teaching as needless."

### John Gill:
1. "I spake openly to the world; with all plainness, freedom, and boldness, without any reserve or ambiguity."
2. "I ever taught in the synagogue; the places of public worship in all parts of the nation."
3. "And in secret have I said nothing; not but that our Lord taught in other places than what are here mentioned, as on mountains, in deserts, by the sea shore, and in private houses, yet generally to great multitudes."

### John Wesley:
1. "I spake openly"
2. "continually"
3. "In secret have I said nothing"
