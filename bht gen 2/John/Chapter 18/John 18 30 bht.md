They were indignant at the interrogative manner in which they were addressed and they were filled with righteous anger. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "They do not mention the charge of blasphemy brought against Him by the Sanhedrim, for fear of the entire rejection of their cause." 
2. "The Procurators in such cases had a discretionary power."
3. "On what they did say, Grot. observes, 'Quod probationibus deerat, id supplere volunt sua auctoritate.'"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "They were conscious they had no case of which Pilate could take cognizance."
2. "...they had already found Him worthy of death by their own law."
3. "...they had come merely for his sanction."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "If he were not a malefactor - A violator of the law. If we had not determined that he was such, and was worthy of death." 
2. "It is probable that in ordinary cases the Roman governor was not accustomed to make very strict inquiry into the justice of the sentence."
3. "He had doubtless heard of the miracles of Jesus. He seems to have been strongly pre-possessed with the belief of his innocence."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Malefactor [κακοποιος] . Rev., evil-doer. From kakon, evil, and poiew, to do."
2. "Luke uses a different word, kakourgov, from kakon, evil, and ergw, to work."
3. "See on 1 Peter 2:12."

### John Calvin:
1. "Such is the manner in which wicked men, whom God has raised to a high degree of honor, blinded as it were by their own greatness, allow themselves to do whatever they choose."
2. "They wish that Christ should be reckoned a malefactor, and for no other reason but because they accuse him."
3. "When men are intoxicated with pride, nothing is more difficult than to arouse them to form a sound and correct judgment."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "There is pride in the reply, a lofty sense of their own importance and dignity, that importance and dignity which they are so soon to sacrifice."
2. "The person whom we bring before thee is a malefactor: is it not enough that we say so, and that we deliver him up to thee?"


### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "This is a pious pose of infallibility not in the Synoptics."
2. "They then proceeded to make the charges (Luke 23:2) as indeed John implies (John 18:31; John 18:33)."
3. "Here the periphrastic present participle ποιων with κακον emphasizes the idea that Jesus was a habitual evil-doer (Abbott)."

### John Gill:
1. "Offended at the question put to them, and filled with indignation that they should be so interrogated..."
2. "... they suggest that he was guilty of some crimes recognizable by Caesar's court..."
3. "... they being men of such rank and dignity, and of so much knowledge, learning, and religion..."
