# John 18:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Christ's command to let his disciples go shows his power over people's actions and their obedience to his word. The fact that they did not seize Peter, even after he cut off the ear of Malchus, demonstrates their obedience to Christ's command.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "I have told you that I am He: if
   therefore ye seek me, let these go their way—Wonderful
   self-possession, and consideration for others, in such circumstances!"
2. "Wonderful self-possession, and consideration for others, in such circumstances!"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "This shows his care and love even in the hour of danger."
2. "He, the faithful Captain of salvation, went foremost into trials; he, the Good Shepherd, secured the safety of the flock, and went before them into danger."
3. "The wisdom, caution, and prudence of Jesus forsook him in no peril, however sudden, and in no circumstances, however difficult or trying."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. These. The disciples. Go their way [υπαγειν] . Withdraw.

### John Calvin:
1. "Here we see how the Son of God not only submits to death of his own accord, that by his obedience he may blot out our transgressions, but also how he discharges the office of a good Shepherd in protecting his flock."
2. "Whenever, therefore, either wicked men or devils make an attack upon us, let us not doubt that this good Shepherd is ready to aid us in the same manner."
3. "Yet by his example Christ has laid down to shepherds a rule which they ought to follow, if they wish to discharge their office in a right manner."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "I told you that I am he: if therefore ye seek me, let these go their way."
2. "The illustration...beheld in this...of the meaning of certain words of Jesus uttered not long before."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Let these go their way."
2. "Jesus shows solicitude for the eleven as he had warned them and prayed for them."
3. "He is trying to help them."

### John Gill:
1. "Christ was about to suffer for them, and therefore it was not just that they should suffer too; nor was it proper that they should suffer with him, lest their sufferings should be thought to be a part of the price of redemption."
2. "This shows the love of Christ to his disciples, and his care of them, and also his power, and that he could have saved himself as well as them."
3. "There is no demand to be made upon them, either by the law or justice of God; there is no wrath or punishment will be inflicted on them, either here or hereafter; and they may, and shall go their way into everlasting life, when time shall be no more with them, neither law nor justice having anything to say to the contrary."

### John Wesley:
1. "If ye seek me, let these (my disciples) go." - It was an eminent instance of his power over the spirits of men, that they so far obeyed this word.
2. "They so far obeyed this word, as not to seize even Peter, when he had cut off the ear of Malchus."
