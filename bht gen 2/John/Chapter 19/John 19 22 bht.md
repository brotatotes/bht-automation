# John 19:22 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Pilate's firmness in not succumbing to the pressure and attempts to change his resolution can be attributed to the providence of God. It is evident that various attempts were made to sway his decision. Pilate's unwavering stance serves as an example for us to remain steadfast in defending the truth, just as he did. Moreover, those who actively work to suppress the knowledge of the truth reveal themselves to be worse than Pilate, highlighting the seriousness of their actions.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### John Calvin:
1. "Pilate’s firmness must be ascribed to the providence of God; for there can be no doubt that they attempted, in various ways, to change his resolution."
2. "The example of Pilate reminds us, also, that it is our duty to remain steady in defending the truth."
3. "What rank, therefore, shall we assign to those who do all that they can to suppress the knowledge of it, since they show that they are worse than Pilate?"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "It is impossible to mistake the feeling of the Evangelist that in all this the finger of God is to be traced."
2. "Those who refuse to ‘believe’ shall yet be compelled to own that Jesus is King."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "What I have written I have written (ο γεγραφα γεγραφα). With emphasis on the permanence of the accusation on the board."
2. "Pilate has a sudden spirit of stubbornness in this detail to the surprise of the chief priests."
3. "Technically he was correct, for he had condemned Jesus on this charge made by the chief priests."

### John Gill:
1. "He seems to say this, as one angry and displeased with them."
2. "This he said, either because he could not alter it after it was written, for it is said that 'a proconsul's table is his sentence, which being once read, not one letter can either be increased or diminished.'"
3. "But was so directed, and overruled by divine providence, as to write, so to persist in, and abide by what he had wrote inviolably."

### John Wesley:
1. What I have written, I have written — That shall stand.
