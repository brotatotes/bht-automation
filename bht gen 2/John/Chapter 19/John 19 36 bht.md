# John 19:36 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The paschal lamb, an eminent type of Christ, was a symbol that pointed to the unbrokenness of Christ's body, as none of its bones were to be broken according to the command in Exodus 12:46.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "These things were a fulfillment of Scripture."
2. "The primary reference is to the Paschal Lamb of Exod."
3. "See 1 Corinthians 5:7."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "But though we are to see here the fulfilment of a very definite typical ordinance, we shall, on searching deeper, see in it a remarkable divine interposition to protect the sacred body of Christ from the last indignity after He had finished the work given Him to do."
2. "Every imaginable indignity had been permitted before that, up to the moment of His death. But no sooner is that over than an Unseen hand is found to have provided against the clubs of the rude soldiers coming in contact with that temple of the Godhead."
3. "Very different from such violence was that spear-thrust, for which not only doubting Thomas would thank the soldier, but intelligent believers in every age, to whom the certainty of their Lord's death and resurrection is the life of their whole Christianity."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "John here regards the paschal lamb as an emblem of Christ; and as in the law it was commanded that a bone of that lamb should not be broken, so, in the providence of God, it was ordered that a bone of the Savior should not be broken."
2. "The Scripture thus received a complete fulfillment respecting both the type and the antitype."
3. "Some have supposed, however, that John referred to Psalms 34:20."

### John Calvin:
1. "Note, Moses takes for granted that that lamb was a figure of the true and only sacrifice, by which the Church was to be redeemed."
2. "From this analogy, or resemblance, faith derives no ordinary advantage, for, in all the ceremonies of the Law, it beholds the salvation which has been manifested in Christ."
3. "Thus also the Jews are reminded that they ought to seek in Christ the substance of all those things which the Law prefigured, but did not actually accomplish."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Be broken (συντριβησετα). Second future passive of συντριβω, to crush together."
2. "A free quotation of Exodus 12:46 about the paschal lamb."

### John Gill:
1. "The words are to be understood of Christ, he is the poor man that is particularly pointed at... who kept his bones from being broken when others were; and by this incident, this passage had its literal fulfilment in him."
2. "The paschal lamb was a type of Christ, whose bones were none of them to be broken, to show that his life was not taken away by men, but was laid down freely by himself; and also the unbroken strength of Christ under the weight of sin, the curse of the law, and the wrath of God, and conflict with Satan, when he obtained eternal redemption for us."
3. "Moreover, as none of the bones of his natural body were to be broken, so none that are members of him in a spiritual sense, who are bone of his bone and flesh of his flesh, shall ever be lost."

### John Wesley:
1. "A bone of it shall not be broken" - This was originally spoken of the paschal lamb, an eminent type of Christ. Exodus 12:46.
