# John 2:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Remove everything from here, including the doves and the cages in which they are kept, Jesus commanded, making it clear that not even the items associated with the questionable trade were to remain in the temple. He warned against turning my Father's house into a place of commercial activity, reminding them of the prophecy from Malachi 3:1 that spoke of the coming of the Messiah. In this act of cleansing the temple, Jesus publicly asserts His authority as the Son of God, determined to restore the purity and holiness of God's sacred dwelling place.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "By this expression thus publicly used, our Lord openly announces His Messiahship."
2. "Nathanael had named Him ‘the Son of God’...these words...could leave no doubt on the minds of the Jews as to their import."
3. "Not yet σπήλαιον λῃστῶν, as at the end of His ministry."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "my Father's house—How close the resemblance of these remarkable words to the same consciousness of intrinsic relation to the temple—as the seat of His Father's most august worship, and so the symbol of all that is due to Him on earth—dictating both speeches."
2. "Only, when but a youth, with no authority, He was simply 'a SON IN His own house'; now He was 'a SON OVER His own house', the proper Representative, and in flesh 'the Heir,' of his Father's rights."
3. "There was nothing wrong in the merchandise; but to bring it, for their own and others' convenience, into that most sacred place, was a high-handed profanation which the eye of Jesus could not endure."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The people had made God's house their own."
2. "Merchandise [εμποριου]. Only here in the New Testament."
3. "The Synoptists say a den of robbers."

### John Calvin:
1. "My Father’s house. Christ declares himself to be the Son of God, in order to show that he has a right and authority to cleanse the Temple."
2. "Why, then, does he drive the buyers and sellers out of the Temple? It is that he may bring back to its original purity the worship of God, which had been corrupted by the wickedness of men, and in this way may restore and maintain the holiness of the Temple."
3. "We ought always, therefore, to keep before our eyes the majesty of God, which dwells in the Church, that it may not be defiled by any pollutions; and the only way in which its holiness can remain unimpaired is, that nothing shall be admitted into it that is at variance with the word of God."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The declaration of Sonship is combined with an act which no true Israelite could fail to approve."
2. "Those who, yielding to the impulse of right, and listening to the voice of conscience, accepted the act, would be led to ponder the words; in them would be fulfilled the promise, ‘To him that hath shall more be given.’"
3. "John does not speak of the cleansing of the temple as miraculous, but the Saviour’s words themselves mark it as a ‘sign;’ and it is only by thinking of a divine awe attending the words that we can explain the immediate submission of the traffickers."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Probably the doves were in baskets or cages and so had to be taken out by the traders."
2. "They had made it a market-house, old word from εμπορος, merchant, one who goes on a journey for traffic, a drummer."
3. "Note the clear-cut Messianic claim here (My Father as in Luke 2:49)."

### John Gill:
1. "Take these things hence; not only the doves, but the pens, coups, or cages, in which they were, and both together." 
2. "Make not my Father's house an house of merchandise."
3. "This instance of Christ now coming into the temple as a public minister, and which was the first time of his entrance into it, after he had taken this character, was a further accomplishment of Malachi 3:1."
