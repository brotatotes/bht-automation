# John 2:24 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Let us be cautious in entrusting ourselves wholly to others, just as Jesus did not trust these disciples who believed in Him because of His miracles. We should strive to find a balance between being overly suspicious and easily giving ourselves to those who may not have our best interests at heart.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "did not commit—'entrust'"
2. "let Himself down familiarly to them"
3. "as to His genuine disciples."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Jesus did not put 'trust' or 'reliance' in them."
2. "He acted cautiously and prudently."
3. "He knew the 'inconstancy' and 'fickleness' of the multitude."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "He had no faith in their faith" (Godet).
2. "John describes the Lord's knowledge by two words which it is important to distinguish."
3. "Ginwskein, as here, implies acquired knowledge; knowledge which is the result of discernment and which may be enlarged."

### John Calvin:
1. "Christ did not reckon them to be genuine disciples, but despised them as volatile and unsteady."
2. "Nothing is more dangerous than hypocrisy...for this reason among others, that it is an exceedingly common fault."
3. "Let us remember, therefore, that none are the true disciples of Christ but those whom He approves, because in such a matter He alone is competent to decide and to judge."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "But Jesus himself kept on refusing (negative imperfect) to trust himself to them."
2. "Simon Magus 'believed' (επιστευσεν) and was baptized, but was unsaved. He merely believed that he wanted what Philip had."
3. "Causal use of δια and the accusative case of the articular infinitive το γινωσκειν (because of the knowing) with the object of the infinitive (παντας, all men) and the accusative of general reference (αυτον, as to himself)."

### John Gill:
1. "He did not commit the whole of the Gospel to them."
2. "He did not trust himself with these persons, who believed in him, on the basis of his miracles."
3. "He knew all men: good and bad...He knows their persons, their sins, their weaknesses and their wants."

### John Wesley:
1. "Let us learn hence not rashly to put ourselves into the power of others."
2. "Let us study a wise and happy medium between universal suspiciousness and that easiness which would make us the property of every pretender to kindness and respect."
