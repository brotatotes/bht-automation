# John 2:25 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
He needed no proofs, testimonies, or evidence to know the true character of men, for he had a deep understanding of their thoughts and motivations. He possessed a supernatural knowledge of mankind, which is a mark of his divine nature. He was able to see beyond the outward actions and appearances and discern the secrets of the heart. This ability to perceive the true nature of humanity reveals his suitability as a Savior and a Judge.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "It is impossible for language more clearly to assert of Christ what in Jeremiah 17:9; Jeremiah 17:10, and elsewhere, is denied of all mere creatures."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Should testify of man - Should give him the character of any man."
2. "There can be no higher evidence than this that he was omniscient, and was therefore divine."
3. "As he knows 'all,' he is acquainted with the false pretentions and professions of hypocrites. None can deceive him."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "He himself knew." The imperfect expresses continuance: He was all along cognizant as the successive cases presented themselves.
2. "No such characteristic as this was attributed to the gods of Paganism."
3. "'He knew all men, and needed not that any should testify of man, for He knew what was in man.'"

### John Calvin:
1. "Christ perceived every thing in men that is concealed from our view, so that he could on his own authority make a distinction among men."
2. "Christ, therefore, who knows the hearts, had no need of any one to inform him what sort of men they were."
3. "That we may not always be deceived by hypocrites, and that the Church may not be too much exposed to their wicked impostures, it belongs to Christ to impart to us the Spirit of discretion."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "And because he did not have need... concerning mankind as in the next clause also." 
2. "For he himself kept on knowing... as he did from the start." 
3. "This supernatural knowledge of man is a mark of deity."

### John Gill:
1. "He needed no proofs to be made, or testimonies bore, or evidence given of men's characters and actions; he was of quick understanding, and could distinguish at once between a wicked man and a good man."
2. "For he knew what was in man; which none but the spirit of a man can know; his inward thoughts, the secrets of the heart."
3. "He knows the secret springs of all actions, good and bad; all which prove his true and proper deity, and show him to be a suitable Saviour of sinners, and qualify him to be the Judge of the whole earth."

### John Wesley:
1. "To whom all things are naked, knew what was in man - Namely, a desperately deceitful heart."
