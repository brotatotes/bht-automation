### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Dear disciple! thy dead Lord is to thee 'the Lord' still."
2. "Thy dead Lord is to thee 'the Lord' still."

### John Calvin:
1. "The Evangelists are the more careful to prove [the resurrection of Christ], as John here collects many proofs, in order to assure us that Christ is risen from the dead."
2. "There certainly was nothing more of earthly grandeur in the disciples than in the women who followed Christ; but as Christ was pleased to reckon them the principal witnesses of his resurrection, on this single ground their testimony is entitled to the greatest deference, and is not liable to any objection."
3. "On the first day of the week; or, literally, on the first day of the Sabbaths. The Evangelists do not relate when or how Christ rose; for it was enough for them to explain at what time, and to what persons, his resurrection was made known."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Few parts of the Fourth Gospel illustrate better than these words the principle of selection upon which it is composed."
2. "It is obvious that here, as elsewhere, we have to deal not so much with events of full historical detail as with events selected on account of their bearing upon the idea which the Evangelist wishes to illustrate."
3. "The stone which had been fitted into the door of the sepulchre had been taken away."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Both Mark and Luke have this very idiom of the cardinal τη μια, instead of the usual ordinal τη πρωτη, an idiom common in the papyri and in the modern Greek."
2. "Cometh Mary Magdalene. Vivid historical present."
3. "Taken away. Perfect passive participle of αιρω, predicate accusative in apposition with τον λιθον."

### John Gill:
1. "On the first day of the week rose from the dead."
2. "Mary's coming so early to the grave shows her great love and affection to Christ, her zeal, courage, and diligence."
3. "The greatest sinners, when converted, are most eminent for grace, particularly faith, love, and humility; and are most diligent in the discharge of duty."
