# John 20:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Peter's faith led him outside of the city, where the sepulchre was, in search of answers and confirmation. The other disciple, John, accompanied him on this journey. By going to the sepulchre, Peter and John demonstrated their dedication and desire to witness the truth for themselves. Their actions exemplify the importance of actively seeking and investigating the evidence of Christ's resurrection.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Luke 24:12 speaks only of Peter's going."
2. "Meyer directs attention to the interchange of aorists and graphic imperfects in this and the following verse."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "These particulars have a singular air of artless truth about them."
2. "Love haply supplying swifter wings."
3. "The bolder Peter, coming up, goes in at once, and is rewarded with bright evidence of what had happened."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The tense is the imperfect; they were coming." 
2. "Rev., they went toward."

### John Calvin:
1. "Some seed of faith, therefore, remained in their hearts, but quenched for a time, so that they were not aware of having what they had."
2. "Thus the Spirit of God often works in the elect in a secret manner."
3. "When Scripture speaks of the feeble beginnings of faith, it says that Christ is born in us, and that we, on the other hand, are born in him."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The word rendered 'went forth' is so often used in this Gospel in regard to the most solemn events in the life of Jesus, as implying a Divine mission, the accomplishment of a Divine purpose..." 
2. "It is possible also that there is design in the manner in which the names of the two apostles are introduced: not 'Peter and the other disciple went forth,' but 'Peter went forth, and the other disciple.'"
3. "The other examples of this construction in the Fourth Gospel tend to show that here John intends to set forth Peter as the main person in the narrative."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They were going."
2. "The two started instantly."

### John Gill:
1. "Peter therefore went forth..."
2. "and that other disciple; John, the Evangelist and Apostle; the rest of the disciples staying at home and continuing together..."
3. "and came to the sepulchre..."

### John Wesley:
1. Peter went out — Of the city.
