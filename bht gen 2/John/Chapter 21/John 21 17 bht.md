# John 21:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Peter, being questioned by Jesus for the third time, feels the weight of doubt and disbelief in Jesus' inquiry. The repetition of the question causes Peter to reflect on his previous denials, stirring up deep remorse within him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "ἐλυπήθη not merely on account of the repetition of the question, but because of τὸ τρίτον , the number of his own denials of Christ."
2. "πάντα οἶδας."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The third time reveals it all, bringing up such a rush of dreadful recollections before his view, of his 'thrice denying that he knew Him,' that he feels it to the quick."
2. "It was fitting that he should it was meant that he should."
3. "Now, Simon, the last speck of the cloud which overhung thee since that night of nights is dispelled: Henceforth thou art to Me and to My work as if no such scene had ever happened."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Thus he tenderly admonished him of his fault and reminded him of his sin, while he solemnly charged him to be faithful and vigilant in the discharge of the duties of the pastoral office."
2. "He made him so prominent as to show the solicitude of the Saviour that, henceforward, he might not be left to dishonor his high calling."
3. "The charge to Peter arose, manifestly, from his prominent and melancholy act in denying him, and was the kind and tender means used by a faithful Saviour to keep him from similar acts in the future dangers and trials of life."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Jesus adopts Peter's word." 
2. "Canon Westcott, however, ascribes Peter's use of filew to his humility."
3. "This seems to me to be less natural, and to be refining too much."

### John Calvin:
1. "Peter undoubtedly did not perceive the object which Christ had in view, in putting the same question so frequently; and therefore he thinks that he is indirectly accused, as if he had not answered with sincerity."
2. "Those who are to undertake the charge of governing the Church are also taught, in his person, not to examine themselves slightly, but to make a thorough scrutiny what zeal they possess, that they may not shrink or faint in the middle of their course."
3. "We are likewise taught, that we ought patiently and mildly to submit, if at any time the Lord subjects us to a severe trial; because he has good reasons for doing so, though they are generally unknown to us."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "In this third question...one word ('lovest') is changed...that constitutes to the apostle the painful force of the third question."
2. "Peter’s grief is at once intelligible...the third time his own statement, twice made, had been taken up, and he had been asked to consider well whether it was really true, whether he might not be again misjudging himself."
3. "The victory of grace is complete...he receives his final charge...Feed my sheep...There is a climax...to nourish it on pastures ever fresh, and with waters ever living, is at once his most difficult and his noblest task."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Peter was cut to the heart because Jesus challenges this very verb, and no doubt the third question vividly reminds him of the three denials in the early morning by the fire."
2. "He repeats his love for Jesus with the plea: 'Thou knowest all things.'"
3. "Many MSS. both here and in verse John 21:16 read προβατα (sheep) instead of προβατια (little sheep or lambs)."

### John Gill:
1. "Simon, son of Jonas, lovest thou me?...is thy love really so hearty and sincere as thou savest? may it be depended upon?" 
2. "Peter was grieved...because his love, which he knew was unfeigned, should seem to be suspected."
3. "It may be observed from the repetition of this phrase following upon Peter's declaration of his love to Christ, that such only are proper persons to feed the lambs and sheep of Christ, who truly and sincerely love him."

### John Wesley:
1. Because he said the third time — As if he did not believe him.
