In the familiar little boat, the disciples made their way to Jesus, opting not to take any unnecessary risks like Peter. This boat symbolizes the arduous journey of Gospel ministers who persevere through the challenges of their ministry. Despite the toil and labor they face, they remain steadfast, bringing souls with them to the Lord with immense satisfaction.

### Henry Alford:

### Albert Barnes:
1. Two hundred cubits - About 350 feet, or a little more than 20 rods.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "A little ship [τω πλοιαριω] . The noun is diminutive. Rev., the little boat."
2. "Two hundred cubits. A little over a hundred yards."
3. "With fishes [των ιχθυων] . Or, the net of the fishes. So Wyc, Rev., full of fishes."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Peter takes the lead, impetuously dashing into the water..."
2. "His fellow-disciples reach land more slowly."
3. "The landing is reserved for him who had displayed greatest earnestness and activity."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "In the little boat (τω πλοιαριω). Locative case of πλοιαριον (diminutive) for the larger boat (πλοιον, verses John 21:3; John 21:6) could come no closer to shore."
2. "About two hundred cubits off (ως απο πηχων διακοσιων). For πηχυς, cubit, see Matthew 6:27 and for ως απο see John 11:18."
3. "Dragging (συροντες). Present active participle of συρω for which see Acts 8:3."

### John Gill:
1. "The same that they were fishing in, in which they came to Christ as soon as they could, not choosing to expose themselves, as Peter did." 
2. "an emblem of laborious Gospel ministers, who being once embarked in the work of the ministry, continue in it to the end, notwithstanding all toil, labour, and difficulties that attend them."
3. "will at last bring the souls with them they have been made useful to, with great satisfaction and joy, to their dear Lord and master."
