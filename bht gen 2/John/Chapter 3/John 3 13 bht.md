# John 3:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus affirms that only He, the Son of Man who came down from heaven, has ascended to heaven. This communicates the exclusive authority and unique nature of Jesus as the Word made Flesh. He is both in heaven and on earth simultaneously, possessing perfect knowledge of heavenly things. Through His incarnation, Jesus makes known the truths of God and offers a way for humanity to be raised up to heavenly realms. By His redemptive work, Jesus accomplishes the divine plan of salvation, granting access to the heavenly realities that were previously inaccessible to humanity.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "No man, therefore, is qualified to speak of heavenly things, John 3:12."
2. "But he that came down ... - The Lord Jesus."
3. "Since Jesus was 'in' heaven - as his proper abode was there - he was fitted to speak of heavenly things, and to declare the will of God to man."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Jesus says that no one has been in heaven except the Son of man who came down out of heaven; because no man could be in heaven without having ascended thither."
2. "Hath ascended. Equivalent to hath been in."
3. "Note the simple connective particle, with nothing to indicate the logical sequence of the thought."

### John Calvin:
1. "For to ascend to heaven means here, 'to have a pure knowledge of the mysteries of God, and the light of spiritual understanding.'"
2. "Christ alone, who is heavenly, ascends to heaven, but that the entrance is closed against all others."
3. "Christ, therefore, who is in heaven, hath clothed himself with our flesh, that, by stretching out his brotherly hand to us, he may raise us to heaven along with him."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "How will ye believe if I tell you the heavenly things? And it is from me alone that ye can learn them." 
2. "He came forth from the Father, and came into the world, that He might declare the Father and speak unto the world what He had heard from Him."
3. "The special thought is not the having gone up into heaven, but the having been in heaven. This was the qualification for revealing the truths which are here spoken of as heavenly things."

### John Gill:
1. "No man had, or could go up to heaven, to bring from thence the knowledge of divine and heavenly things."
2. "[Jesus] came from thence to do the will of God by preaching the Gospel, working miracles, obeying the law, and suffering death in the room of his people, and thereby obtaining eternal redemption for them."
3. "Not that he was in heaven in his human nature, and as he was the son of man; but in his divine nature, as he was the Son of God."

### John Wesley:
1. "For no one...Hath gone up to heaven, but he that came down from heaven." 
2. "Who is in heaven...Therefore he is omnipresent; else he could not be in heaven and on earth at once."
