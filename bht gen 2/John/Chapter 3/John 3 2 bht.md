# John 3:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Nicodemus, a member of the Sanhedrin, approached Jesus under the cover of darkness, likely to avoid attracting attention or scrutiny. Despite his hesitancy, Nicodemus showed genuine interest in Jesus and recognized Him as a teacher sent from God. This acknowledgment was significant, as it revealed Nicodemus' reverence for Jesus and his understanding that Jesus' authority came directly from God.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "the spiritual purity and self-sacrifice which He inculcated"
2. "there is involved in this word a recognition by Nicodemus of the Messianic mission of Jesus"
3. "The Messiah was never designated as a mere teacher, till the days of modern Socinianism"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "John 2:23; John 2:24, yet inwardly craving further satisfaction, Nicodemus comes to Jesus in quest of it, but comes 'by night'."
2. "He avows his conviction that He was come from God—an expression never applied to a merely human messenger, and probably meaning more here—but only as 'a teacher.'"
3. "In His miracles he sees a proof merely that 'God is with Him.' Thus, while unable to repress his convictions, he is afraid of committing himself too far."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The most favorable opportunity for teaching such men the nature of personal religion is when they are alone."
2. "Scarcely any man, of any rank, will refuse to converse on this subject when addressed respectfully and tenderly in private."
3. "When we read the words of Jesus in the Bible, it should be with a belief that he came from God, and was therefore qualified and authorized to teach us the way of life."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "By night, 'when Jewish superstition would keep men at home.'"
2. "We may be sure that a member of the sect that carefully scrutinized the Baptist's credentials would not lightly address Jesus by this title of honor, or acknowledge Him as a teacher."
3. "Thou art come from God as a teacher. It is from God that thou hast come."

### John Calvin:
- "From the circumstance of his coming by night we infer that his timidity was excessive; for his eyes were dazzled, as it were, by the splendor of his own greatness and reputation."
- "And yet there appears in him some seed of piety; for hearing that a Prophet of God had appeared, he does not despise or spurn the doctrine which has been brought from heaven, and is moved by some desire to obtain it, — a desire which sprung from nothing else than fear and reverence for God."
- "It is evident, he says, that Christ has been sent by God, because God displays his power in him so illustriously, that it cannot be denied that God is with him."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "We may be sure that a member of the sect that carefully scrutinised the Baptist’s credentials would not lightly address Jesus by this title of honour, or acknowledge him as Teacher."
2. "The words ‘Thou art come from God’ will appear even more significant, if we keep in mind that the most familiar designation of the Messiah was ‘the coming One,’ He that should come."
3. "The faith indeed which rested on these alone was imperfect, but it was faith; more could be gained; the faith could be educated, raised higher, and made more complete."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He wished to avoid comment by other members of the Sanhedrin and others."
2. "Jesus was already in the public eye and the interest of Nicodemus was real and yet he wished to be cautious."
3. "Nicodemus dares to feel his way."

### John Gill:
1. "It was necessary, that if he would have any discourse with him, that he should take this method; and if it was the same night, in which he had seen his miracles in the day, as is probable, he took the first opportunity he could, and which shows great readiness and respect."
2. "Salutations among the Jews, were forbidden in the night; but here was no such danger; nor was this salutation made in the street, and in the dark, which the canon seems to respect."
3. "Nicodemus acknowledges Jesus as such; and as one that did not come, or was sent by men, as their doctors were; nor did he come of himself, as false teachers did; but he came from God, and had his mission and commission from him."

### John Wesley:
1. "Through desire; but by night - Through shame."
2. "We know - Even we rulers and Pharisees."
