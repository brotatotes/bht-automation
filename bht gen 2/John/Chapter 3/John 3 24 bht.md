## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "There is much difficulty, which probably never will be cleared up, about the date of the imprisonment of John, and its reference to the course of our Lord’s ministry."
2. "Our Evangelist seems here to refer to such records, and to insert this remark, that it might not be imagined, as it would be from them, that our Lord’s public ministry began with the imprisonment of the Baptist."
3. "Between Matthew 4:11-12, there seems to be a wide hiatus, in which the first chapters of this Gospel should be inserted."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "John not yet cast into prison—Hence it is plain that our
Lord's ministry did not commence with the imprisonment of
John."
2. "But for this, we should have drawn that inference from
:- and Mark's (Mark 1:14) express statement."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The design of John was to call men to repentance, and to prepare them for the Messiah."
2. "John still toiled in his work not the 'less' because the Messiah had come."
3. "So ministers should not labor less when Christ appears by his Spirit, and takes the work into his own hands, and turns many to himself."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Prison [την φυλακην] . See on Acts 5:18, Acts 5:21.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The Evangelist vindicates the accuracy of his narrative, and corrects a mistake apparently prevailing in the Church when he wrote."
2. "The false inference is here corrected."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "For John had not yet been cast into prison." 
2. "Periphrastic past perfect indicative of βαλλω explaining (γαρ) why John was still baptizing, the reason for the imprisonment having been given by Luke (Luke 3:19)."

### John Gill:
1. "John was not yet cast into prison. [...] this circumstance shows, that these things were done before that journey of Christ into Galilee."
