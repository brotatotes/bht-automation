# John 3:34 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Christ, who was sent to be the Savior of the world, has the spirit of God in an immeasurable way. Unlike the prophets and ordinary ministers, he has every gift of the Spirit and the fullness of grace. This is why when he speaks, he speaks the words of God with the utmost perfection.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "They have the Spirit in a limited degree; but God giveth not [to Him] the Spirit by measure." 
2. "It means the entire fulness of divine life and divine power."
3. "The present tense 'giveth,' very aptly points out the permanent communication of the Spirit by the Father to the Son."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Whom God hath sent - The Messiah."
2. "For God giveth not the Spirit - The Spirit of God."
3. "The Messiah was continually filled with the Spirit of God."

### John Calvin:
1. "For he whom God hath sent speaketh the words of God."
2. "God giveth not the Spirit by measure."
3. "Christ differs from us in this respect, that the Father has poured out upon him an unlimited abundance of his Spirit."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The last verse rests on the thought that the words of Jesus are the words of God."
2. "He speaketh not ‘words of God’ only, but ‘the words of God,’ giving all the revelation that God gives."
3. "He gives the Spirit not partially, but completely, for the purpose of enabling him who is sent to speak words of God."

### John Gill:
1. "Still meaning Christ, who was sent in human nature, in the likeness of sinful flesh, in the fulness of time; to be the Savior of the world, of that which was lost, of the chief of sinners; and to preach the glad tidings of the Gospel."
2. "For God giveth not the Spirit by measure [unto him], as he did to the prophets of the Old Testament, and to the apostles of the New; and to the ordinary ministers of the word, who have gifts differing one from another."
3. "But the Lord Jesus has every, gift of the Spirit, and the fulness of grace in him: he is anointed with the oil of gladness, with the Holy Ghost above his fellows; and has an immeasurable unction of the holy one."

### John Wesley:
1. "God giveth not him the Spirit by measure — As he did to the prophets, but immeasurably."
2. "Hence he speaketh the words of God in the most perfect manner."
