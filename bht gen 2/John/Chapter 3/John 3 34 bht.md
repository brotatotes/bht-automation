# John 3:34 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
For the one sent by God speaks God's words, in the fullness of time, to save the lost and to proclaim the Gospel. His every word is as true as the oracles of God, for he is anointed with the Holy Ghost above all others. The Spirit is given to him without measure, surpassing even the prophets in his divine communication. In him, the words of God are spoken in the most perfect manner.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The sharpest conceivable line of distinction is drawn between Christ and all human-inspired teachers."
2. "It means the entire fulness of divine life and divine power."
3. "The present tense 'giveth,' very aptly points out the permanent communication of the Spirit by the Father to the Son, so that a constant flow and reflow of living power is to be understood."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "For God giveth not the Spirit - The Spirit of God. Though Jesus was God as well as man, yet, as Mediator, God anointed him, or endowed him with the influences of his Spirit, so as to be completely qualified for his great work."
2. "By measure - Not in a small degree, but fully, completely. The prophets were inspired on particular occasions to deliver special messages. The Messiah was continually filled with the Spirit of God."
3. "The Spirit dwelt in him, not as a vessel, but as in a fountain, as in a bottomless ocean."

### John Calvin:
1. "For he whom God hath sent speaketh the words of God...we have actually to do with God, when we receive the doctrine of Christ; because Christ proceeded from none else than from the Heavenly Father."
2. "God giveth not the Spirit by measure...it is proper that the Spirit should dwell without measure in him, that we may all draw out of his fullness."
3. "Christ, having been appointed by the Father to be the administrator, he distributes to every one as he chooses, and as he finds to be necessary."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The last verse rests on the thought that the words of Jesus are the words of God."
2. "He speaketh not 'words of God' only, but 'the words of God,' giving all the revelation that God gives."
3. "He gives the Spirit not partially, but completely, for the purpose of enabling him who is sent to speak words of God."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "God sent his Son and he speaks God's words." 
2. "God has put no limit to the Spirit's relation to the Son."
3. "God has given the Holy Spirit in his fulness to Christ and to no one else in that sense."

### John Gill:
1. "For he whom God hath sent,.... Still meaning Christ, who was sent in human nature, in the likeness of sinful flesh, in the fulness of time; to be the Saviour of the world, of that which was lost, of the chief of sinners; and to preach the glad tidings of the Gospel"
2. "whatever he spoke were as true as the oracles of God, and were such."
3. "But the Lord Jesus has every gift of the Spirit, and the fulness of grace in him: he is anointed with the oil of gladness, with the Holy Ghost above his fellows; and has an immeasurable unction of the holy one."

### John Wesley:
1. "God giveth not him the Spirit by measure" - As he did to the prophets, but immeasurably.
2. "He speaketh the words of God in the most perfect manner."
