# John 4:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this passage, the woman at the well expresses her skepticism and disbelief towards Jesus. She mocks him, pointing out that he has nothing to draw water with and that the well is deep. Her words are filled with disdain and skepticism, reflecting her inability to understand the deeper spiritual meaning behind Jesus' words. Her initial response highlights the challenge of breaking through her skepticism and leading her to a true understanding of the living water that Jesus offers.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "It is this national antipathy that gives point to the parable of the good Samaritan."
2. "The thankfulness of the Samaritan leper."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Hast nothing to draw with - It seems that there were no means of drawing water affixed to the well, as with us."
2. "The woman, seeing that Jesus had no means of drawing water, and not yet understanding his design, naturally inquired whence he could obtain the water."
3. "The well is deep - If the same one that is there now, it was about 100 feet deep."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The noun [αντλημα] means what is drawn, the act of drawing, and the thing to draw with."
2. "The bucket, of skin, with three cross sticks at the mouth to keep it open, and let down by a goat's-hair rope."
3. "Well [φρεαρ]. See on ver. 6. It may have been fed by living springs [πηγαι]."

### John Calvin:
1. "Sir, thou hast nothing to draw with."
2. "Accordingly, this woman at first not only disdains Christ but even mocks at him."
3. "She understands quite well that Christ is speaking figuratively, but she throws out a jibe by a different figure, intending to say, that he promises more than he can accomplish."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "In the answer of Jesus there was much to cause surprise, especially in the emphatic reference to Himself; but there was nothing in the actual terms used that compelled the hearer to seek for a figurative meaning."
2. "'Living water' was a phrase in ordinary use in speaking of the fresh bubbling spring or the flowing brook."
3. "Wherever running water is spoken of in the ceremonial law, the same expression is used."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "'Sir' (Κυριε). So it has to mean here in the mouth of the Samaritan woman, not Lord." 
2. "Thou hast nothing to draw with and the well is deep (ουτε αντλημα εχεις κα το φρεαρ εστιν βαθυ)." 
3. "That living water (το υδωρ το ζων). 'The water the living,' with the article referring to the language of Jesus in verse John 4:10. She is still thinking only of literal water."

### John Gill:
1. "Thou hast nothing to draw with; no pail, or bucket, or rope, to let it down with..."
2. "And the well is deep...from whence then hast thou that living water?"
3. "She said this in a sneering, scoffing manner."
