# John 4:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
She soon realized that He was more than just an ordinary man, but a prophet who knew the depths of her heart. This realization made her eager to engage in conversation with Him, seeking His guidance and wisdom. Such is the power of encountering the divine in our lives; it opens our eyes and fills us with a deep desire for repentance and transformation.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "In speaking this her conviction, she virtually confesses all the truth."
2. "The idea of her endeavoring to escape from the Lord's rebuke, is quite inconsistent with her recognition of Him as a prophet."
3. "Not σύ (Wordsw.), but προφήτης, is the word of primary emphasis. σύ has the secondary emphasis, by its very expression."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "How slowly does the human heart submit to thorough humiliation!"
2. "The Prince of preachers takes another method: He humors the poor woman, letting her take her own way, allowing her to lead while He follows—but thus only the more effectually gaining His object."
3. "He answers her question, pours light into her mind on the spirituality of all true worship, as of its glorious Object, and so brings her insensibly to the point at which He could disclose to her wondering mind whom she was all the while speaking to."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The word here does not denote one who foretells future events, but one who knew her heart and life, and who must therefore have come from God."
2. "Nothing is more common than for sinners to change the conversation when it begins to bear too hard upon their consciences."
3. "Sinners do not love direct religious conversation, but many are too well-bred to refuse altogether to talk about it."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "I perceive [θεωρω] . See on 1 18. Not immediate perception, but rather, I perceive as I observe thee longer and more carefully."
2. "A prophet. See on Luke 7:26. The order is a prophet art thou; the emphasis being on prophet."

### John Calvin:
1. "The fruit of the reproof now becomes evident; for not only does the woman modestly acknowledge her fault, but, being ready and prepared to listen to the doctrine of Christ, which she had formerly disdained, she now desires and requests it of her own accord."
2. "Repentance, therefore, is the commencement of true docility, as I have already said, and opens the gate for entering into the school of Christ."
3. "Again, the woman teaches us by her example, that when we meet with any teacher, we ought to avail ourselves of this opportunity, that we may not be ungrateful to God, who never sends Prophets to us without, as it were, stretching out the hand to invite us to himself."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Sir, I perceive that thou art a prophet."
2. "Nothing can be more misleading than the idea that she is seeking to turn the conversation from an unwelcome subject, or to lead it to other topics than herself."
3. "Her answer is rather a fresh illustration of her inquiring and earnest character, notwithstanding all the sinfulness of her life."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "I am beginning to perceive" from what you say, your knowledge of my private life. 
2. "That a prophet art thou." She felt that this was the explanation of his knowledge of her life.

### John Gill:
1. "I perceive that thou art a prophet; such an one as Samuel was, who could tell Saul what was in his heart..." 
2. "...such a prophet, that had such a spirit of discerning, this woman took Christ to be; and who indeed is greater than a prophet, and is the omniscient God; who knows all men's hearts, thoughts, words and actions..." 
3. "Now in order either to shift off the discourse from this subject, which touched her to the quick; or else being truly sensible of her sin, and willing to reform, and for the future to worship God in the place and manner he had directed, she addressed Christ in the following words."

### John Wesley:
1. Sir, I perceive — So soon was her heart touched.
