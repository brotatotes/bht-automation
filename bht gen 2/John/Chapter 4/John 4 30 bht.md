### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Went out is the aorist tense, denoting the coming forth from the city as a single act at a point of time."
2. "Came is the imperfect, denoting action in progress. The observance of the distinction makes the narrative more graphic."
3. "The imperfect also is required by the following words: 'In the mean while' (while the woman was still absent and the Samaritans were coming toward Him) 'the disciples were praying' Him to eat."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "No slight evidence of the preparedness of Samaria for the gospel."
2. "The immediate success of the woman's message."
3. "To make plain the words of Jesus in a later verse."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They went out...at once and in a rush." 
2. "And were coming to him...graphically picturing the long procession."

### John Gill:
1. "The men", as the Syriac version expresses it; the inhabitants of Sychar left their business, and came out of the city.
2. ...that they might know who he was: for though the woman had been a woman of ill fame, yet such was the account that she gave of Christ, and such power went along with her words.
3. ...and chiefly through the efficacy of divine grace, at least in many of them, they were moved to regard what she said, and to follow her directions and solicitations.
