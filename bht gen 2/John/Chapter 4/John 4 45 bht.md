# John 4:45 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Galilaeans received him willingly, readily, and cheerfully, with much delight and pleasure, showing great esteem and respect for Jesus. They had seen all the things that he did at Jerusalem during the feast of the passover, witnessing the miracles he performed. It was their practice to keep the feast of the passover and go to Jerusalem yearly. The Galilaeans had a strong faith in Jesus, believing in him because of the signs and wonders they had witnessed.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "They received Him, but in accordance with the proverbial saying just recorded; not for any honour in which they themselves held Him, or value which they had for His teaching"
2. "on account of His fame in Jerusalem, the metropolis, which set them the fashion in their estimate of men and things."
3. "καὶ αὐτοὶ γάρ, inserted for those readers who might not be aware of the practice of the Galilæans to frequent the feasts at Jerusalem."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "saving conversion often begins in less than this"
2. "it was their practice to go up to the feast"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Received him kindly, or as a messenger of God."
2. "They had seen his miracles, and believed on him."

### John Calvin:
1. "There is nothing to which men are more prone than forgetfulness of the gifts of God."
2. "Christ performed miracles in the presence of many witnesses, so that the report of them was spread far and wide."
3. "Miracles prepare the way for doctrine, for they cause reverence to be paid to Christ."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The Galileans received him, having seen all things whatsoever he did at Jerusalem at the feast: for they also went unto the feast."
2. "The faith of these Galileans is precisely similar to that of the 'many' spoken of in John 4:23 of that chapter, real, but not of the highest kind."
3. "The ‘feast’ is no doubt the Passover of which we read in chap. 2."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Received him (εδεξαντο αυτον). First aorist middle of δεχομα, 'welcomed him.' Jesus had evidently anticipated a quiet arrival."
2. "Having seen (εωρακοτες). Perfect active participle of οραω. Note θεωρουντες in John 2:23 about this very thing at the feast in Jerusalem. The miracles of Jesus at that first passover made a stir."
3. "For they also went (κα αυτο γαρ ηλθον). The Samaritans did not go and so Jesus was a new figure to them, but the Galileans, as orthodox Jews, did go and so were predisposed in his favour."

### John Gill:
1. "The Galilaeans received him; willingly, readily, and cheerfully, with much delight and pleasure, and with marks of great esteem and respect."
2. "Having seen all the things that he did at Jerusalem, at the feast of the passover; the miracles he wrought there."
3. "They kept the feast of the passover, and went yearly to Jerusalem on that account."
