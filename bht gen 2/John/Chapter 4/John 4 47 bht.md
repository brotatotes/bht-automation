### Albert Barnes:
1. "He went unto him - Though high in office, yet he did not refuse to go personally to Jesus to ask his aid."

3. "One design of Jesus in working this miracle was to show him that [Jesus' physical presence] was not necessary."

### John Calvin:
1. "For he views the power of Christ as inseparably connected with his bodily presence..."
2. "He saw that his doctrine had no great authority, and was not only neglected but altogether despised..."
3. "They had no religion, no knowledge of God, no practice of godliness, except what consisted in miracles."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The faith of this father rested on the miracles of which he had heard."
2. "Would Jesus but come down from Cana to Capernaum, his son also might be healed."
3. "Jesus must always reprove the spirit which requires 'signs and wonders' before yielding faith; and He does it now."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The news spread rapidly about Jesus."
2. "He had heard the people talk about the miracles in Jerusalem."
3. "Reason (γαρ) for the urgency."

### John Gill:
1. "For the fame of Christ, for his doctrine and miracles, was spread every where; so that it was known, and talked of, in most places, where he was, and what course he was steering"
2. "The nobleman believed that Christ had power to do it, by what he had heard concerning him, but thought his corporeal presence was absolutely necessary to it"
3. "He was very near it; there was no likelihood of his recovery; the physicians had given him over; and when he left him, he seemed to be near his death, and must die for any human help that could be obtained, or natural means that could be used."
