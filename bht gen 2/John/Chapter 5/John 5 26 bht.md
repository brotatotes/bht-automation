# John 5:26 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Son possesses life in himself in the same way as the Father, for he has been given by the Father the authority to have life in himself. This life is not dependent on any external source but is inherent and absolute, in accordance with the Son's eternal generation.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "All that Christ says of His essential relation to the Father is intended to explain and exalt His mediatorial functions."
2. "The one [essential relation to the Father] seems in our Lord's own mind and language mainly the starting-point of the other [mediatorial functions]."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "God is the source of all life." 
2. "He has 'in himself' infinite sources of happiness, and no other being, no change in his universe can destroy that happiness."
3. "He had but to speak, and it was done."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The correspondence is that of fact, not of degree."
2. "Hath he given."
3. "The aorist tense pointing back to the eternal past."

### John Calvin:
1. "He shows whence his voice derives such efficacy; namely, that he is the fountain of life."
2. "God is said to have life in himself, not only because he alone lives by his own inherent power, but because, containing in himself the fullness of life, he communicates life to all things."
3. "We have thus an open fountain placed before us, from which we may draw."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "For even as the Father hath life in himself; so gave he to the Son also to have life in himself." 
2. "The Father who is the primal fountain of life gave to the Son to have life in Himself." 
3. "'To have life in Himself' is the loftiest expression that can be used: the unchangeable possession of life exactly similar and parallel to that of the Father, such possession as enables Him to be the Giver of life to others, belongs to the Son."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The Living God possesses life wholly in himself and so he has bestowed this power of life to the Son as already stated in the Prologue of the Logos (John 1:3)."
2. "For 'gave' (εδωκεν, timeless aorist active indicative) see also John 3:35; John 17:2; John 17:24."
3. "The particles 'as' (ωσπερ) and 'so' (ουτως) mark here the fact, not the degree (Westcott)."

### John Gill:
1. "The Son has life in himself, essentially, originally, and inderivatively as the Father has, being equally the living God, the fountain of life, and donor of it, as he."
2. "He has put into his hands, and secured it in him for them, to give it to as many as he has given him: and he does give it to all his sheep, so that not one of them shall perish."
3. "All that hear Christ's voice spiritually shall live eternally; for these words are a reason of the former, and confirm the truth of them, as well as show the equality of the Son with the Father."

### John Wesley:
1. "He hath given to the Son — By eternal generation, to have life in himself." 
2. "Absolute, independent."
