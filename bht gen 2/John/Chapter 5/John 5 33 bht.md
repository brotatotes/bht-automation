# John 5:33 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The testimony of John the Baptist is crucial in recognizing Jesus as the Christ. The respect shown to John by the Sanhedrin indicates his credibility as a faithful witness. His witness not only affirms the truth of Jesus' person, office, and divine sonship but also testifies to his role as the Mediator who takes away the sins of humanity through his sacrificial blood. Ultimately, John's testimony serves the purpose of pointing to Jesus as the long-awaited Messiah.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "another testifies of Me 'not John only, although he, when sent to, did certainly testify to the truth; for'..."
2. "τῇ ἀληθείᾳ, not merely 'modestly said;' but necessarily said."
3. "ἐμοί would have been asserting what the next verse denies."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Ye sent unto John—(See John 1:19, &c.)."
2. "Receive not testimony . . . from man—that is, depend not on human testimony."
3. "But . . . that ye might be saved—"I refer to him merely to aid your salvation."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "This testimony of John ought to have satisfied them."
2. "John was an eminent man; many of the Pharisees believed on him; he was candid, unambitious, sincere, and his evidence was impartial."
3. "On this Jesus might have rested the proof that he was the Messiah, but he was willing, also, to adduce evidence of a higher order."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The perfect tense, with allusion to something abiding in its results."
2. "Note the expressed ye, emphatically marking the contrast between the human testimony which the Jews demanded, and the divine testimony on which Jesus relies."

### John Calvin:
1. "Before producing the testimony of God, he presses them with the answer of John, from which they could not honorably withhold their belief."
2. "They send to him as a Prophet of God, and thus they pretend that his word will be regarded by them as an oracle."
3. "And, therefore, we see that this circumstance is highly appropriate to the matter in hand, namely, that they sent to John, and — as if their motive had been a desire to learn — inquired at him who was the Messiah, and yet paid no attention to his reply."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Had I not this all-sufficient witness, were it possible for me to appeal to any human witness, I might rest on your own act."
2. "Your mission and his answer are unalterable and abiding facts, which press upon you still and cannot be set aside."
3. "Jesus does not say 'hath borne witness to me,' perhaps because that to which John bore witness was only a revelation from God, a declaration of the truth which he had received from God."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The witness of the Baptist has to be recognized as trustworthy by the Sanhedrin."
2. "The permanent and abiding value of John's testimony to Christ."
3. "This was the purpose of the Baptist's mission."

### John Gill:
1. "Now had they not looked upon him, from what they knew of him, or from the character they had of him, as a faithful witness, they would never have shown him so much respect."
2. "He bore witness unto the truth; to Christ, who is the truth itself; and to the truth of his person, and office; to his dignity, and eternity, as being before him, though coming after him; and to his divine sonship, the thing now in debate, declaring, that he was the Son of God."
3. "He bore witness to his office, as Mediator, pointing to him as the Lamb of God, who, by his blood, and sacrifice, takes away the sins of men."

### John Wesley:
1. He bare testimony — That I am the Christ.
