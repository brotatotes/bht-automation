### Henry Alford:
1. "If ye believed Moses, ye would believe me."
2. "This is an important testimony by the Lord to the subject of the whole Pentateuch; it is περὶ ἐμοῦ."
3. "It is also a testimony to the fact, of Moses having written those books, which were then, and are still, known by his name."

### John Calvin:
1. "We know that it is impossible to offer a greater insult to the servants of God than when their doctrine is despised or reproached."
2. "When Christ says, that Moses wrote concerning him, this needs no long proof with those who acknowledge that Christ is the end and soul of the Law."
3. "Thus, without Christ, the whole ministry of Christ vanishes."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "For if ye believed Moses, ye would believe me: for he wrote concerning me." - Our Lord, no doubt, refers in part to special predictions (such as that of Deuteronomy 18:15; Deuteronomy 18:18); but more especially He refers to the whole revelation contained in the books of Moses, and by parity of reasoning to the whole Old Testament the Scriptures of John 5:39.
2. In all the revelation given through him Moses wrote concerning Jesus. His great purpose was to prepare the way for the true Prophet and Priest and King of Israel.
3. Christ was ‘the end of the law.’ Had, therefore, the Jews ‘believed Moses,’ that is, accepted his witness in its true character, and entered into its spirit, they would have been led by that preparatory prophetic teaching to believe the Christ of whom Moses wrote.

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "This was a home-thrust, proving that they did not really believe Moses."
2. "Deuteronomy 18:18 is quoted by Peter (Acts 3:22) as a prophecy of Christ and also by Stephen in Acts 7:37."
3. "Jesus does here say that Moses wrote concerning him."

### John Gill:
1. "Christ is the end of the law of Moses, and in him is the accomplishment of his writings."
2. "In the books written by Moses, Christ is spoken of, as the seed of the woman, that should bruise the serpent's head; as the seed of Abraham, in whom all nations of the earth should be blessed; as the Shiloh, to whom the gathering of the people should be; and as that prophet, who should be like unto himself, to whom the people of Israel should hearken."
3. "Disbelieving Christ, was disbelieving Moses; who therefore would be an accuser of them, and a witness against them."

### John Wesley:
1. He wrote of me — Every where; in all his writings; particularly Deuteronomy 18:15,18.
