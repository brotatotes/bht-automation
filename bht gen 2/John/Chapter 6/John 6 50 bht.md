# John 6:50 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Eating of this heavenly bread brings life to the soul, nourishing and strengthening it. It is not to be understood in a physical or sacramental sense, but rather as a spiritual act. Those who belong to Christ are given the freedom and warm invitation to partake in this divine feast, ensuring that they will not experience spiritual death or eternal separation from God.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The ‘bread that cometh down out of heaven’ is of such a nature, and has such an object, that one may eat of it and not die."
2. "His studious avoidance of every word of limitation points once more to the unbounded offer of life, the offer to ‘the world.’"
3. "In John 6:50 again physical death may seem to be excluded, but we shall see that John elsewhere regards the believer as freed (in a certain sense) even from this, so entirely has death for him changed its character, so complete is the deliverance granted by his Lord."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "That a man may eat thereof, and not die" (ινα τις εξ αυτου φαγη κα μη αποθανη). Purpose clause with ινα and the second aorist active subjunctive of εσθιω and αποθνησκω.
2. "The wonder and the glory of it all, but quite beyond the insight of this motley crowd."

### John Gill:
1. "This heavenly bread is soul quickening, soul strengthening, and soul satisfying food."
2. "Eating of it is not to be understood corporeally, as these Capernaites took it; nor sacramentally, as if it was confined to the ordinance of the Lord's supper. "
3. "Such as are Christ's beloved, and his friends, 'may' eat; they have liberty, a hearty welcome to eat."

### John Wesley:
1. Not die — Not spiritually; not eternally.
