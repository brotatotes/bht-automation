# John 6:52 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Jews engaged in contentious arguments amongst themselves, disputing and finding fault with the words of Jesus. Some of them understood His spiritual meaning, while others rejected it, finding it morally impossible. It is interesting to note the progression in the disciples' response to Jesus, from questioning Him to eventually abandoning Him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The inference conveyed in φαγεῖν, which first comes from the Jews themselves, is yet a right one."
2. "If He is the Bread, and that Bread is His Flesh, we must eat His Flesh, though not in the sense here meant by them."
3. "They contended against one another, probably some having more insight into the possibility of a spiritual meaning than others."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Jews strove among themselves—arguing the point together."
2. "How can, &c.—that is, Give us His flesh to eat? Absurd."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Strove [εμαχοντο] . The murmuring (ver. 41) now breaks out into open contention among the Jews themselves.

### John Calvin:
2. "He again mentions the Jews, not by way of honor, but to reproach them with their unbelief."
4. "Those persons, therefore, are either led astray through ignorance, or are deficient in candour, who, without taking into account the hardihood and eagerness to quarrel, which alone the Evangelist condemns, direct all their outcry against the wordhow."
7. "Only we ought to preserve such moderation about the secret works of God, as not to desire to know anything more than what he determines by his word."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The Jews take hold of those words which are most susceptible of a merely material sense."
2. "Every word that points to a spiritual meaning they ignore; but in doing so they themselves give evidence of the clearness with which our Lord had now shown that His intention had been to fix the whole thought of His hearers on Himself, and not on His gifts."
3. "The contention of the Jews became violent as they talked of the words of Jesus: the Evangelist’s expression, literally taken, points to ‘fighting’ rather than strife."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They were already murmuring, now they began bitter strife with one another over the last words of Jesus."
2. "There was division of opinion about Jesus in Jerusalem also later."
3. "The mystical appropriation of Christ by the believer they could not comprehend, though some apparently were against this literal interpretation of 'flesh'."

### John Gill:
1. "The Jews therefore strove among themselves,.... Fell to cavilling and disputing one among another." 
2. "Some understanding Christ, and others not; some being for him, and vindicated what he said; and others being against him."
3. "How can this man give us his flesh to eat? which is to be understood, not physically, but as morally impossible and unlawful."

### John Wesley:
1. Observe the degrees: the Jews are tried here; the disciples, John 6:60-66, the apostles, John 6:67.
