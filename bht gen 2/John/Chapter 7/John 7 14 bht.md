# John 7:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
At the midst of the feast, which lasted eight days, Jesus went up into the temple, directly and without stopping anywhere else. It is probable that this happened on the Sabbath day. Jesus, as the Lord and proprietor of the temple, began teaching and expounding the Scriptures to the people, giving them the true sense and instruction they needed.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "This was probably the first time that He did so thus openly in Jerusalem."
2. "He had kept back till the feast was half through, to let the stir about Him subside."
3. "Entering the city unexpectedly, had begun His 'teaching' at the temple, and created a certain awe, before the wrath of the rulers had time to break it."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "About the midst - Or about the middle of the feast. It continued eight days." 
2. "The temple - See the notes at Matthew 21:12."
3. "And taught - Great multitudes were assembled in and around the temple, and it was a favorable time and place to make known his doctrine."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "About the midst of the feast [της εορτης μεσουσης] . A peculiar form of expression found only here. The midst is expressed by a participle from the verb mesow, to be in the middle. Literally, the feast being midway."
2. "Taught [εδιδασκεν] . Or began to teach. Imperfect tense."

### John Calvin:
1. "We may sometimes, therefore, expose ourselves to dangers, but we ought never to disregard or omit a single opportunity of doing good."
2. "As to his teaching in the temple, he does so according to the ancient ordinance and custom... external rites are lively images of spiritual things, when they take their shape from the word of God."
3. "Christ undertook the office of a teacher; and justly, because he was the great High Priest... he attempts nothing but by the command of the Father."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "It is evident that the Evangelist means to impress us with the suddenness of this appearance of Jesus in the temple-courts."
2. "The Lord suddenly comes to His temple, and, at this feast of peculiar joy and hope, He brings with Him a special message and promise of the new covenant."
3. "His teaching during the latter half of the sacred week is to prepare for His words on the last day of the feast."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The feast of tabernacles was originally seven days, but a last day was added, making eight in all." 
2. "He went up. The leaders had asked where Jesus was. There he was now before their very eyes."

### John Gill:
1. "Jesus went up into the temple; as the Lord and proprietor of it, and as was his usual method."
2. "He expounded the Scriptures, gave the true sense of them, and instructed the people out of them."

### John Wesley:
1. "Now at the middle of the feast — Which lasted eight days."
2. "It is probable this was on the Sabbath day."
3. "Jesus went up into the temple — Directly, without stopping anywhere else."
