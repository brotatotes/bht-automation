### John Calvin:
1. "If God acts by the usual means and in the ordinary way, those means which are visible to the eyes are — as it were — veils which hinder us from perceiving the Divine hand."
2. "But if an unwonted power of God shines above the order of nature and the means generally known, we are stunned; and what ought to have deeply affected all our senses passes away as a dream."
3. "It was an astonishing proof of the power and grace of God, that Christ, who had not been taught by any master, was yet eminently distinguished by his knowledge of the Scriptures; and that he, who had never been a scholar, should be a most excellent teacher and instructor."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The marvelling on the part of the 'Jews' is not an astonishment that compels further inquiry and leads towards belief."

3. "The words which crown our Lord’s teaching at this feast enter into the very heart and express the inmost spirit of the whole Old Testament revelation."

### Archibald T. Robertson:

2. "It is not the wisdom of Jesus that disconcerted the Jewish leaders, but his learning."
3. "This is not an argument against education, but it takes more than education to make a real man."

### John Gill:

2. "They were surprised at his knowledge of the Scriptures, that he should be conversant with them, and be able to interpret them, and give the sense and meaning of them, in so full and clear a manner, as he did."
3. "He had not had a liberal education, but was brought up to a trade; he was not trained up at the feet of any of their Rabbins, in any of their universities, or schools of learning."

### John Wesley:
