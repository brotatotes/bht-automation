# John 7:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
If any man desires to do the will of God, he will have a clear understanding of His teachings. This willingness to obey is crucial in discerning the truth from falsehood. It is through obedience and a genuine desire to follow God's will that one can truly come to know Him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The θέλειν should not have been slurred over in the E. V., for it is important."
2. "If any man’s will be, to do His will, &c."
3. "If a man be really anxious to do the will of God, and thus to fulfil this first great commandment of the law, be, as Meyer expresses it, in ethical harmony with God, the singleness of purpose, and subjection to the will of God, will lead him on to faith in the promised and then apparent Messiah, and to a just discrimination of the divine character of his teaching."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "If any man will do his will" - A principle of immense importance, showing that singleness of desire to please God is the grand inlet to light on all questions vitally affecting one's eternal interests.
2. "The want of his [God's will], whether perceived or not, is the chief cause of infidelity amidst the light of revealed religion."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "If there is a disposition in anyone to do that will, though he should not be able perfectly to keep His commandments."
2. "To do the will of God is to obey His commandments; to yield our hearts and lives to His requirements."
3. "An honest desire to obey God will lead a man to embrace the great doctrines of the Bible."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "This is a notable illustration of the frequent blunder of the A. V. in rendering qelein, to will or determine, as a mere auxiliary verb."
2. "Sympathy with the will of God is a condition of understanding it."
3. "Of myself is misleading, being commonly understood to mean concerning myself. Rev., correctly, from myself; without union with the Father."

### John Calvin:
1. "Satan continually plots against us, and spreads his nets in every direction, that he may take us unawares by his delusions."
2. "If we are prepared to obey God, he will never fail to illuminate us by the light of his Spirit, so that we shall be able to distinguish between truth and falsehood."
3. "We need not wonder, therefore, that the doctrine of the Gospel is received by very few persons in the present day, since there is so little of the fear of God in the world."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Had they the will to do God’s will, they would need no miracle in evidence that in His teaching they heard the words of God."
2. "Such recognition of the words of Jesus is the test, therefore, of a will bent on doing the will of God."
3. "The last words must be carefully distinguished from those of chap. John 5:31, etc., ‘bearing witness concerning Myself.’ Here the word used refers to the origin, the source, of the speaking; and the meaning exactly agrees with chap. John 5:30, there ‘doing, here’ speaking, from or of Himself."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "If there be no sympathy there can be no understanding" (Westcott).
2. "Atheists of all types have no point of contact for approach to the knowledge of Christ."
3. "For those who love God it is also true that obedience to God's will brings richer knowledge of God."

### John Gill:
1. "if any man do his will", but "if any man will do" it; that is, is desirous of doing it; who has it wrought in him both to will and do, of the good pleasure of God, by his grace and Spirit.
2. he knows by the efficacy of the doctrine upon his heart, and the influence it has on his life and conversation.
3. by its coming not in word only, but in power; and by its working effectually in him, whether it is divine or human, of God or of man.

### John Wesley:
1. "If any man be willing to do his will, he shall know of the doctrine, whether it be of God" - This is a universal rule, with regard to all persons and doctrines.
2. "He that is thoroughly willing to do it, shall certainly know what the will of God is."
