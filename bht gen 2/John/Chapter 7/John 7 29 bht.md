# John 7:29 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus makes a unique claim in these words, emphasizing that he has a deep and intimate knowledge of God. He knows God's nature, his purposes, his mind, and his will. Only Jesus and those to whom he reveals God truly know him. Jesus is not only from God, but he is also sent by God, highlighting his divine mission. By understanding Jesus' eternal generation and divine mission, we can find the answers to questions about whether we truly know him and where he comes from.

### Marvin Vincent:

### John Calvin:
1. "When he says that he knoweth God, he means that it is not without good grounds that he has risen to so great confidence; and by his example he warns us not to assume lightly the name of God, so as to vaunt of Him as the patron and defender of our cause."
2. "But we are taught by these words of our Lord Jesus Christ that we ought especially to beware of proud and foolish confidence; and that, when we have fully ascertained the truth of God, we ought boldly to resist men."
3. "And he who is fully aware that God is on his side has no reason to dread the charge of being insolent, in trampling under foot all the haughtiness of the world."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "These three words contain the unique claim of Jesus, which is pressed all through the chapters of controversy with the Jews" (Bernard).
2. Jesus is the Interpreter of God to men (John 1:18).
3. He is the Father's Apostle to men.

### John Gill:
1. "I know him, His nature and perfections, his purposes and promises, his council and covenant, his mind and will."
2. "None knows him but he, and those to whom he pleases to reveal him."
3. "I am from him, being the only begotten of him, and as such lay in his bosom, and knew him, and his whole heart, and was privy to all of him."

### John Wesley:
1. "I am from him — By eternal generation: and he hath sent me." 
2. "His mission follows from his generation." 
3. "These two points answer those: Do ye know me? Do ye know whence I am?"
