# John 7:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus remained in Galilee, not going up to Jerusalem with his brothers or at all at present. This indicates his unwavering determination and certainty of purpose. His words were not trivial or inconsistent, but steadfast and constant, reflecting his unwavering character.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### John Calvin:
1. "The Evangelist here places before our eyes the cousins of our Lord Jesus Christ, who, in compliance with ordinary customs, pretend to worship God, but yet are on friendly terms with unbelievers, and therefore walk without any alarm."
2. "On the other hand, he places before our eyes Christ himself, who hated by the world, comes secretly into the city, till the necessity arising out of his office compels him to show himself openly."
3. "But if there be nothing more wretched than to be separated from Christ, accursed be that peace which costs so high a price as to leave and abandon the Son of God."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "As, however, it would seem that His brothers were on the point of setting out for Jerusalem, to be present at the beginning of the festival..." 
2. "He Himself was teaching in the temple when the sacred week had half expired..."
3. "The interval spent in Galilee can hardly have been more than two or three days."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He abode still in Galilee."
2. "No 'still' (ετ) in the Greek text."
3. "The constative aorist active indicative εμεινεν covers a period of some days."

### John Gill:
1. "He abode [still] in Galilee; and went not up with his brethren, nor at all at present; showing hereby a firmness and resolution of mind."
2. "Not using lightness of speech; and his words being not yea, and nay, but all of a piece, and by which he abode."
