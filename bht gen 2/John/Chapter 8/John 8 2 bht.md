# John 8:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Early in the morning, Jesus resumed His teaching with diligence and courage, attracting a large crowd of people who eagerly gathered around Him. He took his seat in the customary manner, fearlessly teaching with authority and a unique boldness that captivated the hearts and minds of those who listened. His diligence in ministering and the diligence of those who came to hear Him exemplify the importance and value of seeking His teachings.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Philip Schaff:
1. "With the return of day Jesus resumed His teaching of the people; and they, on their part, seem to have been powerfully attracted by His words."
2. "According to the custom of the time, He sat with His hearers gathered round Him."
3. "The sitting of Jesus while teaching is not mentioned elsewhere in this Gospel."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Early in the morning (ορθρου). Genitive of time, ορθρος meaning daybreak, old word, not in John, though in Luke 24:1; Acts 5:21."
2. "John uses πρω (John 18:28; John 20:1; John 21:4)."
3. "He took his seat (καθισας, ingressive active participle of καθιζω) as was customary for Jesus and began to teach (inchoative imperfect)."

### John Gill:
1. "Which shows his diligence, constancy, and assiduity, in his ministerial work, as well as his courage and intrepidity; being fearless of his enemies, though careful to give them no advantage against him, before his time."
2. "And all the people came unto him; which also commends the industry and diligence of his hearers, who were forward to hear him, and were early at the temple for that purpose, and that in great numbers."
3. "And he sat down and taught them; he sat, as his manner was; and taught them as one having authority, and such doctrine, and in such a manner, as never man did; with all plainness, boldness, and freedom."
