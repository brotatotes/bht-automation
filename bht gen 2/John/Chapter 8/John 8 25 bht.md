# John 8:25 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
He reiterates his message, saying, Even what I say to you from the beginning. Jesus speaks in a continuous and consistent manner, sharing the same truths throughout his teachings. His message remains unchanging, as he affirms the words he has spoken and the divine sonship he claims. Therefore, his words carry weight and should be carefully considered by all who hear them.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "ὅτι No sense can however be given by ὅτι which will at all harmonize with the context"
2. "λαλῶ will not bear this. It is never ‘to say’ simply, but ‘to discourse,’ or ‘to hold converse,’ ‘to speak.’"
3. "The rendering of καί, ‘even,’ and placing it before τὴν ἀρχ ., as done in E. V., is ungrammatical."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 25. Who art thou?—hoping thus
	to extort an explicit answer; but they are disappointed.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Who art thou that undertakest to threaten us in this manner!" 
2. "We cannot but admire the patience with which all this was borne, and the coolness with which he answered them."
3. "He is the same yesterday, today, and forever; and as he had once professed to be the light of the world, so, in the face of contempt, persecution, and death, he adhered to the profession."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Do you ask that which all along I am even saying to you?"
2. "How is it that I even speak to you at all?"
3. "I am essentially that which I even speak to you."

### John Calvin:
1. "I did not arise suddenly, but as I was formerly promised, so now I come forth publicly."
2. "He asserts that there is such an agreement between his doctrine and the eternity which he has spoken of, that it ought to be reckoned an undoubted confirmation of it."
3. "They pretended to be disciples of the Prophets, and to look to the eternal covenant of God; but still they rejected Christ, who had been promised from the beginning, and presented himself before them."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Had they been patient, willing listeners, they would have seen His meaning."
2. "None of these explanations can be obtained without doing violence to the Greek."
3. "Why am I even speaking to you at all? Much has He to say concerning them and to judge; but why does He any longer speak to men who will not understand His word?"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Thou, who art thou?" - They wish to pin him down and to charge him with blasphemy.
2. "Even that which I have also spoken unto you from the beginning" - It is not clear whether it is an affirmation or a question. The Greek fathers take it as an exclamation.
3. "Jesus avoids the term Messiah with its political connotations. He stands by his high claims already made." - He had virtually claimed to be the Messiah and on a par with God.

### John Gill:
1. "even [the same] that I said unto you from the beginning"
2. "he was the light of the world, and which he continued to assert"
3. "he affirmed that God was his Father, and by many strong arguments proved his divine sonship"

### John Wesley:
1. "Even what I say to you from the beginning."
2. "The same which I say to you, as it were in one discourse."
3. "With one even tenor from the time I first spoke to you."
