# John 8:48 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The intense and virulent scorn exhibited by the Jews is evident as they revile Jesus and label him a Samaritan. This term of contempt highlights their perception of him as an enemy to their church and nation. Furthermore, they accuse him of being possessed by a proud and lying spirit, suggesting that he is influenced by Satan himself. The Jews' desperation and rage are apparent as they hurl these accusations, displaying their complete rejection of Jesus' words and authority.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The Jews attempt no answer, but commence reviling Him. These are now properly οἱ Ἰουδ ., the principal among the Jews."
2. "So they called ‘outcasts from the commonwealth of Israel:’ and so afterwards they called the Christians בותיים, from כּוּתָה."
3. "As in the first clause they sundered Him from the communion of Israel, so now from that of Israel’s God."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "What intense and virulent scorn!" 
2. "The 'say we not well' refers to Hebrews 12:3- :."
3. "'A Samaritan' means more than 'no Israelite at all'; it means one who pretended, but had no manner of claim to the title—retorting perhaps, this denial of their true descent from Abraham."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Thou art a Samaritan - This was a term of contempt and reproach."
2. "And hast a devil - This charge they brought against him because he had said that they were not of God or were not the friends of God."
3. "The devil was an accuser or calumniator and as Jesus charged them with being opposed to God, they considered it as proof that he was influenced by such an evil spirit."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Say we not well. Well [καλως] is literally, finely, beautifully. Sometimes ironical..." 
2. "Thou art a Samaritan [σαμαρειτης ει συ]. Literally, a Samaritan art thou..."

### John Calvin:
1. "They show more and more how greatly they are stupified by Satan; for, though they are fully convicted, still they are enraged, and are not ashamed to show that they are utterly desperate." (243)
2. "Having no crime more heinous, therefore, to reproach Christ with, they seize at random, and without judgment, this vulgar taunt." (243)
3. "To express it in a few words, we see that with effrontery they curse him, as men are wont to do when, infuriated like enraged dogs, they cannot find anything to say." (243)

### Philip Schaff:
1. "To say that Jews were children of the devil seemed an insult, not to themselves only, but to God, whose children they believed themselves to be."
2. "No one but a Samaritan, filled with jealous hatred of the people of God, or one in whom dwelt a demon, one of the spirits whose sole aim was the subversion of God’s kingdom, could utter such words as these."
3. "We must not overlook the fact that those who are now addressing Jesus are ‘the Jews,’ not a part, but the Jews as a body."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "On the spur of the moment in their rage and fury they can think of no meaner things to say."
2. "They know, of course, that Jesus was not a Samaritan, but he had acted like a Samaritan in challenging their peculiar spiritual privileges."
3. "The charge of having a demon was an old one by the Pharisees and it is repeated later."

### John Gill:
1. "They meant by this expression, that he was an irreligious man, and one that had no regard to the law of Moses; or at least played fast and loose with religion and the law, and was for anything, as times served."
2. "And hence, because the Samaritans were had in such abhorrence by the Jews, they would not ask a blessing over food in company with them, nor say Amen after they had asked one."
3. "And since our Lord was used in this abusive manner, it need not seem strange, that his followers should be treated in the same way; as the Apostle Paul and his companions in the ministry were."

### John Wesley:
1. "Thou art a Samaritan - An enemy to our Church and nation."
2. "Art possessed by a proud and lying spirit?
