# John 9:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This man did not know Jesus, whom he had little knowledge of before. But Jesus, being the source of all knowledge and healing, performed a remarkable miracle for him, making clay and anointing his eyes. Jesus' actions show that there is hope for all, even those who may not have known or understood Him before.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Sight being natural to men, the deprivation of it is regarded as a loss, and the reception of it, though never enjoyed before, as a recovery." 
2. "There is no emphasis on μου here (as Bp. Wordsw.) nor in John 9:15; John 9:30: nor on σου in John 9:10; John 9:17; John 9:26." 
3. "See on Matthew 16:18, and compare Luke 12:18."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Go to Siloam."
2. "Received sight [ανεβλεψα]."
3. "Better, I recovered sight."

### John Calvin:
1. "So happy a result of obedience warns us to surmount every obstacle." 
2. "Proceed courageously wherever the Lord calls us."
3. "Not even to entertain a doubt that everything which we undertake by his authority, and under his guidance, will have a prosperous issue."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "This man, then, knew his Deliverer, though not His true nature." 
2. "The wording of the phrase would seem to imply that he had in his thoughts the meaning of the name ‘Jesus,’ so wonderfully illustrated in his own case."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He does not yet know Jesus as the Messiah the Son of God." 
2. "First aorist active indicative of αναβλεπω, old verb to see again, to recover sight, not strictly true of this man who had never seen. He got back sight that he had never had."
3. "Originally the verb means to look up."

### John Gill:
1. "Jesus, whom he had as yet little knowledge of..."
2. "Made clay and anointed mine eyes..."

### John Wesley:
1. A man called Jesus — He seems to have been before totally ignorant of him.
