# John 9:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This man's blindness was not a result of his or his parents' sin. Instead, it happened so that the works of God could be revealed through him. Jesus wants us to focus on God's purpose rather than seek blame. We should not indulge in idle curiosity but recognize the power of God that is being displayed.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "In the economy of God’s Providence, his suffering had its place and aim."
2. "This was to bring out the ἔργα τ . θεοῦ in his being healed by the Redeemer."
3. "His suffering had its place and aim, and this was to bring out the ἔργα τ . θεοῦ in his being healed by the Redeemer."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The cause was neither in himself nor his parents." 
2. "In order to the manifestation of 'the works of God,' in his cure."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "His blindness is not to be traced to any fault of his or of his parents."
2. "This thing has happened that it might appear how great and wonderful are the works of God."
3. "It has all happened, not by the fault of his parents or of himself, but by the wise arrangement of God, that it might be seen in what way calamities come, and in what way God meets and relieves them."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. But that [αλλ ινα] . There is an ellipsis : but (he was born blind) that.

### John Calvin:
1. "Christ does not absolutely say that the blind man, and his parents, were free from all blame; but he declares that we ought not to seek the cause of the blindness in sin."
2. "Consequently, when the causes of afflictions are concealed, we ought to restrain curiosity, that we may neither dishonor God nor be malicious towards our brethren."
3. "So then Christ intended, by these words, to excite in his disciples the expectation of a miracle; but at the same time reminds them in a general manner, that this must be abundantly exhibited on the theater of the world, as the true and lawful cause, when God glorifies his name."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Neither did this man sin nor his parents that he should be born blind, but (he was born blind, he is as he is) that the works of God may be manifested in him."
2. "Not to suggest or unravel speculative questions, but to present a sphere for the manifestation of the works of God, hath this man borne this infirmity."
3. "In him' alike in his physical (John 9:6-7) and in his spiritual healing (John 9:36-38) the love and grace of God are to be made manifest."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Jesus denies both alternatives, and puts God's purpose as the true solution."
2. "It is sometimes true that disease is the result of personal sin... but there are cases free from blame like this."
3. "There is comfort for many sufferers in the words of Jesus here."

### John Gill:
1. "Not but that both were guilty of original sin, and had committed actual transgressions."
2. "It was not any sin that either of them had committed while he was in the womb... that was the cause of this blindness."
3. "That the works of God should be manifest in him... that the power of divine grace might be displayed in bringing them out of darkness into marvelous light."

### John Wesley:
1. "It was not the manner of our Lord to answer any questions that were of no use, but to gratify an idle curiosity."
2. "The scope of his answer is, It was neither for any sins of his own, nor yet of his parents."
3. "But that the power of God might be displayed."
