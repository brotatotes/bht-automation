# John 9:30 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Why, herein is a marvelous thing! It is strange that you, learned individuals, fail to recognize that this man is of God. He has performed such an extraordinary miracle, opening not just the physical eyes of the blind man, but also his spiritual understanding. This should be a clear sign that he is from heaven, sent by God with divine authority.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "ἐν τ. γάρ is well expressed in E.V., Why herein is &c." 
2. "γάρ respicit ad ea quæ alter antea dixerat, et continet cum affirmatione conclusionem, quæ ex rebus ita comparatis facienda sit." 
3. "ὑμεῖς, you, whose business it is to know such things."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "He had no need to say another word; but waxing bolder in defense of his Benefactor, and his views brightening by the very courage which it demanded."
2. "So irresistible was his argument that their rage burst forth in a speech of intense Pharisaism."
3. "Thou wast altogether born in sins, and dost thou teach us?"—thou, a base-born, uneducated, impudent youth, teach us, the trained, constituted, recognized guides of the people in the things of God! Out upon thee!"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "A marvelous thing - This is wonderful and amazing."
2. "Know not from whence he is - That you cannot perceive that he who has performed such a miracle must be from God."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "A marvelous thing. The correct reading adds the article, the marvel."
2. "Ye is emphatic: ye who might be expected to know about a man who has wrought such a miracle."
3. "And yet. See on John 8:20; John 1:10."

### John Calvin:
1. "Certainly this is wonderful. He indirectly reproves them for remaining unmoved by a miracle so illustrious..."
2. "And, in order to show more clearly their stupidity or malice, he magnifies the excellence of the miracle..."
3. "He infers from this, that Christ was sent by God, because he is endued with so great power of the Spirit of God, to procure credit for himself and for his doctrine."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Why, herein is the marvel (εν τουτω γαρ το θαυμαστον εστιν)."
2. "You confess your ignorance of whence he is, ye who know everything, 'and yet (adversative use of κα again) he opened my eyes' (κα ηνοιξεν μου τους οφθαλμους)."
3. "That stubborn fact stands."

### John Gill:
1. "Very appropriately and pertinently, why herein is a marvellous thing; strange and unaccountable, that ye know not from whence he is..."
2. "That you learned doctors, men of sagacity and penetration, should not be able to discern that this man is of God..."
3. "...and that there should be any doubt from whence he comes, or from whom he has his commission: and [yet] he hath opened mine eyes..."

### John Wesley:
2. "So had God opened the eyes of his understanding, as well as his bodily eyes."
3. "Why, herein is a marvellous thing, that ye...should not know, that a man who has wrought a miracle, the like of which was never heard of before, must be from heaven, sent by God."
