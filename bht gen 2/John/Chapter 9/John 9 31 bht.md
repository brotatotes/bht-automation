# John 9:31 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
God does not listen to sinners, meaning that those who do not sincerely repent and turn away from their sins cannot expect God to answer their prayers. This man's confession of truth, despite knowing the potential consequences, displays his courageous and unwavering dedication to the principles he believes in.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "He expresses a general popular conviction, that one who could do these things, must be a pious man."
2. "(Joh 9:32) very eminently so, since this miracle was unprecedented."
3. "John 9:32, says Meyer, is the minor proposition: John 9:33, the conclusion; both in a popular form."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The allusion to his being 'born in sins' seems a tacit admission of his being blind from birth—the very thing they had been so unwilling to own."
2. "But rage and enmity to truth are seldom consistent in their outbreaks."
3. "There was a word for him too, which, if whispered in his ear from the oracles of God, would seem expressly designed to describe his case, and prepare him for the coming interview with his gracious Friend."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Now we know - That is, it is an admitted or conceded point. No one calls it into question."
2. "The meaning of this verse is, therefore, “It is well understood that God will not give miraculous aid to impostors and false prophets."
3. "A worshipper - A sincere worshipper; one who fears, loves, and adores him. Doeth his will - Obeys his commandments. This is infallibly true."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The pronoun we is not emphatic, like the pronouns in vv. 24, 29, but expresses the common information of all concerning a familiar fact." 
2. "Compounded with Qeov, God, and sebomai, to worship, the same verb which appears in eujsebhv, devout (Acts 10:2, Acts 10:7; Acts 22:12), and eujsebeia, godliness (Acts 3:12; 1 Timothy 2:2, etc.)."
3. "These two latter words, while they may mean reverence toward God, may also mean the due fulfillment of human relations; while qeosebhv, worshipper of God, is limited to piety towards God."

### John Calvin:
1. "It is the uniform doctrine of Scripture, that God does not listen to any but those who call upon him with truth and sincerity."
2. "For while faith alone opens the door to us to go to God, it is certain that all wicked men are excluded from approaching to him; and he even declares that he detests their prayers."
3. "No man is properly disposed to pray to God unless his heart be purified by faith."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "God does not hear sinners (ο θεος αμαρτωλων ουκ ακουε)." 
2. "If any man be a worshipper of God (εαν τις θεοσεβης η)." 
3. "And do his will (κα το θελημα αυτου ποιε)."

### John Gill:
1. "There is no man without sin; and God hears such who cry unto him day and night." 
2. "By 'sinners' are meant notorious sinners, such in whom sin reigns, who live in sin, and particularly impostors." 
3. "Not only they, the learned doctors of the nation, but such an illiterate man as himself knew, that notoriously wicked men, cheats, and deceivers, were not heard of God."

### John Wesley:
1. "God heareth not sinners - Not impenitent sinners, so as to answer their prayers in this manner."
2. "The honest courage of this man in adhering to the truth, though he knew the consequence, gives him claim to the title of a confessor."
