# John 9:37 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Lord, I believe. This man possesses an excellent spirit, deep and strong in understanding, yet with a teachable temper. He acknowledges his faith in the One who restored his sight, showing his gratitude and humility.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "These words serve to remind the man of the benefit he has received, and to awaken in him the liveliest gratitude."
2. "They do not refer to a former seeing, when he was healed: this was the first time that he had seen his Benefactor."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The new sense of sight having at that moment its highest exercise, in gazing upon 'the Light of the world'."

### John Calvin:
1. "For Christ does not mention his power, or the reason why he was sent by the Father, or what he has brought to men. But what principally belongs to faith is, to know that, by the sacrifice of his death, atonement has been made for our sins, and we are reconciled to God; that his resurrection was a triumph over vanquished death; that we are renewed by his Spirit, in order that, being dead to the flesh and to sin, we may live to righteousness; that he is the only Mediator; that the Spirit is the earnest of our adoption; in short, that in him is found every thing that belongs to eternal life." (58 words)

### Philip Schaff:
1. "This manifestation is now given; both in word ('he that speaketh') and in the half-veiled, yet clear, reference to the work that had been wrought on him ('thou hast seen Him')..."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Thou hast both seen him (κα εωρακας αυτον). Perfect active indicative (double reduplication) of οραω. Since his eyes were opened." 
2. "And he it is that speaketh with thee (κα ο λαλων μετα σου εκεινος εστιν). 'And the one speaking with thee is that man.' See John 19:35 for εκεινος used of the speaker." 
3. "In John 4:26 Jesus reveals himself in like manner to the Samaritan woman as Messiah while here as the Son of Man (or the Son of God)."

### John Gill:
1. "thou hast both seen him; not that he had seen him before now, with his bodily eyes... but he had seen him, that is, he had perceived and felt the power of him in restoring him to sight" 
2. "and it is he that talketh with thee; in like manner he made himself known to the woman of Samaria"

### John Wesley:
1. "Lord, I believe - What an excellent spirit was this man of!"
2. "Of so deep and strong an understanding; (as he had just shown to the confusion of the Pharisees,) and yet of so teachable a temper!"
