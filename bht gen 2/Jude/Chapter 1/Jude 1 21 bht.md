# Jude 21 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Keep yourselves in the love of God, for it is by his grace that you are able to do so. Maintain and increase the fervor of your love for God, using all proper means. With confidence, await the eternal life that has been purchased for you. Remember that this eternal life is not of your own doing, but is a gift, a mercy, given to you by our Lord Jesus Christ.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Jude combines the Father, the Son, and the Holy Ghost: and faith, hope, and love."
2. "Keep yourselves — not in your own strength, but 'in the love of God,' that is, God’s love to you and all His believing children, the only guarantee for their being kept safe."
3. "Mercy is usually attributed to the Father: here to the Son; so entirely one are they."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "On the duty here enjoined, see the notes at John 15:9."
2. "The phrase ‘the love of God’ may mean either God’s love to us, or our love to him."
3. "We may be commanded to love him, and to ‘keep’ ourselves in that love."

### John Calvin:
1. "He has made love as it were the guardian and the ruler of our life."
2. "The hope of [the last day] alone ought to sustain us, so that we may at no time despond."
3. "He would not have us to hope for eternal life, except through the mercy of Christ."

### John Gill:
1. "Saints should betake themselves to the love of God, as to a strong hold and preservative against sin."
2. "The people of God should make use of all proper means to maintain and increase the fervor of their love for God."
3. "The mercy of Christ, in interceding for his people, in sympathizing with them under all their afflictions, in succoring them under all their temptations, is a present mercy."

### John Wesley:
1. "Through his grace, keep yourselves in the love of God"
2. "In the confident expectation of that eternal life which is purchased for you"
3. "Conferred upon you, through the mere mercy of our Lord Jesus Christ"
