# Luke 1:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
John the Baptist served as a religious and moral reformer, similar to the prophet Elijah. His role was to prepare people's hearts for God and lead them to repentance. He represented the law and the prophets by calling individuals to turn away from their sins and seek the Lord. John's ministry aimed at convincing people of their sinful nature and urging them to forsake their wrongdoings. While his work was instrumental in the conversion of many, it ultimately pointed towards the greater work of God in renewing individuals to a spiritual life.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The work of John was one of preparation and turning men’s hearts towards God." 
2. "It was a concentration of the spirit of the law, whose office it was to convince of sin."
3. "He eminently represented the law and the prophets in their work of preparing the way for Christ."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "A religious and moral reformer, Elijah-like, he should be."
2. "Their success, though great, was partial—the nation was not gained."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Shall he turn - By repentance." 
2. "He shall call them from their sins, and persuade them to forsake them, and to seek the Lord their God."

### John Calvin:
1. "But if the ancient Church was so awfully dissolute, it is a frivolous pretext by which the Papists defend their own superstitions, that it is impossible for the Church to err, particularly since they include under this designation not the genuine and elect children of God, but the crowd of the ungodly."
2. "For conversion to God renews men to a spiritual fife, and therefore is not only God’s own work, but surpasses even the creation of men."
3. "The outward preaching, I acknowledge, can do nothing separately or by itself; but as it is an instrument of divine power for our salvation, and through the grace of the spirit an efficacious instrument, what God hath joined together let us not put asunder."

### Philip Schaff:
1. Luke 1:16.  To the Lord their God. Not to Christ, but to God. A prediction of John’s ministry, as preparatory and reformatory, the baptism of repentance. See on Matthew 3:1.
	

### John Gill:
1. "Great multitudes of them flocked unto him, attended on his ministry, believed in his doctrine, and submitted to his baptism."
2. "Conversion, which is meant by turning to God, is not man's work, but God's; and is effected by his mighty power."
3. "John was to be, and was, an instrument of the conversion of many among the Jews, by preaching the doctrine of repentance towards God, and faith in the Messiah."

### John Wesley:
1. And many of the children of Israel shall he turn — None therefore need be ashamed of "preaching like John the Baptist".To the Lord their God — To Christ.
