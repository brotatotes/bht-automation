# Luke 1:21 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The people were waiting for Zacharias to finish his duties in the temple, and they wondered why he was taking so long. It was common for the priest to complete his task quickly, so the people were curious about the delay. They were eager to receive the customary benediction from him before leaving the temple.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The priest at the time of prayer not to remain long in the holy place, for fear the people who were without might imagine that any vengeance had been inflicted on him for some informality."
2. "He was considered the representative of the people."
3. "The words ἐθαύμαζον ἐν are best taken together, wondered at, as in ref. Sir."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "waited—to receive from him the usual benediction"
2. "tarried so long—It was not usual to tarry long"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The people waited - That is, beyond the usual time."
2. "Marvelled - Wondered."
3. "Having remained on this occasion a longer time, the people became apprehensive of his safety, and wondered what had happened to him."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The priests, especially the chief priests, were accustomed to spend only a short time in the sanctuary, otherwise it was feared that they had been slain by God for unworthiness or transgression."
2. "Waited [ην προσδοκων] . The finite verb and participle, denoting protracted waiting. Hence, better as Rev., were waiting."
3. "Marvelled."

### John Calvin:
1. "It is not without reason, therefore, that they draw this conclusion from obvious symptoms: for it was not an ordinary occurrence, [it was not a common accident, but rather an astonishing work of God]"
2. "He became suddenly dumb without disease, and after a more than ordinary delay came out of the temple in a state of amazement."
3. "The word temple, as we have already mentioned, is put for the sanctuary, where the altar of incense stood."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Were waiting for Zacharias...because it was usual."
2. "Their wonder was both at and during his unusual stay."
3. "The brief stay of the priest is said to have been occasioned by 'the fear that the people who were without might imagine that any vengeance had been inflicted on him for some informality; as he was considered the representative of the people'."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Were waiting (ην προσδοκων). Periphrastic imperfect again. An old Greek verb for expecting. Appears in papyri and inscriptions. It denotes mental direction whether hope or fear."
2. "They marvelled (εθαυμαζον). Imperfect tense, were wondering. The Talmud says that the priest remained only a brief time in the sanctuary."
3. "While he tarried (εν τω χρονιζειν). See verse Luke 1:8 for the same idiom."

### John Gill:
1. "And the people waited for Zacharias.... marvelled that he tarried so long in the temple..." 
2. "The high priest... did not continue long in his prayer, 'that he might not affright' the Israelites." 
3. "They say unto him, why didst thou continue long in thy prayer?... that it might not be destroyed?"

### John Wesley:
1. The people were waiting — For him to come and dismiss them (as usual) with the blessing.
