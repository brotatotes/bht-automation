When the man came out of the temple, he was unable to speak to the people. They immediately realized that he had encountered a profound supernatural experience inside. The man conveyed his message to them through gestures, nodding his head and using his hands. It was clear to all that something extraordinary had happened to him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "They knew, by some excitement, visible in his manner."
2. "His 'not being able to speak,' must mean, in answer to the enquiries which his unusual appearance prompted."
3. "This answer he gave by a sign: and the question was also by signs; for he was deaf, as well as dumb, which indeed is the strict meaning."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 22. speechless—dumb, and deaf
	also (see Luke 1:62).

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The word 'vision' means 'sight, appearance,' or 'spectre,' and is commonly applied to spirits, or to beings from another world."
2. "He was unable to speak, and they had no way of 'perceiving' it but by such a sign."
3. "By beckoning unto them, or by a sign, he informed them of what he had seen."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "They perceived [επεγνωσαν] . Clearly perceived."
2. "He beckoned [ην διανευων] . Better Rev., continued making signs."
3. "Again the participle with the finite verb, denoting frequent repetition of the same signs."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "They probably asked why he had remained so long, and at once found that he was both deaf and dumb."
2. "From this they inferred that he had seen a vision in the temple, which was confirmed by Zacharias himself."
3. "When the voice of the preacher is announced, the priesthood of the Old Testament becomes silent, or can, at best, only make signs."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Clearly knew because he was not able to pronounce the benediction from the steps."
2. "He nodded and beckoned back and forth. Further proof of a vision that caused his dumbness."

### John Gill:
1. "And when he came out, he could not speak unto them...."
2. "and they perceived that he had seen a vision in the temple..."
3. "he beckoned unto them; nodding his head, or by some motions of his hands..."
