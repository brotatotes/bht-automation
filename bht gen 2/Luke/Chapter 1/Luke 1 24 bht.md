# Luke 1:24 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
When she found out about her pregnancy, she chose to withdraw from others and live in seclusion to fully embrace and be amazed by the incredible news of God's favor upon her. This allowed her to spend uninterrupted time praising and giving thanks to God for His extraordinary mercy.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 24. hid five months—till the
	event was put beyond doubt and became apparent. 
	 :-. ANNUNCIATION OF

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Hid herself - Did not go forth into public, and concealed her condition."
2. "This might have been done that she might spend her time more entirely in giving praise to God for his mercies."
3. "That she might have the fullest proof of the accomplishment of the promise before she appeared in public or spoke of the mercies of God."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The number of words referring to pregnancy, barrenness, etc., used by Luke, is almost as large as that used by Hippocrates." 
2. "All of these, except Luke 1:24, are peculiar to himself, and all, of course, in common use among medical writers." 
3. "Hid [περιεκρυβεν]. Only here in New Testament. Peri signifies completely; entire seclusion."

### John Calvin:
1. "She was so fully convinced of the promise made to her, that she had no doubt of its accomplishment."
2. "When she saw a severe punishment inflicted on her husband for ‘speaking unadvisedly with his lips,’ did she, for five successive months, cherish in her mind a similar doubt?"
3. "By saying, ‘thus hath the Lord done to me,’ she expressly and boldly affirms that his favor was ascertained."

### Philip Schaff:
1. Luke 1:24.  And after these days. Probably immediately after.
	 Hid herself five months,  i.e.,  the first five months of her pregnancy.
	

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Luke has almost as many words for pregnancy and barrenness as Hippocrates."
2. "If it is aorist it is the constative aorist."
3. "The preposition περ makes it mean completely (on all sides) hid."

### John Gill:
1. "notwithstanding her barrenness, and the unbelief of her husband"
2. "she hid herself, or lived retired, that she might be fully satisfied that she was with child"
3. "to avoid all discourse with others about it, which might be rumoured abroad"

### John Wesley:
1. Hid herself — She retired from company, that she might have the more leisure to rejoice and bless God for his wonderful mercy.
