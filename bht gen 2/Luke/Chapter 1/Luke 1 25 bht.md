# Luke 1:25 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Because fruitfulness was promised to the righteous, barrenness was considered a great reproach among the Jews. The Lord, in His gracious and bountiful manner, looked upon me and took away my reproach. He granted me strength to conceive a son in my old age, a son who would be great and serve a significant purpose in his lifetime. The removal of my reproach not only brought me joy, but also fulfilled His promise and showcased His faithfulness to His people.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Albert Barnes:
1. "In this merciful manner."
2. "To be 'barren,' therefore, or to be destitute of children, was considered a 'reproach' or a 'disgrace'."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Neither A. V. nor Rev. render oti; taking it, as frequently, merely as recitative or equivalent to quotation marks. But it means because."
2. "Elizabeth assigns the reason for her peculiar seclusion. Her pregnancy was God's work, and she would leave it to him also to announce it and openly to take away her reproach."
3. "Hence the specification of five months, after which her condition would become apparent."

### John Calvin:
1. "Among earthly blessings, Scripture speaks in the highest terms of the gift of offspring. And justly: for, if the productiveness of the inferior animals is his blessing, the increase and fruitfulness of the human race ought to be reckoned a much higher favor."
2. "Not without reason has barrenness been always accounted a reproach: for the blessing of the womb is enumerated among the signal instances of the divine kindness."
3. "Let parents learn to be thankful to God for the children which he has given them, and let those who have no offspring acknowledge that God has humbled them in this matter."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Since God had graciously removed her barrenness, she would leave it to Him to make this mercy manifest to others, and thus to take away her reproach among men."
2. "But she doubtless thus sought greater opportunity for devotion."
3. "In the case of Mary this becomes still more prominent. The blessing on women, especially as mothers, appears thus early in the story of the ‘seed of the woman.’"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "My reproach (ονειδος μου). Keenly felt by a Jewish wife because the husband wanted an heir and because of the hope of the Messiah, and because of the mother's longing for a child."

### John Gill:
1. "Thus hath the Lord dealt with me,.... In a very gracious and bountiful manner; in giving her strength to conceive a son in her old age, and such an one that was to be great, and so useful in his day."
2. "He took away my reproach from among men; as barrenness was accounted, especially among the Israelites, the seed of Abraham; to whom was promised a numerous issue, as the stars in the sky, and as the sand on the sea shore, and particularly the Messiah."

### John Wesley:
1. He looked upon me to take away my reproach — Barrenness was a great reproach among the Jews. Because fruitfulness was promised to the righteous.
