# Luke 1:32 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The prophecy reveals that this child, who will be born and given as the Son of God, will be great in his person as the God-man, being the angel of the great counsel, the mighty God, and the everlasting Father. He shall be called the Son of the Highest, emphasizing his divine nature and his unique relationship with God. Moreover, he will receive the throne of his father David, referring to the spiritual kingdom, of which David's earthly kingdom was a mere foreshadowing.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "This announcement makes it almost certain that Mary also was of the house of David."
2. "No astonishment is expressed by her at this part of the statement, and yet, from the nature of her question, it is clear that she did not explain it by supposing Joseph to be the destined father of her child."
3. "See 2 Samuel 7:13; Psalms 89:3-4; Isaiah 9:7; Jeremiah 33:15."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 32, 33. This is but an echo of
	the sublime prediction in Isaiah 9:6;
	Isaiah 9:7.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "By his being 'great' is meant he shall be distinguished or illustrious; great in power, in wisdom, in dominion on earth and in heaven."
2. "He shall be called...the Son, etc...The Highest - God, who is infinitely exalted; called the Highest, because He is exalted over all his creatures on earth and in heaven."
3. "David is called his father because Jesus was lineally descended from him...the promise was fulfilled by exalting Jesus to be a Prince and a Saviour, and the perpetual King of his people."

### John Calvin:
1. "The greatness of Christ is immediately explained to be such as raises him above all creatures."
2. "Christ is not confounded either with angels or with men, so as to be one of the multitude of the sons of God; but what is given to him no other has a right to claim."
3. "The angel declares that in the person of Christ would be fulfilled the prediction of Amos, 'In that day will I raise up the tabernacle of David that is fallen.'"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Shall be called. Shall be, and also, shall one day be publicly recognized as what He really is: the son of the Most High."
2. "She did not fully comprehend it. Stupendous spiritual truth is rarely comprehended at once."
3. "As Mary takes no exception to this part of the angel’s prediction, it is natural to conclude that she was also of the house of David."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The Son of the Most High (υιος Hυψιστου). There is no article in the Greek, but the use of Most High in verse Luke 1:35 clearly of God as here."
2. "In Luke 6:35 we find 'sons of the Most High' (υιο Hυψιστου) so that we cannot insist on deity here, though that is possible."
3. "The language of 2 Samuel 7:14; Isaiah 9:7 is combined here."

### John Gill:
1. "He shall be great,.... In his person, as God-man; this child born, and Son given, being the angel of the great counsel, the mighty God, and everlasting Father..."
2. "and shall be called the Son of the Highest; that is, of God, of whose names is עליון, 'the Most High'..."
3. "and the Lord God shall give unto him the throne of his father David."


### John Wesley:
1. "He shall be called the Son of the Highest — In this respect also: and that in a more eminent sense than any, either man or angel, can be called so."
2. "The Lord shall give him the throne of his father David — That is, the spiritual kingdom, of which David's was a type."
