# Luke 1:33 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
He is the everlasting King who rules over all believers, both Jews and Gentiles, encompassing the whole Israel of God. His kingdom extends to every corner of the earth and includes all those chosen by God. This reign of His is eternal and will never come to an end.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Albert Barnes:
1. "This was the name by which the ancient people of God were known, and it is the same as saying that he would reign over his own church and people forever."
2. "This he does by giving them laws, by defending them, and by guiding them; and this he will do forever in the kingdom of his glory."
3. "He is the only King that can defend us from all our enemies, sustain us in death, and reward us in eternity."

### John Calvin:
1. "Christ’s throne was, therefore, erected among the people of Israel, that he might thence subdue the whole world."
2. "All whom he has joined by faith to the children of Abraham are accounted the true Israel."
3. "The angel now declares that, when it has been established in the person of Christ, it will not be liable to destruction, and...its true perpetuity relates to the glory to come."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Over the house of Jacob forever, etc. This prediction echoes the Messianic prophecies already mentioned."
2. "Of his kingdom there shall be no end. This, however, hints at the universal spiritual reign of the Messiah."
3. "But the literal sense is also correct 'Salvation is really of the Jews, and will one day return to Israel.'"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Luke reports the perpetuity of this Davidic kingdom over the house of Jacob with no Pauline interpretation of the spiritual Israel."
2. "Joseph was of the house of David (Luke 1:27) and Mary also apparently (Luke 2:5)."

### John Gill:
1. "He shall reign over the house of Jacob...over the whole Israel of God, consisting of Jews and Gentiles."
2. "His kingdom shall be from one end of the earth to the other, even over all the elect of God."
3. "This reign of his shall be 'for ever, and of his kingdom there shall be no end'."

### John Wesley:
1. He shall reign over the house of Jacob — In which all true believers are included.
