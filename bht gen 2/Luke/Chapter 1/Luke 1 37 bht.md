# Luke 1:37 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
No word from God shall be without power is a reminder of God's almighty and faithful nature. It emphasizes that anything God has said, purposed, or promised, He is able and willing to do. We should never doubt or question the power of God's word, for with Him, nothing is impossible. This serves as a strong reassurance for our faith and confidence in His promises.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The future, in Hebrew, expresses that which does not belong to any fixed time, but shall ever be so."
2. "This place, and its original, Genesis 18:14, which are sometimes quoted to shew that ῥῆμα may mean simply “a  thing,” are in fact most decisive against any such supposition."
3. "For the declaration amounts to this, 'Hath the Lord spoken and can He not do it?'"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 37. For, &c.—referring to
	what was said by the angel to Abraham in like case ( :-), to strengthen her faith.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "With God nothing shall be impossible."
2. "The statement is, Every [παν] word of God shall not [ουκ] be powerless."
3. "No word of God shall be void of power."

### John Calvin:
1. "For no word shall be impossible with God...we ought not to allege any impossibility in opposition to his word."
2. "We ought always...to contemplate his boundless power, that it may strengthen our hope and confidence."
3. "We need not wonder if Mary is reminded by the angel of the power of God; for our distrust of it diminishes very greatly our confidence in the promises."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "No word from God shall be without power. This affirms, not only God’s almightiness, but even more fully His absolute faithfulness to His promises, the thought most necessary for Mary."
2. "The denial of what is miraculous is the denial of both almightiness and faithfulness."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  No word  (ουκ ρημα). Ρημα brings out the single item rather than the whole content (λογος). So in verse Luke 1:38. 

### John Gill:
1. "For with God nothing shall be impossible."
2. "That is consistent with his nature and perfections, with his counsels, purposes, and promises."
3. "Every thing that he has said, purposed, or promised, he is able to do, and will."
