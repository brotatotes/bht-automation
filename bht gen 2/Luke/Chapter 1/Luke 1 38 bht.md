# Luke 1:38 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Mary's humble acceptance of God's will is evident in her declaration, Behold the handmaid of the Lord. By acknowledging herself as the handmaid of the Lord, she demonstrates her obedience and trust in His promises. It is in this moment that the miraculous conception of our Lord takes place, as Mary eagerly awaits the fulfillment of God's word. Her unwavering faith and devotion serve as an inspiration for believers, reminding us of the importance of humbly embracing our role in God's divine plan.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Her own faithful and humble assent is here given to the divine announcement which had been made to her."
2. "She was no unconscious vessel of the divine will, but in humility and faith, a fellow-worker with the purpose of the Father."
3. "Her own unity with that purpose was required, and is here recorded."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:

### Albert Barnes:
1. "This was an expression of resignation to the will of God, and of faith in the promise."
2. "To be the 'handmaid of the Lord' is to be submissive and obedient, and is the same as saying, 'I fully credit all that is said, and am perfectly ready to obey all the commands of the Lord.'"

### John Calvin:
1. "This is the real proof of faith, when we restrain our minds, and, as it were, hold them captive, so that they dare not reply this or that to God: for boldness in disputing, on the other hand, is the mother of unbelief."
2. "But if the holy virgin was the handmaid of the Lord, because she yielded herself submissively to his authority, there cannot be worse obstinacy than to fly from him, and to refuse that obedience which he deserves and requires."
3. "I interpret it simply that she is convinced of the power of God, follows cheerfully where he calls, trusts also to his promise, and not only expects, but eagerly desires, its accomplishment."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The humble title she gives herself forms a striking contrast to the fulsome ones given to her by her adorers."
2. "In humble faith she assents; and so it was unto her according to the angel's word."
3. "From this moment, rather than from the words of the angel, we date the miraculous conception of our Lord."

### John Gill:
1. "she expresses her obedience of faith"
2. "she owns herself to be the handmaid of the Lord"
3. "she assented to what the angel said should be unto her; she earnestly desired it might be, and firmly believed it would be"

### John Wesley:
1. "Behold the handmaid of the Lord."
2. "It is not improbable, that this time of the virgin's humble faith, consent, and expectation, might be the very time of her conceiving."
