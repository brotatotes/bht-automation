# Luke 1:41 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The extraordinary movement of the baby in Elizabeth's womb was a result of the inspiration of the Holy Spirit. This maternal sensation and the recognition of the unborn Messiah by the unborn baby were both produced by the Holy Spirit. Elizabeth, filled with the Holy Ghost, was suddenly empowered with the gift of prophecy, understanding the significance of the babe's leaping in her womb as a joyous response to the presence of Mary, who had conceived the Messiah.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The salutation uttered by Elisabeth is clearly implied to have been an inspiration of the Holy Spirit."
2. "The movement of the babe in her womb was part of the effect of the same spiritual influence."
3. "The known mysterious effects of sympathy in such cases, at least lead us to believe that there may be corresponding effects where the causes are of a kind beyond our common experience."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 41. babe leaped—From  :- it is plain that this maternal sensation was something
	extraordinary—a sympathetic emotion of the unconscious babe, at the
	presence of the mother of his Lord.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Elizabeth was filled with the Holy Ghost - The meaning of this seems to be that she was filled with joy; with a disposition to praise God; with a prophetic spirit, or a knowledge of the character of the child that should be born of her."
2. "All these were produced by the Holy Spirit."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. The babe [το βρεφος] . See on 1 Peter 2:2.

### John Calvin:
1. "Luke intended to express an extraordinary occurrence."
2. "No good purpose would be served by involving ourselves in intricate questions, if the child was aware of the presence of Christ, or felt an emotion of piety: it is enough for us that the babe started by a secret movement of the Spirit."
3. "The expression, she was filled with the Holy Ghost, means that she was suddenly endued with the gift of prophecy to an unusual extent."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The babe leaped in her womb. Possibly for the first time...regarded by the Evangelist and by Elisabeth, as something extraordinary." 
2. "This movement of the babe was...a recognition of the unborn Messiah on the part of the unborn babe."
3. "The promise respecting John implies that the unborn infant would be the first to recognize the Lord."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  Leaped  (εσκιρτησεν). A common enough incident with unborn children (Genesis 25:22), but Elisabeth was filled with the Holy Spirit to understand what had happened to Mary. 

### John Gill:
1. "being made at hearing the voice of Mary, who had now conceived the Messiah, whose forerunner this babe, John the Baptist, was to be"
2. "who, by this motion, gave the first notice of his conception"
3. "Elisabeth was filled with the Holy Ghost not with the ordinary graces of the Spirit, but with extraordinary gifts, with a spirit of prophecy"

### John Wesley:
1. "The joy of her soul so affected her body."
2. "The very child in her womb was moved in an uncommon manner."
3. "As if it leaped for joy."
