# Luke 1:47 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Mary's spirit exults in God, recognizing Him as her Savior. This rejoicing is not unique to her, but is a shared blessing among all true believers who have hope in salvation and have received God's gracious regard. We, like Mary, have reason to rejoice in God as our Savior, for He came to our low estate and saved us from sin and misery.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 47. my Saviour—Mary, poor
	heart, never dreamt, we see, of her own "immaculate
	conception"—in the offensive language of the Romanists—any
	more than of her own immaculate life.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "God is called 'Saviour,' as he saves people from sin and death."
2. "He [God] was 'Mary's' Saviour, as he had redeemed her soul and given her a title to eternal life."
3. "...she rejoiced for that, and especially for his mercy in honoring her by her being made the mother of the Messiah."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The God who is the or my Savior."
2. "The title Savior is often applied to God in the Old Testament."
3. "See Septuagint, Deuteronomy 32:15; Psalms 24:5; Psalms 25:5; Psalms 95:1."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The exultation in spirit came first, and as a result her soul magnifies the Lord."
2. "In God my Saviour [...] imply her own need of a 'Saviour', they oppose the papal dogma of the Immaculate Conception."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Mary is not excited like Elisabeth, but breathes a spirit of composed rapture."
2. "One need not press unduly the difference between 'soul' (ψυχη) in verse Luke 1:46 and 'spirit' here."
3. "It is certain that we have an inner spiritual nature for which various words are used in Mark 12:30."

### John Gill:
1. "And my spirit hath rejoiced in God my Saviour."
2. "lays a solid foundation for rejoicing in him"
3. "the consideration of Christ, by Mary, as God her Saviour, as having an interest in him, as a Saviour, and this her Saviour, God, gave her greater joy"

### John Wesley:
1. "My spirit hath rejoiced in God my Saviour...she rejoiced in hope of salvation through faith in him, which is a blessing common to all true believers."
2. "And certainly she had the same reason to rejoice in God her Saviour that we have: because he had regarded the low estate of his handmaid, in like manner as he regarded our low estate; and vouchsafed to come and save her and us."
3. "...when we were reduced to the lowest estate of sin and misery."
