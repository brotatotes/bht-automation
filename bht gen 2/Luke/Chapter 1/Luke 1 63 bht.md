# Luke 1:63 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
As the parents wrote on a small wax-covered table, they named their son John, in accordance with the angel's previous instruction. The little table, also known as a tablet, was used to write down names and other information. Their unity in naming their child, despite Zacharias being deaf and mute, amazed everyone who witnessed it.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "πινακίδ. (= πινάκιον, Aristoph. Vesp. 167), a tablet smeared with wax, on which they wrote with a style." 
2. "On λέγων, a Hebraism, as applied to writing, see reff. and Jos. Antt. xi. 4. 7, Δαρεῖος ἀντιγράφει τῷ Σισίνῃ ... τάδε λέγων."
3. "ἐθαύμ. πάντες. This also confirms the view that Zacharias was deaf. There would be nothing wonderful in his acceding to his wife’s suggestion, if he had known it: the coincidence, apparently without this knowledge, was the matter of wonder."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 63. marvelled all—at his
	giving the same name, not knowing of any communication between them
	on the subject.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He asked - That is, by signs."
2. "A writing table - The table denoted by this word was usually made of wood and covered with wax."
3. "The ancients used to write on such tables, as they had not the use of paper."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Table was formerly used in the sense of tablet."
2. "The meaning is a little writing tablet, probably covered with wax."
3. "Wyc., has a poyntel, i.e., a style for writing."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "His name is John. Not 'shall be;' the declaration of the angel (Luke 1:13) had already settled that question." 
2. "This first writing of the New Testament begins with grace" (in allusion to the meaning of the name).
3. "Marvelled. At this agreement of the father and the mother."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Tablet (πινακιδιον). Diminutive of πινακις. In Aristotle and the papyri for writing tablet, probably covered with wax."
2. "Sometimes it was a little table, like Shakespeare's 'the table of my memory' (Hamlet, i.5)."
3. "It was used also of a physician's note-book."

### John Gill:
1. "The word signifies 'a little table', such as they used to write not only 'upon', but 'in'; and was sometimes of brass, sometimes of wood, and sometimes of wax, on which they wrote with a style or pen."
2. "His name is John: not that he must be, or shall be, so called; but this is his name, and no other; being what the angel had given him before his conception, and Zacharias now confirms."
3. "They were astonished, not so much at the new name brought into the family, as at the agreement between Elisabeth and Zacharias in this point, when the latter was both deaf and dumb."
