## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "For the construction περιοικ. αὐτούς, see Herod."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 65. fear—religious awe; under
	the impression that God's hand was specially in these events (compare
	Luke 5:26; Luke 7:16;
	Luke 8:37).

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The word 'fear' often denotes 'religious reverence.'"
2. "The remarkable circumstances attending the birth of John, and the fact that Zechariah was suddenly restored to speech, convinced them that God was there."
3. "Filled their minds with awe and veneration."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Were noised abroad [διαλελειτο] . Were mutually [δια] talked of.

### John Calvin:
1. "The works of God ought to be contemplated by us with such reverence as to affect our minds with seriousness."
2. "God does not amuse us with his miracles, but arouses the senses of men, which he perceives to be in a dormant state."
3. "It deserves our notice that they were far from mistaking the design when they interpreted the miracles which they saw as relating to the future excellence of the child."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Fear. The first effect produced by events which betoken what is supernatural."
2. "All these sayings, i.e., the story of what had happened at the circumcision of the child, possibly including the whole series of remarkable events in regard to John."
3. "Throughout all the hill-country of Judea, in which the home of Zacharias was situated."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Fear (φοβος). Not terror, but religious awe because of contact with the supernatural as in the case of Zacharias (Luke 1:12)."
2. "Were noised abroad (διελαλειτο). Imperfect passive. Occurs in Polybius. In the N.T. only here and Luke 6:11."
3. "It was continuous talk back and forth between (δια) the people."

### John Gill:
1. "They perceived the hand of God was in these things, and that these were effects of divine power." 
2. "They thought, and spoke of them with great solemnity." 
3. "All these sayings were noised abroad throughout all the hill country of Judea."
