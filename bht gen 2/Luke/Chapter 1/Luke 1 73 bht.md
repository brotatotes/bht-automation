# Luke 1:73 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In Luke 1:72, the mention of the oath emphasizes the depth of God's commitment and the fulfillment of the covenant found in Genesis 22:16-18. This covenant, which promised blessing and victory over enemies, is ultimately realized in Jesus the Messiah. While Zacharias may not fully grasp the concept of spiritual Israel, his allusion to the deliverance from Gentile oppression hints at God's plan for his people. These verses remind us of God's faithfulness and assure us of the redemption brought through Christ.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The Holy Spirit, speaking by Zacharias, seems to refer here to the providential dispensation signified in the names of the Baptist and his parents."
2. "The Baptist, by his name John, spake of the ἔλεος or grace of God."
3. "Zacharias (from זָכַר, recordatus fuit, and יָהּ, Jah, Jehovah) signifies θεὸς ἐμνήσθη, and Elisabeth (from אֵל, El, Deus, and שָׁבַע, sheba, juravit) is connected with the ὅρκος θεοῦ."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The whole work and kingdom of Messiah is represented as a mercy pledged on oath to Abraham and his seed, to be realized at an appointed period."
2. "Hence, not only 'grace,' or the thing promised; but 'truth,' or fidelity to the promise, are said to 'come by Jesus Christ'."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The oath is recorded in Genesis 22:16-17."
2. "It was an oath in which God swore by himself."
3. "That promise was now to be entirely fulfilled by the coming of the Messiah."

### John Calvin:
1. "The oath is mentioned, for the purpose of expressing more fully the firmness and sacredness of his truth: for such is his gracious condescension, that he deigns to employ his name for the support of our weakness."
2. "If his bare promises do not satisfy us, let us at least remember this confirmation of them."
3. "To give to us Zacharias does not enumerate the several points of God’s covenant, but shows that God’s purpose, in dealing so kindly and mercifully with his people, was to redeem them."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The oath. This explains the word 'covenant' in Luke 1:72. God's covenant of mercy had been sealed by an oath."
2. "The Abrahamic covenant becomes prominent as the coming of the Messiah draws near."
3. "The oath is found recorded in Genesis 22:16-18."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The oath appears in Genesis 22:16-18."
2. "The oppression of the Gentiles seems to be in the mind of Zacharias."
3. "It is not certain how clearly he grasped the idea of the spiritual Israel as Paul saw it in Galatians and Romans."

### John Gill:
1. "When he swore by himself, because he could swear by no greater, that in blessing he would bless him."
2. "That his seed should possess the gates of his enemies."
3. "All which have been fulfilled in Jesus the Messiah."
