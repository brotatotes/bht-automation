# Luke 1:78 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The day spring, or the rising sun, refers to Christ who is the source of light and grace. It is through His tender mercy and abundant mercy that the forgiveness of sins is made possible. His mercy shines forth from on high, dispelling the darkness and bringing the light of life to those who were sitting in darkness. He is the one who has visited us, bringing salvation and hope.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "ἀνατολή is... ‘that which springs up or rises,’ as Light."
2. "The day-spring does not come ἐξ ὕψους, but from beneath the horizon; but the Messiah does."
3. "The ἐπιφᾶναι... belongs to the day-spring, and only figuratively to the Messiah."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Through the tender mercy of our God—the sole spring, necessarily, of all salvation for sinners."
2. "dayspring from on high—either Christ Himself, as the 'Sun of righteousness', arising on a dark world, or the light which He sheds."
3. "The sense, of course, is one."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The dayspring ... means the morning light, the aurora, the rising of the sun."
2. "It is called the dayspring 'from on high' because the light of the gospel shines forth from heaven."
3. "There is here, doubtless, a reference to Isaiah 60:1-2; indeed, almost the very words of that place are quoted."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Mercy - Lit., bowels of mercy. See on 1 Peter 3:8; James 5:11. Rev. gives heart of mercy in margin. Wyc., frightfully, entrails of mercy."
2. "The day-spring from on high - Lit., the rising. The word occurs in the Septuagint as a rendering of branch, as something rising or springing up, by which the Messiah is denoted."
3. "Hath visited - See on Matthew 25:36; 1 Peter 2:12. Some, however, read ejpiskeyetai, shall visit. So Rev."

### John Calvin:
1. "He then tells us metaphorically, that the great mercy of God has made the day to give light to those who were sitting in darkness."
2. "Zacharias extols the mercy of God, as manifested in dispelling the darkness of death, and restoring to the people of God the light of life."
3. "In this way, whenever our salvation is the subject, we ought to raise our minds to the contemplation of the divine mercy."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Because of the tender mercy of our God." 
2. "The dayspring from on high." 
3. "Shall visit us."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Tender mercy (σπλαγχνα ελεους). Bowels of mercy literally (1 Peter 3:8; James 3:11)."
2. "The dayspring from on high (ανατολη εξ υψους). Literally, rising from on high, like the rising sun or stars (Isaiah 60:19)."
3. "Shall visit (επεσκεψετα), correct text, cf. Luke 1:68."

### John Gill:
1. "The source and spring of pardon is the free grace and abundant mercy of God."
2. "It was the mercy of God to us that provided a lamb for a burnt offering, and then accepted the sacrifice and satisfaction of his Son, in our room and stead, and forgave all our sins, for his sake."
3. "The Messiah Jesus, is intended here, who is the man, that branch, that has grown up out of his place; not from below, but from above; and who is the phosphorus, or bringer of light, that bright and morning star, that sun of righteousness, who has light in himself, and communicates light to others."

### John Wesley:
1. The day spring — Or the rising sun; that is, Christ.
