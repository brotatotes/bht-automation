# Luke 1:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Zechariah, serving as a priest in the temple, offered incense before God, in the presence of the symbols of God's dwelling. The temple was considered the house of God, where the Shechinah, a visible sign of God's presence, was known to manifest. In the order of his course, Zechariah faithfully carried out his duties, regarding himself as in the sight of God and doing his work diligently. Through this act, Zechariah was not only performing his duty but also experiencing God's favor and presence.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Before God" - In the temple, where God dwelt by the symbols of His presence.
2. The temple was regarded by the Jews as the "house" or dwelling of God.
3. In the "first" temple, there was a "cloud" called the Shechinah, a visible sign of the presence of God.
4. Zechariah offered incense "before God" in the temple.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Served as priest, is more simple than the paraphrase of the E. V."
2. "The words used here and in Luke 1:9 are not the same."
3. "In the order of his course, i.e., during the week his course served in the temple."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "A favourite idiom in Luke, εν with the articular infinitive and the accusative of general reference where the genitive absolute could have been used or a temporal conjunction and finite verb."
2. "The word ιερατευω does not appear in the ancient Greek, but in the LXX and this one example in Luke."
3. "It is on the Rosetta Stone and the early inscriptions so that the word was simply applied by the LXX translators from current usage."

### John Gill:
1. "Whilst he was in the way of his duty, when oftentimes God appears to, and in favour of his people."
2. "Considering himself as in the sight of God, and doing his work faithfully and sincerely."
3. "Taking his turn in the order of the course of Abia, to which he belonged."
