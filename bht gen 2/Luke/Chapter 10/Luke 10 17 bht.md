# Luke 10:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The disciples, having completed their mission, joyfully returned to Jesus and shared the news of their journey and success. Overwhelmed with joy, they exclaimed, Lord, even the devils are subject unto us through thy name: the miraculous power given to them was even greater than they had initially anticipated. The disciples were astonished at the extent of the powers bestowed upon them, recognizing that the demons they encountered were just one manifestation of the ongoing spiritual conflict between Christ and Satan. In essence, this passage highlights the unexpected and extraordinary abilities that believers may experience when connected to Christ.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The ἐν τῷ ὀν . σου is perhaps too much lost sight of in the ἡμῖν here."
2. "I would not lay so much stress on this as Stier has done."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Thou hast exceeded Thy promise, for 'even the devils,'"
2. "The possession of such power, not being expressly in their commission, as in that to the Twelve, filled them with more astonishment and joy than all else."
3. "Taking no credit to themselves, but feeling lifted into a region of unimagined superiority to the powers of evil simply through their connection with Christ."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The devils are subject unto us - The devils obey us."
2. "We have been able to cast them out."
3. "Through thy name - When commanded in thy name to come out of those who are possessed."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The fuller development of the new dispensation begins with the mission of the seventy, and not with the mission of the apostles."
2. "Its ground - work, from Luke's point of sight, is the symbolic evangelization of every nation upon earth, and not the restoration of the twelve tribes of Israel."
3. "According to Jewish tradition, there were seventy or seventy - two different nations and tongues in the world."

### John Calvin:
1. "It is evident that the faith of the seventy disciples in the words of Christ had not been full and complete."
2. "When the matter proceeded to an extent which surpassed their expectations, they were astonished at the sight."
3. "Believers receive from the word but a slight perception of the Divine power, and are afterwards excited to admiration by actual experience."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "How much of permanent good they accomplished we are not told, but in labors of healing they must have had great success; hence their ‘joy,’ and their language: Even the demons are subject to us in thy name."
2. "This power had not been expressly given to them, as to the Twelve (chap. Luke 9:1), and they rejoice that their success exceeded the promise."
3. "Other successes are only implied; this point is brought prominently forward by the Evangelist."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Returned with joy...Joy overflows their faces and their words." 
2. "The demons were merely one sign of the conflict between Christ and Satan."
3. "Every preacher has to grapple with demons in his work."

### John Gill:
1. "The disciples...had finished their embassy, they returned again to him, and gave him an account of their journey and success."
2. "...with great joy...saying, Lord, even the devils are subject unto us through thy name: they found the miraculous power conferred on them was greater than they at first imagined..."
3. "they express themselves as men astonished at the powers bestowed on them."
