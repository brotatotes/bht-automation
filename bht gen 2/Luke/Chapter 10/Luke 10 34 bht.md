# Luke 10:34 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The act of pouring in oil and wine signifies the abundance of Christ's grace and the application of it to heal wounded sinners. Like a balm for fresh wounds, this pouring in of oil and wine brings comfort and restoration to those who are broken-hearted. It is the work of Christ to bind up the broken-hearted, heal wounds, and restore comfort to mourners.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Oil and wine—the remedies used in such cases all over the East... the wine to cleanse the wounds, the oil to assuage their smartings."
2. "On his own beast—himself going on foot."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Pouring in oil and wine - These were often used in medicine to heal wounds."
2. "How strikingly is his conduct contrasted with the priest and Levite!"
3. "He does not merely say 'in general' that he showed him kindness, but he 'told how' it was done."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Wine to cleanse, and oil to soothe."
2. "Usual remedies for sores, wounds, etc."
3. "a place of common reception."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Pouring on them oil and wine. The usual remedies for wounds in the East."
2. "On his own beast. So that he walked himself. True philanthropy involves self-sacrifice."
3. "An inn. Evidently an inn, in our sense of the word, and not a caravanserai."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Bound up his wounds. First aorist active indicative of καταδεω, old verb, but here only in the N.T." 
2. "Oil and wine were household remedies even for wounds (soothing oil, antiseptic alcohol)."
3. "An inn. The old Attic form was πανδοκειον (from παν, all, and δεχομα, to receive). A public place for receiving all comers and a more pretentious caravanserai than a καταλυμα like that in Luke 2:7."

### John Gill:
1. "It being part of the work of Christ, to bind up the broken-hearted, to heal wounded sinners, and restore comforts to mourners."
2. "And the pouring in of these, may denote the plentiful effusion of Christ's blood, and the riches of his grace in the application of it; and the freeness and generousness of this action, which is his own: for man cannot do it."
3. "And hither Christ brings his people, whom he saves and calls; it is his will that they should be in a church state, and it is his own act to bring them there, and it is their great privilege to be thither brought."

### John Wesley:
1. Pouring in oil and wine — Which when well beaten together are one of the best balsams that can be applied to a fresh wound.
