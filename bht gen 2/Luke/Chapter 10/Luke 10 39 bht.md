# Luke 10:39 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Mary and her sister, who was named Mary, both sat at Jesus' feet. Sitting at the feet of a teacher was a common practice for disciples and learners, showing their devotion and desire to learn. Mary's act of sitting at Jesus' feet indicated her deep affection for Christ, her humility, and her attentiveness to his teachings.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Mary sat at Jesus’ feet, as His disciple, while He was discoursing."
2. "Martha was busy about preparing [the meal]."
3. "It does not appear that the meal had begun; far rather is it likely that Martha was busy about preparing it."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Who for her part, in contrast with Martha, sat herself." 
2. "From the custom of sitting beneath an instructor, the phrase 'sitting at one's feet' came to mean being a disciple of any one." 
3. "Kept listening to His word."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Sat at Jesus' feet - This was the ancient posture of disciples or learners."
2. "They sat at the 'feet' of their teachers - that is, beneath them, in a humble place."
3. "When it is said that Mary sat at Jesus' feet, it means that she was 'a disciple' of his; that she listened attentively to his instructions, and was anxious to learn his doctrine."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Sat [παρακαθεσθεισα] . Only here in New Testament. Lit., sat beside [παρα] .

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Mary, the woman, whose subsequent act of love was promised a memory as wide as the spread of the gospel."
2. "Sat down at the Lord's feet. Not as He reclined at table, for the meal was not yet ready, but as a willing disciple."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "She took her seat by the feet of Jesus and went on listening to his talk." 
2. "It is not clear what the point is in κα here. It may mean that Martha loved to sit here also as well as Mary."

### John Gill:
1. "And she had a sister called Mary.... which also sat at Jesus' feet." 
2. "It was usual for disciples, or the scholars of the wise men, to sit at the feet of their masters."
3. "The phrase is expressive of her great affection for Christ, her humble deportment, and close attention."
