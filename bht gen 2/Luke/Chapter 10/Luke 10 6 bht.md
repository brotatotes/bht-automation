# Luke 10:6 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
If there is someone in the house who is receptive to your message of peace and has a disposition of kindness, your wish for peace will be well received and appreciated. However, if there are no individuals in the household who are open to peace, your message may be rejected or ignored.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "υἱὸς εἰρ., a (or more probably, the, as words like πατήρ, μήτηρ, νἱός, &c., are often definite though anarthrous) son of peace: i.e. persons receptive of your message of peace"
2. "persons receptive of your message of peace"
3. "see reff."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The son of peace" means one "disposed" to peace, or peaceful and kind in his disposition.
2. If the "house" or "family" be "worthy," or be disposed to receive you in "peace" and kindness.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. If a son of peace be there. So Rev. A Hebraism, referring to the character of the head of the house, and the tone of the household. Compare Job 21:9.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "A son of peace, i.e., one ‘worthy,’ one whose heart was ready to receive the message of peace they brought."
2. "Upon him, or, ‘it,’ as in E.V. The original may refer either to the man or the house, the former is the more natural sense."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "A son of peace (υιος ειρηνης)."
2. "Shall rest (επαναπαησετα)."
3. "It shall turn to you again (εφ' υμας ανακαμψε)."

### John Gill:
1. "If there be any elect person or persons in the house, whom God has chosen to partake of peace by the blood of Christ, and the benefits arising from it."
2. "Your peace shall rest upon it: the salutation, or wish of peace, shall be well taken, and gratefully received."
3. "If there are no sons of peace, no elect persons there, your salutation, or wish of peace and prosperity to the family, will be despised and rejected."

### John Wesley:
1. A son of peace — That is, one worthy of it.
