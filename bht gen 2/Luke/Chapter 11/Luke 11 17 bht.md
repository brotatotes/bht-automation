# Luke 11:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The saying a house divided against itself refers to a family that is torn apart by internal conflicts. This division within the family ultimately leads to its downfall and destruction. It serves as a warning that unity and harmony are essential for the well-being and survival of any household.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The ordinary rendering 'and house (divided) against house, falleth' is certainly right."
2. "The ruling idea of the saying having been given by the βασ. ἐφʼ ἑαυτήν, the emphatic pronoun need not be expressed again."
3. "Similarly we have, 1 Corinthians 2:11, 'τίς οἶδεν ἀνθρώπων τὰ τοῦ ἀνθρώπου, εἰ μὴ τὸ πν. τοῦ ἀνθρώτου τὸ ἐν αὐτῷ'; the ὁ ἄνθρ. being the same throughout."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "A house divided against itself falleth"
2. "The divided kingdom is brought to desolation, and its families and households in their party strifes are brought to ruin."
3. "One house shall fall upon another."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Jesus knew that they were trying to tempt him." 
2. "A house divided against a house falleth." 
3. "One tumbling house knocking down its neighbour, a graphic picture of what happens when a kingdom is divided against itself."

### John Gill:
1. "Every kingdom divided against itself is brought to desolation; in process of time, division will end in destruction."
2. "A family, in which one part is opposed to the other, issues in the ruin of both."

### John Wesley:
1. A house — That is, a family.
