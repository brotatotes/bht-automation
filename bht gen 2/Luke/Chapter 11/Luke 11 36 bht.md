# Luke 11:36 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
When your soul is completely illuminated by the Gospel light, without any part darkened by sin or worldly desires, it will be full of divine wisdom and understanding. Just as a room is filled with light when a candle shines brightly and burns clearly, your whole being will be filled with the light and joy that the Gospel brings. The purity and clarity of your soul will result in a deep connection with God's truth and a life that reflects His glory.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "When thine eye is single (Luk 11:34), i.e. simple, straight and single-seeing, thy whole body will be light."
2. "If the soul be so, having no part darkened by prejudice or selfish lusts, and approach thus to His teaching, it shall be wholly illuminated by it, as by the candle of the Lord, searching its inward parts."
3. "So this saying, which, even as it stands, is not tautological, for the second clause expresses the further result and waxing onward of the shining light, arising from the singleness of the eye, becomes, in its spiritual significance, a weighty declaration of truth."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The bright shining of a candle [ο λυχνος τη αστραπη] . More correctly, as Rev., the lamp with its bright shining."
2. "jAstraph means lightning : see ch. Luke 10:18; and that is the usual meaning in classical Greek, though it occurs, rarely, of the light of a lamp."
3. "It is used here to emphasize the idea of moral illumination."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Only when thy body is wholly illumined, without having even an obscure corner left therein, will it become so bright and clear as if the full brilliancy of a bright lamp illumined thee; in other words, thou wilt be placed in a normal condition of light." 
2. "The necessity of a state of soul corresponding to and affected by the light which God so fully gives is here emphasized." 
3. "It is glory as the result of holiness."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  With its bright shining  (τη αστραπη). Instrumental case, as if by a flash of lightning the light is revealed in him. See on Luke 10:18. 

### John Gill:
1. "If thy whole body therefore be full of light,.... That is, if the whole soul, as the Ethiopic version reads, be full of Gospel light, through the illuminating influences of the blessed Spirit accompanying the word..."
2. "Having no part dark; every power and faculty of the soul being affected with it, and influenced by it, though, as yet, the light and knowledge of evangelical things is not perfect in any..."
3. "The whole shall be full of light, as when the bright shining of a candle doth give thee light; the whole soul shall be as full of light and joy, which the Gospel always brings with it, as a room is full of light, when a candle is lighted, and shines brightly, and burns clearly in it."

### John Wesley:
1. "If thy whole body be full of light — If thou art filled with holy wisdom, having no part dark, giving way to no sin or folly..."
