# Luke 11:39 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Now, you Pharisees, who may be present at this gathering, need to pay attention. This rebuke is not only for the host, but also for all of you who belong to the Pharisee group. It is important for you to understand the severity of your actions, as your hypocrisy is not limited to this particular incident.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The severest parts of the discourse in Matt. were not uttered on this occasion."
2. "The outside apparent conduct, and the inner unseen motives."
3. "The mind of the hearer being left to find its own way in allotting each its part."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 39-41. cup and platter—remarkable
	example of our Lord's way of drawing the most striking illustrations
	of great truths from the most familiar objects and incidents of life.
	ravening—rapacity.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Here the sense is that the cup and platter were filled with what had been unjustly taken from others."
2. "That is, they lived by their wickedness; their food was procured by dishonesty and extortion."
3. "Even in the Pharisee’s own house, and when expressly invited to partake of his hospitality, he loved his soul so much that he faithfully warned him of his crimes."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Platter [πινακος] . The word rendered charger in Matthew 14:8, on which see note. Compare, also, paroyiv, platter, Matthew 23:25.

### John Calvin:
1. "Now this reproof applies to all hypocrites, even to those who believe that righteousness consists in ceremonies appointed by God."
2. "He condemns generally the error of worshipping God by ceremonies, and not spiritually, by faith and a pure affection of the heart."
3. "The chief reason why men are deceived is, that they do not consider that they have to deal with God, or, they transform Him according to the vanity of their senses, as if there were no difference between Him and a mortal man."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The outside of the cup and of the platter. Comp. Matthew 23:25. The reference is to their ceremonial observances, but the contrast differs from that in Matthew."
2. "There the outward legality and the inward immorality of their enjoyments are in strict contrast; here the outwardly purified cup is opposed to the inwardly corrupted heart of the drinker; external conduct to inner unseen motives."
3. "The comparison is less exact, since the figure and the reality are joined."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "You Pharisees do now what was formerly done." 
2. "They keep the external regulations, but their hearts are full of plunder and wickedness."
3. "Both inside and outside should be clean, but the inside first."

### John Gill:
1. "Now do ye Pharisees make clean the outside of the cup, and the platter... but your inward part is full of ravening and wickedness."
2. "...meaning either their souls, which were full of all manner of sin, the cleansing and purity of which they had no concern about, whilst they were very strict and curious in washing their bodies, their cups and platters..."
3. "...or rather the vessels which were filled with meat and drink got by extortion, rapine, and oppression..."

### John Wesley:
1. Now ye Pharisees — Probably many of them were present at the Pharisee's house. Matthew 23:25.
