### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Our Lord purposely names the most trifling products of the earth, as examples of what they punctiliously exacted the tenth of."
2. "The same tendency to merge greater duties in less besets us still, but it is the characteristic of hypocrites."
3. "There is no need for one set of duties to jostle out another but of the greater, our Lord says, 'Ye ought to have done' them; of the lesser, only 'ye ought not to leave them undone.'"

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Tithe is tenth."
2. "Rue is probably from phgnumi, to make fast, because of its thick, fleshy leaves. Matthew has anise."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Instead of really giving as our Lord enjoined, they had been in the habit of making trifling payments in over-exactness."
2. "They had been in the habit of making trifling payments in over-exactness."
3. "See on Matthew 23:23."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Tithe (αποδεκατουτε). Late verb for the more common δεκατευω. So in Matthew 23:23. Take a tenth off (απο-)." 
2. "Rue (πηγανον). Botanical term in late writers from πηγνυμ, to make fast because of its thick leaves. Here Matthew 23:23 has "anise." Every herb (παν λαχανον). General term as in Mark 4:32. Matthew has "cummin.""
3. "Pass by (παρερχεσθε). Present middle indicative of παρερχομα, common verb, to go by or beside. Matthew 23:23 has 'ye have left undone' (αφηκατε). Luke here has 'love' (αγαπην), not in Matthew."

### John Gill:
1. "Though these words, with several other passages in this chapter, are much alike with those in Matthew 23 yet it is clear that they were spoken at different times."
2. "For ye tithe mint and rue; and all manner of herbs; that is, all sorts of herbs that grow in the garden, and were not common to all."
3. "By 'judgment' may be meant justice, or doing that which is right between man and man, both publicly and privately, which was greatly neglected by these extortioners and unjust men."

### John Wesley:
1. Wo to you — That is, miserable are you. In the same manner is the phrase to be understood throughout the chapter.
