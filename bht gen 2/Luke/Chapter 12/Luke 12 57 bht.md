# Luke 12:57 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Why do you not discern and acknowledge the intrinsic excellence of my doctrine? From your own observation, you have the ability to judge what is right and to recognize the truth without any external sign. Just as you can discern the signs in the heavens to predict the weather, you can also use your own judgment to understand the greatness of my teachings.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Why do ye not discern of yourselves your true state that which is just the justice of your case as before God?" 
2. "You are going with your adversary before the magistrate; therefore by the way take pains to be delivered from him, lest he drag thee to the judge, and the judge deliver thee to the exactor, and the exactor cast thee into prison."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "He sends them to their own conscience, as enough to show them who He was, and win them to immediate discipleship."
2. "To do this requires more knowledge of Scripture and providence than we possess."
3. "They might say, 'To do this requires more knowledge of Scripture and providence than we possess,' but He sends them to their own conscience."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Of yourselves. In the exercise of your ordinary habits of observation which you apply to the heavens.

### John Calvin:
1. "Christ opens up the source of the evil, and, as it were, applies the lancet to the ulcer."
2. "The reason why hypocrites are so much disposed to make objections is, that they throw their swelling words into the air without any concern, and never exercise calm thought, or place themselves at the tribunal of God."
3. "Christ adapted his style generally to the followers and disciples of the scribes, and to other despisers of God who resembled them, of whom he perceived that there were too many."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "A further reproach for want of knowledge of personal duty, which involved great want of prudence."
2. "Even of yourselves. Either independently of their teachers, or independently of the plain signs of the times."
3. "What is right, namely repentance, as appears from the figure which follows."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Without the presence and teaching of Jesus they had light enough to tell what is right."
2. "They had light enough to tell what is right, and so [they are] without excuse."
3. "Even of yourselves, without the presence and teaching of Jesus, they had light enough to tell what is right."

### John Gill:
1. "From their own observation, as in discerning the signs of the weather...they might have concluded, that now was the time of the Messiah's coming; and that he was come, and that Jesus of Nazareth was he..."
2. "This was as easy, by observation, to be discerned, as the face of the sky was; even of themselves, without any hints or directions from others..."
3. "Why do ye not of yourselves judge, what is fit and right to be done between man and man, without going to law? And that, in cases which are plain and clear, the consequences of which may be as easily discerned as what weather it will be by the signs in the heavens..."

### John Wesley:
1. Why even of yourselves, without any external sign, judge ye not what is right? - Why do ye not discern and acknowledge the intrinsic excellence of my doctrine?
