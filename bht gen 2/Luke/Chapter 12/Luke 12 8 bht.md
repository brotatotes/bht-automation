# Luke 12:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
If you openly declare your faith in me without hypocrisy, the Son of man will openly acknowledge you before the angels of God on the last day. This confession is not a secretive act, but a public testimony of your allegiance to the Gospel, with the assurance that Christ Himself will proclaim His followers in the presence of heavenly beings.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The point lies in doing it 'before men,' because one has to do it 'despising the shame.'"
2. "But when done, the Lord holds Himself bound to repay it in kind by confessing such 'before the angels of God.'"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The Hebraistic use of εν after ομολογεω both here and in Matthew is admitted by even Moulton (Prolegomena, p. 104)."
2. "The Son of man (ο υιος του ανθρωπου). Here Matthew 10:32 has κ'αγω (I also) as the equivalent."

### John Gill:
1. "Whosoever shall confess me before men, him shall the son of man also confess before the angels of God." 
2. "Christ, the eternal word, will [praise the followers of God] at the great day."

### John Wesley:
1. "If you avoid all hypocrisy, and openly avow my Gospel."
2. "The Son of man shall confess you - before the angels - At the last day."
