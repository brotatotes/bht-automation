### Henry Alford:
1. "So far am I from thinking a description of this kind to be a mere general close, put in by the Evangelist, that I would take it as an accurate and graphic account of the immediate effect of our Lord’s power and irresistible words..."
2. "...shewing the people the ultimate conquest which the Kingdom of God should obtain over all opposition, however strong."
3. "These two parables, found in Matthew as above, and the former of them in Mark 4:30-32, seem to have been again spoken by our Lord at this time, in reference to the progress of His Gospel..."

### Albert Barnes:

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Were ashamed. Rev., more correctly, were put to shame. Glorious things. See on Matthew 11:10. Were done [γινομενοις] . Lit., are being done, denoting their being then in progress.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "All his adversaries...were done by him. The original indicates continued working, which agrees with Matthew 18:2."
2. "All the multitude rejoiced. This does not oppose the view that the miracle occurred in Perea, late in the ministry. Although Galilee had been abandoned by Him, and Jerusalem had been repeatedly hostile, we infer from Matthew 18:2, that He was still heard with gladness in Perea; in fact some such wave of popularity must have preceded the entry into Jerusalem."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "To make one feel ashamed." (καταισχυνω, old verb, to make ashamed)
2. "To blush with shame at their predicament." (passive here)
3. "Sharp contrast in the emotions of the two groups." (rejoiced vs. were put to shame)

### John Gill:
1. "Shame appeared in their countenances; they could not lift up their heads, and look him in the face."
2. "Some were filled with joy, and others with wrath, malice, and envy."
3. "Glorious things have been done by Christ...they make for the glory of all the divine perfections...and these occasion joy and gladness in the Lord's people."
