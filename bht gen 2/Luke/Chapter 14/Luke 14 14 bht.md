# Luke 14:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Happy is he who shall participate in the resurrection of the just and partake in the blessings of the kingdom of God, where he shall eat bread. This refers to those who have a share in the glorious future resurrection, enjoying the eternal fellowship and provision in the heavenly realm.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  14. ]  ἀναστ  .   τ  .   δικ  ., the  first resurrection, here distinctly asserted by our Lord; otherwise τ . δικ . would be vapid and unmeaning. See 1Co 15:22 f.: 1 Thessalonians 4:16 ; Revelation 20:4-5 .

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 14. blessed—acting from
	disinterested, god-like compassion for the wretched.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Blessed in the 'act' of doing good, which furnishes more 'happiness' than riches can give."
2. "Blessed or rewarded 'by God' in the day of judgment."
3. "Then 'God' shall reward those who have done good to the poor and needy from love to the Lord Jesus Christ."

### John Calvin:
1. "Christ pronounces those to be blessed who exercise liberality without any expectation of earthly reward."
2. "They manifestly look to God."
3. "Those who constantly keep in view their own advantage, or who are driven by the gale of popularity, have no right to expect a reward from God."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "This implies that the benevolence has been done without hope of return, excluding the recompense from 'the praise of men.'"
2. "Earthly recompense amounts to nothing; it gives no blessing. All outlay with the hope of return is a mere squandering upon self."
3. "Our Lord, of course, does not here encourage charity for the purpose of obtaining a future reward. The reward comes, but it is still of grace."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "To recompense thee (ανταποδουνα σο). Second aorist active infinitive of this old and common double compound verb, to give back in return."
2. "The reward will come at the resurrection if not before and thou shalt be happy."

### John Gill:
1. "And thou shalt be blessed,.... By God, with an increase of worldly substance, or with spiritual blessings, and with eternal glory and happiness."
2. "For they cannot recompense thee;... yet a retaliation will be made."
3. "For thou shalt be recompensed at the resurrection of the just; when the just shall rise again, which will be the first resurrection."

### John Wesley:
1. "Happy is he that shall eat bread in the kingdom of God." - Alluding to what had just been spoken. It means, he that shall have a part in the resurrection of the just.
