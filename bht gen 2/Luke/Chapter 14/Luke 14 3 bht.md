# Luke 14:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus, in response to the thoughts of the lawyers and Pharisees, addressed them directly. He was aware of their intentions and understood the motives behind their actions. His reply was not solely in reaction to their spoken words, but rather to the thoughts that were brewing within them.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "To 'answer,' in the Scriptures, does not always imply... that anything had been said before."
2. "He knew that they were watching him... He, therefore, proposed the question to them."
3. "When it was asked, they could not say that it was not lawful."

### John Calvin:
1. "Christ first puts the question to them, and he does so for the purpose of guarding against offense."
2. "He intended to show by this example, that he did not inconsiderately perform miracles on Sabbath, because he was prepared to assign a reason for what he did."
3. "Christ treats with utter indifference their opinion about his action, because it was evident that they intentionally sought out an occasion of offense."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Answering, i.e., the thoughts of the Pharisees."
2. "This unexpected question evidently embarrassed them."
3. "If they answered yes, the occasion of finding fault was taken away; if no, they could be charged with want of compassion."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Jesus answered the thoughts of those mentioned in verse Luke 14:1."
2. "The dilemma forestalled any question by them."
3. "They became silent, more so than before."

### John Gill:
1. "And Jesus answering, spake unto the lawyers and Pharisees... who knew their designs upon him, and the thoughts of their hearts..."
2. "The occasion of the question was the object before him, whom Christ had a compassionate regard to, and determined to cure..."
3. "...he knew full well, what was agreeable to the law of God, and what was not; and what also were the traditions of their elders, which they held, and which allowed of healing on the sabbath day, where life was in danger."

### John Wesley:
1. And Jesus answering, spake — Answering the thoughts which he saw rising in their hearts.
