## BHT:
Jesus took the man by the hand and healed him, miraculously causing the swelling in his body to disappear. The power that Jesus possessed, demonstrated by the ability to calm the sea with a simple rebuke, was also evident in this healing touch. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Albert Barnes:
1. "They held their peace - They were silent. They 'could' not say it was not lawful, for the law did not forbid it. If it had they would have said it."
2. "Here was the time for them to make objections if they had any, and not after the man was healed; and as they 'made' no objection 'then,' they could not with consistency afterward."
3. "They were, therefore, effectually silenced and confounded by the Saviour."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Took. Took hold of him. Luke 20:20; 1 Timothy 6:12.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Formalism is always thus inconsistent. Their silence was a confession of defeat, however." 
2. "They could attend feasts on the Sabbath, but could not say that it was right to heal the sick."
3. "He was not a guest. The rebuke was not given until after the man had been sent away."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Took hold of him and healed him."
2. "Dismissed from the company to get him away from these critics."

### John Gill:
1. "He took him by the hand, or laid his hands on him; he touched him, and, it may be, stroked the part affected, and in an instant the prodigious swelling of his body came down."
2. "For he who at his rebuke could dry up the sea, could by a touch dry up such a quantity of water, as was in this man's body."
3. "And then he dismissed him from the table and company, and he went home perfectly cured."
