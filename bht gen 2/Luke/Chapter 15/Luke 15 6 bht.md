# Luke 15:6 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Christ rejoices as He brings His lost sheep into His house, which can be understood as the church. The friends of Christ, the saints, bear friendship to Him through their affection, attachment, and strict devotion to Him and His Gospel. The joy experienced is mutual, both for Christ and His friends, as they are brought together in the joyous reunion of the lost sheep.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "In this return to His house, must be understood the whole course of seeking and finding which the good Shepherd, either by Himself or His agents, now pursues in each individual case, even until He brings the lost sheep home into heaven to himself."
2. "Not in reality, so that it should not take place till the death of the penitent, but proleptically, till the name is written in heaven; till the sinner is penitent."
3. "The φίλοι καὶ γείτονες = the angels (and spirits of just men made perfect?)"


### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The principle here is, that one feels exuberant joy to be almost too much for himself to bear alone."
2. "...is positively relieved by having others to share it with him."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. With me. "Not with the sheep. Our life is his joy" (Gregory, cited by Trench).

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Hundred measures, or, 'baths,' = the Ephah in dry measure, nearly ten gallons."
2. "Take thy bill, lit, 'writings.' The document in the steward's hands, showing the obligation."
3. "And write fifty, i.e., alter the figure. The old bond is not destroyed, but returned to the debtor to be thus altered."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Joy demands fellowship."
2. "This picture of the Good Shepherd has captured the eye of many artists through the ages."

### John Gill:
1. "The house, or home, to which Christ comes and brings thither his lost sheep on his shoulders when found, is either the church of God, which is Christ's house and home..."
2. "the friends of Christ are the saints...bore friendship to him by their great affection to his person, by their attachment to his cause and interest, by their strict regard to his Gospel and the truths of it..."
3. "the joy of Christ, and his friends, is mutual on this occasion; Christ rejoices himself, and his friends with him..."
