# Luke 17:24 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The coming of the Son of man will be as swift and far-reaching as lightning, enlightening the entire earth. This refers to his imminent arrival to judge the Jewish nation and his ultimate appearance on the last day to judge all. His day signifies his kingdom, glory, and powerful manifestation.

### Henry Alford:
1.  24.   ἐκ τῆς  …   εἰς τὴν  … ] Supply χώρας … χώραν .

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The Lord speaks here of His coming and manifestation in a prophetically indefinite manner, and in these preparatory words blends into one the distinctive epochs."
2. "When the whole polity of the Jews, civil and ecclesiastical alike, was broken up at once, and its continuance rendered impossible by the destruction of Jerusalem, it became as manifest to all as the lightning of heaven that the kingdom of God had ceased to exist in its old, and had entered on a new and perfectly different form."
3. "So it may be again, ere its final and greatest change at the personal coming of Christ, and of which the words in their highest sense are alone true."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Lighteneth [αστραπτουσα] . Only here and ch. 24:4.

### Philip Schaff:
1. Luke 17:24.  For.  Neither time nor place can be determined, for the coming will be sudden and universally perceived. See on Matthew 24:27.
	

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The second coming will be sudden and universally visible."
2. "There are still some poor souls who are waiting in Jerusalem under the delusion that Jesus will come there and nowhere else."

### John Gill:
1. "For as the lightning that lighteneth out of the one [part] under heaven.... and shineth unto the other part under heaven; enlightens the earth, which is under the heaven."
2. "So shall also the son of man be in his day: which is not to be understood of the swift progress of the Gospel, ... but of his sudden coming, first to take vengeance on the Jewish nation for their rejection of him, and then at the last day, to judge both quick and dead."
3. "By his day, is meant his kingdom and glory, or his appearance with power, and great glory."

### John Wesley:
1. So shall also the Son of man be — So swift, so wide, shall his appearing be: In his day - The last day.
