### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Cried [εκραζεν] . A stronger word than ejbohsen, cried, in the previous verse, which is merely to cry or shout, while this is to cry clamorously; to scream or shriek." 
2. "To be brought unto [αχθηναι προς] . Used by Luke alone in the sense of bringing the sick to Christ." 
3. "He also uses the compound verb prsoagw, which was a common medical term for bringing the sick to a physician, both in that and in other senses."

### John Gill:
1. "He neither dropped the character of Christ, nor his request to him..."
2. "...called out more loudly, and with greater vehemence, earnestness, and importunity..."
3. "So persons sensible of their need of Christ, and of his worth, excellency, and ability, are not to be discouraged from an application to him, by whatsoever they meet with from men, or devils."
