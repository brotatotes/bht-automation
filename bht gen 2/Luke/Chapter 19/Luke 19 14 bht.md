# Luke 19:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The citizens of this nobleman, who is the Lord Jesus, rejected and opposed him, demonstrating their hatred towards him. They even went so far as to send a message after him, vehemently declaring that they did not want him to reign over them. This rejection and contempt was shown through their refusal to accept his Gospel or submit to his authority.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The nobleman, son of a king, is the Lord Jesus; the kingdom is that over his own citizens, the Jews."
2. "The parable has a double import: suited both to the disciples, and the multitude."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "his citizens—His proper subjects; meaning the Jews, who expressly repudiating our Lord's claims said, 'We have no king but Cæsar.'"
2. "In Christendom, these correspond to infidel rejecters of Christianity, as distinguished from professed Christians."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "His discontented subjects, fearing what would be the character of his reign, sent an embassy to remonstrate against his being appointed as the ruler."
2. "By this part of it Christ designed to denote that the Jews would reject 'him' - the Messiah, and would say that they did not desire him to reign over them."
3. "So it is true of all sinners that they do not 'wish' Jesus to reign over them, and, if it were possible, would cast him off, and never submit to his reign."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The world hates our Lord unreasonably, and therefore opposes Him."
2. "This positive opposition to the Lord Jesus has manifested itself, ever since He went to receive His kingdom, mainly in persecution of His servants."
3. "Their cry to heaven is the message of hatred from the world; ‘we will not that,’ etc."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  His citizens  (ο πολιτα αυτου). That actually happened with Archelaus. 

### John Gill:
1. "But his citizens hated him...these hated him with a mortal hatred, as appeared by their traducing his person in the most opprobrious manner; vilifying his doctrine as false; ascribing his miracles to a diabolical influence; and by persecuting his disciples and followers..."
2. "...and sent a message after him...when they not only mocked them... but laid hold on them, and put them in hold, even in the common prison, and persecuted them from place to place..."
3. "...we will not have this man to reign over us: they would neither receive his Gospel, nor submit to his ordinances; but put them away from them, and judged themselves unworthy of everlasting life: and this is the language of every graceless soul..."

### John Wesley:
1. "Such were those of Jerusalem, hated him, and sent an embassy after him." 
2. "In such a solemn manner did the Jews protest, as it were, before God, that Christ should not reign over them." 
3. "This man - So they call him in contempt."
