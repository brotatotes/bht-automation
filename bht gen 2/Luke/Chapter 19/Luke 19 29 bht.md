As Jesus spoke these words, he approached a place where the villages of Bethphage and Bethany intersected, at the foot of the Mount of Olives. This location, known for its abundance of olive trees, was near Jerusalem, providing a clear picture of the proximity of Jesus to the city.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The name, when thus put, must be accentuated ἐλαιών."
2. "Luke uses this same expression elsewhere, see reff."
3. "Josephus has διὰ τοῦ ἐλαιῶνος ὄρους, Antt. vii. 9. 2."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 29-38. Bethphage—"house
	of figs," a village which with Bethany lay along the further
	side of Mount Olivet, east of Jerusalem.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Bethphage. See on Matthew 21:1.

### Philip Schaff:
1. Luke 19:29.  And when he had thus spoken,  etc. On the afternoon of Friday, the 8th of Nisan. He could reach the neighborhood of Bethany before sundown.
	

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Unto Bethphage and Bethany (εις Βηθφαγη κα Βηθανια). Both indeclinable forms of the Hebrew or Aramaic names."
2. "In the Greek it is just a matter of accent (circumflex or acute) Olivet is correct in Acts 1:12."

### John Gill:
1. "He was come nigh,.... The other evangelists, Matthew and Mark, add "unto Jerusalem"; but this Luke designs afterwards..." 
2. "he was come nigh to Bethphage and Bethany; two tracts of land which reached from Mount Olivet to Jerusalem; so that when he was there, he was nigh unto the city."
3. "at the mount, called [the Mount] of Olives; or 'Elaion', as the Ethiopic version, which retains the Greek word for it; and which has its name from the great number of olive trees that grew upon it."

### John Wesley:
1. He drew nigh to the place where the borders of Bethphage and Bethany met, which was at the foot of the mount of Olives. Matthew 21:1; Mark 11:1.
