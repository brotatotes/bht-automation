## BHT:
The disciples, filled with awe and amazement at the miracles they had witnessed, could not contain their joy and began to rejoice and praise God with a loud voice. As they descended from the Mount of Olives, Jerusalem came into view, and their excitement reached its climax. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "not merely local, 'at the declivity of,' but expressing the result of ἐγγίζοντες just about to descend the Mount of Olives."
2. "the δύναμις, which dwelt mostly on their minds, was the raising of Lazaraus, John 12:17-18: but as this perhaps was not known to Luke, we must understand him to mean, all that they had seen during their journey with Him."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The language here is very grand, intended to express a burst of admiration..."
2. "...far wider and deeper than ever had been witnessed before."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The descent. Two distinct sights of Jerusalem are caught on this route, an inequality of ground hiding it for a time after one has first seen it."
2. "What is seen is only Mount Zion, now, for the most part, a rough field, crowned with the mosque of David, and the angle of the western walls, but then covered with houses to its base, and surmounted by the castle of Herod, on the supposed site of the palace of David."
3. "It was at this point that the shout of triumph burst forth from the multitude."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "At the descent of the Mount of Olives. On the brow of the hill, as Jerusalem came in sight. A fitting place for the culmination of their enthusiasm."
2. "All the mighty works, etc. All the miracles performed on this journey, but doubtless with special reference to the raising of Lazarus, from whose home they had just come."
3. "(Comp. John 12:9; John 12:17-18.)"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "This was the first sight of the city on this route which is soon obscured in the descent."
2. "It was a shout of triumph from the multitude with their long pent-up enthusiasm, restrained no longer by the parable of the pounds."
3. "The climax had come, Lazarus, Bartimaeus, and the rest."

### John Gill:
1. "The whole multitude of the disciples...began to rejoice, and praise God, with a loud voice, for all the mighty works that they had seen."
2. "Calling to mind the many miracles he had wrought...they might strongly conclude that he must be the Messiah."
3. "Being filled with joy and gladness, at the remembrance of these things and with thankfulness to God, they lifted up their voices together, and exerted them to the uttermost, and made the air ring with their shouts and acclamations of praise to God, on this occasion."

### John Wesley:
1. "The whole multitude began to praise God — Speaking at once, as it seems, from a Divine impulse, words which most of them did not understand."
