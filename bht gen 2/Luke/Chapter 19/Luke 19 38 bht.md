## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  38. ]  ἐν οὐρανῷ  =   ἐν ὑψίστοις  , and was probably added by them to fill out the parallelism.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Hosanna," that is, "Save now," the words of Mark 11:10, which were understood to refer to Messiah; and so they add, "to the Son of David, blessed is He that cometh in the name of the Lord (Psalms 118:26), Hosanna in the highest."
2. "This was the very loftiest style in which He could be saluted as the promised Deliverer."
3. "peace, &c.—(See on Luke 2:13)."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Peace in heaven, and glory in the highest. Here Luke, by a poetic parallelism, paraphrases the ‘Hosanna’ mentioned by the other Evangelists."
2. "Master, or, ‘Teacher.’ They objected to the cry of the disciples, because it recognized Him as more than a ‘Teacher.’ They would, however, hold Him responsible for what they held to be unwise and unwarranted enthusiasm."
3. "If these should hold their peace, the stones, etc. A proverbial expression, to show that this outburst could not and ought not to be restrained, and thus a most pointed rebuke of the objectors."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The Messianic hopes of the people were now all ablaze with expectation of immediate realization."
2. "They are singing from the Hallel in their joy that Jesus at last is making public proclamation of his Messiahship."
3. "This language reminds one strongly of the song of the angels at the birth of Jesus."

### John Gill:
1. "Saying, blessed be the King,.... The King Messiah, the King of Israel, the son of David, the Christ of God; so the Ethiopic version adds, 'blessed be the King of Israel'"
2. "peace in heaven; all heavenly peace and prosperity attend him; or let peace be made with God in heaven, by the Prince of Peace on earth, for sinful men"
3. "glory be given to God for peace, life, and salvation by his son; and that in the highest heavens, by the angels there, as well as by men on earth, and in the highest notes and strains."

### John Wesley:
1. Peace in heaven — God being reconciled to man.
