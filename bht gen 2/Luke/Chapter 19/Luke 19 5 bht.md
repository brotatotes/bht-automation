# Luke 19:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Zaccheus must have experienced a whirlwind of emotions upon hearing someone who not only knew his name but also understood the depths of his heart! This encounter is a beautiful testament to the divine nature of Christ, who sees beyond our exterior and recognizes the innermost desires of our souls. It is a reminder that we are never truly alone or unknown, for Christ knows us intimately and is eager to meet us exactly where we are.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The probability is, that our Lord’s supernatural knowledge of man is intended to be understood as the means of his knowing Zacchæus."
2. "But of what possible import can such a question be, when the narrative plainly shews us that Jesus saw into his heart?"
3. "Cannot He who knows the thoughts, call by the name also?"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "He calleth His own sheep by name and leadeth them out."
2. "Our Lord invites Himself, and in 'royal' style, which waits not for invitations, but as the honor is done to the subject, not the sovereign, announces the purpose of royalty to partake of the subject's hospitalities."
3. "Manifestly our Lord speaks as knowing how the privilege would be appreciated."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It is but one among a thousand instances where the Saviour goes, in bestowing mercies, far beyond the desert, the desire, or the expectation of men."
2. "Jesus was willing to encourage efforts to come to him, and his benevolence prompted him to gratify the desires of the man who was solicitous to see him."
3. "He does not disdain the mansions of the rich any more than he does the dwelling-places of the poor, provided there be a humble heart."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Adopting the royal style which was familiar to him."
2. "Commends the loyalty of a vassal in the most delicate manner by freely exacting his services."

### John Calvin:
1. "It is a remarkable instance of favor, that the Lord anticipates Zaccheus, and does not wait for his invitation..."
2. "But we need not wonder, if he bestows this honor on one who was already drawn to him by a secret movement of the Spirit..."
3. "Besides, the great readiness of Zaccheus to obey, his hastening to come down from the tree, and his joy in receiving Christ, exhibit still more clearly the power and guidance of the Holy Spirit..."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The knowledge of his name is less remarkable than the knowledge of his heart."
2. "The distance was not too great for an afternoon's walk."
3. "All events work out God's purpose, but His purpose is to save sinners."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  Make haste and come down  (σπευσας καταβηθ). Simultaneous aorist active participle (σπευσας) with the second aorist active imperative. "Come down in a hurry." 

### John Gill:
1. "Christ knows where his people are, and where to find them..."
2. "He comes to them before they come to him; and is found of them, and finds them, who sought him not..."
3. "His name was written in the Lamb's book of life, and so must be known to Christ..."

### John Wesley:
1. "What a strange mixture of passions must Zaccheus have now felt, hearing one speak, as knowing both his name and his heart!"
