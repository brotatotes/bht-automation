# Luke 2:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Mary held onto and carefully considered all the things that had been spoken about her child, weighing them against what had been revealed to her by the angel and others. She kept silent about these things, not wanting to appear boastful or misunderstood, instead waiting for the appropriate time for them to be fully revealed.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "in her memory."
2. "those spoken by the shepherds: or its Hebraistic, as above, Luke 2:15, which is more probable all these things now spoken of."
3. "revolving them comparing one with another."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "A 'mother' forgets none of those things which occur respecting her children. Everything they do or suffer - everything that is said of them, is treasured up in her mind; and often she thinks of those things, and anxiously seeks what they may indicate respecting the future character and welfare of her child."
2. "Pondered - Weighed. This is the original meaning of the word 'weighed.' She kept them; she revolved them; she 'weighed' them in her mind, giving to each circumstance its just importance, and anxiously seeking what it might indicate respecting her child."
3. "In her heart - In her mind. She 'thought' of these things often and anxiously."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The word signifies not merely to guard, but to keep, as the result of guarding."
2. "Hence the compound verb is very expressive: kept, sun, with or within herself: closely."
3. "Pondered [συμβαλλουσα]. The present participle, pondering. Lit., bringing together: comparing and weighing facts."

### John Calvin:
1. "Mary kept Mary’s diligence in contemplating the works of God is laid before us for two reasons; first, to inform us, that this treasure was laid up in her heart, for the purpose of being published to others at the proper time..."
2. "For, if we are wise, it will be the chief employment, and the great object of our life, to consider with attention those works of God which build up our faith."
3. "Mary kept all these things...relates to her memory. Συμβάλλειν signifies to throw together, — to collect the several events which agreed in proving the glory of Christ, so that they might form one body."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "She kept, or more exactly, she was keeping, continued to keep, in her memory, all these sayings, i.e. all these things now spoken of."
2. "Revolving, comparing, reflecting upon them in the quietude of her heart."
3. "This accurate detail favors the view that the account was derived, at least indirectly, from her."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "She kept on keeping together all these things. They were meat and drink to her."
2. "Mary would go over each detail in the words of Gabriel and of the shepherds and compare the sayings with the facts so far developed and brood over it all with a mother's high hopes and joy."

### John Gill:
1. "But Mary kept all these things....and pondered [them] in her heart"
2. "or compared them in her mind, with what had been said to herself by the angel, and also by her husband, as well as what was said by Elisabeth at the time she made her a visit"
3. "but she said nothing of them to others, lest she should be thought an enthusiast, or a vain boaster; and therefore left things, till time should make a discovery of them in a proper way, and in the best season."
