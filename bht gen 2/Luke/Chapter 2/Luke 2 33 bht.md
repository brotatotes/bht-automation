# Luke 2:33 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Joseph and his mother were filled with wonder and amazement at the words spoken to them. While they may not have fully comprehended the significance of what was being said, they recognized the greatness and importance of the message. This shows the human nature in their response, as they were in awe of the revelation given to them.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Our Lord Himself would not speak of him thus, see Luke 2:49."
2. "In the simplicity of the narrative we may read οἱ γονεῖς αὐτοῦ and such expressions, without any danger of forgetting the momentous history of the Conception and Nativity."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "His father was and his mother wondering; the finite verb in the singular agreeing with the father, while the plural participle agrees with both."
2. "As usual, this combination of finite verb and participle denotes continuance or progression: they were marvelling while Simeon was speaking."
3. "The best texts read oJ pathr aujtou, his father."

### John Calvin:
1. "Luke does not say that they were astonished at it as a new thing, but that they contemplated with reverence and embraced with becoming admiration this prediction of the Spirit uttered by the lips of Simeon."
2. "We learn from this example that, when we have once come to possess a right faith, we ought to collect, on every hand, whatever may aid in giving to it additional strength."
3. "That man has made great proficiency in the word of God, who does not fail to admire whatever he reads or hears every day, that contributes to his unceasing progress in faith."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The use of [the term 'father'] by Luke, in the legal and popular sense, involves no contradiction of his previous statements."
2. "To have avoided the term would look like the over-carefulness of an inventor."
3. "Their wonder is made more prominent than their faith."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He by no means intends to deny the Virgin Birth of Jesus so plainly stated in Luke 1:34-38. He merely employs here the language of ordinary custom."
2. "This is due to the wide space between copula and participle."
3. "It is a mark of unusual insight for others to see so much that is obvious to the parent."

### John Gill:
1. "They marveled at those things which were spoken of him; the child Jesus... they wondered, that a stranger to them and the child, coming into the temple at this instant, should have such a revelation made to him, and be able to say the things he did."
2. "Moreover, there is no need to confine this passage to what were said by Simeon, but it may reach to, and include every thing... and they were astonished, at the exact agreement there was between them."

### John Wesley:
1. Joseph and his mother marvelled at those things which were spoken — For they did not thoroughly understand them.
