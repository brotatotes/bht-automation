# Luke 2:36 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Anna, a woman of great age, was a recipient of God's grace and served as a messenger of the good news of redemption by Christ. She had a deep understanding of God and was able to interpret oracles. Anna's advanced age is noted to highlight her purity and her honorable conduct during her marriage. She saw the face of Jesus Christ and proclaimed the knowledge of God to others, making her a remarkable witness of God's presence in a time when few possessed the gift of prophecy.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Evidence that 'the last times' in which God was to 'pour out His Spirit upon all flesh' were at hand."
2. "The distinction of tribes, though practically destroyed by the captivity, was well enough known up to their final dispersion."
3. "If she married at the earliest marriageable age, twelve years, she could not at this time be less than a hundred three years old."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The tribe of Aser, or Asher, dwelt in the northern part of the land of Canaan."
2. "Why Anna was called a prophetess is not known."
3. "She herself had foretold future events, being inspired."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "A prophetess [προφητις] . Only here and Revelation 2:20."
2. "Asher. That tribe was celebrated in tradition for the beauty of its women, and their fitness to be wedded to high priests or kings."
3. "Of great age [προβεβηκυια εν ημεραις πολλαις] . Lit., advanced in many days."

### John Calvin:
1. "Luke mentions not more than two persons who received Christ; and this is intended to teach us, that whatever belongs to God, however small it may be, ought to be preferred by us to the whole world."
2. "But, as the Spirit of God, whose presence was not at all enjoyed by those rulers, dwelt in Simeon and Anna, those two persons are entitled to greater reverence than an immense multitude of those whose pride is swelled by nothing but empty titles."
3. "The Spirit of prophecy still shone in a very few, who served as tokens to attest the doctrine of the Law and the Jewish religion, till the coming of Christ."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "One Anna. ‘One’ is supplied to relieve the English construction."
2. "A prophetess, so called previous to this time."
3. "She was of a great age. From this point to ‘fourscore and four years’ (Luke 2:37), the description is parenthetical, referring to the particulars of her great age."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The word προφητις occurs in the N.T. only here and Revelation 2:20."
2. "In old Greek writers it means a woman who interprets oracles."
3. "Montefiore makes it 106 as she was 15 when married, married 7 years, a widow 84."

### John Gill:
1. "One that had the grace of God herself, and was a publisher of the glad tidings of grace and redemption by Christ, to others."
2. "This woman was the daughter of Phanuel...And now Phanuel's daughter both saw and gave the light of the knowledge of God, in the face of Jesus Christ, and now beheld his face in the flesh, who is God over all, blessed forever."
3. "She was of a great age...and had lived with her husband seven years from her virginity...this is mentioned to observe her chastity, that she was in her virginity, or a chaste virgin, when she became a wife...and that she lived honestly and honorably, with her husband, during the term of her marriage state."
