# Luke 2:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Mary, who was Joseph's espoused wife, went with him to be taxed and enrolled. She joined Joseph in this journey because she belonged to the same family of David and the same city of Bethlehem. Despite being great with child, Mary complied with the edict and showed obedience to the civil authorities through her actions.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 5. espoused wife—now, without
	doubt, taken home to him, as related in Matthew 1:18;
	Matthew 25:6.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "To be taxed with Mary. We may read either, went up with Mary, denoting merely the fact of her accompanying him; or, to enroll himself with Mary, implying that both their names must be registered."
2. "Espoused. Not merely betrothed."
3. "Great with child [εγκυω] . See on Chapter Luke 1:24. Only here in New Testament."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Perhaps the prophecy respecting Bethlehem (Micah 5:1; comp. Matthew 2:6) was in her mind."
2. "Some think that she was an heiress, having possessions in Bethlehem, and therefore obliged to appear there to represent an extinct family."
3. "The verse sheds no light on the question, whether she too were of the house of David."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "To enrol himself with Mary (απογραψασθα συν Μαριαμ)... Mary's family register was in Bethlehem also and that she also belonged to the house of David."
2. "It is possible to connect 'with Mary' far back with 'went up' (ανεβη) in verse Luke 2:4, but it is unnatural to do so."
3. "There is no real reason for doubting that Mary herself was a descendant of David and that is the obvious way to understand Luke's genealogy of Jesus in Luke 3:23-38)."

### John Gill:
1. "To be taxed with Mary his espoused wife... she came along with him to be taxed and enrolled also, because she was of the same family of David, and belonged to the same city."
2. "Being great with child... though in such circumstances, was obliged by this edict."
3. "This was an instance, and an example, of obedience to civil magistrates."
