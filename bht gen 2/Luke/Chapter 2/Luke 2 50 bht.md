# Luke 2:50 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Having fulfilled his responsibility as the protector of Jesus' virgin mother and having provided an exemplar of subjection to both parents, it seems likely that Joseph's presence is not mentioned again in the scriptures because he passed away shortly after this time.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The marvel of this condescension lies in its coming after such a scene, and such an assertion of His higher Sonship."
2. "From this time we have no more mention of Joseph. The next we hear is of his 'mother and brethren' (Matthew 12:46); whence it is inferred, that between this time and the commencement of our Lord's public life, Joseph died."
3. "Having now served the double end of being the protector of our Lord's Virgin—mother, and affording Himself the opportunity of presenting a matchless pattern of subjection to both parents."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They understood not ... - It is remarkable that they did not understand Jesus in this, but it shows how slow persons are to believe."
2. "Even his parents, after all that had taken place, did not seem to comprehend that 'he' was to be the Saviour of people, or if they did, they understood it in a very imperfect manner."

### Marvin Vincent:

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "My Father is God," Jesus had virtually said, "and I must be in His house."
2. "Even we do not yet fully understand" (Bruce) what Jesus the boy here said.

### John Gill:
1. "And they understood not the saying,.... What he meant by his Father's house, or his Father's business, and the necessity of his being there..."
2. "...about that which he spake unto them; at that time, and as above related."

### John Wesley:
1. It is observable that Joseph is not mentioned after this time; whence it is probable, he did not live long after.
