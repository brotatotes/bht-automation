# Luke 20:20 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
These spies were sent with the intention to catch Jesus in his words, observing his actions and waiting for the perfect opportunity to accuse him. They pretended to be righteous and conscientious individuals, hoping to gain insight into the truth and act justly. Their goal was to find something in Jesus' speech that could be twisted and misconstrued as sedition or treason, ultimately leading to his arrest and execution.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "having watched an opportunity."
2. "men suborned, instructed and arranged for that purpose."
3. "that they might lay hold of him by some saying; = αὐτὸν ἀγρεύσωσιν λόγῳ."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 20-26. sent forth—after
	consulting (Matthew 22:15) on the
	best plan. 
	spies—"of the
	Pharisees and Herodians" (Matthew 22:15- :). See Mark 3:6.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Spies, from persons sent in for the purpose of espionage." 
2. "Feigning, which feigned."
3. "The former, the Roman power in general; the latter, the specific authority of the official."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Feigning themselves to be righteous. They should come to Him, as though their consciences, not the craft of His enemies, had prompted the following question."
2. "That they (the foiled Sanhedrists) might take hold of his speech. Both the person and the thing taken hold of are expressed in the original."
3. "Unto the magistrate, or, 'ruler,' the civil power, etc. The Roman power in general is first spoken of, then the specific authority to which they wished to deliver Him, that of the governor."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They posed as scrupulous persons with a difficulty of conscience."
2. "These spies are for the purpose of catching hold of the talk of Jesus if they can get a grip anywhere."
3. "The trap is all set now and ready to be sprung by these 'spies'."

### John Gill:
1. "And they watched him,.... What he said, and what he did, and where he went, that they might take an advantage against him, or know where he was, to send to him, as they should think fit, and take the best opportunity of so doing."
2. "And sent forth spies which should feign themselves just men: of virtue and religion, conscientious men, that would do nothing but what was just and right, and were desirous of being exactly informed of the truth of things, that they might act right in every punctilio."
3. "That might take hold of his words; improve them, and form a charge upon them, of sedition and treason: that so they might deliver him unto the power and authority of the governor; the Roman governor, and by him be put to death."

### John Wesley:
1. Just men — Men of a tender conscience.To take hold of his discourse — If he answered as they hoped he would. Matthew 22:16; Mark 12:12.
