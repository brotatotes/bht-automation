# Luke 21:18 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Not a hair of your head shall perish - It does not mean that they will be preserved from all bodily harm, but rather that nothing will happen to them apart from God's providence. He will watch over them and ensure that they receive their full reward at the appointed time.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  18. ] Not  literally , but  really true; not corporeally, but in that real and only  life which the disciple of Christ possesses.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "He had just said they should be put to death; showing that this precious promise is far above immunity from mere bodily harm." 
2. "Sad illustrations of the effect of abounding iniquity in cooling the love of faithful disciples we have in the Epistle of James, written about this period referred to, and too frequently ever since."
3. "God never sends judgment without previous warning; and there can be no doubt that the Jews, already dispersed over most known countries, had nearly all heard the Gospel 'as a witness,' before the end of the Jewish state."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "And not a hair of your head shall perish. Some would add: ‘as long as you are needed for the service of Christ;’" 
2. "Others refer it to the safety of the mass of Christians at the time of the destruction of Jerusalem."
3. "But the fact that Luke 21:16 points to the death of some makes a reference to the spiritual life more probable."

### John Gill:
1. "They should be no losers in the main; all things should work together for their good."
2. "Though even they should be put to death, yet that would be to their advantage, since instead of a temporal, troublesome life, they should enjoy an eternal and happy one."
3. "This cannot be understood of entire preservation from all corporeal damages and hurt; seeing it is, before declared, that they should be put into prisons, and some of them put to death."

### John Wesley:
1. Not a hair of your head — A proverbial expression, shall perish - Without the special providence of God. And then, not before the time, nor without A full reward.
