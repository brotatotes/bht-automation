# Luke 21:21 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
When the time comes for the destruction of Jerusalem, those who are in the city should flee to the mountains. It is Jerusalem, not Judea, that is in the midst of this statement. The urgency of leaving the city is emphasized, as it is important to depart before their escape route is blocked by the united forces. Those who are in the surrounding areas should also avoid entering the city at all costs.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "In the midst of her, i.e., Jerusalem, not Judea, as appears from the last clause of the verse."
2. "This Gospel does not contain so full directions in regard to the flight, as that written more especially for Jewish Christians (Matthew)."

### John Gill:
1. "Then let them which are in Judaea flee to the mountains." 
2. "And let them which are in the midst of it; either of Judea, as the preceding clause seems to direct the sense; or in Jerusalem, and which indeed was in the midst of Judea."
3. "And let not them that are in the countries; either foreign countries, or in towns and villages; enter thereinto."

### John Wesley:
1. "Let them that are in the midst of it...depart out of it, before their retreat is cut off by the uniting of the forces near the city."
2. "Let not them that are in the adjacent countries by any means enter into it."
