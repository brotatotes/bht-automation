# Luke 22:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
With fervent desire, Jesus longed to eat the Passover with his disciples before his impending suffering. This deep desire reflected his intense longing to manifest himself to his disciples during this significant farewell. Additionally, he sought to establish the profound memorial of his sacrificial death, not only for the benefit of his immediate disciples but also for the entire Church. This desire was at the very core of his prayers and intercession.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "This is the only instance in the Gospels, of the absolute use of πάσχω, as in the Creed, 'He suffered.'"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "With desire...desired—'earnestly have I longed...sore longedst.' Why? It was to be His last 'before He suffered'—and so became 'Christ our Passover sacrificed for us' (1 Corinthians 5:7), when it was 'fulfilled in the Kingdom of God,' the typical ordinance thenceforth disappearing."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "With desire I have desired - This is a Hebrew form of expression, and means 'I have greatly desired.'"
2. "The reasons why he desired this we may suppose to have been: Before I suffer - Before I die."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. With desire I have desired. Expressing intense desire. Compare John 3:29, rejoiceth with joy; Acts 4:17, threaten with threatening. 19 - 20. Compare Matthew 26:26-29. Mark 14:22-25. 1 Corinthians 11:23-25.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "With desire I have desired. A Hebrew form of expression, denoting strong desire."
2. "One ground of the strong desire was the certainty that it would be the last one, hence peculiarly solemn and important."
3. "The certainty that this was the last Passover with them rested on the certainty of His sufferings for them; hence the affectionateness of His desire, that before His Passion He might have this privilege."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "With desire I have desired." 
2. "A Hebraism common in the LXX." 
3. "Peculiar to Luke is all this verse."

### John Gill:
1. "With desire have I desired to eat this passover with you before I suffer." 
2. "Our Lord may allude to this; but this was not the thing he meant; nor merely does he say this on account of the passover, as it was God's ordinance; though as he was made under the law, and that was in his heart, he had a great regard to it, and a delight in it, which he had shown in his frequent and constant attendance on it from his youth."
3. "His desire is towards their being with him to all eternity, and which he delighted in the fore views of from eternity; and is the joy set before him, and which carried him through his sufferings and death; and is the amount and accomplishment of all his prayers and intercession."

### John Wesley:
1. "With desire have I desired — That is, I have earnestly desired it."
2. "He desired it, both for the sake of his disciples, to whom he desired to manifest himself farther, at this solemn parting."
3. "And for the sake of his whole Church, that he might institute the grand memorial of his death."
