## BHT:
In the midst of Jesus' distress and temptation, God sent an angel to physically strengthen him, ensuring that his body would not succumb to the overwhelming pressure. This strengthening was a reminder of the future glory and honor that awaited Jesus after his suffering and death, assuring him that his people would be eternally saved. Through this passage, we witness the compassionate care and support of God during our most challenging moments.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "With the early and weighty evidence there cited in favour of the passage, it is impossible that it should have been an apocryphal insertion."
2. "The strengthening by means of the angel is physical and the appearance likewise."
3. "This strengthening probably took place between the first and the second prayer; and the effect of it is the ἐκτενέστερον προσηύχετο of Luke 22:44, and the entire resignation expressed in the second and third prayer of Matthew’s narrative."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The word most commonly used in the New Testament of seeing visions."
2. "Strengthening. Only here and Acts 9:19."
3. "Used transitively only by Hippocrates and Luke."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "An actual coming of an angel, not merely a spiritual accession of strength."
2. "Some think it was a physical strengthening, the imparting to His body, so overwhelmed in this conflict, new power to endure, to drink the cup which would not be removed."
3. "Neither of these is inconsistent with proper views of the Person of Christ. In fact, it is simplest to suppose that both body and soul received direct supplies of strength in this hour of deepest trial."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  An angel  (αγγελος). The angels visited Jesus at the close of the three temptations at the beginning of his ministry (Matthew 4:11). Here the angel comes during the conflict. 

### John Gill:
1. "he came from heaven, and was one of the angels of heaven, sent by God on this occasion"
2. "under his present distress, against the terrors of Satan, and the fears of death"
3. "observing to him the glory and honour he should be crowned with, after his sufferings and death, find the complete salvation of his people"

### John Wesley:
1. Strengthening him — Lest his body should sink and die before the time.
