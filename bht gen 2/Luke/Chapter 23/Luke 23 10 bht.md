### Henry Alford:
1.  10. ] The accusations, of  worldly kingship and of  blasphemy , would probably be here  united , as Herod was a Jew, and able to appreciate the latter.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 10. stood and vehemently accused
	him—no doubt both of treason before the king, and
	of blasphemy, for the king was a Jew.

### Albert Barnes:
1. Vehemently accused him - Violently or unjustly accused him, endeavoring to make it appear that he had been guilty of sedition in Herod’s province.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Vehemently [ευτονως] . Only here and Acts 18:28, of the preaching of Apollos. Originally the word means well - strung; hence, in medical language, of a well - toned body.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Pilate had sent them there."
2. "There is no hint that Herod took any steps toward real investigation."
3. "Finding his curiosity was not to be gratified, he treats the case with contempt."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They stood by while Herod quizzed Jesus and when he refused to answer, they broke loose with their accusations like a pack of hounds with full voice."
2. "Like a pack of hounds with full voice."
3. "Old word, but in the N.T. only here and Acts 18:28."

### John Gill:
1. "The chief priests and Scribes... fearing, lest Herod should be disposed to let him go, should he gratify him by working a miracle..."
