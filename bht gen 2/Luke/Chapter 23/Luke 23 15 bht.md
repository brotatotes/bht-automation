# Luke 23:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Herod, who had examined Jesus and heard all the accusations against him, found no fault in him that would warrant condemnation to death. Even Pilate, who sent Jesus back to Herod, agreed that Jesus had done nothing deserving of death. Both Herod and Pilate concluded that Jesus was innocent and did not deserve the punishment he was facing.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Deserving of death. The charges are not proved against him."
2. "He was declared by both to be innocent."
3. "There could be no better evidence that he 'was' innocent."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Nor yet Herod, who knew Jewish affairs so well."
2. "For he sent him back to us. The correct reading more fully proves Pilate’s assertion."
3. "Hath been done by him, i.e., Herod’s examination failed to elicit any proof that He had committed a crime."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "But not even hath been done by him." 
2. "Periphrastic perfect passive indicative of πρασσω, common verb, to do." 
3. "The case of αυτω can be regarded as either the dative or the instrumental."

### John Gill:
1. "Who had also examined Jesus, and that in their presence, and had heard all that they had to accuse him with, and yet found no fault in him of any such kind, as to condemn him to death for it."
2. "For I sent you to him; along with Jesus, to make your allegations against him good before him."
3. "And lo, nothing worthy of death is done unto him; or 'by him': the sense is, either that it appeared to Herod, that Christ had done nothing that deserved death; or that nothing was done to him by Herod, which showed that he thought he was worthy of it."

### John Wesley:
1. He hath done nothing worthy of death — According to the judgment of Herod also.
