The soldiers cruelly mocked Jesus, stripped Him of His own clothes, and then put them back on Him. They laid the cross upon Simon of Cyrene, who would bear it after Jesus. This was part of their torturous plan to ensure that Jesus would suffer greatly as He carried the cross to His crucifixion.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "He was 'the father of Alexander and Rufus,' probably better known afterwards than himself, as disciples." 
2. "Laid the cross—'Him they compel to bear His cross'—sweet compulsion, if it issued in him or his sons voluntarily 'taking up their cross!'"
3. "It would appear that our Lord had first to bear His own cross, but being from exhaustion unable to proceed, it was laid on another to bear it 'after Him.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "After Jesus - Probably to bear one end of the cross."
2. "Jesus was feeble and unable to bear it alone."
3. "They compelled Simon to help him."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Laid hold on [επιλαβομενοι] . Compare the peculiar word used by Matthew and Mark. See on Matthew 5:41. 27 - 32. Peculiar to Luke. See Introduction, on the gospel of womanhood.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The hinder part alone was laid upon Simon." 
2. "The relief was comparatively slight; there is no proof that our Lord was sinking under the load." 
3. "He who bears the cross after Jesus, bears the lightest end of it."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The soldiers had no scruples about taking hold of any one of themselves."
2. "They laid on him."
3. "To bear it, to go on bearing."

### John Gill:
1. "After they had finished their sport and pastime with him, and had put on him his own clothes again..."
2. "On him they laid the cross; on which Jesus was to be crucified, and which he was bearing himself..."
3. "They put the cross, at least one end of it, upon this man's shoulders: that he might bear it after Jesus..."
