# Luke 23:51 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Joseph of Arimathea, though present in the high priest's palace among the council, did not agree with their counsel and deed. In fact, he opposed it and did not consent to their decision. Joseph of Arimathea was known for his anticipation of the kingdom of God and held sympathies for Jesus, which is why he was not included in the trial.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  51.   οὗτος  … ] Peculiar to Luke. The meaning is, he had absented himself, and taken no part in their (the council’s) determination against Jesus.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Consented [συγκατατεθειμενος] . Only here in New Testament." 
2. "The verb means to put [τιθημι] , down [κατα] , along with [συν] ."
3. "Hence to put down the same vote or opinion with another : to agree with or assent to."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He had not consented." - From chap, Luke 22:70, we may infer that he was absent from the morning meeting of the Sanhedrin, probably from all.
2. "Their counsel." - The formal decision, which resulted in the deed, i.e., crucifixion.
3. "Of, or ‘from,’ Arimathea." - He was ‘of’ that city, but it is possible he came ‘from’ that place at this time.

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He had not consented to their counsel and deed."
2. "Though a councillor, he had not agreed to the vote of the Sanhedrin."
3. "Both Joseph and Nicodemus were suspected of sympathy with Jesus and so were not invited to the trial."

### John Gill:
1. "Though he was with the Jews, the chief priests, Scribes, and elders, in the high priest's palace, being one of that great council; yet he did not agree with them." 
2. "He was against it, at least did not consent to it."
3. "Who also himself waited for the kingdom of God."
