Herod's eagerness to see Jesus was fueled by his insatiable curiosity. He had long been fascinated and eager to catch a glimpse of this man who had caused such a stir. He hoped that seeing Jesus would satisfy his thirst for knowledge and understanding, even though his motives were driven purely by curiosity.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Fine sport thou expectedst, as the Philistines with Samson, O coarse, crafty, cruel tyrant!"
2. "But thou hast been baulked before and shalt be again."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Of a long time [εξ ικανου] . See on ch. Luke 7:6. Hoped [ηλπιζεν] . Imperfect; was hoping - all this long time. Miracle [σημειον] . See on Matthew 11:20; and compare Acts 2:22, Rev.

### John Calvin:
1. "Though Herod did not acknowledge Christ to be the Son of God, he at least reckoned him to be a prophet."
2. "It was therefore most unreasonable cruelty to take pleasure in seeing him treated with contempt and disdain."
3. "And even Herod, though he hoped that some miracle would be performed by Christ, chose to have him placed at his feet as a malefactor rather than to receive him as a teacher."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "This joy of Herod seems all the more frivolous and unkingly."
2. "The frivolous joy arose from the confident expectation that now his long continued desire and hope would be met."
3. "'Jesus was to him what a skilful juggler is to a seated court an object of curiosity.'"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He had long ago gotten over his fright that Jesus was John the Baptist come to life again." 
2. "He wanted to see a miracle happening like a stunt of a sleight-of-hand performer."

### John Gill:
1. "And when Herod saw Jesus, he was exceeding glad...to have a sight of him, was a great indulgence to his curiosity." 
2. "For he was desirous to see him of a long season... and therefore was desirous of seeing him, that he might know whether he was John or not." 
3. "And he hoped to have seen some miracle done by him; which would have still more gratified his curiosity, and have been the subject of further inquiry and conversation."

### John Wesley:
1. He had been long desirous to see him — Out of mere curiosity.
