# Luke 23:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Herod questioned Jesus, speaking to Him in many words and asking about His identity and the miracles that were attributed to Him. However, Jesus remained silent and did not respond to Herod's many questions. Despite Herod's curiosity and desire for Jesus to perform miracles in his presence, Jesus chose not to indulge him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Many [ικανοις] . Compare long, ver. 8.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The character of the questions may be inferred from Herod’s reception of Jesus, as well as from the next clause."
2. "For such a judge, the incestuous adulterer, the murderer of the Baptist, He had neither miracles nor words."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  He questioned  (επηρωτα). Imperfect active, kept on questioning.In many words  (εν λογοις ικανοις). Same use of ικανος as in verse Luke 23:8. 

### John Gill:
1. "talked very much to him, inquiring who he was, whether John the Baptist, or no; whether it was true that he had wrought such miracles he had heard of, and how he wrought them, and by what power he performed them, and how he came by it"
2. "he answered him nothing; would not give him one word in return to his many words, nor work any miracle in his presence"
3. "he would not indulge his curiosity, though he might have obtained his liberty by it"

### John Wesley:
1. He questioned him — Probably concerning the miracles which were reported to have been wrought by him.
