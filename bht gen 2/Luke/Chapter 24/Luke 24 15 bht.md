# Luke 24:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
As the disciples questioned and reasoned with one another about the recent report, Jesus himself drew near to them suddenly. He joined their company and walked along with them, as if he had been a fellow traveler on the road. His purpose in doing so was to guide these sincere seekers of truth and to dispel their doubts. The disciples were earnestly seeking to understand, and Jesus, in his wisdom, chose the opportune time to reveal himself to them.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The reason why they did not know Him was (Luk 24:16), that their eyes were supernaturally influenced, so that they could not: see also Luke 24:31."
2. "No change took place in Him nor apparently in them, beyond a power upon them, which prevented the recognition just so much as to delay it till aroused by the well-known action and manner of His breaking the bread."
3. "The cause of this was the will of the Lord himself, who would not be seen by them till the time when He saw fit."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The disciples were properly employed. Their minds were anxious about the state of things, and they endeavored to arrive at the truth."
2. "Jesus came to solve their doubts, and to establish them in the belief that he was the Christ; and we may learn from this that Christ will guide those who are sincerely endeavoring to know the truth."
3. "Our duty is sincerely to strive to ascertain the truth, and to do his will; and if his people do this, he will not leave them to perplexity and wandering."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The use of the imperfect here is very beautiful."
2. "Jesus drew near while they were absorbed in their talk."
3. "He was already walking with them when they observed him."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Jesus himself drew near...comme with those who commune of Him."


### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Each questioned the other." 
2. "In actual person." 
3. "Was going along with them."

### John Gill:
1. "Jesus himself drew near: the Persic version adds, 'suddenly'; he came up at once to them, as if he had been a traveller on the road, and overtook them."
2. "And it came to pass, that while they communed together... and reasoned; with one another; about the truth and credibility of the late report."
3. "and went with them; joined himself in company to them, and travelled with them."
