### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Made us astonished [εξεστησαν] . Literally the verb means to put out of place; and so, to drive one out of his senses."
2. "Hence the A. V. is feeble. Rev., better, amazed us."
3. "Early [ορθριναι] . Lit., early ones. Only here and Revelation 22:16. Compare orqrov, dawn, ver. 1."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "We were well-nigh hopeless, yet other occurrences aroused our hope, without however fulfilling it."
2. "This strong expression indicates the effect produced upon them in their perplexed state of mind, by the strange, but unsatisfactory state of things mentioned."
3. "Having been early, etc. This should be joined with what follows. It begins the account of the facts that amazed them."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Amazed us (εξεστησαν ημας)."
2. "First aorist active (transitive) indicative with accusative ημας."
3. "The second aorist active is intransitive."

### John Gill:
1. "Yea, and certain women also of our company...made us astonished." 
2. "Surprised us, with an account they brought, so that we could not, nor can we now tell, what to think or say of it; it is such an one, we know not how to believe, nor to disprove."
3. "It is we fear too good to be true, and should it be as they report, it is amazing indeed."
