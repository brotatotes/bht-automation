# Luke 24:25 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
You foolish and slow-hearted people, lacking in understanding and readiness to believe. Surprisingly, despite having the Scriptures and being under Christ's teaching, you remain ignorant and senseless. You have failed to comprehend the prophecies and the testimonies of those who have seen the risen Christ. Open your hearts and minds to the truth and fulfillment of God's plans.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Without understanding; sluggish in disposition to believe."
2. "These were both shown in their not having apprehended, from the fulfillment of the sufferings and death of Christ, the sequel of that death, the resurrection."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 25-27. fools—senseless,
	without understanding.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He reproached them for not seeing what he had himself so clearly predicted, and what had been foretold by the prophets."
2. "The word used in the original does not imply as much 'reproach' as the word 'fool' does among us. It was not an expression of 'contempt;' it was an expression denoting merely that they were 'thoughtless,' and that they did not properly 'attend to' the evidence that he must die and rise again."
3. "They had suffered their previous opinions and prejudices to prevent their seeing the evidence that he must die and rise from the dead."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The word fool...implies, besides seeing, perception of the mind as consequent upon sight...equivalent to dull of perception." 
2. "They had read what the prophets had spoken, but had failed to perceive its application to Christ." 
3. "Your heart is dull and slow to respond to these testimonies of your own prophets."

### John Calvin:
1. "What is here said was not confined to these two persons, but, as a reproof of a common fault, was intended to be conveyed by their lips to the rest of their companions."
2. "This hesitation, therefore, he justly attributes to folly, and assigns as the reason of it their carelessness in not having been more ready to believe."
3. "Christ, perceiving that his disciples are excessively sluggish; commences with reproof, in order to arouse them."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He answers, not in a tone of pity, but of rebuke, as one competent to teach them."
2. "O foolish men, without understanding, unreceptive intellectually, and slow of heart, sluggish in the entire disposition."
3. "Our Lord intimates that the slowness to believe was the ground of the want of understanding."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Foolish men (ανοητο). Literally without sense (νους), not understanding. Common word." 
2. "Slow of heart (βραδεις τη καρδια). Slow in heart (locative case). Old word for one dull, slow to comprehend or to act."
3. "All that (πασιν οις). Relative attracted from the accusative α to the case of the antecedent πασιν (dative). They could only understand part of the prophecies, not all."

### John Gill:
1. "Christ did not act contrary to his own rule, in Matthew 5:22 but because they were so void of understanding in the Scriptures, and were so very ignorant of them, and were so blind as to the knowledge of them."
2. "He therefore expresses himself with much warmth, concern, and surprise, that he should have been so long with them, and they so long under his doctrine and ministry; besides the advantages of having the Scriptures, and being conversant with them from their youth; and which they daily read, and had heard expounded, and yet were so very senseless and stupid."
3. "They were very slow of heart to believe, not only what the women reported from the angels, but even those of their brethren, who had seen him after he was risen; for which Christ upbraids them, Mark 16:14."

### John Wesley:
1. O foolish — Not understanding the designs and works of God: And slow of heart - Unready to believe what the prophets have so largely spoken.
