# Luke 24:31 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
And in an instant, their previously clouded vision was restored, allowing them to see clearly. As he departed, he seemed to fade away, disappearing from their sight without them even realizing it.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "ἄφαντος, not αὐτοῖς, which would imply His Body to have remained, but invisible to them."
2. "ἀπ ʼ αὐτῶν, implying, besides the supernatural disappearance, a real objective removal from them."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Their doubts were gone, and they saw clearly that he was risen, and was truly, as they had long hoped, the Saviour of people."
2. "It is not meant that they were before 'blind,' but that they did not know until then who he was."
3. "He suddenly departed. It does not appear that there was anything miraculous in this, but, during their surprise, he took the opportunity suddenly to withdraw from them."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "They knew [επεγνωσαν]. Clearly recognized."
2. "And he vanished out of their sight [αυτος αφαντος εγενετο απ αυτων]. Lit., he, invisible, became away from them."
3. "It is not simply, he suddenly departed from them, but he passed away from them invisibly."

### John Calvin:
1. "There was not in Christ any metamorphosis, or variety of forms, by which he might impose on the eyes of men."
2. "Christ, as soon as he was recognized, immediately disappeared; for it was not advantageous that they should any longer behold him."
3. "By the sudden departure, he taught them that they must seek him elsewhere than in the world, because the completion of the new life was his ascension to heaven."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The main fact remains: 'their eyes were opened,' and as an immediate result 'they knew Him.'"
2. "Luke certainly means to describe an extraordinary disappearance; not a becoming invisible to them but a supernatural removal from them."
3. "The reason for this sudden removal is to be found in the wise method by which our Lord would teach His bewildered followers that He had actually risen from the dead."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Were opened (διηνοιχθησαν)."
2. "Knew (επεγνωσαν)."
3. "Vanished (αφαντος εγενετο)."

### John Gill:
1. "And their eyes were opened,.... Not that they were before shut, or closed up, but what before held them was removed, and what hindered their sight and knowledge was taken away..."
2. "...perhaps these actions of his taking the bread, and blessing, and breaking, and giving it to them, might put them in mind of him, and cause them to look wistfully at him..."
3. "...agility being a property of his risen body, he very suddenly, and swiftly, and in a moment, withdrew himself from them..."

### John Wesley:
1. Their eyes were opened — That is, the supernatural cloud was removed: And he vanished - Went away insensibly.
