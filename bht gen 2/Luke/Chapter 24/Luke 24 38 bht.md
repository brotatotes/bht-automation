# Luke 24:38 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Why are you troubled? This question from Jesus is a gentle rebuke, deserving of their anxious and agitated state. They allowed doubts and incredulous thoughts to cloud their spiritual perception, preventing them from immediately recognizing Him. They were filled with fear and uneasiness, mistaking Jesus for a spirit or apparition. But Jesus, in His compassion, reassures them and encourages them to trust in Him rather than reason and doubts.

1. "By saying that thoughts arise, he means that the knowledge of the truth is choked in them in such a manner, that seeing they do not see."
2. "We have a right, indeed, when any appearance of absurdity presents itself, to inquire by weighing the arguments on both sides."

### Philip Schaff:

3. "These prevented them from at once and unhesitatingly recognizing Him, identifying Him."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  Why are ye troubled?  (τ τεταραγμενο εστε;). Periphrastic perfect passive indicative of ταρασσω, old verb, to agitate, to stir up, to get excited.
