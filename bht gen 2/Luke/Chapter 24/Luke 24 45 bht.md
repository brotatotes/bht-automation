# Luke 24:45 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus, through the power of the Holy Spirit, not only opened the hearts of the disciples to listen and believe in the resurrection, but He also opened their minds to understand the Scriptures regarding His death and resurrection. This divine illumination from the Spirit was necessary to remove the veil from their eyes and grant them a deeper understanding of God's truth. It is a reminder of the ongoing need for the Holy Spirit's guidance and illumination to comprehend the depths of God's Word.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Then opened he, c.—a statement of unspeakable value expressing, on the one hand, Christ's immediate access to the human spirit and absolute power over it, to the adjustment of its vision, and permanent rectification for spiritual discernment..."
2. "...than which it is impossible to conceive a stronger evidence of His proper divinity."
3. "...making it certain that the manner of interpreting the Old Testament which the apostles afterwards employed (see the Acts and Epistles), has the direct sanction of Christ Himself."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Opened he their understanding - Enabled them fully to comprehend the meaning of the prophecies which foretold his death and resurrection."
2. "Their prejudices were now, by his instructions, and by the facts which they could no longer call in question, removed."
3. "They no longer doubted that he was the Messiah, and that all the 'facts' in the case which had before confounded them could be easily accounted for."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Understanding [νουν] . Which had been closed. See on fools, ver. 25.

### John Calvin:
1. "The heavenly doctrine proves to be useful and efficacious to us, only so far as the Spirit both forms our minds to understand it, and our hearts to submit to its yoke."
2. "The disciples had not the eyes of their mind opened, so as to comprehend the mysteries of God without any assistance, but so far as they are contained in the Scriptures."
3. "What we now read in reference to the apostles is daily accomplished by Christ in all his people, namely, that by his Spirit he guides us to understand the Scriptures, and does not hurry us away into the idle raptures of enthusiasm."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Not only must the Scriptures be opened for the understanding, but the understanding for the Scriptures."
2. "This was doubtless the work of repeated interviews, as is hinted in Acts 1:3, and evident from the remarkable proficiency in the interpretation of Old Testament Scripture."
3. "This verse may therefore bridge over the forty days."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Jesus had all these years been trying to open their minds that they might understand the Scriptures about the Messiah..."
2. "They can now see better the will and way of God, but they will still need the power of the Holy Spirit before they will fully know the mind of Christ."

### John Gill:
1. "He not only opened their hearts, to attend to what he said, and the proofs he gave of his resurrection from the dead in a true body."
2. "He removed the veil from the eyes of their minds, and gave them an understanding of the sacred writings, respecting this matter."
3. "Which shows the necessity of the special illumination of the Spirit, and the influence of his grace to remove the darkness of the mind, and give the true sense of the sacred writings."

### John Wesley:
1. "He had explained them before to the two as they went to Emmaus. But still they understood them not, till he took off the veil from their hearts, by the illumination of his Spirit."
