# Luke 24:48 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The disciples were not the only witnesses of Jesus' ministry, as others may have been present as well. However, they played a crucial role in testifying to the truth of his humanity, his sorrows and sufferings, his death, and his resurrection. They were to proclaim these facts and the message of repentance and remission of sins to all nations. By doing so, they fulfilled the prophecies and commission given to them, showing themselves as the evidence of Christ's mercy and love to the world.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "These words may have been more general, and said to all present."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 48. witnesses—(Compare Acts 1:8;
	Acts 1:22).

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Are witnesses of these things - Of my life, my sufferings, my death, and my resurrection."
2. "How solemn was their office - to 'testify' these things to the world, and, in the face of suffering and death, to go and proclaim them to all nations!"
3. "In like manner, 'all' Christians are witnesses for Christ. They are the 'evidences' of his mercy and his love, and they should so live that others may be brought to see and love the Saviour."

### John Calvin:
1. "He does not yet commission them to preach the gospel, but only reminds them to what service he has appointed them, that they may prepare themselves for it in due time."
2. "He holds out this, partly as a consolation to soothe their grief, and partly as a spur to correct their sloth."
3. "He not only restores them to their former condition, but by the extent of this new favor he utterly obliterates the recollection of their heinous crimes."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Ye. The Apostles, but others may have been present Acts 1:22 hints that others saw Him ascend."
2. "Witnesses. As such they were to proclaim the facts (Luke 24:46), and the repentance and remission based upon them; and thus be the fulfillers of the prophecies summed up in Luke 24:47."
3. "These things. The Gospel facts respecting Christ, centering in His Death and Resurrection, and including His Ascension. The fulfilment of prophecy and the commission to preach remission and repentance, are not excluded."

### John Gill:
1. "And ye are witnesses of these things. As they were witnesses of the truth of his humanity, having seen, and heard, and handled him, both before, and after his resurrection from the dead, and of all that he did..." 
2. "...likewise of his sorrows in the garden, and of his sufferings in the palace of the high priest, and in Pilate's hall, and on the cross; and of his death, which was the death of the cross; and also of his resurrection from the dead, in the same body, to whom he showed himself alive by many infallible proofs..."
3. "...their business was also to testify the doctrine of repentance, and remission of sins, in Jerusalem, in all Judea, and Samaria, and to the uttermost parts of the earth, which they accordingly did..."
