# Luke 3:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
John began his ministry in the wilderness of Judea, preaching and baptizing in the country surrounding the Jordan. He was not confined to one location, but moved throughout the area, preaching about the need for repentance and baptism. This ministry of John, as prophesied by Malachi, was focused on preparing the way for the coming of the Savior. It was an important and impactful work, symbolizing the outward representation of repentance for the forgiveness of sins.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The country about Jordan. Which both Matthew and Mark call the wilderness."
2. "Baptism of repentance...denoting the destination of the rite."
3. "Used in medical language of the relaxation of disease."

### John Calvin:
1. "A sacrament, then, is not a dumb ceremony, exhibiting some unmeaning pomp without doctrine; but the Word of God is joined to it, and gives life to the outward ceremony."
2. "For we are not simply told, that John baptized unto repentance, as if the grace of God were contained in a visible sign; but that he explained, in his preaching, the advantage of baptism, that the sign, through the word preached, might produce its effect."
3. "This is the peculiarity of baptism, that it is said to be an outward representation of repentance for the forgiveness of sins."

### Philip Schaff:
1. Luke 3:3. See on Matthew 3:1; Mark 1:4.

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The wilderness was John's abode so that he began preaching where he was."
2. "Sometimes he was on the eastern bank of the Jordan, though usually on the west side. His baptizing kept him near the river."
3. "The word remission 'occurs in Luke more frequently than in all the other New Testament writers combined' (Vincent)."

### John Gill:
1. "He came out of the wilderness of Judea, where he first began his ministry, to some parts of the country that bordered on Jordan." 
2. "John came into this land, preaching this doctrine." 
3. "This was the work and office of John, as signified by Elias, in Malachi 4:5."
