1. "Luke does not add this, to prove that Jesus was the son of God."
2. "It implies that Adam was  created directly by God, also that he stood in a closer relation to God than other creatures."

### John Gill:
1. "The son of God: not begotten, as all the rest were, by their immediate parents, but created by God, in a supernatural manner, out of the dust of the earth, and quickened with the breath of God."
2. "Thus, as Matthew gives us the regal line of Christ, showing him to be heir to the throne of his father David, Luke gives the natural line of Christ."
3. "Christ, according to his divine nature, was the only begotten of the Father; and as to his human nature, had a body prepared by him, and in the fullness of time was God manifest in the flesh."

### John Wesley:
