# Luke 4:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
When Jesus came to Nazareth, the place where He had grown up, He entered the synagogue as He often did, not only to attend but to teach. It was a significant event, showing His commitment to the public worship of God. As was customary, He stood up to read from the Scriptures, demonstrating His reverence for the Word. It was a testimony to His upbringing and His desire to share God's truth with those in His hometown.

### Henry Alford:
1. "κατὰ τὸ εἰωθός refers to the whole of what He did it is not merely that He had been in the habit of attending the synagogues, but of teaching in them."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:

	Acts 17:2.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "From this we may learn: It is of vast importance that the public worship of God should be maintained; and it is 'our' duty to assist in maintaining it, to show by our example that we love it, and to win others also to love it."
2. "If, therefore, the Unitarian does not worship the true God, and if the Roman Catholic worships God in a manner forbidden and offers homage to the creatures of God, thus being guilty of idolatry, it cannot be a duty to attend on such a place of worship." 
3. "The same ceremonies in regard to conducting public worship which are here described are observed at Jerusalem by the Jews at the present time."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "That Nazareth where he had been brought up."
2. "After the liturgical services...called upon some one to read."
3. "It was this section which Jesus read and expounded."

### John Calvin:
1. "By explaining a passage in Isaiah, and applying it to the instruction which was immediately required, he turned upon him the eyes of all."
2. "Hence we conclude, that not only did he address the people in the open streets and highways, but, as far as he had opportunity, observed the usual order of the church."
3. "Now, if we make a comparison of dates, this passage will be sufficient to prove clearly, that the corruptions of the Papal Hierarchy, in our own time, are more shocking and detestable than those which existed among the Jews under the high priesthood of Annas and Caiaphas."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "His going into the synagogue; probably in this case the place of worship He had attended as a youth... the allusion to His early habits of piety is more suggestive." 
2. "The exercises were under proper control. Our Lord thus asked the privilege, which was the more readily granted, as those present evidently knew of His previous activity elsewhere."
3. "This first appearance of Jesus, as a public instructor, in the synagogue He had attended in youth, before those among whom He had been brought up, assures His sympathy to those placed in similar circumstances."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "This is one of the flashlights on the early life of Jesus."
2. "If the child does not form the habit of going to church, the man is almost certain not to have it."
3. "Jesus could have stood up voluntarily and appropriately because of his interest in his home town."

### John Gill:
1. "And he came to Nazareth,.... After some length of time, when he had gone through all Galilee, and had acquired great credit and reputation by his ministry and miracles; he came to the place, where he had been brought up..."
2. "and therefore it was proper that he should first exercise his ministry, and obtain a character in other places, which would prepare him a reception among his townsmen..."
3. "it was a known custom to this day, that even an unlearned priest read before the greatest wise man in Israel; and he that was greater than his companion in wisdom, read first."

### John Wesley:
1. "He stood up — Showing thereby that he had a desire to read the Scripture to the congregation"
2. "It was the Jewish custom to read standing, but to preach sitting."
