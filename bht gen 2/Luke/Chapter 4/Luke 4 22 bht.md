## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The λόγοι τ. χ. must be the discourse of which Luk 4:21 is a compendium."
2. "While acknowledging the truth of what He said, and the power with which He said it, they wondered, and were jealous of Him, as being the son of Joseph asking πόθεν τούτῳ ταῦτα."
3. "Between this verse and the next, the ἐσκανδαλίζοντο ἐν αὐτῷ is implied, for that is in a tone of reproof."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Gracious words—the words of grace, referring both to the richness of His matter and the sweetness of His manner."
2. "Is not this, &c.—They knew He had received no rabbinical education, and anything supernatural they seemed incapable of conceiving."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "All were witnesses of the power and truth of what he said."
2. "They were struck with the truth and force of his words."
3. "The kind, affectionate, and tender exposition of the words, and explanation of the design of his coming, and the nature of the plan of redemption."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "They confirmed the reports which had been circulated about him."
2. "There was a continuous stream of admiring comment."
3. "Literally and correctly, as Rev., words of grace."

### John Calvin:
1. "Using a Hebrew idiom, he calls them discourses of grace, - that is, discourses which manifested the power and grace of the Holy Spirit."
2. "The inhabitants of Nazareth are thus compelled to acknowledge and admire God speaking in Christ; and yet they voluntarily refuse to render to the heavenly doctrine of Christ the honor which it deserves."
3. "Thus we daily see many who, while they are convinced that what they hear is the word of God, seize on frivolous apologies for refusing to obey it."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Grace' here refers to the beauty of His discourse, and not to its moral quality." 
2. "Marvel at words of gracefulness is a small result for the preacher."
3. "The wonder was that such graceful words could be spoken by 'Joseph's son,' implying a contempt of His supposed origin, and envy of Him as such a preacher."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They all began to bear witness that the rumours were not exaggerations...had foundation in fact if this discourse or its start was a fair sample of his teaching." 
2. "They began to marvel as he proceeded with his address."
3. "The words that came out of the mouth of Jesus...were marked by fascination and charm. They were 'winning words'...gracious in the Pauline sense of 'grace'."

### John Gill:
1. "And all bare him witness,.... That he was right in applying the words to the Messiah; but not that he himself was the Messiah, and that he was right in applying them to himself."
2. "They wondered at the gracious words which proceeded out of his mouth; not so much at the matter, the sum, and substance of them, as expressive of the love, grace, and favour of God shown in the mission and unction of the Messiah, and in that liberty, deliverance, and salvation he was sent to effect and proclaim."
3. "They were more amazed at all this, since they knew his parentage and education, and in what manner he had been brought up among them; and therefore it was astonishing to them, where he had his learning, knowledge, and wisdom."

### John Wesley:
1. "The gracious words which proceeded out of his mouth" - A person of spiritual discernment may find in all the discourses of our Lord a peculiar sweetness, gravity, and becomingness.
2. A person of spiritual discernment may find in all the discourses of our Lord a peculiar sweetness, gravity, and becomingness, such as is not to be found in the same degree, not even in those of the apostles.
