# Luke 4:30 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
He miraculously passed through the midst of them, either by being invisible to their eyes or by overwhelming them with such awe that they could not touch him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "passing through the midst... in a miraculous way"
2. "quite noiselessly, leading them to wonder afterwards"
3. "what spell could have come over them, that they allowed Him to escape"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It is remarkable that he should escape out of their hands when their very object was to destroy him, and that he should escape in so peaceful a manner, without violence or conflict."
2. "A similar case is recorded in John 8:59."
3. "There are but two ways of accounting for this."

### John Calvin:
1. "He means that God rescued him, by an extraordinary miracle, from immediate death."
2. "This example teaches us that, though our adversaries may prevail so far, that our life may seem to be placed at their disposal, yet that the power of God will always be victorious to preserve us."
3. "So long as he shall be pleased to keep us in the world, either by tying their hands, or by blinding their eyes, or by stupifying their minds and hearts."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "There was something miraculous in this escape."
2. "He, visible to them all, passed through them, making them feel His superhuman power restraining them, showing them their own powerlessness against Him."
3. "They had demanded a miracle, and now they obtained one, a miracle of judgment on them all."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  He went his way  (επορευετο). Imperfect tense, he was going on his way. 

### John Gill:
1. "he made use of, and showed his divine power"
2. "he laid down his life freely and voluntarily"
3. "he could then have exerted his power, and delivered himself out of the hands of his enemies"

### John Wesley:
1. Passing through the midst of them — Perhaps invisibly; or perhaps they were overawed; so that though they saw, they could not touch him.
