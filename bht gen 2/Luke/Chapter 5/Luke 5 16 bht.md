### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The multitudes were coming together, but he was engaged in retirement and prayer, so that he was inaccessible." 
2. "The participle with the imperfect of the finite verb denoting something in progress, and thus corresponding to the imperfect in verse 15." 
3. "The word occurs only in Luke, the usual New Testament word for withdraw being ajnacwrew."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "But he withdrew himself in the deserts and prayed...The more the crowds came as a result of the leper's story, the more Jesus turned away from them to the desert regions and prayed with the Father."
2. "It is a picture of Jesus drawn with vivid power. The wild enthusiasm of the crowds was running ahead of their comprehension of Christ and his mission and message."
3. "Hυποχωρεω (perhaps with the notion of slipping away secretly, υπο-) is a very common Greek verb, but in the N.T. occurs in Luke alone. Elsewhere in the N.T. αναχωρεω (to go back) appears."

### John Gill:
1. "Into a desert place, that he might have rest from the fatigues of preaching and healing diseases; and being alone, and free from company, might have an opportunity for private prayer to God..." 
2. "As God, he is the object of prayer, and petitions are often addressed unto him; and as mediator, he offers up the prayers of all saints, and presents them to his Father; which are acceptable to him, through the incense of his mediation..."
3. "Sometimes he prayed for his disciples, and for all that should believe; for their conversion, sanctification, union, perseverance, and glorification; and sometimes for himself, that the cup might pass from him, and he be saved from death; but always with submission to the will of his Father."

### John Wesley:
1. He withdrew — The expression in the original implies, that he did so frequently.
