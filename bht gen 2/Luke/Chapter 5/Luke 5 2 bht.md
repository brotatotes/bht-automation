### Albert Barnes:
1. "The ships used on so small a lake were probably no more than fishing-boats without decks, and easily drawn up on the beach."
2. "That they were small is also clear from the account commonly given of them. A single large draught of fishes endangered them and came near sinking them."
3. "Standing by the lake - Anchored by the lake, or drawn up upon the beach."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Luke uses four different words for washing or cleansing."
2. "Some texts read ploiapia, a diminutive form, meaning little boats."
3. "All the words were common in medical language."

### Philip Schaff:
1. Luke 5:2.  By the lake. Either by the shore of the lake, or possibly drawn up on the shore.
	 Washing their nets. After the night of toil (Luke 5:5).

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Two boats (πλοια δυο). Some MSS. have πλοιαρια, little boats, but πλοια was used of boats of various sizes, even of ships like νηες."
2. "The fishermen (ο αλεεις). It is an old Homeric word that has come back to common use in the Koine. It means 'sea-folk' from αλς, sea."
3. "Vincent comments on Luke's use of five verbs for washing: this one for cleaning, απομασσω for wiping the dust from one's feet (Luke 10:11), εκμασσω of the sinful woman wiping Christ's feet with her hair (Luke 7:38; Luke 7:44), απολουω of washing away sins (symbolically, of course) as in Acts 22:16, and λουω of washing the body of Dorcas (Acts 9:37) and the stripes of the prisoners (Acts 16:33)."

### John Gill:
1. "The fishermen were gone out of them; that is, either the above persons, or their servants."
2. "They were washing their nets; on shore; they having gathered a great deal of soil and filthiness, but had caught no fish."
3. "Finding it to be in vain to make any further attempts with them at present; and which considered, makes the following miracle the more illustrious."
