Those who are in good health do not require a physician, but rather those who are sick. Jesus came to heal not only physical ailments but also the spiritual sickness of humanity. His mission as the Divine Physician was to address the needs of the soul, offering healing and restoration to those who were spiritually unwell.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Marvin Vincent:
1. "This use of the verb in its primary sense, to be in sound health, is found in Luke 7:10; Luke 14:27; and once in John, 3 Ep. verse 2."
2. "For this meaning it is the regular word in medical writings."
3. "Paul uses it only in the metaphorical sense: sound doctrine, sound words, sound in faith, etc."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They that are whole (ο υγιαινοντες). Old Greek word for good health from υγιης, sound in body."
2. "This is the usual word for good health used by Greek medical writers."
3. "Mark 2:17; Matthew 9:12 have ο ισχυοντες (those who have strength)."

### John Gill:
1. "they that are whole need not a physician, but they that are sick."
2. "he came to cure the maladies of the souls, as well as of the bodies of men."
3. "his business, as a physician, lying among the one, and not the other."
