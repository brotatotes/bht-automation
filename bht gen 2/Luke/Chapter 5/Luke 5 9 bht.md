# Luke 5:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The incredible catch of fish left Peter and his companions astonished. The quantity and size of the fish were beyond anything they had ever seen or experienced before. This miracle not only demonstrated Jesus' knowledge but also hinted at the fulfillment of His promise to Peter of becoming a fisher of men. The extraordinary catch held them in a state of awe and wonder.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Marvin Vincent:
1. "He was astonished...amazement encompassed him."
2. "The word [draught] is used both of the act of catching and of that which is caught."
3. "In verse 4 it has the former sense: 'let down your net for catching:' here, the latter, the catch or haul."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "This miracle seems more than one of knowledge. It is true the shoals of fish in the lake are very thick, but the promise of Luke 5:10 ('Henceforth thou shalt catch men') points to an influence of Christ’s upon the fish."
2. "Christ here appears as the ideal man, the second Adam of the eighth Psalm: 'Thou madest him to have dominion over the works of thy hands: Thou hast put all things under His feet the fowl of the air, and the fish of the sea, and whatsoever walketh through the paths of the seas' (Luke 5:6; Luke 5:8)."
3. "From henceforth thou shalt catch men. See on Matthew 4:19. Here the three narratives coincide."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  For he was amazed  (θαμβος γαρ περιεσχεν). Literally,For a wonder held him round . Aorist active indicative. It held Peter fast and all the rest. 

### John Gill:
1. "Far he was astonished, and all that were with him." 
2. "At the draught of the fishes they had taken; being so large and numerous, as the like was never seen, nor known by them before."
