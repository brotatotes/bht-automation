# Luke 6:24 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Woe to the rich, to those who find their comfort and happiness in worldly possessions, for their consolation is fleeting and cannot compare to the true comfort found in Christ.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "I cannot assent to any such view as that taken by Meyer and others, that these ‘woes’ are inserted from later tradition."
2. "Either we must suppose that they ought to follow Matthew 5:12, which is from the context most improbable, or that they, and perhaps the four preceding beatitudes with them, were on some occasion spoken by our Lord in this exact form, and so have been here placed in that form."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Rich and full...who have all their good things and joyous feelings here and now, in perishable objects."
2. "Shall hunger...their inward craving strong as ever, but the materials of satisfaction forever gone."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The verb, compounded of ajpo, off or from, and ecw, to have, literally means to have nothing left to desire."
2. "Thus it embodies the call for help, and the response to the call."
3. "The comfort which Christ gives is not always soothing. The Holy Spirit, the Comforter, is to convince of sin and of judgment."

### John Calvin:
1. "This contrast not only tends to strike terror into the ungodly, but to arouse believers, that they may not be lulled to sleep by the vain and deceitful allurements of the world."
2. "The meaning is: riches are so far from making a man happy, that they often become the means of his destruction."
3. "In any other point of view, the rich are not excluded from the kingdom of heaven, provided they do not become snares for themselves, or fix their hope on the earth, so as to shut against them the kingdom of heaven."

### Philip Schaff:
1. Luke 6:24. Rich  ,  i.e.,   fancying themselves possessed of what they crave and need. This class is made up largely of those actually wealthy.

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "But woe unto you that are rich...The rich Pharisees and Sadducees were the chief opposers of Christ...Ye have received...Consolation."

### John Gill:
1. "But woe unto you that are rich,.... Not in worldly riches and substance, for some of these have been, and are happy persons in a spiritual sense; and at most, it can only mean such, who trust in their riches, and place their happiness in them..."
2. "...but it chiefly regards such, as are rich in their own opinion, and stand in need of nothing; who place their confidence in their own righteousness, and do not apply to Christ, in whom alone are durable riches and righteousness..."
3. "...for there is no true solid comfort but in Christ, and in his righteousness; that administers consolation now, and lays a foundation for everlasting comfort hereafter."

### John Wesley:
1. Miserable are ye rich - If ye have received or sought your consolation or happiness therein.
