# Luke 6:27 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Our Lord's message is directed not only to a specific group of people, but to all individuals. He speaks not just to those who have already been following Him, but to the broader audience. This signifies a shift in His teachings, as He begins to address a wider range of topics that are applicable to everyone.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Which hear. With the sense of hearing in order to heed : giving heed. Compare Matthew 11:15.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Unto you that hear, i.e., who now hear me."
2. "Our Lord could utter woes against these enemies of His people, His people were not to hate them but to love them; so that the connection brings out the Gospel principle of hating sin but not the sinner."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "There is a contrast in this use of αλλα like that in Matthew 5:44. This is the only one of the many examples given by Luke 6:5 of the sharp antithesis between what the rabbis taught and what Jesus said."
2. "Perhaps that contrast is referred to by Luke."
3. "Love of enemies is in the O.T., but Jesus ennobles the word, αγαπαω, and uses it of love for one's enemies."

### John Gill:
1. "To you that hear day by day, the traditions of the elders urged upon you, and the false glosses the Scribes and Pharisees put upon the word of God; in opposition to them, I say to you what follows"
2. "Love your enemies; whereas you have heard them say, hate your enemies, keep enmity in your hearts to them, and revenge yourselves on them"
3. "Do good to them that hate you; whereas you have heard it said, that you should only do good to your friends, and should keep anger in your bosoms to such who hate you, and do you an injury"

### John Wesley:
1. "Hitherto our Lord had spoken only to particular sorts of persons: now he begins speaking to all in general."
