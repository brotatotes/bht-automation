## BHT:
In Matthew 7:3, the concept of a mote and a beam is presented, symbolizing a lesser sin when compared to others. It is important to note that not all sins are the same, as they differ in nature, occasions, circumstances, and aggravations. However, this does not justify or condone any sin, for no one is perfect or free from sin. Therefore, it is crucial to consider these words in light of the understanding that all individuals have their own faults and should be cautious not to focus solely on the faults of others while remaining blind to their own.

### John Gill:
1. "A lesser sin in comparison of others; for all sins are not alike, as the Stoics asserted: and though none are to be countenanced and indulged, yet some are not so severely to be animadverted upon as others, the nature, occasions, circumstances, and aggravations considered."
2. "For no man is perfect, or wholly free from sin; nor are the words preceding to be understood of such a perfection; for which reason perhaps these words, with what follow, are mentioned."
3. "Meaning a greater sin, such are guilty of, who are inquisitive searchers into the faults of others, and severe animadverters on them; and yet are blind to their own iniquities, and take no notice of them."
