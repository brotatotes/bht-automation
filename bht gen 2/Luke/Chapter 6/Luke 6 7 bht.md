# Luke 6:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The religious leaders closely observed Jesus, hoping to catch him in a compromising situation. They were determined to find fault with him and were even willing to conspire against him, just as they had done previously with Jesus' disciples. Their intentions were clear: to find an accusation against him and to undermine his authority.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Watched whether, &c.—In Matthew (Matthew 12:9) this is put as an ensnaring question of theirs to our Lord."
2. "Who accordingly speaks to the state of their hearts, just as if they had spoken it out."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "They kept watching." 
2. "He would heal." 
3. "Emphasizing the eagerness of the Pharisees to discover a ground of accusation."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Watched him. Imperfect middle, were watching for themselves on the side... giving an extra touch, watching either assiduously like the physician at the bedside or insidiously with evil intent as here." 
2. "That they might find out how to accuse him. Second aorist active subjunctive of ευρισκω and the infinitive with it means to find out how to do a thing. They were determined to make a case against Jesus."

### John Gill:
1. "The Scribes and Pharisees watched him...that they might find an accusation against him."
2. "There being such an object before him...that they might find an accusation against him."
3. "As they had before against his disciples."
