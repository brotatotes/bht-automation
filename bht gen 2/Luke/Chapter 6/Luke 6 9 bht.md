# Luke 6:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Our Lord presents a thought-provoking question to those who questioned His actions on the Sabbath: is it lawful to do good or to do evil, to save life or to destroy it? He exposes their hidden intentions and their disregard for human life. By asking this question, Jesus challenges them to reflect on the true purpose of the Sabbath and the importance of doing good.

### Henry Alford:
1. "I will ask you what is allowable on the sabbath, to do good, or to do evil?" 
2. "I will ask you a certain thing: Is it," 
3. "After the question, Mark adds οἱ δὲ ἐσιώπων as they did after the question just referred to in ch. 20, because they were in a dilemma, and either answer would have convicted them."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "By this novel way of putting His case, our Lord teaches the great ethical principle, that to neglect any opportunity of doing good is to incur the guilt of doing evil."
2. "He bound His own spirit" by this law.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "I will ask [επερωτησω] . Peculiar to Luke's narrative."

3. "Life [ψυχην] . Better as Rev., a life."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They had questions in their hearts about Jesus. He now asks in addition (επ') an open question that brings the whole issue into the open."
2. "The words of Jesus cut to the quick."
3. "On this very day these Pharisees were plotting to destroy Jesus."

### John Gill:
1. "I will ask you one thing; is it lawful on the sabbath days to do good, or to do evil? to save life, or to destroy it?"
2. "To save life on the sabbath day was agreeable to their own canons."
3. "He full well knew...the reasonings of their minds, purposes, and intentions."

### John Wesley:
1. To save life or to kill — He just then probably saw the design to kill him rising in their hearts.
