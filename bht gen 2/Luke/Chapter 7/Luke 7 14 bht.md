# Luke 7:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus approached the bier and touched it, causing the bearers to stand still, a moment that demonstrated his authority over death. It is worth noting that touching a bier was not forbidden by the law. With a word of command, Jesus, the Resurrection and the Life, brought life back to the dead body, displaying his divine power and compassion. In this act, he showed that he is the God of both the living and the dead, bringing peace and tranquility as he performs great miracles.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "There was something in the manner of our Lord which caused the bearers to stand still."
2. "All three raisings from the dead are wrought with words of power."
3. "Jésus-Christ ressuscite les morts comme il fait les actions les plus communes: il parle en maître à ceux qui dorment d’un sommeil éternel: et l’on sent bien qu’il est le Dieu des morts comme des vivans, jamais plus tranquille que lorsqu’il opère les plus grandes choses."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The Resurrection and the Life in human flesh, with a word of command, bringing back life to the dead body."
2. "Incarnate Compassion summoning its absolute power to dry a widow's tears!"

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Touched. Not fearing the ceremonial defilement of contact with the dead."
2. "The bier [σορος]. In classical Greek, originally, of a vessel for holding anything: sometimes of a cinerary urn."
3. "Here the open bier. Edersheim says 'of wicker-work.'"

### John Calvin:
1. "By this word Christ proved the truth of the saying of Paul, that God calleth those things which are not, as they were."
2. "He addresses the dead man, and makes himself be heard, so that death is suddenly changed into life."
3. "We are taught in what manner Christ quickens us spiritually by faith. It is when he infuses into his word a secret power, so that it enters into dead souls."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "That they had heard of Him is of course possible, but not certain."
2. "The simplicity of the narrative attests its truthfulness; the simplicity of the command attests the power of the Prince of Life."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Jesus touched the bier to make the bearers stop, which they did." 
2. "Jesus touched the bier to make the bearers stop, which they did (stood still)."

### John Gill:
1. "To touch a dead body, or the bone of man, or a grave, was forbidden by the law...but I do not find that touching a bier was ever forbidden." 
2. "Christ spoke as one that had the keys of death and the grave and divine power went along with his words, which raised the dead man to life."
3. "This he did as a signal, that the bearers should stop."
