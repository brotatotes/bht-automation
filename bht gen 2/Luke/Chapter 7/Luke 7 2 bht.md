# Luke 7:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The centurion highly valued his servant, who was not only a trustworthy and honored individual, but also a beloved and dear one. This servant, a slave, held a special place in his master's heart. The situation was dire, as the servant was gravely ill and on the verge of death, with no apparent human solution to his condition. Despite the difficult circumstances, the centurion's love and concern for his servant were evident, making this bond between master and servant all the more remarkable.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Albert Barnes:
1. Who was dear unto him - That is, he was valuable, trusty, and honored.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The duty of the centurion was chiefly confined to the regulation of his own corps, and the care of the watch."
2. "Dean Howson (" Companions of St. Paul ") remarks on the favorable impression left upon the mind by the officers of the Roman army mentioned in the New Testament."
3. "Dear [εντιμος] . Lit., held in honor or value. It does not necessarily imply an affectionate relation between the master and the servant, though such may well have existed. It may mean only that he was a valuable servant."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Who was highly valued by him as his only and faithful servant."
2. "It is further suggested that he was 'held in honor,' the object of his master's attachment, as was frequently the case in these days, between master and slave."
3. "The sickness was 'palsy' (Matthew)."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The centurions mentioned in the N.T. all seem to be fine men as Polybius states that the best men in the army had this position." 
2. "Even though a slave he was dear to him."
3. "He was about to die."

### John Gill:
1. "The centurion's servant was dear unto him...being an honest, upright, faithful, and obliging servant." 
2. "In all appearance his case was desperate, and there was no help for him by any human means."
