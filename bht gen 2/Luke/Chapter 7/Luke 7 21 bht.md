Luke, a physician himself, carefully distinguishes between those who were possessed by evil spirits and those who were simply diseased. ### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Marking the two classes of disease recognized in medical writings, chronic and acute."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Luke, the physician, distinguishes the possessed from the diseased."
2. "Luke 7:22-28 are almost word for word the same as Matthew 11:4-11."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Jesus gave the two disciples of John an example of the direct method. They had heard. Then they saw for themselves." 
2. "Diseases, plagues, evil spirits, all kinds of bodily ills, and he singles out the blind to whom in particular he bestowed sight, gave as a free gift seeing."

### John Gill:
1. "He, Jesus, cured many of their infirmities; bodily weaknesses and disorders: and plagues; which were inflicted on them as scourges and corrections for sin."
2. "And unto many that were blind he gave sight; freely, as an act of grace and kindness, without any merit, or motive, in them."
