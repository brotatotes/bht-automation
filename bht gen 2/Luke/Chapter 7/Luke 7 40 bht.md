# Luke 7:40 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Christ, in His infinite wisdom, acknowledges the thoughts and doubts of Simon and graciously addresses him by name. Despite Simon's pride and judgmental attitude, Jesus approaches him with gentleness and respect. This interaction reveals Christ's deep understanding and compassion for all people, even those who may seem unworthy.

### Henry Alford:
1. "There is an  inner personal appeal in the words addressing the Pharisee."
2. "The calling by name the especial  ἔχω σοί τι εἰπεῖν  , refer to the  inner thoughts of the heart."
3. "...and at once bring the answer  διδάσ  .,   εἰπέ  , so different from  οὗτος εἰ ἦν προφήτης."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The two debtors are the woman and Simon; the criminality of the one was ten times that of the other... but both being equally insolvent, both are with equal frankness forgiven." 
2. "Simon is made to own that the greatest debtor to forgiving mercy will cling to her Divine Benefactor with the deepest gratitude." 
3. "Does our Lord then admit that Simon was a forgiving man? Let us see."

### John Calvin:
1. "By this reply Christ shows how egregiously Simon was mistaken."
2. "Exposing to public view his silent and concealed thought, he proves himself to possess something more excellent than what belonged to the Prophets."
3. "In order to assure every one of us, that we have no reason to fear lest any sinner be rejected by him, who not only gives them kind and friendly invitations, but is prepared with equal liberality, and—as we might say—with outstretched arms, to receive them all."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Answering, the thought of the Pharisee, not some outward manifestations of displeasure, though such may have been displayed."
2. "I have somewhat to say unto thee. Direct personal address, implying a knowledge of Simon’s heart."
3. "Master, or, 'Teacher,' say on. The tone is respectful, as if the evidence of our Lord’s insight had already checked the doubt in Simon’s mind."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Jesus answers the thoughts and doubts of Simon and so shows that he knows all about the woman also."


### John Gill:
1. "Christ being God omniscient, knew not only the character and conversation of this woman, which were publicly known by all, that knew any thing of her, but also the secret thoughts and reasonings of the Pharisee" 
2. "which shows, that he was a prophet, in the sense of this man; yea, more than a prophet"
3. "This was a way of speaking in use with the Jews, giving leave to proceed in a discourse; and as Christ was now a guest in this man's house, he asks leave of him, and he grants him it"

### John Wesley:
1. And Jesus said, Simon, I have somewhat to say to thee — So tender and courteous am address does our Lord use even to a proud, censorious Pharisee!
