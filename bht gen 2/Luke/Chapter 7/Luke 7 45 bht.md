# Luke 7:45 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This woman, without ceasing, kissed Jesus' feet as an act of great affection and reverence. While the host failed to offer a simple kiss of welcome, she continued to show her love by repeatedly kissing His feet. Her humble actions demonstrated her genuine repentance and adoration for Jesus, contrasting sharply with the insincerity and neglect of the host.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 45. no kiss—of salutation. How
	much love was here? Any at all?

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Thou gavest me no kiss - The kiss was a token of affection or a common mode of salutation, and Simon had even neglected this mark of welcoming him to his house."
2. "How striking the difference between the conduct of Simon and this woman! He, with all the richness of a splendid preparation, had omitted the common marks of regard and affection. She, in humility, had bowed at his feet, had watered them with tears, and had not ceased to kiss them."
3. "The most splendid entertainments do not always express the greatest welcome. There may be in such entertainments much insincerity - much seeking of popularity or some other motive; but no such motive could have operated in inducing a broken-hearted sinner to wash the Saviour’s 'feet' with tears."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Ceased (die lipen). Only here in New Testament. Common in medical language, meaning to be intermittent, and to discontinue giving remedies for a time."
2. "To kiss [καταφιλουσα] . The compound verb has the force of kissing tenderly, caressing."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "No kiss, of welcome, on the face, came from the host."
2. "The unbidden woman coming in with the Guest at once kissed His feet, and continued to do so."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  Hath not ceased to kiss  (ου διελιπεν καταφιλουσα). Supplementary participle. 

### John Gill:
1. "The Jews have a saying that 'all kisses are foolish, excepting three; the kiss of grandeur or dignity... the kiss at parting... and the kiss at meeting.'"
2. "This woman... hath not ceased to kiss my feet; which shows, that this action was repeated by her times without number."
3. "She knowing that he was there, came thither after him."
