## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Marvin Vincent:
1. "This is the prostration of abject terror."
2. "The compound verb with ajna, up, implies what is conveyed by our phrase, lifting up the voice."
3. "Torment."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Fell down (προσεπεσεν). Second aorist active of προσπιπτω, to fall forward, towards, prostrate before one as here."
2. "The Most High God (του θεου του υψιστου). Uncertain whether του θεου genuine or not. But 'the Most High' clearly means God as already seen."
3. "The phrase is common among heathen (Numbers 24:16; Micah 6:6; Isaiah 14:14). The demoniac may have been a Gentile, but it is the demon here speaking."

### John Gill:
1. "He cried out, and fell down before him; that is, the man possessed with the devil did so, under his impulse, and through his agitation of him."
2. "With a loud voice said; which was the unclean spirit in the man: 'what have I to do with thee, Jesus, thou Son of God, most high? I beseech thee torment me not; i.e. before the time.'"
3. "Even afar off, at some considerable distance, he ran towards him."
