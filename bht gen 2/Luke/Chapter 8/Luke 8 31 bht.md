# Luke 8:31 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The devils begged Jesus not to send them into the abyss, the place of punishment for evil spirits. They pleaded to be allowed to go elsewhere, anywhere but to that terrifying place of torment.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "This word is sometimes used for Hades in general, but more usually in Scripture for the abode of damned spirits."
2. "The request is co-ordinate with the fear of torment expressed above."
3. "We must distinguish between ἄβυσσος, the ad interim place of torment, and the lake of fire into which the devil will be cast by Christ at the end."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Command them. The plural, referring to the legion."
2. "The deep [αβυσσον] . Lit., the bottomless. Transcribed into our abyss, as Rev. Mark has a quite different request, that he would not send them out of the country (v. 10)."
3. "The demons refer to their place of abode and torment."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Into the abyss. There can scarcely be a reasonable doubt that this means ‘hell,’ the place of punishment for evil spirits."
2. "We must distinguish between 'abyss,' the ad interim place of torment, and the lake of fire into which the devil will be cast by Christ at the end."
3. "The respite obtained by their entering into the herd of swine was a very brief one."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Into the abyss (εις την αβυσσον). Rare old word common in LXX from α privative and βαθυς (deep). So bottomless place (supply χωρα)." 
2. "The deep sea in Genesis 1:2; Genesis 7:11."
3. "The common receptacle of the dead in Romans 10:7 and especially the abode of demons as here and Revelation 9:1-11; Revelation 11:7; Revelation 17:8; Revelation 20:1; Revelation 20:3."

### John Gill:
1. "That is, all the devils, the whole legion of them, entreated Jesus, under whose power, and at whose dispose they were." 
2. "They desired, that when they went out of this man, they might not be ordered thither, or remanded to their former prison; for they knew that if he gave the word of command, they must obey."
3. "But that they might be suffered to continue in that country, and range about on earth, or be anywhere, rather than in hell."

### John Wesley:
1. The abyss — That is, the bottomless pit.
