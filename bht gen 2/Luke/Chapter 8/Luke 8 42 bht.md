## BHT:
The man had one dearly beloved daughter who was on the verge of death. The crowd pressed in on Jesus, making it difficult for Him to move forward. They all followed Him, eager to witness the miraculous healing that was about to take place.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "ἀπέθν  ., was dying. In Matt. she is represented as already dead."
2. "He is not aware of the subsequent message to Jaeirus, and narrates concisely and generally."
3. "The crowd seems to have followed to see what would happen at Jaeirus’s house: see Luke 8:54."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Thronged [συνεπνιγον] . With the idea of pressing together [συν] upon him : stifling. The simple verb is that rendered choke, as in vv. 8, 33.

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "An only daughter (θυγατηρ μονογενης). The same adjective used of the widow's son (Luke 7:12) and the epileptic boy (Luke 9:38) and of Jesus (John 1:18; John 3:16)."
2. "She lay a dying (απεθνησκεν). Imperfect active, she was dying. Matthew 9:18 has it that she has just died.
3. "Thronged (συνεπνιγον). Imperfect active of συμπνιγω, to press together, the verb used of the thorns choking the growing grain (Luke 8:14). It was a jam."

### John Gill:
1. "For he had one only daughter,.... And so exceedingly dear to him." 
2. "And she lay a dying, or 'was near death', as the Syriac and Persic versions; or 'was just ready to die', as the Ethiopic version."
3. "The people thronged him; such a vast multitude followed him to see the cure, that he was even crowded, and so pressed on all sides, that it was difficult to walk along."
