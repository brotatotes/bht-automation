# Luke 9:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus multiplied the loaves and fish, setting them before the multitude as a nourishing provision. He blessed them, ensuring there would be enough for everyone and that they would be satisfying. This act is described as breaking down the food, which was abundantly blessed and multiplied. He entrusted the disciples with the task of serving the people in an orderly manner.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "To set before [παραθειναι] . Lit., to set beside, since the table was at the side of the guest."
2. "A common word for serving up a meal."
3. "From the sense of placing beside, comes that of putting in charge, committing."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Fine example of the Greek article." 
2. "This picturesque imperfect is preceded by the aorist κατεκλασεν (brake), a single act." 
3. "We say 'break off' where here the Greek has 'break down' (or thoroughly), perfective use of κατα."

### John Gill:
1. "He blessed them...that they might multiply and increase, that there might be a sufficiency for all the people, and that they might be nourishing to them."
2. "In his most ancient copy, it is read, 'he blessed upon them'; which perfectly agrees with the Jewish way of speaking."
3. "He gave to the disciples to set before the multitude, as they sat in ranks, and in their distinct companies."
