## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  36. ] Luke gives the  result of our Lord’s command to them: the  command itself is related in Matthew 17:9 , and Mark 9:9 .

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Jesus was found alone—Moses and Elias are gone. Their work is done, and they have disappeared from the scene, feeling no doubt with their fellow servant the Baptist, 'He must increase, but I must decrease.'"
2. "The cloud too is gone, and the naked majestic Christ, braced in spirit, and enshrined in the reverent affection of His disciples, is left—to suffer!"
3. "kept it close—feeling, for once at least, that such things were unmeet as yet for the general gaze."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "When the voice was past [εν τω γενεσθαι την φωνην] . Lit., in the coming to pass of the voice." 
2. "Compare Matthew 17:14-21; Mark 9:14-29."

### Philip Schaff:
1. Luke 9:36.  And they held their peace.  The result of the command mentioned by Matthew and Mark.
	

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "It does not mean that it was 'after' the voice was past that Jesus was found alone, but simultaneously with it." 
2. "Should be rendered 'alone' there also."
3. "Luke notes that they in awe obeyed that command and it turns out that they finally forgot the lesson of this night's great experience."

### John Gill:
1. "Jesus was found alone; by his disciples; Moses and Elias being gone, and he in the same form in which he was before his transfiguration."
2. "And they kept it close; as Christ strictly charged them, when coming down from the mount with them."
3. "And told no man in those days any of those things which they had seen; no, not any of their fellow disciples, until that Christ was risen from the dead."
