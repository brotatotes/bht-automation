## BHT:
And behold, a man of the company, cried out, saying, Master, I beseech thee, look upon my son. The man, amidst the multitude, approached Jesus, addressing him as 'Teacher' and pleading with great humility for his son's well-being. The father, desperate for help, emphasized that his son was his only child, revealing the deep love and concern he had for him. By sharing this detail, the man hoped to stir Christ's compassion and evoke his healing power.

1. Master [διδασκαλε] . Teacher. Look upon [επιβλεψαι] . Only here and James 2:3. To look with pitying regard; and by medical writers of examining the condition of a patient.

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Teacher as in Mark 9:17." 
2. "Aorist active infinitive of επιβλεπω (επ, upon, βλεπω, look), common verb, but in the N.T. only here and James 2:3 except Luke 1:48 in quotation from LXX."

### John Gill:
1. "And behold, a man of the company,.... One that was in the company, and among the multitude, that met him: cried out; with great vehemence and earnestness." 
2. "Saying, Master; doctor, or 'Rabbi': I beseech thee: most humbly, for he was now on his knees: look upon my son: with pity and compassion, and help him." 
3. "For he is mine only child; wherefore he was dear unto him, and he was greatly concerned for him, and earnestly desirous of his being restored to health; and this he mentions, to move the compassion of Christ."
