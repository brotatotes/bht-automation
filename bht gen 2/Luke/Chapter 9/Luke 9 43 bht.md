# Luke 9:43 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The incredible power of God was clearly evident in this miraculous event, leaving both the disciples and the crowd astonished. It showcased the divine grandeur of Christ, which continued to rise and amaze those who witnessed His works. As the people marveled at the majestic display of God's mightiness in this miracle, the Scribes and Pharisees were left in confusion and wonder. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "the mighty power of God...the divine grandeur of Christ rising upon them daily."
2. "the majesty or mightiness of God in this last miracle, the transfiguration."
3. "By comparing Matthew 17:22 Mark 9:30, we gather that this had been the subject of conversation between the Twelve and their Master as they journeyed along."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Astonished [εξεπησσοντο] . See on Matthew 7:28. Mighty power [μεγαλειοτητι] . Used only by Luke and at 2 Peter 1:16, on which see note. He did [εποιει] . Imperfect. Better, was doing.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "And they were all astonished. The multitude in contrast with the disciples."
2. "At the majesty of God, as displayed in this miracle."
3. "But while all were marvelling. Quite indefinite. The conversation took place on the private journey to Capernaum, as we learn from the other accounts."

### John Gill:
1. "The great power of God was manifestly seen in it, to the astonishment of the disciples, who could not cure this child, and of the parent and friends of it, and of the whole multitude."
2. "...to the confusion of the Scribes and Pharisees: but while they wondered every one, at all things which Jesus did; and were applauding him for them, and speaking in his praise on account of them..."
3. "He said unto his disciples; privately, when they were alone together, the following words, that he might not seem to be lifted up with the praise of men; and also to show their inconstancy, that those who now admired him would one day crucify him; and to take off the thoughts of the disciples from a temporal kingdom and glory..."
