# Luke 9:52 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
As our Lord's ministry drew to a close, he underwent a significant change in behavior. Once avoiding attention, he now actively sought publicity. He even sent messengers ahead to prepare for his arrival in a Samaritan village. This was a bold move considering the Samaritans' usual treatment of Jews. By going through Samaria and openly declaring himself as the Messiah, Jesus challenged the Samaritans' expectations, which included confirmation of their rites and temple. His actions revealed that prior beliefs and superstitions shouldn't hinder us from making necessary preparations or seeking help from others.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The publicity now courted by our Lord is in remarkable contrast to His former avoidance of notice, and is a feature of the close of His ministry, giving rise to the accusation of ch. Luke 23:5." 
2. "It must have been to announce the coming of Jesus as the Messiah, which He did not conceal in Samaria, as in Judæa and Galilee, see John 4:26."
3. "They expected that the Messiah would have confirmed their anti-Jewish rites and Gerizim temple, instead of going up solemnly to Jerusalem, and thereby condemning them."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Messengers before his face... to make ready for him."
2. "He had not done this before; but now, instead of avoiding, He seems to court publicity."
3. "All now hastening to maturity."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The use of that word here shows that the word “angel” in the Bible does not always mean heavenly beings."
2. "He had no reason to expect that he would experience any kind treatment from the Samaritans if he came suddenly among them."
3. "He therefore made provision beforehand, and thus has shown us that it is not 'improper' to look out beforehand for the supply of our wants, and to guard against want and poverty."

### John Calvin:
1. "When men differ among themselves about the doctrines of religion, they readily break out into hatred of each other."
2. "It was an evidence of very bitter hatred to withhold food from the hungry, and lodging from those who were fatigued."
3. "But as the superstition once admitted kept so firm a hold of them, they strove, with wicked emulation, to maintain it to the last."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "To provide food and shelter for Him and the large party accompanying Him."
2. "Yet they probably also announced His coming as the Messiah; since in Samaria this was not concealed."


### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The Samaritans did not object when people went north from Jerusalem through their country."
2. "He was repudiating Mount Gerizim by going by it to Jerusalem."
3. "The only examples of the final use of ως with the infinitive in the N.T. are this one and Hebrews 7:9."

### John Gill:
1. "And sent messengers before his face... and entered into a village of the Samaritans." 
2. "Who very likely were his two disciples, James and John, since they so highly resented the ill treatment their master met with from the Samaritans."
3. "To make ready for him; to prepare a lodging, and proper food for him and his disciples, as they passed on in their journey."

### John Wesley:
1. He sent messengers to make ready — A lodging and needful entertainment for him and those with him.
