# Luke 9:55 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus responded to his disciples, saying, Ye know not what manner of spirit ye are of. He recognized that their actions, driven by anger and revenge, were contrary to the spirit of Christianity. Instead, the spirit of Christianity is one of peace, gentleness, and love. Jesus reminded them that the purpose of his coming was not to destroy lives, but to save them. This serves as a reminder for all believers to examine their own spirits and ensure they are aligned with the teachings of Christ.

### John Calvin:
1. "By this reply he not only restrained the unbridled fury of the two disciples, but laid down a rule to all of us not to indulge our temper."
2. "Frequently, too, it happens, that the impure feelings of the flesh are mingled with their zeal, and that those who appear to be the keenest zealots for the glory of God are blinded by the private feelings of the flesh."
3. "And therefore the examples of the saints are no defense to us, unless the same Spirit that directed them dwell in us."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Ye know not what manner of spirit ye are of."
2. "Ye know not of what spirit you are the instruments when speaking thus; you think that you are working a miracle of faith in my service, but you are obeying a spirit alien from mine."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "But he turned. Dramatic act."
2. "Ye know not what manner of spirit ye are of."
3. "For the Son of Man came not to destroy men's lives, but to save them."

### John Gill:
1. "He turned and rebuked them...sharply reproved them for their intemperate zeal, their passion of wrath, and anger, and desire of revenge."
2. "[Jesus] said, ye know not what manner of spirit ye are of...this is not the true spirit of zeal, but of anger and revenge."
3. "So good men, for want of attention, may not know sometimes from what spirit they act; taking that for a good one, which is a very bad one."

### John Wesley:
1. Ye know not what manner of spirit — The spirit of Christianity is. It is not a spirit of wrath and vengeance, but of peace, and gentleness, and love.
