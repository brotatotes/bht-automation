# Luke 9:60 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Go and proclaim the kingdom of God, announcing that the Gospel dispensation is now unfolding and the reign of the Messiah is being established. Preach about the kingdom of grace, emphasizing that it is not based on external rituals, but on righteousness, peace, and joy in the Holy Spirit. Declare the truths about the kingdom of glory and eternal life, emphasizing the necessity of being born again and having a righteousness beyond one's own to partake in everlasting joy.

### Henry Alford:
1. "διάγ . κ . τ . λ ., peculiar to Luke, and shews the independence of his source of information." 
2. "Am I wrong in supposing also, that it connects this incident with the sending out of the Seventy, which follows immediately afterwards?"

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Their dead [τους εαυτων νεκρους] . As Rev., their own dead. Preach [διαγγελλε] . Publish abroad, as Rev. dia, throughout all regions. 61, 62. Peculiar to Luke.

### John Calvin:
1. "Had he remained in a private station, there would have been no absolute necessity for leaving his father, provided he did not forsake the Gospel on his father’s account."
2. "But the preaching of the Gospel does not allow him to remain at home, and therefore Christ properly takes him away from his father."
3. "While the amazing goodness of Christ appears in bestowing so honorable an office on a man who was still so weak, it deserves our notice, that the fault which still cleaved to him is corrected, and is not overlooked and encouraged."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "'Publish abroad,' pointing to a wide announcement." 
2. "This incident was connected with the sending out of the Seventy."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Leave the dead to bury their own dead... the spiritually dead can bury the literally dead."
2. "The harshness of this proverb to the scribe probably is due to the fact that he was manifestly using his aged father as an excuse for not giving Christ active service."
3. "The scribe's duty is put sharply... Many a preacher has had to face a similar delicate problem of duty to father, mother, brothers, sisters and the call to preach."

### John Gill:
1. "Go thou and preach the kingdom of God; that the kingdom of heaven is at hand, the Gospel dispensation is now ushering in, and the kingdom of the Messiah is setting up."
2. "Go and publish the things concerning the kingdom of grace, which lies not in outward rites and ceremonies, but in righteousness and peace, and joy in the Holy Ghost."
3. "Declare the things relating to the kingdom of glory, and eternal life and happiness; assert, that unless a man is born again, and has a better righteousness than his own, he is neither fit for, nor has he a right unto everlasting bliss."
