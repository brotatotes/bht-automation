# Luke 9:62 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
No one is fit for the kingdom of God if they become distracted by worldly affairs while undertaking the work of God. It is essential for those who are called to the ministry to wholeheartedly commit themselves to serving God, without allowing their attention to be divided. Only by fully devoting themselves to the kingdom of God can they effectively fulfill their calling.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "No man, c.—As ploughing requires an eye intent on the furrow to be made, and is marred the instant one turns about, so will they come short of salvation who prosecute the work of God with a distracted attention, a divided heart."
2. "Though the reference seems chiefly to ministers, the application is general."
3. "It is not actual return to the world, but a reluctance to break with it."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "To put one’s hand to a plow is a proverbial expression to signify undertaking any business."
2. "He that enters on it must do it with his whole heart."
3. "How searching is this test to those who profess to be Christians!"

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The figure is that of a man who, while engaged in labor, instead of keeping his eye on the furrow which he is drawing, looks behind at some object which attracts his interest. He is only half at work, and half - work only will be the result" (Godet).
2. "Fit [ευθετος] . Lit., well - placed : adjusted."

### John Calvin:
1. "He who, after having put his hand to the plough, shall look back, is unfit for the kingdom of God." 
2. "They are said to look back, who become involved in the cares of the world, so as to allow themselves to be withdrawn from the right path."
3. "Particularly, when they plunge themselves into those employments which disqualify them to follow Christ."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "No man, having put his hand to the plough, etc. The figure is easily understood, especially when we remember that the plough used in the East was easily overturned."
2. "Such labor, with divided service and longing looks backward will be profitless and doubly toilsome."
3. "These conversations have one common lesson: conditional following of Christ is impossible."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The ploughman who does not bend attentively to his work goes crooked."
2. "The call to turn back is often urgent."
3. "The first case is that of inconsiderate impulse, the second that of conflicting duties, the third that of a divided mind."

### John Gill:
1. "The ploughman ought to look before him, on his plough, and the ground he is ploughing, or he is not fit to be a ploughman; nor will he make proper furrows, or do his work well."
2. "He that enters upon the ministerial work, and looks back, and engages himself in the affairs of the world, sets his heart on them, and spends his time in them, is not fit for the kingdom of God."
3. "He cannot serve God and mammon, his own interest, and the interest of Christ; he cannot rightly perform the work of the ministry, whilst his thoughts and time are taken up in the affairs of the world."

### John Wesley:
1. Is fit for the kingdom of God — Either to propagate or to receive it.
