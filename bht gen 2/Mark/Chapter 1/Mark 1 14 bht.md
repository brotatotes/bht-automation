# Mark 1:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus went to Galilee after the arrest of John, returning back to where He originally came from. He preached the Gospel of the kingdom of God, the good news of the Messiah and the Gospel dispensation. The true significance of this kingdom lies not in worldly externals, but in righteousness, peace, and joy.

### Henry Alford:
1. "παραδ. seems to have been the usual and well-known term for the imprisonment of John."
2. "τὸ εὐαγ. τ. θ."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Jesus came into Galilee - He left Judea and went into the more retired country of Galilee."
2. "He supposed that if he remained in Judea, Herod would also persecute him and attempt to take his life."
3. "Hence, we may learn that when we have great duties to perform for the church of God, we are not to endanger our lives wantonly."

### John Calvin:
1. "Christ...entered into the exercise of his office, when he arrived at Galilee." 
2. "He exhorts the Jews to conversion, because the kingdom of God is at hand: that is, because God undertakes to govern his people, which is true and perfect happiness." 
3. "When he promises to us mercy, he calls us to deny the flesh. We must observe the designation which Paul gives to the Gospel, the kingdom of God: for hence we learn, that by the preaching of the Gospel the kingdom of God is set up and established among men, and that in no other way does God reign among men."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Jesus came into Galilee...to reach the Galilean masses who had been impressed by the preaching of John."
2. "Preaching the gospel of God...from the latter passage the words: 'of the kingdom' have crept in here."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The arrest of John had much to do with the departure of Jesus from Judea to Galilee."
2. "The gospel that comes from God."
3. "Both repent and believe in the gospel."

### John Gill:
1. "Jesus came into Galilee: again, from whence he came to be baptized of John." 
2. "preaching the Gospel of the kingdom of God: the good news and glad tidings of the kingdom of the Messiah, or Gospel dispensation."
3. "which lies not in worldly pomp and splendour, in outward observances, in legal rites and ceremonies, but in righteousness, peace, and joy; in peace and pardon by the blood of Christ, in justification by his righteousness, and in free and full salvation by him."
