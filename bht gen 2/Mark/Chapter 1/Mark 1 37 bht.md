### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "And when they had found him—evidently after some search."
2. "All men seek for thee—By this time, 'the multitudes' who, according to Luke, 'sought after Him' and who had set out on the same errand—would have arrived."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. AllAll the people of Capernaum, all are seeking thee. The continuous present tense. So Rev., better than A. V. The all is peculiar to Mark.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Search and uncertainty is implied, since He had retired to an unfrequented spot."
2. "Peter said this to induce Him to return, and the crowd besought Him to stay."
3. "The will of the multitude did not govern Him, as they supposed, hence the reply in the next verse."

### John Gill:
1. "In the desert and solitary place, where he had been praying"
2. "All men seek for thee; not all the men in the world, nor, it may be, all the inhabitants of Capernaum, but a large number of them"
3. "The disciples were loath that such an opportunity of doing good should be missed, and therefore sought for him, till they found him."
