### Philip Schaff:
1. "And beyond the Jordan (Perea). The common reading is ‘through the farther side of (beyond) the Jordan.’ John’s narrative shows that he visited Jerusalem at least twice in the interval, and hence this account is literally correct."
2. "As they had done on the previous visit (John 16:41, 42), or as occurred in Galilee (Mark 10:2-9)."
3. "The accounts of Mark and Matthew agree closely, but the former puts the law of Moses first, and then that of Paradise."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "A great deal has intervened between the events at the close of Mark 9 and those in the beginning of Mark 10."
2. "Jesus has begun his last journey to Jerusalem going north through Samaria, Galilee, across the Jordan into Perea, and back into Judea near Jericho."
3. "Many of them are followers of Jesus from Galilee or at least kindly disposed towards him. They go together with Jesus."

### John Gill:
1. "And he arose from thence,.... From Galilee, and particularly from Capernaum:and cometh into the coasts of Judea; into those places, which bordered on that part of the land of Israel, called Judea, as distinct from Galilee."
2. "it had been his custom before, and so it was wherever he went, to preach the word of God, and teach men what was profitable to them, and useful for the good of their immortal souls."
3. "and not only so, but healed many of them of their bodily disorders, as Matthew relates, Matthew 19:2."
