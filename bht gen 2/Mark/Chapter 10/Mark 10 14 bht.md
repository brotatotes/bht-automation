# Mark 10:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus was deeply moved and indignant when his disciples tried to prevent children from coming to him. It was important for them to understand that these children were not at fault and should not be blamed, as they were innocent and humble. Jesus wanted everyone, including children, to have access to his blessings. He emphasized that the kingdom of God is made up of people who have a child-like temperament, reflecting innocence and humility. Jesus was displeased with his disciples for not recognizing the importance of allowing the little children to approach him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Not only may the little infants be brought to Him, but in order for us who are mature to come to Him, we must cast away all that wherein our maturity has caused us to differ from them, and become LIKE THEM."
2. "Not only is Infant Baptism justified, but it is the NORMAL PATTERN OF ALL BAPTISM; none can enter God's kingdom, except as an infant."
3. "In adult baptism, the exceptional case, we strive to secure that state of simplicity and childlikeness, which in the infant we have ready and undoubted to our hands."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "How careful we should be not to call forth His displeasure, by keeping children from Him."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He was moved with indignation...and is a strong word of deep emotion."
2. "Suffer the little children to come unto me...Surely it ought to be a joy to parents to bring their children to Jesus."
3. "There are parents who will have to give answer to God for keeping their children away from Jesus."

### John Gill:
1. "He was much displeased; with his disciples, who took too much upon them; for they ought first, to have known their master's will; whether it was his pleasure to grant the favour desired for these children..."
2. "...suffer the little children to come unto me, and forbid them not, for of such is the kingdom of God; or 'of them who' are, 'as these', as the Syriac version, renders the words; or, as the Arabic, who 'are like to these'; and the Persic, who are 'like to these little children'; in innocence and humility..."

### John Wesley:
1. "Jesus seeing it was much displeased — At their blaming those who were not blame worthy: and endeavouring to hinder the children from receiving a blessing."
2. "Of such is the kingdom of God — The members of the kingdom which I am come to set up in the world are such as these, as well as grown persons, of a child-like temper."
