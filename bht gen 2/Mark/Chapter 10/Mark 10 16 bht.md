## BHT:
Jesus demonstrated his deep love and care for children by taking them up in his arms and putting his hands on them, expressing tenderness and affection. He went beyond what was asked of him, blessing and praying over each child, following the Jewish custom. This precious moment was undoubtedly significant for both the mothers and their children, as they experienced the touch and blessing of the Savior.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "κατ ευλόγει, like all such compounds, is more forcible and complete than the simple verb would have been."
2. "It may be rendered He fervently blessed them."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Took them in his arms." See on 9 86.
2. "Put his hands upon them and blessed them. The best texts read kateulogei, tiqeiv tav ceirav ejp aujta, blessed them, laying his hands upon them; including the laying on of hands in the blessing."
3. "The compound rendered blessed occurs only here in the New Testament. It is stronger than the simple form and expresses the earnestness of Christ 's interest. Alford renders fervently blessed."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Mark loves to tell of our Lord's gestures." Mark 10:16
2. "Christ did more for the children than those who brought them asked, as He always does." Mark 10:16
3. "The servants of such a Master should welcome children to His fold." Mark 10:16

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He took them in his arms (εναγκαλισαμενος). A distinct rebuke to the protest of the over-particular disciples."
2. "So with tender fondling Jesus repeatedly blessed (κατευλογε, imperfect), laying his hands upon each of them (τιθεις, present participle)."
3. "It was a great moment for each mother and child."

### John Gill:
1. "He took them up in his arms... expresses great tenderness towards them, and affection for them." 
2. "He put his hands on the children, according to the custom of the Jews, and then prayed over them, and wished all happiness and prosperity to them."
