# Mark 11:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
When evening arrived, Jesus left the city. He may have gone to Bethany where he had stayed the night before with Lazarus, Martha, and Mary, or perhaps he went to the Mount of Olives where he often spent the night in prayer. It is likely that he took his disciples with him as well.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  19. ] See note on Matthew 21:17 . On the Sunday and Monday evenings, our Lord appears to have gone  to Bethany .

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "When evening was come [οταν]. Lit., whenever evening came on; not on the evening of the purging of the temple merely, but each day at evening."
2. "All the details are peculiar to Mark. Compare Matthew 21:20-22."

### Philip Schaff:
1. Mark 11:19.  Out of the city. To Bethany, as on the evening previous (Mark 11:11). Comp. Matthew 21:17, which also refers to Monday evening.
	

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Every evening (οταν οψε εγενετο). Literally, whenever evening came on or more exactly whenever it became late."
2. "The use of οταν (οτε αν) with the aorist indicative is like οπου αν with the imperfect indicative (εισεπορευετο) and οσο αν with the aorist indicative (ηψαντο) in Mark 6:56."
3. "The use of αν makes the clause more indefinite and general, as here, unless it renders it more definite, a curious result, but true."

### John Gill:
1. "And when even was come, he went out of the city."
- "He went to Bethany, where he had lodged the last night, with Lazarus, Martha, and Mary; or to the Mount of Olives."
2. "probably he went to Bethany, where he had lodged the last night, with Lazarus, Martha, and Mary; or to the Mount of Olives, where he sometimes spent the night in prayer."
3. "Christ took his disciples with him, as is evident from the following verse."
