Say that the Lord, who is Jesus Himself, has need of it; the Lord who is the ruler of everything in heaven and earth. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The pres. ἀποστέλλει, is used of future things whose occurrence is undoubted."
2. "From the ancient interpolation of πάλιν, it seems that they were understood all to belong to ὁ κύριος 'the Lord hath need of it, and will immediately send it [back].'"
3. "But verisimilitude seems to me to be against it: and the final clause in Mark 11:6, καὶ ἀφῆκαν αὐτούς, appears to correspond with this."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "And straightway he will send, literally, ‘sendeth,’ him again hither." 
2. "Here the word ‘again’ (found in the best authorities) compels us to take it as part of the message, a promise to return the colt soon."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The Lord...applied to Jesus by himself." 
2. "Present indicative in futuristic sense." 
3. "Matthew 21:3 has the future αποστελε."

### John Gill:
1. "Say ye that the Lord hath need of him; our Lord and yours, the Lord of heaven and earth, and all things in it..."
2. "...and so would at once understand by the language who it was for, and let it go..."
3. "...he will send him with all readiness and cheerfulness, without the least hesitation, or making any dispute about it."
