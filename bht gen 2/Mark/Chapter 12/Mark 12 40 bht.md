### Albert Barnes:
1. "Which devour the families of widows, or the means of supporting their families... They took advantage of their ignorance and their unprotected state, and either extorted large sums for their counsel, or perverted the property to their own use."
2. "If there is any sin of special enormity, it is that of taking advantage of the circumstances of the poor, the needy, and the helpless, to wrong them out of the pittance on which they depend for the support of their families."
3. "And as God is the friend of the widow and the fatherless, it may be expected that such will be visited with heavy condemnation."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "People often left their whole fortune to the temple, and a good deal of the temple-money went, in the end, to the Scribes and Pharisees."
2. "The Scribes were universally employed in making wills and conveyances of property."
3. "They may have abused their influence with widows."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "They devour widows’ houses, and that too while they are praying at great length."
2. "Ecclesiastical officials may repeat this crime, by attaching to themselves the defenseless classes here represented by 'widows' with the design of obtaining control of their property."
3. "Pharisaism, in all ages and organizations, has encouraged this."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Terrible pictures of civil wrong by graft grabbing the homes of helpless widows." 
2. "They inveigled widows into giving their homes to the temple and took it for themselves."
3. "For a pretence make long prayers...pretence of extra piety while robbing the widows and pushing themselves to the fore."
