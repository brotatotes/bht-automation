# Mark 13:37 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Be on your guard and stay awake, for it is fitting for all to watch, cast off sloth, and be diligent in the face of various temptations. The command to watch applies to believers of all times and places, considering the many dangers and uncertainties we may encounter. We must remain vigilant due to the suddenness of death and the unpredictability of Christ's return.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "And what I say unto you—this discourse, it will be remembered, was delivered in private."
2. "I say unto all, Watch—anticipating and requiring the diffusion of His teaching by them among all His disciples, and its perpetuation through all time."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "I say unto all, Watch - This command was proper...we should therefore be ready."
2. "If we are his friends...we 'are' ready."
3. "Oh how important it is to be ready, and to escape the awful sufferings of an eternal hell!"

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Watch. The closing and summary word is the stronger word of verse 35 Be awake and on guard. 

### Philip Schaff:
1. "I say unto all, watch." 
2. "Though the Apostles and the ministry are watchmen and porters, yet all believers are to be incessantly watchful and for the same reasons."
3. "The time of our Lord's coming, whether at our death or in His personal appearing, is uncertain; therefore we should always be ready."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  Watch  (γρηγορειτε). Be on the watch. Present imperative of a verb made on the second perfect, εγρηγορα, to be awake. Stay awake till the Lord comes. 

### John Gill:
1. "...whom it became to watch, and be upon their guard, and cast off all sloth and negligence..."
2. "...the same exhortation is suitable to saints in all ages, times, and places, on account of the various snares, temptations, and evils..."
3. "...the suddenness of death, and the uncertainty of the second coming of Christ."
