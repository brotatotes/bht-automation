# Mark 14:10 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Judas Iscariot, one of the twelve, took it upon himself to approach the chief priests to betray Jesus. This action of Judas reveals his treacherous intentions, driven by both his greed and his sense of revenge. He carried out this betrayal, prompted by Satanic influence and fueled by his resentment towards Jesus.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "And Judas Iscariot, one of the twelve, went unto the chief priests, to betray him unto them—that is, to make his proposals, and to bargain with them." 
2. "The thirty pieces of silver were thirty shekels, the fine paid for man- or maid-servant accidentally killed, and equal to between four and five pounds sterling." 
3. "A goodly price that I was prized at of them!"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He that was one of the twelve" - Note the article here, "the one of the twelve." 
2. "Mark here calls Judas the primate among the twelve." 
3. "Rather he means to call attention to the idea that he was the one of the twelve who did this deed."

### John Gill:
1. "And Judas Iscariot, one of the twelve... went unto the chief priests... to betray him unto them." 
2. "Understanding the chief priests were in consultation together at Caiaphas's house, how to apprehend Jesus, and put him to death, went directly to them, unsent for, and unthought of by them."
3. "Which Satan had put into his heart, and what his avarice and revenge for the late action of the woman, and Christ's defence of it, prompted him to."

### John Wesley:
1. Judas went to the chief priests — Immediately after this reproof, having anger now added to his covetousness. Matthew 26:14; Luke 22:3.
