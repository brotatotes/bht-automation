# Mark 14:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus sending two disciples, Peter and John, to the city of Jerusalem to prepare for the Passover, demonstrated his knowledge and influence over all things, revealing his divine omniscience. The specific instruction to look for a man carrying a pitcher of water highlighted his authority over even the seemingly mundane details of everyday life. This event served as a powerful testimony to the disciples and those who witnessed it, affirming Jesus' divine nature and reinforcing their faith in him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Marvin Vincent:
1. A man. A slave probably, whose business it was to draw water. See Deuteronomy 19:11. Pitcher. Of earthenware : keramion from keramov potter 's clay.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "A man bearing a pitcher of water. This was the sign by which they should know him."
2. "The vessel was earthen, but the fact has no necessary significance."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Two of his disciples" (δυο των μαθητων αυτου). Luke 22:8 names them, Peter and John.
2. "Bearing a pitcher of water" (κεραμιον υδατος βασταζων). This item also in Luke, but not in Matthew.

### John Gill:
1. "And he sendeth forth two of his disciples,.... Peter and John, as appears from Luke 22:8;"
2. "and saith unto them, go ye into the city; the city of Jerusalem; for there only the passover might be eaten, Deuteronomy 26:2;"
3. "and there shall meet you a man bearing a pitcher of water; a servant of the master of the house that was sent for water, to mix with the wine, at the passover: follow him; into the house to which he goes."

### John Wesley:
1. "It was highly seasonable for our Lord to give them this additional proof both of his knowing all things, and of his influence over the minds of men."
