Witnessing Jesus' last cry and the manner in which He gave up His spirit, the centurion and his fellow soldiers were convinced that Jesus was indeed the Son of God. This powerful declaration from the centurion, along with the soldiers' agreement, signifies the impact of Jesus' death on those who were present. ### Marvin Vincent:
1. Son of God. Not the Son of God, which Rev. has retained, but a son of God. To the centurion Christ was a hero or demigod. See on Matthew 27:54.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The Lion of Judah is, even in His departing, a dying lion."
2. "The centurion saw that he so gave up the ghost."
3. "On the centurion's language, see on Matthew 27:54."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The centurion a Latin word (centurio) used also in verse Mark 15:44 and here only in the N.T."
2. "This description alone in Mark, picturing the centurion 'watching Jesus.'"
3. "With the darkness and the earthquake."

### John Gill:
1. "And when the centurion... saw that he so cried out, and gave up the ghost;... he said, truly this man was the Son of God." 
2. "He said, truly this man was the Son of God; and so said the rest of the soldiers that were with them..."
3. "...the next moment expired: he said, truly this man was the Son of God."
