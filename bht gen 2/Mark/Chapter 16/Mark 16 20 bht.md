## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "No inference can be drawn from this word as to the date of the fragment."
2. "The inference therefore seems to me to be, that it is an authentic fragment, placed as a completion of the Gospel in very early times."
3. "Coming to us with very weighty sanction, and having strong claims on our reception and reverence."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "And they went forth, and preached everywhere, the Lord working with them, and confirming the word with signs following." 
2. "We have in this closing verse a most important link of connection with the Acts of the Apostles, where He who directed all the movements of the infant Church is perpetually styled 'THE LORD'."
3. "Thus illustrating His own promise for the rounding and building up of the Church, 'LO, I AM WITH YOU alway!'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The Lord worked with them - By miracles; by removing obstacles; by supporting them; and by giving the gospel success and making it effectual to saving men."
2. "By signs following - By attending miracles. By raising the dead, healing the sick, etc., as signs that God was with them, and had sent them forth to preach."
3. "The condition of the person who will not believe is fearfully awful. The Son of God has solemnly declared that he shall be damned. He will judge the world, and there is none that can deliver out of his hand."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Following closely: force of ejpi." 
2. "A manuscript of the eighth or ninth century, known as L, has, at the close of verse 8, these words: 'But all the things enjoined they announced without delay to those who were around Peter (i.e., to Peter and those who were with him). And afterward Jesus himself, from the east unto the west, sent forth through them the sacred and incorruptible message of eternal salvation.'"
3. "Some two hundred expressions or phrases may be found which are common to Luke and Paul, and more or less foreign to other New Testament writers."

### John Calvin:
1. "Christ was taken up into heaven, not to enjoy blessed rest at a distance from us, but to govern the world for the salvation of all believers."
2. "There is great emphasis, therefore, in the words, they went out and preached everywhere - men who but lately shut themselves up, trembling and silent, in their prison."
3. "And yet by this mode of expression he does not represent them as sharing their work or labor with the grace of God, as if they contributed anything to it of themselves; but simply means that they were assisted by God."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "And they went forth...to preach everywhere." 
2. "The Lord working, etc. The fulfilment of the promise in Mark 16:17-18, is here stated."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The Lord working with them."
2. "And they announced briefly to Peter and those around him all the things enjoined."
3. "And after these things Jesus himself also sent forth through them from the east even unto the west the holy and incorruptible proclamation of the eternal salvation."

### John Gill:
1. "And preached every where; not only in Judea, and in the neighbouring countries, but all over the world, in process of time."
2. "The Lord working with them; making their ministry useful, for the conviction and conversion of large multitudes, and for the forming and settling abundance of Gospel churches, and for the comfort and edification of the saints."
3. "And confirming the word with signs following; the Arabic version adds 'them'; or 'which they did', as the Syriac and Persic versions render it; not by their own power, but, as the latter of these versions adds, 'by the help of our Lord'."

### John Wesley:
1. "They preached every where."
2. "At the time St. Mark wrote, the apostles had already gone into all the known world. Romans 10:18."
3. "Each of them was known where he preached, and the name of Christ only was known throughout the world."
