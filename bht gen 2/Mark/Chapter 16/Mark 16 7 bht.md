# Mark 16:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
But go your way, tell his disciples and Peter. Though Peter denied Jesus, he is specifically mentioned in this message to comfort him in his great sorrow and encourage him to meet Jesus with the other disciples. This shows the amazing goodness and love of Christ towards Peter, despite his denial.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "But now rather do ye..."
2. "It is hardly perhaps likely that the denial of Peter was the ground of this message, though it is difficult not to connect the two in the mind."
3. "The mention of him here is probably merely official as the ‘primus inter pares.’ We cannot say that others of the Apostles may not have denied their Master besides Peter."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "But go your way, tell his disciples and Peter—This Second Gospel, being drawn up—as all the earliest tradition states—under the eye of Peter, or from materials chiefly furnished by him, there is something deeply affecting in the preservation of this little clause by Mark alone."
2. "That he goeth before you into Galilee; there shall ye see him, as he said unto you—(See on :-)."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "But. Emphatic: instead of lingering here, go tell, etc."
2. "And Peter. A special token of love to this one who had denied Him, and a recognition of his prominence among his equals."
3. "As he said to you. Chap. Mark 14:28; see on Matthew 28:7."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Peter remembered gratefully this special message from the Risen Christ."
2. "An event that changed doubt to certainty with the apostles."
3. "Promised meeting in Galilee."

### John Gill:
1. "Peter is particularly mentioned...to comfort him in his great sorrow, on account of his fall; and to encourage him to meet Christ with the rest of his disciples."
2. "This is a kind intimation, in favour of Peter...to relate everything that either aggravated his own crime, or illustrated the grace of God, and love of Christ towards him."
3. "That he goeth before you into Galilee, there shall ye see him; as he said unto you."

### John Wesley:
1. And Peter — Though he so oft denied his Lord. What amazing goodness was this!
