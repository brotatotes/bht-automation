# Mark 2:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The stunning display of power left the onlookers in awe, even causing wonder among the scribes. This miraculous act was not only physical healing, but it also demonstrated Jesus' authority and ability to forgive sins. The astonishment was so great that they couldn't find words to describe what they had witnessed. This event highlighted the extraordinary nature of Jesus' ministry, leaving the people in a state of mixed emotions, ranging from wonder to gratitude and fear.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The bed had borne the man: now the man bore the bed."
2. "He who could work such a glorious miracle of healing, must indeed 'have power on earth to forgive sins.'"
3. "At forgiving power they wondered not, but that a man, to all appearance like one of themselves, should possess it!"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Before them all. A hint that the account comes from an eye-witness."
2. "The impression produced was a very powerful one, and the emotions were of a mixed character: wonder, gratitude, and fear."
3. "The remarkable feature (Luke: 'strange things'), was the attestation of the miracle to the power to forgive sins."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The amazement (εξιστασθα, our ecstasy, as Luke 5:26 has it), was too general and great for words."
2. "The people could only say: 'We never saw it on this fashion' (Hουτως ουδεποτε ειδαμεν)."
3. "Jesus had acted with the power of God and claimed equality with God and had made good his claim."

### John Gill:
1. "Power going along with the words of Christ, he found himself perfectly well; and at once sprung up from off his bed." 
2. "They easily perceived it was a preternatural action, and what could never be done by any mere man; they therefore attribute it to God, and give him the glory of it."
3. "These were the 'multitude', as Matthew says, who attended on the ministry of Christ, and followed him from place to place, and had a high opinion of him, as a great and good man; though they did not believe in him as the Messiah, and did not know him to be the Son of God."

### John Wesley:
1. They were all amazed — Even the scribes themselves for a time.
