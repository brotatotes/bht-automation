# Mark 3:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Scribes and Pharisees closely watched Him, waiting for an opportunity to accuse Him. They played the spy, diligently observing His actions, seeking to find fault with Him. Their intention was to catch Him in the act of violating their rules, particularly in regard to healing on the Sabbath. Their pride, anger, and shame had transformed into malice, fueling their determination to accuse Him.

### Henry Alford:

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "They watched [παρετηρουν] . Imperfect tense. They kept watching. The compound verb, with para, by the side of, means to watch carefully or closely, as one who dogs another's steps, keeping beside or near him."
2. "Wyc., They aspieden him : i e., played the spy."
3. "He would heal [θεραπευσει] . Future tense : whether he will heal, the reader being placed at the time of the watching, and looking forward to the future."

### Philip Schaff:
1. Mark 3:2.  And they watched him. Watched Him closely.
	 Whether he would. Lit., ‘will’ Mark’s account being in the present tense.
	

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They watched...were there ready to catch him in the act if he should dare to violate their rules." 
2. "Probably the same Pharisees are present now as then."
3. "They were determined to accuse him. The sabbath controversy offered the best opening."

### John Gill:
1. "They observed him diligently, and kept their eyes upon him... knowing his readiness to do good."
2. "They put a question to him, whether it was lawful to heal on the sabbath day? with this view, that they might... have something to accuse him of."
3. "They might expect he would [heal on the sabbath day], that they might accuse him."

### John Wesley:
1. "Pride, anger, and shame, after being so often put to silence, began now to ripen into malice."
