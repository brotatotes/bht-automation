The press of people was so great that Jesus and his disciples could not find any time or space to eat. Despite the necessity of nourishment, Jesus prioritized the needs of the people, putting their spiritual well-being above his physical hunger. ### Albert Barnes:
1. "The affairs of religion may so occupy the attention of ministers and others as to prevent their engaging in their customary pursuits."
2. "Religion is all-important - far more important than the ordinary business of this life."
3. "At the same time, it is true that religion is ordinarily consistent with a close attention to worldly business. It promotes industry, economy, order, neatness, and punctuality - all indispensable to worldly prosperity."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Again. Glancing back to the many notices of crowds in the preceding narrative."
2. "This reassembling of the multitudes, and its interference with the repast of Christ and the disciples."
3. "is peculiar to Mark."

### John Calvin:
1. "It is wonderful that such wickedness should have been found among the relatives of Christ, who ought to have been the first to aid him in advancing the kingdom of God."
2. "Hence we learn, first, how great is the blindness of the human mind, in forming such perverse judgments about the glory of God when openly displayed."
3. "Let us learn, in the second place, that the light of faith does not proceed from flesh and blood, but from heavenly grace, that no man may glory in anything else than in the regeneration of the Spirit."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "If the last clause of Mark 3:19 means a return to Capernaum, ‘again ‘must refer to chap. Mark 2:1."
2. "A vivid description of the thronging. Our Lord and His disciples could not find time to have their regular meals."
3. "Notice the excitement and popularity was now at its height; the opposition now takes definite form and stems the tide."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The jam was so great that they could not rest, could not eat, and apparently Jesus could not even teach."
2. "The crowd reassembled at once on Christ's return from the mountain."

### John Gill:
1. "These understanding that he was come down from thence, and was returned to Capernaum, and was at Simon's house, flocked thither, in great numbers, to see his person, hear his doctrines, and observe his miracles." 
2. "The press was so great, and their importunities so urgent, either to hear him preach, or have their sick healed, that Christ, and his disciples, had neither room nor opportunity to eat some food for the refreshment of nature."
3. "Though it was very necessary, and high time they had, especially Christ, who had been up all night, which he had spent in prayer; and had been very busy that morning in calling and appointing his apostles, and instructing them what they should do."

### John Wesley:
1. To eat bread — That is, to take any subsistence.
