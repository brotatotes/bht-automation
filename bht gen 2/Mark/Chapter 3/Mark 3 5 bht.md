# Mark 3:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus, filled with both anger and compassion, looked upon the people with a stern countenance, expressing indignation at their sinful state yet without sin or desire for revenge. He was pained by their hard-heartedness, but his anger was not inconsistent with his love and pity. Instead, it was a sign of his moral health and righteous anger against wrong. His continuous state of grief was reflected in his compassionate response and miraculous healing, showing that he would not be intimidated or deterred from doing good.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "συνλ. probably implies sympathy with their (spiritually) miserable state of hard-heartedness"
2. "On πώρωσις, see note, Ephesians 4:18, and Fritzsche on Romans 11:7."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The sun therefore implies Christ's condolence with the moral misfortune of these hardhearted ones."
2. "Hardness is better than hardness, because it hints at the process going on."
3. "Mark only records Christ's feeling on this occasion."

### John Calvin:
1. "To convince us that this was a just and holy anger, Mark explains the reason of it to be, that he was grieved on account of the blindness of their hearts."
2. "This is the true moderation of zeal, to be distressed about the destruction of wicked men, and, at the same time, to be filled with wrath at their ungodliness."
3. "In consequence of the corruption of our nature, we do not preserve moderation; and our anger, even when it rests on proper grounds, is never free from sin."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "A holy indignation, mentioned by Mark alone, and no doubt expressed in His look."
2. "Christ manifests the character of God as Holy Love, His anger was the result of holiness, His compassion of love."
3. "'Hardening' is preferable to 'hardness,' since the original suggests a process as well as a result."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The eyes of Jesus swept the room all round and each rabbinical hypocrite felt the cut of that condemnatory glance."
2. "This indignant anger was not inconsistent with the love and pity of Jesus. Murder was in their hearts and Jesus knew it. Anger against wrong as wrong is a sign of moral health."
3. "Jesus is the Man of Sorrows and this present participle brings out the continuous state of grief whereas the momentary angry look is expressed by the aorist participle above."

### John Gill:
1. "His look upon them was with anger, with a stern countenance, which showed indignation at them, though without sin, or any desire of revenge."
2. "He had pity and compassion for them, being grieved for the hardness of their hearts."
3. "As one not to be intimidated by their evil designs against him, or prevented thereby from doing good, he saith unto the man, stretch forth thine hand; that is, the lame one; and such power went along with his words, as at once effected a cure."

### John Wesley:
1. "Looking round upon them with anger, being grieved — Angry at the sin, grieved at the sinner; the true standard of Christian anger."
2. "But who can separate anger at sin from anger at the sinner? None but a true believer in Christ."
