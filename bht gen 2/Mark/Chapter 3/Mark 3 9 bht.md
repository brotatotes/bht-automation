# Mark 3:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The passage shows Jesus's wisdom and foresight in ordering a small boat to wait on him. By doing so, he ensured that he could teach the crowds from the safety of the boat and retreat when necessary. This allowed him to serve the people without being overwhelmed by their pressing needs. The boat served as a practical solution to prevent the crowds from thronging him and enabled him to minister effectively to those in need.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  9. ] Meyer explains the construction  εἶπεν ἵνα  , by that which was said being regarded as the purpose of its being said.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "A small ship - Rather a 'boat.' There were properly speaking, no 'ships' on the Sea of Tiberias."
2. "This was probably a small boat that belonged to the disciples, in which he could draw off from the shore, and teach the people without being pressed by them."
3. "Lest they should throng him - They pressed upon him in great numbers. He had healed many, and those who were still diseased pressed or crowded on him."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Be constantly at His service."
2. "The purpose was probably both to teach from the boat and to retire from the crowd when He wished."
3. "His ministry, rather than His personal comfort, was thus furthered."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "That a little boat should wait on him...Whether he needed it or not is not told, but it was there at hand." 
2. "The boat was to keep close...move as Jesus did." 
3. "Jesus stayed with the crowds for they needed him."

### John Gill:
1. "he ordered and commanded them, that a small ship should wait on him"
2. "lest they should throng him; crowd, press, afflict, and distress"
3. "having a small vessel near the shore, he could go into it, and free himself from such an inconvenience."
