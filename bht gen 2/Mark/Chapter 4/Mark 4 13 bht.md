# Mark 4:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The parable of the sower is a crucial one, serving as the foundation for all the future parables that Jesus will share. It is designed to be easily understood, drawing from common, observable things. If one is unable to grasp the meaning of this parable, it raises doubts about their ability to comprehend the other parables. Therefore, it is important to seek God's assistance in understanding these parables as His guidance is essential for their proper interpretation.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Know ye not this parable? and how then will ye know all parables?"—Probably this was said not so much in the spirit of rebuke, as to call their attention to the exposition of it which He was about to give, and so train them to the right apprehension of His future parables. 
2. "As in the parables which we have endeavored to explain in :-, we shall take this parable and the Lord's own exposition of the different parts of it together."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Peculiar to Mark. Parables [τας παραβολας] . The parables, which I have spoken or may hereafter speak.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "You find you cannot understand this without assistance."
2. "The first parable of the kingdom is the basis of all the rest. If they understand not this, they could not understand any that followed."
3. "To understand the parables, God must help us."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Incapacity to comprehend this parable of the sower raises doubt about all the others on this day and at all times."

### John Gill:
1. "So easy to be understood, taken from things common, and which fall under every one's observation"
2. "How then will you know all parables? If not this single one, and which is so plain"
3. "How will ye be able to understand the numerous parables hereafter to be related, and which will be much more difficult?"

### John Wesley:
1. "Which is as it were the foundation of all those that I shall speak hereafter"
2. "Easy to be understood"
