# Mark 4:24 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Take heed to what you hear, attending to its influence upon you. The measure of assistance and understanding you receive will be determined by the measure of improvement you make with what you have heard. Those who truly hear, understand, and apply what they have learned will be blessed with even more.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "More shall be added, i.e. more knowledge."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Take heed what ye hear—In Luke ( :-) it is, 'Take heed how ye hear.' The one implies the other, but both precepts are very weighty."
2. "With what measure ye mete, it shall be measured to you—See on :-."
3. "And unto you that hear—that is, thankfully, teachably, profitably, shall more be given."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Take heed what ye hear - Or, consider well what you hear. Make a good improvement of it."
2. "With what measure ye mete ... - You shall be treated according to the use you make of your opportunities of learning."
3. "If you consider it well, and make a good improvement of what you hear, you shall be well rewarded. If not, your reward shall be small."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Take heed what ye hear...how ye hear." 
2. "Giving and receiving are reciprocal...As you treat me as your Instructor (giving attention), you will be treated (in receiving profit)." 
3. "And more shall be given...in case you hear properly."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Some things should not be heard at all for they besmirch the mind and heart."
2. "What is worth hearing should be heard rightly and heeded."
3. "With what measure."

### John Gill:
1. "Take heed what you hear: diligently attend to it, seek to understand it, and lay it up in your minds and memories."
2. "With what measure ye mete, it shall be measured unto you;... if the disciples carefully hearkened to what they heard from Christ, and studiously laboured to understand it, and faithfully dispensed it to others, in return, a larger measure, and greater degree of spiritual knowledge, would be bestowed upon them."
3. "Unto you that hear, shall more be given; that is, that hear so as to understand, keep, and make a good use of what they hear, more shall be communicated to them."

### John Wesley:
1. "Take heed what ye hear — That is, attend to what you hear, that it may have its due influence upon you."
2. "With what measure you mete — That is, according to the improvement you make of what you have heard, still farther assistance shall be given."
3. "And to you that hear — That is, with improvement."
