# Mark 4:29 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
When the fruit of the Christian life reaches its fullness and is fully matured, it is then ready to be harvested and gathered in. This process symbolizes the bringing to completion of God's work in the lives of His chosen people. The ministers of the Gospel play a crucial role in this process, as they faithfully sow the seed and eagerly anticipate the bountiful harvest of souls. It is a joyous occasion when they return with fullness, bringing with them the sheaves of the abundant fruit that they have labored to bring forth.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "παραδοῖ...offers itself."
2. "ἀποστέλλει...he puts in i.e. the husbandman."
3. "See Joel 3:13, to which this verse is a reference: also Revelation 14:14-15, and 1 Peter 1:23-25."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "This charmingly points to the transition from the earthly to the heavenly condition of the Christian and the Church."
2. "For the exposition of this portion, see on Mark 4:30-41."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "As soon as the grain is ripe it is cut down. So it is often with the Christian. As soon as he is prepared for heaven he is taken there."
2. "Every parable contains circumstances thrown in to fill up the story, which cannot be literally interpreted."
3. "The seed sown may mean the gospel which he was preaching. In Judea its beginnings were small; yet he would leave it, commit it to his disciples, and return to his Father."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Rev. is ripe, is a free rendering from the margin of A. V."
2. "It is, perhaps, better to explain, as Meyer does, whose rendering is adopted by Rev. in margin : When the fruit shall have allowed, i e., shall have admitted of being harvested."
3. "The verb is the same as that used of sending forth the apostles to reap the harvest of souls."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The thought of independence of human agency is kept up."
2. "We reap in spiritual things, though God alone (by His laws of grace) gives the increase."
3. "The main lesson is: that of spiritual growth independent of our agency, even though we sow the good seed and reap the harvest."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Whenever the fruit yields itself or permits."
2. "Sickle here by metonymy stands for the reapers who use it when the harvest stands ready for it."

### John Gill:
1. "when grace is brought to maturity, and faith is performed with power, and the good work begun is perfected"
2. "when all the elect of God are called by grace, and grace in them is brought to its perfection, and they have brought forth all the fruit they were ordained to bear, they will then be all gathered in"
3. "the ministers of the Gospel, who shall come again with joy, bringing their sheaves with them; being able to observe with pleasure a greater increase, and more fruit of their labours, than they knew of, or expected"
