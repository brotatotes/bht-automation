## BHT:
This man, who lived among the tombs, was in a truly wretched state. It is said that dwelling among the tombs was a sign of madness among the Jews. The demoniac's strength was so extraordinary that no one could bind him, not even with chains. This demonstrates both the power of the demon that possessed him and the desperate situation the man was in.

1. "He wore no clothes, neither abode in any house."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The picture of the miserable man is fearful; and in drawing it, each evangelist has some touches which wonderfully heighten the terribleness of the man's condition, and also magnify the glory of his cure."
2. "The tombs, in unclean places, unclean because of the dead men's bones which were there, would afford ample shelter to those who sought to flee from all fellowship of their kind."
3. "Being without the cities, oftentimes in remote and solitary places, these tombs of the Jews would attract those who sought to flee from all fellowship of their kind."

### John Calvin:
1. "Satan is sometimes permitted to make extraordinary movements, the effect of which goes beyond our comprehension and beyond ordinary means."
2. "We often perceive in madmen much greater strength than belongs to their natural capacity; and we are not at liberty to deny that, in such cases, the devil does his part when God permits him."
3. "It may serve to remind us how wretched and alarming it is to be placed under the tyranny of Satan, and also that bodily agony, however violent or cruel, is not more to be dreaded than distress of mind."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "No man could any more bind him, no, not with a chain." 
2. "This demoniac snapped a handcuff as if a string."

### John Gill:
1. "Who had his dwelling among the tombs,.... Which is one of the characters of a madman among the Jews; who say it is 'the sign of a madman, that he goeth out in the night, and lodges among the tombs'." 
2. "The same they say, in the same place, of an hypochondriac, and melancholy man; and of Kordiacus, which they give out is a demon that possesses, and has power over some sort of persons."
3. "No man could bind him, no, not with chains; so as to hold him for any length of time: not only cords were insufficient to hold, but even chains of iron; so strong was he through the possession."
