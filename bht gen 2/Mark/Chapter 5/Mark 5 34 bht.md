# Mark 5:34 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
He tenderly addresses her as daughter, showing not only affection but also the courteous manner of the Jews. Her faith in Christ, not her own merit, leads to her healing. As she departs, she is blessed with peace and encouraged to let go of any anxieties about the past and instead focus on gratitude and joy.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "καὶ ἴσθι … σου, peculiar to Mark, and inexplicable, except because the Lord really spoke the words..." 
2. "...as a solemn ratification of the healing which she had as it were surreptitiously obtained..." 
3. "...see note on Luke 8:48."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Daughter, be of good comfort, thy faith hath made thee whole; go in peace, and be whole of thy plague." 
2. "Though healed as soon as she believed, it seemed to her a stolen cure—she feared to acknowledge it. Jesus therefore sets His royal seal upon it."
3. "What a glorious dismissal from the lips of Him who is 'our Peace' is that, 'Go in peace!'"

### Marvin Vincent:
1. In peace [εις ειρηνην] . Lit., into peace. Contemplating the peace in store for her. Mark alone adds, Be whole of thy plague.

### John Calvin:
1. "From this exhortation we infer that the benefit which she had obtained was fully ratified, when she heard from the lips of Christ what she had already learned from experience."
2. "We do not truly, or with a safe conscience, enjoy God’s benefits in any other way than by possessing them as contained in the treasury of his promises."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "These words were a gracious and solemn ratification of the healing, which had been stolen, as it were."
2. "Go in peace. Lit, 'into peace.' The state in which she could now live in contrast with her previous suffering and her unquiet up to this moment."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Go in peace." She found sympathy, healing, and pardon for her sins, apparently.
2. "Peace here may have more the idea of the Hebrew shalom, health of body and soul."
3. "Be whole of thy plague." Continue whole and well.

### John Gill:
1. "He speaks to her in a very soft, kind, and tender manner, and called her 'daughter', which was not only an expression of affection and civility, this being an affable, courteous way of speaking used by the Jews."
2. "Thy faith hath made thee whole: through faith in Christ she received the cure from him; for it was not her act of faith that either merited, or procured it, but his power, and he himself the object of her faith that effected it."
3. "Go in peace; to thine house; all health and happiness attend thee; let no uneasy thought, about what has passed, dwell on thy mind; be joyful and thankful for the mercy received, and never fear, or dread, a return of the disorder."
