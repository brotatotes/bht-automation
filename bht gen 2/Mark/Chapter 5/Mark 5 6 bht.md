## BHT:
The possessed man, filled with terror, quickly approached Jesus, falling down at his feet. In this remarkable encounter, the demons in him recognized Christ's authority and paid homage to him, knowing he had power over them. This scene depicts the desperate journey of a sinner, catching a glimpse of Christ from afar, rushing towards him, and surrendering in faith, believing that Christ has the ability to rescue them from the clutches of Satan, the consequences of sin, and eternal destruction.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "But when he saw Jesus afar off, he ran and worshipped him—not with the spontaneous alacrity which says to Jesus, 'Draw me, we will run after thee,' but inwardly compelled, with terrific rapidity, before the Judge, to receive sentence of expulsion."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Afar off [απο μακροθεν] . Peculiar to Mark, as is also he ran.

### John Calvin:
1. "We ought to be aware that they did not come of their own accord into the presence of Christ, but were drawn by a secret exercise of his authority."
2. "Hence we infer, that the whole of Satan’s kingdom is subject to the authority of Christ."
3. "Reluctantly too they worship him, and their rebellious complaints testify that their confession was not made from choice, but was drawn from them by force."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The prominent thought is that he ran from a distance."
2. "This running would look like a violent attack, but instead of this, he worshipped him; Luke: ‘fell down before Him,’ which may be all that the word ‘worshipped ‘means."
3. "But the next verse intimates that it was an acknowledgment of Christ’s power, even if still hostile in its tone."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The onrush of the naked yelling maniac must have tried the newly recovered confidence of the Twelve."
2. "We can imagine their surprise when, on approaching, he threw himself on his knees."

### John Gill:
1. "he made all imaginable haste to him; and when he came up to him, fell down at his feet before him"
2. "This is an instance of the superiority of Christ over the devils, who knowing who he is, are filled with horror at him, fall down before him, and in their way do homage to him"
3. "an emblem of a poor awakened sinner, having a distant sight of Christ, who, upon it, makes haste unto him, and prostrates itself before him, believing he is able, if willing, to save him from the power of Satan, the evil of sin, and from eternal ruin and damnation."
