## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "εὔκαιρος hardly seems to be justified but, a convenient day (see Mar 6:31 and Acts 24:25, and cf. Soph. Œd. Col. 32) for the purposes of Herodias: which shews that the dance, &c. had been all previously contrived by her."
2. "μεγιστᾶνες, a Macedonian word, which came into use at the Alexandrine conquest."
3. "See Lobeck on Phrynichus, p. 197. He adduces the nom. form μεγιστᾶνος from Anna Comnena, xi. 324 c."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "A convenient day being come, when Herod made a supper to his lords, high captains, and chief estates of Galilee." 
2. "This graphic minuteness of detail adds much to the interest of the tragic narrative."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Opportune for the insidious woman, who hoped, through wine, lust, and the concurrence of sycophants, to be able easily to overcome the wavering mind of her husband" (Grotius in Meyer).
2. "The notice of the banquet and of the rank of the guests is peculiar to Mark."
3. "Both civil and military dignitaries were present, with other distinguished men of the district (chief men)."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "A convenient day, i.e., for the purpose which Herodias cherished, not for Herod’s feast, which took place at the fixed time."
2. "Herodias planned the schemes beforehand."
3. "'His' belongs to the first class only. Strictly speaking, Herod had no chief captains (chiliarchs) of his own."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "A day well appointed for the purpose, the day for which she had long waited."
2. "Herod was not to know that he was the mere catspaw of Herodias till it was all over."
3. "A notable gathering that included these three groups at the banquet on Herod's birthday."

### John Gill:
1. "And when a convenient day was come... which he kept as a festival, in eating, and drinking, and dancing; and so was a very opportune and seasonable time for Herodias to take the advantage of Herod when in a good humour, amidst his company, and in his cups, to solicit that, which she had often done without success." 
2. "and so it was now, that Herod on his birthday made a supper to his lords, high captains, and chief estates of Galilee: this birthday, was either the day of his natural, or civil birth; the day when he was born into the world, or of his accession to the throne"
3. "when he made a grand entertainment in the evening for his nobles, and the officers of the army, the captains of thousands, and the principal men, those of the first rank and quality in Galilee, of which he was Tetrarch."

### John Wesley:
1. A convenient day — Convenient for her purpose.His lords, captains, and principal men of Galilee — The great men of the court, the army, and the province.
