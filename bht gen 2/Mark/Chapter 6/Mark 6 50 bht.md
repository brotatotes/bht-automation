# Mark 6:50 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
When the disciples saw Jesus walking on the water, their fear was intensified. However, his words turned their fear into comfort and reassurance. This event was not a mere imagination because all of them witnessed it, which eliminates any doubts. Jesus' voice was immediately recognized, causing their fears to subside. With his comforting words, Be of good cheer, it is I, be not afraid, Jesus reminded them of his presence and power, calming their troubled hearts.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "peculiar to Mark"
2. "After this follows the history respecting Peter"
3. "this is at least doubtful in any general sense"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Here were they in the midst of a raging sea, their little bark the sport of the elements, and with just enough of light to descry an object on the waters which only aggravated their fears. But Jesus deems it enough to dispel all apprehension to let them know that He was there." 
2. "Spoken by One who at that moment was 'treading upon the waves of the sea,' and was about to hush the raging elements with His word, what was it but the Voice which cried of old in the ears of Israel, even from the days of Moses, 'I AM'; 'I, EVEN I, AM HE!'"
3. "Sublime word, issuing from One conscious of power over the raging element, to bid it serve both Himself and whomsoever else He pleased!"

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "They all saw him." 
2. "Mark's with them is more familiar, and gives the idea of a more friendly and encouraging address."
3. "It is significant, in view of Peter's relation to this gospel, that Mark omits the incident of Peter's walk on the waves (Matthew 14:28-31)."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "For they all saw him and were troubled." - A proof that this story was not due to the over-heated imagination of a few of them.
2. "At this point Peter’s attempt to walk on the water occurred."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "It is I. These were the astounding words of cheer."
2. "They did not recognize Jesus in the darkness."
3. "His voice reassured them."

### John Gill:
1. "Had it been only seen by one, it might have been thought a fancy, and the effect of mere imagination; but as every one saw it, it was out of all doubt that so it was."
2. "As soon as they saw him, 'that very moment', as the Syriac renders it; that so by hearing his voice their fears might be allayed."
3. "Be of good cheer, it is I, be not afraid."
