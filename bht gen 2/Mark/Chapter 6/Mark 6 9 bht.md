# Mark 6:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Our Lord's command to be shod with sandals suggests that the disciples should go forth on their mission as they usually would in their everyday lives. This mission was meant to initiate them into their apostolic work and serve as an encouragement for them in the future. It emphasized the importance of relying on God's care and provision, even when they left everything behind and ventured into the unknown.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "With sandals, i.e., such as they had on at the time, without waiting for shoes especially adapted for the journey."
2. "The construction changes into a direct command in the last clause, as if the memory of one present had supplied it."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The mention of 'two' here in all three Gospels probably helps us to understand that the same thing applies to shoes and staff."
2. "Two was a sign of comparative wealth."
3. "In general, these directions are against luxury in equipment, and also against their providing themselves with what they could procure from the hospitality of others."

### John Gill:
1. "A minister of the Gospel ought not to be a worldly minded man, that minds earth and earthly things, and seeks to amass wealth and riches to himself, and preaches for filthy lucre's sake."
2. "He ought to have his mind free from all solicitude and anxious concern, about a subsistence for himself and his, that so he may with greater and more close application attend to his ministry, to preparations for it, and the performance of it."
3. "It is highly necessary that the people to whom he ministers should take care, that a sufficient provision be made for him; that he may live without any anxious care and thought about such things, and his mind be more intent about the work he is called unto."

### John Wesley:
1. "Be shod with sandals — As you usually are."
2. "Our Lord intended by this mission to initiate them into their apostolic work."
3. "And it was doubtless an encouragement to them all their life after, to recollect the care which God took of them, when they had left all they had, and went out quite unfurnished for such an expedition."
