# Mark 7:29 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
This woman's great faith, as shown in Matthew 15:28, was a testimony to her belief in Jesus. Her quick and bright response pleased Him, and He had no hesitation in performing the miracle, immediately casting the devil out of her daughter by His divine power. He didn't need to be physically present or speak to the demon; His power simply went forth, evicting the evil spirit.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "O woman, great is thy faith." 
2. Jesus "marvelled" only at two things - faith and unbelief. 
3. "For this saying go thy way; the devil is gone out of thy daughter."

### Philip Schaff:
1. Mark 7:29.  For this saying. As an evidence of her  faith.
	 The demon is gone out.  As He spoke, the miracle was performed (Matthew 15:28).
	

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "She had faith, great faith as Matthew 15:28 shows..."
2. "It was her quick and bright repartee that pleased Jesus."
3. "He had missed his rest, but it was worth it to answer a call like this."

### John Gill:
1. "the Persic version reads it, 'go thy way; for with the blessing of this word, the devil is gone out of thy daughter': as if this saying referred to the word Christ, and the divine power that went along with it."
2. "Christ, who as God is everywhere, and whose divine power reaches to all places, persons, and things had, in a secret and powerful manner, cast the devil out of this woman's daughter."
3. "without going to her, or speaking to him, his power had wrought the miracle effectually."
