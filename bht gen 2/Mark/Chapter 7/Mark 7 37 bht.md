# Mark 7:37 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The wonders of God's grace are truly astonishing. To witness the opening of the ears and hearts of sinners, causing the tongues of the dumb to sing His praise, is a remarkable display of divine intervention. It is not only amazing that such grace is bestowed upon us, but also that it is extended to unworthy individuals, producing such transformative effects and blessed outcomes. From eternity past to the present, God has always acted with perfect wisdom and goodness, ensuring that all His works are done well.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "This work was properly and worthily compared with that first one of creation." 
2. "It was the same Beneficence which prompted, and the same Power that wrought it."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "He hath done all things well—reminding us, says TRENCH, of the words of the first creation (Genesis 1:31, Septuagint), upon which we are thus not unsuitably thrown back, for Christ's work is in the truest sense 'a new creation.'"
2. "He maketh both the deaf to hear and the dumb to speak."
3. "And they glorified the God of Israel."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Beyond measure - Exceedingly; very much. In the Greek, 'Very abundantly.'"
2. "He hath done all things well - All things in a remarkable manner; or, he has perfectly effected the cure of this deaf-mute."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Astonished. See on Matthew 7:28. To speak [λαλειν] . See on Matthew 28:18. The emphasis is not on the matter, but on the fact of speech. 

### John Calvin:
1. "He hath done all things well." 
2. "All that he did was so far from exposing him to calumny that it deserved the highest praise."
3. "It is what nature teaches us, that nothing is more unjust than to make the bestowal of favors an occasion of envy and ill-will."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Beyond measure. Their excessive zeal was equalled by their excessive astonishment."
2. "He hath done all things well. Perhaps an allusion to Genesis 1:31; the same Power and Beneficence were manifested in His healing as in God’s work of creation."
3. "The dumb to speak. This favors the view that the cured man was entirely speechless. The whole verse intimates that this was but one of many miracles. Comp. Matthew 15:30-31."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He hath done all things well" - The present perfect active shows the settled convictions of these people about Jesus.
2. "Their great amazement" - Their great amazement, imperfect passive and compound adverb, thus found expression in a vociferous championship of Jesus in this pagan land.

### John Gill:
1. "The grace of God, in opening the ears and heart of a sinner, and causing the tongue of the dumb to sing his praise, is very astonishing, to men and angels."
2. "It is amazing that such grace should be bestowed at all; and it is more, that it should be communicated to such unworthy persons it is; as also that it should produce such effects it does; that it should make such a surprising change, and be attended with such blessed consequences."
3. "All that he did in eternity before the world was, he did well; what he did in the council and covenant of grace, in espousing the persons and cause of his people, and in all his federal transactions and suretyship engagements for them."
