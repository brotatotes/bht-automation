## BHT:
The Pharisees, seeking to ensnare Jesus, came forth and began to dispute with him, asking for a sign from heaven to test him. Their deceitful intention was to trap Jesus and undermine his authority. (Matthew 16:1)

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The same simplicity of purpose and careful observation of the symptoms of approaching events which they showed in common things would enable them to 'discern the signs of the times'—or rather 'seasons,' to which the prophets pointed for the manifestation of the Messiah."
2. "The scepter had departed from Judah; Daniel's seventy weeks were expiring, and many other significant indications of the close of the old economy, and preparations for a freer and more comprehensive one, might have been discerned."
3. "But all was lost upon them."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The beginnings of things seem to have a peculiar interest for Mark." 
2. "As applied to the miracles of our Lord, this word emphasizes their ethical purport."
3. "The miraculous act points back of itself to the grace and power or divine character or authority of the doer."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The skeptical Sadducees were entirely hypocritical in asking a sign from heaven."
2. "Spying hostility is implied. He had landed at some retired locality (see on Mark 8:10), where their opposition speedily found Him, since they began, at once, to question with him."
3. "These details are peculiar to Mark."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "And the Pharisees came forth... At once they met Jesus and opened a controversy." 
2. "The Pharisees and Herodians had already joined hands against Jesus in the sabbath controversy."
3. "They began to question with him... dispute, not mere inquiry, they began at once and kept it up."

### John Gill:
1. "And the Pharisees came forth,.... Out of their houses; who dwelt in the coasts of Magdala, and parts of Dalmanutha..." 
2. "And began to question with him; or to dispute with him, it being their manner to carry on disputations by questions and answers." 
3. "Seeking of him a sign from heaven, tempting him."

### John Wesley:
1. Tempting him — That is, trying to ensnare him. Matthew 16:1.
