# Mark 9:22 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The father's plea reflects a twofold belief: that Jesus has the power to heal, and that Jesus has the compassion to extend that healing. The father's words reveal the desperate nature of the situation, as the child has been repeatedly endangered and afflicted by the demon. Through his plea, the father expresses his earnest desire for Jesus to exercise His power and mercy in this moment of great need.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "This bespeaks, if any faith, at most but a very ignorant and weak one."
2. "The wretched father counts his child's misery his own."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The man is but struggling into faith: it must come a step farther."
2. "He had to do with Him who breaks not the bruised reed, and who knew how to inspire what He demanded."
3. "The man had said to Him, 'If Thou canst do.'"

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The father identifies himself with the son's misery."
2. "Compare the Syro-Phoenician, who makes her daughter's case entirely her own."
3. "Have mercy on me."

### John Calvin:
1. "The first foundation of faith is, to embrace the boundless power of God; and the first step to prayer is, to raise it above all opposition by the firm belief that our prayers are not in vain."
2. "As this man did not suppose Christ to be at all different from other men, his false opinion is corrected; for our faith must be so formed as to be capable and prepared for receiving the desired favor."
3. "In his reply Christ does not administer a direct reproof, but indirectly reminding the man of what he had said amiss, points out to him his fault, and informs him how a remedy may be obtained."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The father describes the case still further, representing the demon as a malignant enemy seeking to kill his only son."
2. "The father’s sense of need is stirred by the recital, but his faith is very weak."
3. "The father’s feelings are intense, as he naturally and properly identifies himself with the misery of his son."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The failure of the disciples had not wholly destroyed his faith in the power of Jesus, though the conditional form does suggest doubt whether the boy can be cured at all."
2. "With touching tenderness he makes the boy's case his own as the Syrophoenician woman had said, 'Have mercy on me.'"
3. "The leper had said: 'If thou wilt.' This father says: 'If thou canst.'"

### John Gill:
1. "And ofttimes it hath cast him into the fire....so that one part or other of his body has been scorched, or burnt, and his life in danger."
2. "All this is said to aggravate the case, and show the miserable condition the child was in, from the frequency of the fits, and the danger he was exposed to."
3. "He tries, in very moving language, both the power and pity of Christ; and begs that if he had either, he would exert them on this occasion."

### John Wesley:
1. If thou canst do any thing — In so desperate a case: Have compassion on us - Me as well as him.
