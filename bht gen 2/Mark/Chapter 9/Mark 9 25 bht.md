# Mark 9:25 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
When Jesus encountered the boy with the deaf and dumb spirit, he used his authority to rebuke the foul spirit that had brought disorder upon the child. The demon, distinct from the boy, heard Jesus's command even though the child could not. Jesus addressed the demon in an authoritative manner, demonstrating his power over it. This encounter was purposely public, as a crowd gathered to witness the outcome. Jesus's direct involvement guaranteed the father's faith in the cure, providing him reassurance that the disorder would not return.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The personal pronoun is emphatic, as opposed to the want of power on the part of the disciples."
2. "This is the only place where we have such a charge as μηκέτι εἰσέλθ . εἰς αὐ ., showing the excessive malignity and tenacity of this kind of spirit."
3. "This is also shown by Mark 9:26."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Our Lord would avoid too great publicity."
2. "The father's faith had been sufficiently tested."
3. "These unusual words show the unusual malignity of this kind of a spirit."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The double compound vividly describes the rapid gathering of the crowd to Jesus and the epileptic boy to see the outcome." 
2. "Jesus addresses the demon as a separate being from the boy as he often does."
3. "He evidently regards the demon as the cause in this case of the boy's misfortune."

### John Gill:
1. "He rebuked the foul spirit; that brought this disorder on the child." 
2. "Christ, in an authoritative way, ordered the unclean spirit to leave his possession."
3. "This he said...to confirm his faith in the cure, and that he might be in no pain about the return of the disorder."

### John Wesley:
1. "Thou deaf and dumb spirit — So termed, because he made the child so."
2. "When Jesus spake, the devil heard, though the child could not."
3. "I command thee — I myself now; not my disciples."
