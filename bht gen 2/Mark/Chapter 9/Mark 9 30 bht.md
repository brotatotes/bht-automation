# Mark 9:30 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus, wanting to familiarize his disciples little by little with the announcement of his sufferings, passed through Galilee privately, avoiding public work and instructing his disciples along the way. He did not want anyone to know their location, giving him the opportunity to have private conversations with his disciples about important matters. This included instructing them fully about his upcoming sufferings, emphasizing the certainty of his delivery.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "By comparing Matthew 17:22; Matthew 17:23; Luke 9:43; Luke 9:44 with this, we gather, that as our Lord's reason for going through Galilee more privately than usual on this occasion was to reiterate to them the announcement which had so shocked them at the first mention of it, and thus familiarize them with it by little and little..."
2. "...so this was His reason for enjoining silence upon them as to their present movements."


### Marvin Vincent:
1. Passed through [παρεπορευοντο] . Lit., passed along [παρα] . Not tarrying. Bengel says, "not through the cities, but past them."

### Philip Schaff:
1. Mark 9:30.  Passed through Galilee  ;  probably over by-ways, that opportunity might be given for instructing the disciples about His approaching sufferings.
	

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He would not that any man should know it." 
2. "He was avoiding public work there now."
3. "He had left Caesarea Philippi for Galilee."

### John Gill:
1. "And they departed thence,.... From the coasts of Caesarea Philippi, from that part of the country where the mountain was, on which Christ was transfigured, and at the foot of which the above miracle was wrought."
2. "And passed through Galilee; in order to go to the coasts of Judea, and so to Jerusalem, where he was shortly to suffer."
3. "And he would not that any man should know it; partly for the reason just mentioned, and partly that he might have the opportunity of conversing alone with his disciples, and of reminding, and informing them, of some important things, which it was necessary they should be acquainted with, and observe."

### John Wesley:
1. "He wanted to be alone with them some time, in order to instruct them fully concerning his sufferings."
2. "The Son of man is delivered — It is as sure as if it were done already."
