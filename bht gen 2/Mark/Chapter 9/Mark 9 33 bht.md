## BHT:
Christ came not alone, but with his disciples to Capernaum where a dispute arose among them. Jesus asked them, What were ye reasoning in the way? He already knew their thoughts, but he wanted to address their pride and ambition and teach them about humility and the true nature of his kingdom.

### Henry Alford:

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "What was it that ye disputed among yourselves by the way?" (33 words)

### Philip Schaff:
1. "When he was." Literally, 'being;' but in the singular number. It was immediately after their entrance.
2. "In the house." Probably a particular house, where He usually resided.
3. "In the way." Probably during the journey to Capernaum.

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "In the house (εν τη οικια). Probably Peter's house in Capernaum which was the home of Jesus when in the city."
2. "What were ye reasoning in the way? (Τ εν τη οδω διελογισζεθε;). Imperfect tense. They had been disputing (verse Mark 9:34), not about the coming death of the Master, but about the relative rank of each of them in the political kingdom which they were expecting him to establish."
3. "Jesus had suspected the truth about them and they had apparently kept it up in the house."

### John Gill:
1. "Christ came not alone, but with his disciples." 
2. "This question Christ put, not as ignorant of what had passed among them, but in order to have the case laid before him."
3. "He might reprove them for their pride and ambition, and have an opportunity of teaching them humility, and of informing them of the nature of his kingdom, and subjects; concerning which, they had entertained very wrong notions."
