# Matthew 1:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Though Joseph was a just man, meaning he was a strict observer of the law, he did not wish to expose Mary to public condemnation. Being aware of the gravity of the situation, Joseph chose to protect her and put her away privately.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "He was a strict observer of the law and (yet) not willing to expose her." 
2. "The husband might either do this, or adopt the stronger course of bringing his wife (or betrothed) to justice openly."
3. "θέλω expresses the mere wish, βούλομαι the wish ripened into intention."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Betrothal was, in Jewish law, valid marriage."
2. "In giving Mary up, therefore, Joseph had to take legal steps to effect the separation."
3. "That some communication had passed between him and his betrothed, directly or indirectly, on the subject, after she returned from her three months' visit to Elizabeth, can hardly be doubted."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The word just has not unfrequently this meaning of mildness, or mercy."
2. "Joseph was ardently attached to her, but her character was likely to be ruined, and he deemed it proper to separate her from him."
3. "Thus, our only aim should be to preserve a conscience void of offence, and God will guard our reputation."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Joseph, having the right and power under the (assumed) circumstances to make Mary a public example, resolved to spare her this exposure."
2. "Qelein is the stronger word, and expresses a purpose or determination or decree, the execution of which is, or is believed to be, in the power of him who wills. Boulesqai expresses wish, inclination, or disposition, whether one desires to do a thing himself or wants someone else to do it."
3. "In the New Testament, as observed above, though the words are often interchanged, the same distinction is recognized."

### John Calvin:
1. "That justice, on which a commendation is here bestowed, consisted in hatred and abhorrence of crime."
2. "Joseph, therefore, moved by an ardent love of justice, condemned the crime of which he supposed his wife to have been guilty; while the gentleness of his disposition prevented him from going to the utmost rigor of law."
3. "We see how God chose to employ an angel in informing his servant Joseph, that to others he might be a heavenly herald, and that the intelligence which he conveyed might not be borrowed from his wife, or from any mortal."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He was influenced by justice. Mary had possibly told him of the revelation made to her: he was just in giving her a hearing, and then, in consequence, in not wishing to make her a public example."
2. "The former phrase is the more remarkable, since such Justice is rarely exercised to one in the situation of Mary. So high a regard for the honor and reputation of a woman is most rare in Eastern countries. Mary’s strong faith may have influenced him also."
3. "The eternal Son of God exposed himself, at his very entrance into the world, to the suspicion of illegitimacy! One chosen to be His mother was suspected of un-faithfulness by her husband! The two kinds of divorce among the Jews."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "An upright man," the Braid Scots has it. He had the Jewish conscientiousness for the observance of the law which would have been death by stoning (Deuteronomy 22:23).
2. It is impossible to think of Joseph as the actual father of Jesus according to the narrative of Matthew without saying that Matthew has tried by legend to cover up the illegitimate birth of Jesus.
3. Joseph had "a short but tragic struggle between his legal conscience and his love" (McNeile).

### John Gill:
1. "being a just man, observant of the law of God, particularly that which respected adultery, being wholly good and chaste"
2. "he being tender and compassionate, though strictly just and good, was minded to put her away privily"
3. "he deliberately consulted and determined within himself to dismiss her, or put her away by giving her a bill of divorce, in a very private manner"

### John Wesley:
1. A just man — A strict observer of the law: therefore not thinking it right to keep her.
