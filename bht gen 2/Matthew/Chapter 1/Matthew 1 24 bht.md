# Matthew 1:24 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
When Joseph awakened from his slumber, he demonstrated his faith by promptly obeying the divine messenger. Taking Mary as his wife, he openly acknowledged their union and put aside any doubts or suspicions. His resolute decision to embrace Mary wholeheartedly brought relief and joy to her, as he showed unwavering commitment to their relationship. This act of obedience illustrated Joseph's trust in God's plan and his willingness to fulfill his appointed role without hesitation.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  24. ] ἀπὸ  τοῦ ὕπνου ,  from his sleep the sleep which was on him when he had the dream.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Then Joseph, being raised from sleep—and all his difficulties now removed. did as the angel of the Lord had bidden him..."
2. "With what deep and reverential joy would this now be done on his part; and what balm would this minister to his betrothed one, who had till now lain under suspicions of all others the most trying to a chaste and holy woman..."
3. "suspicions, too, arising from what, though to her an honor unparalleled, was to all around her wholly unknown!"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Being raised from sleep - Having fully awoke."
2. "Did as the angel of the Lord had bidden him - That is, he took Mary to wife."
3. "Probably this was done immediately, since he was now convinced of her innocence, and, by delay, he would not leave any ground of suspicion that he had not confidence in her."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. The or his sleep [του υπνου] . The force of the definite article; the sleep in which he had the vision. So Rev., "Arose from his sleep." 

### John Calvin:
1. "The ready performance, which is here described, serves not less to attest the certainty of Joseph’s faith, than to commend his obedience."
2. "The dream must have carried some mark of Divinity, which did not allow his mind to hesitate."
3. "Having learned the will of God, he instantly prepared himself to obey."

### Philip Schaff:
1. Matthew 1:24.  Then Joseph
	 did.  He believed, therefore he obeyed. Thus early in the Gospel is obedience represented as the fruit of faith.

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Took unto him his wife (παρελαβεν την γυναικα αυτου). The angel had told him not to be afraid to 'take to his side' Mary his wife." 
2. "So when he awoke from his sleep he promptly obeyed the angel and 'took his wife home' (Moffatt)." 
3. "One can only imagine the relief and joy of Mary when Joseph nobly rose to his high duty toward her."

### John Gill:
1. "Firmly believing that it was a messenger of God that was sent to him, and that this matter was of the Lord."
2. "He took unto him his wife, that is, he publicly married her, whom he had before espoused, took her to his house, or continued her there, lived with her as his wife, and owned her to be such."
3. "Henceforwards had no more thoughts of putting her away."
