# Matthew 10:1 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
He gathered them privately, entrusting them with the task of spreading the Gospel and officially appointing them as apostles. To ensure their authority, he bestowed upon them the ability to expel unclean spirits and the power to heal all kinds of sicknesses and diseases. This demonstrated their ability to carry on his work and validated their message.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The word signifies both 'power' and 'authority' or 'right.'" 
2. "He both qualified and authorized them."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "These twelve were the original number of apostles. The word 'apostle' means one that is 'sent,' and was given to them because they were 'sent forth' to preach the gospel." 
2. "In selecting 'twelve' at first, it is probable that the Saviour was somewhat guided by the number of the tribes of Israel. Twelve was, with them, a well-known number, and it was natural that he should select one for every tribe."
3. "They were just such men as are always found the best witnesses in courts of justice - plain men, of good sense, of fair character, of great honesty, and with favorable opportunities of ascertaining the facts to which they bore witness."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. The disciples [τους μαθητας] . The or his, referring to them as already chosen, though he nowhere relates their choosing. See Mark 3:14; Luke 6:13.

### John Calvin:
1. "They are now called to immediate performance, are ordered to prepare themselves for the work, receive injunctions, and, that there may be no want of authority, are endued with the power of the Holy Spirit."
2. "So then they are now sent to proclaim throughout Judea that the time of the promised restoration and salvation is at hand; at a future period, Christ will appoint them to spread the Gospel through the whole world."
3. "As Christ gives to them at the same time, and in immediate connection, the appointment to be preachers of the gospel and ministers of miracles, it is plain that miracles are nothing else than seals of his doctrine, and therefore we are not at liberty to dissolve this close connection."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "There is here an indication that they had been previously chosen."
2. "They are now sent out as 'laborers.'"
3. "The number twelve (3 × 4) has been considered a symbol of the Trinity (3) indwelling in the world (4)."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "His twelve disciples (τους δωδεκα μαθητας αυτου). First mention of the group of 'learners' by Matthew and assumed as already in existence (note the article) as they were (Mark 3:14)."
2. "The physical distress was great, but the spiritual even greater. Power is more likely the idea of εξουσια here."
3. "Jesus is still the master of soul and body. But intelligent faith does not justify us in abstaining from the help of the physician who must not be confounded with the quack and the charlatan."

### John Gill:
1. "He called them together privately; and gave them a commission to preach the Gospel, ordained them ministers of the word, and installed them into the office of apostleship."
2. "And that they might appear to come forth with authority, and that their doctrine might be confirmed, he gave them power against unclean spirits, to cast them out."
3. "He likewise gave them power to heal all manner of sicknesses, and all manner of diseases, as he himself had done."
