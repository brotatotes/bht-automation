# Matthew 10:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
When you enter a house, greet the inhabitants with a salutation, echoing the customary Jewish form of greeting: Peace be to this house. This salutation expresses a desire for all blessings - temporal, spiritual, and eternal - to come upon the household.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Not the house of the ἄξιος, for this would be sure to be worthy; but any house, as is necessary from the subsequent ἐὰν ᾖ ἡ οἰκ. ἀξ., which on the other supposition (Meyer, &c.) would have been ascertained already."
2. "The full command as to their conduct, from arriving to departing, is given in Matthew 10:11."
3. "When they enter into an house, (so, idiomatically, E. V.,) they are to salute it: and if on enquiry it prove worthy, then &c."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 12. And when ye come into an
	house—or "the house," but it means not the worthy
	house, but the house ye first enter, to try if it be worthy. 
	salute it—show it the
	usual civilities.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The Greek indicates more distinctly the simultaneousness of the entrance and the salutation: as ye are entering."
2. "Rev., as ye enter."
3. "So of the departure, as ye are going forth."

### John Calvin:
1. "As they could not distinguish the devout worshippers of God from despisers, he enjoins them to address in a friendly manner any family which they may have occasion to meet."
2. "The act of saluting is a kind of opening to a conversation."
3. "Whoever shall willingly embrace your doctrine, remain in their house, that your salutation may be confirmed."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Conformity to proper social customs, without official pride, with an immediate and friendly recognition of the expected hospitality."
2. "Irrespective of the worthiness or unworthiness of the host."

### John Gill:
1. "And when ye come into an house... salute it; meaning the inhabitants of it... for the usual form of salutation among the Jews was in such words." 
2. "peace be to this house... is a very just, and proper explanation of saluting: for the usual form of salutation among the Jews was in such words." 
3. "which is meant all kind of happiness, and prosperity, temporal, spiritual, and eternal."

### John Wesley:
1. Salute it — In the usual Jewish form, "Peace (that is, all blessings) be to this house."
