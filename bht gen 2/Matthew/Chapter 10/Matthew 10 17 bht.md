## BHT:
Take heed and exercise caution, for there will be those who seek to harm you and exploit any opportunity to do so. You may face persecution even in the places you least expect, such as their synagogues, where the religious leaders hold power and authority. Yet, in the midst of these challenges, do not worry about how to defend yourself, for divine wisdom and guidance will be with you.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "But beware of men; for they will deliver you up to the councils—the local courts, used here for civil magistrates in general."
2. "And they will scourge you in their synagogues—By this is meant persecution at the hands of the ecclesiastics."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Do not run unnecessarily into danger. Use suitable prudence and caution, and do not needlessly endanger your lives."
2. "The law of Moses directed that the number of stripes should not exceed 40, but might be any number less, at the discretion of the judge."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Of men [των ανθρωπων] . Lit., "the men," already alluded to under the term wolves.

### John Calvin:
1. "But he appears to contradict himself: for the best way of exercising caution would have been to remain at home, and not to venture to appear in public. I reply, he points out here a different sort of caution, — not that terror and alarm which would keep them from discharging their duty, but a dread of being excessively annoyed by sudden calamities."
2. "Christ, therefore, desired that his disciples should foresee at a distance what would happen, that their minds might be early prepared for maintaining a conflict."
3. "He warns them, too, that not in Judea only, but in more distant places, they will be called to fight; and he does so, not merely for the purpose of preparing them by long meditation for that warfare, but that, as instructed and experienced masters, they might not scruple to yield themselves to heavenly guidance."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "To 'beware' they must be 'wise.' Not needless suspicion but prudent discernment."
2. "Literally fulfilled in Apostolic times, yet in all ages church courts have been apt to persecute."
3. "Human nature is selfish and intolerant, and slow to learn the lesson of mercy and charity."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Beware of men (προσεχετε απο των ανθρωπων). Hold your mind away from."
2. "In their synagogues (εν τοις συναγωγαις αυτων). Here not merely as the place of assembly for worship, but as an assembly of justice exercising discipline."

### John Gill:
1. "The advice to the apostles is, to take care how they came into their company, and put themselves into their hands; who would seek all opportunities and occasions against them, and use their power and interest to do them hurt."
2. "These things did not befall the apostles till after the death of Christ, it is clear that the context refers not to their first, but to an after mission."
3. "Now as these things did not befall the apostles till after the death of Christ, it is clear that the context refers not to their first, but to an after mission."

### John Wesley:
1. "But think not that all your innocence and all your wisdom will screen you from persecution."
2. "They will scourge you in their synagogues — In these the Jews held their courts of judicature, about both civil and ecclesiastical affairs."
3. "Take no thought - Neither at this time, on any sudden call, need we be careful how or what to answer."
