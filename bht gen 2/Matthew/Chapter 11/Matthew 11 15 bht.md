# Matthew 11:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
He that hath ears to hear, let him hear. These words are a proverbial expression used by Jesus to emphasize the need for deep attention and understanding when listening to his teachings. They imply that not everyone possesses the spiritual ears and understanding necessary to grasp the profound truths being conveyed. Those who are receptive and open to receive these teachings must diligently listen, ponder, and reflect upon the weight and significance of Christ's words.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "These words are generally used by our Lord when there is a further and deeper meaning in His words than is expressed."
2. "If John the Baptist is Elias, and Elias is the forerunner of the coming of the Lord, then know surely that the Lord is come."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He that hath ears ... - This expression is frequently used by Christ. It is a proverbial expression, implying that the highest attention should be given to what was spoken."
2. "The doctrine about John he regarded as of the greatest importance."
3. "He among you, says he, that has the faculty of understanding this, or that will believe that this is the Elijah spoken of, let him attend to it and remember it."

### John Calvin:
1. "He reminds us, at the same time, of the reason why the mysteries of which he speaks are not received by all."
2. "Christ here exhorts the elect of God, whose ears have been pierced, to consider attentively this remarkable secret of God, and not to remain deaf with unbelievers."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He that hath ears to hear, etc. This usually follows an important statement, intimating that he who has the discernment to understand will find the deeper meaning."
2. "Then, as now, properly to understand the Scriptures was to know Christ."
3. "If John wished our Lord to declare Himself, his wish was granted, but the revelation was, as always, only to those who really sought to know Christ."

### John Gill:
1. "He that hath ears to hear, let him hear. A way of speaking used by Christ, when anything serious, and of great importance, was delivered; and which required attention, and was not easily understood."
2. "His words imply, that everyone had not spiritual ears and understandings, to hear and take in things of such an high nature, and excellent use."
3. "None but those to whom they were given; and such ought to attend to them, and seriously weigh and consider the importance of them."

### John Wesley:
1. He that hath ears to hear, let him hear — A kind of proverbial expression; requiring the deepest attention to what is spoken.
