# Matthew 11:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Son of Man came eating and drinking, living a common and relatable life among men. He was criticized by the worldly people, who found fault in His conduct, accusing Him of indulging excessively and hanging out with sinners and tax collectors. However, those who truly understood wisdom recognized that the Son of Man's actions were justified. His wisdom was acknowledged and justified by those who were wise enough to see beyond the surface and understand the purpose behind His actions.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "They were events in which wisdom was justified, &c."
2. "The children of wisdom were led to receive and justify the Wisdom of God, who did these things."
3. "The children of wisdom are opposed to the wayward children, the child like to the childish."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The Son of man...like all other men; going to places of festivity...identifying Himself with men in their ordinary life."
2. "Thoroughly worldly people...extend their criticisms to dress, food, expression of countenance, cut of the beard and parting of the hair."
3. "The judgments of the world are childish, those of the children of wisdom are childlike, in humility and faith, and their 'works' correspond."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Wisdom is justified by her works." 
2. "God's wisdom has planned the different conduct of both John and Jesus."
3. "The plan of God is justified by results."

### John Gill:
1. "The son of man came eating and drinking,.... Meaning himself, who ate and drank as men usually do, lived in the common way of life..."
2. "...behold a man gluttonous, a voracious man, an epicurean, one that indulges his appetite to a very great degree, and in a scandalous manner..."
3. "...a friend of publicans and sinners; such as are openly and notoriously wicked; and loves their company, for the sake of tippling with them; and encourages them in their reveling and drunkenness..."

### John Wesley:
1. "The Son of man came eating and drinking — Conversing in a free, familiar way."
2. "Wisdom is justified by her children — That is, my wisdom herein is acknowledged by those who are truly wise."
