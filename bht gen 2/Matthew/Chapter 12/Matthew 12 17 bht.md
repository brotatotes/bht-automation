# Matthew 12:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
What Christ did by privately departing from the Pharisees, preaching to the Gentiles, healing the sick, and avoiding self-promotion fulfilled the prophecy. The Holy Spirit, in His foreknowledge of Christ's actions, predicted them, providing strong evidence of His messiahship. This prophecy is acknowledged by the Jews as applying to the Messiah.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Neither it nor ὅπως πλ. must be understood 'and thus was fulfilled,' as Webster and Wilkinson: both are used only of the purpose, not of the result, here or anywhere."
2. "The prophecy is partly from the LXX, partly an original translation."
3. "The LXX have Ἰακὼβ ὁ παῖς μου…Ἰσραὴλ ὁ ἐκλεκτός μου…, but the Rabbis generally understood it of the Messiah."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 17. That it might be fulfilled which
	was spoken by Esaias the prophet, saying— ( :-).

### John Calvin:
1. "By this circumstance he intended to show, that the glory of Christ’s divinity ought not to be the less admired, because it appeared under a vail of infirmity."
2. "God appointed for his Son a low and mean appearance, and that ignorant persons may not take offense at an aspect which has no attraction, and is fitted to awaken contempt, both the prophet and Matthew come forward to declare, that it is not by accident, but in consequence of a decree of Heaven, that he assumes such a character."
3. "We are not at liberty to imagine to ourselves a Christ that corresponds to our fancy, but ought simply to embrace him as he is offered by the Father."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "While Mark details the occurrences, Matthew only declares that the retirement of our Lord was a fulfilment of prophecy, however contrary to the popular notions about the Messiah."
2. "A translation from the Hebrew, made by the Evangelist."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "That it might be fulfilled (ινα πληρωθη). The final use of ινα and the sub-final just before (verse Matthew 12:16)."
2. "The passage quoted is Isaiah 42:1-4 'a very free reproduction of the Hebrew with occasional side glances at the Septuagint' (Bruce), possibly from an Aramaic collection of Testimonia (McNeile)."
3. "Matthew applies the prophecy about Cyrus to Christ."

### John Gill:
1. "What was now done by Christ, by his private departure from the Pharisees, being unwilling to irritate them more; by his preaching to the Idumeans, Tyrians, and Sidonians; by healing their diseases, and shunning all ostentation and popular applause; and prohibiting to tell who and where he was, was a fulfilling of this prophecy."
2. "In which the Holy Spirit foreknowing the disposition and actions of Christ, predicted them: which, when considered, must be looked upon as a very large proof of the truth of his being the Messiah."
3. "For that this prophecy belongs to the Messiah, is owned by the Jews themselves."
