# Matthew 12:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The person being described in this passage shows a remarkable level of humility and gentleness. They are not contentious or noisy, but rather choose to be quiet and lowly. They do not engage in arguments or seek attention, preferring to withdraw from controversy. Their mindset is one of selflessness, as they do not complain or accuse others. Instead, they willingly endure wrongdoing without seeking recognition or praise. This portrayal highlights the admirable qualities of meekness and humility that we should strive to embody in our own lives.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 19. He shall not strive nor cry;
	neither shall any man hear his voice in the streets.

### John Calvin:
1. "The general meaning is, that the coming of Christ will not be attended by noise, will have nothing of royal splendor and magnificence."
2. "Were Christ to appear in his glory, what else could be expected, but that it would altogether swallow us up? What wickedness then is it to be less willing to receive him, when on our account he descends from his elevation?"
3. "When Christ is thus pleased to condescend to our weakness, let his unspeakable goodness be embraced by us with joy. Meanwhile, let none flatter himself in his vices, but let each of us labor to make greater proficiency, that we may not be tossed about through our whole life, or bend, like reeds, to the slightest gale."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He shall not strive, nor cry, etc. Not a combatant nor a noisy declaimer in public places, but meek and retiring."
2. "He presents Himself as Judge and yet is meek; He is meek, does not strive, and yet shall be victor."
3. "The lessons are obvious."

### John Gill:
1. "He shall not strive,.... Or contend in a wrangling way... but shall choose rather to withdraw, than to carry on a controversy to a great length, to little purpose."
2. "He shall not complain, or bring in any charge, or accusation against any, but choose rather to suffer wrong, than to contend."
3. "He sought not worldly honour, popular applause, and to be seen of men... was never heard to praise himself, and chose that others should be silent concerning him."

### John Wesley:
1. "He shall not strive, nor clamour; neither shall any man hear his voice in the streets." - That is, he shall not be contentious, noisy, or ostentatious: but gentle, quiet, and lowly.
2. "We may observe each word rises above the other, expressing a still higher degree of humility and gentleness."
