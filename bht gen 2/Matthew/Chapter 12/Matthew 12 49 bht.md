# Matthew 12:49 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus, by stretching forth his hand toward his disciples, was indicating to the crowd that they were his spiritual family. They were not only his followers but also the children of God through grace, making them as dear to him as his own mother and brothers. It is believed that he may have specifically pointed towards the pious women and the twelve apostles when he said, Behold my brethren. This gesture showcased the close bond and unity among those who believed in him.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Disciples [μαθητας] . Not the apostles only, but all who followed him in the character of learners. The Anglo - Saxon renders learning knights. 

### Philip Schaff:
1. "And he stretched forth his hand toward his disciples."
2. "Behold my mother and my brethren."
3. "These are as nearly allied and as dear to me."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Jesus loved his mother and brothers, but they were not to interfere in his Messianic work."
2. "The real spiritual family of Jesus included all who follow him."
3. "But it was hard for Mary to go back to Nazareth and leave Jesus with the excited throng so great that he was not even stopping to eat."

### John Gill:
1. "By this motion of his hand, he pointed out [his disciples] as his spiritual relations, to the multitude that sat round him."
2. "[His disciples] were the children of God by adopting grace, and so his brethren; and were as dear to him as his mother and brethren."
3. "It is reasonable to suppose... he might stretch forth his hand particularly toward the pious and religious women that believed in him... and when he said 'behold my brethren,' he might point directly to the twelve, and the rest of the men that believed in him."
