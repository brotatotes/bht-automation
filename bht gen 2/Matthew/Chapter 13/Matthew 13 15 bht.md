# Matthew 13:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The understanding of the people has been greatly diminished, as their hearts have become dull and lacking in insight. Their persistent resistance and closed-mindedness have resulted in spiritual blindness. Nonetheless, there is a path to redemption and forgiveness if they choose to repent and turn their hearts back to God. This restoration, both physical and spiritual, is intricately intertwined, for the Scriptures teach that the healing of diseases and the pardoning of sins are inseparable concepts.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Marvin Vincent:
1. "They heard heavily with their ears."
2. "The closing of the eyes is described as a judgment of God."
3. "Be converted, turn again, with the idea of their turning from their evil toward God."

### John Calvin:
1. "In the word healing [...] Matthew, as well as the Prophet, includes deliverance from every evil."
2. "Whence comes the mitigation of chastisements, but because God has been reconciled to us, and makes us the objects of his blessing?"
3. "Nothing more is expressed than such an order and connection, that God does not forgive the sins of any but those who are dissatisfied with themselves."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Waxed gross, become fat, carnal, losing its spiritual life."
2. "Their eyes they have closed; a persistent course of action. Lest haply. What they would not do, was what they at length could not do."
3. "The parables themselves betokened the existence of this state of things both as a result and as a punishment."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Made callous or dull -- even fatty degeneration of the heart." 
2. "The hard of hearing are usually sensitive." 
3. "The eyes can be smeared with wax or cataract and thus closed."

### John Gill:
1. "For this people's heart is waxed gross, become stupid and sottish, and without understanding..."
2. "which were partly brought upon themselves by their own wilfulness and obstinacy..."
3. "For healing of diseases and forgiveness of sins, are, in Scripture language, one and the same thing..."
