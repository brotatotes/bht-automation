# Matthew 13:24 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Gospel visible church state, on earth, is called a kingdom of heaven, of which Christ is king. There is a resemblance between a Gospel church state and heaven, and it is very near unto it, serving as the suburbs of heaven. In this parable, the kingdom of heaven signifies the Gospel dispensation. Christ, who represents Himself preaching the Gospel, is likened unto a man sowing good seed. The phrase is like simply means that the following similitude illustrates this concept.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "is like the whole circumstances about to be detailed; like the case of a man"
2. "A similar form of construction is found in ch. Matthew 18:23, and in other parables in Matthew."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The kingdom of heaven is likened unto a man which sowed good seed in his field." 
2. "These exquisite parables are, with like charming simplicity and clearness, expounded to us by the Great Preacher Himself."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Put he forth...means rather to set before or offer." 
2. "Often used of meals, to serve up."
3. "Hence, better, Rev., set he before them."

### John Calvin:
1. "He speaks of a separation, in order to prevent the minds of the godly from giving way to uneasiness or despondency, when they perceive a confused mixture of the good along with the bad."
2. "So long as the pilgrimage of the Church in this world continues, bad men and hypocrites will mingle in it with those who are good and upright, that the children of God may be armed with patience and, in the midst of offenses which are fitted to disturb them, may preserve unbroken steadfastness of faith."
3. "With respect to morals, those faults of men which cannot be corrected must be endured; but we are not at liberty to extend such a toleration to wicked errors, which corrupt the purity of faith."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The kingdom of heaven: The subject in all seven parables. Christ's reign in the new economy of salvation."
2. "Good seed: i.e., of a good kind and good of its kind."
3. "His field: The 'world' is 'His' though 'the devil' works in it."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He placed another parable beside (παρα) the one already given and explained." 
2. "A common way of introducing these parables of the kingdom where a comparison is drawn."
3. "The case of ανθρωπω is associative instrumental."

### John Gill:
1. "The kingdom of heaven is likened unto a man which sowed good seed in his field... the Gospel visible church state, on earth, called a 'kingdom', of which Christ is king..."
2. "There is some resemblance between a Gospel church state and heaven, and it is very near unto it, and is even the suburbs of it."
3. "By 'his field' is meant 'the world', as appears from Matthew 13:38 and means either the whole world, in which both good and bad men live and dwell; and is the field Christ is the proprietor of..."

### John Wesley:
1. "The kingdom of heaven (as has been observed before) sometimes signifies eternal glory: sometimes the way to it, inward religion; sometimes, as here, the Gospel dispensation..."
2. "...the phrase is likewise used for a person or thing relating to any one of those..."
3. "...Christ preaching the Gospel, who is like a man sowing good seed - The expression, is like, both here and in several other places, only means, that the thing spoken of may be illustrated by the following similitude."
