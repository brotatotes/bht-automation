# Matthew 13:31 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The parable of the mustard seed illustrates the kingdom of heaven, representing the Gospel dispensation and the inward kingdom, which begins with small, insignificant beginnings. Despite its initial appearance, the Gospel and the ministry of the word will experience substantial growth and become a source of great blessings for believers. This parable teaches us to not be discouraged by the seemingly insignificant nature of the Gospel but to recognize its immense power and potential. It encourages us to have faith in the transformative and expansive nature of God's kingdom, even when it starts small.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:

### John Calvin:
1. "By these parables Christ encourages his disciples not to be offended and turn back on account of the mean beginnings of the Gospel."
2. "The Lord opens his reign with a feeble and despicable commencement, for the express purpose, that his power may be more fully illustrated by its unexpected progress."
3. "If the aspect of Christ’s kingdom be despicable in the eyes of the flesh, let us learn to raise our minds to the boundless and incalculable power of God."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "A grain of mustard seed’ was the proverbial expression for the smallest thing conceivable."
2. "The plant grows wild, but was often found in the gardens of the Jews."
3. "Took. Probably a hint that the small seed must be taken up carefully or it would be lost."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Grain of mustard seed (κοκκω σιναπεως). Single grain in contrast with the collective σπερμα (Matthew 17:20)."
2. "Took and sowed (λαβων εσπειρεν). Vernacular phrasing like Hebrew and all conversational style."
3. "Is like (ομοια εστιν). Adjective for comparison with associative instrumental as in Matthew 13:13; Matthew 13:44; Matthew 13:45; Matthew 13:47; Matthew 13:52."

### John Gill:
1. "The kingdom of heaven is like to a grain of mustard seed, which a man took and sowed in his field: by 'the kingdom of heaven' is meant, as before, the Gospel dispensation, or the Gospel church state, and the ministry of the word, and the administration of ordinances in it."
2. "By the grain of mustard seed, either the Gospel, or the people of God, or the grace of God in them; and by the man that took and sowed it, the Lord Jesus Christ; and by his field, in which he sowed it, the world, or his church throughout the world."
3. "This parable expresses the small beginnings of the Gospel church state, and the large increase and growth of it, and its great usefulness to the saints."

### John Wesley:
1. "The former parables relate chiefly to unfruitful hearers; these that follow, to those who bear good fruit."
2. "The kingdom of heaven -- Both the Gospel dispensation, and the inward kingdom."
