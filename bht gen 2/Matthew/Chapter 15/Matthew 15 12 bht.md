# Matthew 15:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
When the Pharisees took offense at the saying, the disciples were greatly concerned for what Jesus had said to them and the crowd. They didn't fully understand it themselves and were worried about the Pharisees' negative reaction, thinking it would hinder their chance to bring them to faith. In their concern, they approached Jesus, possibly hoping he would take some precautions to avoid confrontation with the Pharisees.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The Pharisees were offended, after they heard this saying." 
2. "They had given vent to their irritation, and perhaps threats, not to our Lord Himself, from whom they seem to have slunk away, but to some of the disciples, who report it to their Master."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The Pharisees when they heard the saying, took offence." 
2. "They were ready to take offence from the effect of the previous discourse."
3. "The disciples, hearing their disparaging and hostile remarks in the crowd, warn their Master."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They took umbrage at the public rebuke and at such a scorpion sting in it all."
2. "It cut to the quick because it was true."
3. "It showed in the glowering countenances of the Pharisees so plainly that the disciples were uneasy."

### John Gill:
1. "His disciples came to him, being alone, full of concern, for what he had said to the Pharisees, and before all the people; and not so well understanding it themselves."
2. "They seem to wish Christ had not said it; because the Pharisees were, as they thought, grieved and troubled at it, as being contrary to true religion and piety; and lest they should be so stumbled, as no more to attend, and so all hopes of bringing them over to the faith of Christ be lost."
3. "Perhaps it was with some such view, that he would take some prudential step that he might not fall into their hands, that they acquaint him with it."
