# Matthew 15:32 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
After being with Jesus for three days, the people were in need of sustenance. Jesus, moved with compassion, performed a miracle of feeding the five thousand. He did not want to send them away hungry, as he cared for their physical well-being. The people's attachment to Jesus and his miracles demonstrated their deep admiration for him. This act of compassion by Christ reveals his merciful nature and his ability to provide for our needs.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The situation in the wilderness, the kind of food at hand, the blessing and breaking, and distributing by means of the disciples, these are common to the two accounts."
2. "But there is one small token of authenticity which marks these two accounts as referring to two distinct events."
3. "And when our Lord refers to the two miracles, the same distinction is observed; a particularity which could not have arisen except as pointing to a matter of fact."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The Greek order is, and to send them away fasting I am not willing. Therefore Rev. is better: I would not."
2. "Faint (ejkluqwsin). Lit., be unstrung or relaxed."

### John Calvin:
1. "Let us learn from this, that the power of God is not restricted to means or outward assistance, and that it is all one with Him whether there be much or little."
2. "As the blessing of God can make one loaf suffice as well as twenty for satisfying a great multitude, so, if that be wanting, a hundred loaves will not be a sufficient meal for ten men."
3. "The three days’ fasting, of which Christ speaks, must not be understood to mean that they had eaten nothing for three days; but that in desert places they had few conveniences, and must have wanted their ordinary food."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Our Lord Himself takes the first step."
2. "The Lord’s compassion was called out by their physical want, which, however, resulted from their desire to be near Him."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "In the feeding of the five thousand Jesus took compassion on the people and healed their sick."
2. "Here the hunger of the multitude moves him to compassion (σπλαγχνιζομα, in both instances)."
3. "So he is unwilling (ου θελω) to send them away hungry."

### John Gill:
1. "Christ, our high priest, is a merciful one, and is touched with the feeling of the infirmities of men, of every sort, both of soul and body."
2. "This shows the diligence and indefatigableness of Christ, as well as the attachment of the people to him; who were so struck with his miracles and ministry, that though they had been so long from their habitations and families, knew not how to leave him."
3. "He could not bear the thoughts of dismissing them in such a condition; having had but very little sustenance all this while, and so might be said to be in a manner fasting during this time, at least now."

### John Wesley:
1. They continue with me now three days — It was now the third day since they came. Mark 8:1.
