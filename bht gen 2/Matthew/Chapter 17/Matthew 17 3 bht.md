# Matthew 17:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The appearance of Moses and Elijah alongside Jesus on the mount of transfiguration serves as a powerful testament to the fulfillment of the law and the prophecies. Moses, who gave the law, and Elijah, a zealous prophet, represent the entirety of the Old Testament. Their presence affirms that Jesus is the fulfillment of the law and the prophets, demonstrating the perfect harmony between the Old Testament and the work of Christ. Furthermore, God's voice from heaven further confirms and strengthens the disciples' faith in Jesus.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "There need be no question concerning the manner of the recognition of Moses and Elias by the disciples: it may have been intuitive and immediate." 
2. "We can certainly not answer with Olshausen, that it may have arisen from subsequent information derived from our Lord, for Peter’s words in the next verse preclude this."
3. "Luke adds, οἳ ὀφθέντες ἐν δόξῃ ἔλεγον τὴν ἔξοδον αὐτοῦ ἣν ἔμελλεν πληροῦν ἐν Ἱερουσαλήμ."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Moses, a distinguished servant of God, by whom the law was given, and whose institutions typified the Messiah."
2. "It was particularly proper that he should appear, when his prophecies and types were about to be fulfilled."
3. "They appeared 'in glory'...with the glory which the redeemed have there. They conversed about 'his decease which he should accomplish at Jerusalem.'"

### John Calvin:
1. "It is asked, Were Moses and Elijah actually present? or was it only an apparition that was exhibited to the disciples... I think it more probable that they were actually brought to that place."
2. "It was intended to demonstrate that Christ alone is the end of the Law and of the Prophets... Christ did not come into our world without a testimony, but with commendations which God had formerly bestowed."
3. "They talked of the decease which awaited Christ at Jerusalem... to inform us that the same salvation, through the sacrifice of Christ, is held out to us in common with the holy fathers."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "These persons were really present. It was not a vision..."
2. "The two chief representatives of the Old Testament (the law and the prophets)."
3. "Even on the mount of transfiguration the cross is in the foreground..."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Sufficient objectivity is guaranteed by the vision being enjoyed by all three."
2. "Both had mystery connected with their deaths."
3. "They spoke of his decease, the cross, the theme uppermost in the mind of Christ and which the disciples did not comprehend."

### John Gill:
1. "The appearance of these two with Christ was to show that Christ is the end of the law and prophets, that there is an entire agreement between him and them, and that they have their full accomplishment in him."
2. "The Jews have a notion that Moses is not dead, but is ascended, and stands and ministers to God, in the highest heavens."
3. "If it should be asked; how came the disciples to know these two to be Moses and Elias, since they never saw them before, nor could have any statues or pictures of them... they knew them, either by immediate divine revelation, or by the discourse that passed between them and Christ."

### John Wesley:
1. "Here for the full confirmation of their faith in Jesus, Moses, the giver of the law, Elijah, the most zealous of all the prophets, and God speaking from heaven, all bore witness to him."
