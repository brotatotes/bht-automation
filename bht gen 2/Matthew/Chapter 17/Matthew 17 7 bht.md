# Matthew 17:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
He, in his compassionate nature, reassures them, dispelling their fear and bestowing upon them strength and courage. His touch not only brings comfort but also acts as a mediator, lifting them up from their fallen state caused by the awe-inspiring voice of God. With his words and touch, he restores their confidence, encouraging them to rise, stand, and have faith.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### John Calvin:
1. "Christ raises them up when they had fallen, and by so doing performs his office."
2. "He came down to us for this very purpose, that by his guidance believers might boldly enter into the presence of God."
3. "Nor is it only by his words that he comforts, but by touching also that he encourages them."

### Philip Schaff:
1. Matthew 17:7.  Came and touched them. Comp, similar occurrences, Isaiah 6:5-7; Daniel 10:9-10; Revelation 1:17.
	

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  And touched them  (κα αψαμενος αυτων). Tenderness in their time of fear. 

### John Gill:
1. "He observing the fear and surprise they were in, came to their relief and assistance."
2. "He acts the part of a mediator between God and them, and lays hold on them to raise them up, whom the majesty of God's voice had cast down."
3. "Arise, stand on your feet, take heart, and be of good courage, no hurt will come to you."

### John Wesley:
1. Be not afraid — And doubtless the same moment he gave them courage and strength.
