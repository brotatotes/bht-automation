# Matthew 19:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Let the little children come to me, for of such is the kingdom of heaven, Jesus said. This statement reveals both the humility and inclusivity of Christ. He wanted to show that he cared for and valued even the weakest believers, encouraging his disciples to do the same. Jesus wanted the children to be brought to him, acknowledging them as lively symbols of the kind of people who are rightful members of his kingdom. This passage emphasizes that even the little ones, whether in age or in spiritual innocence, have a place in the kingdom of heaven.

### John Calvin:
1. "To exclude from the grace of redemption those who are of that age would be too cruel; and therefore it is not without reason that we employ this passage as a shield against the Anabaptists."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Suffer the little children, etc. The natural impulse would be to bring children to Him, do not check it."
2. "To such belongeth the kingdom of heaven. As in Matthew 18:1-14, the reference is to children in spirit but not to the exclusion of actual children, who probably form the majority in the kingdom of heaven."
3. "Since they are to come (or be brought) to Christ, who is a Saviour, the doctrine of universal depravity is not denied here."
