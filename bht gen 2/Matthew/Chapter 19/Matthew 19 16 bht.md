## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The spirit in which he came...seems to have been that of excessive admiration for Jesus as a man of eminent virtue, and of desire to know from Him by what work of exceeding merit he might win eternal life."
2. "He reproves, by replying that there is but One Good, and that the walking by His grace in the way of holiness is the path to life."
3. "This passage furnishes one of the most instructive and palpable cases of the smoothing down of apparent discrepancies by correcting the Gospels out of one another and thus reducing them to conformity."

### John Calvin:
1. "He did not come treacherously, as the scribes were wont to do, but from a desire of instruction; and, accordingly, both by words and by kneeling, he testifies his reverence for Christ as a faithful teacher."
2. "But, on the other hand, a blind confidence in his works hindered him from profiting under Christ, to whom, in other respects, he wished to be submissive."
3. "He therefore dreams of merits, on account of which he may receive eternal life as a reward due; and therefore Christ appropriately sends him to the keeping of the law, which unquestionably is the way of life."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "This young ruler, who ran and kneeled to Christ, was an honest, earnest seeker after truth and life, with some admiration for, and confidence in, Jesus as a human teacher."
2. "But he was in error, as honest and earnest seekers may be."
3. "Whether a Pharisee or not, he thought to earn eternal life. Hence the passage must not be wrested in favor of legalism."
