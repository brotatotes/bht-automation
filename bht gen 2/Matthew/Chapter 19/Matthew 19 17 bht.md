# Matthew 19:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus responds to a man who addresses him as a good Master by challenging his understanding of goodness. He emphasizes that only God is supremely good. The man's question reflects the belief that eternal life can be obtained through good works, but Jesus redirects him to focus on faith and obedience to God's commandments. Jesus' straightforward response shows his sincerity and highlights the seriousness of the man's inquiry.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Why callest thou me good?...There is none good but one, that is God."
2. "One there is who is good."
3. "The saying of Christ appears especially appropriate in the light of the Rabbinic apothegm, 'There is nothing else that is good but the law.'"

### John Calvin:
1. "Thou falsely callest me a good Master, unless thou acknowledgest that I have come from God."
2. "Christ wishes him to rise higher, that he may hear God speaking."
3. "The only worship of which God approves is that which he has prescribed; because obedience is better to him than all sacrifices."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "There is but one good Being and one good thing, namely, God Himself."
2. "What the young ruler needed was not to do some good work or to learn some speculative morality, but to acknowledge God as the Supreme Good and act accordingly."
3. "The answer rebukes the error of the question, that eternal life can be won by good works."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He evidently had a light idea of the meaning of αγαθος."
2. "This was only a teacher's way of leading on a pupil."
3. "One there is who is good, one alone who is really good in the absolute sense."

### John Gill:
1. "He was good, both as God and man, in his divine and human natures; in all his offices, and the execution of them; he was goodness itself, and did good, and nothing else but good."
2. "There is none good but one, that is God; who is originally, essentially, independently, infinitely, and immutably good, and the author and source of all goodness; which cannot be said of any mere creature."
3. "This Christ said, in order to show, that it is impossible to enter into, or obtain eternal life by the works of the law, since no man can perfectly keep it; and to unhinge this man from off the legal foundation on which he was, that he might drop all his dependencies on doing good things, and come to him for righteousness and life."

### John Wesley:
1. "Why callest thou me good — Whom thou supposest to be only a man.There is none good — Supremely, originally, essentially, but God."
2. "If thou wilt enter into life, keep the commandments — From a principle of loving faith. Believe, and thence love and obey."
3. "Our Lord, therefore, does not answer ironically, which had been utterly beneath his character, but gives a plain, direct, serious answer to a serious question."
