# Matthew 20:30 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Behold, two blind men cried out, seeking mercy from Jesus. Mark and Luke mention one of them, blind Bartimeus, who was notable among the two. It appears that he spoke on behalf of both, expressing their desperate plea for healing.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Marvin Vincent:
1. That Jesus passed by [οτι ιησους παραγει] . The oti is equivalent to quotation marks. They heard the crowd cry Jesus is passing! 

### John Calvin:
1. "They confer on Christ no ordinary honor, when they request him to have mercy, and relieve them."
2. "Their faith is still more clearly exhibited by their acknowledgment of him as Messiah, to whom we know that the Jews gave this designation, Son of David."
3. "The cry proved the ardor of the desire; for, though they knew that what they said exposed them to the hatred of many, their fear was overcome by the ardor of desire, so that they did not refrain from raising their voice aloud."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Mark and Luke mention but one (‘blind Bartimeus, the son of Timeus’), probably a well-known person, and hence especially mentioned."
2. "Lord, have mercy on us, thou Son of David, the better supported order."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They had heard of what he had done for other blind men."
2. "They hail him as 'the son of David' (the Messiah)."
3. "It is just one of many such incidents when Jesus stood still and opened their eyes."

### John Gill:
1. "Calling him Lord, expressing their sense of his deity, dominion, and power; and 'Son of David', thereby owning and professing him to be the Messiah"
2. "The petition they make is, that he would 'have mercy on them', who, through blindness, were in a poor, helpless, and miserable condition; and this was made with great vehemency"
3. "They 'cried' out aloud, that he might hear them, and take pity on them; being eagerly desirous of having their sight, and firmly believing that he was able to restore it to them"

### John Wesley:
1. "Behold two blind men cried out."
2. "St. Mark and St. Luke mention only one of them, blind Bartimeus."
3. "He was far the more eminent of the two, and, as it seems, spoke for both."
