# Matthew 21:25 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The authority of John the Baptist and the dilemma faced by religious leaders are brought to light in the passage. The leaders were forced to address whether John's authority came from heaven or from men. Recognizing that John's baptism represented his entire ministry and teaching, they had to grapple with the choice to believe or reject his testimony. By considering the heavenly authority of John and the implications of their response, this passage challenges us to reflect on the significance of recognizing and embracing God's messengers and their teachings.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The baptism of John—meaning his whole mission and ministry, of which baptism was the proper character."
2. "If we shall say, From heaven; he will say unto us, Why did ye not then believe him?-"Why did ye not believe the testimony which he bore to Me, as the promised and expected Messiah?" for that was the burden of John's whole testimony."

### John Calvin:
1. "For we must bear in mind why John was sent, what was his commission, and on what subject he most of all insisted."
2. "We now see that Christ employed no cunning stratagem in order to escape, but fully and perfectly answered the question which had been proposed."
3. "The discourse relates to John, whom our Lord, in another passage, raises, by a remarkable commendation, above all the prophets."

### Philip Schaff:
1. Matthew 21:25.  The baptism of John. As representing his whole ministry.
	 And they reasoned, consulted, so as to agree upon the answer.
	

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The baptism of John (το βαπτισμα το Ιωανου). This represents his relation to Jesus who was baptized by him."
2. "At once the ecclesiastical leaders find themselves in a dilemma created by their challenge of Christ."
3. "They reasoned with themselves (διελογιζοντο). Picturesque imperfect tense describing their hopeless quandary."

### John Gill:
1. "By what authority did John administer the ordinance of water baptism, which had never been administered before by any?"
2. "Did he receive his authority from heaven, or of men?"
3. "If we shall say from heaven...why did ye not believe him?...why did ye reject the counsel of God against yourselves?"
