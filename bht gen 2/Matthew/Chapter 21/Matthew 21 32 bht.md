# Matthew 21:32 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
John came in the way of righteousness, not only teaching it, but also living it out in his own life. The publicans and harlots, known for their sinful ways, believed in John's message and repented of their sins. Despite witnessing this transformation, those who had initially claimed to go, did not follow through with their repentance.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "ὁδῷ δικ., not only in the way of God's commandments, but in the very path of ascetic purity."
2. "δικαιοσύνης for 'repentance,' as Noah is called δικ. κήρυξ in similar circumstances."
3. "μετεμελ., words repeated from the parable and serving to fasten the application on the hearers."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "John came unto you in the way of righteousness...calling you to repentance."
2. "The publicans and the harlots believed him...these outcasts gladly believed the testimony of John to the coming Savior."
3. "Instead of being 'provoked to jealousy' by their example, ye have seen them flocking to the Savior and getting to heaven, unmoved."

### John Calvin:
1. "God came pointing out the way of righteousness by the mouth of John; but as John spoke in the name of God, and not as a private individual, he is most properly named instead of God."
2. "This passage gives no small authority to the preaching of the word, when those persons are said to have been disobedient and rebellious against God, who despised the pious and holy warnings of a teacher whom tie had sent."
3. "Hence we infer, that faith does not consist solely in a person’s giving his assent to true doctrine, but that it embraces something greater and loftier, that the hearer, renouncing himself, devotes his life wholly to God."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "In the way of repentance, turning to that righteousness of life (which the Pharisees professed to esteem); perhaps with an allusion to Christ Himself as the Way."
2. "Did not even repent afterward. Even after seeing the repentance of these classes, you did not profit by it. Remarkable cases of conversion are designed to be means of influencing others."
3. "The proud and hypocritical are always harder to influence than open sinners."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  In the way of righteousness  (εν οδω δικαιοσυνης). In the path of righteousness. Compare the two ways in Matthew 7:13; Matthew 7:14 and "the way of God" (Matthew 22:16). 

### John Gill:
1. "He had a commission from God; he was no impostor; the doctrine he taught was true... there was nothing in his credentials, ministry, and conduct, that could justly be found fault with."
2. "What he said concerning the wrath to come, and the miserable state and danger they were in; and they repented of their sins, and confessed them, and were baptized of him in Jordan; believing the testimony he gave of Jesus of Nazareth being the Messiah, and Son of God."
3. "After they had seen the effects of John's ministry on these very profligate sinners... that ye might believe him; the testimony he has left behind him concerning the Messiah."

### John Wesley:
1. "John came in a way of righteousness - Walking in it, as well as teaching it."
2. "The publicans and harlots - The most notorious sinners were reformed, though at first they said, I will not."
3. "And ye seeing the amazing change which was wrought in them, though at first ye said, I go, sir, repented not afterward - Were no more convinced than before."
