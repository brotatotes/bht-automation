# Matthew 21:40 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
When the Lord comes to call these husbandmen to account, it is a fulfillment of prophecy and the consequences of their wicked deeds. The question posed seeks to expose their own condemnation through their response. The vineyard owner is not only concerned with their mistreatment of his servants but also their barbarity towards his own son, leading to their own destruction. The question serves to make the chief priests and elders the judges in their own case, highlighting their responsibility in their actions.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "This represents 'the settling time,' which, in the case of the Jewish ecclesiastics, was that judicial trial of the nation and its leaders which issued in the destruction of their whole state."
2. "What will he do unto those husbandmen?"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The question is asked, that they may be warned and condemned out of their own mouth."
2. "Matthew is fuller here than Mark and Luke."

### John Gill:
1. "When the Lord therefore of the vineyard cometh, in a way of providence, to call these husbandmen to an account."
2. "but for their barbarity to his servants, the prophets, time after time; and especially, for the inhuman usage and murder of his own son."
3. "This question is put to the chief priests, elders, and Scribes: and they themselves, who are designed hereby, are made judges in this case."
