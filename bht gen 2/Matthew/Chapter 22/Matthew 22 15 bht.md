# Matthew 22:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
They plotted to trap him in his words (Mark 12:13). The religious leaders consulted together to ensnare him in his talk, in order to accuse him to the governing authorities (Luke 20:20). Their aim was to find fault with Jesus by provoking him to say something that would incriminate him and justify bringing him before the Roman authorities for punishment.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Marvin Vincent:
1. Entangle [παγιδευσωσιν] . From pagiv, a trap or snare. Better, therefore, Rev., ensnare.

### John Calvin:
1. "The Pharisees, perceiving that all their other attempts against Christ had been fruitless, at length concluded that the best and most expeditious method of destroying him was, to deliver him to the governor, as a seditious person and a disturber of the peace."
2. "Their principal object is, to lead the people to dislike him. This is the entrapping to which the Evangelists refer; for they suppose that Christ is surrounded on all sides by nets, so that he can no longer escape."
3. "In addition to the other sects, therefore, there sprung up at that time a religion of the Court."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The main element, no doubt, in the deputation which had assailed Him."
2. "Ensnare him in speech. This mode of attack was adopted in view of the complete failure of the last attempt, and was the most artful of all."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Go and learn."
2. "Took counsel"
3. "Ensnare in his talk"

### John Gill:
1. "They consulted to draw him into a conversation, on a dangerous and ensnaring subject; when they hoped a word might drop unwarily from him, which they might catch at, lay hold on, and improve to his disadvantage..."
2. "...they doubted not of setting the populace against him, and of screening themselves from their resentments; and of gaining their main point, the delivery of him up into the hands of the civil government, who, for treason and sedition, would put him to death."

### John Wesley:
1. Mark 12:13; Luke 20:20.
