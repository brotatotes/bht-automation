# Matthew 22:34 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus is approached by a scribe who seeks to test him. Although the scribe's motivation is to expose Jesus and possibly bring harm upon him, he shows a sign of a teachable and gentle mind by accepting Jesus' response to his question. Jesus, in turn, acknowledges the scribe's proximity to the kingdom of God, highlighting the importance of having the right disposition and openness to learning in one's journey towards the divine.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Put to silence [εφιμωσεν] . There is a kind of grim humor in the use of this word : he had muzzled the Sadducees. Compare ver. 12.

### John Calvin:
1. "They all agree in this, that the scribe put a question for the sake of tempting Christ; but he who is described by Matthew and Mark goes away with no bad disposition; for he acquiesces in Christ’s reply, and shows a sign of a teachable and gentle mind: to which must be added, that Christ, on the other hand, declares that he is not far from the kingdom of God."
2. "At the same time, he is not so much influenced by zeal for the Law, as by displeasure at losing some part of the honor of his teaching."
3. "He therefore inquires at Christ, if he wishes to profess any thing more perfect than the Law; for, though he does not say this in words, yet his question is ensnaring, for the purpose of exposing Christ to the hatred of the people."

### Philip Schaff:
1. Matthew 22:34.    But the Pharisees hearing. Even their gratification at the defeat of their usual opponents, the Sadducees (Mark 12:28; Luke 20:0, did not diminish their enmity. Hence a renewal of the assault.
	

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He had put the Sadducees to silence (εφιμωσεν τους Σαδδουκαιους). Muzzled the Sadducees."
2. "The Pharisees could not restrain their glee though they were joining with the Sadducees in trying to entrap Jesus."
3. "Gathered themselves together (συνηχθησαν επ το αυτο). First aorist passive, were gathered together."

### John Gill:
1. "Christ should be too hard for, and get the victory over all sects among them."
2. "They gathered together at some certain house, where they consulted what to do, what methods to take, to put a stop to his growing interest with the people."
3. "They the rather chose to take this course by setting a single person upon him, that should he succeed, the victory would be the greater, and the whole sect would share in the honour of it."
