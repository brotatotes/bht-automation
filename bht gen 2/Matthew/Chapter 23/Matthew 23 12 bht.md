Whosoever shall exalt himself shall be humbled, and he that shall humble himself shall be exalted. This saying is a universal rule of God's dealings, encompassing both worlds. It is often repeated by our Lord on different occasions, highlighting its significance. Not only does it point to the speedy humiliation of the lofty Pharisees, but it also finds a striking parallel in Ezekiel 21:26-27. Throughout the Gospels, this statement is reiterated at least ten times, emphasizing its importance in understanding the principles of humility and exaltation in God's kingdom.
