# Matthew 24:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Those who remain faithful until the end, enduring through trials and challenges, will experience salvation and be preserved from destruction. This applies not only to the impending destruction of Jerusalem but also to the day of an individual's death or the endurance of the Church until the end of all things. It is a call to persevere in faith and to possess one's soul in patience, knowing that those who bear afflictions and stay true to their beliefs will ultimately be saved and enter into eternal life.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The primary meaning of this seems to be, that whosoever remained faithful till the destruction of Jerusalem, should be preserved from it."
2. "No Christian, that we know of, perished in the siege or after it."
3. "It has ulterior meanings, according to which τέλος will signify, to an individual, the day of his death, his martyrdom, as in the case of some of those here addressed, to the Church, endurance in the faith to the end of all things."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He that shall endure unto the end, the same shall be saved"
2. "He that bears afflictions and persecutions faithfully that constantly adheres to his religion, and does not shrink until death shall be saved, or shall enter heaven."
3. "In your patience possess ye your souls"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "For the individual, ‘the end’ is the day of his death; for the Church, it is the Advent of Christ, the end of all things."
2. "Over against the apostasy of ‘the many’ we have the faithfulness of the few, in spite of false teaching, in spite of prevailing wickedness, an endurance in love."

### John Gill:
1. "But he that shall endure to the end...shall be saved."
2. "In the profession of faith in Christ, amidst violent persecutions...and in holiness of life and conversation...shall patiently bear all afflictions."
3. "Preserved from the general calamity...with an everlasting salvation, which is the case of all that persevere to the end."

### John Wesley:
1.  Matthew 10:22; Mark 13:13; Luke 21:17.
