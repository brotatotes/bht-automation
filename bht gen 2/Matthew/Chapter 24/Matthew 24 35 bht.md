# Matthew 24:35 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The enduring nature of Christ's words is highlighted, emphasizing their unwavering certainty. They are compared to the stability of the heavens and earth, which are fixed and supported by God Himself. While the world and its circumstances may change, Christ's sayings remain steadfast and true. Our salvation is rooted in His promises, which remain unshaken despite the fluctuations of the world. Time may pass, but the faithfulness of Christ's words endures, surpassing the fleeting nature of earthly realities.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Albert Barnes:
1. Heaven and earth shall pass away ... - You may sooner expect to see the heaven and earth pass away and return to nothing, than my words to fail.

### John Calvin:
1. "In order to secure greater confidence in his statements, he illustrates their certainty by this comparison, that it is more firm and stable than the entire structure of the world."
2. "We ought not to judge of his sayings by the changeful character of the world, which resembles the billows of the sea; for we know how easily our minds are carried away by the affairs of the world, when it is undergoing incessant change."
3. "Our salvation, because it is founded on the promises of Christ, does not fluctuate according to the various agitations of the world, but remains unshaken, provided only that our faith rises above heaven and earth, and ascends to Christ himself."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Not merely a strong asseveration...but also a plain declaration that they shall pass away."
2. "The words of Christ will abide as true in the hearts of all His people who look for and haste unto His coming."
3. "It is implied that some time will elapse."

### John Gill:
1. "The bad qualities, or evil circumstances, which attend them through the sin of man, will be removed and pass away, but they themselves will continue in being."
2. "The heavens and the earth, and the ordinances thereof, than which nothing can be more firm and strong, being fixed and supported by God himself, shall sooner pass away, than anything asserted and predicted by Christ shall."
3. "My words shall not pass away; be vain and empty, and unaccomplished; which is true of anything, and everything spoken by Christ."
