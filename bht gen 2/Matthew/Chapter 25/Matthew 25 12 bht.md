# Matthew 25:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
These individuals, who had neglected their spiritual preparation and lacked the righteousness of saints, were not known or acknowledged by the bridegroom. Their exclusion from the wedding feast was a consequence of their own negligence. The solemn warning in this parable emphasizes the importance of endurance and watchfulness until the end, highlighting the blessedness of those who keep their lamps burning for the coming of the bridegroom, regardless of any delay.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Besides being inconsistent with the general tenor of such language, and particularly the solemn moral of the whole, it is a kind of criticism which tampers with some of the most awful warnings regarding the future."
2. "If it be asked why unworthy guests were admitted to the marriage of the King's Son, in a former parable, and the foolish virgins are excluded in this one, we may answer... that those festivities are celebrated in this life, in the Church militant; these at the last day, in the Church triumphant."
3. "To those, even they are admitted who are not adorned with the wedding garment; but to these, only they to whom it is granted to be arrayed in fine linen clean and white, which is the righteousness of saints."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "I know you not - You were not in the company of those who attended me to the marriage-feast, and are unknown to me."
2. "Applied to professing Christians, having only a profession of religion, but no real piety, it means, I do not know or acknowledge you as Christians."
3. "I do not approve of you, or delight in you, or admit that you are my friends."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The refusal is definite and apparently final, and is the basis for the exhortation which follows."
2. "The lesson respects the blessedness of endurance unto the end, of keeping the light bright for the coming of the Bridegroom, however delayed."
3. "Nor are any of them hypocrites, but all faithful souls bearing their lamps; the foolish ones, however, making no provision for the supply of the oil of the Spirit, but trusting that the light once burning, would ever burn, neglecting watchfulness and prayer."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "I know you not." - Hence there was no reason for special or unusual favours to be granted them.
2. "They must abide the consequences of their own negligence."

### John Gill:
1. "they were none of the people whom he foreknew, or knew as his own, and loved with an everlasting love"
2. "he never knew them as his father's choice in him, or as this father's gift to him; he never knew them in the everlasting covenant, or as his sheep, for whom he died"
3. "he never approved of them, liked their persons, or their conduct; or ever owned them as the true companions, either of his bride, or of himself"
