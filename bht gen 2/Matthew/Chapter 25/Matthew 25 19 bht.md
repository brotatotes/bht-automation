# Matthew 25:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
After a long time, the lord of those servants returns to reckon with them, giving them ample opportunity to exercise their gifts and leaving the slothful ones without excuse. The ministers of the word are entrusted with valuable things, such as the Gospel, which is like a pearl of great price, and it is put into their earthen vessels. This highlights the significance and utility of the Gospel ministry in the lives of believers.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "After a long time the lord of those servants cometh and reckoneth with them." 
2. "That any one—within the lifetime of the apostles at least—with such words before them, should think that Jesus had given any reason to expect His Second Appearing within that period."
3. "would seem strange, did we not know the tendency of enthusiastic, ill-regulated love of His appearing ever to take this turn."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "By the return of the lord of those servants to reckon with them is denoted the return of Christ to call people to an account for the manner in which they have improved their talents."
2. "To reckon with them - To reckon is to settle accounts. Here it means to inquire into their faithfulness, and to reward or punish them accordingly."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Long in the history of the whole Church, and long enough in the case of individuals, to allow them to make good use of the trust."
2. "The pre-millennial view places this reckoning at the Second Advent, the general judgment occurring later."
3. "Nothing is said of judgment outside the Church, yet the wicked servant represents one who is not of Christ’s people."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Maketh a reckoning" (συναιρε λογον). As in Matthew 18:23.
2. Deissmann (Light from the Ancient East, p. 117) gives two papyri quotations with this very business idiom and one Nubian ostracon with it.
3. The ancient Greek writers do not show it.

### John Gill:
1. "He stays long, to give time to his laborious ministers to exercise all those gifts he has bestowed upon them, and to leave slothful ones without excuse."
2. "The things that ministers of the word are entrusted with, are things of value; the Gospel is a pearl of great price, or rich treasure, that is put into their earthen vessels."
3. "This shows both the awfulness and usefulness of the Gospel ministry."
