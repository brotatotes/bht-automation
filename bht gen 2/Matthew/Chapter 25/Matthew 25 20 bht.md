# Matthew 25:20 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The servant boldly declares, I have gained, acknowledging that it is through the talents entrusted to him, including spiritual gifts, that his spirituality has increased. He recognizes that while these gifts are from Christ, human effort and responsibility also play a role in utilizing them. As true ministers of the Gospel, they humbly recognize that their gifts are delivered to them by Christ. Furthermore, they are willing to offer all, principal and increase, back to Him, reflecting their willingness to surrender everything to the One who has entrusted them.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The faithful servant does not take the praise to himself μοι παρέδωκας is his confession and ἐπ ʼ αὐτοῖς the enabling cause of his gain."
2. "Without Me, ye can do nothing."
3. "On the joy and alacrity of these faithful servants in the day of reckoning."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "How beautifully does this illustrate what the beloved disciple says of 'boldness in the day of judgment,' and his desire that 'when He shall appear we may have confidence, and not be ashamed before Him at His coming!'" (1 John 4:17; 1 John 2:28).

### Albert Barnes:
1. I have gained - Gained by trading or by honest industry, Matthew 25:16.

### John Calvin:
1. "Those who employ usefully whatever God has committed to them are said to be engaged in trading...the industry with which every man discharges the office assigned him, the calling itself, the power of acting properly, and other gifts, are reckoned to be so many kinds of merchandise."
2. "The gain which Christ mentions is general usefulness, which illustrates the glory of God...when everyone is highly profitable to his brethren, and applies advantageously, for their salvation, the gifts which he has received from God, he is said to yield profit, or gain, to God himself."
3. "According to Luke, he says that he who gained five pounds obtains the government of five cities; by which words he informs them, that the glory of his kingdom will be very different at his last coming from what it now appears."


### Philip Schaff:
1. Matthew 25:20.  I have gained. In addition to and through the talents entrusted. Spiritual gifts are the means of increasing spirituality, yet human effort and responsibility enters.
	

### John Gill:
1. "he came freely and cheerfully, with a holy confidence and intrepidity of mind"
2. "True ministers of the Gospel frankly own, that what gifts they have are delivered to them by Christ"
3. "such are willing that he should have all returned to him, principal and increase"
