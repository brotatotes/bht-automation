# Matthew 25:21 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Gospel ministers recognize their dependence on God and acknowledge their limitations in their service. Their diligence and hard work in preaching the word is highly valued by Christ and should encourage others to be diligent in their own service. The joy that awaits faithful servants is described as full, perfect, and eternal, bringing unspeakable and glorious happiness.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The χαρά here is not a feast, but that joy spoken of in Hebrews 12:2 and Isaiah 53:11, that joy of the Lord arising from the completion of his work and labor of love."
2. "Notice the identity of the praise and portion of him who had been faithful in less, with those of the first."
3. "The words are, as has been well observed, 'not, "good and successful servant," but "good and faithful servant:"' and faithfulness does not depend on amount."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Well done—a single word, not of bare satisfaction, but of warm and delighted commendation. And from what Lips!"
2. "Thou hast been faithful over a few things, I will make thee ruler over many things, &c."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Ruler over many things - I will promote thee to greater honors and to more important trusts."
2. "Joy of thy lord - In the meantime share the pleasures and enjoyments of his palace; be his companion, and receive the rewards which he has promised thee."
3. "Applied to Christians, it means that they who rightly improve their talents will, at the return of Christ, be promoted to great honors in heaven, and be partakers of the joys of their Lord in the world of glory."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "I will set thee over many things. In the kingdom of glory; or on the other theory, during the millenium."
2. "Into the joy of thy Lord. In Luke the official position is recognized in the rule over ‘ten cities,’ etc.; here the reward has a reference to the personal spiritual life. ‘The joy;’ the blessed inheritance which Christ’s servants will have with Him."
3. "The reference to a ‘feast’ seems unnecessary."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The joy of thy lord." 
2. "The word χαρα or joy may refer to the feast on the master's return." 
3. "So in verse Matthew 25:23."

### John Gill:
1. "Gospel ministers do not say so to themselves; they know they can do nothing well of themselves, and when they have done all they can, they own they are but unprofitable servants."
2. "This is said, to show how acceptable a diligent laborious ministry is to Christ, and to encourage industry in the preachers of the word."
3. "Their happiness is expressed by 'joy', which will be full and perfect, and without any interruption or mixture; will be unspeakable and glorious, and continue forever."
