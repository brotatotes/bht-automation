# Matthew 26:46 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Rise, let us be going, Jesus spoke, not only to awaken his sleepy disciples, but also to demonstrate his love for his Father and his submission to his will. Having been relieved of his fears and dreadful apprehensions, Jesus now knew ahead of time who would betray him, when it would happen, and where the betrayer was at that very moment. This portrayal of the scene is vivid and powerful.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Albert Barnes:
1. "Rise, let us be going - That is, probably, “with them.” Let us go wheresoever they shall lead us."
2. "The time when 'I must die' is come. It is no longer proper to attempt an escape."
3. "No more time can be given to repose."

### John Calvin:
1. "Arise, let us go. By these words he declares that, after having prayed, he was furnished with new arms."
2. "He obtained by prayers and tears new strength from heaven; not that he ever hesitated through want of strength, but because under the weakness of the flesh, he had voluntarily undertaken to gain a victory for us in his own person."
3. "But now, when the trembling is allayed, and the fear is subdued, he not only does not retire or conceal himself, but cheerfully advances to death."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Arise, i.e., rouse yourselves, not simply, stand up."
2. "Both expressions imply haste, not necessarily terror. The conflict is over, the spirit of submission reigns; yet He is anxious that the trial of the moment of His betrayal should be over."
3. "His advancing to meet His betrayer may have been to rejoin and protect the eight disciples at the entrance of the garden."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He is at hand (ηγγικεν). The same verb and tense used of the hour above, present perfect active of εγγιζω, to draw near, the very form used by John the Baptist of the coming of the kingdom of heaven (Matthew 3:2)."
2. "Whether Jesus heard the approach of the betrayer with the crowd around him or saw the lights or just felt the proximity of the traitor before he was there (J. Weiss), we do not know and it matters little."
3. "The scene is pictured as it happened with lifelike power."

### John Gill:
1. "Rise, let us be going....this was said, partly to arouse his sleepy disciples; and partly to show his love to his Father, and his submission to his will."
2. "He was now rid of his fears, and free from those agonies and dreadful apprehensions of things, he was but a little while ago possessed of."
3. "He not only knew, as he did from the beginning, who should betray him; but he knew when he would do it; and he knew where the betrayer now was, that he was just now coming upon him, in order to deliver him into the hands of sinful men."
