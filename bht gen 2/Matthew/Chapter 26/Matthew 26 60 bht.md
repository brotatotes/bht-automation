# Matthew 26:60 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
They searched for witnesses who would provide sufficient evidence to condemn him, but they were unable to find any. The false witnesses who eventually came forward were not able to agree on their testimonies, as they differed in the details of what had been said. Some parts of their testimonies were true, but they twisted the meaning and context of the words spoken by our Lord.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  60. ]  οὐχ εὗρον  , i.e. sufficient for the purpose, or perhaps, consistent with itself. See note on ἴσαι , Mark 14:56 .

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They found none on whose testimony they could with any show of reason convict him."
2. "Their witnesses agreed not together... They differed about facts, times, and circumstances, as all false witnesses do."
3. "Two witnesses were required by their law, and they did not 'dare' to condemn him without conforming, 'in appearance' at least, to the requirements of the law."

### John Calvin:
1. "The appellation of false witnesses is applied not to those who contrive a lie which had no foundation, but to those who calumniously pervert what was justly said, and turn it into a crime." 
2. "Christ had indeed said, that when the temple of his body was destroyed, he would raise it up within three days." 
3. "The false witnesses do not now resort to some new contrivance, but they put a wrong interpretation on his words, as if he boasted that he would practice some juggling in building the temple."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "And found it not, i.e., to answer their purpose."
2. "Many false witnesses came, as was natural; but two witnesses to one specific point were required."
3. "Afterward; after numerous vain attempts to find two, even apparently concordant, witnesses."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  They found it not  (κα ουχ ευρον). They found false witnesses in plenty, but not the false witness that would stand any sort of test. 

### John Gill:
1. "According to their law, a man must not die by the mouth of one witness only, but by the mouth of two or three witnesses agreed in a point."
2. "Though they might be willing enough to dispense with the law in this case, yet might have some regard to their own character and reputation."
3. "They knew they must have a charge, and this supported with a proper evidence, or they could not hope to succeed."

### John Wesley:
1. "Yet found they none — On whose evidence they could condemn him to die."
2. "At last came two false witnesses — Such they were, although part of what they said was true; because our Lord did not speak some of those words at all; nor any of them in this sense."
