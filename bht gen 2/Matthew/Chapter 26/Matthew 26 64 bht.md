# Matthew 26:64 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus modestly and plainly speaks of a future event, stating that they will witness the Son of man. This phrase signifies his divinity and humanity. He will sit on the right hand of power, indicating his exalted position alongside God. Additionally, he will come upon the clouds of heaven, aligning with the prophetic depiction by Daniel. Despite the current appearance of Jesus, this proclamation serves as an awe-inspiring reminder of his authority and impending judgment.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "There shall be a sign of the truth of what I say, over and above this confession of Mine."
2. "The glorification of Christ is by Himself said to begin with his betrayal."
3. "The ὄψεσθε is to the council, the representatives of the chosen people, so soon to be judged by Him to whom all judgment is committed."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Thou hast said the truth; or, as Luke 22:70 has it, 'Ye say that I am.'"
2. "Moreover or furthermore. What follows is designed to explain and give confirmation to what he had said."
3. "To sit at the right hand of a prince was the chief place of honor."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Thou hast said...You have spoken the truth."
2. "What thou hast asked me is the fact."
3. "Nevertheless, apart from my affirmation, you shall see for yourself."

### John Calvin:
1. "Though he were to prove to them a hundred times that he was the Christ, it would be of no avail with obstinate men."
2. "Jesus affirms that he is the Christ, not for the purpose of avoiding death, but rather of inflaming the rage of his enemies against him."
3. "The meaning therefore is, that they are widely mistaken, if from his present appearance they form a judgment of what he is; because it is necessary that he should be humbled, and almost reduced to nothing, before he appear adorned with the emblems of his royal power, and with magnificent splendor."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "To be silent would be construed as a confession that He was not the Messiah."
2. "This calm response, drawn out by the oath, is a public declaration of His Messiahship. It ensured His death, but laid full responsibility upon them."
3. "His glory would appear to them as a sign of its truth. He was conscious of His glory in the moment of His condemnation, in His deepest humiliation."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Thou hast said. This is a Greek affirmative reply."
2. "But this is not all that Jesus said to Caiaphas. He claims that the day will come when Jesus will be the Judge and Caiaphas the culprit using the prophetic language in Daniel 7:13 and Psalms 109:1."
3. "It was all that Caiaphas wanted."

### John Gill:
1. "He might truly say he was the Messiah, since all the characters of him in the books of the prophets, met in him; and all the miracles he was to work in proof of his Messiahship were wrought by him."
2. "Sitting" there, denotes his having done his work; and his continuance in his exalted state, until all enemies are subdued under him.
3. "Though this may also be understood of Christ's second coming to judgment, at the last day; when as he went up to heaven in a cloud, he will return, and come also in the clouds of heaven."

### John Wesley:
1. "Hereafter shall ye see the Son of man — He speaks in the third person, modestly, and yet plainly."
2. "Sitting on the right hand of power - That is, the right hand of God."
3. "And coming upon the clouds of heaven - As he is represented by Daniel, Daniel 7:13,14. Our Lord looked very unlike that person now! But nothing could be more awful, more majestic and becoming, than such an admonition in such circumstances!"
