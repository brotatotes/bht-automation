# Matthew 26:67 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
After declaring himself as the Son of God, Jesus was most likely carried out by the Sanhedrin, leaving him in the custody of the soldiers who began to jeer at and assault him. It was during this time that these indignities were inflicted upon him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Luke gives these indignities, and in the same place as here, adding, what indeed might have been suspected, that it was not the members of the Sanhedrim, but the men who held Jesus in custody, who inflicted them on Him."
2. "κολαφίζω is to strike with the fist; ῥαπίζω, generally, to strike a flat blow with the back of the hand but also, and probably here, since another set of persons are described as doing it, to strike with a staff."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Then did they spit in his face - This... [was] significant of the highest contempt and insult."
2. "And buffeted him - That is, they struck him with their hands closed, or with the fist."
3. "Others smote him with the palms of their hands - ... to strike the mouth with the open hand, as if to prevent a person’s speaking, or to evince abhorrence of what he had spoken."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Buffet [εκολαφισαν] . With the fist. Smote with the palms of their hands. All expressed by one word, ejrapisan, from rJapiv, a rod, and meaning to smite with rods, not with the palms."
2. "The same word is employed Matthew 5:39."
3. "It came to mean generally to strike."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Then did they spit in his face... It was an expression of the greatest contempt."
2. "And buffet him. Struck Him with their fists."
3. "And some smote him. Either with the hand, or 'with rods,' probably both."

### John Gill:
1. "...in a way of contempt, spit in his face; than which nothing was more reproachful and disgraceful..."
2. "...buffeted him; cuffed, or boxed him with their double fists:and others smote him, with the palms of their hands..."
3. "...all these indignities were done to Christ..."

### John Wesley:
1. "After he had declared he was the Son of God, the sanhedrim doubtless ordered him to be carried out, while they were consulting what to do."
2. "The soldiers who kept him began these insults upon him."
3. "Then it was that the soldiers who kept him began these insults upon him."
