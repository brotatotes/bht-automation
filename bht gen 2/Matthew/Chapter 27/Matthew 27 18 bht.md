# Matthew 27:18 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Pilate had prior knowledge of Jesus and was aware of the envy and jealousy of the Jewish leaders due to His popularity and the admiration He received from the people. He understood that the charges against Jesus were not based on any genuine concern for Caesar or the well-being of civil order, but rather stemmed from the envy felt by the leaders towards Jesus. Pilate saw through their motives and recognized the ulterior reasons behind their accusations.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  18. ] The whole narrative presupposes what this verse and the next distinctly assert, that  Pilate was before acquainted with the acts and character of Jesus .

### Philip Schaff:
1. "For envy, of His popularity." 
2. "This implies that Pilate knew something of Jesus before; but it shows his injustice, in not protecting Him as innocent." 
3. "Still Pilate, while not wishing to directly oppose the rulers, really desired to thwart them."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Pilate was dense about many things, but he knew that the Jewish leaders were jealous of the power of Jesus with the people."
2. "The envy, of course, came primarily from the leaders."

### John Gill:
1. "He saw in their countenances, and by their charges against him, and by the whole of their conduct, that it was not out of any regard to Caesar, or to the peace and tranquility of the civil government, or to strict justice; but from envy..."
2. "...at his popularity, at his fame and credit, the honor, glory, and applause he had among the people, on account of his doctrine, and miracles, that they had delivered him up into his hands..."
3. "...Pilate might the rather hope to succeed in this scheme of his to release him, by proposing him with so notorious a person to the populace, when the one, as he might reasonably judge, was abhorred by them, and the other had got great credit and esteem among a large number of them."

### John Wesley:
1. He knew that for envy they had delivered him — As well as from malice and revenge; they envied him, because the people magnified him.
