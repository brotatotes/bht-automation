# Matthew 27:28 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
They clothed him in a scarlet robe, a garment that was often worn by kings and military officers. It is likely that this robe was old and worn, symbolizing not only the mockery of Jesus' claims to kingship, but also his identification with the suffering and sin of humanity.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  28. ] Possibly the mantle in which he had been sent back from Herod see note on Luk 23:11 or perhaps one of the ordinary soldiers’ cloaks.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Robe [χλαμυδα] . The short military cloak which kings and emperors as well as soldiers wore.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "His clothing was replaced after the scourging, and probably also the robe which Herod had put on Him to mock Him" 
2. "This robe was removed, and instead they put on him a scarlet robe, the sign of His having attained royal honors"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The scarlet robe (χλαμυδα κοκκινην) was a kind of short cloak worn by soldiers, military officers, magistrates, kings, emperors."
2. "The scarlet color (κοκκινην) was a dye derived from the female insect (κερμες) which gathered on the ιλεξ χοχχιφερα found in Palestine."
3. "This scarlet mantle on Jesus was a mock imitation of the royal purple."

### John Gill:
1. "his clothes on his back seem to be all he had in this world, and of these he is stripped"
2. "an emblem of his being clothed, as it were with our sins, which are as scarlet"
3. "his bloody sufferings in the human nature"

### John Wesley:
1. They put on him a scarlet robe — Such as kings and generals wore; probably an old tattered one.
