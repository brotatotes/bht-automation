# Matthew 27:42 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus, in his great act of sacrifice, saved others but could not save himself. Rather than using his power to free himself, he willingly endured the cross to offer salvation to humanity. Though he could have called upon angels or used his miraculous abilities to escape his captors, Jesus remained steadfast in his purpose, choosing to endure the suffering and death that would bring about spiritual and eternal salvation for all who believe. His selfless act stands as a paradoxical philosophy of life, challenging us to embrace sacrificial love and find life through losing ourselves.

### Henry Alford:

### Marvin Vincent:
1. He saved others, etc. The Greek order is, Others he saved; himself he cannot save.

### John Calvin:
1. "But let us, on the contrary, that our faith may firmly rely on Christ, seek a foundation in his cross; for in no other way could he be acknowledged to be the lawful King of Israel than by fulfilling what belonged to the Redeemer."
2. "And hence we conclude how dangerous it is to depart from the word of God by wandering after our speculations."
3. "We see then that the Jews, through their malice, employed, in defense of their unbelief, those things by which our faith is truly edified."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He saved others. This may be ironical, or it is a recognition of His miracles of mercy, to taunt Him with a supposed loss of power just when He needed it most for Himself." 
2. "His very mercy is used in mockery."
3. "Unless there was an atoning purpose in Christ’s death, it will always seem strange that He did not offer some such miraculous proof of His power."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "If he had saved himself now, he could not have saved any one."
2. "The paradox is precisely the philosophy of life proclaimed by Jesus himself (Matthew 10:39)."
3. "Christ will not give new proofs to the blind in heart."

### John Gill:
1. "He came not to save himself, but others, and to save them spiritually and eternally by dying himself."
2. "He could easily have obtained of his Father more than twelve legions of angels that would have rescued him out of their hands."
3. "He could easily have caused the nails to have given way, and unloosed himself, and come down, who had done such mighty works among them."
