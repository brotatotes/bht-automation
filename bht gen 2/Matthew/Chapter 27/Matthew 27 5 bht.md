# Matthew 27:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
After betraying Jesus, Judas returned to the temple and threw the silver coins down in the very place where the council had assembled. This symbolic act revealed his deep remorse and the weight of his guilt. It was in the midst of this sacred space that Judas grappled with the consequences of his actions, a haunting reminder that even the most religious settings cannot shield one from the consequences of their betrayals.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The sarcastic, diabolical reply... would deepen his remorse into an agony."
2. "But thus were fulfilled the words of the prophet—'I cast them to the potter in the house of the Lord.'"
3. "For the details, see on..."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The consciousness of guilt was fastened to his soul; and Judas found, as all will find, that to cast away or abandon ill-gotten wealth will not alleviate a guilty conscience."
2. "Judas probably passed out of the temple in great haste and perturbation of mind. He sought a place where he might perpetrate this crime.He would not, probably, be very careful about the fitness or the means he used."
3. "Falling headlong - that is, on his face - he burst asunder, and in awful horrors died - a double death, with double pains and double horrors - the reward of his aggravated guilt."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "He cast the pieces over the barrier of the enclosure which surrounded the sanctuary."
2. "Within [the enclosure], only the priests were allowed."
3. "He cast the pieces...into the sanctuary."

### John Calvin:
1. "This is the price for which Satan sells the allurements by which he flatters wicked men for a time."
2. "He throws them into a state of fury, so that, voluntarily cutting themselves off from the hope of salvation, they find no consolation but in death."
3. "Though others would have permitted Judas to enjoy the thirty pieces of silver, by which he had betrayed Christ and his own salvation, he throws them down, and not only deprives himself of the use of them, but, along with the base reward of the death of Christ, he throws away also his own life."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "In God’s temple lay the money for which God’s Son had been sold to death, as a testimony against the Jews."
2. "It would seem that Judas hanged himself over the precipice, fell headlong in consequence of the rope or branch breaking, struck on one of the sharp projecting rocks so common there, and lay ‘burst asunder’ in the field below."
3. "Matthew’s account is part of a history, Luke’s account part of a speech to those who were acquainted with the facts."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "His act was sudden after he hurled the money into the sanctuary, the sacred enclosure where the priests were." 
2. "The motives of Judas in the betrayal were mixed as is usually the case with criminals." 
3. "The money cut a small figure with him save as an expression of contempt as the current price of a slave."

### John Gill:
1. "He cast down the pieces of silver in the temple,.... Upon the ground, in that part of the temple where they were sitting; in their council chamber." 
2. "He was strangled...by a disease called the squinancy, or quinsy, a suffocation brought upon him by excessive grief, deep melancholy, and utter despair." 
3. "Moreover, they affirm...that 'whoever tastes anything before he separates (i.e. lights up the lamp on the eve of the sabbath, to distinguish the night from the day), shall die by "Iscara", or suffocation.'''

### John Wesley:
1. In that part of the temple where the sanhedrim met.
