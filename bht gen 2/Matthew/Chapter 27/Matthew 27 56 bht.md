## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "All this about the women is mentioned for the sake of what is afterwards to be related of their purchasing spices to anoint their Lord's body."
2. "The Taking Down from the Cross and the Burial."
3. "The Women Mark the Sacred Spot that They Might Recognize It on Coming Thither to Anoint the Body."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Mary Magdalene...had a special cause of attachment to the Saviour, having been relieved by him of a most dreadful calamity and restored to her right mind, after being possessed by seven devils."
2. "And the mother of Zebedee’s children - That is, of James and John."
3. "Her name was Salome."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Magdalene [η μαγδαληνη] . Neither Mary of Bethany (Matthew 26:6-13) nor the woman who had been a sinner (Luke 7:37-48). The word denotes merely her town : She of Magdala.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The ministering women, who stood there." 
2. "These pious women, who, with the courage of heroes, witnessed the dying moments of their Lord and Master, and sat over against the lonely sepulchre, are the shining examples of female constancy and devotion to Christ which we now can witness every day in all the churches, and which will never cease."
3. "On the events which immediately succeeded before the request of Joseph, see John 19:31-37."

### John Gill:
1. "She was called Magdalene, either because she was an inhabitant of Magdala, so we read of R. Isaac, or 'Magdalene'; and the rather, because that Magdala was famous, or rather infamous, for whoredom."
2. "Mary the mother of James and Joses: the same with the wife of Cleophas, and sister to Mary, the mother of Jesus."
3. "And the mother of Zebedee's children: that is, of James and John; her name was Salome."

### John Wesley:
1. James — The less: he was so called, to distinguish him from the other James, the brother of John; probably because he was less in stature.
