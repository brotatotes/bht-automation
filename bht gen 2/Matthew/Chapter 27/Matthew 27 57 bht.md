# Matthew 27:57 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
As evening fell, not long after three o'clock, Joseph of Arimathea came forward to request the body of Jesus. His timing was significant, as the period between three to six o'clock was commonly referred to as the evening. This detail adds depth to the narrative and underscores the punctuality and promptness of Joseph's actions. Mark 15:42; Luke 23:50; John 19:38.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The Roman custom was for the bodies to remain on the crosses till devoured by birds of prey." 
2. "The ἦλθεν οὖν καὶ ἦρεν John 19:38, and ἦλθεν δὲ καὶ... ib. John 19:39, which certainly was to Golgotha."
3. "He was also a counsellor, i.e. one of the Sanhedrim."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Before this, the Jews had besought Pilate that the legs of those who were crucified might be broken and the bodies be taken down, that they might not remain on the cross during the Sabbath."
2. "A soldier, however, thrust a spear into his side, and there was furnished the fullest proof that he had expired."
3. "This man sustained a high character. He was an 'honorable counsellor, who also waited for the kingdom of God'; he was 'a good man and a just'; he had nobly set himself against the wicked purposes of the Sanhedrin; he was a disciple of Jesus, though he was not openly his follower, because he feared the Jews."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The Hebrews reckoned two evenings, an earlier and a later."
2. "The reference here is to the earlier evening, though the time may have been well on toward the beginning of the later."
3. "The preparations had to be hurried because the Sabbath would begin at sunset."

### John Calvin:
1. "The three Evangelists satisfied themselves with relating in a brief narrative what was done by Joseph alone."
2. "Joseph's piety and integrity of life are commended, that in the servant of God we may learn to recognize the work of God."
3. "We are taught by this example, that the rich are so far from being excusable, when they deprive Christ of the honor due to him: that they must be held to be doubly criminal, for turning into obstructions those circumstances which ought to have been excitements to activity."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The fact of his being a ‘rich man’ is mentioned here, in allusion to Isaiah 53:9: ‘With the rich in His death.’"
2. "One Joseph takes care of Jesus in His infancy, another provides for His burial."
3. "He seems to have feared that the body might be removed in a disgraceful manner, and his secret faith having been quickened, he took a decisive step."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The request of Joseph of Arimathea for the body of Jesus was a relief to Pilate and to the Jews also."
2. "It was a mournful privilege and dignity that came to Joseph and Nicodemus as they wrapped the body of Jesus in clean linen cloth and with proper spices placed it in this fresh tomb in which no body had yet been placed."
3. "But two women had watched the sad and lonely ceremony, Mary Magdalene and the other Mary. They were sitting opposite and looking in silence."

### John Gill:
1. "His character, as a rich man, is particularly mentioned, not merely to show that such men may be, and sometimes are, instances of the grace of God..."
2. "...as well as to observe the accomplishment of a prophecy, in Isaiah 53:9..."
3. "Some think he is the same with Joseph ben Gorion, the brother of Nicodemus ben Gorion, often spoken of as a priest, and one of the richest of them in Jerusalem."

### John Wesley:
1. When the evening was come — That is, after three o'clock; the time from three to six they termed the evening. Mark 15:42; Luke 23:50; John 19:38.
