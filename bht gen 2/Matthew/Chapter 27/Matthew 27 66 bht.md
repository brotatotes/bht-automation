He set a seal on the stone, securing the tomb with the authority of the Roman government, ensuring that there could be no doubt about the authenticity of Jesus' burial. Despite their intentions to prevent deception, the measures taken by the enemies of Jesus ended up serving as undeniable evidence of his resurrection. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "μετά τινος fieri dicuntur, quæ alicujus voluntate, auxilio, et consilio fiunt." 
2. "The sealing was by means of a cord or string passing across the stone at the mouth of the sepulchre, and fastened at either end to the rock by sealing-clay."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "What more could man do?"
2. "But while they are trying to prevent the resurrection of the Prince of Life, God makes use of their precautions for His own ends."
3. "Their stone-covered, seal-secured sepulchre shall preserve the sleeping dust of the Son of God free from all indignities, in undisturbed, sublime repose; while their watch shall be His guard of honor until the angels shall come to take their place."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "In this way, letters and books were anciently sealed."
2. "We cannot but be struck with the wisdom of God in ordering the circumstances of the Saviour’s burial in such a manner as to avoid the possibility of deception."
3. "His very enemies, therefore, took every possible precaution to place his resurrection beyond the possibility of suspicion of fraud and imposture, and those precautions were the very means of furnishing the most striking proof that his death, burial, and resurrection were not impositions, but most affecting, awful, and yet cheering realities."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Sealing the stone and setting a watch...the rendering rightly corrects the A. V."
2. "The idea is that they sealed the stone in the presence of the guard, and then left them to keep watch."
3. "The sealing was performed by stretching a cord across the stone and fastening it to the rock at either end by means of sealing clay."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Sealing the stone. A string was stretched across the stone, and sealed to the rock at either end, with wax or sealing-clay."
2. "After these precautions, the body could not disappear, except through the miracle of the resurrection."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The sealing was done in the presence of the Roman guard who were left in charge to protect this stamp of Roman authority and power."
2. "They did their best to prevent theft and the resurrection, but they overreached themselves and provided additional witness to the fact of the empty tomb and the resurrection of Jesus."

### John Gill:
1. "they made the sepulchre sure; in the following manner, sealing the stone...that there might be no change of the sentence upon him, and by which it appeared, that his deliverance was by no human assistance"
2. "they made sure the sepulchre with the watch; or sealed the stone, the watch being present...for the greater confirmation of the truth of Christ's resurrection"
3. "by the methods taken, it clearly appears, there could be no fraud in the case...there were more witnesses of Christ's resurrection, than otherwise would have been"

### John Wesley:
1. "They set Pilate's signet, or the public seal of the sanhedrim upon a fastening which they had put on the stone. And all this uncommon caution was overruled by the providence of God, to give the strongest proofs of Christ's ensuing resurrection..."
2. "...there could be no room for the least suspicion of deceit, when it should be found, that his body was raised out of a new tomb, where there was no other corpse..."
3. "...this tomb hewn out of a rock, the mouth of which was secured by a great stone, under a seal, and a guard of soldiers."
