# Matthew 28:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Do not worry, we will convince the governor of the true situation and use our influence to ensure that you are not punished for this. If news spreads among the people that this person has been resurrected, they will believe he is the true Messiah and attempt to establish him as king, causing an uprising. But rest assured, we will protect you from any harm or consequences, so you can be at ease.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Not only 'come to the ears of the governor,' but be borne witness of before the governor, come before him officially."
2. "If a stir be made, and you be in trouble about it."
3. "[πείσομεν], viz. by a bribe of money."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "If this come before the governor — rather, 'If this come before the governor'; that is, not in the way of mere report, but for judicial investigation."
2. "we shall [take care to] persuade him and keep you from trouble," or "save you harmless."
3. "The 'persuasion' of Pilate meant, doubtless, quieting him by a bribe, which we know otherwise he was by no means above taking."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The governor’s ears - To Pilate. If it is reported to him that Jesus was stolen while you slept." 
2. "We will persuade him - We will convince or satisfy him, so that he shall not punish you."
3. "He would not be disposed, 'of himself,' to take any further trouble about the matter. He would feel that all that could be demanded of him had been done, and would not be disposed further to interfere, unless the Sanhedrin should demand it."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "We will persuade [πεισομεν] . i e., satisfy or appease."
2. "Secure you [υμας αμεριμνους ποιησομεν] . Lit., make you without care. The word secure, however, is, etymologically, a correct rendering."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "And if this (i.e., the reported sleeping on guard) comes to the governor's ears... the soldiers ran a risk, against which they are now insured."
2. "We will persuade him... a hint at further and costly bribery; for Pilate was avaricious and corrupt."
3. "Make you secure... they were ready to promise this, though to gain their end they would not scruple to sacrifice their tools."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "We will persuade him, and rid you of care." (ημεις πεισομεν κα υμας αμεριμνους ποιησομεν)
2. "The soldiers have no anxiety." (αμεριμνους, alpha privative and μεριμναω, to be anxious)
3. "This lie lives on through the ages."

### John Gill:
1. "We will persuade him; that this is the true state of the case, and intercede with him, and make use of all our interest, not to punish for it."
2. "Should it spread among the people, that this person was really raised from the dead, they would, one and all, believe he was the true Messiah, and would set him up as a king, and seize upon the government in favor of him."
3. "And will secure you; indemnify you, bear you harmless, keep you from punishment; so that you need not be under any care, or concern on this account."
