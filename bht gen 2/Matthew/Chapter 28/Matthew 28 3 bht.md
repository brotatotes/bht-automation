# Matthew 28:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The angel's appearance was not about his physical form, but rather the radiance and brightness that emanated from him. In his countenance, one could see the glory and purity of the celestial realm. His face exuded such a vivid light, resembling lightning, while his pure white garments symbolized the innocence and purity of his being. This heavenly messenger, adorned in white, was a divine being, fulfilling his purpose to minister and bring forth the message of God.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  3.   ἡ ἰδέα ] not his  form , but  his appearance ; not in  shape (as some would explain it away), but in brightness.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 3. His countenance—appearance.
	was like lightning, and his
	raiment white as snow—the one expressing the glory, the
	other the purity of the celestial abode from which he came.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "His countenance...refers to his 'whole person.' His 'general aspect, or the appearance of the angel himself,' was..." 
2. "Like lightning - Peculiarly bright and shining." 
3. "His raiment white as snow - Celestial beings are usually represented as clothed in white...White, among the Jews, was the symbol of 'purity or innocence.'"

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The word 'countenance' does not refer to the face alone, but to the general aspect."
2. "Matthew alone notes the outward glory, the earthquake, the agency of the angel, and the impotence of the military and priestly power to crush the new faith."
3. "He only notices the adoration of the risen Lord before his ascension, and traces to its origin the calumny current among the Jews to this day."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "His appearance, rather than his form."
2. "As lightning. In its exceeding brightness. Comp. the other descriptions of the angels within the sepulchre (Mark 15:5; Luke 24:4; John 20:12). These angelic appearances cannot be regarded as visions. The accounts are too explicit."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  Appearance  (ειδεα). Here only in the N.T. Compare μορφη and σχημα. 

### John Gill:
1. "There was such a lustre and brightness in his face, that it glittered like lightning."
2. "The angel appeared clad in white, as a token of the purity and innocence of his nature."
3. "Ministering angels were clothed in white."
