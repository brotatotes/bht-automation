# Matthew 28:6 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Come, see the place where the Lord lay, inviting us to witness the empty tomb where Jesus was laid after his crucifixion. It is a powerful reminder of his resurrection, which fulfilled his own predictions. Even the angels at the tomb affirmed this miraculous event, as mentioned by St. John in his gospel. The empty tomb serves as a definitive proof of the Lord's victory over death and his fulfillment of God's plan.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "He is not here; for he is risen, as he said." 
2. "Come, see the place where the Lord lay - Charming invitation!"
3. "Come, feast your eyes on it!"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He has risen, as he said - Jesus had often predicted that he would rise, but the disciples did not understand it, and consequently did not expect it."
2. "The place where the Lord lay - The place where a body was deposited in a sepulchre was commonly a niche cut in the wall of the sepulchre."
3. "The sepulchre was usually large...separated into various apartments. All round the sides of those apartments were niches for the dead."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Through woman death was first introduced into the world; to woman the first announcement was made of the resurrection."
2. "Even as he said... in the sepulchre."
3. "The Lord... speaks of 'Jesus who hath been crucified.'"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Jesus Christ risen from the dead" (ηγερθη απο των νεκρων).
2. "Come, see the place where the Lord lay" (δευτε ιδετε τον τοπον οπου εκειτο ο Κυριος).
3. "The empty tomb is the first great fact confronting the women and later the men"

### John Gill:
1. "He was dead, but is now alive; he was laid in the grave, but God would not leave him there, nor suffer him to see corruption." 
2. "He was risen from the dead, by the power of his Father, and by his own power, as he had before said he should."
3. "Christ, as the Son of God, lay in the bosom of his Father, and in the arms of his love, from all eternity; as mediator, he lay in the womb of God's purposes and decrees, being his elect, in whom his soul delighted."

### John Wesley:
1. "Come, see the place where the Lord lay."
2. "This clearly reconciles what St. John relates, John 20:12, this being one of the two angels there mentioned."
