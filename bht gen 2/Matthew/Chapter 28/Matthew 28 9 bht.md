# Matthew 28:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Behold, in the midst of their fears and doubts, Jesus met the women, bringing them joy and peace. He greeted them with the customary word Hail, which carried the meaning of Rejoice and Happiness attend you. Overwhelmed with awe and gratitude, they fell at his feet and worshiped him, recognizing him as their Lord and God. This encounter with Jesus reinforced their faith and demonstrated that when we faithfully seek him, we can expect to meet him and experience his presence in our lives.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "But (see above) it does not therefore follow that the narratives are inconsistent."
2. "This being taken into account, we may fairly require that the judgment should be suspended in lack of further means of solving the difficulty."
3. "'Jesum ante passionem alii potius alieniores adorarunt quam discipuli.'"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "All hail!—the usual salute, but from the lips of Jesus bearing a higher signification."
2. "And they came and held him by the feet—How truly womanly!"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The meaning of the word 'hail,' here, is rejoice; a term of salutation connected with the idea of joy at his resurrection, and at meeting them again." 
2. "Held him by the feet - Or threw themselves prostrate before him. This was the usual posture of supplication."
3. "In this place the word 'worship' seems to denote the homage due to the Messiah risen from the dead; regarded by them now in a proper light, and entitled to the honor which was due to God."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. All hail [χαιρετε] . The ordinary Greek form of salutation.

### John Calvin:
1. "The Lord, perceiving that Mary was too eager to embrace and kiss his feet, orders her to retire; because it was proper to correct the superstition..."
2. "The Lord permitted her to touch his feet, that nothing might be wanting to give her a full conviction..."
3. "...they worshipped the Lord, which was a proof that they fully recognized him."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Behold, Jesus met them. This (the second) appearance is mentioned by Matthew only."
2. "Took hold of his feet. In mingled fear and joy. The action was very natural in such circumstances, and was allowed to strengthen the evidence of His resurrection."
3. "Before the resurrection, 'worship' had been rendered to Jesus by strangers, not by the disciples. We therefore think that religious worship is here meant, not mere reverence, though the word often has the latter sense."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Jesus met them... as they brooded over the message of the angel and the fact of the empty tomb... the story of this meeting with Jesus... changed their fears into joy and peace." 
2. "His greeting was the ordinary 'Hail'. They fell at his feet... while they worshipped him. Jesus allowed this act of worship... It was a great moment of faith and cheer."

### John Gill:
1. "so souls in the way of their duty, as these women were, oftentimes meet with Jesus, and he with them, as they may expect, and indeed not otherwise"
2. "all hail; all health of soul and body, all happiness and prosperity, both temporal, spiritual, and eternal, attend you"
3. "and worshipped him: with divine adoration, expressing their love to him; their faith and hope in him, owning him to be their Lord and God"

### John Wesley:
1. Hail — The word in its primary sense means, "Rejoice:" in its secondary and more usual meaning, "Happiness attend you."
