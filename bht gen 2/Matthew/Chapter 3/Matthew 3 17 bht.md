# Matthew 3:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The voice from heaven declaring Jesus as the beloved Son is a divine affirmation of unparalleled significance. This declaration surpasses all other forms of praise and testimonies. To be the delight and pleasure of God is the epitome of true glory and the highest expression of virtue. It is in this divine affirmation that the true brilliance and splendor of Jesus as the beloved Son of God is revealed.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Lo, a voice from heaven, saying."
2. "On the solemn import, as regards us, of our Blessed Lord’s baptism, cf. Athanas. Or. i., contra Arianos 47, vol. i. (ii. Migne) p. 355 f."
3. "What follows is well worth reading, shewing the pre-eminence of our Lord’s anointing over that of all others, Psalms 45:7 ; Isaiah 61:1 ; Acts 10:38."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "I delight comes the nearest, perhaps, to that ineffable complacency which is manifestly intended; and this is the rather to be preferred, as it would immediately carry the thoughts back to that august Messianic prophecy to which the voice from heaven plainly alluded."
2. "Nor are the words which follow to be overlooked, 'I have put My Spirit upon Him; He shall bring forth judgment to the Gentiles.'"
3. "Was this voice heard by the by-standers? From Matthew's form of it, one might suppose it so designed; but it would appear that it was not, and probably John only heard and saw anything peculiar about that great baptism."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "A voice from heaven...a public declaration that Jesus was the Messiah." 
2. "The baptism of Jesus...an important part in the work of redeeming people." 
3. "In the preaching of John the Baptist...all the truth of the Gospel must be spoken."

### John Calvin:
1. "The public appearance of Christ, to undertake the office of Mediator, was accompanied by this announcement, in which he was offered to us by the Father, that we may rely on this pledge of our adoption, and boldly call God himself our Father."
2. "The designation of Son belongs truly and naturally to Christ alone: but yet he was declared to be the Son of God in our flesh, that the favor of Him, whom he alone has a right to call Father, may be also obtained for us."
3. "The love of God rests on Christ in such a manner, as to diffuse itself from him to us all; and not to us only, but even to the angels themselves."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "A voice out of the heavens (φωνη εκ των ουρανων). This was the voice of the Father to the Son whom he identifies as His Son, 'my beloved Son.'"
2. "Thus each person of the Trinity is represented (Father, Son, Holy Spirit) at this formal entrance of Jesus upon his Messianic ministry."
3. "It was a momentous occasion for John and for Jesus and for the whole world."

### John Gill:
1. "The Jews...have invented a great many stories concerning 'the voice', or 'the daughter of the voice from heaven'; which they pretend came in the room of prophecy."
2. "This extraordinary voice from heaven...expressed the following words, 'This is my beloved Son.'"
3. "It would be almost unpardonable, not to take notice of the testimony here given to the doctrine of the Trinity; since a voice was heard from the 'father' in heaven, bearing witness to 'the Son' in human nature on earth, on whom 'the Spirit' had descended and now abode."

### John Wesley:
1. "What an encomium is this! How poor to this are all other kinds of praise!"
2. "To be the pleasure, the delight of God, this is praise indeed: this is true glory: this is the highest, the brightest light, that virtue can appear in."
