# Matthew 4:22 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Those who followed Jesus demonstrated their deep commitment by leaving behind not only their nets but even their valuable ship. Their obedience to his call revealed their sincere devotion to him. In their faithfulness, they could trust that the promises of Christ would be fulfilled for them.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "They left their father Zebedee in the ship with the hired servants, showing that the family were in easy circumstances."
 
2. "Andrew and Peter are called together: there, Andrew having been called, with an unnamed disciple, who was clearly the beloved disciple, goes and fetches Peter his brother to Christ, who then calls him."
 
3. "Here, John is called along with James his brother: there, John is called along with Andrew, after having at their own request had an interview with Jesus; no mention being made of James, whose call, if it then took place, would not likely have been passed over by his own brother."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Left their father - This showed how willing they were to follow Jesus. They showed us what we ought to do."
2. "If necessary, we should leave father, and mother, and every friend, Luke 14:26. If they will go with us, and be Christians, it is well; if not, yet they should not hinder us."
3. "We should be the followers of Jesus. And, while in doing it we should treat our friends kindly and tenderly, yet we ought at all hazards to obey God, and do our duty to him."

### John Calvin:
1. "The first thing that strikes us here is the power of Christ’s voice."
2. "Those whom the Lord is pleased to lead and draw to himself, are inwardly addressed by his Spirit, that they may obey his voice."
3. "The commendation bestowed on the docility and ready obedience of his disciples, who prefer the call of Christ to all worldly affairs."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Renounce every human tie, if necessary, to follow Christ."
2. "Yet human ties are not severed by following Christ."
3. "The brothers remained brethren in the Lord, and these four companions in fishing were joined most closely as 'fishers of men.'"

### John Gill:
1. "They not only left their nets, but their ship too; which was of much more value."
2. "They complied with the call of Christ to his people and thereby proved that they were sincerely his followers."
3. "They might expect the gracious promise of Christ to be made good unto them."
