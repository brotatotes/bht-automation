# Matthew 4:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In response to the temptation of Satan, Jesus affirms that humanity does not solely rely on physical sustenance, but rather on every word that proceeds from the mouth of God. He emphasizes the authority of Scripture and models how to combat temptations with the truth of God's word. By identifying with mankind, Jesus overcomes the temptation and expresses unwavering faith and trust in God's provision. He reminds us that the safest and most effective way to resist the devil's schemes is by relying on and applying the truths found in God's Word.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Man shall not live, &c." - "The first word which He uses against him, reaches far deeper."
2. "By ‘every word (or ‘thing,’ for ῥῆμα is not expressed in the original) that proceedeth out of the mouth of God,’ we must understand, every arrangement of the divine will."
3. "God, who ordinarily sustains by bread, can, if it please Him, sustain by any other means."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Not by bread alone shall man live."
2. "Of all passages in Old Testament Scripture, none could have been pitched upon more apposite, perhaps not one so apposite, to our Lord's purpose."
3. "True, the Son of God is able enough to turn stones into bread: but what the Son of God is able to do is not the present question, but what is man's duty under want of the necessaries of life."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The term 'word,' used in this place, means very often, in Hebrew, thing, and clearly in this place has that meaning."
2. "The substance of his answer, then, is: 'It is not so imperiously necessary that I should have bread as to make a miracle proper to procure it. Life depends on the will of God. He can support it in other ways as well as by bread. He has created other things to be eaten, and man may live by everything that his Maker has commanded.'"
3. "We are to meet the temptations of Satan, as the Saviour did, with the plain and positive declarations of Scripture."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The first recorded words of Jesus after his entrance upon his ministry are an assertion of the authority of scripture."
2. "When addressing man, our Lord seldom quoted scripture, but said I say unto you."
3. "In answer to Satan he says, It is written."

### John Calvin:
1. "When you see that you are forsaken by God, you are driven by necessity to attend to yourself. Provide then for yourself the food, with which God does not supply you." 
2. "The Son of God did not choose to undertake any contest of an usual description, but to sustain assaults in common with us, that we might be furnished with the same armor, and might entertain no doubt as to achieving the victory." 
3. "We must now examine more closely the passage, which is quoted by Christ from Moses: that he might make thee know that man doth not live by bread only, but by every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of the Lord doth man live."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Jesus, who was fulfilling the law, answers Satan from the law."
2. "Man. Feeling so keenly His human needs, our Lord does not exert his Divine power, nor assert His Divine dignity, but overcomes the temptation by identifying Himself with ‘man,’ conquering Satan for us men."
3. "We are here taught to overcome Satan with Scripture; to trust God for extraordinary help in extraordinary circumstances."

### John Gill:
1. "The meaning of this scripture is...the soul lives by the word of God, and doctrines of the Gospel; though this is a certain truth." 
2. "Our Lord expresses his strong faith and confidence in God...he overcame this temptation of Satan."
3. "Christ...establishes the authority of the sacred writings; makes them the rule of his conduct...points out to us the safest and best method of opposing Satan's temptations; namely, by applying to, and making use of the word of God."

### John Wesley:
1. "Thus Christ answered, and thus we may answer all the suggestions of the devil."
2. "By every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of God..."
3. "Therefore it is not needful I should work a miracle to procure bread, without any intimation of my Father's will."
