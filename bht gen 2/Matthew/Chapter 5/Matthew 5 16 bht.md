# Matthew 5:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Let your light shine brightly so that others may witness your righteous actions and be inspired to glorify your Heavenly Father. By openly declaring and living out the teachings of the Gospel, you are holding forth the word of light and life for all to see. As a result, when people witness the transformative power of your faith, they may give praise to God and acknowledge the grace that has enabled you to be a vessel of His truth.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The sense of this verse is as if it were ὅπως, ἰδόντες ὑμῶν τ. κ. ἔργ. δοξάσωσιν τ. π. ὑ... the latter verb, and not the former, carrying the purpose of the action."
2. "Thus the praise and glory of a well-lighted and brilliant feast would be given, not to the lights, but to the master of the house; and of a stately city on a hill, not to the buildings, but to those who built them."
3. "The whole of this division of our Lord’s sermon is addressed to all His followers, not exclusively to the ministers of his word. All servants of Christ are the salt of the earth, the light of the world (Php 2:15). And all that is here said applies to us all."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Let your light so shine before men, that they may see your good works, and glorify your Father which is in heaven."
2. "Christians, being the light of the world, instead of hiding their light, are so to hold it forth before men that they may see what a life the disciples of Christ lead, and seeing this, may glorify their Father for so redeeming, transforming, and ennobling earth's sinful children, and opening to themselves the way to like redemption and transformation."
3. "Identity of these principles with those of the ancient economy; in contrast with the reigning traditional teaching."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Let your holy life, your pure conversation, and your faithful instructions, be everywhere seen and known."
2. "The proper motive to influence us is not simply that we may be seen, but it should be that our heavenly Father may be glorified."
3. "Seeing in your lives the excellency of religion, and the power and purity of the gospel, they may be won to be Christians also, and give praise and glory to God for his mercy to a lost world."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "So shine [ουτως]. Often misconceived, as if the meaning were 'Let your light shine in such a way that men may see,' etc."
2. "Standing at the beginning of the sentence, it points back to the illustration just used."
3. "So, even as that lamp just mentioned, let your light shine."

### John Calvin:
1. "He now enjoins them so to regulate their life, as to excite all to glorify God."
2. "The command [...] to seek concealment and a retired situation for their good works, is intended only to forbid ostentation."
3. "We cannot, without offering an open and gross insult to God, extol free will, as if good works proceeded wholly, or in part, from its power."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Not professions or teachings, but what men, with all their prejudices against Christ’s people (Matthew 5:10-12), are forced to acknowledge as real excellences."
2. "The exhortation humbles in order to exalt: all good works, light-giving, purifying and preserving influences, come from God, to whom the glory belongs, but He is ‘your Father.’"
3. "The beatitudes culminated in the promise, ‘for they shall be called sons of God’(Matthew 5:9); the statement of our world to our ‘Father,’ from whom our blessings come, shows us that in the world we may cause position in the world, while leading us above the Him to be glorified."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Thus men are to let their light shine, not to glorify themselves, but 'your Father in heaven.'" 
2. "Light shines to see others by, not to call attention to itself."

### John Gill:
1. "The light of the Gospel...were to be openly declared, and made manifest before men."
2. "They were to hold forth, in the most open and conspicuous manner, the word of light and life."
3. "That when the ministration of the Gospel has been blessed...they may give praise to God, and bless his name for qualifying and sending such Gospel ministers to show unto them the way of salvation."

### John Wesley:
1. That they may see — and glorify - That is, that seeing your good works, they may be moved to love and serve God likewise.
