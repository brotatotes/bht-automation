# Matthew 6:20 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
'Lay up your earthly treasures in heaven,' is a reminder to generously share with the poor, entrusting our possessions to God's care. By doing so, we not only benefit others but also find greater security and eternal rewards. Indeed, heaven itself is often depicted as a treasury, filled with abundant life, peace, and blessings.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Sell that ye have, and give alms; provide yourselves bags which wax not old, a treasure in the heavens that faileth not."
2. "Treasures these, imperishable and unassailable!"
3. "Lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven, where neither moth nor rust doth corrupt, and where thieves do not break through nor steal."

### John Calvin:
1. "Those who assist their poor brethren on the earth lay up for themselves treasures in heaven."
2. "He only intended to show, that we must not be satisfied with bestowing on the poor what we can easily spare, but that we must not refuse to part with our estates, if their revenue does not supply the wants of the poor."
3. "His meaning is, 'Let your liberality go so far as to lessen your patrimony, and dispose of your lands.'"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "‘Heaven’ sometimes means the atmospheric heaven (Matthew 6:26), sometimes the starry heavens (Hebrews 11:12); here it is used in the highest and spiritual sense of the unknown region where God has His throne and reveals His glory." 
2. "This is the ‘place’ where the treasures are laid up; the ‘character’ of the treasures, is therefore, eternal; they are to be collected in a heavenly ‘manner.’" 
3. "Hence the reference is not exclusively to a future locality; nor is there a thought of purchasing a future and heavenly reward by laying up a store of good works."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  Rust  (βρωσις). Something that "eats" (βιβρωσκω) or "gnaws" or "corrodes." 

### John Gill:
1. "Lay up your earthly treasures in heaven; that is, put them into the hands of God in heaven; and this is done, by liberally communicating to the poor."
2. "Treasures are safer here than in our own hands, and will turn to better account, and more to our own advantage, both in this life, and that which is to come."
3. "Heaven is often represented by the Jewish writers as a treasury; and the treasures which are in it are said to be 'treasures of life', and treasures of peace, and treasures of blessing."
