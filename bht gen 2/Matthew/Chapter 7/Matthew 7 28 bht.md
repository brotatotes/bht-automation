# Matthew 7:28 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The people were astonished at his doctrine, a summary of which included teachings on worldly matters, judgment, and the paths to eternal life and destruction. This new and powerful doctrine was unheard of and carried its own evidence, leading to conviction and acknowledgment of its truth.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The people were astonished at his doctrine."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "They were astounded."
2. "We have a similar expression, though not so strong: 'I was struck with this or that remarkable thing.'"

### John Calvin:
1. "By these sayings I understand not only the discourse which he delivered when he came down from the mountain, but the rest of the doctrine, which had already been made known to the people."
2. "The meaning therefore is, that, where he had given the people, on all sides, a taste of his doctrine, all were seized with astonishment, because a strange, indescribable, and unwonted majesty drew to him the minds of men."
3. "What is meant by his teaching them as having authority, and not as the scribes, I have already explained."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "A summary of our Lord’s sayings would not be thus referred to."
2. "The multitudes...must have heard Him."
3. "Teaching...includes the manner as well as the matter of His instruction, both of which awakened astonishment."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The multitudes were astonished... and were left amazed." 
2. "They listened spell-bound to the end."
3. "The verb means literally 'were struck out of themselves.'"

### John Gill:
1. "Concerning the care of worldly things, rash judging, rigid censures, and reproofs; the straitness and narrowness of the way to eternal life, and the largeness and breadth of the way to destruction."
2. "It being something new, and unheard of, what they had not been used to; and coming in the demonstration of the Spirit, and of power, it carried its own evidence along with it, wrought conviction in their minds, and obliged them to acknowledge the truth of it."
