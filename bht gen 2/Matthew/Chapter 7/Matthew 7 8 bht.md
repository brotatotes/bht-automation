# Matthew 7:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The promise of receiving is given to everyone who asks, as long as they ask with faith and a sincere desire to use what they receive for the right purposes. The condition is that they ask in accordance with God's will, seeking what is suitable and beneficial for them.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The only limitation to this promise, which, under various forms, is several times repeated by our Lord..."
2. "...is furnished in Matthew 7:9-11..."
3. "...and in James 4:3, αἰτεῖτε καὶ οὐ λαμβάνετε · διότι κακῶς αἰτεῖσθε."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "For every one that asketh receiveth; and he that seeketh findeth; and to him that knocketh it shall be opened—Of course, it is presumed that he asks aright—that is, in faith—and with an honest purpose to make use of what he receives."
2. "If any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God. But let him ask in faith, nothing wavering (undecided whether to be altogether on the Lord's side). For he that wavereth is like a wave of the sea driven with the wind and tossed. For let not that man think that he shall receive any thing of the Lord."
3. "Ye ask, and receive not, because ye ask amiss, that ye may consume it upon your lusts."

### John Calvin:
1. "Christ presents the grace of his Father to those who pray."
2. "He tells us, that God is of himself prepared to listen to us, provided we pray to him, and that his riches are at our command, provided we ask them."
3. "Though he gives all things freely to us, yet, in order to exercise our faith, he commands us to pray, that he may grant to our requests those blessings which flow from his undeserved goodness."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "For every one that asketh, etc. An invariable rule; a plain promise, not for the future, but for the present, since our Lord says: receiveth, findeth, it is opened." 
2. "This promise, several times repeated by our Lord, is limited only by the verses which follow; comp., however, James 4:3, ‘Ye ask and receive not; because ye ask amiss.’"
3. "God always answers the right kind of prayer, but in His own right way."

### John Gill:
1. "For everyone that asketh receiveth...shall receive all such good things at the hand of God, as are suitable and convenient for him."
2. "And he that seeketh findeth...shall be sure to find them."
3. "And to him that knocketh it shall be opened...he shall have entrance into the holiest of all by the blood of Jesus."

### John Wesley:
1. For every one that asketh receiveth — Provided he ask aright, and ask what is agreeable to God's will.
