# Matthew 8:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus, moved by compassion, reached out and touched the leper, despite it being forbidden and potentially rendering Him unclean. This act showcased both His grace and goodness, showing that His purity and holiness could not be defiled. It serves as an encouraging reminder for sinners to come to Christ with their guilt and filth, as He readily receives them and displays His power and grace. Through His touch, the leper was not only pronounced clean, but was fully healed of his leprosy.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "He who just now expansively fulfilled the law by word and commands, now does the same by act and deed."
2. "The same almighty power which suspends natural laws, supersedes ceremonial laws."
3. "Here is a noble example illustrating His own precept so lately delivered, 'Give to him that asketh thee.' Again, we can hardly forbear to recognize, in His touching the leper, a deed symbolic of His taking on him, touching, laying hold of, our nature."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "And Jesus... moved with compassion... put forth his hand, and touched him." 
2. "How majestic those two words! By not assuring the man of His power to heal him, He delightfully sets His seal to the man's previous confession of that power."
3. "And immediately his leprosy was cleansed. What a contrast this to modern pretended cures!"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It was an offence to the Jews to 'touch' a leprous person, and was regarded as making him who did it ceremonially impure, Leviticus 13:3."
2. "The act of putting forth his hand and 'touching' him, therefore, expressed the intention of Jesus to cure him."
3. "The act of touching was a pledge that he 'was' already cured."

### John Calvin:
1. "He does not, by touching, either pollute himself with leprosy, or become a transgressor of the law."
2. "He chose to take upon him our flesh, that he might cleanse us from our sins."
3. "The stretching out of his hand was therefore an expression and token of infinite grace and goodness."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "And he stretched forth his hand and touched him. Such touch was forbidden."
2. "Despite the consequent healing, it may have been regarded by the Pharisees as rendering Jesus unclean."
3. "The touch of a leper defiled, carried contagion, but the touch of our Lord overcame the defilement and contagion, removing the disease."

### John Gill:
1. "This is a wonderful instance both of the grace and goodness of Christ, in touching this loathsome creature; and of his unspotted purity and holiness, which could not be defiled by it."
2. "A great encouragement this, for poor sensible sinners to betake themselves to Christ, under a sense of their guilt and filth; who readily receives such, in no wise casts them out, but gives immediate discoveries of his power and grace unto them."
3. "He was not only pronounced clean, but was made so; he was thoroughly healed of the disease of leprosy."
