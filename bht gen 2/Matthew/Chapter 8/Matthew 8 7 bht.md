# Matthew 8:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
When the centurion approached Jesus, he asked for healing, but Jesus beyond merely attending to the man's physical needs. With just a few words, Jesus showed his eagerness to do good and responded promptly to the centurion's request, recognizing the prayer of faith and the power it held. Jesus not only offered to come and see the centurion but also pledged to heal him immediately, offering more than what was initially asked. This emphasizes the effectiveness of faith and the undeniable willingness of Christ to provide his presence and assistance to those who believe.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The word, however, originally means to attend, and to treat medically."
2. "Jesus healed all who had need of treatment."
3. "Luke himself does not always observe the distinction."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "And Jesus saith unto him, to those whom he sent (Luke 7:6): I will come and heal him."
2. "According to Luke, our Lord went, expressing in act the willingness here expressed in word, and on the way the occurrences mentioned in the next verses took place."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The word here for heal (θεραπευσω) means first to serve, give medical attention, then cure, restore to health."
2. "Matthew represents the centurion himself as speaking to Jesus while Luke has it that two committees from the centurion brought the messages, apparently a more detailed narrative."
3. "What one does through others he does himself as Pilate 'scourged Jesus' (had him scourged)."

### John Gill:
1. "This answer of Christ's, which is short and full, not only shows the readiness of Christ to do good, how soon and easily he complied with the centurion's request, it being a prayer of faith, and so effectual, and was heard as soon as delivered."
2. "He does not say that he would come and see him, and what his case was, and do what he could for him, as ordinary physicians do; but he would come and heal him at once: and indeed it is a proposal of more than what was asked of him; his presence was not asked, and yet he offered it."
3. "Christ cannot deny anything to faith, his presence or assistance."
