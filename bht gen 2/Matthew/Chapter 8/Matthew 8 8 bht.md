# Matthew 8:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Based on the given commentaries, it is evident that the centurion acknowledges his own unworthiness and shows faith in the power of Jesus. He recognizes that if he, a heathen, is unworthy to welcome Jesus into his home, then how much more unworthy are sinful creatures to have Jesus enter into their hearts. He humbly acknowledges that a word from Jesus is enough to heal and restore, recognizing the unlimited power that is inherent in Christ. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "He knew and felt himself, as a heathen, to be out of the fold of God, a stranger to the commonwealth of Israel..."
2. "...and therefore unworthy to receive under his roof the Redeemer of Israel."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It refers, doubtless, to his view of his 'personal' unworthiness, and not merely to the fact that he was a 'Gentile.'"
2. "It was the expression of a conviction of the great dignity and power of the Saviour, and of a feeling that he was so unlike him that he was not suitable that the Son of God should come into his dwelling."
3. "So every truly penitent sinner feels - a feeling which is appropriate when he comes to Christ."

### John Calvin:
1. "There was astonishing humility in exalting so highly above himself a man who belonged to a conquered and enslaved nation."
2. "The good man, having been informed about the uncommon and truly divine works of Christ, simply acknowledged in him the power of God."
3. "He acknowledges that this is a heavenly power, and does not look upon it as inseparable from the bodily presence, but is satisfied with the word, from which he believes such power to proceed."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "This humility sprang out of his consciousness that he was a heathen, as well as his esteem of our Lord."
2. "But only say in a word. This means one word of command, as Matthew 8:9 shows."
3. "'Humility and faith always go hand in hand.'"

### John Gill:
1. "Lord, I am not worthy that thou shouldst come under my roof. [...] how much more unworthy are poor sinful creatures [...] that Christ should come into their hearts, and dwell there by faith..."
2. "But speak the word only, and my servant shall be healed. [...] it shall be done, which is ascribing omnipotence to him; such power as was put forth in creation, by the all commanding word of God..."
3. "yea, he signifies that if he would but speak a word, the least word whatever; or, as Luke has it, 'say in a word'; let but a word come out of thy mouth, and it will be done."

### John Wesley:
1. The centurion answered — By his second messengers.
