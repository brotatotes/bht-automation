# Matthew 9:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Our Lord speaks their own dialect, reproving their ignorance of the Scriptures. He emphasizes that acts of mercy and compassion are more beloved to God than mere religious rituals. Those who consider themselves righteous in the eyes of men, like the Scribes and Pharisees, are called to repentance and to extend mercy to sinners.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "πορευθέντες μάθετε answers to an expression frequent in the Talmud, צא ולמד."
2. "ἔλεος θέλ."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Sacrifice, the chief part of the ceremonial law, is here put for a religion of literal adherence to mere rules; while 'mercy' expresses such compassion for the fallen as seeks to lift them up." 
2. "The duty of keeping aloof from the polluted, in the sense of 'having no fellowship with the unfruitful works of darkness,' is obvious enough; but to understand this as prohibiting such intercourse with them as is necessary to their recovery, is to abuse it."
3. "When Christ 'called' the sinners... it was to heal them of their spiritual maladies, or save their souls."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "I will have mercy, and not sacrifice." - Hosea 6:6
2. "I am more pleased with acts of benevolence and kindness than with a mere external compliance with the duties of religion."
3. "It is proper, therefore, that I should associate with sinners for the purpose of doing them good."

### John Calvin:
1. "He explains to them, that they are contending with God and the Prophet, when, in pride and cruelty, they are offended at relief which is given to the wretched, and at medicine which is administered to the sick." 
2. "God has no delight in sacrifices, when their minds are destitute of piety, and when their conduct is at variance with uprightness and righteousness."
3. "We are reminded that the grace of Christ is of no advantage to us, unless when, conscious of our sins, and groaning under their load, we approach to him with humility."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "You are students of the Scriptures, yet do not know the meaning of the passage I quote; instead of finding fault, go and learn what you ought to know already."
2. "God prefers mercy to sacrifice, and rejects the latter if it conflicts with the former. This the Pharisees had forgotten in their criticism of His conduct."
3. "The righteous, are those thinking themselves so, sinners, those convinced of their sin; not those actually righteous and sinful. The former view corresponds better with Matthew 9:12, gives a more direct reply to the Pharisees, and enforces the great lesson of the whole passage; sense of need is the first step toward Christ."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "With biting sarcasm Jesus bids these preachers to learn the meaning of Hosea 6:6."
2. "It is repeated in Matthew 12:7."
3. "Ingressive aorist imperative (μαθετε)."

### John Gill:
1. "Our Lord speaks in their own dialect, and tacitly reproves their ignorance of the Scriptures."
2. "God takes more delight and pleasure, either in showing mercy himself to poor miserable sinners; or in acts of mercy, compassion, and beneficence done by men, to fallen creatures in distress."
3. "The persons called to this are not the 'righteous'; meaning either such who are really so, because these are already called to it, though, whilst in a state of imperfection, daily need the exercise of this grace; or rather such who are so in their own opinion, and in the sight of men only, not in the sight of God, which was the case of the Scribes and Pharisees, and very few of these were called and brought to repentance."

### John Wesley:
1. "Go ye and learn — Ye that take upon you to teach others."
2. "I will have mercy and not sacrifice — That is, I will have mercy rather than sacrifice."
3. "I love acts of mercy better than sacrifice itself."
