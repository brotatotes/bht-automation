## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The κράσπεδον, see ref. Num., was the fringe or tassel which the Jews were commanded to wear on each corner of their outer garment, as a sign that they were to be holy unto God."
2. "The article, as in ch. Matthew 14:36, designates the particular tassel which was touched."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Hem [κρασπεδου]. Rev., border. The fringe worn on the border of the outer garment, according to the command in Numbers 14:38."
2. "Dr. Edersheim (" Life and Times of Jesus ") says that, according to tradition, each of the white fringes was to consist of eight threads, one of them wound round the others; first seven times, with a double knot; then eight times with a double knot; then eleven times with a double knot; and, lastly, thirteen times."
3. "The Hebrew characters representing these numbers formed the words Jehovah One."

### John Calvin:
1. "The miracle may shine with brighter glory."
2. "It is perfectly obvious that it was not accomplished by human power."
3. "God deals kindly and gently with his people, accepts their faith, though imperfect and weak, and does not lay to their charge the faults and imperfections with which it is connected."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The border of his garment (του κρασπεδου του ιματιου). The hem or fringe of a garment, a tassel or tuft hanging from the edge of the outer garment according to Numbers 15:38."
2. "The Jews actually counted the words Jehovah One from the numbers of the twisted white threads, a refinement that Jesus had no concern for."
3. "This poor woman had an element of superstition in her faith as many people have, but Jesus honours her faith and cures her."

### John Gill:
1. "This poor woman's case was a very distressed one; she had been attended with an issue of blood twelve years; it was an uncommon flux of a long standing, was inveterate, and become incurable."
2. "She having heard of Jesus, and his miraculous cures, had faith given her to believe, that she also should receive one from him."
3. "However, we learn from hence, that Christ complied with the rites of the ceremonial law in apparel, as well as in other things."

### John Wesley:
1. Coming behind — Out of bashfulness and humility.
