# Matthew 9:27 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
These two blind men, driven by their great faith and fervor, cried out repeatedly, Thou Son of David, have mercy on us. By using the title Son of David, they showed that they firmly believed Jesus was the long-awaited Messiah. The fact that they referred to him in this way indicated their awareness of the prophetic promise that the Messiah would open the eyes of the blind. With such confident hope, they approached Jesus, expecting to receive mercy and the restoration of their sight from him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "ἐκεῖθεν is too vague to be taken as a fixed note of sequence."
2. "εἰς τιμὴν αὐτοῦ τοῦτο κράζουσιν · ἐντιμοτάτη γὰρ παρ ʼ Ἰουδαίοις ἦν ἡ τοιαύτη προσηγορία."
3. "It is remarkable that, in all the three narratives of giving sight to the blind in this Gospel, the title Son of David appears."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "It is remarkable that in the only other recorded case in which the blind applied to Jesus for their sight, and obtained it, they addressed Him, over and over again, by this one Messianic title, so well known—"Son of David" (Matthew 20:30)."
2. "Can there be a doubt that their faith fastened on such great Messianic promises as this, 'Then the eyes of the blind shall be opened,' &c. (Isaiah 35:5)?"
3. "This appeal to Him, as the Consolation of Israel, to do His predicted office, would fall with great weight upon the ears of Jesus."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The blind men probably followed him with their cry for aid immediately on his leaving the house of Jairus."
2. "This was therefore a profession of belief, on the part of these blind men, of the Messiahship of Jesus."
3. "Have mercy on us - That is, show compassion toward us in our affliction, and restore to us the blessing of sight."

### John Calvin:
1. "Now Matthew relates that sight was restored to two blind men, but not so speedily as Christ was wont, on many other occasions, to grant relief to the wretched."
2. "Both by action and by words he intended to make trial of their faith; for he holds them in suspense, — nay, passes by as if he did not hear them, — tries their patience, and what root faith had in their heads."
3. "But it may be asked, if a man is convinced of the power of God and of Christ, is that enough to make him a believer? for such appears to be the meaning of the words."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Blindness was common in the East, and it was natural that the sufferers consorted."
2. "To follow Him, they need only let the crowd take them along."
3. "The title, ‘Son of David,’ applied to Christ by all the blind men whose recovery is mentioned by Matthew, certainly implied His Messiahship."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "It was the supreme opportunity of these two blind men."
2. "Note two demoniacs in Matthew 8:28 and two blind men in Matthew 20:30."
3. "See the same word παραγων used of Jesus in Matthew 9:9."

### John Gill:
1. "With great faith and fervour, with much importunity, and frequently repeating the following words, 'thou son of David, have mercy on us.'"
2. "Whence it appears, that they firmly believed, and were fully persuaded, that he was the true Messiah; for 'the son of David' was a known character of the Messiah among the Jews."
3. "Since it had been prophesied of the Messiah, that he should 'open the eyes of the blind', they might be greatly encouraged to hope and believe they should obtain mercy from him in this respect."
