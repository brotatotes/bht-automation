### Philip Schaff:
1. "And when the demon was cast out, or, ‘the demon having been cast out,’ as a result, the dumb man spake, and the multitudes marvelled. The crowds collected on this eventful day had not yet dispersed."
2. "It was never so seen, lit, ‘Never did it thus appear,’ in Israel. The double cure was remarkable."
3. "There may be a secondary reference of this character expressed indefinitely through fear of the Pharisees."

### John Gill:
1. "The cause of his dumbness being removed, the effect ceased, and the man spake as he did before."
2. "The devil not being able to bear his presence, much less withstand his power."
3. "The vast crowds of people [...] were surprised; and declared very frankly, that though many wonderful things had been done in Israel, [...] yet never were such things seen, or heard, or known of, as were done by Christ."
