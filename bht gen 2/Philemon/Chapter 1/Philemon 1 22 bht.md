# Philemon 22 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The apostle anticipates being granted freedom, emphasizing that it would be given as a favor. He recognizes that his release ultimately depends on the grace of God, acknowledging that his prayers and the prayers of the believers have no power in themselves, but are answered through God's free grace. (41 words)

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "But at the same time (as thou fulfillest my request), also."
2. "We may, perhaps, take this direction as serving to secure the favourable reception of Onesimus."
3. "Philemon was not to consider the Epistle as a mere petition for Onesimus, but as containing special messages on other matters to himself."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "[...] this prospect of Paul’s visiting Colosse would tend to secure a kindly reception for Onesimus, as Paul would know in person how he had been treated."
2. "[...] the same expectation is expressed by him [Paul], [...] written in the same imprisonment."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "While you are granting this favor, do me also another by preparing a lodging for me."
2. "He expected release in answer to the petitions of those who loved him, and the cause in which he was engaged."
3. "I shall be permitted to return to you, as a favor."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Simultaneously with the fulfillment of my request."
2. "The apostle would thus be able to see for himself that Philemon had not disappointed his expectations."
3. "A beautiful assumption of his correspondent's affection for him, in that his visit to them will be a gracious gift [χαρις]."

### John Calvin:
1. "This confidence must have powerfully excited and moved Philemon; and next, he holds out to him the hope of being gratified by his own arrival."
2. "He always kept his mind in suspense, till the will of God was made known by the result."
3. "Here it deserves notice, that he says that everything that believers obtain 'through their prayers,' is 'given' to them; for hence we infer that our prayers, though they are not unsuccessful, yet have no power through their own merit; for what is yielded to them is of free grace."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He did not need much preparation to be made for him, that he adds this clause, but that Philemon may be moved with joy at the prospect of a speedy visit, and also be the more zealous to do everything which St. Paul desires, that he may find nothing failing when he arrives."
2. "Prayer was from the first the Church’s resource when Christ’s messengers were cast into prison. And as God had heard them in the case of St. Peter, so St. Paul feels that they will be effectual on his behalf also."
3. "So he ascribes his expected release already to the grace of God."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "But withal (αμα δε). Along with your kindly reception of Onesimus." 
2. "A lodging (ξενιαν). Old word from ξενος, stranger. In N.T. only here and Acts 28:23."
3. "I shall be granted unto you (χαρισθησομα υμιν). First future passive of χαριζομα. Used either as a favour as here and Acts 3:14 or for destruction (Acts 25:11)."

### John Gill:
1. "Not that the apostle expected or desired any grand apartment to be fitted up for him; a room with such furniture as the Shunamite provided for the man of God was sufficient for him, and what he would have been entirely contented with."
2. "The apostle was a man of prayer himself, and he had a very great regard to the prayers of others, and often desired an interest in them; that he might perform his ministerial work as it should be; that he might have success in it; and that he might be delivered from the unbelieving Jews, and from wicked and unreasonable men."
3. "He knew that the prayers of the saints availed much with God; but whether this hope and expectation of his were answered, is a matter of doubt and question." (30 words)

### John Wesley:
1. Given to you — Restored to liberty.
