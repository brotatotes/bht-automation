# Philemon 4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The apostle, in his heartfelt gratitude, always gives thanks to God for the grace bestowed on others. He not only mentions them in prayer but is a frequent visitor to the throne of grace, interceding for all the saints and churches in his prayers. His devotion and concern for the well-being of others are evident in his continuous prayers and mentions, as he recognizes the work of God in their lives.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "πάντοτε belongs to εὐχαριστῶ, not to μνείαν ποιούμενος." 
2. "The first part, ποιούμενος, expands εὐχαριστῶ."
3. "The second, ἀκούων, gives the ground of the εὐχαριστία for that I hear."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. always
	— joined by Alford with, “I thank my God.”

### Albert Barnes:
1. "I thank my God - That is, for what I hear of you."
2. "It would appear from this that Paul, in his private devotions, was in the habit of mentioning churches and individuals by name."
3. "To remember with affectionate concern these churches and individuals, as he did, Paul must have been a man of much prayer."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The phrase expresses the two ideas, mentioning thee when I call thee to mind." 
2. "Thy love and faith - toward the Lord Jesus and toward all saints."
3. "The clauses are arranged crosswise, love referring to saints, faith to Christ."

### John Calvin:
1. "It deserves attention, that he at the same time prays for that very thing for which he 'gives thanks.'"
2. "Even the most perfect, so long as they live in the world, never have so good ground for congratulation as not to need prayers."
3. "God may grant to them, not only to persevere till the end, but likewise to make progress from day to day."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He has just spoken of God as the giver of Christian grace, and the memory of such grace working in Philemon’s life, calls forth his instant thanksgiving."
2. "How constantly the apostle saw and acknowledged the gifts of God to the infant Church, is testified by the frequent occurrence of this eucharistic expression in all his Epistles."
3. "Whether present or absent, the apostle laid not aside his ‘care of all the churches.’ And here we can note that his heart was full not only of thought for congregations, but for individual members wherever their state was known to him."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Always (παντοτε) goes with ευχαριστω though so far away in the Greek sentence."
2. "Making mention of thee (μνειαν σου ποιουμενος). See 1 Thessalonians 1:2 for this phrase."
3. "In (επ). Upon the occasion of."

### John Gill:
1. "I thank my God.... for the grace bestowed upon him."
2. "The apostle was a man much in prayer, frequent at the throne of grace."
3. "He prayed not for himself only, but for all the saints, for all the churches and ministers of the Gospel."
