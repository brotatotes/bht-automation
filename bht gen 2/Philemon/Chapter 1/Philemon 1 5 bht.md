### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "It is a delicate mark of authenticity, that he says 'hearing' as to churches and persons whom he had not seen or then visited."
2. "The theological order is first faith then love, the fruit of faith. But he purposely puts Philemon’s love in the first place, as it is to an act of love that he is exhorting him."
3. "Towards implies simply direction; unto, to the advantage of."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Hearing of thy love and faith - Either by Onesimus, who, after his conversion, would be disposed to state all that he knew that was favorable of Philemon, or hearing it by some other persons who had come from Colosse to Rome."
2. "The faith which is mentioned here refers to the Lord Jesus; the love, to the saints."
3. "The meaning is, that he had strong faith in the Lord Jesus, and ardent love toward all who were Christians."

### John Calvin:
1. "This praise... includes briefly the whole perfection of a Christian man."
2. "Faith is said to be in Christ, because to him it especially looks; in like manner as in no other way than through him alone can God the Father be known."
3. "The arrangement of the passage is somewhat confused... The meaning may be brought out in this manner, that, whenever the Apostle offered prayer for Philemon, he interwove thanksgiving with it."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The seed sown was bringing forth fruit."
2. "The love was the outward manifest token of the faith within the heart."
3. "But neither is complete without the other, as the apostle testifies in many a place."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Hearing through Epaphras, possibly from Onesimus also."
2. "He spoke of 'thy love and faith' towards the Lord Jesus."
3. "By a sort of momentum, he carries both words over to the saints."

### John Gill:
1. "Faith in the heart is confessed by the mouth; and love, both to Christ and to his people, shows itself, as well as faith, in works of righteousness."
2. "True love believes all things, and hopes all things."
3. "Faith may be considered as being toward our Lord Jesus; which is a looking towards Christ, a moving towards him, a laying hold upon him, and embracing him, a staying and leaning on him, a living upon him, and walking in him."
