# Philemon 6 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
May the communication of your faith be effective, as you not only pray but also thank God, demonstrating through your actions the joy and peace found in believing. Let your acts of beneficence and sharing with others flow from your faith, for it is through these good deeds that your faith will be proved to be true and fruitful. As you communicate and share the blessings of your faith, may others see and acknowledge your piety and charity, giving glory to Christ.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The mixing of prayer and thanksgiving in that clause does not exclude the idea of intercessory prayer."
2. "To join ὅπως κ . τ . λ . with ἣν ἔχεις is flat in the extreme, and perfectly inconceivable as a piece of St. Paul’s writing."
3. "In order that the communication of thy faith (with others)  may become effectual in... the thorough knowledge of every good thing which is in us, to the glory of Christ [Jesus]."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The aim of my thanksgiving and prayers for thee is, in order that the communication of thy faith"
2. "The imparting of it and its fruits"
3. "That thy faith may by acts be proved to be 'a faith which worketh by love.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The phrase translated 'communication of thy faith,' means the making of thy faith common to others; that is, enabling others to partake of the fruits of it, to wit, by good deeds."
2. "What, therefore, did he desire for Philemon? That his faith, expressing itself by good fruits, might be shown to be true and not vain... faith... communicates itself to men by good works."
3. "The truth which is taught in this passage, therefore, is, that when faith exists in the heart, it is very desirable that it should impart its proper fruits toward others in such a way that all may see that it is operative, and may recognize its power."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Koinwnia fellowship is often used in the active sense of impartation, as communication, contribution, almsgiving. [...] the active sympathy and charity growing out of your faith." 
2. "His liberality and love will result in perfect knowledge of God's good gifts. In the sphere of Christian charity he will be helped to a full experience and appropriation of these." 
3. "He that gives for Christ's sake becomes enriched in the knowledge of Christ."

### John Calvin:
1. "That thy faith, by communicating itself, may demonstrate its efficacy in every good thing."
2. "He wishes that, by its effects, faith may be proved to be effectual."
3. "The gifts of God dwell in us in such a manner, that nevertheless, we are partakers of them only so far as we are members of Christ."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "That thy faith being communicated to others may... that they might become faithful and give evidence thereof, even as he was doing."
2. "May become effectual... Good works, the fruits of faith, build up the Christian character to greater perfection."
3. "In the full knowledge of every good thing... To deal in a Christian manner with Onesimus is one of the good things for a full knowledge of which St. Paul intercedes with God on behalf of Philemon."

### John Gill:
1. "An account of it, of its actings and exercises, of the joy of it, and of the peace a soul is filled with through believing, may be given to the mutual comfort and edification of saints."
2. "But here it seems to design acts of beneficence, communicating to the necessities of others, as flowing from faith."
3. "That every good thing that is communicated, or done in faith, which is effectual to any good purpose, should be owned as done by the grace and strength of Christ, and be done to his saints, as if done to himself, and be directed to his glory."

### John Wesley:
1. "I pray that the communication of thy faith may become effectual."
2. "That thy faith may be effectually communicated to others."
3. "Who see and acknowledge thy piety and charity."
