## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "If we read χάριν with the rec., it will be best interpreted by 2 Corinthians 1:15, as a benefit, an outpouring of the divine χάρις not χάρ." 
2. "ἔχειν in the sense of 1Ti 1:12; 2 Timothy 1:3, 'to give thanks' for then it seems always to be followed by a dative."
3. "ἀδελφέ is skilfully placed last, as introducing the request which follows."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "For a reason for the prayer, we have joy and consolation joined in the saints."
2. "Saints are refreshed by thee, His house was open to them."
3. "Put last, to conciliate his favorable attention to the request which follows."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "For we have great joy and consolation in thy love - In thy love toward Christians."
2. "Because the bowels of the saints are refreshed by thee, brother - For your kindness to them."
3. "The sense is, that the kindness which he had shown to Christians had done much to make them happy."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "For we have [γαρ εχομεν]. Read escon I had. Connect with I thank in ver. 4, giving the reason for thankfulness as it lay in his own heart."
2. "Bowels [σπλαγχνα]. Rev., hearts."
3. "Are refreshed [αναπεπαυται]. See on Matthew 11:28. Compare 1 Corinthians 16:18; 2 Corinthians 7:13."

### John Calvin:
1. "We have much grace and consolation."
2. "What has 'grace' to do with 'consolation?'"
3. "To refresh the bowels is an expression used by Paul to mean, to give relief from distresses, or to aid the wretched in such a manner that, having their minds composed, and being free from all uneasiness and grief, they may find repose."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "For I had much joy... in hearing of the various acts of love which had been shown towards all the Christian congregation."
2. "Whether it be the provision which Philemon had made for the Christian worship at Colossæ, or some help which he had bestowed on the poor... we are not told; but if we might judge from the verb employed, which implies a lull or temporary repose, it would seem as though some trouble were in St. Paul’s mind which had been allayed, but yet might rise up again."
3. "The apostle lays aside, as in the greeting, all the authority which his position as evangelist and missionary would have given him, and speaks to Philemon... on the level of common Christian brotherhood."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Paul refers to his joy when he first heard the good news about Philemon's activity."
2. "The hearts (τα σπλαγχνα) [...] for the nobler viscera (heart, lungs, liver) and here for the emotional nature."
3. "Have been refreshed (αναπεπαυτα). Perfect passive indicative of old compound verb αναπαυω as in Matthew 11:28, a relief and refreshment [...] whether temporary or eternal."

### John Gill:
1. "For we have great joy and consolation in thy love"
2. "the bowels of the saints are refreshed by thee, brother"
3. "their hearts were filled with gladness, the load upon their spirits, the pressures upon their minds were removed, and they had an inward pleasure in their souls, and rest, refreshment, and comfort, through the liberal communications of Philemon to them"

### John Wesley:
1. The saints — To whom Philemon's house was open, Philippians 2.
