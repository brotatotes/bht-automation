# Philippians 1:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
These preachers were motivated by envy and strife, but their preaching was focused on Christ alone. They did not preach themselves or doctrines of their own, but purely proclaimed the Gospel of Christ. They emphasized His divinity and humanity, directing souls to Him for all grace and assuring them of His intercession as the risen and exalted Savior.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The two classes mentioned here are not subdivisions of the ἀδελφοὶ ἐν κυρίῳ above, but the first are a new class, over and beyond those ἀδελφοί."
2. "The καί introduces a new motive and consequently, a new class of persons."
3. "The διὰ is not strictly 'for the sake of,' but 'in pursuance of,' to forward and carry out."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Some indeed are preaching Christ even for envy, that is, to carry out the envy which they felt towards Paul."
2. "They wished through envy to transfer the credit of its progress from him to themselves."
3. "Some also of good will—answering to 'the brethren'; some being well disposed to him."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Some indeed preach Christ even of envy and strife... There was a party which was jealous of the influence of Paul, and which supposed that this was a good opportunity to diminish his influence, and to strengthen their own cause."
2. "It was easy, under plausible pretences, to insinuate hints about the ambitious aims, or improper influence of Paul, or to take strong ground against him and in favor of their own views, and they availed themselves of this opportunity."
3. "And some also of good will - From pure motives, having no party aims to accomplish, and not intending in any way to give me trouble."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Strange as it may seem that envy should be associated with the preaching of Christ."
2. "They are jealous of Paul's influence."
3. "Good will toward Paul."

### John Calvin:
1. "Here is another fruit of Paul's bonds, that not only were the brethren stirred up to confidence by his example — some by maintaining their position, others by becoming more eager to teach"
2. "Those who wished him evil were on another account stirred up to publish the gospel"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Some indeed preach Christ even of envy and strife."
2. "There would no doubt be many professing Christians in Rome long before the arrival of St. Paul."
3. "And some also of good will. Really seeking the salvation of their hearers."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Even because of envy and strife. Surely the lowest of motives for preaching Christ."
2. "Envy is an old word and an old sin and strife is more rivalry than schism."
3. "Jealousy is always against those of one's own class or profession as preachers with preachers, doctors with doctors."

### John Gill:
1. "They preached the pure Gospel of Christ, they did not preach themselves, or any doctrines of their own, but Christ, nor the doctrines of other men."
2. "They preached Christ clearly and fully; he was the sum and substance of their ministry; they preached up his person as the true God... and as God and man in one person."
3. "They directed souls to him for all grace, and every supply of it; and assured them that though he died, he rose again from the dead, is ascended on high, is set down at the right hand of God, is an advocate with the Father, and ever lives to make intercession for his people."
