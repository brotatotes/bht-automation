# Philippians 1:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
These individuals who profess to be followers of Christ are divided, with some driven by self-centered motives, while others are driven by genuine love for Christ and for me. The ones motivated by love understand the mission I have been entrusted with and are steadfast in supporting and promoting the gospel. They recognize that I am wholeheartedly committed to this cause, even when circumstances may suggest otherwise.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "but the former out of self-seeking proclaim Christ insincerely"
2. "thinking to raise up tribulation for my bonds"
3. "they do not succeed in their purpose"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Knowing that I am set for the defense of the gospel - They believe that I am an ambassador from God."
2. "To alleviate his sorrows, and to carry forward the great cause to defend which he was particularly appointed, they engaged in the work which he could not now do..."
3. "...he was appointed to be a defender of the gospel, and that they being well convinced of this, went forth to promulgate and defend the truth. That fact was one of Paul’s chief consolations while he was thus in confinement."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "They who are of faction proclaim Christ not sincerely."
2. "Those who are of love do so knowing that I am set."
3. "The phrase 'those who are of faction' occurs Romans 2:8."

### John Calvin:
1. "Those who truly loved Christ reckoned that it would be a disgrace to them if they did not associate themselves with Paul as his companions, when maintaining the cause of the gospel."
2. "We must act in such a manner, as to give a helping hand, as far as possible, to the servants of Christ when in difficulty."
3. "What excuse shall we have, if we shall be traitors to his cause, or what may we expect, if we betray it by our silence, but that he shall in return desert our cause, who is our sole Advocate, or Patron, with the Father?"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The word rendered 'faction' properly implies 'self-seeking.'"
2. "Their motive was not a pure one, though they might call themselves preachers of Christ."
3. "The affliction which is here meant must perhaps be judged of from the character of those who intended to rouse it up."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Out of partisanship."
2. "Not purely, with mixed and impure motives."
3. "To make my chains gall me."

### John Gill:
1. "Those that were truly brethren in the Lord, who had received the grace of God in truth, to whom the Gospel was come in power, and who had a spiritual and experimental knowledge of Christ, and of the mysteries of his grace; these preached Christ of love."
2. "Out of love to the Gospel of Christ, which with them was the pearl of great price, and every truth of it more valuable than gold, silver, and precious stones."
3. "Out of love to the souls of others, knowing their miserable estate by nature, and the danger they were in, and therefore preached Christ as the only way of salvation, whereby they could escape the wrath to come."

### John Wesley:
1. "But the others out of love — To Christ and me." 
2. "Knowing — Not barely, supposing."
3. "That I am set — Literally, I lie; yet still going forward in his work."
