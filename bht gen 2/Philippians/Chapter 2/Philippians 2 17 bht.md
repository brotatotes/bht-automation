# Philippians 2:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Paul willingly presents himself as a sacrificial offering for the Philippians, ready to pour out his life as a libation to strengthen their faith. He views their belief as an offering to God and sees his own blood being poured upon their sacrifice. This depiction highlights the mutual joy and service shared between Paul and the Philippians, as they are united in their commitment to spreading the gospel. The potential sacrifice of his life brings joy and is a cause for celebration, as it advances the work of the gospel and promotes the spiritual growth of the believers.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Yea, if even"; implying that he regarded the contingency as not unlikely.
2. "I am poured out." "I am made a libation."
3. "His expectation of release from prison is much fainter."
4. "I congratulate you all," namely on the honor occurring to you by my blood being poured out on the sacrifice of your faith."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Paul may have regarded himself as a victim prepared for the sacrifice."
2. "The idea of Paul is, that if, in order to render that offering what it should be - to make it as complete and acceptable to God as possible - it were necessary for him to die, pouring out his blood, and strength, and life, as wine was poured out to prepare a sacrifice for the altar and make it complete, he would not refuse to do it, but would rejoice in the opportunity."
3. "Paul was willing to endure anything, even to suffer death in their cause, if it would tend to make their 'service' more pure, spiritual, and acceptable to God."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "I am poured out as a libation. The figure is that of a sacrifice, in which the Philippians are the priests, offering their faith to God, and Paul's life is the libation poured out at this offering."
2. "Upon the sacrifice...The image is probably drawn from heathen rather than from Jewish sacrifices, since Paul was writing to converted heathen."
3. "Sacrifice and service...used of services to both God and man."

### John Calvin:
1. "He calls his death the confirmation of their faith, which it certainly would be."
2. "The gospel is a spiritual sword for slaying victims, so faith is, as it were, the oblation."
3. "The ministers of the gospel have, also, here a singular consolation in being called priests of God, to present victims to him."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "I am ready to die, if my death would help to strengthen you in the faith."
