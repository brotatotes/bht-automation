# Philippians 2:22 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Timothy served alongside Paul in the promotion of the gospel, displaying both fidelity and modesty. This can be seen in his devoted service and submission to Paul as a father figure. Timothy's actions were rooted in a deep faith and love, emulating the way a child serves a father. He proved himself as a laborer in the advancement of the Gospel, serving not just Paul but also God Himself.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "As a child (serveth) a father."
2. "Served with me... in modesty; as Christians are not servants TO one another, but servants of God WITH one another."
3. "Unto, or for the Gospel."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "You have had evidence among yourselves how faithfully Timothy devoted himself to the promotion of the gospel." 
2. "Manifesting the same spirit toward me which a son does toward a father, and evincing the same interest in my work."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. In the Gospel (eijv to eujaggelion). In furtherance of, as ch. 1 5. So Rev.

### John Calvin:
1. "He furnishes Timothy with an attestation of fidelity and modesty."
2. "In evidence of his fidelity, he declares, that he had served with him in the gospel, for such a connection was a token of true sincerity."
3. "In evidence of his modesty, he states, that he had submitted to him as to a father."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "No mention of what was done by Timothy at Philippi occurs in the Acts, but such events call forth display of character; and we may be sure that when St. Paul suffered, Timothy took all the share he could in what his ‘father in Christ’ had to bear."
2. "That his service showed a child’s faith and love we may gather from this verse, that as a child serveth a father, so he served with me."
3. "They were bond-servants unto the Gospel. Whatever, therefore, would advance its progress, it was their bounden duty to undertake, and in this work St. Paul found Timothy a labourer worthy to be set by his own side."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. The proof (την δοκιμην). "The test" as of metals (2 Corinthians 2:9; 2 Corinthians 9:13).
2. Three times they had seen Timothy (Acts 16:13; Acts 19:22; Acts 20:3).
3. In furtherance of (εις). See Philippians 1:5 for this use of εις.

### John Gill:
1. "They had had an experiment of him, a trial of his spirit, and a proof of his gifts and ministry."
2. "He served not the apostle, but with him; he served God as the apostle did, in the Gospel of his Son."
3. "For as a son honours, obeys, and imitates his father, so did he honour the apostle, and give him all respect and reverence that was due to him on account of his office, age, and usefulness."

### John Wesley:
1. As a son with his father — He uses an elegant peculiarity of phrase, speaking partly as of a son, partly as of a fellowlabourer.
