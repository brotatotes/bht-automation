# Philippians 3:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The apostle is expressing not doubt but rather humility, acknowledging that he still has room for growth in his faith. He desires to attain the special resurrection of the righteous, not in a figurative sense, but in a literal sense. He recognizes that this resurrection is not easily attained and requires endurance through trials and tribulations. Ultimately, his hope lies in the resurrection to glory, the culmination of his faith.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "the earnestness of the struggle of faith"
2. "the urgent need of jealous self-watchfulness"
3. "the believer's attainment of the 'resurrection from the (rest of the) dead'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Implying, that he meant to make use of the most strenuous exertions to obtain the object."
2. "The doctrine of the resurrection was, in his view, that which distinguished the true religion, and which made it of such inestimable value."
3. "He sought to participate in the full honor and glory of such a resurrection."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Not an expression of doubt, but of humility." 
2. "The compound noun for resurrection expresses the rising from or from among the dead."
3. "Resurrection from the dead signifies resurrection unto life."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "This is not the language of despondency, but of humility."
2. "St. Paul hopes, because he believes in Christ; but not seldom does his low estimate of himself lead him to speak in language like this, ‘lest I myself should be a castaway.’"
3. "By the ‘resurrection from the dead,’ he means that resurrection in which the righteous will have share."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Not an expression of doubt, but of humility (Vincent), a modest hope (Lightfoot)."
2. "Paul is thinking here only of the resurrection of believers out from the dead and so double εξ."
3. "Paul is not denying a general resurrection by this language, but emphasizing that of believers."

### John Gill:
1. "Not in a figurative sense, the resurrection from the death of sin to a life of grace, of which Christ is the efficient cause, for this the apostle had attained to."
2. "But in a literal sense and design not the general resurrection of the just and unjust, which he believed; but the special and particular resurrection of the righteous, the better resurrection."
3. "And when the apostle says, 'if by any means' he might attain to this, it is not to be understood as if he doubted of it, but it denotes the difficulty of attaining it, since through various afflictions and great tribulations a believer must pass, before he comes to it."

### John Wesley:
1. The resurrection of the dead — That is, the resurrection to glory.
