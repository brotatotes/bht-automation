# Philippians 3:20 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
As followers of Christ, our true citizenship lies in heaven. Our entire way of life, including our thoughts, affections, and desires, should align with the values and principles of our heavenly home. We are but pilgrims in this world, longing for our true dwelling place in the presence of our Savior, the Lord Jesus Christ.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "For our state and feelings are wholly alien from theirs." 
2. "Our country, the state to which we belong, of which we by faith are citizens, is the Kingdom of God, the heavenly Jerusalem." 
3. "We wait for a Saviour, the Lord Jesus Christ."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "We are but pilgrims on earth; how then should we 'mind earthly things?'" (Philippians 3:19; Hebrews 11:9; Hebrews 11:10; Hebrews 11:13-16).
2. "Roman citizenship was then highly prized; how much more should the heavenly citizenship" (Acts 22:28; compare Luke 10:20)?
3. "We wait for (so the same Greek is translated, Romans 8:19) the Lord Jesus as a Saviour" (Romans 8:19- :)

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It is a characteristic of Christians, in contradistinction from those who are the 'enemies of the cross,' that their conversation is in heaven."
2. "They were governed by the laws of heaven; they were a community associated as citizens of that world, and expecting there to dwell."
3. "It led them: (1) To desire to be prepared for his coming; (2) To feel that earthly affairs were of little importance, as the scene here was soon to close; (3) To live above the world, and in the desire of the appearing of the Lord Jesus."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The state of which we are citizens is in heaven." 
2. "It exists now in heaven, having been established there of old."
3. "We await as Savior the Lord, etc."

### John Calvin:
1. "He teaches that nothing is to be reckoned of any value except God’s spiritual kingdom, because believers ought to lead a heavenly life in this world."
2. "We must, nevertheless, be conversant with heaven in mind and affection. For, on the one hand, we must pass quietly through this life, and, on the other hand, we must be dead to the world that Christ may live in us, and that we, in our turn, may live to him."
3. "This passage is a most abundant source of many exhortations, which it were easy for any one to elicit from it. Whence also... is in heaven: let our souls, therefore, dwell with him on high."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "We must pass amongst them, while we live here, as though they were alien unto us, and we merely pilgrims and strangers in their midst."
2. "The apostle means that it is in heaven only that the true Christian can claim (or ought to claim) his rights as a citizen; till he has reached that land, his wanderings are not over."
3. "The verb is always used in the New Testament of that longing for salvation which is expected at the coming of Christ. But this is a longing which, as faith assures those who feel it, will be gratified at last."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "But certainly here Paul's heart is in heaven."
2. "Paul's eagerness for the second coming of Christ as the normal attitude of the Christian colonist whose home is heaven."

### John Gill:
1. "Our citizenship is in heaven"; that is, the city whereof we are freemen is heaven. We behave ourselves here below, as citizens of that city above.
2. "Heaven is the saints' city"; here they have no continuing city, but they seek one to come, which is permanent and durable.
3. "From whence also we look for the Saviour, the Lord Jesus Christ"; the saints have good reason to expect his return as a Saviour, not only as a Judge, and to save and redeem their bodies from the grave and death.

### John Wesley:
1. Our conversation — The Greek word is of a very extenslve meaning: our citizenship, our thoughts, our affections, are already in heaven.
