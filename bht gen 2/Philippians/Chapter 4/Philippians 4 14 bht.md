# Philippians 4:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The apostle wanted to make sure that the Philippians did not misunderstand his words or think that he underestimated their kindness. He praised them for their contribution to his affliction, acknowledging that even though he was content with his situation and had the strength to endure, their support was commendable. In fact, he highlighted that by supporting poor believers and ministers, they were engaging in a commendable act of well-doing that brought glory to Christ.

### Henry Alford:
1. "Cavet, ne fortiter loquendo contempsisse ipsorum beneficium videatur." Calv.
2. "μὴ γὰρ ἐπειδή , φησιν , ἐν χρείᾳ οὐ καθέστηκα , νομίσητε μὴ δεῖσθαί με τοῦ πράγματος · δέομαι δι ʼ ὑμᾶς ." Chrys.
3. "συγκοινωνήσαντές μου τῇ θλίψει ] ὅρα σοφίαν , πῶς ἐπαίρει τὸ πρᾶγμα." Thl.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "He here guards against their thinking from what he has just said, that he makes light of their bounty."
2. "ye did communicate with my affliction—that is, ye made yourselves sharers with me in my present affliction, namely, by sympathy; of which sympathy your contribution is the proof."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It was well for them to show sympathy for his sufferings; for it evinced a proper regard for a benefactor and an apostle."
2. "You took part with my affliction. That is, you sympathized with me, and assisted me in bearing it."
3. "The relief which they had sent, not only supplied his wants, but it sustained him by the certainty that he was not forgotten."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Notwithstanding. Lest, in declaring his independence of human aid, he should seem to disparage the Philippians' gift.

### John Calvin:
1. "By proclaiming in magnificent terms his steadfastness, he meant to provide against the Philippians supposing that he had given way under the pressure of want."
2. "He now takes care that it may not, from his speaking in high terms, appear as though he despised their kindness — a thing that would not merely show cruelty and obstinacy, but also haughtiness."
3. "He at the same time provides for this, that if any other of the servants of Christ should stand in need of their assistance they may not be slow to give him help."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The apostle through the sustaining power of Christ would surely, he feels, have been supported to do the work for which the Lord was pleased to use him; yet it was good that the Philippians sent him aid, for it was a proof of their own stedfastness in the faith..."
2. "...and was a service rendered not to the apostle only, but to the whole cause of Christ through their example, and so was acceptable unto God."
3. "And their action showed still more, that the Philippians suffered in the apostle’s suffering, took their part not only in supplying his bodily needs, but so far as sympathy could do it, in sharing his persecution."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  That ye had fellowship  (συνκοινωνησαντες). First aorist active participle (simultaneous action with the principal verb καλως εποιησατε). "Ye did well contributing for my affliction." 

### John Gill:
1. "This he says lest they should think he slighted their kindness, and lest they should be discouraged from doing any such thing of this kind another time, either to himself or others."
2. "Though he was so well contented with his state, and knew how to be abased and to suffer need, and could do all things through the strength of Christ, yet he observes they did well in communicating to him."
3. "Communicating to poor saints or ministers is a considerable branch of well doing; it is a good work when it is done in faith, and from love, and with a view to the glory, honour, and interest of Christ."
