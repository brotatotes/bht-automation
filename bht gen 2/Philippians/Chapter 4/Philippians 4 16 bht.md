This act of generosity and care ensured that Paul had the necessary resources to continue his ministry and meet his personal needs. The thoughtfulness of the believers in providing for his necessities demonstrated their commitment to supporting and assisting the apostle in his work.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "ye were the only ones, &c., and ye began as early as ἐν Θεσσ ., i.e. when I was at Thessalonica."
2. "In such brachylogical constructions the preposition of rest, as belonging to the act accomplished, overbears the preposition of motion, as belonging to it only in its imperfect state."
3. "when the first arrived, they had sent once: when the second, not only once, but twice."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 16. even in Thessalonica—"even"
	as early as when I had got no further than Thessalonica, ye sent me
	supplies for my necessities more than once.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Paul remained there long enough to establish a flourishing church."
2. "He met, indeed, with much opposition and persecution there."
3. "It was necessary that his wants should be supplied by others."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Even in Thessalonica [και] . Better also : in addition to the contribution received at Corinth.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The hurried departure of the apostle from Philippi gave little time for arranging a provision for his further support and journeying."
2. "The condition to which he had been reduced by the scourging made it impossible that he should attempt, for a time at least, to work with his hands."
3. "We can therefore picture the congregation in Philippi gathering together what they could at once, and then sending, as they were collected, further supplies for the apostle’s needs."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Both once and twice" they did it "even in Thessalonica" and so before Paul went to Corinth."
2. "See the same Greek idiom in 1 Thessalonians 2:18."

### John Gill:
1. "For even in Thessalonica,.... When the apostle was there; for from Philippi he went to Thessalonica" 
2. "ye sent once and again unto my necessity" 
3. "for his use and service, to support him while he was at that place, and relieve and assist him in his necessities"
