# Philippians 4:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Apostle's main concern was not for his own gain, but rather for the spiritual benefit of those who gave. He rejoiced at the generosity of their gift because it not only multiplied their faith, but it also contributed to their spiritual well-being. He sought their spiritual good and took joy in seeing their right spirit, knowing that they would be rewarded. The gift they gave would result in a harvest of blessings for them in the eyes of the Lord.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "It was not for his own sake but for theirs that he rejoiced at their liberality, because it multiplied the fruits of their faith."
2. "Not that I seek the gift, but I do seek the fruit which abounds to your account."
3. "The result of your labour for me in the Lord."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "It is not that I seek after the gift, but I do seek after the fruit that aboundeth to your account." 
2. "What I do seek is your spiritual good, in the abounding of fruits of your faith which shall be put down to your account."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The reason why I rejoice in the reception of what you have sent to me, is not that I am covetous...his chief interest in it arose from the fact that it would contribute ultimately to their own good."
2. "All our selfish feelings and gratifications may be absorbed and lost in the superior joy which we have in seeing others actuated by a right spirit, and in the belief that they will be rewarded."
3. "The language is taken from the fact, that a man reaps or gathers the fruit or result of that which he plants... He desired that when they came to appear before God, they might reap the benefit of all the acts of kindness which they had shown him."

### John Calvin:
1. "Not that I demand a gift."
2. "He accordingly declares, that he consulted not so much his own advantage as theirs."
3. "For there are just so many articles that you may reckon to have been transferred to the table of accounts."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Had it only represented material aid, it would have found scant praise from him, but he knows that it is the indication of spiritual life and faith."
2. "He cannot repay them what they have given. He can only keep in the records of his heart the memory of their much love. But the account is kept elsewhere for them, and to their benefit goes on increasing."
3. "In the word ‘fruit’ there is implied the interest on that which is laid out, and which God reckons on to their credit. So St. Paul, in the great Householder’s eye, is to the Philippians as ‘an exchanger,’ with whom, when they bestow their talent, it shall gain its usury, and they shall in no wise lose their reward."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "the Apostle's nervous anxiety to clear himself" of wanting more gifts.
2. "Why not say his delicate courtesy?"

### John Gill:
1. "He sought not theirs, but them."
2. "His great desire was to see fruits of righteousness grow upon them."
3. "His view was, that the balance might be on their side, and that much might be received by them."

### John Wesley:
1. Not that I desire — For my own sake, the very gift which I receive of you.
