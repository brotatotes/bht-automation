# Philippians 4:23 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen. This closing benediction is a token of the genuineness of the epistle, marking it as an original work of the Apostle Paul. The phrase with your spirit is found in some versions and manuscripts and is reminiscent of the desire for the grace of Christ to be present in the innermost being of the readers. The subscription added at the end of the epistle indicates that it was written by Paul from Rome and delivered to the Philippians by their minister, Epaphroditus.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ be with your spirit."
2. "Such changes from the primitive text have been introduced by a desire to bring all the closing benedictions as nearly as possible into one form."
3. "Probably at first no more was done than to write the different form on the margin. After this, some future scribe, having a text and margin, considered it best to bring all into the same shape."

### John Gill:
1. "The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ [be] with you all,.... The Vulgate Latin and Ethiopic versions read, 'with your spirit', as in Galatians 6:18; and so the Alexandrian copy and some others read. This is the apostle's token in all his epistles of the genuineness of them, and which he wrote with his own hand, 2 Thessalonians 3:17."
2. "Amen: with which all the epistles are concluded."
3. "The subscription is,[it was] written [to] the Philippians from Rome, by Epaphroditus; that this epistle was written to the Philippians by the Apostle Paul, when he was a prisoner at Rome, and sent to them by Epaphroditus, their minister, when he returned from him to them."
