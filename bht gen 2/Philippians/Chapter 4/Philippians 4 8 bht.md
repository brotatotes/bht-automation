# Philippians 4:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Let us not only speak the truth, but also live a life of integrity and goodness. In a world filled with deceit and falsehood, it is important to uphold what is true and commendable. Let us focus our thoughts on these virtuous qualities, both in our own lives and in how we inspire others.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "τί ἐστι τὸ λοιπόν: ἀντὶ τοῦ, πάντα ἡμῖν εἴρηται."
2. "This beautiful sentence, full of the Apostle’s fervour and eloquence, derives much force from the frequent repetition of ὅσα, and then of εἴ τις."
3. "ταῦτα viz., all the foregoing the ἀληθῆ &c., the ἀρετή, and the ἔπαινος these things meditate: let them be your νοήματα."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "True—sincere, in words. Honest—Old English for 'seemly,' namely, in action literally, grave, dignified."
2. "Pure—'chaste,' in relation to ourselves. Lovely—lovable. Of good report—referring to the absent, as 'lovely' refers to what is lovable face to face."
3. "Despise not anything that is good in itself; only let it keep its due place. Think on—have a continual regard to, so as to 'do' these things whenever the occasion arises."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They were to do everything that could be regarded as the fair subject of commendation, and that was implied in the highest moral character."
2. "A Christian should be just in all his dealings. His religion does not exempt him from the strict laws which bind people to the exercise of this virtue."
3. "The Christian should be a pattern and an example of every virtue."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "There lies in it the idea of a dignity or majesty which is yet inviting and attractive, and which inspires reverence."
2. "Ellicott's venerable is perhaps as near as any word, if venerable be divested of its modern conventional sense as implying age, and confined to its original sense, worthy of reverence."
3. "The kindred verb is commonly used in an active sense. Hence not well spoken of, but fairspeaking, and so winning, gracious (Rev., in margin)."

### John Calvin:
1. "He commends truth, which is nothing else than the integrity of a good conscience, with the fruits of it."
2. "Paul does not bid them try to gain applause or commendation by virtuous actions, but simply means that they should devote themselves to the performance of good works, which merit commendation."
3. "The word προσφιλὢ καὶ εὔφημα...among the Greeks, is employed, like cogitare among the Latins, to mean, meditate. Now meditation comes first, afterwards follows action."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Not merely in words only, but in thoughts and actions."
2. "Unspotted chasteness in the whole behaviour."
3. "Count up these things, he would say, for yourselves, and as you do so, try to cultivate the whole."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Without these no ideals can exist. They are pertinent now when so much filth is flaunted before the world in books, magazines and moving-pictures under the name of realism." 
2. "There are clean things, thoughts, words, deeds."
3. "We are responsible for our thoughts and can hold them to high and holy ideals."

### John Gill:
1. "The apostle exhorts to a regard to everything that is true; that is agreeable to the Scriptures of truth, to the Gospel the word of truth, or to the law and light of nature."
2. "In opposition to levity, frothiness, or foppery."
3. "Which are amiable in themselves, and to be found even among mere moral men, as in the young man whom Christ as man is said to love."

### John Wesley:
1. "Whatsoever things are true — Here are eight particulars placed in two fourfold rows; the former containing their duty; the latter, the commendation of it."
2. "Lovely — And what more lovely than truth? Of good report - As is honesty, even where it is not practised."
3. "Think on these things — That ye may both practise them yourselves, and recommend them to others."
