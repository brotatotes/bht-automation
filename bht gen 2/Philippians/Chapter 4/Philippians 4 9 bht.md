# Philippians 4:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Practicing what you have learned, received, heard, and seen is vital. It is not simply a matter of knowledge, but of fully embracing and applying these teachings. By doing so, the God of peace will be with you, providing not only the peace of God but His very presence, the source of peace itself.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "These general abstract things he now particularizes in the concrete."
2. "The καί expressly provides for their being of the kinds specified above."
3. "That λογισμός being eminently practical, and issuing, in the concrete, in the ταῦτα πράσσειν, after Paul’s example."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The things also which ye have learned . . . these practice."
2. "The things which besides recommending them in words, have been also recommended by my example, carry into practice."
3. "Not only 'the peace of God,' but 'the God of peace' Himself 'shall be with you.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "What you have witnessed in me, and what you have learned of me, and what you have heard about me, practice yourselves."
2. "It could have been only the consciousness of a pure and upright life which would make such counsel proper."
3. "The way to obtain the blessing of the God of peace, is to lead a holy life, and to perform with faithfulness all the duties which we owe to God and to our fellow-men."

### John Calvin:
1. "This was my doctrine - my instruction - my discourse among you."
2. "The main thing in a public speaker should be, that he may speak, not with his mouth merely, but by his life, and procure authority for his doctrine by rectitude of life."
3. "For the presence of God brings us every kind of blessing: as though he had said, that they would feel that God was present with them to make all things turn out well and prosperously, provided they apply themselves to pious and holy actions."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Knowing how much more telling example often is than precept, the apostle points to his own teaching and life as they had known them."
2. "He knows that his own pursuit of the high standard which he is setting before the Philippians has brought him peace, even amid the greatest afflictions, through the indwelling presence of God."
3. "And his constant feeling of joy in the Lord, even in his present chains, is a telling evidence that the God of peace is with him."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Paul dares to point to his life in Philippi as an illustration of this high thinking."
2. "The preacher is the interpreter of the spiritual life and should be an example of it."
3. "Practise as a habit."

### John Gill:
1. "These things which ye have both learned,.... Meaning from himself, in a doctrinal way: and received; not only into their heads but hearts, had embraced cordially, with great affection, in the love thereof, as well as given a full assent to."
2. "and heard; either publicly or privately, from the pulpit, or in conversation; or had heard of him when absent, or from him when present."
3. "and seen in me: in his life and conversation, which were well known, and were a pattern to them that believe."

### John Wesley:
1. "These do, and the God of peace shall be with you."
2. "Not only the peace of God, but God himself, the fountain of peace."
