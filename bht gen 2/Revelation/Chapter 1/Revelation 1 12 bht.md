# Revelation 1:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
When the voice spoke, the speaker turned around to see who was speaking, wanting to see the person behind the voice. In doing so, they beheld seven golden candlesticks, which represented the seven churches. These candlesticks, reminiscent of the lamps in the sanctuary, served the purpose of holding forth light. Just as the candlesticks spread light, the churches of Christ also serve as the bearers of the light of the Gospel, bringing illumination to both sinners and believers.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "And I turned about to see the voice which was speaking with me."
2. "When I had turned about I saw seven golden candlesticks."
3. "The seven golden candlesticks are part of the furniture of the tabernacle."
4. "Each local church has now its candlestick, to be retained or removed from its place according to its own works."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The voice is that of God the Father, as at Christ's baptism and transfiguration, so here in presenting Christ as our High Priest."
2. "The several churches are mutually independent as to external ceremonies and government, yet one in the unity of the Spirit and the Headship of Christ."
3. "The candlestick is not light, but the bearer of light, holding it forth to give light around. The light is the Lord's, not the Church's; from Him she receives it."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "To see the 'voice' here means to see the 'person' who spake." 
2. "The lamp-bearers evidently sustained each a light, and these gave a special brilliancy to the scene."
3. "Light is often used in the Scriptures as an emblem of true religion; Christians are represented as 'the light of the world'."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The Seer naturally turns to see; and the first thing that strikes his eyes as the outer circle of the vision is seven golden candlesticks."
2. "That we have seven candlesticks instead of one points to the richness and fulness of the New Testament Dispensation in its contrast with the Old."
3. "The idea that we have before us only one candlestick with seven branches is to be rejected as alike inconsistent with the language of St. John and with the symbolism of the book."
