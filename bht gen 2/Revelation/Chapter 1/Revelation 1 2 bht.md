### Henry Alford:
1. "The reasons given by Ebrard for such reference will not hold."
2. "But the usage is not thus confined, as every scholar knows."
3. "Taken then as representing the present book, τὸν λόγον here will be the aggregate of οἱ λόγοι Revelation 1:3 ; ἡ μαρτυρία Ἰης . χρ . will be the πνεῦμα τῆς προφητείας , embodied in writing for the Church in all ages)."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The word of God constitutes his testimony." 
2. "The testimony of Jesus—"the Spirit of prophecy."" 
3. "Translate, "whatsoever things he saw," in apposition with "the word of God and the testimony of Jesus Christ.""

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He regarded himself merely as a 'witness' of what he had seen, and claimed only to make a fair and faithful 'record' of it."
2. "The fair meaning of this expression would be, that John had borne faithful witness to, or testimony of, the truth which God had spoken to man in the gospel of Christ."
3. "He had always borne this testimony. He always regarded himself as such a witness... and he was now about to carry out the same characteristic of himself by bearing witness to what he saw in these new revelations."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The reference is to the present book and not to the Gospel. The aorist tense is the epistolary aorist."
2. "The words and the ideas are characteristic of Revelation as of the Gospel and Epistles."
3. "In Apoc. it is constantly used of the seeing of visions. Compare Revelation 1:19."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Individually St. John is nothing: he is only a witness to the Divine, to the word of God, and to the testimony given by Jesus Christ 'the Faithful Witness'."
2. "The verse as a whole is thus to be understood of the revelation of this book."
3. "The emphasis lies upon 'bare witness,' upon the attitude of the Seer rather than upon the things seen."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The prophetic word as in Revelation 1:9; Revelation 6:9; Revelation 20:4, not the personal Word as in Revelation 19:14." 
2. "Subjective genitive, given by God. The prophetic word as in Revelation 1:9; Revelation 6:9; Revelation 20:4, not the personal Word as in Revelation 19:14."
3. "Subjective genitive again, borne witness to by Jesus Christ."

### John Gill:
1. "Who bore record of the word of God... a clear evidence of his being the writer of it."
2. "The Gospel, which testifies of the person of Christ, of the truth of his divinity, and reality of his human nature."
3. "A faithful witness serves greatly to confirm the authority of this book, and to recommend the perusal of it."

### John Wesley:
1. "The word of God — Given directly by God."
2. "And the testimony of Jesus — Which he hath left us, as the faithful and true witness."
3. "Whatsoever things he saw — In such a manner as was a full confirmation of the divine original of this book."
