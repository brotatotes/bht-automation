# Revelation 1:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The glorious and visible coming of Jesus Christ is referred to in this passage. It emphasizes that all people, especially the Jews, will have the opportunity to see him. The response to his coming will differ depending on whether individuals accepted or rejected him. Those who believed in him will witness his splendor and find contentment, while those who denied him will experience fear and apprehension at his righteous judgment. This prophecy applies not only to the Jews but to all of humanity, highlighting that people's reception of Jesus will determine their reactions.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Clouds are the symbols of wrath to sinners."
2. "His coming shall therefore be a personal, visible appearing."
3. "Even the godly while rejoicing in His love shall feel penitential sorrow at their sins, which shall all be manifested at the general judgment."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The design of introducing this representation of the Saviour, and of the manner in which he would appear, seems to be to impress the mind with a sense of the majesty and glory."
2. "None can be indifferent to it, for all will see him. His friends will hail his advent, but all who were engaged in putting him to death, and all who in any manner have pierced his heart by sin and ingratitude, unless they shall have repented, will have occasion of bitter lamentation when he shall come."
3. "Surely people would not dread his appearing if they really believed that all will be saved."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The clouds are frequently used in the descriptions of the Lord's second coming."
2. "The verb denotes the physical act, but emphasizes the mental discernment accompanying it, and points to the result rather than to the act of vision."
3. "The expression here refers not to the Jews only, but to all who reject the Son of Man; those who 'in any age have identified themselves with the Spirit of the Savior's murderers.'"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Another great truth is connected with Him, that He will come again, to complete His victory, and to be acknowledged by all in His glory and His majesty."
2. "The eyes of all who, in any age and of any nation, have rejected His redemption."
3. "The 'tribes' of Israel are the figure by which God's believing people, whether Jew or Gentile, are represented. In like manner all unbelievers are now set before us as 'tribes,' the mocking counterpart of the true Israel of God."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
