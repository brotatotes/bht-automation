# Revelation 12:12 Commentary Help Text

Woe to the earth and the sea as this denunciation brings about the most grievous consequences. We have now reached a critical moment in time, where the end is quickly approaching. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Tabernacle" for dwell is used to mark that, though still on the earth, they in spirit are hidden "in the secret of God's tabernacle."
2. The more light becomes victorious, the greater will be the struggles of the powers of darkness; whence, at the last crisis, Antichrist will manifest himself with an intensity of iniquity greater than ever before.
3. Though no longer able to accuse the elect in heaven, [Satan] can tempt and persecute on earth.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Heaven itself was secure from any further rebellion or invasion, and the foundation was laid for a final victory over Satan everywhere."
2. "This is an instance of the sympathy of the heavenly inhabitants - the unfallen and holy beings before the throne - with the church on earth, and with all that may affect its welfare."
3. "Satan would ultimately be entirely overcome - a fact that was symbolized by his being cast out of heaven; but there would be still temporary war upon the earth, as if he were permitted to roam over the world for a time and to spread woe and sorrow there."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Dwell [σκηνουντες] . See on John 1:14. Compare ch. Revelation 7:15; Revelation 13:6; Revelation 21:3."
2. "To the inhabiters (toiv katoikousin). Omit. Read, as Rev., woe for the earth and for the sea."
3. "Wrath [θυμον] . See on John 3:36. Time [καιρον] . See on Matthew 12:1; Mark 1:15; Acts 1:7."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "For this reason" as in Revelation 7:15; Revelation 18:8 (15 times in John's Gospel, Charles notes). It points back to verse Revelation 12:10.
2. "The inhabitants of heaven (angels and saints) have cause to rejoice, and earth reason to mourn."
3. "The devil's departure from his warfare in the heavens reveals (ειδως, knowing, perfect active participle) to him that his time for doing harm to men is limited, and hence his great wrath (θυμον, boiling rage)."

### John Gill:
1. "the angels of heaven, who rejoice at every advance of Christ's kingdom and interest..."
2. "such as are of the earth, earthy, sensual, and earthly minded persons... the antichristian party, which quickly upon this sprung up, may be intended, on whom this woe is denounced..."
3. "a greater woe cannot be upon men on earth, than to have the devil among them, who always brings mischief with him, and breathes nothing but ruin and destruction to men..."

### John Wesley:
1. "Woe to the earth and the sea — This is the fourth and last denunciation of the third woe, the most grievous of all."
2. "We are now come to a most important period of time. The non - chronos hastens to an end."
3. "God has not given this prophecy, in so solemn a manner, only to show his providence over his church, but also that his servants may know at all times in what particular period they are."
