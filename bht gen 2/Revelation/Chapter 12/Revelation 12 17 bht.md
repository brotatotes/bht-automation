# Revelation 12:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The devil, infuriated by his failed attempts to destroy the church and her faithful followers, relentlessly wages war against those who keep the commandments of God and uphold the testimony of Jesus. These individuals are the spiritual offspring of the church, chosen to bear witness to Christ in times of corruption. They faithfully maintain the commandments and ordinances of the Gospel, even amidst the onslaught of the antichristian party.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Satan's first effort was to root out the Christian Church, so that there should be no visible profession of Christianity."
2. "Foiled in this, he wars against the invisible Church, namely, 'those who keep the commandments of God, and have the testimony of Jesus.'"
3. "The Church, in her beauty and unity, is now not manifested, but awaiting the manifestations of the sons of God at Christ's coming."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The church, as such, he could not destroy; therefore he turned his wrath against individual Christians, to bring as many of them as possible to death."
2. "This warfare against individual Christians was continued long in the papal church, and tens of thousands of true friends of the Saviour suffered every form of cruelty and wrong as the result."
3. "The scene, then, in this chapter is this: John saw a most beautiful woman, suitably adorned, representing the church as about to he enlarged, and to become triumphant in the earth."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The best texts add to this chapter the opening words of ch. 13 (A. V.), 'And I stood upon the sand of the sea.'"
2. "Some, however, change ejstaqhn I stood, to ejstaqh he stood, referring to the dragon."
3. "So Rev."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The rest of her seed, as appears from the immediately following description of their character, can only mean that portion of the woman’s seed which remained faithful to its trust."
2. "We have here...a separation between the Church as a whole and a part of the members, which prepare us for the further manifestation of this mystery in later chapters of the book."
3. "The dragon took his stand upon the sand of the sea, upon the margin of that ‘earth’ and ‘sea’ in which he finds his prey."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The devil carries on relentless war with all those 'which keep the commandments of God and hold the testimony of Jesus'." 
2. "These two marks excite the wrath of the devil then and always."

### John Gill:
1. "The devil was very angry with the church... because he could not destroy her... and because he could not carry her away with the flood... and because he could not... come at her... where she was taken care of." 
2. "The persons with whom he went to make war are described as 'her seed'; the seed of the church, her spiritual offspring... and who were called out to bear a testimony for Christ in corrupt times." 
3. "These are further described as those who keep the commandments of God... the ordinances of the Gospel... which were kept by these faithful ones... when they began now to be sadly corrupted by the antichristian party... and have the testimony of Jesus Christ."
