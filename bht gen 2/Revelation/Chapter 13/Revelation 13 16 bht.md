# Revelation 13:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The mark described in the passage represents a mark of submission and loyalty to the beast's authority. It is not limited to a specific group, but encompasses individuals of various ranks and conditions. Some interpretations suggest that the mark could resemble brands used on slaves or soldiers under a particular leader. Others connect it to the profession of faith and service. While the exact nature of the mark is not specified, it serves as a clear symbol of allegiance to the beast. Those who bear this mark demonstrate their complete devotion to the beast's dominion.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Soldiers voluntarily punctured their arms with marks of the general under whom they served."
2. "The mark in the right hand and forehead implies the prostration of bodily and intellectual powers to the beast's domination."
3. "In the forehead by way of profession; in the hand with respect to work and service."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He claims jurisdiction, in the matters here referred to, over all classes of persons, and compels them to do his will." 
2. "The idea is, that, in the matter referred to, none were exempt, either on account of their exalted rank, or on account of their humble condition; either because they were so mighty as to be beyond control, or so mean and humble as to be beneath notice."
3. "Applied to the papacy, the meaning is, that there would be some mark of distinction; some indelible sign; something which would designate, with entire certainty, those persons who belonged to it, and who were subject to it."
