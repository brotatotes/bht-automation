# Revelation 14:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
These chosen individuals were not led astray from their faith by the temptations of idolatry and immoral practices. They are the faithful followers of the Lamb, emulating His example and adhering to His teachings. Their dedication to purity and righteousness has resulted in them being rewarded with a special closeness to Christ. They are like firstfruits, representing a special portion of the harvest, redeemed and transformed souls who will reign with Christ in glory.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Their not being defiled with women means they were not led astray from Christian faithfulness by the tempters who jointly constitute the spiritual 'harlot'." 
2. "Follow the Lamb whithersoever he goeth - in glory, being especially near His person; the fitting reward of their following Him so fully on earth."
3. "The Jewish and Gentile elect Church is the harvest; in a further sense, the whole of the transfigured and translated Church which reigns with Christ at His coming, is the first-fruit, and the consequent general ingathering of Israel and the nations, ending in the last judgment, is the full and final harvest."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The general idea is, that they are chaste; that they are the followers of the Lamb; that they are redeemed from among people; and that they are without guile."
2. "On no point are there more frequent exhortations in the Scriptures than on this; on no point is there more solicitude manifested that the professed friends of the Saviour should be without blame."
3. "This is another characteristic of those who are redeemed - that they are followers of the Lamb of God. That is, they are his disciples; they imitate his example; they obey his instructions; they yield to his laws; they receive him as their counselor and their guide."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The verb means properly to besmear or besmirch, and is never used in a good sense."
2. "Virgins: Either celibate or living in chastity whether in married or single life."
3. "First-fruits."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The use of this word rules out marriage, which was not considered sinful." 
2. "The Christian life is following the Lamb of God as Jesus taught." 
3. "This seems to mean that the 144,000 represent not the whole, but only a portion of the great harvest to come."

### John Gill:
1. "These are they which were not defiled with women,.... With the whore of Rome, and her harlots... these were free from... idolatrous practices." 
2. "These follow Christ in the exercise of the graces of humility, patience, and love; and in the performance of the several duties of religion, and subjection to ordinances, and in the path of sufferings; and in every way in which Christ the Lamb has gone before them."
3. "They follow him to heaven, where he is... they were called, and delivered from this present evil world, and the men of it, and from a vain, wicked, and idolatrous conversation with it."

### John Wesley:
1. "It seems that the deepest defilement, and the most alluring temptation, is put for every other."
2. "Unspotted souls; such as have preserved universal purity."
3. "Who are nearest to him. This is not their character, but their reward Firstfruits - Of the glorified spirits."
