### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "I heard the altar [personified] saying." 
2. "On it the prayers of saints are presented before God: beneath it are the souls of the martyrs crying for vengeance on the foes of God."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "True and righteous are thy judgments - Responding to what is said in Revelation 16:5... these judgments had come upon those who had shed the blood of the martyrs, and they were just." (28 words)
2. "These scenes followed closely on the French revolution... These wars occurred in regions under the influence of the papacy, for these were all papal states and territories." (28 words)
3. "One has only to look upon a map of Northern Italy to see that there is no other portion of the world which would more naturally be suggested when speaking of a country abounding in 'rivers and fountains of water.'" (32 words)

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "I heard the altar."
2. "The souls of the martyrs are seen under the altar and cry how long."
3. "Add the article: the Almighty."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Not only is an acknowledgment of the righteousness of God’s judgment given by the ‘waters;’ the martyred saints also respond."
2. "They who have suffered own that the judgments of the Almighty upon those who persecuted them even unto death are true and righteous."
3. "Conformable to the realities of things and to the demands of perfect righteousness."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "O Lord God, the Almighty" (Κυριε ο θεος ο παντοκρατωρ). Just as in Revelation 15:3 in the Song of Moses and of the Lamb, vocative with the article ο. 
2. "Judgments" (κρισεις) here instead of "ways" (οδο) there, and with the order of the adjectives reversed (αληθινα κα δικαια, true and righteous).

### John Gill:
1. "I heard another out of the altar say,.... That is, another angel that came out of the altar... represents the souls under the altar, whose blood had been shed by the above persons..."
2. "...even so, Lord God Almighty, true and righteous are thy judgments; the same as in Revelation 15:3. This angel joins the other, and approves and confirms what he says; applauding the judgements of Christ upon the worshippers of the beast, from the verity of them, being what were threatened, and from the justice of them, being what they deserved."

### John Wesley:
1. Yea — Answering the angel of the waters, and affirming of God's judgments in general, what he had said of one particular judgment.
