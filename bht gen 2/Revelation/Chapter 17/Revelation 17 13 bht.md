# Revelation 17:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The different kingdoms and their people, over time, align themselves with the same religious beliefs, forming a united front. This alliance leads to the granting of power to exercise ecclesiastical authority within their domains. Additionally, they contribute their riches and wealth, which are considered sources of strength, to further support their shared cause.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "one mind—one sentiment."
2. "They become his dependent allies."
3. "the true KING OF KINGS appears and dashes him down in a moment to destruction."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "These have one mind - That is, they are united in the promotion of the same object."
2. "Shall lend their influence to the support of the papacy, and become the upholders of that power."
3. "All 'their power and strength was given unto the beast'; all was made subservient to the purposes of papal Rome."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Mind [γνωμην] . Meaning primarily the faculty of knowing, mind, reason; then that which is thought or known; opinion, purpose."
2. "Shall give [διαδιδωσουσιν] . didoasin, the present tense, give. The force of dia is over; give over."
3. "Power and authority [δυναμιν και εξουσιαν] . For the distinction, see on 2 Peter 2:11."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "These have one mind, and they give their power and authority unto the beast."
2. "The brute power of the world could of itself effect nothing were it not served by the spiritual forces of the false prophet."
3. "The harlot's witcheries have drawn kings who now give their power and authority to the beast."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Have one mind" - "One purpose" (Acts 20:3; 1 Corinthians 1:10).
2. "Give their power and authority unto the beast" - "Present active indicative of διδωμ."

### John Gill:
1. "The princes of these kingdoms, and their subjects, become, in time, of one religion."
2. "They have given power to exercise all ecclesiastical authority in their kingdoms."
3. "They have given their riches and wealth, which are called the forces of strength."
