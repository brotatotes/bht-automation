## BHT:
I saw another angel coming down out of heaven, distinct from the previous one, to bring a new message. The earth was illuminated with his glory, signifying the significance and impact of his task. The brightness of this angel's glory gives us a glimpse of the splendor of the Lord Himself, who has innumerable glorious attendants serving and worshiping Him.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "After the vision referred to in the previous chapter.I saw another angel come down from heaven - Different from the one that had last appeared, and therefore coming to make a new communication to him."
2. "It is not unusual in this book that different communications should be entrusted to different angels."
3. "And the earth was lightened with his glory - The usual representation respecting the heavenly beings."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Was lightened. Compare Ezekiel 43:2.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The earth was lightened with his glory."
2. "They illustrate the greatness of his mission and the manner in which the whole 'earth' shall be struck with its glorious accomplishment."
3. "This angel has a closer than ordinary connection with the Lord Himself."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Coming down out of heaven... one announcing the doom of Babylon (Rome)."
2. "He flings a broad belt of light across the dark earth."

### John Gill:
1. "This likewise expresses the truth of their message, since both that and they come from heaven."
2. "Having great power; to do great work, to declare the fall of Babylon the great."
3. "The earth was lightened with his glory; see Ezekiel 43:2 by which is meant the glorious Gospel of Christ, the light of which will at this then be very great."

### John Wesley:
1. "And I saw another angel coming down out of heaven — Termed another, with respect to him who 'came down out of heaven,' Revelation 10:1."
2. "And the earth was enlightened with his glory — To make his coming more conspicuous."
3. "If such be the lustre of the servant, what images can display the majesty of the Lord, who has 'thousand thousands' of those glorious attendants 'ministering to him, and ten thousand times ten thousand standing before him?'"
