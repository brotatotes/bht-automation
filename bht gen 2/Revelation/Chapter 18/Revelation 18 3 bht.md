# Revelation 18:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The downfall of the city is caused by its influence and corruption over all nations, leading them astray and into sin. The merchants, who engage in extensive traffic and commerce, play a significant role in the city's demise. The kings of the earth, both endorsing and participating in her idolatrous practices, also contribute to her downfall. Additionally, the city is characterized by the sale of indulgences and spiritual wares, exploiting people's ignorance for monetary gain.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "But A, B, and C read, '(owing to the wrath of her fornication all nations) have fallen.'"
2. "Babylon, the whore, is destroyed before the beast slays the two witnesses, and then the beast himself is destroyed."
3. "The reference is not to earthly merchandise, but to spiritual wares, indulgences, idolatries, superstitions, worldly compromises, wherewith the harlot, that is, the apostate Church, has made merchandise of men."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "She had beguiled and corrupted the nations of the earth, leading them into estrangement from God, and into pollution and sin."
2. "She has been the means of seducing them from God and leading them into sinful practices."
3. "The reference is to such luxuries as are found commonly in a great, a frivolous, and a splendid city. These, of course, give rise to much traffic, and furnish employment to many merchants and sailors, who thus procure a livelihood, or become wealthy as the result of such traffic."

3. "Merchants [εμποροι]. The word originally means one on a journey by sea or land, especially for traffic."

### Philip Schaff:

1. "As a result of."
