# Revelation 19:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The passage unveils the magnificent sight of the heavenly armies, made up of pure and holy creatures, following Christ on white horses in his triumphant return. This includes both angels and saints who have overcome through Christ. They join him to witness his victory and to add to the splendor of this glorious event.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The glorified saints whom God 'will bring with' Christ at His advent; compare Revelation 17:14, 'they that are with Him, called, chosen, faithful'; as also 'His mighty angels.'" 
2. "White and clean—Greek, 'pure.' A, B, Vulgate, Syriac, and CYPRIAN omit 'and,' which ORIGEN and ANDREAS retain, as English Version."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The heavenly hosts; particularly, it would seem, the redeemed, as there would be some incongruity in representing the angels as riding in this manner."
2. "These hosts of the redeemed on white horses accompany him to be witnesses of his victory, and to participate in the joy of the triumph, not to engage in the work of blood."
3. "Emblems of triumph or victory."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Followed [ηκολουθει] . Note the imperfect tense denoting progression, and thus describing the advancing movement of the host.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "These armies comprise in all probability both the angels and the saints." 
2. "All triumph with their triumphant Head and King." 
3. "They are ‘willing in the day that He warreth’."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The armies which are in heaven (τα στρατευματα τα εν τω ουρανω)."
2. "Followed (ηκολουθε)."
3. "Imperfect active and singular (στρατευματα, neuter plural) of ακολουθεω, graphic picture of the celestial Warrior with his angelic hosts 'upon white horses' (εφ' ιπποις λευκοις) like the Leader and, like him 'clothed in fine linen white and pure' (ενδεδυμενο βυσσινον λευκον καθαρον) like the Leader again (Revelation 19:8)."
4. "Note ενδεδυμενο here as in Revelation 1:13; Revelation 15:6."

### John Gill:
1. "And the armies which were in heaven...they are pure and holy creatures, and will come with Christ to judgment." 
2. "The saints are in a state of warfare...they are very numerous, and always more than conquerors through Christ."
3. "These followed him...to add to the glorious and triumphant appearance of their General."
