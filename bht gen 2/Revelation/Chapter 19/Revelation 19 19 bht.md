# Revelation 19:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The kings of the earth, both the antichristian civil powers and those who are in alliance with them, gather together to make war with the true Messiah, who sits upon the horse. This war is not limited to earthly forces, but includes all beings, both visible and invisible. It is a grand and decisive contest, involving the true religion against the forces of evil. This battle is foretold in various passages of scripture, including Zechariah 14:1.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "gathered together at Armageddon, under the sixth vial."
2. "For 'their armies' in B and ANDREAS, there is found 'His armies' in A."
3. "But A and B read 'the war,' namely, that foretold, Revelation 16:14; Revelation 17:4."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "There can be no doubt that the writer in these passages designed to refer to the same events - the still future scenes that are to occur when the Roman, the pagan, and the Muhammedan powers shall be aroused to make common cause against the true religion, and shall stake all on the issue of the great conflict." 
2. "Against him that sat on the horse - The Messiah - the Son of God."
3. "And against his army - The hosts that are associated with him - his redeemed people."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The ‘war’ is that of chap. Revelation 16:14."
2. "The thought of the unity of the one compared with the inner dissensions of the other lies at the bottom of the change (comp. chap. Revelation 11:8)."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Gathered together (συνηγμενα). Perfect passive participle of συναγω. In battle array."
2. "To make war against (ποιησα πολεμον μετα). First aorist active infinitive of ποιεω, to express purpose."
3. "The beast (for his army see Revelation 16:13) led a league of ten kings against Babylon in Revelation 17:16, but with the purpose also of fighting the Lamb (Revelation 17:14)."

### John Gill:
1. "The beast, ... the antichristian civil powers, or antichrist in his civil capacity..."
2. "The kings of the earth; these, as they stand distinguished from the beast, or the antichristian kings, and civil states..."
3. "and against his army, who, though unarmed, and only clothed in fine linen, have nothing to fear, since Christ, the Captain of their salvation, is at the head of them."

### John Wesley:
1. "And I saw the kings of the earth — The ten kings mentioned Revelation 17:12; who had now drawn the other kings of the earth to them, whether Popish, Mahometan, or pagan."
2. "Gathered together to make war with him that sat upon the horse — All beings, good and evil, visible and invisible, will be concerned in this grand contest."
3. "See Zechariah 14:1, etc."
