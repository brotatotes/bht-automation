# Revelation 19:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The destruction of Babylon is described as rising up a second time, indicating the importance and significance of this event. The smoke that ascends from the city represents its final ruin, referencing the destruction of Sodom and Gomorrah. This destruction brings joy to the righteous, as seen in the expressions of praise and celebration. Moreover, the everlasting nature of the destruction signifies the eternal punishment that awaits the enemies of God, serving as a warning and a symbol of the final judgment.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The event was so glorious and so important; the final destruction of the great enemy of the church was of so much moment in its bearing on the welfare of the world, as to call forth repeated expressions of praise."
2. "This is an image of final ruin; the image being derived probably from the description in Genesis of the smoke that ascended from the cities of the plain, Genesis 19:28."
3. "On the joy expressed here in her destruction, compare the notes on Revelation 18:20."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Her smoke, etc. Compare Isaiah 34:10.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The thought of a ‘second’ time has peculiar importance in the eyes of St. John (comp. John 4:54). It confirms with a singular degree of emphasis the idea with which it is connected."
2. "The fate of the first city out of which God’s people were called turns out to have been a prophecy of the fate of the last."
3. "But it was more tolerable for Sodom than it will be for Babylon; for...its fires were quenched in the waters of the Dead Sea. This fire goes up ‘for ever and ever’ (comp. Isaiah 66:24)."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "A second time (δευτερον). Adverbial accusative, a heavenly encore."
2. "The smoke of the city's ruin (Revelation 14:11; Revelation 18:8; Revelation 18:18) instead of incense (Revelation 8:4)."
3. "A last touch to the description already given (Revelation 18:21) of Babylon's utter collapse."

### John Gill:
1. "The repeating of the word shows how hearty, earnest, and constant they were in the work of praise on this account."
2. "It looks as if Rome, like another Sodom and Gomorrah, would sink into a sulphurous burning lake, and continue so."
3. "Respect is had to the everlasting punishment of antichrist and his followers in hell, and to the everlasting burnings that will follow Rome's temporal destruction, which was an example and symbol of the vengeance of eternal fire."
