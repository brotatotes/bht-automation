# Revelation 19:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
A voice came from the throne, calling on all to praise our God, not only for His judgment on the great whore, but also for Him taking the kingdom and avenging Himself on His enemies. This shows that there is real and even terrible anger in God, as confirmed by the inhabitants of heaven who joined in praising Him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "out from the throne"
2. "Praise our God"
3. "the small and the great."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It would seem rather that it was a responsive voice that came from those nearest the throne, calling on all to unite in praising God in view of what was done."
2. "The meaning then will be, that all heaven was interested in the triumph of the church, and that one portion of the dwellers there called on the others to unite in offering thanksgiving."
3. "All in heaven and earth; all have occasion for thankfulness."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. All ye His servants - small and great. Compare Psalms 114:13; Psalms 134:1.

### Philip Schaff:
1. Revelation 19:5. A voice is next heard from the throne calling upon all God’s people to  give praise  to Him.  The voice is immediately answered.
	

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "A voice from the throne. Not the voice of God, nor of the Lamb, nor from the temple, but from an angel of the Presence."
2. "This angel summons all the servants of God to join in the antiphonal praise to God."
3. "Give praise to our God. Present active imperative of αινεω, old verb, with the accusative elsewhere in N.T., but here with the dative as occasionally in the LXX."

### John Gill:
1. "Praise our God, all ye his servants;... all the people of God;... called upon, as a part of their service, to say hallelujah, or to sing the praises of God for his judgments on antichrist."
2. "And ye that fear him, both small and great;... whether greater or lesser believers, fathers, young men, or children; whether Jews or Gentiles, or of whatsoever nation, kindred, or people."
3. "Though they are sons, and no more servants to sin and Satan, and the world, yet they are servants of God and of righteousness, and serve him willingly and cheerfully in a way of duty, and without slavish fear, and with a godly one, and from principles of love and gratitude, and without mercenary views and selfish ends."

### John Wesley:
1. "And a voice came forth from the throne... saying, Praise our God." 
2. "God was praised before, for his judgment of the great whore. Now for that which follows it: for that the Lord God, the Almighty, takes the kingdom to himself, and avenges himself on the rest of his enemies." 
3. "Were all these inhabitants of heaven mistaken? If not, there is real, yea, and terrible anger in God."
