# Revelation 2:18 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Behold the greatness of the Son of Man, who is truly the Son of God, possessing divine nature and perfection. His eyes, like a flame of fire, see through the darkness and expose the wickedness of mankind. His feet, like fine brass, signify his immense strength. He is the one who searches the hearts and minds of individuals, revealing the truth and executing divine justice.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The title 'Son of God,' is from Psalms 2:7;
	Psalms 2:9, which is referred to in
	Revelation 2:27."
2. "The attribute, 'eyes
	like a flame,' &c., answers to Revelation 2:27- :, 'I am He which searcheth the reins and hearts.'"
3. "The attribute, 'feet like . . . brass,' answers to Revelation 2:27- :, 'as the vessels of a potter shall they be broken to
	shivers,' He treading them to pieces with His
	strong feet."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Perhaps the severity of the rebuke contemplated here made it proper that there should be a more impressive reference to the authority of the speaker..."
2. "Before the glance of his eye all is light, and nothing can be concealed from his view."
3. "Perhaps indicative of majesty and glory as he walked in the midst of the churches."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "It is interesting to notice in connection with Acts 16:14, though it does not concern us at present, that Thyatira was famous for its purple or scarlet dyes."
2. "For the description now given of Himself by the great Head of the Church, cp. chap. Revelation 1:14-15. The most remarkable part of it is that in which He designates Himself the Son of God."
3. "Perhaps also the distinct phase of the Church upon which we enter in the second group of these Epistles may explain the prominence given to the thought of the ‘Son of God.’"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "In Thyatira, a centre of trade, especially for the royal purple, home of Lydia of Philippi, shown by inscriptions to be full of trade guilds.".
2. "The Son of God. Here Jesus is represented as calling himself by this title as in John 11:4 and as he affirms on oath in Matthew 26:63.".
3. "His eyes like a flame of fire. As in Revelation 1:14. His feet like burnished brass. As in Revelation 1:15.".

### John Gill:
1. "Seleucus, the son of Nicanor, being at war with Lysimachus, and hearing that he had a daughter born, called this city Thygatira, which was before called Pelopia, and Semiramis; which is a very fit name for this church, and expresses the effeminacy of it." 
2. "These things saith the Son of God; he who is truly, properly, naturally, and essentially the Son of God: this character Christ makes use of to assert his proper deity, as being of the same nature, and having the same perfections with his Father."
3. "Who hath his eyes like unto a flame of fire; quick and sharp, and penetrating through the darkness of this state; seeing into, discovering, and exposing the horrid actions and wickednesses of men done in the dark; expressing fury, wrath, and vengeance against the Romish antichrist and his followers."

### John Wesley:
1. "See how great he is, who appeared 'like a son of man!'"
2. "Who hath eyes as a flame of fire - 'Searching the reins and the heart.'"
3. "And feet like fine brass - Denoting his immense strength."
