# Revelation 2:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
He that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith to the churches. This call to attentive listening is found throughout the letters to the churches. It is a reminder that everyone, regardless of their spiritual state, should carefully heed these words. In Revelation, the promise to eat of the tree of life symbolizes participation in eternal life, reflecting the ultimate restoration of all things. The mention of the paradise of God evokes images of a heavenly garden of delight, where the redeemed dwell in joy and felicity, in the presence of God Himself.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "He that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith to the churches."
2. "To him that conquereth, I will give to him to eat of the tree of life, which is in the paradise of my God."
3. "The two former words as following παραδείσῳ, come from Ezekiel 28:13, and set forth the holiness and glory of that paradise as consisting in God’s dwelling and delighting in it."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "He that hath an ear—This clause precedes the promise in the first three addresses, succeeds it in the last four. Thus the promises are enclosed on both sides with the precept urging the deepest attention as to the most momentous truths."
2. "The tree of life in Paradise, lost by the fall, is restored by the Redeemer."
3. "The main bliss of Paradise is that it is the Paradise of God; God Himself dwelling there."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The language addressed to the church of Smyrna is throughout that of commiseration and comfort."
2. "It was a very ancient city; but having been destroyed by the Lydians, it lay waste four hundred years to the time of Alexander the Great."
3. "There are in it about 20,000 Greeks, 8,000 Armenians, 1,000 Europeans, and 9,000 Jews. It is now the seat of important missionary operations in the East, and much has been done there to spread the gospel in modern times."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "To eat of the tree of life expresses participation in the life eternal."
2. "The revival of the symbol in Apoc. is in accordance with the theme of the restitution of all things."
3. "Paradeisov Paradise 'passes through a series of meanings, each one higher than the last. From any garden of delight, which is its first meaning, it comes to be predominantly applied to the garden of Eden, then to the resting - place of separate souls in joy and felicity, and lastly to the very heaven itself."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith unto the churches."
2. "To him that overcometh. The expression is a characteristic one with St. John."
3. "I will give to him to eat out of the tree of fife, which is in the paradise of God."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He that hath an ear... a reminiscence of the words of Jesus in the Synoptics." 
2. "The Spirit of Christ in the prophet is the interpreter of Christ's voice."
3. "This tree is now in the Garden of God... the home of the redeemed with Christ, not a mere intermediate state."

### John Gill:
1. "Let him hear what the Spirit saith unto the churches; let such hearken, and listen with attention to what is said by the Spirit."
2. "It may be concluded from hence, that there are in this epistle, and so in all the rest, ... some things which are parabolical and prophetic, and not obvious to everyone's understanding and view."
3. "By which is meant Jesus Christ himself, in allusion to the tree of life in the garden of Eden; and is so called, because he is the author of life, natural, spiritual, and eternal."

### John Wesley:
1. "He that hath an ear, let him hear — Every man, whoever can hear at all, ought carefully to hear this."
2. "To eat of the tree of life — The first thing promised in these letters is the last and highest in the accomplishment, Revelation 22:2,14,19."
3. "In the paradise of my God — The word paradise means a garden of pleasure. In the earthly paradise there was one tree of life: there are no other trees in the paradise of God."
