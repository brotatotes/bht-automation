# Revelation 21:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The placement of the tribes and gates in the city symbolizes universal access to the kingdom: people from all corners of the earth will have the opportunity to enter. The distribution of the gates on the east, north, south, and west demonstrates the gathering of saints from every part of the world to sit down in the kingdom of God. This arrangement signifies the inclusivity and universality of God's plan for redemption and salvation.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Joseph, Benjamin, Dan (for which Manasseh is substituted in Revelation 7:6), are on the east."
2. "Reuben, Judah, Levi, are on the north."
3. "Simeon, Issachar, Zebulun, on the south."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "There does not appear to be any special significancy in this fact, unless it be to denote that there is access to this city from all quarters of the world."
2. "...and that they who dwell there will have come from each of the great divisions of the earth - that is, from every land."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. East [ανατολης] . See on Matthew 2:2, and on day - spring, Luke 1:78. See the tribes arranged by gates in Ezekiel 48:31-34. West [δυσμων] . Lit., the goings down or settings.

### Philip Schaff:
1. Revelation 21:13. The distribution of the gates follows in this verse.
	

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Three gates on each of the four sides as in Ezekiel 42:16" 
2. "Starting from the east, from the north, from the south, from the west"

### John Gill:
1. "This denotes the collection of the saints from all parts of the world, who shall come from east, west, north, and south, and sit down in the kingdom of God."
