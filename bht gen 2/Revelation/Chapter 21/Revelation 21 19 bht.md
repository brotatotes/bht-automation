# Revelation 21:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The radiant and superb structure in question is adorned with various precious stones, emphasizing their beauty, worth, and durability akin to the foundation of Christ himself. These stones bear a resemblance to the ones on the high priest's breastplate, symbolizing the happiness of God's church and the perfect glory of the city's inhabitants. Each stone serves as more than just an ornament; it is an integral part of the foundation, highlighting their visible and prominent role.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "These precious stones constituted the 'foundations.'"
2. "Chalcedony—agate from Chalcedon: semi-opaque, sky-blue, with stripes of other colors."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The foundations were composed of precious stones, giving them this highly ornamented and brilliant appearance."
2. "Sapphire is a precious stone, next in hardness to the diamond, usually of an azure or sky-blue color, but of various shades."
3. "The chalcedony is an uncrystallized translucent variety of quartz, having a whitish color, and of a luster nearly like wax."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Before the true precious stones were introduced from India, the lapis lazuli held the highest place in the estimation of the primitive nations of Asia and Greece; in fact it was almost the only stone known to them having beauty of color to recommend it."
2. "Chalcedony... was probably an inferior species of emerald, as crystal of carbonate of copper, which is still popularly called 'the copper emerald.'"
3. "Pliny describes [emerald] as small and brittle, changing its color when moved about, like the green feathers in the necks of peacocks and pigeons."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The writer is simply trying to convey the impression of a radiant and superb structure."
2. "The twelve gems do correspond closely (only eight in common) with the twelve stones on the high priest's breastplate."
3. "These foundation stones are visible."

### John Gill:
1. "They set forth the worth and preciousness of Christ, who is the pearl of great price; the pleasure and delight had in viewing his excellencies and perfections; his brightness, purity, and glory, and his firmness and durableness, as a foundation."
2. "There may be some allusion to the twelve stones in the breastplate of the high priest, on which the names of the tribes of Israel were written."
3. "The best of this sort are the eastern ones."


### John Wesley:
1. "The precious stones on the high priest's breastplate of judgment were a proper emblem to express the happiness of God's church in his presence with them, and in the blessing of his protection."
2. "The like ornaments on the foundations of the walls of this city may express the perfect glory and happiness of all the inhabitants of it from the most glorious presence and protection of God."
3. "Each precious stone was not the ornament of the foundation, but the foundation itself."
