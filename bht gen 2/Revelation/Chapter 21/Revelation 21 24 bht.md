# Revelation 21:24 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The nations, representing the saved, will walk in the glorious light of the city. Among them, the kings of the earth bring their new and fitting glory into it. This glory is not their former earthly splendor, but something greater and befitting the city.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Her brightness shall supply them with light."
2. "The kings of the earth...now in the new Jerusalem do bring their glory into it, to lay it down at the feet of their God and Lord."
3. "And honor."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "And the nations of them which are saved ... Shall walk in the light of it - Shall enjoy its splendor, and be continually in its light."
2. "And the kings of the earth do bring their glory and honour into it - All that they consider as constituting their glory, treasures, crowns, scepters, robes."
3. "The idea is, that all these will be devoted to God in the future days of the church in its glory, and will be, as it were, brought and laid down at the feet of the Saviour in heaven."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Of them which are saved."
2. "In the light [...] amidst the light" or "by the light."
3. "Do bring [...] the present tense, denoting habit."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The 'peoples' of Revelation 21:3 are the 'nations' of this verse and of chap. Revelation 22:2; and the choice of the different expressions is probably determined by the consideration that in the one God is thought of as 'tabernacling' in the midst of His people, in the other as being His people's 'light' (comp. note on chap. Revelation 1:20, where we have a remarkable parallel both in thought and structure)." 
2. "The 'nations' are not converted heathen alone, but all who, whether Jew or Gentile, walk in the light."
3. "Not the masses of the nations only, but their highest representatives and dignitaries submit themselves with all that they have to the sway of Him who now rules in righteousness, the universal King."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "All the moral and spiritual progress of moderns is due to Christ."
2. "The nations of earth will be represented, including 'the kings', mentioned also in Isaiah 60:3."
3. "The picture is beautiful and glorious even if not realized here, but only in heaven."

### John Gill:
1. "These are not all the nations of the world, which will be gathered before Christ, the Judge, for all will not be saved, there is a world that will be condemned..."
2. "...but all the elect of God, both Jews and Gentiles, whom God has chosen, Christ has redeemed, and the Spirit has called out of all the nations of the earth: these are only saved ones; such are already saved, not only are determined to be saved, and for whom salvation is wrought out by Christ..."
3. "...but these shall walk by sight, enjoying the beatific vision of God, and the Lamb."

### John Wesley:
1. "Shall walk by the light thereof - Which throws itself outward from the city far and near."
2. "And the kings of the earth - Those of them who have a part there."
3. "Bring their glory into it - Not their old glory, which is now abolished; but such as becomes the new earth, and receives an immense addition by their entrance into the city."
