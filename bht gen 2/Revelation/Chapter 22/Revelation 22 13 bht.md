# Revelation 22:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
These statements affirm the profound significance of Jesus being the Alpha and the Omega, the beginning and the end. They highlight the divine attributes of Christ, emphasizing His eternal existence and His role in the entire plan of salvation. As the Creator of the universe and the One who will bring about the consummation of redemption, His deity and glory are unequivocally demonstrated. This declaration solidifies His position as the Lord who transcends time and is deserving of our utmost reverence and worship.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. ". . . the Alpha and the Omega." A, B, Vulgate, Syriac, ORIGEN, and CYPRIAN transpose thus, "the First and the Last, the Beginning and the End."
2. Compare with these divine titles assumed here by the Lord Jesus, Revelation 1:8; Revelation 1:17; Revelation 21:6. At the winding up of the whole scheme of revelation He announces Himself as the One before whom and after whom there is no God.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He originated the whole plan of salvation, and he will determine its close; he formed the world, and he will wind up its affairs."
2. "In the beginning, the continuance, and the end, he will be recognized as the same being presiding over and controlling all."

### Philip Schaff:
1. These words confirm the statement made in the previous verse (comp. chap. Revelation 21:6).
2. They take us back also to chap. Revelation 1:8.

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "I am the Alpha and the Omega...crowning proof in this book of Christ's deity." 
2. "Solemn assurance is thus given that Christ is qualified to be the Judge."
3. "Christ was the Creator of the universe...now he is the Consummation of redemption."

### John Gill:
1. "These characters are all put together here, which are before used in Revelation 1:8 and are very pertinently mentioned in this place, when all promises and prophecies, relating to the glorious kingdom of Christ, were just finishing, and that itself was ready to appear..."
2. "...in which Christ alone should be exalted, the mystery of God would be completed, and time itself be no more."
3. "A testimony this, of the deity, eternity, infinity, and perfection of Christ."

### John Wesley:
1. "I am the Alpha and the Omega, the first and the last — Who exist from everlasting to everlasting." 
2. "How clear, incontestable a proof, does our Lord here give of his divine glory!"
