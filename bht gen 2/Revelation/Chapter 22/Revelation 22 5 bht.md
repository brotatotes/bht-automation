# Revelation 22:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In the future, there will be no more night. This blessed state is free from the consequences of sin, with no tears, sorrow, death, or pain. Rest and light from lamps or the sun are unnecessary because the Lord Himself is their everlasting, unfading light. The saints will reign with Christ forever, sharing in His divine authority without interruption. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "There shall be no night any longer."
2. "They shall not have need."
3. "Reign with a glory probably transcending that of their reign in heaven with Christ."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It seems to be proper, therefore, to pause on it for a moment, and to state in a summary manner what will be the principal features of that blessedness." 
2. "That future state will be entirely and forever free from all the consequences of the apostasy as now seen on the earth. There will be neither tears, nor sorrow, nor death, nor crying, nor pain, nor curse." 
3. "All this will be eternal. The universe, so vast and so wonderful, seems to have been made to be suited to the eternal contemplation of created minds."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. No night there [εκει] . Substitute eti any more. Rev., there shall be night no more.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Having entered, they shall suffer no interruption in their joyful service, and shall need no nightly rest to recruit the weary frame for the service of the following day."
2. "He who is 'without variableness or shadow cast by turning' is now their light, and that light never fades."
3. "They share the Divine authority over all things around them, and their authority is without interruption and without end. They reign 'for ever and ever.'"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They need (εχουσιν χρειαν). Present active indicative, 'They have need,' though A has εξουσιν (shall have), future like εστα. Here again there is repetition of part of Revelation 21:23, but for the purpose of showing the delightsomeness of the New Jerusalem with no need of lamp or sun."
2. "Shall give them light (φωτισε). Future active of φωτιζω, while aorist εφωτισεν in Revelation 21:23."
3. "They shall reign (βασιλευσουσιν). Future active of βασιλευω. Reign eternally in contrast with the limited millennial reign of Revelation 20:4; Revelation 20:6. This glorious eternal reign with Christ occurs repeatedly in the book (Revelation 1:6; Revelation 3:21; Revelation 5:10) as in Luke 22:30."

### John Gill:
1. "The happiness of this state will greatly lie in the light thereof; it will be one everlasting day."
2. "They need no candle, nor the light of the sun; neither artificial nor natural light."
3. "They shall reign for ever and ever; they are made kings now, and in this state they shall reign with Christ for the space of a thousand years; and when they are ended, they shall not cease to reign."

### John Wesley:
1. "What encouragement is this to the patience and faithfulness of the saints, that, whatever their sufferings are, they will work out for them 'an eternal weight of glory!'"
2. "Thus ends the doctrine of this Revelation, in the everlasting happiness of all the faithful."
3. "The mysterious ways of Providence are cleared up, and all things issue in an eternal Sabbath, an everlasting state of perfect peace and happiness, reserved for all who endure to the end."
