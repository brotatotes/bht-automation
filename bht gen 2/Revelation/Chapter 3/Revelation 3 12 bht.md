# Revelation 3:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
He who overcomes will have a distinguished and unshakeable position in the eternal church. Like a pillar, he will provide support and stability. It will be evident that he belongs to God, with His name inscribed upon him. He will enjoy eternal holiness and happiness, never to be cast out.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "He that conquereth, I will make him a pillar in the temple of my God...he shall have a fixed and important place in the glorified church hereafter."
2. "None shall thrust him out, nor shall he be any more in danger of falling, and thus thrusting himself out."
3. "By the inscription of this new name of the glorified Savior is declared, that he belongs to Him in His new and glorious state of eternal rest and triumph."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "In one sense there shall be 'no temple' in the heavenly city because there shall be no distinction of things into sacred and secular, for all things and persons shall be holy to the Lord."
2. "The city shall be all one great temple, in which the saints shall be not merely stones, as in the spiritual temple now on earth, but all eminent as pillars: immovably firm, like the colossal pillars before Solomon's temple."
3. "The saints in their heavenly royal priesthood shall bear His name openly, as consecrated to Him."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The characteristic of the church: it was 'lukewarm' - neither 'cold nor hot'".
2. "A solemn reproof of their self-confidence, of their ignorance of themselves, and of their pride, when they were in fact poor, and blind, and naked; and a solemn counsel to them to apply to him for those things which would make them truly rich - which would cover up the shame of their nakedness, and which would give them clear spiritual vision."
3. "An assurance that an opportunity is still offered for repentance, represented by his standing at the door and praying for admittance."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The reference here is not to any prominence in the earthly church, but to blessedness in the future state."
2. "The conqueror, not the pillar... Probably with reference to the golden plate inscribed with the name of Jehovah, and worn by the High-Priest upon his forehead."
3. "The new Jerusalem is not a city freshly built, but is new in contrast with the old, outworn, sinful city."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Him will I make a pillar in the temple of my God. He shall not merely be a living stone in the temple, but something much more beautiful and glorious."
2. "And he shall in no wise come forth any more...the believer, when crowned with his glory, shall in like manner be safe from all future trial."
3. "They express in one way or another the relation of the believer to God as his Father, to Christ as the Revelation of the Father, and to the privileges and joys of citizenship in the kingdom made known to us in the Father and the Son."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Metaphorical and personal use with a double significance of being firmly fixed and giving stability to the building."
2. "Fixity of character is at last achieved." 
3. "He receives this triple name (of God, of the city of God, of Christ) on his forehead just as the high-priest wore the name of Jehovah upon his forehead."

### John Gill:
1. "He that overcometh,.... In the hour of temptation, in this period of time... will I make a pillar in the temple of my God." 
2. "they become very ornamental in the church, they are made honourable members of it... and, like pillars, are a support to it... steady, firm, and constant." 
3. "The sense is, that it should be manifest that such are interested in God, as their covenant God and Father, in like manner as he is the God and Father of Christ... as plain and as evident as an inscription on a pillar, or as if it was written upon their foreheads."

### John Wesley:
1. "I will make him a pillar in the temple of my God — I will fix him as beautiful, as useful, and as immovable as a pillar in the church of God."
2. "And he shall go out no more — But shall be holy and happy forever."
3. "And I will write upon him the name of my God — So that the nature and image of God shall appear visibly upon him."
