# Revelation 3:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
He who overcomes shall be adorned in white raiment, symbolizing victory, joy, and triumph. His name shall not be erased from the book of life, ensuring his eternal life. Moreover, his name shall be publicly acknowledged and commended by Christ himself on the day of judgment, validating his faithfulness and loyalty as a servant of God.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The body transfigured into the likeness of Christ's body, and emitting beams of light reflected from Him, is probably the 'white raiment' promised here."
2. "He that overcometh shall receive the same reward as they who 'have not defiled their garments' therefore the two are identical."
3. "An undesigned coincidence, proving that these epistles are, as they profess, in their words, as well as substance, Christ's own addresses."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Whosoever he may be that shall overcome sin and the temptations of this world, shall be admitted to this glorious reward."
2. "The hope that is thus held out before us, is that of appearing with the Redeemer in his kingdom, clad in robes expressive of holiness and joy."
3. "The expression 'I will not blot out' means, that the names would be found there on the great day of final account, and would be found there forever."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Book of life. Lit., the book of the life." 
2. "For the figure, see Exodus 32:32; Psalms 69:28; Daniel 12:1; Philippians 4:3. Compare Luke 10:20; Hebrews 12:23."
3. "I will confess [εξομλογησομαι]. Openly confess [εξ]. See on Matthew 11:25; Acts 19:18; James 5:16."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He that overcometh shall thus be clothed in white garments. He shall be clothed about, shall be wrapped round and round with the glistering glory."
2. "The ‘book of life’ is a book conceived of as a register, containing the names of the true citizens of Zion... There is no statement here that there is such a process of erasure of names from the book of life as may warrant us in saying that names once admitted to that book are being continually blotted out."
3. "And I will confess his name before my Father, and before his angels. He who has sought no name before men shall have his ‘name’ confessed by his Lord in the great day."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Shall be arrayed (περιβαλειτα)." 
2. "In white garments (εν ιματιοις λευκοις)." 
3. "I will in no wise blot out (ου μη εξαλειψω)." 
4. "Of the book of life (εκ της βιβλου της ζωης)." 
5. "His name (το ονομα αυτου)."

### John Gill:
1. "He that overcometh... shall be clothed in white raiment... and triumph over all his enemies... and shall enjoy eternal glory and happiness!" 
2. "And I will not blot out his name out of the book of life;... those whose names are written in it shall never be blotted out, they will always remain in the number of God's elect, and can never become reprobates, or shall ever perish." 
3. "But I will confess his name before my Father and before his angels... and this will be made before his Father, who chose these persons, and gave them to him to preserve and save; and before the angels, who rejoice at their salvation and happiness; and this will be at the last day."

### John Wesley:
1. "He shall be clothed in white raiment — The colour of victory, joy, and triumph."
2. "And I will not blot his name out of the book of life — Like that of the angel of the church at Sardis: but he shall live forever."
3. "I will confess his name — As one of my faithful servants and soldiers."
