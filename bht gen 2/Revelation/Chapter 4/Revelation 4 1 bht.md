# Revelation 4:1 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Following these events, a door opened in heaven, providing John with a vision of both heavenly and earthly scenes. The invitation to 'come up hither' was not a physical ascent, but a spiritual one, granting John a new perspective and understanding of future events. The voice he heard was like a trumpet, captivating and authoritative, delivering divine messages to John.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "After these things...I saw...a door set open...in heaven...and behold, a door set open in heaven." 
2. "Henceforth usually he looks from the heaven down on the earth, seeing however both alike, and being present in either, as the localities of his various visions require."
3. "Come up hither (viz. through the opened door), and I will shew thee the things which must take place after these things."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "After these things," marking the opening of the next vision in the succession... from "the things which are" to "the things which shall be hereafter."
2. "I saw" in vision... not as English Version means, I directed my look that way.
3. John is transported in vision through an open door up into heaven, whence he can see things passing on earth or in heaven, according as the scenes of the several visions require.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "After this - Greek, 'After these things'; that is, after what he had seen, and after what he had been directed to record in the preceding chapters."
2. "I looked - Greek, 'I saw' - εἶδον eidon. Our word 'look' would rather indicate purpose or intentions, as if he had designedly directed his attention to heaven, to see what could be discovered there."
3. "Come up hither - To the place whence the voice seemed to proceed - heaven. And I will show thee things which must be hereafter - Greek, 'after these things.' The reference is to future events; and the meaning is, that there would be disclosed to him events that were to occur at some future period."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Not indicating a break in the ecstatic state of the seer, but only a succession of separate visions."
2. "A door set open. The A. V. implies that the seer witnessed the opening of the door. In Heaven."
3. "A voice as of a trumpet. Properly a war - trumpet, though the word was also used of a sacred trumpet, with the epithet iJera sacred."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "After these things denotes succession of visions, not of time."
2. "A door opened in heaven, not opening but open, so that there may be the freest intercourse between heaven and earth."
3. "The Seer is invited to ascend to the place whence the voice issued, and is told what will be shown him."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "This vision is of heaven, not of earth as was true of chapters Revelation 4:1; Revelation 4:2."
2. "Behold (ιδου). Exclamation of vivid emotion as John looked."
3. "Come up (αναβα). Short Koine form for αναβηθ (second aorist active imperative second person singular of αναβαινω)."

### John Gill:
1. "the eye is never satisfied with seeing"
2. "a figurative sense; the phrase is to be understood of a discovery of things that were, or were to be in the church of God"
3. "the voice of the Son of God is heard as the sound of a trumpet, talking loudly and familiarly to John"

### John Wesley:
1. "After I had written these letters from the mouth of the Lord."
2. "By each of these St. John gains a new and more extended prospect."
3. "Said, Come up hither — Not in body, but in spirit; which was immediately done."
