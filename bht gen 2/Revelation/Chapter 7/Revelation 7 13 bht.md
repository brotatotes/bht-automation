# Revelation 7:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
And one of the elders responds, indicating that there is something significant to be understood about these individuals who are dressed in white robes. The elder's answer is in direct response to John's inquiry about their identity and origins. This interaction serves to draw John's attention to the importance of these individuals and to provide a deeper understanding of their role and significance within the vision.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "One of the twenty-four elders, representing the Old and New Testament ministry, appropriately acts as interpreter of this vision of the glorified Church."
2. "What, &c.—Greek order, 'These which are arrayed in white robes, WHO are they?'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The object evidently is to bring the case of these persons more particularly into view."
2. "There was a propriety in thus directing the mind of John to the martyrs as triumphing in heaven in a time when the churches were suffering persecution."
3. "The object is to fix the attention more distinctly on what is said of them, that they came up out of great tribulation."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Answered. In the sense of taking up speech in connection with some given occasion..."
2. "What are these, etc... The Rev., properly, follows the Greek order, which places first 'These which are arrayed in the white robes, who are they?'"
3. "For what, render who, as Rev."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The question is not asked by the Seer. It is Addressed to him in order that his attention may be drawn to it with greater force." 
2. "In chap, vi the four living creatures spoke, because they represented creation, and were the instruments of vengeance."
3. "Now one of the elders speaks, because the elders represent the triumphant Church."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "An elder intervenes, though no question has been asked to interpret the vision."
2. "Prophetic predicate nominative put before 'who are they'."
3. "Both 'who' and 'whence' as in Joshua 9:8."

### John Gill:
1. "This elder...is much more likely, according to others, to be Constantine himself, the first of the elders, or the chief magistrate when the church sprung out of its troubles, and enjoyed rest and peace."
2. "The word 'answered'... signifies here, that the elder opened his mouth, began to speak, and called to John, and said as follows."
3. "He said, not as being ignorant of them, or of the reason of their being clothed in this manner, nor of the place and state from whence they came, as appears by the account afterwards given of them by him; but to stir up John to take more notice of them, as being a body of men that were worthy of observation and contemplation."

### John Wesley:
1. "And one of the elders - What stands, verses 13-17, Revelation 7:13-17 might have immediately followed the tenth verse; but that the praise of the angels, which was at the same time with that of the 'great multitude,' came in between."
2. "Answered - He answered St. John's desire to know, not any words that he spoke."
