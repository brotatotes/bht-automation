# Romans 1:25 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
They distorted the truth of God and embraced falsehood. Their worship was directed towards idols, forsaking the one true God. Both inwardly and outwardly, they devoted themselves to serving these false deities.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The truth of God (the true notion of Him as the Creator) which they professed, they changed into a lie, thus counteracting its legitimate agency and depriving it of all power for good."
2. "Both verbs belong to 'the creature'; though 'σεβάζομαι' would require an accusative, 'λατρεύω', the nearest, takes the government."
3. "The doxology expresses the horror of the Apostle at this dishonour, and puts their sin in a more striking light. But we need not supply 'εἰ καὶ οὗτοι ὕβρισαν', as Chrys."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Who changed the truth of God into a lie—that is, the truth concerning God into idol falsehood."
2. "Professing merely to worship the Creator by means of the creature, they soon came to lose sight of the Creator in the creature."
3. "How aggravated is the guilt of the Church of Rome, which, under the same flimsy pretext, does shamelessly what the heathen are here condemned for doing, and with light which the heathen never had!"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The phrase, 'the truth of God' is a Hebrew phrase, meaning 'the true God.' In such a case, where two nouns come together, one is employed as an adjective to qualify the other."
2. "Idols are not infrequently called falsehood and lies because they are not true representations of God."
3. "The mention of the degrading idolatry of the pagans was strongly calculated to impress on his mind the superior excellency and glory of the one living God."

### John Calvin:
1. "When the truth of God is turned to a lie, his glory is obliterated. It is then but just, that they should be besprinkled with every kind of infamy, who strive to take away from God his honor, and also to reproach his name."
2. "He points out especially the sin of idolatry; for religious honor cannot be given to a creature, without taking it away, in a disgraceful and sacrilegious manner, from God."
3. "He is one whom they ought alone to have honored and worshipped, and from whom it was not right to take away any thing, no, not even the least."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Being such as, or, ‘since they were such as.’ Here the Apostle reverts to the reason for the punishment."
2. "Exchanged. A stronger phrase than that in Romans 1:23."
3. "The nature of the case leads us to prefer ‘rather than’ to ‘more than’; for idolatry is in compatible with the worship of the true God, who shares His honor with none of His creatures."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "What a bargain they made, 'the truth of God for the lie.'" 
2. "They preferred the creature rather than the Creator."
3. "One of Paul's doxologies which may come at any moment when he is greatly stirred."

### John Gill:
1. "They changed the truth of God into a lie; by ascribing to false deities, which were lying vanities, those things which were known of God."
2. "They worshipped and served the creature more than the Creator."
3. "Who is blessed for ever, Amen; is blessed in himself, and the fountain of all blessedness to his creatures."

### John Wesley:
1. Who changed the truth — The true worship of God.Into a lie — False, abominable idolatries.And worshipped — Inwardly.And served — Outwardly.
