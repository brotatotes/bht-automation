# Romans 1:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this passage, the Apostle expresses his gratitude for the faith of the readers, which is widely recognized and celebrated throughout the world. Through Jesus Christ, the mediator, he presents his thanksgiving to God, acknowledging that faith is a gift from Him. The personal address of my God emphasizes the intimate relationship between the Apostle and God, reflecting his faith, hope, and love. It is through Christ that all blessings are received and all prayers and thanksgivings are directed to God. This opening of the epistle reveals the Apostle's deep spiritual affection, particularly his thankfulness to God.

### Henry Alford:
1. "This placing himself in intimate connexion with his readers by mention of and thankfulness for their faith or Christian graces, is the constant habit of Paul." 
2. "For only by the Spirit of Christ dwelling in men’s hearts are thanksgivings and prayer acceptable to God."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Your faith is spoken of throughout the whole world."
2. "The influence they would exercise upon others."
3. "Given thanks for such faith to 'his God through Jesus Christ.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The duty of presenting our thanks to God 'through' Christ is often enjoined in the New Testament."
2. "Christ is the mediator between God and human beings, or the medium by which we are to present our prayers and also our thanksgivings."
3. "The readiness with which the Romans had embraced the gospel, the firmness with which they adhered to it, was so remarkable, that it was known and celebrated everywhere."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Through Jesus Christ. As the medium of his thanksgiving: 'As one who is present to his grateful thoughts; in so far, namely, as that for which he thanks God is vividly perceived and felt by him to have been brought about through Christ.'"
2. "In penitence and in thanksgiving alike, Jesus Christ is the one mediator through whom we have access to God."
3. "Is proclaimed. The different compounds of the simple verb ajggellw to announce, are interesting...Thus in ajnaggellein the recipient of the news is contemplated; in ajpaggellein the source; in kataggellein the relation of the bearer and hearer of the message."

### John Calvin:
1. "He intimates that they being honored with the public approbation of the churches, could not reject an Apostle of the Lord, without disappointing the good opinion entertained of them by all; and such a thing would have been extremely uncourteous and in a manner bordering on perfidy."
2. "We are here taught that faith is God’s gift; for thanksgiving is an acknowledgment of a benefit. He who gives thanks to God for faith, confesses that it comes from him."
3. "I observe in the last place, that he calls him his God. This is the faithful’s special privilege, and on them alone God bestows this honor."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The Apostle pursues the natural course of first placing himself, so to speak, in relation with his readers; and his first point of contact with them is gratitude for their participation in Christianity."
2. "There is a touching emphasis in the phrase 'my God' with its personal appropriation and corresponding sense of personal obligation."
3. "The thanksgiving is through Christ; Jesus Christ is also the medium through whom came the blessings for which he is thankful."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The rush of thoughts crowds out the balanced phraseology." 
2. "As the mediator or medium of thanksgiving." 
3. "Either declarative or causal οτ makes sense here."

### John Gill:
1. "The object of thanksgiving is God not merely as a creator and preserver, but as a Father, the Father of Christ, and our Father in Christ."
2. "The apostle styles him, my God; which distinguishes him from all others, points out his particular interest in him, expresses his knowledge of him and faith in him."
3. "The thing for which the apostle was thankful for particularly was, not that their city was mistress of the whole world, and their fame for power, wealth, and grandeur, was spread abroad far and near; but, says he, that your faith is spoken of throughout the whole world."

### John Wesley:
1. "In the very entrance of this one epistle are the traces of all spiritual affections; but of thankfulness above all..."
2. "This very word [My God] expresses faith, hope, love, and consequently all true religion."
3. "The gifts of God all pass through Christ to us; and all our petitions and thanksgivings pass through Christ to God."
