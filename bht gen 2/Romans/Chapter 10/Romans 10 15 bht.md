# Romans 10:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The preaching of the gospel is a fulfillment of prophecy, bringing the glad tidings of peace and good things. Those who are sent with this message are commissioned by God and their arrival is eagerly anticipated. Their feet are beautiful as they bring the news of reconciliation and salvation, their walk aligned with the doctrine they preach. This preaching is not to be taken lightly, for it holds the power to bring peace and joy to individuals and the world.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The whole chapter of Isaiah from which this is taken, and the three that follow, are so richly Messianic"
2. "There can be no doubt 'the glad tidings' there spoken of announce a more glorious release than of Judah from the Babylonish captivity"
3. "The very feet of its preachers are called 'beautiful' for the sake of their message."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "And how shall they preach - In what way shall there be preachers, unless they are commissioned by God?"
2. "That preach ... - Literally, 'that evangelize peace. That proclaim the good news of peace; or bring the glad message of peace. And bring glad tidings ... - Literally, 'and evangelize good things;' or that bring the glad message of good things."
3. "The coming of those messengers who proclaim it [peace] is joyful to the world. It fills the bosom of the anxious sinner with peace; and they and their message will be regarded with deep interest, as sent by God, and producing joy in an agitated bosom, and peace to the world."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Beautiful [ωραιοι]. From wra the time of full bloom or development. Hence the radical idea of the word includes both blooming maturity and vigor."
2. "Feet. Emphasizing the rapid approach of the messenger. 'In their running and hastening, in their scaling obstructing mountains, and in their appearance and descent from mountains, they are the symbols of the earnestly - desired, winged movement and appearance of the Gospel itself' (Lange)."
3. "Obeyed [υπηκουσαν]. Obeyed as the result of listening, and so especially appropriate here. Compare head and hear, ver. 14. For the same reason hearken (Rev.) is better than obeyed."

### John Calvin:
1. "He intimates that it is a proof and a pledge of divine love when any nation is favored with the preaching of the gospel."
2. "The Lord, when he gave hope of deliverance to his people, commended the advent of those who brought the glad tidings of peace, by a remarkable eulogy; by this very circumstance he has made it evident that the apostolic ministry was to be held in no less esteem, by which the message of eternal life is brought to us."
3. "Nor is there indeed a doubt, but that God has thus highly spoken of the incomparable value of this treasure, for the purpose of awakening the minds of all, so that they may anxiously desire it."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Sent by Christ is implied, but the main thought is, sent, ‘through the word of God’."
2. "The prophecy is undoubtedly Messianic, and, hence, properly applied by the Apostle to the preachers of the gospel."
3. "The necessity and dignity of the preachers of the gospel, as here set forth, form a solemn warning to all who attempt to preach without being sent, as well as an encouragement to all, however feeble, who have been sent."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "How beautiful (Hως ωραιο). A quotation from Isaiah 52:7 more like the Hebrew than the LXX, picturing the messengers of the restoration from the Jewish captivity."
2. "Paul assumes that the missionaries (αποστολο) have been sent as implied in verse Romans 10:14."

### John Gill:
1. "There is no proper, rightful, regular, and lawful preaching of the word without a mission."
2. "Their work is also to 'bring glad tidings of good things'; such as reconciliation, righteousness, pardon, life, and eternal salvation, by a crucified Christ."
3. "The reason of [their feet being beautiful] is, partly because of the agreeableness of their walk and conversation to the doctrine they preach; and partly because of their readiness to preach it everywhere, though they run the utmost risk in so doing."

### John Wesley:
1. "But how shall they preach, unless they be sent — Thus by a chain of reasoning, from God's will that the gentiles also should 'call upon him,' St. Paul infers that the apostles were sent by God to preach to the gentiles also."
2. "The feet — Their very footsteps; their coming. Isaiah 52:7."
