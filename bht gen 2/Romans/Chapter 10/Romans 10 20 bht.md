# Romans 10:20 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Isaiah boldly speaks with great clarity and openness, expressing what Moses only hinted at. Through Isaiah's words in Isaiah 65:1,2, we can understand the straightforward message and courage displayed in his prophetic revelation.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Esaias is very bold and says (i.e. as we say, ‘dares to say,’ ‘ventures to speak thus plainly.’)"
2. "I was found by (or among) those who sought me not, I became manifest to those who asked not after me."
3. "The whole passage is thoroughly gone into and its true meaning satisfactorily shewn."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "I was found of them that sought me not—until I sought them."
2. "I was made manifest unto them that asked not after me—until the invitation from Me came to them."
3. "That the calling of the Gentiles was meant by these words of the prophet (Isaiah 65:1) is manifest from what immediately follows, 'I said, Behold Me, behold Me, unto a nation that was not called by My name.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Expresses the doctrine openly, boldly, without any reserve."
2. "This means that the Gentiles, whose characteristic was not that they sought God, would have the gospel sent to them, and would embrace it."
3. "The phrase, 'I was found,' in the past tense here, is in the present in the Hebrew, intimating that the time would come when God would say this of himself."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Very bold [αποτολμα] . Only here in the New Testament. Plato, 'Laws,' 701, uses it of liberty as too presumptuous [αποτετολμημενης] . The force of the preposition is intensive, or possibly pointing to him from whom the action proceeds; bold of himself."
2. "The simple verb means primarily to dare, and implies the manifestation of that boldness or confidence of character which is expressed by qarjrJew."
3. "Following the Septuagint, with the inversion of the first two clauses. Hebrew: 'I have offered to give answers to those who asked not. I have put myself in the way of those who sought me not. I have spread out my hand all the day to a refractory people.'"

### John Calvin:
1. "The Prophet did not speak in a figurative language, or with hesitation, but had in plain and clear words declared the calling of the Gentiles."
2. "He speaks of aliens; for it follows in the same context, 'I have said, Behold I come to a people, on whom my name is not called.'"
3. "There is no one who anticipates the Lord; but we are all, without exception, delivered by his free mercy from the deepest abyss of death, when there is no knowledge of him, no desire of serving him, in a word, no conviction of his truth."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "But Isaiah even ventures to say" - Lange
2. "Paul understood the original prophecy as referring to the Gentiles" - commentary
3. "The privilege of being called by the name of Jehovah was ever cherished by the ancient Jews" - commentary

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Paul cites Isaiah 65:1 in support of his own courage against the prejudice of the Jews."
2. "Isaiah 'breaks out boldly' (Gifford)."
3. "I was found (ευρεθην). First aorist passive indicative of ευρισκω."

### John Gill:
1. "He did not mince the matter, or cover it with dark sayings, but with all plainness and perspicuity, and with great courage and intrepidity declared it..."
2. "...they did not so much as ask after him; they did not ask counsel of him, nor ask concerning him, nor ask for him..."
3. "...they were brought under the hearing of it; and by the Spirit of God directed to him in it, in whom they found life, peace, pardon, righteousness, food, and rest, and every valuable blessing; a pearl of great price they found, a finding which can never be lost..."

### John Wesley:
1. But Isaiah is very bold — And speaks plainly what Moses but intimated. Isaiah 65:1,2.
