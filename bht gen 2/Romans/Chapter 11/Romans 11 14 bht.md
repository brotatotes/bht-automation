# Romans 11:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
My kinsmen, those belonging to the same family or nation. As the apostle reflects on his own people, he expresses a sense of connection and concern for his fellow Jews. He acknowledges that they are his flesh, his kinsmen. This reveals the depth of his love for his own people and his desire to see them come to faith in Christ. Despite their rejection of the gospel, Paul's heart is still tied to the salvation of his fellow Jews.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 14. If . . . I may provoke, &c.
	(See on Romans 11:1.) 
	my flesh—Compare Romans 11:1- :.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "If by any means - If even by stating unpleasant truths, if by bringing out all the counsel of God, even what threatens their destruction, I may arrest their attention, and save them."
2. "My flesh - My countrymen. My kinsmen, Those belonging to the same family or nation."
3. "No truth could be more likely to irritate and offend than that the Jews would be cast off; and yet the apostle used this so faithfully, and yet so tenderly, that he expected and desired it might be the means of saving the souls of his countrymen."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Some of them. A modest expression which recalls Paul 's limited success among his own countrymen.

### John Calvin:
1. "The minister of the word is said in some way to save those whom he leads to the obedience of faith."
2. "The whole power and efficacy of [the ministry of salvation] depends on God."
3. "Preaching is an instrument for effecting the salvation of the faithful, and though it can do nothing without the Spirit of God, yet through his inward operation it produces the most powerful effects."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The faithful discharge of his duty to the Gentiles had this as its attempted result." 
2. "My own flesh; comp. chap. Romans 9:3." 
3. "Save some of them, i.e., of the Jews. Notice the modesty of the expression, which, however, recalls Paul’s ill-success among his own countrymen."

### John Gill:
1. "What he had in view, even in discharging his office among the Gentiles with so much labour, assiduity, and indefatigableness, was, that if possible he might stir up the Jews to emulate and imitate the Gentiles, in seeking after Christ."
2. "The ministers of the Gospel may be said to save souls, not efficiently, for the author or efficient cause of salvation is God only; but instrumentally, as the word preached by them is the means of regeneration, faith, and conversion, with which salvation is connected."
3. "Now the apostle argues from his office, and the usefulness of it, to some among the Jews, to saving purposes, to prove that their rejection was not total."

### John Wesley:
1. My flesh — My kinsmen.
