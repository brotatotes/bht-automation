# Romans 13:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Resisting the established authority goes against the order that God has ordained among men. This does not mean that magistrates are above the law, but rather, resisting them is opposing their rightful discharge of duties. Such resistance brings both human and divine condemnation upon oneself.

### Henry Alford:

	 ἑαυτοῖς κρῖμαλ .]  shall receive for themselves (the dat. incommodi)  condemnation , viz.  punishment from God , through His minister, the civil power.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Whosoever therefore resisteth the power, resisteth the authority."
2. "They that resist shall receive to themselves damnation, or condemnation."
3. "God, whose authority in the magistrate's is resisted."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It is implied, however, that those laws shall not be such as to violate the rights of conscience, or oppose the laws of God."
2. "This means clearly that we are to regard 'government' as instituted by God, and as agreeable to his will."
3. "The word 'damnation' we apply now exclusively to the punishment of hell; to future torments. But this is not necessarily the meaning of the word which is used here... It often simply denotes 'punishment.'"

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "He that resisteth... setteth himself in array against."
2. "He setteth himself against that which is divinely set."
3. "Damnation... better, judgment."

### John Calvin:
1. "As no one can resist God but to his own ruin, he threatens, that they shall not be unpunished who in this respect oppose the providence of God."
2. "Let us then beware, lest we incur this denunciation."
3. "He teaches us in general what end awaits those who contend with God."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He who resisteth (or, ‘setteth himself against’) the authority, that particular existing authority, to which he should submit himself."
2. "The ordinance of God. The word ‘ordinance’ corresponds with ‘ordained’ (Romans 13:1)."
3. "They that withstand shall receive to themselves judgment, or, ‘condemnation.’ The former is more literal, but the latter sense is evidently implied."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He that resisteth, he that lines himself up against."
2. "Withstandeth, has taken his stand against."
3. "The ordinance of God, the divine commandment."

### John Gill:
1. "Who denies that there is, or ought to be any such order among men, despises it, and opposes it, and withdraws himself from it, and will not be subject to it in any form: resisteth the ordinance of God."
2. "This is not to be understood, as if magistrates were above the laws... but the apostle is speaking of resisting magistrates in the right discharge of their office, and in the exercise of legal power and authority."
3. "They that resist them, in this sense, shall receive to themselves damnation; that is, punishment; either temporal... or eternal punishment, unless the grace of God prevents."

### John Wesley:
1. Whosoever resisteth the power — In any other manner than the laws of the community direct.Shall receive condemnation — Not only from the magistrate, but from God also.
