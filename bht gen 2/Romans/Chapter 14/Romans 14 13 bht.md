# Romans 14:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Let us refrain from passing judgment on one another and instead focus on making a decision about our own actions. Rather than causing our brothers and sisters to stumble or be offended by our conduct, let us choose to forego our freedoms in order to preserve unity and love in the body of Christ.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "But determine this rather."
2. "An occasion of stumbling, in act."
3. "An occasion of offence, in thought."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Let us not therefore judge, but judge this rather, a beautiful sort of play upon the word 'judge', meaning..."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Since we must be there on the same 'level,' let us not suppose that we have a right here to sit in judgment on our fellow-Christians."
2. "If disposed to 'judge,' let us be employed in a better kind of judging; let us come 'to a determination' not to injure the cause of Christ."
3. "We should resolve to act so as not 'by any means' to be the occasion of leading our brethren into sin, either by our example or by a severe and harsh judgment."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Stumbling - block [προσκομμα]."
2. "Skandalon occasion of falling is also rendered stumbling - block in other passages."
3. "Some regard the two as synonymous, others as related to different results in the case of the injured brother."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Let us not therefore judge one another any more."
2. "But judge this rather, not to put, etc."
3. "The 'brother' is assumed to have a conscience more enlightened than that of an unbeliever, whose judgment and ground of offence cannot therefore have the same weight."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Let us no longer have the habit of criticizing one another."
2. "Judge this rather, the not putting a stumbling block or a trap for his brother."

### John Gill:
1. "They should not censure and judge each other's characters and states, but leave all to the decisive day, to Christ the Judge." 
2. "Reckon this to be the most proper, fit, and advisable: that no man put a stumblingblock or occasion to fall in his brother's way."
3. "It was much better... to forego the use of their liberty."

### John Wesley:
1. "But judge this rather — Concerning ourselves."
2. "Not to lay a stumblingblock — By moving him to do as thou doest, though against his conscience."
3. "Or a scandal — Moving him to hate or judge thee."
