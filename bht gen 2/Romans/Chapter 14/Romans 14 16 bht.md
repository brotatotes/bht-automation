# Romans 14:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Your good and lawful liberty should not be evil spoken of. It is important to be mindful of how your actions may appear to others and ensure that you do not cause offense. By using your freedom in a considerate and respectful manner, you can avoid giving others reason to criticize and speak ill of your beliefs and practices.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Your strength of faith is a good thing; let it not pass into bad repute."
2. "Use it so that it may be honored, and encourage others."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Let not then your good—that is, this liberty of yours as to Jewish meats and days, well founded though it be."
2. "Be evil spoken of—for the evil it does to others."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "You are free from the yoke of bondage. This freedom you esteem to be a good - a favor - a high privilege."
2. "Do not so use your Christian liberty as to give occasion for railing and unkind remarks from your brethren, so as to produce contention and strife."
3. "In relation to dress, and rites, and ceremonies in the church, we may be conscious that we are right; but an obstinate adherence to them may only give rise to contention and angry discussions, and to evil reports among men, of the tendency of religion."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Your good [υμων το αγαθον]. Referring, most probably, to the liberty of the strong."
2. "Others think that the whole Church is addressed, in which case good would refer to the gospel doctrine."
3. "In 1 Corinthians 10:30, it is used of evil-speaking by members of the Church, which favors the reference of good to the strong."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "'Then' implies that to act in the way forbidden in Romans 14:15 would have this result."
2. "The exhortation may be applied to the strong; 'good' referring accordingly to their Christian liberty, or strength of faith, which grieved the weak brethren, and would lead to censure."
3. "Those who 'blasphemed' would be such of the outside heathen world as noticed the discord."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Your good (υμων το αγαθον). "The good thing of you" = the liberty or Christian freedom which you claim."
2. "Be evil spoken of (βλασφημεισθω). Present passive imperative of βλασφημεω for which see Matthew 9:3; Romans 3:8."

### John Gill:
1. "The Gospel in general... might be blasphemed by the men of the world, on account of the divisions and contentions among the professors of it, about such little trivial things..." 
2. "The doctrine of Christian liberty in particular, which is a good thing... may be evil spoken of by those, who do not so well understand it, through an imprudent use of it by those who do..."
3. "Rather than this should be the case, forego the use of it, in things of an indifferent nature..."

### John Wesley:
1. Let not then your good and lawful liberty be evil spoken of - By being offensive to others.
