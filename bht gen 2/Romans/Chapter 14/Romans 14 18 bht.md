# Romans 14:18 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
When we serve Christ by embracing and obeying the principles of righteousness, peace, and joy, we not only honor Him but also gain the approval and admiration of wise and good individuals. In this way, our actions display the excellence of the Christian faith. Such a life, characterized by righteousness, peace, and spiritual joy, is bound to receive recognition as a life that is pleasing to both God and others.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "I believe τούτῳ to express the aggregate of the three, and ἐν τούτῳ to be equivalent to οὕτως"
2. "It would be unnatural that a subordinate member of the former sentence, belonging only to χαρά, should be at once raised to be the emphatic one in this, and the three graces just emphatically mentioned, lost sight of."
3. "οὐ γὰρ οὕτω σε θαυμάσονται τῆς τελειότητος, ὡς τῆς εἰρήνης κ . τῆς ὁμονοίας πάντες · τούτου μὲν γὰρ τοῦ καλοῦ πάντες ἀπολαύσονται, ἐκείνου δὲ οὐδὲ εἷς."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "For he that in these things serveth Christ—Here again observe how, though we do these three things as a 'kingdom of God,' yet it is 'Christ' that we serve in so doing...in a way to us inconceivable, if Christ had been viewed as a mere creature."
2. "These being the things which God delights in, and men are constrained to approve."


### Albert Barnes:
1. "To do these things is to do honor to Christ, and to show the excellency of his religion."
2. "People will 'approve' of such conduct; they will esteem it to be right, and to be in accordance with the spirit of Christianity."
3. "It will commend itself to them as such a life as people ought to lead."

### John Calvin:
1. "It is impossible, but that when anyone is acceptable to God and approved by men, the kingdom of God fully prevails and flourishes in him."
2. "Wherever then there is righteousness and peace and spiritual joy, there the kingdom of God is complete in all its parts: it does not then consist of material things."
3. "Paul speaks here of honest judgment, blended with no moroseness, no hatred, no superstition."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Herein points to the sphere of life, just described, and the verse confirms the statement of Romans 14:17." 
2. "This phrase not only indicates the moral reference of what precedes, but shows that duty in the kingdom of God consists in service of Christ." 
3. "Since such service is what He enjoins, and approved of men; standing the test of their moral judgment."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "On the principle implied by these virtues." 
2. "Acceptable to men." 
3. "Stands the test for men."

### John Gill:
1. "He whose faith is an obedient one, and embraces these things, and from the heart obeys them."
2. "Who seeks righteousness alone by Christ, and peace and pardon through his blood, who rejoices in Christ Jesus."
3. "And who, from principles of grace, serves Christ in a way of righteousness, wherein he possesses true peace of conscience, and abundance of spiritual joy and comfort."

### John Wesley:
1. In these — Righteousness, peace, and joy.Men — Wise and good men.
