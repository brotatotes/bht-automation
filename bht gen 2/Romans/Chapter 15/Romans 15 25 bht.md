### Albert Barnes:
1. "But now I go... - I am about to go now."
2. "To minister to the saints - To supply their necessities by bearing the contribution which the churches have made for them."

### John Calvin:
1. "It was indeed a rare instance of kindness, that the Grecians, having heard that their brethren at Jerusalem were laboring under want, considered not the distance at which they were separated from them; but esteeming those sufficiently nigh, to whom they were united by the bond of faith, they relieved their necessities from their own abundance." 
2. "The word communication, which is here employed, ought to be noticed; for it well expresses the feeling, by which it behooves us to succor the wants of our brethren, even because there is to be a common and mutual regard on account of the unity of the body." 
3. "I boast,” he says, “of your promptitude to all the Churches, that they may be stirred up by your example."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "I am journeying unto Jerusalem (on the point of doing so) ministering unto the saints."
2. "The present participle indicates that the journey is part of the ministering."
3. "On this service, see 2 Corinthians 9:1-2; Acts 24:27."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "But now (νυν δε). Repeats the very words used in Romans 15:23."
2. "I go (πορευομα). Futuristic present as in John 14:2."
3. "Ministering unto the saints (διακονον τοις αγιοις). Present active participle of purpose like ευλογουντα in Acts 3:26."

### John Gill:
1. "Whither he was bound in spirit, not knowing what should befall him there, from which he could not be dissuaded by his friends." 
2. "To distribute among the poor saints what had been raised for them by the Greek churches; who had entreated him to take upon him this service, even the fellowship of ministering to the saints."
