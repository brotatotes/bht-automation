# Romans 15:27 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Gentiles are indebted to the Jews for the spiritual blessings they have received. It is not just a matter of benevolence, but of repayment. The Gentiles have an obligation to support the Jews in both spiritual and earthly matters. By contributing to their needs, the Gentiles express their worship and gratitude for the gospel they have received. This is a sacrificial service that reflects the value and importance of the gospel.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The fact is re-stated, with a view to an inference from it, viz. that the εὐδόκησαν was not merely a matter of benevolence, but of repayment."
2. "The Gentiles being debtors to the Jews for spiritual blessings."
3. "It is suggested by Grot., al., that by this Paul wished to hint to the Romans the duty of a similar contribution."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "For if the Gentiles have been made partakers of their spiritual things, their duty is also—'they owe it also'." 
2. "To minister unto them in carnal things." 
3. "They owe it to them to financially support them."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It becomes a matter of 'debt' where the hearer of the gospel 'receives,' in spiritual blessings, far more than he confers by supporting the ministry."
2. "Every man who contributes his due proportion to support the gospel may receive far more, in return, in his own peace, edification, and in the order and happiness of his family, than his money could purchase in any other way."
3. "The minister is not a beggar; and what is necessary to his support is not almsgiving. He has an equitable claim - as much as a physician, or a lawyer, or a teacher of youth has - on the necessaries and comforts of life."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Paul puts the ministry of almsgiving on the footing of a sacrificial service."
2. "It expresses the worship of giving."

### John Calvin:
1. "He adds the ground of this obligation, — that they had received the gospel from them: and he takes his argument from the comparison of the less with the greater."
2. "And it shows the value of the gospel, when he declares, that they were indebted not only to its ministers, but also to the whole nation, from whom they had come forth."
3. "Nor do I doubt but that Paul meant that it is a kind of sacrifice when the faithful gave of their own to relieve the wants of their brethren; for they thus perform that duty of love which they owe, and offer to God a sacrifice of an acceptable odor."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The Apostle emphasizes by the repetition the willingness of the Grecian Christians, but adds another statement to mark the reasonableness of such contributions: they were a matter of repayment."
2. "To such priestly service belongs the privilege and duty of providing for the poor saints."
3. "This thought is the more emphatic in view of the antithesis between spiritual things and carnal things; the former referring to the gifts of the Holy Spirit which came to the Gentiles from the mother church at Jerusalem."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The Gentiles are debtors to the Jews."

### John Gill:
1. "It arose from themselves; it was the pure effect of their good will and pleasure; the first motion was from among themselves; it was their own thought, mind, and will; they were willing of themselves unto it."
2. "They performed this service with great alacrity and cheerfulness; they gave not sparingly, but largely; it was not a matter of covetousness, but of bounty; and they did it not grudgingly, but cheerfully; they took delight and pleasure in it; their hearts and souls were in it."
3. "Moreover, it was not merely a debt of love which these Gentiles owed, and in this way paid to the believing Jews; but it was a debt of justice and equity; they had received what was of valuable consideration from them, and by their means."

### John Wesley:
1. "It hath pleased them; and they are their debtors — That is, they are bound to it, in justice as well as mercy." 
2. "Spiritual things — By the preaching of the gospel." 
3. "Carnal things — Things needful for the body."
