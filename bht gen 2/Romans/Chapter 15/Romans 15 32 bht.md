# Romans 15:32 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The apostle's desire was to come to the believers with joy, finding rest alongside them. He longed for their prayers so that nothing would hinder his journey, and he could experience refreshment after his labors. The Apostle trusted in the will of God, knowing that his arrival in Rome would ultimately bring joy, even if he faced opposition and challenges along the way. His hope was that their prayers would align with the divine purpose, enabling him to reach them in a spirit of joy and find mutual refreshment.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "that we may mutually refresh ourselves, I after my dangers and deliverance, you after your anxieties for me."
2. "But the text is in some confusion."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "That I may come unto you with joy by the will of God." 
2. "With you refresh myself, after all his labors and anxieties, and so be refitted for future service."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "That I may not be impeded in my intended journey by opposition in Judea."
2. "Joy to myself in being permitted to come; and producing joy to you by my presence."
3. "This should be the end of our most ardent desires, and this the object of all our prayers, that the will of God should be done."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. With you be refreshed [συναναπαυσωμαι υμιν] . See on give rest, Matthew 11:28. 

### Philip Schaff:
1. "That in joy (the emphasis rests on this phrase) coming unto you through the will of God, I may together with you find rest."
2. "The reality was entirely different from this hope and prayer; but we cannot doubt that the Apostle’s arrival at Rome was ‘in joy,’ even though in bonds, since in all he submitted himself to the will of God."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "This one final with συναναπαυσωμα, first aorist middle subjunctive of the double compound verb συναναπαυομα." 
2. "To refresh (αναπαυω as in Matthew 11:28) one's spirit with (συν), with the associative instrumental case υμιν (with you), only here in the N.T."

### John Gill:
1. "The end the apostle had, in desiring them to request the above things at the throne of grace for him, was, that he might come to them."
2. "He puts them upon praying for success in this affair, that so when he came among them he should have no uneasiness upon his mind."
3. "He knew his deliverance from his enemies must be by, and the success of his services owing to, and his coming to Rome entirely according to, the will of God."

### John Wesley:
1. That I may come to you — This refers to the former, With joy - To the latter, part of the preceding verse.
