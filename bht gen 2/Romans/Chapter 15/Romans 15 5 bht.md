# Romans 15:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The mention of the power of Christ Jesus in the passage highlights the authority and strength that comes from Him. It underscores the importance of recognizing that our ability to persevere, find comfort, and exercise patience is ultimately derived from Christ. He is the source of our empowerment and equips us with what we need as we navigate our Christian walk. It serves as a reminder that our dependence should be on Him for strength and sustenance.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Now the God of patience and consolation—Such beautiful names of God are taken from the graces which He inspires."
2. "It is not mere unanimity which the apostle seeks for them; for unanimity in evil is to be deprecated. But it is 'according to Christ Jesus'—after the sublimest model of Him whose all-absorbing desire was to do, 'not His own will, but the will of Him that sent Him.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The God who is 'himself' long-suffering, who bears patiently with the errors and faults of his children..."
2. "If God bears long and patiently with 'our' infirmities, 'we' ought to bear with each other."
3. "If all Christians would imitate the example of Christ, and follow his instructions, there would be no contentions among them."

### John Calvin:
1. "God alone is doubtless the author of patience and of consolation; for he conveys both to our hearts by his Spirit: yet he employs his word as the instrument."
2. "The sum of his prayer is, - that he would bring their minds to real unanimity, and make them united among themselves: he also shows at the same time what is the bond of unity."
3. "What confirms the former, in addition to the general import of the context, is the clause which follows, 'according to Christ Jesus,' which evidently means, 'according to his example,' as mentioned in verse 3."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He well knows that the Scripture itself is inefficacious without help of the God of the Scriptures."
2. "For such harmony patience and comfort are needed; only the God of patience and comfort can produce these, but He produces them through the Scriptures."
3. "According to His example (Romans 15:3), but also according to His will as Head of the Church and according to His Spirit as the Life of the Church."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The God of patience and comfort (ο θεος της υπομονης κα της παρακλησεως)."
2. "Grant you (δωιη υμιν). Second aorist active optative (Koine form for older δοιη) as in 2 Thessalonians 3:16; Ephesians 1:17; 2 Timothy 1:16; 2 Timothy 1:18; 2 Timothy 2:25."
3. "According to Christ Jesus (κατα Χριστον Ιησουν). 'According to the character or example of Christ Jesus' (2 Corinthians 11:17; Colossians 2:8; Ephesians 5:24)."

### John Gill:
1. "The efficacy and usefulness of [the Scriptures], in producing and promoting patience and comfort, entirely depend upon God the author of them."
2. "He is patient himself, and bears much and long with the children of men... and is in all a pattern to be imitated by his people."
3. "The petition [...] regards a likeness of affection, the sameness of mutual love, that they be of one heart, and one soul; that [...] they should love one another, and cease either to despise or judge each other."

### John Wesley:
1. According to the power of Christ Jesus.
