# Romans 16:20 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
May the God of peace bring about the fulfillment of His promise to bruise Satan, the adversary, beneath your feet. Be encouraged that in a little time you will experience victory over the enemy's schemes and be more than conquerors through Christ, the One who will crush the serpent's head. This assurance comes from the God who not only promotes peace but also blesses your wisdom and discretion in overcoming the enemy's tactics and fostering love and unity among believers.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The usage of the expression (see reff.) seems to favour this." 
2. "συντρίψει, not as Stuart, ‘for optative,’ nor does it express any wish, but a prophetic assurance and encouragement in bearing up against all adversaries, that it would not be long before the great Adversary himself would be bruised under their feet."
3. "It appears as if the Epistle was intended to conclude with this usual benediction, but the Apostle found occasion to add more."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The apostle encourages the Romans to persevere in resisting the wiles of the devil with the assurance that, as good soldiers of Jesus Christ, they are 'shortly' to receive their discharge, and have the satisfaction of 'putting their feet upon the neck' of that formidable enemy."
2. "This assurance is but a reproduction of the first great promise, that the Seed of the woman should bruise the Serpent's head."
3. "The 'Amen' here...is simply a mark of genuineness."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "And the God of peace - The God who promotes peace."
2. "He denotes Paul’s confidence that they 'would' gain the victory, and would be able to overcome all the arts of those who were endeavoring to sow discord and contention among them."
3. "God would disappoint their malignant purposes, and promote the prevalence of peace."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Shall bruise [συντριψει] . See on Mark 5:4; Luke 9:39.

### John Calvin:
1. "He indeed exhorts them to fight manfully against Satan, and promises that they should shortly be victorious."
2. "He was indeed once conquered by Christ, but not in such a way but that he renews the war continually."
3. "He promises ultimate defeat, which does not appear in the midst of the contest."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Shall bruise. Blessed promise of final victory over Satan by 'the God of peace.'"
2. "Shortly. As God counts time. Meanwhile patient loyalty from us."

### John Gill:
1. "may the God of peace bruise"
2. "in a little time they might be assured of victory over Satan"
3. "he shall be more than a conqueror over him through Christ"

### John Wesley:
1. "And the God of peace — The Author and Lover of it, giving a blessing to your discretion."
2. "Shall bruise Satan under your feet — Shall defeat all the artifices of that sower of tares, and unite you more and more together in love."
