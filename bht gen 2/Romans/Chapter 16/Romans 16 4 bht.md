# Romans 16:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
These individuals risked their own lives to save the apostle's, going so far as to offer their own necks in his place. Their actions were not only dear to the apostle, but also to the Church as a whole, as the Gentile churches recognized their obligation to these brave individuals who preserved such a valuable life.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "who did for my life lay down" 
2. "risked their lives"
3. "they were at least the most endeared to our apostle"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Laid down their own necks - To 'lay down the neck' is to lay the head on a block to be cut off with the axe; or to bow down the head as when the neck was exposed to be cut off by the sword of the executioner."
2. "The meaning is, that they had hazarded their lives, had exposed themselves to imminent danger, to save the life of Paul."
3. "All the churches of the Gentiles - All the churches that had been founded by the apostles. They 'felt' their deep obligation to them for having saved the life of him who had been their founder, and who was their spiritual father."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The double relative, with an explanatory force: seeing that they." 
2. "Whether the expression is literal or figurative, or if literal, when the incident occurred, cannot be determined."

### John Calvin:
1. "And deservedly dear and precious to all the Gentiles was the life of such a man, as it was an incomparable treasure: it was therefore no wonder that all the Churches of the Gentiles thought themselves to be under obligations to his preservers." (472)
2. "What he adds respecting the Church in their house is worthy of being observed; for he could not have more splendidly adorned their household than by giving it the title of a Church." (473)
3. "The word congregation, which [Erasmus ] has adopted, I do not approve; for it is plainly evident, that Paul, by way of honor, had used the sacred name of Church." (473)

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Who for my life laid down, etc. Lit., ‘laid under,’ used of submitting to execution." 
2. "That they underwent peril of their life for the sake of Paul is clearly meant; whether at Ephesus or Corinth is uncertain, since in both places Paul had been exposed to violence." 
3. "The Gentile churches owed gratitude for what was done in behalf of the Apostle to the Gentiles."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Laid down their own necks. First aorist active of υποτιθημ, old verb to place under (the axe of the executioner), only here in N.T. in this sense, though in 1 Timothy 4:16 to suggest." 
2. "If literal or figurative, the incident may be connected with the uproar created by Demetrius in Ephesus."
3. "Not only I (ουκ εγω μονος). Rather, 'not I alone' (adjective μονος). The Gentile churches also (great mission workers)."

### John Gill:
1. "Who for my life laid down their necks,.... That is, exposed themselves to great danger to save the apostle's life: the allusion is to the manner of executions by beheading, and to one person's laying down his head, and offering his neck to the executioner the room of another..."
2. "...they risked their own lives for the apostle's; which might be done either at the insurrection in Corinth against Paul, when the Jews dragged him to the judgment seat of Gallio, and beat Sosthenes the ruler of the synagogue before him; or the uproar in Ephesus by Demetrius and the craftsmen, when Paul and his companions were in great danger..."
3. "...for the care they took of him, and the danger they exposed themselves to on his account; which the apostle expresses his sense of gratitude for, and which all the Gentile churches were under obligations to do likewise..."

### John Wesley:
1. "Who have for my life, as it were, laid down their own necks - That is, exposed themselves to the utmost danger." 
2. "But likewise all the churches of the gentiles — Even that at Rome, for preserving so valuable a life."
