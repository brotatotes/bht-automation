# Romans 2:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
For those who seek glory, it is not just about pure love, but also about faith, hope, and desire. Seeking eternal life requires perseverance and steadfastness in doing good. The promised reward of eternal life is the object of pursuit, described as glory, honor, and incorruption. It is the righteous character and enduring nature of the new life that will be judged in the final judgment. Therefore, let us diligently seek after eternal life through patient continuance in well-doing, knowing that God will render to each according to their works.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The final judgment will turn upon character alone."
2. "Patient continuance in well-doing."
3. "Denoting the enduring and progressive character of the new life."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It is the uniform doctrine of the Bible that none will be saved but those who persevere in a life of holiness."
2. "The words well doing here denote such conduct as shall be conformed to the Law of God; not merely external conduct, but that which proceeds from a heart attached to God and his cause."
3. "These expressions are undoubtedly descriptive of a state of things beyond the grave."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Eternal life. Supply He will render.

### John Calvin:
1. "For it is perseverance, when one is not wearied in constantly doing good; but patience also is required in the saints, by which they may continue firm, though oppressed with various trials."
2. "The Lord will give eternal life to those who, by attention to good works, strive to attain immortality."
3. "Whenever judgment is spoken of even in the New Testament, it is ever represented in the same way, as being regulated in righteousness, according to the works of every individual."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "By endurance, perseverance, steadfastness, rather than 'patience,' is the idea of the word."
2. "The singular is used to express the character as a unit."
3. "Future salvation is...described as the object of pursuit; it is 'glory,' because of its splendid manifestation; 'honor,' because it is a reward; 'incorruption,' because it is eternal."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "To them that seek."
2. "Eternal life."
3. "To them that seek eternal life."

### John Gill:
1. "These words are descriptive of one sort of persons, to whom God will render according to their works."
2. "The manner in which these things are sought is, 'by patient continuance in well doing'"
3. "yet [eternal life] may be sought for, and looked unto, as an encouragement to well doing, and continuance therein"

### John Wesley:
1. To them that seek for glory — For pure love does not exclude faith, hope, desire, 1 Corinthians 15:58.
