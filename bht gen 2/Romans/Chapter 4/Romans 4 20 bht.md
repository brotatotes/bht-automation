# Romans 4:20 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Through the promises of God, there is no reason to doubt or hesitate, for He is faithful and will fulfill every word. Abraham's unwavering faith is an example for us to be strong and unmoved by obstacles, fully convinced of God's faithfulness. Those who trust in God's promises bring Him the greatest glory and find deep comfort in their souls.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "On δέ, see above."
2. "But with regard to the promise of God he doubted not through unbelief, but was strong in faith."
3. "δοὺς δόξῃ τῷ θεῷ" - "by recognizing His Almighty power."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 20. He staggered—hesitated 
	not . . . but was strong in
	faith, giving glory to God—as able to make good His own word in
	spite of all obstacles.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He staggered not - He steadily and firmly believed the promise."
2. "Giving glory to God - Giving honor to God by the firmness with which he believed his promises."
3. "In this way all who believe in the promises of God do honor to him. They bear testimony to him that he is worthy of confidence."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The word implies a mental struggle." 
2. "Promise." 
3. "Was strengthened, or endued with strength."

### John Calvin:
1. "The saints burst forth into expressions of wonder; but from this wonder they soon pass on to lay hold on the power of God: on the contrary, the wicked scoff at and reject it as a fable."
2. "Let us remember that the condition of us all is the same with that of Abraham: He promises immortality; we are surrounded with mortality and corruption."
3. "No one comes forth a conqueror from this contest without borrowing weapons and strength from the word of God. No greater honor can be given to God than by faith to seal his truth; no greater dishonor can be done to him than to refuse his offered favor or to discredit his word." (148 words)

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Yet with regard to the promise of God."
2. "Did not waver in unbelief."
3. "Instead of being 'made weak,' he was 'made strong.'"
4. "In faith."
5. "Giving glory to God."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He wavered not through unbelief."
2. "He was not divided in his mind by unbelief."
3. "Waxed strong through faith."

### John Gill:
1. "There is no reason to stagger at, or hesitate about any of the promises of God, since they are made by him that cannot lie; his faithfulness is engaged to perform them; with him all things are possible; every promise is in Christ, yea and amen, and never did any fail."
2. "Abraham was strong in faith; nothing moved him, no difficulties discouraged him, he made no demur upon the promise, nor had the least hesitation in his mind about the accomplishment of it; but was fully assured that so it would be, as God had said."
3. "Such persons bring the most glory to God, and are the most comfortable in their own souls."
