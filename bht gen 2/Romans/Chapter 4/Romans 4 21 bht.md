# Romans 4:21 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Having full assurance of faith, Abraham believed in the power and promises of God. He honored God by trusting Him completely, despite the obstacles and challenges that arose. Abraham's faith was not just strong, but it was grounded in a deep conviction and confidence that God was able to do what He had promised. His faith in God's power was unwavering, as he recognized that nothing is impossible for God.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "being fully persuaded."
2. "ἐπήγγελται is not passive (nor ὅ nom.), but middle, and ‘God’ the subject; that, what He has promised, He is able also to perform."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "the glory which Abraham's faith gave to God consisted in this"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "And being fully persuaded - Thoroughly or entirely convinced..."
2. "He was able - Compare Genesis 18:14. This was not the only time in which Abraham evinced this confidence."
3. "His faith was equally implicit and strong when he was commanded to sacrifice his promised son."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Being fully persuaded [πληροφορηθεις] . Rev., more accurately, fully assured." 
2. "The primary idea is, being filled with a thought or conviction." 
3. "Able [δυνατος] . The sense is stronger : mighty."

### John Calvin:
1. "There is in deed no obstacle, however small and insignificant, by which the flesh imagines the hand of God is restrained from working."
2. "That it may obtain from us its right and its honor, when a contest comes, we ought to determine thus, — That it is no less sufficient to overcome the obstacles of the world, than the strong rays of the sun are to dissipate the mists."
3. "We do not sufficiently exalt the power of God, unless we think it to be greater than our weakness."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "This simple confidence in God’s promise gave glory to God, and is the essence of faith."
2. "Confidence in God’s word, a full persuasion that He can do what seems to us impossible, is as necessary in the one case as in the other."
3. "The sinner honors God, in trusting His grace, as much as Abraham did in trusting His power."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Being fully assured (πληροφορηθεις). First aorist passive participle of πληροφορεω, from πληροφορος and this from πληρης and φερω, to bear or bring full (full measure), to settle fully."
2. "What he had promised (ο επηγγελτα). Perfect middle indicative of επαγγελλομα, to promise, retained in indirect discourse according to usual Greek idiom."
3. "He was able (δυνατος εστιν). Present active indicative retained in indirect discourse. The verbal adjective δυνατος with εστιν is here used in sense of the verb δυνατα (Luke 14:31; Acts 11:17)."

### John Gill:
1. "He had not only faith, a strong faith, but full assurance of faith."
2. "Though it was so long ago, and there were so many difficulties in the way."
3. "His faith rested upon the power of God, with whom nothing is impossible."
