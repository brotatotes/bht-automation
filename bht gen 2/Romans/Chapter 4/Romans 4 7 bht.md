# Romans 4:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Blessed are those whose sins are covered with the veil of divine mercy. The happiness of this person is unparalleled, for they have received the manifestation of their sins being forgiven. In light of this pardon, they can endure the challenges of life with steadfastness and face death with unwavering comfort.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "David here sings in express terms only of 'transgression forgiven, sin covered, iniquity not imputed'".
2. "The negative blessing necessarily includes the positive".
3. "The passage is strictly in point".

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Blessed - Happy are they: they are highly favored."
2. "Whose sins are covered - Are concealed; or hidden from the view. On which God will no more look, and which he will no more remember."
3. "By these words, we are taught that justification with Paul is nothing else but pardon of sin."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Iniquities [ανομιαι] . Lit., lawlessnesses. Are forgiven [αφεθησαν] . Lit., were forgiven. See on Matthew 6:12; James 5:15; 1 John 1:9. Also on remission, Luke 3:3.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Whoso sins are covered. The idea of the first clause is repeated under another figure, according to the parallelism of Hebrew poetry."
2. "Their sins are hid by God Himself, which is the same as ‘forgiven,’ ‘not reckoned.’"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Blessed (μακαριο). See on Matthew 5:3."
2. "Are forgiven (αφεθησαν). First aorist passive indicative of αφιημ, without augment (αφειθησαν, regular form)."
3. "Are covered (επεκαλυφθησαν). First aorist passive of επικαλυπτω, old verb, to cover over (upon, επ) as a shroud. Only here in N.T."

### John Gill:
1. "Blessed are they...whose iniquities are forgiven, and whose sins are covered: such whom God justifies by imputing the righteousness of his Son to them, he removes their iniquities from them...so that they shall never be found more." 
2. "Their sins are covered from the eye of divine justice, and shall never be seen more, or be brought against them to their condemnation, and therefore must be happy persons." 
3. "The mercy of God covering the sins of his people, through the purple blood of his Son; which is the purple covering of Christ, under which the saints go safe to glory, and by which blood their crimson and scarlet sins are blotted out, so as never to be seen more."

### John Wesley:
1. "Happy are they whose sins are covered — With the veil of divine mercy."
2. "If there be indeed such a thing as happiness on earth, it is the portion of that man whose iniquities are forgiven, and who enjoys the manifestation of that pardon."
3. "Well may he endure all the afflictions of life with cheerfulness, and look upon death with comfort."
