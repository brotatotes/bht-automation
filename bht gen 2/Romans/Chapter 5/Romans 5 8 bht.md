# Romans 5:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
God powerfully displays His own love towards us, giving proof of its certainty and completeness. This is evident in the fact that, while we were still sinners and enemies of Him, Christ died for us. The death of Christ remains the most striking manifestation of God's love, as it reconciles us to Him and justifies us. This love is not based on anything we have done or who we are, but rather is a demonstration of His kindness and willingness to sacrifice for our benefit. It is an unparalleled love that goes beyond human understanding.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "He (i.e. God) gives proof of His own love towards us."
2. "In that while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "But God commendeth his love toward us, in that, while we were yet sinners." 
2. "Christ died for us—Now comes the overpowering inference, emphatically redoubled."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "God commendeth ... - God has exhibited or showed his love in this unusual and remarkable manner."
2. "His love - His kind feeling; his beneficence; his willingness to submit to sacrifice to do good to others."
3. "While we were yet sinners - And of course his enemies. In this, his love surpasses all that has ever been manifested among people."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "God continuously establishes His love in that the death of Christ remains as its most striking manifestation." 
2. "His love [εαυτου]...not in contrast with human love, but as demonstrated by Christ's act of love."

### John Calvin:
1. "He then confirms, that is, exhibits his love to us as most certain and complete, inasmuch as for the sake of the ungodly he spared not Christ his own Son."
2. "Since Christ has attained righteousness for sinners by his death, much more shall he protect them, being now justified, from destruction."
3. "For inasmuch as he has reconciled us to the Father, our condition is such, that he purposes more efficaciously to put forth and daily to increase his favor towards us."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The proof is of such a character as to render the love conspicuous, and thus to ‘commend’ it."
2. "The atoning death of Christ is the fact which remains the most striking manifestation of the love of God."
3. "His death was the ground of our justification; God’s love provided this ground, while we were yet sinners."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "His own love (την εαυτου αγαπην). See John 3:16 as the best comment here."
2. "Not because we were Jews or Greeks, rich or poor, righteous or good, but plain sinners."
3. "Cf. Luke 18:13, the plea of the publican, 'μοι τω αμαρτωλω.'"

### John Gill:
1. "God commendeth his love towards us,.... That is, he hath manifested it, which was before hid in his heart."
2. "He has given clear evidence of it, a full proof and demonstration of it; he has so confirmed it by this instance, that there is no room nor reason to doubt of it."
3. "And that before conversion is particularly mentioned here, to illustrate the love of God to them, notwithstanding this their character and condition; and to show that the love of God to them was very early."

### John Wesley:
1. "But God recommendeth — A most elegant expression. Those are wont to be recommended to us, who were before either unknown to, or alienated from, us."
2. "While we were sinners — So far from being good, that we were not even just."
