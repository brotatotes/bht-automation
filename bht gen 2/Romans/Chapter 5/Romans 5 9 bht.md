# Romans 5:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The bloodshedding of Christ, evidenced by his violent death, serves as the foundation for our justification before God. It is through his shed blood that we find salvation from the punishment of God's wrath. This understanding applies to God's anger toward sin, as well as his compassionate benevolence toward sinners, both of which are ascribed to God in a way that can be comprehended by us mortals.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 9, 10. Much more then, being—"having
	been" 
	now justified by his blood,
	we shall be saved from wrath through him.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "If, when we were enemies, he overcame all that was in the way of our salvation; much more have we reason to expect that he will afford us protection now that we are his friends."
2. "Being now justified - Pardoned; accepted as his friends."
3. "The fact that we are purchased by his blood, and sanctified by it, renders us sacred in the eye of God; bestows a value on us proportionate to the worth of the price of our redemption; and is a pledge that he will keep what has been so dearly bought."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Wrath [της οργης] . Rev., better, "the wrath of God." the article specifying. See on ch. Romans 12:19.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "An argument from the greater to the less. 'If Christ died for His enemies, He will surely save His friends'" (Hodge).
2. "Saved through him from the wrath. That this means the wrath of God admits of no doubt... The Apostle thus joins together the certainty of salvation with the fact of God’s wrath against sin and the certainty of its execution upon unbelieving sinners" (commentary).
3. "The feeling of anger towards sin is not incompatible with the feeling of compassionate benevolence towards the sinner... The very Being who is displeased, is the very same Being who, through a placatory atonement of His own providing, saves from the displeasure" (Shedd).

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The great thing is the justification in Christ's blood."
2. "The final salvation is less of a mystery."

### John Gill:
1. "Justification in God's mind from eternity proceeded upon the suretyship engagements of Christ to be performed in time..."
2. "The blood of Christ intends his death, as appears from the context, and shows it to be a violent death; death by the effusion of blood."
3. "Our justification before God proceeds upon the foot of a satisfaction made to the law and justice of God: hence such as are interested in it shall be saved from wrath through him."

### John Wesley:
1. "By his blood — By his bloodshedding."
2. "We shall be saved from wrath through him — That is, from all the effects of the wrath of God."
3. "Therefore the inspired writers ascribe both the one and the other to God only in an analogical sense."
