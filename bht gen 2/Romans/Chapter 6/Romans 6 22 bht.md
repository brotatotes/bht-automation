# Romans 6:22 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Having been freed from the grasp of sin and now serving God, believers experience the fruit of holiness in their lives. This process of sanctification, fueled by the Spirit, begins at regeneration and conversion. Embracing holiness not only brings personal transformation but also benefits others and leads to the ultimate reward of eternal life.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Contrast of your present state to that former one: freedom from sin as a master, servitude to God."
2. "The devil would be the corresponding antithetical power: and not unfrequently appears in the teaching of Paul."
3. "Fruit unto sanctification, and the end life everlasting."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "being made free from Sin, and become servants to God—in the absolute sense intended throughout all this passage."
2. "your fruit unto holiness—"sanctification," as in :-; meaning that permanently holy state and character which is built up out of the whole 'fruits of righteousness,' which believers successively bring forth."
3. "and the end everlasting life—as the final state of the justified believer; the beatific experience not only of complete exemption from the fall with all its effects, but of the perfect life of acceptance with God, and conformity to His likeness, of unveiled access to Him, and ineffable fellowship with Him through all duration."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Being made free from sin - Being delivered from its dominion, and from bondage."
2. "This service produces holiness, as the other did sin."
3. "The final result - the ultimate consequence will be... everlasting life."

### John Calvin:
1. "Sin in this life brings the torments of an accusing conscience, and in the next eternal death."
2. "We now gather the fruit of righteousness, even holiness; we hope in future to gain eternal life."
3. "These things, unless we are beyond measure stupid, ought to generate in our minds a hatred and horror of sin, and also a love and desire for righteousness."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "But now, as opposed to 'then', being made free; comp. Romans 6:18." 
2. "Their relation is now one of personal love." 
3. "This fruit is of such a kind as at once results in 'sanctification', the progressive state, the ultimate issue being eternal life."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Freedom from sin and slavery to God bring permanent fruit that leads to sanctification."
2. "Ye have your fruit unto sanctification."
3. "And the end eternal life."

### John Gill:
1. "Holiness is a fruit of freedom from the bondage of sin, and of serving God."
2. "Holiness begun in regeneration, calling, and conversion, is a fruit of the Spirit."
3. "Holiness, as it denotes an external course of life, is useful and profitable on many accounts."
