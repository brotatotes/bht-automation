# Romans 7:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The commandment refers to every part of the law, which is holy, just, and good. This means that the law is in accordance with God's holy nature, righteous in its requirements, and beneficial for humanity. The law should be regarded with utmost reverence as it comes from God and is meant to lead us to a life of righteousness and wellbeing.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The Law is not to be blamed, though these are its effects under existing circumstances."
2. "The source of all this is not the Law, but the corrupt nature of man."
3. "The sin and condemnation of the guilty is not the fault of the Law. If obeyed, it would produce happiness everywhere."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Holy, just, good. Holy as God 's revelation of Himself; just (Rev., righteous) in its requirements, which correspond to God 's holiness; good, salutary, because of its end.

### John Calvin:
1. "The law itself and whatever is commanded in the law, is holy, and therefore to be regarded with the highest reverence."
2. "He thus defends the law against every charge of blame, that no one should ascribe to it what is contrary to goodness, justice, and holiness."
3. "Sin works depraved desires and lusts; the law is holy: sin deceives and acts the traitor, the law is plain-dealing and just: sin leads to death and misery; the law is good and leads to happiness."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The result of the whole discussion (Romans 7:7-11) is not to cast doubt upon the law, but to maintain its character as worthy of God who gave it."
2. "The law is holy. This positive character of the law Paul does not stop to prove; for the only suspicion against its holy character came from the sinful results already spoken of."
3. "Holy and just and good, 'Holy,' because it comes from a holy God; 'just,' because of its form; 'good,' because of its end."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Holy, and righteous, and good. This is the conclusion... The commandment is God's and so holy like Him, just in its requirements and designed for our good." 
2. "The modern revolt against law needs these words."

### John Gill:
1. "By 'the law' is meant the whole body of the precepts of it in general..."
2. "Nothing is more certain than that the moral law is only spoken of in this context..."
3. "It may be truly called just, or righteous, as it demands perfect obedience to all its precepts..."

### John Wesley:
1. "The commandment — That is, every branch of the law."
2. "Is holy, and just, and good — It springs from, and partakes of, the holy nature of God; it is every way just and right in itself; it is designed wholly for the good of man."
