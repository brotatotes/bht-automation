# Romans 7:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
When someone does something contrary to God's law, despite not wanting to or approving of it, it becomes evident that they actually affirm the goodness of the law. This goodness encompasses the holiness, justice, and excellence of the law, exemplifying a beautiful standard of obedience and conduct.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "But if what I wish not, that I do, I agree with the law that it is good." 
2. "The law and I are as one in proscribing the thing, the law, and my wish, tend the same way."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "If then I do that which I would not—'But if what I would not that I do,'"
2. "I consent unto the law that it is good—'the judgment of my inner man going along with the law.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The very struggle with evil shows that it is not loved, or approved, but that the Law which condemns it is really loved."
2. "The fact of struggling against evil, the desire to be free from it, and to overcome it, the anxiety and grief which it causes, is evidence that we do not love it, and that therefore we are the friends of God."
3. "Perhaps nothing can be a more decisive test of piety than a long-continued and painful struggle against evil passions and desires in every form, and a panting of the soul to be delivered from the power and dominion of sin."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "I consent [συμφημι] . Lit., speak together with; concur with, since the law also does not desire what I do."
2. "Good [καλος] . See on John 10:11, John 10:32; Matthew 26:10; James 2:7. Morally excellent."

### John Calvin:
1. "When my heart acquiesces in the law, and is delighted with its righteousness, it then perceives and acknowledges the goodness of the law..."
2. "...we are fully convinced, experience itself being our teacher, that no evil ought to be imputed to the law; nay, that it would be salutary to men, were it to meet with upright and pure hearts."
3. "But the godly man consents to the law with the real and most cheerful desire of his heart; for he wishes nothing more than to mount up to heaven."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "What I wish not, that I do. Compare the similar clause in Romans 7:15. Even this negative attitude proves the character of the law."
2. "I agree with the law that it is good. Some conviction of sin is implied, and must exist in every man awakened by the claims of the law."
3. "My conduct, therefore, so far as my desire is opposed to it, appears according to this contradiction, as a proof that I concur with the law that it is beautiful, morally good."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "I speak with."
2. "My wanting (θελω) to do the opposite of what I do proves my acceptance of God's law as good (καλος)."
3. "Old verb, here only in N.T., with associative instrumental case."


### John Gill:
1. "Since what he did, though it was contrary to the law of God, yet was what he did not will nor allow of, but hated, it must be a clear point, that he consented to the law, that it was good."
2. "Lovely and amiable; that it forbade those things which were hateful, and commanded those things which were desirable to a good man."
3. "A very beautiful rule of obedience, walk, and conversation."

### John Wesley:
1. It is good — This single word implies all the three that were used before, Romans 7:12, "holy, just, and good."
