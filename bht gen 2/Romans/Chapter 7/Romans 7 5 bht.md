# Romans 7:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Before our faith in Christ, we were in a state of being carnally minded and in a state of nature. Our sins were accidentally brought about or irritated by the law. They spread throughout our entire being and affected all aspects of our lives.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "For when we were in the flesh—in our unregenerate state, as we came into the world."
2. "The motions—"passions" (Margin), "affections" (as in Romans 1:26), or "stirrings."
3. "Which were by the law—by occasion of the law, which fretted, irritated our inward corruption by its prohibitions."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "In all circumstances where the Law was applied to the corrupt mind of man, it produced this agitation and conflict."
2. "The design of this illustration is to show the effect of the Law before a man is converted."
3. "Those sinful propensities made use of our members as instruments, to secure gratification; to bring forth fruit unto death."

### John Calvin:
1. "As long, then, as man is kept under the yoke of the law, he can, as he is sinning continually, procure nothing for himself but death."
2. "They must therefore necessarily remain altogether sinful and perverse, until a better remedy succeeds to heal their diseases."
3. "The law, instead of making men holy, made them, through the perversity of human nature, to sin the more."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "When we were in the flesh, i.e., in the natural condition of depravity"
2. "The passions of sins. The passions which led to sins"
3. "Wrought in our members; to be explained literally as in chap. Romans 6:13; Romans 6:19."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The 'flesh' is not inherently sinful, but is subject to sin."
2. "He uses σαρξ in a good many senses."
3. "Vivid picture of the seeds of sin working for death."

### John Gill:
1. "For when we, Jews and Gentiles, who are now believers in Christ, were formerly, before our conversion to, and faith in Christ, in the flesh, that is, in a corrupt, carnal, and unregenerate state and condition..."
2. "By 'the motions of sin' are meant, the evil passions and affections of the mind, the lusts of the heart, sinful desires, evil thoughts, the imaginations of the thoughts of the heart, the first motions of the mind to sin..."
3. "These motions of sin are said to 'work in our members'; in the members of our bodies, which these sinful affections of the soul make use of to put them into actions, and so they bring forth fruit; very evil fruit indeed, for nothing else can be expected from such an evil tree as the corrupt nature of man is..."

### John Wesley:
Apologies for the confusion. Here are the exact quotes from the commentary:
1. "When ye were in the flesh — Carnally minded, in a state of nature; before we believed in Christ."
2. "Our sins which were by the law — Accidentally occasioned, or irritated thereby."
3. "Wrought in our members — Spread themselves all over the whole man."
