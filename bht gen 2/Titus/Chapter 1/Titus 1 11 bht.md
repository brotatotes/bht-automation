# Titus 1:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Whose mouths must be stopped, implies that these individuals are like unruly horses that need to be controlled with a bit. They need to be silenced and prevented from spreading their harmful teachings. Their words need to be restrained and controlled through the use of solid arguments and teachings from the word of God.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Muzzled," "bridled" as an unruly beast.
2. "They are the devil's levers by which he subverts the houses of God."
3. "For filthy lucre."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The apostle gives Timothy no civil power to do it, nor does he direct him to call in the aid of the civil arm. All the agency which he specifies as proper for this, is that of argument and exhortation." 
2. "Religion is the most powerful principle that ever governs the mind; and if a man has the control of that, it is no difficult thing to induce men to give up their worldly possessions."
3. "No people can be too vigilant in regard to pretended religious teachers; and while it is undoubtedly their duty to contribute liberally for the support of the gospel, and the promotion of every good cause, it is no less their duty to examine with care every proposed object of benevolence."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Whose mouths must be stopped [ους δει επιστομιζειν] . Lit. whom it is necessary to silence."
2. "Epistomizein, N. T. o. o LXX Originally, to put something into the mouth, as a bit into a horse 's mouth."
3. "Who subvert [οιτινες ανατρεπουσιν] . The double relative is explanatory of must; in as much as they, etc."

### John Calvin:
1. "A good pastor ought therefore to be on the watch, so as not to give silent permission to wicked and dangerous doctrines to make gradual progress, or to allow wicked men an opportunity of spreading them."
2. "When they have been smitten down by the sword of God’s word, and overwhelmed by the force of the truth, the Church may command them to be silent; and if they persevere, they may at least be banished from the society of believers, so that they shall have no opportunity of doing harm."
3. "Thus it is in reality, that, amidst so great weakness of the flesh, we are exceedingly prone to fall; and hence it arises, that Satan easily and speedily destroys, by his ministers, what godly teachers had reared with great and long-continued toil."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Stopped; literally, muzzled; best done by exposing them as persons who ‘overturn entire houses,' i.e. families, through anarchic doctrine subversive of domestic authority..."
2. "...probably lax theories of Christian freedom in reference to wedlock and the duties of children and slaves."
3. "Their motive was 'base gain' (better than 'filthy lucre') to be won from their perverts."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Whose mouths must be stopped."
2. "To stop the mouth either with bridle or muzzle or gag."
3. "The Cretans are given a bad reputation for itinerating prophets for profit."

### John Gill:
1. "Whose mouths must be stopped,.... Or they be silenced, by reasons and arguments fetched out of the word of God..."
2. "...it was high time to attempt to convince them, and stop their mouths, that they might proceed no further."
3. "...having no regard to the glory of God, the honour and interest of Christ, or the good of immortal souls; only seeking to gain popular applause and honour from men."

### John Wesley:
1. Stopped — The word properly means, to put a bit into the mouth of an unruly horse.
