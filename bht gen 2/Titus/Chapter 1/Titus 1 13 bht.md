The writings of the Heathen poets may be used to advantage if truth is carefully discerned. Rebukes should be given according to the nature of offenses, cutting deep and laying open wounds. Rebukes should always aim for the good of the person being admonished.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Reprove them sharply...that they may be healthy in the faith." 
2. "The Κρῆτες indicated here, who are to be thus rebuked in order to their soundness in the faith, are manifestly not the false teachers, but the ordinary believers."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "This testimony (though coming from a Cretan) is true."
2. "Gentleness would not reclaim so perverse offenders."
3. "Those seduced by the false teachers may be brought back to soundness in the faith."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "This witness is true - That is, this testimony long before borne by one of their own number, was true when the apostle wrote to Titus."
2. "He was not to be mealy-mouthed, but he was to call things by their right names, and not to spare their faults."
3. "That they may be sound in the faith - That they may not allow the prevailing vices to corrupt their views of religion."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Sharply [αποτομως] . Only here and 2 Corinthians 13:10 (note)."
2. "Paul has ajpotomia severity, Romans 11:22 (note)."
3. "From ajpotemnein to cut off. It signifies abrupt, harsh, summary dealing."

### John Calvin:
1. "The Apostle, who is wont to reprove mildly those who deserved to be treated with extreme severity, would never have spoken so harshly of the Cretans, if he had not been moved by very strong reasons."
2. "It was truly a wonderful purpose of God, that he called a nation so depraved, and so infamous on account of its vices, to be among the first who should partake of the gospel."
3. "Of that circumspection and prudence with which a bishop ought to be endowed, it is not the least part, that he regulate his manner of teaching by the dispositions and conduct of men."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Paul endorses it from his recent knowledge."
2. "It is necessary to appear rude sometimes for safety, if the house is on fire and life is in danger."
3. "Final clause with ινα and present active subjunctive of υγιαινω, for which verb see on 1 Timothy 1:10."

### John Gill:
1. "The writings of the Heathen poets may be read with profit, and be used to advantage, if carefully and prudently attended to...what is truth, let it come from whom, or by what means it will, ought to be received."
2. "Rebukes ought to be given according to the nature of offenses, and the circumstances of them, and the offenders...doing, as surgeons do by wounds, though they take the knife, and use it gently, yet cut deep, to the quick, and go to the bottom of the wound, and lay it open."
3. "Rebukes being to persons infected with bad principles and practices, like physic to sickly constitutions, a means of removing the causes of disorder; and in rebukes, admonitions, and censures, this always ought to be the end proposed, the good of the persons rebuked, admonished, and censured."
