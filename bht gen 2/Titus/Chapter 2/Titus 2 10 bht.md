# Titus 2:10 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Servants are advised against stealing or embezzling their master's possessions, instead being encouraged to show good fidelity and faithfulness. It is emphasized that their conduct should adorn the doctrine of God, reflecting the teachings of Christ and bringing beauty and honor to the Gospel. This exhortation is not limited to those in higher positions but applies to all, including slaves. By demonstrating honorable behavior, even in lowly circumstances, servants have the opportunity to rise above societal expectations and contribute to the exaltation of their Christian faith.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Not appropriating" what does not belong to one. It means "keeping back" dishonestly or deceitfully. 
2. "The heathen do not judge of the Christian's doctrines from the doctrine, but from his actions and life."
3. "Even slaves, low as is their status, should not think the influence of their example a matter of no consequence to religion: how much more those in a high position."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "That they may adorn the doctrine of God our Saviour in all things - That they may show the fair influence of religion on them, in all respects, making them industrious, honest, kind, and obedient."
2. "They were to show that the effect of the religion which they professed was to make them better fitted to discharge the duties of their station in life, however humble; or that its influence on them was desirable in every respect."
3. "If in this situation of life, it is a duty so to live as to adorn religion, it cannot be less so in more elevated situations. A master should feel the obligation not to be surpassed in religious character by his servant."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The fundamental idea of the word is to put far away from another; to set apart for one's self; hence to purloin and appropriate to one's own use."
2. "Shewing all good fidelity."
3. "Adorn the doctrine."

### John Calvin:
1. "He censures two vices that are common among servants, petulant replies, and a propensity to steal."
2. "That they may adorn the doctrine of God our Savior in all things...our becoming conduct adorns the doctrine of God, which, at the same time, is a mirror of his glory."
3. "And if the life of those men is an ornament to the Christian name, much more let those who are in honor take care that they do not stain it by their baseness."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Not purloining... in middle to set apart for oneself, to embezzle..." 
2. "Nowhere else in the N.T. do we have αγαθη with πιστις as here..." 
3. "Paul shows slaves how they may 'adorn' the teaching of God."

### John Gill:
1. "Not purloining..... Or stealing, embezzling their master's substance, taking away, and making use of what is their property, keeping back part of money or goods committed to their trust."
2. "That they may adorn the doctrine of God our Saviour in all things; Christ is our alone Saviour, and he is truly and properly God, and so fit and able to be a Saviour; and the Gospel is his doctrine, not only what he himself preached, when on earth, but it is a doctrine concerning him."
3. "This is what becomes the Gospel of Christ, throws a beauty upon it, and is ornamental to it; and in this way the doctrine of Christ may be, and ought to be, adorned by servants, as well as others."

### John Wesley:
1. "Showing all good fidelity - Soft, obliging faithfulness."
2. "That they may adorn the doctrine of God our Saviour - More than St. Paul says of kings."
3. "How he raises the lowness of his subject! So may they, the lowness of their condition."
