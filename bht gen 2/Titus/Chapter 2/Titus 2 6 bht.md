# Titus 2:6 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
To be discreet—to exercise good judgment and self-control, which is a virtue that is not often seen in young people. It requires wisdom and prudence to make sound decisions and to refrain from impulsive and reckless behavior.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The younger men in like manner exhort to be self-restrained, showing thyself in all matters an example of good works, in thy teaching incorruption, gravity, a discourse healthy, not to be condemned, that he of the opposite part may be ashamed."
2. "It may be objective of the contents of the teaching that it should set forth purity as its character and aim: or subjective, that he should be, in his teaching, pure in motive, uncorrupted."
3. "Having nothing to say of us evil, no evil thing to report of us, no evil, whether seen in our demeanour, or arising from our teaching."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:

	younger men." 
	sober-minded—self-restrained
	[ALFORD]. "Nothing is

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They should be 'steady in their behaviour, superior to sensual temptations, and constant in the exercise of every part of self-government.'"
2. "A young man who cannot govern himself gives little promise of being useful or happy."
3. "Indulgence in the propensities to which young men are prone will, sooner or later, bring ruin to the body and the soul."

### John Calvin:
1. "Temperance, as Plato shows, cures the whole understanding of man."
2. "Let them be well regulated and obedient to reason."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Titus 2:6 sums up in the same comprehensive term the peculiar duty of the Christian young man - the opposite being the defect of character conspicuous in his class."
2. "Also, the special sin of heathenism lay in the excessive indulgence of natural desires, on which heathen philosophy had striven in vain to apply a curb."
3. "Wisdom requires a similar restraint or balance in the formation and the holding of intellectual opinions."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The younger men. Just one item, besides 'likewise', 'to be soberminded'."
2. "It is possible to take 'in all things' with 'to be soberminded', though the editors take it with verse Titus 2:7."

### John Gill:
1. "Temperate, chaste, modest, moderate, wise, and prudent in all things."
2. "Proper and convenient for aged women to teach the young women how they should behave and conduct themselves."

### John Wesley:
1. To be discreet — A virtue rarely found in youth.
