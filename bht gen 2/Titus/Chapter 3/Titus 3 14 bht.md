# Titus 3:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Let our people learn and be characterized by good works of all kinds, including acts of charity and honest toil, so that they may bear fruit to God's praise. It is not enough to have knowledge or discuss theories; we are called to excel in doing good and serving one another. Let us support and contribute to the needs of our fellow believers, showing that our faith is not in vain, but produces a tangible positive impact on ourselves and society.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Moreover, let also our people learn to practise good works, contributions to the necessary wants which arise, that they may not be unfruitful."
2. "The contrast in the ‘but’ is, 'and I will not that thou only shouldest thus forward them, though I use the singular number; but see that the other brethren also join with thee in contributing to their outfit.'"
3. "Implying, that in the supply by us of such necessary wants, our ordinary opportunities are to be found of bearing fruit to God’s praise."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Not only thou, but let others also of 'our' fellow believers with thee."
2. "Supply the necessary wants of Christian missionaries and brethren, according as they stand in need in their journeys for the Lord's cause."
3. "A lover of hospitality."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He [Paul] here adds that he wished that others who were Christians would be characterized by good works of all kinds." 
2. "It rather means, that they should be distinguished for good works, including benevolent deeds, acts of charity, honest toil, and whatever would enter into the conception of an upright life."
3. "That it may be seen that their religion is not barren and worthless, but that it produces a happy effect on themselves and on society."

### John Calvin:
1. "He contrasts this with the foolish presumption but too common among those who thought that they were clever men, when they had speculated on this and the other subject... You have fine speculations, says he, but yet consider what is the true excellence of the children of God; it is to show that they have profited well in doing good."
2. "Henceforth you must excel in doing good, and not in rambling talk. Instead of being led by curiosity and ambition, let every man be employed in doing good to his neighbors."
3. "Let every man consider what is his ability; and according to the power which God has given us, let us serve one another. Thus shall we show that it is not in vain that we have received the gospel."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Not Titus alone, but the Cretan brethren were to share in this fitting out of the two travellers."
2. "It would be an exercise in Christian beneficence and a lesson to 'practise' similar 'good works,' as often as 'necessary wants' arose."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Our people (ο ημετερο). 'Our folks.' The Cretan converts, not just Paul's friends."
2. "Let learn (μανθανετωσαν). Present active imperative, keep on learning how."
3. "For necessary uses (εις αναγκαιας χρειας). 'For necessary wants.' No idlers wanted."

### John Gill:
1. "By which are not only meant honest trades... The Jews say that he that does not teach his son a trade, it is all one as if he taught him to rob or steal."
2. "But though this may be part of the sense of these words, it is not the whole of it; nor are acts of beneficence to the poor of Christ... only intended; but all good works in general."
3. "To maintain them, is to endeavour to outdo others in them... and to be constant in the performance of them."

### John Wesley:
1. "And let ours... Learn." 
2. "Both by thy admonition... they ought to have done."
