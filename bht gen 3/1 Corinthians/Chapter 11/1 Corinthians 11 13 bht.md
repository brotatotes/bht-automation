# 1 Corinthians 11:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Judge in yourselves: is it seemly that a woman pray unto God (in public) unveiled? By rejecting the emblem of subjection (the head-covering), she passes at one leap in praying publicly beyond both the man and angels. Paul appeals to the sense of propriety among the Corinthians, asking them to judge for themselves if it is decent and becoming for a woman to pray to God with her head uncovered. He leaves it with them, confident that upon reflection, they will recognize the importance of this symbol of modesty in Christian assemblies.

### John Gill:
1. "Judge in yourselves... suggesting that the thing was so clear, and he so certain of what he had advanced being right, that he leaves it with them, not doubting but that they would, upon a little reflection within themselves, join with him in this point."
