## BHT:
In coming together to eat, Paul instructs the brethren to wait for one another, contrasting with the previously condemned hurry. This waiting signifies sharing and participating in the common feast of fellowship, emphasizing the New Testament sense of expectancy and common courtesy. By waiting in turn and avoiding the despising of poorer guests, the brethren exemplify equality, unity, and Christian respect in the Lord's Supper. This practice of waiting for one another fosters brotherly love, encourages the church to gather together, and promotes a sense of togetherness in the ordinance.
