# 1 Corinthians 11:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The order of creation is highlighted, emphasizing that man was created before woman, and that the woman was created for the sake of the man. This parallels the relationship between Christ and the Church, with the woman representing the Church, the bride, created to fulfill her purpose and bring glory to both herself and her Creator. The woman was made to be a companion, helper, and comforter for the man, not to be a slave or solely for his pleasure. Her role is honorable, although subordinate, as she was created to aid and support the man.

### Henry Alford:

2. "The manner of creation, out of the man, is insisted on."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The man was not created for the sake of the woman; but the woman for the sake of the man."
2. "Just as the Church, the bride, is made for Christ; and yet in both the natural and the spiritual creations, the bride, while made for the bridegroom, in fulfilling that end, attains her own true 'glory.'"
3. "The bride brings 'shame' and 'dishonor' on herself by any departure from fulfilling her purpose."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The woman was made for the comfort and happiness of the man."
2. "Not to be a slave, but a help-meet; not to be the minister of his pleasures, but to be his aid and comforter in life."
3. "Her rank is therefore honorable, though it is subordinate."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "For the woman. Because of the woman." 
2. "The record in Genesis gives the man as the origin of the woman." 
3. "The reason for the creation of woman."

### John Gill:
1. "Neither was the man created for the woman...he was made for God, for his service and glory." 
2. "but the woman for the man...to be a companion and associate of his."
3. "and for the procreation and education of children."
