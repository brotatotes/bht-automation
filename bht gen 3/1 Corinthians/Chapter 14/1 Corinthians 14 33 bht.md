# 1 Corinthians 14:33 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
God, the God of peace, requires assemblies conducted in His name to be peaceful and orderly. This proper conclusion calls for orderly reverence as a mark of churches. An atmosphere of quarrel does not align with God's reign, as he desires a peace characterized by the truth of God as its bond. In all the churches of the saints, women are expected to maintain silence, emphasizing the significance of peace and order among His people.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "He is the God of peace, not confusion: therefore those assemblies which are His, must be peacefully and orderly conducted."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "In all the churches of the saints God is a God of peace; let Him not among you be supposed to be a God of confusion." 
2. "As in all churches of the saints, let your women keep silence in your churches."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "God is not the author of confusion. His religion cannot tend to produce disorder. It is calm, peaceful, thoughtful."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Confusion. See on commotions, Luke 21:9; and unruly, James 3:8. Compare 2 Corinthians 6:5."
2. "As in all the churches of the saints."
3. "The old arrangement is retained by Rev. and by Westcott and Hort, though the latter regard the words and the spirits - of peace as parenthetical."

### John Calvin:
1. "Whenever, therefore, there is a disposition to quarrel, there, it is certain, God does not reign."
2. "We must understand by this a peace of which the truth of God is the bond."
3. "For accursed is that peace of which revolt from God is the bond, and blessed are those contentions by which it is necessary to maintain the kingdom of Christ."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "God is not a God of confusion, but of peace." 
2. "As in all the churches of the saints and so surely in yours."
3. "Let the women keep silence, etc."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "God is not a God of disorder, but of peace. We need this reminder today."
2. "Orderly reverence is a mark of the churches."
3. "This is a proper conclusion of his argument."

### John Gill:
1. "God is not the author of confusion."
2. "He is the author of peace, harmony, unity, and concord among his prophets and teachers."
3. "God is the God of peace, he calls for, requires, disposes, and approves of peace and order among all his people."
