## BHT:
To show them that their hopes of salvation depended on themselves and not their teachers, the Corinthians were urged to watch, stand, and be strong. They were slumbering, wavering, and showing themselves as cowards. Engaged in strife, they needed to be vigilant against surrounding evils and hold fast to their faith. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "To shew them that not on their teachers but on themselves depended their hopes of salvation." 
2. "Watch, for that they were sleeping. Stand, for that they were wavering. Quit you like men, be strong, for they were shewing themselves cowards." 
3. "Let all you do be done in love, for that they were in strife."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Watch ye": for ye are slumbering. "Stand": for ye are like men tottering. "Quit you like men; be strong": for ye are effeminate. 
2. "Let all your things be done with charity": not with strifes as at present. 
3. "In the faith" which was assailed by some.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They were to watch, or be vigilant, against all the evils of which he had admonished them... They were to watch with the same vigilance that is required of a sentinel who guards a camp, lest an enemy should come suddenly upon them, and surprise the camp when the army was locked in sleep."
2. "Be firm in holding and defending the truths of the gospel... Be firm in maintaining what you believe to be true, and in holding on to your personal confidence in God, notwithstanding all the arts, insinuations, and teachings of seducers and the friends of false doctrine."
3. "It means, to render one manly or brave; to show oneself a man; that is, not to be a coward, or timid, or alarmed at enemies, but to be bold and brave."

### John Calvin:
1. "He exhorts them to watch, in order that Satan may not oppress them, finding them off their guard. For as the warfare is incessant, the watching requires to be incessant too."
2. "The second thing is that they persevere in the faith, or that they hold fast the faith, so as to stand firm; because that is the foundation on which we rest."
3. "In the third exhortation, which is much of the same nature, he stirs them up to manly fortitude."

### Philip Schaff:

2. "In the Greek of the Old Testament we find these words more than once together (Psalms 27:14; Psalms 31:24)."
