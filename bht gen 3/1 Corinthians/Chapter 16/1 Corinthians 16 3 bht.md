# 1 Corinthians 16:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
To ensure a reputable and reliable administration, the Corinthians were advised to select trustworthy messengers who would be accompanied by letters of recommendation as they traveled with Paul to Jerusalem. This practice was typical for Paul, who often wrote letters to both churches and individuals. Providing letters served as an introduction and recommendation, ensuring that the messengers would be respected and treated well by the church in Jerusalem. By offering his own services, Paul demonstrated his commitment to the cause, underscoring the gracious and free nature of the Corinthians' charitable gift.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "See, how so great a man is unwilling to open any chink to suspicion."
2. "Letters, plural - meaning, either that each should have his letter of credentials, or more probably, that Paul would give them letters to several persons in Jerusalem."
3. "Hence we see how common in Paul's practice was the writing of Epistles. Who knows how many private letters of his, not addressed to churches, have been lost?"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Whomsoever ye shall approve, them will I send with letters": namely, letters to several persons at Jerusalem, which would be their credentials.
2. "by letters"; an abbreviated expression for "I will send, recommending them by letters" [GROTIUS].
3. "gracious or free gift" [2 Corinthians 2:4].

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Whomsoever ye shall approve by your letters...whom you approve, or designate, them I will send with letters to convey your charity to Jerusalem."
2. "For it is evident (see 1 Corinthians 16:4) that, though Paul was willing to go, yet he was not expecting to go. If he did not go, what was more natural than that he should offer to give them letters of commendation to his brethren in Judea?"
3. "The proper construction, therefore, is, that Paul would give them letters to his friends in Jerusalem, and certify their appointment to dispense the charity, and commend the persons sent to the favor and hospitality of the church there."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Approve by your letters." So A. V. and Rev. Others, however, connect by letters with will I send, making the letters to be Paul 's introduction to the church at Jerusalem. The latter is preferable.
2. "The givers are to choose the bearers of the collection."
3. "Paul, as the originator and apostolic steward of the collection, will send the money."

### John Calvin:
1. "He points out to the Corinthians a method, by which they may be assured of a good and faithful administration."
2. "Nay more, he offers his own services, if desired, which is an evidence that he has the matter at heart."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "When I arrive, whenever I arrive..."
2. "To make it formal and regular and Paul would approve their choice of messengers to go with him to Jerusalem."
3. "As a matter of fact, the messengers of the churches went along with Paul to Jerusalem."

### John Gill:
1. "which approbation and choice they would signify by letters to the church, and principal men of it in Jerusalem, giving them a character as men of probity and faithfulness."
2. "such as the church should choose for this service, the apostle would send with letters of commendation from him, to the elders and church at Jerusalem, recommending them as brethren in the Lord, and to be had in respect, and treated in a Christian manner by them."
3. "because it was owing to the goodness of God, that they were in a capacity to contribute to others, and to the grace of God that they had a heart to do it; and because it was in a free and gracious manner, and in the exercise of grace, of faith in Christ, and love to the saints, that they did it, and with a view to the glory of the grace of God, of which this was a fruit and evidence."
