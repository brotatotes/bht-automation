# 1 Corinthians 16:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Paul speaks of a great and effectual door that has been opened for him, offering him a significant opportunity for effective work. This door represents the wide and hopeful field of missionary usefulness in Ephesus. However, along with this great opportunity, Paul acknowledges the presence of many adversaries, both from the Jewish and Gentile communities. Nevertheless, Paul is not discouraged by opposition, recognizing that where there is great good, evil is sure to arise. These adversaries serve as a reason for Paul to stay in Ephesus, defending the Gospel and strengthening the minds of believers.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "door, see reff.: an opportunity of action, great refers to the extent of the action thus opened before him"
2. "effectual to its requirements: neither of them (though 'great' may be referred to a door) properly agreeing with the figure, but both with the reality."
3. "many adversaries"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Wise men are on the watch for, and avail themselves of, opportunities." 
2. "Not here false teachers, but open adversaries: both Jews and heathen." 
3. "Where great good is, there evil is sure to start up as its antagonist."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "There is abundant opportunity for usefulness."
2. "It was regarded by him as an evidence that the Holy Spirit was there."
3. "Ministers should not be discouraged because there is opposition to the gospel. It is one ground of encouragement."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Great and effectual door. Door metaphorically for opportunity : great as to its extent; effectual as to the result." 
2. "The figure of an effectual door, as it stands, is of course clumsy, but the idea as a whole is clear : a great opportunity for effective work."

### John Calvin:
1. "For the Lord opened up a way for him for the furtherance of the gospel."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "a great door and effectual is opened unto me, and there are many adversaries."
2. "He is eager to enter in on a wide and most hopeful field of missionary usefulness, nothing daunted by the resistance expected."
3. "But the Gentile adversaries were at Ephesus even more formidable; when, encountering the 'worshippers of the great goddess Diana,' he was like to be torn in pieces by the 'beasts at Ephesus.'"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "A wide open door."
2. "Paul means that he has at least a great opportunity for work in Ephesus."
3. "These Paul mentions as a reason for staying in, not for leaving, Ephesus."

### John Gill:
1. "For a great door....it seems rather to intend the desire that there appeared in many persons here to have the Gospel preached unto them...and great numbers believed."
2. "And effectual is opened to me...the door of faith being opened by him, it was effectual to the quickening of sinners dead in trespasses and sins, to the enlightening of blind eyes, unstopping of deaf ears, and softening hard hearts."
3. "and there are many adversaries; as there always are where the Gospel is preached...all which he had some foreviews of, and found to be true by experience...was a reason with him to stay; to bear his testimony to the Gospel, to appear in the defence of it, against the disputers of this world, and to strengthen and establish the minds of weak believers in it."

### John Wesley:
1. "A great door...and effectual." 
2. "And there are many adversaries."
