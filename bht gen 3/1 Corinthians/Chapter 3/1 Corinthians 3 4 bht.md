# 1 Corinthians 3:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In the context of the Corinthians' sectarianism, Paul challenges their divisive mindset, particularly their allegiances to him and Apollos. He describes their unregenerate nature as being carnal or mere men, not guided by the Spirit. Instead of defending his own followers, Paul emphasizes the need for unity and reminds them that they are merely servants, not leaders of factions. Their envy, strife, and divisions are evidence of their carnal state and hinder the success of the gospel. Thus, Paul urges them to reject division, even if it benefits him or his close associates.
