# 1 Corinthians 6:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The apostle Paul ironically criticizes the Corinthians for taking their trivial disputes to the unbelieving judges. In his scathing tone, Paul questions their choice and suggests that even the least esteemed members of the church would be better qualified to judge such matters. By involving pagan magistrates, the Corinthians not only overlook the insignificance of earthly property disputes but also underestimate the role of Christians as judges over the world and angels. It is clear that Paul disapproves of Christians seeking worldly judgments rather than resolving conflicts within the church.
