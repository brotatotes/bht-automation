# 1 Corinthians 9:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The apostle Paul stressed that the reason he made a point of boasting about preaching the gospel without charge was because his mission itself gave him no advantage in this regard, being an office entrusted to him and for which he was accountable. He wanted to make it clear that his preaching was not a matter of distinction, but rather the work of a Christian missionary. The necessity and divine call to preach laid upon him removed any grounds for glorying, as he could do no other and deserved no credit for it.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The reason why he made so much of this matter of boasting, viz. that his mission itself gave him no advantage this way, being an office entrusted to him, and for which he was solemnly accountable."
2. "But in this thing only had he an advantage to be able to boast of it, that he preached the gospel without charge."
3. "The English reader must beware not to ion 'preach the gospel,' as if it made a distinction between preaching 'the gospel' and preaching something else: it simply represents the word 'evangelize,' i.e. perform the work of a Christian missionary."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "If I preach the Gospel, and do so not gratuitously, I have no matter for 'glorying.'" 
2. "The 'necessity' that is laid on me to preach does away with ground for 'glorying.'"
3. "The sole ground for the latter that I have, is my preaching without charge."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "But all idea of 'glorying,' or of 'reward,' must be connected with some voluntary service - something which would show the inclination, disposition, desire of the soul."
2. "My preaching is in a manner inevitable, and cannot therefore be regarded as that in which I especially glory."
3. "I should be miserable and wretched if I did not preach. My preaching, therefore, in itself considered, cannot be a subject of glorying."

### John Calvin:
1. "Let not the man, then, who has been once called to it, imagine that he is any longer at liberty to withdraw when he chooses, if, perhaps, he is harassed with vexatious occurrences, or weighed down with misfortunes, for he is devoted to the Lord and to the Church, and bound by a sacred tie, which it were criminal to break asunder."
2. "As to the second point, he says that a curse was ready to fall upon him, if he did not preach the gospel... What sort of successors, then, have the Apostles in the Pope and the other mitred bishops, who think that there is nothing that is more unbecoming their station, than the duty of teaching!"
3. "How, therefore, will anyone who succeeds to his office avoid this necessity?"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "To glory in preaching without charge is reasonable enough, but to boast of preaching the Gospel itself were shameful."
2. "Necessity is upon me; for woe is unto me if I preach not the gospel."
3. "Shut up to it by the call of Heaven, it is at my peril if I shrink from it."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "For necessity is laid upon me... He could do no other and deserves no credit for doing it."
2. "Woe is me... Paul had to heed the call of Christ that he had heard. He had a real call to the ministry."
3. "Would that this were the case with every modern preacher."

### John Gill:
1. "Though he preached the Gospel ever so well, or ever so freely, and might glory before men...yet not before God, from whom he received all his gifts, abilities, and qualifications, to preach the Gospel." 
2. "Necessity is laid upon me...of obligation from the divine call to this work, and from his own conscience, in which he knew it to be a heavenly one, and from the nature of the trust committed to him."
3. "Woe is unto me if I preach not the Gospel...to show what he or any other minister of the Gospel would deserve, at the hand of God, who having abilities to preach, should not make use of them."
