# 1 John 1:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
True confession of sin requires an external spoken acknowledgment and genuine inward contrition. God's faithfulness and justice are manifested in the forgiveness and cleansing of sins, leading to complete justification and sanctification. Confessing sin to God and to one another is a recurring theme in the New Testament. Forgiveness is not about justice, but about God's mercy, available to those who repent, believe, and sincerely confess. Through Christ's sacrifice, forgiveness of sin is immediate and comprehensive, while confessing with a penitent and believing heart guarantees God's faithful forgiveness and cleansing from all unrighteousness.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Pardon in the Scriptures always supposes that there is confession, and there is no promise that it will be imparted unless a full acknowledgment has been made."
2. "If it were an act of justice it could be demanded or enforced, and that is the same as to say that it is not forgiveness, for in that case there could have been no sin to be pardoned."
3. "No one can come to God without finding him ready to do all that is appropriate for a God to do in pardoning transgressors; no one who will not, in fact, receive forgiveness if he repents, and believes, and makes confession."

### John Calvin:
1. "It is of great moment to be fully persuaded, that when we have sinned, there is a reconciliation with God ready and prepared for us: we shall otherwise carry always a hell within us."
2. "The more, then, it becomes us to receive with the whole heart this promise which offers free pardon to all who confess their sins."
3. "But this confession, as it is made to God, must be in sincerity; and the heart cannot speak to God without newness of life. It then includes true repentance."

### Archibald T. Robertson:

### John Gill:
1. "Confession of sin is to be made to God, against whom it is committed, and who only can pardon."

3. "Forgiveness of sin springs from the tender mercies of our God, and is both an act of justice and of mercy."
