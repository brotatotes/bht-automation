### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The first thing that the little children realize is that God is their Father; answering in the parallel clause to 'little sons . . . your sins are forgiven you for His name's sake,' the universal first privilege of all those really-dear sons of God."
2. "The fathers are characterized as reverting back to the first great truths of spiritual childhood, and the sum and ripest fruit of advanced experience, the knowledge of Him that was from the beginning."
3. "Many of them had probably known Jesus in person, as well as by faith."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The thing to which John appeals here is the energy of those at this period of life, and it is proper at all times to make this the ground of appeal in addressing a church."
2. "Thus, the apostle calls on the three great classes into which a community or a church may be considered as divided: youth, because their sins were already forgiven, and, though young, they had actually entered on a career of virtue and religion, a career which by all means they ought to be exhorted to pursue; 'fathers', or aged men, because they had had long experience in religion, and had a thorough acquaintance with the doctrines and duties of the gospel, and they might be expected to stand steadfastly as examples to others; and 'young men', those who were in the vigor and prime of life, because they had shown that they had power to resist evil, and were endowed with strength, and it was proper to call on them to exert their vigor in the sacred cause of religion."
3. "Even children may learn to regard God as their Father, and may have toward him all the affectionate interest which grows out of this relation."
