# 1 John 3:24 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Keeping God's commandments is the key to abiding in Him and having Him abide in us. This mutual indwelling is signified by the Holy Spirit, given to us by God Himself, who confirms that He abides in us. The divine seed, the new life within believers, is incompatible with sin and demonstrates our divine sonship. The Gospel aims to foster love and counters the spirit of Cain. By abiding in God and allowing God to abide in us, we become a dwelling place for Him.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Thus he returns to the great keynote of the Epistle, abide in Him, with which the former part concluded."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The Holy Spirit is imparted to his people to enlighten their minds; to elevate their affections; to sustain them in times of trial; to quicken them in the performance of duty; and to imbue them with the temper and spirit of the Lord Jesus." 
2. "When these effects exist, we may be certain that the Spirit of God is with us; for these are the 'fruits' of that Spirit, or these are the effects which he produces in the lives of men."
3. "No man can be a true Christian in whom that Spirit does not constantly dwell, or to whom he is not 'given.' And yet no one can determine that the Spirit dwells in him, except by the 'effects' produced in his heart and life."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Therefore let God be a home to thee, and be thou the home of God: abide in God, and let God abide in thee." 
2. "The divine seed - the divine principle of growth - the germ of the new life is in the true believer; and the ideas of divine sonship and sin are mutually exclusive." 
3. "The whole aim of the Gospel is the creation and strengthening of love; and the type of life in God through Christ is therefore the direct opposite of Cain, who being of the evil one, slew his brother."

### John Calvin:
