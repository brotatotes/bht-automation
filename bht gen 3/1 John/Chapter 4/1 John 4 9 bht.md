# 1 John 4:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
God's grand proof of love is seen in His act of sending His one and only Son, who is the Life and has redeemed us. This sending not only demonstrates God's love, but also motivates us to love one another. The incarnation, described by John as a sending, has abiding results and brings forth life. Jesus, being the only begotten Son, perfectly represents God's nature. Through His coming, we are able to experience abundant life even in the present.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "It is the grand proof of God's love, His having sent 'His only-begotten Son, that we might live through Him,' who is the Life, and who has redeemed our forfeited life." 
2. "It is also the grand motive to our mutual love."
3. "Otherwise, too, He could not have been our life, our 'propitiation,' or our 'Saviour'."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "In this was manifested the love of God... this was a most signal proof of it."
2. "He died that we might have eternal life through the merits of his sacrifice."
3. "All these things will magnify themselves as we draw near to eternity; and in that eternity to which we go, whether saved or lost, we shall have an ever-expanding view of the wonderful love of God."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "John describes the incarnation as a sending, more frequently than in any other way."
2. "The perfect tense, hath sent, points to the abiding results of the sending."
3. "A mode of expression common in John, enlarging upon the meaning of a noun by the addition of an adjective or a participle with the article."

### John Calvin:
1. "Christ, then, is so illustrious and singular a proof of divine love towards us, that whenever we look upon him, he fully confirms to us the truth that God is love."
2. "He calls him his only begotten, for the sake of amplifying. For in this he more clearly shewed how singularly he loved us, because he exposed his only Son to death for our sakes."
3. "He expresses the end for which Christ has been sent by the Father, even that we may live through him, for without him we are all dead, but by his coming he brought life to us."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The Incarnation as in 1 John 3:5."
2. "Jesus alone completely reproduces the nature and character of God."
3. "This life begins here and now."

### John Gill:
1. "The love of God here spoken of, and instanced in, is not his general love to all his creatures, but his special love towards his elect."
2. "This is a most flagrant and notorious instance of [God's love], in which it is exceedingly conspicuous; this is a most clear proof, a plain and full demonstration of it."
3. "Christ came, being sent, that they might have life, and that more abundantly than Adam had in innocence, or man lost by the fall."
