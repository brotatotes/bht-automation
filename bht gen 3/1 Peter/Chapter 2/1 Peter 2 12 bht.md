# 1 Peter 2:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Lead an upright and consistent life, even when faced with persecution and the criticism of your enemies. Let your good works serve as a testament to the truth of your faith, causing others to glorify God. In this day of visitation, when God offers His mercy and grace, ensure that your conduct reflects your heavenly position. Though it may be initially met with hatred and raillery, your virtuous behavior can ultimately move even the heathen to see the goodness of God in you.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "That conduct, which was to them an occasion of speaking against you as evil-doers, may by your good works become to them an occasion of glorifying God."
2. "Often the Christians would be compelled to diverge from heathen customs and even to break human laws, and thus would incur the imputation of malefactors."
3. "Glorify God in [the] day of visitation (i.e. the day when God visits... mankind with His offers of mercy and grace)."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Our conversation should correspond to our Saviour's condition; this is in heaven, so ought that to be." 
2. "A good walk does not make us pious, but we must first be pious and believe before we attempt to lead a good course."
3. "The very works 'which on more careful consideration, must move the heathen to praise God, are at first the object of hatred and raillery."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Lead upright and consistent lives." 
2. "It should be true that at the very time when the enemies of religion reproach us, they should see that we are actuated by Christian principles, and that in the very matter for which we are reproached we are conscientious and honest." 
3. "The truth taught here is, 'that our conduct as Christians should be such as to bear the strictest scrutiny; such that the closest examination will lead our enemies to the conviction that we are upright and honest.'"

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Wherein; in the matter in which. They speak against... evil-speakings." (Commentary)
2. "Evil-doers... applied by the priests to Christ himself." (Commentary)
3. "Visiting grows naturally out of this, as visitare from visere, to look at attentively... The 'day of visitation' is the day of looking upon." (Commentary)

### John Calvin:
1. "The more carefully, therefore, ought they to have labored to wipe off the odium and infamy attached to their name by a holy life and a well-regulated conduct."
2. "Therefore the evil speakings and the wicked insinuations of the ungodly ought to stimulate us to lead an upright life; for it is no time for living listlessly and securely, when they sharply watch us in order to find out whatever we do amiss."
3. "He intimates that we ought thus to strive, not for our own sake, that men may think and speak well of us; but that we may glorify God, as Christ also teaches us."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Having your manner of life among the Gentiles seemly... so good, so fair and honourable, that even the Gentiles may confess its attractiveness."
2. "Make that a spectacle of good works which even prejudiced and hostile eyes shall be unable to contest."
3. "[The] exhortation is not to isolate themselves, but to be careful of their behaviour in the sight of the heathen till they found a 'silent witness and ally' in the hearts of their calumniators themselves."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The Gentiles are on the watch for slips in moral conduct by the Christians."
2. "Heathen talk against us gleefully."
3. "Your good works... which they behold."

### John Gill:
1. "To have the conversation honest, is to provide things honest in the sight of men; to live and walk honestly before all; to do those things which are right and honest in the sight of God, and among men."
2. "Let the saints attend to all the duties of civil life, that when Heathen magistrates come to visit their several districts, and inquire and examine into the conduct of men, and seeing and finding that the Christians behave well and orderly, instead of persecuting them, they will bless God that they are such good subjects."
3. "That when wicked men take notice of and observe the good works of the saints, their civil, honest, and orderly conversation, they shall glorify God on that account, who has enabled them to perform them; and acknowledge the goodness of them, and the wrong judgment they have passed upon them, and the ill measure they have measured out to them."

### John Wesley:
1. "Honest - Not barely unblamable, but virtuous in every respect."
2. "That they by your good works which they shall behold - See with their own eyes."
3. "May glorify God - By owning his grace in you, and following your example."
