# 1 Peter 5:1 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Peter, as a fellow elder and witness of Christ's sufferings, urges the elders to willingly tend God's flock, serving as examples and not as lords. The use of the term fellow elder shows Peter's humility and refutes the idea of his primacy. By emphasizing his role as a witness, he encourages believers to endure suffering after Christ's example. The exhortation is not given in a commanding manner, but with a desire to transfer the impression of Christ's sufferings to inspire faithfulness. The elders will receive the crown of glory when the chief Shepherd is revealed.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The language which Peter uses is not that of stern and arbitrary command; it is that of kind and mild Christian exhortation."
2. "He does not put it on the ground of his apostolical authority; or urge it because he was the vicegerent of Christ; or because he was the head of the church; or because he had any pre-eminence over others in any way."
3. "The impression which Peter had of [the crucifixion] scene he would desire to have transferred to those whom he addressed, that by a lively view of the sufferings of their Saviour they might be excited to fidelity in his cause."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The expression is decisive against the primacy of Peter."
2. "The word is used in the New Testament to denote: a spectator or eye-witness, one who testifies to what he has seen, one who vindicates his testimony by suffering: a martyr."
3. "This use of the word, expressing a present realization of something not yet attained, occurs in no other writer in the New Testament."

### John Calvin:

3. "But we must observe that Peter confidently declares that he would be a partaker of that glory which was not yet revealed; for it is the character of faith to acquiesce in hidden blessings."
