# 1 Thessalonians 2:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Apostle Paul finds his hope and joy in the Thessalonians being found in the Lord at His appearing. Their conversion and salvation serve as grounds for his hope and a source of satisfaction for him. On the day of the Lord, the Thessalonians are Paul's boast and he is theirs, as their acceptance as true Christians brings him glory, joy, and pleasure. This hope of seeing his converts in Christ at the coming of Jesus is the chief hope of Paul, and their presence will be an honor to him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The Apostle... breaks off, and specifies that wherein this hope and joy mainly consisted, viz. the glorious prospect of their being found in the Lord at his appearing."
2. "He does not look forward to this as anticipating a reward for the conversion of the Thessalonians, or that their conversion will compensate for his having persecuted the Church before, but from generous desire to be found at that day with the fruits of his labour."
3. "They might be his boast and he theirs before the Lord."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Are not even ye our hope, joy, and crown of rejoicing before Jesus, when He shall come?"
2. "The 'hope' here meant is his hope (in a lower sense), that these his converts might be found in Christ at His advent."
3. "Paul's chief 'hope' was JESUS CHRIST."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The meaning, when he says that they were his 'hope,' is, that their conversion and salvation was one of the grounds of his hope of future blessedness."
2. "The source of joy here and in heaven."
3. "When the Lord Jesus appears at the end of the world, then our highest source of happiness and honor will be your conversion and salvation."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The Thessalonians were 'a chaplet of victory of which Paul might justly make his boast in the day of the Lord'." 
2. "The phrase crown of rejoicing or boasting." 
3. "Hope. Used of the object of hope."

### John Calvin:
1. "Unless I forget myself, I must necessarily desire your presence, for ye are our glory and joy."
2. "Let them therefore now learn to rejoice and glory in nothing but the prosperous issue of their labors, when they see that the glory of Christ is promoted by their instrumentality."
3. "The consequence will be, that they will be actuated by that spirit of affection to the Church with which they ought."

### Philip Schaff:

2. "The brightest point in our future is your acceptance as true Christians by the Lord Jesus Christ at His coming."
3. "The apostle felt thorough satisfaction in the Thessalonians as evidence that his labours had not been in vain."
