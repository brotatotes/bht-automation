# 1 Thessalonians 3:10 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Contemplating the depth of his prayers, Paul questions the adequacy of the thanks that can be rendered to God. He highlights the significance of night as a time of profound meditation and prayer for believers. Recognizing that even the Thessalonians had room for improvement, he zealously prays to mend and perfect any deficiencies in their faith. Overflowing with earnestness, Paul's prayers bring both comfort and excitement while fostering continuous growth and an unwavering pursuit of understanding God's truth.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "What thanks can we render, &c., proportioned to the earnestness of our prayers, &c.?"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Night is the season for the saint's holiest meditations and prayers."
2. "What thanks can we render to God while we pray?"
3. "Even the Thessalonians had points in which they needed improvement."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Night and day - Constantly."
2. "Praying exceedingly - Greek, abundantly; that is, there was much more than ordinary prayer."
3. "And might perfect that which is lacking in your faith - Might render it complete, or fill up anything which is missing."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Exceedingly [υπερεκπερισσου]. Comp. Ephesians 3:20. Paul is fond of compounds with uJper above. Of the 28 N. T. words compounded with uJper, 22 are found in Paul, and 20 of them only there."
2. "Perfect [καταρτισαι]. Primarily, to adjust, fit together; so mend, Matthew 4:21. Of the creation of the world, Hebrews 11:3. See on Matthew 21:16; Luke 6:40; 1 Peter 5:10; Romans 9:22."

### John Calvin:
1. "For it is never allowable for us to congratulate men, while they live in this world, in such unqualified terms as not always to desire something better for them."
2. "But from this we infer, that those who far surpass others are still far distant from the goal."
3. "When he says that he prayed night and day beyond all ordinary measure, we may gather from these words how assiduous he was in praying to God, and with what ardor and earnestness he discharged that duty."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The good tidings which Paul had received did not quench but rather intensified his desire to see them."
2. "From this intense longing we get a glimpse into the joys of the apostolic life."
3. "So long as Christians are in this life, there is room for growth, and those who have made greatest proficiency are precisely those who may most hopefully be stimulated to further attainment."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Like piling Ossa on Pelion, περισσως, abundantly, εκ περισσου, out of bounds, υπερεκπερισσου, more than out of bounds (overflowing all bounds)."
2. "And perfect (κα καταρτισα). First aorist active articular infinitive of purpose (εις το ιδειν--κα) of καταρτιζω, to mend nets (Matthew 4:21) or men (Galatians 6:1) repair. Chiefly late."
3. "That which is lacking in (τα υστερηματα). The shortcomings, the lacks or left-overs (Colossians 1:24). From υστερεω (υστερον), to be late."

### John Gill:
1. "This good news, not only comforted their hearts, and revived their spirits, and filled them with joy and thankfulness, but also sent them to the throne of grace to pray without ceasing." 
2. "They knew it was not perfect, there was unbelief attending them [...] and needed to be taught the way of God, and truths of the Gospel more perfectly."
3. "The ministry of the word is for the perfecting of the saints in the knowledge of the Son of God, and of other truths."

### John Wesley:
1. "Perfect that which is wanting in your faith."
2. "They who are once upon the rock no longer need to be taught by man."
