# 1 Thessalonians 4:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Remember the commandments delivered to you by the Lord Jesus Himself. The Apostle Paul appeals to their memory, using the term commandments to emphasize the divine authority with which he spoke. These commandments cover matters of faith, love, worship, and conduct. It is important to obey these instructions, as they come from the Lord Jesus Himself, marking His paramount authority. Implicit obedience is required, for those who know the Lord's will and do not follow it may face consequences.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "takes up the 'as ye received of us' of the former verse, and appeals to their memory in its confirmation."
2. "See similar appeals in Gal. iv. 13; 1 Cor. xv. 1."
3. "by the Lord Jesus] i.e. coming from Him, by His order and appointment."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "He uses the strong term, 'commandments,' in writing to this Church not long founded, knowing that they would take it in a right spirit, and feeling it desirable that they should understand he spake with divine authority."
2. "He seldom uses the term in writing subsequently, when his authority was established, to other churches."
3. "'The Lord' marks His paramount authority, requiring implicit obedience."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "For ye know what commandments - It was but a short time since Paul was with them, and they could not but recollect the rules of living which he had laid down."
2. "By the Lord Jesus - By the authority of the Lord Jesus."
3. "Some of those rules, or commandments, the apostle refers to, probably, in the following verses."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Commandments. Better, charges... often used in Class of military orders." 
2. "The verb paraggellein to command or charge is frequent." 
3. "Used in N. T. o LXX [Old Testament] four times."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "For ye know. I give you no new code of morals, but beseech you to live up to the instructions I formerly gave you." 
2. "I refer you to my original teaching, 'for ye know,' etc."
3. "By the Lord Jesus. It was the Lord Jesus who moved the apostle to deliver these commandments. Christ was the agent in the matter."
