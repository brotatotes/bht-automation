# 1 Thessalonians 5:21 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Believers are instructed to test all spiritual gifts and manifestations, discerning their true nature before accepting them. They should hold fast to what is good, embracing teachings that are in line with sound doctrine, even if they go against popular opinion. By examining everything according to the Word of God, believers show discernment and avoid being swayed by falsehood. This cautious approach distinguishes Christianity from other religions that demand blind acceptance. Ultimately, believers are encouraged to abstain from evil and embrace that which is edifying and aligns with the truth.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "This refers back to the foregoing: but try all (such spiritual gifts): see 1 Cor. xii. 10; xiv. 29; 1 John iv. 1." 
2. "Hold fast that which is good is best regarded as beginning a new sentence, and opposed to that which follows: not however as disconnected from the preceding, but suggested by it." 
3. "In this, and in all things, hold fast the good."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "You ought indeed not to 'quench' the manifestations of 'the Spirit,' nor 'despise prophesyings'; 'but,' at the same time, do not take 'all' as genuine which professes to be so; 'prove (test) all' such manifestations."
2. "The means of testing them existed in the Church, in those who had the 'discerning of spirits.'"
3. "As the result of your 'proving all things,' and especially all prophesyings, 'hold fast the good, and hold yourselves aloof from every appearance of evil.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Prove all things - Subject everything submitted to you to be believed to the proper test."
2. "Christianity does not require people to disregard their reason, or to be credulous."
3. "Other religions require their votaries to receive everything upon trust; Christianity asks us to examine everything."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Prove all things...a test be applied to phenomena which claim to be supernatural." 
2. "Hold fast that which is good...show yourselves approved money-changers."
3. "Prove all things, hold fast the good, abstain from every form of evil."

### John Calvin:
1. "Prove all things, meaning, that although all do not speak precisely according to set rule, we must, nevertheless, form a judgment, before any doctrine is condemned or rejected."
2. "Paul admonishes the Thessalonians to keep the middle path between these two extremes, while he prohibits them from condemning anything without first examining it; and, on the other hand, he admonishes them to exercise judgment, before receiving, what may be brought forward, as undoubted truth."
3. "For it does not become the pious to show such lightness, as indiscriminately to lay hold of what is false equally with what is true."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Prove all things... they were to put things to the proof, to test them." 
2. "The Thessalonians were to judge by the moral quality of the prophesyings, or of whatever else came before them."
3. "Having tested all things by their real character, they are to abstain from evil of every form."
