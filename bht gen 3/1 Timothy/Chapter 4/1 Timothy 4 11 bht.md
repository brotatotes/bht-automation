# 1 Timothy 4:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Command and teach these truths, rejecting useless and injurious teachings as you weigh them well. A minister of the Gospel is bound to teach that God is the Savior of all people and that salvation is provided for all. Give thanks for this glorious truth and proclaim it unceasingly. Emphasize these teachings, for they cannot be too much inculcated. Command believers to reject false doctrines, embrace true godliness, and labor and suffer for Christ and his Gospel, in hope of enjoying the promised salvation.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  11.] Command (see ch. i. 3) these things (viz. those insisted on since ver. 7) and teach them.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 11. These truths, to the
	exclusion of those useless and even injurious teachings ( :-), while weighing well thyself, charge also upon others.


### Albert Barnes:
1. "A minister of the gospel is solemnly bound to teach that there is a sense in which God is the Saviour of all people." 
2. "He is just as much bound to teach this, as he is that only those will be saved who believe."
3. "It is a glorious truth - and it is a thing for which a man should unceasingly give thanks to God that he may go and proclaim that He has provided salvation for all, and is willing that all should come and live."

### John Calvin:
1. "He means that the doctrine is of such a kind, that men ought not to be weary of it, though they heard it every day."
2. "There are, no doubt, other things to be taught; but there is emphasis in the demonstrative these; for it means that they are not things of small importance, of which it is enough to take a passing and brief notice; but, on the contrary, that they deserve to be repeated every day, because they cannot be too much inculcated."
3. "A prudent pastor ought, therefore, to consider what things are chiefly necessary, that he may dwell on them. Nor is there reason to dread that it shall become wearisome; for whosoever is of God will gladly hear frequently those things which need to be so often uttered."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The exhortation becomes more personal, as if the writer called to mind all that he had observed of the strength and weakness of his young disciple." 
2. "Command and teach. The first word points to single precepts and counsels, the latter to more systematic instruction."
3. "These things, i.e. the precepts of this chapter specially."

### John Gill:
1. "Command to refuse and reject all profane and fabulous doctrines, and exhort to the exercise of true godliness." 
2. "Teach the profitableness of true godliness, and declare the promises made unto it."
3. "Command, order, and encourage believers to labor and suffer reproach for the sake of Christ, and his Gospel, in hope of enjoying the said promises."
