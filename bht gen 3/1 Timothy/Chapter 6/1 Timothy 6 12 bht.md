# 1 Timothy 6:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Strive the good strife of the faith, contending and making the good confession that every servant of Christ must make. Fight the good fight, with such striving earnestness that you lay hold on the prize, eternal life. By calling this fight good, it encourages you with the assurance of success and animates you to persevere, knowing that God has called you and will not leave your labor fruitless. This fight of faith is a state of warfare, and as a soldier, do not entangle yourself with the things of this life.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Strive the good strife of the faith... the noble conflict which the faith entails on him." 
2. "Lay hold upon eternal life, to which thou wast called... while doing so, endeavor to attain."
3. "Confess the good confession of faith in Christ... the confession, which every servant of Christ must make."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Fight the good fight...the 'good profession' is connected with the good fight."
2. "Fight (literally, 'strive') with such striving earnestness as to lay hold on the prize, eternal life."
3. "didst confess THE good confession...the confession that Christ's kingdom is the kingdom of the truth."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Fight the good fight of faith - The noble conflict in the cause of religion."
2. "Seize this as eagerly as the competitors at the Grecian games laid hold on the prize."
3. "One of the best ways of stimulating ourselves or others to the faithful performance of duty, is the remembrance of the vows then made."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Fight the good fight. Not necessarily a metaphor from the gymnasium or arena, although a contest was applied originally to athletic struggles. But it is also used of any struggle, outward or inward."
2. "Lay hold. Frequent in Luke and Acts. Occasionally in this strong sense, but not usually."
3. "Professed a good profession. Rend. confessed the good confession. It is important to preserve the force of the article, a point in which the A. V. is often at fault."

### John Calvin:
1. "For the purpose of encouraging him to fight such a fight courageously, he calls it good; that is, successful, and therefore not to be shunned."
2. "When he commands them to 'lay hold on [eternal life],' he forbids them to pause or slacken in the middle of their course; as if he had said, that 'nothing has been done, till we have obtained the life to come, to which God invites us.'"
3. "To which also thou has been called... for there is nothing that ought to animate us with greater courage than to learn that we have been 'called' by God; for we conclude from this, that our labor, which God directs, and in which he stretches out his hand to us, will not be fruitless."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Fight the good fight of faith...the conflict of the faith." 
2. "Lay hold on eternal life...one vigorous act."
3. "Didst confess the good confession...something analogous to our Lord's good confession before Pilate."
