# 1 Timothy 6:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Believers can wisely lay up a treasure in heaven by distributing their earthly riches and giving generously to others. By scattering their wealth in faith, they can store up heavenly riches that continue beyond the unsubstantial goods of this fleeting life. The true life, the life that lasts forever, can be grasped by laying hold of eternal life through acts of benevolence and treasuring up heavenly rewards. As they set their affections on things above and seek the things that truly matter, believers secure a good foundation for themselves in the world to come.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "This is a treasure which we act wisely in laying up in store, whereas the wisest thing we can do with earthly treasures is 'to distribute' them and give others a share of them." 
2. "Earthly riches scattered in faith lay up in store a sure increase of heavenly riches. We gather by scattering." 
3. "The life that now is cannot be called so, its goods being unsubstantial, and itself a vapor. 'In order that ('with their feet so to speak on this foundation') they may lay hold on that which is life indeed.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The meaning of this verse is, that they were to make such a use of their property that it would contribute to their eternal welfare."
2. "It might be the means of exalted happiness and honor in heaven, if they would so use it as not to interfere with religion in the soul, and so as to do the most good possible."
3. "See the sentiment in this verse explained at length in the notes on Luke 16:9."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Laying up in store...Laying away...Eternal life...that which is truly life." 
2. "The life which is life indeed."
3. "That which is truly life."

### John Calvin:
1. "By bestowing and communicating, they will procure for themselves a better treasure than they can have on earth." 
2. "The spiritual riches which we 'lay up for ourselves' in heaven, are not exposed to the ravages of worms or thieves, or fires, but continue always to be placed beyond all danger."
3. "This recompense [...] does not depend on considerations of merit, but on God's gracious acceptance, and is so far from being inconsistent with the righteousness of faith, that it may be viewed as an appendage to it."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "We need not be startled at the apparent contradiction between this reference to good works as a foundation, and the language in which St. Paul elsewhere asserts that the one foundation is Christ (1 Corinthians 3:11)."
2. "Men do not commonly check their figurative speech by the rules of a rigid consistency."
3. "On the assumption of some acquaintance on St. Paul's part with our Lord's teaching, the language of Luke 6:48 would suggest the aspect of the figure now brought before us."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Laying up in store by giving it away."
2. "This life is merely the shadow of the eternal reality to come."

### John Gill:
