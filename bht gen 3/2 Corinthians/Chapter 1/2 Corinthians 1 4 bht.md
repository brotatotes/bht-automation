# 2 Corinthians 1:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The purpose of affliction in a preacher's life, in any Christian's life, is to equip them to be able to comfort others. By having a rich experience of divine consolation, ministers are able to impart consolation to others. The apostle Paul considered the graces given to him not for himself alone but for the greater ability to help others. Thus, the purpose of affliction is not for their own sake, but for the good of others, as they are fitted to comfort others in all their tribulations.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The apostle lived, not to himself, but to the Church."
2. "whatever graces God conferred on him, he considered granted not for himself alone, but that he might have the greater ability to help others."
3. "participation in all the afflictions of man peculiarly qualified Jesus to be man's comforter in all his various afflictions."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It is an object which he seeks, that his people in their afflictions should be supported and comforted; and for this purpose he fills the hearts of his ministers with consolation; gives them personal experience of the sustaining power of grace in their trials; and enables them to speak of what they have felt in regard to the consolations of the gospel of the Lord Jesus."
2. "By the same topics of consolation; by the same sources of joy which have sustained us."
3. "It is only by personal experience that we are able to impart consolation to others."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "In all our tribulation - in any trouble [...]. Note the nice use of the article : all our tribulation, collectively; any or every trouble, specifically."
2. "In is literally upon; the trouble forming the ground of the comfort. So in hope, Romans 4:18; Romans 5:2."
3. "We ourselves are comforted. An illustration of the personal character which pervades this epistle."

### John Calvin:
1. "As, however, the Apostle lived not for himself but for the Church, so he reckoned, that whatever favors God conferred upon him, were not given for his own sake merely, but in order that he might have more in his power for helping others."
2. "The riches of the Spirit, therefore, are not to be kept by us to ourselves, but every one must communicate to others what he has received."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Paul here gives the purpose of affliction in the preacher's life, in any Christian's life, to qualify him for ministry to others. Otherwise it will be professional and perfunctory."
2. "Personal experience of God's comfort is necessary before we can pass it on to others."
3. "The purpose of affliction is to equip us to be able to comfort others."

### John Gill:
1. "Who comforteth us in all our tribulation... by his own experience, and that of his fellow ministers... had much consolation and sweet refreshment administered to them by the presence of God with them."
2. "The end of this... was not so much on their own account... but for the good of others."
3. "And that they may be able to do so, that they may be fitted and furnished for so good a work, they are blessed with a rich experience of divine consolation in themselves."

### John Wesley:
1. "He that has experienced one kind of affliction is able to comfort others in that affliction." 
2. "He that has experienced all kinds of affliction is able to comfort them in all."
