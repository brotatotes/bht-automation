# 2 Corinthians 12:10 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In the midst of weaknesses and infirmities, the speaker finds contentment and pleasure, recognizing that it is through their limitations that they experience the power of Christ. Feeling weak, they receive a fresh access of power from Christ for the emergency. These challenges serve as exercises for discovering their own weakness, fostering self-distrust and humility. As they lean on the strength of Christ, they are upheld and enabled to triumph over adversities.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Wherefore (because of this relation to human weakness and divine power) I am well content (it is the same word as that used Matt. iii. 17, 'in whom I am well pleased') in infirmities..."
2. "...all coming also, as well as infirmities proper, under the category of infirmities, as hindrances and bafflings of human strength."
3. "...for whenever I am weak (applying to all five situations above), then I am mighty."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "I am well contented in."
2. "Two pairs of species follow, partly coming from 'Satan's messenger,' partly from men."
3. "Then especially in 'the power of Christ.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "There is often real pleasure in affliction, paradoxical as it may appear. Some of the happiest persons I have known are those who have been deeply afflicted; some of the purest joys which I have witnessed have been manifested on a sick-bed, and in the prospect of death."
2. "Philosophy blunts the feelings: infidelity leaves people to complain and repine in trial; the pleasures of this world have no power even to support or comfort in times of affliction; but Christianity furnishes positive pleasure in trial, and enables the sufferer to smile through his tears."
3. "When I feel weak; when I am subjected to trial, and nature faints and fails, then strength is imparted to me, and I am enabled to bear all. The more I am borne down with trials, the more do I feel my need of divine assistance, and the more do I feel the efficacy of divine grace."

### John Calvin:
1. "The more deficiency there is in me, so much the more liberally does the Lord, from his strength, supply me with whatever he sees to be needful for me."
2. "If a man is desirous to be truly strong, let him not refuse to be at the same time weak... Let him be weak in himself that he may be strong in the Lord."
3. "All these things are exercises for discovering to us our own weakness... a feeling of our own weakness, self-distrust, and humility."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "I take pleasure in weaknesses, in injuries ... for Christ's sake."
2. "I take pleasure in them for Christ's sake."
3. "When I am weak, then am I strong."
