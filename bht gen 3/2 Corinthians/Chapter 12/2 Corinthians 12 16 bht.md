# 2 Corinthians 12:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Granted that Paul did not burden the Corinthians himself, he cleverly caught them with guile, making a gain by sending others to secure money for himself. Crafty and skilful, Paul used guile as bait to catch the Corinthians, alluding to their charge that he used the collection for the poor for his personal gain. This serves as a warning to be cautious when choosing servants of God, as the wicked would impute their own actions onto them if given the opportunity.

### John Calvin:
1. "For it is customary for the wicked impudently to impute to the servants of God, whatever they would themselves do, if they had it in their power."

3. "Let his case, however, be a warning to us, not to look upon it as a thing that is new and intolerable, if at any time we find occasion to answer similar calumnies."
