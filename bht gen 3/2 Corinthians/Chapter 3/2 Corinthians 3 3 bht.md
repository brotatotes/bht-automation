# 2 Corinthians 3:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Corinthians serve as Christ's letter of recommendation, ministered by Paul, with the Spirit of the living God writing on their hearts instead of ink on stone tablets. Through their transformed hearts of flesh, the Corinthians embody Christ's message, attesting to Paul's faithfulness and the work of the Spirit. This epistle carries more weight than an inscription on stone, for only God can inscribe His truths on the human heart. Thus, the Corinthians become a living testament, allowing others to read Christ in them.

### Henry Alford:
1. "He is the Recommender of us, the Head of the church and sender of us His ministers."
2. "The Corinthians are his Epistle of recommendation, both to themselves and others; an Epistle, written by Christ, ministered by St. Paul."
3. "I bear on my heart, as a testimony to all men, that which Christ has by His Spirit written in your hearts."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Being manifestly shown to be an Epistle of Christ" - a letter coming manifestly from Christ, and "ministered by us."
2. "Not with ink, but with the Spirit of the living God" - Christ's Epistle (His believing members converted by Paul) is written with the "Spirit of the living God."
3. "On [your] hearts [which are] tables of flesh" - God has taken away the stony heart and given you a heart of flesh, making your hearts the tables on which His message is written.
