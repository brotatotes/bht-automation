# 2 Corinthians 4:1 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Because we have received mercy from God, we do not shrink back from our ministry. We renounce shameful actions and deceitful handling of God's word. Instead, we manifest the truth, commending ourselves to everyone's conscience. It is the mercy of God that makes us earnest and sincere in our ministry. We do not become faint or cowardly, for we have the same faith spirit. Just as Paul speaks of himself, we can all relate to the mercy and empowerment we receive from God to fulfill our ministry.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "For this cause, seeing we have this ministry, even as we received mercy, we shrink not back..."
2. "But we have renounced the hidden things of shame, not walking in craftiness, nor handling deceitfully the word of God..."
3. "But by the manifestation of the truth, recommending ourselves to every conscience of men in the sight of God."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Because we have the liberty-giving Spirit of the Lord, and with unveiled face behold His glory."
2. "The ministry of such a spiritual, liberty-giving Gospel."
3. "The sense of 'mercy' received from God, makes men active for God."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Because the light of the gospel is so clear; because it reveals so glorious truths, and all obscurity is taken away, and we are permitted to behold as in a mirror the glory of the Lord." 
2. "The gospel ministry, so much more glorious than that of Moses" 
3. "It is by the mere mercy and favor of God, that he had been entrusted with the ministry...that it was to be traced entirely to God that he was put into the ministry."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Having this ministry as a gift of divine mercy."
2. "The mercy of God, by which the ministry is received, makes us earnest and sincere."
3. "The mercy of God...makes us earnest and sincere."

### John Calvin:
1. "Having, says he, this ministry... that ministry, the excellence of which he had extolled in terms so magnificent, and the power and usefulness of which he had so abundantly shown forth." 
2. "Now there was more implied in making the mercy of God the reason of his Apostleship, than if he had attributed it to the grace of God." 
3. "We faint not... that is, we are not deficient in our duty, so as not to discharge it with fidelity."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "We faint not... the faint-hearted coward." 
2. "Paul speaks of himself... Can he not speak for all of us?"

### John Gill:
1. "we faint not...we go on cheerfully in our work, in the teeth of all opposition, encouraged by the consideration of the excellency of the ministry"
2. "the mercy of God they were partakers of...so influenced their minds, to hold forth the riches of abounding grace and mercy to poor sinners in the Gospel, that nothing could deter them from it"
3. "the grace, favour, and good will of God, in making, supporting, and continuing them as ministers of the word; all which, they were sensible, was owing not to men, but God"

### John Wesley:
1. "We desist not in any degree from our glorious enterprise."
2. "As we have received mercy - Have been mercifully supported in all our trials."
3. "Having this ministry - Spoken of, 2 Corinthians 3:6."
