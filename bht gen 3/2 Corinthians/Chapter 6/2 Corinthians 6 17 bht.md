# 2 Corinthians 6:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Believers are called to separate themselves from the pollutions of the world, as touching is more polluting than seeing. They are to resemble angels in their prompt and joyful obedience to God. By separating from the world, they become part of a pure community distinguished by moral purity. This echoes Isaiah's call to Israel to cast off defilement acquired from Babylon. Believers find a deep connection with God, surpassing earthly relationships, by keeping themselves undefiled as vessels dedicated to His worship. Christians must separate themselves, avoiding idolatry and evil customs, to experience reception into God's house and family.

### John Calvin:

3. "The result on a principle obvious enough is, not that the 'righteousness' of the one party dissolves the 'unrighteousness' of the other, but that the lower drags down the higher."
