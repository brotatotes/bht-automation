## BHT:
Titus, filled with zealous care, willingly accepted Paul's exhortation to go to the Corinthians. Paul believed that every inclination to serve God or benefit others was a gift from God. The act of benevolence shown by Titus towards the Corinthians was seen as an act of kindness done to them. ### Henry Alford:
1.  16.] The sense is taken up from ver. 6. the same, viz. as I have in my heart.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Which put the same earnest care for you into the heart of Titus, as was in myself."
2. "My care for you led me to 'desire' him, but Titus had of himself the same care."
3. "Titus accepted (gladly) my exhortation to go to you."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Paul regarded every right feeling, and every pure desire; every inclination to serve God or to benefit a fellow mortal, as the gift of God." 
2. "The earnest care here referred to was that the Corinthians might complete the collection, and finish what they had proposed. Titus was willing to undertake this, and see that it was done." 
3. "A man who presents to Christians a feasible object of benevolence, and who furnishes them an opportunity of doing good to others, is doing good to them, and they should esteem it an act of kindness done to them."

### John Calvin:
1. "There are no pious affections that do not proceed from the Spirit of God." 
2. "God's concern for his people, that he raises up ministers and guardians, to make it their endeavor to relieve their necessities."
3. "It is His special and peculiar work to raise up pastors."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "But thanks be to God, which putteth the same earnest care for you into the heart of Titus."
