# 2 Corinthians 8:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The brother appointed by the churches accompanied Paul and his companions in their act of love, showcasing the influence of religion in producing true benevolence. His appointment aimed to promote the glory of the Lord and reflect the readiness of Paul and his fellow believers to undertake this act. Chosen by the churches themselves, this brother's participation demonstrated their confidence and active involvement in this gracious endeavor, stirring up a generous spirit among the believers.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Praised in all the churches." 
2. "Appointed by the churches as our fellow-traveler." 
3. "To subserve the glory of the Lord and our readiness."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "not only praised in all the churches."
2. "these companions of Paul are called 'messengers of the churches'."
3. "in order to produce a 'readiness' on the part of Paul and the brother to undertake the office which each, by himself, would have been less ready to undertake, for fear of suspicions arising."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Chosen by the churches. Many concurred in the choice, showing that they had entire confidence in him."
2. "Paul had been unwilling to have charge of this contribution alone and he had procured the appointment of someone to undertake it."
3. "The design was to promote the glory of the Lord by showing the influence of religion in producing true benevolence."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The reference is, of course, to the contribution as a work of love; cariv being used in the sense of benefaction or bounty."
2. "Paul says that the brother was appointed as his fellow - traveller in the matter of this bounty; in the prosecution of this kindly act."
3. "For appointed, see on Acts 14:23; Acts 10:41."

### John Calvin:
1. "He says, that it tends to promote the glory of God, and their kindness of disposition." 
2. "Hence it comes, that these two things are conjoined - the glory of God and their liberality."
3. "There is, in addition to this, the labor of those distinguished men, which it were very inconsistent to reject or allow to pass unimproved."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "appointed by the churches to travel with us in the matter of this grace."
2. "ministered by us to the glory of the Lord (the Lord Jesus)."
3. "to show our readiness."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The idea is that this brother was chosen by the churches, not by Paul." (9 words)
2. "Only here in N.T. save Acts 14:23 where it means to appoint without notion of raising the hands." (18 words)
3. "Late word for travelling companion." (5 words)

### John Gill:
1. "This brother was chosen by the churches who collected, and not by the churches in Judea, for whom the collections were made."
2. "the bounty or beneficence of the churches, what they had freely and liberally contributed for the supply of the poor, from a principle of grace, and by the assistance of it"
3. "to stir up their ready mind to increase it and promote it in them, as well to make it manifest, and that it might appear to others, how readily and freely they came into this service"

### John Wesley:
1. "He was appointed by the churches." 
2. "With this gift."
3. "For the declaration of our ready mind."
