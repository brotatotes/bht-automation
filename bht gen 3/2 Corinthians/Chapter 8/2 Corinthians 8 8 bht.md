# 2 Corinthians 8:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
To induce the Corinthians, Paul uses the zeal of others as an instrument to produce the effect. He wants to test the genuineness of their love and prove its sincerity. Giving should be done out of sincerity and love, not by commandment. By giving generously, the Corinthians can demonstrate the sincerity of their love to God, the Redeemer, Paul, and the church. The collection is a real test of one's love, and Paul calls on them to show that they are not behind others in the outcome of their love.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Not as a command, but by way of inducement."
2. "He treats the zeal of others as the instrument by which, in the way of emulation, the effect was to be produced."
3. "To try the genuineness of their love."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "not by way of commandment."
2. "But by (mention of) the forwardness of others (as an inducement to you), and to prove the sincerity of your love."
3. "By means of the forwardness of others, proving the sincerity of your love ALSO."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "People cannot be induced to give to objects of charity by command, or by a spirit of dictation and authority. The only successful, as well as the only lawful appeal, is to their hearts and consciences, and sober judgments."
2. "I make use of the example of the churches of Macedonia as an argument to induce you to give liberally to the cause."
3. "Liberality in assisting the poor and afflicted people of God would be the best evidence of the sincerity of their love to God, to the Redeemer, to him, and to the church. Religion is love; and that love is to be manifested by doing good to all people as we have opportunity."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Sincerity...contracted from genhsiov legitimately born: hence genuine." 
2. "Paul calls Timothy his lawful son in the faith." 
3. "The kindred adverb gnhsiwv sincerely (A. V. naturally), occurs once."

### John Calvin:
1. "He nowhere binds us to circumstances of times, or persons, but calls us to take the rule of love as our guide."
2. "He does not command as if he reckoned that they required to be constrained by command and requirement, as though they refused to do their duty, unless shut up to it by necessity."
3. "He rather wished that all should have evidence of it."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "I speak not by way of commandment as laying my commands upon you to exercise your liberality..."
2. "...proving through the earnestness of others the sincerity also of your love..."
3. "...let it be seen that ye are not behind others in the outcome of your love."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "A collection is a test of one's love for Christ, not the only test, but a real one."
2. "Testing and so proving."
3. "Legitimately born, not spurious."

### John Gill:
1. "Either of God, who has not fixed the certain times when, or certain sums which persons are to give, and other circumstances, which are left to discretion."
2. "He did not go about to force or oblige them to it, for men in such cases must act willingly, and what they do, must do of their own accord with cheerfulness, and not through constraint or grudgingly."
3. "To prove the sincerity of your love;... that their love might appear to be true, genuine, hearty, and real to others, to all men as well as to them the apostles."
