## BHT:
Peter, knowing that his foretold martyrdom was near, refers to his impending death as the putting off of his earthly body. This metaphor of putting off a garment emphasizes the sudden and quick nature of his departure, even if it is a violent death. Peter's use of the word departure highlights his understanding of the purpose behind following Jesus, as he has fully grasped the teachings of Christ. His reference to the incident in John 21:18, of which he was personally aware before it was written down, further emphasizes the certainty and significance of his approaching martyrdom.
