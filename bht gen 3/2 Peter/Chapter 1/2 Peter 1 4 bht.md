# 2 Peter 1:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Through the precious and exceeding great promises of God, we may escape the corruption and lust of the world. These promises grant us sanctification, imparting God Himself through the Holy Spirit in our souls. By clinging to these promises with unwavering confidence, we can become partakers of the divine nature and experience a growth in our moral character. These promises are a gift from God, as sure and reliable as if they were already fulfilled, leading us to eternal life and glory.

### Henry Alford:
1. "that by means of these (promises: i.e. their fulfilment) ye may become partakers of the divine nature."
2. "having escaped...from the corruption...which is in the world in lust."
3. "This corruption he shews to be not in the elements which surround us, but in our own heart, because there reign the vicious and wicked affections, whose source and root he denotes by the word lust."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Precious promises are the object of precious faith."
2. "The promises themselves are a gift: for God's promises are as sure as if they were fulfilled."
3. "Sanctification is the imparting to us of God Himself by the Holy Spirit in the soul."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The promises referred to are doubtless those which are made in connection with the plan of salvation revealed in the gospel, for there are no other promises made to man. They refer to the pardon of sin; strength, comfort, and support in trial; a glorious resurrection; and a happy immortality."
2. "For the promises of God, therefore, we should be in the highest degree grateful, and in the trials of life we should cling to them with unwavering confidence as the only things which can be an anchor to the soul."
3. "The reference then, in this place, must be to the 'moral' nature of God; and the meaning is, that they who are renewed become participants of the same 'moral' nature; that is, of the same views, feelings, thoughts, purposes, principles of action."

1. "By means of those same revealed and efficient perfections by which He called us, God has put us in actual possession of those incalculable bestowals of grace which are identified with the Coming of Christ and the gift of the Spirit."

### John Gill:
1. "The promises of the new and everlasting covenant [...] are absolute, free, and unconditional, and are irreversible and unchangeable; and they answer great ends and purposes, the glory of God, and the everlasting good and happiness of his people; and therefore must be 'precious', of more value and worth than thousands of gold and silver."
