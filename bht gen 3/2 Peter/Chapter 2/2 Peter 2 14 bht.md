# 2 Peter 2:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
These false teachers are perpetually consumed by their adulterous desires, their eyes always fixed on temptation. Their hearts are trained in pursuing immoral desires, unable to resist sin. Like baiters, they entice and prey upon unstable souls, using deceitful arts to allure. Let us remain cautious of their flatteries and steadfast in our faith, as an excessive love of money is the root of all evil and the source of heresy.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "full of adultery...carried about adulteresses always dwelling in their eyes."
2. "that cannot be made to cease from sin."
3. "children of curse, that is, devoted to the curse."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The eye was, as it were, full of this passion; it saw nothing else but some occasion for its indulgence; it expressed nothing else but the desire."
2. "There are many men in whom the presence of the most virtuous woman only excites impure and corrupt desires."
3. "We should always be on our guard when professedly religious teachers propose to have much to do with money matters."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Eyes. Another illustration of Peter's emphasis on sight. It is the instrument of evil no less than of good. Compare Matthew 5:28." 
2. "Beguiling. An appropriate word from Peter the fisherman. Rev., enticing." 
3. "An heart they have exercised. Better, Rev., having a heart exercised. Exercised is the word used for gymnastic training, from which gymnastic is derived."

### John Calvin:
1. "By the metaphor of baiting he reminds the faithful to beware of their hidden and deceitful arts...they have no excuse who suffer themselves to be baited or lured by such flatteries."
2. "Let there be then a stability of faith, and we shall be safe from the artifices of the ungodly."
3. "By calling them cursed or execrable children, he may be understood to mean, that they were so either actively or passively, that is, that they brought a curse with them wherever they went, or that they deserved a curse."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "having eyes full of an adulteress...having eyes for nothing else but an adulteress, or revealing in their very eyes the adulterous object of their desire."
2. "cannot be made to cease from sin...adds the strokes of restlessness and persistence to the picture of their sensual profligacy."
3. "enticing unstable souls...to allure by holding out a bait to one...it means to allure by holding out a bait to one."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Vivid picture of a man who cannot see a woman without lascivious thoughts toward her."
2. "Unable to stop. Insatiable lust."
3. "Rhetorical metaphor from the gymnasium."

### John Gill:
1. "The heart and the 'eye' are sin's two brokers."
2. "Having a lewd and infamous woman always in mind and sight, continually looking at her and lusting after her."
3. "An immoderate love of money, a covetous desire after it, is the root of all evil, the bane of religion, and source of heresy."
