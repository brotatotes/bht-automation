# 2 Thessalonians 1:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In the face of their shared suffering, the believers can find solace in the idea of a relaxing of the strings of endurance. A rest is promised by the Lord Jesus upon His revelation, a rest that will turn weariness into strength and broken words into perfect praise. This anticipated rest reflects God's care for the afflicted, to be enjoyed in the company of the apostles and fellow believers. It is a hopeful and joyous reward for enduring persecution for righteousness' sake.

1. "relaxing of the strings of endurance now so tightly drawn."
2. "the writers, who are troubled like yourselves."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It will be a righteous thing for God to give to you who are persecuted rest in the last day."
2. "The future happiness of believers is often represented under the image of rest."
3. "It always augments the anticipated joy of heaven to reflect that we are to share its blessedness with them."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Rest. With this exception only in Paul."
2. "With us. According to Paul's habit of identifying his experience with that of his Christian readers."
3. "When the Lord Jesus shall be revealed. Lit. in the revelation of the Lord Jesus."

### John Calvin:
1. "The pious may understand that God is so much the more concerned as to their afflictions in proportion to the dreadfulness of the judgment that awaits his enemies."
2. "The metaphor, however, of flame and fire, is abundantly common in Scripture, when the anger of God is treated of."
3. "By the angels of his power, he means those in whom he will exercise his power; for he will bring the angels with him for the purpose of displaying the glory of his kingdom."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Their rest is not the rest of a stone, cold and lifeless, but of wearied humanity."
2. "Now is their weariness changed into refreshment, their weakness into excellence of strength, their wasting into a spirit ever new, their broken words into the perfection of praise, their weeping into a chant of bliss."
3. "Paul does not present death to them as their release, but the second coming of Christ; which indicates that he considered it possible, if not even probable, that this would occur during their lifetime."
