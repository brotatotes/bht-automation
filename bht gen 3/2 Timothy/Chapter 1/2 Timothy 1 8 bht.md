# 2 Timothy 1:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Do not be ashamed of the testimony of our Lord, but share in the sufferings for the gospel according to the power of God. Witness a good confession and do not fear the noise raised by the world. Embrace boldness in confessing the gospel openly, for whoever is ashamed of it, Christ will be ashamed of them. Bear afflictions patiently and constantly for the sake of the gospel. Rely on the power of God to overcome all things and endure hardships for the gospel's sake.

### Henry Alford:
1. "Be not then ashamed of the testimony of our Lord, nor of me His prisoner." 
2. "But suffer hardship with me for the Gospel according to the power of God."
3. "For I know whom I have trusted, and am persuaded that He is able to keep my deposit against that day."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Shame (2 Timothy 1:8) is the companion of fear (2 Timothy 1:7); if fear be overcome, false shame flees."
2. "The testimony which Christ gave before Pilate is an incentive to the believer that he should, after His Lord's example, witness a good testimony or confession."
3. "Think not that thou hast to bear these afflictions by thine own power; nay, it is by the power of God."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Do not be ashamed to bear your testimony to the doctrines taught by the Lord Jesus." 
2. "Timothy knew that he had been thrown into prison on account of his love for the gospel."
3. "The apostle...asks him to endure those trials, relying on that strength, and not on his own."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Be not ashamed." 
2. "Testimony." 
3. "Share afflictions with me for the gospel."

### John Calvin:
1. "He who is armed with the power of God will not tremble at the noise raised by the world, but will reckon it honorable that wicked men mark them with disgrace."
2. "Accordingly, the more hateful the doctrine of the gospel is in the world, the more earnestly should they labor to confess it openly."
3. "Not without good reason, therefore, does Paul, while he exhorts to boldness of confession, in order that he may not exhort in vain, speak to him also about bearing the cross."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Be not thou therefore ashamed. The exhortation... implies some fear that Timothy was acting as if ashamed of the testimony of Jesus."
2. "Timothy had drawn back, as if ashamed of him, as well as of the Gospel."
3. "Be thou a partaker in my sufferings for the gospel."

### John Wesley:
