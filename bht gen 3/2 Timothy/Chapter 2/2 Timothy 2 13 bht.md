# 2 Timothy 2:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Even if we are faithless or unbelieving, Christ remains faithful and true to His nature and word. God cannot be unfaithful or deny Himself. Our lack of faith does not change His faithfulness or the certainty of His promises. He remains committed to His covenant engagements and will bring believers to glory. Our salvation does not depend on our acts of believing or our own faithfulness, but on the unwavering and unchanging faithfulness of Christ.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "If we believe not...are faithless or untrue to him."

### John Calvin:
1. "Let them desert Christ who will, yet they take nothing from him; for when they perish, he remaineth unchanged."
2. "Christ is not like us, to swerve from his truth."
3. "Our faith is founded on the eternal and unchangeable truth of Christ, in order that it may not waver through the unsteadfastness or apostasy of men."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "If we lose our faith. He still remains faithful."
2. "We may turn a deaf ear to them, refuse to believe them, but they will be found true at last."
3. "The faithfulness of Christ is pledged to the words of judgment as well as those of promise."

### John Gill:
1. "This unbelief of theirs will not make void his faith or faithfulness." 
2. "Christ is faithful to all his covenant engagements for them, to bring them to glory, and to every word of promise concerning their happiness." 
3. "His salvation is an everlasting and unchangeable one; nor do the saints' interest in it, and security by it, depend upon their acts of believing, or their frames, but upon the firmness and unchangeableness of Christ, the object of faith."
