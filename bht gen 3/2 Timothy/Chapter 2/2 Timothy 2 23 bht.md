# 2 Timothy 2:23 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The apostle Paul instructs Timothy to decline and refuse foolish and undisciplined questionings that only serve to breed contention. He emphasizes that a servant of the Lord should not engage in strife, but rather be gentle and patient, seeking to correct others with meekness. These unwise and uninstructive questions come from untrained minds and result in unnecessary battles. Paul urges Timothy to avoid these speculations of uneducated thinking and instead focus on matters of true importance. These pointless disputes only lead to jangling and debates, hindering the growth and edification of believers.

### Henry Alford:
1. "Foolish and undisciplined questionings decline, being aware that they gender strifes."
2. "The servant of the Lord must not strive but be gentle towards all, apt to teach, patient of wrong."
3. "In meekness correcting those who oppose themselves, if at any time God may give them repentance in order to the knowledge of [the] truth."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Undisciplined; not tending to promote the discipline of faith and morals."
2. "Uninstructive; in contrast with 'instructing' and 'wise unto salvation.'"
3. "Avoid; decline."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The word 'unlearned,' here, means 'trifling; that which does not tend to edification; stupid.'"
2. "The only effect of such disputes is to engender harsh contention."
3. "Points of real importance can be discussed with no injury to the temper; but people cannot safely dispute about trifles."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "questions which do not proceed from any trained habit of thinking."
2. "questions of an untrained mind, carried away with novelties"
3. "refuse or decline"

### John Calvin:
1. "When we are wise in a useful manner, then alone are we truly wise."
2. "Let us always remember this remarkable testimony of Paul, that questions, which are held in high estimation, are nevertheless foolish, because they are unprofitable."
3. "They give occasion for jangling and debates. And yet the greater part of men, after having received so many instructions, do not at all profit by them."

### Philip Schaff:
