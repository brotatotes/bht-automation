# 2 Timothy 2:25 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
To effectively address those who indulge in fruitless arguments, it is essential to correct them with meekness and gentleness, rather than responding with anger or denunciation. Repentance, which is the first step towards change, is not within the capability of humans, but a gift from God. The process of instruction begins with imparting knowledge and understanding of the truth, leading to a transformation of the mind. While it is uncertain if these individuals will repent, ministers should continue to patiently educate, trusting in God's power to grant repentance and the acknowledgment of the truth.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "instructing with correction, which those who deal in 'uninstructive' or 'undisciplined questions' need"
2. "oppositely affected"; those of a different opinion.
3. "repentance is their first need. That, not man, but God alone can 'give'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "We are not to become angry with such persons, and denounce them at once as heretics. We are not to hold them up to public reproach and scorn; but we are to set about the business of patiently 'instructing them.'"
2. "Meekness' and 'gentleness' are the very best things, if you wish to convince another that he is wrong."
3. "After all our care in teaching others the truth, our only dependence is on God for its success."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "In meekness. A Pauline word, only here in Pastorals. But comp. praupaqia, 1 Timothy 6:11 (note). Const. With instruction."
2. "Those that oppose themselves. The meaning is, those who oppose the servant of the Lord. Paul's word is ajntikeisqai to oppose."
3. "To the acknowledging of the truth. More correctly, the knowledge. The formula Past o."

### John Calvin:
1. "Paul means that even towards the most unworthy we must exercise meekness; and although at first there be no appearance of having gained advantage, still we must make the attempt."
2. "Since the conversion of a man is in the hand of God, who knows whether they who today appear to be unteachable shall be suddenly changed by the power of God, into other men?"
3. "We may learn from this what is the actual repentance of those who for a time were disobedient to God; for Paul declares that it begins with 'the knowledge of the truth.'"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The word never means simple instruction, but always education and discipline..." (2 Timothy 2:25)
2. "If at some time or other God should give repentance." (2 Timothy 2:25)
3. "...the remedial process was to begin at the beginning, first amendment of life, and then intellectual perception of the truth." (2 Timothy 2:25)

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Schooling" (Parry).
2. "Change of mind" (2 Corinthians 7:10; Romans 2:4).
3. Paul's word "full knowledge" (Colossians 1:9).

### John Gill:
1. "In meekness instructing those that oppose themselves... these are to be instructed, being ignorant, and in a tender and gentle manner, though very perverse and obstinate."
2. "Repentance here designs a repentance of errors in principle, a change of mind upon conviction, and such as issues in a free and ingenuous confession, and acknowledgment of the truth before opposed; and such a repentance is the gift of God."
3. "And though this is not certain, that God will give repentance to such contradictors and blasphemers of his Gospel; yet as it is his will, that all his chosen ones should come to repentance, and that some of all sorts should be saved, and come to the knowledge of the truth; and seeing these things have been brought about under and by the ministry of the word, it is an encouragement to the ministers of the Gospel to continue their instructions in the manner here directed."
