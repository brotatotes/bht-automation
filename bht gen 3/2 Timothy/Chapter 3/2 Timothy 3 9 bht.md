# 2 Timothy 3:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Their folly shall be thoroughly manifested, and their senselessness and immoral teachings will be brought into the open. Though they may proceed and advance in ungodliness, there is a final limit beyond which they cannot go. They may deceive for a time, but ultimately their falsehood and deception will be exposed. The world will arise and cast off their deceit. Their errors may spread, but they will not be allowed to go beyond a certain point.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Notwithstanding, they shall not advance further... for their folly shall be thoroughly manifested to all" (2 Timothy 3:9).
2. "it is manifest, that while there the Apostle is speaking of an immediate spread of error, here he is looking to its ultimate defeat, and extinction."
3. "the allusion is to their traditional end."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Though for a time they shall advance or proceed unto more ungodliness, yet there is a final limit beyond which they shall not be able to proceed further."
2. "Often malice proceeds deeper down, when it cannot extend itself."
3. "Their folly, wise though they think themselves, shall be brought forth from concealment into open day."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "There is a certain point beyond which they will not be allowed to go. Their folly will become manifest, and the world will understand it."
2. "Error will advance only to a certain point, when it will be 'seen' to be falsehood and folly, and when the world will arise and cast it off."
3. "By smooth sophistry, and cunning arts, they will not be able always to deceive mankind."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The senselessness of their teaching, with an implication of its immoral character."
2. "Shall proceed."
3. "Manifest."

### John Calvin:
1. "He encourages Timothy for the contest, by the confident hope of victory; for, although false teachers give him annoyance, he promises that they shall be, within a short time, disgracefully ruined."
2. "It is more correct to say, that he looked at them in various aspects; for the affirmation, that they will not make progress, is not universal; but he only means, that the Lord will discover their madness to many whom they had, at first, deceived by their enchantments."
3. "But that they may not think, on the other hand, that they are discharged from future service, after one or two battles, they must be reminded that there will always be new occasion for fighting."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "They shall proceed no further."
2. "The earlier passage refers to the development of evil tendencies from their germ, and the later to their ultimate failure and decay."
3. "Heresies spread rapidly but are short-lived."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They shall proceed no further."
2. "Folly, want of sense."
3. "Evident, outstanding."

### John Gill:
1. "They may proceed to more ungodliness, and wax worse and worse in error; but they shall proceed no further than the magicians of Egypt."
2. "These wicked men do false miracles, harden the popes of Rome, and deceive the nations subject to them; but they cannot deceive the elect of God, nor destroy the church of God."
3. "It is notorious in how many instances the frauds, impostures, tricks, and villanies of the church of Rome, and its votaries, have been detected and exposed; which have been the means of hindering them from proceeding any further than they have."
