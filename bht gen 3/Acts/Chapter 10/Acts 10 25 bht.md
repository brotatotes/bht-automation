# Acts 10:25 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Recognizing Peter as an ambassador of God, Cornelius, a man of honor, instinctively offered him deep respect and reverence. While Cornelius's act of falling at Peter's feet was not idolatry, it was a gesture of thankfulness and esteem. However, Peter understood that worship should be reserved solely for the divine, therefore declining this homage. This encounter between Cornelius and Peter highlights the importance of distinguishing between civil respect and true worship, emphasizing that the adoration due to God alone should not be ascribed to mortal beings.

### Albert Barnes:
