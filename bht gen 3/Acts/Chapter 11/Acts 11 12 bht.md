# Acts 11:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Peter defended his actions by recounting the events and was supported by the six brethren who accompanied him. He made no mention of Cornelius' high position, focusing solely on the uncircumcised man to whom he had been sent. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1.  12. these six brethren] They had accompanied him to Jerusalem, and were there to substantiate the facts, as far as they had witnessed them.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "No mention of Cornelius' name, much less of his high position, as if that affected the question."
2. "To the charge, 'Thou wentest in to men uncircumcised,' he simply speaks of the uncircumcised 'man' to whom he had been divinely sent."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Nothing doubting... making no distinction, taking the verb in its original sense, which is to separate or distinguish... doubting being a common rendering in the New Testament and giving a perfectly good sense here." 
2. "It was natural that Peter should hesitate."
3. "The six brethren. The men of Joppa who had gone with Peter to Cornelius, and had accompanied him also to Jerusalem, either as witnesses for him or for their own vindication, since they had committed the same offense."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Moreover these six brethren accompanied me...showing how deep an impression had been made by the recent events at Caesarea and Joppa."
2. "St. Peter simply terms Cornelius 'the man'...These particulars would have been positively distasteful to his Jewish listeners."
3. "There was much point in his saying, however briefly, that he 'entered into the man's house.' This was the very ground of the censure under which Peter had fallen."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Making no distinction." 
2. "We entered into the man's house."

### John Gill:
1. "And the Spirit bade me go with them, nothing doubting." 
2. "Moreover, these six brethren accompanied me... to be witnesses for him, of what they had seen and heard, which was a very wise and prudential step." 
3. "And we entered into the man's house; the house of Cornelius, for entering into whose house they were contending with him, and where he entered not alone, but the six brethren with him."
