# Acts 11:14 Commentary Help Text

The angel's speech suggests that both Cornelius and his household would be saved. Salvation is tied to the Gospel message about Christ and depends on words. Cornelius, despite his interest in Jewish worship, remained unsaved until he heard Peter's words. The conversion of his household preceded their baptism, indicating the significance of faith. ### Henry Alford:
1. "Whereby thou and all thy house shall be saved."
2. "This is implied in the angel's speech."
3. "Especially if the prayer of Cornelius had been for such a boon, of which there can be little doubt."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Observe, 'salvation' is here made to hang upon 'words,' that is, the Gospel message concerning Christ."
2. "But as Peter tarried with Cornelius...perhaps this fuller and richer form of what the angel said was given to Peter; or the apostle himself may have expressed what the angel certainly designed by directing them to send for him."
3. "But on the 'salvation' of Cornelius, see on Acts 11:1. On that of his 'house,' see on Acts 11:1-."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "And all thy house - Thy family."
2. "It is evident from Acts 10:48 that the family also received the ordinance of baptism, and was received into the church."
3. "This is a circumstance which his omitted in the account in Acts 10:0."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Words, whereby thou and all thy house shall be saved."
2. "'All thy house' is a special addition here."
3. "The promise is in harmony with the preparation made for the Gospel in the house of Cornelius."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Clearly Cornelius was unsaved in spite of his interest in Jewish worship."
2. "Clearly also the household of Cornelius would likewise be won to Christ by the words of Simon Peter."
3. "This is household conversion before the household baptism."

### John Gill:
1. "The Gospel only points out the way of salvation, and is only effectual to it, when it is accompanied with the power of God."
2. "It is not bare hearing the word and attending on ordinances that will save any, only such who believe in Christ with the heart, who is the sum and substance of the Gospel, are saved."
3. "The house or family of Cornelius were not saved by his believing in Christ; but the words of faith and sound doctrine being preached to them, as to him, and faith coming thereby to one as to another, they were saved in one and the same way, namely, through faith in Christ Jesus."
