# Acts 11:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Peter defended himself against the accusation of associating with the uncircumcised by emphasizing the divine intervention that occurred while he spoke to Cornelius. Instead of mentioning Cornelius by name, Peter focused on the uncircumcised man to whom he was divinely sent. He pointed out that while he was speaking, the Holy Spirit fell upon the Gentiles just as it did on the Jews during Pentecost. Peter's defense rested not on his words, but on the undeniable actions of God.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "No mention of Cornelius' name, much less of his high position, as if that affected the question."
2. "To the charge, 'Thou wentest in to men uncircumcised,' he simply speaks of the uncircumcised 'man' to whom he had been divinely sent."

### Albert Barnes:
1. And as I began to speak - Or, while I was speaking.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "From this we see that St. Peter was intending to say more than, in consequence of the Divine interruption, he was permitted to say."
2. "And therefore miraculously, with signs audible or visible or both. This seems a natural and almost inevitable conclusion."
3. "St. Peter appears to claim Pentecost as the starting-point of a new dispensation. And yet eight or ten years had elapsed since that day."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "In the beginning to speak". 
2. "Even as on us at the beginning".
3. "He rests his defence, not on what he said, but on what God did".

### John Gill:
1. "The Holy Ghost fell on them, as on us at the beginning."
2. "Meaning on the day of Pentecost."
3. "The Holy Ghost fell on Cornelius and those that were with him, in a like visible form, and in the same kind of gifts, as speaking with tongues, as on them."
