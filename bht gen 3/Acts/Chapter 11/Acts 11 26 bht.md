## BHT:
The disciples in Antioch received the name Christian as an official title given by the Gentiles to distinguish them from Jews. This name, which means the Anointed, became a matter of glorying among the believers. It marked the formation of a distinct body composed of Jews and Gentiles, and the disciples did not assume the name themselves. The term Christian transcended other appellations, uniting followers of Christ from different backgrounds and blessing humanity through its existence.

### Albert Barnes:

3. "This name binds them all together - a name which rises above every other appellation; which unites in one the inhabitants of distant nations and tribes of men; which connects the extremes of society, and places them in most important respects on a common level."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The meaning of the word in this passage is illustrated by Romans 7:3. The disciples were called. They did not assume the name themselves."
2. "The name was probably not assumed by the disciples themselves; for they were in the habit of styling each other believers, disciples, saints, brethren, those of the way."
3. "The Antiochenes mistook the nature of the name, not understanding its use among the disciples as an official title - the Anointed - but using it as a personal name, which they converted into a party name."

### John Calvin:

### Philip Schaff:
