## BHT:
James, the representative of the strictest adherence to the pure standard of legal morality, silenced the disputers alongside Peter and gave his judgment in favor of the freedom of the Gentiles. As the head of the church in Jerusalem, his decision carried weight despite his conservative reverence for Jewish usages. James, holding an authority not less than Peter, recommended a few general restrictive rules for the Gentiles to observe while maintaining their worldly relations.
