### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "It may have been afterwards added to explain." 
2. "Doubtless the attraction to Antioch for Silas was Paul's presence there." 
3. "He seems to have now formed that permanent attachment which the sequel of this book and Paul's Epistles show to have existed."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "This whole verse is missing in many mss.; ... and is regarded as spurious by Mill, Griesbach, and by other critics."
2. "It was probably introduced by some early transcriber, who judged it necessary to complete the narrative."
3. "The Latin Vulgate reads, 'It seemed good to Silas to remain, but Judas went alone to Jerusalem.'"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Notwithstanding it pleased Silas to abide there still." 
2. "This verse is wanting in the older MSS., and in many of the chief versions." 
3. "It is evidently a marginal gloss, originally inserted to explain how Silas, notwithstanding the statement of Acts 15:33, was at hand (Acts 15:40) conveniently for Paul to choose him as companion in travel."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "But it seemed good unto Silas to abide there." 
2. "It is clearly an addition to help explain the fact that Silas is back in Antioch in verse Acts 15:40." 
3. "The 'some days' of verse Acts 15:36 afforded abundant time for him to return from Jerusalem."

### John Gill:
1. "the design of Providence in it seems to have been this; that he might be a companion with the Apostle Paul in his travels among the Gentiles, as he afterwards was, and was very useful to him."
2. "This verse is wanting in the Alexandrian copy, and in the Syriac and Arabic versions."
3. "the Vulgate Latin version here adds, and 'Judas went alone to Jerusalem'; and so it is read in one of Beza's copies, and in one of Stephens's."
