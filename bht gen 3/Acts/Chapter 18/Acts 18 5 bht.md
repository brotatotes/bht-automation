## BHT:
When Silas and Timotheus returned from Macedonia, they found Paul in a state of more than ordinary anxiety, absorbed in the work of testifying to the Jews. His power as a preacher was felt to lie in the demonstration of the Spirit, and he was relieved of anxiety by the arrival of his friends. The coming of Silas and Timothy set Paul free from tent-making for a while so that he began to devote himself with fresh consecration to preaching, with the goal of showing the Jews that Jesus of Nazareth is the Messiah.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "When Silas and Timotheus returned from Macedonia, they found Paul anxiously occupied in discoursing to the Jews."
2. "It seems to be implied, that they found him in a state of more than ordinary anxiety, more than usually absorbed in the work of testifying to the Jews."
3. "A crisis in the work being imminent, which resulted in their rejection of the word of life."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Paul was pressed with the word" - expressing not only his zeal and assiduity in preaching it, but some inward pressure which at this time he experienced in the work.
2. "His power as a preacher was more than ever felt to lie in demonstration of the Spirit."
3. "Such emotions are known only to the ministers of Christ, and, even of them, only to such as 'travail in birth until Christ be formed in' their hearers."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Paul was pressed - Was urged; was borne away by an unusual impulse. It was deeply impressed on him as his duty."
2. "In spirit - In his mind; in his feelings. His love to Christ was so great, and his conviction of the truth so strong."
3. "The presence of Silas and Timothy animated him; and the certainty of aid in his work urged him to zeal in making known the Saviour."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Paul was engrossed by the word."
2. "He was relieved of anxiety by the arrival of his friends."
3. "He was stimulated to greater activity in the work of preaching the word."

### Philip Schaff:

### Archibald T. Robertson:

### John Gill:

3. "He continued to produce more testimonies out of the writings of Moses, and the prophets, to prove that Jesus of Nazareth was the Christ, or Messiah, prophesied of in those writings."

### John Wesley:
1. "And when Silas and Timotheus were come from Macedonia."
