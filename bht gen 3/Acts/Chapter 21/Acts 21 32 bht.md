## BHT:
When Paul was being beaten, the Roman tribune, the chief captain, swiftly intervened, ordering the centurions to stop the beating. The providence of God was evident in this situation as the apostle's life was preserved amidst the chaos and uproar. This severe beating, known as the rebels' beating, was inflicted upon those who rebelled or disobeyed. ### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 32. the chief captain—"the
	chiliarch," or tribune of the Roman cohort, whose full number
	was one thousand men.


### Albert Barnes:
1. Centurions - Captains of 100 men.


### Marvin Vincent:
1. Centurions. See on Luke 7:2. 
Unto them [επ αυτους] . Better, upon them.


### John Calvin:
1. "Those whose fury neither the majesty of God, nor yet the reverence of the temple, could once stay, begin to relent when they see a profane man."
2. "The Spirit hath depainted out unto us the providence of God as in a table [picture] reigning amidst the confused uproars of men."
3. "He bindeth him with chains, as if he were some evil-doer, or some wicked person; yet doth he vouchsafe to hear him when he is bound, whom they did beat unmercifully."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Forthwith (εξαυτης). Common in the Koine (εξ αυτης, supply ωρας, hour)."
2. "Left off beating Paul (επαυσαντο τυπτοντες τον Παυλον). The participle with παυομα describes what they were already doing, the supplementary participle (Robertson, Grammar, p. 1121)."
3. "Some ten years before in a riot at the passover the Roman guard marched down and in the panic several hundred were trampled to death."

### John Gill:
1. "In which there was a remarkable appearance of divine providence in favour of the apostle, who otherwise in all likelihood would have quickly lost his life."
2. "This beating was what the Jews call מכות מרדית, 'the rebels' beating'; or beating, on account of rebellion and obstinacy."
3. "This was inflicted upon various offenders, particularly on such who received not admonitions given them, or transgressed by doing what was forbidden by the words of the wise men."
