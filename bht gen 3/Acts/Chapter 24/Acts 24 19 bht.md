# Acts 24:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Apostle powerfully defends himself by exposing the absence of his true accusers, the Asiatic Jews, and the lack of proper witnesses. The present accusers did not witness the alleged offense, making their accusations unreliable. The exclusion of the witnesses who could provide direct evidence suggests their unpreparedness. This reveals the world's tendency to unjustly persecute God's children and commit grave errors in its treatment. Paul's skillful argumentation highlights the importance of fair judgment and integrity of evidence in any case.
