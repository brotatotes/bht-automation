# Acts 26:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Paul, the witness of the glory of Christ, became more than all the other apostles through divine grace. He condensed various sayings of Jesus into one statement to emphasize the grandeur of his commission. As a minister and a witness, he had firsthand experiences of Christ and was called to testify. This appearance of Christ was not for destruction but for honor and comfort in his honorable ministry. Paul's role as a minister was appointed by Christ and his visions are a testimony of what he saw and would continue to see.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Here the apostle appears to condense into one statement various sayings of his Lord to him in visions at different times, in order to present at one view the grandeur of the commission with which his Master had clothed him."
2. "Putting him on a footing with those 'eye-witnesses and ministers of the word' mentioned in Luke 1:2."
3. "Referring to visions he was thereafter to be favored with such as Acts 18:9; Acts 18:10; Acts 22:17-21; Acts 23:11; 2 Corinthians 12:1-10, &amp;c. (Galatians 1:12)."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It is the duty of ministers to bear witness not only to the truth of religion in general, or of that which they can demonstrate by argument, but more especially of that which they experience in their own hearts, and which they understand by having themselves been the subjects of it."
2. "No man is qualified to enter the ministry who has not a personal saving view of the glory and perfections of the Lord Jesus, and who does not go to his work as a witness of those things which he has felt."
3. "The highest personal consolation in this work is the promise of being admitted to ever-growing and expanding views of the glory of the Lord Jesus, and of experiencing his presence, guidance, and protection."
