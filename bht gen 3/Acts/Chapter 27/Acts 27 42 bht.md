## BHT:
The soldiers, responsible for the lives of the prisoners, suggested a cruel plan to kill them in order to prevent any possible escape. This proposal reflects the extreme cruelty of the Roman military system and shows the soldiers' ungratefulness towards Paul, who had been instrumental in saving their lives. Their plan demonstrates the natural ferocity and cruelty that can persist even when experiencing the tender mercy of God. ### John Calvin:
1. "Though the soldiers might thank Paul twice or thrice for their lives, yet are they minded to kill him, for whose sake they ought to have spared the rest."

3. "But it behoveth us to consider the wonderful counsel of God, as well in saving Paul as in fulfilling his promise; when as he bringeth those men to land, who did what they could to make his promise of none effect."
