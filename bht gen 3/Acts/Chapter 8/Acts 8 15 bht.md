Through prayer and the laying on of hands, the Samaritans received the superadded communication of the Holy Spirit, accompanied by miraculous manifestations, confirming their genuine conversion to Christ. The coming of the Holy Spirit with obvious signs in Jerusalem provided undeniable proof that the Samaritans were true believers. ### Henry Alford:
1.  15. prayed for them] So laying on of hands is preceded by prayer, ch. vi. 6; xiii, 3.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "As the baptism of adults presupposed 'the renewing of the Holy Ghost', of which the profession of faith had to be taken for evidence, this communication of the Holy Ghost by the laying on of the apostles' hands was clearly a superadded thing." 
2. "It was only occasional, so it was invariably attended with miraculous manifestations."
3. "Beautiful, too, was the spectacle exhibited of Jew and Samaritan, one in Christ."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "They sought at the hand of God the extraordinary communications of the Holy Spirit. They did not even pretend to have the power of doing it without the aid of God."
2. "The phrase 'the gift of the Holy Spirit,' and 'the descent of the Holy Spirit,' signified not merely his 'ordinary' influences in converting sinners, but those 'extraordinary' influences that attended the first preaching of the gospel - the power of speaking with new tongues, the power of working miracles, etc."
3. "If it be asked 'why' this power was conferred on the early Christians, it may be replied that it was to furnish striking proof of the truth of the Christian religion; to impress the people, and thus to win them to embrace the gospel."

### John Calvin:
1. "They prayed. Undoubtedly they taught first, for we know that they were no dumb persons."
2. "Luke passeth over that which was common to them and Philip, and declareth only what new thing the Samaritans had by their coming."
3. "They had the Spirit given them then."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Did they wish the Samaritan Pentecost to prove beyond a doubt that the Samaritans were really converted when they believed?" 
2. "They had been baptized on the assumption that the Holy Spirit had given them new hearts."
3. "The coming of the Holy Spirit with obvious signs as in Jerusalem would make it plain."

### John Gill:
1. "That they might receive the Holy Ghost; the extraordinary gifts of the Holy Ghost, as to be able to speak with tongues, to prophesy and work miracles."
2. "They might pray for them all, that they might have a larger measure of grace, and more spiritual light and knowledge."
3. "That these are meant by the Holy Ghost is clear from what follows, since he was not yet fallen on any of them, which cannot be understood in any other sense."
