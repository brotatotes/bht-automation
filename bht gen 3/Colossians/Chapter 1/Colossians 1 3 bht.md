### John Calvin:
1. "We must always take notice that he makes use of thanksgiving in place of congratulation, by which he teaches us, that in all our joys we must readily call to remembrance the goodness of God."
2. "If, however, even the smallest favors come to us from that source, how much more ought this same acknowledgment to be made in reference to those two gifts, in which the entire sum of our excellence consists?"
3. "By this he intimates, that the condition of believers is never in this world perfect, so as not to have, invariably, something wanting. For even the man who has begun admirably well, may fall short in a hundred instances every day; and we must ever be making progress while we are as yet on the way."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The plural does not stand for the singular, but is used when the Apostle, in thought, associates others with himself." 
2. "To God the Father of our Lord Jesus Christ. And' is to be omitted, although found in Aleph and A (retained by Tischendorf), because the copyists would be far more likely to insert it than to omit it." 
3. "When praying. The force of the participle is better expressed by supplying 'when.'"
