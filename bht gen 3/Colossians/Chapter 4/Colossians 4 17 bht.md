# Colossians 4:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Church congregations possess remarkable authority to exercise discipline over their teachers, showing that Scripture benefits both clergy and laity. Archippus is urged to diligently fulfill his ministry as it draws to a close. This emphasizes the importance of constant watchfulness, as the ministry carries great responsibility to both Christ and His people. The ministry is a laborious and painful service that requires ministers to remain receptive to admonishment from the congregation and to demonstrate teachability.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The authority hereby implied on the part of the congregation to exercise reproof and discipline over their teachers is remarkable."
2. "The hierarchical turn given to the passage by some... belongs to a later age."
3. "Look to the ministry which thou receivedst in the Lord... that thou fulfill it."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "This proves that Scripture belongs to the laity as well as the clergy; and that laymen may profitably admonish the clergy in particular cases when they do so in meekness."
2. "The word, 'fulfil,' accords with his ministry being near its close."
3. "The element in which every work of the Christian, and especially the Christian minister, is to be done."

### Albert Barnes:
Quote 1: "It is always proper to caution even the most faithful and self-denying servants of the Lord to 'take heed,' or see to it, that they perform their duties with fidelity."
Quote 2: "The office of the ministry is such, and the temptations to unfaithfulness are so great, that we need constant watchfulness."
Quote 3: "That thou fulfil it - That there be nothing wanting, or lacking, in any of the departments of labor which you are called to perform."

### John Calvin:
1. "The ministers of the word are endowed with signal authority, but such at the same time as is not exempt from laws."
2. "It is necessary that they should shew themselves teachable if they would duly teach others."
3. "Without doubt he felt some twinges of [human infirmity] in himself, inasmuch as he was so very urgent that all pious persons should be mindful of his distresses."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Take heed to the ministry, etc...Archippus might have been a 'deacon,' though the word does not necessarily suggest this; or he may have been the most prominent elder in the Colossian congregation."

3. "Official gifts bring responsibility, both to Christ and to His people. What we receive in the Lord, but increases the need of watchfulness on our part."
