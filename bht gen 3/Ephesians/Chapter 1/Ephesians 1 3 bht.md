# Ephesians 1:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Blessed be God who blessed us with every spiritual blessing in the heavenly places. Through the work of the Holy Spirit, we become citizens of heaven, receiving blessings that originate from there. The believers' true abode is heaven, and the spiritual blessings count the most for them. Let us praise and thank God for all that He has done, for He is the source of every blessing. These blessings are ours through our fellowship with Christ, who is the conduit for them.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:

### Marvin Vincent:

3. "The meaning is that the spiritual blessings of God are found in heaven and are brought thence to us."

### John Calvin:
1. "They who perceive in themselves discoveries of the Divine goodness, so full and absolutely perfect, and who make them the subject of earnest meditation, will never embrace new doctrines, by which the very grace which they feel so powerfully in themselves is thrown into the shade."
2. "The design of the apostle, therefore, in asserting the riches of divine grace toward the Ephesians, was to protect them against having their faith shaken by the false apostles, as if their calling were doubtful, or salvation were to be sought in some other way."
3. "The word bless is here used in more than one sense, as referring to God, and as referring to men... Paul therefore blesses God, because he hath blessed us, that is, hath enriched us with all blessing and grace."
