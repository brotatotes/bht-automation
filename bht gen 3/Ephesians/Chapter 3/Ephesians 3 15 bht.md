### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "God, in His relation of Father to us His adopted children, is the great prototype of the paternal relation wherever found."
2. "Hence angels are termed our 'brethren' and 'sons of God' by creation, as we are by adoption."
3. "To bear God's name is to belong to God as His own peculiar people."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The whole 'family of God,' means all his children; and the idea is, that they all bear the same name, derived from the Redeemer; all are Christians." 
2. "They are all of one family. They all have one father, and are all of one community. The expression is taken from the custom in a family, where all bear the name of the 'head' of the family." 
3. "Part are in heaven, near his throne; part in distant worlds; part are angels of light; part redeemed and happy spirits; part are in the church on earth; but they are all united as one family, and have one head and Father."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The word occurs only three times in the New Testament: here, Luke 2:4; Acts 3:25. In the last-named passage it is used in a wide, general sense, of nations." 
2. "Family is perhaps the best translation, if taken in its wider meaning of a body belonging to a common stock - a clan."
3. "To the angelic hosts and the tribes of men alike, God is Father."

3. "There is but one relationship which ought to be reckoned...a union to the body of Christ."
