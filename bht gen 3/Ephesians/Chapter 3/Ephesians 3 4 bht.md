# Ephesians 3:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Within the Epistle of Ephesians, there is an urging to deeply read and comprehend its mysteries, particularly the revelation of Christ Himself. Through perceiving the preacher's discernment of the mystery of Christ, readers can gain insight into the plan of salvation and the inclusion of Gentiles. The Apostle Paul considered this knowledge to be of great worth and emphasized the importance of understanding it. By immersing themselves in this Epistle, readers can acquire knowledge and greater understanding of this mystery.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "When ye read, deep as are the mysteries of this Epistle, the way for all to understand them is to read it." 
2. "By perceiving his understanding of the mysteries, they, too, will be enabled to understand." 
3. "The 'mystery' is Christ Himself, once hidden, but now revealed."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "By the bare reading of which you may understand the view which I entertain of the plan of salvation, and the knowledge which I have of God’s method of saving people." 
2. "It means the hitherto concealed doctrine that through the Messiah, the Gentiles were to be received to the same privileges as the Jews, and that the plan of salvation was to be made equally free for all." 
3. "Paul evidently supposed that the knowledge which he had on that subject was of eminent value; that it was possessed by few; that it was important to understand it."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Whereby [προς ο] . Lit., agreeably to which, namely, what he had written. 
Mystery of Christ. The mystery which is Christ. See on Colossians 1:26; Romans 11:25.


### John Calvin:
1. "If we duly consider Paul’s earnestness, — his watchfulness and care, — his zeal and fervor, — his kindness and readiness in assisting brethren, — we shall be led to regard it as highly probable that he would write many epistles."
2. "Let us rest assured, that what is left is enough for us, and that the smallness of the remaining number is not the result of accident; but that the body of Scripture, which is in our possession, has been adjusted by the wonderful counsel of God."
3. "The frequent mention of this point shews the necessity that the calling of ministers should be firmly believed both by themselves and by their people."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "In accordance with which, i.e., what he had written was to be the measure, or standard, by which they could determine his knowledge."
2. "Ye can perceive. ‘Can’ (E. V., incorrectly: ‘may’) is the emphatic word, and ‘perceive’ is preferable to ‘understand,’ referring to an immediate perception, as if it were a single act."
3. "The mystery had been revealed, hence the Apostle had an ‘understanding’ in regard to it, as could be perceived by his readers. He does not refer to his labors among them, since he has in mind what he has written."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "When ye read (αναγινωσκοντες). This Epistle will be read in public."
2. "My understanding in the mystery of Christ (την συνεσιν μου εν τω μυστηριω του Χριστου)."
3. "Every sermon reveals the preacher's grasp of 'the mystery of Christ.' If he has no insight into Christ, he has no call to preach."

### John Gill:
1. "Ye may understand my knowledge in the mystery of Christ of his person, office, and grace..."
