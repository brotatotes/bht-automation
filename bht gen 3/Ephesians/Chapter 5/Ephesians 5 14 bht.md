# Ephesians 5:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Awake, you who sleep, and arise from the dead, and Christ shall shine upon you. This passage calls upon believers to awaken from their spiritual slumber and be enlightened by the manifestation of Christ within their hearts. The urgent call to wake up applies to both believers and unbelievers, as the light of Christ dispels darkness. The use of personal pronouns emphasizes the intimate and personal nature of this awakening experience. Its roots in Isaiah 60:1 and Isaiah 26:19 suggest that it might have originated as an early Christian hymn.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "It only remains that the man should be shone upon inwardly by the same Christ revealed in his awakened heart."
2. "Awake, thou that sleepest, and arise from the dead, and Christ shall shine upon thee."
3. "The Church is set forth as being in a state of darkness and of death, and is exhorted to awake, and become light, for that her light is come, and the glory of Jehovah has arisen upon her."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Seeing that light (spiritual) dispels the pre-existing darkness, He (God) saith . . ."
2. "Believers are called on to 'awake' out of sleep; unbelievers, to 'arise' from the dead."
3. "Shall shine upon thee" (so enabling thee by being "made manifest" to become, and be, by the very fact, "light").

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Be separate from sinners. Come out from among the pagan. Do not mingle with their abominations; do not name them. You are the children of light; and God says to you, awake from false security, rouse from the death of sin, and Christ shall enlighten you."
2. "Sleep and death" are striking representations of the state in which people are by nature. In "sleep" we are, though living, insensible to any danger that may be near; we are unconscious of what may he going on around us; we hear not the voice of our friends; we see not the beauty of the grove or the landscape; we are forgetful of our real character and condition. So With the sinner. It is as if his faculties were locked in a deep slumber.
3. "Christ is the light of the world...if they will use all the powers with which God has endowed them, and arouse from their spiritual slumber, and make an appropriate effort for salvation, then they may expect that Christ will shine upon them, and bless them in their efforts."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "This use of the personal pronoun is frequent in Paul's writings."
2. "The quotation is probably a combination and free rendering of Isaiah 60:1; Isaiah 26:19."
3. "Rev., correctly, shall shine upon thee."

### John Calvin:
1. "What other object do they propose than to raise the dead to life?"
2. "Let us therefore endeavor, as far as lies in our power, to rouse the sleeping and dead, that we may bring them to the light of Christ."
3. "That light from Christ flashes upon the awakened and resuscitated; nay, it awakens and resuscitates them. As it streams upon the dead, it startles them into life."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Let your light shine, so as to reprove, in the hope that Christ will shine upon the convicted heart."
2. "Awake thou that sleepest. 'Awake;' the word used in arousing a sleeper: 'up.'"
3. "And Christ shall shine upon thee. The figure is that of the morning sun; comp. Isaiah 60:1-2."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Wherefore he saith... Shall shine." 
2. "Apparently a free adaptation of Isaiah 26:19; Isaiah 60:1." 
3. "The last line suggests the possibility that we have here the fragment of an early Christian hymn like 1 Timothy 3:16."

### John Gill:
1. "awake ye that sleep, from your sleep, and ye that slumber rouse up from your slumber, and search into your actions, and return by repentance, and remember your Creator"
2. "awake thou that sleepest: the children of God are sometimes asleep, and need awaking"
3. "And arise from the dead; living saints are sometimes among dead sinners, and it becomes them to arise from among them"
