# Ephesians 5:21 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
As believers, we are called to subject ourselves to one another in the fear of Christ. This submission is not a result of fear of judgment, but rather a reverence for Christ as our Head. It is through our love for Him that we can truly be free, willingly serving and submitting to our fellow believers. This mutual submission extends to all areas of life, recognizing and respecting the authority and order that God has established. This submission is a reflection of our love for Christ and our desire to honor Him in all things.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The believer passes from under the bondage of the law as a letter, to be 'the servant of Christ'."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Submitting yourselves one to another - Maintaining due subordination in the various relations of life."
2. "The general meaning here is, that Christianity does not break up the relations of life, and produce disorder, lawlessness, and insubordination; but that it will confirm every proper authority, and make every just yoke lighter."
3. "Infidelity is always disorganizing; Christianity, never."

### John Calvin:
1. "God has bound us so strongly to each other, that no man ought to endeavor to avoid subjection; and where love reigns, mutual services will be rendered."
2. "I do not except even kings and governors, whose very authority is held for the service of the community."
3. "He directs us to the fear of Christ, who alone can subdue our fierceness, that we may not refuse the yoke, and can humble our pride, that we may not be ashamed of serving our neighbors."

### Philip Schaff:
