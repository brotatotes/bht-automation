# Ephesians 6:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Put on the entire armor of God - this divine and spiritual armor, supplied and ministered by God, is to be our defense against the crafty and insidious attacks of the devil. Just as the Apostle Paul would have witnessed the Roman method of arming in his imprisonment, believers are called to equip themselves with the complete panoply provided by God - offensive and defensive, without leaving any weak spots for Satan to exploit. With this God-given armor, we are able to stand our ground, to overcome the enemy, and to withstand the wiles and schemes of the devil.

### Henry Alford:
1. "Put on the entire armour of God."
2. "It is probable that the Apostle was daily familiarized in his imprisonment with the Roman method of arming."
3. "The armour of God is supplied, ministered by God."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "No opening at the head, the feet, the heart, the belly, the eye, the ear, or the tongue, is to be given to Satan."
2. "Believers have once for all overcome him; but on the ground of this fundamental victory gained over him, they are ever again to fight against and overcome him."
3. "Spiritual, therefore, and mighty through God, not carnal."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The armor of God" is not that which God wears, but that which he has provided for the Christian soldier.
2. "We are to put on the 'whole armor.' We are not to go armed partly with what God has appointed, and partly with such weapons as people use; nor are we to put on 'a part' of the armor only, but the 'whole' of it."
3. "Satan does not openly appear. He approaches us not in repulsive forms, but comes to recommend some plausible doctrine, to lay before us some temptation that shall not immediately repel us."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The armor is a defense against strategy as well as assault."
2. "The devil, used as the equivalent of Satan, of a single person, the enemy of mankind."
3. "The Septuagint usage implies enmity in general, without accusation either true or false."

### John Calvin:
1. "God has furnished us with various defensive weapons, provided we do not indolently refuse what is offered."
2. "We ought to be prepared on all sides, so as to want nothing. The Lord offers to us arms for repelling every kind of attack."
3. "He reminds us that we must not only engage in open warfare, but that we have a crafty and insidious foe to encounter, who frequently lies in ambush."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Put on the whole armour...the entire equipment, offensive and defensive...the choice troops of those days." 
2. "Of God. Supplied by God, 'altogether of a Divine kind, in contrast to the arms of the opponent'" 
3. "Against the wiles of the devil...the crafty assaults of the devil...he is the real enemy against whom we contend, and without the Divine equipment he will be too powerful for us."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Put on... The whole armour... Our 'panoply.'"
2. "That ye may be able to stand... Against... The wiles of the devil."
3. "He is a crafty foe and knows the weak spots in the Christian's armour."

### John Gill:
1. "the armour of God" - because it is prepared by him for his people, and is bestowed on them by him; and because it is in its own nature divine and spiritual, and not carnal."
2. "it is whole, complete, and perfect; and all of it is useful, and no part to be neglected, but all to be taken and 'put on.'"
3. "they not only stand their ground in the strength of Christ, and by the use of their armour confound his schemes, and baffle all his arts and stratagems, but are more than conquerors through him that has loved them."
