# Ephesians 6:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The shield of faith is crucial in the Christian's spiritual battle, as it intercepts and quenches all the fiery darts of the evil one. These darts, sometimes ablaze, are thrown by Satan from a distance, attacking through unsuspected avenues. Faith takes away the fuel for the enemy's darts by removing dependence on self and creating sensitivity to holy influences. By exercising strong and lively faith, the believer can confidently wield faith as a shield to withstand the furious and sudden temptations of the devil.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "As long as the soldier had his shield, he felt secure; and as long as a Christian has faith, he is safe. It comes to his aid in every attack that is made on him, no matter from what quarter; it is the defense and guardian of every other Christian grace; and it secures the protection which the Christian needs in the whole of the spiritual war."
2. "There seems a little incongruity in the idea of 'quenching' darts by 'a shield.' But the word 'quench,' here, means only that they would be 'put out' by being thrown 'against' the shield, as a candle would by being thrown against anything."
3. "By the 'fiery darts of the wicked,' Paul here refers, probably, to the temptations of the great adversary, which are like fiery darts; or those furious suggestions of evil, and excitements to sin, which he may throw into the mind like fiery darts."

### Marvin Vincent:
