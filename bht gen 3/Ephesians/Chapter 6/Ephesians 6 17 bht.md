# Ephesians 6:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Take the helmet of salvation and receive it as the Lord offers. Let this protective helmet defend your mind from false doctrine and Satan's temptations to despair. With the hope of salvation firmly in place, your soul is fortified to resist the enemy without turning back. Wield the sword of the Spirit, the word of God, which serves as both an offensive and defensive weapon. The interpretation of this sword requires the aid of the Spirit. Remember, our victory lies in salvation, our hope in Christ, and the power of God's word.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The head is the seat of the mind, which, when it has laid hold of the sure Gospel 'hope' of eternal life, will not receive false doctrine, or give way to Satan's temptations to despair."
2. "The two-edged sword, cutting both ways, striking some with conviction and conversion, and others with condemnation, is in the mouth of Christ, in the hand of His saints."
3. "There is no armor specified for the back, but only for the front of the body; implying that we must never turn our back to the foe; our only safety is in resisting ceaselessly."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The helmet defended the head, a vital part; and so the hope of salvation will defend the soul, and keep it from the blows of the enemy."
2. "A soldier would not fight well without a hope of victory. A Christian could not contend with his foes, without the hope of final salvation; but, sustained by this, what has he to dread?"
3. "A single text of Scripture is better to meet a temptation, than all the philosophy which the world contains. The tempter can reason, and reason plausibly too. But he cannot resist a direct and positive command of the Almighty."
