### Henry Alford:
1. "In [at] the opening of my mouth, i.e. when I undertake to speak: thus we keep the meaning of opening the mouth..." 
2. "The best rendering is, 'in boldness (freedom of speech) to make known the mystery of the gospel."
3. "The mystery of the gospel, contained in the gospel: subjective genitive."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Translate, therefore, 'on my behalf.'" (Ephesians 6:19) 
2. "Bold plainness of speech was the more needed, as the Gospel is a 'mystery' undiscoverable by mere reason, and only known by revelation." (Ephesians 6:19) 
3. "Paul looked for utterance to be given him, he did not depend on his natural or acquired power." (Ephesians 6:19)

### Marvin Vincent:

### John Calvin:
1. "There is no man so richly endowed with gifts as not to need this kind of assistance from his brethren, so long as he remains in this world."
2. "Who will ever be better entitled to plead exemption from this necessity than Paul? Yet he entreats the prayers of his brethren, and not hypocritically, but from an earnest desire of their aid."
3. "And what does he wish that they should ask for him? That utterance may be given to me."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "And on my behalf, especially for him among the saints. Not for himself, but for the sake of the gospel he thus speaks."
2. "In boldness is emphatic, and to be connected with to make known, etc., which indicates the purpose for which he desired utterance and asked their supplications."
3. "The mystery of the gospel; the mystery contained in the gospel; comp. chaps. Ephesians 1:9; Ephesians 3:9."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "That utterance may be given unto me."
2. "Paul wishes their prayer for courage for himself."

### John Gill:
1. "This shows the great humility of the apostle, and the sense he had of the greatness of the work of the ministry; and that it is the duty of people to pray for their ministers; and that no man is perfect in this life."
2. "The apostle desired he might have something to say, to object to, and to reprove and convince the unbelieving Jews that he might do this with boldness, with all faithfulness with Courage, and intrepidity, and with freedom of speech."
3. "Now the apostle's work was to make known these mysteries, to prove the truth of them from the word of God, and to defend them against the opposers of them; and that he might be able to do this he entreats the prayers of the saints."
