# Galatians 1:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
There is only one Gospel, and any claim of a different Gospel is not truly another Gospel, but rather a perversion of the one true Gospel of Christ. This false teaching troubled the Galatians and sought to subvert the true Gospel of grace and freedom in Christ. Recognizing the leading points of the pure Gospel is crucial, for when they are attacked, the very existence of the Gospel is at stake.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "[The different Gospel] is not another, not a second, besides the one Gospel; except that there are some that trouble you." 
2. "That is: 'This different Gospel is only in so far another, that there are certain, who&c.'"
3. "The nature of this 'different Gospel,' as gathered from the data in our Epistle, was (1), though recognizing Jesus as the Christ, it insisted on circumcision and the observance of the Mosaic ordinances as to times, &c.: (2) it professed to rest on the authority of some of the other Apostles."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "There is really but one Gospel, and no other gospel."
2. "All I meant by the 'different gospel' was nothing but a perversion by 'some' of the one Gospel of Christ."
3. "Though acknowledging Christ, they insisted on circumcision and Jewish ordinances and professed to rest on the authority of other apostles, namely, Peter and James. But Paul recognizes no gospel, save the pure Gospel."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Which, however, is not another gospel, nor indeed the gospel at all, or true."
2. "Though this is most manifestly another system, and not the gospel at all, yet there are some persons who are capable of giving trouble and of unsettling your minds, by making it plausible."
3. "The tendency of their doctrine is wholly to turn away, to destroy, or render useless the gospel of Christ."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "A different gospel is not another gospel. There is but one gospel."
2. "There is no other gospel, but there are some who trouble you with a different kind of teaching which they offer as a gospel."
3. "The article with the participle marks these persons as characteristically troublesome - the troublers."

### John Calvin:
1. "They merely trouble you, and subvert the gospel. They do nothing more."
2. "Subversion is an enormous crime. It is worse than corruption."
3. "To know what are the leading points of the gospel, is a matter of unceasing importance. When these are attacked, the gospel is destroyed."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Which (pseudo-gospel of the heretical teachers) is not another, i.e., no gospel at all, but a perversion and corruption of the one unchangeable gospel." 
2. "The former makes good works the effect, the latter the cause, of justification; and this is thus in fact a relapse into the Jewish standpoint under a Christian name." 
3. "Only in this sense is it another gospel that it is a perversion of the true gospel of Christ by those well-known troublers of your conscience."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "It is no 'gospel' (good news) at all, but a yoke of bondage to the law and the abolition of grace."
2. "'Only (Îµ Î¼Î·). Literally, 'except,' that is, 'Except in this sense,' 'in that it is an attempt to pervert the one true gospel.'"
3. "The very existence of the gospel of Christ was at stake."

### John Gill:
1. "There is but one pure Gospel of the grace of God, and Christ, and his apostles; there is not one and another; there is but one faith, one doctrine and scheme of faith; the Gospel is single and uniform, all of a piece, has no yea and nay, or contradiction in it; this trumpet gives no uncertain sound, nor any dreadful, but a joyful one."
2. "These troubled the churches with their doctrines and principles, by raising disputes and controversies among them, injecting doubts and scruples into their minds, which puzzled and confounded them, and made them uneasy, and which broke in upon that peace of soul which the Gospel brings and establishes."
3. "And would pervert the Gospel of Christ which has Christ for its author, subject, and preacher; and particularly the doctrine of justification by his righteousness, which they sought to change, to throw into a different shape and form, to adulterate by mixing it with the works of the law, and so, if possible, destroy it."

### John Wesley:
1. "For what ye have now received is no gospel at all; it is not glad, but heavy, tidings, as setting your acceptance with God upon terms impossible to be performed."
2. "But there are some that trouble you."
3. "And would subvert or overthrow the gospel of Christ."
