# Galatians 6:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
These proponents of circumcision, who themselves fail to keep the law, exhibit hypocrisy. Their focus on circumcision is driven by a desire to evade persecution and flaunt their external change, rather than a sincere commitment to the law. They selectively choose circumcision as a means to boast and seek acclaim, neglecting the weightier matters of the law such as judgment, mercy, and faith. Their insistence on circumcision exposes their insincerity and lack of genuine devotion to God's commands.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "For (proof that they wish only to escape persecution) not even they who are being circumcised themselves keep the law..."
2. "But wish you to be circumcised, that in your flesh they may make their boast..."
3. "In this way they escaped the scandal of the Cross at the hands of the Jews, by making in fact their Christian converts into Jewish proselytes."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "For not even do they who submit to circumcision, keep the law themselves, but they wish you to be circumcised..." (Galatians 6:13). 
2. "They arbitrarily selected circumcision out of the whole law, as though observing it would stand instead of their non-observance of the rest of the law." 
3. "That they may glory in your flesh, namely, in the outward change... which they have effected in bringing you over to their own Jewish-Christian party."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Paul insists on it that if they were circumcised, and depended on that for salvation, they were under obligation to keep the whole law. But they did not."
2. "No person perfectly observes the whole law of God."
3. "They may glory in your flesh - In having you as converts, and in persuading you to be circumcised, that they may show their zeal for the Law, and thus escape persecution."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The persons referred to are the same as those in ver. 12. The participle tells nothing as to the antecedents of these persons, whether Jewish or heathen. It is general, those who are receiving circumcision."
2. "Hence, if circumcision develops no justifying results, it is apparent that their insistence on circumcision proceeds not from moral, but from fleshly motives."
3. "They desire only that you, like them, should make a fair show in the flesh."

### John Calvin:
1. "It is not from a strong attachment to the law that they bind you with the yoke of ceremonies; for, even with their own circumcision, they do not keep the law."
2. "He rather insinuates against them a charge of insincerity, because, except when it suited their own designs, they found themselves at liberty to despise the law."
3. "They wish to triumph over you, and to gratify their own desire of applause, by offering up your mutilated flesh to the false zealots of the law, as a token of peace and harmony."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "For not even they who are circumcised keep the law (in all its details, comp. Galatians 5:3)."
2. "The advocates of circumcision are not sincere in their zeal, but want to gratify their vanity in making proselytes."
3. "Comp. Matthew 23:15."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They who receive circumcision." 
2. "Those who are having themselves circumcised."
3. "They who have been circumcised."

### John Gill:
1. "But neither they themselves that are circumcised,.... That is, the pleaders for, and preachers of circumcision, whether they themselves were actually circumcised, or not; it means those that were of the circumcision, on that side of the question, whether heartily or not."
2. "These men were so zealous for the lighter matters of the law, the rites and ceremonies of it, as circumcision, c. they disregarded the weightier and more material parts of it, as judgment, mercy, and faith these they kept not, nor attended to."
3. "Their view in bringing persons to submit to circumcision was, that they might have occasion to glory among the Jews, and boast to them of the numbers of proselytes they brought, and of the service they did to Judaism, and so increase their fame and glory among them."

3. "And make a merit of this with the other Jews."
