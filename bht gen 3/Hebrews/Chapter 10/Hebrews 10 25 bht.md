# Hebrews 10:25 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Gathering together as a congregation signifies the assembled body and should not be neglected. The day of Christ's second coming, which brings judgment, is always near. Assembling together fosters love, provokes good works, and strengthens unity. It is a sacred duty for believers to gather for public worship, as our graces are enriched in community. We should be diligent and encourage one another, especially as the day of Christ's return draws nearer. Neglecting the act of assembling may result in spiritual loss and potential apostasy.

### Henry Alford:
1. "The congregation has come from the act of assembling to signify the body thus assembled."
2. "This pretty plainly shows that not formal apostasies, but habits of negligence, are in the Writer's view."
3. "That Day, indeed, in its great final sense, is always near, always ready to break forth upon the church."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The assembling or gathering of ourselves for Christian communion in private and public, is an earnest of our being gathered together to Him at His appearing."
2. "Union is strength; continual assemblings together beget and foster love, and give good opportunities for 'provoking to good works,' by 'exhorting one another'."
3. "He avoids the Greek term 'sunagoge,' as suggesting the Jewish synagogue meetings."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "With all who bear the Christian name, with all who expect to make advances in piety and religious knowledge, it should be regarded as a sacred duty to assemble together for public worship."
2. "Religion is social; and our graces are to be strengthened and invigorated by waiting together on the Lord."
3. "We should be the more diligent when we see the indications that the great Messenger is about to come to summon us into the presence of our final Judge."
