# Hebrews 11:30 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
By faith, the people of Israel obeyed the command of Joshua, persevering through the seemingly irrational act of surrounding the walls of Jericho for seven days. They believed that faith can do all things and trusted that the walls would fall as God had promised. This story displays the power of faith in accomplishing what appears impossible, even when the means used seem inadequate. It serves as a reminder that when we have faith in God, we can be confident that He will act in His own time and in His own way.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "By faith (of Israel, who obeyed the command of Joshua through all the days, which to the unbeliever would seem irrational."
2. "Chrysostom, says, 'For there is no power in the sound of a trumpet to throw down stones, even if a man go on blowing for a thousand years: but faith can do all things'"
3. "the Walls of Jericho fell, having been compassed about during seven days."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The soundings of trumpets, though one were to sound for ten thousand years, cannot throw down walls, but faith can do all things." 
2. "Sieges often lasted for years."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "It was only by confidence in God that means so little adapted to such a purpose could have been employed at all."
2. "The strength of the faith evinced on this occasion appears from such circumstances as these: ... that there was great apparent improbability that the effect would follow."
3. "Yet let them not despair. Let them blow on. Let them still lift up their voice with faith in God, and in due time the walls of the citadel will totter and fall."

### John Calvin:
1. "As he had before taught us, that the yoke of bondage was by faith broken asunder, so now he tells us, that by the same faith the people gained the possession of the promised land."
2. "It appeared to be a work childish and ridiculous; and yet they obeyed the divine command; nor did they do so in vain, for success according to the promise followed."
3. "It is evident, that the walls did not fall through the shout of men, or the sound of trumpets; but because the people believed that the Lord would do what he had promised."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The writer now leaves the Book of the Law for the Book of Joshua, the record of the conquest of the land and of the complete fulfillment of the ancient promise."
2. "By faith (of Joshua and the whole people, the correlative of that Divine power which really did the deed) the walls, etc."
3. "As the great deliverance from Egypt was effected by faith and the boldness it produced, so the first victory in Canaan was achieved by persevering faith, the wall having been compassed about for seven whole days."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Fell down." 
2. "After they had been compassed." 
3. "Antecedent action here."

### John Gill:
1. "And this was by the faith of Joshua, and the Israelites, who believed the walls would fall, at the sound of the rams' horns, as God said they should: after they were compassed about seven days... faith stops at nothing, when it has the word of God to rest upon." 
2. "What God does, He does in his own time, and in his own way."
3. "It may be also an emblem of the fall of Babylon, and other antichristian cities."

### John Wesley:
1. By the faith of Joshua.
