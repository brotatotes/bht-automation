# Hebrews 12:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Looking away from distractions and fixing our eyes on Jesus, the pioneer and leader of our faith, we are encouraged to endure the present pain in light of the coming joy. Jesus perfected faith through his example, renouncing the joy already in his possession in exchange for shame and death. He endured the cross with patience and constancy, despising the shame, and in doing so, he became the author of eternal salvation. As we face trials and suffering, we must emulate Jesus' unwavering faith and trust that all our troubles will ultimately lead to our salvation and glory.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Looking from afar" (see on :-); fixing the eyes upon Jesus seated on the throne of God.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He is the first and the last as an example of faith or of confidence in God - occupying in this, as in all other things, the pre-eminence, and being the most complete model that can be placed before us."
2. "He stands at the head as the most eminent example that can be referred to on the subject of faith."
3. "He, in view of the honor and joy before him, endured the most severe sufferings to which the human frame can be subjected, and the form of death which is regarded as the most shameful. So amidst all the severe trials to which you are exposed on account of religion, patiently endure all - for the glorious rewards, the happiness and the triumph of heaven, are before you."
