### Albert Barnes:
1. "The word 'peace' in the New Testament is used to denote every kind of blessing or happiness."

### John Calvin:
1. "We are by no means fit to do good until we are made or formed for the purpose by God, and that we shall not continue long in doing good unless he strengthens us; for perseverance is his peculiar gift."
2. "God is then only prayed to aright by us, to lead us on to perfection, when we acknowledge his power in the resurrection of Christ, and acknowledge Christ himself as our pastor."
3. "God raised up his own son, but in such a way that the blood he shed once for all in his death is efficacious after his resurrection for the ratification of the everlasting covenant, and brings forth fruit the same as though it were flowing always."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "God is the author and giver of peace."
2. "The shepherd of the sheep the great one."
3. "The language reminds us of Christ's own words about 'my blood of the covenant.'"

### John Gill:
1. "The God of peace is manifestly God the Father, who is distinguished from Christ his Son."
2. "The apostle addresses the God and Father of Christ in prayer, under this consideration, to observe his power and ability to help in the greatest distress, and in the most difficult and desperate case; to encourage faith and hope in him, when things are at the worst, and most discouraging."
3. "The blood of Christ may be called the blood of it [the covenant], because the shedding of it is a principal article in it; by it the covenant is ratified and confirmed; and all the blessings of it come through it."

### John Wesley:
1. "The Christian covenant, which is not temporary, like the Jewish, but designed to remain for ever."
2. "By the application of that blood, may he make you, inwardly and outwardly holy!"
