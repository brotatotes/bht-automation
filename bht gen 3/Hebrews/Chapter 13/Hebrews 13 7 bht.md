# Hebrews 13:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Remember your leaders who spoke the word of God to you. Immerse yourself in contemplating their faith and the outcome of their lives. Look up to them as guides and diligently observe their admonitions. Focusing on the end of their conversation, their death, and their faithful testimony, consider the issues of their lives. By remembering their teachings and emulating their faith, we can be inspired to lead lives of holiness.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Remember...your leaders...the which spoke to you the word of God...of whom surveying the termination of their conversation, imitate the faith."
2. "Remember to...imitate them [the leaders in the faith]."
3. "It is remembering with a view to imitation that is enjoined."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Rememberâ€”so as to imitate: not to invoke in prayer, as Rome teaches."
2. "Looking up to," "diligently contemplating all over," as an artist would a model.
3. "Considering how they manifested the soundness of their faith by their holy walk, which they maintained even to the end of that walk (their death by martyrdom)."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Remember them which have the rule over you... The duty here enjoined is that of remembering them; that is, remembering their counsel; their instructions; their example." 
2. "Respect is to be shown to the ministerial office, by whomsoever it is borne." 
3. "The proper remembrance of those now deceased who were once our spiritual instructors and guides should be allowed to have an important influence in inducing us to lead a holy life."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Remember them which have the rule over you...with a view to observing their admonitions." 
2. "Follow, or imitate."
3. "Considering...the end of their conversation, meaning outcome or issue."

### John Calvin:
1. "He first sets before the Jews the example of those by whom they had been taught."
2. "...those who had persevered in the true faith to the end, and had rendered a faithful testimony to sound doctrine through their whole life as well as in death."
3. "Since then they had seen them continuing firm and unmoved in the midst of much persecutions and of various other conflicts, they ought in all reason to have been deeply moved and affected."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Leadership is the prominent thought with so much of ruling as is essential to lead."
2. "The which (who have this quality that a word which defines the ground and the limit of their authority) have spoken to you the word of God (the Gospel);"
3. "Whose faith (not their creed, but their blessed trust in trouble and fidelity to principle) copy (or imitate), thoroughly considering what a blessed end their life had."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Keep in mind the preaching of these early disciples, apostles, and prophets."
2. "Consider the issue of whose life and imitate their faith."
3. "Few lessons are harder for the average Christian to learn, viz., good following."

### John Gill:
1. "The word may be rendered "guides" or "leaders"; for such point out the way of peace, life, and salvation to men, and direct them to Christ; and guide them into the understanding of the Scriptures, and the truths of the Gospel; and lead them in the paths of faith and holiness, and are examples to them."
2. "To 'remember' them is to know, own, acknowledge, and respect them as their governors; to obey them, and submit to them; to treasure up in memory their doctrines and exhortations; to be mindful of them at the throne of grace, to pray for them; and to take care of their maintenance and outward supply of life."
3. "Considering the end of their conversation; which may intend the whole of their conduct in the discharge of the several duties of their office; the end of which designs either the manner of it... or the drift and scope of it, which was Christ, his honour and glory, as in connection with the following verse; or the event of it in life, being for the glory of God, and the good of men; or rather the issue of it in death, or what a comfortable end they made."
