# Hebrews 3:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
We have become partakers of Christ by holding fast the beginning of our confidence firm until the end. This means that the faith with which we began our Christian life is to be maintained throughout, as it is the proof of our election. Therefore, let us not be deceived into apostasy, but rather, let us persevere and stand strong until the end, knowing that our spiritual union with Christ lasts throughout our entire lives and into eternity.

### Henry Alford:
1. "For we have become partakers of Christ, if, that is, we hold fast the beginning of our confidence firm unto the end."
2. "Some take these words to signify 'fellow-partakers with Christ;' but improperly."
3. "The end thought of is, not the death of each individual, but the coming of the Lord, which is constantly called by this name."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "partakers of the Holy Ghost" (Hebrews 3:6)
2. "hold fast." (Hebrews 3:14)
3. "the beginning of our confidence... A Christian so long as he is not made perfect, considers himself as a beginner" [BENGEL] (Hebrews 3:14)

### Albert Barnes:
1. "We are spiritually united to the Saviour. We become one with him. We partake of his spirit and his allotments."
2. "The Christian is to be distinguished in temper, feeling, deportment, aims, plans, from the people of this world - and unless those characteristics are shown in the life and deportment, there can be no well-founded evidence of religion."
3. "Religion is something that lasts throughout life. It goes with a person everywhere...It lives with him in all his external changes, and goes with him through the dark valley of death, and accompanies him up to the bar of God, and is with him forever."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "We are made partakers of Christ...we are become fellows with Christ."
2. "Beginning of our confidence...The believing confidence with which we began our Christian life."
3. "Unto the end...the point into which the whole life of faith finally gathers itself up."

### John Calvin:
1. "He commends them for having begun well; but lest, under the pretext of the grace which they had obtained, they should indulge themselves in carnal security, he says that there was need of perseverance..."
2. "Thus it is that they not only stop in the middle of their race, yea, nigh the startingÂ­posts, but turn another way."
3. "The sum of the whole then is, that faith whose beginnings only appear in us, is to make constant and steady progress to the end."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "We are made partaken...we are now what we were not originally." 
2. "The words describe a present character and an acquired character." 
3. "On this condition we are partakers of Christ, united with Him...even as He is united with us."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "We have become partners with Christ and hence should not be tricked into apostasy."
2. "The sure proof of election is that one holds out to the end."
3. "These faltering believers (some even apostates) began with loud confidence and profession of loyalty. And now?"

### John Gill:
1. "For we are made partakers of Christ...this respects something past, and may be rendered, 'we have been made'."
2. "By 'confidence' is meant faith, which is an hypostasis, or subsistence...it gives a kind of subsistence, substance, or being to things it is concerned with."
3. "The 'beginning' of it, which is to be held fast, is either Christ himself...or else the grace of faith itself, which is a grace but begun, not yet finished, but shall continue."

### John Wesley:
1. "We shall still partake of him and all his benefits, if we hold fast our faith unto the end."
2. "A supposition made by the Holy Ghost is equal to the strongest assertion."
3. "Both the sentiment and the manner of expression are the same."
