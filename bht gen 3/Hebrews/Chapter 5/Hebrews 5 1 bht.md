# Hebrews 5:1 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Every high priest, chosen from among men, is appointed by God to act on behalf of humanity in matters relating to God. They offer gifts and sacrifices for sins, without any clear distinction between the two. Christ, as our ultimate High Priest, possesses all the qualities of the Levitical priests and more. His empathy for sinners and divine appointment enable him to efficiently fulfill his role as mediator between God and humankind. With both gifts and sacrifices, he encompasses all types of offerings.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Every high priest, being taken from among men, is appointed for men, in matters relating to God, that he may offer both gifts and sacrifices for sins." 
2. "The stress is both times on this noun and its preposition, 'being taken from among men, is appointed for men,' the former justifying the latter." 
3. "No satisfactory distinction can be set up between gifts and sacrifices: properly speaking, the former would be any manner of offerings, the latter slain beasts only: but this usage is not observed in Scripture."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Whatever is excellent in the Levitical priests, is also in Christ, and besides excellencies which are not in the Levitical priests."
2. "Being 'from men,' He can be 'for (that is, in behalf of, for the good of) men.'"
3. "'Sacrifices' are the animal sacrifices due according to the legal ordinance."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The proper office of a priest is to present a 'sacrifice' for sin."
2. "It is 'improper' to give the name 'priest' to a minister of the gospel."
3. "The ministers of the gospel are appointed to proclaim that truth to men, not to offer sacrifices for sin."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The point is that the high priest's efficiency for men depends on his being taken from among men."
2. "The priest's efficiency is especially called out, and he who has not genuine compassion for the sinful cannot do this efficiently."
3. "Every high priest is ordained for men, on behalf of men, in things pertaining to God."

### John Calvin:
1. "For had he been taken from among angels or any other beings, we could not by him be united to God, as he could not reach down to us."
2. "The salvation of us all is connected with and revolves on the priesthood of Christ."
3. "We have no intercourse with God, except there be a priest; for, as we are unholy, what have we to do with holy things?"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Taken as he is from among men affirms part of the quality of a priest, and is so regarded by most commentators."
2. "Is ordained; properly, 'is appointed;' 'ordained even as Aaron was [ordained],' misleads. Ordination in any technical sense is not here, but Divine appointment simply."
3. "Both gifts and sacrifices for sins are naturally the offerings or gifts of the law other than sin offerings and the sacrifices... Both the ideas are blended in the work of our Lord, 'who gave Himself for us.'"
