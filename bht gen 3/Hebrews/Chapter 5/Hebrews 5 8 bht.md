# Hebrews 5:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Though He was God's divine Son, Jesus learned His obedience through sufferings. As the Son, He was always obedient, but the specific obedience required to qualify Him as our High Priest was learned through practical suffering. Jesus' training for the priesthood involved suffering, even though he was a Son. He did not have to learn to obey, but required the discipline of severe human experiences to be a high priest who could sympathize with us. Through suffering, Jesus learned what it meant to obey God fully and willingly.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Though He WAS God's divine Son, yet He learned His obedience, not from His Sonship, but from His sufferings."
2. "As the Son, He was always obedient to the Father's will; but the special obedience needed to qualify Him as our High Priest, He learned experimentally in practical suffering."
3. "Praying and obeying, as in Christ's case, ought to go hand in hand."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Though the Son of God...he was willing to learn experimentally what is meant by obedience in the midst of sufferings."
2. "The 'object' here is, to show how well suited the Lord Jesus was to be a Saviour for mankind...to lead us 'to obey God.'"
3. "Some of the most valuable lessons of obedience are learned in the furnace of affliction; and many of the most submissive children of the Almighty have been made so as the result of protracted woes."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "His training for the priesthood involved suffering, even though he was a son."
2. "Jesus did not have to learn to obey, but he required the special discipline of a severe human experience as a training for his office as a high priest who could be touched with the feeling of human infirmities."
3. "Growth in experience was an essential part of his humanity."
