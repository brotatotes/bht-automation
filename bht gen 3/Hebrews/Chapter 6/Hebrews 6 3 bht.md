# Hebrews 6:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Let us go on to perfection, allowing God to work in us both to will and to do of his good pleasure. However, without God's blessing and guidance, our efforts to progress will be in vain. The writer warns that if we have once fallen away, it becomes impossible for us to repent on our own. We must acknowledge our complete dependence on God, as progress in our spiritual journey is a special gift from Him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "This will we do: what? go on to perfection."
2. "It is God who worketh in us both to will and to do of his good pleasure."
3. "If, I say, 'God permit': for when men have once fallen away, it is a thing impossible."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Let us do." "This," that is, "Go on unto perfection." 
2. "For even in the case of good resolutions, we cannot carry them into effect, save through God 'working in us both to will and to do of His good pleasure'".
3. "Without God's blessing, the cultivation of the ground does not succeed."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "We will make these advances toward a higher state of knowledge and piety."
2. "If he would spare their lives, their health, and their reason; if he would continue the means of grace, and would impart his Holy Spirit; if he would favor their efforts and crown them with success, they would make these advances."
3. "In reference to anything that we undertake, however pleasing to God in itself, it is proper to recognize our entire dependence on God."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "An ominous hint is conveyed that the spiritual dullness of the readers may prevent the writer from developing his theme and them from receiving his higher instruction."
2. "The issue is dependent on the power which God may impart to his teaching, but his efforts may be thwarted by the impossibility of repentance on their part."
3. "No such impossibility is imposed by God, but it may reside in a moral condition which precludes the efficient action of the agencies which work for repentance, so that God cannot permit the desired consequence to follow the word of teaching."

### John Calvin:
1. "There ought not in this case it to be any delay; for there will not always be the opportunity for making progress." 
2. "It is not in man's power to bound at once, whenever he pleases, from the starting point to the goal."
3. "Progress in our course is the special gift of God."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Let us try to raise each other to the higher ground of matured intelligence."
2. "Whether any of us have so far forfeited His grace as to be incapable of further progress, God only knows."
3. "The position is therefore very solemn, will anyhow need special help, and the work may be even impossible."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He means that he will go on with the argument and not attempt to lay again the foundation (the elements)." 
2. "Moffatt takes him to mean that he will teach them the elements at a later time (Hebrews 13:23) if the way opens, a less probable interpretation."

### John Gill:
1. "It is, or at least should be, the determination of a Gospel minister, to preach Christ, and the great truths of the Gospel; and wheresoever God has called him to it, though there may be many adversaries, and though he may be reproached, calumniated, and deserted: resolution in preaching the Gospel, and adhering to it, is very commendable in a minister."
2. "It is very laudable in hearers to attend to it, stand by it, and search further into it; and which both should determine upon with a regard to the will of God."
3. "God's permission is much to be observed in the ministry of the word, in giving gifts to men, in placing them out here and there, where they shall exercise them, in directing them to subjects, and in making their ministry useful and successful."
