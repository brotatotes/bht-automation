# Hebrews 7:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The old law, weak and unprofitable, could not bring true perfection or sanctification before God. Through faith, the better hope found in Christ allows us to approach God's throne directly. This new system brings us the possibility of intimate fellowship with God, without the need for a priest as a mediator. By fulfilling the promises and prophecies of the Old Testament, Christ's priesthood brings perfect atonement, reconciliation, righteousness, and redemption, enabling us to draw near to God and experience true perfection.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The law had not the power to bring any thing whatever to perfection, to its appointed end and excellence."
2. "The contrast is between the preceding commandment, weak and unprofitable, and a better thing, viz. the hope which brings us near to God."
3. "We being justified by faith can approach the very throne of God."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The law could not bring men to true justification or sanctification before God, which is the 'perfection' that we all need in order to be accepted of Him, and which we have in Christ."
2. "The law brought nothing to its perfected end everything in it was introductory to its antitype in the Christian economy, which realizes the perfection contemplated."
3. "The access through the legal sacrifices was only symbolical and through the medium of a priest; that through Christ is immediate, perfect, and spiritual."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The Levitical, ceremonial law. It did not produce a perfect state; it did not do what was desirable to be done for a sinner..."
2. "There is now the introduction of a better system by which we can approach a reconciled God."
3. "We have access to him; notes, Romans 5:1-2."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The writer means to say is that, according to the Psalm, there takes place, on the one hand, a disannulling of the preliminary commandment because it was weak and unprofitable, unable to perfect anything, and on the other hand, the introduction of a better hope." 
2. "Christianity is the religion of good hope because by it men first enter into intimate fellowship with God. The old priesthood could not effect this." 
3. "The new priesthood was established with the oath of God; it is held perpetually by one person, in contrast with the old priesthood which was administered by a succession of priests."

### John Calvin:
1. "The Law was only a beginning; then something more perfect was necessarily, to follow; for it is not fit that Godâ€™s children should always continue in childish elements."
2. "By a better hope is to be understood the condition of the faithful under the reign of Christ... They were therefore led by the hand of the Law as a schoolmaster, that they might advance farther."
3. "And whosoever continues in the Law, knowingly and willingly deprives himself of the privilege of approaching nigh to God."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "And yet law is necessary."
2. "A bringing in thereupon...for the introduction of a new wife in place of the repudiated one."
3. "This better hope does bring us near to God as we come close to God's throne through Christ."

### John Gill:
1. "The law made nothing perfect... it could not perfectly make atonement for sin; nor make men perfectly holy or righteous; it could neither justify nor sanctify..."
2. "But the bringing in of a better hope did... the priesthood of Christ... is generally understood, which is the ground of hope; for all promises respecting eternal life are confirmed by it, and all blessings connected with it procured..."
3. "...the bringing in of him [Christ] and his priesthood did make something perfect; it brought to perfection all the types, promises, and prophecies of the Old Testament, the whole law, moral and ceremonial; it brought in perfect atonement, reconciliation, pardon, righteousness, and redemption..."

### John Wesley:
1. "The law...made nothing perfect."
2. "The bringing in of a better hope...does [make us draw nigh to God]."
3. "Yea, so nigh as to be one spirit with him. And this is true perfection."
