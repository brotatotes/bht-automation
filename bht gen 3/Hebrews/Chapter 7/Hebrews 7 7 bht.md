## BHT:
The principle that the blesser is superior to him whom he blesses holds good in a blessing given with divine authority. Christ's blessing exemplifies this, as it is divinely efficient in working its purport. It is an admitted point about which there can be no dispute that the one who blesses is greater than the one who is blessed. Melchisedec's act of blessing Abraham showcases this, as it implies superiority of rank. The writer of Hebrews asserts a self-evident truth that the act of pronouncing a blessing implies a higher honor and authority.
