# Hebrews 9:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Because of the all-cleansing power of His blood, Jesus is fit to be the Mediator of the new covenant, securing forgiveness for sins not covered by the former imperfect covenant and guaranteeing an eternal inheritance. At the moment of His death, the Old Testament gave way to the New Testament. The Levitical system was insufficient for full salvation, but Christ's sacrifice as the Mediator of the new covenant is the basis for the salvation of all who believe, past and present. Through Christ, those who are called can receive the promise of the eternal inheritance.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "This pre-eminent spiritual virtue of His redeeming blood constitutes his fitness to be the Mediator of the new covenant." (Quote 1)
2. "The main blessing of which, forgiveness, extends even back over the insufficient former one, and ensures the inheritances to the called." (Quote 2)
3. "This end, of the called being put in possession of the promise of the eternal inheritance, is to be attained 'by death having taken place for the propitiation of the transgressions under the first testament.'" (Quote 3)

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Because of the all-cleansing power of His blood, this fits Him to be Mediator of the new covenant, which secures both forgiveness for the sins not covered by the former imperfect covenant or testament, and also an eternal inheritance to the called."
2. "At the moment that His death took place, the necessary effect is, 'the called receive the (fulfilment of the) promise' that moment divides the Old from the New Testament."
3. "The 'first testament' thus includes the whole period from Adam to Christ, and not merely that of the covenant with Israel, which was a concentrated representation of the covenant made with mankind by sacrifice, down from the fall to redemption."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He is the Mediator between God and man in respect to that new covenant which he has made, or that new dispensation by which people are to be saved." 
2. "That by means of death - His own death as a sacrifice for sin." 
3. "The blood of Christ would now avail for the remission of all those sins which could not be expiated by the sacrifices offered under the Law."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The efficacy of Christ's sacrifice is bound up with a covenant. His priesthood involves a new and a better covenant."
2. "The intimation is in the same direction with that of the phrase through an eternal spirit - that the ideal redemption must be eternal."
3. "The whole statement implies that the provisions of the Levitical system were inadequate to procure and ensure full salvation."

### John Calvin:
1. "He alone can be justly deemed a Mediator."
2. "If the Law was so weak that all the remedies it applied for expiating sins did by no means accomplish what they represented, who could rest in it as in a safe harbor?"
3. "We obtain [full redemption] in [Christ], there is nothing which can any more distress us."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The death of Christ...extends its efficacy forward to the end of time and backward to the entrance of the Law."
2. "It is the procuring cause of forgiveness for all dispensations."
3. "They that have been called may receive the promise of the eternal inheritance."
