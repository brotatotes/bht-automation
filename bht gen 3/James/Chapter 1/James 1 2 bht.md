# James 1:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
When facing trials, believers should count them as matters of pure joy, devoid of sorrow. These trials, encountered along the Christian journey, may initially cause grief and sorrow, but as children of God guided by the Spirit, they can rise above the sorrow of the flesh. Trials serve as tests of faith and other graces of the Spirit, working for the spiritual and eternal good of believers, leading them to the peaceable fruit of righteousness. So, when unexpectedly falling into various trials, count it as the highest form of joy, exhibiting patience and trust in God.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Count all 'divers temptations' to be each matter of joy [BENGEL]." 
2. "Nothing but joy [PISCATOR]." 
3. "Every possible trial to the child of God is a masterpiece of strategy of the Captain of his salvation for his good."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Count it all joy - Regard it as a thing to rejoice in; a matter which should afford you happiness. You are not to consider it as a punishment, a curse, or a calamity, but as a fit subject of felicitation." 
2. "These [trials] cannot be said to be direct inducements or allurements to sin, but they try the faith, and they show whether he who is tried is disposed to adhere to his faith in God, or whether he will apostatize."
3. "They were to count it a matter of joy that their religion was subjected to anything that tried it. It is well for us to have the reality of our religion tested, in whatever way it may be done."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Count it a thing wholly joyful, without admixture of sorrow."
2. "Temptation may be expected all along the Christian course."
3. "Manifold trials in the general sense of trials."

### John Calvin:
1. "He means, in short, that there is nothing in afflictions which ought to disturb our joy." 
2. "It is, indeed, certain, that all the senses of our nature are so formed, that every trial produces in us grief and sorrow; and no one of us can so far divest himself of his nature as not to grieve and be sorrowful whenever he feels any evil. But this does not prevent the children of God to rise, by the guidance of the Spirit, above the sorrow of the flesh." 
3. "Hence it is, that in the midst of trouble they cease not to rejoice."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "count it all joy, that is, complete or pure joy a joy which excludes trouble and sorrow."
2. "when ye fall into, when ye become unexpectedly surrounded or encompassed by."
3. "The idea of surprise is here to be taken into account Trials are not to be sought for or rushed into; believers fall into them."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Do it now and once for all."
2. "Trials rightly faced are harmless, but wrongly met become temptations to evil."
3. "It is a bold demand that James here makes."

### John Gill:
1. "count it all joy when ye fall into divers temptations; not the temptations of Satan, or temptations to sin; but afflictions and persecutions for the sake of the Gospel, which are trials of the faith of God's people, and of other graces of the Spirit of God."
2. "and these they 'fall' into; not by chance, nor altogether at an unawares, or unexpectedly; but they fell into them through the wickedness and malice of their enemies, and did not bring them upon themselves through any crime or enormity they were guilty of"
3. "count it all joy, or matter of joy, of exceeding great joy, even of the greatest joy; not that these afflictions were joyous in themselves, but in their circumstances, effects, and consequences; as they tried, and exercised, and improved the graces of the Spirit, and worked for their good, spiritual and eternal, and produced in them the peaceable fruit of righteousness"
