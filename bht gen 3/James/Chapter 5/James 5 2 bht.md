# James 5:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The wealthy are rebuked for storing up corrupted riches and moth-eaten garments. This aligns with Jesus' instruction in Matthew about the temporary nature of worldly possessions. Hoarding wealth, which perishes without serving others or oneself, is condemned. The warning extends to the selfishness of retaining useless riches while neglecting the suffering of the poor. Ultimately, these perishable possessions will be stripped away, revealing the emptiness of finding glory in material wealth.
