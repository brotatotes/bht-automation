# James 5:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The cries of oppressed workers whose wages are withheld by their rich employers reflect a sinful act of fraud and an injustice towards those dependent on their earnings. It serves as a reminder that the Lord, as the defender and avenger of the poor, hears their pleas and will ensure justice. God's title as the Lord of hosts signifies His power to respond to the cries of the oppressed. This evokes a sense of terror for those who believe the poor have no protector, as the Lord is their guardian.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "the hire of the workmen who mowed your fields, which has been held back, crieth out from you."
2. "the cries of them who reaped have entered into the ears of the Lord of hosts."
3. "He calls God the Lord of armies, to strike terror into those who imagine that the poor have no defender."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The 'keeping back of the hire' was, on the part of the rich, virtually an act of 'fraud,' because the poor laborers were not immediately paid... The rich ought to have given freely to the poor; their not doing so was sin." 
2. "A still greater sin was their not paying their debts. Their greatest sin was not paying them to the poor, whose wages is their all." 
3. "It reminds the rich who think the poor have no protector, that the Lord of the whole hosts in heaven and earth is the guardian and avenger of the latter."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The very essence of slavery, therefore, is fraud; and there is nothing to which the remarks of the apostle here are more applicable than to that unjust and oppressive system."
2. "Any one of these things would come under the denomination of fraud."
3. "If everywhere the workman was fairly paid for his earnings; if the poor slave who cultivates the fields of the rich were properly compensated for his toil; if he received what a freeman would contract to do the work for; if there was no fraud in withholding what he earns, the system would soon cease in the earth."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The secondary, which some mistake for the primary sense, is to gather, as for harvest." 
2. "In two cases it refers to a rich man's estates; and in John 4:35, the Lord directs the attention of the disciples to a broad area or series of fields." 
3. "An inarticulate cry. Compare Genesis 4:10."

### John Calvin:
1. "For if a humane and a just man, as Solomon says in Proverbs 12:10, regards the life of his beast, it is a monstrous barbarity, when man feels no pity towards the man whose sweat he has employed for his own benefit."
2. "Hence the Lord has strictly forbidden, in the law, the hire of the laborer to sleep with us (Deuteronomy 24:15)."
3. "We ought to notice what he adds, that the cries of the poor come to the ears of God, so that we may know that the wrong done to them shall not be unpunished."
