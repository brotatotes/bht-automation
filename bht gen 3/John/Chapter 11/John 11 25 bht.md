# John 11:25 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In these powerful words, Jesus declares Himself to be the resurrection and the life, emphasizing His role as the source of life and the ultimate testimony to Himself. He proclaims that even in death, believers will live through their faith in Him. This proclamation is not merely about future events, but a present reality in Jesus Himself. Through Him, believers find comfort and assurance that their life is preserved and death is ultimately conquered. In Jesus, death is transformed into everlasting life.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "These words, as Stier observes, are the central point of the history; the great testimony to Himself, of which the subsequent miracle is the proof."
2. "This He does by announcing Himself...as 'THE RESURRECTION'...and more than that, THE LIFE ITSELF."
3. "So that he that believeth in me...even though he have died, shall live."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The whole power to restore, impart, and maintain life, resides in Me."
2. "The believer's death shall be swallowed up in life, and his life shall never sink into death."
3. "The temporary separation of soul and body is here regarded as not even interrupting, much less impairing, the new and everlasting life imparted by Jesus to His believing people."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "I am the resurrection - I am the author or the cause of the resurrection."
2. "He intimated that it was not indispensable that it should be deferred to the last day. He had power to do it now as well as then."
3. "Even if he dies, he shall hereafter live."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "I am the resurrection, I am the life." 
2. "In His own person, representing humanity, He exhibits man as immortal, but immortal only through union with Him."
3. "This life is not affected by death. 'Every believer is in reality and forever sheltered from death.'"

### John Calvin:
1. "Christ shows that he is the commencement of life."
2. "That men live and breathe, and are endued with sense, understanding, and will, all this tends to their destruction, because there is no part or faculty of the soul that is not corrupted and turned aside from what is right."
3. "This is truly a remarkable commendation of faith, that it conveys to us the life of Christ, and thus frees us from death."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They are not mere doctrines about future events, but present realities in Jesus himself."
2. "The Resurrection is one manifestation of the Life: it is involved in the Life."
3. "Even if he die... yet shall he live."

### John Gill:
1. "I am the resurrection and the life,.... Signifying, that he was able of himself to raise men from death to life, without asking it of his Father; and that he could do it now, as well as at the general resurrection."
2. "This is true of Christ, with regard to a spiritual resurrection, from a death of sin, to a life of grace; he is concerned both in the life itself, and in the resurrection to it."
3. "He that believeth in me, though he were dead, yet shall he live: believers in Christ die as well as others, though death is not a penal evil to them; its curse is removed, its sting is taken away, being satisfied for by Christ, and so becomes a blessing and privilege to them."
