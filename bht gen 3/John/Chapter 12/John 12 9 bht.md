### Henry Alford:
1.  9 ff.] Remember here, as elsewhere in John, the Jews are not the people, but the rulers, and persons of repute: the representatives of the Jewish opposition to Jesus.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Crowds of the Jerusalem Jews hastened to Bethany, not so much to see Jesus, whom they knew to be there, as to see dead Lazarus alive." 
2. "To such a pitch had these chief priests come of diabolical determination to shut out the light from themselves, and quench it from the earth!" 
3. "Christ's triumphant entry into Jerusalem."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The best texts insert the article, which converts the expression into the current phrase, the common people."
2. "They came to know."
3. "Rev., more correctly, learned."

### John Calvin:
1. "The more nearly the time of the death of Christ approached, it became the more necessary that his name should be universally celebrated, in order that it might be a preparation for stronger faith after his death."
2. "As Christ showed in it a remarkable proof of his Divinity, God intended that [the resurrection of Lazarus] should have many witnesses."
3. "They came not on account of Jesus only, but also for the sake of Lazarus, [to] behold the astonishing display of the power of Christ in Lazarus."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Faith and unbelief have revealed themselves in the case of the friends and the enemies of Jesus, and especially in the deed of Mary and the words of Judas."
2. "But the sifting process which accompanies every manifestation of Jesus extends to a wider circle."
3. "That very circle of Jewish influence and power in which till lately the spirit of narrow bigotry and fanaticism had found its expression in determined hostility to Jesus is divided into two classes, 'the common people of the Jews,' and the rulers in this ruling faction, 'the high priests'."
