# John 13:32 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Christ's glory, revealed through his resurrection and exaltation, is intertwined with the divine glory of God. The Father bestows upon him even greater honor and glory for his sacrifice. This eternal glory marks him as the Head of the new creation. The unity of being with God is evident, as Christ's glory is united with the divine glory of God. God not only glorifies Christ but also demonstrates approval for those who uphold his honor. Through the cross, Christ's exalted rank is displayed, unveiling the perfection of God.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "in himself is in God (the Father), not in Christ."
2. "by the resurrection of Him into that glory."
3. "The Father recompenses to Him more than the Son of Man hath done."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "If God be glorified in him, God shall also... glorify him in himself." 
2. "Referring now to the Resurrection and Exaltation of Christ after this service was over, including all the honor and glory then put upon Him."
3. "That will forever encircle Him as Head of the new creation."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "If the life and death of the Messiah be such as to lead to the honor of God, such as shall manifest its perfections, and show his goodness, truth, and justice, then he will show that he thus approves his work."
2. "God will always, in the proper time and way, manifest his approbation of those who live so as to promote the honor of his name."
3. "Not by the ministry of angels or by any other subordinate attestation, but by an expression that shall be direct from him."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "His glory will be contained in and identified with the divine glory."
2. "En in, indicates unity of being; para with, unity of position."

### John Calvin:
1. "He promises, therefore, that when the ignominy which he shall endure for a short time has been effaced, illustrious honor will be displayed in his death."
2. "The death of the cross, which Christ suffered, is so far from obscuring his high rank, that in that death his high rank is chiefly displayed."
3. "Though the whole world should conspire to cover us with infamy, yet if we sincerely and honestly endeavor to promote the glory of God, we ought not to doubt that God will also glorify us."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "God is the source of the glory and is the glory succeeding the Cross."
2. "No postponement now. First and quickly the Cross, then the Ascension."
3. "The glory with the Father in heaven."

### John Gill:
1. "Seeing this is a certain truth, is indisputably matter of fact, that all the perfections of God are glorified in Christ, by his sufferings and death."
2. "God shall also glorify him in himself; either 'with himself'; with his own glory, which was promised to Christ, and which he had before the world was, and for which he prays."
3. "This he will do very quickly, he will not leave him in the grave, nor suffer him to see corruption; he will raise him again the third day, and give him glory."
