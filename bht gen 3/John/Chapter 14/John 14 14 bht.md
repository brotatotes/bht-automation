# John 14:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus solemnly promises that whatever is asked in his name, he will do, emphasizing his own power and authority. This truth holds that when we pray directly to Jesus, as demonstrated in Acts 7:59 and Revelation 22:20, our prayers are heard and answered. By calling upon the name of Jesus, acknowledging his sacrifice and righteousness, we align our prayers with the will of God, ensuring that our requests are in accordance with His glory and our ultimate good. It is a reminder to approach God through Jesus' intercession, recognizing the fullness of His power and goodness.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. If ye shall ask anything. Some authorities insert me. So Rev. This implies prayer to Christ.


### John Calvin:
1. "All see and feel that they are unworthy to approach God; and yet the greater part of men burst forward, as if they were out of their senses, and rashly and haughtily address God."
2. "The reason why they [people] do so is, that they have but a poor and slender perception of the power and goodness of God in Christ."
3. "When Christ repeats, a second time, that we must pray to the Father in his name, he lays his hand on us, as it were, that we may not lose our pains by fruitlessly seeking other intercessors."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Just this phrase does not occur elsewhere in John and seems awkward, but see John 16:23."
2. "If it is genuine, as seems likely, here is direct prayer to Jesus taught as we see it practiced by Stephen in Acts 7:59; and in Revelation 22:20."
3. "Direct prayer to Jesus taught as we see it practiced by Stephen in Acts 7:59; and in Revelation 22:20."

### John Gill:
1. "Whatever they asked in my name, either calling upon it, he being equally the object of prayer with the Father."
2. "Making mention of it, pleading the merits of his blood, righteousness, and sacrifice."
3. "Whatever was according to the will of God, was for his glory, and their real good, he would do it for them."
