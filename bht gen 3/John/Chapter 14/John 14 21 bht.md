### Henry Alford:
1. "He who has my commandments, as being my disciple by outward profession... and keeps them..."
2. "This keeping is more of the inner will to keep them, than the absolute observance..."
3. "I will manifest myself to him... This is the highest promise which can be made to man, and yet it is made to every man who has and keeps the commandments of the Lord Jesus."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "He that hath my commandments and keepeth them...my Father and I will love him." 
2. "Mark the sharp line of distinction here, not only between the Divine Persons but the actings of love in Each respectively, towards true disciples."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Will manifest myself to him - To manifest is to show, to make appear, to place before the eyes so that an object may be seen."
2. "This means that Jesus would so show himself to his followers that they should see and know that he was their Savior."
3. "In what way this is done, see John 14:23."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Who has in memory and keeps in life."
2. "A clear, conspicuous manifestation is indicated."
3. "It conveys more than the disclosing of an undiscovered presence, or the manifesting of a hidden one."

### John Calvin:
1. "The undoubted proof of our love to him lies in our keeping his commandments."
2. "The fruit of piety is progress in the knowledge of Christ."
3. "A more abundant knowledge of Christ is here represented as an extraordinary reward of our love to Christ; and hence it follows that it is an invaluable treasure."

### Philip Schaff:
