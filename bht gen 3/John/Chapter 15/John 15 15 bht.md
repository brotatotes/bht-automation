# John 15:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus proclaims to his disciples that they are no longer mere servants, but have been elevated to the esteemed status of friends. As friends, they have been granted the privilege of knowing all that Jesus heard from the Father, receiving a complete revelation. This transition signifies a profound change in their relationship, moving beyond the limitations of servitude under the law to a state of new dignity and intimacy with Christ. This friendship entails a deeper sense of connection, trust, and understanding, as they are entrusted with the knowledge necessary for their salvation.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "It is the lower sense of the word rendered servant (signifying both servant and slave), which is brought out in this verse."
2. "The anticipatory character of the saying is clearly shewn in the words, 'Knoweth not what his lord doeth;' for this was precisely their present condition, but was after His Ascension changed into light and knowledge."
3. "As we look for immortality of the flesh and salvation of the soul in the future, although, having received the pledge of both, we are said to be already saved: so we should hope for in the future the knowledge of all things which the Only-begotten has heard from the Father, although Christ says He has already made them known to us."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Henceforth I call you not servants... but... friends, for all things that I have heard of my Father I have made known unto you." 
2. "Servants He still calls them... in the sense of being 'under law to Christ'." 
3. "the servant knoweth not what his lord doeth... but simply receives and executes his orders."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The servant knoweth not ... It is the privilege of friendship to be made acquainted with the plans wishes, and wants of the friend."
2. "I have called you friends - I have given you the name of friends."
3. "For all things ... it was proper that he should give them the name."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "No longer servants, as you were under the dispensation of the law." 
2. "Has no instinctive perception."
3. "You I have called friends."

### John Calvin:
1. "Let us be satisfied with this testimony of his love toward us which is contained in the Gospel, for it will never deceive us."
2. "So much the greater is the ingratitude and wickedness of those who, not satisfied with the admirable wisdom of the Gospel, fly with proud eagerness to new speculations."
3. "Not one of those things, therefore, which related to our salvation, and which it was of importance for us to know was omitted by Christ in the instructions given to his disciples."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "I have called you friends, because all things that I heard from my Father I made known unto you."
2. "In one sense, indeed, they would be always 'servants'...but that is not their only relationship to their Lord."
3. "The livelier our sense of the privilege of friendship the deeper will be our humility, and that the more truly we feel Jesus to be our 'Lord and Master' the more shall we be prepared to enter into the fulness of the privilege bestowed by Him."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He was their Rabbi and Lord."
2. "I have called you friends... permanent state of new dignity."
3. "Are we friends of Christ?"

### John Gill:
1. "Henceforth I call you not servants... now Christ being come in the flesh... henceforward he would not use, treat, or account them as servants."
2. "I have called you friends; that is, accounted, reckoned of them, used them as his friends and familiar acquaintance; whom he told all his mind unto."
3. "For all things I have heard of my Father, I have made known unto you... all things which he himself was to do and suffer, in order to obtain eternal redemption; and the whole of the Gospel, as to the essential and substantial parts of it, they were to preach."
