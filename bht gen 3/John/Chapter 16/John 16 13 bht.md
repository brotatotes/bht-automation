# John 16:13 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Holy Spirit, sent by both the Father and the Son, acts as a perfect Teacher, leading believers into the complete truth of God. He guides them in understanding the truths passed down by the Apostles and reveals the truth that is found in Christ. He goes before believers, removing obstacles and making things clear, while also showing them what is to come in the future. Resting in the doctrine taught by the apostles is a way of expressing gratitude, as it contains the highest perfection of heavenly wisdom.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The Holy Spirit shall teach and lead us, not as children, under the tutors and governors of legal and imperfect knowledge, but as sons, making known to us the whole truth of God."
2. "The Spirit does not, any more than the Son, work or speak of Himself: both are sent, the one from the Father, the other from the Father and Son."
3. "As the direct fulfilment to the Apostles of the leading into the whole truth was the unfolding before them those truths which they have delivered down to us in their Epistles."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The Spirit of truth - So called because he would teach them all necessary truth."
2. "We may here remark that this is a full promise that they would be inspired and guided in founding the new church."
3. "The more we study the plan of Christ, the more shall we admire the profound wisdom of the Christian scheme."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The Spirit does not reveal all truth to men, but He leads them to the truth as it is in Christ."

### John Calvin:
1. "The Spirit, whom Christ promised to the apostles, is declared to be perfect Master or Teacher of truth."
2. "Whoever imagines that anything must be added to their doctrine, as if it were imperfect and but half-finished, not only accuses the apostles of dishonesty, but blasphemes against the Spirit."
3. "If we do not wish to be ungrateful to God, let us rest satisfied with that doctrine of which the writings of the apostles declare them to be the authors, since in it the highest perfection of heavenly wisdom is made known to us."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He shall guide' implies not merely that He shall show the way, but that He shall Himself experimentally go before them in the way."
2. "'All the truth,' He would say, 'which I have proclaimed, of which I am Myself the substance. He will guide you, for it is not from Himself that He will speak: He comes as My Representative, not for new and independent offices of grace: He will carry on My work.'"
3. "The things that are coming' are the things that happen when 'He who is to come' begins in the power of His Spirit the great conflict carried on throughout all the ages of the Christian Church in her militant condition."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Whenever he comes." 
2. "He shall guide you." 
3. "The things that are yet to come."

### John Gill:
1. "He is as a guide, he goes before, leads the way, removes obstructions, opens the understanding, makes things plain and clear, teaches to profit, and leads in the way men should go." 
2. "For he shall not speak of himself... in perfect agreement with both; being, as of the same nature and essence, power and glory, so of the same mind, understanding, and will."
3. "He will show you things to come; which would come to pass after the death, resurrection, and ascension of Christ; things relating to the state and settlement of the Gospel church."

### John Wesley:
1. "It is universally allowed that the Father, Son, and Holy Ghost dwell in all believers."
2. "And the internal agency of the Holy Ghost is generally admitted."
3. "That of the Father and the Son, as represented in this Gospel, deserves our deepest consideration."
