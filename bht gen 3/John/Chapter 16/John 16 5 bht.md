# John 16:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
None of the disciples ask Jesus about the nature and advantages of his departure, showing their lack of inquiry and anxiousness to understand. Jesus points out their grief and reprimands them for their attachment to his physical presence. While the world's hatred was directed towards Jesus, his departure would bring it down upon the disciples as his representatives. Although Peter had previously asked about Jesus' destination, none of the disciples ask at this moment. Nonetheless, Jesus comforts them by explaining that going to the Father means being received into heavenly glory and possessing the highest authority, to protect believers.
