## BHT:
When Jesus crossed over the brook Kedron, a deep ravine to the East of Jerusalem, towards Gethsemane, a garden at the foot of the Mount of Olives, he willingly submitted himself to his approaching fate. This brook, which at times became a storm brook, was associated with impurities and idol-worship, marking it as a symbol of tribulation and judgment. Jesus' decision to enter the garden wasn't for concealment but for a better opportunity to pray, exemplifying his obedience and voluntary sacrifice in the face of a profound anguish.
