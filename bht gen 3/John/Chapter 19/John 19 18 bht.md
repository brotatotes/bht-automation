# John 19:18 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In the midst of His crucifixion, Jesus obediently fulfills His own teachings by pleading with the Father to forgive those who were responsible for His death. While the Synoptists describe the individuals crucified with Him as robbers and malefactors, John refers to them simply as two others, hinting that they may have been members of their own band. This arrangement of having the robbers on both sides of Jesus possibly served to emphasize His royal status and the mockery of Pilate towards the Jews. Despite the wickedness of His executioners, it is crucial to remember that everything was determined by the hand and purpose of God, showcasing the valuable token of His power, wisdom, righteousness, and goodness. Jesus willingly took our place as our surety, showcasing His love, grace, and the fulfillment of God's purposes, promises, and predictions.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "FATHER, FORGIVE THEM, FOR THEY KNOW NOT WHAT THEY DO"
2. "And He made intercession for the transgressors"
3. "Often have we occasion to observe how our Lord is the first to fulfill His own precepts"

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "All the Synoptists describe the character of the two who were crucified with Jesus."
2. "Matthew and Mark, robbers; Luke, malefactors."
3. "John says nothing about the character of these two, but simply describes them as two others."

### John Calvin:
1. "We ought always to remember, that the wicked executioners of Christ did nothing but what had been determined by the hand and purpose of God."
2. "In no other way could our guilt be removed than by the Son of God becoming a curse for us."
3. "Whoever, then, takes a just view of the causes of the death of Christ, together with the advantage which it yields to us, will not... regard it as an offense, but rather as an invaluable token and pledge of the power, and wisdom, and righteousness, and goodness of God."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "On the lingering torture of death by crucifixion it is unnecessary to dwell."
 
2. "To this death they too must have been doomed by the Roman power, and as we find the Roman governor writing the inscription and Roman soldiers taking part in the crucifixion...it is reasonable to think that it was also a Roman, not a Jewish, arrangement by which the two robbers were suspended on either side of Jesus."
 
3. "If so, the object must have been still more to bring out that idea of His royalty with which Pilate to the last mocked the Jews."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "And Jesus in the midst."
2. "A robber was on each side of Jesus."
3. "Probably members of his band, malefactors."

### John Gill:
1. "He, as a surety, standing in the legal place, and stead of his people; hence he died in their room, and for their sins: this shows the low estate of Christ, the strictness of justice, the wisdom of God in salvation, and the grace and love of the Redeemer."
2. "Who condescended to everything, and every circumstance, though ever so reproachful, which were necessary for the redemption of his people, and the glory of the divine perfections, and for the fulfillment of purposes, promises, and predictions."
3. "He was accounted as such by men, and was treated as if he had been one by the justice of God."
