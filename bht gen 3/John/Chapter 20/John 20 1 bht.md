## BHT:
Dear disciple! Thy dead Lord is still 'the Lord' to thee. Cometh Mary Magdalene, vivid historical present, to find the stone taken away from the sepulchre. God chooses what is weak and contemptible in the world, and as Christ reckons the women the principal witnesses of his resurrection, their testimony is entitled to the greatest deference. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Dear disciple! thy dead Lord is to thee 'the Lord' still." 
2. "thy dead Lord is to thee 'the Lord' still."

### John Calvin:
1. "God chooseth what is weak, and foolish, and contemptible in the world, that he may bring to nought the wisdom, and excellence, and glory, of the flesh."
2. "As Christ was pleased to reckon them the principal witnesses of his resurrection, on this single ground their testimony is entitled to the greatest deference, and is not liable to any objection."
3. "The women, therefore, came to the sepulcher on the day after the Sabbath, having on the same day (but after sunset) bought spices; and afterwards went out of the city secretly, and during the darkness of the night, as people are wont to do when they are afraid."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Few parts of the Fourth Gospel illustrate better than these words the principle of selection upon which it is composed."
2. "It is obvious that here, as elsewhere, we have to deal not so much with events of full historical detail as with events selected on account of their bearing upon the idea which the Evangelist wishes to illustrate."
3. "The stone which had been fitted into the door of the sepulchre had been taken away."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Both Mark and Luke have this very idiom of the cardinal, instead of the usual ordinal, an idiom common in the papyri and in the modern Greek."
2. "Cometh Mary Magdalene, vivid historical present. Mary Magdalene is not to be confounded with Mary of Bethany."
3. "Taken away, perfect passive participle, predicate accusative in apposition with the stone."

### John Gill:
1. "On the first day of the week rose from the dead."
2. "Mary's coming so early to the grave, shows her great love and affection to Christ, her zeal, courage, and diligence."
3. "The greatest sinners, when converted, are most eminent for grace, particularly faith, love, and humility; and are most diligent in the discharge of duty."
