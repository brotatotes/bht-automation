### Philip Schaff:
1. "While Peter takes the lead, impetuously dashing into the water." 
2. "Yet they do not actually land the net: they only drag it to the shore."
3. "The landing is reserved for him who had displayed greatest earnestness and activity."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "In the little boat (τω πλοιαριω). Locative case of πλοιαριον (diminutive) for the larger boat (πλοιον, verses John 21:3; John 21:6)."
2. "About two hundred cubits off (ως απο πηχων διακοσιων). For πηχυς, cubit, see Matthew 6:27 and for ως απο see John 11:18."
3. "Dragging (συροντες). Present active participle of συρω for which see Acts 8:3."

### John Gill:
1. "and the rather, they did not think fit to do as he did, for they were not far from land, but, as it were, two hundred cubits; which was about an hundred yards."
2. "towing the net full of fishes all along in the water, till they came to land; an emblem of laborious Gospel ministers"
3. "who being once embarked in the work of the ministry, continue in it to the end, notwithstanding all toil, labour, and difficulties that attend them"
