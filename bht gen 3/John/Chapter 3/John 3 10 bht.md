# John 3:10 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The doctrine of regeneration, well-known in the Old Testament, eluded Nicodemus, a teacher of Israel, due to his entrenched Pharisaic theology and reliance on tradition. Jesus, emphasizing Nicodemus' role as an authorized instructor, questions his lack of comprehension. It is astonishing that someone responsible for teaching would remain ignorant of fundamental religious realities. This underscores the importance of humility and genuine faith to grasp the profound truths of Scripture.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The question clearly implies that the doctrine of regeneration is so far disclosed in the Old Testament that Nicodemus was culpable in being ignorant of it."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "No man can understand the Bible fully unless he is a humble Christian, and the easiest way to comprehend the truths of religion is to give the heart to God and live to his glory."
2. "A child thus may have more real knowledge of the way of salvation than many who are pretended masters and teachers of Israel."
3. "It may seem surprising that a man whose business it was to teach the people should be a stranger to so plain and important a doctrine..."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "A master is more correctly rendered by Rev., the teacher."
2. "Nicodemus is not reproved for the want of previous knowledge, but for the want of perception or understanding when these truths are expounded to him."
3. "Art thou a master of Israel? The su, thou, is emphatic."

### John Calvin:
1. "Such hesitation, certainly, is base and shameful. For what religion have we, what knowledge of God, what rule of living well, what hope of eternal life, if we do not believe that man is renewed by the Spirit of God?" 
2. "It is utterly beyond endurance that any man should be ignorant and unskilled in it, who professes to be a teacher in the Church of God."
3. "This is, very properly, placed first in order; for in the very matter in which he chiefly plumes himself on his acuteness and sagacity, Christ censures his ignorance."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Thou art the teacher of Israel; and perceivest thou not these things?"
2. "For our Lord does not say 'and knowest not;' Nicodemus is not blamed for any want of previous knowledge of these things, but because he does not perceive the truth of the teaching when presented to him."
3. "If Nicodemus would really know the fact, it must be by the knowledge of experience."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The teacher of Israel. The well-known or the authorized teacher of the Israel of God."
2. "His Pharisaic theology had made him almost proof against spiritual apprehension."
3. "It was outside of his groove, rote, rut, rot, the three terrible r's of mere traditionalism."

### John Gill:
1. "Upbraiding him with his continued and invincible ignorance, which was aggravated by his dignified character."
2. "He was not a common teacher, not a teacher of babes, nor a teacher in their synagogues, or in their 'Midrashim', or divinity schools, but in their great sanhedrim."
3. "Yet was he ignorant of the first and most important things in religion."
