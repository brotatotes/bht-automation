# John 3:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus, by using the pronouns we and our, emphasizes the unreasonableness of unbelief and the contrast between Himself and those who claim to know but do not truly understand. Through the new birth, believers gain a new vision that imparts eternal life and knowledge of God. Jesus claims personal knowledge of the truths He explains, offering His experience of grace as a qualification for bearing witness. Though some may not receive His doctrine, it remains certain and durable, for the unbelief of men cannot prevent God from remaining true.

### John Calvin:
1. "Christ claims it as peculiar to himself and all the servants of God, that they deliver no doctrine but what is certain."
2. "Let every man, therefore, see what the Lord has revealed to him, that no man may go beyond the bounds of his faith."
3. "Though you do not receive my doctrine, it remains nevertheless certain and durable; for the unbelief of men will never prevent God from remaining always true."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The key to the plural is found in John 3:8. Every one who dwells in the spiritual world of which Jesus has been speaking is a witness to its reality and its wonders." 
2. "Jesus now contrasts with these another class, consisting of all who from their own experience could join Him in His testimony to the reality of the spiritual kingdom."
3. "In John 3:8, where the wind was taken as the emblem of the Spirit, the sense which bore witness was that of hearing. This verse speaks of something more convincing still, the sense of sight."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Jesus simply claims knowledge of what he has tried to make plain to the famous Rabbi without success." 
2. "He is bearing witness from personal knowledge, strange as this may seem to Nicodemus."
3. "Jesus probably associates here with himself ('we') those who have personal experience of grace and so are qualified as witnesses."
