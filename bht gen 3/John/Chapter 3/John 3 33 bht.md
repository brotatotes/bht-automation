# John 3:33 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Those who accept the witness of Jesus are attesting to the fact that Jesus speaks the message of God. By fully believing His doctrine, they seal it as their own, expressing conviction in its truth, and giving glory to God. This act of faith is a way of worshiping God's truth. Conversely, those who disbelieve the witness of Jesus are disbelieving God and declaring Him to be false. Through our faith, we rely on God's words, finding confirmation in them and setting our seal that God is true.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Hath received and fully believed his doctrine. Hath yielded his heart to its influence."
2. "To believe a doctrine is to 'seal it,' or by believing it we express our firm conviction that it is true, and that God who has spoken it is true."
3. "To be a believer is to 'pledge ourselves' for the truth, to seal it as our own, to adopt it, to choose it, and solemnly assent to it."

### John Calvin:
1. "Though the whole world should refuse or withhold faith in the Gospel, this ought not to prevent good men from giving their assent to God."
2. "It is peculiar to faith to rely on God, and to be confirmed by his words; for there can be no assent, unless God have, first of all, come forward and spoken."
3. "As nothing is more dear to him than his truth, so we cannot render to him more acceptable worship than when we acknowledge by our faith that He is true."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He that received his witness set his seal to this, that God is true."
2. "Every man who accepts His witness and thus declares that Jesus is true, in that very act attests, sets his seal to, the declaration that God is true."
3. "To disbelieve Him is to disbelieve God, to declare Him true is to declare God true."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The metaphor of sealing is a common one for giving attestation."

### John Gill:

2. "He firmly believes that God is true to every word of his, and will make good every promise."
3. "On the contrary, he that believes not makes God a liar, than which, nothing can be more reproachful to him."
