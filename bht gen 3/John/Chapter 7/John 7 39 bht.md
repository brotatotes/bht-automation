# John 7:39 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Spirit, as the glorious manifestation of the Father's will, flows forth from the temple of Christ's body to quench the spiritual thirst of all who believe. This precious Gift, direct from the hands of the ascended Savior, reveals that He is the chosen one in whom the Father delights. By His agency, the Spirit opens up a wellspring of life within us. This promise of the Spirit, yet to be fully given, alludes to the coming of the Holy Spirit at Pentecost, fulfilling the Promise of the Father.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The mind of the Spirit, is life."
2. "The Spirit is life."
3. "The glorified Body of the Lord is the temple from under whose threshold the Holy Spirit flows forth to us."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "By His direct personal agency, [the Spirit] opens up this spring of living waters in the human spirit."
2. "As the Holy Ghost is, in the redemption of man, entirely at the service of Christ, as His Agent, so it is only in believing connection with Christ that anyone 'receives' the Spirit."
3. "This illustrious Gift, direct from the hands of the ascended Saviour, was God's intimation to the world that He whom it had cast out, crucified, and slain, was 'His Elect, in whom His soul delighted,' and that it was through the smiting of that Rock that the waters of the Spirit had gushed forth upon a thirsty world."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Of the Spirit - Of the Holy Spirit, that should be sent down to attend their preaching and to convert sinners."
2. "For the Holy Ghost was not yet given - Was not given in such full and large measures as should be after Jesus had ascended to heaven."
3. "Jesus was not yet glorified - Jesus had not yet ascended to heaven - to the glory and honor that awaited him there."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The word spirit, standing thus alone, marks, not the personal Spirit, but His operation or gift or manifestation."
2. "His glory was the expression of the Father's will."
3. "The passion is the way to glory."

### John Calvin:
1. "We are destitute of all the sap and moisture of life, unless when the Spirit of God quickens us, and when he waters us, as it were, by secret vigor."
2. "All who have not been regenerated by the Spirit of Christ ought to be reckoned dead, whatever may be the pretended life of which they boast."
3. "That we lie on earth poor, and famished, and almost destitute of spiritual blessings, while Christ now sits in glory at the right hand of the Father, and clothed with the highest majesty of government, ought to be imputed to our slothfulness, and to the small measure of our faith."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The word is a promise still, speaking of a future not a present gift ('were to receive')."
2. "Only when Jesus was glorified, that is, only when He had died, had risen, had ascended on high, had been invested with the glory which was His own at the right hand of the Father, would man receive that spiritual power which is the condition of all spiritual life."
3. "When Jesus Himself, the God-man, is perfected, then and not till then does He receive power to bestow the Holy Spirit on mankind."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Even here one should say 'whom,' not which, of the Spirit of God."
2. "The reference is not to the existence of the Spirit, but to the dispensation of the Spirit."
3. "John, writing at the close of the century, inserts this comment and interpretation of the language of Jesus as an allusion to the coming of the Holy Spirit at pentecost (the Promise of the Father)."

### John Gill:
1. "By rivers of living water, he meant the Spirit in his gifts and graces." 
2. "The Holy Ghost was not yet given...he had been bestowed in his grace upon the Old Testament saints, and rested in his gifts upon the prophets of that dispensation."
3. "Though he had removed, he was to return again; but as yet the time was not come, at least for the more plentiful donation of him: the reason of which was, because that Jesus was not yet glorified."
