Mary, the mother of Jesus, was betrothed to Joseph, of the house of David. While not explicitly mentioned in the Gospels, it is implied in Scripture, and has been the general Christian belief. The oneness in their marriage should be considered, allowing Mary to be reckoned as that which her husband was. Betrothal was a serious commitment, lasting a year, with unfaithfulness being punishable by death. This highlights the significance of Mary being a pure and faithful virgin, as it was necessary for the promised Son of David to be born from his lineage.
