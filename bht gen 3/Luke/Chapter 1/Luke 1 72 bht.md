## BHT:
The fulfillment of God's promises in his holy covenant is displayed through the mercy he promised. By showing kindness and mercy to our fathers, God blessed our nation and proved his faithfulness. This covenant underscores God's mercy and forgiveness of sins through Christ's blood. Salvation is offered to believers through Christ's death and resurrection. God's sending of Jesus as our Savior demonstrates his remembrance of the covenant and his redemption for his people.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "To make mercy with our fathers" - that is, to show kindness to our fathers; and the propriety of it is founded on the fact that mercy to "children" is regarded as kindness to the "parent."
2. "Blessing the 'children' was blessing the 'nation;'" was fulfilling the promises made to the fathers, and "showing" that he regarded them in mercy.
3. Such a transaction [covenant] evidently can never take place between man and God, for they are not equal. Man is not at liberty to "decline" what God proposes, and he is under obligation to do "all" that God commands.

### John Calvin:
1. "He assigns the reason why God was pleased to save his people. It was because, being mindful of his promise, he displayed his mercy."
2. "Forgiveness of sins is promised in the covenant, but it is in the blood of Christ. Righteousness is promised, but it is offered through the atonement of Christ. Life is promised, but it must be sought only in the death and resurrection of Christ."
3. "As neither Abraham, nor any of the saints, could procure salvation to himself by his own power or merits, so to all believers, whether living or dead, the same salvation has been exhibited in Christ."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The pious Jews of old had wept over the decay of their nation, and even though dead and living with God, the fulfilment of their hopes and wishes might be called showing mercy toward them."
2. "To show mercy toward our fathers."
3. "To remember his holy covenant, i.e., by the act of fulfilling what He had promised therein to show His mindfulness of it."

### John Gill:
1. "By 'mercy' is meant salvation by Christ, which springs from the mercy of God..."
2. "His blood was shed for the remission of sins that were past, and for the redemption of transgressions under the first Testament..."
3. "Now raising up, and sending Jesus a Saviour, showed that God was mindful of this covenant, and therefore sent redemption to his people."
