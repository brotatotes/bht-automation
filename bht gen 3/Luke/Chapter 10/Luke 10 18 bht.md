### Henry Alford:
1. "The words, I beheld Satan as lightning fall from heaven, refer to the original fall of Satan, when he lost his place as an angel of light, not keeping his first estate."
2. "And this I beheld belongs to the period before the foundation of the world when He abode in the bosom of the Father. He is to be the great Victor over the Adversary, and this victory when Satan fell from heaven."
3. "The description is not figurative, but literal; i.e, as far as divine words can be said to be literal, being accommodated to our sensuous conceptions."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "I followed you on your mission, and watched its triumphs; while you were wondering at the subjection to you of devils in My name, a grander spectacle was opening to My view; sudden as the darting of lightning from heaven to earth, lo! Satan was beheld falling from heaven!"
2. "How remarkable is this, that by that law of association which connects a part with the whole, those feeble triumphs of the Seventy seem to have not only brought vividly before the Redeemer the whole ultimate result of His mission, but compressed it into a moment and quickened it into the rapidity of lightning!"
3. "Jesus meant to raise their minds not only from the particular to the general, but from a very temporary form of satanic operation to the entire kingdom of evil."

### Marvin Vincent:

3. "Describing vividly a dazzling brilliance suddenly quenched."

3. "The thunder of the Gospel makes Satan fall like lightning; for it expresses the divine and astonishing power of the doctrine, which throws down, in a manner so sudden and violent, the prince of the world armed with such abundant forces."
