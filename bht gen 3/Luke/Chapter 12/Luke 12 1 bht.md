# Luke 12:1 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Beware of the leaven of the Pharisees, which is hypocrisy. Jesus openly demonstrated His own teachings and saw the need to warn His disciples about the rising tension with His enemies. The multitude that gathered to listen to Him included many thousands who were drawn by the attacks of the rabbis against Jesus. Jesus classified the leaven of the Pharisees as hypocrisy, which was a dominant vice among them. Therefore, He urged His disciples to be on guard against the deceiving nature of hypocrisy and not be ensnared by the cunning tactics of the Pharisees.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Our Lord had been speaking out more plainly than ever before, as matters were coming to a head between Him and His enemies, and this seems to have suggested to His own mind the warning here."
2. "He had just Himself illustriously exemplified His own precepts."
3. "Covered—from the view."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Men’s consciences are on the side of the faithful reprover of their sins."
2. "Above all things beware."
3. "Hypocrisy is like leaven or yeast."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "An innumerable multitude [των μυριαδων του οχλου]. The word muriav strictly means a number of ten thousand. It is our word myriad. Hence, generally, of any countless number."
2. "First of all beware." 
3. "Classifying the leaven : which belongs to the category of hypocrisy."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Leaven of the Pharisees, i.e., their doctrine."
2. "Which is hypocrisy."
3. "Their leaven (doctrine) was of such a kind that its essence was hypocrisy."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Many thousands (μυριαδων)...a very large crowd apparently drawn together by the violent attacks of the rabbis against Jesus."
2. "Unto his disciples first of all (προς τους μαθητας αυτου πρωτον). This long discourse in Luke 12:12 is really a series of separate talks to various groups in the vast crowds around Jesus."
3. "The leaven of the Pharisees which is hypocrisy (της ζυμης ητις εστιν υποχρισις των Φαρισαιων). The occasion was ripe here for this crisp saying. In Matthew 13:33 leaven does not have an evil sense as here, which see. See Matthew 23:13 for hypocrites. Hypocrisy was the leading Pharisaic vice (Bruce) and was a mark of sanctity to hide an evil heart."

### John Gill:
1. "before all things, beware of the leaven of the Pharisees, which is hypocrisy"
2. "very aptly is hypocrisy in doctrine and manners, compared to leaven"
3. "when our Lord bids his disciples beware of it, his meaning not only is, that they take heed that they were not infected with it themselves, but that they were not imposed upon by the specious pretences of these artful and designing men."
