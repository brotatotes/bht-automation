At the heavenly banquet, people from all corners of the earth will recline together, signifying that salvation is universal and not achieved through different means. The Jews, once deeming themselves the exclusive heirs of God, will be replaced by the Gentiles, prompting them to embrace faith with fervor. ### John Calvin:
1. "He now draws a larger illustration from the fact, that the Jews, who reckoned themselves the only lawful heirs of God, were to be rejected, and that the Gentiles were to be substituted in their room, and obtain the life which was promised to Abraham and his posterity."
2. "He contrasts the Gentiles with them, in order to excite them to faith by a holy jealousy."
3. "The Jews must have been stung by it: for they had an inordinate love of themselves, and proudly despised God and his gifts."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Recline, of course, is the figure of this heavenly banquet."
2. "Jesus does not mean that these will be saved in different ways, but only that many will come from all the four quarters of the earth."

1. "God has his chosen ones, and Christ has a people in all parts of the world; and therefore his Gospel must be

2. "They shall sit down in the kingdom of God; in Christ's kingdom, in the new heavens, and new earth, as persons that sit down at a table, to partake of a feast."
3. "They shall rest from all their labour, and be in perfect ease and peace."
