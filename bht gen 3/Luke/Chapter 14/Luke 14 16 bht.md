# Luke 14:16 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The great supper represents the Kingdom of God and the feast mentioned in Isaiah. The first bidden guests are the Pharisees, Scribes, and learned Jews. By rejecting the invitation, they insult the host. Jesus uses this parable to challenge the misconception that external religiosity guarantees godliness. This parable reflects the offer of salvation and Gospel privileges extended to all, but not all are aware or willing to participate. Refusing the invitation is a missed opportunity for many to partake in the great supper.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "A certain man. Here representing God, since the parable conveys a lesson about eating bread 'in the kingdom of God' (Luke 14:15)."
2. "A great supper. The figure suggested by the last remark is taken up. God prepares 'a feast of fat things' (Isaiah 25:6), which is to culminate in the marriage supper of the Lamb. The immediate reference is to gospel privileges."
3. "And bade many. The 'many' represent the Jewish nation, but especially the Pharisees and the rulers (see Luke 14:21). The first invitation was given through the ancient prophets, the feast being still in the future."
