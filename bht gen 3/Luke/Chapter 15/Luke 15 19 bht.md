## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Albert Barnes:
1. "Such has been my conduct that I have been a disgrace to my father. I am not fit to be honored by being called the son of a man so kind and virtuous."
2. "Treat me as a servant. Let me come again into your family, but I do not ask to be treated as a son. I am willing to come in if you will give me only the support that you give to a servant."
3. "This evinced"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "I am no more worthy, etc. Genuine penitence!"
2. "Make me as one, etc. He does not give up his sonship, but asks only the treatment given to a hireling, for he does not even deserve that."
3. "The main point is, that the prodigal makes no excuse for his sins, but acknowledges his unworthiness."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "No longer worthy. Confession of the facts. He sees his own pitiful plight and is humble."
2. "As one. The hired servants in his father's house are high above him now."

### John Gill:
1. "And am no more worthy to be called thy son... he now sees, and was ready to own his unworthiness to be called so in any sense; and much more to be called and accounted a Son of God by special adoption."
2. "Make me as one of thy hired servants... what an happiness and honour it would be to him, could he be the meanest in his family, a doorkeeper in his house; which was more eligible to him, than to dwell in the tents of sin, or continue in this hungry and starving condition."
