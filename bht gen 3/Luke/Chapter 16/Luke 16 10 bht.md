# Luke 16:10 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Fidelity in stewardship depends not on the amount entrusted but on a sense of responsibility. Being faithful in small matters prepares us for larger responsibilities, while those who act improperly in earthly possessions may be unworthy of heavenly treasures. It is important to be trustworthy in both small and large matters, as our use of earthly stewardship can impact the reception of spiritual gifts. Thus, we must cultivate integrity in all aspects of life, recognizing that faithfulness in the least of things extends to matters of greater importance.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "A man that shows fidelity in small matters will also in large."
2. "He that will cheat and defraud in little things will also in those involving more trust and responsibility."
3. "Fidelity is required in small matters as well as in those of more importance."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. That which is least. A general proposition, yet with a reference to mammon as the least of things. See next verse.


### John Calvin:
1. "Christ, therefore, exhorts his disciples to act faithfully in small matters, in order to prepare themselves for the exercise of fidelity in matters of the highest importance." 
2. "Those persons, he tells us, who act improperly and unfaithfully in things of small value, such as the transitory riches of the world, do not deserve that God should entrust to them the inestimable treasure of the Gospel, and of similar gifts." 
3. "There is, therefore, in these words an implied threatening, that there is reason to fear lest, on account of our abuse of an earthly stewardship, we fail to obtain heavenly gifts."
