The rich man's heartless indifference towards the poor Lazarus who desired scraps of food and suffered with sores is highlighted. Even the dogs showed more pity towards Lazarus. This serves as a reminder that when we fail to help those in need, even strangers and animals become witnesses to our negligence. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The word desiring... must mean that he looked for it, willingly took it."
2. "This verse relating the two points of contrast to the rich man: his only food, the crumbs... his only clothing, nakedness and sores."
3. "None to pity him but the dogs, who licked... his sores, as they do their own."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The words may mean indeed 'was fain to feed on,' or 'gladly fed on,' as in [...]. But the context rather favors the former.".
2. "It is a case of heartless indifference, amidst luxuries of every kind, to one of God's poorest and most afflicted ones, presented daily before the eye."
3. "A touching act of brute pity, in the absence of human relief."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Desiring eagerly, and not receiving what he desired."
2. "The things falling. The best texts omit crumbs."
3. "But (instead of finding compassion), even the dogs... licked them."

### John Calvin:
1. "It was quite enough to prove the hardened cruelty of the rich man, that the sight of wretchedness like this did not move him to compassion."
2. "Christ certainly produces them here as witnesses to convict him of unfeeling and detestable cruelty."
3. "When strangers, or even brute animals, supply our place, by performing an office which ought rather to have been discharged by ourselves, let us conclude that they are so many witnesses and judges appointed by God, to make our criminality the more manifest."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Some think he did not even obtain this desire, and thus heighten the negligence of the rich man."
2. "These would scarcely satisfy him ; in any case the rich man gave himself no concern about the matter."
3. "The pity of the wild and masterless dogs is contrasted with the indifference of the rich man."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Even the wild street dogs would get them also."
2. "If he means that he was dependent on casual scraps and it was so bad that even the wild dogs moreover were his companions in misery, the climax came that he was able to drive away the dogs."
3. "It is not clear whether the licking of the sores by the dogs added to the misery of Lazarus or gave a measure of comfort, as he lay in his helpless condition."

### John Gill:
1. "he did not desire to live in fulness and affluence, but avoided and shunned every step that led unto it."
2. "they were like crumbs their small degree of worldly wisdom and knowledge, being babes, simple, and foolish, who followed Christ, while the learned, wise, and prudent despised him."
3. "it was his meat and drink, to work out their salvation; and it will be his highest joy and pleasure to present them to his Father, and himself, complete and perfect, and introduce them into his kingdom and glory: he will be fully satisfied in them, and they in him, when they shall awake in his likeness."
