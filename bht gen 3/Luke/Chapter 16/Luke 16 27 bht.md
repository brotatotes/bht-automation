# Luke 16:27 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The rich man, now awakened to the truth, sympathizes with his brethren, emphasizing the misery the lost spirits endure due to their elevated faculties and feelings. In his plea to send someone to his father's house, he bitterly reproaches God and the old economy for not warning him sufficiently. Though the rich man's request may be used to justify prayer to saints, there is no communication between Abraham and his brothers. Nonetheless, the rich man's concern for others in his state of torment is evident.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "His eyes are now opened to the truth; and no wonder that his natural sympathies are awakened for his brethren." 
2. "That a lost spirit should feel and express such sympathy, is not to be wondered at." 
3. "The misery of such will be very much heightened by the awakened and active state of those higher faculties and feelings which selfishness and the body kept down here."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Abandoning all hope for himself."
2. "No waking up of good in the heart of the lost, but bitter reproach against God and the old economy, as not warning him sufficiently."
3. "They are sufficiently warned."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Send him to my father's house."
2. "But when thou art again in the sweet world, I pray thee to the mind of others bring me."
3. "Inferno, 6, 88."

### John Calvin:
1. "Here the Papists exercise their ingenuity very foolishly, by attempting to prove that the dead feel solicitude about the living."
2. "If no man holds such extravagant views, the Papists are not entitled to congratulate themselves on the other supposition."
3. "It is not my intention, however, to debate the point, or to defend either one side or another; but I thought it right to advert, in passing, to the futility of the arguments on which they rest their belief that the dead intercede with God on our behalf."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The Roman Catholics probably justify prayer to saints from this petition from the Rich Man to Abraham, but both are in Hades (the other world)."
2. "It is to be observed besides, that Abraham makes no effort to communicate with the five brothers."
3. "But heavenly recognition is clearly assumed."

### John Gill:
1. "I pray thee therefore father, that thou wouldst send him to my father's house." 
2. "Finding he could have no redress of his misery, nor any relief for himself, he applies for others."
3. "The dead seek for mercy for them."
