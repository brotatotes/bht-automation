# Luke 18:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus uses the child as a model for those seeking entrance to the kingdom of God, highlighting the need for humility and a childlike mindset. He emphasizes that the child is not already in the kingdom, but rather depicts the qualities necessary to enter it. By understanding the child and opening the door to them, Jesus exemplifies the importance of innocence and purity in entering the kingdom. This lesson serves to teach his disciples about the significance of humility and to caution against pride and vanity.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Jesus makes the child the model for those who seek entrance into the kingdom of God, not the adult the model for the child."
2. "He does not say that the child is already in the kingdom without coming to him."
3. "Jesus has made the child's world by understanding the child and opening the door for him."

### John Gill:
1. "Christ takes an occasion from hence to teach his disciples humility, and guard them against pride and vanity."
2. "Whosoever shall not receive the kingdom of God...as a little child; without prejudice, pride, ambition, and vanity, with meekness, and humility."
3. "A very unfit and improper person to be a professor of the Gospel; or to be admitted to Gospel ordinances: or be a member of a Gospel church; or be reckoned a subject of the Messiah's kingdom...shall never see it, and enjoy it."
