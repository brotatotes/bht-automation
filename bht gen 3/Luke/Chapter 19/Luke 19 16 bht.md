### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Hath gained [προσηργασατο]... hath worked besides the original sum."
2. "Have thou authority [ισθι εξουσιαν εχων] ... Be thou having authority." 
3. "A city for a pound, yet not even a cottage could be bought for a pound."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Thy pound; not ‘I have gained’ (Matt.). In the latter case, the trust was according to ability, here it was the same in every case; there the gain was proportioned to the trust, but here there was no such proportion; hence the more modest answer."
2. "This also favors the view which interprets the ‘pound’ as the one official gift, with varied results."
3. "Hade ten pounds more, i.e., in addition to itself."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1.  Hath made (προσηργασατο). Only here in the N.T. Note προσ- in addition, besides, more. 


### John Gill:
1. "saying, Lord; acknowledging the sovereignty and dominion of Christ over them"
2. "thy pound hath gained ten pounds; it had been increased tenfold"
3. "This servant owns, that the gifts he had were Christ's"
