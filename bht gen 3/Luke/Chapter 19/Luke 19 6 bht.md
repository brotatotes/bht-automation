### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Whence this so sudden 'joy' in the cold bosom of an avaricious publican?"
2. "The internal revolution was as perfect as instantaneous."
3. "Then shall the lame man leap as an hart, and the tongue of the dumb sing."

### Philip Schaff:
1. Luke 19:6. Joyfully. The curiosity was not a vain one; the presence and words of our Lord had wrought their appropriate effect.
	


### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "He made haste and came down." - Luke repeats the very words of Jesus with the same idiom.
2. "Received him joyfully." - The very verb used of Martha's welcome to Jesus (Luke 10:38).
3. "Joyfully" is the present active participle, "rejoicing."

### John Gill:
1. "Such power went along with the words of Christ, that they immediately reached his heart, awakened his conscience, affected his mind, and drew his soul to Christ."
2. "So souls sensible of their dangerous state and condition by nature...flee with all haste to him for refuge, for righteousness, peace, pardon, life, and salvation."
3. "Christ was a welcome guest to him, as he is to every sensible sinner, who by faith receives him...and that with the greatest joy; as there is reason for it, since with him, he receives the free and full forgiveness of his sins, a justifying righteousness, an abundance of grace, and a right unto, and meetness for heaven."
