# Luke 2:23 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Luke emphasizes that Jesus was made under the law, elaborating on the fulfillment of the law. The frequent mention of the word law in this chapter emphasizes its importance. The purification and presentation in the temple were in accordance with the law, with the purification focusing on the mother and the presentation on the son. This ceremony reminds us that every male who opens the womb, including Jesus as Mary's firstborn, is devoted and consecrated to the Lord. Jesus willingly subjected himself to the law to redeem his people from its bondage and condemnation.

### Henry Alford:

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Luke emphasizes the fact that Jesus 'was made under the law' (Galatians 4:4), and accordingly elaborates the details of the fulfilment of the law..."
2. "The word law occurs in this chapter five times; oftener than in all the rest of this Gospel put together."
3. "Luke emphasizes the fact that Jesus 'was made under the law'..."

### John Calvin:
1. "Such was the ancient ceremony: and, as the Lord is the common Redeemer of all, he has a right to claim us as his own, from the least to the greatest."
2. "Nor is it without a good reason, that Luke so frequently repeats the statement, that Joseph and Mary did what was written in the law of the Lord. For these words teach us, that we must not, at our own suggestion, attempt any thing in the worship of God, but must obediently follow what he requires in his Word."
3. "Since the Lord is Redeemer of all the world at large."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Every male that openeth the womb, i.e., every first-born male"
2. "The requirement respecting the first-born was in remembrance of the sparing of the first-born of the Israelites in Egypt"
3. "He who was Himself Priest and Temple, doing God's service as none ever did, probably submitted to the form of redemption from the temple service."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Paul (Galatians 4:4) will urge that Jesus 'was made under the law' as Luke here explains."
2. "The law did not require that the child be brought to Jerusalem."
3. "The purification concerned the mother, the presentation the son."

### John Gill:
1. "Every male that openeth the womb, shall be called holy to the Lord; that is, devoted and consecrated to him, and so to be redeemed."
2. "This law our Lord came under as Mary's firstborn, and as one holy to the Lord; and such a sum of money was now paid for his redemption, who was the great Redeemer of his people."
3. "He being made under the law, and in all things subject to it, that he might redeem them from the bondage, curse, and condemnation of it."

### John Wesley:
1. Exodus 13:2.
