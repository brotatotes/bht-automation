1. "armies, that is, besieged: "the abomination of desolation" (meaning the Roman ensigns, as the symbols of an idolatrous, pagan, unclean power)"
2. "spoken of by Daniel the prophet" (Whoso readeth [that prophecy] let him understand)

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Compassed with armies. The plainest and most graphic form of the prediction."
2. "There was abundant time, after the first approach of the Roman armies, for the Christians to flee."
3. "Her desolation did not then begin, but was at hand."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The point of this warning is the present tense, being encircled. It will be too late after the city is surrounded."
2. "One may ask why not, if such a thing as predictive prophecy can exist and especially in the case of the Lord Jesus."
3. "Christians did flee from Jerusalem to Pella before it was too late as directed in Luke 21:21; Mark 13:14; Matthew 24:16."

### John Gill:
1. "And when ye shall see Jerusalem compassed with armies, then know that the desolation thereof is nigh."
2. "There would be no deliverance to be expected."
3. "The siege raised until it was destroyed, which was about four years after."
