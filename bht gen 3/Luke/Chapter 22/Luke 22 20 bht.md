# Luke 22:20 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus, after the supper, declared that the cup was the new covenant in his blood. This is significant because shedding blood was commonly associated with covenant ratification, and blood was believed to make a covenant indelible. The cup represents the wine poured out, symbolizing the blood of Christ. While there may be debate about the number of cups used during the meal, the emphasis is on the cup being a sign and seal of the New Testament. Thus, the cup figuratively represents both the wine and the blood of Christ shed to confirm the New Testament.
