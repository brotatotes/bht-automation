# Luke 24:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
As Jesus approached the two disciples on the road, he noticed their earnest and sad conversation. They had been deeply affected by the death of Jesus and were discussing the overwhelming events that had transpired. Though they may have taken different views in their dispute, there was no blame implied. Jesus interrupted their discussion, curious about the subject that had so much affected their minds. Their conversation held a reverence for Jesus, as they navigated their grief and sought understanding on their journey.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The term used by our Lord implies that they had been disputing with some earnestness: but there is no blame implied in the word."
2. "Possibly, though both were sad, they may have taken different views."
3. "In the answer of Cleopus we have that of the one who was most disposed to abandon all hope."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 17-24. communications, &amp;c.—The
	words imply the earnest discussion that had appeared in their manner.


### Albert Barnes:
1. "What manner of communications ... - What is the subject of your conversation? What is it that has so much affected your minds?"
2. "They were deeply affected in the recollection of the death of Jesus; and, as became all Christians, they were conversing about him."
3. "They were sad at the overwhelming events that had come upon them."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Discussed a doubt and tossed it to and fro" (Tennyson).
2. "And are sad" - Only here and Matthew 6:16, on which see note.
3. "And they stood still, looking sad."

### John Calvin:
1. "They had in their hearts a reverence for his doctrine, so that they were far from having any inclination to revolt."
2. "The name of Christ was, at that time, so generally held in hatred and detestation, that it was not safe to speak of him respectfully."
3. "Certain it is, that a little afterwards, he does not simply place Christ in the ordinary rank of prophets, but says that he and others believed him to be the redeemer."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Some earnest disputing is meant, though no blame is implied." 
2. "It suggests that the interruption was unwelcome."
3. "A briefer reading gives: 'as ye walk (being) sad?'"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "That you have with another (ους αντιβαλλετε προς αλληλους). Αντι-βαλλω is an old verb and means to throw in turn, back and forth like a ball, from one to another, a beautiful picture of conversation as a game of words."
2. "They stood still (εσταθησαν). First aorist passive of ιστημ, intransitive. They stopped."
3. "Looking sad (σκυθρωπο). This is the correct text. It is an old adjective from σκυθρος, gloomy and οπς, countenance. Only here in the N.T."

### John Gill:
1. "What manner of communications are these, that ye have one to another, as ye walk?"
