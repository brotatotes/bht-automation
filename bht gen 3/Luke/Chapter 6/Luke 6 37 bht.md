# Luke 6:37 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus teaches his disciples about judgment and forgiveness, urging them to release others from condemnation and not form opinions rashly or unfairly. By forgiving offenses, believers will be forgiven in return. However, this promise is not absolute, as believers themselves may provoke similar judgment if they are excessively severe in their treatment of others. Jesus warns against being judgmental, as it may result in falling under God's righteous condemnation and being treated in the same manner by others.

### John Calvin:
1. "This promise, which is added by Luke, means, that the Lord will cause him, who is indulgent, kind, and just to his brethren, to experience the same gentleness from others." 
2. "We know that the promises which relate to the present life do not always hold, and are not without exceptions."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Jesus does not mean that we are not to form opinions, but not to form them rashly, unfairly, like our prejudice."
2. "Censoriousness is a bad habit."
3. "Release: Positive command the opposite of the censoriousness condemned."

### John Gill:
1. "Judge not, and ye shall not be judged... lest you should fall under the righteous censure, judgment, and condemnation of God."
2. "Condemn not, and ye shall not be condemned... lest you should be treated in like manner by others."
3. "Forgive offences and trespasses committed against you, bear with, and pass by injuries and affronts... and ye shall be forgiven."
