### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Can the blind? The interrogative particle expects a negative reply. Surely the blind cannot, etc."
2. "Lead. Better, guide, as Rev., since the word combines the ideas of leading and instructing."

### John Calvin:
1. "Luke relates this saying without mentioning any occurrence, but states generally, that Christ made use of this parable." 
2. "It is no doubt possible that Christ may have spoken this parable more than once."
3. "I have not hesitated to insert here what Luke relates without fixing the time."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "And he spake also a parable to them. This indicates plainly that the connection with what precedes is broken off." 
2. "The close connection with what follows forbids the view that they are inserted here out of theft place."
3. "Can the blind, etc. See Matthew 15:14. Probably a familiar saying of our Lord."

### John Gill:
1. "Can the blind lead the blind? They may do so, as the blind Scribes and Pharisees led the blind people of the Jews."
2. "Shall they not both fall into the ditch? Yes, verily, what else can be expected?"
3. "These proverbs and sentences were repeated at different places and times, which is not improbable."

### John Wesley:
1. "Can the blind lead the blind? - Can the scribes teach this way, which they know not themselves? Will not they and their scholars perish together?" 
2. "Can they make their disciples any better than themselves?" 
3. "But as for those who will be my disciples, they shall be all taught of God; who will enable them to come to the measure of the stature of the fulness of their Master."
