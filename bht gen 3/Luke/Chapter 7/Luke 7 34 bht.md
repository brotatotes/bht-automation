# Luke 7:34 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus faced criticism for his association with sinners and for partaking in worldly activities. Despite being called a glutton and a winebibber, the wisdom of God is vindicated by those who truly understand His methods of calling sinners to repentance. Outward austerity does not necessarily equate to the highest perfection, as Jesus exemplified a divine sobriety while engaging in the customs of ordinary life. It is crucial to avoid mistaking Christ's actions for endorsement of sinful excess, as the truly wise recognize and approve of God's multifaceted approach to salvation.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### John Calvin:
1. "Those who think that the highest perfection consists in outward austerity of life...ought to attend to this passage." 
2. "On this principle John would rank higher than the Son of God; but, on the contrary, we ought to maintain, that bodily exercise profiteth little, but godliness is profitable to all things."
3. "Besides, while Christ accommodated himself to the usages of ordinary life, he maintained a sobriety truly divine, and did not encourage the excesses of others by his dissimulation or by his example."

### John Gill:
1. "The son of man is come eating and drinking....and carries it freely and courteously to all men." 
2. "and ye say, behold a gluttonous man and a wine bibber; an epicurian, a drunkard, a mere sot, one that gives up himself to sensual pleasures." 
3. "a friend of publicans and sinners; a good fellow, a boon companion, that sits with them, and encourages them in their revellings and drunkenness."

### John Wesley:
1. "But wisdom is justified by all her children." 
2. "The children of wisdom are those who are truly wise unto salvation." 
3. "The wisdom of God in all these dispensations, these various methods of calling sinners to repentance, is owned and heartily approved by all these."
