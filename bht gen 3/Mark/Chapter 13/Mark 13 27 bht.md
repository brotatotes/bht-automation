### Henry Alford:
1.  27.] from the uttermost part of the earth, from the extremity of the visible plane of the earth, shall the collecting begin: and shall proceed to the uttermost part of heaven, to the point where the sky touches that mate the other side.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "As the tribes of Israel were anciently gathered together by sound of trumpet, so any mighty gathering of God's people, by divine command, is represented as collected by sound of trumpet."
2. "When Jerusalem shall be reduced to ashes, and that wicked nation cut off and rejected, then shall the Son of man send His ministers with the trumpet of the Gospel, and they shall gather His elect of the several nations, from the four corners of heaven."
3. "But though something like this appears to be the primary sense of the verse, in relation to the destruction of Jerusalem, no one can fail to see that the language swells beyond any gathering of a human family into a Church upon earth, and forces the thoughts onward to that gathering of the Church 'at the last trump,' to meet the Lord in the air, which is to wind up the present scene."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "From the uttermost part of the earth to the uttermost part of heaven." 
2. "From the outermost border of the earth, conceived as a flat surface, to where the outermost border of the heaven sets a limit to the earth." 
3. "Mark's expression is more poetical."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "From the uttermost part of the earth, etc. Probably an allusion to the apparent junction of earth and sky at the visible horizon, but in any case it refers to the whole world."
2. "Matthew gives a different form, and inserts 'with a trumpet of great sound.'"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Shall gather together his elect... This is the purpose of God through the ages."
2. "From the uttermost part of the earth to the uttermost part of heaven... from the tip of earth to the tip of heaven."
3. "The Greek is very brief, 'from the tip of earth to the tip of heaven.' This precise phrase occurs nowhere else."

### John Gill:
1. "And then he shall send his angels... and shall gather together his elect..."
2. "...these ministers shall be the means of gathering such whom God has chosen from all eternity..."
3. "...from the uttermost part of the earth, to the uttermost part of the heaven..."
