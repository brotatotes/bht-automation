# Mark 2:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The repetition in the last clause reveals the authority of the report and aligns with Mark's penchant for such solemn repetitions. It reflects the somber atmosphere of the occasion, contrasting with the joyous wedding feast. By identifying himself as the bridegroom, Jesus symbolically connects with the covenant relationship between God and Israel. This challenges the importance placed on rituals and ceremonies, emphasizing the significance of spiritual matters. Therefore, it would be inappropriate for the disciples to fast while the bridegroom is present.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The repetition in the last clause, contained neither in Matthew nor Luke, is inconsistent with the design of an abridger; and sufficiently shews the primary authority of this report." (Quote 1)
2. "St. Mark especially loves these solemn repetitions." (Quote 2)
3. "It is a touching way, as Meyer well observes, of expressing 'in that dark day.'" (Quote 3)

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The sons of the bride chamber are different from the groomsmen."
2. "The scene is laid in Galilee, where groomsmen were not customary, as in Judaea."
3. "Hence there is no mention of them in the account of the marriage at Cana."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Jesus identifies himself with the bridegroom of the O.T., God in his covenant relation with Israel."
2. "Mourning does not suit the wedding feast."
3. "Jesus here seems iconoclastic to the ecclesiastics and revolutionary in emphasis on the spiritual instead of the ritualistic and ceremonial."

### John Gill:
1. "Can the children of the bride chamber fast, while the bridegroom is with them?" 
2. "It was very unsuitable for them, and very unreasonable to desire them to fast at such a time, and under such a character."
3. "As long as they have the bridegroom with them, they cannot fast."
