# Mark 3:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The unclean spirits, through the possessed individuals, repeatedly recognized Jesus as the Son of God, falling down before Him and crying out. These evil spirits acknowledged Jesus' divinity, emphasizing their subjection to Him and His extraordinary power over them. Jesus, fully aware of His identity and mission, charged the spirits not to make Him known, as He had done on previous occasions. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The unclean spirits are here spoken of in the person of those possessed by them, and the two fused together."
2. "As it was impossible that any but the spirits could have known that He was the Son of God, so it was the material body of the possessed which fell down before Him, and their voice which uttered the cry."
3. "The notion of the semirationalists that the sick identified themselves with the demons is at once refuted by the universal agreement of the testimony given on such occasions, that Jesus was the Son of God."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The spirits, the unclean ones."
2. "The imperfect tense denotes a repeated act."
3. "As often as they might see him."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The demon identified himself with the person, since the confession was undoubtedly that of the evil spirit."
2. "The possessed man fell down, and his voice uttered the cry; but both acts are attributed to the evil spirit; hence the intimate possession."
3. "The son of God."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They kept falling down before him and crying."
2. "The unclean spirits (demons) recognize Jesus as the Son of God."
3. "Jesus charged them not to make him known as he had also done before."

### John Gill:
1. "As soon as ever they beheld Christ, though they had never seen him before, and he was an entire stranger to them, yet fell down before him." 
2. "The unclean spirits being said to do that, which they that were possessed with them did; and which, notwithstanding their possession of them, they could not prevent, but were obliged to admit of it, as a token of their subjection to Christ."
3. "Even the devils themselves in the men cried, saying, thou art the Son of God; a divine person, equal with God; and such his power over them, and his healing all manner of diseases, by a word, or touch, showed him to be."
