### Marvin Vincent:

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Growing up and increasing." The words are peculiar to Mark. This is spoken of the 'fruit,' but in the wider sense of the whole progress of the plant, since all this is necessary to the real fruit or grain, which was brought forth.
2. "This verse puts the smallest proportion first; in Matthew's account it is put last." Other verbal differences attesting the independence of the Evangelists, are indicated as far as possible in the footnotes to the text.

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Growing up and increasing." 
2. "A vivid detail enlarging on the continued growth implied in the imperfect 'yielded fruit.'"
3. "Fruit is what matters."

### John Gill:
1. "The word was preached to some whose hearts were disposed by the Spirit and grace of God to receive it."
2. "They not only appeared, and made an outward profession of the word, and brought forth a little show of fruit, which comes to nothing, as in others; but they were filled with the fruits of righteousness."
3. "In some the word of God produced larger and greater effects; the grace of God was more in exercise in some than in others, and some were more fruitful and useful."
