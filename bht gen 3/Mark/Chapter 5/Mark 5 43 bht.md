# Mark 5:43 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
An eyewitness account of the girl being raised from the dead is provided, while Matthew gives details of the spreading report. Her recovery of strength is left to natural causes. Jesus charges those present to remain silent, perhaps to manage public perception of him and delay the expected climax. The instruction to provide her with food shows the compassionate care of the Great Physician. While the miracle's concealment would not last long, Jesus intends for a delay in order to reflect on God's work calmly.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Betokens an eyewitness, who relates what passed within."
2. "St. Matthew says nothing of this, but tells what took place without, viz. the spreading abroad of the report."
3. "Notice in the last words, that her further recovery of strength is left to natural causes."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The only reason we can assign for this is His desire not to let the public feeling regarding Him come too precipitately to a crisis."
2. "In token of perfect restoration."
3. "He charged them straitly."

### John Calvin:
1. "The miracle might not have remained long concealed."
2. "He wished them to be silent, and that only for a time."
3. "He therefore intended to allow some delay, that they might in quietness and composure revolve the work of God."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Charged them much. A tumult might be excited, the carnal expectations about the Messiah might be roused." 
2. "The miraculous power now ceased: she needed food; her strength would be recovered by natural means."
3. "At the same time it was an evidence that she was actually restored."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "She could walk and eat; not only alive, but well" (Bruce).
2. "Both Mark and Luke note that Jesus ordered that food be given to the child given her to eat, a natural care of the Great Physician."
3. "There was the girl besides."

### John Gill:
1. "This charge he gave, to show his dislike of ostentation and popular applause, and to avoid the envy of the Scribes and Pharisees, and prevent the people from making any attempts to proclaim him king..."
2. "His time not being yet come to die, he having some other work to do; and a more full manifestation of him being reserved for another time, and to be done in another way."
3. "She did not rise with it, but was free from it; and was now like one that had been asleep for a while, and was hungry upon it; as children of such an age generally are upon rising from sleep."
