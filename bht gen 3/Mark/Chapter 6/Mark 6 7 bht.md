### Albert Barnes:
1. "Amid the trials and opposition with which they would meet, mutual counsel and aid would greatly lighten their burdens and alleviate their calamities."
2. "Mutual counsel might also contribute to their success, and lead to 'united' plans to advance the kingdom of the Redeemer."
3. "Jesus here, as in all the work of religion, consulted at the same time the 'happiness' and the 'usefulness' of his disciples; nor are they ever separated."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. By two and two. To help and encourage each other, and also for fullness of testimony. 
8 - 12. See Matthew 10:0.


### Philip Schaff:
1. "A proof both of truthfulness and of independence."
2. "Power over the unclean spirits. Peculiar to Mark, and characteristic of his narrative."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Mark preserves the vernacular Koine better than the other Gospels and this detail suits his vivid style." 
2. "The six pairs of apostles could thus cover Galilee in six different directions."
3. "They were to preach and to heal."

### John Gill:
1. "He first sent forth one couple, and then another; the reason of his sending them by pairs, was partly for the sake of company, and that they might be useful and assisting to one another."
2. "Being thus sent by pairs into different parts, their message would be the sooner dispatched than if they had all went together."
3. "The reason of all which seems to be, because he refers not to the same time as Matthew does, to their appointment and ordination; but to the time they were sent out and proceeded on their journey."
