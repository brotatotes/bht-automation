# Mark 6:8 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
These verses highlight the independence and simplicity of the travelers and pilgrims in their journey. They carried only a staff, a symbol of austere simplicity and self-denial. Carrying a staff was common for travelers, but the disciples were instructed to go even without one, relying solely on the provision that would be made for them. This shows the spirit of relying on God's provision and not making special preparations or taking special care. The disciples were to be unencumbered, always ready for motion, and their labor would not be in vain.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Save a staff only...make no special preparation, take no special care." 
2. "No wallet. A leathern pouch."
3. "No money in their purse, lit., 'not brass into the girdle.'"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Save a staff only... Every traveller and pilgrim carried his staff." 
2. "Without even a staff is the ne plus ultra of austere simplicity, and self-denial." 
3. "Men who carry out the spirit of these precepts will not labour in vain."

### John Gill:
1. "Travellers used to put their bread, or any other sort of food into their scrips, and their money in their girdles; but the disciples were not allowed to carry either, because provision was to be made for them wherever they came at free cost, it being what their labour was worthy of." 
2. "Staves in the plural number are forbidden."
3. "A single one, for staves in the plural number are forbidden."

### John Wesley:
1. "That they might be always unincumbered, free, ready for motion."
2. "Save a staff only."
3. "He that had one might take it; but he that had not was not to provide one."
