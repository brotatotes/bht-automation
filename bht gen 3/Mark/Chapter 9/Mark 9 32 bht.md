2. "While the other Evangelists...notice their ignorance and their fear, Matthew, who was one of them, retains a vivid recollection of their sorrow."
3. "Strife among the Twelve, with Relative Teaching."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They were agnostics on the subject of the death and resurrection even after the Transfiguration experience."
2. "They were afraid to ask him, continued to be afraid, perhaps with a bitter memory of the term 'Satan' hurled at Peter."
3. "It was concealed from them, probably partly by their own preconceived ideas and prejudices."

### John Gill:
1. "They could not understand how it could be, and upon what account, and for what end, so holy and good, and innocent a man as he was, could be put to death."
2. "They could not understand how this could consist with his character, as the Son of God, the Messiah, and king of Israel."
3. "They were afraid to ask him; lest they should be upbraided with their ignorance and stupidity."

### John Wesley:
1. "They did not understand how to reconcile the death of our Saviour (nor consequently his resurrection) with their notions of his temporal kingdom."
2. "They did not understand the word."
3. "They did not understand his death and resurrection."
