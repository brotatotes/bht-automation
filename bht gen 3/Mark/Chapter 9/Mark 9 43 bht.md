# Mark 9:43 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
It is better to cut off any dispositions or temptations that hinder one's spiritual well-being, even if they are as dear as a hand or an eye. By renouncing these detrimental influences and associations, one can enter into eternal life. Gehenna, a cursed place where fires burned and worms gnawed, symbolizes the consequences of not severing these harmful ties. Therefore, it is crucial to strike at the root of such inclinations and renounce all association with them.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "And if thy hand offend thee, cut it off: it is better for thee to enter into life maimed, than having two hands to go into hell." 
2. "The only difference between the words there and here is that there they refer to impure inclinations; here, to an ambitious disposition, an irascible or quarrelsome temper, and the like." 
3. "The injunction is to strike at the root of such dispositions and cut off the occasions of them."

### Marvin Vincent:

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Not Hades, but Gehenna." 
2. "The Valley of Hinnom had been desecrated by the sacrifice of children to Moloch so that as an accursed place it was used for the city garbage where worms gnawed and fires burned."
3. "It is thus a vivid picture of eternal punishment."

### John Gill:
1. "It is better to part with everything here that is detrimental to a man's doing, or enjoying, what is spiritually good, and enter into eternal life."
2. "By enjoying such persons and things, agreeable to the flesh, to the ruin of the soul, and be cast into hell."
3. "Gehinnom is a place known, near to Jerusalem, and a valley, whose fire is never quenched; and in which they burn bones of defilement, and carcasses, and other polluted things."

### John Wesley:
1. "If one who is as useful or dear to thee as a hand or eye, hinder or slacken thee in the ways of God, renounce all intercourse with him."
2. "This primarily relates to persons, secondarily to things."
3. "Renounce all intercourse with him."
