# Matthew 10:1 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Notice that the disciples were not chosen at this point, but rather empowered and authorized by Jesus for a specific mission. He gave them power and authority, not only to heal the sick, cast out demons, and raise the dead, but also to preach the gospel. These men were plain, honest, and trustworthy witnesses, uniquely qualified to testify about Jesus and His teachings. The number twelve, symbolizing the Trinity indwelling in the world, holds great significance. Jesus equipped them with heavenly power to heal physical ailments and cure souls through the preaching of the Gospel.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The word signifies both 'power' and 'authority' or 'right.' Even if it were not evident that here both ideas are included, we find both words expressly used in the parallel passage of Luke." 
2. "He both qualified and authorized them."
3. "He gave them power and authority."

### Albert Barnes:

3. "No witnesses were ever so well qualified to give testimony as they, and none ever gave so much evidence of their sincerity as they did."
