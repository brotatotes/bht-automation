# Matthew 10:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
When facing the prospect of defending their faith, Jesus instructed his disciples not to be anxious or solicitous about their speech, emphasizing the importance of real heroism and loyalty to Him. Rather than worrying about how or what to speak, believers were assured that in that same moment, the Spirit would provide them with the most fitting words. The Lord's promise of divine assistance was meant to alleviate their fears and objections, empowering them with eloquence beyond what they could have prepared on their own.

1. "In vain would Christ have given a hundred exhortations to the disciples, if he had not, at the same time, promised that God would be with them, and that through his power they would assuredly be victorious."

### John Gill:
1. "Now, in order to remove these their fears and objections, and to strengthen and comfort their minds, our Lord bids them, when this would be their ease, that the Jews would deliver them to the Roman magistrates, to take no thought how, or what ye shall speak."
2. "They should have no occasion, as orators do, to take pains, and rack their thoughts, to prepare a studied, elaborate oration, dressed with all the flowers of rhetoric, filled with the most moving and powerful arguments, and clothed with diction of the strictest propriety and elegance; for they should want neither words, nor things."
3. "Immediate assistance should be afforded them either by his father, or himself; or rather, the blessed Spirit, who would suggest unto them, at once, things, the most proper to be said, and help them to deliver them in the most proper manner: and these are the most convincing arguments, and that the best elocution, which the Spirit of God helps men to."
