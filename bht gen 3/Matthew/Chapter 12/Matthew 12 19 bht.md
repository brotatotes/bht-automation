## BHT:
He shall not strive nor cry; neither shall any man hear his voice in the streets. In this portrayal of Christ, we witness a humble and gentle Judge who refrains from engaging in public conflicts or seeking attention. He chooses to withdraw instead of prolonging fruitless disputes and willingly endures mistreatment without complaint. Christ's quiet and peaceful approach should be embraced with joy, as it showcases His remarkable humility and gentleness.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 19. He shall not strive nor cry;
	neither shall any man hear his voice in the streets.


### John Calvin:
1. "Were Christ to appear in his glory, what else could be expected, but that it would altogether swallow us up? What wickedness then is it to be less willing to receive him, when on our account he descends from his elevation?" 
2. "Each of us is conscious of his own weakness; and therefore we ought to consider of what importance it is that Christ should treat us with kindness." 
3. "When Christ is thus pleased to condescend to our weakness, let his unspeakable goodness be embraced by us with joy."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He shall not strive, nor cry, etc. Not a combatant nor a noisy declaimer in public places, but meek and retiring."
2. "He presents Himself as Judge and yet is meek; He is meek, does not strive, and yet shall be victor."
3. "The lessons are obvious."

### John Gill:
1. "He shall not strive... but shall choose rather to withdraw, than to carry on a controversy to a great length, to little purpose." 
2. "He shall not complain, or bring in any charge, or accusation against any, but choose rather to suffer wrong, than to contend." 
3. "He silently put up all abuses, and patiently bore every affront, and behaved peaceably, quietly, committing himself and cause to a righteous God."

### John Wesley:
1. "He shall not be contentious, noisy, or ostentatious."
2. "But gentle, quiet, and lowly."
3. "Each word rises above the other, expressing a still higher degree of humility and gentleness."
