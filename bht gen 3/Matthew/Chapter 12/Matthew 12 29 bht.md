# Matthew 12:29 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Christ, as the stronger one, compares himself to a spoiler who enters a strong man's house to spoil his goods. In this analogy, the strong man represents Satan, and his house represents the world, where he has reigned for a long time. By binding the strong man first, Christ demonstrates his power and authority over Satan. Through his victorious conquest, Christ liberates us from Satan's tyranny and brings us into the freedom and grace of his kingdom.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Christ is not citing a general illustration, but is pointing to a specific enemy - Satan."
2. "How can I despoil Satan without first having conquered him?"
3. "Goods...the entire equipment of a house, collectively: chattels, house-gear."

### John Calvin:
1. "He maintains that a strong and powerful tyrant cannot be deprived of his dominion, till he is stripped of his armor; for if he is not met by a force superior to his own, he will never yield of his own accord."
2. "Now though it is contrary to nature that the devil reigns, and though it is by the just punishment of God, on account of sin, that men are subjected to his tyranny, yet he remains in quiet possession of his kingdom, and may insult us at his pleasure, till a stronger than he shall rise up against him."
3. "Let us also learn that, as we are all subject to the tyranny of Satan, there is no other way in which he commences his reign within us, than when he rescues us, by the powerful and victorious arm of Christ, from that wretched and accursed bondage."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "If I were not the Messiah, stronger than Satan, how could I thus spoil him?"
2. "The strong man represents Satan; his 'house' the world where he has long reigned; 'his goods,' the possessed or the evil spirits possessing them."
3. "The word 'spoil' here is stronger than the one used in the last clause, indicating a complete victory over Satan in this world."

### John Gill:
1. "...he could never spoil his goods, as he did; or dispossess devils out of the bodies or souls of men..."
2. "...unless he has a power superior to his, and uses it; except he first bind the strong man, and then he will spoil his house..."
3. "...Christ is stronger than he, and attacked him, and dispossessed him of the bodies of men; and restraining him from doing them any hurt, enters into the souls of men, dethrone him, and leads him captive..."

### John Wesley:
1. "How can one enter into the strong one's house, unless he first bind the strong one." 
2. "So Christ coming into the world, which was then eminently the strong one's, Satan's house, first bound him."
3. "And then took his spoils."
