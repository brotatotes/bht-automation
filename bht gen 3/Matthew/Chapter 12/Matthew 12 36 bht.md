# Matthew 12:36 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Our words, as the index of the heart, whether good or bad, will be taken account of in estimating character in the day of judgment. An idle word is morally useless and unprofitable. Jesus teaches us that our words reveal our thoughts and form a just basis for the interpretation of character. Let us be cautious and serious with our speech, abstaining from trifling fooleries and slanderous attacks. Remember, even the idle words we speak will be accounted for in the day of judgment.

1. "Idle is perhaps best taken here in its milder and negative sense, as not yet determined on till the judgment."
2. "Our Lord's declaration is a deduction 'a minori,' and if of every idle saying, then how much more of every wicked saying!"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Christ closes this address to his malignant and wicked hearers by a solemn declaration that for these things God would bring them into judgment." 
2. "Idle word - This literally means a vain, thoughtless, useless word; a word that accomplishes no good."
3. "Here it means, evidently, 'wicked, injurious, false, malicious, for such' were the words which they had spoken."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "An idle word is a non-working word; and inoperative word."
2. "It has no legitimate work, no office, no business."
3. "Morally useless and unprofitable."

### John Calvin:
1. "An idle word means one that is useless, or that yields no edification or advantage."
2. "If we consider the purpose for which our tongues were made, we will acknowledge that those men are justly held guilty who unthinkingly devote them to trifling fooleries."
3. "Let us speak of the sacred mysteries of God with the utmost reverence and sobriety; let us abstain from talkativeness, buffoonery, and vain jests, and much more from slanderous attacks; and let us endeavor to have our speech seasoned with salt."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "A word that does no good and so is pernicious like pernicious anaemia."

### John Gill:
1. "By an 'idle word' is meant, what the Jews call, 'light conversation', and 'vain discourse', as the Hebrew Gospel of Munster reads it here; frothy language, unprofitable talk, which, though it does not directly hurt God or man, yet is of no use to speaker or hearer."
2. "Even this, in the last general and awful judgment, if not forgiven, and repented of, must be accounted for."
3. "And much more such horrid blasphemies the Pharisees had vented against Christ, and the Spirit of Christ."

### John Wesley:

3. "For want of seriousness or caution; for every discourse which is not conducive to the glory of God, they shall give account in the day of judgment."
