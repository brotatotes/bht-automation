# Matthew 12:48 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Christ's unique family relation signifies the high honor bestowed on his disciples and believers. By claiming them as his own, he reveals the sacredness of spiritual relationships and the close union between himself and those who have been regenerated by the Spirit. In disclaiming his natural relations, Jesus prioritizes his heavenly Father above all else, showing both severity towards those who oppose God and goodness towards those who obey Him. Such disownment highlights the contrasting nature of his interactions with his natural and spiritual relations.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### John Calvin:
1. "He admits all his disciples and all believers to the same honorable rank, as if they were his nearest relatives, or rather he places them in the room of his mother and brethren."
2. "He tells us also, that there is no tie of relationship more sacred than spiritual relationship."
3. "This passage, first, teaches us to behold Christ with the eyes of faith; and, secondly, it informs us, that every one who is regenerated by the Spirit...is thus admitted to the closest union with Christ, and becomes one with him."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Implying, not contempt nor carelessness, but that the family relation in His case was peculiar."
2. "He was more than man, or was not justified in thus speaking."
3. "The family relation in His case was peculiar."

### John Gill:
1. "Who is my mother? and who are my brethren? These questions are put, not as if he himself was ignorant who were his mother or his brethren."
2. "His heavenly Father was preferred by him to any his natural relations could have with him."
3. "He might have an opportunity of pointing out who were his relations in a spiritual sense."

### John Wesley:
1. "See the highest severity, and the highest goodness!"
2. "Severity to his natural, goodness to his spiritual relations!"
3. "In a manner disclaiming the former, who opposed the will of his heavenly Father, and owning the latter, who obeyed it."
