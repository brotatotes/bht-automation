# Matthew 12:50 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Through filial subjection to the will of Jesus' Heavenly Father, believers become part of a family that surpasses earthly ties. This deep bond goes beyond mere profession of discipleship and is grounded in genuine obedience. Even those who have not physically seen Jesus on earth can experience His presence and affection through faith. By becoming part of this hallowed circle, believers are honored as Jesus' brothers, sisters, and mothers, solidifying their intimate relationship with Him. This spiritual connection supersedes earthly relationships and serves as a constant encouragement to holiness.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "There stand here the members of a family transcending and surviving this of earth."
2. "Filial subjection to the will of My Father in heaven is the indissoluble bond of union between Me and all its members."
3. "Whosoever enters this hallowed circle becomes to Me brother, and sister, and mother!"

### John Calvin:
1. "His design is, to bestow the highest commendation on faith, which is the source and origin of holy obedience, and at the same time covers the defects and sins of the flesh, that they may not be imputed."
2. "...in comparison of spiritual relationship, no regard, or very little, is due to the relationship of the flesh."
3. "As Christ bestows on the disciples of his Gospel the inestimable honor of being reckoned as his brethren, we must be held guilty of the basest ingratitude, if we do not disregard all the desires of the flesh, and direct every effort towards this object."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Mere profession of discipleship does not entitle to such a position."
2. "Christ loves His people with a love human as well as Divine; there can be no closer relationship to Him than that of real discipleship which manifests itself in this obedience to His Heavenly Father."
3. "Those who have not seen Jesus on earth, are here assured of His presence and affection in a way that should be a constant stimulant to holiness."

### John Gill:
1. "This is not to be understood of a perfect obedience to the will of God, revealed in his righteous law; but of the obedience of faith to the will of God, revealed in the Gospel; which is to believe in Christ, and have everlasting life." 
2. "Hear the Gospel, understand and believe it, and become obedient to the faith of it; these are in this near manner related to Christ, evidentially and openly." 
3. "These natural relations serve to convey some ideas of that relation, union, nearness, and communion, there are between Christ and his people."
