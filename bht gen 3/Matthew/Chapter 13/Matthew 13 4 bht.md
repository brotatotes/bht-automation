# Matthew 13:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In the parable of the sower, the imagery of the cornfield is used to illustrate the diverse conditions of people's hearts. The trodden pathway represents the wayside hearers who receive the seed without understanding, hindering their own comprehension of the word. These careless, negligent, and inattentive hearers are susceptible to the birds of Satan, who come to devour the seed. To avoid being like the wayside hearers, let us ensure that our hearts are receptive and attentive to the word of God, cultivating fertile ground for it to bear fruit in our lives.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "By the way-side."
2. "The seed was exposed, and quickly picked up by the birds."
3. "The paths or roads pass close to the edge of the ploughed ground in unenclosed fields."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Literally, "in the sowing as to him," a neat Greek idiom unlike our English temporal conjunction." 
2. "People will make paths along the edge of a ploughed field or even across it where the seed lies upon the beaten track." 
3. "'Ate down.' We say, 'ate up.'"

### John Gill:
1. "Whilst he was sowing, some seeds fell... by the wayside; by the common road, or private paths, which led through corn fields... and designs such hearers as are careless, negligent, and inattentive."
2. "They hear without understanding, judgment, and affection."
3. "Satan, and his principalities, and powers, rove about in the air, come down on earth, and seek whom they may devour."

### John Wesley:
1. "The first danger is, that the birds will devour the seed."
2. "A vast majority of those who hear the word of God, receive the seed as by the highway side."
3. "Yet in all these cases, it is not the will of God that hinders, but their own voluntary perverseness."
