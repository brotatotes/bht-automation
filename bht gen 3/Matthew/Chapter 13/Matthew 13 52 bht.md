# Matthew 13:52 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Every spiritually-learned scribe of the Kingdom of Heaven possesses a treasure of divine knowledge from which they bring forth things new and old. Through fresh forms, illustrations, and applications, they communicate instructions with zeal and demonstrate their ability to value both old and fresh spiritual truths. Equipped with this treasure, whether it be Christ Himself, the word of God, or the Gospel provisions, they provide a variety of instructions suited for every situation, always remaining fresh-minded as they share their knowledge and experience with others.

### Henry Alford:
1. "Every real spiritually-learned scribe of the Kingdom of Heaven is able, from the increasing stores of his genuine experimental knowledge of the word, to bring forth things new and old."
2. "The seven Parables compose, in their inner depth of connexion, a great united whole, beginning with the first sowing of the Church, and ending with the consummation."
3. "Then is it, during the increase of secular knowledge and cultivation of the powers of the intellect, that merchantmen shall seek goodly pearls up and down the world, and many shall find, each for himself, the Pearl of Price."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Therefore... every scribe... which is instructed unto the kingdom of heaven... is like unto a man that is an householder which bringeth forth out of his treasure... things new and old."
 
2. "Old truths in ever new forms, aspects, applications, and with ever new illustrations."
 
3. "Every scribe... himself taught in the mysteries of the Gospel which he has to teach to others."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The kingdom of heaven is personified. The disciples of Christ are disciples of that kingdom of which he is the representative."
2. "The pronoun marks the householder as belonging to a class and exhibiting the characteristic of the class: a householder - one of those who bring forth, etc."
3. "Indicating his zeal in communicating instruction and the fulness out of which he speaks."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Every scribe...hath been made a disciple, of Jesus, the Teacher as well as King."
2. "The 'treasure' is a constantly increasing knowledge of God's word, in the Bible, in nature, in experience."
3. "He who forgets the old, will get hold of novelties, but bring few new things out of his treasure; he who forgets the new, will find that his old methods have become antiquated even to himself."

### John Gill:
1. "Therefore every Scribe; meaning not legal ones, Scribes in the law of Moses, a sort of letter men, often mentioned by the evangelists, and the same with the lawyers, who were conversant with the letter of the law, and only understood that; as for the kingdom of heaven, they were so far from being instructed unto it, that they shut it up, and would neither go in themselves, nor suffer others; but evangelical Scribes are here meant..."
2. "Which bringeth forth out of his treasure, things new and old: by 'his treasure' is meant, either Christ, who is the great treasury and storehouse of grace and truth; from whence his ministers receive all their gifts, grace, light, and knowledge; or the word of God, the Scriptures of truth, by which the men of God are thoroughly furnished for every good work; or the treasure of the Gospel, which is put into their earthen vessels, into their own hearts, and that stock of Gospel knowledge and experience they are blessed with..."
3. "The phrase seems to denote the plenty and variety of Gospel provisions, which the ministers of it are to bring forth, suited to the various cases of such who are under their care."

### John Wesley:
1. "Every scribe instructed unto the kingdom of heaven... has a treasure of Divine knowledge." 
2. "Out of which he is able to bring forth all sorts of instructions."
3. "The word treasure signifies any collection of things whatsoever, and the places where such collections are kept."
