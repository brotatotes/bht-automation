### John Calvin:
1. "Christ reproves here that kind of doubt which was directly opposed to faith."
2. "A man may sometimes doubt without any fault on his part; and that is, when the word of the Lord does not speak with certainty on the matter."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "We need not fear the tempest, but only the weakness of our faith."
2. "Peter is here the image of all the faithful of all ages, in the seasons of their weakness and their fear."
3. "Christ does not calm the storm, but takes Peter by the hand."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Peter's trust in the power of Christ gave way to his dread of the wind and waves."
2. "Jesus had to take hold of Peter and pull him up while still walking on the water."

### John Gill:
1. "Christ readily gave it; he reached out his hand at once, being just by him, and caught him."
2. "He does not say, O thou unbeliever! or, O thou who hast no faith! for some faith he had, though but small."
3. "He was worthy of reproof, since he had had the order of Christ to come to him upon the water; and an experience of his power in supporting him thus far."
