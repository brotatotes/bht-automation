# Matthew 15:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Jews held the traditionary exposition in higher regard than the Scripture itself. One such tradition was the duty of washing hands before eating, which was not required by the law but by the oral teachings of the Scribes. This ceremonial washing required a complete immersion of the hands, followed by the water running down to the wrist. The rabbis elevated this tradition, considering it not just a matter of hygiene but a mortal sin. They questioned Jesus, highlighting his disciples' failure to adhere to these traditions.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "If 'holy,' i.e., sacrificial food was to be partaken of, a complete immersion of the hands, and not a mere 'uplifting' was prescribed."
2. "The water was poured on both hands, which must be free of anything covering them, such as gravel, mortar, etc."
3. "In the 'first affusion,' which was all that originally was required when the hands were not levitically 'defiled,' the water had to run down to the wrist. If the water remained short of the wrist, the hands were not clean."

### John Calvin:
1. "But as there are various kinds of human traditions, we must make some distinction among them."
2. "For it was always the will of God, as we have already said, that he should be worshipped according to the rule laid down in his word, and therefore no addition to his Law can be endured."
3. "They were desirous to warn a person not to take food while he was unclean, through want of consideration; but the Lord reckoned it enough to wash away those defilements of which they were aware."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The tradition of the elders. Certain rules handed down by word of mouth from Moses and the fathers of the nation."
2. "'The Jews attached greater value to tradition than even to the written law, appealing in support of it to Deuteronomy 4:14; Deuteronomy 17:10."
3. "The washing referred to was not an act of cleanliness, but a ceremonial washing, performed with scrupulous care."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The tradition of the elders... was magnified at great length in the oral teaching." 
2. "It was, we know, a good thing for sanitary reasons, but the rabbis made it a mark of righteousness for others at any rate."
3. "Thus a real issue is raised between Jesus and the rabbis. It was far more than a point of etiquette or of hygienics. The rabbis held it to be a mortal sin."

### John Gill:
1. "The words of the Scribes are more lovely than the words of the law: for, says R. Tarphon, if a man does not read, he only transgresses an affirmative; but if he transgresses the words of the school of Hillell, he is guilty of death..."
2. "Everyone that transgresses the words of the Scribes is guilty of death."
3. "Whoever eats bread without washing of hands is as if he lay with a whore: and, whoever despises washing of hands, shall be rooted out of the world."
