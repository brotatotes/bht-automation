# Matthew 16:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
By using the metaphor of leaven, Jesus referred to teachings, specifically those of the Pharisees and Sadducees. The leaven symbolized how teachings permeate and shape beliefs. The Pharisees' leaven was hypocrisy, while the Sadducees espoused liberal beliefs. Jesus warned against mixing personal inventions with God's word, emphasizing the need to discern true teachings. He sought to expose and diminish the doctrine of the Pharisees and Sadducees, highlighting the importance of adhering to the pure and uncorrupted word of God.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### John Calvin:
1. "The word leaven is very evidently used by Christ as contrasted with the pure and uncorrupted word of God."
2. "The naked truth of God, and the inventions which men contrive out of their own brain, are unquestionably the two things that are contrasted."
3. "All who mingle their own inventions with the word of God, or who advance anything that does not belong to it, must be rejected, how honorable soever may be their rank, or whatever proud titles they may wear."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The leaven of the Pharisees is 'hypocrisy.' But the Sadducees, the 'liberal Jews' of that age, went to the other extreme."
2. "Without Him strict morality ('Pharisees') and free inquiry ('Sadducees') inevitably become hypocritical."
3. "Principles, tendencies, 'teachings,' are most permeating, and if evil, most dangerous."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "They saw the point after this elaborate rebuke and explanation that by 'leaven' Jesus meant 'teaching.'"


### John Gill:
1. "They perceived that he did not speak of [leaven in bread], but of the doctrine of the Pharisees and of the Sadducees."
2. "[Christ] calls their doctrine [leaven in the lump], because it proceeded from thence, and was agreeable thereunto."
3. "[Jesus] uses the phrase on purpose to expose it [the doctrine of the Pharisees and Sadducees] and bring it into neglect and contempt."
