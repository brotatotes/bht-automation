# Matthew 20:25 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Christ called the disciples to Himself, not to assert His own authority, but to show them the folly of desiring superiority and power. The Jewish form of government, as ordained by God, was designed to exclude tyranny. The apostolic office has no resemblance to the governments of the world, where rulers exercise lordship over others. In the kingdom of Christ, greatness is measured by humble service, not by exercising authority. Christ distinguishes between the apostles and the rank of kings, as the station of royalty is different from the apostolic office.

### John Calvin:
1. "He does not explain generally how deadly a plague ambition is, but simply warns them, that nothing is more foolish than to fight about nothing."
2. "Christ only takes occasion from the present occurrence to show that it is absurd in the apostles to dispute about the degree of power and honor in their own rank, because the office of teaching, to which they were appointed, has no resemblance to the governments of the world."
3. "Christ distinguishes between the apostles and the rank of kings, not because kings have a right to act haughtily, but because the station of royalty is different from the apostolic office."
