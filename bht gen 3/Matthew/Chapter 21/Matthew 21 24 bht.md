# Matthew 21:24 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In response to their question about his authority, Jesus challenges the religious leaders: I will ask you one thing. The answer to his question will reveal the answer to their query. Just as John's authority came from God, so does Jesus' authority. By asking about John's authority, Jesus prompts them to consider its origin. Was it from heaven or from men? This question ultimately points to Jesus as the Christ, as John testified to his identity.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. 24. And Jesus answered and said unto
	them, I also will ask you one thing, &amp;c.


### Philip Schaff:
1. "Our Lord places His authority and that of John together."
2. "If they were incompetent to decide in the one case, they were in the other."
3. "The opportunity to decide aright was given them, but they refused it."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The answer to Christ's word will give the answer to their query."
2. "The only human ecclesiastical authority that Jesus had came from John."

### John Gill:
1. "I also will ask you one thing, word, or question, which if ye tell me; honestly, and plainly answer to it, I likewise will tell you by what authority I do these things." 
2. "Christ promises, that if they would return a plain answer to the question he had to put to them, and which was no unreasonable, nor impertinent one, he would thoroughly satisfy them in this point."
3. "He would expressly declare his commission and authority, what it was, and from whence he had it."

### John Wesley:
1. "Who have asked me many: The baptism, that is, the whole ministry of John, was it from heaven or from men?"
2. "By what authority did he act and teach? Did man or God give him that authority? Was it not God?"
3. "But if so, the consequence was clear. For John testified that Jesus was the Christ."
