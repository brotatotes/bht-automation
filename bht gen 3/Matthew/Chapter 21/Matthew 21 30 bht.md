# Matthew 21:30 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The man in this scenario exemplifies the self-righteous arrogance of those who thank God for not being like others. His initial declaration to go is hypocritical, as it remains unfulfilled. Jesus uses this illustration to highlight the disdain God has for empty promises and false righteousness. The actions of the scribes and Pharisees mirror this man's hypocrisy; they appear devoted to God's service but fail to align their actions with their professed beliefs. Such hypocrisy is deemed unacceptable, reflecting a faith devoid of genuine obedience.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The emphatic "I," here, denotes the self-righteous complacency which says, 'God, I thank thee that I am not as other men.'"
2. "He did not 'afterward repent' and refuse to go; for there was here no intention to go. It is the class that 'say and do not.'"
3. "Falseness more abominable to God, says STIER, than any 'I will not.'"

### John Calvin:
1. "It is a laudable virtue in itself, as soon as God has spoken, to yield to Him ready and cheerful obedience."
2. "Christ shows that this hypocrisy is less to be endured than the fierceness which, in process of time, is subdued."
3. "Both [delaying before doing your duty and promising what you do not perform] are improper."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "I will go, sir. I, in contrast with this one who refuses; an expression of pride."
2. "The answer was hypocritical, since it is not added that he changed his mind, but simply went not."


### John Gill:
1. "Urging also the relation they stood in to God, as a part of the Jewish body; to whom, among other external privileges, the adoption belonged."
2. "So, the Scribes and Pharisees seemed pleased with the ministry of John for a while, and at first were forward to submit to his baptism, and were very inquisitive about the Messiah: yet, when he was declared in John's ministry, and by his own doctrine, and miracles, they refused to give in to the belief of him."
3. "They, were like some other persons, who promise fair, and talk much of doing good works, but do none; teach the people to do them, but do not perform them themselves, though they would seem to do them; make great pretensions to them, boast of them, and trust in them; and therefore, of all men, ought to be careful to maintain them, and yet do the least."

### John Wesley:
1. "Just so did the scribes and Pharisees: they professed the greatest readiness and zeal in the service of God: but it was bare profession, contradicted by all their actions."
