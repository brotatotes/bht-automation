# Matthew 23:22 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Jesus emphasizes the significance of oaths by highlighting that heaven is God's throne. Swearing by an altar, gift, or temple lacks power unless it is an appeal to God Himself. The crucial aspect of an oath is invoking God as a witness to our honesty. These verses entail more than just swearing, emphasizing the necessity of truthfulness in both words and actions. False distinctions used to justify lying are condemned, as swearing by anything connected to God is implicitly swearing by Him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Albert Barnes:
1. "Heaven is his throne, Matthew 5:34. It is so called as being the place where he sits in glory."
2. "To swear by an altar, a gift, or a temple is of no force unless it be meant to appeal to God himself."
3. "The essential thing in an oath is calling God to witness our sincerity."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Every oath is by God; hence make no distinctions between oaths." 
2. "These verses really refer, not only to swearing, but to truthfulness, in word and act."
3. "They forbid those false distinctions used to palliate the crime of lying."

### John Gill:
1. "Swearing by anything that has any relation to God, is implicitly swearing by him; and therefore ought to be considered as binding..." 
2. "Since an appeal cannot be made to things inanimate, nor indeed to any creature, but to God, the searcher of hearts." 
3. "Thus swearing by anything that has any relation to God is implicitly swearing by him."
