# Matthew 23:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The verse warns against the danger of spiritual pride, specifically highlighting the violation of the letter of the law. The repetition of the title Rabbi reveals the desire of some ecclesiastics for recognition and their tendency to indulge in spiritual pride. This craving for recognition and desire for honor can still be seen today in ministers who resent not being recognized or called upon in church services, indicating a sense of unacknowledged ministerial dignity. While salutations and respect in public places are not forbidden, the verse serves as a reminder to guard against the Pharisaical pride of seeking public recognition.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Markets were places where multitudes of people were assembled together. They were pleased with special attention in public places, and desired that all should show them particular respect."

3. "Every time it [the title Rabbi] was given to them it implied their superiority to the persons who used it, and they were fond, therefore, of hearing it often applied to them."
