# Matthew 24:35 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Christ's words are more certain and enduring than anything in the world, surpassing the stability of heaven and earth. While the physical realm may ultimately pass away, His words remain unchanging and trustworthy. By placing our faith in Him, we can find security amidst the ever-changing circumstances of life. His words, particularly those predicting the impending destruction of the Jewish nation, reveal His divine foresight and authority, ensuring that they will not be empty or unaccomplished.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Albert Barnes:
1. Heaven and earth shall pass away ... - You may sooner expect to see the heaven and earth pass away and return to nothing, than my words to fail.


### John Calvin:
1. "In order to secure greater confidence in his statements, he illustrates their certainty by this comparison, that it is more firm and stable than the entire structure of the world."
2. "Christ enjoins his disciples not to allow their attention to be occupied by the world, but to look down, from what may be called the lofty watch-tower of divine providence, on all that he foretold would happen."
3. "Our salvation, because it is founded on the promises of Christ, does not fluctuate according to the various agitations of the world, but remains unshaken, provided only that our faith rises above heaven and earth, and ascends to Christ himself."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Heaven and earth shall pass away...a plain declaration that they shall pass away."
2. "But my words shall not pass away...the words of Christ will abide as true in the hearts of all His people who look for and haste unto His coming."
3. "It is implied that some time will elapse."

### John Gill:
1. "Heaven and earth shall pass away [...] they will be renewed and refined, but not destroyed; the bad qualities, or evil circumstances, which attend them through the sin of man, will be removed and pass away, but they themselves will continue in being."
2. "The heavens and the earth, and the ordinances thereof, than which nothing can be more firm and strong, being fixed and supported by God himself, shall sooner pass away, than anything asserted and predicted by Christ."
3. "My words shall not pass away; be vain and empty, and unaccomplished; which is true of anything, and everything spoken by Christ; and especially here regards all that he had said concerning the calamities that should befall the Jews, before, at, or upon the destruction of their nation, city, and temple."
