# Matthew 25:28 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this command, the gift is to be given to the one who already possesses many talents. What may seem like an act of judgment on the slothful servant ends up being an act of mercy towards the faithful one. It is important to note that this gift does not represent special grace, as special grace cannot be taken away. However, it serves as a reminder that gifts can fail and be taken away, highlighting the significance of diligently utilizing our gifts and avoiding laziness.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "This command will be given, whether the latter be a spiritual or temporal gift."
	
2. "Give it unto him that hath the ten talents."

### John Gill:
1. "This shows it was not special grace, which is intended by the talent; for the gift and calling of special grace are without repentance, and are that good part which shall not be taken away."
2. "Gifts may fail, cease, and vanish; they may be taken away from men, and men from them; a right arm may be clean dried up, and a right eye be utterly darkened."
3. "But that their gifts appear the more illustrious through the slothfulness of others."
