# Matthew 25:30 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The unprofitable servant is likened to one who does his Master no service and is cast into the darkness outside, where there will be weeping and gnashing of teeth. Doing nothing is doing harm. This parable emphasizes the importance of being profitable to God and not relying on mere harmlessness or negative goodness. It serves as a warning to be diligent in using the gifts and talents bestowed by God for the conversion of sinners and the edification of the body of Christ, to avoid the fate of weeping and gnashing of teeth.

### John Gill:
1. "All the servants of Christ are unprofitable with respect to God; for no man can be profitable to him, as he that is wise may be profitable to himself, or others."
