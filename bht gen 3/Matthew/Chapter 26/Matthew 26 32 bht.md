# Matthew 26:32 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this promise to go before his disciples into Galilee after his resurrection, Jesus links himself to the figure of a shepherd. Despite their momentary scattering, this prediction encourages the disciples to rise above the offense, as God recognizes and gathers his sheep, extending his hand towards them. This assurance is meant to console them, assuring them that although Jesus will be apprehended, condemned, crucified, and laid in the grave, he will rise again. His appearance as their shepherd in Galilee showcases his unwavering commitment to his followers.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "In this announcement our Lord seems to have in mind the remainder of the verse in Zechariah: 'and I will turn mine hand upon the little ones.'"
2. "Our Lord gives the announcement of its fulfilment, in a promise to precede them (a pastoral office, see John x. 4) into Galilee."
3. "Whither they should naturally return after the feast was over: see ch. xxviii. 7, 10, 16."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "I will go before you, as a shepherd before his flock."
2. "The thought links itself with what Christ had just said about the shepherd and the sheep."
3. "Compare John 10:4."

### John Calvin:
1. "By this prediction he encourages them to rise above the offense, because God does not cease to recognize as his sheep those who are driven out and scattered in every direction for a time."
2. "And yet this scattering did not prevent the Lord from gathering his sheep at length, by stretching out his hand towards them."
3. "But yet let the promise which is added exhilarate and support you, that God will stretch out his hand, to bring back to Him the scattered sheep."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "But after I am raised up. The resurrection is again announced."
2. "I will go before you. The figure of a shepherd is continued."
3. "Into Galilee. In Galilee He collected His disciples: chap. Matthew 28:16; John 21:0, 1 Corinthians 15:6. This gathering was the pastoral work after the resurrection, hence the other interviews in Jerusalem are not referred to."

### John Gill:
1. "This he says for their comfort, that though he, their shepherd, should be apprehended, condemned, and crucified, should be smitten with death, and be laid in the grave, yet he should rise again." 
2. "He would after his resurrection, appear to them, be at the head of them, and go before them, as a shepherd goes before his sheep."
3. "I will go before you into Galilee; the native place of most, if not all of them."

### John Wesley:
1. But notwithstanding this, after I am risen I will go before you (as a shepherd before his sheep) into Galilee. Though you forsake me, I will not for this forsake you.
