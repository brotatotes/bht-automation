# Matthew 26:52 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
When Peter raised his sword, Jesus firmly scolded him for misconstruing His previous teachings on nonviolence. Jesus explicitly stated that resorting to violence leads to destruction. He condemned offensive war entirely, promoting the pursuit of peace instead. As a spiritual entity, the Church is called to employ spiritual weapons rather than resorting to violence. This portion reinforces the prohibition against private individuals wielding the sword, except when authorized appropriately. Ultimately, Jesus wanted to instruct His followers to renounce violence and trust in God's justice and retribution.

### John Calvin:
1. "By these words, Christ confirms the precept of the Law, which forbids private individuals to use the sword."
2. "If, in such a case of moderate defense, an exception was not allowed, Christ appears to tie up the hands of all."
3. "As this is of rare occurrence, or rather, as it scarcely ever happens, Christ properly reminds his people of the general rule, that they should entirely abstain from using the sword."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "For all they that take the sword. A general proposition in regard to unwarranted recourse to measures of violence."
2. "Shall perish with the sword. The special reference is to Peter."
3. "The great lesson is: The Church, a spiritual body, may use spiritual weapons only; never carnal and violent measures."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Turn back thy sword into its place. It was a stern rebuke for Peter who had misunderstood the teaching of Jesus in Luke 22:38 as well as in Matthew 5:39 (cf. John 18:36)."
2. "The reason given by Jesus has had innumerable illustrations in human history. The sword calls for the sword. Offensive war is here given flat condemnation."
3. "The will to peace is the first step towards peace, the outlawing of war. Our American cities are often ruled by gangsters who kill each other off."

### John Gill:
1. "This Christ said not only to rebuke Peter for his rashness, but to soften the minds of the multitude, who must be enraged at such an action."
2. "For all they that take the sword, shall perish with the sword. This is not to be understood of magistrates who bear not the sword in vain, but of private persons that use the sword for private revenge."
3. "And as an argument to make and keep Peter easy and quiet, and exercise patience, since, in a little time, God would avenge himself of them; and that the Jews, who now made use of the sword of the Roman soldiers, would perish by the sword of the Romans."
