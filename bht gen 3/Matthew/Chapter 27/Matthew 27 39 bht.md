## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Albert Barnes:
1. Wagging their heads - In token of derision and insult. See Job 16:4; Psalms 109:25.


### John Calvin:
1. "These circumstances carry great weight; for they place before us the extreme abasement of the Son of God, that we may see more clearly how much our salvation cost him."
2. "But all that Christ endured in himself ought to be applied for our consolation."
3. "And, indeed, there is nothing that inflicts a more painful wound on pious minds than when ungodly men, in order to shake their faith, upbraid them with being deprived of the assistance and favor of God."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The morbid taste for horrors no doubt existed then."
2. "The elevation seems to have formed a natural stage for the public exposure of the crucified."
3. "Wagging their heads (comp. Psalms 22:6), in malignant triumph mingled with contempt."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Jews again appear on the scene, with a malice like that shewn in the trial before the Sanhedrin" (McNeile).
2. "To us it may seem incredible that even his worst enemies could be guilty of anything so brutal as to hurl taunts at one suffering the agonies of crucifixion" (Bruce).
3. "They jeer at the fallen foe."

### John Gill:
1. "They spoke all manner of evil of him, they could think of, to which he answered not a word; and which may teach us patience under the revilings of men."
2. "His last times, towards the close of his days; and cites that passage in the Misna, 'in the heels, or, as Buxtorf renders it, in the end of the days of the Messiah impudence shall be multiplied,' as it now was exceedingly."
3. "In derision of him, and as exulting in his misery."
