# Matthew 6:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Forgiving others is not just a mere formality in our spiritual life; it is a genuine reflection of the reality before God. It is closely tied to our experience of God's forgiveness for our sins. If we refuse to forgive, it raises doubts about whether we truly grasp the grace and goodness of God's forgiveness. As we approach God in prayer, our forgiveness of others is directly connected to how we ourselves are forgiven. Refusing to forgive hinders our experience of God's forgiveness.

1. "Forgive thy neighbour the hurt that he hath done unto thee, so shall thy sins also be forgiven when thou prayest."
2. "Another department of the spiritual life, in which reality in the sight of God, and not appearance in the sight of man, must be our object."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "But if ye forgive not, &c." 
2. "Having concluded His supplementary directions on the subject of prayer with this Divine Pattern, our Lord now returns to the subject of Unostentatiousness in our deeds of righteousness."
3. "To give one more illustration of it, in the matter of fasting."

### John Gill:
1. "Had they any right apprehensions of the grace and goodness of God, in the forgiveness of their sins, this would influence their minds, and engage their hearts to forgive such who have offended them."
2. "It is a plain case, that your Father has not given you a true sense of the pardon of your sins, nor can you be certain that he will; nor have you any reason to expect it, when you are so cruel and revengeful to others."
3. "If men, who are upon an equal foot with each other, should not forgive one another, how should it be expected that our Father which is in heaven, who is so much above, and no ways obliged to us, should forgive us?"
