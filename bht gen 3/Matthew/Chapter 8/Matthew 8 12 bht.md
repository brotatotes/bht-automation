# Matthew 8:12 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Rebellion against God's will leads to being cast into the darkness outside the illuminated banqueting chamber of the kingdom, where there will be weeping and gnashing of teeth. The Jews, who believed their descent from Abraham entitled them to kingdom privileges, will be expelled into the outer darkness, representing the dreadful anguish of a future state of punishment. In contrast, the unexpected inclusion of Gentiles in the same body causes consternation among those who deemed only the Jews worthy.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "the natural heirs but disinherited by rebellion."
2. "the darkness outside, i.e. outside the lighted chamber of the feast"
3. "God said to the Israelites, In the world to come I will spread for you a vast table, which the Gentiles shall see and be confounded."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The children of the kingdom...supposed themselves to be the special favorites of heaven."
2. "Many of the pagans would be saved, and many Jews lost."
3. "The image expresses the fact that the wicked who are lost will be shut out from the light of heaven, and from peace, and joy, and hope; will weep in hopeless grief, and will gnash their teeth in indignation against God, and complain against his justice."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "They shall be cast forth into the darkness, the outer (darkness)." 
2. "The picture is of an illuminated banqueting chamber, outside of which is the thick darkness of night."

### John Calvin:
1. "Though they did not actually belong to the Church of God, yet, as they occupied a place in the Church, he allows them this designation."
2. "That the Gentiles should be admitted, by a free adoption, into the same body with the posterity of Abraham, could scarcely be endured."
3. "By darkness Scripture points out that dreadful anguish, which can neither be expressed nor conceived in this life."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The Jews, who, by hereditary right and according to the ordinary law of gracious influences, might be expected to enter, shall be cast out, expelled from the feast or home of their patriarchal ancestors, into the outer darkness."
2. "There shall be the weeping and the gnashing of teeth, the sorrow and the rage consequent upon such expulsion."
3. "Also a hint at the wretchedness of a future state of punishment. The figures are fearful: black night, grief and rage."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The Jews felt that they had a natural right to the privileges of the kingdom because of descent from Abraham... But mere natural birth did not bring spiritual sonship."
2. "Into the outer darkness... one of the figures for hell or punishment... where the wailing and gnashing of teeth is heard in the thick blackness of night."

### John Gill:
1. "These branches were broken off, and the Gentiles grafted in, in their room; and will be excluded from the kingdom of heaven, where they hoped to have a place." 
2. "Phrases expressive of the miserable state and condition of persons out of the kingdom of heaven; who are weeping for what they have lost, and gnashing their teeth with the pain of what they endure."
3. "Christ may also be thought to speak in the language, and according to the notions of the Jews, who ascribe gnashing of teeth to the devils in hell."
