# Philippians 2:17 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
He views his apostolic work as a sacrifice, willing to pour out his life as a libation upon it if needed. Paul commends the Philippians for their faith, regarding it as the sacrifice he offers to God. Even if he were to die before Christ's return, he rejoices in this sacrifice. He envisions his life-blood poured upon the sacrifice of their faith, bringing mutual joy through mutual service. His readiness to die exemplifies his advanced understanding of the Christian faith, where death is gain, confirming and ratifying the gospel.

### Henry Alford:
1. "He represents his whole apostolic work for the faith of the Philippians, as a sacrifice: if he is put to death in the course of it, he will be, by the shedding of his blood, poured out as a libation upon this sacrifice."
2. "Your faith is the sacrifice, which I, as a priest, offer to God."
3. "I rejoice for myself and congratulate you on the fact that I have been thus poured out for your faith, which would be an honor and a boast for you."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "He had assumed the possibility of his being found alive at Christ's coming...he here puts a supposition which he regards as more likely, namely, his own death before Christ's coming."

3. "I congratulate you all," namely on the honor occurring to you by my blood being poured out on the sacrifice of your faith."
