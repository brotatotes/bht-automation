## BHT:
Paul expresses his hope in the Lord, trusting that through His help, Timothy will be able to come to the Philippians soon. Paul desired this because he knew that hearing about Timothy's state would bring comfort to him and encourage the Philippians in their faith. Paul's trust and hope were centered on the Lord Jesus, acknowledging His sovereignty and power. He looked to Jesus in his time of need and expected the Lord to intervene in his circumstances. ### Albert Barnes:
1. "But I trust in the Lord Jesus - His hope was that the Lord Jesus would so order affairs as to permit this - an expression that no man could use who did not regard the Lord Jesus as on the throne, and as more than human."
2. "From this passage, as well as from Philippians 1:1, where Timothy is joined with Paul in the salutation, it is evident that he had been with the apostle at Philippi."
3. "When I know your state - It was a considerable time since Epaphroditus had left the Philippians, and since, therefore, Paul had been informed of their condition."
