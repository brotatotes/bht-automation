# Philippians 3:14 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
As Christians, we are called to pursue the heavenly prize that comes from faithfully carrying out God's calling. This calling is not limited to a select few, but it is the common calling of all believers to salvation in Christ. The prize we seek is the crown of righteousness, the crown of life, and the crown of glory, eternal rewards that never fade. With our eyes fixed on Jesus, we press on towards the goal, knowing that our prize is the blessedness of dwelling with God and the hope of eternal glory and happiness.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "I pursue towards the goal for (to reach, with a view to; or perhaps simply in the direction of) the prize."
2. "the prize consequent on the faithful carrying out of that summons which I received from God in heaven."
3. "the heavenly calling represents real matter of fact."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The high, or heavenly calling, is not restricted...but the common calling of all Christians to salvation in Christ." 
2. "The prize is the crown of righteousness...the crown of life...a crown of glory that fadeth not away." 
3. "The high calling...coming from heaven invites us to heaven, whither accordingly our minds ought to be uplifted."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The 'mark' means properly the object set up at a distance at which one looks or aims, and hence the goal, or post which was set up at the end of a race-course, and which was to be reached in order that the prize might be won."
2. "The prize of the Christian is the crown that is incorruptible in heaven."
3. "The calling of the Christian is from heaven, and to heaven; It may be his, if he will not faint or tire or look backward."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "I press on towards the goal. This is a consequence of the forgetting of all that is behind. Nothing is suffered to draw off the gaze from that end which is to be reached, and which, like the winning-post in a race, is kept steadily in view."
2. "The prize is in the original a word which means the garland bestowed by the judges at the end of the race. That this idea of a crown was continually in the minds of the New Testament writers..."
3. "The real prize of this calling is the blessedness of dwelling with God, and this is the hope of the Christian calling."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Down upon the goal," who is Jesus himself to whom we must continually look as we run (Hebrews 12:2)." 
2. "The goal continually moves forward as we press on, but yet never out of sight."
3. "The word means a watchman, then the goal or mark."

### John Gill:
1. "The mark the apostle intends is the Lord Jesus Christ, who is 'the scope', or 'mark', of all the thoughts, purposes, and counsels of God, to which they all aim, and in which they all centre."
2. "To this mark they press, or 'run', they look to Jesus, while they are running their Christian race; they keep him in their view, and follow after him, because he is their forerunner."
3. "[The prize] is what God in the effectual calling calls his people to, even to a kingdom and glory, and to eternal glory and happiness."

### John Wesley:
1. "Forgetting the things that are behind" - Even that part of the race which is already run.
2. "And reaching forth unto" - Literally, stretched out over the things that are before - Pursuing with the whole bent and vigour of my soul, perfect holiness and eternal glory.
3. "In Christ Jesus" - The author and finisher of every good thing.
