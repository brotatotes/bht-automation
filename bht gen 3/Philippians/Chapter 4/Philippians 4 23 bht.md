### Albert Barnes:
1. "It is our duty to be firm in the Lord, in all the trials, temptations, and persecutions to which we may be exposed... Like Paul, we should appeal to others as 'brethren dearly beloved and longed for,' and by all their affection for us we should entreat them to be steadfast in the Christian profession." 
2. "It is the duty of those who have from any cause been alienated, to seek to be reconciled... If you are conscious that you have injured him [your brother], then nothing is more proper than that you should go and make confession." 
3. "It is our duty and our privilege to rejoice in the Lord always... The Christian, therefore, may be always happy. If he will look to God and not to himself; to heaven and not to earth, he will find permanent and substantial sources of enjoyment."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ be with your spirit." 
2. "Such changes from the primitive text have been introduced by a desire to bring all the closing benedictions as nearly as possible into one form."
3. "After this, some future scribe, having a text and margin, considered it best to bring all into the same shape."

### John Gill:
1. "The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ [be] with you all...." - This is the apostle's token in all his epistles of the genuineness of them. 
2. "Amen: with which all the epistles are concluded." - The subscription is... written [to] the Philippians from Rome, by Epaphroditus.
3. "This epistle was written to the Philippians by the Apostle Paul, when he was a prisoner at Rome, and sent to them by Epaphroditus their minister."
