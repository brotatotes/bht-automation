# Philippians 4:6 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this teaching, we are encouraged to present our worries and anxieties to God through prayer and supplication, not holding anything back. It is vital to approach God with confidence, pouring out our hearts before Him, expressing our desires, complaints, and weaknesses. Thanksgiving should accompany our requests as we acknowledge God's sovereignty and faithfulness. By surrendering our cares to God, we find solace and can experience a soul free from distress. Let us cultivate a habit of bringing all aspects of our lives to God and trusting in His provision.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "On the difference between prayer and supplication see on Eph. vi. 18, 1 Tim. ii. 1."
2. "Not 'with your thanksgiving,' because the matters themselves may not be recognized as grounds of thanksgiving, but it should accompany every request."
3. "By the prayer and the supplication appropriate to each thing."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Be anxious about nothing." Care and prayer are as mutually opposed as fire and water [BENGEL].
2. "Thanksgiving gives effect to prayer and frees from anxious carefulness by making all God's dealings matter for praise, not merely for resignation, much less murmuring."
3. "Let your requests be made known unto God with generous, filial, unreserved confidence; not keeping aught back, as too great, or else too small, to bring before God, though you might feel so as to your fellow men."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Be careful for nothing - That is, be not anxious or solicitous about the things of the present life."
2. "There is nothing which pertains to body, mind, estate, friends, conflicts, losses, trials, hopes, fears, in reference to which we may not go and spread it all out before the Lord."
3. "We can always find something to be thankful for, no matter what may be the burden of our wants, or the special subject of our petitions."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Be anxious." 
2. "Prayer and supplication." 
3. "Specific details of supplication."

### John Calvin:
1. "But this is our consolation, this is our solace â€” to deposit, or (to speak with greater propriety) to disburden in the bosom of God everything that harasses us."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The feeling which the apostle wishes to check is that undue care for the things of this life which puts this world and its concerns before the service of the Lord."
2. "In all things come to God, not merely in those which may be called strictly religious concerns, but whenever the over-anxiety is in danger of becoming too great."
3. "The Christian's forbearance is to be known unto all men, not published or paraded, but so visible in the life that it cannot fail to be recognized."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Stop being anxious." 
2. "In all the forms of prayer here named thanksgiving should appear."

### John Gill:
1. "Saints should not be anxiously, or in a distressing manner concerned for the things of this world, but be content, whether they have less or more."
2. "Use familiarity with God, tell him as you would do a friend, freely and fully, all your case, pour out your souls and your complaints before him."
3. "Be the case made known in what way or manner soever, with ever so much weakness, so be it, it is made known, it is enough, it shall be regarded and not despised."

### John Wesley:
1. "Carefulness and prayer cannot stand together."
2. "They who by a preposterous shame or distrustful modesty, cover, stifle, or keep in their desires, as if they were either too small or too great, must be racked with care."
3. "The surest mark of a soul free from care, and of prayer joined with true resignation."
