## BHT:
In this text, the writer, John, humbly identifies himself as a brother and fellow-partaker with the readers in their shared tribulation and experience of the kingdom of God. The endurance of suffering is portrayed as an essential precursor to the kingdom, underscoring the intertwined nature of tribulation, endurance, and the kingdom. John's presence in Patmos was a result of persecution, and this book, given to him in exile, brings solace and understanding to those who face hardships for their faith. Through their willingness to suffer, believers partake in the present reality of the kingdom of God.
