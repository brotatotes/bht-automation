# Revelation 12:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The woman in this text represents the Church, awaiting the second coming of Christ. The imagery of labor pains and being tormented signifies the Church's longing for the birth of the Messiah and the multiplication of believers. The cries and prayers of the saints are like the pains of childbirth, expressing the Church's eager anticipation of Christ's reign over all nations. Like a woman in labor, the Church anxiously expects the appearance of the Shepherd and King who will establish His kingdom.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The woman is evidently designed to symbolize the church."
2. "The time there referred to is at the early period of the history of the church, when, as it were, it first appears on the theater of things, and going forth in its beauty and majesty over the earth."
3. "The design of the whole, therefore, I apprehend, is to represent the peril of the church at the time when it was about to be greatly enlarged, or in a season of prosperity, from the rise of a formidable enemy that would stand ready to destroy it."
