## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "And He hath upon His vesture and upon His thigh a name written." 
2. "The usual way of taking the words is to suppose the "and" explanatory or definitive of the former words, 'on His vesture,' and that on the part of it covering His thigh."
3. "King of Kings, and Lord of Lords."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "His name written on His vesture and on His thigh." 
2. "The thigh symbolizes Christ's humanity as having come, after the flesh, from the loins of David, and now appearing as the glorified 'Son of man.'"
3. "His incommunicable divine name, 'which no man knew,' is on His head."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "This name was conspicuously written on his garment - probably his military robe."
2. "The name was conspicuously written not only on the flowing robe, but on the other parts of his dress, so that it must be conspicuous whether his military cloak were wrapped closely around him, or whether it was open to the breeze."
3. "It means that he is, in fact, the sovereign over the kings of the earth, and that all nobles and princes are under his control - a rank that properly belongs to the Son of God."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "On His thigh. Some explain, on the garment where it covers the thigh to which the sword is bound."
2. "Others, partly on the vesture, partly on the thigh itself, where, in an equestrian figure, the robe drops from the thigh."
3. "According to the former explanation kai and is to be taken as explanatory or definitive of the words on His vesture."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The name mentioned in Revelation 19:12 was probably written on the forehead. The place of this name is different."
2. "It seems to have been written on the garment where it covers the thigh to which the sword is bound."
3. "The warfare of the Lord is ended: 'All kings shall fall down before Him: all nations shall serve Him.'"

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Even upon his thigh."
2. "King of kings, and Lord of lords."
3. "The title already given to the Lamb in Revelation 17:14, but in reverse order."

### John Gill:
1. "This name, afterwards expressed, is said to be written on his vesture, in allusion to the custom of persons of note and eminence having their names interwoven in their garments."
2. "And its being said to be written on his thigh may mean either that it was upon that part of his garment which covered his thigh; or else that it was also on his sword, which he sometimes girt upon his thigh."
3. "And this name is King of kings and Lord of lords; which will well suit him now when he shall be openly King over all the earth."

### John Wesley:
1. "And he hath on his vesture and on his thigh... A name written." 
2. "It was usual of old, for great personages in the eastern countries, to have magnificent titles affixed to their garments."
