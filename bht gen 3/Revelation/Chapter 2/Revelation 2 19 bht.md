# Revelation 2:19 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
As love is inward, so service is its outward manifestation. Faith and persevering endurance, or 'patient continuance in well-doing,' are connected. And I know thy works which are last more in number than the first. More praise is given when fault follows than when no fault is found. ## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "As love is inward, so service is its outward manifestation."
2. "Faith and persevering endurance, or 'patient continuance in well-doing,' are connected."
3. "And (I know) thy works which are last (to be) more in number than the first."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "There is no reason for restricting this word here to the comparatively narrow sense which it now bears."
2. "The word here would include not only trust in Christ for salvation, but what is the proper result of such trust - fidelity in his service."
3. "Religion in the soul, and in a community, is designed to be progressive; and while we should seek to live in such a manner always that we may have the commendation of the Saviour, we should regard it as a thing to be greatly desired that we may be approved as making advances in knowledge and holiness."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The fact commended is that there is progress in them all."
2. "The path of the church has been as the morning light shining unto the perfect day."
3. "She has not fallen back like Ephesus; she has advanced."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Four items are given, with separate feminine article for each...a longer list of graces than in Revelation 2:2 for Ephesus."
2. "More praise is given in the case of Ephesus and Thyatira when blame follows than in the case of Smyrna and Philadelphia when no fault is found."
3. "Love comes first in this list in true Johannine fashion."

### John Gill:
1. "Good works, as appears from the particular enumeration of them afterwards, and the commendation of proficiency in them, the last being more than the first." 
2. "But the divine grace of love to God and Christ, and the saints, without which a profession of religion is a vain thing."
3. "Their works or acts of love to God and Christ and one another, and of service to God and to the saints, and of faith and faithfulness in the cause of God, and of patience in suffering for the Gospel of Christ, were more in quantity, and greater in quality, toward the close of this period, which brought on the Reformation, than at the beginning of it."

### John Wesley:
1. "I know thy love." 
2. "Love is shown, exercised, and improved by serving God and our neighbour." 
3. "Faith is shown by patience and good works."
