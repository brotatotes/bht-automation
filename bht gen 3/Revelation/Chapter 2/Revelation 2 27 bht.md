# Revelation 2:27 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Christ's rule is depicted as both rigorous and benevolent, with a scepter of iron symbolizing severe judgment and a scepter of gold representing grace. The breaking of a potter's vessel signifies God's sovereign power to destroy those who fail to fulfill their purpose. By overcoming the world through faith, believers are granted the opportunity to share in Christ's rule. This imagery emphasizes the firm and invincible power with which Christ will rule, ensuring that there will be no opposition to His government.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Christ shall rule them with a scepter of iron, to make them capable of being ruled with a scepter of gold; severity first, that grace may come after."
2. "A potter's vessel dashed to pieces, because of its failing to answer the design of the maker, is the image to depict God's sovereign power to give reprobates to destruction, not by caprice, but in the exercise of His righteous judgment."
3. "Having by faith 'overcome the world,' they shall also rule the world."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "To rule with a scepter of iron, is not to rule with a harsh and tyrannical sway, but with power that is firm and invincible."
2. "That is, as applied to the nations, there would be no power to oppose His rule; the enemies of his government would be destroyed."
3. "As, in respect to all the others of the seven epistles to the churches, the rewards promised refer to heaven, and to the happy state of that blessed world."
