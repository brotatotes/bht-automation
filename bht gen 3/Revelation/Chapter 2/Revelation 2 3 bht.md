# Revelation 2:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Ephesian church is praised for their unwavering loyalty to Christ, diligently laboring without growing weary. They bear the reproach of Christ with constancy, cheerfulness, and without complaint. Their endurance is reflected in their discernment and rejection of false teachings, passionately upholding the true doctrine of Christ. Despite facing trials and opposition, they remain steadfast in their commitment. Their unfaltering dedication is evident in their ability to withstand hardships with perseverance and without faltering.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Thou hast borne My reproach, but thou canst not bear the evil." 
2. "Thou hast laboured, and hast not fainted."
3. "Thou hast not been wearied out with labor."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "That is, you have not given way to murmuring or complaints in trial, you have not abandoned the principles of truth and yielded to the prevalence of error."
2. "The state of mind, considered as the state of mind appropriate to a Christian, here represented, is, that we should not tolerate error and sin, but that we should bear up under the trials which they may incidentally occasion us."
3. "...we should not be weary of it; and that though we may have many perplexities, and may meet with much opposition, yet we should not relax our zeal, but should persevere with an ardor that never faints, until our Saviour calls us to our reward."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The best texts omit hast not grown weary and read hast not grown weary."
2. "The transcribers supposed the verb kopiao to mean only to labor."
3. "It includes the sense of weariness from labor."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The chief point of commendation in the state of the Christians at Ephesus is their instinctive discernment and rejection of false teachers, and their zeal for the true doctrine of Christ."
2. "Here was the 'toil' that never wearied, the 'endurance' that never failed, the 'bearing' of that bitter cross which consisted...in contending against the 'grievous wolves' that had entered into God's heritage."
3. "The first 'work' of Christ, to maintain God's true revelation of Himself against selfish error, appears in the Ephesian church."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Continued possession of patience."
2. "The Ephesian church can bear anything except the presence of impostors in her membership."
3. "Tired in loyalty, not of it."

### John Gill:
1. "And hast borne them with constancy and cheerfulness, and were not moved by them."
2. "It was in exercise in a suitable time, and it continued with them; it was not worn out through the length and greatness of their trials."
3. "And there never was an interval in which this was more true: and hast not fainted."
