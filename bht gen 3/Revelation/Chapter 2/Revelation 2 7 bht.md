# Revelation 2:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
He that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith to the churches. This call to deep attention precedes the promise of the restored tree of life, symbolizing eternal life through Christ. Refraining from indulgences and idol-meats leads to partaking in the infinitely superior fruit of the tree of life and the hidden manna. The phrase is characteristic of John, denoting radical truths and great promises. It is an individualizing note, urging each listener to heed the voice of Christ's interpreter and find their eternal home with God.

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The phrase is not found in John's Gospel. It is used always of radical truths, great principles and promises." 
2. "The verb is used absolutely without any object expressed. It is characteristic of John, occurring once in the Gospel, six times in the First Epistle, sixteen times in Revelation." 
3. "To eat of the tree of life expresses participation in the life eternal."
