In the latter days, the adversaries led by Satan surround the camp of the saints and the beloved city, Jerusalem. This invading army spreads across the land, encircling the city, displaying their vast numbers. The eventual destruction is sudden, certain, and complete, symbolized by fire descending from heaven and consuming them. The wicked come from different parts of the world to cover the holy land, while there remains a distinction between transfigured humanity and humanity in the flesh. ### Henry Alford:
1. "And they went up...and encompassed the camp of the saints, and the beloved city." 
2. "The beloved city is Jerusalem...destined to play so glorious a part in the latter days."
3. "And there came down fire out of heaven...and devoured them."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Perhaps we ought to translate, '...of the [holy] land'." 
2. "Gog and Magog are mystical names representing the final adversaries led by Satan in person."
3. "Even during the millennium there is a separation between heaven and earth, transfigured humanity and humanity in the flesh."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The image is that of an invading army that seems, in its march, to spread all over a land."
2. "The fact that the foes were able to 'compass this camp about,' and to encircle the city at the same time, shows the greatness of the numbers of the invaders."
3. "What is obviously meant is, that their destruction would be sudden, certain, and entire, and that thus the last enemy of God and the church would be swept away."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "On the breadth. They overspread the earth." 
2. "The camp. Encompassing and defending the city." 
3. "The beloved city."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The beloved city' can be no other than Jerusalem, and this is allowed by all commentators."
2. "As 'the nations' denote the enemies, 'the beloved city' denotes the people of God; and surely not a select number, but all the 'saints.'"
3. "The destruction is complete even without mention of a battle being fought."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The hosts of Satan spread over the earth."
2. "The camp of the saints ... for an army in line of battle."
3. "And fire came down out of heaven... Devoured them."

### John Gill:
1. "the wicked being raised, will come up from the several parts of the world, and spread themselves over the holy land"
2. "the blessed and Holy Ones, who have part in the first resurrection, even all the saints; not only the martyrs under the Heathen persecutions, and the confessors of Christ under the Papacy, but all the saints from the beginning of the world"
3. "the fiery indignation of God, or his wrath, which will be poured out like fire, is here meant, which will destroy both body and soul"
