# Revelation 22:1 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In Revelation, a river of water of life, bright as crystal, is described as flowing from the throne of God and the Lamb. This river symbolizes the uninterrupted continuance of life derived by the saints, ever fresh and pure like a crystal. It serves as an emblem of peace, happiness, and abundance, reminiscent of the river in the Garden of Eden. The river represents the everlasting love of God, bringing refreshment and vitality to the heavenly city.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The phrase 'water of life,' means living or running water, like a spring or fountain, as contrasted with a stagnant pool."
2. "The allusion here is doubtless to the first Eden, where a river watered the garden."
3. "Here, the river is an emblem of peace, happiness, plenty; and the essential thought in its flowing from the throne is, that all the happiness of heaven proceeds from God."
