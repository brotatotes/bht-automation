# Revelation 22:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
In this solemnly ironic statement, the unjust are given permission to continue their unjust behavior, while the morally polluted are allowed to further defile themselves. However, the righteous are urged to persist in their pursuit of righteousness, and the holy are called to deepen their sanctification. This language does not commend the wicked, but rather emphasizes the irrevocable nature of their condition, contrasting it with the secure state of the righteous. It serves as a reminder that our actions shape our character and determine our destiny by the transformative power of the Gospel.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Let him that is unjust commit injustice still: and let the filthy (morally pollated) pollute himself still."
2. "Let the righteous do righteousness still, and the holy sanctify himself still."
3. "The saying has solemn irony."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "He that doeth unjustly, let him do unjustly still."
2. "The punishment of sin is sin, the reward of holiness is holiness."
3. "No worse punishment can God lay on ungodly men than to give them up to themselves."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "That this is the meaning here seems to me to be plain; and this sentiment accords with all that is said in the Bible of the final condition of the righteous and the wicked."
2. "Every assurance is held out in the Bible that the righteous will be secure in holiness and happiness, and that there will be no danger - no possibility - that they will fall into sin, and sink to woe."
3. "The argument for the eternal punishment of the wicked is as strong as that for the eternal happiness of the righteous; and if the one is open to doubt, there is no security for the permanence of the other."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Let him do unrighteousness."
2. "Let him be made filthy."
3. "Let him do righteousness."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The words contain that solemn lesson often taught in Scripture, but nowhere so impressively as in the writings of St. John, that the revelation of Christ is the final test of the character, and the final arbiter of the fate, of man."
2. "Under the influences of the Gospel of Christ we make out our own destinies; we sow the harvest that we shall eventually reap."
3. "We need not remain unrighteous and filthy: we may not remain righteous and holy; but, whatever the changes that we experience, this is true, that we are fixing our own character and conduct every day we live."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Let him do unrighteousness still. The language is probably ironical, with a reminder of Daniel 12:10, in no sense a commendation of their lost estate."
2. "It is the hopelessness of the final state of the wicked which is here pictured."
3. "The righteous is to do righteousness still and the holy to be made holy still. The states of both the evil and the good are now fixed forever."

### John Gill:
1. "He that is unjust, let him be unjust still... as a prophecy of what would be at the close of time, at the second coming of Christ." 
2. "He that is filthy, let him be filthy still... he will continue to defile himself; the same evil thoughts, c. will proceed out of him as ever, which defile the man." 
3. "And he that is righteous, let him be righteous still... they will ever remain in a justified state, and never enter into condemnation, their righteousness being an everlasting one."
