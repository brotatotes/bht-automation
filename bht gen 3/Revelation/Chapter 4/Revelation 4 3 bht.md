# Revelation 4:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The symbolism in the vivid description of God's appearance in gemstones reflects His multifaceted character and interactions with humanity. The emerald's green color represents God's consolatory promises amidst judgment, while the jasper stone signifies His holiness. The fiery sardius conveys His wrath, and the rainbow symbolizes the shipwreck of the world through sin as well as the subsequent calm and sunshine. All these elements combine to highlight the complexity and harmony of God's providential plan.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The same union of white or watery brightness and fiery redness appears in... symbolize the varied aspects of God's providential dealings uniting in one harmonious whole."
2. "Here, however, the predominating color among the prismatic colors is green, the most refreshing of colors to look upon, and so symbolizing God's consolatory promises in Christ to His people amidst judgments on His foes."
3. "The heavenly bow speaks of the shipwreck of the world through sin: it speaks also of calm and sunshine after the storm."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "He appeared like a prince in his royal robes."
2. "The appearance of the rainbow, therefore, around the throne, was a beautiful emblem of the mercy of God, and of the peace that was to pervade the world."
3. "The emerald is green, and this color so predominated in the bow that it seemed to be made of this species of precious stone."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The stone itself was of different colors, the best being purple. According to chapter Revelation 21:11, it represents a crystalline brightness."
2. "Some interpreters, assuming the jasper to be sparkling white, find in it a representation of the holiness of God, and in the fiery sardius a representation of His wrath."
3. "For him, as for the more primitive artists, the rainbow reflecting rainbow is the only adequate symbol of the" God of God, Light of Light "of the Nicene Creed, while the fire of love that breathes from both is that of the Holy Spirit," proceeding from the Father and the Son.

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The colours of these stones, as well as of the emerald to which the rainbow round about the throne was like, are to be understood symbolically."
2. "The first [jasper] can hardly denote anything but that holiness of God which this very chapter shows to be the feature of His character mainly in view of the sacred writer at the time."
3. "The object itself, its colour, its Old Testament history, and even the mode of its formation in nature, combine to suggest the meaning of the rainbow, the holiness and wrath of God encompassed by His covenant grace."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Like a jasper stone... Certainly not our cheap modern jasper." 
2. "The carnelian or other red stone, derived from Sardis." 
3. "John sees no form for God, but only the brilliant flashing gems."

### John Gill:
1. "And he that sat was to look upon like a jasper,.... For the brightness, glory, and majesty of his countenance, and for his being light itself, clothed with it, and dwelling in it."
2. "The rainbow is a reverberation, or a reflection of the beams of the sun upon a thin watery cloud; and the covenant of grace is owing to Jesus Christ, the sun of righteousness; it is he that has formed it, and filled it with blessings and promises; he is the Mediator, surety, and messenger of it"
3. "The emerald is very bright, clear, and transparent; it is reported of Nero that he could see in his emerald the combat of the gladiators in the theatre; in the covenant of grace, as in a glass, may be seen the glory of all the three Persons in the Godhead, for it is ordered in all things for the glory of each Person."

### John Wesley:
1. "Shone with a visible lustre, like that of sparkling precious stones." 
2. "The jasper, which is transparent and of a glittering white, with an intermixture of beautiful colours, may be a symbol of God's purity, with various other perfections." 
3. "The sardine stone, of a blood-red colour, may be an emblem of his justice, and the vengeance he was about to execute on his enemies."
