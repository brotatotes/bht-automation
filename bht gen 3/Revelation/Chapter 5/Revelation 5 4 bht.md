# Revelation 5:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
John's abundant tears in the book of Revelation reveal his earnest desire to comprehend the revelations of the future that he had been promised. His weeping acts as a representation of the human longing to penetrate the mysteries of what is to come, accompanied by the sorrow that arises when such efforts fail. Despite his tears, John recognizes that there is one who possesses the authority and wisdom to open the sealed book. Thus, his tears serve as a reminder for us to approach the book with humility and a teachable spirit to truly fathom its profound teachings.

1. "One oldest manuscript, ORIGEN, CYPRIAN, and HILARY omit the clause."
2. "John having been promised a revelation of 'things which must be hereafter,' weeps now at his earnest desire being apparently frustrated."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The tears of the apostle here may be regarded as an illustration of two things which are occurring constantly in the minds of people: The strong desire to penetrate the future [...] and The weeping of the apostle may be regarded as an instance of the deep grief which people often experience when all efforts to penetrate the future fail."
2. "Often is the soul overpowered with grief, and often are the eyes filled with sadness at the reflection that there is an absolute limit to the human powers; that all that man can arrive at by his own efforts is uncertain conjecture, and that there is no way possible by which he can make nature speak out and disclose what is to come."
3. "Nowhere does man find himself more fettered and limited in his powers than here; nowhere does he feel that there is such an intense disproportion between his desires and his attainments."
