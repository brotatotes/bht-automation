# Revelation 6:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The red horse represents the taking away of peace from the earth and the reign of the sword, reflecting the growing and conquering power of Christ. It symbolizes the slaughtering of one another and the perversion of the Gospel, leading to bloodshed. It is not limited to any specific time or persecution; rather, it signifies the nature of war and its impact on humanity. The red horse is an emblem of suffering, contentions, and bloody wars, where people kill one another. Its color signifies bloodshed, as indicated by the great sword that strikes both near and far.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "The colour of the horse in each case has reference to the employment of the rider."
2. "It represents to us the taking away of peace from the earth, the slaying one another, the reign of the sword, as one of the destined concomitants of the growing and conquering power of Christ."
3. "Observe, all limitations of this meaning are wrong; whether to the persecutions of the Christians, or to any period of time, ancient or modern."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The color of the horse in each case answers to the mission of the rider." 
2. "The white horse of Christ's bloodless victories is soon followed, through man's perversion of the Gospel, by the red horse of bloodshed." 
3. "The saints amidst judgments on the earth in patience 'endure to the end.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "This symbol cannot be mistaken. As the white horse denoted prosperity, triumph, and happiness, so this would denote carnage, discord, bloodshed."
2. "The characteristics of the period referred to by this are: (a) That peace and tranquility existed before; (b) That such peace and tranquility were now taken away, and were succeeded by confusion and bloodshed; and,
(c) That the particular form of that confusion was civil discord, producing mutual slaughter: 'that they should kill one another.'"
3. "Does any portion of the history of the world present a similar period of connected history that would be so striking a fulfillment of the symbols used here of 'peace being taken from the earth,' and 'men killing one another?'"
