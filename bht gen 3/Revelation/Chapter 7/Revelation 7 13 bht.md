### Henry Alford:
1. "And one of the elders answered (on this use of the word answered see Matt. xi. 25, Deut. xxv. 9. The reply is made, not to words actually uttered, but to thoughts, or to circumstances requiring remark), saying to me..."
2. "the elders symbolizing the Church, one of them fitly stands out as the interpreter of this vision in which the glorified Church is represented"
3. "These that are clothed in the white robes, who are they, and whence came they (the questions are those ordinarily put when we seek for information respecting strangers; but put here for the sake of furnishing the explanation. Both inquiries are answered in ver. 14)?"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "One of the twenty-four elders, representing the Old and New Testament ministry, appropriately acts as interpreter of this vision of the glorified Church." 
2. "These which are arrayed in white robes, WHO are they?"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "That is, as there understood, one of the representatives of the church before the throne."
2. "The word 'answer,' with us, means 'to reply to something which has been said.' In the Bible, however, the word is not infrequently used in the beginning of a speech..."
3. "All those who are before the throne are represented as clothed in white robes, but the eye might be directed to a particular part of them as grouped together, and as having something special in their position or appearance."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Answered. In the sense of taking up speech in connection with some given occasion." 
2. "These which are arrayed in the white robes, who are they?" 
3. "For what, render who, as Rev."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The question is not asked by the Seer. It is addressed to him in order that his attention may be drawn to it with greater force." 
2. "In chap, vi the four living creatures spoke, because they represented creation, and were the instruments of vengeance."
3. "Now one of the elders speaks, because the elders represent the triumphant Church."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "An elder intervenes, though no question has been asked to interpret the vision."
2. "Prophetic predicate nominative put before 'who are they.'"
3. "Both 'who' and 'whence' as in Joshua 9:8."

### John Gill:
1. "This he said, not as being ignorant of them, or of the reason of their being clothed in this manner, nor of the place and state from whence they came, as appears by the account afterwards given of them by him."
2. "to stir up John to take more notice of them, as being a body of men that were worthy of observation and contemplation."
3. "to bring him to a confession of his ignorance; and that he might have an opportunity of giving him some hints about them, which might be useful to him, and to the churches, and for the explanation of this vision, and other parts of this prophecy."

### John Wesley:

2. "He answered St. John's desire to know..."
3. "Revelation 7:13-17 might have immediately followed the tenth verse..."
