# Revelation 7:5 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The twelve thousand sealed ones of Israel form the nucleus of transfigured humanity, joined by the elect Gentiles as a multitude which no man could number. Both groups, while in heaven, have a role in ruling over the earth as ministers of blessing. The twelve apostles stand at the head of the congregation, with the upper and lower assembly distinct but intimately associated. Judah is mentioned first, as Christ's tribe, signifying the need to praise Him for temporal, spiritual, and eternal blessings. The mention of Reuben and Gad further highlights the significance of righteousness, life, and victory through Christ.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The twelve times twelve thousand sealed ones of Israel are the nucleus of transfigured humanity, to which the elect Gentiles are joined, 'a multitude which no man could number.'" 
2. "Both are in heaven, yet ruling over the earth, as ministers of blessing to its inhabitants."
3. "The twelve apostles stand at the head of the whole. The upper and the lower congregation, though distinct, are intimately associated."

### John Gill:
1. "Judah is mentioned first, because Christ sprung from that tribe...and shows, that it becomes all the sealed ones...to praise him for all favours and blessings, temporal, spiritual, and eternal."
2. "Reuben was Jacob's firstborn, but by his sin he lost the honour and privilege of birthright...and shows that the Son of God is to be looked unto for righteousness, life, and salvation."
3. "Gad...may denote that there would be a numerous company of saints and faithful witnesses...who in the issue would be conquerors, and more than conquerors, through Christ."

### John Wesley:
1. Judah is mentioned first, in respect of the kingdom, and of the Messiah sprung therefrom.
