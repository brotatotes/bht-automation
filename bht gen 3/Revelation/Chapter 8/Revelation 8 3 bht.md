# Revelation 8:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Christ, the true High Priest and Mediator, stands before the golden altar of incense, offering up the prayers of His people. The saints' prayers and the angel's incensing occur simultaneously, ascending together. The angel with the golden censer adds incense to the prayers, creating a memorial of the trials faced by God's people. Christ as the Angel of the Covenant intercedes perpetually, drawing from His suffering and death, and the prayers of the saints are likened to sweet incense, pleasing to God.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "He, in Revelation, is always designated by one of His proper titles; though, doubtless, He is the only true High Priest, the Angel of the Covenant, standing before the golden altar of incense, and there, as Mediator, offering up His people's prayers, rendered acceptable before God through the incense of His merit."
2. "Christ alone is the Mediator through whom, and to whom, prayer is to be offered."
3. "The saints' praying on earth, and the angel's incensing in heaven, are simultaneous."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The design seems to be, to represent the prayers of the saints as ascending in the anticipation of the approaching series of wonders in the world - and there would be a beautiful propriety in representing them as offered by an angel, feeling deep interest in the church, and ministering in behalf of the saints."
2. "The firepan, made for the purpose of carrying fire, on which to burn incense in time of worship... There seems reason to suppose that the incense that was offered in the ancient worship was designed to be emblematic of the prayers of saints."
3. "The idea seems to be, that, at this time, all the saints would unite in calling on God, and in deprecating his wrath. As the events which were about to occur were a matter of common interest to the people of God, it was to be supposed that they would unite in common supplication."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The best texts read qusiasthriou, which justifies the Rev., 'over the altar.'"
2. "There seems to be no reason for changing the reference from the brazen altar..."
3. "So that the prayers and the incense might ascend together."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "He is distinctly pointed to as the Mediator of the prayers of the saints, and to Him all judgment is committed."
2. "Beside this altar then the angel appears standing with a golden censer. Much incense is given him that he should add it unto the prayers of all the saints, so that the prayers and the incense might ascend together, a memorial before God of the trials and sufferings of His people."
3. "These prayers are obviously those of the suffering Church; and they are offered, not that she may be prepared to meet the coming judgments, but that she may hasten them."
