# Revelation 9:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Smoke rose from the pit like that of a great furnace, causing the sun to be darkened. The air was filled with the smoke, resulting in a deep darkness. This imagery depicts the release of a plague of demonic locusts, symbolizing the hidden things of darkness. The thick and black smoke obscured the sun, representing Satan's kingdom, while Christ's kingdom is associated with light. The increasing darkness caused by the thickening smoke mirrors the intensifying darkness experienced by the Jews in Persia.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "there went up smoke from the pit as smoke of a great furnace"
2. "the sun was darkened"
3. "the air by reason of the smoke of the pit"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The common image of the place of punishment, in the Scriptures, is that of a 'lake that burns with fire and brimstone.'"
2. "The purpose of this is, probably, to indicate the origin of the plague that was about to come upon the world."
3. "The meaning here is, that an effect would be produced as if a dense and dark vapor should ascend from the under-world."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. Smoke of a great furnace. Compare Genesis 19:28; Exodus 19:18; Matthew 13:42, Matthew 13:50.


### Philip Schaff:
1. "The smoke must be thought of as so thick and black that the sun was shrouded from view and the whole air darkened."
2. "Darkness is the note of Satan's kingdom as light is of Christ's."
3. "Satan and his angels are called 'the world-rulers of this darkness'."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "As the smoke of a great furnace." 
2. "The plague of demonic locusts is here turned loose."
3. "Were darkened... by reason of."

### John Gill:
1. "And he opened the bottomless pit,.... With the key that was given him; he made use of his universal power over all bishops and churches, enacted laws, issued out decrees, made articles of faith, and imposed them on men's consciences, and obliged all to submit to his hellish principles and practices..."
2. "and smoke being a dark thin vapour, and very troublesome to the eyes and nose, and of a perishing nature, which soon vanishes away, these are fitly expressed by it; for they are the hidden things of darkness, and the authors and abettors of them are such who darken counsel by words without knowledge; they are empty things, have no solidity and substance in them..."
3. "Christ, the sun of righteousness, was greatly obscured by the Romish antichrist, by his false doctrine and worship, in his offices, merits, and grace, he taking upon him to be head of the church, the infallible interpreter of Scripture, and to give out pardons and indulgences..."

### John Wesley:
1. "The locusts, who afterwards rise out of it, seem to be, as we shall afterwards see, the Persians; agreeable to which, this smoke is their detestable idolatrous doctrine, and false zeal for it, which now broke out in an uncommon paroxysm."
2. "Where the clouds of it rise thicker and thicker, spread far and wide, and press one upon another, so that the darkness increases continually."
3. "This smoke occasioned more and more such darkness over the Jews in Persia."
