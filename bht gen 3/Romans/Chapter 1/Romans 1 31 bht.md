### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Gave them over—or 'up'."
2. "To do those things which are not convenient."
3. "In the old sense of that word, that is, 'not becoming,' 'indecorous,' 'shameful'."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "And yet the apostle charges on the pagan generally the lack of this affection."
2. "The Roman father had an absolute right over the life of his child, and we have abundant proof that that right was often exercised."
3. "Pure religion, only, opens the heart to the appeals of want; and nothing but Christianity has yet expanded the hearts of people to make public provisions for the poor, the ignorant, and the afflicted."

### John Calvin:
1. "Without the feelings of humanity are they who have put off the first affections of nature towards their own relations."
2. "As he mentions the want of mercy as an evidence of human nature being depraved."
3. "Mercy is a Christian virtue."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "Without understanding; the same word as ‘senseless’ (E. V. ‘foolish’), Romans 1:21."
2. "Covenant breakers. In the original there is another play upon the sound of the words."
3. "Unmerciful. This concludes the list, marking the absence of the least principle of moral action."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Covenant-breakers (ασυνθετους). Another paronomasia or pun. Α privative and verbal συνθετος from συντιθημ, to put together. Old word, common in LXX (Jeremiah 3:7), men 'false to their engagements' (Sanday and Headlam), who treat covenants as 'a scrap of paper.'"
2. "Without natural affection (αστοργους). Late word, α privative and στοργη, love of kindred. In N.T. only here and 2 Timothy 3:3."
3. "Unmerciful (ανελεημονας). From α privative and ελεημων, merciful. Late word, only here in N.T. Some MSS. add ασπονδους, implacable, from 2 Timothy 3:3. It is a terrible picture of the effects of sin on the lives of men and women."

### John Gill:
1. "Without understanding,.... Of God, of his nature and worship, of things divine and even moral, being given up to a reprobate mind."
2. "Covenant breakers; had no regard to private or public contracts."
3. "Without natural affection; to their parents, children, relations and friends."

### John Wesley:
1. "Covenant-breakers — It is well known, the Romans, as a nation, from the very beginning of their commonwealth, never made any scruple of vacating altogether the most solemn engagement, if they did not like it..." (Romans 1:31)
2. "Without natural affection — The custom of exposing their own new-born children to perish by cold, hunger, or wild beasts...was an amazing instance of this..." (Romans 1:31)
3. "...as is also that of killing their aged and helpless parents, now common among the American heathens." (Romans 1:31)
