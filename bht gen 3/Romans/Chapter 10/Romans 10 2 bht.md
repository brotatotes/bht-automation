# Romans 10:2 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The people mentioned here have a sincere zeal for God, but their passion is not based on accurate knowledge. Their well-meaning enthusiasm leads them to seek God through outward rituals and rules, rather than truly knowing Him. Despite their spiritual blindness, there is still hope for them, as their zealousness may indicate an openness to the truth. However, it is important to understand that zeal without proper understanding can lead to misguided actions and a failure to grasp the true nature of God and His purposes.

### Henry Alford:
1. "I bear witness to them that they have a zeal for God."
2. "Not according to knowledge."
3. "Accurate apprehension of the way of righteousness as revealed to them."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "For I bear them record...that they have a zeal of God, but not according to knowledge." 
2. "He alludes to this well-meaning of his people, notwithstanding their spiritual blindness, not certainly to excuse their rejection of Christ and rage against His saints, but as some ground of hope regarding them." 
3. "He alludes to this well-meaning of his people, notwithstanding their spiritual blindness, not certainly to excuse their rejection of Christ and rage against His saints, but as some ground of hope regarding them."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Zeal for God here means passionate ardor in the things pertaining to God, or in the things of religion."
2. "A minister of the gospel should not be blind to the amiable qualities of people or to their zeal; and should be willing to speak of it tenderly, even when he is proclaiming the doctrine of depravity, or denouncing the just judgments of God."
3. "It is the union of the two - the action of the man called forth to intense effort by just views of truth and by right feeling - that constitutes true religion."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "He seems to be alluding to his conduct of former days, and to say, 'I know something of it, of that zeal.'" (Godet).
2. "Zeal for God." (Rev., zeal for God).
3. "Full or correct and vital knowledge." (See on ch. Romans 1:28; Romans 3:20).

### John Calvin:
1. "Let us hence learn where our good intentions may guide us, if we yield to them."
2. "Away then with these vain evasions as to good intention; if we seek God sincerely, let us follow the way by which alone we can come to him."
3. "And further, since we see that they perish, who with good intention wander in darkness, let us bear in mind, that we are worthy of thousand deaths, if after having been illuminated by God, we wander knowingly and willfully from the right way."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "They have a seal for God, i.e., of which God is the object, not great zeal, or, godly zeal. Their zeal was religious, conscientious, but misdirected."
2. "But not according to knowledge. The word often means full knowledge, and is here used to denote correct, vital knowledge."
3. "Answering to the objective advantages of the Jews (chap. Romans 9:1-5) was this religious zeal, which degenerated into blind fanaticism. But this, we infer from the passage, is better than indifferentism."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "A zeal for God."
2. "They sought God in an external way by rules and rites and missed him."
3. "They became zealous for the letter and the form instead of for God himself."

### John Gill:
1. "They had a zeal of God, but not according to knowledge: it was not well regulated, it proceeded on mistaken principles, and moved in a wrong way." 
2. "They did not know God in Christ, nor Jesus to be the true Messiah; they understood neither law nor Gospel truly, and fancied the Gospel was contrary to the law, and an enemy to it."
3. "They were zealous of the law, and of doing the commands of it, but knew not the true nature, use, and end of the law."
