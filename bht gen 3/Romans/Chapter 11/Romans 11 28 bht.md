The unbelieving Jews, regarded and treated as enemies for the benefit of the Gentiles, are still beloved for the fathers' sakes and the covenant with Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob. The grace of the divine calling cannot be made void, and their rejection of the gospel becomes an opportunity for its preaching to the Gentiles. ### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "They are regarded and treated as enemies for the benefit of you Gentiles." 
2. "They are beloved even in their state of exclusion for the fathers' sakes."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The enemies of God denote all who are not his true friends."
2. "Their rejection has become the occasion by which the gospel has been preached to you."
3. "Yet this should not be abused by others to lead them to security in sin. No man has any security of happiness, and of the favor of God, but he who complies with the terms of his mercy."

### John Calvin:
1. "He then admits, that they were for the present alienated from God on account of the gospel, that thus the salvation, which at first was deposited with them, might come to the Gentiles."
2. "God was not unmindful of the covenant which he had made with their fathers, and by which he testified that according to his eternal purpose he loved that nation."
3. "The grace of the divine calling cannot be made void; for this is the import of the words."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "As touching the gospel...they (the unbelieving Jews, at that time including the mass of the nation) are enemies."
2. "For your sakes...Israel was the chosen nation."
3. "Beloved, i.e., of God, for the fathers' sakes...they are still regarded with peculiar favor."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "According to the gospel" - Paul has shown...the gospel order as it has developed.
2. "Enemies" - Treated as enemies (of God)...
3. "For the fathers' sake" - As in Romans 9:4; Romans 11:16.

### John Gill:
1. "They [were] enemies to God and Christ, to the Gospel, and the ministers of it, and particularly to the apostle."
2. "The present enmity of the Jews to the Gospel turned to the advantage of the Gentiles."
3. "As many of them as belong to the election of grace, are beloved of God; and will appear to be so when they are called by grace."
