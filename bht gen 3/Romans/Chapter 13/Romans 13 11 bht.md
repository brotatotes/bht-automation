# Romans 13:11 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Awake from worldly carelessness and indifference to sin, for the Lord's coming is rapidly approaching. The time has come to cast off the deeds of darkness and embrace the day of salvation. Salvation is nearer to us now than when we first believed, and it is high time to rise from the sleep of fatal indifference. Cast aside the works of darkness and walk in the light, for it is in this light that we must engage in the spiritual battle. Salvation is advancing, steadily nearing its consummation in heaven.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Sleep here imports the state of worldly carelessness and indifference to sin, which allows and practises the works of darkness."
2. "Without denying the legitimacy of an individual application of this truth, and the importance of its consideration for all Christians of all ages, a fair exposition of this passage can hardly fail to recognize the fact, that the Apostle here as well as elsewhere speaks of the coming of the Lord as rapidly approaching."
3. "While the prophetic declarations of the events of that time remain to instruct us, the eager expectation of the time, which they expressed in their day, has also remained, a token of the true frame of mind in which each succeeding age should contemplate the ever-approaching coming of the Lord."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "And that [do] knowing the time, that now it is high time - literally, 'the hour has already come.'"
2. "To awake out of sleep - of stupid, fatal indifference to eternal things."
3. "For now is our salvation - rather, 'the salvation,' or simply 'salvation.'"

### Albert Barnes:
1. "A deep conviction of the nearness of eternity will prompt to an upright life in the contact of man with man."
2. "It is 'time,' therefore, for people to cast off the deeds of darkness, and rise to life, and purity, and action."
3. "In prospect of that state, we ought to lay aside every sin, and live more and more in preparation for a world of light and glory."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "And that knowing the time - now."
2. "Salvation is nearer to us."
3. "The reference is apparently to the Lord's second coming, rather than to future glory."

### John Calvin:
1. "Since we know that the seasonable time has already come, in which we should awake from sleep, let us cast aside whatever belongs to the night, let us shake off all the works of darkness, since the darkness itself has been dissipated, and let us attend to the works of light, and walk as it becomes those who are enjoying the day."
2. "[The] armor of light represents good, and temperate, and holy actions, such as are suitable to the day; and armor is mentioned rather than works, because we are to carry on a warfare for the Lord."
3. "Nearer is salvation now to us than at that time when we began to believe: so that a reference is made to the time which had preceded as to their faith."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "It is already time that you should awake out of sleep." 
2. "For now is salvation nearer to us than whom we first believed."
3. "The Apostle taught the truth, as well as rebuked error."
