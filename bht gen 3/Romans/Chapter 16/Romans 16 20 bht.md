## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Seeing that he had mentioned those who caused divisions and offences, he says the God of peace that he might encourage them to expect liberation from these persons."
2. "Shall bruise Satan is a similitude from Gen. iii. 15. It does not express any wish, but a prophetic assurance, and encouragement in bearing up against all adversaries, that it would not be long before the great Adversary himself would be bruised under their feet."
3. "It appears as if the Epistle was intended to conclude with this usual benediction, but the Apostle found occasion to add more."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The apostle encourages the Romans to persevere in resisting the wiles of the devil with the assurance that, as good soldiers of Jesus Christ, they are 'shortly' to receive their discharge, and have the satisfaction of 'putting their feet upon the neck' of that formidable enemy."
2. "This sublime appellation of God has here a wider sense, pointing to the whole 'purpose for which the Son of God was manifested, to destroy the works of the devil.'"
3. "What comes after this, where one would have expected the epistle to close, has its parallel in Genesis 3:15, and being in fact common in epistolary writings, is simply a mark of genuineness."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The God of peace - The God who promotes peace..."

3. "The grace - The favor; the mercy, etc. The Lord Jesus is the Prince of peace...that the design of his coming might be accomplished in promoting the prevalence of order and peace."
