# Romans 4:21 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Abraham's unwavering faith in God's promise brought glory to Him, as he was fully persuaded and convinced of God's ability to fulfill what He had promised. Regardless of the difficulties he faced, his faith remained steadfast, rooted in his complete assurance and full conviction of God's mighty power. Abraham's faith was not dependent on his own righteousness or worthiness, but solely on God's power, for whom nothing is impossible. His unwavering confidence in God's word is a powerful example for us to trust in God's grace and honor Him.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The glory which Abraham's faith gave to God consisted in this..."
2. "...firm in the persuasion of God's ability to fulfill his promise..."
3. "...no difficulties shook him."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "And being fully persuaded - Thoroughly or entirely convinced."
2. "He was able - Compare Genesis 18:14."
3. "His faith was equally implicit and strong when he was commanded to sacrifice his promised son; Hebrews 11:19."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Being fully persuaded [πληροφορηθεις]. The primary idea is, being filled with a thought or conviction."
2. "Able [δυνατος]. The sense is stronger: mighty."
3. "The sense is stronger: mighty; compare Luke 1:49; Luke 24:19; Acts 18:24; 2 Corinthians 10:4; Revelation 6:15."

### John Calvin:
1. "There is in deed no obstacle, however small and insignificant, by which the flesh imagines the hand of God is restrained from working."
2. "That it may obtain from us its right and its honor, when a contest comes, we ought to determine thus, — That it is no less sufficient to overcome the obstacles of the world, than the strong rays of the sun are to dissipate the mists."
3. "Faith then ought not to regard our weakness, misery, and defects, but to fix wholly its attention on the power of God alone; for if it depends on our righteousness or worthiness, it can never ascend to the consideration of God’s power."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "This simple confidence in God’s promise gave glory to God, and is the essence of faith." 
2. "Confidence in God’s word, a full persuasion that He can do what seems to us impossible, is as necessary in the one case as in the other."
3. "The sinner honors God, in trusting His grace, as much as Abraham did in trusting His power."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Being fully assured (πληροφορηθεις). First aorist passive participle of πληροφορεω, from πληροφορος and this from πληρης and φερω, to bear or bring full (full measure), to settle fully."
2. "What he had promised (ο επηγγελτα). Perfect middle indicative of επαγγελλομα, to promise, retained in indirect discourse according to usual Greek idiom."
3. "He was able (δυνατος εστιν). Present active indicative retained in indirect discourse. The verbal adjective δυνατος with εστιν is here used in sense of the verb δυνατα."

### John Gill:
1. "He had not only faith, a strong faith, but full assurance of faith."
2. "Though it was so long ago, and there were so many difficulties in the way."
3. "His faith rested upon the power of God, with whom nothing is impossible."
