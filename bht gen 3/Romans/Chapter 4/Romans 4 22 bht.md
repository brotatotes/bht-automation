# Romans 4:22 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Abraham's righteousness was not earned through his own merit, but solely because of his unwavering and unconditional faith in God and His promise. It is vital to understand that this righteousness was imputed to him, not because of any works or obedience, but simply because he believed. His faith was a demonstration of his friendship and deep attachment to God, showing his complete trust in His power and faithfulness. This emphasizes the importance of a genuine and steadfast faith, as it is through faith alone that one is justified.

### Albert Barnes:
1. "His faith was so implicit, and so unwavering, that it was a demonstration that he was the firm friend of God."
2. "He had such confidence in God that he showed that he was supremely attached to him, and would obey and serve him."
3. "This was reckoned as a full proof of friendship; and he was recognized and treated as righteous; that is, as the friend of God."

### John Calvin:
1. "Faith can bring us nothing more than what it receives from the word."
2. "He does not become immediately just, who is imbued only with a general and confused idea that God is true, except he reposes on the promise of his favor."
3. "It is most true, as Paul says to the Romans, that by faith Abraham was justified, and not by obedience: but it is just as true what he says to the Hebrews, that it was by faith that Abraham obeyed."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The whole discussion is here summed up, the last clause of Romans 4:3 being repeated."
2. "The immediate connection is with Romans 4:18-21."
3. "Because Abraham had believed God in the way there described."

### John Gill:
1. "Not because his faith was strong, and he had a full assurance of it, but because it was right, resting on the promise of God, and relying upon his power and faithfulness to perform it."
2. "Though the righteousness of faith is not imputed to any sort of believers, not to mere nominal ones, yet to all such as have true faith, though it may be but weak."
3. "For faith, as to nature, kind, and object, though not as to degree, is the same in all true believers, and the same righteousness is imputed to one as to another."
