# Romans 6:9 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Resurrection-life of Christ Himself, which believers are meant to have, is expounded upon in this passage. When Jesus submitted to Death, He surrendered Himself to its power, but Death could not hold Him and had no power over Him beyond His own sufferance. Christ's voluntary surrender to death dissolved its dominion over us, and now death has no dominion over Him anymore. His resurrection is the pledge of His enduring life, refuting the sacrificial character of the mass and emphasizing the transitoriness of death's dominion.

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "Christ being raised from the dead dieth no more; death hath no more dominion over him." 
2. "Though Christ's death was in the most absolute sense a voluntary act, that voluntary surrender gave death such rightful 'dominion over Him' as dissolved its dominion over us."
3. "But this once past, 'death hath,' even in that sense, 'dominion over Him no more.'"

### John Calvin:
1. "He, therefore, by submitting to its dominion, as it were, for a moment, destroyed it for ever."
2. "The meaning is, that Christ, who now vivifies the faithful by his Spirit, or breathes his own life into them by his secret power from heaven."
3. "He arose, that by virtue of the same dominion he might render free all his people."
