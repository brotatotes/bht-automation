# Romans 8:1 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Through incorporation and union with Christ Jesus, believers are freed from the power of sin and death, experiencing no condemnation. This union is a union in life, as believers have one life with Christ, just as the head and the members of a body have one life. With the indwelling of Christ's Spirit in them, believers can confidently lead a consecrated and crucified life. Through Jesus Christ, there is freedom from condemnation and the capability to live a life dedicated to God.

### Henry Alford:
1. "There is therefore... no condemnation to them which are in Christ Jesus."
2. "This... setting forth the fact of the deliverance."
3. "The expression 'in Christ Jesus'... incorporation into and union with Him."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The scope...is to show how 'the law of sin and death' is deprived of its power to bring believers again into bondage, and how the holy law of God receives in them the homage of a living obedience." 
2. "As Christ...was, to all legal effects, 'made sin for us,' so are we...to all legal effects, 'made the righteousness of God in Him' and thus, one with Him in the divine reckoning. there is to such 'NO CONDEMNATION.'"
3. "But this is no mere legal arrangement: it is a union in life; believers, through the indwelling of Christ's Spirit in them, having one life with Him, as truly as the head and the members of the same body have one life."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The function of the law is to condemn. The one never affords deliverance, but always condemns; the object of the other is to free from condemnation, and to set the soul at liberty."
2. "There is no final condemnation under the gospel. The function, design, and tendency of the gospel is to free from the condemning sentence of law."
3. "If a man has these fruits of the Spirit, he is a Christian; if not, he is a stranger to religion, whatever else he may possess."
