# Romans 8:21 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The creation itself will be delivered into freedom from debility and decay, sharing in the glory of the children of God. This deliverance is not a mere release from bondage, but a transformation into a glorious state. The hope of creation is tied to its unmerited suffering, with a presentiment of its future deliverance. It anticipates being freed from the bondage of corruption and entering into the liberty of the children of God's glory.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The creation itself shall, in a glorious sense, be delivered into that freedom from debility and decay in which the children of God, when raised up in glory, shall expatiate."
2. "Into this freedom from corruptibility the creation itself shall, in a glorious sense, be delivered."
3. "The creation itself shall be delivered from the bondage of corruption into the liberty of the glory of the children of God."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "The hope is, that this same renewed man shall be delivered from all the toils, and cares, and sins of this state."
2. "The identical person shall be admitted to heaven without essential change."
3. "It is freedom from sin; from corruption; from evil desires; from calamity; from death."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "The creation was subjected in the hope that," etc. - The hope is that of the subjected, not of the subjector.
2. "Nature 'possesses in the feeling of her unmerited suffering, a sort of presentiment of her future deliverance'" - The creation has a presentiment of its future deliverance.
3. "Liberty is one of the elements of the glorious state and is dependent upon it." - Liberty is dependent upon the glorious state.

### John Calvin:
1. "It is then indeed meet for us to consider what a dreadful curse we have deserved, since all created things...undergo punishment for our sins."
2. "Thus the condemnation of mankind is imprinted on the heavens, and on the earth, and on all creatures."
3. "But he means not that all creatures shall be partakers of the same glory with the sons of God; but that they, according to their nature, shall be participators of a better condition."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The phrase 'the creation itself' is repeated in contrast with 'children of God.'"
2. "The bondage which consists in corruption."
3. "Not only delivered from bondage, but transferred into this freedom, which consists in, or at least results from, a share in the glory of the children of God."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "The hope of creation, not of the Creator."
2. "Nature possess in the feeling of her unmerited suffering a sort of presentiment of her future deliverance."
3. "The creation itself... a sort of presentiment of her future deliverance."

### John Gill:
1. "The earnest expectation of the creature waiteth for the manifestation of the sons of God, in hope that the creature itself also shall be delivered from the bondage of corruption."
2. "The Gentiles earnestly wait and expect a larger number of converts among them, in hopes that ere long the whole Gentile world will be freed from the bondage of corruption."
3. "They hope for a deliverance from hence, into the glorious liberty of the children of God."

### John Wesley:
1. "Destruction is not deliverance: therefore whatsoever is destroyed, or ceases to be, is not delivered at all."
2. "Will, then, any part of the creation be destroyed?"
3. "Into the glorious liberty - The excellent state wherein they were created."
