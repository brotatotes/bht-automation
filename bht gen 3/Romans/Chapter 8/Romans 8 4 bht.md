# Romans 8:4 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
Through the sanctification of our redemption, the aim of God in giving the Law is fulfilled in us, accomplished by His grace. It is not our own work, but that of God's by His Grace, that allows us to walk in the Spirit and not according to the flesh, thus fulfilling the righteousness of the law. The Spirit, renewing our mind and actuated by the Holy Ghost, guides us to confess, know God's love, and experience peace and joy in faith and hope.

### Henry Alford:
1. "the aim of God in giving the Law might be accomplished in us, in our sanctification, which is the ultimate end of our redemption"
2. "The passive is used, to shew that the work is not ours, but that of God by His Grace"
3. "who, notwithstanding that we are bound up with a flesh of sin, do not walk in our daily life according to, or led by, the law of sin which is in our members, but according to and led by the law of the Spirit of life in Christ Jesus"
