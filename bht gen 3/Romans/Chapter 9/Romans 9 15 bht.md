# Romans 9:15 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
God's mercy is pure and without any consideration of human desert. It is solely based on His will, and He has the right to choose whom He will have mercy on. There is no unrighteousness in God's selection, as He Himself claims the right to do so. The expression of God's mercy is not just a sentiment, but a determination to show compassion to those in need. His mercy is an act of free grace, unaffected by any human merit or service.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "Whenever I have mercy on any, it shall be pure mercy, no human desert contributing."
2. "God's mercy be pure mercy without any desert on man's part."
3. "His will being the only assignable cause of the selection."

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "There can be no unrighteousness in God's choosing whom He will, for to Moses He expressly claims the right to do so."
2. "Yet it is worthy of notice that this is expressed in the positive rather than the negative form: not, 'I will have mercy on none but whom I will'; but, 'I will have mercy on whomsoever I will.'"
3. "I will have mercy on whom I have mercy, and I will have compassion on whom I have compassion."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "When people, therefore, under the influence of an unrenewed and hostile heart, charge this as an unjust and arbitrary proceeding, they are resisting and perverting what God regards as the very demonstration of his benevolence."
2. "He has a right to pardon whom he pleases, and to save people on his own terms, and according to his sovereign will and pleasure."
3. "It should be a matter of joy that any can be pardoned and saved; and not a subject of murmuring and complaint that those who shall finally deserve to die shall be consigned to woe."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Have mercy therefore contemplates, not merely the sentiment in itself, but the determination of those who should be its objects."
2. "The former verb emphasizes the sense of human wretchedness in its active manifestation; the latter the inward feeling expressing itself in sighs and tears."
3. "God asserts that His gift is of His own free grace, without any recognition of Moses ' right to claim it on the ground of merit or service."

### John Calvin:
1. "With regard to the elect, God cannot be charged with any unrighteousness; for according to his good pleasure he favors them with mercy."
2. "The petulancy of men quarrels with God, as though he deferred to persons more than what is right."
3. "The mercy of God, being gratuitous, is under no restraint, but turns wherever it pleases."

### Philip Schaff:
1. "If God accorded it to him, it was not because it was Moses who besought Him, or had any right to it; it was pure grace on His part."
2. "The word 'whom' in both instances might be rendered 'whomsoever,' and has an emphasis here, describing not merely the mercy, but the choice of the individual objects, as the free act of God."
3. "Have compassion is stronger than 'have mercy;' it ordinarily includes outward manifestations of compassion."
