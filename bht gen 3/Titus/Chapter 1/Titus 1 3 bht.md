# Titus 1:3 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
God's word is made manifest in its own seasons through the proclamation entrusted to the speaker, in accordance with the command of our Savior God. This proclamation unveils the hidden promise, which lay in God's eternal purpose, through the preaching of the Gospel. The times and the work being carried out are under God's control, ensuring that everything is done in the proper order and at the most opportune moment.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Henry Alford:
1. "made manifest in its own seasons His word"
2. "the proclamation, with which I was entrusted"
3. "according to the command of our Saviour God"

### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "The 'promise' had lain hidden in His eternal purpose heretofore."
2. "His word—equivalent to 'eternal life.'"
3. "In the (Gospel) proclamation (the thing preached, the Gospel) with which I was entrusted."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "But hath in due times - At the proper time; the time which he had intended; the best time." 
2. "Manifested his word through preaching - ...he has made known his eternal purpose through the preaching of the gospel."
3. "According to the commandment of God our Saviour - Paul always claimed to be divinely commissioned, and affirmed that he was engaged in the work of preaching by the authority of God."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "In due times [καιροις ιδιοις] . Better, in his (or its) own seasons."
2. "Through preaching [εν κηρυγματι] . Rather, in a proclamation."
3. "Which is committed unto me [ο επιστευθην εγω] . Betters wherewith I was intrusted."

### John Calvin:
1. "He reminds us that the time when it pleased the Lord to do this — must have been the most seasonable time for doing it." 
2. "the 'times' are placed in the hand, and at the disposal, of God, in such a manner that we ought to think that he does everything in the proper order and at the most seasonable time." 
3. "the honor is not due to any man, till he has proved that God has ordained him"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The Gospel is the final revelation of life eternal."
2. "Things promised are still in part concealed; performance alone is full manifestation."
3. "Saviour is applied elsewhere to God only in Luke 1:47 (with Old Testament reference) and Jude 1:25."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "In the message (εν κηρυγματ). See 1 Corinthians 1:21; 1 Corinthians 2:4 for this word, the human proclamation (preaching) of God's word."
2. "Wherewith I was intrusted (ο επιστευθην). Accusative relative ο retained with the first aorist passive indicative of πιστευω as in 1 Timothy 1:11. See 1 Timothy 2:7."
3. "Of God our Saviour (του σωτηρος ημων θεου). In verse Titus 1:4 he applies the words 'του σωτηρος ημων' to Christ. In Titus 2:13 he applies both θεου and σωτηρος to Christ."

### John Gill:
1. "...in which Christ is revealed in the glory of his person, and the fulness of his grace, and in the efficacy of his blood, righteousness, and sacrifice..."
2. "...in which the promise of eternal life, which lay in God's heart, in the covenant of grace, and in the hands of Christ...is clearly made known..."
3. "...who is truly and properly God, the great God, and our Saviour, and who is the only Saviour of lost sinners..."

### John Wesley:
1. "And his own times are fittest for his own work. What creature dares ask, 'Why no sooner?'" 
2. "Manifested his word - Containing that promise, and the whole 'truth which is after godliness.'"
3. "Through the preaching wherewith I am intrusted according to the commandment of God our Saviour - And who dares exercise this office on any less authority?"
