# Titus 2:7 Commentary Help Text

## BHT:
The Christian minister's teaching is useless if their example does not confirm their words. Titus, as a minister, should display uncorrupted integrity and a serious demeanor when presenting the truth. He is called to show himself as an example, living a life of uncorruptness and sincerity. His teaching should be filled with uncorrupted doctrine, while his conduct should reflect this doctrine. Through being a pattern of good works, showing gravity and uncorruptness, Titus will not only teach, but also inspire others to lead pure and upright lives.

## Choicest Commentary Quotes:
### Jamieson-Fausset-Brown:
1. "All teaching is useless unless the teacher's example confirm his word."
2. "As 'gravity' refers to Titus himself, so 'uncorruptness' though, doubtless, uncorruptness of the doctrine will be sure to follow as a consequence of the Christian minister being of simple, uncorrupt integrity himself."
3. "Gravity - dignified seriousness in setting forth the truth."

### Albert Barnes:
1. "Not merely teaching others, but showing them by example how they ought to live."
2. "Everything in his teaching was to be such as to make men purer and better."
3. "The manner in which a preacher delivers his message should be such as to command respect."

### Marvin Vincent:
1. "Shewing thyself [σεαυτον παρεχομενος] . See on 1 Timothy 1:4. The phrase N. T. o. but occurs in Class., as, to show one's self holy or righteous; wise or skillful; parecein to make himself scarce."
2. "Incorruptness [αφθοριαν] . Const. with shewing. N. T. o. o Class. LXX once, Haggai 2:18."
3. "Omit sincerity."

### John Calvin:
1. "For doctrine will otherwise carry little authority, if its power and majesty do not shine in the life of the bishop, as in a mirror."
2. "He wishes, therefore, that the teacher may be a pattern, which his scholars may copy."
3. "In order that he may exhibit a representation of his doctrine in morals, he bids him be 'upright and grave.'"

### Philip Schaff:
1. "The teacher of others should be like a basin which ere it can overflow must first be itself filled from the fountain."
2. "Specially in his public teaching, which is to exhibit a character sincere (‘uncorrupt’) and ‘grave.’"
3. "To this class Titus belonged; therefore he was to be its model as well as preceptor."

### Archibald T. Robertson:
1. "Shewing thyself (σεαυτον παρεχομενος). Present middle (redundant middle) participle of παρεχω with the reflexive pronoun σεαυτον as if the active voice παρεχων." 
2. "An ensample (τυπον). For this word see 2 Thessalonians 3:9; Philippians 3:17." 
3. "Uncorruptness (αφθοριαν). Only example, from late adjective αφθορος (α privative and φθειρω)."

### John Gill:
1. "He should through the whole of his conversation be a pattern of every good work unto them."
2. "In doctrine, showing uncorruptness, gravity, sincerity."
3. "In, or with doctrine, along with the doctrine preached, let the conversation be pure and incorrupt, free from the pollutions of the world."

### John Wesley:
1. "Showing thyself a pattern." 
2. "In the doctrine which thou teachest...uncorruptness."
3. "As to the manner of delivering it, seriousness - Weightiness, solemnity."
