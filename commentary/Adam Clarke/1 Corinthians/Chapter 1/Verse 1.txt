<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:1">1 Corinthians 1:1</span>. <i><b>Paul, called</b></i><b> to be </b><i><b>an apostle</b></i> — Bishop Pearce contends that a <i>comma</i> should be placed after κλητος, <i>called</i>, which should not be joined to αποστολος, <i>apostle</i>: the first signifies being <i>called to</i>, the other <i>sent from</i>. He reads it, therefore, <i>Paul the called the apostle of Jesus Christ</i>. The word κλητος, <i>called</i>, may be here used, as in some other places, for <i>constituted</i>. For this, and the meaning of the word <i>apostle</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:1">Romans 1:1</span>.</p>
<p>As the apostle had many irregularities to reprehend in the Corinthian Church, it was necessary that he should be explicit in stating his <i>authority</i>. He was <i>called</i>-invited to the <i>Gospel feast;</i> had partaken of it, and, by the grace he received, was qualified to proclaim salvation to others: Jesus Christ therefore made him an <i>apostle</i>, that is, gave him a Divine commission to preach the Gospel to the Gentiles.</p>
<p><i><b>Through the will of God</b></i> — By a particular appointment from God alone; for, being an <i>extraordinary</i> messenger, he derived no part of his authority from man.</p>
<p><i><b>Sosthenes</b></i> our <i>brother</i>] Probably the same person mentioned <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:17">Acts 18:17</span>, where see the note.</p>
