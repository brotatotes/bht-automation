<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:11">1 Corinthians 1:11</span>. <i><b>By them</b></i><b> which are of the house </b><i><b>of Chloe</b></i> — This was doubtless some very religious matron at Corinth, whose family were converted to the Lord; some of whom were probably sent to the apostle to inform him of the dissensions which then prevailed in the Church at that place. <i>Stephanas, Fortunatus</i>, and <i>Achaicus</i>, mentioned <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:17">1 Corinthians 16:17</span>, were probably the sons of this <i>Chloe</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>Contentions</b></i> — εριδες, <i>Altercations</i>; produced by the σχισματα <i>divisions</i>, mentioned above. When once they had <i>divided</i>, they must necessarily <i>have contended</i>, in order to support their respective parties.</p>
