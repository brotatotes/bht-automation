<p>Verse 30. <i><b>But of him are ye in Christ Jesus</b></i> — Even the good which you possess is granted by God, for it is by and through him that Christ Jesus comes, and all the blessings of the Gospel dispensation.</p>
<p><i><b>Who of God is made unto us wisdom</b></i> — As being the author of that evangelical wisdom which far excels the wisdom of the philosopher and the scribe, and even that <i>legal constitution</i> which is called the <i>wisdom</i> of the Jews, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="de+4:6">Deuteronomy 4:6</span>.</p>
<p><i><b>And righteousness</b></i> — δικαιοσυνη, <i>Justification</i>, as procuring for us that remission of sins which the law could not give, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:21">Galatians 2:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:21">Galatians 3:21</span>.</p>
<p><i><b>And sanctification</b></i> — As procuring for and working in us, not only an external and relative <i>holiness</i>, as was that of the Jews, but οσιοτητα της αληθειας, <i>true</i> and eternal <i>holiness</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:24">Ephesians 4:24</span>, wrought in us by the Holy Spirit.</p>
<p><i><b>And redemption</b></i> — He is the author of <i>redemption</i>, not from the Egyptian <i>bondage</i>, or Babylonish <i>captivity</i>, but from the <i>servitude</i> <i>of Satan</i>, the <i>dominion of sin</i> and <i>death</i>, and from the <i>bondage</i> <i>of corruption into the glorious liberty of the sons of God</i>, or the <i>redemption of the body</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:21">Romans 8:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:23">Romans 8:23</span>. See <i>Whitby</i>.</p>
<p>The object of the apostle is to show that man of himself possesses no good, that whatever he has comes from God, and from God only through Christ. For the different acceptations of the word <i>righteousness</i> the reader may consult the note on <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:17">Romans 1:17</span>, where the subject is considered in every point of view.</p>
