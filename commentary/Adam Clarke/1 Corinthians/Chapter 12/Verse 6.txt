<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:6">1 Corinthians 12:6</span>. <i><b>Diversities of operations</b></i> — ενεργηματων. Miraculous influences exerted on others; such as the expulsion of demons, inflicting extraordinary punishments, as in the case of Ananias and Sapphira, Elymas the sorcerer, c., the healing of different diseases, raising the dead, &amp;c.: all these proceeded from God the Father, as the <i>fountain</i> of all <i>goodness</i> and <i>power</i>, and the immediate dispenser of every good and perfect gift.</p>
<p>In the three preceding verses we find more than an indirect reference to the doctrine of the sacred Trinity.</p>
<p>GIFTS are attributed to the Holy Spirit, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:4">1 Corinthians 12:4</span>.</p>
<p>ADMINISTRATIONS to the Lord Jesus, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:5">1 Corinthians 12:5</span>.</p>
<p>OPERATIONS to God the Father, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:6">1 Corinthians 12:6</span>.</p>
<p>He who may think this <i>fanciful</i> must account for the very evident <i>distinctions</i> here in some more satisfactory way.</p>
