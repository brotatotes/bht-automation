<p>Verse 35. <i><b>But some</b></i><b> man </b><i><b>will say</b></i> — αλλα ερει τις. It is very likely that the apostle, by τις <i>some, some one, some man</i>, means particularly the <i>false apostle</i>, or teacher at Corinth, who was chief in the opposition to the pure doctrine of the Gospel, and to whom, in this covert way, he often refers.</p>
<p>The <i>second</i> part of the apostle's discourse begins at this verse. What shall be the nature of the resurrection body?</p>
<p>1. The question is <i>stated</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:35">1 Corinthians 15:35</span>.</p>
<p>2. It is <i>answered</i>: first, by a <i>similitude</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:36-38">1 Corinthians 15:36-38</span>;</p>
<p>secondly, by an <i>application</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:33-41">1 Corinthians 15:33-41</span>;</p>
<p>and thirdly, by <i>explication</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:42-50">1 Corinthians 15:42-50</span>.</p>
