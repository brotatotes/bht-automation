<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:1">1 Corinthians 16:1</span>. <i><b>The collection for the saints</b></i> — περι της λογιας, from λεγω, to <i>gather</i>, or <i>collect</i> translated by the <i>Vulgate, de</i> <i>collectis</i>, a contribution made by the rich for the relief of the poor. The <i>Christians</i> living at Jerusalem, we may naturally suppose, were greatly straitened; as the enmity of their countrymen to the Gospel of Christ led them to treat those who professed it with cruelty, and spoil them of their goods; (see <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:34">Hebrews 10:34</span>; and <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:26">Romans 15:26</span>; and <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:27">Romans 15:27</span>;) and the apostle hereby teaches that it was the duty of one Christian congregation to help another when in distress.</p>
