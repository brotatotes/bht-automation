<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:1">1 John 2:1</span>. <i><b>My little children</b></i> — τεκνιαμου. <i>My beloved</i> <i>children</i>; the address of an affectionate father to children whom he tenderly loves. The term also refers to the apostle's <i>authority</i> as their spiritual father, and their <i>obligation</i> to obey as his spiritual children.</p>
<p><i><b>That ye sin not.</b></i> — This is the language of the whole Scripture; of every dispensation, ordinance, institution, doctrine, and word of God. <i>Sin not</i>-do not run into ruin; live not so as to promote your own misery; be happy, for it is the will of God that ye should be so; therefore he wills that ye should be holy: <i>holiness</i> and <i>happiness</i> are inseparable; <i>sin</i> and <i>misery</i> are equally so.</p>
<p><i><b>And if any man sin</b></i> — If, through ignorance, inexperience, the violence of temptation, unwatchfulness, c., ye have fallen into sin, and grieved the Spirit of God, do not continue in the sin, nor under the guilt do not despair of being again restored to the favour of God; your case, it is true, is deeply deplorable, but not desperate; there is still hope, for--</p>
<p><i><b>We have an advocate with the Father</b></i> — We still have him before the throne who died for our offences, and rose again for our justification; and <i>there</i> he makes intercession for us. He is the <i>righteous</i>; he who suffered, the just for the <i>unjust</i>, that he might bring us to God. Do not, therefore, despair, but have immediate recourse to God through him.</p>
