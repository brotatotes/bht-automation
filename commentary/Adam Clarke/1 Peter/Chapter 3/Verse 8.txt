<p>Verse 8. <b>Be ye </b><i><b>all of one mind</b></i> — Unity, both in the family and in the Church, being essentially necessary to <i>peace</i> and <i>salvation</i>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:16">Romans 12:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:5">Romans 15:5</span>.</p>
<p><i><b>Having compassion</b></i> — συμπαθεις. Being <i>sympathetic</i>; feeling for each other; bearing each other's burdens.</p>
<p><i><b>Love as brethren</b></i> — φιλαδελφοι. <i>Be lovers of the brethren</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>Pitiful</b></i> — ευσπλαγχνοι. <i>Tender-hearted</i>; let your <i>bowels yearn</i> over the distressed and afflicted.</p>
<p><i><b>Courteous</b></i> — φιλοφρονες. Be <i>friendly-minded</i>; acquire and cultivate a friendly disposition. But instead of this word, ταπεινοφρονες, <i>be humble-minded</i>, is the reading of ABC, more than twenty others, with the <i>Syriac, Arabic</i> of Erpen, <i>Coptic,</i> <i>Armenian, Slavonic</i>, and some of the <i>fathers</i>. This is probably the true reading, and <i>Griesbach</i> has admitted it into the text.</p>
