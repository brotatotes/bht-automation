<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:4">1 Thessalonians 2:4</span>. <i><b>But as we were allowed of God</b></i> — καθως δεδοκιμασμεθα. <i>As we were accounted worthy</i> to be put in trust-as God put confidence in us, and sent us on his most especial message to mankind, <i>even so we speak</i>, keeping the dignity of our high calling continually in view; and, acting as in the <i>sight of God</i>, we speak <i>not to please</i> or flatter men, though our doctrine is the most <i>consolatory</i> that can be conceived, but to <i>please</i> that <i>God who</i> <i>searcheth the heart</i>, and before whom all our motives are constantly without a veil.</p>
