<p>Verse 6. <i><b>Nor of men sought we glory</b></i> — As we preached not for worldly gain, so we preached not for popular applause; we had what we sought for-the approbation of God, and the testimony of a good conscience.</p>
<p><i><b>When we might have been burdensome</b></i> — They had a right to their maintenance while they devoted themselves wholly to the work of the Gospel for the sake of the people's souls. Others understand the words εν βαρει ειναι, <i>to be honourable</i>; we sought no <i>glory</i> of you nor of others, though we were <i>honourable</i> as the apostles of Christ. כבוד <i>cabod</i>, in Hebrew, to which the Greek βαρος answers, signifies not only <i>weight</i> but <i>glory</i>; and in both these senses the apostle uses it, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:17">2 Corinthians 4:17</span>.</p>
