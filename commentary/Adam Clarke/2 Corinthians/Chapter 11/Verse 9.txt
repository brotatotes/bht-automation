<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:9">2 Corinthians 11:9</span>. <i><b>And when I was present with you</b></i> — The particle και which we translate <i>and</i>, should be rendered <i>for</i> in this place: <i>For</i> <i>when I was with you, and was in want, I was chargeable to no man</i>. I preferred to be, for a time, even without the <i>necessaries</i> of life, rather than be a burden to you. To <i>whom</i> was this a reproach, to <i>me</i> or to <i>you</i>?</p>
<p><i><b>The brethren which came from Macedonia</b></i> — He probably refers to the supplies which he received from the Church at Philippi, which was in <i>Macedonia</i>; of which he says, that <i>in the beginning of the</i> <i>Gospel no Church communicated with me, as concerning giving and</i> <i>receiving, but you only; for even at Thessalonica ye sent once and</i> <i>again to my necessity</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="php+4:15-16">Philippians 4:15-16</span>. See the <i>Introduction</i>, sec. vi.</p>
