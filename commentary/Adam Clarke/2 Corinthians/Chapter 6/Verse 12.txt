<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:12">2 Corinthians 6:12</span>. <i><b>Ye are not straitened in us</b></i> — That is, Ye have not a <i>narrow</i> place in our affections: the metaphor here is taken from the case of a person pent up in a small or narrow place, where there is scarcely room to breathe.</p>
<p><i><b>Ye are straitened in your own bowels.</b></i> — I have not the same place in your affections which you have in mine. The <i>bowels</i> are used in Scripture to denote the most <i>tender affections</i>. <span class="_0000ff"><span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:36">Matthew 9:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:36">Matthew 9:36</span></span>.</p>
