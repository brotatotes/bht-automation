<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:11">2 Corinthians 7:11</span>. <i><b>What carefulness it wrought in you</b></i> — Carefulness of obeying my directions, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:15">2 Corinthians 7:15</span>; <i>yea</i>, what <i>clearing of yourselves</i> from guilt by inflicting censures on the guilty person, and putting away evil from among you, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:13">1 Corinthians 5:13</span>; <i>yea, what indignation</i> against him who had dishonoured his profession, and defiled the Church; <i>yea, what fear</i> of my displeasure, and the <i>rod</i> which I threatened, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:21">1 Corinthians 4:21</span>; <i>yea, what vehement desire</i> to rectify what was amiss in this matter, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:7">2 Corinthians 7:7</span>; <i>yea, what zeal</i> for me; <i>yea, what revenge</i> in punishing the delinquent. See <i>Whitby</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>In all things</b></i>, c.] In the whole of your conduct in this affair since <i>ye</i> have received my letter, <i>ye have approved yourselves to</i> <i>be clear</i>, αγνους; not only to be clear of contumacy and obstinate persistance in your former conduct, but to have done all in the compass of your power to rectify the abuses which had crept in among you. The Corinthians were not <i>clear</i>, i.e. innocent or void of blame in the fact, but they were <i>clear</i> of all blame in their endeavours to remove the evil.</p>
