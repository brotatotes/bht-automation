<p>Verse 9. <i><b>Who hath saved us</b></i> — From sin; the spirit of bondage, and all tormenting fear. This is the design of the Gospel.</p>
<p><i><b>And called</b></i><b> us </b><i><b>with a holy calling</b></i> — <i>Invited</i> us to holiness and comfort here; and to eternal glory hereafter.</p>
<p><i><b>Not according to our works</b></i> — We have not <i>deserved</i> any part of the good we have received; and can never merit one moment of the exceeding great and eternal weight of glory which is promised. See the notes on the parallel passages.</p>
<p><i><b>Before the world began</b></i> — προ χρονων αιωνιων. Before the <i>Mosaic</i> <i>dispensation</i> took place, God purposed the salvation of the Gentiles by Christ Jesus; and the Mosaic dispensation was intended only as the introducer of the Gospel. <i>The law was our</i> <i>schoolmaster unto Christ</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:24">Galatians 3:24</span>. See the parallel places, and the notes there.</p>
