<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:1">Acts 1:1</span>. <i><b>The former treatise</b></i> — The Gospel according to Luke, which is here most evidently intended.</p>
<p><i><b>O Theophilus</b></i> — <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:3">Luke 1:3</span>.</p>
<p><i><b>To do and teach</b></i> — These two words comprise his <i>miracles</i> and <i>sermons</i>. This introduction seems to intimate that, as he had already in his <i>Gospel</i> given an account of the <i>life</i> and <i>actions</i> of our Lord, so in this <i>second treatise</i> he was about to give an account of the <i>lives</i> and <i>acts</i> of some of the <i>chief apostles</i>, such as <i>Peter</i> and <i>Paul</i>.</p>
