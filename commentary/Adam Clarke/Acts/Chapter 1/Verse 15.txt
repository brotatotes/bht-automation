<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:15">Acts 1:15</span>. <i><b>In the midst of the disciples</b></i> — μαθητων; but instead of this, αδελφων, <i>brethren</i>, is the reading of ABC, a few others, with the <i>Coptic, AEthiopic, Armenian</i>, and <i>Vulgate</i>. This seems the best reading, because of what immediately follows; for it was not among the <i>disciples</i> merely that he stood, but among the <i>whole company</i>, which amounted to one hundred and twenty. It is remarkable that this was the number which the Jews required to form a <i>council</i> in any city; and it is likely that in reference to this the disciples had gathered together, with themselves, the number of one hundred and twenty, chosen out of the many who had been already converted by the ministry of our Lord, the twelve disciples, and the seventy-two whom he had sent forth to preach, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:1">Luke 10:1</span>, &amp;c., thus they formed a complete <i>council</i> in presence of which the important business of electing a person in the place of Judas was to be transacted.</p>
