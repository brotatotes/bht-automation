<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:17">Acts 1:17</span>. <i><b>Obtained part of this ministry.</b></i> — ελαχε τον κληρον, <i>He</i> <i>obtained the lot of this ministry</i>-not that <i>he</i> or any of the twelve apostles, was chosen to this ministry by <i>lot</i>, but as <i>lot</i> signifies the <i>portion</i> a man has in life, what comes to him in the course of the <i>Divine providence</i>, or as an especial <i>gift of God's goodness</i>, it is used here, as in many other parts of the sacred writings, to signify <i>office</i> or <i>station</i>. On this subject the reader is referred to on <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="le+16:8">Leviticus 16:8</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="le+16:9">Leviticus 16:9</span> (note); <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="jos+14:2">Joshua 14:2</span> (note): see also <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:26">Acts 1:26</span> (note).</p>
