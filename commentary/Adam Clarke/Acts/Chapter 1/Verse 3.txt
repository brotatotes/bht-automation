<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:3">Acts 1:3</span>. <i><b>To whom - he showed himself alive - by many infallible</b></i> <i>proofs</i>] πολλοιςτεκμηριοις; by many proofs of such a nature, and connected with such circumstances, as to render them <i>indubitable</i>; for this is the import of the Greek word τεκμηριον. The proofs were such as these:</p>
<p>1. Appearing to several different persons at different times.</p>
<p>2. His eating and drinking with them.</p>
<p>3. His meeting them in Galilee according to his own appointment.</p>
<p>4. His subjecting his body to be touched and handled by them.</p>
<p>5. His instructing them in the nature and doctrines of his kingdom.</p>
<p>6. His appearing to upwards of five hundred persons at once, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:6">1 Corinthians 15:6</span>. And,</p>
<p>7. Continuing these public manifestations of himself for <i>forty</i> days.</p>
<p>The several appearances of Jesus Christ, during the forty days of his sojourning with his disciples, between his resurrection and ascension, are thus enumerated by Bishop Pearce: The <i>first</i> was to Mary Magdalene, and the other Mary, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:1-9">Matthew 28:1-9</span>. The <i>second</i>, to the two disciples on their way to Emmaus, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:15">Luke 24:15</span>. The <i>third</i>, to Simon Peter, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:34">Luke 24:34</span>. The <i>fourth</i>, to ten of the apostles, Thomas being absent, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:36">Luke 24:36</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:19">John 20:19</span>. (All these four appearances took place on the day of his resurrection.) The <i>fifth</i> was to the eleven disciples, Thomas being then with them, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:26">John 20:26</span>. The <i>sixth</i>, to seven of the apostles in Galilee, at the sea of Tiberias, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:4">John 21:4</span>. The <i>seventh</i>, to James, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:7">1 Corinthians 15:7</span>, most probably in Jerusalem, and when Jesus gave an order for all his apostles to <i>assemble</i> <i>together</i>, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:4">Acts 1:4</span>. The <i>eighth</i>, when they were assembled together, and when he led them unto Bethany, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:50">Luke 24:50</span>, from whence he ascended to heaven. But <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:14">John 21:14</span>, for farther particulars.</p>
<p><i><b>Pertaining to the kingdom of God</b></i> — Whatever concerned the doctrine, discipline, and establishment of the Christian Church.</p>
