<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:19">Acts 11:19</span>. <i><b>The persecution that arose about Stephen</b></i> — That is, those who were obliged to flee from Jerusalem at the time of that persecution in which Stephen lost his life. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:1">Acts 8:1</span>.</p>
<p><i><b>Phoenice</b></i> — Phoenicia, a country between Galilee and Syria, along the coast of the Mediterranean Sea, including Tyre, Sidon, c. It is often mentioned as a part of Syria. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:2-3">Acts 21:2-3</span>.</p>
<p><i><b>Cyprus</b></i> — An island of the Mediterranean Sea, over against Syria. <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:30">Acts 4:30</span>.</p>
<p><i><b>Antioch</b></i> — A city of Syria, built by Antiochus Seleucus, near the river <i>Orontes</i> at that time one of the most celebrated cities of the east. For the situation of all these, see the <i>map</i> accompanying this book.</p>
<p><i><b>Unto the Jews only.</b></i> — For they knew nothing of the vision of St. Peter; and did not believe that God would open the door of faith to the Gentiles. The next verse informs us that there were others who were better instructed. See below.</p>
