<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:1">Acts 12:1</span>. <i><b>Herod the king</b></i> — This was <i>Herod Agrippa</i>, the son of <i>Aristobulus</i>, and grandson of <i>Herod the Great</i>; he was nephew to <i>Herod Antipas</i>, who beheaded John they Baptist, and brother to <i>Herodias</i>. He was made king by the Emperor Caligula, and was put in possession of all the territories formerly held by his uncle <i>Philip</i> and by <i>Lysanias</i>; viz. <i>Iturea, Trachonitis, Abilene</i>, with <i>Gaulonitis, Batanaea</i>, and <i>Penias</i>. To these the Emperor Claudius afterwards added <i>Judea</i> and <i>Samaria</i>; which were nearly all the dominions possessed by his grandfather, Herod the Great. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:1">Luke 3:1</span>; see also an account of the Herod family, <i>see in Clarke's note on "</i><span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:1">Matthew 2:1</span><i>"</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>To vex certain of the Church.</b></i> — That is, to destroy its chief ornaments and supports.</p>
