<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:17">Acts 13:17</span>. <i><b>The God of - our fathers</b></i> — The apostle begins his discourse with the Egyptian bondage, and their deliverance from it, as points the most remarkable and striking in their history; in which the providence and mighty power of God, exerted so frequently in their behalf, were peculiarly conspicuous.</p>
<p><i><b>Exalted the people</b></i> — Even when they were strangers in the land, and greatly oppressed, God exalted them; made them a terror to their enemies, and multiplied them greatly.</p>
<p><i><b>With a high arm</b></i> — A literal translation of the Hebrew phrase, בזרוע רמה <i>bezeroa ramah</i>, with a lifted-up arm, to protect them and destroy their enemies. The meaning of the phrase is, <i>a manifest</i> <i>display of the Divine power</i>.</p>
