<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:5">Acts 13:5</span>. <i><b>Salamis</b></i> — The capital of the island of Cyprus; afterwards called <i>Constantia</i>, and now <i>Salina</i>, situated on the eastern part of the island.</p>
<p><i><b>They preached the word of God</b></i> — τον λογον, The doctrine of God, the Christian religion, emphatically so called.</p>
<p><i><b>They had also John to</b></i> their <i>minister.</i>] This was <i>John Mark</i>, of whom we heard, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:25">Acts 12:25</span>; <i>for</i> their <i>minister</i>, υπηρετην, to assist them in minor offices, as <i>deacon</i> or <i>servant</i>, that they might give themselves wholly to the doctrine of the Lord.</p>
