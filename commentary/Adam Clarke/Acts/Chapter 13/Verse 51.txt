<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:51">Acts 13:51</span>. <i><b>They shook off the dust of their feet against them</b></i> — This was a very significant rite; by it, they in effect said: Ye are worse than the heathen: even your very land is accursed for your opposition to God, and we dare not permit even its dust to cleave to the soles of our feet; and we shake it off, in departing from your country, according to our Lord's command, (<span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:14">Matthew 10:14</span>,) for a testimony against you, that we offered you salvation, but ye rejected <i>it</i> and persecuted <i>us</i>. The Jews, when travelling in <i>heathen</i> countries, took care, when they came to the <i>borders</i> of their own, to shake off the dust of their feet, lest any of the unhallowed ground should defile the sacred land of <i>Israel</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>Came unto Iconium.</b></i> — According to Strabo, Iconium was a small fortified town, the capital of Lycaonia, at present called <i>Cogni</i>. "Lycaonia was a province at the back of Pamphylia, higher up in Asia Minor, and to the northeast of Pamphylia." <i>Pearce</i>.</p>
