<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:10">Acts 14:10</span>. <i><b>Said with a loud voice</b></i> — After this clause the following is found in CD, and several others, either in the text or margin: σοι λεγω εν τῳ ονοματι του Κυριου Ιησου Χριστου, <i>I say</i> <i>unto thee, In the name of the Lord Jesus Christ</i>, "stand upright on thy feet." This reading is also in several <i>versions</i>; and though it may not stand on such evidence as to entitle it to a place in the text, yet it is not likely that St. Paul would not have used the <i>sacred name</i> on such an occasion; especially as this appears to have been the usual form. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:6">Acts 3:6</span>.</p>
<p><i><b>He leaped and walked.</b></i> — Giving the fullest proof of his restoration: his <i>leaping</i>, however, might have been through joy of having received his cure.</p>
