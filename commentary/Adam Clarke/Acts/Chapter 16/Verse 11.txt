<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:11">Acts 16:11</span>. <i><b>Loosing from Troas</b></i> — Setting sail from this place.</p>
<p><i><b>With a straight course to Samothracia</b></i> — This was an island of the AEgean Sea, contiguous to <i>Thrace</i>, and hence called <i>Samothracia</i>, or the <i>Thracian Samos</i>. It is about twenty miles in circumference, and is now called <i>Samandrachi</i> by the Turks, who are its present masters.</p>
<p><i><b>And the next day to Neapolis.</b></i> — There were many cities of this name; but this was a sea-port town of Macedonia, a few miles eastward of Philippi. <i>Neapolis</i> signifies the <i>new city</i>.</p>
