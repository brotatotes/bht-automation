<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:11">Acts 17:11</span>. <i><b>These were more noble than those in Thessalonica</b></i> — ησαν ευγενεστεροι, <i>Were of a better race, extraction</i>, or <i>birth</i>, than those at Thessalonica; but the word refers more to their <i>conduct</i>, as a proof of their <i>better disposition</i>, than to their <i>birth</i>, or any peculiar <i>lineal nobility</i>. It was a maxim among the Jews, that "none was of a <i>noble spirit</i> who did not employ himself in the study of the law." It appears that the <i>Bereans</i> were a <i>better</i> <i>educated</i> and <i>more polished</i> people than those at <i>Thessalonica</i>; in consequence far from persecuting:</p>
<p>1. They heard the doctrine of the Gospel attentively.</p>
<p>2. They received this doctrine with readiness of mind: when the evidence of its truth appeared to them sufficiently convincing, they had too much dignity of mind to refuse their assent, and too much ingenuousness to conceal their approbation.</p>
<p>3. They searched the Scriptures, i.e. of the Old Testament, to see whether these thing were so: to see whether the <i>promises</i> and <i>types</i> corresponded with the alleged fulfilment in the person, works, and sufferings of Jesus Christ.</p>
<p>4. They continued in this work; they searched the Scriptures <i>daily</i>, whether those things were so.</p>
