<p>Verse 29. <i><b>Forasmuch then as we are the offspring of God</b></i>, c.] This inference of the apostle was very strong and conclusive and his argument runs thus: "If we are the offspring of God, he cannot be like those <i>images of gold, silver</i>, and <i>stone</i>, which are formed by the <i>art</i> and <i>device of man</i>; for the parent must resemble his offspring. Seeing, therefore, that <i>we</i> are <i>living</i> and <i>intelligent</i> beings, HE from whom we have derived that being must be <i>living</i> and <i>intelligent</i>. It is necessary, also, that the object of religious worship should be much more <i>excellent</i> than the worshipper; but a <i>man</i> is, by innumerable degrees, more excellent than an <i>image</i> made out of <i>gold, silver</i>, or <i>stone</i>; and yet it would be impious to worship a <i>man</i>: how much more <i>so</i> to worship these <i>images</i> as <i>gods</i>!
