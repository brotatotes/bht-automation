<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:14">Acts 18:14</span>. <i><b>Paul was now about to open</b></i><b> his </b><i><b>mouth</b></i> — He was about to enter on his defense; but Gallio, perceiving that the prosecution was through envy and malice, would not put Paul to any farther trouble, but determined the matter as follows.</p>
<p><i><b>If it were a matter of wrong</b></i> — αδικημα, Of <i>injustice</i>; any thing contrary to the <i>rights</i> of the subject.</p>
<p><i><b>Or wicked lewdness</b></i> — ραδιουργημα πονηρον, <i>Destructive mischief</i>. (<span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:10">Acts 13:10</span>, where the word is explained.) Something by which the subject is grievously <i>wronged</i>; were it any crime against <i>society</i> or against the <i>state</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>Reason would that I should bear with you.</b></i> — κατα λογον αν ηνεσχομην υμων, <i>According to reason</i>, or <i>the merit of the case, I</i> <i>should patiently hear you</i>.</p>
