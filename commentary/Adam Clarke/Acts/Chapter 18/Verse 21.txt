<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:21">Acts 18:21</span>. <i><b>I must - keep this feast</b></i> â€” Most likely the <i>passover</i>, at which he wished to attend for the purpose of seeing many of his friends, and having the most favourable opportunity to preach the Gospel to thousands who would attend at Jerusalem on that occasion. The whole of this clause, <i>I must by all means keep this</i> <i>feast that cometh in Jerusalem</i>, is wanting in ABE, six others; with the <i>Coptic, AEthiopic, Armenian</i>, and <i>Vulgate</i>. Griesbach leaves it in the text, with the mark of <i>doubtfulness</i>; and Professor White, in his <i>Crisews</i>, says, probabiliter <i>delenda</i>. Without this clause the verse will read thus: <i>But he bade them</i> <i>farewell, saying, I will return again unto you, if God will</i>. And this he did before the expiration of that same year, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:1">Acts 19:1</span>, and spent three years with them, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:31">Acts 20:31</span>, extending and establishing the Church at that place.</p>
