<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:4">Acts 18:4</span>. <i><b>He reasoned in the synagogue every Sabbath</b></i> — Discoursed at large concerning Jesus as the Messiah, proving this point from their own Scriptures, collated with the facts of our Lord's life, c.</p>
<p><i><b>And persuaded the Jews and the Greeks.</b></i> — Many, both Jews and proselytes, were convinced of the truth of his doctrine. Among his converts was <i>Epenetus</i>, the first fruit of his labour in Achaia, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:5">Romans 16:5</span> and the family of <i>Stephanas</i> was the next; and then <i>Crispus</i> and <i>Caius</i>, or <i>Gaius</i>; all of whom the apostle himself baptized, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:14-16">1 Corinthians 1:14-16</span>. <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:8">Acts 18:8</span>.</p>
