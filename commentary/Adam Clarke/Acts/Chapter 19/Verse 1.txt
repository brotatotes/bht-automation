<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:1">Acts 19:1</span>. <i><b>And it came to pass - while Apollos was at Corinth</b></i> — The <i>Codex Bezae</i> begins this chapter differently. <i>But then Paul was</i> <i>desirous, according to his own counsel, to go to Jerusalem, the</i> <i>Spirit commanded him to return into Asia: then, passing through</i> <i>the upper parts, he came to Ephesus</i>. This addition is also found in the <i>Latin</i> or <i>Itala</i> part of the same MS., and in the <i>margin</i> of the <i>later Syriac</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>Paul having passed through the upper coasts</b></i> — That is, through those parts of Asia Minor that lay eastward of Ephesus, such as Galatia, Phrygia, and probably Lycaonia and Lydia; and it is in reference to Ephesus that these are called the <i>upper</i> coasts. See their situation on the map.</p>
