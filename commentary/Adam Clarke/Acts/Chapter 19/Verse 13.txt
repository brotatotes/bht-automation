<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:13">Acts 19:13</span>. <i><b>Certain of the vagabond Jews, exorcists</b></i> — Τινες απο των περιερχομενων Ιουδαιων εξορκιϚων; <i>Certain of the Jews who went</i> <i>about practicing exorcisms</i>. <i>Vagabond</i> has a very bad acceptation among us; but, literally, <i>vagabundus</i> signifies a <i>wanderer</i>, one that has no settled place of abode. These, like all their countrymen, in all places, went about to get their bread in what way they could; making trial of every thing by which they could have the prospect of gain. Finding that Paul cast out demons through the name of Jesus, they thought, by using the same, they might produce the same effects; and, if they could, they knew it would be to them an ample source of revenue; for demoniacs abounded in the land.</p>
