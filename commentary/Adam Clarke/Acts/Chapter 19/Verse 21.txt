<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:21">Acts 19:21</span>. <i><b>Paul purposed in the spirit</b></i>, c.] Previously to this he appears to have concerted a journey to Macedonia, and a visit to Corinth, the capital of Achaia, where he seems to have spent a considerable time, probably the whole winter of A.D. 58 see <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:5-6">1 Corinthians 16:5-6</span>; and afterwards to go to Jerusalem; but it is likely that he did not leave Ephesus till after pentecost, a.d. 59. (<span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:8">1 Corinthians 16:8</span>) And he resolved, if possible, to see Rome, which had been the object of his wishes for a considerable time. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:10">Romans 1:10</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:13">Romans 1:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:23">Romans 16:23</span>.</p>
<p>It is generally believed that, during this period, while at Ephesus, he wrote his first epistle to the Corinthians. He had heard that some strange disorders had entered into that Church:-</p>
<p>1. That there were <i>divisions</i> among them; some extolling <i>Paul</i>, beyond all others; some, <i>Peter</i>; others, <i>Apollos</i>.</p>
<p>2. He had learned from Stephanas, Fortunatus, and Achaicus, whom he saw at Ephesus, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:17">1 Corinthians 16:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:1">1 Corinthians 7:1</span>, that several <i>abuses</i> had crept into their religious assemblies.</p>
<p>3. That even the <i>Christians</i> went to law with each other, and that before the <i>heathens</i>. And,</p>
<p>4. That a person professing Christianity in that city, had formed a matrimonial contract with his step-mother. It was to remedy those disorders that he wrote his first epistle to the Corinthians, in which he strongly reprehends all the above evils.</p>
