<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:22">Acts 19:22</span>. <i><b>So he sent into Macedonia</b></i> — He desired <i>Timothy</i> to go as far as Corinth, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:18">1 Corinthians 4:18</span>, and after that to return to him at Ephesus, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:11">1 Corinthians 16:11</span>; but he himself continued in Asia some time longer; probably to make collections for the poor saints in Jerusalem. <i>Erastus</i>, mentioned here for the first time, appears to have been the <i>chamberlain</i>, οικονομος, either of <i>Ephesus</i> or <i>Corinth</i>; see <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:23">Romans 16:23</span>. He was one of St. Paul's companions, and is mentioned as being left by the apostle at Corinth, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:20">2 Timothy 4:20</span>.</p>
