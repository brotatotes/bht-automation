<p>Verse 31. <i><b>Certain of the chief of Asia</b></i> — Τινες των Ασιαρχων; Some of the <i>Asiarchs</i>. The <i>Asiarchs</i> were those to whom the care and regulation of the public games were intrusted: they were a sort of <i>high priests</i>, and were always persons of considerable riches and influence. These could not have been Christians; but they were what the sacred text states them to have been, αυτω φιλοι, <i>his</i> <i>friends</i>; and foreseeing that Paul would be exposed to great danger if he went into the theatre, amidst such a tumultuous assembly, they sent a message to him, entreating him not to go into danger so apparent. Query: Did he not go, and fight with these wild beasts at Ephesus? <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:32">1 Corinthians 15:32</span>.</p>
