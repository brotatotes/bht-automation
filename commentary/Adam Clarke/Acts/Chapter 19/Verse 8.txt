<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:8">Acts 19:8</span>. <i><b>Spake boldly - three months</b></i> — We have often remarked that St. Paul, in every place, made his first offers of salvation to the <i>Jews</i>; and it was only when <i>they</i> rejected it, that he turned to the <i>Gentiles</i>; see <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:6">Acts 18:6</span>. And the same line of conduct he pursues here: he goes to the school of Tyrannus, at least a public place, to which <i>all</i> might resort, when they obstinately rejected the Gospel in the synagogue.</p>
<p><i><b>Disputing and persuading</b></i> — Διαλεγομενος, και πειθων, Holding conversations with them, in order to persuade them of the truth of the doctrine of Christ.</p>
