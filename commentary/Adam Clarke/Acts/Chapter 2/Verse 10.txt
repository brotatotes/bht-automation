<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:10">Acts 2:10</span>. <i><b>PHRYGIA</b></i> — A country in Asia Minor, southward of <i>Pontus</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>PAMPHYLIA</b></i> — The ancient name of the country of <i>Natolia</i>, now called <i>Caramania</i>, between <i>Lycia</i> and <i>Cilicia</i>, near the <i>Mediterranean Sea</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>EGYPT</b></i> — A very extensive country of African bounded by the <i>Mediterranean</i> on the <i>north</i>; by the <i>Red Sea</i> and the <i>Isthmus</i> of <i>Suez</i>, which divide it from <i>Arabia</i>, on the <i>east</i>; by <i>Abyssinia</i> or <i>AEthiopia</i> on the <i>south</i>; and by the <i>deserts</i> of <i>Barca</i> and <i>Nubia</i> on the <i>west</i>. It was called <i>Mizraim</i> by the ancient Hebrews, and now <i>Mesr</i> by the Arabians. It extends 600 miles from north to south; and from 100 to 250 in breadth, from east to west.</p>
<p><i><b>LIBYA</b></i> — In a general way, among the Greeks, signified Africa; but the northern part, in the vicinity of <i>Cyrene</i>, is here meant.</p>
<p><i><b>CYRENE</b></i> — A country in Africa on the coast of the Mediterranean Sea, southward of the most western point of the Island of Crete.</p>
<p><i><b>Strangers of Rome</b></i> — Persons dwelling at Rome, and speaking the Latin language, partly consisting of regularly descended <i>Jews</i> and <i>proselytes</i> to the Jewish religion.</p>
