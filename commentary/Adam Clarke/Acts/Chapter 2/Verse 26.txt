<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:26">Acts 2:26</span>. <i><b>And my tongue was glad</b></i> — In the Hebrew it is ויגל כבודי <i>vaiyagel kebodi</i>, "And my glory was glad:" but the evangelist follows the <i>Septuagint</i>, in reading και ηγαλλιασατο ἡ γλωσσα μου, what all the other Greek interpreters in the Hexapla translate δοξα μον, <i>my glory</i>. And what is to be understood by <i>glory</i> here! Why the <i>soul</i>, certainly, and not the <i>tongue</i>; and so some of the best critics interpret the place.</p>
