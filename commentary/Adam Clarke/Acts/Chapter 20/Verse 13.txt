<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:13">Acts 20:13</span>. <i><b>Sailed unto Assos</b></i> — <i>Assos</i>, according to <i>Pausanias</i>, Eliac. ii. 4, and <i>Pliny</i>, Hist. Nat. xxxvi. 27, was a maritime town of Asia, in the <i>Troad</i>. <i>Strabo</i> and <i>Stephanus</i> place it in <i>Mysia.</i> It was also called <i>Apollonia</i>, according to <i>Pliny</i>, Ib. lib. v. 30. The passage by sea to this place was much longer than by land; and therefore St. Paul chose to go by <i>land</i>, while the others went by sea.</p>
<p><i><b>Intending to take in Paul</b></i> — αναλαμβανειν, <i>To take him in AGAIN</i>; for it appears he had already been aboard that same vessel: probably the same that had carried them from Philippi to Troas, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:6">Acts 20:6</span>.</p>
