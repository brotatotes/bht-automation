<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:15">Acts 20:15</span>. <i><b>Over against Chios</b></i> — This was a very celebrated island between <i>Lesbos</i> and <i>Samos</i>, famous in antiquity for its extraordinary <i>wines</i>. At this island the apostle did not touch.</p>
<p><i><b>Arrived at Samos</b></i> — This was another island of the <i>AEgean Sea</i>, or <i>Archipelago</i>. It does not appear that they landed at <i>Samos</i>: they passed close by it, and anchored at <i>Trogyllium</i>. This was a promontory of <i>Ionia</i>, which gave name to some small islands in the vicinity of Samos: Της δε Τρωγιλιου προκειται νησιον ὁμωνυμον: before Trogyllium is situated an island of the same name. <i>Strabo</i>, lib. xiv. p. 635. <i>Pliny</i> also mentions this place, <i>Hist. Nat</i>. lib. v. cap. 31. Near this place was the mouth of the famous river <i>Maeander</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>Came to Miletus.</b></i> — A celebrated city in the province of <i>Caria</i>, about twelve or fifteen leagues from <i>Ephesus</i>, according to <i>Calmet</i>. <i>Miletus</i> is famous for being the birthplace of <i>Thales</i>, one of the seven wise men of Greece, and founder of the <i>Ionic</i> sect of philosophers. <i>Anaximander</i> was also born here, and several other eminent men. The Turks, who lately possessed it, call it <i>Melas</i>.</p>
