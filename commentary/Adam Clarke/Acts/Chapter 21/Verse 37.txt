<p>Verse 37. <i><b>Canst thou speak Greek?</b></i> — Claudius Lysias was not a <i>Roman</i>; he had, as himself informs us, purchased his citizenship of Rome with a great sum of money; (see <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:28">Acts 22:28</span>;) and it is very likely that he was but imperfectly acquainted with the Latin tongue; and the tumult that was now made, and the discordant noise, prevented him from clearly apprehending what was said; and, as he wished to know the merit of the cause, he accosted Paul with, ἙλληνιϚι γινωσκεις, <i>Dost thou understand Greek</i>? And when he found that he did understand it, he proceeded to question him as below.</p>
