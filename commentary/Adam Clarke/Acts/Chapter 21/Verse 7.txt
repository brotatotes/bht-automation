<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:7">Acts 21:7</span>. <i><b>We came to Ptolemais</b></i> â€” This was a seaport town of Galilee, not far from Mount Carmel, between Tyre and Caesarea, where the river Belus empties itself into the sea. It was at first called Accho, (and this is the reading of the Syriac and Arabic,) and belonged to the tribe of Asher, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="jdg+1:31">Judges 1:31</span>; it was enlarged and beautified by the first of the Egyptian Ptolemies, from whom it was called <i>Ptolemais</i>. This place terminated St. Paul's voyage; and this is what is expressed in the text: <i>And we came from Tyre to</i> <i>Ptolemais, where our voyage ended</i>. See the Greek text.</p>
