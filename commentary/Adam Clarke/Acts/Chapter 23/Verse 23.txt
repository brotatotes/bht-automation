<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:23">Acts 23:23</span>. <i><b>Two hundred soldiers</b></i> — στρατιωτας, <i>Infantry</i> or <i>foot</i> <i>soldiers</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>Horsemen threescore and ten</b></i> — There was always a certain number of horse, or cavalry, attached to the <i>foot</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>Spearmen</b></i> — δεξιολαβους, Persons who held a spear or javelin in their hand; from εν τῃ δεξιᾳ λαβειν <i>taking or holding a thing in</i> <i>the right hand</i>. But the <i>Codex Alexandrinus</i> reads δεξιοβολους, from δεξια, the <i>right hand</i>, and βαλλειν, <i>to cast</i> or <i>dart</i>, persons who threw javelins. But both words seem to mean nearly the same thing.</p>
<p><i><b>The third hour of the night</b></i> — About nine o'clock P.M., for the greater secrecy, and to elude the cunning, active malice of the Jews.</p>
