<p>Verse 31. <i><b>Antipatris.</b></i> â€” This place, according to Josephus, Antiq. lib. xiii. cap. 23, was anciently called <i>Capharsaba</i>, and is supposed to be the same which, in <i>1 Macc. vii. 31</i>, is called <i>Capharsalama</i>, or <i>Carphasalama</i>. It was rebuilt by Herod the Great, and denominated <i>Antipatris</i>, in honour of his father <i>Antipater</i>. It was situated between Joppa and Caesarea, on the road from Jerusalem to this latter city. Josephus says it was fifty <i>stadia</i> from Joppa. The distance between Jerusalem and Caesarea was about <i>seventy</i> miles.</p>
