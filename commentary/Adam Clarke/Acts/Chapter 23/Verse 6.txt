<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:6">Acts 23:6</span>. <i><b>I am a Pharisee, the son of a Pharisee</b></i> — Instead of φαρισαιου, <i>of a Pharisee</i>, ABC, some others, with the <i>Syriac</i> and <i>Vulgate</i>, have φαρισαιων, <i>of the Pharisees</i>; which, if acknowledged to be the genuine reading, would alter the sense thus, <i>I am a Pharisee, and a disciple of the Pharisees</i>, for so the word <i>son</i> is frequently understood.</p>
<p><i><b>Of the hope and resurrection</b></i> — Concerning the hope of the resurrection, the και, <i>and</i>, being here redundant; indeed, it is omitted by the <i>Syriac</i>, all the <i>Arabic</i>, and <i>AEthiopic</i>. St. Paul had preached the resurrection of the dead, on the foundation and evidence of the resurrection of Christ. For <i>this</i>, he and the apostles were, some time before, imprisoned by the high priest and elders, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:1-3">Acts 4:1-3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:17">Acts 5:17</span>, because they preached, THROUGH JESUS, the resurrection of the dead. This they could not bear; for, if Jesus Christ rose from the dead, their malice and wickedness, in putting him to death, were incontrovertibly established.</p>
