<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:21">Acts 25:21</span>. <i><b>Unto the hearing of Augustus</b></i> — Εις την του ΣεβαϚου διαγνωσιν; <i>To the discrimination of the emperor</i>. For, although σεβαστος is usually translated <i>Augustus</i>, and the Roman emperors generally assumed this epithet, which signifies no more than <i>the</i> <i>venerable, the august</i>, get here it seems to be used merely to express the <i>emperor</i>, without any reference to any of his attributes or titles.</p>
