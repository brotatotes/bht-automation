<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:6">Acts 25:6</span>. <i><b>When he had tarried - more than ten days</b></i> — The <i>strangeness</i> of this mode of expression suggests the thought that our printed text is not quite correct in this place; and this suspicion is confirmed by an examination of MSS. and versions: ἡμερας ου πλειους οκτω η δεκα, <i>NOT more than EIGHT</i> OR <i>ten days</i>, is the reading of ABC, several others of great respectability, with the <i>Coptic, Armenian</i>, and <i>Vulgate</i>. Griesbach admits this reading into the text: and of it Professor White says, <i>Lectio</i> indubie <i>genuina</i>: "This is doubtless the genuine reading."</p>
