<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:7">Acts 27:7</span>. <i><b>Sailed slowly many days</b></i> — Partly because the <i>wind</i> was contrary, and partly because the vessel was <i>heavy laden</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>Over against Cnidus</b></i> — This was a city or promontory of Asia, opposite to Crete, at one corner of the peninsula of Caria. Some think that this was an island between Crete and a promontory of the same name.</p>
<p><i><b>Over against Salmone</b></i> — We have already seen that the island formerly called <i>Crete</i> is now called <i>Candia</i>; and Salmone or <i>Sammon,</i> or <i>Samonium</i>, now called Cape <i>Salamon</i>, or <i>Salamina</i>, was a promontory on the eastern coast of that island.</p>
