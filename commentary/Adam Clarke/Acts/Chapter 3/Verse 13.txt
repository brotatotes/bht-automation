<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:13">Acts 3:13</span>. <i><b>The God of Abraham</b></i>, c.] This was wisely introduced, to show them that HE whom they called <i>their God</i> had acknowledged Jesus Christ for <i>his Son</i>, and wrought this miracle in his name and, by thus honouring Jesus whom they slew, he had charged home the guilt of that murder upon them.</p>
<p><i><b>Denied him in the presence of Pilate</b></i> — ηρνησασθε, Ye have renounced him as your king, and denounced him to death as a malefactor, when Pilate, convinced of his perfect innocence, was determined, κριναντος, <i>judged</i> it proper and just, to let him go. Pilate wished to act <i>according</i> to justice; you acted <i>contrary</i> to justice and equity in all their forms.</p>
