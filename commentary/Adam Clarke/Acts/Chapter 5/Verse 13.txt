<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:13">Acts 5:13</span>. <i><b>And of the rest, durst no man join him self to them</b></i> — Who were these called <i>the rest</i>, των λοιπων? Dr. Lightfoot thinks the 120 are intended, of which he supposes Ananias to have been one; who, all seeing such wonders wrought by the <i>apostles</i>, were afraid to associate themselves with them in any way of <i>equality</i>, as they saw that God put <i>peculiar</i> honour upon them. Calmet more rationally observes, that the Jewish nation was then divided into many <i>different sects</i>, who entertained widely different opinions on various articles. The apostles adopted none of these jarring sentiments, and none of the <i>different sects dared to join</i> <i>themselves to them</i>; neither <i>Pharisees, Sadducees</i>, nor <i>Herodians</i>, as such, were found in this simple, holy Church. <i>The people</i> felt the force and power of the apostles' doctrine, and <i>magnified them</i>, no more attending to the teaching of the others: the apostles taught them as men having <i>authority</i>, and not as the scribes and Pharisees. This irritated the high priest and his Sadducean council, and led them to adopt the measures mentioned below, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:17">Acts 5:17</span>.</p>
