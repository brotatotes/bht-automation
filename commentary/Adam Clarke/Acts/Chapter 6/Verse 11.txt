<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:11">Acts 6:11</span>. <i><b>Then they suborned men</b></i> — υπεβαλον. They made <i>underhand</i> work; got associated to themselves profligate persons, who for money would swear any thing.</p>
<p><i><b>Blasphemous words against Moses, and</b></i><b> against </b><i><b>God.</b></i> — This was the most deadly charge they could bring against him. We have already seen, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:4">Matthew 9:4</span>, that <i>blasphemy</i>, when against GOD, signifies speaking <i>impiously</i> of his <i>nature, attributes</i>, or <i>works</i>; and, when against <i>men</i>, it signifies speaking <i>injuriously</i> of their <i>character, blasting</i> their <i>reputation</i>, c. These false witnesses came to prove that he had blasphemed <i>Moses</i> by representing him as an impostor, or the like and GOD, by either denying his being, his providence, the justice of his government, &amp;c.</p>
