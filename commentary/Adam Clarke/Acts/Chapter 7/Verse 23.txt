<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:23">Acts 7:23</span>. <i><b>When he was full forty years old</b></i> — This was a general tradition among the Jews: "Moses was forty years in Pharaoh's court, forty years in Midian, and forty years he served Israel."</p>
<p><i><b>To visit his brethren</b></i> — Probably on the ground of trying to deliver them from their oppressive bondage. This desire seems to have been early infused into his mind by the Spirit of God; and the effect of this desire to deliver his oppressed countrymen was his refusing to be called the son of Pharoah's daughter-see <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:24">Hebrews 11:24</span>, and thus renouncing all <i>right</i> to the Egyptian crown, <i>choosing rather to endure addiction</i> <i>with the people of God than enjoy the pleasures of sin for a</i> <i>season</i>.</p>
