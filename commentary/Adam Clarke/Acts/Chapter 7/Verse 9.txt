<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:9">Acts 7:9</span>. <i><b>And the patriarchs</b></i> — The twelve sons of Jacob, thus called because each was <i>chief</i> or <i>head</i> of his respective <i>family</i> or tribe.</p>
<p><i><b>Moved with envy</b></i> — ζηλωσαντες. We translate ζηλος variously: <i>zeal</i> or <i>fervent affection</i>, whether its object be good or bad, is its general meaning; and ζηλοω signifies to be <i>indignant, envious</i>, c. <span class="_0000ff"><span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:17">Acts 5:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:17">Acts 5:17</span></span>. The brethren of Joseph, hearing of his dreams, and understanding them to portend his future advancement, filled with envy, (with which no ordinary portion of malice was associated,) sold Joseph into the land of Egypt, hoping by this means to prevent his future grandeur <i>but God</i>, from whom the portents came, <i>was with him</i>, and made their envy the direct means of accomplishing the great design.</p>
