<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:23">Acts 8:23</span>. <i><b>The gall of bitterness</b></i> — A Hebraism for <i>excessive</i> <i>bitterness</i>: gall, wormwood, and such like, were used to express the dreadful effects of <i>sin</i> in the soul; the bitter <i>repentance</i>, bitter <i>regret</i>, bitter <i>sufferings</i>, bitter <i>death</i>, c., c., which it produces. In <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="de+29:18">Deuteronomy 29:18</span>, idolatry and its consequences are expressed, by having among them <i>a root that beareth GALL and</i> <i>WORMWOOD</i>. And in <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:15">Hebrews 12:15</span>, some grievous sin is intended, when the apostle warns them, <i>lest any root of BITTERNESS springing up,</i> <i>trouble you</i>, and <i>thereby many be defiled</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>Bond of iniquity.</b></i> — An allusion to the mode in which the Romans secured their prisoners, chaining the <i>right hand</i> of the <i>prisoner</i> to the <i>left hand</i> of the <i>soldier</i> who guarded him as if the apostle had said, Thou art tied and bound by the chain of thy sin justice hath laid hold upon thee, and thou hast only a short respite before thy execution, to see if thou wilt repent.</p>
