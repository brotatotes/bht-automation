<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:6">Acts 9:6</span>. <i><b>Trembling</b></i> — Under a strong apprehension of meeting the judgment he deserved.</p>
<p><i><b>And astonished</b></i> — At the <i>light</i>, the <i>thunder</i>, and the <i>voice</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>Lord, what wilt thou have me to do?</b></i> — The word κυριε, <i>Lord</i>, is here to be understood in its proper sense, as expressing <i>authority</i> and <i>dominion</i>: in the 5th verse it appears to be equivalent to our word <i>sir</i>.</p>
<p>The pride of the Pharisee is now brought down to the dust; and the fury of the persecutor is not only restrained, but the lion becomes a lamb. <i>What wilt thou have me to do</i>? Wilt thou condescend to <i>employ me</i> among thy meanest <i>servants</i>?</p>
<p><i><b>Go into the city, and it shall be told thee</b></i>, c.] Jesus could have informed him at once what was his will concerning him but he chose to make one of those very disciples whom he was going to bring in <i>bonds</i> to Jerusalem the <i>means</i> of his <i>salvation</i>:</p>
<p>1. To show that God will help man by man, that they may learn to love and respect each other.</p>
<p>2. That in the benevolence of Ananias he might see the spirit and tendency of that religion which he was persecuting, and of which he was shortly to become an <i>apostle</i>.</p>
