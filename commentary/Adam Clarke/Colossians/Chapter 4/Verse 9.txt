<p>Verse 9. <i><b>With Onesimus - who is</b></i><b> one </b><i><b>of you.</b></i> — Onesimus was a native of some part of Phrygia, if not of Colosse itself; and being lately converted to the Christian faith by the instrumentality of the apostle, he would be able, on this account, to give them satisfactory information concerning the apostle's state, which would be doubly acceptable to them as he was their countryman. See the <i>Epistle</i> to <i>Philemon</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>All things which</b></i> are done <i>here.</i>] FG, the <i>Vulgate, Itala,</i> <i>Jerome</i>, and <i>Bede</i>, add here πραττομενα, <i>what is done</i>, which we have supplied in <i>Italics</i> in our translation. These brethren could give an account of the transactions at Rome, relative to the apostle and Christianity, which it might not be prudent for him to commit to writing. <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="col+4:7">Colossians 4:7</span>. The reign of Nero was not only cruel, but suspicious, jealous, and dangerous.</p>
