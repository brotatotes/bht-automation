<p>Verse 9. <i><b>Having made known unto us the mystery</b></i> — That the Gentiles should ever be received into the Church of God, and have all the privileges of the Jews, without being obliged to submit to circumcision, and perform the rites and ceremonies of the Jewish law was a <i>mystery</i>-a <i>hidden thing</i> which had never been published before; and <i>now</i> revealed only to the apostles. It was <i>God's will</i> that it should be so, but that will he kept <i>hidden</i> to the present time. A <i>mystery</i> signifies something <i>hidden</i>, but it ceases to be a <i>mystery</i> as soon as it is <i>revealed</i>. <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:11">Matthew 13:11</span>; and particularly that on, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:25">Romans 11:25</span>.</p>
<p><i><b>Good pleasure</b></i> — την ευδοκιαν. <i>That benevolent design which he</i> <i>had purposed in himself</i>, not being induced by any consideration from <i>without</i>.</p>
