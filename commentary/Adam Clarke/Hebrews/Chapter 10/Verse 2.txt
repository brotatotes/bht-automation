<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:2">Hebrews 10:2</span>. <i><b>Would they not have ceased to be offered?</b></i> â€” Had they made an effectual reconciliation for the sins of the world, and contained in their once offering a plenitude of permanent merit, they would have ceased to be offered, at least in reference to any individual who had once offered them; because, in such a case, his conscience would be satisfied that its guilt had been taken away. But no Jew pretended to believe that even the annual atonement cancelled his sin before God; yet he continued to make his offerings, the law of God having so enjoined, because these sacrifices pointed out that which was to come.
