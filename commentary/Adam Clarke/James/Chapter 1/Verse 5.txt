<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:5">James 1:5</span>. <i><b>If any of you lack wisdom</b></i> — Wisdom signifies in general <i>knowledge of the best end</i>, and <i>the best means of attaining</i> <i>it</i>; but in Scripture it signifies the same as <i>true religion</i>, the thorough practical knowledge of God, of one's self, and of a Saviour.</p>
<p><i><b>Let him ask of God</b></i> — Because God is the only teacher of this wisdom.</p>
<p><i><b>That giveth to all</b></i><b> men </b><i><b>liberally</b></i> — Who has all good, and gives all necessary good to every one that asks fervently. He who does not ask thus does not feel his need of Divine teaching. The ancient Greek maxim appears at first view strange, but it is literally true: - </p>
<p style="margin-left: 0.9em; "> Αρχη γνωσεως της αγνοιας ἡ γνωσις.</p>
<p style="margin-left: 0.9em; "> </p>
<p style="margin-left: 0.9em; "> "The knowledge of ignorance is the beginning of knowledge."</p>
<p style="margin-left: 0.9em; "> </p>
<p style="margin-left: 0.9em; "> In knowledge we may distinguish these four things: -</p>
<p style="margin-left: 0.9em; "> </p>
<p style="margin-left: 0.9em; "> 1. INTELLIGENCE, the object of which is <i>intuitive truths</i>.</p>
<p style="margin-left: 0.9em; "> </p>
<p style="margin-left: 0.9em; "> 2. WISDOM, which is employed in finding out the <i>best end</i>.</p>
<p style="margin-left: 0.9em; "> </p>
<p style="margin-left: 0.9em; "> 3. PRUDENCE, which <i>regulates</i> the whole <i>conduct</i> through life.</p>
<p style="margin-left: 0.9em; "> </p>
<p style="margin-left: 0.9em; "> 4. ART, which provides infallible rules to reason by. </p>
