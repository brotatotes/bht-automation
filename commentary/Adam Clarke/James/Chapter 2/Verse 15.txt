<p>Verse 15. <i><b>If a brother or sister be naked</b></i> — That is, ill-clothed; for γυμνος, <i>naked</i>, has this meaning in several parts of the New Testament, signifying bad clothing, or the want of some particular article of dress. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:36">Matthew 25:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:38">Matthew 25:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:43">Matthew 25:43</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:44">Matthew 25:44</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:7">John 21:7</span>. It has the same <i>comparative</i> signification in most languages.</p>
