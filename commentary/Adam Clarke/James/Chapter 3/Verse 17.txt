<p>Verse 17. <i><b>The wisdom that is from above</b></i> — The pure religion of the Lord Jesus, bought by his blood, and infused by his Spirit. See the rabbinical meaning of this phrase at the end of this chapter.</p>
<p><i><b>Is first pure</b></i> — αγνη. <i>Chaste, holy</i>, and <i>clean</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>Peaceable</b></i> — ειρηνικη. Living in peace with others, and promoting peace among men.</p>
<p><i><b>Gentle</b></i> — επιεικης. <i>Meek, modest</i>, of an <i>equal mind</i>, taking every thing in good part, and putting the best construction upon all the actions of others.</p>
<p><i><b>Easy to be entreated</b></i> — ευπειθης. Not stubborn nor obstinate; of a yielding disposition in all indifferent things; obsequious, docile.</p>
<p><i><b>Full of mercy</b></i> — Ready to pass by a transgression, and to grant forgiveness to those who offend, and performing every possible act of kindness.</p>
<p><i><b>Good fruits</b></i> — Each temper and disposition producing fruits suited to and descriptive of its nature.</p>
<p><i><b>Without partiality</b></i> — αδιακριτος. <i>Without making a</i> <i>difference</i>-rendering to every man his due; and being never swayed by self-interest, worldly honour, or the fear of man; knowing no man after the flesh. One of the <i>Itala</i> has it <i>irreprehensible</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>Without hypocrisy.</b></i> — ανυποκριτος. <i>Without dissimulation</i>; without <i>pretending to be what it is not</i>; acting always in <i>its own</i> <i>character</i>; never <i>working under a mask</i>. Seeking nothing but God's glory, and using no other means to attain it than those of his own prescribing.</p>
