<p>Verse 45. <i><b>Nathanael</b></i> — This apostle is supposed to be the same with <i>Bartholomew</i>, which is very likely, for these reasons</p>
<p>1. That the evangelists who mention <i>Bartholomew</i> say nothing of <i>Nathanael</i>; and that St. John, who speaks of <i>Nathanael</i>, says nothing of <i>Bartholomew</i>.</p>
<p>2. No notice is taken any where of Bartholomew's vocation, unless his and that of Nathanael mentioned here be the same.</p>
<p>3. The name of Bartholomew is not a <i>proper</i> name; it signifies the <i>son of Ptolomy</i>; and Nathanael might have been his own name.</p>
<p>4. St. John seems to rank Nathanael with the apostles, when he says that Peter and Thomas, the two sons of Zebedee, Nathanael, and two other disciples, being gone a fishing, Jesus showed himself to them, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:2-4">John 21:2-4</span>.</p>
