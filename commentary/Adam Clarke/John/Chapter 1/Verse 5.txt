<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:5">John 1:5</span>. <i><b>And the light shineth in darkness</b></i> — By <i>darkness</i> here may be understood:</p>
<p>1. The heathen world, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:8">Ephesians 5:8</span>.</p>
<p>2. The Jewish people.</p>
<p>3. The fallen spirit of man.</p>
<p><i><b>Comprehended it not.</b></i> — αυτο ου κατελαβεν, <i>Prevented it</i> <i>not-hindered it not</i>, says Mr. Wakefield, who adds the following judicious note:-"Even in the midst of that darkness of ignorance and idolatry which overspread the world, this light of Divine wisdom was not totally eclipsed: the Jewish nation was a lamp perpetually shining to the surrounding nations; and many bright luminaries, among the <i>heathen</i>, were never wanting in just and worthy notions of the attributes and providence of God's wisdom; which enabled them to shine in some degree, though but <i>as lights</i> <i>in a dark place</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:19">2 Peter 1:19</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:17">Acts 14:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:28-29">Acts 17:28-29</span>."</p>
