<p>Verse 24. <i><b>How long dost than make us to doubt?</b></i> — Or, <i>How long</i> <i>dost thou kill us with suspense</i>? Ἑως ποτε την ψυχην ἡμων αιρεις, literally, <i>How long wilt thou take away our life</i>? Mr. <i>Markland</i> would read αιωρεις for αιρεις, which amounts nearly to the same sense with the above. The Jews asked this question through extreme perfidiousness: they wished to get him to declare himself king of the Jews, that they might accuse him to the Roman governor; and by it they insolently insinuated that all the proofs he had hitherto given them of his Divine mission were good for nothing.</p>
