<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:20">John 11:20</span>. <i><b>Martha - went and met him</b></i> — Some suppose she was the eldest of the two sisters - she seems to have had the management of the house. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:40">Luke 10:40</span>.</p>
<p><i><b>Mary sat</b></i><b> still </b><i><b>in the house.</b></i> — It is likely that by this circumstance the evangelist intended to convey the idea of her sorrow and distress; because anciently afflicted persons were accustomed to put themselves in this posture, as expressive of their distress; their grief having rendered them as it were immovable. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ezr+9:3-4">Ezra 9:3-4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ne+1:4">Nehemiah 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ps+137:1">Psalms 137:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="isa+47:1">Isaiah 47:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:79">Luke 1:79</span>; and <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:61">Matthew 27:61</span>.</p>
