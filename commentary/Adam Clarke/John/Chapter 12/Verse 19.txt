<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:19">John 12:19</span>. <i><b>Ye prevail nothing</b></i> — Either by your <i>threatening</i> or <i>excommunications</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>The world is gone after him.</b></i> — The whole mass of the people are becoming his disciples. This is a very common form of expression among the Jews, and simply answers to the French, <i>tout le monde</i>, and to the English, <i>every body </i>- the bulk of the people. Many MSS., versions, and fathers, add ολος, <i>the WHOLE world</i>. As our Lord's converts were rapidly increasing, the Pharisees thought it necessary to execute without delay what they had purposed at their first council. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:53">John 11:53</span>.</p>
