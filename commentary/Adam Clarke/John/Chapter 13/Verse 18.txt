<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:18">John 13:18</span>. <i><b>I speak not of you all</b></i> — This is a continuation of that discourse which was left off at the tenth verse. The preceding verses may be read in a parenthesis.</p>
<p><i><b>I know whom I have chosen</b></i> — I am not deceived in my choice; I perfectly foresaw every thing that has happened, or can happen. I have chosen Judas, not as a wicked man, nor that he should become such; but I plainly foresaw that he would abuse my bounty, give way to iniquity, deliver me into the hands of my enemies, and bring ruin upon himself.</p>
<p><i><b>That the scripture may be fulfilled</b></i> — Or, <i>thus the scripture is</i> <i>fulfilled</i>. Christ applies to Judas what David had said of his rebellious son Absalom, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ps+41:9">Psalms 41:9</span>, who was one of the most express emblems of this traitor. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:38-39">John 12:38-39</span>.</p>
<p><i><b>He that eateth bread with me</b></i> — That is, he who was in habits of the utmost intimacy with me.</p>
<p><i><b>Hath lifted up his heel</b></i> — An allusion to a restive, ill-natured horse, that sometimes kicks even the person who feeds and takes care of him.</p>
