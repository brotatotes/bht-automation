<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:7">John 13:7</span>. <i><b>What I do thou knowest not now</b></i>, &amp;c.] As if our Lord had said, Permit me to do it now, and I will shortly explain to you the nature of this action, and my motives for doing it.</p>
<p><i><b>Thou shalt know hereafter.</b></i> — μετα ταυτα, after this business is finished. And so we find he explained the whole to them, as soon as he had finished the washing: see <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:12-17">John 13:12-17</span>. I cannot think that this refers to any particular instruction received on this head after the day of pentecost, as some have conjectured.</p>
