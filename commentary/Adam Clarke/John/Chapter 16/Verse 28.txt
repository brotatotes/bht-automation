<p>Verse 28. <i><b>I came forth from the Father</b></i> — With whom I existed from eternity in glory.</p>
<p><i><b>Am come into the world</b></i> — By my incarnation.</p>
<p><i><b>I leave the world</b></i> — By my death.</p>
<p><i><b>And go to the Father.</b></i> — By my ascension. These four words contain the whole economy of the Gospel of man's salvation, and a consummate <i>abridgment</i> of the Christian faith. This gave the disciples a <i>key</i> to the whole of our Lord's discourse; and especially to that part, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:16">John 16:16</span>, that had so exceedingly embarrassed them, as appears by <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:17-18">John 16:17-18</span>.</p>
