<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:10">John 18:10</span>. <i><b>Having a sword</b></i> — <span class="_0000ff"><span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:36">Luke 22:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:36">Luke 22:36</span></span>.</p>
<p><i><b>Cut off his right ear.</b></i> — He probably designed to have cloven his scull in two, but God turned it aside, and only permitted the ear to be taken off; and this he would not have suffered, but only that he might have the opportunity of giving them a most striking proof of his Divinity in working an astonishing miracle on the occasion: see the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:51-56">Matthew 26:51-56</span>.</p>
<p>The other three evangelists mention this transaction; but neither give the name of <i>Peter</i> nor of <i>Malchus</i>, probably because both persons were <i>alive</i> when they wrote; but it is likely both had been long dead before St. John published his history.</p>
