<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:7">John 19:7</span>. <i><b>We have a law</b></i> — In <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="le+24:14-16">Leviticus 24:14-16</span>, we find that blasphemers of God were to be put to death; and the chief priests having charged Jesus with blasphemy, they therefore voted that he deserved to die. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:65-66">Matthew 26:65-66</span>. They might refer also to the law against false prophets, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="de+18:20">Deuteronomy 18:20</span>.</p>
<p><i><b>The Son of God.</b></i> — It is certain that the Jews understood this in a very peculiar sense. When Christ called himself <i>the Son of God</i>, they understood it to imply positive <i>equality</i> to the Supreme Being: and, if they were wrong, our Lord never attempted to correct them.</p>
