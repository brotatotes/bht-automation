<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:22">John 2:22</span>. <i><b>Remembered that he had said this unto them</b></i> — αυτοις, <i>to</i> <i>them</i>, is wanting in AEHLMS, Matt. BV, upwards of <i>one hundred</i> others; both the <i>Syriac; Persic, Arabic, Coptic, AEthiopic,</i> <i>Armenian, Slavonic, Vulgate</i>, and <i>Itala</i>. <i>Griesbach</i> has left it out of the text.</p>
<p><i><b>They believed the scripture</b></i> — The scripture which the evangelist immediately refers to may have been <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ps+16:10">Psalms 16:10</span>. Compare this with <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:31-32">Acts 2:31-32</span>, and with <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:35-37">Acts 13:35-37</span>. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ps+2:7">Psalms 2:7</span>, and compare it with <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:5">Hebrews 1:5</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:5">Hebrews 5:5</span>, and with <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:33">Acts 13:33</span>. They understood these scriptures in a sense in which they never before understood them.</p>
<p>It is the property of many prophecies never to be understood except by their accomplishment; but these are so marked that, when their fulfilment takes place, they cannot be misunderstood, or applied to any other event.</p>
