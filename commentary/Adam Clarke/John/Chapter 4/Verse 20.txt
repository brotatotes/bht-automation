<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:20">John 4:20</span>. <i><b>Worshipped in this mountain</b></i> â€” Probably pointing to Mount <i>Gerizim</i>, at the foot of which Sychar was situated. The patriarchs had worshipped here - Jacob builded an altar on this mountain, and worshiped the true God: see <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ge+22:2">Genesis 22:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ge+33:20">Genesis 33:20</span>. Thus she could say, <i>Our fathers worshipped in this mountain</i>. On this mountain Sanballat had built them a temple, about 332 years before our Lord's incarnation. See Joseph. Antiq. xi. c. viii. s. 4, and 2 Macc. <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:2">John 6:2</span>.</p>
<p>Many heathens considered particular places as having a peculiar <i>sanctity</i> or <i>fitness</i>, for the worship of their deities, beyond others. Such places abound in <i>Hindostan</i>; and in <i>them</i> they think <i>men ought to worship</i>.</p>
<p>In the Hebrew Pentateuch, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="de+27:4">Deuteronomy 27:4</span>, c., where the Israelites are commanded to build an altar on mount EBAL, and offer sacrifices, &amp;c., the Samaritan Pentateuch has GERIZIM instead of <i>Ebal</i> and Dr. Kennicott strongly contends, Dissert. vol. ii. p. 20, &amp;c., that <i>Gerizim</i> is the genuine reading: but our blessed Lord, by the following answer, shows that the <i>place</i> was a matter of little importance, as the Divine worship was no longer to be confined to either: <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:21">John 4:21</span>. <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="de+27:4">Deuteronomy 27:4</span>.</p>
