<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:19">John 6:19</span>. <i><b>Had rowed</b></i> — Their vessel was a small one only, something of the boat kind: as to <i>sails</i>, if they had any, they could not now venture to carry them, because of the storm.</p>
<p><i><b>Five and twenty or thirty furlongs</b></i> — Between three and four miles. The sea of Tiberias, on which they now were, was, according to Josephus, War, book iii. chap. 25, <i>forty</i> furlongs, or <i>five</i> miles in breadth; and <i>one hundred</i> and <i>forty</i> furlongs, or <i>eighteen</i> miles, in length. <i>Pliny</i>, lib. v. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:0">John 15:0</span>, makes it about <i>six</i> miles broad, and <i>sixteen</i> long.</p>
