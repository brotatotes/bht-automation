<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:5">John 6:5</span>. <i><b>Saw a great company</b></i> — See this miracle explained at large on <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:13">Matthew 14:13</span>, c. <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:31">Mark 6:31</span>, c. <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:10">Luke 9:10</span>, c.</p>
<p>In speaking of the passovers, and various other matters, it does not appear that John follows any strict <i>chronological</i> order.</p>
<p>From <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:15">John 6:15</span>, it appears that our Lord had come down from the mountain, and fed the multitudes in a plain at the foot of it.</p>
<p><i><b>Saith unto Philip</b></i> — This, with what follows, to the end of <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:7">John 6:7</span> the seventh verse, is not mentioned by any of the other evangelists.</p>
<p><i>Philip</i> was probably the <i>provider</i> for the disciples, as <i>Judas</i> was the <i>treasurer</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>Whence shall we buy bread</b></i> — Instead of αγορασομεν, <i>shall we buy</i>, I should read αγορασωμεν, <i>may we buy</i>, which is the reading of ABDEHLS, Mt. VB, and many others. As Philip was of <i>Bethsaida</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:44">John 1:44</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:21">John 12:21</span>, he must have been much better acquainted with the country in which they then were than any other of the disciples.</p>
