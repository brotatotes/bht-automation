<p>Verse 39. <i><b>Was not yet</b></i> given] δεδομενον, <i>given</i> is added by the <i>Codex Vaticanus</i>, (B.) the <i>Syriac</i>, all the <i>Persic</i>, later <i>Syriac</i> with an asterisk, three copies of the <i>Slavonic, Vulgate</i>, and all the <i>Itala</i> but three; and several of the primitive fathers. The word seems necessary to the completion of the sense.</p>
<p>Certain measures of the Holy Spirit had been vouchsafed from the beginning of the world to believers and unbelievers: but that <i>abundant effusion</i> of his graces spoken of by Joel, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joe+2:28">Joel 2:28</span>, which peculiarly characterized the Gospel times, was not granted till after the ascension of Christ: 1. Because this Spirit in its plenitude was to come in consequence of his atonement; and therefore could not come till <i>after</i> his <i>crucifixion</i>. 2. It was to supply the place of Christ to his disciples and to all true believers; and therefore it was not necessary till after the removal of his bodily presence from among them. See our Lord's own words, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:16-18">John 14:16-18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:26">John 14:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:26">John 15:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:7-15">John 16:7-15</span>.</p>
