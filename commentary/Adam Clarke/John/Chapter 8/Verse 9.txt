<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:9">John 8:9</span>. <i><b>Being convicted by</b></i><b> their own </b><i><b>conscience</b></i> — So it is likely they were all guilty of similar crimes. <i>Their own</i> is not in the original, and is needless: <i>being convicted by conscience</i> is expressive enough.</p>
<p><i><b>Beginning at the eldest</b></i><b> even </b><i><b>unto the last</b></i> — Απο των πρεσβυτερων ἑως των εσχατων, <i>from the most honourable to those of the least</i> <i>repute</i>. In this sense the words are undoubtedly to be understood.</p>
<p><i><b>The woman standing in the midst.</b></i> — But if they <i>all</i> went out, how could she be in the midst? It is not said that <i>all the people</i> whom our Lord had been instructing went out, but only her <i>accusers</i>: see <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:2">John 8:2</span>. The rest undoubtedly continued with their teacher.</p>
