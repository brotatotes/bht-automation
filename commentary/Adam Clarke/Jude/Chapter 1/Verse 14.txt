<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:14">Jude 1:14</span>. <i><b>Enoch also, the seventh from Adam</b></i> — He was the <i>seventh</i> patriarch, and is distinguished thus from <i>Enoch, son of</i> <i>Cain</i>, who was but the <i>third</i> from Adam; this appears plainly from the genealogy, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1ch+1:1">1 Chronicles 1:1</span>: Adams Seth, Enosh, Kenan, Mahalaleel, Jered, Henoch or <i>Enoch</i>, c. Of the <i>book of Enoch</i>, from which this prophecy is thought to have been taken, much has been said but as the work is <i>apocryphal</i>, and of no authority, I shall not burden my page with <i>extracts</i>. See the <i>preface</i>.</p>
<p>Perhaps the word προεφητευσε, <i>prophesied</i>, means no more than <i>preached, spoke</i>, made <i>declarations</i>, c., concerning these things and persons for doubtless he reproved the ungodliness of his own times. It is certain that a <i>book</i> of <i>Enoch</i> was known in the earliest ages of the primitive Church, and is quoted by <i>Origen</i> and <i>Tertullian</i>; and is mentioned by St. <i>Jerome</i> in the <i>Apostolical</i> <i>Constitutions</i>, by <i>Nicephorus, Athanasius</i>, and probably by St. <i>Augustine</i>. See <i>Suicer's</i> Thesaurus, vol. i., col. 1131. Such a work is still extant among the <i>Abyssinians</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>Ten thousand of his saints</b></i> — This seems to be taken from <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="da+7:10">Daniel 7:10</span>.</p>
