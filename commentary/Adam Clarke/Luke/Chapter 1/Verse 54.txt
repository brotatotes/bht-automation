<p>Verse 54. <i><b>He hath holpen</b></i> [<i>supported</i>, αντελαβετο]<b> </b><i><b>his servant</b></i><b> </b><i><b>Israel</b></i> — Israel is here represented as <i>falling</i>, and the Lord comes speedily in and <i>props</i> him up. The house of David was now ready to <i>fall</i> and rise no more; Jesus, being born of the very <i>last branch</i> of the regal line, revived the family, and restored the dominion.</p>
<p><i><b>In remembrance of</b></i><b> his </b><i><b>mercy</b></i> — By <i>mercy</i>, the covenant which God made with Abraham, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ge+15:18">Genesis 15:18</span>, is intended; which covenant proceeded from God's eternal mercy, as in it salvation was promised to all the nations of the earth. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ge+17:19">Genesis 17:19</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ge+22:18">Genesis 22:18</span>, and this promise was, in one form or other, given to <i>all</i> <i>the fathers</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:55">Luke 1:55</span>.</p>
<p>This song properly consists of <i>three</i> parts.</p>
<p>1. In the <i>first</i> part Mary praises God for what he had done for <i>herself</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:46-50">Luke 1:46-50</span>.</p>
<p>2. In the <i>second</i>, she praises him for what he had done, and would do, against the oppressors of <i>his people</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:51-53">Luke 1:51-53</span>.</p>
<p>3. In the <i>third</i>, she praises him for what he had done, and would do, for his <i>Church</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:53-56">Luke 1:53-56</span>.</p>
