<p>Verse 34. <i><b>Pouring in oil and wine</b></i> — These, beaten together, appear to have been used formerly as a common medicine for fresh wounds. Bind up a fresh cut immediately in a soft rag or lint, moistened with pure olive oil, and the parts will heal by what is called the <i>first intention</i>, and more speedily than by any other means.</p>
<p><i><b>An inn</b></i> — πανδοχειον, from παν, <i>all</i>, and δεχομαι, <i>I</i> <i>receive</i>; because it <i>receives all comers</i>.</p>
