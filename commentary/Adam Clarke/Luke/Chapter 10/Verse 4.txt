<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:4">Luke 10:4</span>. <i><b>Carry neither purse nor scrip</b></i> — <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:9">Matthew 10:9</span>, c., and <span class="_0000ff"><span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:8">Mark 6:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:8">Mark 6:8</span></span>, &amp;c.</p>
<p><i><b>Salute no man by the way.</b></i> — According to a canon of the Jews, a man who was about any sacred work was exempted from all <i>civil</i> obligations for the time forasmuch as obedience to God was of infinitely greater consequence than the cultivation of private friendships, or the returning of civil compliments.</p>
