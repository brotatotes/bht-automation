<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:6">Luke 10:6</span>. <i><b>The son of peace</b></i> â€” In the Jewish style, a man who has any <i>good</i> or <i>bad</i> quality is called <i>the son</i> of it. Thus, wise men are called <i>the children of wisdom</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:19">Matthew 11:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:35">Luke 7:35</span>. So, likewise, what a man is <i>doomed</i> to, he is called <i>the son</i> of, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:3">Ephesians 2:3</span>, wicked men are styled the <i>children of wrath</i>: so Judas is called <i>the son of perdition</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:12">John 17:12</span>; and a man who <i>deserves to die</i> is called, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="2sa+12:5">2 Samuel 12:5</span>, a <i>son of death</i>. <i>Son of peace</i> in the text not only means a <i>peaceable, quiet</i> man, but one also of <i>good report</i> for his <i>uprightness</i> and <i>benevolence</i>. It would have been a dishonour to this mission, had the missionaries taken up their lodgings with those who had not a good report among them who were without.</p>
