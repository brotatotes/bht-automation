<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:50">Luke 11:50</span>. <i><b>That the blood</b></i> — That the particle ινα may be translated <i>so that</i>, pointing out the <i>event</i> only, not the <i>design</i> or <i>intention</i>, Bishop Pearce has well shown in his note on this place, where he refers to a like use of the word in <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:45">Luke 9:45</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:10">Luke 14:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:17">John 10:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:20">Romans 5:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:11">Romans 11:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:15">1 Corinthians 1:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:31">1 Corinthians 1:31</span>, &amp;c.</p>
