<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:1">Luke 12:1</span>. <i><b>An innumerable multitude of people</b></i> — Των μυριαδων του οχλου, <i>myriads of people</i>. A myriad is ten thousand, and myriads must, at the very lowest, mean twenty thousand. But the word is often used to signify a crowd or multitude which cannot be readily numbered. There was doubtless a vast crowd assembled on this occasion, and many of them were deeply instructed by the very important discourse which our Lord delivered.</p>
<p><i><b>Leaven of the Pharisees</b></i> — See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:1-12">Matthew 16:1-12</span>.</p>
<p><i><b>Which is hypocrisy.</b></i> — These words are supposed by some to be an addition to the text, because it does not appear that it is their <i>hypocrisy</i> which Christ alludes to, but their <i>false doctrines</i>. They had, however, a large proportion of <i>both</i>.</p>
