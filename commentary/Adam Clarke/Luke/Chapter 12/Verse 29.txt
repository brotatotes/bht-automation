<p>Verse 29. <i><b>Neither be ye of doubtful mind.</b></i> — Or, <i>in anxious</i> <i>suspense</i>, μη μετεωριζεσθε. Raphelius gives several examples to prove that the meaning of the word is, <i>to have the mind agitated</i> <i>with useless thoughts, and vain imaginations concerning food,</i> <i>raiment, and riches, accompanied with perpetual uncertainty</i>.</p>
