<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:35">Luke 13:35</span>. <i><b>Your house</b></i> — Ὁ οικος, <i>the temple</i>-called here <i>your</i> <i>house</i>, not <i>my house </i>- I acknowledge it no longer; I have abandoned it, and will dwell in it no more for ever. So he said, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="2ch+36:17">2 Chronicles 36:17</span>, when he delivered the temple into the hands of the Chaldeans - <i>the</i> <i>house of YOUR sanctuary</i>. A similar form of speech is found, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ex+32:7">Exodus 32:7</span>, where the Lord said to Moses, <i>THY people</i>, c., to intimate that he acknowledged them no longer for his followers. See the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:21">Matthew 23:21</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:38">Matthew 23:38</span>. But some think that our Lord means, not the <i>temple</i>, but the whole commonwealth of the Jews.</p>
<p>The principal subjects it this chapter may be found considered at large, on the parallel places in Matthew and Mark, to which the reader is referred. As to the account of the woman with the spirit of infirmity, which is not mentioned by any other of the evangelists, see it largely illustrated in the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:11">Luke 13:11</span>, &amp;c.</p>
