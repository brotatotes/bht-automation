<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:6">Luke 13:6</span>. <i><b>A certain</b></i> man] Many meanings are given to this parable, and divines may abound in them; the sense which our Lord designed to convey by it appears to be the following: -</p>
<p>1. <i>A person</i>, τις, God Almighty.</p>
<p>2. <i>Had a fig tree</i>, the Jewish Church.</p>
<p>3. <i>Planted in his vineyard </i>- established in the land of Judea.</p>
<p>4. <i>He came seeking fruit </i>- he required that the Jewish people should walk in righteousness, in proportion to the spiritual culture he bestowed on them.</p>
<p>5. <i>The vine-dresser </i>- the Lord Jesus, for God hath committed all judgment to the Son, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:22">John 5:22</span>.</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:6">John 5:6</span>. <i>Cut it down </i>- let the Roman sword be unsheathed against it.</p>
<p>7. <i>Let</i> <i>it alone </i>- Christ is represented as <i>intercessor</i> for sinners, for whose sake the day of their probation is often lengthened; during which time he is constantly employed in doing every thing that has a tendency to promote their salvation.</p>
<p>8. <i>Thou shalt cut it down </i>- a time will come, that those who have not turned at God's invitations and reproofs shall be cut off, and numbered with the transgressors.</p>
