<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:10">Luke 18:10</span>. <i><b>A Pharisee</b></i> — For a description of the Pharisees and their tenets, <span class="_0000ff"><span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:1">Matthew 16:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:1">Matthew 16:1</span></span>.</p>
<p><i><b>Publican.</b></i> — See an account of these on <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:46">Matthew 5:46</span>. Both these persons <i>went to the temple to pray</i>, i.e. to worship God: they were probably both <i>Jews</i>, and felt themselves led by different motives to attend at the temple, at the <i>hour</i> of <i>prayer</i>: the one to return <i>thanks</i> for the mercies he had received; the other to implore that grace which alone could redeem him from his sins.</p>
