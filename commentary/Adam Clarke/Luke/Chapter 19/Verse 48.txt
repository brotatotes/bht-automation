<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:48">Luke 19:48</span>. <i><b>Were very attentive to hear him.</b></i> — Or, <i>They heard him</i> <i>with the utmost attention</i>, εξεκρεματο αυτου ακουων, literally, <i>They hung upon him, hearing</i>. The same form of speech is used often by both Greek and Latin writers of the best repute. </p>
<p>THE reader is requested to refer to <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:0">Matthew 24:0</span>, and to <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:14">Matthew 25:14</span>, for more extensive information on the different subjects in this chapter, and to the other parallel places marked in the margin. The prophecy relative to the destruction of Jerusalem is one of the most circumstantial, and the most literally fulfilled, of any prediction ever delivered. See this particularly remarked at the conclusion of <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:0">Matthew 24:0</span>, where the whole subject is amply reviewed.</p>
