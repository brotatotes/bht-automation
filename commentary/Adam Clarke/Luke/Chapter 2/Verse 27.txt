<p>Verse 27. <i><b>He came by the Spirit into the temple</b></i> — Probably he had in view the prophecy of Malachi, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mal+3:1">Malachi 3:1</span>, <i>The Lord, whom ye seek, shall suddenly come to his temple</i>. In this <i>messenger</i> of the <i>covenant</i>, the soul of Simeon <i>delighted</i>. Now the prophecy was just going to be fulfilled; and the Holy Spirit, who dwelt in the soul of this righteous man, directed him to go and see its accomplishment. Those who come, under the influence of God's Spirit, to places of public worship, will undoubtedly meet with him who is the <i>comfort</i> and <i>salvation</i> of Israel.</p>
<p><i><b>After the custom of the law</b></i> — To present him to the Lord, and then redeem him by paying <i>five shekels</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="nu+18:15-16">Numbers 18:15-16</span>, and to offer those sacrifices appointed by the law. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:24">Luke 2:24</span>.</p>
