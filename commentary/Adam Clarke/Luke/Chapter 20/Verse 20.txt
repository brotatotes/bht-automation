<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:20">Luke 20:20</span>. <i><b>They watched him</b></i> — παρατηρησαντες, <i>Insidiously</i> <i>watching</i>. <span class="_0000ff"><span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:1">Luke 14:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:1">Luke 14:1</span></span>.</p>
<p><i><b>Spies</b></i> — εγκαθετους, from εν, <i>in</i>, and καθιημι, <i>I let</i> <i>down, to set in ambush</i>. One who <i>crouches</i> in some secret place to <i>spy, listen, catch</i>, or <i>hurt</i>. <i>Hesychius</i> explains the word by ενεδρευοντες, <i>those who lie in wait</i>, or in <i>ambush</i>, to surprise and slay. <i>Josephus</i> uses the word to signify a person <i>bribed</i> for a particular purpose. See War, b. ii. c. ii. s. 5, and b. vi. c. v. s. 2. No doubt the persons mentioned in the text were men of the basest principles, and were <i>hired</i> by the malicious Pharisees to do what they attempted in vain to perform.</p>
