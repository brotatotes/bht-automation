<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:19">Luke 22:19</span>. <i><b>Took bread</b></i> — See the nature and design of the Lord's Supper explained in Clarke's notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:26-29">Matthew 26:26-29</span>.</p>
<p><i><b>This do in remembrance of me.</b></i> — That the Jews, in eating the passover, did it to represent the sufferings of the Messiah, as evident from the tract <i>Pesachim</i>, fol. 119, quoted by <i>Schoettgen</i>. <i>Why do we call this the great hallel</i>? (i.e. the hymn composed of several psalms, which they sung after the paschal supper.) Ans. Because in it these five things are contained:</p>
<p>1. The exodus from Egypt.</p>
<p>2. The dividing of the Red Sea.</p>
<p>3. The promulgation of the law.</p>
<p>4. The resurrection of the dead. And,</p>
<p>5. The sufferings of the Messiah.</p>
<p>The <i>first</i> is referred to, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ps+114:1">Psalms 114:1</span>, <i>When Israel went out of Egypt</i>, c. The <i>second</i> in <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ps+114:3">Psalms 114:3</span>, <i>The sea saw it and fled</i>. The <i>third</i> in <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ps+114:4">Psalms 114:4</span>, <i>The mountains skipped like rams</i>, &amp;c. The <i>fourth</i> in <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ps+116:9">Psalms 116:9</span>, <i>I will walk before the Lord in the land of the living</i>. The <i>fifth</i> in <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ps+115:1">Psalms 115:1</span>, <i>Not unto us, O Lord, not unto us, but unto thy name give glory</i> <i>for thy mercy and thy truth's sake</i>. <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:30">Matthew 26:30</span>.</p>
