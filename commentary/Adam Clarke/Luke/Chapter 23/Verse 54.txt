<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:54">Luke 23:54</span>. <i><b>And the Sabbath drew on.</b></i> — Or, <i>The Sabbath was lighting</i> <i>up</i>, επεφωσκε, i.e. with the <i>candles</i> which the <i>Jews</i> light just before six in the evening, when the <i>Sabbath</i> commences. The same word is used for the dawning of the day, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:1">Matthew 28:1</span>. <i>Wakefield</i>. The Jews always lighted up candles on the Sabbath; and it was a solemn precept that, "if a man had not bread to eat, he must beg from door to door to get a little oil to set up his Sabbath light." The night of the Sabbath drew on, which the Jews were accustomed to call the <i>light</i>. See <i>Lightfoot</i>.</p>
