<p>Verse 29. <i><b>For it is toward evening</b></i> — And consequently both inconvenient and unsafe to proceed to another village. Reader! it is probably the <i>eve</i> of thy life, whether thou be <i>old</i> or <i>young</i>: thy day may have <i>already declined</i>, and there is, possibly, but <i>a</i> <i>step</i> between thee and the eternal world! Hath the Lord Jesus <i>taught</i> thee by his <i>word</i> and <i>Spirit</i> to believe in him, that thou mightest be saved? Is he come into thy heart? Hast thou the <i>witness</i> of his <i>Spirit</i> that thy <i>sin</i> is <i>blotted out</i> through his blood? <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:16">Romans 8:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:6">Galatians 4:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:10-12">1 John 5:10-12</span>. If thou have not, get thee to God right humbly. Jesus is about to <i>pass by</i>, perhaps for ever! O, <i>constrain</i> him, by earnest <i>faith</i> and <i>prayer</i>, to <i>enter</i> into thy soul, and <i>lodge</i> with <i>thee</i>! May God <i>open</i> THY <i>eyes</i>! May he stir up and <i>inflame</i> THY heart!</p>
<p><i><b>And he went in</b></i> — And so he will to <i>thee</i>, thou penitent soul! Therefore take courage, and be not faithless but believing.</p>
