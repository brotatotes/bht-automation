<p>Verse 47. <i><b>Repentance</b></i> — See its nature fully explained on <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:1">Matthew 3:1</span>.</p>
<p><i><b>Remission of sins</b></i> — αφεσιν αμαρτιων, The <i>taking away </i>- removal <i>of</i> <i>sins</i>, in general every thing that relates to the <i>destruction</i> of the <i>power</i>, the <i>pardoning</i> of the <i>guilt</i>, and the <i>purification</i> of the heart from the very <i>nature</i> of sin.</p>
<p><i><b>Should be preached in his name</b></i> — See the office of a <i>proclaimer,</i> <i>herald</i>, or <i>preacher</i>, explained, <i>Clarke's note on "</i><span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:1">Matthew 3:1</span><i>"</i>, and particularly at the end of that chapter. <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:1- :">Matthew 3:1- :</span></p>
<p><i>In his name </i>- On his authority, and in virtue of the atonement made by him: for on what other ground could the inhabitants of the earth expect <i>remission of sins</i>?</p>
<p><i><b>Among all nations</b></i> — Because God wills the salvation of ALL; and Jesus Christ by his grace has tasted death for EVERY <i>man</i>. <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:9">Hebrews 2:9</span>.</p>
<p><i><b>Beginning at Jerusalem</b></i> — Making the first overtures of mercy to my murderers! If, then, the sinners of Jerusalem might repent, believe, and be saved, none, on this side hell, need despair.</p>
