<p>Verse 33. <i><b>A spirit of an unclean devil</b></i> — As <i>demon</i> was used both in a <i>good</i> and <i>bad</i> sense before and after the time of the evangelists the word <i>unclean</i> may have been added here by St. Luke, merely to express the quality of <i>this</i> spirit. But it is worthy of remark, that the inspired writers never use the word δαιμων, <i>demon</i>, in a <i>good</i> sense. See the whole of this case explained, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:23">Mark 1:23</span>, &amp;c.</p>
