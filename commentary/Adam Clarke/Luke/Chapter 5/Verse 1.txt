<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:1">Luke 5:1</span>. <i><b>The people pressed upon him</b></i> — There was a glorious prospect of a plentiful harvest, but how few of these <i>blades</i> came to <i>full corn in the ear</i>! To hear with <i>diligence</i> and <i>affection</i> is well; but a preacher of the Gospel may expect that, out of crowds of hearers, only a few, comparatively, will fully receive the truth, and hold out to the end.</p>
<p><i><b>To hear the word of God</b></i> — Του λογον του Θεου, <i>The doctrine of</i> <i>God</i>, or, <i>the heavenly doctrine</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>The lake of Gennesaret</b></i> — Called also the <i>sea of Galilee</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:18">Matthew 4:18</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:16">Mark 1:16</span>; and the <i>sea of Tiberias</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:1">John 6:1</span>. It was, according to <i>Josephus</i>, forty furlongs in breadth, and one hundred and forty in length. No synagogue could have contained the multitudes who attended our Lord's ministry; and therefore he was obliged to preach in the open air. But this also some of the most eminent rabbins were in the habit of doing; though among some of their brethren it was not deemed reputable.</p>
