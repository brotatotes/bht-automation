<p>Verse 29. <i><b>A great feast</b></i> — δοχην μεγαλην, <i>A splendid</i> <i>entertainment</i>. The word refers more properly to the <i>number</i> of the <i>guests</i>, and the manner in which they were <i>received</i>, than to the <i>quality</i> or <i>quantity</i> of the <i>fare</i>. A great number of his friends and acquaintance was collected on the occasion, that they might be convinced of the propriety of the change he had made, when they had the opportunity of seeing and hearing his heavenly teacher.</p>
