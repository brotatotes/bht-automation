<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:4">Mark 1:4</span>. <i><b>John</b></i> — The original name is <i>nearly</i> lost in the Greek ιωαννης, and in the Latin <i>Johannes</i>, and almost <i>totally</i> so in the English <i>John</i>. The original name is יהוחנן <i>Yehochanan</i>, compounded of יהוה חנן <i>Yehovah chanan</i>, the <i>grace</i> or <i>mercy of Jehovah</i>: a most proper and significant name for the forerunner of the <i>God of</i> ALL GRACE. It was John's business to proclaim the <i>Gospel of the </i> <i>grace of God</i>, and to point out that <i>Lamb</i> or <i>sacrifice of God</i> <i>which takes away the sin of the world</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>For the remission of sins.</b></i> — Or, <i>toward the remission </i>- εις αφεσιν. They were to repent, and be baptized in <i>reference</i> to the remission of sins. REPENTANCE <i>prepared</i> the soul for it, and BAPTISM was the <i>type</i> or <i>pledge</i> of it. <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:2">Matthew 3:2</span>.</p>
