<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:16">Mark 11:16</span>. <i><b>Should carry</b></i><b> any </b><i><b>vessel</b></i> — Among the Jews the word <i>keli, vessel</i>, had a vast latitude of meaning; it signified <i>arms</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="jer+21:4">Jeremiah 21:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="eze+9:1">Ezekiel 9:1</span>; <i>clothes</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="de+22:5">Deuteronomy 22:5</span>, and <i>instruments of music</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ps+71:22">Psalms 71:22</span>. It is likely that the evangelist uses the Greek word σκευος in the same sense, and by it points out any of the <i>things</i> which were <i>bought</i> and <i>sold</i> in the temple.</p>
