<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:9">Mark 13:9</span>. <i><b>Councils</b></i> — συνεδρια, <i>Sanhedrins</i>. The <i>grand</i> <i>Sanhedrin</i> consisted of seventy-two elders; six chosen out of each tribe; this was the <i>national council</i> of state; and the <i>small</i> <i>Sanhedrins</i>, which were composed of twenty-three counsellors.</p>
<p><i><b>Synagogues</b></i> — <i>Courts</i> of <i>justice</i> for <i>villages</i>, &amp;c., consisting of three <i>magistrates</i>, chosen out of the principal directors of the synagogue in that place.</p>
<p><i><b>Rulers</b></i> — Or <i>governors</i>. The Roman <i>deputies</i>, such as Pontius Pilate, &amp;c.</p>
<p><i><b>Kings</b></i> — The <i>tetrarchs</i> of Judea and Galilee, who bore this name. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:27">Mark 6:27</span>.</p>
