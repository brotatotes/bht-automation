<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:3">Matthew 10:3</span>. <i><b>Bartholomew</b></i> — Many are of opinion that this was <i>Nathanael</i>, mentioned <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:46">John 1:46</span>, whose name was probably <i>Nathanael bar Talmai</i>, Nathanael, the son of <i>Talmai</i>: here, his own name is repressed, and he is called <i>Bar Talmai</i>, or <i>Bartholomew</i>, from his <i>father</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>Matthew the publican</b></i> — The writer of this history. See the preface.</p>
<p><i><b>James</b></i><b> the son </b><i><b>of Alpheus</b></i> — This person was also called <i>Cleopas</i>, or <i>Clopas</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:18">Luke 24:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:25">John 19:25</span>. He had married <i>Mary</i>, sister to the blessed Virgin, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:25">John 19:25</span>.</p>
