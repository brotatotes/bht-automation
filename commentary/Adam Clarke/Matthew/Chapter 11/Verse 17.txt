<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:17">Matthew 11:17</span>. <i><b>We have piped unto you, and ye have not danced</b></i> — We have begun the music, which should have been followed by the dance, but ye have not attended to it.</p>
<p><i><b>We have mourned - and ye have not lamented.</b></i> — <i>Ye have not smote</i> <i>the breast</i>: ουκ εκοψασθε, from κοπτομαι, <i>to strike</i>, or <i>beat</i> <i>the breasts with the hands</i>, particularly in <i>lamentation</i>. So used, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="na+2:7">Nahum 2:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:13">Luke 18:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:48">Luke 23:48</span>, and by the best Greek and Roman writers. There is an allusion here to those funeral lamentations explained <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:23">Matthew 9:23</span>.</p>
