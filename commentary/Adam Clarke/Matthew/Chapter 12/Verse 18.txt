<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:18">Matthew 12:18</span>. <i><b>Behold my servant</b></i> — This title was given to our blessed Lord in several prophecies. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="isa+42:1">Isaiah 42:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="isa+53:2">Isaiah 53:2</span>. Christ assumes it, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ps+40:7-9">Psalms 40:7-9</span>. Compare these with <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:4">John 17:4</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="php+2:7">Philippians 2:7</span>. God required an <i>acceptable</i> and <i>perfect service</i> from man; but man, being <i>sinful</i>, could not perform it. Jesus, taking upon him the nature of man, fully performed the whole will of God, and communicates grace to all his followers, to enable them perfectly to love and worthily to magnify their Maker.</p>
<p><i><b>And he shall show judgment to the Gentiles.</b></i> — That is, He will publish the Gospel to the heathens; for the word κρισιν here answers to the word משפט <i>mishpat</i> of the prophet, and it is used among the Hebrews to signify laws, precepts, and a whole system or body of doctrine. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ps+19:9">Psalms 19:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ps+119:30">Psalms 119:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ps+119:39">Psalms 119:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="isa+58:2">Isaiah 58:2</span>.</p>
