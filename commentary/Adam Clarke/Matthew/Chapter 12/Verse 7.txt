There are <i>four</i> ways in which <i>positive laws</i> may cease to oblige.</p>
<p>First, by the natural law of <i>necessity</i>.</p>
<p>Secondly, by a <i>particular law</i>, which is <i>superior</i>.</p>
<p>Thirdly, by the <i>law</i> of <i>charity</i> and <i>mercy</i>.</p>
<p>Fourthly, by the <i>dispensation</i> and <i>authority</i> of the <i>Lawgiver</i>.</p>
<p>These cases are all exemplified from <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:4-8">Matthew 12:4-8</span>.</p>
