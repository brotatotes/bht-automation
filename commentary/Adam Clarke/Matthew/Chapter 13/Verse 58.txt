<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:58">Matthew 13:58</span>. <i><b>And he did not many mighty works there because of</b></i><b> </b><i><b>their unbelief</b></i> — δυναμεις, <i>miracles</i>. So the word is used, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:22">Matthew 7:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:20">Matthew 11:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:11">Acts 19:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:28">1 Corinthians 12:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:5">Galatians 3:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:4">Hebrews 2:4</span>. The Septuagint translates נפלאות אל <i>niphleoth el, the miraculous</i> <i>works of God</i>, by δυναμιν κυριε.</p>
<p>Unbelief and contempt drive Christ out of the heart, as they did out of his own country. <i>Faith</i> seems to put the almighty power of God into the hands of men; whereas <i>unbelief</i> appears, to tie up even the hands of the Almighty. A man, generally speaking, can do but little good among his relatives, because it is difficult for them to look with the <i>eyes</i> of <i>faith</i> upon one whom they have been accustomed to behold with the <i>eyes</i> of the <i>flesh</i>. - QUESNEL.</p>
