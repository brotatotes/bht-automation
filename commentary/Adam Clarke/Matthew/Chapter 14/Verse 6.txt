<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:6">Matthew 14:6</span>. <i><b>Herod's birth-day</b></i> — Either the day in which he was <i>born</i>, or the day on which he <i>began to reign</i>; for both were termed <i>birth-days</i>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1sa+13:1">1 Samuel 13:1</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ho+7:5">Hosea 7:5</span>. The kings of Persia were accustomed to reject no petition that was preferred to them during the entertainment. See <i>Herodotus</i> in <i>Calliope</i>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="es+5:3">Esther 5:3</span>.</p>
<p><i><b>The daughter - danced</b></i> — This was <i>Salome</i>, mentioned before. <i>Danced</i>: by a literal rendering of the <i>saltavit</i> of the Vulgate, in my old MS. of the English Bible, the whole of this business seems to be treated with sovereign contempt: for thus says the translator, <i>Shee leped in the myddle</i>.</p>
