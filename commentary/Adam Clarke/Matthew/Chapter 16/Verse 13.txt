<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:13">Matthew 16:13</span>. <i><b>Casarea Philippi</b></i> — A city, in the tribe of Naphtali, near to Mount Libanus, in the province of <i>Iturea</i>. Its ancient name was <i>Dan</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ge+14:14">Genesis 14:14</span>; afterwards it was called <i>Lais</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="jdg+18:7">Judges 18:7</span>. But Philip the tetrarch, having rebuilt and beautified it, gave it the name of <i>Cesarea</i>, in honour of Tiberius Caesar, the reigning emperor: but to distinguish it from another Caesarea, which was on the coast of the Mediterranean Sea, and to perpetuate the fame of him who rebuilt it, it was called <i>Caesarea Philippi</i>, or <i>Caesarea</i> <i>of Philip</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>When Jesus came</b></i> — Ελθων δε ο Ιησους - <i>when Jesus was coming</i>. Not, <i>when Jesus came</i>, or <i>was come</i>, for Mark expressly mentions that it happened εν τη οδω, <i>in the way</i> to <i>Caesarea Philippi</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:27">Mark 8:27</span>, and he is Matthew's best interpreter.-WAKEFIELD.</p>
<p><i><b>Whom do men say</b></i> — He asked his disciples this question, not because he was ignorant what the people thought and spoke of him; but to have the opportunity, in getting an express declaration of their faith from themselves, to confirm and strengthen them in it: but see on <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:20">Luke 9:20</span>. <i>Some, John the Baptist, c</i>. By this and other passages we learn, that the Pharisaic doctrine of the <i>Metempsychosis</i>, or transmigration of souls, was pretty general for it was upon this ground that they believed that the soul of the <i>Baptist</i>, or of <i>Elijah, Jeremiah</i>, or some of the prophets, had come to a <i>new life</i> in the body of Jesus.</p>
