<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:1">Matthew 17:1</span>. <i><b>After six days</b></i> — <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:2">Mark 9:2</span>, has the same number; but Luke says, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:28">Luke 9:28</span>, after <i>eight</i> days. The reason of this difference seems to be the following: Matthew and Mark reckon the days <i>from</i> that mentioned in the preceding chapter, <i>to</i> that mentioned in this; Luke includes <i>both</i> days, as well as the <i>six</i> intermediate: hence, the one makes <i>eight</i>, the other <i>six</i>, without any contradiction.</p>
<p><i><b>Peter, James, and John</b></i> — He chose those that they might be witnesses of his transfiguration: two or three witnesses being required by the Scripture to substantiate any fact. Eminent communications of the Divine favour <i>prepare</i> for, and <i>entitle</i> to, great <i>services</i> and great <i>conflicts</i>. The same three were made witnesses of his <i>agony</i> in the garden, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:37">Matthew 26:37</span>.</p>
<p><i><b>A high mountain</b></i> — This was one of the mountains of <i>Galilee</i>; but whether Mount <i>Tabor</i> or not, is uncertain. Some think it was Mount <i>Hermon</i>. St. Luke says, Christ and his disciples went up into the mountain to <i>pray</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:28">Luke 9:28</span>.</p>
