<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:10">Matthew 17:10</span>. <i><b>His disciples</b></i> — instead of HIS disciples, some MSS., with the <i>Coptic, Armenian, Vulgate</i>, all the <i>Itala</i> except two, and <i>Origen</i>, read simply, οι μαθηται, THE <i>disciples</i>, i.e. those only who had been with him on the mount, <i>Peter, James</i>, and <i>John</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>Why then say the scribes that Elias must first come?</b></i> — As the disciples saw that <i>Elijah</i> returned to heaven, knowing the tradition of the elders, and the prophecy on which the tradition was founded, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mal+4:5-6">Malachi 4:5-6</span>, <i>Behold I send you Elijah the prophet, before the great and</i> <i>terrible day of the Lord shall come; and he shall turn the hearts,</i> <i>&amp;c.</i>, it was natural enough for them to inquire what the meaning of the <i>tradition</i>, and the intention of the <i>prophecy</i>, were.</p>
