<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:2">Matthew 17:2</span>. <i><b>Was transfigured</b></i> — That fulness of the Godhead, which dwelt bodily in Christ, now shone forth through the human nature, and manifested to his disciples not only that <i>Divinity</i> which Peter had before confessed, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:16">Matthew 16:16</span>, but also the glorious resurrection body, in which they should exist in the presence of God to eternity.</p>
<p><i><b>White as the light.</b></i> — But the Cod. Bezae, some of the ancient versions, and several of the fathers, read ως χιων, <i>as snow</i>; and this is the reading in <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:3">Mark 9:3</span>.</p>
