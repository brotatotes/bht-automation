<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:22">Matthew 17:22</span>. <i><b>They abode in Galilee</b></i> — Lower <i>Galilee</i>, where the city of <i>Capernaum</i> was.</p>
<p><i><b>The Son of man shall be betrayed into the hands of men</b></i> — Μελλει - παραδιδοσθαι εις χειρας - <i>The Son of man is about to be</i> <i>delivered into the hands</i>, c. I am fully of the mind of two eminent critics, <i>Grotius</i> and <i>Wakefield</i>, that παραδιδοσθαι should be here translated <i>delivered</i>, or <i>delivered up</i>, not <i>betrayed</i> and that the agency, in this case, should be referred to <i>God</i>, not to <i>Judas</i>. Jesus was <i>delivered up</i>, by the counsel of God, to be an atonement for the sin of the world. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:27-28">Acts 4:27-28</span>. <i>Against thy holy child Jesus, whom thou hast anointed to do what</i> <i>thy hand and thy counsel determined before to be done, Herod and</i> <i>Pontius Pilate-were gathered together</i>.</p>
