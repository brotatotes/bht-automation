<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:3">Matthew 17:3</span>. <i><b>Tell</b></i><b> it </b><i><b>unto the Church</b></i> — Lay the whole matter before the congregation of Christian believers, in that place of which he is a member, or before the <i>minister</i> and <i>elders</i>, as the <i>representatives</i> of the Church or assembly. If all this avail not, then,</p>
<p><i><b>Let him be unto thee as a heathen man and a publican.</b></i> — To whom thou art, as a Christian, to owe earnest and persevering good will, and acts of kindness; but have no <i>religious</i> communion with him, till, if he have been convicted, he <i>acknowledge</i> his fault. Whosoever follows this threefold rule will seldom offend others, and never be offended himself. - Rev. J. WESLEY.</p>
<p><i>Reproving</i> a brother who had sinned was a positive command under the law. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="le+19:17">Leviticus 19:17</span>. And the Jews have a saying, that one of the causes of the ruin of their nation was, "<i>No man reproved</i> <i>another</i>." On the word <i>Church</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:28">Matthew 16:28</span>.</p>
