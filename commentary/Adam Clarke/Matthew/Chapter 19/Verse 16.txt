<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:16">Matthew 19:16</span>. <i><b>One came</b></i> — Instead of εις one, several MSS., the Slavonic version, and <i>Hilary</i>, read νεανισκος τις, <i>a certain young</i> <i>man</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>Good</b></i>, c.] Much instruction may be had from seriously attending to the <i>conduct, spirit</i>, and <i>question</i> of this person.</p>
<p>1. He came running, (<span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:17">Mark 10:17</span>,) for he was deeply convinced of the importance of his business, and seriously determined to <i>seek</i> so as to find.</p>
<p>2. <i>He kneeled</i>, or caught him by the knees, thus evidencing his <i>humility</i>, and addressing himself only to <i>mercy</i>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:14">Matthew 17:14</span>.</p>
<p>3. He came in the spirit of a <i>disciple</i>, or <i>scholar</i>, desiring to be <i>taught</i> a matter of the utmost importance to him - <i>Good teacher</i>.</p>
<p>4. He came in the spirit of <i>obedience</i> he had worked hard to no purpose, and he is still willing to work, provided he can have a prospect of succeeding - <i>What good thing shall I do</i>?</p>
<p>5. His question was the most interesting and important that any soul can ask of God - <i>How shall I be saved</i>?</p>
