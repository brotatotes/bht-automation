<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:21">Matthew 19:21</span>. <i><b>If thou wilt be perfect</b></i> — τελειος ειναι, To be <i>complete</i>, to have the business <i>finished</i>, and all <i>hinderances</i> to thy salvation removed, <i>go and sell that thou hast</i>-go and dispose of thy possessions, to which it is evident his heart was too much attached, <i>and give to the poor </i>- for thy goods will be a continual snare to thee if thou keep them; <i>and thou shalt have treasure in</i> <i>heaven </i>- the loss, if it can be called such, shall be made amply up to thee in that eternal life about which thou inquirest; <i>and come</i> and <i>follow me </i>- be my disciple, and I will appoint thee to preach the kingdom of God to others. This was the usual <i>call</i> which Christ gave to his disciples. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:19">Matthew 4:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:22">Matthew 8:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:9">Matthew 9:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:14">Mark 2:14</span>; and it is pretty evident, from this, that he intended to make him a preacher of his salvation. How many, by their attachment to filthy lucre, have lost the honour of <i>becoming</i> or <i>continuing</i> ambassadors for the <i>Most High</i>! See on <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:21">Mark 10:21</span>.</p>
