<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:7">Matthew 19:7</span>. <i><b>Why did Moses then command to give a writing of</b></i><b> </b><i><b>divorcement?</b></i> — It is not an unusual case for the impure and unholy to seek for a justification of their conduct from the law of God itself, and to wrest Scripture to their own destruction. I knew a gentleman, so called, who professed deep reverence for the sacred writings, and, strange as it may appear, was outwardly irreproachable in every respect but <i>one</i>; that was, he kept more women than his wife. This man frequently read the Bible, and was particularly conversant with those places that spoke of or seemed to legalize the <i>polygamy of the patriarchs</i>!</p>
<p><i><b>A writing of divorcement</b></i> — See the <i>form</i> of it in <i>Clarke's note on "</i><span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:31">Matthew 5:31</span><i>"</i>.</p>
