<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:44">Matthew 22:44</span>. <i><b>The Lord</b></i><b> (</b><b>יהוה </b>Yeve<b> or </b><i><b>Jehovah</b></i><b>) </b><i><b>said unto my</b></i><b> </b><i><b>Lord</b></i><b>, (</b><b>אדניע </b>Adni<b> or </b><i><b>Adonai</b></i><b>, my </b><i><b>prop, stay, master</b></i><b>, </b><i><b>support</b></i><b>), </b><i><b>Sit thou on my right hand</b></i> — Take the place of the greatest eminence and authority. <i>Till I make thine enemies thy</i> <i>footstool </i>- till I subdue both Jews and Gentiles under thee, and cause them to acknowledge thee as their <i>sovereign</i> and <i>Lord</i>. This quotation is taken from <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ps+110:1">Psalms 110:1</span>; and, from it, these two points are clear:</p>
<p>1. That David wrote it by the inspiration of God; and</p>
<p>2. That it is a prophetic declaration of the <i>Messiah</i>.</p>
