<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:10">Matthew 23:10</span>. <i><b>Neither be ye called masters</b></i> — καθηγηται, <i>leaders</i>. God is in all these respects jealous of his honour. To him alone it belongs to guide and <i>lead</i> his Church, as well as to govern and defend it. Jesus is the sole teacher of righteousness. It is he alone, (who is the word, light, and eternal truth,) that can illuminate every created mind; and who, as Saviour and Redeemer, speaks to every heart by his Spirit.</p>
<p>Though the title of Rabbi, mentioned above, was comparatively recent in the time of our Lord, yet it was in great vogue, as were the others-<i>father</i> and <i>master</i>, mentioned in this and the following verse: some had all <i>three</i> titles, for thus in <i>Bab. Maccoth</i>, fol. 24. It is feigned," says Dr. <i>Lightfoot</i>, "that when King Jehosaphat saw a disciple of the wise men, he rose up out of his throne, and embraced him, and said, אבי אבי רבי רבי מרי מרי, <i>Abbi,</i> <i>Abbi! Rabbi, Rabbi! Mori, Mori! - Father, Father! Rabbi, Rabbi!</i> <i>Master, Master!</i>" Here then are the three titles which, in <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:7-8">Matthew 23:7-8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:10">Matthew 23:10</span>, our blessed Lord condemns; and these were titles that the Jewish doctors greatly affected.</p>
