<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:15">Matthew 23:15</span>. <i><b>Compass sea and land</b></i> — A proverbial expression, similar to ours, <i>You leave no stone unturned</i>; intimating that they did all in their power to gain converts, not to God, but to their sect. These we may suppose were principally sought for among the <i>Gentiles</i>, for the bulk of the Jewish nation was already on the side of the Pharisees.</p>
<p><i><b>Proselyte</b></i> — προσηλυτος, a <i>stranger</i>, or <i>foreigner</i>; one who <i>is come from his own people</i> and country, to sojourn <i>with another</i>. See the different kinds of proselytes explained in <i>Clarke's note on "</i><span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ex+12:43">Exodus 12:43</span><i>"</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>The child of hell</b></i> — A Hebraism for an excessively wicked person, such as might claim <i>hell</i> for his <i>mother</i>, and the <i>devil</i> for his <i>father</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>Twofold - the child of</b></i> — The Greek word διπλοτερον, which has generally been translated <i>twofold</i>, KYPKE has demonstrated to mean <i>more deceitful</i>. απλους is used by the best Greek writers for <i>simple, sincere</i>, απλοτης for <i>simplicity, sincerity</i>; so διπλους, <i>deceitful, dissembling</i>, and διπλοη, <i>hypocrisy,</i> <i>fraudulence</i>, and διπλοτερον, <i>more fraudulent, more deceitful,</i> <i>more hypocritical</i>. See also <i>Suidas</i> in διπλοη.</p>
<p>Dr. <i>Lightfoot</i>, and others, observe, that the proselytes were considered by the Jewish nation as the <i>scabs of the Church</i>, and hindered the coming of the Messiah; and <i>Justin Martyr</i> observes, that "the proselytes did not only disbelieve Christ's doctrine, but were <i>abundantly more blasphemous</i> against him than the Jews themselves, endeavouring to torment and cut off the Christians wherever they could; they being in this the <i>instruments</i> of the scribes and Pharisees."</p>
