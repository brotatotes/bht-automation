<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:2">Matthew 25:2</span>. <i><b>Five of them were wise</b></i> — Or, <i>provident</i>, φρονιμοι - they took care to make a proper provision beforehand, and left nothing to be done in the last moment.</p>
<p><i><b>Five</b></i><b> were </b><i><b>foolish</b></i> — μωροι, which might be translated <i>careless</i>, is generally rendered <i>foolish</i>; but this does not agree so well with φρονιμοι, <i>provident</i>, or <i>prudent</i>, in the first clause, which is the proper meaning of the word. μωρος in the Etymologicon, is thus defined, μη ὁρᾶ το δεον, <i>he who sees not</i> <i>what is proper</i> or <i>necessary</i>. These did not see that it was necessary to have <i>oil in their vessels</i>, (the salvation of God in their souls,) as well as a burning <i>lamp</i> of religious profession, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:3">Matthew 25:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:4">Matthew 25:4</span>.</p>
