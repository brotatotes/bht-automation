<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:12">Matthew 26:12</span>. <i><b>She did</b></i><b> it </b><i><b>for my burial.</b></i> — Or, <i>She hath done it to</i> <i>embalm me </i>- ενταφιασαι με. The Septuagint use ενταφιαστης for the person whose office it was to <i>embalm</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ge+50:2">Genesis 50:2</span>, and ενταφιαζω for the Hebrew הנט which signifies to <i>prepare with spices</i>, or <i>aromatics</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ge+50:3">Genesis 50:3</span>. Our Lord took this opportunity to tell them, once more, that he was shortly to die.</p>
