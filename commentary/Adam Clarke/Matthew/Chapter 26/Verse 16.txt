<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:16">Matthew 26:16</span>. <i><b>He sought opportunity</b></i> — ευκαιριαν, a convenient or fit opportunity. Men seldom leave a crime imperfect: when once sin is <i>conceived</i>, it meets, in general, with few obstacles, till it brings forth <i>death</i>. How deceitful, how deeply damning, is the love of money! Well might a heathen exclaim, while contemplating the grave of a person who was murdered for the sake of his wealth: - </p>

<p>"O! cursed lust of gold! what wilt thou not compel the human heart to perpetrate?."</p>
<p>Judas is deservedly considered as one of the most infamous of men, his conduct base beyond description, and his motives vile. But how many, since his time, have walked in the same way! How many, for the sake of worldly wealth, have renounced the religion of their Lord and Master, and sold Jesus, and their interest in heaven, for a short-lived portion of secular good! From <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:6">John 12:6</span>, we learn that Judas, who was treasurer to our Lord and his disciples, (for he carried the bag,) was a thief, and frequently purloined a portion of what was given for the support of this holy family. Being disappointed of the prey he hoped to have from the sale of the precious ointment, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:9">Matthew 26:9</span>, he sold his Master to make up the sum. A thorough <i>Jew</i>!</p>
