<p>Verse 40. <i><b>Thou that destroyest</b></i> — Who didst pretend that thou couldst have destroyed the temple, and built it up again in three days. This malicious torturing of our Lord's words has been noticed before. <i>Cruelty</i> is obliged to take refuge in <i>lies</i>, in order to vindicate its infamous proceedings.</p>
<p><i><b>If thou be the Son of God</b></i> — Or rather, Υἱος του Θεου A <i>son of</i> <i>God</i>, i.e. a peculiar favourite of the Most-High; not Ὁ Υἱος του Θεου, THE <i>Son of God</i>. "It is not to be conceived," says a learned man, "that every passenger who was going to the city had a competent knowledge of Christ's supernatural conception by the Holy Spirit, or an adequate comprehension of his character as <i>the</i> Messiah, and (κατ' εξοχην) THE SON OF GOD. There is not a single passiage where <i>Jesus</i> is designed to be pointed out as the MESSIAH, THE SON OF GOD, where the <i>article</i> is omitted: nor, on the other hand, is this designation ever specified <i>without</i> the <i>article</i>, thus, 'Ὁ Υἱος του Θεου. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:16">Matthew 16:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:63">Matthew 26:63</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:19">Matthew 28:19</span>."</p>
