<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:20">Matthew 9:20</span>. <i><b>A woman which was diseased with an issue of blood</b></i> — γυνη αιμορρουσα. <i>Mulier sanguinis profluvio laborans.</i> <i>Significatur hoc loco, fluxus muliebris, in</i> SANIS, <i>menstruus; in</i> HAC <i>perpetuus</i>. It would be easy to explain the nature and properties of the disease here mentioned; but, when it is said that prudence forbids it, the intimation itself may be thought sufficiently explanatory of the disorder in question. There are some remarkable circumstances relative to this case mentioned by St. Mark, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:25">Mark 5:25</span>, &amp;c., which shall be properly noticed in the notes on that place.</p>
<p><i><b>The hem of his garment</b></i> — The ציצית <i>tsitsith</i>, or <i>fringes</i>, which the Jews were commanded to wear on their garments. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="nu+15:38">Numbers 15:38</span>, and the note there.</p>
