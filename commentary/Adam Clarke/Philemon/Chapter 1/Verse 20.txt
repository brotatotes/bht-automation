<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:20">Philemon 1:20</span>. <i><b>Yea, brother</b></i> — It is even so, that thou art thus indebted to me. <i>Let me have joy of thee</i>, in forgiving Onesimus, and receiving him into thy favour. In the words εγε σου οναιμην, which we should translate, <i>let me have</i> PROFIT <i>of thee</i>, there is an evident <i>paronomasia</i>, or play on the name of <i>Onesimus</i>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:2">Philemon 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:11">Philemon 1:11</span>.</p>
<p><i><b>Refresh my bowels</b></i> — Gratify the earnest longing of my soul in this. I ask neither thy money nor goods; I ask what will <i>enrich</i>, not <i>impoverish</i>, thee to give.</p>
