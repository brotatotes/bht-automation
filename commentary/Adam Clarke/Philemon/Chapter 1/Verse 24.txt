<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:24">Philemon 1:24</span>. <i><b>Marcus, Aristarchus</b></i>, c.] These were all acquaintances of Philemon, and probably Colossians and may be all considered as joining here with St. Paul in his request for Onesimus. Some think that <i>Marcus</i> was either the evangelist, or <i>John Mark</i>, the nephew of Barnabas, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:12">Acts 12:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:25">Acts 12:25</span>. <i>Aristarchus</i> was probably the same with him mentioned <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:29">Acts 19:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:4">Acts 20:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:2">Acts 27:2</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="col+4:10">Colossians 4:10</span>.</p>
<p><i><b>Demas</b></i> — Is supposed to be the same who continued in his attachment to Paul till his last imprisonment at Rome; after which he left him for what is supposed to have been the <i>love of the</i> <i>world</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:10">2 Timothy 4:10</span>; but see the note.</p>
<p><i><b>Lucas</b></i> — Is supposed to be <i>Luke</i> the <i>evangelist</i>, and <i>author</i> of the <i>Acts of the Apostles</i>. On these suppositions little confidence can be placed: they may be correct; they may be otherwise.</p>
