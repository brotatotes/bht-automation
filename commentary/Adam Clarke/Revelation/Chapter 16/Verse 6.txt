It is said that when <i>Tomyris</i>, queen of the Scythians, had vanquished Cyrus, she cut off his head and threw it into a vessel of blood, saying these words: <i>Satia te sanguine,</i> <i>quem sitisti, cujusque insatiabilis semper fuisti</i>; "Satisfy thyself with blood, for which thou hast thirsted, and for which thy desire has been insatiable." See <i>Justin</i>. Hist., lib. i. c. 8. This figure of speech is called <i>sarcasm</i> in rhetoric. </p>
