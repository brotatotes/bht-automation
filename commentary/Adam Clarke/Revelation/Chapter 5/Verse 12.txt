<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="re+5:12">Revelation 5:12</span>. <i><b>To receive power</b></i> — That is, Jesus Christ is worthy <i>to</i> <i>take</i>, λαβειν, to have ascribed to him, <i>power</i>-omnipotence; <i>riches</i>-beneficence; <i>wisdom</i>-omniscience; <i>strength</i>-power in prevalent exercise; <i>honour</i>-the highest reputation for what he has done; <i>glory</i>-the praise due to such actions; <i>and blessing</i>-the thankful acknowledgments of the whole creation. Here are seven different species of praise; and this is exactly agreeable to the rabbinical forms, which the author of this book keeps constantly in view. See <i>Sepher Rasiel</i>, fol. 39, 2: "To thee belongs כבוד cabod, <i>glory</i>; גדולה gedulah, <i>magnitude</i>; גבורה geburah, <i>might</i>; הממלכה hammamlakah, <i>the kingdom</i>; התפארת hattiphereth, <i>the honour</i>; הנצח hannetsach, <i>the victory</i>; וההוד vehahod, and <i>the praise</i>."</p>
