<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="re+5:5">Revelation 5:5</span>. <i><b>The Lion of the tribe of Juda</b></i> — Jesus Christ, who sprang from this tribe, as his genealogy proves; see on <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+1:2">Matthew 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="mt+1:3">Matthew 1:3</span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:33">Luke 3:33</span>. There is an allusion here to <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ge+49:9">Genesis 49:9</span>, <i>Judah is a lion's whelp</i>; the <i>lion</i> was the emblem of this tribe, and was supposed to have been embroidered on its ensigns.</p>
<p><i><b>The Root of David</b></i> — See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="isa+11:1">Isaiah 11:1</span>. Christ was the <i>root</i> of David as to his <i>Divine nature</i>; he was a <i>branch out of</i> <i>the stem of Jesse</i> as to his <i>human nature</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>Hath prevailed</b></i> — By the merit of his incarnation, passion, and death.</p>
<p><i><b>To open the book</b></i> — To <i>explain</i> and <i>execute</i> all the purposes and decrees of God, in relation to the government of the world and the Church.</p>
