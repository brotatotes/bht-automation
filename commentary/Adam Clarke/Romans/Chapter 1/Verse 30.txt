<p>Verse 30. <i><b>Backbiters</b></i> — καταλαλους, from κατα, <i>against</i>, and λαλεω, <i>I speak</i>; those who speak against others; false accusers, <i>slanderers</i>.</p>
<p><i><b>Haters of God</b></i> — θεοστυγεις, <i>atheists, contemners of sacred</i> <i>things, maligners of providence, scorners</i>, c. All profligate deists are of this class and it seems to be the finishing part of a diabolic character.</p>
<p><i><b>Despiteful</b></i> — υβριστας, from υβριζω, to <i>treat with injurious</i> <i>insolence</i>; stormy, boisterous; abusing both the characters and persons of those over whom they can have any power.</p>
<p><i><b>Proud</b></i> — υπερηφανους, from υπερ, <i>above</i> or <i>over</i>, and φαινω, <i>I show</i> or <i>shine</i>. They who are continually exalting themselves and depressing others; magnifying themselves at the expense of their neighbours; and wishing all men to receive their <i>sayings</i> as oracles.</p>
<p><i><b>Boasters</b></i> — αλαζονας, from λαζομαι, <i>to assume</i>; self-assuming, vain-glorious, and arrogant men.</p>
<p><i><b>Inventors of evil things</b></i> — εφευρετας κακων. Those who have <i>invented</i> destructive <i>customs, rites, fashions</i>, c. such as the different religious <i>ceremonies</i> among the <i>Greeks</i> and <i>Romans</i>-the <i>orgies</i> of <i>Bacchus</i>, the <i>mysteries</i> of Ceres, the <i>lupercalia</i>, feasts of the <i>Bona Dea</i>, &amp;c., &amp;c. Multitudes of which evil things, destructive and abominable ceremonies, are to be found in every part of the heathen worship.</p>
<p><i><b>Disobedient to parents</b></i> — Though filial affection was certainly more recommended and cultivated than many other virtues, yet there are many instances on record of the grossest violation of this great branch of the law of nature.</p>
