<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:2">Romans 11:2</span>. <i><b>God hath not cast away his people which he foreknew.</b></i> — God has not finally and irrecoverably rejected a people whom he has <i>loved</i> (or <i>approved</i>) <i>so long</i>, ον προεγνω, for this is evidently the meaning of the word in this place, as we have already seen, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:29">Romans 8:29</span>, and is a very general meaning of the original verb ידע <i>yada</i> in Hebrew and γινωσκω in Greek; as I have had often occasion to notice in different parts of this work, and what none will deny who consults the original. See <i>Schleusner,</i> <i>Parkhust</i>, c.</p>
