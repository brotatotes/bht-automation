<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:9">Romans 11:9</span>. <i><b>And David saith, Let their table</b></i>, c.] And from their present disposition it is reasonable to conclude that the same evils will fall upon them as fell upon the disobedient in former times, as predicted by David, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ps+69:22">Psalms 69:22</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ps+69:23">Psalms 69:23</span>,that their very <i>blessings</i> should become <i>curses</i> to them, and their <i>temporal</i> <i>mercies</i> be their only recompense and yet even these earthly blessings, by not being enjoyed in the Lord, should be a stumbling block over which they should fall, and, instead of being a <i>blessing</i>, should be the means of their <i>punishment</i>. They <i>would</i> have a <i>worldly</i> Messiah, and therefore they rejected him whose kingdom was not of this world.</p>
