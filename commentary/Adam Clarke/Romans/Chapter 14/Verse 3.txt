<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:3">Romans 14:3</span>. <i><b>Let not him that eateth</b></i> — The <i>Gentile</i>, who eats flesh, <i>despise him</i>, the <i>Jew</i>, who <i>eateth not</i> flesh, but <i>herbs</i>. <i>And let not him</i>, the Jew, <i>that eateth not</i> indiscriminately, <i>judge </i>- condemn <i>him</i>, the Gentile, <i>that eateth</i> indiscriminately flesh or vegetables.</p>
<p><i><b>For God hath received him.</b></i> — Both being <i>sincere</i> and upright, and acting in the fear of God, are <i>received</i> as <i>heirs</i> of <i>eternal</i> <i>life</i>, without any difference on account of these religious scruples or prejudices.</p>
