<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:7">Romans 4:7</span>. <i><b>Blessed</b></i><b> are </b><i><b>they whose iniquities are forgiven</b></i> — That is, the man is truly <i>happy</i> whose iniquities αιανομιαι, whose <i>transgressions of the law</i> are forgiven; for by these he was exposed to the most grievous punishment. <i>Whose sins</i>, αιαμαρτιαι, his innumerable <i>deviations</i> from the strict rule of truth and righteousness, <i>are covered</i>-entirely removed out of sight, and thrown into oblivion. See the meaning of the word <i>sin</i> in <i>Clarke's note on "</i><span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ge+13:13">Genesis 13:13</span><i>"</i>.</p>
