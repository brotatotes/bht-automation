<p>Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:7">Romans 9:7</span>. <i><b>Neither because they are the seed of Abraham</b></i>, c.] Nor can they conclude, because they are the natural descendants of Abraham, that therefore they are all of them, without exception, the <i>children</i> in whom the promise is to be fulfilled.</p>

For Abraham had several sons besides <i>Isaac</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ge+25:1">Genesis 25:1</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ge+25:2">Genesis 25:2</span>, particularly <i>Ishmael</i>, who was circumcised before <i>Isaac</i> was born, and in whom Abraham was desirous that the <i>promise</i> should be fulfilled, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ge+17:18">Genesis 17:18</span>, and in him God might have fulfilled the promise, had he so pleased and yet he said to Abraham, <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ge+21:12">Genesis 21:12</span>: Not in <i>Ishmael</i>, but in <i>Isaac, shall thy seed be called</i>.</p>
