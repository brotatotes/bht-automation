<p>Verse 13. <i><b>Bring Zenas the lawyer</b></i> — This person is only mentioned in this place; whether he was a <i>Jewish, Roman</i>, or <i>Greek</i> lawyer, we cannot tell.</p>
<p><i><b>And Apollos</b></i> — Of this person we have some valuable particulars in <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:24">Acts 18:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:12">1 Corinthians 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:5-6">1 Corinthians 3:5-6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:6">1 Corinthians 4:6</span>. Either St. Paul had left these at Crete when he visited that island, or he had heard that, in their evangelical itinerancy, they were about to pass through it.</p>
<p><i><b>On their journey diligently</b></i> — Afford them the <i>means</i> to defray their expenses. The Churches through which these evangelists passed, bore their expenses from one to the other. See <span class="scriptRef" com="acc" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:6">3 John 1:6</span>.</p>
