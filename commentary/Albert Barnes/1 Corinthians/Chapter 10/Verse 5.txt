<p style=" "><b>But with many of them ... - </b>That is, with their conduct. They rebelled and sinned, and were destroyed. The design of the apostle here is, to remind them that although they enjoyed so many privileges, yet they were destroyed; and thus to admonish the Corinthians that their privileges did not constitute an absolute security from danger, and that they should be cautious against the indulgence of sin. The phrase rendered here “with many” <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ἐν τοῖς πλείων</span></span> en tois pleiōn should have been rendered “with most of them,” literally” with the many; and it means that with the greater part of them God was not well pleased; that is, he was pleased with but few of them.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Was not well pleased - </b>Was offended with their ingratitude and rebellion.</p>
<p style=" "><b>For they were overthrown ... - </b>That is, by the pestilence, by wars, or died by natural and usual diseases, so that they did not reach the land of Canaan. But two men of that generation, Caleb and Joshua, were permitted to enter the land of promise; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="nu+14:29-30">Numbers 14:29-30</span>.</p>
