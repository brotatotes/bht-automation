<p style=" "><b>And whatsoever we ask, we receive of him - </b>If we are truly his children, and ask in a proper manner. See the notes at <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:7">Matthew 7:7</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:24">Mark 11:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:9">Luke 11:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:1">Luke 18:1</span> ff; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:13">John 14:13</span>; <span class="_0000ff"><u>Joh 15:7</u></span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:14">1 John 5:14</span>. The declaration here made must be understood with these limitations:</p>
<p style="margin-left: 2.0em;  "> (1)That we ask in a proper manner, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:3">James 4:3</span>; and,</p>
<p style="margin-left: 2.0em;  "> (2)That the thing asked shall be such as will be consistent for God to give; that is, such as he shall see to be best for us, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:14">1 John 5:14</span>. See the notes at this latter passage.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Because we keep his commandments - </b>Not that this is the meritorious ground of our being heard, but that it furnishes evidence that we are his children, and he hears his children as such.</p>
<p style=" "><b>And do those things that are pleasing in his sight - </b>As a parent is disposed to bestow favors on obedient, affectionate, and dutiful children, so God is on those who please him by their obedience and submission to his will. We can have no hope that he will hear us unless we do so live as to please him.</p>
