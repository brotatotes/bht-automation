<p style=" "><b>And if ye call on the Father - </b>That is, if you are true Christians, or truly pious - piety being represented in the Scriptures as calling on God, or as the worship of God. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:11">Acts 9:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ge+4:26">Genesis 4:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1ki+18:24">1 Kings 18:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ps+116:17">Psalms 116:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2ki+5:11">2 Kings 5:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1ch+16:8">1 Chronicles 16:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="joe+2:32">Joel 2:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:13">Romans 10:13</span>; <span class="_0000ff"><u>Zep 3:9</u></span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:2">1 Corinthians 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:21">Acts 2:21</span>. The word “Father” here is used evidently not to denote the Father in contradistinction to the Son, but as referring to God as the Father of the universe. See <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:14">1 Peter 1:14</span> - “As obedient children.” God is often spoken of as the Father of the intelligent beings whom he has made. Christians worship Him as a Father - as one having all the feelings of a kind and tender parent toward them. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ps+103:13">Psalms 103:13</span>, following.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Who without respect of persons - </b>Impartiality. One who is not influenced in His treatment of people by a regard to rank, wealth, beauty, or any external distinction. See the <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:34">Acts 10:34</span> note, and <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:11">Romans 2:11</span> note.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Judgeth according to every man’s work - </b>He judges each one according to his character; or to what he has done, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="re+22:12">Revelation 22:12</span>. See the notes at <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:10">2 Corinthians 5:10</span>. The meaning is: “You worship a God who will judge every person according to his real character, and you should therefore lead such lives as he can approve.”</p>
<p style=" "><b>Pass the time of your sojourning - </b>“Of your temporary residence on earth. This is not your permanent home, but you are strangers and sojourners.” See the notes at <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:13">Hebrews 11:13</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>In fear - </b>See the <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="php+2:12">Philippians 2:12</span> note; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:28">Hebrews 12:28</span> note. With true reverence or veneration for God and His law. Religion is often represented as the reverent fear of God, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="de+6:2">Deuteronomy 6:2</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="de+6:13">Deuteronomy 6:13</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="de+6:24">Deuteronomy 6:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="pr+1:7">Proverbs 1:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="pr+3:13">Proverbs 3:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="pr+14:26-27">Proverbs 14:26-27</span>, et saepe al.</p>
