<p style=" "><b>Paul, and Silvanus, and Timotheus - </b>On the reasons why Paul associated other names with his in his epistles, see the <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:1">1 Corinthians 1:1</span> note, and <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:1">2 Corinthians 2:1</span> note. Silvanus, or Silas, and Timothy were properly united with him on this occasion, because they had been with him when the church was founded there, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:0">Acts 17:0</span>, and because Timothy had been sent by the apostle to visit them after he had himself been driven away; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:1-2">1 Thessalonians 2:1-2</span>. Silas is first mentioned in the New Testament as one who was sent by the church at Jerusalem with Paul to Antioch (notes, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:22">Acts 15:22</span>); and he afterward became his traveling companion.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Which is in God the Father, and in the Lord Jesus Christ - </b>Who are united to the true God and to the Redeemer; or who sustain an intimate relation to the Father and the Lord Jesus. This is strong language, denoting, that they were a true church; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:20">1 John 5:20</span>. “Grace be unto you,” etc.; see the notes, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:7">Romans 1:7</span>.</p>
