<p style=" "><b>Ye remember, brethren, our labour - </b>Doubtless in the occupation of a tent-maker; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:34">Acts 20:34</span> note; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:12">1 Corinthians 4:12</span> note.</p>
<p style=" "><b>And travail - </b>see the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:27">2 Corinthians 11:27</span>. The word means “wearisome labor.”</p>
<p style=" "><b>For labouring night and day - </b>That is, when he was not engaged in preaching the gospel. He appears to have labored through the week and to have preached on the Sabbath; or if engaged in preaching in the day time during the week, he made it up by night labor.</p>
<p style=" "><b>We preached unto you the gospel of God - </b>That is, I supported myself when I preached among you. No one, therefore, could say that I was disposed to live in idleness; no one that I sought to make myself rich at the expense of others.</p>
