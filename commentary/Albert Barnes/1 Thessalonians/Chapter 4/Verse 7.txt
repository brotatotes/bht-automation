<p style=" "><b>For God hath not called us unto uncleanness - </b>When he called us to be his followers, it was not that we should lead lives of impurity, but of holiness. We should, therefore, fulfil the purposes for which we were called into his kingdom. The word “uncleanness” (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ἀκαθαρσία</span></span> akatharsia), means, properly, “impurity, filth;” and then, in a moral sense, “pollution, lewdness,” as opposed to chastity; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:24">Romans 1:24</span>; <span class="_0000ff"><u>Rom 6:19</u></span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:21">2 Corinthians 12:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:19">Galatians 5:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:19">Ephesians 4:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:3">Ephesians 5:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="col+3:5">Colossians 3:5</span>.</p>
