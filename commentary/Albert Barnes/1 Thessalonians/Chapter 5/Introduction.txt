<p align="CENTER">Analysis Of The Chapter</p>
<p style=" ">This chapter consists of two parts:</p>
<p style="margin-left: 2.0em;  "> I. In the first part, the apostle states:</p>
<p style="margin-left: 2.75em;  "> (1)That it was well understood by the Thessalonians that the coming of the Lord would be sudden, and at an unexpected moment,<span class="_0000ff"><span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:1-2">1 Thessalonians 5:1-2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:1-2">1 Thessalonians 5:1-2</span></span>;</p>
<p style="margin-left: 2.75em;  "> (2)He refers to the effect of his coming on the wicked and the righteous, and says that it would be attended with the sudden and inevitable destruction of the former, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:3">1 Thessalonians 5:3</span>; but that the result of his coming would be far different on the righteous; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:4-11">1 Thessalonians 5:4-11</span>.</p>
<p style=" ">The prospect of his coming was fitted to make them watchful and sober, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:6-8">1 Thessalonians 5:6-8</span>; and his advent would be attended with their certain salvation; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:9">1 Thessalonians 5:9</span>.</p>
<p style="margin-left: 2.0em;  "> II. In the second part of the chapter, he exhorts them to show proper respect for their spiritual teachers and rulers, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:12-13">1 Thessalonians 5:12-13</span>; to endeavor to restrain the unruly, to support the feeble, and to evince toward all the spirit of patience and forbearance, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:14">1 Thessalonians 5:14</span>; to manifest a meek and benevolent manner of life, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:15">1 Thessalonians 5:15</span>; to rejoice always, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:16">1 Thessalonians 5:16</span>; to pray constantly, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:17">1 Thessalonians 5:17</span>; to render thanks to God in every situation, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:18">1 Thessalonians 5:18</span>; to cherish the influences of the Holy Ghost on their souls, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:19">1 Thessalonians 5:19</span>; to show respect for all the divine prophetic communications, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:20">1 Thessalonians 5:20</span>; to consider and examine carefully everything submitted to them for belief; to adhere steadfastly to all that was good and true, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:21">1 Thessalonians 5:21</span>; and to avoid the very appearance of evil, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:22">1 Thessalonians 5:22</span>. The Epistle closes with a fervent prayer that God would sanctify them entirely; with an earnest entreaty that they would pray for him; with a command that the Epistle should be read to all the churches, and with the benediction; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:22-28">1 Thessalonians 5:22-28</span>.</p>
