<p style=" ">(6) A solemn charge is given to Timothy to keep these commandments; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:21">1 Timothy 5:21</span>.</p>
<p style=" ">(7) The statement of his duty not to ordain any person rashly or hastily to the sacred office; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:22">1 Timothy 5:22</span>.</p>
<p style=" ">(8) To guard his health; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:23">1 Timothy 5:23</span>.</p>
<p style=" ">(9) A declaration respecting sin - that sometimes it is open beforehand, and sometimes it is concealed until it is revealed at the judgment, closes the chapter; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:24-25">1 Timothy 5:24-25</span>. The design of this closing statement seems to be, to show Timothy that he should not judge people by appearances, but that he should evince great caution in forming his estimate of their character.</p>
