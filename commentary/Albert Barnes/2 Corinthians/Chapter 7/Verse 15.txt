<p style=" "><b>And his inward affection ... - </b>He has become deeply and tenderly attached to you. His affectionate regard for you has been greatly increased by his visit. On the meaning of the word here rendered “inward affection” (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπλάγχια</span></span> splangchia, Margin, bowels) see the note on <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:12">2 Corinthians 6:12</span>. It denotes here: deep, tender attachment, or love.</p>
<p style=" "><b>How with fear and trembling ye received him - </b>With fear of offending, and with deep apprehension of the consequences of remaining in sin. He saw what a fear there was of doing wrong, and what evidence there was, therefore, that you were solicitous to do right.</p>
