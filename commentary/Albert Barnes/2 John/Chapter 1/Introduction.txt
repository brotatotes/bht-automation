<p style=" ">The points embraced in this Epistle are these: A salutation to the female to whom it is addressed, and an expression of warm attachment to her family, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:1-3">2 John 1:1-3</span>. An expression of joy and gratitude that he had been permitted to learn that her children had embraced the truth, and were walking in it, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:4">2 John 1:4</span>. An exhortation to live in the exercise of mutual love, in obedience to the great commandment of the Saviour, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:5-6">2 John 1:5-6</span>. The fact that many deceivers had gone out into the world, and an exhortation to be on their guard against their arts, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:7-8">2 John 1:7-8</span>. A test by which they might be known, and their true character ascertained, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:9">2 John 1:9</span>. An exhortation to show them no countenance whatever; not to treat them in any such way, even in the rites of hospitality, as to give occasion to the charge that she was friendly to their doctrines, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:10-11">2 John 1:10-11</span>. A statement that, as he hoped to see her soon, he would not write more to her, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:12">2 John 1:12</span>. And the salutation of the children of someone who is spoken of as her elect sister, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:13">2 John 1:13</span>.</p>
