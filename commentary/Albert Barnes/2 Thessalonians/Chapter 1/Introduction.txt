<p align="CENTER">Analysis Of The Chapter</p>
<p style=" "><span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:1-12">2 Thessalonians 1:1-12</span> comprises the following points:</p>
<p style="margin-left: 2.0em;  "> (2)An expression of thanks for the progress which the Thessalonians had made in piety, and especially for the manner in which they had been enabled to bear their trials; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:3-4">2 Thessalonians 1:3-4</span>.</p>
<p style="margin-left: 2.0em;  "> (3)An assurance that the manner in which they had been enabled to bear their trials was an evidence that they were true Christians; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:5">2 Thessalonians 1:5</span>.</p>
<p style="margin-left: 2.0em;  "> (4)A declaration that those who had persecuted them, and all others who were wicked, would be punished when the Lord Jesus should come, and that when this should occur the righteous would appear in glory and honor; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:6-10">2 Thessalonians 1:6-10</span>.</p>
<p style="margin-left: 2.0em;  "> (5)The expression of an earnest desire that they might be prepared for the solemn scenes of that day; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:11-12">2 Thessalonians 1:11-12</span>.</p>
