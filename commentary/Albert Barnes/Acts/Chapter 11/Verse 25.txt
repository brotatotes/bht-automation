<p style=" "><b>Then departed ... - </b>Why Barnabas sought Saul is not known. It is probable, however, that it was owing to the remarkable success which he had in Antioch. There was a great revival of religion, and there was need of additional labor. In such times the ministers of the gospel need additional help, as men in harvest-time need the aid of others. Saul was in this vicinity <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:30">Acts 9:30</span>, and he was eminently suited to assist in this work. With him Barnabas was well acquainted <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:27">Acts 9:27</span>, and probably there was no other one in that vicinity whose help he could obtain.</p>
