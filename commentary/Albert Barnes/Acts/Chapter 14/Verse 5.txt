<p style=" "><b>An assault made - </b>Greek: a “rush” - <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ὁρμή</span></span> hormē. It denotes “an impetuous excitement and aggression; a rush to put them to death.” It rather describes a popular tumult than a calm and deliberate purpose. There was a violent, tumultuous excitement.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Both of the Gentiles ... - </b>Of that part of them which was opposed to the apostles.</p>
<p style=" "><b>To use them despitefully - </b>See the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:44">Matthew 5:44</span>. To reproach them; to bring contempt upon them; to injure them.</p>
<p style=" "><b>And to stone them - </b>To put them to death by stoning; probably as blasphemers, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:57-59">Acts 7:57-59</span>.</p>
