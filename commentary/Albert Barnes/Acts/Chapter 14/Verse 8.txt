<p style=" "><b>And there sat - </b>There dwelt, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:16">Matthew 9:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:11">Acts 18:11</span> (margin). The word “sat,” however, indicates his usual posture, his helpless condition. Such persons commonly sat by the wayside, or in some public place, to ask for alms, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:46">Mark 10:46</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Impotent in his feet - </b><span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ἀδύνατος</span></span> adunatos. Without any power. Entirely deprived of the use of his feet.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Being a cripple - </b>Lame.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Who never had walked - </b>The miracle, therefore, would be more remarkable, as the man would be well known. As they were persecuted from place to place, and opposed in every manner, it was desirable that a signal miracle should be performed to carry forward and establish the work of the gospel.</p>
