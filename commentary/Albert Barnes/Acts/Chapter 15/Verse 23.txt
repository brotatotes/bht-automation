<p style=" "><b>And wrote letters - </b>Greek: “Having written.” It does not mean that they wrote more than one epistle.</p>
<p style=" "><b>By them - </b>Greek: by their hand.”</p>
<p style=" "><b>After this manner - </b>Greek: these things.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Send greeting - </b>A word of salutation, expressing their desire of the happiness (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαίρειν</span></span> chairein) of the persons addressed. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:49">Matthew 26:49</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:29">Matthew 27:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:28">Luke 1:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:3">John 19:3</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>In Antioch - </b>Where the difficulty first arose.</p>
<p style=" "><b>And Syria - </b>Antioch was the capital of Syria, and it is probable that the dispute was not confined to the capital.</p>
<p style=" "><b>And Cilicia - </b>See the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:9">Acts 6:9</span>. Cilicia was adjacent to Syria. Paul and Barnabas had traveled through it, and it is probable that the same difficulty would exist there which had disturbed the churches in Syria.</p>
