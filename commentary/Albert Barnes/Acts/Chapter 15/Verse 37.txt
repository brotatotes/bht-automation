<p style=" "><b>And Barnabas determined - </b>Greek: willed, or was disposed to (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ἐβουλεύσαντο</span></span> ebouleusanto).</p>
<p style=" "><b>John ... - </b>See the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:12">Acts 12:12</span>. He had been with them before as a traveling companion, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:25">Acts 12:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:5">Acts 13:5</span>. He was the son of a sister of Barnabas <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="col+4:10">Colossians 4:10</span>, and it is probable that Barnabas’ affection for his nephew was the main reason for inducing him to wish to take him with him in the journey.</p>
