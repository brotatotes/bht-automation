<p style=" "><b>And the contention was so sharp - </b>The word used here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παροξυσμός</span></span> paroxusmos is that from which our word “paroxysm” is derived. It may denote “any excitement of mind,” and is used in a good sense in <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:24">Hebrews 10:24</span>. It here means, however, “a violent altercation” that resulted in their separation for a time, and in their engaging in different spheres of labor.</p>
<p style=" "><b>And sailed into Cyprus - </b>This was the native place of Barnabas. See the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:36">Acts 4:36</span>.</p>
