<p style=" "><b>Then he called for a light - </b>Greek: lights, in the plural. Probably several torches were brought by his attendants.</p>
<p style=" "><b>And came trembling - </b>Alarmed at the earthquake; amazed that the prisoners were still there; confounded at the calmness of Paul and Silas and overwhelmed at the proof of the presence of God. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="jer+5:22">Jeremiah 5:22</span>, “Fear ye not me, saith the Lord? will ye not tremble at my presence? etc.”</p>
<p style=" "><b>And fell down - </b>This was an act of profound reverence. See the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:11">Matthew 2:11</span>. It is evident that he regarded them as the favorites of God, and was con strained to recognize them as religious teachers.</p>
