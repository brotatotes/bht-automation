<p style=" "><b>And as they went through the cities - </b>The cities of Syria, Cilicia, etc.</p>
<p style=" "><b>They delivered them - </b>Paul and Silas delivered to the Christians in those cities.</p>
<p style=" "><b>The decrees - </b><span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τὰ δόγματα</span></span> ta dogmata. The decrees in regard to the four things specified in <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:20">Acts 15:20</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:29">Acts 15:29</span>. The word translated “decrees” occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:1">Luke 2:1</span>, “A decree from Caesar Augustus”; in <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:7">Acts 17:7</span> “The decrees of Caesar”; in <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:15">Ephesians 2:15</span>; and in <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="col+2:14">Colossians 2:14</span>. It properly means a law or edict of a king or legislature. In this instance it Was the decision of the council in a case submitted to it, and implied an obligation on the Christians to submit to that decision, since they had submitted the matter to them. The same principles, also, would be applicable everywhere, and the decision, therefore, at Jerusalem became conclusive. It is probable that a correct and attested copy of the letter <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:23-29">Acts 15:23-29</span> would be sent to the various churches of the Gentiles.</p>
<p style=" "><b>To keep - </b>To obey, or to observe.</p>
<p style=" "><b>That were ordained - </b>Greek: that were adjudged or determined.</p>
