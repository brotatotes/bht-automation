<p style=" "><b>Stirred up the people - </b>The word used here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαλεύειν</span></span> saleuein denotes properly “to agitate” or “excite,” as the waves of the sea are agitated by the wind. It is with great beauty used to denote the “agitation and excitement of a popular tumult,” from its resemblance to the troubled waves of the ocean. The figure is often employed by the Classic writers, and also occurs in the Scriptures. See <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ps+65:7">Psalms 65:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="isa+17:12-13">Isaiah 17:12-13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="jer+46:7-8">Jeremiah 46:7-8</span>.</p>
