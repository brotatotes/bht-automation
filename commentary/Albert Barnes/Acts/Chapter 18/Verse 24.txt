<p style=" "><b>And a certain Jew named Apollos - </b>Apollos afterward became a distinguished and successful preacher of the gospel, <span class="_0000ff"><u>1Co 1:12</u></span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:5-6">1 Corinthians 3:5-6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:6">1 Corinthians 4:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:13">Titus 3:13</span>. Nothing more is known of him than is stated in these passages.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Born at Alexandria - </b>Alexandria was a celebrated city in Egypt, founded by Alexander the Great. There were large numbers of Jews resident there. See the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:9">Acts 6:9</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>An eloquent man - </b>Alexandria was famous for its schools, and it is probable that Apollos, in addition to his natural endowments, had enjoyed the benefit of these schools.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Mighty in the scriptures - </b>Well instructed, or able in the Old Testament. The foundation was thus laid for future usefulness in the Christian church. See the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:19">Luke 24:19</span>.</p>
