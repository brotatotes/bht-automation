<p style=" "><b>And when they opposed themselves - </b>To him and his message.</p>
<p style=" "><b>And blasphemed - </b>See the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:45">Acts 13:45</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>He shook his raiment - </b>As an expressive act of shaking off the guilt of their condemnation. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:45">Acts 13:45</span>. He shook his raiment to show that he was resolved henceforward to have nothing to do with them; perhaps, also, to express the fact that God would soon slake them off, or reject them (Doddridge).</p>
<p style=" "><b>Your blood ... - </b>The guilt of your destruction is your own. You only are the cause of the destruction that is coming upon you. See the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:25">Matthew 27:25</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>I am clean - </b>I am not to blame for your destruction. I have done my duty. The gospel had been fairly offered and deliberately rejected; and Paul was not to blame for their ruin, which he saw was coming upon them.</p>
<p style=" "><b>I will go ... - </b>See <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:46">Acts 13:46</span>.</p>
