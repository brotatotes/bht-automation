<p style=" "><b>And fear came - </b>That is, there was great reverence or awe. The multitude had just before derided them <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:13">Acts 2:13</span>; but so striking and manifest was the power of God on this occasion, that it silenced all clamors, and produced a general veneration and awe. The effect of a great work of Godâ€™s grace is commonly to produce an unusual seriousness and solemnity in a community, even among those who are not converted. It restrains, subdues, and silences opposition.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Every soul - </b>Every person or individual; that is, upon the people generally; not only on those who became Christians, but upon the multitudes who witnessed these things. All things were suited to produce this fear: the recent crucifixion of Jesus of Nazareth; the wonders that attended that event; the events of the day of Pentecost; and the miracles performed by the apostles, were all suited to diffuse solemnity, thought, anxiety through the community.</p>

Some of the miracles which they performed are specified in the following chapters.</p>
