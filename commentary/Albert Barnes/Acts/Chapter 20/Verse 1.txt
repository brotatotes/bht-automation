<p style=" "><b>The uproar - </b>The tumult excited, by Demetrius and the workmen. After it had been quieted by the town-clerk, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:40-41">Acts 19:40-41</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Embraced them - </b>Saluted them; gave them parting expressions of kindness. Compare the <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:45">Luke 7:45</span> note; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:16">Romans 16:16</span> note; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:20">1 Corinthians 16:20</span> note; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2co+13:12">2 Corinthians 13:12</span> note; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:26">1 Thessalonians 5:26</span> note; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:14">1 Peter 5:14</span> note. The Syriac translates this, “Paul caned the disciples, and consoled them, and kissed them.”</p>
<p style=" "><b>To go into Macedonia - </b>On his way to Jerusalem, agreeably to his purpose, as recorded in <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:21">Acts 19:21</span>.</p>
