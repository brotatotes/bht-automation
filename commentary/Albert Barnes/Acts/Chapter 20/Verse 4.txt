<p style=" "><b>And there accompanied him - </b>It was usual for some of the disciples to attend the apostles in their journeys.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Into Asia - </b>It is not meant that they attended him from Greece through Macedonia, but that they went with him to Asia, having gone before him, and joined him at Troas.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Sopater of Berea - </b>Perhaps the same person who, in <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:21">Romans 16:21</span>, is called Sosipater, and who is there said to have been a kinsman of Paul.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Aristarchus - </b><span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:29">Acts 19:29</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Gaius of Derbe - </b>See the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:29">Acts 19:29</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Tychicus - </b>This man was high in the confidence and affection of Paul. In <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:21-22">Ephesians 6:21-22</span> he styles him “a beloved brother, and faithful minister in the Lord.”</p>
<p style=" "><b>And Trophimus - </b>Trophimus was from Ephesus, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:29">Acts 20:29</span>. When Paul wrote his Second Epistle to Timothy he was at Miletum, sick, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:20">2 Timothy 4:20</span>.</p>
