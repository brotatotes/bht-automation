<p style=" "><b>A Jew of Tarsus - </b>A Jew by birth.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Of no mean city - </b>Not obscure, or undistinguished. He could claim an honorable birth, so far as the place of his nativity was concerned. See the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:11">Acts 9:11</span>. Tarsus was much celebrated for its learning, and was at one time the rival of Alexandria and Athens. Xenophon calls it a great and flourishing city. Josephus (<i>Antiq.</i>, book 2, chapter 6, section 6) says that it was the metropolis, and most renowned city among them (the Cilicians).</p>
