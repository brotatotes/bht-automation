<p style=" "><b>Born in Tarsus - </b>See the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:11">Acts 9:11</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Brought up in this city - </b>In Jerusalem, sent there for the advantage of more perfect instruction in the Law.</p>
<p style=" "><b>At the feet of Gamaliel - </b>As a scholar or disciple of Gamaliel. The phrase to sit at the feet of one is expressive of the condition of a disciple or learner. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="de+33:3">Deuteronomy 33:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:39">Luke 10:39</span>. It is probable that the expression arose from the fact that the learners occupied a lower place or seat than the teacher. On the character and rank of Gamaliel, see the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:34">Acts 5:34</span>. Paul mentions his having been instructed in this manner in order to show that he was entitled to the full privileges of a Jew, and that he had had every opportunity to become fully acquainted with the nature of the Law.</p>
<p style=" "><b>According to the perfect manner - </b><span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατὰ ἀκρίβειαν</span></span> kata akribeian . By strict diligence or exact care; or in the utmost rigor and severity of that instruction. No pains were Spared to make him understand and practice the Law of Moses.</p>
<p style=" "><b>The law of the fathers - </b>The law of our fathers; that is, the law which they received and handed down to us. Paul was a Pharisee, and the law in which he had been taught was not only the written Law of Moses, but the traditional law which had been handed down from former times. See the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:6">Matthew 3:6</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>And was zealous toward God - </b><span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:14">Galatians 1:14</span>. He had a constant burning zeal for God and His Law, which was expressed not only by scrupulous adherence to its forms, but by persecuting all who opposed it, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:4-5">Acts 22:4-5</span>.</p>
