<p style=" "><b>And have hope toward God - </b>Having a hope of the resurrection of the dead, which arises from the promises of God.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Which they themselves ... - </b>That is, the Pharisees. Perhaps he designated in this remark the Pharisees who were present. He held nothing in this great cardinal point which they did not also hold. For the reasons why he introduced this point so prominently, and the success of thus introducing it, see the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:1-9">Acts 23:1-9</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Both of the just and unjust - </b>Of the righteous and the wicked; that is, of all the race. As they held this, they could not arraign him for holding it also.</p>
