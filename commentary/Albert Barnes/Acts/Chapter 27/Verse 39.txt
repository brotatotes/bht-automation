<p style=" "><b>They knew not the land - </b>They had been driven with a tempest, without being able to make any observation, and it is probable that they were entire strangers to the coast and to the whole island,</p>
<p style=" "><b>A certain creek with a shore - </b>Greek: a certain bosom <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κόλπος</span></span> kolpos or bay. By its having a shore is probably meant that it had a level shore, or one that was convenient for landing. It was not a high bluff of rocks, but was accessible. Kuinoel thinks that the passage should be construed, “they found a certain shore, having a bay,” etc.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Were minded - </b>Were resolved.</p>
