<p style=" "><b>And falling - </b>Being carried by the wind and waves.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Into a place where two seas met - </b>Greek: into a place of a double sea - <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διθάλασσον</span></span> dithalasson. That is, a place which was washed on both sides by the sea. It refers properly to an isthmus, tongue of land, or a sand-bar stretching out from the mainland, and which was washed on both sides by the waves. It is evident that this was not properly an isthmus that was above the waves, but was probably a long sand-bank that stretched far out into the sea, and which they did not perceive. In endeavoring to make the harbor, they ran into this bar (sand-bank).</p>
<p style=" "><b>They ran the ship aground - </b>Not designedly, but in endeavoring to reach the harbor, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:39">Acts 27:39</span>.<b>The hinder part was broken - </b>The stern was broken or staved in. By this means the company was furnished with boards, etc., on which they were safely conveyed to shore, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:44">Acts 27:44</span>.</p>
