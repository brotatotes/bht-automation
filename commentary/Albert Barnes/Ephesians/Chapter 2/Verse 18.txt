<p style=" "><b>For through him - </b>That is, he has secured this result that we have access to God. This he did by his death - reconciling us to God by the doctrines which he taught - acquainting us with God; and by his intercession in heaven - by which our “prayers gain acceptance” with him.</p>
<p style=" "><b>We both have access - </b>Both Jews and Gentiles; see the notes at <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:2">Romans 5:2</span>. We are permitted to approach God through him, or in his name. The Greek word here - <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσαγωγή</span></span> prosagōgē - relates properly to the introduction to, or audience which we are permitted to have with a prince or other person of high rank. This must be effected through an officer of court to whom the duty is entrusted. “Rosenmuller,” <i>Alt und neu Morgenland</i>, in loc.</p>
<p style=" "><b>By one Spirit - </b>By the aid of the same Spirit - the Holy Spirit; see notes, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:4">1 Corinthians 12:4</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Unto the Father - </b>We are permitted to come and address God as our Father; see the <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:15">Romans 8:15</span>, note 26, note.</p>
