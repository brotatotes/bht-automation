<p style=" ">This chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:0">Ephesians 6:0</span> comprises the following subjects:</p>
<p style=" ">(1) An exhortation to children to obey their parents, with a promise of the blessing that would follow from obedience; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:1-3">Ephesians 6:1-3</span>.</p>
<p style=" ">(2) An exhortation to fathers to manifest such a character that children could properly obey them, and to train them up in a proper manner; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:4">Ephesians 6:4</span>.</p>
<p style=" ">(3) The duty of servants; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:5-8">Ephesians 6:5-8</span>.</p>
<p style=" ">(4) The duty of masters toward their servants; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:9">Ephesians 6:9</span>.</p>
<p style=" ">(5) An exhortation to put on the whole armor of God, with a description of the Christian soldier, and of the Christian panoply; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:10-17">Ephesians 6:10-17</span>.</p>
<p style=" ">(6) The duty of prayer, and especially of prayer for the apostle himself, that he might be enabled to speak with boldness in the cause of his Master; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:18-20">Ephesians 6:18-20</span>.</p>
<p style=" ">(7) In the conclusion <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:21-24">Ephesians 6:21-24</span>, he informs them that if they wished to make any inquiries about his condition, Tychicus, who conveyed this letter, could acquaint them with his circumstances; and then closes the Epistle with the usual benedictions.</p>
