<p style=" "><b>Peace be to the brethren - </b>The Epistle is closed with the usual salutations. The expression “peace to you,” was the common form of salutation in the East (see the <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:13">Matthew 10:13</span> note; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:36">Luke 24:36</span> note; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:33">Romans 15:33</span> note; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:16">Galatians 6:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:14">1 Peter 5:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:14">3 John 1:14</span>), and is still the “salam” which is used - the word “salam” meaning “peace.”</p>
<p style=" "><b>And love with faith - </b>Love united with faith; not only desiring that they might have faith, but the faith which worked by love.</p>
<p style=" "><b>From God the Father and the Lord Jesus Christ - </b>The Father and the Son are regarded as equally the author of peace and love; compare notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2co+13:14">2 Corinthians 13:14</span>.</p>
