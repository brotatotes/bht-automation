<p style=" "><b>For by it - </b>That is, by that faith which gives reality to things hoped for, and a certain persuasion to the mind of the existence of those things which are not seen.</p>
<p style=" "><b>The elders - </b>The ancients; the Hebrew patriarchs and fathers.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Obtained a good report - </b>Literally, “were witnessed of;” that is, an honorable testimony was borne to them in consequence of their faith. The idea is, that their acting under the influence of faith, in the circumstances in which they were, was the ground of the honorable testimony which was borne to them in the Old Testament; see this use of the word in <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:8">Hebrews 7:8</span>, and in <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:4">Hebrews 7:4</span> of this chapter. Also <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:22">Luke 4:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:8">Acts 15:8</span>. In the cases which the apostle proceeds to enumerate in the subsequent part of the chapter, he mentions those whose piety is particularly commended in the Old Testament, and who showed in trying circumstances that they had unwavering confidence in God.</p>
