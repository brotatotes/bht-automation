<p style=" "><b>And in this place again - </b><span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ps+95:11">Psalms 95:11</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>If they shall enter - </b>That is, they shall not enter; see the notes at <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:11">Hebrews 3:11</span>. The object of quoting this here seems to be two-fold:</p>
<p style="margin-left: 2.0em;  "> (2)It is connected with <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:6">Hebrews 4:6</span>, and is designed to show that it was implied that a rest yet remained. “That which deserves to be called “the divine rest” is spoken of in the Scriptures, and as “they” did not enter into it, it follows that it must be in reserve for some others, and that the promise must still remain.”</p>
