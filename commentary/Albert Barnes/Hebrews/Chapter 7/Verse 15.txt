<p style=" "><b>And it is yet far more evident - </b>Not that our Lord would spring out of Judah, but the point which he was endeavoring to establish that there must be a change of the priesthood, was rendered still more evident from another consideration. A strong proof of the necessity of such a change of the priesthood was furnished from the fact that the Messiah was to be of the tribe of Judah; but a much stronger, because “as a priest” he was to be of the order of Melchizedek - that is, he was of the same rank with one who did not even belong to that tribe.</p>
<p style=" "><b>After the similitude - </b>Resembling; that is, he was to be of the order of Melchizedek.</p>
