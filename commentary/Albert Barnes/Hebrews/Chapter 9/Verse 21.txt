<p style=" "><b>He sprinkled ...both the tabernacle - </b>This circumstance is not stated by Moses. On the probability that this was done, see notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:19">Hebrews 9:19</span>. The account of setting up the tabernacle occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ex+11:1-10">Exodus 11:1-10</span>. In that account it is said that Moses “anointed” the tabernacle with the holy anointing oil; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:9-11">Hebrews 9:9-11</span>. Josephus (<i>Ant</i>. book iii, chapter 8, section 6), says that he consecrated it and the vessels thereto belonging with the blood of bulls and of rams. This was undoubtedly the tradition in the time of Paul, and no one can prove that it is not correct.</p>
<p style=" "><b>And all the vessels of the ministry - </b>Employed in the service of God. The altar, the laver, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ex+40:10-11">Exodus 40:10-11</span>, the censers, dishes, bowls, etc., which were used in the tabernacle.</p>
