<p style=" "><b>But above all things - </b> That is be especially careful on this point; whatever else is done, let not this be. The manner in which James speaks of the practice referred to here, shows that he regarded it as a sin of a very heinous nature; one that was by all means to be avoided by those whom he addressed. The habit of swearing by various things was a very common one among the Jews, and it was important to guard those who from among them had been converted to Christianity on that subject.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Swear not - </b>See this command illustrated in the notes at <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:33-34">Matthew 5:33-34</span>. Nearly the same things are mentioned here, as objects by which they were accustomed to swear, which are referred to by the Saviour.</p>
<p style=" "><b>But let our yea be yea - </b>Let there be a simple affirmation, unaccompanied by any oath or appeal to God or to any of his works. A man who makes that his common method of speech is the man who will be believed. See the notes at <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:37">Matthew 5:37</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Lest you fall into condemnation - </b>That is, for profaning the name of God. “The Lord will not hold him guiltless that taketh his name in vain,” <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ex+20:7">Exodus 20:7</span>.</p>
