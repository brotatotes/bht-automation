<p style=" "><b>Then Martha ... - </b>To Martha was intrusted the management of the affairs of the family, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:40">Luke 10:40</span>. It is probable that she first heard of his coming, and, without waiting to inform her sister, went immediately out to meet him. See <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:28">John 11:28</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Sat still in the house - </b>The word “still” is not in the original. It means that she remained sitting in the house. The common posture of grief among the Jews was that of sitting, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="job+2:8">Job 2:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="eze+8:14">Ezekiel 8:14</span>. Often this grief was so excessive as to fix the person in astonishment, and render him immovable, or prevent his being affected by any external objects. It is possible that the evangelist meant to intimate this of Mary’s grief. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ezr+9:3-4">Ezra 9:3-4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ne+1:4">Nehemiah 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="isa+47:1">Isaiah 47:1</span>.</p>
