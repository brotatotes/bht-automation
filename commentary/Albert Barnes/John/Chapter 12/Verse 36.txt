<p style=" "><b>While ye have light - </b>This implied two things:</p>
<p style="margin-left: 2.0em;  "> 1.That he was the light, or was the Messiah.</p>
<p style="margin-left: 2.0em;  "> 2.That he was soon to be taken away by death.</p>
<p style=" ">In this manner he answered their question - not directly, but in a way to convey the truth to their minds, and at the same time to administer to them a useful admonition. Jesus never aroused the prejudices of men unnecessarily, yet he never shrank from declaring to them the truth in some way, however unpalatable it might be.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Believe in the light - </b>That is, in the Messiah, who is the light of the world.</p>
<p style=" "><b>That ye may be the children ... - </b>That ye may be the friends and followers of the Messiah. See the notes at <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+1:1">Matthew 1:1</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:12">John 8:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:8">Ephesians 5:8</span>; “Now are ye light in the Lord; walk as children of light.”</p>
<p style=" "><b>Did hide himself from them - </b><span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:59">John 8:59</span>. He went out to Bethany, where he commonly passed the night, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:37">Luke 21:37</span>.</p>
