<p style=" "><b>Six water-pots of stone - </b>Made of stone; or, as we should say, stoneware.</p>
<p style=" "><b>After the manner - </b>After the usual custom.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Of the purifying - </b>Of the “washings” or ablutions of the Jews. They were for the purpose of washing the hands before and after eating <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:2">Matthew 15:2</span>, and for the formal washing of vessels, and even articles of furniture, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:39">Luke 11:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:3-4">Mark 7:3-4</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Two or three firkins - </b>It is not quite certain what is meant here by the word “firkins.” It is probable that the measure intended is the Hebrew “bath,” containing about 7 12 gallons.</p>
