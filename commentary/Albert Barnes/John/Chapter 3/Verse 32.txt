<p style=" "><b>And what he hath seen ... - </b>See <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:11">John 3:11</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>No man receiveth his testimony - </b>The words “no man” are here to be understood in the sense of “few.” Though his doctrine is pure, plain, sublime, yet “few,” comparatively, received it in faith. Though multitudes came to him, drawn by various motives <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:26">John 6:26</span>, yet “few” became his “real” disciples, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:56">Matthew 26:56</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:22">Matthew 7:22</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>His testimony - </b>His doctrine. The truth to which he bears “witness” as having “seen” and “known” it, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:11">John 3:11</span>. Often many persons “appear” for a time to become the followers of Christ, who in the end are seen to have known nothing of religion, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:6">Matthew 13:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:13">Luke 8:13</span>.</p>
