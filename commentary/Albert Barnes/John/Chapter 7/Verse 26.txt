Do the rulers know indeed ... - It seems from this that they supposed that the rulers had been convinced that Jesus was the Messiah, but that from some cause they were not willing yet to make it known to the people. The reasons of this opinion were these:They concluded, therefore, that some change had taken place in the sentiments of the rulers in regard to him, though they had not yet made it public.The rulers - The members of the Sanhedrin, or great council of the nation, who had charge of religious affairs.Indeed - Truly; certainly. Have they certain evidence, as would appear from their suffering him to speak without interruption?The very Christ - Is truly or really the Messiah.
