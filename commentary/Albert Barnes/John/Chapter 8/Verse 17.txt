<p style=" "><b>In your law - </b><span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="de+17:6">Deuteronomy 17:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="de+19:15">Deuteronomy 19:15</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:16">Matthew 18:16</span>. This related to cases in which the life of an individual was involved. Jesus says that if, in such a case, the testimony of two men were sufficient to establish a fact, his own testimony and that of his Father ought to be esteemed ample evidence in the case of religious doctrine.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Two men - </b>If two men could confirm a case, the evidence of Jesus and of God ought not to be deemed insufficient.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Is true - </b>In Deuteronomy, “established.” This means the same thing. It is confirmed; is worthy of belief.</p>
