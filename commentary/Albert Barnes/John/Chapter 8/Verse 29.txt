<p style=" "><b>Is with me - </b>In working miracles, etc.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Hath not left me alone - </b>Though men had forsaken and rejected him, yet God attended him.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Those things that please him - </b>See <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:17">Matthew 3:17</span>; “This is my beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased,” <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="php+2:8">Philippians 2:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="isa+53:10-12">Isaiah 53:10-12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:17">2 Peter 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:22">Luke 3:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:5">Matthew 17:5</span>. His undertaking the work of redemption was pleasing to God, and he had the consciousness that in executing it he did those things which God approved. It is a small matter to have men opposed to us, if we have a conscience void of offence, and evidence that we please God. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:5">Hebrews 11:5</span>; “Enoch - before his translation had this testimony that he pleased God.” See also <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:3">1 Corinthians 4:3</span>.</p>
