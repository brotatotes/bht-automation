<p style=" "><b>Moses in the law ... - </b>The punishment of adultery commanded by Moses was death, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="le+20:10">Leviticus 20:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="de+22:22">Deuteronomy 22:22</span>. The particular manner of the death was not specified in the law. The Jews had themselves, in the time of Christ, determined that it should be by stoning. See this described in the notes at <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:35">Matthew 21:35</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:44">Matthew 21:44</span>. The punishment for adultery varied. In some cases it was strangling. In the time of Ezekiel <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="eze+16:38-40">Ezekiel 16:38-40</span> it was stoning and being thrust through with a sword. If the adulteress was the daughter of a priest, the punishment was being burned to death.</p>
