<p style=" "><b>Thus - </b>In this merciful manner.</p>
<p style=" "><b>To take away my reproach - </b>Among the Jews, a family of children was counted a signal blessing, an evidence of the favor of God, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ps+113:9">Psalms 113:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ps+128:3">Psalms 128:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="isa+4:1">Isaiah 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="isa+44:3-4">Isaiah 44:3-4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="le+26:9">Leviticus 26:9</span>. To be “barren,” therefore, or to be destitute of children, was considered a “reproach” or a “disgrace,” <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1sa+1:6">1 Samuel 1:6</span>.</p>
