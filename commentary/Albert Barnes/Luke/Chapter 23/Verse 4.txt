<p style=" "><b>I find no fault - </b>I see no evidence that he is guilty of what you charge him with. This was after Pilate had taken Jesus into the judgment-hall by himself and examined him “privately,” and had been satisfied in regard to the nature of his kingdom. See <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:33-38">John 18:33-38</span>. He was “then” satisfied that though he claimed to be “a king,” yet his kingdom was not of this world, and that “his” claims did not interfere with those of Caesar.</p>
