<p style=" "><b>Take heed to yourselves - </b>Be cautious that no man deceive you; or, take care of your lives, not to run into unnecessary danger.</p>
<p style=" "><b>To councils - </b>The higher ecclesiastical courts of the Jews, including the Sanhedrin, or great council of the nation.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Rulers and kings - </b>Referring to Roman officers.</p>
<p style=" "><b>For a testimony against them - </b>Rather to bear testimony to them, or to be witnesses “before them” of the truth. This was” for the sake” of Jesus, or because they were attached to him; and God would overrule it so that at the same time they should bear witness “to” the rulers of the truth, as was the case with Peter and John, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:0">Acts 4:0</span>; with Stephen, Acts 6–7; and with Paul, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:0">Acts 23:0</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:24-25">Acts 24:24-25</span>.</p>
