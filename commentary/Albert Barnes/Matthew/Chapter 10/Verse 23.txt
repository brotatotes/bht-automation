<p style=" "><b>When they persecute ... - </b>The apostles were not permitted to “throw away” their lives. Where they could preserve them without denying their Lord, they were to do it. Yet all the commands of Christ, as well as their conduct, show that they were rather to lay down their lives than deny their Saviour. We are to preserve our lives by all proper means, but we are rather to die than save ourselves by doing anything wrong.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Ye shall not have gone over the cities of Israel ... - </b>That is, in fleeing from persecutors from one city to another, you shall not have gone to every city in Judea until the end of the Jewish economy shall occur. See the notes at <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:28-30">Matthew 24:28-30</span>. By “the coming of the Son of Man,” that is, of “Christ,” is probably meant the destruction of Jerusalem, which happened about thirty years after this was spoken. The words are often used in this sense. See <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:30">Matthew 24:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:26">Mark 13:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:27">Luke 21:27</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:32">Luke 21:32</span>.</p>
