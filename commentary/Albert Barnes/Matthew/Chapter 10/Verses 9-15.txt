<p style=" "><b>It shall be more tolerable for the land of Sodom ... - </b>The cities here mentioned, together with Admah and Zeboim, were destroyed by fire and brimstone on account of their great wickedness.</p>
<p style=" ">They occupied the place afterward covered by the Dead Sea, bounding Palestine on the southeast, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ge+19:24-25">Genesis 19:24-25</span>. Christ said that their punishment will be more “tolerable” - that is, more easily borne - than that of the people who reject his gospel. The reason is, that they were not favored with so much light and instruction. See <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:23-24">Matthew 11:23-24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:47-48">Luke 12:47-48</span>. Sodom and Gomorrah are often referred to as signal instances of divine vengeance, and as sure proofs that the wicked shall not go unpunished. See <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:6">2 Peter 2:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:7">Jude 1:7</span>.</p>
