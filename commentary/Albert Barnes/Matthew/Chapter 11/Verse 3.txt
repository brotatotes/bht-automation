<p style=" "><b>Art thou he that should come? - </b>That is, Art thou the Messiah, or the Christ? The Jews expected a Saviour. His coming had been long foretold, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ge+49:10">Genesis 49:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="isa+9:1-6">Isaiah 9:1-6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="isa+11:1-5">Isaiah 11:1-5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="isa+35:4-6">Isaiah 35:4-6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="isa+53:1-12">Isaiah 53:1-12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="da+9:24-27">Daniel 9:24-27</span>. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:14">John 6:14</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="de+18:18-19">Deuteronomy 18:18-19</span>. In common language, therefore, he was familiarly described as “he that was to come.” Luke adds here <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:21">Luke 7:21</span>, that at the time when the messengers came to him, Jesus “cured many of their infirmities, and plagues, and of evil spirits.” An answer was therefore ready to the inquiries of John.</p>
