<p style=" "><b>Then were brought little children - </b>See also <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:13-16">Mark 10:13-16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:15-17">Luke 18:15-17</span>. Probably these were brought by some of his followers, who desired not only to devote themselves to Jesus, but all that they had - their children as well as themselves. All the Jews were accustomed to devote their children to God by circumcision. It was natural, therefore, under the new dispensation, that it should be done. Luke says they were infants. They were undoubtedly those who were not old enough to come by choice, but their coming was an act of the parents.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Put his hands on them and pray - </b>It was customary among the Jews, when blessings were sought for others in prayer, to lay the hands on the head of the person prayed for, implying a kind of consecration to God. See <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ge+48:14">Genesis 48:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:18">Matthew 9:18</span>. They had also much confidence in the prayers of pious men, believing that those blessed by a saint or a prophet would be happy. See <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="nu+22:6">Numbers 22:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:28">Luke 2:28</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>The disciples rebuked them - </b>That is, reproved them, or told them it was improper. This they did, probably, either:</p>
<p style="margin-left: 2.0em;  "> 1.Because they thought that they were too young; or,</p>
<p style="margin-left: 2.0em;  "> 2.Because they thought that they would be troublesome to their Master.</p>
