<p style=" "><b>All this was done ... - </b>The prophecy here quoted is found in <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="zec+9:9">Zechariah 9:9</span>. It was always, by the Jews, applied to the Messiah.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Daughter of Zion - </b>That is, “Jerusalem.” “Zion” was one of the hills on which the city of Jerusalem was built. On this stood the city of David and some strong fortresses. The names “daughter and virgin” were given to it often, in accordance with the Oriental figurative manner of expression. See the notes at <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="isa+1:8">Isaiah 1:8</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="am+5:2">Amos 5:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ps+45:13">Psalms 45:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ps+137:8">Psalms 137:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="isa+47:1">Isaiah 47:1</span>. It was given to them as an expression of their beauty or comeliness.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Meek - </b>See the notes at <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:5">Matthew 5:5</span>. The expression here rather denotes “peaceful, not warlike;” not with pomp, and state, and the ensigns of ambition. He came in the manner in which kings were accustomed to ride, but with none of their pride and ambitious feeling.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Sitting upon an ass ... - </b>He rode on the colt (Mark and Luke). This expression in Matthew is one which is common with all writers. See <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ge+19:29">Genesis 19:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="jdg+12:7">Judges 12:7</span>.</p>
