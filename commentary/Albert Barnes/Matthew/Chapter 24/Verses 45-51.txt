<p style=" "><b>Shall cut him asunder - </b>This kind of punishment was anciently practiced.</p>
<p style=" ">Sometimes it was done by the sword. sometimes by saws. It was practiced among the Chaldeans <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="da+2:5">Daniel 2:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="da+3:29">Daniel 3:29</span>, and among the Hebrews, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2sa+12:31">2 Samuel 12:31</span>; <span class="_0000ff"><u>1Sa 15:33</u></span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1ki+3:25">1 Kings 3:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:37">Hebrews 11:37</span>. It was also practiced by the Egyptians and Romans. It is not, perhaps, here to be taken literally, but signifies that the wicked servant should be severely punished.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Hypocrites - </b>See the notes at <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:2">Matthew 6:2</span>. They are spoken of here as the worst of people.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Weeping and gnashing of teeth - </b>See the notes at <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:12-13">Matthew 8:12-13</span>. The unfaithful and wicked minister of God, who lives without expectation or fear of judgment, shall suffer the severest punishment inflicted on sinners in the world of woe.</p>
