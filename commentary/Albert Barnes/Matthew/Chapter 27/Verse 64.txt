<p style=" "><b>Until the third day - </b>That is, during two nights and the intervening day. This proves that when the Jews spoke of “three days,” they did not of necessity mean three “whole days,” but parts of three days, as was the case in our Saviour’s lying in the grave. See the notes at <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:40">Matthew 12:40</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>The last error shall be worse than the first - </b>That is, the last “deception,” or the taking him from the tomb, pretending that he rose, will have a wider influence among the people than the first, or his pretending to be the Messiah.</p>
