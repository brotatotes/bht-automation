<p style=" ">This chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:0">Philippians 4:0</span> comprises the following points:</p>
<p style="margin-left: 2.0em;  "> I. Exhortations; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:1-3">Philippians 4:1-3</span>.</p>
<p style="margin-left: 2.75em;  "> (1)He exhorts them to stand fast in the Lord; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:1">Philippians 4:1</span>.</p>
<p style="margin-left: 2.75em;  "> (2)He entreats Euodias and Syntyche, who appear to have been alienated from each other, to be reconciled; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:2">Philippians 4:2</span>.</p>
<p style="margin-left: 2.75em;  "> (3)He entreats one whom he calls a “true yoke-fellow” to render assistance to those women who had labored with him in the gospel; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:3">Philippians 4:3</span>.</p>
<p style="margin-left: 2.0em;  "> II. Commands; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:4-9">Philippians 4:4-9</span>. He commands them to rejoice in the Lord always, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:4">Philippians 4:4</span>; to let their moderation be known to all, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:5">Philippians 4:5</span>; to have no anxiety about worldly matters, but in all their necessities to go to God, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:6-7">Philippians 4:6-7</span>; and to do whatever was honest, just, pure, lovely, and of good report, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:8-9">Philippians 4:8-9</span>.</p>
<p style="margin-left: 2.0em;  "> III. A grateful acknowledgment of their kindness; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:10-19">Philippians 4:10-19</span>. He says that their care of him had been manifested again, in such a way as to be highly grateful to his feelings; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:10">Philippians 4:10</span>. He did not indeed say that he had suffered, for he had learned, in whatever state he was, to be content <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:11-13">Philippians 4:11-13</span>; but they had shown a proper spirit in endeavoring to relieve his necessities;<span class="_0000ff"><span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:14">Philippians 4:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:14">Philippians 4:14</span></span>. He remarks that their church was the only one that had aided him when he was in Macedonia, and that they had sent to him more than once when he was in Thessalonica, and says that their favor now was an offering acceptable to God, who would abundantly reward them; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:15-20">Philippians 4:15-20</span>.</p>
<p style="margin-left: 2.0em;  "> IV. Salutations; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:21-23">Philippians 4:21-23</span>.</p>
