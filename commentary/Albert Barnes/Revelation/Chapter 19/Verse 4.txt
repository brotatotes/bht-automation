<p style=" "><b>And the four and twenty elders and the four beasts - </b>See the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="re+4:4">Revelation 4:4</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="re+4:6-7">Revelation 4:6-7</span>. As representatives of the church, and as interested in its welfare, they are now introduced as rejoicing in its final triumph, and in the destruction of its last foe.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Fell down - </b>Prostrated themselves - the usual posture of worship.</p>
<p style=" "><b>And worshipped God that sat on the throne - </b><span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="re+4:2-3">Revelation 4:2-3</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="re+4:10">Revelation 4:10</span>. That is, they now adored him for what he had done in delivering the church from all its persecutions, and causing it to triumph in the world.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Saying, Amen - </b>See the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:13">Matthew 6:13</span>. The word here is expressive of approbation of what God had done; or of their solemn assent to all that had occurred in the destruction of the great enemy of the church.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Alleluia - </b>See the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="re+19:1">Revelation 19:1</span>. The repetition of this word so many times shows the intenseness of the joy of heaven in view of the final triumph of the church.</p>
