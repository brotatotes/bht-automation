<p style=" "><b>For without are dogs - </b>The wicked, the depraved, the vile: for of such characters the dogs, an unclean animal among the Jews, was regarded as a symbol, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="de+23:18">Deuteronomy 23:18</span>. On the meaning of the expression, see the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="php+3:2">Philippians 3:2</span>. The word “without” means that they would not be admitted into the heavenly city, the New Jerusalem, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="re+21:8">Revelation 21:8</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="re+21:27">Revelation 21:27</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>And sorcerers, ... - </b>All these characters are specified in <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="re+21:8">Revelation 21:8</span>, as excluded from heaven. See the notes on that verse. The only change is, that those who “love and make a lie” are added to the list; that is, who delight in lies, or what is false.</p>
