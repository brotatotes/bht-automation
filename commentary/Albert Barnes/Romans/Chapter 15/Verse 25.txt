<p style=" "><b>But now I go ... - </b>I am about to go now. The mention of this intended journey to Jerusalem is introduced in several other places, and is so mentioned that Dr. Paley has derived from it a very strong argument for the genuineness of this Epistle. This intended journey is mentioned in <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:21">Acts 19:21</span>, “Paul purposed in the spirit, when he had passed through Macedonia and Achaia, to go to Jerusalem, saying after I have been there, I must also see Rome;” see also <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:2-3">Acts 20:2-3</span>. That he “went” to Jerusalem according to his purpose is recorded in his defense before Felix <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:17">Acts 24:17</span>, “Now after many years, I came to bring alms to my nation and offerings.”</p>
<p style=" "><b>To minister to the saints - </b>To supply their necessities by bearing the contribution which the churches have made for them.</p>
