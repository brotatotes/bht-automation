<p style=" "><b>Have sealed to them - </b>That is, have “secured it” to them. To seal an instrument of writing, a contract, deed, etc. is to “authenticate it,” to make it “sure.” In this sense it is used here. Paul was going himself to see that it was placed “securely” in their hands.</p>
<p style=" "><b>This fruit - </b>This result of the liberality of the Gentile churches - the fruit which their benevolence had produced.</p>
<p style=" "><b>I will come ... - </b>This was Paul’s full purpose; but it is not clear that he ever accomplished it; Note, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:24">Romans 15:24</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>By you - </b>Taking Rome in my way.</p>
