<p style=" "><b>For all have sinned - </b>This was the point which he had fully established in the discussion in these chapters.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Have come short - </b>Greek, “Are deficient in regard to;” are lacking, etc. Here it means, that they had failed to obtain, or were destitute of.</p>
<p style=" "><b>The glory of God - </b>The praise or approbation of God. They had sought to be justified, or approved, by God; but all had failed. Their works of the Law had not secured his approbation; and they were therefore under condemnation. The word “glory” (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δόξα</span></span> doxa) is often used in the sense of praise, or approbation, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:41">John 5:41</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:44">John 5:44</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:18">John 7:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:50">John 8:50</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:54">John 8:54</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:43">John 12:43</span>.</p>
