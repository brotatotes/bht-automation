<p style=" "><b>Who are Israelites - </b>Descended from Israel, or Jacob; honored by having such an ancestor, and by bearing a name so distinguished as that of his descendants. It was formerly the honorable appellation of the people of God.</p>
<p style=" "><b>To whom pertaineth - </b>To whom it belongs. It was their elevated external privilege.</p>
<p style=" "><b>The adoption - </b>Of the nation into the family of God, or to be regarded as His special people; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="de+7:6">Deuteronomy 7:6</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>And the glory - </b>The symbol of the divine presence that attended them from Egypt, and that finally rested over the ark in the first temple - “the Shechinah;” <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ex+13:21-22">Exodus 13:21-22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ex+25:22">Exodus 25:22</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>And the covenants - </b>The various compacts or promises which had been made from time to time with Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, and with the nation; the pledges of the divine protection.</p>
<p style=" "><b>The giving of the law - </b>On Mount Sinai; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ex+20:0">Exodus 20:0</span>; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ps+147:19">Psalms 147:19</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>And the service of God - </b>The temple service; regarded by them as the pride and ornament of their nation.</p>
<p style=" "><b>And the promises - </b>Of the Messiah; and of the spread of the true religion from them as a nation.</p>
