<p style=" "><b>For there are many unruly and vain talkers and deceivers - </b>There are many persons who are indisposed to submit to authority (see the word unruly in <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:6">Titus 1:6</span>); many who are vain talkers - who are more given to talk than to the duties of practical religion (see the character of “Talkative,” in the Pilgrim’s Progress); and many who live to deceive others under the mask of religion. They make great pretensions to piety; they are fluent in argument, and they urge their views in a plausible manner.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Specially they of the circumcision - </b>Jews, spoken of here as “of the circumcision” particularly, because they urged the necessity of circumcision in order that men might be saved; Notes, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:1">Acts 15:1</span>. This proves that there were not a few Jews in the island of Crete.</p>
