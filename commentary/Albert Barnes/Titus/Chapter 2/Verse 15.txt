<p style=" "><b>These things speak and exhort - </b>Notes, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:2">1 Timothy 6:2</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>And rebuke with all authority - </b><span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:1">1 Timothy 5:1</span>, note, 20, note; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:2">2 Timothy 4:2</span> note. The word “authority” here means command - <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ἐπιταγὴ</span></span> epitagē; <span class="_0000ff"><u>1Co 7:6</u></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:25">1 Corinthians 7:25</span>; <span class="_0000ff"><u>2Co 8:8</u></span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:1">1 Timothy 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:3">Titus 1:3</span>. The sense here is, he was to do it decidedly, without ambiguity, without compromise, and without keeping anything back. He was to state these things not as being advice or counsel, but as the requirement of God.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Let no man despise thee - </b>That is, conduct yourself, as you may easily do, so as to command universal respect as a minister of God; see the notes at <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:12">1 Timothy 4:12</span>.</p>
