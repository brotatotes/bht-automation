<p style=" "><b>The aged women likewise - </b>Not only those who may have the office of deaconesses, but all aged females.</p>
<p style=" "><b>That they be in behaviour as becometh holiness - </b>Marg, “holy women.” The Greek word is not found elsewhere in the New Testament. It means appropriate to a sacred place or person, or becoming to religion. Their conduct should be such as the gospel requires.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Not false accusers - </b> - Margin, “make-bates.” Greek, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαβόλους</span></span> diabolous - the word commonly applied to the devil - “as the accuser.” See it explained in the notes at <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:11">1 Timothy 3:11</span>, where it is rendered slanderers.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Not given to much wine - </b>Notes, <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:0">1 Timothy 3:0</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Teachers of good things - </b>That is instructing the younger - whether their own children, or whether they sustain the office of deaconness, and are appointed to give instruction to younger females; compare the notes at <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:2-6">1 Timothy 5:2-6</span>.</p>
