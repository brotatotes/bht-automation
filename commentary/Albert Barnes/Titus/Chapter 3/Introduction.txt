<p style=" ">This chapter comprises the following subjects:</p>
<p style=" ">(1) Titus was to instruct his hearers to be subject to lawful authority, and in general to manifest meekness and gentleness towards all classes of men; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:1-2">Titus 3:1-2</span>.</p>
<p style=" ">(2) A reason is assigned why they should do this; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:3-8">Titus 3:3-8</span>. They who were Christians were once, indeed, like others, disobedient and unholy; they were regardless of law, and gave free indulgence to their evil propensities, but they had been redeemed for a better purpose, and it was the design of God in redeeming them, that they should manifest every kind of virtue.</p>
<p style=" ">(3) Titus was to avoid foolish questions, and contentions, and strifes about the law; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:9">Titus 3:9</span>.</p>
<p style=" ">(4) He who was a heretic was to be rejected after suitable admonitions; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:10-11">Titus 3:10-11</span>.</p>
<p style=" ">(5) Paul directs Titus to come to him at Nicopolis, and to bring Zenas and Apollos with him; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:12-14">Titus 3:12-14</span>.</p>
<p style=" ">(6) He closes with the customary salutations; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:15">Titus 3:15</span>.</p>
