<p style=" "><b>But after that - </b>Greek, when - <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ὅτε</span></span> hote The meaning is, that “when the love of God was manifested in the plan of salvation, he saved us from this state God appeared” after we had sinned in this way, but that when his mercy was thus displayed we were converted from our sins, and made pure in his sight.</p>
<p style=" "><b>The kindness - </b><span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρηστότης</span></span> chrēstotēs - “the goodness, or the benignity.” The word is rendered “goodness” and “good” in <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:4">Romans 2:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:12">Romans 3:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:22">Romans 11:22</span>, thrice; “kindness,” <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:6">2 Corinthians 6:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:7">Ephesians 2:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="col+3:12">Colossians 3:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:4">Titus 3:4</span>; and “gentleness,” <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:22">Galatians 5:22</span>. The act of redeeming us was one of great kindness, or goodness.</p>
<p style=" "><b>And love of God - </b>Margin, “pity.” The Greek word is <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλανθρωπία</span></span> philanthrōpia - “philanthropy - the love of man.” The plan of salvation was founded on love to man, and was the highest expression of that love; the notes at <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:16">John 3:16</span>. The Greek of this verse is, “When the kindness and love of God our Saviour to man was manifested, he saved us” <span class="scriptRef" com="bnb" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:5">Titus 3:5</span>, to wit, from those sins of which we had before been guilty.</p>
