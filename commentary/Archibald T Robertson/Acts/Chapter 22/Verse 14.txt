 Hath appointed thee  (προεχειρισατο). First aorist middle indicative of προχειριζω, old verb to put forth into one's hands, to take into one's hands beforehand, to plan, propose, determine. In the N.T. only in Acts 3:20; Acts 22:14; Acts 26:16. Three infinitives after this verb of God's purpose about Paul:to know  (γνωνα, second aorist active of γινωσκω) his will,to see  (ιδειν, second aorist active of οραω) the Righteous One (cf. Acts 3:14),to hear  (ακουσα, first aorist active of ακουω) a voice from his mouth.
