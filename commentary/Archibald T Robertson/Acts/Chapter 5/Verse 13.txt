 Durst  (ετολμα). Imperfect active of τολμαω, old verb, not to fear or shun through fear, boldly to take a stand. The fate of Ananias and Sapphira continued to hold many in check.Join  (κολλασθα). Present middle infinitive of κολλαω, old verb to cleave to like glue as in Luke 15:15 which see. Seven times in Acts (Acts 9:26; Acts 10:28; Acts 17:34). The outsiders (the rest) preferred, many of them, to remain outside for the present, especially the rulers.Howbeit the people  (αλλ'--ο λαος). Probably individuals among the people, the populace as distinct from the rulers and hostile outsiders.
