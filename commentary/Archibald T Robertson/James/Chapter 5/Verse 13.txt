 Is any suffering?  (κακοπαθε τισ;). See verse James 5:10 for κακοπαθια. The verb in N.T. occurs only here and in 2 Timothy 2:3; 2 Timothy 2:9; 2 Timothy 4:5. The lively interrogative is common in the diatribe and suits the style of James.Among you  (εν υμιν). As in James 3:13.Let him pray  (προσευχεσθω). Present middle imperative, "let him keep on praying" (instead of cursing as in verse James 5:12).Is any cheerful  (ευθυμει;). Present active indicative of ευθυμεω, old verb from ευθυμος (Acts 27:36), in N.T. only here and Acts 27:22; Acts 27:25.Let him sing praise  (ψαλλετω). Present active imperative of ψαλλω, originally to twang a chord as on a harp, to sing praise to God whether with instrument or without, in N.T. only here, 1 Corinthians 14:15; Romans 15:9; Ephesians 5:19. "Let him keep on making melody."
