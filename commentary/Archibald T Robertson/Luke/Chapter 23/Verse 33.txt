 The skull  (το κρανιον). Probably because it looked like a skull. See on Matthew 27:33; Mark 15:22.There they crucified him  (εκε εσταυρωσαν). There between the two robbers and on the very cross on which Barabbas, the leader of the robber band, was to have been crucified.One  (ον μεν),the other  (ον δε). Common idiom of contrast with this old demonstrative ος and μεν and δε.
