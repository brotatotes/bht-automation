<p><b> Seeing that </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επειδη</span></span>). Resumes from verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:21">1 Corinthians 1:21</span>. The structure is not clear, but probably verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:23">1 Corinthians 1:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:24">1 Corinthians 1:24</span> form a sort of conclusion or apodosis to verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:22">1 Corinthians 1:22</span> the protasis. The resumptive, almost inferential, use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span> like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα</span></span> in the apodosis is not unusual.</p>
<p><b>Ask for signs </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σημεια αιτουσιν</span></span>). The Jews often came to Jesus asking for signs (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:38">Matthew 12:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:1">Matthew 16:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:30">John 6:30</span>).</p>
<p><b>Seek after wisdom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σοφιαν ζητουσιν</span></span>). "The Jews claimed to <i>possess</i> the truth: the Greeks were seekers, <i>speculators</i>" (Vincent) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:23">Acts 17:23</span>. </p>
