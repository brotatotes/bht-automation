<p><b> But we preach Christ crucified </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημεις δε κηρυσσομεν Χριστον εσταυρωμενον</span></span>). Grammatically stated as a partial result (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span>) of the folly of both Jews and Greeks, actually in sharp contrast. We proclaim, "we do not discuss or dispute" (Lightfoot). Christ (Messiah) as crucified, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:2">1 Corinthians 2:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:1">Galatians 3:1</span>, "not a sign-shower nor a philosopher" (Vincent). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σταυροω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Stumbling-block </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκανδαλον</span></span>). Papyri examples mean trap or snare which here tripped the Jews who wanted a conquering Messiah with a world empire, not a condemned and crucified one (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:42">Matthew 27:42</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:21">Luke 24:21</span>).</p>
<p><b>Foolishness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μωριαν</span></span>). Folly as shown by their conduct in Athens (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:32">Acts 17:32</span>). </p>
