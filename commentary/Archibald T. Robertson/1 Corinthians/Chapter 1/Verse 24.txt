<p><b> But to them that are called </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτοις δε τοις κλητοις</span></span>). Dative case, to the called themselves.</p>
<p><b>Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χριστον</span></span>). Accusative case repeated, object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κηρυσσομεν</span></span>, both</p>
<p><b>the power of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεου δυναμιν</span></span>) and</p>
<p><b>the wisdom of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεου σοφιαν</span></span>). No article, but made definite by the genitive. Christ crucified is God's answer to both Jew and Greek and the answer is understood by those with open minds. </p>
