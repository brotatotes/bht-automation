<p><b> The foolishness of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το μωρον του θεου</span></span>). Abstract neuter singular with the article, the foolish act of God (the Cross as regarded by the world).</p>
<p><b>Wiser than men </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σοφωτερον των ανθρωπων</span></span>). Condensed comparison, wiser than the wisdom of men. Common Greek idiom (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:20">Matthew 5:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:36">John 5:36</span>) and quite forcible, brushes all men aside.</p>
<p><b>The weakness of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το ασθενες του θεου</span></span>). Same idiom here,</p>
<p><b>the weak act of God </b>, as men think,</p>
<p><b>is stronger </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ισχυροτερον</span></span>). The Cross seemed God's defeat. It is conquering the world and is the mightiest force on earth. </p>
