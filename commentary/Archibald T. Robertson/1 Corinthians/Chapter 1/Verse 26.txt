<p><b> Behold </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλεπετε</span></span>). Same form for imperative present active plural and indicative. Either makes sense as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:39">John 5:39</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εραυνατε</span></span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:1">1 Corinthians 14:1</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστευετε</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Calling </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλησιν</span></span>). The act of calling by God, based not on the external condition of those called (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλητο</span></span>, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:2">1 Corinthians 1:2</span>), but on God's sovereign love. It is a clinching illustration of Paul's argument, an <i>argumentum ad hominen</i>.</p>
<p><b>How that </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span>). Explanatory apposition to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλησιν</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>After the flesh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα σαρκα</span></span>). According to the standards of the flesh and to be used not only with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σοφο</span></span> (wise, philosophers), but also <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυνατο</span></span> (men of dignity and power), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευγενεις</span></span> (noble, high birth), the three claims to aristocracy (culture, power, birth).</p>
<p><b>Are called </b>. Not in the Greek, but probably to be supplied from the idea in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλησιν</span></span>. </p>
