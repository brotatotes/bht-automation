<p><b> God chose </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξελεξατο ο θεος</span></span>). First aorist middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκλεγω</span></span>, old verb to pick out, to choose, the middle for oneself. It expands the idea in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλησιν</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:26">1 Corinthians 1:26</span>). Three times this solemn verb occurs here with the purpose stated each time. Twice the same purpose is expressed,</p>
<p><b>that he might put to shame </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα καταισχυνη</span></span>, first aorist active subjunctive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> of old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταισχυνω</span></span>, perfective use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span>). The purpose in the third example is</p>
<p><b>that he might bring to naught </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα καταργηση</span></span>, make idle, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αργος</span></span>, rare in old Greek, but frequent in Paul). The contrast is complete in each paradox:</p>
<p><b>the foolish things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα μωρα</span></span>),</p>
<p><b>the wild men </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους σοφους</span></span>);</p>
<p><b>the weak things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα ασθενη</span></span>),</p>
<p><b>the strong things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα ισχυρα</span></span>);</p>
<p><b>the things that are not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα μη οντα</span></span>),</p>
<p><b>and that are despised </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα εξουθενημενα</span></span>, considered nothing, perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξουθενεω</span></span>),</p>
<p><b>the things that are </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα οντα</span></span>). It is a studied piece of rhetoric and powerfully put. </p>
