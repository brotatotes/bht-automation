<p><b> So that ye come behind in no gift </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωστε υμας μη υστερεισθα εν μηδεν χαρισματ</span></span>). Consecutive clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωστε</span></span> and the infinitive and the double negative. Come behind (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υστερεισθα</span></span>) is to be late (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υστερος</span></span>), old verb seen already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:21">Mark 10:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:20">Matthew 19:20</span>. It is a wonderful record here recorded. But in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:7-11">2 Corinthians 8:7-11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:1-7">2 Corinthians 9:1-7</span> Paul will have to complain that they have not paid their pledges for the collection, pledges made over a year before, a very modern complaint.</p>
<p><b>Waiting for the revelation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεκδεχομενους την αποκαλυψιν</span></span>). This double compound is late and rare outside of Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:7">1 Corinthians 1:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:5">Galatians 5:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:19">Romans 8:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:23">Romans 8:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:25">Romans 8:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:20">Philippians 3:20</span>), <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:20">1 Peter 3:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:28">Hebrews 9:28</span>. It is an eager expectancy of the second coming of Christ here termed revelation like the eagerness in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσδεχομενο</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:13">Titus 2:13</span> for the same event. "As if that attitude of expectation were the highest posture that can be attained here by the Christian" (F.W. Robertson). </p>
