<p><b> Shall confirm </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βεβαιωσε</span></span>). Direct reference to the same word in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:6">1 Corinthians 1:6</span>. The relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος</span></span> (who) points to Christ.</p>
<p><b>Unto the end </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως τελους</span></span>). End of the age till Jesus comes, final preservation of the saints.</p>
<p><b>That ye be unreproveable </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεγκλητους</span></span>). Alpha privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγκαλεω</span></span>, to accuse, old verbal, only in Paul in N.T. Proleptic adjective in the predicate accusative agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμας</span></span> (you) without <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωστε</span></span> and the infinitive as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:13">1 Thessalonians 3:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:23">1 Thessalonians 5:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:21">Philippians 3:21</span>. "Unimpeachable, for none will have the right to impeach" (Robertson and Plummer) as Paul shows in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:33">Romans 8:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:22">Colossians 1:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:28">Colossians 1:28</span>. </p>
