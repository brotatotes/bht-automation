<p><b> Neither murmur ye </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηδε γογγυζετε</span></span>). Implying that some of them were murmuring. For this late picturesque onomatopoetic verb see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:11">Matthew 20:11</span>. The reference seems to be to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+16:41">Numbers 16:41</span> after the punishment of Korah.</p>
<p><b>By the destroyer </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο του ολοθρευτου</span></span>). This word, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ολοθρευω</span></span> (late verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ολεθρος</span></span>, destruction) occurs only here, so far as known. The reference is to the destroying angel of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+12:23">Exodus 12:23</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ολοθρευων</span></span>). </p>
