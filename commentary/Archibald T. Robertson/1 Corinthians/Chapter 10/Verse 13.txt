<p><b> Hath taken </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειληφεν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>But such as man can bear </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε μη ανθρωπινος</span></span>). Except a human one. Old adjective meaning falling to the lot of man.</p>
<p><b>Above that ye are able </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ ο δυνασθε</span></span>). Ellipsis, but plain. There is comfort in that God is faithful, trustworthy (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστος</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>The way of escape </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την εκβασιν</span></span>). "The way out" is always there right along with (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν</span></span>) the temptation. This old word only here in N.T. and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:7">Hebrews 13:7</span> about death. It is cowardly to yield to temptation and distrustful of God. </p>
