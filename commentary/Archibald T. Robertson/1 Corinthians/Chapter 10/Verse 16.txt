<p><b> The cup of blessing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το ποτηριον της ευλογιας</span></span>). The cup over which we pronounce a blessing as by Christ at the institution of the ordinance.</p>
<p><b>A communion of the blood of Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινωνια του αιματος του Χριστου</span></span>). Literally, a participation in (objective genitive) the blood of Christ. The word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινωνια</span></span> is an old one from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινωνος</span></span>, partner, and so here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:1">Philippians 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:10">Philippians 3:10</span>. It can mean also fellowship (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:9">Galatians 2:9</span>) or contribution (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:4">2 Corinthians 8:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:5">Philippians 1:5</span>). It is, of course, a spiritual participation in the blood of Christ which is symbolized by the cup. Same meaning for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινωνια</span></span> in reference to "the body of Christ."</p>
<p><b>The bread which we break </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον αρτον ον κλωμεν</span></span>). The loaf. Inverse attraction of the antecedent (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρτον</span></span>) to the case (accusative) of the relative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ον</span></span>) according to classic idiom (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 488). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αρτος</span></span> probably from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρω</span></span>, to join or fit (flour mixed with water and baked). The mention of the cup here before the bread does not mean that this order was observed for see the regular order of bread and then cup in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:24-27">1 Corinthians 11:24-27</span>. </p>
