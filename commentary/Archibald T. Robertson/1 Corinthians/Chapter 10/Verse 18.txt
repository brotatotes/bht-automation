<p><b> After the flesh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα σαρκα</span></span>). The literal Israel, the Jewish people, not the spiritual Israel (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ισραηλ κατα πνευμα</span></span>) composed of both Jews and Gentiles, the true children of faith (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:28">Romans 2:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:8">Romans 9:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:7">Galatians 3:7</span>).</p>
<p><b>Communion with the altar </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινωνο του θυσιαστηριου</span></span>). Same idea in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινωνο</span></span> participators in, partners in, sharers in (with objective genitive). The word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυσιαστηριον</span></span> is from late verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυσιαζω</span></span>, to offer sacrifice, and that from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυσια</span></span>, sacrifice, and that from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυω</span></span>, common verb to slay, to sacrifice (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:20">1 Corinthians 10:20</span>). The Israelites who offer sacrifices have a spiritual participation in the altar. </p>
