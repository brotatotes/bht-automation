<p><b> But if any man say unto you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν δε τις υμιν ειπη</span></span>). Condition of third class. Suppose at such a banquet a "weak" brother makes the point to you: "This hath been offered in sacrifice" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτο ιεροθυτον εστιν</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hιεροθυτον</span></span>, late word in Plutarch, rare in inscriptions and papyri, only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Eat not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη εσθιετε</span></span>). Present imperative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> prohibiting the habit of eating then. Pertinent illustration to the point of doing what is expedient and edifying.</p>
<p><b>That shewed it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον μηνυσαντα</span></span>). First aorist active articular participle (accusative case because of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span>) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηνυω</span></span>, old verb, to point out, to disclose. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:37">Luke 20:37</span>. </p>
