<p><b> With most of them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τοις πλειοσιν αυτων</span></span>). "A mournful understatement," for only two (Caleb and Joshua) actually reached the Promised Land (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+14:30-32">Numbers 14:30-32</span>). All the rest were rejected or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδοκιμο</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:27">1 Corinthians 9:27</span>).</p>
<p><b>Were overthrown </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεστρωθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταστρωννυμ</span></span>, old compound verb, to stretch or spread down as of a couch, to lay low (Euripides), as if by a hurricane. Powerful picture of the desolation wrought by the years of disobedience and wanderings in the desert by this verb quoted from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+14:16">Numbers 14:16</span>. </p>
