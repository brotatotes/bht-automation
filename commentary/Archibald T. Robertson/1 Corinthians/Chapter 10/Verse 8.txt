<p><b> Neither let us commit fornication </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηδε πορνευωμεν</span></span>). More exactly, And let us cease practicing fornication as some were already doing (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:11">1 Corinthians 6:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:2">1 Corinthians 7:2</span>). The connection between idolatry and fornication was very close (see Jowett, <i>Epistles of Paul</i>, II, p. 70) and see about Baal-Peor (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+25:1-9">Numbers 25:1-9</span>). It was terribly true of Corinth where prostitution was part of the worship of Aphrodite.</p>
<p><b>In one day </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μια ημερα</span></span>). An item that adds to horror of the plague in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+25:9">Numbers 25:9</span> where the total number is 24,000 instead of 23,000 as here for one day. </p>
