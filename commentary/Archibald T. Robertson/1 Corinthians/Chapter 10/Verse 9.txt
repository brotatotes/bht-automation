<p><b> Neither let us tempt the Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηδε εκπειραζωμεν τον Κυριον</span></span>). So the best MSS. instead of Christ. This compound occurs in LXX and in N.T. always about Christ (here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:7">Matthew 4:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:12">Luke 4:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:25">Luke 10:25</span>). Let us cease sorely (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ-</span></span>) tempting the Lord by such conduct.</p>
<p><b>And perished by the serpents </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα υπο των οφεων απωλλυντο</span></span>). Vivid imperfect middle (cf. aorist middle <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απωλοντο</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:10">1 Corinthians 10:10</span>), were perishing day by day. The story is told in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+21:6">Numbers 21:6</span>. The use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο</span></span> for agent with the intransitive middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολλυμ</span></span> is regular. Note the Ionic uncontracted genitive plural <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφεων</span></span> rather than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφων</span></span>. </p>
