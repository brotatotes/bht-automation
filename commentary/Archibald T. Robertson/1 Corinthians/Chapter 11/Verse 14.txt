<p><b> Nature itself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η φυσις αυτη</span></span>). He reenforces the appeal to custom by the appeal to nature in a question that expects the affirmative answer (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδε</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Φυσις</span></span>, from old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυω</span></span>, to produce, like our word nature (Latin <i>natura</i>), is difficult to define. Here it means native sense of propriety (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:14">Romans 2:14</span>) in addition to mere custom, but one that rests on the objective difference in the constitution of things. </p>
