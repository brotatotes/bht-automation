<p><b> Contentious </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλονεικος</span></span>). Old adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλοσ, νεικος</span></span>), fond of strife. Only here in N.T. If he only existed in this instance, the disputatious brother.</p>
<p><b>Custom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνηθειαν</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνηθης</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν, ηθος</span></span>), like Latin <i>consuetudo</i>, intercourse, intimacy. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+8:7">1 Corinthians 8:7</span> which see. "In the sculptures of the catacombs the women have a close-fitting head-dress, while the men have the hair short" (Vincent). </p>
