<p><b> This </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτο</span></span>). Probably the preceding one about the head-dress of women, and transition to what follows.</p>
<p><b>I praise you not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ επαινω</span></span>). In contrast to the praise in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:2">1 Corinthians 11:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>For the better </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το κρεισσον</span></span>). Neuter articular comparative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρατυς</span></span>, but used as comparative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλος</span></span>, good. Attic form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρειττον</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>For the worse </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το ησσον</span></span>). Old comparative from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκα</span></span>, softly, used as comparative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακος</span></span>, bad. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:15">2 Corinthians 12:15</span>. </p>
