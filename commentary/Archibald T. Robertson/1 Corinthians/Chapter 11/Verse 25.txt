<p><b> After supper </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα το δειπνησα</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μετα</span></span> and the articular aorist active infinitive, "after the dining" (or the supping) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:20">Luke 22:20</span>.</p>
<p><b>The new covenant </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η καινη διαθηκη</span></span>). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαθηκη</span></span> see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:28">Matthew 26:28</span>. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καινος</span></span> see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:38">Luke 5:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:20">Luke 22:20</span>. The position of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστιν</span></span> before <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω αιματ</span></span> (in my blood) makes it a secondary or additional predicate and not to be taken just with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαθηκη</span></span> (covenant or will).</p>
<p><b>As oft as ye drink it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οσακις αν πινητε</span></span>). Usual construction for general temporal clause of repetition (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span> and the present subjunctive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οσακις</span></span>). So in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:26">1 Corinthians 11:26</span>. </p>
