<p><b> Till he come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχρ ου ελθη</span></span>). Common idiom (with or without <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span>) with the aorist subjunctive for future time (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 975). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:18">Luke 22:18</span> we have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως ου ελθη</span></span>. The Lord's Supper is the great preacher (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταγγελλετε</span></span>) of the death of Christ till his second coming (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:29">Matthew 26:29</span>). </p>
