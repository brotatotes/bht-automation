<p><b> If he discern not the body </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη διακρινων το σωμα</span></span>). So-called conditional use of the participle, "not judging the body." Thus he eats and drinks judgment (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κριμα</span></span>) on himself. The verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια-κρινω</span></span> is an old and common word, our</p>
<p><b>dis-cri-minate </b>, to distinguish. Eating the bread and drinking the wine as symbols of the Lord's body and blood in death probes one's heart to the very depths. </p>
