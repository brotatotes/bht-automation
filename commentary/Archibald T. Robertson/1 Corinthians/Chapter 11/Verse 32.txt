<p><b> Ye are chastened of the Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο του Κυριου παιδευομεθα</span></span>). On this sense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παιδευω</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παις</span></span>, child, to train a child (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:22">Acts 7:22</span>), to discipline with words (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:25">2 Timothy 2:25</span>), to chastise with scourges see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:16">Luke 23:16</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:7">Hebrews 12:7</span>), and so by afflictions as here (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:6">Hebrews 12:6</span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hυπο του Κυριου</span></span> can be construed with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρινομενο</span></span> instead of with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παιδευομεθα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>With the world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν τω κοσμω</span></span>). Along with the world. Afflictions are meant to separate us from the doom of the wicked world. Final use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη</span></span> here with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατακριθωμεν</span></span> (first aorist passive subjunctive). </p>
