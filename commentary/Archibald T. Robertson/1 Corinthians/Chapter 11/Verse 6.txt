<p><b> Let her also be shorn </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα κειρασθω</span></span>). Aorist middle imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κειρω</span></span>, to shear (as sheep). Let her cut her hair close. A single act by the woman.</p>
<p><b>If it is a shame </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε δε αισχρον</span></span>). Condition of first class assumed to be true. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αισχρον</span></span> is old adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αισχος</span></span>, bareness, disgrace. Clearly Paul uses such strong language because of the effect on a woman's reputation in Corinth by such conduct that proclaimed her a lewd woman. Social custom varied in the world then as now, but there was no alternative in Corinth.</p>
<p><b>To be shorn or shaven </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το κειρασθα κα ξυρασθα</span></span>). Articular infinitives subject of copula <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστιν</span></span> understood, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κειρασθα</span></span> first aorist middle, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξυρασθα</span></span> present middle. Note change in tense.</p>
<p><b>Let her be veiled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατακαλυπτεσθω</span></span>). Present middle imperative of old compound <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα-καλυπτω</span></span>, here alone in N.T. Let her cover up herself with the veil (down, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span>, the Greek says, the veil hanging down from the head). </p>
