<p><b> If the foot shall say </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν ειπη ο πους</span></span>). Condition of third class (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and second aorist subjunctive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειπη</span></span>). In case the foot say.</p>
<p><b>I am not of the body </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ ειμ εκ του σωματος</span></span>). I am independent of the body, not dependent on the body.</p>
<p><b>It is not therefore not of the body </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου παρα τουτο ουκ εστιν εκ του σωματος</span></span>). Thinking or saying so does not change the fact. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Παρα τουτο</span></span> here means "alongside of this" (cf. IV Macc. 10:19) and so "because of," a rare use (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 616). The two negatives (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου--ουκ</span></span>) do not here destroy one another. Each retains its full force. </p>
