<p><b> The greater gifts </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα χαρισματα τα μειζονα</span></span>). Paul unhesitatingly ranks some spiritual gifts above others. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ζηλοω</span></span> here has good sense, not that of envy as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:9">Acts 7:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:4">1 Corinthians 13:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>And a still more excellent way </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ετ καθ' υπερβολην οδον</span></span>). In order to gain the greater gifts. "I show you a way <i>par excellence</i>," beyond all comparison (superlative idea in this adjunct, not comparative), like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθ' υπερβολην εις υπερβολην</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:17">2 Corinthians 4:17</span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hυπερβολη</span></span> is old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερβαλλω</span></span>, to throw beyond, to surpass, to excel (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:10">2 Corinthians 3:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:19">Ephesians 1:19</span>). "I show you a supremely excellent way." Chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:1">1 Corinthians 12:1</span> is this way, the way of love already laid down in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+8:1">1 Corinthians 8:1</span> concerning the question of meats offered to idols (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:7">1 John 4:7</span>). Poor division of chapters here. This verse belongs with chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:1">1 Corinthians 12:1</span>. </p>
