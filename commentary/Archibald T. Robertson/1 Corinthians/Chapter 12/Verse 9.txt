<p><b> Faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστις</span></span>). Not faith of surrender, saving faith, but wonder-working faith like that in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:2">1 Corinthians 13:2</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:20">Matthew 17:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:21">Matthew 21:21</span>). Note here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω αυτω πνευματ</span></span> (in the same Spirit) in contrast with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:8">1 Corinthians 12:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>Gifts of healings </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρισματα ιαματων</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιαμα</span></span>, old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιαομα</span></span>, common in LXX, in N.T. only in this chapter. It means acts of healing as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:30">Acts 4:30</span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:14">James 5:14</span>) and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:21">Luke 7:21</span> (of Jesus). Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> here as just before. </p>
