<p><b> A child </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νηπιος</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:1">1 Corinthians 3:1</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νηπιος</span></span> in contrast with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελειος</span></span> (adult).</p>
<p><b>I spake </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελαλουν</span></span>). Imperfect active, I used to talk.</p>
<p><b>I felt </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφρονουν</span></span>). Imperfect active, I used to think. Better, I used to understand.</p>
<p><b>I thought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελογιζομην</span></span>). Imperfect middle, I used to reason or calculate.</p>
<p><b>Now that I am become </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτε γεγονα</span></span>). Perfect active indicative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεγονα</span></span>, I have become a man (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανηρ</span></span>) and remain so (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:14">Ephesians 4:14</span>).</p>
<p><b>I have put away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηργηκα</span></span>). Perfect active indicative. I have made inoperative (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:8">1 Corinthians 13:8</span>) for good. </p>
