<p>Verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:4-7">1 Corinthians 13:4-7</span> picture the character or conduct of love in marvellous rhapsody.</p>
<p><b>Suffereth long </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακροθυμε</span></span>). Late <i>Koine</i> word (Plutarch) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακρος</span></span>, long, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυμος</span></span>, passion, ardour. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:7">James 5:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>Is kind </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρηστευετα</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρηστος</span></span> (useful, gracious, kind) and that from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χραομα</span></span>, to use. Not found elsewhere save in Clement of Rome and Eusebius. "Perhaps of Paul's coining" (Findlay). Perhaps a vernacular word ready for Paul. Gentle in behaviour.</p>
<p><b>Envieth not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου ζηλο</span></span>). Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζηλοω</span></span> (contraction <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οει=ο</span></span>, same as subjunctive and optative forms). Bad sense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζηλος</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζεω</span></span>, to boil, good sense in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:31">1 Corinthians 12:31</span>. Love is neither jealous nor envious (both ideas).</p>
<p><b>Vaunteth not itself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου περπερευετα</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περπερος</span></span>, vainglorious, braggart (Polybius, Epictetus) like Latin <i>perperus</i>. Only here in N.T. and earliest known example. It means play the braggart. Marcus Anton. V. 5 uses it with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρεσκευομα</span></span>, to play the toady.</p>
<p><b>Is not puffed up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου φυσιουτα</span></span>). Present direct middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυσιοω</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυσις</span></span> (late form for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυσαω, φυσιαω</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυσα</span></span>, bellows), to puff oneself out like a pair of bellows. This form in Herodas and Menander. Is not arrogant. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:6">1 Corinthians 4:6</span>. </p>
