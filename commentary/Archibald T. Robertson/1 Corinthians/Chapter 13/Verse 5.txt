<p><b> Doth not behave itself unseemly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ ασχημονε</span></span>). Old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασχημων</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:23">1 Corinthians 12:23</span>). In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:36">1 Corinthians 7:36</span>. Not indecent.</p>
<p><b>Seeketh not its own </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου ζητε τα εαυτης</span></span>). Its own interests (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:24">1 Corinthians 10:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:33">1 Corinthians 10:33</span>).</p>
<p><b>Is not provoked </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου παροξυνετα</span></span>). Old word. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:16">Acts 17:16</span> which see. Irritation or sharpness of spirit. And yet Paul felt it in Athens (exasperation) and he and Barnabas had <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παροξυσμος</span></span> (paroxysm) in Antioch (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:39">1 Corinthians 15:39</span>). See good sense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παροξυσμος</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:24">Hebrews 10:24</span>.</p>
<p><b>Taketh not account of evil </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου λογιζετα το κακον</span></span>). Old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογος</span></span>, to count up, to take account of as in a ledger or note-book, "the evil" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το κακον</span></span>) done to love with a view to settling the account. </p>
