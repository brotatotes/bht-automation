<p><b> Beareth all things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντα στεγε</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Στεγω</span></span> is old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στεγη</span></span>, roof, already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:12">1 Corinthians 9:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:1">1 Thessalonians 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:5">1 Thessalonians 3:5</span> which see. Love covers, protects, forbears (<i>suffert</i>, Vulgate). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:8">1 Peter 4:8</span> "because love covers a multitude of sins" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ αγαπη καλυπτε φηθος αμαρτιων</span></span>), throws a veil over.</p>
<p><b>Believeth all things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντα πιστευε</span></span>). Not gullible, but has faith in men.</p>
<p><b>Hopeth all things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντα ελπιζε</span></span>). Sees the bright side of things. Does not despair. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ενδυρεθ αλλ θινγς</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντα υπομενε</span></span>). Perseveres. Carries on like a stout-hearted soldier. If one knows Sir Joshua Reynolds's beautiful painting of the Seven Virtues (the four cardinal virtues of the Stoics--temperance, prudence, fortitude, justice--and the three Christian graces--faith, hope, love), he will find them all exemplified here as marks of love (the queen of them all). </p>
