<p><b> Love never faileth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hη αγαπη ουδεποτε πιπτε</span></span>). New turn for the perpetuity of love. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πιπτε</span></span> correct text, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκπιπτε</span></span>, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:17">Luke 16:17</span>. Love survives everything.</p>
<p><b>They shall be done away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταργηθησοντα</span></span>). First future passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταργεω</span></span>. Rare in old Greek, to make idle (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αργος</span></span>), inoperative. All these special spiritual gifts will pass. It is amazing how little of human work lasts.</p>
<p><b>They shall cease </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παυσοντα</span></span>). Future middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παυω</span></span>, to make cease. They shall make themselves cease or automatically cease of themselves. </p>
