<p><b> Follow after love </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διωκετε την αγαπην</span></span>). As if a veritable chase. Paul comes back to the idea in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:31">1 Corinthians 12:31</span> (same use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζηλουτε</span></span>) and proves the superiority of prophecy to the other spiritual gifts not counting faith, hope, love of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:13">1 Corinthians 13:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>But rather that ye may prophesy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαλλον δε ινα προφητευητε</span></span>). Distinct aim in view as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:5">1 Corinthians 14:5</span>. Old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προφητης</span></span>, common in N.T. Present subjunctive, "that ye may keep on prophesying." </p>
