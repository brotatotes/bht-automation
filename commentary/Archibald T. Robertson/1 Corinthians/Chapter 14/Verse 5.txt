<p><b> Except he interpret </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκτος ε μη διερμηνευη</span></span>). Pleonastic combination of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκτος</span></span> (preposition except) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε μη</span></span> (if not, unless) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:2">1 Corinthians 15:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:19">1 Timothy 5:19</span>. For use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span> with subjunctive rather than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:12">Philippians 3:12</span> (common enough in the <i>Koine</i>, Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, pp. 1017f., condition of third class). On the verb see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:30">1 Corinthians 12:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:27">Luke 24:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:36">Acts 9:36</span>.</p>
<p><b>Receive </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαβη</span></span>). Second aorist (ingressive) active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>, may get edification. </p>
