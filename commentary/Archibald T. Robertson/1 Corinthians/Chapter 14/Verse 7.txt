<p><b> Things without life </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αψυχα</span></span>). Without a soul (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψυχη</span></span>) or life. Old word only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Pipe </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυλος</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αω, αυω</span></span>, to blow), only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Harp </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κιθαρα</span></span>). Old word. Stringed instrument as pipe, a wind instrument.</p>
<p><b>If they give not a distinction in the sounds </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν διαστολην τοις φθογγοις μη δω</span></span>). Third class condition with second aorist active subjunctive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δω</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>. Common word in late Greek for difference (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαστελλω</span></span>, to send apart). In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:22">Romans 3:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:12">Romans 10:12</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Φθογγος</span></span> old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φθεγγομα</span></span>) for musical sounds vocal or instrumental. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:18">Romans 10:18</span>. </p>
