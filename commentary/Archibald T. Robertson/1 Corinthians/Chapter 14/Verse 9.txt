<p><b> Unless ye utter speech easy to be understood </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν μη ευσημον λογον δωτε</span></span>). Condition of third class again (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and aorist subjunctive). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ευσημον</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ</span></span>, well, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σημα</span></span>, sign) is old word, here only in N.T., well-marked, distinct, clear. Good enunciation, a hint for speakers.</p>
<p><b>Ye will be speaking into the air </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσεσθε εις αερα λαλουντες</span></span>). Periphrastic future indicative (linear action). Cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αερα δερων</span></span> (beating the air) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:26">1 Corinthians 9:26</span>. Cf. our talking to the wind. This was before the days of radio. </p>
