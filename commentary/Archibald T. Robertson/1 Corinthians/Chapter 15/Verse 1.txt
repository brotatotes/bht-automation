<p><b> I make known </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνωριζω</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:3">1 Corinthians 12:3</span> for this common verb. As if in reproach.</p>
<p><b>The gospel which I preached unto you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το ευαγγελιον ο ευηγγελισαμην υμιν</span></span>). Cognate accusative, "the gospel which I gospelized unto you." Note augment <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ-</span></span> like compound verb with preposition. Note repetition of relative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο, εν ωι, δ ου</span></span>, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιν</span></span> like relative) without <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span> (and), asyndeton. </p>
