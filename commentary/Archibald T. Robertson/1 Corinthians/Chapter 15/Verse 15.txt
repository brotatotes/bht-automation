<p><b> False witnesses of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευδομαρτυρες του θεου</span></span>). Late word, but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευδομαρτυρεω</span></span>, to bear false witness, old and common. The genitive (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του θεου</span></span>) can be either subjective (in God's service) or objective (concerning God). Either makes good sense.</p>
<p><b>Because we witnessed of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ εμαρτυρησαμεν κατα του θεου</span></span>). Vulgate has <i>adversus Deum</i>. This is the more natural way to take <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span> and genitive,</p>
<p><b>against God </b> not as equal to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ</span></span> (concerning). He would indeed make God play false in that case,</p>
<p><b>if so be that the dead are not raised </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειπερ αρα νεκρο ουκ εγειροντα</span></span>). Condition of first class, assumed as true. Note both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ</span></span> intensive particle</p>
<p><b>indeed </b> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρα</span></span> inferential particle</p>
<p><b>therefore </b>. </p>
