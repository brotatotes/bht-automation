<p><b> In what words I preached it unto you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιν λογο ευηγγελισαμην υμιν</span></span>). Almost certainly <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τις</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιν λογο</span></span>, locative or instrumental, in or with) here is used like the relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος</span></span> as is common in papyri (Moulton, <i>Prolegomena</i>, p. 93f.; Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 737f.). Even so it is not clear whether the clause depends on <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνωριζω</span></span> like the other relatives, but most likely so.</p>
<p><b>If we hold it fast </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε κατεχετε</span></span>). Condition of first class. Paul assumes that they are holding it fast.</p>
<p><b>Except ye believed in vain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκτος ε μη εικη επιστευσατε</span></span>). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκτος ε μη</span></span> see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:5">1 Corinthians 14:5</span>. Condition of first class, unless in fact ye did believe to no purpose (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εικη</span></span>, old adverb, only in Paul in N.T.). Paul holds this peril over them in their temptation to deny the resurrection. </p>
