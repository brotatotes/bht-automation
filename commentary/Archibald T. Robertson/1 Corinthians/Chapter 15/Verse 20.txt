<p><b> But now </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυν δε</span></span>). Emphatic form of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυν</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-</span></span> added (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:18">1 Corinthians 12:18</span>). It is the logical triumph of Paul after the <i>reductio ad impossibile</i> (Findlay) of the preceding argument.</p>
<p><b>The first-fruits </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαρχη</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαρχομα</span></span>, to offer firstlings or first-fruits. In LXX for first-fruits. In papyri for legacy-duty, entrance-fee, and also first-fruits as here. See also verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:23">1 Corinthians 15:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:15">1 Corinthians 16:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:23">Romans 8:23</span>, etc. Christ is "first-born from the dead" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:18">Colossians 1:18</span>). Others raised from the dead died again, but not so Jesus.</p>
<p><b>That sleep </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των κεκοιμημενων</span></span>). Perfect middle participle as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:52">Matthew 27:52</span> which see. Beautiful picture of death from which word (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοιμαομα</span></span>) comes our <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χεμετερψ</span></span>. </p>
