 But now  (νυν δε). Emphatic form of νυν with - added (cf. 1 Corinthians 12:18). It is the logical triumph of Paul after the reductio ad impossibile (Findlay) of the preceding argument.The first-fruits  (απαρχη). Old word from απαρχομα, to offer firstlings or first-fruits. In LXX for first-fruits. In papyri for legacy-duty, entrance-fee, and also first-fruits as here. See also verse 1 Corinthians 15:23; 1 Corinthians 16:15; Romans 8:23, etc. Christ is "first-born from the dead" (Colossians 1:18). Others raised from the dead died again, but not so Jesus.That sleep  (των κεκοιμημενων). Perfect middle participle as in Matthew 27:52 which see. Beautiful picture of death from which word (κοιμαομα) comes our χεμετερψ.
