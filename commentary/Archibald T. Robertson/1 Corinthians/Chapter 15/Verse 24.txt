<p><b> Then cometh the end </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειτα το τελος</span></span>). No verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινετα</span></span> in the Greek. Supply "at his coming," the end or consummation of the age or world (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:39">Matthew 13:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:49">Matthew 13:49</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:7">1 Peter 4:7</span>),</p>
<p><b>When he shall deliver up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν παραδιδω</span></span>). Present active subjunctive (not optative) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραδιδωμ</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν</span></span>, whenever, and so quite indefinite and uncertain as to time. Present subjunctive rather than aorist <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραδω</span></span> because it pictures a future proceeding.</p>
<p><b>To God, even the Father </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω θεω κα πατρ</span></span>). Better, "to the God and Father" or to "His God and Father." The Kingdom belongs to the Father.</p>
<p><b>When he shall have abolished </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν καταργηση</span></span>). First aorist active subjunctive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν</span></span>, indefinite future time. Simply, "whenever he shall abolish," no use in making it future perfect, merely aorist subjunctive. On <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταργεω</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:13">1 Corinthians 6:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:8">1 Corinthians 13:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:10">1 Corinthians 13:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:11">1 Corinthians 13:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>Rule </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρχην</span></span>),</p>
<p><b>authority </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξουσιαν</span></span>),</p>
<p><b>power </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυναμιν</span></span>). All forms of power opposing the will of God. Constative aorist tense covering the whole period of conflict with final victory as climax. </p>
