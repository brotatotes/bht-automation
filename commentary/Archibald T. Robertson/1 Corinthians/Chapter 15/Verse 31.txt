<p><b> I protest by that glorying in you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νη την υμετεραν καυχησιν</span></span>). No word for "I protest." Paul takes solemn oath by the use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νη</span></span> (common in Attic) with the accusative. Only here in N.T., but in LXX (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+42:15">Genesis 42:15</span>). For other solemn oaths by Paul see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:18">2 Corinthians 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:23">2 Corinthians 1:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:10">2 Corinthians 11:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:31">2 Corinthians 11:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:1">Romans 9:1</span>. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καυχησις</span></span> see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:19">1 Thessalonians 2:19</span>. The possessive pronoun (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμετεραν</span></span>) is objective as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμην</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:24">1 Corinthians 11:24</span>.</p>
<p><b>I die daily </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθ' ημεραν αποθνησκω</span></span>). I am in daily peril of death (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:11">2 Corinthians 4:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:23">2 Corinthians 11:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:36">Romans 8:36</span>). </p>
