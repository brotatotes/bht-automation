<p><b> Not the body which shall be </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου το σωμα το γενησομενον</span></span>). Articular future participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span>, literally, "not the body that will become." The new</p>
<p><b>body </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωμα</span></span>) is not yet in existence, but only the seed (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοκκος</span></span>, grain, old word, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:31">Matthew 13:31</span>).</p>
<p><b>It may chance </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε τυχο</span></span>). Fourth class condition as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:10">1 Corinthians 14:10</span> which see. Paul is rich in metaphors here, though usually not so (Howson, <i>Metaphors of St. Paul</i>). Paul was a city man. We sow seeds, not plants (bodies). The butterfly comes out of the dying worm. </p>
