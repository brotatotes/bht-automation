<p><b> For one star differeth from another star in glory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αστηρ γαρ αστερος διαφερε εν δοξη</span></span>). A beautiful illustration of Paul's point. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αστερος</span></span> is the ablative case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαφερε</span></span> (old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαφερω</span></span>, Latin <i>differo</i>, our <i>differ</i>, bear apart). On <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αστηρ</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:7">Matthew 2:7</span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αστρον</span></span> <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:25">Luke 21:25</span>. Stars differ in magnitude and brilliancy. The telescope has added more force to Paul's argument.</p>
<p><b>In glory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν δοξη</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκεω</span></span>, to think, to seem. So opinion, estimate, then the shekinah glory of God in the LXX, glory in general. It is one of the great words of the N.T. Jesus is termed the glory in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:1">James 2:1</span>. </p>
