<p><b> A mystery </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυστηριον</span></span>). He does not claim that he has explained everything. He has drawn a broad parallel which opens the door of hope and confidence.</p>
<p><b>We shall not all sleep </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντες ου κοιμηθησομεθα</span></span>). Future passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοιμαομα</span></span>, to sleep. Not all of us shall die, Paul means. Some people will be alive when he comes. Paul does not affirm that he or any then living will be alive when Jesus comes again. He simply groups all under the phrase "we all."</p>
<p><b>But we shall all be changed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντες δε αλλαγησομεθα</span></span>). Second future passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλασσω</span></span>. Both living and dead shall be changed and so receive the resurrection body. See this same idea at more length in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:13-18">1 Thessalonians 4:13-18</span>. </p>
