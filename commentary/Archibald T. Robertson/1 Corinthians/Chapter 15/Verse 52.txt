<p><b> In a moment </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ατομω</span></span>). Old word, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεμνω</span></span>, to cut, indivisible: Scientific word for <i>atom</i> which was considered indivisible, but that was before the day of electrons and protons. Only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>In the twinkling of an eye </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ριπη οφθαλμου</span></span>). Old word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ριπη</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ριπτω</span></span>, to throw. Only here in N.T. Used by the Greeks for the flapping of a wing, the buzz of a gnat, the quivering of a harp, the twinkling of a star.</p>
<p><b>At the last trump </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη εσχατη σαλπιγγ</span></span>). Symbolical, of course. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:16">1 Thessalonians 4:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:31">Matthew 24:31</span>. </p>
