<p><b> Victory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νικος</span></span>). Late form of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νικη</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>O death </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θανατε</span></span>). Second instance. Here Paul changes Hades of the LXX for Hebrew Sheol (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ho+13:14">Hosea 13:14</span>) to death. Paul never uses Hades.</p>
<p><b>Thy sting </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σου το κεντρον</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεντρεω</span></span>, to prick, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:14">Acts 26:14</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:10">Revelation 9:10</span> of the sting of locusts, scorpions. The serpent death has lost his poison fangs. </p>
