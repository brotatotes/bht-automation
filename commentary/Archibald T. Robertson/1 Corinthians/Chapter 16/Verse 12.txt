<p><b> And it was not at all his will to come now </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα παντως ουκ ην θελημα ινα νυν ελθη</span></span>). Adversative use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span> = "but." Apollos had left Corinth in disgust over the strife there which involved him and Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:1">1 Corinthians 16:1</span>). He had had enough of partisan strife over preachers. </p>
