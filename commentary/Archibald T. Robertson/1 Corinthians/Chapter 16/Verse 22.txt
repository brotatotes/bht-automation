<p><span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αναθεμα</span></span>. The word seems a bit harsh to us, but the refusal to love Christ (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου φιλε</span></span>) on the part of a nominal Christian deserves <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναθεμα</span></span> (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:3">1 Corinthians 12:3</span> for this word). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μαραν αθα</span></span>. This Aramaic phrase means "Our Lord (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαραν</span></span>) cometh (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθα</span></span>)" or, used as a proleptic perfect, "has come." It seems to be a sort of watchword (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:14">1 Thessalonians 4:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:7">James 5:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:5">Philippians 4:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:7">Revelation 1:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:11">Revelation 3:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:20">Revelation 22:20</span>), expressing the lively hope that the Lord will come. It was a curious blunder in the King James Version that connected <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μαραν αθα</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αναθεμα</span></span>. </p>
