<p><b> When I arrive </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν παραγενωμα</span></span>). Whenever I arrive, indefinite temporal conjunction <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν</span></span> and second aorist middle subjunctive.</p>
<p><b>Whomsoever ye shall approve by letters </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ους εαν δοκιμασητε δι' επιστολων</span></span>). Indefinite relative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and aorist subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκιμαζω</span></span> (to test and so approve as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:10">Philippians 1:10</span>). "By letters" to make it formal and regular and Paul would approve their choice of messengers to go with him to Jerusalem (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:20">2 Corinthians 8:20</span>). Curiously enough no names from Corinth occur in the list in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:4">Acts 20:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>To carry </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απενεγκειν</span></span>). Second aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποφερω</span></span>, to bear away.</p>
<p><b>Bounty </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριν</span></span>). Gift, grace, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:4-7">2 Corinthians 8:4-7</span>. As a matter of fact, the messengers of the churches (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστολο εκκλησιων</span></span> <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:23">2 Corinthians 8:23</span>) went along with Paul to Jerusalem (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:4">Acts 20:4</span>). </p>
