<p><b> It may be </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυχον</span></span>). Neuter accusative of second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυγχανω</span></span> used as an adverb (in Plato and Xenophon, but nowhere else in N.T.).</p>
<p><b>Or even winter </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η κα παραχειμασω</span></span>). Future active of late verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραχειμαζω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χειμων</span></span>, winter). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:12">Acts 27:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:11">Acts 28:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:12">Titus 3:12</span>. He did stay in Corinth for three months (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:3">Acts 20:3</span>), probably the coming winter.</p>
<p><b>Whithersoever I go </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου εαν πορευωμα</span></span>). Indefinite local clause with subjunctive. As a matter of fact, Paul had to flee from a conspiracy in Corinth (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:3">Acts 20:3</span>). </p>
