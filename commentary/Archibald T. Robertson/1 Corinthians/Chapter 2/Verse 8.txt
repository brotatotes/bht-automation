<p><b> Knoweth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγνωκεν</span></span>). Has known, has discerned, perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span>. They have shown amazing ignorance of God's wisdom.</p>
<p><b>For had they known it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε γαρ εγνωσαν</span></span>). Condition of the second class, determined as unfulfilled, with aorist active indicative in both condition (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγνωσαν</span></span>) and conclusion with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ αν εσταυρωσαν</span></span>). Peter in the great sermon at Pentecost commented on the "ignorance" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα αγνοιαν</span></span>) of the Jews in crucifying Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:17">Acts 3:17</span>) as the only hope for repentance on their part (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:19">Acts 3:19</span>).</p>
<p><b>The Lord of glory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον Κυριον της δοξης</span></span>). Genitive case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξης</span></span>, means characterized by glory, "bringing out the contrast between the indignity of the Cross (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:2">Hebrews 12:2</span>) and the majesty of the Victim (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:69">Luke 22:69</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:43">Luke 23:43</span>)" (Robertson and Plummer). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:1">James 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:2">Acts 7:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:17">Ephesians 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:5">Hebrews 9:5</span>. </p>
