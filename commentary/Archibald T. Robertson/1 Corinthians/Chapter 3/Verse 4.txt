<p><b> For when one saith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν γαρ λεγη τις</span></span>). Indefinite temporal clause with the present subjunctive of repetition (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 972). Each instance is a case in point and proof abundant of the strife.</p>
<p><b>Of Paul </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Παυλου</span></span>). Predicate genitive, belong to Paul, on Paul's side.</p>
<p><b>Of Apollos </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Απολλω</span></span>). Same genitive, but the form is the so-called Attic second declension. See the nominative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Απολλως</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:5">1 Corinthians 3:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>Men </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωπο</span></span>). Just mere human creatures (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωπο</span></span>, generic term for mankind), in the flesh (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαρκινο</span></span>), acting like the flesh (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαρκικο</span></span>), not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευματικο</span></span>, as if still <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψυχικο</span></span>. It was a home-thrust. Paul would not even defend his own partisans. </p>
