<p><b> So then neither--neither--but </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hωστε ουτε--ουτε--αλλ'</span></span>). Paul applies his logic relentlessly to the facts. He had asked</p>
<p><b>what </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τ</span></span>) is Apollos or Paul (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:5">1 Corinthians 3:5</span>). The answer is here.</p>
<p><b>Neither is anything </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τ</span></span>)</p>
<p><b>the one who plants nor the one who waters </b>. God is the whole and we are not anything. </p>
