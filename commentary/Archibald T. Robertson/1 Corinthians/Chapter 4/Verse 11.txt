<p><b> Even unto this present hour </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχρ της αρτ ωρας</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αρτ</span></span> (just now, this very minute) accents the continuity of the contrast as applied to Paul. Ten verbs and four participles from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:11-13">1 Corinthians 4:11-13</span> give a graphic picture of Paul's condition in Ephesus when he is writing this epistle.</p>
<p><b>We hunger </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεινωμεν</span></span>),</p>
<p><b>we thirst </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διψωμεν</span></span>),</p>
<p><b>are naked </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γυμνιτευομεν</span></span>), late verb for scant clothing from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γυμνητης</span></span>,</p>
<p><b>are buffeted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολαφιζομεθα</span></span>), to strike a blow with the fist from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολαφος</span></span> and one of the few N.T. and ecclesiastical words and see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:67">Matthew 26:67</span>,</p>
<p><b>have no certain dwelling place </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αστατουμεν</span></span>) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αστατος</span></span>, strolling about and only here save Anthol. Pal. and Aquila in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+58:7">Isaiah 58:7</span>. Field in <i>Notes</i>, p. 170 renders <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:11">1 Corinthians 4:11</span> "and are vagabonds" or spiritual hobos. </p>
