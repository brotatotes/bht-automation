<p><b> We toil </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοπιωμεν</span></span>). Common late verb for weariness in toil (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:5">Luke 5:5</span>),</p>
<p><b>working with our own hands </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργαζομενο ταις ιδιαις χερσιν</span></span>) instrumental case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χερσιν</span></span> and not simply for himself but also for Aquila and Priscilla as he explains in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:34">Acts 20:34</span>. This personal touch gives colour to the outline. Paul alludes to this fact often (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:9">1 Thessalonians 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:8">2 Thessalonians 3:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:6">1 Corinthians 9:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:7">2 Corinthians 11:7</span>). "Greeks despised manual labour; St. Paul glories in it" (Robertson and Plummer). Cf. Deissmann, <i>Light, etc.</i>, p. 317.</p>
<p><b>Being reviled we bless </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λοιδορουμενο ευλογουμεν</span></span>). Almost the language of Peter about Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:23">1 Peter 2:23</span>) in harmony with the words of Jesus in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:44">Matthew 5:44</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:27">Luke 6:27</span>.</p>
<p><b>Being persecuted we endure </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διωκομενο ανεχομεθα</span></span>). We hold back and do not retaliate. Turn to Paul's other picture of his experiences in the vivid contrasts in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:7-10">2 Corinthians 4:7-10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:3-10">2 Corinthians 6:3-10</span> for an interpretation of his language here. </p>
