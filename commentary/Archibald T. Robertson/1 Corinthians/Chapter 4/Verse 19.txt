<p><b> If the Lord will </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν ο κυριος θεληση</span></span>). Third-class condition. See James <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:15">1 Corinthians 4:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:21">Acts 18:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:7">1 Corinthians 16:7</span> for the use of this phrase. It should represent one's constant attitude, though not always to be spoken aloud.</p>
<p><b>But the power </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα την δυναμιν</span></span>). The puffed up Judaizers did a deal of talking in Paul's absence. He will come and will know their real strength. II Corinthians gives many evidences of Paul's sensitiveness to their talk about his inconsistencies and cowardice (in particular chs. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:2">1 Corinthians 4:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:2">1 Corinthians 4:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:10">1 Corinthians 4:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:11">1 Corinthians 4:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:12">1 Corinthians 4:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:13">1 Corinthians 4:13</span>). He changed his plans to spare them, not from timidity. It will become plain later that Timothy failed on this mission and that Titus succeeded. </p>
