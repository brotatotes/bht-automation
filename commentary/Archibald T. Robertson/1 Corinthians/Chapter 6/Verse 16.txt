<p><b> One body </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν σωμα</span></span>). With the harlot. That union is for the harlot the same as with the wife. The words quoted from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+2:24">Genesis 2:24</span> describing the sexual union of husband and wife, are also quoted and explained by Jesus in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:5">Matthew 19:5</span> which see for discussion of the translation Hebraism with use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Saith he </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φησιν</span></span>). Supply either <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο θεος</span></span> (God) or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η γραφη</span></span> (the Scripture). </p>
