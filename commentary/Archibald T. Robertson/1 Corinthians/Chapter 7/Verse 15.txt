<p><b> Is not under bondage </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου δεδουλωτα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δουλοω</span></span>, to enslave, has been enslaved, does not remain a slave. The believing husband or wife is not at liberty to separate, unless the disbeliever or pagan insists on it. Wilful desertion of the unbeliever sets the other free, a case not contemplated in Christ's words in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:32">Matthew 5:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:9">Matthew 19:9</span>. Luther argued that the Christian partner, thus released, may marry again. But that is by no means clear, unless the unbeliever marries first.</p>
<p><b>But God hath called us in peace </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν δε ειρηνη κεκληκεν ημας</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμας</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλεω</span></span>, permanent call in the sphere or atmosphere of peace. He does not desire enslavement in the marriage relation between the believer and the unbeliever. </p>
