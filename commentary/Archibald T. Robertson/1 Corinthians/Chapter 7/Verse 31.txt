<p><b> Those that use the world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο χρωμενο τον κοσμον</span></span>). Old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χραομα</span></span>, usually with the instrumental case, but the accusative occurs in some Cretan inscriptions and in late writers according to a tendency of verbs to resume the use of the original accusative (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 468).</p>
<p><b>As not abusing it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως μη καταχρημενο</span></span>). Perfective use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span> in composition, old verb, but here only in N.T., to use up, use to the full. Papyri give examples of this sense. This is more likely the idea than "abusing" it.</p>
<p><b>For the fashion of this world passeth away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραγε γαρ το σχημα του κοσμου τουτου</span></span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:17">1 John 2:17</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Σχημα</span></span> is the <i>habitus</i>, the outward appearance, old word, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:7">Philippians 2:7</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Παραγε</span></span> (old word) means "passes along" like a moving panorama (movie show!). Used of Jesus passing by in Jericho (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:30">Matthew 20:30</span>). </p>
