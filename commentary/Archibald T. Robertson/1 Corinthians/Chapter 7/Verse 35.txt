<p><b> For your own profit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς το υμων αυτων συμφορον</span></span>). Old adjective, advantageous, with neuter article here as substantive, from verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμφερω</span></span>. In N.T. here only and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:33">1 Corinthians 10:33</span>. Note reflexive plural form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμων αυτων</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Not that I may cast a snare upon you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουχ ινα βροχον υμιν επιβαλω</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Βροχον</span></span> is a noose or slip-knot used for lassoing animals, old word, only here in N.T. Papyri have an example "hanged by a noose." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Επιβαλω</span></span> is second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιβαλλω</span></span>, old verb to cast upon. Paul does not wish to capture the Corinthians by lasso and compel them to do what they do not wish about getting married.</p>
<p><b>For that which is seemly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς το ευσχημον</span></span>). Old adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ</span></span>, well, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σχημων</span></span>, shapely, comely, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σχημα</span></span>, figure). For the purpose of decorum.</p>
<p><b>Attend upon the Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευπαρεδρον</span></span>). Adjective construed with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς το</span></span>, before, late word (Hesychius) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ</span></span>, well, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεδρος</span></span>, sitting beside, "for the good position beside the Lord" (associative instrumental case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κυριω</span></span>). Cf. Mary sitting at the feet of Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:39">Luke 10:39</span>).</p>
<p><b>Without distraction </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απερισπαστως</span></span>). Late adverb (Polybius, Plutarch, LXX) from the adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απερισπαστος</span></span> (common in the papyri) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περισπαω</span></span>, to draw around (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:40">Luke 10:40</span>). </p>
