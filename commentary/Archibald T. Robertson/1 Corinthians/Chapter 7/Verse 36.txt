<p><b> That he behaveth himself unseemly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασχημονειν</span></span>). Old verb, here only in N.T., from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασχημων</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:23">1 Corinthians 12:23</span>), from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σχημα</span></span>. Occurs in the papyri. Infinitive in indirect discourse after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νομιζε</span></span> (thinks) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span> (condition of first class, assumed as true).</p>
<p><b>If she be past the flower of her age </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν η υπερακμος</span></span>). Old word, only here in N.T., from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ</span></span> (over) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακμη</span></span> (prime or bloom of life), past the bloom of youth, <i>superadultus</i> (Vulgate). Compound adjective with feminine form like masculine. Apparently the Corinthians had asked Paul about the duty of a father towards his daughter old enough to marry.</p>
<p><b>If need so requireth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ουτως οφειλε γινεσθα</span></span>). "And it ought to happen." Paul has discussed the problem of marriage for virgins on the grounds of expediency. Now he faces the question where the daughter wishes to marry and there is no serious objection to it. The father is advised to consent. Roman and Greek fathers had the control of the marriage of their daughters. "My marriage is my father's care; it is not for me to decide about that" (Hermione in Euripides' <i>Andromache</i>, 987).</p>
<p><b>Let them marry </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γαμειτωσαν</span></span>). Present active plural imperative (long form). </p>
