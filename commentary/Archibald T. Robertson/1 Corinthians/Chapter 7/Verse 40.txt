<p><b> Happier </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακαριωτερα</span></span>). Comparative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακαριος</span></span> used in the Beatitudes (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:3">Matthew 5:3</span>).</p>
<p><b>After my judgment </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα την εμην γνωμην</span></span>). The same word used in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:25">1 Corinthians 7:25</span>, not a command.</p>
<p><b>I think </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκω</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκεω</span></span>, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νομιζω</span></span> of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:26">1 Corinthians 7:26</span>. But he insists that he has "the spirit of God" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα θεου</span></span>) in the expression of his inspired judgment on this difficult, complicated, tangled problem of marriage. But he has discharged his duty and leaves each one to decide for himself. </p>
