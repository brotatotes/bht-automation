<p><b> But if they have not continency </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε δε ουκ εγκρατευοντα</span></span>). Condition of the first class, assumed as true. Direct middle voice <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγκρατευοντα</span></span>, hold themselves in, control themselves.</p>
<p><b>Let them marry </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γαμησατωσαν</span></span>). First aorist (ingressive) active imperative. Usual <i>Koine</i> form in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-τωσαν</span></span> for third plural.</p>
<p><b>Better </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρειττον</span></span>). Marriage is better than continued sexual passion. Paul has not said that celibacy is</p>
<p><b>better </b> than marriage though he has justified it and expressed his own personal preference for it. The metaphorical use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυρουσθα</span></span> (present middle infinitive) for sexual passion is common enough as also for grief (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:29">2 Corinthians 11:29</span>). </p>
