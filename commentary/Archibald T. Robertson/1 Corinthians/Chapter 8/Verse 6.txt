<p><b> Yet to us there is one God, the Father </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ' ημιν εις θεος ο πατηρ</span></span>). B omits <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ'</span></span> here, but the sense calls for it anyhow in this apodosis, a strong antithesis to the protasis (<b> even if at least </b>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ειπερ</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Of whom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ ου</span></span>). As the source (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ</span></span>) of the universe (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα παντα</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:36">Romans 11:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:16">Colossians 1:16</span>) and also our goal is God (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις αυτον</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:36">Romans 11:36</span> where <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι' αυτου</span></span> is added whereas here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι' ου</span></span> (through whom) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι' αυτου</span></span> (through him) point to Jesus Christ as the intermediate agent in creation as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:15-20">Colossians 1:15-20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:3">John 1:3</span>. Here Paul calls Jesus</p>
<p><b>Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κυριος</span></span>) and not</p>
<p><b>God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεος</span></span>), though he does apply that word to him in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:5">Romans 9:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:13">Titus 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:9">Colossians 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:28">Acts 20:28</span>. </p>
