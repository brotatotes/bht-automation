<p><b> Sacred things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα ιερα</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Of the temple </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του ιερου</span></span>). Play on the same word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιερου</span></span> (sacred). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+18:8-20">Numbers 18:8-20</span> for the details. This is a very pertinent illustration.</p>
<p><b>They which wait upon the altar </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο τω θυσιαστηριω παρεδρευοντες</span></span>). Old word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεδρευω</span></span>, to sit beside, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρ--εδρος</span></span>, like Latin <i>assidere</i>, and so constant attendance. Only here in the N.T. Locative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυσιαστηριω</span></span>, late word found so far only in LXX, Philo, Josephus, N.T., and ecclesiastical writers. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:23">Matthew 5:23</span>. </p>
