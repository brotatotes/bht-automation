<p><b> In a race </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν σταδιω</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span>, to place. A stated or fixed distance, 606 3/4 feet, both masculine <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σταδιο</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:24">Matthew 14:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:13">Luke 24:13</span>) and neuter as here. Most of the Greek cities had race-courses for runners like that at Olympia.</p>
<p><b>The prize </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το βραβειον</span></span>). Late word, in inscriptions and papyri. Latin <i>brabeum</i>. In N. T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:14">Philippians 3:14</span>. The victor's prize which only one could receive.</p>
<p><b>That ye may attain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα καταλαβητε</span></span>). Final use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and perfective use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα-</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαβητε</span></span> (effective aorist active subjunctive, grasp and hold). Old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλαμβανω</span></span> and used in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:12">Philippians 3:12</span>. </p>
