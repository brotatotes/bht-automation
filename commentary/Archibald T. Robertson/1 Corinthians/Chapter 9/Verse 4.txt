<p><b> Have we no right? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μη ουκ εχομεν εξουσιαν;</span></span>). Literary plural here though singular in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:1-3">1 Corinthians 9:1-3</span>. The <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> in this double negative expects the answer "No" while <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ</span></span> goes with the verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχομεν</span></span>. "Do we fail to have the right?" Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:18">Romans 10:18</span> (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 1173). </p>
