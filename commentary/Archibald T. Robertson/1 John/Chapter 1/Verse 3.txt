<p><b> That which we have seen </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο εωρακαμεν</span></span>). Third use of this form (verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:1">1 John 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:2">1 John 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:3">1 John 1:3</span>), this time resumption after the parenthesis in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:2">1 John 1:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>And heard </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ακηκοαμεν</span></span>). Second (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:1">1 John 1:1</span> for first) use of this form, a third in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:5">1 John 1:5</span>. Emphasis by repetition is a thoroughly Johannine trait.</p>
<p><b>Declare we </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαγγελλομεν</span></span>). Second use of this word (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:2">1 John 1:2</span> for first), but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγγελια</span></span> (message) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγγελλομεν</span></span> (announce) in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:5">1 John 1:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>That ye also may have </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα κα υμεις εχητε</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span> (may keep on having). "Ye also" who have not seen Jesus in the flesh as well as those like John who have seen him. Like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα υμιν</span></span> (to you also) just before.</p>
<p><b>Fellowship with us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινωνιαν μεθ' ημων</span></span>). Common word in this Epistle, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινωνος</span></span>, partner (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:10">Luke 5:10</span>), and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινωνεω</span></span>, to share, in (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:13">1 Peter 4:13</span>), with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα</span></span> emphasising mutual relationship (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:42">Acts 2:42</span>). This Epistle often uses <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span> with a substantive rather than a verb.</p>
<p><b>Yea, and our fellowship </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα η κοινωνια δε η ημετερα</span></span>). Careful explanation of his meaning in the word "fellowship" (partnership), involving fellowship with the Father and with his Son Jesus Christ and only possible in Christ. </p>
