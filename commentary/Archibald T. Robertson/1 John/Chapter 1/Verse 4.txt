<p><b> We write </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γραφομεν ημεις</span></span>). Literary plural present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γραφω</span></span>, which see in the singular in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:12-14">1 John 2:12-14</span>.</p>
<p><b>May be fulfilled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η πεπληρωμενη</span></span>). Periphrastic perfect passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροω</span></span>, stressing the state of completion in the purpose (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>), remain full, precisely as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:24">John 16:24</span>. See aorist subjunctive in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:11">John 15:11</span> and perfect indicative in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:13">John 17:13</span>. The MSS. differ as often between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημων</span></span> (our) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμων</span></span> (your). </p>
