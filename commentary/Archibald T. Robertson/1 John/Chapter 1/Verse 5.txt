<p><b> And </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span>). Mutual fellowship depends on mutual knowledge (Westcott).</p>
<p><b>Message </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγγελια</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγγελος</span></span>, messenger), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:11">1 John 3:11</span>, and note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απ' αυτου</span></span> (from God like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαγγελλω</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:3">1 John 1:3</span>) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγγελλομεν</span></span>, to announce, to disclose, here as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:25">John 4:25</span>.</p>
<p><b>God is light </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο θεος φως εστιν</span></span>). Precisely so the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Λογος</span></span> is light (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:4-9">John 1:4-9</span>) and what Jesus claimed to be (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:12">John 8:12</span>). John repeats it in negative form as he often does (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:3">John 1:3</span>). </p>
