<p><b> If we say </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν ειπωμεν</span></span>). Condition of third class with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and second aorist (ingressive, up and say) active subjunctive. Claiming fellowship with God (see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:3">1 John 1:3</span>) involves walking in the light with God (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:5">1 John 1:5</span>) and not in the darkness (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκοτος</span></span> here, but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκοτια</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:5">John 1:5</span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:11">1 John 2:11</span> also for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη σκοτια περιπατεω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>We lie </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευδομεθα</span></span>). Present middle indicative, plain Greek and plain English like that about the devil in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:44">John 8:44</span>.</p>
<p><b>Do not the truth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου ποιουμεν την αληθειαν</span></span>). Negative statement of the positive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευδομεθα</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:44">John 8:44</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:21">John 3:21</span> for "doing the truth," like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ne+9:33">Nehemiah 9:33</span>. </p>
