<p><b> If we confess </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν ομολογωμεν</span></span>). Third-class condition again with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομολογεω</span></span>, "if we keep on confessing." Confession of sin to God and to one another (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:16">James 5:16</span>) is urged throughout the N.T. from John the Baptist (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:5">Mark 1:5</span>) on.</p>
<p><b>Faithful </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστος</span></span>). Jesus made confession of sin necessary to forgiveness. It is God's promise and he is "righteous" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιος</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>To forgive </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα αφη</span></span>). Sub-final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφιημ</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>And to cleanse </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα αγιαση</span></span>). So again with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθαριζω</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:7">1 John 1:7</span>). </p>
