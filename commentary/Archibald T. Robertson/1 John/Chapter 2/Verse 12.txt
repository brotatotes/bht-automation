<p><b> I write </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γραφω</span></span>). Present active indicative, repeated three times, referring to this Epistle. For "the name" see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:23">1 John 3:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:7">3 John 1:7</span>. They were loyal to the name of Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:22">Matthew 10:22</span>).</p>
<p><b>Are forgiven </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφεωντα</span></span>). Doric perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφιημ</span></span> (seen also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:20">Luke 5:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:23">Luke 5:23</span>) for the usual <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφειντα</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Τεκνια</span></span> (little children) probably includes all, as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:1">1 John 2:1</span>. </p>
