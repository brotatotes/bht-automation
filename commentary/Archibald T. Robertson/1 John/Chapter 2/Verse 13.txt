<p><b> Fathers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πατερες</span></span>). Those mature believers with long and rich experience (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγνωκατε</span></span>, ye have come to know and still know).</p>
<p><b>Him which is from the beginning </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον απ' αρχης</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:1">1 John 1:1</span> as explaining this crisp description of the Word of life (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:1-18">John 1:1-18</span>).</p>
<p><b>Young men </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νεανισκο</span></span>). The younger element in contrast to the fathers, full of vigor and conflict and victory.</p>
<p><b>Ye have overcome the evil one </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νενικηκατε τον πονηρον</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νικαω</span></span>, a permanent victory after conflict. The masculine article <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον</span></span> shows that the prince of darkness is the one defeated in this struggle, the devil plain in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:8">1 John 3:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:10">1 John 3:10</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:44">John 8:44</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:2">John 13:2</span>). </p>
