<p><b> I have not written </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ εγραψα</span></span>). Not epistolary aorist (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:14">1 John 2:14</span>), but a reference to what he has just said.</p>
<p><b>And because no lie is of the truth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα οτ παν ψευδος εκ της αληθειας ουκ εστιν</span></span>). Not certain whether <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span> here is causal (because) or declarative (that). Either makes sense. Note the idiomatic use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παν--ουκ=ουδεν</span></span> (no) as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:19">1 John 2:19</span>. </p>
