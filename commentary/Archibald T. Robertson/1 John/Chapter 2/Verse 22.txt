<p><b> The liar </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ψευστης</span></span>). The liar (with the article) <i>par excellence</i>. Rhetorical question to sharpen the point made already about lying in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:6">1 John 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:10">1 John 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:4">1 John 2:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:21">1 John 2:21</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:5">1 John 5:5</span> for a like rhetorical question.</p>
<p><b>But </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε μη</span></span>). Except, if not.</p>
<p><b>That denieth that Jesus is the Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αρνουμενος οτ Ιησους ουκ εστιν ο Χριστος</span></span>). Common Greek idiom for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ</span></span> to appear after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρνεομα</span></span> like redundant <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:27">Luke 20:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:19">Hebrews 12:19</span>. The old Latin retains <i>non</i> here as old English did (Shakespeare, <i>Comedy of Errors</i> IV. ii. 7, "He denied you had in him no right"). The Cerinthian Gnostics denied the identity of the man Jesus and Christ (an <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αεον</span></span>, they held) like the modern Jesus or Christ controversy.</p>
<p><b>This is the antichrist </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτος εστιν ο αντιχριστος</span></span>). The one just mentioned, Cerinthus himself in particular.</p>
<p><b>Even he that denieth the Father and the Son </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αρνουμενος τον πατερα κα τον υιον</span></span>). This is the inevitable logic of such a rejection of the Son of God. Jesus had himself said this very same thing (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:23">John 5:23</span>). </p>
