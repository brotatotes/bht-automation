<p><b> As for you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμεις</span></span>). Emphatic proleptic position before the relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span> and subject of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκουσατε</span></span>, a familiar idiom in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:45">John 8:45</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:29">John 10:29</span>, etc. Here for emphatic contrast with the antichrists. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:1">1 John 1:1</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απ' αρχης</span></span> (from the beginning).</p>
<p><b>Let abide in you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν υμιν μενετω</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενω</span></span>, to remain. Do not be carried away by the new-fangled Gnostic teaching. </p>
