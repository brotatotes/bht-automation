<p><b> And this is the promise </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα αυτη εστιν η επαγγελια</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:5">1 John 1:5</span> for the same idiom with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγγελια</span></span> (message). This is the only instance of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαγγελια</span></span> in the Johannine writings. Here "the promise" is explained to be "the life eternal" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:2">1 John 1:2</span>). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:4">Acts 1:4</span> the word is used for the coming of the Holy Spirit.</p>
<p><b>He promised </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτος επηγγειλατο</span></span>). First aorist middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαγγελλω</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αυτος</span></span> (he) is Christ as is seen in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:3">1 John 3:3</span> by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινος</span></span>. </p>
