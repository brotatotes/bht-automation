<p><b> If ye know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν ειδητε</span></span>). Third-class condition again with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and second perfect active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιδα</span></span>. If ye know by intuitive or absolute knowledge that Christ (because of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:28">1 John 2:28</span>) is righteous, then "ye know" or "know ye" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκετε</span></span> either indicative or imperative) by experimental knowledge (so <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span> means in contrast with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιδα</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Is begotten </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεγεννητα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενναω</span></span>, stands begotten, the second birth (regeneration) of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:3-8">John 3:3-8</span>.</p>
<p><b>Of him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ αυτου</span></span>). Plainly "of God" in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:9">1 John 2:9</span> and so apparently here in spite of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιος</span></span> referring to Christ. Doing righteousness is proof of the new birth. </p>
