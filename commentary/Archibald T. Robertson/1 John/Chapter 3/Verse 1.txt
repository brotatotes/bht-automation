<p><b> What manner of love </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποταπην αγαπην</span></span>). Qualitative interrogative as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:11">2 Peter 3:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:27">Matthew 8:27</span>. Only here in John's writings. Originally of what country or race.</p>
<p><b>Hath bestowed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεδωκεν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>, state of completion, "the endowment of the receiver" (Vincent).</p>
<p><b>That we should be called </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα κληθωμεν</span></span>). Sub-final use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> with the first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλεω</span></span>, to call or name, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:23">Matthew 2:23</span>.</p>
<p><b>Children </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεκνα</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:12">John 1:12</span> and with an allusion to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεγεννητα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:29">1 John 2:29</span> in an effort "to restore the waning enthusiasm of his readers, and to recall them to their first love" (Brooke).</p>
<p><b>And such we are </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα εσμεν</span></span>). "And we are." A parenthetical reflection characteristic of John (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα νυν εστιν</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:25">John 5:25</span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ουκ εισιν</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:2">Revelation 2:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:9">Revelation 3:9</span>) omitted by Textus Receptus, though, in the old MSS.</p>
<p><b>Because it knew him not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ ουκ εγνω αυτον</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span>, precisely the argument in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:18">John 15:18</span>. </p>
