<p><b> Message </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγγελια</span></span>). In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:5">1 John 1:5</span>, but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαγγελια</span></span> (promise) fifty-one times.</p>
<p><b>From the beginning </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απ' αρχης</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:1">1 John 1:1</span> for this phrase and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:7">1 John 2:7</span> for the idea. They had the message of love for the brotherhood from the beginning of the gospel and it goes back to the time of Cain and Abel (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:12">1 John 3:12</span>).</p>
<p><b>That we should love one another </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα αγαπωμεν αλληλους</span></span>). Sub-final clause (content of the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγγελια</span></span>) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and present active subjunctive. John repeats the message of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:7">1 John 2:7</span>. </p>
