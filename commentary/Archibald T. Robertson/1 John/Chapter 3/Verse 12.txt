<p><b> Of the evil one </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ του πονηρου</span></span>). Ablative case and the same for neuter and masculine singular, but verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:10">1 John 3:10</span> makes it clear that the reference is to the devil.</p>
<p><b>Slew </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσφαξεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σφαζω</span></span>, old verb, to slay, to butcher, to cut the throat (Latin <i>jugulare</i>) like an ox in the shambles, in N.T. only here and Rev. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:6">Revelation 5:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:9">Revelation 5:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:12">Revelation 5:12</span>, etc.).</p>
<p><b>Wherefore? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριν τινοσ;</span></span>). "For the sake of what?" Post-positive preposition (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:1">Ephesians 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:14">Ephesians 3:14</span>) except here. The interpretation of the act of Cain (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+4:8">Genesis 4:8</span>) is an addition to the narrative, but in accord with <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:4">Hebrews 11:4</span>. Jealousy led to murder. </p>
