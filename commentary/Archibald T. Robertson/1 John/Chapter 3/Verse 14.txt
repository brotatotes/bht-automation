<p><b> We know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημεις οιδαμεν</span></span>). Emphatic expression of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημεις</span></span> (we) in contrast to the unregenerate world, the Christian consciousness shared by writer and readers.</p>
<p><b>We have passed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταβεβηκαμεν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταβαινω</span></span>, old compound to pass over from one place to another (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:3">John 7:3</span>), to migrate, out of death into life. We have already done it while here on earth.</p>
<p><b>Because </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span>). Proof of this transition, not the ground of it.</p>
<p><b>We love the brethren </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπωμεν τους αδελφους</span></span>). Just this phrase (plural) here alone, but see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:9">1 John 2:9</span> for the singular.</p>
<p><b>He that loveth not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο μη αγαπων</span></span>). "The not loving man," general picture and picture of spiritual death. </p>
