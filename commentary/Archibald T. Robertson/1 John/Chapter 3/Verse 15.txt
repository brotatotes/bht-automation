<p><b> A murderer </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωποκτονος</span></span>). Old compound (Euripides) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωπος</span></span> (man) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κτεινω</span></span> (to kill), a man-killer, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:44">John 8:44</span> (of Satan).</p>
<p><b>No </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πασ--ου</span></span>). According to current Hebraistic idiom= <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδεις</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:19">1 John 2:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:21">1 John 2:21</span>.</p>
<p><b>Abiding </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενουσαν</span></span>). Present active feminine accusative predicate participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενω</span></span>, "a continuous power and a communicated gift" (Westcott). </p>
