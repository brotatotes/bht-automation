<p><b> Know we </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγνωκαμεν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative, "we have come to know and still know." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:3">1 John 2:3</span> for "hereby" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τουτω</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Love </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την αγαπην</span></span>). "The thing called love" (D. Smith).</p>
<p><b>He for us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινος υπερ ημων</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εκεινος</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:6">1 John 2:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:3">1 John 3:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:5">1 John 3:5</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ</span></span> here alone in this Epistle, though common in John's Gospel (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+10:11">1 John 10:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+10:15">1 John 10:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+11:50">1 John 11:50</span>, etc.) and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:7">3 John 1:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>Laid down his life </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την ψυχην αυτου εθηκεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span>, the very idiom used by Jesus of himself in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:11">John 10:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:17">John 10:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>We ought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημεις οφειλομεν</span></span>). Emphatic <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημεις</span></span> again. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφειλω</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:6">1 John 2:6</span>. Of course our laying down our lives for the brethren has no atoning value in our cases as in that of Christ, but is a supreme proof of one's love (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:37">John 13:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:13">John 15:13</span>), as often happens. </p>
