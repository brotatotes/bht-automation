<p><b> Whoso hath </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος αν εχη</span></span>). Indefinite relative clause with modal <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος</span></span> and the present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>The world's goods </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον βιον του κοσμου</span></span>). "The living or livelihood (not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωη</span></span>, the principle of life, and see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:16">1 John 2:16</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βιος</span></span>) of the world" (not in the sense of evil or wicked, but simply this mundane sphere).</p>
<p><b>Beholdeth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεωρε</span></span>). Present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεωρεω</span></span>, like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχε</span></span> just before.</p>
<p><b>In need </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρειαν εχοντα</span></span>). "Having need" (present active predicate participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>, agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδελφον</span></span>). See the vivid picture of a like case in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:15">James 2:15</span>.</p>
<p><b>Shutteth up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλειση</span></span>). First aorist (effective) active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλειω</span></span>, to close like the door, changed on purpose from present tense to aorist (graphic slamming the door of his compassion, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπλαγχνα</span></span>, common in LXX and N.T. for the nobler viscera, the seat of the emotions, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:11">Philippians 2:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:12">Colossians 3:12</span>). Only here in John.</p>
<p><b>How </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πως</span></span>). Rhetorical question like that in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:16">James 2:16</span> (what is the use?). It is practical, not speculative, that counts in the hour of need. </p>
