<p><b> Sinneth not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουχ αμαρτανε</span></span>). Linear present (linear <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενων</span></span>, keeps on abiding) active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτανω</span></span>, "does not keep on sinning." For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενω</span></span> (abide) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:6">1 John 2:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:4-10">John 15:4-10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Whosoever sinneth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αμαρτανων</span></span>). Present (linear) active articular participle like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενων</span></span> above, "the one who keeps on sinning" (lives a life of sin, not mere occasional acts of sin as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτησας</span></span>, aorist active participle, would mean).</p>
<p><b>Hath not seen him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουχ εωρακεν αυτον</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span>. The habit of sin is proof that one has not the vision or the knowledge (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγνωκεν</span></span>, perfect active also) of Christ. He means, of course, spiritual vision and spiritual knowledge, not the literal sense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:18">John 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:29">John 20:29</span>. </p>
