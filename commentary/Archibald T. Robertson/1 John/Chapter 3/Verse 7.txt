<p><b> Let no man lead you astray </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηδεις πλανατω υμας</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλαναω</span></span>, "let no one keep on leading you astray." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:8">1 John 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:26">1 John 2:26</span>. Break the spell of any Gnostic charmer.</p>
<p><b>He that doeth righteousness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ποιων την δικαιοσυνην</span></span>). "He that keeps on doing (present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span>) righteousness." For this idiom with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:6">1 John 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:4">1 John 3:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>He </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινος</span></span>). Christ as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:5">1 John 3:5</span>. </p>
