<p><b> Doeth no sin </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτιαν ου ποιε</span></span>). Linear present active indicative as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:4">1 John 3:4</span> like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτανε</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:8">1 John 3:8</span>. The child of God does not have the habit of sin.</p>
<p><b>His seed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπερμα αυτου</span></span>). God's seed, "the divine principle of life" (Vincent). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:1">1 John 3:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>And he cannot sin </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ου δυνατα αμαρτανειν</span></span>). This is a wrong translation, for this English naturally means "and he cannot commit sin" as if it were <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ου δυνατα αμαρτειν</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτησα</span></span> (second aorist or first aorist active infinitive). The present active infinitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτανειν</span></span> can only mean "and he cannot go on sinning," as is true of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτανε</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:8">1 John 3:8</span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτανων</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:6">1 John 3:6</span>. For the aorist subjunctive to commit a sin see <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτητε</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτη</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:1">1 John 2:1</span>. A great deal of false theology has grown out of a misunderstanding of the tense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτανειν</span></span> here. Paul has precisely John's idea in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:1">Romans 6:1</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιμενωμεν τη αμαρτια</span></span> (shall we continue in sin, present active linear subjunctive) in contrast with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτησωμεν</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:15">Romans 6:15</span> (shall we commit a sin, first aorist active subjunctive). </p>
