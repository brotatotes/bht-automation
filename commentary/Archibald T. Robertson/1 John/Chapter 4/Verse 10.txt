<p><b> Not that </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουχ οτ</span></span>)</p>
<p><b>--but that </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ' οτ</span></span>). Sharp contrast as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:22">John 7:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:9">2 Corinthians 7:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:17">Philippians 4:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>We loved </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγαπησαμεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative, but B reads <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγαπηκαμεν</span></span> (perfect active, we have loved).</p>
<p><b>He </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτος</span></span>). Emphatic nominative (God).</p>
<p><b>To be the propitiation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιλασμον</span></span>). Merely predicate accusative in apposition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υιον</span></span> (Son). For the word see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:2">1 John 2:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:25">Romans 3:25</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιλαστηριον</span></span>, and for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ</span></span> see also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:2">1 John 2:2</span>. </p>
