<p><b> If God so loved us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε ουτως ο θεος ηγαπησεν ημας</span></span>). Condition of first class with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span> and the first aorist active indicative. As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:16">John 3:16</span>, so here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτως</span></span> emphasises the manifestation of God's love both in its manner and in its extent (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:32">Romans 8:32</span>).</p>
<p><b>Ought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφειλομεν</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:6">1 John 2:6</span>. <i>Noblesse oblige</i>. "Keep on loving," (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπαιν</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:11">1 John 3:11</span>. </p>
