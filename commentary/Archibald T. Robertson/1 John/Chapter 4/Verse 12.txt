<p><b> No one hath beheld God at any time </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεον ουδεις πωποτε τεθεατα</span></span>). Perfect middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεαομα</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:14">John 1:14</span>). Almost the very words of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:18">John 1:18</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεον ουδεις πωποτε εωρακεν</span></span> (instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεθεατα</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>If we love one another </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν αγαπωμεν αλληλους</span></span>). Third-class condition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and the present active subjunctive, "if we keep on loving one another."</p>
<p><b>God abideth in us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο θεος εν ημιν μενε</span></span>). Else we cannot go on loving one another.</p>
<p><b>His love </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η αγαπη αυτου</span></span>). More than merely subjective or objective (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:5">1 John 2:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:9">1 John 4:9</span>). "Mutual love is a sign of the indwelling of God in men" (Brooke).</p>
<p><b>Is perfected </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τετελειωμενη εστιν</span></span>). Periphrastic (see usual form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τετελειωτα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:5">1 John 2:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:17">1 John 4:17</span>) perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελειοω</span></span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:4">1 John 1:4</span>). See verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:18">1 John 4:18</span> for "perfect love." </p>
