<p><b> We have beheld </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεθεαμεθα</span></span>). Perfect middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεαομα</span></span> as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:12">1 John 4:12</span>, though the aorist in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:1">1 John 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:14">John 1:14</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εθεασαμεθα</span></span>). John is qualified to bear witness (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρουμεν</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:2">1 John 1:2</span>) as Jesus had charged the disciples to do (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:8">Acts 1:8</span>).</p>
<p><b>Hath sent </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεσταλκεν</span></span>). As in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:9">1 John 4:9</span>, though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεστειλεν</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:10">1 John 4:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>To be the Saviour of the world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωτηρα του κοσμου</span></span>). Predicate accusative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωτηρ</span></span> (Saviour), like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιλασμον</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:10">1 John 4:10</span>. This very phrase occurs elsewhere only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:42">John 4:42</span> as the confession of the Samaritans, but the idea is in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:17">John 3:17</span>. </p>
