<p><b> Whosoever shall confess </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος εαν ομολογηση</span></span>). Indefinite relative clause with modal <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> (=an) and the first aorist active subjunctive, "whoever confesses." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:23">1 John 2:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:2">1 John 4:2</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομολογεω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>That </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span>). Object clause (indirect assertion) after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομολογεω</span></span>. This confession of the deity of Jesus Christ implies surrender and obedience also, not mere lip service (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:3">1 Corinthians 12:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:6-12">Romans 10:6-12</span>). This confession is proof (if genuine) of the fellowship with God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:3">1 John 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:24">1 John 3:24</span>). </p>
