<p><b> Fear </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φοβος</span></span>). Like a bond-slave (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:15">Romans 8:15</span>), not the reverence of a son (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλαβεια</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:7">Hebrews 5:7</span>) or the obedience to a father (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν φοβω</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:17">1 Peter 1:17</span>). This kind of dread is the opposite of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρρησια</span></span> (boldness).</p>
<p><b>Perfect love </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η τελεια αγαπη</span></span>). There is such a thing, perfect because it has been perfected (verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:12">1 John 4:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:17">1 John 4:17</span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:4">James 1:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>Casteth out fear </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξω βαλλε τον φοβον</span></span>). "Drives fear out" so that it does not exist in real love. See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκβαλλω εξω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:37">John 6:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:34">John 9:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:31">John 12:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:6">John 15:6</span> to turn out-of-doors, a powerful metaphor. Perfect love harbours no suspicion and no dread (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:1">1 John 4:1</span>).</p>
<p><b>Hath punishment </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολασιν εχε</span></span>). Old word, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:46">Matthew 25:46</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Τιμωρια</span></span> has only the idea of penalty, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολασις</span></span> has also that of discipline, while <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παιδεια</span></span> has that of chastisement (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:7">Hebrews 12:7</span>). The one who still dreads (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φοβουμενος</span></span>) has not been made perfect in love (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου τετελειωτα</span></span>). Bengel graphically describes different types of men: "sine timore et amore; cum timore sine amore; cum timore et amore; sine timore cum amore." </p>
