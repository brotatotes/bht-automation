<p><b> Have overcome them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νενικηκατε αυτους</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νικαω</span></span>, calm confidence of final victory as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:13">1 John 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:33">John 16:33</span>. The reference in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτους</span></span> (them) is to the false prophets in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:1">1 John 4:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Because </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span>). The reason for the victory lies in God, who abides in them (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:20">1 John 3:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:24">1 John 3:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:20">John 14:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:4">John 15:4</span>). God is greater than Satan, "he that is in the world" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο εν τω κοσμω</span></span>), the prince of this world (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:31">John 12:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:30">John 14:30</span>), the god of this age (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:4">2 Corinthians 4:4</span>), powerful as he seems. </p>
