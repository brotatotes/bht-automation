<p><b> Was manifested </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφανερωθη</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανεροω</span></span>. The Incarnation as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:5">1 John 3:5</span>. Subjective genitive as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:5">1 John 2:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>In us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ημιν</span></span>). In our case, not "among us" nor "to us." Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:16">Galatians 1:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>Hath sent </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεσταλκεν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστελλω</span></span>, as again in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:14">1 John 4:14</span>, the permanent mission of the Son, though in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:10">1 John 4:10</span> the aorist <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεστειλεν</span></span> occurs for the single event. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:16">John 3:16</span> for this great idea.</p>
<p><b>His only-begotten Son </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον υιον αυτου τον μονογενη</span></span>). "His Son the only-begotten" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:16">John 3:16</span>. John applies <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μονογενης</span></span> to Jesus alone (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:14">John 1:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:18">John 1:18</span>), but Luke (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:12">Luke 7:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:42">Luke 8:42</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:38">Luke 9:38</span>) to others. Jesus alone completely reproduces the nature and character of God (Brooke).</p>
<p><b>That we might live through him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα ζησωμεν δι' αυτου</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the first aorist (ingressive, get life) active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζαω</span></span>. "Through him" is through Christ, who is the life (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:6">John 14:6</span>). Christ also lives in us (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:20">Galatians 2:20</span>). This life begins here and now. </p>
