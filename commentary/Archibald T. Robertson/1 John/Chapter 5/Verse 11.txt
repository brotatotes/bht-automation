<p><b> That God gave </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ εδωκεν ο θεος</span></span>). Declarative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span> in apposition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρια</span></span> as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:14">1 John 5:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:19">John 3:19</span>. Note aorist active indicative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδωκεν</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:23">1 John 3:23</span>, the great historic fact of the Incarnation (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:16">John 3:16</span>), but the perfect <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεδωκεν</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:1">1 John 3:1</span> to emphasize the abiding presence of God's love.</p>
<p><b>Eternal life </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωην αιωνιον</span></span>). Anarthrous emphasizing quality, but with the article in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:2">1 John 1:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>In his Son </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω υιω αυτου</span></span>). This life and the witness also. This is why Jesus who is life (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:6">John 14:6</span>) came to give us abundant life (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:10">John 10:10</span>). </p>
