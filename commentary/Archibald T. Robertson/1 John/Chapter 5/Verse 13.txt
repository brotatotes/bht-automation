<p><b> I have written </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγραψα</span></span>). Not epistolary aorist, but refers to verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:1-12">1 John 5:1-12</span> of this Epistle as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:26">1 John 2:26</span> to the preceding verses.</p>
<p><b>That ye may know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα ειδητε</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the second perfect active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιδα</span></span>, to know with settled intuitive knowledge. He wishes them to have eternal life in Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:31">John 20:31</span>) and to know that they have it, but not with flippant superficiality (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:3">1 John 2:3</span>).</p>
<p><b>Unto you that believe on </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις πιστευουσιν εις</span></span>). Dative of the articular present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστευω</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:10">1 John 5:10</span>. For this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ονομα</span></span> (name) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστευω</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:23">1 John 3:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:23">John 2:23</span>. </p>
