<p><b> We know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιδαμεν</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:2">1 John 3:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:14">1 John 3:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:15">1 John 5:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:19">1 John 5:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:20">1 John 5:20</span>. He has "ye know" in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:20">1 John 2:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:5">1 John 3:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:15">1 John 3:15</span>.</p>
<p><b>Sinneth not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουχ αμαρτανε</span></span>). Lineal present active indicative, "does not keep on sinning," as he has already shown in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:4-10">1 John 3:4-10</span>.</p>
<p><b>He that was begotten of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο γεννηθεις εκ του θεου</span></span>). First aorist passive articular participle referring to Christ, if the reading of A B is correct (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρε αυτον</span></span>, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρε εαυτον</span></span>). It is Christ who keeps the one begotten of God (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεγεννημενος εκ του θεου</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:9">1 John 3:9</span> and so different from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο γεννηθεις</span></span> here). It is a difficult phrase, but this is probably the idea. Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:37">John 18:37</span>) uses <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεγεννημα</span></span> of himself and uses also <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρεω</span></span> of keeping the disciples (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:12">John 17:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:15">John 17:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:10">Revelation 3:10</span>).</p>
<p><b>The evil one </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο πονηρος</span></span>). Masculine and personal as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:13">1 John 2:13</span>, not neuter, and probably Satan as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:13">Matthew 6:13</span>, not just any evil man.</p>
<p><b>Touchest him not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουχ απτετα αυτου</span></span>). Present middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απτω</span></span>, elsewhere in John only <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:17">John 20:17</span>. It means to lay hold of or to grasp rather than a mere superficial touch (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θιγγανω</span></span>, both in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:21">Colossians 2:21</span>). Here the idea is to touch to harm. The devil cannot snatch such a man from Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:38">John 6:38</span>). </p>
