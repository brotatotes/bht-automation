<p><b> Of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ του θεου</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:10">1 John 3:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:6">1 John 4:6</span> for this idiom.</p>
<p><b>Lieth in the evil one </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω πονηρω κειτα</span></span>). Present middle indicative of the defective verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κειμα</span></span>, to lie, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:12">Luke 2:12</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πονηρω</span></span> is masculine, like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο πονηρος</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:18">1 John 5:18</span>. This is a terrible picture of the Graeco-Roman world of the first century A.D., which is confirmed by Paul in Romans 1 and 2 and by Horace, Seneca, Juvenal, Tacitus. </p>
