<p><b> This </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτη</span></span>)</p>
<p><b>--that </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>). Explanatory use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτη</span></span>, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:3">John 17:3</span>, to show what "the love of God" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:9">1 John 4:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:12">1 John 4:12</span>) in the objective sense is, not mere declamatory boasting (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:20">1 John 4:20</span>), but obedience to God's commands, "that we keep on keeping (present active subjunctive as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:3">1 John 2:3</span>) his commandments." This is the supreme test.</p>
<p><b>Are not grievous </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαρεια ουκ εισιν</span></span>). "Not heavy," the adjective in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:4">Matthew 23:4</span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φορτια</span></span> (burdens), with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυπο</span></span> (wolves) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:29">Acts 20:29</span>, of Paul's letters in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:10">2 Corinthians 10:10</span>, of the charges against Paul in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:7">Acts 25:7</span>. Love for God lightens his commands. </p>
