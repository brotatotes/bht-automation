<p><b> For </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span>). The reason why God's commandments are not heavy is the power that comes with the new birth from God.</p>
<p><b>Whatsoever is begotten of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παν το γεγεννημενον εκ του θεου</span></span>). Neuter singular perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενναω</span></span> rather than the masculine singular (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:1">1 John 5:1</span>) to express sharply the universality of the principle (Rothe) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:6">John 3:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:8">John 3:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:37">John 6:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:39">John 6:39</span>.</p>
<p><b>Overcometh the world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νικα τον κοσμον</span></span>). Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νικαω</span></span>, a continuous victory because a continuous struggle, "keeps on conquering the world" ("the sum of all the forces antagonistic to the spiritual life," D. Smith).</p>
<p><b>This is the victory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτη εστιν η νικη</span></span>). For this form of expression see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:5">1 John 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:19">John 1:19</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Νικη</span></span> (victory, cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νικαω</span></span>), old word, here alone in N.T., but the later form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νικος</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:20">Matthew 12:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:54">1 Corinthians 15:54</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:57">1 Corinthians 15:57</span>.</p>
<p><b>That overcometh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η νικησασα</span></span>). First aorist active articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νικαω</span></span>. The English cannot reproduce the play on the word here. The aorist tense singles out an individual experience when one believed or when one met temptation with victory. Jesus won the victory over the world (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:33">John 16:33</span>) and God in us (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:4">1 John 4:4</span>) gives us the victory.</p>
<p><b>Even our faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η πιστις ημων</span></span>). The only instance of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστις</span></span> in the Johannine Epistles (not in John's Gospel, though in the Apocalypse). It is our faith in Jesus Christ as shown by our confession (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:1">1 John 5:1</span>) and by our life (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:2">1 John 5:2</span>). </p>
