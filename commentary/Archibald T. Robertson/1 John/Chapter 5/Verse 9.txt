<p><b> If we receive </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε λαμβανομεν</span></span>). Condition of first class with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span> and the present active indicative, assumed as true. The conditions for a legally valid witness are laid down in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+19:15">Deuteronomy 19:15</span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:16">Matthew 18:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:17">John 8:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:25">John 10:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+13:1">2 Corinthians 13:1</span>).</p>
<p><b>Greater </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μειζων</span></span>). Comparative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεγας</span></span>, because God is always true.</p>
<p><b>For </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span>). So it applies to this case.</p>
<p><b>That </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span>). Thus taken in the declarative sense (the fact that) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:19">John 3:19</span>, though it can be causal (because) or indefinite relative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεμαρτυρηκεν</span></span> (what he hath testified, perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρεω</span></span>, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:32">John 1:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:44">John 4:44</span>, etc.), a harsh construction here because of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρια</span></span>, though some MSS. do read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> to agree with it (cf. verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:10">1 John 5:10</span>). See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ εαν</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:20">1 John 3:20</span> for that idiom. Westcott notes the Trinity in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:6-9">1 John 5:6-9</span>: the Son comes, the Spirit witnesses, the Father has witnessed. </p>
