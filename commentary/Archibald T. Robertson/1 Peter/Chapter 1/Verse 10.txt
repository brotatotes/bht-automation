<p><b> Concerning which salvation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ ης σωτηριας</span></span>). Another relative clause (taking up <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωτηρια</span></span> from verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:9">1 Peter 1:9</span> and incorporating it) in this long sentence (verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:3-12">1 Peter 1:3-12</span>, inclusive, all connected by relatives). Peter lingers over the word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωτηρια</span></span> (salvation) with something new to say each time (Bigg). Here it is the general sense of the gospel of grace.</p>
<p><b>Sought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεζητησαν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκζητεω</span></span>, to seek out (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:17">Acts 15:17</span>), late and rare compound, only in LXX and N.T. save once in Aristides.</p>
<p><b>Searched diligently </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξηραυνησαν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεραυναω</span></span>, old and common compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξερευναω</span></span>), to search out diligently, here only in N.T. Both of these words occur together in I Macc. 9:26.</p>
<p><b>Of the grace that should come unto you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ της εις υμας χαριτος</span></span>). "Concerning the for you grace" (meant for you). </p>
