<p><b> To whom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οις</span></span>). Dative plural of the relative pronoun. To the prophets who were seeking to understand. Bigg observes that "the connexion between study and inspiration is a great mystery." Surely, but that is no argument for ignorance or obscurantism. We do the best that we can and only skirt the shore of knowledge, as Newton said.</p>
<p><b>It was revealed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεκαλυφθη</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκαλυπτω</span></span>, old verb, to reveal, to unveil. Here is revelation about the revelation already received, revelation after research.</p>
<p><b>Did they minister </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διηκονουν</span></span>). Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονεω</span></span>, old verb, to minister, "were they ministering."</p>
<p><b>Have been announced </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανηγγελη</span></span>). Second aorist passive indicative of</p>
<p><b>anaggello </b>, to report, to bring back tidings (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:25">John 4:25</span>).</p>
<p><b>Through them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια των</span></span>). Intermediate agent (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span>), "the gospelizers" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των ευαγγελισαμενων</span></span>, articular first aorist middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευαγγελιζω</span></span>, to preach the gospel).</p>
<p><b>By the Holy Ghost </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευματ αγιω</span></span>). Instrumental case of the personal agent, "by the Holy Spirit" (without article).</p>
<p><b>Sent forth from heaven </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποσταλεντ</span></span>). Second aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστελλω</span></span> in instrumental case agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευματ αγιω</span></span> (the Spirit of Christ of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:11">1 Peter 1:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>Desire </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιθυμουσιν</span></span>). Eagerly desire (present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιθυμεω</span></span>, to long for).</p>
<p><b>To look into </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρακυψα</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρακυπτω</span></span>, old compound to peer into as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:12">Luke 24:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:5">John 20:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:11">John 20:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:25">James 1:25</span>, which see. For the interest of angels in the Incarnation see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:13">Luke 2:13</span>. </p>
