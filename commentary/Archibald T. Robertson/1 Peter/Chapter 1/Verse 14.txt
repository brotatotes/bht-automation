<p><b> As children of obedience </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως τεκνα υπακοης</span></span>). A common Hebraism (descriptive genitive frequent in LXX and N.T., like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υιο της απειθειας</span></span>, children of disobedience, in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:2">Ephesians 2:2</span>) suggested by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπακοην</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:2">1 Peter 1:2</span>, "children marked by obedience."</p>
<p><b>Not fashioning yourselves </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη συνσχηματιζομενο</span></span>). Usual negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> with the participle (present direct middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνσχηματιζω</span></span>, a rare (Aristotle, Plutarch) compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν, σχηματιζω</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σχημα</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:2">Romans 12:2</span> (the outward pattern in contrast with the inward change <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταμορφοω</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:6">Philippians 2:6</span> for contrast between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σχημα</span></span> (pattern) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μορφη</span></span> (form).</p>
<p><b>According to your former lusts </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταις προτερον επιθυμιαις</span></span>). Associative instrumental case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνσχηματιζομενο</span></span> and the bad sense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιθυμια</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:2">1 Peter 4:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:4">2 Peter 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:14">James 1:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>In the time of your ignorance </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη αγνοια υμων</span></span>). "In your ignorance," but in attributive position before "lusts." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αγνοια</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγνοεω</span></span>, to be ignorant) is old word, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:17">Acts 3:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:30">Acts 17:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:18">Ephesians 4:18</span>. </p>
