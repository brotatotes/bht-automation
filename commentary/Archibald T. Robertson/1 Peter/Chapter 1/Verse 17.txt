<p><b> If ye call </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε επικαλεισθε</span></span>). Condition of first class and present middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επικαλεω</span></span>, to call a name on, to name (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:18">Acts 10:18</span>).</p>
<p><b>As Father </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πατερα</span></span>). Predicate accusative in apposition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον--κρινοντα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Without respect of persons </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απροσωπολημπτως</span></span>). Found nowhere else except in the later Ep. of Clem. of Rome and Ep. of Barn., from alpha privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσωπολημπτης</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:34">Acts 10:34</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:9">James 2:9</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσωπολημπτεω</span></span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:1">1 Peter 1:1</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσωπολημψια</span></span>) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσωπον λαμβανω</span></span> (in imitation of the Hebrew).</p>
<p><b>According to each man's work </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα το εκαστου εργον</span></span>). "According to the deed of each one" God judges (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρινοντα</span></span>) just as Christ judges also (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:10">2 Corinthians 5:10</span>).</p>
<p><b>Pass </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναστραφητε</span></span>). Second aorist passive imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναστρεφω</span></span>, metaphorical sense as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:12">2 Corinthians 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:18">2 Peter 2:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>The time </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον χρονον</span></span>). Accusative case of extent of time.</p>
<p><b>Of your sojourning </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της παροικιας υμων</span></span>). A late word, found in LXX (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+119:5">Psalms 119:5</span>) and in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:17">Acts 13:17</span> and in ecclesiastical writers (one late Christian inscription). It comes from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παροικεω</span></span>, old verb, to dwell beside (in one's neighbourhood), and so of pilgrims or strangers (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παροικος</span></span> <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:6">Acts 7:6</span>) as of Jews away from Palestine or of Christians here on earth, then of a local region (our "parish"). Peter here recurs to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:1">1 Peter 1:1</span> ("sojourners of the Dispersion").</p>
<p><b>In fear </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν φοβω</span></span>). Emphatic position at beginning of the clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναστραφητε</span></span> at the end. </p>
