<p><b> But with precious blood </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα τιμιω αιματ</span></span>). Instrumental case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιμα</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελυτρωθητε</span></span> (repeated from verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:18">1 Peter 1:18</span>). Peter here applies the old adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιμιος</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιμη</span></span>, of Christ in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:7">1 Peter 2:7</span>) to Christ as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:7">1 Peter 1:7</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολυτιμοτερον</span></span> to testing of faith. The blood of anyone is "precious" (costly), far above gold or silver, but that of Jesus immeasurably more so.</p>
<p><b>As of a lamb </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως αμνου</span></span>). This word occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="le+12:8">Leviticus 12:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+15:11">Numbers 15:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+14:4">Deuteronomy 14:4</span> of the lamb prescribed for the passover sacrifice (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+12:5">Exodus 12:5</span>). John the Baptist applies it to Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:29">John 1:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:36">John 1:36</span>). It occurs also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:32">Acts 8:32</span> quoted from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+53:7">Isaiah 53:7</span>. Undoubtedly both the Baptist and Peter have this passage in mind. Elsewhere in the N.T. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρνιον</span></span> is used of Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:6">Revelation 5:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:12">Revelation 5:12</span>). Jesus is the Paschal Lamb. Peter sees clearly that it was by the blood of Christ that we are redeemed from sin.</p>
<p><b>Without blemish </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμωμου</span></span>). Without (alpha privative) spot (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μωμος</span></span>) as the paschal lamb had to be (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="le+22:21">Leviticus 22:21</span>). So <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:14">Hebrews 9:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>Without spot </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασπιλου</span></span>). Without (alpha privative) stain (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπιλος</span></span> spot) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:27">James 1:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:14">2 Peter 3:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+6:14">1 Peter 6:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>Even the blood of Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χριστου</span></span>). Genitive case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιματ</span></span>, but in unusual position for emphasis and clearness with the participles following. </p>
