<p><b> Who was foreknown indeed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προεγνωσμενου μεν</span></span>). Perfect passive participle (in genitive singular agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χριστου</span></span>) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προγινωσκω</span></span>, old verb, to know beforehand (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:29">Romans 8:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:17">2 Peter 3:17</span>). See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προγνωσιν θεου</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:2">1 Peter 1:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>Before the foundation of the world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προ καταβολης κοσμου</span></span>). This precise curious phrase occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:24">John 17:24</span> in the Saviour's mouth of his preincarnate state with the Father as here and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:4">Ephesians 1:4</span>. We have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο καταβολης κοσμου</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:34">Matthew 25:34</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοσμου</span></span> omitted in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:35">Matthew 13:35</span>); <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:50">Luke 11:50</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:3">Hebrews 4:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:26">Hebrews 9:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:8">Revelation 13:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:8">Revelation 17:8</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Καταβολη</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταβαλλω</span></span>) was originally laying the foundation of a house (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:1">Hebrews 6:1</span>). The preincarnate Messiah appears in the counsels of God also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:7">1 Corinthians 2:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:26">Colossians 1:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:9">Ephesians 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:9-11">Ephesians 3:9-11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:25">Romans 16:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:9">1 Timothy 1:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>But was manifested </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανερωθεντος δε</span></span>). First aorist (ingressive) passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανεροω</span></span>, referring to the Incarnation in contrast with the preexistence of Christ (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:31">John 1:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:5">1 John 3:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:8">1 John 3:8</span>).</p>
<p><b>At the end of the times </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ' εσχατου των χρονων</span></span>). Like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ' εσχατου των ημερων</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:2">Hebrews 1:2</span>). The plural <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρονο</span></span>, doubtless referring to successive periods in human history until the fullness of the time came (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:4">Galatians 4:4</span>).</p>
<p><b>For your sake </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι' υμας</span></span>). Proof of God's love, not of their desert or worth (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:30">Acts 17:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:39">Hebrews 11:39</span>). </p>
