<p><b> Who through him are believers in God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους δι' αυτου πιστους εις θεον</span></span>). Accusative case in apposition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμας</span></span> (you), "the through him (that is Christ as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:8">1 Peter 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:16">Acts 3:16</span>) believers (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστους</span></span> correct text of A B) in God."</p>
<p><b>Which raised </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον εγειραντα</span></span>). Accusative singular articular (agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεον</span></span>) first aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγειρω</span></span> (cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι' αναστασεως Ιησου</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:3">1 Peter 1:3</span>).</p>
<p><b>Gave glory to him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξαν αυτω δοντα</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span> agreeing also with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεον</span></span>. See Peter's speech in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:13">Acts 3:13</span> about God glorifying (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδοξασεν</span></span>) Jesus and also the same idea by Peter in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:33-36">Acts 2:33-36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:31">Acts 5:31</span>.</p>
<p><b>So that your faith and hope might be in God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωστε την πιστιν υμων κα ελπιδα εις θεον</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hωστε</span></span> with the infinitive (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εινα</span></span>) and the accusative of general reference (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστιν κα ελπιδα</span></span>) is used in the N.T. as in the <i>Koine</i> for either purpose (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:1">Matthew 10:1</span>) or usually result (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:37">Mark 4:37</span>). Hence here result (so that is) is more probable than design. </p>
