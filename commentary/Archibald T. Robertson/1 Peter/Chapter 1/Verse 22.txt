<p><b> Seeing ye have purified </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγνικοτες</span></span>). Perfect active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγνιζω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγνος</span></span> (pure), here with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψυχας</span></span> (souls), with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καρδιας</span></span> (hearts) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:8">James 4:8</span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:3">1 John 3:3</span> of moral cleansing also. See the ceremonial sense of the word as in LXX in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:55">John 11:55</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:24">Acts 21:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:26">Acts 21:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:18">Acts 24:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>In your obedience </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη υπακοη</span></span>). With repetition of the idea in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:2">1 Peter 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:14">1 Peter 1:14</span> (children of obedience).</p>
<p><b>To the truth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της αλεθειας</span></span>). Objective genitive with which compare <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:17">John 17:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:19">John 17:19</span> about sanctification in the truth and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:12">2 Thessalonians 2:12</span> about believing the truth. There is cleansing power in the truth of God in Christ.</p>
<p><b>Unfeigned </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανυποκριτον</span></span>). Late and rare double compound, here alone in Peter, but see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:17">James 3:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:6">2 Corinthians 6:6</span>, etc. No other kind of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλαδελφια</span></span> (brotherly love) is worth having (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:9">1 Thessalonians 4:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:1">Hebrews 13:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:7">2 Peter 1:7</span>).</p>
<p><b>From the heart fervently </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ καρδιας εκτενως</span></span>). Late adverb (in inscriptions, Polybius, LXX). The adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκτενης</span></span> is more common (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:8">1 Peter 4:8</span>). </p>
