<p><b> Having been begotten again </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγεγεννημενο</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγενναω</span></span>, which see in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:2">1 Peter 1:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>Not of corruptible seed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ εκ σπορας φθαρτης</span></span>). Ablative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span> as the source, for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φθαρτος</span></span> see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:18">1 Peter 1:18</span>, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπορας</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπειρω</span></span> to sow), old word (sowing, seed) here only in N.T., though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπορος</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:26">Mark 4:26</span>, etc. For "incorruptible" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφθαρτου</span></span>) see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:4">1 Peter 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:4">1 Peter 3:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>Through the word of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια λογου θεου</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:18">James 1:18</span> for "by the word of truth," verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:25">1 Peter 1:25</span> here, and Peter's use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογος</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:36">Acts 10:36</span>. It is the gospel message.</p>
<p><b>Which liveth and abideth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωντος κα μενοντος</span></span>). These present active participles (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζαω</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενω</span></span>) can be taken with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεου</span></span> (God) or with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογου</span></span> (word). In verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:25">1 Peter 1:25</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενε</span></span> is used with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρημα</span></span> (word). Still in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="da+6:26">Daniel 6:26</span> both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενων</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζων</span></span> are used with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεος</span></span>. Either construction makes sense here. </p>
