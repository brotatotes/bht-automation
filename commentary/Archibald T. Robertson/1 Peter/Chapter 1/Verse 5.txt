<p><b> By the power of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν δυναμε θεου</span></span>). No other <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυναμις</span></span> (power) like this (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:3">Colossians 1:3</span>).</p>
<p><b>Are guarded </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φρουρουμενους</span></span>). Present (continuous process) passive articular (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους</span></span>) participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φρουρεω</span></span>, to garrison, old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φρουρος</span></span> sentinel), a military term (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:24">Acts 9:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:32">2 Corinthians 11:32</span>), used of God's love (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:7">Philippians 4:7</span>) as here. "The inheritance is kept; the heirs are guarded" (Bengel).</p>
<p><b>Through faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια πιστεως</span></span>). Intermediate agency (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span>), the immediate being (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span>, in, by) God's power.</p>
<p><b>Unto a salvation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις σωτηριαν</span></span>). Deliverance is the goal (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span>) of the process and final salvation here, consummation as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:8">1 Thessalonians 5:8</span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωτηρ</span></span> (Saviour, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωζω</span></span>, to save).</p>
<p><b>Ready </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετοιμην</span></span>). Prepared awaiting God's will (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:23">Galatians 3:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:18">Romans 8:18</span>).</p>
<p><b>To be revealed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκαλυφθηνα</span></span>). First aorist passive infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκαλυπτω</span></span>, to unveil. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:4">Colossians 3:4</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανεροω</span></span> (to manifest) in this sense.</p>
<p><b>In the last time </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν καιρω εσχατω</span></span>). This precise phrase nowhere else, but similar ones in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:39">John 6:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:17">Acts 2:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:3">James 5:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:1">2 Timothy 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:3">2 Peter 3:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:2">Hebrews 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jdg+1:18">Judges 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:18">1 John 2:18</span>. Hort translates it here "in a season of extremity," but it is usually taken to refer to the Day of Judgment. That day no one knows, Jesus said. </p>
