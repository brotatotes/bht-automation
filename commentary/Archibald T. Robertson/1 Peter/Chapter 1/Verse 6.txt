<p><b> Wherein </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ω</span></span>). This translation refers the relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ω</span></span> to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιρω</span></span>, but it is possible to see a reference to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χριστου</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:3">1 Peter 1:3</span>) or to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεου</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:5">1 Peter 1:5</span>) or even to the entire content of verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:3-5">1 Peter 1:3-5</span>. Either makes sense, though possibly <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιρω</span></span> is correct.</p>
<p><b>Ye greatly rejoice </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαλλιασθε</span></span>). Present middle indicative (rather than imperative) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαλλιαομα</span></span>, late verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαλλομα</span></span>, to rejoice, only in LXX, N.T., and ecclesiastical literature as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:12">Matthew 5:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>Now for a little while </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ολιγον αρτ</span></span>). Accusative case of time (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ολιγον</span></span>) probably as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:31">Mark 6:31</span>, though it can be used of space (to a small extent) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:3">Luke 5:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>If need be </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε δεον</span></span>). Present active neuter singular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span> (it is necessary). Some MSS. have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστιν</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεον</span></span> (periphrastic construction). Condition of first class.</p>
<p><b>Though ye have been put to grief </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυπηθεντες</span></span>). First aorist passive participle (concessive circumstantial use) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυπεω</span></span>, to make sorrowful (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυπη</span></span>, sorrow), old and common verb. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:10">2 Corinthians 6:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>In manifold temptations </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ποικιλοις πειρασμοις</span></span>). Just the phrase in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:2">James 1:2</span>, which see for discussion. "Trials" clearly right here as there. Seven N.T. writers use <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποικιλος</span></span> (varied). </p>
