<p><b> The proof of your faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το δοκιμιον υμων της πιστεως</span></span>). The identical phrase in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:3">James 1:3</span> and probably derived from there by Peter. See there for discussion of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το δοκιμιον</span></span> (the test or touchstone of faith).</p>
<p><b>Being more precious </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολυτιμοτερον</span></span>). No word for "being" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ον</span></span>) in the Greek. The secondary uncials have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολυ τιμιωτερον</span></span>. The text is the comparative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολυτιμος</span></span>, late adjective (Plutarch) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολυ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιμη</span></span> (of great price) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:46">Matthew 13:46</span>.</p>
<p><b>Than gold </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρυσιου</span></span>). Ablative case after the comparative adjective.</p>
<p><b>That perisheth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του απολλυμενου</span></span>). Present middle articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολλυμ</span></span> to destroy. Even gold perishes (wears away).</p>
<p><b>Though it is proved by fire </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια πυρος δε δοκιμαζομενου</span></span>). Present passive articular participle (in the ablative like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρυσιου</span></span>) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκιμαζω</span></span> (common verb for testing metals) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span>, which gives a concessive sense to the participle. Faith stands the test of fire better than gold, but even gold is refined by fire.</p>
<p><b>That might be found </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα ευρεθη</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρισκω</span></span>, common verb, to find. As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:14">2 Peter 3:14</span>, this is the result of the probation by God as the Refiner of hearts.</p>
<p><b>Unto praise and glory and honour </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις επαινον κα δοξαν κα τιμην</span></span>). Here probably both to God and man in the result. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:11">Matthew 5:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:7">Romans 2:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:10">Romans 2:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:17">1 Timothy 1:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>At the revelation of Jesus Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αποκαλυψε Ιησου Χριστου</span></span>). So also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:13">1 Peter 1:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:13">1 Peter 4:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:7">2 Thessalonians 1:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:7">1 Corinthians 1:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:30">Luke 17:30</span> of the second coming of Christ as the Judge and Rewarder (Bigg). </p>
