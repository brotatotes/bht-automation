<p><span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:24">1 Peter 1:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:25">1 Peter 1:25</span> Quotation from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+40:6-8">Isaiah 40:6-8</span> (partly like the LXX, partly like the Hebrew).</p>
<p><b>For </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διοτ</span></span>). As in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:16">1 Peter 1:16</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span>), "for that." So in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:6">1 Peter 2:6</span>. See a free use of this imagery about the life of man as grass and a flower in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:11">James 1:11</span>. The best MSS. here read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτης</span></span> (thereof) after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξα</span></span> (glory) rather than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωπου</span></span> (of man).</p>
<p><b>Withereth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξηρανθη</span></span>). First aorist (gnomic, timeless) passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξηραινω</span></span> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:11">James 1:11</span>).</p>
<p><b>Falleth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεπεσεν</span></span>). Second aorist (gnomic, timeless) active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκπιπτω</span></span> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:11">James 1:11</span>). In verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:25">1 Peter 1:25</span> note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις υμας</span></span> (unto you) like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις υμας</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:4">1 Peter 1:4</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμιν</span></span> dative). </p>
