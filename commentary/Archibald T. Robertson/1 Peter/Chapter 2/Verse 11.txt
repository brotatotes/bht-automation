<p><b> As sojourners and pilgrims </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως παροικους κα παρεπιδημους</span></span>). This combination from the LXX (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+33:4">Genesis 33:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+39:13">Psalms 39:13</span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:1">1 Peter 1:1</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεπιδημος</span></span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:17">1 Peter 1:17</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παροικια</span></span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:19">Ephesians 2:19</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παροικος</span></span> (only there and here in N.T., Christians whose fatherland is heaven).</p>
<p><b>To abstain from </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεχεσθα</span></span>). Present middle (direct) infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεχω</span></span>, old verb, to hold back from (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:3">1 Thessalonians 4:3</span>). In indirect command (to keep on abstaining from) after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρακαλω</span></span> (I beseech). With the ablative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των σαρκικων επιθυμιων</span></span>, the grosser sins of the flesh (for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαρκικος</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:3">1 Corinthians 3:3</span>) like the list in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:3">1 Peter 4:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Which </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιτινες</span></span>). "Which very ones." Like Latin <i>quippe qui</i>.</p>
<p><b>War against the soul </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρατευοντα κατα της ψυχης</span></span>). Present middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρατευω</span></span>, to carry on a campaign (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:1">James 4:1</span>). See this struggle between the flesh and the spirit vividly pictured by Paul in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:16-24">Galatians 5:16-24</span>. </p>
