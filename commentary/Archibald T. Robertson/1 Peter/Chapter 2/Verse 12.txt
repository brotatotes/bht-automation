<p><b> Seemly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλην</span></span>). Predicate adjective with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναστροφην</span></span>, for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:15">1 Peter 1:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:18">1 Peter 1:18</span>. The Gentiles are on the watch for slips in moral conduct by the Christians.</p>
<p><b>That </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>). Final conjunction with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξασωσιν</span></span> (they may glorify, first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξαζω</span></span>, the purpose of the Christians about the Gentiles.</p>
<p><b>Wherein </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ω</span></span>). "In what thing."</p>
<p><b>As evil-doers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως κακοποιων</span></span>). As they did and do, old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακον</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:30">John 18:30</span>), in N.T. only here and verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:14">1 Peter 2:14</span> in correct text. Heathen talk against us (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλαλουσιν</span></span>) gleefully.</p>
<p><b>By your good works </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ των καλων εργων</span></span>). "Out of (as a result of) your good (beautiful) deeds."</p>
<p><b>Which they behold </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εποπτευοντες</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εποπτευω</span></span>, old verb (from, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εποπτης</span></span>, overseer, spectator, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:16">2 Peter 1:16</span>), to be an overseer, to view carefully, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:2">1 Peter 3:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>In the day of visitation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ημερα επισκοπης</span></span>). From <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+10:33">Isaiah 10:33</span>. Cf. its use in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:44">Luke 19:44</span>, which see for the word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισκοπη</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισκοπεω</span></span>, to inspect (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:15">Hebrews 12:15</span>). Clear echo here of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:16">Matthew 5:16</span>. </p>
