<p><b> Be subject to </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποταγητε</span></span>). Second aorist passive imperative second person plural of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποτασσω</span></span>, to subject to, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:22">1 Peter 3:22</span>.</p>
<p><b>Every ordinance of man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παση ανθρωπινη κτισε</span></span>). Dative case of old and common word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κτισις</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κτιζω</span></span>, to create, to found), act of creation (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:20">Romans 1:20</span>), a creature or creation (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:25">Romans 1:25</span>), all creation (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:15">Colossians 1:15</span>), an institution as here (in Pindar so). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωπινος</span></span> (human) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:7">James 3:7</span>. Peter here approves no special kind of government, but he supports law and order as Paul does (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:1-8">Romans 13:1-8</span>) unless it steps in between God and man (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:20">Acts 4:20</span>).</p>
<p><b>For the Lord's sake </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια τον κυριον</span></span>). For Jesus' sake. That is reason enough for the Christian not to be an anarchist (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:21">Matthew 22:21</span>). The heathen were keen to charge the Christians with any crime after Nero set the fashion. "It should not be forgotten that, in spite of the fine language of the philosophers, the really popular religions in Greece and Rome were forms of devil-worship, intimately blended with magic in all its grades" (Bigg).</p>
<p><b>As supreme </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως υπερεχοντ</span></span>). Dative singular of present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερεχω</span></span>, old verb (intransitive), to stand out above (to have it over), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:1">Romans 13:1</span>. It is not the divine right of kings, but the fact of the king as the outstanding ruler. </p>
