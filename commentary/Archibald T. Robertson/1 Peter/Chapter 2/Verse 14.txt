<p><b> Unto governors </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγεμοσιν</span></span>). Dative again of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγεμων</span></span>, a leader (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγεομα</span></span>, to lead), old and common word (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:18">Matthew 10:18</span>).</p>
<p><b>As sent by him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως δι' αυτου πεμπομενοις</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεμπω</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Δι' αυτου</span></span> is "by God," as Jesus made plain to Pilate; even Pilate received his authority ultimately "from above" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:11">John 18:11</span>).</p>
<p><b>For vengeance on evil-doers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις εκδικησιν κακοποιων</span></span>). Objective genitive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκδικησιν</span></span>, for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:7">Luke 18:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>For praise to them that do well </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαινον αγαθοποιων</span></span>). Objective genitive again, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαθοποιος</span></span>, a late word (Plutarch, Sirach) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαθον</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span> here only in N.T. Found in a magical papyrus. </p>
