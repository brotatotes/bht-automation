<p><b> By well-doing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαθοποιουντας</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαθοποιεω</span></span>, only in LXX and N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:4">Mark 3:4</span>). In accusative case agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμας</span></span> understood, accusative of general reference with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιμοιν</span></span>, present active infinitive (epexegetic infinitive after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το θελημα του θεου</span></span>, the will of God), late and rare verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιμος</span></span> muzzle), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:12">Matthew 22:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>The ignorance of foolish men </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την των αφρονων ανθρωπων αγνωσιαν</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αγνωσια</span></span> is late and rare word (in the papyri) from alpha privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνωσις</span></span> (knowledge), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:24">1 Corinthians 15:24</span> (disgraceful ignorance in both instances). Note alliteration. </p>
