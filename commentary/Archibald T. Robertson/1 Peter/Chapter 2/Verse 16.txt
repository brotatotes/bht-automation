<p><b> As free </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως ελευθερο</span></span>). Note nominative again connected with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποταγητε</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:13">1 Peter 2:13</span>, not with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιμοιν</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:14">1 Peter 2:14</span> (a parenthesis in fact). For this ethical sense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελευθερος</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:26">Galatians 4:26</span>.</p>
<p><b>And not using your freedom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα μη εχοντες την ελευθεριαν</span></span>). "And not holding your liberty" (present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>, with usual negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> with participle.</p>
<p><b>For a cloke of wickedness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως επικαλυμμα της κακιας</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Επικαλυμμα</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επικαλυπτω</span></span> <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:7">Romans 4:7</span>) is a rare word (Aristotle, LXX) for veil, here only in N.T. and in figurative sense for pretext to do wickedness under, a thing, alas, that sometimes happens.</p>
<p><b>But as bondservants of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ' ως θεου δουλο</span></span>). Paul's proud title. There is no such thing as absolute freedom (personal freedom), for that is anarchy. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:22">Romans 6:22</span> "enslaved to God." </p>
