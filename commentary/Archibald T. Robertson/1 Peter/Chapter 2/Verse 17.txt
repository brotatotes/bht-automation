<p><b> Honour all men </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντας τιμησατε</span></span>). Not with the same honour. Constative use of the aorist imperative.</p>
<p><b>Love the brotherhood </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την αδελφοτητα αγαπατε</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπαω</span></span>, keep on doing it. Note the abstract <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδελφοτης</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδελφος</span></span>, brother) in the collective sense, rare save in ecclesiastical literature, though in I Macc. 12:10; IV Macc. 10:3, and in late papyri. It is a word for all Christians.</p>
<p><b>Fear God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον θεον φοβεισθε</span></span>). In both senses of reverence and dread, and keep it up (present middle imperative).</p>
<p><b>Honour the king </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον βασιλεα τιματε</span></span>). Keep that up also. A fine motto in this verse. </p>
