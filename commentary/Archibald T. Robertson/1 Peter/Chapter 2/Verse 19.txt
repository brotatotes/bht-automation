<p><b> For this is acceptable </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτο γαρ χαρις</span></span>). "For this thing (neuter singular <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτο</span></span>, obedience to crooked masters) is grace" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρις</span></span> is feminine, here "thanks" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:25">Romans 7:25</span>). "Acceptable" calls for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευπροσδεκτον</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:5">1 Peter 2:5</span>), which is not the text here.</p>
<p><b>If a man endureth griefs </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε υοφερε τις λυπας</span></span>). Condition of first class with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span> and present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποφερω</span></span>, old verb, to bear up under, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:13">1 Corinthians 10:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:11">2 Timothy 3:11</span>. Note plural of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυπη</span></span> (grief).</p>
<p><b>For conscience toward God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια συνειδησιν θεου</span></span>). Suffering is not a blessing in and of itself, but, if one's duty to God is involved (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:20">Acts 4:20</span>), then one can meet it with gladness of heart. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Θεου</span></span> (God) is objective genitive. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνειδησις</span></span> (conscience) see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:1">Acts 23:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+8:7">1 Corinthians 8:7</span>. It occurs again in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:16">1 Peter 3:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>Suffering wrongfully </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πασχων αδικως</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πασχω</span></span> and the common adverb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδικως</span></span>, unjustly, here alone in N.T. This is the whole point, made clear already by Jesus in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:10-12">Matthew 5:10-12</span>, where Jesus has also "falsely" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευδομενο</span></span>). See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:32-34">Luke 6:32-34</span>. </p>
