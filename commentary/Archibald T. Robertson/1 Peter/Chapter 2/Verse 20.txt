<p><b> For what glory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιον γαρ κλεος</span></span>). Qualitative interrogative (what kind of glory). "What price glory?" <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κλεος</span></span> is old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλεω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλεω</span></span>, to call), report, praise, glory, here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>If ye shall take it patiently </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε υπομενειτε</span></span>). First-class condition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span> and future active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπομενω</span></span>, for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:12">James 1:12</span>. Same condition also in next sentence (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ' ε</span></span>, etc.).</p>
<p><b>When ye sin </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτανοντες</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτανω</span></span> (continued repetition).</p>
<p><b>And are buffeted for it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα κολαφιζομενο</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολαφιζω</span></span>, late word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολαφος</span></span> fist), only in N.T. (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:67">Matthew 26:67</span>) and ecclesiastical writers. Repeated action again. No posing as a martyr allowed here. Christians do sometimes deserve persecution, as Jesus implied (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:10-12">Matthew 5:10-12</span>).</p>
<p><b>When ye do well </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαθοποιουντες</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαθοποιεω</span></span> as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:15">1 Peter 2:15</span>.</p>
<p><b>And suffer for it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα πασχοντες</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πασχω</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:19">1 Peter 2:19</span>). No "for it" in the Greek here or in the previous sentence.</p>
<p><b>This is acceptable with God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτο χαρις παρα θεω</span></span>). "This thing (neuter) is thanks (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:19">1 Peter 2:19</span>) by the side of (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span>) God (as God looks at it)." </p>
