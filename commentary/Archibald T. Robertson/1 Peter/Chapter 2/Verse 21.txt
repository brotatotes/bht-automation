<p><b> For hereunto were ye called </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τουτο γαρ εκληθητε</span></span>). First aorist indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλεω</span></span>, to call. They were called to suffer without flinching (Hort), if need be.</p>
<p><b>Because </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span>). The fact that Christ suffered (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαθεν</span></span>) lifts their suffering to a new plane.</p>
<p><b>Leaving you an example </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμιν υπολιμπανων υπογραμμον</span></span>). Present active participle of the late Ionic verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπολιμπανω</span></span> (in the papyri) for the common <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπολειπω</span></span>, to leave behind (under), here only in N.T. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hυπογραμμος</span></span> is also a late and rare word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπογραφω</span></span>, to write under), a writing-copy for one to imitate, in II Macc. 2:28; Philo, Clement of Rome, here only in N.T. Clement of Alex. (<i>Strom</i>. V. 8. 49) uses it of the copy-head at the top of a child's exercise book for the child to imitate, including all the letters of the alphabet. The papyri give many examples of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπογραφη</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπογραφω</span></span> in the sense of copying a letter.</p>
<p><b>That ye should follow his steps </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα επακολουθησητε τοις ιχνεσιν αυτου</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επακολουθεω</span></span>, old verb, to follow closely upon, with the associative-instrumental (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:10">1 Timothy 5:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:24">1 Timothy 5:24</span>) or the locative here. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιχνος</span></span> is old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ικω</span></span>, to go), tracks, footprints, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:18">2 Corinthians 12:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:12">Romans 4:12</span>. Peter does not mean that Christ suffered only as an example (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:18">1 Peter 1:18</span>), but he did leave us his example for our copying (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:6">1 John 2:6</span>). </p>
