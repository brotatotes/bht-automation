<p><b> Who did no sin </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος αμαρτιαν ουκ εποιησεν</span></span>). Quotation from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+53:9">Isaiah 53:9</span>. He has already expressed the sinlessness of Christ in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:19">1 Peter 1:19</span>. The next clause is a combination of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+53:9">Isaiah 53:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="zep+3:13">Zephaniah 3:13</span>. For "guile" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δολος</span></span>) see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:1">1 Peter 2:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Was found </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρεθη</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρισκω</span></span>. Christ's guilelessness stood the test of scrutiny (Vincent), as Peter knew (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:60">Matthew 26:60</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:38">John 18:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:4">John 19:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:6">John 19:6</span>). </p>
