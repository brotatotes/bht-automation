<p><b> Who his own self </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος αυτος</span></span>). Intensive pronoun with the relative referring to Christ (note relatives also in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:22">1 Peter 2:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:23">1 Peter 2:23</span>).</p>
<p><b>Bare our sins </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανηνεγκεν τας αμαρτιας ημων</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναφερω</span></span>, common verb of bringing sacrifice to the altar. Combination here of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+53:12">Isaiah 53:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+21:23">Deuteronomy 21:23</span>. Jesus is the perfect sin offering (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:28">Hebrews 9:28</span>). For Christ's body (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωμα</span></span>) as the offering see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:24">1 Corinthians 11:24</span>. "Here St. Peter puts the Cross in the place of the altar" (Bigg).</p>
<p><b>Upon the tree </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ το ξυλον</span></span>). Not tree here as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:31">Luke 23:31</span>, originally just wood (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:12">1 Corinthians 3:12</span>), then something made of wood, as a gibbet or cross. So used by Peter for the Cross in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:30">Acts 5:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:39">Acts 10:39</span>; and by Paul in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:13">Galatians 3:13</span> (quoting <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+21:23">Deuteronomy 21:23</span>).</p>
<p><b>Having died unto sins </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταις αμαρτιαις απογενομενο</span></span>). Second aorist middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απογινομα</span></span>, old compound to get away from, with dative (as here) to die to anything, here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>That we might live unto righteousness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα τη δικαιοσυνη ζησωμεν</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζαω</span></span> with the dative (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:20">Romans 6:20</span>). Peter's idea here is like that of Paul in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:1-23">Romans 6:1-23</span>, especially verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:2">1 Peter 2:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:10">1 Peter 2:10</span>).</p>
<p><b>By whose stripes ye were healed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου τω μωλωπ ιαθητε</span></span>). From <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+53:5">Isaiah 53:5</span>. First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιαομα</span></span>, common verb to heal (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:16">James 5:16</span>) and the instrumental case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μωλωπς</span></span>, rare word (Aristotle, Plutarch) for bruise or bloody wound, here only in N.T. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:18">1 Peter 1:18</span>. Writing to slaves who may have received such stripes, Peter's word is effective. </p>
