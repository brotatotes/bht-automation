<p><b> If ye have tasted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε εγευσασθε</span></span>). Condition of first class with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span> and first aorist middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γευω</span></span> in figurative sense as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:4">Hebrews 6:4</span>. "A taste excites the appetite" (Bengel).</p>
<p><b>Gracious </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρηστος</span></span>). Quotation from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+34:8">Psalms 34:8</span>. The Hebrew for the LXX <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρηστος</span></span> is simply <i>tobh</i> (good). Plato used the word for food also, and Peter carries out the metaphor in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γαλα</span></span> (milk) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:39">Luke 5:39</span>. </p>
