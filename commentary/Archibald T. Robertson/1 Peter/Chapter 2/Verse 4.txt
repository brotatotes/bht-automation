<p><b> Unto whom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς ον</span></span>). The Lord, carrying on the imagery and language of the Psalm.</p>
<p><b>Coming </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσερχομενο</span></span>). Present middle participle masculine plural of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσερχομα</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσελθατε</span></span> in the Psalm) agreeing with the subject of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικοδομεισθε</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>A living stone </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λιθον ζωντα</span></span>). Accusative case in apposition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ον</span></span> (whom, the Lord Christ). There is apparent an intentional contradiction between "living" and "stone." Cf. "living hope" in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:3">1 Peter 1:3</span> and "living word" in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:23">1 Peter 1:23</span>.</p>
<p><b>Rejected indeed of men </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο ανθρωπων μεν αποδεδοκιμασμενον</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποδοκιμαζω</span></span>, old verb to repudiate after test (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:22">Luke 9:22</span>), in the accusative case agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λιθον</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>But with God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα δε θεω</span></span>). "By the side of God," as he looks at it, in contrast with the rejection "by men" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο ανθρωπων</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Elect </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκλεκτον</span></span>). From <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+28:6">Isaiah 28:6</span> as in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντιμον</span></span> (precious, for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:2">Luke 7:2</span>) rather than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκιμον</span></span> (proved) expected after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποδεδοκιμασμενον</span></span> as meaning far more in God's sight, "a pre-eminence of position with" (Hort). </p>
