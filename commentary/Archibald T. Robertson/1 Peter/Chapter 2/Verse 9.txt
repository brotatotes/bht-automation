<p><b> But ye </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμεις δε</span></span>). In contrast with the disobedient ones.</p>
<p><b>An elect race </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενος εκλεκτον</span></span>). From <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+43:20">Isaiah 43:20</span>. The blood relation of the spiritual Israel (not the Jewish race) through the new birth (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:23">1 Peter 1:23</span>).</p>
<p><b>A royal priesthood </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασιλειον ιερατευμα</span></span>). From <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+19:6">Exodus 19:6</span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:6">Revelation 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:10">Revelation 5:10</span>). The official in Christian churches is <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρεσβυτεροσ=επισχοπος</span></span>, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιερευς</span></span>. We are all <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιερεις</span></span> (priests). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:5">1 Peter 2:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>A holy nation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εθνος αγιον</span></span>). Also from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+19:6">Exodus 19:6</span>, but here applied, not to the national Israel, but to the spiritual Israel of believers (both Jews and Gentiles).</p>
<p><b>A people for God's own possession </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαος εις περιποιησιν</span></span>). The idea here occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+19:5">Exodus 19:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+7:6">Deuteronomy 7:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+14:2">Deuteronomy 14:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+26:18">Deuteronomy 26:18</span>, where we have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαος περιουσιος</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:14">Titus 2:14</span> (alone in the N.T.), and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mal+3:17">Malachi 3:17</span> we find <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις περιποιησιν</span></span> (for a possession). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Περιουσιος λαος</span></span> is a people over and above the others and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιποιησις</span></span> is a possession in a special sense (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:14">Ephesians 1:14</span>). See Paul's use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιεποιησατο</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:28">Acts 20:28</span>. The old rendering, "a peculiar people," had this idea of possession, for "peculiar" is from <i>pecus</i> (Latin for flock).</p>
<p><b>That ye may shew forth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπως εξαγγειλητε</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπως</span></span>, rather than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>, with the first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαγγελλω</span></span>, old verb, to tell out, here alone in N.T.</p>
<p><b>The excellencies </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τας αρετας</span></span>). From <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+43:21">Isaiah 43:21</span>. Old word for any preeminence (moral, intellectual, military), often for "virtue," but not in that sense in the O.T. or the N.T. The word has the sense of moral worth in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:3">2 Peter 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:5">2 Peter 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:8">Philippians 4:8</span>; and the Apocrypha. In Isaiah (here quoted) it means praise and glory to God. So also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+42:12">Isaiah 42:12</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:11">Acts 2:11</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα μεγαλεια του θεου</span></span> (the mighty works of God).</p>
<p><b>Darkness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκοτους</span></span>). Heathenism.</p>
<p><b>His marvellous light </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το θαυμαστον αυτου φως</span></span>). Christianity. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θαυμαστον</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θαυμαζω</span></span>) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:42">Matthew 21:42</span>. For the change from heathenism to Christianity see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:12">Colossians 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:8-14">Ephesians 5:8-14</span>. </p>
