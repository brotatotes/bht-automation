<p><b> For </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γαρ</span></span>). Reason for the entire exhortation in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:8">1 Peter 3:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:9">1 Peter 3:9</span> and introducing in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:10-12">1 Peter 3:10-12</span> a quotation from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+34:13-17">Psalms 34:13-17</span> with some slight changes.</p>
<p><b>Would love life </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θελων ζωην αγαπαιν</span></span>). "Wishing to love life." This present life. The LXX expressions are obscure Hebraisms. The LXX has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπων</span></span> (participle present active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπαω</span></span>, not the infinitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπαιν</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Let him refrain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παυσατω</span></span>). Third person singular first aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παυω</span></span> to make stop, whereas the LXX has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παυσον</span></span> (second person singular).</p>
<p><b>His tongue </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την γλωσσαν</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:1-12">James 3:1-12</span>.</p>
<p><b>That they speak no guile </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του μη λαλησα δολον</span></span>). Purpose clause with genitive article <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του</span></span> (negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span>) and the first aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαλεω</span></span>. But it can also be explained as the ablative case with the redundant negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> after a verb of hindering (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παυσατω</span></span>) like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:42">Luke 4:42</span>. See Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 1061. "Let him refrain his lips from speaking guile." </p>
