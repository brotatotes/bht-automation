<p><b> Let him turn away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκκλινατω</span></span>). First aorist active imperative third person singular of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκκλινω</span></span>, where the LXX has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκκλινον</span></span> (second person singular). Old verb, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:12">Romans 3:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:17">Romans 16:17</span>. Peter adapted the passage all through to his own construction and use. So as to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιησατω</span></span> (let him do) for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιησον</span></span> (do thou), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζητησατω</span></span> (let him seek) for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζητησον</span></span> (do thou seek), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διωξατω</span></span> (let him pursue) for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διωξον</span></span> (do thou pursue), all first aorist active imperatives (of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω, ζητεω, διωκω</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:14">Hebrews 12:14</span> for "pursuing peace." If men only did! </p>
