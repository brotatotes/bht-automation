<p><b> Upon </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>). In the case of righteous (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιους</span></span>, in the O.T. sense like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιον Λοτ</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:7">2 Peter 2:7</span>) for their good, but in the case of men "that do evil" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ ποιουντας κακα</span></span>, "upon men doing evil things") "the face of the Lord" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσωπον κυριου</span></span>) is not for their good, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> here approaching "against" in idea. </p>
