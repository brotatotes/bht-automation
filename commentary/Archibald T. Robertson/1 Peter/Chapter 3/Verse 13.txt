<p><b> That will harm you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο κακωσων υμας</span></span>). Future active articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακοω</span></span>, old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακος</span></span>, bad) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:6">Acts 7:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:19">Acts 7:19</span>. Any real hurt, either that wishes to harm you or that can harm. See the words in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+50:9">Isaiah 50:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>If ye be </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν γενησθε</span></span>). Rather, "if ye become" (condition of third class with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and second aorist middle subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Zealous of that which is good </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του αγαθου ζηλωτα</span></span>). "Zealots for the good" (objective genitive after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζηλωτα</span></span> (zealots, not zealous), old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζηλοω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:12">1 Corinthians 12:12</span>). </p>
