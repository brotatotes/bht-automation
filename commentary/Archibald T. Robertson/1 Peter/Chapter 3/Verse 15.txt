<p><b> Sanctify </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγιασατε</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγιαζω</span></span>. This instead of being afraid.</p>
<p><b>Christ as Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυριον τον Χριστον</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Τον Χριστον</span></span>, direct object with article and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυριον</span></span> predicate accusative (without article). This is the correct text, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον θεον</span></span> of the Textus Receptus. An adaptation to Christ of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+8:13">Isaiah 8:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Being ready always </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετοιμο αε</span></span>). No participle in the Greek, old adjective (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:1">Titus 3:1</span>).</p>
<p><b>To give answer </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς απολογιαν</span></span>). "For an apology," the old sense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολογια</span></span>, an answer back, a defence (not excuse), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:1">Acts 22:1</span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολογεομα</span></span> to defend (not to apologize).</p>
<p><b>A reason concerning the hope that is in you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογον περ της εν υμιν ελπιδος</span></span>). Original sense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογον</span></span> (accusative of the thing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιτουντ</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμας</span></span>, accusative of the person) "concerning the in you hope." Ready with a spoken defence of the inward hope. This attitude calls for an intelligent grasp of the hope and skill in presenting it. In Athens every citizen was expected to be able to join in the discussion of state affairs.</p>
<p><b>Yet with meekness and fear </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα μετα πραυτητος κα φοβου</span></span>). Of God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:18">1 Peter 2:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:2">1 Peter 3:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:4">1 Peter 3:4</span>), not of man. </p>
