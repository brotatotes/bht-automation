<p><b> Having a good conscience </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνειδησιν εχοντες αγαθην</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:18">1 Peter 2:18</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνειδησιν</span></span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:21">1 Peter 3:21</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνειδησις αγαθη</span></span> again ("a quasi-personification," Hart).</p>
<p><b>That they may be put to shame </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα καταισχυνθωσιν</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταισχυνω</span></span>, old verb, to put to shame (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:17">Luke 13:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:6">1 Peter 2:6</span>).</p>
<p><b>Wherein ye are spoken against </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ω καταλαλεισθε</span></span>). Present passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλαλεω</span></span>, for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:12">1 Peter 2:12</span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ω</span></span> also. Peter may be recalling (Hart) his own experience at Pentecost when the Jews first scoffed and others were cut to the heart (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:13">Acts 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:37">Acts 2:37</span>).</p>
<p><b>Who revile </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο επηρεαζοντες</span></span>). Articular present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επηρεαζω</span></span>, old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επηρεια</span></span>, spiteful abuse), to insult, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:28">Luke 6:28</span>.</p>
<p><b>In Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν Χριστω</span></span>). Paul's common mystical phrase that Peter has three times (here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:10">1 Peter 5:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:14">1 Peter 5:14</span>), not in John, though the idea is constantly in John. Peter here gives a new turn (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:12">1 Peter 2:12</span>) to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναστροφη</span></span> (manner of life). "Constantly the apostle repeats his phrases with new significance and in a new light" (Bigg). </p>
