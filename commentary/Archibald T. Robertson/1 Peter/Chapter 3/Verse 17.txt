<p><b> Better </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρειττον</span></span>). Comparative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρατυς</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:21">2 Peter 2:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:4">Hebrews 1:4</span>. Patient endurance not only silences calumny (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:16">1 Peter 3:16</span>), is Christlike (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:18">1 Peter 3:18</span>), but it has a value of its own (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:17">1 Peter 3:17</span>).</p>
<p><b>If the will of God should so will </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε θελο το θελημα του θεου</span></span>). Condition of the fourth class again (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ει--θελο</span></span>) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span> and the optative. For a like pleonasm see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:17">John 7:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>For well-doing than for evil-doing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαθοποιουντας η κακοποιουντας</span></span>). Accusative plural agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμας</span></span> understood (accusative of general reference with the infinitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πασχειν</span></span> (to suffer) of the participles from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαθοποιεω</span></span> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:15">1 Peter 2:15</span>) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακοποιεω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:4">Mark 3:4</span>, and see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:14">1 Peter 2:14</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακοποιος</span></span>). </p>
