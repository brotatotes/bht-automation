<p><b> But the hidden man of the heart </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ' ο κρυπτος της καρδιας ανθρωπος</span></span>). Here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωπος</span></span> is in contrast with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοσμος</span></span> just before. See Paul's use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωπος</span></span> for the outer and old, the inner and new man (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:16">2 Corinthians 4:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:22">Romans 7:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:9">Colossians 3:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:16">Ephesians 3:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:22">Ephesians 4:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:24">Ephesians 4:24</span>). See also the Jew <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν κρυπτω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:29">Romans 2:29</span>) and what Jesus said about God seeing "in secret" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:4">Matthew 6:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:6">Matthew 6:6</span>).</p>
<p><b>In the incorruptible apparel of a meek and quiet spirit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω αφθαρτω του ησυχιου κα πραεως πνευματος</span></span>). No word in the Greek for "apparel" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοσμω</span></span>). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφθαρτος</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:4">1 Peter 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:23">1 Peter 1:23</span>. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πραυς</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:5">Matthew 5:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:29">Matthew 11:29</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πνευμα</span></span> (spirit) is here disposition or temper (Bigg), unlike any other use in the N.T. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:18">1 Peter 3:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:19">1 Peter 3:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:6">1 Peter 4:6</span> it means the whole inner man as opposed to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαρξ</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωμα</span></span>, very much as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψυχη</span></span> is used as opposed to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωμα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Which </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span>). Spirit just mentioned.</p>
<p><b>Of great price </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολυτελες</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολυ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελος</span></span>, cost), in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:3">Mark 14:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:9">1 Timothy 2:9</span>. </p>
