<p><b> For as much then as Christ suffered in the flesh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χριστου ουν παθοντος σαρκ</span></span>). Genitive absolute with second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πασχω</span></span>, to suffer, and the locative case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαρξ</span></span> (flesh). The <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουν</span></span> (then, therefore) draws and applies the main lesson of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:18-22">1 Peter 3:18-22</span>, the fact that Christ suffered for us.</p>
<p><b>Arm ye yourselves also </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα υμεις οπλισασθε</span></span>). Direct middle first aorist imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπλιζω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπλον</span></span> (weapon, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:3">John 18:3</span>), in metaphorical sense, here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>With the same mind </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την αυτην εννοιαν</span></span>). Accusative of the thing (content), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εννοιαν</span></span>, old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν, νους</span></span>), putting in mind, thinking, will, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:12">Hebrews 4:12</span>. "Here again <i>Christus Patiens</i> is our <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπογραμμος</span></span>" (Bigg).</p>
<p><b>For </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span>). Reason for the exhortation.</p>
<p><b>Hath ceased from sin </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπαυτα αμαρτιας</span></span>). Perfect middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παυω</span></span> to make cease and the ablative singular <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτιας</span></span>, but B reads the dative plural <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτιαις</span></span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:1">Romans 6:1</span>). Temptation has lost its appeal and power with such a man. </p>
