<p><b> Gift </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρισμα</span></span>). Late N.T. word (in late papyri) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριζομα</span></span>, to give graciously. It is used here by Peter as one of the gifts of the Holy Spirit (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:4">1 Corinthians 12:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:9">1 Corinthians 12:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:29-31">1 Corinthians 12:29-31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:6">Romans 12:6</span>).</p>
<p><b>Ministering </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονουντες</span></span>). Present active participle plural of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονεω</span></span>, common verb (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:28">Matthew 20:28</span>), though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκαστος</span></span> (each) is singular.</p>
<p><b>As good stewards </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως καλο οικονομο</span></span>). For "steward" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικονομος</span></span>, house-manager) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:1">Luke 16:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:1">1 Corinthians 4:1</span> (used by Paul of himself) and of any bishop (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:7">Titus 1:7</span>), but here of any Christian. See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλος</span></span> used with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονος</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:6">1 Timothy 4:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Of the manifold grace of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποικιλης χαριτος θεου</span></span>). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποικιλος</span></span> (many-colored) see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:6">1 Peter 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:2">James 1:2</span>. </p>
