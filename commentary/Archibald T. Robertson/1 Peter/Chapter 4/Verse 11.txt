<p><b> If any man speaketh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε τις λαλε</span></span>). Condition of first class, assumed as a fact.</p>
<p><b>Speaking as it were oracles of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως λογια θεου</span></span>). No predicate in this conclusion of the condition. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογια θεου</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:38">Acts 7:38</span> (Mosaic law); <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:2">Romans 3:2</span> (the Old Testament); <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:12">Hebrews 5:12</span> (the substance of Christian teaching), here of the utterances of God through Christian teachers. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Λογιον</span></span> (old word) is a diminutive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογος</span></span> (speech, word). It can be construed here as nominative or as accusative. The verb has to be supplied.</p>
<p><b>If any one ministereth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε τις διακονε</span></span>). First-class condition again. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:2-4">Acts 6:2-4</span> for the twofold division of service involved here.</p>
<p><b>Which God supplieth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ης χορηγε ο θεος</span></span>). Ablative case (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ης</span></span>) of the relative attracted from the accusative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην</span></span>, object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χορηγε</span></span> (present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χορηγεω</span></span>, old verb, to supply from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χορηγος</span></span>, chorus leader, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:10">2 Corinthians 9:10</span>). Peter has the compound <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιχορηγεω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:5">2 Peter 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:11">2 Peter 1:11</span>. God is the supplier of strength.</p>
<p><b>That God may be glorified </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα δοξαζητα ο θεος</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the present passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξαζω</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:8">John 15:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>Whose is </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ω εστιν</span></span>). "To whom (dative) is," that is to Jesus Christ the immediate antecedent, but in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:27">Romans 16:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jdg+1:25">Judges 1:25</span> the doxology is to God through Christ. For other doxologies see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:11">1 Peter 5:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:18">2 Peter 3:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:5">Galatians 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:5">Romans 9:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:36">Romans 11:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:20">Philippians 4:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:21">Ephesians 3:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:17">1 Timothy 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:16">1 Timothy 6:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:18">2 Timothy 4:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:21">Hebrews 13:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:6">Revelation 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:13">Revelation 5:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:12">Revelation 7:12</span>. The others addressed to Christ are <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:18">2 Peter 3:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:18">2 Timothy 4:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:6">Revelation 1:6</span>. </p>
