<p><b> Think it not strange </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη ξενιζεσθε</span></span>). Prohibition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> and the present passive imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξενιζω</span></span>, for which verb see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:4">1 Peter 4:4</span>. "Be not amazed."</p>
<p><b>Concerning the fiery trial among you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τε εν υμιν πυρωσε</span></span>). Instrumental case, "by the among you burning," metaphorical sense of old word (since Aristotle), from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυροω</span></span>, to burn (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυρ</span></span> fire). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:7">1 Peter 1:7</span> for the metaphor. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:9">Revelation 18:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:18">Revelation 18:18</span> only other N.T. examples. It occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="pr+27:21">Proverbs 27:21</span> for the smelting of gold and silver and so in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+56:10">Psalms 56:10</span> (LXX 65:10): "Thou didst smelt us as silver is smelted" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επυρωσας ημας ως πυρουτα το αργυριον</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Which cometh upon you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμιν γινομενη</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span> (already coming) with dative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμιν</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>To prove you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς πειρασμον</span></span>). "For testing."</p>
<p><b>As though a strange thing happened unto you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως ξενου υμιν συμβαινοντος</span></span>). Genitive absolute with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως</span></span>, giving the alleged reason, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμιν</span></span>, dative case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμβαινοντος</span></span> (present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμβαινω</span></span>, to go together, to happen (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:32">Mark 10:32</span>), agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξενου</span></span> (strange, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:9">Hebrews 13:9</span>). </p>
