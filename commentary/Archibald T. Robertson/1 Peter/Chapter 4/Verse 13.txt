<p><b> Inasmuch </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθο</span></span>). "In so far forth as" ("according to which thing"), old conjunction, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:12">2 Corinthians 8:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:26">Romans 8:26</span>.</p>
<p><b>Ye are partakers of </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινωνειτε</span></span>). Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινωνεω</span></span>, old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινωνος</span></span>, partner), to share in either with genitive (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:14">Hebrews 2:14</span>) or dative as here (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παθημασιν</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>That ye may rejoice with exceeding joy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα χαρητε αγαλλιωμενο</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and second aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαιρω</span></span>, with the present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαλλιαω</span></span> to exult (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:8">1 Peter 1:8</span>), "that ye may rejoice exulting." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:6-8">1 Peter 1:6-8</span> for this same idea associated with the second coming of Christ as here. </p>
