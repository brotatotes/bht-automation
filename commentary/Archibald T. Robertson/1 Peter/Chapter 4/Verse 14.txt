<p><b> If ye are reproached </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε ονειδιζεσθε</span></span>). Condition of first class assumed as true with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span> and present passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ονειδιζω</span></span>, for which verb see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:5">James 1:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>For the name of Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ονοματ Χριστου</span></span>). "In the matter of the name of Christ." For the idea see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:11">Matthew 5:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:29">Matthew 19:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:41">Acts 5:41</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:16">Acts 9:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:13">Acts 21:13</span>. This is the only N.T. example of just <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ονομα Χριστου</span></span>, here used because of the use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χριστιανος</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:16">1 Peter 4:16</span>. For the beatitude <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακαριο</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:11">Matthew 5:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>The Spirit of glory and the Spirit of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το της δοξης κα το του θεου πνευμα</span></span>). Note repetition of the article (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το</span></span>) though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα</span></span> only once. The reference is to the Holy Spirit, who is the Spirit of Glory and of God.</p>
<p><b>Resteth upon you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφ' ημας αναπαυετα</span></span>). Quotation from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+11:2">Isaiah 11:2</span>. Present middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπαυω</span></span>, to give rest, refresh (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:28">Matthew 11:28</span>). "He rests upon the Christian as the Shechinah rested upon the tabernacle" (Bigg). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:8">1 Peter 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:16">Matthew 3:16</span>. </p>
