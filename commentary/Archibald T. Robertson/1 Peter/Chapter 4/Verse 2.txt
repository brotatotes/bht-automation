<p><b> That ye no longer should live </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το μηκετ βιωσα</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το</span></span> (negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span>) and the first aorist (for the Attic second aorist <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βιωνα</span></span>) active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βιοω</span></span>, old verb, to spend a life (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βιος</span></span>, course of life, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:14">Luke 8:14</span>), here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>The rest of your time in the flesh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον επιλοιπον εν σαρκ χρονον</span></span>). Accusative of time (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρονον</span></span>, period of time). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Επιλοιπον</span></span> is old adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επι, λοιπος</span></span>, remaining in addition), here only in N.T. But <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το</span></span> here can be result (so that) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:20">Romans 1:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:18">Romans 4:18</span>. </p>
