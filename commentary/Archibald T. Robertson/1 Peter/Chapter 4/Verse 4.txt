<p><b> Wherein </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ω</span></span>). "In which thing" (manner of life).</p>
<p><b>They think it strange </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξενιζοντα</span></span>). Present passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξενιζω</span></span>, old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξενος</span></span>, stranger), to entertain a guest (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:23">Acts 10:23</span>), to astonish (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:20">Acts 17:20</span>). See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:12">1 Peter 4:12</span>. "They are surprised or astonished."</p>
<p><b>That ye run not with them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη συντρεχοντων υμων</span></span>). Genitive absolute (negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span>) with present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συντρεχω</span></span>, old compound, to run together like a crowd or a mob as here (just like our phrase, "running with certain folks").</p>
<p><b>Into the same excess of riot </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις την αυτην της ασωτιας αναχυσιν</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αναχυσιν</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναχεω</span></span> to pour forth) is a late and rare word, our overflowing, here only in N.T. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ασωτιας</span></span> is the character of an abandoned man (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασωτος</span></span>, cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασωτως</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:13">Luke 15:13</span>), old word for a dissolute life, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:18">Ephesians 5:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:6">Titus 1:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Speaking evil of you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλασφημουντες</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλασφημεω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:65">Luke 22:65</span>. "The Christians were compelled to stand aloof from all the social pleasures of the world, and the Gentiles bitterly resented their puritanism, regarding them as the enemies of all joy, and therefore of the human race" (Bigg). </p>
