<p><b> Who shall give account </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αποδωσουσιν λογον</span></span>). Future active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποδιδωμ</span></span>. For this use with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογον</span></span> (account) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:36">Matthew 12:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:2">Luke 16:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:40">Acts 19:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:17">Hebrews 13:17</span>. For the sudden use of the relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:8">Romans 3:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>To him that is ready to judge </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω ετοιμως κρινοντ</span></span>). Dative, "to the one readily judging," correct text, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετοιμως εχοντ κρινα</span></span>, "to the one ready to judge," which "softens the rugged original" (Hart). That is Christ apparently (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:13">1 Peter 1:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:10">2 Corinthians 5:10</span>), but the Father in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:17">1 Peter 1:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>The quick and the dead </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωντας κα νεκρους</span></span>). "Living and dead." Those living at the time and those already dead (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:15">1 Thessalonians 4:15</span>). </p>
