<p><b> But the end of all things is at hand </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντων δε το τελος ηγγικεν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγγιζω</span></span>, to draw near, common late verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγγυς</span></span>), same form used by the Baptist of the Messiah's arrival (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:2">Matthew 3:2</span>) and by James in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:8">1 Peter 5:8</span> (of the second coming). How near Peter does not say, but he urges readiness (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:5">1 Peter 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:6">1 Peter 4:6</span>) as Jesus did (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:38">Mark 14:38</span>) and Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:6">1 Thessalonians 5:6</span>), though it is drawing nearer all the time (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:11">Romans 12:11</span>), but not at once (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:2">2 Thessalonians 2:2</span>).</p>
<p><b>Be ye therefore of sound mind </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωφρονησατε ουν</span></span>). In view of the coming of Christ. First aorist (ingressive) active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωφρονεω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σως</span></span>, sound, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φρην</span></span>, mind) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:15">Mark 5:15</span>.</p>
<p><b>Be sober unto prayer </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νηψατε εις προσευχας</span></span>). First aorist (ingressive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νηφω</span></span> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:13">1 Peter 1:13</span>) and plural <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσευχας</span></span>, (prayers). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:18">Ephesians 6:18</span>. </p>
