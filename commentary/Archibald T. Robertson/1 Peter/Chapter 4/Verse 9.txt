<p><b> Using hospitality </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλοξενο</span></span>). "Friendly to strangers," old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλοσ, ξενος</span></span>), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:2">1 Timothy 3:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:8">Titus 1:8</span>. No verb here in the Greek.</p>
<p><b>Without murmuring </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανευ γογγυσμου</span></span>). Like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χωρις γογγυσμων</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:14">Philippians 2:14</span>. Complaint spoils hospitality. Jesus enjoined the entertainment of strangers (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:35">Matthew 25:35</span>). Inns were rare and very poor. Hospitality made mission work possible (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:5">3 John 1:5</span>). </p>
