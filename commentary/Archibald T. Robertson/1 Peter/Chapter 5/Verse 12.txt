<p><b> By Silvanus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια Σιλουανου</span></span>). Probably this postscript (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:12-14">1 Peter 5:12-14</span>) is in Peter's own handwriting, as Paul did (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:17">2 Thessalonians 3:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:11-18">Galatians 6:11-18</span>). If so, Silvanus (Silas) was the amanuensis and the bearer of the Epistle.</p>
<p><b>As I account him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως λογιζομα</span></span>). Peter uses Paul's phrase (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:1">1 Corinthians 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:18">Romans 8:18</span>) in giving approval to Paul's former companion (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:40">Acts 15:40</span>).</p>
<p><b>I have written </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγραψα</span></span>). Epistolary aorist applying to this Epistle as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:11">1 Corinthians 5:11</span> (not <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:9">1 Corinthians 5:9</span>); <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:15">1 Corinthians 9:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:11">Galatians 6:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:15">Romans 15:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:19">Philemon 1:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:21">Philemon 1:21</span>.</p>
<p><b>Briefly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι' ολιγων</span></span>). "By few words," as Peter looked at it, certainly not a long letter in fact. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:22">Hebrews 13:22</span>.</p>
<p><b>Testifying </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιμαρτυρων</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιμαρτυρεω</span></span>, to bear witness to, old compound, here alone in N.T., though the double compound <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεπιμαρτυρεω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:4">Hebrews 2:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>That this is the true grace of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταυτην εινα αληθη χαριν του θεου</span></span>). Infinitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εινα</span></span> in indirect assertion and accusative of general reference (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταυτην</span></span>) and predicate accusative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριν</span></span>. Peter includes the whole of the Epistle by God's grace (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:10">1 Peter 1:10</span>) and obedience to the truth (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:17">John 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:5">Galatians 2:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:6">Colossians 1:6</span>).</p>
<p><b>Stand ye fast therein </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις ην στητε</span></span>). "In which (grace) take your stand" (ingressive aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span>). </p>
