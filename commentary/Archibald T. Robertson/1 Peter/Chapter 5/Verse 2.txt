<p><b> Tend </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιμανατε</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιμαινω</span></span>, old verb, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιμην</span></span> (shepherd) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:7">Luke 17:7</span>. Jesus used this very word to Peter in the interview by the Sea of Galilee (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:16">John 21:16</span>) and Peter doubtless has this fact in mind here. Paul used the word to the elders at Miletus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:28">Acts 20:28</span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:25">1 Peter 2:25</span> for the metaphor.</p>
<p><b>Flock </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιμνιον</span></span>). Old word, likewise from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιμην</span></span>, contraction of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιμενιον</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:32">Luke 12:32</span>).</p>
<p><b>Exercising the oversight </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισκοπουντες</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισκοπεω</span></span>, old word (in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:15">Hebrews 12:15</span> alone in N.T.), omitted here by Aleph B.</p>
<p><b>Not by constraint </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη αναγκαστως</span></span>). Negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> because of the imperative. Old adverb from verbal adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγκαστος</span></span>, here alone in N.T.</p>
<p><b>But willingly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα εκουσιως</span></span>). By contrast. Old adverb, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:26">Hebrews 10:26</span>.</p>
<p><b>Nor yet for filthy lucre </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηδε αισχροκερδως</span></span>). A compound adverb not found elsewhere, but the old adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αισχροκερδης</span></span> is in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:8">1 Timothy 3:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:7">Titus 1:7</span>. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:11">Titus 1:11</span> "for the sake of filthy lucre" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αισχρου κερδους χαριν</span></span>). Clearly the elders received stipends, else there could be no such temptation.</p>
<p><b>But of a ready mind </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα προθυμως</span></span>). Old adverb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προθυμος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:41">Matthew 26:41</span>), here only in N.T. </p>
