<p><b> Lording it over </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατακυριευοντες</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατακυριευω</span></span>, late compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα, κυριος</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:25">Matthew 20:25</span>.</p>
<p><b>The charge allotted to you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των κληρων</span></span>). "The charges," "the lots" or "the allotments." See it in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:17">Acts 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:25">Acts 1:25</span> in this sense. The old word meant a die (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:25">Matthew 27:25</span>), a portion (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:12">Colossians 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:4">1 Peter 1:4</span>), here the charges assigned (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:4">Acts 17:4</span>). From the adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κληρικος</span></span> come our cleric, clerical, clerk. Wycliff translated it here "neither as having lordship in the clergie."</p>
<p><b>Making yourselves ensamples </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυπο γινομενο</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span> and predicate nominative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυπο</span></span> (types, models) for which phrase see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:7">1 Thessalonians 1:7</span>. Continually becoming. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:21">1 Peter 2:21</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπογραμμος</span></span> (writing-copy).</p>
<p><b>To the flock </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του ποιμνιου</span></span>). Objective genitive. </p>
