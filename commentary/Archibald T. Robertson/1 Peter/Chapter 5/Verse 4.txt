<p><b> When the chief Shepherd shall be manifested </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανερωθεντος του αρχιποιμενος</span></span>). Genitive absolute with first aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανεροω</span></span>, to manifest, and genitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρχιποιμην</span></span>, a compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρχι, ποιμην</span></span>) after analogy of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρχιερευς</span></span>, here only in N.T., but in <i>Testam. of Twelve Patrs</i>. (Jud. 8) and on a piece of wood around an Egyptian mummy and also on a papyrus A.D. 338 (Deissmann, <i>Light, etc.</i>, p. 100). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:20">Hebrews 13:20</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ποιμην ο μεγας</span></span> (the Shepherd the great).</p>
<p><b>Ye shall receive </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κομιεισθε</span></span>). Future of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κομιζω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:9">1 Peter 1:9</span>, which see).</p>
<p><b>The crown of glory that fadeth not away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον αμαραντινον της δοξης στεφανον</span></span>). For "crown" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στεφανος</span></span>) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:12">James 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:25">1 Corinthians 9:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:8">2 Timothy 4:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:10">Revelation 2:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:10">Revelation 3:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:4">Revelation 4:4</span>. In the Gospels it is used only of the crown of thorns, but Jesus is crowned with glory and honor (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:9">Hebrews 2:9</span>). In all these passages it is the crown of victory as it is here. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:4">1 Peter 1:4</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαραντος</span></span>, unfading. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αμαραντινος</span></span> is made from that word as the name of a flower <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρανθ</span></span> (so called because it never withers and revives if moistened with water and so used as a symbol of immortality), "composed of amaranth" or "amarantine," "the amarantine (unfading) crown of glory." </p>
