<p><b> Be subject </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οποταγητε</span></span>). Second aorist passive imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποτασσω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Unto the elder </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρεσβυτεροις</span></span>). Dative case. Here the antithesis between younger and elder shows that the word refers to age, not to office as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:1">1 Peter 5:1</span>. See a like change in meaning in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:1">1 Timothy 5:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:17">1 Timothy 5:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>All </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντες</span></span>). All ages, sexes, classes.</p>
<p><b>Gird yourselves with humility </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την ταπεινοφροσυνην εγκομβωσασθε</span></span>). First aorist middle imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγκομβοομα</span></span>, late and rare verb (in Apollodorus, fourth cent. B.C.), here only in N.T., from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κομβος</span></span> (knot, like the knot of a girdle). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εγκομβωμα</span></span> was the white scarf or apron of slaves. It is quite probable that Peter here is thinking of what Jesus did (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:4">John 13:4</span>) when he girded himself with a towel and taught the disciples, Peter in particular (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:9">John 13:9</span>), the lesson of humility (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:15">John 13:15</span>). Peter had at last learned the lesson (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:15-19">John 21:15-19</span>).</p>
<p><b>The proud </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερηφανοις</span></span>). Dative plural of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερηφανος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:6">James 4:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:30">Romans 1:30</span>) after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντιτασσετα</span></span> (present middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντιτασσω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:6">James 4:6</span> (quoted there as here from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="pr+3:34">Proverbs 3:34</span>). </p>
