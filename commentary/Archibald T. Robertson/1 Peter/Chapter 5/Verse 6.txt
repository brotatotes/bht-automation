<p><b> Humble yourselves therefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταπεινωθητε ουν</span></span>). First aorist passive imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταπεινοω</span></span>, old verb, for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:4">Matthew 18:4</span>. Peter is here in the role of a preacher of humility. "Be humbled."</p>
<p><b>Under the mighty hand of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο την κραταιαν χειρα του θεου</span></span>). Common O.T. picture (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+3:19">Exodus 3:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+20:33">Exodus 20:33</span>, etc.).</p>
<p><b>That he may exalt you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα υψωση</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υψοω</span></span>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:11">Luke 14:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:9">Philippians 2:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>In due time </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν καιρω</span></span>). Same phrase in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:45">Matthew 24:45</span>. </p>
