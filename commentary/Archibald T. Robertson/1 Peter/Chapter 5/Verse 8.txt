<p><b> Be watchful </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γρηγορησατε</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γρηγορεω</span></span>, late present imperative from perfect <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγρηγορα</span></span> (to be awake) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγειρω</span></span> (to arouse), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:42">Matthew 24:42</span>. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νηψατε</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:13">1 Peter 1:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:7">1 Peter 4:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>Your adversary </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αντιδικος υμων</span></span>). Old word for opponent in a lawsuit (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:25">Matthew 5:25</span>).</p>
<p><b>The devil </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαβολος</span></span>). Slanderer. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:1">Matthew 4:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>As a roaring lion </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως ωρυομενος λεων</span></span>). But Jesus is also pictured as the Lion of the tribe of Judah (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:5">Revelation 5:5</span>). But Satan</p>
<p><b>roars </b> at the saints. Present middle participle <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωρυομα</span></span>, old verb, here only in N.T., to howl like a wolf, dog, or lion, of men to sing loud (Pindar). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+22:13">Psalms 22:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Whom he may devour </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταπιειν</span></span>). Second aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταπινω</span></span>, to drink down. B does not have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τινα</span></span>, Aleph has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τινα</span></span> (somebody), "to devour some one," while A has interrogative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τινα</span></span>, "whom he may devour" (very rare idiom). But the devil's purpose is the ruin of men. He is a "peripatetic" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιπατε</span></span>) like the peripatetic philosophers who walked as they talked. Satan wants all of us and sifts us all (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:31">Luke 22:31</span>). </p>
