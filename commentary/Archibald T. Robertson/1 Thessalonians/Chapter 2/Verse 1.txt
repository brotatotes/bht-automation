<p><b> For yourselves know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτο γαρ οιδατε</span></span>). This explanatory <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γαρ</span></span> takes up in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:1-12">1 Thessalonians 2:1-12</span> the allusion in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:9">1 Thessalonians 1:9</span> about the "report" concerning the entrance (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισοδον</span></span>, way in, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισ, οδον</span></span>),</p>
<p><b>unto you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την προς υμας</span></span>). Note repeated article to sharpen the point. This proleptic accusative is common enough. It is expanded by the epexegetic use of the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span> clause</p>
<p><b>that it hath not been found vain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ ου κενη γεγονεν</span></span>). Literally,</p>
<p><b>that it has not become empty </b>. Second perfect active (completed state) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span>. Every pastor watches wistfully to see what will be the outcome of his work. Bengel says: <i>Non inanis, sed plena virtutis</i>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:5">1 Thessalonians 1:5</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κενος</span></span> is hollow, empty, while <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ματαιος</span></span> is fruitless, ineffective. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:14">1 Corinthians 15:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:17">1 Corinthians 15:17</span> Paul speaks of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κενον το κηρυγμα</span></span> (<b> empty the preaching </b>) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ματαια η πιστις</span></span> (<b> vain the faith </b>). One easily leads to the other. </p>
