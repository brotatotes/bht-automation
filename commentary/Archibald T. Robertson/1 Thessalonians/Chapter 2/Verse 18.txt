<p><b> Because </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διοτ</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:8">1 Thessalonians 2:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>We would fain have come to you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηθελησαμεν ελθειν προς υμας</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θελω</span></span>. Literally,</p>
<p><b>we desired to come to you. I Paul </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγω μεν Παυλος</span></span>). Clear example of literary plural <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηθελεσαμεν</span></span> with singular pronoun <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγω</span></span>. Paul uses his own name elsewhere also as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:1">2 Corinthians 10:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:2">Galatians 5:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:23">Colossians 1:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:1">Ephesians 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:19">Philemon 1:19</span>.</p>
<p><b>Once and again </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα απαξ κα δις</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Both once and twice </b> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:16">Philippians 4:16</span>. Old idiom in Plato.</p>
<p><b>And Satan hindered us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ενεκοψεν ημας ο Σατανας</span></span>). Adversative use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">και=</span></span> but or and yet. First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενκοπτω</span></span>, late word to cut in, to hinder. Milligan quotes papyrus example of third century, B.C. Verb used to cut in a road, to make a road impassable. So Paul charges Satan with cutting in on his path. Used by Paul in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:4">Acts 24:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:7">Galatians 5:7</span> and passive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεκοπτομην</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:22">Romans 15:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:7">1 Peter 3:7</span>. This hindrance may have been illness, opposition of the Jews in Corinth, what not. </p>
