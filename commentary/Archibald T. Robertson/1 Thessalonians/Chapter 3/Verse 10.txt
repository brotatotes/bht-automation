<p><b> Exceedingly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερεκπερισσου</span></span>). Double compound adverb, only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:10">1 Thessalonians 3:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:13">1 Thessalonians 5:13</span> (some MSS. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-ως</span></span>). Like piling Ossa on Pelion, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περισσως</span></span>, abundantly, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ περισσου</span></span>, out of bounds, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερεκπερισσου</span></span>, more than out of bounds (overflowing all bounds).</p>
<p><b>And perfect </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα καταρτισα</span></span>). First aorist active articular infinitive of purpose (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το ιδειν--κα</span></span>) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταρτιζω</span></span>, to mend nets (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:21">Matthew 4:21</span>) or men (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:1">Galatians 6:1</span>) repair. Chiefly late.</p>
<p><b>That which is lacking in </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα υστερηματα</span></span>). The shortcomings, the lacks or left-overs (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:24">Colossians 1:24</span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υστερεω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υστερον</span></span>), to be late. </p>
