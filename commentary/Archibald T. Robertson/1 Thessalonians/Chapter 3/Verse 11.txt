<p><b> Our God and Father himself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτος ο θεος κα πατηρ ημων</span></span>). Note one article with both substantives for one person.</p>
<p><b>And our Lord Jesus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ο Κυριος ημων Ιησους</span></span>). Separate article here with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιησους</span></span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:13">Titus 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:1">2 Peter 1:1</span> only one article (not two) treating "our God and Saviour Jesus Christ" as one just like "our Lord and Saviour Jesus Christ" in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:11">2 Peter 1:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:20">2 Peter 2:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:18">2 Peter 3:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>Direct our way </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατευθυνα την οδον ημων</span></span>). First aorist optative (acute accent on penult, not circumflex first aorist active infinitive) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατευθυνω</span></span>, old verb to make straight path. Singular verb also, though both God and Christ mentioned as subject (unity in the Godhead). Apart from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη γενοιτο</span></span> (<b> may it not come to pass </b>) the optative in a wish of the third person is found in N.T. only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:11">1 Thessalonians 3:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:12">1 Thessalonians 3:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:23">1 Thessalonians 5:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:17">2 Thessalonians 2:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:5">2 Thessalonians 3:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:16">2 Thessalonians 3:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:5">Romans 15:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:13">Romans 15:13</span>. </p>
