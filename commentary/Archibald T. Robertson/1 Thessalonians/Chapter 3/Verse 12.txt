<p><b> The Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο Κυριος</span></span>). The Lord Jesus. Paul prays to Christ.</p>
<p><b>Make you to increase </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμας πλεονασα</span></span>). First aorist active optative (wish for future) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλεοναζω</span></span>, late verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλεον</span></span> (more),</p>
<p><b>to superabound </b>.</p>
<p><b>And abound </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περισσευσα</span></span>). First aorist active optative (wish for future) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περισσευω</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περισσος</span></span>, old verb, to be over (common in N.T.). It is hard to see much difference between the two verbs. </p>
