<p><b> To the end he may stablish </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το στηριξα</span></span>). Another example of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> and the articular infinitive of purpose. Same idiom in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:2">1 Thessalonians 3:2</span>. From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στηριζω</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στηριγξ</span></span>, a support.</p>
<p><b>Unblameable </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμεμπτους</span></span>). Old compound adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and verbal of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεμφομα</span></span>, to blame). Rare in N.T.
