<p><b> Render again unto God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω θεω ανταποδουνα</span></span>). Second aorist active infinitive of double compound verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντ-αποδιδωμ</span></span>, to give back (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span>) in return for (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντ</span></span>). Old verb rare in N.T., but again in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:6">2 Thessalonians 1:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>For you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ υμων</span></span>). Around (concerning) you, while in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:2">1 Thessalonians 3:2</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ</span></span> (over is used for "concerning your faith."</p>
<p><b>For </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>). Basis again as cause or ground for the joy.</p>
<p><b>Wherewith we joy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η χαιρομεν</span></span>). Probably cognate accusative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαιρομεν</span></span> attracted to locative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρα</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:10">Matthew 2:10</span>). </p>
