<p><b> When we could no longer forbear </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηκετ στεγοντες</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Στεγω</span></span> is old verb to cover from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στεγη</span></span>, roof (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:4">Mark 2:4</span>), to cover with silence, to conceal, to keep off, to endure as here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:12">1 Corinthians 9:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:7">1 Corinthians 13:7</span>. In the papyri in this sense (Moulton and Milligan's <i>Vocabulary</i>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μηκετ</span></span> usual negative with participle in the <i>Koine</i> rather than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκετ</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>We thought it good </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηυδοκησαμεν</span></span>). Either literary plural as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:18">1 Thessalonians 2:18</span> or Paul and Silas as more likely. If so, both Timothy and Silas came to Athens (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:15">Acts 17:15</span>), but Timothy was sent (<b> we sent </b>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεμψαμεν</span></span>, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:2">1 Thessalonians 3:2</span>) right back to Thessalonica and later Paul sent Silas on to Beroea or Thessalonica (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:5">1 Thessalonians 3:5</span>,</p>
<p><b>I sent </b>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεμψα</span></span>). Then both Silas and Timothy came from Macedonia to Corinth (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:5">Acts 18:5</span>).</p>
<p><b>Alone </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μονο</span></span>). Including Silas.</p>
<p><b>God's minister </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονον του θεου</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:13">Matthew 22:13</span> for this interesting word, here in general sense not technical sense of deacon. Some MSS. have</p>
<p><b>fellow-worker </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεργον</span></span>). Already</p>
<p><b>apostle </b> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:7">1 Thessalonians 2:7</span> and now</p>
<p><b>brother, minister </b> (and possibly</p>
<p><b>fellow-worker </b>). </p>
