<p><b> Finally </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λοιπον</span></span>). Accusative of general reference of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λοιπος</span></span>, as for the rest. It does not mean actual conclusion, but merely a colloquial expression pointing towards the end (Milligan) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+13:11">2 Corinthians 13:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:8">2 Timothy 4:8</span>. So <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το λοιπον</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:1">2 Thessalonians 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:1">Philippians 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:8">Philippians 4:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>We beseech </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερωτωμεν</span></span>). Not "question" as in ancient Greek, but as often in N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:12">1 Thessalonians 5:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:1">2 Thessalonians 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:3">Philippians 4:3</span>) and also in papyri to make urgent request of one.</p>
<p><b>How ye ought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το πως δε υμας</span></span>). Literally, explanatory articular indirect question (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το πως</span></span>) after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρελαβητε</span></span> according to common classic idiom in Luke (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:62">Luke 1:62</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:2">Luke 22:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:4">Luke 22:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:23">Luke 22:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:24">Luke 22:24</span>) and Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:26">Romans 8:26</span>).</p>
<p><b>That ye abound </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα περισσευητε</span></span>). Loose construction of the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> clause with present subjunctive after two subordinate clauses with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθως</span></span> (as, even as) to be connected with "beseech and exhort."</p>
<p><b>More and more </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαλλον</span></span>). Simply</p>
<p><b>more </b>, but added to same idea in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περισσευητε</span></span>. See also verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:11">1 Thessalonians 4:11</span>. </p>
