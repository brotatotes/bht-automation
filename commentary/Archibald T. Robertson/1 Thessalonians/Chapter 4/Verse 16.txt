<p><b> With a shout </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν κελευσματ</span></span>). Note this so-called instrumental use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span>. Old word, here only in N.T., from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κελευω</span></span>, to order, command (military command). Christ will come as Conqueror.</p>
<p><b>With the voice of the archangel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν φωνη αρχαγγελου</span></span>). Further explanation of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κελευσματ</span></span> (command). The only archangel mentioned in N.T. is Michael in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:9">Jude 1:9</span>. But note absence of article with both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωνη</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρχαγγελου</span></span>. The reference may be thus indefinite.</p>
<p><b>With the trump of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν σαλπιγγ θεου</span></span>). Trumpet. See same figure in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:52">1 Corinthians 15:52</span>.</p>
<p><b>The dead in Christ shall rise first </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο νεκρο εν Χριστω αναστησοντα πρωτον</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>First </b> here refers plainly to the fact that, so far from the dead in Christ having no share in the Parousia, they will rise before those still alive are changed. </p>
