<p><b> Concerning love of the brethren </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ της φιλαδελφιας</span></span>). Late word, love of brothers or sisters. In profane Greek (one papyrus example) and LXX the word means love of those actually kin by blood, but in the N.T. it is the kinship in the love of Christ as here.</p>
<p><b>Are taught by God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεοδιδακτο εστε</span></span>). Only here and ecclesiastical writers. Passive verbal adjective in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-τος</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδασκω</span></span> as if <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεο-</span></span> in ablative case like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδακτο θεου</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:45">John 6:45</span>).</p>
<p><b>To love one another </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το αγαπαιν αλληλους</span></span>). Another example of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το</span></span> and the infinitive. Only those taught of God keep on loving one another, love neighbours and even enemies as Jesus taught (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:44">Matthew 5:44</span>). Note the use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπαω</span></span>, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλεω</span></span>. </p>
