<p><b> For us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ ημων</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Around us </b>. So Westcott and Hort, but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ</span></span> (over, in behalf of) as in many MSS. These prepositions often interchanged in N.T. MSS.</p>
<p><b>Whether we wake or sleep </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειτε γρηγορωμεν ειτε καθευδωμεν</span></span>). Alternative condition of third class with present subjunctive, though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαντε--εαντε</span></span> more usual conjunction (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, P. 1017). Used here of life and death, not as metaphor.</p>
<p><b>That we should live together with him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα αμα συν αυτω ζησωμεν</span></span>). First aorist active subjunctive constative aorist covering all life (now and hereafter) together with (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμα συν</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:17">1 Thessalonians 5:17</span>) Jesus. </p>
