<p><b> Build each other up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικοδομειτε εις τον ενα</span></span>). Literally, build ye, one the one (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> nominative in partitive apposition with unexpressed <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμεις</span></span> subject of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικοδομειτε</span></span>. Then <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον ενα</span></span> the accusative in partitive apposition with the unexpressed <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαυτους</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλληλους</span></span>. See the same idiom in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:6">1 Corinthians 4:6</span> <b> one in behalf of the one </b>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις υπερ του ενος</span></span>. Build is a favourite Pauline metaphor. </p>
