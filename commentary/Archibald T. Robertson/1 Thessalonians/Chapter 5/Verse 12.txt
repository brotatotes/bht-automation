<p><b> Them that labour among you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους κοπιωντας εν υμιν</span></span>). Old word for toil even if weary.</p>
<p><b>And are over you in the Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα προισταμενους υμων εν Κυριω</span></span>). Same article with this participle. Literally, those who stand in front of you, your leaders in the Lord, the presbyters or bishops and deacons. Get acquainted with them and follow them.</p>
<p><b>And admonish you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα νουθετουντας υμας</span></span>). Old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νουθετης</span></span> and this from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νους</span></span> (mind) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span>, to put. Putting sense into the heads of people. A thankless, but a necessary, task. The same article connects all three participles, different functions of the same leaders in the church. </p>
