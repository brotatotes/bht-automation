<p><b> See to it that no one render unto any one evil for evil </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορατε μη τις κακον αντ κακου αποδω</span></span>). Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> with the aorist subjunctive (negative purpose) <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποδω</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποδιδωμ</span></span>, to give back. Retaliation, condemned by Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:38-42">Matthew 5:38-42</span>) and by Paul in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:17">Romans 12:17</span>, usually takes the form of "evil for evil," rather than "good for good" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλον αντ καλου</span></span>). Note idea of exchange in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντ</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Follow after </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διωκετε</span></span>). Keep up the chase (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διωκω</span></span>) after the good. </p>
