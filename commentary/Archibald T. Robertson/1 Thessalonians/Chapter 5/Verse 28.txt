<p><b> The grace </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η χαρις</span></span>). Paul prefers this noble word to the customary <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερρωσθε</span></span> (Farewell, Be strong). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:18">2 Thessalonians 3:18</span> for identical close save added <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντων</span></span> (all). A bit shorter form in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:23">1 Corinthians 16:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:20">Romans 16:20</span> and still shorter in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+4:18">Colossians 4:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:21">1 Timothy 6:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:15">Titus 3:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:22">2 Timothy 4:22</span>. The full Trinitarian benediction we find in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+13:13">2 Corinthians 13:13</span>. </p>
