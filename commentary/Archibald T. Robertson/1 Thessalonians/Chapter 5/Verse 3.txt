<p><b> When they are saying </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν λεγωσιν</span></span>). Present active subjunctive picturing these false prophets of</p>
<p><b>peace and safety </b> like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+13:10">Ezekiel 13:10</span> (Peace, and there is no peace). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ασφαλεια</span></span> only in N.T. in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:4">Luke 1:4</span> (which see); <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:23">Acts 5:23</span> and here.</p>
<p><b>Sudden destruction </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιφνιδιος ολεθρος</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ολεθρος</span></span> old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ολλυμ</span></span>, to destroy. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:9">2 Thessalonians 1:9</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αιφνιδιος</span></span>, old adjective akin to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφνω</span></span> and in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:34">Luke 21:34</span> where Westcott and Hort spell it <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφνιδιος</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Cometh upon them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτοις επιστατα</span></span>). Unaspirated form instead of the usual <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφιστατα</span></span> (present middle indicative) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφιστημ</span></span> perhaps due to confusion with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισταμα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>As travail upon a woman with child </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωσπερ η ωδιν τη εν γαστρ εχουση</span></span>). Earlier form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωδις</span></span> for birth-pang used also by Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:8">Mark 13:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:8">Matthew 24:8</span>). Technical phrase for pregnancy,</p>
<p><b>to the one who has it in belly </b> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+1:18">Matthew 1:18</span> of Mary).</p>
<p><b>They shall in no wise escape </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη εκφυγωσιν</span></span>). Strong negative like that in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:15">1 Thessalonians 4:15</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη</span></span> (double negative) and the second aorist active subjunctive. </p>
