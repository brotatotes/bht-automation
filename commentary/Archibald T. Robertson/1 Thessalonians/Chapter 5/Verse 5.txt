<p><b> Sons of light </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υιο φωτος</span></span>),</p>
<p><b>sons of day </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υιο ημερας</span></span>). Chiefly a translation Hebraism (Deissmann, <i>Bible Studies</i>, pp. 161ff.). Cf. words of Jesus in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:8">Luke 16:8</span> and Paul in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:9">Ephesians 5:9</span>. He repeats the same idea in turning from "ye" to "we" and using <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυκτος</span></span> (night) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκοτους</span></span> (darkness), predicate genitives. </p>
