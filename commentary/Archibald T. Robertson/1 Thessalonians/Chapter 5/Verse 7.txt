<p><b> They that be drunken are drunken in the night </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο μεθυσκομενο νυκτος μεθυουσιν</span></span>). No need of "be" here, they that are drunken. No real difference in meaning between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεθυσκω</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεθυω</span></span>, to be drunk, except that <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεθυσκω</span></span> (inceptive verb in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-σκω</span></span>) means to get drunk.</p>
<p><b>Night </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυκτος</span></span>, genitive by night) is the favourite time for drunken revelries. </p>
