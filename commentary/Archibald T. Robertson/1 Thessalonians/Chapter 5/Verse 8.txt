<p><b> Putting on the breastplate of faith and love </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδυσαμενο θωρακα πιστεως κα αγαπης</span></span>). First aorist (ingressive) middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδυω</span></span>. The same figure of breastplate in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:14">Ephesians 6:14</span>, only there "of righteousness." The idea of watchfulness brings the figure of a sentry on guard and armed to Paul's mind as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:12">Romans 13:12</span> "the weapons of light." The word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θωραξ</span></span> (breastplate) is common in the LXX.</p>
<p><b>For a helmet, the hope of salvation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περικεφαλαιαν ελπιδα σωτηριας</span></span>). Same figure in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:17">Ephesians 6:17</span> and both like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+59:17">Isaiah 59:17</span>. Late word meaning around (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ</span></span>) the head (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεφαλη</span></span>) and in Polybius, LXX, and in the papyri. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Σωτηριας</span></span> is objective genitive. </p>
