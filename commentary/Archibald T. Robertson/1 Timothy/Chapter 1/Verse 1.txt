<p><b> According to the commandment </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατ' επιταγην</span></span>). A late <i>Koine</i> word (Polybius, Diodorus), but a Pauline word also in N.T. This very idiom ("by way of command") in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:6">1 Corinthians 7:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:8">2 Corinthians 8:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:26">Romans 16:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:1">1 Timothy 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:3">Titus 1:3</span>. Paul means to say that he is an apostle under orders.</p>
<p><b>Of God our Saviour </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεου σωτηρος ημων</span></span>). Genitive case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιταγην</span></span>. In the LXX <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωτηρ</span></span> (old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωζω</span></span> for agent in saving, applied to deities, princes, kings, etc.) occurs 20 times, all but two to God. The Romans called the emperor "Saviour God." In the N.T. the designation of God as Saviour is peculiar to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:47">Luke 1:47</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jdg+1:25">Judges 1:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:3">1 Timothy 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:3">1 Timothy 2:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:10">1 Timothy 4:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:3">Titus 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:10">Titus 2:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:4">Titus 3:4</span>. In the other Epistles Paul uses it of Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:20">Philippians 3:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:23">Ephesians 5:23</span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:10">2 Timothy 1:10</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:1">2 Peter 1:1</span> we have "our God and Saviour Jesus Christ" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:13">Titus 2:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Our hope </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της ελπιδος ημων</span></span>). Like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:27">Colossians 1:27</span>. More than the author and object of hope, "its very substance and foundation" (Ellicott). </p>
