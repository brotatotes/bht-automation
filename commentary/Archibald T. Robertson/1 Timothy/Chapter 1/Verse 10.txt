<p><b> For abusers of themselves with men </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρσενοκοιταις</span></span>). Late compound for sodomites. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:9">1 Corinthians 6:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>Men-stealers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανδραποδισταις</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανδραποδιζω</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανηρ</span></span>, man, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πους</span></span>, foot, to catch by the foot), to enslave. So enslavers, whether kidnappers (men-stealers) of free men or stealers of the slaves of other men. So slave-dealers. By the use of this word Paul deals a blow at the slave-trade (cf. Philemon).</p>
<p><b>Liars </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευσταις</span></span>). Old word, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:4">Romans 3:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>False swearers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιορκοις</span></span>). Old word (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επι, ορκος</span></span>, oath). Perjurers. Only here in N.T. For similar lists, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:11">1 Corinthians 5:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:9">1 Corinthians 6:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:19">Galatians 5:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:28">Romans 1:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:13">Romans 13:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:5">Colossians 3:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:5">Ephesians 5:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:2">2 Timothy 3:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>The sound doctrine </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη υγιαινουση διδασκαλια</span></span>). Dative case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντικειτα</span></span>, for which verb see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:17">Galatians 5:17</span> for the conflict between the Spirit and the flesh. "The healthful (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υγιαινω</span></span>, old word for being well, as <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:31">Luke 5:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:2">3 John 1:2</span>, in figurative sense in N.T. only in the Pastorals) teaching." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:9">Titus 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:3">2 Timothy 4:3</span>. </p>
