<p><b> I thank </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριν εχω</span></span>). "I have gratitude to." Common phrase (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:9">Luke 17:9</span>), not elsewhere in Paul.</p>
<p><b>That enabled me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω ενδυναμωσαντ με</span></span>). First aorist active articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδυναμοω</span></span>. Late verb, but regular Pauline idiom (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:20">Romans 4:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:13">Philippians 4:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:10">Ephesians 6:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:12">1 Timothy 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:17">2 Timothy 4:17</span>).</p>
<p><b>Appointing me to his service </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεμενος εις διακονιαν</span></span>). Second aorist middle participle. Pauline phrase and atmosphere (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:24">Acts 20:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:5">1 Corinthians 3:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:18">1 Corinthians 12:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:28">1 Corinthians 12:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:6">2 Corinthians 3:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:1">2 Corinthians 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:23">Colossians 1:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:7">Colossians 3:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:6">1 Timothy 4:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:5">2 Timothy 4:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:11">2 Timothy 4:11</span>). </p>
