<p><b> Before </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το προτερον</span></span>). Accusative of general reference of the articular comparative, "as to the former-time," formerly, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:13">Galatians 4:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Though I was </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οντα</span></span>). Concessive participle agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">με</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Blasphemer </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλασφημον</span></span>). Old word either from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλαξ</span></span> (stupid) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φημη</span></span>, speech, or from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλαπτω</span></span>, to injure. Rare in N.T. but Paul uses <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλασφημεω</span></span>, to blaspheme in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:24">Romans 2:24</span>.</p>
<p><b>Persecutor </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διωκτης</span></span>). So far found only here. Probably made by Paul from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διωκω</span></span>, which he knew well enough (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:4">Acts 22:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:7">Acts 22:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:14">Acts 26:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:13">Galatians 1:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:23">Galatians 1:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:6">Philippians 3:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:12">2 Timothy 3:12</span>).</p>
<p><b>Injurious </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υβριστην</span></span>). Substantive, not adjective, "an insolent man." Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υβριζω</span></span>, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:30">Romans 1:30</span>.</p>
<p><b>I obtained mercy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεηθην</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεεω</span></span>, old verb. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:1">2 Corinthians 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:30">Romans 11:30</span>.</p>
<p><b>Ignorantly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγνοων</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγνοεω</span></span>, "not knowing." Old verb (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:4">Romans 2:4</span>). In a blindness of heart.</p>
<p><b>In unbelief </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν απιστια</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:20">Romans 11:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:25">Romans 11:25</span>. </p>
