<p><b> Abounded exceedingly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερεπλεονασεν</span></span>). Aorist active indicative of the late and rare (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="so+5:19">Song of Solomon 5:19</span> and in Herond.) compound <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερπλεοναζω</span></span> (here alone in N.T.), in later ecclesiastical writers. The simplex <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλεοναζω</span></span> Paul used in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:20">Romans 5:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:1">Romans 6:1</span> and the kindred <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερεπερισσευσεν</span></span> used also with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η χαρις</span></span>. Paul is fond of compounds with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ</span></span>. For "faith in Christ Jesus" see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:26">Galatians 3:26</span>, for "faith and love in Christ Jesus" as here, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:13">2 Timothy 1:13</span>. </p>
