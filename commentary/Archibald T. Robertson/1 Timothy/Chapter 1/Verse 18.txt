<p><b> I commit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρατιθεμα</span></span>). Present middle indicative of old and common verb, to place beside (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span>) as food on table, in the middle to entrust (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:48">Luke 12:48</span>) and used by Jesus as he was dying (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:46">Luke 23:46</span>). Here it is a banking figure and repeated in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:2">2 Timothy 2:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>According to the prophecies which went before on thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα τας προαγουσας επ σε προφητειας</span></span>). Intransitive use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προαγω</span></span>, to go before. When Timothy first comes before us (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:2">Acts 16:2</span>) "he was testified to" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμαρτυρειτο</span></span>) by the brethren. He began his ministry rich in hopes, prayers, predictions.</p>
<p><b>That by them thou mayest war the good warfare </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα στρατευη εν αυταις την καλην στρατειαν</span></span>). Cognate accusative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρατειαν</span></span>, old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρατευω</span></span>, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:4">2 Corinthians 4:4</span>) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρατευη</span></span> (second person singular middle present subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρατευω</span></span>, old verb chiefly in Paul in N.T., <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:7">1 Corinthians 9:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:3">2 Corinthians 10:3</span>). As if in defensive armour. </p>
