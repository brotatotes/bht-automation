<p><b> Holding faith and a good conscience </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχων πιστιν κα αγαθην συνειδησιν</span></span>). Possibly as a shield (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:16">Ephesians 6:16</span>) or at any rate possessing (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:20">Romans 2:20</span>) faith as trust and a good conscience. A leader expects them of his followers and must show them himself.</p>
<p><b>Having thrust from them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απωσαμενο</span></span>). First aorist indirect middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απωθεω</span></span>, to push away from one. Old verb (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:1">Romans 11:1</span>).</p>
<p><b>Made shipwreck </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εναυαγησαν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ναυαγεω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ναυαγος</span></span> (shipwrecked, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ναυς</span></span>, ship, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγνυμ</span></span>, to break), to break a ship to pieces. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:25">2 Corinthians 11:25</span>.</p>
<p><b>Concerning the faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ την πιστιν</span></span>). Rather, "concerning their faith" (the article here used as a possessive pronoun, a common Greek idiom). </p>
