<p><b> Hymenaeus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hυμεναιος</span></span>). The same heretic reappears in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:17">2 Timothy 2:17</span>. He and Alexander are the chief "wreckers" of faith in Ephesus.</p>
<p><b>Alexander </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αλεξανδρος</span></span>). Probably the same as the one in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:14">2 Timothy 4:14</span>, but not the Jew of that name in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:33">Acts 19:33</span>, unless he had become a Christian since then.</p>
<p><b>I delivered unto Satan </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεδωκα τω Σατανα</span></span>). See this very idiom (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραδουνα τω Σατανα</span></span>) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:5">1 Corinthians 5:5</span>. It is a severe discipline of apostolic authority, apparently exclusion and more than mere abandonment (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:18">1 Thessalonians 2:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:11">1 Corinthians 5:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:11">2 Corinthians 2:11</span>), though it is an obscure matter.</p>
<p><b>That they might be taught not to blaspheme </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα παιδευθωσιν μη βλασφημειν</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παιδευω</span></span>. For this use of this common late verb, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:32">1 Corinthians 11:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:9">2 Corinthians 6:9</span>. </p>
