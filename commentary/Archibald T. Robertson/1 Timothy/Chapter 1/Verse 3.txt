<p><b> As I exhorted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθως παρεκαλεσα</span></span>). There is an ellipse of the principal clause in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:4">1 Timothy 1:4</span> (<b> so do I now </b> not being in the Greek).</p>
<p><b>To tarry </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσμεινα</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσμενω</span></span>, old verb, attributed by Luke to Paul in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:43">Acts 13:43</span>.</p>
<p><b>That thou mightest charge </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα παραγγειληις</span></span>). Subfinal clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραγγελλω</span></span>, old verb, to transmit a message along (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span>) from one to another. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:4">2 Thessalonians 3:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:6">2 Thessalonians 3:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:10">2 Thessalonians 3:10</span>. Lock considers this idiom here an elliptical imperative like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:29">Ephesians 4:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:33">Ephesians 5:33</span>.</p>
<p><b>Certain men </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τισιν</span></span>). Dative case. Expressly vague (no names as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:20">1 Timothy 1:20</span>), though Paul doubtless has certain persons in Ephesus in mind.</p>
<p><b>Not to teach a different doctrine </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη ετεροδιδασκαλειν</span></span>). Earliest known use of this compound like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακοδιδασκαλειν</span></span> of Clement of Rome. Only other N.T. example in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:3">1 Timothy 6:3</span>. Eusebius has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετεροδιδασκαλος</span></span>. Same idea in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:6">Galatians 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:4">2 Corinthians 11:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:17">Romans 16:17</span>. Perhaps coined by Paul. </p>
