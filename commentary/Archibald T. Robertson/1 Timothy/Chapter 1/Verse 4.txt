<p><b> To give heed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσεχειν</span></span>). With <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νουν</span></span> understood. Old and common idiom in N.T. especially in Luke and Acts (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:10">Acts 8:10</span>). Not in Paul's earlier Epistles. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:8">1 Timothy 3:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:1">1 Timothy 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:13">1 Timothy 4:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:14">Titus 1:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>To fables </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυθοις</span></span>). Dative case of old word for speech, narrative, story, fiction, falsehood. In N.T. only <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:16">2 Peter 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:4">1 Timothy 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:7">1 Timothy 4:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:14">Titus 1:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:4">2 Timothy 4:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>Genealogies </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενεαλογιαις</span></span>). Dative of old word, in LXX, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:9">Titus 3:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>Endless </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεραντοις</span></span>). Old verbal compound (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περαινω</span></span>, to go through), in LXX, only here in N.T. Excellent examples there for old words used only in the Pastorals because of the subject matter, describing the Gnostic emphasis on aeons.</p>
<p><b>Questionings </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκζητησεις</span></span>). "Seekings out." Late and rare compound from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκζητεω</span></span> (itself <i>Koine</i> word, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:11">Romans 3:11</span> from LXX and in papyri). Here only in N.T. Simplex <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζητησις</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:2">Acts 15:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:4">1 Timothy 6:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:9">Titus 3:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:23">2 Timothy 2:23</span>.</p>
<p><b>A dispensation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικονομιαν</span></span>). Pauline word (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:17">1 Corinthians 9:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:25">Colossians 1:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:9">Ephesians 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:9">Ephesians 3:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:4">1 Timothy 1:4</span>), <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:2-4">Luke 16:2-4</span> only other N.T. examples.</p>
<p><b>In faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν πιστε</span></span>). Pauline use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστις</span></span>. </p>
