<p><b> Having swerved </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αστοχησαντες</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αστοχεω</span></span>, compound <i>Koine</i> verb (Polybius, Plutarch) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αστοχος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στοχος</span></span>, a mark), "having missed the mark." In N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:21">1 Timothy 6:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:18">2 Timothy 2:18</span>. With the ablative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ων</span></span> (which).</p>
<p><b>Have turned aside </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξετραπησαν</span></span>). Second aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκτρεπω</span></span>, old and common verb, to turn or twist out or aside. In medical sense in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:13">Hebrews 12:13</span>. As metaphor in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:6">1 Timothy 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:20">1 Timothy 6:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:4">2 Timothy 4:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>Vain talking </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ματαιολογιαν</span></span>). Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ματαιολογος</span></span>, only here in N.T., in the literary <i>Koine</i>. </p>
