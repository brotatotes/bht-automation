<p><b> Teachers of the law </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νομοδιδασκαλο</span></span>). Compound only in N.T. (here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:17">Luke 5:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:34">Acts 5:34</span>) and ecclesiastical writers.</p>
<p><b>Though they understand </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νοουντες</span></span>). Concessive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νοεω</span></span>, old verb (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:4">Ephesians 3:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:20">Ephesians 3:20</span>).</p>
<p><b>Neither what </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μητε α</span></span>). Relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> (which things).</p>
<p><b>Nor whereof </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μητε περ τινων</span></span>). Here the interrogative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τινων</span></span> used in sense of relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ων</span></span>. It may be regarded as the use of an indirect question for variety (Parry).</p>
<p><b>They confidently affirm </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαβεβαιουντα</span></span>). Present middle indicative of the common <i>Koine</i> compound, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:8">Titus 3:8</span>. </p>
