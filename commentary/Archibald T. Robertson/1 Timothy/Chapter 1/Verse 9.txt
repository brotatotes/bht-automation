<p><b> Is not made for </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου κειτα</span></span>). The use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κειτα</span></span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεθειτα</span></span> (perfect passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span>) is a common enough idiom. See the same point about law in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:18-23">1 Timothy 1:18-23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:13">Romans 13:13</span>. For "knowing this" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδως τουτο</span></span>) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:5">Ephesians 5:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>Unruly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανυποτακτοις</span></span>). Dative (like all these words) of the late verbal (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποτασσω</span></span>). In N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:6">Titus 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:10">Titus 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:8">Hebrews 2:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>Ungodly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασεβεσ</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:5">Romans 4:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:6">Romans 5:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Sinners </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτωλοις</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:7">Romans 3:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>Unholy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανοσιοις</span></span>). Common word (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οσιος</span></span>. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:2">2 Timothy 3:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>Profane </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βεβηλοις</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαινω</span></span>, to go, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βηλος</span></span>, threshold. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:16">Hebrews 12:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>Murderers of fathers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πατρολωιαις</span></span>). Late form for common Attic <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πατραλωιαις</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πατηρ</span></span>, father, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλοιαω</span></span>, to smite) only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Murderers of mothers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μητρολωιαις</span></span>). Late form Attic <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μητραλωιαις</span></span>. Only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Manslayers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανδραφονοις</span></span>). Old compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανηρ</span></span>, man, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φονος</span></span>, murder). Only here in N.T. </p>
