<p><b> First of all </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτον παντων</span></span>). Take with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρακαλω</span></span>. My first request (first in importance).</p>
<p><b>Intercessions </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντευξεις</span></span>). Late word (Polybius, Plutarch, etc.), only here in N.T. and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:5">1 Timothy 4:5</span>, though the verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντυγχανω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:27">Romans 8:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:34">Romans 8:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:2">Romans 11:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:25">Romans 11:25</span>. The other three words for prayer are common (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:6">Philippians 4:6</span>).</p>
<p><b>For all men </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ παντων ανθρωπων</span></span>). The scope of prayer is universal including all kinds of sinners (and saints). </p>
