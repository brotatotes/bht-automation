<p><b> Being beguiled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαπατηθεισα</span></span>). First aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαπατεω</span></span>, old compound verb, in N.T. only by Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:3">2 Thessalonians 2:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:18">1 Corinthians 3:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:3">2 Corinthians 11:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:11">Romans 7:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:18">Romans 16:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:14">1 Timothy 2:14</span>). Not certain that <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ-</span></span> here means "completely deceived" in contrast to simplex (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ ηπατηθη</span></span>) used of Adam, though possible.</p>
<p><b>Hath fallen </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεγονεν</span></span>). Second perfect indicative active, permanent state. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:7">1 Corinthians 11:7</span>. </p>
