<p><b> For kings </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ βασιλεων</span></span>). And this included Nero who had already set fire to Rome and laid it on the Christians whom he was also persecuting.</p>
<p><b>And all them that are in high place </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα παντων των εν υπεροχη οντων</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hυπεροχη</span></span> is old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπεροχος</span></span> and this from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>), but in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:1">1 Corinthians 2:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>That we may lead </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα διαγωμεν</span></span>). Purpose clause with present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαγω</span></span>, an old and common verb, but in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:3">Titus 3:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Tranquil </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηρεμον</span></span>). Late adjective from the old adverb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηρεμα</span></span> (stilly, quietly). Here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Quiet </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ησυχιον</span></span>). Old adjective, once in LXX (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+66:2">Isaiah 66:2</span>), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:4">1 Peter 3:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>Life </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βιον</span></span>). Old word for course of life (not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωη</span></span>). So <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:14">Luke 8:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>Gravity </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σεμνοτητ</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σεμνος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:8">Philippians 4:8</span>), in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:4">1 Timothy 3:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:7">Titus 2:7</span>. </p>
