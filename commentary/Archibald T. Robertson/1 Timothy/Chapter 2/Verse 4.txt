<p><b> Willeth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θελε</span></span>). God's wish and will in so far as he can influence men.</p>
<p><b>That all men should be saved </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντας ανθρωπους σωθηνα</span></span>). First aorist passive infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωζω</span></span> with accusative of general reference. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:33">1 Corinthians 10:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:18">2 Corinthians 5:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>To the knowledge </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις επιγνωσιν</span></span>). "The full knowledge" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:6">Colossians 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:13">Ephesians 4:13</span> (ten times in Paul). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:7">2 Timothy 3:7</span> for the whole phrase "full knowledge of the truth" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αληθεια</span></span> 14 times in the Pastorals). Paul is anxious as in Colossians and Ephesians that the Gnostics may not lead the people astray. They need the full intellectual apprehension of Christianity. </p>
