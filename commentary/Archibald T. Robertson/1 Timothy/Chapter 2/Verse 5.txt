<p><b> One God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις θεος</span></span>). Regular Pauline argument for a universal gospel (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:20">Galatians 3:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:30">Romans 3:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:6">Ephesians 4:6</span>).</p>
<p><b>One mediator </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις μεσιτης</span></span>). Late word (Polybius, Philo) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεσος</span></span> (middle), a middle man. In N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:20">Galatians 3:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:6">Hebrews 8:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:15">Hebrews 9:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:24">Hebrews 12:24</span>.</p>
<p><b>Between God and men </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεου κα ανθρωπων</span></span>). Ablative case (though objective genitive may explain it) after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεσιτης</span></span> (notion of separation) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:12">Romans 10:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:14">Hebrews 5:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>Himself man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωπος</span></span>). No "himself" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτος</span></span>) in the Greek. </p>
