<p><b> For which </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις ο</span></span>). The testimony of Jesus in his self-surrender (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:6">1 Timothy 2:6</span>). See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις ο</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:11">2 Timothy 1:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>I was appointed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετεθην εγω</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Preacher and apostle </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κηρυξ κα αποστολος</span></span>). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:10">2 Timothy 1:10</span> Paul adds <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδασκαλος</span></span> (herald, apostle, teacher) as he does here with emphasis. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:23">Colossians 1:23</span> he has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονος</span></span> (minister). He frequently uses <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κηρυσσω</span></span> of himself (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:23">1 Corinthians 1:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:27">1 Corinthians 9:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:2">Galatians 2:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:8">Romans 10:8</span>).</p>
<p><b>I speak the truth, I lie not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αληθειαν λεγω, ου ψευδομα</span></span>). A Pauline touch (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:1">Romans 9:1</span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:20">Galatians 1:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:31">2 Corinthians 11:31</span>. Here alone he calls himself "a teacher of the Gentiles," elsewhere apostle (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:13">Romans 11:13</span>), minister (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:16">Romans 15:16</span>), prisoner (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:1">Ephesians 3:1</span>). </p>
