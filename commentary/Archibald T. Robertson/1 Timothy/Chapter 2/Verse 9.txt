<p><b> In like manner that women </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οσαυτως γυναικας</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Βουλομα</span></span> must be repeated from verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:8">1 Timothy 2:8</span>, involved in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οσαυτως</span></span> (old adverb, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:26">Romans 8:26</span>). Parry insists that <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσευχομενας</span></span> (when they pray) must be supplied also. Grammatically that is possible (Lock), but it is hardly consonant with verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:11-15">1 Timothy 2:11-15</span> (White).</p>
<p><b>Adorn themselves </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοσμειν εαυτας</span></span>). Present active infinitive after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βουλομα</span></span> understood. Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοσμος</span></span> (arrangement, ornament, order, world). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:5">Luke 21:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:10">Titus 2:10</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:5">1 Corinthians 11:5</span> for Paul's discussion of women's dress in public worship.</p>
<p><b>In modest apparel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν καταστολη κοσμιω</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Καταστολη</span></span> is a late word (a letting down, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταστελλω</span></span>, of demeanour or dress, arrangement of dress). Only here in N.T. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κοσμιος</span></span> is old adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοσμος</span></span> and means well-arranged, becoming. W. H. have adverb in margin (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοσμιως</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>With shamefastness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα αιδους</span></span>). Old word for shame, reverence, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:28">Hebrews 12:28</span>.</p>
<p><b>Sobriety </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωφροσυνης</span></span>). Old word, in N.T. only here, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:15">1 Timothy 2:15</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:15">Acts 26:15</span> (Paul also).</p>
<p><b>Not with braided hair </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη εν πλεγμασιν</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλεκω</span></span>, to plait, to braid, for nets, baskets, here only in N.T. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:1">1 Peter 3:1</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμπλοκης</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>And gold </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν χρυσιω</span></span>). Locative case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> repeated. Some MSS. read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρυσω</span></span>. Both used for gold ornaments.</p>
<p><b>Or pearls </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η μαργαριταις</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:6">Matthew 7:6</span> for this word.</p>
<p><b>Or costly raiment </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η ιματισμω πολυτελε</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hιματισμος</span></span> a common <i>Koine</i> word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιματιζω</span></span>, to clothe. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πολυτελης</span></span>, old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολυς</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελος</span></span> (great price). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:3">Mark 14:3</span>. </p>
