<p><b> Faithful is the saying </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστος ο λογος</span></span>). Here the phrase points to the preceding words (not like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:15">1 Timothy 1:15</span>) and should close the preceding paragraph.</p>
<p><b>If a man seeketh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε τις ορεγετα</span></span>). Condition of first class, assumed as true. Present middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορεγω</span></span>, old verb to reach out after something, governing the genitive. In N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:10">1 Timothy 6:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:16">Hebrews 11:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>The office of a bishop </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισκοπης</span></span>). Genitive case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορεγετα</span></span>. Late and rare word outside of LXX and N.T. (in a Lycaonian inscription). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισκοπεω</span></span> and means "over-seership" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:20">Acts 1:20</span>. </p>
