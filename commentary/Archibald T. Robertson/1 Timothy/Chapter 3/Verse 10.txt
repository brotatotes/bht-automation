<p><b> First be proved </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκιμαζεσθωσαν πρωτον</span></span>). Present passive imperative third plural of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκιμαζω</span></span>, old and common verb, to test as metals, etc. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:4">1 Thessalonians 2:4</span>, and often in Paul). How the proposed deacons are to be "first" tested before approved Paul does not say. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:10">Philippians 1:10</span> for the two senses (test, approve) of the word.</p>
<p><b>Let them serve as deacons </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονειτωσαν</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονεω</span></span> (same root as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονος</span></span>), common verb, to minister, here "to serve as deacons." Cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονειν</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:2">Acts 6:2</span>. See also verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:13">1 Timothy 3:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>If they be blameless </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεγκλητο οντες</span></span>). "Being blameless" (conditional participle, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οντες</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:8">1 Corinthians 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:22">Colossians 1:22</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεγκλητος</span></span>. </p>
