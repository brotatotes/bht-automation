<p><b> The bishop </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον επισκοπον</span></span>). The overseer. Old word, in LXX, and inscriptions and papyri. Deissmann (<i>Bible Studies</i>, pp. 230f.) has shown it is applied to communal officials in Rhodes. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:28">Acts 20:28</span> for its use for the elders (presbyters) in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:17">1 Timothy 3:17</span>. So also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:5">Titus 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:7">Titus 1:7</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:1">Philippians 1:1</span>. The word does not in the N.T. have the monarchical sense found in Ignatius of a bishop over elders.</p>
<p><b>Without reproach </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεπιλημπτον</span></span>). Accusative case of general reference with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εινα</span></span>. Old and common verbal (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλαμβανω</span></span>, not to be taken hold of), irreproachable. In N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:7">1 Timothy 5:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:14">1 Timothy 6:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>Of one wife </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιας γυναικος</span></span>). One at a time, clearly.</p>
<p><b>Temperate </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νηφαλιον</span></span>). Old adjective. In N.T. only here, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:11">1 Timothy 3:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:2">Titus 2:2</span>. But see <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νηφω</span></span>, to be sober in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:6">1 Thessalonians 5:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:8">1 Thessalonians 5:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>Soberminded </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωφρονα</span></span>). Another old adjective (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαος</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σως</span></span>, sound, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φρην</span></span>, mind) in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:8">Titus 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:2">Titus 2:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:5">Titus 2:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>Orderly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοσμιον</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:9">1 Timothy 2:9</span>. Seemly, decent conduct.</p>
<p><b>Given to hospitality </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλοξενον</span></span>). Old word (see <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλοξενια</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:13">Romans 12:13</span>), from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλος</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξενος</span></span>, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:8">Titus 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:9">1 Peter 4:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>Apt to teach </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδακτικον</span></span>). Late form for old <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδασκαλικος</span></span>, one qualified to teach. In Philo and N.T. only (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:2">1 Timothy 3:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:24">2 Timothy 2:24</span>). </p>
