<p><b> No brawler </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη παροινον</span></span>). Later word for the earlier <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παροινιος</span></span>, one who sits long at (beside, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span>) his wine. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:3">Titus 1:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>No striker </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη πληκτην</span></span>). Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλησσω</span></span>, to strike. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:3">Titus 1:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Gentle </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιεικη</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:5">Philippians 4:5</span> for this interesting word.</p>
<p><b>Not contentious </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαχον</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαχη</span></span>), not a fighter. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:2">Titus 3:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>No lover of money </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφιλαργυρον</span></span>). Late word (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and compound <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλ-αργυρος</span></span>) in inscriptions and papyri (Nageli; also Deissmann, <i>Light</i>, etc., pp. 85f.). In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:5">Hebrews 13:5</span>. </p>
