<p><b> If a man knoweth not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε τις ουκ οιδεν</span></span>). Condition of first class, assumed as true.</p>
<p><b>How to rule </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προστηνα</span></span>). Second aorist active infinitive of same verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προιστημ</span></span> and with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιδεν</span></span> means "know how to rule," not "know that he rules."</p>
<p><b>How </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πως</span></span>). Rhetorical question expecting negative answer.</p>
<p><b>Shall he take care of </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιμελησετα</span></span>). Future middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιμελεομα</span></span>, old compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>, direction of care towards) verb, in LXX, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:34">Luke 10:34</span>.</p>
<p><b>The church of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκκλησιας θεου</span></span>). Anarthrous as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:15">1 Timothy 3:15</span>, elsewhere with article (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:32">1 Corinthians 10:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:9">1 Corinthians 15:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:1">2 Corinthians 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:13">Galatians 1:13</span>). The local church described as belonging to God. No one in N.T. but Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:28">Acts 20:28</span>) so describes the church. This verse is a parenthesis in the characteristics of the bishop. </p>
