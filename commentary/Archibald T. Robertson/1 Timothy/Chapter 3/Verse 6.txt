<p><b> Not a novice </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη νεοφυτον</span></span>). Our "neophyte." Vernacular word from Aristophanes on, in LXX, and in papyri in the original sense of "newly-planted" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νεοσ, φυω</span></span>). Only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Lest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη</span></span>). "That not."</p>
<p><b>Being puffed up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυφωθεις</span></span>). First aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυφοω</span></span>, old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυφος</span></span>, smoke, pride), to raise a smoke or mist (a smoke-screen of pride). In N.T. only here; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:4">1 Timothy 6:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:4">2 Timothy 3:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>He fall into </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμπεση εις</span></span>). Second aorist active subjunctive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη</span></span>, negative purpose, of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμπιπτω</span></span>, old verb, to fall into. Note both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:11">Matthew 12:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:36">Luke 10:36</span>.</p>
<p><b>The condemnation of the devil </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κριμα του διαβολου</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:8">Romans 3:8</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κριμα</span></span>. Best to take <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του διαβολου</span></span> as objective genitive, though subjective in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:7">1 Timothy 3:7</span>, "the condemnation passed on or received by the devil" (not just "the slanderer," any slanderer). </p>
