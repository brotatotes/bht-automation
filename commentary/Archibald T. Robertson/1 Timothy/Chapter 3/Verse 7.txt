<p><b> From them that are without </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο των εξωθεν</span></span>). "From the outside (of the church) ones." Paul's care for the witness of outsiders is seen in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:12">1 Thessalonians 4:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:32">1 Corinthians 10:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+4:5">Colossians 4:5</span>. There are, of course, two sides to this matter.</p>
<p><b>Reproach </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ονειδισμον</span></span>). Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ονειδιζω</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:3">Romans 15:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>The snare of the devil </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παγιδα του διαβολου</span></span>). Here subjective genitive, snare set by the devil. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Παγις</span></span>, old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πηγνυμ</span></span>, to make fast. So a snare for birds (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:35">Luke 21:35</span>), any sudden trap (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:9">Romans 11:9</span>), of sin (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:9">1 Timothy 6:9</span>), of the devil (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:7">1 Timothy 3:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:26">2 Timothy 2:26</span>). Ancients used it of the snares of love. The devil sets special snares for preachers (conceit verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:6">1 Timothy 3:6</span>, money <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:9">1 Timothy 6:9</span>, women, ambition). </p>
