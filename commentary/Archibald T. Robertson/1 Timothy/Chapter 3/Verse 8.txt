<p><b> Deacons </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονους</span></span>). Accusative case of general reference like the preceding with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε εινα</span></span> understood. Technical sense of the word here as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:1">Philippians 1:1</span> which see (two classes of church officers, bishops or elders, deacons).</p>
<p><b>Grave </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σεμνους</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:8">Philippians 4:8</span>. Repeated in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:11">1 Timothy 3:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:2">Titus 2:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>Not double-tongued </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη διλογους</span></span>). Rare word (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δισ, λεγω</span></span>) saying same thing twice. Xenophon has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διλογεω</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διλογια</span></span>. In Pollux, but LXX has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διγλωσσος</span></span> (double-tongued, Latin <i>bilinguis</i>). Only here in N.T. One placed between two persons and saying one thing to one, another to the other. Like Bunyan's Parson "Mr. Two-Tongues."</p>
<p><b>Not given to much wine </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη οινω πολλω προσεχοντας</span></span>). "Not holding the mind (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον νουν</span></span> understood as usual with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσεχω</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:4">1 Timothy 1:4</span>) on much wine" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οινω</span></span>, dative case). That attitude leads to over-indulgence.</p>
<p><b>Not greedy of filthy lucre </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη αισχροκερδεις</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αισχρος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:12">Ephesians 5:12</span>) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κερδος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:21">Philippians 1:21</span>). "Making small gains in mean ways" (Parry). Not genuine in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:3">1 Timothy 3:3</span>. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:7">Titus 1:7</span> (of bishops). </p>
