<p><b> Expressly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρητως</span></span>). Late adverb, here alone in N.T., from verbal adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρητος</span></span> (from root <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρεω</span></span>). The reference is to the Holy Spirit, but whether to O.T. prophecy (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:16">Acts 1:16</span>) or to some Christian utterance (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:2">2 Thessalonians 2:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:1">1 Corinthians 14:1</span>) we do not know. Parry recalls the words of Jesus in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:10">Matthew 24:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:24">Matthew 24:24</span>.</p>
<p><b>In later times </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν υστεροις καιροις</span></span>). Old adjective (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:31">Matthew 21:31</span>) usually as adverb, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υστερον</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:2">Matthew 4:2</span>). Relative time from the prediction, now coming true (a present danger).</p>
<p><b>Some shall fall away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστησοντα τινες</span></span>). Future middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφιστημ</span></span>, intransitive use, shall stand off from, to fall away, apostatize (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:8">2 Corinthians 12:8</span>).</p>
<p><b>From the faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της πιστεως</span></span>). Ablative case (separation). Not creed, but faith in God through Christ.</p>
<p><b>Giving heed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσεχοντες</span></span>). Supply <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον νουν</span></span> (the mind) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:8">1 Timothy 3:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>Seducing spirits </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμασιν πλανοις</span></span>). Old adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλανη</span></span>, wandering), here active sense (deceiving). As substantive in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:8">2 Corinthians 6:8</span>. Probably some heathen or the worst of the Gnostics.</p>
<p><b>Doctrines of devils </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδασκαλιαις δαιμονιων</span></span>). "Teachings of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δαιμονς</span></span>." Definite explanation of the preceding. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:20">1 Corinthians 10:20</span>. </p>
