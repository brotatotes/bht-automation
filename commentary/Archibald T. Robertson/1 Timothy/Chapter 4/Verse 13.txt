<p><b> Till I come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως ερχομα</span></span>). "While I am coming" (present indicative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως</span></span>), not "till I come" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως ελθω</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Give heed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσεχε</span></span>). Present active imperative, supply <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον νουν</span></span>, "keep on putting thy mind on."</p>
<p><b>The reading </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη αναγνωσε</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγινωσκω</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:14">2 Corinthians 3:14</span>. Probably in particular the public reading of the Scriptures (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:15">Acts 13:15</span>), though surely private reading is not to be excluded.</p>
<p><b>To exhortation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη παρακλησε</span></span>),</p>
<p><b>to teaching </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη διδασκαλια</span></span>). Two other public functions of the minister. Probably Paul does not mean for the exhortation to precede the instruction, but the reverse in actual public work. Exhortation needs teaching to rest it upon, a hint for preachers today. </p>
