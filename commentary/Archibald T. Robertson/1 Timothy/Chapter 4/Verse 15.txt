<p><b> Be diligent in these things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταυτα μελετα</span></span>). Old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μελετη</span></span> (care, practice), present active imperative, "keep on practising these things." In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:25">Acts 4:25</span>.</p>
<p><b>Give thyself wholly to them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τουτοις ισθ</span></span>). Present imperative second person singular of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειμ</span></span>, "keep on in these things." Note five uses of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:12">1 Timothy 4:12</span> and three datives in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:14">1 Timothy 4:14</span>. Plutarch (Pomp. 656 B) says Caesar was <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τουτοις</span></span> ("in these things"). It is like our "up to his ears" in work (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιν μεδιας ρες</span></span>) and sticking to his task.</p>
<p><b>Thy progress </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σου η προκοπη</span></span>). <i>Koine</i> word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προκοπτω</span></span>, to cut forward, to blaze the way, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:12">Philippians 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:25">Philippians 1:25</span>. Paul's concern (purpose, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and present subjunctive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η</span></span> of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειμ</span></span>) is that Timothy's "progress" may be "manifest to all." It is inspiring to see a young preacher grow for then the church will grow with him. </p>
