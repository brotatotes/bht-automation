<p><b> Take heed to thyself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεχε σεαυτω</span></span>). Present active imperative of old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεχω</span></span>, to hold upon (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:1">Philippians 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:16">Philippians 2:16</span>), but here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον νουν</span></span> (the mind) must be supplied as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:5">Acts 3:5</span> and as is common with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσεχω</span></span>. With dative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σεαυτω</span></span>. "Keep on paying attention to thyself." Some young preachers are careless about their health and habits. Some are too finical.</p>
<p><b>And to the teaching </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα τη διδασκαλια</span></span>). This is important also.</p>
<p><b>Continue in these things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιμενε αυτοις</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιμενω</span></span>, old and common verb to stay by the side of a person or thing. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:1">Romans 6:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:23">Colossians 1:23</span>. "Stay by them," "stick to them," "see them through." "Stick to the business of framing your own life and your teaching on right lines" (Parry).</p>
<p><b>Thou shalt save </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωσεις</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωζω</span></span>, effective future, finally save. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:27">1 Corinthians 9:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:9">John 10:9</span>. </p>
