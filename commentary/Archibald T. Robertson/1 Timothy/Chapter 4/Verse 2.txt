<p><b> Through the hypocrisy of men that speak lies </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν υποκρισε ψευδολογων</span></span>). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποκρισις</span></span>, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:13">Galatians 2:13</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ψευδολογος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευδησ, λεγω</span></span>) <i>Koine</i> word from Aristophanes on. Here only in N.T. "A good classical word for liars on a large scale" (Parry).</p>
<p><b>Branded in their own conscience as with a hot iron </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεκαυστηριασμενων την ιδιαν συνειδησιν</span></span>). Accusative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνειδησιν</span></span> retained with the perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καυστηριαζω</span></span>, a rare verb only here and once in Strabo. Branded with the mark of Satan (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:26">2 Timothy 2:26</span>) as Paul was with the marks of Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:17">Galatians 6:17</span>). Agreeing in case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευδολογων</span></span>. </p>
