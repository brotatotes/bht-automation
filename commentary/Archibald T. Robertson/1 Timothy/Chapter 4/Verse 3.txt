<p><b> Forbidding to marry </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κωλυοντων γαμειν</span></span>). Present active participle of common verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κωλυω</span></span>, to hinder, genitive case agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευδολογων</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:16">Colossians 2:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:21">Colossians 2:21</span>, where Paul condemns the ascetic practices of the Gnostics. The Essenes, Therapeutae and other oriental sects forbade marriage. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:1">1 Timothy 4:1</span> Paul does not condemn marriage.</p>
<p><b>To abstain from meats </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεχεσθα βρωματων</span></span>). Infinitive dependent, not on <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κωλυοντων</span></span>, but on the positive idea <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κελευοντων</span></span> (implied, not expressed). Ablative case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βρωματων</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεχεσθα</span></span> (present direct middle, to hold oneself away from). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:1">1 Timothy 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:14">1 Timothy 4:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:15">1 Timothy 4:15</span> for disputes about "meats offered to idols" and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:22">Colossians 1:22</span> for the Gnostic asceticism.</p>
<p><b>Which God created </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α ο θεος εκτισεν</span></span>). First active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κτιζω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:16">Colossians 1:16</span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:25">1 Corinthians 10:25</span>.</p>
<p><b>To be received </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις μεταλημψιν</span></span>). "For reception." Old word, only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>By them that believe and know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις πιστοις κα επεγνωκοσ</span></span>). Dative case, "for the believers and those who (one article unites closely) have known fully" (perfect active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιγινωσκω</span></span>), a Pauline use of the word (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:6">Colossians 1:6</span>). </p>
