<p><b> If thou put the brethren in mind of these things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταυτα υποτιθεμενος τοις αδελφοις</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποτιθημ</span></span>, to place under, to suggest, old and common verb, here only in N.T., "suggesting these things to the brethren."</p>
<p><b>Thou shalt be a good minister of Christ Jesus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλος εση διακονος Χριστου Ιησου</span></span>). This beautiful phrase covers one's whole service for Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:1-7">1 Timothy 3:1-7</span>).</p>
<p><b>Nourished in </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντρεφομενος</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντρεφω</span></span>, old verb, to nourish in, used by Plato of "nourished in the laws," here only in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>The words of the faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις λογοις της πιστεως</span></span>). Locative case. The right diet for babes in Christ. The Bolshevists in Russia are feeding the children on atheism to get rid of God.</p>
<p><b>Which thou hast followed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η παρηκολουθηκας</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρακολουθεω</span></span>, old verb, to follow beside, of persons (often in old Greek) or of ideas and things (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:3">Luke 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:6">1 Timothy 4:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:10">2 Timothy 3:10</span>). With associative instrumental case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η</span></span> (which). </p>
