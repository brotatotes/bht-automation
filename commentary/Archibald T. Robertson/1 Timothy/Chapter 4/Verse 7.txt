<p><b> Refuse </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραιτου</span></span>). Present middle imperative second person singular of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραιτεω</span></span>, old verb, to ask of one and then to beg off from one as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:18">Luke 14:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:11">Acts 25:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:7">1 Timothy 4:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:11">1 Timothy 5:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:10">Titus 3:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:23">2 Timothy 2:23</span>.</p>
<p><b>Profane </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βεβηλους</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:9">1 Timothy 1:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>Old wives' fables </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γραωδεις μυθους</span></span>). On <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυθος</span></span>, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:4">1 Timothy 1:4</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Γραωδεις</span></span>, late word (Strabo, Galen) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γραυς</span></span>, old woman, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδος</span></span> (look, appearance). Such as old women tell to children like the Gnostic aeons.</p>
<p><b>Exercise thyself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γυμναζε σεαυτον</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γυμναζω</span></span>, originally to exercise naked (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γυμνος</span></span>). Old and common verb, but in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:14">Hebrews 5:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:11">Hebrews 12:11</span>. </p>
