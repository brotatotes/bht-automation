<p><b> Bodily exercise </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η σωματικη γυμνασια</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Γυμνασια</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γυμναζω</span></span>), also a common old word, here only in N.T. So also <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωματικη</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωμα</span></span>, body) in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:22">Luke 3:22</span>.</p>
<p><b>Profitable </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωφελιμος</span></span>). Another old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωφελεω</span></span>, to help, to profit), in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:8">Titus 3:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:16">2 Timothy 3:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>For a little </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς ολιγον</span></span>). "For little." Probably extent in contrast to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς παντα</span></span> (for all things), though in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:14">James 4:14</span> it is time "for a little while."</p>
<p><b>Which now is </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της νυν</span></span>). "The now life."</p>
<p><b>Of that which is to come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της μελλουσης</span></span>). "Of the coming (future) life." </p>
