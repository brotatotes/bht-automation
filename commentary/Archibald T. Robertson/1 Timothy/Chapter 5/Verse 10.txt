<p><b> If she hath brought up children </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε ετεκνοτροφησεν</span></span>). Condition of first class. Late and rare word (Aristotle, Epictetus), first aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεκνοτροφεω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεκνοτροφος</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεκνον, τρεφω</span></span>), here only in N.T. Qualification for her work as leader.</p>
<p><b>If she hath used hospitality to strangers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε εξενοδοχησεν</span></span>). First aorist again and same condition. Late form (Dio Cassius) of old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξενοδοκεω</span></span> (Herodotus), to welcome strangers (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξενους δεχομα</span></span>). Only here in N.T. Hospitality another qualification for such leadership (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:2">1 Timothy 3:2</span>).</p>
<p><b>If she hath washed the saints' feet </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε αγιων ποδας ενιψεν</span></span>). Same condition and tense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νιπτω</span></span> (old form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νιζω</span></span>), common in N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:5">John 13:5</span>). Proof of her hospitality, not of its being a church ordinance.</p>
<p><b>If she hath relieved the afflicted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε θλιβομενοις επηρκεσεν</span></span>). Same condition and tense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαρκεω</span></span>, to give sufficient aid, old word, in N.T. only here and verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:16">1 Timothy 5:16</span>. Experience that qualified her for eleemosynary work.</p>
<p><b>If she hath diligently followed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε επηκολουθησεν</span></span>). Same condition and tense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επακολουθεω</span></span>, old verb, to follow close upon (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>). So here, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:24">1 Timothy 5:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:21">1 Peter 2:21</span>. In a word such a widow must show her qualifications for leadership as with bishops and deacons. </p>
