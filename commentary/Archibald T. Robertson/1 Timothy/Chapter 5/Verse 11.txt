<p><b> But younger widows refuse </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νεωτερας δε χηρας παραιτου</span></span>). Present middle imperative as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:7">1 Timothy 4:7</span>. "Beg off from." They lack experience as above and they have other ambitions.</p>
<p><b>When they have waxed wanton </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν καταστρηνιασωσιν</span></span>). First aorist (ingressive) active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταστρηνιαω</span></span>, late compound (only here and Ignatius), to feel the impulse of sexual desire, but simplex <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρηνιαω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:7">Revelation 18:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:9">Revelation 18:9</span>). Souter renders it here "exercise youthful vigour against Christ" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του Χριστου</span></span>, genitive case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span> in composition). </p>
