<p><b> And withal </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμα δε κα</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:22">Philemon 1:22</span> for this very phrase, "and at the same time also." Such young enrolled widows have other perils also.</p>
<p><b>They learn to be idle </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αργα μανθανουσιν</span></span>). There is no <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εινα</span></span> (to be) in the Greek. This very idiom without <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εινα</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μανθανω</span></span> occurs in Plato and Dio Chrysostom, though unusual. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αργα</span></span> (idle) is old adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργον</span></span>, without work). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:3">Matthew 20:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:12">Titus 1:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>Going about </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιερχομενα</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιερχομα</span></span>, old compound verb. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:13">Acts 19:13</span> of strollers.</p>
<p><b>From house to house </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τας οικιας</span></span>). Literally "the houses," "wandering around the houses." Vivid picture of idle tattlers and gossipers.</p>
<p><b>But tattlers also </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα κα φλυαρο</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φλυω</span></span> (to boil up, to throw up bubbles, like blowing soap bubbles). Only here in N.T. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Φλυαρεω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:10">3 John 1:10</span> only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>And busybodies </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα περιεργο</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περι, εργον</span></span>), busy about trifles to the neglect of important matters. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:19">Acts 19:19</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:11">2 Thessalonians 3:11</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιεργαζομα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Things which they ought not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα μη δεοντα</span></span>). "The not necessary things," and, as a result, often harmful. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:11">Titus 1:11</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α μη δε</span></span> (which things are not necessary). </p>
