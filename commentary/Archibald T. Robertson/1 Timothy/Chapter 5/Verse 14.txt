<p><b> I desire </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βουλομα</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:8">1 Timothy 2:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>The younger widows </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νεωτερας</span></span>). No article and no word for widows, though that is clearly the idea. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Νεωτερας</span></span> is accusative of general reference with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γαμειν</span></span> (to marry) the object (present infinitive active) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βουλομα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Bear children </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεκνογονειν</span></span>). A compound verb here only in N.T. and nowhere else save in Anthol. See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεκνογονια</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:15">1 Timothy 2:15</span>.</p>
<p><b>Rule the household </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικοδεσποτειν</span></span>). Late verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικοδεσποτης</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:14">Mark 14:14</span>), twice in the papyri, only here in N.T. Note that the wife is here put as ruler of the household, proper recognition of her influence, "new and improved position" (Liddon) .</p>
<p><b>Occasion </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφορμην</span></span>). Old word (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο, ορμη</span></span>), a base to rush from, Pauline use in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:12">2 Corinthians 5:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:12">2 Corinthians 11:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:13">Galatians 5:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>To the adversary </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω αντικειμενω</span></span>). Dative case of the articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντικειμα</span></span>, a Pauline idiom (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:28">Philippians 1:28</span>).</p>
<p><b>Reviling </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λοιδοριας</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λοιδορεω</span></span>), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:9">1 Peter 3:9</span>. Genitive case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριν</span></span>. </p>
