<p><b> That believeth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστη</span></span>). "Believing woman."</p>
<p><b>Hath widows </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχε χηρας</span></span>). The "any believing woman" is one of the household-rulers of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:14">1 Timothy 5:14</span>. The "widows" here are the widows dependent on her and who are considered as candidates to be enrolled in the list.</p>
<p><b>Let her relieve them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαρκειτω αυταις</span></span>). For this verb (imperative present active) see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:10">1 Timothy 5:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Let not be burdened </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη βαρεισθω</span></span>). Present passive imperative (in prohibition <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span>) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαρεω</span></span>, old verb (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαρος</span></span>, burden), Pauline word (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:8">2 Corinthians 1:8</span>).</p>
<p><b>That are widows indeed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταις οντως χηραις</span></span>). Dative case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαρκεση</span></span> (first aorist active subjunctive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>, final clause). See verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:3">1 Timothy 5:3</span> for this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οντως</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χηραις</span></span> "the qualified and enrolled widows." Cf. verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:9">1 Timothy 5:9</span>. </p>
