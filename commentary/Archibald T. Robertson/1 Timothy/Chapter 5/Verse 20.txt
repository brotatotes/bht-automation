<p><b> Them that sin </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους αμαρτανοντας</span></span>). The elders who continue to sin (present active participle).</p>
<p><b>In the sight of all </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενωπιον παντων</span></span>). "In the eye of (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο εν οπ ων</span></span>, the one who is in the eye of, then combined = <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενωπιον</span></span>) all" the elders (or even of the church). See next verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:21">1 Timothy 5:21</span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:20">Galatians 1:20</span>. Public rebuke when a clear case, not promiscuous gossip.</p>
<p><b>May be in fear </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φοβον εχωσιν</span></span>). Present active subjunctive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> (final clause), "may keep on having fear" (of exposure). Possibly, "the rest of the elders." </p>
