<p><b> Lay hands hastily </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χειρας ταχεως επιτιθε</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιτιθημ</span></span> in the sense of approval (ordination) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:6">Acts 6:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:3">Acts 13:3</span>. But it is not clear whether it is the case of ministers just ordained as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:14">1 Timothy 4:14</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιθεσις</span></span>), or of warning against hasty ordination of untried men, or the recognition and restoration of deposed ministers (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:20">1 Timothy 5:20</span>) as suits the context. The prohibition suits either situation, or both.</p>
<p><b>Be partakers of other men's sins </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινωνε αμαρτιαις αλλοτριαις</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινωνεω</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινωνος</span></span>, partner) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> in prohibition with associative instrumental case as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:11">2 John 1:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:13">Romans 12:13</span>. On <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλοτριος</span></span> (belonging to another) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:4">Romans 14:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>Keep thyself pure </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σεαυτον αγνον τηρε</span></span>). "Keep on keeping thyself pure." Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρεω</span></span>. </p>
