<p><b> Be no longer a drinker of water </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηκετ υδροποτε</span></span>). Present active imperative (prohibition) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υδροποτεω</span></span>, old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υδροποτης</span></span>, water drinker, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υδωρ, πινω</span></span>), here only in N.T. Not complete asceticism, but only the need of some wine urged in Timothy's peculiar physical condition (a sort of medical prescription for this case).</p>
<p><b>But use a little wine </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα αινω ολιγω χρω</span></span>). Present middle imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χραομα</span></span> with instrumental case. The emphasis is on <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ολιγω</span></span> (a little).</p>
<p><b>For thy stomach's sake </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια τον στομαχον</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στομα</span></span> (mouth). In Homer throat, opening of the stomach (Aristotle), stomach in Plutarch. Here only in N.T. Our word "stomach."</p>
<p><b>Thine often infirmities </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τας πυκνας σου ασθενειας</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πυκνος</span></span> is old word, dense, frequent. In N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:33">Luke 5:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:26">Acts 24:26</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ασθενειας</span></span> = weaknesses, lack of strength (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:26">Romans 8:26</span>). Timothy was clearly a semi-invalid. </p>
