<p><b> Grandchildren </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκγονα</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκγινομα</span></span>, here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Let them learn </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μανθανετωσαν</span></span>). The children and grandchildren of a widow. Present active imperative third person plural of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μανθανω</span></span>. "Let them keep on learning."</p>
<p><b>First </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτον</span></span>). Adverb, first before anything else. No "corban" business here. No acts of "piety" toward God will make up for impiety towards parents.</p>
<p><b>To shew piety </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευσεβειν</span></span>). Present active infinitive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μανθανετωσαν</span></span> and old verb, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:23">Acts 17:23</span>. From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευσεβης</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ, σεβομα</span></span>), pious, dutiful.</p>
<p><b>Their own family </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον ιδιον οικον</span></span>). "Their own household." Filial piety is primary unless parents interfere with duty to Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:26">Luke 14:26</span>).</p>
<p><b>To requite </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμοιβας αποδιδονα</span></span>). Present active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποδιδωμ</span></span>, to give back, old and common verb (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:6">Romans 2:6</span>), to keep on giving back. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αμοιβας</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμειβομα</span></span>, to requite like for like) is old and common word, but here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Their parents </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις προγονοις</span></span>). Dative case of old and common word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προγονος</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προγινομα</span></span>, to come before), "ancestor." In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:3">2 Timothy 1:3</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:3">1 Timothy 2:3</span> for "acceptable" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποδεκτον</span></span>). </p>
