<p><b> Desolate </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεμονωμενη</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μονοω</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μονος</span></span>), "left alone," old verb, here alone in N.T. Without husband, children, or other close kin.</p>
<p><b>Hath her hope set on God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηλπικεν επ θεον</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελπιζω</span></span>, "hath placed her hope (and keeps it) on God." Text doubtful whether God (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεον</span></span>) or Lord (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κυριον</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Continues </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσμενε</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:3">1 Timothy 1:3</span>. With dative case here.</p>
<p><b>Night and day </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυκτος κα ημερας</span></span>). "By night and by day" (genitive, not accusative). Paul does not say that she should pray "all night and day." </p>
