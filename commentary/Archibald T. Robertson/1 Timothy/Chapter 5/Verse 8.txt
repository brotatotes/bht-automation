<p><b> Provideth not for his own </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των ιδιων ου προνοε</span></span>). Condition of first class with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span> and present active (or middle <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προνοειτα</span></span>) indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προνοεω</span></span>, old verb, to think beforehand. Pauline word in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:21">2 Corinthians 8:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:7">Romans 12:7</span>. With genitive case.</p>
<p><b>He hath denied the faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την πιστιν ηρνητα</span></span>). Perfect middle indicative of old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρνεομα</span></span>. His act of impiety belies (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:16">Titus 1:16</span>) his claim to the faith (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:13">Revelation 2:13</span>).</p>
<p><b>Worse than an unbeliever </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απιστου χειρων</span></span>). Ablative case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απιστου</span></span> after the comparative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χειρων</span></span>. Who makes no profession of piety. </p>
