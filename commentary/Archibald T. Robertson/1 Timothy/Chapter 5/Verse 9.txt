<p><b> Let none be enrolled as a widow </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χηρα καταλεγεσθω</span></span>). Present passive imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλεγω</span></span>, old verb, to set down in an official list, only here in N.T. "Let a widow be enrolled," the negative coming later, "having become of no less than sixty years" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη ελαττον ετων εξηκοντα γεγονυια</span></span>). Second perfect active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span>. For the case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετων</span></span>, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:42">Luke 2:42</span>. This list of genuine widows (verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:3">1 Timothy 5:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:5">1 Timothy 5:5</span>) apparently had some kind of church work to do (care for the sick, the orphans, etc.).</p>
<p><b>The wife of one man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενος ανδρος γυνη</span></span>). Widows on this list must not be married a second time. This interpretation is not so clear for <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:2">1 Timothy 3:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:12">1 Timothy 3:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:6">Titus 1:6</span>. </p>
