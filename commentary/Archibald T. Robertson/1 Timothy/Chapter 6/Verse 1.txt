<p><b> Under the yoke </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο ζυγον</span></span>). As slaves (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δουλο</span></span>, bondsmen). Perhaps under heathen masters (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:18">1 Peter 2:18</span>). For the slave problem, see also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:1">1 Timothy 6:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:22">Colossians 3:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:5">Ephesians 6:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:9">Titus 2:9</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:29">Matthew 11:29</span> for Christ's "yoke" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζυγον</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζευγνυμ</span></span>, to join).</p>
<p><b>Their own masters </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους ιδιους δεσποτας</span></span>). That is always where the shoe pinches. Our "despot" is this very Greek word, the strict correlative of slave (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δουλος</span></span>), while <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυριος</span></span> has a wider outlook. Old word only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:9">Titus 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:21">2 Timothy 2:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:18">1 Peter 2:18</span> for human masters. Applied to God in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:29">Luke 2:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:24">Acts 4:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:29">Acts 4:29</span> and to Christ in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:1">2 Peter 2:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>The name of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το ονομα του θεου</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:24">Romans 2:24</span>. If the heathen could say that Christian slaves were not as dependable as non-Christian slaves. Negative purpose with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη</span></span> and present passive subjunctive (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλασφημητα</span></span>). </p>
