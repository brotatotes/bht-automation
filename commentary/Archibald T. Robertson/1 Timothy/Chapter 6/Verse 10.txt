<p><b> The love of money </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η φιλαργυρια</span></span>). Vulgate, <i>avaritia</i>. Common word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλαργυρος</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:12">2 Timothy 3:12</span>, and that from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλοσ, αργυρος</span></span>), only here in N.T. Refers to verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:9">1 Timothy 6:9</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βουλομενο πλουτειν</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>A root of all kinds of evil </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ριζα παντων των κακων</span></span>). A root (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ριζα</span></span>). Old word, common in literal (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:10">Matthew 3:10</span>) and metaphorical sense (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:11-18">Romans 11:11-18</span>). Field (<i>Ot. Norv</i>.) argues for "the root" as the idea of this predicate without saying that it is the only root. Undoubtedly a proverb that Paul here quotes, attributed to Bion and to Democritus (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την φιλαργυριαν εινα μητροπολιν παντων των κακων</span></span>), where "metropolis" takes the place of "root." Surely men today need no proof of the fact that men and women will commit any sin or crime for money.</p>
<p><b>Reaching after </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορεγομενο</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορεγω</span></span> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:1">1 Timothy 3:1</span>) with genitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ης</span></span> (which).</p>
<p><b>Have been led astray </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεπλανηθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποπλαναω</span></span>, old compound verb, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:22">Mark 13:22</span>.</p>
<p><b>Have pierced themselves through </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαυτους περιεπειραν</span></span>). First aorist active (with reflexive pronoun) of late compound <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιπειρω</span></span>, only here in N.T. Perfective use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ</span></span> (around, completely to pierce).</p>
<p><b>With many sorrows </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδυναις πολλαις</span></span>). Instrumental case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδυνη</span></span> (consuming, eating grief). In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:2">Romans 9:2</span>. </p>
