<p><b> Fight the good fight </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγωνιζου τον καλον αγωνα</span></span>). Cognate accusative with present middle imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγωνιζω</span></span>, Pauline word (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:25">1 Corinthians 9:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:29">Colossians 1:29</span>).</p>
<p><b>Lay hold on </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλαβου</span></span>). Second (ingressive) aorist middle imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλαμβανω</span></span>, "get a grip on." See same verb with genitive also in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:19">1 Timothy 6:19</span>.</p>
<p><b>Thou wast called </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκληθης</span></span>). First aorist passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλεω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:9">1 Corinthians 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:15">Colossians 3:15</span>.</p>
<p><b>The good confession </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την καλην ομολογιαν</span></span>). Cognate accusative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωμολογησας</span></span> (first aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομολογεω</span></span>, the public confession in baptism which many witnessed. See it also in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:13">1 Timothy 6:13</span> of Jesus. </p>
