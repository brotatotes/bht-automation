<p><b> That thou keep </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρησα σε</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρεω</span></span>, with accusative of general reference (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σε</span></span>) in indirect command after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραγγελλω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Without spot </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασπιλον</span></span>). Late adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπιλος</span></span>, spot, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:27">Ephesians 5:27</span>). In inscription and papyri.</p>
<p><b>Without reproach </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεπιλημπτον</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:2">1 Timothy 3:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:7">1 Timothy 5:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>Until the appearing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεχρ της επιφανειας</span></span>). "Until the epiphany" (the second epiphany or coming of Christ). Late word in inscriptions for important event like the epiphany of Caligula, in the papyri as a medical term. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:18">2 Thessalonians 2:18</span> we have both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιφανεια</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρουσια</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:13">Titus 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:10">2 Timothy 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:1">2 Timothy 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:8">2 Timothy 4:8</span>. </p>
