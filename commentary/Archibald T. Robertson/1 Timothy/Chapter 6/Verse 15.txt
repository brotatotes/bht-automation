<p><b> In its own times </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιροις ιδιοις</span></span>). Locative case. May be "in his own times." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:6">1 Timothy 2:6</span>. Clearly not for us to figure out.</p>
<p><b>Who is the blessed and only Potentate </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο μακαριος κα μονος δυναστης</span></span>). "The happy and alone Potentate." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Δυναστης</span></span>, old word, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:52">Luke 1:52</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:27">Acts 8:27</span> (the Eunuch). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:11">1 Timothy 1:11</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακαριος</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>The King of kings </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο βασιλευς των βασιλευοντων</span></span>). "The King of those who rule as kings." Oriental title. So with "Lord of lords." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:16">Revelation 10:16</span>. </p>
