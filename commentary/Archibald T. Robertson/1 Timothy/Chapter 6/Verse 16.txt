<p><b> Who only hath immortality </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο μονος εχων αθανασιαν</span></span>). "The one who alone has immortality." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αθανασια</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθανατος</span></span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θανατος</span></span>), old word, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:53">1 Corinthians 15:53</span>. Domitian demanded that he be addressed as "<i>Dominus et Deus noster</i>." Emperor worship may be behind the use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μονος</span></span> (alone) here.</p>
<p><b>Unapproachable </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απροσιτον</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+104:2">Psalms 104:2</span>. Late compound verbal adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσ, ιενα</span></span>, to go). Here only in N.T. Literary <i>Koine</i> word.</p>
<p><b>Nor can see </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδε ιδειν δυνατα</span></span>). See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αορατον</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:15">Colossians 1:15</span> and also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:18">John 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:27">Matthew 11:27</span>. The "amen" marks the close of the doxology as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:17">1 Timothy 1:17</span>. </p>
