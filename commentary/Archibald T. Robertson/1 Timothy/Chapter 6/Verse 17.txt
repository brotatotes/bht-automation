<p><b> In this present world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω νυν αιων</span></span>). "In the now age," in contrast with the future.</p>
<p><b>That they be not high-minded </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη υψηλοφρονειν</span></span>). Present active infinitive with negative in indirect command after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραγγελλε</span></span>, "not to be high-minded." Only instance of the word save some MSS. of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:20">Romans 11:20</span> (for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη υψηλαφρονε</span></span>) and a scholion on Pindar.</p>
<p><b>Have their hope set </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηλπικενα</span></span>). Perfect active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελπιζω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>On the uncertainty of riches </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ πλουτου αδηλοτητ</span></span>). Literary <i>Koine</i> word (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδηλοτης</span></span>), only here in N.T. A "vigorous oxymoron" (White). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:4">Romans 6:4</span>. Riches have wings.</p>
<p><b>But on God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ' επ θεω</span></span>). He alone is stable, not wealth.</p>
<p><b>Richly all things to enjoy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντα πλουσιως εις απολαυσιν</span></span>). "A lavish emphasis to the generosity of God" (Parry). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Απολαυσις</span></span> is old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολαυω</span></span>, to enjoy, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:25">Hebrews 11:25</span>. </p>
