<p><b> Let not despise them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη καταφρονειτωσαν</span></span>). Negative imperative active third plural of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταφρονεω</span></span>, to think down on. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:12">1 Timothy 4:12</span>. He must not presume on the equality of Christian brotherhood not allowed by the state's laws. Some of these Christian slaves might be pastors of churches to which the master belonged. For the difficulty of the Christian master's position, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:22">1 Corinthians 7:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:16">Philemon 1:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>But rather </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα μαλλον</span></span>). Render the Christian Master better service.</p>
<p><b>They that partake of the benefit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο της ενεργεσιας αντιλαμβανομενο</span></span>). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευεργεσιας</span></span> (genitive case after participle) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:9">Acts 4:9</span>, only other N.T. example of this old word. Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντιλαμβανω</span></span>, old verb, to take in turn, to lay fast hold of, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:54">Luke 1:54</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:35">Acts 20:35</span>. </p>
