<p><b> Teacheth a different doctrine </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετεροδιδασκαλε</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:3">1 Timothy 1:3</span> for this verb, present active indicative here in condition of first class.</p>
<p><b>Consenteth not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη προσερχετα</span></span>). Also condition of first class with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Προσερχομα</span></span> (old verb, to come to, to approach, with dative) is common enough in N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:16">Hebrews 4:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:25">Hebrews 7:25</span>, etc.), but in the metaphorical sense of coming to one's ideas, assenting to, here only in N.T., but is so used in Philo and Irenaeus (Ellicott).</p>
<p><b>Sound words </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υγιαινουσιν λογοις</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:10">1 Timothy 1:10</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υγιαινω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>The words of our Lord Jesus Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις του κυριου ημων Ιησου Χριστου</span></span>). Either subjective genitive (the words from the Lord Jesus, a collection of his sayings in Lock's opinion like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:18">1 Timothy 5:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:35">Acts 20:35</span>, at least in the Spirit of Jesus as <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:7">Acts 16:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:23">1 Corinthians 11:23</span>) or objective genitive about Jesus like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:8">2 Timothy 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:18">1 Corinthians 1:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>According to godliness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα ευσεβειαν</span></span>). Promoting (designed for) godliness as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:1">Titus 1:1</span>. </p>
