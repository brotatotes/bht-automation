<p><b> He is puffed up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τετυφωτα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυφοω</span></span>, for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:6">1 Timothy 3:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Knowing nothing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηδεν επισταμενος</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισταμα</span></span>. Ignorance is a frequent companion of conceit.</p>
<p><b>Doting </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νοσων</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νοσεω</span></span>, to be sick, to be morbid over, old word, only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Disputes of words </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογομαχιας</span></span>). Our "logomachy." From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογομαχεω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:14">2 Timothy 2:14</span>), and that from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογος</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαχομα</span></span>, to fight over words, late and rare word, here only in N.T. See Plato (<i>Tim</i>. 1085 F) for "wars in words" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαχας εν λογοις</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Whereof </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ ων</span></span>). "From which things."</p>
<p><b>Surmisings </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπονοια</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπονοεω</span></span>, to surmise, to suspect (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:18">Acts 25:18</span>), only here in N.T. All these words are akin (envy, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φθονος</span></span>, strife, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερις</span></span>, railings or slanders, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλασφημια</span></span>), all products of an ignorant and conceited mind. </p>
