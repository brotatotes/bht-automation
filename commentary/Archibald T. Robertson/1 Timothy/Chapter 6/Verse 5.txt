<p><b> Wranglings </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαπαρατριβα</span></span>). Late and rare (Clem. of Alex.) double compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span>, mutual or thorough, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρατριβα</span></span>, irritations or rubbings alongside). "Mutual irritations" (Field).</p>
<p><b>Corrupted in mind </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διεφθαρμενων τον νουν</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαφθειρω</span></span>, to corrupt, genitive case agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωπων</span></span> (of men) and retaining the accusative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον νουν</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Bereft of the truth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεστερημενων της αληθειας</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστερεω</span></span>, old verb (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:8">1 Corinthians 6:8</span>) with the ablative case after it (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αληθειας</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>A way of gain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορισμον</span></span>). Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποριζω</span></span>, to provide, to gain. Only here in N.T. "Rich Christians." Predicate accusative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εινα</span></span> (indirect assertion) in apposition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευσεβειαν</span></span>, the accusative of general reference. </p>
