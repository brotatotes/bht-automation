<p><b> Out of so great a death </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ τηλικουτου θανατου</span></span>). He had considered himself as good as dead.</p>
<p><b>Delivered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερυσατο</span></span>)</p>
<p><b>--will deliver </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρυσετα</span></span>). Old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρυω</span></span>, middle, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρυομα</span></span>, draw oneself, as out of a pit, rescue. So Paul faces death without fear.</p>
<p><b>On whom we have set our hope </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις ον ηλπικαμεν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελπιζω</span></span>. We still have that hope, emphasized by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετ ρυσετα</span></span> (he will still deliver). </p>
