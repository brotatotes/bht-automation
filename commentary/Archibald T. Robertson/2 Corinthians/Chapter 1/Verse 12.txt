<p><b> Glorying </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καυχησις</span></span>). Act of glorying, while in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:14">2 Corinthians 1:14</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καυχημα</span></span> is the thing boasted of.</p>
<p><b>The testimony of our conscience </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το μαρτυριον της συνειδησεως ημων</span></span>). In apposition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καυχησις</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Sincerity of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειλικρινεια του θεου</span></span>). Like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιοσυνη θεου</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:17">Romans 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:21">Romans 3:21</span>), the God-kind of righteousness. So the God-kind (genitive case) of sincerity. Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειλικρινης</span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:8">1 Corinthians 5:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>Not in fleshly wisdom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ εν σοφια σαρκικη</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:17">1 Corinthians 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:4">1 Corinthians 2:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:13">1 Corinthians 2:13</span>. Paul uses <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαρκικος</span></span> five times and it occurs only twice elsewhere in N.T. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:3">1 Corinthians 3:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>We behaved ourselves </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεστραφημεν</span></span>). Second aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναστρεφω</span></span>, old verb, to turn back, to turn back and forth, to walk. Here the passive is used as in late Greek as if middle.</p>
<p><b>More abundantly to you-ward </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περισσοτερως προς υμας</span></span>). They had more abundant opportunity to observe how scrupulous Paul was (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:11">Acts 18:11</span>). </p>
