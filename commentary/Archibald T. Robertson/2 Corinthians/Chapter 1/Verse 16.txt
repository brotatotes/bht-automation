<p><b> And again </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα παλιν</span></span>). This would have been the second benefit or joy. But he changed his plans and did not make that trip directly to Corinth, but came on to Macedonia first (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:21">Acts 19:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:1">Acts 20:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:2">1 Corinthians 16:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:12">2 Corinthians 2:12</span>).</p>
<p><b>To be set forward by you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υφ' υμων προπεμφθηνα</span></span>). First aorist passive infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προπεμπω</span></span>. Paul uses this same verb in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:24">Romans 15:24</span> for the same service by the Roman Christians on his proposed trip to Spain. The Corinthians, especially the anti-Pauline party, took advantage of Paul's change of plans to criticize him sharply for vacillation and flippancy. How easy it is to find fault with the preacher! So Paul has to explain his conduct. </p>
