<p><b> But I call God for a witness upon my soul </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εγω δε μαρτυρα τον θεον επικαλουμα επ την εμην ψυχην</span></span>). Solemn attestation, "calling heaven to witness is frequent in literature from Homer onwards" (Plummer). Thus God is described above (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:5">1 Thessalonians 2:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:10">1 Thessalonians 2:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:9">Romans 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:20">Galatians 1:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:8">Philippians 1:8</span>).</p>
<p><b>To spare you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φειδομενος υμων</span></span>). Present middle participle (causal rather than final) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φειδομα</span></span>, old verb, to hold back, to spare. Ablative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμων</span></span>. </p>
