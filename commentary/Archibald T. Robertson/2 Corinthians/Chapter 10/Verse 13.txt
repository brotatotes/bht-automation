<p><b> Beyond our measure </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τα αμετρα</span></span>). "Into the unmeasured things," "the illimitable." Old word, here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Of the province </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του κανονος</span></span>). Old word (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καννα</span></span> like Hebrew) a reed, a measuring rod. Numerous papyri examples for measuring rod and rules (our word canon). Only twice in N.T., here (also verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:15">2 Corinthians 10:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:16">2 Corinthians 10:16</span>) and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:16">Galatians 6:16</span> (rule to walk by).</p>
<p><b>To reach even unto you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφικεσθα αχρ κα υμων</span></span>). Second aorist middle infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφικνεομα</span></span>, old verb, only here and verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:14">2 Corinthians 10:14</span> in N.T. Paul's measuring-rod extends to Corinth. </p>
