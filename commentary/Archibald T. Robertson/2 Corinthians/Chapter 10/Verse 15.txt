<p><b> In other men's labours </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αλλοτριοις κοποις</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αλλοτριος</span></span> means belonging to another as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:12">Luke 16:12</span>. Paul founded the church in Corinth.</p>
<p><b>As your faith groweth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυξανομενης της πιστεως</span></span>). Genitive absolute of the present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυξανω</span></span>, to grow.</p>
<p><b>We shall be magnified </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεγαλυνθηνα</span></span>). First aorist passive infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεγαλυνω</span></span>, old verb (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:46">Luke 1:46</span>) to make great (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:20">Philippians 1:20</span> of Christ). Indirect discourse after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελπιδα</span></span> (hope) with the construction of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελπιζω</span></span>, to hope. </p>
