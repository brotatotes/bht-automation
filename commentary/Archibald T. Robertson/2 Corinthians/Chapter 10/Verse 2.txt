<p><b> I beseech </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεομα</span></span>). So here, but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρακαλω</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:1">2 Corinthians 10:1</span>. Perhaps, "I beg" suits the new turn here.</p>
<p><b>That I may not when present show courage </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το μη παρων θαρρησα</span></span>). Articular infinitive (aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θαρρεω</span></span>) in the accusative case with negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> the direct object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεομα</span></span>. Literally, "I beg the not when present (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρων</span></span> nominative present participle agreeing with subject of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θαρρω</span></span> in spite of being in the accusative infinitive clause, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το μη θαρρησα</span></span>) showing courage." The example of humility in Christ makes Paul drop "from magisterial exhortation to earnest entreaty" (Plummer).</p>
<p><b>As if we walked according to the flesh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως κατα σαρκα περιπατουντας</span></span>). Another sneering charge as made plain by the use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως</span></span> with the participle for the alleged reason. </p>
