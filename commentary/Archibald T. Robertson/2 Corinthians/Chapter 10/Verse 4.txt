<p><b> The weapons of our warfare </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα οπλα της στρατειας</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Στρατεια</span></span> (old word, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:18">1 Timothy 1:18</span>) is</p>
<p><b>campaign </b> and not army as some MSS. have (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρατια</span></span>). But both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρατεια</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρατια</span></span> occur in the papyri for the same word (Deissmann, <i>Bible Studies</i>, p. 181f.). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπλα</span></span> (Latin <i>arma</i>) see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:7">2 Corinthians 6:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:13">Romans 6:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:12">Romans 13:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>Of the flesh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαρκικα</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:3">1 Corinthians 3:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:12">2 Corinthians 1:12</span>. They had accused him of artifices and craft.</p>
<p><b>Mighty before God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυνατα τω θεω</span></span>). This dative of personal interest (ethical dative) can be like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αστειος τω θεω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:20">Acts 7:20</span>), in God's eyes, as it looks to God.</p>
<p><b>To the casting down of strongholds </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς καθαιρεσιν οχυρωματων</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Καθαιρεσις</span></span> is old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθαιρεω</span></span>, to take down, to tear down walls and buildings. Carries on the military metaphor. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Οχυρωμα</span></span> is old word, common in the Apocrypha, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οχυροω</span></span>, to fortify, and that from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οχυρος</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>, to hold fast). Nowhere else in N.T. In Cilicia the Romans had to tear down many rocky forts in their attacks on the pirates. </p>
