<p><b> Casting down imaginations </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογισμους καθαιρουντες</span></span>). The same military figure (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθαιρεσις</span></span>) and the present active participle agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρατευομεθα</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:3">2 Corinthians 10:3</span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:4">2 Corinthians 10:4</span> a parenthesis). The reasonings or imaginations (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογισμους</span></span>, old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογιζομα</span></span>, to reckon, only here in N.T. and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:15">Romans 2:15</span>) are treated as forts or citadels to be conquered.</p>
<p><b>Every high thing that is exalted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παν υψωμα επαιρομενον</span></span>). Same metaphor. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hυψωμα</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υψοω</span></span> is late <i>Koine</i> word (in LXX, Plutarch, Philo, papyri) for height and that figure carried on by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαιρομενον</span></span>. Paul aims to pull down the top-most perch of audacity in their reasonings against the knowledge of God. We need Paul's skill and courage today.</p>
<p><b>Bringing every thought into captivity </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιχμαλωτιζοντες παν νοημα</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιχμαλωτιζω</span></span>, common <i>Koine</i> verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιχμαλωτος</span></span>, captive in war (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιχμη</span></span>, spear, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλωτος</span></span> verbal of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλισκομα</span></span>, to be taken). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:24">Luke 21:24</span>. Paul is the most daring of thinkers, but he lays all his thoughts at the feet of Jesus. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νοημα</span></span> (device) see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:11">2 Corinthians 2:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>To the obedience of Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις την υπακοην του Χριστου</span></span>). Objective genitive, "to the obedience unto Christ." That is Paul's conception of intellectual liberty, freedom in Christ. Deissmann (<i>St. Paul</i>, p. 141) calls this "the mystic genitive." </p>
