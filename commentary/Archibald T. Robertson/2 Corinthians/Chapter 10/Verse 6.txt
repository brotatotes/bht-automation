<p><b> Being in readiness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ετοιμω εχοντες</span></span>). This very idiom occurs in Polybius, Philo, etc. "Holding in readiness." In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:14">2 Corinthians 12:14</span> we have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετοιμως εχω</span></span> for the same idea (adverb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετοιμως</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Disobedience </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρακοην</span></span>). Rare word (Plato, papyri) hearing amiss (aside), failing to hear, refusing to heed (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:17">Matthew 18:17</span> for same idea in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρακουω</span></span>). In N.T. only here; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:19">Romans 5:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:2">Hebrews 2:2</span>. In contrast with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπακοη</span></span> (obedience) rather than the common <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απειθια</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:30">Romans 11:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:32">Romans 11:32</span>).</p>
<p><b>When your obedience shall be fulfilled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν πληρωθη υμων η υπακοη</span></span>). Indefinite temporal clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν</span></span> and first aorist passive subjunctive. Paul expects that the whole church will become obedient to Christ's will soon as came true. </p>
