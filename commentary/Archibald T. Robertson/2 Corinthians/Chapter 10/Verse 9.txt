<p><b> As if I would terrify you by my letters </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως αν εκφοβειν υμας δια των επιστολων</span></span>). This use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως αν</span></span> with the infinitive is seen in the papyri (Moulton, <i>Prolegomena</i>, p. 167) and it is not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span> in the apodosis (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, pp. 974, 1040). The active of this old compound verb means to frighten, to terrify. Here only in N.T. It is common in the LXX (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+7:14">Job 7:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+33:16">Job 33:16</span>). Note plural (letters) here and cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:9">1 Corinthians 5:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:3">2 Corinthians 2:3</span>. </p>
