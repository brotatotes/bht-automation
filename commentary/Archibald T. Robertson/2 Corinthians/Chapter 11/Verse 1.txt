<p><b> Would that ye could bear with me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφελον ανειχεσθε μου</span></span>). <i>Koine</i> way of expressing a wish about the present, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφελον</span></span> (as a conjunction, really second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφειλω</span></span> without augment) and the imperfect indicative instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειθε</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε γαρ</span></span> (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 1003). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:15">Revelation 3:15</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:12">Galatians 5:12</span> for future indicative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφελον</span></span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:8">1 Corinthians 4:8</span> for aorist. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μου</span></span> is ablative case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανειχεσθε</span></span> (direct middle, hold yourselves back from me). There is a touch of irony here.</p>
<p><b>Bear with me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεχεσθε μου</span></span>). Either imperative middle or present middle indicative (ye do bear with me). Same form.</p>
<p><b>In a little foolishness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μικρον τ αφροσυνης</span></span>). Accusative of general reference (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μικρον τ</span></span>). "Some little foolishness" (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφρων</span></span>, foolish). Old word only in this chapter in N.T. </p>
