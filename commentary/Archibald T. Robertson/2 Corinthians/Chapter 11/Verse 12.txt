<p><b> That I may cut off occasion </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα εκκοψω την αφορμην</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκκοπτω</span></span>, old verb to cut out or off (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:10">Matthew 3:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:30">Matthew 5:30</span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:12">2 Corinthians 5:12</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφορμην</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>From them which desire an occasion </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των θελοντων αφορμην</span></span>). Ablative case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκκοψω</span></span>. There are always some hunting for occasions to start something against preachers.</p>
<p><b>They may be found </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρεθωσιν</span></span>). First aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρισκω</span></span>, to find with final conjunction <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>. </p>
