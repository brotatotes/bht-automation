<p><b> False apostles </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευδαποστολο</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευδης</span></span>, false, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστολος</span></span>. Paul apparently made this word (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:2">Revelation 2:2</span>). In verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:26">2 Corinthians 11:26</span> we have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευδαδελφος</span></span>, a word of like formation (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:4">Galatians 2:4</span>). See also <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευδοχριστο</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευδοπροφητα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:22">Mark 13:22</span>.</p>
<p><b>Deceitful </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δολιο</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δολος</span></span> (lure, snare), only here in N.T. (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:18">Romans 16:18</span>).</p>
<p><b>Fashioning themselves </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετασχηματιζομενο</span></span>). Present middle (direct) participle of the old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετασχηματιζω</span></span> for which see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:6">1 Corinthians 4:6</span>. Masquerading as apostles of Christ by putting on the outward habiliments, posing as ministers of Christ ("gentlemen of the cloth," nothing but cloth). Paul plays with this verb in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:13">2 Corinthians 11:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:14">2 Corinthians 11:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:15">2 Corinthians 11:15</span>. </p>
