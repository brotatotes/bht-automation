<p><b> In journeyings </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδοιποριαις</span></span>). Locative case of old word, only here in N.T. and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:6">John 4:6</span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδοιπορος</span></span>, wayfarer.</p>
<p><b>In perils </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κινδυνοις</span></span>). Locative case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κινδυνος</span></span>, old word for danger or peril. In N.T. only this verse and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:35">Romans 8:35</span>. The repetition here is very effective without the preposition <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> (in) and without conjunctions (asyndeton). They are in contrasted pairs. The rivers of Asia Minor are still subject to sudden swellings from floods in the mountains. Cicero and Pompey won fame fighting the Cilician pirates and robbers (note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ληιστων</span></span>, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλεπτων</span></span>, thieves, brigands or bandits on which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:55">Matthew 26:55</span>). The Jewish perils (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ γενους</span></span>, from my race) can be illustrated in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:23">Acts 9:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:29">Acts 9:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:50">Acts 13:50</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:5">Acts 14:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:5">Acts 17:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:13">Acts 17:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:12">Acts 18:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:12">Acts 23:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:27">Acts 24:27</span>, and they were all perils in the city also. Perils from the Gentiles (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ εθνων</span></span>) we know in Philippi (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:20">Acts 16:20</span>) and in Ephesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:23">Acts 19:23</span>). Travel in the mountains and in the wilderness was perilous in spite of the great Roman highways.</p>
<p><b>Among false brethren </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ψευδαδελφοις</span></span>). Chapters <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:2">2 Corinthians 11:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:11">2 Corinthians 11:11</span> throw a lurid light on this aspect of the subject. </p>
