<p><b> Rude in speech </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδιωτης τω λογω</span></span>). Locative case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδιωτης</span></span> for which word see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:13">Acts 4:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:16">1 Corinthians 14:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:23">1 Corinthians 14:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:24">1 Corinthians 14:24</span>. The Greeks regarded a man as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδιωτης</span></span> who just attended to his own affairs (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα ιδια</span></span>) and took no part in public life. Paul admits that he is not a professional orator (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:10">2 Corinthians 10:10</span>), but denies that he is unskilled in knowledge (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ' ου τη γνωσε</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Among all men </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν πασιν</span></span>). He has made his mastery of the things of Christ plain among all men. He knew his subject. </p>
