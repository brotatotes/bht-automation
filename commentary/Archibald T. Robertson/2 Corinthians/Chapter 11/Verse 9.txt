<p><b> I was not a burden to any man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου κατεναρκησα ουθενος</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταναρκαω</span></span>. Jerome calls this word one of Paul's <i>cilicisms</i> which he brought from Cilicia. But the word occurs in Hippocrates for growing quite stiff and may be a medical term in popular use. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ναρκαω</span></span> means to become numb, torpid, and so a burden. It is only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:13">2 Corinthians 12:13</span>. Paul "did not benumb the Corinthians by his demand for pecuniary aid" (Vincent).</p>
<p><b>From being burdensome </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αβαρη</span></span>). Old adjective, free from weight or light (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαρος</span></span>, weight) . See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:9">1 Thessalonians 2:9</span> for same idea. Paul kept himself independent. </p>
