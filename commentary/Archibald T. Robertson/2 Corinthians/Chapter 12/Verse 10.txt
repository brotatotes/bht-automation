<p><b> Wherefore I take pleasure </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διο ευδοκω</span></span>). For this noble word see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:17">Matthew 3:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:8">2 Corinthians 5:8</span>. The enemies of Paul will have a hard time now in making Paul unhappy by persecutions even unto death (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:20-26">Philippians 1:20-26</span>). He is not courting martyrdom, but he does not fear it or anything that is "for Christ's sake" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ Χριστου</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>For when </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν γαρ</span></span>). "For whenever," indefinite time.</p>
<p><b>Then I am strong </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοτε δυνατος ειμ</span></span>). At that very time, but not in myself, but in the fresh access of power from Christ for the emergency. </p>
