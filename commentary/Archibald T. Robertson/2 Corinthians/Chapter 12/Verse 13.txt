<p><b> Wherein ye were made inferior </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ησσωθητε</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ησσοομα</span></span>, the text of Aleph B D instead of the usual <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηττηθητε</span></span> from the common <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ητταομα</span></span> to be inferior or less from the comparative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηττων</span></span>. See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ησσων</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:15">2 Corinthians 12:15</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hο</span></span> is the neuter accusative with the passive verb (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 479).</p>
<p><b>Forgive me this wrong </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρισασθε μο την αδικιαν ταυτην</span></span>). Consummate irony to the stingy element in this church (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:9">2 Corinthians 11:9</span>). </p>
