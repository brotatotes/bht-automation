<p><b> Did I take advantage </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επλεονεκτησα</span></span>). Paul goes right to the point without hedging. For this verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλεον</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>, to have more, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:11">2 Corinthians 2:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:2">2 Corinthians 7:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>By any one of them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τινα--δι' αυτου</span></span>). An anacoluthon for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τινα</span></span> is left in the accusative without a verb and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι' αυτου</span></span> takes up the idea, "as to any one by him."</p>
<p><b>Whom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ων</span></span>). The genitive relative is attracted from the accusative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ους</span></span> into the case of the unexpressed antecedent <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτον</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μη</span></span> expects the negative answer as does <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μητ</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:18">2 Corinthians 12:18</span>. </p>
