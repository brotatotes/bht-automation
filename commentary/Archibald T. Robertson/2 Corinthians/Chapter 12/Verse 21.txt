<p><b> When I come again </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παλιν ελθοντος μου</span></span>). Genitive absolute. Paul assumes it as true.</p>
<p><b>Lest my God humble me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη ταπεινωση με ο θεος μου</span></span>). Negative final clause (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> and first aorist active subjunctive), going back to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φοβουμα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:20">2 Corinthians 12:20</span>. He means a public humiliation as his fear. The conduct of the church had been a real humiliation whether he refers to a previous visit or not.</p>
<p><b>That have sinned heretofore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των προημαρτηκοτων</span></span>). Genitive plural of the articular perfect active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προαμαρτανω</span></span> to emphasize continuance of their sinful state as opposed to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη μετανοησαντων</span></span> (did not repent) in the aorist tense. </p>
