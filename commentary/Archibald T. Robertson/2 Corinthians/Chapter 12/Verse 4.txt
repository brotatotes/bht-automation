<p><b> Into Paradise </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις παραδεισον</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:43">Luke 23:43</span> for this interesting word. Paul apparently uses paradise as the equivalent of the third heaven in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:2">2 Corinthians 12:2</span>. Some Jews (<i>Book of the Secrets of Enoch</i>, chapter viii) make Paradise in the third heaven. The rabbis had various ideas (two heavens, three, seven). We need not commit Paul to any "celestial gradation" (Vincent).</p>
<p><b>Unspeakable words </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρρητα ρηματα</span></span>). Old verbal adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρητος</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρεω</span></span>), only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Not lawful </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ εξον</span></span>). Copula <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστιν</span></span> omitted. Hence Paul does</p>
<p><b>not </b> give these words. </p>
