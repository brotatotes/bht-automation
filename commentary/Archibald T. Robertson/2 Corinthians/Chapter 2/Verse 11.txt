<p><b> That no advantage may be gained over us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη πλεονεκτηθωμεν</span></span>). First aorist passive subjunctive after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη</span></span> (negative purpose) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλεονεκτεω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλεονεκτης</span></span>, a covetous man (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:10">1 Corinthians 5:10</span>), to take advantage of, to gain, to overreach. In N.T. only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:6">1 Thessalonians 4:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:11">2 Corinthians 2:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:2">2 Corinthians 7:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:17">2 Corinthians 12:17</span>. "That we may not be overreached by Satan."</p>
<p><b>His devices </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτου τα νοηματα</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Νοημα</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νοεω</span></span> to use the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νους</span></span> is old word, especially for evil plans and purposes as here. </p>
