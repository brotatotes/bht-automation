<p><b> To Troas </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις την Τρωιαδα</span></span>). Luke does not mention this stop at Troas on the way from Ephesus to Macedonia (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:1">Acts 20:1</span>), though he does mention two other visits there (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:8">Acts 16:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:6">Acts 20:6</span>).</p>
<p><b>When a door was opened unto me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυρας μο ανεωιγμενης</span></span>). Genitive absolute with second perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανοιγνυμ</span></span>. Paul used this very metaphor in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:9">1 Corinthians 16:9</span>. He will use it again in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+4:3">Colossians 4:3</span>. Here was an open door that he could not enter. </p>
