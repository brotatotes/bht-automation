<p><b> Who then? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα τισ?</span></span>). For this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span> see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:26">Mark 10:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:36">John 9:36</span>. The <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span> accepts the condition (first class <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ει--λυπω</span></span>) and shows the paradox that follows. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Λυπεω</span></span> is old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυπη</span></span> (sorrow) in causative sense, to make sorry.</p>
<p><b>Maketh glad </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευφραινων</span></span>). Present active participle of old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ</span></span>, well, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φρην</span></span>, mind, to make joyful, causative idea like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυπεω</span></span>. </p>
