<p><b> Anguish </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνοχης</span></span>). Ablative case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span> (out of). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεχω</span></span>, to hold together. So contraction of heart (Cicero, <i>contractio animi</i>), a spiritual <i>angina pectoris</i>. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:25">Luke 21:25</span>.</p>
<p><b>With many tears </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια πολλων δακρυων</span></span>). He dictated that letter "through tears" (accompanied by tears). Paul was a man of heart. He writes to the Philippians with weeping (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλαιων</span></span>) over the enemies of the Cross of Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:18">Philippians 3:18</span>). He twice mentions his tears in his speech at Miletus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:19-31">Acts 20:19-31</span>).</p>
<p><b>But that ye might know the love </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα την αγαπην ινα γνωτε</span></span>). Proleptic position of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπην</span></span> and ingressive second aorist active subjunctive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνωτε</span></span>, come to know. </p>
