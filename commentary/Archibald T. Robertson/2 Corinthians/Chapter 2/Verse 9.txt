<p><b> That I might know the proof of you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα γνω την δοκιμην υμων</span></span>). Ingressive second aorist active subjunctive, come to know. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Δοκιμη</span></span> is proof by testing. Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκιμος</span></span> and is in Dioscorides, medical writer in reign of Hadrian. Earliest use in Paul and only in him in N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:9">2 Corinthians 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:2">2 Corinthians 8:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:13">2 Corinthians 9:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+13:3">2 Corinthians 13:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:4">Romans 5:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:22">Philippians 2:22</span>).</p>
<p><b>Obedient </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπηκοο</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπακουω</span></span>, to give ear. In N.T. only in Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:9">2 Corinthians 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:8">Philippians 2:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:39">Acts 7:39</span>). </p>
