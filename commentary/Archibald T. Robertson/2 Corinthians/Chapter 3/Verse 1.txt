<p><b> To commend ourselves? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαυτους συνιστανειν?</span></span>). Late (<i>Koine</i>) form of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνιστημ</span></span>, to place one with another, to introduce, to commend. Paul is sensitive over praising himself, though his enemies compelled him to do it.</p>
<p><b>Epistles of commendation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συστατικων επιστολων</span></span>). Late verbal adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνιστημ</span></span> and often in the papyri and in just this sense. In the genitive case here after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρηιζομεν</span></span>. Such letters were common as seen in the papyri (Deissmann, <i>Light from the Ancient East</i>, p. 226). N.T. examples of commending individuals by letters occur in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:25">Acts 15:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:27">Acts 18:27</span> (Apollos), <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:10">1 Corinthians 16:10</span> (Timothy); <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:1">Romans 16:1</span> (Phoebe with the verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνιστημ</span></span>); <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+4:10">Colossians 4:10</span> (Mark); <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:22">2 Corinthians 8:22</span> (Titus and his companion). </p>
