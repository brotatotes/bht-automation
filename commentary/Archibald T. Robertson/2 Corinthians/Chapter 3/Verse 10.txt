<p><b> In this respect </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τουτω τω μερε</span></span>). The glory on the face of Moses was temporary, though real, and passed away (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:7">2 Corinthians 3:7</span>), a type of the dimming of the glory of the old dispensation by the brightness of the new. The moon makes a dim light after the sun rises, "is not glorified" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου δεδοξαστα</span></span>, perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξαζω</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>By reason of the glory that surpasseth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εινεκεν της υπερβαλλουσης δοξης</span></span>). The surpassing (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ-βαλλω</span></span>, throwing beyond) glory. Christ as the Sun of Righteousness has thrown Moses in the shade. Cf. the claims of superiority by Christ in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:5-7">2 Corinthians 3:5-7</span>. </p>
