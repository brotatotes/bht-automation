<p><b> But their minds were hardened </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα επωρωθη τα νοηματα αυτων</span></span>). Their thoughts (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νοηματα</span></span>) literally. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πωροω</span></span> (first aorist passive indicative here) is late verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πωρος</span></span>, hard skin, to cover with thick skin (callus), to petrify. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:52">Mark 6:52</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:17">Mark 8:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>Of the old covenant </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της παλαιας διαθηκης</span></span>). The Old Testament. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Παλαιος</span></span> (ancient) in contrast to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καινος</span></span> (fresh, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:6">2 Corinthians 3:6</span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:52">Matthew 13:52</span>.</p>
<p><b>The same veil </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το αυτο καλυμμα</span></span>). Not that identical veil, but one that has the same effect, that blinds their eyes to the light in Christ. This is the tragedy of modern Judaism.</p>
<p><b>Unlifted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη ανακαλυπτομενον</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανακαλυπτω</span></span>, old verb, to draw back the veil, to unveil.</p>
<p><b>Is done away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταργειτα</span></span>). Same verb as in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:7">2 Corinthians 3:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:11">2 Corinthians 3:11</span>. </p>
