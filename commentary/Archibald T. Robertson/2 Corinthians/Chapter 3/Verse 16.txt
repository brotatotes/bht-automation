<p><b> It shall turn </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιστρεψε</span></span>). The heart of Israel.</p>
<p><b>The veil is taken away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιαιρειτα το καλυμμα</span></span>). Present passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιαιρεω</span></span>, old verb, to take from around, as of anchors (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:40">Acts 27:40</span>), to cut loose (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:13">Acts 28:13</span>), for hope to be taken away (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:20">Acts 27:20</span>). Here Paul has in mind <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+34:34">Exodus 34:34</span> where we find of Moses that <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιηιρειτο το καλυμμα</span></span> (the veil was taken from around his face) whenever he went before the Lord. After the ceremony the veil is taken from around (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περι-</span></span>) the face of the bride. </p>
