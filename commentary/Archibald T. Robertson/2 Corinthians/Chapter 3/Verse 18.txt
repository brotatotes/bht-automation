<p><b> We all </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημεις παντες</span></span>). All of us Christians, not merely ministers.</p>
<p><b>With unveiled face </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανακεκαλυμμενω προσωπω</span></span>). Instrumental case of manner. Unlike and like Moses.</p>
<p><b>Reflecting as in a mirror </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατοπτριζομενο</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατοπτριζω</span></span>, late verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατοπτρον</span></span>, mirror (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα, οπτρον</span></span>, a thing to see with). In Philo (<i>Legis Alleg</i>. iii. 33) the word means beholding as in a mirror and that idea suits also the figure in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:12">1 Corinthians 13:12</span>. There is an inscription of third century B.C. with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγκατοπτρισασθα εις το υδωρ</span></span>, to look at one's reflection in the water. Plutarch uses the active for mirroring or reflecting and Chrysostom takes it so here. Either makes good sense. The point that Paul is making is that we shall not lose the glory as Moses did. But that is true if we keep on beholding or keep on reflecting (present tense). Only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Are transformed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταμορφουμεθα</span></span>). Present passive (are being transformed) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταμορφοω</span></span>, late verb and in papyri. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:2">Matthew 17:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:2">Mark 9:2</span> where it is translated "transfigured." It is the word used for heathen mythological metamorphoses.</p>
<p><b>Into the same image </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την αυτην εικονα</span></span>). Accusative retained with passive verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταμορφουμεθα</span></span>. Into the likeness of God in Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:48-53">1 Corinthians 15:48-53</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:17">Romans 8:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:29">Romans 8:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:4">Colossians 3:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:2">1 John 3:2</span>).</p>
<p><b>As from the Lord the Spirit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθαπερ απο Κυριου πνευματος</span></span>). More likely, "as from the Spirit of the Lord." </p>
