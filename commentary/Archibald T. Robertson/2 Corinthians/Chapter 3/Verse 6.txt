<p><b> Who also made us sufficient for such confidence </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος κα ικανωσεν ημας</span></span>). Late causative verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ικανος</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:5">2 Corinthians 3:5</span>) first aorist active indicative, "who (God) rendered us fit." In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:12">Colossians 1:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>As ministers of a new covenant </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονους καινης διαθηκης</span></span>). Predicate accusative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ικανωσεν</span></span>. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαθηκη</span></span> see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:28">Matthew 26:28</span> and for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονος</span></span> on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:26">Matthew 20:26</span> and for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καινης</span></span> (fresh and effective) on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:38">Luke 5:38</span>. Only God can make us that. </p>
