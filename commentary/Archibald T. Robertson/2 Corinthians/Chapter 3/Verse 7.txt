<p><b> Of death </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του θανατου</span></span>). Subjective genitive, marked by death in its outcome (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:56">1 Corinthians 15:56</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:10">Galatians 3:10</span>). The letter kills.</p>
<p><b>Engraven on stones </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντετυπωμενη λιθοις</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντυποω</span></span>, late verb, to imprint a figure (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυπος</span></span>). Used by Aristias (67) of the "inlaid" work on the table sent by Ptolemy Philadelphus to Jerusalem. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Λιθοις</span></span> in locative case.</p>
<p><b>Came with glory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγενηθη εν δοξη</span></span>). In glory. As it did, condition of first class, assumed as true. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+34:29">Exodus 34:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+34:35">Exodus 34:35</span>.</p>
<p><b>Look steadfastly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ατενισα</span></span>). Late verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ατενης</span></span> (stretched, intent, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεινω</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> intensive) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:20">Luke 4:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:4">Acts 3:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>Was passing away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταργουμενην</span></span>). Late verb, to render of no effect, and present passive participle here as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:6">1 Corinthians 2:6</span>. </p>
