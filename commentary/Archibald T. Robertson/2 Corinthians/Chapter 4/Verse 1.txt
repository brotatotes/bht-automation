<p><b> We faint not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ εγκακουμεν</span></span>). Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγκακεω</span></span>, late verb (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν, κακος</span></span>) to behave badly in, to give in to evil, to lose courage. In Symmachus (LXX), Polybius, and papyri. It is the faint-hearted coward. Paul speaks of himself (literary plural). Can he not speak for all of us? </p>
