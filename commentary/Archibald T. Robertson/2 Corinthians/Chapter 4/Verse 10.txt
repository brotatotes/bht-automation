<p><b> Bearing about </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιφεροντες</span></span>). Ignatius was called <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Θεοφορος</span></span>, God-bearer. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:31">1 Corinthians 15:31</span> where Paul says "I die daily" and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:10">Philippians 3:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:24">Colossians 1:24</span>.</p>
<p><b>The dying of Jesus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την νεκρωσιν του Ιησου</span></span>). Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νεκροω</span></span>, to put to death. In Galen. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:19">Romans 4:19</span>. </p>
