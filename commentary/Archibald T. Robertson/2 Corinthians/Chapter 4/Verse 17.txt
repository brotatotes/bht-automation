<p><b> Our light affliction which is for the moment </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το παραυτικα ελαφρον της θλιπεσεως ημων</span></span>). Literally, "the for the moment (old adverb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραυτικα</span></span>, here only in N.T.) lightness (old word, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:30">Matthew 11:30</span>)."</p>
<p><b>More and more exceedingly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθ' υπερβολην εις υπερβολην</span></span>). Like piling Pelion on Ossa, "according to excess unto excess." See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:31">1 Corinthians 12:31</span>.</p>
<p><b>Eternal weight of glory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιωνιον βαρος δοξης</span></span>). Careful balancing of words in contrast (affliction vs. glory, lightness vs. weight, for the moment vs. eternal). </p>
