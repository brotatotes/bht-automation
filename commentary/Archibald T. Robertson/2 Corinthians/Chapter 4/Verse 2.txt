<p><b> But we have renounced </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα απειπαμεθα</span></span>). Indirect middle second aorist (timeless aorist) indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απειπον</span></span> (defective verb) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> of first aorist ending, to speak forth, to speak off or away from. Common verb in the active, but rare in middle and only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>The hidden things of shame </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα κρυπτα της αισχυνης</span></span>). They do attack the minister. His only safety is in instant and courageous defiance to all the powers of darkness. It is a terrible thing to see a preacher caught in the toils of the tempter.</p>
<p><b>In craftiness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν πανουργια</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πανουργος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παν, εργον</span></span>), a doer of any deed (good or bad), clever, cunning, deceitful. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:23">Luke 20:23</span>.</p>
<p><b>Handling deceitfully </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δολουντες</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δολοω</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δολος</span></span>, deceit (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δελω</span></span>, to catch with bait), old and common verb, in papyri and inscriptions, to ensnare, to corrupt with error. Only here in N.T. Used of adulterating gold or wine.</p>
<p><b>To every conscience of men </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς πασαν συνειδησιν ανθρωπων</span></span>). Not to whim, foible, prejudice. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:1-6">2 Corinthians 3:1-6</span> for "commending" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνιστανοντες</span></span>). </p>
