<p><b> Pressed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θλιβομενο</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θλιβω</span></span>, to press as grapes, to contract, to squeeze. Series of present passive participles here through verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:9">2 Corinthians 4:9</span> that vividly picture Paul's ministerial career.</p>
<p><b>Yet not straitened </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ' ου στενοχωρουμενο</span></span>). Each time the exception is stated by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ' ου</span></span>. From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στενοχωρεω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στενοχωρος</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στενος</span></span>, narrow, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χωρος</span></span>, space), to be in a narrow place, to keep in a tight place. Late verb, in LXX and papyri. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:12">2 Corinthians 6:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>Yet not unto despair </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ' ουκ εξαπορουμενο</span></span>). Late perfective compound with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ-</span></span> of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαπορεω</span></span>. A very effective play on words here, lost, but not lost out. </p>
