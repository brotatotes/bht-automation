<p><b> If--be dissolved </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν--καταλυθη</span></span>). Third class condition, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and first aorist passive subjunctive. The very word used (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλυω</span></span>) for striking down a tent.</p>
<p><b>The earthly house of our tabernacle </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η επιγειος ημων οικια του σκηνους</span></span>). Rather, "If our earthly (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:40">1 Corinthians 15:40</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιγειος</span></span>) house of the tent (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκηνος</span></span>, another form of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκηνη</span></span>, tent, from root <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκα</span></span>, to cover)." Appositive genitive, the house (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικια</span></span>) is the tent.</p>
<p><b>We have </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχομεν</span></span>). Present indicative. We possess the title to it now by faith. "Faith is the title-deed (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποστασις</span></span>) to things hoped for" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:7">Hebrews 11:7</span>).</p>
<p><b>A building from God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικοδομην εκ θεου</span></span>). This <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικοδομη</span></span> (found in Aristotle, Plutarch, LXX, etc., and papyri, though condemned by Atticists) is more substantial than the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκηνος</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Not made with hands </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχειροποιητον</span></span>). Found first in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:58">Mark 14:58</span> in charge against Jesus before the Sanhedrin (both the common verbal <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χειροποιητον</span></span> and the newly made vernacular <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχειροποιητον</span></span>, same verbal with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative). Elsewhere only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:11">Colossians 2:11</span>. Spiritual, eternal home. </p>
