<p><b> The fear of the Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον φοβον του Κυριου</span></span>). Many today regard this a played-out motive, but not so Paul. He has in mind verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:10">2 Corinthians 5:10</span> with the picture of the judgment seat of Christ.</p>
<p><b>We persuade </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειθομεν</span></span>). Conative present active, we try to persuade. It is always hard work.</p>
<p><b>Unto God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεω</span></span>). Dative case. God understands whether men do or not.</p>
<p><b>That we are made manifest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεφανερωσθα</span></span>). Perfect passive infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανεροω</span></span> in indirect discourse after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελπιζω</span></span>. Stand manifested, state of completion. </p>
