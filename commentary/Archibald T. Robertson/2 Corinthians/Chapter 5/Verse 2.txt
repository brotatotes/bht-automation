<p><b> To be clothed upon with our habitation which is from heaven </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το οικητηριον ημων το εξ ουρανου επενδυσασθα</span></span>). First aorist middle infinitive of late verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επενδυω</span></span>, double compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ, εν</span></span>) to put upon oneself. Cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επενδυτης</span></span> for a fisherman's linen blouse or upper garment (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:7">John 21:7</span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Οικητηριον</span></span> is old word used here of the spiritual body as the abode of the spirit. It is a mixed metaphor (putting on as garment the dwelling-place). </p>
