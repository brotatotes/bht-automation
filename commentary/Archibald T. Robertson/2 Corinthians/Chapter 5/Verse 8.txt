<p><b> We are of good courage </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θαρρουμεν</span></span>). Good word for cheer and same root as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θαρσεω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:2">Matthew 9:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:22">Matthew 9:22</span>). Cheer up.</p>
<p><b>Are willing rather </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευδοκουμεν</span></span>). Rather, "We are well-pleased, we prefer" if left to ourselves. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:21">Philippians 1:21</span>. Same <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευδοκεω</span></span> used in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:22">Luke 3:22</span>.</p>
<p><b>To be at home with the Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδημησα προς τον Κυριον</span></span>). First aorist (ingressive) active infinitive, to attain that goal is bliss for Paul. </p>
