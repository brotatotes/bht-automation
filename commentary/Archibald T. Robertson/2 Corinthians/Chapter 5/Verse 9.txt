<p><b> We make it our aim </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλοτιμουμεθα</span></span>). Old and common verb, present middle, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλοτιμος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλοσ, τιμη</span></span>, fond of honour), to act from love of honour, to be ambitious in the good sense (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:11">1 Thessalonians 4:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:9">2 Corinthians 5:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:20">Romans 15:20</span>). The Latin <i>ambitio</i> has a bad sense from <i>ambire</i>, to go both ways to gain one's point.</p>
<p><b>To be well-pleasing to him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευαρεστο αυτω εινα</span></span>). Late adjective that shows Paul's loyalty to Christ, his Captain. Found in several inscriptions in the <i>Koine</i> period (Deissmann, <i>Bible Studies</i>, p. 214; Moulton and Milligan's <i>Vocabulary</i>). </p>
