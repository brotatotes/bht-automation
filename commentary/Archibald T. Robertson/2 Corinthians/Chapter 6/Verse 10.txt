<p><b> Always rejoicing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αε χαιροντες</span></span>). Even in sorrow (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:9">2 Corinthians 11:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:16">1 Thessalonians 5:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:3-5">Romans 5:3-5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:2">Romans 9:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:18">Philippians 2:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:27">Philippians 2:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:1">Philippians 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:4">Philippians 4:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:15">Philippians 4:15</span>).</p>
<p><b>Yet making many rich </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολλους δε πλουτιζοντες</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλουτος</span></span> (wealth), to enrich. Spiritual riches Paul has in mind as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:5">1 Corinthians 1:5</span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:37">Matthew 5:37</span>).</p>
<p><b>As having nothing and yet possessing all things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως μηδεν εχοντες κα παντα κατεχοντες</span></span>). Contrast between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηδεν</span></span> (nothing) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντα</span></span> (all things, cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:22">1 Corinthians 3:22</span>) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span> (to have) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεχω</span></span> (to hold down, to hold fast). Play on words (simple and compound) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:2">2 Corinthians 3:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:8">2 Corinthians 4:8</span>. Climax of Paul's panegyric on the Christian ministry. He now resumes the thread of the story broken off in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:14">2 Corinthians 2:14</span>. </p>
