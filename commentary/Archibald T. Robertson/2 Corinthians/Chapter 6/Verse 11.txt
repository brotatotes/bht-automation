<p><b> Our mouth is open unto you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το στομα ημων ανεωιγεν προς υμας</span></span>). Second perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανοιγω</span></span> and intransitive, stand open. He has kept back nothing in his portrayal of the glory of the ministry as the picture of the open mouth shows.</p>
<p><b>Our heart is enlarged </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η καρδια ημων πεπλατυντα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλατυνω</span></span>, to broaden, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλατυς</span></span>, broad. In N T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:5">Matthew 23:5</span> (cf. phylacteries). Hence his freedom of speech for "out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaks" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:34">Matthew 12:34</span>). </p>
