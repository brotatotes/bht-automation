<p><b> Now for a recompense in like kind </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την δε αυτην αντιμισθιαν</span></span>). No example of this expressive word outside of this passage and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:27">Romans 1:27</span> and later Christian writers. Paul may have found it in use in the <i>Koine</i> vernacular or he may have coined it from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντιμισθος</span></span>, remunerating (paying back). There is no verb here to explain the accusative which may be the accusative of general reference or the object of a verb not expressed.</p>
<p><b>Be ye also enlarged </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλατυνθητε κα υμεις</span></span>). As I have been (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:11">2 Corinthians 6:11</span>). First aorist passive imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλατυνω</span></span>. </p>
