<p><b> Behold, now is the acceptable time </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδου νυν καιρος ευπροσδεκτος</span></span>). Here is another "Pauline parenthesis" (Plummer) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:7">2 Corinthians 5:7</span> by the quotation from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+49:8">Isaiah 49:8</span>. The LXX has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεκτος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεκτο</span></span>) verbal of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεχομα</span></span>, but Paul employs the double compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ, προσ, δεκτος</span></span>), well-received. It occurs in Aristophanes, Plutarch, inscription, etc. </p>
