<p><b> Giving no occasion of stumbling in any thing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηδεμιαν εν μηδεν διδοντες προσκοπην</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Προσκοπη</span></span>, late word (Polybius, LXX), from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσκοπτω</span></span>, to strike against, to stumble. Only here in N.T. Note double negative in the Greek.</p>
<p><b>That the ministry be not blamed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη μωμηθη η διακονια</span></span>). Negative purpose (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη</span></span>). First aorist passive subjunctive of old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μωμαομα</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μωμος</span></span>, blot, blemish. One can read with profit J. A. Hutton's Warrack Lectures, <i>That the Ministry Be Not Blamed</i>. </p>
