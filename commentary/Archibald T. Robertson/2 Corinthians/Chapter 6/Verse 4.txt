<p><b> But in everything commending ourselves </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ' εν παντ συνιστανοντες εαυτους</span></span>). Paul gives a marvellous summary of his argument about the dignity and glory of ministers of Christ as</p>
<p><b>ministers of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως θεου διακονο</span></span>) under three aspects, the first with</p>
<p><b>in </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span>) verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:3-7">2 Corinthians 6:3-7</span>, the second with</p>
<p><b>by </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span>) verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:7">2 Corinthians 6:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:8">2 Corinthians 6:8</span>, the third with</p>
<p><b>as </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως</span></span>) verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:9-10">2 Corinthians 6:9-10</span>. The negative view with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> we have in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:3">2 Corinthians 6:3</span>, then the positive in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:4-7">2 Corinthians 6:4-7</span>. Each word carries a story that can be filled in from Paul's own life as a preacher with an echo in that of us all.</p>
<p><b>In distresses </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν στενοχωριαις</span></span>). In tight places (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:10">2 Corinthians 12:10</span>). Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στενοχωρεω</span></span> (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:8">2 Corinthians 4:8</span>). </p>
