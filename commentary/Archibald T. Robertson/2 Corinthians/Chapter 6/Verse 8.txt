<p><b> By glory and dishonour </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια δοξης κα ατιμιας</span></span>). Here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span> is no longer instrument, but state or condition. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Δοξα</span></span> here is glory. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:21">Romans 9:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:20">2 Timothy 2:20</span> for contrast between honour and dishonour (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιμη, ατιμια</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>By evil report and good report </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια δυσφημιας κα ευφημιας</span></span>). Play on the words with prefixes <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυσ-</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ-</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φημη</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Δυσφημια</span></span> is a late word, only here in N.T. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ευφημια</span></span>, old and common word, only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>As deceivers and yet true </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως πλανο κα αληθεις</span></span>). Paul takes up <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως</span></span> now in place of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span> which succeeded <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span>. Note use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span> in sense of "and yet" (adversative). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πλανος</span></span> is late word (Diodorus, Josephus) for wandering, vagabond, impostor (cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλαναω</span></span>, to lead astray, used of Christ, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:12">John 7:12</span>). In N.T. only here; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:63">Matthew 27:63</span> (of Christ by Pharisees); <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:7">2 John 1:7</span>. "In the Clementines St. Paul is expressly described by his adversaries as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλανος</span></span> and as disseminating deceit (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλανην</span></span>)" (Bernard). Such slander from one's enemies is praise. </p>
