<p><b> These promises </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταυτας τας επαγγελιας</span></span>). So many and so precious (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:4">2 Peter 2:4</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαγγελματα</span></span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:39">Hebrews 11:39</span>).</p>
<p><b>Let us cleanse ourselves </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθαρισωμεν εαυτους</span></span>). Old Greek used <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθαιρω</span></span> (in N.T. only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:2">John 15:2</span>, to prune). In <i>Koine</i> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθαριζω</span></span> occurs in inscriptions for ceremonial cleansing (Deissmann, <i>Bible Studies</i>, p. 216f.). Paul includes himself in this volitive aorist subjunctive.</p>
<p><b>From all defilement </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο παντος μολυσμου</span></span>). Ablative alone would have done, but with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span> it is plainer as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:14">Hebrews 9:14</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μολυσμος</span></span> is a late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μολυνω</span></span>, to stain (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+8:7">1 Corinthians 8:7</span>), to pollute. In the LXX, Plutarch, Josephus. It includes all sorts of filthiness, physical, moral, mental, ceremonial, "of flesh and spirit." Missionaries in China and India can appreciate the atmosphere of pollution in Corinth, for instance.</p>
<p><b>Perfecting holiness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιτελουντες αγιοσυνην</span></span>). Not merely negative goodness (cleansing), but aggressive and progressive (present tense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιτελεω</span></span>) holiness, not a sudden attainment of complete holiness, but a continuous process (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:13">1 Thessalonians 3:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:4">Romans 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:6">Romans 1:6</span>). </p>
