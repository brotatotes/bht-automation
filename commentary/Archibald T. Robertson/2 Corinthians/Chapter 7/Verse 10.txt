<p><b> For godly sorrow </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η γαρ κατα θεον λυπη</span></span>). "For the sorrow according to God" (God's ideal, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:9">2 Corinthians 7:9</span>).</p>
<p><b>Worketh repentance unto salvation a repentance without regret </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετανοιαν εις σωτηριαν αμεταμελητον εργαζετα</span></span>). This clause alone should have prevented the confusion between mere "sorrow" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυπη</span></span>) as indicated in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταμελομα</span></span>, to regret (to be sorry again) and "change of mind and life" as shown by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετανοιαν</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετανοεω</span></span>) and wrongly translated "repentance." The sorrow according to God does work this "change of mind and life" unto salvation, a change "not to be regretted" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμεταμελητον</span></span>, an old verbal adjective of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταμελομα</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative, but here alone in N.T.). It agrees with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετανοιαν</span></span>, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωτηριαν</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>But the sorrow of the world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η δε του κοσμου λυπη</span></span>). In contrast, the kind of sorrow that the world has, grief "for failure, not for sin" (Bernard), for the results as seen in Cain, Esau (his tears!), and Judas (remorse, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετεμεληθη</span></span>). Works out (perfective use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατ-</span></span>) death in the end. </p>
