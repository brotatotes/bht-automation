<p><b> We joyed the more exceedingly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περισσοτερως μαλλον εχαρημεν</span></span>). Double comparative (pleonastic use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαλλον</span></span>, more, with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περισσοτερως</span></span>, more abundantly) as is common in the <i>Koine</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:36">Mark 7:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:23">Philippians 1:23</span>).</p>
<p><b>For the joy of Titus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ τη χαρα Τιτου</span></span>). On the basis of (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>) the joy of Titus who was proud of the outcome of his labours in Corinth.</p>
<p><b>Hath been refreshed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπεπαυτα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπαυω</span></span>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:18">1 Corinthians 16:18</span> for this striking verb. </p>
