<p><b> Whilst he remembereth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναμιμνησκομενου</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναμιμνησκω</span></span>, to remind, in the genitive case agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτου</span></span> (his, of him).</p>
<p><b>The obedience of you all </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την παντων υμων υπακουην</span></span>). A remarkable statement of the complete victory of Titus in spite of a stubborn minority still opposing Paul.</p>
<p><b>With fear and trembling </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα φοβου κα τρομου</span></span>). He had brought a stern message (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:5">1 Corinthians 5:5</span>) and they had trembled at the words of Titus (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:5">Ephesians 6:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:12">Philippians 2:12</span>). Paul had himself come to the Corinthians at first with a nervous dread (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:3">1 Corinthians 2:3</span>). </p>
