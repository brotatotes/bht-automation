<p><b> When we had come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελθοντων ημων</span></span>). Genitive absolute with second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομα</span></span>. Paul now returns to the incident mentioned in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:12">2 Corinthians 2:12</span> before the long digression on the glory of the ministry.</p>
<p><b>Had no relief </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδεμιαν εσχηκεν ανεσιν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative precisely as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:13">2 Corinthians 2:13</span> which see, "has had no relief" (dramatic perfect).</p>
<p><b>Afflicted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θλιβομενο</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θλιβω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:8">2 Corinthians 4:8</span>, but with anacoluthon, for the nominative case agrees not with the genitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημων</span></span> nor with the accusative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημας</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:6">2 Corinthians 7:6</span>. It is used as if a principal verb as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:11">2 Corinthians 9:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:6">2 Corinthians 11:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:16">Romans 12:16</span> (Moulton, <i>Prolegomena</i>, p. 182; Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, pp. 1132-35).</p>
<p><b>Without were fightings </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξωθεν μαχα</span></span>). Asyndeton and no copula, a parenthesis also in structure. Perhaps pagan adversaries in Macedonia (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:32">1 Corinthians 15:32</span>).</p>
<p><b>Within were fears </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσωθεν φοβο</span></span>). Same construction. "Mental perturbations" (Augustine) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:28">2 Corinthians 11:28</span>. </p>
