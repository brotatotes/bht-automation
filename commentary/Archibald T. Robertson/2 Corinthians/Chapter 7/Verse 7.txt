<p><b> Wherewith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η</span></span>). Either locative case with preceding <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> or instrumental of the relative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεκληθη</span></span> (first aorist passive indicative). "The manner in which Paul, so to speak, <i>fondles</i> this word (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρακαλεω</span></span>) is most beautiful" (Vincent).</p>
<p><b>In you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφ' υμιν</span></span>). Over you, upon you.</p>
<p><b>Your longing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την υμων επιποθησιν</span></span>). Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιποθεω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>, directive, longing towards, yearning). Only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Mourning </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδυρμον</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδυρομα</span></span>, to lament. Only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>So that I rejoiced yet more </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωστε με μαλλον χαρηνα</span></span>). Result expressed by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωστε</span></span> and the second aorist passive infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαιρω</span></span> with accusative of general reference. </p>
