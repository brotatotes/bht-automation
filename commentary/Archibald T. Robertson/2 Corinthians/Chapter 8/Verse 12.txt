<p><b> Is there </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προκειτα</span></span>). Lies before one. Old word.</p>
<p><b>Acceptable </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευπροσδεκτος</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:2">2 Corinthians 6:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>According as a man hath </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθο εαν εχη</span></span>). Indefinite comparative clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and present subjunctive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχε</span></span>. Clearly God does not expect us to give what we do not have.</p>
<p><b>Not according as he hath not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου καθο ουκ εχε</span></span>). Note present indicative rather than subjunctive because a specific case is presented. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:7">2 Corinthians 9:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:43">Mark 12:43</span>. </p>
