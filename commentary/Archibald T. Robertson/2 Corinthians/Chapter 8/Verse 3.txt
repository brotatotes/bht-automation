<p><b> Beyond their power </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα δυναμιν</span></span>). "Alongside" with accusative like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ δυναμιν</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:8">2 Corinthians 1:8</span>. Field (<i>Ot. Nov</i>.) quotes Josephus (<i>Ant</i>. iii. 6, 1) for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα δυναμιν</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα δυναμιν</span></span> as here. Few give <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα δυναμιν</span></span> (according to actual ability). Paul commends this high pressure collection because of the emergency.</p>
<p><b>Of their own accord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυθαιρετο</span></span>). Old verbal adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτοσ, αιρετος</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιρεομα</span></span>, to choose), of their own initiative, voluntary. Only here and verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:17">2 Corinthians 8:17</span> in N.T. Papyri often have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκουσιως κα αυθαιρετως</span></span> (willingly and voluntarily). </p>
