<p><b> Insomuch that we exhorted Titus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το παρακαλεσα ημας Τιτον</span></span>). Use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το</span></span> and the infinitive for result with accusative of general reference (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημας</span></span>). See Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 1003.</p>
<p><b>He had made a beginning before </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προενηρξατο</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of the double compound verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προ-εν-αρχομα</span></span>, still found only here and verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:10">2 Corinthians 8:10</span>, to make a start before others.</p>
<p><b>Complete </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιτελεσε</span></span>) First aorist (effective) active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιτελεω</span></span>, to finish, with perfective use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> in composition. </p>
