<p><b> Though he was rich </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλουσιος ων</span></span>). Concessive present participle <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ων</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειμ</span></span>, to be.</p>
<p><b>Be became poor </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επτωχευσεν</span></span>). Ingressive aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πτωχευω</span></span> (see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:2">2 Corinthians 8:2</span> on <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πτωχεια</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Through his poverty </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη εκεινου πτωχεια</span></span>). Instrumental case, by means of.</p>
<p><b>Might become rich </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλουτησητε</span></span>). Ingressive first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλουτεω</span></span>, to be rich with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> (that). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:53">Luke 1:53</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:8">1 Corinthians 4:8</span>. </p>
