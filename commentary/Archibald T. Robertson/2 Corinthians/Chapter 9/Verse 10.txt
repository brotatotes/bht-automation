<p><b> Supplieth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιχορηγων</span></span>). Late <i>Koine</i> compound verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χορηγεω</span></span>, just below (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:11">1 Peter 4:11</span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χορηγος</span></span> is old word for leader of a chorus (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χοροσ, ηγεομα</span></span>) or chorus-leader. The verb means to furnish a chorus at one's own expense, then to supply in general. N.T. examples of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιχορηγεω</span></span> are <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:10">2 Corinthians 9:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:15">Galatians 3:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:19">Colossians 2:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:5">2 Peter 1:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>Shall multiply </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληθυνε</span></span>). Future active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληθυνω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληθυς</span></span>, fulness. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:1">Acts 6:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Fruits </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενηματα</span></span>). Correct reading (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span>, to become) and not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεννηματα</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενναω</span></span>, to beget). This spelling is supported by LXX where Thackeray shows that <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενηματα</span></span> in LXX refers to vegetables and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεννηματα</span></span> to animals. The papyri support this distinction (Moulton and Milligan's <i>Vocabulary</i>). </p>
