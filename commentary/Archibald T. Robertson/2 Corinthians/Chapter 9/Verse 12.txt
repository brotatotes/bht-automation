<p><b> Service </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λειτουργιας</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεως</span></span> (people, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαος</span></span>), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λειτος</span></span> like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δημοσιος</span></span>, public, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργον</span></span>, work. So public service either in worship to God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:23">Luke 1:23</span>) or benefaction to others (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:12">2 Corinthians 9:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:30">Philippians 2:30</span>). Our word liturgy is this word.</p>
<p><b>Filleth up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστιν προσαναπληρουσα</span></span>). Present active periphrastic indicative of double compound verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσαναπληροω</span></span>, <i>Koine</i> word, here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:9">2 Corinthians 11:9</span> only in N.T., to fill up by adding to. The Corinthians simply added to the total from others.</p>
<p><b>Unto God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω θεω</span></span>). Dative case and with a certain suddenness as at close of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:11">2 Corinthians 9:11</span>, really a parenthesis between in the somewhat tangled sentence. </p>
