<p><b> I glory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καυχωμα</span></span>). Present middle indicative. I still am glorying, in spite of the poor performance of the Corinthians.</p>
<p><b>Hath been prepared </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεσκευαστα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρασκευαζω</span></span>, to make ready, "stands prepared."</p>
<p><b>Stirred up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηρεθισε</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερεθιζω</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερεθω</span></span>, to excite), to excite in a good sense here, in a bad sense in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:21">Colossians 3:21</span>, the only N.T. examples.</p>
<p><b>Very many of them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους πλειονας</span></span>). The more, the majority. </p>
