<p><b> He hath purposed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προηιρητα</span></span>). Perfect middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προαιρεομα</span></span>, to choose beforehand, old verb, here only in N.T. Permanent purpose also.</p>
<p><b>Not grudgingly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη εκ λυπης</span></span>). The use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> rather than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου</span></span> shows that the imperative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιειτω</span></span> (do) or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδοτω</span></span> (give) is to be supplied. Not give as out of sorrow.</p>
<p><b>Or of necessity </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η εξ αναγκης</span></span>). As if it were like pulling eye-teeth.</p>
<p><b>For God loveth a cheerful giver </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιλαρον γαρ δοτην αγαπα ο θεος</span></span>). Our word "hilarious" comes from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιλαρον</span></span> which is from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιλαος</span></span> (propitious), an old and common adjective, only here in N.T. </p>
