<p><b> As it is written </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθως γεγραπτα</span></span>). <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+92:3">Psalms 92:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+92:9">Psalms 92:9</span>. Picture of the beneficent man.</p>
<p><b>He hath scattered abroad </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσκορπισεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκορπιζω</span></span>, to scatter, <i>Koine</i> verb for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκεδαννυμ</span></span> of the Attic. Probably akin to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκορπιος</span></span> (scorpion) from root <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκαρπ</span></span>, to cut asunder. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:30">Matthew 12:30</span>. It is like sowing seed.</p>
<p><b>To the poor </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις πενησιν</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεναμα</span></span>, to work for one's living. Latin <i>penuria</i> and Greek <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειναω</span></span>, to be hungry, are kin to it. Only N.T. instance and to be distinguished from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πτωχος</span></span>, beggar, abjectly poor. </p>
