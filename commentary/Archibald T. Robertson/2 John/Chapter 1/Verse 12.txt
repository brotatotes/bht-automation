<p><b> I would not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ εβουληθην</span></span>). Epistolary aorist (first passive indicative).</p>
<p><b>With paper and ink </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια χαρτου κα μελανος</span></span>). The <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρτης</span></span> was a leaf of papyrus prepared for writing by cutting the pith into strips and pasting together, old word (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+43:23">Jeremiah 43:23</span>), here only in N.T. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μελας</span></span> is old adjective for black (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:36">Matthew 5:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:5">Revelation 6:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:12">Revelation 6:12</span>), and for black ink here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:13">3 John 1:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:3">2 Corinthians 3:3</span>. Apparently John wrote this little letter with his own hand.</p>
<p><b>To come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενεσθα</span></span>). Second aorist middle infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελπιζω</span></span>, I hope.</p>
<p><b>Face to face </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στομα προς στομα</span></span>). "Mouth to mouth." So in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:14">3 John 1:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+12:8">Numbers 12:8</span>. "Face to face" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσωπον προς προσωπον</span></span>) we have in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:12">1 Corinthians 13:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>Your </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμων</span></span>). Or "our" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημων</span></span>). Both true.</p>
<p><b>That may be fulfilled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα πεπληρωμενη η</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the periphrastic perfect passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροω</span></span>, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:4">1 John 1:4</span>, which see. </p>
