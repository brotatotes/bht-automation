<p><b> I rejoice </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχαρην</span></span>). Second aorist passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαιρω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:3">3 John 1:3</span>, "of a glad surprise" (D. Smith), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:11">Mark 14:11</span>, over the discovery about the blessing of their godly home on these lads.</p>
<p><b>Greatly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λιαν</span></span>). Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:3">3 John 1:3</span> in John's writings.</p>
<p><b>I have found </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρηκα</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρισκω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:41">John 1:41</span>, our "eureka," here with its usual force, a continued discovery. "He sits down at once and writes to Kyria. How glad she would be that her lads, far away in the great city, were true to their early faith" (David Smith).</p>
<p><b>Certain of thy children </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ των τεκνων</span></span>). No <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τινας</span></span> as one would expect before <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span>, a not infrequent idiom in the N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:17">John 16:17</span>).</p>
<p><b>Walking </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιπατουντας</span></span>). Present active accusative supplementary participle agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τινας</span></span> understood. Probably members of the church off here in Ephesus.</p>
<p><b>In truth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αληθεια</span></span>). As in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:1">2 John 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:4">3 John 1:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>We received </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελαβομεν</span></span>). Second aorist active (possibly, though not certainly, literary plural) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>. This very idiom (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντολην λαμβανω</span></span>) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:18">John 10:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:15">Acts 17:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+4:10">Colossians 4:10</span>. Perhaps the reference here is to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:7">1 John 2:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:23">1 John 3:23</span>. </p>
