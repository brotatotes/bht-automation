<p><b> Love </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η αγαπη</span></span>). The love just mentioned.</p>
<p><b>That we should walk </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα περιπατωμεν</span></span>). Object clause in nominative case in apposition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπη</span></span>, with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιπατεω</span></span>, "that we keep on walking."</p>
<p><b>The commandment </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η εντολη</span></span>). The one just mentioned with the same construction with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:23">1 John 3:23</span>. John changes from the first person plural to the second (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκουσατε</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:7">1 John 2:7</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιπατητε</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:5">1 John 2:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:7">1 John 2:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>In it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αυτη</span></span>). Either to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αληθεια</span></span> (truth) of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:4">2 John 1:4</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπη</span></span> of this verse, or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντολη</span></span> of this verse. Either makes good sense, probably "in love." With <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιπατεω</span></span> (walk) we have often <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:7">1 John 1:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:11">1 John 1:11</span>, etc.) or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span> (according to) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:5">Mark 7:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:3">1 Corinthians 3:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:2">2 Corinthians 10:2</span>, etc. </p>
