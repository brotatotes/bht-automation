<p><b> Deceivers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλανο</span></span>). Late adjective (Diodorus, Josephus) meaning wandering, roving (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:1">1 Timothy 4:1</span>). As a substantive in N.T. of Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:63">Matthew 27:63</span>), of Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:8">2 Corinthians 6:8</span>), and here. See the verb (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των πλανοντων υμας</span></span>) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:26">1 John 2:26</span> of the Gnostic deceivers as here and also of Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:12">John 7:12</span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:8">1 John 1:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>Are gone forth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξηλθαν</span></span>, alpha ending). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξερχομα</span></span>, perhaps an allusion to the crisis when they left the churches (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:19">1 John 2:19</span>, same form).</p>
<p><b>Even they that confess not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο μη ομολογουντες</span></span>). "The ones not confessing" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> regular negative with the participle). The articular participle describes the deceivers (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλανο</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>That Jesus Christ cometh in the flesh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιησουν Χριστον ερχομενον εν σαρκ</span></span>). "Jesus Christ coming in the flesh." Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομα</span></span> treating the Incarnation as a continuing fact which the Docetic Gnostics flatly denied. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:2">1 John 4:2</span> we have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εληλυθοτα</span></span> (perfect active participle) in this same construction with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομολογεω</span></span>, because there the reference is to the definite historical fact of the Incarnation. There is no allusion here to the second coming of Christ.</p>
<p><b>This </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτος</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:18">1 John 2:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:22">1 John 2:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:6">1 John 5:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:20">1 John 5:20</span>.</p>
<p><b>The deceiver and the antichrist </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο πλανος κα ο αντιχριστος</span></span>). Article with each word, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:17">Revelation 1:17</span>, to bring out sharply each separate phrase, though one individual is referred to. The one <i>par excellence</i> in popular expectation (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:22">1 John 2:22</span>), though many in reality (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:18">1 John 2:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:7">3 John 1:7</span>). </p>
