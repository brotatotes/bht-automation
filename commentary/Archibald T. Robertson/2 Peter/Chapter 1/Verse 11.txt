<p><b> Thus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτως</span></span>). As shown in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:10">2 Peter 1:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Shall be supplied </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιχορηγηθησετα</span></span>). Future passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιχορηγεω</span></span>, for which see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:5">2 Peter 1:5</span>. You supply the virtues above and God will supply the entrance (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η εισοδος</span></span>, old word already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:9">1 Thessalonians 1:9</span>, etc.).</p>
<p><b>Richly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλουσιως</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:16">Colossians 3:16</span> for this adverb.</p>
<p><b>Into the eternal kingdom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις την αιωνιον βασιλειαν</span></span>). The believer's inheritance of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:4">1 Peter 1:4</span> is here termed kingdom, but "eternal" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιωνιον</span></span> feminine same as masculine). Curiously again in the Stratonicea inscription we find <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της αιωνιου αρχης</span></span> (of the eternal rule) applied to "the lords of Rome." But this is the spiritual reign of God in men's hearts here on earth (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:9">1 Peter 2:9</span>) and in heaven.</p>
<p><b>Of our Lord and Saviour Jesus Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του κυριου ημων κα σωτηρος Ιησου Χριστου</span></span>). For which idiom see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:1">2 Peter 1:1</span>. </p>
