<p><b> I think it right </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιον ηγουμα</span></span>). Peter considers this to be his solemn duty, "right" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιον</span></span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:1">Philippians 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:1">Ephesians 6:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>So long as </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφ' οσον</span></span>). For this phrase see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:15">Matthew 9:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:13">Romans 11:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Tabernacle </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκηνωματ</span></span>). Old word, in literal sense in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+33:18">Deuteronomy 33:18</span> for the usual <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκηνη</span></span> (Peter's word at the Transfiguration, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:5">Mark 9:5</span>), earliest use (in N.T. only here, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:14">2 Peter 1:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:46">Acts 7:46</span> of the tabernacle of the covenant) in this metaphorical sense of life as a pilgrimage (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:1">1 Peter 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:11">1 Peter 2:11</span>), though Paul has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκηνος</span></span>, so in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:1">2 Corinthians 5:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:4">2 Corinthians 5:4</span>. Peter feels the nearness of death and the urgency upon him.</p>
<p><b>To stir you up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διεγειρειν υμας</span></span>). Present active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διεγειρω</span></span>, late (Arist., Hippocr., Herodian, papyri), perfective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span> = thoroughly) compound, to wake out of sleep (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:39">Mark 4:39</span>), "to keep on rousing you up."</p>
<p><b>By putting you in remembrance </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν υπομνησε</span></span>). Old word, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπομιμνησκω</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:12">2 Peter 1:12</span>), in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:1">2 Peter 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:5">2 Timothy 1:5</span>. "By way of reminding you." </p>
