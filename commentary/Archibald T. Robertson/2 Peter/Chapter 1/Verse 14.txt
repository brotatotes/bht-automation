<p><b> The putting off of my tabernacle </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η αποθεσις του σκηννωματος μου</span></span>). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποθεσις</span></span> see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:21">1 Peter 3:21</span> and for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκηνωμα</span></span> verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:13">2 Peter 1:13</span>. For the metaphor see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:3">2 Corinthians 5:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Cometh swiftly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταχινη εστιν</span></span>). Late adjective (Theocritus, LXX, inscription), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:1">2 Peter 2:1</span>. It is not clear whether <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταχινος</span></span> means soon or speedy as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+59:7">Isaiah 59:7</span> and like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταχυς</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:19">James 1:19</span>, or sudden, like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταχυς</span></span> in Plato (<i>Republ</i>. 553 D). Either sense agrees with the urgent tone of Peter here, whether he felt his death to be near or violent or both.</p>
<p><b>Signified unto me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδηλωσεν μο</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δηλοω</span></span>, old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δελος</span></span>), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:11">1 Peter 1:11</span>. Peter refers to the incident told in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:18">John 21:18</span>, which he knew by personal experience before John wrote it down. </p>
