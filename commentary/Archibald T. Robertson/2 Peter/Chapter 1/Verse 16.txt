<p><b> We did not follow </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ εξακολουθησαντες</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξακολουθεω</span></span>, late compound verb, to follow out (Polybius, Plutarch, LXX, papyri, inscriptions as of death following for any Gentile in the temple violating the barrier), with emphatic negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ</span></span>, "not having followed." See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:2">2 Peter 2:2</span> for this verb.</p>
<p><b>Cunningly devised fables </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σεσοφισμενοις μυθοις</span></span>). Associative instrumental case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυθος</span></span> (old term for word, narrative, story, fiction, fable, falsehood). In N.T. only here and the Pastoral Epistles (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:4">1 Timothy 1:4</span>, etc.). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σοφιζω</span></span>, old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σοφος</span></span>), only twice in N.T., in causative sense to make wise (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:15">2 Timothy 3:15</span>), to play the sophist, to invent cleverly (here) and so also in the old writers and in the papyri. Some of the false teachers apparently taught that the Gospel miracles were only allegories and not facts (Bigg). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:3">2 Peter 2:3</span> for "feigned words."</p>
<p><b>When we made known unto you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγνωρισαμεν υμιν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνωριζω</span></span>, to make known unto you. Possibly by Peter himself.</p>
<p><b>The power and coming </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την δυναμιν κα παρουσιαν</span></span>). These words can refer (Chase) to the Incarnation, just as is true of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιφανεια</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:10">2 Timothy 1:10</span> (second coming in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:14">1 Timothy 6:14</span>), and is true of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρουσια</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:6">2 Corinthians 7:6</span> of Titus). But elsewhere in the N.T. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρουσια</span></span> (technical term in the papyri for the coming of a king or other high dignitary), when used of Christ, refers to his second coming (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:4">2 Peter 3:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:12">2 Peter 3:12</span>).</p>
<p><b>But we were eye-witnesses </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ' εποπτα γενηθεντες</span></span>). First aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span>, "but having become eye-witnesses." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εποπτα</span></span>, old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εποπτω</span></span> like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εποπτευω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:12">1 Peter 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:2">1 Peter 3:2</span>), used of those who attained the third or highest degree of initiates in the Eleusinian mysteries (common in the inscriptions). Cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτοπτης</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:2">Luke 1:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>Of his majesty </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της εκεινου μεγαλειοτητος</span></span>). Late and rare word (LXX and papyri) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεγαλειος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:11">Acts 2:11</span>), in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:43">Luke 9:43</span> (of God); <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:27">Acts 19:27</span> (of Artemis). Peter clearly felt that he and James and John were lifted to the highest stage of initiation at the Transfiguration of Christ. Emphatic <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινου</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:26">2 Timothy 2:26</span>. </p>
