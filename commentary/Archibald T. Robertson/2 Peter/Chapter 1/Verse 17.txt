<p><b> For he received </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαβων γαρ</span></span>). Second aorist active participle nominative singular of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>, "he having received," but there is no finite verb, anacoluthon, changing in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:19">2 Peter 1:19</span> (after parenthesis in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:18">2 Peter 1:18</span>) to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχομεν βεβαιοτερον</span></span> rather than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβεβαιωσεν</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>When there came such a voice to him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωνης ενεχθεισης αυτω τοιασδε</span></span>). Genitive absolute with first aorist passive participle feminine singular of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φερω</span></span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:13">1 Peter 1:13</span>), repeated <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεχθεισαν</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:18">2 Peter 1:18</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Φωνη</span></span> (voice) is used also of Pentecost (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:6">Acts 2:6</span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Τοιοσδε</span></span> (classical demonstrative) occurs here alone in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>From the excellent glory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο της μεγαλοπρεπους δοξης</span></span>). "By the majestic glory." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μεγαλοπρεπης</span></span>, old compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεγας</span></span>, great, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρεπε</span></span>, it is becoming), here only in N.T., several times in O.T., Apocr. (II Macc. 8:15), adverb in the inscriptions. Probably a reference to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νεφελη φωτεινη</span></span> (bright cloud, shekinah) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:5">Matthew 17:5</span>. The words given here from the "voice" agree exactly with <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:5">Matthew 17:5</span> except the order and the use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις ον</span></span> rather than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ω</span></span>. Mark (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:7">Mark 9:7</span>) and Luke (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:35">Luke 9:35</span>) have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουετε</span></span>. But Peter did not need any Gospel for his report here. </p>
