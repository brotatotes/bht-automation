<p><b> The word of prophecy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον προφητικον λογον</span></span>). "The prophetic word." Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:10">1 Peter 1:10</span>, a reference to all the Messianic prophecies.</p>
<p><b>Made more sure </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βεβαιοτερον</span></span>). Predicate accusative of the comparative adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βεβαιος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:10">2 Peter 1:10</span>). The Transfiguration scene confirmed the Messianic prophecies and made clear the deity of Jesus Christ as God's Beloved Son. Some with less likelihood take Peter to mean that the word of prophecy is a surer confirmation of Christ's deity than the Transfiguration.</p>
<p><b>Whereunto </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ω</span></span>). Dative of the relative referring to "the prophetic word made more sure."</p>
<p><b>That ye take heed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσεχοντες</span></span>). Present active participle with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νουν</span></span> (mind) understood, "holding your mind upon" with the dative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ω</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>As unto a lamp </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως λυχνω</span></span>). Dative also after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσεχοντες</span></span> of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυχνος</span></span>, old word (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:15">Matthew 5:15</span>).</p>
<p><b>Shining </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φαινοντ</span></span>). Dative also present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φαινω</span></span>, to shine (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:5">John 1:5</span>). So of the Baptist (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:35">John 5:35</span>).</p>
<p><b>In a dark place </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αυχμηρω τοπω</span></span>). Old adjective, parched, squalid, dirty, dark, murky, here only in N.T., though in Aristotle and on tombstone for a boy.</p>
<p><b>Until the day dawn </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως ου ημερα διαυγαση</span></span>). First aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαυγαζω</span></span> with temporal conjunction <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως ου</span></span>, usual construction for future time. Late compound verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαυγαζω</span></span> (Polybius, Plutarch, papyri) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυγη</span></span>, to shine through, here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>The day-star </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωσφορος</span></span>). Old compound adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φως</span></span>, light, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φερω</span></span>, to bring), light-bringing, light-bearer (Lucifer) applied to Venus as the morning star. Our word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φοσφορυς</span></span> is this word. In the LXX <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εωσφορος</span></span> occurs. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mal+4:2">Malachi 4:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:76-79">Luke 1:76-79</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:16">Revelation 22:16</span> for "dawn" applied to the Messiah.</p>
<p><b>Arise </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανατειλη</span></span>). First aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανατελλω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:11">James 1:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:45">Matthew 5:45</span>). </p>
