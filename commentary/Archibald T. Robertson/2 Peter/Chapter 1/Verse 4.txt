<p><b> Whereby </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι' ων</span></span>). Probably the "glory and virtue" just mentioned, though it is possible to take it with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντα τα προς</span></span>, etc., or with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημιν</span></span> (unto us, meaning "through whom").</p>
<p><b>He hath granted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεδωρητα</span></span>). Perfect middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δωρεω</span></span>, for which see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:3">2 Peter 1:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>His precious and exceeding great promises </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα τιμια κα μεγιστα επαγγελματα</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Επαγγελμα</span></span> is an old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαγγελλω</span></span>) in place of the common <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαγγελια</span></span>, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:13">2 Peter 3:13</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Τιμιος</span></span> (precious, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιμη</span></span>, value), three times by Peter (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:7">1 Peter 1:7</span> of faith; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:19">2 Peter 1:19</span> of the blood of Christ; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:4">2 Peter 1:4</span> of Christ's promises). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μεγιστα</span></span> is the elative superlative used along with a positive adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιμια</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>That ye may become </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα γενησθε</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and second aorist middle subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Through these </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια τουτων</span></span>). The promises.</p>
<p><b>Partakers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινωνο</span></span>). Partners, sharers in, for which word see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:1">1 Peter 5:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Of the divine nature </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θειας φυσεως</span></span>). This phrase, like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το θειον</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:29">Acts 17:29</span>, "belongs rather to Hellenism than to the Bible" (Bigg). It is a Stoic phrase, but not with the Stoic meaning. Peter is referring to the new birth as <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:23">1 Peter 1:23</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγεγεννημενο</span></span>). The same phrase occurs in an inscription possibly under the influence of Mithraism (Moulton and Milligan's <i>Vocabulary</i>).</p>
<p><b>Having escaped </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποφυγοντες</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποφευγω</span></span>, old compound verb, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:18-20">2 Peter 2:18-20</span>, with the ablative here (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φθορας</span></span>, old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φθειρω</span></span>, moral decay as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:12">2 Peter 2:12</span>) and the accusative there.</p>
<p><b>By lust </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν επιθυμια</span></span>). Caused by, consisting in, lust. "Man becomes either regenerate or degenerate" (Strachan). </p>
