<p><b> Yea, and for this very cause </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα αυτο τουτο δε</span></span>). Adverbial accusative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτο τουτο</span></span>) here, a classic idiom, with both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span>. Cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα τουτο</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:29">Philippians 1:29</span>), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτο μεν--τουτο δε</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:33">Hebrews 10:33</span>). "The soul of religion is the practical part" (Bunyan). Because of the new birth and the promises we have a part to play.</p>
<p><b>Adding on your part </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεισενεγκαντες</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεισφερω</span></span>, old double compound, to bring in (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισφερω</span></span>), besides (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span>), here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>All diligence </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπουδην πασαν</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπευδω</span></span> to hasten (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:5">Luke 19:5</span>). This phrase (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πασαν σπουδην</span></span>) occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:3">Jude 1:3</span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιουμενος</span></span> and on the inscription in Stratonicea (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:3">2 Peter 1:3</span>) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ισφερεσθα</span></span> (certainly a curious coincidence, to say the least, though common in the <i>Koine</i>).</p>
<p><b>In your faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη πιστε υμων</span></span>). Faith or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστις</span></span> (strong conviction as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:1">Hebrews 11:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:3">Hebrews 11:3</span>, the root of the Christian life <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:8">Ephesians 2:8</span>) is the foundation which goes through various steps up to love (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπη</span></span>). See similar lists in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:30">James 1:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:3">1 Thessalonians 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:3">2 Thessalonians 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:22">Galatians 5:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:3">Romans 5:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:29">Romans 8:29</span>. Hermas (Vis. iii. 8. 1-7) has a list called "daughters" of one another. Note the use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> (in, on) with each step.</p>
<p><b>Supply </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιχορηγησατε</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιχορηγεω</span></span>, late and rare double compound verb (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χορηγεω</span></span> <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:11">1 Peter 4:11</span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χορηγος</span></span>, chorus-leader, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χορος</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγεομα</span></span>, to lead), to fit out the chorus with additional (complete) supplies. Both compound and simplex (more common) occur in the papyri. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:11">2 Peter 1:11</span> and already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:10">2 Corinthians 9:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:5">Galatians 3:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:19">Colossians 2:19</span>.</p>
<p><b>Virtue </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρετην</span></span>). Moral power, moral energy, vigor of soul (Bengel). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:3">2 Peter 1:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Knowledge </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνωσιν</span></span>). Insight, understanding (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:18">1 Corinthians 16:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:15">John 15:15</span>). </p>
