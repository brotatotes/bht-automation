<p><b> Temperance </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την εγκρατειαν</span></span>). Self-control. Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγκρατης</span></span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρατος</span></span>, one holding himself in as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:8">Titus 1:8</span>), in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:25">Acts 24:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:23">Galatians 5:23</span>. The opposite of the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλεονεξια</span></span> of the heretics.</p>
<p><b>Patience </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την υπομονην</span></span>). For which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:3">James 1:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Godliness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την ευσεβειαν</span></span>). For which see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:3">2 Peter 1:3</span>. </p>
