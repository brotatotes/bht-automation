<p><b> For if these things are yours and abound </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταυτα γαρ υμιν υπαρχοντα κα πλεοναζοντα</span></span>). Present active circumstantial (conditional) participles neuter plural of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπαρχω</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλεοναζω</span></span> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:12">1 Thessalonians 3:12</span>) with dative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμιν</span></span>, "these things existing for you (or in you) and abounding."</p>
<p><b>They make you to be </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθιστησιν</span></span>). "Render" (present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθιστημ</span></span>, old verb, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:6">James 3:6</span>), singular because <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταυτα</span></span> neuter plural.</p>
<p><b>Not idle nor unfruitful </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ αργους ουδε ακαρπους</span></span>). Accusative predicative plural with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμας</span></span> understood, both adjectives with alpha privative, for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αργος</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:20">James 2:20</span> and for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακαρπος</span></span> <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:22">Matthew 13:22</span>.</p>
<p><b>Knowledge </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιγνωσιν</span></span>). "Full (additional) knowledge" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:2">2 Peter 1:2</span>. </p>
