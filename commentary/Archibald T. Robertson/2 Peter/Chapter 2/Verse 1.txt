<p><b> But there arose </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγενοντο δε</span></span>). Second aorist middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span> (cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινετα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:20">2 Peter 1:20</span>).</p>
<p><b>False prophets also </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ψευδοπροφητα</span></span>). In contrast with the true prophets just pictured in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:20">2 Peter 1:20</span>. Late compound in LXX and Philo, common in N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:15">Matthew 7:15</span>). Allusion to the O.T. times like Balaam and others (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+6:13">Jeremiah 6:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+28:9">Jeremiah 28:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+13:9">Ezekiel 13:9</span>).</p>
<p><b>False teachers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευδοδιδασκαλο</span></span>). Late and rare compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευδησ, διδασκαλος</span></span>) here alone in N.T. Peter pictures them as in the future here (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσοντα</span></span>, shall be) and again as already present (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισιν</span></span>, are, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:17">2 Peter 2:17</span>), or in the past (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επλανηθησαν</span></span>, they went astray, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:15">2 Peter 2:15</span>).</p>
<p><b>Shall privily bring in </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεισαξουσιν</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεισαγω</span></span>, late double compound <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεισαγω</span></span>, to bring in (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισαγω</span></span>), by the side (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span>), as if secretly, here alone in N.T., but see <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεισακτους</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:4">Galatians 2:4</span> (verbal adjective of this same verb).</p>
<p><b>Destructive heresies </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιρεσεις απωλειας</span></span>). Descriptive genitive, "heresies of destruction" (marked by destruction) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:8">Luke 16:8</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hαιρεσις</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιρεω</span></span>) is simply a choosing, a school, a sect like that of the Sadducees (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:17">Acts 5:17</span>), of the Pharisees (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:5">Acts 15:5</span>), and of Christians as Paul admitted (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:5">Acts 24:5</span>). These "tenets" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:20">Galatians 5:20</span>) led to destruction.</p>
<p><b>Denying </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρνουμενο</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρνεομα</span></span>. This the Gnostics did, the very thing that Peter did, alas (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:70">Matthew 26:70</span>) even after Christ's words (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:33">Matthew 10:33</span>).</p>
<p><b>Even the Master </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα τον δεσποτην</span></span>). Old word for absolute master, here of Christ as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:4">Jude 1:4</span>, and also of God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:24">Acts 4:24</span>). Without the evil sense in our "despot."</p>
<p><b>That bought them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον αγορασαντα αυτους</span></span>). First aorist active articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγοραζω</span></span>, same idea with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυτροω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:18">1 Peter 1:18</span>. These were professing Christians, at any rate, these heretics.</p>
<p><b>Swift destruction </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταχινην απωλειαν</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:14">2 Peter 1:14</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταχινην</span></span> and note repetition of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απωλειαν</span></span>. This is always the tragedy of such false prophets, the fate that they bring on (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαγοντες</span></span>) themselves. </p>
