<p><b> Chiefly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαλιστα</span></span>). Especially. He turns now to the libertine heretics (verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:2">2 Peter 2:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:7">2 Peter 2:7</span>).</p>
<p><b>After the flesh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπισω σαρκος</span></span>). Hebraistic use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπισω</span></span> as with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτιων</span></span> (sins) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+65:2">Isaiah 65:2</span>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:19">Matthew 4:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:15">1 Timothy 5:15</span>.</p>
<p><b>Of defilement </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιασμου</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιαινω</span></span> <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:15">Titus 1:15</span>), here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Despise dominion </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυριοτητος καταφρονουντας</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κυριοτης</span></span> is late word for lordship (perhaps God or Christ) (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κυριος</span></span>), in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:16">Colossians 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:21">Ephesians 1:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jdg+1:8">Judges 1:8</span>. Genitive case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταφρουντας</span></span> (thinking down on, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:24">Matthew 6:24</span>).</p>
<p><b>Daring </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τολμητα</span></span>). Old substantive (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τολμαω</span></span>, to dare), daring men, here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Self-willed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυθαδεις</span></span>). Old adjective (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτος</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηδομα</span></span>), self-pleasing, arrogant, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:7">Titus 1:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>They tremble not to rail at dignities </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξας ου τρεμουσιν βλασφημουντες</span></span>). "They tremble not blaspheming dignities." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Τρεμω</span></span> is old verb (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:33">Mark 5:33</span>), used only in present as here and imperfect. Here with the complementary participle <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλασφημουντες</span></span> rather than the infinitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλασφημειν</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:8">Jude 1:8</span>. Perhaps these dignities (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξας</span></span>) are angels (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εςιλ</span></span>). </p>
