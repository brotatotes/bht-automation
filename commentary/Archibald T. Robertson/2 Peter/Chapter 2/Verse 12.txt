<p><b> But these </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτο δε</span></span>). The false teachers of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:1">2 Peter 2:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>As creatures </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωα</span></span>). Living creatures, old word, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωος</span></span> (alive), <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jdg+1:10">Judges 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:6-9">Revelation 4:6-9</span>.</p>
<p><b>Without reason </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλογα</span></span>). Old adjective, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jdg+1:10">Judges 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:27">Acts 25:27</span>. Brute beasts like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θηρια</span></span> (wild animals).</p>
<p><b>Born </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεγεννημενα</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενναω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Mere animals </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυσικα</span></span>). Old adjective in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-ικος</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυσις</span></span>, nature), natural animals, here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>To be taken </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις αλωσιν</span></span>). "For capture" (old substantive, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλοω</span></span>, here only in N.T.).</p>
<p><b>And destroyed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα φθοραν</span></span>). "And for destruction" just like a beast of prey caught. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:4">2 Peter 1:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>In matters whereof they are ignorant </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν οις αγνοουσιν</span></span>). "In which things they are ignorant." Here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν οις</span></span> = <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τουτοις α</span></span> (in those things which), a common Greek idiom. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγνοεω</span></span> (present active indicative) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:13">1 Thessalonians 4:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:7">1 Timothy 1:7</span> for a like picture of loud ignoramuses posing as professional experts.</p>
<p><b>Shall in their destroying surely be destroyed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη φθορα αυτων φθαρησοντα</span></span>). Second future passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φθειρω</span></span>. Rhetorical Hebraism in the use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν φθορα</span></span> (same root as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φθειρω</span></span>), word four times in II Peter. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:10">Jude 1:10</span>. </p>
