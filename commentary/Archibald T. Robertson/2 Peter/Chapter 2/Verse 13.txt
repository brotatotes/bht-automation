<p><b> Suffering wrong </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδικουμενο</span></span>). Present middle or passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδικεω</span></span> to do wrong. So Aleph B P, but A C K L have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κομιουμενο</span></span> (future middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κομιζω</span></span>), shall receive.</p>
<p><b>As the hire of wrong-doing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μισθον αδικιας</span></span>). The Elephantine papyrus has the passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδικεω</span></span> in the sense of being defrauded, and that may be the idea here. Peter plays on words again here as often in II Peter. The picture proceeds now with participles like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγουμενο</span></span> (counting).</p>
<p><b>Pleasure </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηδονην</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:1">James 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:3">James 4:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>To revel in the daytime </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την εν ημερα τρυφην</span></span>). "The in the daytime revel" (old word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρυφη</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θρυπτω</span></span>, to enervate, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:25">Luke 7:25</span>).</p>
<p><b>Spots </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπιλο</span></span>). Old word for disfiguring spot, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:27">Ephesians 5:27</span>.</p>
<p><b>Blemishes </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μωμο</span></span>). Old word for blot (kin to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυω</span></span>), only here in N.T. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:19">1 Peter 1:19</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμωμος κα ασπιλος</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Revelling </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντρυφωντες</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντρυφαω</span></span>, old compound for living in luxury, only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>In their love-feasts </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ταις αγαπαις</span></span>). So B Sah, but Aleph A C K L P read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαταις</span></span> (in their deceivings). If <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπαις</span></span> is genuine as it is in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:12">Jude 1:12</span>, they are the only N.T. examples of this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπη</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>While they feast with you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνευωχουμενο</span></span>). Present passive participle of late and rare verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνευωχεω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν</span></span>, together, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευωχεω</span></span>, to feed abundantly) to entertain with. Clement of Alex. (<i>Paed</i>. ii. I. 6) applies <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευωχια</span></span> to the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπη</span></span>. </p>
