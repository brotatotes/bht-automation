<p><b> Of adultery </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μοιχαλιδος</span></span>). Rather, "of an adulteress," like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:4">James 4:4</span>. Vivid picture of a man who cannot see a woman without lascivious thoughts toward her (Mayor). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:28">Matthew 5:28</span>.</p>
<p><b>That cannot cease </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακαταπαστους</span></span>). Reading of A B in place of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακαταπαυστους</span></span> (alpha privative and verbal of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταπαυω</span></span>, to cease). "Unable to stop." This a late verbal, only here in N.T. It is probable that <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακαταπαστους</span></span> is merely a misspelling of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακαταπαυστους</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>From sin </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτιας</span></span>). Ablative case as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:1">1 Peter 4:1</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτιας</span></span>). Insatiable lust.</p>
<p><b>Enticing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δελεαζοντες</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δελεαζω</span></span>, to catch by bait as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:18">2 Peter 2:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:14">James 1:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>Unsteadfast </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αστηρικτους</span></span>). Late verbal adjective (alpha privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στηριζω</span></span>), in Longinus and Vettius Valens, here alone in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Exercised </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεγυμνασμενην</span></span>). Perfect passive predicate participle with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχοντες</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γυμναζω</span></span> precisely as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:14">Hebrews 5:14</span>. Rhetorical metaphor from the gymnasium.</p>
<p><b>In covetousness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλεονεξιας</span></span>). Genitive case after the participle.</p>
<p><b>Children of cursing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταρας τεκνα</span></span>). Hebraism like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεκνα υπακοης</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:14">1 Peter 1:14</span> = accursed (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταρατο</span></span>). </p>
