<p><b> But he was rebuked </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεγξιν δε εσχεν</span></span>). "But he had rebuke." Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span> and accusative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεγξις</span></span> (late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεγχω</span></span>, a periphrasis for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεγχω</span></span>, here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>For his own transgression </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδιας παρανομιας</span></span>). Objective genitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρανομια</span></span>, old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρανομος</span></span> lawbreaker), here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>A dumb ass </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποζυγιον αφωνον</span></span>). Dumb is without voice, old word for idols and beasts. The adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποζυγιος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο ζυγον ον</span></span>) "being under a yoke," is applied to the ass as the common beast of burden (papyri, Deissmann, <i>Bible Studies</i>, p. 160), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:5">Matthew 21:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>Spake </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φθεγξαμενον</span></span>). First aorist middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φθεγγομα</span></span>, old verb, to utter a sound, in N.T. only here, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:18">2 Peter 2:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:18">Acts 4:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>Stayed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκωλυσεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κωλυω</span></span>, to hinder.</p>
<p><b>Madness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραφρονιαν</span></span>). Only known example of this word instead of the usual <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραφροσυνη</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραφρονησις</span></span>. It is being beside one's wits. </p>
