<p><b> Without water </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανυδρο</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:43">Matthew 12:43</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:24">Luke 11:24</span>. Old word for common and disappointing experience of travellers in the orient.</p>
<p><b>Mists </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομιχλα</span></span>). Old word for fog, here alone in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Driven by a storm </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο λαιλαπος ελαυνομενα</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Λαιλαπς</span></span> is a squall (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:37">Mark 4:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:23">Luke 8:23</span>, only other N.T. examples). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:4">James 3:4</span> for another example of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελαυνω</span></span> for driving power of wind and waves.</p>
<p><b>For whom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οις</span></span>). Dative case of personal interest.</p>
<p><b>The blackness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ζοφος</span></span>). See verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:4">2 Peter 2:4</span> for this word.</p>
<p><b>Hath been reserved </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τετηρητα</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρεω</span></span>, for which see verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:4">2 Peter 2:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:9">2 Peter 2:9</span>. </p>
