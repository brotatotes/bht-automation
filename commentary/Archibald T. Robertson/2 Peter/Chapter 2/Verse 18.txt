<p><b> Great swelling words </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερογκα</span></span>). Old compound adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ογκος</span></span>, a swelling, swelling above and beyond), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:16">Jude 1:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>Of vanity </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ματαιοτητος</span></span>). Late and rare word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ματαιος</span></span>, empty, vain), often in LXX, in N.T. here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:20">Romans 8:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:17">Ephesians 4:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>By lasciviousness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασελγειαις</span></span>). Instrumental plural, "by lascivious acts." Note asyndeton as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:9">2 Peter 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:17">2 Peter 1:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>Those who are just escaping </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους ολιγως αποφευγοντας</span></span>). So A B read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ολιγως</span></span> (slightly, a little), while Aleph C K L P read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οντως</span></span> (actually). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ολιγως</span></span> late and rare, only here in N.T. So again the Textus Receptus has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποφυγοντας</span></span> (second aorist active participle, clean escaped) while the correct text is the present active <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποφευγοντας</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>From them that live in error </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους εν πλανη αναστρεφομενους</span></span>). Accusative case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποφευγοντας</span></span> (escaping from) according to regular idiom. Peter often uses <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναστρεφω</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναστροφη</span></span>. </p>
