<p><b> Liberty </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελευθεριαν</span></span>). Promising "personal liberty," that is license, after the fashion of advocates of liquor today, not the freedom of truth in Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:32">John 8:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:1">Galatians 5:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:13">Galatians 5:13</span>).</p>
<p><b>Themselves bondservants </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτο δουλο</span></span>). "Themselves slaves" of corruption and sin as Paul has it in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:20">Romans 6:20</span>.</p>
<p><b>Of whom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ω</span></span>). Instrumental case, but it may mean "of what."</p>
<p><b>Is overcome </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηττητα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ητταω</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηττων</span></span>, less) old verb, in N.T. only here, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:20">2 Peter 2:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:13">2 Corinthians 12:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Of the same </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτω</span></span>). "By this one (or thing)."</p>
<p><b>Is brought into bondage </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεδουλωτα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δουλοω</span></span>. Like Paul again (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:16">Romans 6:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:18">Romans 6:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:21">Romans 8:21</span>). </p>
