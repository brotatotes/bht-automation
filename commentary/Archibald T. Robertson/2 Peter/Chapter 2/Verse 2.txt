<p><b> Lascivious doings </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασελγειαις</span></span>). Associative instrumental ease after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξακολουθησουσιν</span></span> (future active, for which verb see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:16">2 Peter 1:16</span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:3">1 Peter 4:3</span> for this word.</p>
<p><b>By reason of whom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι' ους</span></span>). "Because of whom" (accusative case of relative, referring to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολλο</span></span>, many). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αυτων</span></span> (their) refers to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευδοδιδασκαλο</span></span> (false teachers) while <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολλο</span></span> to their deluded followers. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:23">Romans 2:23</span> for a picture of such conduct by Jews (quotation from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+52:5">Isaiah 52:5</span>, with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλασφημεω</span></span> used as here with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι' υμας</span></span>, because of you).</p>
<p><b>The way of truth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η οδος της αληθειας</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hοδος</span></span> (way) occurs often in N.T. for Christianity (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:2">Acts 9:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:17">Acts 16:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:25">Acts 18:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:4">Acts 22:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:14">Acts 24:14</span>). This phrase is in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+24:48">Genesis 24:48</span> as "the right road," and that is what Peter means here. So <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+119:30">Psalms 119:30</span>. See again <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:15">2 Peter 2:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:21">2 Peter 2:21</span>. </p>
