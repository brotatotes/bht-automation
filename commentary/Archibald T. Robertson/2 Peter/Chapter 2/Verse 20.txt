<p><b> After they have escaped </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποφυγοντες</span></span>). Second aorist active participle here (see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:18">2 Peter 2:18</span>).</p>
<p><b>The defilements </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα μιασματα</span></span>). Old word miasma, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιαινω</span></span>, here only in N.T. Our "miasma." The body is sacred to God. Cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιασμου</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:10">2 Peter 2:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>They are again entangled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παλιν εμπλακεντες</span></span>). Second aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμπλεκω</span></span>, old verb, to inweave (noosed, fettered), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:4">2 Timothy 2:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>Overcome </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηττωντα</span></span>). Present passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ητταοω</span></span>, for which see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:19">2 Peter 2:19</span>, "are repeatedly worsted." Predicate in the condition of first class with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span>. It is not clear whether the subject here is "the deluded victims" (Bigg) or the false teachers themselves (Mayor). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:26">Hebrews 10:26</span> for a parallel.</p>
<p><b>Therein </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτοις</span></span>). So locative case (in these "defilements"), but it can be instrumental case ("by these," Strachan).</p>
<p><b>With them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτοις</span></span>). Dative of disadvantage, "for them."</p>
<p><b>Than the first </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των πρωτων</span></span>). Ablative case after the comparative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χειρονα</span></span>. See this moral drawn by Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:45">Matthew 12:45</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:26">Luke 11:26</span>). </p>
