<p><b> It were better </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρειττον ην</span></span>). Apodosis of a condition of second class without <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span>, as is usual with clauses of possibility, propriety, obligation (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:24">Matthew 26:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:10">1 Corinthians 5:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:7">Romans 7:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:26">Hebrews 9:26</span>).</p>
<p><b>Not to have known </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη επεγνωκενα</span></span>). Perfect active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιγινωσκω</span></span> (cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιγνωσε</span></span>, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:20">2 Peter 2:20</span>) to know fully.</p>
<p><b>The way of righteousness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την οδον της δικαιοσυνης</span></span>). For the phrase see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:33">Matthew 21:33</span>, also the way of truth (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:2">2 Peter 2:2</span>), the straight way (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:15">2 Peter 2:15</span>).</p>
<p><b>After knowing it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιγνουσιν</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιγινωσκω</span></span> (just used) in the dative plural agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτοις</span></span> (for them).</p>
<p><b>To turn back </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποστρεψα</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποστρεφω</span></span>, old and common verb, to turn back, to return.</p>
<p><b>From </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span>). Out of. So in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:25">Acts 12:25</span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποστρεφω</span></span>. With ablative case. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:12">Romans 7:12</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγια</span></span> applied to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η εντολη</span></span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:14">1 Timothy 6:14</span>). II Peter strikes a high ethical note (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:5">2 Peter 1:5</span>).</p>
<p><b>Delivered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραδοθεισης</span></span>). First aorist passive participle feminine ablative singular of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραδιδωμ</span></span>. </p>
