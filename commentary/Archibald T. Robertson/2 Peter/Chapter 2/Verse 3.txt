<p><b> In covetousness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν πλεονεξια</span></span>). As did Balaam (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:15">2 Peter 2:15</span>). These licentious Gnostics made money out of their dupes. A merely intellectual Gnosticism had its fruit in immorality and fraud.</p>
<p><b>With feigned words </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλαστοις λογοις</span></span>). Instrumental case. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πλαστος</span></span> is verbal adjective (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλασσω</span></span>, to mould as from clay, for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:20">Romans 9:20</span>), here only in N.T. "With forged words." See sample in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:4">2 Peter 3:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>Shall make merchandise of you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμας εμπορευσοντα</span></span>). Future middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμπορευομα</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμπορος</span></span>, a travelling merchant), old word, to go in for trade, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:13">James 4:13</span>, which see. Cf. our emporium (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:16">John 2:16</span>, market house).</p>
<p><b>Whose sentence </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οις το κριμα</span></span>). "For whom (dative case) the sentence" (verdict, not process <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρισις</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Now from of old </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκπαλα</span></span>). Late and common compound adverb, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:5">2 Peter 3:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>Lingereth not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ αργε</span></span>). "Is not idle," old verb, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αργεω</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αργος</span></span> not working, alpha privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργον</span></span>), here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Slumbereth not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου νυσταζε</span></span>). Old and common verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυω</span></span> to nod), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:5">Matthew 25:5</span>. Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απωλεια</span></span> (destruction) three times in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:1-3">2 Peter 2:1-3</span>. </p>
