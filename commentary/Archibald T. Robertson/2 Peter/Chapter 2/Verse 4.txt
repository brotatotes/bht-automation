<p><b> For if God spared not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε γαρ ο θεος ουκ εφεισατο</span></span>). First instance (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γαρ</span></span>) of certain doom, that of the fallen angels. Condition of the first class precisely like that in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:21">Romans 11:21</span> save that here the normal apodosis (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμων ου φεισετα</span></span>) is not expressed as there, but is simply implied in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:9">2 Peter 2:9</span> by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιδεν κυριος ρυεσθα</span></span> (the Lord knows how to deliver) after the parenthesis in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:8">2 Peter 2:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>Angels when they sinned </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγγελων αμαρτησαντων</span></span>). Genitive case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφεισατο</span></span> (first aorist middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φειδομα</span></span>) and anarthrous (so more emphatic, even angels), first aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτανω</span></span>, "having sinned."</p>
<p><b>Cast them down to hell </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταρταρωσας</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταρταροω</span></span>, late word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταρταρος</span></span>, old word in Homer, Pindar, LXX <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+40:15">Job 40:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+41:23">Job 41:23</span>, Philo, inscriptions, the dark and doleful abode of the wicked dead like the Gehenna of the Jews), found here alone save in a scholion on Homer. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ταρταρος</span></span> occurs in Enoch 20:2 as the place of punishment of the fallen angels, while Gehenna is for apostate Jews.</p>
<p><b>Committed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεδωκεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραδιδωμ</span></span>, the very form solemnly used by Paul in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:21">Romans 1:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:26">Romans 1:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:28">Romans 1:28</span>.</p>
<p><b>To pits of darkness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σειροις ζοφου</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ζοφος</span></span> (kin to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνοφοσ, νεφος</span></span>) is an old word, blackness, gloom of the nether world in Homer, in N.T. only here, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:17">2 Peter 2:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jdg+1:13">Judges 1:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:18">Hebrews 12:18</span>. The MSS. vary between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σειραις</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σειρα</span></span>, chain or rope) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σειροις</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σειρος</span></span>, old word for pit, underground granary). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Σειροις</span></span> is right (Aleph A B C), dative case of destination.</p>
<p><b>To be reserved unto judgment </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις κρισιν τηρουμενους</span></span>). Present (linear action) passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρεω</span></span>. "Kept for judgment." Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:4">1 Peter 1:4</span>. Aleph A have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολαζομενους τηρειν</span></span> as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:9">2 Peter 2:9</span>. Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρισις</span></span> (act of judgment). </p>
