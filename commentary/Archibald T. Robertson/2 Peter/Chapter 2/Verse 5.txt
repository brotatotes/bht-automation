<p><b> The ancient world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρχαιου κοσμου</span></span>). Genitive case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφεισατο</span></span> (with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span> understood) repeated (the second example, the deluge). This example not in Jude. Absence of the article is common in the prophetic style like II Peter. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρχαιος</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:8">Luke 9:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>Preserved </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφυλαξεν</span></span>). Still part of the long protasis with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span>, first aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυλασσω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>With seven others </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ογδοον</span></span>). "Eighth," predicate accusative adjective (ordinal), classic idiom usually with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτον</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:20">1 Peter 3:20</span> for this same item. Some take <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ογδοον</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κηρυκα</span></span> (eighth preacher), hardly correct.</p>
<p><b>A preacher of righteousness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιοσυνης κηρυκα</span></span>). "Herald" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:7">1 Timothy 2:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:11">2 Timothy 1:11</span> alone in N.T., but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κηρυσσω</span></span> is common. It is implied in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:20">1 Peter 3:20</span> that Noah preached to the men of his time during the long years.</p>
<p><b>When he brought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαξας</span></span>). First aorist active participle (instead of the common second aorist active <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαγαγων</span></span>) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισαγω</span></span>, old compound verb to bring upon, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:28">Acts 5:28</span> (by Peter here also).</p>
<p><b>A flood </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατακλυσμον</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατακλυζω</span></span>, to inundate), only of Noah's flood in N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:38">Matthew 24:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:27">Luke 17:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:5">2 Peter 2:5</span>).</p>
<p><b>Upon the world of the ungodly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοσμο ασεβων</span></span>). Anarthrous and dative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοσμω</span></span>. The whole world were "ungodly" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασεβεις</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:18">1 Peter 4:18</span>) save Noah's family of eight. </p>
