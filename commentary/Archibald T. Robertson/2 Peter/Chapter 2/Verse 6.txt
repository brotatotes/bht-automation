<p><b> Turning into ashes </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεφρωσας</span></span>). First aorist participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεφροω</span></span>, late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεφρα</span></span>, ashes (in Dio Cassius of an eruption of Vesuvius, Philo), here alone in N.T.</p>
<p><b>The cities of Sodom and Gomorrah </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολεις Σοδομων κα Γομορρας</span></span>). Genitive of apposition after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολεις</span></span> (cities), though it makes sense as possessive genitive, for <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:7">Jude 1:7</span> speaks of the cities around these two. The third example, the cities of the plain. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+19:24">Genesis 19:24</span>.</p>
<p><b>Condemned them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεκρινεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατακρινω</span></span>, still part of the protasis with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>With an overthrow </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταστροφη</span></span>). Instrumental case or even dative like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θανατω</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατακρινω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:18">Matthew 20:18</span>. But Westcott and Hort reject the word here because not in B C Coptic.</p>
<p><b>Having made them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεθεικως</span></span>). Perfect active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>An example </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποδειγμα</span></span>). For which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:10">James 5:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:15">John 13:15</span>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:21">1 Peter 2:21</span>.</p>
<p><b>Unto those that should live ungodly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μελλοντων ασεβεσιν</span></span>). Rather, "unto ungodly men of things about to be" (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:20">Hebrews 11:20</span> for this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μελλοντων</span></span>). But Aleph A C K L read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασεβειν</span></span> (present active infinitive) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μελλοντων</span></span>=<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασεβησοντων</span></span> (future active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασεβεω</span></span>), from which we have our translation. </p>
