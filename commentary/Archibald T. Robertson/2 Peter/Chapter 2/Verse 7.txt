<p><b> And delivered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ερυσατο</span></span>). First aorist middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρυομα</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:13">Matthew 6:13</span>, still part of the protasis with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Righteous Lot </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιον Λοτ</span></span>). This adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιος</span></span> occurs three times in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:7">2 Peter 2:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:8">2 Peter 2:8</span>. See Wisdom 10:6.</p>
<p><b>Sore distressed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταπονουμενον</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταπονεω</span></span>, late and common verb, to work down, to exhaust with labor, to distress, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:24">Acts 7:24</span>.</p>
<p><b>By the lascivious life of the wicked </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο της των αθεσμων εν ασελγεια αναστροφης</span></span>). "By the life in lasciviousness of the lawless." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αθεσμος</span></span> (alpha privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεσμος</span></span>), late and common adjective (cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθεμιτος</span></span> <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:3">1 Peter 4:3</span>) for rebels against law (of nature and conscience here). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αναστροφη</span></span> is frequent in I Peter. </p>
