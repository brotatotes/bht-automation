<p><b> The Lord knoweth how </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιδεν κυριος</span></span>). The actual apodosis of the long protasis begun in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:4">2 Peter 2:4</span>. God can deliver his servants as shown by Noah and Lot and he will deliver you. The idiomatic use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιδα</span></span> and the infinitive (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρυεσθα</span></span> present middle and see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:7">2 Peter 2:7</span>) for knowing how as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:11">Matthew 7:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:17">James 4:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>The godly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευσεβεις</span></span>). Old anarthrous adjective (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σεβομα</span></span>, to worship), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:2">Acts 10:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:7">Acts 10:7</span> (by Peter). For</p>
<p><b>temptation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειρασμου</span></span>) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:2">James 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:12">James 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:6">1 Peter 1:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>To keep </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρειν</span></span>). Present active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρεω</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιδεν</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Unrighteous </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδικους</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:18">1 Peter 3:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>Under punishment </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολαζομενους</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολαζω</span></span>, old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολος</span></span>, lopped off), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:21">Acts 4:21</span>. Present tense emphasises continuity of the punishment. See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολασιν αιωνιον</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:46">Matthew 25:46</span>. </p>
