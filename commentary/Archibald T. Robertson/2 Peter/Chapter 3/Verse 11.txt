<p><b> To be dissolved </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυομενων</span></span>). Present passive participle (genitive absolute with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτων παντων</span></span>, these things all) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυω</span></span>, either the futuristic present or the process of dissolution presented.</p>
<p><b>What manner of persons </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποταπους</span></span>). Late qualitative interrogative pronoun for the older <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποδαπος</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:27">Matthew 8:27</span>, accusative case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε υπαρχειν</span></span> agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμας</span></span> (you). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:8">2 Peter 1:8</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπαρχω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>In all holy living and godliness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αγιαις αναστροφαις κα ευσεβειαις</span></span>). "In holy behaviours and pieties" (Alford). Plural of neither word elsewhere in N.T., but a practical plural in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πασα αναστροφη</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:15">1 Peter 1:15</span>. </p>
