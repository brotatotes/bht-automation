<p><b> Looking for </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσδοκωντας</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσδοκαω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:3">Matthew 11:3</span>) agreeing in case (accusative plural) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμας</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Earnestly desiring </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπευδοντας</span></span>). Present active participle, accusative also, of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπευδω</span></span>, old verb, to hasten (like our speed) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:16">Luke 2:16</span>, but it is sometimes transitive as here either (preferably so) to "hasten on the parousia" by holy living (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:12">1 Peter 2:12</span>), with which idea compare <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:10">Matthew 6:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:19">Acts 3:19</span>, or to desire earnestly (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+16:5">Isaiah 16:5</span>).</p>
<p><b>Being on fire </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυρουμενο</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυροω</span></span>, old verb (from pur), same idea as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:10">2 Peter 3:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Shall melt </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηκετα</span></span>). Futuristic present passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηκω</span></span>, old verb, to make liquid, here only in N.T. Hort suggests <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηξετα</span></span> (future middle), though <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+34:4">Isaiah 34:4</span> has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τακησοντα</span></span> (second future passive). The repetitions here make "an effective refrain" (Mayor). </p>
