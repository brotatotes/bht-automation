<p><b> But grow </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυξανετε δε</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυξανω</span></span>, in contrast with such a fate pictured in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:17">2 Peter 3:17</span>, "but keep on growing."</p>
<p><b>In the grace and knowledge </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν χαριτ κα γνωσε</span></span>). Locative case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span>. Grow in both. Keep it up. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:1">2 Peter 1:1</span> for the idiomatic use of the single article (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του</span></span>) here, "of our Lord and Saviour Jesus Christ."</p>
<p><b>To him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτω</span></span>). To Christ.</p>
<p><b>For ever </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις ημεραν αιωνος</span></span>). "Unto the day of eternity." So Sirach 18:9f. One of the various ways of expressing eternity by the use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιων</span></span>. So <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τον αιωνα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:5">John 6:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:34">John 12:34</span>. </p>
