<p><b> Is not slack concerning his promise </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου βραδυνε της επαγγελιας</span></span>). Ablative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαγγελιας</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βραδυνε</span></span> (present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βραδυνω</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βραδυς</span></span>, slow), old verb, to be slow in, to fall short of (like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λειπετα σοφιας</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:5">James 1:5</span>), here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:15">1 Timothy 3:15</span> only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Slackness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βραδυτητα</span></span>). Old substantive from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βραδυς</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:19">James 1:19</span>), here only in N.T. God is not impotent nor unwilling to execute his promise.</p>
<p><b>To youward </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις υμας</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Προς</span></span> rather than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακροθυμε</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:14">1 Thessalonians 5:14</span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:7">James 5:7</span>, etc.</p>
<p><b>Not wishing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη βουλομενος</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βουλομα</span></span>. Some will perish (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:7">2 Peter 3:7</span>), but that is not God's desire. Any (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τινας</span></span>). Rather than "some" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τινες</span></span>) above. Accusative with the infinitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολεσθα</span></span> (second aorist middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολλυμ</span></span>. God wishes "all" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντας</span></span>) to come (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χωρησα</span></span> first aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χωρεω</span></span>, old verb, to make room). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:30">Acts 17:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:32">Romans 11:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:4">1 Timothy 2:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:9">Hebrews 2:9</span> for God's provision of grace for all who will repent. </p>
