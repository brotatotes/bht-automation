<p><b> When he shall come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν ελθη</span></span>). Second aorist active subjunctive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν</span></span>, future and indefinite temporal clause (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, pp. 971ff.) coincident with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη αποκαλυψε</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:7">2 Thessalonians 1:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>To be glorified </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδοξασθηνα</span></span>). First aorist passive infinitive (purpose) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδοξαζω</span></span>, late verb, in N.T. only here and verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:12">2 Thessalonians 1:12</span>, in LXX and papyri.</p>
<p><b>In his saints </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τοις αγιοις αυτου</span></span>). The sphere in which Christ will find his glory at the Revelation.</p>
<p><b>And to be marvelled at </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα θαυμασθηνα</span></span>). First aorist passive infinitive (purpose), common verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θαυμαζω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>That believed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις πιστευσασιν</span></span>). Why aorist active participle instead of present active <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστευουσιν</span></span> (that believe)? Frame thinks that Paul thus reassures those who believed his message when there (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:6">1 Thessalonians 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:13">1 Thessalonians 2:13</span>). The parenthetical clause, though difficult, falls in with this idea:</p>
<p><b>Because our testimony unto you was believed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ επιστευθη το μαρτυριον ημων εφ' υμας</span></span>). Moffatt calls it an anti-climax.</p>
<p><b>On that day </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη ημερα εκεινη</span></span>). The day of Christ's coming (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:12">2 Timothy 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:18">2 Timothy 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:8">2 Timothy 4:8</span>). </p>
