<p><b> A manifest token of the righteous judgment of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδειγμα της δικαιας κρισεως του θεου</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδεικνυμ</span></span>, to point out, result reached (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-μα</span></span>), a thing proved. It is either in the accusative of general reference in apposition with the preceding clause as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:3">Romans 8:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:1">Romans 12:1</span>, or in the nominative absolute when <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο εστιν</span></span>, if supplied, would explain it as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:28">Philippians 1:28</span>. This righteous judgment is future and final (verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:6-10">2 Thessalonians 1:6-10</span>).</p>
<p><b>To the end that you may be counted worthy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το καταξιωθηνα υμας</span></span>). Another example of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το</span></span> for purpose with first aorist passive infinitive from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταξιοω</span></span>, old verb, with accusative of general reference <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμας</span></span> and followed by the genitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της βασιλειας</span></span> (kingdom of God). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:12">1 Thessalonians 2:12</span> for</p>
<p><b>kingdom of God </b>.</p>
