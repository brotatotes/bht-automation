<p><b> Rest with us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεσιν μεθ' ημων</span></span>). Let up, release. Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανιημ</span></span>, from troubles here (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:13">2 Corinthians 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:5">2 Corinthians 7:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:13">2 Corinthians 8:13</span>), and hereafter as in this verse. Vivid word. They shared suffering with Paul (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:5">2 Thessalonians 1:5</span>) and so they will share (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεθ'</span></span>) the</p>
<p><b>rest </b>.</p>
<p><b>At the revelation of the Lord Jesus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη αποκαλυψε του Κυριου Ιησου</span></span>). Here the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Παρουσια</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:19">1 Thessalonians 2:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:13">1 Thessalonians 3:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:23">1 Thessalonians 5:23</span>) is pictured as a</p>
<p><b>Revelation </b> (Un-veiling, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο-καλυψις</span></span>) of the Messiah as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:7">1 Corinthians 1:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:7">1 Peter 1:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:13">1 Peter 1:13</span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:30">Luke 17:30</span>). At this Unveiling of the Messiah there will come the</p>
<p><b>recompense </b> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:6">2 Thessalonians 1:6</span>) to the persecutors and the</p>
<p><b>rest </b> from the persecutions. This Revelation will be</p>
<p><b>from heaven </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απ' ουρανου</span></span>) as to place and</p>
<p><b>with the angels of his power </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετ' αγγελων δυναμεως αυτου</span></span>) as the retinue and</p>
<p><b>in flaming fire </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν πυρ φλογος</span></span>, in a fire of flame, fire characterized by flame). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:30">Acts 7:30</span> the text is</p>
<p><b>flame of fire </b> where <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυρος</span></span> is genitive (like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+66:15">Isaiah 66:15</span>) rather than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φλογος</span></span> as here (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+3:2">Exodus 3:2</span>). </p>
