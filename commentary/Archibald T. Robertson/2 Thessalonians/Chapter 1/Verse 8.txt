<p><b> Rendering </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδοντος</span></span>). Genitive of present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>, to give, agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιησου</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Vengeance </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκδικησιν</span></span>). Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκδικεω</span></span>, to vindicate, in Polybius and LXX.</p>
<p><b>To them that know not God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις μη ειδοσιν θεον</span></span>). Dative plural of perfect active participle <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδως</span></span>. Apparently chiefly Gentiles in mind (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:3">1 Thessalonians 4:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:8">Galatians 4:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:28">Romans 1:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:12">Ephesians 2:12</span>), though Jews are also guilty of wilful ignorance of God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:14">Romans 2:14</span>).</p>
<p><b>And to them that obey not the gospel of our Lord Jesus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα τοις μη υπακουουσιν τω ευαγγελιω του κυριου ημων Ιησου</span></span>). Repetition of the article looks like another class and so Jews (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:16">Romans 10:16</span>). Both Jews as instigators and Gentiles as officials (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολιταρχς</span></span>) were involved in the persecution in Thessalonica (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:5-9">Acts 17:5-9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:6">2 Thessalonians 1:6</span>). Note the use of "gospel" here as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:15">Mark 1:15</span> "believe in the gospel." </p>
