<p><b> Touching the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ της παρουσιας του Κυριου (ημων) Ιησου Χριστου</span></span>). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερωτωμεν</span></span>, to beseech, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:1">1 Thessalonians 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:12">1 Thessalonians 4:12</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hυπερ</span></span> originally meant over, in behalf of, instead of, but here it is used like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ</span></span>, around, concerning as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:4">2 Thessalonians 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:2">1 Thessalonians 3:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:10">1 Thessalonians 5:10</span>, common in the papyri (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 632). For the distinction between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Παρουσια, Επιφανεια</span></span> (Epiphany), and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αποκαλυψις</span></span> (Revelation) as applied to the Second Coming of Christ see Milligan on <i>Thessalonian Epistles</i>, pp. 145-151, in the light of the papyri. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Παρουσια</span></span> lays emphasis on the</p>
<p><b>presence </b> of the Lord with his people, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιφανεια</span></span> on his</p>
<p><b>manifestation </b> of the power and love of God, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκαλυψις</span></span> on the</p>
<p><b>revelation </b> of God's purpose and plan in the Second Coming of the Lord Jesus.</p>
<p><b>And our gathering together unto him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ημων επισυναγωγης επ' αυτον</span></span>). A late word found only in II Macc. 2:7; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:1">2 Thessalonians 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:25">Hebrews 10:25</span> till Deissmann (<i>Light from the Ancient East</i>, p. 103) found it on a stele in the island of Syme, off Caria, meaning "collection." Paul is referring to the rapture, mentioned in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:15-17">1 Thessalonians 4:15-17</span>, and the being forever with the Lord thereafter. Cf. also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:31">Matthew 24:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:27">Mark 13:27</span>. </p>
