<p><b> With all deceit of unrighteousness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν παση απατη αδικιας</span></span>). This pastmaster of trickery will have at his command all the energy and skill of Satan to mislead and deceive. How many illustrations lie along the pathway of Christian history.</p>
<p><b>For them that are perishing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις απολλυμενοις</span></span>). Dative case of personal interest. Note this very phrase in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:15">2 Corinthians 2:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:3">2 Corinthians 4:3</span>. Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αππολλυμ</span></span>, to destroy, the dreadful process goes on.</p>
<p><b>Because </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθ' ον</span></span>). In return for which things (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντ</span></span> and the genitive of the relative pronoun). Same idiom in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:20">Luke 1:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:3">Luke 12:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:44">Luke 19:44</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:23">Acts 12:23</span> and very common in the LXX.</p>
<p><b>The love of the truth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την αγαπην της αληθειας</span></span>). That is the gospel in contrast with <i>lying</i> and <i>deceit</i>.</p>
<p><b>That they might be saved </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το σωθηνα αυτους</span></span>). First aorist passive infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωζω</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το</span></span>, again, epexegetic purpose of</p>
<p><b>the truth </b> if they had heeded it. </p>
