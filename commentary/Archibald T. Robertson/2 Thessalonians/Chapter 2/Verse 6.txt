<p><b> That which restraineth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το κατεχον</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>And now you know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα νυν οιδατε</span></span>), says Paul in this cryptic apocalyptic passage. Unfortunately we do not know what Paul means by</p>
<p><b>that which restrains </b> (holds back, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεχον</span></span>), neuter here and masculine in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:7">2 Thessalonians 2:7</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο κατεχων</span></span>. "This impersonal principle or power is capable also of manifesting itself under a personal form" (Milligan). "He is Satan's messiah, an infernal caricature of the true Messiah" (Moffatt). Warfield (<i>Expositor</i>, III, iv, pp. 30ff.) suggested that the man of lawlessness is the imperial line with its rage for deification and that the Jewish state was the restraining power. But God overrules all human history and his ultimate purpose is wrought out.</p>
<p><b>To the end that </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το</span></span>). Another example of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το</span></span> and the infinitive for purpose.</p>
<p><b>In his own season </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω αυτου καιρω</span></span>). Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτου</span></span> (his), not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαυτου</span></span> (his own),</p>
<p><b>revealed in his time </b>, in the time set him by God. </p>
