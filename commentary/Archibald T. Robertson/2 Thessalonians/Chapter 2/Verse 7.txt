<p><b> For the mystery of lawlessness doth already work </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το γαρ μυστηριον ηδη ενεργειτα της ανομιας</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:13">1 Thessalonians 2:13</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεργειτα</span></span>. The genitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της ανομιας</span></span> (lawlessness) describes <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το μυστηριον</span></span> (note emphatic position of both). This mystery (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυστηριον</span></span> secret, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυστης</span></span>, an initiate, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυεω</span></span>, to wink or blink) means here the secret purpose of lawlessness already at work, the only instance of this usage in the N.T. where it is used of the kingdom of God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:11">Matthew 13:11</span>), of God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:1">1 Corinthians 2:1</span>) and God's will (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:9">Ephesians 1:9</span>), of Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:4">Ephesians 3:4</span>), of the gospel (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:9">Ephesians 6:9</span>), of faith (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:9">1 Timothy 3:9</span>), of godliness (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:16">1 Timothy 3:16</span>), of the seven stars (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:20">Revelation 1:20</span>), of the woman (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:7">Revelation 17:7</span>). But this secret will be "revealed" and then we shall understand clearly what Paul's meaning is here.</p>
<p><b>Until he be taken out of the way </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως εκ μεσου γενητα</span></span>). Usual construction with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως</span></span> for the future (aorist middle subjunctive, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενητα</span></span>). Note absence of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span> as often in N.T. and the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κοινη</span></span>. Paul uses <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως</span></span> only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:5">1 Corinthians 4:5</span>. When the obstacle is removed then the mystery of lawlessness will be revealed in plain outline. </p>
