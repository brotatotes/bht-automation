<p><b> We command and exhort </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραγγελλομεν κα παρακαλουμεν</span></span>). Paul asserts his authority as an apostle and pleads as a man and minister.</p>
<p><b>That with quietness they work, and eat their own bread </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μετα ησυχιας εργαζομενο τον εαυτων αρτον εσθιωσιν</span></span>). Substance of the command and exhortation by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the present subjunctive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσθιωσιν</span></span>. Literally,</p>
<p><b>that working with quietness they keep on eating their own bread </b>. The precise opposite of their conduct in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:11">2 Thessalonians 3:11</span>. </p>
