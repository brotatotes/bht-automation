<p><b> But ye, brethren, be not weary in well-doing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμεις δε, αδελφοι, μη ενκακησητε καλοποιουντες</span></span>). Emphatic position of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμεις</span></span> in contrast to these piddlers. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μη</span></span> and the aorist subjunctive is a prohibition against beginning an act (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, pp. 851-4). It is a late verb and means to behave badly in, to be cowardly, to lose courage, to flag, to faint, (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν, κακος</span></span>) and outside of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:1">Luke 18:1</span> in the N.T. is only in Paul's Epistles (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:13">2 Thessalonians 3:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:1">2 Corinthians 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:16">2 Corinthians 4:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:9">Galatians 6:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:13">Ephesians 3:13</span>). It occurs in Polybius. The late verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλοποιεω</span></span>, to do the fair (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλος</span></span>) or honourable thing occurs nowhere else in the N.T., but is in the LXX and a late papyrus. Paul uses <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το καλον ποιειν</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+13:7">2 Corinthians 13:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:9">Galatians 6:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:21">Romans 7:21</span> with the same idea. He has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαθοποιεω</span></span>, to do good, in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:18">1 Timothy 6:18</span>. </p>
