<p><b> And we have confidence </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεποιθομεν</span></span>). Second perfect indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειθω</span></span>, to persuade, intransitive in this tense, we are in a state of trust.</p>
<p><b>In the Lord touching you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν κυριω εφ' υμας</span></span>). Note the two prepositions, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> in the sphere of the Lord (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:1">1 Thessalonians 4:1</span>) as the <i>ground</i> of Paul's confident trust, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφ'</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>) with the accusative (towards you) where the dative could have been used (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:3">2 Corinthians 2:3</span>).</p>
<p><b>Ye both do and will do </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">[και] ποιειτε κα ποιησετε</span></span>). Compliment and also appeal, present and future tenses of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>The things which we command </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α παραγγελλομεν</span></span>). Note of apostolic authority here, not advice or urging, but command. </p>
