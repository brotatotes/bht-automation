<p><b> Not because we have not the right </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουχ οτ ουκ εχομεν εξουσιαν</span></span>). Paul is sensitive on his</p>
<p><b>right </b> to receive adequate support (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:6">1 Thessalonians 2:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:1">1 Thessalonians 2:1</span> where he uses the same word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξουσιαν</span></span> in the long defence of this</p>
<p><b>right </b>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:1-27">1 Corinthians 9:1-27</span>). So he here puts in this limitation to avoid misapprehension. He did allow churches to help him where he would not be misunderstood (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:7-11">2 Corinthians 11:7-11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:45">Philippians 4:45</span>). Paul uses <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουχ οτ</span></span> elsewhere to avoid misunderstanding (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:24">2 Corinthians 1:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:5">2 Corinthians 3:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:17">Philippians 4:17</span>).</p>
<p><b>But to make ourselves an ensample unto you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ' ινα εαυτους τυπον δωμεν υμιν</span></span>). Literally,</p>
<p><b>but that we might give ourselves a type to you </b>. Purpose with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>. On <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυπον</span></span> see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:7">1 Thessalonians 1:7</span>. </p>
