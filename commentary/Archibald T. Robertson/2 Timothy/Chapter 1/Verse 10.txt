<p><b> But hath now been manifested </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανερωθεισαν δε νυν</span></span>). First aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανεροω</span></span> agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριν</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:3">Titus 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:26">Colossians 1:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:4">Colossians 3:4</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανεροω</span></span> and the contrast made.</p>
<p><b>By the appearing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια της επιφανειας</span></span>). Only here of the Incarnation (except the verb, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:11">Titus 2:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:4">Titus 3:4</span>), but for the second coming see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:13">Titus 2:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Who abolished death </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταργησαντος μεν τον θανατον</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταργεω</span></span>, the very phrase in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:26">1 Corinthians 15:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:14">Hebrews 2:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>Brought to light </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωτισαντος δε</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωτιζω</span></span>, literary <i>Koine</i> word for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:5">1 Corinthians 4:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:18">Ephesians 1:18</span>, to turn the light on.</p>
<p><b>Life and incorruption </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωην κα αφθαρσιαν</span></span>). The opposite of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θανατος</span></span>, "life and immortality" (unchangeable life). </p>
