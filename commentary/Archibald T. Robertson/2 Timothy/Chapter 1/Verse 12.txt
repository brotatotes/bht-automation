<p><b> These things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταυτα</span></span>). His imprisonment in Rome.</p>
<p><b>Yet I am not ashamed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ' ουκ επαισχυνομα</span></span>). Plain reference to the exhortation to Timothy in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:8">2 Timothy 1:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>Him whom I have believed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ω πεπιστευκα</span></span>). Dative case of the relative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ω</span></span>) with the perfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστευω</span></span>, the antecedent to the relative not expressed. It is not an indirect question. Paul knows Jesus Christ whom he has trusted.</p>
<p><b>I am persuaded </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπεισμα</span></span>). See verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:5">2 Timothy 1:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>To guard </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυλαξα</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυλασσω</span></span>, the very word used in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:20">1 Timothy 6:20</span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραθηκην</span></span> as here, to guard against robbery or any loss.</p>
<p><b>That which I have committed unto him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την παραθηκην μου</span></span>). Literally, "my deposit," as in a bank, the bank of heaven which no burglar can break (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:19">Matthew 6:19</span>). See this word also in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:14">2 Timothy 1:14</span>. Some MSS. have the more common <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρακαταθηκη</span></span> (a sort of double deposit, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span>, beside, down, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Against that day </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις εκεινην την ημεραν</span></span>). The day of Christ's second coming. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:18">2 Timothy 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:8">2 Timothy 4:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:10">2 Thessalonians 1:10</span>, and often in the Gospels. Elsewhere, the day of the Lord (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:2">1 Thessalonians 5:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:2">2 Thessalonians 2:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:8">1 Corinthians 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:14">2 Corinthians 1:14</span>), the day of Christ or Jesus Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:6">Philippians 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:10">Philippians 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:16">Philippians 2:16</span>), the day (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:4">1 Thessalonians 5:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:13">1 Corinthians 3:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:12">Romans 13:12</span>), the day of redemption (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:20">Ephesians 4:20</span>), the day of judgment (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:5">Romans 2:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:16">Romans 2:16</span>). </p>
