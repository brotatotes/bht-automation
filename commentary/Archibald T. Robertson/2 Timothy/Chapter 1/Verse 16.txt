<p><b> Grant mercy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δωιη ελεος</span></span>). The phrase nowhere else in the N.T. Second aorist active optative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>, the usual form being <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοιη</span></span>. This is the usual construction in a wish about the future.</p>
<p><b>Unto the house of Onesiphorus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω Ονησιφορου οικω</span></span>). The same phrase in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:19">2 Timothy 4:19</span>. Apparently Onesiphorus is now dead as is implied by the wish in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:18">2 Timothy 1:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>For he oft refreshed me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ πολλακις με ανεψυξεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναψυχω</span></span>, old verb, to cool again, in LXX and <i>Koine</i> often, here only in N.T., but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναψυξις</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:20">Acts 3:20</span>. In the first imprisonment or the second. If he lost his life for coming to see Paul, it was probably recently during this imprisonment.</p>
<p><b>Was not ashamed of my chain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλυσιν μου ουκ επαισχυνθη</span></span>). Passive deponent again (first aorist indicative) with accusative as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:8">2 Timothy 1:8</span>. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλυσιν</span></span> (chain) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:20">Ephesians 6:20</span>. Note absence of augment in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαισχυνθη</span></span>. </p>
