<p><b> I thank </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριν εχω</span></span>). "I have gratitude." As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:12">1 Timothy 1:12</span>. Robinson cites examples of this phrase from the papyri. It occurs also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:9">Luke 17:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:47">Acts 2:47</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χαρις</span></span> in doxologies Paul uses (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:57">1 Corinthians 15:57</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:14">1 Corinthians 2:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+8:16">1 Corinthians 8:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:15">1 Corinthians 9:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:17">Romans 6:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:25">Romans 7:25</span>). His usual idiom is <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευχαριστω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:4">1 Corinthians 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:8">Romans 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:4">Philemon 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:3">Philippians 1:3</span>) or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευχαριστουμεν</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:2">1 Thessalonians 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:3">Colossians 1:3</span>) or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου παυομα ευχαριστων</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:16">Ephesians 1:16</span>) or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευχαριστειν οφειλομεν</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:3">2 Thessalonians 1:3</span>).</p>
<p><b>Whom I serve from my forefathers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ω λατρευω απο προγονων</span></span>). The relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ω</span></span> is the dative case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λατρευω</span></span> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:9">Romans 1:9</span> for this verb), progressive present (I have been serving). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προγονων</span></span> (forefathers) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:4">1 Timothy 5:4</span>. Paul claims a pious ancestry as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:14">Acts 24:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:5">Acts 26:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:14">Galatians 2:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:4-7">Philippians 3:4-7</span>.</p>
<p><b>In a pure conscience </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν καθαρα συνειδησε</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:5">1 Timothy 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:1">Acts 23:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Unceasing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδιαλειπτον</span></span>). Late and rare compound, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:2">Romans 9:2</span> which see. The adverb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδιαλειπτως</span></span> is more frequent (in the papyri, literary <i>Koine</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:2">1 Thessalonians 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:9">Romans 1:9</span>). The adjective here is the predicate accusative, "how I hold the memory concerning thee unceasing." The use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδιαλειπτως</span></span> (adverb) is a sort of epistolary formula (papyri, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:2">1 Thessalonians 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:13">1 Thessalonians 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:17">1 Thessalonians 5:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:9">Romans 1:9</span>).</p>
<p><b>Remembrance </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μνειαν</span></span>). Old word, in N.T. only Pauline (seven times, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:2">1 Thessalonians 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:9">Romans 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:3">Philippians 1:3</span>). </p>
