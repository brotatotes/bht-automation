<p><b> Night and day </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυκτος κα ημερας</span></span>). Genitive of time, "by night and by day." As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:9">1 Thessalonians 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:10">1 Thessalonians 3:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Longing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιποθων</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιποθεω</span></span>, old word, eight times in Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:6">1 Thessalonians 3:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:8">Philippians 1:8</span>, etc.).</p>
<p><b>Remembering thy tears </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεμνημενος σου των δακρυων</span></span>). Perfect middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιμνησκω</span></span>, old and common verb with the genitive, only here in the Pastorals and elsewhere by Paul only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:2">1 Corinthians 11:2</span>. Probably an allusion to the scene at Miletus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:37">Acts 20:37</span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:19">Acts 20:19</span>.</p>
<p><b>That I may be filled with joy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα χαρας πληρωθω</span></span>). Final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροω</span></span> (with genitive case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρας</span></span>), a verb common with Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:4">Romans 8:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:8">Romans 13:8</span>). </p>
