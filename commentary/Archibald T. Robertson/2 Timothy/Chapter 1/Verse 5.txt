<p><b> Having been reminded </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπομνησιν λαβων</span></span>). "Having received (second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>) a reminder" (old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπομιμνησκω</span></span>, to remind, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:13">1 Peter 1:13</span>). For the idiom see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:8">Romans 7:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:11">Romans 7:11</span>. A reminder by another while <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναμνησις</span></span> remembrance (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:24">1 Corinthians 11:24</span>) is rather a recalling by oneself (Vincent).</p>
<p><b>Of the unfeigned faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της ανυποκριτου πιστεως</span></span>). Late compound for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:6">2 Corinthians 6:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:9">Romans 12:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>Dwelt </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενωικησεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενοικεω</span></span>, old verb, in N.T. only in Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:11">Romans 8:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:16">Colossians 3:16</span>).</p>
<p><b>First </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτον</span></span>). Adverb, not adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτη</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>In thy grandmother Lois </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη μαμμη Λωιδ</span></span>). Old word, originally the infantile word for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μητηρ</span></span> (mother), then extended by writers to grandmother as here. Common for grandmother in the papyri. Lois is the mother of Eunice, Timothy's mother, since Timothy's father was a Greek (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:1">Acts 16:1</span>). Probably both grandmother and mother became Christians.</p>
<p><b>I am persuaded </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπεισμα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειθω</span></span>, "I stand persuaded." In the Pastorals only here and verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:12">2 Timothy 1:12</span>, common in Paul's other writings (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:38">Romans 8:38</span>, etc.). </p>
