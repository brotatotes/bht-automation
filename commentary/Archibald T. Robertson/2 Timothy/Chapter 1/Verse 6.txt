<p><b> For the which cause </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι' ην αιτιαν</span></span>). "For which cause," stronger than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διο</span></span>. So in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:12">2 Timothy 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:13">Titus 1:13</span>. Only example of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιτια</span></span> by Paul save in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:20">Acts 28:20</span>.</p>
<p><b>I put thee in remembrance </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναμιμνησκω</span></span>). Old compound to remind (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:17">1 Corinthians 4:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:15">2 Corinthians 7:15</span>).</p>
<p><b>That thou stir up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σε αναζωπυρειν</span></span>). Present active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναζωπυρεω</span></span>, old double compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανα</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωπυρον</span></span>, live coal, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωος</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυρ</span></span>, then the bellows for kindling), to rekindle, to stir into flame, to keep blazing (continuous action, present time), only here in N.T. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:19">1 Thessalonians 5:19</span> for the figure of fire concerning the Holy Spirit. See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπτω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:49">Luke 12:49</span>.</p>
<p><b>The gift of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το χαρισμα του θεου</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:14">1 Timothy 4:14</span>. Here Paul says <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μου</span></span> (my), there he mentions the presbytery. Paul felt a deep personal interest in Timothy. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:7">1 Corinthians 7:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:23">Romans 6:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:29">Romans 11:29</span> for the gift of God. </p>
