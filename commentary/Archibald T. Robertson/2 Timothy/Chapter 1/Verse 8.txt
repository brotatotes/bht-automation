<p><b> Be not ashamed of </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη επαισχυνθηις</span></span>). First aorist (ingressive) passive subjunctive (in prohibition) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαισχυνομα</span></span>, old word, to be ashamed. Again in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:16">2 Timothy 1:16</span> without augment (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαισχυνθην</span></span>), transitive use of the passive voice as often in the <i>Koine</i> (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 818). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:16">Romans 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:21">Romans 6:21</span>. "Do not become ashamed" (as he had not).</p>
<p><b>The testimony of our Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το μαρτυριον του κυριου</span></span>). For the old word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυριον</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:6">1 Corinthians 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:1">1 Corinthians 2:1</span>. Paul probably has in mind the saying of Jesus preserved in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:38">Mark 8:38</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:26">Luke 9:26</span>). See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:12">2 Timothy 2:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>His prisoner </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον δεσμιον αυτου</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:12">Philippians 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:1">Philemon 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:9">Philemon 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:1">Ephesians 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:1">Ephesians 4:1</span> (the first Roman captivity). Paul is in his last captivity and refers to it again in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:16">2 Timothy 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:9">2 Timothy 2:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>Suffer hardship with </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνκακοπαθησον</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of the double compound <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνκακοπαθεω</span></span>, first known use and in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:3">2 Timothy 2:3</span> (in eccles. writers). But <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακοπαθεω</span></span>, to suffer evil, is old verb (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:9">2 Timothy 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:5">2 Timothy 4:5</span>). Paul is fond of compounds of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν</span></span>. Paul challenges Timothy by this verb which he apparently coins for the purpose to a joint (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν</span></span>) suffering with the Lord Jesus and Paul "for the gospel" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω ευαγγελιω</span></span>, dative case rather than associative instrumental "with").</p>
<p><b>According to the power of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα δυναμιν θεου</span></span>). Given by God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:7">2 Corinthians 6:7</span>). </p>
