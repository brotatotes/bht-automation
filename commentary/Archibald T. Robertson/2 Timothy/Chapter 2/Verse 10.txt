<p><b> For the elect's sake </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια τους εκλεκτους</span></span>). "Because of the elect." God's elect (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:33">Romans 8:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:12">Colossians 3:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:1">Titus 1:1</span>) for whom Paul suffered so much (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:6">Colossians 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+12:15">Colossians 12:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:17">Philippians 2:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:1">Ephesians 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:13">Ephesians 3:13</span>).</p>
<p><b>That they also may obtain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα κα αυτο τυχωσιν</span></span>). Purpose clause with second aorist (effective) active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυγχανω</span></span> with genitive. "They as well as I," Paul means.</p>
<p><b>The salvation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της σωτηριας</span></span>). The final salvation "with eternal glory" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα δοξης αιωνιου</span></span>). This phrase only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:10">1 Peter 5:10</span>, but in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:17">2 Corinthians 4:17</span> we have "eternal weight of glory." </p>
