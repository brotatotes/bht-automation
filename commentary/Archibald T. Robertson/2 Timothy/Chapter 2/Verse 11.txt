<p><b> Faithful is the saying </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστος ο λογος</span></span>). The saying which follows here though it can refer to the preceding as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:9">1 Timothy 4:9</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:15">1 Timothy 1:15</span>. It is possible that from here to the end of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:13">2 Timothy 2:13</span> we have the fragment of an early hymn. There are four conditions in these verses (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:11-13">2 Timothy 2:11-13</span>), all of the first class, assumed to be true. Parallels to the ideas here expressed are found in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:5">2 Thessalonians 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:8">1 Corinthians 4:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:3">2 Corinthians 7:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:3-8">Romans 6:3-8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:1-4">Colossians 3:1-4</span>. Note the compounds with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συναπεθανομεν</span></span>,</p>
<p><b>we died with </b>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συναποθνεσκο</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:3">2 Corinthians 7:3</span>; <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνζησομεν</span></span>,</p>
<p><b>we shall live with </b>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνζαω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:3">2 Corinthians 7:3</span>; <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμβασιλευσομεν</span></span>,</p>
<p><b>we shall reign with </b>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμβασιλευω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:8">1 Corinthians 4:8</span>). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπομενομεν</span></span> (we endure) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:7">1 Corinthians 13:7</span> and for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απιστουμεν</span></span> (we are faithless) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:3">Romans 3:3</span>. The verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρνεομα</span></span>, to deny (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρνησομεθα</span></span>, we shall deny, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρνησετα</span></span>, he will deny, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρνησασθα</span></span>, deny, first aorist middle infinitive) is an old word, common in the Gospels in the sayings of Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:33">Matthew 10:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:9">Luke 12:9</span>), used of Peter (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:70">Mark 14:70</span>), and is common in the Pastorals (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:8">1 Timothy 5:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:12">Titus 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:5">2 Timothy 3:5</span>). Here in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:13">2 Timothy 2:13</span> it has the notion of proving false to oneself, a thing that Christ "cannot" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου δυνατα</span></span>) do. </p>
