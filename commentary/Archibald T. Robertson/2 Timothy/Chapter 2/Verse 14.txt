<p><b> That they strive not about words </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη λογομαχειν</span></span>). Word apparently coined by Paul from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογομαχια</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:4">1 Timothy 6:4</span> which see), a back formation in that case. A mere war of words displeases Paul. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:9">Titus 3:9</span>).</p>
<p><b>Useful </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρησιμον</span></span>). Late and rare word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χραομα</span></span>, here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>To the subverting </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ καταστροφη</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταστρεφω</span></span>, to turn down or over), here only in N.T. (except <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:6">2 Peter 2:6</span> in some MSS., not in Westcott and Hort)." Because of the overthrow" (result <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>, not aim), useless for this reason. Such war of words merely upsets the hearers. </p>
