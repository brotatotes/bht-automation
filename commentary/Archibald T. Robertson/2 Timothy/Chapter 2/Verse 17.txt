<p><b> Will eat </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νομην εξε</span></span>). "Will have (future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>) pasturage or increase" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νομη</span></span>, old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νεμω</span></span>, to pasture, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:9">John 10:9</span>).</p>
<p><b>As doth gangrene </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως γαγγραινα</span></span>). Late word (medical writers and Plutarch), only here in N.T. From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γραω</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γραινω</span></span>, to gnaw, to eat, an eating, spreading disease. Hymenaeus is probably the one mentioned in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:20">1 Timothy 1:20</span>. Nothing is known of Philetus. </p>
