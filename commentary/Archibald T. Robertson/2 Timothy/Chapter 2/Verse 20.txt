<p><b> In a great house </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν μεγαλη οικια</span></span>). Metaphor of a palace. He doubtless has the Kingdom of God in mind, but he works out the metaphor of a great house of the rich and mighty.</p>
<p><b>Vessels </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκευη</span></span>). Old word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκευος</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:21">Romans 9:21</span> for the same double use as here.</p>
<p><b>Of gold </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρυσα</span></span>). Old contracted adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρυσεος</span></span>, only here by Paul.</p>
<p><b>Of silver </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αργυρα</span></span>). Old contracted adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αργυρεος</span></span>, in N.T. here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:24">Acts 19:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:20">Revelation 9:20</span>.</p>
<p><b>Of wood </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξυλινα</span></span>). Old adjective, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:20">Revelation 9:20</span>.</p>
<p><b>Of earth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οστρακινα</span></span>). Late adjective, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οστρακον</span></span>, baked clay, in LXX, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:7">2 Corinthians 4:7</span>. </p>
