<p><b> If a man purge himself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν τις εκκαθαρη</span></span>). Paul drops the metaphor of the house and takes up the individual as one of the "vessels." Condition of third class with first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκκαθαιρω</span></span>, old verb, to cleanse out, in LXX, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:7">1 Corinthians 5:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>From these </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο τουτων</span></span>). From the vessels for dishonour of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:20">2 Timothy 2:20</span>.</p>
<p><b>Sanctified </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγιασμενον</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγιαζω</span></span>, for which verb see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:11">1 Corinthians 6:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>Meet for the master's use </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευχρηστον τω δεσποτω</span></span>). Dative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεσποτη</span></span> (for which word see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:1">1 Timothy 6:1</span>) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευχρηστον</span></span>, neuter singular like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγιασμενον</span></span> agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκευος</span></span>. Old verbal adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χραομα</span></span>, to use well), useful or usable for the master. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:11">2 Timothy 4:11</span>. See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχρηστον</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:11">2 Timothy 2:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>Prepared </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ητοιμασμενον</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετοιμαζω</span></span>, in a state of readiness, old and common word, elsewhere by Paul only <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:9">1 Corinthians 2:9</span> (LXX). </p>
