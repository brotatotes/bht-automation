<p><b> Must not strive </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου δε μαχεσθα</span></span>). Rather, "it is not necessary for him to fight" (in such verbal quibbles). The negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου</span></span> goes with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span>, not with the infinitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαχεσθα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Gentle </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηπιον</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επος</span></span>, speech), affable, mild, in N.T. only here (and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:7">1 Thessalonians 2:7</span> in some MSS.; W. H. have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νηπιος</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Teachable </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδακτικον</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:2">1 Timothy 3:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>Forbearing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεξικακον</span></span>). Late compound (from future of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεχω</span></span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεξω</span></span>, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακον</span></span>, putting up with evil). Here only in N.T. </p>
