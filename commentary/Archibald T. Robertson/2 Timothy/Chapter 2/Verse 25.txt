<p><b> Correcting </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παιδευοντα</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:12">Titus 2:12</span>. "Schooling" (Parry).</p>
<p><b>Oppose themselves </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντιδιατιθεμενους</span></span>). Present middle (direct) participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντιδιατιθημ</span></span>, late double compound (Diodorus, Philo) to place oneself in opposition, here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>If peradventure God may give </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη ποτε δωιη ο θεος</span></span>). Here Westcott and Hort read the late form of the second aorist active optative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span> for the usual <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοιη</span></span> as they do in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:18">2 Timothy 1:18</span>. But there it is a wish for the future and so regular, while here the optative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη ποτε</span></span> in a sort of indirect question is used with a primary tense <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span> (present) and parallel with an undoubted subjunctive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανανηψωσιν</span></span>, while in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:15">Luke 3:15</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη ποτε ειε</span></span> is with a secondary tense. Examples of such an optative do occur in the papyri (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 989) so that we cannot go as far as Moulton does and say that we "must" read the subjunctive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δωη</span></span> here (<i>Prolegomena</i>, pp. 55, 193).</p>
<p><b>Repentance </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετανοιαν</span></span>). "Change of mind" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:10">2 Corinthians 7:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:4">Romans 2:4</span>).</p>
<p><b>Unto the knowledge of the truth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις επιγνωσιν αληθειας</span></span>). Paul's word "full knowledge" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:9">Colossians 1:9</span>). </p>
