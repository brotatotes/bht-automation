<p><b> No soldier on service </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδεις στρατευομενος</span></span>). "No one serving as a soldier." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:7">1 Corinthians 9:7</span> for this old verb and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:3">2 Corinthians 10:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:18">1 Timothy 1:18</span> for the metaphorical use.</p>
<p><b>Entangleth himself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμπλεκετα</span></span>). Old compound, to inweave (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:29">Matthew 27:29</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλεκω</span></span>), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:20">2 Peter 2:20</span>. Present middle (direct) indicative.</p>
<p><b>In the affairs </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταις πραγματειαις</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πραγματευομα</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:13">Luke 19:13</span>), business, occupation, only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Of this life </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του βιου</span></span>). No "this" in the Greek, "of life" (course of life as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:2">1 Timothy 2:2</span>, not existence <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωη</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Him who enrolled him as a soldier </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω στρατολογησαντ</span></span>). Dative case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρεση</span></span> (first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρεσκω</span></span>, to please, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:4">1 Thessalonians 2:4</span>, purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>) of the articular first aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρατολογεω</span></span>, literary <i>Koine</i> word (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρατολογος</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρατος</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγω</span></span>), only here in N.T. </p>
