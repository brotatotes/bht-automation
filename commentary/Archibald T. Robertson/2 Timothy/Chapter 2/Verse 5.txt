<p><b> If also a man contend in the games </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν δε κα αθλη τις</span></span>). Condition of third class with present (linear) active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθλεω</span></span>, old and common verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθλος</span></span>, a contest), only this verse in N.T., but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συναθλεω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:27">Philippians 1:27</span>. Note sharp distinction between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθλη</span></span> (present subjunctive, engage in a contest in general) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθληση</span></span> (first aorist active subjunctive, engage in a particular contest). Not "except he have contended," but simply "unless he contend" (in any given case) "lawfully" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νομιμως</span></span>). Old adverb, agreeably to the law, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:8">1 Timothy 1:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>Is not crowned </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου στεφανουτα</span></span>). Present passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στεφανοω</span></span>, old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στεφανος</span></span>, crown), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:7">Hebrews 2:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:9">Hebrews 2:9</span>. One apodosis for two protases. The victor in the athletic contests was crowned with a garland. </p>
