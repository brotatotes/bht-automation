<p><b> The husbandman that laboureth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον κοπιωντα γεωργον</span></span>). "The toiling tiller of the soil" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεωργον</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γη</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργω</span></span>, worker of the earth). See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεωργιον</span></span> (field) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:9">1 Corinthians 3:9</span> and also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:7">1 Corinthians 9:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>First </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτον</span></span>). As is natural and right.</p>
<p><b>To partake </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταλαμβανειν</span></span>). Old word as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:46">Acts 2:46</span> to share in. Paul elsewhere uses <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετεχω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:12">1 Corinthians 9:12</span>. </p>
