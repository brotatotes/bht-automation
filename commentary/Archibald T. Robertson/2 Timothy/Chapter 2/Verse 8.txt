<p><b> Risen from the dead </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγηγερμενον εκ νεκρων</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγειρω</span></span>, still risen as the perfect tense shows in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:4">1 Corinthians 15:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:12-20">1 Corinthians 15:12-20</span>. Predicate accusative. "Remember Jesus Christ as risen from the dead." This is the cardinal fact about Christ that proves his claim to be the Messiah, the Son of God. Christ is central for Paul here as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:5-11">Philippians 2:5-11</span>.</p>
<p><b>Of the seed of David </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ σπερματος Δαυειδ</span></span>). The humanity of Christ as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:3">Romans 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:7">Philippians 2:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>According to my gospel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα το ευαγγελιον μου</span></span>). Paul's very phrase in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:16">Romans 2:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:25">Romans 16:25</span>. Not a written gospel, but my message. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:1">1 Corinthians 15:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:7">2 Corinthians 11:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:11">Galatians 1:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:2">Galatians 2:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:11">1 Timothy 1:11</span>. </p>
