<p><b> But abide thou </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συ δε μενε</span></span>). Emphatic contrast (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συ δε</span></span>), "But thou." Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενω</span></span>, common verb, to remain.</p>
<p><b>In the things which </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν οις</span></span>). The antecedent to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οις</span></span> is not expressed ("in which things") and the relative is attracted from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> accusative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμαθες</span></span> (didst learn, second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μανθανω</span></span>) to the case of the unexpressed antecedent (locative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Hast been assured of </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιστωθης</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστοω</span></span>, old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστος</span></span>, faithful), to make reliable, only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Knowing from whom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδως παρα τινων</span></span>). Second perfect active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιδα</span></span>. Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τινων</span></span> (ablative case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span> in an indirect question). The list included the O.T. prophets, Paul, Eunice, Lois. There ought to be moral authority in such personages. </p>
