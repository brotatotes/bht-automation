<p><b> From a babe </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο βρεφους</span></span>). Only here in the Pastorals. This teaching from the fifth year, covering the whole of Timothy's recollections. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:21">Mark 9:21</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ παιδιοθεν</span></span>, from a child.</p>
<p><b>Thou has known </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιδας</span></span>). Present active indicative, progressive perfect reaching from a babe till now. Would that Christian parents took like pains today.</p>
<p><b>The sacred writings </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιερα γραμματα</span></span>). "Sacred writings" or "Holy Scriptures." Here alone in N.T., though in Josephus (Proem to <i>Ant</i>. 3; <i>Apion</i> 1, etc.) and in Philo. The adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιερος</span></span> occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:13">1 Corinthians 9:13</span> of the temple worship, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γραμμα</span></span> in contrast to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:6">2 Corinthians 3:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:29">Romans 2:29</span> and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:47">John 5:47</span> of Moses' writings, in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:21">Acts 28:21</span> of an epistle, in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:11">Galatians 6:11</span> of letters (characters). In Ephesus there were <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εφεσια γραμματα</span></span> that were <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βεβηλα</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:19">Acts 19:19</span>), not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιερα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>To make thee wise </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σε σοφισα</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σοφιζω</span></span>, old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σοφος</span></span>), in N.T. only here, and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:16">2 Peter 1:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>Which is in </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της εν</span></span>). Common idiom with the article, "the in." The use of the Scriptures was not magic, but of value when used "through faith that is in Christ Jesus." </p>
