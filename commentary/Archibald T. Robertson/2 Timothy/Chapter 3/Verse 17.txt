<p><b> The man of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο του θεου ανθρωπος</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:11">1 Timothy 6:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>May be complete </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα η αρτιος</span></span>). Final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and present subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειμ</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αρτιος</span></span> is old word (from root <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρω</span></span>, to fit), specially adapted, here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Furnished completely </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξηρτισμενος</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαρτιζω</span></span>, rare verb, to furnish (fit) fully (perfective use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ</span></span>), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:5">Acts 21:5</span>. In Josephus. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταρτιζω</span></span>, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:40">Luke 6:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+13:11">2 Corinthians 13:11</span>. </p>
