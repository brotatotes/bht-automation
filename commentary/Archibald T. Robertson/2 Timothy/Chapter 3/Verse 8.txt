<p><b> Like as </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ον τροπον</span></span>). "In which manner." Adverbial accusative and incorporation of the antecedent <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τροπον</span></span> into the relative clause.</p>
<p><b>Jannes and Jambres </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιαννης κα Ιαμβρης</span></span>). Traditional names of the magicians who withstood Moses (<i>Targum of Jonathan</i> on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+7:11">Exodus 7:11</span>).</p>
<p><b>Withstood </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντεστησαν</span></span>). Second aorist active (intransitive) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθιστημ</span></span>, to stand against, "they stood against" (with dative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μωυσε</span></span>). Same word used of Elymas in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:8">Acts 13:8</span> and repeated here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθισταντα</span></span> (present middle indicative). Paul here pictures the seducers of the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γυναικαρια</span></span> above.</p>
<p><b>Corrupted in mind </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεφθαρμενο τον νουν</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταφθειρω</span></span>, old compound, in N.T. only here in critical text. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:3">2 Corinthians 11:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:5">1 Timothy 6:5</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαφθειρω</span></span>. The accusative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νουν</span></span> is retained in the passive.</p>
<p><b>Reprobate </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδοκιμο</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:27">1 Corinthians 9:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:16">Titus 1:16</span>. They had renounced their trust (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστιν</span></span>) in Christ. </p>
