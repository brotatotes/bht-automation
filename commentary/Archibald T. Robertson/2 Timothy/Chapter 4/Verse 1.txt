<p><b> I charge thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαμαρτυρομα</span></span>). Rather, "I testify." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:6">1 Thessalonians 4:6</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:21">1 Timothy 5:21</span> for this verb and appeal to God and Christ.</p>
<p><b>Who shall judge </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του μελλοντος κρινειν</span></span>). "The one going or about to judge" (regular idiom with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μελλω</span></span>). The quick and the dead (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωντας κα νεκρους</span></span>). "Living and dead." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:16">1 Thessalonians 4:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>And by his appearing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα την επιφανειαν</span></span>). Accusative of conjuration (verbs of swearing), after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαμαρτυρομα</span></span> as is <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασιλειαν</span></span> (by his kingdom). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:27">1 Thessalonians 5:27</span>. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιφανειαν</span></span>, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:10">2 Timothy 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:13">Titus 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:14">1 Timothy 6:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:8">2 Thessalonians 2:8</span>. </p>
