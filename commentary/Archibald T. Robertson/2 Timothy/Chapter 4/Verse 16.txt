<p><b> At my first defence </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη πρωτη απολογια</span></span>). Original sense of "apology" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:7">Philippians 1:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:16">Philippians 1:16</span>. Either the first stage in this trial or the previous trial and acquittal at the end of the first Roman imprisonment. Probably the first view is correct, though really there is no way to decide.</p>
<p><b>No one took my part </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδεις μο παρεγενετο</span></span>). "No one came by my side" (second aorist middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραγινομα</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:3">1 Corinthians 16:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>But all forsook me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα παντες με εγκατελειπον</span></span>). Same verb and tense used of Demas above (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:10">2 Timothy 4:10</span>), "But all were forsaking me" (one by one) or, if aorist <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγκατελιπον</span></span>, "all at once left me."</p>
<p><b>May it not be laid to their account </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη αυτοις λογισθειη</span></span>). First aorist passive optative in future wish with negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span>. Common Pauline verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογιζομα</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:5">1 Corinthians 13:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:3">Romans 4:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:5">Romans 4:5</span>). </p>
