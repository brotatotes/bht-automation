<p><b> But the Lord stood by me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο δε κυριος μο παρεστη</span></span>). Second aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παριστημ</span></span> (intransitive use), "took his stand by my side." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:2">Romans 16:2</span>. Clearly Jesus appeared to Paul now at this crisis and climax as he had done so many times before.</p>
<p><b>Strengthened me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεδυναμωσεν με</span></span>). "Poured power into me." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:13">Philippians 4:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>That through me the message might be fully proclaimed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα δι' εμου το κηρυγμα πληροφορηθη</span></span>). Final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροφορεω</span></span> (see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:5">2 Timothy 4:5</span>). Either to the rulers in Rome now or, if the first imprisonment, by his release and going to Spain.</p>
<p><b>And that all the Gentiles might hear </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ακουσωσιν παντα τα εθνη</span></span>). Continuation of the purpose with the aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>I was delivered out of the mouth of the lion </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερυσθην εκ στοματος λεοντος</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρυομα</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:10">1 Thessalonians 1:10</span>). A proverb, but not certain what the application is whether to Nero or to Satan (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:18">1 Thessalonians 2:18</span>) or to the lion in the arena where Paul could not be sent because a Roman citizen. </p>
