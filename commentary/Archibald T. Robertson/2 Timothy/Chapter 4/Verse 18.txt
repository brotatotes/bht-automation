<p><b> Will deliver me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρυσετα με</span></span>). Future middle. Recall the Lord's Prayer. Paul is not afraid of death. He will find his triumph in death (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:21">Philippians 1:21</span>).</p>
<p><b>Unto his heavenly kingdom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις την βασιλειαν αυτου την επουρανιον</span></span>). The future life of glory as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:24">1 Corinthians 15:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:50">1 Corinthians 15:50</span>. He will save (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωσε</span></span>, effective future) me there finally and free from all evil.</p>
<p><b>To whom be the glory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ω η δοξα</span></span>). No verb in the Greek. Paul's final doxology, his Swan Song, to Christ as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:5">Romans 9:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:27">Romans 16:27</span>. </p>
