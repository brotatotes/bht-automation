<p><b> A time when </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιρος οτε</span></span>). One of the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακαιρως</span></span> (out of season) times.</p>
<p><b>Will not endure </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ ανεξοντα</span></span>). Future middle (direct) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεχω</span></span>. "Will not hold themselves back from" (Col. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:13">2 Timothy 3:13</span>). Having itching ears (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κνηθομενο την ακοην</span></span>). Present middle (causative) participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κνηθω</span></span>, late and rare form of the Attic <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κναω</span></span>, to scratch, to tickle, here only in N.T. "Getting the ears (the hearing, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την ακοην</span></span>) tickled." The Vulgate has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρυριεντες</span></span>. Cf. the Athenians (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:21">Acts 17:21</span>). Clement of Alexandria tells of speakers tickling (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κνηθοντες</span></span>) the ears of those who want to be tickled. This is the temptation of the merely "popular" preacher, to furnish the latest tickle. </p>
