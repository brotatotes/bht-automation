<p><b> Will turn away their ears </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την ακοην αποστρεψουσιν</span></span>). Future active of old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστρεφω</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:17">1 Corinthians 12:17</span> for this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακοη</span></span>. The people stopped their ears and rushed at Stephen in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:57">Acts 7:57</span>.</p>
<p><b>Will turn aside </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκτραπησοντα</span></span>). Second future passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκτρεπω</span></span>. They prefer "myths" to "the truth" as some today turn away to "humanism," "bolshevism," "new thought" or any other fad that will give a new momentary thrill to their itching ears and morbid minds. </p>
