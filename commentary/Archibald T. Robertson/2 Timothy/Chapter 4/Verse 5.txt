<p><b> But be thou sober </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συ δε νηφε</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νηφω</span></span>, for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:6">1 Thessalonians 5:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:8">1 Thessalonians 5:8</span>. "Be sober in thy head."</p>
<p><b>Suffer hardship </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακοπαθησον</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:9">2 Timothy 2:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>Do the work of an evangelist </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργον ποιησον ευαγγελιστου</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:17">1 Corinthians 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:11">Ephesians 4:11</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευαγγελιστης</span></span>, gospelizer.</p>
<p><b>Fulfil </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροφορησον</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροφορεω</span></span>, for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+4:12">Colossians 4:12</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+4:17">Colossians 4:17</span> Paul uses <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροω</span></span> to Archippus about his ministry as he here employs <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροφορεω</span></span>. Both verbs mean to fill full. </p>
