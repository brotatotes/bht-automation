<p><b> I am already being offered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηδη σπενδομα</span></span>). Present (progressive) passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπενδω</span></span>, old verb, to pour out a libation or drink offering. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:17">Philippians 2:17</span>. "What was then a possibility is now a certainty" (Parry). The sacrifice of Paul's life-blood has begun.</p>
<p><b>Of my departure </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της αναλυσεως μου</span></span>). Our very word "analysis." Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναλυω</span></span>, to loosen up or back, to unloose. Only here in N.T., though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναλυσα</span></span> for death is used by Paul in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:23">Philippians 1:23</span> which see for the metaphor.</p>
<p><b>Is come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφεστηκεν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφιστημ</span></span> (intransitive use). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:3">1 Thessalonians 5:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:34">Luke 21:34</span>. The hour has struck. The time has come. </p>
