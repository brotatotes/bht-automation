<p><b> Henceforth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λοιπον</span></span>). Accusative case, "for the rest."</p>
<p><b>There is laid up for me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκειτα μο</span></span>). Present passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκειμα</span></span>, old verb, to be laid away. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:5">Colossians 1:5</span> for the hope laid away. Paul's "crown of righteousness" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο της δικαιοσυνης στεφανος</span></span>, genitive of apposition, the crown that consists in righteousness and is also the reward for righteousness, the victor's crown as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:25">1 Corinthians 9:25</span> which see) "is laid away" for him.</p>
<p><b>At that day </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν εκεινη τη ημερα</span></span>). That great and blessed day (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:12">2 Timothy 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:18">2 Timothy 1:18</span>).</p>
<p><b>The righteous judge </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο δικαιος κριτης</span></span>). "The just judge," the umpire who makes no mistakes who judges us all (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:10">2 Corinthians 5:10</span>).</p>
<p><b>Shall give me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποδωσε μο</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποδιδωμ</span></span>. "Will give back" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:6">Romans 2:6</span> and in full.</p>
<p><b>But also to all them that have loved his appearing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα πασιν τοις ηγαπηκοσιν την επιφανειαν αυτου</span></span>). Dative case of the perfect active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπαω</span></span>, to love, who have loved and still love his second coming. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Επιφανεια</span></span> here can as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:10">2 Timothy 1:10</span> be interpreted of Christ's Incarnation. </p>
