<p><b> Imitate not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη μιμου</span></span>). Present middle imperative in prohibition (do not have the habit of imitating) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιμεομα</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιμος</span></span>, actor, mimic), old word, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:7">2 Thessalonians 3:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:9">2 Thessalonians 3:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:7">Hebrews 13:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>That which is evil </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το κακον</span></span>). "The bad," as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:21">Romans 12:21</span> (neuter singular abstract).</p>
<p><b>But that which is good </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα το αγαθον</span></span>). "But the good." As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:21">Romans 12:21</span> again. Probably by the contrast between Diotrephes and Demetrius.</p>
<p><b>He that doeth good </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αγαθοποιων</span></span>). Articular present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαθοποιεω</span></span>, late and rare verb, in contrast with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο κακοποιων</span></span> (old and common verb) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:4">Mark 3:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:9">Luke 6:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:17">1 Peter 3:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>Is of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ του θεου εστιν</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:9">1 John 3:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>Hath not seen God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουχ εωρακεν τον θεον</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:6">1 John 3:6</span>. He does not say <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ του διαβολου</span></span> as Jesus does in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:44">John 8:44</span>, but he means it. </p>
