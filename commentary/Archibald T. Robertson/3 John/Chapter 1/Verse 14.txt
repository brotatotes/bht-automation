<p><b> I hope </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελπιζω</span></span>)</p>
<p><b>--We shall speak </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαλησομεν</span></span>). Literary plural really singular like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελπιζω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Face to face </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στομα προς στομα</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:12">2 John 1:12</span>. </p>
<p><b> Peace to thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειρηνη σο</span></span>). <i>Pax tibi</i> like the Jewish greeting <i>shalom</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:5">Luke 10:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:36">Luke 24:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:19">John 20:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:21">John 20:21</span>).</p>
<p><b>The friends </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο φιλο</span></span>). Those in Ephesus.</p>
<p><b>By name </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατ' ονομα</span></span>). John knew the friends in the church (at Pergamum or wherever it was) as the good shepherd calls his sheep by name (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:3">John 10:3</span>, the only other N.T. example of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατ' ονομα</span></span>). The idiom is common in the papyri letters (Deissmann, <i>Light</i>, etc., p. 193, note 21). </p>
