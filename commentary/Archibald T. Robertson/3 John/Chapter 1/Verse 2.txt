<p><b> I pray </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευχομα</span></span>). Here only in John's writings. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:3">Romans 9:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>In all things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ παντων</span></span>). To be taken with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευοδουσθα</span></span> and like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:1">1 Corinthians 16:1</span>, "concerning all things."</p>
<p><b>Thou mayest prosper </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σε ευοδουσθα</span></span>). Infinitive in indirect discourse (object infinitive) after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευχομα</span></span>, with accusative of general reference <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σε</span></span> (as to thee). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ευοδοω</span></span> is old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευοδος</span></span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδος</span></span>, prosperous in a journey), to have a good journey, to prosper, in LXX, in N.T. only this verse (twice), <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:2">1 Corinthians 16:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:10">Romans 1:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Be in health </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υγιαινειν</span></span>). In Paul this word always means sound teaching (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:10">1 Timothy 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:3">1 Timothy 6:3</span>), but here and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:31">Luke 5:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:10">Luke 7:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:27">Luke 15:27</span>, of bodily health. Brooke wonders if Gaius' health had caused his friends anxiety.</p>
<p><b>Even as thy soul prospereth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθως ευοδουτα σου η ψυχη</span></span>). A remarkable comparison which assumes the welfare (present middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευοδοω</span></span>) of his soul (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψυχη</span></span> here as the principle of the higher life as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:27">John 12:27</span>, not of the natural life as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:25">Matthew 6:25</span>). </p>
