<p><b> I rejoiced greatly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχαρην λιαν</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:4">2 John 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:10">Philippians 4:10</span>, not epistolary aorist, but reference to his emotions at the good tidings about Gaius.</p>
<p><b>When brethren came </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομενων αδελφων</span></span>). Genitive absolute with present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομα</span></span>, and so with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρουντων</span></span> (bare witness, present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρεω</span></span>). Present participle here denotes repetition, from time to time.</p>
<p><b>To the truth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη αληθεια</span></span>). Dative case. "As always in the Johannine writings, 'truth' covers every sphere of life, moral, intellectual, spiritual" (Brooke).</p>
<p><b>Even as thou walkest in truth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθως συ εν αληθεια περιπατεις</span></span>). "Thou" in contrast to Diotrephes (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:9">3 John 1:9</span>) and others like him. On <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιπατεω</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:6">1 John 1:6</span> and on <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αληθεια</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:4">2 John 1:4</span>. </p>
