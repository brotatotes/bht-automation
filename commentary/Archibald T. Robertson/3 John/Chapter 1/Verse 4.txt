<p><b> Greater </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μειζοτεραν</span></span>). A double comparative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-τερος</span></span> added to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μειζων</span></span>, like our "lesser" and like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαλλον κρεισσον</span></span> (more better) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:23">Philippians 1:23</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:8">Ephesians 3:8</span> we have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελαχιστοτερω</span></span>, a comparative on a superlative. Like forms occur in the vernacular papyri and even in Homer (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χειροτερος</span></span>, more worse) as also in Shakespeare.</p>
<p><b>Joy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαραν</span></span>). B reads <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριν</span></span> (grace).</p>
<p><b>Than this </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτων</span></span>). Ablative neuter plural after the comparative.</p>
<p><b>To hear of </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα ακουω</span></span>). Object clause (epexegetic) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουω</span></span>, the present active subjunctive (keep on hearing of) in apposition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτων</span></span>,</p>
<p><b>Walking in truth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αληθεια περιπατουντα</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:4">2 John 1:4</span>, which see. By the use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεκνα</span></span> John may mean that Gaius is one of his converts (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:1">1 Timothy 1:1</span>). </p>
