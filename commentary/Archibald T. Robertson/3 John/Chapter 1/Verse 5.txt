<p><b> A faithful work </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστον</span></span>). Either thus or "thou makest sure," after an example in Xenophon quoted by Wettstein (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιειν πιστα</span></span>) and parallel to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καινα ποιεω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:5">Revelation 21:5</span>. But it is not certain.</p>
<p><b>In whatsoever thou doest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο εαν εργαση</span></span>). Indefinite relative with modal <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> (=<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span>) and the first aorist middle subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργαζομα</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:23">Colossians 3:23</span> for both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργαζομα</span></span> in the same sentence.</p>
<p><b>And strangers withal </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα τουτο ξενους</span></span>). "And that too" (accusative of general reference as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:6">1 Corinthians 6:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:28">Philippians 1:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:8">Ephesians 2:8</span>). This praise of hospitality (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:13">Romans 12:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:9">1 Peter 4:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:2">1 Timothy 3:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:10">1 Timothy 5:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:8">Titus 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:2">Hebrews 13:2</span>) shows that in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:10">2 John 1:10</span> John has a peculiar case in mind. </p>
