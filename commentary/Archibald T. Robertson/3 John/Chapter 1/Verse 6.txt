<p><b> Before the church </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενωπιον εκκλησιας</span></span>). Public meeting as the anarthrous use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκκλησια</span></span> indicates, like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν εκκλησια</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:19">1 Corinthians 14:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:35">1 Corinthians 14:35</span>.</p>
<p><b>Thou wilt do well </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλως ποιησεις</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span> with adverb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλως</span></span>, a common polite phrase in letters (papyri) like our "please." See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:33">Acts 10:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:19">James 2:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:37">1 Corinthians 7:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:14">Philippians 4:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:19">2 Peter 1:19</span>.</p>
<p><b>To set forward on their journey </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προπεμψας</span></span>). First aorist active participle (simultaneous action) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προπεμπω</span></span>, to send forward, "sending forward," old word, in N.T. in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:3">Acts 15:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:38">Acts 20:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:5">Acts 21:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:6">1 Corinthians 16:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:11">1 Corinthians 16:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:16">2 Corinthians 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:24">Romans 15:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:13">Titus 3:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Worthily of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αξιως του θεου</span></span>). Precisely this phrase in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:12">1 Thessalonians 2:12</span> and the genitive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αξιως</span></span> also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:2">Romans 16:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:27">Philippians 1:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:10">Colossians 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:1">Ephesians 4:1</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:20">John 13:20</span> for Christ's words on the subject. "Since they are God's representatives, treat them as you would God" (Holtzmann). From Homer's time (<i>Od</i>. XV. 74) it was customary to speed the parting guest, sometimes accompanying him, sometimes providing money and food. Rabbis were so escorted and Paul alludes to the same gracious custom in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:24">Romans 15:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:13">Titus 3:13</span>. </p>
