<p><b> Ought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφειλομεν</span></span>). See for this word <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:6">1 John 2:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:16">1 John 3:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:11">1 John 4:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>To welcome </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπολαμβανειν</span></span>). Present active infinitive (habit of welcoming) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπολαμβανω</span></span>, old word, to take up under, to carry off (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:9">Acts 1:9</span>), to reply (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:30">Luke 10:30</span>), to suppose (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:15">Acts 2:15</span>), only here in N.T. in this sense of receiving hospitably or to take under one's protection like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποδεχομα</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:38">Luke 10:38</span>).</p>
<p><b>Such </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους τοιουτους</span></span>). "The such" according to the Greek idiom (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:16">1 Corinthians 16:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:18">1 Corinthians 16:18</span>).</p>
<p><b>That we may be </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα γινωμεθα</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the present middle subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span>, "that we may keep on becoming."</p>
<p><b>Fellow-workers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεργο</span></span>). Old compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν, εργον</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>With the truth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη αληθεια</span></span>). So associative instrumental case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν</span></span> in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεργο</span></span>, but it is not certain that this is the idea, though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεργεω</span></span> is so used with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργοις</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:22">James 2:22</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Συνεργος</span></span> itself occurs with the genitive of the person as in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεου συνεργο</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:9">1 Corinthians 3:9</span>) or with genitive of the thing <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της χαρας</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:9">1 Corinthians 3:9</span>). So then here the meaning may be either "co-workers with such brethren for the truth" (dative of advantage) or "co-workers with the truth" (associative instrumental case). </p>
