<p><b> Were looking steadfastly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ατενιζοντες ησαν</span></span>). Periphrastic imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ατενιζω</span></span>, a late intensive verb (intensive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεινω</span></span>, to stretch). Common in Acts and also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:20">Luke 4:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:56">Luke 22:56</span> as well as <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:4">Acts 10:4</span>, which see.</p>
<p><b>As he went </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορευομενου αυτου</span></span>). Genitive absolute of present middle participle. They saw him slipping away from their eyes as the cloud bore him away.</p>
<p><b>Stood by them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρειστηκεισαν αυτοις</span></span>). Past perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παριστημ</span></span> and intransitive (note in B instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span> for augment, mere itacism). </p>
