<p><b> With one accord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοθυμαδον</span></span>). Old adverb in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-δον</span></span> from adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοθυμος</span></span> and that from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομος</span></span>, same, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυμος</span></span>, mind or spirit, with the same mind or spirit. Common in ancient Greek and papyri. In the N.T. eleven times in Acts and nowhere else save <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:6">Romans 15:6</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:19">Matthew 18:19</span>.</p>
<p><b>Continued </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ησαν προσκαρτερουντες</span></span>). Periphrastic imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσκαρτερεω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς</span></span> (perfective use) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καρτερεω</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καρτερος</span></span>, strong, steadfast, like the English "carry on." Already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:9">Mark 3:9</span> which see and several times in Acts and Paul's Epistles. They "stuck to" the praying (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη προσευχη</span></span>, note article) for the promise of the Father till the answer came.</p>
<p><b>With the women </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν γυναιξιν</span></span>). Associative instrumental case plural of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γυνη</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν</span></span>. As one would expect when praying was the chief work on hand. More women certainly included than in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:2">Luke 8:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:40">Mark 15:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:55">Matthew 27:55</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:49">Luke 23:49</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:47">Mark 15:47</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:61">Matthew 27:61</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:55">Luke 23:55</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+16:1">Mark 16:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:1">Matthew 28:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:1">Luke 24:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:1">John 20:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:11-18">John 20:11-18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:9">Matthew 28:9</span>. There were probably other women also whose testimony was no longer scouted as it had been at first. Codex Bezae adds here "and children."</p>
<p><b>And Mary the mother of Jesus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα Μαριαμ τη μητρ του Ιησου</span></span>). A delicate touch by Luke that shows Mary with her crown of glory at last. She had come out of the shadow of death with the song in her heart and with the realization of the angel's promise and the prophecy of Simeon. It was a blessed time for Mary.</p>
<p><b>With his brethren </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν τοις αδελφοις αυτου</span></span>). With his brothers, it should be translated. They had once disbelieved in him (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:5">John 7:5</span>). Jesus had appeared to James (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:7">1 Corinthians 15:7</span>) and now it is a happy family of believers including the mother and brothers (half-brothers, literally) of Jesus. They continue in prayer for the power from on high. </p>
