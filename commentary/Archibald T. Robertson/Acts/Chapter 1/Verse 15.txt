<p><b> Brethren </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδελφων</span></span>). Codex Bezae has "disciples."</p>
<p><b>Multitude of persons </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οχλος ονοματων</span></span>). Literally, multitude of names. This Hebraistic use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ονομα</span></span>=person occurs in the LXX (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+1:2">Numbers 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+18:20">Numbers 18:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+3:40">Numbers 3:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+3:43">Numbers 3:43</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+26:53">Numbers 26:53</span>) and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:4">Revelation 3:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:13">Revelation 11:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Together </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ το αυτο</span></span>). The word "gathered" is not in the Greek here, but it does occur in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:34">Matthew 22:34</span> and that is undoubtedly the idea in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:35">Luke 17:35</span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:1">Acts 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:44">Acts 2:44</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:47">Acts 2:47</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:20">1 Corinthians 11:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:23">1 Corinthians 14:23</span>. So also here. They were in the same place (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το αυτο</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>About a hundred and twenty </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως εκατον εικοσ</span></span>). A crowd for "the upper room." No special significance in the number 120, just the number there. </p>
