<p><b> Was numbered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηριθμενος ην</span></span>). Periphrastic past perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταριθμεω</span></span>, old verb, but here only in the N.T. (perfective use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Received his portion </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελαχεν τον κληρον</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαγχανω</span></span>, old verb, to obtain by lot as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:9">Luke 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:24">John 19:24</span>, especially by divine appointment as here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:1">2 Peter 2:1</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κληρος</span></span> also means lot, an object used in casting lots (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:26">Acts 1:26</span>), or what is obtained by lot as here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:21">Acts 8:21</span>, of eternal salvation (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:18">Acts 26:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:12">Colossians 1:12</span>), of persons chosen by divine appointment (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:3">1 Peter 5:3</span>). From this latter usage the Latin <i>cleros, clericus</i>, our clergy, one chosen by divine lot. So Peter says that Judas "obtained by lot the lot of this ministry" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονιας</span></span>) which he had when he betrayed Jesus. The Master chose him and gave him his opportunity. </p>
