<p><b> Must </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span>). Present necessity corresponding to the old necessity (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδε</span></span>) about Judas (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:16">Acts 1:16</span>). This sentence in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:21">Acts 1:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:22">Acts 1:22</span> begins with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>That </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ω</span></span>). Locative case of the relative attracted to the case of the antecedent.</p>
<p><b>Went in and went out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισηλθεν κα εξηλθεν</span></span>). Constative aorist active.</p>
<p><b>With us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφ' ημας</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Over us </b>, the margin has it. But the full phrase would be <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφ' ημας κα αφ' ημων</span></span>. He came to us and went from us (Knowling). </p>
