<p><b> Show us the one whom thou hast chosen </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναδειξον ον εξελεξω</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναδεικνυμ</span></span>, to show up, make plain. First aorist middle indicative second person singular of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκλεγω</span></span>, to pick out, choose, select. In this prayer they assume that God has made a choice. They only wish to know his will. They call God the</p>
<p><b>heart-searcher </b> or</p>
<p><b>heart-knower </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καρδιογνωστα</span></span>, vocative singular), a late word, here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:8">Acts 15:8</span> only in the N.T. Modern physicians have delicate apparatus for studying the human heart. </p>
