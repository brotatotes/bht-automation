<p><b> As they were looking </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλεποντων αυτων</span></span>). Genitive absolute. The present participle accents the fact that they were looking directly at Jesus.</p>
<p><b>He was taken up </b> (eprth). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαιρω</span></span>, old and common verb meaning to lift up. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:51">Luke 24:51</span> we have "he was borne up" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεφερετο</span></span>) and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:2">Acts 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:11">Acts 1:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:6">1 Timothy 3:6</span> "was received up" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανελημπθη</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Received </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπελαβεν</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπολαμβανω</span></span>, literally here "took under him." He seemed to be supported by the cloud. "In glory" Paul adds in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:16">1 Timothy 3:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>Out of their sight </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο των οφθαλμων αυτων</span></span>). From their eyes (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span> with ablative case). </p>
