<p><b> Beholdeth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεωρε</span></span>). Vivid historical present and change from past time.</p>
<p><b>Opened </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεωιγμενον</span></span>, perfect passive participle with double reduplication, state of completion).</p>
<p><b>Descending </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταβαινον</span></span>). Present active participle describing the process.</p>
<p><b>Sheet </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οθονην</span></span>). Old word for linen cloth and only here in the N.T. Accusative case in apposition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκευος</span></span> (vessel).</p>
<p><b>Let down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Καθιεμενον</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Καθιημ</span></span>. Old verb, but in the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:19">Luke 5:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:25">Acts 9:25</span>. Linear action here picturing the process, "being let down."</p>
<p><b>By four corners </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεσσαρσιν αρχαις</span></span>). Instrumental case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρχη</span></span>, beginning. We say "end" or extremity for this use of the word. The picture is the sheet held up by four cords to which the sheet is fastened. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+11:12">Isaiah 11:12</span> had said that Israel would be gathered from the four corners of the earth. Knowling follows Hobart in taking the four corners of the sheet to be a medical phrase for bandage (the end of a bandage). </p>
