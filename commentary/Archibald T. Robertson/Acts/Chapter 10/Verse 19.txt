<p><b> Thought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διενθυμουμενου</span></span>). Genitive absolute of present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διενθυμεομα</span></span>, a double compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν-</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυμος</span></span>) and another <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαξ λεγομενον</span></span> save in ecclesiastical writers, though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενθυμεομα</span></span> is common enough and Textus Receptus so reads here. Peter was revolving in his mind, through and through, in and out, to find the meaning of the strange vision. </p>
