<p><b> Righteous </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιος</span></span>). In the Jewish sense as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:6">Luke 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:25">Luke 2:25</span>.</p>
<p><b>Well reported of </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρουμενος</span></span>). Present passive participle as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:3">Acts 6:3</span>. Cf. the other centurion in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:4">Luke 7:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>Nation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εθνους</span></span>). Not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαου</span></span>, for the speakers are Gentiles.</p>
<p><b>Was warned </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχρηματισθη</span></span>). First aorist passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρηματιζω</span></span>, old word for doing business, then consulting an oracle, and here of being divinely (word God not expressed) warned as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:12">Matthew 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:22">Matthew 2:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:26">Luke 2:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:7">Hebrews 11:7</span>. Then to be called or receive a name from one's business as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:26">Acts 11:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:3">Romans 7:3</span>. </p>
