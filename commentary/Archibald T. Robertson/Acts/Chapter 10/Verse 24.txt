<p><b> Was waiting </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην προσδοκων</span></span>). Periphrastic imperfect active, in eager expectation and hope, directing the mind (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκαω</span></span>) towards (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς</span></span>) anything. Old and common verb.</p>
<p><b>Near </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγκαιους</span></span>). Only instance in the N.T. of this sense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγκαιος</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγκη</span></span>, necessity, what one cannot do without, necessary (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:22">1 Corinthians 12:22</span>), duty (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:46">Acts 13:46</span>), or blood relations as here. The ancient Greek writers combined these two words (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συγγενεις</span></span>, kinsmen, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγκαιους</span></span>, necessary friends) as here. It was a homogeneous group of Gentiles close to Cornelius and predisposed to hear Peter favourably. </p>
