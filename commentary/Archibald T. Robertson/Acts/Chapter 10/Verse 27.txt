<p><b> As he talked with him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνομιλων αυτω</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνομιλεω</span></span>, rare compound and here alone in the N.T., with associative instrumental case. The uncompounded verb is common enough though in the N.T. only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:14">Luke 24:14</span> which see and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:11">Acts 20:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:26">Acts 24:26</span>.</p>
<p><b>Findeth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρισκε</span></span>). Vivid historical present indicative active.</p>
<p><b>Come together </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεληλυθοτας</span></span>). Second perfect active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνερχομα</span></span>. It was an expectant group of Gentiles eager for Peter's interpretation of the vision of Cornelius. </p>
