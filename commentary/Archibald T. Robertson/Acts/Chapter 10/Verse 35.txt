<p><b> Acceptable to him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεκτος αυτω</span></span>). Verbal adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεχομα</span></span>. <i>Acceptabilis</i>. That is to say, a Gentile would not have to become a Jew in order to become a Christian. Evidently Peter had not before perceived this fact. On the great Day of Pentecost when he spoke of the promise "to all those afar off" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:39">Acts 2:39</span>) Peter understood that they must first become Jews and then Christians. The new idea that now makes a revolution in Peter's outlook is precisely this that Christ can and will save Gentiles like this Cornelius group without their becoming Jews at all. </p>
