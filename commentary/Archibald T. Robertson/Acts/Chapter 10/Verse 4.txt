<p><b> Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυριε</span></span>). Cornelius recognizes the angel of God (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:3">Acts 10:3</span>) as God's messenger.</p>
<p><b>Are gone up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεβησαν</span></span>). Timeless second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναβαινω</span></span>. Gone up like the smoke of incense in sacrifices.</p>
<p><b>For a memorial </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις μνημοσυνον</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μνημων</span></span>. The only other instance in the N.T. is by Jesus about the act of Mary of Bethany (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:13">Matthew 26:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:9">Mark 14:9</span>). His prayers and his alms proved his sincerity and won the ear of God. </p>
