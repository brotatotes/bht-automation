<p><b> They of the circumcision which believed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο εκ περιτομης πιστο</span></span>). The believing ones of the circumcision, more exactly.</p>
<p><b>Were amazed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεστησαν</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative, intransitive, of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξιστημ</span></span>. They stood out of themselves.</p>
<p><b>On the Gentiles also </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα επ τα εθνη</span></span>). Or, even upon the Gentiles.</p>
<p><b>Was poured out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκκεχυτα</span></span>). Present perfect passive retained in indirect discourse of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκχεω</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκχυνω</span></span>, old verb, used metaphorically of the Holy Spirit also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:17">Acts 2:17</span> (from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joe+2:28">Joel 2:28</span>), <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:33">Acts 2:33</span>. </p>
