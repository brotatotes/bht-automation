<p><b> They heard </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκουον</span></span>). Imperfect active, were hearing, kept on hearing.</p>
<p><b>Speak </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαλουντων</span></span>). Present active participle, speaking, for they kept it up.</p>
<p><b>With tongues </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γλωσσαις</span></span>). Instrumental case as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:4">Acts 2:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:11">Acts 2:11</span> which see. The fuller statement there makes it clear that here it was new and strange tongues also as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:6">Acts 19:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:4-19">1 Corinthians 14:4-19</span>. This sudden manifestation of the Holy Spirit's power on uncircumcised Gentiles was probably necessary to convince Peter and the six brethren of the circumcision that God had opened the door wide to Gentiles. It was proof that a Gentile Pentecost had come and Peter used it effectively in his defence in Jerusalem (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:15">Acts 11:15</span>). </p>
