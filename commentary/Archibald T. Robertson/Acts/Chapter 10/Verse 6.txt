<p><b> Lodgeth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξενιζετα</span></span>). Present passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξενιζω</span></span> old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξενος</span></span>, a stranger as a guest. So to entertain a guest as here or to surprise by strange acts (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:20">Acts 17:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:4">1 Peter 4:4</span>).</p>
<p><b>Whose </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ω</span></span>). To whom, dative of possession.</p>
<p><b>By the seaside </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα θαλασσαν</span></span>). Along by the sea. Note accusative case. Outside the city walls because a tanner and to secure water for his trade. Some tanneries are by the seashore at Jaffa today. </p>
