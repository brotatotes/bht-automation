<p><b> On the morrow </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη επαυριον</span></span>). Locative case of article with the compound adverb (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημερα</span></span> day being understood), the second day after leaving Caesarea, 28 miles from Joppa. The third day (the next morrow, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:23">Acts 10:23</span>) they start back home and the fourth day (on the morrow again, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:24">Acts 10:24</span>) they reach Caesarea.</p>
<p><b>As they </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινων</span></span>). The party of three from Caesarea. Genitive absolute with present participle <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδοιπορουντων</span></span> (journeying) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγγιζοντων</span></span> (drew nigh).</p>
<p><b>The housetop </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το δωμα</span></span>). Old word and in Gospels (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:19">Luke 3:19</span>, etc.), but only here in Acts. From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεμω</span></span>, to build, and so any part of the building (hall, dining room, and then roof). The roof was nearly flat with walls around and so was a good place for meditation and prayer and naps. </p>
