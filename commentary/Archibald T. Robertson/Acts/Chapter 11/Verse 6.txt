<p><b> When I had fastened my eyes </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ατενισας</span></span>). This personal touch Peter adds from his own experience. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:20">Luke 4:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:4">Acts 3:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:12">Acts 3:12</span> for this striking verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ατενιζω</span></span>, to stretch the eyes towards, first aorist active participle here.</p>
<p><b>I considered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατανοεω</span></span>). Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατανεοω</span></span> to put the mind down on, to ponder, I was pondering.</p>
<p><b>And saw </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ειδον</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative, saw in a flash. </p>
