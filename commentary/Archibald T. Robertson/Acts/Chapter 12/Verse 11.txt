<p><b> Was come to himself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν εαυτω γενομενος</span></span>). Second aorist middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> and the locative case, "becoming at himself." In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:17">Luke 15:17</span> we have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις εαυτον ελθων</span></span> (coming to himself, as if he had been on a trip away from himself).</p>
<p><b>Now I know of a truth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυν οιδα αληθως</span></span>). There was no further confusion of mind that it was an ecstasy as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:10">Acts 10:10</span>. But he was in peril for the soldiers would soon learn of his escape, when the change of guards came at 6 A.M.</p>
<p><b>Delivered me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξειλατο με</span></span>). Second aorist middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαιρεω</span></span>. The Lord rescued me of himself by his angel.</p>
<p><b>Expectation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσδοκιας</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσδοκαω</span></span>, to look for. In the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:26">Luke 21:26</span>. James had been put to death and the Jewish people were eagerly waiting for the execution of Peter like hungry wolves. </p>
