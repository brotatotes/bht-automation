<p><b> When he knocked at the door of the gate </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρουσαντος αυτου την θυραν του πυλωνος</span></span>). Genitive absolute with aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρουω</span></span>, common verb to knock or knock at. So from the outside (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:25">Luke 13:25</span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πυλων</span></span> here is the gateway or passageway from the door (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυρα</span></span>) that leads to the house. In verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:14">Acts 12:14</span> it is still the passageway without the use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυρα</span></span> (door, so for both door and passageway).</p>
<p><b>To answer </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπακουσα</span></span>). To listen under before opening. First aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπακουω</span></span>, common verb to obey, to hearken.</p>
<p><b>A maid </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παιδισκη</span></span>). Portress as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:17">John 18:17</span>. A diminutive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παις</span></span>, a female slave (so on an ostracon of second century A.D., Deissmann, <i>Light from the Ancient East</i>, p. 200).</p>
<p><b>Rhoda </b>. A rose. Women can have such beautiful names like Dorcas (Gazelle), Euodia (Sweet Aroma), Syntyche (Good Luck). Mark or Peter could tell Luke her name. </p>
