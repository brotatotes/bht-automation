<p><b> When she knew </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιγνουσα</span></span>). Second aorist (ingressive) active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιγινωσκω</span></span>, to know fully or in addition (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>), to recognize. She knew Peter and his voice from his frequent visits there.</p>
<p><b>For joy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο της χαρας</span></span>). From her joy (ablative case), life-like picture of the maid who left Peter standing outside with the door to the passageway unopened. Note the aorist tenses for quick action (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ ηνοιξεν</span></span>), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισδραμουσα</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειστρεχω</span></span>, defective verb, only here in the N.T.), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απηγγειλεν</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Stood </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστανα</span></span>). Second perfect active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span>, intransitive, in indirect assertion with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον Πετρον</span></span> (Peter) accusative of general reference. The slave girl acted as if she were a member of the family (Furneaux), but she left Peter in peril. </p>
