<p><b> He examined </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανακρινας</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανακρινω</span></span>, old verb to sift up and down, to question thoroughly, in a forensic sense (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:14">Luke 23:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:9">Acts 4:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:19">Acts 12:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:18">Acts 28:18</span>).</p>
<p><b>That they should be put to death </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαχθηνα</span></span>). First aorist passive infinitive (indirect command) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαγω</span></span>, old verb to lead away, especially to execution as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:31">Matthew 27:31</span>. Here it is used absolutely. This was the ordinary Roman routine and not a proof of special cruelty on the part of Herod Agrippa.</p>
<p><b>Tarried </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διετριβεν</span></span>). Imperfect active. Herod Agrippa made his home in Jerusalem, but he went to Caesarea to the public games in honour of Emperor Claudius. </p>
