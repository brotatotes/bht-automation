<p><b> Was highly displeased </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην θυμομαχων</span></span>). Periphrastic imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυμομαχεω</span></span>, late compound of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυμος</span></span> (passionate heat) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαχομα</span></span>, to fight. Only here in the N.T., to fight desperately, to have a hot quarrel. Whether it was open war with the Phoenicians or just violent hostility we do not know, save that Phoenicia belonged to Syria and Herod Agrippa had no authority there. The quarrel may have been over commercial matters.</p>
<p><b>They came with one accord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοθυμαδον παρησαν</span></span>). The representatives of Tyre and Sidon. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:14">Acts 1:14</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοθυμαδον</span></span>. Tyre was a colony of Sidon and had become one of the chief commercial cities of the world by reason of the Phoenician ships.</p>
<p><b>The king's chamberlain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον επ του κοιτωνος του βασιλεος</span></span>). The one over the bedchamber (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοιτωνος</span></span>, late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοιτη</span></span>, bed, here only in the N.T.).</p>
<p><b>Made their friend </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεισαντες</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειθω</span></span>, to persuade. Having persuaded (probably with bribes as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:14">Matthew 28:14</span>).</p>
<p><b>They asked for peace </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηιτουντο ειρηνην</span></span>). Imperfect middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιτεω</span></span>, kept on asking for peace.</p>
<p><b>Because their country was fed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια το τρεφεσθα αυτων την χοραν</span></span>). Causal sentence with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span> and the articular infinitive (present passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρεφω</span></span>, to nourish or feed) and the accusative of general reference, "because of the being fed as to their country." Tyre and Sidon as large commercial cities on the coast received large supplies of grain and fruits from Palestine. Herod had cut off the supplies and that brought the two cities to action. </p>
