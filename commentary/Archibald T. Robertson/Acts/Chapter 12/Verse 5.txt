<p><b> Therefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεν ουν</span></span>). Because of the preceding situation.</p>
<p><b>Was kept </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετηρειτο</span></span>). Imperfect passive, continuously guarded, waiting for the feast to be over.</p>
<p><b>But prayer was made earnestly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσευχη δε ην εκτενως γινομενη</span></span>). Probably <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span> here is not adversative (but), merely parallel (and) as Page argues. It was a crisis for the Jerusalem church. James had been slain and Peter was to be the next victim. Hence "earnestly" (late adverb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκτενης</span></span>, strained, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκτεινω</span></span>, to stretch. In the N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:44">Luke 22:44</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:22">1 Peter 1:22</span>) prayer was</p>
<p><b>going up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομενη</span></span>, present middle participle, periphrastic imperfect with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην</span></span>). It looked like a desperate case for Peter. Hence the disciples prayed the more earnestly. </p>
