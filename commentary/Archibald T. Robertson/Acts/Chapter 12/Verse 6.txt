<p><b> Was about to bring him forth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ἤμελλεν προσαγαγειν</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προαγαγειν</span></span>). The MSS. vary, but not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγαγειν</span></span> of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:4">Acts 12:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>The same night </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη νυκτ εκεινη</span></span>). Locative case,</p>
<p><b>on that (very) night </b>.</p>
<p><b>Was sleeping </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην κοιμωμενος</span></span>). Periphrastic middle imperfect.</p>
<p><b>Bound with two chains </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεδεμενος αλυσεσιν δυσιν</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεω</span></span>, to bind, followed by instrumental case. One chain was fastened to each soldier (one on each side of Peter).</p>
<p><b>Kept </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετηρουν</span></span>). Imperfect active, were keeping. Two guards outside before the door and two inside, according to Roman rule. Did Peter recall the prophecy of Jesus that he should be put to death in his old age (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:18">John 21:18</span>)? Jesus had not said, as Furneaux does, that he would die by crucifixion. </p>
