<p><b> Gird thyself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωσα</span></span>). Direct middle first aorist (ingressive) imperative (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, pp. 806f.) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωννυμ</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωννυω</span></span>). Old verb, but in the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:18">John 21:18</span> (twice to Peter) where the active voice and the reflexive pronoun occur in the first example. The girdle was worn round the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χιτων</span></span> or undergarment.</p>
<p><b>Bind on </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποδησα</span></span>). Indirect middle (by yourself or for yourself) first aorist imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποδεω</span></span>, to bind under, old verb, only three times in the N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:9">Mark 6:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:8">Acts 12:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:15">Ephesians 6:15</span> (middle)).</p>
<p><b>Sandals </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σανδαλια</span></span>). Persian word common from Herodotus on, a sole made of wood or leather covering the bottom of the foot and bound on with thongs. In the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:9">Mark 6:9</span>. In the LXX used indiscriminately with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποδημα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Cast about thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιβαλου</span></span>). Second aorist middle (indirect) imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιβαλλω</span></span>, old and common verb to throw around, especially clothing around the body as here. The <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιματιον</span></span> (outer garment) was put over the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χιτων</span></span>. It was not a hurried flight.</p>
<p><b>Follow me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακολουθε μο</span></span>). Present (linear) active imperative, keep on following me (associative instrumental case). </p>
