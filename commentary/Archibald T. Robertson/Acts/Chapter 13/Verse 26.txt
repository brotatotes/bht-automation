<p><b> To us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημιν</span></span>). Both Jews and Gentiles, both classes in Paul's audience, dative of advantage.</p>
<p><b>Is sent forth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαπεσταλη</span></span>). Second aorist passive indicative of the double compound verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαποστελλω</span></span>, common verb to send out (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ</span></span>) and forth (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span>). It is a climacteric or culminative aorist tense. It has come to us in one day, this glorious promise.</p>
<p><b>The word of this salvation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο λογος της σωτηριας ταυτης</span></span>). The message of Jesus as Saviour (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:23">Acts 13:23</span>), long ago promised and now come to us as Saviour. </p>
