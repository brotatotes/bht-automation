<p><b> Though they found no cause of death </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηδεμιαν αιτιαν θανατου ευροντες</span></span>). Second aorist active with usual negative of the participle. As a matter of fact the Sanhedrin did charge Jesus with blasphemy, but could not prove it (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:65">Matthew 26:65</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:24">Matthew 27:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:22">Luke 23:22</span>). At this time no Gospel had probably been written, but Paul knew that Jesus was innocent. He uses this same idiom about his own innocence (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:18">Acts 28:18</span>).</p>
<p><b>That he should be slain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναιρεθηνα αυτον</span></span>). First aorist passive infinitive, the accusative case, the direct object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηιτησαντο</span></span> (first aorist middle indicative, asked as a favour to themselves). </p>
