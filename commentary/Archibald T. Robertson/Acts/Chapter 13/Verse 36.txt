<p><b> His own generation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδια γενεα</span></span>). Either locative case, "in his own generation" or dative object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπηρετησας</span></span> (served).</p>
<p><b>The counsel of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη του θεου βουλη</span></span>). So here, either the dative, the object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπηρετησας</span></span> if <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενεα</span></span> is locative, or the instrumental case "by the counsel of God" which again may be construed either with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπηρετησας</span></span> (having served) or after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκοιμηθη</span></span> (fell on sleep). Either of the three ways is grammatical and makes good sense. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κοιμαομα</span></span> for death we have already had (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:60">Acts 7:60</span>). So Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:11">John 11:11</span>) and Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:6">1 Corinthians 15:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:51">1 Corinthians 15:51</span>).</p>
<p><b>Was laid </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσετεθη</span></span>). Was added unto (first aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προστιθημ</span></span>). See the verb in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:47">Acts 2:47</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:14">Acts 5:14</span>. This figure for death probably arose from the custom of burying families together (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+15:15">Genesis 15:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jdg+2:10">Judges 2:10</span>).</p>
<p><b>Saw corruption </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδεν διαφθοραν</span></span>). As Jesus did not (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:31">Acts 2:31</span>) as he shows in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:37">Acts 13:37</span>. </p>
