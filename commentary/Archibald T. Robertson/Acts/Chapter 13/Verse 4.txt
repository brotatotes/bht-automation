<p><b> So they </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτο μεν ουν</span></span>). They themselves indeed therefore. No contrast is necessary, though there is a slight one in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:5">Acts 13:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:6">Acts 13:6</span>. Luke again refers to the Holy Spirit as the source of their authority for this campaign rather than the church at Antioch.</p>
<p><b>Sent forth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκπεμφθεντες</span></span>). Old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκπεμπω</span></span> and first aorist passive participle, but in the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:10">Acts 17:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Sailed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεπλευσαν</span></span>). Effective aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποπλεω</span></span>, old verb to sail away, depart from. In the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:26">Acts 14:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:15">Acts 20:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:1">Acts 27:1</span>. Barnabas was from Cyprus where there were many Jews. </p>
