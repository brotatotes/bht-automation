<p><b> Ye despisers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο καταφρονητα</span></span>). Not in the Hebrew, but in the LXX. It is pertinent for Paul's purpose.</p>
<p><b>Perish </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφανισθητε</span></span>). Or vanish away. First aorist passive imperative. Added by the LXX to the Hebrew.</p>
<p><b>If one declare it unto you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν τις εκδιηγητα υμιν</span></span>). Condition of third class with present middle subjunctive, if one keep on outlining (double compound, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ-δι-ηγεομα</span></span>) it unto you. Paul has hurled a thunderbolt at the close. </p>
