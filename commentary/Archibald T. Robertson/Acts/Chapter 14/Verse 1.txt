<p><b> They entered together </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα το αυτο εισελθειν</span></span>). Like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ το αυτο</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:1">Acts 3:1</span>. The infinitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισελθειν</span></span> is the subject of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγενετο</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>So spake that </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαλησα ουτως ωστε</span></span>). Infinitive again parallel to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισελθειν</span></span>. With the result that, actual result here stated with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωστε</span></span> and the aorist infinitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστευσα</span></span> (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, pp. 999f.) rather than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωστε</span></span> and the indicative like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:16">John 3:16</span>. It was a tremendous first meeting. </p>
