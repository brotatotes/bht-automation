<p><b> Upright </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορθος</span></span>). Predicate adjective. In this sense Galen and Hippocrates frequently use <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορθος</span></span> (erect, straight). Paul spoke in a loud (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεγαλη</span></span>) voice so that all could hear and know.</p>
<p><b>He leaped up and walked </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηλατο κα περιεπατε</span></span>). Rather, He leaped up with a single bound and began to walk. The second aorist middle indicative (with first aorist vowel <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span>) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλομα</span></span> (late verb, in papyri) and inchoative imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιπατεω</span></span>, common verb to walk around. This graphic picture is concealed by the usual English rendering. It is possible that Luke obtained the vivid report of this incident from Timothy who may have witnessed it and who was probably converted during Paul's stay here (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:3">Acts 16:3</span>). His father was a prominent Greek and his mother Eunice, possibly a widow, may have lived here with her mother Lois (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:5">2 Timothy 1:5</span>). </p>
