<p><b> Lifted up their voice </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επηραν την φωνην αυτων</span></span>). First aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαιρω</span></span>. In their excitement they elevated their voices.</p>
<p><b>In the speech of Lycaonia </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Λυκαονιστ</span></span>). Adverb from verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυκαονιζω</span></span>, to use the language of Lycaonia found here alone, but formed regularly like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εβραιστ</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:2">John 5:2</span>), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hελληνιστ</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:37">Acts 21:37</span>), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ρωμαιστ</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:20">John 19:20</span>). Paul was speaking in Greek, of course, but the excitement of the crowd over the miracle made them cry out in their native tongue which Paul and Barnabas did not understand. Hence it was not till preparations for offering sacrifice to them had begun that Paul understood the new role in which he and Barnabas were held.</p>
<p><b>In the likeness of men </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοιωθεντες ανθρωποις</span></span>). First aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοιω</span></span>, to liken, with the associative instrumental case. In this primitive state the people hold to the old Graeco-Roman mythology. The story of Baucis and Philemon tells how Jupiter (Zeus) and Mercury (Hermes) visited in human form the neighbouring region of Phrygia (Ovid, <i>Meta</i>. VIII. 626). Jupiter (Zeus) had a temple in Lystra. </p>
