<p><b> In the generations gone by </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ταις παρωιχημεναις γενεαις</span></span>). Perfect middle participle from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παροιχομα</span></span>, to go by, old verb, here alone in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>Suffered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειασεν</span></span>). Constative aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαω</span></span> (note syllabic augment). Paul here touches God in history as he did just before in creation. God's hand is on the history of all the nations (Gentile and Jew), only with the Gentiles he withdrew the restraints of his grace in large measure (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:30">Acts 17:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:24">Romans 1:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:26">Romans 1:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:28">Romans 1:28</span>), judgment enough for their sins.</p>
<p><b>To walk in their ways </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορευεσθα ταις οδοις αυτων</span></span>). Present middle infinitive, to go on walking, with locative case without <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span>. This philosophy of history does not mean that God was ignorant or unconcerned. He was biding his time in patience. </p>
