<p><b> Scarce </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μολις</span></span>). Adverb in same sense as old <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μογις</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μολος</span></span>, toil.</p>
<p><b>Restrained </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεπαυσαν</span></span>). Effective first aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταπαυω</span></span>, old verb in causative sense to make abstain from.</p>
<p><b>From doing sacrifice unto them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του μη θυειν αυτοις</span></span>). Ablative case of the articular infinitive with redundant negative after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεπαυσαν</span></span>, regular Greek idiom (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, pp. 1094, 1171). It had been a harrowing and well-nigh a horrible ordeal, but finally Paul had won. If only nobody else had interposed! </p>
