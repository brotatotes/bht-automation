<p><b> Kept silence </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσιγησεν</span></span>). Ingressive first aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σιγαω</span></span>, old verb, to hold one's peace. All the multitude became silent after Peter's speech and because of it.</p>
<p><b>Hearkened </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκουον</span></span>). Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουω</span></span>, descriptive of the rapt attention, were listening.</p>
<p><b>Unto Barnabas and Paul </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Βαρναβα κα Παυλου</span></span>). Note placing Barnabas before Paul as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:25">Acts 15:25</span>, possibly because in Jerusalem Barnabas was still better known than Paul.</p>
<p><b>Rehearsing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξηγουμενων</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξηγεομα</span></span>, old verb, to go through or lead out a narrative of events as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:35">Luke 24:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:8">Acts 10:8</span> which see. Three times (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:27">Acts 14:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:4">Acts 15:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:12">Acts 15:12</span>) Paul is described as telling the facts about their mission work, facts more eloquent than argument (Page). One of the crying needs in the churches is fuller knowledge of the facts of mission work and progress with enough detail to give life and interest. The signs and wonders which God had wrought among the Gentiles set the seal of approval on the work done through (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span>) Barnabas and Paul. This had been Peter's argument about Cornelius (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:17">Acts 11:17</span>). This same verb (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξηγησατο</span></span>) is used by James in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:14">Acts 15:14</span> referring to Peter's speech. </p>
