<p><b> I will build again </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανοικοδομησω</span></span>). Here LXX has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναστησω</span></span>. Compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανα</span></span>, up or again) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικοδομεω</span></span>, the verb used by Jesus in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:18">Matthew 16:18</span> of the general church or kingdom as here which see.</p>
<p><b>The tabernacle of David </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την σκηνην Δαυειδ</span></span>), a poetical figure of the throne of David (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2sa+7:12">2 Samuel 7:12</span>) now "the fallen tent" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την πεπτωκυιαν</span></span>), perfect active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιπτω</span></span>, state of completion.</p>
<p><b>The ruins thereof </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα κατεστραμμενα αυτης</span></span>). Literally, "the ruined portions of it." Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταστρεφω</span></span>, to turn down. It is a desolate picture of the fallen, torn down tent of David.</p>
<p><b>I will let it up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανορθωσω</span></span>). Old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανορθοω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανα, ορθος</span></span>), to set upright. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:13">Luke 13:13</span> of the old woman whose crooked back was set straight. </p>
