<p><b> They therefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο μεν ουν</span></span>). Luke's favourite method of resumptive narrative as we have seen (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:19">Acts 11:19</span>, etc.), demonstrative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεν</span></span> (indeed) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουν</span></span> (therefore).</p>
<p><b>Being brought on their way by the church </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προπεμφθεντες υπο της εκκλησιας</span></span>). First aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προπεμπω</span></span>, old verb, to send forward under escort as a mark of honour as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:38">Acts 20:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:5">Acts 21:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:6">3 John 1:6</span>. They were given a grand send-off by the church in Antioch.</p>
<p><b>Passed through </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διηρχοντο</span></span>). Imperfect middle describing the triumphal procession through both (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τε κα</span></span>) Phoenicia and Samaria.</p>
<p><b>The conversion </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την επιστροφην</span></span>). The turning.</p>
<p><b>They caused great joy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εποιουν χαραν μεγαλην</span></span>). Imperfect active. They were raising a constant paean of praise as they proceeded toward Jerusalem. Probably the Judaizers had gone on or kept still. </p>
