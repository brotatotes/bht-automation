<p><b> So they </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο μεν ουν</span></span>). As in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:3">Acts 15:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>When they were dismissed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολυθεντες</span></span>). First aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολυω</span></span>, common verb to loosen, to dismiss. Possibly (Hackett) religious services were held as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:33">Acts 15:33</span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:3">Acts 13:3</span>) and perhaps an escort for part of the way as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:3">Acts 15:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>The multitude </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το πληθος</span></span>). Public meeting of the church as in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:1-3">Acts 15:1-3</span>. Deissmann (<i>Bible Studies</i>, p. 232) gives illustrations from the inscriptions of the use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληθος</span></span> for official, political, and religious gatherings. The committee formally "delivered" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεδωκαν</span></span>) the epistle to the church authorities. </p>
