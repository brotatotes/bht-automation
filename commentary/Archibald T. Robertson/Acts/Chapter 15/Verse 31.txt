<p><b> When they had read it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγνοντες</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγινωσκω</span></span>. Public reading, of course, to the church.</p>
<p><b>They rejoiced </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχαρησαν</span></span>). Second aorist (ingressive) passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαιρω</span></span>. They burst into exultant joy showing clearly that they did not consider it a weak compromise, but a glorious victory of Gentile liberty.</p>
<p><b>For the consolation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ τη παρακλησε</span></span>). The encouragement, the cheer in the letter. See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεκαλεσαν</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:32">Acts 15:32</span>. Consolation and exhortation run into one another in this word. </p>
