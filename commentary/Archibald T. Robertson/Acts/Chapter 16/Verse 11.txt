<p><b> Setting sail </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναχθεντες</span></span>). Same word in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:13">Acts 13:13</span> which see.</p>
<p><b>We made a straight course </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευθυδρομησαμεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of compound verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευθυδρομεω</span></span> (in Philo) from adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευθυδρομος</span></span> (in Strabo), running a straight course (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευθυσ, δρομος</span></span>). In the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:1">Acts 21:1</span>. It is a nautical term for sailing before the wind. Luke has a true feeling for the sea.</p>
<p><b>To Samothrace </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις Σαμοθραικην</span></span>). A small island in the Aegean about halfway between Troas and Neapolis.</p>
<p><b>The day following </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη επιουση</span></span>). Locative case of time with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημερα</span></span> (day) to be supplied (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:26">Acts 7:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:15">Acts 20:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:18">Acts 21:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:11">Acts 23:11</span>). With adverse winds it took five days to make the run of 125 miles (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:6">Acts 20:6</span>).</p>
<p><b>To Neapolis </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις Νεαν Πολιν</span></span>). To New Town (Newton, Naples, Neapolis). The port of Philippi ten miles distant, Thracian, but reckoned as Macedonian after Vespasian. </p>
