<p><b> Was gone </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξηλθεν</span></span>). Was gone out of the slave girl, second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξερχομα</span></span>. "The two most important social revolutions worked by Christianity have been the elevation of woman and the abolition of slavery" (Furneaux). Both are illustrated here (Lydia and this slave girl). "The most sensitive part of 'civilized' man is the pocket" (Ramsay).</p>
<p><b>Laid hold on </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλαβομενο</span></span>). Second aorist middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλαμβανω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:27">Acts 9:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:19">Acts 17:19</span>, but here with hostile intent.</p>
<p><b>Dragged </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειλκυσαν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελκυω</span></span>, late form of the old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελκω</span></span> (also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:6">James 2:6</span>) to draw as a sword, and then to drag one forcibly as here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:30">Acts 21:30</span>. It is also used of spiritual drawing as by Jesus in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:32">John 12:32</span>. Here it is by violence.</p>
<p><b>Into the marketplace </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις την αγοραν</span></span>). Into the Roman forum near which would be the courts of law as in our courthouse square, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:17">Acts 17:17</span>. Marketing went on also (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:4">Mark 7:4</span>), when the crowds collect (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:56">Mark 6:56</span>), from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγειρω</span></span>, to collect or gather.</p>
<p><b>Unto the rulers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ τους αρχοντας</span></span>). General Greek term for "the magistrates." </p>
