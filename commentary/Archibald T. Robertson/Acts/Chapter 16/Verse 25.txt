<p><b> About midnight </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα δε μεσονυκτιον</span></span>). Middle of the night, old adjective seen already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:35">Mark 13:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:5">Luke 11:5</span> which see.</p>
<p><b>Were praying and singing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσευχομενο υμνουν</span></span>). Present middle participle and imperfect active indicative: Praying they were singing (simultaneously, blending together petition and praise). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hυμνεω</span></span> is an old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμνος</span></span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+12:4">Isaiah 12:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="da+3:23">Daniel 3:23</span>). Paul and Silas probably used portions of the Psalms (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:39">Luke 1:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:67">Luke 1:67</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:28">Luke 2:28</span>) with occasional original outbursts of praise.</p>
<p><b>Were listening to them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επηκροωντο αυτων</span></span>). Imperfect middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επακροαομα</span></span>. Rare verb to listen with pleasure as to a recitation or music (Page). It was a new experience for the prisoners and wondrously attractive entertainment to them. </p>
