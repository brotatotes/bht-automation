<p><b> They delivered them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεδιδοσαν αυτοις</span></span>). Imperfect active, kept on delivering to them in city after city. This is a proof of Paul's loyalty to the Jerusalem compact (Knowling). The circumcision of Timothy would indicate also that the points involved were under discussion and that Paul felt no inconsistency in what he did.</p>
<p><b>The decrees </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα δογματα</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκεω</span></span>, to give an opinion. It is used of public decrees of rulers (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:1">Luke 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:7">Acts 17:7</span>), of the requirements of the Mosaic law (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:14">Colossians 2:14</span>), and here of the regulations or conclusions of the Jerusalem Conference. Silas was with Paul and his presence gave added dignity to the passing out of the decrees, a charter of Gentile freedom, since he was one of the committee from Jerusalem to Antioch (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:22">Acts 15:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:27">Acts 15:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:32">Acts 15:32</span>).</p>
<p><b>Which had been ordained </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα κεκριμενα</span></span>). Perfect passive articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρινω</span></span>, to judge, emphasizing the permanence of the conclusions reached by the apostles and elders in Jerusalem.</p>
<p><b>For to keep </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυλασσειν</span></span>). This present active infinitive likewise accents that it is a charter of liberty for continual living, not a temporary compromise. </p>
