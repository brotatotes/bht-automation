<p><b> Many therefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πολλο μεν ουν</span></span>). As a result of this Bible study.</p>
<p><b>Also of the Greek women of honourable estate </b>. The word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hελληνις</span></span> means Greek woman, but the word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γυνη</span></span> is added. In particular women of rank (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευσχημονων</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>, graceful figure and the honourable standing) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:50">Acts 13:50</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:43">Mark 15:43</span>). Probably Luke means by implication that the "men" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανδρων</span></span>) were also noble Greeks though he does not expressly say so. So then the Jews were more open to the message, the proselytes or God-fearers followed suit, with "not a few" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ ολιγο</span></span>) real Greeks (both men and women) believing. It was quick and fine work. </p>
