<p><b> For thou bringest certain strange things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξενιζοντα γαρ τινα εισφερεις</span></span>). The very verb used by Xenophon (<i>Mem</i>. I) about Socrates. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ξενιζοντα</span></span> is present active neuter plural participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξενιζω</span></span> and from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξενος</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:18">Acts 17:18</span>), "things surprising or shocking us."</p>
<p><b>We would know therefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βουλομεθα ουν γνωνα</span></span>). Very polite still, we wish or desire, and repeating <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνωνα</span></span> (the essential point). </p>
