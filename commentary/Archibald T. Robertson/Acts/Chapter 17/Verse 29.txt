<p><b> We ought not to think </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ οφειλομεν νομιζειν</span></span>). It is a logical conclusion (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουν</span></span>, therefore) from the very language of Aratus and Cleanthes.</p>
<p><b>That the Godhead is like </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το θειον εινα ομοιον</span></span>). Infinitive with accusative of general reference in indirect discourse. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Το θειον</span></span> is strictly "the divine" nature like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θειοτης</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:20">Romans 1:20</span>) rather than like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεοτης</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:9">Colossians 2:9</span>). Paul may have used <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το θειον</span></span> here to get back behind all their notions of various gods to the real nature of God. The Athenians may even have used the term themselves. After <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοιος</span></span> (like) the associative instrumental case is used as with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρυσωι, αργυρωι, λιθω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Graven by art and device of man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαραγματ τεχνης κα ενθυμησεως ανθρωπου</span></span>). Apposition with preceding and so <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαραγματ</span></span> in associative instrumental case. Literally, graven work or sculpture from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρασσω</span></span>, to engrave, old word, but here alone in N.T. outside of Revelation (the mark of the beast). Graven work of art (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεχνης</span></span>) or external craft, and of thought or device (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενθυμησεως</span></span>) or internal conception of man. </p>
