<p><b> They took him unto them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσελαβοντο</span></span>). Second aorist middle (indirect) indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσλαμβανω</span></span>, old verb, to their home and heart as companion (cf. the rabbis and the ruffians in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:5">Acts 17:5</span>). Probably for dinner after service.</p>
<p><b>Expounded </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεθεντο</span></span>). Second aorist (effective) middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκτιθημ</span></span> seen already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:4">Acts 11:4</span>, to set forth.</p>
<p><b>More carefully </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακριβεστερον</span></span>). Comparative adverb of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακριβως</span></span>. More accurately than he already knew. Instead of abusing the young and brilliant preacher for his ignorance they (particularly Priscilla) gave him the fuller story of the life and work of Jesus and of the apostolic period to fill up the gaps in his knowledge. It is a needed and delicate task, this thing of teaching gifted young ministers. They do not learn it all in schools. More of it comes from contact with men and women rich in grace and in the knowledge of God's ways. He was not rebaptized, but only received fuller information. </p>
