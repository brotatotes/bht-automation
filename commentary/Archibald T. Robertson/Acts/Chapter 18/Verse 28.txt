<p><b> Powerfully </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευτονως</span></span>). Adverb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευτονος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ</span></span>, well, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεινω</span></span>, to stretch), well-strung, at full stretch.</p>
<p><b>Confuted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακατηλεγχετο</span></span>). Imperfect middle of the double compound verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια-κατ-ελεγχομα</span></span>, to confute with rivalry in a contest, here alone. The old Greek has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διελεγχω</span></span>, to convict of falsehood, but not this double compound which means to argue down to a finish. It is the imperfect tense and does not mean that Apollos convinced these rabbis, but he had the last word.</p>
<p><b>Publicly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δημοσια</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:18">Acts 5:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:37">Acts 16:37</span>. In open meeting where all could see the victory of Apollos.</p>
<p><b>Shewing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιδεικνυς</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιδεικνυμ</span></span>, old verb to set forth so that all see.</p>
<p><b>By the Scriptures </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια των γραφων</span></span>). In which Apollos was so "mighty" (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:24">Acts 18:24</span>) and the rabbis so weak for they knew the oral law better than the written (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:8-12">Mark 7:8-12</span>).</p>
<p><b>That Jesus was the Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εινα τον Χριστον Ιησουν</span></span>). Infinitive and the accusative in indirect assertion. Apollos proclaims the same message that Paul did everywhere (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:3">Acts 17:3</span>). He had not yet met Paul, but he had been instructed by Priscilla and Aquila. He is in Corinth building on the foundation laid so well by Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:4-17">1 Corinthians 3:4-17</span>). Luke has here made a brief digression from the story of Paul, but it helps us understand Paul better There are those who think that Apollos wrote Hebrews, a guess that may be correct. </p>
