<p><b> When they opposed themselves </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντιτασσομενων αυτων</span></span>). Genitive absolute with present middle (direct middle again) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντιτασσω</span></span>, old verb to range in battle array (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τασσω</span></span>) face to face with or against (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντ</span></span>). In the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:2">Romans 13:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:6">James 4:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:5">1 Peter 5:5</span>. Paul's fresh activity roused the rabbis as at Antioch in Pisidia and at Thessalonica in concerted opposition and railing (blasphemy).</p>
<p><b>He shook out his raiment </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκτιναξαμενος τα ιματια</span></span>). First aorist middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκτινασσω</span></span>, old verb, in the N.T. only here as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:51">Acts 13:51</span> (middle) and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:11">Mark 6:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:15">Matthew 10:15</span> where active voice occurs of shaking out dust also. Vivid and dramatic picture here like that in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ne+5:13">Nehemiah 5:13</span>, "undoubtedly a very exasperating gesture" (Ramsay), but Paul was deeply stirred.</p>
<p><b>Your blood be upon your own heads </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Το αιμα υμων επ την κεφαλην υμων</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+3:18">Ezekiel 3:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+33:4">Ezekiel 33:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+33:8">Ezekiel 33:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2sa+1:16">2 Samuel 1:16</span>. Not as a curse, but "a solemn disclaimer of responsibility" by Paul (Page) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:26">Acts 20:26</span>. The Jews used this very phrase in assuming responsibility for the blood of Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:25">Matthew 27:25</span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:35">Matthew 23:35</span>.</p>
<p><b>I am clean </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθαρος εγω</span></span>). Pure from your blood. Repeats the claim made in previous sentence. Paul had done his duty.</p>
<p><b>From henceforth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο του νυν</span></span>). Turning point reached in Corinth. He will devote himself to the Gentiles, though Jews will be converted there also. Elsewhere as in Ephesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:1-10">Acts 19:1-10</span>) and in Rome (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:23-28">Acts 28:23-28</span>) Paul will preach also to Jews. </p>
