<p><b> Handkerchiefs </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σουδαρια</span></span>). Latin word for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συδορ</span></span> (sweat). Used in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:20">Luke 19:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:44">John 11:44</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:7">John 20:7</span>. In two papyri marriage-contracts this word occurs among the toilet articles in the dowry (Deissmann, <i>Bible Studies</i>, p. 223).</p>
<p><b>Aprons </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σιμικινθια</span></span>). Latin word also, <i>semicinctilum</i> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σεμι, χινγο</span></span>). Only here in the N.T. Linen aprons used by servants or artisans (Martial XIV. 153). Paul did manual work at Ephesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:34">Acts 20:34</span>) and so wore these aprons.</p>
<p><b>Departed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαλλαλσεθα</span></span>). Present passive infinitive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωστε</span></span> for actual result as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:10">Acts 19:10</span>. If one wonders how God could honour such superstitious faith, he should remember that there is no power in superstition or in magic, but in God. If God never honoured any faith save that entirely free from superstition, how about Christian people who are troubled over the number 13, over the moon, the rabbit's foot? The poor woman with an issue of blood touched the hem of Christ's garment and was healed (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:44-46">Luke 8:44-46</span>) as others sought to do (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:36">Matthew 14:36</span>). God condescends to meet us in our ignorance and weakness where he can reach us. Elisha had a notion that some of the power of Elijah resided in his mantle (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ki+2:13">2 Kings 2:13</span>). Some even sought help from Peter's shadow (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:15">Acts 5:15</span>). </p>
