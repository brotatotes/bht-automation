<p><b> Cannot be gainsaid </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναντιρητων ουν οντων</span></span>). Genitive absolute with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουν</span></span> (therefore). Undeniable (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν, αντι, ρητος</span></span>), verbal adjective. Occasionally in late Greek (Polybius, etc.), only here in N.T., but adverb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναντιρητως</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:29">Acts 10:29</span>. These legends were accepted as true and appeased the mob.</p>
<p><b>Ye ought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεον εστιν</span></span>). It is necessary. Periphrastic present indicative instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span> like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:6">1 Peter 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:13">1 Timothy 5:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Be quiet </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεσταλμενους</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταστελλω</span></span> (see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:35">Acts 19:35</span>).</p>
<p><b>Rash </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προπετες</span></span>). Old adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πετω</span></span>, to fall forward, headlong, precipitate. In the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:4">2 Timothy 3:4</span>, though common in the <i>Koine</i>. Better look before you leap. </p>
