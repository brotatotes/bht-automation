<p><b> For indeed we are in danger to be accused concerning this day's riot </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα γαρ κινδυνευομεν εγκαλεισθα στασεως περ της σημερον</span></span>). The text is uncertain. The text of Westcott and Hort means "to be accused of insurrection concerning today's assembly." The peril was real. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κινδυνευομεν</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κινδυνος</span></span>, danger, peril. Old verb, but in the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:23">Luke 8:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:30">1 Corinthians 15:30</span>.</p>
<p><b>There being no cause for it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηδενος αιτιου υπαρχοντος</span></span>). Genitive absolute with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιτιος</span></span>, common adjective (cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιτια</span></span>, cause) though in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:9">Hebrews 5:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:4">Luke 23:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:14">Luke 23:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:22">Luke 23:22</span>.</p>
<p><b>And as touching it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ ου</span></span>). "Concerning which." But what? No clear antecedent, only the general idea.</p>
<p><b>Give an account of this concourse </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποδουνα λογον περ της συστροφης ταυτης</span></span>). <i>Rationem reddere</i>. They will have to explain matters to the proconsul. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Συστροφη</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν</span></span>, together, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρεφω</span></span>, to turn) is a late word for a conspiracy (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:12">Acts 23:12</span>) and a disorderly riot as here (Polybius). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:12">Acts 28:12</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συστρεφω</span></span> is used of gathering up a bundle of sticks and of men combining in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:22">Matthew 17:22</span>. Seneca says that there was nothing on which the Romans looked with such jealousy as a tumultuous meeting. </p>
