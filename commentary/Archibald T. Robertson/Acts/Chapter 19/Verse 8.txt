<p><b> Spake boldly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαρρησιαζετο</span></span>). Imperfect middle, kept on at it for three months. Cf. same word in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:26">Acts 18:26</span>.</p>
<p><b>Persuading </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειθων</span></span>). Present active conative participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειθω</span></span>, trying to persuade (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:23">Acts 28:23</span>). Paul's idea of the Kingdom of God was the church of God which he (Jesus, God's Son) had purchased with his own blood (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:28">Acts 20:28</span>, calling Christ God). Nowhere else had Paul apparently been able to speak so long in the synagogue without interruption unless it was so at Corinth. These Jews were already interested (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:30">Acts 18:30</span>). </p>
