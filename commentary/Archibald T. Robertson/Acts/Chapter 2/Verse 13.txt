<p><b> Mocking </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαχλευαζοντες</span></span>). Old verb, but only here in the N.T., though the simple verb (without <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span>) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:32">Acts 17:32</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χλευη</span></span> means a joke.</p>
<p><b>With new wine </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γλευκους</span></span>). Sweet wine, but intoxicating. Sweet wine kept a year was very intoxicating. Genitive case here after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεμεστωμενο εισιν</span></span> (periphrastic perfect passive indicative), old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεστοω</span></span>, only here in the N.T. Tanked up with new wine, state of fulness. </p>
