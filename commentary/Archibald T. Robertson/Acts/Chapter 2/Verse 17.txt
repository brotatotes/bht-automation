<p><b> In the last days </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ταις εσχαταις ημεραις</span></span>). Joel does not have precisely these words, but he defines "those days" as being "the day of the Lord" (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+2:2">Isaiah 2:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mic+4:1">Micah 4:1</span>).</p>
<p><b>I will pour forth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκχεω</span></span>). Future active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκχεω</span></span>. This future like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδομα</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιομα</span></span> is without tense sign, probably like the present in the futuristic sense (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 354). Westcott and Hort put a different accent on the future, but the old Greek had no accent. The old Greek had <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκχευσω</span></span>. This verb means to pour out.</p>
<p><b>Of my Spirit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο του πνευματος</span></span>). This use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span> (of) is either because of the variety in the manifestations of the Spirit (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:1">Acts 2:1</span>) or because the Spirit in his entirety remains with God (Holtzmann, Wendt). But the Hebrew has it: "I will pour out my Spirit" without the partitive idea in the LXX.</p>
<p><b>And your daughters </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα α θυγατερες υμων</span></span>). Anna is called a prophetess in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:36">Luke 2:36</span> and the daughters of Philip prophesy (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:9">Acts 21:9</span>) and verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:18">Acts 2:18</span> (handmaidens). See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:5">1 Corinthians 11:5</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προφητουσα</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Visions </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορασεις</span></span>). Late word for the more common <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραμα</span></span>, both from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span>, to see. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:3">Revelation 4:3</span> it means appearance, but in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:17">Revelation 9:17</span> as here an ecstatic revelation or vision.</p>
<p><b>Dream dreams </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενυπνιοις ενυπνιασθησοντα</span></span>). Shall dream with (instrumental case) dreams. First future passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενυπνιαζω</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενυπνιος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπνος</span></span>, in sleep), a common late word. Only here in the N.T. (this from Joel as all these verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:17-21">Acts 2:17-21</span> are) and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:8">Jude 1:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>Yea and </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα γε</span></span>). Intensive particle <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γε</span></span> added to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span> (and), an emphatic addition (=Hebrew <i>vegam</i>).</p>
<p><b>Servants </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δουλους</span></span>),</p>
<p><b>handmaidens </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δουλας</span></span>). Slaves, actual slaves of men. The humblest classes will receive the Spirit of God (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:26-31">1 Corinthians 1:26-31</span>). But the word "prophesy" here is not in the LXX (or the Hebrew). </p>
