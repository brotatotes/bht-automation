<p><b> I may say </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξον ειπειν</span></span>). Supply <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστιν</span></span> before <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξον</span></span>, periphrastic present indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξειμ</span></span>, to allow, permit. The Authorized Version has "Let me speak," supplying <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστο</span></span> present imperative.</p>
<p><b>Freely </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα παρρησιας</span></span>). Telling it all (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παν, ρησια</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειπον</span></span>, to speak), with fulness, with boldness. Luke is fond of the phrase (as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:13">Acts 4:13</span>). It is a new start for Simon Peter, full of boldness and courage.</p>
<p><b>The patriarch </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του πατριαρχου</span></span>). Transliteration of the word, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πατρια</span></span>, family, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρχω</span></span>, to rule, the founder of a family. Late word in LXX. Used of Abraham (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:4">Hebrews 7:4</span>), of the twelve sons of Jacob as founders of the several tribes (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:8">Acts 7:8</span>), and here of David as head of the family from whom the Messiah comes.</p>
<p><b>Was buried </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εταφη</span></span>). Second aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θαπτω</span></span>. His tomb was on Mt. Zion where most of the kings were buried. The tomb was said to have fallen into ruins in the time of the Emperor Hadrian. Josephus (<i>Ant</i>. XVI. 7, 1) attributes most of the misfortunes of Herod's family to the fact that he tried to rifle the tomb of David. </p>
