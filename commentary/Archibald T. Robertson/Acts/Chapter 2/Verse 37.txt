<p><b> They were pricked in their heart </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατενυγησαν την καρδιαν</span></span>). Second aorist indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατανυσσω</span></span>, a rare verb (LXX) to pierce, to sting sharply, to stun, to smite. Homer used it of horses dinting the earth with their hoofs. The substantive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατανυξις</span></span> occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:8">Romans 11:8</span>. Here only in the N.T. It is followed here by the accusative of the part affected, the heart.</p>
<p><b>What shall we do? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Τ ποιησωμεν</span></span>). Deliberative subjunctive first aorist active. The sermon went home, they felt the sting of Peter's words, compunction (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χομπυνγο</span></span>). Codex Bezae adds: "Show us." </p>
