<p><b> They then </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hο μεν ουν</span></span>). A common phrase in Acts either without antithesis as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:6">Acts 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:41">Acts 5:41</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:4">Acts 8:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:25">Acts 8:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:31">Acts 9:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:19">Acts 11:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:5">Acts 16:5</span>; or with it as here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:25">Acts 8:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:4">Acts 13:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:3">Acts 14:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:17">Acts 17:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:31">Acts 23:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:4">Acts 25:4</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ουν</span></span> connects with what precedes as the result of Peter's sermon while <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεν</span></span> points forward to what is to follow.</p>
<p><b>Were baptized </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβαπτισθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative, constative aorist. Note that only those who had already received the word and were converted were baptized.</p>
<p><b>There were added </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσετεθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προστιθημ</span></span>, old verb to add, to join to. Luke means that the 3,000 were added to the 120 already enlisted. It is not stated they were all baptized by Peter or the twelve or all on the same day, though that is the natural implication of the language. The numerous pools in Jerusalem afforded ample opportunity for such wholesale baptizing and Hackett notes that the habit of orientals would place no obstacle in the way of the use of the public reservoirs. Furneaux warns us that all the 3,000 may not have been genuine converts and that many of them were pilgrims at the passover who returned home.</p>
<p><b>Souls </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψυχα</span></span>). Persons as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:43">Acts 2:43</span>. </p>
