<p><b> Were together </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ησαν επ το αυτο</span></span>). Some MSS. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ησαν κα</span></span> (were and). But they were together in the same place as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:1">Acts 2:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>And had </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ειχον</span></span>). Imperfect active, kept on having, a habit in the present emergency.</p>
<p><b>Common </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινα</span></span>). It was not actual communism, but they held all their property ready for use for the common good as it was needed (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:32">Acts 4:32</span>). This situation appears nowhere else except in Jerusalem and was evidently due to special conditions there which did not survive permanently. Later Paul will take a special collection for the poor saints in Jerusalem. </p>
