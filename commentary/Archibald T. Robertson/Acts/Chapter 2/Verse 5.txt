<p><b> Were dwelling </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ησαν κατοικουντες</span></span>). Periphrastic imperfect active indicative. Usually <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατοικεω</span></span> means residence in a place (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:16">Acts 4:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:24">Acts 7:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:22">Acts 9:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:32">Acts 9:32</span>) as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:14">Acts 2:14</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:4">Luke 13:4</span>). Perhaps some had come to Jerusalem to live while others were here only temporarily, for the same word occurs in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:9">Acts 2:9</span> of those who dwell in Mesopotamia, etc.</p>
<p><b>Devout </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλαβεις</span></span>). Reverent (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ</span></span>, well, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>, to take). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:25">Luke 2:25</span> like Simeon waiting for the consolation of Israel or hoping to die and be buried in the Holy City and also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:2">Acts 8:2</span>. </p>
