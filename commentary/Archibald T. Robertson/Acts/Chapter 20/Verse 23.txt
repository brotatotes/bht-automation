<p><b> Save that </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλην οτ</span></span>). The <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span> clause is really in the ablative case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλην</span></span>, here a preposition as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:18">Philippians 1:18</span>, this idiom <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλην οτ</span></span> occasionally in ancient Greek.</p>
<p><b>In every city </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα πολιν</span></span>). Singular here though plural in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατ' οικους</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:20">Acts 20:20</span>).</p>
<p><b>Bonds and afflictions </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεσμα κα θλιψεις</span></span>). Both together as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:17">Philippians 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:8">2 Corinthians 1:8</span>. Literal bonds and actual pressures.</p>
<p><b>Abide me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">με μενουσιν</span></span>). With the accusative as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:5">Acts 20:5</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμενον ημας</span></span>) and nowhere else in the N.T. </p>
