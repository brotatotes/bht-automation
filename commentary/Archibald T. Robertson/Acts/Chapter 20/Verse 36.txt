<p><b> He kneeled down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεις τα γονατα αυτου</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span>, to place. The very idiom used in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:60">Acts 7:60</span> of Stephen. Not in ancient writers and only six times in the N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:19">Mark 15:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:41">Luke 22:41</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:60">Acts 7:60</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:40">Acts 9:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:36">Acts 20:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:5">Acts 21:5</span>). Certainly kneeling in prayer is a fitting attitude (cf. Jesus, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:41">Luke 22:41</span>), though not the only proper one (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:5">Matthew 6:5</span>). Paul apparently prayed aloud (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσηυξατο</span></span>). </p>
