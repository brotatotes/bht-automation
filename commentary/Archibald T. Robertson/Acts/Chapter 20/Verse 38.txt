<p><b> Sorrowing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδυνωμενο</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδυναω</span></span>, old verb to cause intense pain, to torment (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:24">Luke 16:24</span>), middle to distress oneself (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:48">Luke 2:48</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:38">Acts 20:38</span>). Nowhere else in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Which he had spoken </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ω ειρηκε</span></span>). Relative attracted to the case of the antecedent <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογω</span></span> (word). Past perfect indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειπον</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>They brought him on his way </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προεπεμπον αυτον</span></span>). Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προπεμπω</span></span>, old verb to send forward, to accompany as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:3">Acts 15:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:38">Acts 20:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:5">Acts 21:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:6">1 Corinthians 16:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:11">1 Corinthians 16:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:16">2 Corinthians 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:13">Titus 3:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:6">3 John 1:6</span>. Graphic picture of Paul's departure from this group of ministers. </p>
