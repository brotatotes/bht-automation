<p><b> Having found a ship </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευροντες πλοιον</span></span>). Paul had used a small coasting vessel (probably hired) that anchored each night at Cos, Rhodes, Patara. He was still some four hundred miles from Jerusalem. But at Patara Paul caught a large vessel (a merchantman) that could sail across the open sea.</p>
<p><b>Crossing over unto Phoenicia </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαπερων εις Φοινικην</span></span>). Neuter singular accusative (agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλοιον</span></span>) present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαπεραω</span></span>, old verb to go between (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span>) and so across to Tyre.</p>
<p><b>We went aboard </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιβαντες</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιβαινω</span></span>. </p>
