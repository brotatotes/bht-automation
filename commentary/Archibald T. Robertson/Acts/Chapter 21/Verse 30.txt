<p><b> All the city was shaken </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκινηθη η πολις ολη</span></span>). First aorist passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κινεω</span></span>, common verb for violent motion and emotion. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:5">Acts 24:5</span> where the word is used by Tertullus of Paul as the stirrer up of riots!</p>
<p><b>The people ran together </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγενετο συνδρομη του λαου</span></span>). Rather, There came a running together (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν-δρομη</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν-τρεχω</span></span>) of the people. The cry spread like wildfire over the city and there was a pell-mell scramble or rush to get to the place of the disturbance.</p>
<p><b>They laid hold on Paul </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλαβομενο του Παυλου</span></span>). Second aorist middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλαμβανομα</span></span> with the genitive (cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεβαλαν</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:27">Acts 21:27</span>).</p>
<p><b>Dragged </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειλκον</span></span>). Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελκω</span></span> (and also <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελκυω</span></span>), old verb to drag or draw. Imperfect tense vividly pictures the act as going on. They were saving the temple by dragging Paul outside. Curiously enough both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλαβομενο</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειλκυσαν</span></span> occur in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:19">Acts 16:19</span> about the arrest of Paul and Silas in Philippi.</p>
<p><b>Straightway the doors were shut </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευθεως εκλεισθησαν α θυρα</span></span>). With a bang and at once. First aorist (effective) passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλειω</span></span>. The doors between the inner court and the court of the Gentiles. But this was only the beginning, the preparation for the real work of the mob. They did not wish to defile the holy place with blood. The doors were shut by the Levites. </p>
