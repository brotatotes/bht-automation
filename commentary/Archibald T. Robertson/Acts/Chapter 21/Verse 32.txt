<p><b> Forthwith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαυτης</span></span>). Common in the <i>Koine</i> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ αυτης</span></span>, supply <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωρας</span></span>, hour).</p>
<p><b>He took </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραλαβων</span></span>). See verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:24">Acts 21:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:26">Acts 21:26</span>.</p>
<p><b>Centurions </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκατονταρχας</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:2">Luke 7:2</span> for discussion. Plural shows that Lysias the chiliarch took several hundred soldiers along (a centurion with each hundred).</p>
<p><b>Ran down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεδραμεν</span></span>). Effective second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατατρεχω</span></span>. From the tower of Antonia, vivid scene.</p>
<p><b>And they </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο δε</span></span>). Demonstrative use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span>. The Jewish mob who had begun the work of killing Paul (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:31">Acts 21:31</span>).</p>
<p><b>Left off beating Paul </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαυσαντο τυπτοντες τον Παυλον</span></span>). The participle with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παυομα</span></span> describes what they were already doing, the supplementary participle (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 1121). They stopped before the job was over because of the sudden onset of the Roman soldiers. Some ten years before in a riot at the passover the Roman guard marched down and in the panic several hundred were trampled to death. </p>
