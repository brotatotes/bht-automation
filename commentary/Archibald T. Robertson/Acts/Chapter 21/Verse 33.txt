<p><b> Came near </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγγισας</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγγιζω</span></span>, to draw near, <i>Koine</i> verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγγυς</span></span>, near, and common in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>Laid hold on him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επελαβετο αντου</span></span>). See same verb in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:30">Acts 21:30</span>.</p>
<p><b>To be bound </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεθηνα</span></span>). First aorist passive infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεω</span></span> (see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:11">Acts 21:11</span>).</p>
<p><b>With two chains </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλυσεσ δυσ</span></span>). Instrumental case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλυσις</span></span>, old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυω</span></span> (not loosing, i.e. chaining). With two chains as a violent and seditious person, probably leader of a band of assassins (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:38">Acts 21:38</span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:4">Mark 5:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>Inquired </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επυνθανετο</span></span>). Imperfect middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυνθανομα</span></span>, old and common verb used mainly by Luke in the N.T. Lysias repeated his inquiries.</p>
<p><b>Who he was </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τις ειη</span></span>). Present active optative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειμ</span></span> changed from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστιν</span></span> (present indicative) in the indirect question, a change not obligatory after a past tense, but often done in the older Greek, rare in the N.T. (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 1043f.).</p>
<p><b>And what he had done </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα τ εστιν πεποιηκως</span></span>). Periphrastic perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span> here retained, not changed to the optative as is true of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειη</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστιν</span></span> in the same indirect question, illustrating well the freedom about it. </p>
