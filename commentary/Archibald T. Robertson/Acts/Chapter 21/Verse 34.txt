<p><b> Some shouting one thing, some another </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλο αλλο τ επεφωνουν</span></span>). Same idiom of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλο αλλο</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:32">Acts 19:32</span> which see. The imperfect of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιφωνεω</span></span>, to call out to, suits well the idiom. This old verb occurs in the N.T. only in Luke and Acts (already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:22">Acts 12:22</span>).</p>
<p><b>When he could not know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη δυναμενου αυτου γνωνα</span></span>). Genitive absolute of present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυναμα</span></span> with negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> and second aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>The certainty </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το ασφαλες</span></span>). Neuter articular adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σφαλλω</span></span>, to make totter or fall. Old word, in the N.T. only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:34">Acts 21:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:30">Acts 22:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:26">Acts 25:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:1">Philippians 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:19">Hebrews 6:19</span>.</p>
<p><b>Into the castle </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις την παρεμβολην</span></span>). <i>Koine</i> word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεμβαλλω</span></span>, to cast in by the side of, to assign soldiers a place, to encamp (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:43">Luke 19:43</span>). So <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεμβολη</span></span> comes to mean an interpolation, then an army drawn up (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:34">Hebrews 11:34</span>), but mainly an encampment (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:11">Hebrews 13:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:13">Hebrews 13:13</span>), frequent in Polybius and LXX. So here barracks of the Roman soldiers in the tower of Antonia as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:37">Acts 21:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:24">Acts 22:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:10">Acts 23:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:16">Acts 23:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:32">Acts 23:32</span>. </p>
