<p><b> Upon the stairs </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ τους αναβαθμους</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανα</span></span>, up, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαινω</span></span>, to go. Late word, in LXX and <i>Koine</i> writers. In the N.T. only here and verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:40">Acts 21:40</span>.</p>
<p><b>So it was </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεβη</span></span>). Second aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμβαινω</span></span>, to happen (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:19">Acts 20:19</span>) with infinitive clause as subject here as often in the old Greek.</p>
<p><b>He was borne </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασταζεσθα αυτον</span></span>). Accusative of general reference with this subject infinitive, present passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασταζω</span></span>, to take up with the hands, literally as here.</p>
<p><b>Violence </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βιαν</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:26">Acts 5:26</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Βιαζω</span></span>, to use force, is from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βια</span></span>. </p>
