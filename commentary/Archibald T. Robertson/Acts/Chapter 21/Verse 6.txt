<p><b> Beach </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιγιαλον</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:2">Matthew 13:2</span> which see. This scene is in public as at Miletus, but they did not care.</p>
<p><b>Bade each other farewell </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεσπασαμεθα αλληλους</span></span>). First aorist middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απασπαζομα</span></span>. Rare compound, here alone in the N.T. Tender scene, but "no bonds of long comradeship, none of the clinging love" (Furneaux) seen at Miletus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:37">Acts 20:37</span>).</p>
<p><b>Home again </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τα ιδια</span></span>). To their own places as of the Beloved Disciple in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:27">John 19:27</span> and of Jesus in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:11">John 1:11</span>. This idiom in the papyri also. </p>
