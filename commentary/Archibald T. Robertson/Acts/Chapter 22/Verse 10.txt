<p><b> Into Damascus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις Δαμασκον</span></span>). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:6">Acts 9:6</span> simply "into the city" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις την πολιν</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Of all things which </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ παντων ων</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hων</span></span>, relative plural attracted to genitive of antecedent from accusative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span>, object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιησα</span></span> (do).</p>
<p><b>Are appointed for thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τετακτα σο</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τασσω</span></span>, to appoint, to order, with dative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σο</span></span>. Compare with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ σε δε</span></span> of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:6">Acts 9:6</span>. The words were spoken to Paul, of course, in the Aramaic, Saoul, Saoul. </p>
