<p><b> Hath appointed thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προεχειρισατο</span></span>). First aorist middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προχειριζω</span></span>, old verb to put forth into one's hands, to take into one's hands beforehand, to plan, propose, determine. In the N.T. only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:20">Acts 3:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:14">Acts 22:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:16">Acts 26:16</span>. Three infinitives after this verb of God's purpose about Paul:</p>
<p><b>to know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνωνα</span></span>, second aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span>) his will,</p>
<p><b>to see </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδειν</span></span>, second aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span>) the Righteous One (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:14">Acts 3:14</span>),</p>
<p><b>to hear </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουσα</span></span>, first aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουω</span></span>) a voice from his mouth. </p>
