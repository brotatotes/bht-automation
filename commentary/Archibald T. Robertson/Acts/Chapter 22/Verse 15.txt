<p><b> A witness for him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυς αυτω</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:8">Acts 1:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>Of what </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ων</span></span>). Attraction of the accusative relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> to the genitive case of the unexpressed antecedent <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτων</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Thou hast seen and heard </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εωρακας</span></span>, present perfect active indicative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ηκουσας</span></span>, first aorist active indicative). This subtle change of tense is not preserved in the English. Blass properly cites the perfect <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εωρακα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:1">1 Corinthians 9:1</span> as proof of Paul's enduring qualification for the apostleship. </p>
