<p><b> Doth bear me witness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρε μο</span></span>). Present active indicative as if still living. Caiaphas was no longer high priest now, for Ananias is at this time (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:2">Acts 23:2</span>), though he may be still alive.</p>
<p><b>All the estate of the elders </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παν το πρεσβυτεριον</span></span>). All the eldership or the Sanhedrin (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:5">Acts 4:5</span>) of which Paul was probably then a member (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:10">Acts 26:10</span>). Possibly some of those present were members of the Sanhedrin then (some 20 odd years ago).</p>
<p><b>From whom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρ' ων</span></span>). The high priest and the Sanhedrin.</p>
<p><b>Letters unto the brethren </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισταλας προς τους αδελφους</span></span>). Paul still can tactfully call the Jews his "brothers" as he did in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:3">Romans 9:3</span>. There is no bitterness in his heart.</p>
<p><b>Journeyed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επορευομην</span></span>). Imperfect middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορευομα</span></span>, and a vivid reality to Paul still as he was going on towards Damascus.</p>
<p><b>To bring also </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αξων κα</span></span>). Future active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγω</span></span>, to express purpose, one of the few N.T. examples of this classic idiom (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 1118).</p>
<p><b>Them which were there </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους εκεισε οντας</span></span>). <i>Constructio praegnans</i>. The usual word would be <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκε</span></span> (there), not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεισε</span></span> (thither). Possibly the Christians who had fled to Damascus, and so were there (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 548).</p>
<p><b>In bonds </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεδεμενους</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεω</span></span>, predicate position, "bound."</p>
<p><b>For to be punished </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα τιμωρηθωσιν</span></span>). First aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιμωρεω</span></span>, old verb to avenge, to take vengeance on. In the N.T. only here, and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:11">Acts 26:11</span>. Pure final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>. He carried his persecution outside of Palestine just as later he carried the gospel over the Roman empire. </p>
