<p><b> And it came to pass </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγενετο δε</span></span>). Rather than the common <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα εγενετο</span></span> and with the infinitive (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιαστραψα</span></span>), one of the three constructions with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα (δε) εγενετο</span></span> by Luke (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, pp. 1042f.), followed by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span>, by finite verb, by subject infinitive as here.</p>
<p><b>As I made my journey </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μο πορευομενω</span></span>). To me (dative after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγενετο</span></span>, happened to me) journeying (participle agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μο</span></span>). See this same idiom in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:17">Acts 22:17</span>. Luke uses <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγενετο δε</span></span> seventeen times in the gospel and twenty-one in the Acts.</p>
<p><b>Unto Damascus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη Δαμασκω</span></span>). Dative after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγγιζοντ</span></span> (drawing nigh to).</p>
<p><b>About noon </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ μεσημβριαν</span></span>). Mid (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεσος</span></span>) day (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημερα</span></span>), old word, in the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:26">Acts 8:26</span> which see where it may mean "toward the south." An item not in ch. 9.</p>
<p><b>Shone round about me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιαστραψα περ εμε</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιαστραπτω</span></span>, to flash around, in LXX and late Greek, in the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:3">Acts 9:3</span> which see. Note repetition of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>A great light </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φως ικανον</span></span>). Luke's favourite word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ικανον</span></span> (considerable). Accusative of general reference with the infinitive. </p>
