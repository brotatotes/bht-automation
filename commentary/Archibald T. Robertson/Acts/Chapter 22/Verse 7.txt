<p><b> I fell </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεσα</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-α</span></span> rather than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεσον</span></span>, the usual form of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιπτω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Unto the ground </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το εδαφος</span></span>). Old word, here alone in N.T. So the verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδαφιζω</span></span>, is in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:44">Luke 19:44</span> alone in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>A voice saying </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωνης λεγουσης</span></span>). Genitive after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκουσα</span></span>, though in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:14">Acts 26:14</span> the accusative is used after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκουσα</span></span>, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:14">Acts 22:14</span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουσα</span></span>, either being allowable. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:7">Acts 9:7</span> for discussion of the difference in case. Saul's name repeated each time (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:4">Acts 9:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:7">Acts 22:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:14">Acts 26:14</span>). Same question also in each report: "Why persecuted thou me?" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Τ με διωκεισ?</span></span>). These piercing words stuck in Paul's mind. </p>
