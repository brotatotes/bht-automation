<p><b> When there arose a great dissension </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολλης της γινομενης στασεως</span></span>). Present middle participle (genitive absolute). Literally, "dissension becoming much."</p>
<p><b>Lest Paul should be torn in pieces by them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη διασπασθη ο Παυλος</span></span>). First aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διασπαω</span></span>, to draw in two, to tear in pieces, old verb, in the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:4">Mark 5:4</span> of tearing chains in two. The subjunctive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> is the common construction after a verb of fearing (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 995).</p>
<p><b>The soldiers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το στρατευμα</span></span>). The army, the band of soldiers and so in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:27">Acts 23:27</span>.</p>
<p><b>To go down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταβαν</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταβαινω</span></span>, having gone down.</p>
<p><b>Take him by force </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρπασα</span></span>). To seize. The soldiers were to seize and save Paul from the midst of (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ μεσου</span></span>) the rabbis or preachers (in their rage to get at each other). Paul was more of a puzzle to Lysias now than ever. </p>
