<p><b> Ye </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμεις</span></span>). Emphatic.</p>
<p><b>Signify </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμφανισατε</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμφανιζω</span></span>. Make plain from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμφανης</span></span>, chiefly in Acts. Repeated in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:22">Acts 23:22</span>. The authority is with the chiliarch not with the Sanhedrin, but he had appealed to the Sanhedrin for advice.</p>
<p><b>As though ye would judge of his case more exactly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως μελλοντας διαγινωσκειν ακριβεστερον τα περ αυτου</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hως</span></span> with the participle gives the alleged reason as here. So also in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:20">Acts 23:20</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Διαγνοσκω</span></span>, old verb to distinguish accurately, only here in N.T. and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:22">Acts 24:22</span>.</p>
<p><b>Or ever come near </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προ του εγγισα αυτον</span></span>). "Before the coming near as to him." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Προ</span></span> and the genitive of the articular infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγγιζω</span></span> with accusative of general reference.</p>
<p><b>We are ready to slay him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετοιμο εσμεν του ανελειν αυτον</span></span>). Genitive of purpose of the articular infinitive after the adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετοιμο</span></span> (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 1061). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ανελειν</span></span>, second aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναιρεω</span></span>. </p>
