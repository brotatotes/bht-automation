<p><b> Took him by the hand </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλαβομενος της χειρος αυτου</span></span>). Kindly touch in Lysias, <i>ut fiduciam adolescentis confirmaret</i> (Bengel). Note genitive with the second aorist middle (indirect, to himself) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλαμβανω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:54">Luke 8:54</span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρατησας</span></span> which see. How old the young man (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νεανιας</span></span>) was we do not know, but it is the very word used of Paul in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:58">Acts 7:58</span> when he helped in the killing of Stephen, a young man in the twenties probably. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:9">Acts 20:9</span> of Eutychus. He is termed <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νεανισκος</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:22">Acts 23:22</span>.</p>
<p><b>Asked him privately </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατ' ιδιαν επυνθανετο</span></span>). Imperfect middle, began to ask (inchoative). </p>
