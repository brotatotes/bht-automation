<p><b> Two </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τινας δυο</span></span>). "Some two" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:19">Luke 7:19</span>, indicating (Page) that they were not specially chosen.</p>
<p><b>Soldiers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρατιωτας</span></span>),</p>
<p><b>horsemen </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιππεις</span></span>),</p>
<p><b>spearmen </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεξιολαβους</span></span>). The three varieties of troops in a Roman army like the cohort of Lysias (Page). The <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρατιωτα</span></span> were the heavy-armed legionaries, the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιππεις</span></span> belonged to every legion, the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεξιολαβο</span></span> were light-armed supplementary troops who carried a lance in the right hand (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεξιος</span></span>, right, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>, to take). Vulgate, <i>lancearios</i>. At the third hour of the night (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο τριτης ωρας της νυκτος</span></span>). About nine in the evening. </p>
