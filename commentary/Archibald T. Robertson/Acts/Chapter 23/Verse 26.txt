<p><b> Most excellent </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρατιστω</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:3">Luke 1:3</span> to Theophilus though not in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:1">Acts 1:1</span>. It is usual in addressing men of rank as here, like our "Your Excellency" in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:3">Acts 24:3</span> and Paul uses it to Festus in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:25">Acts 26:25</span>.</p>
<p><b>Greeting </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαιρειν</span></span>). Absolute infinitive with independent or absolute nominative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κλαυδιος Λυσιας</span></span>) as is used in letters (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:23">Acts 15:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:1">James 1:1</span>) and in countless papyri (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 1092). </p>
