<p><b> When it was shown to me that there would be a plot </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηνυθεισης μο επιβουλης εσεσθα</span></span>). Two constructions combined; genitive absolute (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηνυθεισης επιβουλης</span></span>, first aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηνυω</span></span>) and future infinitive (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσεσθα</span></span> as if <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιβουλην</span></span> accusative of general reference used) in indirect assertion after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηνυω</span></span> (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 877).</p>
<p><b>Charging his accusers also </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραγγειλας κα τοις κατηγοροις</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραγγελλω</span></span> with which compare <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαθων</span></span> above (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:27">Acts 23:27</span>), not subsequent action. Dative case in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηγοροις</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Before thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ σου</span></span>). Common idiom for "in the presence of" when before a judge (like Latin <i>apud</i>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:20">Acts 24:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:21">Acts 24:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:26">Acts 25:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:2">Acts 26:2</span>. What happened to the forty conspirators we have no way of knowing. Neither they nor the Jews from Asia are heard of more during the long five years of Paul's imprisonment in Caesarea and Rome. </p>
