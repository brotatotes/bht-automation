<p><b> And they </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιτινες</span></span>). Which very ones, the cavalry, the horsemen of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:31">Acts 23:31</span>.</p>
<p><b>Delivered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναδοντες</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναδιδωμ</span></span>, old verb to give up, to hand over, here only in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>Presented Paul also </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεστησαν κα τον Παυλον</span></span>). First aorist active (transitive, not second aorist intransitive) indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παριστημ</span></span>, common verb to present or place beside. What would Paul's friends in Caesarea (Philip and his daughters) think of the prophecy of Agabus now so quickly come true. </p>
