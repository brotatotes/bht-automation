<p><b> I will hear thy cause </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακουσομα</span></span>). "I will hear thee fully" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>When--are come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραγενωντα</span></span>). Second aorist middle subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραγινομα</span></span> with temporal conjunction <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν</span></span>, indefinite temporal clause of future time (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 972), "whenever thine accusers come."</p>
<p><b>In Herod's palace </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω πραιτωριω</span></span>). The Latin word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πραετοριυμ</span></span>. The word meant the camp of the general, then the palace of the governor as here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:27">Matthew 27:27</span> which see, and then the camp of praetorian soldiers or rather the praetorian guard as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:13">Philippians 1:13</span>. </p>
