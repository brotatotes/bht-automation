<p><b> When he had so said </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτο αυτου λαλουντος</span></span>). Genitive absolute of present participle (Westcott and Hort) rather than aorist (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειποντος</span></span>). While he was saying this.</p>
<p><b>A dissension </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στασις</span></span>). This old word for standing or station (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:8">Hebrews 9:8</span>) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span>, to place, we have seen already to mean insurrection (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:40">Acts 19:40</span> which see). Here it is strife as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:2">Acts 15:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>Was divided </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσχισθη</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:4">Acts 14:4</span>. </p>
