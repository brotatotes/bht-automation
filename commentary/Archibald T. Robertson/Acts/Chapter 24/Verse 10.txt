<p><b> When the governor had beckoned to him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νευσαντος αυτω του ηγεμονος</span></span>). Genitive absolute again with first aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νευω</span></span>, to give a nod, old word, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:24">John 13:24</span>. "The governor nodding to him."</p>
<p><b>Forasmuch as I know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισταμενος</span></span>). Knowing, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισταμα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>That thou hast been of many years a judge </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ πολλων ετων οντα σε κριτην</span></span>). The participle in indirect assertion after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισταμενος</span></span> (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 1041). Paul goes as far as he can in the way of a compliment. For seven years Felix has been governor, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οντα</span></span> being a sort of progressive present participle with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ πολλων ετων</span></span> (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 892).</p>
<p><b>Cheerfully </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευθυμως</span></span>). Old adverb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευθυμος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυμος</span></span>, good spirit), here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Make my defence </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολογουμα</span></span>). Old and regular word for this idea as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:14">Luke 21:14</span> which see. </p>
