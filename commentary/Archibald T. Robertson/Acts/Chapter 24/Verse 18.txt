<p><b> Amidst which </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αιλ</span></span>). That is, "in which offerings" (in presenting which offerings, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:27">Acts 21:27</span>).</p>
<p><b>They found me </b> (my accusers here present, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρον με</span></span>),</p>
<p><b>purified in the temple </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγνισμενον εν τω ιερω</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγνιζω</span></span> (same verb in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:24">Acts 21:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:26">Acts 21:26</span>) state of completion of the Jewish sacrifices which had gone on for seven days (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:27">Acts 21:27</span>), the very opposite of the charges made.</p>
<p><b>With no crowd </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μετα οχλου</span></span>). "Not with a crowd" till the Asiatic Jews gathered one (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:27">Acts 21:27</span>).</p>
<p><b>Nor yet with tumult </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδε μετα θορυβου</span></span>). They made the tumult (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:30">Acts 27:30</span>), not Paul. Till they made the stir, all was quiet. </p>
