<p><b> These men themselves </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτο ουτο</span></span>). Since the Asiatic Jews are not present and these men are.</p>
<p><b>Wrong doing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδικημα</span></span>). Or misdeed. Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδικεω</span></span>, to do wrong. In the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:14">Acts 18:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:5">Revelation 18:5</span>. Paul uses "<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδικημα</span></span>" from the standpoint of his accusers. "To a less sensitive conscience his action before the Sanhedrin would have seemed venial enough" (Furneaux).</p>
<p><b>When I stood </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σταντος μου</span></span>). Genitive absolute, second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span> (intransitive), "when I took my stand."</p>
<p><b>Before the council </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ του συνεδριου</span></span>). Same use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> with genitive as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:19">Acts 24:19</span>. </p>
