<p><b> Except it be </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span>). Literally, "than," but after interrogative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τ = τ αλλο</span></span> "what else than."</p>
<p><b>For this one voice </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ μιας ταυτης φωνης</span></span>). The normal Greek idiom with the attributive use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτος</span></span> calls for the article before <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιας</span></span>, though some inscriptions show it as here (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 702).</p>
<p><b>That </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ης</span></span>). Genitive of the relative attracted to the case of the antecedent</p>
<p><b>phons </b>.</p>
<p><b>I cried </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεκραξα</span></span>). Reduplicated aorist as is usual with this verb in the LXX (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jdg+3:15">Judges 3:15</span>). Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 348.</p>
<p><b>Touching </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ</span></span>). Concerning (around, about).</p>
<p><b>I am called in question </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρινομα</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:6">Acts 23:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Before you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφ' υμων</span></span>). Same idiom as in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:19">Acts 24:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:20">Acts 24:20</span>. </p>
