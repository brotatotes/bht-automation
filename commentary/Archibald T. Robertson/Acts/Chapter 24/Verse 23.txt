<p><b> And should have indulgence </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχειν τε ανεσιν</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανιημ</span></span>, to let loose, release, relax. Old word, in the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:7">2 Thessalonians 1:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:13">2 Corinthians 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:5">2 Corinthians 7:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:13">2 Corinthians 8:13</span>. It is the opposite of strict confinement, though under guard, "kept in charge" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρεισθα</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Forbid </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κωλυειν</span></span>). To hinder "no one of his friends" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηδενα των ιδιων</span></span>). No one of Paul's "own" (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:23">Acts 4:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:11">John 1:11</span>) or intimates. Of these we know the names of Luke, Aristarchus, Trophimus, Philip the Evangelist. </p>
