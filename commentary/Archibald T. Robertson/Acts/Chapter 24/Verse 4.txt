<p><b> That I be not further tedious unto thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη επ πλειον σε ενκοπτω</span></span>). <i>Koine</i> verb (Hippocrates, Polybius) to cut in on (or into), to cut off, to impede, to hinder. Our modern telephone and radio illustrate it well. In the N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:4">Acts 24:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:18">1 Thessalonians 2:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:7">Galatians 5:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:22">Romans 15:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:7">1 Peter 3:7</span>). "That I may not cut in on or interrupt thee further (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ πλειον</span></span>) in thy reforms." Flattery still.</p>
<p><b>Of thy clemency </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη ση επιεικεια</span></span>). Instrumental case of old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιεικης</span></span> and this from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εικος</span></span> (reasonable, likely, fair). "Sweet Reasonableness" (Matthew Arnold), gentleness, fairness. An <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιεικης</span></span> man is "one who makes reasonable concessions" (Aristotle, <i>Eth</i>. V. 10), while <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιος</span></span> is "one who insists on his full rights" (Plato, <i>Leg</i>. 757 D) as translated by Page.</p>
<p><b>A few words </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συντομως</span></span>). Old adverb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συντεμνω</span></span>, to cut together (short), abbreviate. Like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια βραχεων</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:22">Hebrews 13:22</span>. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:16">Acts 24:16</span> (shorter conclusion). </p>
