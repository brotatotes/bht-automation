<p><b> Joined in the charge </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεπεθεντο</span></span>). Second aorist middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεπιτιθημ</span></span>, old verb, double compound, to place upon (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>) together with (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν</span></span>), to make a joint attack, here only in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>Affirming </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φασκοντες</span></span>). Alleging, with the accusative in indirect assertion as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:19">Acts 25:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:22">Romans 1:22</span> (nominative with infinitive, Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 1038).</p>
<p><b>Were so </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτως εχειν</span></span>), "held thus," common idiom. </p>
