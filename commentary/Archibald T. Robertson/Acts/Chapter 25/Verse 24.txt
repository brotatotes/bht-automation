<p><b> Which are here present with us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο συνπαροντες ημιν</span></span>). Present articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνπαρειμ</span></span> (only here in N.T.) with associative instrumental case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημιν</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Made suit to me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενετυχον μο</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντυγχανω</span></span>, old verb to fall in with a person, to go to meet for consultation or supplication as here. Common in old Greek and <i>Koine</i>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:27">Romans 8:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:34">Romans 8:34</span>. See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντευξις</span></span> (petition) <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:1">1 Timothy 2:1</span>. Papyri give many examples of the technical sense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντευξις</span></span> as petition (Deissmann, <i>Bible Studies</i>, p. 121). Some MSS. have plural here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενετυχον</span></span> rather than the singular <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενετυχεν</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Crying </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βοωντες</span></span>). Yelling and demanding with loud voices.</p>
<p><b>That he ought not to live any longer </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη δειν αυτον ζηιν μηκετ</span></span>). Indirect command (demand) with the infinitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δειν</span></span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span> (it is necessary). The double negative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη--μηκετ</span></span>) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζηιν</span></span> intensifies the demand. </p>
