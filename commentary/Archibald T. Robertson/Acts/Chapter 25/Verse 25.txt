<p><b> But I found </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγω δε κατελαβομην</span></span>). Second aorist middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλαμβανω</span></span>, to lay hold of, to grasp, to comprehend as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:13">Acts 4:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:34">Acts 10:34</span>.</p>
<p><b>That he had committed nothing worthy of death </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηδεν αξιον αυτον θανατου πεπραχενα</span></span>). Perfect active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρασσω</span></span> in indirect assertion with negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> and accusative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτον</span></span> of general reference, the usual idiom. Verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:25">Acts 25:25</span> repeats the statement in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:21">Acts 25:21</span>, perhaps for the benefit of the assembled dignitaries. </p>
