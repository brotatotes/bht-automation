<p><b> Asking favour against him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιτουμενο χαριν κατ' αυτου</span></span>). A favour to themselves (middle voice), not to Paul, but "against" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατ'</span></span>, down, against) him.</p>
<p><b>That he would send for </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπως μεταπεμψητα</span></span>). First aorist middle subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταπεμπω</span></span> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:24">Acts 24:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:26">Acts 24:26</span>) with final particle <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπως</span></span> like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>. Aorist tense for single case.</p>
<p><b>Laying wait </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεδραν ποιουντες</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:16">Acts 23:16</span> for the word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεδρα</span></span>. Old idiom (Thucydides) for laying a plot or ambush as here. Only these two uses of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεδρα</span></span> in N.T. Two years before the Sanhedrin had agreed to the plot of the forty conspirators. Now they propose one on their own initiative.</p>
<p><b>On the way </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα την οδον</span></span>). Down along, up and down along the way. Plenty of opportunity would occur between Caesarea and Jerusalem for ambush and surprise attacks. </p>
