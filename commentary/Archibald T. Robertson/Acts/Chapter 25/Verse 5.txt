<p><b> Them therefore which are of power among you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ουν εν υμιν δυνατο</span></span>). "The mighty ones among you," "the men of power" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυνατο</span></span>) and authority, "the first men," the Sanhedrin, in other words. Note change here by Luke from indirect discourse in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:4">Acts 25:4</span>, to direct in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:5">Acts 25:5</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φησιν</span></span>, says he).</p>
<p><b>Go down with me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνκαταβαντες</span></span>). Double compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν, κατα</span></span>) second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνκαταβαινω</span></span>. It was a fair proposal.</p>
<p><b>If there is anything amiss in the man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε τ εστιν εν τω ανδρ ατοπον</span></span>). Condition of the first class, assuming that there is (to be courteous to them), but not committing himself on the merits of the case. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ατοπον</span></span> is an old word, specially common in Plato, meaning "out of place." In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:41">Luke 23:41</span> which see; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:6">Acts 28:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:2">2 Thessalonians 3:2</span>. Note present tense active voice of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηγορειτωσαν</span></span> (imperative) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηγορεω</span></span>, repeat their accusations. </p>
