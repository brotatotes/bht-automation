<p><b> On the morrow </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη επαυριον</span></span>). Locative case of the article with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημερα</span></span> understood (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαυριον</span></span>, adverb, tomorrow). Festus lost no time for the chief men had come down with him.</p>
<p><b>Sat on the judgment seat </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθισας επ του βηματος</span></span>). A legal formality to give weight to the decision. Ingressive aorist active participle. For this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βημα</span></span> for judgment seat see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:19">Acts 27:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:13">John 19:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:21">Acts 12:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:12">Acts 18:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:10">Acts 25:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:17">Acts 25:17</span>. Same phrase repeated in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:17">Acts 25:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>To be brought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχθηνα</span></span>). First aorist passive infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγω</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκελευσεν</span></span> (commanded). Same words repeated in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:17">Acts 25:17</span> by Festus. </p>
