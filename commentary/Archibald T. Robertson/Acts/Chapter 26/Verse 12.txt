<p><b> Whereupon </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν οις</span></span>). "In which things" (affairs of persecution), "on which errand." Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:18">Acts 24:18</span>. Paul made them leave Palestine (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:19">Acts 11:19</span>) and followed them beyond it (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:2">Acts 9:2</span>).</p>
<p><b>With the authority and commission </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετ' εξουσιας κα επιτροπης</span></span>). Not merely "authority" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξουσια</span></span>), but express appointment (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιτροπη</span></span>, old word, but here only in N.T., derived from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιτροπος</span></span>, steward, and that from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιτρεπω</span></span>, to turn over to, to commit). </p>
