<p><b> At midday </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημερας μεσης</span></span>). Genitive of time and idiomatic use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεσος</span></span>, in the middle of the day, more vivid than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεσημβριαν</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:6">Acts 22:6</span>).</p>
<p><b>Above the brightness of the sun </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ την λαμπροτητα του ηλιου</span></span>). Here alone not in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:9">Acts 26:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:22">Acts 26:22</span>, though implied in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:3">Acts 9:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:6">Acts 22:6</span>, "indicating the supernatural character of the light" (Knowling). Luke makes no effort to harmonize the exact phrases here with those in the other accounts and Paul here (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:16">Acts 26:16</span>) blends together what Jesus said to him directly and the message of Jesus through Ananias (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:15">Acts 9:15</span>). The word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμπροτης</span></span>, old word, is here alone in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>Shining round about me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιλαμψαν με</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιλαμπω</span></span>, common <i>Koine</i> verb, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:9">Luke 2:9</span>. </p>
