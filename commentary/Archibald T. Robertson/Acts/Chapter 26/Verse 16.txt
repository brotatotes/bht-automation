<p><b> Arise and stand </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναστηθ κα στηθ</span></span>). "Emphatic assonance" (Page). Second aorist active imperative of compound verb (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανιστημ</span></span>) and simplex (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span>). "Stand up and take a stand."</p>
<p><b>Have I appeared unto thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωφθην σο</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:43">Luke 22:43</span>.</p>
<p><b>To appoint thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προχειρισασθα σε</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:30">Acts 3:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:14">Acts 22:14</span> for this verb.</p>
<p><b>Both of the things wherein thou hast seen me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ων τε ειδες με</span></span>). The reading <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">με</span></span> (not in all MSS.) makes it the object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδες</span></span> (didst see) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ων</span></span> is genitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> (accusative of general reference) attracted to the case of the unexpressed antecedent <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτων</span></span>. Paul is thus a personal eyewitness of the Risen Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:1">Luke 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:1">1 Corinthians 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:1">1 Corinthians 9:1</span>).</p>
<p><b>And of the things wherein I will appear unto thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ων τε οφθησομα σο</span></span>). Here again <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ων</span></span> is genitive of the accusative (general reference) relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> attracted to the case of the antecedent <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτων</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινων</span></span> as before. But <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφθησομα</span></span> is first future passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span> and cannot be treated as active or middle. Page takes it to mean "the visions in which I shall be seen by you," the passive form bringing out the agency of God. See those in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:9">Acts 18:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:11">Acts 23:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:2">2 Corinthians 12:2</span>. The passive voice, however, like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεκριθην</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφοβηθην</span></span>, did become sometimes transitive in the <i>Koine</i> (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 819). </p>
