<p><b> To open </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανοιξα</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of purpose.</p>
<p><b>That they may turn </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του επιστρεψα</span></span>). Another infinitive of purpose first aorist active (genitive case and articular), epexegetic to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανοιξα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>That they may receive </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του λαβειν</span></span>). Another genitive articular infinitive of purpose subordinate (epexegetic) to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του επιστρεψα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Sanctified by faith in me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγιασμενοις πιστε τη εις εμε</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγιαζω</span></span>, instrumental case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστε</span></span>, article before <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις εμε</span></span> ("by faith, that in me"). These important words of Jesus to Paul give his justification to this cultured audience for his response to the command of Jesus. This was the turning point in Paul's career and it was a step forward and upward. </p>
