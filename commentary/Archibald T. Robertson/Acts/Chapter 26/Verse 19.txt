<p><b> Wherefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οθεν</span></span>). This relatival adverb (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:26">Acts 14:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:13">Acts 28:13</span>) gathers up all that Paul has said.</p>
<p><b>I was not disobedient </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ εγενομην απειθης</span></span>). Litotes again, "I did not become (second aorist middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span>) disobedient" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απειθης</span></span>, old word already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:17">Luke 1:17</span>).</p>
<p><b>Unto the heavenly vision </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη ουρανιω οπτασια</span></span>). A later form of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οψις</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπταζω</span></span>, in LXX, and in N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:22">Luke 1:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:23">Luke 24:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:19">Acts 26:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:1">2 Corinthians 12:1</span>). Only time that Paul uses it about seeing Christ on the Damascus road, but no reflection on the reality of the event. </p>
