<p><b> I think myself happy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγημα εμαυτον μακαριον</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:3">Matthew 5:3</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακαριος</span></span>. Blass notes that Paul, like Tertullus, begins with <i>captatio benevolentiae</i>, but <i>absque adulatione</i>. He says only what he can truthfully speak. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγημα</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:7">Philippians 3:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:1">1 Timothy 6:1</span> (perfect middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγεομα</span></span>), I have considered.</p>
<p><b>That I am to make my defence </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μελλων απολογεισθα</span></span>). Literally, "being about to make my defence."</p>
<p><b>Whereof I am accused </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ων εγκαλουμα</span></span>). Genitive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγκαλουμα</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:40">Acts 19:40</span> or by attraction from accusative of relative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span>) to case of antecedent (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντων</span></span>). </p>
