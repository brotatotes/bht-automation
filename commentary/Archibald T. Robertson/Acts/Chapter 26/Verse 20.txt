<p><b> But declared </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα απηγγελλον</span></span>). Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαγγελλω</span></span>, repeatedly.</p>
<p><b>Throughout all the country of Judea </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πασαν τε την χωραν της Ιουδαιας</span></span>). The accusative here in the midst of the datives (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις εν Δαμασκωι, Ιεροσολυμοισ, τοις εθνεσιν</span></span>) seems strange and Page feels certain that <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> should be here even though absent in Aleph A B. But the accusative of extent of space will explain it (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 469).</p>
<p><b>Doing works worthy of repentance </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αξια της μετανοιας εργα πρασσοντας</span></span>). Accusative case of present active participle <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρασσοντας</span></span> because of the implied <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτους</span></span> with the present infinitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετανοειν</span></span> (repent) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιστρεφειν</span></span> (turn), though the dative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρασσουσιν</span></span> could have been used to agree with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εθνεσιν</span></span> (Gentiles). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:8">Matthew 3:8</span> for similar language used of the Baptist. Paul, the greatest of theologians, was an interesting practical preacher. </p>
