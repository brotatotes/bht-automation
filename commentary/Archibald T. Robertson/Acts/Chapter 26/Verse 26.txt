<p><b> For the king knoweth of these things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιστατα γαρ περ τουτων ο βασιλευς</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Επιστατα</span></span> (present middle probably Ionic form of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφιστημ</span></span>) is a literary word and suits well here (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:10">Acts 24:10</span>).</p>
<p><b>Freely </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρρησιαζομενος</span></span>). Present middle participle, speaking fully, making a clean breast of it. From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρρησια</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παν, ρησις</span></span>) (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:46">Acts 13:46</span>).</p>
<p><b>Is hidden from him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λανθανειν αυτον</span></span>). Escapes his notice. Infinitive in indirect discourse after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειθομα</span></span> (I am persuaded). </p>
