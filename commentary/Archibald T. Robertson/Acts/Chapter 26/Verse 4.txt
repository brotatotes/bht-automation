<p><b> My manner of life </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την μεν ουν βιωσιν μου</span></span>). With <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεν ουν</span></span> Paul passes from the <i>captatio benevolentiae</i> (verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:1">Acts 26:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:2">Acts 26:2</span>) "to the <i>narratio</i> or statement of his case" (Page). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Βιωσις</span></span> is from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βιοω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:2">1 Peter 4:2</span>) and that from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βιος</span></span> (course of life). This is the only instance of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βιωσις</span></span> yet found except the Prologue (10) of Ecclesiasticus and an inscription given in Ramsay's <i>Cities and Bishoprics of Phrygia</i>, Vol II, p. 650.</p>
<p><b>Know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ισασ</span></span>). Literary form instead of the vernacular <i>Koine</i> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιδασιν</span></span>. Paul's early life in Tarsus and Jerusalem was an open book to all Jews. </p>
