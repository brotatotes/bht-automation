<p><b> Having knowledge of me from the first </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προγινωσκοντες με ανωθεν</span></span>). Literally, "knowing me beforehand" (both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανωθεν</span></span>), from the beginning of Paul's public education in Jerusalem (Knowling). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:17">2 Peter 3:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>If they be willing to testify </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν θελωσιν μαρτυρειν</span></span>). Condition of third class (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and subjunctive). A neat turning of the tables on the distinguished audience about Paul's Jerusalem reputation before his conversion.</p>
<p><b>After the straitest sect </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την ακριβεστατην αιρεσιν</span></span>). This is a true superlative (not elative) and one of the three (also <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγιωτατος</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:20">Jude 1:20</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιμιωτατος</span></span> <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:12">Revelation 18:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:11">Revelation 21:11</span>) superlatives in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-τατος</span></span> in the N.T. (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, pp. 279f., 670), though common enough in the LXX and the papyri. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hαιρεσιν</span></span> (choosing) is properly used here with Pharisees (Josephus, <i>Life</i>, 38).</p>
<p><b>Religion </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θρησκειας</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θρησκευω</span></span> and this from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θρησκος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:26">James 1:26</span>), old word for religious worship or discipline, common in the papyri and inscriptions (Moulton and Milligan's <i>Vocabulary</i>) for reverent worship, not mere external ritual. In N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:26">James 1:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:18">Colossians 2:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>I lived a Pharisee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εζησα Φαρισαιος</span></span>). Emphatic position. Paul knew the rules of the Pharisees and played the game to the full (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:14">Galatians 1:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:5">Philippians 3:5</span>). The Talmud makes it plain what the life of a Pharisee was. Paul had become one of the leaders and stars of hope for his sect. </p>
