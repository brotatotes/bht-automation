<p><b> And now </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα νυν</span></span>). Sharp comparison between his youth and the present.</p>
<p><b>To be judged for the hope </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ' ελπιδι--κρινομενος</span></span>). The hope of the resurrection and of the promised Messiah (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:32">Acts 13:32</span>). Page calls verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:6-8">Acts 26:6-8</span> a parenthesis in the course of Paul's argument by which he shows that his life in Christ is a real development of the best in Pharisaism. He does resume his narrative in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:9">Acts 26:9</span>, but verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:6-8">Acts 26:6-8</span> are the core of his defence already presented in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:3">Acts 26:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:9-11">Acts 26:9-11</span> where he proves that the children of faith are the real seed of Abraham. </p>
