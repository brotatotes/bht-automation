<p><b> When the south wind blew softly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποπνευσαντος νοτου</span></span>). Genitive absolute with aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποπνεω</span></span>, old verb to blow under, then to blow gently, here only in N.T. "A south wind having blown gently," in marked contrast to the violent northwest wind that they had faced so long. They were so sure of the wisdom of their decision that they did not even draw up the small boat attached by a rope to the vessel's stern (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:16">Acts 27:16</span>). It was only some forty miles to Lutro.</p>
<p><b>Their purpose </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της προθεσεως</span></span>, set before them, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προτιθημ</span></span>), genitive after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρατεω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεκρατηκενα</span></span>, perfect active infinitive in indirect discourse).</p>
<p><b>They weighed anchor </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αραντες</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιρω</span></span>, old verb used in technical sense with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τας αγκυρας</span></span> (anchors) understood as in Thucydides I. 52; II. 23, "having lifted the anchors." Page takes it simply as "moving."</p>
<p><b>Sailed along Crete </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρελεγοντο την Κρητην</span></span>). Imperfect middle. See verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:8">Acts 27:8</span>, "were coasting along Crete."</p>
<p><b>Close in shore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασσον</span></span>). Comparative adverb of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγκ</span></span>, near, and so "nearer" to shore. Only here in N.T. </p>
