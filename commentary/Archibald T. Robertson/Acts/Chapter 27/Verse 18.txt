<p><b> As we laboured exceedingly with the storm </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σφοδρως χειμαζομενων ημων</span></span>). Genitive absolute with present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χειμαζω</span></span>, old verb to afflict with a tempest (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χειμα</span></span>, stormy weather), to toss upon the waves, here alone in N.T.</p>
<p><b>They began to throw overboard </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκβαλην εποιουντο</span></span>). Literally, "They began to make (inchoative imperfect middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span>) a casting out" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκβολην</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκβαλλω</span></span>, to cast out, old word, only here in N.T.). Cf. Latin <i>jacturam facere</i>. This to lighten the ship by throwing overboard the cargo. The grain in the ship would shift and make it list and so added to the danger.</p>
<p><b>They cast out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εριψαν</span></span>). Third person plural aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ριπτω</span></span>, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εριψαμεν</span></span> as Textus Receptus.</p>
<p><b>With their own hands </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτοχειρες</span></span>). Old word (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτοσ, χειρ</span></span>) but here alone in N.T. Vivid and graphic touch by Luke who, of course, watched every movement day by day.</p>
<p><b>The tackling </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την σκευην</span></span>). The furniture of the ship that could be spared. It was becoming desperate. </p>
