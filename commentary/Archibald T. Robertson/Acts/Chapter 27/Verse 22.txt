<p><b> And now </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα τα νυν</span></span>). Accusative plural neuter article of general reference in contrast with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεν</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:21">Acts 27:21</span>. Paul shows modesty (Bengel) in the mild contrast.</p>
<p><b>No loss of life </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποβολη ψυχης ουδεμια</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποβαλλω</span></span>, to throw away, only twice in N.T. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:15">Romans 11:15</span> (rejection) and here. He had foretold such loss of life as likely (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:10">Acts 27:10</span>), but he now gives his reason for his changed view. </p>
