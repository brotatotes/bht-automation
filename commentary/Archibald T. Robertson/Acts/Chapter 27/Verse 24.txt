<p><b> Thou must stand before Caesar </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Καισαρ σε δε παραστηνα</span></span>). Note the same <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span> (must) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:11">Acts 23:11</span> when Jesus appeared to Paul in Jerusalem and the same verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραστηνα</span></span> (second aorist active infinitive) used in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:23">Acts 27:23</span>.</p>
<p><b>Hath granted thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεχαριστα σο</span></span>). Perfect middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριζομα</span></span> and that from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρις</span></span>, a gift or grace. The lives of those that sailed with Paul God had spared as a gift (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρις</span></span>) to Paul. </p>
