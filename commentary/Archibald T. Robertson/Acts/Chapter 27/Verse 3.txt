<p><b> The next day </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη ετερα</span></span>). Locative case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημερα</span></span> understood.</p>
<p><b>We touched </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηχθημεν</span></span>). First aorist passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταγω</span></span>, the usual term for "coming down" from the seas as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγω</span></span> above (and verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:4">Acts 27:4</span>) is for "going up" to sea. So it <i>looks</i> to sailors. Sidon was 67 miles from Caesarea, the rival of Tyre, with a splendid harbour. The ship stopped here for trade.</p>
<p><b>Treated Paul kindly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλανθρωπως τω Παυλω χρησαμενος</span></span>). "Using (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρησαμενος</span></span>, first aorist middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χραομα</span></span>, to use) Paul (instrumental case used with this verb) kindly" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλανθρωπως</span></span>, "philanthropically," adverb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλ-ανθρωπος</span></span>, love of mankind). He was kindly to Paul throughout the voyage (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:43">Acts 27:43</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:16">Acts 28:16</span>), taking a personal interest in his welfare.</p>
<p><b>Refresh himself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιμελειας τυχειν</span></span>). Second aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυγχανω</span></span> (to obtain) with the genitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιμελειας</span></span>, old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιμελης</span></span>, careful, only here in the N.T. Whether it was mere hospitality we do not know. It may have been medical attention required because of Paul's long confinement. This is Paul's first visit to Sidon, but Christians were already in Phoenicia (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:19">Acts 11:19</span>) and so Paul had "friends" here. </p>
