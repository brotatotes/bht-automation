<p><b> The sailors </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των ναυτων</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ναυς</span></span> (ship), in N.T. only here, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:30">Acts 27:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:17">Revelation 18:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>Were seeking </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζητουντων</span></span>). Genitive absolute again with present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζητεω</span></span> to seek.</p>
<p><b>Had lowered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαλασαντων</span></span>). Aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαλαζω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Under colour </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προφασε</span></span>). Possibly the same word as "prophecy" (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προ-φημ</span></span>, to speak forth), but here pretence, pretext, although it may come from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προφαινω</span></span>, to show forth. The use here is an old one and appears also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:40">Mark 12:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:47">Luke 20:47</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:5">1 Thessalonians 2:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:18">Philippians 1:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>As though </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως</span></span>). The alleged reason, a common Greek idiom with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως</span></span> and the participle (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 966). Here with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μελλοντων</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>From the foreship </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ πρωιρης</span></span>). Old word for prow of the ship. In the N.T. only here and verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:41">Acts 27:41</span>. Note here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκτεινειν</span></span> (lay out, stretch out) rather than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ριψαντες</span></span> (casting) in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:29">Acts 27:29</span>, for they pretended to need the small boat to stretch out or lay out the anchors in front. </p>
