<p><b> When they had eaten enough </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κορεσθεντες τροφης</span></span>). First aorist passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κορεννυμ</span></span>, old verb to satisfy, to satiate, with the genitive. Literally, "Having been satisfied with food." Here only in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>They lightened </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκουφιζον</span></span>). Inchoative imperfect active, began to lighten. Old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κουφος</span></span> and originally to be light, but transitive to lighten, as here, from Hippocrates on.</p>
<p><b>Throwing out the wheat </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκβαλλομενο τον σιτον</span></span>). The cargo of wheat. The second <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκβολη</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:18">Acts 27:18</span>) or casting out and overboard which was only partially done at first. </p>
