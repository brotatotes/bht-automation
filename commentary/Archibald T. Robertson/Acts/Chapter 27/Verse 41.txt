<p><b> But lighting upon </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιπεσοντες δε</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιπιπτω</span></span>, old verb to fall into and so be encompassed by as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:30">Luke 10:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:2">James 1:2</span>. There is a current on one side of St. Paul's Bay between a little island (Salmonetta) and Malta which makes a sand bank between the two currents. Unexpectedly the ship stuck in this sandbar.</p>
<p><b>Where two seas met </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διθαλασσον</span></span>). Used in Strabo and Dio Chrysostom for divided seas (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δισ, θαλασσα</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>They ran the vessel aground </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεκειλαν την ναυν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επικελλω</span></span>, to run a ship ashore. Only here in N.T. Here also we have the only N.T. use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ναυς</span></span> for ship (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ναω, νεω</span></span>, to swim) so common in ancient Greek. Our word navy is from this word through the Latin.</p>
<p><b>Struck </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερεισασα</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερειδω</span></span>, old verb to fix firmly. Only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Unmoveable </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασαλευτος</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαλευω</span></span> to shake. Old word. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:28">Hebrews 12:28</span>.</p>
<p><b>Began to break up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελυετο</span></span>). Inchoative imperfect passive of the old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυω</span></span>, to loosen. The prow was stuck in the sand-bar, and the stern was breaking to pieces by the opposing waves lashing on both sides. It was a critical moment. </p>
