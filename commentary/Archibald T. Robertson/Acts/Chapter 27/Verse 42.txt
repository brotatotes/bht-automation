<p><b> Counsel was to kill </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βουλη εγενετο ινα--αποκτεινωσιν</span></span>). The soldiers did not relish the idea of the escape of the prisoners. Hence there came this "counsel" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βουλη</span></span>). Regular Greek idiom for purpose (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκτεινω</span></span>, to kill). Soldiers were responsible for the lives of prisoners (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:19">Acts 12:19</span>).</p>
<p><b>Swim out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκκολυμβησας</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκκολυμβαω</span></span>, old verb to swim out and so away.</p>
<p><b>Escape </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαφυγη</span></span>). Second aorist (effective) active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαφευγω</span></span>, to make a clean (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span>) escape. </p>
