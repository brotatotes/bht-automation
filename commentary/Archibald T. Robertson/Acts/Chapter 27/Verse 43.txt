<p><b> To save Paul </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διασωσα τον Παυλον</span></span>). Effective first aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διασωζω</span></span>. And no wonder for the centurion knew now how much they all owed to Paul.</p>
<p><b>Stayed them from their purpose </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκωλευσεν αυτους του βουληματος</span></span>.) Ablative case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βουλημα</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκωλευσεν</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κωλευω</span></span>, to hinder, common verb).</p>
<p><b>And get first to land </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτους εις την γην εξιενα</span></span>). This classic verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξειμ</span></span> occurs four times in Acts (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:42">Acts 13:42</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:15">Acts 17:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:7">Acts 20:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:32">Acts 27:32</span>) and nowhere else in the N.T. It was a wise command. </p>
