<p><b> When we had sailed across </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαπλευσαντες</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαπλεω</span></span> (another compound of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλεω</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>The sea which is off Cilicia and Pamphylia </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το πελαγος το κατα την Κιλικιαν κα Παμφυλιαν</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πελαγος</span></span> is properly the high sea as here. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:6">Matthew 18:6</span> (which see) Jesus uses it of "the depth of the sea." Only these examples in the N.T. The current runs westward along the coast of Cilicia and Pamphylia and the land would protect from the wind.</p>
<p><b>We came to Myra of Lycia </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηλθαμεν εις Μυρρα της Λυκιας</span></span>). Literally, "We came down." This town was two and a half miles from the coast of Lycia. The port Andriace had a fine harbour and did a large grain business. No disciples are mentioned here nor at Lasea, Melita, Syracuse, Rhegium. </p>
