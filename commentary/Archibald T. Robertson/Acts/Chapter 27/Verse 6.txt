<p><b> Sailing for Italy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλεον εις την Ιταλιαν</span></span>). This was the opportunity for which Lysias had been looking. So he put (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεβιβασεν</span></span>, first aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμβιβαζω</span></span>, to cause to enter. Cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιβαντες</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:2">Acts 27:2</span>) prisoners and soldiers on board. This was a ship of Alexandria bound for Rome, a grain ship (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:38">Acts 27:38</span>) out of its course because of the wind. Such grain ships usually carried passengers. </p>
