<p><b> When we had sailed slowly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βραδυπλοουντες</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βραδυπλοεω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βραδυς</span></span>, slow, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλους</span></span>, voyage). Literally, "sailing slowly," not "having or had sailed slowly." Only here and in Artemidorus (sec. cent. A.D.). It may mean "tacking" before the wind. Polybius uses <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταχυπλοεω</span></span>, to sail swiftly.</p>
<p><b>Many days </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ικαναις ημεραις</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:6">Luke 7:6</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ικανος</span></span>. Literally, "in considerable days."</p>
<p><b>With difficulty </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μολις</span></span>). Used in old Greek, like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μογις</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:39">Luke 9:39</span>) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μολος</span></span>, toil (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:18">Acts 14:18</span>).</p>
<p><b>Over against Cnidus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα την Κνιδον</span></span>). "Down along Cnidus." A hundred and thirty miles from Myra, the southwest point of Asia Minor and the western coast. Here the protection of the land from the northwest wind ceased.</p>
<p><b>The wind not further suffering us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη προσεωντος ημας του ανεμου</span></span>). Genitive absolute with present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσεαω</span></span>, one of the few words still "not found elsewhere" (Thayer). Regular negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> with participles. They could not go on west as they had been doing since leaving Myra.</p>
<p><b>We sailed under the lee of Crete </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπεπλευσαμεν την Κρητην</span></span>). See under verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:4">Acts 27:4</span>. Instead of going to the right of Crete as the straight course would have been they sailed southwest with Crete to their right and got some protection against the wind there.</p>
<p><b>Over against Salmone </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα Σαλμωνην</span></span>). Off Cape Salmone, a promontory on the east of the island. </p>
