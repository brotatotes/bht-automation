<p><b> Then we knew </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοτε επεγνωμεν</span></span>). Second aorist (ingressive) active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιγινωσκω</span></span>. Then we recognized. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:39">Acts 27:39</span>.</p>
<p><b>Was called </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλειτα</span></span>). Present passive indicative retained in indirect discourse.</p>
<p><b>Melita </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μελιτη</span></span>). Not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μιλετενη</span></span> as only B reads, a clerical error, but retained in the text of Westcott and Hort because of B. Page notes that the island was Malta as is shown from the name, the location, the presence of a ship from Alexandria bound for Rome wintering there (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:11">Acts 28:11</span>), and the mention of Syracuse as the next stop after leaving (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:12">Acts 28:12</span>). </p>
