<p><b> Touching </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταχθεντες</span></span>). First aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταγω</span></span>, to go down to land, just the opposite of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανηχθημεν</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:11">Acts 28:11</span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγω</span></span>, to go up to sea.</p>
<p><b>At Syracuse </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις Συρακουσας</span></span>). The chief city of Sicily and eighty miles from Malta. Perhaps open weather and a southerly wind helped them across. Here it was that Alcibiades wrecked the power and glory of Athens. Why the ship spent three days we do not know. </p>
