<p><b> We made a circuit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιελθοντες</span></span>). Second aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιερχομα</span></span>, to go around, old verb, already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:13">Acts 19:13</span>. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:37">Hebrews 11:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:13">1 Timothy 5:13</span>. But Westcott and Hort read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιελοντες</span></span> after Aleph B (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιαιρεω</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:40">Acts 27:40</span>, though here it could only mean casting loose, for which no other authority exists. At any rate the ship had to tack to reach Rhegium and was not able to make a straight course (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενθυδρομεω</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:11">Acts 16:11</span>).</p>
<p><b>Rhegium </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ρηγιον</span></span>) is from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρηγνυμ</span></span>, to break off, the place where the land breaks off, the southern entrance to the straits of Messina.</p>
<p><b>A south wind sprang up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιγενομενου νοτου</span></span>). Genitive absolute again, and for all the world like that fatal south wind in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:13">Acts 27:13</span>, but with no bad results this time, though the weather was plainly treacherous at this early season.</p>
<p><b>On the second day </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δευτεραιο</span></span>). This is the classical use of the predicate adjective, "We second day men" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:22">Luke 24:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:39">John 11:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:5">Philippians 3:5</span> instead of the adverb (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 657).</p>
<p><b>To Puteoli </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις Ποτιολους</span></span>). It was 182 miles from Rhegium and would require 26 hours (Page). It was eight miles northwest from Neapolis (Naples) and the chief port of Rome, the regular harbour for the Alexandrian ships from Rome. Portions of the great mole are said to be still visible. </p>
