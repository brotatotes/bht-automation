<p><b> When they had examined me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανακριναντες με</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανακρινω</span></span>, the same verb used already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:8">Acts 24:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:6">Acts 25:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:26">Acts 25:26</span> of the judicial examinations by Felix and Festus.</p>
<p><b>Desired </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβουλοντο</span></span>). Imperfect middle of attempted action or picture of their real attitude. This is a correct statement as the words of both Felix and Festus show.</p>
<p><b>Because there was </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια το--υπαρχειν</span></span>). Accusative case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span> (causal use) with the articular infinitive, "Because of the being no cause of death in me" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν εμο</span></span>, in my case, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιτια</span></span>, usual word for crime or charge of crime). </p>
