<p><b> Appointed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταξαμενο</span></span>). First aorist middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τασσω</span></span>. Formal arrangement as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:16">Matthew 28:16</span> when Jesus appointed the mountain for his meeting in Galilee.</p>
<p><b>In great number </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλειονες</span></span>). Comparative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολυς</span></span>, "more than a few."</p>
<p><b>Expounded </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξετιθετο</span></span>). Imperfect middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκτιθημ</span></span>, to set forth, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:4">Acts 11:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:26">Acts 18:26</span>. He did it with detail and care and spent all day at it, "from morning till evening" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο πρω εως εσπερας</span></span>). In N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:3">Acts 4:3</span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:29">Luke 24:29</span>, though common word.</p>
<p><b>Persuading them concerning Jesus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειθων αυτους περ του Ιησου</span></span>). Conative present active participle, trying to persuade. It was only about Jesus that he could make good his claim concerning the hope of Israel (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:20">Acts 28:20</span>). It was Paul's great opportunity. So he appealed both to Moses and to the prophets for proof as it was his custom to do. </p>
