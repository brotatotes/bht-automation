<p><b> When they agreed not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασυμφωνο οντες</span></span>). Old adjective, only here in N.T., double compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμ, φωνη</span></span>), without symphony, out of harmony, dissonant, discordant. It was a triumph to gain adherents at all in such an audience.</p>
<p><b>They departed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απελυοντο</span></span>). Imperfect middle (direct) indicative, "They loosed themselves from Paul." Graphic close.</p>
<p><b>After that Paul had spoken one word </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειποντος του Παυλου ρημα εν</span></span>). Genitive absolute. One last word (like a preacher) after the all day exposition.</p>
<p><b>Well </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλως</span></span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:7">Matthew 14:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:6">Mark 7:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:9">Mark 7:9</span> (irony). Here strong indignation in the very position of the word (Page).</p>
<p><b>To your fathers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς τους πατερας υμων</span></span>). So Aleph A B instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημων</span></span> (our) like Stephen in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:52">Acts 7:52</span> whose words Paul had heard. By mentioning the Holy Spirit Paul shows (Knowling) that they are resisting God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:52">Acts 7:52</span>). </p>
