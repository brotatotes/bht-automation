<p><b> Answered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεκρινατο</span></span>). First aorist middle indicative. The people looked their amazement and Peter answered that.</p>
<p><b>Ye men of Israel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ανδρες Ισραηλειτα</span></span>). Covenant name and so conciliatory, the stock of Israel (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:5">Philippians 3:5</span>).</p>
<p><b>At this man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ τουτω</span></span>). Probably so, though it could be "at this thing."</p>
<p><b>Fasten you your eyes </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ατενιζετε</span></span>). The very verb used about Peter in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:4">Acts 3:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>On us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημιν</span></span>). Dative case, emphatic proleptical position before <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τ ατενιζετε</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>On us why do ye fasten your eyes? As though </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hως</span></span> with the participle gives the alleged reason, not always the true one.</p>
<p><b>Power </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυναμε</span></span>). Instrumental case, <i>causa effectiva</i>.</p>
<p><b>Godliness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευσεβεια</span></span>). <i>Causa meritoria</i>.</p>
<p><b>Had made </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεποιηκοσιν</span></span>). Perfect active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>To walk </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του περιπατειν</span></span>). Articular infinitive in the genitive case of result, purpose easily shading off into result (ecbatic infinitive) as here as is true also of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>. </p>
