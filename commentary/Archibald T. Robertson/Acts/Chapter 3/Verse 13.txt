<p><b> His servant Jesus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον παιδα Ιησουν</span></span>). This phrase occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+42:1">Isaiah 42:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+52:13">Isaiah 52:13</span> about the Messiah except the name "Jesus" which Peter adds, the first part of the quotation is from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+3:6">Exodus 3:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+5:30">Exodus 5:30</span>. The LXX translated the Hebrew <i>ebhedh</i> by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παις</span></span>, the servant of Jehovah being a Messianic designation. But the phrase "servant of God" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παις θεου</span></span>) is applied also to Israel (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:54">Luke 1:54</span>) and to David (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:69">Luke 1:69</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:25">Acts 4:25</span>). Paul terms himself <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δουλος θεου</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:1">Titus 1:1</span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Παις</span></span> is just child (boy or girl), and it was also used of a slave (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:6">Matthew 8:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:8">Matthew 8:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:13">Matthew 8:13</span>). But it is not here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υιος</span></span> (son) that Peter uses, but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παις</span></span>. Luke quotes Peter as using it again in this Messianic sense in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:26">Acts 3:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:27">Acts 4:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:30">Acts 4:30</span>.</p>
<p><b>Whom ye delivered up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ον υμεις μεν παρεδωκατε</span></span>). Note emphatic use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμεις</span></span> (ye). No <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span> to correspond to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεν</span></span>. First aorist active (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κ</span></span> aorist) plural indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραδιδωμ</span></span> (usual form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεδοτε</span></span>, second aorist).</p>
<p><b>When he </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινου</span></span>). Emphatic pronoun, that one, in contrast with "ye" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμεις</span></span>), genitive absolute with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κριναντος</span></span>, here the nearest word (Pilate), the latter. </p>
