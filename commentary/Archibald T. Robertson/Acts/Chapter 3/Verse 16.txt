<p><b> By faith in his name </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη πιστε του ονοματος αυτου</span></span>). Instrumental case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστε</span></span> (Aleph and B do not have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>) and objective genitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ονοματος</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>His name </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το ονομα αυτου</span></span>). Repeats the word name to make the point clear. Cf. verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:6">Acts 3:6</span> where Peter uses "the name of Jesus Christ of Nazareth" when he healed the man.</p>
<p><b>Made strong </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστερεωσεν</span></span>). Same verb used in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:7">Acts 3:7</span> (and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:5">Acts 16:5</span>). Nowhere else in the N.T. Old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στερεος</span></span>, firm, solid.</p>
<p><b>Through him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι' αυτου</span></span>). Through Jesus, the object of faith and the source of it.</p>
<p><b>Perfect soundness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ολοκληριαν</span></span>). Perfect in all its parts, complete, whole (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ολος</span></span>, whole, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κληρος</span></span>, allotment). Late word (Plutarch) once in LXX (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+1:6">Isaiah 1:6</span>) and here alone in the N.T., but adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ολοκληρος</span></span>, old and common (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:4">James 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:23">1 Thessalonians 5:23</span>). </p>
