<p><b> Ye </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hυμεις</span></span>). Emphatic position.</p>
<p><b>The covenant which God made </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της διαθηκης ης ο θεος διεθετο</span></span>). Literally, "the covenant which God covenanted." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Διαθηκη</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διεθετο</span></span> (second aorist middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαθημ</span></span>) are the same root. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:28">Matthew 26:28</span>. The covenant (agreement between two, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια, τιθημ</span></span>) was with Abraham (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+12:1-3">Genesis 12:1-3</span>) and repeated at various times (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+18:18">Genesis 18:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+22:18">Genesis 22:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+26:4">Genesis 26:4</span>, etc.). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:15-18">Hebrews 9:15-18</span> the word is used both for covenant and will. The genitive relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ης</span></span> attracted to case of the antecedent. </p>
