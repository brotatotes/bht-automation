<p><b> Unto you first </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hυμιν πρωτον</span></span>). The Jews were first in privilege and it was through the Jews that the Messiah was to come for "all the families of the earth."</p>
<p><b>His servant </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον παιδα αυτου</span></span>). As in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:13">Acts 3:13</span>, the Messiah as God's Servant.</p>
<p><b>To bless you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλογουντα υμας</span></span>). Present active participle to express purpose, blessing you (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 991). In turning away (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω αποστρεφειν</span></span>). Articular infinitive in the locative case, almost preserved in the English. </p>
