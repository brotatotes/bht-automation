<p><b> In the name </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω ονοματ</span></span>). The healing power is in that name (Page) and Peter says so. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:49">Luke 9:49</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:17">Luke 10:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:7">Acts 4:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:10">Acts 4:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:27">Acts 19:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:18">Acts 16:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>Walk </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιπατε</span></span>). Present imperative, inchoative idea, begin to walk and then go on walking. But the beggar does not budge. He knows that he cannot walk. </p>
