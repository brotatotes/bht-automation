<p><b> Be it known </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνωστον εστω</span></span>). Imperative present active third singular of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειμ</span></span>, to be, and the verbal adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνωστον</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Whom ye crucified </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ον υμεις εσταυρωσατε</span></span>). Too good a chance to miss, and so Peter boldly charges the Sanhedrin with responsibility for the death of Jesus. Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμεις</span></span> (ye) again.</p>
<p><b>Whom God raised from the dead </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ον ο θεος ηγειρεν εκ νεκρων</span></span>). Note repetition of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ον</span></span> (whom). This is God's answer to their act of crucifixion.</p>
<p><b>In him doth this man stand </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τουτω ουτος παρεστηκεν</span></span>). Rather (note play on <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτος</span></span>), "In this one (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ον, ον</span></span>) this one stands (present perfect active indicative, intransitive)." In Jesus this man stands before you whole (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υγιης</span></span>). It was a centre shot. </p>
