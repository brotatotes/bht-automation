<p><b> That it spread no further </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη επ πλειον διανεμηθη</span></span>). First aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διανεμω</span></span>, to distribute with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη</span></span>, negative purpose.</p>
<p><b>Let us threaten them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απειλησωμεθα αυτοις</span></span>). Hortatory aorist middle subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απειλεω</span></span>, old verb (note middle voice). In the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:23">1 Peter 2:23</span>.</p>
<p><b>That they speak henceforth to no man in this name </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηκετ λαλειν επ τω ονοματ τουτω μηδεν ανθρωπων</span></span>). Indirect command with the infinitive and double negative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηκετι, μηδεν</span></span>). They will not say "Jesus," but make a slur at "this name," contemptuous use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτος</span></span>, though they apparently do mention the name "Jesus" in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:18">Acts 4:18</span>. </p>
