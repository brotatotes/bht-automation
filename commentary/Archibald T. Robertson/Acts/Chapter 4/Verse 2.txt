<p><b> Being sore troubled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαπονουμενο</span></span>). Present passive participle of old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαπονεω</span></span> (perfective use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span>) to be worked up, indignant. In the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:8">Acts 16:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>Because </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια το</span></span>). The articular infinitive with two accusatives, one the object (the people), the other ("they") of general reference.</p>
<p><b>In Jesus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν Ιησου</span></span>). In the case of Jesus, an actual instance of resurrection which the Sadducees denied (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:23">Matthew 22:23</span>). This same use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> appears in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:6">1 Corinthians 4:6</span> (in us). The Sadducees were also aristocrats and political ecclesiastics who disliked popular disturbances. In particular, they resented the claim about Jesus whom they had helped crucify. </p>
