<p><b> When they had further threatened them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσαπειλησαμενο</span></span>). The "further" is in "pros" (in addition),</p>
<p><b>Finding nothing how they might punish them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηδεν ευρισκοντες το πως κολασωντα αυτους</span></span>). Note the article "to" before <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πως</span></span> (how), "the how." Aorist middle deliberative subjunctive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολασωντα</span></span> in indirect question after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πως</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολαζω</span></span>, to lop (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολος</span></span>, lopped), to curb, to prune, to correct, to punish. Old verb, in the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:9">2 Peter 2:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>Glorified God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδοξαζον τον θεον</span></span>). Imperfect active, kept on glorifying God while the Sanhedrin were threatening Peter and John. It was to laugh at the helplessness of the Sanhedrin. </p>
