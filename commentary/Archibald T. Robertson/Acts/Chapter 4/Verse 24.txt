<p><b> With one accord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοθυμαδον</span></span>). A concert of voices as already seen by the word in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:14">Acts 1:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:46">Acts 2:46</span> and later in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:12">Acts 5:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:57">Acts 7:57</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:25">Acts 15:25</span>.</p>
<p><b>O Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Δεσποτα</span></span>). Our word despot. Old word for relation of master to slaves or household servants (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:1">1 Timothy 6:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:21">2 Timothy 2:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:9">Titus 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:18">1 Peter 2:18</span>). Simeon thus addressed God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:29">Luke 2:29</span>). So in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:1">2 Peter 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jdg+1:4">Judges 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:10">Revelation 6:10</span>. See "slaves" in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:29">Acts 4:29</span>. </p>
