<p><b> The place was shaken </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσαλευθη ο τοπος</span></span>). By an earthquake most likely as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:26">Acts 16:26</span>, but none the less a token of God's presence and power (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+114:7">Psalms 114:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+2:19">Isaiah 2:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+2:21">Isaiah 2:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:26">Hebrews 12:26</span>).</p>
<p><b>Were gathered together </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ησαν συνηγμενο</span></span>). Periphrastic past perfect passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συναγω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>They spake </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελαλουν</span></span>). Imperfect active indicative, began to speak, after being filled (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επλησθησαν</span></span>, aorist passive indicative) with the Holy Spirit. Luke uses the very words of the prayer in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:29">Acts 4:29</span> to describe their conduct. </p>
