<p><b> That lacked </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδεης</span></span>). Literally, in need, old adjective, here only in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>Were </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπηρχον</span></span>). Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπαρχω</span></span>, to exist.</p>
<p><b>Sold them and brought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πωλουντες εφερον</span></span>). Present active participle and imperfect active indicative. Selling they brought from time to time, as there was occasion by reason of need. Hence the wants were kept supplied.</p>
<p><b>Laid them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετιθουν</span></span>). Imperfect active again, <i>repetition</i>, of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span>, late omega form for the usual <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετιθεσαν</span></span>. </p>
