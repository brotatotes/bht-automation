<p><b> Were wrought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγινετο</span></span>). Imperfect middle, wrought from time to time.</p>
<p><b>With one accord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοθυμαδον</span></span>). As already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:14">Acts 1:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:46">Acts 2:46</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:24">Acts 4:24</span> and later <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:57">Acts 7:57</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:6">Acts 8:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:20">Acts 12:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:25">Acts 15:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:21">Acts 18:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:29">Acts 19:29</span>, old adverb and only in Acts in the N.T. Here "all" is added. In Solomon's Porch again as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:11">Acts 3:11</span> which see. </p>
