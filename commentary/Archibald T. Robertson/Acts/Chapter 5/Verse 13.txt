<p><b> Durst </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετολμα</span></span>). Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τολμαω</span></span>, old verb, not to fear or shun through fear, boldly to take a stand. The fate of Ananias and Sapphira continued to hold many in check.</p>
<p><b>Join </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολλασθα</span></span>). Present middle infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολλαω</span></span>, old verb to cleave to like glue as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:15">Luke 15:15</span> which see. Seven times in Acts (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:26">Acts 9:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:28">Acts 10:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:34">Acts 17:34</span>). The outsiders (the rest) preferred, many of them, to remain outside for the present, especially the rulers.</p>
<p><b>Howbeit the people </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ'--ο λαος</span></span>). Probably individuals among the people, the populace as distinct from the rulers and hostile outsiders. </p>
