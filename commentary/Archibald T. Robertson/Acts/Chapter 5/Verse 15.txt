<p><b> Insomuch that </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωστε</span></span>). With the present infinitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκφερειν</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθενα</span></span>, regular Greek idiom for result.</p>
<p><b>Into the streets </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τας πλατειας</span></span>). Supply <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδους</span></span> (ways), into the broad ways.</p>
<p><b>On beds and couches </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ κλιναριων κα κραβαττων</span></span>). Little beds (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλιναρια</span></span> diminutive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλινη</span></span>) and camp beds or pallets (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:4">Mark 2:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:9">Mark 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:11">Mark 2:11</span>).</p>
<p><b>As Peter came by </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομενου Πετρου</span></span>). Genitive absolute with present middle participle.</p>
<p><b>At the least his shadow might overshadow </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καν η σκια επισκιασε</span></span>). Future active indicative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> (common with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπως</span></span> in ancient Greek) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καν</span></span> (crasis for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα εαν</span></span>=even if), even if only the shadow. The word for shadow (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκια</span></span>, like our "sky") is repeated in the verb and preserved in our "overshadow." There was, of course, no virtue or power in Peter's shadow. That was faith with superstition, of course, just as similar cases in the Gospels occur (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:20">Matthew 9:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:56">Mark 6:56</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:5">John 9:5</span>) and the use of Paul's handkerchief (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:12">Acts 19:12</span>). God honours even superstitious faith if it is real faith in him. Few people are wholly devoid of superstition. </p>
