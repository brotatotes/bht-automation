<p><b> Kept back </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενοσφισατο</span></span>). First aorist middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νοσφιζω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νοσφ</span></span>, afar, apart, and so to set apart, to separate for oneself, but only here, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:3">Acts 5:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:10">Titus 2:10</span> in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>His wife also being privy to it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνειδυιης κα της γυναικος</span></span>). Genitive absolute with second perfect participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνοιδα</span></span>, to know together with one, "his wife also knowing it together with him."</p>
<p><b>Brought a certain part </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεγκας μερος τ</span></span>). Aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φερω</span></span>, for a definite act. The praise of Joseph was too much for Ananias, but he was not willing to turn over all. He wanted praise for giving all and yet he took care of himself by keeping some. Thus he started the Ananias Club that gave a new meaning to his lovely name (God is gracious). </p>
