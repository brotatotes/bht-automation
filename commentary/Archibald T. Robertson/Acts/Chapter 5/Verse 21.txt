<p><b> About daybreak </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο τον ορθρον</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορνυμ</span></span>, to stir up, to arouse, so the dawn (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:1">Luke 24:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:2">John 8:2</span>). Old word, but in the N.T. only these three passages. "Under the dawn" or "about dawn." <i>Sub lucem</i>. The temple doors would be open for early worshippers and traffickers (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:14">John 2:14</span>).</p>
<p><b>Taught </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδιδασκον</span></span>). Imperfect active, began to teach.</p>
<p><b>The council </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το συνεδριον</span></span>). The Sanhedrin.</p>
<p><b>The senate </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την γερουσιαν</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γερων</span></span>, an old man, just as the Latin <i>senatus</i> is from <i>senex</i>, old. Like the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεροντες</span></span> in Homer and the Elder Statesmen in Japan. Apparently the senate of the people were also part of the Sanhedrin and the use of "and" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span>) is explanatory and adds this item in particular. Page thinks that this group of elders were not members of the Sanhedrin at all.</p>
<p><b>To the prison house </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το δεσμωτηριον</span></span>), another word for prison (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρησις δημοσια</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:18">Acts 5:18</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η φυλακη</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:19">Acts 5:19</span>). See also verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:22">Acts 5:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:23">Acts 5:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:25">Acts 5:25</span>. This from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεσμος</span></span>, bond, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρεω</span></span>, to keep, place where bound men are kept. </p>
