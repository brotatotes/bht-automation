<p><b> Brought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγεν</span></span>). Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγω</span></span>, was bringing (leading), slowly no doubt, and solemnly.</p>
<p><b>But without violence </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μετα βιας</span></span>). Literally, not with violence.</p>
<p><b>For they feared </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφοβουντο γαρ</span></span>). Imperfect middle, still feared, kept on fearing.</p>
<p><b>Lest they be stoned </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη λιθασθωσιν</span></span>). Negative purpose with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> (like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη</span></span>), probably with "not with violence," though possible with "they feared." They handled the apostles gently for fear of being stoned themselves by the people. First aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λιθαζω</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λιθος</span></span>, stone), old verb to pelt with stones (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:19">Acts 14:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:31-33">John 10:31-33</span>). </p>
