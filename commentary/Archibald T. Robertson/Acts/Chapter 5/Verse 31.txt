<p><b> Exalt </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υψωσεν</span></span>) In contrast to their murder of Christ as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:23">Acts 2:23</span>. Peter repeats his charges with increased boldness.</p>
<p><b>With his right hand </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη δεξια αυτου</span></span>). So instrumental case, or at his right hand (locative case), or even "to his right hand" (dative case) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:33">Acts 2:33</span>.</p>
<p><b>Prince and Saviour </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρχηγον κα σωτηρα</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:15">Acts 3:15</span>. Clearly "Prince" here.</p>
<p><b>To give </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του δουνα</span></span>). Genitive of articular infinitive (second aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>) of purpose. </p>
