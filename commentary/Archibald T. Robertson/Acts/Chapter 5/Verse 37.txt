<p><b> Of the enrolment </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της απογραφης</span></span>). Described by Josephus (<i>Ant</i>. XV. 1, 1). The same word used by Luke of the first enrolment started by Augustus B.C. 8 to 6 (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:2">Luke 2:2</span>). See the discussion on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:2">Luke 2:2</span>. This is the second enrolment in the fourteen year cycle carried on for centuries as shown by numerous dated papyri. Ramsay (<i>The Bearing of Recent Discovery on the Trustworthiness of the N.T.</i>) has produced proof from inscriptions that Quirinius was twice in Syria as Luke reports (Robertson, <i>Luke the Historian in the Light of Research</i>).</p>
<p><b>Drew away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεστησε</span></span>). Causative sense of the first aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφιστημ</span></span>, made people (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαον</span></span>, no need of "some of the") to revolt (apostatize) with him.</p>
<p><b>He also </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακεινος</span></span>, crasis for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα εκεινος</span></span>). That one, also.</p>
<p><b>Were scattered abroad </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διεσκορπισθησαν</span></span>). First aorist (effective) passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διασκορπιζω</span></span>, old verb to disperse. Used of sheep (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:27">Mark 14:27</span>), of property (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:13">Luke 15:13</span>). Aorist here after imperfect (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επειθοντο</span></span>) as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:36">Acts 5:36</span>. </p>
