<p><b> Hearing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουων</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουω</span></span>, while hearing.</p>
<p><b>Fell down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεσων</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιπτω</span></span>, fell all of a sudden while listening.</p>
<p><b>Gave up the ghost </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεψυξεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκψυχω</span></span>, late verb in LXX and Hippocrates, to breathe out, to expire. In the N.T. only here, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:10">Acts 5:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:23">Acts 12:23</span>. It is needless to blame Peter for the death of Ananias. He had brought the end upon himself. It was the judgment of God. Physically the nervous shock could have caused the collapse. </p>
