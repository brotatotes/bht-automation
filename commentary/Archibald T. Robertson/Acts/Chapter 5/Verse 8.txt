<p><b> For so much </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοσουτου</span></span>). Genitive of price. Perhaps Peter pointed to the pile of money at the feet of the apostles (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:2">Acts 5:2</span>). The use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span> in direct questions appears in Luke (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:23">Luke 13:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:49">Luke 22:49</span>) as in the LXX like the Hebrew <i>im</i> and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:6">Acts 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:2">Acts 19:2</span>, etc. </p>
