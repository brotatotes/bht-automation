<p><b> They stirred up the people </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεκινησαν τον λαον</span></span>). They shook the people together like an earthquake. First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνκινεω</span></span>, to throw into commotion. Old verb, but here only in the N.T. The elders and the scribes (Pharisees) are reached, but no word about the Sadducees. This is the first record of the hostility of the masses against the disciples (Vincent).</p>
<p><b>Came upon him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισταντες</span></span>). Second aorist (ingressive) active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφιστημ</span></span>. Rushed at him.</p>
<p><b>Seized </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνηρπασαν</span></span>). Effective aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συναρπαζω</span></span> as if they caught him after pursuit. </p>
