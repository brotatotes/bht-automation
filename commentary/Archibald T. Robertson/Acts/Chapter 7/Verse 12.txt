<p><b> That there was corn </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οντα σιτια</span></span>). Participle (present active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειμ</span></span>) in indirect discourse, after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουσας</span></span>, "heard of corn being in Egypt." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Σιτια</span></span> is diminutive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σιτος</span></span> and means grain (wheat, barley, not our maize or Indian corn), old word also for provisions, victuals, here only in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>The first time </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτον</span></span>). While Jacob himself remained in Canaan before he went down to Egypt and died there (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:15">Acts 7:15</span>). </p>
