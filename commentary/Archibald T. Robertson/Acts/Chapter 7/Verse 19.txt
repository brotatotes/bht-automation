<p><b> Dealt subtilly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατασοφισαμενος</span></span>). First aorist middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατασοφιζομα</span></span>, late compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σοφιζω</span></span>, old verb, to make wise, to become wise, then to play the sophist), perfective use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span>. In the LXX, but here only in the N.T. To use fraud, craft, deceit.</p>
<p><b>That they should cast out their babes </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του ποιειν τα βρεφη εκθετα</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Του ποιειν</span></span> (genitive of the articular present infinitive) can be either design or result. The Revised Version here takes it as purpose while the Authorized as result. In either case Pharaoh required the Israelites to expose their children to death, a possible practice done voluntarily in heathen China and by heathen in so-called Christian lands. But the Israelites fought against such an iniquity. The word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκθετα</span></span> (exposed, cast out) is a verbal adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκτιθημ</span></span>. It is an old word, but here only in the N.T. and not in the LXX.</p>
<p><b>To the end they might not live </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το μη ζωογονεισθα</span></span>). Purpose with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> and the articular infinitive (present middle). This compound verb is from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωογονος</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωος</span></span>, alive, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενω</span></span>, to bear) and is used by late writers and the LXX. It is three times in the N.T. (here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:33">Luke 17:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:13">1 Timothy 6:13</span>) in the sense to preserve alive. </p>
