<p><b> Brethren and fathers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανδρες αδελφο κα πατερες</span></span>). The spectators (brethren) and members of the Sanhedrin (fathers) as Paul in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:1">Acts 22:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Hearken </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουσατε</span></span>). First aorist (ingressive) active imperative, Give me your attention now.</p>
<p><b>The God of glory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hο θεος της δοξης</span></span>). The God characterized by glory (genitive case, genus or kind) as seen in the Shekinah, the visible radiance of God. Jesus is also called "the Glory"=the Shekinah in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:1">James 2:1</span>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+25:22">Exodus 25:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+40:34">Exodus 40:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="le+9:6">Leviticus 9:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:5">Hebrews 9:5</span>. By these words Stephen refutes the charge of blasphemy against God in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:11">Acts 6:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>Appeared </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωφθη</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:43">Luke 23:43</span>. Before there was temple or tabernacle and away over in Mesopotamia (Ur of the Chaldees, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+11:31">Genesis 11:31</span>), even before (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πριν η</span></span> with the infinitive) he dwelt in Haran (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χαρραν</span></span>, or Carrae not far from Edessa, where Crassus met death after his defeat by the Parthians B.C. 53). </p>
