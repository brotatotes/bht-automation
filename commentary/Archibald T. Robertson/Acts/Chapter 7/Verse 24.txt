<p><b> Suffer wrong </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδικουμενον</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδικηο</span></span>. By blows (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+2:11">Exodus 2:11</span>).</p>
<p><b>Avenged </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εποιησεν εκδικησιν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span>. This idiom occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:7">Luke 18:7</span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκδικησιν</span></span> (this from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκδικεω</span></span> and that from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκδικος</span></span> without right or law <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικη</span></span> and then exacting law of right out of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span> one, exacting vengeance).</p>
<p><b>Him that was oppressed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω καταπονουμενω</span></span>). Present passive articular participle in the dative case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταπονεο</span></span>, to tire down with toil, to treat roughly, common in late Greek, in the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:7">2 Peter 2:7</span> (sore distressed). The man was on the point of being overcome.</p>
<p><b>Smiting </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παταξας</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πατασσω</span></span>, in the old Greek the beat of the heart, only in the LXX and N.T. to smite a deadly blow as here like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλησσω</span></span>. </p>
