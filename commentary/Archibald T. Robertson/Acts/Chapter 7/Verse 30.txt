<p>Sentence begins with genitive absolute again.</p>
<p><b>In a flame of fire in a bush </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν φλογ πυρος βατου</span></span>). Horeb in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+3:1">Exodus 3:1</span>; but Sinai and Horeb were "probably peaks of one mountain range" (Page), Horeb "the mountain of the dried-up ground," Sinai "the mountain of the thorns." Literally, "in the flame of fire of a bush" (two genitives, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυρος</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βατου</span></span> dependent on <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φλογ</span></span>, flame). Descriptive genitives as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:15">Acts 9:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:8">2 Thessalonians 1:8</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Βατος</span></span> (bush) is the wild acacia (<i>mimosa nilotica</i>). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+3:20">Exodus 3:20</span> it is Jehovah who speaks. Hence "angel" here with Stephen is understood to be the Angel of the Presence, the Eternal Logos of the Father, the Angel of Jehovah. </p>
