<p><b> The sight </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το οραμα</span></span>). Used of visions in the N.T. as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:9">Matthew 17:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>As he drew near </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσερχομενου αυτου</span></span>). Genitive absolute with present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσερχομα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>A voice of the Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωνη κυριου</span></span>). Here the angel of Jehovah of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:30">Acts 7:30</span> is termed Jehovah himself. Jesus makes powerful use of these words in his reply to the Sadducees in defence of the doctrine of the resurrection and the future life (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:26">Mark 12:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:32">Matthew 22:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:37">Luke 20:37</span>) that God here describes himself as the God of the living.</p>
<p><b>Trembled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντρομος γενομενος</span></span>). Literally, becoming tremulous or terrified. The adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντρομος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν, τρομος</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρεμω</span></span>, to tremble, to quake) occurs in Plutarch and the LXX. In the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:29">Acts 16:29</span>.</p>
<p><b>Durst not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ ετολμα</span></span>). Imperfect active, was not daring, negative conative imperfect. </p>
