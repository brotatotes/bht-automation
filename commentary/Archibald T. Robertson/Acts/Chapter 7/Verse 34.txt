<p><b> I have surely seen </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδων ειδον</span></span>). Imitation of the Hebrew infinitive absolute, (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+3:7">Exodus 3:7</span>) "Seeing I saw" (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:14">Hebrews 6:14</span>).</p>
<p><b>The affliction </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την κακωσιν</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακοω</span></span>, to treat evilly (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακος</span></span>, evil). Old word, here only in the N.T. and from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+3:7">Exodus 3:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>Groaning </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στεναγμου</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στεναζω</span></span>, to sigh, to groan. In the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:26">Romans 8:26</span>. Root <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στεν</span></span> in our word stentorian.</p>
<p><b>I am come down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεβην</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταβαινω</span></span>, I came down.</p>
<p><b>To deliver </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξελεσθα</span></span>). Second aorist middle infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαιρεω</span></span>, to take out for myself.</p>
<p><b>I will send </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστειλω</span></span>). First aorist active subjunctive (hortatory of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστελλω</span></span>, "Let me send"). </p>
