<p><b> This Moses </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Τουτον τον Μωυσην</span></span>). Rhetorical repetition follows this description of Moses (five times, anaphora, besides the use here, six cases of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτος</span></span> here about Moses: verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:35">Acts 7:35</span> twice, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:36">Acts 7:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:37">Acts 7:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:38">Acts 7:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:40">Acts 7:40</span>). Clearly Stephen means to draw a parallel between Moses and Jesus. They in Egypt</p>
<p><b>denied </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηρνησαντο</span></span>) Moses as now you the Jews denied (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηρνησασθε</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:13">Acts 3:13</span>) Jesus. Those in Egypt scouted Moses as "ruler and judge" (verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:27">Acts 7:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:35">Acts 7:35</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρχοντα κα δικαστην</span></span>) and God "hath sent" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεσταλκεν</span></span>, perfect active indicative, state of completion) Moses "both a ruler and a deliverer" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρχοντα κα λυτρωτην</span></span>) as Jesus was to be (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:68">Luke 1:68</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:38">Luke 2:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:12">Hebrews 9:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:14">Titus 2:14</span>). "Ransomer" or "Redeemer" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυτρωτης</span></span>) is not found elsewhere, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυτρον</span></span> (ransom), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυτροω</span></span>, to ransom, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυτρωσις</span></span>, ransoming or redemption, are found often. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:31">Acts 5:31</span> Christ is termed "Prince and Saviour."</p>
<p><b>With the hand </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν χειρ</span></span>). So the correct text. The Pharisees had accused Stephen of blaspheming "against Moses and God" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:11">Acts 6:11</span>). Stephen here answers that slander by showing how Moses led the people out of Egypt in co-operation (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν</span></span>) with the hand of the Angel of Jehovah. </p>
