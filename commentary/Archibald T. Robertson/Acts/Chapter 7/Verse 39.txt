<p><b> To whom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ω</span></span>). That is Moses, this Moses.</p>
<p><b>Would not be </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ ηθελησαν γενεσθα</span></span>). Aorist active, negative aorist, were unwilling to become (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενεσθα</span></span>) obedient.</p>
<p><b>Thrust him from them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απωσαντο</span></span>). Indirect middle of the very verb used of the man (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:27">Acts 7:27</span>) who "thrust" Moses away from him.</p>
<p><b>Turned back </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστραφησαν</span></span>). Second aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρεφω</span></span>, to turn. They yearned after the fleshpots of Egypt and even the gods of Egypt. It is easy now to see why Stephen has patiently led his hearers through this story. He is getting ready for the home-thrust. </p>
