<p><b> The tabernacle of the testimony </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η σκηνη του μαρτυριου</span></span>). Probably suggested by the mention of "the tabernacle of Moloch" (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:43">Acts 7:43</span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:4">Matthew 17:4</span> for discussion of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκηνη</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκια</span></span>, shadow, root <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκα</span></span>, to cover). This first sanctuary was not the temple, but the tent in the wilderness. "Stephen passes on from the conduct of the Israelites to his other argument that God is not necessarily worshipped in a particular spot" (Page).</p>
<p><b>According to the figure </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα τον τυπον</span></span>). According to the type or pattern. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Τυπος</span></span> is from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυπτω</span></span>, to strike, to smite, and is the print of the blow (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:25">John 20:25</span>), then the figure formed by a blow or impression like our type, a model or example. Quoted from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+25:40">Exodus 25:40</span>. Common word in the old Greek.</p>
<p><b>That he had seen </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ον εωρακε</span></span>). Past perfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span>, to see (double reduplication). </p>
