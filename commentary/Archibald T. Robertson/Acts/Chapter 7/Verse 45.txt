<p><b> Which </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην</span></span>). Agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκηνην</span></span>, not with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυπον</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>In their turn </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαδεξαμενο</span></span>). First aorist middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαδεχομα</span></span>, to receive through another, to receive in sucession or in turn. Late Greek, only here in N.T. Deissmann (<i>Bible Studies</i>, p. 115) argues from a second century B.C. papyrus that <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαδοχος</span></span> means rather deputy or court official than successor.</p>
<p><b>With Joshua </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα Ιησου</span></span>). With Jesus, the Greek form of Joshua (contracted from Jehoshua, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+1:21">Matthew 1:21</span>), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:8">Hebrews 4:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>When they entered on the possession of the nations </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη κατασχεσε των εθνων</span></span>). Literally "in (or at the time of) the possession of the nations." See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:5">Acts 7:5</span> for the only other N.T. instance of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατασχεσις</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Which </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ων</span></span>). The nations, genitive by attraction to case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εθνων</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Thrust out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξωσεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξωθεω</span></span>, to push out, common verb, here, only in N.T. save some MSS. in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:39">Acts 27:39</span>. </p>
