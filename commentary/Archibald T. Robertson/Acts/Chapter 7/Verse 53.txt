<p><b> Ye who </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιτινες</span></span>). The very ones who, <i>quippe qui</i>, often in Acts when the persons are enlarged upon (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:15">Acts 8:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:35">Acts 9:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:41">Acts 10:41</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:47">Acts 10:47</span>).</p>
<p><b>As it was ordained by angels </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις διαταγας αγγελων</span></span>). About angels see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:38">Acts 7:38</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Διαταγη</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διατασσω</span></span>, to arrange, appoint) occurs in late Greek, LXX, inscriptions, papyri, Deissmann, <i>Light from the Ancient East</i>, pp. 89ff., and in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:2">Romans 13:2</span>. At (or as) the appointment of angels (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:41">Matthew 10:41</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:41">Matthew 12:41</span> for this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>And kept it not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ουκ εφυλαξατε</span></span>). Like a whipcracker these words cut to the quick. They gloried in possessing the law and openly violated it (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:23">Romans 2:23</span>). </p>
