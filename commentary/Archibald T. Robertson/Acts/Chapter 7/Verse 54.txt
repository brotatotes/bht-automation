<p><b> When they heard </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουοντες</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουω</span></span>, while hearing.</p>
<p><b>They were cut to the heart </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διεπριοντο ταις καρδιαις</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:33">Acts 5:33</span> where the same word and form (imperfect passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαπριω</span></span>) is used of the effect of Peter's speech on the Sadducees. Here Stephen had sent a saw through the hearts of the Pharisees that rasped them to the bone.</p>
<p><b>They gnashed on him with their teeth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβρυχον τους οδοντας επ' αυτον</span></span>). Imperfect (inchoative) active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βρυχω</span></span> (Attic <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βρυκω</span></span>), to bite with loud noise, to grind or gnash the teeth. Literally, They began to gnash their teeth at (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ'</span></span>) him (just like a pack of hungry, snarling wolves). Stephen knew that it meant death for him. </p>
