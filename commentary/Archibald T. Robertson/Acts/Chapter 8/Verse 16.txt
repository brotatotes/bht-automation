<p><b> He was fallen </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην επιπεπτωκος</span></span>). Periphrastic past perfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιπιπτω</span></span>, old verb. The participle is neuter here because of the grammatical gender of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα</span></span>, but the translation should be "he" (natural gender), not "it." We should not use "it" for the Holy Spirit.</p>
<p><b>Only they had been baptized </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μονον δε βαβαπτισμενο υπηρχον</span></span>). Periphrastic past perfect passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαπτιζω</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπαρχω</span></span> (see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:9">Acts 8:9</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προυπηρχον</span></span>), instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ησαν</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Into the name </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το ονομα</span></span>). Better, in the name (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:38">Acts 2:38</span>). </p>
