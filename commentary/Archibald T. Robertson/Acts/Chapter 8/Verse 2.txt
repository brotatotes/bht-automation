<p><b> Devout </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλαβεις</span></span>). Only four times in the N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:25">Luke 2:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:5">Acts 2:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:2">Acts 8:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:12">Acts 22:12</span>). Possibly some non-Christian Jews helped. The burial took place before the Christians were chiefly scattered.</p>
<p><b>Buried </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεκομισαν</span></span>). Aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνκομιζω</span></span>, old verb to bring together, to collect, to join with others in carrying, to bury (the whole funeral arrangements). Only here in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>Lamentation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοπετον</span></span>). Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοπτομα</span></span>, to beat the breast, in LXX, Plutarch, etc., only here in the N.T. </p>
