<p><b> That thou art </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σε οντα</span></span>). Participle in indirect discourse after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορω</span></span> (I see).</p>
<p><b>In the gall of bitterness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις χολην πικριας</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χολας</span></span> either from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χεω</span></span>, to pour, or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χλοη</span></span>, yellowish green, bile or gall. In the N.T. only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:34">Matthew 27:34</span> and here. In LXX in sense of wormwood as well as bile. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+29:18">Deuteronomy 29:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+32:32">Deuteronomy 32:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="la+3:15">Lamentations 3:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+16:14">Job 16:14</span>. "Gall and bitterness" in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+29:18">Deuteronomy 29:18</span>. Here the gall is described by the genitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πικριας</span></span> as consisting in "bitterness." In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:15">Hebrews 12:15</span> "a root of bitterness," a bitter root. This word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πικρια</span></span> in the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:15">Hebrews 12:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:14">Romans 3:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:31">Ephesians 4:31</span>. The "bond of iniquity" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνδεσμον αδικιας</span></span>) is from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+58:6">Isaiah 58:6</span>. Paul uses this word of peace (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:3">Ephesians 4:3</span>), of love (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:14">Colossians 3:14</span>), of the body (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:19">Colossians 2:19</span>). Peter describes Simon's offer as poison and a chain. </p>
