<p><b> Out of the water </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ του υδατος</span></span>). Not from the edge of the water, but up out of the water as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:10">Mark 1:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Caught away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηρπασεν</span></span>). Suddenly and miraculously, for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρπαζω</span></span>, like the Latin <i>rapio</i>, means to carry off. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:2">2 Corinthians 12:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:17">1 Thessalonians 4:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>Went on his way </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επορευετο</span></span>). Kept on going, imperfect active. </p>
