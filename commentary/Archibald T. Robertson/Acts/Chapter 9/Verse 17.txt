<p><b> Laying his hands on him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιθεις επ' αυτον τας χειρας</span></span>). As in the vision Saul saw (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:12">Acts 9:12</span>).</p>
<p><b>Brother Saul </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Σαουλ αδελφε</span></span>). All suspicion has vanished and Ananias takes Saul to his heart as a brother in Christ. It was a gracious word to Saul now under suspicion on both sides.</p>
<p><b>The Lord, even Jesus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο κυριοσ, Ιησους</span></span>). Undoubted use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυριος</span></span> as Lord and applied to Jesus.</p>
<p><b>Who appeared </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο οφθεις</span></span>). First aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span>, was seen as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:16">Acts 26:16</span> and with the dative also (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σο</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Thou camest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηρχου</span></span>). Imperfect indicative middle, "thou wert coming."</p>
<p><b>Be filled with the Holy Spirit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλησθεις πνευματος αγιου</span></span>). This enduement of special power he will need as an apostle (Hackett) and as promised by Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:8">Acts 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:7">Galatians 2:7</span>). </p>
