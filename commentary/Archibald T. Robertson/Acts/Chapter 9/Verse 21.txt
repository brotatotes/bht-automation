<p><b> Were amazed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξισταντο</span></span>). Imperfect middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξιστημ</span></span>. They continued to stand out of themselves in astonishment at this violent reversal in Saul the persecutor.</p>
<p><b>Made havock </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορθησας</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορθεω</span></span>, to lay waste, an old verb, but only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:13">Galatians 1:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:23">Galatians 1:23</span> by Paul, an interesting coincidence. It is the old proverb about Saul among the prophets (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1sa+10:12">1 Samuel 10:12</span>) revived with a new meaning (Furneaux).</p>
<p><b>Had come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εληλυθε</span></span>). Past perfect indicative active.</p>
<p><b>Might bring </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαγη</span></span>). Second aorist (effective) active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγω</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> (purpose).</p>
<p><b>Bound </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεδεμενους</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεω</span></span>. Interesting tenses. </p>
