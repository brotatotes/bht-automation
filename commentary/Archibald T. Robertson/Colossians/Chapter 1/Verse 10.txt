<p><b> To walk worthily of the Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιπατησα αξιως του Κυριου</span></span>). This aorist active infinitive may express purpose or result. Certainly this result is the aim of the right knowledge of God. "The end of all knowledge is conduct" (Lightfoot). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:12">1 Thessalonians 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:27">Philippians 1:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:1">Ephesians 4:1</span> for a like use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αξιως</span></span> (adverb) with the genitive.</p>
<p><b>In the knowledge of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη επιγνωσε του θεου</span></span>). Instrumental case, "by means of the full knowledge of God." This is the way for fruit-bearing and growth to come. Note both participles (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καρποφορουντες κα αυξανομενο</span></span>) together as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:6">Colossians 1:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Unto all pleasing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις πασαν αρεσκιαν</span></span>). In order to please God in all things (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:1">1 Thessalonians 4:1</span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αρεσκια</span></span> is late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρεσκευω</span></span>, to be complaisant (Polybius, Plutarch) and usually in bad sense (obsequiousness). Only here in N.T., but in good sense. It occurs in the good sense in the papyri and inscriptions. </p>
