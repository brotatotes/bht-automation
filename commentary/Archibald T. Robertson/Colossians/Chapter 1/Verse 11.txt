<p><b> Strengthened </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυναμουμενο</span></span>). Present passive participle of late verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυναμοω</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυναμις</span></span>), to empower, "empowered with all power." In LXX and papyri and modern Greek. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:34">Hebrews 11:34</span> and MSS. in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:10">Ephesians 6:10</span> (W H in margin).</p>
<p><b>According to the might of his glory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα το κρατος της δοξης αυτου</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κρατος</span></span> is old word for perfect strength (cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρατεω, κρατιλος</span></span>). In N.T. it is applied only to God. Here his might is accompanied by glory (<i>Shekinah</i>).</p>
<p><b>Unto all patience and longsuffering </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις πασαν υπομονην κα μακροθυμιαν</span></span>). See both together also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:10">James 5:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:4">2 Corinthians 6:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:6">2 Corinthians 6:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:10">2 Timothy 3:10</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hυπομονη</span></span> is remaining under (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπομενω</span></span>) difficulties without succumbing, while <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακροθυμια</span></span> is the long endurance that does not retaliate (Trench). </p>
