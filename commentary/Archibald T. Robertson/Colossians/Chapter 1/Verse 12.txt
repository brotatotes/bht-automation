<p><b> Who made us meet </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω ικανωσαντ ημας</span></span>). Or "you" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμας</span></span>). Dative case of the articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ικανοω</span></span>, late verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ικανος</span></span> and in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:6">2 Corinthians 3:6</span> (which see), "who made us fit or adequate for."</p>
<p><b>To be partakers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις μεριδα</span></span>). "For a share in." Old word for share or portion (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μερος</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:21">Acts 8:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:12">Acts 16:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:15">2 Corinthians 6:15</span> (the only other N.T. examples).</p>
<p><b>Of the inheritance </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του κληρου</span></span>). "Of the lot," "for a share of the lot." Old word. First a pebble or piece of wood used in casting lots (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:26">Acts 1:26</span>), then the allotted portion or inheritance as here (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:21">Acts 8:21</span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:7-4">Hebrews 3:7-4</span>.</p>
<p><b>In light </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω φωτ</span></span>). Taken with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεριδα</span></span> (portion) "situated in the kingdom of light" (Lightfoot). </p>
