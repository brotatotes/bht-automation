<p><b> In whom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ω</span></span>). In Christ as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:7">Ephesians 1:7</span>. This great sentence about Christ carries on by means of three relatives (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ω</span></span> <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:14">Colossians 1:14</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος</span></span> <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:15">Colossians 1:15</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος</span></span> <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:18">Colossians 1:18</span>) and repeated personal pronoun (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτος</span></span>), twice with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:15">Colossians 1:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:19">Colossians 1:19</span>), thrice with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:17">Colossians 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:18">Colossians 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:20">Colossians 1:20</span>), twice alone (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:16">Colossians 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:20">Colossians 1:20</span>).</p>
<p><b>Our redemption </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την απολυτρωσιν</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:24">Romans 3:24</span> for this great word (<i>Koine</i>), a release on payment of a ransom for slave or debtor (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:15">Hebrews 9:15</span>) as the inscriptions show (Deissmann, <i>Light, etc.</i>, p. 327).</p>
<p><b>The forgiveness of our sins </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την αφεσιν των αμαρτιων</span></span>). Accusative case in apposition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολυτρωσιν</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:7">Ephesians 1:7</span> (<b> remission </b>, sending away, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφεσις</span></span>, after the</p>
<p><b>redemption </b> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολυτρωσις</span></span>, buying back). Only here we have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτιων</span></span> (sins, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτανω</span></span>, to miss) while in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:7">Ephesians 1:7</span> we find <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραπτωματων</span></span> (slips, fallings aside, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραπιπτω</span></span>). </p>
