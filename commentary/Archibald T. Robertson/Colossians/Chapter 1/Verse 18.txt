<p><b> The head of the body </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η κεφαλη του σωματος</span></span>). Jesus is first also in the spiritual realm as he is in nature (verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:18-20">Colossians 1:18-20</span>). Paul is fond of the metaphor of the body (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωμα</span></span>) for believers of which body Christ is the head (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεφαλη</span></span>) as seen already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:3">1 Corinthians 11:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:12">1 Corinthians 12:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:27">1 Corinthians 12:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:5">Romans 12:5</span>. See further <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:24">Colossians 1:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:22">Ephesians 1:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:2">Ephesians 4:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:15">Ephesians 4:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:30">Ephesians 5:30</span>.</p>
<p><b>The church </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της εκκλησιας</span></span>) Genitive case in explanatory apposition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του σωματος</span></span>. This is the general sense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκκλησια</span></span>, not of a local body, assembly, or organization. Here the contrast is between the realm of nature (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα παντα</span></span>) in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:15-17">Colossians 1:15-17</span> and the realm of spirit or grace in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:18-20">Colossians 1:18-20</span>. A like general sense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκκλησια</span></span> occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:22">Ephesians 1:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:24-32">Ephesians 5:24-32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:23">Hebrews 12:23</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:11-22">Ephesians 2:11-22</span> Paul uses various figures for the kingdom of Christ (commonwealth <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολιτεια</span></span>, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:12">Colossians 1:12</span>, one new man <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις ενα καινον ανθρωπον</span></span>, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:15">Colossians 1:15</span>, one body <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν εν σωματ</span></span>, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:16">Colossians 1:16</span>, family of God <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικειο του θεου</span></span>, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:19">Colossians 1:19</span>, building or temple <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικοδομη</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ναος</span></span>, verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:20-22">Colossians 1:20-22</span>).</p>
<p><b>Who </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος</span></span>). Causal use of the relative, "in that he is."</p>
<p><b>The beginning </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η αρχη</span></span>). It is uncertain if the article (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η</span></span>) is genuine. It is absolute without it. Christ has priority in time and in power. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:14">Revelation 3:14</span> for his relation as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρχη</span></span> to creation and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:20">1 Corinthians 15:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:23">1 Corinthians 15:23</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαρχη</span></span> used of Christ and the resurrection and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:14">Acts 3:14</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρχηγος</span></span> used of him as the author of life and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:10">Hebrews 2:10</span> of Jesus and salvation and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:12-2">Colossians 1:12-2</span> of Jesus as the pioneer of faith.</p>
<p><b>That in all things he might have the preeminence </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα γενητα εν πασιν αυτος πρωτευων</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the second aorist middle subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span>, "that he himself in all things (material and spiritual) may come to (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενητα</span></span>, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η</span></span>, be) hold the first place" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτευων</span></span>, present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτευω</span></span>, old verb, to hold the first place, here only in the N.T.). Christ is first with Paul in time and in rank. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:5">Revelation 1:5</span> for this same use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτοτοκος</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των νεκρων</span></span> (the dead). </p>
