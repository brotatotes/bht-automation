<p><b> And you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα υμας</span></span>). Accusative case in a rather loose sentence, to be explained as the object of the infinitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραστησα</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:22">Colossians 1:22</span> (note repeated <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμας</span></span> there) or as the anticipated object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκατηλλαξεν</span></span> if that be the genuine form in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:22">Colossians 1:22</span>. It can be the accusative of general reference followed by anacoluthon. See similar idiom in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:1">Ephesians 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:12">Ephesians 2:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>Being in time past alienated </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποτε οντας απηλλοτριωμενους</span></span>). Periphrastic perfect passive participle (continuing state of alienation) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαλλοτριοω</span></span>, old word from Plato on, to estrange, to render <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλοτριος</span></span> (belonging to another), alienated from God, a vivid picture of heathenism as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:20-23">Romans 1:20-23</span>. Only other N.T. examples in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:12">Ephesians 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:18">Ephesians 4:18</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ενεμιες</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξθρους</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχθος</span></span> (hatred). Active sense here,</p>
<p><b>hostile </b> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:28">Matthew 13:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:7">Romans 8:7</span>, not passive</p>
<p><b>hateful </b> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:28">Romans 11:28</span>).</p>
<p><b>In your mind </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη διανοια</span></span>). Locative case. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Διανοια</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια, νους</span></span>), mind, intent, purpose. Old word. It is always a tragedy to see men use their minds actively against God.</p>
<p><b>In your evil works </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τοις εργοις τοις πονηροις</span></span>). Hostile purpose finds natural expression in evil deeds. </p>
