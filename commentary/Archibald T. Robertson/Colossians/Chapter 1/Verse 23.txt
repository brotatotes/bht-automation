<p><b> If so be that ye continue in the faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε γε επιμενετε τη πιστε</span></span>). Condition of the first class (determined as fulfilled), with a touch of eagerness in the use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γε</span></span> (at least). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Επ</span></span> adds to the force of the linear action of the present tense (continue and then some).</p>
<p><b>Pistei </b> is in the locative case (in faith).</p>
<p><b>Grounded </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεθεμελιωμενο</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεμελιοω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεμελιος</span></span> (adjective, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεμα</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span>, laid down as a foundation, substantive, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:11">1 Corinthians 3:11</span>). Picture of the saint as a building like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:20">Ephesians 2:20</span>.</p>
<p><b>Steadfast </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδραιο</span></span>). Old adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδρα</span></span> (seat). In N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:37">1 Corinthians 7:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:58">1 Corinthians 15:58</span>. Metaphor of seated in a chair.</p>
<p><b>Not moved away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη μετακινουμενο</span></span>). Present passive participle (with negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span>) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετακινεω</span></span>, old verb, to move away, to change location, only here in N.T. Negative statement covering the same ground.</p>
<p><b>From the hope of the gospel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο της ελπιδος του ευαγγελιου</span></span>). Ablative case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span>. The hope given by or in the gospel and there alone.</p>
<p><b>Which ye heard </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου ηκουσατε</span></span>). Genitive case of relative either by attraction or after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκουσατε</span></span>. The Colossians had in reality heard the gospel from Epaphras.</p>
<p><b>Preached </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κηρυχθεντος</span></span>). First aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κηρυσσω</span></span>, to herald, to proclaim.</p>
<p><b>In all creation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν παση κτισε</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κτισις</span></span> is the act of founding (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:20">Romans 1:20</span>) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κτιζω</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:16">Colossians 1:16</span>), then a created thing (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:25">Romans 1:25</span>), then the sum of created things as here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:14">Revelation 3:14</span>. It is hyperbole, to be sure, but Paul does not say that all men are converted, but only that the message has been heralded abroad over the Roman Empire in a wider fashion than most people imagine.</p>
<p><b>A minister </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονος</span></span>). General term for service (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια, κονις</span></span>, raising a dust by speed) and used often as here of preachers like our "minister" today, one who serves. Jesus used the verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονησα</span></span> of himself (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:45">Mark 10:45</span>). Our "deacon" is this word transliterated and given a technical meaning as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:1">Philippians 1:1</span>. </p>
