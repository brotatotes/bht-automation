<p><b> According to the dispensation of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα την οικονομιαν του θεου</span></span>). "According to the economy of God." An old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικονομεω</span></span>, to be a house steward (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικοσ, νεμω</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:2-4">Luke 16:2-4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:17">1 Corinthians 9:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:9">Ephesians 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:9">Ephesians 3:9</span>. It was by God's stewardship that Paul was made a minister of Christ.</p>
<p><b>To fulfil the word of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληρωσα τον λογον του θεου</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of purpose (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροω</span></span>), a fine phrase for a God-called preacher, to fill full or to give full scope to the Word of God. The preacher is an expert on the word of God by profession. See Paul's ideal about preaching in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:1">2 Thessalonians 3:1</span>. </p>
