<p><b> The mystery </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το μυστηριον</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:7">1 Corinthians 2:7</span> for this interesting word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυστης</span></span> (initiate), from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυεω</span></span>, to wink, to blink. The Gnostics talked much of "mysteries." Paul takes their very word (already in common use, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:11">Matthew 13:11</span>) and uses it for the gospel.</p>
<p><b>Which hath been hid </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το αποκεκρυμμενον</span></span>). Perfect passive articular participle from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκρυπτω</span></span>, old verb, to hide, to conceal from (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:7">1 Corinthians 2:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:9">Ephesians 3:9</span>).</p>
<p><b>But now it hath been manifested </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυν δε εφανερωθη</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανεροω</span></span>, to make manifest (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανερος</span></span>). The construction is suddenly changed (anacoluthon) from the participle to the finite verb. </p>
