<p><b> Whereunto </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις ο</span></span>). That is "to present every man perfect in Christ."</p>
<p><b>I labour also </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα κοπιω</span></span>). Late verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοπιαω</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοπος</span></span> (toil), to grow weary from toil (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:28">Matthew 11:28</span>), to toil on (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:16">Philippians 2:16</span>), sometimes for athletic training. In papyri.</p>
<p><b>Striving </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγωνιζομενος</span></span>). Present middle participle of common verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγωνιζομα</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγων</span></span>, contest, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:1">Colossians 2:1</span>), to contend in athletic games, to agonize, a favourite metaphor with Paul who is now a prisoner.</p>
<p><b>Working </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεργειαν</span></span>). Our word "energy." Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεργης</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν, εργον</span></span>), efficiency (at work). Play on the word here with the present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεργεω, ενεργουμενην</span></span> (energy energized) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:19">Ephesians 1:19</span>. Paul was conscious of God's "energy" at work in him "mightily" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν δυναμε</span></span>), "in power" like dynamite. </p>
