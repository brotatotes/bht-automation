<p><b> God the Father of our Lord Jesus Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω θεω πατρ του κυριου ημων Ιησου Χριστου</span></span>). Correct text without <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span> (and) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:17">Colossians 3:17</span>, though usually "the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:3">2 Corinthians 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:31">2 Corinthians 11:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:6">Romans 15:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:3">1 Peter 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:6">Revelation 1:6</span>). In verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:2">Colossians 1:2</span> we have the only instance in the opening benediction of an epistle when the name of "Jesus Christ" is not joined with "God our Father."</p>
<p><b>Always </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντοτε</span></span>). Amphibolous position between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευχαριστουμεν</span></span> (we give thanks) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσευχομενο</span></span> (praying). Can go with either. </p>
