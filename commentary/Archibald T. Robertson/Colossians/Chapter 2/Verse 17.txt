<p><b> A shadow </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκια</span></span>). Old word, opposed to substance (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωμα</span></span>, body). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:1">Hebrews 10:1</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκια</span></span> is distinguished from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εικων</span></span> (picture), but here from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωμα</span></span> (body, substance). The <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωμα</span></span> (body) casts the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκια</span></span> (shadow) and so belongs to Christ (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χριστου</span></span>, genitive case). </p>
