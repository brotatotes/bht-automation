<p><b> May be comforted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρακληθωσιν</span></span>). First aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρακαλεω</span></span> (for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:3-7">2 Corinthians 1:3-7</span>) in final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Being knit together </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνβιβασθεντες</span></span>). First aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνβιβαζω</span></span>, old verb, causal of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαινω</span></span>, to make go together, to coalesce in argument (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:10">Acts 16:10</span>), in spiritual growth (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:19">Colossians 2:19</span>), in love as here. Love is the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνδεσμος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:14">Colossians 3:14</span>) that binds all together.</p>
<p><b>Unto all riches </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις παν πλουτος</span></span>). Probably some distinction intended between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> (in love as the sphere) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> (unto as the goal).</p>
<p><b>Of the full assurance of understanding </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της πληροφοριας της συνεσεως</span></span>). On <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροφορια</span></span>, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:5">1 Thessalonians 1:5</span>. From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροφορεω</span></span> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:1">Luke 1:1</span>) and only in N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:5">1 Thessalonians 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:2">Colossians 2:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:11">Hebrews 6:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:22">Hebrews 10:22</span>), Clement of Rome (<i>Cor</i>. 42) and one papyrus example. Paul desires the full use of the intellect in grasping the great mystery of Christ and it calls for the full and balanced exercise of all one's mental powers.</p>
<p><b>That they may know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις επιγνωσιν</span></span>). "Unto full knowledge." This use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιγνωσις</span></span> (full, additional knowledge) is Paul's reply to the Gnostics with the limited and perverted <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνωσις</span></span> (knowledge).</p>
<p><b>The mystery of God, even Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του μυστηριου του θεου, Χριστου</span></span>). The MSS. differ widely here, but this is Westcott and Hort's reading. Genitive (objective) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιγνωσιν</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χριστου</span></span> in apposition. Christ is "the mystery of God," but no longer hidden, but manifested (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:26">Colossians 1:26</span>) and meant for us to know to the fulness of our capacity. </p>
