<p><b> Rooted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερριζωμενο</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ριζοω</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ριζα</span></span>, root. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:17">Ephesians 3:17</span>. Paul changes the figure from walk to growing tree.</p>
<p><b>Builded up in him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εποικοδομουμενο εν αυτω</span></span>). Present passive participle (rooted to stay so) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εποικοδομεω</span></span>, old verb, to build upon as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:10">1 Corinthians 3:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:12">1 Corinthians 3:12</span>. The metaphor is changed again to a building as continually going up (present tense).</p>
<p><b>Stablished </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βεβαιουμενο</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βεβαιοω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βεβαιος</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαινω, βαιω</span></span>), to make firm or stable.</p>
<p><b>In your faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη πιστε</span></span>). Locative case, though the instrumental case,</p>
<p><b>by your faith </b>, makes good sense also.</p>
<p><b>Even as ye were taught </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθως εδιδαχθητε</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδασκω</span></span>, an allusion to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρελαβετε</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:6">Colossians 2:6</span> and to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμαθετε</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:7">Colossians 1:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>In thanksgiving </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ευχαριστια</span></span>). Hence they had no occasion to yield to the blandishments of the Gnostic teachers. </p>
