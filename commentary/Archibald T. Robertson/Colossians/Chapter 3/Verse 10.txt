<p><b> And have put on </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ενδυσαμενο</span></span>). First aorist middle participle (in causal sense as before) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδυνω</span></span>, old and common verb (Latin <i>induo</i>, English endue) for putting on a garment. Used of putting on Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:27">Galatians 3:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:14">Romans 13:14</span>).</p>
<p><b>The new man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον νεον</span></span>). "The new (young as opposed to old <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παλαιον</span></span>) man" (though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωπον</span></span> is not here expressed, but understood from the preceding phrase). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:24">Ephesians 4:24</span> Paul has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδυσασθα τον καινον</span></span> (fresh as opposed to worn out) <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωπον</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Which is being renewed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον ανακαινουμενον</span></span>). Present passive articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανακαινοω</span></span>. Paul apparently coined this word on the analogy of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανανεομα</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ανακαινιζω</span></span> already existed (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:6">Hebrews 6:6</span>). Paul also uses <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανακαινωσις</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:2">Romans 12:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:5">Titus 3:5</span>) found nowhere before him. By this word Paul adds the meaning of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καινος</span></span> to that of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νεος</span></span> just before. It is a continual refreshment (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καινος</span></span>) of the new (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νεος</span></span>, young) man in Christ Jesus.</p>
<p><b>Unto knowledge </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις επιγνωσιν</span></span>). "Unto full (additional) knowledge," one of the keywords in this Epistle.</p>
<p><b>After the image </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατ' εικονα</span></span>). An allusion to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+1:26">Genesis 1:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+1:28">Genesis 1:28</span>. The restoration of the image of God in us is gradual and progressive (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:18">2 Corinthians 3:18</span>), but will be complete in the final result (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:29">Romans 8:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:2">1 John 3:2</span>). </p>
