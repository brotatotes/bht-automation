<p><b> Put on therefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδυσασθε ουν</span></span>). First aorist middle imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδυνω</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:10">Colossians 3:10</span>). He explains and applies (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουν</span></span> therefore) the figure of "the new man" as "the new garment."</p>
<p><b>As God's elect </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως εκλεκτο του θεου</span></span>). Same phrase in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:33">Romans 8:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:1">Titus 1:1</span>. In the Gospels a distinction exists between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλητος</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκλεκτος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:22">Matthew 24:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:24">Matthew 24:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:31">Matthew 24:31</span>), but no distinction appears in Paul's writings. Here further described as "holy and beloved" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγιο κα ηγαπημενο</span></span>). The items in the new clothing for the new man in Christ Paul now gives in contrast with what was put off (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:8">Colossians 3:8</span>). The garments include a heart of compassion (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπλαγχνα οικτιρμου</span></span>, the nobler <i>viscera</i> as the seat of emotion as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:78">Luke 1:78</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:8">Philippians 1:8</span>), kindness (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρηστοτητα</span></span>, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:22">Galatians 5:22</span>), humility (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταπεινοφροσυνην</span></span>, in the good sense as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:3">Philippians 2:3</span>), meekness (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πραυτητα</span></span>, in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:23">Galatians 5:23</span> and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:2">Ephesians 4:2</span> also with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταπεινοφροσυνη</span></span>), long-suffering (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακροθυμιαν</span></span>, in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:22">Galatians 5:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:11">Colossians 1:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:10">James 5:10</span>). </p>
