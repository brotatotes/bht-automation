<p><b> Forbearing one another </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεχομενο αλληλων</span></span>). Present middle (direct) participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεχω</span></span> with the ablative case (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλληλων</span></span>), "holding yourselves back from one another."</p>
<p><b>Forgiving each other </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριζομενο εαυτοις</span></span>). Present middle participle also of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριζομα</span></span> with the dative case of the reflexive pronoun (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαυτοις</span></span>) instead of the reciprocal just before (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλληλων</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>If any man have </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν τις εχη</span></span>). Third class condition (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Complaint </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μομφην</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεμφομα</span></span>, to blame. Only here in N.T. Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς</span></span> here with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τινα</span></span> in the sense of against for comparison with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:31">Colossians 2:31</span>.</p>
<p><b>Even as the Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθως κα ο Κυριος</span></span>). Some MSS. read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χριστος</span></span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κυριος</span></span>. But Christ's forgiveness of us is here made the reason for our forgiveness of others. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:12">Matthew 6:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:14">Matthew 6:14</span> where our forgiveness of others is made by Jesus a prerequisite to our obtaining forgiveness from God. </p>
