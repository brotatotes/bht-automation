<p><b> Provoke not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη ερεθιζετε</span></span>). Present imperative of old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερεθω</span></span>, to excite. Only twice in N.T., here in bad sense, in good sense in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:2">2 Corinthians 9:2</span> (to stimulate). Here it means to nag and as a habit (present tense).</p>
<p><b>That they be not discouraged </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη αθυμωσιν</span></span>). Negative purpose (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη</span></span>) with the present subjunctive (continued discouragement) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθυμεω</span></span>, old verb, but only here in N.T., from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθυμος</span></span> (dispirited, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυμος</span></span>, spirit or courage). One does not have to read <i>Jane Eyre</i> or <i>Oliver Twist</i> to know something of the sorrows of childhood as is witnessed by runaway children and even child suicides. </p>
