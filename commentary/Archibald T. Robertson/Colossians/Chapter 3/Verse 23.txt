<p><b> Whatsoever ye do </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο εαν ποιητε</span></span>). See same idiom in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:17">Colossians 3:17</span> except <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span> instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παν οτ</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Heartily </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ ψυχης</span></span>). From the soul and not with mere eye service. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:7">Ephesians 6:7</span> Paul adds <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετ' ευνοιας</span></span> (with good will) in explanation of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ ψυχης</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>As unto the Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως τω Κυριω</span></span>). Even when unto men. This is the highest test of worthwhile service. If it were only always true! </p>
