<p><b> Ye shall receive </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολημψεσθε</span></span>). Future middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολαμβανω</span></span>, old verb, to get back (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span>), to recover.</p>
<p><b>The recompense </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανταποδοσιν</span></span>). "The full recompense," old word, in LXX, but only here in N.T., but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανταποδομα</span></span> twice (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:12">Luke 14:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:9">Romans 11:9</span>). Given back (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span>) in return (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντ</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Ye serve the Lord Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το Κυριω Χριστω δουλευετε</span></span>). As his slaves and gladly so. Perhaps better as imperatives, keep on serving. </p>
