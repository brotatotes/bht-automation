<p><b> But now </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυν δε</span></span>). Emphatic form of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυν</span></span> in decided contrast (to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποτε</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:7">Colossians 3:7</span>) in the resurrection life of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:12">Colossians 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:1">Colossians 3:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Put ye also away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποθεσθε κα υμεις</span></span>). Second aorist middle imperative of old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποτιθημ</span></span>, to put away, lay aside like old clothes. This metaphor of clothing Paul now uses with several verbs (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποθεσθε</span></span> here, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεκδυσαμενο</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:9">Colossians 3:9</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδυσαμενο</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:10">Colossians 3:10</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδυσασθε</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:12">Colossians 3:12</span>).</p>
<p><b>All these </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα παντα</span></span>). The whole bunch of filthy rags (anger <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οργην</span></span>, wrath <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυμον</span></span>, malice <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακιαν</span></span>, railing <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλασφημιαν</span></span>, shameful speaking <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αισχρολογιαν</span></span>). See somewhat similar lists of vices in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:5">Colossians 3:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:20">Galatians 5:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:29-31">Ephesians 4:29-31</span>. These words have all been discussed except <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αισχρολογιαν</span></span>, an old word for low and obscene speech which occurs here only in the N.T. It is made from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αισχρολογος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αισχρος</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:6">1 Corinthians 11:6</span> and that from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αισχος</span></span>, disgrace). Note also the addition of "out of your mouth" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ του στοματος υμων</span></span>). The word was used for both abusive and filthy talk and Lightfoot combines both ideas as often happens. Such language should never come out of the mouth of a Christian living the new life in Christ. </p>
