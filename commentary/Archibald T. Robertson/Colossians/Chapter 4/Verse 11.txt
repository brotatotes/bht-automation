<p><b> Jesus which is called Justus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιησους ο λεγομενος Ιουστος</span></span>). Another illustration of the frequency of the name Jesus (Joshua). The surname Justus is the Latin <i>Justus</i> for the Greek <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Δικαιος</span></span> and the Hebrew <i>Zadok</i> and very common as a surname among the Jews. The name appears for two others in the N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:23">Acts 1:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:7">Acts 18:7</span>).</p>
<p><b>Who are of the circumcision </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο οντες εκ περιτομης</span></span>). Jewish Christians certainly, but not necessarily Judaizers like those so termed in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:3">Acts 11:3</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο εκ περιτομης</span></span>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+35:1">Acts 35:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+35:5">Acts 35:5</span>).</p>
<p><b>These only </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτο μονο</span></span>). "Of the circumcision" (Jews) he means.</p>
<p><b>A comfort unto me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μο παρηγορια</span></span>). Ethical dative of personal interest. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Παρηγορια</span></span> is an old word (here only in N.T.) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρηγορεω</span></span>, to make an address) and means solace, relief. A medical term. Curiously enough our word paregoric comes from it (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρηγορικος</span></span>). </p>
