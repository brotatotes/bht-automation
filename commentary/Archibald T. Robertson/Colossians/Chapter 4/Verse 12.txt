<p><b> Epaphras who is one of you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Επαφρας ο εξ υμων</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:7">Colossians 1:7</span> for previous mention of this brother who had brought Paul news from Colossae.</p>
<p><b>Always striving for you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντοτε αγωνιζομενος υπερ ημων</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:29">Colossians 1:29</span> of Paul.</p>
<p><b>That ye may stand </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα σταθητε</span></span>). Final clause, first aorist passive subjunctive (according to Aleph B) rather than the usual second aorist active subjunctives (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στητε</span></span>) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span> (according to A C D).</p>
<p><b>Fully assured </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπληροφορημενο</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροφορεω</span></span>, late compound, for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:1">Luke 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:5">Romans 14:5</span>. </p>
