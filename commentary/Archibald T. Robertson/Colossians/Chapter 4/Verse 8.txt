<p><b> I have sent </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεμψα</span></span>). Epistolary aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεμπω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:22">Ephesians 6:22</span>.</p>
<p><b>That ye may know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα γνωτε</span></span>). Second aorist (ingressive) active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span>, "that ye may come to know." This the correct text, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνω</span></span> (third singular).</p>
<p><b>Our estate </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα περ ημων</span></span>). "The things concerning us."</p>
<p><b>May comfort </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρακαλεση</span></span>). First aorist active subjunctive. Proper rendering here and not "may exhort." </p>
