<p><b> In him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αυτω</span></span>). Repeats the idea of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω Χριστω</span></span> of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:10">Ephesians 1:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>We were made a heritage </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκληρωθημεν</span></span>). First aorist passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κληροω</span></span>, an old word, to assign by lot (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κληρος</span></span>), to make a <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κληρος</span></span> or heritage. So in LXX and papyri. Only time in N.T., though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσκληροω</span></span> once also (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:4">Acts 17:4</span>).</p>
<p><b>Purpose </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προθεσιν</span></span>). Common substantive from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προτιθημ</span></span>, a setting before as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:23">Acts 11:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:13">Acts 27:13</span>. </p>
