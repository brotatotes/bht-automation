<p><b> Ye also </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα υμεις</span></span>). Ye Gentiles (now Christians), in contrast to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημας</span></span> (we) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:12">Ephesians 1:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>In whom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ω</span></span>). Repeated third time (once in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:11">Ephesians 1:11</span>, twice in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:13">Ephesians 1:13</span>), and note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:14">Ephesians 1:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>Ye were sealed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσφραγισθητε</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σφραγιζω</span></span>, old verb, to set a seal on one as a mark or stamp, sometimes the marks of ownership or of worship of deities like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στιγματα</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:17">Galatians 6:17</span>). Marked and authenticated as God's heritage as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:30">Ephesians 4:30</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:22">2 Corinthians 1:22</span> for the very use of the metaphor here applied to the Holy Spirit even with the word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρραβων</span></span> (earnest).</p>
<p><b>Spirit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευματ</span></span>). In the instrumental case. </p>
