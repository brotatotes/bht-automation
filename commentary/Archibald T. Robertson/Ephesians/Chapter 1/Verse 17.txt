<p><b> The Father of glory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο πατηρ της δοξης</span></span>). The God characterized by glory (the Shekinah, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:5">Hebrews 9:5</span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:2">Acts 7:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:8">1 Corinthians 2:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:3">2 Corinthians 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:1">James 2:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>That--may give </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα--δωιη</span></span>). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:9">Colossians 1:9</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> is preceded by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιτουμενο</span></span>, but here the sub-final use depends on the general idea asking in the sentence. The form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δωιη</span></span> is a late <i>Koine</i> optative (second aorist active) for the usual <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοιη</span></span>. It occurs also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:16">2 Thessalonians 3:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:5">Romans 15:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:16">2 Timothy 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:18">2 Timothy 1:18</span> in the text of Westcott and Hort. Here B 63 read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δω</span></span> (like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:16">John 15:16</span>) second aorist active subjunctive, the form naturally looked for after a primary tense (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παυομα</span></span>). This use of the volitive optative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> after a primary tense is rare, but not unknown in ancient Greek.</p>
<p><b>A spirit of wisdom and revelation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα σοφιας κα αποκαλυψεως</span></span>). The Revised Version does not refer this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα</span></span> to the Holy Spirit (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:1">Galatians 6:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:15">Romans 8:15</span>), but it is open to question if it is possible to obtain this wisdom and revelation apart from the Holy Spirit.</p>
<p><b>In the knowledge of him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν επιγνωσε αυτου</span></span>). In the full knowledge of Christ as in Colossians. </p>
