<p><b> Which he wrought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενηργηκεν</span></span>). Reading of A B rather than aorist <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενηργησεν</span></span>. Perfect active indicative, "which he has wrought." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hην</span></span> is cognate accusative of the relative referring to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεργειαν</span></span> (energy) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενηργηκεν</span></span> and note also <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρατους</span></span> (strength) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ισχυος</span></span> (might), three words trying to express what surpasses (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερβαλλον</span></span>) expression or comprehension.</p>
<p><b>Made him to sit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθισας</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθιζω</span></span> in causative sense as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:4">1 Corinthians 6:4</span>. Metaphorical local expression like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεξια</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τοις επουρ ανιοις</span></span>. </p>
