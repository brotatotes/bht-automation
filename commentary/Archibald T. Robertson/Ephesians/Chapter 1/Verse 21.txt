<p><b> Far above all rule </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερανω πασης αρχης</span></span>). Late compound adverbial preposition (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ, ανω</span></span>) with the ablative case. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:5">Hebrews 9:5</span>. As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:16">Colossians 1:16</span>, so here Paul claims primacy for Jesus Christ above all angels, aeons, what not. These titles all were used in the Gnostic speculations with a graduated angelic hierarchy.</p>
<p><b>World </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιων</span></span>). "Age." See this identical expression in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:32">Matthew 12:32</span> for the present time (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:4">Galatians 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:17">1 Timothy 6:17</span>) and the future life (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:7">Ephesians 2:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:35">Luke 20:35</span>). Both combined in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:30">Mark 10:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:30">Luke 18:30</span>. </p>
