<p><b> Blessed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλογητος</span></span>). Verbal of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλογεω</span></span>, common in the LXX for Hebrew <i>baruk</i> (Vulgate <i>benedictus</i>) and applied usually to God, sometimes to men (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+24:31">Genesis 24:31</span>), but in N.T. always to God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:68">Luke 1:68</span>), while <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλογημενος</span></span> (perfect passive participle) is applied to men (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:42">Luke 1:42</span>). "While <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλογημενος</span></span> points to an isolated act or acts, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλογητος</span></span> describes the intrinsic character" (Lightfoot). Instead of the usual <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευχαριστουμεν</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:3">Colossians 1:3</span>) Paul here uses <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλογητος</span></span>, elsewhere only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:3">2 Corinthians 1:3</span> in opening, though in a doxology in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:25">Romans 1:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:5">Romans 9:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:31">2 Corinthians 11:31</span>. The copula here is probably <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστιν</span></span> (is), though either <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστω</span></span> (imperative) or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειη</span></span> (optative as wish) will make sense.</p>
<p><b>The God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο θεος κα πατηρ του Κυριου ημων Ιησου Χριστου</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κα</span></span> is genuine here, though not in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:3">Colossians 1:3</span>. The one article (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span>) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεος κα πατηρ</span></span> links them together as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:3">1 Thessalonians 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:11">1 Thessalonians 3:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:13">1 Thessalonians 3:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:4">Galatians 1:4</span>. See also the one article in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:1">2 Peter 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:11">2 Peter 1:11</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:17">Ephesians 1:17</span> we have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο θεος του Κυριου ημων Ιησου Χριστου</span></span>, and the words of Jesus in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:17">John 20:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>Who hath blessed us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ευλογησας υμας</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλογεω</span></span>, the same word, antecedent action to the doxology (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλογητος</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>With </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span>). So-called instrumental use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> though</p>
<p><b>in </b> is clear.</p>
<p><b>Every spiritual blessing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παση ευλογια πνευματικη</span></span>). Third use of the root <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλογ</span></span> (verbal, verb, substantive). Paul lovingly plays with the idea. The believer is a citizen of heaven and the spiritual blessings count for most to him.</p>
<p><b>In the heavenly places in Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τοις επουρανιοις εν Χριστω</span></span>). In four other places in Eph. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:20">Ephesians 1:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:6">Ephesians 2:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:10">Ephesians 3:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:12">Ephesians 6:12</span>). This precise phrase (with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span>) occurs nowhere else in the N.T. and has a clearly local meaning in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:20">Ephesians 1:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:6">Ephesians 2:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:10">Ephesians 3:10</span>, doubtful in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:12">Ephesians 6:12</span>, but probably so here. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:6">Ephesians 2:6</span> the believer is conceived as already seated with Christ. Heaven is the real abode of the citizen of Christ's kingdom (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:20">Philippians 3:20</span>) who is a stranger on earth (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:27">Philippians 1:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:19">Ephesians 2:19</span>). The word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επουρανιος</span></span> (heavenly) occurs in various passages in the N.T. in contrast with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα επιγεια</span></span> (the earthly) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:12">John 3:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:40">1 Corinthians 15:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:48">1 Corinthians 15:48</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:49">1 Corinthians 15:49</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:10">Philippians 2:10</span>, with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πατρις</span></span> (country) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:16">Hebrews 11:16</span>, with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλησις</span></span> (calling) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:1">Hebrews 3:1</span>, with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δωρεα</span></span> (gift) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:4">Hebrews 6:4</span>, with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασιλεια</span></span> (kingdom) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:18">2 Timothy 4:18</span>. </p>
