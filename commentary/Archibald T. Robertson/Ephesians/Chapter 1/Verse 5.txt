<p><b> Having foreordained us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Προορισας ημας</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προοριζω</span></span>, late and rare compound to define or decide beforehand. Already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:28">Acts 4:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:7">1 Corinthians 2:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:29">Romans 8:29</span>. See also verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:11">Ephesians 1:11</span>. Only other N.T. example in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:11">Ephesians 1:11</span>. To be taken with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξελεξατο</span></span> either simultaneous or antecedent (causal).</p>
<p><b>Unto adoption as sons </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις υιοθεσιαν</span></span>). For this interesting word see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:5">Galatians 4:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:15">Romans 8:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:4">Romans 9:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>Unto himself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις αυτον</span></span>). Unto God.</p>
<p><b>According to the good pleasure of his will </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα την ευδοκιαν του θεληματος αυτου</span></span>). Here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευδοκιαν</span></span> means</p>
<p><b>purpose </b> like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βουλην</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:11">Ephesians 1:11</span> rather than</p>
<p><b>benevolence </b> (good pleasure). Note the preposition <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span> here for standard. </p>
