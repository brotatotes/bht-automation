<p><b> To the praise </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις επαινον</span></span>). Note the prepositions in this sentence.</p>
<p><b>Which </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ης</span></span>). Genitive case of the relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην</span></span> (cognate accusative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχαριτωσεν</span></span> (he freely bestowed), late verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριτοω</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρις</span></span>, grace), in N.T. attracted to case of antecedent <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριτος</span></span> only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:28">Luke 1:28</span>.</p>
<p><b>In the Beloved </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω ηγαπημενω</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπαω</span></span>. This phrase nowhere else in the N.T. though in the Apostolic Fathers. </p>
