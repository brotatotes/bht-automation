<p><b> According to the riches of his grace </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα το πλουτος της χαριτος αυτου</span></span>). A thoroughly Pauline phrase, riches of kindness (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:4">Romans 2:4</span>), riches of glory (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:27">Colossians 1:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:16">Ephesians 3:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:19">Philippians 4:19</span>), riches of fulness of understanding (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:7">Colossians 2:7</span>), riches of Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:8">Ephesians 3:8</span>), and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:7">Ephesians 2:7</span> "the surpassing riches of grace."</p>
<p><b>Which </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ης</span></span>). Genitive attracted again to case of antecedent <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριτος</span></span>. </p>
