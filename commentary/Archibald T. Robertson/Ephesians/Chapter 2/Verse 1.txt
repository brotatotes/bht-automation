<p><b> And you did he quicken </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα υμας</span></span>). The verb for</p>
<p><b>did he quicken </b> does not occur till verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:5">Ephesians 2:5</span> and then with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημας</span></span> (us) instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμας</span></span> (you). There is a like ellipsis or anacoluthon in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:21">Colossians 1:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:22">Colossians 1:22</span>, only there is no change from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμας</span></span> to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημας</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>When ye were dead </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οντας νεκρους</span></span>). Present active participle referring to their former state. Spiritually dead.</p>
<p><b>Trespasses and sins </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραπτωμασιν κα αμαρτιαις</span></span>). Both words (locative case) though only one in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:5">Ephesians 2:5</span>. </p>
