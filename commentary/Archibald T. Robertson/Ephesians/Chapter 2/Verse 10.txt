<p><b> Workmanship </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιημα</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span> with the ending <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-ματ</span></span> meaning result. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:20">Revelation 1:20</span>.</p>
<p><b>Created </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κτισθεντες</span></span>). First aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κτιζω</span></span>, not the original creation as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:16">Colossians 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:9">Ephesians 3:9</span>, but the moral and spiritual renewal in Christ, the new birth, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:15">Ephesians 2:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:24">Ephesians 4:24</span>.</p>
<p><b>For good works </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ εργοις αγαθοις</span></span>). Probably the true dative of purpose here with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 605). Purpose of the new creation in Christ.</p>
<p><b>Which </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οις</span></span>). Attraction of the relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> (accusative after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προητοιμασεν</span></span>) to case of the antecedent <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργοις</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Afore prepared </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προητοιμασεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προητοιμαζω</span></span>, old verb to make ready beforehand. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:23">Romans 9:23</span>. Good works by us were included in the eternal foreordination by God.</p>
<p><b>That we should walk in them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα εν αυτοις περιπατησωμεν</span></span>). Expexegetic final clause explanatory of the election to good works. </p>
