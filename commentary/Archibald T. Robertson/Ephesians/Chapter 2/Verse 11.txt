<p><b> Wherefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διο</span></span>). This conjunction applies to the Gentile Christians the arguments in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:1-10">Ephesians 2:1-10</span>.</p>
<p><b>That aforetime ye </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ ποτε υμεις</span></span>). No verb is expressed, but in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:12">Ephesians 2:12</span> Paul repeats <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ εν τω καιρω εκεινω</span></span> (for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποτε</span></span>) "that at that time" and inserts <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ητε</span></span> (ye were).</p>
<p><b>Uncircumcision </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακροβυστια</span></span>),</p>
<p><b>circumcision </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιτομης</span></span>). The abstract words are used to describe Gentiles and Jews as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:6">Galatians 5:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:27">Romans 2:27</span>.</p>
<p><b>Made by hands </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χειροποιητου</span></span>). Agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιτομης</span></span>. Verbal (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:58">Mark 14:58</span>) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χειροποιεω</span></span> like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχειροποιητος</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:11">Colossians 2:11</span>. </p>
