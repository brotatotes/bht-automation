<p><b> Separate from Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χωρις Χριστου</span></span>). Ablative case with adverbial preposition <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χωρις</span></span>, describing their former condition as heathen.</p>
<p><b>Alienated from the commonwealth of Israel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απηλλοτριωμενο της πολιτειας του Ισραηλ</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαλλοτριοω</span></span>, for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:21">Colossians 1:21</span>. Here followed by ablative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολιτειας</span></span>, old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολιτευω</span></span>, to be a citizen (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:27">Philippians 1:27</span>) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολιτης</span></span> and that from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολις</span></span> (city). Only twice in N.T., here as commonwealth (the spiritual Israel or Kingdom of God) and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:28">Acts 22:28</span> as citizenship.</p>
<p><b>Strangers from the covenants of the promise </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξενο των διαθηκων της επαγγελιας</span></span>). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξενος</span></span> (Latin <i>hospes</i>), as stranger see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:35">Matthew 25:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:38">Matthew 25:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:43">Matthew 25:43</span>, as guest-friend see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:23">Romans 16:23</span>. Here it is followed by the ablative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαθηκων</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Having no hope </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελπιδα μη εχοντες</span></span>). No hope of any kind. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:8">Galatians 4:8</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ</span></span> (strong negative) occurs with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδοτες θεον</span></span>, but here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> gives a more subjective picture (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:5">1 Thessalonians 4:5</span>).</p>
<p><b>Without God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθεο</span></span>). Old Greek word, not in LXX, only here in N.T. Atheists in the original sense of being without God and also in the sense of hostility to God from failure to worship him. See Paul's words in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:18-32">Romans 1:18-32</span>. "In the world" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω κοσμω</span></span>) goes with both phrases. It is a terrible picture that Paul gives, but a true one. </p>
