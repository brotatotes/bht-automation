<p><b> So then </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρα ουν</span></span>). Two inferential particles (accordingly therefore).</p>
<p><b>No more </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκετ</span></span>). No longer.</p>
<p><b>Sojourners </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παροικο</span></span>). Old word for dweller by (near by, but not in). So <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:6">Acts 7:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:29">Acts 7:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:11">1 Peter 2:11</span> (only other N.T. examples). Dwellers just outside the house or family of God.</p>
<p><b>Fellow-citizens </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνπολιτα</span></span>, old, but rare word, here only in N.T.), members now of the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολιτεια</span></span> of Israel (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:12">Ephesians 2:12</span>), the opposite of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξενο κα παροικο</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Of the household of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικειο του θεου</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικος</span></span> (house, household), but in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:10">Galatians 6:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:8">1 Timothy 5:8</span>. Gentiles now in the family of God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:29">Romans 8:29</span>). </p>
