<p><b> Being built upon </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εποικοδομηθεντες</span></span>). First aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εποικοδομεω</span></span>, for which double compound verb see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:10">1 Corinthians 3:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:17">1 Corinthians 2:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>The foundation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ τω θεμελιω</span></span>). Repetition of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> with the locative case. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:11">1 Corinthians 3:11</span> for this word.</p>
<p><b>Of the apostles and prophets </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον αποστολων κα προφητων</span></span>). Genitive of apposition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεμελιω</span></span>, consisting in. If one is surprised that Paul should refer so to the apostles, he being one himself, Peter does the same thing (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:2">2 Peter 3:2</span>). Paul repeats this language in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:5">Ephesians 3:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>Christ Jesus himself being the chief corner stone </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οντως ακρογωνιανιου αυτου Χριστου Ιησου</span></span>). Genitive absolute. The compound <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακρογωνιαιος</span></span> occurs only in the LXX (first in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+28:16">Isaiah 28:16</span>) and in the N.T. (here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:6">1 Peter 2:6</span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Λιθος</span></span> (stone) is understood. Jesus had spoken of himself as the stone, rejected by the Jewish builders (experts), but chosen of God as the head of the corner (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:42">Matthew 21:42</span>), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις κεφαλην γωνιας</span></span>. "The <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακρογωνιαιος</span></span> here is the primary foundation-stone at the angle of the structure by which the architect fixes a standard for the bearings of the walls and cross-walls throughout" (W. W. Lloyd). </p>
