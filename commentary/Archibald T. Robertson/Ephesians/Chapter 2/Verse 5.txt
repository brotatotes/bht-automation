<p><b> Even when we were dead </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα οντας ημας νεκρους</span></span>). Repeats the beginning of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:1">Ephesians 2:1</span>, but he changes <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμας</span></span> (you Gentiles) to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημας</span></span> (us Jews).</p>
<p><b>Quickened us together with Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεζωοποιησεν τω Χριστω</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of the double compound verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνζωοποιεω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:13">Colossians 2:13</span> which see. Associative instrumental case in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χριστω</span></span>. Literal resurrection in the case of Jesus, spiritual in our case as pictured in baptism.</p>
<p><b>By grace have ye been saved </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριτ εστε σεσωσμενο</span></span>). Instrumental case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριτ</span></span> and perfect passive periphrastic indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωζω</span></span>. Parenthetical clause interjected in the sentence. All of grace because we were dead. </p>
