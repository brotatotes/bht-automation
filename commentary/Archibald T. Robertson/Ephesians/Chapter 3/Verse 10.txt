<p><b> To the intent that </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>). Final clause.</p>
<p><b>Might be made known </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνωρισθη</span></span>). First aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνωριζω</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>. The mystery was made known to Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:3">Ephesians 3:3</span>) and now he wants it blazoned forth to all powers (Gnostic aeons or what not).</p>
<p><b>Through the church </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια της εκκλησιας</span></span>). The wonderful body of Christ described in chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:2">Ephesians 3:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>The manifold wisdom of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η πολυποικιλος σοφια του θεου</span></span>). Old and rare word, much-variegated, with many colours. Only here in N.T. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ποικιλος</span></span> (variegated) is more common (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:24">Matthew 4:24</span>). </p>
