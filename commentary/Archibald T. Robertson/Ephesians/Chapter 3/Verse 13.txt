<p><b> That ye faint not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη ενκακειν</span></span>). Object infinitive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιτουμα</span></span>. The infinitive (present active) <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενκακειν</span></span> is a late and rare word (see already <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:1">Luke 18:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:13">2 Thessalonians 3:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:1">2 Corinthians 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:16">2 Corinthians 4:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:9">Galatians 6:9</span>) and means to behave badly in, to give in to evil (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν, κακος</span></span>). Paul urges all his apostolic authority to keep the readers from giving in to evil because of his tribulations for them.</p>
<p><b>Your glory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξα υμων</span></span>). As they could see. </p>
