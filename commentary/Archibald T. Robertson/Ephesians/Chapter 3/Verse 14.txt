<p><b> I bow my knees </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καμπτω τα γονατα μου</span></span>). He now prays whether he had at first intended to do so at <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:1">Ephesians 3:1</span> or not. Calvin supposes that Paul knelt as he dictated this prayer, but this is not necessary. This was a common attitude in prayer (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:41">Luke 22:41</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:40">Acts 7:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:36">Acts 20:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:5">Acts 21:5</span>), though standing is also frequent (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:25">Mark 11:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:11">Luke 18:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:13">Luke 18:13</span>). </p>
