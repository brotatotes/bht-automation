<p><b> That he would grant you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα δω υμιν</span></span>). Sub-final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>, to give. There are really five petitions in this greatest of all Paul's prayers (one already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:16-23">Ephesians 1:16-23</span>), two by the infinitives after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα δω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κραταιωθηναι, κατοικησα</span></span>), two infinitives after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα εξισχυσητε</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλαβεσθαι, γνωνα</span></span>), and the last clause <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα πληρωθητε</span></span>. Nowhere does Paul sound such depths of spiritual emotion or rise to such heights of spiritual passion as here. The whole seems to be coloured with "the riches of His glory."</p>
<p><b>That ye may be strengthened </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κραταιωθηνα</span></span>). First aorist passive infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κραταιοω</span></span>, late and rare (LXX, N.T.) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κραταιος</span></span>, late form from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρατος</span></span> (strength). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:80">Luke 1:80</span>. Paul adds <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυναμε</span></span> (with the Spirit). Instrumental case.</p>
<p><b>In the inward man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τον εσω ανθρωπον</span></span>). Same expression in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:16">2 Corinthians 4:16</span> (in contrast with the outward <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξω</span></span>, man) and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:22">Romans 7:22</span>. </p>
