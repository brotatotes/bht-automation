<p><b> That ye may be strong </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα εξισχυσητε</span></span>). Sub-final clause again with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξισχυω</span></span>, a late and rare compound (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ, ισχυω</span></span>) to have full strength. Here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>To apprehend </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλαβεσθα</span></span>). Second aorist middle infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλαμβανω</span></span>, old and common verb, to lay hold of effectively (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα-</span></span>), here with the mind, to grasp (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:25">Acts 25:25</span>).</p>
<p><b>With all the saints </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν πασιν τοις αγιοις</span></span>). No isolated privilege. Fellowship open to all. Paul gives a rectangular (four dimension) measure of love (breadth <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλατος</span></span>, length <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηκος</span></span>, height <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υψος</span></span>, depth <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαθος</span></span>, all common enough words). </p>
