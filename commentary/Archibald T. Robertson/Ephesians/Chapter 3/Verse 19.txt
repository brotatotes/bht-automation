<p><b> And to know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνωνα τε</span></span>). Second aorist active infinitive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξισχυσητε</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Which passeth knowledge </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την υπερβαλλουσαν της γνωσεως</span></span>). Ablative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνωσεως</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερβαλλουσαν</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερβαλλω</span></span>). All the same Paul dares to scale this peak.</p>
<p><b>That ye may be filled with all the fulness of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα πληρωθητε εις παν το πληρωμα του θεου</span></span>). Final clause again (third use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> in the sentence) with first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροω</span></span> and the use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> after it. One hesitates to comment on this sublime climax in Paul's prayer, the ultimate goal for followers of Christ in harmony with the injunction in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:48">Matthew 5:48</span> to be perfect (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελειο</span></span>) as our heavenly Father is perfect. There is nothing that any one can add to these words. One can turn to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:29">Romans 8:29</span> again for our final likeness to God in Christ. </p>
