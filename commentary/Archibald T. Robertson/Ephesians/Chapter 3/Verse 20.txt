<p><b> That is able to do </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω δυναμενω ποιησα</span></span>). Dative case of the articular participle (present middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυναμα</span></span>). Paul is fully aware of the greatness of the blessings asked for, but the Doxology ascribes to God the power to do them for us.</p>
<p><b>Above all </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ παντα</span></span>). Not simply <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντα</span></span>, but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ</span></span> beyond and above all.</p>
<p><b>Exceedingly abundantly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερεκπερισσου</span></span>). Late and rare double compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ, εκ, περισσου</span></span>) adverb (LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:10">1 Thessalonians 3:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:13">1 Thessalonians 5:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:20">Ephesians 3:20</span>). It suits well Paul's effort to pile Pelion on Ossa.</p>
<p><b>That we ask </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ων αιτουμεθα</span></span>). Ablative of the relative pronoun attracted from the accusative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> to the case of the unexpressed antecedent <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτων</span></span>. Middle voice (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιτουμεθα</span></span>) "we ask for ourselves."</p>
<p><b>Or think </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η νοουμεν</span></span>). The highest aspiration is not beyond God's "power" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυναμιν</span></span>) to bestow. </p>
