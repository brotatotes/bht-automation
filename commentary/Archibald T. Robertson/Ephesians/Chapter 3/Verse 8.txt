<p><b> Unto me who am less than the least of all saints </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμο τω ελαχιστοτερω παντων αγιων</span></span>). Dative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμο</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελοθη</span></span>. The peculiar form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελαχιστοτερω</span></span> (in apposition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμο</span></span>) is a comparative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-τερος</span></span>) formed on the superlative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελαχιστος</span></span>. This sort of thing was already done in the older Greek like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσχατοτερος</span></span> in Xenophon. It became more common in the <i>Koine</i>. So the double comparative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μειζοτεραν</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:4">3 John 1:4</span>. The case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγιων</span></span> is ablative. This was not mock humility (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+15:19">Ephesians 15:19</span>), for on occasion Paul stood up for his rights as an apostle (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:5">2 Corinthians 11:5</span>).</p>
<p><b>The unsearchable riches of Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το ανεξιχνιαστον πλουτος του Χριστου</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ανεξιχνιαστος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and verbal of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξιχνιαζω</span></span>, to track out, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιχνος</span></span>, track) appears first in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+5:9">Job 5:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+9:10">Job 9:10</span>. Paul apparently got it from Job. Nowhere else in N.T. except <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:33">Romans 11:33</span>. In later Christian writers. Paul undertook to track out the untrackable in Christ. </p>
