<p><b> Wherewith ye were called </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ης εκληθητε</span></span>). Attraction of the relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ης</span></span> to the genitive of the antecedent <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλησεως</span></span> (calling) from the cognate accusative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκληθητε</span></span> (first aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλεω</span></span>, to call. For the list of virtues here see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:12">Colossians 3:12</span>. To <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεχομενο αλληλων</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:13">Colossians 3:13</span>) Paul here adds "in love" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αγαπη</span></span>), singled out in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:14">Colossians 3:14</span>. </p>
