<p><b> Is the same also </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτος εστιν</span></span>). Rather, "the one who came down (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο καταβας</span></span>, the Incarnation) is himself also the one who ascended (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αναβας</span></span>, the Ascension)."</p>
<p><b>Far above </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερανω</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:21">Ephesians 1:21</span>.</p>
<p><b>All the heavens </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντων των ουρανων</span></span>). Ablative case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερανω</span></span>. For the plural used of Christ's ascent see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:14">Hebrews 4:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:27">Hebrews 7:27</span>. Whether Paul has in mind the Jewish notion of a graded heaven like the third heaven in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:2">2 Corinthians 12:2</span> or the seven heavens idea one does not know.</p>
<p><b>That he might fill all things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα πληρωση τα παντα</span></span>). This purpose we can understand, the supremacy of Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:9">Colossians 2:9</span>). </p>
