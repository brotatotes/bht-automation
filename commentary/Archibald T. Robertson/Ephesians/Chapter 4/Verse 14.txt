<p><b> That we may be no longer children </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μηκετ ωμεν νηπιο</span></span>). Negative final clause with present subjunctive. Some Christians are quite content to remain "babes" in Christ and never cut their eye-teeth (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:11-14">Hebrews 5:11-14</span>), the victims of every charlatan who comes along.</p>
<p><b>Tossed to and fro </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλυδωνιζομενο</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλυδωνιζομα</span></span>, late verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλυδων</span></span> (wave, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:6">James 1:6</span>), to be agitated by the waves, in LXX, only here in N.T. One example in Vettius Valens.</p>
<p><b>Carried about </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιφερομενο</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιφερω</span></span>, old verb, to carry round, whirled round "by every wind (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεμω</span></span>, instrumental case) of teaching." In some it is all wind, even like a hurricane or a tornado. If not anchored by full knowledge of Christ, folks are at the mercy of these squalls.</p>
<p><b>By the sleight </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη κυβια</span></span>). "In the deceit," "in the throw of the dice" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυβια</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυβος</span></span>, cube), sometimes cheating.</p>
<p><b>In craftiness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν πανουργια</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πανουργος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παν, εργον</span></span>, any deed, every deed), cleverness, trickiness.</p>
<p><b>After the wiles of error </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς την μεθοδιαν της πλανης</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μεθοδια</span></span> is from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεθοδευω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα, οδος</span></span>) to follow after or up, to practise deceit, and occurs nowhere else (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:13">Ephesians 4:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:11">Ephesians 6:11</span>) save in late papyri in the sense of method. The word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλανης</span></span> (wandering like our "planet") adds to the evil idea in the word. Paul has covered the whole ground in this picture of Gnostic error. </p>
