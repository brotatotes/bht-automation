<p><b> Being past feeling </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απηλγηκοτες</span></span>). Perfect active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαλγεω</span></span>, old word to cease to feel pain, only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>To lasciviousness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη ασελγεια</span></span>). Unbridled lust as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:21">2 Corinthians 12:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:19">Galatians 5:19</span>.</p>
<p><b>To work all uncleanness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις εργασιαν ακαθαρσιας πασης</span></span>). Perhaps prostitution, "for a trading (or work) in all uncleanness." Certainly Corinth and Ephesus could qualify for this charge.</p>
<p><b>With greediness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν πλεονεξια</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλεονεκτης</span></span>, one who always wants more whether money or sexual indulgence as here. The two vices are often connected in the N.T. </p>
