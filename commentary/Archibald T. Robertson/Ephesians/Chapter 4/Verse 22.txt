<p><b> That ye put away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποθεσθα</span></span>). Second aorist middle infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποτιθημ</span></span> with the metaphor of putting off clothing or habits as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποθεσθε</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:8">Colossians 3:8</span> (which see) with the same addition of "the old man" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον παλαιον ανθρωπον</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:9">Colossians 3:9</span>. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναστροφην</span></span> (manner of life) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:13">Galatians 1:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Which waxeth corrupt </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον φθειρομενον</span></span>). Either present middle or passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φθειρω</span></span>, but it is a process of corruption (worse and worse). </p>
