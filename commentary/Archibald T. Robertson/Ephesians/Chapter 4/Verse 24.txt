<p><b> Put on </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδυσασθα</span></span>). First aorist middle infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδυω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-νω</span></span>), for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:10">Colossians 3:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>The new man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον καινον ανθρωπον</span></span>). "The brand-new (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:15">Ephesians 2:15</span>) man," though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον νεον</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:10">Colossians 3:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>After God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα θεον</span></span>). After the pattern God, the new birth, the new life in Christ, destined to be like God in the end (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:29">Romans 8:29</span>). </p>
