<p><b> Be ye angry and sin not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οργιζεσθε κα μη αμαρτανετε</span></span>). Permissive imperative, not a command to be angry. Prohibition against sinning as the peril in anger. Quotation from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+4:4">Psalms 4:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>Let not the sun go down upon your wrath </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ηλιος μη επιδυετω επ παροργισμω</span></span>). Danger in settled mood of anger. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Παροργισμος</span></span> (provocation), from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παροργιζω</span></span>, to exasperate to anger, occurs only in LXX and here in N.T. </p>
