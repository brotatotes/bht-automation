<p><b> Corrupt </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαπρος</span></span>). Rotten, putrid, like fruit (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:17">Matthew 7:17</span>), fish (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:48">Matthew 13:48</span>), here the opposite of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαθος</span></span> (good).</p>
<p><b>For edifying as the need may be </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς οικοδομην της χρειας</span></span>). "For the build-up of the need," "for supplying help when there is need." Let no other words come out.</p>
<p><b>That it may give </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα δω</span></span>). For this elliptical use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:33">Ephesians 5:33</span>. </p>
