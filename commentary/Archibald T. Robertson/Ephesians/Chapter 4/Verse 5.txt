 One Lord  (εις Κυριος). The Lord Jesus Christ and he alone (no series of aeons).One faith  (μια πιστις). One act of trust in Christ, the same for all (Jew or Gentile), one way of being saved.One baptism  (εν βαπτισμα). The result of baptizing (βαπτισμα), while βαπτισμος is the act. Only in the N.T. (βαπτισμος in Josephus) and ecclesiastical writers naturally. See Mark 10:38. There is only one act of baptism for all (Jews and Gentiles) who confess Christ by means of this symbol, not that they are made disciples by this one act, but merely so profess him, put Christ on publicly by this ordinance.
