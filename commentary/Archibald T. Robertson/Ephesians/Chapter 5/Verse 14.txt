<p><b> Wherefore he saith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διο λεγε</span></span>). Apparently a free adaptation of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+26:19">Isaiah 26:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+60:1">Isaiah 60:1</span>. The form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναστα</span></span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναστηθ</span></span> (second person singular imperative second aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανιστημ</span></span>) occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:7">Acts 12:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>Shall shine </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιφαυσε</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιφαυσκω</span></span>, a form occurring in Job (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+25:5">Job 25:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+31:26">Job 31:26</span>), a variation of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιφωσκω</span></span>. The last line suggests the possibility that we have here the fragment of an early Christian hymn like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:16">1 Timothy 3:16</span>. </p>
