<p><b> Be not drunken with wine </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη μεθυσκεσθε οινω</span></span>). Present passive imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεθυσκω</span></span>, old verb to intoxicate. Forbidden as a habit and to stop it also if guilty. Instrumental case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οινω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Riot </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασωτια</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασωτος</span></span> (adverb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασωτως</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:13">Luke 15:13</span>), in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:6">Titus 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:4">1 Peter 4:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>But be filled with the Spirit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα πληρουσθε εν πνευματ</span></span>). In contrast to a state of intoxication with wine. </p>
