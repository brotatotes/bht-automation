<p><b> That he might present </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα παραστηση</span></span>). Final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παριστημ</span></span> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:22">Colossians 1:22</span> for parallel) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:2">2 Corinthians 11:2</span> of presenting the bride to the bridegroom. Note both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτος</span></span> (himself) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαυτω</span></span> (to himself).</p>
<p><b>Glorious </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδοξον</span></span>). Used of splendid clothing in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:25">Luke 7:25</span>.</p>
<p><b>Spot </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπιλος</span></span>). Late word, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:13">2 Peter 2:13</span>, but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπιλοω</span></span>, to defile in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:6">James 3:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jdg+1:23">Judges 1:23</span>.</p>
<p><b>Wrinkle </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρυτιδα</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρυω</span></span>, to contract, only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>But that it should be holy and without blemish </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ' ινα η αγια κα αμωμος</span></span>). Christ's goal for the church, his bride and his body, both negative purity and positive. </p>
