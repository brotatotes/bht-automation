<p><b> Nourisheth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκτρεφε</span></span>). Old compound with perfective sense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span> (to nourish up to maturity and on). In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:4">Ephesians 6:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>Cherisheth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θαλπε</span></span>). Late and rare word, once in a marriage contract in a papyrus. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:7">1 Thessalonians 2:7</span>. Primarily it means to warm (Latin <i>foveo</i>), then to foster with tender care as here.</p>
<p><b>Even as Christ also </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθως κα ο Χριστος</span></span>). Relative (correlative) adverb pointing back to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτως</span></span> at the beginning of the sentence (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:28">Ephesians 5:28</span>) and repeating the statement in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:25">Ephesians 5:25</span>. </p>
