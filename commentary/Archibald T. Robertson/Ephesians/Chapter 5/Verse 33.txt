<p><b> Nevertheless </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλην</span></span>). "Howbeit," not to dwell unduly (Abbott) on the matter of Christ and the church.</p>
<p><b>Do ye also severally love </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα υμεις ο καθ' ενα εκαστος αγαπατω</span></span>). An unusual idiom. The verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπατω</span></span> (present active imperative) agrees with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκαστος</span></span> and so is third singular instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπατε</span></span> (second plural) like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμεις</span></span>. The use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο καθ' ενα</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμεις</span></span> = " ye one by one " and then <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκαστος</span></span> takes up (individualizes) the "one" in partitive apposition and in the third person.</p>
<p><b>Let the wife see that she fear </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η γυνη ινα φοβητα</span></span>). There is no verb in the Greek for "let see" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλεπετω</span></span>). For this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> with the subjunctive as a practical imperative without a principal verb (an elliptical imperative) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:23">Mark 5:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:32">Matthew 20:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:29">1 Corinthians 7:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:7">2 Corinthians 8:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:29">Ephesians 4:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:33">Ephesians 5:33</span> (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 994). "Fear" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φοβητα</span></span>, present middle subjunctive) here is "reverence." </p>
