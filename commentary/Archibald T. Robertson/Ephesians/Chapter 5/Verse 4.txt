<p><b> Filthiness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αισχροτης</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αισχρος</span></span> (base), here alone in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Foolish talking </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μωρολογια</span></span>). Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μωρολογος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μωροσ, λογος</span></span>), only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Jesting </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευτραπελια</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευτραπελος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ, τρεπω</span></span>, to turn) nimbleness of wit, quickness in making repartee (so in Plato and Plutarch), but in low sense as here ribaldry, scurrility, only here in N.T. All of these disapproved vices are <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαξ λεγομενα</span></span> in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>Which are not befitting </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α ουκ ανηκεν</span></span>). Same idiom (imperfect with word of propriety about the present) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:18">Colossians 3:18</span>. Late MSS. read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα ουκ ανηκοντα</span></span> like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα μη καθηκοντα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:28">Romans 1:28</span>. </p>
