<p><b> Ye know of a surety </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστε γινωσκοντες</span></span>). The correct text has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστε</span></span>, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστε</span></span>. It is the same form for present indicative (second person plural) and imperative, probably indicative here, "ye know." But why <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκοντες</span></span> added? Probably, "ye know recognizing by your own experience."</p>
<p><b>No </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πασ--ου</span></span>). Common idiom in the N.T. like the Hebrew= <i>oudeis</i> (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 732).</p>
<p><b>Covetous man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλεονεκτησ, πλεον εχω</span></span>). Old word, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:10">1 Corinthians 5:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:10">1 Corinthians 6:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Which is </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο εστιν</span></span>). So Aleph B. A D K L have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος</span></span> (who), but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span> is right. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:14">Colossians 3:14</span> for this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span> (which thing is). On <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδωλολατρης</span></span> (idolater) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:10">1 Corinthians 5:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>In the Kingdom of Christ and God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη βασιλεια του Χριστου κα θεου</span></span>). Certainly the same kingdom and Paul may here mean to affirm the deity of Christ by the use of the one article with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χριστου κα θεου</span></span>. But Sharp's rule cannot be insisted on here because <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεος</span></span> is often definite without the article like a proper name. Paul did teach the deity of Christ and may do it here. </p>
