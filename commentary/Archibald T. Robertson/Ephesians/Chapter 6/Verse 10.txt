<p><b> Finally </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του λοιπου</span></span>). Genitive case, "in respect of the rest," like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:17">Galatians 6:17</span>. D G K L P have the accusative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το λοιπον</span></span> (as for the rest) like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:1">2 Thessalonians 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:1">Philippians 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:8">Philippians 4:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>Be strong in the Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδυναμουσθε εν κυριω</span></span>). A late word in LXX and N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:22">Acts 9:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:20">Romans 4:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:13">Philippians 4:13</span>), present passive imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδυναμοω</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυναμις</span></span>, to empower. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:10">Ephesians 1:10</span> for "in the strength of his might." Not a hendiadys. </p>
