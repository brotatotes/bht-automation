<p><b> Take up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναλαβετε</span></span>). Second aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναλαμβανω</span></span>, old word and used (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναλαβων</span></span>) of "picking up" Mark in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:11">2 Timothy 4:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>That ye may be able to withstand </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα δυνηθητε αντιστηνα</span></span>). Final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυναμα</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντιστηνα</span></span> (second aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθιστημ</span></span>, to stand face to face, against).</p>
<p><b>And having done all to stand </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα απαντα κατεργασα μενο στηνα</span></span>). After the fight (wrestle) is over to stand (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στηνα</span></span>) as victor in the contest. Effective aorist here. </p>
