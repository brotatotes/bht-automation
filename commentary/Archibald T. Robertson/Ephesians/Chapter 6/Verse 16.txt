<p><b> Taking up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναλαβοντες</span></span>). See verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:13">Ephesians 6:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>The shield of faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον θυρεον της πιστεως</span></span>). Late word in this sense a large stone against the door in Homer, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυρα</span></span>, door, large and oblong (Latin <i>scutum</i>), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασπις</span></span> being smaller and circular, only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>To quench </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σβεσα</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σβεννυμ</span></span>, old word, to extinguish (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:20">Matthew 12:20</span>).</p>
<p><b>All the fiery darts </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντα τα βελη τα πεπυρωμενα</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Βελος</span></span> is an old word for missile, dart (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαλλω</span></span>, to throw), only here in N.T. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πεπυρωμενα</span></span> is perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυροω</span></span>, old verb, to set on fire, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυρ</span></span> (fire). These darts were sometimes ablaze in order to set fire to the enemies' clothing or camp or homes just as the American Indians used to shoot poisoned arrows. </p>
