<p><b> The helmet of salvation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την περικεφαλαιαν του σωτηριου</span></span>). Late word (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περι, κεφαλη</span></span>, head, around the head), in Polybius, LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:8">1 Thessalonians 5:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:17">Ephesians 6:17</span> alone in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Which is the word of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο εστιν το ρημα του θεου</span></span>). Explanatory relative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span>) referring to the sword (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαχαιραν</span></span>). The sword given by the Spirit to be wielded as offensive weapon (the others defensive) by the Christian is the word of God. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:12">Hebrews 4:12</span> where the word of God is called "sharper than any two-edged sword." </p>
