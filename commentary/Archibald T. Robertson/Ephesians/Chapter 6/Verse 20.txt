<p><b> For which I am an ambassador in chains </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ ου πρεσβευω εν αλυσε</span></span>). "For which mystery" of the gospel (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:19">Ephesians 6:19</span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πρεσβευω</span></span> is an old word for ambassador (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρεσβυς</span></span>, an old man) in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:20">2 Corinthians 5:20</span>. Paul is now an old man (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρεσβυτης</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:9">Philemon 1:9</span>) and feels the dignity of his position as Christ's ambassador though "in a chain" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αλυσε</span></span>, old word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλυσις</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυω</span></span>, to loosen). Paul will wear a chain at the close of his life in Rome (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:16">2 Timothy 1:16</span>).</p>
<p><b>In it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αυτω</span></span>). In the mystery of the gospel. This is probably a second purpose (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>), the first for utterance (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα δοθη</span></span>), this for boldness (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα παρρησιασωμα</span></span>, first aorist middle subjunctive, old word to speak out boldly). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:2">1 Thessalonians 2:2</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+4:4">Colossians 4:4</span> for "as I ought." </p>
