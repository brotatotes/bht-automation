<p><b> That it may be well with thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα ευ σο γενητα</span></span>). From <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+20:12">Exodus 20:12</span>, "that it may happen to thee well."</p>
<p><b>And thou mayest live long on the earth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα εση μακροχρονιος επ της γης</span></span>). Here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εση</span></span> (second person singular future middle) takes the place of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενη</span></span> in the LXX (second person singular second aorist middle subjunctive). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μακροχρονιος</span></span> is a late and rare compound adjective, here only in N.T. (from LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+20:12">Exodus 20:12</span>). </p>
