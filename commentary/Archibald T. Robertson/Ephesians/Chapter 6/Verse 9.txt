<p><b> And forbear threatening </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανιεντες την απειλην</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανιημ</span></span>, old verb, to loosen up, to relax. "Letting up on threatening." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Απειλη</span></span> is old word for threat, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:29">Acts 4:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:1">Acts 9:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Both their Master and yours </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα αυτων κα υμων ο κυριος</span></span>). He says to "the lords" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο κυριο</span></span>) of the slaves. Paul is not afraid of capital nor of labour.</p>
<p><b>With him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρ' αυτω</span></span>). "By the side of him (God)." </p>
