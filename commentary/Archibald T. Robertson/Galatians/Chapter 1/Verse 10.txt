<p><b> Am I persuading? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειθω?</span></span>). Conative present, trying to persuade like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζητω αρεσκειν</span></span> (seeking to please) where the effort is stated plainly. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:11">2 Corinthians 5:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>I should not be </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ αν ημην</span></span>). Conclusion of second class condition, determined as unfulfilled. Regular construction here (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span> and imperfect indicative in the condition <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηρεσκον, ουκ αν</span></span> and imperfect in the conclusion). About pleasing men see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:4">1 Thessalonians 2:4</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:22">Colossians 3:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+6:6">Colossians 6:6</span> Paul uses the word "men-pleasers" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωπαρεσκο</span></span>). </p>
