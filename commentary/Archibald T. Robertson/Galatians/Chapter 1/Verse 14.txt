<p><b> I advanced </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προεκοπτον</span></span>). Imperfect active again of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προκοπτω</span></span>, old verb, to cut forward (as in a forest), to blaze a way, to go ahead. In N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:12">Romans 13:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:16">2 Timothy 2:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:9">2 Timothy 3:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:13">2 Timothy 3:13</span>. Paul was a brilliant pupil under Gamaliel. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:4-6">Philippians 3:4-6</span>. He was in the lead of the persecution also.</p>
<p><b>Beyond many of mine own age </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ πολλους συνηλικιωτας</span></span>). Later compound form for the Attic <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηλικιωτης</span></span> which occurs in Dion Hal. and inscriptions (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν</span></span>, with, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηλικια</span></span>, age). Paul modestly claims that he went "beyond" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ</span></span>) his fellow-students in his progress in Judaism.</p>
<p><b>More exceedingly zealous </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περισσοτερως ζηλοτης</span></span>). Literally, "more exceedingly a zealot." See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:13">Acts 1:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:20">Acts 21:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:12">1 Corinthians 14:12</span>. Like Simon Zelotes.</p>
<p><b>For the traditions of my fathers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των πατρικων μου παραδοσεων</span></span>). Objective genitive after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζηλοτης</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πατρικων</span></span> only here in N.T., though old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πατηρ</span></span> (father), paternal, descending from one's father. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πατρωιος</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:3">Acts 22:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:14">Acts 22:14</span>. Tradition (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραδοσις</span></span>) played a large part in the teaching and life of the Pharisees (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:1-23">Mark 7:1-23</span>). Paul now taught the Christian tradition (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:15">2 Thessalonians 2:15</span>). </p>
