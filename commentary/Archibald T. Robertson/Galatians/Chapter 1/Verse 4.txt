<p><b> For our sins </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ των αμαρτιων</span></span>). Some MSS. have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ</span></span> (concerning). In the <i>Koine</i> this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ</span></span> as like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ</span></span> has come to be common. He refers to the death of Christ (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:3">1 Corinthians 15:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:20">Galatians 2:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:6">Romans 5:6</span>). As a rule <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ</span></span> occurs of things, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ</span></span> of persons.</p>
<p><b>Deliver </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξελητα</span></span>). Second aorist middle subjunctive (final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπως</span></span>) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαιρεω</span></span>, old verb to pluck out, to rescue (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:27">Acts 23:27</span>). "Strikes the keynote of the epistle. The gospel is a rescue, an emancipation from a state of bondage" (Lightfoot).</p>
<p><b>Out of this present evil world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ του αιωνος του ενεστωτος πονηρου</span></span>). Literally, "out of the age the existing one being evil." The predicate position of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πονηρου</span></span> calls emphatic attention to it. Each word here is of interest and has been already discussed. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:22">Matthew 13:22</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιων</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:23">Matthew 6:23</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πονηρος</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ενεστωτος</span></span> is genitive masculine singular of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεστως</span></span> second perfect (intransitive) participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενιστημ</span></span> for which see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:12">2 Thessalonians 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:22">1 Corinthians 3:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:26">1 Corinthians 7:26</span>. It is present as related to future (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:38">Romans 8:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:9">Hebrews 9:9</span>).</p>
<p><b>According to the will of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα το θελημα του θεου</span></span>). Not according to any merit in us. </p>
