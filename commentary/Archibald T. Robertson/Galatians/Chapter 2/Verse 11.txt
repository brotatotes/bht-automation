<p><b> I resisted him to the face </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα προσωπον αυτω αντεστην</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative (intransitive) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθιστημ</span></span>. "I stood against him face to face." In Jerusalem Paul faced Peter as his equal in rank and sphere of work. In Antioch he looked him in the eye as his superior in character and courage.</p>
<p><b>Because he stood condemned </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ κατεγνωσμενος ην</span></span>). Periphrastic past perfect passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταγινοσκω</span></span>, old verb to know against, to find fault with. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:20">1 John 3:20</span>. </p>
