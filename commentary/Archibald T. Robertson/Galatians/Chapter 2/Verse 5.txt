<p><b> No, not for an hour </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδε προς ωραν</span></span>). Pointed denial that he and Barnabas yielded at all "in the way of subjection" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη υποταγη</span></span>, in the subjection demanded of them). The compromisers pleaded for the circumcision of Titus "because of the false brethren" in order to have peace. The old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εικω</span></span>, to yield, occurs here alone in the N.T. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:13">2 Corinthians 9:13</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποταγη</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>The truth of the gospel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η αληθεια του ευαγγελιου</span></span>). It was a grave crisis to call for such language. The whole problem of Gentile Christianity was involved in the case of Titus, whether Christianity was to be merely a modified brand of legalistic Judaism or a spiritual religion, the true Judaism (the children of Abraham by faith). The case of Timothy later was utterly different, for he had a Jewish mother and a Greek father. Titus was pure Greek. </p>
