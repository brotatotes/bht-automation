<p><b> After the manner of men </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα ανθρωπον</span></span>). After the custom and practice of men, an illustration from life.</p>
<p><b>Though it be but a man's covenant, yet when it hath been confirmed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομως ανθρωπου κεκυρωμενην διαθηκην</span></span>). Literally, "Yet a man's covenant ratified." On <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Διαθηκη</span></span> as both covenant and will see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:28">Matthew 26:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:25">1 Corinthians 11:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:6">2 Corinthians 3:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:16">Hebrews 9:16</span>. On <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυροω</span></span>, to ratify, to make valid, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:8">2 Corinthians 2:8</span>. Perfect passive participle here, state of completion, authoritative confirmation.</p>
<p><b>Maketh it void </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθετε</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:21">Galatians 2:21</span> for this verb. Both parties can by agreement cancel a contract, but not otherwise.</p>
<p><b>Addeth thereto </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιδιατασσετα</span></span>). Present middle indicative of the double compound verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιδιατασσομα</span></span>, a word found nowhere else as yet. But inscriptions use <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διατασσομαι, διαταξισ, διαταγη, διαταγμα</span></span> with the specialized meaning to "determine by testamentary disposition" (Deissmann, <i>Light from the Ancient East</i>, p. 90). It was unlawful to add (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>) fresh clauses or specifications (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαταξεις</span></span>). </p>
