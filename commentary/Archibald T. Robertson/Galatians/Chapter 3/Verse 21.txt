<p><b> Against the promises </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα των επαγγελιων</span></span>). A pertinent question again. Far from it (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη γενοιτο</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Which could make alive </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο δυναμενος ζωοποιησα</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωοποιεω</span></span>, late compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωος</span></span>, alive, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span>, to make) verb for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:22">1 Corinthians 15:22</span>. Spiritual life, he means, here and hereafter.</p>
<p><b>Verily </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οντως</span></span>). "Really" (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:32">Mark 11:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:34">Luke 24:34</span>). Condition and conclusion (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν ην</span></span>) of second class, determined as unfulfilled. He had already said that Christ died to no purpose in that case (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:21">Galatians 2:21</span>). </p>
