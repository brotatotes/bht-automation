<p><b> Hath shut up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεκλεισεν</span></span>). Did shut together. First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνκλειω</span></span>, old verb to shut together, on all sides, completely as a shoal of fish in a net (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:6">Luke 5:6</span>). So verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:23">Galatians 3:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:32">Romans 11:32</span>.</p>
<p><b>Under sin </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο αμαρτιαν</span></span>). See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο καταραν</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:10">Galatians 3:10</span>. As if the lid closed in on us over a massive chest that we could not open or as prisoners in a dungeon. He uses <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα παντα</span></span> (the all things), the totality of everything. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:10-19">Romans 3:10-19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:32">Romans 11:32</span>.</p>
<p><b>That </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>). God's purpose, personifying scripture again.</p>
<p><b>Might be given </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοθη</span></span>). First aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>. </p>
