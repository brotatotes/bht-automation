<p><b> Before faith came </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προ του ελθειν την πιστιν</span></span>). "Before the coming (second aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομα</span></span>, definite event) as to the Faith" (note article, meaning the faith in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:22">Galatians 3:22</span> made possible by the historic coming of Christ the Redeemer), the faith in Christ as Saviour (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:22">Galatians 3:22</span>).</p>
<p><b>We were kept in ward under the law </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ νομον εφρουρουμεθα</span></span>). Imperfect passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φρουρεω</span></span>, to guard (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φρουρος</span></span>, a guard). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:24">Acts 9:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:32">2 Corinthians 11:32</span>. It was a long progressive imprisonment.</p>
<p><b>Unto the faith which should afterwards be revealed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις την μελλουσαν πιστιν αποκαλυφθηνα</span></span>). "Unto the faith (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:22">Galatians 3:22</span> again) about to be revealed." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μελλω</span></span> and the first aorist passive infinitive (regular idiom). </p>
