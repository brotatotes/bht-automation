<p><b> Supplieth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιχορηγων</span></span>). It is God. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:10">2 Corinthians 9:10</span> for this present active participle. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:19">Philippians 1:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:5">2 Peter 1:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>Worketh miracles </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεργων δυναμεις</span></span>). On the word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεργεω</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:13">1 Thessalonians 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:6">1 Corinthians 12:6</span>. It is a great word for God's activities (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:13">Philippians 2:13</span>). "In you" (Lightfoot) is preferable to "among you" for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν υμιν</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:10">1 Corinthians 13:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:2">Matthew 14:2</span>). The principal verb for "doeth he it" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιε</span></span>) is not expressed. Paul repeats the contrast in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:2">Galatians 3:2</span> about "works of the law" and "the hearing of faith." </p>
