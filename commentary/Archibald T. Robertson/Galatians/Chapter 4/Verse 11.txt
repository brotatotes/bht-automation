<p><b> I am afraid of you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φοβουμα υμας</span></span>). He shudders to think of it.</p>
<p><b>Lest by any means I have bestowed labour upon you in vain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη πως εικη κεκοπιακα εις υμας</span></span>). Usual construction after a verb of fearing about what has actually happened (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη πως</span></span> and the perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοπιαω</span></span>, to toil wearily). A fear about the future would be expressed by the subjunctive. Paul fears that the worst has happened. </p>
