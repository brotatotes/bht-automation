<p><b> Your enemy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχθρος υμων</span></span>). Active sense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχθρος</span></span>, hater with objective genitive. They looked on Paul now as an enemy to them. So the Pharisees and Judaizers generally now regarded him.</p>
<p><b>Because I tell you the truth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αληθευων υμιν</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αληθευω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αληθης</span></span>, true. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:15">Ephesians 4:15</span>. "Speaking the truth." It is always a risky business to speak the truth, the whole truth. It may hit and hurt. </p>
