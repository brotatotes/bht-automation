<p><b> They zealously seek you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζηλουσιν υμας</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ζηλοω</span></span> is an old and a good word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζηλος</span></span> (zeal, jealousy), but one can pay court with good motives or evil. So here in contrast with Paul's plain speech the Judaizers bring their fawning flattery.</p>
<p><b>To shut you out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκκλεισα υμας</span></span>). From Christ as he will show (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:4">Galatians 5:4</span>).</p>
<p><b>That ye may seek them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα αυτους ζηλουτε</span></span>). Probably present active indicative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> as in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυσιουσθε</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:6">1 Corinthians 4:6</span>) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκομεν</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:20">1 John 5:20</span>). The contraction <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-οητε</span></span> would be <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-ωτε</span></span>, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-ουτε</span></span> (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 325). </p>
