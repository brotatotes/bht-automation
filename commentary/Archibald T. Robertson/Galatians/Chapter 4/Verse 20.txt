<p><b> I could with </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηθελον</span></span>). Imperfect active, I was wishing like Agrippa's use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβουλομην</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:22">Acts 25:22</span>, "I was just wishing. I was longing to be present with you just now (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρτ</span></span>)."</p>
<p><b>To change my voice </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλαξα την φωνην μου</span></span>). Paul could put his heart into his voice. The pen stands between them. He knew the power of his voice on their hearts. He had tried it before.</p>
<p><b>I am perplexed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απορουμα</span></span>). I am at a loss and know not what to do. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Απορεω</span></span> is from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορος</span></span>, way. I am lost at this distance from you.</p>
<p><b>About you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν υμιν</span></span>). In your cases. For this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:16">2 Corinthians 7:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:24">Galatians 1:24</span>. </p>
