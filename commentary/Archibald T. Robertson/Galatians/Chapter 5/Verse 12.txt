<p><b> I would </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφελον</span></span>). Would that, used as conjunction in wishes. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:2">1 Corinthians 4:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:1">2 Corinthians 11:1</span>. Here a wish about the future with future indicative.</p>
<p><b>They which unsettle you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αναστατουντες υμας</span></span>). Late verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναστατος</span></span>, driven from one's abode, and in papyri in this sense as well as in sense of upsetting or disturbing one's mind (boy's letter) as here. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:6">Acts 17:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:38">Acts 21:38</span> we have it in sense of making a commotion.</p>
<p><b>Cut themselves off </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκοψοντα</span></span>). Future middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκοπτω</span></span>, old word to cut off as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:32">Acts 27:32</span>, here to mutilate. </p>
