<p><b> If ye bite and devour one another </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε αλληλους δακνετε κα κατεσθιετε</span></span>). Condition of first class assumed as true. Two common and old verbs often used together of wild animals, or like cats and dogs.</p>
<p><b>That ye be not consumed one of another </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη υπ' αλληλων αναλωθητε</span></span>). Negative final clause with first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναλισκω</span></span>, old word to consume or spend. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:54">Luke 9:54</span>. There is a famous story of two snakes that grabbed each other by the tail and each swallowed the other. </p>
