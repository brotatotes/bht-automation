<p><b> The fruit of the Spirit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο καρπος του πνευματος</span></span>). Paul changes the figure from</p>
<p><b>works </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργα</span></span>) in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:19">Galatians 5:19</span> to fruit as the normal out-cropping of the Holy Spirit in us. It is a beautiful tree of fruit that Paul pictures here with nine luscious fruits on it:</p>
<p><b>Love </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπη</span></span>). Late, almost Biblical word. First as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:1">Galatians 5:1</span>, which see for discussion as superior to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλια</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερως</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Joy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρα</span></span>). Old word. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:6">1 Thessalonians 1:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Peace </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειρηνη</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:1">1 Thessalonians 1:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Long-suffering </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακροθυμια</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:6">2 Corinthians 6:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Kindness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρηστοτης</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:6">2 Corinthians 6:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Goodness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαθωσυνη</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:11">2 Thessalonians 1:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>Faithfulness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστις</span></span>). Same word as "faith." See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:33">Matthew 23:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:7">1 Corinthians 13:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:13">1 Corinthians 13:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Meekness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πραυτης</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:21">1 Corinthians 4:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:1">2 Corinthians 10:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Temperance </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγκρατεια</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:25">Acts 24:25</span>. Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγκρατης</span></span>, one holding control or holding in. In N.T. only in these passages and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:6">2 Peter 1:6</span>. Paul has a better list than the four cardinal virtues of the Stoics (temperance, prudence, fortitude, justice), though they are included with better notes struck. Temperance is alike, but kindness is better than justice, long-suffering than fortitude, love than prudence. </p>
