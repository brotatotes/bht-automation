<p><b> Let us not be </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη γινωμεθα</span></span>). Present middle subjunctive (volitive), "Let us cease becoming vainglorious" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κενοδοξο</span></span>), late word only here in N.T. (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κενοσ, δοξα</span></span>). Once in Epictetus in same sense.</p>
<p><b>Provoking one another </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλληλους προκαλουμενο</span></span>). Old word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προκαλεω</span></span>, to call forth, to challenge to combat. Only here in N.T. and in bad sense. The word for "provoke" in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:24">Hebrews 10:24</span> is <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παροξυσμον</span></span> (our "paroxysm").</p>
<p><b>Envying </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φθονουντες</span></span>). Old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φθονος</span></span>. Only here in N.T. </p>
