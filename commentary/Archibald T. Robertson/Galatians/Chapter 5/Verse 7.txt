<p>Who did hinder you? (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τις υμας ενεκοψεν?</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενκοπτω</span></span>, to cut in on one, for all the world like our use of one cutting in on us at the telephone. For this late verb see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:4">Acts 24:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:18">1 Thessalonians 2:18</span>. Note the singular <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τις</span></span>. There was some ringleader in the business. Some one "cut in" on the Galatians as they were running the Christian race and tried to trip them or to turn them. </p>
