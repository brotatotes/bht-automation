<p><b> If a man be overtaken </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν κα προλημφθη ανθρωπος</span></span>). Condition of third class, first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προλαμβανω</span></span>, old verb to take beforehand, to surprise, to detect.</p>
<p><b>Trespass </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραπτωματ</span></span>). Literally, a falling aside, a slip or lapse in the papyri rather than a wilful sin. In Polybius and Diodorus. <i>Koine</i> word.</p>
<p><b>Ye which are spiritual </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο πνευματικο</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:1">1 Corinthians 3:1</span>. The spiritually led (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:18">Galatians 5:18</span>), the spiritual experts in mending souls.</p>
<p><b>Restore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταρτιζετε</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταρτιζω</span></span>, the very word used in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:21">Matthew 4:21</span> of mending nets, old word to make <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρτιος</span></span>, fit, to equip thoroughly.</p>
<p><b>Looking to thyself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκοπων σεαυτον</span></span>). Keeping an eye on as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:18">2 Corinthians 4:18</span> like a runner on the goal.</p>
<p><b>Lest thou also be tempted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη κα συ πειρασθηις</span></span>). Negative purpose with first aorist passive subjunctive. Spiritual experts (preachers in particular) need this caution. Satan loves a shining mark. </p>
