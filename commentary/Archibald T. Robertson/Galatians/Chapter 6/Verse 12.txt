<p><b> To make a fair show </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευπροσωπησα</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευπροσωπεω</span></span>, late verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευπροσωπος</span></span>, fair of face (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ, προσωπον</span></span>). Here only in N.T., but one example in papyri (Tebt. I. 19 12 B.C. 114) which shows what may happen to any of our N.T. words not yet found elsewhere. It is in Chrysostom and later writers.</p>
<p><b>They compel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγκαζουσιν</span></span>). Conative present active indicative, "they try to compel."</p>
<p><b>For the cross of Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω σταυρω του Χριστου</span></span>). Instrumental case (causal use, Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 532). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:13">2 Corinthians 2:13</span>. "For professing the cross of Christ" (Lightfoot). </p>
