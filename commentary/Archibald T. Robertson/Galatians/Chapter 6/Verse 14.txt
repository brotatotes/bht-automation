<p><b> Far be it from me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμο μη γενοιτο</span></span>). Second aorist middle optative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span> in a negative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span>) wish about the future with dative case: "May it not happen to me." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:17">Galatians 2:17</span>. The infinitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καυχασθα</span></span> (to glory) is the subject of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενοιτο</span></span> as is common in the LXX, though not elsewhere in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>Hath been crucified unto me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμο εσταυρωτα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σταυροω</span></span>, stands crucified, with the ethical dative again (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμο</span></span>). This is one of the great sayings of Paul concerning his relation to Christ and the world in contrast with the Judaizers. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:19">Galatians 2:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:13">Galatians 3:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:4">Galatians 4:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:23">1 Corinthians 1:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:16">Romans 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:21">Romans 3:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:25">Romans 4:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:18">Romans 5:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>World </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοσμος</span></span>) has no article, but is definite as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:19">2 Corinthians 5:19</span>. Paul's old world of Jewish descent and environment is dead to him (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:3">Philippians 3:3</span>). </p>
