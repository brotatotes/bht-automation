<p><b> Bear ye one another's burdens </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλληλων τα βαρη βασταζετε</span></span>). Keep on bearing (present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασταζω</span></span>, old word, used of Jesus bearing his Cross in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:17">John 19:17</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Βαρος</span></span> means weight as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:12">Matthew 20:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:17">2 Corinthians 4:17</span>. It is when one's load (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φορτιον</span></span>, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:5">Galatians 6:5</span>) is about to press one down. Then give help in carrying it.</p>
<p><b>Fulfil </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπληρωσατε</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπληροω</span></span>, to fill up, old word, and see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:32">Matthew 23:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:16">1 Thessalonians 2:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:16">1 Corinthians 14:16</span>. Some MSS. have future indicative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπληρωσετε</span></span>). </p>
