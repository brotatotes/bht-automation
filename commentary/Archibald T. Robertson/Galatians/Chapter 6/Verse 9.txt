<p><b> Let us not be weary in well-doing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το καλον ποιουντες μη ενκακωμεν</span></span>). Volitive present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενκακεω</span></span> on which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:1">Luke 18:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:13">2 Thessalonians 3:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:1">2 Corinthians 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:16">2 Corinthians 4:16</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν, κακος</span></span>, evil). Literally, "Let us not keep on giving in to evil while doing the good." It is curious how prone we are to give in and to give out in doing the good which somehow becomes prosy or insipid to us.</p>
<p><b>In due season </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιρω ιδιω</span></span>). Locative case, "at its proper season" (harvest time). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:6">1 Timothy 2:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:15">1 Timothy 6:15</span> (plural).</p>
<p><b>If we faint not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη εκλυομενο</span></span>). Present passive participle (conditional) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span>. Cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκλυω</span></span>, old verb to loosen out. Literally, "not loosened out," relaxed, exhausted as a result of giving in to evil (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενκακωμεν</span></span>). </p>
