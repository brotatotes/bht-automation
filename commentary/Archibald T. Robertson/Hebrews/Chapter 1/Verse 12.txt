<p><b> A mantle </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιβολαιον</span></span>). Old word for covering from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παριβαλλω</span></span>, to fling around, as a veil in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:15">1 Corinthians 11:15</span>, nowhere else in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Shalt thou roll up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελιξεις</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελισσω</span></span>, late form for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειλισσω</span></span>, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:14">Revelation 6:14</span>, to fold together.</p>
<p><b>As a garment </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως ιματιον</span></span>). LXX repeats from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:11">Hebrews 1:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>They shall be changed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλαγησοντα</span></span>). Second future passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλασσω</span></span>, old verb, to change.</p>
<p><b>Shall not fail </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ εκλειψουσιν</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκλειπω</span></span>, to leave out, to fail, used of the sun in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:45">Luke 23:45</span>. "Nature is at his mercy, not he at nature's" (Moffatt). </p>
