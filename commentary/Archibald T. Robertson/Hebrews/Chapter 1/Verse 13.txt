<p><b> Hath he said </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειρηκεν</span></span>). Perfect active common use of the perfect for permanent record. This seventh quotation is proof of the Son's superiority as the Son of God (his deity) to angels and is from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+110:1">Psalms 110:1</span>, a Messianic Psalm frequently quoted in Hebrews.</p>
<p><b>Sit thou </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθου</span></span>). Second person singular imperative middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθημα</span></span>, to sit, for the longer form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθησο</span></span>, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:44">Matthew 22:44</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:3">James 2:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>On my right hand </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ δεξιων μου</span></span>). "From my right." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:3">Hebrews 1:3</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν δεξια</span></span> "at the right hand."</p>
<p><b>Till I make </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως αν θω</span></span>). Indefinite temporal clause about the future with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως</span></span> and the second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span> (often not used), a regular and common idiom. Quoted also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:43">Luke 20:43</span>. For the pleonasm in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποδιον</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των ποδων</span></span> (objective genitive) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:35">Matthew 5:35</span>. </p>
