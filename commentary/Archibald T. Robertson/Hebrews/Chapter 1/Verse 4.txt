<p><b> Having become </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενομενος</span></span>). Second aorist middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span>. In contrast with on in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:3">Hebrews 1:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>By so much </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοσουτω</span></span>). Instrumental case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοσουτος</span></span> correlative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οσω</span></span> (as) with comparative in both clauses (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρειττων</span></span>, better, comparative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρατυς</span></span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαφορωτερον</span></span>, more excellent, comparative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαφορος</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Than the angels </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των αγγελων</span></span>). Ablative of comparison after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρειττων</span></span>, as often.</p>
<p><b>Than they </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρ' αυτους</span></span>). Instead of the ablative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτων</span></span> here the preposition <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span> (along, by the side of) with the accusative occurs, another common idiom as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:3">Hebrews 3:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:23">Hebrews 9:23</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Διαφορος</span></span> only in Hebrews in N.T. except <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:6">Romans 12:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Hath inherited </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεκληρονομηκεν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κληρονομεω</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κληρονομος</span></span>, heir, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:2">Hebrews 1:2</span>), and still inherits it, the name (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ονομα</span></span>, oriental sense of rank) of "Son" which is superior to prophets as already shown (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:2">Hebrews 1:2</span>) and also to angels (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:4-2">Hebrews 1:4-2</span>) as he now proceeds to prove. Jesus is superior to angels as God's Son, his deity (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:4-2">Hebrews 1:4-2</span>). The author proves it from Scripture (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:4-14">Hebrews 1:4-14</span>). </p>
