<p><b> Hath anointed thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχρισεν σε</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χριω</span></span>, to anoint, from which verb the verbal <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χριστος</span></span> (Anointed One) comes. See Christ's use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχρισεν</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:18">Luke 4:18</span> from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+66:1">Isaiah 66:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>With the oil of gladness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελαιον αγαλλιασεως</span></span>). Accusative case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχρισεν</span></span> (second accusative besides <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σε</span></span>). Perhaps the festive anointing on occasions of joy (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:2">Hebrews 12:2</span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:44">Luke 1:44</span>.</p>
<p><b>Fellows </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετοχους</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετεχω</span></span>, partners, sharers, in N.T. only in Hebrews save <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:7">Luke 5:7</span>. Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span> with accusative here, beside, beyond, above (by comparison, extending beyond). </p>
