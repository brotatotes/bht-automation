<p><b> When he had offered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσενεγκας</span></span>). Second aorist active participle (with first aorist ending <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-ας</span></span> in place of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-ον</span></span>) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσφερω</span></span>, single act in contrast to present participle <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσφερων</span></span> above.</p>
<p><b>One sacrifice </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιαν θυσιαν</span></span>). This the main point. The one sacrifice does the work that the many failed to do. One wonders how priests who claim that the "mass" is the sacrifice of Christ's body repeated explain this verse.</p>
<p><b>For ever </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το διηνεκες</span></span>). Can be construed either with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιαν θυσιαν</span></span> or with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκαθισεν</span></span> (sat down). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:3">Hebrews 1:3</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκαθισεν</span></span>. </p>
