<p><b> Henceforth expecting </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το λοιπον εκδεχομενος</span></span>). "For the rest" or "for the future" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το λοιπον</span></span>, accusative of extent of time). The expectant attitude of Christ here is that of final and certain victory (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:33">John 16:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:24-28">1 Corinthians 15:24-28</span>).</p>
<p><b>Till his enemies be made </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως τεθωσιν ο εχθρο αυτου</span></span>). Purpose and temporal clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως</span></span> and the first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span>. He quotes <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+110:1">Psalms 110:1</span> again. </p>
