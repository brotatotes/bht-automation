<p><b> He hath perfected </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τετελειωκεν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελειοω</span></span>. He has done what the old sacrifices failed to do (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:1">Hebrews 10:1</span>).</p>
<p><b>Them that are sanctified </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους αγιαζομενους</span></span>). Articular participle (accusative case) present passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγιαζω</span></span> (note perfect in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:10">Hebrews 10:10</span>) either because of the process still going on or because of the repetition in so many persons as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:11">Hebrews 2:11</span>. </p>
