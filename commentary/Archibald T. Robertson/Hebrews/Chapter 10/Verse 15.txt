<p><b> And the Holy Ghost also beareth witness to us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρε δε ημιν κα το πνευμα το αγιον</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μαρτυρεω</span></span> is common in Philo for Scripture quotation. The author confirms his interpretation of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+40:7-9">Psalms 40:7-9</span> by repeating from Jeremiah (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+31:31">Jeremiah 31:31</span>) what he had already quoted (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:8-12">Hebrews 8:8-12</span>).</p>
<p><b>After he hath said </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα το ειρηκενα</span></span>). Accusative case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα</span></span> of the articular infinitive perfect active, "after the having said." </p>
