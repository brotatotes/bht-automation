<p><b> Else they would not have ceased? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επε ουκ αν επαυσαντο;</span></span>). Ellipsis of condition after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επε</span></span> (since if they really did perfect) with the conclusion of the second-class condition (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span> and the aorist middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παυομα</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>To be offered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσφερομενα</span></span>). Regular idiom, participle (present passive) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παυομα</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:42">Acts 5:42</span>).</p>
<p><b>Because </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια το</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Δια</span></span> with the accusative of the articular infinitive, "because of the having" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχειν</span></span>) as to the worshippers (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους λατρευοντας</span></span>, accusative of general reference of the articular participle), not "would have had."</p>
<p><b>No more conscience of sins </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηδεμιαν ετ συνειδησιν αμαρτιων</span></span>). Rather "consciousness of sins" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:14">Hebrews 9:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>Having been once cleansed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαξ κεκαθαρισμενους</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθαριζω</span></span>, "if they had once for all been cleansed." </p>
