<p><b> Let us draw near </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσερχωμεθα</span></span>). Present middle volitive subjunctive as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:16">Hebrews 4:16</span> with which exhortation the discussion began. There are three exhortations in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+22:25">Hebrews 22:25</span> (Let us draw near, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσερχωμεθα</span></span>, let us hold fast, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεχωμεν</span></span>, let us consider one another, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατανοωμεν αλληλους</span></span>). Four items are added to this first exhortation.</p>
<p><b>With a true heart </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα αληθινης καρδιας</span></span>). With loyalty and fealty.</p>
<p><b>In fulness of faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν πληροφορια πιστεως</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:11">Hebrews 6:11</span> for this very phrase.</p>
<p><b>Having our hearts sprinkled from an evil conscience </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρεραντισμενο τας καρδιας απο συνειδησεως πονηρας</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ραντιζω</span></span> with the accusative retained in the passive, an evident allusion to the sprinkling of blood in the old tabernacle (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:18-22">Hebrews 9:18-22</span>) and the shedding of Christ's blood for the cleansing of our consciences (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:1-4">Hebrews 10:1-4</span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:2">1 Peter 1:2</span> for "the sprinkling of the blood of Jesus Christ."</p>
<p><b>Our body washed with pure water </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λελουσμενο το σωμα υδατ καθαρω</span></span>). Perfect passive (or middle) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λουω</span></span>, old verb to bathe, to wash. Accusative also retained if passive. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hυδατ</span></span> can be either locative (in) or instrumental (with). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:26">Ephesians 5:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:5">Titus 3:5</span> for the use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λουτρον</span></span>. If the reference here is to baptism (quite doubtful), the meaning is a symbol (Dods) of the previous cleansing by the blood of Christ. </p>
