<p><b> Let us hold fast </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεχωμεν</span></span>). Present (keep on holding fast) active volitive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεχω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:6">Hebrews 3:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:14">Hebrews 3:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>That it waver not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακλινη</span></span>). Common compound adjective (alpha privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλινω</span></span>, unwavering, not leaning, here only in N.T. It is a confession of hope, not of despair.</p>
<p><b>That promised </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο επαγγειλαμενος</span></span>). First aorist middle articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαγγελλω</span></span>. This is the argument remaining to be discussed (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:26-12">Hebrews 10:26-12</span>) and already alluded to (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:13">Hebrews 6:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:6">Hebrews 8:6</span>). The ministry of Jesus rests upon "better promises." How better? God is "faithful," but he made the other promises also. We shall see. </p>
