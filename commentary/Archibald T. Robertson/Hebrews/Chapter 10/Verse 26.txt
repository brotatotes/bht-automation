<p><b> If we sin wilfully </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκουσιως αμαρτανοντων ημων</span></span>). Genitive absolute with the present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτανω</span></span>, circumstantial participle here in a conditional sense.</p>
<p><b>After that we have received </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα το λαβειν</span></span>). "After the receiving" (accusative case of the articular infinitive second aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Knowledge </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιγνωσιν</span></span>). "Full knowledge," as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:4">Hebrews 6:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>There remaineth no more </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκετ απολειπετα</span></span>). "No longer is there left behind" (present passive indicative as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:9">Hebrews 4:9</span>), for one has renounced the one and only sacrifice for sin that does or can remove sin (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:1-18">Hebrews 10:1-18</span>). </p>
