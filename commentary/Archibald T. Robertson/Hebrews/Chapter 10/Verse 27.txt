<p><b> Expectation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκδοχη</span></span>). Usually reception or interpretation from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκδεχομα</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:10">Hebrews 11:10</span>), only here in N.T. and in unusual sense like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσδοκια</span></span>, like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεκδεχομα</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:19">Romans 8:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:23">Romans 8:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:25">Romans 8:25</span>), this sense apparently "coined by the writer" (Moffatt) from his use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκδεχομα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:13">Hebrews 10:13</span>. The papyri have it in the sense of interpretation.</p>
<p><b>A fierceness of fire </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυρος ζηλος</span></span>). An anger (zeal, jealousy) marked (genitive) by fire. Language kin to that in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+26:11">Isaiah 26:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="zep+1:19">Zephaniah 1:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+79:5">Psalms 79:5</span>. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:8-10">2 Thessalonians 1:8-10</span> for a like picture of destined doom.</p>
<p><b>Devour </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσθιειν</span></span>). "To eat" (figuratively), present active infinitive.</p>
<p><b>The adversaries </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους υπεναντιους</span></span>). Old double compound adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο, εν, αντιος</span></span>), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:14">Colossians 2:14</span>. Those directly opposite. </p>
