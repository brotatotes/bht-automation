<p><b> Hath set at naught </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθετησας</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθετεω</span></span>, late compound, very common in LXX, from alpha privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span>, to render null and void, to set aside, only here in Hebrews (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:9">Mark 7:9</span>), but note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθετησις</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:18">Hebrews 7:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:26">Hebrews 9:26</span>).</p>
<p><b>Without mercy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χωρις οικτιρμων</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:3">2 Corinthians 1:3</span>. This was the law (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+17:6">Deuteronomy 17:6</span>) for apostates.</p>
<p><b>On the word of two or three </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ δυσιν η τρισιν</span></span>). "On the basis of two or three." For this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> with the locative see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:17">Hebrews 9:17</span>. </p>
