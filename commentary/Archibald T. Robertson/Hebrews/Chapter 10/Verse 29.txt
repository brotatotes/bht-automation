<p><b> How much </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποσω</span></span>). Instrumental case of degree or measure. An argument from the less to the greater, "the first of Hillel's seven rules for exegesis" (Moffatt).</p>
<p><b>Think ye </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκειτε</span></span>). An appeal to their own sense of justice about apostates from Christ.</p>
<p><b>Sorer </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χειρονος</span></span>). "Worse," comparative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακος</span></span> (bad).</p>
<p><b>Punishment </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιμωριας</span></span>). Genitive case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αξιωθησετα</span></span> (first future passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αξιοω</span></span>, to deem worthy). The word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιμωρια</span></span> originally meant vengeance. Old word, in LXX, only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Who hath trodden under foot the Son of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο τον υιον του θεου καταπατησας</span></span>). First aorist active articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταπατεω</span></span>, old verb (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:13">Matthew 5:13</span>) for scornful neglect like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="zec+12:3">Zechariah 12:3</span>. See same idea in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:6">Hebrews 6:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Wherewith he was sanctified </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ω ηγιασθη</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγιαζω</span></span>. It is an unspeakable tragedy that should warn every follower of Christ not to play with treachery to Christ (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:4-8">Hebrews 6:4-8</span>).</p>
<p><b>An unholy thing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινον</span></span>). Common in the sense of uncleanness as Peter used it in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:14">Acts 10:14</span>. Think of one who thus despises "the blood of Christ wherewith he was sanctified." And yet there are a few today who sneer at the blood of Christ and the gospel based on his atoning sacrifice as "a slaughter house" religion!</p>
<p><b>Hath done despite </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενυβρισας</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενυβριζω</span></span>, old verb to treat with contumely, to give insult to, here only in the N.T. It is a powerful word for insulting the Holy Spirit after receiving his blessings (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:4">Hebrews 6:4</span>). </p>
