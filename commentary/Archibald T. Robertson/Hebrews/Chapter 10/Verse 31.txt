<p><b> A fearful thing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φοβερον</span></span>). Old adjective (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φοβεω</span></span>, to frighten). In N.T. only in Heb. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:27">Hebrews 10:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:31">Hebrews 10:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:21">Hebrews 12:21</span>). The sense is not to be explained away. The wrath of God faces wrongdoers.</p>
<p><b>To fall </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το εμπεσειν</span></span>). "The falling" (articular infinitive second aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμπιπτω</span></span>, to fall in, followed here by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span>). We are not dealing with a dead or an absentee God, but one who is alive and alert (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:12">Hebrews 3:12</span>). </p>
