<p><b> Call to remembrance </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναμιμνησκεσθε</span></span>). Present middle imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναμιμνησκω</span></span>, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:15">2 Corinthians 7:15</span> "remind yourselves." The former days were some distance in the past (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:12">Hebrews 5:12</span>), some years at any rate. It is a definite experience of people in a certain place. Jerusalem Christians had had experiences of this nature, but so had others.</p>
<p><b>After ye were enlightened </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωτισθεντες</span></span>). First aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωτιζω</span></span> in the same sense as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:4">Hebrews 6:4</span> (regeneration) and like "the full knowledge of the truth" in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:26">Hebrews 10:26</span>.</p>
<p><b>Conflict </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθλησιν</span></span>). Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθλεω</span></span>, to engage in a public contest in the games (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:5">2 Timothy 2:5</span>), only here in the N.T. It occurs in the inscriptions. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:10">Hebrews 2:10</span> for the benefit of "sufferings" in training. </p>
