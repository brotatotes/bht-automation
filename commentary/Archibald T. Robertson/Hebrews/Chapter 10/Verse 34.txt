<p><b> Ye had compassion on </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεπαθησατε</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνπαθεω</span></span>, old verb to have a feeling with, to sympathize with.</p>
<p><b>Them that were in bonds </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις δεσμιοις</span></span>). Associative instrumental case, "with the prisoners" (the bound ones). Used of Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:1">Ephesians 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:8">2 Timothy 1:8</span>).</p>
<p><b>Took joyfully </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα χαρας προσεδεξασθε</span></span>). First aorist middle (indirect) indicative, "ye received to yourselves with joy." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:1">Romans 13:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:3">Romans 13:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:7">Romans 15:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>The spoiling </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την αρπαγην</span></span>). "The seizing," "the plundering." Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρπαζω</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:35">Matthew 23:35</span>.</p>
<p><b>Of your possessions </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των υπαρχοντων υμων</span></span>). "Of your belongings." Genitive of the articular present active neuter plural participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπαρχω</span></span> used as a substantive (cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμων</span></span> genitive) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:21">Matthew 19:21</span>.</p>
<p><b>That ye yourselves have </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχειν εαυτους</span></span>). Infinitive (present active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>) in indirect discourse after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκοντες</span></span> (knowing) with the accusative of general reference (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαυτους</span></span>, as to yourselves), though some MSS. omit <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαυτους</span></span>, some have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαυτοις</span></span> (dative, for yourselves), and some <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν εαυτοις</span></span> (in yourselves). The predicate nominative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτο</span></span> could have been used agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκοντες</span></span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:22">Romans 1:22</span>).</p>
<p><b>A better possession </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρεισσονα υπαρξιν</span></span>). Common word in the same sense as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα υπαρχοντα</span></span> above, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:45">Acts 2:45</span>. In place of their plundered property they have treasures in heaven (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:20">Matthew 6:20</span>).</p>
<p><b>Abiding </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενουσαν</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενω</span></span>. No oppressors (legal or illegal) can rob them of this (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:19">Matthew 6:19</span>). </p>
