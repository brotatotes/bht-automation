<p><b> But we </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημεις δε</span></span>). In contrast to renegades who do flicker and turn back from Christ.</p>
<p><b>Of them that shrink back unto perdition </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποστολης εις απωλειαν</span></span>). Predicate genitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποστολη</span></span>, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:11">Hebrews 12:11</span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποστελλω</span></span> with same sense here, stealthy retreat in Plutarch, dissimulation in Josephus. Here alone in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>Unto the saving of the soul </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις περιποιησιν ψυχης</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιποιεω</span></span>, to reserve, to preserve (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:33">Luke 17:33</span>) to purchase (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:28">Acts 20:28</span>). So here preserving or saving one's life as in Plato, but possession in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:14">Ephesians 1:14</span>, obtaining in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:9">1 Thessalonians 4:9</span>. Papyri have it in sense of preservation. </p>
