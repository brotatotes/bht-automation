<p><b> Then </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοτε</span></span>). When it was plain that God could not be propitiated by such sacrifices.</p>
<p><b>Lo, I am come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιδου ηκω</span></span>). The Messiah is represented as offering himself to do God's will (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του ποιησα το θελημα σου</span></span>, the genitive articular infinitive of purpose).</p>
<p><b>In the roll of the book it is written of me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν κεφαλιδ βιβλιου γεγραπτα περ εμου</span></span>). Stands written (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεγραπτα</span></span>, perfect passive indicative). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κεφαλις</span></span> is a diminutive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεφαλη</span></span> (head), a little head, then roll only here in N.T., but in the papyri. Here it refers "to the O.T. as a prediction of Christ's higher sacrifice" (Moffatt). </p>
