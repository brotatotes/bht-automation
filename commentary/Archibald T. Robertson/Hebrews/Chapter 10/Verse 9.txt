<p><b> The which </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιτινες</span></span>). "Which very things" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυσια</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Then hath he said </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοτε ειρηκεν</span></span>). That is Christ. Perfect active indicative with which compare <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοτε ειπον</span></span> (second aorist active) in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:7">Hebrews 10:7</span> which is quoted again.</p>
<p><b>He taketh away the first </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναιρε το πρωτον</span></span>). Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναιρεω</span></span>, to take up, to abolish, of a man to kill (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:16">Matthew 2:16</span>). By "the first" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το πρωτον</span></span>) he means the system of animal sacrifices in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:8">Hebrews 10:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>That he may establish the second </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα το δευτερον στηση</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the first aorist active (transitive) subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span>, to place. By "the second" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το δευτερον</span></span>) he means doing God's will as shown in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:9">Hebrews 10:9</span> (following verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:8">Hebrews 10:8</span>). This is the author's exegesis of the Psalm. </p>
