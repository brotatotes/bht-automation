<p><b> He looked for </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεδεχετο</span></span>). Imperfect middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκδεχομα</span></span> (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:13">Hebrews 10:13</span>) picturesque progressive imperfect, his steady and patient waiting in spite of disappointment.</p>
<p><b>The foundations </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους θεμελιους</span></span>). Not just "tents" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκηναις</span></span>, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:9">Hebrews 11:9</span>). Ahraham set his steady gaze on heaven as his real home, being a mere pilgrim (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παροικος</span></span>) on earth.</p>
<p><b>Builder </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεχνιτης</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεχνη</span></span> (craft) or trade (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:29">Acts 17:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:3">Acts 18:3</span>), craftsman, artificer, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:24">Acts 19:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:38">Acts 19:38</span>.</p>
<p><b>Maker </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δημιουργος</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δημιος</span></span> (public) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργον</span></span>, a worker for the public, artisan, framer, here only in N.T. </p>
