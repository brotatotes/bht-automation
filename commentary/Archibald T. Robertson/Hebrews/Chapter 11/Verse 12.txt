<p><b> And that as good as dead </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ταυτα νενεκρωμενου</span></span>). Accusative of general reference (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταυτα</span></span>), sometimes singular as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:8">1 Corinthians 6:8</span>. The perfect passive participle from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νεκροω</span></span>, late verb to make dead, to treat as dead (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:19">Romans 4:19</span>), here by hyperbole.</p>
<p><b>By the sea shore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα το χειλος της θαλασσης</span></span>). "Along the lip of the sea" (from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+22:17">Genesis 22:17</span>), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χειλος</span></span> here alone in this sense in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>Innumerable </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναριθμητος</span></span>). Old compound verbal adjective (alpha privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αριθμεω</span></span>, to number), here alone in N.T. </p>
