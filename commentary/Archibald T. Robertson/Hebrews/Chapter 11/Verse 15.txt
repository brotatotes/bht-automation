<p><b> Had been mindful </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμνημονευον</span></span>)--</p>
<p><b>would have had </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειχον αν</span></span>). Condition of second class (note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span> in conclusion) with the imperfect (not aorist) in both condition and conclusion. So it means: "If they had continued mindful, they would have kept on having (linear action in both cases in past time).</p>
<p><b>Opportunity to return </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιρον ανακαμψα</span></span>). Old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανακαμπτω</span></span> to bend back, to turn back (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:12">Matthew 2:12</span>), here first aorist active infinitive. Continual hankering would have found a way. Cf. the Israelites in the wilderness yearning after Egypt. </p>
