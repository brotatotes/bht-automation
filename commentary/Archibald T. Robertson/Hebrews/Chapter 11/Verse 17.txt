<p><b> Being tried </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειραζομενος</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειραζω</span></span>. The test was still going on.</p>
<p><b>Offered up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσενηνοχεν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσφερω</span></span>, the verb so often used in this Epistle. The act was already consummated so far as Abraham was concerned when it was interrupted and it stands on record about him. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+22:1-18">Genesis 22:1-18</span>.</p>
<p><b>He that had gladly received the promises </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο τας επαγγελιας αναδεξαμενος</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αναδεχομα</span></span> is old verb to welcome, to entertain, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:7">Acts 28:7</span>. It seemed the death of his hopes.</p>
<p><b>Was offering up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσεφερεν</span></span>). It is the imperfect of an interrupted action like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκαλουν</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:59">Luke 1:59</span>. </p>
