<p><b> Accounting </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογισαμενος</span></span>). First aorist middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογιζομα</span></span>. Abraham had God's clear command that contravened God's previous promise. This was his solution of his difficult situation.</p>
<p><b>God is able </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυνατα ο θεος</span></span>). God had given him Isaac in his old age. God can raise him from the dead. It was Abraham's duty to obey God.</p>
<p><b>In a parable </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν παραβολη</span></span>). See already <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:9">Hebrews 9:9</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραβολη</span></span>. Because of (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οθεν</span></span>, whence) Abraham's superb faith Isaac was spared and so he received him back (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκομισατο</span></span>) as almost from the dead. This is the test that Abraham stood of which James speaks (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:23">James 2:23</span>). </p>
