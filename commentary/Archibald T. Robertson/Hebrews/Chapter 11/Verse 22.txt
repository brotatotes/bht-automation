<p><b> When his end was nigh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελευτων</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελευταω</span></span>, to finish or close (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:19">Matthew 2:19</span>), "finishing his life."</p>
<p><b>Of the departure </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ της εξοδου</span></span>). Late compound for way out, exit as here, metaphorically of death as here (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:31">Luke 9:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:15">2 Peter 1:15</span>).</p>
<p><b>Concerning his bones </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ των οστεων αυτου</span></span>). Uncontracted form as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:27">Matthew 23:27</span>. </p>
