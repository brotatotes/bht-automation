<p><b> Choosing rather </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαλλον ελομενος</span></span>). "Rather having chosen" (second aorist middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιρεω</span></span>, to take for oneself a position).</p>
<p><b>To be entreated with </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνκακουχεισθα</span></span>). Present passive infinitive of the double compound <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνκακουχεω</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν, κακοσ, εχω</span></span>), to treat ill with (associative instrumental case), only known example save one in the papyri (second century A.D.), though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακουχεω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:37">Hebrews 11:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:3">Hebrews 13:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>To enjoy the pleasures of sin for a season </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσκαιρον εχειν αμαρτιας απολαυσιν</span></span>). Literally, "to have temporary pleasure of sin." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Απολαυσις</span></span> is old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολαυω</span></span>, to enjoy, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:17">1 Timothy 6:17</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Προσκαιρος</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσ, καιρος</span></span>) is a common <i>Koine</i> word as the antithesis to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιωνιος</span></span> (eternal) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:21">Matthew 13:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:17">Mark 4:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:18">2 Corinthians 4:18</span> (only N.T. examples). To have been disloyal to God's people would have brought enjoyment to Moses in the Egyptian Court for a short while only. </p>
