<p><b> Which assaying to do </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ης πιεραν λαβοντες</span></span>). Literally, "of which taking trial" (second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>, to take). The idiom <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειραν λαμβανειν</span></span> occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+28:56">Deuteronomy 28:56</span>, in N.T. only here and verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:36">Hebrews 11:36</span>, though a classical idiom (Demosthenes, etc.).</p>
<p><b>Were swallowed up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεποθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταπινω</span></span>, to drink down, to swallow down (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:24">Matthew 23:24</span>). </p>
