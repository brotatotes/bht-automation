<p><b> And what shall I more say? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κα τ ετ λεγω;</span></span>). Deliberative present active subjunctive (same form as indicative, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγω</span></span>). It is both a literary and an oratorical idiom here. He feels helpless to go on in the same style as he has done from Abel to Rahab (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:4-31">Hebrews 11:4-31</span>).</p>
<p><b>Will fail me if I tell about </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλειψε με διηγουμενον περ</span></span>). Literally, "will leave me telling about." Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διηγεομα</span></span>, to lead through, carry a discussion through, and masculine (disposing of Priscilla as possible author) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">με</span></span>. Vivid and picturesque description of the author's embarrassment of riches as he contemplates the long list of the heroes of faith during the long years in Palestine. He mentions six names (Gideon, Barak, Samson, Jephtha, David, Samuel) and then summarizes the rest under "the prophets" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των προφητων</span></span>, the for-speakers for God) of whom Samuel was the leader. </p>
