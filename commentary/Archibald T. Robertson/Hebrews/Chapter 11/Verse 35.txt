<p><b> By a resurrection </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ αναστασεως</span></span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ki+17:17">1 Kings 17:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ki+4:8-37">2 Kings 4:8-37</span>.</p>
<p><b>Were tortured </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετυμπανισθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυμπανιζω</span></span>, late verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυμπανον</span></span> (kettledrum, drumstick), to beat the drum, to beat to death (cf. II Macc. 7 about Eleazar and the Mother and the seven sons), once in LXX (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1sa+21:13">1 Samuel 21:13</span>).</p>
<p><b>Not accepting their deliverance </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου προσδεξαμενο την απολυτρωσιν</span></span>). Offered at the price of disloyalty as in II Macc. 6:21-27.</p>
<p><b>That they might obtain a better resurrection </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα κρειττονος αναστασεως τυχωσιν</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυγχανω</span></span> to obtain with the genitive case. A "better resurrection" than the temporary ones alluded to in this verse by the women. </p>
