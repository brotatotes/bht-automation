<p><b> They were stoned </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελιθασθησαν</span></span>). Like Zechariah son of Jehoiada (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ch+24:20">2 Chronicles 24:20</span>). "A characteristic Jewish punishment" (Vincent). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λιθαζω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:31">John 10:31</span>).</p>
<p><b>They were sawn asunder </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επρισθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πριω</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πριζω</span></span>, old verb (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πριον</span></span>, a saw). Cruel Jewish punishment (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="am+1:3">Amos 1:3</span>) said to have been inflicted on Isaiah.</p>
<p><b>They were tempted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επειρασθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειραζω</span></span>. The MSS. vary greatly in the text here and the order of these two items. This mild word seems an anticlimax after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επρισθησαν</span></span>. One of the seven brothers was fried (II Macc. 7:4) and so <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επρησθεσαν</span></span> (were burned) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιμπραω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:6">Acts 28:6</span>) has been suggested.</p>
<p><b>With the sword </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν φονω μαχαιρης</span></span>). "In (by) slaughter of the sword" (Ionic form of the genitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαχαιρες</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+17:13">Exodus 17:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+21:24">Numbers 21:24</span>). The fate of unpopular prophets (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ki+10:10">1 Kings 10:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+26:23">Jeremiah 26:23</span>).</p>
<p><b>They went about </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιηλθον</span></span>). Constative aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιερχομα</span></span> (picturesque compound verb). Here the sufferings of the living.</p>
<p><b>In sheep skins </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν μηλωταις</span></span>). Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηλον</span></span> (sheep), rough garment of prophets as Elijah (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ki+19:13">1 Kings 19:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ki+19:19">1 Kings 19:19</span>), here only in N.T. In Byzantine Greek a monk's garb.</p>
<p><b>In goatskins </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αιγειοις δερμασιν</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Δερμα</span></span>, old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δερω</span></span>, to flay (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:35">Matthew 21:35</span>), here only in N.T. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αιγειος</span></span>, old adjective (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιξ</span></span>, goat), here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Being destitute </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υστερουμενο</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υστερεω</span></span>, old verb to be left behind, used by Paul of himself (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:9">2 Corinthians 11:9</span>).</p>
<p><b>Afflicted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θλιβομενο</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θλιβω</span></span>, common verb to oppress.</p>
<p><b>Evil entreated </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακουχουμενο</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακουχεω</span></span>, late compound verb from obsolete <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακουχος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακος</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>), in LXX (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ki+2:26">1 Kings 2:26</span>), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:3">Hebrews 13:3</span>. See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνκακουχεισθα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:25">Hebrews 11:25</span>. </p>
