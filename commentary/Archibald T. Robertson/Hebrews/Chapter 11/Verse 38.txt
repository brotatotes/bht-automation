<p><b> Of whom the world was not worthy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ων ουκ ην αξιος ο κοσμος</span></span>) Graphic picture in a short parenthetical relative clause (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ων</span></span>, genitive plural with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αξιος</span></span>), a phrase to stir the blood of the readers.</p>
<p><b>Wandering </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλανωμενο</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλαναω</span></span>, like lost sheep, hunted by wolves.</p>
<p><b>Caves </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπηλαιοις</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπεος</span></span> (cavern) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:13">Matthew 21:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Holes </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπαις</span></span>). Old word, perhaps from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπς</span></span> (root of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span>, to see), opening, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:11">James 3:11</span>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ki+18:4">1 Kings 18:4</span>; II Macc. 5:27; 10:6 (about Judas Maccabeus and others). </p>
