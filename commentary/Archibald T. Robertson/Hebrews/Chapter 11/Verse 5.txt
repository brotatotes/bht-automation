<p><b> Was translated </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετετεθη</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετατιθημ</span></span>, old verb to transpose, to change as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:12">Hebrews 7:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:16">Acts 7:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>That he should not see death </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του μη ιδειν θανατον</span></span>). Here again <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του</span></span> with the infinitive usually expresses purpose, but in this case result is the idea as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:23">Matthew 21:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:24">Romans 1:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:3">Romans 7:3</span>, etc. (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 1002).</p>
<p><b>He was not found </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουχ ηυρισκετο</span></span>). Imperfect passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρισκω</span></span> from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+5:24">Genesis 5:24</span>. Was still not found.</p>
<p><b>Translated </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετεθηκεν</span></span>). First aorist active of same verb as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετετεθη</span></span> just before.</p>
<p><b>Translation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταθεσεως</span></span>). Substantive from the same verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετατιθημ</span></span>, used already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:12">Hebrews 7:12</span> for change. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:27">Hebrews 12:27</span>. Our very word "metathesis."</p>
<p><b>He hath had witness borne him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεμαρτυρητα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρεω</span></span>, stands on record still, "he has been testified to."</p>
<p><b>That he had been well-pleasing unto God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευαρεστηκενα τω θεω</span></span>). Perfect active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευαρεστεω</span></span>, late compound from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευαρεστος</span></span> (well-pleasing), in N.T. only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:5">Hebrews 11:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:16">Hebrews 13:16</span>. With dative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεω</span></span>. Quoted here from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+5:22">Genesis 5:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+5:24">Genesis 5:24</span>. The word is common of a servant pleasing his master. </p>
