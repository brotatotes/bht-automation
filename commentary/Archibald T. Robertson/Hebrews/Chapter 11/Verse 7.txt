<p><b> Being warned of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρηματισθεις</span></span>). First aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρηματιζω</span></span>, old word for oracular or divine communications as already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:5">Hebrews 8:5</span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:12">Matthew 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:22">Matthew 2:22</span>, etc.).</p>
<p><b>Moved with godly fear </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλαβηθη</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλαβεομα</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλαβης</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαβειν</span></span>, to take hold well or carefully), to show oneself <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλαβης</span></span>, to act circumspectly or with reverence, here only in N.T. (save Textus Receptus in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:10">Acts 23:10</span>), often in LXX.</p>
<p><b>An ark </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κιβωτον</span></span>). <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+6:15">Genesis 6:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:38">Matthew 24:38</span>. Shaped like a box (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:4">Hebrews 9:4</span>).</p>
<p><b>Through which </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι' ης</span></span>). Through his faith as shown in building the ark.</p>
<p><b>The world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον κοσμον</span></span>). Sinful humanity as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:38">Hebrews 11:38</span>.</p>
<p><b>Heir </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κληρονομος</span></span>). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:5">2 Peter 2:5</span> Noah is called "a preacher of righteousness" as here "heir of righteousness." He himself believed his message about the flood. Like Enoch he walked with God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+6:9">Genesis 6:9</span>). </p>
