<p><b> They </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο μεν</span></span>). Demonstrative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span> in contrast (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεν</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Chastened </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαιδευον</span></span>). Imperfect active, used to chasten.</p>
<p><b>As seemed good to them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα το δοκουν αυτοις</span></span>). "According to the thing seeming good to them." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Δοκουν</span></span> is present active neuter singular articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκεω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>But he </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο δε</span></span>). Demonstrative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span> vs. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεν</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>For our profit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ το συμφερον</span></span>). Present active articular neuter singular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμφερω</span></span>, to bear together as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:7">1 Corinthians 12:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>That we may be partakers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το μεταλαβειν</span></span>). Articular second aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταλαμβανω</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> for purpose, "for the partaking."</p>
<p><b>Of his holiness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της αγιοτητος αυτου</span></span>). Genitive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταλαβειν</span></span> (to share in). Rare word, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:12">2 Corinthians 1:12</span>. </p>
