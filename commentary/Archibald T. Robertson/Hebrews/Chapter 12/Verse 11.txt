<p><b> For the present </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς το παρον</span></span>). A classical phrase (Thucydides), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς</span></span> with the accusative neuter singular articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρειμ</span></span>, to be beside.</p>
<p><b>Not joyous, but grievous </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου χαρασ, αλλα λυπης</span></span>). Predicate ablative (springing from) or predicate genitive (marked by). Either makes sense, but note predicate ablative in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:7">2 Corinthians 4:7</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα του θεου κα μη εξ ημων</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Peaceable fruit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καρπον ειρηνικον</span></span>). Old adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειρηνη</span></span> (peace), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:17">James 3:17</span>. Peaceable after the chastening is over.</p>
<p><b>Exercised thereby </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι' αυτης γεγυμνασμενοις</span></span>). Perfect passive participle (dative case) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γυμναζω</span></span>, state of completion, picturing the discipline as a gymnasium like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:14">Hebrews 5:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:17">1 Timothy 4:17</span>. </p>
