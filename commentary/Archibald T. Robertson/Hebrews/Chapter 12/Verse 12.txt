<p><b> Wherefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διο</span></span>). Because of the chastening.</p>
<p><b>Lift up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανορθωσατε</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανορθοω</span></span>, old compound (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανα, ορθος</span></span>) to make straight, in N.T. here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:13">Luke 13:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:16">Acts 15:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>Hang down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρειμενας</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παριημ</span></span>, old verb to let pass, to relax, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:42">Luke 11:42</span>.</p>
<p><b>Palsied </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραλελυμενα</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραλυω</span></span>, old verb to loosen on the side, to dissolve, to paralyze (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:18">Luke 5:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:24">Luke 5:24</span>). </p>
