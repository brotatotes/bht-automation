<p><b> Straight paths </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τροχιας ορθας</span></span>). Track of a wheel (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τροχος</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:6">James 3:6</span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρεχω</span></span>, to run), here only in N.T. "Straight (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορθας</span></span>) wheel tracks."</p>
<p><b>Be not turned out of the way </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη εκτραπη</span></span>). Negative final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη</span></span> and second aorist passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκτρεπω</span></span>, old verb to turn out, to twist, to put out of joint. So <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:6">1 Timothy 1:6</span>. Vivid picture of concern for the lame (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χωλον</span></span>, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:5">Matthew 11:5</span>). Graphic picture of concern for the weak, a good argument for prohibition also. </p>
