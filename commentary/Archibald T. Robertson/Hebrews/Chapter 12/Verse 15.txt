<p><b> Looking carefully </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισκοπουντες</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισκοπεω</span></span>, to have oversight, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:2">1 Peter 5:2</span>. Cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισχοπος</span></span> (bishop).</p>
<p><b>Lest there be any man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη τις</span></span>). Negative purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span> (present active subjunctive) omitted.</p>
<p><b>Falleth short of </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υστερων απο</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υστερεω</span></span> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:1">Hebrews 4:1</span>) agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τις</span></span>. Followed here by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span> and the ablative.</p>
<p><b>Root of bitterness </b>. (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ριζα πικριας</span></span>). Quoted from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+29:18">Deuteronomy 29:18</span>. Vivid picture.</p>
<p><b>Springing up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανω φυουσα</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυω</span></span>, to sprout. Pictured here as a quick process. Also from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+29:18">Deuteronomy 29:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>Trouble </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενοχλη</span></span>). Present active subjunctive (in final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη τις</span></span>) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενοχλεω</span></span>, old verb to trouble with a crowd, to annoy. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:18">Luke 6:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>Be defiled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιανθωσιν</span></span>). First aorist passive subjunctive (in final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span>) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιανω</span></span>, old verb to dye, to stain, to defile as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:15">Titus 1:15</span> (the conscience). The contagion of sin is terrible as any disease. </p>
