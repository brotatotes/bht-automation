<p><b> Ye are not come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου προσεληλυθατε</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσερχομα</span></span>. There is no word here in the Greek for "a mount" like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορε</span></span> in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:20">Hebrews 12:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:22">Hebrews 12:22</span> (and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+19:12">Exodus 19:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+4:11">Deuteronomy 4:11</span>), but it is clearly understood since the dative participles agree with it unless they be taken as descriptive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυρ</span></span> ("a palpable and kindled fire " when <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυρ</span></span> would be the dative case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσεληλυθατε</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>That might be touched </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψηλαφωμενω</span></span>). Present passive participle (dative case) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψηλαφαω</span></span>, old verb to handle, to touch (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:39">Luke 24:39</span>).</p>
<p><b>That burned with fire </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεκαυμενω πυρ</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιω</span></span>, old verb to burn, with instrumental case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυρ</span></span> (fire), unless the other view (above) is correct. </p>
