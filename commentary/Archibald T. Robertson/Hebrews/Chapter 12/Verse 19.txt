<p><b> Unto blackness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνοφω</span></span>). Dative case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνοφος</span></span> (late form for earlier <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δνοφος</span></span> and kin to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νεφος</span></span>, cloud), here only in N.T. Quoted here from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+10:22">Exodus 10:22</span>.</p>
<p><b>Darkness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζοφω</span></span>). Old word, in Homer for the gloom of the world below. In the Symmachus Version of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+10:22">Exodus 10:22</span>, also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jdg+1:6">Judges 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:4">2 Peter 2:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:15">2 Peter 2:15</span>.</p>
<p><b>Tempest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυελλη</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυω</span></span> (to boil, to rage), a hurricane, here only in N.T. From <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+10:22">Exodus 10:22</span>.</p>
<p><b>The sound of a trumpet </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαλπιγγος ηχω</span></span>). From <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+19:16">Exodus 19:16</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εχος</span></span> is an old word (our <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχο</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:25">Luke 21:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:2">Acts 2:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>The voice of words </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωνη ρηματων</span></span>). From <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+19:19">Exodus 19:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+4:12">Deuteronomy 4:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>Which voice </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ης</span></span>). Relative referring to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωνη</span></span> (voice) just before, genitive case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουσαντες</span></span> (heard, aorist active participle).</p>
<p><b>Intreated </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρηιτησαντο</span></span>). First aorist middle (indirect) indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραιτεομα</span></span>, old verb, to ask from alongside (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:6">Mark 15:6</span>), then to beg away from oneself, to depreciate as here, to decline (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:11">Acts 25:11</span>), to excuse (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:18">Luke 14:18</span>), to avoid (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:7">1 Timothy 4:7</span>).</p>
<p><b>That no word should be spoken unto them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προστεθηνα αυτοις λογον</span></span>). First aorist passive infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προστιθημ</span></span>, old word to add, here with accusative of general reference (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογον</span></span>), "that no word be added unto them." Some MSS. have here a redundant negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> with the infinitive because of the negative idea in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρηιτησαντο</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:7">Galatians 5:7</span>. </p>
