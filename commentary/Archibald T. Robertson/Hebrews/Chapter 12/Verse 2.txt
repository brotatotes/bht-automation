<p><b> Looking unto </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφορωντες εις</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφοραω</span></span>, old verb to look away, "looking away to Jesus." In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:23">Philippians 2:23</span>. Fix your eyes on Jesus, after a glance at "the cloud of witnesses," for he is the goal. Cf. Moses in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:26">Hebrews 11:26</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεβλεπεν</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>The author </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον αρχηγον</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:10">Hebrews 2:10</span> for this word. "The pioneer of personal faith" (Moffatt).</p>
<p><b>Perfecter </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελειωτην</span></span>). A word apparently coined by the writer from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελειοω</span></span> as it has been found nowhere else. Vulgate has <i>consummator</i>.</p>
<p><b>For the joy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντ της χαρας</span></span>). Answering to, in exchange for (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:16">Hebrews 12:16</span>), at the end of the race lay the joy "set before him" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προκειμενης αυτω</span></span>), while here was the Cross (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σταυρον</span></span>) at this end (the beginning of the race) which he endured (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπεμεινεν</span></span>, aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπομενω</span></span>),</p>
<p><b>despising shame </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αισχυνης καταφρονησας</span></span>). The cross at his time brought only shame (most shameful of deaths, "yea, the death of the cross" <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:8">Philippians 2:8</span>). But Jesus despised that, in spite of the momentary shrinking from it, and did his Father's will by submitting to it.</p>
<p><b>Hath sat down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεκαθικεν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθιζω</span></span>, and still is there (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:3">Hebrews 1:3</span>). </p>
