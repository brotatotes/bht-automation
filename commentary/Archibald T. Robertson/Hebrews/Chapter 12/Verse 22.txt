<p><b> But </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα</span></span>). Sharp contrast to verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:18">Hebrews 12:18</span> with same form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσεληλυθατε</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Unto Mount Zion </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Σιων ορε</span></span>). Dative case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορος</span></span>, as with the other substantives. In contrast to Mount Sinai (verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:18-21">Hebrews 12:18-21</span>). Paul has contrasted Mount Sinai (present Jerusalem) with the Jerusalem above (heaven) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:21-31">Galatians 4:21-31</span>.</p>
<p><b>City </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολε</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:10">Hebrews 11:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:16">Hebrews 11:16</span>. Heaven is termed thus a spiritual mountain and city.</p>
<p><b>The heavenly Jerusalem </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιερουσαλεμ επουρανιω</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:10">Hebrews 11:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:16">Hebrews 11:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+60:14">Isaiah 60:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>Innumerable hosts of angels </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυριασιν αγγελων</span></span>). "Myriads of angels." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μυριας</span></span> is an old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυριος</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:15">1 Corinthians 4:15</span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:1">Luke 12:1</span>. </p>
