<p><b> To the general assembly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πανηγυρε</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πας</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγυρισ, αγειρω</span></span>). Here only in N.T. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πανηγυριζω</span></span> occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+66:10">Isaiah 66:10</span> for keeping a festal holiday. Possibly to be connected with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγγελων</span></span>, though not certain.</p>
<p><b>Church of the firstborn </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκκλησια πρωτοτοκων</span></span>). Probably an additional item besides the angelic host as the people of Israel are called firstborn (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+4:22">Exodus 4:22</span>). The word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκκλησια</span></span> here has the general sense of all the redeemed, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:18">Matthew 16:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:18">Colossians 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:24-32">Ephesians 5:24-32</span>, and equivalent to the kingdom of God.</p>
<p><b>Who are enrolled in heaven </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απογεγραμμενων εν ουρανοις</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απογραφω</span></span>, old verb to write off, to copy, to enroll as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:1">Luke 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:3">Luke 2:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:5">Luke 2:5</span> (only N.T. examples). Enrolled as citizens of heaven even while on earth (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:20">Luke 10:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:27">Philippians 1:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:20">Philippians 3:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:3">Philippians 4:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:8">Revelation 13:8</span>, etc.).</p>
<p><b>To God the Judge of all </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κριτη θεω παντων</span></span>). All these chief substantives in the dative case. People should not forget that God is the Judge of all men.</p>
<p><b>Made perfect </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τετελειωμενων</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελειοω</span></span>, perfected at last (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:40">Hebrews 11:40</span>). </p>
