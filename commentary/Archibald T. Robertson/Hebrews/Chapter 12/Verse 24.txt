<p><b> To Jesus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιησου</span></span>). This great fact is not to be overlooked (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:10">Philippians 2:10</span>). He is there as Lord and Saviour and still "Jesus."</p>
<p><b>The mediator of a new covenant </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαθηκης νεας μεσιτη</span></span>). As already shown (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:22">Hebrews 7:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:6">Hebrews 8:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:8">Hebrews 8:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:9">Hebrews 8:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:10">Hebrews 8:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:15">Hebrews 9:15</span>) and now gloriously consummated.</p>
<p><b>To the blood of sprinkling </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιματ ραντισμου</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:19-28">Hebrews 9:19-28</span>.</p>
<p><b>Than Abel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα τον Αβελ</span></span>). Accusative as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:4">Hebrews 1:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>Better </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρειττον</span></span>). Comparative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλος</span></span>. Abel's blood still speaks (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:4">Hebrews 11:4</span>), but it is as nothing compared to that of Jesus. </p>
