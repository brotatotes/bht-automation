<p><b> Then shook </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσαλευσεν τοτε</span></span>). Old verb as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:7">Matthew 11:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>He hath promised </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επηγγελτα</span></span>). Perfect middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαγγελλω</span></span> and it still holds. He quotes <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="hag+2:6">Haggai 2:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Will I make to tremble </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σεισω</span></span>). Old and strong verb (here future active) <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σειω</span></span>, to agitate, to cause to tremble as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:10">Matthew 21:10</span>. The author applies this "yet once more" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετ απαξ</span></span>) and the reference to heaven (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον ουρανον</span></span>) to the second and final "shaking" at the Second Coming of Jesus Christ for judgement (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:28">Hebrews 9:28</span>). </p>
