<p><b> And this word </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το δε</span></span>). He uses the article to point out "<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετ απαξ</span></span>" which he explains (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δηλο</span></span>, signifies, present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δηλοω</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>The removing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την μεταθεσιν</span></span>). For this word see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:12">Hebrews 7:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:5">Hebrews 11:5</span>. For the transitory nature of the world see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:31">1 Corinthians 7:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:17">1 John 2:17</span>. "There is a divine purpose in the cosmic catastrophe" (Moffatt).</p>
<p><b>Made </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεποιημενων</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span>. Made by God, but made to pass away.</p>
<p><b>That those things which are not shaken may remain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μεινη τα μη σαλευομενα</span></span>). Final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> and the first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενω</span></span>. The Kingdom of God is not shaken, fearful as some saints are about it. </p>
