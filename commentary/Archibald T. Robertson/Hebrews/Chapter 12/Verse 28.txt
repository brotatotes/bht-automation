<p><b> Wherefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διο</span></span>). Ground for loyalty to Christ and for calm trust in God.</p>
<p><b>That cannot be shaken </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασαλευτον</span></span>). Old compound with alpha privative and the verbal adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαλευω</span></span> just used. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:41">Acts 27:41</span>.</p>
<p><b>Let us have grace </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχωμεν χαριν</span></span>). Present active volitive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>, "Let us keep on having grace" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:16">Hebrews 4:16</span>, though it can mean "Let us keep on having gratitude" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:9">Luke 17:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>Whereby </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι' ης</span></span>). That is <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια χαριτος</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>We may offer service </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λατρευωμεν</span></span>). This subjunctive in a relative clause can be volitive like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχωμεν</span></span> just before (cf. imperative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στητε</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:12">1 Peter 5:12</span>) or it might be the futuristic subjunctive as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:3">Hebrews 8:3</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο προσενεγκη</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Well pleasing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευαρεστως</span></span>). Old compound adverb, here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>With reverence and awe </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα ευλαβειας κα δεους</span></span>). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλαβεια</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:7">Hebrews 5:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:7">Hebrews 11:7</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Δεος</span></span> is apprehension of danger as in a forest. "When the voice and tread of a wild beast are distinctly heard close at hand the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεος</span></span> becomes <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φοβος</span></span>" (Vincent). </p>
