<p><b> Resisted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντικατεστητε</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative (intransitive) of the double compound <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντικαθιστημ</span></span>, old verb to stand in opposition against in line of battle, intransitively to stand face to face (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντ</span></span>) against (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span>), here only in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>Unto blood </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεχρις αιματος</span></span>). "Up to blood." As was true of Jesus and many of the other heroes of faith in chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:11">Hebrews 12:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>Striving </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανταγωνιζομενο</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανταγωνιζομα</span></span>, old verb with the same figure in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντικατεστητε</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Against sin </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς αμαρτιαν</span></span>). Face to face with sin as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:1">Hebrews 12:1</span>. </p>
