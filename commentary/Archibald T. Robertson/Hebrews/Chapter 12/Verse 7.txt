<p><b> That ye endure </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπομενετε</span></span>). Present active indicative or present active imperative and so just "endure for chastening."</p>
<p><b>Dealeth with you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμιν προσφερετα</span></span>). Present middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσφερω</span></span>, but this sense of bearing oneself towards one with the dative here only in the N.T., though often in the older Greek.</p>
<p><b>What </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τις</span></span>). Interrogative.</p>
<p><b>Whom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ον</span></span>). Relative. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:9">Matthew 7:9</span>. </p>
