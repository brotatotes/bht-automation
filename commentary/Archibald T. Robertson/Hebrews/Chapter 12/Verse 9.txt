<p><b> Furthermore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειτα</span></span>). The next step in the argument (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:17">Mark 4:17</span>).</p>
<p><b>We had </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειχομεν</span></span>). Imperfect indicative of customary action, "we used to have."</p>
<p><b>To chasten us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παιδευτας</span></span>). Predicate accusative after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειχομεν</span></span>, "as chasteners." Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παιδευω</span></span>, as agent (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-της</span></span>). Only once in LXX (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ho+5:2">Hosea 5:2</span>) and twice in N.T. (here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:20">Romans 2:20</span>).</p>
<p><b>We gave them reverence </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενετρεπομεθα</span></span>). Imperfect middle of old word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντρεπω</span></span>, to turn in or at. Here "we turned ourselves to" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:37">Matthew 21:37</span>, habitual attitude of reverence.</p>
<p><b>Shall we be in subjection </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποταγησομεθα</span></span>). Second future passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποτασσω</span></span>. There is no <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span> here to correspond to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεν</span></span> in the first part of the verse.</p>
<p><b>Unto the father of spirits </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω πατρ των πνευματων</span></span>). Rather, "Unto the Father of our spirits" (note article <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον</span></span>). As God is. </p>
