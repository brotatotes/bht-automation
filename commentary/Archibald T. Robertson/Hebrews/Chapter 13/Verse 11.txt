<p><b> Of those beasts whose blood </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ων ζωων το αιμα τουτων</span></span>). The antecedent (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωων</span></span>) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ων</span></span> is here incorporated and attracted into the case of the relative, "the blood of which beasts" and then <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτων</span></span> (genitive demonstrative) is added, "of these." Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="le+4:12">Leviticus 4:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="le+4:21">Leviticus 4:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="le+16:27">Leviticus 16:27</span> for the Old Testament ritual in such cases. This is the only example in the LXX or N.T. where <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωων</span></span> (animal) is used of a sacrificial victim. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+29:14">Exodus 29:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+32:26">Exodus 32:26</span> for burning without the camp. </p>
