<p><b> Wherefore Jesus also </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διο κα Ιησους</span></span>). The parallel is drawn between the O.T. ritual and the better sacrifice of Jesus already discussed (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:13-10">Hebrews 9:13-10</span>). The purpose of Jesus is shown (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα αγιαση</span></span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγιαζω</span></span>, to sanctify), the means employed (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια του ιδιου αιματος</span></span>, by his own blood), the place of his suffering (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαθεν</span></span>, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:8">Hebrews 5:8</span>) is also given (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξω της πυλης</span></span>, outside the gate, implied in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:17">John 19:17</span>) which phrase corresponds to "outside the camp" of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:11">Hebrews 13:11</span>. </p>
