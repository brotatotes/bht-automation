<p><b> Through him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι' αυτου</span></span>). That is Jesus. He is our Priest and Sacrifice, the only efficient and sufficient one.</p>
<p><b>Let us offer up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναφερωμεν</span></span>). Present active volitive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναφερω</span></span>, "let us keep on offering up." Jesus is living and let us go to him.</p>
<p><b>A sacrifice of praise </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυσιαν αινεσεως</span></span>). This phrase occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="le+7:12">Leviticus 7:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+54:8">Psalms 54:8</span>. The word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αινεσις</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αινεω</span></span>, to praise), common in LXX, is only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>The fruit of lips </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καρπον χειλεων</span></span>). In apposition (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτ 'εστιν</span></span>) and explanation of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυσιαν αινεσεως</span></span>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ho+14:3">Hosea 14:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+57:19">Isaiah 57:19</span>.</p>
<p><b>Which made confession to his name </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομολογουντων τω ονοματ αυτου</span></span>). This use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομολογεω</span></span> with the dative in the sense of praise like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξομολογεω</span></span> is unique, though the papyri furnish examples in the sense of gratitude (Moulton and Milligan, <i>Vocabulary</i>). </p>
