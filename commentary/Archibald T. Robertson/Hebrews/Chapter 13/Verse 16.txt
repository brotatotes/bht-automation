<p><b> To do good </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της ευποιιας</span></span>). Genitive case. Late compound from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευποιος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευποιεω</span></span>), common in Epictetus, but here only in N.T., a doing good.</p>
<p><b>To communicate </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινωνιας</span></span>). Genitive case. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:13">2 Corinthians 9:13</span> for use for contribution, beneficence. Moffatt notes that the three great definitions of worship and religious service in the N.T. (here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:1">Romans 12:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:27">James 1:27</span>) are all inward and ethical.</p>
<p><b>Forget not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη επιλανθανεσθε</span></span>). Prohibition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> and the present middle imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλανθανω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:10">Hebrews 6:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:2">Hebrews 13:2</span>). Here with the genitive case.</p>
<p><b>Is well pleased </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευαρεστειτα</span></span>). Present passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευαρεστεω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:5">Hebrews 11:5</span>). With the associative instrumental case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυσιαις</span></span> (sacrifices). </p>
