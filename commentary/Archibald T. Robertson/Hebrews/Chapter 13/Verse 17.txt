<p><b> Obey </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειθεσθε</span></span>). Present middle imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειθω</span></span> with dative case.</p>
<p><b>Submit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπεικετε</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπεικω</span></span>, old compound to yield under, to give up. Here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>They watch </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγρυπνουσιν</span></span>). Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγρυπνεω</span></span> old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγρεω</span></span>, to search, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπνος</span></span>, sleep), to seek after sleep, to be sleepless, be watchful (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:33">Mark 13:33</span>).</p>
<p><b>As they that shall give account </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως λογον αποδωσοντες</span></span>). Regular Greek idiom with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως</span></span> and the future participle. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογον αποδιδωμ</span></span>, to render account, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:36">Matthew 12:36</span>. These leaders as good shepherds recognize keenly their responsibility for the welfare of the flock.</p>
<p><b>And not with grief </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα μη στεναζοντες</span></span>). "And not groaning" (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:23">Romans 8:23</span>).</p>
<p><b>Unprofitable </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλυσιτελες</span></span>). Old double compound adjective (alpha privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυσιτελης</span></span> and this from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυω</span></span>, to pay, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελος</span></span>, tax, useful or profitable as <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:2">Luke 17:2</span>), not profitable, not advantageous, by <i>litotes</i>, hurtful, pernicious. Common rhetorical <i>litotes</i>, here only in N.T. </p>
