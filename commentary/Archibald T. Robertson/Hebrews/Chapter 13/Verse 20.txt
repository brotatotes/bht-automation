<p><b> The God of peace </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο θεος της ειρηνης</span></span>). God is the author and giver of peace, a Pauline phrase (6 times) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:23">1 Thessalonians 5:23</span>.</p>
<p><b>Who brought again from the dead </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αναγαγων εκ νεκρων</span></span>). Second aorist active articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγω</span></span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:7">Romans 10:7</span>), the only direct mention of the resurrection of Jesus in the Epistle, though implied often (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:3">Hebrews 1:3</span>, etc.).</p>
<p><b>That great shepherd of the sheep </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον ποιμενα των προβατων τον μεγαν</span></span>). This phrase occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+63:11">Isaiah 63:11</span> except <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον μεγαν</span></span> which the author adds as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:14">Hebrews 4:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:21">Hebrews 10:21</span>. So here, "the shepherd of the sheep the great one."</p>
<p><b>With the blood of the eternal covenant </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αιματ διαθηκης αιωνιου</span></span>). This language is from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="zec+9:11">Zechariah 9:11</span>. The language reminds us of Christ's own words in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:24">Mark 14:24</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:28">Matthew 26:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:20">Luke 22:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:25">1 Corinthians 11:25</span>) about "my blood of the covenant." </p>
