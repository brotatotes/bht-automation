<p><b> Make you perfect </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταρτισα</span></span>). First aorist active optative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταρτιζω</span></span>, to equip, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:5">Hebrews 10:5</span>. A wish for the future. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:10">1 Corinthians 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+13:11">2 Corinthians 13:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:17">2 Timothy 3:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>Working in us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιων εν εμιν</span></span>). "Doing in us." Some MSS. read "in you."</p>
<p><b>Well-pleasing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευαρεστον</span></span>). Compound adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ, αρεστος</span></span>). Usually with the dative (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:2">Romans 12:2</span>), here with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενωπιον αυτου</span></span> more like the Hebrew. This is one of the noblest doxologies in the N.T. </p>
