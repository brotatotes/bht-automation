<p><b> Bear with </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεχεσθε</span></span>). Present middle imperative (some MSS. have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεχεσθα</span></span>, infinitive) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεχω</span></span> with the ablative, "hold yourselves back from" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:13">Colossians 3:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>The word of exhortation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του λογου της παρακλησεως</span></span>). His description of the entire Epistle. It certainly is that, a powerful appeal in fact.</p>
<p><b>I have written </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεστειλα</span></span>). First aorist active indicative (epistolary aorist) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιστελλω</span></span>, old word to send a letter (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιστολη</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:20">Acts 15:20</span>.</p>
<p><b>In few words </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια βραχεων</span></span>). Common Greek idiom, here only in N.T. (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βραχυς</span></span>, brief, short). Cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι' ολιγων εγραψα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:12">1 Peter 5:12</span>. </p>
