<p><b> Let marriage be </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο γαμος</span></span>). No verb in the Greek. The copula can be supplied either <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστιν</span></span> (is) or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστω</span></span> (let be, imperative).</p>
<p><b>Had in honour </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιμιος</span></span>). Old adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιμη</span></span> (honour) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:34">Acts 5:34</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Γαμος</span></span> elsewhere in the N.T., means the wedding or wedding feast (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:29">Matthew 22:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:1">John 2:1</span>).</p>
<p><b>Undefiled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμιαντος</span></span>). Old compound word (alpha privative and verbal of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιαινω</span></span>, to defile), already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:26">Hebrews 7:26</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μιαινω την κοιτην</span></span> is a common expression for adultery.</p>
<p><b>Fornicators </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορνους</span></span>). Unmarried and impure.</p>
<p><b>Adulterers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μοιχους</span></span>). Impure married persons. God will judge both classes whether men do or not. </p>
