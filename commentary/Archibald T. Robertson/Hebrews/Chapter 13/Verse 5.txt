<p><b> Be ye free from the love of money </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφιλαργυρος ο τροπος</span></span>). No copula, but supply <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστο</span></span>: "Let your manner of life (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τροπος</span></span>, way, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:37">Matthew 23:37</span>), be without love of money" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφιλαργυρος</span></span>, double compound), once found only in the N.T., here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:3">1 Timothy 3:3</span>, but now several times--or the adverb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφιλαργυρως</span></span> --in papyri and inscriptions (Deissmann, <i>Light</i>, etc., pp. 85f.). Alpha privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλος</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αργυρος</span></span>. The N.T. is full of the peril of money on the character as modern life is also.</p>
<p><b>Content with such things as ye have </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρκουμενο τοις παρουσιν</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρκεω</span></span>, to suffice, to be content as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:14">Luke 3:14</span>. Cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυταρκης</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:11">Philippians 4:11</span>. Here in the nominative plural with no substantive or pronoun (anacoluthon, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:7">2 Corinthians 1:7</span>) or the participle used as a principal verb as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:16">Romans 12:16</span>. "Contented with the present things" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις παρουσιν</span></span>, associative instrumental case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα παροντα</span></span>, present active neuter plural participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρειμ</span></span>, to be present or on hand).</p>
<p><b>For himself hath said </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτος γαρ ειρηκεν</span></span>). God himself as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:33">Acts 20:33</span> of Christ. Perfect active indicative as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:13">Hebrews 1:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:3">Hebrews 4:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:9">Hebrews 10:9</span>. The quotation is a free paraphrase of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+28:15">Genesis 28:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+31:8">Deuteronomy 31:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jos+1:5">Joshua 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ch+28:20">1 Chronicles 28:20</span>. Philo (de Confus. Ling. 32) has it in this form, "a popular paraphrase" (Moffatt). Note the five negatives strengthening each other (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη</span></span> with the second aorist active subjunctive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανω</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανιημ</span></span>, to relate, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:26">Acts 16:26</span>; <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδ' ου μη</span></span> with second aorist active subjunctive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγκαταλιπω</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγκαταλειπω</span></span>, to leave behind, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:46">Matthew 27:46</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:10">2 Timothy 4:10</span>). A noble promise in times of depression. </p>
