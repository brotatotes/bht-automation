<p><b> So that we say </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωστε ημας λεγειν</span></span>). The usual construction (the infinitive) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωστε</span></span> in the <i>Koine</i> even when the idea is result instead of purpose. The accusative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημας</span></span> is that of general reference.</p>
<p><b>With good courage </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θαρρουντας</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θαρρεω</span></span> (Ionic and early Attic <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θαρσεω</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:2">Matthew 9:2</span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:6">2 Corinthians 5:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:8">2 Corinthians 5:8</span>. The accusative agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημας</span></span>, "being of good courage." The quotation is from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+118:6">Psalms 118:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>My helper </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμο βοηθος</span></span>). "Helper to me" (ethical dative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμο</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Βοηθος</span></span> is old adjective (cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βοηθεω</span></span>, to help, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:18">Hebrews 2:18</span>), often in LXX as substantive, here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>I will not fear </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου φοβηθησομα</span></span>). Volitive first future passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φοβεομα</span></span>. </p>
