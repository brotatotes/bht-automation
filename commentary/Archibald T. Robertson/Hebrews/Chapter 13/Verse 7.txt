<p><b> Remember </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μνημονευετε</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μνημονευω</span></span>, old verb to be <i>mindful</i> of (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μνημων</span></span>, mindful) with genitive (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:20">John 15:20</span>) or accusative (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:9">Matthew 16:9</span>). "Keep in mind." Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:22">Hebrews 11:22</span>.</p>
<p><b>Them that had the rule over you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των ηγουμενων υμων</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγεομα</span></span> with genitive of the person (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμων</span></span>) as in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:17">Hebrews 13:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:24">Hebrews 13:24</span>. The author reminds them of the founders of their church in addition to the long list of heroes in chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:11">Hebrews 13:11</span>. See a like exhortation to respect and follow their leaders in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:12">1 Thessalonians 5:12</span>. Few lessons are harder for the average Christian to learn, viz., good following.</p>
<p><b>The word of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον λογον του θεου</span></span>). The preaching of these early disciples, apostles, and prophets (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:17">1 Corinthians 1:17</span>).</p>
<p><b>And considering the issue of their life </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ων αναθεωρουντες την εκβασιν της αναστροφης</span></span>). No "and" in the Greek, but the relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ων</span></span> (whose) in the genitive case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναστροφης</span></span>, "considering the issue of whose life." Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναθεωρεω</span></span>, late compound, to look up a subject, to investigate, to observe accurately, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:23">Acts 17:23</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εκβασις</span></span> is an old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκβαινω</span></span>, to go out (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:15">Hebrews 11:15</span>, here only in N.T.), originally way out (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:13">1 Corinthians 10:13</span>), but here (only other N.T. example) in sense of end or issue as in several papyri examples (Moulton and Milligan, <i>Vocabulary</i>).</p>
<p><b>Imitate their faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιμεισθε την πιστιν</span></span>). Present middle imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιμεομα</span></span>, old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιμος</span></span>, actor, mimic), in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:7">2 Thessalonians 3:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:9">2 Thessalonians 3:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:11">3 John 1:11</span>. Keep on imitating the faith of the leaders. </p>
