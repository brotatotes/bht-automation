<p><b> He that sanctifieth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αγιαζων</span></span>). Present active articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγιαζω</span></span>. Jesus is the sanctifier (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:13">Hebrews 9:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:12">Hebrews 13:12</span>).</p>
<p><b>They that are sanctified </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αγιαζομενο</span></span>). Present passive articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγιαζω</span></span>. It is a process here as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:14">Hebrews 10:14</span>, not a single act, though in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:10">Hebrews 10:10</span> the perfect passive indicative presents a completed state.</p>
<p><b>Of one </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ ενος</span></span>). Referring to God as the Father of Jesus and of the "many sons" above (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:10">Hebrews 2:10</span>) and in harmony with verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:14">Hebrews 2:14</span> below. Even before the incarnation Jesus had a kinship with men though we are not sons in the full sense that he is.</p>
<p><b>He is not ashamed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ επαισχυνετα</span></span>). Present passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαισχυνομα</span></span>, old compound (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:16">Romans 1:16</span>). Because of the common Father Jesus is not ashamed to own us as "brothers" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδελφους</span></span>), unworthy sons though we be. </p>
