<p><b> In that </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ω</span></span>). Literally, "In which" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">=εν τουτω εν ω</span></span>, in that in which), a causal idea, though in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:22">Romans 14:22</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ω</span></span> means "wherein."</p>
<p><b>Hath suffered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπονθεν</span></span>). Second perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πασχω</span></span>, permanent part of Christ's experience.</p>
<p><b>Being tempted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειρασθεις</span></span>). First aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειραζω</span></span>. The temptation to escape the shame of the Cross was early and repeatedly presented to Christ, by Satan in the wilderness (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:8-11">Matthew 4:8-11</span>), by Peter in the spirit of Satan (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:22">Matthew 16:22</span>), in Gethsemane (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:39">Matthew 26:39</span>), and caused intense suffering to Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:44">Luke 22:44</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:8">Hebrews 5:8</span>).</p>
<p><b>He is able </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυνατα</span></span>). This word strikes the heart of it all. Christ's power to help is due not merely to his deity as God's Son, but also to his humanity without which he could not sympathize with us (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:15">Hebrews 4:15</span>).</p>
<p><b>To succour </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βοηθησα</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of the old compound verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βοηθεω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βοη</span></span>, a cry, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεω</span></span>, to run), to run at a cry or call for help (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:25">Matthew 15:25</span>).</p>
<p><b>Them that are tempted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις πειραζομενοις</span></span>). Dative plural of the articular participle (present passive) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειραζω</span></span>. These Jewish Christians were daily tempted to give up Christ, to apostatize from Christianity. Jesus understands himself (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτος</span></span>) their predicament and is able to help them to be faithful. </p>
