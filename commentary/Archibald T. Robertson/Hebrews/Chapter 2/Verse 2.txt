<p><b> For if ... proved steadfast </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε γαρ ... εγενετο βεβαιος</span></span>). Condition of first class, assumed as true.</p>
<p><b>Through angels </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι' αγγελων</span></span>). Allusion to the use of angels by God at Sinai as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:38">Acts 7:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:53">Acts 7:53</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:19">Galatians 3:19</span>, though not in the O.T., but in Josephus (<i>Ant</i>. XV. 156).</p>
<p><b>Transgression and disobedience </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραβασις κα παρακοη</span></span>). Both words use <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span> as in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραρυωμεν</span></span>, refused to obey (stepping aside, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα-βασις</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:23">Romans 2:23</span>), neglect to obey (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρ-ακοη</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:19">Romans 5:19</span>), more than a mere hendiadys.</p>
<p><b>Recompense of reward </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μισθαποδοσιαν</span></span>). Late double compound, like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μισθαποδοτης</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:6">Hebrews 11:6</span>), from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μισθος</span></span> (reward) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποδιδωμ</span></span>, to give back. The old Greeks used <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μισθοδοσια</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Just </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδικον</span></span>). Old compound adjective, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:8">Romans 3:8</span>. </p>
