<p><b> How shall we escape? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πως ημεις εκφευξομεθα;</span></span>). Rhetorical question with future middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκφευγω</span></span> and conclusion of the condition.</p>
<p><b>If we neglect </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμελησαντες</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμελεω</span></span>, "having neglected."</p>
<p><b>So great salvation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηλικαυτης σωτηριας</span></span>). Ablative case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμελησαντες</span></span>. Correlative pronoun of age, but used of size in the N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:4">James 3:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:10">2 Corinthians 1:10</span>).</p>
<p><b>Which </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ητις</span></span>). "Which very salvation," before described, now summarized.</p>
<p><b>Having at the first been spoken </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρχην λαβουσα λαλεισθα</span></span>). Literally, "having received a beginning to be spoken," "having begun to be spoken," a common literary <i>Koine</i> idiom (Polybius, etc.).</p>
<p><b>Through the Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια του κυριου</span></span>). The Lord Jesus who is superior to angels. Jesus was God's full revelation and he is the source of this new and superior revelation.</p>
<p><b>Was confirmed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβεβαιωθη</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βεβαιοω</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βεβαιος</span></span> (stable), old verb as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:6">1 Corinthians 1:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>By them that heard </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο των ακουσαντων</span></span>). Ablative case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο</span></span> of the articular first aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουω</span></span>. Those who heard the Lord Jesus. Only one generation between Jesus and the writer. Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:11">Galatians 1:11</span>) got his message directly from Christ. </p>
