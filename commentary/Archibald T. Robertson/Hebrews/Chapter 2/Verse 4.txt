<p><b> God also bearing witness with them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεπιμαρτυρουντος του θεου</span></span>). Genitive absolute with the present active participle of the late double compound verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεπιμαρτυρεω</span></span>, to join (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν</span></span>) in giving additional (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>) testimony (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρεω</span></span>). Here only in N.T., but in Aristotle, Polybius, Plutarch.</p>
<p><b>Both by signs </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σημειοις τε κα</span></span>)</p>
<p><b>and wonders </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα τερασιν</span></span>)</p>
<p><b>and by manifold powers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ποικιλαις δυναμεσιν</span></span>)</p>
<p><b>and by gifts of the Holy Ghost </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα πνευματος αγιου μερισμοις</span></span>). Instrumental case used with all four items. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:22">Acts 2:22</span> for the three words for miracles in inverse order (powers, wonders, signs). Each word adds an idea about the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργα</span></span> (works) of Christ. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Τερας</span></span> (wonder) attracts attention, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυναμις</span></span> (power) shows God's power, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σημειον</span></span> reveals the purpose of God in the miracles. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποικιλαις</span></span> (manifold, many-coloured) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:24">Matthew 4:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:2">James 1:2</span>. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μερισμος</span></span> for distribution (old word, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:12">Hebrews 4:12</span>) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:4-30">1 Corinthians 12:4-30</span>.</p>
<p><b>According to his own will </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα την αυτου θελησιν</span></span>). The word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θελησις</span></span> is called a vulgarism by Pollux. The writer is fond of words in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-ις</span></span>. </p>
