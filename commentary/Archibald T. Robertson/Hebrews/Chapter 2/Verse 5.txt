<p><b> For not unto angels </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου γαρ αγγελοις</span></span>). The author now proceeds to show (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:5-18">Hebrews 2:5-18</span>) that the very humanity of Jesus, the Son of Man, likewise proves his superiority to angels.</p>
<p><b>The world to come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την οικουμενην την μελλουσαν</span></span>). The new order, the salvation just described. See a like use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μελλω</span></span> (as participle) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωτηρια</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:14">Hebrews 1:14</span>), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιων</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:4">Hebrews 6:4</span>), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαθα</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:11">Hebrews 9:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:1">Hebrews 10:1</span>), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολις</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:14">Hebrews 13:14</span>).</p>
<p><b>Whereof we speak </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ ης λαλουμεν</span></span>). The author is discussing this new order introduced by Christ which makes obsolete the old dispensation of rites and symbols. God did not put this new order in charge of angels. </p>
