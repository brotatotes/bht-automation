<p><b> But one somewhere </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε που τις</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:4">Hebrews 4:4</span> for a like indefinite quotation. Philo uses this "literary mannerism" (Moffatt). He quotes <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+8:5-7">Psalms 8:5-7</span> and extends here to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:8">Hebrews 2:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>Hath testified </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διεμαρτυρατο</span></span>). First aorist middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαμαρτυρομα</span></span>, old verb to testify vigorously (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:40">Acts 2:40</span>).</p>
<p><b>What </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Τ</span></span>). Neuter, not masculine <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τις</span></span> (who). The insignificance of man is implied.</p>
<p><b>The son of man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υιος ανθρωπου</span></span>). Not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο υιος του ανθρωπου</span></span> which Jesus used so often about himself, but literally here "son of man" like the same words so often in Ezekiel, without Messianic meaning here.</p>
<p><b>Visited </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισκεπτη</span></span>). Second person singular present indicative middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισκεπτομα</span></span>, old verb to look upon, to look after, to go to see (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:36">Matthew 25:36</span>), from which verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισχοπος</span></span>, overseer, bishop, comes. </p>
