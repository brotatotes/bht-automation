<p><b> In that he subjected </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω υποταξα</span></span>). First aorist active articular infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπατασσω</span></span> in the locative case, "in the subjecting."</p>
<p><b>He left </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφηκεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative (kappa aorist) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφιημ</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Nothing that is not subject to him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδεν αυτω ανυποτακτον</span></span>). Later verbal of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποτασσω</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative. Here in passive sense, active sense in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:9">1 Timothy 1:9</span>. Man's sovereignty was meant to be all-inclusive including the administration of "the world to come." "He is crowned king of nature, invested with a divine authority over creation" (Moffatt). But how far short of this destiny has man come!</p>
<p><b>But now we see not yet </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυν δε ουπω ορωμεν</span></span>). Not even today in the wonderful twentieth century with man's triumphs over nature has he reached that goal, wonderful as are the researches by the help of telescope and microscope, the mechanism of the airplane, the submarine, steam, electricity, radio. </p>
