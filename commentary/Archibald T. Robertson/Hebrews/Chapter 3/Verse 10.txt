<p><b> And saw </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ειδον</span></span>). "And yet saw."</p>
<p><b>Wherefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διο</span></span>). Not in the LXX, but it makes clear the argument in the Psalm.</p>
<p><b>I was displeased </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσωχθισα</span></span>). First aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσοχθιζω</span></span>, late compound for extreme anger and disgust. In N.T. only here and verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:17">Hebrews 3:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>Err </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλανωντα</span></span>). Present middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλαναω</span></span>, to wander astray, common verb.</p>
<p><b>They did not know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ εγνωσαν</span></span>). In spite of God's works (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργα</span></span>) and loving patience the Israelites failed to understand God's ways with them. Are we any better? They "cared not to take my road" (Moffatt). </p>
