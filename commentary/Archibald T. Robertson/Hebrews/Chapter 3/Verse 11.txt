<p><b> As I sware </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως ωμοσα</span></span>). "Correlating the oath and the disobedience" (Vincent). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομνυω</span></span>, old verb for solemn oath (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:13">Hebrews 6:13</span>).</p>
<p><b>They shall not enter </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε εισελευσοντα</span></span>). Future middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισερχομα</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span> as an anacoluthon for the Hebrew <i>im</i> (not). Really it is a condition of the first class with the conclusion not expressed, common in the LXX as here (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 1024).</p>
<p><b>Into my rest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις την καταπαυσιν μου</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταπαυω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:8">Hebrews 4:8</span>), to give rest, in LXX, in N.T. only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:49">Acts 7:49</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:11-4">Hebrews 3:11-4</span>. Primarily the rest in Canaan and then the heavenly rest in which God dwells. </p>
