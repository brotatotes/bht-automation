<p><b> So long as it is called today </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχρις ου το σημερον καλειτα</span></span>). The only instance in the N.T. of this conjunction (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχρ</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχρις</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχρις ου</span></span>, etc.) with the present indicative in the sense of "so long as" or "while" like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως</span></span>. Elsewhere it means "until" and with either the aorist indicative (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:18">Acts 7:18</span>), the future (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:17">Revelation 17:17</span>), or the aorist subjunctive (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:3">Revelation 7:3</span>).</p>
<p><b>Lest any one of you be hardened </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη σκληρυνθη τις εξ υμων</span></span>). Negative purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη</span></span> (that not) and the first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκληρυνω</span></span>, the vivid verb from verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:8">Hebrews 3:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>By the deceitfulness of sin </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απατη της αμαρτιας</span></span>). Instrumental case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απατη</span></span> (trick, fraud) as is always the case with sin (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:11">Romans 7:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:10">2 Thessalonians 2:10</span>). Apostasy (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:4">Hebrews 12:4</span>) is their peril and it is a trick of sin. </p>
