<p><b> Let us fear therefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φοβηθωμεν ουν</span></span>). First aorist passive volitive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φοβεομα</span></span>, to be afraid. There is no break in the argument on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:95">Hebrews 4:95</span>. This is a poor chapter division. The Israelites perished because of disbelief. We today face a real peril.</p>
<p><b>Lest haply </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη ποτε</span></span>) Here with the present subjunctive (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκε</span></span>), but future indicative in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:12">Hebrews 3:12</span>, after the verb of fearing. For the optative see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:25">2 Timothy 2:25</span>.</p>
<p><b>A promise being left </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλειπομενης επαγγελιας</span></span>). Genitive absolute of the present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλειπω</span></span>, to leave behind. God's promise still holds good for us in spite of the failure of the Israelites.</p>
<p><b>Should seem to have come short of it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκε υστερηκενα</span></span>). Perfect active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υστερεω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υστερος</span></span> (comparative of root <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υδ</span></span> like our out, outer, outermost), to be too late, to fail to reach the goal as here, common in the N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:37">Hebrews 11:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:15">Hebrews 12:15</span>). </p>
