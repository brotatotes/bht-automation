<p><b> Let us therefore give diligence </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπουδασωμεν ουν</span></span>). Volitive subjunctive aorist of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπουδαζω</span></span>, old verb to hasten (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:9">2 Timothy 4:9</span>), to be eager and alert (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:17">1 Thessalonians 2:17</span>). The exhortation has a warning like that in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:1">Hebrews 4:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>That no man fall </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη πεση</span></span>). Negative purpose with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη</span></span> and the second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιπτω</span></span>, to fall.</p>
<p><b>After the same example of disobedience </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω αυτω υποδειγματ της απειθειας</span></span>). The unbelief is like that seen in the Israelites (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:12">Hebrews 3:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:18">Hebrews 3:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:2">Hebrews 4:2</span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hυποδειγμα</span></span> is a late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποδεικνυμ</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:7">Matthew 3:7</span>) and means a copy (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:15">John 13:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:10">James 5:10</span>). The Israelites set a terrible example and it is so easy to copy the bad examples. </p>
