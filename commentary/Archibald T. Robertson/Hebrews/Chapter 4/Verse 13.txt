<p><b> That is not manifest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφανης</span></span>). Old adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φαινω</span></span>, to show), here only in the N.T. God's microscope can lay bare the smallest microbe of doubt and sin.</p>
<p><b>Naked </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γυμνα</span></span>). Both soul and body are naked to the eye of God.</p>
<p><b>Laid open </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τετραχηλισμενα</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τραχηλιζω</span></span>, late verb to bend back the neck (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τραχηλος</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:6">Matthew 18:6</span>) as the surgeon does for operating, here only in N.T. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:4">Romans 16:4</span> for the peril of risking one's neck (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τραχηλον υποτιθενα</span></span>). God's eyes see all the facts in our inmost hearts. There are no mental reservations from God.</p>
<p><b>With whom we have to do </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς ον ημιν ο λογος</span></span>). "With whom the matter or account for us is." There is a slight play here on <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογος</span></span> of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:12">Hebrews 4:12</span>. Surely every servant of Christ today needs to gaze into this revealing mirror and be honest with himself and God. </p>
