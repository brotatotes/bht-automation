<p><b> Let us therefore draw near </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσερχωμεθα ουν</span></span>). Present active middle volitive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσερχομα</span></span>. "Let us keep on coming to" our high priest, this sympathizing and great high priest. Instead of deserting him, let us make daily use of him. This verb in Hebrews means reverent approach for worship (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:25">Hebrews 7:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:1">Hebrews 10:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:22">Hebrews 10:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:6">Hebrews 11:6</span>).</p>
<p><b>Unto the throne of grace </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω θρονω της χαριτος</span></span>). This old word (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θρονος</span></span>) we have taken into English, the seat of kings and of God and so of Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:3">Hebrews 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:8">Hebrews 1:8</span>), but marked by grace because Jesus is there (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:28">Matthew 19:28</span>). Hence we should come "with boldness" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα παρρησιας</span></span>). Telling Jesus the whole story of our shortcomings.</p>
<p><b>That we may receive mercy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα λαβωμεν ελεος</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>And find grace </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα χαριν ευρωμεν</span></span>). Second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρισκω</span></span>. We are sure to gain both of these aims because Jesus is our high priest on the throne.</p>
<p><b>To help us in time of need </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις ευκαιρον βοηθειαν</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Βοηθεια</span></span> is old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βοηθεω</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:18">Hebrews 2:18</span> which see), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:17">Acts 27:17</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ευκαιρος</span></span> is an old word also (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ</span></span>, well, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιρος</span></span>, opportunity), only here in N.T. "For well-timed help," "for help in the nick of time," before too late. </p>
