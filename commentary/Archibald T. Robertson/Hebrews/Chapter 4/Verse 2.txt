<p><b> For indeed we have had good tidings preached unto us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα γαρ εσμεν ευηγγελισμενο εσμεν</span></span>). Periphrastic perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευαγγελιζω</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευαγγελιον</span></span>, good news, glad tidings) to bring good news, used here in its original sense as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:6">Hebrews 4:6</span> of the Israelites (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευαγγελισθεντες</span></span> first aorist passive participle).</p>
<p><b>Even as also they </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθαπερ κακεινο</span></span>). See verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:6">Hebrews 4:6</span>. We have the promise of rest as the Israelites had. The parallel holds as to the promise, the privilege, the penalty.</p>
<p><b>The word of hearing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο λογος της ακοης</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:13">1 Thessalonians 2:13</span>. Genitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακοης</span></span> describing <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογος</span></span>, the word marked by hearing (the word heard).</p>
<p><b>Because they were not united by faith with them that heard </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη συνκεκερασμενους τη πιστε τοις ακουσασιν</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μη</span></span>, the usual negative of the participle. A very difficult phrase. The text is uncertain whether the participle (perfect passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνκεραννυμ</span></span>, old verb to mix together) ends in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-ος</span></span> agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογος</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-ους</span></span> agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινους</span></span> (them). Taking it in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-ους</span></span> the translation is correct. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πιστε</span></span> is in the instrumental case and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις ακουσασιν</span></span> in the associative instrumental after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν</span></span>. </p>
