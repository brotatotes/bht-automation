<p><b> Teachers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδασκαλο</span></span>). Predicate nominative after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εινα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>By reason of the time </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια τον χρονον</span></span>). Alas, what a commentary on modern Christians.</p>
<p><b>That some one teach you the rudiments </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του διδασκειν υμας τινα τα στοιχεια</span></span>). Neat Greek idiom, genitive case of the articular infinitive (need of the teaching) with two accusatives of the person (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμας</span></span>, you) and the thing (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα στοιχεια</span></span>, the rudiments) and the accusative of general reference (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τινα</span></span>, as to some one). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στοιχεια</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:3">Galatians 4:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:9">Galatians 4:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:8">Colossians 2:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>Of the first principles of the oracles of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της αρχης των λογιων του θεου</span></span>). Three genitives linked to each other. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αρχης</span></span> (beginning) illustrates <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα στοιχεια</span></span>, just before, the A B C of Christian teaching like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:1">Hebrews 6:1</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Λογιον</span></span> is a diminutive of logos, divine oracles being usually brief, common in the O.T. and Philo for God's words, in N.T. used for the O.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:38">Acts 7:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:2">Romans 3:2</span>), of God's word through Christians (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:11">1 Peter 4:11</span>), of the substance of Christian teaching (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:12">Hebrews 5:12</span>).</p>
<p><b>Of milk </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γαλακτος</span></span>). Because still babes (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:2">1 Corinthians 3:2</span>) and not able to chew "solid food" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στερεας τροφης</span></span>), without intellectual and spiritual teeth. </p>
