<p><b> For full-grown men </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελειων</span></span>). Predicate genitive. The word is for adults, relative perfection (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελειο</span></span>) in contrast with babes as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:6">1 Corinthians 2:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:1">1 Corinthians 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:11">1 Corinthians 13:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:15">Philippians 3:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:4">Ephesians 4:4</span>, not absolute perfection (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:48">Matthew 5:48</span>).</p>
<p><b>Their senses </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα αισθητηρια</span></span>). The organs of perception (Stoic term for sense organs) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αισθανομα</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:45">Luke 9:45</span>), in Plato, Galen, Hippocrates, here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Exercised </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεγυμνασμενα</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γυμναζω</span></span>, to exercise (naked, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γυμνος</span></span>). Galen uses <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αισθητηρια γεγυμνασμενα</span></span> together after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span> as we have here. For this predicate use of the participle with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:6">Luke 13:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:19">Luke 14:19</span>. "By reason of use" one gains such skill.</p>
<p><b>To discern </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς διακρισιν</span></span>). "For deciding between" (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακρινω</span></span>), old word with ablative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλου τε κα κακου</span></span> (between good and evil). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:1">1 Corinthians 12:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:1">Romans 14:1</span>. </p>
