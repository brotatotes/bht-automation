<p><b> Though he was a Son </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιπερ ων υιος</span></span>). Concessive participle with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιπερ</span></span>, regular Greek idiom as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:5">Hebrews 7:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:17">Hebrews 12:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>Yet learned obedience </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμαθεν υπακοην</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μανθανω</span></span>. Succinct and crisp statement of the humanity of Jesus in full harmony with <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:40">Luke 2:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:52">Luke 2:52</span> and with <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:10">Hebrews 2:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>By the things which he suffered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφ' ων επαθεν</span></span>). There is a play on the two verbs (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμαθεν--επαθεν</span></span>), paronomasia. Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πασχω</span></span>. He always did his Father's will (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:29">John 8:29</span>), but he grew in experience as in wisdom and stature and in the power of sympathy with us. </p>
