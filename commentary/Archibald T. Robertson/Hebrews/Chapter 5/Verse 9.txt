<p><b> Having been made perfect </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελειωθεις</span></span>). First aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελειοω</span></span>, the completion of the process of training mentioned by this same verb in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:10">Hebrews 2:10</span> "by means of sufferings" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια παθηματων</span></span>) as stated again here in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:8">Hebrews 5:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>The author of eternal salvation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιτιος σωτηριας αιωνιου</span></span>). Common adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιτια</span></span> (cause), causing, often in Greek with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωτηριας</span></span> (Aeschines, Philo), in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:4">Luke 23:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:14">Luke 23:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:22">Luke 23:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:40">Acts 19:40</span>. See same idea in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:10">Hebrews 2:10</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρχηγον</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+45:17">Isaiah 45:17</span>. </p>
