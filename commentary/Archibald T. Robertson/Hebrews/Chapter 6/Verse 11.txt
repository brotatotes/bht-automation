<p><b> And we desire </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιθυμουμεν δε</span></span>). Literary plural again like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπεισμεθα</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:9">Hebrews 6:9</span>). He is not wholly satisfied with them as he had already shown (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:11-14">Hebrews 5:11-14</span>). They have not given up Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:4-8">Hebrews 6:4-8</span>), but many of them are still babes (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νηπιο</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:13">Hebrews 5:13</span>) and not adults (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελειο</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:14">Hebrews 5:14</span>) and others are in peril of becoming so.</p>
<p><b>Unto the fulness of hope </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς την πληροφοριαν της ελπιδος</span></span>). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροφορια</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:5">1 Thessalonians 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:2">Colossians 2:2</span>.</p>
