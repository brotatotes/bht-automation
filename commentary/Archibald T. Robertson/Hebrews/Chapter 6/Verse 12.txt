<p><b> That ye be not sluggish </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη νωθρο γενησθε</span></span>). Negative final clause with second aorist middle subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span>, "that ye become not sluggish (or dull of hearing)" as some already were (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:11">Hebrews 5:11</span>).</p>
<p><b>Imitators </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιμητα</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:6">1 Thessalonians 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:14">1 Thessalonians 2:14</span> for this word (our "mimic" in good sense). The writer wishes to hold and develop these sluggards through those who inherit the promises (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:19-12">Hebrews 10:19-12</span>), one of his great appeals later in ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:11">Hebrews 6:11</span> full of examples of "faith and long-suffering." </p>
