<p><b> By two immutable things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια δυο πραγματων αμεταθετων</span></span>). See verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:17">Hebrews 6:17</span>. God's promise and God's oath, both unchangeable.</p>
<p><b>In which it is impossible for God to lie </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν οις αδυνατον ψευσασθα θεον</span></span>). Put this "impossibility" by that in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:4-6">Hebrews 6:4-6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Theon </b> is accusative of general reference with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευσασθα</span></span>, first aorist middle infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευδομα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>That we may have </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα εχωμεν</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>, "that we may keep on having."</p>
<p><b>Strong consolation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ισχυραν παρακλησιν</span></span>). "Strong encouragement" by those two immutable things.</p>
<p><b>Who have fled for refuge </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο καταφυγοντες</span></span>). Articular effective second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταφευγω</span></span>, old verb, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:6">Acts 14:6</span>. The word occurs for fleeing to the cities of refuge (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+4:42">Deuteronomy 4:42</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+19:5">Deuteronomy 19:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jos+20:9">Joshua 20:9</span>).</p>
<p><b>To lay hold of </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρατησα</span></span>). First aorist active (single act) infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρατεω</span></span> in contrast with present tense in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:14">Hebrews 4:14</span> (hold fast).</p>
<p><b>Set before us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προκειμενης</span></span>). Placed before us as the goal. See this same participle used with the "joy" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρας</span></span>) set before Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:2">Hebrews 12:2</span>). </p>
