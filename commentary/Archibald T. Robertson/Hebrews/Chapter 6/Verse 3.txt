<p><b> If God permit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εανπερ επιτρεπη ο θεος</span></span>). Condition of the third class with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εανπερ</span></span> (note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ</span></span> indeed). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:7">1 Corinthians 16:7</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν ο κυριος επιτρεψη</span></span>) and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:21">Acts 18:21</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του θεου θελοντος</span></span>). It is not an idle form with the author. He means that he will go on with the argument and not attempt to lay again the foundation (the elements). Moffatt takes him to mean that he will teach them the elements at a later time (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:23">Hebrews 13:23</span>) if the way opens, a less probable interpretation. </p>
