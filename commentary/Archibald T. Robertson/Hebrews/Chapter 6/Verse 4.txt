<p><b> As touching those who were once enlightened </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους απαξ φωτισθεντας</span></span>). First aorist passive articular participle (the once for all enlightened) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φοτιζω</span></span>, old and common verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φως</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:36">Luke 11:36</span>. The metaphorical sense here (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:9">John 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:18">Ephesians 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:32">Hebrews 10:32</span>) occurs in Polybius and Epictetus. The accusative case is due to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανακαινιζειν</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:6">Hebrews 6:6</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hαπαξ</span></span> here is "once for all," not once upon a time (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποτε</span></span>) and occurs again (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:7">Hebrews 9:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:26">Hebrews 9:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:27">Hebrews 9:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:28">Hebrews 9:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:26">Hebrews 12:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:27">Hebrews 12:27</span>).</p>
<p><b>Tasted of the heavenly gift </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γευσαμενους της δωρεας της επουρανιου</span></span>). First aorist middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γευω</span></span>, old verb once with accusative (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:5">Hebrews 6:5</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλον ρημα, δυναμεις</span></span>), usually with genitive (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:9">Hebrews 2:9</span>) as here.</p>
<p><b>Partakers of the Holy Ghost </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετοχους πνευματος αγιου</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:14">Hebrews 3:14</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετοχο</span></span>. These are all given as actual spiritual experiences.</p>
<p><b>And then fell away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα παραπεσοντας</span></span>). No "then" here, though the second aorist (effective) active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραπιπτω</span></span>, old verb to fall beside (aside), means that. Only here in N.T. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:4">Galatians 5:4</span> we have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της χαριτος εξεπεσατε</span></span> (ye fell out of grace, to law, Paul means). </p>
