<p><b> Which hath drunk </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η πιουσα</span></span>). Articular second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πινω</span></span>, to drink.</p>
<p><b>Herbs </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βοτανην</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βοσκω</span></span>, to feed, green plant, only here in N.T. Cf. our botany.</p>
<p><b>Meet </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευθετον</span></span>). Old compound verbal (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ, τιθημ</span></span>) well-placed, fit (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:62">Luke 9:62</span>).</p>
<p><b>It is tilled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεωργειτα</span></span>). Present passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεωργεω</span></span>, old and rare verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεωργος</span></span> (tiller of the soil, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γη, εργον</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:6">2 Timothy 2:6</span>), here only in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>Receives </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταλαμβανε</span></span>). Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταλαμβανω</span></span>, old verb to share in, with genitive (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλογιας</span></span>) as here (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:46">Acts 2:46</span>) or with accusative (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:25">Acts 24:25</span>). </p>
