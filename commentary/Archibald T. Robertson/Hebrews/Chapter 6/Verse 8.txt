<p><b> If it beareth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκφερουσα</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκφερω</span></span>, conditional participle. For "thorns and thistles" see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:16">Matthew 7:16</span> for both words (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακανθας κα τριβολους</span></span>). Roman soldiers scattered balls with sharp iron spikes, one of which was called <i>tribulus</i>, to hinder the enemy's cavalry.</p>
<p><b>Rejected </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδοκιμος</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:27">1 Corinthians 9:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:28">Romans 1:28</span>. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταρας εγγυς</span></span> (nigh unto a curse) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:10">Galatians 3:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>To be burned </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις καυσιν</span></span>). "For burning." Common sight in clearing up ground. </p>
