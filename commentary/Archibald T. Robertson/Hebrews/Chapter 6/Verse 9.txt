<p><b> But we are persuaded </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπεισμεθα δε</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειθω</span></span>, literary plural. Note Paul's use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπεισμα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:12">2 Timothy 1:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>Better things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα κρεισσονα</span></span>). "The better things" than those pictures in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:4-8">Hebrews 6:4-8</span>.</p>
<p><b>That accompany salvation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχομενα σωτηριας</span></span>). "Things holding on to salvation" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:38">Mark 1:38</span>), a common Greek phrase <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχομενα</span></span>, present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Though we thus speak </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε κα ουτως λαλουμεν</span></span>). Concessive condition of the first class. Explanatory, not apologetic, of his plain talk.</p>
<p><b>Not unrighteous to forget </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου γαρ αδικος επιλαθεσθα</span></span>). Second aorist middle infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλανθανω</span></span> with genitive case (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργου</span></span>, work, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπης</span></span>, love). But even God cannot remember what they did not do.</p>
<p><b>In that ye ministered and still do minister </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονησαντες κα διακονουντες</span></span>). First aorist active and present active participle of the one verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονεω</span></span>, the sole difference being the tense (single act <i>aorist</i>, repeated acts <i>present</i>). </p>
