<p><b> A disannulling </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθετησις</span></span>). Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθετεω</span></span> (alpha privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span>), to set aside (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:26">Mark 6:26</span>), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:26">Hebrews 9:26</span>. Common in the papyri in a legal sense of making void. Involved in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταθεσις</span></span> (change in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:12">Hebrews 7:12</span>).</p>
<p><b>Foregoing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προαγουσης</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προαγω</span></span>, to go before (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:18">1 Timothy 1:18</span>).</p>
<p><b>Because of its weakness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια το αυτης ασθενες</span></span>). Neuter abstract adjective with article for quality as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:7">Hebrews 7:7</span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span> and accusative case for reason.</p>
<p><b>Unprofitableness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανωφελες</span></span>). Old compound (alpha privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφελος</span></span>) useless, and neuter singular like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασθενες</span></span>. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:9">Titus 3:9</span>. </p>
