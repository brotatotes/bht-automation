<p><b> Made nothing perfect </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδεν ετελειωσεν</span></span>). Another parenthesis. First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελειοω</span></span>. See verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:11">Hebrews 7:11</span>. And yet law is necessary.</p>
<p><b>A bringing in thereupon </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεισαγωγη</span></span>). An old double compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>, additional, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισαγωγη</span></span>, bringing in from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισαγω</span></span>). Here only in N.T. Used by Josephus (<i>Ant</i>. XI. 6, 2) for the introduction of a new wife in place of the repudiated one.</p>
<p><b>Of a better hope </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρειττονος ελπιδος</span></span>). This better hope (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:18-20">Hebrews 6:18-20</span>) does bring us near to God (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγγιζομεν τω θεω</span></span>) as we come close to God's throne through Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:16">Hebrews 4:16</span>). </p>
