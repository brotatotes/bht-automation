<p><b> A tenth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεκατην</span></span>). It was common to offer a tenth of the spoils to the gods. So Abraham recognized Melchizedek as a priest of God.</p>
<p><b>Divided </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμερισεν</span></span>). First aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεριζω</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μερος</span></span> (portion), to separate into parts. From this point till near the end of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:3">Hebrews 7:3</span> (the Son of God) is a long parenthesis with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτος</span></span> of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:1">Hebrews 7:1</span> as the subject of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενε</span></span> (abideth) as the Revised Version punctuates it. Philo had made popular the kind of exegesis used here. The author gives in Greek the meaning of the Hebrew words Melchizedek (King of righteousness, cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:8">Hebrews 1:8</span>) and Salem (peace). </p>
