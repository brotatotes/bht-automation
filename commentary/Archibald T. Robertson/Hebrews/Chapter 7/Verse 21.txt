<p><b> Have been made </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισιν γεγονοτες</span></span>). Periphrastic perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span> (perfect active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span>) and then <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισιν</span></span>. The parenthesis runs from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο μεν γαρ</span></span> (for they) to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τον αιωνα</span></span> (for ever, end of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:21">Hebrews 7:21</span>).</p>
<p><b>But he with an oath </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο δε μετα ορκωμοσιας</span></span>). Positive statement in place of the negative one in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:20">Hebrews 7:20</span>. </p>
