<p><b> By so much also </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα τοσουτο κα</span></span>). Correlative demonstrative corresponding to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθ' οσον</span></span> (the relative clause) in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:20">Hebrews 7:20</span>.</p>
<p><b>The surety </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγγυος</span></span>). Vulgate <i>sponsor</i>. Old word, here only in the N.T., adjective (one pledged, betrothed), from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγγυη</span></span>, a pledge, here used as substantive like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγγυητης</span></span>, one who gives a pledge or guarantee. There may be a play on the word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγγιζω</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:19">Hebrews 7:19</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εγγυαω</span></span> is to give a pledge, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγγυαλιζω</span></span>, to put a pledge in the hollow of the hand. It is not clear whether the author means that Jesus is God's pledge to man, or man's to God, or both. He is both in fact, as the Mediator (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο μεσιτης</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:6">Hebrews 8:6</span>) between God and man (Son of God and Son of man). </p>
