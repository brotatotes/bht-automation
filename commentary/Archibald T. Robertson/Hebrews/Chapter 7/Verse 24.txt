<p><b> Because he abideth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια το μενειν αυτον</span></span>). Same idiom as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:23">Hebrews 7:23</span>, "because of the abiding as to him" (accusative of general reference, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτον</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Unchangeable </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαραβατον</span></span>). Predicate adjective in the accusative (feminine of compound adjective like masculine), late double compound verbal adjective in Plutarch and papyri, from alpha privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραβαινω</span></span>, valid or inviolate. The same idea in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:3">Hebrews 7:3</span>. God placed Christ in this priesthood and no one else can step into it. See verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:11">Hebrews 7:11</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιερωσυνη</span></span>. </p>
