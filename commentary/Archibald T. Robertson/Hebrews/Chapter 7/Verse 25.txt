<p><b> Wherefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οθεν</span></span>). Since he alone holds this priesthood.</p>
<p><b>To the uttermost </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το παντελες</span></span>). Old idiom, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:10">Luke 13:10</span>. Vulgate renders it <i>in perpetuum</i> (temporal idea) or like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντοτε</span></span>. This is possible, but the common meaning is completely, utterly.</p>
<p><b>Draw near </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσερχομενους</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσερχομα</span></span>, the verb used in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:16">Hebrews 4:16</span> which see.</p>
<p><b>To make intercession </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το εντυγχανειν</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> and the articular present active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντυγχανω</span></span> for which verb see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:34">Romans 8:34</span>. "His intercession has red blood in it, unlike Philo's conception" (Moffatt). </p>
