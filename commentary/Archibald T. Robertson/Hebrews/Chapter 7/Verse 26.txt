<p><b> Became us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημιν επρεπεν</span></span>). Imperfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρεπω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:10">Hebrews 2:10</span>, only there it was applied to God while here to us. "Such" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοιουτος</span></span>) refers to the Melchizedek character of Jesus as high priest and in particular to his power to help and save (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:17">Hebrews 2:17</span>) as just explained in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:24">Hebrews 7:24</span> Moffatt notes that "it is generally misleading to parse a rhapsody" but the adjectives that follow picture in outline the qualities of the high priest needed by us.</p>
<p><b>Holy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οσιος</span></span>). Saintly, pious, as already noted. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:24">Acts 2:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:35">Acts 13:35</span>.</p>
<p><b>Guileless </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακακος</span></span>). Without malice, innocent. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:18">Romans 16:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>Undefiled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμιαντος</span></span>). Untainted, stainless. In the papyri. Not merely ritual purity (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="le+21:10-15">Leviticus 21:10-15</span>), but real ethical cleanness.</p>
<p><b>Separated from sinners </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεχωρισμενος απο των αμαρτωλων</span></span>). Perfect passive participle. Probably referring to Christ's exaltation (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:28">Hebrews 9:28</span>).</p>
<p><b>Made higher than the heavens </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υψηλοτερος των ουρανων γενομενος</span></span>). "Having become higher than the heavens." Ablative case (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουρανων</span></span>) after the comparative adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υψηλοτερος</span></span>). </p>
