<p><b> First </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προτερον</span></span>). Regular adverb for comparison between two, though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτον</span></span> often occurs also (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:41">John 1:41</span>), with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επειτα</span></span> (then) following.</p>
<p><b>For the sins </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον</span></span>). Only the article in the Greek with repetition of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ</span></span> or of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτιων</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>When he offered up himself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαυτον ανενεγκας</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναφερω</span></span>, to offer up. See same idea in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:14">Hebrews 9:14</span> where <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαυτον προσηνεγκεν</span></span> is used. Old verb for sacrifice to place on the altar (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:5">1 Peter 2:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:24">1 Peter 2:24</span>). </p>
