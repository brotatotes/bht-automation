<p><b> He whose genealogy is not counted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο μη γενεαλογουμενος</span></span>). Articular participle with negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> (usual with participles) of the old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενεαλογεω</span></span> trace ancestry (cf. verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:3">Hebrews 7:3</span>)</p>
<p><b>Hath taken tithes </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεδεκατωκεν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεκατοω</span></span>, standing on record in Genesis.</p>
<p><b>Hath blessed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλογηκεν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλογεω</span></span>, likewise standing on record. Note the frequent perfect tenses in Hebrews.</p>
<p><b>Him that hath the promises </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον εχοντα τας επαγγελιας</span></span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:12">Hebrews 6:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:13-15">Hebrews 6:13-15</span> for allusion to the repeated promises to Abraham (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+12:3">Genesis 12:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+12:7">Genesis 12:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+13:14">Genesis 13:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+15:5">Genesis 15:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+17:5">Genesis 17:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+22:16-18">Genesis 22:16-18</span>). </p>
