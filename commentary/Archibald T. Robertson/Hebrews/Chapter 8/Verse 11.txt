<p><b> They shall not teach </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη διδαξωσιν</span></span>). Strong double negative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη</span></span>) with the first aorist active (futuristic) subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδασκω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>His fellow-citizen </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον πολιτην αυτου</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:15">Luke 15:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:14">Luke 19:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>Know the Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Γνωθ τον κυριον</span></span>). Second aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span>. In the new covenant all will be taught of God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+54:13">Isaiah 54:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:45">John 6:45</span>), whereas under the old only the educated scribe could understand the minutiae of the law (Dods). See Paul's comparison in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:7-18">2 Corinthians 3:7-18</span>.</p>
<p><b>Shall know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδησουσιν</span></span>). Future perfect active, old form of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιδα</span></span> (note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span> just before of recognizing God), one of the rare future perfects (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:13">Hebrews 2:13</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσομα πεποιθως</span></span>). </p>
