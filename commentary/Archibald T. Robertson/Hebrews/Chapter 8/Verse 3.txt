<p><b> Is appointed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθιστατα</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:1">Hebrews 5:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>To offer </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το προσφερειν</span></span>). Articular infinitive accusative case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> as is common while <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα προσφερη</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> with present active subjunctive) for purpose in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:1">Hebrews 5:1</span>, with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δωρα τε κα θυσιας</span></span> as there.</p>
<p><b>It is necessary </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγκαιον</span></span>). A moral and logical necessity (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγκη</span></span> necessity) as seen in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:46">Acts 13:46</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:24">Philippians 1:24</span>.</p>
<p><b>This high priest also </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα τουτον</span></span>). "This one also," no word for high priest, accusative of general reference with the infinitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχειν</span></span> (have).</p>
<p><b>Somewhat to offer </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τ ω προσενεγκη</span></span>). Second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσφερω</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:3">Hebrews 8:3</span>). Vulgate <i>aliquid quod offerat</i>. The use of the subjunctive in this relative clause is probably volitive as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:16">Acts 21:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:28">Hebrews 12:28</span> (possibly here merely futuristic), but note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο προσφερε</span></span> (present indicative) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:7">Hebrews 9:7</span>. See Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 955. </p>
