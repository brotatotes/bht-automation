<p><b> On earth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ γης</span></span>). As opposed to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τοις ουρανοις</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:1">Hebrews 8:1</span>). Condition of second class, determined as unfulfilled.</p>
<p><b>He would not be a priest at all </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδ' αν ην ιερευς</span></span>). "Not even would he be a priest." Conclusion of second class condition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span> and imperfect indicative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Seeing there are those </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οντων των</span></span>). Genitive absolute with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οντων</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειμ</span></span>) and the articular present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσφερω</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:3">Hebrews 8:3</span>). Jesus was not of the tribe of Levi and so could not serve here. </p>
