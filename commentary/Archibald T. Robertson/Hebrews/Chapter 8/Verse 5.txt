<p><b> Serve </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λατρευουσιν</span></span>). Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λατρευω</span></span> for which verb see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:10">Matthew 4:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>A copy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποδειγματ</span></span>). Dative case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λατρευουσιν</span></span>. See already on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:15">John 13:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:11">Hebrews 4:11</span> for this interesting word.</p>
<p><b>Shadow </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκια</span></span>). Dative case. Old word for which see already <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:16">Matthew 4:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:32">Mark 4:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:17">Colossians 2:17</span>. See same idea in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:23">Hebrews 9:23</span>. For difference between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκια</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εικων</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:1">Hebrews 10:1</span>. Here "copy and shadow" form a practical hendiadys for "a shadowy out- line" (Moffatt).</p>
<p><b>Is warned of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεχρηματιστα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρηματιζω</span></span>, old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρημα</span></span>, business) for which see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:12">Matthew 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:22">Matthew 2:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:26">Luke 2:26</span>. The word "God" is not used, but it is implied as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:22">Acts 10:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:25">Hebrews 12:25</span>. So in LXX, Josephus, and the papyri.</p>
<p><b>For saith he </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γαρ φησ</span></span>). Argument from God's command (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+25:40">Exodus 25:40</span>).</p>
<p><b>See that thou make </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hορα ποιησεις</span></span>). Common Greek idiom with present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span> and the volitive future of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span> without <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> (asyndeton, Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 949).</p>
<p><b>The pattern </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον τυπον</span></span>). The very word used in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+25:40">Exodus 25:40</span> and quoted also by Stephen in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:44">Acts 7:44</span>. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυπος</span></span> see already <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:25">John 20:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:17">Romans 6:17</span>, etc. The tabernacle was to be patterned after the heavenly model. </p>
