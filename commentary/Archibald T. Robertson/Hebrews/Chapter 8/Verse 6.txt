<p><b> But now </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυν δε</span></span>). Logical use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυν</span></span>, as the case now stands, with Jesus as high priest in heaven.</p>
<p><b>Hath he obtained </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τετυχεν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυγχανω</span></span> with the genitive, a rare and late form for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τετευχεν</span></span> (also <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τετευχηκεν</span></span>), old verb to hit the mark, to attain.</p>
<p><b>A ministry the more excellent </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαφορωτερας λειτουργιας</span></span>). "A more excellent ministry." For the comparative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαφορος</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:4">Hebrews 1:4</span>. This remark applies to all the five points of superiority over the Levitical priesthood.</p>
<p><b>By how much </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οσω</span></span>). Instrumental case of the relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οσος</span></span> between two comparative adjectives as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:4">Hebrews 1:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>The mediator </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεσιτης</span></span>). Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεσος</span></span> (amid) and so a middle man (arbitrator). Already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:19">Galatians 3:19</span> and see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:5">1 Timothy 2:5</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:15">Hebrews 9:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:24">Hebrews 12:24</span> for further use with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαθηκη</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Of a better covenant </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρειττονος διαθηκης</span></span>). Called "new" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καινησ, νεας</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:15">Hebrews 9:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:24">Hebrews 12:24</span>). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαθηκη</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:28">Matthew 26:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:72">Luke 1:72</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:17">Galatians 3:17</span>, etc. This idea he will discuss in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:7-13">Hebrews 8:7-13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Hath been enacted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νενομοθετητα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νομοθετεω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:11">Hebrews 7:11</span> which see.</p>
<p><b>Upon better promises </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ κρειττοσιν επαγγελιαις</span></span>). Upon the basis of (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>). But how "better" if the earlier were also from God? This idea, alluded to in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:12-17">Hebrews 6:12-17</span>, Will be developed in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:19-12">Hebrews 10:19-12</span> with great passion and power. Thus it is seen that "better" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρεισσων</span></span>) is the keynote of the Epistle. At every point Christianity is better than Judaism. </p>
