<p><b> Finding fault with them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεμφομενος αυτους</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεμφομα</span></span> (cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμεμπτος</span></span>), old verb, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:19">Romans 9:19</span>. The covenant was all right, but the Jews failed to keep it. Hence God made a new one of grace in place of law. Why do marriage covenants so often fail to hold? The author quotes in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:8-12">Hebrews 8:8-12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+38:31-34">Jeremiah 38:31-34</span> (in LXX <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+31:31-34">Hebrews 31:31-34</span>) in full which calls for little explanation or application to prove his point (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:13">Hebrews 8:13</span>).</p>
<p><b>I will make </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συντελεσω</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συντελεω</span></span>, old compound verb to accomplish as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:4">Mark 13:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:28">Romans 9:28</span>.</p>
<p><b>A new covenant </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαθηκην καινην</span></span>). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:24">Hebrews 12:24</span> we have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαθηκης νεας</span></span>, but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καινης</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:25">1 Corinthians 11:25</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Καινος</span></span> is fresh, on new lines as opposed to the old (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παλαιος</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:6">2 Corinthians 3:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:14">2 Corinthians 3:14</span>; <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νεος</span></span> is young or not yet old. </p>
