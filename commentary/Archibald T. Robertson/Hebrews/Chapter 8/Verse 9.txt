<p><b> In the day that I took them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ημερα επιλαβομενου μου</span></span>). Genitive absolute (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μου</span></span> and second aorist middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλαμβανω</span></span>), "a Hellenistic innovation" (Moffatt) in imitation of the Hebrew after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημερα</span></span> in place of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν η επελαβομεν</span></span>, occurring also in Barn. 2:28.</p>
<p><b>By the hand </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της χειρος</span></span>). Technical use of the genitive of the part affected.</p>
<p><b>To lead them forth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαγαγειν αυτους</span></span>). Second aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαγω</span></span> to denote purpose.</p>
<p><b>For they continued not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ αυτο ουκ ενεμειναν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμμενω</span></span>, old verb to remain in (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:22">Acts 14:22</span>). The Israelites broke the covenant. Then God annulled it.</p>
<p><b>I regarded not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημελησα</span></span>). "I neglected" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:3">Hebrews 2:3</span>. The covenant was void when they broke it. </p>
