<p><b> Even the first covenant </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα η πρωτη</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κα</span></span> (even) is doubtful. No word for covenant with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτε</span></span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:7">Hebrews 8:7</span>).</p>
<p><b>Had </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειχε</span></span>). Imperfect active, used to have.</p>
<p><b>Ordinances </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιωματα</span></span>). Regulations (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιοω</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:6">Luke 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:16">Romans 5:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>Of divine service </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λατρειας</span></span>). No word for "divine," though worship is meant as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:4">Romans 9:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:3">Philippians 3:3</span>. Genitive case.</p>
<p><b>And its sanctuary, a sanctuary of this world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το τε αγιον κοσμικον</span></span>). By <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το αγιον</span></span> the author describes the whole sanctuary (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+36:3">Exodus 36:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+3:38">Numbers 3:38</span>) like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των αγιων</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:2">Hebrews 8:2</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κοσμικον</span></span> is a late adjective (Aristotle, Plutarch) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοσμος</span></span>, relating to this world, like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ γης</span></span> (upon earth) of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:4">Hebrews 8:4</span>. It is in the predicate position, not attributive. </p>
