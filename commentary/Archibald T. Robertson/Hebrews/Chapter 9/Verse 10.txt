<p><b> Only with meats and drinks and divers washings </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μονον επ βρωμασιν κα πομασιν κα διαφοροις βαπτισμοις</span></span>). The parenthesis of the Revised Version here is unnecessary. The use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> here with the locative case is regular, "in the matter of" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:52">Luke 12:52</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:16">John 12:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:24">Acts 21:24</span>). What ritual value these Levitical sacrifices had was confined to minute regulations about diet and ceremonial cleansing (clean and unclean). For "divers" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαφοροις</span></span>, late adjective, in N.T. only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:4">Hebrews 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:6">Hebrews 8:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:10">Hebrews 9:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:6">Romans 12:6</span>) say "different" or "various." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Βαπτισμοις</span></span> is, of course, the Jewish ceremonial immersions (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:4">Mark 7:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+29:4">Exodus 29:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="le+11:25">Leviticus 11:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="le+11:28">Leviticus 11:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+8:7">Numbers 8:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:2">Revelation 6:2</span>).</p>
<p><b>Carnal ordinances </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιωμασιν σαρκος</span></span>). But the correct text is undoubtedly simply <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιωματα σαρκος</span></span> (nominative case), in apposition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δωρα τε κα θυσια</span></span> (gifts and sacrifices). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:1">Hebrews 9:1</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιωματα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Imposed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επικειμενα</span></span>). Present middle or passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επικειμα</span></span>, old verb to lie upon (be laid upon). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:16">1 Corinthians 9:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>Until a time of reformation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεχρ καιρου διορθωσεως</span></span>). Definite statement of the temporary nature of the Levitical system already stated in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:10-17">Hebrews 7:10-17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:13">Hebrews 8:13</span> and argued clearly by Paul in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:15-22">Galatians 3:15-22</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Διορθωσις</span></span> is a late word, here alone in N.T. (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διορθοω</span></span>, to set right or straight), used by Hippocrates for making straight misshapen limbs like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανορθοω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:12">Hebrews 12:12</span>. Here for reformation like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διορθωμα</span></span> (reform) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:2">Acts 24:2</span>. Christianity itself is the great Reformation of the current Judaism (Pharisaism) and the spiritual Judaism foreshadowed by the old Abrahamic promise (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:3">Hebrews 9:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:9">Hebrews 9:9</span>). </p>
