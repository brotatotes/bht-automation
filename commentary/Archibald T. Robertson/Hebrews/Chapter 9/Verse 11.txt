<p><b> Having come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραγενομενος</span></span>). Second aorist middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραγινομα</span></span>. This is the great historic event that is the crux of history. "Christ came on the scene, and all was changed" (Moffatt).</p>
<p><b>Of the good things to come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των μελλοντων αγαθων</span></span>). But B D read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενομενων</span></span> (that are come). It is a nice question which is the true text. Both aspects are true, for Christ is High Priest of good things that have already come as well as of the glorious future of hope. Westcott prefers <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενομενων</span></span>, Moffatt <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μελλοντων</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Through the greater and more perfect tabernacle </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια της μειζονος κα τελειοτερας σκηνης</span></span>). Probably the instrumental use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:4">2 Corinthians 2:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:27">Romans 2:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:20">Romans 14:20</span>) as accompaniment, not the local idea (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:14">Hebrews 4:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:20">Hebrews 10:20</span>). Christ as High Priest employed in his work the heavenly tabernacle (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:2">Hebrews 8:2</span>) after which the earthly was patterned (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:24">Hebrews 9:24</span>).</p>
<p><b>Not made with hands </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου χειροποιητου</span></span>). Old compound verbal for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:58">Mark 14:58</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:48">Acts 7:48</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:24">Acts 17:24</span>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:2">Hebrews 8:2</span>. Here in the predicate position.</p>
<p><b>Not of this creation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου ταυτης της κτισεως</span></span>). Explanation of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου χιεροποιητου</span></span>. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κτισις</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:17">2 Corinthians 5:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:19">Romans 8:19</span>. For the idea see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:18">2 Corinthians 4:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:2">Hebrews 8:2</span>. This greater and more perfect tabernacle is heaven itself (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:24">Hebrews 9:24</span>). </p>
