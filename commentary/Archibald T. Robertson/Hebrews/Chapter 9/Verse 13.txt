<p><b> Ashes </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σποδος</span></span>). Old word, in N. T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:21">Matthew 11:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:13">Luke 10:13</span>. Common in LXX.</p>
<p><b>Of a heifer </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δαμαλεως</span></span>). Old word (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δαμαλις</span></span>), a red heifer whose ashes mingled with water (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα υδατος</span></span>, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:19">Hebrews 9:19</span>) were sprinkled (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ραντιζουσα</span></span>, present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ραντιζω</span></span>, in LXX, though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ραινω</span></span> more common) on the contaminated or defiled ones (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:19">Hebrews 9:19</span>) as the blood of bulls and goats was offered for sins (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:16">Hebrews 9:16</span>).</p>
<p><b>Sanctify </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγιαζε</span></span>). First-class condition, assumed as true. This ceremonial ritual does serve "for the cleansing (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθαροτητα</span></span>, old word here only in N.T.) of the flesh," but not for the conscience (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:9">Hebrews 9:9</span>). The cow was <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμωμον</span></span>, the individual <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθαρος</span></span>. </p>
