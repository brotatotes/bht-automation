<p><b> Mediator of a new covenant </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαθηκης καινης μεσιτης</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:6">Hebrews 8:6</span> for this phrase with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρειττονος</span></span> instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καινης</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>A death having taken place </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θανατου γενομενου</span></span>). Genitive absolute, referring to Christ's death.</p>
<p><b>For the redemption </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις απολυτρωσιν</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Of the transgressions </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των παραβασεων</span></span>). Really ablative case, "from the transgressions." See verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:12">Hebrews 9:12</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυτρωσιν</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Under the first covenant </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ τη πρωτη διαθηκη</span></span>). Here there is a definite statement that the real value in the typical sacrifices under the Old Testament system was in the realization in the death of Christ. It is Christ's death that gives worth to the types that pointed to him. So then the atoning sacrifice of Christ is the basis of the salvation of all who are saved before the Cross and since.</p>
<p><b>That they may receive </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπως λαβωσιν</span></span>). Purpose clause (God's purpose in the rites and symbols) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπως</span></span> and the second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>. </p>
