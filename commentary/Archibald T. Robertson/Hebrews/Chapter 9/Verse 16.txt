<p><b> A testament </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαθηκη</span></span>). The same word occurs for covenant (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:15">Hebrews 9:15</span>) and will (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:16">Hebrews 9:16</span>). This double sense of the word is played upon also by Paul in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:15">Galatians 3:15</span>. We say today "The New Testament" (<i>Novum Testamentum</i>) rather than " The New Covenant." Both terms are pertinent.</p>
<p><b>That made it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του διαθεμενου</span></span>). Genitive of the articular second aorist middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διατιθημ</span></span> from which <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαθηκη</span></span> comes. The notion of will here falls in with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κληρονομια</span></span> (inheritance, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:4">1 Peter 1:4</span>) as well as with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θανατος</span></span> (death).</p>
<p><b>Of force </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βεβαια</span></span>). Stable, firm as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:6">Hebrews 3:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:14">Hebrews 3:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>Where there hath been death </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ νεκροις</span></span>). "In the case of dead people." A will is only operative then.</p>
<p><b>For doth it ever avail while he that made it liveth? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επε μη ποτε ισχυε οτε ζη ο διαθεμενοσ;</span></span>). This is a possible punctuation with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη ποτε</span></span> in a question (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:26">John 7:26</span>). Without the question mark, it is a positive statement of fact. Aleph and D read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοτε</span></span> (then) instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποτε</span></span>. The use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> in a causal sentence is allowable (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:18">John 3:18</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ μη</span></span>). </p>
