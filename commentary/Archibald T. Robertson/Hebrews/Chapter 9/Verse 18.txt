<p><b> The first covenant </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η πρωτη</span></span>). Supply <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαθηκη</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:1">Hebrews 9:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Has been dedicated </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενκεκαινιστα</span></span>). Stands dedicated. Perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενκαινιζο</span></span>, a late verb in LXX, one papyrus, and in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:20">Hebrews 10:20</span>. It means to renew, to inaugurate (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1sa+11:14">1 Samuel 11:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ch+15:8">2 Chronicles 15:8</span>) and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ki+8:63">1 Kings 8:63</span> to dedicate. Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα ενκαινια</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:22">John 10:22</span>) for the feast of dedication. </p>
