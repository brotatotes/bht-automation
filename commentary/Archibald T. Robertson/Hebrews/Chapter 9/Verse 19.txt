<p><b> When every commandment had been spoken </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαληθεισης</span></span>). Genitive absolute with first aorist passive participle feminine singular of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαλεω</span></span>. The author uses the account in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+24:3">Exodus 24:3</span> "with characteristic freedom" (Moffatt). There is nothing there about the water, the scarlet wool (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εριον</span></span>, diminutive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εροσ, ειρος</span></span>, old word, here and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:14">Revelation 1:14</span>; for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοκκινος</span></span> see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:6">Matthew 27:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:28">Matthew 27:28</span>), and hyssop (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υσσωπου</span></span>, a plant mentioned in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:29">John 19:29</span>). It had become the custom to mingle water with the blood and to use a wisp of wool or a stem of hyssop for sprinkling (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+10:2-10">Numbers 10:2-10</span>).</p>
<p><b>Both the book itself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτο τε το βιβλιον</span></span>). There is nothing in Exodus about sprinkling the book of the covenant, though it may very well have been done. He omits the use of oil in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+40:9">Exodus 40:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="le+8:10">Leviticus 8:10</span> and applies blood to all the details.</p>
<p><b>Sprinkled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εραντισεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ραντιζω</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ραντος</span></span> and this from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ραινω</span></span>), like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαπτιζω</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαπτω</span></span>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:4">Mark 7:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:22">Hebrews 10:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:13">Revelation 19:13</span>. </p>
