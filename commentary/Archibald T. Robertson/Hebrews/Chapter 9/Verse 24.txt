<p><b> Made with hands </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χειροποιητα</span></span>). See verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:11">Hebrews 9:11</span> for this word.</p>
<p><b>Like in pattern to the true </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντιτυπα των αληθινων</span></span>). Late compound word, only twice in N.T. (here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:21">1 Peter 3:21</span>). Polybius uses <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντιτυπος</span></span> for infantry "opposite" to the cavalry. In modern Greek it means a copy of a book. Here it is the "counterpart of reality" (Moffatt). Moses was shown a <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυπος</span></span> (model) of the heavenly realities and he made an <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντιτυπον</span></span> on that model, "answering to the type" (Dods) or model. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:21">1 Peter 3:21</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντιτυπος</span></span> has the converse sense, "the reality of baptism which corresponds to or is the antitype of the deluge" (Dods).</p>
<p><b>Now to appear </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυν εμφανισθηνα</span></span>). Purpose clause by the first aorist passive infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμφανιζω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:53">Matthew 27:53</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:21">John 14:21</span>). For the phrase see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+42:3">Psalms 42:3</span>. For this work of Christ as our High Priest and Paraclete in heaven see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:25">Hebrews 7:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:34">Romans 8:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:1">1 John 2:1</span>. </p>
