<p><b> That he should offer himself often </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα πολλακις προσφερη εαυτον</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσφερω</span></span> (keep on offering himself, like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:1">Hebrews 5:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:3">Hebrews 5:3</span>).</p>
<p><b>With blood not his own </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αιματ αλλοτριω</span></span>). So-called instrumental use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> (accompaniment). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλοτριος</span></span> means "belonging to another," "not one's own" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:12">Luke 16:12</span>). </p>
