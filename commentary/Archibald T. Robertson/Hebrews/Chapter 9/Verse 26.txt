<p><b> Else must he often have suffered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επε εδε αυτον πολλακις παθειν</span></span>). A common elliptical use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επε</span></span> after which one must supply "if that were true" or "in that case," a protasis of a condition of the second class assumed to be untrue. The conclusion with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδε</span></span> is without <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span> (verbs of necessity, obligation, etc.). See Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 963. The conclusion with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span> occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:2">Hebrews 10:2</span>. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:10">1 Corinthians 5:10</span>. "Since, if that were true, it would be necessary for him to suffer often."</p>
<p><b>Since the foundation of the world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο καταβολης κοσμου</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:3">Hebrews 4:3</span> for this phrase. The one sacrifice of Christ is of absolute and final value (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:19">1 Peter 1:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:8">Revelation 13:8</span>).</p>
<p><b>At the end </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ συντελεια</span></span>). Consummation or completion as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:39">Matthew 13:39</span> which see.</p>
<p><b>Hath he been manifested </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεφανερωτα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανεροω</span></span>, permanent state. See "the primitive hymn or confession of faith" (Moffatt) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:16">1 Timothy 3:16</span> and also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:20">1 Peter 1:20</span>. Jesus came once for all (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:2">Hebrews 1:2</span>).</p>
<p><b>To put away sin </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις αθετησιν της αμαρτιας</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:18">Hebrews 7:18</span> for the word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθετησις</span></span>. "The sacrifice of Christ dealt with sin as a principle: the Levitical sacrifices with individual transgressions" (Vincent). </p>
