<p><b> Above it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερανω αυτης</span></span>). Up above, in local sense as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:10">Ephesians 4:10</span>, with ablative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτης</span></span> (it, the ark).</p>
<p><b>Cherubim of glory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χερουβειν δοξης</span></span>). Hebrew word (dual form), two in number, made of gold (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+25:18-22">Exodus 25:18-22</span>). They are called <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωα</span></span> (living creatures) in the LXX (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+6:2">Isaiah 6:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+1:5-10">Ezekiel 1:5-10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+10:5-20">Ezekiel 10:5-20</span>).</p>
<p><b>Overshadowing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατασκιαζοντα</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατασκιαζω</span></span>, old verb to shadow down on, cover with shade, only here in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>The mercy seat </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το ιλαστηριον</span></span>). The pinions of the Cherubim spread over the rectangular gold slab on top of the ark termed the mercy seat. Here the adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιλαστηριος</span></span> has to mean mercy seat, the place, not the propitiatory gift or propitiation, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:25">Romans 3:25</span> (Deissmann, <i>Bible Studies</i>, pp. 124-35).</p>
<p><b>Severally </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα μερος</span></span>). In detail, distributive use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μερος</span></span> (part). </p>
