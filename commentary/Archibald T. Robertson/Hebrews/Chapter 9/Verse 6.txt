<p><b> These things having been thus prepared </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτων ουτως κατεσκευασμενων</span></span>). Genitive absolute with the perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατασκευαζω</span></span> for which verb see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:2">Hebrews 9:2</span>. A mere summary has been made of the furniture.</p>
<p><b>Go in </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισιασιν</span></span>). Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισειμ</span></span>, to go in, old verb, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:3">Acts 3:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:18">Acts 21:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:26">Acts 21:26</span>.</p>
<p><b>Accomplishing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιτελουντες</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιτελεω</span></span> for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:5">Hebrews 8:5</span>. </p>
