<p><b> Alone </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μονος</span></span>). Predicate adjective with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αρχιερευς</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Once in the year </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαξ του ενιαυτου</span></span>). Once for each year (not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποτε</span></span>, at any time) with genitive of time.</p>
<p><b>Not without blood </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου χωρις αιματος</span></span>). According to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="le+16:14">Leviticus 16:14</span>. Not even he could enter the second tent (Holy of Holies) without blood.</p>
<p><b>The errors of the people </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των του λαου αγνοηματων</span></span>). Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγνοεω</span></span>, not to know (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:2">Hebrews 5:2</span>), only here in the N.T., but in LXX, papyri, and inscriptions where a distinction is drawn between errors (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγνοηματα</span></span>) and crimes (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρμαρτηματα</span></span>). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+43:12">Genesis 43:12</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγνοημα</span></span> is "an oversight." But these sins of ignorance (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγνοηματα</span></span>) were sins and called for atonement. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:26">Hebrews 10:26</span> for wilful sinning. </p>
