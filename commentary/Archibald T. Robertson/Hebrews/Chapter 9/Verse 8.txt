<p><b> The Holy Ghost this signifying </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτο δηλουντος του πνευματος του αγιου</span></span>). Genitive absolute with present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δηλοω</span></span>, to make plain. Used as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:27">Hebrews 12:27</span>.</p>
<p><b>The way into the Holy place </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την των αγιων οδον</span></span>). Here as in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:12">Hebrews 9:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:25">Hebrews 9:25</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των αγιων</span></span> is used for the very Presence of God as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:2">Hebrews 8:2</span> and is in the objective genitive. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hοδον</span></span> is the accusative of general reference with the infinitive.</p>
<p><b>Hath not yet been made manifest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηπω πεφανερωσθα</span></span>). Perfect passive infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανεροω</span></span>, to make plain (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανερος</span></span>) in indirect discourse after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δηλουντος</span></span> with negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηπω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>While as the first tabernacle is yet standing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετ της πρωτης σκηνης εχουσης στασιν</span></span>). Another genitive absolute with present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span> (having standing <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στασιν</span></span>), "the first tabernacle still having a place." The veil at the entrance kept the people out of the first tent as the second veil (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:3">Hebrews 9:3</span>) kept the priests out of the Holy of Holies (the very Presence of God). </p>
