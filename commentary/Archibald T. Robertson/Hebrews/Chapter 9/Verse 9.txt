<p><b> Which </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ητις</span></span>). "Which very thing," the first tent (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της πρωτης σκηνης</span></span>, division of the tabernacle), a parenthesis and explanation.</p>
<p><b>A parable </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραβολη</span></span>). Only in the Synoptic Gospels in the N.T. and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:9">Hebrews 9:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:19">Hebrews 11:19</span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:3">Matthew 13:3</span> for the word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραβαλλω</span></span>, to place alongside). Here like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυπος</span></span> (type or shadow of "the heavenly reality," Moffatt).</p>
<p><b>For the time now present </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τον καιρον τον ενεστηκοτα</span></span>). "For the present crisis " (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιρον</span></span>, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιωνα</span></span>, age, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρονον</span></span>, time). Perfect active articular (repeated article) participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενιστημ</span></span> (intransitive), the age in which they lived, not the past, not the future. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:22">1 Corinthians 3:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:38">Romans 8:38</span> for contrast between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεστωτα</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μελλοντα</span></span>. This age of crisis, foreshadowed by the old tabernacle, pointed on to the richer fulfilment still to come.</p>
<p><b>According to which </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθ' ην</span></span>). Here the relative refers to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραβολη</span></span> just mentioned, not to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκηνης</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:1">Hebrews 5:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:3">Hebrews 8:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>As touching the conscience </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα συνειδησιν</span></span>). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνειδησις</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+8:10">1 Corinthians 8:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:17">1 Corinthians 10:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:15">Romans 2:15</span>. This was the real failure of animal sacrifice (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:1-4">Hebrews 10:1-4</span>).</p>
<p><b>Make the worshipper perfect </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελειωσα τον λατρευοντα</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:10">Hebrews 2:10</span>). At best it was only ritual or ceremonial purification (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:11">Hebrews 7:11</span>), that called for endless repetition (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:1-4">Hebrews 10:1-4</span>). </p>
