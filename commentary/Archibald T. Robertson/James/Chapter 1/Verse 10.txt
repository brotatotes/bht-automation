<p><b> In that he is made low </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη ταπεινωσε αυτου</span></span>). "In his low estate." Play on <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταπεινωσις</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταπεινοω</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:7">Philippians 3:7</span>), like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταπεινος</span></span> of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:9">James 1:9</span>, old word in various senses, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:48">Luke 1:48</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:33">Acts 8:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:21">Philippians 3:21</span>. The Cross of Christ lifts up the poor and brings down the high. It is the great leveller of men.</p>
<p><b>As the flower of the grass </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως ανθος χορτου</span></span>). From the LXX (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+40:6">Isaiah 40:6</span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χορτος</span></span> means pasture, then grass (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:39">Mark 6:39</span>) or fodder. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ανθος</span></span> is old word, in N.T. only here, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:11">James 1:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:24">1 Peter 1:24</span> (same quotation). This warning is here applied to "the rich brother," but it is true of all.</p>
<p><b>He shall pass away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρελευσετα</span></span>). Future middle indicative (effective aoristic future, shall pass completely away from earth). </p>
