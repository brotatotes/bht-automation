<p><b> Ariseth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανετειλεν</span></span>). Gnomic or timeless aorist active indicative of the old compound <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανατελλω</span></span>, used here of plants (cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναθαλλω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:10">Philippians 4:10</span>), often of the sun (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:6">Matthew 13:6</span>).</p>
<p><b>With the scorching wind </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν τω καυσων</span></span>). Associative instrumental case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν</span></span>. In the LXX this late word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καυσος</span></span>) is usually the sirocco, the dry east wind from the desert (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+1:19">Job 1:19</span>). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:12">Matthew 20:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:55">Luke 12:55</span> it is the burning heat of the sun. Either makes sense here.</p>
<p><b>Withereth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξηρανεν</span></span>). Another gnomic aorist active indicative (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 837) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξηραινω</span></span>, old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξηρος</span></span>, dry or withered, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:10">Matthew 12:10</span>), to dry up. Grass and flowers are often used to picture the transitoriness of human life.</p>
<p><b>Falleth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεπεσεν</span></span>). Another gnomic aorist (second aorist active indicative) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκπιπτω</span></span> to fall out (off).</p>
<p><b>The grace </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η ευπρεπεια</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευπρεπης</span></span> well-looking, not in the N.T.), only here in N.T. Goodly appearance, beauty.</p>
<p><b>Of the fashion of it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του προσωπου αυτου</span></span>). "Of the face of it." The flower is pictured as having a "face," like a rose or lily.</p>
<p><b>Perisheth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απωλετο</span></span>). Another gnomic aorist (second aorist middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολλυμ</span></span>, to destroy, but intransitive here, to perish). The beautiful rose is pitiful when withered.</p>
<p><b>Shall fade away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρανθησετα</span></span>). Future passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαραινω</span></span>, old verb, to extinguish a flame, a light. Used of roses in Wisdom 2:8.</p>
<p><b>Goings </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορειαις</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορευω</span></span> to journey, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:22">Luke 13:22</span> (of Christ's journey toward Jerusalem). The rich man's travels will come to "journey's end." </p>
