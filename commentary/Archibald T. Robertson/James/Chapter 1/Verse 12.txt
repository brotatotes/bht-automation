<p><b> Endureth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπομενε</span></span>). Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπομενω</span></span>. Cf. verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:3">James 1:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Temptation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειρασμον</span></span>). Real temptation here. See verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:2">James 1:2</span> for "trials."</p>
<p><b>When he hath been approved </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκιμος γενομενος</span></span>). "Having become approved," with direct reference to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το δοκιμιον</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:3">James 1:3</span>. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:4">Romans 5:4</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκιμη</span></span> (approval after test as of gold or silver). This beatitude (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακαριος</span></span>) is for the one who has come out unscathed. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:9">1 Timothy 6:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>The crown of life </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον στεφανον της ζωης</span></span>). The same phrase occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:10">Revelation 2:10</span>. It is the genitive of apposition, life itself being the crown as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:4">1 Peter 5:4</span>. This crown is "an honourable ornament" (Ropes), with possibly no reference to the victor's crown (garland of leaves) as with Paul in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:25">1 Corinthians 9:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:8">2 Timothy 4:8</span>, nor to the linen fillet (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαδημα</span></span>) of royalty (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+20:3">Psalms 20:3</span>, where <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στεφανος</span></span> is used like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαδημα</span></span>, the kingly crown). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Στεφανος</span></span> has a variety of uses. Cf. the thorn chaplet on Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:29">Matthew 27:29</span>).</p>
<p><b>The Lord </b>. Not in the oldest Greek MSS., but clearly implied as the subject of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επηγγειλατο</span></span> (<b> he promised </b>, first aorist middle indicative). </p>
