<p><b> When he is drawn away by his own lust </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο της ιδιας επιθυμιας εξελκομενος</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Επιθυμια</span></span> is old word for craving (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιθυμεω</span></span>, to have a desire for) either good (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:23">Philippians 1:23</span>) or evil (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:7">Romans 7:7</span>) as here. Like a fish drawn out from his retreat.</p>
<p><b>Enticed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δελεαζομενος</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δελεαζω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δελεαρ</span></span> (bait), to catch fish by bait or to hunt with snares and Philo has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υφ' ηδονης δελεαζετα</span></span> (is enticed by pleasure). In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:14">2 Peter 2:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:18">2 Peter 2:18</span>. Allured by definite bait. </p>
