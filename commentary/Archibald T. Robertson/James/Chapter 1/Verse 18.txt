<p><b> Of his own will </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βουληθεις</span></span>). First aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βουλομα</span></span>. Repeating the metaphor of birth in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:15">James 1:15</span>, but in good sense. God as Father acted deliberately of set purpose.</p>
<p><b>He brought us forth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεκυησεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκυεω</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:15">James 1:15</span>), only here of the father (4 Macc. 15:17), not of the mother. Regeneration, not birth of all men, though God is the Father in the sense of creation of all men (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:28">Acts 17:28</span>).</p>
<p><b>By the word of truth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογω αληθειας</span></span>). Instrumental case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογω</span></span>. The reference is thus to the gospel message of salvation even without the article (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:7">2 Corinthians 6:7</span>) as here, and certainly with the article (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:5">Colossians 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:13">Ephesians 1:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:15">2 Timothy 2:15</span>). The message marked by truth (genitive case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αληθειας</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>That we should be </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το εινα ημας</span></span>). Purpose clause <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το</span></span> and the infinitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εινα</span></span> with the accusative of general reference <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημας</span></span> (as to us).</p>
<p><b>A kind of first-fruits </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαρχην τινα</span></span>). "Some first-fruits" (old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαρχομα</span></span>), of Christians of that age. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:5">Romans 16:5</span>. </p>
