<p><b> Wherefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διο</span></span>). Because of this principle. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:25">Ephesians 4:25</span>.</p>
<p><b>Putting away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποθεμενο</span></span>). Second aorist middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποτιθημ</span></span>, to put off, metaphor of removing clothing as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:12">Romans 13:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:8">Colossians 3:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:22">Ephesians 4:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:25">Ephesians 4:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:1">1 Peter 2:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Filthiness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρυπαριαν</span></span>). Late word (Plutarch) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρυπαρος</span></span>, dirty (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:2">James 2:2</span>), here only in N.T. Surely a dirty garment.</p>
<p><b>Overflowing of wickedness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περισσειαν κακιας</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Περισσεια</span></span> is a late word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περισσος</span></span>, abundant, exceeding), only four times in N.T., in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:2">2 Corinthians 8:2</span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρας</span></span> (of joy), in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:17">Romans 5:17</span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριτος</span></span> (of grace). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κακια</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακος</span></span>, evil) can be either general like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρυπαρια</span></span> (filthiness, naughtiness), or special like "malice." But any of either sense is a "superfluity."</p>
<p><b>With meekness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν πραυτητ</span></span>). In docility. "The contrast is with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οργη</span></span> rather than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακιας</span></span>" (Ropes).</p>
<p><b>The implanted word </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον εμφυτον λογον</span></span>). This old verbal adjective (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμφυω</span></span> to implant, to grow in), only here in N.T., meaning properly ingrown, inborn, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμφυτευτον</span></span> (engrafted). It is "the rooted word" (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:18">James 1:18</span>), sown in the heart as the soil or garden of God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:3-23">Matthew 13:3-23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:13">Matthew 15:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:6">1 Corinthians 3:6</span>).</p>
<p><b>Able to save </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυναμενον σωσα</span></span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:9">1 Peter 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:14">James 2:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:12">James 4:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:20">James 5:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:16">Romans 1:16</span>. Ultimate salvation (effective aorist active infinitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωσα</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωζω</span></span>). </p>
