<p><b> But be ye </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινεσθε δε</span></span>). Rather, "But keep on becoming" (present middle imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Doers of the word </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιητα λογου</span></span>). Old word for agent (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-της</span></span>) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span> to do as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:11">James 4:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:13">Romans 2:13</span>, but in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:28">Acts 17:28</span> our "poet" (long regarded as a "doer" or "maker").</p>
<p><b>Hearers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακροατα</span></span>). Old word for agent again from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακροαμα</span></span> (to be a hearer), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:13">Romans 2:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Deluding yourselves </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραλογιζομενο εαυτους</span></span>). Present middle (direct) participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραλογιζομα</span></span>, to reckon aside (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span>) and so wrong, to cheat, to deceive. Redundant reflexive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαυτους</span></span> with the middle. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:4">Colossians 2:4</span>. Such a man does not delude anyone but himself. </p>
