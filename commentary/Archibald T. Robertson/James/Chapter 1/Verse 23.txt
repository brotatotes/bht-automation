<p><b> And not a doer </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ου ποιητης</span></span>). Condition of first class, assumed as true, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου</span></span> (rather than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span>) contrasts <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιητης</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακροατης</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Unto a man beholding </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανδρ κατανοουντ</span></span>). Associative instrumental case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εοικεν</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:6">James 1:6</span>. Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανδρ</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:8">James 1:8</span> in contrast with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γυναικ</span></span> (woman), not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωπω</span></span> (general term for man). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατανοεω</span></span> to put the mind down on (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα, νους</span></span>), to consider attentively, to take note of, as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:24">James 1:24</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατενοησεν</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>His natural face </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το προσωπον της γενεσεως αυτου</span></span>). "The face of his birth" (origin, lineage, nativity). For this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενεσις</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:6">James 3:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+1:1">Matthew 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+1:18">Matthew 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:13">Luke 1:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>In a mirror </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν εσοπτρω</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισ, οπτω</span></span>) in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:12">1 Corinthians 13:12</span>. The mirrors of the ancients were not of glass, but of polished metal (of silver or usually of copper and tin). See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατοπτριζομα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:18">2 Corinthians 3:18</span>. </p>
