<p><b> He that looketh into </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο παρακυψας</span></span>). First aorist active articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρακυπτω</span></span>, old verb, to stoop and look into (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:5">John 20:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:11">John 20:11</span>), to gaze carefully by the side of, to peer into or to peep into (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:12">1 Peter 1:12</span>). Here the notion of beside (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span>) or of stooping (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυπτω</span></span>) is not strong. Sometimes, as Hort shows, the word means only a cursory glance, but the contrast with verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:24">James 1:24</span> seems to preclude that here.</p>
<p><b>The perfect law </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νομον τελειον</span></span>). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελειον</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:17">James 1:17</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:12">Romans 7:12</span> for Paul's idea of the law of God. James here refers to the word of truth (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:18">James 1:18</span>), the gospel of grace (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:2">Galatians 6:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:2">Romans 12:2</span>).</p>
<p><b>The law of liberty </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον της ελευθεριας</span></span>). "That of liberty," explaining why it is "perfect" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:12">James 2:12</span> also), rests on the work of Christ, whose truth sets us free (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:32">John 8:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:16">2 Corinthians 3:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:2">Romans 8:2</span>).</p>
<p><b>And so continueth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα παραμεινας</span></span>). First aorist active articular participle again of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραμενω</span></span>, parallel with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρακυψας</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Παραμενω</span></span> is to stay beside, and see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:25">Philippians 1:25</span> for contrast with the simplex <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Being </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενομενος</span></span>). Rather, "having become" (second aorist middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span> to become).</p>
<p><b>Not a hearer that forgetteth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ ακροατης επιλησμονης</span></span>). "Not a hearer of forgetfulness" (descriptive genitive, marked by forgetfulness). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Επιλησμονη</span></span> is a late and rare word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλησμων</span></span>, forgetful, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλανθομα</span></span>, to forget, as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:24">James 1:24</span>), here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>But a doer that worketh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα ποιητης εργου</span></span>). "But a doer of work," a doer marked by work (descriptive genitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργου</span></span>), not by mere listening or mere talk.</p>
<p><b>In his doing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη ποιησε αυτου</span></span>). Another beatitude with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακαριος</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:12">James 1:12</span>, like the Beatitudes in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:3-12">Matthew 5:3-12</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ποιησις</span></span> is an old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span> for the act of doing), only here in N.T. </p>
