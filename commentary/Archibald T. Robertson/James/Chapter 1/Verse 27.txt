<p><b> Pure religion and undefiled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θρησκεια καθαρα κα αμιαντος</span></span>). Numerous examples in papyri and inscriptions of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θρησκεια</span></span> for ritual and reverential worship in the Roman Empire (Moulton and Milligan's <i>Vocabulary</i>; Deissmann, <i>St. Paul</i>, p. 251). As Hort shows, this is not a definition of religion or religious worship, but only a pertinent illustration of the right spirit of religion which leads to such acts.</p>
<p><b>Before our God and Father </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα τω θεω κα πατρ</span></span>). By the side of (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span>) and so from God's standpoint (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:27">Mark 10:27</span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αμιαντος</span></span> (compound verbal adjective, alpha privative, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιαινω</span></span> to defile), puts in negative form (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:4">James 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:6">James 1:6</span>) the idea in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθαρα</span></span> (pure, clean). This (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτη</span></span>). Feminine demonstrative pronoun in the predicate agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θρησκεια</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>To visit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισκεπτεσθα</span></span>). Epexegetic (explaining <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτη</span></span>) present middle infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισκεπτομα</span></span>, common verb to go to see, to inspect, present tense for habit of going to see. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:36">Matthew 25:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:43">Matthew 25:43</span> for visiting the sick.</p>
<p><b>The fatherless and widows </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορφανους κα χηρας</span></span>). "The natural objects of charity in the community" (Ropes). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ορφανος</span></span> is old word for bereft of father or mother or both. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:18">John 14:18</span>. Note order (orphans before widows).</p>
<p><b>Unspotted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασπιλον</span></span>). Old adjective (alpha privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπιλος</span></span>, spot), spotless. This the more important of the two illustrations and the hardest to execute.</p>
<p><b>To keep </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρειν</span></span>). Present active infinitive, "to keep on keeping oneself un-specked from the world" (a world, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοσμος</span></span>, full of dirt and slime that bespatters the best of men). </p>
