<p><b> My brethren </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδελφο μου</span></span>). Transition to a new topic as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:19">James 1:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:5">James 2:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:14">James 2:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:1">James 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:7">James 5:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>Hold not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη εχετε</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span> with negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span>, exhortation to stop holding or not to have the habit of holding in the fashion condemned.</p>
<p><b>The faith of our Lord Jesus Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την πιστιν του κυριου ημων Ιησου Χριστου</span></span>). Clearly objective genitive, not subjective (faith of), but "faith in our Lord Jesus Christ," like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχετε πιστιν θεου</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:22">Mark 11:22</span>), "have faith in God." See the same objective genitive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστις</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:6">Acts 3:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:16">Galatians 2:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:22">Romans 3:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:12">Revelation 14:12</span>. Note also the same combination as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:1">James 1:1</span> "our Lord Jesus Christ" (there on a par with God).</p>
<p><b>The Lord of Glory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της δοξης</span></span>). Simply "the Glory." No word for "Lord" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυριου</span></span>) in the Greek text. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Της δοξης</span></span> clearly in apposition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του κυριου Ιησου Χριστου</span></span>. James thus terms "our Lord Jesus Christ" the Shekinah Glory of God. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:5">Hebrews 9:5</span> for "the cherubim of Glory." Other New Testament passages where Jesus is pictured as the Glory are <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:4">Romans 9:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:6">2 Corinthians 4:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:17">Ephesians 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:3">Hebrews 1:3</span>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:9">2 Corinthians 8:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:5-11">Philippians 2:5-11</span>.</p>
<p><b>With respect of persons </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν προσωπολημψιαις</span></span>). A Christian word, like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσωπολημπτης</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:34">Acts 10:34</span>) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσωπολημπτειτε</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:9">James 2:9</span>), not in LXX or any previous Greek, but made from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσωπον λαμβανειν</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:21">Luke 20:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:6">Galatians 2:6</span>), which is <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> Hebrew idiom for <i>panim nasa</i>, "to lift up the face on a person," to be favorable and so partial to him. See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσωπολημψια</span></span> in this sense of partiality (respect of persons) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:11">Romans 2:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:25">Colossians 3:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:9">Ephesians 6:9</span> (nowhere else in N.T.). Do not show partiality. </p>
