<p><b> He that said </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ειπων</span></span>)</p>
<p><b>--said also </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειπεν κα</span></span>). The unity of the law lies in the Lawgiver who spoke both prohibitions (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> and the aorist active subjunctive in each one, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μοιχευσηισ, φονευσηις</span></span>). The order here is that of B in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:20">James 2:20</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:20">Luke 18:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:9">Romans 13:9</span>), but not in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:21">Matthew 5:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:27">Matthew 5:27</span> (with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου</span></span> and future indicative).</p>
<p><b>Now if thou dost not commit adultery, but killest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε δε ου μοιχευεισ, φονευεις δε</span></span>). Condition of first class with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου</span></span> (not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span>) because of the contrast with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span>, whereas <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε μη</span></span> would mean "unless," a different idea. So <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:23">James 1:23</span>.</p>
<p><b>A transgressor of the law </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραβατης νομου</span></span>) as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:9">James 2:9</span>. Murder springs out of anger (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:21-26">Matthew 5:21-26</span>). People free from fleshly sins have often "made their condemnation of fleshly sins an excuse for indulgence towards spiritual sins" (Hort). </p>
