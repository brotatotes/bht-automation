<p><b> So speak ye, and so do </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτως λαλειτε κα ουτως ποιειτε</span></span>). Present active imperatives as a habit. For the combination see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:19-21">James 1:19-21</span> contrasted with <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:22-25">James 1:22-25</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:26">James 1:26</span> with <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:27">James 1:27</span>.</p>
<p><b>By a law of liberty </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια νομου ελευθεριας</span></span>). The law pictured in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:25">James 1:25</span>, but law, after all, not individual caprice of "personal liberty." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:12">Romans 2:12</span> for this same use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρινω</span></span> in the sense of accompaniment as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:27">Romans 2:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:11">Romans 4:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:20">Romans 14:20</span>. "Under the law of liberty." </p>
