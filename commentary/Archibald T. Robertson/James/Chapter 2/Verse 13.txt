<p><b> Without mercy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανελεος</span></span>). Found here only save a doubtful papyrus example (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανελεως</span></span>) for the vernacular <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανιλεως</span></span> and the Attic <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανηλεης</span></span>. For this principle of requital see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:7">Matthew 5:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:14">Matthew 6:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:1">Matthew 7:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:33">Matthew 18:33</span>.</p>
<p><b>Glorieth against </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατακαυχατα</span></span>). Present middle indicative of the old compound verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατακαυχαομα</span></span>, to exult over (down), in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:14">James 3:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:18">Romans 11:18</span>. Only mercy can triumph over justice with God and men. "Mercy is clothed with the divine glory and stands by the throne of God" (Chrysostom). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:31-39">Romans 8:31-39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:13">Matthew 9:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:7">Matthew 12:7</span>. </p>
