<p><b> If a brother or sister be naked </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν αδελφος η αδελφη γυμνο υπαρχωσιν</span></span>). Condition again of third class (supposable case) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπαρχω</span></span>, to exist, in the plural though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η</span></span> (or) is used and not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span> (and). Hence <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γυμνο</span></span> is masculine plural in the predicate nominative. It does not here mean absolutely naked, but without sufficient clothing as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:36">Matthew 25:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:7">John 21:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:16">Acts 19:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>In lack of daily food </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λειπομενο της εφημερου τροφης</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λειπω</span></span> and ablative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τροφης</span></span> like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λειπετα σοφιας</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:5">James 1:5</span>). The old adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφημερος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο επ ημεραν ων</span></span>, that which is for a day) occurs here only in the N.T., though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφημερια</span></span> (daily routine) is found in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:5">Luke 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:8">Luke 1:8</span>. This phrase occurs in Diodorus, but not in LXX. </p>
