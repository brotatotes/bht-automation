<p><b> If it have not works </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν μη εχη εργα</span></span>). Another condition of the third class with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> and the present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>, "if it keep on not having works."</p>
<p><b>In itself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθ' εαυτην</span></span>). In and of itself (according to itself), inwardly and outwardly dead (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νεκρα</span></span>). Same idiom in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:16">Acts 28:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:22">Romans 14:22</span>. It is a dead faith. </p>
