<p><b> Thou seest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλεπεις</span></span>). Obvious enough with any eyes to see. This may be a question, seest thou?</p>
<p><b>Wrought with </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνηργε</span></span>). Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεργεω</span></span>, old verb for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:28">Romans 8:28</span>. Followed by associative-instrumental case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργοις</span></span>. Faith cooperated with the deed of offering up Isaac.</p>
<p><b>Was made perfect </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετελειωθη</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελειοω</span></span>, to carry to the end, to complete like love in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:18">1 John 4:18</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:4">James 1:4</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελειον εργον</span></span>. </p>
