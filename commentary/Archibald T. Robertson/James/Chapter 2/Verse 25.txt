<p><b> Rahab the harlot </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ρααβ η πορνη</span></span>). Her vicious life she left behind, but the name clung to her always. For our purposes the argument of James may seem stronger without the example of Rahab (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jos+2:1-21">Joshua 2:1-21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jos+6:17">Joshua 6:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jos+6:22-25">Joshua 6:22-25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+1:5">Matthew 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:31">Hebrews 11:31</span>). It is even said in Jewish Midrash that Rahab married Joshua and became an ancestor of Jeremiah and Ezekiel.</p>
<p><b>In that she received </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποδεξαμενη</span></span>). First aorist middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποδεχομα</span></span>, to welcome.</p>
<p><b>The messengers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους αγγελους</span></span>). Original meaning of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγγελος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:10">Matthew 11:10</span>). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:31">Hebrews 11:31</span> we have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατασκοπους</span></span> (spies, scouts).</p>
<p><b>Sent out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκβαλουσα</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκβαλλω</span></span>, to hurl out.</p>
<p><b>Another way </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετερα οδω</span></span>). "By another way" (instrumental case), by a window instead of a door (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jos+2:15">Joshua 2:15</span>). </p>
