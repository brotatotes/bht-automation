<p><b> And ye have regard to </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιβλεψητε δε επ</span></span>). First aorist active subjunctive (still with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:2">James 2:2</span>) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιβλεπω</span></span>, followed by repeated preposition <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>, to gaze upon, old compound, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:48">Luke 1:48</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:38">Luke 9:38</span>.</p>
<p><b>Weareth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φορουντα</span></span>). "Wearing," present active participle of the old frequentative verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φορεω</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φερω</span></span>), to bear constantly, to wear (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:8">Matthew 11:8</span>). Note repeated article <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την</span></span> (the) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσθητα</span></span> pointing to verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:2">James 2:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>And say </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ειπητε</span></span>). Continuing the third-class condition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειπον</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Sit thou here in a good place </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συ καθου ωδε καλως</span></span>). Emphatic position of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συ</span></span>, "Do thou sit here in a good place." Present middle imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθημα</span></span> to sit for the literary <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθησο</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:6">Matthew 23:6</span> for the first seats in the synagogue (places of honour).</p>
<p><b>And ye say to the poor man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα τω πτωχω ειπητε</span></span>). Third class condition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> continued as before (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειπητε</span></span>). Note article <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω</span></span> pointing to verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:2">James 2:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>Stand thou there </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συ στηθ εκε</span></span>). Second aorist (intransitive) active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span>, to place. Ingressive aorist, Take a stand. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Συ</span></span> emphatic again. The MSS. vary in the position of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκε</span></span> (there).</p>
<p><b>Or sit under my footstool </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η καθου υπο το υποποδιον μου</span></span>). For this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο</span></span> "down against" or "down beside" see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+19:17">Exodus 19:17</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο το ορος</span></span> ("at the foot of the mountain") and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο σε</span></span> ("at thy feet") (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+33:3">Deuteronomy 33:3</span>). Conquerors often placed their feet on the necks of the victims (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:43">Luke 20:43</span>). </p>
