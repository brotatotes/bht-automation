<p><b> Are ye not divided in your own mind? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου διεκριθητε εν εαυτοισ;</span></span>). First aorist (gnomic) passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακρινω</span></span>, to separate, conclusion of the third-class condition (future) in a rhetorical question in the gnomic aorist (as if past) with ou expecting an affirmative answer. For this idiom (gnomic aorist) in a conclusion of the third-class condition see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:28">1 Corinthians 7:28</span>. "Were ye not divided in (among) yourselves?" Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:6">James 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:21">Matthew 21:21</span>.</p>
<p><b>Judges with evil thoughts </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κριτα διαλογισμων πονηρων</span></span>). Descriptive genitive as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:25">James 1:25</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Διαλογισμος</span></span> is an old word for reasoning (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:21">Romans 1:21</span>). Reasoning is not necessarily evil, but see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:19">Matthew 15:19</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πονηρο</span></span>) and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:21">Mark 7:21</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακο</span></span>) for evil reasonings, and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:8">1 Timothy 2:8</span> without an adjective. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:8">James 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:8">James 4:8</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διψυχος</span></span>. They are guilty of partiality (a divided mind) as between the two strangers. </p>
