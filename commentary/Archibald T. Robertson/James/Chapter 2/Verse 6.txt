<p><b> But ye have dishonoured the poor man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμεις δε ητιμασατε τον πτωχον</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ατιμαζω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ατιμος</span></span>, dishonoured (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:57">Matthew 13:57</span>). In the act of partiality pictured in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:3">James 2:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Oppress you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταδυναστευουσιν υμων</span></span>). Not very common compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταδυναστευω</span></span>, present active indicative, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυναστης</span></span>, potentate, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:52">Luke 1:52</span>), used of the devil in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:38">Acts 10:38</span> (only other N.T. example). Examples in papyri of harsh treatment by men in authority. Already poor Christians are feeling pressure from rich Jews as overlords.</p>
<p><b>Drag you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελκουσιν υμας</span></span>). Old and vigorous word for violent treatment, as of Paul in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:19">Acts 16:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:30">Acts 21:30</span>. Cf. such violence in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:58">Luke 12:58</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:3">Acts 8:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Before the judgment-seats </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις κριτηρια</span></span>). "To courts of justice" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:2">1 Corinthians 6:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:4">1 Corinthians 6:4</span> (only other N.T. examples). Common in the papyri in this sense. From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρινω</span></span> to judge, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κριτης</span></span> (judge), place where judgment is given. </p>
