<p><b> Blaspheme </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλασφημουσιν</span></span>). Present active indicative of common verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλασφημεω</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλασφημος</span></span>, speaking evil, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλαξ</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλαπτω</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φημη</span></span>), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:65">Luke 22:65</span>.</p>
<p><b>The honourable name </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το καλον ονομα</span></span>). "The beautiful name."</p>
<p><b>By the which ye were called </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το επικληθεν εφ' υμας</span></span>). "The one called upon you" (first aorist passive articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επικαλεω</span></span>, to put a name upon, to give a surname to, as <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:18">Acts 10:18</span>). What name is that? Almost certainly the name of Christ as we see it in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:26">Acts 11:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:28">Acts 26:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:14">1 Peter 4:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:16">1 Peter 4:16</span>. It was blasphemy to speak against Christ as some Jews and Gentiles were doing (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:45">Acts 13:45</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:6">Acts 18:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:11">Acts 26:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:3">1 Corinthians 12:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:13">1 Timothy 1:13</span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:17">Acts 15:17</span>. </p>
