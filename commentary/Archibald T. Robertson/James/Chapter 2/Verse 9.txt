<p><b> But if ye have respect of persons </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε δε προσωπολημπτειτε</span></span>). Condition of first class by contrast with that in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:8">James 2:8</span>. For this verb (present active indicative), formed from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσωπον λαμβανω</span></span>, here alone in the N.T., see in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:1">James 2:1</span>. A direct reference to the partiality there pictured.</p>
<p><b>Ye commit sin </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτιαν εργαζεσθε</span></span>). "Ye work a sin." A serious charge, apparently, for what was regarded as a trifling fault. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:23">Matthew 7:23</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο εργαζομενο την ανομιαν</span></span> (ye that work iniquity), an apparent reminiscence of the words of Jesus there (from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+6:8">Psalms 6:8</span>).</p>
<p><b>Being convicted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεγχομενο</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεγχω</span></span>, to convict by proof of guilt (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:20">John 3:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:9">John 8:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:46">John 8:46</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:24">1 Corinthians 14:24</span>).</p>
<p><b>As transgressors </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως παραβατα</span></span>). For this word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραβαινω</span></span>, to step across, to transgress, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:18">Galatians 2:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:25">Romans 2:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:27">Romans 2:27</span>. See this very sin of partiality condemned in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="le+19:15">Leviticus 19:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+1:17">Deuteronomy 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+16:19">Deuteronomy 16:19</span>. To the law and to the testimony. </p>
