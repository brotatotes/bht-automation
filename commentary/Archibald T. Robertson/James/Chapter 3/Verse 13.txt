<p><b> Who </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Τις</span></span>). Rhetorical interrogative like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:11">Luke 11:11</span>. Common in Paul and characteristic of the diatribe. James here returns to the standpoint of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:1">James 3:1</span> about many teachers. Speech and wisdom are both liable to abuse (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:5">1 Corinthians 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:17">1 Corinthians 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:1-3">1 Corinthians 2:1-3</span>).</p>
<p><b>Wise and understanding </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σοφος κα επιστημων</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Σοφος</span></span> is used for the practical teacher (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:1">James 3:1</span>), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιστημων</span></span> (old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισταμα</span></span>, here only in N.T.) for an expert, a skilled and scientific person with a tone of superiority. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+1:13">Deuteronomy 1:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+1:15">Deuteronomy 1:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+4:6">Deuteronomy 4:6</span>, the two terms are practically synonyms.</p>
<p><b>Let him shew </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δειξατω</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεικνυμ</span></span>, old verb to show. As about faith in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:18">James 2:18</span>. Emphatic position of this verb.</p>
<p><b>By his good life </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ της καλης αναστροφης</span></span>). For this literary <i>Koine</i> word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναστρεφομα</span></span> (walk, conduct) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:13">Galatians 1:13</span>. Actions speak louder than words even in the case of the professional wise man. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:15">1 Peter 1:15</span>.</p>
<p><b>In meekness of wisdom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν πραυτητ σοφιας</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:21">James 1:21</span> of the listener, so here of the teacher. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:5">Matthew 5:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:29">Matthew 11:29</span> and Zac 9:9 of King Messiah quoted in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:5">Matthew 21:5</span>. Startling combination. </p>
