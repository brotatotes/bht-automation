<p><b> Bitter jealousy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζηλον πικρον</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ζηλος</span></span> occurs in N.T. in good sense (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:17">John 2:17</span>) and bad sense (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:17">Acts 5:17</span>). Pride of knowledge is evil (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+8:1">1 Corinthians 8:1</span>) and leaves a bitter taste. See "root of bitterness" in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:14">Hebrews 12:14</span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:31">Ephesians 4:31</span>). This is a condition of the first class.</p>
<p><b>Faction </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εριθιαν</span></span>). Late word, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εριθος</span></span> (hireling, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εριθευω</span></span> to spin wool), a pushing forward for personal ends, partisanship, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:16">Philippians 1:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>In your heart </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη καρδια υμων</span></span>). The real fountain (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πηγη</span></span>, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:11">James 3:11</span>).</p>
<p><b>Glory not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη κατακαυχασθε</span></span>). Present middle imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατακαυχαομα</span></span>, for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:13">James 2:13</span>. Wisdom is essential for the teacher. Boasting arrogance disproves the possession of wisdom.</p>
<p><b>Lie not against the truth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευδεσθε κατα της αληθειας</span></span>). Present middle imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευδομα</span></span>, old verb, to play false, with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> carried over. Lying against the truth is futile. By your conduct do not belie the truth which you teach; a solemn and needed lesson. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:18">Romans 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:18">Romans 2:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:20">Romans 2:20</span>. </p>
