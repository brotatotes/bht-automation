<p><b> This wisdom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτη η σοφια</span></span>). All talk and disproved by the life, counterfeit wisdom, not real wisdom (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:5">James 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:17">James 3:17</span>).</p>
<p><b>Coming down from above </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατερχομενη ανωθεν</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:5">James 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:17">James 1:17</span>. All true wisdom comes from God.</p>
<p><b>Earthly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιγειος</span></span>). Old adjective, on earth (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επι, γη</span></span>), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:12">John 3:12</span>, then with earthly limitations (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:19">Philippians 3:19</span>), as here.</p>
<p><b>Sensual </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψυχικη</span></span>). Old adjective, belonging to the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψυχη</span></span>, the sensuous or animal life (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:14">1 Corinthians 2:14</span> and here).</p>
<p><b>Devilish </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δαιμονιωδης</span></span>). Late adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δαιμονιον</span></span> (demon) and so demoniacal or demon-like, here only in N.T. </p>
