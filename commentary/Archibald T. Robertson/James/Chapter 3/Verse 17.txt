<p><b> First pure </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτον μεν αγνη</span></span>). First in rank and time. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hαγνος</span></span> is from the same root as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγιος</span></span> (holy), old adjective, pure from fault, not half-good and half-bad, like that above.</p>
<p><b>Then peaceable </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επειτα ειρηνικη</span></span>). Old adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειρηνη</span></span> (peace), loving peace here, bringing peace in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:11">Hebrews 12:11</span> (only N.T. examples). But clearly great as peace is, purity (righteousness) comes before peace and peace at any price is not worth the having. Hence Jesus spurned the devil's peace of surrender.</p>
<p><b>Gentle </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιεικης</span></span>). Old adjective (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εικος</span></span>, reasonable, fair), equitable (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:5">Philippians 4:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:18">1 Peter 2:18</span>). No English word renders it clearly.</p>
<p><b>Easy to be entreated </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευπειθης</span></span>). Old adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ, πειθομα</span></span>), compliant, approachable. Only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Mercy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεους</span></span>). Practical help (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:13">James 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:16">James 2:16</span>).</p>
<p><b>Good fruits </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καρπων αγαθων</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Καλο καρπο</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:17">Matthew 7:17</span>. Good deeds the fruit of righteousness (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:11">Philippians 1:11</span>).</p>
<p><b>Without variance </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδιακριτος</span></span>). Late verbal adjective (from alpha privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακρινω</span></span>, to distinguish). "Unhesitating," not doubting (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακρινομενος</span></span>) like the man in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:6">James 1:6</span>. Here only in N.T. This wisdom does not put a premium on doubt.</p>
<p><b>Without hypocrisy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανυποκριτος</span></span>). Late and rare verbal adjective (alpha privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποκρινω</span></span>). Not hypocritical, sincere, unfeigned (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:9">Romans 12:9</span>). </p>
