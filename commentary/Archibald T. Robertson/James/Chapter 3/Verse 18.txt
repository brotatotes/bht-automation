<p><b> Is sown in peace </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ειρηνη σπειρετα</span></span>). Present passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπειρω</span></span>, to sow. The seed which bears the fruit is sown, but James catches up the metaphor of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καρπος</span></span> (fruit) from verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:17">James 3:17</span>. Only in peace is the fruit of righteousness found.</p>
<p><b>For them that make peace </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις ποιουσιν ειρηνην</span></span>). Dative case of the articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:15">Ephesians 2:15</span> for this phrase (doing peace), and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:20">Colossians 1:20</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειρηνοποιεω</span></span>, of Christ, and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:9">Matthew 5:9</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειρηνοποιο</span></span> (peacemakers). Only those who act peaceably are entitled to peace. </p>
