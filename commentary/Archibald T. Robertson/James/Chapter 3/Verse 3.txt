<p><b> If we put </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε βαλλομεν</span></span>). Condition of the first class assumed as true.</p>
<p><b>The horses' bridles </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των ιππων τους χαλινους</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hιππων</span></span> (genitive plural of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιππος</span></span>, horse, old word, in N.T. only here except in the Apocalypse), put first because the first of the several illustrations of the power and the peril of the tongue. This is the only N.T. example of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαλινος</span></span>, old word for bridle (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαλαω</span></span> to slacken, let down), except <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:20">Revelation 14:20</span>.</p>
<p><b>That they may obey us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το πειθεσθα αυτους ημιν</span></span>). Present middle infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειθω</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το</span></span> as a purpose clause with the dative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημιν</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειθεσθα</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτους</span></span> the accusative of general reference.</p>
<p><b>We turn about </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταγομεν</span></span>). Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταγω</span></span>, late compound to change the direction (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα</span></span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγω</span></span>), to guide, in N.T. only here and verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:4">James 3:4</span>. The body of the horse follows his mouth, guided by the bridle. </p>
