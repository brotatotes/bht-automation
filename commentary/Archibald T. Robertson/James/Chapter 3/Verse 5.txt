<p><b> A little member </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μικρον μελος</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μελος</span></span> is old and common word for members of the human body (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:12">1 Corinthians 12:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:13">Romans 6:13</span>, etc.).</p>
<p><b>Boasteth great things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεγαλα αυχε</span></span>). Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυχεω</span></span>, old verb, here only in N.T. The best MSS. here separate <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεγαλα</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυχεω</span></span>, though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεγαλαυχεω</span></span> does occur in Aeschylus, Plato, etc. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μεγαλα</span></span> is in contrast with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μικρον</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>How much--how small </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηλικον--ηλικην</span></span>). The same relative form for two indirect questions together, "What-sized fire kindles what-sized forest?" For double interrogatives see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:24">Mark 15:24</span>. The verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπτε</span></span> is present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπτω</span></span>, to set fire to, to kindle (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:49">Luke 12:49</span>, only other N.T. example except some MSS. in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:2">Acts 28:2</span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hυλην</span></span> is accusative case, object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπτε</span></span>, and occurs here only in N.T., though old word for forest, wood. Forest fires were common in ancient times as now, and were usually caused by small sparks carelessly thrown. </p>
