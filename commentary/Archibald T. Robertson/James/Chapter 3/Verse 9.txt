<p><b> Therewith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αυτη</span></span>). This instrumental use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> is not merely Hebraistic, but appears in late <i>Koine</i> writers (Moulton, <i>Prol.</i>, pp. 11f., 61f.). See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:6">Romans 15:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>We bless </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλογουμεν</span></span>). Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλογεω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλογος</span></span> (a good word, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ, λογος</span></span>), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:64">Luke 1:64</span> of God. "This is the highest function of speech" (Hort).</p>
<p><b>The Lord and Father </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον κυριον κα πατερα</span></span>). Both terms applied to God.</p>
<p><b>Curse we </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταρωμεθα</span></span>). Present middle indicative of the old compound verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταραομα</span></span>, to curse (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταρα</span></span> a curse), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:28">Luke 6:28</span>.</p>
<p><b>Which are made after the likeness of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους καθ' ομοιωσιν θεου γεγονοτας</span></span>). Second perfect articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοιωσις</span></span>, old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοιοω</span></span> (to make like), making like, here only in N.T. (from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+1:26">Genesis 1:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+9:6">Genesis 9:6</span>), the usual word being <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοιωμα</span></span>, resemblance (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:7">Philippians 2:7</span>). It is this image of God which sets man above the beasts. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:18">2 Corinthians 3:18</span>. </p>
