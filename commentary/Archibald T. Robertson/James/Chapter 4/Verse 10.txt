<p><b> Humble yourselves </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταπεινωθητε</span></span>). First aorist passive imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταπεινοω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταπεινος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:9">James 1:9</span>), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:4">Matthew 18:4</span>. The passive here has almost the middle or reflexive sense. The middle voice was already giving way to the passive. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:6">1 Peter 5:6</span> for this same form with the same promise of exaltation.</p>
<p><b>He shall exalt you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υψωσε υμας</span></span>). Future active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υψοω</span></span>, common verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υψος</span></span> (height), used by Jesus in contrast with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταπεινοω</span></span> as here (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:12">Matthew 23:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:11">Luke 14:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:14">Luke 18:14</span>). </p>
