<p><b> Go to now </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγε νυν</span></span>). Interjectional use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγε</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγω</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:1">James 5:1</span> (only N.T. instances) with a plural verb (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο λεγοντες</span></span>, present active articular participle, ye that say) as is common in ancient Greek like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδε νυν ηκουσατε</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:65">Matthew 26:65</span>).</p>
<p><b>Today or tomorrow </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σημερον η αυριον</span></span>). Correct text (Aleph B), not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span> (and).</p>
<p><b>Into this city </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τηνδε την πολιν</span></span>). Old demonstrative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδε</span></span>, rare in N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:39">Luke 10:39</span>) save in neuter plural <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταδε</span></span> (these things <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:11">Acts 21:11</span>). One would point out the city on the map (Mayor) as he made the proposal (we will go, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορευσομεθα</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>And spend a year there </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ποιησομεν εκε ενιαυτον</span></span>). Another future (active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span>). "We will do a year there."</p>
<p><b>And trade </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα εμπορευσομεθα</span></span>). Future middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμπορευομα</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν, πορευομα</span></span>, to go in), old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμπορος</span></span> (a merchant or trader, a drummer, one going in and getting the trade, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:45">Matthew 13:45</span>), a vivid picture of the Jewish merchants of the time.</p>
<p><b>And get gain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα κερδησομεν</span></span>). Future (Ionic form) active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κερδαινω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κερδος</span></span> (gain, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:21">Philippians 1:21</span>), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:26">Matthew 16:26</span>. </p>
