<p><b> For that ye ought to say </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντ του λεγειν υμας</span></span>). "Instead of the saying as to you" (genitive of the articular infinitive with the preposition <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντ</span></span> and the accusative of general reference with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγειν</span></span>), "instead of your saying."</p>
<p><b>If the Lord will </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν ο κυριος θελη</span></span>). Condition of the third class with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and the present active subjunctive (or first aorist active <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θελεση</span></span> in some MSS). The proper attitude of mind (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:21">Acts 18:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:19">1 Corinthians 4:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:7">1 Corinthians 16:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:19">Romans 1:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:19">Philippians 2:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:24">Philippians 2:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:3">Hebrews 6:3</span>), not to be uttered always in words like a charm. This Hellenistic formula was common among the ancient heathen, as today among modern Arabs like the Latin <i>deo volente</i>.</p>
<p><b>This or that </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτο η εκεινο</span></span>). Applicable to every act. </p>
