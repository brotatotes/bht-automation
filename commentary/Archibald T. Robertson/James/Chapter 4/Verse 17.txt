<p><b> To him that knoweth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδοτ</span></span>). Dative case of second perfect participle <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδως</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιδα</span></span>), and with the infinitive to know how, "to one knowing how."</p>
<p><b>To do good </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλον ποιειν</span></span>). "To do a good deed."</p>
<p><b>And doeth it not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα μη ποιουντ</span></span>). Dative again of the present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span>, "and to one not doing it." Cf. "not a doer" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:23">James 1:23</span>) and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:26">Matthew 7:26</span>.</p>
<p><b>Sin </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτια</span></span>). Unused knowledge of one's duty is sin, the sin of omission. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:23">Matthew 23:23</span>. </p>
