<p><b> More grace </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μειζονα χαριν</span></span>). "Greater grace." Greater than what? "Greater grace in view of the greater requirement" (Ropes), like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:20">Romans 5:20</span>. God does this.</p>
<p><b>Wherefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διο</span></span>). To prove this point James quotes <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="pr+3:34">Proverbs 3:34</span>.</p>
<p><b>God resisteth the proud </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο θεος υπερηφανοις αντιτασσετα</span></span>). Present middle (direct) indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντιτασσω</span></span>, old military term, to range in battle against, with dative case (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:2">Romans 13:2</span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:6">James 5:6</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hυπερηφανοις</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ, φαινομα</span></span>) is like our vernacular "stuck-up folks" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:30">Romans 1:30</span>), "haughty persons."</p>
<p><b>But giveth grace to the humble </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταπεινοις δε διδωσιν χαριν</span></span>). Anarthrous adjective again, "to humble or lowly persons," for which word see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:9">James 1:9</span>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:5-7">James 2:5-7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:1-6">James 5:1-6</span>. </p>
