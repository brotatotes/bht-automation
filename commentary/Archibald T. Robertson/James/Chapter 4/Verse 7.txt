<p><b> Be subject therefore unto God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποταγητε ουν τω θεω</span></span>). Second aorist (ingressive) passive imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποτασσω</span></span>, old verb, to range under (military term also). Same form in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:23">1 Peter 2:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:5">1 Peter 5:5</span>. With the dative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεω</span></span> (unto God). The aorist has the note of urgency in the imperative. Note the ten aorist imperatives in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:7-10">James 4:7-10</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποταγητε, αντιστητε, εγγισατε, καθαρισατε, αγνισατε, ταλαιπωρησατε, πενθησατε, κλαυσατε, μετατραπητω, ταπεινωθητε</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>But resist the devil </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντιστητε δε τω διαβολω</span></span>). Second aorist (ingressive) active (intransitive) imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθιστημ</span></span>, "take a stand against." Dative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαβολω</span></span>. Result of such a stand is that the devil will flee (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φευξετα</span></span>, future middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φευγω</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:8">1 Peter 5:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:11">Ephesians 6:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:17">Luke 10:17</span>. </p>
