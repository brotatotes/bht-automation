<p><b> Be afflicted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταλαιπωρησατε</span></span>). First aorist active imperative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταλαιπωρεω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταλαιπωρος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:24">Romans 7:24</span>), to endure toils, here only in N.T. Cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταλαιπωριαις</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:1">James 5:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Mourn </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πενθησατε</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πενθεω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πενθος</span></span> (mourning, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:9">James 4:9</span>), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:4">Matthew 5:4</span>. Often in N.T. joined as here with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλαιω</span></span>, to weep (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+16:10">Mark 16:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:25">Luke 6:25</span>). A call to the godly sorrow spoken of in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:10">2 Corinthians 7:10</span> (Mayor), like an O.T. prophet.</p>
<p><b>Weep </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλαυσατε</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλαιω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Laughter </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γελως</span></span>). Old word from Homer down, only here in N.T. as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γελαω</span></span>, to</p>
<p><b>laugh </b> (opposite of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλαιω</span></span>), in N.T. only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:21">Luke 6:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:25">Luke 6:25</span>, but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταγελαω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:53">Luke 8:53</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:40">Mark 5:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:24">Matthew 9:24</span>).</p>
<p><b>Be turned </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετατραπητω</span></span>). Second aorist passive imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετατρεπω</span></span>, old word, to turn about, to transmute, in Homer (not in Attic), here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Heaviness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηφειαν</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηφης</span></span> (of a downcast look, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φαη</span></span> eyes), hanging down of the eyes like the publican in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:13">Luke 18:13</span>, here only in N.T. </p>
