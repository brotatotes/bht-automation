<p><b> Come now, ye rich </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγε νυν ο πλουσιο</span></span>). Exclamatory interjection as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:13">James 4:13</span>. Direct address to the rich as a class as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:17">1 Timothy 6:17</span>. Apparently here James has in mind the rich as a class, whether believer, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:10">James 1:10</span>, or unbeliever, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:1">James 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:6">James 2:6</span>. The plea here is not directly for reform, but a warning of certain judgment (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:1-6">James 5:1-6</span>) and for Christians "a certain grim comfort in the hardships of poverty" (Ropes) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:7-11">James 5:7-11</span>.</p>
<p><b>Weep and howl </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλαυσατε ολολυζοντες</span></span>). "Burst into weeping (ingressive aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλαιω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:9">James 4:9</span>), howling with grief" (present active participle of the old onomatopoetic verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ολολυζω</span></span>, here only in N.T., like Latin <i>ululare</i>, with which compare <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλαλαζω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:38">Matthew 5:38</span>.</p>
<p><b>For your miseries </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ ταις ταλαιπωριαις υμων</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταλαιπωρος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:24">Romans 7:24</span>) and like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταλαιπωρεω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:9">James 4:9</span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τλαω</span></span> to endure and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πωρος</span></span> a callus).</p>
<p><b>That are coming upon you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταις επερχομεναις</span></span>). Present middle participle of the old compound <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επερχομα</span></span> to come upon, used here in futuristic prophetic sense. </p>
