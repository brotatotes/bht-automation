<p><b> For an example </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποδειγμα</span></span>). Late word for the old <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραδειγμα</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποδεικνυμ</span></span>, to copy under, to teach (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:47">Luke 6:47</span>), here for copy to be imitated as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:15">John 13:15</span>, as a warning (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:11">Hebrews 4:11</span>). Here predicate accusative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους προφητας</span></span> (the prophets) as the direct object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαβετε</span></span> (second aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Of suffering </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της κακοπαθιας</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακοπαθης</span></span> (suffering evil, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακοπαθεω</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:13">James 5:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:3">2 Timothy 2:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:9">2 Timothy 2:9</span>), here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Of patience </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακροθυμιας</span></span>). Like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακροθυμεω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:7">James 5:7</span>. See both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακροθυμια</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπομονη</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:6">2 Corinthians 4:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:11">Colossians 1:11</span> (the one restraint from retaliating, the other not easily succumbing).</p>
<p><b>In the name of </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω ονοματ</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+20:9">Jeremiah 20:9</span>. With the authority of the Lord (Deissmann, <i>Bible Studies</i>, p. 198). </p>
