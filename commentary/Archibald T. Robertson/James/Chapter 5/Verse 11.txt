<p><b> We call blessed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακαριζομεν</span></span>). Old word (present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακαριζω</span></span>), from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακαριος</span></span> (happy), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:48">Luke 1:48</span>. "We felicitate." As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:3">James 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:12">James 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="da+12:12">Daniel 12:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>Ye have heard </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκουσατε</span></span>). First aorist (constative) active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουω</span></span>. As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:21">Matthew 5:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:27">Matthew 5:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:33">Matthew 5:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:38">Matthew 5:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:43">Matthew 5:43</span>. Ropes suggests in the synagogues.</p>
<p><b>Of Job </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιωβ</span></span>). Job did complain, but he refused to renounce God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+1:21">Job 1:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+2:10">Job 2:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+13:15">Job 13:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+16:19">Job 16:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+19:25">Job 19:25</span>). He had become a stock illustration of loyal endurance.</p>
<p><b>Ye have seen </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδετε</span></span>). Second aorist (constative) active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span>. In Job's case.</p>
<p><b>The end of the Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το τελος κυριου</span></span>). The conclusion wrought by the Lord in Job's case (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+42:12">Job 42:12</span>).</p>
<p><b>Full of pity </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολυσπλαγχνος</span></span>). Late and rare compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολυσ, σπλαγχνον</span></span>), only here in N.T. It occurs also in Hermas (<i>Sim</i>. v. 7. 4; <i>Mand</i>. iv, 3). "Very kind."</p>
<p><b>Merciful </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικτιρμων</span></span>). Late and rare adjective (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικτειρω</span></span> to pity), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:36">Luke 6:36</span>. </p>
