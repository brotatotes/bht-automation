<p><b> Is any suffering? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακοπαθε τισ;</span></span>). See verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:10">James 5:10</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακοπαθια</span></span>. The verb in N.T. occurs only here and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:3">2 Timothy 2:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:9">2 Timothy 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:5">2 Timothy 4:5</span>. The lively interrogative is common in the diatribe and suits the style of James.</p>
<p><b>Among you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν υμιν</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:13">James 3:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Let him pray </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσευχεσθω</span></span>). Present middle imperative, "let him keep on praying" (instead of cursing as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:12">James 5:12</span>).</p>
<p><b>Is any cheerful </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευθυμει;</span></span>). Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευθυμεω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευθυμος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:36">Acts 27:36</span>), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:22">Acts 27:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:25">Acts 27:25</span>.</p>
<p><b>Let him sing praise </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψαλλετω</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψαλλω</span></span>, originally to twang a chord as on a harp, to sing praise to God whether with instrument or without, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:15">1 Corinthians 14:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:9">Romans 15:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:19">Ephesians 5:19</span>. "Let him keep on making melody." </p>
