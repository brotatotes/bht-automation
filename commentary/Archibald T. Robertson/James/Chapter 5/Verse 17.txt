<p><b> Of like passions with us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοιοπαθης ημιν</span></span>). Associative-instrumental case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημιν</span></span> as with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοιος</span></span>. This old compound adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοιοσ, πασχω</span></span>), suffering the like with another, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:15">Acts 14:15</span>.</p>
<p><b>He prayed fervently </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσευχη προσηυξατο</span></span>). First aorist middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσευχομα</span></span> and the instrumental case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσευχη</span></span> (cognate substantive), after idiom for intensity in classical Greek, like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φευγειν φυγη</span></span>, to flee with all speed (<i>figura etymologica</i>), but particularly frequent in the LXX (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+2:17">Genesis 2:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+31:30">Genesis 31:30</span>) in imitation of the Hebrew infinitive absolute. So <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:15">Luke 22:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:29">John 3:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:17">Acts 4:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>That it might not rain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του μη βρεξα</span></span>). Genitive of the articular infinitive (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βρεξα</span></span>, first aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βρεχω</span></span>, old verb, to moisten, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:38">Luke 7:38</span>, to rain, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:45">Matthew 5:45</span>) with negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> used either for direct purpose, for an object clause as here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:12">Acts 3:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:20">Acts 15:20</span>, or even for result.</p>
<p><b>For three years and six months </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενιαυτους τρεις κα μηνας εξ</span></span>). Accusative of extent of time. </p>
