<p><b> Gave rain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υετον εδωκεν</span></span>). This idiom is in the LXX of God as here of heaven (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1sa+12:17">1 Samuel 12:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ki+18:1">1 Kings 18:1</span>) and also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:17">Acts 14:17</span> instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβρεξεν</span></span> of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:17">James 5:17</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hυετον</span></span> is old word for rain (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υω</span></span>, to rain), genuine here, but not in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:7">James 5:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>Brought forth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβλαστησεν</span></span>). First aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλαστανω</span></span>, old verb, to sprout (intransitive as <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:27">Mark 4:27</span>), here as occasionally in later Greek transitive with accusative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καρπον</span></span>. </p>
