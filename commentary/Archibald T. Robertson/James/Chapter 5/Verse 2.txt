<p><b> Riches </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο πλουτος</span></span>). Masculine singular, but occasionally neuter <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το πλουτος</span></span> in nominative and accusative (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:2">2 Corinthians 8:2</span>). Apparently <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλεοτος</span></span> fulness (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλεος</span></span> full, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιμπλημ</span></span> to fill). "Wealth."</p>
<p><b>Are corrupted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σεσηπεν</span></span>). Second perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σηπω</span></span> (root <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαπ</span></span> as in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαπρος</span></span>, rotten), to corrupt, to destroy, here intransitive "has rotted." Only here in N.T. On the worthlessness of mere wealth see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:19">Matthew 6:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:24">Matthew 6:24</span>.</p>
<p><b>Were moth-eaten </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σητοβρωτα γεγονεν</span></span>). "Have become (second perfect indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span>, singular number, though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιματια</span></span>, neuter plural, treated collectively) moth-eaten" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σητοβρωτα</span></span>, late and rare compound from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σης</span></span>, moth, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:19">Matthew 6:19</span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βρωτος</span></span>, verbal adjective of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βιβρωσκω</span></span> to eat <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:13">John 6:13</span>. This compound found only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+13:28">Job 13:28</span>, Sibyll. Orac. <i>Proem</i>. 64). Rich robes as heirlooms, but moth-eaten. Vivid picture. Witness the 250 "lost millionaires" in the United States in 1931 as compared with 1929. Riches have wings. </p>
