<p><b> The hire </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο μισθος</span></span>). Old word for wages (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:8">Matthew 20:8</span>).</p>
<p><b>Labourers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργατων</span></span>). Any one who works (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργαζομα</span></span>), especially agricultural workers (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:37">Matthew 9:37</span>).</p>
<p><b>Who mowed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των αμησαντων</span></span>). Genitive plural of the articular first aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαω</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμα</span></span>, together), old verb, to gather together, to reap, here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Fields </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χωρας</span></span>). Estates or farms (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:16">Luke 12:16</span>).</p>
<p><b>Which is of you kept back by fraud </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αφυστερημενος αφ' υμων</span></span>). Perfect passive articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφυστερεω</span></span>, late compound (simplex <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υστερεω</span></span> common as <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:20">Matthew 19:20</span>), to be behindhand from, to fail of, to cause to withdraw, to defraud. Pitiful picture of earned wages kept back by rich Jews, old problem of capital and labour that is with us yet in acute form.</p>
<p><b>The cries </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α βοα</span></span>). Old word from which <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βοαω</span></span> comes (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:3">Matthew 3:3</span>), here only in N.T. The stolen money "cries out" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κραζε</span></span>), the workers cry out for vengeance.</p>
<p><b>That reaped </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των θερισαντων</span></span>). Genitive plural of the articular participle first aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεριζω</span></span> (old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θερος</span></span>, summer, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:32">Matthew 24:32</span>), to reap, to harvest while summer allows (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:26">Matthew 6:26</span>).</p>
<p><b>Have entered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισεληλυθαν</span></span>). Perfect active third person plural indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισερχομα</span></span>, old and common compound, to go or come into. This late form is by analogy of the aorist for the usual form in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-ασ</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Of the Lord of Sabaoth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κυριου Σαβαωθ</span></span>). "Of the Lord of Hosts," quotation from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+5:9">Isaiah 5:9</span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:29">Romans 9:29</span>, transliterating the Hebrew word for "Hosts," an expression for the omnipotence of God like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Παντοκρατωρ</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:8">Revelation 4:8</span>). God hears the cries of the oppressed workmen even if the employers are deaf. </p>
