<p><b> Ye have lived delicately </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετρυφησατε</span></span>). First aorist (constative, summary) active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρυφαω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρυφη</span></span> (luxurious living as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:25">Luke 7:25</span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θρυπτω</span></span>, to break down, to enervate), to lead a soft life, only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Taken your pleasure </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσπαταλησατε</span></span>). First aorist (constative) active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπαταλαω</span></span>, late and rare verb to live voluptuously or wantonly (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπαταλη</span></span>, riotous living, wantonness, once as bracelet), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:6">1 Timothy 5:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Ye have nourished </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εθρεψατε</span></span>). First aorist (constative) active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρεφω</span></span>, old verb, to feed, to fatten (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:26">Matthew 6:26</span>). They are fattening themselves like sheep or oxen all unconscious of "the day of slaughter" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ημερα σφαγης</span></span>, definite without the article) ahead of them. For this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σφαγης</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:36">Romans 8:36</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προβατα σφαγης</span></span>, sheep for the slaughter, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σφαγη</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σφαζω</span></span>, to slay), consummate sarcasm on the folly of sinful rich people. </p>
