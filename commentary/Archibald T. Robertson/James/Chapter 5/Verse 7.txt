<p><b> Be patient therefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακροθυμησατε ουν</span></span>). A direct corollary (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουν</span></span>, therefore) from the coming judgment on the wicked rich (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:1-6">James 5:1-6</span>). First aorist (constative) active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακροθυμεω</span></span>, late compound (Plutarch, LXX) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακροθυμος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακροσ, θυμος</span></span>, of long spirit, not losing heart), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:26">Matthew 18:26</span>. The appeal is to the oppressed brethren. Catch your wind for a long race (long-tempered as opposed to short-tempered). See already the exhortation to patience (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπομονη</span></span>) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:3">James 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:12">James 1:12</span> and repeated in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:11">James 5:11</span>. They will need both submission (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπομενω</span></span> <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:11">James 5:11</span>) and steadfastness (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακροθυμια</span></span> <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:10">James 5:10</span>).</p>
<p><b>Until the coming of the Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως της παρουσιας</span></span>). The second coming of Christ he means, the regular phrase here and in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:8">James 5:8</span> for that idea (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:3">Matthew 24:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:37">Matthew 24:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:39">Matthew 24:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:19">1 Thessalonians 2:19</span>, etc.).</p>
<p><b>The husbandman </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο γεωργος</span></span>). The worker in the ground (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γη, εργω</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:33">Matthew 21:33</span>.</p>
<p><b>Waiteth for </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκδεχετα</span></span>). Present middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκδεχομα</span></span>, old verb for eager expectation as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:16">Acts 17:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>Precious </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιμιον</span></span>). Old adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιμη</span></span> (honor, price), dear to the farmer because of his toil for it. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:19">1 Peter 1:19</span>.</p>
<p><b>Being patient over it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακροθυμων επ' αυτω</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακροθυμεω</span></span> just used in the exhortation, picturing the farmer longing and hoping over his precious crop (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:7">Luke 18:7</span> of God).</p>
<p><b>Until it receive </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως λαβη</span></span>). Temporal clause of the future with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως</span></span> and the second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>, vividly describing the farmer's hopes and patience.</p>
<p><b>The early and latter rain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προιμον κα οψιμον</span></span>). The word for rain (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υετον</span></span> <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:17">Acts 14:17</span>) is absent from the best MSS. The adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προιμος</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρω</span></span>, early) occurs here only in N.T., though old in the form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προιμος</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωις</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+11:14">Deuteronomy 11:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+5:24">Jeremiah 5:24</span>, etc. for these terms for the early rain in October or November for the germination of the grain, and the latter rain (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οψιμον</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οψε</span></span>, late, here only in N.T.) in April and May for maturing the grain. </p>
