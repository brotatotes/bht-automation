<p><b> They said therefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειπαν ουν</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of defective verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειπον</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> instead of usual <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span>. Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουν</span></span>, inferential here as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:21">John 1:21</span> though often merely transitional in John.</p>
<p><b>Who art thou? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Τις ει;</span></span>). Same question as at first (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:19">John 1:19</span>), but briefer.</p>
<p><b>That we give answer </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα αποκρισιν δωμεν</span></span>). Final use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> with second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκρισιν</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκρινομα</span></span>, above, old substantive as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:47">Luke 2:47</span>.</p>
<p><b>To those that sent </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις πεμψασιν</span></span>). Dative case plural of the articular participle first aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεμπω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>What sayest thou of thyself? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Τ λεγεις περ σεαυτου;</span></span>). This time they opened wide the door without giving any hint at all. </p>
