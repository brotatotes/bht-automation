<p><b> Coming after me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπισω μου ερχομενος</span></span>). No article (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span>) in Aleph B. John as the forerunner of the Messiah has preceded him in time, but not in rank as he instantly adds.</p>
<p><b>The latchet of whose shoe I am not worthy to unloose </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου ουκ ειμ αξιος ινα λυσω αυτου τον ιμαντα του υποδηματος</span></span>). Literally, "of whom I am not worthy that I unloose the latchet (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:7">Mark 1:7</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιμας</span></span>) of his sandal (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:11">Matthew 3:11</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποδημα</span></span>, bound under the foot)." Only use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αξιος</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> in John, though used by Paul in this saying of the Baptist (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:25">Acts 13:25</span>), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ικανος ινα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:8">Matthew 3:8</span>, but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ικανος λυσα</span></span> (aorist active infinitive instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυσω</span></span>, aorist active subjunctive) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:7">Mark 1:7</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:16">Luke 3:16</span>) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαστασα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:11">Matthew 3:11</span>. </p>
