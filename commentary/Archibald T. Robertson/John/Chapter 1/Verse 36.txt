<p><b> He looked </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμβλεψας</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμβλεπω</span></span>, antecedent action before <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγε</span></span> (says).</p>
<p><b>As he walked </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιπατουντ</span></span>). Present active participle in dative case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμβλεψας</span></span> and like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομενον</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:29">John 1:29</span> vividly pictures the rapture of John in this vision of Jesus, so far as we know the third and last glimpse of Jesus by John (the baptism, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:29">John 1:29</span>, and here).</p>
<p><b>Saith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγε</span></span>). Historical present, change from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστηκε</span></span> before. He repeats part of the tribute in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:29">John 1:29</span>. </p>
