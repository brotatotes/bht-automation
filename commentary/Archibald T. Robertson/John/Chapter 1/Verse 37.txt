<p><b> Heard him speak </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκουσαν αυτου λαλουντος</span></span>). First active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουω</span></span> and present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαλεω</span></span> in genitive case agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτου</span></span>, object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουω</span></span>. "Heard him speaking" (kind of indirect discourse). John had disciples (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαθητα</span></span>, learners, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μανθανω</span></span>, to learn).</p>
<p><b>They followed Jesus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκολουθησαν τω Ιησου</span></span>). Associative instrumental case after verb (first aorist active indicative, ingressive aorist, of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακολουθεω</span></span>). These two disciples of the Baptist (Andrew and John) took him at his word and acted on it. John the Baptist had predicted and portrayed the Messiah, had baptized him, had interpreted him, and now for the second time had identified him. </p>
