<p><b> Andrew </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ανδρεας</span></span>). Explained by John as one of the two disciples of the Baptist and identified as the brother of the famous Simon Peter (cf. also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:8">John 6:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:22">John 12:22</span>). The more formal call of Andrew and Simon, James and John, comes later (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:16">Mark 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:18">Matthew 4:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:1-11">Luke 3:1-11</span>).</p>
<p><b>That heard John speak </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των ακουσαντων παρα Ιωανου</span></span>). "That heard from John," a classical idiom (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span> with ablative after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουω</span></span>) seen also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:45">John 6:45</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:51">John 7:51</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:26">John 8:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:40">John 8:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:15">John 15:15</span>. </p>
