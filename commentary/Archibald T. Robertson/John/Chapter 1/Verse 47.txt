<p><b> Behold </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδε</span></span>). Here an exclamation (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:29">John 1:29</span>) as often like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδου</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>An Israelite indeed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αληθως Ισραηλειτης</span></span>). "Truly an Israelite," one living up to the covenant name, Israel at its best (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:29">Romans 2:29</span>), without the guile (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δολος</span></span>, deceit, bait for fish, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δελεαζω</span></span>, to catch with bait) that Jacob once had of which Isaac complained (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+27:35">Genesis 27:35</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δολος</span></span>, here in LXX). The servant of Jehovah was to be without guile (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+53:9">Isaiah 53:9</span>). </p>
