<p><b> There came a man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγενετο ανθρωπος</span></span>). Definite event in the long darkness, same verb in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:3">John 1:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Sent </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεσταλμενος</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστελλω</span></span>, to send.</p>
<p><b>From God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα θεου</span></span>). From the side of (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span>) God (ablative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεου</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Whose name </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ονομα αυτω</span></span>). "Name to him," nominative parenthetic and dative (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 460).</p>
<p><b>John </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιωανης</span></span>). One <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ν</span></span> in Westcott and Hort. In the giving of the name see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:59-63">Luke 1:59-63</span>, Hellenized form of Jonathan, Joanan (Gift of God), used always of the Baptist in this Gospel which never mentions the name of John son of Zebedee (the sons of Zebedee once, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:2">John 21:2</span>). </p>
