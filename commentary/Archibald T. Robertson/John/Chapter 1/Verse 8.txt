<p><b> He </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινος</span></span>). "That one," i.e. John. He was a light (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:35">John 5:35</span>) as all believers are (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:14">Matthew 5:14</span>), but not "the light" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το φως</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>But came </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ'</span></span>). No verb in the Greek, to be supplied by repeating <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηλθεν</span></span> of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:7">John 1:7</span>. See similar ellipses in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:3">John 9:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:18">John 13:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:25">John 15:25</span>. In Johannine fashion we have the final <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> clause of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:7">John 1:7</span> repeated. </p>
