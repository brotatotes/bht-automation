<p><b> Took up stones again </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβαστασαν παλιν λιθους</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασταζω</span></span>, old verb to pick up, to carry (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:6">John 12:6</span>), to bear (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:5">Galatians 6:5</span>). The <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παλιν</span></span> refers to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:59">John 8:59</span> where <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηραν</span></span> was used. They wanted to kill him also when he made himself equal to God in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:18">John 5:18</span>. Perhaps here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβαστασαν</span></span> means "they fetched stones from a distance."</p>
<p><b>To stone him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα λιθασωσιν αυτον</span></span>). Final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λιθαζω</span></span>, late verb (Aristotle, Polybius) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λιθος</span></span> (stone, small, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:6">Matthew 4:6</span>, or large, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:2">Matthew 28:2</span>), in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:31-33">John 10:31-33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:8">John 11:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:26">Acts 5:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:19">Acts 14:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:25">2 Corinthians 11:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:37">Hebrews 11:37</span>, but not in the Synoptics. It means to pelt with stones, to overwhelm with stones. </p>
