<p><b> From the Father </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ του πατρος</span></span>). Proceeding out of the Father as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:65">John 6:65</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:28">John 16:28</span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:17">John 7:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:42">John 8:42</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:47">John 8:47</span>) rather than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:14">John 1:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:46">John 6:46</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:29">John 7:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:7">John 17:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>For which of those works </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια ποιον αυτων εργον</span></span>). Literally, "For what kind of work of them" (referring to the "many good works" <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολλα εργα καλα</span></span>). Noble and beautiful deeds Jesus had done in Jerusalem like healing the impotent man (chapter 5) and the blind man (chapter 9). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ποιον</span></span> is a qualitative interrogative pronoun pointing to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλα</span></span> (good).</p>
<p><b>Do ye stone me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λιθαζετε</span></span>). Conative present active indicative, "are ye trying to stone me." They had the stones in their hands stretched back to fling at him, a threatening attitude. </p>
