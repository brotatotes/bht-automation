<p><b> They sought again to seize him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εζητουν αυτον παλιν πιαζα</span></span>). Imperfect active, "They kept on seeking to seize (ingressive aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιαζω</span></span> for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:30">John 7:30</span>) as they had tried repeatedly (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:1">John 7:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:30">John 7:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:44">John 7:44</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:20">John 8:20</span>), but in vain. They gave up the effort to stone him.</p>
<p><b>Out of their hand </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ της χειρος αυτων</span></span>). Overawed, but still angry, the stones fell to the ground, and Jesus walked out. </p>
