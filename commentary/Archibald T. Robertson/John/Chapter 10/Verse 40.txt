<p><b> Again </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παλιν</span></span>). Referring to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:28">John 1:28</span> (Bethany beyond Jordan). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Παλιν</span></span> does not mean that the other visit was a recent one.</p>
<p><b>At the first </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το πρωτον</span></span>). Adverbial accusative (extent of time). Same idiom in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:16">John 12:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:39">John 19:39</span>. Here the identical language of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:28">John 1:28</span> is used with the mere addition of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το πρωτον</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπου ην Ιωανης βαπτιζων</span></span>, "where John was baptizing").</p>
<p><b>And there he abode </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα εμενεν εκε</span></span>). Imperfect (continued) active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενω</span></span>, though some MSS. have the constative aorist active <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμεινεν</span></span>. Probably from here Jesus carried on the first part of the later Perean Ministry (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:22-16">Luke 13:22-16</span>) before the visit to Bethany at the raising of Lazarus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:1-44">John 11:1-44</span>). </p>
