<p><b> But if a man walk in the night </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν δε τις περιπατη εν τη νυκτ</span></span>). Third condition again. It is spiritual darkness that Jesus here pictures, but the result is the same. See the same figure in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:35">John 12:35</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:11">1 John 2:11</span>). The ancients had poor illumination at night as indeed we did before Edison gave us electric lights. Pedestrians actually used to have little lamps fastened on the feet to light the path.</p>
<p><b>In him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αυτω</span></span>). Spiritual darkness, the worst of all (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:23">Matthew 6:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:12">John 8:12</span>). Man has the capacity for light, but is not the source of light. "By the application of this principle Christianity is distinguished from Neo-Platonism" (Westcott). </p>
