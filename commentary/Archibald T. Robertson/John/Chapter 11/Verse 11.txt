<p><b> Is fallen asleep </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεκοιμητα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοιμαω</span></span>, old verb to put to sleep. Common as a metaphor for death like our cemetery.</p>
<p><b>I go </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορευομα</span></span>). Futuristic use of the present tense as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:2">John 14:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>That I may awake him out of sleep </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα εξυπνισω αυτον</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξυπνιζω</span></span>, a late compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ, υπνος</span></span>, sleep) for the older <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφυπνιζω</span></span>, here only in the N.T. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+14:12">Job 14:12</span> where also it occurs along with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοιμαομα</span></span>. </p>
