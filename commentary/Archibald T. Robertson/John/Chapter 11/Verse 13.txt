<p><b> Had spoken </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειρηκε</span></span>). Past perfect of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειπον</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερω</span></span>). The disciples had misunderstood Christ's metaphor for death.</p>
<p><b>That he spake </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ λεγε</span></span>). Present active indicative retained in indirect discourse after the secondary tense (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδοξαν</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Of taking rest in sleep </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ της κοιμησεως του υπου</span></span>). Only use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοιμησις</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοιμαω</span></span>) in the N.T., but it also was used of death (Sirach 46:19). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hυπνου</span></span> (in sleep) is objective genitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπνος</span></span> (sleep, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+1:24">Matthew 1:24</span>). </p>
