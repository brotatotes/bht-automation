<p><b> Found </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρεν</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρισκω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>That he had been in the tomb four days already </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτον τεσσαρας ηδη ημερας εχοντα</span></span>). Literally, "him (accusative object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρεν</span></span>) having already four days in the tomb." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:5">John 5:5</span> for the same idiom (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετη εχων</span></span>) for expression of time (having 38 years). In Jewish custom burial took place on the day of death (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:6">Acts 6:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:10">Acts 6:10</span>). </p>
