<p><b> About fifteen furlongs off </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως απο σταδιων δεκαπεντε</span></span>). The idiom of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span> with the ablative for distance is like the Latin <i>a millibus passum duobus</i> (Caesar, <i>Bell. Gall</i>. ii. 7), but it (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προ</span></span> also, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:1">John 12:1</span>) occurs already in the Doric and in the <i>Koine</i> often (Moulton, <i>Proleg</i>., p. 101; Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 110). See it again in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:8">John 21:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:20">Revelation 14:20</span>. </p>
