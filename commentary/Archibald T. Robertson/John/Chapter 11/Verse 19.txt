<p><b> Had come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εληλυθεισαν</span></span>). Past perfect of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομα</span></span>. These Jews were probably not hostile to Jesus. There were seven days of solemn mourning (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1sa+31:13">1 Samuel 31:13</span>). The presence of so many indicates the prominence of the family.</p>
<p><b>To Martha and Mary </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς την Μαρθαν κα Μαριαμ</span></span>). Correct text, not the Textus Receptus <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς τας περ Μαρθαν κα Μαριαμ</span></span> (to the women about Martha and Mary).</p>
<p><b>To console them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα παραμυθησωντα</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and first aorist middle subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραμυθεομα</span></span>, old verb (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span>, beside, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυθος</span></span>, word), to put in a word beside, to offer consolation. Again in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:31">John 11:31</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:11">1 Thessalonians 2:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:14">1 Thessalonians 5:14</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+2:13">Job 2:13</span> for these visits of consolation, often deplorable enough, though kindly meant. </p>
