<p><b> That Jesus was coming </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ Ιησους ερχετα</span></span>). Present middle indicative retained in indirect discourse after the secondary tense <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκουσεν</span></span> (first aorist active).</p>
<p><b>Went and met him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπηντησεν αυτω</span></span>). First aorist (ingressive) active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπανταω</span></span>, old compound verb, to go to meet (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:28">Matthew 8:28</span>) with the associative instrumental case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>But Mary still sat in the house </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μαριαμ δε εν τω οικω εκαθεζετο</span></span>). Imperfect middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθεζομα</span></span>, old verb to sit down, graphic picture of Mary, "while Mary was sitting in the house." Both Martha and Mary act true to form here as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:38-42">Luke 10:38-42</span>. </p>
