<p><b> Called Mary </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφωνησεν Μαριαμ</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωνεω</span></span>. Out of the house and away from the crowd.</p>
<p><b>Secretly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαθρα</span></span>). Old adverb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαθρος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λανθανω</span></span>). To tell her the glad news.</p>
<p><b>The Master </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο διδασκαλος</span></span>). "The Teacher." So they loved to call him as he was (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:13">John 13:13</span>).</p>
<p><b>Is here </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεστιν</span></span>). "Is present."</p>
<p><b>Calleth thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωνε σε</span></span>). This rouses Mary. </p>
