<p><b> Sent saying </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεστειλαν λεγουσα</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστελλω</span></span> and present active participle. The message was delivered by the messenger.</p>
<p><b>Thou lovest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλεις</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Φιλεω</span></span> means to love as a friend (see <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλος</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:11">John 11:11</span>) and so warmly, while <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπαω</span></span> (akin to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαμα</span></span>, to admire, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαθος</span></span>, good) means high regard. Here both terms occur of the love of Jesus for Lazarus (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγαπα</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:5">John 11:5</span>). Both occur of the Father's love for the Son (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:35">John 3:35</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλε</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:20">John 5:20</span>). Hence the distinction is not always observed. </p>
