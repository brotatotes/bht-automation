<p><b> He that was dead came forth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξηλθεν ο τεθνηκως</span></span>). Literally, "Came out the dead man," (effective aorist active indicative and perfect active articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θνησκω</span></span>). Just as he was and at once.</p>
<p><b>Bound hand and foot </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεδεμενος τους ποδας κα τας χειρας</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεω</span></span> with the accusative loosely retained according to the common Greek idiom (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 486), but literally "as to the feet and hands" (opposite order from the English). Probably the legs were bound separately.</p>
<p><b>With grave-clothes </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κειριαις</span></span>). Or "with bands." Instrumental case of this late and rare word (in Plutarch, medical papyrus in the form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κηρια</span></span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="pr+7:16">Proverbs 7:16</span>). Only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>His face </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η οψις αυτου</span></span>). Old word, but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσωπον</span></span> is usual in N.T. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:16">Revelation 1:16</span> for another instance.</p>
<p><b>Was bound about </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιεδεδετο</span></span>). Past perfect passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιδεω</span></span>, old verb to bind around, only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>With a napkin </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σουδαριω</span></span>). Instrumental case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σουδαριον</span></span> (Latin word <i>sudarium</i> from <i>sudor</i>, sweat). In N.T. here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:7">John 20:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:20">Luke 19:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:12">Acts 19:12</span>. Our handkerchief.</p>
<p><b>Loose him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυσατε αυτον</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυω</span></span>. From the various bands.</p>
<p><b>Let him go </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφετε αυτον υπαγειν</span></span>). Second aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφιημ</span></span> and present active infinitive. </p>
