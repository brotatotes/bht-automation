<p><b> Gathered a council </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνηγαγον συνεδριον</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συναγω</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεδριον</span></span>, the regular word for the Sanhedrin (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:22">Matthew 5:22</span>, etc.), only here in John. Here a sitting or session of the Sanhedrin. Both chief priests (Sadducees) and Pharisees (mentioned no more in John after <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:57">John 7:57</span> save <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:19">John 12:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:42">John 12:42</span>) combine in the call (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:32">John 7:32</span>). From now on the chief priests (Sadducees) take the lead in the attacks on Jesus, though loyally supported by their opponents (the Pharisees).</p>
<p><b>And said </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ελεγον</span></span>). Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγω</span></span>, perhaps inchoative, "began to say."</p>
<p><b>What do we? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Τ ποιουμεν;</span></span>). Present active (linear) indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span>. Literally, "What are we doing?"</p>
<p><b>Doeth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιε</span></span>). Better, "is doing" (present, linear action). He is active and we are idle. There is no mention of the raising of Lazarus as a fact, but it is evidently inoluded in the "many signs." </p>
