<p><b> The chief priests and the Pharisees </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αρχιερεις κα ο Φαρισαιο</span></span>). The Sanhedrin.</p>
<p><b>Had given commandment </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεδωκεισαν εντολας</span></span>). Past perfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>That he should shew it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μηνυση</span></span>). Sub-final <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> with first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηνυω</span></span>, old verb to disclose, to report formally (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:30">Acts 23:30</span>).</p>
<p><b>If any man knew </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν τις γνω</span></span>). Third-class condition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Where he was </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">που εστιν</span></span>). Indirect question with interrogative adverb and present indicative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστιν</span></span> retained like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνω</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηνυση</span></span> after the secondary tense <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεδωκεισαν</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>That they might take him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπως πιασωσιν αυτον</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπως</span></span> instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιαζω</span></span> so often used before (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:44">John 7:44</span>, etc.). </p>
