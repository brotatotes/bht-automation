<p><b> Then after this </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επειτα μετα τουτο</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Επειτα</span></span> (only here in John) means thereafter (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:7">Luke 16:7</span>) and it is made plainer by the addition of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα τουτο</span></span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:12">John 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:11">John 11:11</span>), meaning after the two days had elapsed.</p>
<p><b>Let us go into Judea again </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αγωμεν εις την Ιουδαιαν παλιν</span></span>). Volitive (hortative) subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγω</span></span> (intransitive use as in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:11">John 11:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:16">John 11:16</span>). They had but recently escaped the rage of the Jews in Jerusalem (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:39">John 10:39</span>) to this haven in Bethany beyond Jordan (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:40">John 10:40</span>). </p>
