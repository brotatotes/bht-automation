<p><b> Because that </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span>). Causal use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>By reason of him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι' αυτον</span></span>). "Because of him," regular idiom, accusative case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Went away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπηγον</span></span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:67">John 6:67</span> for this verb. Inchoative imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπαγω</span></span>, "began to withdraw" as happened at the time of the raising of Lazarus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:45">John 11:45</span>) and the secession was still going on.</p>
<p><b>And believed on Jesus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα επιστευον εις τον Ιησουν</span></span>). Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστευω</span></span> (note aorist in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:45">John 11:45</span>). There was danger of a mass movement of the people to Jesus. </p>
