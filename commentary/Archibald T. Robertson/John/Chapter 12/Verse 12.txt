<p><b> On the morrow </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη επαυριον</span></span>). Locative case. Supply <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημερα</span></span> (day) after the adverb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαυριον</span></span> ("on the tomorrow day"). That is on our Sunday, Palm Sunday.</p>
<p><b>A great multitude </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο οχλος πολυς</span></span>). Same idiom rendered "the common people" in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:9">John 12:9</span> and should be so translated here.</p>
<p><b>That had come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ελθων</span></span>). Second aorist active participle, masculine singular of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομα</span></span> agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οχλος</span></span>, "that came."</p>
<p><b>When they heard </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουσαντες</span></span>). First aorist active masculine plural participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουω</span></span>, construction according to sense (plural, though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οχλος</span></span> singular).</p>
<p><b>Was coming </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχετα</span></span>). Present middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομα</span></span> retained in indirect discourse after a secondary tense. It is a vivid picture. What they heard was: "Jesus is coming into Jerusalem." He is defying the Sanhedrin with all their public advertisement for him. </p>
