<p><b> Found </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρων</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρισκω</span></span>. Through the disciples, of course, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:2-6">Mark 11:2-6</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:2-3">Matthew 21:2-3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:6">Matthew 21:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:30">Luke 19:30</span>).</p>
<p><b>A young ass </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οναριον</span></span>). Late diminutive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ονος</span></span>, in Epictetus and the papyri (even the double diminitive, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οναριδιον</span></span>), only here in the N.T. See discussion of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:5">Matthew 21:5</span> where <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span> has been wrongly rendered "and" instead of "even." Rightly understood Matthew has Jesus riding only the colt like the rest. </p>
