<p><b> Father, glorify thy name </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πατερ, δοξασον σου το ονομα</span></span>). First aorist (note of urgency) active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξαζω</span></span> and in the sense of his death already in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:16">John 12:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:23">John 12:23</span> and again in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:31">John 13:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:5">John 17:5</span>. This is the prayer of the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα</span></span> (or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψυχη</span></span>) as opposed to that of the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαρξ</span></span> (flesh) in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:27">John 12:27</span>. The "name" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ονομα</span></span>) of God expresses the character of God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:12">John 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:43">John 5:43</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:11">John 17:11</span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:9">Matthew 6:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>A voice out of heaven </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωνη εκ του ουρανου</span></span>). This was the Father's answer to the prayer of Jesus for help. See already the Father's voice at the baptism of Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:11">Mark 1:11</span>) and at the transfiguration (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:7">Mark 9:7</span>). The rabbis called the audible voice of God <i>bath-qol</i> (the daughter of a voice).</p>
<p><b>I have both glorified it and will glorify it again </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα εδοξασα κα παλιν δοξασω</span></span>). This definite assurance from the Father will nerve the soul of Jesus for the coming ordeal. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:40">John 11:40</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδοξασα</span></span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:31">John 13:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:5">John 17:5</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξασω</span></span>. </p>
