<p><b> That it had thundered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βροντην γεγονενα</span></span>). Perfect active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span> in indirect discourse after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεγεν</span></span> and the accusative of general reference (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βροντην</span></span>, thunder, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:17">Mark 3:17</span>), "that thunder came to pass." So the crowd "standing by" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστως</span></span>, second perfect active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span>), but Jesus understood his Father's voice.</p>
<p><b>An angel hath spoken to him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αγγελος αυτω λελαληκεν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαλεω</span></span>. So, when Jesus spoke to Saul on the way to Damascus, those with Saul heard the voice, but did not understand (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:7">Acts 9:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:9">Acts 22:9</span>). </p>
