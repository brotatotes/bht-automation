<p><b> A pound </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λιτραν</span></span>). Latin <i>libra</i>, late <i>Koine</i> (Polybius, Plutarch) word with weight of 12 ounces, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:39">John 19:39</span>. Mark (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:3">Mark 14:3</span>) and Matthew (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:7">Matthew 26:7</span>) have alabaster cruse.</p>
<p><b>Of ointment of spikenard </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυρου ναρδου πιστικης</span></span>). "Of oil of nard." See already <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:2">John 11:2</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυρου</span></span> (also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:7">Matthew 26:7</span>). Nard is the head or spike of an East Indian plant, very fragrant. Occurs also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:3">Mark 14:3</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πιστικης</span></span> here and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:3">Mark 14:3</span> probably means genuine (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστικος</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστος</span></span>, reliable). Only two instances in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>Very precious </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολυτιμου</span></span>). Old compound adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολυς</span></span>, much, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιμη</span></span>), in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:46">Matthew 13:46</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:7">1 Peter 1:7</span>. Mark has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολυτελους</span></span> (very costly). Matthew (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:7">Matthew 26:7</span>) has here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαρυτιμου</span></span> of weighty value (only N.T. instance).</p>
<p><b>Anointed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηλειψεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλειφω</span></span>, old word (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+16:1">Mark 16:1</span>).</p>
<p><b>The feet </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους ποδας</span></span>). Mark (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:3">Mark 14:3</span>) and Matthew (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:7">Matthew 26:7</span>) have "his head." Why not both, though neither Gospel mentions both? The Latin MS. <i>fuldensis</i> and the Syriac Sinatic do give both head and feet here.</p>
<p><b>Wiped </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεμαξεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκμασσω</span></span>, old verb to wipe off already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:2">John 11:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:38">Luke 7:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:44">Luke 7:44</span>.</p>
<p><b>With her hair </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταις θριξιν αυτης</span></span>). Instrumental plural. It is this item that is relied on largely by those who identify Mary of Bethany with the sinful woman in Luke 7 and with Mary Magdalene. It is no doubt true that it was usually considered immodest for a woman to wear her hair loose. But it is not impossible that Mary of Bethany in her carefully planned love-offering for Jesus on this occasion was only glad to throw such a punctilio to the winds. Such an act on this occasion does not brand her a woman of loose character.</p>
<p><b>Was filled with the odour of the ointment </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επληρωθη εκ της οσμης του μυρου</span></span>). Effective first aorist passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροω</span></span> and a natural result. </p>
