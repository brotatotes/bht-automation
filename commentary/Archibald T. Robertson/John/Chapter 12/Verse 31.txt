<p><b> The judgement </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρισις</span></span>). No article, "A judgement." The next few days will test this world.</p>
<p><b>The prince of this world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αρχων του κοσμου τουτου</span></span>). This phrase here, descriptive of Satan as in possession of the evil world, occurs again in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:30">John 14:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:11">John 16:11</span>. In the temptations Satan claims power over the world and offers to share it with Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:8-10">Matthew 4:8-10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:5-8">Luke 4:5-8</span>). Jesus did not deny Satan's power then, but here proclaims final victory over him.</p>
<p><b>Shall be cast out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκβληθησετα εξω</span></span>). Future passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκβαλλω</span></span>. Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξω</span></span>, clean out. The Book of Revelation also proclaims final victory over Satan. </p>
