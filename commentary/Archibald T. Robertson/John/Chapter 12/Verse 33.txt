<p><b> Signifying </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σημαινων</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σεμαινω</span></span>, old verb to give a sign (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σημειον</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:27">Acts 25:27</span>, and the whole phrase repeated in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:32">John 18:32</span> and nearly so in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:19">John 21:19</span>. The indirect question here and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:32">John 18:32</span> has the imperfect <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμελλεν</span></span> with present infinitive rather than the usual present <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μελλε</span></span> retained while in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:19">John 21:19</span> the future indicative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξασε</span></span> occurs according to rule. The point in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιω</span></span> (qualitative relative in the instrumental case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θανατω</span></span>) is the Cross (lifted up) as the kind of death before Christ. </p>
