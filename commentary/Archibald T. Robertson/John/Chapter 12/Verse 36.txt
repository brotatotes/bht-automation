<p><b> Believe in the light </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστευετε εις το φως</span></span>). That is, "believe in me as the Messiah" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:12">John 8:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:5">John 9:5</span>).</p>
<p><b>That ye may become sons of light </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα υιο φωτος γενησθε</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and second aorist subject of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span>, to become. They were not "sons of light," a Hebrew idiom (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:12">John 17:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:8">Luke 16:8</span> with the contrast), an idiom used by Paul in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:5">1 Thessalonians 5:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:8">Ephesians 5:8</span>. It is equivalent to "enlightened men" (Bernard) and Jesus called his disciples the light of the world (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:14">Matthew 5:14</span>).</p>
<p><b>Hid himself from them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκρυβη απ' αυτων</span></span>). Second aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρυπτω</span></span>, late form (in LXX) for old <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκρυφη</span></span>, "was hidden from them," as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:59">John 8:59</span>. This part of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:36">John 12:36</span> begins a new paragraph. </p>
