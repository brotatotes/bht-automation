<p><b> He hath blinded </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τετυφλωκεν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυφλοω</span></span>, old causative verb to make blind (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυφλος</span></span>, blind), in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:4">2 Corinthians 4:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:11">1 John 2:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>He hardened </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επωρωσεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πωροω</span></span>, a late causative verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πωρος</span></span>, hard skin), seen already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:52">Mark 6:52</span>, etc. This quotation is from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+6:10">Isaiah 6:10</span> and differs from the LXX.</p>
<p><b>Lest they should see </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη ιδωσιν</span></span>). Negative purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη</span></span> instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηποτε</span></span> (never used by John) of the LXX. Matthew (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:15">Matthew 13:15</span>) has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηποτε</span></span> and quotes Jesus as using the passage as do Mark (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:12">Mark 4:12</span>) and Luke (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:10">Luke 8:10</span>). Paul quotes it again (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:26">Acts 28:26</span>) to the Jews in Rome. In each instance the words of Isaiah are interpreted as forecasting the doom of the Jews for rejecting the Messiah. Matthew (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:15">Matthew 13:15</span>) has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνωσιν</span></span> where John has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νοησωσιν</span></span> (perceive), and both change from the subjunctive to the future (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ιασομα</span></span>), "And I should heal them." John has here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στραφωσιν</span></span> (second aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρεφω</span></span>) while Matthew reads <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιστρεψωσιν</span></span> (first aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιστρεφω</span></span>). </p>
