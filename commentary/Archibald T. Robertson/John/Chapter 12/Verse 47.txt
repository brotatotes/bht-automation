<p><b> If any one </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν τις</span></span>). Third-class condition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and first aorist active subjunctive (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουση</span></span>) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουω</span></span> and same form (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυλαξη</span></span>) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυλασσω</span></span> with negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>But to save the world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ' ινα σωσω τον κοσμον</span></span>). Purpose clause again (cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα κρινω</span></span>, just before) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and first aorist active of sozo. Exaggerated contrast again, "not so much to judge, but also to save." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:17">John 3:17</span> for same contrast. And yet Jesus does judge the world inevitably (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:15">John 8:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:39">John 9:39</span>), but his primary purpose is to save the world (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:16">John 3:16</span>). See close of the Sermon on the Mount for the same insistence on hearing and keeping (obeying) the words of Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:24">Matthew 7:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:26">Matthew 7:26</span>) and also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:28">Luke 11:28</span>. </p>
