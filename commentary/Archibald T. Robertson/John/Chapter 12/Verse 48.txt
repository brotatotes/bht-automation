<p><b> Rejecteth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθετων</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθετεω</span></span>, late <i>Koine</i> verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθετος</span></span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span>), to render null and void, only here in John, but see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:26">Mark 6:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:9">Mark 7:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>One that judgeth him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον κρινοντα αυτον</span></span>). Articular present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρινω</span></span>. See same idea in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:45">John 5:45</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:50">John 9:50</span>.</p>
<p><b>The same </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινος</span></span>). "That" very word of Christ which one rejects will confront him and accuse him to the Father "at the last day" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη εσχατη ημερα</span></span>, this phrase peculiar to John). There is no escaping it. And yet Jesus himself will bear witness for or against the one whose conduct has already revealed his attitude towards the message of God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:32">Matthew 10:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:8">Luke 12:8</span>). </p>
