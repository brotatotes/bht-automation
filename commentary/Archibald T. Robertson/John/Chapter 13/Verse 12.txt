<p><b> Sat down again </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεπεσεν παλιν</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπιπτω</span></span>, old compound verb to fall back, to lie down, to recline. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Παλιν</span></span> (again) can be taken either with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεπεσεν</span></span>, as here, or with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειπεν</span></span> (he said again).</p>
<p><b>Know ye what I have done to you? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκετε τ πεποιηκα υμιν;</span></span>). "Do ye understand the meaning of my act?" Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span> with dative case (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμιν</span></span>). It was a searching question, particularly to Simon Peter and Judas. </p>
