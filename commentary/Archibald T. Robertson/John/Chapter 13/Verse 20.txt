<p><b> Whomsoever I send </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν τινα πεμψω</span></span>). More precisely, "If I send any one" (third-class condition, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν=εαν</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τινα</span></span>, indefinite pronoun accusative case, object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεμψω</span></span>, first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεμπω</span></span>, to send). This use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε τις</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν τις</span></span> (if any one) is very much like the indefinite relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οστις</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος αν</span></span> (or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span>), but the idiom is different. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:34">Mark 8:34</span> we have both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε τις θελε</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος εαν</span></span> while in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:13">John 14:13</span> we find <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ αν</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν τ</span></span> (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 956). </p>
