<p><b> He was troubled in the spirit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εταραχθη το πνευματ</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταρασσω</span></span> and the locative case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα</span></span>. See already <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:33">John 11:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:27">John 12:27</span> for this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταρασσω</span></span> for the agitation of Christ's spirit. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:1">John 14:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:27">John 14:27</span> it is used of the disciples. Jesus was one with God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:19">John 5:19</span>) and yet he had our real humanity (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:14">John 1:14</span>).</p>
<p><b>Testified </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμαρτυρησεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρεω</span></span>, definite witness as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:44">John 4:44</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:37">John 18:37</span>.</p>
<p><b>One of you shall betray me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις εξ υμων παραδωσε με</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραδιδωμ</span></span>, to betray, the word so often used of Judas. This very language occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:18">Mark 14:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:21">Matthew 26:21</span> and the idea in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:21">Luke 22:21</span>. Jesus had said a year ago that "one of you is a devil" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:70">John 6:70</span>), but it made no such stir then. Now it was a bolt from the blue sky as Jesus swept his eyes around and looked at the disciples. </p>
