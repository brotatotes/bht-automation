<p><b> Beckoneth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νευε</span></span>). Old verb to nod, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:10">Acts 24:10</span>. They were all looking in surprise at each other.</p>
<p><b>Tell us who it is of whom he speaketh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειπε τις εστιν περ ου λεγε</span></span>). Second aorist active imperative with indirect question (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τις</span></span>) and relative clause (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ ου</span></span>). Peter was cautious, but could not contain his curiosity. John in front of Jesus was in a favourable position to have a whispered word with him.</p>
<p><b>Breast </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στηθος</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:20">John 21:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:13">Luke 18:13</span> in place of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολπον</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:23">John 13:23</span>). This is the moment represented in Leonardo da Vinci's "Last Supper," only he shows the figures like the monks for whom he painted it. </p>
