<p><b> Then entered Satan into him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοτε εισηλθεν εις εκεινον ο Σατανας</span></span>). The only time the word Satan occurs in the Gospel. As he had done before (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:2">John 13:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:3">Luke 22:3</span>) until Christ considered him a devil (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:70">John 6:70</span>). This is the natural outcome of one who plays with the devil.</p>
<p><b>That thou doest, do quickly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hο ποιεις ποιησον ταχειον</span></span>). Aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span>. "Do more quickly what thou art doing." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ταχειον</span></span> is comparative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταχεως</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:31">John 11:31</span>) and in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:4">John 20:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:19">Hebrews 13:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:23">Hebrews 13:23</span>. See the eagerness of Jesus for the passion in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:50">Luke 12:50</span>. </p>
