<p><b> Some thought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τινες εδοκουν</span></span>). Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκεω</span></span>. Mere inference in their ignorance.</p>
<p><b>The bag </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το γλωσσοκομον</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:6">John 12:6</span> for this word.</p>
<p><b>What things we have need of </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ων χρειαν εχομεν</span></span>). Antecedent (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταυτα</span></span>) of the relative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ον</span></span>) not expressed.</p>
<p><b>For the feast </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις την εορτην</span></span>). The feast of unleavened bread beginning after the passover meal and lasting eight days. If this was twenty-four hours ahead of the passover meal, there was no hurry for next day would be in ample time.</p>
<p><b>Or that he should give something to the poor </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η τοις πτωχοις ινα τ δω</span></span>). Another alternative in their speculation on the point. Note prolepsis of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις πτωχοις</span></span> (dative case) before <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα δω</span></span> (final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>). </p>
