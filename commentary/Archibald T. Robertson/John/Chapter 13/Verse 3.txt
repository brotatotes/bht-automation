<p><b> Knowing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδως</span></span>). Repeated from verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:1">John 13:1</span>, accenting the full consciousness of Jesus.</p>
<p><b>Had given </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδωκεν</span></span>). So Aleph B L W, aorist active instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεδωκεν</span></span> (perfect active) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:31">John 3:31</span> for a similar statement with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:27">Matthew 11:27</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:22">Luke 10:22</span>) and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+28:18">John 28:18</span> for like claim by Jesus to complete power.</p>
<p><b>And that he came forth from God, and goeth unto God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα οτ απο θεου εξηλθεν κα προς τον θεον υπαγε</span></span>). See plain statement by Jesus on this point in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:28">John 16:28</span>. The use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς τον θεον</span></span> recalls the same words in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:1">John 1:1</span>. Jesus is fully conscious of his deity and Messianic dignity when he performs this humble act. </p>
