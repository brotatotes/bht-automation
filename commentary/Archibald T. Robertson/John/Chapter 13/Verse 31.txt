<p><b> Now </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυν</span></span>). Now at last, the crisis has come with a sense of deliverance from the presence of Judas and of surrender to the Father's will (Westcott).</p>
<p><b>Is glorified </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδοξασθη</span></span>). First aorist passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξαζω</span></span>, consummation of glory in death both for the Son and the Father. For this verb in this sense see already <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:39">John 7:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:16">John 12:16</span> and later <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:3">John 17:3</span>. Four times here in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:31">John 13:31</span>. </p>
