<p><b> Little children </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεκνια</span></span>). Diminutive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεκνα</span></span> and affectionate address as Jesus turns to the effect of his going on these disciples. Only here in this Gospel, but common in I John (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:1">1 John 2:1</span>, etc.), and nowhere else in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Yet a little while </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετ μικρον</span></span>). Accusative of extent of time. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:33">John 7:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:21">John 8:21</span> (to which Jesus here refers); <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:16-19">John 16:16-19</span>.</p>
<p><b>So now I say unto you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα υμιν λεγω αρτ</span></span>). This juncture point (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρτ</span></span>) of time relatively to the past and the future (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:25">John 9:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:12">John 16:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:31">John 16:31</span>). </p>
