<p><b> Wilt thou lay down? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θησεισ;</span></span>). Jesus picks up Peter's very words and challenges his boasted loyalty. See such repetition in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:16">John 16:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:31">John 16:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:17">John 21:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>Shall not crow </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωνηση</span></span>). Aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωνεω</span></span>, to use the voice, used of animals and men. Note strong double negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη</span></span>. Mark adds <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δις</span></span> (twice). John's report is almost identical with that in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:34">Luke 22:34</span>. The other disciples joined in Peter's boast (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:31">Mark 14:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:35">Matthew 26:35</span>).</p>
<p><b>Till thou hast denied </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως ου αρνηση</span></span>). Future middle indicative or aorist middle subjunctive second person singular (form identical) with compound conjunction <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως ου</span></span> (until which time), "till thou deny or deniest" (<i>futurum exactum</i> needless). Peter is silenced for the present. They all "sat astounded and perplexed" (Dods). </p>
