<p><b> Riseth from supper </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγειρετα εκ του δειπνου</span></span>). Vivid dramatic present middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγειρω</span></span>. From the couch on which he was reclining.</p>
<p><b>Layeth aside </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθησιν</span></span>). Same dramatic present active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>His garments </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα ιματια</span></span>). The outer robe <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταλλιθ</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιματιον</span></span>) and with only the tunic (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χιτων</span></span>) on "as one that serveth" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:27">Luke 22:27</span>). Jesus had already rebuked the apostles for their strife for precedence at the beginning of the meal (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:24-30">Luke 22:24-30</span>).</p>
<p><b>A towel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεντιον</span></span>). Latin word <i>linteum</i>, linen cloth, only in this passage in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>Girded himself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διεζωσεν εαυτον</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαζωννυω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-υμ</span></span>), old and rare compound (in Plutarch, LXX, inscriptions, and papyri), to gird all around. In N.T. only in John (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:4">John 13:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:5">John 13:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:7">John 21:7</span>). Did Peter not recall this incident when in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:5">1 Peter 5:5</span> he exhorts all to "gird yourselves with humility" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την ταπεινοφροσυνην εγκομβωσασθε</span></span>)? </p>
