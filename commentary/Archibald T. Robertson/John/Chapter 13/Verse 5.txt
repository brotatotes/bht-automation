<p><b> Poureth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαλλε</span></span>). Vivid present again. Literally, "putteth" (as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:2">John 13:2</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαλλω</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Into the basin </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τον νιπτηρα</span></span>). From verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νιπτω</span></span> (later form of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νιζω</span></span> in this same verse and below) to wash, found only here and in quotations of this passage. Note the article, "the basin" in the room.</p>
<p><b>Began to wash </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηρξατο νιπτειν</span></span>). Back to the aorist again as with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διεζωσεν</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:4">John 13:4</span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Νιπτω</span></span> was common for washing parts of the body like the hands or the feet.</p>
<p><b>To wipe </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκμασσειν</span></span>). "To wipe off" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:3">John 12:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>With the towel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω λεντιω</span></span>). Instrumental case and the article (pointing to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεντιον</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:4">John 13:4</span>).</p>
<p><b>Wherewith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ω</span></span>). Instrumental case of the relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>He was girded </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην διεζωσμενος</span></span>). Periphrastic past perfect of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαζωννυω</span></span> for which verb see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:4">John 13:4</span>. </p>
