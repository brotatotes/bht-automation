<p><b> So he cometh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχετα ουν</span></span>). Transitional use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουν</span></span> and dramatic present again (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχετα</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Lord, dost thou wash my feet? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κυριε, συ μου νιπτεις τους ποδασ;</span></span>). Emphatic contrast in position of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συ μου</span></span> (away from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποδας</span></span>), "Dost thou my feet wash?" "Peter, we may suppose, drew his feet up, as he spoke, in his impulsive humility" (Bernard). </p>
