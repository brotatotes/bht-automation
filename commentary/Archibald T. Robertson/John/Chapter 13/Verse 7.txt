<p><b> I ... thou </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγω ... συ</span></span>). Jesus repeats the pronouns used by Peter in similar contrast.</p>
<p><b>Not now </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ αρτ</span></span>). Just now <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρτ</span></span> means (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:19">John 9:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:25">John 9:25</span>). Used again by Jesus (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:33">John 13:33</span>) and Peter (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:37">John 13:37</span>).</p>
<p><b>But thou shalt understand hereafter </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνωση δε μετα ταυτα</span></span>). Future middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span> (instead of the verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιδα</span></span>) to know by experience. "Thou shalt learn after these things," even if slowly. </p>
