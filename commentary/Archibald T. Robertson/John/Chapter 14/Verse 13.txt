<p><b> Whatsoever ye shall ask </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ αν αιτησητε</span></span>). Indefinite relative clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span> (neuter accusative singular of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οστις</span></span>), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span> and the aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιτεω</span></span>. This is an advance thought over verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:12">John 14:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>In my name </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω ονοματ μου</span></span>). First mention of his "name" as the open sesame to the Father's will. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:26">John 14:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:16">John 15:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:23">John 16:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:24">John 16:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:26">John 16:26</span>.</p>
<p><b>That will I do </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτο ποιησω</span></span>). The Father answers prayers (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:16">John 15:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:23">John 16:23</span>), but so does the Son (here and verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:14">John 14:14</span>). The purpose (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> clause with first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξαζω</span></span>) is "that the Father may be glorified in the Son." Plead Christ's name in prayer to the Father. </p>
