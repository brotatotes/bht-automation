<p><b> If ye love me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν αγαπατε με</span></span>). Third-class condition "if ye keep on loving (present active subjunctive, same contract form as indicative) me." Cf. verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:23">John 14:23</span>.</p>
<p><b>Ye will keep </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρησετε</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρεω</span></span>, not aorist imperative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρησατε</span></span> (keep) as some MSS. have. For this phrase see also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:51">John 8:51</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:23">John 14:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:24">John 14:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:20">John 14:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:5">1 John 2:5</span>. Continued love prevents disobedience. </p>
