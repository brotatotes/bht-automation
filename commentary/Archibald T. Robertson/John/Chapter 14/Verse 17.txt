<p><b> The Spirit of truth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το πνευμα της αληθειας</span></span>). Same phrase in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:27">John 15:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:13">John 16:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:6">1 John 4:6</span>, "a most exquisite title" (Bengel). The Holy Spirit is marked by it (genitive case), gives it, defends it (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:17">John 1:17</span>), in contrast to the spirit of error (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:6">1 John 4:6</span>).</p>
<p><b>Whom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span>). Grammatical neuter gender (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span>) agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα</span></span> (grammatical), but rightly rendered in English by "whom" and note masculine <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινος</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:26">John 14:26</span>). He is a person, not a mere influence.</p>
<p><b>Cannot receive </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου δυνατα λαβειν</span></span>). Left to itself the sinful world is helpless (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:14">1 Corinthians 2:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:7">Romans 8:7</span>), almost Paul's very language on this point. The world lacks spiritual insight (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου θεωρε</span></span>) and spiritual knowledge (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδε γινωσκε</span></span>). It failed to recognize Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:10">John 1:10</span>) and likewise the Holy Spirit.</p>
<p><b>Ye know him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμεις γινωσκετε αυτο</span></span>). Emphatic position of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμεις</span></span> (ye) in contrast with the world (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:19">John 15:19</span>), because they have seen Jesus the Revealer of the Father (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:9">John 14:9</span>).</p>
<p><b>Abides </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενε</span></span>). Timeless present tense.</p>
<p><b>With you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρ' υμιν</span></span>). "By your side," "at home with you," not merely "with you" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεθ' υμων</span></span>) "in the midst of you."</p>
<p><b>In you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν υμιν</span></span>). In your hearts. So note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:16">John 14:16</span>), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα, εν</span></span>. </p>
