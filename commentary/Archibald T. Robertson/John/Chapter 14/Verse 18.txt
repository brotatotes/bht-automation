<p><b> I will not leave </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ αφησω</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφιημ</span></span>, to send away, to leave behind.</p>
<p><b>Desolate </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορφανους</span></span>). Old word (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορφος</span></span>, Latin <i>orbus</i>), bereft of parents, and of parents bereft of children. Common in papyri of orphan children. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:33">John 13:33</span> Jesus called the disciples <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεκνια</span></span> (little children), and so naturally the word means "orphans" here, but the meaning may be "helpless" (without the other Paraclete, the Holy Spirit). The only other N.T. example is in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:27">James 1:27</span> where it means "fatherless."</p>
<p><b>I come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομα</span></span>). Futuristic present as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:3">John 14:3</span>. </p>
