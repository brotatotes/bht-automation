<p><b> But ye behold me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμεις δε θεωρειτε με</span></span>). Emphatic position of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμεις</span></span> (ye) in contrast to the blind, unseeing world. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:33">John 13:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:10">John 16:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:16">John 16:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>Because I live, ye shall live also </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ εγω ζω κα υμεις ζησετε</span></span>). This is our blessed guarantee of immortal, eternal life, the continued living of Jesus. He is the surety of a better covenant (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:22">Hebrews 7:22</span>), the Risen Christ Jesus. He had said it before (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:57">John 6:57</span>). </p>
