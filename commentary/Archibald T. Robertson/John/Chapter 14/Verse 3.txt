<p><b> If I go </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν πορευθω</span></span>). Third-class condition (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορευομα</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>And prepare </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ετοιμασω</span></span>). Same condition and first aorist active subjunctive of the same verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετοιμαζω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>I come again </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παλιν ερχομα</span></span>). Futuristic present middle, definite promise of the second coming of Christ.</p>
<p><b>And will receive you unto myself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα παραλημψομα υμας προς εμαυτον</span></span>). Future middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραλαμβανω</span></span>. Literally, "And I shall take you along (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα-</span></span>) to my own home" (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:36">John 13:36</span>). This blessed promise is fulfilled in death for all believers who die before the Second Coming. Jesus comes for us then also.</p>
<p><b>That where I am there ye may be also </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα οπου ειμ εγω κα υμεις ητε</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειμ</span></span>. This the purpose of the departure and the return of Christ. And this is heaven for the believer to be where Jesus is and with him forever. </p>
