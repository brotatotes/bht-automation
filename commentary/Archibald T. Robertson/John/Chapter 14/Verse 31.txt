<p><b> But that the world may know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ' ινα γνω ο κοσμος</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span>. Elliptical construction (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:3">John 9:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:18">John 13:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:25">John 15:25</span>). "But I surrendered myself to death," etc., before <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Arise, let us go hence </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγειρεσθε, αγωμεν εντευθεν</span></span>). Imperative present middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγειρω</span></span> and the volitive (hortatory) subjunctive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγωμεν</span></span> (the word used in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:7">John 11:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:16">John 11:16</span>) of going to meet death. Apparently the group arose and walked out into the night and the rest of the talk (chs. 15 and 16) and prayer (ch. 17) was in the shadows on the way to Gethsemane. </p>
