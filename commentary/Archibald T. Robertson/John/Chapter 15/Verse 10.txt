<p><b> Ye will abide </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενειτε</span></span>). Future tense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενω</span></span>, conclusion of the third-class condition (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and first aorist active subjunctive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρησητε</span></span>). The correlative of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:15">John 14:15</span>. Each involves the other (love and keeping the commandments of Jesus).</p>
<p><b>And abide </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα μενω</span></span>). The high example of Jesus (the Son) in relation to the Father is set before us as the goal. </p>
