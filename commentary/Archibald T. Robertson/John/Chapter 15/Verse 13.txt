<p><b> Than this </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταυτης</span></span>). Ablative case after the comparative adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μειζονα</span></span> and feminine agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της αγαπης</span></span> (love) understood.</p>
<p><b>That a man lay down his life </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα τις την ψυχην αυτου θη</span></span>). Object clause (non-final use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> in apposition with the ablative pronoun <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταυτης</span></span> and the second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span>. For the phrase see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:11">John 10:11</span> of the good shepherd. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:16">1 John 3:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:7">Romans 5:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>For his friends </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ των φιλων αυτου</span></span>). "In behalf of his friends" and so "in place of his friends." "Self-sacrifice is the high-water mark of love" (Dods). For this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:50">John 11:50</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:13">Galatians 3:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:14">2 Corinthians 5:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:7">Romans 5:7</span>. </p>
