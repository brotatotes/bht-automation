<p><b> No longer </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκετ</span></span>). As he had done in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:16">John 13:16</span>. He was their Rabbi (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:38">John 1:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:13">John 13:13</span>) and Lord (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:13">John 13:13</span>). Paul gloried in calling himself Christ's <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δουλος</span></span> (bond-slave).</p>
<p><b>Servants </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δουλους</span></span>). Bond-servants, slaves.</p>
<p><b>I have called you friends </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμας ειρηκα φιλους</span></span>). Perfect active indicative, permanent state of new dignity. They will prove worthy of it by continued obedience to Christ as Lord, by being good <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δουλο</span></span>. Abraham was called the Friend of God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:23">James 2:23</span>). Are we friends of Christ? </p>
