<p><b> But I chose you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ' εγω εξελεξαμην υμας</span></span>). First aorist middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκλεγω</span></span>. See this same verb and tense used for the choice of the disciples by Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:70">John 6:70</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:18">John 13:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:19">John 15:19</span>). Jesus recognizes his own responsibility in the choice after a night of prayer (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:13">Luke 6:13</span>). So Paul was "a vessel of choice" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκευος εκλογης</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:15">Acts 9:15</span>). Appointed (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εθηκα</span></span>). First aorist active indicative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κ</span></span> aorist) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span>. Note three present active subjunctives with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> (purpose clause) to emphasize continuance (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπαγητε</span></span>, keep on going, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φερητε</span></span>, keep on bearing fruit, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενη</span></span>, keep on abiding), not a mere spurt, but permanent growth and fruit-bearing.</p>
<p><b>He may give </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δω</span></span>). Second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> (purpose clause). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:13">John 14:13</span> for the same purpose and promise, but with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιησω</span></span> (I shall do). See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:23">John 16:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:26">John 16:26</span>. </p>
