<p><b> If the world hateth you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε ο κοσμος υμας μισε</span></span>). Condition of the first class. As it certainly does.</p>
<p><b>Ye know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκετε</span></span>). Present active second person plural indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span> or present active imperative (know), same form.</p>
<p><b>Hath hated </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεμισηκεν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative, "has hated and still hates."</p>
<p><b>Before it hateth you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτον υμων</span></span>). Ablative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμων</span></span> after the superlative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτον</span></span> as with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτος μου</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:15">John 1:15</span>. </p>
