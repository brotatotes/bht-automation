<p><b> But this cometh to pass </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ'</span></span>). Ellipsis in the Greek (no verb), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:3">John 9:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:18">John 13:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>In their law </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω νομω αυτων</span></span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:17">John 8:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:34">John 10:34</span> for this standpoint. "Law" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νομος</span></span>) here is for the whole of Scripture as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:34">John 12:34</span>. The allusion is to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+69:4">Psalms 69:4</span> (or <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+35:19">Psalms 35:19</span>). The hatred of the Jews toward Jesus the promised Messiah (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:11">John 1:11</span>) is "part of the mysterious purpose of God" (Bernard) as shown by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα πληρωθη</span></span> (first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροω</span></span>, to fulfil).</p>
<p><b>Without a cause </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δωρεαν</span></span>). Adverbial accusative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δωρεα</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>, gratuitously, then unnecessarily or <i>gratis</i> (in two <i>Koine</i> tablets, Nageli) as here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:21">Galatians 2:21</span>. </p>
