<p><b> When the Comforter is come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν ελθη ο παρακλητος</span></span>). Indefinite temporal clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν</span></span> and the second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομα</span></span>, "whenever the Comforter comes."</p>
<p><b>Whom I will send unto you from the Father </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ον εγω πεμψω υμιν παρα του πατρος</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:7">John 16:7</span>, but in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:16">John 14:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:26">John 14:26</span> the Father sends at the request of or in the name of Jesus. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:49">Luke 24:49</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:33">Acts 2:33</span>. This is the Procession of the Holy Spirit from the Father and from the Son.</p>
<p><b>Which </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span>). Grammatical neuter to agree with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα</span></span>, and should be rendered "who" like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:26">John 14:26</span>.</p>
<p><b>Proceedeth from the Father </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα του πατρος εκπορευετα</span></span>). "From beside the Father" as in the preceding clause.</p>
<p><b>He </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινος</span></span>). Emphatic masculine pronoun, not neuter (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινο</span></span>) though following <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Shall bear witness of me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρησε περ εμου</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρεω</span></span>. This is the mission of the Paraclete (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:14">John 16:14</span>) as it should be ours. </p>
