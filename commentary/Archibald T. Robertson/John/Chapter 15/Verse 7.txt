<p><b> Ask whatsoever ye will </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο εαν θελητε αιτησασθε</span></span>). Indefinite relative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θελω</span></span>, to wish, to will, and aorist middle imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιτεω</span></span>, to ask. This astounding command and promise (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενησετα</span></span>, future middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span>, it will come to pass) is not without conditions and limitations. It involves such intimate union and harmony with Christ that nothing will be asked out of accord with the mind of Christ and so of the Father. Christ's name is mentioned in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:16">John 15:16</span>; cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:13">John 14:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:23">John 16:23</span>. </p>
