<p><b> Howbeit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span>). One of the most delicate and difficult particles to translate, varying from "and" to "but."</p>
<p><b>When he, the Spirit of truth, is come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν ελθη εκεινοσ, το πνευμα της αληθειας</span></span>). Indefinite relative clause (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν</span></span> and the second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομα</span></span>, no <i>futurum exactum</i>), "whenever he comes." Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινος</span></span> (masculine demonstrative pronoun, though followed by neuter <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα</span></span> in apposition. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:26">John 15:26</span> for this phrase about the Holy Spirit. He shall guide you (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδηγησε υμας</span></span>). Future active of old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδηγεω</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδηγος</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδος</span></span>, way, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγεομα</span></span>, to lead). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+24:5">Psalms 24:5</span> for "lead me into thy truth" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδηγησον με εις την αληθειαν σου</span></span>). Christ is both the Way and the Truth (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:6">John 14:6</span>) and the Holy Spirit is the Guide who shows the way to the Truth (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:14">John 16:14</span>). This he does gradually. We are still learning the truth in Christ.</p>
<p><b>From himself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφ' εαυτου</span></span>). In this he is like Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:26">John 1:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:49">John 12:49</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:10">John 14:10</span>).</p>
<p><b>He shall declare </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγγελε</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγγελλω</span></span>, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:25">John 4:25</span>. See it also repeated in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:14">John 16:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>The things that are yet to come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα ερχομενα</span></span>). Neuter plural articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομα</span></span>, "the coming things." This phrase only here in the N.T. The things already begun concerning the work of the Kingdom (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:19">Luke 7:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:30">Luke 18:30</span>) not a chart of future history. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:20">Luke 7:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:14">John 6:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:27">John 11:27</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ερχομενος</span></span> (the coming one) used of the Messiah. </p>
