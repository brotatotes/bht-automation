<p><b> He shall glorify me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινος εμε δοξασε</span></span>). This is the glory of the Holy Spirit, to glorify Jesus Christ.</p>
<p><b>For he shall take of mine </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ εκ του εμου λημψετα</span></span>). Future middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span> and a definite promise of the Spirit's guidance in interpreting Christ. One need only refer to Peter's sermon at pentecost after the coming of the Holy Spirit, to Peter's Epistles, to Paul's Epistles, to Hebrews, to John's Epistles, to see how under the tutelage of the Holy Spirit the disciples grew into the fulness of the knowledge of God in the face of Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:4">2 Corinthians 6:4</span>). </p>
