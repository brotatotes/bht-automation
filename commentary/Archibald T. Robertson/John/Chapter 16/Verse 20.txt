<p><b> Ye shall weep and lament </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλαυσετε κα θρηνησετε</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλαιω</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θρηνεω</span></span>, both old words (for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλαιω</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:31">John 11:31</span>, for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θρηνεω</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:17">Matthew 11:17</span>), both words used of the loud lamentations so common in the east.</p>
<p><b>Shall rejoice </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρησετα</span></span>). Second future passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαιρω</span></span> in violent contrast. Picture the women on the way to the Cross (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:27">Luke 23:27</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκοπτοντο κα εθρηνουν</span></span>, two descriptive imperfects) and Mary Magdalene by the tomb (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:11">John 20:11</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλαιουσα</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Ye shall be sorrowful </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυπηθησεσθε</span></span>). First future passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυπεω</span></span>, word for inward grief. See the change from sorrow to joy in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:14-16">John 20:14-16</span> when "they disbelieved for joy" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:41">Luke 24:41</span>). So violent was the reaction on the sudden appearance of Jesus. </p>
