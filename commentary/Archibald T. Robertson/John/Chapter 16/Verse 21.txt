<p><b> A woman </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η γυνη</span></span>). "The woman," any woman.</p>
<p><b>When she is in travail </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν τικτη</span></span>). Indefinite temporal clause, "whenever she is about to bear (or give birth)," <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν</span></span> and present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τικτω</span></span>, common O.T. image for pain.</p>
<p><b>Her hour is come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηλθεν η ωρα αυτης</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative, timeless aorist, "her hour" for giving birth which she knows is like a living death.</p>
<p><b>But when she is delivered of the child </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν δε γεννηση το παιδιον</span></span>). Indefinite temporal clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν</span></span> and first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενναω</span></span>. "But whenever she bears the child."</p>
<p><b>The anguish </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της θλιψεως</span></span>). Genitive case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μνημονευε</span></span> of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θλιψις</span></span>, usual word for tribulation (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:21">Matthew 13:21</span>).</p>
<p><b>Is born </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγεννηθη</span></span>). First aorist (effective) passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενναω</span></span>. </p>
