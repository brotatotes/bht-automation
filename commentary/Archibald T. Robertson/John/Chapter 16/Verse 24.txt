<p><b> Hitherto </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως αρτ</span></span>). Up till now the disciples had not used Christ's name in prayer to the Father, but after the resurrection of Jesus they are to do so, a distinct plea for parity with the Father and for worship like the Father.</p>
<p><b>May be fulfilled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η πεπληρωμενη</span></span>). Periphrastic perfect passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροω</span></span> in a purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:11">John 15:11</span> for some verb (first aorist passive subjunctive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>) and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:4">1 John 1:4</span> for same form as here, emphasizing the abiding permanence of the joy. </p>
