<p><b> I say not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου λεγω</span></span>). "I speak not." Christ did pray for the disciples before his death (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:16">John 14:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:9">John 17:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:15">John 17:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:24">John 17:24</span>) and he prays also for sinners (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:34">Luke 23:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:1">1 John 2:1</span>). Here it is the special love of God for disciples of Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:21">John 14:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:23">John 14:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:23">John 17:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:19">1 John 4:19</span>). Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιτεω</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερωταω</span></span> used in practically the same sense as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:23">John 16:23</span>. </p>
