<p><b> Loveth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλε</span></span>). Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλεω</span></span>, the word for warm and friendly love, here used of God's love for the disciples, while in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:16">John 3:16</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπαω</span></span> occurs of God's love for the world.</p>
<p><b>Ye have loved me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεφιληκατε</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλεω</span></span>, "loved and still love me warmly."</p>
<p><b>And have believed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπιστευκατε</span></span>). Perfect active indicative again. Recall the exhortation in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:1">John 14:1</span>. </p>
