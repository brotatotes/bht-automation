<p><b> Now know we </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυν οιδαμεν</span></span>). They had failed to understand the plain words of Jesus about going to the Father heretofore (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:5">John 16:5</span>), but Jesus read their very thoughts (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:19">John 16:19</span>) and this fact seemed to open their minds to grasp his idea.</p>
<p><b>Should ask </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερωτα</span></span>). Present active subjunctive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> in original sense of asking a question.</p>
<p><b>By this </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τουτω</span></span>). In Christ's supernatural insight into their very hearts.</p>
<p><b>From God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο θεου</span></span>). Compare <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα του πατρος</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:27">John 16:27</span>) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ του πατρος</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:28">John 16:28</span>), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο, εκ, παρα</span></span> all with the ablative of source or origin. </p>
