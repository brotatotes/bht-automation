<p><b> Cometh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχετα</span></span>). Futuristic present middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Yea, is come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα εληλυθεν</span></span>). Explanatory use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span> and the perfect active indicative as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:23">John 12:23</span>. The long-looked-for hour (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωρα</span></span>) is so close that it has virtually begun. The time for the arrest of Jesus is near. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:1">John 17:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>That </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>). See verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:2">John 16:2</span> for this same use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> (not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτε</span></span>) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομα ωρα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Ye shall be scattered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκορπισθητε</span></span>). First aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκορπιζω</span></span>, used in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:12">John 10:12</span> of sheep scampering from the wolf. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:30">Matthew 12:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:33">Luke 11:33</span>.</p>
<p><b>To his own </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τα ιδια</span></span>). "To his own home" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:11">John 1:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:27">John 19:27</span>. So Appian VI. 23.</p>
<p><b>Shall leave </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφητε</span></span>). Second aorist subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφιημ</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>And yet </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span>). Clear case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span> in adversative sense, not just "and." </p>
