<p><b> Have I spoken </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λελαληκα</span></span>). Perfect active indicative as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:11">John 15:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:1">John 16:1</span>. Solemn repetition.</p>
<p><b>When their hour is come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν ελθη η ωρα αυτων</span></span>). Indefinite temporal clause, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν</span></span> with the second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομα</span></span>, "whenever their hour comes." The time appointed for these things.</p>
<p><b>Now that </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span>). Simply "that" (declarative conjunction in indirect discourse. Forewarned is to be forearmed. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:19">John 13:19</span>.</p>
<p><b>From the beginning </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ αρχης</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:64">John 6:64</span> but practically like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απ' αρχης</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:27">John 15:27</span>. While Christ was with them, he was the object of attack (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:18">John 15:18</span>). </p>
