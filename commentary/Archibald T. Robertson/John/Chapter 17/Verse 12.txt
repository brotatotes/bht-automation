<p><b> I kept </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετηρουν</span></span>). Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρεω</span></span>, "I continued to keep."</p>
<p><b>I guarded </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφυλαξα</span></span>). First aorist (constative) active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυλασσω</span></span>. Christ was the sentinel (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυλαξ</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:23">Acts 5:23</span>) for them. Is he our sentinel now?</p>
<p><b>But the son of perdition </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε μη ο υιος της απωλειας</span></span>). The very phrase for antichrist (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:3">2 Thessalonians 2:3</span>). Note play on <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απωλετο</span></span>, perished (second aorist middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολλυμ</span></span>). It means the son marked by final loss, not annihilation, but meeting one's destiny (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:25">Acts 2:25</span>). A sad and terrible exception (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:21">Mark 14:21</span>).</p>
<p><b>The scripture </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η γραφη</span></span>). It is not clear whether this is John's own comment or the word of Jesus. Not in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:9">John 18:9</span>. The Scripture referred to is probably <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+41:9">Psalms 41:9</span> quoted in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:18">John 13:18</span> with the same formula <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα πληρωθη</span></span> which see there. </p>
