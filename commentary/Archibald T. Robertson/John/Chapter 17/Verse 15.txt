<p><b> Shouldest take </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρηις</span></span>). First aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιρω</span></span> (liquid verb).</p>
<p><b>From the evil one </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ του πονηρου</span></span>). Ablative case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span>, but can mean the evil man, Satan, or the evil deed. See same ambiguity in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:13">Matthew 6:13</span>. But in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:18">1 John 5:18</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο πονηρος</span></span> is masculine (the evil one). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:10">Revelation 3:10</span>. </p>
