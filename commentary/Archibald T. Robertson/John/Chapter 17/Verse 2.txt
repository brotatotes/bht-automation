<p><b> Authority over all flesh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξουσιαν πασης σαρκος</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Σαρκος</span></span> is objective genitive. Stupendous claim impossible for a mere man to make. Made already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:27">Matthew 11:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:22">Luke 10:22</span> (Q, the Logia of Jesus, our earliest known document about Jesus) and repeated in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:18">Matthew 28:18</span> after his resurrection.</p>
<p><b>That </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>). Secondary purpose with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα δωσε</span></span> (future active indicative) carrying on the idea of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα δοξαση</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:34">John 13:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:21">John 17:21</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα, καθωσ, ινα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Whatsoever </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παν ο</span></span>). A peculiar classical Greek idiom, the collective use of the singular <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παν ο</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:37">John 6:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:39">John 6:39</span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:24">John 17:24</span> and the nominative absolute (<i>nom. pendens</i>) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτοις</span></span> (to them), the dative plural explaining the construction. See Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 653. </p>
