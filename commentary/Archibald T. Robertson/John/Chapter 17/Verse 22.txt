<p><b> And the glory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καγω την δοξαν</span></span>). Literally, "And I the glory," with emphasis on "I." It is the glory of the Incarnate Word (Bernard), cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:14">John 1:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:11">John 2:11</span>, not the glory of the Eternal Word mentioned in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:24">John 17:24</span>. Bengel says: <i>Quanta majestas Christianorum!</i> Then verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:22">John 17:22</span> repeats the unity prayed for in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:21">John 17:21</span>. </p>
