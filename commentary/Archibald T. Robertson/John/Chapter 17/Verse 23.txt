<p><b> That they may be perfected into one </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα ωσιν τετελειωμενο εις εν</span></span>). Purpose clause again with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> (nineteen times in this prayer, this the fifteenth) with the periphrastic perfect passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελειοω</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:4">John 17:4</span>), permanent state, with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις εν</span></span> (into one) as the goal and final result.</p>
<p><b>That the world may know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα γινωσκη</span></span>). Present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> like the present tense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστευω</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:21">John 17:21</span>, "that the world may keep on knowing" with the same pregnant phrase "that thou me didst send" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ συ με απεστειλας</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:8">John 17:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:25">John 17:25</span>.</p>
<p><b>And lovedst them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ηγαπησας αυτους</span></span>). Timeless aorist, but love shown by sending Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:16">John 3:16</span>) and illustrated and proven by the way Christians love one another. </p>
