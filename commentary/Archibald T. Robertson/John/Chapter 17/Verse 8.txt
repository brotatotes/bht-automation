<p><b> The words </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα ρηματα</span></span>). Plural, each word of God, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:34">John 3:34</span>, and of Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:47">John 5:47</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:63">John 6:63</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:68">John 6:68</span>), while the singular (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον λογον σου</span></span>) in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:6">John 17:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:14">John 17:14</span> views God's message as a whole.</p>
<p><b>Knew </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγνωσαν</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span> like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελαβον</span></span> in contrast with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγνωκαν</span></span> (perfect) in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:7">John 17:7</span>. They definitely "received and recognized truly" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αληθως</span></span>). There was comfort to Christ in this fact.</p>
<p><b>They believed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιστευσαν</span></span>). Another aorist parallel with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελαβον</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγνωσαν</span></span>. The disciples believed in Christ's mission from the Father (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:69">John 6:69</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:16">Matthew 16:16</span>). Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεστειλας</span></span> here as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:3">John 17:3</span>. Christ is God's</p>
<p><b>Apostle </b> to man (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:1">Hebrews 3:1</span>). This statement, like a solemn refrain (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Θου διδστ σενδ με</span></span>), occurs five times in this prayer (verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:8">John 17:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:18">John 17:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:21">John 17:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:23">John 17:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:25">John 17:25</span>). </p>
