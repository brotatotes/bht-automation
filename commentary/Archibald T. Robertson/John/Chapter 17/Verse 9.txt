<p><b> I pray </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγω ερωτω</span></span>). Request, not question, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:23">John 16:23</span>.</p>
<p><b>Not for the world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου περ του κοσμου</span></span>). Now at this point in the prayer Christ means. In verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:19">John 17:19</span> Jesus does pray for the world (for future believers) that it may believe (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:21">John 17:21</span>). God loves the whole world (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:16">John 3:16</span>). Christ died for sinners (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:8">Romans 5:8</span>) and prayed for sinners (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:34">Luke 23:34</span>) and intercedes for sinners (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:1">1 John 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:34">Romans 8:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:25">Hebrews 7:25</span>).</p>
<p><b>For those whom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ ων</span></span>). A condensed and common Greek idiom for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ τουτων ους</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτων</span></span> (the demonstrative antecedent) omitted and the relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ους</span></span> attracted from the accusative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ους</span></span> (object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεδωκας</span></span>) to the case (genitive) of the omitted antecedent. </p>
