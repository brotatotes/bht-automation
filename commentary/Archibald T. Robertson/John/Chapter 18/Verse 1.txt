<p><b> With </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:2">John 12:2</span> for another example of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν</span></span> in John (common in Paul). The usual <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα</span></span> reappears in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:2">John 18:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>Over </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περαν</span></span>). "Beyond," preposition with the ablative as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:22">John 6:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:25">John 6:25</span>.</p>
<p><b>Brook </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χειμαρρου</span></span>). Old word, flowing (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ροοσ, ρεω</span></span>) in winter (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χειμα</span></span>), only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Kidron </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον Κεδρων</span></span>). Literally, "of the Cedars," "Brook of the Cedars." Only here in N.T. So <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2sa+15:23">2 Samuel 15:23</span>. Textus Receptus like Josephus (<i>Ant</i>. VIII, 1, 5) has the singular <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του Κεδρων</span></span> (indeclinable). As a matter of fact it was always dry save after a heavy rain.</p>
<p><b>A garden </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κηπος</span></span>). Old word, in N.T. only here, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:26">John 18:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:41">John 19:41</span> (Joseph's); <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:19">Luke 13:19</span>. John, like Luke, does not give the name Gethsemane (only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:32">Mark 14:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:36">Matthew 26:36</span>). The brook of the cedars had many unhallowed associations (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ki+2:37">1 Kings 2:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ki+15:13">1 Kings 15:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ki+23:4">2 Kings 23:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ch+29:16">2 Chronicles 29:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+31:40">Jeremiah 31:40</span>). </p>
