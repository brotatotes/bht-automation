<p><b> Into the sheath </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις την θηκην</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span>, to put for box or sheath, only here in N.T. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:52">Matthew 26:52</span> Christ's warning is given.</p>
<p><b>The cup </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το ποτηριον</span></span>). Metaphor for Christ's death, used already in reply to request of James and John (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:39">Mark 10:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:22">Matthew 20:22</span>) and in the agony in Gethsemane before Judas came (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:36">Mark 14:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:39">Matthew 26:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:42">Luke 22:42</span>), which is not given by John. The case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το ποτηριον</span></span> is the suspended nominative for note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτο</span></span> (it) referring to it.</p>
<p><b>Shall I not drink? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη πιω;</span></span>). Second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πινω</span></span> with the double negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη</span></span> in a question expecting the affirmative answer. Abbott takes it as an exclamation and compares <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:37">John 6:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:25">Mark 14:25</span>. </p>
