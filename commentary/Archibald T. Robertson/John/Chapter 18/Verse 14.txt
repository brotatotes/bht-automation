<p><b> He which gave command </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο συμβουλευσας</span></span>). First aorist active articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμβουλευω</span></span>, old verb (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:4">Matthew 26:4</span>). The reference is to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:50">John 11:50</span>.</p>
<p><b>It was expedient </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμφερε</span></span>). Present active indicative retained in indirect assertion after secondary tense (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην</span></span>, was). Here we have the second aorist active infinitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποθανειν</span></span> as the subject of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμφερε</span></span>, both good idioms in the <i>Koine</i>. </p>
