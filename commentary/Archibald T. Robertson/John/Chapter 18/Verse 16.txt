<p><b> Was standing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστηκε</span></span>). Same form in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:5">John 18:5</span> which see. So also <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστηκεισαν</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:18">John 18:18</span>. Picture of Peter standing outside by the door.</p>
<p><b>Unto the high priest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του αρχιερεως</span></span>). Objective genitive here, but dative in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:15">John 18:15</span>.</p>
<p><b>Unto her that kept the door </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη θυρωρω</span></span>). Old word (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυρα</span></span>, door, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωρα</span></span>, care), masculine in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:3">John 10:3</span>, feminine here, door-keeper (male or female). </p>
