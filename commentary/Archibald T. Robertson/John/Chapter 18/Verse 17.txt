<p><b> The maid </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η παιδισκη</span></span>). Feminine form of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παιδισκος</span></span>, diminutive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παις</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:69">Matthew 26:69</span>. When "the maid the portress" (apposition).</p>
<p><b>Art thou also? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη κα συ ει;</span></span>). Expecting the negative answer, though she really believed he was.</p>
<p><b>This man's </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του ανθρωπου τουτου</span></span>). Contemptuous use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτος</span></span> with a gesture toward Jesus. She made it easy for Peter to say no. </p>
