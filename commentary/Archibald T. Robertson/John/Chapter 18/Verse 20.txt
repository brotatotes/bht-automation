<p><b> Openly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρρησια</span></span>). As already shown (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:4">John 7:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:26">John 8:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:24">John 10:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:39">John 10:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:25">John 16:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:29">John 16:29</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:4">John 7:4</span> for same contrast between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν παρρησια</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν κρυπτω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>I ever taught </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγω παντοτε εδιδαξα</span></span>). Constative aorist active indicative. For the temple teaching see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:19">John 2:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:14">John 7:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:28">John 7:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:20">John 8:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:23">John 19:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:49">Mark 14:49</span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:59">John 6:59</span> for the synagogue teaching (often in the Synoptics). Examples of private teaching are Nicodemus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:3">John 18:3</span>) and the woman of Samaria (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:4">John 18:4</span>). Jesus ignores the sneer at his disciples, but challenges the inquiry about his teaching as needless. </p>
