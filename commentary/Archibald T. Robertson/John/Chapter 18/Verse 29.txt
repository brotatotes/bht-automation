<p><b> Went out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξηλθεν εξω</span></span>). Note both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξω</span></span> (went out outside), since the Sanhedrin would not come into Pilate's palace. Apparently on a gallery over the pavement in front of the palace (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:13">John 19:13</span>).</p>
<p><b>Accusation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηγοριαν</span></span>). Old word for formal charge, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:19">1 Timothy 5:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:6">Titus 1:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Against this man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του ανθρωπου τουτου</span></span>). Objective genitive after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηγοριαν</span></span>. A proper legal inquiry. </p>
