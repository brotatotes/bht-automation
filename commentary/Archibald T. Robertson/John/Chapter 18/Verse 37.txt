<p><b> Art thou a king then? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκουν βασιλευς ε συ;</span></span>). Compound of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουν</span></span> and is clearly ironical expecting an affirmative answer, only here in the N.T., and in LXX only in A text in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ki+5:23">2 Kings 5:23</span>.</p>
<p><b>Thou sayest that </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συ λεγεις οτ</span></span>). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:11">Matthew 27:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:2">Mark 15:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:3">Luke 23:3</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συ λεγεις</span></span> clearly means "yes," as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συ ειπας</span></span> (thou saidst) does in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:64">Matthew 26:64</span> (= "I am," <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγω ειμ</span></span>, in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+41:62">Mark 41:62</span>). Hence here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span> had best be taken to mean "because": "Yes, because I am a king."</p>
<p><b>Have I been born </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγω γεγεννημα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενναω</span></span>. The Incarnation was for this purpose. Note repetition of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τουτο</span></span> (for this purpose), explained by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μαρτυρησω τη αληθεια</span></span> (that I may bear witness to the truth), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> with first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρεω</span></span>. Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:13">1 Timothy 6:13</span>) alludes to this good confession when Christ bore witness (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρησαντος</span></span>) before Pilate. Jesus bore such witness always (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:11">John 3:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:32">John 3:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:7">John 7:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:14">John 8:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:5">Revelation 1:5</span>). </p>
