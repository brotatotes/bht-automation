<p><b> Sat down on the judgement seat </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκαθισεν επ βηματος</span></span>). "Took his seat upon the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βημα</span></span>" (the raised platform for the judge outside the palace as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:5">Acts 7:5</span>). The examination is over and Pilate is now ready for the final stage.</p>
<p><b>The Pavement </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Λιθοστρωτον</span></span>). Late compound from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λιθος</span></span>, stone, and the verbal adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρωτος</span></span> form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρωννυμ</span></span>, to speak, a mosaic or tesselated pavement, spread with stones, in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ch+7:3">2 Chronicles 7:3</span>, Josephus, Epictetus, papyri. The Chaldean name <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Γαββαθα</span></span>, an elevation, was apparently given because of the shape. </p>
