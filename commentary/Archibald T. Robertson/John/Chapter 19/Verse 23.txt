<p><b> Four parts </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεσσερα μερη</span></span>). There were four soldiers, the usual quaternion (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τετραδιον</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:9">Acts 12:9</span>) besides the centurion (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:39">Mark 15:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:54">Matthew 27:54</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:47">Luke 23:47</span>). The clothes (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιματια</span></span>, outer clothes) of the criminal were removed before the crucifixion and belonged to the soldiers. Luke (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:34">Luke 23:34</span>) mentions the division of the garments, but not the number four. The four pieces would be the head gear, the sandals, the girdle, the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταλλιθ</span></span> (outer garment with fringes).</p>
<p><b>The coat was without seam </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο χιτων αραφος</span></span>). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χιτων</span></span> (the inner garment) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:40">Matthew 5:40</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αραφος</span></span> is compound of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ραπτω</span></span>, to sew together, and so seamless (unsewed together), only here in N.T. It occurs elsewhere in Josephus, <i>Ant</i>. III. 6, 4.</p>
<p><b>Woven </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υφαντος</span></span>). Verbal (old word) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υφαινω</span></span> (some MSS. in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:27">Luke 12:27</span>), only here in N.T. </p>
