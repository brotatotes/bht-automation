<p><b> Was set </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκειτο</span></span>). Imperfect middle. John, as eyewitness, had noticed it there.</p>
<p><b>Of vinegar </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οξους</span></span>). Not vinegar drugged with myrrh (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:23">Mark 15:23</span>) and gall (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:34">Matthew 27:34</span>) which Jesus had refused just before the crucifixion.</p>
<p><b>Sponge </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπογγον</span></span>). Old word, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:36">Mark 15:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:48">Matthew 27:48</span>, our "sponge."</p>
<p><b>They put </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιθεντες</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιτιθημ</span></span>, to place around.</p>
<p><b>Upon hyssop </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υσσωπω</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>A reed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλαμω</span></span>) as Mark and Matthew have it. The reed of the hyssop bush was only three or four feet long. </p>
