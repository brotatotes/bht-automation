<p><b> They came </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηρχοντο</span></span>). Imperfect middle of repeated action, "they kept coming and saying" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεγον</span></span>) in derision and mock reverence with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αςε</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαιρε</span></span>, Hail!) as if to Caesar. Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο βασιλευς</span></span> (the king) in address.</p>
<p><b>They struck him with their hands </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδιδοσαν αυτω ραπισματα</span></span>). Imperfect of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>, repetition, "they kept on giving him slaps with their hands." See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:22">John 18:22</span> for this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ραπισμα</span></span>. </p>
