<p><b> Had received </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελαβεν</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>. Jesus took the vinegar (a stimulant), though he had refused the drugged vinegar. It is finished (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τετελεστα</span></span>). Same for as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:28">John 19:28</span>. A cry of victory in the hour of defeat like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νενικηκα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:33">John 16:33</span>. Jesus knew the relation of his death to redemption for us (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:45">Mark 10:45</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:28">Matthew 20:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:28">Matthew 26:28</span>).</p>
<p><b>Bowed his head </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλινας την κεφαλην</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλινω</span></span>. This vivid detail only in John.</p>
<p><b>Gave up his spirit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεδωκεν το πνευμα</span></span>). With the quotation of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+31:5">Psalms 31:5</span> according to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:46">Luke 23:46</span>, "Father, into thy hands I commend my spirit" (the last of the seven sayings of Jesus on the Cross that are preserved for us). Jesus died with the words of this Psalm upon his lips. The apostle John had come back to the Cross. </p>
