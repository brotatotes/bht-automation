<p><b> But secretly for fear of the Jews </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεκρυμμενος δε δια τον φοβον των Ιουδαιων</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρυπτω</span></span>. An example of the rulers described in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:41-43">John 12:41-43</span> who through cowardice feared to own their faith in Jesus as the Messiah. But it must be put down to the credit of Joseph that he showed courage in this darkest hour when the majority had lost heart.</p>
<p><b>That he might take away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα αρη</span></span>). Final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιρω</span></span>. Else the body of Jesus might have gone to the potter's field. Pilate gladly consented. </p>
