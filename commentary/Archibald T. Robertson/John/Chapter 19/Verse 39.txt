<p><b> Nicodemus also </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα Νικοδημος</span></span>). The Synoptics tell about Joseph of Arimathea, but only John adds the help that Nicodemus gave him in the burial of Jesus, these two timid disciples, Nicodemus now at last taking an open stand.</p>
<p><b>At the first </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το πρωτον</span></span>). Adverbial accusative and reference to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:1">John 3:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Mixture </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιγμα</span></span>). Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιγνυμ</span></span>, to mix, only here in the N.T. Many old MSS. have here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελιγμα</span></span> (roll), from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελισσω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:12">Hebrews 1:12</span>), another late word here only in N.T. It was common to use sweet-smelling spices in the burial (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ch+16:14">2 Chronicles 16:14</span>).</p>
<p><b>Pound </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λιτρας</span></span>). Late word for twelve ounces, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:3">John 12:3</span>. Nicodemus was a rich man and probably covered the entire body with the spices. </p>
