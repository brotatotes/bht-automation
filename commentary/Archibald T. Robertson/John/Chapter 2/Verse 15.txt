<p><b> A scourge of cords </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φραγελλιον εκ σχοινιων</span></span>). The Latin <i>flagellum</i>. In papyri, here only in N.T. and note Latin <i>l</i> becomes <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρ</span></span> in <i>Koine</i>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Σχοινιων</span></span> is a diminutive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σχοινος</span></span> (a rush), old word for rope, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:32">Acts 27:32</span>.</p>
<p><b>Cast out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεβαλεν</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκβαλλω</span></span>. It is not said that Jesus smote the sheep and oxen (note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τε κα</span></span>, both and), for a flourish of the scourge would answer.</p>
<p><b>He poured out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεχεεν</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκχεω</span></span>, to pour out.</p>
<p><b>The changers' money </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των κολλυβιστων τα κερματα</span></span>). "The small pieces of money (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κερματα</span></span>, cut in pieces, change) of the bankers (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολλυβιστης</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολλυβος</span></span>, clipped, late word see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:12">Matthew 21:12</span>)." Perhaps he took up the boxes and emptied the money.</p>
<p><b>Overthrew their tables </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τας τραπεζας ανετρεψεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανατρεπω</span></span>, to turn up, though some MSS. have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεστρεψεν</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναστρεφω</span></span>, also to turn up. </p>
