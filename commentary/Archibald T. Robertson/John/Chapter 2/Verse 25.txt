<p><b> And because he needed not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα οτ χρειαν ειχεν</span></span>). Imperfect active, "and because he did not have need."</p>
<p><b>That any one should bear witness concerning man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα τις μαρτυρηση περ του ανθρωπου</span></span>). Non-final use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> with first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρεω</span></span> and the generic article (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ του ανθρωπου</span></span>) concerning mankind as in the next clause also.</p>
<p><b>For he himself knew </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτος γαρ εγινωσκεν</span></span>). Imperfect active, "for he himself kept on knowing" as he did from the start.</p>
<p><b>What was in man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τ ην εν τω ανθρωπω</span></span>). Indirect question with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστιν</span></span> of the direct changed to the imperfect <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην</span></span>, a rare idiom in the <i>Koine</i>. This supernatural knowledge of man is a mark of deity. Some men of genius can read men better than others, but not in the sense meant here. </p>
