<p><b> Waterpots </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υδρια</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υδωρ</span></span> (water) and used in papyri for pots or pans for holding money or bread as well as water. These stone (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λιθινα</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:3">2 Corinthians 3:3</span>) jars full of water were kept handy (<b> set there </b>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κειμενα</span></span>, present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κειμα</span></span>) at a feast for ceremonial cleansing of the hands (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ki+3:11">2 Kings 3:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:3">Mark 7:3</span>), "after the Jews' manner of purifying" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα τον καθαρισμον των Ιουδαιων</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:44">Mark 1:44</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:22">Luke 2:22</span> for the word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθαρισμος</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθαριζω</span></span>) which fact also raised a controversy with disciples of John because of his baptizing (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:25">John 3:25</span>).</p>
<p><b>Containing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χωρουσα</span></span>). Present active participle feminine plural of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χωρεω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χωρος</span></span>, place, space, having space or room for.</p>
<p><b>Two or three firkins apiece </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανα μετρητας δυο η τρεις</span></span>). The word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετρητης</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετρεω</span></span>, to measure, simply means "measurer," an amphora for measuring liquids (in Demosthenes, Aristotle, Polybius), the Hebrew <i>bath</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ch+4:5">2 Chronicles 4:5</span>), here only in N.T., about 8 1/2 English gallons. Each <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υδρια</span></span> thus held about 20 gallons. This common distributive use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανα</span></span> occurs here only in this Gospel, but is in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:8">Revelation 4:8</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:28">John 4:28</span> a much smaller <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υδρια</span></span> was used for carrying water. </p>
