<p><b> Tasted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγευσατο</span></span>). First aorist middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γευομα</span></span>. As it was his function to do.</p>
<p><b>The water now become wine </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το υδωρ οινον γεγενημενον</span></span>). Accusative case, though the genitive also occurs with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γευομα</span></span>. Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οινον</span></span>, predicative accusative. The tablemaster knew nothing of the miracle, "whence it was" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποθεν εστιν</span></span>, indirect question retaining present indicative). The servants knew the source of the water, but not the power that made the wine.</p>
<p><b>Calleth the bridegroom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωνε τον νυμφιον</span></span>). As apparently responsible for the supply of the wine (<b> thou hast kept </b> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τετηρηκας</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:15">Matthew 9:15</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυμφιος</span></span>. When men have drunk freely (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν μεθυσθωσιν</span></span>). Indefinite temporal clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν</span></span> and first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεθυσκω</span></span>. The verb does not mean that these guests are now drunk, but that this is a common custom to put "the worse" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον ελασσω</span></span>, the less, the inferior) wine last. It is real wine that is meant by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οινος</span></span> here. Unlike the Baptist Jesus mingled in the social life of the time, was even abused for it (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:19">Matthew 11:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:34">Luke 7:34</span>). But this fact does not mean that today Jesus would approve the modern liquor trade with its damnable influences. The law of love expounded by Paul in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:1">John 2:1</span> and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:14">John 2:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:15">John 2:15</span> teaches modern Christians to be willing gladly to give up what they see causes so many to stumble into sin. </p>
