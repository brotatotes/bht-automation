<p><b> Now on the first day of the week </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη δε μια των σαββατων</span></span>). Locative case of time when. Both Mark (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+16:2">Mark 16:2</span>) and Luke (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:1">Luke 24:1</span>) have this very idiom of the cardinal <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη μια</span></span>, instead of the usual ordinal <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη πρωτη</span></span> (first), an idiom common in the papyri and in the modern Greek (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 671). In all three instances also we have the genitive plural <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των σαββατων</span></span> for "the week" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:7">Acts 20:7</span>. The singular <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαββατον</span></span> also occurs for "the week" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:12">Luke 18:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+16:9">Mark 16:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>Cometh Mary Magdalene </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μαρια η Μαγδαληνη ερχετα</span></span>). Vivid historical present. Mary Magdalene is not to be confounded with Mary of Bethany.</p>
<p><b>While it was yet dark </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκοτιας ετ ουσης</span></span>). Genitive absolute. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκοτια</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:17">John 6:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:27">Matthew 10:27</span>. Mark (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+16:2">Mark 16:2</span>) says the sun was risen on their actual arrival. She started from the house while still dark.</p>
<p><b>Taken away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηρμενον</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιρω</span></span>, predicate accusative in apposition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον λιθον</span></span>. </p>
