<p><b> She turned herself back </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστραφη εις τα οπισω</span></span>). Second aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρεφω</span></span> in an intransitive and almost reflective sense. In the disappearance of the aorist middle before the aorist passive see Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p.817. See also <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στραφεισα</span></span> (second aorist passive participle) in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:16">John 20:16</span>. On <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τα οπισω</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:66">John 6:66</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:6">John 18:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Standing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστωτα</span></span>). Second perfect active (intransitive) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span>. Instinctively Mary felt the presence of some one behind her.</p>
<p><b>Was </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστιν</span></span>). Present active indicative retained in indirect discourse after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηιδε</span></span> (knew). </p>
