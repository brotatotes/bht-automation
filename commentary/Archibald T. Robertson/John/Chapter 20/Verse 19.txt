<p><b> When therefore it was evening on that day </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουσης ουν οψιας τη ημερα εκεινε</span></span>). Genitive absolute with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οψια</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οψιος</span></span>, late), old word with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωρα</span></span> (hour) understood and here for the time from six to nine (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:16">John 6:16</span>) and the locative case of time with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημερα</span></span> (day). John often uses this note of time (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:39">John 1:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:9">John 5:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:53">John 11:53</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:20">John 14:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:23">John 16:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:26">John 16:26</span>). The addition of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη μια σαββατων</span></span> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:1">John 20:1</span> for this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μια</span></span> like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτη</span></span>) proves that John is using Roman time, not Jewish, for here evening follows day instead of preceding it.</p>
<p><b>When the doors were shut </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των θυρων κεκλεισμενων</span></span>). Genitive absolute again with perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλειω</span></span>, shut to keep the Jews out. News of the empty tomb had already spread (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:11">Matthew 28:11</span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:13">John 7:13</span> for the phrase "for fear of the Jews"; cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:42">John 12:42</span>.</p>
<p><b>Stood in the midst </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστη εις το μεσον</span></span>). Second aorist (ingressive) active (intransitive) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span>, "stepped into the midst."</p>
<p><b>Peace be unto you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ειρηνη υμιν</span></span>). The usual oriental salutation as in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:21">John 20:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:26">John 20:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:36">Luke 24:36</span>, here with probable reference to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:27">John 14:27</span> (Christ's legacy of peace). </p>
