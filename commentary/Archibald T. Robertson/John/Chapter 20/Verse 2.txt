<p><b> Runneth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρεχε</span></span>). Vivid dramatic present indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρεχω</span></span>. John deals only with Mary Magdalene. She left the tomb at once before the rest and without seeing the angels as told in the Synoptics (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+16:2-8">Mark 16:2-8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:5-8">Matthew 28:5-8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:1-8">Luke 24:1-8</span>). Luke (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:9-12">Luke 24:9-12</span>) does not distinguish between the separate report of Mary Magdalene and that of the other women.</p>
<p><b>To Simon Peter </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς Σιμωνα Πετρον</span></span>). Full name as usual in John and back with John and the other disciples. The association of Peter and the other disciple in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:18-21">John 20:18-21</span> is like that between Peter and John in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:1-5">John 20:1-5</span>.</p>
<p><b>Loved </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφιλε</span></span>). Imperfect of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλεω</span></span> for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:20">John 5:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:3">John 11:3</span> and for distinction from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπαω</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:5">John 11:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:23">John 13:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:7">John 21:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:15">John 21:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:17">John 21:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>They have taken away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηραν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιρω</span></span>, indefinite plural.</p>
<p><b>We know not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ οιδαμεν</span></span>). Mary associates the other women with her in her ignorance. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εθηκαν</span></span> (have laid) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:42">John 19:42</span>. Mary fears a grave robbery. She has no idea of the resurrection of Jesus. </p>
