<p><b> Even so send I you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καγω πεμπω υμας</span></span>). Jesus has often spoken of the Father's sending him using both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστελλω</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεμπω</span></span>. Here he employs both words in practically the same sense. Jesus still bears the Commission of the Father (perfect active indicative). For this balanced contention (as ... so) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:57">John 6:57</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:15">John 10:15</span>. This is the first of the three commissions given by the Risen Christ (another on the mountain in Galilee (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:16-20">Matthew 28:16-20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:6">1 Corinthians 15:6</span>), another on the Mount of Olives (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:44-51">Luke 24:44-51</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:3-11">Acts 1:3-11</span>). </p>
