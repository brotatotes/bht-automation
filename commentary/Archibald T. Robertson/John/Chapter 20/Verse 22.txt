<p><b> He breathed on them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεφυσησεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμφυσαω</span></span>, late verb, here only in N.T. though eleven times in the LXX and in the papyri. It was a symbolic art with the same word used in the LXX when God breathed the breath of life upon Adam (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+2:7">Genesis 2:7</span>). It occurs also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+37:9">Ezekiel 37:9</span>. See Christ's promise in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:23">John 16:23</span>. Jesus gives the disciples a foretaste of the great pentecost.</p>
<p><b>Receive ye the Holy Ghost </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαβετε πνευμα αγιον</span></span>). Second aorist (ingressive) active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>. Note absence of article here (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα αγιον</span></span>) though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το πνευμα το αγιον</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:26">John 14:26</span>. No real distinction is to be observed, for Holy Spirit is treated as a proper name with or without the article. </p>
