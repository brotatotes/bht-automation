<p><b> They both </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο δυο</span></span>). "The two" (Peter and the other disciple whom Jesus loved).</p>
<p><b>Ran together </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετρεχον ομου</span></span>). Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρεχω</span></span>. It was a race in eagerness to reach the tomb of Jesus.</p>
<p><b>Outran Peter </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προεδραμεν ταχειον του Πετρου</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προτρεχω</span></span>, old verb, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:4">Luke 19:4</span>, to run on before (ahead). "He ran ahead more swiftly (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:27">John 13:27</span>) than Peter" (ablative case after comparative adverb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταχειον</span></span>, <i>Koine</i> for older <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θασσον</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>First </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτος</span></span>). Predicative nominative (not adverb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προτον</span></span>) and superlative used where only two involved. John won the race. </p>
