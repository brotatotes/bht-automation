<p><b> The napkin </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το σουδαριον</span></span>). Already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:44">John 11:44</span> which see. This napkin for the head was in a separate place.</p>
<p><b>Rolled up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντετυλιγμενον</span></span>). Perfect passive participle, predicate accusative like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κειμενον</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντυλισσω</span></span>, late verb, to wrap in, to roll up, already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:59">Matthew 27:59</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:53">Luke 23:53</span>. It was arranged in an orderly fashion. There was no haste.</p>
<p><b>By itself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χωρις</span></span>). Old adverb, "apart," "separately." </p>
