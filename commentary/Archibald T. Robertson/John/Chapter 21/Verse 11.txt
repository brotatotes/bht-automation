<p><b> Went up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεβη</span></span>). Into the little boat or dinghy.</p>
<p><b>Drew </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειλκυσεν</span></span>). Same verb as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελκυσα</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:6">John 21:6</span>. Peter now did what they had failed to do.</p>
<p><b>Three </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τριων</span></span>). The addition "three" to the "hundred and fifty" looks as if they were actually counted these "large" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεγαλων</span></span>) fish. It was a great fish story that John recalls vividly.</p>
<p><b>Was not rent </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ εσχισθη</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σχιζω</span></span>, to split (our word "schism"). </p>
