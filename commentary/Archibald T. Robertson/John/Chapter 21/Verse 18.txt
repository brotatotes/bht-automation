<p><b> Thou girdest thyself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εζωννυες σεαυτον</span></span>). Imperfect active of customary action of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωννυω</span></span>, old verb, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:8">Acts 12:8</span>. So as to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιεπατεις</span></span> (walkedst) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηθελες</span></span> (wouldest), two other imperfects of customary action.</p>
<p><b>When thou shalt be old </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν γηρασηις</span></span>). Indefinite temporal clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν</span></span> and the first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γηρασκω</span></span>, old verb to grow old, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:13">Hebrews 8:13</span>, "whenever thou growest old." </p>
