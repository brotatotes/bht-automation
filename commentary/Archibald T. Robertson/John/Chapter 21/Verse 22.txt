<p><b> If I will </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν θελω</span></span>). Condition of the third class with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and the present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θελω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Till I come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως ερχομα</span></span>). Literally, "while I am coming" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως</span></span> and the present indicative, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως ελθω</span></span> (second aorist active subjunctive).</p>
<p><b>What is that to thee? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τ προς σε;</span></span>). A sharp rebuke to Peter's keen curiosity.</p>
<p><b>Follow thou me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συ μο ακολουθε</span></span>). "Do thou me keep on following." That lesson Peter needed. </p>
