<p><b> For God sent not the Son </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου γαρ απεστειλεν ο θεος τον υιον</span></span>). Explanation (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γαρ</span></span>) of God's sending the Son into the world. First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστελλω</span></span>. John uses both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστελλω</span></span> from which comes <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστολος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:34">John 3:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:36">John 5:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:38">John 5:38</span>, etc.) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεμπω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:34">John 4:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:23">John 5:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:24">John 5:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:30">John 5:30</span>, etc.) for God's sending the Son and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεμπω</span></span> more frequently, but with no real difference in meaning. All the Gospels use <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο υιος</span></span> in the absolute sense in contrast with the Father (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:32">Mark 13:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:27">Matthew 11:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:22">Luke 10:22</span>).</p>
<p><b>To judge </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα κρινη</span></span>). Final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the present (or aorist) active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρινω</span></span>. The Messiah does judge the world as Jesus taught (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:31">Matthew 25:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:27">John 5:27</span>), but this was not the primary or the only purpose of his coming. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:1">Matthew 7:1</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρινω</span></span>, to pick out, select, approve, condemn, used so often and in so many varying contexts in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>But that the world should be saved through him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ ινα σωθη ο κοσμος δι' αυτου</span></span>). First aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωζω</span></span>, the common verb to save (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σως</span></span>, safe and sound), from which <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωτηρ</span></span> (Saviour) comes (the Saviour of the world, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:42">John 4:42</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:14">1 John 4:14</span>) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωτηρια</span></span> (salvation, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:22">John 4:22</span> here only in John). The verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωζω</span></span> is often used for physical health (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:28">Mark 5:28</span>), but here of the spiritual salvation as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:34">John 5:34</span>. </p>
