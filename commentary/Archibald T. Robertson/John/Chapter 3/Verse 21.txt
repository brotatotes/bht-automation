<p><b> That doeth the truth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ποιων την αληθειαν</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:6">1 John 1:6</span> for this striking phrase.</p>
<p><b>Comes to the light </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχετα προς το φως</span></span>). Is drawn by the light, spiritual heliotropes, not driven from it.</p>
<p><b>That may be made manifest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα φανερωθη</span></span>). Final <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> with first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανεροω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>They have been wrought in God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν θεω εστιν ειργασμενα</span></span>). Periphrastic perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργαζομα</span></span>. He does not claim that they are perfect, only that they have been wrought in the sphere of and in the power of God. Hence he wants the light turned on. </p>
