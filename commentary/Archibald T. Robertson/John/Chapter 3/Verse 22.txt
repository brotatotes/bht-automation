<p><b> After these things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα ταυτα</span></span>). Transition after the interview with Nicodemus. For the phrase see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:1">John 5:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:1">John 6:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:1">John 7:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Into the land of Judea </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις την Ιουδαιαν γην</span></span>). Into the country districts outside of Jerusalem. The only example of this phrase in the N.T., but "the region of Judea" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η Ιουδαια χωρα</span></span>) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:5">Mark 1:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>He tarried </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διετριβεν</span></span>). Descriptive imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διατριβω</span></span>, old verb to rub between or hard, to spend time (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:3">Acts 14:3</span>).</p>
<p><b>Baptized </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβαπτιζεν</span></span>). Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαπτιζω</span></span>. "He was baptizing." The six disciples were with him and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:2">John 4:2</span> John explains that Jesus did the baptizing through the disciples. </p>
