<p><b> The bridegroom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυμφιος</span></span>). Predicate nominative without article. Both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυμφη</span></span> (bride) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυμφιος</span></span> are old and common words. Jesus will use this metaphor of himself as the Bridegroom (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:19">Mark 2:19</span>) and Paul develops it (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:2">2 Corinthians 11:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:23-32">Ephesians 5:23-32</span>) and so in Revelation (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:7">John 19:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:2">John 21:2</span>). John is only like the <i>paranymph</i> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρανυμφιος</span></span>) or "the friend of the bridegroom." His office is to bring groom and bride together. So he stands expectant (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστηκως</span></span>, second perfect active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span>) and listens (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουων</span></span>, present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουω</span></span>) with joy (<b> rejoiceth greatly </b>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρα χαιρε</span></span>, "with joy rejoices") to the music of the bridegroom's voice.</p>
<p><b>This my joy therefore is fulfilled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτη ουν η χαρα πεπληρωτα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροω</span></span>, stands filled like a cup to the brim with joy. </p>
