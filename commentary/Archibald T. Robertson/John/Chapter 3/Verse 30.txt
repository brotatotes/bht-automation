<p><b> Must </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span>). It has to be (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:14">John 3:14</span>). He is to go on growing (present active infinitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυξανειν</span></span>) while I go on decreasing (present passive infinitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελαττουσθα</span></span>, from comparative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελαττων</span></span>, less). These are the last words that we have from John till the despondent message from the dungeon in Machaerus whether Jesus is after all the Messiah (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:2">Matthew 11:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:19">Luke 7:19</span>). He went on to imprisonment, suspense, martyrdom, while Jesus grew in popular favour till he had his <i>via dolorosa</i>. "These last words of St. John are the fulness of religious sacrifice and fitly close his work" (Westcott). </p>
