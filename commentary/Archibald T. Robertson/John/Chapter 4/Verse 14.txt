<p><b> That I shall give him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου εγω δωσω αυτω</span></span>). Relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου</span></span> attracted to the case (genitive) of the antecedent (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υδατος</span></span>). Future active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Shall never thirst </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη διψησε εις τον αιονα</span></span>). The double negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη</span></span> is used with either the future indicative as here or the aorist subjunctive, the strongest possible negative. See both constructions (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη πειναση</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου με διψησε</span></span>) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:35">John 6:35</span>. Jesus has not answered the woman's question save by the necessary implication here that he is superior to Jacob.</p>
<p><b>A well of water springing up unto eternal life </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πηγη υδατος αλλομενου εις ζωην αιωνιον</span></span>). "Spring (or fountain) of water leaping (bubbling up) unto life eternal." Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλομα</span></span>, old verb, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:8">Acts 3:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:10">Acts 14:10</span>. The woman's curiosity is keenly excited about this new kind of water. </p>
