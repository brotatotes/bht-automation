<p><b> Sir </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κυριε</span></span>). So still.</p>
<p><b>I perceive </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεωρω</span></span>). "I am beginning to perceive" from what you say, your knowledge of my private life (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:29">John 4:29</span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:23">John 2:23</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεωρεω</span></span> which John's Gospel has 23 times, of bodily sight (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:6">John 20:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:14">John 20:14</span>), of mental contemplation (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:45">John 12:45</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:17">John 14:17</span>). See both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεωρεω</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπτομα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:51">John 1:51</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:16">John 16:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>That thou art a prophet </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ προφητης ε συ</span></span>). "That a prophet art thou" (emphasis on "thou"). She felt that this was the explanation of his knowledge of her life and she wanted to change the subject at once to the outstanding theological dispute. </p>
