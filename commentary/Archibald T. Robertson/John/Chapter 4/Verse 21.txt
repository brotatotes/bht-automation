<p><b> Believe me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστευε μο</span></span>). Correct text. Present active imperative. Unique phrase in place of the common <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμην αμην</span></span> (verily, verily).</p>
<p><b>The hour cometh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχετα ωρα</span></span>). "There is coming an hour." The same idiom occurs also in John <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:34">John 4:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:25">John 5:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:28">John 5:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:2">John 16:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:25">John 16:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:32">John 16:32</span>.</p>
<p><b>Neither in this mountain nor in Jerusalem </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτε εν τω ορε τουτω ουτε εν Ιεροσολυμοις</span></span>). The worship of God will be emancipated from bondage to place. Both Jews and Samaritans are wrong as to the "necessity" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span>). "These ancient rivalries will disappear when the spirituality of true religion is fully realized." Jesus told this sinful woman one of his greatest truths. </p>
