<p><b> Meat </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βρωσιν</span></span>). Originally the act of eating (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:17">Romans 14:17</span>) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βιβρωσκω</span></span>, but soon and commonly as that which is eaten like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βρωμα</span></span> once in John (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:34">John 4:34</span>). So here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:27">John 6:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:55">John 6:55</span>. Cf. vernacular English "good eating," "good eats."</p>
<p><b>I ... ye </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγω ... υμεις</span></span>). Emphatic contrast. Spiritual food Jesus had. </p>
