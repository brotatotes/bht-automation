<p><b> For herein </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν γαρ τουτω</span></span>). In this relation between the sower and the reaper.</p>
<p><b>The saying </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο λογος</span></span>). Like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:15">1 Timothy 1:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:1">1 Timothy 3:1</span>, etc. Probably a proverb that is particularly true (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αληθινος</span></span> for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:9">John 1:9</span>) in the spiritual realm.</p>
<p><b>One soweth, and another reapeth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλος εστιν ο σπειρων κα αλλος ο θεριζων</span></span>). "One is the sower and another the reaper." It is sad when the sower misses the joy of reaping (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+31:8">Job 31:8</span>) and has only the sowing in tears (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+126:5">Psalms 126:5</span>). This may be the punishment for sin (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+28:30">Deuteronomy 28:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mic+6:15">Micah 6:15</span>). Sometimes one reaps where he has not sown (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+6:11">Deuteronomy 6:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jos+24:13">Joshua 24:13</span>). It is the prerogative of the Master to reap (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:26">Matthew 25:26</span>), but Jesus here lets the disciples share his joy. </p>
