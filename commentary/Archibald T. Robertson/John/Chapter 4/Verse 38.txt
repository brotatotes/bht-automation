<p><b> I sent </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγω απεστειλα</span></span>). Emphatic use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγω</span></span> and first aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστελλω</span></span> common in John for to send.</p>
<p><b>Whereon ye have not laboured </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ουχ υμεις κεκοπιακατε</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοπιαω</span></span> for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:6">John 4:6</span>. So also <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεκοπιακασιν</span></span> in next line. The disciples had done no sowing here in Sychar, only Jesus and the woman.</p>
<p><b>Others </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλο</span></span>: Jesus, the Baptist, the prophets).</p>
<p><b>And ye </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα υμεις</span></span>). Emphatic contrast.</p>
<p><b>Have entered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισεληλυθατε</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισερχομα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Into their labour </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τον κοπον αυτων</span></span>). Into the fruit and blessed results of their toil (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοπος</span></span>). This is always true as seen in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:5-7">Acts 8:5-7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:14">Acts 8:14</span>. </p>
