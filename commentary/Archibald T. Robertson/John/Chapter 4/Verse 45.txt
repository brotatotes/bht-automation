<p><b> So when </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτε ουν</span></span>). Transitional use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουν</span></span>, sequence, not consequence.</p>
<p><b>Received him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδεξαντο αυτον</span></span>). First aorist middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεχομα</span></span>, "welcomed him." Jesus had evidently anticipated a quiet arrival.</p>
<p><b>Having seen </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εωρακοτες</span></span>). Perfect active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span>. Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεωρουντες</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:23">John 2:23</span> about this very thing at the feast in Jerusalem. The miracles of Jesus at that first passover made a stir.</p>
<p><b>For they also went </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα αυτο γαρ ηλθον</span></span>). The Samaritans did not go and so Jesus was a new figure to them, but the Galileans, as orthodox Jews, did go and so were predisposed in his favour. </p>
