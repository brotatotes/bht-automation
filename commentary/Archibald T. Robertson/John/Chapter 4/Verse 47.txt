<p><b> When he heard </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουσας</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουω</span></span>. The news spread rapidly about Jesus.</p>
<p><b>Was come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκε</span></span>). Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκω</span></span>, one of the perfective presents, retained in indirect discourse. He had heard the people talk about the miracles in Jerusalem and the first one in Cana.</p>
<p><b>Went and besought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απηλθεν κα ηρωτα</span></span>). Ingressive aorist indicative (went off at once) and imperfect active (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηρωτα</span></span>, began to beg and kept it up).</p>
<p><b>That he would come down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα καταβη</span></span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταβαινω</span></span>, come down at once)</p>
<p><b>and heal his son </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ιασητα αυτου τον υιον</span></span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> construction, sub-final use or object clause, with first aorist middle subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιαομα</span></span>, completely heal).</p>
<p><b>For he was at the point of death </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημελλεν γαρ αποθνησκειν</span></span>). Reason (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γαρ</span></span>) for the urgency. Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μελλω</span></span> with present active infinitive old and common verb for what is about to be and it is used with the infinitive present as here, the aorist infinitive (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:16">Revelation 13:16</span>), or the future infinitive (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:28">Acts 11:28</span>). The idiom is used of the impending death of Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:51">John 11:51</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:33">John 12:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:32">John 18:32</span>). </p>
