<p><b> Except ye see </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν μη ιδητε</span></span>). Condition of the third class (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν μη</span></span>, negative, with second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span>). Jesus is not discounting his "signs and wonders" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σημεια κα τερατα</span></span>, both words together here only in John, though common in N.T. as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:24">Matthew 24:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:22">Mark 13:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:19">Acts 2:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:22">Acts 2:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:43">Acts 2:43</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:9">2 Thessalonians 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:4">Hebrews 2:4</span>), though he does seem disappointed that he is in Galilee regarded as a mere miracle worker.</p>
<p><b>Ye will in no wise believe </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη πιστευσητε</span></span>). Strong double negative with aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστευω</span></span>, picturing the stubborn refusal of people to believe in Christ without miracles. </p>
