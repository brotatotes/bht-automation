<p><b> Sir </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κυριε</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:38">John 1:38</span>.</p>
<p><b>Come down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταβηθ</span></span>). Second aorist active imperative, tense and tone of urgency. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ερε μψ χιλδ διε</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πριν αποθανειν το παιδιον μου</span></span>). Regular idiom with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πριν</span></span> in positive clause, second aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποθνησκω</span></span> and accusative of general reference, "before dying as to my child." Bengel notes that he only thought Jesus had power before death as even Martha and Mary felt at first (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:21">John 11:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:32">John 11:32</span>). But the father's heart goes out to Jesus. </p>
