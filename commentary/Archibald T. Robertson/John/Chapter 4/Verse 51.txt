<p><b> As he was now going down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηδη αυτου καταβαινοντος</span></span>). Genitive absolute in-spite of the fact that <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτω</span></span> (associative instrumental case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπηντησαν</span></span> aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπανταω</span></span>) is near.</p>
<p><b>That his son lived </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ ο παις αυτου ζη</span></span>). Present active indicative preserved in indirect discourse (cf. the words of Jesus in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:50">John 4:50</span>). Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παις</span></span> here (only example in John), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υιος</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:50">John 4:50</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παιδιον</span></span> (diminutive of tenderness) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:49">John 4:49</span>. </p>
