<p><b> Jacob's well </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πηγη του Ιακωβ</span></span>). "A spring of Jacob" (here and verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:14">John 4:14</span>), but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φρεαρ</span></span> (well, pit, cistern) in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:11">John 4:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:12">John 4:12</span>. It is really a cistern 100 feet deep dug by a stranger apparently in a land of abundant springs (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+26:19">Genesis 26:19</span>).</p>
<p><b>Wearied </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεκοπιακως</span></span>). Perfect active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοπιαω</span></span>, a state of weariness. The verb means to toil excessively (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:5">Luke 5:5</span>). John emphasizes the human emotions of Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:14">John 1:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:3">John 11:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:33">John 11:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:35">John 11:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:38">John 11:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:41">John 11:41</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:27">John 12:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:21">John 13:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:28">John 19:28</span>).</p>
<p><b>With his journey </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ της οδοιποριας</span></span>). As a result (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span>) of the journey. Old compound word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδοπορος</span></span> (wayfarer), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:26">2 Corinthians 11:26</span>.</p>
<p><b>Sat </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκαθεζετο</span></span>). Imperfect (descriptive) middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθεζομα</span></span>, "was sitting."</p>
<p><b>Thus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτως</span></span>). Probably "thus wearied," graphic picture.</p>
<p><b>By the well </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ τη πηγη</span></span>). Literally, "upon the curbstone of the well."</p>
<p><b>Sixth hour </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως εκτη</span></span>). Roman time, about 6 P.M., the usual time for drawing water. </p>
