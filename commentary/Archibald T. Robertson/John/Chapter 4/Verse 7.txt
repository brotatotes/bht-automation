<p><b> There cometh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχετα</span></span>). Vivid historical present as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:5">John 4:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>A woman of Samaria </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γυνη εκ της Σαμαριας</span></span>). The country, not the city which was two hours away.</p>
<p><b>To draw water </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντλησα υδωρ</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of purpose of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντλεω</span></span> for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:8">John 2:8</span>. Cf. Rebecca in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+24:11">Genesis 24:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+24:17">Genesis 24:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>Give me to drink </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δος μο πειν</span></span>). Second aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span> and second aorist active infinitive (object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δος</span></span>) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πινω</span></span>, shortened form of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιειν</span></span>. A polite request. </p>
