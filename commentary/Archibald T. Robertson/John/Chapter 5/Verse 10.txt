<p><b> Unto him that was cured </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω τεθεραπευμενω</span></span>). Perfect passive articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεραπευω</span></span> (only example in John), "to the healed man." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:7">Matthew 8:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>To take up thy bed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρα τον κραβαττον</span></span>). The very words of Jesus (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:8">John 5:8</span>), only infinitive (first aorist active). Carrying burdens was considered unlawful on the Sabbath (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+23:12">Exodus 23:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ne+13:19">Nehemiah 13:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+17:21">Jeremiah 17:21</span>). Stoning was the rabbinical punishment. The healing of the man was a minor detail. </p>
