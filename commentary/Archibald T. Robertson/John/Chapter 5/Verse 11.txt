<p><b> But he answered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος δε απεκριθη</span></span>). Demonstrative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος</span></span> (But this one) and deponent use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεκριθη</span></span> (first aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκρινομα</span></span> with no passive force).</p>
<p><b>The same </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινος</span></span>). "That one," emphatic demonstrative as often in John (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:18">John 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:33">John 1:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:37">John 9:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:1">John 10:1</span>, etc.). The man did not know who Jesus was nor even his name. He quotes the very words of Jesus.</p>
<p><b>Whole </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υγιη</span></span>). Predicate accusative agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">με</span></span> (me). </p>
