<p><b> Hath eternal life </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχε ζωην αιωνιον</span></span>). Has now this spiritual life which is endless. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:36">John 3:36</span>. In verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:24">John 5:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:25">John 5:25</span> Jesus speaks of spiritual life and spiritual death. In this passage (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:21-29">John 5:21-29</span>) Jesus speaks now of physical life and death, now of spiritual, and one must notice carefully the quick transition. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:14">Revelation 20:14</span> we have the phrase "the second death" with which language compare <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:4-6">Revelation 20:4-6</span>.</p>
<p><b>But hath passed out of death into life </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα μεταβεβηκεν εκ του θανατου εις την ζωην</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταβαινω</span></span>, to pass from one place or state to another. Out of spiritual death into spiritual life and so no judgement (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρισις</span></span>). </p>
