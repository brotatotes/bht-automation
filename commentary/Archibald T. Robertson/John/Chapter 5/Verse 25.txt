<p><b> And now is </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα νυν εστιν</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:23">John 4:23</span> for this phrase. Not the future resurrection in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:28">John 5:28</span>, but the spiritual resurrection here and now.</p>
<p><b>The dead </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο νεκρο</span></span>). The spiritually dead, dead in trespasses and sins (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:1">Ephesians 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:5">Ephesians 2:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:14">Ephesians 5:14</span>).</p>
<p><b>Shall hear the voice of the Son of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουσουσιν της φωνης του υιου του θεου</span></span>). Note three genitives (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωνης</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουσουσιν</span></span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υιου</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωνης</span></span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεου</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υιου</span></span>). Note three articles (correlation of the article) and that Jesus here calls himself "the Son of God" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:36">John 10:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:4">John 11:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>Shall live </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζησουσιν</span></span>). Future active indicative, shall come to life spiritually. </p>
