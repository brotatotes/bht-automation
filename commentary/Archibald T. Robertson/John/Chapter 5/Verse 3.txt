<p><b> In these </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ταυταις</span></span>). In these five porches.</p>
<p><b>Lay </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεκειτο</span></span>). Imperfect middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατακειμα</span></span>, to lie down, singular number because <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληθος</span></span> (multitude) is a collective substantive.</p>
<p><b>Withered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξηρων</span></span>). Old adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξηρος</span></span> for dry, wasted as the hand (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:10">Matthew 12:10</span>). The oldest and best manuscripts omit what the Textus Receptus adds here "waiting for the moving of the water" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκδεχομενον την του υδατος κινησιν</span></span>), a Western and Syrian addition to throw light on the word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταραχθη</span></span> (is troubled) in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:7">John 5:7</span>. </p>
