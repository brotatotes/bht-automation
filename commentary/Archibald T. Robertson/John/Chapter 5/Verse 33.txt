<p><b> Ye have sent </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμεις απεσταλκατε</span></span>). Emphatic use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμεις</span></span> (ye) and perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστελλω</span></span>, official and permanent fact and so the witness of the Baptist has to be recognized as trustworthy by the Sanhedrin. The reference is to the committee in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:19-28">John 1:19-28</span>.</p>
<p><b>He hath borne witness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεμαρτυρηκεν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρεω</span></span> showing the permanent and abiding value of John's testimony to Christ as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:34">John 1:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:26">John 3:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:37">John 5:37</span>. So also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:35">John 19:35</span> of the testimony concerning Christ's death. This was the purpose of the Baptist's mission (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:7">John 1:7</span>). </p>
