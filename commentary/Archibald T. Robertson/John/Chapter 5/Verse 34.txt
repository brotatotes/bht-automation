<p><b> But the witness which I receive </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εγω δε ου την μαρτυριαν λαμβανω</span></span>). "But I do not receive the witness" simply from a man (like John). The <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγω</span></span> (I) in sharp contrast with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμεις</span></span> (ye) of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:33">John 5:33</span>. Jesus complained of Nicodemus for not accepting his witness (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:11">John 3:11</span>). Cf. also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:32">John 3:32</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:9">1 John 5:9</span> the witness of God is greater than that of men and this Jesus has.</p>
<p><b>That ye may be saved </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα υμεις σωθητε</span></span>). Final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωζω</span></span>. This was the purpose of Christ's coming, that the world might be saved (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:17">John 3:17</span>). </p>
