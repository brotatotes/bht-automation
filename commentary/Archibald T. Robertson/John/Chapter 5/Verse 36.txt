<p><b> But the witness which I have is greater than that of John </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εγω δε εχω την μαρτυριαν μειζω του Ιωανου</span></span>). Literally, "But I have the witness greater than John's." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μειζω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μειζονα</span></span>) is predicate accusative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιωανου</span></span> is ablative of comparison after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μειζω</span></span>. Good as the witness of John is, Christ has superior testimony.</p>
<p><b>To accomplish </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα τελειωσω</span></span>). Final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελειοω</span></span>, the same idiom in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:34">John 4:34</span>. Jesus felt keenly the task laid on him by the Father (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:35">John 3:35</span>) and claimed at the end that he had performed it (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:4">John 17:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:30">John 19:30</span>). Jesus held that the highest form of faith did not require these "works" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργα</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:23">John 2:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:38">John 10:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:11">John 14:11</span>. But these "works" bear the seal of the Father's approval (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:20">John 5:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:36">John 5:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:25">John 10:25</span>) and to reject their witness is wrong (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:25">John 10:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:37">John 10:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:24">John 15:24</span>).</p>
<p><b>The very works </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτα τα εργα</span></span>). "The works themselves," repeating <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα εργα</span></span> just before for vernacular emphasis.</p>
<p><b>Hath sent me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">με απεσταλκεν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστελλω</span></span>, the permanence of the mission. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:17">John 3:17</span>. The continuance of the witness is emphasized in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:32">John 5:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:18">John 8:18</span>. </p>
