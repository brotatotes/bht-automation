<p><b> And </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span>). "And yet" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:10">John 1:10</span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:40">John 5:40</span> below.</p>
<p><b>His word abiding in you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον λογον αυτου εν υμιν μενοντα</span></span>). But God's word had come to them through the centuries by the prophets. For the phrase see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:35">John 10:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:3">John 15:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:6">John 17:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:10">1 John 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:14">1 John 2:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>Him ye believe not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτω υμεις ου πιστευετε</span></span>). "This one" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτω</span></span>, dative case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστευετε</span></span>) in emphatic relation to preceding "he" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινος</span></span>, God). Jesus has given them God's word, but they reject both Jesus and God's word (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:9">John 14:9</span>). </p>
