<p><b> But I know you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα εγνωκα υμας</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span>, "I have come to know and still know," the knowledge of personal experience (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:24">John 2:24</span>).</p>
<p><b>The love o' God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την αγαπην του θεου</span></span>). Objective genitive, "the love toward God." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:42">Luke 11:42</span> for this phrase in the same sense (only other instance in the Gospels, but common in 1John (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:5">1 John 2:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:17">1 John 3:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:7">1 John 4:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:9">1 John 4:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:3">1 John 5:3</span>) and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:5">2 Thessalonians 3:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+13:14">2 Corinthians 13:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:5">Romans 5:5</span>. The sense of God's love for man occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:1">1 John 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:9">1 John 4:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:10">1 John 4:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:16">1 John 4:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:9">John 15:9</span> of Christ's love for man. These rabbis did not love God and hence did not love Christ. </p>
