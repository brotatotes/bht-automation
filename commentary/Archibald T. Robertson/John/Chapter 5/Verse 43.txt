<p><b> In my Father's name </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω ονοματ του πατρος μου</span></span>). Seven times Jesus in John speaks of the "Name" of the Father (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:43">John 5:43</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:25">John 10:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:28">John 12:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:6">John 17:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:11">John 17:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:12">John 17:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:26">John 17:26</span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:12">John 1:12</span> for use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ονομα</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:49">Luke 1:49</span>).</p>
<p><b>And ye receive me not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ου λαμβανετε με</span></span>). "And yet ye do not receive me," as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:40">John 5:40</span>, "the Gospel of the Rejection" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:11">John 1:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:11">John 3:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:32">John 3:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:37">John 12:37</span>) often applied to the Fourth Gospel.</p>
<p><b>If another come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν αλλος ελθη</span></span>). Condition of third class (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομα</span></span>). Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλος</span></span>, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετερος</span></span>, like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλον Ιησουν</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:4">2 Corinthians 11:4</span>. Similar prophecies occur in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:6">Mark 13:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:22">Mark 13:22</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:5">Matthew 24:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:24">Matthew 24:24</span>), all general in character like Antichrist in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:8-12">2 Thessalonians 2:8-12</span>. There is no occasion for a reference to any individual like Barcochba (about A.D. 134) as Pfleiderer and Schmiedel hold. These Messianic upstarts all come "in their own name" and always find a following.</p>
<p><b>Him ye will receive </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινον λημψεσθε</span></span>). "That one," whoever he is, as Jesus said. Future active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>. Credulous about the false Messiahs, incredulous about Christ. </p>
