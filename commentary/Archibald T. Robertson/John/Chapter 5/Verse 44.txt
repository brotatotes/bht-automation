<p><b> How can ye believe? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πως δυνασθε υμεις πιστευσαι;</span></span>). Emphasis on "ye" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμεις</span></span>), ye being what ye are. They were not true Jews (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:29">Romans 2:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="es+9:28">Esther 9:28</span>) who cared for the glory of God, but they prefer the praise of men (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:1">Matthew 6:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:5">Matthew 23:5</span>) like the Pharisees who feared to confess Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:43">John 12:43</span>).</p>
<p><b>From the only God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα του μονου θεου</span></span>). B and W omit <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεου</span></span> which is certainly meant even if not genuine here. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:3">John 17:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:27">Romans 16:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:15">1 Timothy 6:15</span>. </p>
