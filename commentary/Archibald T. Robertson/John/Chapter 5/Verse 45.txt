<p><b> Think not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη δοκειτε</span></span>). Prohibition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> and the present imperative. See on verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:39">John 5:39</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκεω</span></span> for mistaken opinions in John.</p>
<p><b>I will accuse you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγω κατηγορησω υμων</span></span>). Emphasis on <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγω</span></span> (I). Future active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηγορεω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span>, against, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγορευω</span></span>, to speak in the assembly <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγορα</span></span>, to bring an accusation in court, a public accusation). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:9">Romans 3:9</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προαιτιαομα</span></span> for making previous charge and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:1">Luke 16:1</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαβαλλω</span></span>, a secret malicious accusation, and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:33">Romans 8:33</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγκαλεω</span></span>, for public charge, not necessarily before tribunal.</p>
<p><b>Even Moses </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μωυσης</span></span>). No "even" in the Greek.</p>
<p><b>On whom ye have set your hope </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις ον υμεις ηλπικατε</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελπιζω</span></span>, state of repose in Moses. Only example of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελπιζω</span></span> in John. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:10">2 Corinthians 1:10</span> for use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελπιζω</span></span> instead of the usual <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:10">1 Timothy 4:10</span>). </p>
