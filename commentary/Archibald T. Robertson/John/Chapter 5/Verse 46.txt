<p><b> Ye would believe me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιστευετε αν εμο</span></span>). Conclusion of condition of second class (determined as unfulfilled) with imperfect indicative in both protasis and apodosis and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span> in apodosis. This was a home-thrust, proving that they did not really believe Moses.</p>
<p><b>For he wrote of me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ γαρ εμου εκεινος εγραψεν</span></span>). <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+18:18">Deuteronomy 18:18</span> is quoted by Peter (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:22">Acts 3:22</span>) as a prophecy of Christ and also by Stephen in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:37">Acts 7:37</span>. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:14">John 3:14</span> about the brazen serpent and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:56">John 8:56</span> about Abraham foreseeing Christ's day. Jesus does here say that Moses wrote concerning him. </p>
