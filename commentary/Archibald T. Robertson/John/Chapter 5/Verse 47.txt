<p><b> His writings </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις εκεινου γραμμασιν</span></span>). Dative case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστυετε</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:31">Luke 16:31</span> for a like argument. The authority of Moses was the greatest of all for Jews. There is a contrast also between</p>
<p><b>writings </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γραμμασιν</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γραφω</span></span>, to write) and</p>
<p><b>words </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρημασιν</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειπον</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Γραμμα</span></span> may mean the mere letter as opposed to spirit (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:6">2 Corinthians 3:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:27">Romans 2:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:29">Romans 2:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:6">Romans 7:6</span>), a debtor's bond (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:6">Luke 16:6</span>), letters or learning (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:15">John 7:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:24">Acts 26:24</span>) like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγραμματο</span></span> for unlearned (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:13">Acts 4:13</span>), merely written characters (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:38">Luke 23:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:7">2 Corinthians 3:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:11">Galatians 6:11</span>), official communications (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:21">Acts 28:21</span>), once <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιερα γραμματα</span></span> for the sacred writings (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:15">2 Timothy 3:15</span>) instead of the more usual <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α αγια γραφα</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Γραφη</span></span> is used also for a single passage (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:10">Mark 12:10</span>), but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βιβλιον</span></span> for a book or roll (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:17">Luke 4:17</span>) or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βιβλος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:42">Luke 20:42</span>). Jesus clearly states the fact that Moses wrote portions of the Old Testament, what portions he does not say. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:27">Luke 24:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:44">Luke 24:44</span> for the same idea. There was no answer from the rabbis to this conclusion of Christ. The scribes (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο γραμματεις</span></span>) made copies according to the letter (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα το γραμμα</span></span>). </p>
