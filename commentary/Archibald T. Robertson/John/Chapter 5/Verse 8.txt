<p><b> Arise, take up thy bed, and walk </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εγειρε, αρον τον κραβαττον σου κα περιπατε</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγειρω</span></span>, a sort of exclamation, like our "Get up." The first active imperative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρον</span></span> of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιρω</span></span>) means to pick up the pallet, and then "go on walking" (present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιπατεω</span></span>). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κραβαττον</span></span> (pallet) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:2-12">Mark 2:2-12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:55">Mark 6:55</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:15">Acts 5:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:33">Acts 9:33</span>. </p>
