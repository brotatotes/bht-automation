<p><b> Sit down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπεσειν</span></span>). Literally, "fall back," lie down, recline. Second aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπιπτω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Much grass </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χορτος πολυς</span></span>). Old word for pasture, green grass (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:39">Mark 6:39</span>) or hay (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:12">1 Corinthians 3:12</span>). It was spring (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:4">John 6:4</span>) and plenty of green grass on the hillside.</p>
<p><b>The men </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ανδρες</span></span>). Word for men as distinct from women, expressly stated in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:21">Matthew 14:21</span>.</p>
<p><b>In number </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον αριθμον</span></span>). Adverbial accusative (of general reference).</p>
<p><b>About </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος</span></span>). General estimate, though they were arranged in orderly groups by hundreds and fifties, "in ranks" like "garden beds" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρασια</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:40">Mark 6:40</span>). </p>
