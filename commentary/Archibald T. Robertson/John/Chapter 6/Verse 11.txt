<p><b> The loaves </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους αρτους</span></span>). Those of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:9">John 6:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>Having given thanks </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευχαριστησας</span></span>). The usual grace before meals (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+8:10">Deuteronomy 8:10</span>). The Synoptics use "blessed" <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλογησεν</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:41">Mark 6:41</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:19">Matthew 14:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:16">Luke 9:16</span>).</p>
<p><b>He distributed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διεδωκεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαδιδωμ</span></span>, old verb to give to several (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span>, between).</p>
<p><b>To them that were set down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις ανακειμενοις</span></span>). Present middle participle (dative case) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανακειμα</span></span>, old verb to recline like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπεσειν</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:10">John 6:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>As much as they would </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οσον ηθελον</span></span>). Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θελω</span></span>, "as much as they wished." </p>
