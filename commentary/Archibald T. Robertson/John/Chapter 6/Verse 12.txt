<p><b> And when they were filled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως δε ενεπλησθησαν</span></span>). First aorist (effective) passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμπιμπλημ</span></span>, old verb to fill in, to fill up, to fill completely. They were all satisfied. The Synoptics have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχορτασθησαν</span></span> like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:26">John 6:26</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχορτασθητε</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Gather up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συναγαγετε</span></span>). Second aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συναγω</span></span>, to gather together.</p>
<p><b>Broken pieces </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλασματα</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλαω</span></span>, to break. Not crumbs or scraps on the ground, but pieces broken by Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:41">Mark 6:41</span>) and not consumed.</p>
<p><b>Be lost </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολητα</span></span>). Second aorist middle subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολλυμ</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> in purpose clause. Only in John. There was to be no wastefulness in Christ's munificence. The Jews had a custom of leaving something for those that served. </p>
