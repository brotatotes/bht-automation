<p><b> Twelve baskets </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δωδεκα κοφινους</span></span>). One for each of the apostles. What about the lad? Stout wicker baskets (coffins, Wycliff) in distinction from the soft and frail <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σφυριδες</span></span> used at the feeding of the four thousand (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:8">Mark 8:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:37">Matthew 15:37</span>). Here all the Gospels (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:43">Mark 6:43</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:20">Matthew 14:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:17">Luke 9:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:13">John 6:13</span>) use <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοφινο</span></span>. The same distinction between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοφινο</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σφυριδες</span></span> is preserved in the allusion to the incidents by Jesus in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:19">Mark 8:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:20">Mark 8:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:9">Matthew 16:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:10">Matthew 16:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Unto them that had eaten </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις βεβρωκοσιν</span></span>). Articular perfect active participle (dative case) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βιβρωσκω</span></span>, old verb to eat, only here in N.T., though often in LXX. </p>
