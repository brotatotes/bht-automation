<p><b> Perceiving </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνους</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span>. It was not hard for Christ to read the mind of this excited mob.</p>
<p><b>They were about </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μελλουσιν</span></span>). Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μελλω</span></span>. Probably the leaders were already starting.</p>
<p><b>Take him by force </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρπαζειν</span></span>). Present active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρπαζω</span></span>, old verb for violent seizing (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:12">Matthew 11:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:19">Matthew 13:19</span>). There was a movement to start a revolution against Roman rule in Palestine by proclaiming Jesus King and driving away Pilate.</p>
<p><b>To make him king </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα ποιησωσιν βασιλεα</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασιλεα</span></span> as predicate accusative. It was a crisis that called for quick action.</p>
<p><b>Himself alone </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτος μονος</span></span>). At first he had the disciples with him (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:3">John 6:3</span>). But he sent them hurriedly by boat to the western side (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:45">Mark 6:45</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:22">Matthew 14:22</span>) because clearly the apostles were sympathetic with the revolutionary impulse of the crowd. Then Jesus sent the multitudes away also and went up into the mountain alone. He was alone in every sense, for no one but the Father understood him at this stage, not even his own disciples. He went up to pray (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:46">Mark 6:46</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:23">Matthew 14:23</span>). </p>
