<p><b> When therefore they had rowed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εληλακοτες ουν</span></span>). Perfect active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελαυνω</span></span>, old verb to march (Xenophon), to drive (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:4">James 3:4</span>), to row (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:48">Mark 6:48</span>).</p>
<p><b>Furlongs </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σταδιους</span></span>). Stadia, accusative of extent of space, a little over halfway across, "in the midst of the sea" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:47">Mark 6:47</span>). It was about forty stadia (six miles) across.</p>
<p><b>They behold </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεωρουσιν</span></span>). Graphic dramatic present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεωρεω</span></span>, vividly preserving the emotions of the disciples.</p>
<p><b>Walking </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιπατουντα</span></span>). Present active participle in the accusative case agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιησουν</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Drawing nigh unto the boat </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγγυς του πλοιου γινομενον</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span> describing the process. "Coming near the boat." They behold Jesus slipping closer and closer to them on the water.</p>
<p><b>They were afraid </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφοβηθησαν</span></span>). Ingressive aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φοβεομα</span></span>, "they became afraid." Sudden change to the regular historical sequence. </p>
