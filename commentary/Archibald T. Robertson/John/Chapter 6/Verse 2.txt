<p><b> Followed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκολουθε</span></span>). Descriptive imperfect active, picturing the crowd, but without the details of the boat for Christ and the rapid race of the crowd on foot (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:32">Mark 6:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:13">Matthew 14:13</span>).</p>
<p><b>They beheld </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εθεωρουν</span></span>). Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεωρεω</span></span>. They had been beholding the signs which Jesus had been doing (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εποιε</span></span>, imperfect again) for a long time (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:23">John 2:23</span>), most of which John has not given (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:29">Mark 1:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:1">Mark 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:1">Mark 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:5">Mark 6:5</span>). The people were eager to hear Jesus again (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:11">Luke 9:11</span>) and to get the benefit of his healing power "on them that were sick" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ των ασθενουντων</span></span>, the weak or feeble, without strength, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σθενος</span></span>, strength). </p>
