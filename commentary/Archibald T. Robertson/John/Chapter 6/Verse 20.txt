<p><b> Be not afraid </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη φοβεισθε</span></span>). Prohibition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> and present middle imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φοβεομα</span></span>. So in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:50">Mark 6:50</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:27">Matthew 14:27</span>). John does not tell that the disciples thought Jesus was an apparition (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:49">Mark 6:49</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:26">Matthew 14:26</span>), nor does he give the account of Peter walking on the water (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:28-31">Matthew 14:28-31</span>). </p>
