<p><b> They were willing therefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηθελον ουν</span></span>). Inchoative imperfect, "they began to be willing." This does not contradict <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:51">Mark 6:51</span> as Bernard thinks. Both Jesus and Peter climbed into the boat.</p>
<p><b>Whither they were going </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις ην υπηγον</span></span>). Progressive imperfect active, "to which land they had been going" (intransitive use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπαγω</span></span>, to lead under, to go under or away as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:67">John 6:67</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:33">John 7:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:11">John 12:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:8">John 18:8</span>. </p>
