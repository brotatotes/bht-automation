<p><b> Which stood </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο εστηκως</span></span>). Perfect active (intransitive) participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span>, to put, to stand. Jesus had sent the multitudes away the evening before (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:45">Mark 6:45</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:22">Matthew 14:22</span>), but evidently some did not go very far, still lingering in excitement on the eastern side of the lake next morning.</p>
<p><b>Boat </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλοιαριον</span></span>). Diminutive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλοιον</span></span>, little boat (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:9">Mark 3:9</span>).</p>
<p><b>Entered not with </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου συνεισηλθεν</span></span>). Second aorist active of the double compound verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεισερχομα</span></span>, followed by associative instrumental case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαθηταις</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Went away alone </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μονο απηλθον</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απερχομα</span></span>, to go away or off. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μονο</span></span> is predicate nominative. These people noted these three items. </p>
