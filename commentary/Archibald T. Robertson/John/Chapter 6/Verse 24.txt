<p><b> When the multitude therefore saw </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτε ουν ειδεν ο οχλος</span></span>). Resumption and clarification of the complicated statements of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:22">John 6:22</span>.</p>
<p><b>That Jesus was not there </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ Ιησους ουκ εστιν εκε</span></span>). Present indicative retained in indirect discourse. They still did not understand how Jesus had crossed over, but they acted on the basis of the plain fact.</p>
<p><b>They themselves got into </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεβησαν αυτο εις</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμβαινω</span></span> followed by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> (both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> together as often in N.T.).</p>
<p><b>Seeking Jesus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζητουντες τον Ιησουν</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζητεω</span></span>. They had a double motive apart from the curiosity explained in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:22">John 6:22</span>. They had clearly not given up the impulse of the evening before to make Jesus king (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:15">John 6:15</span>) and they had hopes of still another bountiful repast at the hands of Jesus as he said (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:26">John 6:26</span>). </p>
