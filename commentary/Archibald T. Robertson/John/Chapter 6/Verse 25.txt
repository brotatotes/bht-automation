<p><b> When they found him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευροντες αυτον</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρισκω</span></span>. Found him after search and in the synagogue as John explains (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:59">John 6:59</span>) in Capernaum, perhaps that very synagogue built by a centurion (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:5">Luke 7:5</span>).</p>
<p><b>Rabbi </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ραββε</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:38">John 1:38</span> for this courteous title.</p>
<p><b>When camest thou hither? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποτε ωδε γεγονασ;</span></span>). Second perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span>. "When hast thou come?" We sought you anxiously on the other side of the lake and could not see how you came across (verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:22-24">John 6:22-24</span>). </p>
