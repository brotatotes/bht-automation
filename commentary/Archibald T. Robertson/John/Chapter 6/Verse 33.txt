<p><b> The bread of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αρτος του θεου</span></span>). All bread is of God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:11">Matthew 6:11</span>). The manna came down from heaven (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+11:9">Numbers 11:9</span>) as does this bread (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο καταβαινων</span></span>). Refers to the bread (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αρτος</span></span>, masculine). Bernard notes that this phrase (coming down) is used seven times in this discourse (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:33">John 6:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:38">John 6:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:41">John 6:41</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:42">John 6:42</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:50">John 6:50</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:51">John 6:51</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:58">John 6:58</span>).</p>
<p><b>Giveth life </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωην διδους</span></span>). Chrysostom observes that the manna gave nourishment (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τροφη</span></span>), but not life (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωη</span></span>). This is a most astounding statement to the crowd. </p>
