<p><b> All that </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παν ο</span></span>). Collective use of the neuter singular, classic idiom, seen also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:39">John 6:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:2">John 17:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:24">John 17:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:4">1 John 5:4</span>. Perhaps the notion of unity like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:21">John 17:21</span> underlies this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παν ο</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Giveth me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωσιν μο</span></span>). For the idea that the disciples are given to the Son see also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:39">John 6:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:65">John 6:65</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:29">John 10:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:2">John 17:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:6">John 17:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:9">John 17:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:12">John 17:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:24">John 17:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:9">John 18:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>I will in no wise cast out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη εκβαλω εξω</span></span>). Strong double negation as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:35">John 6:35</span> with second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαλλω</span></span>. Definite promise of Jesus to welcome the one who comes. </p>
