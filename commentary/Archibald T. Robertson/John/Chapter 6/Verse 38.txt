<p><b> I am come down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταβεβηκα</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταβαινω</span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:33">John 6:33</span> for frequent use of this phrase by Jesus. Here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span> is correct rather than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του ουρανου</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Not to do </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουχ ινα ποιω</span></span>). "Not that I keep on doing" (final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>But the will </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα το θελημα</span></span>). Supply <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα ποιω</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα</span></span>, "but that I keep on doing." This is the fulness of joy for Jesus, to do his Father's will (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:34">John 4:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:30">John 5:30</span>). </p>
