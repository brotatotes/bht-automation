<p><b> Except the Father draw him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν μη ελκυση αυτον</span></span>). Negative condition of third class with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν μη</span></span> and first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελκυω</span></span>, older form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελκω</span></span>, to drag like a net (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:6">John 21:6</span>), or sword (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:10">John 18:10</span>), or men (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:19">Acts 16:19</span>), to draw by moral power (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:32">John 12:32</span>), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+31:3">Jeremiah 31:3</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Συρω</span></span>, the other word to drag (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:3">Acts 8:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:19">Acts 14:19</span>) is not used of Christ's drawing power. The same point is repeated in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:65">John 6:65</span>. The approach of the soul to God is initiated by God, the other side of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:37">John 6:37</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:7">Romans 8:7</span> for the same doctrine and use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδε δυνατα</span></span> like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδεις δυνατα</span></span> here. </p>
