<p><b> Lifting up his eyes </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαρας τους οφθαλμους</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαιρω</span></span>. See the same phrase in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:35">John 4:35</span> where it is also followed by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεαομα</span></span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:41">John 11:41</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:1">John 17:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:20">Luke 6:20</span>. Here it is particularly expressive as Jesus looked down from the mountain on the approaching multitude.</p>
<p><b>Cometh unto him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχετα προς αυτον</span></span>). Present middle indicative, "is coming to him." The same <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οχλος πολυς</span></span> (here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολυς οχλος</span></span>) of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:2">John 6:2</span> that had followed Jesus around the head of the lake.</p>
<p><b>Whence are we to buy? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ποθεν αγορασωμεν;</span></span>). Deliberative subjunctive (aorist active). John passes by the earlier teaching and healing of the Synoptics (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:34">Mark 6:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:14">Matthew 14:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:11">Luke 9:11</span>) till mid-afternoon. In John also Jesus takes up the matter of feeding the multitude with Philip (from the other Bethsaida, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:44">John 1:44</span>) whereas in the Synoptics the disciples raise the problem with Jesus. So the disciples raise the problem in the feeding of the four thousand (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:4">Mark 8:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:33">Matthew 15:33</span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+11:13-22">Numbers 11:13-22</span> (about Moses) and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ki+4:42">2 Kings 4:42</span>. (about Elisha).</p>
<p><b>Bread </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρτους</span></span>). "Loaves" (plural) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:3">Matthew 4:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>That these may eat </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα φαγωσιν ουτο</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσθιω</span></span> (defective verb). </p>
