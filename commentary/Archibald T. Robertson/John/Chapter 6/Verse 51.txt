<p><b> The living bread </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αρτος ο ζων</span></span>). "The bread the living." Repetition of the claim in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:35">John 6:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:41">John 6:41</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:48">John 6:48</span>, but with a slight change from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωης</span></span> to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζων</span></span> (present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζαω</span></span>). It is alive and can give life. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:10">John 4:10</span> for living water. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:17">Revelation 1:17</span> Jesus calls himself the Living One (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ζων</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>For ever </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τον αιωνα</span></span>). Eternally like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιωνιον</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωην</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:47">John 6:47</span>.</p>
<p><b>I shall give </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγω δωσω</span></span>). Emphasis on <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγω</span></span> (I). Superior so to Moses.</p>
<p><b>Is my flesh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η σαρξ μου εστιν</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:14">John 1:14</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαρξ</span></span> the Incarnation. This new idea creates far more difficulty to the hearers who cannot grasp Christ's idea of self-sacrifice.</p>
<p><b>For the life of the world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ της του κοσμου ζωης</span></span>). Over, in behalf of, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ</span></span> means, and in some connexions instead of as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:50">John 11:50</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:30">John 1:30</span> for the Baptist's picture of Christ as the Lamb of God that taketh away the sin of the world. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:17">John 3:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:42">John 4:42</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:16">1 John 3:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:28">Matthew 20:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:13">Galatians 3:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:14">2 Corinthians 5:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:8">Romans 5:8</span>. Jesus has here presented to this Galilean multitude the central fact of his atoning death for the spiritual life of the world. </p>
