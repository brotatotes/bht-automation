<p><b> Abideth in me and I in him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν εμο μενε καγω εν αυτω</span></span>). Added to the phrase in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:54">John 6:54</span> in the place of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχε ζωην αιωνιον</span></span> (has eternal life). The verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενω</span></span> (to abide) expresses continual mystical fellowship between Christ and the believer as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:4-7">John 15:4-7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:6">1 John 2:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:27">1 John 2:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:28">1 John 2:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:6">1 John 3:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:24">1 John 3:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:12">1 John 4:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:16">1 John 4:16</span>. There is, of course, no reference to the Lord's Supper (Eucharist), but simply to mystical fellowship with Christ. </p>
