<p><b> Knowing in himself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδως εν εαυτω</span></span>). Second perfect active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιδα</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:25">John 2:25</span> for this supernatural insight into men's minds.</p>
<p><b>Murmured </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γογγυζουσιν</span></span>). Present active indicative retained in indirect discourse. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:41">John 6:41</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γογγυζω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>At this </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ τουτου</span></span>). "Concerning this word."</p>
<p><b>Cause to stumble </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκανδαλιζε</span></span>). Common Synoptic verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκανδαλον</span></span> for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:29">Matthew 5:29</span>. In John again only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:1">John 16:1</span>. </p>
