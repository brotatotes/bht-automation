<p><b> What then if ye should behold </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν ουν θεωρητε</span></span>). No "what" in the Greek. Condition of third class with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and present active subjunctive, "if ye then behold."</p>
<p><b>Ascending </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναβαινοντα</span></span>). Present active participle picturing the process.</p>
<p><b>Where he was before </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπου ην το προτερον</span></span>). Neuter articular adjective as adverb (accusative of general reference, at the former time as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:8">John 9:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:13">Galatians 3:13</span>). Clear statement of Christ's pre-existence in his own words as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:13">John 3:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:5">John 17:5</span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:1-18">John 1:1-18</span>). </p>
