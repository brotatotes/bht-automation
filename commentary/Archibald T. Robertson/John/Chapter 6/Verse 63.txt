<p><b> That quickeneth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το ζωοποιουν</span></span>). Articular present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωοποιεω</span></span> for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:21">John 5:21</span>. For the contrast between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα</span></span> (spirit) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαρξ</span></span> (flesh) see already <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:6">John 3:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>The words </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα ρηματα</span></span>). Those in this discourse (I have just spoken, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λελαληκα</span></span>), for they are the words of God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:34">John 3:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:47">John 8:47</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:8">John 17:8</span>). No wonder they "are spirit and are life" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα εστιν κα ζωη εστιν</span></span>). The breath of God and the life of God is in these words of Jesus. Never man spoke like Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:46">John 7:46</span>). There is life in his words today. </p>
