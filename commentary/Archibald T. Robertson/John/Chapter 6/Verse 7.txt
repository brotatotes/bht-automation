<p><b> Two hundred pennyworth of bread </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακοσιων δηναριων αρτο</span></span>). "Loaves of two hundred denarii." The Roman coin originally for ten asses (afterwards sixteen), about 16 2/3 cents. The denarius was the usual pay for a day's labour (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:2">Matthew 20:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:9">Matthew 20:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:13">Matthew 20:13</span>). This item in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:37">Mark 6:37</span>, but not in Matthew or Luke.</p>
<p><b>That every one may take a little </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα εκαστος βραχυ λαβη</span></span>). Final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>. This detail in John alone. </p>
