<p><b> A lad here </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παιδαριον ωδε</span></span>). Old word, diminutive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παις</span></span>, here only in N.T., not genuine in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:16">Matthew 11:16</span>. How he came to have this small supply we do not know.</p>
<p><b>Barley </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κριθινους</span></span>). Adjective, here and verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:13">John 6:13</span> only in N.T., in the papyri, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κριθη</span></span>, barley (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:6">Revelation 6:6</span>). Considered an inferior sort of bread.</p>
<p><b>Fishes </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οψαρια</span></span>). Late diminutive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οψον</span></span>, common in papyri and inscriptions for delicacies with bread like fish. In N.T. only here, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:11">John 6:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:9-13">John 21:9-13</span>. Synoptics have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιχθυας</span></span>. </p>
