<p><b> Much murmuring </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γογγυσμος πολυς</span></span>). This Ionic onomatopoetic word is from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γογγυζω</span></span> for which verb see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:41">John 6:41</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:61">John 6:61</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:32">John 7:32</span>, for secret displeasure (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:1">Acts 6:1</span>) or querulous discontent (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:14">Philippians 2:14</span>).</p>
<p><b>Among the multitudes </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τοις οχλοις</span></span>). "The multitudes" literally, plural here only in John. These different groups were visitors from Galilee and elsewhere and were divided in their opinion of Jesus as the Galileans had already become (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:66">John 6:66</span>).</p>
<p><b>A good man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαθος</span></span>). Pure in motive. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:17">Mark 10:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:7">Romans 5:7</span> (absolute sense of God). Superior to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιος</span></span>. Jesus had champions in these scattered groups in the temple courts.</p>
<p><b>Not so, but he leadeth the multitude astray </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου, αλλα πλανα τον οχλον</span></span>). Sharp clash in the crowd. Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλαναω</span></span>, to go astray (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:12">Matthew 18:12</span>), like our "planets," to lead others astray (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:4">Matthew 24:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:5">Matthew 24:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:11">Matthew 24:11</span>, etc.). In the end the rulers will call Jesus "that deceiver" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινος ο πλανος</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:63">Matthew 27:63</span>). The Jewish leaders have a following among the crowds as is seen (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:31">John 7:31</span>). </p>
