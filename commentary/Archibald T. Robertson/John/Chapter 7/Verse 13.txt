<p><b> Howbeit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεντο</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:27">John 4:27</span> for this compound particle (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεν, το</span></span>), by way of exception, but yet.</p>
<p><b>Spake </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελαλε</span></span>). Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαλεω</span></span>, "was speaking," picturing the whispering or secret talk (<b> no man openly </b>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδεις παρρησια</span></span>). Best MSS. do not have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> here with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρρησια</span></span> (locative or instrumental case of manner) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:26">John 7:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:24">John 10:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:54">John 11:54</span>, but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> genuine in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:4">John 7:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:15">Colossians 2:15</span>. This adverbial use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρρησια</span></span> is common enough (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:37">Mark 8:37</span>).</p>
<p><b>For fear of the Jews </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια τον φοβον των Ιουδαιων</span></span>). Objective genitive. The crowds really feared the Jewish leaders and evidently did not wish to involve Jesus or themselves. See the same phrase and attitude on the part of the disciples in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:38">John 19:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:19">John 20:19</span>. </p>
