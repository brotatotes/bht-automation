<p><b> The feast of tabernacles </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η σκηνοπηγια</span></span>). Only New Testament example of this word (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκηνη</span></span>, tent, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πηγνυμ</span></span>, to fasten as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:2">Hebrews 8:2</span>). Technical name of this feast (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+16:13">Deuteronomy 16:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="le+23:34">Leviticus 23:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="le+23:43">Leviticus 23:43</span>). It began on the 15th of the month Tisri (end of September) and lasted seven days and finally eight days in post-exilic times (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ne+8:18">Nehemiah 8:18</span>). It was one of the chief feasts of the Jews. </p>
