<p><b> That the law of Moses may not be broken </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη λυθη ο νομος Μωυσεως</span></span>). Purpose clause with negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> and first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυω</span></span>. They are punctilious about their Sabbath rules and about circumcision on the eighth day. When they clash, they drop the Sabbath rule and circumcise.</p>
<p><b>Are ye wroth with me? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμο χολατε;</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χολη</span></span> (bile, gall), possibly from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χλοη</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χλωρος</span></span> (yellowish green). Only here in N.T. So to be mad. With dative. Vivid picture of bitter spleen against Jesus for healing a man on the sabbath when they circumcise on the Sabbath.</p>
<p><b>A man every whit whole </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ολον ανθρωπον υγιη</span></span>). Literally, "a whole (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ολον</span></span>) man (all the man) sound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υγιη</span></span>, well)," not just one member of the body mended. </p>
