<p><b> The Pharisees </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο Φαρισαιο</span></span>). This group of the Jewish rulers (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:11">John 7:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:15">John 7:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:25">John 7:25</span>) was particularly hostile to Christ, though already the Sadducees had become critical (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:6">Matthew 16:6</span>) and they join here (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αρχιερεις</span></span>, the chief priests being Sadducees) in determining to silence Jesus by bringing him before the Sanhedrin. They had heard the whispered talk about Jesus before he arrived (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:12">John 7:12</span>) and still more now.</p>
<p><b>Heard the multitude murmuring </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκουσαν του οχλου γογγυζοντος</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουω</span></span> with the genitive case and the descriptive participle of the vivid onomatopoetic verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γογγυζω</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:12">John 7:12</span>) now grown louder like the hum of bees. It was the defence of Jesus by a portion of the crowd (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:31">John 7:31</span>) that irritated the Pharisees. Here the Pharisees take the initiative and enlist the Sadducees in the Sanhedrin (for this combination see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:45">John 7:45</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:47">John 11:47</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:57">John 11:57</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:45">Matthew 21:45</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:62">Matthew 27:62</span>, the organized court) to send "officers" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπηρετας</span></span>) "to take him" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα πιασωσιν αυτον</span></span>, final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιαζω</span></span> for which verb see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:30">John 7:30</span>). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπηρετας</span></span> (temple police here) see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:45">John 7:45</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:3">John 18:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:12">John 18:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:22">John 18:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:6">John 19:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:22">Acts 5:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:26">Acts 5:26</span>. For the word see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:25">Matthew 5:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:2">Luke 1:2</span>, "an under rower" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο, ερετης</span></span>), any assistant. </p>
