<p><b> Among themselves </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς εαυτους</span></span>). These Jewish leaders of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:32">John 7:32</span> talk among themselves about what Jesus said in a spirit of contempt (this man or fellow, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτος</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>That </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span>). Almost result like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:27">Matthew 8:27</span>.</p>
<p><b>Will he go? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη μελλε πορευεσθαι;</span></span>). Negative answer expected in an ironical question, "Is he about to go?"</p>
<p><b>Unto the Dispersion among the Greeks </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις την διασποραν των Hελληνων</span></span>). Objective genitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των Hελληνων</span></span> (of the Greeks) translated here "among," because it is the Dispersion of Jews among the Greeks. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Διασπορα</span></span> is from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διασπειρω</span></span>, to scatter apart (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:1">Acts 8:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:4">Acts 8:4</span>). It occurs in Plutarch and is common in the LXX, in the N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:1">James 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:1">1 Peter 1:1</span>. There were millions of these scattered Jews.</p>
<p><b>And teach the Greeks </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα διδασκειν τους Hελληνας</span></span>). Confessing his failure to teach the Jews in Palestine, "thus ignorantly anticipating the course Christianity took; what seemed unlikely and impossible to them became actual" (Dods). </p>
