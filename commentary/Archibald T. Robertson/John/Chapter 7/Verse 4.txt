<p><b> In secret </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν κρυπτω</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:4">Matthew 6:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:6">Matthew 6:6</span> for this phrase.</p>
<p><b>Openly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν παρρησια</span></span>). "In public" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παν, ρησις</span></span>, telling it all). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:32">Matthew 8:32</span>. Common in John (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:13">John 7:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:26">John 7:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:24">John 10:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:25">John 16:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:29">John 16:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:20">John 18:20</span>; here again contrasted with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν κρυπτω</span></span>). It is wise advice in the abstract that a public teacher must allow inspection of his deeds, but the motive is evil. They might get Jesus into trouble. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιφ θου δοεστ θεσε θινγς</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε ταυτα ποιεις</span></span>). This condition of the first class assumes the reality of the deeds of Jesus, but the use of the condition at all throws doubt on it all as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:3">Matthew 4:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:6">Matthew 4:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Manifest thyself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανερωσον σεαυτον</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανεροω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>To the world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω κοσμω</span></span>). Not just to "thy disciples," but to the public at large as at the feast of tabernacles. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:26">John 8:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:22">John 14:22</span> for this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοσμος</span></span>. </p>
