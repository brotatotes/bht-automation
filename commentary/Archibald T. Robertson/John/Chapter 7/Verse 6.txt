<p><b> My time is not yet come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο καιρος ο εμος ουπω παρεστιν</span></span>). Only use with verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:8">John 7:8</span> of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιρος</span></span> in this Gospel, elsewhere <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρονος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:6">John 5:6</span>) or more often <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωρα</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:4">John 2:4</span>) "the predestined hour" (Bernard). Here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιρος</span></span> is the fitting or proper occasion for Christ's manifesting himself publicly to the authorities as Messiah as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:8">John 7:8</span>. At the feast of tabernacles Jesus did make such public claims (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:29">John 7:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:33">John 7:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:12">John 8:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:28">John 8:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:38">John 8:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:42">John 8:42</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:58">John 8:58</span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Παρεστιν</span></span> is present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρειμ</span></span>, old compound, to be by, to be present. The brothers of Jesus had the regular Jewish obligation to go up to the feast, but the precise day was a matter of indifference to them. </p>
