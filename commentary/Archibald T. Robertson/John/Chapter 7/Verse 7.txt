<p><b> Cannot hate </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου δυνατα μισειν</span></span>). Because of "the law of moral correspondence" (Westcott), often in John for "inherent impossibility" (Vincent). The brothers of Jesus here belong to the unbelieving world (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοσμος</span></span>) which is unable to love Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:18">John 15:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:23">John 15:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:24">John 15:24</span>) and which Jesus had already exposed ("testify," <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρω</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:42">John 5:42</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:45">John 5:45</span>). This unbelieving "world" resented the exposure (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:19">John 3:19</span>, cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:37">John 18:37</span>). </p>
