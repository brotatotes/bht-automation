<p><b> For except ye believe </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν γαρ μη πιστευσητε</span></span>). Negative condition of third class with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν μη</span></span> and ingressive aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστευω</span></span>, "For unless ye come to believe."</p>
<p><b>That I am he </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ εγω ειμ</span></span>). Indirect discourse, but with no word in the predicate after the copula <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειμ</span></span>. Jesus can mean either "that I am from above" (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:23">John 8:23</span>), "that I am the one sent from the Father or the Messiah" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:18">John 7:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:28">John 7:28</span>), "that I am the Light of the World" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:12">John 8:12</span>), "that I am the Deliverer from the bondage of sin" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:28">John 8:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:31">John 8:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:36">John 8:36</span>), "that I am" without supplying a predicate in the absolute sense as the Jews (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+32:39">Deuteronomy 32:39</span>) used the language of Jehovah (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+43:10">Isaiah 43:10</span> where the very words occur <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα πιστευσητε--οτ εγω ειμ</span></span>). The phrase <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγω ειμ</span></span> occurs three times here (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:24">John 8:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:28">John 8:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:58">John 8:58</span>) and also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:19">John 13:19</span>. Jesus seems to claim absolute divine being as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:58">John 8:58</span>. </p>
