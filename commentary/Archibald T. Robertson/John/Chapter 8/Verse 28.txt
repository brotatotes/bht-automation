<p><b> When ye have lifted up the Son of man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν υψωσητε τον υιον του ανθρωπου</span></span>). Indefinite temporal clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτε + αν</span></span>) and the first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υψοω</span></span>, to lift up (<i>Koine</i> verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υψος</span></span>, height), used several times in John of the Cross of Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:14">John 3:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:28">John 8:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:32">John 12:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:34">John 12:34</span>). It is unnecessary to render the aorist subjunctive as if a future perfect, simply "whenever ye lift up" (actually lift up, ingressive aorist). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:33">Acts 2:33</span> the verb is used of the Ascension.</p>
<p><b>Shall ye know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνωσεσθε</span></span>). Future (ingressive aoristic) middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span>. <i>Cognoscetis ex re quod nunc ex verbo non creditis</i> (Bengel). But the knowledge from the facts like the fall of Jerusalem will come too late and will not bring a change of heart. The Holy Spirit will convict them concerning judgment (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:8">John 16:8</span>). For</p>
<p><b>I am </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγω ειμ</span></span>) see on verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:24">John 8:24</span>.</p>
<p><b>As the Father taught me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Καθως εδιδασκεν με ο πατηρ</span></span>). This claim Jesus repeats (see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:26">John 8:26</span>) and clearly makes on his arrival at the feast (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:16">John 7:16</span>). This fact marks Jesus off from the rabbis. </p>
