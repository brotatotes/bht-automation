<p><b> Is with me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετ' εμου εστιν</span></span>). The Incarnation brought separation from the Father in one sense, but in essence there is complete harmony and fellowship as he had already said (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:16">John 8:16</span>) and will expand in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:21-26">John 17:21-26</span>.</p>
<p><b>He hath not left me alone </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ αφηκεν με μονον</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφιημ</span></span>. "He did not leave me alone." However much the crowds and the disciples misunderstood or left Jesus, the Father always comforted and understood him (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:46">Mark 6:46</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:23">Matthew 14:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:15">John 6:15</span>).</p>
<p><b>That are pleasing to him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα αρεστα αυτω</span></span>). This old verbal adjective, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρεσκω</span></span>, to please, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:2">Acts 6:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:3">Acts 12:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:32">1 John 3:32</span>. The joy of Jesus was in doing the will of the Father who sent him (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:34">John 4:34</span>). </p>
