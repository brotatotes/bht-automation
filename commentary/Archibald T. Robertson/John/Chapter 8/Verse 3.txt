<p><b> The scribes and the Pharisees </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο γραμματεις κα ο Φαρισαιο</span></span>). John does not mention "scribes," though this combination (note two articles) is common enough in the Synoptics (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:30">Luke 5:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:7">Luke 6:7</span>, etc.).</p>
<p><b>Bring </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγουσιν</span></span>). Vivid dramatic present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγω</span></span>. Dods calls this "in itself an unlawful thing to do" since they had a court for the trial of such a case. Their purpose is to entrap Jesus.</p>
<p><b>Taken in adultery </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ μοιχεια κατειλεμμενην</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλαμβανω</span></span>, old compound to seize (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:18">Mark 9:18</span>), to catch, to overtake (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:35">John 12:35</span>), to overcome (or overtake) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:5">John 1:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>Having let her in the midst </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στησαντες αυτην εν μεσω</span></span>). First aorist active (transitive) participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span>. Here all could see her and what Jesus did with such a case. They knew his proneness to forgive sinners. </p>
