<p><b> Yet ye seek to kill me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα ζητειτε με αποκτεινα</span></span>). As at the recent feast (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:20">John 7:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:25">John 7:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:30">John 7:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:32">John 7:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:20">John 8:20</span>). Some of these very professed believers were even now glowering with murderous vengeance.</p>
<p><b>Hath not free course in you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου χωρε εν υμιν</span></span>). Intransitive use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χωρεω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χωρος</span></span> (space, place), to have space or room for. They would not abide in Christ's word (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:31">John 8:31</span>). They had no longer room for his word when once they understood the spiritual aspect of his message. Jerusalem was now just like Galilee once before (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:60-66">John 6:60-66</span>). </p>
