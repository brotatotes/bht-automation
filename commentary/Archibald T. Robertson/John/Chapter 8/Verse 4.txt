<p><b> Hath been taken </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατειληπτα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλαμβανω</span></span> (see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:3">John 8:3</span>), caught and still guilty.</p>
<p><b>In adultery </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μοιχευομενη</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μοιχευω</span></span>, "herself suffering adultery" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:32">Matthew 5:32</span>). Used of married people. Not in John.</p>
<p><b>In the very act </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ' αυτοφωρω</span></span>). Old adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτοφωροσ, αυτος</span></span>, self, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωρ</span></span>, thief) caught in the act of theft, then extended to any crime in which one is caught. Old idiom, but not elsewhere in the Greek Bible. One example in a Berlin papyrus. </p>
