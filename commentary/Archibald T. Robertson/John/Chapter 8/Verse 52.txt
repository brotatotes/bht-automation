<p><b> Now we know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυν εγνωκαμεν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span>, state of completion, "Now since such talk we have come to certain knowledge that thou hast a demon" (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:48">John 8:48</span>).</p>
<p><b>Is dead </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεθανεν</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποθνησκω</span></span>. "Abraham died."</p>
<p><b>And thou sayest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα συ λεγεις</span></span>). Adversative use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span>, "and yet." Emphatic position of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συ</span></span> (thou). Same condition quoted as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:51">John 8:51</span>.</p>
<p><b>He shall never taste of death </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου με γευσητα θανατου εις τον αιονα</span></span>). Same emphatic negative with subjunctive as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:51">John 8:51</span>, but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γευσητα</span></span> (first aorist middle subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γευω</span></span> with genitive case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θανατου</span></span> (death). Another Hebraism for dying like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεωρηση</span></span> (see) in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:51">John 8:51</span>. Used in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:9">Hebrews 2:9</span> of the death of Jesus and in Synoptics (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:28">Matthew 16:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:1">Mark 9:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:27">Luke 9:27</span>). It occurs in the Talmud, but not in the O.T. The Pharisees thus did not misquote Jesus, though they misunderstood him. </p>
