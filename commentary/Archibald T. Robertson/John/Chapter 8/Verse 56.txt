<p><b> Rejoiced </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγαλλιασατο</span></span>). First aorist middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαλλιαομα</span></span>, a word of Hellenistic coinage from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαλλομα</span></span>, to rejoice.</p>
<p><b>To see </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα ιδη</span></span>). Sub-final use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span>. This joy of Abraham is referred to in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:13">Hebrews 11:13</span> (saluting, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασπασαμενο</span></span>, the promises from afar). There was a Jewish tradition that Abraham saw the whole history of his descendants in the vision of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+15:6">Genesis 15:6</span>, but that is not necessary here. He did look for and welcome the Messianic time, "my day" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την ημεραν την εμην</span></span>). "He saw it, and was glad" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδεν κα εχαρη</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span> and second aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαιρω</span></span>. Ye see it and are angry! </p>
