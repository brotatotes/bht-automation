<p><b> Before Abraham was </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πριν Αβρααμ γενεσθα</span></span>). Usual idiom with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πριν</span></span> in positive sentence with infinitive (second aorist middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span>) and the accusative of general reference, "before coming as to Abraham," "before Abraham came into existence or was born."</p>
<p><b>I am </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγω ειμ</span></span>). Undoubtedly here Jesus claims eternal existence with the absolute phrase used of God. The contrast between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενεσθα</span></span> (entrance into existence of Abraham) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειμ</span></span> (timeless being) is complete. See the same contrast between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:1">John 1:1</span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγενετο</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:14">John 1:14</span>. See the contrast also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+90:2">Psalms 90:2</span> between God (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span>, art) and the mountains (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενηθηνα</span></span>). See the same use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειμ</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:20">John 6:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:9">John 9:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:24">John 8:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:28">John 8:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:6">John 18:6</span>. </p>
