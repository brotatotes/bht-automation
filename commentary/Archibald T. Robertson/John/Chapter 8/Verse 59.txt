<p><b> They took up stones therefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηραν ουν λιθους</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιρω</span></span>, inferential use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουν</span></span>. The time for argument had past.</p>
<p><b>To cast at him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα βαλωσιν επ' αυτον</span></span>). Final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαλλω</span></span>. Vivid picture of a mob ready to kill Jesus, already beginning to do so.</p>
<p><b>Hid himself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκρυβη</span></span>). Second aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρυπτω</span></span>. He was hidden. No Docetic vanishing, but quietly and boldly Jesus went out of the temple. His hour had not yet come. Once again three months later the Pharisees will try to kill him, but he will pass out of their hands (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:39">John 10:39</span>). </p>
