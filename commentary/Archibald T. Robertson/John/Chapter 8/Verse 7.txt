<p><b> When they continued asking </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως επεμενον ερωτωντες</span></span>). Imperfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιμενω</span></span> (waiting in addition or still, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>, old verb) with supplementary active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερωταω</span></span>, to question. See same construction in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:16">Acts 12:16</span> The verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιμενω</span></span> does not occur in John. They saw that Jesus seemed embarrassed, but did not know that it was as much because of "the brazen hardness of the prosecutors" as because of the shame of the deed.</p>
<p><b>He lifted himself up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεκυψεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανακυπτω</span></span>, the opposite of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατακυπτω</span></span>, to bend down (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:8">John 8:8</span>) or of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατω κυπτω</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:6">John 8:6</span>).</p>
<p><b>He that is without sin </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αναμαρτητος</span></span>). Verbal adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτητος</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτανω</span></span>), old word, either one who has not sinned as here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+29:19">Deuteronomy 29:19</span> or one who cannot sin, not in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>Among you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμων</span></span>). Objective genitive.</p>
<p><b>First cast </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτος βαλετω</span></span>). The nominative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτος</span></span> means first before others, be the first to cast, not cast before he does something else. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:4">John 20:4</span>. The verb is second aorist imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαλλω</span></span>, old verb to fling or cast. Jesus thus picks out the executioner in the case. </p>
