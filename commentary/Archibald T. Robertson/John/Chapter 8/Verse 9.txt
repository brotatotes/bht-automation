<p><b> Went out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξηρχοντο</span></span>). Inchoative imperfect. Graphic picture.</p>
<p><b>One by one </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις καθ' εις</span></span>). Not a Johannine phrase, but in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:19">Mark 14:19</span> where also the second nominative is retained as if <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθ'</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span>) is regarded as a mere adverb and not as a preposition.</p>
<p><b>Beginning from the eldest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρξαμενο απο των πρεσβυτερων</span></span>). "From the elder (comparative form, common in <i>Koine</i> as superlative) men," as was natural for they had more sins of this sort which they recalled. "They are summoned to judge themselves rather than the woman" (Dods).</p>
<p><b>Was left alone </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατελειφθη μονος</span></span>). First aorist effective passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλειπω</span></span>, to leave behind, with predicate nominative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μονος</span></span>. "Jesus was left behind alone."</p>
<p><b>And the woman, where she was, in the midst </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα η γυνη εν μεσω ουσα</span></span>). The woman was left behind also "being in the midst" as they had placed her (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:3">John 8:3</span>) before they were conscience stricken and left. </p>
