<p><b> But how he now seeth we know not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πως δε νυν βλεπε ουκ οιδαμεν</span></span>). Concerning the third question they profess ignorance both as to the "how" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πως</span></span>) and the "who" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τις</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Opened </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηνοιξεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative with single augment of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανοιγω</span></span>, same form as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηνεωιξεν</span></span> (triple augment) in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:17">John 9:17</span>. They were not witnesses of the cure and had the story only from the son as the Pharisees had.</p>
<p><b>He is of age </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηλικιαν εχε</span></span>). "He has maturity of age." He is an adult. A regular classical phrase in Plato, etc. The parents were wholly right and within their rights. </p>
