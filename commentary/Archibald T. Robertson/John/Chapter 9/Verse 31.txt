<p><b> God does not hear sinners </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο θεος αμαρτωλων ουκ ακουε</span></span>). Note genitive case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουε</span></span>. This was the argument of the Pharisees in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:16">John 9:16</span>. It is frequent in the O.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+27:9">Job 27:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+66:18">Psalms 66:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+1:15">Isaiah 1:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+59:2">Isaiah 59:2</span>, etc.). The conclusion is inevitable from this premise. Jesus is not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτωλος</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>If any man be a worshipper of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν τις θεοσεβης η</span></span>). Condition of third class with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and present active subjunctive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Θεοσεβης</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεος</span></span>, God, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σεβομα</span></span>, to worship) is an old compound adjective, here alone in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>And do his will </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα το θελημα αυτου ποιε</span></span>). Same condition with present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span>, "keep on doing his will." </p>
