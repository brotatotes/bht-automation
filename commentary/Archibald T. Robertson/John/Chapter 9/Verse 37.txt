<p><b> Thou hast both seen him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα εωρακας αυτον</span></span>). Perfect active indicative (double reduplication) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span>. Since his eyes were opened.</p>
<p><b>And he it is that speaketh with thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ο λαλων μετα σου εκεινος εστιν</span></span>). "And the one speaking with thee is that man." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:35">John 19:35</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινος</span></span> used of the speaker. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:26">John 4:26</span> Jesus reveals himself in like manner to the Samaritan woman as Messiah while here as the Son of Man (or the Son of God). </p>
