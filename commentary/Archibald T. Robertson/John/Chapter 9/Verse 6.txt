<p><b> He spat on the ground </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επτυσεν χαμα</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of the old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πτυω</span></span> for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:33">Mark 7:33</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χαμα</span></span> is an old adverb either in the dative or locative (sense suits locative), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:6">John 18:6</span>. Jesus was not asked to cure this man. The curative effects of saliva are held in many places. The Jews held saliva efficacious for eye-trouble, but it was forbidden on the Sabbath. "That Jesus supposed some virtue lay in the application of the clay is contradicted by the fact that in other cases of blindness He did not use it" (Dods). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:23">Mark 8:23</span>. Why he here accommodated himself to current belief we do not know unless it was to encourage the man to believe.</p>
<p><b>He made clay </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εποιησεν πηλον</span></span>). Only use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πηλος</span></span>, old word for clay, in N.T. in this chapter and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:21">Romans 9:21</span>. The kneading of the clay and spittle added another offence against the Sabbath rules of the rabbis.</p>
<p><b>Anointed his eyes with the clay </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεχρισεν αυτου τον πηλον επ τους οφθαλμους</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιχριω</span></span>, old verb, to spread on, anoint, here only and verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:11">John 9:11</span> in N.T. "He spread the clay upon his eyes." B C read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεθηκεν</span></span> (first aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιτιθημ</span></span>, to put on). </p>
