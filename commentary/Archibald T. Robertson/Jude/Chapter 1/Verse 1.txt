<p><b> Servant </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δουλος</span></span>). Precisely as James (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:1">James 1:1</span>), only James added <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυριου</span></span> (Lord).</p>
<p><b>Brother of James </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδελφος Ιακωβου</span></span>). Thus Jude identifies himself. But not the "Judas of James" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:16">Luke 6:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:13">Acts 1:13</span>).</p>
<p><b>To them that are called </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοισ--κλητοις</span></span>). But this translation (treating <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλητοις</span></span> as a substantive like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:6">Romans 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:24">1 Corinthians 1:24</span>) is by no means certain as two participles come in between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλητοις</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κλητοις</span></span> may be in the predicate position (being called), not attributive. But see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:1">1 Peter 1:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Beloved in God the Father </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν θεω πατρ ηγαπημενοις</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπαω</span></span>, but no precise parallel to this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπαω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Kept for Jesus Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιησου Χριστω τετηρημενοις</span></span>). Perfect passive participle again with dative, unless it is the instrumental, "kept by Jesus Christ," a quite possible interpretation. </p>
