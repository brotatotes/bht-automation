<p><b> Woe to them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουα αυτοις</span></span>). Interjection with the dative as is common in the Gospels (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:21">Matthew 11:21</span>).</p>
<p><b>Went </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επορευθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive (deponent) indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορευομα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>In the way of Cain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη οδω του Καιν</span></span>). Locative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδω</span></span>. Cain is Jude's fourth example. Not in II Peter, but in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:4">Hebrews 11:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:11">1 John 3:11</span>. From <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+4:7">Genesis 4:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>Ran riotously </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεχυθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκχεω</span></span>, to pour out, "they were poured out," vigorous metaphor for excessive indulgence. But it is used also of God's love for us (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:5">Romans 5:5</span>).</p>
<p><b>In the error of Balaam </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη πλανη του Βαλααμ</span></span>). The fifth example in Jude. In II Peter also (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:15">2 Peter 2:15</span>). Either locative case (in) or instrumental (by). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πλανη</span></span> (in Peter also) is the common word for such wandering (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:4">Matthew 24:4</span>, etc.).</p>
<p><b>Perished </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απωλοντο</span></span>). Second aorist middle (intransitive) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολλυμ</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>In the gainsaying of Korah </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη αντιλογια του Κορε</span></span>). Again either locative or instrumental. The word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντιλογια</span></span> is originally answering back (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:16">Hebrews 6:16</span>), but it may be by act also (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:21">Romans 10:21</span>) as here. This is the sixth example in Jude, not in II Peter. </p>
