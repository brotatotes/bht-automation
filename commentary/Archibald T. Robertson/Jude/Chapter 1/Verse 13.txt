<p><b> Wild waves </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυματα αγρια</span></span>). Waves (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:24">Matthew 8:24</span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυεω</span></span>, to swell) wild (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγρος</span></span>, field, wild honey <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:4">Matthew 3:4</span>) like untamed animals of the forest or the sea.</p>
<p><b>Foaming out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαφριζοντα</span></span>). Late and rare present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαφριζω</span></span>, used in Moschus for the foaming waves as here. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+57:20">Isaiah 57:20</span>.</p>
<p><b>Shame </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αισχυνας</span></span>). Plural "shames" (disgraces). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:19">Philippians 3:19</span>.</p>
<p><b>Wandering stars </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αστερες πλανητα</span></span>). "Stars wanderers." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πλανητης</span></span>, old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλαναω</span></span>), here alone in N.T. Some refer this to comets or shooting stars. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+14:12">Isaiah 14:12</span> for an allusion to Babylon as the day-star who fell through pride.</p>
<p><b>For ever </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις αιωνα</span></span>). The rest of the relative clause exactly as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:17">2 Peter 2:17</span>. </p>
