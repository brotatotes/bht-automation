<p><b> And to these also </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε κα τουτοις</span></span>). Dative case, for these false teachers as well as for his contemporaries.</p>
<p><b>Enoch the seventh from Adam </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβδομος απο Αδαμ Hενωχ</span></span>). The genealogical order occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+5:4-20">Genesis 5:4-20</span>, with Enoch as seventh. He is so termed in Enoch 60:8; 93:3.</p>
<p><b>Prophesied </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επροφητευσεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προφητευω</span></span>. If the word is given its ordinary meaning as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:10">1 Peter 1:10</span>, then Jude terms the Book of Enoch an inspired book. The words quoted are "a combination of passages from Enoch" (Bigg), chiefly from Enoch 1:9.</p>
<p><b>With ten thousand of his holy ones </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αγιαις μυριασιν αυτου</span></span>). "With (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> of accompaniment, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:31">Luke 14:31</span>) his holy ten thousands" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυριας</span></span> regular word, feminine gender, for ten thousand, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:19">Acts 19:19</span>, there an unlimited number like our myriads, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:1">Luke 12:1</span>). </p>
