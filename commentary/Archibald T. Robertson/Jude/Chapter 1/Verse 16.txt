<p><b> Murmurers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γογγυστα</span></span>). Late onomatopoetic word for agent, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γογγυζω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:11">Matthew 20:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:10">1 Corinthians 10:10</span>) in the LXX (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+16:8">Exodus 16:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+11:1">Numbers 11:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+11:14-29">Numbers 11:14-29</span>).</p>
<p><b>Complainers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεμψιμοιρο</span></span>). Rare word (Isocrates, Aristotle, Plutarch) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεμφομα</span></span> to complain and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μοιρα</span></span> lot or fate. Here alone in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Lusts </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιθυμιας</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:3">2 Peter 3:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Swelling </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερογκα</span></span>). So in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:18">2 Peter 2:18</span> (big words).</p>
<p><b>Showing respect of persons </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θαυμαζοντες προσωπα</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θαυμαζω</span></span> to admire, to wonder at. Nowhere else in N.T. with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσωπα</span></span>, but a Hebraism (in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="le+19:15">Leviticus 19:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+13:10">Job 13:10</span>) like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανειν προσωπον</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:21">Luke 20:21</span>) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλεπειν προσωπον</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:16">Matthew 22:16</span>) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσοπωλεμπτεω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:9">James 2:9</span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:1">James 2:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>For the sake of advantage </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωφελειας χαριν</span></span>). To themselves. See also verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:11">Jude 1:11</span>. The covetousness of these Gnostic leaders is plainly shown in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:3">2 Peter 2:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:14">2 Peter 2:14</span>. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριν</span></span> as preposition with genitive see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:1">Ephesians 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:14">Ephesians 3:14</span>. </p>
