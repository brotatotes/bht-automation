<p><b> They who make separations </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αποδιοριζοντες</span></span>). Present active articular participle of the double compound <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποδιοριζω</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο, δια, οριζω, ορος</span></span>, boundary, to make a horizon), rare word, in Aristotle for making logical distinctions, here only in N.T. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Διοριζω</span></span> occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="le+20:24">Leviticus 20:24</span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφοριζω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:32">Matthew 25:32</span>, etc. See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιρεσεις</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:1">2 Peter 2:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Sensual </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψυχικο</span></span>). Old adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψυχη</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:14">1 Corinthians 2:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:44">1 Corinthians 15:44</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:15">James 3:15</span>. Opposed to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευματικος</span></span>. Not used by Peter.</p>
<p><b>Having not the Spirit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα μη εχοντες</span></span>). Usual negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> with the participle (present active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>). Probably <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα</span></span> here means the Holy Spirit, as is plain in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:20">Jude 1:20</span>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:9">Romans 8:9</span>. </p>
