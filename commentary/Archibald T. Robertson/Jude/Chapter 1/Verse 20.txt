<p><b> Building up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εποικοδομουντες</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εποικοδομεω</span></span>, old compound with metaphor of a house (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικος</span></span>), common in Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:9-17">1 Corinthians 3:9-17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:7">Colossians 2:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:20">Ephesians 2:20</span>).</p>
<p><b>On your most holy faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη αγιωτατη υμων πιστε</span></span>). For the spiritual temple see also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:3-5">1 Peter 2:3-5</span>. See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστις</span></span> (faith) in this sense (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:1">Hebrews 11:1</span>) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:5">2 Peter 1:5</span> with the list of graces added. A true superlative here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγιωτατη</span></span>, not elative.</p>
<p><b>Praying in the Holy Spirit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν πνευματ αγιω προσευχομενο</span></span>). This is the way to build themselves up on their faith. </p>
