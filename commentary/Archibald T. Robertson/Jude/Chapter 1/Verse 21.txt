<p><b> Keep yourselves </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαυτους τηρησατε</span></span>). First aorist active imperative (of urgency) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρεω</span></span>. In verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:1">Jude 1:1</span> they are said to be kept, but note the warning in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:5">Jude 1:5</span> from the angels who did not keep their dominion. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:27">James 1:27</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:12">Philippians 2:12</span> both sides (human responsibility and divine sovereignty are presented side by side).</p>
<p><b>Looking for </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσδεχομενο</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσδεχομα</span></span>, the very form in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:13">Titus 2:13</span>. The same idea in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσδοκωντες</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:14">2 Peter 3:14</span>. </p>
