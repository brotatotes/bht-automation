<p><b> And on some </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ους μεν</span></span>). Demonstrative plural of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος μεν--ος δε</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ους δε</span></span>, below), not the relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ους</span></span>, but by contrast (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεν, δε</span></span>). So <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:8">Matthew 13:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>Have mercy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεατε</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεαω</span></span> (rare form in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:16">Romans 9:16</span> also for the usual <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεεω</span></span> <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:27">Matthew 9:27</span>). But A C read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεγχετε</span></span>, refute, in place of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεατε</span></span>. The text of this verse is in much confusion.</p>
<p><b>Who are in doubt </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακρινομενους</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακρινω</span></span>, in the accusative case agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ους μεν</span></span>, though K L P have the nominative. If the accusative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεατε</span></span> is read, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:6">James 1:6</span> for the idea (doubters). If <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεγχετε</span></span> is read, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:9">Jude 1:9</span> for the idea (disputers). </p>
