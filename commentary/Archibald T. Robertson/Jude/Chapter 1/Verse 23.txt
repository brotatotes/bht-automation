<p><b> And some save </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ους δε σωζετε</span></span>). B omits <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ους δε</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Snatching them out of the fire </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ πυρος αρπαζοντες</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρπαζω</span></span>, old verb, to seize. Quotation from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="am+4:11">Amos 4:11</span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="zec+3:3">Zechariah 3:3</span>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+106:18">Psalms 106:18</span>. Firemen today literally do this rescue work. Do Christians?</p>
<p><b>And on some have mercy with fear </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ους δε ελεατε εν φοβω</span></span>). In fear "of the contagion of sin while we are rescuing them" (Vincent). For this idea see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:17">1 Peter 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:15">1 Peter 3:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:1">2 Corinthians 7:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:12">Philippians 2:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>Spotted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσπιλωμενον</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπιλοω</span></span>, late and common verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπιλος</span></span>, spot, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:13">2 Peter 2:13</span>), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:6">James 3:6</span>. </p>
