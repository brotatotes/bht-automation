<p><b> From stumbling </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απταιστους</span></span>). Verbal from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πταιω</span></span>, to stumble (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:2">James 3:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:10">2 Peter 1:10</span>), sure-footed as of a horse that does not stumble (Xenophon), and so of a good man (Epictetus, Marcus Antoninus).</p>
<p><b>Before the presence of his glory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατενωπιον της δοξης αυτου</span></span>). Late compound preposition (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα, εν, ωπς</span></span>), right down before the eye of his glory as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:4">Ephesians 1:4</span>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:31-33">Matthew 25:31-33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:22">Colossians 1:22</span>, where Paul has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραστησα</span></span> like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στησα</span></span> here (first aorist active infinitive) and also <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμωμους</span></span> as here, but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμωμητος</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:14">2 Peter 3:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>In exceeding joy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αγαλλιασε</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:14">Luke 1:14</span>. </p>
