<p><b> To the only God our Saviour </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μονω θεω σωτηρ ημων</span></span>). Dative in the noble doxology. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:27">Romans 16:27</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μονω σοφω θεω</span></span> (to the alone wise God), where also we have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια Ιησου Χριστου</span></span>, but without <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του κυριου ημων</span></span> (our Lord) as here. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Σωτηρ</span></span> is used of God eight times in the N.T., six of them in the Pastoral Epistles. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Δοξα</span></span> (glory) to God or Christ in all the doxologies except <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:16">1 Timothy 6:16</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μεγαλοσυνη</span></span> (Majesty) is a late LXX word, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:3">Hebrews 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:1">Hebrews 8:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Before all time </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προ παντος του αιωνος</span></span>). Eternity behind us. See same idea in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:7">1 Corinthians 2:7</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προ των αιωνων</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Now </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυν</span></span>). The present.</p>
<p><b>For ever more </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις παντας τους αιωνας</span></span>). "Unto all the ages." All the future. As complete a statement of eternity as can be made in human language. </p>
