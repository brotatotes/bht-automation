<p><b> Are crept in </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεισεδυησαν</span></span>). Second aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεισδυω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-νω</span></span>), late (Hippocrates, Plutarch, etc.) compound of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span> (beside) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> (in) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυω</span></span> to sink or plunge, so to slip in secretly as if by a side door, here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Set forth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προγεγραμμενο</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προγραφω</span></span>, to write of beforehand, for which verb see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:1">Galatians 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:4">Romans 15:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>Unto this condemnation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τουτο το κριμα</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:3">2 Peter 2:3</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κριμα</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκπαλα</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Παλα</span></span> here apparently alludes to verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:14">Jude 1:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:15">Jude 1:15</span> (Enoch).</p>
<p><b>Ungodly men </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασεβεις</span></span>). Keynote of the Epistle (Mayor), in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:15">Jude 1:15</span> again as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:5">2 Peter 2:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:7">2 Peter 3:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>Turning </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετατιθεντες</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετατιθημ</span></span>, to change, for which verb see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:6">Galatians 1:6</span>. For the change of "grace" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριτα</span></span>) into "lasciviousness (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις ασελγειαν</span></span>) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:16">1 Peter 2:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:3">1 Peter 4:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:19">2 Peter 2:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:16">2 Peter 3:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>Our only Master and Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον μονον δεσποτην κα κυριον ημων</span></span>). For the force of the one article for one person see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:1">2 Peter 1:1</span>. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεσποτην</span></span> of Christ see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:1">2 Peter 2:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Denying </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρνουμενο</span></span>). So <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:1">2 Peter 2:1</span>. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:33">Matthew 10:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:8">1 Timothy 5:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:16">Titus 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:22">1 John 2:22</span>. </p>
