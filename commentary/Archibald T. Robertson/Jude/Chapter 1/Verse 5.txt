<p><b> To put you in remembrance </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπομνησα</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:12">2 Peter 1:12</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπομιμνησκειν</span></span> (present active infinitive there, first aorist active infinitive here).</p>
<p><b>Though ye know all things once for all </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδοτας απαξ παντα</span></span>). Concessive perfect (sense of present) active participle as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:12">2 Peter 1:12</span>, but without <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιπερ</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>The Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυριος</span></span>). Some MSS. add <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιησους</span></span>. The use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυριος</span></span> here is usually understood to mean the Lord Jesus Christ, as Clement of Alex. (<i>Adumbr</i>. p. 133) explains, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+23:20">Exodus 23:20</span>, by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο μυστικος εκεινος αγγελος Ιησους</span></span> (that mystical angel Jesus). For the mystic reference to Christ see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:4">1 Corinthians 10:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:9">1 Corinthians 10:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:26">Hebrews 11:26</span>. Some MSS. here add <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεος</span></span> instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιησους</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Afterward </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το δευτερον</span></span>). Adverbial accusative, "the second time." After having saved the people out of Egypt.</p>
<p><b>Destroyed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απωλεσεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολλυμ</span></span>, old verb, to destroy.</p>
<p><b>Them that believed not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους μη πιστευσαντας</span></span>). First aorist active articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστευω</span></span>. The reference is to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+14:27-37">Numbers 14:27-37</span>, when all the people rescued from Egypt perished except Caleb and Joshua. This first example by Jude is not in II Peter, but is discussed in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:5-11">1 Corinthians 10:5-11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:18-4">Hebrews 3:18-4</span>. </p>
