<p><b> Even as </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως</span></span>). Just "as." The third instance (Jude passes by the deluge) in Jude, the cities of the plain.</p>
<p><b>The cities about them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α περ αυτας πολεις</span></span>). These were also included, Admah and Zeboiim (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+29:23">Deuteronomy 29:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ho+11:8">Hosea 11:8</span>). Zoar, the other city, was spared.</p>
<p><b>In like manner </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον ομοιον τροπον</span></span>). Adverbial accusative (cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως</span></span>). Like the fallen angels.</p>
<p><b>Having given themselves over to fornication </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκπορνευσασα</span></span>). First aorist active participle feminine plural of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκπορνευω</span></span>, late and rare compound (perfective use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span>, outside the moral law), only here in N.T., but in LXX (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+38:24">Genesis 38:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+34:15">Exodus 34:15</span>, etc.). Cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασελγειαν</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:4">Jude 1:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>Strange flesh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαρκος ετερας</span></span>). Horrible licentiousness, not simply with women not their wives or in other nations, but even unnatural uses (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:27">Romans 1:27</span>) for which the very word "sodomy" is used (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+19:4-11">Genesis 19:4-11</span>). The pronoun <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετερας</span></span> (other, strange) is not in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:10">2 Peter 2:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Are set forth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προκειντα</span></span>). Present middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προκειμα</span></span>, old verb, to lie before, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:1">Hebrews 12:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>As an example </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δειγμα</span></span>). Predicate nominative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δειγμα</span></span>, old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεικνυμ</span></span> to show), here only in N.T., sample, specimen. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:6">2 Peter 2:6</span> has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποδειγμα</span></span> (pattern).</p>
<p><b>Suffering </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπεχουσα</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπεχω</span></span>, old compound, to hold under, often with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικην</span></span> (right, justice, sentence <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:9">2 Thessalonians 1:9</span>) to suffer sentence (punishment), here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Of eternal fire </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυρος αιωνιου</span></span>). Like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεσμοις αιδιοις</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:7">Jude 1:7</span>. Cf. the hell of fire (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:22">Matthew 5:22</span>) and also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:46">Matthew 25:46</span>. Jude has no mention of Lot. </p>
