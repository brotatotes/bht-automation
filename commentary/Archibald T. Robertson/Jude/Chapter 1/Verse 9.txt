<p><b> Michael the archangel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο Μιχαελ ο αρχαγγελος</span></span>). Michael is mentioned also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="da+10:13">Daniel 10:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="da+10:21">Daniel 10:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="da+12:1">Daniel 12:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:7">Revelation 12:7</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αρχαγγελος</span></span> in N.T. occurs only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:16">1 Thessalonians 4:16</span>, but in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="da+10:13">Daniel 10:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="da+10:20">Daniel 10:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="da+12:1">Daniel 12:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Contending with the devil </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω διαβολω διακρινομενος</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακρινω</span></span>, to separate, to strive with as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:2">Acts 11:2</span>. Dative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαβολω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>When he disputed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτε διελεγετο</span></span>). Imperfect middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαλεγομα</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:34">Mark 9:34</span>.</p>
<p><b>Concerning the body of Moses </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ του Μωυσεως σωματος</span></span>). Some refer this to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="zec+3:1">Zechariah 3:1</span>, others to a rabbinical comment on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+34:6">Deuteronomy 34:6</span>. There is a similar reference to traditions in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:22">Acts 7:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:19">Galatians 3:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:2">Hebrews 2:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:8">2 Timothy 3:8</span>. But this explanation hardly meets the facts.</p>
<p><b>Durst not bring </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ ετολμησεν επενεγκειν</span></span>). "Did not dare (first aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τολμαω</span></span>), to bring against him" (second aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιφερω</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>A railing accusation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρισιν βλασφημιας</span></span>). "Charge of blasphemy" where <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:11">2 Peter 2:11</span> has "<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλασφημον κρισιν</span></span>." Peter also has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα κυριω</span></span> (with the Lord), not in Jude.</p>
<p><b>The Lord rebuke thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιτιμησα σο κυριος</span></span>). First aorist active optative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιτιμαω</span></span>, a wish about the future. These words occur in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="zec+3:1-10">Zechariah 3:1-10</span> where the angel of the Lord replies to the charges of Satan. Clement of Alex. (<i>Adumb. in Ep. Judae</i>) says that Jude quoted here the <i>Assumption of Moses</i>, one of the apocryphal books. Origen says the same thing. Mayor thinks that the author of the <i>Assumption of Moses</i> took these words from Zechariah and put them in the mouth of the Archangel Michael. There is a Latin version of the <i>Assumption</i>. Some date it as early as B.C. 2, others after A.D. 44. </p>
