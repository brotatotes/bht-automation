<p><b> Strong drink </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σικερα</span></span>). A Hebrew word transliterated into Greek, an intoxicating drink. Here only in the N.T. John was to be a personal "dry" or Nazarite (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+6:3">Numbers 6:3</span>).</p>
<p><b>Shall not drink </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη πιη</span></span>). Strong prohibition, double negative and second aorist subjunctive.</p>
<p><b>The Holy Ghost </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευματος αγιου</span></span>). The Holy Spirit in contrast to the physical excitement of strong drink (Plummer). Luke uses this phrase 53 times, 12 in the Gospel, Mark and John 4 each, Matthew 5 times.</p>
<p><b>Even from his mother's womb </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετ εκ κοιλιας μητρος αυτου</span></span>). A manifest Hebraism. Cf. verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:41">Luke 1:41</span>. </p>
