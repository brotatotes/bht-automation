<p><b> Ministration </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λειτουργιας</span></span>). Our word liturgy. A common word in ancient Greek for public service, work for the people (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεως εργον</span></span>). It is common in the papyri for the service of the Egyptian priesthood as we see it in the LXX of Hebrew priests (see also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:6">Hebrews 8:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:21">Hebrews 9:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:12">2 Corinthians 9:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:17">Philippians 2:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:30">Philippians 2:30</span>). </p>
