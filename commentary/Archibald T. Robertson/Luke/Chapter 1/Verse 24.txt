<p><b> Conceived </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνελαβεν</span></span>). Luke uses this word eleven times and it occurs only five other times in the N.T. It is a very old and common Greek word. He alone in the N.T. has it for conceiving offspring (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:24">Luke 1:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:31">Luke 1:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:36">Luke 1:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:21">Luke 2:21</span>) though <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:15">James 1:15</span> uses it of lust producing sin. Hobart (<i>Medical Language of Luke</i>, p. 91) observes that Luke has almost as many words for pregnancy and barrenness as Hippocrates (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν γαστρ εχειν</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:23">Luke 21:23</span>; <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγκυος</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:5">Luke 2:5</span>; <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στειρα</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:7">Luke 1:7</span>; <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ατεκνος</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:28">Luke 20:28</span>).</p>
<p><b>Hid </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιεκρυβεν</span></span>). Only here in the N.T., but in late <i>Koine</i> writers. Usually considered second aorist active indicative from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περικρυπτω</span></span>, though it may be the imperfect indicative of a late form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περικρυβω</span></span>. If it is aorist it is the constative aorist. The preposition <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ</span></span> makes it mean completely (on all sides) hid. </p>
