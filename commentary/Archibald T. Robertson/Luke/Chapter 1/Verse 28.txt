<p><b> Highly favoured </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεχαριτωμενη</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριτοω</span></span> and means endowed with grace (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρις</span></span>), enriched with grace as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:6">Ephesians 1:6</span>, <i>non ut mater gratiae, sed ut filia gratiae</i> (Bengel). The Vulgate <i>gratiae plena</i> "is right, if it means 'full of grace <i>which thou hast received</i>'; wrong, if it means 'full of grace <i>which thou hast to bestow</i>"' (Plummer). The oldest MSS. do not have "Blessed art thou among women" here, but in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:42">Luke 1:42</span>. </p>
