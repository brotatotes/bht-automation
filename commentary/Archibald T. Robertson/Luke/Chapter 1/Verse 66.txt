<p><b> What then </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τ αρα</span></span>). With all these supernatural happenings they predicted the marvellous career of this child. Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Τ</span></span>, <b>what </b>, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Τις</span></span>, <b>who</b>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:18">Acts 12:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>They laid them up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εθεντο</span></span>, second aorist middle indicative) as Mary did (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:19">Luke 2:19</span>).</p>
<p><b>The hand of the Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χειρ Κυριου</span></span>). Luke's explanation in addition to the supernatural events. The expression occurs only in Luke's writing (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:21">Acts 11:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:11">Acts 13:11</span>). </p>
