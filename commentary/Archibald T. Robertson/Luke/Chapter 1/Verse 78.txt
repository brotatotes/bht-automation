<p><b> Tender mercy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπλαγχνα ελεους</span></span>). Bowels of mercy literally (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:8">1 Peter 3:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:11">James 3:11</span>). Revised margin has it, hearts of mercy.</p>
<p><b>The dayspring from on high </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανατολη εξ υψους</span></span>). Literally, rising from on high, like the rising sun or stars (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+60:19">Isaiah 60:19</span>). The word is used also of a sprouting plant or branch (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+23:5">Jeremiah 23:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="zec+6:12">Zechariah 6:12</span>), but that does not suit here.</p>
<p><b>Shall visit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεσκεψετα</span></span>), correct text, cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:68">Luke 1:68</span>. </p>
