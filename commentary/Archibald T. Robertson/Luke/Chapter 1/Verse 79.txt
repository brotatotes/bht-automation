<p><b> To shine upon </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιφανα</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιφαινω</span></span> (liquid verb). An old verb to give light, to shine upon, like the sun or stars. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:20">Acts 27:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:11">Titus 2:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:4">Titus 3:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>The shadow of death </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκια θανατου</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+107:10">Psalms 107:10</span>, where darkness and shadow of death are combined as here. Cf. also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+9:1">Isaiah 9:1</span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:16">Matthew 4:16</span>. To guide (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του κατευθυνα</span></span>). Genitive of the articular infinitive of purpose. The light will enable them in the dark to see how to walk in a straight path that leads to "the way of peace." We are still on that road, but so many stumble for lack of light, men and nations. </p>
