<p><b> Rejecteth him that sent me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθετε τον αποστειλαντα με</span></span>). These solemn words form a fit close for this discourse to the Seventy. The fate of Chorazin, Bethsaida, Capernaum will befall those who set aside (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θετεω</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span>) the mission and message of these messengers of Christ. See this verb used in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:30">Luke 7:30</span> of the attitude of the scribes and Pharisees toward John and Jesus. It is this thought that makes it so grave a responsibility to be co-workers with Christ, high privilege as it is (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:4">John 9:4</span>). </p>
