<p><b> I beheld Satan fallen </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εθεωρουν τον Σαταναν πεσοντα</span></span>). Imperfect active (I was beholding) and second aorist (constative) active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιπτω</span></span> (not</p>
<p><b>fallen </b>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπτωκοτα</span></span>, perfect active participle, nor</p>
<p><b>falling </b>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιπτοντα</span></span>, present active participle, but</p>
<p><b>fall </b>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεσοντα</span></span>). As a flash of lightning out of heaven, quick and startling, so the victory of the Seventy over the demons, the agents of Satan, forecast his downfall and Jesus in vision pictured it as a flash of lightning. </p>
