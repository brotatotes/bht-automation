<p><b> Made answer </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπολαβων</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπολαμβανω</span></span> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:43">Luke 7:43</span>), to take up literally, and then in thought and speech, old verb, but in this sense of interrupting in talk only in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>Was going down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεβαινεν</span></span>). Imperfect active describing the journey.</p>
<p><b>Fell among robbers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ληισταις περιεπεσεν</span></span>). Second aorist ingressive active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιπιπτω</span></span>, old verb with associative instrumental case, to fall among and to be encompassed by (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ</span></span>, around), to be surrounded by robbers. A common experience to this day on the road to Jericho. The Romans placed a fort on this "red and bloody way." These were bandits, not petty thieves.</p>
<p><b>Stripped </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκδυσαντες</span></span>). Of his clothing as well as of his money, the meanest sort of robbers.</p>
<p><b>Beat him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληγας επιθεντες</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιτιθημ</span></span>, a common verb. Literally, "placing strokes or blows" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληγας</span></span>, plagues) upon him. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:48">Luke 12:48</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:23">Acts 16:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:1">Revelation 15:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:6">Revelation 15:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:8">Revelation 15:8</span> for "plagues."</p>
<p><b>Half-dead </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημιθανη</span></span>). Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημ</span></span>, half, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θνησκω</span></span>, to die. Only here in the N.T. Vivid picture of the robbery. </p>
