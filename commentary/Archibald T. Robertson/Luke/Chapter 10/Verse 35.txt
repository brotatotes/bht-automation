<p><b> On the morrow </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ την αυριον</span></span>). Towards the morrow as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:5">Acts 4:5</span>. (Cf. also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:1">Acts 3:1</span>). Syriac Sinaitic has it "at dawn of the day." An unusual use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Took out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκβαλων</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκβαλλω</span></span>. It could mean, "fling out," but probably only means "drew out." Common verb.</p>
<p><b>Two pence </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυο δηναρια</span></span>). About thirty-five cents, but worth more in purchasing power.</p>
<p><b>To the host </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω πανδοχε</span></span>). The innkeeper. Here only in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>Whatsoever thou spendest more </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ αν προσδαπανησηις</span></span>). Indefinite relative clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span> and the aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσδαπαναω</span></span>, to spend besides (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς</span></span>), a late verb for the common <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσαναλισκω</span></span> and here only in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>I will repay </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγο αποδωσω</span></span>). Emphatic. What he had paid was merely by way of pledge. He was a man of his word and known to the innkeeper as reliable.</p>
<p><b>When I come back again </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω επανερχεσθα με</span></span>). Luke's favourite idiom of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> and the articular infinitive with accusative of general reference. Double compound verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επανερχομα</span></span>. </p>
