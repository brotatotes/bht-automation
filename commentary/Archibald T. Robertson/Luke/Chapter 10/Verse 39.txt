<p><b> Which also sat </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η κα παρακαθεσθεισα</span></span>). First aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρακαθεζομα</span></span>, an old verb, but only here in the N.T. It means to sit beside (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span>) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς</span></span> means right in front of the feet of Jesus. It is not clear what the point is in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span> here. It may mean that Martha loved to sit here also as well as Mary.</p>
<p><b>Heard </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκουεν</span></span>). Imperfect active. She took her seat by the feet of Jesus and went on listening to his talk. </p>
