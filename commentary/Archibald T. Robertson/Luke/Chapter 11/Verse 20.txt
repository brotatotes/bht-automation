<p><b> By the finger of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν δακτυλω θεου</span></span>). In distinction from the Jewish exorcists. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:28">Matthew 12:28</span> has "by the Spirit of God."</p>
<p><b>Then is come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρα εφθασεν</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Φθανω</span></span> in late Greek comes to mean simply to come, not to come before. The aorist indicative tense here is timeless. Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρα</span></span> (accordingly) in the conclusion (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποδοσις</span></span>). </p>
