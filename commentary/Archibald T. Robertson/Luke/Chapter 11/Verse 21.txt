<p><b> Fully armed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθωπλισμενος</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθοπλιζω</span></span>, an old verb, but here only in the N.T. Note perfective use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span> in composition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπλιζω</span></span>, to arm (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπλα</span></span>, arms). Note indefinite temporal clause (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν</span></span> and present subjunctive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυλασση</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>His own court </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την εαυτου αυλην</span></span>). His own homestead. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:27">Mark 3:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:29">Matthew 12:29</span> has "house" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικιαν</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αυλη</span></span> is used in the N.T. in various senses (the court in front of the house, the court around which the house is built, then the house as a whole).</p>
<p><b>His goods </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα υπαρχοντα αυτου</span></span>). "His belongings." Neuter plural present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπαρχω</span></span> used as substantive with genitive. </p>
