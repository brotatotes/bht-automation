<p><b> But when </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαν δε</span></span>). Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:21">Luke 11:21</span>.</p>
<p><b>Stronger than he </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ισχυροτερος αυτου</span></span>). Comparative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ισχυρος</span></span> followed by the ablative.</p>
<p><b>Come upon him and overcome him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επελθων νικηση αυτον</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επερχομα</span></span> and first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νικαω</span></span>. Aorist tense here because a single onset while in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:22">Luke 11:22</span> the guarding (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυλασση</span></span>, present active subjunctive) is continuous.</p>
<p><b>His whole armour </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την πανοπλιαν αυτου</span></span>). An old and common word for all the soldier's outfit (shield, sword, lance, helmet, greaves, breastplate). Tyndale renders it "his harness." In the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:11">Ephesians 6:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:13">Ephesians 6:13</span> where the items are given.</p>
<p><b>Wherein he trusted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφ' η επεποιθε</span></span>). Second past perfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειθω</span></span>, to persuade. The second perfect <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεποιθα</span></span> is intransitive, to trust. Old and common verb. He trusted his weapons which had been so efficacious.</p>
<p><b>His spoils </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα σκυλα αυτου</span></span>). It is not clear to what this figure refers. Strong as Satan is Jesus is stronger and wins victories over him as he was doing then. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:15">Colossians 2:15</span> Christ is pictured as triumphing openly over the powers of evil by the Cross. </p>
