<p><b> And finding none </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα μη ευρισκον</span></span>). Here <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:43">Matthew 12:43</span> has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ουχ ευρισκε</span></span> (present active indicative instead of present active participle). <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:24-26">Luke 11:24-26</span> is almost verbatim like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:43-45">Matthew 12:43-45</span>, which see. Instead of just "taketh" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραλαμβανε</span></span>) in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:26">Luke 11:26</span>, Matthew has "taketh with himself" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραλαμβανε μεθ' εαυτου</span></span>). And Luke omits: "Even so shall it be also unto this evil generation" of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:45">Matthew 12:45</span>.</p>
<p><b>Than the first </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των πρωτων</span></span>). Ablative case after the comparative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χειρονα</span></span>. The seven demons brought back remind one of the seven that afflicted Mary Magdalene (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:2">Luke 8:2</span>). </p>
