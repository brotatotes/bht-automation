<p><b> As he said these things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω λεγειν αυτον</span></span>). Luke's common idiom, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> with articular infinitive. Verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:27">Luke 11:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:28">Luke 11:28</span> are peculiar to Luke. His Gospel in a special sense is the Gospel of Woman. This woman "speaks well, but womanly" (Bengel). Her beatitude (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακαρια</span></span>) reminds us of Elisabeth's words (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:42">Luke 1:42</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλογημενη</span></span>). She is fulfilling Mary's own prophecy in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:48">Luke 1:48</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακαριουσιν με</span></span>, shall call me happy). </p>
