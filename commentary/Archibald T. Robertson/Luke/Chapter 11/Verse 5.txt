<p><b> At midnight </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεσονυκτιου</span></span>). Genitive of time.</p>
<p><b>And say to him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ειπη αυτω</span></span>). This is the deliberative subjunctive, but it is preceded by two future indicatives that are deliberative also (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξει, πορευσετα</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Lend me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρησον μο</span></span>). First aorist active imperative second singular. Lend me</p>
<p><b>now </b>. From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κιχρημ</span></span>, an old verb, to lend as a matter of friendly interest as opposed to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δανειζω</span></span>, to lend on interest as a business. Only here in the N.T. </p>
