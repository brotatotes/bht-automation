<p><b> That ... may be required </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα ... εκζητηθη</span></span>). Divinely ordered sequence, first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκζητεω</span></span>, a late and rare verb outside of LXX and N.T., requiring as a debt the blood of the prophets.</p>
<p><b>Which was shed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το εκκεχυμενον</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκχεω</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκχυννω</span></span> (an Aeolic form appearing in the margin of Westcott and Hort here, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκχυννομενον</span></span>, present passive participle). If the present passive is accepted, it means the blood which is perpetually shed from time to time.</p>
<p><b>From the foundation of the world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο καταβολης κοσμου</span></span>). See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:34">Matthew 25:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:24">John 17:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:4">Ephesians 1:4</span>, etc. It is a bold metaphor for the purpose of God. </p>
