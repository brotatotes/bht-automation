<p><b> And he </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακεινος</span></span>). Emphatic.</p>
<p><b>Shall say </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειπη</span></span>). Still the aorist active deliberative subjunctive as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:5">Luke 11:5</span> (the same long and somewhat involved sentence).</p>
<p><b>Trouble me not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη μο κοπους παρεχε</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μη</span></span> and the present imperative active. Literally, "Stop furnishing troubles to me." On this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοπους παρεχω</span></span> see also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:10">Matthew 26:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:6">Mark 14:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:17">Galatians 6:17</span> and the singular <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοπον</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:5">Luke 18:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>The door is now shut </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηδη η θυρα κεκλειστα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative, shut to stay shut. Oriental locks are not easy to unlock. From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλειω</span></span>, common verb.</p>
<p><b>In bed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τεν κοιτην</span></span>). Note use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> in sense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span>. Often a whole family would sleep in the same room.</p>
<p><b>I cannot </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου δυναμα</span></span>). That is, I am not willing. </p>
