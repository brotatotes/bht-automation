<p><b> Be not anxious </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη μεριμνησητε</span></span>). First aorist active subjunctive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> in prohibition. Do not become anxious. See a similar command to the Twelve on their Galilean tour (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:19">Matthew 10:19</span>) and in the great discourse on the Mount of Olives at the end (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:11">Mark 13:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:14">Luke 21:14</span>), given twice by Luke as we see.</p>
<p><b>How or what ye shall answer </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πως η τ απολογησησθε</span></span>). Indirect question and retaining the deliberative subjunctive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολογησησθε</span></span> and also <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειπητε</span></span> (say). </p>
