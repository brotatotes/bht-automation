<p><b> I will pull down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθελω</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθαιρεω</span></span>, an old verb, the usual future being <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθαιρησω</span></span>. This second form from the second aorist <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθειλον</span></span> (from obsolete <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελω</span></span>) like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφελε</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:19">Revelation 22:19</span>.</p>
<p><b>My barns </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μου τας αποθηκας</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποτιθημ</span></span>, to lay by, to treasure. So a granary or storehouse, an old word, six times in the N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:12">Matthew 3:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:26">Matthew 6:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:30">Matthew 13:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:17">Luke 3:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:18">Luke 12:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:24">Luke 12:24</span>).</p>
<p><b>All my corn </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντα τον σιτον</span></span>). Better grain (wheat, barley), not maize or Indian corn.</p>
<p><b>My goods </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα αγαθα μου</span></span>). Like the English, my good things. So the English speak of goods (freight) train. </p>
