<p><b> Thou foolish one </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφρων</span></span>). Fool, for lack of sense (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φρην</span></span>, sense) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:40">Luke 11:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:19">2 Corinthians 11:19</span>. Old word, used by Socrates in Xenophon. Nominative form as vocative.</p>
<p><b>Is thy soul required of thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την ψυχην σου αιτουσιν απο σου</span></span>). Plural active present, not passive: "They are demanding thy soul from thee." The impersonal plural (aitousin) is common enough (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:38">Luke 6:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:11">Luke 12:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:9">Luke 16:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:31">Luke 23:31</span>). The rabbis used "they" to avoid saying "God." </p>
