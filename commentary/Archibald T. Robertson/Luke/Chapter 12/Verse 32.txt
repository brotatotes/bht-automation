<p><b> Little flock </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το μικρον ποιμνιον</span></span>). Vocative with the article as used in Hebrew and often in the <i>Koine</i> and so in the N.T. See both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πατερ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο πατηρ</span></span> in the vocative in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:21">Luke 10:21</span>. See Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, pp. 465f. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ποιμνιον</span></span> (flock) is a contraction from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιμενιον</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιμην</span></span> (shepherd) instead of the usual <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιμνη</span></span> (flock). So it is not a diminutive and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μικρον</span></span> is not superfluous, though it is pathetic.</p>
<p><b>For it is your Father's good pleasure </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ ευδοκησεν ο πατηρ υμων</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευδοκεω</span></span>. Timeless aorist as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:22">Luke 3:22</span>. This verse has no parallel in Matthew. </p>
