<p><b> Who then </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τις αρα</span></span>). Jesus introduces this parable of the wise steward (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:42-48">Luke 12:42-48</span>) by a rhetorical question that answers itself. Peter is this wise steward, each of the Twelve is, anyone is who acts thus.</p>
<p><b>The faithful and wise steward </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο πιστος οικονομος ο φρονιμος</span></span>). The faithful steward, the wise one. A steward is house manager (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικοσ, νεμω</span></span>, to manage). Each man is a steward in his own responsibilities.</p>
<p><b>Household </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεραπειας</span></span>). Literally, service from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεραπευω</span></span>. medical service as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:11">Luke 9:11</span>, by metonymy household (a body of those domestics who serve).</p>
<p><b>Their portion of food </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το σιτομετριον</span></span>). Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σιτομετρεω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+47:12">Genesis 47:12</span>) for the Attic <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον σιτον μετρεω</span></span>, to measure the food, the rations. Here only in the N.T. or anywhere else till Deissmann (<i>Bible Studies</i>, p. 158) found it in an Egyptian papyrus and then an inscription in Lycia (<i>Light from the Ancient East</i>, p. 104). </p>
