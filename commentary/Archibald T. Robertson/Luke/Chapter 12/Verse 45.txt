<p><b> Shall say </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειπη</span></span>). Second aorist subjunctive, with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span>, condition of the third class, undetermined, but with prospect of being determined.</p>
<p><b>Delayeth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρονιζε</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρονος</span></span>, time, spends time, lingers.</p>
<p><b>Shall begin </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρξητα</span></span>). First aorist middle subjunctive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and the same condition as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειπη</span></span>, above.</p>
<p><b>The menservants </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους παιδας</span></span>)</p>
<p><b>and the maidservants </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα τας παιδισκας</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Παιδισκη</span></span> is a diminutive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παις</span></span> for a young female slave and occurs in the papyri, orginally just a damsel. Here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παις</span></span> can mean slave also though strictly just a boy. </p>
