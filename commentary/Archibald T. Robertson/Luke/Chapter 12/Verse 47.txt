<p><b> Which knew </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο γνους</span></span>). Articular participle (second aorist active, punctiliar and timeless). The one who knows. So as to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη ετοιμασας η ποιησας</span></span> (does not make ready or do).</p>
<p><b>Shall be beaten with many stripes </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δαρησετα πολλας</span></span>). Second future passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δερω</span></span>, to skin, to beat, to flay (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:35">Matthew 21:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:3">Mark 12:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:5">Mark 12:5</span>). The passive voice retains here the accusative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολλας</span></span> (supply <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληγας</span></span>, present in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:30">Luke 10:30</span>). The same explanation applies to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ολιγας</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:48">Luke 12:48</span>. </p>
