<p><b> Whom ye shall fear </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τινα φοβηθητε</span></span>). First aorist passive subjunctive deliberative retained in the indirect question. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Τινα</span></span> is the accusative, the direct object of this transitive passive verb (note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:4">Luke 12:4</span>).</p>
<p><b>Fear him who </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φοβηθητε τον</span></span>). First aorist passive imperative, differing from the preceding form only in the accent and governing the accusative also.</p>
<p><b>After he hath killed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα το αποκτεινα</span></span>). Preposition <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα</span></span> with the articular infinitive. Literally, "After the killing" (first aorist active infinitive of the common verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκτεινω</span></span>, to kill.</p>
<p><b>Into hell </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις την γεενναν</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:22">Matthew 5:22</span>. Gehenna is a transliteration of <i>Ge-Hinnom</i>, Valley of Hinnon where the children were thrown on to the red-hot arms of Molech. Josiah (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ki+23:10">2 Kings 23:10</span>) abolished these abominations and then it was a place for all kinds of refuse which burned ceaselessly and became a symbol of punishment in the other world.</p>
<p><b>This one fear </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτον φοβηθητε</span></span>). As above. </p>
