<p><b> Give diligence to be quit of him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δος εργασιαν απηλλαχθα απ' αυτου</span></span>). Second aorist active imperative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δος</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Απηλλαχθα</span></span>, perfect passive infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαλλασσω</span></span> an old verb common, but only twice in the N.T. (here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:12">Acts 19:12</span>). Used here in a legal sense and the tense emphasizes a state of completion, to be rid of him for good.</p>
<p><b>Hale thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατασυρη</span></span>). Drag down forcibly, old verb, only here in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>To the officer </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω πρακτορ</span></span>). The doer, the proctor, the exactor of fines, the executor of punishment. Old word, only here in the N.T. </p>
