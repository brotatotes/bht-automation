<p><b> Everyone who shall confess me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πας ος αν ομολογησε εν εμο</span></span>). Just like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:32">Matthew 10:32</span> except the use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span> here which adds nothing. The Hebraistic use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομολογεω</span></span> both here and in Matthew is admitted by even Moulton (<i>Prolegomena</i>, p. 104).</p>
<p><b>The Son of man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο υιος του ανθρωπου</span></span>). Here <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:32">Matthew 10:32</span> has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κ'αγω</span></span> (I also) as the equivalent. </p>
