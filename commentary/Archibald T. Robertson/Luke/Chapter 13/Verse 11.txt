<p><b> A spirit of infirmity </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα ασθενειας</span></span>). A spirit that caused the weakness (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασθενειας</span></span>, lack of strength) like a spirit of bondage (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:15">Romans 8:15</span>), genitive case.</p>
<p><b>She was bowed together </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην συνκυπτουσα</span></span>). Periphrastic imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνκυπτω</span></span>, old verb, here only in the N.T., to bend together, medical word for curvature of the spine.</p>
<p><b>And could in no wise lift herself up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα μη δυναμενη ανακυψα εις το παντελες</span></span>). Negative form of the previous statement. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ανακυψα</span></span>, first aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανακυπτω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανα, κυπτω</span></span>, same verb above compounded with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν</span></span>). Unable to bend herself up or back at all (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το παντελες</span></span>, wholly as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:25">Hebrews 7:25</span> only other passage in the N.T. where it occurs). The poor old woman had to come in all bent over. </p>
