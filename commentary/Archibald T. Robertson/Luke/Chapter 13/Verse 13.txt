<p><b> He laid his hands upon her </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεθηκεν αυτη τας χειρας</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιτιθημ</span></span>. As the Great Physician with gentle kindness.</p>
<p><b>She was made straight </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανωρθωθη</span></span>). First aorist (effective) passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανορθοω</span></span>, old verb, but only three times in the N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:13">Luke 13:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:12">Hebrews 12:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:16">Acts 15:16</span>), to make straight again. Here it has the literal sense of making straight the old woman's crooked back.</p>
<p><b>She glorified God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδοξαζεν τον θεον</span></span>). Imperfect active. Began it (inchoative) and kept it up. </p>
