<p><b> The Lord answered him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεκριθη δε αυτω ο Κυριος</span></span>). Note use of "the Lord" of Jesus again in Luke's narrative. Jesus answered the ruler of the synagogue who had spoken to the crowd, but about Jesus. It was a crushing and overwhelming reply.</p>
<p><b>Hypocrites </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποκριτα</span></span>). This pretentious faultfinder and all who agree with him.</p>
<p><b>Each of you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκαστος υμων</span></span>). An <i>argumentum ad hominen</i>. These very critics of Jesus cared too much for an ox or an ass to leave it all the sabbath without water.</p>
<p><b>Stall </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φατνης</span></span>). Old word, in the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:7">Luke 2:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:12">Luke 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:16">Luke 2:16</span> the manger where the infant Jesus was placed.</p>
<p><b>To watering </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποτιζε</span></span>). Old verb, causative, to give to drink. </p>
