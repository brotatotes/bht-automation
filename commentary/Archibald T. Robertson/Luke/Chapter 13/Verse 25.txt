<p><b> When once </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφ' ου αν</span></span>). Possibly to be connected without break with the preceding verse (so Westcott and Hort), though Bruce argues for two parables here, the former (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:24">Luke 13:24</span>) about being in earnest, while this one (verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:25-30">Luke 13:25-30</span>) about not being too late. The two points are here undoubtedly. It is an awkward construction, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφ' ου = απο τουτου οτε</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span> and the aorist subjunctive (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγερθη</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκλειση</span></span>). See Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 978.</p>
<p><b>Hath shut to </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκλειση</span></span>), first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκλειω</span></span>, old verb, but only here in the N.T. Note effective aorist tense and perfective use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span>, slammed the door fast.</p>
<p><b>And ye begin </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα αρξησθε</span></span>). First aorist middle subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρχομα</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφ' ου αν</span></span> like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγερθη</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκλειση</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>To stand </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστανα</span></span>). Second perfect active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span>, intransitive tense</p>
<p><b>and to knock </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα κρουειν</span></span>). Present active infinitive, to keep on knocking.</p>
<p><b>Open to us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανοιξον ημιν</span></span>). First aorist active imperative, at once and urgent.</p>
<p><b>He shall say </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερε</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειπον</span></span> (defective verb). This is probably the apodosis of the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφ' ου</span></span> clause. </p>
