<p><b> I know not whence ye are </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ οιδα ποθεν εστε</span></span>). This blunt statement cuts the matter short and sweeps away the flimsy cobwebs. Acquaintance with Christ in the flesh does not open the door. Jesus quotes <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+8:9">Psalms 8:9</span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:23">Matthew 7:23</span>, there as in the LXX, here with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντες εργατα αδικιας</span></span>, there with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο εργαζομενο την ανομιαν</span></span>. But <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστητε</span></span> (second aorist active imperative) here, and there <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποχωρειτε</span></span> (present active imperative). </p>
