<p><b> There </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκε</span></span>). Out there, outside the house whence they are driven.</p>
<p><b>When ye shall see </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν οψησθε</span></span>). First aorist middle subjunctive (of a late aorist <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωψαμην</span></span>) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span>, though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οψεσθε</span></span> (future middle) in margin of Westcott and Hort, unless we admit here a "future" subjunctive like Byzantine Greek (after Latin).</p>
<p><b>And yourselves cast forth without </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμας δε εκβαλλομενους εξω</span></span>). Present passive participle, continuous action, "you being cast out" with the door shut. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:11">Matthew 8:11</span> for this same picture. </p>
