<p><b> Sit down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπεσε</span></span>). Second aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπιπτω</span></span>, to fall up or back, to lie back or down. Late Greek word for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανακλινω</span></span> (cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατακλινω</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:8">Luke 14:8</span>).</p>
<p><b>He that hath bidden thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο κεκληκως σε</span></span>). Perfect active participle as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:12">Luke 14:12</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω κεκληκοτ</span></span>) with which compare <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο καλεσας</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:9">Luke 14:9</span> (first aorist active participle).</p>
<p><b>He may say </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερε</span></span>). The future indicative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> does occur in the <i>Koine</i> (papyri) and so in the N.T. (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 984).</p>
<p><b>Go up higher </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσαναβηθ</span></span>). Second aorist active imperative second singular of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσαναβαινω</span></span>, an old double compound verb, but here only in the N.T. Probably, "Come up higher," because the call comes from the host and because of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς</span></span>. </p>
