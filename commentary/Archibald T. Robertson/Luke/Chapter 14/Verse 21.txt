<p><b> Being angry </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οργισθεις</span></span>). First aorist (ingressive) passive, becoming angry.</p>
<p><b>Quickly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταχεως</span></span>). The dinner is ready and no time is to be lost. The invitation goes still to those in the city.</p>
<p><b>Streets and lanes </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τας πλατειας κα ρυμας</span></span>). Broadways and runways (broad streets and narrow lanes).</p>
<p><b>Maimed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπειρους</span></span>). So Westcott and Hort for the old word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπηρους</span></span>, due to itacism (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ει=η</span></span> in pronunciation). The word is compounded of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανα</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πηρος</span></span>, lame all the way up. </p>
