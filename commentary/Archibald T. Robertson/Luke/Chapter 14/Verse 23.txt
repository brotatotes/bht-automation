<p><b> The highways and hedges </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τας οδους κα φραγμους</span></span>). The public roads outside the city of Judaism just as the streets and lanes were inside the city. The heathen are to be invited this time.</p>
<p><b>Hedges </b> is fenced in places from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φρασσω</span></span>, to fence in (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:19">Romans 3:19</span>).</p>
<p><b>Compel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγκασον</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγκαζω</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγκη</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:18">Luke 14:18</span>). By persuasion of course. There is no thought of compulsory salvation. "Not to use force, but to constrain them against the reluctance which such poor creatures would feel at accepting the invitation of a great lord" (Vincent). As examples of such "constraint" in this verb see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:22">Matthew 14:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:11">Acts 26:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:12">Galatians 6:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>That my house may be filled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα γεμισθη μου ο οικος</span></span>). First aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεμιζω</span></span>, to fill full, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεμω</span></span>, to be full. Effective aorist. Subjunctive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> in final clause. The Gentiles are to take the place that the Jews might have had (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:25">Romans 11:25</span>). Bengel says: <i>Nec natura nec gratia patitur vacuum</i>. </p>
