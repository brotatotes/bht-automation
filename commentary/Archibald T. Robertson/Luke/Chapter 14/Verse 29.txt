<p><b> Lest haply </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μηποτε</span></span>). Double final particles (positive and negative with addition of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποτε</span></span>). Used here with aorist middle subjunctive in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρξωντα</span></span> (begin).</p>
<p><b>When he hath laid ... and was not able </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεντος αυτου ... κα μη ισχυοντος</span></span>)</p>
<p><b>to finish </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκτελεσα</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive. Note perfective use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span>, to finish out to the end. Two genitive absolutes, first, second aorist active participle <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεντος</span></span>; second, present active participle <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ισχυοντος</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>To mock him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτω εμπαιζειν</span></span>). An old verb, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμ-παιζω</span></span>, to play like a child (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παις</span></span>), at or with, to mock, scoff at, to trifle with like Latin <i>illudere</i>. </p>
