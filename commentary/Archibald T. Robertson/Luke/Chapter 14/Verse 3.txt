<p><b> Answering </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκριθεις</span></span>). First aorist passive participle without the passive meaning. Jesus answered the thoughts of those mentioned in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:1">Luke 14:1</span>. Here "lawyers and Pharisees" are treated as one class with one article (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους</span></span>) whereas in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:30">Luke 7:30</span> they are treated as two classes with separate articles.</p>
<p><b>Or not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η ου</span></span>). The dilemma forestalled any question by them.</p>
<p><b>They held their peace </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ησυχασαν</span></span>). Ingressive aorist active of old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ησυχαζω</span></span>. They became silent, more so than before. </p>
