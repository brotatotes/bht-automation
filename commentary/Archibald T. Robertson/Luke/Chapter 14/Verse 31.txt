<p><b> To encounter </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνβαλειν</span></span>). Second aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνβαλλω</span></span>, old and common verb, to throw or bring together, to dispute, to clash in war as here.</p>
<p><b>Another king </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετερω βασιλε</span></span>), to grapple with another king in war or for war (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις πολεμον</span></span>). Associative instrumental case.</p>
<p><b>Take counsel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βουλευσετα</span></span>). Future middle indicative of old and common verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βουλευω</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βουλη</span></span>, will, counsel. The middle means to take counsel with oneself, to deliberate, to ponder.</p>
<p><b>With ten thousand </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν δεκα χιλιασιν</span></span>). Literally, in ten thousand. See this so-called instrumental use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:14">Jude 1:14</span>. Equipped in or with ten thousand. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:17">Luke 1:17</span>. Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα εικοσ χιλιαδων</span></span> just below (midst of twenty thousand).</p>
<p><b>To meet </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπαντησα</span></span>). Common verb (like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απανταω</span></span>) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανταω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντα</span></span>, end, face to face, from which <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντ</span></span>) with preposition <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο</span></span> (or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span>), to go to meet. Here it has a military meaning. </p>
