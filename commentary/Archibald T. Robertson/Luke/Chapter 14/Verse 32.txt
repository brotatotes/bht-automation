<p><b> Or else </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε δε μηγε</span></span>). Same idiom in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:36">Luke 5:36</span>. Luke is fond of this formula.</p>
<p><b>An ambassage </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρεσβειαν</span></span>). Old and common word for the office of ambassador, composed of old men (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρεσβεις</span></span>) like Japanese Elder Statesmen who are supposed to possess wisdom. In the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:14">Luke 19:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>Asketh conditions of peace </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερωτα προς ειρηνην</span></span>). The use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερωταω</span></span> in this sense of beg or petition is common in the papyri and <i>Koine</i> generally. The original use of asking a question survives also. The text is uncertain concerning <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς ειρηνην</span></span> which means with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερωταω</span></span>, to ask negotiations for peace. In B we have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς</span></span> like verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:28">Luke 14:28</span>. Most MSS. have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα</span></span> before <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span>, but not in Aleph and B. It is possible that the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα</span></span> was omitted because of preceding <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοεοτελευτον</span></span>), but the sense is the same. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:19">Romans 14:19</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα της ειρηνης</span></span>, the things of peace, which concern or look towards peace, the preliminaries of peace. </p>
