<p><b> A parable for those which were bidden </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς τους κεκλημενους παραβολην</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλεω</span></span>, to call, to invite. This parable is for the guests who were there and who had been watching Jesus.</p>
<p><b>When he marked </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεχων</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεχω</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον νουν</span></span> understood, holding the mind upon them, old verb and common.</p>
<p><b>They chose out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξελεγοντο</span></span>). Imperfect middle, were picking out for themselves.</p>
<p><b>The chief seats </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τας πρωτοκλισιας</span></span>). The first reclining places at the table. Jesus condemned the Pharisees later for this very thing (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:6">Matthew 23:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:39">Mark 12:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:46">Luke 20:46</span>). On a couch holding three the middle place was the chief one. At banquets today the name of the guests are usually placed at the plates. The place next to the host on the right was then, as now, the post of honour. </p>
