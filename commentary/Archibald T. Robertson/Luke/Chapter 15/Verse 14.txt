<p><b> When he had spent </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δαπανησαντος αυτου</span></span>). Genitive absolute. The verb is here used in a bad sense as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:3">James 4:3</span>. See on <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δαπανη</span></span> <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:28">Luke 14:28</span>.</p>
<p><b>He </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτος</span></span>). Emphasis.</p>
<p><b>To be in want </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υστερεισθα</span></span>). The verb is from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υστερος</span></span>, behind or later (comparative). We use "fall behind" (Vincent) of one in straitened circumstances. Plummer notes the coincidences of Providence. The very land was in a famine when the boy had spent all. </p>
