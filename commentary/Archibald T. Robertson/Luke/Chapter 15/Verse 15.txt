<p><b> Joined himself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκολληθη</span></span>). First aorist passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολλαω</span></span>, an old verb to glue together, to cleave to. In the N.T. only the passive occurs. He was glued to, was joined to. It is not necessary to take this passive in the middle reflexive sense.</p>
<p><b>The citizens </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των πολιτων</span></span>). Curiously enough this common word citizen (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολιτης</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολις</span></span>, city) is found in the N.T. only in Luke's writings (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:15">Luke 15:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:14">Luke 19:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:39">Acts 21:39</span>) except in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:11">Hebrews 8:11</span> where it is quoted from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+38:34">Jeremiah 38:34</span>.</p>
<p><b>To feed swine </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βοσκειν χοιρους</span></span>). A most degrading occupation for anyone and for a Jew an unspeakable degradation. </p>
