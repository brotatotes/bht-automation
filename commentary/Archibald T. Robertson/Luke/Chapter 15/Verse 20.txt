<p><b> To his father </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς τον πατερα εαυτου</span></span>). Literally, to his own father. He acted at once on his decision.</p>
<p><b>Yet afar off </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετ αυτου μακραν απεχοντος</span></span>). Genitive absolute. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μακραν</span></span> agrees with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδον</span></span> understood: While he was yet holding off a distant way. This shows that the father had been looking for him to come back and was even looking at this very moment as he came in sight.</p>
<p><b>Ran </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δραμων</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of the defective verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρεχω</span></span>. The eager look and longing of the father.</p>
<p><b>Kissed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεφιλησεν</span></span>). Note perfective use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span> kissed him much, kissed him again and again. The verb occurs so in the older Greek. </p>
