<p><b> The fatted calf </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον μοσχον τον σιτευτον</span></span>). The calf the fatted one. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Σιτευτον</span></span> is the verbal adjective of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σιλευω</span></span>, to feed with wheat (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σιτος</span></span>). The calf was kept fat for festive occasions, possibly in the hope of the son's return.</p>
<p><b>Kill </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυσατε</span></span>). Not as a sacrifice, but for the feast.</p>
<p><b>Make merry </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευφρανθωμεν</span></span>). First aorist passive subjunctive (volitive). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευφραινω</span></span>, an old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ</span></span> (well) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φρην</span></span> (mind). </p>
