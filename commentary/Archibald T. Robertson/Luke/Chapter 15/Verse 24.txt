<p><b> And is alive </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ανεζησεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναζαω</span></span>, to live again. Literally, he was dead and he came back to life.</p>
<p><b>He was lost </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην απολωλως</span></span>, periphrastic past perfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολλυμ</span></span> and intransitive, in a lost state) and he was found (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρεθη</span></span>). He was found, we have to say, but this aorist passive is really timeless, he is found after long waiting (effective aorist) The artists have vied with each other in picturing various items connected with this wonderful parable. </p>
