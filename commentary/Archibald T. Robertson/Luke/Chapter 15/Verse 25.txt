<p><b> As he came and drew nigh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως ερχομενος ηγγισεν</span></span>). More exactly, "As, coming, he drew nigh," for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομενος</span></span> is present middle participle and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγγισεν</span></span> is aorist active indicative.</p>
<p><b>Music </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμφωνιας</span></span>). Our word "symphony." An old Greek word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμφωνος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν</span></span>, together, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωνη</span></span>, voice or sound),</p>
<p><b>harmony, concord </b>, by a band of musicians. Here alone in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>And dancing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα χορων</span></span>). An old word again, but here alone in the N.T. Origin uncertain, possibly from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορχος</span></span> by metathesis (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορχεομα</span></span>, to dance). A circular dance on the green. </p>
