<p><b> Do I serve thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δουλευω σο</span></span>). Progressive present tense of this old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δουλος</span></span> (slave) which the elder son uses to picture his virtual slavery in staying at home and perhaps with longings to follow the younger son (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 879).</p>
<p><b>Transgressed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρηλθον</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρερχομα</span></span>, to pass by. Not even once (aorist) in contrast with so many years of service (linear present).</p>
<p><b>A kid </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εριφον</span></span>). Some MSS. have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εριφιον</span></span>, diminutive, a little kid. So margin of Westcott and Hort. B has it also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:32">Matthew 25:32</span>, the only other N.T. passage where the word occurs.</p>
<p><b>That I might make merry </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα ευφρανθω</span></span>). Final clause, first aorist passive subjunctive of the same verb used in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:23">Luke 15:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:25">Luke 15:25</span>. </p>
