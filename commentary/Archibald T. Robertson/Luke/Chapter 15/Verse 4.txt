<p><b> In the wilderness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη ερημω</span></span>). Their usual pasturage, not a place of danger or peril. It is the owner of the hundred sheep who cares so much for the one that is lost. He knows each one of the sheep and loves each one.</p>
<p><b>Go after that which is lost </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορευετα επ το απολωλος</span></span>). The one lost sheep (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολωλος</span></span>, second perfect active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολλυμ</span></span>, to destroy, but intransitive, to be lost). There is nothing more helpless than a lost sheep except a lost sinner. The sheep went off by its own ignorance and folly. The use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> for the goal occurs also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:9">Matthew 22:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:26">Acts 8:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:11">Acts 9:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>Until he find it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως ευρη αυτο</span></span>). Second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρισκω</span></span>, common verb, with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως</span></span>, common Greek idiom. He keeps on going (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορευετα</span></span>, linear present middle indicative) until success comes (effective aorist, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρη</span></span>). </p>
