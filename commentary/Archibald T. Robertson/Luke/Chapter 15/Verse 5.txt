<p><b> On his shoulders </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ τους ωμους αυτου</span></span>). He does it himself in exuberant affection and of necessity as the poor lost sheep is helpless. Note the plural shoulders showing that the sheep was just back of the shepherd's neck and drawn around by both hands. The word for shoulder (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωμος</span></span>) is old and common, but in the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:4">Matthew 23:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>Rejoicing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαιρων</span></span>). "There is no upbraiding of the wandering sheep, nor murmuring at the trouble" (Plummer). </p>
