<p><b> Rejoice with me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνχαρητε μο</span></span>). Second aorist passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνχαιρω</span></span>, an old and common verb for mutual joy as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:17">Philippians 2:17</span>. Joy demands fellowship. Same form in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:9">Luke 15:9</span>. So the shepherd</p>
<p><b>calls together </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνκαλε</span></span>, note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν</span></span> again) both his friends and his neighbours. This picture of the Good Shepherd has captured the eye of many artists through the ages. </p>
