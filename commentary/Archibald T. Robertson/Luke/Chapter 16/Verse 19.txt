<p><b> He was clothed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεδιδυσκετο</span></span>). Imperfect middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδιδυσκω</span></span>, a late intensive form of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδυω</span></span>. He clothed himself in or with. It was his habit.</p>
<p><b>Purple </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορφυραν</span></span>). This purple dye was obtained from the purple fish, a species of mussel or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυρεξ</span></span> (1Macc. 4:23). It was very costly and was used for the upper garment by the wealthy and princes (royal purple). They had three shades of purple (deep violet, deep scarlet or crimson, deep blue). See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:17">Mark 15:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:20">Mark 15:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:12">Revelation 18:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>Fine linen </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βυσσον</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Byssus </b> or Egyptian flax (India and Achaia also). It is a yellowed flax from which fine linen was made for undergarments. It was used for wrapping mummies. "Some of the Egyptian linen was so fine that it was called <i>woven air</i>" (Vincent). Here only in the N.T. for the adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βυσσινος</span></span> occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:12">Revelation 18:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:8">Revelation 19:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:14">Revelation 19:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>Faring sumptuously </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευφραινομενος λαμπρως</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Making merry brilliantly </b>. The verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευφραινομα</span></span> we have already had in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:19">Luke 12:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:23">Luke 15:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:25">Luke 15:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:32">Luke 15:32</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Λαμπρως</span></span> is an old adverb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμπρος</span></span>, brilliant, shining, splendid, magnificent. It occurs here only in the N.T. This parable apparently was meant for the Pharisees (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:14">Luke 16:14</span>) who were lovers of money. It shows the wrong use of money and opportunity. </p>
