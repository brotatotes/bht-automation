<p><b> In Hades </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω Hαιδη</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:18">Matthew 16:18</span> for discussion of this word. Lazarus was in Hades also for both Paradise (Abraham's bosom) and Gehenna are in the unseen world beyond the grave.</p>
<p><b>In torments </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν βασανοις</span></span>). The touchstone by which gold and other metals were tested, then the rack for torturing people. Old word, but in the N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:28">Luke 16:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:24">Matthew 4:24</span>.</p>
<p><b>Sees </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορα</span></span>). Dramatic present indicative. The Jews believed that Gehenna and Paradise were close together. This detail in the parable does not demand that we believe it. The picture calls for it.</p>
<p><b>From afar </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο μακροθεν</span></span>). Pleonastic use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span> as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακροθεν</span></span> means</p>
<p><b>from afar </b>. </p>
