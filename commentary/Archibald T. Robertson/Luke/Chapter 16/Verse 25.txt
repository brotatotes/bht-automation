<p><b> Receivedst </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απελαβες</span></span>). Second aorist indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολαμβανω</span></span>, old verb to get back what is promised and in full. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:34">Luke 6:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:30">Luke 18:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:41">Luke 23:41</span>.</p>
<p><b>Evil things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα κακα</span></span>). Not "his," but "the evil things" that came upon him.</p>
<p><b>Thou art in anguish </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδυνασα</span></span>). Like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καυχασα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:17">Romans 2:17</span>. They contracted <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-αεσα</span></span> without the loss of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ς</span></span>. Common in the <i>Koine</i>. </p>
