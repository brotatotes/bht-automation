<p><b> I am resolved </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγνων</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span>. A difficult tense to reproduce in English. I knew, I know, I have known, all miss it a bit. It is a burst of daylight to the puzzled, darkened man: I've got it, I see into it now, a sudden solution.</p>
<p><b>What to do </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τ ποιησω</span></span>). Either deliberative first aorist active subjunctive or deliberative future active indicative.</p>
<p><b>When I am put out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν μετασταθω</span></span>). First aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεθιστημ</span></span>, (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα, ιστημ</span></span>), old verb, to transpose, transfer, remove. He is expecting to be put out.</p>
<p><b>They may receive me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεξωντα</span></span>). First aorist middle subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεχομα</span></span>, common verb. Subjunctive with final particle <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>. He wishes to put the debtors under obligation to himself.</p>
<p><b>Debtors </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των χρεοφιλετων</span></span>). A late word. In the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:41">Luke 7:41</span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρεος</span></span>, loan, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφειλετης</span></span>, debtor. It is probable that he dealt with "each one" separately. </p>
