<p><b> Let him not go down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη καταβατω</span></span>). Second aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταβαινω</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> in a prohibition in the third person singular. The usual idiom here would be <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> and the aorist subjunctive. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:15">Mark 13:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:17">Matthew 24:17</span> when these words occur in the great eschatological discussion concerning flight before the destruction of Jerusalem. Here the application is "absolute indifference to all worldly interests as the attitude of readiness for the Son of Man" (Plummer). </p>
