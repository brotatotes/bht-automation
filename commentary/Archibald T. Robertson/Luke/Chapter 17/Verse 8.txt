<p><b> And will not rather say </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ' ουκ ερε</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>But will not say? </b> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ουκ</span></span> in a question expects the affirmative answer.</p>
<p><b>Gird thyself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιζωσαμενος</span></span>). Direct middle first aorist participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιζωννυμ</span></span>, to gird around.</p>
<p><b>Till I have eaten and drunken </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως φαγω κα πιω</span></span>). More exactly, till I eat and drink. The second aorist subjunctives are not future perfects in any sense, simply punctiliar action, effective aorist.</p>
<p><b>Thou shalt eat and drink </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φαγεσα κα πιεσα</span></span>). Future middle indicative second person singular, the uncontracted forms <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-εσα</span></span> as often in the <i>Koine</i>. These futures are from the aorist stems <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφαγον</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιον</span></span> without <i>sigma</i>. </p>
