<p><b> To the end that </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς το δειν</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>With a view to the being necessary </b>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς</span></span> and the articular infinitive. The impersonal verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span> here is in the infinitive and has another infinitive loosely connected with it <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσευχεσθα</span></span>, to pray.</p>
<p><b>Not to faint </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη ενκακειν</span></span>). Literally, not to give in to evil (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν, κακεω</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακος</span></span>, bad or evil), to turn coward, lose heart, behave badly. A late verb used several times in the N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:1">2 Corinthians 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:16">2 Corinthians 4:16</span> etc.). </p>
