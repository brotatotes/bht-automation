<p><b> Stood </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σταθεις</span></span>). First aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span>. Struck an attitude ostentatiously where he could be seen. Standing was the common Jewish posture in prayer (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:5">Matthew 6:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:25">Mark 11:25</span>).</p>
<p><b>Prayed thus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταυτα προσηυχετο</span></span>). Imperfect middle, was praying these things (given following).</p>
<p><b>With himself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς εαυτον</span></span>). A soliloquy with his own soul, a complacent recital of his own virtues for his own self-satisfaction, not fellowship with God, though he addresses God.</p>
<p><b>I thank thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευχαριστω σο</span></span>). But his gratitude to God is for his own virtues, not for God's mercies to him. One of the rabbis offers a prayer like this of gratitude that he was in a class by himself because he was a Jew and not a Gentile, because he was a Pharisee and not of the <i>am-haaretz</i> or common people, because he was a man and not a woman.</p>
<p><b>Extortioners </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρπαγες</span></span>). An old word, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρπαξ</span></span> from same root as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρπαζω</span></span>, to plunder. An adjective of only one gender, used of robbers and plunderers, grafters, like the publicans (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:13">Luke 3:13</span>), whether wolves (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:15">Matthew 7:15</span>) or men (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:19">1 Corinthians 5:19</span>). The Pharisee cites the crimes of which he is not guilty.</p>
<p><b>Or even </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η κα</span></span>). As the climax of iniquity (Bruce), he points to "this publican." Zaccheus will admit robbery (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:8">Luke 19:8</span>).</p>
<p><b>God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο θεος</span></span>). Nominative form with the article as common with the vocative use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεος</span></span> (so verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:13">Luke 18:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:28">John 20:28</span>). </p>
