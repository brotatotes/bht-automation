 This man  (ουτος). This despised publican referred to contemptuously in verse Luke 18:11 as "this" (ουτος) publican.Rather than the other  (παρ' εκεινον). In comparison with (placed beside) that one. A neat Greek idiom after the perfect passive participle δεδικαιομενος.For  (οτ). This moral maxim Christ had already used in Luke 14:11. Plummer pertinently asks: "Why is it assumed that Jesus did not repeat his sayings?"
