<p><b> They brought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσεφερον</span></span>). Imperfect active, they were bringing. So <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:13">Mark 10:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Their babes </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα βρεφη</span></span>). Old word for</p>
<p><b>infants </b>. Here <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:13">Mark 10:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:13">Matthew 19:13</span> have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παιδια</span></span> (little children). Note "also" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span>) in Luke, not in Mark and Matthew.</p>
<p><b>That he should touch them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα αυτων απτητα</span></span>). Present middle subjunctive (linear action, repeatedly touch or one after the other), where <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:13">Mark 10:13</span> has aorist middle subjunctive (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αψητα</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Rebuked </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επετιμων</span></span>). Imperfect indicative active. Either inchoative began to rebuke, or continued, kept on rebuking. Matthew and Mark have the aorist <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επετιμησαν</span></span>. </p>
