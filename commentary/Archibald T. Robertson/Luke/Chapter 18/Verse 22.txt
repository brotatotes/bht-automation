<p><b> One thing thou lackest yet </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετ εν σο λειπε</span></span>). Literally, one thing still fails thee or is wanting to thee. An old verb with the dative of personal interest. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:21">Mark 10:21</span> has here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υστερε σε</span></span>, which see. It was an amazing compliment for one who was aiming at perfection (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:21">Matthew 19:21</span>). The youth evidently had great charm and was sincere in his claims.</p>
<p><b>Distribute </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαδος</span></span>). Second aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαδιδωμ</span></span> (give to various ones, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια-</span></span>). Here Mark and Matthew simply have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δος</span></span> (give). The rest the same in all three Gospels. </p>
