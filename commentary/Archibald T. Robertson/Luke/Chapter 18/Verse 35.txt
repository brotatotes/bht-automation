<p><b> Unto Jericho </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις Ιερειχω</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:29">Matthew 20:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:46">Mark 10:46</span>, for discussion of the two Jerichos in Mark and Matt. (the old and the new as here).</p>
<p><b>Begging </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαιτων</span></span>). Asking for something. He probably was by the wayside between the old Jericho and the new Roman Jericho. Mark gives his name Bartimaeus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:46">Luke 10:46</span>). <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:30">Matthew 20:30</span> mentions two. </p>
