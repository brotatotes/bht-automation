<p><b> He added and spake </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσθεις ειπεν</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προστιθημ</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειπεν</span></span>. It is a Hebrew idiom seen also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:1">Luke 20:1</span> he added to send (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσεθετο πεμψα</span></span>) and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:3">Acts 12:3</span> "he added to seize" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσεθετο συλλαβειν</span></span>). This undoubted Hebraism occurs in the N.T. in Luke only, probably due to the influence of the LXX on Luke the Greek Christian.</p>
<p><b>To appear </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναφαινεσθα</span></span>). Present passive infinitive of an old verb to be made manifest, to be shown up. In the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:3">Acts 21:3</span>. </p>
