<p><b> Trade ye herewith till I come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πραγματευσασθε εν ω ερχομα</span></span>). First aorist middle imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πραγματευομα</span></span>, an old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πραγμα</span></span>, business. Here only in the N.T. Westcott and Hort in their text read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πραγματευσασθα</span></span>, first aorist middle infinitive (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-α</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-ε</span></span> were pronounced alike). The infinitive makes it indirect discourse, the imperative direct.</p>
<p><b>While I am coming </b> is what <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ω ερχομα</span></span> really means. </p>
