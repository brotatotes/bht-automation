<p><b> When he was come back again </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω επανελθειν αυτον</span></span>). "On the coming back again as to him." Luke's favourite idiom of the articular infinitive after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> and with the accusative of general reference.</p>
<p><b>Had given </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεδωκε</span></span>). Past perfect active indicative without augment of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>That he might know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα γνο</span></span>). Second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινοσκω</span></span>. The optative would be <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνοιη</span></span>. </p>
