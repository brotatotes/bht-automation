<p><b> I feared </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφοβουμην</span></span>). Imperfect middle, I continued to fear.</p>
<p><b>Austere </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυστηρος</span></span>). Old Greek word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυω</span></span>, to dry up. Reproduced in Latin <i>austeros</i> and English <i>austere</i>. It means rough to the taste, stringent. Here only in the N.T. Compare <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκληρος</span></span> (hard) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:24">Matthew 25:24</span>. "Harsh in flavour, then in disposition" (Bruce).</p>
<p><b>Thou layedst not down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ εθηκας</span></span>). Probably a proverb for a grasping profiteer. </p>
