<p><b> Then wherefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα δια τ</span></span>). Note this inferential use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">και-</span></span> in that case.</p>
<p><b>Into the bank </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ τραπεζαν</span></span>). Literally,</p>
<p><b>upon a table </b>. This old word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τραπεζα</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τετραπεζα</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τετρα</span></span>, four, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πους</span></span>, foot). It means then any table (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:28">Mark 7:28</span>), food on the table (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:34">Acts 16:34</span>), feast or banquet (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:9">Romans 11:9</span>), table of the money-changers (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:15">John 2:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:15">Mark 11:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:12">Matthew 21:12</span>), or bank as here. Our word bank is from Old English <i>bench</i>.</p>
<p><b>With interest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν τοκω</span></span>). Not usury, but proper and legal interest. Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τικτω</span></span>, to bring forth. In the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:27">Matthew 25:27</span>.</p>
<p><b>Should have required it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν αυτο επραξα</span></span>). Conclusion of second-class condition the condition or apodosis being implied in the participle "coming" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελθων</span></span>), and the previous question. On this technical use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρασσω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επραξα</span></span>) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:13">Luke 3:13</span>. </p>
