<p><b> As they were loosing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυοντων αυτων</span></span>). Genitive absolute.</p>
<p><b>The owners thereof </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο κυριο αυτου</span></span>). The same word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυριος</span></span> used of the Lord Jesus in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:31">Luke 19:31</span> (and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:34">Luke 19:34</span>) and which these "owners" would understand. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:3">Matthew 21:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:3">Mark 11:3</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυριος</span></span> used by Jesus about himself with the expectation that these disciples would recognize him by that title as they did. The word in common use for the Roman emperor and in the LXX to translate the Hebrew <i>Elohim</i> (God). </p>
