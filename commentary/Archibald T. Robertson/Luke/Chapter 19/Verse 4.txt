<p><b> Ran on before </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προδραμων εις το εμπροσθεν</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προτρεχω</span></span> (defective verb). "Before" occurs twice (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προ-</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το εμπροσθεν</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Into a sycamore tree </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ συκομορεαν</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συκον</span></span>, fig, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μορον</span></span>, mulberry. The fig-mulberry and quite a different tree from the sycamine tree in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:6">Luke 17:6</span>, which see. It bore a poor fruit which poor people ate (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="am+7:14">Amos 7:14</span>). It was a wide open tree with low branches so that Zacchaeus could easily climb into it.</p>
<p><b>That way </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινης</span></span>). Feminine for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδος</span></span> (way) is understood. Genitive case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δ</span></span> in composition (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διερχεσθα</span></span>) or as an adverbial use. </p>
