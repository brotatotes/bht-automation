<p><b> Shall cast up a bank </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεμβαλουσιν χαρακα</span></span>). Future active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεμβαλλω</span></span>, a double compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα, εν, βαλλω</span></span>) of long usage, finally in a military sense of line of battle or in camp. Here alone in the N.T. So also the word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρακα</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαραξ</span></span>) for bank, stake, palisade, rampart, is here alone in the N.T., though common enough in the old Greek.</p>
<p><b>Compass thee round </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περικυκλωσουσιν σε</span></span>). Future active indicative. Another common compound to make a circle (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυκλος</span></span>) around (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ</span></span>), though here only in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>Keep thee in </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεξουσιν σε</span></span>). Shall hold thee together on every side (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντοθεν</span></span>). See about <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεχω</span></span> on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:38">Luke 4:38</span>. </p>
