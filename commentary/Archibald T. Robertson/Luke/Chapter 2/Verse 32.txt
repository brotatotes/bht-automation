<p><b> Revelation to the Gentiles </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκαλυψιν εθνων</span></span>). Objective genitive. The Messiah is to be light (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φως</span></span>) for the Gentiles in darkness (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:70">Luke 1:70</span>) and glory (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξα</span></span>) for Israel (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:1-5">Romans 9:1-5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+49:6">Isaiah 49:6</span>). The word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εθνος</span></span> originally meant just a crowd or company, then a race or nation, then the nations other than Israel (the people, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο λαος</span></span>) or the people of God. The word Gentile is Latin from <i>gens</i>, a tribe or nation. But the world-wide mission of the Messiah comes out clearly in these early chapters in Luke. </p>
