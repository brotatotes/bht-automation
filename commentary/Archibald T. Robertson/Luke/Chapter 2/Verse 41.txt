<p><b> Every year </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατ' ετος</span></span>). This idiom only here in the N.T., a common Greek construction. Every male was originally expected to appear at the passover, pentecost, and tabernacles (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+23:14-17">Exodus 23:14-17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+34:23">Exodus 34:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+16:16">Deuteronomy 16:16</span>). But the Dispersion rendered that impossible. But pious Palestinian Jews made a point of going at least to the passover. Mary went with Joseph as a pious habit, though not required by law to go. </p>
