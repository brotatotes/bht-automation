<p><b> Were amazed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξισταντο</span></span>). Imperfect indicative middle, descriptive of their continued and repeated astonishment. Common verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξιστημ</span></span> meaning that they stood out of themselves as if their eyes were bulging out. The boy had a holy thirst for knowledge (Plummer), and he used a boy's way of learning.</p>
<p><b>At his understanding </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ τη συνεσε</span></span>). Based on (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>), the grasp and comprehension from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνιημ</span></span>, comparing and combining things. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:33">Mark 12:33</span>.</p>
<p><b>His answers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταις αποκρισεσιν αυτου</span></span>). It is not difficult to ask hard questions, but this boy had astounding answers to their questions, revealing his amazing intellectual and spiritual growth. </p>
