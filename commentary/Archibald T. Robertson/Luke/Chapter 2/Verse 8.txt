<p><b> Abiding in the field </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγραυλουντες</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγρος</span></span>, field and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυλη</span></span>, court. The shepherds were making the field their court. Plutarch and Strabo use the word.</p>
<p><b>Keeping watch </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυλασσοντες φυλακας</span></span>). Cognate accusative. They were bivouacking by night and it was plainly mild weather. In these very pastures David had fought the lion and the bear to protect the sheep (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1sa+17:34">1 Samuel 17:34</span>). The plural here probably means that they watched by turns. The flock may have been meant for the temple sacrifices. There is no way to tell. </p>
