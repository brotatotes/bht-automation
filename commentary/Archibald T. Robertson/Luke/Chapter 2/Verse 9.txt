<p><b> Stood by them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεστη αυτοις</span></span>). Ingressive aorist active indicative. Stepped by their side. The same word in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:7">Acts 12:7</span> of the angel there. Paul uses it in the sense of standing by in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:20">Acts 22:20</span>. It is a common old Greek word, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφιστημ</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Were sore afraid </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφοβηθησαν φοβον μεγαν</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative with cognate accusative (the passive sense gone), they feared a great fear. </p>
