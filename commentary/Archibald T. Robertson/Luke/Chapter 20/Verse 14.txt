<p><b> That the inheritance may be ours </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα ημων γενητα η κληρονομια</span></span>). That the inheritance may become (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενητα</span></span>, second aorist middle subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span>). Here <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:39">Matthew 21:39</span> has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σχωμεν</span></span> "let us get, ingressive aorist active subjunctive." Cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχωμεν</span></span>, present subjunctive of the same verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:1">Romans 5:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:7">Mark 12:7</span> has "and it will be ours" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστα</span></span>). </p>
