<p><b> He looked upon them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμβλεψας αυτοις</span></span>). Not in Mark and Matthew. First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμβλεπω</span></span>, to look on. It was a piercing glance. The scripture quoted is from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+118:22">Psalms 118:22</span> and is in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:10">Mark 11:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:42">Matthew 21:42</span>, which see for the inverted attraction of the case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λιθον</span></span> (stone) to that of the relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ον</span></span> (which). </p>
