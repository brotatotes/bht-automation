<p><b> They watched him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρατηρησαντες</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρατηρεω</span></span>, a common Greek verb to watch on the side or insidiously or with evil intent as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:7">Luke 6:7</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρετηρουντο</span></span>) of the scribes and Pharisees. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:2">Mark 3:2</span>. There is no "him" in the Greek. They were watching their chance.</p>
<p><b>Spies </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενκαθετους</span></span>). An old verbal adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενκαθιημ</span></span>, to send down in or secretly. It means liers in wait who are suborned to spy out, one who is hired to trap one by crafty words. Only here in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>Feigned themselves </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποκρινομενους εαυτους</span></span>). Hypocritically professing to be "righteous" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιους</span></span>). "They posed as scrupulous persons with a difficulty of conscience" (Plummer).</p>
<p><b>That they might take hold of his speech </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα επιλαβωντα αυτου λογου</span></span>). Second aorist middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλαμβανω</span></span>, an old verb for seizing hold with the hands and uses as here the genitive case. These spies are for the purpose of (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>) catching hold of the talk of Jesus if they can get a grip anywhere. This is their direct purpose and the ultimate purpose or result is also stated, "so as to deliver him up" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωστε παραδουνα αυτον</span></span>). Second aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραδιδωμ</span></span>, to hand over, to give from one's side to another. The trap is all set now and ready to be sprung by these "spies."</p>
<p><b>Of the governor </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του ηγεμονος</span></span>). The Sanhedrin knew that Pilate would have to condemn Jesus if he were put to death. So then all their plans focus on this point as the goal. Luke alone mentions this item here. </p>
