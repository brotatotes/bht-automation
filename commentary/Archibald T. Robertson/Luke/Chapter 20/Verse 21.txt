<p><b> Rightly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορθως</span></span>). Matthew (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:16">Matthew 22:16</span>) notes that these "spies" were "disciples" (students) of the Pharisees and Mark (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:13">Mark 12:13</span>) adds that the Herodians are also involved in the plot. These bright theologues are full of palaver and flattery and openly endorse the teaching of Jesus as part of their scheme.</p>
<p><b>Acceptest not the person of any </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου λαμβανεις προσωπον</span></span>). Dost not take the face (or personal appearance) as the test. It is a Hebraism from which the word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσωπολεμψια</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:1">James 2:1</span>) comes. Originally it meant to lift the face, to lift the countenance, to regard the face, to accept the face value. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:13-17">Mark 12:13-17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:15-22">Matthew 22:15-22</span> for discussion of details here. They both have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλεπεις</span></span> here. </p>
