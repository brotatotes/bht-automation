<p><b> They reasoned with themselves </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνελογισαντο</span></span>). First aorist middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συλλογιζομα</span></span>, to bring together accounts, an old word, only here in the N.T. Mark and Matthew have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διελογιζοντο</span></span> (imperfect middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαλογιζομα</span></span>, a kindred verb, to reckon between one another, confer). This form (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διελογιζοντο</span></span>) in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:14">Luke 20:14</span> below.</p>
<p><b>If we shall say </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν ειπωμεν</span></span>). Third-class condition with second aorist active subjunctive. Suppose we say! So in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:6">Luke 20:6</span>. </p>
