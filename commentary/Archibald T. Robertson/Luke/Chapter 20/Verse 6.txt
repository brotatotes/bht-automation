<p><b> Will stone us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλιθασε</span></span>). Late verb and here only in the N.T. Literally, will throw stones down on us, stone us down, overwhelm us with stones.</p>
<p><b>They be persuaded </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπεισμενος εστιν</span></span>). Periphrastic perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειθω</span></span>, to persuade, a settled state of persuasion, "is persuaded" (no reason for use of "be" here).</p>
<p><b>That John was a prophet </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιωανην προφητην εινα</span></span>). Accusative and infinitive in indirect assertion. </p>
