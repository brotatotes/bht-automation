<p><b> Vineyard </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμπελωνα</span></span>). Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμπελος</span></span> (vine), place of vines. So in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:1">Mark 12:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:33">Matthew 21:33</span>.</p>
<p><b>Let it out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεδετο</span></span>). Second aorist middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκδιδωμ</span></span>, but with variable vowel <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span> in place of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span> of the stem <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δο</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεδοτο</span></span>). Same form in Mark and Matthew.</p>
<p><b>For a long time </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρονους ικανους</span></span>). Accusative of extent of time, considerable times or periods of time. Not in Mark and Matthew, though all three have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεδημησεν</span></span> (went off from home). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:6">Luke 7:6</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ικανος</span></span>. </p>
