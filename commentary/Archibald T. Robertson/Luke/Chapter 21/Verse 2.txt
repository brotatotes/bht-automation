<p><b> Poor </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πενιχραν</span></span>). A rare word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πενης</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πενομα</span></span>, to work for one's living). Latin <i>penuria</i> and Greek <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειναω</span></span>, to be hungry are kin to it. Here only in the N.T. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:42">Mark 12:42</span> has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πτωχη</span></span>, a more common word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πτωσσω</span></span>, to be frightened, to strike and hide from fear, to be in beggary. And Luke uses this adjective also of her in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:3">Luke 21:3</span>. </p>
