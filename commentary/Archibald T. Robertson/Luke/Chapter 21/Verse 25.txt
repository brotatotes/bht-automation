<p><b> Distress </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνοχη</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεχω</span></span>. In the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:4">2 Corinthians 2:4</span>. Anguish.</p>
<p><b>In perplexity </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν απορια</span></span>). State of one who is <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απορος</span></span>, who has lost his way (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορος</span></span>). Here only in the N.T. though an old and common word.</p>
<p><b>For the roaring of the sea </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηχους θαλασσης</span></span>). Our word echo (Latin <i>echo</i>) is this word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηχος</span></span>, a reverberating sound. Sense of rumour in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:37">Luke 4:37</span>.</p>
<p><b>Billows </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαλου</span></span>). Old word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαλος</span></span> for the swell of the sea. Here only in the N.T. </p>
