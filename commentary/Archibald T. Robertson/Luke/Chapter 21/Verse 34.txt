<p><b> Lest haply your hearts be overcharged </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη ποτε βαρηθωσιν α καρδια υμων</span></span>). First aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαρεω</span></span>, an old verb to weigh down, depress, with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη ποτε</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>With surfeiting </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν κρεπαλη</span></span>). A rather late word, common in medical writers for the nausea that follows a debauch. Latin <i>crapula</i>, the giddiness caused by too much wine. Here only in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>Drunkenness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεθη</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεθυ</span></span> (wine). Old word but in the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:13">Romans 13:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:21">Galatians 5:21</span>.</p>
<p><b>Cares of this life </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεριμναις βιωτικαις</span></span>). Anxieties of life. The adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βιωτικος</span></span> is late and in the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:3">1 Corinthians 6:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Come on you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιστη</span></span>). Second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφιστημ</span></span>, ingressive aorist. Construed also with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη ποτε</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Suddenly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφνιδιος</span></span>). Adjective in predicate agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημερα</span></span> (day).</p>
<p><b>As a snare </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως παγις</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πηγνυμ</span></span>, to make fast a net or trap. Paul uses it several times of the devil's snares for preachers (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:7">1 Timothy 3:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:26">2 Timothy 2:26</span>). </p>
