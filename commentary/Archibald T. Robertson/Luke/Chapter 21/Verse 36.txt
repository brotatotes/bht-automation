<p><b> But watch ye </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγρυπνειτε δε</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αγρυπνεω</span></span> is a late verb to be sleepless (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπνος</span></span>, sleep). Keep awake and be ready is the pith of Christ's warning.</p>
<p><b>That ye may prevail to escape </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα κατισχυσητε εκφυγειν</span></span>). First aorist active subjunctive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> of purpose. The verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατισχυω</span></span> means to have strength against (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:18">Matthew 16:18</span>). Common in later writers. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εκφυγειν</span></span> is second aorist active infinitive, to escape out.</p>
<p><b>To stand before the Son of man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σταθηνα εμπροσθεν του υιου του ανθρωπου</span></span>). That is the goal. There will be no dread of the Son then if one is ready. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Σταθηνα</span></span> is first aorist passive infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span>. </p>
