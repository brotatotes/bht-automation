<p><b> That ye be not led astray </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη πλανηθητε</span></span>). First aorist passive subjunctive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> (lest). This verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλαναω</span></span> occurs here only in Luke though often in the rest of the N.T. (as <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:4">Matthew 24:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:5">Matthew 24:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:11">Matthew 24:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:24">Matthew 24:24</span>, which see). Our word <i>planet</i> is from this word.</p>
<p><b>The time is at hand </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο καιρος ηγγικεν</span></span>). Just as John the Baptist did of the kingdom (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:2">Matthew 3:2</span>) and Jesus also (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:15">Mark 1:15</span>).</p>
<p><b>Go ye not after them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη πορευθητε οπισω αυτων</span></span>). First aorist passive subjunctive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span>. A needed warning today with all the false cries in the religious world. </p>
