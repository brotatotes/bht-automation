<p><b> With desire I have desired </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιθυμια επεθυμησα</span></span>). A Hebraism common in the LXX. Associative instrumental case of substantive and first aorist active indicative of same like a cognate accusative. Peculiar to Luke is all this verse. See this idiom in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:29">John 3:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:17">Acts 4:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>Before I suffer </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προ του με παθειν</span></span>). Preposition <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προ</span></span> with articular infinitive and accusative of general reference, "before the suffering as to me." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Παθειν</span></span> is second aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πασχω</span></span>. </p>
