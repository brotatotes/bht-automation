<p><b> The fruit of the vine </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του γενηματος της αμπελου</span></span>). So <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:25">Mark 14:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:29">Matthew 26:29</span> and not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οινος</span></span> though it was wine undoubtedly. But the language allows anything that is "the fruit of the vine."</p>
<p><b>Come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελθη</span></span>). Second aorist active subjunctive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως</span></span> as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:16">Luke 22:16</span>. Here it is the consummation of the kingdom that Jesus has in mind, for the kingdom had already come. </p>
