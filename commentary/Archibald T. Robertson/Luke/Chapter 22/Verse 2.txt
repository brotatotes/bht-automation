<p><b> Sought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εζητουν</span></span>). Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζητεω</span></span>, were seeking, conative imperfect.</p>
<p><b>How they might put him to death </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το πως ανελωσιν αυτον</span></span>). Second aorist active deliberative subjunctive (retained in indirect question) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναιρεω</span></span>, to take up, to make away with, to slay. Common in Old Greek. Luke uses it so here and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:32">Luke 23:32</span> and eighteen times in the Acts, a favourite word with him. Note the accusative neuter singular article <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το</span></span> with the whole clause, "as to the how, etc."</p>
<p><b>For they feared </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφοβουντο γαρ</span></span>). Imperfect middle describing the delay of the "how." The triumphal entry and the temple speeches of Jesus had revealed his tremendous power with the people, especially the crowds from Galilee at the feast. They were afraid to go on with their plan to kill him at the feast. </p>
