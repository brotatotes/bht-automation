<p><b> Which of them it was </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το τις αρα ειη εξ αυτων</span></span>). Note the article <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το</span></span> with the indirect question as in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:2">Luke 22:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:4">Luke 22:4</span>. The optative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειη</span></span> here is changed from the present active indicative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστιν</span></span>, though it was not always done, for see <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκε</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:24">Luke 22:24</span> where the present indicative is retained. They all had their hands on the table. Whose hand was it? </p>
