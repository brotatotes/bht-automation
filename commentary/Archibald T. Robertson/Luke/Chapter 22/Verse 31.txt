<p><b> Asked to have you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξηιτησατο</span></span>). First aorist indirect middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαιτεω</span></span>, an old verb to beg something of one and (middle) for oneself. Only here in the N.T. The verb is used either in the good or the bad sense, but it does not mean here "obtained by asking" as margin in Revised Version has it.</p>
<p><b>That he might sift you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του σινιασα</span></span>). Genitive articular infinitive of purpose. First aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σινιαζω</span></span>, to shake a sieve, to sift, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σινιον</span></span>, a winnowing fan. Later word. Here only in the N.T. </p>
