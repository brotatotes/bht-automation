<p><b> Went away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απελθων</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απερχομα</span></span>. He went off under the impulse of Satan and after the indignation over the rebuke of Jesus at the feast in Simon's house (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:4-6">John 12:4-6</span>).</p>
<p><b>Captains </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρατηγοις</span></span>). Leaders of the temple guards (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:1">Acts 4:1</span>), the full title, "captains of the temple," occurs in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:52">Luke 22:52</span>.</p>
<p><b>How he might deliver him unto them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το πως αυτοις παραδω αυτον</span></span>). The same construction as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:2">Luke 22:2</span>, the article <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το</span></span> with the indirect question and deliberative subjunctive second aorist active (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραδω</span></span>). </p>
