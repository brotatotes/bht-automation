<p><b> About a stone's throw </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωσε λιθου βολην</span></span>). Accusative of extent of space. Luke does not tell of leaving eight disciples by the entrance to Gethsemane nor about taking Peter, James, and John further in with him.</p>
<p><b>Kneeled down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεις τα γονατα</span></span>). Second aorist active participle from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span>. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:35">Mark 14:35</span> says "fell on the ground" and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:39">Matthew 26:39</span> "fell on his face." All could be true at different moments.</p>
<p><b>Prayed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσηυχετο</span></span>). Imperfect middle, was praying, kept on praying. </p>
