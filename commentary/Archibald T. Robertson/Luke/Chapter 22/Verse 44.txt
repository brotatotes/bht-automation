<p><b> In an agony </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αγωνια</span></span>). It was conflict, contest from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγων</span></span>. An old word, but only here in the N.T. Satan pressed Jesus harder than ever before.</p>
<p><b>As it were great drops of blood </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωσε θρομβο αιματος</span></span>). Thick, clotted blood. An old word (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θρομβο</span></span>) common in medical works, but here only in the N.T. This passage (verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:43">Luke 22:43</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:44">Luke 22:44</span>) is absent from some ancient documents. Aristotle speaks of a bloody sweat as does Theophrastus. </p>
