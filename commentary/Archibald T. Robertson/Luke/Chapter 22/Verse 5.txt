<p><b> Were glad </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχαρησαν</span></span>). Second aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαιρω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:11">Mark 14:11</span>. Ingressive aorist, a natural exultation that one of the Twelve had offered to do this thing.</p>
<p><b>Covenanted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεθεντο</span></span>). Second aorist indicative middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συντιθημ</span></span>. An old verb to put together and in the middle with one another. In the N.T. outside of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:22">John 9:22</span> only in Luke (here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:20">Acts 23:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:9">Acts 24:9</span>). Luke only mentions "money" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αργυριον</span></span>), but not "thirty pieces" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:15">Matthew 26:15</span>). </p>
