<p><b> When they had kindled a fire </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιαψαντων πυρ</span></span>). Genitive absolute, first aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιαπτω</span></span>, an old verb, but here only in the N.T. Kindle around, make a good fire that blazes all over. It was April and cool at night. The servants made the fire.</p>
<p><b>And had sat down together </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα συνκαθισαντων</span></span>). Genitive absolute again. Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν-</span></span> (together), all had taken seats around the fire.</p>
<p><b>Peter sat in the midst of them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκαθητο ο Πετρος μεσος αυτων</span></span>). Imperfect tense, he was sitting, and note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεσος</span></span>, nominative predicate adjective with the genitive, like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:26">John 1:26</span>, good Greek idiom. </p>
