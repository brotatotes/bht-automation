<p><b> After a little while another </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα βραχυ ετερος</span></span>). <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:71">Matthew 26:71</span> makes it after Peter had gone out into the porch and mentions a maid as speaking as does <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:69">Mark 14:69</span>, while here the "other" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετερος</span></span>) is a man (masculine gender). It is almost impossible to co-ordinate the three denials in the four accounts unless we conceive of several joining in when one led off. This time Peter's denial is very blunt, "I am not." </p>
