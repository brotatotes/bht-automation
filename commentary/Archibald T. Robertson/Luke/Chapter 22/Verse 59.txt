<p><b> After the space of about one hour </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαστασης ωσε ωρας μιας</span></span>). Genitive absolute with second aorist active participle feminine singular of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διιστημ</span></span>. This classical verb in the N.T. is used only by Luke (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:59">Luke 22:59</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:51">Luke 24:51</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:28">Acts 27:28</span>). It means standing in two or apart, about an hour intervening.</p>
<p><b>Confidently affirmed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διισχυριζετο</span></span>). Imperfect middle, he kept affirming strongly. An old verb (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια, ισχυριζομα</span></span>), to make oneself strong, to make emphatic declaration. In the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:15">Acts 12:15</span>.</p>
<p><b>For he is a Galilean </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα γαρ Γαλιλαιος εστιν</span></span>). <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:73">Matthew 26:73</span> makes it plain that it was his speech that gave him away, which see. </p>
