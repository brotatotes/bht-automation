<p><b> That held </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο συνεχοντες</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:45">Luke 8:45</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:43">Luke 19:43</span> for this verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεχω</span></span>. Here alone in the N.T. for holding a prisoner (holding together). The servants or soldiers, not the Sanhedrin.</p>
<p><b>Mocked </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεπαιζον</span></span>). Imperfect active, were mocking, inchoative, began to mock, to play like boys.</p>
<p><b>And beat him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεροντες</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δερω</span></span>, to flay, tan, or hide. Literally, "beating." </p>
