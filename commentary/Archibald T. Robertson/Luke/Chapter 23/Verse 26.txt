<p><b> They laid hold </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλαβομενο</span></span>). Second aorist middle participle of the common verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλαμβανω</span></span>. The soldiers had no scruples about taking hold of any one of themselves (middle voice). <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:21">Mark 15:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+27:32">Luke 27:32</span> use the technical word for this process <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγγαρευω</span></span>, which see for discussion and also about Cyrene.</p>
<p><b>Laid on him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεθηκαν</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κ</span></span> first aorist of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιτιθημ</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>To bear it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φερειν</span></span>). Present infinitive, to go on bearing. </p>
