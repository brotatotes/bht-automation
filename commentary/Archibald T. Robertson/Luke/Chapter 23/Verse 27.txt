<p><b> Followed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκολουθε</span></span>). Imperfect active, was following. Verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:27-32">Luke 23:27-32</span> are peculiar to Luke.</p>
<p><b>Bewailed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκοπτοντο</span></span>). Imperfect middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοπτω</span></span>, to cut, smite, old and common verb. Direct middle, they were smiting themselves on the breast. "In the Gospels there is no instance of a woman being hostile to Christ" (Plummer). Luke's Gospel is appropriately called the Gospel of Womanhood (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:39-56">Luke 1:39-56</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:36-38">Luke 2:36-38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:11-15">Luke 7:11-15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:37-50">Luke 7:37-50</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:1-3">Luke 8:1-3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:38-42">Luke 10:38-42</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:27">Luke 11:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:11-16">Luke 13:11-16</span>).</p>
<p><b>Lamented </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εθρηνουν</span></span>). Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θρηνεω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θρεομα</span></span>, to cry aloud, lament. </p>
