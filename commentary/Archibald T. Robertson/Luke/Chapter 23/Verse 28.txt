<p><b> Turning </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στραφεις</span></span>). Luke is fond of this second aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρεφω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:9">Luke 7:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:44">Luke 7:44</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:55">Luke 7:55</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:23">Luke 10:23</span>). If he had been still carrying the Cross, he could not have made this dramatic gesture.</p>
<p><b>Weep not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη κλαιετε</span></span>). Present active imperative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span>, Stop weeping. </p>
