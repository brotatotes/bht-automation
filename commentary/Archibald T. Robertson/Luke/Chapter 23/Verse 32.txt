<p><b> Were led </b> (gonto). Imperfect passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγω</span></span>, were being led.</p>
<p><b>Malefactors </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακουργο</span></span>). Evil (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακον</span></span>), doers (work, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργον</span></span>). Old word, but in the N.T. only in this passage (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:32">Luke 23:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:33">Luke 23:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:39">Luke 23:39</span>) and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:9">2 Timothy 2:9</span>. Luke does not call them "robbers" like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:27">Mark 15:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:38">Matthew 27:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:44">Matthew 27:44</span>.</p>
<p><b>To be put to death </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναιρεθηνα</span></span>). First aorist passive infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναιρεω</span></span>, old verb, to take up, to take away, to kill. </p>
