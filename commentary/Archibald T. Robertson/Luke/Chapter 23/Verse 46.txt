<p><b> Father </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πατερ</span></span>). Jesus dies with the words of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+31:5">Psalms 31:5</span> on his lips.</p>
<p><b>Gave up the ghost </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεπνευσεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκπνεω</span></span>, to breathe out, to expire, old word, but in the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:37">Mark 15:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:39">Mark 15:39</span>. There is no special reason for retaining "ghost" in the English as both <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:50">Matthew 27:50</span> (yielded up his spirit, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφηκεν το πνευμα</span></span>) and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:30">John 19:30</span> (gave up his spirit, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεδωκεν το πνευμα</span></span>) use <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα</span></span> which is the root of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκπνεω</span></span>, the verb in Mark and Luke. </p>
