<p><b> Certainly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οντως</span></span>). Really, old adverb from the participle <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ον</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειμ</span></span>, to be. Used also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:34">Luke 24:34</span> of the resurrection of Jesus.</p>
<p><b>A righteous man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιος</span></span>). <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:39">Mark 15:39</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:54">Matthew 27:54</span>) which see, represents the centurion as saying <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεου υιος</span></span> (God's Son) which may mean to him little more than "righteous man."</p>
<p><b>That came together </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνπαραγενομενο</span></span>). Double compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν</span></span>, together, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span>, along), that came along together.</p>
<p><b>To this sight </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ την θεωριαν ταυτην</span></span>). This spectacle (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεωριαν</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεωρεω</span></span>, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:35">Luke 23:35</span>).</p>
<p><b>Returned </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπεστρεφον</span></span>). Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποστρεφω</span></span>. See them slowly wending their way back to the city from this Tragedy of the Ages which they had witnessed in awe. </p>
