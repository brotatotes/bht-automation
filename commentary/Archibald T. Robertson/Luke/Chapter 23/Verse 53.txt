<p><b> Took it down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθελων</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθαιρεω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:46">Mark 15:46</span>.</p>
<p><b>Wrapped </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενετυλιξεν</span></span>), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:59">Matthew 27:59</span> where <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:46">Mark 15:46</span> has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενειλησεν</span></span> (wound), which see. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:40">John 19:40</span> has "bound" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδησαν</span></span>). See Matt. and Mark also for the linen cloth (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σινδον</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Hewn in stone </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαξευτω</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαξευω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λας</span></span>, a stone, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξεω</span></span>, to polish). In the LXX and here only in the N.T. Nowhere else so far as known. See the usual Greek verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λατομεω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:46">Mark 15:46</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:60">Matthew 27:60</span>.</p>
<p><b>Where never man had yet lain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου ουκ εν ουδεις ουπω κειμενος</span></span>). Triple negative and periphrastic past perfect passive in sense (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κειμα</span></span>), though periphrastic imperfect passive in form. Same item in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:40">John 19:40</span> who uses <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην τεθειμενος</span></span> (periphrastic past perfect passive in form). </p>
