<p><b> When he knew </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιγνους</span></span>). Second aorist active participle from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιγινωσκω</span></span>, having gained full (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>, added knowledge).</p>
<p><b>Of Herod's jurisdiction </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ της εξουσιας Hηρωιδου</span></span>). Herod was naturally jealous of any encroachment by Pilate, the Roman Procurator of Judea. So here was a chance to respect the prerogative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξουσια</span></span>) of Herod and get rid of this troublesome case also.</p>
<p><b>Sent him up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεπεμψεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπεμπω</span></span>. This common verb is used of sending back as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:11">Luke 23:11</span> or of sending up to a higher court as of Paul to Caesar (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:21">Acts 25:21</span>).</p>
<p><b>Who himself also was </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οντα κα αυτον</span></span>). Being also himself in Jerusalem. Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειμ</span></span>. </p>
