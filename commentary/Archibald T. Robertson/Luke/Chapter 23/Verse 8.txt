<p><b> Was exceeding glad </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχαρη λιαν</span></span>). Second aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαιρω</span></span>, ingressive aorist, became glad.</p>
<p><b>Of a long time </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ ικανων χρονων</span></span>). For this idiom see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:27">Luke 8:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:9">Luke 20:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:11">Acts 8:11</span>).</p>
<p><b>He hoped </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηλπιζεν</span></span>). Imperfect active. He was still hoping. He had long ago gotten over his fright that Jesus was John the Baptist come to life again (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:7-9">Luke 9:7-9</span>).</p>
<p><b>Done </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομενον</span></span>). Present middle participle. He wanted to see a miracle happening like a stunt of a sleight-of-hand performer. </p>
