<p>This entire verse is a Western non-interpolation. This incident is given in complete form in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:2-10">John 18:2-10</span> and most of the words in this verse are there also. It is of a piece with many items in this chapter about which it is not easy to reach a final conclusion.</p>
<p><b>Stooping and looking in </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρακυψας</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρακυπτω</span></span>, to stoop besides and peer into. Old verb used also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:5">John 20:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:11">John 20:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:25">James 1:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:12">1 Peter 1:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>By themselves </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μονα</span></span>). Without the body.</p>
<p><b>To his home </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς αυτον</span></span>). Literally, "to himself." </p>
