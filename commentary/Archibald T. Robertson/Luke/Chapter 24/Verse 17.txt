<p><b> That you have with another </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ους αντιβαλλετε προς αλληλους</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αντι-βαλλω</span></span> is an old verb and means to throw in turn, back and forth like a ball, from one to another, a beautiful picture of conversation as a game of words. Only here in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>They stood still </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσταθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span>, intransitive. They stopped.</p>
<p><b>Looking sad </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκυθρωπο</span></span>). This is the correct text. It is an old adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκυθρος</span></span>, gloomy and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπς</span></span>, countenance. Only here in the N.T. </p>
