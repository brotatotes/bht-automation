<p><b> Interpreted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διηρμηνευσεν</span></span>). First aorist active (constative aorist) indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διερμηνευω</span></span> (Margin has the imperfect <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διηρμηνευεν</span></span>), intensive compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span>) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερμηνευω</span></span>, the old verb to interpret from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερμηνευς</span></span>, interpreter, and that from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hερμης</span></span>, the messenger of the gods as the people of Lystra took Paul to be (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:12">Acts 14:12</span>). But what wonderful exegesis the two disciples were now hearing!</p>
<p><b>Concerning himself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ εαυτον</span></span>). Jesus found himself in the Old Testament, a thing that some modern scholars do not seem able to do. </p>
