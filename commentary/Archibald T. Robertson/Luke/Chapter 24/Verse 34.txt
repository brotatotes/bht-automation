<p><b> Saying </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγοντας</span></span>). Accusative present active participle agreeing with "the eleven and those with them" in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:33">Luke 24:33</span>.</p>
<p><b>Indeed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οντως</span></span>). Really, because "he has appeared to Simon" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωπθη Σιμων</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span>. This is the crucial evidence that turned the scales with the disciples and explains "indeed." Paul also mentions it (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:5">1 Corinthians 15:5</span>). </p>
