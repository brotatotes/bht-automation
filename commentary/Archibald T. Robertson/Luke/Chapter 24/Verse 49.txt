<p><b> Until ye be clothed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως ου ενδυσησθε</span></span>). First aorist middle subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδυω</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδυνω</span></span>. It is an old verb for putting on a garment. It is here the indirect middle, put on yourselves power from on high as a garment. They are to wait till this experience comes to them. This is "the promise of the Father." It is an old metaphor in Homer, Aristophanes, Plutarch, and Paul uses it often. </p>
