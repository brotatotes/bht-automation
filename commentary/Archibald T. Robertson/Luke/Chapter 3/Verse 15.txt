<p><b> Were in expectation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσδοκωντος</span></span>). Genitive absolute of this striking verb already seen in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:21">Luke 1:21</span>.</p>
<p><b>Reasoned </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαλογιζομενων</span></span>). Genitive absolute again. John's preaching about the Messiah and the kingdom of God stirred the people deeply and set them to wondering.</p>
<p><b>Whether haply he were the Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηποτε αυτος ειη ο Χριστος</span></span>). Optative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειη</span></span> in indirect question changed from the indicative in the direct (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 1031). John wrought no miracles and was not in David's line and yet he moved people so mightily that they began to suspect that he himself (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτος</span></span>) was the Messiah. The Sanhedrin will one day send a formal committee to ask him this direct question (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:19">John 1:19</span>). </p>
