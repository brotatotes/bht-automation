<p><b> Came down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηλθεν</span></span>). <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:21">Mark 1:21</span> has the historical present,</p>
<p><b>they go into </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισπορευοντα</span></span>). Capernaum (Tell Hum) is now the headquarters of the Galilean ministry, since Nazareth has rejected Jesus. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:31-37">Luke 4:31-37</span> is parallel with <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:21-28">Mark 1:21-28</span> which he manifestly uses. It is the first of Christ's miracles which they give.</p>
<p><b>Was teaching them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην διδασκων αυτους</span></span>). Periphrastic imperfect. Mark has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδιδασκεν</span></span> first and then <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν διδασκων</span></span>. "Them" here means the people present in the synagogue on the sabbath, construction according to sense as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:22">Mark 1:22</span>. </p>
