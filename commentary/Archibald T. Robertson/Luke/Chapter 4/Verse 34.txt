<p><b> Ah! </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εα</span></span>). An interjection frequent in the Attic poets, but rare in prose. Apparently second person singular imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαω</span></span>, to permit. It is expressive of wonder, fear, indignation. Here it amounts to a diabolical screech. For the rest of the verse see discussion on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:24">Mark 1:24</span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:29">Matthew 8:29</span>. The muzzle (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιμος</span></span>) occurs literally in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:9">1 Corinthians 9:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:18">1 Timothy 5:18</span>, and metaphorically here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:25">Mark 1:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:39">Mark 4:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:12">Matthew 22:12</span>. </p>
