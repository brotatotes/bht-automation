<p><b> When the sun was setting </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυνοντος του ηλιου</span></span>). Genitive absolute and present participle (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυνω</span></span>, late form of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυω</span></span>) picturing the sunset scene. Even <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:32">Mark 1:32</span> has here the aorist indicative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδυσεν</span></span> (punctiliar active). It was not only cooler, but it was the end of the sabbath when it was not regarded as work (Vincent) to carry a sick person (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:10">John 5:10</span>). And also by now the news of the cure of the demoniac of Peter's mother-in-law had spread all over the town.</p>
<p><b>Had </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειχον</span></span>). Imperfect tense including all the chronic cases.</p>
<p><b>With divers diseases </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νοσοις ποικιλαις</span></span>). Instrumental case. For "divers" say "many coloured" or "variegated." See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:24">Matthew 4:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:34">Mark 1:34</span>.</p>
<p><b>Brought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγαγον</span></span>). Constative summary second aorist active indicative like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:16">Matthew 8:16</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσενεγκαν</span></span>, where <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:32">Mark 1:32</span> has the imperfect <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφερον</span></span>, brought one after another.</p>
<p><b>He laid his hands on every one of them and healed them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο δε εν εκαστω αυτων τας χειρας επιτιθεις εθεραπευεν αυτους</span></span>). Note the present active participle <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιτιθεις</span></span> and the imperfect active <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εθεραπευεν</span></span>, picturing the healing one by one with the tender touch upon each one. Luke alone gives this graphic detail which was more than a mere ceremonial laying on of hands. Clearly the cures of Jesus reached the physical, mental, and spiritual planes of human nature. He is Lord of life and acted here as Master of each case as it came. </p>
