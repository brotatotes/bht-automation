<p><b> Came out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξηρχετο</span></span>, singular, or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξηρχοντο</span></span>, plural). Imperfect tense, repetition, from one after another.</p>
<p><b>Thou art the Son of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Συ ε ο υιος του θεου</span></span>). More definite statement of the deity of Jesus than the witness of the demoniac in the synagogue (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:34">Luke 4:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:24">Mark 1:24</span>), like the words of the Father (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:22">Luke 3:22</span>) and more so than the condition of the devil (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:3">Luke 4:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:9">Luke 4:9</span>). In the Canterbury Revision "devils" should always be "demons" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δαιμονια</span></span>) as here.</p>
<p><b>Suffered them not to speak </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ εια αυτα λαλειν</span></span>). Imperfect third singular active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαω</span></span>, very old and common verb with syllabic augment <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span>. The tense accents the continued refusal of Jesus to receive testimony to his person and work from demons. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:4">Matthew 8:4</span> to the lepers.</p>
<p><b>Because they knew </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ ηιδεισαν</span></span>). Causal, not declarative, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span>. Past perfect of the second perfect <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιδα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>That he was the Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον Χριστον αυτον εινα</span></span>). Infinitive in indirect assertion with the accusative of general reference. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Τον Χριστον</span></span> =</p>
<p><b>the Anointed </b>, the Messiah. </p>
