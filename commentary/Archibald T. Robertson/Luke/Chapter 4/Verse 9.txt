<p><b> Led him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγαγεν</span></span>). Aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγω</span></span>. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:5">Matthew 4:5</span> has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραλαμβανε</span></span> (dramatic present).</p>
<p><b>The wing of the temple </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το πτερυγιον του ιερου</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:5">Matthew 4:5</span>. It is not easy to determine precisely what it was.</p>
<p><b>From hence </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντευθεν</span></span>). This Luke adds to the words in Matthew, which see.</p>
<p><b>To guard thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του διαφυλαξα σε</span></span>). Not in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:6">Matthew 4:6</span> quoted by Satan from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+91:11">Psalms 91:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+91:12">Psalms 91:12</span>. Satan does not misquote this Psalm, but he misapplies it and makes it mean presumptuous reliance on God. This compound verb is very old, but occurs here alone in the N.T. and that from the LXX. Luke repeats <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span> (recitative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεγραπτα</span></span>, is written) after this part of the quotation. </p>
