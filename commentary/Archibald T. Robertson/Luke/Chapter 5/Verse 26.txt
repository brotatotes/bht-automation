<p><b> Amazement </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκστασις</span></span>). Something out of its place, as the mind. Here the people were almost beside themselves as we say with the same idiom. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:42">Mark 5:42</span>. So they kept glorifying God (imperfect tense, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδοξαζον</span></span>) and at the same time "were filled with fear" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επλησθησαν φοβου</span></span>, aorist passive).</p>
<p><b>Strange things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραδοξα</span></span>). Our very word paradox, contrary to (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span>) received opinion (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξα</span></span>). Plato, Xenophon, and Polybius use it. Here alone in the N.T. </p>
