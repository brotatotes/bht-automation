<p><b> A publican named Levi </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελωνεν ονοματ Λευειν</span></span>). <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:13">Mark 2:13</span> has also "The son of Alphaeus" while <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:9">Matthew 9:9</span> calls him "Matthew." He had, of course, both names. All three use the same words (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ το τελωνιον</span></span>) for the place of toll. See discussion of <b>publican </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελωνης</span></span>) on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:9">Matthew 9:9</span>. All three Gospels give the command of Jesus, <b>Follow me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακολουθε</span></span>). </p>
