<p><b> To put out a little </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαναγαγειν ολιγον</span></span>). Second aorist infinitive of the double compound verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ-αν-αγω</span></span>, found in Xenophon and late Greek writers generally. Only twice in the N.T. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:18">Matthew 21:18</span> in the sense of leading back or returning and here in the sense of leading a ship up upon the sea, to put out to sea, a nautical term.</p>
<p><b>Taught </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδικασκεν</span></span>). Imperfect active, picturing Jesus teaching from the boat in which he was seated and so safe from the jam of the crowd. "Christ uses Peter's boat as a pulpit whence to throw the net of the Gospel over His hearers" (Plummer). </p>
