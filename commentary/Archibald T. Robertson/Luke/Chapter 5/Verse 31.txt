<p><b> They that are whole </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο υγιαινοντες</span></span>). Old Greek word for good health from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υγιης</span></span>, sound in body. So also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:10">Luke 7:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:27">Luke 15:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:2">3 John 1:2</span>. This is the usual word for good health used by Greek medical writers. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:17">Mark 2:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:12">Matthew 9:12</span> have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ισχυοντες</span></span> (those who have strength). </p>
