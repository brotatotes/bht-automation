<p><b> Now </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυν</span></span>). Luke adds this adverb here and in the next sentence after "weep." This sharpens the contrast between present sufferings and the future blessings.</p>
<p><b>Filled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χορτασθησεσθε</span></span>). Future passive indicative. The same verb in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:6">Matthew 5:6</span>. Originally it was used for giving fodder (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χορτος</span></span>) to animals, but here it is spiritual fodder or food except in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:16">Luke 15:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:21">Luke 16:21</span>. Luke here omits "and thirst after righteousness."</p>
<p><b>Weep </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλαιοντες</span></span>). Audible weeping. Where <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:4">Matthew 5:4</span> has "mourn" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πενθουντες</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Shall laugh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γελασετε</span></span>). Here <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:4">Matthew 5:4</span> has "shall be comforted." Luke's words are terse. </p>
