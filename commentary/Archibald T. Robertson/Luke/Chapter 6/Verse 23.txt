<p><b> Leap for joy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκιρτησατε</span></span>). Old verb and in LXX, but only in Luke in the N.T. (here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:41">Luke 1:41</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:44">Luke 1:44</span>). It answers to Matthew's (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:12">Matthew 5:12</span>) "be exceeding glad."</p>
<p><b>Did </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εποιουν</span></span>). Imperfect active, the habit of "their fathers" (peculiar to both here). <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:12">Matthew 5:12</span> has "persecuted." Thus they will receive a prophet's reward (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+1:41">Matthew 1:41</span>). </p>
