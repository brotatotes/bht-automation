<p><b> And judge not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα μη κρινετε</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μη</span></span> and the present active imperative, forbidding the habit of criticism. The common verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρινω</span></span>, to separate, we have in our English words critic, criticism, criticize, discriminate. Jesus does not mean that we are not to form opinions, but not to form them rashly, unfairly, like our prejudice.</p>
<p><b>Ye shall not be judged </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη κριθητε</span></span>). First aorist passive subjunctive with double negative ou <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span>, strong negative.</p>
<p><b>Condemn not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη καταδικαζετε</span></span>). To give judgment (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικη, διξαζω</span></span>) against (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span>) one. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μη</span></span> and present imperative. Either cease doing or do not have the habit of doing it. Old verb.</p>
<p><b>Ye shall not be condemned </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη καταδικασθητε</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative again with the double negative. Censoriousness is a bad habit.</p>
<p><b>Release </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολυετε</span></span>). Positive command the opposite of the censoriousness condemned. </p>
