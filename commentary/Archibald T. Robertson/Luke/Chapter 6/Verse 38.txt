<p><b> Pressed down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπιεσμενον</span></span>). Perfect passive participle from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιεζω</span></span>, old verb, but here alone in the N.T., though the Doric form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιαζω</span></span>, to seize, occurs several times (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:30">John 7:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:32">John 7:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:44">John 7:44</span>).</p>
<p><b>Shaken together </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σεσαλευμενον</span></span>). Perfect passive participle again from common verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαλευω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Running over </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερεκχυννομενον</span></span>). Present middle participle of this double compound verb not found elsewhere save in A Q in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joe+2:24">Joel 2:24</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χυνω</span></span> is a late form of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χεω</span></span>. There is asyndeton here, no conjunction connecting these participles. The present here is in contrast to the two preceding perfects. The participles form an epexegesis or explanation of the "good measure" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετρον καλον</span></span>). Into your bosom (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τον κολπον υμων</span></span>). The fold of the wide upper garment bound by the girdle made a pocket in common use (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+4:6">Exodus 4:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="pr+6:27">Proverbs 6:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+79:12">Psalms 79:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+65:6">Isaiah 65:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+32:18">Jeremiah 32:18</span>). So <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+65:7">Isaiah 65:7</span>:</p>
<p><b>I will measure their former work unto their bosom. Shall be measured to you again </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντιμετρηθησετα</span></span>). Future passive indicative of the verb here only in the N.T. save late MSS. in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:2">Matthew 7:2</span>. Even here some MSS. have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετρηθησετα</span></span>. The <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντ</span></span> has the common meaning of in turn or back, measured back to you in requital. </p>
