<p><b> Digged and went deep </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσκαψεν κα εβαθυνεν</span></span>). Two first aorist indicatives. Not a <i>hendiadys</i> for dug deep. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Σκαπτω</span></span>, to dig, is as old as Homer, as is <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαθυνω</span></span>, to make deep.</p>
<p><b>And laid a foundation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα εθηκεν θεμελιον</span></span>). That is the whole point. This wise builder struck the rock before he laid the foundation.</p>
<p><b>When a flood arose </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλημμυρης γενομενης</span></span>). Genitive absolute. Late word for flood, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλημμυρα</span></span>, only here in the N.T., though in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+40:18">Job 40:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>Brake against </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσερηξεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσρηγνυμ</span></span> and in late writers <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσρησσω</span></span>, to break against. Only here in the N.T. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:25">Matthew 7:25</span> has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσεπεσαν</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσπιπτω</span></span>, to fall against.</p>
<p><b>Could not shake it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ ισχυσεν σαλευσα αυτην</span></span>). Did not have strength enough to shake it.</p>
<p><b>Because it had been well builded </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια το καλως οικοδομησθα αυτην</span></span>). Perfect passive articular infinitive after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span> and with accusative of general reference. </p>
