<p><b> He that heareth and doeth not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο δε ακουσας κα μη ποιησας</span></span>). Aorist active participle with article. Particular case singled out (punctiliar, aorist).</p>
<p><b>Like a man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοιος εστιν ανθρωπω</span></span>). Associative instrumental case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοιος</span></span> as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:47">Luke 6:47</span>.</p>
<p><b>Upon the earth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ την γην</span></span>). <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:26">Matthew 7:26</span> has "upon the sand" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ την αμμον</span></span>), more precise and worse than mere earth. But not on the rock.</p>
<p><b>Without a foundation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χωρις θεμελιου</span></span>). The foundation on the rock after deep digging as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:48">Luke 6:48</span>.</p>
<p><b>It fell in </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεπεσεν</span></span>). Second aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνπιπτω</span></span>, to fall together, to collapse. An old verb from Homer on, but only here in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>The ruin </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το ρηγμα</span></span>). The crash like a giant oak in the forest resounded far and wide. An old word for a rent or fracture as in medicine for laceration of a wound. Only here in the N.T. </p>
