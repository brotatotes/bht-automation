<p><b> The Lord saw her </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδων αυτην ο κυριος</span></span>). The Lord of Life confronts death (Plummer) and Luke may use <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κυριος</span></span> here purposely.</p>
<p><b>Had compassion </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσπλαγχθη</span></span>). First aorist (ingressive) passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπλαγχνιζομα</span></span>. Often love and pity are mentioned as the motives for Christ's miracles (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:14">Matthew 14:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:32">Matthew 15:32</span>, etc.). It is confined to the Synoptics in the N.T. and about Christ save in the parables by Christ.</p>
<p><b>Weep not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη κλαιε</span></span>). Present imperative in a prohibition. Cease weeping. </p>
