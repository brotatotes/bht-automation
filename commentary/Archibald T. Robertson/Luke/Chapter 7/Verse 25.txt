<p><b> Gorgeously apparelled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ιματισμω ενδοξω</span></span>). In splendid clothing. Here alone in this sense in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>And live delicately </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρυφη</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θρυπτω</span></span> to break down, to enervate, an old word for luxurious living. See the verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρυφαω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:5">James 5:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>In kings' courts </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τοις βασιλειοις</span></span>). Only here in the N.T. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:8">Matthew 11:8</span> has it "in kings' houses." Verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:26">Luke 7:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:27">Luke 7:27</span> are precisely alike in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:9">Matthew 11:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:10">Matthew 11:10</span>, which see for discussion. </p>
