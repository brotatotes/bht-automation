<p><b> Justified God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδικαιωσαν τον θεον</span></span>). They considered God just or righteous in making these demands of them. Even the publicans did. They submitted to the baptism of John (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαπτισθεντες το βαπτισμα του Ιωανου</span></span>. First aorist passive participle with the cognate accusative retained in the passive. Some writers consider verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:29">Luke 7:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:30">Luke 7:30</span> a comment of Luke in the midst of the eulogy of John by Jesus. This would be a remarkable thing for so long a comment to be interjected. It is perfectly proper as the saying of Jesus. </p>
