<p><b> For </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γαρ</span></span>). This clause gives the reason why the elders of the Jews consider him "worthy" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αξιος</span></span>, drawing down the scale, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αξις</span></span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγο</span></span>). He was hardly a proselyte, but was a Roman who had shown his love for the Jews.</p>
<p><b>Himself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτος</span></span>). All by himself and at his own expense.</p>
<p><b>Us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημιν</span></span>). Dative case, for us. It is held by some archaeologists that the black basalt ruins in Tell Hum are the remains of the very synagogue (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την συναγωγην</span></span>). Literally,</p>
<p><b>the synagogue </b>, the one which we have, the one for us. </p>
