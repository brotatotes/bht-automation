<p><b> And they launched forth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ανηχθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγω</span></span>, an old verb, to lead up, to put out to sea (looked at as going up from the land). This nautical sense of the verb occurs only in Luke in the N.T. and especially in the Acts (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:13">Acts 13:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:11">Acts 16:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:21">Acts 18:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:3">Acts 20:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:13">Acts 20:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:21">Acts 20:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:2">Acts 20:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:2">Acts 27:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:4">Acts 27:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:12">Acts 27:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:21">Acts 27:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:10">Acts 28:10</span>). </p>
