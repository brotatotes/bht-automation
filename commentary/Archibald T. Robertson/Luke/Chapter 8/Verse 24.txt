<p><b>We perish </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολλυμεθα</span></span>). <p><b>The raging of the water </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω κλυδον του υδατος</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κλυδων</span></span>, common Greek word, is a boisterous surge, a violent agitation. Here only in the N.T. save <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:6">James 1:6</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κυμα</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:37">Mark 4:37</span>) is the regular swell or wave. A <b>calm </b>(<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γαληνη</span></span>). Only in the parallels in the N.T., though common word. Here <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:39">Mark 4:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:26">Matthew 8:26</span> add <b>great </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεγαλη</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>That </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span>). This use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span> as explanatory of the demonstrative pronoun <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτος</span></span> occurs in the parallels <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:36">Mark 4:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:27">Matthew 8:27</span> and also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:36">Luke 4:36</span>. It is almost result.</p>
