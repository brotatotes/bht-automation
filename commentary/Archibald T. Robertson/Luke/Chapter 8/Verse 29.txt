<p><b> For he commanded </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρηγγελλεν γαρ</span></span>). Imperfect active, correct text, for he was commanding.</p>
<p><b>Often times </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολλοις χρονοις</span></span>). Or "for a long time" like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρονω πολλω</span></span> of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:27">Luke 8:27</span> (see Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 537, for the plural here).</p>
<p><b>It had seized </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνηρπακε</span></span>). Past perfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συναρπαζω</span></span>, to lay hold by force. An old verb, but only in Luke in the N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:29">Luke 8:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:12">Acts 6:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:29">Acts 19:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:15">Acts 27:15</span>).</p>
<p><b>Was kept under guard </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδεσμευετο</span></span>). Imperfect passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεσμευω</span></span> to put in chains, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεσμος</span></span>, bond, and that from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεω</span></span> to bind. Old, but rather rare verb. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:4">Acts 22:4</span> in this sense. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:4">Matthew 23:4</span> it means to bind together. Some MSS. read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεσμεω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:29">Luke 8:29</span>.</p>
<p><b>Breaking the bands asunder </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαρησσων τα δεσμα</span></span>). Old verb, the preposition <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span> (in two) intensifying the meaning of the simple verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρησσω</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρηγνυμ</span></span>, to rend.</p>
<p><b>Was driven </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηλαυνετο</span></span>). Imperfect passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελαυνω</span></span>, to drive, to row, to march (Xenophon). Only five times in the N.T. Here alone in Luke and peculiar to Luke in this incident. </p>
