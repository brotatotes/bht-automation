<p><b> A herd of many swine </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγελη χοιρων ικανων</span></span>). Word <b>herd </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγελη</span></span>) old as Homer, but in N.T. only here and parallels (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:11">Mark 5:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:30">Matthew 8:30</span>). Luke shows his fondness for adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ικανος</span></span> here again (see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:27">Luke 8:27</span>) where Mark has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεγαλη</span></span> and Matthew <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολλων</span></span>. </p>
