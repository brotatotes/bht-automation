<p><b> An only daughter </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυγατηρ μονογενης</span></span>). The same adjective used of the widow's son (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:12">Luke 7:12</span>) and the epileptic boy (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:38">Luke 9:38</span>) and of Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:18">John 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:16">John 3:16</span>).</p>
<p><b>She lay a dying </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεθνησκεν</span></span>). Imperfect active, she was dying. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:18">Matthew 9:18</span> has it that she has just died.</p>
<p><b>Thronged </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεπνιγον</span></span>). Imperfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμπνιγω</span></span>, to press together, the verb used of the thorns choking the growing grain (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:14">Luke 8:14</span>). It was a jam. </p>
