<p><b> Upon the rock </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ την πετραν</span></span>). <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:5">Mark 4:5</span> "the rocky ground" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ το πετρωδες</span></span>), <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:5">Matthew 13:5</span> "the rocky places.</p>
<p><b>As soon as it grew </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυεν</span></span>). Second aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυω</span></span>, an old verb to spring up like a sprout.</p>
<p><b>Withered away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξηρανθη</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζηραινω</span></span>, old verb, to dry up.</p>
<p><b>Moisture </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ικμαδα</span></span>). Here only in the N.T., though common word. </p>
