<p><b> To wear away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλινειν</span></span>). Old verb usually transitive, to bend or bow down. Many compounds as in English decline, incline, recline, clinic (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλινη</span></span>, bed), etc. Luke alone in the N.T. uses it intransitively as here. The sun was turning down towards setting.</p>
<p><b>Lodge </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλυσωσιν</span></span>). First aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλυω</span></span>, a common verb, to dissolve, destroy, overthrow, and then of travellers to break a journey, to lodge (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλυμα</span></span>, inn, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:7">Luke 2:7</span>). Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:7">Luke 19:7</span> in the N.T. in this sense.</p>
<p><b>Get victuals </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρωσιν επισιτισμον</span></span>). Ingressive aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρισκω</span></span>, very common verb.</p>
<p><b>Victuals </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισιτισμον</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισιτιζομα</span></span>, to provision oneself, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σιτιζω</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σιτον</span></span>, wheat) only here in the N.T., though common in ancient Greek, especially for provisions for a journey (snack). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:32-44">Mark 6:32-44</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:13-21">Matthew 14:13-21</span> for discussion of details. </p>
