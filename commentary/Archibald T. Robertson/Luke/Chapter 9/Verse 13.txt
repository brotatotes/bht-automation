<p><b> Except we should go and buy food </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε μητ πορευθεντες ημεις αγορασωμεν βρωματα</span></span>). This is a condition of the third class with the aorist subjunctive (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγορασωμεν</span></span>), where the conjunction is usually <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> (with negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν μη</span></span>), but not always or necessarily so especially in the <i>Koine</i>. So in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:5">1 Corinthians 14:5</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε μη διερμηνευη</span></span> and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:12">Philippians 3:12</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε κα καταλαβω</span></span>. "Unless" is better here than "except."</p>
<p><b>Food </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βρωματα</span></span>), means eaten pieces from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βιβρωσκω</span></span>, to eat, somewhat like our "edibles" or vernacular "eats." </p>
