<p><b> About </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωσε</span></span>). Luke as <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:21">Matthew 14:21</span> adds this word to the definite statement of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:44">Mark 6:44</span> that there were 5,000 men, a hundred companies of fifty each.</p>
<p><b>Sit down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατακλινατε</span></span>). First aorist active imperative. Recline, lie down. Only in Luke in the N.T. See also verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:15">Luke 9:15</span>.</p>
<p><b>In companies </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλισιας</span></span>). Cognate accusative after</p>
<p><b>kataklinate </b>. Only here in the N.T. A row of persons reclining at meals (table company).</p>
<p><b>About fifty each </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωσε ανα πεντηκοντα</span></span>). Distributive use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανα</span></span> and approximate number again (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωσε</span></span>). </p>
