<p><b> He sent them forth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεστειλεν αυτους</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστελλω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>To preach the kingdom of God and to heal the sick </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κηρυσσειν την βασιλειαν του θεου κα ιασθα</span></span>). Present indicative for the continuous functions during this campaign. This double office of herald (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κηρυσσειν</span></span>) and healer (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιασθα</span></span>) is stated directly in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:7-8">Matthew 10:7-8</span>. Note the verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιαομα</span></span> for healing here, though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεραπευειν</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:1">Luke 9:1</span>, apparently used interchangeably. </p>
