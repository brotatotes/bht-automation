<p><b> Whosoever shall be ashamed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος αν επαισχυνθη</span></span>). Rather,</p>
<p><b>Whosoever is ashamed </b> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:38">Mark 8:38</span>. The first aorist passive subjunctive in an indefinite relative clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span>. The passive verb is transitive here also. This verb is from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αισχυνη</span></span>, shame (in the eyes of men). Jesus endured the shame of the cross (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:2">Hebrews 12:2</span>). The man at the feast who had to take a lower seat did it with shame (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:9">Luke 14:9</span>). Paul is not ashamed of the Gospel (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:16">Romans 1:16</span>). Onesiphorus was not ashamed of Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:16">2 Timothy 1:16</span>).</p>
<p><b>In his own glory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη δοξη αυτου</span></span>). This item added to what is in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:38">Mark 8:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:27">Matthew 16:27</span>. </p>
