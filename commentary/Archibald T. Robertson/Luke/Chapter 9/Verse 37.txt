<p><b> On the next day </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη εξης ημερα</span></span>). Alone in Luke. It shows that the Transfiguration took place on the preceding night.</p>
<p><b>They were come down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατελθοντων αυτων</span></span>). Genitive absolute of second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατερχομα</span></span>, a common enough verb, but in the N.T. only in Luke's writings save <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:15">James 3:15</span>.</p>
<p><b>Met him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνηντησεν αυτω</span></span>). First aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνανταω</span></span>, common compound verb, to meet with, only in Luke's writings in the N.T. save <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:1">Hebrews 7:1</span>. With associative instrumental case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτω</span></span>. </p>
