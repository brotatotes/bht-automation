<p><b> Master </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Διδασκαλε</span></span>). Teacher as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:17">Mark 9:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>Lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυριε</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:15">Matthew 17:15</span>).</p>
<p><b>To look upon </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιβλεψα</span></span>). Aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιβλεπω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>, upon, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλεπω</span></span>, look), common verb, but in the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:3">James 2:3</span> except <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:48">Luke 1:48</span> in quotation from LXX. This compound verb is common in medical writers for examining carefully the patient.</p>
<p><b>Mine only child </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μονογενης μο</span></span>). Only in Luke as already about an only child in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:12">Luke 7:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:42">Luke 8:42</span>. </p>
