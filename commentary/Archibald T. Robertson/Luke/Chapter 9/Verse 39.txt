<p><b> Suddenly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεφνης</span></span>). Old adverb, but in the N.T. only in Luke's writings save <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:36">Mark 13:36</span>. Used by medical writers of sudden attacks of disease like epilepsy.</p>
<p><b>It teareth him that he foameth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπαρασσε αυτον μετα αφρου</span></span>). Literally, "It tears him with (accompanied with, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα</span></span>) foam" (old word, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφρος</span></span>, only here in the N.T.). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπαρασσω</span></span>, to convulse, a common verb, but in the N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:26">Mark 1:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:26">Mark 9:26</span> (and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνσπαρασσω</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:20">Mark 9:20</span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:17">Mark 9:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:15">Matthew 17:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:39">Luke 9:39</span> for variations in the symptoms in each Gospel. The use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα αφρου</span></span> is a medical item.</p>
<p><b>Hardly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μολις</span></span>). Late word used in place of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μογις</span></span>, the old Greek term (in some MSS. here) and alone in Luke's writings in the N.T. save <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:18">1 Peter 4:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:7">Romans 5:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>Bruising him sorely </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συντριβον αυτον</span></span>). Common verb for rubbing together, crushing together like chains (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:4">Mark 5:4</span>) or as a vase (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:3">Mark 14:3</span>). See on Matthew and Mark for discussion of details here. </p>
