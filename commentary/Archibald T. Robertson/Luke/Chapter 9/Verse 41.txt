<p><b> How long shall I be with you and bear with you? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως ποτε εσομα προς υμας κα ανεξομα υμων;</span></span>). Here the two questions of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:19">Mark 9:19</span> (only one in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:17">Matthew 17:17</span>) are combined in one sentence.</p>
<p><b>Bear with </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεξομα</span></span>, direct middle future) is, hold myself from you (ablative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμων</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Faithless </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απιστος</span></span>) is disbelieving and perverse (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διεστραμμενη</span></span>, perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαστρεφω</span></span>), is twisted, turned, or torn in two. </p>
