<p><b> Sink into your ears </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Θεσθε υμεις εις τα ωτα υμων</span></span>). Second aorist imperative middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span>, common verb. "Do you (note emphatic position) yourselves (whatever others do) put into your ears." No word like "sink" here. The same prediction here as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:31">Mark 9:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:22">Matthew 17:22</span> about the Son of man only without mention of death and resurrection as there, which see for discussion. </p>
