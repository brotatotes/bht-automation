<p><b> Took a little child </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλαβομενος παιδιον</span></span>). Second aorist middle participle of the common verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλαμβανω</span></span>. Strictly, Taking a little child to himself (indirect middle). <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:36">Mark 9:36</span> has merely the active <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαβων</span></span> of the simple verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>. Set him by his side (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστησεν αυτο παρ' εαυτω</span></span>). "In his arms" <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:36">Mark 9:36</span> has it, "in the midst of them" <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:3">Matthew 18:3</span> says. All three attitudes following one another (the disciples probably in a circle around Jesus anyhow) and now the little child (Peter's child?) was slipped down by the side of Jesus as he gave the disciples an object lesson in humility which they sorely needed. </p>
