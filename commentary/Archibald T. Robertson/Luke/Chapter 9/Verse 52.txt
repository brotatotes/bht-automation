<p><b> Sent messengers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεστειλεν αγγελους</span></span>). As a precaution since he was going to Jerusalem through Samaria. The Samaritans did not object when people went north from Jerusalem through their country. He was repudiating Mount Gerizim by going by it to Jerusalem. This was an unusual precaution by Jesus and we do not know who the messengers (<b> angels </b>) were.</p>
<p><b>To make ready for him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως ετοιμασα αυτω</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hως</span></span> is correct here, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωστε</span></span>. The only examples of the final use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως</span></span> with the infinitive in the N.T. are this one and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:9">Hebrews 7:9</span> (absolute use). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:24">Acts 20:24</span> Westcott and Hort read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως τελειωσω</span></span> and put <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως τελειωσα</span></span> in the margin (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 1091). </p>
