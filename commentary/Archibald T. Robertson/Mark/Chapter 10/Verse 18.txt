<p><b> Why callest thou me good? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Τ με λεγεις αγαθον;</span></span>). So <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:19">Luke 18:19</span>. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:17">Matthew 19:17</span> has it: "Why asketh thou concerning that which is good? "The young ruler was probably sincere and not using mere fulsome compliment, but Jesus challenges him to define his attitude towards him as was proper. Did he mean "good" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαθος</span></span>) in the absolute sense as applied to God? The language is not a disclaiming of deity on the part of Jesus.</p>
<p><b>That I may inherit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα κληρονομησω</span></span>). <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:16">Matthew 19:16</span> has (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σχω</span></span>), that I may "get." </p>
