<p><b> But his countenance fell </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο δε στυγνασας</span></span>). In the LXX and Polybius once and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:3">Matthew 16:3</span> (passage bracketed by Westcott and Hort). The verb is from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στυγνος</span></span>, sombre, gloomy, like a lowering cloud. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:22">Matthew 19:22</span> for discussion of "sorrowful" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυπουμενος</span></span>). </p>
