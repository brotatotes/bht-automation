<p><b> Needle's eye </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρυμαλιας ραφιδος</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:24">Matthew 19:24</span> for discussion. Luke uses the surgical needle, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βελονης</span></span>. Matthew has the word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ραφις</span></span> like Mark from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ραπτω</span></span>, to sew, and it appears in the papyri. Both Matthew and Luke employ <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρηματος</span></span> for eye, a perforation or hole from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιτραω</span></span>, to bore. Mark's word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρυμαλιας</span></span> is from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρυω</span></span>, to wear away, to perforate. In the LXX and Plutarch. </p>
