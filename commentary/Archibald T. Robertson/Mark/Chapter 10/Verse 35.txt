<p><b> There come near unto him James and John </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα προσπορευοντα Ιακωβος κα Ιωανης</span></span>). Dramatic present tense. Matthew has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοτε</span></span>, then, showing that the request of the two brothers with their mother (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:20">Matthew 20:20</span>) comes immediately after the talk about Christ's death.</p>
<p><b>We would </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θελομεν</span></span>). We wish, we want, bluntly told.</p>
<p><b>She came worshipping </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσκυνουσα</span></span>) Matthew says. The mother spoke for the sons. But they try to commit Jesus to their desires before they tell what they are, just like spoiled children. </p>
