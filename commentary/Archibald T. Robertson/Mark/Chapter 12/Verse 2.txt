<p><b> At the season </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω καιρω</span></span>). For fruits as in the end of the sentence.</p>
<p><b>A servant </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δουλον</span></span>). Bondslave. Matthew has plural.</p>
<p><b>That he might receive </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα λαβη</span></span>). Purpose clause with second aorist subjunctive. Matthew has infinitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαβειν</span></span>, purpose also.</p>
<p><b>Wounded in the head </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεφαλιωσαν</span></span>). An old verb (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεφαλαιω</span></span>), to bring under heads (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεφαλη</span></span>), to summarize. Then to hit on the head. Only here in the N.T. </p>
