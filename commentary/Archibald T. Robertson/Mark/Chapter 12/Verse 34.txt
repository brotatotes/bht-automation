<p><b> Discreetly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νουνεχως</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νους</span></span> (intellect) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>, to have. Using the mind to good effect is what the adverb means. He had his wits about him, as we say. Here only in the N.T. In Aristotle and Polybius. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Νουνεχοντως</span></span> would be the more regular form, adverb from a participle.</p>
<p><b>Not far </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μακραν</span></span>). Adverb, not adjective, feminine accusative, a long way (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδον</span></span> understood). The critical attitude of the lawyer had melted before the reply of Jesus into genuine enthusiasm that showed him to be near the kingdom of God.</p>
<p><b>No man after that </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδεις ουκετ</span></span>). Double negative. The debate was closed (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετολμα</span></span>, imperfect tense, dared). Jesus was complete victor on every side. </p>
