<p><b> One poor widow </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μια χηρα πτωχη</span></span>). Luke has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πενιχρα</span></span>, a poetical late form of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πενης</span></span>. In the N.T. the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πτωχος</span></span> is the pauper rather than the mere peasant, the extreme opposite of the rich (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλουσιο</span></span>). The money given by most was copper (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαλκον</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Two mites </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυο λεπτα</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Λεπτος</span></span> means peeled or stripped and so very thin. Two <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεπτα</span></span> were about two-fifths of a cent.</p>
