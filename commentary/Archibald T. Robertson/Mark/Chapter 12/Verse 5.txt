<p><b> Beating some and killing some </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ους μεν δεροντεσ, ους δε αποκτεννυντες</span></span>). This distributive use of the demonstrative appears also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:35">Matthew 21:35</span> in the singular (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ον μεν, ον δε, ον δε</span></span>). Originally <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δερω</span></span> in Homer meant to skin, flay, then to smite, to beat. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αποκτεννυντες</span></span> is a <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μ</span></span> form of the verb (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκτεννυμ</span></span>) and means to kill off. </p>
