<p><b> But he that endureth to the end </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο δε υπομεινας εις τελος</span></span>). Note this aorist participle with the future verb. The idea here is true to the etymology of the word, remaining under (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπομενω</span></span>) until the end. The divisions in families Jesus had predicted before (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:52">Luke 12:52</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:25">Luke 14:25</span>).</p>
<p><b>Be saved </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωθησετα</span></span>). Here Jesus means final salvation (effective aorist future passive), not initial salvation. </p>
