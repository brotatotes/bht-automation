<p><b> The sun shall be darkened </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ελιος σκοτισθησετα</span></span>). Future passive indicative. These figures come from the prophets (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+13:9">Isaiah 13:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+32:7">Ezekiel 32:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joe+2:1">Joel 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joe+2:10">Joel 2:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="am+8:9">Amos 8:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="zep+1:14-16">Zephaniah 1:14-16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="zec+12:12">Zechariah 12:12</span>). One should not forget that prophetic imagery was not always meant to be taken literally, especially apocalyptic symbols. Peter in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:15-21">Acts 2:15-21</span> applies the prophecy of Joel about the sun and moon to the events on the day of Pentecost. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:29-31">Matthew 24:29-31</span> for details of verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:24-27">Mark 13:24-27</span>. </p>
