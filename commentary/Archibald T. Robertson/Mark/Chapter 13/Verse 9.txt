<p><b> But take heed to yourselves </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Βλεπετε δε υμεις εαυτους</span></span>). Only in Mark, but dominant note of warning all through the discourse. Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμεις</span></span> here, very emphatic.</p>
<p><b>Councils </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεδρια</span></span>). Same word as the Sanhedrin in Jerusalem. These local councils (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν, εδρα</span></span>, sitting together) were modelled after that in Jerusalem.</p>
<p><b>Shall ye be beaten </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δαρησεσθε</span></span>). Second future passive indicative second person plural. The word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δερω</span></span> means to flay or skin and here has been softened into</p>
<p><b>beat </b> like our tan or skin in the vernacular. Aristophanes has it in this colloquial sense as have the papyri in the <i>Koine</i>. Before governors and kings (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ ηγεμονων κα βασιλεων</span></span>). Gentile rulers as well as before Jewish councils.</p>
<p><b>Shall stand </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σταθησεσθε</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative second person plural of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span>. </p>
