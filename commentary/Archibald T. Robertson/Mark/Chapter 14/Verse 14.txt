<p><b> The goodman of the house </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω οικοδεσποτη</span></span>). A non-classical word, but in late papyri. It means master (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεσποτ</span></span>) of the house, householder. The usual Greek has two separate words, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικου δεσποτης</span></span> (master of the house).</p>
<p><b>My guest-chamber </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το καταλυμα μου</span></span>). In LXX, papyri, and modern Greek for lodging-place (inn, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:7">Luke 2:7</span> or guest-chamber as here). It was used for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καν</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαραςανσερα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>I shall eat </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φαγω</span></span>). Futuristic aorist subjunctive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπου</span></span>. </p>
