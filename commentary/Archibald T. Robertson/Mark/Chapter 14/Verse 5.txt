<p><b> Above three hundred pence </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επανω δηναριων τριακοσιων</span></span>). Matthew has "for much" while <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:5">John 12:5</span> has "for three hundred pence." The use of "far above" may be a detail from Peter's memory of Judas' objection whose name in this connection is preserved in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:4">John 12:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>And they murmured against her </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ενεβριμωντο αυτη</span></span>). Imperfect tense of this striking word used of the snorting of horses and seen already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:43">Mark 1:43</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:38">Mark 11:38</span>. It occurs in the LXX in the sense of anger as here (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="da+11:30">Daniel 11:30</span>). Judas made the complaint against Mary of Bethany, but all the apostles joined in the chorus of criticism of the wasteful extravagance. </p>
