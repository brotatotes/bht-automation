<p><b> They compel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγγαρευουσιν</span></span>). Dramatic present indicative again where <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:32">Matthew 27:32</span> has the aorist. For this Persian word see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:41">Matthew 5:41</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:32">Matthew 27:32</span>.</p>
<p><b>Coming out of the country </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομενον απ' αγρου</span></span>). Hence Simon met the procession. Mark adds that he was "the father of Alexander and Rufus." Paul mentions a Rufus in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:13">Romans 16:13</span>, but it was a common name and proves nothing. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:32">Matthew 27:32</span> for discussion of cross-bearing by criminals. Luke adds "after Jesus" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπισθεν του Ιησου</span></span>). But Jesus bore his own cross till he was relieved of it, and he walked in front of his own cross for the rest of the way. </p>
