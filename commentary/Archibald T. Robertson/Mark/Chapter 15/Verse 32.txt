<p><b> Now come down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταβατω νυν</span></span>). Now that he is nailed to the cross.</p>
<p><b>That we may see and believe </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα ιδωμεν κα πιστευσωμεν</span></span>). Aorist subjunctive of purpose with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>. They use almost the very language of Jesus in their ridicule, words that they had heard him use in his appeals to men to see and believe.</p>
<p><b>Reproached him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωνειδιζον αυτον</span></span>). Imperfect tense. They did it several times. Mark and Matthew both fail to give the story of the robber who turned to Christ on the Cross as told in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:39-43">Luke 23:39-43</span>. </p>
