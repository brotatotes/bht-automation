<p><b> The centurion </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο κεντυριων</span></span>). A Latin word (<i>centurio</i>) used also in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:44">Mark 15:44</span> and here only in the N.T.</p>
<p><b>Which stood by over against him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο παρεστηκως εξ εναντιας αυτου</span></span>). This description alone in Mark, picturing the centurion "watching Jesus" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:54">Matthew 27:54</span>).</p>
<p><b>So </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτως</span></span>). With the darkness and the earthquake. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:54">Matthew 27:54</span> for discussion of "the Son of God," more probably "a Son of God." </p>
