<p><b> When the sabbath was past </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαγενομενου του σαββατου</span></span>). Genitive absolute, the sabbath having come in between, and now over. For this sense of the verb (common from Demosthenes on) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:13">Acts 25:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:9">Acts 27:9</span>. It was therefore after sunset.</p>
<p><b>Bought spices </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγορασαν αρωματα</span></span>). As Nicodemus did on the day of the burial (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:40">John 19:40</span>). Gould denies that the Jews were familiar with the embalming process of Egypt, but at any rate it was to be a reverential anointing (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα αλειψωσιν</span></span>) of the body of Jesus with spices. They could buy them after sundown. Salome in the group again as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:40">Mark 15:40</span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:1">Matthew 28:1</span> for discussion of "late on the sabbath day" and the visit of the women to the tomb before sundown. They had returned from the tomb after the watching late Friday afternoon and had prepared spices (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:56">Luke 23:56</span>). Now they secured a fresh supply. </p>
