<p><b> Disbelieved </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηπιστησαν</span></span>). This verb is common in the ancient Greek, but rare in the N.T. and here again verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+16:16">Mark 16:16</span> and nowhere else in Mark. The usual N.T. word is <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απειθεω</span></span>. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:11">Luke 24:11</span> uses this verb (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηπιστουν</span></span>) of the disbelief of the report of Mary Magdalene and the other women. The verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εθεαθη</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεαωμα</span></span>) occurs only here and in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+16:14">Mark 16:14</span> in Mark. </p>
