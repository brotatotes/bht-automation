<p><b> Before them all </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμπροσθεν παντων</span></span>). <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:25">Luke 5:25</span> follows Mark in this detail. He picked up (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρας</span></span>) his pallet and walked and went home as Jesus had commanded him to do (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:11">Mark 2:11</span>). It was an amazing proceeding and made it unnecessary for Jesus to refute the scribes further on this occasion. The amazement (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξιστασθα</span></span>, our <i>ecstasy</i>, as <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:26">Luke 5:26</span> has it), was too general and great for words. The people could only say: "We never saw it on this fashion" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hουτως ουδεποτε ειδαμεν</span></span>). Jesus had acted with the power of God and claimed equality with God and had made good his claim. They all marvelled at the</p>
<p><b>paradoxes </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραδοξα</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:26">Luke 5:26</span>) of that day. For it all they glorified God. </p>
