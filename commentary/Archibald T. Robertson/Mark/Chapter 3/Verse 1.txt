<p><b> Had his hand withered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξηραμμενην εχων την χειρα</span></span>). He had his (<b> the </b> in the Greek, common idiom with article as possessive) hand (right hand, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:6">Luke 6:6</span>) in a withered state, perfect passive participle (adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξηραν</span></span> in Matthew and Luke), showing that it was not congenital, but the result of injury by accident or disease. Bengel: <i>Non ex utero, sed morbo aut vulnere</i>. </p>
