<p><b> Is ripe </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραδο</span></span>, second aorist subjunctive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν</span></span>). Whenever the fruit yields itself or permits.</p>
<p><b>Putteth forth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστελλε</span></span>). Sends forth the sickle. The word for <i>apostle</i> comes from this verb. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:38">John 4:38</span>: "I sent you forth to reap" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγο απεστειλα υμας θεριζειν</span></span>). Sickle (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δρεπανον</span></span>) here by metonymy stands for the reapers who use it when the harvest stands ready for it (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεστηκεν</span></span>, stands by the side, present perfect indicative). </p>
