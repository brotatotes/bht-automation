<p><b> Groweth up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναβαινε</span></span>). <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:32">Matthew 13:32</span> <b> When it is grown </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν αυξηθη</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Under the shadow thereof </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο την σκιαν αυτου</span></span>). A different picture from Matthew's</p>
<p><b>in the branches thereof </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τοις κλαδοις αυτου</span></span>). But both use <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατασκηνοιν</span></span>, to tent or camp down, make nests in the branches in the shade or hop on the ground under the shade just like a covey of birds. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:20">Matthew 8:20</span> the birds have nests (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατασκηνωσεις</span></span>). The use of the mustard seed for smallness seems to have been proverbial and Jesus employs it elsewhere (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:20">Matthew 17:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:6">Luke 17:6</span>). </p>
