<p><b> My little daughter </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το θυγατριον μου</span></span>). Diminutive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυγατηρ</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:18">Matthew 9:18</span>). "This little endearing touch in the use of the diminutive is peculiar to Mark" (Vincent). "Is at the point of death" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσχατως εχε</span></span>). Has it in the last stages. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:18">Matthew 9:18</span> has: "has just died" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρτ ετελευσεν</span></span>), Luke "she lay a dying" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεθνησκεν</span></span>, imperfect, she was dying). It was a tragic moment for Jairus.</p>
<p><b>I pray thee </b>, not in the Greek. This ellipsis before <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> not uncommon, a sort of imperative use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the subjunctive in the <i>Koine</i> (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 943). </p>
