<p><b> He went with him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απηλθεν</span></span>). Aorist tense. Went off with him promptly, but a great multitude followed him (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκολουθε</span></span>), was following, kept following (imperfect tense).</p>
<p><b>They thronged him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεθλιβον αυτον</span></span>). Imperfect tense again. Only example of (here and in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:31">Mark 5:31</span>) this compound verb in the N.T., common in old Greek. Were pressing Jesus so that he could hardly move because of the jam, or even to breathe (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεπνιγον</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:42">Luke 8:42</span>). </p>
