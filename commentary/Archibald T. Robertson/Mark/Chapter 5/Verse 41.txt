<p><b> Talitha cumi </b>. These precious Aramaic words, spoken by Jesus to the child, Peter heard and remembered so that Mark gives them to us. Mark interprets the simple words into Greek for those who did not know Aramaic (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το κορασιον, εγειρε</span></span>), that is,</p>
<p><b>Damsel, arise </b>. Mark uses the diminutive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κορασιων</span></span>, a little girl, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κορη</span></span>, girl. <i>Braid Scots</i> has it: "Lassie, wauken." <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:5-9">Luke 8:5-9</span> has it <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hη παισ, εγειρε</span></span>,</p>
<p><b>Maiden, arise </b>. All three Gospels mention the fact that Jesus took her by the hand, a touch of life (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρατησας της χειρος</span></span>), giving confidence and help. </p>
