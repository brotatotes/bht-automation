<p><b> Into his own country </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις την πατριδα αυτου</span></span>). So <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:54">Matthew 13:54</span>. There is no real reason for identifying this visit to Nazareth with that recorded in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:26-31">Luke 4:26-31</span> at the beginning of the Galilean Ministry. He was rejected both times, but it is not incongruous that Jesus should give Nazareth a second chance. It was only natural for Jesus to visit his mother, brothers, and sisters again. Neither Mark nor Matthew mention Nazareth here by name, but it is plain that by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πατριδα</span></span> the region of Nazareth is meant. He had not lived in Bethlehem since his birth. </p>
