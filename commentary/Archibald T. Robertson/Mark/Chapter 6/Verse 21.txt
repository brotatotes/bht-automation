<p><b> When a convenient day was come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενομενης ημερας ευκαιρου</span></span>). Genitive absolute. A day well appointed <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ</span></span>, well, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιρος</span></span>, time) for the purpose, the day for which she had long waited. She had her plans all laid to spring a trap for her husband Herod Antipas and to make him do her will with the Baptist. Herod was not to know that he was the mere catspaw of Herodias till it was all over. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:6">Matthew 14:6</span> for discussion of Herod's birthday (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενεσιοις</span></span>, locative case or associative instrumental of time).</p>
<p><b>Made a supper </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δειπνον εποιησεν</span></span>). Banquet.</p>
<p><b>To his lords </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις μεγιστασιν αυτου</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεγισταν</span></span> (that from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεγας</span></span>, great), common in the LXX and later Greek. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:15">Revelation 6:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:23">Revelation 18:23</span>. In the papyri. The grandees, magnates, nobles, the chief men of civil life.</p>
<p><b>The high captains </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις χιλιαρχοις</span></span>). Military tribunes, commanders of a thousand men.</p>
<p><b>The chief men of Galilee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις πρωτοις της Γαλιλαιας</span></span>). The first men of social importance and prominence. A notable gathering that included these three groups at the banquet on Herod's birthday. </p>
