<p><b> Seeing them distressed in rowing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδων αυτους βασανιζομενους εν τω ελαυνειν</span></span>). See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:29">Matthew 8:29</span> for the word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασανιζω</span></span>, to torture, torment (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:24">Matthew 4:24</span>) with a touch-stone, then to distress as here. Papyri have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια βασανων</span></span> used on slaves like our third degree for criminals. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ελαυνειν</span></span> is literally to drive as of ships or chariots. They drove the boat with oars. Common in Xenophon for marching.</p>
<p><b>About the fourth watch of the night </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ τεταρτην φυλακην της νυκτος</span></span>). That is, between three and six A.M. The wind was</p>
<p><b>contrary to them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εναντιος αυτοις</span></span>), that is in their faces and rowing was difficult, "a great wind" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:18">John 6:18</span>), and as a result the disciples had made little progress. They should have been over long before this.</p>
<p><b>And he would have passed by them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ηθελεν παρελθειν αυτους</span></span>). Only in Mark. He wished to pass by them, <i>praeterire eos</i> (Vulgate). Imperfect tense <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηθελεν</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>They thought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδοξαν</span></span>). A natural conclusion.</p>
<p><b>And cried out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεκραξαν</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Cried up </b>, literally, a shriek of terror, or scream. </p>
