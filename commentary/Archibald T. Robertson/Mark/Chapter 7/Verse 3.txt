<p><b> Diligently </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυγμη</span></span>). Instrumental case,</p>
<p><b>with the fist </b>, up to the elbow, rubbing one hand and arm with the other hand clenched. Aleph had <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυκνα</span></span> probably because of the difficulty about <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυγμη</span></span> (kin to Latin <i>pugnus</i>). Schultess considers it a dry wash or rubbing of the hands without water as a ritualistic concession. The middle voice <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νιψωντα</span></span> means their own hands. This verb is often used for parts of the body while <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λουω</span></span> is used of the whole body (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:10">John 13:10</span>). On the tradition of the elders see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:2">Matthew 15:2</span>. </p>
