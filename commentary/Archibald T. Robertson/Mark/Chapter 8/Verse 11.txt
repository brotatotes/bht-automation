<p><b> And the Pharisees came forth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα εξηλθον ο Φαρισαιο</span></span>). At once they met Jesus and opened a controversy. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:1">Matthew 16:1</span> adds "and Sadducees," the first time these two parties appear together against Jesus. See discussion on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:1">Matthew 16:1</span>. The Pharisees and Herodians had already joined hands against Jesus in the sabbath controversy (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:6">Mark 3:6</span>). They</p>
<p><b>began to question with him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηρξαντο συνζητειν αυτω</span></span>). Dispute, not mere inquiry, associative instrumental case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτο</span></span>. They began at once and kept it up (present infinitive). </p>
