<p><b> He looked steadfastly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διεβλεψεν</span></span>). He saw thoroughly now, effective aorist (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διεβλεψεν</span></span>), he was completely restored (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεκατεστη</span></span>, second aorist, double compound and double augment), and kept on seeing (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεβλεπεν</span></span>, imperfect, continued action) all things clearly or at a distance (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηλαυγως</span></span>, common Greek word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηλε</span></span>, afar, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυγη</span></span>, radiance, far-shining). Some manuscripts (margin in Westcott and Hort) read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δηλαυγως</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δηλος</span></span>, plain, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυγη</span></span>, radiance. </p>
