<p><b> Tare him grievously </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεσπαραξεν αυτον</span></span>). <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:42">Luke 9:42</span> has both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερρηξεν</span></span> (dashed down, like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:18">Mark 9:18</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρησσε</span></span>) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεσπαραξεν</span></span> (convulsed). This compound with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν-</span></span> (together with), strengthens the force of the verb as in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνπνιγω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:7">Mark 4:7</span>) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συντηρεω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:20">Mark 6:20</span>). The only other instance of this compound verb known is in Maximus Tyrius (second century B.C.).</p>
<p><b>Wallowed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκυλιετο</span></span>). Imperfect passive, was rolled. A pitiful sight. Late form of the old <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυλινδω</span></span>. </p>
