<p><b> Cried out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κραξας</span></span>). Loud outcry and at once (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευθυς</span></span>). The later manuscripts have "with tears" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα δακρυων</span></span>), not in the older documents.</p>
<p><b>I believe; help my unbelief </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πιστευω: βοηθε τη απιστια</span></span>). An exact description of his mental and spiritual state. He still had faith, but craved more. Note present imperative here (continuous help) <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βοηθε</span></span>, while aorist imperative (instant help) <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βοηθησον</span></span>, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:22">Mark 9:22</span>. The word comes from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βοη</span></span>, a cry and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεω</span></span>, to run, to run at a cry for help, a vivid picture of this father's plight. </p>
