<p><b> Glistering, exceeding white </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στιλβοντα λευκα λιαν</span></span>). Old words, all of them. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:2">Matthew 17:2</span> has</p>
<p><b>white as the light </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λευκα ως το φως</span></span>), <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:29">Luke 9:29</span> "white and dazzling" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λευκος εξαστραπτων</span></span>) like lightning.</p>
<p><b>So as no fuller on earth can whiten them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οια γναφευς επ της γης ου δυνατα ουτως λευκανα</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Γναφω</span></span> is an old word to card wool. Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτως</span></span>, so, so white. Some manuscripts in Matthew add <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως χιων</span></span>, as snow. Probably the snow-capped summit of Hermon was visible on this very night. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:2">Matthew 17:2</span> for "transfigured." </p>
