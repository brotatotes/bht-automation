<p><b> Shake off the dust </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκτιναξατε τον κονιορτον</span></span>). Shake out, a rather violent gesture of disfavour. The Jews had violent prejudices against the smallest particles of Gentile dust, not as a purveyor of disease of which they did not know, but because it was regarded as the putrescence of death. If the apostles were mistreated by a host or hostess, they were to be treated as if they were Gentiles (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:17">Matthew 18:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:6">Acts 18:6</span>). Here again we have a restriction that was for this special tour with its peculiar perils. </p>
