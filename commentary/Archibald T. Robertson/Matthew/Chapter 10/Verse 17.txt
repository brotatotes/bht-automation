<p><b> Beware of men </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσεχετε απο των ανθρωπων</span></span>). Ablative case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span>. Hold your mind (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νουν</span></span> understood) away from. The article with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωπων</span></span> points back to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυκων</span></span> (wolves) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:16">Matthew 10:16</span>. </p>
<p><b> To councils </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις συνεδρια</span></span>). The local courts of justice in every Jewish town. The word is an old one from Herodotus on for any deliberative body (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χονχιλιυμ</span></span>). The same word is used for the Sanhedrin in Jerusalem.</p>
<p><b>In their synagogues </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τοις συναγωγαις αυτων</span></span>). Here not merely as the place of assembly for worship, but as an assembly of justice exercising discipline as when the man born blind was cast out of the synagogue (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:35">John 9:35</span>). They were now after the exile in every town of any size where Jews were. </p>
