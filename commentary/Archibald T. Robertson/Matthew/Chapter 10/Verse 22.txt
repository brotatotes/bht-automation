<p><b> Ye shall be hated </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσεσθε μισουμενο</span></span>). Periphrastic future passive, linear action. It will go on through the ages.</p>
<p><b>For my name's sake </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια το ονομα μου</span></span>). In the O.T. as in the Targums and the Talmud "the name" as here stands for the person (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:29">Matthew 19:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:41">Acts 5:41</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:16">Acts 9:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:26">Acts 15:26</span>). "He that endureth to the end" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο υπομεινας εις τελος</span></span>). Effective aorist participle with future indicative. </p>
