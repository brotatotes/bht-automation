<p><b> Fear them not therefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη ουν φοβηθητε αυτους</span></span>). Repeated in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:28">Matthew 10:28</span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:31">Matthew 10:31</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη φοβεισθε</span></span> present middle imperative here in contrast with aorist passive subjunctive in the preceding prohibitions). Note also the accusative case with the aorist passive subjunctive, transitive though passive. See same construction in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:5">Luke 12:5</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:28">Matthew 10:28</span> the construction is with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span> and the ablative, a translation Hebraism as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:4">Luke 12:4</span> (Robertson, <i>Grammar of the Greek N.T. in the Light of Historical Research</i>, p. 577). </p>
