<p><b> Two sparrows </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυο στρουθια</span></span>). Diminutive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρουθος</span></span> and means any small bird, sparrows in particular. They are sold today in the markets of Jerusalem and Jaffa. "For a farthing" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασσαριου</span></span>) is genitive of price. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:6">Luke 12:6</span> in the N.T. Diminutive form of the Roman <i>as</i>, slightly more than half an English penny.</p>
<p><b>Without your Father </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανευ του πατρος υμων</span></span>). There is comfort in this thought for us all. Our father who knows about the sparrows knows and cares about us. </p>
