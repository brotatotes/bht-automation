<p><b> A bruised reed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλαμον συντετριμμενον</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συντριβω</span></span>. A crushed reed he will not break. The curious augment in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεαξε</span></span> (future active indicative) is to be noted. The copyists kept the augment where it did not belong in this verb (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 1212) even in Plato. "Smoking flax" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λινον τυφομενον</span></span>). The wick of a lamp, smoking and flickering and going out. Only here in N.T. Flax in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+9:31">Exodus 9:31</span>. Vivid images that picture Jesus in the same strain as his own great words in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:28-30">Matthew 11:28-30</span>. </p>
