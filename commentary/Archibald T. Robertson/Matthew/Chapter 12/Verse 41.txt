<p><b> In the judgment </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη κρισε</span></span>). Except here and in the next verse Matthew has "day of judgment" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημερα κρισεως</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:15">Matthew 10:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:22">Matthew 11:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:24">Matthew 11:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:36">Matthew 12:36</span>. Luke (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:14">Luke 10:14</span>) has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη κρισε</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>They repented at the preaching of Jonah </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετενοησον εις το κηρυγμα Ιωνα</span></span>). Note this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> just like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span>. Note also <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλειον</span></span> (neuter), not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλειων</span></span> (masc.). See the same idiom in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:6">Matthew 12:6</span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:48">Matthew 12:48</span>. Jesus is something greater than the temple, than Jonah, than Solomon. "You will continue to disbelieve in spite of all I can say or do, and at last you will put me to death. But I will rise again, a sign for your confusion, if not for your conversion" (Bruce). </p>
