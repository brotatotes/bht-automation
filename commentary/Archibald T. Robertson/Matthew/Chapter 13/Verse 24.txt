<p><b> Set he before them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεθηκεν</span></span>). So again in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:31">Matthew 13:31</span>. He placed another parable beside (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span>) the one already given and explained. The same verb (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραθεινα</span></span>) occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:16">Luke 9:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>Is likened </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωμοιωθη</span></span>). Timeless aorist passive and a common way of introducing these parables of the kingdom where a comparison is drawn (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:23">Matthew 18:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:2">Matthew 22:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:1">Matthew 25:1</span>). The case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωπω</span></span> is associative instrumental. </p>
