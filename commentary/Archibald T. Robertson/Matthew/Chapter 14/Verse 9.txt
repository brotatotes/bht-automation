<p><b> Grieved </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυπηθεις</span></span>). Not to hurt, for in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:5">Matthew 14:5</span> we read that he wanted (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θελων</span></span>) to put him to death (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκτεινα</span></span>). Herod, however, shrank from so dastardly a deed as this public display of brutality and bloodthirstiness. Men who do wrong always have some flimsy excuses for their sins. A man here orders a judicial murder of the most revolting type "for the sake of his oath" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια τους ορκους</span></span>). "More like profane swearing than deliberate utterance once for all of a solemn oath" (Bruce). He was probably maudlin with wine and befuddled by the presence of the guests. </p>
