<p><b> Are ye also even yet without understanding? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ακμην κα υμεις ασυνετο εστε</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ακμην</span></span> is an adverbial accusative (classic <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιχμη</span></span>, point (of a weapon)=<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακμην χρονου</span></span> at this point of time, just now=<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετ</span></span>. It occurs in papyri and inscriptions, though condemned by the old grammarians. "In spite of all my teaching, are ye also like the Pharisees without spiritual insight and grasp?" One must never forget that the disciples lived in a Pharisaic environment. Their religious world-outlook was Pharisaic. They were lacking in spiritual intelligence or sense, "totally ignorant" (Moffatt). </p>
