<p><b> If thy brother sin against thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν αμαρτηση αδελφος σου</span></span>). Literally, commit a sin (ingressive aorist subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτανω</span></span>). Aleph B Sahidic do not have "against thee" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις σε</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Shew him his fault </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεγξον</span></span>). Such private reproof is hard to do, but it is the way of Christ.</p>
<p><b>Thou hast gained </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκερδησας</span></span>). Aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κερδαινω</span></span> in conclusion of a third-class condition, a sort of timeless aorist, a blessed achievement already made. </p>
