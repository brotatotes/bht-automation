<p><b> And he would not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο δε ουκ ηθελεν</span></span>). Imperfect tense of persistent refusal.</p>
<p><b>Till he should pay </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως αποδω</span></span>). This futuristic aorist subjunctive is the rule with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως</span></span> for a future goal. He was to stay in prison till he should pay. "He acts on the instinct of a base nature, and also doubtless in accordance with long habits of harsh tyrannical behaviour towards men in his power" (Bruce). On imprisonment for debt among the Greeks and Romans see Deissmann, <i>Light from the Ancient East</i>, pp. 270,330. </p>
