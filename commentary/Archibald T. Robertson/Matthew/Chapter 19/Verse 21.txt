<p><b> If thou wouldest be perfect </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε θελεις τελειος εινα</span></span>). Condition of the first class, determined as fulfilled. Jesus assumes that the young man really desires to be perfect (a big adjective that, perfect as God is the goal, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:48">Matthew 5:48</span>).</p>
<p><b>That thou hast </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σου τα υπαρχοντα</span></span>). "Thy belongings." The Greek neuter plural participle used like our English word "belongings." It was a huge demand, for he was rich. </p>
