<p><b> For your hardness of heart </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς την σκληροκαρδιαν υμων</span></span>). The word is apparently one of the few Biblical words (LXX and the N.T.). It is a heart dried up (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκληρος</span></span>), hard and tough.</p>
<p><b>But from the beginning it hath not been so </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απ' αρχης δε ουκ γεγονεν ουτως</span></span>). The present perfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span> to emphasize the permanence of the divine ideal. "The original ordinance has never been abrogated nor superseded, but continues in force" (Vincent). "How small the Pharisaic disputants must have felt in presence of such holy teaching, which soars above the partisan view of controversialists into the serene region of ideal, universal, eternal truth" (Bruce). </p>
