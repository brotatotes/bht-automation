<p><b> For </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γαρ</span></span>). The parable of the house illustrates the aphorism in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:30">Matthew 19:30</span>.</p>
<p><b>A man that is a householder </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωπω οικοδεσποτη</span></span>). Just like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωπω βασιλε</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:23">Matthew 18:23</span>). Not necessary to translate <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωπω</span></span>, just "a householder." </p>
<p><b> Early in the morning </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμα πρω</span></span>). A classic idiom. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hαμα</span></span> as an "improper" preposition is common in the papyri. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πρω</span></span> is just an adverb in the locative. At the same time with early dawn, break of day, country fashion for starting to work.</p>
<p><b>To hire </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μισθωσασθα</span></span>). The middle voice aorist tense, to hire for oneself. </p>
