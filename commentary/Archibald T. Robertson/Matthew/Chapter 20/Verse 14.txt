<p><b> Take up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρον</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιρω</span></span>. Pick up, as if he had saucily refused to take it from the table or had contemptuously thrown the denarius on the ground. If the first had been paid first and sent away, there would probably have been no murmuring, but "the murmuring is needed to bring out the lesson" (Plummer). The <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δηναριυς</span></span> was the common wage of a day labourer at that time.</p>
<p><b>What I will </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο θελω</span></span>). This is the point of the parable, the <i>will</i> of the householder.</p>
<p><b>With mine own </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τοις εμοις</span></span>). In the sphere of my own affairs. There is in the <i>Koine</i> an extension of the instrumental use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span>. </p>
