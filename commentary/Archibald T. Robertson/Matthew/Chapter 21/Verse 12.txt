<p><b> Cast out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεβαλεν</span></span>). Drove out, assumed authority over "the temple of God" (probably correct text with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του θεου</span></span>, though only example of the phrase). John (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:14">John 2:14</span>) has a similar incident at the beginning of the ministry of Jesus. It is not impossible that he should repeat it at the close after three years with the same abuses in existence again. It is amazing how short a time the work of reformers lasts. The traffic went on in the court of the Gentiles and to a certain extent was necessary. Here the tables of</p>
<p><b>the money-changers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των κολλυβιστων</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολλυβος</span></span>, a small coin) were overturned. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:24">Matthew 17:24</span> for the need of the change for the temple tax. The doves were the poor man's offering. </p>
