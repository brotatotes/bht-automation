<p><b> The most part of the multitude </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο πλειστος οχλος</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:20">Matthew 11:20</span> for this same idiom, article with superlative, a true superlative (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 670).</p>
<p><b>In the way </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη οδω</span></span>). This the most of the crowd did. The disciples put their garments on the asses. Note change of tenses (constative aorist <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστρωσαν</span></span>, descriptive imperfects <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκοπτον κα εστρωννυον</span></span> showing the growing enthusiasm of the crowd). When the colt had passed over their garments, they would pick the garments up and spread them again before. </p>
