<p><b> That went before him and that followed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο προαγοντες αυτον κα ο ακολουθουντες</span></span>). Note the two groups with two articles and the present tense (linear action) and the imperfect <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκραζον</span></span> "were crying" as they went.</p>
<p><b>Hosanna to the Son of David </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hοσαννα τω υιω Δαυειδ</span></span>). They were now proclaiming Jesus as the Messiah and he let them do it. "Hosanna" means "Save, we pray thee." They repeat words from the <i>Hallel</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+148:1">Psalms 148:1</span>) and one recalls the song of the angelic host when Jesus was born (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:14">Luke 2:14</span>). "Hosanna in the highest" (heaven) as well as here on earth. </p>
