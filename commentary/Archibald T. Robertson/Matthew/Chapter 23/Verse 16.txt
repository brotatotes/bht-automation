<p><b> Ye blind guides </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδηγο τυφλο</span></span>). Note omission of "Scribes and Pharisees, hypocrites" with this third woe. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:14">Matthew 15:14</span> Jesus had already called the Pharisees "blind guides" (leaders). They split hairs about oaths, as Jesus had explained in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:33-37">Matthew 5:33-37</span>, between the temple and the gold of the temple.</p>
<p><b>He is a debtor </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφειλε</span></span>). He owes his oath, is bound by his oath. A.V.,</p>
<p><b>is guilty </b>, is old English, obsolete sense of guilt as fine or payment. </p>
