<p><b> Carcase </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πτωμα</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:12">Matthew 14:12</span>, the corpse. Originally a fallen body from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιπτω</span></span>, to fall, like Latin <i>cadaver</i> from <i>cado</i>, to fall. The proverb here as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:37">Luke 17:37</span>, is like that in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+39:30">Job 39:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="pr+30:17">Proverbs 30:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>Eagles </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αετο</span></span>). Perhaps the griffon vulture, larger than the eagle, which (Aristotle) was often seen in the wake of an army and followed Napoleon's retreat from Russia. </p>
