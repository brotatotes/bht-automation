<p><b> Going into another country </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποδημων</span></span>). About to go away from one's people (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δημος</span></span>), on the point of going abroad. This word in ancient use in this sense. There is an ellipse here that has to be supplied,</p>
<p><b>It is as when </b> or</p>
<p><b>The kingdom of heaven is as when </b>. This Parable of the Talents is quite similar to the Parable of the Pounds in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:11-28">Luke 19:11-28</span>, but they are not variations of the same story. Some scholars credit Jesus with very little versatility.</p>
<p><b>His goods </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα υπαρχοντα αυτου</span></span>). His belongings, neuter participle used as a substantive. </p>
