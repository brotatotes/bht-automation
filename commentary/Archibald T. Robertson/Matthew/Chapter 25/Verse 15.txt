<p><b> To one </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ω μεν, ω δε, ω δε</span></span>). Demonstrative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος</span></span>, not the relative. Neat Greek idiom.</p>
<p><b>According to his several ability </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα την ιδιαν δυναμιν</span></span>). According to his own ability. Each had all that he was capable of handling. The use that one makes of his opportunities is the measure of his capacity for more. One talent represented a considerable amount of money at that time when a <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεναριυς</span></span> was a day's wage. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:24">Matthew 18:24</span> for the value of a talent. </p>
