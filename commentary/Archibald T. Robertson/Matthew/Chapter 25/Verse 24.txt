<p><b> That had received the one talent </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο το ταλεντον ειληφως</span></span>). Note the perfect active participle to emphasize the fact that he still had it. In verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:20">Matthew 25:20</span> we have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο--λαβων</span></span> (aorist active participle).</p>
<p><b>I knew thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγνων σε</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative. Experimental knowledge (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span>) and proleptical use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σε</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>A hard man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκληρος</span></span>). Harsh, stern, rough man, worse than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυστηρος</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:21">Luke 19:21</span>, grasping and ungenerous.</p>
<p><b>Where thou didst not scatter </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οθεν ου διεσκορπισας</span></span>). But this scattering was the chaff from which wheat was winnowed, not the scattering of seed. </p>
