<p><b> I know not what thou sayest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ οιδα τ λεγεις</span></span>). It was an affectation of extreme ignorance (Bruce) that deceived no one. It was an easy and ancient dodge and easy subterfuge. Dalman (<i>Words of Jesus</i>, 80f.) suggests that Peter used the Galilean Aramaean word for know instead of the Judean Aramaean word which betrayed at once his Galilean residence. </p>
