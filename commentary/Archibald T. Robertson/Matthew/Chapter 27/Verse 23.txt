<p><b> Why, what evil hath he done? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τ γαρ κακον εποιησεν</span></span>;). This was a feeble protest by a flickering conscience. Pilate descended to that level of arguing with the mob now inflamed with passion for the blood of Jesus, a veritable lynching fiasco. But this exhibition of weakness made the mob fear refusal by Pilate to proceed. So they "kept crying exceedingly" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περισσως εκραζον</span></span>, imperfect tense of repeated action and vehemently) their demand for the crucifixion of Jesus. It was like a gladiatorial show with all thumbs turned down. </p>
