<p><b> His accusation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την αιτιαν αυτου</span></span>). The title (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιτλος</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:19">John 19:19</span>) or placard of the crime (the inscription, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε επιγραφη</span></span>) which was carried before the victim or hung around his neck as he walked to execution was now placed above (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ' ανω</span></span>) the head of Jesus on the projecting piece (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρυξ ιμμυρυς</span></span>). This inscription gave the name and home,</p>
<p><b>Jesus of Nazareth </b>, and the charge on which he was convicted,</p>
<p><b>the King of the Jews </b> and the identification,</p>
<p><b>This is </b>. The four reports all give the charge and vary in the others. The inscription in full was: This is Jesus of Nazareth the King of the Jews. The three languages are mentioned only by John (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:20">John 19:20</span>), Latin for law, Hebrew (Aramaic) for the Jews, Greek for everybody. The accusation (charge, cause, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιτια</span></span>) correctly told the facts of the condemnation. </p>
