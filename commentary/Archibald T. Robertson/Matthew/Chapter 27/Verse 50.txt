<p><b> Yielded up his spirit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφηκεν το πνευμα</span></span>). The loud cry may have been <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+31:5">Psalms 31:5</span> as given in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:46">Luke 23:46</span>: "Father, into thy hands I commend my spirit." John (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:30">John 19:30</span>) gives</p>
<p><b>It is finished </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τετελεστα</span></span>), though which was actually last is not clear. Jesus did not die from slow exhaustion, but with a loud cry.</p>
<p><b>He breathed out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεπνευσεν</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:37">Mark 15:37</span>),</p>
<p><b>sent back his spirit </b> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:50">Matthew 27:50</span>),</p>
<p><b>gave up his spirit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεδωκεν το πνευμα</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:30">John 19:30</span>). "He gave up his life because he willed it, when he willed it, and as he willed it" (Augustine). Stroud (<i>Physical Cause of the Death of Christ</i>) considers the loud cry one of the proofs that Jesus died of a ruptured heart as a result of bearing the sin of the world. </p>
