<p><b> Casting a net into the sea </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαλλαντας αμφιβληστρον εις την θαλασσαν</span></span>). The word here for net is a casting-net (compare <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμφιβαλλω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:16">Mark 1:16</span>, casting on both sides). The net was thrown over the shoulder and spread into a circle (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμφ</span></span>). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:20">Matthew 4:20</span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:21">Matthew 4:21</span> another word occurs for nets (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικτυα</span></span>), a word used for nets of any kind. The large drag-net (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαγηνη</span></span>) appears in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:47">Matthew 13:47</span>. </p>
