<p><b> Agree with </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ισθ ευνοων</span></span>). A present periphrastic active imperative. The verb is from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευνοος</span></span> (friendly, kindly disposed). "Mak up wi' yere enemy" (<i>Braid Scots</i>). Compromise is better than prison where no principle is involved, but only personal interest. It is so easy to see principle where pride is involved.</p>
<p><b> The officer </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω υπηρετη</span></span>). This word means "under rower" on the ship with several ranks of rowers, the bottom rower (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο</span></span> under and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηρεσσω</span></span>, to row), the galley-slave, then any servant, the attendant in the synagogue (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:20">Luke 4:20</span>). Luke so describes John Mark in his relation to Barnabas and Saul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:5">Acts 13:5</span>). Then it is applied to the "ministers of the word" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:2">Luke 1:2</span>). </p>
