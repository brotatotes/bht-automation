<p><b> Thou shalt not commit adultery </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μοιχευσεις</span></span>). These quotations (verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:21">Matthew 5:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:27">Matthew 5:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:33">Matthew 5:33</span>) from the Decalogue (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:20">Matthew 5:20</span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:5">Matthew 5:5</span>) are from the Septuagint and use <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου</span></span> and the future indicative (volitive future, common Greek idiom). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:43">Matthew 5:43</span> the positive form, volitive future, occurs (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπησεις</span></span>). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:41">Matthew 5:41</span> the third person (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοτω</span></span>) singular second aorist active imperative is used. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:38">Matthew 5:38</span> no verb occurs. </p>
