<p><b> Turn not thou away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη αποστραφηις</span></span>). Second aorist passive subjunctive in prohibition. "This is one of the clearest instances of the necessity of accepting the spirit and not the letter of the Lord's commands (see vv.<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:32">Matthew 5:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:34">Matthew 5:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:38">Matthew 5:38</span>). Not only does indiscriminate almsgiving do little but injury to society, but the words must embrace far more than almsgiving" (McNeile). Recall again that Jesus is a popular teacher and expects men to understand his paradoxes. In the organized charities of modern life we are in danger of letting the milk of human kindness dry up. </p>
