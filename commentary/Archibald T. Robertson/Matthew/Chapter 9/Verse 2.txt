<p><b> They brought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσεφερον</span></span>). Imperfect, "were bringing," graphic picture made very vivid by the details in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:1-4">Mark 2:1-4</span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:17">Luke 5:17</span>. "<b> Lying on a bed </b>" (stretched on a couch), perfect passive participle, a little bed or couch (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλινιδιον</span></span>) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:19">Luke 5:19</span>, "a pallet" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κραβατος</span></span>) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:4">Mark 2:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:9">Mark 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:11">Mark 2:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>Thy sins are forgiven </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφιεντα</span></span>). Present passive indicative (aoristic present). Luke (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:21">Luke 5:21</span>) has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφεωντα</span></span>, Doric and Ionic perfect passive indicative for the Attic <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφειντα</span></span>, one of the dialectical forms appearing in the <i>Koine</i>. </p>
