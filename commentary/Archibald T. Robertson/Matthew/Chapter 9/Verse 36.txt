<p><b> Were distressed and scattered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ησαν εσκυλμενο κα εριμμενο</span></span>). Periphrastic past perfect indicative passive. A sad and pitiful state the crowds were in. Rent or mangled as if by wild beasts. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Σκυλλω</span></span> occurs in the papyri in sense of plunder, concern, vexation. "Used here of the common people, it describes their religious condition. They were harassed, importuned, bewildered by those who should have taught them; hindered from entering into the kingdom of heaven (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:13">Matthew 23:13</span>), laden with the burdens which the Pharisees laid upon them (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:3">Matthew 23:3</span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εριμμενο</span></span> denotes men cast down and prostrate on the ground, whether from drunkenness, Polyb. v. 48.2, or from mortal wounds" (Allen): This perfect passive participle from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ριπτω</span></span>, to throw down. The masses were in a state of mental dejection. No wonder that Jesus was moved with compassion (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσπλαγχνισθη</span></span>). </p>
