<p><b> Onesimus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ονησιμον</span></span>). A common name among slaves and made like Chresimus, Chrestus. The word is from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ονησις</span></span> (profit) and that from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ονινημ</span></span>, to profit, to help.</p>
<p><b>Who was aforetime unprofitable to thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον ποτε σο αχρηστον</span></span>). "The once to thee useless one." Play (pun) on the meaning of the name Onesimus (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ονησιμος</span></span>, useful) as once "useless" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχρηστος</span></span>, verbal adjective, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χραομα</span></span>, to use).</p>
<p><b>But now is profitable to thee and to me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυν δε σο κα εμο ευχρηστον</span></span>). "But now to thee and to me useful." Still further play on the name Onesimus by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευχρηστον</span></span> (verbal adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χραομα</span></span>, to use). Ethical dative here (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σοι, εμο</span></span>). </p>
