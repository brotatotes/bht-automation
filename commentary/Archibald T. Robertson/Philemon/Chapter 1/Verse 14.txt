<p><b> Without thy mind </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χωρις της σης γνωμης</span></span>). Judgment, purpose (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:10">1 Corinthians 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:25">1 Corinthians 7:25</span>). Ablative case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χωρις</span></span> (apart from).</p>
<p><b>I would do nothing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδεν ηθελησα ποιησα</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θελω</span></span>, I decided, I wished, decision reached (cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβουλομην</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:13">Philemon 1:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Thy goodness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το αγαθον σου</span></span>). Neuter articular adjective (thy good deed).</p>
<p><b>As of necessity </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως κατα αναγκην</span></span>). "As if according to compulsion." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:7">2 Corinthians 9:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>But of free will </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα κατα εκουσιον</span></span>). According to what is voluntary (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+15:3">Numbers 15:3</span>). Perhaps <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τροπον</span></span> (way, manner) is to be understood with the adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκουσιος</span></span> (old word, here alone in N.T.), from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκων</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:17">1 Corinthians 9:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:20">Romans 8:20</span>). </p>
