<p><b> But if he hath wronged thee at all </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε δε τ ηδικησε σε</span></span>). Condition of the first class, assumed to be true. Onesimus did wrong (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηδικησε</span></span>, first aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδικηο</span></span>, to wrong, without justice). He had probably robbed Philemon before he ran away.</p>
<p><b>Or oweth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η οφειλε</span></span>). Delicate way of putting the stealing.</p>
<p><b>Put that to mine account </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτο εμο ελλογα</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελλογαω</span></span>. In the <i>Koine</i> verbs in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-εω</span></span> often appear in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-αω</span></span> like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεεω, ελεαω</span></span>. So with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελλογεω</span></span> as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελλογαω</span></span>, late verb in inscriptions and papyri (Deissmann, <i>Light, etc.</i>, p. 84), though in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:13">Romans 5:13</span>. It means to set to one's account. </p>
