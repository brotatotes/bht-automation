<p><b> That </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπως</span></span>). Rather than the more common final particle <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>. Connected with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μνειαν ποιουμενος</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>The fellowship of thy faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η κοινωνια της πιστεως σου</span></span>). Partnership like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:5">Philippians 1:5</span> in (objective genitive, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστεως</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Effectual </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεργης</span></span>). Common adjective, like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεργος</span></span> (at work), in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:9">1 Corinthians 16:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:12">Hebrews 4:12</span>. Papyri use <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεργος</span></span> of a mill in working order, of ploughed land, etc.</p>
<p><b>In you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν υμιν</span></span>). Some MSS. have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ημιν</span></span> (in us), itacism and common. </p>
