<p><b> I had </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσχον</span></span>). Ingressive second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειχομην</span></span> as the Textus Receptus has it. Paul refers to his joy when he first heard the good news about Philemon's activity (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:5">Philemon 1:5</span>).</p>
<p><b>The hearts </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα σπλαγχνα</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:8">Philippians 1:8</span> for this use of this word for the nobler viscera (heart, lungs, liver) and here for the emotional nature.</p>
<p><b>Have been refreshed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπεπαυτα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of old compound verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπαυω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:28">Matthew 11:28</span>, a relief and refreshment whether temporary (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:31">Mark 6:31</span>) or eternal (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:13">Revelation 14:13</span>). </p>
