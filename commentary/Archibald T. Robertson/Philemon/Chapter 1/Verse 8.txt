<p><b> Though I have </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχων</span></span>). Concessive participle (present active).</p>
<p><b>That which is befitting </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το ανηκον</span></span>). Neuter singular accusative of the articular participle (present active) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανηκω</span></span>, to come up to requirements and so to be befitting. For idea in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανηκω</span></span>, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:18">Colossians 3:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:4">Ephesians 5:4</span>. This idiom is in later writers.</p>
<p><b>I rather beseech </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαλλον παρακαλω</span></span>). Rather than command (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιτασσω</span></span>) which he has a perfect right to do. </p>
