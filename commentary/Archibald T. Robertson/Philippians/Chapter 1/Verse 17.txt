<p><b> Of faction </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ εριθειας</span></span>). Out of partisanship. From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εριθευω</span></span>, to spin wool, and that from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εριθος</span></span>, a hireling. The papyri examples suit the idea of selfish ambition (Moulton and Milligan's <i>Vocabulary</i>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:20">2 Corinthians 12:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:20">Galatians 5:20</span>.</p>
<p><b>Not sincerely </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουχ αγνως</span></span>). "Not purely," that is with mixed and impure motives.</p>
<p><b>To raise up affliction for my bonds </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θλιψιν εγειρειν τοις δεσμοις μου</span></span>). Now that Paul is down they jump on him in mean and nagging ways. Dative case in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεσμοις</span></span>. "To make my chains gall me" (Lightfoot). </p>
