<p><b> Will turn </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποβησετα</span></span>). Future middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποβαινω</span></span>, old verb, to come from, to come back, to turn out.</p>
<p><b>To my salvation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις σωτηριαν</span></span>). For his release from prison as he strongly hopes to see them again (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:26">Philippians 1:26</span>). Lightfoot takes the word to be Paul's eternal salvation and it must be confessed that verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:20">Philippians 1:20</span> (the close of this sentence) does suit that idea best. Can it be that Paul carried both conceptions in the word here?</p>
<p><b>Supply </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιχορηγιας</span></span>). Late and rare word (one example in inscription of first century A.D.). In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:16">Ephesians 4:16</span>. From the late verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιχορηγεω</span></span> (double compound, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επι, χοροσ, ηγεομα</span></span>, to furnish supply for the chorus) which see in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:10">2 Corinthians 9:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:5">Galatians 3:5</span>. </p>
