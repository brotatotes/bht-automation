<p><b> I am in a strait </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεχομα</span></span>). "I am held together." Present passive indicative of the common compound verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεχω</span></span>, to hold together, to hem together as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:45">Luke 8:45</span>. "I am hemmed in on both sides" (Lightfoot).</p>
<p><b>Betwixt the two </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ των δυο</span></span>). "From the two (sides)." Pressure to live on, pressure to die and be with Christ.</p>
<p><b>To depart </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το αναλυσα</span></span>). Purpose clause, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το</span></span> and the aorist active infinitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναλυσα</span></span>, old compound verb, to unloose (as threads), to break up, to return (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:36">Luke 12:36</span>, only other N.T. example), to break up camp (Polybius), to weigh anchor and put out to sea, to depart (often in old Greek and papyri). Cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλυω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:1">2 Corinthians 5:1</span> for tearing down the tent.</p>
<p><b>Very far better </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολλω μαλλον κρεισσον</span></span>). Double comparative (triple Lightfoot calls it because of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολλω</span></span>) like Isocrates and the <i>Koine</i> often. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:13">2 Corinthians 7:13</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περισσοτερως μαλλον</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πολλω</span></span> is the instrumental case of measure (by much). </p>
