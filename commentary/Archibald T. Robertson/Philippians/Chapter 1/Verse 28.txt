<p><b> Affrighted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πτυρομενο</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πτυρω</span></span>, old verb, to frighten. The metaphor is of a timid or scared horse and from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πτοεω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πτοα</span></span>, terror). "Not startled in anything."</p>
<p><b>By the adversaries </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο των αντικειμενων</span></span>). These men who were lined up against (present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντικειμα</span></span>) may have been Jews or Gentiles or both. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:4">2 Thessalonians 2:4</span> for this late verb. Any preacher who attacks evil will have opposition.</p>
<p><b>Evident token </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδειξις</span></span>). Old word for proof. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:24">2 Corinthians 8:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:25">Romans 3:25</span>. "An Attic law term" (Kennedy) and only in Paul in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Perdition </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απωλειας</span></span>). "Loss" in contrast with "salvation" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωτηριας</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>And that </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα τουτο</span></span>). Idiomatic adverbial accusative. "It is a direct indication from God. The Christian gladiator does not anxiously await the signal of life or death from the fickle crowd" (Lightfoot). </p>
