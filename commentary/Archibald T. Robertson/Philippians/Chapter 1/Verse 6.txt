<p><b> Being confident </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεποιθως</span></span>). Second perfect active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειθω</span></span>, to persuade.</p>
<p><b>This very thing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτο τουτο</span></span>). Accusative of the inner object with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεποιθως</span></span>, "this thing itself."</p>
<p><b>Will perfect it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιτελεσε</span></span>). Future active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιτελεω</span></span>, will fully (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επι-</span></span>) finish. God began and God will consummate it (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:6">2 Corinthians 8:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:3">Galatians 3:3</span> where both words occur together as here), but not without their cooperation and partnership.</p>
<p><b>Until the day of Jesus Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχρ ημερας Χριστου Ιησου</span></span>). The second coming as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:10">Philippians 1:10</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:2">1 Thessalonians 5:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:4">1 Thessalonians 5:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:10">2 Thessalonians 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:2">2 Thessalonians 2:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:18">1 Corinthians 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:13">1 Corinthians 3:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:14">2 Corinthians 1:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:12">Romans 13:12</span>. Paul never sets the time for the Lord's return, but he is cheered by that blessed hope. </p>
