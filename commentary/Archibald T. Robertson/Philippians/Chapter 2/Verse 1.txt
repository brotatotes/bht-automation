<p><b> If </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span>). Paul uses four conditions in this verse, all of the first class, assuming the condition to be true.</p>
<p><b>Comfort </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρακλησις</span></span>). Rather, "ground of appeal to you in Christ." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:10">1 Corinthians 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:1">Ephesians 4:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Consolation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραμυθιον</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραμυθεομα</span></span>, persuasive address, incentive.</p>
<p><b>Of love </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπης</span></span>). Objective genitive, "in love" (undefined as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:1">Philippians 2:1</span>).</p>
<p><b>Fellowship </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινωνια</span></span>). Partnership in the Holy Spirit "whose first fruit is love" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:22">Galatians 5:22</span>).</p>
<p><b>Any tender mercies </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τις σπλαγχνα</span></span>). Common use of this word for the nobler <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ςισχερα</span></span> and so for the higher emotions. But <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τις</span></span> is masculine singular and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπλαγχνα</span></span> is neuter plural. Lightfoot suggests an error of an early transcriber or even of the amanuensis in writing <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε τις</span></span> instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε τινα</span></span>. </p>
