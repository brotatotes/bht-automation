<p><b> That in the name of Jesus every knee should bow </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα εν τω ονοματ Ιησου παν γονυ καμψη</span></span>). First aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καμπτω</span></span>, old verb, to bend, to bow, in purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>. Not perfunctory genuflections whenever the name of Jesus is mentioned, but universal acknowledgment of the majesty and power of Jesus who carries his human name and nature to heaven. This universal homage to Jesus is seen in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:22">Romans 8:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:20-22">Ephesians 1:20-22</span> and in particular <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:13">Revelation 5:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Under the earth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταχθονιων</span></span>). Homeric adjective for departed souls, subterranean, simply the dead. Here only in the N.T. </p>
