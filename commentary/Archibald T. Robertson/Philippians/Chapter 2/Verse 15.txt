<p><b> That ye may be </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα γενησθε</span></span>). Rather, "that ye may become" (second aorist middle subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span>, to become).</p>
<p><b>Blameless </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμεμπτο</span></span>). Free from censure (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεμφομα</span></span>, to blame).</p>
<p><b>Harmless </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακεραιο</span></span>). Unmixed, unadulterated as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:19">Romans 16:19</span>.</p>
<p><b>Without blemish </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμωμα</span></span>). Without spot, "unblemished in reputation and in reality" (Vincent).</p>
<p><b>In the midst of </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεσον</span></span>). Preposition with genitive.</p>
<p><b>Crooked </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκολιας</span></span>). Old word, curved as opposed to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορθος</span></span>, straight. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:40">Acts 2:40</span>.</p>
<p><b>Perverse </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διεστραμμενης</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαστρεφω</span></span>, to distort, to twist, to turn to one side (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span>, in two). Old word. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:17">Matthew 17:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:10">Acts 13:10</span>. </p>
