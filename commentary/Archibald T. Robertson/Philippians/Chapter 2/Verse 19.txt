<p><b> That I also may be of good comfort </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα καγω ευψυχω</span></span>). Present subjunctive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> in purpose clause of the late and rare verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευψυχεω</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευψυχος</span></span> (cheerful, of good spirit). In papyri and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευψυχε</span></span> (be of good cheer) common in sepulchral inscriptions.</p>
<p><b>When I know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνους</span></span>). Second aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span>. </p>
