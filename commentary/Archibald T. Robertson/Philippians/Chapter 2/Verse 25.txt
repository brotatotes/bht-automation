<p><b> I counted it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγησαμην</span></span>). Epistolary aorist from the point of view of the readers.</p>
<p><b>Epaphroditus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Επαφροδιτον</span></span>). Common name, though only in Philippians in N.T., contracted into Epaphras, though not the same man as Epaphras in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:7">Colossians 1:7</span>. Note one article <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον</span></span> (the) with the three epithets given in an ascending scale (Lightfoot), brother (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδελφον</span></span>, common sympathy), fellow-worker (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεργον</span></span>, common work), fellow-soldier (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνστρατιωτην</span></span>, common danger as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:2">Philemon 1:2</span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μου</span></span> (my) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμων</span></span> (your) come together in sharp contrast.</p>
<p><b>Messenger </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστολον</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:23">2 Corinthians 8:23</span> for this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστολος</span></span> as messenger (missionary).</p>
<p><b>Minister </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λειτουργον</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:6">Romans 13:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:16">Romans 15:16</span> for this ritualistic term. </p>
