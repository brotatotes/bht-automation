<p><b> Ye may rejoice </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρητε</span></span>). Second aorist passive subjunctive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> in final clause of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαιρω</span></span>, to rejoice.</p>
<p><b>That I may be the less sorrowful </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καγω αλυποτερος ω</span></span>). Present subjunctive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and comparative of old compound adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλυπος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυπη</span></span>, more free from grief). Beautiful expression of Paul's feelings for the Philippians and for Epaphroditus. </p>
