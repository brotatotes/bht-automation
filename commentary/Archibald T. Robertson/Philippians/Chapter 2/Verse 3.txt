<p><b> Through vainglory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα κενοδοξιαν</span></span>). Late word, only here in N.T., from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κενοδοξος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κενοσ, δοξα</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:26">Galatians 5:26</span>, only here in N.T.), empty pride.</p>
<p><b>In lowliness of mind </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη ταπεινοφροσυνη</span></span>). Late and rare word. Not in O.T. or early Greek writers. In Josephus and Epictetus in bad sense (pusillanimity). For ostentatious humility in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:18">Colossians 2:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:23">Colossians 2:23</span>. One of the words, like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταπεινος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:29">Matthew 11:29</span>) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταπεινοφρων</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:8">1 Peter 3:8</span>, here alone in N.T.) that Christianity has ennobled and dignified (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:19">Acts 20:19</span>).</p>
<p><b>Better than himself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερεχοντας εαυτων</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερεχω</span></span> in intransitive sense to excel or surpass with the ablative, "excelling themselves." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:10">Romans 12:10</span>. </p>
