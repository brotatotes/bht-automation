<p><b> In fashion </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σχηματ</span></span>). Locative case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σχημα</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>, to have, to hold. Bengel explains <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μορφη</span></span> by <i>forma</i>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοιωμα</span></span> by <i>similitudo</i>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σχημα</span></span> by <i>habitus</i>. Here with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σχημα</span></span> the contrast "is between what He is in Himself, and what He <i>appeared</i> in the eyes of men" (Lightfoot).</p>
<p><b>He humbled himself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εταπεινωσεν εαυτον</span></span>). First aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταπεινοω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταπεινος</span></span>. It is a voluntary humiliation on the part of Christ and for this reason Paul is pressing the example of Christ upon the Philippians, this supreme example of renunciation. See Bruce's masterpiece, <i>The Humiliation of Christ</i>.</p>
<p><b>Obedient </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπηκοος</span></span>). Old adjective, giving ear to. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:39">Acts 7:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:9">2 Corinthians 2:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>Unto death </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεχρ θανατου</span></span>). "Until death." See "until blood" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεχρις αιματος</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:4">Hebrews 12:4</span>).</p>
<p><b>Yea, the death of the cross </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θανατου δε σταυρου</span></span>). The bottom rung in the ladder from the Throne of God. Jesus came all the way down to the most despised death of all, a condemned criminal on the accursed cross. </p>
