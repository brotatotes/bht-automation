<p><b> That I may know him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του γνωνα αυτον</span></span>). Genitive of the articular second aorist (ingressive) active infinitive (purpose) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span>, to have personal acquaintance or experience with. This is Paul's major passion, to get more knowledge of Christ by experience.</p>
<p><b>The power of his resurrection </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την δυναμιν της αναστασεως αυτου</span></span>). Power (Lightfoot) in the sense of assurance to believers in immortality (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:14">1 Corinthians 15:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:11">Romans 8:11</span>), in the triumph over sin (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:24">Romans 4:24</span>), in the dignity of the body (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:13">1 Corinthians 6:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:21">Philippians 3:21</span>), in stimulating the moral and spiritual life (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:20">Galatians 2:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:4">Romans 6:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:12">Colossians 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:5">Ephesians 2:5</span>). See Westcott's <i>The Gospel of the Resurrection</i>, ii, 31.</p>
<p><b>The fellowship of his sufferings </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την κοινωνιαν των παθηματων αυτου</span></span>). Partnership in (objective genitive) his sufferings, an honour prized by Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:24">Colossians 1:24</span>).</p>
<p><b>Becoming conformed to his death </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμμορφιζομενος τω θανατω αυτου</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμμορφιζω</span></span>, late verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμμορφος</span></span>, found only here and ecclesiastical writers quoting it. The Latin Vulgate uses <i>configuro</i>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:4">Romans 6:4</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμφυτο</span></span> in like sense and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:10">2 Corinthians 4:10</span>. "The agony of Gethsemane, not less than the agony of Calvary, will be reproduced however faintly in the faithful servant of Christ" (Lightfoot). "In this passage we have the deepest secrets of the Apostle's Christian experience unveiled" (Kennedy). </p>
