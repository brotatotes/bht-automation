<p><b> If by any means I may attain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε πως καταντησω</span></span>). Not an expression of doubt, but of humility (Vincent), a modest hope (Lightfoot). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε πως</span></span>, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:10">Romans 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:14">Romans 11:14</span> where <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραζηλωσω</span></span> can be either future indicative or aorist subjunctive like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταντησω</span></span> here (see subjunctive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλαβω</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:12">Philippians 3:12</span>), late compound verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατανταω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Resurrection </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαναστασιν</span></span>). Late word, not in LXX, but in Polybius and one papyrus example. Apparently Paul is thinking here only of the resurrection of believers out from the dead and so double <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεν εξαναστασιν την εκ νεκρων</span></span>). Paul is not denying a general resurrection by this language, but emphasizing that of believers. </p>
