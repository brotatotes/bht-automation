<p><b> Imitators together of me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνμιμητα μου</span></span>). Found only here so far, though Plato uses <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμμιμεισθα</span></span>. "Vie with each other in imitating me" (Lightfoot).</p>
<p><b>Mark </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκοπειτε</span></span>). Old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκοπος</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:14">Philippians 3:14</span>). "Keep your eyes on me as goal." Mark and follow, not avoid as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:17">Romans 16:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>An ensample </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυπον</span></span>). Originally the impression left by a stroke (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:25">John 20:25</span>), then a pattern (mould) as here (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:7">1 Thessalonians 1:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:6">1 Corinthians 10:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:11">1 Corinthians 10:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:14">Romans 5:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:17">Romans 6:17</span>). </p>
