<p><b> For we </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημεις γαρ</span></span>). We believers in Christ, the children of Abraham by faith, whether Jew or Gentile, the spiritual circumcision in contrast to the merely physical (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:25-29">Romans 2:25-29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:11">Colossians 2:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:11">Ephesians 2:11</span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:12">Galatians 5:12</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποτεμνειν</span></span> (to cut off) in sense of mutilation also.</p>
<p><b>By the Spirit of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευματ θεου</span></span>). Instrumental case, though the dative case as the object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λατρευω</span></span> makes good sense also (worshipping the Spirit of God) or even the locative (worshipping in the Spirit of God).</p>
<p><b>No </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ</span></span>). Actual condition rather than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> with the participle.</p>
<p><b>In the flesh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν σαρκ</span></span>). Technical term in Paul's controversy with the Judaizers (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:18">2 Corinthians 11:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:13">Galatians 6:13</span>). External privileges beyond mere flesh. </p>
