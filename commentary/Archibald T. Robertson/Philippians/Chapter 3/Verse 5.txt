<p><b> Thinketh to have confidence </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκε πεποιθενα</span></span>). Second perfect active infinitive. Old idiom, "seems to himself to have confidence." Later idiom like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:9">Matthew 3:9</span> "think not to say" and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:16">1 Corinthians 11:16</span>, "thinks that he has ground of confidence in himself."</p>
<p><b>I yet more </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγω μαλλον</span></span>). "I have more ground for boasting than he" and Paul proceeds to prove it in the rest of verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:5">Philippians 3:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:6">Philippians 3:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Circumcised the eighth day </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιτομη οκταημερος</span></span>). "In circumcision (locative case) an eighth day man." Use of the ordinal with persons like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεταρταιος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:39">John 11:39</span>). Ishmaelites were circumcised in the thirteenth year, proselytes from Gentiles in mature age, Jews on the eighth day (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:21">Luke 2:21</span>).</p>
<p><b>Of the stock of Israel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ γενους Ισραηλ</span></span>). Of the original stock, not a proselyte.</p>
<p><b>Benjamin </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Βενιαμιν</span></span>). Son of the right hand (that is, left-handed), son of Rachel. The first King, Saul (Paul's own Hebrew name) was from this little tribe. The battle cry of Israel was "After thee, O Benjamin" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jdg+5:14">Judges 5:14</span>).</p>
<p><b>A Hebrew of the Hebrews </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εβραιος εξ Εβραιων</span></span>). Of Hebrew parents who retained the characteristic qualities in language and custom as distinct from the Hellenistic Jews (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:1">Acts 6:1</span>). Paul was from Tarsus and knew Greek as well as Aramaic (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:40">Acts 21:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:2">Acts 22:2</span>) and Hebrew, but he had not become Hellenized.</p>
<p><b>A Pharisee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Φαρισαιος</span></span>). In distinction from the Sadducees (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:14">Galatians 1:14</span>) and he continued a Pharisee in many essential matters like the doctrine of the resurrection (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:6">Acts 23:6</span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:22">2 Corinthians 11:22</span>. </p>
