<p><b> Were gain to me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν μο κερδη</span></span>). "Were gains (plural, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:21">Philippians 1:21</span>) to me (ethical dative)." Paul had natural pride in his Jewish attainments. He was the star of hope for Gamaliel and the Sanhedrin.</p>
<p><b>Have I counted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγημα</span></span>). Perfect middle indicative, state of completion and still true.</p>
<p><b>Loss </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζημιαν</span></span>). Old word for damage, loss. In N.T. only in Phil. and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:10">Acts 27:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:21">Acts 27:21</span>. Debit side of the ledger, not credit. </p>
