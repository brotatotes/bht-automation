<p><b> Be found in him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρεθω εν αυτω</span></span>). First aorist (effective) passive subjunctive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρισκω</span></span>. At death (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:3">2 Corinthians 5:3</span>) or when Christ comes. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:8">Philippians 2:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:17">Galatians 2:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>Through faith in Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια πιστεως Χριστου</span></span>). The objective genitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χριστου</span></span>, not subjective, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:16">Galatians 2:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:20">Galatians 2:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:22">Romans 3:22</span>. Explained further by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ τη πιστε</span></span> (on the basis of faith) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:16">Acts 3:16</span>. </p>
