<p><b> I rejoice </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχαρην</span></span>). Second aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαιρω</span></span>, a timeless aorist. I did rejoice, I do rejoice.</p>
<p><b>Greatly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεγαλως</span></span>). Old adverb, only here in N.T., from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεγας</span></span> (great).</p>
<p><b>Now at length </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηδη ποτε</span></span>). In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:10">Romans 1:10</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ποτε</span></span> is indefinite past (interval), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηδη</span></span> immediate present.</p>
<p><b>Ye have revived </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεθαλετε</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of old poetic word (Homer), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναθαλλω</span></span>, to sprout again, to shoot up, to blossom again. So in the LXX five times, though rare and literary word.</p>
<p><b>Your thought for me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το υπερ εμου φρονειν</span></span>). Accusative case of the articular present active infinitive the object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεθαλετε</span></span> used transitively. "You caused your thinking of me to bloom afresh."</p>
<p><b>Wherein </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφ' ω</span></span>). "In which," "upon which" (locative case). A loose reference to Paul's interests as involved in their thinking of him.</p>
<p><b>Ye did indeed take thought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα εφρονειτε</span></span>). Imperfect active, "ye were also (or had been also) thinking."</p>
<p><b>Ye lacked opportunity </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκαιρεισθε</span></span>). Imperfect middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακαιρεομα</span></span>, late and rare word, here only in N.T., from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακαιρος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιρος</span></span>), not to have a chance, the opposite of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευκαιρεω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:31">Mark 6:31</span>). </p>
