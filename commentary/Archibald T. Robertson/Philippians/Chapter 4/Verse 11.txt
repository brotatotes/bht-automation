<p><b> In respect of want </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθ' υστερησιν</span></span>). Late and rare word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υστερεω</span></span>, to be behind or too late, only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:44">Mark 12:44</span> in N.T.</p>
<p><b>I have learned </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμαθον</span></span>). Simply, "I did learn" (constative second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μανθανω</span></span>, to learn, looking at his long experience as a unit.</p>
<p><b>In whatsoever state I am </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν οις ειμ</span></span>). "In what things (circumstances) I am."</p>
<p><b>To be content </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυταρκης εινα</span></span>). Predicate nominative with the infinitive of the old adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυταρκης</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτος</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρκεω</span></span>, to be self-sufficient), self-sufficing. Favourite word with the Stoics, only here in N.T., though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυταρκεια</span></span> occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:8">2 Corinthians 9:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:6">1 Timothy 6:6</span>. Paul is contented with his lot and he learned that lesson long ago. Socrates said as to who is wealthiest: "He that is content with least, for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυταρκεια</span></span> is nature's wealth." </p>
