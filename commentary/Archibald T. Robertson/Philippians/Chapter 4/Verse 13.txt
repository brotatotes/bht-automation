<p><b> I can do all things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντα ισχυω</span></span>). Old verb to have strength (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ισχυς</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>In him that strengtheneth me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω ενδυναμουντ με</span></span>). Late and rare verb (in LXX) from adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδυναμος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν, δυναμις</span></span>). Causative verb to empower, to pour power into one. See same phrase in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:12">1 Timothy 1:12</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω ενδυναμωσαντ με</span></span> (aorist tense here). Paul has such strength so long as Jesus keeps on putting power (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυναμις</span></span>) into him. </p>
