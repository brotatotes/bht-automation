<p><b> I have all things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεχω παντα</span></span>). As a receipt in full in appreciation of their kindness. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Απεχω</span></span> is common in the papyri and the ostraca for "receipt in full" (Deissmann, <i>Bible Studies</i>, p. 110). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:2">Matthew 6:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:5">Matthew 6:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:16">Matthew 6:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>I am filled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπληρωμα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροω</span></span>. "Classical Greek would hardly use the word in this personal sense" (Kennedy).</p>
<p><b>An odour of a sweet smell </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οσμην ευωδιας</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Οσμη</span></span>, old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οζω</span></span>, to smell. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ευωδια</span></span>, old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οζω</span></span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:2">Ephesians 5:2</span> both words come together as here and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:15">2 Corinthians 2:15</span> we have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευωδια</span></span> (only other N.T. example) and in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:16">2 Corinthians 2:16</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οσμη</span></span> twice. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ευωδιας</span></span> here is genitive of quality.</p>
<p><b>Sacrifice </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυσιαν</span></span>). Not the act, but the offering as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:1">Romans 12:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Well-pleasing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευαρεστον</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:1">Romans 12:1</span>. </p>
