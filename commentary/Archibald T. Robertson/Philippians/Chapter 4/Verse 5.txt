<p><b> Your forbearance </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το επιεικες υμων</span></span>). "Your gentleness," "your sweet reasonableness" (Matthew Arnold), "your moderation." Old adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επι, εικος</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:17">James 3:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:3">1 Timothy 3:3</span>. Article and neuter singular here= <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η επιεικεια</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:4">Acts 24:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:1">2 Corinthians 10:1</span>) like to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρηστον</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:4">Romans 2:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>The Lord is at hand </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο κυριος εγγυς</span></span>). "The Apostle's watchword" (Lightfoot), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:22">1 Corinthians 16:22</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μαραν αθα</span></span>, Aramaic equivalent, Our Lord cometh). Unless, indeed, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγγυς</span></span> here means near in space instead of</p>
<p><b>nigh </b> in time. </p>
