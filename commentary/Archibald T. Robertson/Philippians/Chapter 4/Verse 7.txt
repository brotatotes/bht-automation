<p><b> The peace of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η ειρηνη του θεου</span></span>). See in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:16">2 Thessalonians 3:16</span> "the Lord of peace" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο Κυριος της ειρηνης</span></span>) and verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:9">Philippians 4:9</span> for "the God of peace" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο θεος της ειρηνης</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Shall guard </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φρουρησε</span></span>). "Shall garrison," future active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φρουρεω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φρουρος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προ-οροσ, προοραω</span></span>, to see before, to look out). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:24">Acts 9:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:32">2 Corinthians 11:32</span>. God's peace as a sentinel mounts guard over our lives as Tennyson so beautifully pictures Love as doing. </p>
