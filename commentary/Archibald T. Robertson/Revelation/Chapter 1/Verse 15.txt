<p><b> Burnished brass </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαλκολιβανω</span></span>). Associative-instrumental case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοιο</span></span>. This word has so far been found nowhere else save here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:18">Revelation 2:18</span>. Suidas defines it as an <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηλεχκτρον</span></span> (amber) or a compound of copper and gold and silver (<i>aurichalcum</i> in the Latin Vulgate). It is in reality an unknown metal.</p>
<p><b>As if it had been refined </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως πεπυρομενης</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυροω</span></span>, old verb, to set on fire, to glow, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:16">Ephesians 6:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:18">Revelation 3:18</span>. The feminine gender shows that <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η χαλκολιβανος</span></span> is referred to with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της χαλκολιβανου</span></span> understood, for it does not agree in case with the associative-instrumental <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαλκολιβανω</span></span> just before. Some would call it a slip for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπυρομενω</span></span> as Aleph, and some cursives have it (taking <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαλκολιβανω</span></span> to be neuter, not feminine). But P Q read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπυρωμενο</span></span> (masculine plural), a correction, making it agree in number and gender with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποδες</span></span> (feet).</p>
<p><b>In a furnace </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν καμινω</span></span>). Old word, in N.T. also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:2">Revelation 9:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:42">Matthew 13:42</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:50">Matthew 13:50</span>.</p>
<p><b>As the voice of many waters </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως φωνη υδατων πολλων</span></span>). So the voice of God in the Hebrew (not the LXX) of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+43:2">Ezekiel 43:2</span>. Repeated in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:2">Revelation 14:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:6">Revelation 19:6</span>. </p>
