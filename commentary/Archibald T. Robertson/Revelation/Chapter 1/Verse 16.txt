<p><b> And he had </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα εχων</span></span>). "And having," present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>, loose use of the participle (almost like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειχε</span></span>, imperfect) and not in agreement with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτου</span></span>, genitive case. This is a common idiom in the book; a Hebraism, Charles calls it.</p>
<p><b>In his right hand </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη δεξια χειρ</span></span>). For safe keeping as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:28">John 10:28</span>.</p>
<p><b>Seven stars </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αστερας επτα</span></span>). Symbols of the seven churches (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:20">Revelation 1:20</span>), seven planets rather than Pleiades or any other constellation like the bear.</p>
<p><b>Proceeded </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκπορευομενη</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκπορευομα</span></span>, old compound (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:5">Matthew 3:5</span>) used loosely again like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχων</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>A sharp two-edged sword </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρομφαια διστομος οξεια</span></span>). "A sword two-mouthed sharp." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ρομφαια</span></span> (as distinct from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαχαιρα</span></span>) is a long sword, properly a Thracian javelin, in N.T. only <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:35">Luke 2:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:16">Revelation 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:12">Revelation 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:12">Hebrews 4:12</span>. See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στομα</span></span> used with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαχαιρης</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:24">Luke 21:24</span> (by the mouth of the sword).</p>
<p><b>Countenance </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οψις</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπτω</span></span>), in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:24">John 7:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:44">John 11:44</span>.</p>
<p><b>As the sun shineth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως ο ηλιος φαινε</span></span>). Brachylogy, "as the sun when it shines." For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φαινε</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:5">John 1:5</span>. </p>
