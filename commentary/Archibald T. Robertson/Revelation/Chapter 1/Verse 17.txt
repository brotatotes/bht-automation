<p><b> I fell </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεσα</span></span>). Late form for the old <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεσον</span></span> (second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιπτω</span></span>, to fall). Under the over-powering influence of the vision as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:10">Revelation 19:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>He laid </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εθηκεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span>. The act restored John's confidence.</p>
<p><b>Fear not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη φοβου</span></span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:13">Luke 1:13</span> to Zacharias to give comfort.</p>
<p><b>I am the first and the last </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγω ειμ ο πρωτος κα ο εσχατος</span></span>). Used in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+44:6">Isaiah 44:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+48:12">Isaiah 48:12</span> of God, but here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:8">Revelation 2:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:13">Revelation 22:13</span> of Christ.</p>
<p><b>And the Living One </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ο ζων</span></span>). Present active articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζαω</span></span>, another epithet of God common in the O.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+32:40">Deuteronomy 32:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+49:18">Isaiah 49:18</span>, etc.) and applied purposely to Jesus, with which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:26">John 5:26</span> for Christ's own words about it. </p>
