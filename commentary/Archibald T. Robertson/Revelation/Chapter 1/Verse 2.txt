<p><b> Bare witness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμαρτυρησεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρεω</span></span>, which, along with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυς</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρια</span></span>, is common in all the Johannine books (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:18">Revelation 22:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:20">Revelation 22:20</span>), usually with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span>, but with cognate accusative as here in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:16">Revelation 22:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:20">Revelation 22:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:10">1 John 5:10</span>. Epistolary aorist here, referring to this book.</p>
<p><b>The word of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον λογον του θεου</span></span>). Subjective genitive, given by God. The prophetic word as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:9">Revelation 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:9">Revelation 6:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:4">Revelation 20:4</span>, not the personal Word as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:14">Revelation 19:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>The testimony of Jesus Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την μαρτυριαν Ιησου Χριστου</span></span>). Subjective genitive again, borne witness to by Jesus Christ.</p>
<p><b>Even of all the things that he saw </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οσα ειδεν</span></span>). Relative clause in apposition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογον</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυριαν</span></span>. </p>
