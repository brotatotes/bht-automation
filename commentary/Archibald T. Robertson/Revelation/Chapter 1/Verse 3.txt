<p><b> Blessed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακαριος</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:3">Matthew 5:3</span>. This endorses the book as a whole.</p>
<p><b>He that readeth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αναγινωσκων</span></span>). Present active singular articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγινωσκω</span></span> (as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:16">Luke 4:16</span>). Christians in their public worship followed the Jewish custom of public reading of the Scriptures (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:14">2 Corinthians 3:14</span>). The church reader (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγνωστης</span></span>, lector) gradually acquired an official position. John expects this book to be read in each of the seven churches mentioned (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:4">Revelation 1:4</span>) and elsewhere. Today the public reading of the Bible is an important part of worship that is often poorly done.</p>
<p><b>They that hear </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ακουοντες</span></span>). Present active plural articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουω</span></span> (the audience).</p>
<p><b>And keep </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα τηρουντες</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρεω</span></span>, a common Johannine word (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:4">1 John 2:4</span>, etc.). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:24">Matthew 7:24</span>. "The content of the Apocalypse is not merely prediction; moral counsel and religious instruction are the primary burdens of its pages" (Moffatt).</p>
<p><b>Written </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεγραμμενα</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γραφω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>For the time is at hand </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο γαρ καιρος εγγυς</span></span>). Reason for listening and keeping. On <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιρος</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:1">Matthew 12:1</span>, time of crisis as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:29">1 Corinthians 7:29</span>. How near <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγγυς</span></span> (at hand) is we do not know any more than we do about <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ταχε</span></span> (shortly) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:1">Revelation 1:1</span>. </p>
