<p><b> Who is the faithful witness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο μαρτυς ο πιστος</span></span>). "The witness the faithful," nominative in apposition like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτοτοκος</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρχων</span></span> with the preceding ablative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιησου Χριστου</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span>, a habit of John in this book (apparently on purpose) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:13">Revelation 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:20">Revelation 2:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:12">Revelation 3:12</span>, etc. See this same phrase in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:13">Revelation 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:14">Revelation 3:14</span>. The use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυς</span></span> of Jesus here is probably to the witness (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:1">Revelation 1:1</span>) in this book (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:16">Revelation 22:16</span>), not to the witness of Jesus before Pilate (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:13">1 Timothy 6:13</span>).</p>
<p><b>The first-born of the dead </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο πρωτοτοκος των νεκρων</span></span>). A Jewish Messianic title (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+88:28">Psalms 88:28</span>) and as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:18">Colossians 1:18</span> refers to priority in the resurrection to be followed by others. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:7">Luke 2:7</span> for the word.</p>
<p><b>The ruler of the kings of the earth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αρχων των βασιλεων της γης</span></span>). Jesus by his resurrection won lordship over the kings of earth (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:14">Revelation 17:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:16">Revelation 19:16</span>), what the devil offered him by surrender (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:8">Matthew 4:8</span>).</p>
<p><b>Unto him that loveth us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω αγαπωντ ημας</span></span>). Dative of the articular present (not aorist <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπησαντ</span></span>) active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπαω</span></span> in a doxology to Christ, the first of many others to God and to Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:6">Revelation 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:11">Revelation 4:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:9">Revelation 5:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:12">Revelation 5:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:10">Revelation 7:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:12">Revelation 7:12</span>, etc.). For the thought see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:16">John 3:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>Loosed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυσαντ</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυω</span></span> (Aleph A C), though some MSS. (P Q) read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λουσαντ</span></span> (washed), a manifest correction. Note the change of tense. Christ loosed us once for all, but loves us always.</p>
<p><b>By his blood </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω αιματ αυτου</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:9">Revelation 5:9</span>. John here as in the Gospel and Epistles states plainly and repeatedly the place of the blood of Christ in the work of redemption. </p>
