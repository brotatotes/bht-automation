<p><b> The Alpha and the Omega </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το Αλφα κα το Ο</span></span>). The first and the last letters of the Greek alphabet, each with its own neuter (grammatical gender) article. This description of the eternity of God recurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:6">Revelation 21:6</span> with the added explanation <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η αρχη κα το τελος</span></span> (the Beginning and the End) and of Christ in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:13">Revelation 22:13</span> with the still further explanation <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο πρωτος κα ο εσχατος</span></span> (the First and the Last). This last phrase appears also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:17">Revelation 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:8">Revelation 2:8</span> without <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το Αλφα κα το Ο</span></span>. The change of speaker here is unannounced, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:15">Revelation 16:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:20">Revelation 18:20</span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:5">Revelation 21:5</span>. is God introduced as the speaker. The eternity of God guarantees the prophecy just made.</p>
<p><b>The Lord God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κυριος ο θεος</span></span>). "The Lord the God." Common phrase in Ezekiel (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+6:3">Ezekiel 6:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+6:11">Ezekiel 6:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+7:2">Ezekiel 7:2</span>, etc.) and in this book (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:8">Revelation 4:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:17">Revelation 11:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:3">Revelation 15:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:7">Revelation 16:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:6">Revelation 19:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:22">Revelation 21:22</span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:4">Revelation 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:8">Revelation 4:8</span> for the triple use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span>, etc. to express the eternity of God.</p>
<p><b>The Almighty </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο παντοκρατωρ</span></span>). Late compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πας</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρατεω</span></span>), in Cretan inscription and a legal papyrus, common in LXX and Christian papyri, in N.T. only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:18">2 Corinthians 6:18</span> (from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+38:35">Jeremiah 38:35</span>) and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:8">Revelation 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:8">Revelation 4:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:17">Revelation 11:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:3">Revelation 15:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:7">Revelation 16:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:14">Revelation 16:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:6">Revelation 19:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:15">Revelation 19:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:22">Revelation 21:22</span>. </p>
