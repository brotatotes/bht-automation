<p><b> Another strong angel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλον αγγελον ισχυρον</span></span>). But the seventh trumpet does not sound till <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:15">Revelation 11:15</span>. This angel is not one of the seven or of the four, but like the other strong angel in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:2">Revelation 5:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:21">Revelation 18:21</span> or the other angel in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:6">Revelation 14:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:15">Revelation 14:15</span>. The sixth trumpet of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:13">Revelation 9:13</span> ends in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:21">Revelation 9:21</span>. The opening of the seventh seal was preceded by two visions (chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:7">Revelation 10:7</span>) and so here the sounding of the seventh trumpet (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:15">Revelation 11:15</span>) is preceded by a new series of visions (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:1-11">Revelation 10:1-11</span>).</p>
<p><b>Coming down out of heaven </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταβαινοντα εκ του ουρανου</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταβαινω</span></span> picturing the process of the descent as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:1">Revelation 20:1</span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:12">Revelation 3:12</span>).</p>
<p><b>Arrayed with a cloud </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιβεβλημενον νεφελην</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιβαλλω</span></span> with accusative case retained as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:9">Revelation 7:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:13">Revelation 7:13</span>. Not proof that this angel is Christ, though Christ will come on the clouds (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:7">Revelation 1:7</span>) as he ascended on a cloud (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:9">Acts 1:9</span>). God's chariot is in the clouds (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+104:3">Psalms 104:3</span>), but this angel is a special messenger of God's.</p>
<p><b>The rainbow </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η ιρις</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:3">Revelation 4:3</span> for this word. The construction here is changed from the accusative to the nominative.</p>
<p><b>As the sun </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως ο ηλιος</span></span>). The very metaphor applied to Christ in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:16">Revelation 1:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>As pillars of fire </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως στυλο πυρος</span></span>). Somewhat like the metaphor of Christ in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:15">Revelation 1:15</span>, but still no proof that this angel is Christ. On <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στυλος</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:12">Revelation 3:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:9">Galatians 2:9</span>. </p>
