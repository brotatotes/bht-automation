<p><b> They say </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγουσιν</span></span>). Present active of vivid dramatic action and the indefinite statement in the plural as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:16">Revelation 13:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:15">Revelation 16:15</span>. It is possible that the allusion is to the heavenly voice (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:4">Revelation 10:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:8">Revelation 10:8</span>) and to the angel (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:9">Revelation 10:9</span>).</p>
<p><b>Thou must prophesy again </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε σε παλιν προφητευσα</span></span>). Not a new commission (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:19">Revelation 1:19</span>), though now renewed. C.f. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+4:7">Ezekiel 4:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+6:2">Ezekiel 6:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+1:10">Jeremiah 1:10</span>. The <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παλιν</span></span> (again) points to what has preceded and also to what is to come in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:15">Revelation 11:15</span>. Here it is predictive prophecy (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προφητευσα</span></span>, first aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προφητευω</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Over </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>). In the case, in regard to as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:16">John 12:16</span> (with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γραφω</span></span>), not in the presence of (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> with genitive, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:9">Mark 13:9</span>) nor against (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> with the accusative, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:53">Luke 22:53</span>). For this list of peoples see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:9">Revelation 5:9</span>, occurring seven times in the Apocalypse. </p>
