<p><b> Again speaking and saying </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παλιν λαλουσαν κα λεγουσαν</span></span>). Present active predicate participles feminine accusative singular agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην</span></span> (object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκουσα</span></span>), not with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωνη</span></span> (nominative) as most of the cursives have it (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαλουσα κα λεγουσα</span></span>). Ordinarily it would be <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελαλε κα ελεγεν</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:1">Revelation 4:1</span> for like idiom. This is the voice mentioned in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:4">Revelation 10:4</span>. No great distinction is to be made here between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαλεω</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Go, take </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hυπαγε λαβε</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπαγω</span></span> and second aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>. The use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπαγε</span></span> (exclamation like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδε</span></span>) is common in N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:24">Matthew 5:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:4">Matthew 8:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:21">Matthew 19:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:16">John 4:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:7">John 9:7</span>). Charles calls it a Hebraism (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:1">Revelation 16:1</span>). Note the repeated article here (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το</span></span>) referring to the open book in the hand of the angel (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:2">Revelation 10:2</span>), only here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βιβλιον</span></span> is used, not the diminutive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βιβλαριδιον</span></span> of verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:2">Revelation 10:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:9">Revelation 10:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:10">Revelation 10:10</span>. </p>
