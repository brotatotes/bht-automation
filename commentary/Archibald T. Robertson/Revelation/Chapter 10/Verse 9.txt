<p><b> I went </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απηλθα</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-α</span></span> form), "I went away" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απ-</span></span>) to the angel. John left his position by the door of heaven (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:1">Revelation 4:1</span>).</p>
<p><b>That he should give </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δουνα</span></span>). Second aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>, indirect command after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγων</span></span> (bidding) for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δος</span></span> in the direct discourse (second aorist active imperative second person singular). This use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγω</span></span> to bid occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:14">Revelation 13:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:21">Acts 21:21</span>.</p>
<p><b>He saith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγε</span></span>). Dramatic vivid present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Take it and eat it up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαβε κα καταφαγε αυτο</span></span>). Second aorist (effective) active imperatives of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεσθιω</span></span> (perfective use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span>, "eat down," we say "eat up"). See the same metaphor in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+3:1-3">Ezekiel 3:1-3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+15:6">Jeremiah 15:6</span>. The book was already open and was not to be read aloud, but to be digested mentally by John.</p>
<p><b>It shall make thy belly bitter </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πικρανε σου την κοιλιαν</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πικραινω</span></span>, for which verb see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:11">Revelation 8:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:10">Revelation 10:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:19">Colossians 3:19</span>. There is no reference in Ezekiel or Jeremiah to the bitterness here mentioned.</p>
<p><b>Sweet as honey </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γλυκυ ως μελ</span></span>). For the sweetness of the roll see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+19:10">Psalms 19:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+119:103">Psalms 119:103</span>. "Every revelation of God's purposes, even though a mere fragment, a <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βιβλαριδιον</span></span>, is 'bitter-sweet,' disclosing judgement as well as mercy" (Swete). Deep and bitter sorrows confront John as he comes to understand God's will and way. </p>
