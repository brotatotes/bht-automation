<p><b> A reed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλαμος</span></span>). Old word for a growing reed (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:7">Matthew 11:7</span>) which grew in immense brakes in the Jordan valley, a writer's reed (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:7">3 John 1:7</span>), a measuring-rod (here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:15">Revelation 21:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+40:3-6">Ezekiel 40:3-6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+42:16-19">Ezekiel 42:16-19</span>).</p>
<p><b>Like a rod </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοιος ραβδω</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:27">Revelation 2:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:8">Mark 6:8</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ραβδος</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>And one said </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγων</span></span>). "Saying" (present active masculine participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγω</span></span>) is all that the Greek has. The participle implies <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδωκεν</span></span> (he gave), not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδοθη</span></span>, a harsh construction seen in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+22:20">Genesis 22:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+38:24">Genesis 38:24</span>, etc.</p>
<p><b>Rise and measure </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγειρε κα μετρησον</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγειρω</span></span> (intransitive, exclamatory use as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:11">Mark 2:11</span>) and first aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετρεω</span></span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+42:2">Ezekiel 42:2</span> the prophet measures the temple and that passage is probably in mind here. But modern scholars do not know how to interpret this interlude (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:1-13">Revelation 11:1-13</span>) before the seventh trumpet (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:15">Revelation 11:15</span>). Some (Wellhausen) take it to be a scrap from the Zealot party before the destruction of Jerusalem, which event Christ also foretold (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:2">Mark 13:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:2">Matthew 24:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:6">Luke 21:6</span>) and which was also attributed to Stephen (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:14">Acts 6:14</span>). Charles denies any possible literal interpretation and takes the language in a wholly eschatological sense. There are three points in the interlude, however understood: the chastisement of Jerusalem or Israel (verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:1">Revelation 11:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:2">Revelation 11:2</span>), the mission of the two witnesses (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:3-12">Revelation 11:3-12</span>), the rescue of the remnant (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:13">Revelation 11:13</span>). There is a heavenly sanctuary (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:15">Revelation 7:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:19">Revelation 11:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:15">Revelation 14:15</span>, etc.), but here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ναος</span></span> is on earth and yet not the actual temple in Jerusalem (unless so interpreted). Perhaps here it is the spiritual (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:12">Revelation 3:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:4">2 Thessalonians 2:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:16">1 Corinthians 3:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:16">2 Corinthians 6:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:19">Ephesians 2:19</span>). For altar (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυσιαστηριον</span></span>) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:3">Revelation 8:3</span>. Perhaps measuring as applied to "them that worship therein" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους προσκυνουντας εν αυτω</span></span>) implies a word like numbering, with an allusion to the 144,000 in chapter 7 (a zeugma). </p>
