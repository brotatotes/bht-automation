<p><b> They that dwell upon the earth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο κατοικουντες επ της γης</span></span>). Present active articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατοικεω</span></span>, "an Apocalyptic formula" (Swete) for the non-Christian world (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:10">Revelation 3:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:10">Revelation 6:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:13">Revelation 8:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:8">Revelation 13:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:12">Revelation 13:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:14">Revelation 13:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:8">Revelation 17:8</span>).</p>
<p><b>Rejoice </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαιρουσιν</span></span>). Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαιρω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Over them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ' αυτοις</span></span>). Locative (or dative) case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:11">Revelation 10:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>Make merry </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευφραινοντα</span></span>). Present middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευφραινω</span></span>, old verb (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ, φρην</span></span>, jolly mind), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:32">Luke 15:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:12">Revelation 12:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:20">Revelation 18:20</span>. Jubilant jollification over the cessation of the activity of the two prophets.</p>
<p><b>They shall send gifts to one another </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δωρα πεμψουσιν αλληλοις</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεμπω</span></span> with dative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλληλοις</span></span>. Just as we see it done in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="es+9:19">Esther 9:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="es+9:22">Esther 9:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ne+8:10">Nehemiah 8:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ne+8:12">Nehemiah 8:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>Tormented </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβασανισαν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασανιζω</span></span>, for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:5">Revelation 9:5</span>. This is the reason (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span>) of the fiendish glee of Jew and Gentile, who no longer will have to endure the prophecies (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:3">Revelation 11:3</span>) and dread miracles (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:5">Revelation 11:5</span>) of these two prophets. "Such a sense of relief is perhaps not seldom felt today by bad men when a preacher of righteousness or a signal example of goodness is removed" (Swete). </p>
