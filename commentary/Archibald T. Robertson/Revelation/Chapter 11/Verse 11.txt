<p><b> After the </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα τας</span></span> etc.). The article <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τας</span></span> (the) points back to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:9">Revelation 11:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>The breath of life from God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα ζωης εκ του θεου</span></span>). This phrase (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα ζωης</span></span>) occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+6:17">Genesis 6:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+7:15">Genesis 7:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+7:22">Genesis 7:22</span> of the lower animals, but here there is clearly an allusion to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+37:5">Ezekiel 37:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+37:10">Ezekiel 37:10</span> (also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ki+13:21">2 Kings 13:21</span>), where the dead bones lived again.</p>
<p><b>Entered into them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισηλθεν εν αυτοις</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισερχομα</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> rather than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> after it (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:46">Luke 9:46</span>). The prophecy has here become fact (change from future <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεμψουσιν</span></span> to aorist <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισηλθεν</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>They stood upon their feet </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστησαν επ τους ποδας αυτων</span></span>). Ingressive second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span> (intransitive). Reference to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+37:10">Ezekiel 37:10</span>, but with the accusative in place of genitive there after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ki+13:21">2 Kings 13:21</span>.</p>
<p><b>Fell upon </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεπεσεν επ</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιπιπτω</span></span> with repetition of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>. The same prophetic use of the aorist as in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισηλθεν</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστησαν</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Beheld </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεωρουντας</span></span>). Present active articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεωρεω</span></span>. "The spectators were panic-stricken" (Swete). </p>
