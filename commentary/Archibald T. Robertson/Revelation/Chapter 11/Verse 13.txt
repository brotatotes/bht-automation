<p><b> There was </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγενετο</span></span>). "There came to pass" (second aorist middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span>). Earthquakes are often given as a symbol of great upheavals in social and spiritual order (Swete) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+37:7">Ezekiel 37:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+38:19">Ezekiel 38:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="hag+2:6">Haggai 2:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:8">Mark 13:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:26">Hebrews 12:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:12">Revelation 6:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:18">Revelation 16:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>Fell </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεσεν</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιπτω</span></span>, to fall. Only the tenth (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το δεκατον</span></span>) of the city fell. Cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το τριτον</span></span> (the third) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:7-12">Revelation 8:7-12</span>, perhaps a conventional number.</p>
<p><b>Were killed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεκτανθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκτεινω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:18">Revelation 9:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>Seven thousand persons </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ονοματα ανθρωπων χιλιαδες επτα</span></span>). This use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ονοματα</span></span> (names of men here) is like that in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:4">Revelation 3:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:15">Acts 1:15</span> and occurs in the papyri (Deissmann, <i>Bible Studies</i>, p. 196f.).</p>
<p><b>Were affrighted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμφοβο εγενοντο</span></span>). "Became terrified," old adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν, φοβος</span></span>, fear) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:5">Luke 24:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:4">Acts 10:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:5">Acts 24:5</span>. "A general movement toward Christianity, induced by fear or despair--a prediction fulfilled more than once in ecclesiastical history" (Swete).</p>
<p><b>Gave glory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδωκαν δοξαν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>, when they saw the effect of the earthquake, recognition of God's power (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:24">John 9:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:23">Acts 12:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:20">Romans 4:20</span>). </p>
