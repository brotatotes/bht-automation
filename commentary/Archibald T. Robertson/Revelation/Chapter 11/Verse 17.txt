<p><b> O Lord God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κυριε ο θεος</span></span>). Vocative form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυριε</span></span> and nominative form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο θεος</span></span> (vocative in use). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:8">Revelation 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:8">Revelation 4:8</span> for this combination with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο παντοκρατωρ</span></span> (the Almighty). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ων κα ο ην</span></span> (which art and which wast) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:4">Revelation 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:8">Revelation 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:8">Revelation 4:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:5">Revelation 16:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>Thou hast taken </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειληφες</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>, emphasizing the permanence of God's rule, "Thou hast assumed thy power."</p>
<p><b>Didst reign </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβασιλευσας</span></span>). Ingressive first aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασιλευω</span></span>, "Didst begin to reign." See this combination of tenses (perfect and aorist) without confusion in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:3">Revelation 3:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:7">Revelation 5:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:5">Revelation 8:5</span>. </p>
