<p><b> To shut the heaven </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλεισα τον ουρανον</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλειω</span></span>. As Elijah did by prayer (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ki+17:1">1 Kings 17:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:25">Luke 4:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:17">James 5:17</span>).</p>
<p><b>That it rain not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη υετος βρεχη</span></span>). Sub-final use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη</span></span> with the present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βρεχω</span></span>, old verb to rain (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:45">Matthew 5:45</span>), here with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υετος</span></span> as subject.</p>
<p><b>During the days </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τας ημερας</span></span>). Accusative of extent of time. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:25">Luke 4:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:17">James 5:17</span> the period of the drouth in Elijah's time was three and a half years, just the period here.</p>
<p><b>Of their prophecy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της προφητειας αυτων</span></span>). Not here the gift of prophecy (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:10">1 Corinthians 12:10</span>) or a particular prophecy or collection of prophecies (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:3">Revelation 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:7">Revelation 22:7</span>), but "the execution of the prophetic office" (Swete).</p>
<p><b>Over the waters </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ των υδατων</span></span>). "Upon the waters." As Moses had (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+7:20">Exodus 7:20</span>).</p>
<p><b>Into blood </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις αιμα</span></span>). As already stated in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:8">Revelation 8:8</span> about the third trumpet and now again here.</p>
<p><b>To smite </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παταξα</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πατασσω</span></span>, used here with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξουσιαν εχουσιν</span></span> (they have power), as is <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρεφειν</span></span> (to turn).</p>
<p><b>With every plague </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν παση πληγη</span></span>). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ki+4:8">1 Kings 4:8</span>, but with reference to the plagues in Egypt.</p>
<p><b>As often as they shall desire </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οσακις εαν θελησωσιν</span></span>). Indefinite temporal clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οσακις</span></span> and modal <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> (= <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span>) and the first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θελω</span></span>, "as often as they will." </p>
