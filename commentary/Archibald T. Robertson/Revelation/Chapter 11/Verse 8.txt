<p><b> Their dead bodies lie </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το πτωμα αυτων</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιπτω</span></span> (to fall), a fall, especially of bodies slain in battle, a corpse, a carcase (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:12">Matthew 14:12</span>), here the singular (some MSS. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πτωματα</span></span>, plural) as belonging to each of the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτων</span></span> (their) like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στοματος αυτων</span></span> (their mouth) in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:5">Revelation 11:5</span>. So also in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:9">Revelation 11:9</span>. No word in the Greek for "lie."</p>
<p><b>In </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>). "Upon," as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:6">Revelation 11:6</span>, with genitive (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της πλατειας</span></span>), the broad way (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδου</span></span> understood), from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλατυς</span></span> (broad) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:5">Matthew 6:5</span>, old word (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:21">Revelation 21:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:2">Revelation 22:2</span>).</p>
<p><b>Of the great city </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της πολεως της μεγαλης</span></span>). Clearly Jerusalem in view of the closing clause (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπου--εσταυρωθη</span></span>), though not here called "the holy city" as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:2">Revelation 11:2</span>, and though elsewhere in the Apocalypse Babylon (Rome) is so described (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:8">Revelation 14:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:19">Revelation 16:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:5">Revelation 17:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:2">Revelation 18:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:10">Revelation 18:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:16">Revelation 18:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:18">Revelation 18:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:19">Revelation 18:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:21">Revelation 18:21</span>).</p>
<p><b>Which </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ητις</span></span>). Which very city, not "whichever."</p>
<p><b>Spiritually </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευματικως</span></span>). This late adverb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευματικος</span></span> (spiritual) occurs in the N.T. only twice, in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:14">1 Corinthians 2:14</span> for the help of the Holy Spirit in interpreting God's message and here in a hidden or mystical (allegorical sense). For this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευματικος</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:3">1 Corinthians 10:3</span>. Judah is called Sodom in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+1:9">Isaiah 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+16:46">Ezekiel 16:46</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+16:55">Ezekiel 16:55</span>. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:15">Matthew 10:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:23">Matthew 11:23</span>. Egypt is not applied to Israel in the O.T., but is "an obvious symbol of oppression and slavery" (Swete).</p>
<p><b>Where also their Lord was crucified </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπου κα ο κυριος αυτων εσταυρωθη</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σταυροω</span></span>, to crucify, a reference to the fact of Christ's crucifixion in Jerusalem. This item is one of the sins of Jerusalem and the disciple is not greater than the Master (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:20">John 15:20</span>). </p>
