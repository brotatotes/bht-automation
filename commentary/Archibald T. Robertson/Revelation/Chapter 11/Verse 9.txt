<p><b> Men from among </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ των</span></span> etc.). No word for "men" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωπο</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολλο</span></span>) before <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ των</span></span>, but it is implied (partitive use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:10">Revelation 2:10</span> and often. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:9">Revelation 5:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:9">Revelation 7:9</span> for this enumeration of races and nations.</p>
<p><b>Do look upon </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλεπουσιν</span></span>). Present (vivid dramatic) active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλεπω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Three days and a half </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημερας τρεις κα ημισυ</span></span>). Accusative of extent of time. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hημισυ</span></span> is neuter singular though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημερας</span></span> (days) is feminine as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:23">Mark 6:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:14">Revelation 12:14</span>. The days of the gloating over the dead bodies are as many as the years of the prophesying by the witnesses (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:3">Revelation 11:3</span>), but there is no necessary correspondence (day for a year). This delight of the spectators "is represented as at once fiendish and childish" (Swete).</p>
<p><b>Suffer not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ αφιουσιν</span></span>). Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφιω</span></span>, late form for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφιημ</span></span>, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:34">Mark 1:34</span> (cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφεις</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:20">Revelation 2:20</span>). This use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφιημ</span></span> with the infinitive is here alone in the Apocalypse, though common elsewhere (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:44">John 11:44</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:48">John 11:48</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:7">John 12:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:8">John 18:8</span>).</p>
<p><b>Their dead bodies </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα πτωματα αυτων</span></span>). "Their corpses," plural here, though singular just before and in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:8">Revelation 11:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>To be laid in a tomb </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεθηνα εις μνημα</span></span>). First aorist passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span>, to place. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μνημα</span></span> (old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιμνησκω</span></span>, to remind) is a memorial, a monument, a sepulchre, a tomb (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:3">Mark 5:3</span>). "In a country where burial regularly took place on the day of death the time of exposure and indignity would be regarded long" (Beckwith). See Tobit 1:18ff. </p>
