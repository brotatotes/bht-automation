<p><b> A great voice saying </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωνην μεγαλην λεγουσαν</span></span>). Accusative after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκουσα</span></span> in this phrase as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:11">Revelation 5:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:4">Revelation 10:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:2">Revelation 14:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:4">Revelation 18:4</span>, but the genitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωνης λεγουσης</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:12">Revelation 11:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:13">Revelation 14:13</span>. We are not told whence this voice or song comes, possibly from one of the twenty-four elders (Swete) or some other heavenly beings (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:15">Revelation 11:15</span>) who can sympathize with human beings (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:10">Revelation 19:10</span>), the martyrs in heaven (Charles).</p>
<p><b>Now is come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρτ εγενετο</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αρτ</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:33">John 13:33</span>) shows how recent the downfall of Satan here proleptically pictured as behind us in time (aorist tense <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγενετο</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>The salvation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η σωτηρια</span></span>). Here "the victory" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:10">Revelation 7:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:1">Revelation 19:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>The power </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η δυναμις</span></span>). Gods power over the dragon (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:12">Revelation 7:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:17">Revelation 11:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:1">Revelation 19:1</span>).</p>
<p><b>The kingdom </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η βασιλεια</span></span>). "The empire of God" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:15">Revelation 11:15</span>.</p>
<p><b>The authority of his Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η εξουσια του Χριστου αυτου</span></span>). Which Christ received from the Father (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:18">Matthew 28:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:2">John 17:2</span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:15">Revelation 11:15</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+2:2">Psalms 2:2</span>) for "his Anointed."</p>
<p><b>The accuser </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο κατηγωρ</span></span>). The regular form, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηγορος</span></span>, occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:10">John 8:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:30">Acts 23:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:35">Acts 23:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:16">Acts 25:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:18">Acts 25:18</span> and in many MSS. here in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:10">Revelation 12:10</span>, but A reads <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηγωρ</span></span>, which Westcott and Hort accept. It was once considered a Greek transliteration of a Hebrew word, but Deissmann (<i>Light</i>, etc., p. 93f.) quotes it from a vernacular magical papyrus of the fourth century A.D. with no sign of Jewish or Christian influence, just as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακων</span></span> appears as a vernacular form of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονος</span></span>. Only here is the word applied to Satan in the N.T. In late Judaism Satan is the accuser, and Michael the defender, of the faithful.</p>
<p><b>Of our brethren </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των αδελφων ημων</span></span>). The saints still on earth battling with Satan and his devices.</p>
<p><b>Which accuseth them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο κατηγορων αυτους</span></span>). Articular present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηγορεω</span></span>, old verb, to accuse, usually with the genitive of the person (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:45">John 5:45</span>), but here with the accusative. This is the devil's constant occupation (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+1:6">Job 1:6</span>).</p>
<p><b>Day and night </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημερας κα νυκτος</span></span>). Genitive of time. "By day and by night." </p>
