<p><b> Therefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια τουτο</span></span>). "For this reason" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:15">Revelation 7:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:8">Revelation 18:8</span> (15 times in John's Gospel, Charles notes). It points back to verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:10">Revelation 12:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Rejoice </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευφραινεσθε</span></span>). Present middle imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευφραινω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:10">Revelation 11:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:20">Revelation 18:20</span>.</p>
<p><b>O heavens </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ουρανο</span></span>). Plural here alone in the Apocalypse, though common elsewhere in the N.T. Satan is no longer in the heavens.</p>
<p><b>They that dwell therein </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο εν αυτοις σκηνουντες</span></span>). Present active articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκηνοω</span></span> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:15">Revelation 7:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:6">Revelation 13:6</span>) to dwell (tabernacle) as of Christ in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:14">John 1:14</span> and of God in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:3">Revelation 21:3</span>. The inhabitants of heaven (angels and saints) have cause to rejoice, and earth reason to mourn.</p>
<p><b>Woe for the earth and for the sea </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουα την γην κα την θαλασσαν</span></span>). The accusative after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουα</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:13">Revelation 8:13</span>, but nominative in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:10">Revelation 18:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:16">Revelation 18:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:19">Revelation 18:19</span> in place of the usual dative (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:21">Matthew 11:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:7">Matthew 18:7</span>, etc.).</p>
<p><b>Is gone down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεβη</span></span>). Second aorist (effective) active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταβαινω</span></span>, "did go down."</p>
<p><b>But a short time </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ολιγον καιρον</span></span>). Accusative of extent of time, "a little time." The devil's departure from his warfare in the heavens reveals (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδως</span></span>, knowing, perfect active participle) to him that his time for doing harm to men is limited, and hence his great wrath (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυμον</span></span>, boiling rage). </p>
