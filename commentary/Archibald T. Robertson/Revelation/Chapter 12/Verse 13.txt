<p><b> He persecuted </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδιωξεν</span></span>). First aorist active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διωκω</span></span>, to pursue, to chase, hostile pursuit here as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:10">Matthew 5:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:23">Matthew 10:23</span>, etc. John now, after the "voice" in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:10-13">Revelation 12:10-13</span>, returns to the narrative in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:9">Revelation 12:9</span>. The child was caught away in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:5">Revelation 12:5</span>, and now the woman (the true Israel on earth) is given deadly persecution. Perhaps events since A.D. 64 (burning of Rome by Nero) amply illustrated this vision, and they still do so.</p>
<p><b>Which </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ητις</span></span>). "Which very one." </p>
