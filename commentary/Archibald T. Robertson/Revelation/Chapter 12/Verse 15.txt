<p><b> Water as a flood </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υδωρ ως ποταμον</span></span>). "Water as a river," accusative case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβαλεν</span></span> (cast). The serpent could not follow the woman or stop her flight and so sought to drown her.</p>
<p><b>That he might cause her to be carried away by the stream </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα αυτην ποταμοφορητον ποιηση</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span>. For this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:16">Revelation 17:16</span>. This compound verbal <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποταμοφορητον</span></span> in the predicate accusative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποταμος</span></span>, river, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φορητον</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φορεω</span></span>, to bear) was not coined by John, but occurs in a papyrus of B.C. 110 and in several others after N.T. times. It means simply "carried away by the river." </p>
