<p><b> And she was with child </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα εν γαστρ εχουσα</span></span>). Perhaps <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστιν</span></span> to be supplied or the participle used as a finite verb as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:2">Revelation 10:2</span>. This is the technical idiom for pregnancy as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+1:18">Matthew 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+1:23">Matthew 1:23</span>, etc.</p>
<p><b>Travailing in birth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωδινουσα</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωδινω</span></span>, old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωδιν</span></span> birth-pangs <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:3">1 Thessalonians 5:3</span>), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:27">Galatians 4:27</span>.</p>
<p><b>And in pain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα βασανιζομενη</span></span>). "And tormented" (present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασανιζω</span></span>, for which see already <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:5">Revelation 9:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:10">Revelation 11:10</span>), only here in N.T. in sense of childbirth.</p>
<p><b>To be delivered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεκειν</span></span>). Second aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τικτω</span></span>, to give birth, epexegetical use. Also in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:4">Revelation 12:4</span>. </p>
