<p><b> Was cast down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβληθη</span></span>). Effective first aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαλλω</span></span>, cast down for good and all, a glorious consummation. This vision of final victory over Satan is given by Jesus in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:18">Luke 10:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:31">John 12:31</span>. It has not come yet, but it is coming, and the hope of it should be a spur to missionary activity and zeal. The word megas (great) occurs here with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δρακων</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:3">Revelation 12:3</span>, and the whole picture is repeated in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:2">Revelation 20:2</span>. The dragon in both places is identified with the old serpent (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+3:1">Genesis 3:1</span>) and called <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρχαιος</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρχη</span></span>, beginning), as Jesus said that the devil was a murderer "from the beginning" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:44">John 8:44</span>). Both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαβολος</span></span> (slanderer) and Satan (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Σατανας</span></span>) are common in N.T. for this great dragon and old serpent, the chief enemy of mankind. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:1">Matthew 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:10">Revelation 2:10</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαβολος</span></span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:18">Luke 10:18</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Σατανας</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>The deceiver of the whole world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο πλανων την οικουμενην ολην</span></span>). This is his aim and his occupation, pictured here by the nominative articular present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλαναω</span></span>, to lead astray. For "the inhabited world" see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:1">Luke 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:10">Revelation 3:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:14">Revelation 16:14</span>. Satan can almost "lead astray" the very elect of God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:24">Matthew 24:24</span>), so artful is he in his beguilings as he teaches us how to deceive ourselves (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:8">1 John 1:8</span>).</p>
<p><b>He was cast down to the earth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβληθη εις την γην</span></span>). Effective aorist repeated from the beginning of the verse. "The earth was no new sphere of Satan's working" (Swete).</p>
<p><b>Were cast down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβληθησαν</span></span>). Triple use of the same verb applied to Satan's minions. The expulsion is complete. </p>
