<p><b> There was given to him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδοθη αυτω</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>, to give, as in next line and verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:7">Revelation 13:7</span>. Perhaps a reference to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδωκεν</span></span> (he gave) in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:4">Revelation 13:4</span>, where the dragon (Satan) gave the beast his power. The ultimate source of power is God, but the reference seems to be Satan here.</p>
<p><b>Speaking great things and blasphemies </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαλουν μεγαλα κα βλασφημιας</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαλεω</span></span>, agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στομα</span></span> (nominative neuter singular and subject of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδοθη</span></span>). The words are like Daniel's description of the Little Horn (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:8">Revelation 7:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:20">Revelation 7:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:25">Revelation 7:25</span>) and like the description of Antiochus Epiphanes (I Macc. 1:24). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:11">2 Peter 2:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>To continue </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιησα</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive (epexegetic use) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span>, either in the sense of working (signs), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="da+8:12-14">Daniel 8:12-14</span>, with the accusative of duration of time (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηνας</span></span> months), or more likely in the sense of doing time, with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηνας</span></span> as the direct object as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:12">Matthew 20:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:3">Acts 20:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:13">James 4:13</span>. </p>
