<p><b> Shall worship him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσκυνησουσιν αυτον</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσκυνεω</span></span> with the accusative here as some MSS. in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:4">Revelation 13:4</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το θηριον</span></span>), both constructions in this book.</p>
<p><b>Whose </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου--αυτου</span></span>). Redundant use of genitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτου</span></span> (his) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου</span></span> (whose) as common in this book, and singular instead of plural <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ων</span></span> with antecedent <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντες</span></span> (all, plural), thus calling attention to the responsibility of the individual in emperor-worship.</p>
<p><b>Hath not been written </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου γεγραπτα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γραφω</span></span>, permanent state, stands written.</p>
<p><b>In the book of life of the Lamb </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω βιβλιω της ζωης του αρνιου</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:5">Revelation 3:5</span> for this phrase and the O.T. references. It occurs again in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:8">Revelation 17:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:12">Revelation 20:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:15">Revelation 20:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:27">Revelation 21:27</span>. "Here and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:27">Revelation 21:27</span>, the Divine Register is represented as belonging to 'the Lamb that was slain'" (Swete).</p>
<p><b>That hath been slain from the foundation of the world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του εσφαγμενου</span></span> (for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:6">Revelation 5:6</span>) <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο καταβολης κοσμου</span></span>). For the phrase <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο καταβολης κοσμου</span></span> (not in the LXX) there are six other N.T. uses (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:35">Matthew 13:35</span> without <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοσμου</span></span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+25:34">Revelation 25:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:50">Luke 11:50</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:3">Hebrews 4:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:26">Hebrews 9:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:8">Revelation 17:8</span>), and for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προ καταβολης κοσμου</span></span> three (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:24">John 17:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:4">Ephesians 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:20">1 Peter 1:20</span>). It is doubtful here whether it is to be taken with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του εσφαγμενου</span></span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:20">1 Peter 1:20</span>) or with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεγραπτα</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:8">Revelation 17:8</span>. Either makes sense, and here the most natural use is with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσφαγμενου</span></span>. At any rate the death of Christ lies in the purpose of God, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:16">John 3:16</span>. </p>
