<p><b> The Lamb </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το αρνιον</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:6">Revelation 5:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:17">Revelation 7:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:11">Revelation 12:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:8">Revelation 13:8</span> and is in contrast with the anarthrous <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρνιον</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:11">Revelation 13:11</span>. This proleptic vision of the Lamb "standing on the mount Zion" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστος επ το ορος Σιων</span></span>, second perfect active participle neuter of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> and accusative) is reasoning after the visions of the two beasts. Mount Zion is the site of the new city of God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:22">Hebrews 12:22</span>), the Jerusalem above (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:26">Galatians 4:26</span>), the seat of the Messianic Kingdom whether heaven or the new earth (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:21">Revelation 14:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:22">Revelation 14:22</span>). These victors have the name of the Lamb and God upon their foreheads as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:12">Revelation 3:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:4">Revelation 22:4</span>, in place of the mark of the beast above (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:16">Revelation 13:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:11">Revelation 14:11</span>). This seal protects them (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:4">Revelation 9:4</span>).</p>
<p><b>A hundred and forty and four thousand </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκατον τεσσερακοντα τεσσαρες χιλιαδες</span></span>). "Thousands" literally (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χιλιας</span></span> feminine word for a thousand and so <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχουσα</span></span> feminine plural). For the 144,000 see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:5">Revelation 7:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:8">Revelation 7:8</span>, though some scholars seek a distinction somehow. </p>
