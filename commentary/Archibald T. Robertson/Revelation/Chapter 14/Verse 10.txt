<p><b> He also shall drink </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα αυτος πιετα</span></span>). Future middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πινω</span></span>. Certainty for him as for Babylon and her paramours (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:17">Revelation 16:17</span>).</p>
<p><b>Of the wine of the wrath of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ του οινου του θυμου του θεου</span></span>). Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span> (partitive) after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιετα</span></span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:19">Revelation 16:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:15">Revelation 19:15</span> we have both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυμου</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οργης</span></span> (wrath of the anger of God). The white heat of God's anger, held back through the ages, will be turned loose.</p>
<p><b>Prepared unmixed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του κεκερασμενου ακρατου</span></span>). A bold and powerful oxymoron, "the mixed unmixed." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ακρατος</span></span> is an old adjective (alpha privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεραννυμ</span></span> to mix) used of wine unmixed with water (usually so mixed), here only in N.T. So it is strong wine mixed (perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεραννυμ</span></span>) with spices to make it still stronger (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+75:9">Psalms 75:9</span>).</p>
<p><b>In the cup of his anger </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω ποτηριω της οργης αυτου</span></span>). Both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυμος</span></span> (vehement fury) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οργη</span></span> (settled indignation).</p>
<p><b>He shall be tormented </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασανισθησετα</span></span>). Future passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασανιζω</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:5">Revelation 9:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:10">Revelation 11:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>With fire and brimstone </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν πυρ κα θειω</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:17">Revelation 9:17</span> for fire and brimstone and also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:20">Revelation 19:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:10">Revelation 20:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:8">Revelation 21:8</span>. The imagery is already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+19:24">Genesis 19:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+30:33">Isaiah 30:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+38:22">Ezekiel 38:22</span>.</p>
<p><b>In the presence of the holy angels and in the presence of the Lamb </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενωπιον αγγελων αγιων κα ενωπιον του αρνιου</span></span>). This holy environment adds to the punishment. </p>
