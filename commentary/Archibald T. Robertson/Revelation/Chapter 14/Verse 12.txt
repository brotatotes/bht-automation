<p><b> Here is the patience of the saints </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hωδε η υπομονη των αγιων εστιν</span></span>). John's own comment as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:10">Revelation 13:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:9">Revelation 17:9</span>. In this struggle against emperor worship lay their opportunity (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:3">Romans 5:3</span>). It was a test of loyalty to Christ.</p>
<p><b>They that keep </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο τηρουντες</span></span>). In apposition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των αγιων</span></span> (genitive), though nominative, a frequent anacoluthon in this book (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:20">Revelation 2:20</span>, etc.). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:17">Revelation 12:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>The faith of Jesus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την πιστιν Ιησου</span></span>). "The faith in Jesus" (objective genitive) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:13">Revelation 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:22">Mark 11:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:1">James 2:1</span>. </p>
