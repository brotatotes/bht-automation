<p><b> A white cloud </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νεφελη λευκη</span></span>). Like the "bright cloud" of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:5">Matthew 17:5</span> (Transfiguration), a familiar object in the Mediterranean lands. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="da+7:13">Daniel 7:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:30">Matthew 24:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:64">Matthew 26:64</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:9">Acts 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:11">Acts 1:11</span> for the picture of Christ's return.</p>
<p><b>I saw one sitting </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθημενον</span></span>). No <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδον</span></span> here, but the accusative follows the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδον</span></span> at the beginning, as <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νεφελη</span></span> is nominative after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδου</span></span>, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:1">Revelation 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:4">Revelation 4:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>Like unto a son of man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοιον υιον ανθρωπου</span></span>). Accusative here after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοιον</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:13">Revelation 1:13</span>, instead of the usual associative instrumental (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:4">Revelation 13:4</span>).</p>
<p><b>Having </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχων</span></span>). Nominative again after the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδου</span></span> construction, just before, not after, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδον</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>A golden crown </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στεφανον χρυσουν</span></span>). Here a golden wreath, not the diadems of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:12">Revelation 19:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>A sharp sickle </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δρεπανον οξυ</span></span>). Old form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δρεπανη</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δρεπω</span></span>, to pluck), pruning-hook, in N.T. only in this chapter and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:29">Mark 4:29</span>. Christ is come for reaping this time (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:28">Hebrews 9:28</span>) for the harvesting of earth (verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:15-17">Revelation 14:15-17</span>). The priesthood of Christ is the chief idea in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:12-20">Revelation 1:12-20</span> and "as the true <i>Imperator</i>" (Swete) in chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:19">Revelation 14:19</span>. </p>
