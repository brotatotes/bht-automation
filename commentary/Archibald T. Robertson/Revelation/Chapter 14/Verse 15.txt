<p><b> Send forth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεμψον</span></span>). First aorist (urgency) active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεμπω</span></span>. "Thrust in thy sickle now," this angel urges Christ.</p>
<p><b>And reap </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα θερισον</span></span>). First aorist (urgency) active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεριζω</span></span>, old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θερος</span></span>, summer), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:26">Matthew 6:26</span>. See verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:7">Revelation 14:7</span> for "the hour is come." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Θερισα</span></span> (to reap) is epexegetical infinitive (first aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεριζω</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>The harvest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο θερισμος</span></span>). Old, but rare word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεριζω</span></span>, to harvest), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:30">Matthew 13:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:35">John 4:35</span>, here only in Revelation.</p>
<p><b>Is over-ripe </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξηρανθη</span></span>). First aorist (prophetic as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:17">Revelation 10:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:1">Revelation 15:1</span>) passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξηραινω</span></span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:11">James 1:11</span>), to wither, to dry up. Perhaps just "ripe," not "over-ripe." Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joe+1:17">Joel 1:17</span>. </p>
