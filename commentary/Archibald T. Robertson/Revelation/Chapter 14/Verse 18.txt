<p><b> Another angel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλος αγγελος</span></span>). The fifth angel above Swete terms "the Angel of vengeance." He responds to the call of the sixth angel here as Christ does to the call of the fourth angel in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:15">Revelation 14:15</span>.</p>
<p><b>Out from the altar </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ του θυσιαστηριου</span></span>). From the altar of incense where he is in charge of the fire (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξουσιαν επ του πυρος</span></span>). If it is the altar of burnt offering (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:9">Revelation 6:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:1">Revelation 11:1</span>), we are reminded of the blood of the martyrs (Swete), but if the altar of incense (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:3">Revelation 8:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:5">Revelation 8:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:13">Revelation 9:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:7">Revelation 16:7</span>), then of the prayers of the saints.</p>
<p><b>The sharp sickle </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το δρεπανον το οξυ</span></span>). Useful for vintage as for harvesting. So "send forth" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεμψον</span></span>) as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:15">Revelation 14:15</span>.</p>
<p><b>Gather </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρυγησον</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρυγαω</span></span>, old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρυγη</span></span> dryness, ripeness), in N.T. only <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:18">Revelation 15:18</span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:44">Luke 6:44</span>.</p>
<p><b>The clusters </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους βοτρυας</span></span>). Old word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βοτρυς</span></span>, here only in N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+40:10">Genesis 40:10</span>).</p>
<p><b>Her grapes </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α σταφυλα αυτης</span></span>). Old word again for grapes, bunch of grapes, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:16">Matthew 7:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:44">Luke 6:44</span>.</p>
<p><b>Are fully ripe </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκμασαν</span></span>). Old and common verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακμη</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:16">Matthew 15:16</span>), to come to maturity, to reach its acme, here only in N.T. </p>
