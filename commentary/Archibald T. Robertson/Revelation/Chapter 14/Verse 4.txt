<p><b> Were not defiled with women </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα γυναικων ουκ εμολυνθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μολυνω</span></span>, old verb, to stain, already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:4">Revelation 3:4</span>, which see. The use of this word rules out marriage, which was not considered sinful.</p>
<p><b>For they are virgins </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρθενο γαρ εισιν</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Παρθενος</span></span> can be applied to men as well as women. Swete takes this language "metaphorically, as the symbolical character of the Book suggests." Charles considers it an interpolation in the interest of celibacy for both men and women. If taken literally, the words can refer only to adultery or fornication (Beckwith). Jesus recognised abstinence only for those able to receive it (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:12">Matthew 19:12</span>), as did Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:1">1 Corinthians 7:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:8">1 Corinthians 7:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:32">1 Corinthians 7:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:36">1 Corinthians 7:36</span>). Marriage is approved by Paul in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:3">1 Timothy 4:3</span> and by <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:4">Hebrews 13:4</span>. The New Testament exalts marriage and this passage should not be construed as degrading it.</p>
<p><b>Whithersoever he goeth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπου αν υπαγε</span></span>). Indefinite local clause with modal <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span> and the present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπαγω</span></span>. The Christian life is following the Lamb of God as Jesus taught (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:14">Mark 2:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:21">Mark 10:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:59">Luke 9:59</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:43">John 1:43</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:19">John 21:19</span>, etc.) and as Peter taught (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:21">1 Peter 2:21</span>) and John (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:6">1 John 2:6</span>).</p>
<p><b>Were purchased from among men </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγορασθησαν απο των ανθρωπων</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγοραζω</span></span>, repeating the close of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:3">Revelation 14:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>First fruits </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαρχη</span></span>). See for this word <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:15">1 Corinthians 16:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:16">Romans 11:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:5">Romans 16:5</span>. This seems to mean that the 144,000 represent not the whole, but only a portion of the great harvest to come (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:37">Matthew 9:37</span>), not only the first installment, but those marked by high spiritual service to God and the Lamb (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:1">Romans 12:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:15">Hebrews 13:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:5">1 Peter 2:5</span>). </p>
