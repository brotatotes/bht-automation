<p><b> Was found no lie </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουχ ευρεθη ψευδος</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρισκω</span></span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:23">1 Peter 2:23</span> this passage (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+53:9">Isaiah 53:9</span>) is quoted with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δολος</span></span> (deceit, guile) instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευδος</span></span> (lie), but the difference is not great.</p>
<p><b>Without blemish </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμωμο</span></span>). Alpha privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μωμος</span></span> (blemish, spot). As Christ the Paschal Lamb is (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:19">1 Peter 1:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:14">Hebrews 9:14</span>), so the followers of the Lamb are to be in the end (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:15">Philippians 2:15</span>). </p>
