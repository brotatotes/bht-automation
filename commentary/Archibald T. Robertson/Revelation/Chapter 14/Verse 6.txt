<p><b> Another angel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλον αγγελον</span></span>). A new turn in the drama comes with each angel (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:2">Revelation 7:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:3">Revelation 8:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:13">Revelation 8:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:1">Revelation 10:1</span>). Here the angel is seen "flying in mid heaven" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πετομενον εν μεσουρανηματ</span></span>), while in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:13">Revelation 8:13</span> John heard him "flying in mid heaven" (genitive case of same participle, which see). This one is in the sight and hearing of all.</p>
<p><b>Having </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχοντα</span></span>). Accusative singular agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγγελον</span></span> like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πετομενον</span></span> (flying), but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγων</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:7">Revelation 14:7</span> is nominative, as if a new sentence like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγων</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:1">Revelation 4:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>An eternal gospel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευαγγελιον αιωνιον</span></span>). The only use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευαγγελιον</span></span> in John's writings, though the verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευαγγελισα</span></span> (first aorist active infinitive epexegetical with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχοντα</span></span> like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:12">John 16:12</span>) occurs here and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:7">Revelation 10:7</span>. Here it is not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το ευαγγελιον</span></span> (the gospel), but merely a proclamation of God's eternal (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιωνιος</span></span> here alone in the Apocalypse, though common in the Fourth Gospel and I John) purpose. Origen even took this "eternal gospel" to be another book to be written! Note the double use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> (with accusative after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευαγγελισα</span></span> and the genitive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γης</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:9">Revelation 5:9</span> for the races, etc. </p>
