<p><b> As it were a glassy sea </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως θαλασσαν υαλινην</span></span>). Accusative case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδον</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως</span></span> here, not in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:6">Revelation 4:6</span>, which see for the symbol.</p>
<p><b>Mingled with fire </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεμιγμενην πυρ</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιγνυμ</span></span>, to mix, and the associative instrumental case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυρ</span></span>. This item not in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:6">Revelation 4:6</span> (a vision of peace), but here it adds to the splendour of the vision. This parenthesis (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:2-4">Revelation 15:2-4</span>) gives a picture of the martyrs in their state of bliss.</p>
<p><b>Them that come off victorious </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους νικωντας</span></span>). Present active articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νικαω</span></span>, accusative after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδον</span></span>, "those that come off victorious" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:4">Revelation 14:4</span>).</p>
<p><b>From the beast and from his image </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ του θηριου κα εκ της εικονος αυτου</span></span>). This use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νικαω</span></span> is unusual, also with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ του αριθμου</span></span>. For these items see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:1">Revelation 13:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:14">Revelation 13:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:17">Revelation 13:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:9">Revelation 14:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:11">Revelation 14:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:20">Revelation 19:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:4">Revelation 20:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>By the glassy sea </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ την θαλασσαν την υαλινην</span></span>). Or "upon" more likely (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:6">Revelation 4:6</span>) with the accusative as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:25">Matthew 14:25</span>.</p>
<p><b>Harps of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κιθαρας του θεου</span></span>). Objective genitive, for the worship of God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:8">Revelation 5:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:2">Revelation 14:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ch+16:42">1 Chronicles 16:42</span>). </p>
