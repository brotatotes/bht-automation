<p><b> The song of Moses </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την ωιδην του Μωυσεως</span></span>). <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+14:31">Exodus 14:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+15:1-19">Exodus 15:1-19</span>. A song of victory like that of Moses after crossing the Red Sea.</p>
<p><b>And the song of the Lamb </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την ωιδην του αρνιου</span></span>). A separate note of victory like that of Moses, though one song, not two. Charles finds it impossible to reconcile the two expressions, if genuine, but it is a needless objection. The words come from the O.T.: "great" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεγαλα</span></span>) from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+111:2">Psalms 111:2</span>, "wonderful" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θαυμαστα</span></span>) from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+139:14">Psalms 139:14</span>, "O Lord God the Almighty" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κυριε ο θεος ο παντοκρατωρ</span></span>) from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="am+4:13">Amos 4:13</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:8">Revelation 4:8</span>), "righteous and true" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαια κα αληθινα</span></span>) from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+32:4">Deuteronomy 32:4</span>, "Thou King of the ages" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο βασιλευς των αιωνων</span></span>) like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+10:10">Jeremiah 10:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:17">1 Timothy 1:17</span>. Some MSS. have "the king of the saints" and some "the king of the nations," like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+10:7">Jeremiah 10:7</span>. John thus combines in Hebraic tone the expressions of the old and the new in the song to the Glorified Messiah. </p>
