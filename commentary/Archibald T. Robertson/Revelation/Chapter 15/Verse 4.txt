<p><b> Who shall not fear? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τις ου μη φοβηθηι;</span></span>). Rhetorical question with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη</span></span> (double negative) and first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φοβεομα</span></span> future passive in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+10:7">Jeremiah 10:7</span>).</p>
<p><b>And glorify </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα δοξασε</span></span>). Change here to the future indicative instead of the aorist subjunctive, as often. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+86:9">Psalms 86:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>Thou only art holy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μονος οσιος</span></span>). Both predicate adjectives, "Thou art alone holy." God alone is perfectly holy (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:5">Revelation 16:5</span>).</p>
<p><b>Shall come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηξουσιν</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>And worship </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα προσκυνησουσιν</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσκυνεω</span></span>. Both from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+86:9">Psalms 86:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>Have been made manifest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφανερωθησαν</span></span>). Prophetic first aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανεροω</span></span>. This martyr's song has the ring of great poetry. </p>
