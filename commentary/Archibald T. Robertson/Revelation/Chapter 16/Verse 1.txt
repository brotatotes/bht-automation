<p><b> A great voice </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεγαλης φωνης</span></span>). Not an angel as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:2">Revelation 5:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:2">Revelation 7:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:3">Revelation 10:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:7">Revelation 14:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:9">Revelation 14:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:15">Revelation 14:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:18">Revelation 14:18</span>, but of God as <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:8">Revelation 15:8</span> shows, since no one could enter the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ναος</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Pour out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκχεετε</span></span>). Second aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκχεω</span></span> (same form as present active imperative). Blass would change to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκχεατε</span></span> (clearly aorist) as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:6">Revelation 16:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>The seven bowls </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τας επτα φιαλας</span></span>). The article points to verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:7">Revelation 16:7</span>. </p>
