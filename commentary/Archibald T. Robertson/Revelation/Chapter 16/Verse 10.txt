<p><b> Upon the throne of the beast </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ τον θρονον του θηριου</span></span>). That is Rome (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:2">Revelation 13:2</span>). The dragon gave the beast his throne (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:13">Revelation 2:13</span>).</p>
<p><b>Was darkened </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγενετο εσκοτωμενη</span></span>). Periphrastic past perfect passive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκοτοω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:2">Revelation 9:2</span>). Like the darkness of the Egyptian plague (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+10:22">Exodus 10:22</span>) and worse, for the effects of the previous plagues continue.</p>
<p><b>They gnawed their tongues </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμασωντο τας γλωσσας αυτων</span></span>). Imperfect middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μασαομα</span></span>, old verb (to chew), from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαω</span></span> (to knead), only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>For pain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ του πονου</span></span>). "Out of distress" (cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:13">Revelation 8:13</span>), rare sense of old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πενομα</span></span> to work for one's living), in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:4">Revelation 21:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+4:13">Colossians 4:13</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:12">Matthew 8:12</span>. </p>
