<p><b> Upon the great river, the river Euphrates </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ τον ποταμον τον μεγαν τον Ευφρατην</span></span>). The sixth trumpet brings up the river Euphrates also (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:14">Revelation 9:14</span>), only there <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> with the locative, while here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> with the accusative. Note triple use of the article <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον</span></span> here.</p>
<p><b>Was dried up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξηρανθη</span></span>). First aorist (prophetic) passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξηραινω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:15">Revelation 14:15</span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="zec+10:11">Zechariah 10:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>That may be made ready </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα ετοιμασθη</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the first aorist passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετοιμαζω</span></span>. Common verb in Rev. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:6">Revelation 8:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:7">Revelation 9:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:15">Revelation 9:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:6">Revelation 12:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:7">Revelation 19:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:2">Revelation 21:2</span>).</p>
<p><b>The way for the kings </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η οδος των βασιλεων</span></span>). Objective genitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασιλεων</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>That come from the sunrising </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των απο ανατολης ελιου</span></span>). "Those from the rising of the sun," the kings from the east (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:2">Matthew 2:2</span>) in their march against Rome. Parthia in particular resisted Rome before Trajan's day. </p>
