<p><b> Coming out of </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span> alone, no participle <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομενα</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Of the dragon </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του δρακοντος</span></span>). That is Satan (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:3">Revelation 12:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:9">Revelation 12:9</span>).</p>
<p><b>Of the beast </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του θηριου</span></span>). The first beast (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:1">Revelation 13:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:12">Revelation 13:12</span>) and then just the beast (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:14">Revelation 13:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:9">Revelation 14:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:11">Revelation 14:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:2">Revelation 15:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:2">Revelation 16:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:10">Revelation 16:10</span>), the brute force of the World-power represented by the Roman Empire" (Swete).</p>
<p><b>Of the false prophet </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του ψευδοπροφητου</span></span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:15">Matthew 7:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:6">Acts 13:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:22">1 John 2:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:3">1 John 4:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:7">2 John 1:7</span>. Identified with the second beast (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:11-14">Revelation 13:11-14</span>) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:20">Revelation 19:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:10">Revelation 20:10</span>. So the sixth bowl introduces the dragon and his two subalterns of chapters <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:12">Revelation 16:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:13">Revelation 16:13</span> (the two beasts).</p>
<p><b>Three unclean spirits </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευματα τρια ακαθαρτα</span></span>). Out of the mouths of each of the three evil powers (the dragon and the two beasts) comes an evil spirit. See the use of mouth in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:16">Revelation 1:16</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:17">Revelation 9:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:5">Revelation 11:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:15">Revelation 12:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:15">Revelation 19:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:21">Revelation 19:21</span>) as a chief seat of influence. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:8">2 Thessalonians 2:8</span> we have "the breath of his mouth" (the other sense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα</span></span>). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακαθαρτον</span></span> (unclean) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:23">Mark 1:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:11">Mark 3:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:2">Mark 5:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:16">Acts 5:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:7">Acts 8:7</span>. Christ expelled unclean spirits, but His enemies send them forth" (Swete). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="zec+13:2">Zechariah 13:2</span> "the false prophets and the unclean spirits."</p>
<p><b>As it were frogs </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως βατραχο</span></span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+8:5">Exodus 8:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="le+11:10">Leviticus 11:10</span>. Old word, here alone in N.T. Like loathsome frogs in form. </p>
