<p><b> Spirits of devils </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευματα δαιμονιων</span></span>). "Spirits of demons." Explanation of the simile <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως βατραχο</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:1">1 Timothy 4:1</span> about "deceiving spirits and teachings of demons."</p>
<p><b>Working signs </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιουντα σημεια</span></span>). "Doing signs" (present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span>). The Egyptian magicians wrought "signs" (tricks), as did Simon Magus and later Apollonius of Tyana. Houdini claimed that he could reproduce every trick of the spiritualistic mediums.</p>
<p><b>Which go forth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α εκπορευετα</span></span>). Singular verb with neuter plural (collective) subject.</p>
<p><b>Unto the kings </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ τους βασιλεις</span></span>). The three evil spirits (dragon and the two beasts) spur on the kings of the whole world to a real world war. "There have been times when nations have been seized by a passion for war which the historian can but imperfectly explain" (Swete).</p>
<p><b>To gather them together </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συναγαγειν</span></span>). Second aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συναγω</span></span>, to express purpose (that of the unclean spirits).</p>
<p><b>Unto the war of the great day of God, the Almighty </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τον πολεμον της ημερας της μεγαλης του θεου του παντοκρατορος</span></span>). Some take this to be war between nations, like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:8">Mark 13:8</span>, but it is more likely war against God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+2:2">Psalms 2:2</span>) and probably the battle pictured in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:14">Revelation 17:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:19">Revelation 19:19</span>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:12">2 Peter 3:12</span>, "the day of God," his reckoning with the nations. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joe+2:11">Joel 2:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joe+3:4">Joel 3:4</span>. Paul uses "that day" for the day of the Lord Jesus (the Parousia) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:2">1 Thessalonians 5:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:10">2 Thessalonians 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:2">2 Thessalonians 2:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:8">1 Corinthians 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:14">2 Corinthians 1:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:6">Philippians 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:16">Philippians 2:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:12">2 Timothy 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:18">2 Timothy 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:8">2 Timothy 4:8</span>. </p>
