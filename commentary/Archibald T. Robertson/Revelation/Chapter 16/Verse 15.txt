<p><b> Behold, I come as a thief </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδου ερχομα ως κλεπτης</span></span>). The voice of Christ breaks in with the same metaphor as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:3">Revelation 3:3</span>, which see. There comes one of seven beatitudes in Rev. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:3">Revelation 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:13">Revelation 14:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:15">Revelation 16:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:9">Revelation 19:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:6">Revelation 20:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:7">Revelation 22:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:14">Revelation 22:14</span>). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γρηγορων</span></span> (watching) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:2">Revelation 3:2</span>, and for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρων</span></span> (keeping), <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:3">Revelation 1:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Lest he walk naked </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη γυμνος περιπατη</span></span>). Negative purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη</span></span> and the present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιπατεω</span></span>, and note predicate nominative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γυμνος</span></span> (naked).</p>
<p><b>And they see his shame </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα βλεπωσιν την ασχημοσυνην αυτου</span></span>). Continuation of the final clause with present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλεπω</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ασχημοσυνην</span></span> is old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασχημων</span></span>, indecent, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:23">1 Corinthians 12:23</span>), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:27">Romans 1:27</span>, a euphemism for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την αισχυνην</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:18">Revelation 3:18</span>). </p>
