<p><b> They gathered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνηγαγεν</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συναγω</span></span>, singular (the three unclean spirits), like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκπορευετα</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:14">Revelation 16:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>Har-Magedon </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hαρ-Μαγεδων</span></span>). John proceeds now after the interruption in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:15">Revelation 16:15</span>. Perhaps "the mountains of Megiddo" though not certain. Megiddo is in the valley of Esdraelon, and by the waters of Megiddo (the Kishon) Israel gained a decisive victory over Sisera (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jdg+5:19">Judges 5:19</span>), celebrated in Deborah's song. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:8">Revelation 20:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:4">Revelation 20:4</span>. </p>
