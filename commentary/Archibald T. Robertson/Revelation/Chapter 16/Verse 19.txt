<p><b> Was divided into three parts </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγενετο εις τρια μερη</span></span>). "Came into three parts" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span> again). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:3">Revelation 11:3</span> a tenth part of the city fell. Babylon (Rome) is meant (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:18">Revelation 17:18</span>).</p>
<p><b>Fell </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεσαν</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιπτω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-αν</span></span> form in place of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-ον</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Was remembered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμνησθη</span></span>). First aorist (prophetic) passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιμνησκω</span></span>. Babylon (Rome) had not been overlooked. God was simply biding his time with Rome.</p>
<p><b>To give unto her </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δουνα αυτη</span></span>). Second aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>, epexegetic use as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:18">Revelation 11:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:9">Revelation 16:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>The cup of the wine of the fierceness of his wrath </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το ποτηριον του οινου του θυμου της οργης αυτου</span></span>). "The cup of the wine of the wrath of his anger," using both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυμος</span></span> (boiling rage) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οργη</span></span> (settled anger). See both in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+30:24">Jeremiah 30:24</span>. </p>
