<p><b> Went and poured out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απηλθεν κα εξεχεεν</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απερχομα</span></span> (redundant use like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπαγετε</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκχεετε</span></span>, "go and pour out," in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:1">Revelation 16:1</span>) and of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκχεω</span></span>. Each angel "went off" to perform his task. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεχεεν</span></span> see it repeated in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:3">Revelation 16:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:4">Revelation 16:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:8">Revelation 16:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:10">Revelation 16:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:12">Revelation 16:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:17">Revelation 16:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>Into the earth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις την γην</span></span>). This same use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεχεεν</span></span> in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:3">Revelation 16:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:4">Revelation 16:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>It became </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγενετο</span></span>). "There came" (second aorist middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>A noisome and grievous sore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελκος κακον κα πονηρον</span></span>). "Bad and malignant sore." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hελκος</span></span> is old word for a suppurated wound (Latin <i>ulcus</i>), here, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:11">Revelation 16:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:21">Luke 16:21</span>. See the sixth Egyptian plague (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+9:10">Exodus 9:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+28:27">Deuteronomy 28:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+28:35">Deuteronomy 28:35</span>) and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+2:7">Job 2:7</span>. The magicians were attacked in Egypt and the worshippers of Caesar here (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:17">Revelation 13:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:9">Revelation 14:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:11">Revelation 14:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:20">Revelation 19:20</span>). </p>
