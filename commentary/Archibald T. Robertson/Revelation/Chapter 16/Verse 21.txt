<p><b> Hail </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαλαζα</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:17">Revelation 8:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:19">Revelation 11:19</span>.</p>
<p><b>Every stone about the weight of a talent </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως ταλαντιαια</span></span>). Old adjective (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταλαντον</span></span>), here only in N.T., but in Polybius and Josephus. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+9:24">Exodus 9:24</span> for the great hail in Egypt and also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jos+10:11">Joshua 10:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+28:2">Isaiah 28:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+38:22">Ezekiel 38:22</span> for hail as the symbol of God's wrath. In the LXX a <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταλαντον</span></span> ranged in weight from 108 to 130 pounds.</p>
<p><b>Because of the plague of hail </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ της πληγης της χαλαζης</span></span>). "As a result of the plague of hail." This punishment had the same effect as in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:9">Revelation 16:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:11">Revelation 16:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>Exceeding great </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μεγαλη--σφροδρα</span></span>). Emphatic positions at ends of the clause (great--exceedingly). </p>
