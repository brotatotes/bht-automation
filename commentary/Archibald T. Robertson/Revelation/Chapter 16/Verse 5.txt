<p><b> The angel of the waters </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του αγγελου τον υδατων</span></span>). Genitive case object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκουσα</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:1">Revelation 7:1</span> for the four angels in control of the winds and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:18">Revelation 14:18</span> for the angel with power over fire. The rabbis spoke also of an angel with power over the earth and another over the sea.</p>
<p><b>Which art and which wast </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ων κα ο ην</span></span>). See this peculiar idiom for God's eternity with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span> as relative before <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:4">Revelation 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:8">Revelation 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:8">Revelation 4:8</span>, but without <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ερχομενος</span></span> (the coming on, the one who is to be) there for the future as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:17">Revelation 11:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>Thou Holy One </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο οσιος</span></span>). Nominative form, but vocative case, as often. Note both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιος</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οσιος</span></span> applied to God as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:1">Revelation 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:3">Revelation 15:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Because thou didst thus judge </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ ταυτα εκρινας</span></span>). Reason for calling God <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιος</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οσιος</span></span>. The punishment on the waters is deserved. First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρινω</span></span>, to judge. </p>
