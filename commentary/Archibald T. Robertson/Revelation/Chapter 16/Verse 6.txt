<p><b> For </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span>). Second causal conjunction (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span>) explanatory of the first <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span>, like the two cases of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:4">Revelation 15:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>They poured out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεχεαν</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκχεω</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-αν</span></span> instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-ον</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Blood hast thou given them to drink </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιμα αυτοις δεδωκας πειν</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hαιμα</span></span> (blood) is the emphatic word, measure for measure for shedding the blood of saints and prophets (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:18">Revelation 11:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:24">Revelation 18:24</span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>, and so a permanent and just punishment. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πειν</span></span> is the abbreviated second aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πινω</span></span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιειν</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιον</span></span>). It is the epexegetical infinitive after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεδωκας</span></span>. There was no more drinking-water, but only this coagulated blood.</p>
<p><b>They are worthy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αξιο εισιν</span></span>). "Terrible antithesis" (Swete) to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:4">Revelation 3:4</span>. The asyndeton adds to it (Alford). </p>
