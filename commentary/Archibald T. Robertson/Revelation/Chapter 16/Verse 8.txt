<p><b> Upon the sun </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ τον ηλιον</span></span>). Not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> (into) as in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:2">Revelation 16:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:3">Revelation 16:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:4">Revelation 16:4</span>. The fourth trumpet (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:12">Revelation 8:12</span>) affected a third of the sun, moon, and stars with a plague of darkness, but here it is a plague of extreme heat.</p>
<p><b>To scorch with fire </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καυματισα εν πυρ</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καυματιζω</span></span>, late (Plutarch, Epictetus) causative verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καυμα</span></span>, heat), in N.T. only here and verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:9">Revelation 16:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:6">Matthew 13:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:6">Mark 4:6</span>. The addition of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν πυρ</span></span> (in fire, with fire) intensifies the picture. </p>
