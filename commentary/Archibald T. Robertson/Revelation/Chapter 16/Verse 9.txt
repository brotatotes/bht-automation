<p><b> Were scorched </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκαυματισθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of same verb.</p>
<p><b>With great heat </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καυμα μεγα</span></span>). Cognate accusative retained with the passive verb. Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιω</span></span> to burn), in N.T. only <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:16">Revelation 7:16</span> and here. For blaspheming the name of God see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:6">Revelation 13:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:7">James 2:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:24">Romans 2:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:1">1 Timothy 6:1</span>. They blamed God for the plagues.</p>
<p><b>They repented not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μετενοησαν</span></span>). This solemn negative aorist of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετανοεω</span></span> is a refrain like a funeral dirge (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:20">Revelation 9:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:11">Revelation 16:11</span>). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:13">Revelation 11:13</span> some did repent because of the earthquake. Even deserved punishment may harden the heart.</p>
<p><b>To give him glory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δουνα αυτω δοξαν</span></span>). Second aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>, almost result. For the phrase see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:13">Revelation 11:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:7">Revelation 14:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:7">Revelation 19:7</span>. </p>
