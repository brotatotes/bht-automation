<p><b> Which have received no kingdom as yet </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιτινες βασιλειαν ουπω ελαβον</span></span>). Second aorist (proleptic and prophetic) active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>. The heads are emperors and the horns are kings (both called <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασιλεις</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>As kings </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως βασιλεις</span></span>). Compared to kings (see <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:10">Revelation 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:6">Revelation 4:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:7">Revelation 9:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:3">Revelation 13:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:3">Revelation 14:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:21">Revelation 16:21</span>) without identification with the emperors, though succeeding them with "quasi-imperial powers" with the beast.</p>
<p><b>For one hour </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιαν ωραν</span></span>). Accusative of extent of time, and that a brief time (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:10">Revelation 18:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:16">Revelation 18:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:19">Revelation 18:19</span>) in comparison with the beast (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:2">Revelation 13:2</span>). </p>
