<p><b> Where the harlot sitteth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου η πορνος καθητα</span></span>). Relative adverb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου</span></span> (where) referring to the waters (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υδατα</span></span>) of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:1">Revelation 17:1</span> on which the harlot sits. Present middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθημα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Are peoples, and multitudes, and nations, and tongues </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαο κα οχλο εισιν κα εθνη κα γλωσσα</span></span>). The O.T. uses "waters" as symbol for "peoples" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+8:7">Isaiah 8:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+47:2">Jeremiah 47:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+29:10">Psalms 29:10</span>, etc.). "Rome's greatest danger lay in the multitudes which were under her sway" (Swete). </p>
