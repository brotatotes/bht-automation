<p><b> These shall hate the harlot </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτο μισησουσιν την πορνην</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μισεω</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hουτο</span></span> is resumptive demonstrative pronoun (masculine) referring to the ten horns and the beast (neuter); construction according to sense. The downfall of Rome will come from the sudden change in subject peoples.</p>
<p><b>Shall make her desolate and naked </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηρημωμενην ποιησουσιν αυτην κα γυμνην</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span> and perfect passive predicate accusative participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερημοω</span></span>, old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερημος</span></span> desolate), again in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:16">Revelation 18:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:19">Revelation 18:19</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Γυμνην</span></span> (naked) is predicate adjective.</p>
<p><b>Shall eat her flesh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τας σαρκας αυτης φαγοντα</span></span>). Future middle of the defective verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσθιω</span></span>, to eat. Note plural <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαρκας</span></span>, portions of flesh (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:3">James 5:3</span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+27:2">Psalms 27:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mic+3:3">Micah 3:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Shall burn her utterly with fire </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτην κατακαυσουσιν εν πυρ</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατακαιω</span></span>, to burn down (perfective use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιω</span></span>). John wrote before the days of Alaric, Genseric, Ricimer, Totila, with their hordes which devastated Rome and the west in the fifth and sixth centuries. "No reader of the <i>Decline and Fall</i> can be at a loss for materials which will at once illustrate and justify the general trend of St. John's prophecy" (Swete). </p>
