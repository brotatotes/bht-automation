<p><b> Did put </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδωκεν</span></span>). "Did give" (first aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>To do his mind </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιησα την γνωμην αυτου</span></span>). Epexegetic first aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδωκεν</span></span>, as often in this book. They are of one mind (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:13">Revelation 17:13</span>) because God put them up to it, clear statement of God's over-ruling hand among the nations.</p>
<p><b>Until the words of God should be accomplished </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχρ τελεσθησοντα ο λογο του θεου</span></span>). Temporal clause about the future with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχρ</span></span> (like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως</span></span>), with the future indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελεω</span></span>, but with aorist passive subjunctive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελεσθωσιν</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:8">Revelation 15:8</span>. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελεω</span></span> see also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:7">Revelation 10:7</span>. For "the words of God" see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:9">Revelation 19:9</span>. They will be fulfilled. </p>
