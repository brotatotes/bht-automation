<p><b> The kings of the earth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο βασιλεις της γης</span></span>). Repeated in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:5">Revelation 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:15">Revelation 6:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:18">Revelation 17:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:3">Revelation 18:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:9">Revelation 18:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:19">Revelation 19:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:24">Revelation 21:24</span> and "the kings of the inhabited earth" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:14">Revelation 16:14</span>) either for human rulers in general or the vassal kings absorbed by the Roman Empire.</p>
<p><b>Committed fornication </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επορνευσαν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορνευω</span></span>. "In purchasing the favour of Rome by accepting her suzerainty and with it her vices and idolatries" (Swete).</p>
<p><b>Were made drunken </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμεθυσθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεθυσκω</span></span>, old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεθυ</span></span>), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:45">Luke 12:45</span>, here only in the Apocalypse. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+51:7">Isaiah 51:7</span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεποτικεν</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:8">Revelation 14:8</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:3">Revelation 18:3</span>. </p>
