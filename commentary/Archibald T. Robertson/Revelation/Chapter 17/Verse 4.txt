<p><b> Was arrayed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην περιβεβλημενη</span></span>). Periphrastic past perfect indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιβαλλω</span></span>, to fling round one.</p>
<p><b>In purple and scarlet </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορφυρουν κα κοκκινον</span></span>). Accusative retained after this passive verb of clothing, as so often. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πορφυρους</span></span> is old adjective for purple (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορφυρα</span></span>), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:2">John 19:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:5">John 19:5</span>. See preceding verse for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοκκινος</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Decked </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεχρυσωμενη</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρυσοω</span></span>, old verb, to gild, to adorn with gold, here alone in N.T.</p>
<p><b>With gold and precious stone and pearls </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρυσιω κα λιθω τιμιω κα μαργαριταις</span></span>). Instrumental case. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χρυσιω</span></span> is cognate with the participle. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Λιθω τιμιω</span></span> is collective (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:12">Revelation 18:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:16">Revelation 18:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:19">Revelation 21:19</span>). There is a <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζευγμα</span></span> also with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαργαριταις</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:12">Revelation 18:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:16">Revelation 18:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:21">Revelation 21:21</span>), for which word see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:6">Matthew 7:6</span>. Probably John is thinking of the finery of the temple prostitutes in Asia Minor.</p>
<p><b>Full of abominations </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεμον βδελυγματων</span></span>). Agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποτηριον</span></span>, "cup" (neuter singular accusative). Some MSS. read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεμων</span></span> (nominative masculine like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχων</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:3">Revelation 17:3</span>, quite irregular). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βδελυγματων</span></span> (genitive after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεμον</span></span>) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:15">Matthew 24:15</span>; (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:14">Mark 13:14</span>), common in the LXX for idol worship and its defilements (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βδελυσσω</span></span>, to render foul), both ceremonial and moral. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+15:7">Jeremiah 15:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>Even the unclean things of her fornication </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα τα ακαθαρτα της πορνειας αυτης</span></span>). Either the accusative after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεμον</span></span> as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:3">Revelation 17:3</span> (and full of the unclean things of her fornication) or the object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχουσα</span></span>, like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποτηριον</span></span>. </p>
