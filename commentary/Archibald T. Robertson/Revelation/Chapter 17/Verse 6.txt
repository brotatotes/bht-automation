<p><b> Drunken with the blood of the saints </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεθυουσαν εκ του αιματος των αγιων</span></span>). Present active feminine accusative singular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεθυω</span></span>, old verb, to be drunk (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:49">Matthew 24:49</span>).</p>
<p><b>Of the martyrs of Jesus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των μαρτυρων Ιησου</span></span>). "Witnesses" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:13">Revelation 2:13</span>) for Jesus (objective genitive) unto blood (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:6">Revelation 16:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:24">Revelation 18:24</span>) and so martyrs in the modern sense of the word. "Drunk with blood" is a common idea with the ancients (Euripides, Josephus, Philo, Cicero, Pliny).</p>
<p><b>With a great wonder </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θαυμα μεγα</span></span>). Cognate accusative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εθαυμασα</span></span>. </p>
