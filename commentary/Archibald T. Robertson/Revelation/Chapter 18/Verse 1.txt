<p><b> Coming down out of heaven </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταβαινοντα εκ του ουρανου</span></span>). Present active predicate participle. Not the angel of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:1">Revelation 17:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:7">Revelation 17:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:15">Revelation 17:15</span> (John's guide), but one announcing the doom of Babylon (Rome). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:1">Revelation 10:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:1">Revelation 20:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Was lightened </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφωτισθη</span></span>). First aorist passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωτιζω</span></span>, old causative verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φως</span></span>, light), common in N.T. as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:1">Revelation 18:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:23">Revelation 21:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:5">Revelation 22:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>With his glory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ της δοξης αυτου</span></span>). "By reason of (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:13">Revelation 8:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:10">Revelation 16:10</span>) his glory." "So recently has he come from the Presence that in passing he flings a broad belt of light across the dark earth" (Swete). </p>
