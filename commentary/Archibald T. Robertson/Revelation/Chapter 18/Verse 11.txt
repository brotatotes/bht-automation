<p><b> The merchants </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο εμπορο</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:3">Revelation 18:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:15">Revelation 18:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:23">Revelation 18:23</span>. The dirge of the merchants follows the wail of the kings.</p>
<p><b>Weep and mourn </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλαιουσιν κα πενθουσιν</span></span>). Present active indicatives of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλαιω</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πενθεω</span></span> as in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:9">Revelation 18:9</span> (for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλαιω</span></span>), <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:15">Revelation 18:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:19">Revelation 18:19</span>.</p>
<p><b>For no man buyeth their merchandise any more </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ τον γομον αυτων ουδεις αγοραζε ουκετ</span></span>). Reason enough for their sorrow over Rome's fall. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Γομος</span></span> is old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεμω</span></span> to be full) for a ship's cargo (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:3">Acts 21:3</span>) and then any merchandise (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:11">Revelation 18:11</span>). Galen, Pliny, Aristides tell of the vastness of the commerce and luxury of Rome, the world's chief market. Many of the items here are like those in the picture of the destruction of Tyre in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:26">Revelation 18:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:27">Revelation 18:27</span>. There are twenty-nine items singled out in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:12">Revelation 18:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:13">Revelation 18:13</span> of this merchandise or cargo (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γομον</span></span>), imports into the port of Rome. Only a few need any comment. </p>
