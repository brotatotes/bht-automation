<p><b> Of fine linen </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βυσσινου</span></span>). Genitive case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γομον</span></span>, as are all the items to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοκκινου</span></span>. Old adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βυσσος</span></span> (linen, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:19">Luke 16:19</span>), here a garment of linen, in N.T. only <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:12">Revelation 18:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:16">Revelation 18:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:8">Revelation 19:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:14">Revelation 19:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>Purple </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορφυρας</span></span>). Fabric colored with purple dye (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορφυρεος</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:4">Revelation 17:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:16">Revelation 18:16</span>), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:17">Mark 15:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:20">Mark 15:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:19">Luke 16:19</span>.</p>
<p><b>Silk </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σιρικου</span></span>). So the uncials here. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Το σηρικον</span></span> (the silken fabric) occurs in Plutarch, Strabo, Arrian, Lucian, only here in N.T. Probably from the name of the Indian or Chinese people (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο Σηρες</span></span>) from whom the fabric came after Alexander invaded India. Silk was a costly article among the Romans, and for women as a rule.</p>
<p><b>Scarlet </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοκκινου</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:4">Revelation 17:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:16">Revelation 18:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>All thyine wood </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παν ξυλον θυινον</span></span>). Now accusative again without <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γομον</span></span> dependence. An odoriferous North African citrus tree, prized for the colouring of the wood for dining-tables, like a peacock's tail or the stripes of a tiger or panther. Here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Of ivory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεφαντινον</span></span>). Old adjective (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεφας</span></span> elephant) agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκευος</span></span> (vessel), here only in N.T. Cf. Ahab's ivory palace (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ki+22:39">1 Kings 22:39</span>).</p>
<p><b>Of marble </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρμαρου</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρμαιρω</span></span>, to glisten), genitive after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκευος</span></span> (vessel), here only in N.T. </p>
