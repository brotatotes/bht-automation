<p><b> Cinnamon </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κινναμωμον</span></span>). Old word transliterated into English, here only in N.T. Of Phoenician origin (Herodotus) as to name and possibly from South China.</p>
<p><b>Spice </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμωμον</span></span>). A fragrant plant of India, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμομυμ</span></span>, for perfume.</p>
<p><b>Incense </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυμιαματα</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:8">Revelation 5:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:3">Revelation 8:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Ointment </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυρον</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:7">Matthew 26:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>Frankincense </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λιβανον</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:3">Revelation 8:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Fine flour </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σεμιδαλιν</span></span>). Old word for finest wheaten flour, here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Of horses </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιππων</span></span>). Here then is a return to the construction of the genitive after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γομον</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:12">Revelation 18:12</span>, though not used here, an anomalous genitive construction (Charles).</p>
<p><b>Of chariots </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρεδων</span></span>). A Gallic word for a vehicle with four wheels, here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Of slaves </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σοματων</span></span>). "Of bodies," treated as animals or implements, like the horses and the chariots (cf. <i>rickshaw</i> men in China). This use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωμα</span></span> for slave occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+34:29">Genesis 34:29</span>; Tob 10:11 (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωματα κα κτηνη</span></span>, slaves and cattle); II Macc. 8:11.</p>
<p><b>Souls of men </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψυχας ανθρωπων</span></span>). Deissmann (<i>Bible Studies</i>, p. 160) finds this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωμα</span></span> for slave in the Egyptian Delta. Return to the accusative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψυχας</span></span>. From <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="nu+31:35">Numbers 31:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ch+5:21">1 Chronicles 5:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+27:13">Ezekiel 27:13</span>. This addition is an explanation of the use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωματα</span></span> for slaves, "human live stock" (Swete), but slaves all the same. Perhaps <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span> here should be rendered "even," not "and": "bodies even souls of men." The slave merchant was called <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωματεμπορος</span></span> (body merchant). </p>
