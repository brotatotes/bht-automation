<p><b> The fruits </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η οπωρα</span></span>). The ripe autumn fruit (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+40:10">Jeremiah 40:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+40:12">Jeremiah 40:12</span>). Here only in N.T. Of uncertain etymology (possibly <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπος</span></span>, sap, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωρα</span></span>, hour, time for juicy sap). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:12">Jude 1:12</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δενδρα φθινοπωρινος</span></span> (autumn trees).</p>
<p><b>Which thy soul lusteth after </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σου της επιθυμιας της ψυχης</span></span>). "Of the lusting of thy soul."</p>
<p><b>Are gone from thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απηλθεν απο σου</span></span>). Prophetic aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απερχομα</span></span> with repetition of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>All things that were dainty and sumptuous </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντα τα λιπαρα κα τα λαμπρα</span></span>). "All the dainty and the gorgeous things." <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Λιπαρος</span></span> is from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λιπος</span></span> (grease) and so fat, about food (here only in N.T.), while <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμπρος</span></span> is bright and shining (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:2">James 2:2</span>), about clothing.</p>
<p><b>Are perished from thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απωλετο απο σου</span></span>). Prophetic second aorist middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολλυμ</span></span> (intransitive).</p>
<p><b>Shall find them no more at all </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκετ ου μη αυτα ευρησουσιν</span></span>). Doubled double negative with future active, as emphatic a negation as the Greek can make. </p>
