<p><b> Shipmaster </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυβερνητης</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυβερναω</span></span>, to steer), helmsman, sailing-master, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:11">Acts 27:11</span>. Subordinate to the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ναυκληρος</span></span> (supreme commander).</p>
<p><b>That saileth any whither </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο επ τοπον πλεων</span></span>). "The one sailing to a place." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:2">Acts 27:2</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους κατα την Ασιαν πλεοντας</span></span> (those sailing down along Asia). Nestle suggests <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποντον</span></span> (sea) here for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοπον</span></span> (place), but it makes sense as it is.</p>
<p><b>Mariners </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ναυτα</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ναυς</span></span>, ship), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:27">Acts 27:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:30">Acts 27:30</span>.</p>
<p><b>Gain their living by the sea </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την θαλασσαν εργαζοντα</span></span>). "Work the sea." This idiom is as old as Hesiod for sailors, fishermen, etc. See verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:10">Revelation 18:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:15">Revelation 18:15</span>. </p>
