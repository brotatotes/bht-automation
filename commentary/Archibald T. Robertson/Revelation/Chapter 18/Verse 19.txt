<p><b> They cast dust </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβαλον χουν</span></span>). Second aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαλλω</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χους</span></span> is old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χεω</span></span> to pour) for heap of earth, dust, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:11">Mark 6:11</span>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+27:30">Ezekiel 27:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:13">Luke 10:13</span>. This is the dirge of the sea-folk (cf. verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:10">Revelation 18:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:16">Revelation 18:16</span>).</p>
<p><b>By reason of her costliness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ της τιμιοτητος αυτης</span></span>). Occasionally in later literary Greek, though here only in N.T. and not in LXX. The same use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιμη</span></span> appears in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:7">1 Peter 2:7</span>. Common in the papyri as a title like "Your Honor" (Moulton and Milligan's <i>Vocabulary</i>). </p>
