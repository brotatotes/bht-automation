<p><b> Fallen, fallen is Babylon the great </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεσεν, επεσεν Βαβυλων η μεγαλη</span></span>). The very words of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:8">Revelation 14:8</span>: "Did fall, did fall Babylon the great." Prophetic aorists of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιπτω</span></span> repeated like a solemn dirge of the damned.</p>
<p><b>Is become </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγενετο</span></span>). Prophetic aorist middle.</p>
<p><b>A habitation of devils </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατοικητηριον</span></span>). Late word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατοικεω</span></span>, to dwell), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:22">Ephesians 2:22</span>. Devils should be demons, of course. So Isaiah prophesied of Babylon (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+12:21">Isaiah 12:21</span>) and also Jeremiah (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+50:39">Jeremiah 50:39</span>) and Zephaniah of Nineveh (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="zep+2:14">Zephaniah 2:14</span>). Both Babylon and Nineveh are ruins.</p>
<p><b>A hold of every unclean spirit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυλακη παντος πνευματος ακαθαρτου</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Φυλακη</span></span> is garrison or watch-tower as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="hab+2:1">Habakkuk 2:1</span>, rather than a prison (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:7">Revelation 20:7</span>).</p>
<p><b>A hold of every unclean and hateful bird </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυλακη παντος ορνεου ακαθαρτου κα μεμισημενου</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ορνεου</span></span> is old word for bird, in N.T. only <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:2">Revelation 18:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:17">Revelation 19:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:21">Revelation 19:21</span>. "The evil spirits, watching over fallen Rome like night-birds or harpies that wait for their prey, build their eyries in the broken towers which rise from the ashes of the city" (Swete). Long ago true of Babylon and Nineveh, some day to be true of Rome. </p>
