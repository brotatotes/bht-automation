<p><b> A strong angel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις αγγελος ισχυρος</span></span>). Here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> = a, just an indefinite article, not "one" as a numeral.</p>
<p><b>Took up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηρεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιρω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>As it were a great millstone </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως μυλινον μεγαν</span></span>). Late adjective, in inscriptions, here only in N.T., made of millstone (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυλος</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:6">Matthew 18:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:22">Revelation 18:22</span>), while <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυλικος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:2">Luke 17:2</span>) means belonging to a mill. This is not a small millstone turned by women (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:41">Matthew 24:41</span>), but one requiring an ass to turn it (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:42">Mark 9:42</span>), and so "a great" one.</p>
<p><b>Cast </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβαλεν</span></span>). Second aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαλλω</span></span>, to hurl.</p>
<p><b>With a mighty fall </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορμηματ</span></span>). Instrumental case (manner) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορμημα</span></span>, a rush, old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορμαω</span></span>, to rush (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:32">Matthew 8:32</span>), here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Shall be cast down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλεθησετα</span></span>). Future (first) passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαλλω</span></span>, the same verb (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβαλεν</span></span>), effective punctiliar future. Like a boulder hurled into the sea.</p>
<p><b>Shall be found no more at all </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη ευρεθη ετ</span></span>). Double negative with first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρισκω</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:6">Revelation 9:6</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη</span></span> with the active voice of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρισκω</span></span>. Already the old Babylon was a desert waste (Strabo, XVI. 1073). </p>
