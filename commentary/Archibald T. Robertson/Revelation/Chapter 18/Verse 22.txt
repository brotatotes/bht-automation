<p><b> The voice </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωνη</span></span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+26:13">Ezekiel 26:13</span>. Or "sound" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:8">1 Corinthians 14:8</span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαλπιγξ</span></span> (trumpet). For this song of judgment see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+25:10">Jeremiah 25:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Of harpers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κιθαρωιδων</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κιθαρα</span></span>, harp, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωιδος</span></span>, singer) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:2">Revelation 14:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>Of minstrels </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μουσικων</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μουσα</span></span>, music), here only in N.T., one playing on musical instruments.</p>
<p><b>Of flute-players </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυλητων</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυλεω</span></span>, to play on a flute, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:17">Matthew 11:17</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυλος</span></span>, flute, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:7">1 Corinthians 14:7</span>), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:23">Matthew 9:23</span>.</p>
<p><b>Of trumpeters </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαλπιστων</span></span>). Late form for the earlier <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαλπιγκτης</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαλπιζω</span></span>), here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Shall be heard no more at all </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη ακουσθη</span></span>). First aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακουω</span></span> with the double negative as below, with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωνη μυλου</span></span> (sound of the millstone), and as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:21">Revelation 18:21</span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου με ευρεθη</span></span> and again with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πας τεχνιτης</span></span> (craftsman). This old word is from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεχνη</span></span>, art, as here in some MSS. ("of whatsoever craft," <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πασης τεχνης</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Τεχνιτης</span></span> occurs also in this sense in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:24">Acts 19:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:38">Acts 19:38</span>; and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:10">Hebrews 11:10</span> of God as the Architect. There is power in this four-fold sonorous repetition of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη</span></span> and the subjunctive with two more examples in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:23">Revelation 18:23</span>. </p>
