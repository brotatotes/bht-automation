<p><b> In her </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αυτη</span></span>). In Rome.</p>
<p><b>Was found </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρεθη</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρισκω</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:6">Revelation 16:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:6">Revelation 17:6</span> for the blood already shed by Rome. Rome "butchered to make a Roman holiday" (Dill, <i>Roman Society</i>, p. 242) not merely gladiators, but prophets and saints from Nero's massacre A.D. 64 to Domitian and beyond.</p>
<p><b>Of all that have been slain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντων των εσφαγμενων</span></span>). Perfect passive articular participle genitive plural of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σφαζω</span></span>, the verb used of the Lamb slain (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:9">Revelation 5:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:12">Revelation 5:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:8">Revelation 13:8</span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:35">Matthew 23:35</span> about Jerusalem. </p>
