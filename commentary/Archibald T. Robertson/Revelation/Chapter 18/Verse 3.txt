<p><b> By </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span>). "As a result of." Some MSS. omit "of the wine" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του οινου</span></span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:10">Revelation 14:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:10">Revelation 16:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Have fallen </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπτωκαν</span></span>). Perfect active third personal of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιπτω</span></span> for usual <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπτωκασ</span></span>. Some MSS. read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπωκαν</span></span> (have drunk), from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πινω</span></span> like the metaphor in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:8">Revelation 14:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:10">Revelation 14:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:19">Revelation 16:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:2">Revelation 17:2</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:2">Revelation 17:2</span> for the same charge about the kings of the earth.</p>
<p><b>The merchants of the earth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο εμπορο της γης</span></span>). Old word for one on a journey for trade (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν, πορος</span></span>), like drummers, in N.T. only <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:45">Matthew 13:45</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:3">Revelation 18:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:11">Revelation 18:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:15">Revelation 18:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:23">Revelation 18:23</span>. Like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμποριον</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:16">John 2:16</span>) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμπορευομα</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:13">James 4:13</span>).</p>
<p><b>Waxed rich </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επλουτησαν</span></span>). First ingressive aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλουτεω</span></span>, to be rich (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:17">Revelation 3:17</span>). Here alone in the N.T. do we catch a glimpse of the vast traffic between east and west that made Rome rich.</p>
<p><b>Of her wantonness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του στρηνους αυτης</span></span>). Late word for arrogance, luxury, here alone in N.T. See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρηνιαω</span></span> in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:7">Revelation 18:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:9">Revelation 18:9</span>, to live wantonly. </p>
