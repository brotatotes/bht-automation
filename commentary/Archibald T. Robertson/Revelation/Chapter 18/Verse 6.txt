<p><b> Render as she rendered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποδοτε ως απεδωκεν</span></span>). Second aorist (effective) active imperative and first aorist (effective) active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποδιδωμ</span></span>, old and common verb for requital, to give back, the <i>lex talionis</i> which is in the O.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+50:15">Jeremiah 50:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+50:29">Jeremiah 50:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+51:24">Jeremiah 51:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+51:56">Jeremiah 51:56</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+137:8">Psalms 137:8</span>), and in the N.T. also (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:2">Matthew 7:2</span>). Here the reference is to persecutions by Rome, particularly the martyrdom of the saints (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:24">Revelation 18:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:2">Revelation 19:2</span>).</p>
<p><b>Double the double </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διπλωσατε τα διπλα</span></span>). First aorist imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διπλοω</span></span>, old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διπλοος</span></span>, double, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:15">Matthew 23:15</span>), here only in N.T. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Διπλα</span></span> is simply the neuter plural accusative (cognate) contract form for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διπλοα</span></span> (not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διπλω</span></span>). Requite here in double measure, a full requital (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+22:4">Exodus 22:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+22:7">Exodus 22:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+22:9">Exodus 22:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+40:2">Isaiah 40:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+16:18">Jeremiah 16:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+17:18">Jeremiah 17:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="zec+9:12">Zechariah 9:12</span>). The double recompense was according to the Levitical law.</p>
<p><b>Which she mingled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ω εκερασεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεραννυμ</span></span>. The relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ω</span></span> is attracted to the locative case of its antecedent <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποτηριω</span></span> (cup), for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:8">Revelation 14:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:10">Revelation 14:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:4">Revelation 17:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:3">Revelation 18:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Mingle unto her double </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κερασατε αυτη διπλουν</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of the same verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεραννυμ</span></span>, with the same idea of double punishment. </p>
