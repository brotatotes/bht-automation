<p><b> How much soever </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οσα</span></span>). Indefinite quantitative relative pronoun <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οσος</span></span> in the accusative (cognate) neuter plural object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδοξασεν</span></span> (first aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξαζω</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Herself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτην</span></span>). Reflexive pronoun, accusative also with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδοξασεν</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Waxed wanton </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστρηνιασεν</span></span>). First aorist (ingressive) active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρηνιαω</span></span> (to live luxuriously), verb in late comedy instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρυφαω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:5">James 5:5</span>), from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρηνος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:3">Revelation 18:3</span>), only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>So much give her of torment and mourning </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοσουτον δοτε αυτη βασανισμον κα πενθος</span></span>). Second aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>, to give. The correlative pronoun <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοσουτον</span></span> is masculine singular accusative, agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασανισμον</span></span>, for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:5">Revelation 9:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:11">Revelation 14:11</span>, and is understood with the neuter word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πενθος</span></span> (mourning), in N.T. only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:9">James 4:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:7">Revelation 18:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:4">Revelation 21:4</span> (kin to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παθοσ, πενομα</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>I sit a queen </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθημα βασιλισσα</span></span>). Predicate nominative for the old form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασιλεια</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασιλις</span></span>), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:42">Matthew 12:42</span>. Babylon and Tyre had preceded Rome in such boasting (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+47:7-9">Isaiah 47:7-9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+27:3">Ezekiel 27:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+28:2">Ezekiel 28:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="zep+2:15">Zephaniah 2:15</span>).</p>
<p><b>And am no widow </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα χηρα ουκ ειμ</span></span>). Feminine of the adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χηρος</span></span> (barren), old word (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:40">Mark 12:40</span>).</p>
<p><b>Shall in no wise see mourning </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πενθος ου μη ιδω</span></span>). Confident boast of security with emphatic position of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πενθος</span></span> (see above) and double negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη</span></span> with the second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span> (defective verb). </p>
