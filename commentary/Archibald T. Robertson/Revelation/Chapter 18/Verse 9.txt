<p><b> Shall weep </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλαυσουσιν</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλαιω</span></span>, middle <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλαυσοντα</span></span> in Attic, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:20">John 16:20</span>.</p>
<p><b>And wail over her </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα κοψοντα επ' αυτην</span></span>). Future direct middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοπτω</span></span>, old verb, to beat, to cut, middle to beat oneself (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:7">Revelation 1:7</span>). For combination with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλαιω</span></span> as here see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:52">Luke 8:52</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:2">Revelation 17:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:3">Revelation 18:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:7">Revelation 18:7</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο πορνευσαντες κα στρηνιασαντες</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>When they look upon </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν βλεπωσιν</span></span>). Indefinite temporal clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν</span></span> and the present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλεπω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>The smoke of her burning </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον καπνον της πυρωσεως αυτης</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πυρωσις</span></span> is an old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυροω</span></span> to burn), in N.T. only <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:12">1 Peter 4:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:9">Revelation 18:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:18">Revelation 18:18</span>. See verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:8">Revelation 18:8</span> for other plagues on Rome, but fire seems to be the worst (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:16">Revelation 17:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:8">Revelation 18:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:9">Revelation 18:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:17">Revelation 18:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:3">Revelation 19:3</span>). </p>
