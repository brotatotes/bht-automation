<p><b> To worship him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσκυνησα αυτω</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of purpose. John either felt that the angel represented God or he was beside himself with excitement over the glorious consummation. He was tempted to worship an angel (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:18">Colossians 2:18</span>).</p>
<p><b>See thou do it not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορα μη</span></span>). Repeated in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:9">Revelation 22:9</span>. Here there is no verb after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> (ellipse of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιησηις τουτο</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:44">Mark 1:44</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:15">1 Thessalonians 5:15</span>), the aorist subjunctive of negative purpose with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορα</span></span> (present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span>), a common enough idiom.</p>
<p><b>Fellow-servant </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνδουλος</span></span>). The angel refuses worship from John on this ground. All Christians are <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνδουλο</span></span> (fellow-servants) as Christ taught (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:28">Matthew 18:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:49">Matthew 24:49</span>) and as Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:7">Colossians 1:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+4:7">Colossians 4:7</span>) and John (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:11">Revelation 6:11</span>) taught. Angels are God's servants also (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:4-14">Hebrews 1:4-14</span>). For "the testimony of Jesus see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:2">Revelation 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:9">Revelation 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:9">Revelation 6:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:17">Revelation 12:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:4">Revelation 22:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>Worship God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω θεω προσκυνησον</span></span>). And Christ, who is the Son of God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:13">Revelation 5:13</span>).</p>
<p><b>The spirit of prophecy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το πνευμα της προφητειας</span></span>). Explanatory use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γαρ</span></span> (for) here as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:8">Revelation 19:8</span>. The possession of the prophetic spirit shows itself in witness to Jesus. In illustration see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:10">Mark 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:16">Matthew 3:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:21">Luke 3:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:51">John 1:51</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:1">Revelation 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:1">Revelation 10:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:19">Revelation 11:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:17">Revelation 14:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:5">Revelation 15:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:1">Revelation 18:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:1">Revelation 19:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:7-9">Revelation 19:7-9</span>. </p>
