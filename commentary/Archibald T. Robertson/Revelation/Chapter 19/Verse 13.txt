<p><b> Arrayed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιβεβλημενος</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιβαλλω</span></span>, to clothe, often in this book.</p>
<p><b>In a garment </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιματιον</span></span>). Accusative case after the passive participle <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιβεβλημενος</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Sprinkled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρεραντισμενον</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ραντιζω</span></span>, in the predicate accusative case agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιματιον</span></span>. A Q here read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βεβαμμενον</span></span> (perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαπτω</span></span>, to dip). Probably <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρεραντισμενον</span></span> (sprinkled) is correct, because the picture comes from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+63:3">Isaiah 63:3</span>, where Aquila and Symmachus use <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ραντιζω</span></span>. The use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βεβαμμενον</span></span> (dipped) is a bolder figure and Charles considers it correct. In either case it is the blood of Christ's enemies with which his raiment (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιματιον</span></span>, perhaps a <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χλαμυς</span></span> <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:28">Matthew 27:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:31">Matthew 27:31</span>) is sprinkled or dipped as the case may be, not his own blood on Calvary (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:5">Revelation 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:9">Revelation 5:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:14">Revelation 7:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:11">Revelation 12:11</span>), but proleptically and prophetically the blood of Christ's enemies. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hαιματ</span></span> can be either locative case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βεβαμμενον</span></span> (dipped in blood) or instrumental with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρεραντισμενον</span></span> (sprinkled with blood).</p>
<p><b>The Word of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο Λογος του θεου</span></span>). Some scholars hold this addition inconsistent with verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:12">Revelation 19:12</span>, but it may be merely the explanation of the secret name or still another name besides that known only to himself. The personal use of the Logos applied to Christ occurs only in the Johannine writings unless that is the idea in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:12">Hebrews 4:12</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:1">John 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:14">John 1:14</span> it is merely <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο Λογος</span></span> (the Word), in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:1">1 John 1:1</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο Λογος της ζωης</span></span> (the Word of Life), while here it is <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο Λογος του θεου</span></span> (the Word of God), one of the strongest arguments for identity of authorship. The idiom here is one common in Luke and Paul for the teaching of Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:1">Luke 5:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:11">Luke 8:11</span>, etc.; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:36">1 Corinthians 14:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:17">2 Corinthians 2:17</span>, etc.). Jesus is himself the final and perfect revelation of God to men (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:1">Hebrews 1:1</span>). </p>
