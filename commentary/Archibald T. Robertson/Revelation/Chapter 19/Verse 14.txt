<p><b> The armies which are in heaven </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα στρατευματα τα εν τω ουρανω</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:7">Revelation 12:7</span> for Michael and angels warring with the dragon, and also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:53">Matthew 26:53</span> for the angels at Christ's call, not to say <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:6">Hebrews 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:14">Hebrews 1:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:41">Matthew 13:41</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:11">Revelation 5:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>Followed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκολουθε</span></span>). Imperfect active and singular (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στρατευματα</span></span>, neuter plural) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακολουθεω</span></span>, graphic picture of the celestial Warrior with his angelic hosts "upon white horses" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφ' ιπποις λευκοις</span></span>) like the Leader and, like him "clothed in fine linen white and pure" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδεδυμενο βυσσινον λευκον καθαρον</span></span>) like the Leader again (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:8">Revelation 19:8</span>). Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδεδυμενο</span></span> here as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:13">Revelation 1:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:6">Revelation 15:6</span>. </p>
