<p><b> A sharp sword </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρομφαια οξεια</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:16">Revelation 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:12">Revelation 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:15">Revelation 2:15</span>.</p>
<p><b>That he should smite </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα παταξη</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πατασσω</span></span>, old verb already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:6">Revelation 11:6</span> and like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+11:4">Isaiah 11:4</span>, a figure here for forensic and judicial condemnation.</p>
<p><b>And he shall rule them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα αυτος ποιμανε</span></span>). Emphatic use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτος</span></span> twice (he himself). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιμαινω</span></span>, to shepherd as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:27">Revelation 2:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:5">Revelation 12:5</span> "with a rod of iron" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ραβδω σιδηρα</span></span>) as there. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:25">1 Peter 2:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:20">Hebrews 13:20</span> for Christ as Shepherd.</p>
<p><b>And he treadeth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα αυτος πατε</span></span>). Change to present tense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πατεω</span></span>, to tread (here transitive), with solemn repetition of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα αυτος</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>The winepress of the fierceness of the wrath of Almighty God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την ληνον του οινου του θυμου της οργης του θεου του παντοκρατορος</span></span>). Literally, "the winepress of the wine of the wrath of the anger of God the Almighty" (four genitives dependent on one another and on <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ληνον</span></span>). These images are here combined from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:8">Revelation 14:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:10">Revelation 14:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:19">Revelation 14:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:19">Revelation 16:19</span>. The fact is already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:13">Revelation 19:13</span> after <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+63:1">Isaiah 63:1</span>. </p>
