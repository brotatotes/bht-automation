<p><b> Gathered together </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνηγμενα</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συναγω</span></span>. In battle array.</p>
<p><b>To make war against </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιησα πολεμον μετα</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span>, to express purpose. See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολεμεω μετα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:7">Revelation 12:7</span> and the use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συναγω εις πολεμον</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:14">Revelation 16:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:8">Revelation 20:8</span>. The beast (for his army see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:13">Revelation 16:13</span>) led a league of ten kings against Babylon in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:16">Revelation 17:16</span>, but with the purpose also of fighting the Lamb (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:14">Revelation 17:14</span>). </p>
