<p><b> A second time </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δευτερον</span></span>). Adverbial accusative, a heavenly encore.</p>
<p><b>They say </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειρηκαν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειπον</span></span>. "They have said," not an "aoristic" perfect for "they say," but vivid dramatic perfect as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:7">Revelation 5:7</span> and the form in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-αν</span></span> instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-ασιν</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:3">Revelation 18:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:6">Revelation 21:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Goeth up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναβαινε</span></span>). Linear present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναβαινω</span></span>, "keeps on going up," "a last touch to the description already given (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:21">Revelation 18:21</span>) of Babylon's utter collapse" (Swete). The smoke of the city's ruin (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:11">Revelation 14:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:8">Revelation 18:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:18">Revelation 18:18</span>) instead of incense (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:4">Revelation 8:4</span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+34:9">Isaiah 34:9</span>. </p>
