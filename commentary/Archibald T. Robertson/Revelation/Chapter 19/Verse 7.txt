<p><b> Let us rejoice and be exceeding glad </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαιρωμεν κα αγαλλιωμεν</span></span>). Present active subjunctive (volitive) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαιρω</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαλλιαω</span></span> (elsewhere in N.T. in the middle except <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:47">Luke 1:47</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:8">1 Peter 1:8</span>). For both verbs together see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:12">Matthew 5:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>Let us give </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δωμεν</span></span>). Second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>, but A reads <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δωσομεν</span></span> (future active) and P <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δωσωμεν</span></span>. If the future indicative is read, the tone is changed from exhortation to declaration (we shall give glory unto him).</p>
<p><b>The marriage of the Lamb </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο γαμος του αρνιου</span></span>). In the O.T. God is the Bridegroom of Israel (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ho+2:16">Hosea 2:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+54:6">Isaiah 54:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+16:7">Ezekiel 16:7</span>). In the N.T. Christ is the Bridegroom of the Kingdom (the universal spiritual church as seen by Paul, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:2">2 Corinthians 11:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:25">Ephesians 5:25</span>, and by John in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:20">Revelation 3:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:7">Revelation 19:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:9">Revelation 19:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:2">Revelation 21:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:9">Revelation 21:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:17">Revelation 22:17</span>. In the Gospels Christ appears as the Bridegroom (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:19">Mark 2:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:15">Matthew 9:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:34">Luke 5:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:29">John 3:29</span>). The figure of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γαμος</span></span> occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:2-14">Matthew 22:2-14</span>. Three metaphors of women appear in the Apocalypse (the Mother in chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:12">Revelation 19:12</span>, the Harlot in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:17-19">Revelation 19:17-19</span>, and the Bride of Christ here to the end). "The first and third present the Church under two different aspects of her life, while the second answers to her great rival and enemy" (Swete).</p>
<p><b>Is come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηλθεν</span></span>). Prophetic aorist, come at last.</p>
<p><b>Made herself ready </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ητοιμασεν εαυτην</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετοιμαζω</span></span> and the reflexive pronoun. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:2">Revelation 22:2</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ητοιμασμενην ως νυμφην</span></span> (prepared as a bride). There is something for her to do (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:3">1 John 3:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jdg+1:21">Judges 1:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:1">2 Corinthians 7:1</span>), but the chief preparation is the act of Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:25">Ephesians 5:25</span>). </p>
