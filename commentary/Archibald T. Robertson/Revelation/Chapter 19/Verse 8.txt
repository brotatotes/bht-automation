<p><b> That she should array herself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα περιβαλητα</span></span>). Sub-final object clause subject of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδοθη</span></span> (was given to her) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the second aorist middle (direct) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιβαλλω</span></span> to fling around. This bridal dress is a gift from Christ. This form, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδοθη</span></span> (it was given), occurs some 20 times in this book.</p>
<p><b>In fine linen, bright and pure </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βυσσινον λαμπρον καθαρον</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:14">Revelation 19:14</span> for the same raiment on those accompanying "The Word of God" and for the seven angels in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:6">Revelation 15:6</span>. See by contrast the garments of the harlot (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:4">Revelation 17:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:16">Revelation 18:16</span>). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βυσσινον</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:16">Revelation 18:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>The righteous acts of the saints </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα δικαιωματα των αγιων</span></span>). This is the explanation (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γαρ</span></span>) of the bridal dress and explains why there is work for the Bride as well as for Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:12">Philippians 2:12</span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:4">Revelation 15:4</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιωμα</span></span> (also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:18">Romans 5:18</span>). </p>
