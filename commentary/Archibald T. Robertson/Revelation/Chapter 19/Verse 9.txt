<p><b> Write </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Γραψον</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γραφω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:11">Revelation 1:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:13">Revelation 14:13</span>. The speaker may be the angel guide of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:1">Revelation 17:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>It is another beatitude </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακαριο</span></span>, Blessed) like that in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:13">Revelation 14:13</span> (fourth of the seven in the book).</p>
<p><b>They which are bidden </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο κεκλημενο</span></span>). Articular perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλεω</span></span>, like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:3">Matthew 22:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:17">Luke 14:17</span>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:14">Revelation 17:14</span>. This beatitude reminds us of that in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:15">Luke 14:15</span>. (Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:11">Matthew 8:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:29">Matthew 26:29</span>.)</p>
<p><b>These are true words of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hουτο ο λογο αληθινο του θεου εισιν</span></span>). Undoubtedly, but one should bear in mind that apocalyptic symbolism "has its own methods and laws of interpretation, and by these the student must be guided" (Swete). </p>
