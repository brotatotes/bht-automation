<p><b> I gave her time </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδωκα αυτη χρονον</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>, allusion to a definite visit or message of warning to this woman.</p>
<p><b>That she should repent </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μετανοηση</span></span>). Sub-final use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> with first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετανοεω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>And she willeth not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ου θελε</span></span>). "And she is not willing." Blunt and final like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:37">Matthew 23:37</span>.</p>
<p><b>To repent of </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετανοησα εκ</span></span>). First aorist (ingressive) active infinitive with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span>, "to make a change out of," the usual construction with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετανοεω</span></span> in this book (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:22">Revelation 2:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:20">Revelation 9:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:11">Revelation 16:11</span>), with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:22">Acts 8:22</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πορνεια</span></span> (fornication) here, but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μοιχευω</span></span> (to commit adultery) in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:22">Revelation 2:22</span>. </p>
