<p><b> I will kill with death </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκτενω εν θανατω</span></span>). Future (volitive) active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκτεινω</span></span> with the tautological (cognate) <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν θανατω</span></span> (in the sense of pestilence) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+33:27">Ezekiel 33:27</span>.</p>
<p><b>Her children </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα τεκνα αυτης</span></span>). Either her actual children, like the fate of Ahab's sons (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ki+10:7">2 Kings 10:7</span>) or "her spiritual progeny" (Swete) who have completely accepted her Nicolaitan practices.</p>
<p><b>Shall know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνωσοντα</span></span>). Future (ingressive punctiliar) middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span>, "shall come to know." "The doom of the offenders was to be known as widely as the scandal had been" (Charles).</p>
<p><b>Searcheth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εραυνων</span></span>). Present active articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εραυναω</span></span>, to follow up, to track out, late form for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερευναω</span></span>, from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+17:10">Jeremiah 17:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Reins </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νεφρους</span></span>). Old word for kidneys, here only in N.T., quoted also with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καρδιας</span></span> from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+17:10">Jeremiah 17:10</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:17">Revelation 22:17</span> for the reward of punishment. </p>
