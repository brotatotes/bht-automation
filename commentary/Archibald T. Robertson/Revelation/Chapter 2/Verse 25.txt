<p><b> Howbeit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλην</span></span>). Common after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ αλλο</span></span> as a preposition with the ablative (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:32">Mark 12:32</span>), but here a conjunction as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:18">Philippians 1:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>Hold fast </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρατησατε</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρατεω</span></span>, either ingressive (get a grip on) or constative (hold on as a single decisive effort). See present imperative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρατε</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:11">Revelation 3:11</span> (keep on holding).</p>
<p><b>Till I come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχρ ου αν ηξω</span></span>). Indefinite temporal clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχρ ου</span></span> (until which time) with modal <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span> and either the future active indicative or the first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκω</span></span> (usual idiom with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχρ</span></span> in Revelation as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:3">Revelation 7:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:8">Revelation 15:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:3">Revelation 20:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:5">Revelation 20:5</span>). </p>
