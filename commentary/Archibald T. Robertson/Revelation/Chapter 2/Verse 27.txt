<p><b> He shall rule </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιμανε</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιμαινω</span></span>, to shepherd (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιμην</span></span>, shepherd), also from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+2:8">Psalms 2:8</span>. See again <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:17">Revelation 7:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:5">Revelation 12:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:15">Revelation 19:15</span>.</p>
<p><b>With a rod of iron </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ραβδω σιδηρα</span></span>). Continuing the quotation. Instrumental use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ραβδος</span></span> (feminine) is the royal sceptre and indicates rigorous rule.</p>
<p><b>The vessels of the potter </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα σκευη τα κεραμικα</span></span>). Old adjective, belonging to a potter (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεραμευσ, κεραμος</span></span>), here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Are broken to shivers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συντριβετα</span></span>). Present passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συντριβω</span></span>, old verb, to rub together, to break in pieces (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:3">Mark 14:3</span>). </p>
