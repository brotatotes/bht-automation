<p><b> Thou hast </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχεις</span></span>). Continued possession of patience.</p>
<p><b>Didst bear </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβαστασας</span></span>). First aorist indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασταζω</span></span>, repeated reference to the crisis in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:2">Revelation 2:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>And hast not grown weary </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ου κεκοπιακες</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοπιαω</span></span>, old verb, to grow weary (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:28">Matthew 6:28</span>), play on the word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοπος</span></span>, late form in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-ες</span></span>, for the regular <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-ας</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λελυκας</span></span>). like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφηκες</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:4">Revelation 2:4</span>) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπτωκες</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:5">Revelation 2:5</span>). "Tired in loyalty, not of it. The Ephesian church can bear anything except the presence of impostors in her membership" (Moffatt). </p>
