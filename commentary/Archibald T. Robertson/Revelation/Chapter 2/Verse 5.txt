<p><b> Remember </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μνημονευε</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μνημονευω</span></span>, "continue mindful" (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μνημων</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Thou art fallen </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπτωκες</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιπτω</span></span>, state of completion. Down in the valley, look up to the cliff where pure love is and whence thou hast fallen down.</p>
<p><b>And repent </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα μετανοησον</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετανοεω</span></span>, urgent appeal for instant change of attitude and conduct before it is too late.</p>
<p><b>And do </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ποιησον</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span>, "Do at once."</p>
<p><b>The first works </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα πρωτα εργα</span></span>). Including the first love (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:20">Acts 19:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:37">Acts 20:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:3">Ephesians 1:3</span>) which has now grown cold (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:12">Matthew 24:12</span>).</p>
<p><b>Or else </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε δε μη</span></span>). Elliptical condition, the verb not expressed (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετανοεις</span></span>), a common idiom, seen again in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:16">Revelation 2:16</span>, the condition expressed in full by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν μη</span></span> in this verse and verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:22">Revelation 2:22</span>.</p>
<p><b>I come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομα</span></span>). Futuristic present middle (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:2">John 14:2</span>).</p>
<p><b>To thee </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σο</span></span>). Dative, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:16">Revelation 2:16</span> also.</p>
<p><b>Will move </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κινησω</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κινεω</span></span>. In Ignatius' Epistle to Ephesus it appears that the church heeded this warning.</p>
<p><b>Except thou repent </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν μη μετανοησηις</span></span>). Condition of third class with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν μη</span></span> instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε μη</span></span> above, with the first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετανοεω</span></span>. </p>
