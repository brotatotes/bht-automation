<p><b> He that hath an ear </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο εχων ους</span></span>). An individualizing note calling on each of the hearers (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:3">Revelation 1:3</span>) to listen (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:7">Revelation 2:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:11">Revelation 2:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:17">Revelation 2:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:28">Revelation 2:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:3">Revelation 3:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:6">Revelation 3:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:13">Revelation 3:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:22">Revelation 3:22</span>) and a reminiscence of the words of Jesus in the Synoptics (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:15">Matthew 11:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:9">Matthew 13:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:43">Matthew 13:43</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:9">Mark 4:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:23">Mark 4:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:8">Luke 8:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:35">Luke 14:35</span>), but not in John's Gospel.</p>
<p><b>The spirit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το πνευμα</span></span>). The Holy Spirit as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:13">Revelation 14:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:17">Revelation 22:17</span>. Both Christ and the Holy Spirit deliver this message. "The Spirit of Christ in the prophet is the interpreter of Christ's voice" (Swete).</p>
<p><b>To him that overcometh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω νικωντ</span></span>). Dative of the present (continuous victory) active articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νικαω</span></span>, a common Johannine verb (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:33">John 16:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:13">1 John 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:4">1 John 4:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:4">1 John 5:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:7">Revelation 2:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:11">Revelation 2:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:17">Revelation 2:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:26">Revelation 2:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:5">Revelation 3:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:12">Revelation 3:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:21">Revelation 3:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:5">Revelation 5:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:11">Revelation 12:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:2">Revelation 15:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:14">Revelation 17:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:7">Revelation 21:7</span>). Faith is dominant in Paul, victory in John, faith is victory (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:4">1 John 5:4</span>). So in each promise to these churches.</p>
<p><b>I will give </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δωσω</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:10">Revelation 2:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:17">Revelation 2:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:23">Revelation 2:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:26">Revelation 2:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:28">Revelation 2:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:8">Revelation 3:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:21">Revelation 3:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:4">Revelation 6:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:3">Revelation 11:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:6">Revelation 21:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>To eat </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φαγειν</span></span>). Second aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσθιω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Of the tree of life </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ του ξυλου της ζωης</span></span>). Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span> with the ablative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φαγειν</span></span>, like our "eat of" (from or part of). From <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+2:9">Genesis 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+3:22">Genesis 3:22</span>. Again in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:2">Revelation 22:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:14">Revelation 22:14</span> as here for immortality. This tree is now in the Garden of God. For the water of life see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:6">Revelation 21:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:17">Revelation 22:17</span> (Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:10">John 4:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:13">John 4:13</span>).</p>
<p><b>Which </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span>). The <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ξυλον</span></span> (tree).</p>
<p><b>In the Paradise of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω παραδεισω του θεου</span></span>). Persian word, for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:43">Luke 23:43</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:4">2 Corinthians 12:4</span>. The abode of God and the home of the redeemed with Christ, not a mere intermediate state. It was originally a garden of delight and finally heaven itself (Trench), as here. </p>
