<p><b> Were cast </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβληθησαν</span></span>). As the devil (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:10">Revelation 20:10</span>) followed the two beasts (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:20">Revelation 19:20</span>) into the same dread lake of fire. Death is personified and is disposed of, "the last enemy" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:26">1 Corinthians 15:26</span>) and Paul sings the paean of victory over death (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:54">1 Corinthians 15:54</span>, from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ho+13:14">Hosea 13:14</span>). Hades has no more terrors, for the saints are in heaven. There is no more fear of death (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:15">Hebrews 2:15</span>), for death is no more (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:4">Revelation 21:4</span>). The second death (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:11">Revelation 2:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:6">Revelation 20:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:8">Revelation 21:8</span>) is here identified as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:8">Revelation 21:8</span> with the lake of fire. </p>
