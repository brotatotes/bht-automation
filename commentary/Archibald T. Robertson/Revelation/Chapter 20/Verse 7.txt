<p><b> When are finished </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν τελεσθη</span></span>). Indefinite future temporal clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν</span></span> and the first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελεω</span></span>, "whenever are finished."</p>
<p><b>Shall be loosed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυθησετα</span></span>). Future passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυω</span></span>, no longer bound as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:2">Revelation 20:2</span>. He uses the future as a prophet in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:7">Revelation 20:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:8">Revelation 20:8</span>, but in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:9">Revelation 20:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:10">Revelation 20:10</span> he uses the aorist as a seer.</p>
<p><b>Out of his prison </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ της φυλακης αυτου</span></span>). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυλακη</span></span> in this sense see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:10">Revelation 2:10</span>. Out of the abyss of verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:2">Revelation 20:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:3">Revelation 20:3</span>. </p>
