<p><b> They went up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεβησαν</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναβαινω</span></span>, a return to the manner of the seer as in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:4">Revelation 20:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:5">Revelation 20:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>Over the breadth of the earth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ το πλατος της γης</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πλατος</span></span> is old word, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:16">Revelation 21:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:18">Ephesians 3:18</span>. The hosts of Satan spread over the earth.</p>
<p><b>Compassed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκυκλευσαν</span></span>). First aorist (prophetic) active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυκλευω</span></span>, to encircle, late verb (Strabo) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυκλος</span></span> (circle), in N.T. only here and margin in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:24">John 10:24</span> (for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκυκλωσαν</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυκλοω</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>The camp of the saints </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την παρεμβολην των αγιων</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Παρεμβολη</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα, εν, βαλλω</span></span>) is common late word for military camp, in LXX for the Israelites in the desert (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+29:14">Exodus 29:14</span>, etc.), in N.T. for Roman barracks (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:34">Acts 24:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:37">Acts 24:37</span>) and for an army in line of battle (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:34">Hebrews 11:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:9">Revelation 20:9</span>).</p>
<p><b>The beloved city </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την πολιν την ηγαπημενην</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπαω</span></span>, "the city the beloved." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+78:68">Psalms 78:68</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+87:2">Psalms 87:2</span> for Jerusalem so described. So Charles takes it here, but Swete holds it to be "the Church the New Zion" that is meant.</p>
<p><b>And fire came down out of heaven </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα κατεβη πυρ εκ του ουρανου</span></span>). Second aorist (prophetic) active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταβαινω</span></span>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+19:24">Genesis 19:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+39:6">Genesis 39:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+38:22">Ezekiel 38:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ki+1:10">2 Kings 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ki+1:12">2 Kings 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:54">Luke 9:54</span> (about John).</p>
<p><b>Devoured them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεφαγεν αυτους</span></span>). Second aorist (prophetic) active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεσθιω</span></span>, to eat up (down). Vivid climax to this last great battle with Satan. </p>
