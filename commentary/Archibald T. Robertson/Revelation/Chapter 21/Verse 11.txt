<p><b> Having the glory of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχουσαν την δοξαν του θεου</span></span>). Syntactically this clause goes with verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:10">Revelation 21:10</span>, the feminine accusative singular participle <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχουσαν</span></span> agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολιν</span></span>, the radiance of the dazzling splendour of God as seen in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+60:1">Isaiah 60:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+43:5">Ezekiel 43:5</span>. God's very presence is in the Holy City (the Bride).</p>
<p><b>Light </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωστηρ</span></span>). "Luminary," late word (in LXX, papyri), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:15">Philippians 2:15</span>. Christ is the light (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φως</span></span>) of the world (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:12">John 8:12</span>) and so are Christians (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:14">Matthew 5:14</span>) who have received the illumination (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωτισμος</span></span>) of God in the face of Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:6">2 Corinthians 4:6</span>) and who radiate it to men (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:15">Philippians 2:15</span>). See both words in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+1:3">Genesis 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+1:14">Genesis 1:14</span>. "The 'luminary' of the Holy City is her witness to Christ" (Swete).</p>
<p><b>Like unto a stone most precious </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοιος λιθω τιμιωτατω</span></span>). Associative instrumental case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοιος</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Τιμιωτατω</span></span> is the elative superlative.</p>
<p><b>As it were a jasper stone </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως λιθω ιασπιδ</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:3">Revelation 4:3</span>, which see.</p>
<p><b>Clear as crystal </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρυσταλλιζοντ</span></span>). Verb not found elsewhere from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρυσταλλος</span></span> (old word, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:6">Revelation 4:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:1">Revelation 22:1</span>), "of crystalline brightness and transparency" (Thayer), "transparent and gleaming as rock-crystal" (Moffatt). </p>
