<p><b> Having a wall great and high </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχουσα τειχος μεγα κα υψηλον</span></span>). John returns, after the parenthesis in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:11">Revelation 21:11</span>, to the structure in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:10">Revelation 21:10</span>, only to use the accusative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχουσαν</span></span> as before to agree with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολιν</span></span>, but the nominative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχουσα</span></span> as again with "twelve gates" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυλωνας δωδεκα</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πυλων</span></span> is an old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυλη</span></span> gate) for a large gate as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:20">Luke 16:20</span> and six times in Rev. for the gate tower of a city wall (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:12">Revelation 21:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:13">Revelation 21:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:15">Revelation 21:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:21">Revelation 21:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:25">Revelation 21:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:14">Revelation 22:14</span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ki+17:10">1 Kings 17:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:13">Acts 14:13</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+48:31">Ezekiel 48:31</span> for these twelve gates, one for each tribe (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:1-8">Revelation 7:1-8</span>).</p>
<p><b>At the gates </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ τοις πυλωσιν</span></span>). "Upon the gate towers."</p>
<p><b>Twelve angels </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγγελους δωδεκα</span></span>). As <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυλωρο</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυλακες</span></span> according to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+62:6">Isaiah 62:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ch+8:14">2 Chronicles 8:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>Names written thereon </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ονοματα επιγεγραμμενα</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιγραφω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Which are the names </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α εστιν</span></span>). Just as in Ezekiel's vision (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+48:31">Revelation 48:31</span>), so here the names of the twelve tribes of Israel appear, one on each gate. </p>
