<p><b> Had </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειχεν</span></span>). Regular imperfect here, no longer <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχων</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>For a measure a golden reed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετρον καλαμον χρυσουν</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:1">Revelation 11:1</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλαμος</span></span> (reed). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μετρον</span></span> is an old word, kin to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μητηρ</span></span> (mother, moulder, manager), an instrument for measuring (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετρεω</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:2">Matthew 7:2</span>, here in the predicate accusative.</p>
<p><b>To measure </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μετρηση</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετρεω</span></span>. The rod of gold was in keeping with the dignity of the service of God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:12">Revelation 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:8">Revelation 5:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:3">Revelation 8:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:13">Revelation 9:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:7">Revelation 15:7</span>). </p>
