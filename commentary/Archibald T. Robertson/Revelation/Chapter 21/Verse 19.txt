<p><b> Were adorned </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεκοσμημενο</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοσμεω</span></span> as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:2">Revelation 21:2</span>, but without the copula <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ησαν</span></span> (were), followed by instrumental case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λιθω</span></span> (stone).</p>
<p><b>With all manner of precious stones </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντ λιθω τιμιω</span></span>). "With every precious stone." The list of the twelve stones in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:19">Revelation 21:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:20">Revelation 21:20</span> has no necessary mystical meaning. "The writer is simply trying to convey the impression of a radiant and superb structure" (Moffatt). The twelve gems do correspond closely (only eight in common) with the twelve stones on the high priest's breastplate (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+28:17-20">Exodus 28:17-20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+39:10">Exodus 39:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+28:13">Ezekiel 28:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+54:11">Isaiah 54:11</span>). Charles identifies them with the signs of zodiac in reverse order, a needless performance here. See the stones in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:3">Revelation 4:3</span>. These foundation stones are visible. For jasper (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιασπις</span></span>) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:3">Revelation 4:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:11">Revelation 21:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:18">Revelation 21:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+54:12">Isaiah 54:12</span>; sapphire (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαπφειρος</span></span>) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+24:10">Exodus 24:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+54:11">Isaiah 54:11</span> (possibly the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαπις λαζυλ</span></span> of Turkestan); chalcedony (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαλκηδων</span></span>) we have no other reference in N.T. or LXX (described by Pliny, H.N. XXXIII.21), possibly a green silicate of copper from near Chalcedon; emerald (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σμαραγδος</span></span>) here only in N.T., see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:3">Revelation 4:3</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σμαραγδινος</span></span>, and like it a green stone. </p>
