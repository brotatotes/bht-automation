<p><b> Twelve pearls </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δωδεκα μαργαριτα</span></span>). These gate towers (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυλωνες</span></span>) were mentioned in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:12">Revelation 21:12</span>. Each of these (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+54:12">Isaiah 54:12</span>) is a pearl, one of the commonest of jewels (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:6">Matthew 7:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:46">Matthew 13:46</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:9">1 Timothy 2:9</span>).</p>
<p><b>Each one </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανα εις εκαστος</span></span>). Distributive use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανα</span></span>, but with the nominative (used as adverb, not preposition) rather than the accusative (as a preposition) as appears also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:19">Mark 14:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:9">John 8:9</span>; with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:5">Romans 12:5</span>, "a barbaric construction" according to Charles.</p>
<p><b>Street </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλατεια</span></span>). For which word (broad way, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδος</span></span> understood) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:5">Matthew 6:5</span>, here the singular, but includes all the streets.</p>
<p><b>Transparent </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαυγης</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span>, through, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυγη</span></span>, ray, shining through), here alone in N.T. </p>
