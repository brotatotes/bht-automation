<p><b> They are come to pass </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Γεγοναν</span></span>). Second perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-αν</span></span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-ασ</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:17">Revelation 16:17</span> for a like use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεγονεν</span></span>, "They have come to pass." Here again it is the voice of God because, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:8">Revelation 1:8</span>, He says:</p>
<p><b>I am the Alpha and the Omega </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εγω το Αλφα κα το Ο</span></span>) with the addition "the beginning and the end" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η αρχη κα το τελος</span></span>), the whole used in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:13">Revelation 22:13</span> of Christ. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+44:6">Isaiah 44:6</span> there is something like the addition, and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:18">Colossians 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:14">Revelation 3:14</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η αρχη</span></span> is applied to Christ, while here God is the First Cause (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρχη</span></span>) and the Finality (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελος</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:36">Romans 11:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:6">Ephesians 4:6</span>. But God works through Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:3">John 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:2">Hebrews 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:12-20">Colossians 1:12-20</span>). God is the bountiful Giver (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:5">James 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:17">James 1:17</span>) of the Water of Life. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:17">Revelation 7:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:1">Revelation 22:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:17">Revelation 22:17</span> for this metaphor, which is based on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+55:1">Isaiah 55:1</span>. It is God's own promise (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εγω δωσω</span></span>), "I will give."</p>
<p><b>Of the fountain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ της πηγης</span></span>). For this partitive use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:8">Matthew 25:8</span>, without <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span> <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:17">Revelation 2:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>Freely </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δωρεαν</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:8">Matthew 10:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:10">John 4:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:24">Romans 3:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:20">Acts 8:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:17">Revelation 22:17</span>. </p>
