<p><b> He that overcometh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο νικων</span></span>). Recalls the promises at the close of each of the Seven Letters in chapters 2 and 3.</p>
<p><b>Shall inherit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κληρονομησε</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κληρονομεω</span></span>, word with great history (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:17">Mark 10:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:4">1 Peter 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:7">Galatians 4:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:17">Romans 8:17</span>), here interpreted for the benefit of these who share in Christ's victory.</p>
<p><b>I will be his God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εσομα αυτω θεος</span></span>). Repeated Old Testament promise (first to Abraham, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+17:7">Genesis 17:7</span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:3">Revelation 21:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>He shall be my son </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτος εστα μο υιος</span></span>). Made first of Solomon (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2sa+7:14">2 Samuel 7:14</span>) and applied to David later in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+89:26">Psalms 89:26</span>. </p>
