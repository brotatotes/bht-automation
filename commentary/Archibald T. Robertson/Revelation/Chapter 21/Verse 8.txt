<p><b> Their part shall be </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το μερος αυτων</span></span>). In contrast to the state of the blessed (verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:3-7">Revelation 21:3-7</span>) the state of "those who have disfranchised themselves from the Kingdom of God" (Charles) is given. They are with Satan and the two beasts, and are the same with those not in the book of life (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:15">Revelation 20:15</span>) in the lake of fire and brimstone (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:20">Revelation 19:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:10">Revelation 20:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:14">Revelation 20:14</span>), that is the second death (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:11">Revelation 2:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:6">Revelation 20:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:14">Revelation 20:14</span>). See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:10">Revelation 14:10</span>. There are eight epithets here used which apply to various sections of this direful list of the doomed and the damned, all in the dative (case of personal interest).</p>
<p><b>For the fearful </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις δειλοις</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δειδω</span></span>, to fear) for the cowardly, who recanted under persecution, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:26">Matthew 8:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:40">Mark 4:40</span>.</p>
<p><b>Unbelieving </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απιστοις</span></span>). "Faithless," "untrustworthy," in contrast with Christ "<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο πιστος</span></span>" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:5">Revelation 1:5</span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:10">Revelation 2:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:13">Revelation 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:14">Revelation 3:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:14">Revelation 17:14</span>. Disloyalty is close kin to cowardice.</p>
<p><b>Abominable </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβδελυγμενοις</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βδελυσσω</span></span>, old verb, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:22">Romans 2:22</span>, common in LXX, to pollute (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+5:21">Exodus 5:21</span>). Those who have become defiled by the impurities of emperor-worship (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:4">Revelation 7:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:27">Revelation 21:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:22">Romans 2:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:16">Titus 1:16</span>).</p>
<p><b>Murderers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φονευσιν</span></span>). As a matter of course and all too common always (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:21">Mark 7:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:29">Romans 1:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:21">Revelation 9:21</span>).</p>
<p><b>Fornicators </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορνοις</span></span>). Again all too common always, then and now (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:10">1 Corinthians 5:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:9">1 Timothy 1:9</span>). These two crimes often go together.</p>
<p><b>Sorcerers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φαρμακοις</span></span>). Old word, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:15">Revelation 22:15</span>. Closely connected with idolatry and magic (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:21">Revelation 9:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:13">Revelation 13:13</span>).</p>
<p><b>Idolaters </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδωλολατραις</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:10">1 Corinthians 5:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:7">1 Corinthians 10:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:5">Ephesians 5:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:15">Revelation 22:15</span>. With a powerful grip on men's lives then and now.</p>
<p><b>All liars </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πασ τοις ψευδεσιν</span></span>). Repeated in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:15">Revelation 22:15</span> and stigmatized often (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:2">Revelation 2:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:9">Revelation 3:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:5">Revelation 14:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:8">Revelation 21:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:27">Revelation 21:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:15">Revelation 22:15</span>). Not a "light" sin. </p>
