<p><b> He shewed me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδειξεν μο</span></span>). The angel as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:9">Revelation 21:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:10">Revelation 21:10</span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:1">Revelation 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:1">Revelation 4:1</span>). Now the interior of the city.</p>
<p><b>A river of water of life </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποταμον υδατος ζωης</span></span>). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υδωρ ζωης</span></span> (water of life) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:17">Revelation 7:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:6">Revelation 21:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:17">Revelation 22:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:14">John 4:14</span>. There was a river in the Garden of Eden (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+2:10">Genesis 2:10</span>). The metaphor of river reappears in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="zec+14:8">Zechariah 14:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+47:9">Ezekiel 47:9</span>, and the fountain of life in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joe+3:18">Joel 3:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+2:13">Jeremiah 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="pr+10:11">Proverbs 10:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="pr+13:14">Proverbs 13:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="pr+14:27">Proverbs 14:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="pr+16:22">Proverbs 16:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+36:10">Psalms 36:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Bright as crystal </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμπρον ως κρυσταλλον</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:6">Revelation 4:6</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρυσταλλον</span></span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:6">Revelation 15:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:8">Revelation 19:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:16">Revelation 22:16</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμπρον</span></span>. "Sparkling like rock crystal" (Swete), shimmering like mountain water over the rocks.</p>
<p><b>Proceeding out of the throne of God and of the Lamb </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκπορευομενον εκ του θρονου του θεου κα του αρνιου</span></span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+47:1">Ezekiel 47:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="zec+14:8">Zechariah 14:8</span>. Already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:21">Revelation 3:21</span> Christ is pictured as sharing the Father's throne as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:3">Hebrews 1:3</span>. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:3">Revelation 22:3</span>. This phrase has no bearing on the doctrine of the Procession of the Holy Spirit. </p>
