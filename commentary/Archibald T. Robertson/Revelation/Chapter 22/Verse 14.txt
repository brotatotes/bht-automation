<p><b> Blessed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακαριο</span></span>). This is the last beatitude of the book and "deals with the issues of the higher life" (Swete).</p>
<p><b>They that wash their robes </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο πλυνοντες τας στολας αυτων</span></span>). Present active articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλυνω</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:14">Revelation 7:14</span> for this very verb with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στολας</span></span>, while in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:4">Revelation 3:4</span> the negative statement occurs. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:11">1 Corinthians 6:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>That they may have the right </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα εστα η εξουσια αυτων</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the future middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειμ</span></span> (a common construction in this book, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:4">Revelation 6:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:11">Revelation 6:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:5">Revelation 9:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:20">Revelation 9:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:12">Revelation 13:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:13">Revelation 14:13</span>), that there may be their right."</p>
<p><b>To come to the tree of life </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ το ξυλον της ζωης</span></span>). "Over the tree of life." On <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξουσια επ</span></span> = "power over" see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:8">Revelation 6:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:7">Revelation 13:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:9">Revelation 16:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:1">Luke 9:1</span>. On "the tree of life" see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:7">Revelation 2:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:2">Revelation 22:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>May enter in </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισελθωσιν</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισερχομα</span></span> parallel with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα εστα</span></span> (future).</p>
<p><b>By the gates </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις πυλωσιν</span></span>). Associative instrumental case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυλων</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:12">Revelation 21:12</span>), "by the gate towers." </p>
