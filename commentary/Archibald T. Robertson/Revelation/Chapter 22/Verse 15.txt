<p><b> Without </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξω</span></span>). Outside the holy city, with which compare <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:8">Revelation 21:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:27">Revelation 21:27</span>. Dustierdieck supplies an imperative: "Out, ye dogs."</p>
<p><b>The dogs </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο κυνες</span></span>). Not literal dogs, but the morally impure (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+23:18">Deuteronomy 23:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ki+8:13">2 Kings 8:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+22:17">Psalms 22:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+22:21">Psalms 22:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:6">Matthew 7:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:27">Mark 7:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:3">Philippians 3:3</span>). Dogs in the Oriental cities are the scavengers and excite unspeakable contempt.</p>
<p><b>The sorcerers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο φαρμακο</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:8">Revelation 21:8</span>, where are listed "the fornicators and the murderers and the idolaters," all "outside" the holy city here as there "in the lake that burns with fire and brimstone, the second death." Both are pictures (symbolic language) of hell, the eternal absence from fellowship with God. Another time Jesus spoke of "the outer darkness" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το σκοτος το εξωτερον</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:12">Matthew 8:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:13">Matthew 22:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:30">Matthew 25:30</span>), outside of lighted house, as the abode of the damned. Another symbol is the worm that dies not (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:48">Mark 9:48</span>).</p>
<p><b>Every one that loveth and maketh a lie </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πας φιλων κα ποιων ψευδος</span></span>). An interpretation of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πασιν τοις ψευδεσιν</span></span> (all liars) of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:8">Revelation 21:8</span> and of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιων ψευδος</span></span> (doing a lie) of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:27">Revelation 21:27</span>. Satan is the father of lying (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:44">John 8:44</span>) and Satan's home is a congenial place for those who love and practise lying (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:12">2 Thessalonians 2:12</span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:6">1 John 1:6</span> for not doing the truth and see also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:25">Romans 1:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:25">Ephesians 4:25</span>. </p>
