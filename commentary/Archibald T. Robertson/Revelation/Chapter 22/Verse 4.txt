<p><b> They shall see his face </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οψοντα το προσωπον αυτου</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span>. This vision of God was withheld from Moses (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+33:20">Exodus 33:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+33:23">Exodus 33:23</span>), but promised by Jesus to the pure in heart (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:8">Matthew 5:8</span>) and mentioned in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:14">Hebrews 12:14</span> as possible only to the holy, and promised in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+17:15">Psalms 17:15</span>. Even here on earth we can see God in the face of Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:6">2 Corinthians 4:6</span>), but now in the New Jerusalem we can see Christ face to face (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:12">1 Corinthians 13:12</span>), even as he is after we are made really like him (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:18">2 Corinthians 3:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:29">Romans 8:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:2">1 John 3:2</span>). It is anthropomorphic language, to be sure, but it touches the essential reality of religion. "The supreme felicity is reached, immediate presence with God and the Lamb" (Beckwith).</p>
<p><b>His name on their foreheads </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το ονομα αυτου επ των μετωπων αυτων</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:12">Revelation 3:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:3">Revelation 7:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:1">Revelation 14:1</span>. </p>
