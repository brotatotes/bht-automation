<p><b> Patience </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπομενης</span></span>). "Endurance" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:10">Revelation 13:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:12">Revelation 14:12</span> as also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:5">2 Thessalonians 3:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>Thou didst keep </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετηρησας</span></span>)</p>
<p><b>--I also will keep </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καγω τηρησω</span></span>). Aorist active indicative and future active corresponding to each other. For a like play on the tenses of this verb by Christ see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:6">John 17:6</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τετηρηκαν</span></span>), <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:11">John 17:11</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρησον</span></span>), <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:12">John 17:12</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετηρουν</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>From the hour of trial </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ της ωρας του πειρασμου</span></span>). This use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρεω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:15">John 17:15</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:27">James 1:27</span>. Trial brings temptation often (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:2">James 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:13">James 1:13</span>). Jesus endured (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:1">Hebrews 12:1</span>) and he will help them. There is still a church in Philadelphia in spite of the Turks.</p>
<p><b>Which is to come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της μελλουσης ερχεσθα</span></span>). Agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωρας</span></span> (feminine), not with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειρασμου</span></span> (masculine).</p>
<p><b>Upon the whole world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ της εποικουμενης ολης</span></span>). The inhabited earth (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γης</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:19">Revelation 12:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:1">Luke 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:6">Acts 16:6</span>, etc.), not the physical earth, but the world of men as explained by the next clause.</p>
<p><b>To try </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειρασα</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of purpose from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειραζω</span></span>, probably to tempt (cf. the demons in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:1-21">Revelation 9:1-21</span>), not merely to afflict (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:10">Revelation 2:10</span>).</p>
<p><b>That dwell upon the earth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους κατοικουντας επ της γης</span></span>). Present active articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατοικεω</span></span>, explaining "the whole world" just before. </p>
