<p><b> I come quickly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομα ταχυ</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:16">Revelation 2:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:7">Revelation 22:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:12">Revelation 22:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:20">Revelation 22:20</span>. "The keynote of the book" (Beckwith). But allow the author's own meaning of "quickly."</p>
<p><b>Hold fast that which thou hast </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρατε ο εχεις</span></span>). Sort of motto for each church (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:25">Revelation 2:25</span>).</p>
<p><b>That no one take </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μηδεις λαβη</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>. Here to take away "thy crown" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:10">Revelation 2:10</span>) which will be thine if really won and not forfeited by failure (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:8">2 Timothy 4:8</span>). In that case it will go to another (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:28">Matthew 25:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:17">Romans 11:17</span>). </p>
