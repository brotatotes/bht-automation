<p><b> Neither cold </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτε ψυχρος</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψυχω</span></span>, to grow cold (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:12">Matthew 24:12</span>), in N.T. only <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:42">Matthew 10:42</span> and this passage.</p>
<p><b>Nor hot </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτε ζεστος</span></span>). Late verbal from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζεω</span></span>, to boil, (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:11">Romans 12:11</span>), boiling hot, here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>I would thou wert </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφελον ης</span></span>). Wish about the present with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφελον</span></span> (really <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωφελον</span></span>, second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφειλω</span></span>, without augment) with the imperfect <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ης</span></span> (instead of the infinitive) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:1">2 Corinthians 11:1</span>, when the old Greek used <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειθε</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε γαρ</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:8">1 Corinthians 4:8</span> for the aorist indicative and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:12">Galatians 5:12</span> for the future. </p>
