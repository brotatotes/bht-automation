<p><b> I counsel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμβουλευω</span></span>). Present active indicative, old compound from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμβουλος</span></span>, counsellor (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:34">Romans 11:34</span>), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:14">John 18:14</span>. Almost ironical in tone.</p>
<p><b>To buy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγορασα</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγοραζω</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγορα</span></span>, market-place), rich as they think themselves to be.</p>
<p><b>From me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρ' εμου</span></span>). From my side, emphatic.</p>
<p><b>Refined by fire </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπυρωμενον εκ πυρος</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυροω</span></span> (as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:15">Revelation 1:15</span>) and the metaphor carried on by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ πυρος</span></span>, "fired by fire." Purity by removing dross (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+66:10">Psalms 66:10</span>) like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:7">1 Peter 1:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>That thou mayest become rich </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα πλουτησηις</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the ingressive first aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλουτεω</span></span>, spiritual riches.</p>
<p><b>That thou mayest clothe thyself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα περιβαλη</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and second aorist middle (direct) subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιβαλλω</span></span>, to fling round one as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:5">Revelation 3:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>Be not made manifest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη φανερωθη</span></span>). Continued purpose clause with negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> and first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανεροω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Nakedness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γυμνοτητος</span></span>). Late and rare word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γυμνος</span></span>, naked, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:27">2 Corinthians 11:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:35">Romans 8:35</span>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:15">Revelation 16:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:13">Revelation 20:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:2">2 Corinthians 5:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>Eye-salve </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολλουριον</span></span>). Diminutive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολλυρα</span></span> (coarse bread of cylindrical shape), object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγορασα</span></span>, name for a famous Phrygian powder for the eyes made in Laodicea (Charles), Latin <i>collyrium</i> (used for eye-salve by Horace and Juvenal).</p>
<p><b>To anoint </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγχρισα</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive (epexegetic) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγχριω</span></span>, late compound (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν, χριω</span></span>, Strabo, Epictetus), to rub in, here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>That thou mayest see </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα βλεπηις</span></span>). Another purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the present active subjunctive (keep on seeing). </p>
