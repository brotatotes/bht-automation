<p><b> Be thou watchful </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινου γρηγορων</span></span>). Periphrastic imperative with present middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span> (keep on becoming) and present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γρηγορεω</span></span> (late present from perfect <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγρηγορα</span></span> and that from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγειρω</span></span>, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:42">Matthew 24:42</span>) and see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:15">Revelation 16:15</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γρηγορεω</span></span> also. He does not say "Arise from the dead" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:14">Ephesians 5:14</span>), for there are vestiges of life. Those still alive are addressed through the angel of the church.</p>
<p><b>Stablish the things that remain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στηρισον τα λοιπα</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στηριζω</span></span>, to make stable. Those not actually dead, but in grave peril. See a like command to Titus in Crete (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:5">Titus 1:5</span>). Every new pastor faces such a problem.</p>
<p><b>Which were ready to die </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α εμελλον αποθανειν</span></span>). Imperfect active plural because the individuals, though neuter plural, are regarded as living realities. The imperfect looking on the situation "with a delicate optimism" (Swete) as having passed the crisis, a sort of epistolary imperfect.</p>
<p><b>For I have found no works of thine </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου γαρ ευρηκα σου εργα</span></span>). "For I have not found any works of thine." Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευρισκω</span></span>. The church as a whole represented by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σου</span></span> (thy).</p>
<p><b>Fulfilled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπληρωμενα</span></span>). Perfect passive predicate participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροω</span></span>. Their works have not measured up to God's standard (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενωπιον του θεου μου</span></span>). </p>
