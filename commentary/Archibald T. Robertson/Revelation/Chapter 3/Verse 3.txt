<p><b> Remember </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μνημονευε</span></span>). "Keep in mind," as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:5">Revelation 2:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>Therefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουν</span></span>). Resumptive and coordinating as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:19">Revelation 1:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:5">Revelation 2:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>Thou hast received </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειληφας</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>, "as a permanent deposit" (Vincent).</p>
<p><b>Didst hear </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκουσας</span></span>). First aorist active indicative, the act of hearing at the time.</p>
<p><b>And keep it </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα τηρε</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηρεω</span></span>, "hold on to what thou hast."</p>
<p><b>And repent </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα μετανοησον</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετανοεω</span></span>, "Turn at once."</p>
<p><b>If therefore thou shalt not watch </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν ουν μη γρηγορησηις</span></span>). Condition of third class with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν μη</span></span> and the first aorist (ingressive) active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γρηγορεω</span></span>, "if then thou do not wake up."</p>
<p><b>I will come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηξω</span></span>). Certainly future active here, though probably aorist subjunctive in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:25">Revelation 2:25</span>.</p>
<p><b>As a thief </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως κλεπτης</span></span>). As Jesus had already said (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:43">Matthew 24:43</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:39">Luke 12:39</span>), as Paul had said (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:2">1 Thessalonians 5:2</span>), as Peter had said (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:10">2 Peter 3:10</span>), as Jesus will say again (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:15">Revelation 16:15</span>).</p>
<p><b>Thou shalt not know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη γνωις</span></span>). Strong double negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη</span></span> with second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span>, though some MSS. have the future middle indicative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνωση</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>What hour </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιαν ωραν</span></span>). A rare classical idiom (accusative) surviving in the <i>Koine</i> rather than the genitive of time, somewhat like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:52">John 4:52</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:16">Acts 20:16</span> (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 470f.). Indirect question with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιαν</span></span>. </p>
