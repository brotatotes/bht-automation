<p><b> Shall be arrayed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιβαλειτα</span></span>). Future middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιβαλλω</span></span>, to fling around one, here and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:4">Revelation 4:4</span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> and the locative, but usually in this book with the accusative of the thing, retained in the passive or with the middle (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:9">Revelation 7:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:13">Revelation 7:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:1">Revelation 10:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:3">Revelation 11:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:1">Revelation 12:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:4">Revelation 17:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:16">Revelation 18:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:8">Revelation 19:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:13">Revelation 19:13</span>).</p>
<p><b>In white garments </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ιματιοις λευκοις</span></span>). Apparently the spiritual bodies in the risen life as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:1">2 Corinthians 5:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:4">2 Corinthians 5:4</span> and often in Revelation (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:4">Revelation 3:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:5">Revelation 3:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:11">Revelation 6:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:9">Revelation 7:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:13">Revelation 7:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:8">Revelation 19:8</span>).</p>
<p><b>I will in no wise blot out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη εξαλειψω</span></span>). Strong double negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη</span></span> and the first aorist active (or future) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαλειφω</span></span>, old word, to wipe out (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:19">Acts 3:19</span>).</p>
<p><b>Of the book of life </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ της βιβλου της ζωης</span></span>). Ablative case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span>. This divine register first occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+32:32">Exodus 32:32</span> and often in the O.T. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:20">Luke 10:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+4:3">Philippians 4:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:8">Revelation 13:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:15">Revelation 20:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:27">Revelation 21:27</span>. The book is in Christ's hands (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:8">Revelation 13:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:27">Revelation 21:27</span>).</p>
<p><b>His name </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το ονομα αυτου</span></span>). The name of the one who overcomes (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο νικων</span></span>). Clear reminiscence of the words of Christ about confessing to the Father those who confess him here (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:32">Matthew 10:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:38">Mark 8:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:26">Luke 9:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:8">Luke 12:8</span>). Whether John knew the Synoptic Gospels (and why not?) he certainly knew such sayings of Jesus. </p>
