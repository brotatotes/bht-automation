<p><b> I give </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδω</span></span>). Late omega form for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>, but the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-μ</span></span> form in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:13">Revelation 17:13</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδοασιν</span></span>). These Jewish converts are a gift from Christ. For this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:27">Acts 2:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:40">Acts 10:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:3">Acts 14:3</span>. There is ellipse of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τινας</span></span> before <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:10">Revelation 2:10</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ υμων</span></span>) and see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:9">Revelation 2:9</span> for "the synagogue of Satan."</p>
<p><b>Of them which say </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των λεγοντων</span></span>). Ablative plural in apposition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συναγωγης</span></span>. On the construction of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαυτους Ιουδαιους εινα</span></span> see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:9">Revelation 2:9</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιουδαιους εινα εαυτους</span></span>, the order of words being immaterial).</p>
<p><b>But do lie </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα ψευδοντα</span></span>). Present middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευδομα</span></span>, explanatory positive, addition here to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ουκ εισιν</span></span> of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:9">Revelation 2:9</span>, in contrast also with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αληθινος</span></span> of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:7">Revelation 3:7</span> and in Johannine style (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:44">John 8:44</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:10">1 John 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:4">1 John 2:4</span>).</p>
<p><b>I will make them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιησω αυτους</span></span>). Future active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span>, resuming the prophecy after the parenthesis (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των--ψευδοντα</span></span>, which say--but do lie).</p>
<p><b>To come and worship </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα ηξουσιν κα προσκυνησουσιν</span></span>). "That they come and worship" (final clause, like <i>facio ut</i> in Latin, with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκω</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσκυνεω</span></span>). The language is based on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+45:14">Isaiah 45:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+60:14">Isaiah 60:14</span>. The Jews expected homage (not worship in the strict sense) from the Gentiles, but it will come to the Christians at last (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:24">1 Corinthians 14:24</span>). Later Ignatius (<i>Philad</i>. 6) warns this church against Judaizing Christians, perhaps one result of an influx of Jews.</p>
<p><b>And to know </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα γνωσιν</span></span>). Continuation of the purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>, but with the second aorist active subjunctive rather than the less usual future indicative. See both constructions also with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:14">Revelation 22:14</span>. Probably a reminiscence of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+43:4">Isaiah 43:4</span> in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγω ηγαπησα σε</span></span> (I loved thee), first aorist active indicative. </p>
