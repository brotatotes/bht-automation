<p><b> After these things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα ταυτα</span></span>). Change in the panorama, not chronology (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:1">Revelation 7:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:9">Revelation 7:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:5">Revelation 15:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:1">Revelation 18:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:1">Revelation 19:1</span>). This vision is of heaven, not of earth as was true of chapters <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:1">Revelation 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:2">Revelation 4:2</span>. The first vision of Christ and the messages to the seven churches began in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:12">Revelation 1:12</span>. This new vision of the throne in heaven (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:1-11">Revelation 4:1-11</span>) succeeds that to which it here alludes.</p>
<p><b>I saw </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδον</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Behold </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδου</span></span>). Exclamation of vivid emotion as John looked. No effect on the structure and nominative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυρα</span></span> (door) follows it.</p>
<p><b>Opened </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηνεωιγμενη</span></span>). Perfect (triple reduplication) passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανοιγω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:8">Revelation 3:8</span> (door of opportunity) and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:20">Revelation 3:20</span> (door of the heart), here the door of revelation (Swete).</p>
<p><b>In heaven </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω ουρανω</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+1:1">Ezekiel 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:10">Mark 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:51">John 1:51</span>. In Revelation always in singular except <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:12">Revelation 12:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>The first </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η πρωτη</span></span>). Reference is to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:10">Revelation 1:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Speaking </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαλουσης</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαλεω</span></span>, rather <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγουσης</span></span> of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:10">Revelation 1:10</span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγω</span></span>, both agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαλπιγγος</span></span> (trumpet).</p>
<p><b>Saying </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγων</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγω</span></span> repeating the idea of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαλουσης</span></span>, but in the nominative masculine singular construed with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωνη</span></span> (feminine singular), construction according to sense because of the person behind the voice as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:15">Revelation 11:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:14">Revelation 19:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>Come up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναβα</span></span>). Short <i>Koine</i> form for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναβηθ</span></span> (second aorist active imperative second person singular of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναβαινω</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Hither </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωδε</span></span>). Originally "here," but vernacular use (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:25">John 6:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:27">John 10:27</span>).</p>
<p><b>I will show </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δειξω</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεικνυμ</span></span> in same sense in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:1">Revelation 1:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Hereafter </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα ταυτα</span></span>). Some editors (Westcott and Hort) connect these words with the beginning of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:2">Revelation 4:2</span>. </p>
