<p><b> Our Lord and our God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο κυριος κα ο θεος ημων</span></span>). The nominative form here used as vocative as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:28">John 20:28</span> and often.</p>
<p><b>To receive </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαβειν</span></span>). Epexegetic second aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αξιος</span></span> (worthy).</p>
<p><b>The glory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την δοξαν</span></span>). The article referring to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξαν</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:9">Revelation 4:9</span> and so with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την τιμην</span></span> (the honour), though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την δυναμιν</span></span> (the power) is not in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:9">Revelation 4:9</span>, but is the power due to be ascribed to God.</p>
<p><b>Thou didst create </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συ εκτισας</span></span>). Emphasis on <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συ</span></span> (thou), first aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κτιζω</span></span>, the verb used about the act of creation by Paul in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:16">Colossians 1:16</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκτισθη, εκτιστα</span></span>), constative aorist giving a summary picture of the whole (not as a process).</p>
<p><b>Because of thy will </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια το θελημα σου</span></span>). Reason for creation of the universe as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:10">Hebrews 2:10</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι' ον</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>They were </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ησαν</span></span>). Imperfect tense with a cursory glance at the universe as a fact, possibly a potential existence in God's purpose in the eternal past before the actual creation in time.</p>
<p><b>And were created </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα εκτισθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of the same verb, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κτιζω</span></span>, just used and in the plural, while Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:16">Colossians 1:16</span>) uses the singular <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκτισθη</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+8:6">1 Corinthians 8:6</span>. God's will wrought through the Logos (Christ). </p>
