<p><b> Straightway I was in the Spirit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευθεως εγενομην εν πνευματ</span></span>). But John had already "come to be in the Spirit" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:10">Revelation 1:10</span>, the very same phrase). Perhaps here effective aorist middle indicative while ingressive aorist in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:10">Revelation 1:10</span> (sequel or result, not entrance), "At once I found myself in the Spirit" (Swete), not "I came to be in the Spirit" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:10">Revelation 1:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Was set </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκειτο</span></span>). Imperfect middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κειμα</span></span>, old verb, used as passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span>. As the vision opens John sees the throne already in place as the first thing in heaven. This bold imagery comes chiefly from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ki+22:19">1 Kings 22:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+6:1">Isaiah 6:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+1:26-28">Ezekiel 1:26-28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="da+7:9">Daniel 7:9</span>. One should not forget that this language is glorious imagery, not actual objects in heaven. God is spirit. The picture of God on the throne is common in the O.T. and the N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:34">Matthew 5:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:22">Matthew 23:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:3">Hebrews 1:3</span> and in nearly every chapter in the Revelation, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:4">Revelation 1:4</span>, etc.). The use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθημενος</span></span> (sitting) for the name of God is like the Hebrew avoidance of the name <i>Jahweh</i> and is distinguished from the Son in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:16">Revelation 6:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:10">Revelation 7:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Upon the throne </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ τον θρονον</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Επ</span></span> with the accusative, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:4">Revelation 4:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:2">Revelation 6:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:4">Revelation 6:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:16">Revelation 11:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:4">Revelation 20:4</span>, but in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:9">Revelation 4:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:10">Revelation 4:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:1">Revelation 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:7">Revelation 4:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:13">Revelation 4:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:16">Revelation 6:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:15">Revelation 7:15</span> we have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ του θρονου</span></span> (genitive), while in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:10">Revelation 7:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:14">Revelation 19:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:5">Revelation 21:5</span> we have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ τω θρονω</span></span> (locative) with no great distinction in the resultant idea. </p>
