<p><b> To look upon </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορασε</span></span>). Locative case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορασις</span></span>, old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οραω</span></span>, to see) for appearance (in appearance) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+1:5">Ezekiel 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+1:26">Ezekiel 1:26</span>.</p>
<p><b>Like a jasper stone </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοιος ιασπιδ</span></span>). Associative-instrumental case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιασπις</span></span>, old word (Persian), used for stones of different colors, one opaque like opal, one translucent (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:11">Revelation 21:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:18">Revelation 21:18</span>, possibly here, only N.T. examples), one a red or yellow stone (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+54:12">Isaiah 54:12</span>). Some even take it for the diamond. Certainly not our cheap modern jasper.</p>
<p><b>A sardius </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαρδιω</span></span>). Old word, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:20">Revelation 21:20</span>. The carnelian or other red stone, derived from Sardis (Pliny).</p>
<p><b>Rainbow </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιρις</span></span>). Old word, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:1">Revelation 10:1</span>. From <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+1:28">Ezekiel 1:28</span>.</p>
<p><b>An emerald </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σμαραγδινω</span></span>). Adjective (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σμαραγδος</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:19">Revelation 21:19</span>), of emerald (supply <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λιθω</span></span>), in associative instrumental case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοιος</span></span>. John sees no form for God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+24:10">Exodus 24:10</span>), but only the brilliant flashing gems. "In the vision the flashing lustre of the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιασπις</span></span> and the fiery red of the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαρδ</span></span> are relieved by the halo (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιρις</span></span>) of emerald which encircled the Throne" (Swete). A complete circle. </p>
