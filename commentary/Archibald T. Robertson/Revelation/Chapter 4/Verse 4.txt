<p><b> Round about the throne </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυκλοθεν του θρονου</span></span>). Here as a preposition with the genitive, though only adverb in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:8">Revelation 4:8</span> (only N.T. examples save Textus Rec. in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:11">Revelation 5:11</span>).</p>
<p><b>Four and twenty thrones </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θρονο εικοσ τεσσαρες</span></span>). So P Q, but Aleph A have accusative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θρονους</span></span> (supply <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδον</span></span> from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:1">Revelation 4:1</span>) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεσσαρες</span></span> (late accusative in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-ες</span></span>). This further circle of thrones beyond the great throne.</p>
<p><b>I saw four and twenty elders </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εικοσ τεσσαρας πρεσβυτερους</span></span>). No <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδον</span></span> in the text, but the accusative case calls for it. Twenty-four as a symbolic number occurs only in this book and only for these elders (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:4">Revelation 4:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:10">Revelation 4:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:8">Revelation 5:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:16">Revelation 11:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:4">Revelation 19:4</span>). We do not really know why this number is chosen, perhaps two elders for each tribe, perhaps the twelve tribes and the twelve apostles (Judaism and Christianity), perhaps the twenty-four courses of the sons of Aaron (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ch+24:1-19">1 Chronicles 24:1-19</span>), perhaps some angelic rank (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:16">Colossians 1:16</span>) of which we know nothing. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:6">Ephesians 2:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Sitting </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθημενους</span></span>). Upon their thrones.</p>
<p><b>Arrayed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιβεβλημενους</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιβαλλω</span></span> (to throw around).</p>
<p><b>In white garments </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιματιοις λευκοις</span></span>). Locative case here as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:5">Revelation 3:5</span> (with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span>), though accusative in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:9">Revelation 7:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:13">Revelation 7:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Crowns of gold </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στεφανους χρυσους</span></span>). Accusative case again like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρεσβυτερους</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδον</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:1">Revelation 4:1</span>), not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδου</span></span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:14">Revelation 19:14</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχων</span></span> (having) is added. John uses <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαδημα</span></span> (diadem) for the kingly crown in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:3">Revelation 12:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:1">Revelation 13:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:12">Revelation 19:12</span>, but it is not certain that the old distinction between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαδεμ</span></span> as the kingly crown and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στεφανος</span></span> as the victor's wreath is always observed in late Greek. </p>
