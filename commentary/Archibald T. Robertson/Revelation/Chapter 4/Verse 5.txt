<p><b> Out of the throne </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ του θρονου</span></span>). Back to the throne itself. The imagery is kin to that in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+19:16">Exodus 19:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+24:9">Exodus 24:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+1:22">Ezekiel 1:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+1:27">Ezekiel 1:27</span>.</p>
<p><b>Proceed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκπορευοντα</span></span>). Graphic historical present.</p>
<p><b>Lightnings and voices and thunders </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αστραπα κα φωνα κα βροντα</span></span>). So exactly in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:19">Revelation 11:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:18">Revelation 16:18</span>, but in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:5">Revelation 8:5</span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βροντα</span></span> first, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αστραπα</span></span> last, all old and common words. "The thunderstorm is in Hebrew poetry a familiar symbol of the Divine power: cf., e.g., <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1sa+2:10">1 Samuel 2:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+18:9">Psalms 18:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+37:4">Job 37:4</span>." (Swete).</p>
<p><b>Seven lamps of fire </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επτα λαμπαδες πυρος</span></span>). Return to the nominative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδου</span></span>, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδον</span></span>) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ησαν</span></span> (were) understood. Metaphor drawn from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+1:13">Ezekiel 1:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="zec+4:12">Zechariah 4:12</span>. Our word "lamp," but here a torch as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:10">Revelation 8:10</span>, identified with the Holy Spirit (the Seven Spirits of God) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:4">Revelation 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:1">Revelation 3:1</span>, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυχνια</span></span> (lampstands) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:12">Revelation 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:20">Revelation 1:20</span>, nor <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυχνος</span></span> a hand-lamp with oil (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:15">Matthew 5:15</span>). "These torches blaze perpetually before the throne of God" (Swete). </p>
