<p><b> Like a lion </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοιον λεοντ</span></span>). Associative-instrumental case again. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:6">Revelation 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:10">Revelation 1:10</span>) each <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωον</span></span> has four faces, but here each has a different face. "The four forms represent whatever is noblest, strongest, wisest, and swiftest in nature" (Swete). But it is not necessary to try to find a symbolism in each face here like the early baseless identification with the Four Evangelists (the lion for Mark, the man for Matthew, the calf for Luke, the eagle for John). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μοσχος</span></span> is first a sprout, then the young of animals, then a calf (bullock or heifer) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:23">Luke 15:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:27">Luke 15:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:30">Luke 15:30</span>, or a full-grown ox (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+1:10">Ezekiel 1:10</span>).</p>
<p><b>Had </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχων</span></span>). Masculine singular (some MSS. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχον</span></span> neuter singular agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωον</span></span>) present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>, changing the construction with the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τριτον ζωον</span></span> almost like a finite verb as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:8">Revelation 4:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>A face as of a man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσωπον ως ανθρωπου</span></span>). Shows that the likeness in each instance extended only to the face.</p>
<p><b>Like an eagle flying </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοιον αετω πετομενω</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πετομα</span></span>, to fly, old verb, in N.T. only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:7">Revelation 4:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:13">Revelation 8:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:14">Revelation 12:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:6">Revelation 14:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:17">Revelation 19:17</span>. The <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αετος</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:28">Matthew 24:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:37">Luke 17:37</span> may be a form of vulture going after carrion, but not in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:13">Revelation 8:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:14">Revelation 12:14</span>. </p>
