<p><b> Each one of them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν καθ' εν αυτων</span></span>). "One by one of them," a vernacular idiom like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις κατα εις</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:19">Mark 14:19</span>.</p>
<p><b>Having </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχων</span></span>). Masculine participle again as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:7">Revelation 4:7</span>, though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωον</span></span> neuter.</p>
<p><b>Six wings </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανα πτερυγας εξ</span></span>). Distributive use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανα</span></span>, "six wings apiece" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:1">Luke 10:1</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανα δυο</span></span>, by twos). Like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+6:2">Isaiah 6:2</span>, not like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+1:6">Ezekiel 1:6</span>, where only four wings are given apiece.</p>
<p><b>Are full of </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεμουσιν</span></span>). Plural verb, though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωα</span></span> neuter, to individualize each one.</p>
<p><b>Round about and within </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυκλοθεν κα εσωθεν</span></span>). Perhaps before and behind (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:6">Revelation 4:6</span>) and under the wings, "pointing to the secret energies of nature" (Swete).</p>
<p><b>Rest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπαυσιν</span></span>). See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:11">Revelation 14:11</span>. Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπαυω</span></span>, to relax), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:29">Matthew 11:29</span>. God and Christ cease not their activity (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:17">John 5:17</span>). "This ceaseless activity of nature under the hand of God is a ceaseless tribute of praise" (Swete).</p>
<p><b>Day and night </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημερας κα νυκτος</span></span>). Genitive of time, by day and by night.</p>
<p><b>Holy, holy, holy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγιοσ, αγιοσ, αγιος</span></span>). "The task of the Cherubim together with the Seraphim and Ophannim is to sing the praises of God" (Charles) in the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρισαγιον</span></span> (triple repetition of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγιος</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Is the Lord God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κυριος ο θεος</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+6:3">Isaiah 6:3</span>. The copula <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστιν</span></span> (is) is not expressed, but is implied.</p>
<p><b>The Almighty </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο παντοκρατωρ</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:8">Revelation 1:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>Which was and which is and which is to come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ην κα ο ων κα ο ερχομενος</span></span>). Just as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:4">Revelation 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:8">Revelation 1:8</span>, but with the order changed. </p>
