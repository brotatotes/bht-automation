<p><b> In the right hand </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ την δεξιαν</span></span>). "Upon the right hand" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span>), the open palm. Anthropomorphic language drawn from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+2:9">Ezekiel 2:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>A book </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βιβλιον</span></span>). Diminutive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βιβλος</span></span>, but no longer so used, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βιβλαριδιον</span></span> occurring instead (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:2">Revelation 10:2</span>).</p>
<p><b>Written </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεγραμμενον</span></span>). Perfect passive predicate participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γραφω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Within and on the back </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσωθεν κα οπισθεν</span></span>). "Within and behind." Description of a roll like that in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:17">Luke 4:17</span>, not a codex as some scholars think. Usually these papyrus rolls were written only on the inside, but this one was so full of matter that it was written also on the back side (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπισθεν</span></span>), and so was an <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπισθογραφον</span></span> like that in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+2:10">Ezekiel 2:10</span>. There are many allegorical interpretations of this fact which are all beside the point.</p>
<p><b>Sealed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεσφραγισμενον</span></span>). Perfect passive predicate participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατασφραγιζω</span></span>, old compound (perfective use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span>), to seal up (down), here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>With seven seals </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σφραγισιν επτα</span></span>). Instrumental case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σφραγις</span></span>, old word used in various senses, proof or authentication (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:2">1 Corinthians 9:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:11">Romans 4:11</span>), signet-ring (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:2">Revelation 7:2</span>), impression made by the seal (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:4">Revelation 9:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:19">2 Timothy 2:19</span>), the seal on books closing the book (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:1">Revelation 5:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:2">Revelation 5:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:5">Revelation 5:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:9">Revelation 5:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:1">Revelation 6:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:3">Revelation 6:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:5">Revelation 6:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:7">Revelation 6:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:9">Revelation 6:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:12">Revelation 6:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:1">Revelation 8:1</span>). "A will in Roman law bore the seven seals of the seven witnesses" (Charles). But this sealed book of doom calls for no witnesses beyond God's own will. Alford sees in the number seven merely the completeness of God's purposes. </p>
