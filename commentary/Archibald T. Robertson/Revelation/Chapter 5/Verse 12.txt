<p><b> Worthy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αξιον</span></span>). Agreeing in gender (grammatical neuter) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρνιον</span></span>, but some MSS. have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αξιος</span></span> (masculine, natural gender). Note change to third person <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστιν</span></span> instead of second <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span>. The point of the song is the same as that in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:9">Revelation 5:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:10">Revelation 5:10</span>, but the language differs. Note the repeated article <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το</span></span> (the lamb the slain) referring to verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:6">Revelation 5:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:9">Revelation 5:9</span>. Note also the one article <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την</span></span> before <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυναμιν</span></span> for all the seven grounds of praise (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυναμιν</span></span>, power, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλουτον</span></span>, wealth, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σοφιαν</span></span>, wisdom, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ισχυν</span></span>, strength, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιμην</span></span>, honor, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξαν</span></span>, glory, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλογιαν</span></span>, blessing), though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλουτον</span></span> is masculine, in contrast with separate article for each item (all three feminine) in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:11">Revelation 4:11</span>, here grouping them all together, "a heptad of praise" (Swete). </p>
