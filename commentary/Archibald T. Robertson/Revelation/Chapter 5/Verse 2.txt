<p><b> A strong angel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγγελον ισχυρον</span></span>). One needed (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:1">Revelation 10:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:21">Revelation 18:21</span>) "whose call could reach to the farthest limits of the universe" (Beckwith) and so "with a great voice" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν φωνη μεγαλη</span></span>, in a great voice, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:7">Revelation 14:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:9">Revelation 14:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:15">Revelation 14:15</span>, and without <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:12">Revelation 5:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:10">Revelation 6:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:2">Revelation 7:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:10">Revelation 7:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:13">Revelation 8:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:3">Revelation 10:3</span>, etc.). See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ισχυρα φωνη</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:2">Revelation 18:2</span>).</p>
<p><b>Proclaiming </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κηρυσσοντα</span></span>). Present active predicate participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κηρυσσω</span></span>, to herald, to preach.</p>
<p><b>Worthy to open and to loose </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αξιος ανοιξα κα λυσα</span></span>). Worthy by rank and character (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:27">John 1:27</span>) as well as by ability (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδυνατο</span></span>, verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:3">Revelation 5:3</span>), followed by two infinitives (first aorist active) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανοιγω</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυω</span></span>, though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the subjunctive can be used after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αξιος</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:27">John 1:27</span>. Here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αξιος</span></span> is like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ικανος</span></span> (capable, qualified) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:8">Matthew 8:8</span>. The articles here (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το, τας</span></span>) refer to the book and the seals in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:1">Revelation 5:1</span>. It is a husteron-proteron, since the loosing of the seals precedes the opening of the book. </p>
