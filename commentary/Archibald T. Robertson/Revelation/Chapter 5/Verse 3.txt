<p><span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εν</span></span> (in) with locative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουρανω</span></span>), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> (upon) with genitive (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γης</span></span>), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποκατω</span></span> (under) with ablative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γης</span></span>), as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:13">Revelation 5:13</span>, including the whole universe, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+20:4">Exodus 20:4</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:10">Philippians 2:10</span>). The MSS. vary in the negative conjunctions after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδεις</span></span> (no one) between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδε--ουδε</span></span> (continuative, and not--nor) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτε--ουτε</span></span> (disjunctive, neither--nor).</p>
<p><b>To look thereon </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλεπειν αυτο</span></span>). Into the contents of the book. The universe declines the challenge. </p>
