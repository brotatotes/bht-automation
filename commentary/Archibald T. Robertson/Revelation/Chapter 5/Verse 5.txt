<p><b> One of the elders </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις εκ των πρεσβυτερων</span></span>). "One from among the elders" of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:4">Revelation 4:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:10">Revelation 4:10</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span> with the ablative 8 times in the Apocalypse, 12 in the Fourth Gospel, 10 in rest of the N.T., in place of the mere partitive genitive). No particular reason for one elder as the agent over another (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:13">Revelation 7:13</span>).</p>
<p><b>Saith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγε</span></span>). Dramatic vivid present.</p>
<p><b>Weep not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη κλαιε</span></span>). "Cease weeping" (prohibition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> and the present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλαιω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>The Lion </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο λεων</span></span>). Satan is called a lion by Peter (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:8">1 Peter 5:8</span>), but the metaphor belongs to Jesus also. Judah is called a lion in the blessing of Jacob (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+49:9">Genesis 49:9</span>) and Jesus as the greatest of the tribe of Judah, "the Root of David" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η ριζα Δαυειδ</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+11:1">Isaiah 11:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+11:10">Isaiah 11:10</span>) or the Branch from this root (the Messiah).</p>
<p><b>Hath overcome </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενικησεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νικαω</span></span>, "did overcome," coming first in the sentence as "the great historical fact of the victory of the Christ" (Swete). </p>
