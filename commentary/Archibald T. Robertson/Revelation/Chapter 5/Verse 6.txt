<p><b> And I saw </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ειδον</span></span>). Stirred by the words of the elder in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:5">Revelation 5:5</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδου</span></span>, behold). "I beheld."</p>
<p><b>In the midst </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν μεσω</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:6">Revelation 4:6</span> for this idiom. It is not quite clear where the Lamb was standing in the vision, whether close to the throne or in the space between the throne and the elders (perhaps implied by "came" in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:7">Revelation 5:7</span>, but nearness to the throne is implied by <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:1">Revelation 14:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:56">Acts 7:56</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:11">Hebrews 10:11</span>).</p>
<p><b>A Lamb </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρνιον</span></span>). Elsewhere in the N.T. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αμνος</span></span> is used of Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:29">John 1:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:36">John 1:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:32">Acts 8:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:19">1 Peter 1:19</span> like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+53:7">Isaiah 53:7</span>), but in the Apocalypse <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το αρνιον</span></span> occurs for the Crucified Christ 29 times in twelve chapters.</p>
<p><b>Standing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστηκος</span></span>). Second perfect active (intransitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span>) neuter accusative singular (grammatical gender like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρνιον</span></span>), though some MSS. read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστηκως</span></span> (natural gender masculine and nominative in spite of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδον</span></span> construction according to sense).</p>
<p><b>As though it had been slain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως εσφαγμενον</span></span>). Perfect passive predicate participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σφαζω</span></span>, old word, in N.T. only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:6">Revelation 5:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:9">Revelation 5:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:12">Revelation 5:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:4">Revelation 6:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:9">Revelation 6:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:3">Revelation 13:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:24">Revelation 18:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:12">1 John 3:12</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hως</span></span> (as if) is used because the Lamb is now alive, but (in appearance) with the marks of the sacrifice. The Christ as the Lamb is both sacrifice and Priest (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:12">Hebrews 9:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:11">Hebrews 10:11</span>).</p>
<p><b>Having </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχων</span></span>). Construction according to sense again with masculine nominative participle instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχοντα</span></span> (masculine accusative singular) or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχον</span></span> (neuter accusative singular). Seven horns (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κερας</span></span>) is a common symbol in the O.T. for strength and kingly power (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1sa+2:10">1 Samuel 2:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ki+22:11">1 Kings 22:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+112:9">Psalms 112:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="da+7:7">Daniel 7:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="da+7:20">Daniel 7:20</span>) and often in Rev. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:3">Revelation 12:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:1">Revelation 13:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:3">Revelation 17:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:12">Revelation 17:12</span>). Fulness of power (the All-powerful one) is symbolized by seven.</p>
<p><b>Seven eyes </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφθαλμους επτα</span></span>). Like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="zec+3:9">Zechariah 3:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="zec+4:10">Zechariah 4:10</span> and denotes here, as there, omniscience. Here they are identified with the seven Spirits of Christ, while in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:4">Revelation 1:4</span> the seven Spirits are clearly the Holy Spirit of God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:1">Revelation 3:1</span>), and blaze like torches (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:5">Revelation 4:5</span>), like the eyes of Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:14">Revelation 1:14</span>). The Holy Spirit is both Spirit of God and of Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:9">Romans 8:9</span>).</p>
<p><b>Sent forth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεσταλμενο</span></span>). Perfect passive predicate participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστελλω</span></span>, masculine plural (agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφθαλμους</span></span> in gender), but some MSS. have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεσταλμενα</span></span> agreeing with the nearer <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευματα</span></span>. </p>
