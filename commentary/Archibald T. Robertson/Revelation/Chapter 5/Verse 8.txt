<p><b> He had taken </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελαβεν</span></span>). Here John drops back to the narrative tense (the second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>), not the past perfect as the English rendering might indicate, merely "when he took." For like vivid variation (not confusion) of tenses with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειληφεν</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:3">Revelation 3:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:5">Revelation 8:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:17">Revelation 11:17</span> and with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειρηκα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:13">Revelation 7:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:3">Revelation 19:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Fell down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεσαν</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιπτω</span></span> with first aorist (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-αν</span></span>) ending, just "fell."</p>
<p><b>Having </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχοντες</span></span>). "Holding."</p>
<p><b>A harp </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κιθαραν</span></span>). Old word, the traditional instrument (lyre or zithern) for psalmody (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+33:2">Psalms 33:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+98:5">Psalms 98:5</span>, etc.).</p>
<p><b>Golden bowls </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιαλας χρυσας</span></span>). Broad shallow saucers, old word, in N.T. only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:8">Revelation 5:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:7">Revelation 15:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:1-4">Revelation 16:1-4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:8">Revelation 16:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:10">Revelation 16:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:12">Revelation 16:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:17">Revelation 16:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:1">Revelation 17:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:9">Revelation 21:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>Of incense </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυμιαματων</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυμιαω</span></span>, to burn incense (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:9">Luke 1:9</span>), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:10">Luke 1:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Which are </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α εισιν</span></span>). "Which (these bowls of incense) symbolize the prayers of the saints as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+140:2">Psalms 140:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:10">Luke 1:10</span>. </p>
