<p><b> They sing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιδουσιν</span></span>). Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιδω</span></span>. Old verb, to chant with lyrical emotion (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:16">Colossians 3:16</span>).</p>
<p><b>A new song </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωιδην καινην</span></span>). Cognate accusative for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιδε</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωιδη</span></span>, song) is <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αοιδε</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αειδω</span></span>, that is <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιδω</span></span> (the verb used), old word already used (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:16">Colossians 3:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:19">Ephesians 5:19</span>), called <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καινην</span></span> because a fresh song for new mercies (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+42:10">Isaiah 42:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+33:3">Psalms 33:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+40:3">Psalms 40:3</span>, etc.), here in praise of redemption to Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:3">Revelation 14:3</span>) like the new name (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:17">Revelation 2:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:12">Revelation 3:12</span>), the new Jerusalem (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:12">Revelation 3:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:2">Revelation 21:2</span>), the new heaven and the new earth (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:1">Revelation 21:1</span>), not the old song of creation (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:8">Revelation 4:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:11">Revelation 4:11</span>) to God.</p>
<p><b>For thou wast slain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ εσφαγης</span></span>). Second aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σφαζω</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αγοραζω</span></span> used by Paul and Peter of our purchase from sin by Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:20">1 Corinthians 6:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:23">1 Corinthians 7:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:13">Galatians 3:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:5">Galatians 4:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:1">2 Peter 2:1</span>; cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:18">1 Peter 1:18</span>).</p>
<p><b>Unto God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω θεω</span></span>). Dative case of advantage as also in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:10">Revelation 5:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>With thy blood </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω αιματ σου</span></span>). Instrumental use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:5">Revelation 1:5</span>. The blood of Christ as the price of our redemption runs all through the Apocalypse. This is the reason why Christ is worthy to "take the book and open its seals." That is, he is worthy to receive adoration and worship (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:11">Revelation 4:11</span>) as the Father does.</p>
<p><b>Men of every </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ πασης</span></span>). No <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωπους</span></span> (men) or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τινας</span></span> (some) before <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span> in the Greek. See a like ellipsis in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:9">Revelation 11:9</span> with a like grouping of words for all mankind, representatives of all races and nations (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:9">Revelation 7:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:7">Revelation 13:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:6">Revelation 14:6</span>). </p>
