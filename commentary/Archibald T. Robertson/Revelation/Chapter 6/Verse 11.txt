<p><b> A white robe </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στολη λευκη</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στελλω</span></span>, to equip, an equipment in clothes, a flowing robe (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:38">Mark 12:38</span>). For the white robe for martyrs see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:4">Revelation 3:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:4">Revelation 4:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:9">Revelation 7:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:13">Revelation 7:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:14">Revelation 19:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>That they should rest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα αναπαυσοντα</span></span>). Sub-final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the future indicative (as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:9">Revelation 3:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:4">Revelation 6:4</span>) middle rather than the aorist middle subjunctive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπαυσωντα</span></span> of Aleph C.</p>
<p><b>Yet for a little time </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετ χρονον μικρον</span></span>). Accusative of extension of time as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:3">Revelation 20:3</span>. Perhaps rest from their cry for vengeance and also rest in peace (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:13">Revelation 14:13</span>). For the verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπαυω</span></span> see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:28">Matthew 11:28</span>.</p>
<p><b>Until should be fulfilled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως πληρωθωσιν</span></span>). Future indefinite temporal clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως</span></span> and the first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροω</span></span>, to fill full (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:32">Matthew 23:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:10">Colossians 2:10</span>), "until be filled full" (the number of), regular Greek idiom.</p>
<p><b>Which should be killed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο μελλοντες αποκτεννεσθα</span></span>). Regular construction of articular present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μελλω</span></span> (about to be, going to be) with the present passive infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκτεννω</span></span>, Aeolic and late form for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκτεινω</span></span>, to kill (also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:5">Mark 12:5</span>). John foresees more persecution coming (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:10">Revelation 2:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:10">Revelation 3:10</span>). </p>
