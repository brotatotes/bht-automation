<p><b> Her unripe figs </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους ολυνθους αυτης</span></span>). An old word (Latin <i>grossi</i>) for figs that grow in winter and fall off in the spring without getting ripe (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="so+2:11">Song of Solomon 2:11</span>), here only in N.T. Jesus used the fig tree (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:28">Mark 13:28</span>) as a sign of the "end of the world's long winter" (Swete). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+34:4">Isaiah 34:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="na+3:12">Nahum 3:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>When she is shaken of a great wind </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο ανεμου μεγαλου σειομενη</span></span>). Present passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σειω</span></span>, "being shaken by a great wind." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:7">Matthew 11:7</span> for the reed so shaken. </p>
