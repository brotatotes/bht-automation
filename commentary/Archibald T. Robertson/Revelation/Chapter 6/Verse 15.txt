<p><b> The princes </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο μεγιστανες</span></span>). Late word from the superlative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεγιστος</span></span>, in LXX, Josephus, papyri, in N.T. only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:21">Mark 6:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:15">Revelation 6:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:23">Revelation 18:23</span>, for the grandees, the persecuting proconsuls (Swete).</p>
<p><b>The chief captains </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο χιλιαρχο</span></span>). The commanders of thousands, the military tribunes (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:21">Mark 6:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+19:18">Mark 19:18</span>).</p>
<p><b>The rich </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο πλουσιο</span></span>). Not merely those in civil and military authority will be terror-stricken, but the self-satisfied and complacent rich (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:4">James 5:4</span>).</p>
<p><b>The strong </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ισχυρο</span></span>). Who usually scoff at fear. See the list in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:16">Revelation 13:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:18">Revelation 19:18</span>. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:26">Luke 21:26</span>.</p>
<p><b>Every bondman </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πας δουλος</span></span>)</p>
<p><b>and freeman </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ελευθερος</span></span>). The two extremes of society.</p>
<p><b>Hid themselves in the caves and in the rocks of the mountains </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκρυψαν εαυτους εις τα σπηλαια κα εις τας πετρας των ορεων</span></span>). Based on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+2:10">Isaiah 2:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+2:18">Isaiah 2:18</span>. First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρυπτω</span></span> with the reflexive pronoun. For the old word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπηλαιον</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:13">Matthew 21:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:38">Hebrews 11:38</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ορεων</span></span> is the uncontracted Ionic form (for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορων</span></span>) of the genitive plural of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορος</span></span> (mountain). </p>
