<p><b> They say </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγουσιν</span></span>). Vivid dramatic present active indicative, as is natural here.</p>
<p><b>Fall on us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πεσατε εφ' ημας</span></span>). Second aorist (first aorist ending) imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιπτω</span></span>, tense of urgency, do it now.</p>
<p><b>And hide us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα κρυψατε ημας</span></span>). Same tense of urgency again from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρυπτω</span></span> (verb in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:15">Revelation 6:15</span>). Both imperatives come in inverted order from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ho+10:8">Hosea 10:8</span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλυψατε</span></span> (cover) in place of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρυψατε</span></span> (hide), quoted by Jesus on the way to the Cross (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:30">Luke 23:30</span>) in the order here, but with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλυψατε</span></span>, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρυψατε</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>From the face of him that </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο προσωπου του</span></span>, etc.). "What sinners dread most is not death, but the revealed Presence of God" (Swete). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+3:8">Genesis 3:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>And from the wrath of the Lamb </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα απο της οργης του αρνιου</span></span>). Repetition of "the grave irony" (Swete) of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:5">Revelation 5:5</span>. The Lamb is the Lion again in the terribleness of his wrath. Recall the mourning in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:7">Revelation 1:7</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:41">Matthew 25:41</span> where Jesus pronounces the woes on the wicked. </p>
