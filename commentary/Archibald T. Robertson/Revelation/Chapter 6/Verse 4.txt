<p><b> A red horse </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιππος πυρρος</span></span>). Old adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυρ</span></span> (fire), flame-coloured, blood-red (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ki+3:22">2 Kings 3:22</span>), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:3">Revelation 12:3</span>, like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="zec+1:8">Zechariah 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="zec+6:2">Zechariah 6:2</span> (roan horse).</p>
<p><b>To take peace from the earth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαβειν την ειρηνην εκ της γης</span></span>). Second aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>, and here the nominative case, the subject of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδοθη</span></span> (see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:2">Revelation 6:2</span>), "to take peace out of the earth." Alas, how many red horses have been ridden through the ages.</p>
<p><b>And that they should slay one another </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ινα αλληλους σφαξουσιν</span></span>). Epexegetical explanatory purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σφαζω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:6">Revelation 5:6</span>) instead of the more usual subjunctive (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:2">Revelation 6:2</span>). Cf. Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 998f. This is what war does to perfection, makes cannon fodder (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:27">John 14:27</span>) of men.</p>
<p><b>A great sword </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαχαιρα μεγαλη</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μαχαιρα</span></span> may be a knife carried in a sheath at the girdle (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:10">John 18:10</span>) or a long sword in battle as here. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ρομφαια</span></span>, also a large sword, is the only other word for sword in the N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:16">Revelation 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:12">Revelation 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:16">Revelation 2:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:8">Revelation 6:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:15">Revelation 19:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:21">Revelation 19:21</span>). </p>
