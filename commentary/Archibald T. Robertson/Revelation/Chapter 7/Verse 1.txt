<p><b> After this </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα τουτο</span></span>). Instead of the seventh seal (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:1">Revelation 8:1</span>) being opened, two other episodes or preliminary visions occupy chapter 7 (the sealing of the servants of God <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:1-8">Revelation 7:1-8</span> and the vision of the redeemed before the throne <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:9-17">Revelation 7:9-17</span>).</p>
<p><b>Standing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστωτας</span></span>). Second perfect predicate participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span>, intransitive and followed by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> and the accusative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γωνιας</span></span> as already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:20">Revelation 3:20</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ θυριαν</span></span>) and often again (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:3">Revelation 8:3</span> some MSS., others genitive; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:11">Revelation 11:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:18">Revelation 12:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:1">Revelation 14:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:2">Revelation 15:2</span>), but note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> with genitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θαλασσης</span></span> in the next clause, like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ κεφαλης</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:1">Revelation 12:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:3">Revelation 7:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Corners </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γωνιας</span></span>). Old word for angle (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:5">Matthew 6:5</span>), also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:8">Revelation 20:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>Holding </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρατουντας</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρατεω</span></span>, to hold fast (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:3">Mark 7:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:23">John 20:23</span>). The four winds (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:31">Matthew 24:31</span>) are held prisoner by angels at each of the four corners. Some Jews held the winds from due north, south, east, west to be favourable, while those from the angles (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:14">Acts 27:14</span>) were unfavourable (Charles). There is an angel of the fire (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:18">Revelation 14:18</span>) and an angel of the waters (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:5">Revelation 16:5</span>).</p>
<p><b>That no wind should blow </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη πνεη ανεμος</span></span>). Negative purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη</span></span> and the present active subjunctive, "lest a wind keep on blowing."</p>
<p><b>Upon any tree </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ παν δενδρον</span></span>). Accusative case here with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> rather than the preceding genitives (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γησ, θαλασσης</span></span>), "upon the land or upon the sea," but "against any tree" (picture of attack on the tree like a tornado's path). </p>
