<p><b> Answered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεκριθη</span></span>). First aorist passive (deponent) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκρινομα</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγων</span></span> (saying), a common (only here in the Apocalypse) Hebrew redundancy in the Gospels (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:5">Mark 9:5</span>). An elder intervenes, though no question has been asked to interpret the vision (Swete).</p>
<p><b>These </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτο</span></span>). Prophetic predicate nominative put before <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τινες εισιν</span></span> (who are they). Note article repeated with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στολας</span></span> pointing to verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:9">Revelation 7:9</span>, and accusative also retained after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιβεβλημενο</span></span> as there. Both "who" and "whence" as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jos+9:8">Joshua 9:8</span>. </p>
