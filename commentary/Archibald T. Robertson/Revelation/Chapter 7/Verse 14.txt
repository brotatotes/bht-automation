<p><b> I say </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειρηκα</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειπον</span></span>, "I have said." "To the Seer's mind the whole scene was still fresh and vivid" (Swete) like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεκραγεν</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:15">John 1:15</span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειληφεν</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:7">Revelation 5:7</span>, not the so-called "aoristic perfect" which even Moulton (<i>Prol</i>. p. 145) is disposed to admit.</p>
<p><b>My lord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κυριε μου</span></span>). "An address of reverence to a heavenly being" (Vincent), not an act of worship on John's part.</p>
<p><b>Thou knowest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συ οιδας</span></span>). "At once a confession of ignorance, and an appeal for information" (Swete), not of full confidence like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συ οιδας</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:15">John 21:15</span>.</p>
<p><b>They which come out of the great tribulation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ερχομενο εκ της θλιψεως της μεγαλης</span></span>). Present middle participle with the idea of continued repetition. "The martyrs are still arriving from the scene of the great tribulation" (Charles). Apparently some great crisis is contemplated (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:19">Matthew 13:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:21">Matthew 24:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:10">Mark 13:10</span>), though the whole series may be in mind and so may anticipate final judgment.</p>
<p><b>And they washed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα επλυναν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλυνω</span></span>, old verb, to wash, in N.T. only <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:2">Luke 5:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:14">Revelation 7:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:14">Revelation 22:14</span>. This change of construction after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ερχομενο</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο πλυνησαντες</span></span> to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα επλυναν</span></span> is common in the Apocalypse, one of Charles's Hebraisms, like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα εποιησεν</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:6">Revelation 1:6</span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα πλανα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:20">Revelation 2:20</span>.</p>
<p><b>Made them white </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελευκαναν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λευκαινω</span></span>, to whiten, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λευκος</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:13">Revelation 7:13</span>), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:3">Mark 9:3</span>. "Milligan remarks that <i>robes</i> are the expression of character and compares the word <i>habit</i> used of dress" (Vincent). The language here comes partly from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+49:11">Genesis 49:11</span> and partly from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+19:10">Exodus 19:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+19:14">Exodus 19:14</span>. For the cleansing power of Christ's blood see also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:25">Romans 3:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:9">Romans 5:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:20">Colossians 1:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:2">1 Peter 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:14">Hebrews 9:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:7">1 John 1:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:5">Revelation 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:9">Revelation 5:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:14">Revelation 22:14</span>. "The aorists look back to the life on earth when the cleansing was effected" (Swete). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:12">Philippians 2:12</span> for both divine and human aspects of salvation.</p>
<p><b>In the blood of the Lamb </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω αιματ του αρνιου</span></span>). There is power alone in the blood of Christ to cleanse from sin (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:7">1 John 1:7</span>), not in the blood of the martyrs themselves. The result is "white," not "red," as one might imagine. </p>
