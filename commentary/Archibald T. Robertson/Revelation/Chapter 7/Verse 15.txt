<p><b> Therefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια τουτο</span></span>). Because of the washing described in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:14">Revelation 7:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>They serve him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λατρευουσιν αυτω</span></span>). Dative case with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λατρευω</span></span> (present active indicative, old verb, originally to serve for hire <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λατρον</span></span>, then service in general, then religious service to God, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:10">Matthew 4:10</span>, then in particular ritual worship of the priests, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:5">Hebrews 8:5</span>). All the redeemed are priests (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:5">Revelation 16:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:10">Revelation 16:10</span>) in the heavenly temple (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:9">Revelation 6:9</span>) as here. But this service is that of spiritual worship, not of external rites (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:1">Romans 12:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:3">Philippians 3:3</span>).</p>
<p><b>Day and night </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημερας κα νυκτος</span></span>). Genitive of time, "by day and night," as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:8">Revelation 4:8</span> of the praise of the four living creatures.</p>
<p><b>Shall spread his tabernacle over them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκηνωσε επ' αυτους</span></span>). Future (change of tense from present in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λατρευουσιν</span></span>) active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκηνοω</span></span>, old verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκηνος</span></span> (tent, tabernacle), used in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:14">John 1:14</span> of the earthly life of Christ, elsewhere in N.T. only in Rev. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:14">Revelation 7:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:12">Revelation 12:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:6">Revelation 13:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:3">Revelation 21:3</span>). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:12">Revelation 12:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:6">Revelation 13:6</span> of those who dwell in tents, here of God spreading his tent "over" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ' αυτους</span></span>) the redeemed in heaven, in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:3">Revelation 21:3</span> of God tabernacling "with" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετ' αυτων</span></span>) the redeemed, in both instances a picture of sacred fellowship, and "the further idea of God's Presence as a protection from all fear of evil" (Swete) like the overshadowing of Israel by the Shekinah and a possible allusion also to the tents (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκηνα</span></span>) of the feast of tabernacles and to the tent of meeting where God met Moses (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+33:7-11">Exodus 33:7-11</span>). </p>
