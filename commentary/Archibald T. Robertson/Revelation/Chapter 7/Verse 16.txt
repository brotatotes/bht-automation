<p><b> They shall hunger no more </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου πεινασουσιν ετ</span></span>). Future tense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειναω</span></span>, old verb with late form instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεινησουσιν</span></span> like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:25">Luke 6:25</span>. It is a free translation of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+49:10">Isaiah 49:10</span> (not quotation from the LXX).</p>
<p><b>Neither thirst any more </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδε διψησουσιν ετ</span></span>). Future tense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διψαω</span></span>, the two strong human appetites will be gone, a clear refutation of a gross materialistic or sensual conception of the future life. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:35">John 6:35</span>.</p>
<p><b>Neither shall strike </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδε μη πεση</span></span>). Strong double negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδε μη</span></span> with second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιπτω</span></span>, to fall. They will no longer be under the rays of the sun as upon earth.</p>
<p><b>Nor any heat </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδε παν καυμα</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιω</span></span>, to burn, painful and burning heat, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:9">Revelation 16:9</span> (picture of the opposite condition). The use of the negative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παν</span></span> (all) for "not any" is common in N.T. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+121:6">Psalms 121:6</span>. </p>
