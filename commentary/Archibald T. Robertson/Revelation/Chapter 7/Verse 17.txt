<p><b> In the midst </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανα μεσον</span></span>). In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:6">Revelation 5:6</span> we have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν μεσω του θρονου</span></span> as the position of the Lamb, and so that is apparently the sense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανα μεσον</span></span> here as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:25">Matthew 13:25</span>, though it can mean "between," as clearly so in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:5">1 Corinthians 6:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>Shall be their shepherd </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παιμανε αυτους</span></span>). "Shall shepherd them," future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιμαινω</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιμην</span></span>, shepherd), in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:16">John 21:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:28">Acts 20:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:2">1 Peter 5:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:27">Revelation 2:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:17">Revelation 7:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:5">Revelation 12:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:15">Revelation 19:15</span>. Jesus is still the Good Shepherd of his sheep (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:11">John 10:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:14">John 10:14</span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+23:1">Psalms 23:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Shall guide them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδη γησε αυτους</span></span>). Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδηγεω</span></span>, old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδηγος</span></span>, guide, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:14">Matthew 15:14</span>), used of God's guidance of Israel (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+15:13">Exodus 15:13</span>), of God's guidance of individual lives (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+5:9">Psalms 5:9</span>), of the guidance of the Holy Spirit (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:13">John 16:13</span>), of Christ's own guidance here (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:4">John 14:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:4">Revelation 14:4</span>).</p>
<p><b>Unto fountains of waters of life </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ ζωης πηγας υδατων</span></span>). The language is like that in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+49:10">Isaiah 49:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+2:13">Jeremiah 2:13</span>. Note the order, "to life's water springs" (Swete) like the Vulgate <i>ad vitae fontes aquarum</i>, with emphasis on <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωης</span></span> (life's). For this idea see also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:12">John 4:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:14">John 4:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:38">John 7:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:6">Revelation 21:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:1">Revelation 22:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:17">Revelation 22:17</span>. No special emphasis on the plural here or in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:10">Revelation 8:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:7">Revelation 14:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:4">Revelation 16:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>And God shall wipe away </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα εξαλειψε ο θεος</span></span>). Repeated in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+21:4">Revelation 21:4</span> from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+25:8">Isaiah 25:8</span>. Future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαλειφω</span></span>, old compound, to wipe out (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ</span></span>), off, away, already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:5">Revelation 3:5</span> for erasing a name and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:19">Acts 3:19</span> for removing the stain (guilt) of sin.</p>
<p><b>Every tear </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παν δακρυον</span></span>). Old word, with other form, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δακρυ</span></span>, in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:38">Luke 7:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:44">Luke 7:44</span>. Note repetition of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφθαλμων</span></span> (out of their eyes). "Words like these of vv. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:15-17">Revelation 7:15-17</span> must sound as a divine music in the ears of the persecuted. God will comfort as a mother comforts" (Baljon). </p>
