<p><b> Hurt not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη αδικησητε</span></span>). Prohibition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> and the ingressive aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδικεω</span></span>, not to begin to hurt.</p>
<p><b>Till we shall have sealed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχρ σφραγισωμεν</span></span>). Temporal clause of indefinite action for the future with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχρ</span></span> (sometimes <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχρις ου</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχρις ου αν</span></span>) and the aorist subjunctive as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:8">Revelation 15:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:3">Revelation 20:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:5">Revelation 20:5</span> or the future indicative (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:7">Revelation 17:7</span>), usually with the notion of ascent (up to) rather than extent like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεχρ</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>An </b> (modal) sometimes occurs, but it is not necessary. But there is no <i>futurum exactum</i> idea in the aorist subjunctive, simply "till we seal," not "till we shall have sealed."</p>
<p><b>Upon their foreheads </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ των μετωπων</span></span>). From <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+9:4">Ezekiel 9:4</span>. Old word (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα, ωπς</span></span>, after the eye, above the eye, the space above or between the eyes), in N.T. only in the Apocalypse (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:3">Revelation 7:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:4">Revelation 9:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:16">Revelation 13:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:1">Revelation 14:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:9">Revelation 14:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:5">Revelation 17:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:4">Revelation 20:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:4">Revelation 22:4</span>). For "the servants of God" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους δουλους του θεου</span></span>) who are to be thus marked linked with angels in the service of God see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+1:1">Revelation 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:20">Revelation 2:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:2">Revelation 19:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:5">Revelation 19:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:3">Revelation 22:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:6">Revelation 22:6</span>. </p>
