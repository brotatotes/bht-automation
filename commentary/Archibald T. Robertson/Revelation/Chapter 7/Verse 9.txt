<p><b> Which no man could number </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ον αριθμησα αυτον ουδεις εδυνατο</span></span>). Redundant repetition of the pronoun <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτον</span></span> after the relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ον</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:5">Revelation 7:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:8">Revelation 3:8</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εδυνατο</span></span> imperfect indicative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αριθμησα</span></span> first aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αριθμεω</span></span>, old verb, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:30">Matthew 10:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:7">Luke 12:7</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:9">Revelation 5:9</span> (also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:9">Revelation 11:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:7">Revelation 13:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:10">Revelation 14:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:15">Revelation 17:15</span>) for the list of words after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span> (the spiritual Israel carried on all over the world), "a polyglott cosmopolitan crowd" (Swete).</p>
<p><b>Standing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστωτες</span></span>). Same form in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:1">Revelation 7:1</span>, only nominative masculine plural referring to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οχλος</span></span> (masculine singular), construction according to sense like the plural <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγοντων</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οχλου</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:1">Revelation 19:1</span>.</p>
<p><b>Arrayed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιβεβλημενους</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιβαλλω</span></span>, but in the accusative plural (not nominative like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστωτες</span></span>), a common variation in this book when preceded by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδον</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδου</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:4">Revelation 4:4</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θρονοι, πρεσβυτερους</span></span>). Charles regards this as a mere slip which would have been changed to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιβεβλημενο</span></span> if John had read the MS. over.</p>
<p><b>In white robes </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στολας λευκας</span></span>). Predicate accusative retained with this passive verb of clothing as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:13">Revelation 7:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:1">Revelation 10:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:3">Revelation 11:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:1">Revelation 12:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:4">Revelation 17:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:16">Revelation 18:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:13">Revelation 19:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Palms </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φοινικες</span></span>). Nominative again, back to construction with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδου</span></span>, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδον</span></span>. Old word, in N.T. only here for palm branches and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:13">John 12:13</span> for palm trees. Both these and the white robes are signs of victory and joy. </p>
