<p><b> Wormwood </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο Αψινθος</span></span>). Absinthe. Usually feminine (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η</span></span>), but masculine here probably because <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αστηρ</span></span> is masculine. Only here in N.T. and not in LXX (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πικρια</span></span>, bitterness, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χολη</span></span>, gall, etc.) except by Aquila in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="pr+5:4">Proverbs 5:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+9:15">Jeremiah 9:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+23:15">Jeremiah 23:15</span>. There are several varieties of the plant in Palestine.</p>
<p><b>Became wormwood </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγενετο εις αψινθον</span></span>). This use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> in the predicate with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span> is common in the LXX and the N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:19">Revelation 16:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:20">John 16:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:36">Acts 5:36</span>).</p>
<p><b>Of the waters </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ των υδατων</span></span>). As a result of (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span>) the use of the poisoned waters.</p>
<p><b>Were made bitter </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επικρανθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πικραινω</span></span>. Old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πικρος</span></span>, bitter), as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+10:9">Revelation 10:9</span>. In a metaphorical sense to embitter in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:19">Colossians 3:19</span>. </p>
