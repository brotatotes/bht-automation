<p><b> Was smitten </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επληγη</span></span>). Second aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλησσω</span></span>, old verb (like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληγη</span></span> plague), here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>That should be darkened </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα σκοτισθη</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and the first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκοτιζω</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκοτος</span></span> (darkness) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:29">Matthew 24:29</span>, but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκοτοω</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:2">Revelation 9:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>And the day should not shine </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα η ημερα μη φανη</span></span>). Negative purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη</span></span> and the first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φαινω</span></span>, to shed light upon, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:23">Revelation 18:23</span>, not the second aorist passive subjunctive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανη</span></span> with different accent. The eclipse here is only partial and is kin to the ninth Egyptian plague (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+10:21">Exodus 10:21</span>). </p>
