<p><b> An eagle </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενος αετου</span></span>). "One eagle," perhaps <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span>) used as an indefinite article (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:13">Revelation 9:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:21">Revelation 18:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:17">Revelation 19:17</span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:7">Revelation 4:7</span> also for the flying eagle, the strongest of birds, sometimes a symbol of vengeance (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+28:49">Deuteronomy 28:49</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ho+8:1">Hosea 8:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="hab+1:8">Habakkuk 1:8</span>).</p>
<p><b>Flying in mid-heaven </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πετομενου εν μεσουρανηματ</span></span>). Like the angel in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:6">Revelation 14:6</span> and the birds in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+19:17">Revelation 19:17</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μεσουρανημα</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεσουρανεω</span></span> to be in mid-heaven) is a late word (Plutarch, papyri) for the sun at noon, in N.T. only these three examples. This eagle is flying where all can see, and crying so that all can hear.</p>
<p><b>Woe, woe, woe </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουαι, ουαι, ουα</span></span>). Triple because three trumpets yet to come. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:10">Revelation 18:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:16">Revelation 18:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:19">Revelation 18:19</span> the double <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουα</span></span> is merely for emphasis.</p>
<p><b>For them that dwell on the earth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους κατοικουντας</span></span>). Accusative of the articular present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατοικεω</span></span>, is unusual (Aleph Q here and also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:12">Revelation 12:12</span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:21">Matthew 11:21</span>. There is even a nominative in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:10">Revelation 18:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>By reason of the other voices </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ των λοιπων φωνων</span></span>). "As a result of (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span>) the rest of the voices." There is more and worse to come, "of the three angels who are yet to sound" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των τριων αγγελων των μελλοντων σαλπιζειν</span></span>). </p>
