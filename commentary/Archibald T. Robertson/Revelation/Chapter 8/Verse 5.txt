<p><b> Taketh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειληφεν</span></span>). Vivid dramatic perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+5:7">Revelation 5:7</span>, "has taken." The angel had apparently ]aid aside the censer. Hardly merely the pleonastic use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:23">John 19:23</span>). John pictures the scene for us.</p>
<p><b>Filled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγεμισεν</span></span>). He drops back to the narrative use of the first aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεμιζω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>With the fire </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ του πυρος</span></span>), live coals from the altar (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+6:6">Isaiah 6:6</span>).</p>
<p><b>Cast </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβαλεν</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαλλω</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+19:24">Genesis 19:24</span> (Sodom); <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eze+10:2">Ezekiel 10:2</span> and Christ's bold metaphor in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:49">Luke 12:49</span>. See this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαλλω</span></span> also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:7">Revelation 8:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:4">Revelation 12:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:9">Revelation 12:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:13">Revelation 12:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:19">Revelation 14:19</span>.</p>
<p><b>Followed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγενοντο</span></span>). Came to pass naturally after the casting of fire on the earth. Same three elements in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:5">Revelation 4:5</span>, but in different order (lightnings, voices, thunders), lightning naturally preceding thunder as some MSS. have it here. Perhaps <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωνα</span></span>, the voices of the storm (wind, etc.). </p>
