<p><b> Fallen </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπτωκοτα</span></span>). Perfect active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιπτω</span></span>, already down. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:18">Luke 10:18</span> note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεσοντα</span></span> (constative aorist active, like a flash of lightning) after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εθεωρουν</span></span> and in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:2">Revelation 7:2</span> note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναβαινοντα</span></span> (present active and linear, coming up, picturing the process) after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδον</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Of the pit of the abyss </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του φρεατος της αβυσσου</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αβυσσος</span></span> is an old adjective (alpha privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βυθος</span></span>, depth, without depth), but <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η αβυσσος</span></span> (supply <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χωρα</span></span> place), the bottomless place. It occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:7">Romans 10:7</span> for the common receptacle of the dead for Hades (Sheol), but in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:31">Luke 8:31</span> a lower depth is sounded (Swete), for the abode of demons, and in this sense it occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:1">Revelation 9:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:2">Revelation 9:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:11">Revelation 9:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:7">Revelation 11:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+17:8">Revelation 17:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:1">Revelation 20:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:3">Revelation 20:3</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Φρεαρ</span></span> is an old word for well or cistern (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:5">Luke 14:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:11">John 4:11</span>) and it occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:1">Revelation 9:1</span> for the mouth of the abyss which is pictured as a cistern with a narrow orifice at the entrance and this fifth angel holds the key to it. </p>
