<p><b> One saying to the sixth angel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγοντα τω εκτω</span></span>). Accusative masculine singular active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγω</span></span>, personifying <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωνην</span></span> and agreeing with it in case, though not in gender. This voice speaks to the sixth angel (dative case).</p>
<p><b>Which had the trumpet </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο εχων την σαλπιγγα</span></span>). Nominative case in apposition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγγελω</span></span> (dative), the same anomalous phenomenon in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:20">Revelation 2:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:12">Revelation 3:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:12">Revelation 14:12</span>. Swete treats it as a parenthesis, like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+4:1">Revelation 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:15">Revelation 11:15</span>.</p>
<p><b>Loose </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυσον</span></span>). First aorist (ingressive) active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυω</span></span>, "let loose." Another group of four angels (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:1">Revelation 7:1</span>) like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:4">Acts 12:4</span>, described here "which are bound" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους δεδεμενους</span></span>). Perfect passive articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεω</span></span>, evidently the leaders of the demonic horsemen (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:15">Revelation 9:15</span>) as the four angels let loose the demonic locusts (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+7:1">Revelation 7:1</span>), both quaternions agents of God's wrath.</p>
<p><b>At the great river Euphrates </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ τω ποταμω τω μεγαλω Ευφρατη</span></span>). A regular epithet of the Euphrates (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:12">Revelation 16:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+15:18">Genesis 15:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+1:7">Deuteronomy 1:7</span>). It rises in Armenia and joins the Tigris in lower Babylonia, a total length of nearly 1800 miles, the eastern boundary of the Roman Empire next to Parthia. </p>
