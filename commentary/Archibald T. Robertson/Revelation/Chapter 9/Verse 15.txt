<p><b> Were loosed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελυθησαν</span></span>). First aorist (ingressive) passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυω</span></span>, "were let loose."</p>
<p><b>Which had been prepared </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ητοιμασμενο</span></span>). Perfect passive articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετοιμαζω</span></span>, to make ready (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετοιμος</span></span>), in a state of readiness prepared by God (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:6">Revelation 12:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:12">Revelation 16:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:34">Matthew 25:34</span>).</p>
<p><b>For the hour and day and month and year </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις την ωραν κα ημεραν κα μηνα κα ενιαυτον</span></span>). For this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ητοιμασμενον</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:21">2 Timothy 2:21</span>. All preparation over, the angels are waiting for the signal to begin.</p>
<p><b>That they should kill </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα αποκτεινωσιν</span></span>). The same idiom in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:5">Revelation 9:5</span> about the fifth trumpet, which brought torture. This one brings death. </p>
