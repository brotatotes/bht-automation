<p><b> By these three plagues </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο των τριων πληγων τουτων</span></span>). Our "plague" or stroke from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλησσω</span></span>, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:30">Luke 10:30</span> and often in Rev. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:20">Revelation 9:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:6">Revelation 11:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:1">Revelation 15:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:6">Revelation 15:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+15:8">Revelation 15:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:9">Revelation 16:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:4">Revelation 18:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:8">Revelation 18:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+22:18">Revelation 22:18</span>). It is used in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+11:1">Exodus 11:1</span> for the plagues in Egypt. The three plagues here are the fire, smoke, and brimstone which proceed from the mouths of the horses.</p>
<p><b>Was killed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεκτανθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκτεινω</span></span>, to kill, third person plural, though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το τριτον</span></span> is neuter singular because a collective idea. See same form in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:20">Revelation 9:20</span>. </p>
