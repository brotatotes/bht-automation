<p><b> The power </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η εξουσια</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:26">Revelation 2:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:8">Revelation 6:8</span>. This power of the horses is both in their mouths (because of the fire, smoke, brimstone) and in their tails, "for their tails are like unto serpents" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α γαρ ουρα αυτων ομοια οφεσιν</span></span>). Associative-instrumental case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφεσιν</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοια</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Οφις</span></span> is old word for snake (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:10">Matthew 7:10</span>).</p>
<p><b>Having heads </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχουσα κεφαλας</span></span>). Feminine present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>, agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουρα</span></span> (tails).</p>
<p><b>With them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αυταις</span></span>). Instrumental use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span>. Surely dreadful monsters. </p>
