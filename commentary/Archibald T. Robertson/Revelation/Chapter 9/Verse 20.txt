<p><b> Repented not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μετενοησαν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετανοεω</span></span>. The two-thirds of mankind still spared did not change their creed or their conduct.</p>
<p><b>Of the works </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ των εργων</span></span>). For this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετανοεω</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+2:21">Revelation 2:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:21">Revelation 9:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:11">Revelation 16:11</span>. By "works" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργων</span></span>) here idolatries are meant, as the next verse shows.</p>
<p><b>That they should not worship </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη προσκυνησουσιν</span></span>). Negative purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη</span></span> and the future active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσκυνεω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:5">Revelation 9:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>Devils </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα δαιμονια</span></span>). Both in the O.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+32:17">Deuteronomy 32:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+96:5">Psalms 96:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+106:37">Psalms 106:37</span>) and in the N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:21">1 Corinthians 10:21</span>) the worship of idols is called the worship of unclean spirits. Perhaps this is one explanation of the hideous faces given these images. "The idols" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα ειδωλα</span></span> <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:21">1 John 5:21</span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδος</span></span>, form, appearance) represented "demons," whether made of gold (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα χρυσα</span></span>) or of silver (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα αργυρα</span></span>) or of brass (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα χαλκα</span></span>) or of stone (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα λιθινα</span></span>) or of wood (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα ξυλινα</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="da+5:23">Daniel 5:23</span> for this picture of heathen idols. The helplessness of these idols, "which can neither see nor hear nor walk" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α ουτε βλεπειν δυναντα ουτε ακουειν ουτε περιπατειν</span></span>), is often presented in the O.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+113:12">Psalms 113:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+115:4">Psalms 115:4</span>). </p>
