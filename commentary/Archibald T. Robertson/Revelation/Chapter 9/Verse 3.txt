<p><b> Locusts </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακριδες</span></span>). Also verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:7">Revelation 9:7</span> and already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:4">Matthew 3:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:6">Mark 1:6</span> (diet of the Baptist). The Israelites were permitted to eat them, but when the swarms came like the eighth Egyptian plague (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+10:13">Exodus 10:13</span>) they devoured every green thing. The smoke was worse than the fallen star and the locusts that came out of the smoke were worse still, "a swarm of hellish locusts" (Swete).</p>
<p><b>The scorpions </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο σκορπιο</span></span>). Old name for a little animal somewhat like a lobster that lurks in stone walls in warm regions, with a venomous sting in its tail, in N.T. in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:19">Luke 10:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:12">Luke 11:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:3">Revelation 9:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:5">Revelation 9:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:10">Revelation 9:10</span>. The scorpion ranks with the snake as hostile to man. </p>
