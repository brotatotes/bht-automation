<p><b> That they should not kill them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη αποκτεινωσιν αυτους</span></span>). Sub-final object clause (subject of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδοθη</span></span>) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη</span></span> and the subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκτεινω</span></span> either present (continued action) or aorist (constative, form the same), the usual construction with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>. The locusts are charged to injure men, but not to kill them.</p>
<p><b>But that they should be tormented </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ' ινα βασανισθησοντα</span></span>). Sub-final clause again with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span>, but this time with the first future passive indicative (like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+3:9">Revelation 3:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+6:4">Revelation 6:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+8:3">Revelation 8:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+13:12">Revelation 13:12</span>) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασανιζω</span></span>, old verb, to test metals (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασανος</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:24">Matthew 4:24</span>) by touchstone, then to torture like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:29">Matthew 8:29</span>, further in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+11:10">Revelation 11:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+12:2">Revelation 12:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:10">Revelation 14:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+20:10">Revelation 20:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Five months </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηνας πεντε</span></span>). Accusative of extent of time. The actual locust is born in the spring and dies at the end of summer (about five months).</p>
<p><b>Torment </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασανισμος</span></span>). Late word for torture, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασανιζω</span></span>, in N.T. only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+9:5">Revelation 9:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+14:11">Revelation 14:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:7">Revelation 18:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:10">Revelation 18:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+18:15">Revelation 18:15</span>. The wound of the scorpion was not usually fatal, though exceedingly painful.</p>
<p><b>When it striketh a man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν παιση ανθρωπον</span></span>). Indefinite temporal clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν</span></span> and the first aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παιω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:51">Matthew 26:51</span>), old verb, to smite, "whenever it smites a man." </p>
