<p><b> They had </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειχαν</span></span>). Imperfect active, late form as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:7">Mark 8:7</span> in place of the usual <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειχον</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>As hair of women </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως τριχας γυναικων</span></span>). That is long hair (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:15">1 Corinthians 11:15</span>), with no reference to matters of sex at all, for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωπων</span></span> just before is used, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανδρων</span></span> (men as distinct from women). Perhaps the antennae of the locust were unusually long.</p>
<p><b>As the teeth of lions </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως λεοντων</span></span>). Supply <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο οδοντες</span></span> (the teeth) before <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεοντων</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joe+1:6">Joel 1:6</span>. The locust is voracious. </p>
