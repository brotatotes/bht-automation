<p><b> If by any means now at length </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε πως ηδη ποτε</span></span>). A condition of the first class in the form of an indirect question (aim) or elliptical condition like <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:12">Acts 27:12</span> (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 1024). Note the four particles together to express Paul's feelings of emotion that now at length somehow it may really come true.</p>
<p><b>I may be prospered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευοδωθησομα</span></span>). First future passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευοδοω</span></span> for which verb see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:2">1 Corinthians 16:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>By the will of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω θεληματ του θεου</span></span>). Paul's way lay "in" God's will. </p>
