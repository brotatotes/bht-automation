<p><b> Impart </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταδω</span></span>). Second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταδιδωμ</span></span>, to share with one. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:11">Luke 3:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:8">1 Thessalonians 2:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>To the end ye may be established </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το στηριχθηνα υμας</span></span>). Final clause (common in Paul) with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το</span></span> and the first aorist passive infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στηριζω</span></span> for which verb see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:32">Luke 22:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:3">1 Thessalonians 3:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:13">1 Thessalonians 3:13</span>. </p>
