<p><b> Oftentimes I purposed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολλακις προεθεμην</span></span>). Second aorist middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προτιθημ</span></span>, old verb to place, to propose to oneself, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:25">Romans 3:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:9">Ephesians 1:9</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:21">Acts 19:21</span> for this purpose.</p>
<p><b>And was hindered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα εκωλυθην</span></span>). "But was hindered," adversative use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>That I might have some fruit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα τινα καρπον σχω</span></span>). Second aorist (ingressive), active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span>, to have, and here means "might get (ingressive aorist) some fruit." </p>
