<p>On</p>
<p><b>debtor </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφειλετης</span></span>) see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:3">Galatians 5:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Both to Greeks and to Barbarians </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hελλησιν τε κα βαρβαροις</span></span>). The whole human race from the Greek point of view, Jews coming under <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαρβαροις</span></span>. On this word see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:2">Acts 18:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:4">Acts 18:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:11">1 Corinthians 4:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:11">Colossians 3:11</span> (only N.T. instances). The Greeks called all others barbarians and the Jews termed all others Gentiles. Did Paul consider the Romans as Greeks? They had absorbed the Greek language and culture. </p>
