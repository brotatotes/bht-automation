<p><b> So as much as in me is I am ready </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτω το κατ' εμε προθυμον</span></span>). Literally, "Thus the according to me affair is ready" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προθυμος</span></span>, old adjective, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προ, θυμος</span></span>). It is an awkward idiom like to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ υμων</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:18">Romans 12:18</span>. The plural <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα κατ' εμε</span></span> we find in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+1:12">Philippians 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+4:7">Colossians 4:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:21">Ephesians 6:21</span>. </p>
