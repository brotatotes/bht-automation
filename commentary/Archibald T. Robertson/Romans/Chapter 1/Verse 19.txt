<p><b> Because </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διοτ</span></span>). Gives the reason (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια, οτ</span></span> like our "for that") for the revelation of God's wrath.</p>
<p><b>That which may be known of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το γνωστον του θεου</span></span>). Verbal adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span>, either "the known" as elsewhere in N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:19">Acts 1:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:18">Acts 15:18</span>, etc.) or "the knowable" as usual in ancient Greek, that is "the knowledge" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η γνωσις</span></span>) of God. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:8">Philippians 3:8</span>. Cf. same use of the verbal <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρηστον</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:4">Romans 2:4</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμεταθετον</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:17">Hebrews 6:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>Manifest in them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανερον εν αυτοις</span></span>). In their hearts and consciences.</p>
<p><b>God manifested </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο θεος εφανερωσεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανεροω</span></span>. Not mere tautology. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:14-16">Romans 2:14-16</span>. </p>
