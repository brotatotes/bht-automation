<p><b> Exchanged </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετηλλαξαν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταλλασσω</span></span>, old word for exchanging trade, only here and verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:26">Romans 1:26</span> in N.T. What a bargain they made, "the truth of God for (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span>) the (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω</span></span>) lie." "The price of mythology" (Bengel).</p>
<p><b>Worshipped </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσεβασθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive (used transitively) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σεβαζομα</span></span>, old verb, used in late Greek like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σεβομα</span></span>, to worship.</p>
<p><b>Rather than the Creator </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα τον κτισαντα</span></span>). Placed side by side (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span>, the Creator and the creature, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κτισις</span></span>) they preferred the creature.</p>
<p><b>Who is blessed forever. Amen </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος εστιν ευλογητοσ. Αμην</span></span>). One of Paul's doxologies which may come at any moment when he is greatly stirred, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:5">Romans 9:5</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ευλογητος</span></span> is verbal of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλογεω</span></span>. </p>
