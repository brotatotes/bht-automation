<p><b> Turned </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεκαυθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative, causative aorist, of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκκαιω</span></span>, old verb, to burn out, to set on fire, to inflame with anger or lust. Here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Lust </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορεξε</span></span>). Only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Unseemliness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασχημοσυνην</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασχημον</span></span> (deformed). In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="re+16:15">Revelation 16:15</span>.</p>
<p><b>Recompense </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντιμισθιαν</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:13">2 Corinthians 6:13</span> for only other N.T. instance of this late Pauline word, there in good sense, here in bad.</p>
<p><b>Which was due </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην εδε</span></span>). Imperfect active for obligation still on them coming down from the past. This debt will be paid in full (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολαμβανοντες</span></span>, pay back as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:34">Luke 6:34</span>, and due as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:41">Luke 23:41</span>). Nature will attend to that in their own bodies and souls. </p>
