<p><b> Being called with </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπληρωμενους</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of the common verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροω</span></span>, state of completion, "filled to the brim with" four vices in the associative instrumental case (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδικια</span></span>, unrighteousness as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:18">Romans 1:18</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πονηρια</span></span>, active wickedness as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:22">Mark 7:22</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλεονεξια</span></span>, covetousness as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:5">1 Thessalonians 2:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:15">Luke 12:15</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακια</span></span>, maliciousness or inward viciousness of disposition as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:8">1 Corinthians 5:8</span>). Note asyndeton, no connective in the lists in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:29-31">Romans 1:29-31</span>. Dramatic effect. The order of these words varies in the MSS. and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορνεια</span></span>, fornication, is not genuine here (absent in Aleph A B C).</p>
<p><b>Full of </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεστους</span></span>). Paul changes from participle to adjective. Old adjective, rare in the N.T., like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεστοω</span></span>, to fill full (only in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:13">Acts 2:13</span> in N.T.), stuffed full of (with genitive). Five substantives in the genitive (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φθονου</span></span>, envy, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:21">Galatians 5:21</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φονου</span></span>, murder, and so a paronomasia or combination with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φθονου</span></span>, of like sounding words, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εριδος</span></span>, strife, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:16">2 Corinthians 12:16</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακοηθιας</span></span>, malignity, and here only in N.T. though old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακοηθης</span></span> and that from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακος</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηθος</span></span>, a tendency to put a bad construction on things, depravity of heart and malicious disposition. </p>
