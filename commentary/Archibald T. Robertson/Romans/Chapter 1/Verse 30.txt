<p>Paul changes the construction again to twelve substantives and adjectives that give vivid touches to this composite photograph of the God abandoned soul.</p>
<p><b>Whisperers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψιθυριστας</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψιθυριζω</span></span>, to speak into the ear, to speak secretly, an onomatopoetic word like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψιθυρισμος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:20">2 Corinthians 12:20</span>) and only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Backbiters </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλαλους</span></span>). Found nowhere else except in Hermas, compound like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλαλεω</span></span>, to talk back (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:11">James 4:11</span>), and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλαλια</span></span>, talking back (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:20">2 Corinthians 12:20</span>), talkers back whether secretly or openly.</p>
<p><b>Hateful to God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεοστυγεις</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεος</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στυγεω</span></span>. All the ancient examples take it in the passive sense and so probably here. So <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στυγητος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:13">Titus 3:13</span>). Vulgate has <i>deo odibiles</i>.</p>
<p><b>Insolent </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υβριστας</span></span>). Old word for agent from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υβριζω</span></span>, to give insult to, here alone in N.T. save <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:13">1 Timothy 1:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Haughty </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερηφανους</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φαινομα</span></span>, to appear above others, arrogant in thought and conduct, "stuck up."</p>
<p><b>Boastful </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλαζονας</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλη</span></span>, wandering. Empty pretenders, swaggerers, braggarts.</p>
<p><b>Inventors of evil things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφευρετας κακων</span></span>). Inventors of new forms of vice as Nero was. Tacitus (<i>Ann</i>. IV. ii) describes Sejanus as <i>facinorum omnium repertor</i> and Virgil (<i>Aen</i>. ii. 163) <i>scelerum inventor</i>.</p>
<p><b>Disobedient to parents </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γονευσιν απειθεις</span></span>). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:9">1 Timothy 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:2">2 Timothy 3:2</span>. An ancient and a modern trait. </p>
