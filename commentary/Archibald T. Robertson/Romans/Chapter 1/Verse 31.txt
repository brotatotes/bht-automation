<p><b> Without understanding </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασυνετους</span></span>). Same word in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:21">Romans 1:21</span>.</p>
<p><b>Covenant-breakers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασυνθετους</span></span>). Another paronomasia or pun. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Α</span></span> privative and verbal <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνθετος</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συντιθημ</span></span>, to put together. Old word, common in LXX (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jer+3:7">Jeremiah 3:7</span>), men "false to their engagements" (Sanday and Headlam), who treat covenants as "a scrap of paper."</p>
<p><b>Without natural affection </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αστοργους</span></span>). Late word, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στοργη</span></span>, love of kindred. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:3">2 Timothy 3:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Unmerciful </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανελεημονας</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεημων</span></span>, merciful. Late word, only here in N.T. Some MSS. add <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασπονδους</span></span>, implacable, from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:3">2 Timothy 3:3</span>. It is a terrible picture of the effects of sin on the lives of men and women. The late Dr. R. H. Graves of Canton, China, said that a Chinaman who got hold of this chapter declared that Paul could not have written it, but only a modern missionary who had been to China. It is drawn to the life because Paul knew Pagan Graeco-Roman civilization. </p>
