<p><b> First </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτον μεν</span></span>). Adverb in the accusative case, but no <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επειτα δε</span></span> (in the next place) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:2">Hebrews 7:2</span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επειτα</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:17">James 3:17</span> follows. The rush of thoughts crowds out the balanced phraseology as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:2">Romans 3:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:18">1 Corinthians 11:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>Through </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span>). As the mediator or medium of thanksgiving as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:25">Romans 7:25</span>.</p>
<p><b>For </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περ</span></span>). Concerning, about.</p>
<p><b>That </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span>). Or because. Either declarative or causal <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτ</span></span> makes sense here.</p>
<p><b>Your faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η πιστις υμων</span></span>). "Your Christianity" (Sanday and Headlam).</p>
<p><b>Is proclaimed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταγγελλετα</span></span>). Present passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταγγελλω</span></span>, to announce (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγγελλω</span></span>) up and down (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span>). See also <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγγελλω</span></span>, to bring back news (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:15">John 5:15</span>), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαγγελλω</span></span>, to announce from one as the source (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:8">Matthew 2:8</span>), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προκαταγγελλω</span></span>, to announce far and wide beforehand (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:18">Acts 3:18</span>).</p>
<p><b>Throughout all the world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ολω τω κοσμω</span></span>). Natural hyperbole as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:6">Colossians 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:6">Acts 17:6</span>. But widely known because the church was in the central city of the empire. </p>
