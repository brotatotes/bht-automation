<p><b> Did they not hear? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη ουκ ηκουσαν?</span></span>). Rather, "Did they fail to hear?" (expecting the negative answer <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span>, while <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ</span></span> blends with the verb). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:5">1 Corinthians 9:5</span> for this construction.</p>
<p><b>Yea, verily </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενουνγε</span></span>). Triple particle (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεν, ουν, γε</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:20">Romans 9:20</span>.</p>
<p><b>Sound </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φθογγος</span></span>). Vibration of a musical string. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:7">1 Corinthians 14:7</span>. Only two N.T. examples.</p>
<p><b>The world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της οικουμενης</span></span>). The inhabited earth as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:1">Luke 2:1</span>. </p>
