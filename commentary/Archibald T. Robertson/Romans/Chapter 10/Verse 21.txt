<p><b> All the day long </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ολην την ημεραν</span></span>). Accusative of extent of time. He quotes <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+65:2">Isaiah 65:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>Did I spread out </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεπετασα</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκπεταννυμ</span></span>, old verb, to stretch out, bold metaphor, only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Unto a disobedient and a gainsaying people </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς λαον απειθουντα κα αντιλεγοντα</span></span>). "Unto a people disobeying and talking back." The two things usually go together. Contrary and contradictory (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:34">Luke 13:34</span>). </p>
