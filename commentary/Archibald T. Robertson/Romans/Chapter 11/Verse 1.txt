<p><b> I say then </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγω ουν</span></span>). As in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:11">Romans 11:11</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ουν</span></span> looks back to <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:16-33">Romans 9:16-33</span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:19-21">Romans 10:19-21</span>.</p>
<p><b>Did God cast off? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη απωσατο ο θεοσ?</span></span>). An indignant negative answer is called for by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> and emphasized by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη γενοιτο</span></span> (God forbid). Paul refers to the promise in the O.T. made three times: <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1sa+12:22">1 Samuel 12:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+94:14">Psalms 94:14</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+93:14">Psalms 93:14</span> LXX); <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+94:4">Psalms 94:4</span>. First aorist middle indicative (without augment) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απωθεω</span></span>, to push away, to repel, middle, to push away from one as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:27">Acts 7:27</span>.</p>
<p><b>For I also </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα γαρ εγω</span></span>). Proof that not all the Jews have rejected Christ. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:5">Philippians 3:5</span> for more of Paul's pedigree. </p>
