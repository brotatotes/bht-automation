<p><b> Let their eyes be darkened </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκοτισθητωσαν ο οφθαλμο αυτων</span></span>). First aorist passive imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκοτιζω</span></span>, to darken. A terrible imprecation.</p>
<p><b>That they may not see </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του μη βλεπειν</span></span>). Repeated from verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:8">Romans 11:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>Bow down </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνκαμψον</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνκαμπτω</span></span>, old verb, to bend together as of captives whose backs (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νωτον</span></span>, another old word, only here in N.T.) were bent under burdens. Only here in N.T. </p>
