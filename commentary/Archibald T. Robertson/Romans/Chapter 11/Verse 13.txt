<p><b> To you that are Gentiles </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμιν τοις εθνεσιν</span></span>). "To you the Gentiles." He has a serious word to say to them.</p>
<p><b>Inasmuch then </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφ' οσον μεν ουν</span></span>). Not temporal, <i>quamdiu</i>, "so long as" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:15">Matthew 9:15</span>), but qualitative <i>quatenus</i> "in so far then as" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:40">Matthew 25:40</span>).</p>
<p><b>I glorify my ministry </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την διακονιαν μου δοξαζω</span></span>). As apostle to the Gentiles (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εθνων αποστολος</span></span>, objective genitive). Would that every minister of Christ glorified his ministry.</p>
<p><b>If by any means </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε πως</span></span>). This use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span> with purpose or aim is a kind of indirect discourse.</p>
<p><b>I may provoke </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραζηλωσω</span></span>). Either future active indicative or first aorist active subjunctive, see same uncertainty in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:10">Philippians 3:10</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταντησω</span></span>, but in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:11">Romans 3:11</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλαβω</span></span> after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε</span></span> is subjunctive. The future indicative is clear in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:10">Romans 1:10</span> and the optative in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:12">Acts 27:12</span>. Doubtful whether future indicative or aorist subjunctive also in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωσω</span></span> (save). </p>
