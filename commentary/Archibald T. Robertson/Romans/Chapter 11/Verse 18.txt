<p><b> Glory not over the branches </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη κατακαυχω των κλαδων</span></span>). Genitive case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span>. Present middle imperative second person singular of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατακαυχαομα</span></span> with negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span>, "stop glorying" or "do not have the habit of glorying over the branches." The conclusion of the preceding condition.</p>
<p><b>Gloriest </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατακαυχασα</span></span>). Late form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-αεσα</span></span> retaining <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ς</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Not thou </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου συ</span></span>). Very emphatic position. The graft was upon the stock and root, though each affected the other. </p>
