<p><b> The goodness and the severity of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρηστοτητα κα αποτομιαν θεου</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:2">Romans 2:2</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρηστοτης</span></span>, kindness of God. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αποτομια</span></span> (here alone in the N.T.) is from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποτομος</span></span>, cut off, abrupt, and this adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποτεμνω</span></span>, to cut off. This late word occurs several times in the papyri.</p>
<p><b>If thou continue </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν επιμενηις</span></span>). Third class condition, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and present active subjunctive.</p>
<p><b>Otherwise </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επε</span></span>). Ellipse after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επε</span></span>, "since if thou dost not continue."</p>
<p><b>Thou also </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα συ</span></span>). Precisely as the Jewish branches of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:17">Romans 11:17</span> were.</p>
<p><b>Shalt be cut off </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκκοπηση</span></span>). Second future passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκκοπτω</span></span>, to cut out. </p>
