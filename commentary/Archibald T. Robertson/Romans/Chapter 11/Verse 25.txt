<p><b> This mystery </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το μυστηριον τουτο</span></span>). Not in the pagan sense of an esoteric doctrine for the initiated (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυεω</span></span>, to blink, to wink), unknown secrets (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:7">2 Thessalonians 2:7</span>), or like the mystery religions of the time, but the revealed will of God now made known to all (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:1">1 Corinthians 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:7">1 Corinthians 2:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:1">1 Corinthians 4:1</span>) which includes Gentiles also (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:25">Romans 16:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:26">Colossians 1:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:3">Ephesians 3:3</span>) and so far superior to man's wisdom (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:2">Colossians 2:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+4:13">Colossians 4:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:9">Ephesians 3:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:32">Ephesians 5:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:19">Ephesians 6:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:11">Matthew 13:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:11">Mark 4:11</span>). Paul has covered every point of difficulty concerning the failure of the Jews to accept Jesus as the Messiah and has shown how God has overruled it for the blessing of the Gentiles with a ray of hope still held out for the Jews. "In early ecclesiastical Latin <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυστηριον</span></span> was rendered by <i>sacramentum</i>, which in classical Latin means <i>the military oath</i>. The explanation of the word <i>sacrament</i>, which is so often founded on this etymology, is therefore mistaken, since the meaning of sacrament belongs to <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυστηριον</span></span> and not to <i>sacramentum</i> in the classical sense" (Vincent).</p>
<p><b>Wise in your own conceits </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν εαυτοις φρονιμο</span></span>). "Wise in yourselves." Some MSS. read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρ' εαυτοις</span></span> (by yourselves). Negative purpose here (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη ητε</span></span>), to prevent self-conceit on the part of the Gentiles who have believed. They had no merit in themselves</p>
<p><b>A hardening </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πωρωσις</span></span>). Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πωροω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:7">Romans 11:7</span>). Occurs in Hippocrates as a medical term, only here in N.T. save <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:5">Mark 3:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:18">Ephesians 4:18</span>. It means obtuseness of intellectual discernment, mental dulness.</p>
<p><b>In part </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο μερους</span></span>). Goes with the verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεγονεν</span></span> (has happened in part). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο μερους</span></span>, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:14">2 Corinthians 1:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:5">2 Corinthians 2:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:24">Romans 15:24</span>; for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανα μερος</span></span>, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:27">1 Corinthians 14:27</span>; for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ μερους</span></span>, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:27">1 Corinthians 12:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:9">1 Corinthians 13:9</span>; for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα μερος</span></span>, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:5">Hebrews 9:5</span>; for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μερος τ</span></span> (adverbial accusative) partly see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:18">1 Corinthians 11:18</span>. Paul refuses to believe that no more Jews will be saved.</p>
<p><b>Until the fulness of the Gentiles be come in </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχρ ου το πληρωμα των εθνων εισελθη</span></span>). Temporal clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχρ ου</span></span> (until which time) and the second aorist active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εισερχομα</span></span>, to come in (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:13">Matthew 7:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:21">Matthew 7:21</span>).</p>
<p><b>For fulness of the Gentiles </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το πληρωμα των εθνων</span></span>) see on verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:12">Romans 11:12</span>, the complement of the Gentiles. </p>
