<p><b> And so </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ουτως</span></span>). By the complement of the Gentiles stirring up the complement of the Jews (verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:11">Romans 11:11</span>).</p>
<p><b>All Israel </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πας Ισραηλ</span></span>). What does Paul mean? The immediate context (use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πας</span></span> in contrast with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο μερουσ, πληρωμα</span></span> here in contrast with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληρωμα</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:12">Romans 11:12</span>) argues for the Jewish people "as a whole." But the spiritual Israel (both Jews and Gentiles) may be his idea in accord with <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:6">Romans 9:6</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:16">Galatians 6:16</span>) as the climax of the argument. At any rate we should strive for and pray for the conversion of Jews as a whole. Paul here quotes from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+59:20">Isaiah 59:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+27:9">Isaiah 27:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>The Deliverer </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ρυομενος</span></span>). Present middle articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρυομα</span></span>, to rescue, to deliver. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:10">1 Thessalonians 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:10">2 Corinthians 1:10</span>. The Hebrew <i>Goel</i>, the Avenger, the Messiah, the Redeemer (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+25:5-10">Deuteronomy 25:5-10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="job+19:25">Job 19:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ru+3:12">Ruth 3:12</span>). Paul interprets it of Jesus as Messiah. </p>
