<p><b> Hath shut up </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεκλεισεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνκλειω</span></span>, to shut together like a net (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:6">Luke 5:6</span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:22">Galatians 3:22</span> for this word with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο αμαρτιαν</span></span> (under sin). This is a resultant (effective) aorist because of the disbelief and disobedience of both Gentile (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:17-32">Romans 1:17-32</span>) and Jew (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:1-3">Romans 2:1-3</span>).</p>
<p><b>All </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους παντας</span></span>). "The all" (both Gentiles and Jews).</p>
<p><b>That he might have mercy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα--ελεηση</span></span>). Purpose with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and aorist active subjunctive. No merit in anyone, but all of grace. "The all" again, who receive God's mercy, not that "all" men are saved. </p>
