<p><b> Who hath known? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τις εγνω?</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκω</span></span>, a timeless aorist, did know, does know, will know. Quotation from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+40:13">Isaiah 40:13</span>. Quoted already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:16">1 Corinthians 2:16</span>.</p>
<p><b>Counsellor </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμβουλος</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βουλη</span></span>. Only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>His </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτου</span></span>). Objective genitive, counsellor to him (God). Some men seem to feel competent for the job. </p>
