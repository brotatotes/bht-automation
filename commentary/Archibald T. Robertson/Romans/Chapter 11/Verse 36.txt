<p><b> Of him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ αυτου</span></span>),</p>
<p><b>through him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι' αυτου</span></span>),</p>
<p><b>unto him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις αυτον</span></span>). By these three prepositions Paul ascribes the universe (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα παντα</span></span>) with all the phenomena concerning creation, redemption, providence to God as the</p>
<p><b>Source </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ</span></span>), the</p>
<p><b>Agent </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δ</span></span>), the</p>
<p><b>Goal </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>For ever </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τους αιωνας</span></span>). "For the ages." Alford terms this doxology in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:33-36">Romans 11:33-36</span> "the sublimest apostrophe existing even in the pages of inspiration itself." </p>
