<p><b> The answer of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο χρηματισμος</span></span>). An old word in various senses like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρηματιζω</span></span>, only here in N.T. See this use of the verb in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:12">Matthew 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:22">Matthew 2:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:26">Luke 2:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:22">Acts 10:22</span>.</p>
<p><b>To Baal </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη Βααλ</span></span>). Feminine article. In the LXX the name <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Βααλ</span></span> is either masculine or feminine. The explanation is that the Jews put <i>Bosheth</i> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αισχυνη</span></span>, shame) for Baal and in the LXX the feminine article occurs because <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αισχυνη</span></span> is so, though here the LXX has the masculine <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω</span></span>. </p>
