<p><b> What then? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τ ουν?</span></span>). Since God did not push Israel away (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:1">Romans 11:1</span>), what is true?</p>
<p><b>The election </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η εκλογη</span></span>). Abstract for concrete (the elect).</p>
<p><b>Obtained </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επετυχεν</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιτυγχανω</span></span>, old verb, to hit upon, only here in Paul. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:30-33">Romans 9:30-33</span> for the failure of the Jews.</p>
<p><b>Were hardened </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επωρωθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πωροω</span></span>, late verb, to cover with thick skin (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πωρος</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:14">2 Corinthians 3:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:5">Mark 3:5</span>. </p>
