<p><b> A spirit of stupor </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα κατανυξεως</span></span>). The quotation is a combination of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+19:4">Deuteronomy 19:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+29:10">Isaiah 29:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+6:9">Isaiah 6:9</span>. This phrase is from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+29:10">Isaiah 29:10</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Κατανυξις</span></span> is a late and rare word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατανυσσω</span></span>, to prick or stick (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:37">Acts 2:37</span>), in LXX, here only in N.T., one example in <i>Pelagia-Legende</i>. The torpor seems the result of too much sensation, dulled by incitement into apathy.</p>
<p><b>That they should not see </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του μη βλεπειν</span></span>). Genitive articular infinitive of negative purpose.</p>
<p><b>That they should not hear </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του μη ακουειν</span></span>). So here also. See Stephen's speech (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:51">Acts 7:51</span>). </p>
