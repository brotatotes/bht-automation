<p><b> David says </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Δαυειδ λεγε</span></span>). From <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+69:23">Psalms 69:23</span>; (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+68:23">Romans 68:23</span> LXX); <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+34:8">Romans 34:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+28:4">Romans 28:4</span> (combined quotation).</p>
<p><b>Table </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τραπεζα</span></span>). For what is on the table, "a feast."</p>
<p><b>A snare </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις παγιδα</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πηγνυμ</span></span>, to make fast, old word for snares for birds and beasts. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:35">Luke 21:35</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εις</span></span> in predicate with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span> is a translation-Hebraism.</p>
<p><b>A trap </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις θηραν</span></span>). Old word for hunting of wild beasts, then a trap. Only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>A stumbling-block </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις σκανδαλον</span></span>). A third word for trap, snare, trap-stick or trigger over which they fall. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:23">1 Corinthians 1:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:33">Romans 9:33</span>.</p>
<p><b>A recompense </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις ανταποδομα</span></span>). Late word from double compound verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανταποδιδωμ</span></span>, to repay (both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span>). Ancient Greeks used <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανταποδοσις</span></span>. In LXX and Didache. In N.T. only here (bad sense) and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:12">Luke 14:12</span> (good sense). </p>
