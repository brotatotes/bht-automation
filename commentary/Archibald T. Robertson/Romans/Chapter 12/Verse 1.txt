<p><b> Therefore </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουν</span></span>). This inferential participle gathers up all the great argument of chapters <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:1-11">Romans 12:1-11</span>. Now Paul turns to exhortation (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρακαλω</span></span>), "I beseech you."</p>
<p><b>By the mercies </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια των οικτιρμων</span></span>). "By means of the mercies of God" as shown in his argument and in our lives. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:3">2 Corinthians 1:3</span> for "the Father of mercies."</p>
<p><b>To present </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραστησα</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παριστημ</span></span>, for which verb see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:13">Romans 6:13</span>, a technical term for offering a sacrifice (Josephus, <i>Ant</i>. IV. 6, 4), though not in the O.T. Used of presenting the child Jesus in the temple (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:22">Luke 2:22</span>), of the Christian presenting himself (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:13">Romans 6:13</span>), of God presenting the saved (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:27">Ephesians 5:27</span>), of Christ presenting the church (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:28">Colossians 1:28</span>).</p>
<p><b>Bodies </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωματα</span></span>). So literally as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:13">Romans 6:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:19">Romans 6:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:10">2 Corinthians 5:10</span> and in contrast with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νους</span></span> (mind) in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:2">Romans 12:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>A living sacrifice </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυσιαν ζωσαν</span></span>). In contrast with the Levitical sacrifices of slain animals. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:8">Romans 6:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:11">Romans 6:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:13">Romans 6:13</span>. Not a propitiatory sacrifice, but one of praise.</p>
<p><b>Acceptable </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευαρεστον</span></span>). "Well-pleasing." See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:9">2 Corinthians 5:9</span>.</p>
<p><b>Which is your reasonable service </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την λογικην υμων λατρειαν</span></span>). "Your rational (spiritual) service (worship)." For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λατρεια</span></span>, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:4">Romans 9:4</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Λογικος</span></span> is from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογος</span></span>, reason. The phrase means here "worship rendered by the reason (or soul)." Old word, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:2">1 Peter 2:2</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το λογικον γαλα</span></span> (not logical milk, but the milk nourishing the soul). </p>
