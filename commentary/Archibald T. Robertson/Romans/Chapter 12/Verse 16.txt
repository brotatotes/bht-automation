<p><b> Be of the same mind </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το αυτο φρονουντες</span></span>). Absolute or independent use of the participle again as with all the participles through verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:18">Romans 12:18</span>, "thinking the same thing."</p>
<p><b>Set not your mind on high things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη τα υψηλα φρονουντες</span></span>). "Not thinking the high things" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υψηλος</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υψος</span></span>, height). Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:5">1 Corinthians 13:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>Condescend to things that are lowly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις ταπεινοις συναπαγομενο</span></span>). "Be carried away with (borne along with) the lowly things" (in contrast with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα υψηλα</span></span>, though the associative instrumental case may be masculine, "with lowly men." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:13">Galatians 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:17">2 Peter 3:17</span> for the only other N.T. examples of this old verb.</p>
<p><b>Be not wise </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη γινεσθε φρονιμο</span></span>). "Do not have the habit of becoming (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινεσθε</span></span>) wise in your own conceits" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρ' εαυτοις</span></span>, beside yourselves). Note the imperative in the midst of infinitives and participles. </p>
