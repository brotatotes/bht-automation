<p><b> Avenge not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη εκδικουντες</span></span>). Independent participle again of late verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκδικεω</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκδικος</span></span>, exacting justice (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:4">Romans 13:4</span>). See already <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:5">Luke 18:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:6">2 Corinthians 10:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>But give place unto wrath </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα δοτε τοπον τη οργη</span></span>). Second aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδωμ</span></span>, to give. "Give room for the (note article as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:9">Romans 5:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:16">1 Thessalonians 2:16</span>) wrath" of God instead of taking vengeance in your own hands. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:27">Ephesians 4:27</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδοτε τοπον</span></span>. Paul quotes <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="de+32:35">Deuteronomy 32:35</span> (the Hebrew rather than the LXX). So have <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:30">Hebrews 10:30</span> and the Targum of Onkelos, but the relation between them and Paul we cannot tell. Socrates and Epictetus condemned personal vindictiveness as Paul does here.</p>
<p><b>I will recompense </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανταποδωσω</span></span>). Future active of the double compound verb quoted also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:35">Romans 11:35</span>. </p>
