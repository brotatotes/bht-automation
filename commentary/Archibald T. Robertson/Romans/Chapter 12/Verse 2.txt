<p><b> Be not fashioned </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη συνσχηματιζεσθε</span></span>). Present passive imperative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span>, stop being fashioned or do not have the habit of being fashioned. Late Greek verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συσχηματιζω</span></span>, to conform to another's pattern (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:31">1 Corinthians 7:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:7">Philippians 2:7</span>). In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:14">1 Peter 1:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>According to this world </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω αιων τουτω</span></span>). Associative instrumental case. Do not take this age as your fashion plate.</p>
<p><b>Be ye transformed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταμορφουσθε</span></span>). Present passive imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταμορφοω</span></span>, another late verb, to transfigure as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:2">Matthew 17:2</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:2">Mark 9:2</span>); <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:18">2 Corinthians 3:18</span>, which see. On the distinction between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σχημα</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μορφη</span></span>, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:7">Philippians 2:7</span>. There must be a radical change in the inner man for one to live rightly in this evil age, "by the renewing of your mind" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη ανακαινωσε του νοος</span></span>). Instrumental case. The new birth, the new mind, the new (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καινος</span></span>) man.</p>
<p><b>That ye may prove </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το δοκιμαζειν</span></span>). Infinitive of purpose with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το</span></span>, "to test" what is God's will, "the good and acceptable and perfect" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το αγαθον κα ευαρεστον κα τελειον</span></span>). </p>
