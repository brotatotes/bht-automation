<p><b> Feed him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψωμιζε αυτον</span></span>). Quotation from LXX text of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="pr+25:21">Proverbs 25:21</span>. Present active imperative of verb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψωμος</span></span>, a morsel, and so to feed crumbs to babies, then to feed in general. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:3">1 Corinthians 13:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Thou shalt heap </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωρευσεις</span></span>). Future active of old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωρευω</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωρος</span></span>, a heap. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:6">2 Timothy 3:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Coals of fire </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρακας πυρος</span></span>). That is, burning or live coals.</p>
<p><b>Anthrax </b> (our "anthracite") is an old word, only here in N.T. It is a metaphor for keen anguish. The Arabs have a proverb "coals in the heart," "fire in the liver." Such kindness may lead to repentance also. </p>
