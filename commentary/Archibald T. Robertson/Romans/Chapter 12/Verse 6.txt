<p><b> Differing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαφορα</span></span>). Old adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαφερω</span></span>, to differ, to vary. So <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:10">Hebrews 9:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>According to the proportion of our faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα την αναλογιαν της πιστεως</span></span>). The same use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστις</span></span> (faith) as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:3">Romans 12:3</span> "the measure of faith." Old word. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναλογια</span></span> (our word "analogy") from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναλογος</span></span> (analogous, conformable, proportional). Here alone in N.T. The verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προφητευωμεν</span></span> (present active volitive subjunctive, let us prophesy) must be supplied with which <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχοντες</span></span> agrees. The context calls for the subjective meaning of "faith" rather than the objective and outward standard though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστις</span></span> does occur in that sense (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:23">Galatians 1:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:23">Galatians 3:23</span>). </p>
