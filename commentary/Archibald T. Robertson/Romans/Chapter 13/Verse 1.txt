<p><b> Every soul </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πασα ψυχη</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:9">Romans 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:43">Acts 2:43</span>. A Hebraism for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πας ανθρωπος</span></span> (every man).</p>
<p><b>To the higher powers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξουσιαις υπερεχουσαις</span></span>). Abstract for concrete. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:10">Mark 2:10</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξουσια</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hυπερεχω</span></span> is an old verb to have or hold over, to be above or supreme, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:13">1 Peter 2:13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Except by God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε μη υπο θεου</span></span>). So the best MSS. rather than <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο θεου</span></span> (from God). God is the author of order, not anarchy.</p>
<p><b>The powers that be </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α ουσα</span></span>). "The existing authorities" (supply <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξουσια</span></span>). Art ordained (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεταγμενα εισιν</span></span>). Periphrastic perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τασσω</span></span>, "stand ordained by God." Paul is not arguing for the divine right of kings or for any special form of government, but for government and order. Nor does he oppose here revolution for a change of government, but he does oppose all lawlessness and disorder. </p>
