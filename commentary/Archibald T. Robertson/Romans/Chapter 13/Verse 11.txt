<p><b> And this </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα τουτο</span></span>). Either nominative absolute or accusative of general reference, a common idiom for "and that too" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:6">1 Corinthians 6:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:8">1 Corinthians 6:8</span>, etc.).</p>
<p><b>Knowing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδοτες</span></span>). Second perfect active participle, nominative plural without a principal verb. Either we must supply a verb like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιησωμεν</span></span> (let us do it) or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιησατε</span></span> (do ye do it) or treat it as an independent participle as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:10">Romans 12:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>The season </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον καιρον</span></span>). The critical period, not <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρονος</span></span> (time in general).</p>
<p><b>High time </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωρα</span></span>). Like our the "hour" has come, etc. MSS. vary between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημας</span></span> (us) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμας</span></span> (you), accusative of general reference with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγερθηνα</span></span> (first aorist passive infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγειρω</span></span>, to awake, to wake up), "to be waked up out of sleep" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ υπνου</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Nearer to us </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγγυτερον ημων</span></span>). Probably so, though <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημων</span></span> can be taken equally well with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η σωτηρια</span></span> (our salvation is nearer). Final salvation, Paul means, whether it comes by the second coming of Christ as they all hoped or by death. It is true of us all. </p>
