<p><b> Honestly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευσχημονως</span></span>). Paul is fond of the metaphor "walk" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιπατεω</span></span>), 33 times though not in the Pastoral Epistles. This old adverb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευσχημων</span></span>, graceful) occurs also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:12">1 Thessalonians 4:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:40">1 Corinthians 14:40</span>. The English word "honest" means honourable (Latin <i>honor</i>) and so decent. Wycliff translates <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:32">1 Corinthians 12:32</span> by "unhonest," "honesty," "honest" for "less honourable, honour, honourable."</p>
<p><b>Not in revelling </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη κωμοις</span></span>). Plural "revellings." See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:21">Galatians 5:21</span>.</p>
<p><b>Drunkenness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεθαις</span></span>). Plural again, "drunkennesses." See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:21">Galatians 5:21</span>.</p>
<p><b>In chambering </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοιταις</span></span>). Plural also. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:10">Romans 9:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Wantonness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασελγειαις</span></span>). Plural likewise. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:21">2 Corinthians 12:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:19">Galatians 5:19</span>.</p>
<p><b>Not in strife and jealousy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη εριδ κα ζηλω</span></span>). Singular here, but some MSS. have the plural like the previous words. Quarrelling and jealousy go with the other vices (Shedd). </p>
