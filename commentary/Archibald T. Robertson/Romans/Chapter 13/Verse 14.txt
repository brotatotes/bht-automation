<p><b> But ye on </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδυσασθε</span></span>). The same metaphor as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:12">Romans 13:12</span>. The Lord Jesus Christ is the garment that we all need. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:27">Galatians 3:27</span> with baptism as the symbol.</p>
<p><b>Provision </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προνοιαν</span></span>). Old word for forethought (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προνοος</span></span>). In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:2">Acts 24:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>For the flesh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της σαρκος</span></span>). Objective genitive.</p>
<p><b>To fulfil the lusts thereof </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις επιθυμιας</span></span>). "For lusts." No verb. </p>
