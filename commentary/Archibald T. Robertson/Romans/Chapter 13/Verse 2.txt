<p><b> He that resisteth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αντιτασσομενος</span></span>). Present middle articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντιτασσω</span></span>, old verb to range in battle against as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:6">Acts 18:6</span>, "he that lines himself up against."</p>
<p><b>Withstandeth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθεστηκεν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθιστημ</span></span> and intransitive, "has taken his stand against."</p>
<p><b>The ordinance of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη του θεου διαταγη</span></span>). Late word, but common in papyri (Deissmann, <i>Light, etc.</i>, p. 89), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:53">Acts 7:53</span>. Note repetition of root of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τασσω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>To themselves </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαυτοις</span></span>). Dative of disadvantage. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:40">Mark 12:40</span> for "shall receive a judgment" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρινα λημψοντα</span></span>). Future middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανω</span></span>. </p>
