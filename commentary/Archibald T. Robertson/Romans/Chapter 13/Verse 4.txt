<p><b> A minister of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεου διακονος</span></span>). General sense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονος</span></span>. Of course even Nero was God's minister "to thee (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σο</span></span> ethical dative) for good (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το αγαθον</span></span>, for the good)." That is the ideal, the goal.</p>
<p><b>Beareth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φορε</span></span>). Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φορεω</span></span>, old frequentative form of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φερω</span></span>, to bear, to wear.</p>
<p><b>But if thou do </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν δε ποιηις</span></span>). Condition of third class, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span>, "if thou continue to do."</p>
<p><b>Sword </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαχαιραν</span></span>). Symbol of authority as to-day policemen carry clubs or pistols. "The Emperor Trajan presented to a provincial governor on starting for his province, a dagger, with the words, '<i>For me</i>. If I deserve it, <i>in</i> me'" (Vincent).</p>
<p><b>An avenger </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκδικος</span></span>). Old adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικη</span></span> (right), "outside of penalty," unjust, then in later Greek "exacting penalty from one," in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:6">1 Thessalonians 4:6</span>. </p>
