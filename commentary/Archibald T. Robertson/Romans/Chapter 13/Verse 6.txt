<p><b> Ye pay </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελειτε</span></span>). Present active indicative (not imperative) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελεω</span></span>, to fulfil.</p>
<p><b>Tribute </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φορους</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φερω</span></span>, to bring, especially the annual tax on lands, etc. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:22">Luke 20:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:1">Luke 23:1</span>). Paying taxes recognizes authority over us.</p>
<p><b>Ministers of God's service </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λειτουργο θεου</span></span>). Late word for public servant (unused <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λειτος</span></span> from Attic <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεως</span></span>, people, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργω</span></span>, to work). Often used of military servants, servants of the king, and temple servants (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:2">Hebrews 8:2</span>). Paul uses it also of himself as Christ's <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λειτουργος</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:16">Romans 15:16</span>) and of Epaphroditus as a minister to him (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:25">Philippians 2:25</span>). See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεου διακονος</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:4">Romans 13:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>Attending continually </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσκαρτερουντες</span></span>). Present active participle of the late verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσκαρτερεω</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καρτερεω</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καρτος</span></span> or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρατος</span></span>, strength) to persevere. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:42">Acts 2:42</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:13">Acts 8:13</span>. </p>
