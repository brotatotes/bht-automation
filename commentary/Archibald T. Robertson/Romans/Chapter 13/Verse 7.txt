<p><b> Dues </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφειλας</span></span>). Debts, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφειλω</span></span>, to owe. Often so in the papyri, though not in Greek authors. In N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:32">Matthew 18:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:3">1 Corinthians 7:3</span>. Paying debts needs emphasis today, even for ministers.</p>
<p><b>To whom tribute is due </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω τον φορον</span></span>). We must supply a participle with the article <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω</span></span> like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαιτουντ</span></span> ("to the one asking tribute"). So with the other words (to whom custom, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω το τελος απαιτουντ</span></span>; to whom fear, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω τον φοβον απαιτουντ</span></span>; to whom honour, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω την τιμην απαιτουντ</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Φορος</span></span> is the tribute paid to a subject nation (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:22">Luke 20:22</span>), while <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελος</span></span> is tax for support of civil government (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:25">Matthew 17:25</span>). </p>
