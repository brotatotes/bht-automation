<p><b> Him that is weak </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον ασθενουντα</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+8:7-12">1 Corinthians 8:7-12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:22">1 Corinthians 9:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:19">Romans 4:19</span>.</p>
<p><b>Receive ye </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσλαμβανεσθε</span></span>). Present middle imperative (indirect), "take to yourselves."</p>
<p><b>Yet not to doubtful disputations </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη εις διακρισεις διαλογισμων</span></span>). "Not for decisions of opinions." Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span> (between, two or <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυο</span></span>) in both words. Discriminations between doubts or hesitations. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακρισις</span></span>, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:10">1 Corinthians 12:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:14">Hebrews 5:14</span> (only N.T. examples). For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαλογισμος</span></span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:35">Luke 2:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:38">Luke 24:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:14">Philippians 2:14</span>. The "strong" brother is not called upon to settle all the scruples of the "weak" brother. But each takes it on himself to do it. </p>
