<p><b> But thou, why dost thou judge? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συ δε τ συ κρινεισ?</span></span>). Referring to the conduct of the "weak" brother in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:3">Romans 14:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Or thou again </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η κα συ</span></span>). Referring to the "strong" brother.</p>
<p><b>Shall stand before </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραστησομεθα</span></span>). Future middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παριστημ</span></span> and intransitive, to stand beside (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span>) with the locative case (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω βεματ</span></span>, the judgment seat) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:24">Acts 27:24</span>. See the same figure of God in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:10">2 Corinthians 5:10</span>. </p>
