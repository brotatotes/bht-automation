<p><b> Let us not therefore judge one another any more </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηκετ ουν αλληλους κρινωμεν</span></span>). Present active subjunctive (volitive). "Let us no longer have the habit of criticizing one another." A wonderfully fine text for modern Christians and in harmony with what the Master said (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:1">Matthew 7:1</span>).</p>
<p><b>That no man put a stumbling block in his brother's way or an occasion of falling </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το μη τιθενα προσκομμα τω αδελφω η σκανδαλον</span></span>). Articular present active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιθημ</span></span> in apposition with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτο</span></span>, accusative case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρινατε</span></span>: "Judge this rather, the not putting a stumbling block (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:32">Romans 9:32</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσκομμα</span></span>) or a trap (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκανδαλον</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:33">Romans 9:33</span>) for his brother" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδελφω</span></span>, dative of disadvantage). </p>
