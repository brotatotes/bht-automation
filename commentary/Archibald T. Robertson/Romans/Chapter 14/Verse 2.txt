<p><b> One man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος μεν</span></span>). "This one," demonstrative pronoun <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεν</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Hath faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστευε</span></span>). Like <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχε πιστιν</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:9">Acts 14:9</span>).</p>
<p><b>But he that is weak </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο δε ασθενων</span></span>). One would expect <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος δε</span></span> (but that one) in contrast with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος μεν</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hο</span></span> is demonstrative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span> sometimes, but here is probably just the article with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασθενων</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Herbs </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαχανα</span></span>). From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαχανω</span></span>, to dig. Hence garden herbs or vegetables. Denney feels certain that Paul has in mind a party of vegetarians in Rome. </p>
