<p><b> Set at nought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξουθενειτω</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξουθενεω</span></span>, to treat as nothing and so with contempt (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:11">Luke 23:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:20">1 Thessalonians 5:20</span>).</p>
<p><b>Judge </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρινετω</span></span>). Present active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρινω</span></span>, criticize. One side (the meat-eaters) despises the vegetarians, while the vegetarians criticize the meat-eaters.</p>
<p><b>Received him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτον προσελαβετο</span></span>). Aorist middle (indirect) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσλαμβανω</span></span>, same verb used in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:1">Romans 14:1</span>. God took both sides into his fellowship without requiring that they be vegetarians or meat-eaters. </p>
