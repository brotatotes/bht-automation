<p><b> Who art thou? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συ τις ει?</span></span>). Proleptic position of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συ</span></span>, "thou who art thou?"</p>
<p><b>The servant of another </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλοτριον οικετην</span></span>). Not another (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλον</span></span>) servant (household servant, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικετην</span></span>), but "another's servant." For the adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλοτριος</span></span>, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:12">Luke 16:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:15">2 Corinthians 10:15</span>.</p>
<p><b>Shall be made to stand </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σταθησετα</span></span>). Future passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιστημ</span></span>. In spite of your sharp criticisms of one another.</p>
<p><b>Hath power </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυνατε</span></span>). Verb found only in Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:8">2 Corinthians 9:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+13:3">2 Corinthians 13:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:4">Romans 14:4</span>), from verbal adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυνατος</span></span>. </p>
