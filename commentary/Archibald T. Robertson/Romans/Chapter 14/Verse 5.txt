<p><b> One man </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος μεν</span></span>),</p>
<p><b>another </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος δε</span></span>). Regular idiom of contrasted demonstratives (this one, that one).</p>
<p><b>One day above another </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημεραν παρ' ημεραν</span></span>). "Day beyond day." For this use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span> (beside) in comparison see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:25">Romans 1:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:2">Luke 13:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>Be fully assured </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροφορεισθω</span></span>). Present passive imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροφορεω</span></span>, late compound verb for which see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:1">Luke 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:21">Romans 4:21</span>.</p>
<p><b>In his own mind </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω ιδιω νο</span></span>). Intelligent and honest decision according to the light possessed by each. </p>
