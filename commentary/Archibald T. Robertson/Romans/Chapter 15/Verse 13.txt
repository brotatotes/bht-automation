<p><b> The God of hope </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο θεος της ελπιδος</span></span>). Taking up the idea in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:12">Romans 15:12</span> as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:5">Romans 15:5</span> from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:4">Romans 15:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>Fill you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληρωσα υμας</span></span>). Optative (first aorist active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροω</span></span>) of wish for the future. Cf. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δωιη</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:5">Romans 15:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>In believing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω πιστευειν</span></span>). "In the believing" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span> with locative of the articular infinitive, the idiom so common in Luke's Gospel).</p>
<p><b>That ye may abound </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το περισσευειν υμας</span></span>). Purpose clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το</span></span>, as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:8">Romans 15:8</span>, with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περισσευειν</span></span> (present active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περισσευω</span></span>, with accusative of general reference, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμας</span></span>). This verse gathers up the points in the preceding quotations. </p>
