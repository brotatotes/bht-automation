<p><b> I write </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγραψα</span></span>). Epistolary aorist.</p>
<p><b>The more boldly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τολμηροτερως</span></span>). Old comparative adverb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τολμηρως</span></span>. Most MSS. read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τολμηροτερον</span></span>. Only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>In some measure </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο μερους</span></span>). Perhaps referring to some portions of the Epistle where he has spoken plainly (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:12">Romans 6:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:19">Romans 6:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:9">Romans 8:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:17">Romans 11:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:3">Romans 14:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:4">Romans 14:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:10">Romans 14:10</span>, etc.).</p>
<p><b>As putting you again in remembrance </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ος επαναμιμνησκων υμας</span></span>). Delicately put with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span> in the verb, "as if calling back to mind again" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>). This rare verb is here alone in the N.T. </p>
