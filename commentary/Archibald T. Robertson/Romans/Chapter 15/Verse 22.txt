<p><b> I was hindered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεκοπτομην</span></span>). Imperfect passive (repetition) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενκοπτω</span></span>, late verb, to cut in, to cut off, to interrupt. Seen already in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:4">Acts 24:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:18">1 Thessalonians 2:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:7">Galatians 5:7</span>. Cf. modern telephone and radio and automobile.</p>
<p><b>These many times </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα πολλα</span></span>). "As to the many things." In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:13">Romans 1:13</span> Paul used <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολλακις</span></span> (many times) and B D read it here. But Paul's work (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα πολλα</span></span>) had kept him away.</p>
<p><b>From coming to you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του ελθειν προς υμας</span></span>). Ablative case (after the verb of hindering) of the articular infinitive, "from the coming." </p>
