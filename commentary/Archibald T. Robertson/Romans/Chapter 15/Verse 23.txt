<p><b> Having no more any place in these regions </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηκετ τοπον εχων εν τοις κλιμασιν</span></span>). Surprising frankness that the average preacher would hardly use on such a matter. Paul is now free to come to Rome because there is no demand for him where he is. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλιμα</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλινω</span></span>, to incline), slope, then tract of land, region, see already <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:10">2 Corinthians 11:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:21">Galatians 1:21</span> (the only N.T. examples).</p>
<p><b>A longing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιποθειαν</span></span>). A <i>hapax legomenon</i>, elsewhere <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιποθησις</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:7">2 Corinthians 7:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:11">2 Corinthians 7:11</span>), from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιποθεω</span></span> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:11">Romans 1:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>These many years </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο ικανων ετων</span></span>). "From considerable years." So B C, but Aleph A D have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολλων</span></span>, "from many years." </p>
