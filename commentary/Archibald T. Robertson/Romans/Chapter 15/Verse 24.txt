<p><b> Whensoever I go </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως αν πορευωμα</span></span>). Indefinite temporal clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως αν</span></span> and the present middle subjunctive (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:34">1 Corinthians 11:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:23">Philippians 2:23</span> with aorist subjunctive).</p>
<p><b>Into Spain </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις την Σπανιαν</span></span>). It was a Roman province with many Jews in it. The Greek name was <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιβερια</span></span>, the Latin <i>Hispania</i>. The Textus Receptus adds here <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελευσομα προς υμας</span></span> (I shall come to you), but it is not in Aleph A B C D and is not genuine. Without it we have a parenthesis (or anacoluthon) through the rest of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:24">Romans 15:24</span>.</p>
<p><b>In my journey </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαπορευομενος</span></span>). Present middle participle, "passing through." Paul planned only a brief stay in Rome since a strong church already existed there.</p>
<p><b>To be brought on my way thitherward </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προπεμφθηνα εκε</span></span>). "To be sent forward there." First aorist passive infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προπεμπω</span></span>, common word for escorting one on a journey (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:6">1 Corinthians 16:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:11">1 Corinthians 16:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:16">2 Corinthians 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:13">Titus 3:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:6">2 John 1:6</span>).</p>
<p><b>If first in some measure I shall have been satisfied with your company </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν υμων προτων απο μερους εμπλησθω</span></span>). Condition of third class with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμπιμπλημ</span></span>, old verb, to fill up, to satisfy, to take one's fill. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:25">Luke 6:25</span>. Literally, "if I first in part be filled with you" (get my fill of you). delicate compliment for the Roman church. </p>
