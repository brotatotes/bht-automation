<p><b> Their debtors </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφειλετα αυτων</span></span>). Objective genitive: the Gentiles are debtors to the Jews. See the word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφειλετης</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:14">Romans 1:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:12">Romans 8:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>For if </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε γαρ</span></span>). Condition of the first class, assumed as true, first aorist active indicative (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκοινωνησαν</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινωνεω</span></span>, to share) with associative instrumental case (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευματικοις</span></span>, spiritual things).</p>
<p><b>To minister unto </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λειτουργησα</span></span>, first aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λειτουργεω</span></span> with dative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτοις</span></span>, to them), but here certainly with no "sacerdotal" functions (cf. verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:16">Romans 15:16</span>).</p>
<p><b>In carnal things </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τοις σαρκικοις</span></span>). Things which belong to the natural life of the flesh (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαρξ</span></span>), not the sinful aspects of the flesh at all. </p>
