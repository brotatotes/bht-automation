<p><b> When I come </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομενος</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομα</span></span> with the time of the future middle indicative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελευσομα</span></span> (coming I shall come).</p>
<p><b>In the fulness of the blessing of Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν πληρωματ ευλογιας Χριστου</span></span>). On <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληρωματ</span></span>, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:12">Romans 11:12</span>. Paul had already (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:11">Romans 1:11</span>) said that he had a <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρισμα πνευματικον</span></span> (spiritual blessing) for Rome. He did bring that to them. </p>
