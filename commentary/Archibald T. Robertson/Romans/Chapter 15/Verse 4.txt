<p><b> Were written aforetime </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προεγραφη</span></span>). Second aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προγραφω</span></span>, old verb, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:1">Galatians 3:1</span> (which see); <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:3">Ephesians 3:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jdg+1:4">Judges 1:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>For our learning </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις την ημετεραν διδασκαλιαν</span></span>). "For the instruction of us." Objective sense of possessive pronoun <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημετερος</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:9">Matthew 15:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:16">2 Timothy 3:16</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδασκαλιαν</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδασκω</span></span>, to teach).</p>
<p><b>We might have hope </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την ελπιδα εχωμεν</span></span>). Present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχω</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> in final clause, "that we might keep on having hope." One of the blessed uses of the Scriptures. </p>
