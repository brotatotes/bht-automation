<p><b> The God of patience and comfort </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο θεος της υπομονης κα της παρακλησεως</span></span>). Genitive case of the two words in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:4">Romans 15:4</span> used to describe God who uses the Scriptures to reveal himself to us. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:3">2 Corinthians 1:3</span> for this idea; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:13">Romans 15:13</span> for "the God of hope"; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:33">Romans 15:33</span> for "the God of peace."</p>
<p><b>Grant you </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δωιη υμιν</span></span>). Second aorist active optative (<i>Koine</i> form for older <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοιη</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:16">2 Thessalonians 3:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:17">Ephesians 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:16">2 Timothy 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:18">2 Timothy 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:25">2 Timothy 2:25</span>, though MSS. vary in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:17">Ephesians 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:25">2 Timothy 2:25</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δωη</span></span> (subjunctive). The optative here is for a wish for the future (regular idiom).</p>
<p><b>According to Christ Jesus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα Χριστον Ιησουν</span></span>). "According to the character or example of Christ Jesus" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:17">2 Corinthians 11:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:8">Colossians 2:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:24">Ephesians 5:24</span>). </p>
