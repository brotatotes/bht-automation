<p><b> With one accord </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομοθυμαδον</span></span>). Here alone in Paul, but eleven times in Acts (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:14">Acts 1:14</span>, etc.).</p>
<p><b>With one mouth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν εν στοματ</span></span>). Vivid outward expression of the unity of feeling.</p>
<p><b>May glorify </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξαζητε</span></span>). Present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξαζω</span></span>, final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> "that ye may keep on glorifying." For "the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ" see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:3">2 Corinthians 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:31">2 Corinthians 9:31</span> for discussion. It occurs also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:3">Ephesians 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:3">1 Peter 1:3</span>. </p>
