<p><b> And that the Gentiles might praise </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα δε εθνη δοξασα</span></span>). Coordinate with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βεβαιωσα</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το</span></span>, to be repeated with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα εθνη</span></span>, the accusative of general reference and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον θεον</span></span> the object of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξασα</span></span>. Thus the Gentiles were called through the promise to the Jews in the covenant with Abraham (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:11">Romans 4:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:16">Romans 4:16</span>). Salvation is of the Jews. Paul proves his position by a chain of quotations from the O.T., the one in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:9">Romans 15:9</span> from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+18:50">Psalms 18:50</span>. For <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξομολογεω</span></span>, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:10">Romans 14:10</span>.</p>
