<p><b> Mark </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκοπειτε</span></span>). Keep an eye on so as to avoid. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Σκοπος</span></span> is the goal, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκοπεω</span></span> means keeping your eye on the goal.</p>
<p><b>Divisions </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διχοστασιας</span></span>). Old word for "standings apart," cleavages. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:20">Galatians 5:20</span>.</p>
<p><b>Those which are causing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουσ--ποιουντας</span></span>). This articular participle clause has within it not only the objects of the participle but the relative clause <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην υμεις εμαθετε</span></span> (which you learned), a thoroughly Greek idiom. </p>
