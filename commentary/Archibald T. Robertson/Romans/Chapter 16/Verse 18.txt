<p><b> But their own belly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα τη εαυτων κοιλια</span></span>). Dative case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δουλευουσιν</span></span>. A blunt phrase like the same picture in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:19">Philippians 3:19</span> "whose god is the belly," more truth than caricature in some cases.</p>
<p><b>By their smooth and fair speech </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια της χρηστολογιας κα ευλογιας</span></span>). Two compounds of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογος</span></span> (speech), the first (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρηστος</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογος</span></span>) is very rare (here only in N.T.), the second is very common (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευ</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογος</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Beguile </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαπατωσιν</span></span>). Present active indicative of the double compound verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαπαταω</span></span> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:3">2 Thessalonians 2:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:18">1 Corinthians 3:18</span>).</p>
<p><b>Of the innocent </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των ακακων</span></span>). Old adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακος</span></span>), without evil or guile, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:26">Hebrews 7:26</span> (of Christ). </p>
