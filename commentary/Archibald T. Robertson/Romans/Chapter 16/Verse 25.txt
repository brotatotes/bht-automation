Verses Romans 16:25-27 conclude the noble Epistle with the finest of Paul's doxologies.To him that is able  (τω δυναμενω). Dative of the articular participle of δυναμα. See similar idiom in Ephesians 3:20.To stablish  (στηριξα). First aorist active infinitive of στηριζω, to make stable.According to my gospel  (κατα το ευαγγελιον μου). Same phrase in Romans 2:16; 2 Timothy 2:8. Not a book, but Paul's message as here set forth.The preaching  (το κηρυγμα). The proclamation, the heralding.Of Jesus Christ  (Ιησου Χριστου). Objective genitive, "about Jesus Christ."Revelation  (αποκαλυψιν). "Unveiling."Of the mystery  (μυστηριου). Once unknown, but now revealed.Kept in silence  (σεσιγημενου). Perfect passive participle of σιγαω, to be silent, state of silence.Through times eternal  (χρονοις αιωνιοις). Associative instrumental case, "along with times eternal" (Robertson, Grammar, p. 527). See 1 Corinthians 2:6; 1 Corinthians 2:7; 1 Corinthians 2:10.
