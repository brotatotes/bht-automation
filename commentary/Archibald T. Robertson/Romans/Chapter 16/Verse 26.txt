<p><b> But now is manifested </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανερωθεντος δε νυν</span></span>). First aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανεροω</span></span>, to make plain, genitive case in agreement with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυστηριου</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>By the scriptures of the prophets </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια γραφων προφητικων</span></span>). "By prophetic scriptures." Witnessed by the law and the prophets (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:21">Romans 3:21</span>). This thread runs all through Romans.</p>
<p><b>According to the command of the eternal God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατ' επιταγην του αιωνιου θεου</span></span>). Paul conceives that God is in charge of the redemptive work and gives his orders (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:1-5">Romans 1:1-5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:15">Romans 10:15</span>). The same adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιωνιος</span></span> is here applied to God that is used of eternal life and eternal punishment in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:46">Matthew 25:46</span>.</p>
<p><b>Unto obedience of faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις υπακοην της πιστεως</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:5">Romans 1:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>Made known unto all the nations </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις παντα τα εθνη γνωρισθεντος</span></span>). First aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνωριζω</span></span>, still the genitive case agreeing with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυστηριου</span></span> in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:25">Romans 16:25</span>. </p>
