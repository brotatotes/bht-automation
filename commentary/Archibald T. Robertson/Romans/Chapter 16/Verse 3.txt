<p>In verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:3-16">Romans 16:3-16</span> Paul sends his greetings to various brethren and sisters in Rome.</p>
<p><b>Prisca and Aquila </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Πρισκαν κα Ακυλαν</span></span>). This order always (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:18">Acts 18:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:26">Acts 18:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:19">2 Timothy 4:19</span>, and here) save in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:2">Acts 18:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:19">1 Corinthians 16:19</span>, showing that Prisca was the more prominent. Priscilla is a diminutive of Prisca, a name for women in the Acilian gens. She may have been a noble Roman lady, but her husband was a Jew of Pontus and a tent-maker by trade. They were driven from Rome by Claudius, came to Corinth, then to Ephesus, then back to Rome, and again to Ephesus. They were good travelling Christians.</p>
<p><b>My fellow-workers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους συνεργους μου</span></span>). Both in tent-making and in Christian service in Corinth and Ephesus. </p>
