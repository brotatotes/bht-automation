<p><b> The church that is in their house </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την κατ' οικον αυτων εκκλησιαν</span></span>). The early Christians had no church buildings. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:2">Acts 12:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:19">1 Corinthians 16:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:2">Philemon 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+4:15">Colossians 4:15</span>. The Roman Christians had probably several such homes where they would meet.</p>
<p><b>Epainetus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Επαινετον</span></span>). Nothing is known of him except this item, "the first-fruits of Asia" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαρχη της Ασιας</span></span>). An early convert from the province of Asia. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:9">Acts 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:15">1 Corinthians 16:15</span> (about Stephanus and Achaia). </p>
