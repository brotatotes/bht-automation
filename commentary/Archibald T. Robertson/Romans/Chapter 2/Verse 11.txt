<p><b> Respect of persons </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσωπολημψια</span></span>). Milligan (<i>Vocabulary</i>) considers this word (in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:25">Colossians 3:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:9">Ephesians 6:9</span>) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσωπολημπτης</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:34">Acts 10:34</span>) and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσωπολημπτεω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:9">James 2:9</span>) the earliest definitely known Christian words, not in LXX or non-Christian writings. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:34">Acts 10:34</span> for the formation in imitation of the Hebrew to take note of the face (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσωπον, λαμβανω</span></span>), to judge by the face or appearance. </p>
