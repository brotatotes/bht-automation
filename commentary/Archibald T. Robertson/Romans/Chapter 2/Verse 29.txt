<p><b> Who is one inwardly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο εν τω κρυπτω</span></span>). Repeat <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιουδαιος</span></span> (Jew) here also, "the in the inward part Jew" (circumcision of the heart <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιτομη καρδιας</span></span> and not a mere surgical operation as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:11">Colossians 2:11</span>, in the spirit <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν πνευματ</span></span>, with which compare <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:3">2 Corinthians 3:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:6">2 Corinthians 3:6</span>). This inward or inside Jew who lives up to his covenant relation with God is the high standard that Paul puts before the merely professional Jew described above.</p>
<p><b>Whose praise </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου ο επαινος</span></span>). The antecedent of the relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου</span></span> is <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιουδαιος</span></span> (Jew). Probably (Gifford) a reference to the etymology of Judah (praise) as seen in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+49:8">Galatians 49:8</span>. </p>
