<p><b> And doest the same </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα ποιων αυτα</span></span>). "And doest them occasionally."</p>
<p><b>That thou shalt escape </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συ εκφευξη</span></span>). Emphasis on <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συ</span></span>, "thou conceited Jew expecting to escape God's <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κριμα</span></span> because thou art a Jew." Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:8">Matthew 3:8</span>. Paul justifies the bitter words of the Baptist to the Pharisees and Sadducees. The future middle of the old verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκφευγω</span></span> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:3">1 Thessalonians 5:3</span>). The Jew posed as immune to the ordinary laws of ethics because a Jew. Alas, some Christians affect the same immunity. </p>
