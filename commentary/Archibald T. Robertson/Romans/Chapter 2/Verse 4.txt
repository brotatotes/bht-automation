<p><b> Or despiseth thou? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η καταφρονεισ?</span></span>). Another alternative, that of scorn of God's kindness (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρηστοτητος</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:6">2 Corinthians 6:6</span>) and forbearance (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανοχης</span></span>, old word, holding back from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεχω</span></span>, only here in N.T.) and longsuffering (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακροθυμιας</span></span>, late word for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:4">2 Corinthians 6:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:6">2 Corinthians 6:6</span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Καταφρονεω</span></span> is old verb to think down on (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα, φρονεω</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:24">Matthew 6:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:22">1 Corinthians 11:22</span>. This upstart Jew actually thinks down on God. And then "the riches" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του πλουτου</span></span>) of all that comes from God.</p>
<p><b>Leadeth thee to repentance </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις μετανοιαν σε αγε</span></span>). The very kindness (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το χρηστον</span></span>, the kindly quality) of God is trying to lead (conative present <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγε</span></span>) thee to a right-about face, a change of mind and attitude (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετανοιαν</span></span>) instead of a complacent self-satisfaction and pride of race and privilege. </p>
