<p><b> After thy hardness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα την σκληροτητα σου</span></span>). "According to thy hardness (old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκληρος</span></span>, hard, stiff, only here in N.T.) will God's judgment be."</p>
<p><b>And impenitent heart </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα αμετανοητον καρδιαν</span></span>). See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετανοιαν</span></span> just before. "Thy unreconstructed heart," "with no change in the attitude of thy heart."</p>
<p><b>Treasurest up for thyself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θησαυριζεις σεαυτω</span></span>). See for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θησαυριζω</span></span> on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:19">Matthew 6:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:21">Luke 12:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:14">2 Corinthians 12:14</span>. Dative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σεαυτω</span></span> (for thyself) with a touch of irony (Vincent).</p>
<p><b>Wrath </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οργην</span></span>). For such a Jew as already stated for the Gentile (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:18">Romans 1:18</span>). There is a revelation (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκαλυψεως</span></span>) of God's wrath for both in the day of wrath and righteous judgment (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιοκρισιας</span></span>, a late compound word, in LXX, two examples in the Oxyrhynchus papyri, only here in N.T.). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:5">2 Thessalonians 1:5</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιας κρισεως</span></span>. Paul looks to the judgment day as certain (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:10-12">2 Corinthians 5:10-12</span>), the day of the Lord (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:14">2 Corinthians 1:14</span>). </p>
