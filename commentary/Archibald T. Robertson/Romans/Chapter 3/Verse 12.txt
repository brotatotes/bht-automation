<p><b> They are together become unprofitable </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμα ηχρεωθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχρεοω</span></span>. Late word in Polybius and Cilician inscription of first century A.D. Some MSS. read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηχρειωθησαν</span></span> from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχρειος</span></span>, useless (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρειος</span></span>, useful) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:10">Luke 17:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:30">Matthew 25:30</span>, but Westcott and Hort print as above from the rarer spelling <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχρεος</span></span>. Only here in N.T. The Hebrew word means to go bad, become sour like milk (Lightfoot).</p>
<p><b>No, not so much as one </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ εστιν εως ενος</span></span>). "There is not up to one." </p>
