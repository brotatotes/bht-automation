<p><b> Throat </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαρυγξ</span></span>). Old word, larynx.</p>
<p><b>Open sepulchre </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταφος ανεωιγμενος</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανοιγω</span></span>, "an opened grave." Their mouth (words) like the odour of a newly opened grave. "Some portions of Greek and Roman literature stink like a newly opened grave" (Shedd).</p>
<p><b>They have used deceit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδολιουσαν</span></span>). Imperfect (not perfect or aorist as the English implies) active of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δολιοω</span></span>, only in LXX and here in the N.T. from the common adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δολιος</span></span>, deceitful (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:13">2 Corinthians 11:13</span>). The regular form would be <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδολιουν</span></span>. The <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">-οσαν</span></span> ending for third plural in imperfect and aorist was once thought to be purely Alexandrian because so common in the LXX, but it is common in the Boeotian and Aeolic dialects and occurs in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειχοσαν</span></span> in the N.T. (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:22">John 15:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:24">John 15:24</span>). "They smoothed their tongues" in the Hebrew.</p>
<p><b>Poison </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιος</span></span>). Old word both for rust (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:3">James 5:3</span>) and poison (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:8">James 3:8</span>).</p>
<p><b>Of asps </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασπιδων</span></span>). Common word for round bowl, shield, then the Egyptian cobra (a deadly serpent). Often in LXX. Only here in the N.T. The poison of the asp lies in a bag under the lips (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χειλη</span></span>), often in LXX, only here in N.T. Genitive case after <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεμε</span></span> (is full). </p>
