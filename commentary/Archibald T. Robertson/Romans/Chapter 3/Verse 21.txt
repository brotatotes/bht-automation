<p><b> But now apart from the law </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυν δε χωρις νομου</span></span>). He now (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυν</span></span> emphatic logical transition) proceeds carefully in verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:21-31">Romans 3:21-31</span> the</p>
<p><b>nature </b> of the God-kind of righteousness which stands manifested (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιοσυνη θεου πεφανερωτα</span></span>, perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανεροω</span></span>, to make manifest), the</p>
<p><b>necessity </b> of which he has shown in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:18-3">Romans 1:18-3</span>. This God kind of righteousness is "apart from law" of any kind and all of grace (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριτ</span></span>) as he will show in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:24">Romans 3:24</span>. But it is not a new discovery on the part of Paul, but "witnessed by the law and the prophets" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρουμενη</span></span>, present passive participle, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο του νομου κα των προφητων</span></span>), made plain continuously by God himself. </p>
