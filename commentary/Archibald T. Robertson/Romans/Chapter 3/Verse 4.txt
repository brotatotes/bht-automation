<p><b> Let God be found true </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινεσθω ο θεος αληθης</span></span>). "Let God continue to be true" (present middle imperative).</p>
<p><b>But every man a liar </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πας δε ανθρωπος ψευστης</span></span>). The contrast in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span> really means, "though every man be found a liar." Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+116:12">Psalms 116:12</span>.</p>
<p><b>As it is written </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθως γεγραπτα</span></span>). <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+51:6">Psalms 51:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>That thou mightest be justified </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπως αν δικαιωθηις</span></span>). <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hοπως</span></span> rather than the common <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> for purpose and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span> with the first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιοω</span></span>. Used of God this verb here has to mean "declared righteous," not "made righteous."</p>
<p><b>Mightest prevail </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νικησεις</span></span>). Future active indicative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπως</span></span> of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νικαω</span></span>, to win a victory, though B L have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νικησηις</span></span> (first aorist active subjunctive, the usual construction).</p>
<p><b>When thou comest into judgement </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω κρινεσθα σε</span></span>). "In the being judged as to thee" (present passive infinitive or, if taken as middle, "in the entering upon trial as to thee"). Common construction in the LXX from the Hebrew infinitive construct. </p>
