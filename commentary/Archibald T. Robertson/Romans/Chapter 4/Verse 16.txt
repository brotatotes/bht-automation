<p><b> Of faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ πιστεως</span></span>). As the source.</p>
<p><b>According to grace </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα χαριν</span></span>). As the pattern.</p>
<p><b>To the end that </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το εινα</span></span>). Purpose again as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:11">Romans 4:11</span>.</p>
<p><b>Sure </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βεβαιαν</span></span>). Stable, fast, firm. Old adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βαινω</span></span>, to walk.</p>
<p><b>Not to that only which is of the law </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου τω εκ του νομου μονον</span></span>). Another instance where <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μονον</span></span> (see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:12">Romans 4:12</span>) seems in the wrong place. Normally the order would be, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μονον τω εκ του νομου, αλλα κα κτλ</span></span>. </p>
