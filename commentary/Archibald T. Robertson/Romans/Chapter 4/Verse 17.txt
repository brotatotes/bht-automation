<p><b> A father of many nations </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πατερα πολλων εθνων</span></span>). Quotation from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+17:5">Genesis 17:5</span>. Only true in the sense of spiritual children as already explained, father of believers in God.</p>
<p><b>Before him whom he believed even God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεναντ ου επιστευσεν θεου</span></span>). Incorporation of antecedent into the relative clause and attraction of the relative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ω</span></span> into <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:2">Mark 11:2</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεναντ</span></span>, "right in front of."</p>
<p><b>Calleth the things that are not as though they were </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλουντος τα μη οντα ως οντα</span></span>). "Summons the non-existing as existing." Abraham's body was old and decrepit. God rejuvenated him and Sarah (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:19">Hebrews 11:19</span>). </p>
