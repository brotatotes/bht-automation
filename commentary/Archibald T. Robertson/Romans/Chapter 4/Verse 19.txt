<p><b> Without being weakened in faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη ασθενησας τη πιστε</span></span>). "Not becoming weak in faith." Ingressive first aorist active participle with negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Now as good as dead </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηδη νενεκρωμενον</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νεκροω</span></span>, "now already dead." B omits <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηδη</span></span>. He was, he knew, too old to become father of a child.</p>
<p><b>About </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">που</span></span>). The addition of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">που</span></span> (somewhere, about) "qualifies the exactness of the preceding numeral" (Vaughan). The first promise of a son to Abraham and Sarah came (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+15:3">Genesis 15:3</span>) before the birth of Ishmael (86 when Ishmael was born). The second promise came when Abraham was 99 years old (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+17:1">Genesis 17:1</span>), calling himself 100 (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+17:17">Genesis 17:17</span>). </p>
