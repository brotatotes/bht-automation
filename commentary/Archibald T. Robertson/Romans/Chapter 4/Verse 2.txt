<p><b> The Scripture </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η γραφη</span></span>). <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+15:6">Genesis 15:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Was justified by works </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ εργων εδικαιωθη</span></span>). Condition of first class, assumed as true for the sake of argument, though untrue in fact. The rabbis had a doctrine of the merits of Abraham who had a superfluity of credits to pass on to the Jews (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:8">Luke 3:8</span>).</p>
<p><b>But not towards God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ' ου προς θεον</span></span>). Abraham deserved all the respect from men that came to him, but his relation to God was a different matter. He had <i>there</i> no ground of boasting at all. </p>
