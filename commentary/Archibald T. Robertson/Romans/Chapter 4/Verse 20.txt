<p><b> He wavered not through unbelief </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου διεκριθη τη απιστια</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of old and common verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακρινω</span></span>, to separate, to distinguish between, to decide between, to desert, to dispute, to be divided in one's own mind. This last sense occurs here as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:22">Matthew 21:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:23">Mark 11:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:23">Romans 14:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:6">James 1:6</span>. "He was not divided in his mind by unbelief" (instrumental case).</p>
<p><b>Waxed strong through faith </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεδυναμωθη τη πιστε</span></span>). First aorist passive again of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδυναμοω</span></span>, late word to empower, to put power in, in LXX and Paul and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:22">Acts 9:22</span>. </p>
