<p><b> Being fully assured </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροφορηθεις</span></span>). First aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροφορεω</span></span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροφορος</span></span> and this from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληρης</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φερω</span></span>, to bear or bring full (full measure), to settle fully. Late word, first in LXX but frequent in papyri in sense of finishing off or paying off. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:1">Luke 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:5">Romans 14:5</span>.</p>
<p><b>What he had promised </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο επηγγελτα</span></span>). Perfect middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαγγελλομα</span></span>, to promise, retained in indirect discourse according to usual Greek idiom.</p>
<p><b>He was able </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυνατος εστιν</span></span>). Present active indicative retained in indirect discourse. The verbal adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυνατος</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστιν</span></span> is here used in sense of the verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυνατα</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:31">Luke 14:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:17">Acts 11:17</span>). </p>
