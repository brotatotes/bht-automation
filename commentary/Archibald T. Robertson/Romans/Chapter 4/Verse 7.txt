<p><b> Blessed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακαριο</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:3">Matthew 5:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Are forgiven </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφεθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφιημ</span></span>, without augment (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφειθησαν</span></span>, regular form). Paul quotes <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+32:1">Psalms 32:1</span> and as from David. Paul thus confirms his interpretation of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+15:6">Genesis 15:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Iniquities </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανομια</span></span>). Violations of law whereas <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτια</span></span> (sins) include all kinds.</p>
<p><b>Are covered </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεκαλυφθησαν</span></span>). First aorist passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επικαλυπτω</span></span>, old verb, to cover over (upon, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>) as a shroud. Only here in N.T. </p>
