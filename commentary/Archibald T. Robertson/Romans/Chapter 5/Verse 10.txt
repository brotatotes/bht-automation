<p><b> We were reconciled to God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηλλαγημεν τω θεω</span></span>). Second aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλλασσω</span></span> for which great Pauline word see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:18">2 Corinthians 5:18</span>. The condition is the first class. Paul does not conceive it as his or our task to reconcile God to us. God has attended to that himself (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:25">Romans 3:25</span>). We become reconciled to God by means of the death of God's Son. "Much more" again we shall be saved "by his life" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη ζωη αυτου</span></span>). "In his life," for he does live, "ever living to intercede for them" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:25">Hebrews 7:25</span>). </p>
