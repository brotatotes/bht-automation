<p><b> But not as the trespass </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλ' ουχ ως</span></span>). It is more contrast than parallel: "the trespass" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το παραπτωμα</span></span>, the slip, fall to one side) over against the free gift (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το χαρισμα</span></span>, of grace <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρις</span></span>).</p>
<p><b>Much more </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολλω μαλλον</span></span>). Another <i>a fortiori</i> argument. Why so? As a God of love he delights</p>
<p><b>much more </b> in showing mercy and pardon than in giving just punishment (Lightfoot). The gift surpasses the sin. It is not necessary to Paul's argument to make "the many" in each case correspond, one relates to Adam, the other to Christ. </p>
