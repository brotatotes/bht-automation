<p><b> So then </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρα ουν</span></span>). Conclusion of the argument. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:3">Romans 7:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:25">Romans 7:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:12">Romans 8:12</span>, etc. Paul resumes the parallel between Adam and Christ begun in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:12">Romans 5:12</span> and interrupted by explanation (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:13">Romans 5:13</span>) and contrast (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:15-17">Romans 5:15-17</span>).</p>
<p><b>Through one trespass </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι' ενος παραπτωματος</span></span>). That of Adam.</p>
<p><b>Through one act of righteousness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι' ενος δικαιωματος</span></span>). That of Christ. The first "unto all men" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις παντας ανθρωπους</span></span>) as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:12">Romans 5:12</span>, the second as in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:17">Romans 5:17</span> "they that receive, etc." </p>
