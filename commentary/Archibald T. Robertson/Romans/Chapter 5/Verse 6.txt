<p><b> For </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετ γαρ</span></span>). So most documents, but B reads <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ε γε</span></span> which Westcott and Hort use in place of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γαρ</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>While we were yet weak </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οντων ημων ασθενων ετ</span></span>). Genitive absolute. The second <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετ</span></span> (yet) here probably gave rise to the confusion of text over <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετ γαρ</span></span> above.</p>
<p><b>In due season </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα καιρον</span></span>). Christ came into the world at the proper time, the fulness of the time (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:4">Galatians 4:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:10">Ephesians 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:3">Titus 1:3</span>).</p>
<p><b>I or the ungodly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ ασεβων</span></span>). In behalf, instead of. See about <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ</span></span> on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:13">Galatians 3:13</span> and also verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:7">Romans 5:7</span> here. </p>
