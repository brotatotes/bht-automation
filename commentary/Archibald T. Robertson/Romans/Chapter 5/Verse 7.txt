<p><b> Scarcely </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μολις</span></span>). Common adverb from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μολος</span></span>, toil. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:18">Acts 14:18</span>. As between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιος</span></span>, righteous, and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαθος</span></span>, good, Lightfoot notes "all the difference in the world" which he shows by quotations from Plato and Christian writers, a difference of sympathy mainly, the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιος</span></span> man being "absolutely without sympathy" while the <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαθος</span></span> man "is beneficent and kind."</p>
<p><b>Would even dare </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα τολμα</span></span>). Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τολμαω</span></span>, to have courage. "Even dares to." Even so in the case of the kindly sympathetic man courage is called for to make the supreme sacrifice.</p>
<p><b>Perhaps </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταχα</span></span>). Common adverb (perhaps instrumental case) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταχυς</span></span> (swift). Only here in N.T. </p>
