<p><b> Neither present </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηδε παριστανετε</span></span>). Present active imperative in prohibition of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παριστανω</span></span>, late form of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παριστημ</span></span>, to place beside. Stop presenting your members or do not have the habit of doing so, "do not go on putting your members to sin as weapons of unrighteousness."</p>
<p><b>Instruments </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπλα</span></span>). Old word for tools of any kind for shop or war (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:3">John 18:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:7">2 Corinthians 6:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:4">2 Corinthians 10:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:12">Romans 13:12</span>). Possibly here figure of two armies arrayed against each other (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:16-24">Galatians 5:16-24</span>), and see <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπλα δικαιοσυνης</span></span> below. The two sets of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οπλα</span></span> clash.</p>
<p><b>But present yourselves unto God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα παραστησατε εαυτους τω θεω</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παριστημ</span></span>, same verb, but different tense, do it now and completely. Our "members" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μελη</span></span>) should be at the call of God "as alive from the dead." </p>
