<p><b> Beguiled me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξηπατησεν με</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαπαταω</span></span>, old verb, completely (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ</span></span>) made me lose my way (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πατεω</span></span>, to walk). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:18">1 Corinthians 3:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:3">2 Corinthians 11:3</span>. Only in Paul in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Slew me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεκτεινεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκτεινω</span></span>, old verb. "Killed me off," made a clean job of it. Sin here is personified as the tempter (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ge+3:13">Genesis 3:13</span>). </p>
