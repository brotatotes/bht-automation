<p><b> Become death unto me? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμο εγενετο θανατοσ?</span></span>). Ethical dative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμο</span></span> again. New turn to the problem. Admitting the goodness of God's law, did it issue in death for me? Paul repels (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη γενοιτο</span></span>) this suggestion. It was sin that (But sin, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα η αμαρτια</span></span>) "became death for me."</p>
<p><b>That it might be shown </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα φανη</span></span>). Final clause, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and second aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φαινω</span></span>, to show. The sinfulness of sin is revealed in its violations of God's law.</p>
<p><b>By working death to me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μο κατεργαζομενη θανατον</span></span>). Present middle participle, as an incidental result.</p>
<p><b>Might become exceedingly sinful </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενητα καθ' υπερβολην αμαρτωλος</span></span>). Second aorist middle subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινομα</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> in final clause. On <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθ' υπερβολην</span></span>, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:31">1 Corinthians 12:31</span>. Our <i>hyperbole</i> is the Greek <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερβολη</span></span>. The excesses of sin reveal its real nature. Only then do some people get their eyes opened. </p>
