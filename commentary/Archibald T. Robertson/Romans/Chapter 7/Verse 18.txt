<p><b> In me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν εμο</span></span>). Paul explains this by "in my flesh" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη σαρκ μου</span></span>), the unregenerate man "sold under sin" of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:14">Romans 7:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>No good thing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ--αγαθον</span></span>). "Not absolutely good." This is not a complete view of man even in his unregenerate state as Paul at once shows.</p>
<p><b>For to will is present with me </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το γαρ θελειν παρακειτα μο</span></span>). Present middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρακειμα</span></span>, old verb, to lie beside, at hand, with dative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μο</span></span>. Only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>The wishing </b> is the better self,</p>
<p><b>the doing not </b> the lower self. </p>
