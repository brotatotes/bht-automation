<p><b> The wife that hath a husband </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η υπανδρος γυνη</span></span>). Late word, under (in subjection to) a husband. Here only in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Is bound </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεδετα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative, stands bound.</p>
<p><b>By law </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νομω</span></span>). Instrumental case.</p>
<p><b>To the husband while he liveth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω ζωντ ανδρ</span></span>). "To the living husband," literally.</p>
<p><b>But if the husband die </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν δε αποθανη ο ανηρ</span></span>). Third class condition, a supposable case (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν</span></span> and the second aorist active subjunctive).</p>
<p><b>She is discharged </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηργητα</span></span>). Perfect passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταργεω</span></span>, to make void. She stands free from the law of the husband. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:6">Romans 6:6</span>. </p>
