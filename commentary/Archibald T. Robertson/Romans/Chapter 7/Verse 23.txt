<p><b> A different law </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετερον νομον</span></span>). For the distinction between <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετερος</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλος</span></span>, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:6">Galatians 1:6</span>.</p>
<p><b>Warring against </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντιστρατευομενον</span></span>). Rare verb (<i>Xenophon</i>) to carry on a campaign against. Only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>The law of my mind </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω νομω του νοος</span></span>). The reflective intelligence Paul means by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νοος</span></span>, "the inward man" of verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:22">Romans 7:22</span>. It is this higher self that agrees that the law of God is good (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:12">Romans 7:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:16">Romans 7:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:22">Romans 7:22</span>).</p>
<p><b>Bringing me into captivity </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιχμαλωτιζοντα</span></span>). See on this late and vivid verb for capture and slavery <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:24">Luke 21:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:5">2 Corinthians 10:5</span>. Surely it is a tragic picture drawn by Paul with this outcome, "sold under sin" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:14">Romans 7:14</span>), "captivity to the law of sin" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:23">Romans 7:23</span>). The ancient writers (Plato, Ovid, Seneca, Epictetus) describe the same dual struggle in man between his conscience and his deeds. </p>
