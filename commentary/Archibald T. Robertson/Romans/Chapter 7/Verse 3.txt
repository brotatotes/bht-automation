<p><b> While the husband liveth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωντος του ανδρος</span></span>). Genitive absolute of present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζαω</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>She shall be called </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρηματισε</span></span>). Future active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρηματιζω</span></span>, old verb, to receive a name as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:26">Acts 11:26</span>, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρημα</span></span>, business, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χραομα</span></span>, to use, then to give an oracle, etc.</p>
<p><b>An adulteress </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μοιχαλις</span></span>). Late word, in Plutarch, LXX. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:39">Matthew 12:39</span>.</p>
<p><b>If she be joined </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εαν γενητα</span></span>). Third class condition, "if she come to."</p>
<p><b>So that she is no adulteress </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του μη εινα αυτην μοιχαλιδα</span></span>). It is a fact that <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του</span></span> and the infinitive is used for result as we saw in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:24">Romans 1:24</span>. Conceived result may explain the idiom here. </p>
