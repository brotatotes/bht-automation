<p><b> In the flesh </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη σαρκ</span></span>). Same sense as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:19">Romans 6:19</span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:18">Romans 7:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:25">Romans 7:25</span>. The "flesh" is not inherently sinful, but is subject to sin. It is what Paul means by being "under the law." He uses <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαρξ</span></span> in a good many senses.</p>
<p><b>Sinful passions </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα παθηματα των αμαρτιων</span></span>). "Passions of sins" or marked by sins.</p>
<p><b>Wrought </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεργειτο</span></span>). Imperfect middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεργεω</span></span>, "were active."</p>
<p><b>To bring forth fruit unto death </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το καρποφορησα τω θανατω</span></span>). Purpose clause again. Vivid picture of the seeds of sin working for death. </p>
