<p><b> The Spirit himself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτο το πνευμα</span></span>). The grammatical gender of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμα</span></span> is neuter as here, but the Greek used also the natural gender as we do exclusively as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:13">John 16:13</span> <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινος</span></span> (masculine</p>
<p><b>he </b>), <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το πνευμα</span></span> (neuter). See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:26">John 16:26</span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο--εκεινος</span></span>). It is a grave mistake to use the neuter "it" or "itself" when referring to the Holy Spirit.</p>
<p><b>Beareth witness with our spirit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμμαρτυρε τω πνευματ ημων</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:15">Romans 2:15</span> for this verb with associative instrumental case. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:10">1 John 5:10</span> for this double witness. </p>
