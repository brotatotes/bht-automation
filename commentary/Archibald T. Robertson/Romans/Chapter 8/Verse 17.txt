<p><b> Joint-heirs with Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνκληρονομο Χριστου</span></span>). A late rare double compound, in Philo, an Ephesian inscription of the imperial period (Deissmann, <i>Light from the Ancient East</i>, p. 92), papyri of the Byzantine period. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:29">Romans 8:29</span> for this idea expanded. Paul is fond of compounds of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν</span></span>, three in this verse (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνκληρονομοι, συνπασχωμεν, συνδοξασθωμεν</span></span>). The last (first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνδοξαζω</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> (purpose), late and rare, here only in N.T. </p>
