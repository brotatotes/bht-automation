<p><b> Was subjected </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπεταγη</span></span>). Second aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπατασσω</span></span> (cf. verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:7">Romans 8:7</span>).</p>
<p><b>To vanity </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη ματαιοτητ</span></span>). Dative case. Rare and late word, common in LXX. From <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ματαιος</span></span>, empty, vain. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:17">Ephesians 4:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:18">2 Peter 2:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>Not of its own will </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουχ εκουσα</span></span>). Common adjective, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:27">1 Corinthians 9:27</span>. It was due to the effect of man's sin.</p>
<p><b>But by reason of him </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα δια τον</span></span>). Because of God.</p>
<p><b>In hope that </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφ' ελπιδ οτ</span></span>). Note the form <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελπιδ</span></span> rather than the usual <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελπιδ</span></span> and so <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφ'</span></span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Hοτ</span></span> can be causal "because" instead of declarative "that." </p>
