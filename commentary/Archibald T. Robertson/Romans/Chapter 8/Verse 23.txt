<p><b> The first fruits </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την απαρχην</span></span>). Old and common metaphor.</p>
<p><b>Of the Spirit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του πνευματος</span></span>). The genitive of apposition. The Holy Spirit came on the great Pentecost and his blessings continue as seen in the "gifts" in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:1">Romans 8:1</span>, in the moral and spiritual gifts of <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:22">Galatians 5:22</span>. And greater ones are to come (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:44">1 Corinthians 15:44</span>).</p>
<p><b>Even we ourselves </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κα αυτο</span></span>). He repeats for emphasis. We have our "groaning" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στεναζομεν</span></span>) as well as nature.</p>
<p><b>Waiting for </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεκδεχομενο</span></span>). The same verb used of nature in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:19">Romans 8:19</span>.</p>
<p><b>Our adoption </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υιοθεσιαν</span></span>). Our full "adoption" (see verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:15">Romans 8:15</span>), "the redemption of our body" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την απολυτρωσιν του σωματος ημων</span></span>). That is to come also. Then we shall have complete redemption of both soul and body. </p>
