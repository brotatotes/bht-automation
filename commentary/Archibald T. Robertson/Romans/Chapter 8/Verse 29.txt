<p><b> Foreknew </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προεγνω</span></span>). Second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προγινωσκω</span></span>, old verb as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:5">Acts 26:5</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ps+1:6">Psalms 1:6</span> (LXX) and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:23">Matthew 7:23</span>. This fore-knowledge and choice is placed in eternity in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:4">Ephesians 1:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>He foreordained </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προωρισεν</span></span>). First aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προοριζω</span></span>, late verb to appoint beforehand as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:28">Acts 4:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:7">1 Corinthians 2:7</span>. Another compound with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προ-</span></span> (for eternity).</p>
<p><b>Conformed to the image </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμμορφους της εικονος</span></span>). Late adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μορφη</span></span> and so an inward and not merely superficial conformity. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εικων</span></span> is used of Christ as the very image of the Father (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:4">2 Corinthians 4:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:15">Colossians 1:15</span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+2:6">Philippians 2:6</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μορφη</span></span>. Here we have both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μορφη</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εικων</span></span> to express the gradual change in us till we acquire the likeness of Christ the Son of God so that we ourselves shall ultimately have the family likeness of sons of God. Glorious destiny.</p>
<p><b>That he might be </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το εινα αυτον</span></span>). Common idiom for purpose.</p>
<p><b>First born among many brethren </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτοτοκον εν πολλοις αδελφοις</span></span>). Christ is "first born" of all creation (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:15">Colossians 1:15</span>), but here he is "first born from the dead" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:18">Colossians 1:18</span>), the Eldest Brother in this family of God's sons, though "Son" in a sense not true of us. </p>
