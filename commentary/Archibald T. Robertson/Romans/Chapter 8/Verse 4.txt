<p><b> The ordinance of the law </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το δικαιωμα του νομου</span></span>). "The requirement of the law."</p>
<p><b>Might be fulfilled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα πλερωθη</span></span>). Purpose of the death of Christ by <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and first aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πληροω</span></span>. Christ met it all in our stead (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:21-26">Romans 3:21-26</span>).</p>
<p><b>Not after the flesh, but after the Spirit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη κατα σαρκα αλλα κατα πνευμα</span></span>). The two laws of life (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα σαρκα</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:7-24">Romans 7:7-24</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα πνευμα</span></span> <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:1-11">Romans 8:1-11</span>). Most likely the Holy Spirit or else the renewed spirit of man. </p>
