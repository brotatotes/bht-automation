<p><b> Is not subject </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουχ υποτασσετα</span></span>). Present passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποτασσω</span></span>, late verb, military term for subjection to orders. Present tense here means continued insubordination.</p>
<p><b>Neither indeed can it be </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουδε γαρ δυνατα</span></span>). "For it is not even able to do otherwise." This helpless state of the unregenerate man Paul has shown above apart from Christ. Hope lies in Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:25">Romans 7:25</span>) and the Spirit of life (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:2">Romans 8:2</span>). </p>
