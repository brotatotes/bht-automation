<p><b> The children being not yet born </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηπω γεννηθεντων</span></span>). Genitive absolute with first aorist passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενναω</span></span>, to beget, to be born, though no word for children nor even the pronoun <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτων</span></span> (they).</p>
<p><b>Neither having done anything good or bad </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηδε πραξαντων τ αγαθον η φαυλον</span></span>). Genitive absolute again with first active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρασσω</span></span>. On <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φαυλον</span></span>, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:10">2 Corinthians 5:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>The purpose of God </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η προθεσις του θεου</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:28">Romans 8:28</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προθεσις</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>According to election </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατ' εκλογην</span></span>). Old word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκλεγω</span></span>, to select, to choose out. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:4">1 Thessalonians 1:4</span>. Here it is the purpose (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προθεσις</span></span>) of God which has worked according to the principles of election.</p>
<p><b>Not of works </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ εξ εργων</span></span>). Not of merit. </p>
