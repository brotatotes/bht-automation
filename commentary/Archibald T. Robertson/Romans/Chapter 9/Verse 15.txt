<p><b> For he says to Moses </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω Μωυσε γαρ λεγε</span></span>). He has an Old Testament illustration of God's election in the case of Pharaoh (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ex+33:19">Exodus 33:19</span>).</p>
<p><b>On whom I have mercy </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ον αν ελεω</span></span>). Indefinite relative with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αν</span></span> and the present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεαω</span></span>, late verb only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:23">Jude 1:23</span> in N.T. "On whomsoever I have mercy." The same construction in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ον αν οικτειρω</span></span>, "on whomsoever I have compassion." </p>
