<p><b> Why doth he still find fault? </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τ ετ μεμφεται?</span></span>). Old verb, to blame. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:8">Hebrews 8:8</span>. Paul's imaginary objector picks up the admission that God hardened Pharaoh's heart. "Still" (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετ</span></span>) argues for a change of condition since that is true.</p>
<p><b>Withstandeth his will </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω βουληματ αυτου ανθεστηκεν</span></span>). Perfect active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθιστημ</span></span>, old verb, maintains a stand (the perfect tense). Many have attempted to resist God's will (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βουλημα</span></span>, deliberate purpose, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:43">Acts 27:43</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:3">1 Peter 4:3</span>). Elsewhere <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θελημα</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:10">Matthew 6:10</span>). </p>
