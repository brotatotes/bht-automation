<p><b> Sorrow </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυπη</span></span>). Because the Jews were rejecting Christ the Messiah. "We may compare the grief of a Jew writing after the fall of Jerusalem" (Sanday and Headlam).</p>
<p><b>Unceasing pain in my heart </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδιαλειπτος οδυνη τη καρδια</span></span>). Like <i>angina pectoris</i>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Οδυνη</span></span> is old word for consuming grief, in N.T. only here and and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:10">1 Timothy 6:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Unceasing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδιαλειπτος</span></span>). Late and rare adjective (in an inscription 1 cent. B.C.), in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:3">2 Timothy 1:3</span>. Two rare words together and both here only in N.T. and I and II Timothy (some small argument for the Pauline authorship of the Pastoral Epistles). </p>
