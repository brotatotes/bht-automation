<p><b> Isaiah </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Εσαιας</span></span>). Shortened quotation from <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="isa+10:22">Isaiah 10:22</span> (LXX).</p>
<p><b>It is the remnant that shall be saved </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το υπολειμμα σωθησετα</span></span>). First future passive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωζω</span></span>. Literally, "the remnant will be saved." Late word from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπολειπω</span></span>, to leave behind (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:3">Romans 11:3</span>), here only in N.T. Textus Receptus has <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταλειμμα</span></span>, but Aleph A B have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπολειμμα</span></span>. Isaiah cries in anguish over the outlook for Israel, but sees hope for the remnant. </p>
