<p><b> Vain talkers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ματαιολογο</span></span>). Late and rare compound, empty talkers, in Vett. Val. and here. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:6">1 Timothy 1:6</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ματαιολογια</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Deceivers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φρεναπατα</span></span>). Late and rare compound, in papyri, eccl. writers, here alone in N.T. "Mind-deceivers." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:3">Galatians 6:3</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φρεναπαταιν</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Specially they of the circumcision </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαλιστα ο εκ της περιτομης</span></span>). Same phrase in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:2">Acts 11:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:12">Galatians 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+4:11">Colossians 4:11</span>. Jews are mentioned in Crete in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:11">Acts 2:11</span>. Apparently Jewish Christians of the Pharisaic type tinged with Gnosticism. </p>
