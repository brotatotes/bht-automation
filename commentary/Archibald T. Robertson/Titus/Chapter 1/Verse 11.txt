<p><b> Whose mouths must be stopped </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ους δε επιστομιζειν</span></span>). Literally, "whom it is necessary to silence by stopping the mouth." Present active infinitive <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιστομιζειν</span></span>, old and common verb (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ</span></span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στομα</span></span>, mouth), here only in N.T. To stop the mouth either with bridle or muzzle or gag.</p>
<p><b>Overthrow </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανατρεπουσιν</span></span>). Old and common verb, to turn up, to overturn. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:18">2 Timothy 2:18</span>. In papyri to upset a family by perversion of one member.</p>
<p><b>Things which they ought not </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α μη δε</span></span>). Note subjective negative <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη</span></span> with indefinite relative and indicative mode.</p>
<p><b>For filthy lucre's sake </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αισχρου κερδους χαριν</span></span>). The Cretans are given a bad reputation for itinerating prophets for profit by Polybius, Livy, Plutarch. Paul's warnings in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:3">1 Timothy 3:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:8">1 Timothy 3:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:5">1 Timothy 6:5</span> reveal it as "a besetting temptation of the professional teacher" (Parry). See verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:7">Titus 1:7</span> above. Disgraceful gain, made in shameful ways. </p>
