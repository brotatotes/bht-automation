<p>See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:4">1 Timothy 1:4</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσεχω</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυθοις</span></span>, only here we have <i>Jewish</i> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ιουδαικοις</span></span>) added. Perhaps a reference to the oral traditions condemned by Christ in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:2-8">Mark 7:2-8</span>. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+2:22">Colossians 2:22</span>, apparently Pharisaic type of Gnostics.</p>
<p><b>Who turn away from the truth </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστρεφομενων</span></span>). Present middle (direct) participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστρεφω</span></span>, "men turning themselves away from the truth" (accusative according to regular idiom). "The truth" (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:3">1 Timothy 4:3</span>) is the gospel (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:21">Ephesians 4:21</span>). </p>
