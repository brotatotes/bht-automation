<p><b> To them that are defiled </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις μεμιαμμενοις</span></span>). Perfect passive articular participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιαινω</span></span>, old verb, to dye with another colour, to stain, in N.T. only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="jdg+1:8">Judges 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:15">Hebrews 12:15</span>. See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεμιαντα</span></span> (perf. pass. indic.) in this verse. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Μολυνω</span></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+8:7">1 Corinthians 8:7</span>) is to smear.</p>
<p><b>Unbelieving </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απιστοις</span></span>). As in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:12">1 Corinthians 7:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:8">1 Timothy 5:8</span>. The principle or proverb just quoted appears also in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:12">1 Corinthians 6:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:23">1 Corinthians 10:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:20">Romans 14:20</span>. For the defilement of mind (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νους</span></span>) and conscience (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνειδησις</span></span>) in both Gentile and Jew by sin, see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:18-2">Romans 1:18-2</span>. </p>
