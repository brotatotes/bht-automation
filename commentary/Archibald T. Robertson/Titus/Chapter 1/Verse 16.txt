<p><b> They profess </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομολογουσιν</span></span>). Present active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομολογεω</span></span>, common verb (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομου, λεγω</span></span>) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:10">Romans 10:10</span>. <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ειδενα</span></span> (know) is second perfect active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιδα</span></span> in indirect assertion.</p>
<p><b>By their works </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις εργοις</span></span>). Instrumental case.</p>
<p><b>They deny </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρνουντα</span></span>). Present middle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρνεομα</span></span>, old verb, common in the Gospels and the Pastoral Epistles (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:8">1 Timothy 5:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:12">Titus 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:12">2 Timothy 2:12</span>).</p>
<p><b>Abominable </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βδελυκτο</span></span>). Verbal adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βδελυσσομα</span></span>. Only in LXX and here.</p>
<p><b>Disobedient </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απειθεις</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:30">Romans 1:30</span>.</p>
<p><b>Reprobate </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδοκιμο</span></span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:27">1 Corinthians 9:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:28">Romans 1:28</span>. </p>
