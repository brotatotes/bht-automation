<p><b> God who cannot lie </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αψευδης θεος</span></span>). "The non-lying God." Old adjective (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευδης</span></span>), here only in N.T. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:13">2 Timothy 2:13</span>. In Polycarp's last prayer.</p>
<p><b>Promised </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επηγγειλατο</span></span>). First aorist middle indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαγγελλω</span></span>. Antithesis in <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφανερωσεν δε</span></span> (manifested) in verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:3">Titus 1:3</span> (first aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανεροω</span></span>). Same contrast in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:25">Romans 16:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:26">Colossians 1:26</span>.</p>
<p><b>Before times eternal </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προ χρονων αιωνων</span></span>). Not to God's purpose before time began (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:4">Ephesians 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:9">2 Timothy 1:9</span>), but to definite promises (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:4">Romans 9:4</span>) made in time (Lock). "Long ages ago." See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:25">Romans 16:25</span>. </p>
