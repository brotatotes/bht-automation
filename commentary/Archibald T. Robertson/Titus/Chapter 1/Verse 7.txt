<p><b> The bishop </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον επισκοπον</span></span>). Same office as "elder" in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:5">Titus 1:5</span>. "Elder is the title, oversight is the function" (B. Weiss).</p>
<p><b>As God's steward </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως θεου οικονομον</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:1">1 Corinthians 4:1</span> for Paul's idea of the bishop (elder) as God's steward (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:17">1 Corinthians 9:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+1:25">Colossians 1:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:2">Ephesians 3:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:4">1 Timothy 1:4</span>).</p>
<p><b>Not self-willed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη αυθαδη</span></span>). Old word (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτοσ, ηδομα</span></span>), self-pleasing, arrogant. In N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:10">2 Peter 2:10</span>.</p>
<p><b>Not soon angry </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οργιλον</span></span>). Old adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οργη</span></span> (anger). Here only in N.T. Vulgate, <i>iracundum</i>. For "brawler" and "striker" see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:2">1 Timothy 3:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>Not greedy of filthy lucre </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αισχροκερδη</span></span>). "Not greedy of shameful gain." Used of deacons in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:8">1 Timothy 3:8</span>, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφιλαργυρον</span></span> used of elders in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:3">1 Timothy 3:3</span>. </p>
