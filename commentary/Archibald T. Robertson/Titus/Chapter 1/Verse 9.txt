<p><b> Holding to </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντεχομενον</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντεχω</span></span>, old verb, to hold back, in middle to hold oneself face to face with, to cling to, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:14">1 Thessalonians 5:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>The faithful word </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του πιστου λογου</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:15">1 Timothy 1:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:3">1 Timothy 6:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:17">Romans 16:17</span>. Some would see a reference here to Christ as the Personal Logos.</p>
<p><b>That he may be able </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα δυνατος η</span></span>). Final clause with present active subjunctive. Paul several times uses <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυνατος ειμ</span></span> in the sense of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυναμα</span></span>, with infinitive as here (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:21">Romans 4:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:23">Romans 11:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:12">2 Timothy 1:12</span>).</p>
<p><b>The gainsayers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους αντιλεγοντας</span></span>). Present active participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντιλεγω</span></span>, old word, to answer back, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:21">Romans 10:21</span>. "The talkers back." </p>
