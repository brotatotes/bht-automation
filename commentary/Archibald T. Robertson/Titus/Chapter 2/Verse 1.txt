<p><b> But speak thou </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συ δε λαλε</span></span>). In contrast to these Pharisaic Gnostics in Crete.</p>
<p><b>Befit </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρεπε</span></span>). Old verb to be becoming, seemly. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:10">1 Timothy 2:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:3">Ephesians 5:3</span>. With dative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδασκαλια</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Sound </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υγιαινουση</span></span>). Healthful as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:13">Titus 1:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:2">Titus 2:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:10">1 Timothy 1:10</span>, common word in the Pastorals. </p>
