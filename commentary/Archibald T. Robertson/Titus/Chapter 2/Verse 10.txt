<p><b> Not purloining </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη νοσφιζομενους</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νοσφιζω</span></span>, old verb (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νοσφ</span></span>, apart), in middle to set apart for oneself, to embezzle, in N.T. only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:2">Acts 5:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>Fidelity </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστιν</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:22">Galatians 5:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:12">1 Timothy 5:12</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστις</span></span> in the sense of faithfulness. Nowhere else in the N.T. do we have <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαθη</span></span> with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστις</span></span> as here, but an Oxyr. papyrus (iii. 494, 9) has this very phrase (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πασαν πιστιν ενδεικνυμενη</span></span>). Westcott and Hort put <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπην</span></span> in the margin. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:2">Titus 3:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>That they may adorn </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα κοσμωσιν</span></span>). Final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and present active subjunctive. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:9">1 Timothy 2:9</span> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοσμεω</span></span>. Paul shows slaves how they may "adorn" the teaching of God. </p>
