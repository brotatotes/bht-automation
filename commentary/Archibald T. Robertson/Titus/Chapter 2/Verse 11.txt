<p><b> Hath appeared </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεφανη</span></span>). "Did appear," the first Epiphany (the Incarnation). Second aorist passive indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιφαινω</span></span>, old verb, in N.T. here, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:4">Titus 3:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:79">Luke 1:79</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:20">Acts 27:20</span>.</p>
<p><b>Bringing salvation </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωτηριος</span></span>). Old adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωτηρ</span></span> (Saviour), here alone in N.T. except <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το σωτηριον</span></span> (salvation, "the saving act") in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:30">Luke 2:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:6">Luke 3:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:17">Ephesians 6:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>Instructing </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παιδευουσα</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:20">1 Timothy 1:20</span>.</p>
<p><b>Ungodliness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασεβειαν</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:18">Romans 1:18</span>.</p>
<p><b>Worldly lusts </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τας κοσμικας επιθυμιας</span></span>). Aristotle and Plutarch use <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοσμικος</span></span> (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοσμος</span></span>) about the universe as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:1">Hebrews 9:1</span> about the earthly. Here it has alone in N.T. the sense of evil "in this present age" as with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοσμος</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:16">1 John 2:16</span>. The three adverbs set off the opposite (soberly <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωφρονως</span></span>, righteously <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιως</span></span>, godly <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευσεβως</span></span>). </p>
