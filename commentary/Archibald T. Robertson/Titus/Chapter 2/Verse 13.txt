<p><b> Looking for </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσδεχομενο</span></span>). Present middle participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσδεχομα</span></span>, old verb, the one used of Simeon (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:25">Luke 2:25</span>) and others (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:38">Luke 2:38</span>) who were looking for the Messiah.</p>
<p><b>The blessed hope and appearing of the glory </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την μακαριαν ελπιδα κα επιφανειαν της δοξης</span></span>). The word <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιφανεια</span></span> (used by the Greeks of the appearance of the gods, from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιφανησ, επιφαινω</span></span>) occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:10">2 Timothy 1:10</span> of the Incarnation of Christ, the first Epiphany (like the verb <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεφανη</span></span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:11">Titus 2:11</span>), but here of the second Epiphany of Christ or the second coming as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:14">1 Timothy 6:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:1">2 Timothy 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:8">2 Timothy 4:8</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:8">2 Thessalonians 2:8</span> both <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιφανεια</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρουσια</span></span> (the usual word) occur together of the second coming.</p>
<p><b>Of our great God and Saviour Jesus Christ </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του μεγαλου θεου κα σωτηρος Ιησου Χριστου</span></span>). This is the necessary meaning of the one article with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεου</span></span> and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωτηρος</span></span> just as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:1">2 Peter 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:11">2 Peter 1:11</span>. See Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 786. Westcott and Hort read <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Χριστου Ιησου</span></span>. </p>
