<p><b> Aged women </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρεσβυτιδας</span></span>). Old word, feminine of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρεσβυτης</span></span>, only here in N.T. See <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρεσβυτερας</span></span> in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:2">1 Timothy 5:2</span>.</p>
<p><b>Reverent </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιεροπρεπεις</span></span>). Old word (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειροσ, πρεπε</span></span>). Only here in N.T. Same idea in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:10">1 Timothy 2:10</span>. Like people engaged in sacred duties (Lock).</p>
<p><b>In demeanour </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν καταστηματ</span></span>). Late and rare word (inscriptions) from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθιστημ</span></span>, deportment, only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>Not slanderers </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη διαβολους</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:11">1 Timothy 3:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:3">2 Timothy 3:3</span>.</p>
<p><b>Nor enslaved to much wine </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηδε οινω πολλω δεδουλωμενας</span></span>). Perfect passive participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δουλοω</span></span>, with dative case <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οινω</span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:8">1 Timothy 3:8</span>. "It is proved by experience that the reclamation of a woman drunkard is almost impossible" (White). But God can do the "impossible."</p>
<p><b>Teachers of that which is good </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλοδιδασκαλους</span></span>). Compound word found here alone, <i>bona docentes</i> (teaching good and beautiful things). A sorely needed mission. </p>
