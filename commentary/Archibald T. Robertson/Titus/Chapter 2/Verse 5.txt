<p><b> Workers at home </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικουργους</span></span>). So the oldest MSS. (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικοσ, εργου</span></span>) instead of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικουρους</span></span>, keepers at home (from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοισο, ουρος</span></span>, keeper). Rare word, found in Soranus, a medical writer, Field says. Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:13">1 Timothy 5:13</span>. "Keepers at home" are usually "workers at home."</p>
<p><b>Kind </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαθας</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:7">Romans 5:7</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+3:18">Colossians 3:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:22">Ephesians 5:22</span> for the same use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υποτασσομα</span></span>, to be in subjection. Note <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδιοις</span></span> (their own). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:1">1 Timothy 6:1</span> for the same negative purpose clause (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη βλασφημητα</span></span>). </p>
