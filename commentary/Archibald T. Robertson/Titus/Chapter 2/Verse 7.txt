<p><b> Shewing thyself </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σεαυτον παρεχομενος</span></span>). Present middle (redundant middle) participle of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεχω</span></span> with the reflexive pronoun <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σεαυτον</span></span> as if the active voice <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεχων</span></span>. The <i>Koine</i> shows an increasing number of such constructions (Robertson, <i>Grammar</i>, p. 811). See active in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:4">1 Timothy 1:4</span>.</p>
<p><b>An ensample </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυπον</span></span>). For this word see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:9">2 Thessalonians 3:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="php+3:17">Philippians 3:17</span>.</p>
<p><b>Uncorruptness </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφθοριαν</span></span>). Only example, from late adjective <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφθορος</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φθειρω</span></span>). </p>
