<p><b> Sound </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υγιη</span></span>, Attic usually <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υγια</span></span> in accusative singular), elsewhere in Pastorals participle <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υγιανων</span></span> (verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:1">Titus 2:1</span>).</p>
<p><b>That cannot be condemned </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακαταγνωστον</span></span>). Only N.T. example (verbal, <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α</span></span> privative and <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταγνωστος</span></span>) and in IV Macc. 4:47. Deissmann (<i>Bible Studies</i>, p. 200) quotes it from an inscription and the adverb from a papyrus.</p>
<p><b>He that is of the contrary part </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο εξ εναντιας</span></span>). "The one on the opposite side" (your opponent). Cf. verse <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:9">Titus 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:14">1 Timothy 5:14</span>.</p>
<p><b>May be ashamed </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα εντραπη</span></span>). Final clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and second aorist passive subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντρεπω</span></span>, to turn, in middle and passive to turn one on himself and so be ashamed (to blush) as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:14">2 Thessalonians 3:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:14">1 Corinthians 4:14</span>. This sense in the papyri.</p>
<p><b>Evil </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φαυλον</span></span>). Old word, easy (easy morals), worthless; bad, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:10">2 Corinthians 5:10</span>. </p>
