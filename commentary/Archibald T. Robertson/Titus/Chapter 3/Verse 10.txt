<p><b> Heretical </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιρετικον</span></span>). Old adjective from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιρεσις</span></span> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιρεομα</span></span>, to choose), a choosing of a party (sect, <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:17">Acts 5:17</span>) or of teaching (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:1">2 Peter 2:1</span>). Possibly a schism had been started here in Crete.</p>
<p><b>Refuse </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραιτου</span></span>). Present middle imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραιτεω</span></span>, to ask from, to beg off from. See same form in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:7">1 Timothy 4:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:11">1 Timothy 5:11</span>. Possibly an allusion here to Christ's directions in <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:15-17">Matthew 18:15-17</span>. </p>
