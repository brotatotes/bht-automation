<p><b> When I shall send </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν πεμψω</span></span>). Indefinite temporal clause with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν</span></span> and the first aorist active subjunctive (or future indicative) of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεμπω</span></span> (same form).</p>
<p><b>Artemas </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Αρτεμαν</span></span>). Perhaps abbreviation of Artemidorus. Nothing more is known of him.</p>
<p><b>Or Tychicus </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η Τυχικον</span></span>). Paul's well-known disciple (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="col+4:7">Colossians 4:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:21">Ephesians 6:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:12">2 Timothy 4:12</span>).</p>
<p><b>To Nicopolis </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις Νικοπολιν</span></span>). Probably in Epirus, a good place for work in Dalmatia (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:10">2 Timothy 4:10</span>).</p>
<p><b>I have determined </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεκρικα</span></span>). Perfect active indicative. I have decided.</p>
<p><b>To winter there </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκε παραχειμασα</span></span>). First aorist active infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραχειμαζω</span></span>, a literary <i>Koine</i> word for which see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:12">Acts 27:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:6">1 Corinthians 16:6</span>. </p>
