<p><b> Zenas the lawyer </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Ζηναν τον νομικον</span></span>). Possibly abbreviation of Zenodorus and may be one of the bearers of the Epistle with Apollos. Probably an expert in the Mosaic law as the word means in the Gospels. A converted Jewish lawyer. The Latin term is <i>jurisconsultum</i> for <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νομικον</span></span>.</p>
<p><b>Apollos </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">Απολλων</span></span>). Paul's friend (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:24-19">Acts 18:24-19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:12">1 Corinthians 1:12</span>).</p>
<p><b>Set forward </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προπεμψον</span></span>). First aorist active imperative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προπεμπω</span></span>, old verb, to send on ahead (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:6">1 Corinthians 16:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:11">1 Corinthians 16:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:24">Romans 15:24</span>).</p>
<p><b>That nothing be wanting unto them </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μηδεν αυτοις λειπη</span></span>). Purpose with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> and present (or second aorist <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λιπη</span></span>, some MSS.) subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λειπω</span></span>, old verb to leave, to remain, to lack. With dative case here (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτοις</span></span>). </p>
