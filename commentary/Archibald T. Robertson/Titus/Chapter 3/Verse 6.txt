<p><b> Which </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου</span></span>). Genitive case by attraction from <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span> (grammatical gender) to the case of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευματος αγιου</span></span>. We do not have grammatical gender (only natural) in English. Hence here we should say "whom," even if it does not go smoothly with <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεχεεν</span></span> (he poured out, second aorist active indicative of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκχεω</span></span>). The reference is to the great Pentecost (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:33">Acts 2:33</span>) as foretold by Joel (<span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="joe+2:28">Joel 2:28</span>).</p>
<p><b>Richly </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλουσιως</span></span>). Then and to each one in his own experience. See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:12">Romans 10:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:17">1 Timothy 6:17</span>. </p>
