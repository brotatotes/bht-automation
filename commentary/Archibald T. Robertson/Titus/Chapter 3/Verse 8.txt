<p><b> The saying </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο λογος</span></span>). In verses <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:4-7">Titus 3:4-7</span>.</p>
<p><b>I will </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βουλομα</span></span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:8">1 Timothy 2:8</span>.</p>
<p><b>That thou affirm confidently </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σε διαβεβαιουσθα</span></span>). Indirect command. For the verb see <span class="scriptRef" com="rwp" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:7">1 Timothy 1:7</span>.</p>
<p><b>That they may be careful </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα φροντιζωσιν</span></span>). Sub-final use of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα</span></span> with present active subjunctive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φροντιζω</span></span>, old verb, only here in N.T.</p>
<p><b>To maintain good works </b> (<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλων εργων προιστασθα</span></span>). Present middle infinitive of <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προιστημ</span></span>, intransitive use, to stand before, to take the lead in, to care for. Paul is anxious that "believers" may take the lead in good works. </p>
