<p><strong> 10.</strong> ] <strong> δέ</strong> introduces the <em> contrast</em> to the thankful assurance just expressed.
	</p>
<p><strong> διὰ τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ὀν</strong> <strong> .</strong> , as διὰ τῶν οἰκτιρμῶν τοῦ θεοῦ , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:1">Romans 12:1</span> ; “as the bond of union, and as the most holy name by which they could be adjured.” Stanley.
	</p>
<p>ἵνα (reff.) not only introduces the result of the fulfilment of the exhortation, but includes its import.
	</p>
<p><strong> τὸ αὐτὸ λέγητε</strong> contrast to λέγει ἐγὼ μὲν … ἐγὼ δὲ … ἐγὼ δὲ … ἐγὼ δέ of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:12">1 Corinthians 1:12</span> , but further implying the <em> having the same sentiments</em> on the subjects which divided them: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="php+2:2">Philippians 2:2</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἦτε δέ</strong> ] δέ here implies <strong> but rather</strong> , as in Thuc. ii. 98, ἀπεγίγνετο μὲν αὐτῷ οὐδὲν τοῦ στρατοῦ , … προσεγίγνετο δέ . Hartung Partikellehre, i. 171, gives many other examples. καταρτίζω is the exact word for the healing or repairing of the breaches made by the σχίσματα , <strong> perfectly united</strong> . So Herod. v. 28, ἡ Μίλητος … ἐπὶ δύο γενεὰς ἀνδρῶν νοσήσασα ἐς τὰ μάλιστα <strong> στάσει</strong> , μέχρι οὗ μιν Πάριοι <strong> κατήρτισαν</strong> .
	</p>
