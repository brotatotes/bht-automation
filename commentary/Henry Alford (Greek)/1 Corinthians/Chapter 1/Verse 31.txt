<p><strong> 31.</strong> ] The construction is an anacoluthon, the citation being retained in the original imperative, though the ἵνα required a subjunctive. It is freely made from the LXX. This verse declaring, in opposition to <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:29">1 Corinthians 1:29</span> , the only true ground of boasting, viz. in God and His mercies to us in Christ, closes the description of <em> God’s dealing</em> in this matter. He now reverts to the subject of <em> his own preaching</em> .</p>
