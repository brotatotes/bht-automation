<p><strong> 7.</strong> ] <strong> So that ye are behind</strong> (others) <strong> in no gift of grace</strong> ; not, <em> lack no gift of grace</em> , which would be genitive. <strong> χάρισμα</strong> here has its widest sense, of <em> that which is the effect of</em> <strong> χάρις</strong> , not meaning ‘spiritual gifts’ in the narrower sense, as in ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:4">1 Corinthians 12:4</span> . This is plain from the whole strain of the passage, which dwells not on outward gifts, but on the inward graces of the Christian life.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀπεκδεχ</strong> .] which is the greatest proof of maturity and richness of the spiritual life; implying the coexistence and co-operation of <em> faith</em> , whereby they believed the promise of Christ, <em> hope</em> , whereby they looked on to its fulfilment, and <em> love</em> , whereby that anticipation was lit up with earnest desire; compare πᾶσιν τοῖς <strong> ἠγαπηκόσιν</strong> τὴν ἐπιφάνειαν αὐτοῦ , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:8">2 Timothy 4:8</span> .
	</p>
<p>ἀπεκδ . κ . τ . λ ., is taken by Chrys., who understands χαρίσματα of miraculous powers, as implying that <em> besides them they needed patience</em> to wait till the coming of Christ; and by Calv., “ideo addit <em> expectantes revelationem</em> , quo significat, non talem se affluentiam illis affingere in qua nihil desideretur; sed tantum quæ sufficiet usquedum ad perfectionem perventum fuerit.” But I much prefer taking ἀπεκδεχομένους as parallel with and giving the result of μὴ ὑστ . κ . τ . λ .</p>
