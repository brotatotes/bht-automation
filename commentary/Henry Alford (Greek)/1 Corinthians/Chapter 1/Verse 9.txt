The <strong> κοιν</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τοῦ υἱ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> αὐτ</strong> ., as Meyer well remarks, is the δόξα τῶν τέκνων τοῦ θεοῦ , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:21">Romans 8:21</span> ; for they will be συγκληρονόμοι τοῦ χριστοῦ , and συνδοξασθέντες with Him, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:17">Romans 8:17</span> ; Rom 8:23 ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:14">2 Thessalonians 2:14</span> . The mention of κοινωνία may perhaps have been intended to prepare the way, as was before done in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:2">1 Corinthians 1:2</span> , for the reproof which is coming.
