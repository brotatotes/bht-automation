<p>The plural συνέβαινον expresses the plurality of events separately happening: the singular ἐγράφη , their union in the common record of Scripture. Similarly <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:10">2 Peter 3:10</span> , στοιχεῖα … λυθήσονται … τὰ ἐν αὐτῇ ἔργα κατακαήσεται . See reff. and Winer, edn. 6, § 58. 3. a.
	</p>
<p><strong> δέ</strong> conveys a slight opposition to συνέβαινον ἐκείνοις .
	</p>
<p><strong> τὰ τέλη τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> αἰών</strong> .] = ἡ συντέλεια τοῦ αἰῶνος of reff. Matt., and τὸ ἔσχατον τῶν ἡμερῶν τούτων of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:1">Hebrews 1:1</span> , where see note: <strong> the ends of the ages</strong> of this world’s lifetime. So Chrys.; οὐδὲν ἄλλο λέγει ἢ ὅτι ἐφέστηκε λοιπὸν τὸ δικαστήριον τὸ φοβερόν .
	</p>
<p>The form νουθεσία belongs to later Greek. The classical word is νουθέτησις or νουθετία : see Lobeck on Phrynichus, p. 512.
	</p>
<p><strong> κατήντ</strong> .] <strong> have reached.</strong> The ages are treated as occupying space, and their extent as just coincident with our own time. See a similar figure in ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:36">1 Corinthians 14:36</span> .</p>
