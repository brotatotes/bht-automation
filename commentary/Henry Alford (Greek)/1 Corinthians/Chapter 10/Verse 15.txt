<p><strong> 15</strong> .] An appeal to their own sense of what is congruous and possible, as introducing what is to follow.
	</p>
<p><strong> ὡς</strong> expresses an assumption on the Apostle’s part, that they <em> are</em> φρόνιμοι . De W. compares Plato, Alcib. i. 104, ὡς ἀκουσομένῳ λέγω .
	</p>
<p><strong> λέγω</strong> and <strong> φημί</strong> both refer to what follows, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:16-21">1 Corinthians 10:16-21</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ὑμεῖς</strong> is emphatic <strong> be</strong> YE <strong> the judges of what I am saying</strong> .</p>
