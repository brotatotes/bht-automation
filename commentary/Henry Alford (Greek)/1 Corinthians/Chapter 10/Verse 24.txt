<p><strong> 24.</strong> ] Further following out of οἰκοδομεῖ . This ought to be our object: the bringing on one another to perfection, not the pleasing ourselves, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:2-3">Romans 15:2-3</span> . In the second clause, ἕκαστος must be supplied from μηδείς (hence it has found its way into the rec.): so Plato, Rep. ii. p. 366 D, οὐδεὶς ἑκὼν δίκαιος , ἀλλ ʼ … ψέγει τὸ ἄδικον , i.e. ἕκαστος ψέγει . See Bernhardy, Syntax, p. 458.</p>
