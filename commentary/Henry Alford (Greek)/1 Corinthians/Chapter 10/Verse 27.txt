<p><strong> 27.</strong> ] The same maxim applied to their conduct at <em> a banquet given by a heathen</em> . A <em> miscellaneous banquet</em> , and not a sacrificial feast, is meant. At such, there might be meat which had been offered to idols. Grot, says well on θέλετε πορεύεσθαι , “Admonet tacite, melius forte facturos, si non eant: ire tamen non prohibet: supra, cap. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:10">1 Corinthians 5:10</span> .”
