<p><strong> 30.</strong> ] Experimental proof of the κρῖμα ἑαυτῷ , from the present sicknesses and frequent deaths among the Corinthian believers.
	</p>
<p>Meyer distinguishes <strong> ἀσθενεῖς</strong> , <em> weaklings</em> , persons whose powers have failed spontaneously, from <strong> ἄῤῥωστοι</strong> , <em> invalids</em> , persons whose powers are enfeebled by sickness; and cites Tittmann, Synon. p. 76.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀσθ</strong> . and <strong> ἄῤῥ</strong> . refer to <em> physical</em> , not (as Olsh., altern.) <em> moral</em> weaknesses.</p>
