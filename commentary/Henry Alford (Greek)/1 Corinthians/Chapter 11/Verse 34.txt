<p><strong> 34.</strong> ] The ἀγάπαι were not meals to satiate the bodily appetites, but for a higher and holier purpose: let the hungry take off the edge of his hunger at home: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:22">1 Corinthians 11:22</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> τὰ δὲ λοιπά</strong> ] viz. <em> things omitted</em> (probably matters of detail) <em> in the above directions</em> . Perhaps they had asked him questions respecting the most convenient time or manner of celebration of the Lord’s supper: points on which primitive practice widely differed.
	</p>
<p><strong> ὡς ἂν ἔλθω</strong> , see reff., <strong> whenever I shall have come</strong> . ὡς ἄν , as ὅτ ʼ ἄν , implies uncertainty as to the event anticipated: see Kühner, vol. ii. p. 535, § 807.</p>
