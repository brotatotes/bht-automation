<p><strong> 8.</strong> ] <strong> γάρ</strong> gives the reason of the former assertion γυνὴ δόξα ἀνδρός , viz. that <strong> the man is not</strong> (emphasis on ἐστιν , which prevents the ἐκ having a figurative sense, of <em> dependence</em> : ‘takes not his being,’ in the fact of his <em> original creation</em> . The <em> propagation</em> of the species is not here in view) <strong> out of the woman, but the woman out of the man</strong> (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ge+2:23">Genesis 2:23</span> , κληθήσεται γυνή , ὅτι <strong> ἐκ τοῦ ἀνδρὸς αὐτῆς ἐλήφθη</strong> ).</p>
