<p><strong> 2.</strong> ] <strong> τὰ μυστήρ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> πάντα</strong> are all <em> the secrets of the divine counsel</em> , see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:25">Romans 11:25</span> (note); <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:25">Romans 16:25</span> , and reff. The knowledge of these would be the perfection of the gift of prophecy. The verb belongs to both μυστ . and γνῶσιν . The full construction would be εἰδῶ μυστ . and ἔχω γνῶσιν .
	</p>
<p><strong> πᾶσαν τὴν πίστιν</strong> hardly, as Stanley, implies ‘ <em> all the faith in the world</em> ,’ but rather, ‘all the faith required to,’ &amp;c.: or perhaps the art. conveys the allusion to our Lord’s saying, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:20">Matthew 17:20</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:21">Matthew 21:21</span> ; ‘all that faith,’so as, &amp;c.</p>
