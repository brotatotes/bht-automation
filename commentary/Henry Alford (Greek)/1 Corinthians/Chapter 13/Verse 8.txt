<p><strong> 8.</strong> <strong> πίπτει</strong> ] The exact word is that of the E. V., faileth: so Theod.: οὐ διασφάλλεται , ἀλλ ʼ ἀεὶ μένει βεβαία κ . ἀσάλευτος κ . ἀκίνητος , ἐς ἀεὶ διαμένουσα . τοῦτο γὰρ διὰ τῶν ἐπαγομένων ἐδίδαξεν . Of the two readings, we may illustrate <strong> πίπτει</strong> by Plato, Phileb., p. 22 E, ἀλλὰ μήν , ὦ Σώκρατες , ἔμοιγε δοκεῖ νῦν μὲν ἡδονή σοι πεπτωκέναι καθαπερεὶ πληγεῖσα ὑπὸ τῶν νῦν δὴ λόγων : and Polyb. x. 33. 4, κἄν ποτε πέσῃ τὰ ὅλα , “in case the whole plan should fail:” id. i. 35. 5: and ἐκπίπτει by Plato, Gorg. p. 517, εἰ οὗτοι ῥήτορες ἦσαν , οὔτε τῇ ἀληθινῇ ῥητορικῇ ἐχρῶντο ( οὐ γὰρ ἂν ἐξέπεσον ) οὔτε τῇ κολακικῇ : where Heindorf, ‘proprie usurpatur de actoribus, citharœdis, aliisque, qui a spectatoribus exploduntur et exsibilantur:’ and by the celebrated passage in Demosthenes περὶ στεφ . p. 315, ἐτριταγωνίστεις , ἐγὼ δ ʼ ἐθεώρουν . ἐξέπιπτες , ἐγὼ δ ʼ ἐσύριττον : where also, by the way, ἔπιπτες is a various reading.
	</p>
<p>By <strong> εἴπε</strong> <strong> , </strong> <strong> εἴτε</strong> <strong> , </strong> <strong> εἴτε</strong> the <em> general</em> idea, <strong> χαρίσματα</strong> , is split into its <em> species</em> <strong> be there prophesyings, be there</strong> (speakings in) <strong> tongues, be there knowledge</strong> .
	</p>
<p>Chrys., al., understand the two first futures, <strong> καταργ</strong> <strong> ., </strong> <strong> παύς</strong> <strong> .</strong> , of the time when, the faith being every where dispersed, these gifts should be <em> no longer needed</em> . But unquestionably the time alluded to is that of <em> the coming of the Lord</em> ; see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:12">1 Corinthians 13:12</span> , and this applies to <em> all these</em> , not to the last ( γνῶσις ) only. The two first, <strong> προφ</strong> . and <strong> γλῶσσ</strong> ., shall be <em> absolutely</em> superseded: <strong> γνῶσις</strong> , <em> relatively</em> : the imperfect, by the perfect.</p>
