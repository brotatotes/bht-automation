<p><strong> 15.</strong> ] <strong> What then is (the case)</strong> (i.e. as our ‘ <em> What then</em> ?’ Cf. τί οὖν , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:9">Romans 3:9</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:15">Romans 6:15</span> . ‘What is my determination thereupon?’) <strong> I will pray</strong> (on the reading προσεύξωμαι , see note on Rom 5:1 ) <strong> with the</strong> (my) <strong> spirit: I will pray also with my mind</strong> (i.e. will interpret my prayer for the benefit of myself and the church), &amp;c. This resolution, or expression of self-obligation, evidently leads to the inference, by and by clearly expressed, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:28">1 Corinthians 14:28</span> , that <em> if he could not</em> pray τῷ νοΐ , he would <em> keep silence</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ψαλῶ</strong> ] hence we gather that the two departments in which the gift of tongues was exercised were <em> prayer and praise</em> . On the day of Pentecost it was confined to the latter of these.</p>
