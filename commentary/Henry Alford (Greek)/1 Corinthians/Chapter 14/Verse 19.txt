<p><strong> 19.</strong> ] <strong> ἐκ ἐκκλησίᾳ</strong> , <strong> in</strong> (the) assembly, ‘in the congregation’ [this is the better rendering here, and wherever there is a chance of the word <em> church</em> being mistaken as meaning a building], not ‘in <em> an</em> assembly,’ as Meyer. The art. is omitted after a preposition: so Middleton, ch. vi. § 1; the logical account of which is, that the prep. serves to <em> categorize</em> the substantive following it, and so make it general instead of particular.
	</p>
