<p><strong> 30.</strong> ] <strong> But if a revelation shall have been made to another</strong> (prophet) <strong> while sitting by, let the first</strong> (who was prophesying) <strong> hold his peace</strong> (give place to the other: but clearly, not as ejected by the second in any disorderly manner: probably, by being made aware of it and ceasing his discourse). The rendering of Grot., al., ‘ <em> let him</em> (the second) <em> wait till the first has done speaking</em> ,’ q. d., ‘ <em> let the first have left off</em> ,’ is ungrammatical.
