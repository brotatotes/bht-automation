<p><strong> 4.</strong> ] <strong> ἑαυτ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> οἰκ</strong> . does not necessarily involve his <em> understanding</em> what he speaks: the <em> exercise of the gift</em> in accordance with the prompting of the Spirit may be regarded as an οἰκοδομή : the intensity of the feeling of prayer or praise in which he utters the words is edifying to him, though the words themselves are unintelligible. This view is necessary on account of what is said in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:5">1 Corinthians 14:5</span> , that if he can <em> interpret</em> , he can edify not only himself but the church.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐκκλησίαν</strong> ] [i.e. the assembled Christians: see note on ch. 1Co 11:18 ] not, as Meyer, <em> a congregation</em> , but = τὴν ἐκκλησίαν : the art. being often omitted when a noun in government has an emphatic place before the verb: accordingly in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:5">1 Corinthians 14:5</span> , it is ἡ ἐκκλ . which is edified.</p>
