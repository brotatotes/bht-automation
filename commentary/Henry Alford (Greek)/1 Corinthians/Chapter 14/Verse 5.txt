<p><strong> 5.</strong> ] He shews that it is from no <em> antipathy</em> to or <em> jealousy</em> of the gift of tongues that he thus speaks: but (force of the <strong> δέ</strong> ) that he <em> wished them all to speak with tongues, but rather that they should prophesy</em> . The distinction between the acc. and inf. after θέλω , as the simple direct object of the wish, and ἵνα with the subj., as its higher and ulterior object, has been lost in the E. V. The second δέ is opposed to the subordinate λαλ . γλ ., as in 1Co 14:1 to τὰ πνευματικά .
