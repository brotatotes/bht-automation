<p><strong> 9.</strong> ] <em> Application of these instances</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> διὰ τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> γλώσσης</strong> is most naturally understood <em> physically</em> , <strong> by means of your tongue,</strong> as answering to the utterance of the sound by the musical instruments. But the technical rendering, by means of <em> the tongue</em> (in the sense of γλώσσῃ λαλεῖν ), is allowable.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἔσεσθε</strong> <strong> … </strong> <strong> λαλ</strong> .] This periphrasis of the future implies, <strong> ye will be</strong> , so long as ye speak, <strong> speaking</strong> , … On <strong> εἰς ἀέρα</strong> , see ref.: it implies the <em> non-reception by hearers</em> of what is said.</p>
