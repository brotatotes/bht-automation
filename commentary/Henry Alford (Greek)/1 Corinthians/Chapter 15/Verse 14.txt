<p><strong> οὐκ ἐγ</strong> .] Again repeating and using as matter of fact ( οὐκ ) the inference of the last verse; q. d. εἰ δὲ χρ . οὐκ - ἐγήγερται .
	</p>
<p><strong> κενόν</strong> ] <strong> idle</strong> , ‘empty,’ ‘without result:’ placed first for emphasis.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἄρα</strong> ] <strong> then</strong> : ‘rebus ita comparatis’ (Meyer).
	</p>
<p><strong> καί</strong> ] <strong> also</strong> , q. d. “If Christ’s Resurrection be gone, then also our faith is gone.” Without the copula δέ , the clause is much more forcible: <strong> idle also is our preaching</strong> , <strong> idle also is your faith</strong> . Thus καί <em> both times</em> refers to the hypothesis, εἰ χρ . οὐκ ἐγήγ .</p>
