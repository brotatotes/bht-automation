<p><strong> 17.</strong> <strong> ματαία</strong> ] from μάτην , and thus more directly pointing at the <em> frustration</em> of all on which faith relies as accomplished, e.g. the removal of the guilt and power of sin; and of all to which hope looks forward, e.g. bliss after death for those who die in Christ. This is so, because Christ’s Resurrection <em> accomplished our justification</em> ( Rom 4:25 ), and, through justification, our <em> future bliss</em> , even in the <em> disembodied state</em> (for that seems <em> here</em> to be treated of).</p>
