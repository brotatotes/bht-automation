<p><strong> 26</strong> .] Connect <strong> ἔσχατ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἐχθρός</strong> together; not as Bloomf., “last of all, the enemy Death is to be destroyed,” which is ungrammatical. If ἔσχ . is to stand alone, ἐχθρὸς καταργεῖται must be “is destroyed as an enemy.” Death is <em> the last enemy</em> , as being the <em> consequence of sin</em> : when he is overcome and done away with, the whole end of Redemption is shewn to have been accomplished. Death is personified, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="re+20:14">Revelation 20:14</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> καταργεῖται</strong> , pres., either as a prophetic certainty as παραδιδοῖ above, or as an axiomatic truth.</p>
