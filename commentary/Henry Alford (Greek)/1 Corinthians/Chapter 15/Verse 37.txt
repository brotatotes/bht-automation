<p><strong> 37</strong> .] Before, the <em> death</em> of the seed was insisted on: now, the <em> non-identity of the seed with the future plant</em> . There is a mixture of construction, the words ὃ σπείρεις being <em> pendent</em> , as the sentence now stands. The two constructions as De W. observes are, εἴ τι σπείρεις , οὐ τὸ σ . τὸ γεν . σπείρεις , and ὃ σπείρεις , οὐ τὸ σ . τὸ γεν . ἐστιν .
	</p>
<p>He names the <em> plant</em> <strong> τὸ σῶμα τὸ γενησόμενον</strong> , having already in his eye the application to the Resurrection.
	</p>
