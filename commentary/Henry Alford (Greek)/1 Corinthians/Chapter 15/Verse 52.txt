<p><strong> ἐν τῇ ἐσχ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> σάλπ</strong> . <strong> at</strong> (in, as part of the events of) <strong> the last trumpet-blowing</strong> . The word ἐσχ . must obviously not be refined upon as some ( τινές in Theophyl. and Olsh.) have done, identifying it with the <em> seventh trumpet</em> of the Apocalypse; nor pressed too closely as if there were necessarily no trump after it, but is <em> the trump at the time of the end</em> , <strong> the last trump</strong> , in a wide and popular sense. See ref. 1 Thess.
	</p>
<p><strong> σαλπίσει</strong> ] impersonal, ὁ σαλπιγκτής , scil. So Od. φ . 142, ἀρξάμενοι τοῦ χώρου ὅθεν τέ περ οἰνοχοεύει (scil. ὁ οἰνόχοος ): Herod. ii. 47, ἐπεὰν θύσῃ : Xen. Anab. i. 2. 17, ἐπεὶ ἐσάλπιγξε : iii. 4. 36, ἐκήρυξε : vi. 5. 25, ἕως σημαίνοι τῇ σάλπιγγι Kühner, § 414. 2.
	</p>
