<p><strong> 58</strong> .] <em> Conclusion of the whole by an earnest exhortation</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ὥστε</strong> ] ‘ <em> quæ cum ita sint</em> ,’ seeing that the victory is sure.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἑδρ</strong> <strong> ., </strong> <strong> ἀμετακίν</strong> .] a climax (Mey.); in reference, viz. to the doubt which is attempted to be raised among you on this matter.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐν τῷ ἔργῳ τοῦ κυρ</strong> .] The <em> work of the Lord</em> is the <em> Christian life</em> , with its active and passive duties and graces, the bringing forth the fruits of the Spirit.
	</p>
<p><strong> εἰδότες</strong> ] <strong> Knowing</strong> (as you do being convinced by what has been said), <strong> that your labour</strong> (bestowed on the ἔργ . τοῦ κυρ .) <strong> is not vain</strong> (which it would be, were there no resurrection: see reff.) <strong> in the Lord</strong> . These last words cannot belong to ὁ κόπος ὑμ ., nor very well to οὐκ ἔστι κενός (as Meyer), but are best taken with the whole sentence, <em> your labour is not in vain</em> : so ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:1">1 Corinthians 9:1</span> .</p>
