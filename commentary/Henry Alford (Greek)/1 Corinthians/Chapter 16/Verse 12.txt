<p><strong> 12</strong> .] <em> Of Apollos: that he was not willing at present to go to them</em> . <strong> δέ</strong> , transitional.
	</p>
<p>On the construction of <strong> περὶ</strong> <strong> .… </strong> <strong> ἀδ</strong> ., see on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:1">1 Corinthians 16:1</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> παρεκάλ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἵνα ἔλθῃ</strong> ] ἵνα denotes the aim, not only the purport of the exhortation. See remarks on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:13">1 Corinthians 14:13</span> .
	</p>
<p>“Ideo excusat, ne suspicentur Corinthii ab eo fuisse impeditum.… Apud se quærere poterant: Cur hos potius quam Apollo nobis misit? Respondet, minime per so stetisse, &amp;c.” Calvin. Meyer remarks, perhaps the Corinthians had expressly desired that Apollos should be sent to them. <strong> μετὰ τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἀδελφ</strong> .] perhaps, those who went with Timotheus (see above): perhaps, those who were to bear this letter ( 1Co 16:17 ).
	</p>
<p><strong> καί</strong> ] <strong> and</strong> , not, ‘ <em> but</em> :’ see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:32">John 16:32</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:13">Romans 1:13</span> . It merely couples the exhortation with its result.
	</p>
<p><strong> θέλημα</strong> ] Evidently the <em> will of Apollos</em> , not, as Theophyl.: τουτέστιν , ὁ θεὸς οὐκ ἤθελεν .
	</p>
<p><strong> ὅταν εὐκαιρ</strong> .] The present καιρός not seeming to him a suitable one: apparently on account of the divisions hinted at in the beginning of the Epistle.</p>
