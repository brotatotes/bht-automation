<p><strong> 2.</strong> ] <strong> μίαν σαββ</strong> .] For this Hebraism, and σαβ . in the singular, signifying <em> week</em> , see reff.
	</p>
<p>On the observance of the first day of the week, see notes, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:7">Acts 20:7</span> , and <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:5">Romans 14:5</span> . Here there is no mention of their <em> assembling</em> , which we have in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:7">Acts 20:7</span> , but a plain indication that the day was already considered as a special one, and one more than others fitting for the performance of a religious duty.
	</p>
<p><strong> παρ</strong> <strong> ʼ </strong> <strong> ἑαυτῷ τιθ</strong> .] <strong> let each of you lay up at home</strong> (reff.) <strong> in store whatsoever he may by prosperity have acquired</strong> (lit. ‘ <em> whatsoever he may be prospered in</em> :’ i.e. the pecuniary result of any prosperous adventure, or dispensation of Providence): not, as Bengel, al.: ‘ <em> quod commodum sit</em> ,’ a meaning which the word will not bear.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἵνα μή</strong> <strong> , …</strong> ] <strong> that there may not, when I come,</strong> THEN <strong> be collections to be made</strong> . His time would be better employed in imparting to them a spiritual benefit, than in urging them to and superintending this duty.</p>
