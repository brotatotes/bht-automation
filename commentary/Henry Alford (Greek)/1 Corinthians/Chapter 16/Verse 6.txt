<p><strong> 6.</strong> <strong> παραμενῶ</strong> ] This, of which he speaks uncertainly, was accomplished; he spent ( Act 20:3 ) three months, and those (ib. Act 20:6 ) the three winter months, in Greece (at Corinth).
	</p>
<p><strong> ὑμεῖς</strong> , Meyer justly remarks, is emphatic, and conveys an affectionate preference, in his present plan, for them.
	</p>
<p><strong> οὗ</strong> , with a verb of motion. The account of this is that the ideas of motion and rest are both involved in the verb: rest, when the motion is accomplished. So <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:1">Luke 10:1</span> ; Soph. Trach. 40, κεῖνος δ ʼ ὅπου βέβηκεν οὐδεὶς οἶδε : Xen. Hell. vii. 1. 25, ὅπου βουληθεῖεν ἐξελθεῖν . See Kühner, § 623, Anm. 2.
	</p>
<p>Whither he should go from Corinth, was as yet uncertain, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:4">1 Corinthians 16:4</span> .</p>
