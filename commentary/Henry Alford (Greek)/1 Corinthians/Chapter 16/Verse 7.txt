<p><strong> 7.</strong> ] <strong> For I am not willing, this time to see you in passing</strong> . There is a slight, but a very slight, reference to his change of purpose (see above); but we must not take ἄρτι with θέλω (which Meyer charges Neander with doing, but clearly in error, see Pfl. u. Leit. p. 415 note): rather the ἄρτι refers to the <em> occasion</em> , the news from ‘them of Chloe,’ which had made it advisable that he should not <em> now</em> pay them a mere passing visit.
	</p>
