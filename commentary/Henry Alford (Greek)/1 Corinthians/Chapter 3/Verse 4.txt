<p><strong> 4.</strong> ] He names <em> but two</em> of the foregoing designations, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:12">1 Corinthians 1:12</span> ; intending, both there more fully, and here briefly, rather to give a <em> sample of the sectarian spirit</em> prevalent, than to describe, as matter of fact, any sects into which they were actually divided: see note there, and on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:6">1 Corinthians 4:6</span> . Meyer sees in the mention here of Paul and Apollos only, a reference to the two methods of teaching which have been treated of in this section: but as I have before said, the German Commentators are misled by too <em> definite</em> a view of the Corinthian parties.
