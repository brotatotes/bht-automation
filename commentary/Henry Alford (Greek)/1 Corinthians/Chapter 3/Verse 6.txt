<p><strong> 6.</strong> ] The similitude is to a <em> tilled field</em> ( γεώργιον , 1Co 3:9 ): the <em> plants</em> are the Corinthians, as members of Christ, vines bearing fruit: these do not yet appear in the construction: so that I prefer, with De Wette, supplying nothing after ἐφύτευσα and ἐπότισεν , regarding merely the <em> acts themselves</em> , as in E. V. If any thing be supplied, it must be ὑμᾶς , which would but ill fit <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:7">1 Corinthians 3:7</span> .
	</p>
<p>Apollos was sent over to Corinth after Paul had left it ( Act 18:27 ), at his own request, and remained there preaching during Paul’s journey through Upper Asia (ib. Act 19:1 ).</p>
