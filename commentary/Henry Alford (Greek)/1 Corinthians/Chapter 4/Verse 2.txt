<p><strong> 2.</strong> ] <strong> Moreover, here (on earth)</strong> (see var. readd. and reff. <strong> ὧδε</strong> is emphatic, and points to what follows, that though in the case of stewards enquiry was necessarily made <em> here below</em> , yet he, God’s steward, awaited no such enquiry ὑπὸ ἀνθρωπίνης ἡμέρας , but one at the coming of the Lord. Lachmann, I cannot but think somewhat strangely, places ὧδε at the end of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:1">1 Corinthians 4:1</span> ; οἰκονόμους μυστηρίων θεοῦ ὧδε . Stanley takes ὧδε for ‘in this matter,’ and supports the meaning by <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="re+13:10">Revelation 13:10</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="re+13:18">Revelation 13:18</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="re+14:12">Revelation 14:12</span> ; Rev 17:9 ) <strong> enquiry is made in the case of stewards</strong> (or, <em> it is required</em> in the case of stewards), <strong> in order that</strong> (or <em> that</em> , the purport of the requirement expressed as its purpose) <strong> a man may be found</strong> (proved to be) <strong> faithful</strong> (emph.).</p>
