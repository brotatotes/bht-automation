<p><strong> 7.</strong> ] <strong> For</strong> (reason why this puffing up should be avoided) <strong> who separates thee</strong> (distinguishes thee from others? meaning, that all such conceits of pre-eminence are unfounded. That <em> pre-eminence</em> , and not merely distinction (Meyer), is meant, is evident from what follows? <strong> And</strong> ( δέ <em> connects interrogative clauses</em> , as Od. α . 225, τίς δαίς , τίς δὲ ὅμιλος ὅδ ʼ ἔπλετο ; and Il. ε . 704, ἔνθα τίνα πρῶτον , τίνα δ ʼ ὕστατον ἐξενάριξεν ; See Hartung, Partikellehre, i. 169) <strong> what hast thou which thou receivedst not</strong> (‘ <em> from God</em> ’ not, ‘from me as thy father in the faith’)? <strong> but if</strong> (which I concede; στέγαι δὲ εἰ καὶ ἡμῖν αὐτοῖς εἰσιν , ἀλλὰ μὰ Δί ʼ οὐχ ἵπποις ; Xen. Cyr. vi. 1. 14. Hartung, i. 140) <strong> thou receivedst</strong> it, &amp;c. He speaks not only to the leaders, but to the members of parties, who imagined themselves superior to those of other parties, as if all, for every good thing, were not dependent on God the Giver.</p>
