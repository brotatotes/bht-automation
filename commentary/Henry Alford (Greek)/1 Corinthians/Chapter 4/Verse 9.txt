<p><strong> 9.</strong> ] <strong> For</strong> (and there is abundant reason for this wish in our present afflicted state) <strong> I think, God set forth</strong> (before the eyes of the world, the similitude is in θέατρον following) <strong> us the Apostles</strong> (meaning all the Apostles, principally himself and Apollos) <strong> last</strong> (the rendering of Erasm., Clav., Beza, al., <em> us who were last called to be Apostles</em> , q. d. τοὺς ἀπ . τοὺς ἐσχ ., or τοὺς ἐσχ . ἀποστ ., is ungrammatical.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐσχάτους</strong> , <em> last and vilest</em> : not, ‘respectu priorum,’ <em> last, as the prophets were before us</em> , as Corn.-a-lap., and in part, Bengel) <strong> as persons condemned to death</strong> ( ὡς καταδίκους , Chrys. Tertullian seems to define the meaning too closely when, De Pudic. 14, vol. ii. p. 1006, he interprets it ‘ <em> veluti bestiarios</em> .’ Dion. Hal. vii. 35, says of the Tarpeian rock, ὅθεν αὐτοῖς ἔθος βάλλειν τοὺς ἐπιθανατίους ) <strong> for we are become a spectacle</strong> ( θέατρον = θέαμα : so Achilles Tatius, i. p. 55 (Kypke), and θέατρα ποιητῶν , Æschines, Dial. Socr. iii. 20: see θεατριζόμενοι , Heb 10:33 ) <strong> to the world, as well to angels</strong> ( <em> good angels</em> : ἄγγελοι absol., never either includes, or signifies, <em> bad angels</em> ) <strong> as to men</strong> ( κόσμῳ being afterwards specialized into angels and men).</p>
