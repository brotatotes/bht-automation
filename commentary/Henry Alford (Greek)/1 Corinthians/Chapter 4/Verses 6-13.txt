<p><strong> 6 13.</strong> ] <em> He explains to them</em> ( 1Co 4:6 ) <em> that the mention hitherto of himself and Apollos</em> (and by parity of reasoning, of Cephas and of Christ, in ch. 1Co 1:12 ) <em> has a more general design</em> , viz. <em> to abstract them from all party spirit and pride: which pride he then blames, and puts to shame by depicting, as a contrast, the low and afflicted state of the Apostles themselves</em> .</p>
