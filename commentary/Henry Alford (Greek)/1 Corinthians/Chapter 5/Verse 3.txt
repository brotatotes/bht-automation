<p><strong> 3</strong> .] ἐγὼ μὲν γάρ , <strong> I for my part</strong> … ‘ego certe:’ so Aristoph. Plut. 355, μὰ Δί ʼ, ἐγὼ μὲν οὔ : see Hartung, Partikellchre, ii. 413.
	</p>
<p><strong> ὡς παρών</strong> , <strong> as if really present</strong> , not, <em> as being present in spirit</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> τὸν οὕτως τοῦτο κατ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] The <em> object</em> is put foremost for emphasis’ sake, and after several intervening clauses, taken up again with τὸν τοιοῦτον , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:5">1 Corinthians 5:5</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> οὕτως</strong> , Meyer thinks, alludes to <em> some peculiarly offensive method</em> in which he had brought about the marriage, which was known to the Corinthians, but unknown to us. Olsh. understands it, ‘ <em> under such circumstances</em> ,’ ‘being such as he is, a member of Christ’s body.’ But this, being before patent, would hardly be thus emphatically denoted. Perhaps after all, τοῦτο κατεργασάμενον refers to πορνεία generally, οὕτως to <strong> τοιαύτη</strong> πορνεία , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:1">1 Corinthians 5:1</span> .</p>
