<p><strong> 19</strong> .] Justification of the εἰς τὸ ἴδ . σῶμ . ἁμαρτ . above, and this by an amplification of the above σῶμα τῷ κυρίῳ , and ἓν πνεῦμά ἐστιν . <strong> Your body</strong> (i.e. the body of each man among you, but put singular, to keep, as in ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:16">1 Corinthians 3:16</span> , the unity of the idea of God’s temple, or perhaps because <em> the body</em> in its attributes is in question here) <strong> is the temple of</strong> (possessed by, as His residence: <em> the</em> temple, not a temple, see note on ch. 1Co 3:16 ) <strong> the Holy Spirit who is in you</strong> (reminiscence of the reality of His indwelling), <strong> whom ye have from God</strong> (reminiscence, <em> whose</em> Spirit He is, and so preparation for the following inference), <strong> and are not your own</strong> (so that ye have no right to alienate your body, not being <em> yours</em> ).</p>
