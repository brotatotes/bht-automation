<p><strong> 3</strong> .] The same glorious office of Christians is again referred to, and even a more striking point of contrast brought out.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀγγέλους</strong> ] always, where not otherwise specified, <em> good angels</em> : and therefore here; the λειτουργικὰ πνεύματα of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:14">Hebrews 1:14</span> ; but exactly <em> how</em> we shall judge them, is not revealed to us. Chrys., Theodoret, Œcum., Theophyl., and most Commentators interpret it of <em> bad angels</em> , or <em> of bad and good</em> together: and Chrys. as before, understands that the bad angels will be condemned by comparison with us, ὅταν γὰρ αἱ ἀσώματοι δυνάμεις αὐταὶ ἔλαττον ἡμῶν εὑρεθῶσιν ἔχουσαι τῶν σάρκα περιβεβλημένων , χαλεπώτερον δώσουσι δίκην . p. 138. But see above on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:2">1 Corinthians 6:2</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> μήτιγε</strong> <strong> , to say nothing of</strong> , ‘ <em> ut omittam</em> :’ so Demosth. p. 24. 23, οὐκ ἔνι δ ʼ αὐτὸν ἀργοῦντα οὐδὲ τοῖς φίλοις ἐπιτάττειν ὑπὲρ αὐτοῦ τι ποιεῖν , μή τί γε δὴ τοῖς θεοῖς . See Hartung, Partikellehre, ii. 155.
	</p>
<p><strong> βιωτικά</strong> , matters relating to ὁ βίος , <em> a man’s livelihood</em> : see ref. and Clem [12] Alex. Strom. vii. 12 [69], p. 873 P., θλιβόμενον ἐπικουφίζει παραμνθίαις …, ταῖς βιωτικαῖς χρείαις ἐπικουρῶν . It is a word of later Greek usage, see Lexx. In classic Greek it would be τὰ τοῦ βίου .
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [12] Clement of Alexandria, fl. 194
	</p>
<p>The meaning here then will be <strong> civil causes</strong> , matters of <em> meum</em> and <em> tuum</em> , as De Wette. The sense is best with only a comma at κρινοῦμεν .</p>
