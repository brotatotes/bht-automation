<p><strong> 24</strong> .] <em> The rule it again repeated</em> , but with the addition παρὰ θεῷ , reminding them of the relations of Christ’s freedman and Christ’s slave, and of the price paid, just mentioned: of that relation to God in which they stood by means of their Christian calling. “The <em> usual</em> rendering, <em> Deo inspectante</em> (Grot.), i.e. ‘perpetuo memores, vos in ejus conspectu versari’ (Beza), does not so well suit the <em> local</em> word μενέτω .” Meyer.</p>
