<p><strong> 27</strong> .] τὸ οὕτως εἶναι <em> restated</em> and <em> illustrated</em> : neither the married nor the unmarried are to <em> seek for a change</em> . The general recommendation here is referable alike to <em> all</em> cases of marriage, and does not touch on the prohibition of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:10">1 Corinthians 7:10</span> , only <em> dissuading from a spirit of change, in consideration of the</em> <strong> ἐνεστῶσα ἀνάγκη</strong> . It seems better to take the verse thus, than with Meyer and De Wette, to regard it as inserted to guard against misunderstanding of the preceding γνώμη of the Apostle.
	</p>
<p><strong> λέλυσαι</strong> does not imply <em> previous marriage</em> , but as Phot [30] , οὐχὶ πρὸς τοὺς συναφθέντας , εἶτα διαλυθέντας , … ἀλλα ʼ ἁπλῶς πρὸς τοὺς μὴ συνελθόντας ὅλως εἰς γάμου κοινωνίαν , ἀλλὰ λελυμένους ὄντας τοῦ τοιούτου δεσμοῦ , and Estius, “intelligit liberum a conjugio, sive uxorem aliquando habuerit, sive non.”
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [30] Photius, Bp. of Constantinople, 858 891</p>
