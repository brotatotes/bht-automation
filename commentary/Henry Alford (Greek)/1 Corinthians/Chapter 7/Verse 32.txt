<p><strong> 32. </strong> <strong> θέλω δὲ</strong> <strong> …</strong> ] <strong> But</strong> (i.e. since this is so since the time is short, and <em> that</em> , in order that we Christians may sit loose to the world) <strong> I wish you to be without wordly cares</strong> (undistracted). Then he explains how this touches on the subject.
	</p>
<p><strong> πῶς ἀρέσῃ</strong> <strong> how he may please</strong> : πῶς ἀρέσει ‘how he <em> shall</em> please.’ The variety being not in reality a various reading, but only an itacism, I retain the form found in the most ancient MSS.</p>
