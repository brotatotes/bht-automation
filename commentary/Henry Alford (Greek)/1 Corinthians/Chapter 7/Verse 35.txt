<p><strong> 35</strong> .] <em> Caution against mistaking what has been said</em> for an <em> imperative order</em> , whereas it was only <em> a suggestion for their best interest</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> τοῦτο</strong> ] <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:32-34">1 Corinthians 7:32-34</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> πρὸς τὸ ὑμ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> αὐτ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> σύμ</strong> .] <strong> For your own</strong> (emph.) <strong> profit</strong> , i.e. not for my own purposes not to exercise my apostolic authority: <strong> not that I may cast a snare</strong> (lit. ‘a noose;’ the metaphor is from throwing the noose in hunting, or in war; so Herod. vii. 85, ἡ δὲ μάχη τούτεων τῶν ἀνδρῶν ἥδε . ἐπεὰν συμμίσγωσι τοῖς πολεμίοις , βάλλουσι τὰς σειρὰς ἐπ ʼ ἄκρῳ βρόχους ἔχουσας , ὅτευ δ ʼ ἂν τύχῃ ἤντε ἵππου ἤντε ἀνθρώπου , ἐπ ʼ ἑωϋτὸν ἕλκει · οἱ δὲ ἐν ἕρκεσι ἐμπαλασσόμενοι διαφθείρονται . See other examples in Wetst.) <strong> over you</strong> (i.e. entangle and encumber you with difficult precepts), <strong> but with a view to seemliness</strong> (cf. Rom 13:13 ) <strong> and waiting upon the Lord without distraction</strong> . De W. remarks, that πρὸς τὸ παρεδρεύειν τῷ κ . ἀπερ . would be the easier construction. Stanley draws out the parallel to the story in ref. Luke.</p>
