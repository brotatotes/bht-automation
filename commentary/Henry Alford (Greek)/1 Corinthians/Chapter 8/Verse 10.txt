<p><strong> 10</strong> .] <em> Explanation how the</em> <strong> πρόσκομμα</strong> <em> may arise</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> τίς</strong> , scil. (see below) ἀσθενὴς ὤν .
	</p>
<p><strong> τὸν ἔχοντα γνῶσιν</strong> seems to imply that the weak brother <em> is aware of this</em> , and <em> looks up</em> <em> to thee as such</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐν εἰδωλείῳ κατ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] See on εἰδωλοθ ., <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+8:1">1 Corinthians 8:1</span> .
	</p>
<p>εἰδωλεῖον , as Ποσειδεῖον , Ἀπολλωνεῖον , Ἰσεῖον , &amp;c.
	</p>
<p>“ <strong> οἰκοδομηθήσεται</strong> is not a <em> vox media</em> , as Le Clerc, Elsner, Wolf, al., nor is it <em> impelletur</em> , as Castal., Bengel, Kypke, al., nor <em> confirmabitur</em> , as Syr., Grot., Billroth, al.” (Mey.), but as Meyer and De Wette, <em> ædificabitur</em> , not without a certain irony, seeing it is accompanied by ἀσθενοῦς ὄντος , for thus the building up would be without solid foundation a <em> ruinosa ædificatio</em> , as Calv.</p>
