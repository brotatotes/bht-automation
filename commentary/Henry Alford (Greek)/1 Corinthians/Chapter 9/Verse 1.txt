<p><strong> 1</strong> .] He sets forth, (1) his <em> independence</em> of men (contrast 1Co 9:19 ); (2) his apostolic <em> office</em> (for the order, see var. readd.): (3) his <em> dignity</em> as an Apostle, in having been vouchsafed a sight of Christ Jesus our Lord; (4) his <em> efficiency</em> in the office, as having converted them to God.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐλεύθ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] So that the resolution of ch. 1Co 8:13 is not necessitated by any <em> dependence</em> on my part <em> on the opinion of others</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἑώρακα</strong> ] Not, during the life of our Lord on earth, as Schrader, nor is such an idea supported by <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:16">2 Corinthians 5:16</span> ; see note there; but, in the <em> appearance</em> of the Lord to him <em> by the way to Damascus</em> (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:17">Acts 9:17</span> ; ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:8">1 Corinthians 15:8</span> ; see Neand. Pfl. u. Leit. p. 151, note); and also, secondarily, in those <em> other visions</em> and <em> appearance</em> , recorded by him, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:9">Acts 18:9</span> (?), <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:18">Acts 22:18</span> , and possibly on other occasions since his conversion. οὐ μικρὸν δὲ καὶ τοῦτο ἀξίωμα ἦν , Chrys. Hom. xxi. p.180.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐν κυρίῳ</strong> is not a mere humble qualification of τὸ ἔργον μου , as Chrys. ib., τουτέστι τοῦ θεοῦ τὸ ἔργον ἐστίν , οὐκ ἐμοῦ , but designates, as elsewhere, the <em> element, in which</em> the work is done: they were his work <em> as an Apostle</em> , i.e. <em> as the servant of the Lord enabled by the Lord</em> , and SO IN THE LORD. See ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:15">1 Corinthians 4:15</span> .</p>
