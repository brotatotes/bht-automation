<p><strong> 20. </strong> <strong> τοῖς Ἰουδ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ὡς Ἰουδ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] See examples, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:3">Acts 16:3</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:26">Acts 21:26</span> . οὐκ εἶπεν , Ἰουδαῖος , ἀλλ ʼ <strong> ὡς</strong> Ἰουδαῖος , ἵνα δείξῃ ὅτι οἰκονομία τὸ πρᾶγμα ἦν , Theophyl. after Chrys. The <em> Jews</em> here are not <em> Jewish converts</em> , who would be already <em> won</em> in the sense of this passage.
	</p>
<p><strong> τοῖς ὑπὸ νόμον</strong> <strong> …</strong> ] These again are not Jewish converts (see above); nor <em> proselytes</em> , who would not be thus <em> distinguished</em> from other Jews, but are much the same as Ἰουδαῖοι , only to the number of these the Apostle <em> did not belong</em> , not being himself ( <strong> αὐτός</strong> contrasts with <strong> ὡς</strong> above) under the law, whereas he <em> was nationally a Jew</em> .</p>
