<p><strong> 4</strong> .] He resumes the questions which had been interrupted by giving the proof of his Apostleship.
	</p>
<p><strong> μὴ οὐκ ἔχ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] μή <em> asks the question</em> : οὐκ ἔχομεν is the <em> thing in question</em> : <strong> Is it so, that we have not power.…</strong> ? The plur. seems to apply to <em> Paul alone</em> : for though Barnabas is introduced momentarily in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:6">1 Corinthians 9:6</span> , there can be no reference to him in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:11">1 Corinthians 9:11</span> . It may perhaps be used as pointing out a matter of <em> right</em> , which any would have had on the same conditions (see 1Co 9:11 ), and as thus not belonging personally to Paul, as do the things predicated in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:1-2">1 Corinthians 9:1-2</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:15">1 Corinthians 9:15</span> . This however will not apply to <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:12">1 Corinthians 9:12</span> , where the emphatic ἡμεῖς <em> is</em> personal.
	</p>
<p><strong> φαγεῖν κ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> πεῖν</strong> ] <strong> To eat and to drink</strong> , sc. <em> at the cost of the churches</em> : not with any reference to the eating of things offered to idols (as Schrader, iv. 132), nor to Jewish distinctions of clean and unclean (as Billroth and Olshausen); see below, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:6-7">1 Corinthians 9:6-7</span> .</p>
