<p><strong> 11</strong> .] <strong> But</strong> (= whereas) <strong> he that hateth his brother</strong> (see above) <strong> is in the darkness</strong> (has never come out of it: corresponds to ἐν τῷ φωτὶ μένει above: denotes his state, whereas περιπατεῖ indicates more his outward acts), <strong> and walketh in the darkness, and knoweth not where</strong> (see reff. ποῦ with a verb of motion obviously <em> includes</em> that motion: but it includes also the spot on which the motion is taking place: e. g., here, not only the destination to which, but also the way by which, he is going. In some places, this cannot be pressed, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:14">John 8:14</span> , where πόθεν ἦλθον and ποῦ ( ποῖ ) ὑπάγω are opposed to one another) <strong> he goeth, because the darkness blinded</strong> (it is a matter of old standing: “ <em> blinded</em> ,” and not “ <em> hath blinded</em> ,” because it is no new effect of a state into which he has lately come, but the long past work of a state which is supposed to be gone by, and is not) <strong> his eyes</strong> .</p>
