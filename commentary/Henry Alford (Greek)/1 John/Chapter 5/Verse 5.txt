<p><strong> 5</strong> .] If it be asked, How does our faith overcome the world? this verse furnishes the answer; because it brings us into union with Jesus Christ the Son of God, making us as He is, and partakers of His victory, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:33">John 16:33</span> . Through this belief we are born again as sons of God; we have Him in us, One greater than he who is in the world, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:4">1 John 4:4</span> . And this conclusion is put in the form of a triumphant question: What other person can do it? Who that believes this, can fail to do it? <strong> Who is be that conquereth the world, except he that believeth that Jesus is the Son of God?</strong> By comparing <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:1">1 John 5:1</span> a, we find 1) that ὁ χριστός there answers to ὁ υἱὸς τοῦ θεοῦ here; 2) that by the combination of the two verses, we get the proposition of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:4">1 John 5:4</span> a.
	</p>
<p>Episcopius gives well the meaning: “Lustrate universum mundum et ostendite mihi vel unum, de quo vere affirmari possit, quod mundum vincat, qui Christianus et fide hac præditus non est.”</p>
