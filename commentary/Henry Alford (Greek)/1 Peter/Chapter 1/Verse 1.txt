<p><strong> 1</strong> .] <strong> Peter</strong> (the Greek form of the name Cephas, a stone, given him by our Lord, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:43">John 1:43</span> ; in 2Pe 1:1 it is “Symeon Peter”) <strong> an apostle of Jesus Christ, to the elect strangers</strong> (see on <strong> παρεπιδήμοις</strong> , Heb 11:13 note. <strong> ἐκλεκτοῖς</strong> , chosen of God to His adopted family in Christ. The construction is irregularly carried on from ἐκλ . by κατὰ πρόγνωσιν κ . τ . λ . below, where see) <strong> of the dispersion</strong> (i. e. belonging to the <em> Jewish</em> dispersion, as in reff. This leading character of the readers of 1 Peter has been acknowledged generally: see testimonies in Prolegg. At the same time, as there argued (§ iii. 3 ff.), there is no reason to exclude Gentile Christians from among them, as forming part of the Israel of God. Indeed, such readers are presupposed in the Epistle itself: cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:14">1 Peter 1:14</span> ; ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:10">1 Peter 2:10</span> ; 1Pe 4:3 ) <strong> of Pontus</strong> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:9">Acts 2:9</span> , note), <strong> Galatia</strong> (see Prolegg. to Gal. § ii.), <strong> Cappadocia</strong> (Acts, ut supra), <strong> Asia</strong> (not quite as in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:9">Acts 2:9</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:6">Acts 16:6</span> , where Phrygia is distinguished from it: here it must be included) <strong> and Bithynia</strong> (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:7">Acts 16:7</span> , note: and on the whole geographical extent embraced by the terms, and inferences to be gathered from their order of sequence, see Prolegg. § iii. 6 ff., iv. 17).</p>
