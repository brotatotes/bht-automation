<p><strong> 5</strong> .] <strong> For</strong> (enforcing of the same by example) <strong> in this manner</strong> (i. e. with the ornament of a meek and quiet spirit) <strong> formerly also</strong> (as well as you, if you obey) <strong> the holy women</strong> ( <strong> ἅγιαι</strong> , as in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:70">Luke 1:70</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:21">Acts 3:21</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:5">Ephesians 3:5</span> ; women of blessed note in the sacred history as servants of God), <strong> who hoped</strong> ( <strong> ἐλπίζουσαι</strong> , part. of the imperfect, according to Winer, § 45. 1: but is it not rather the indefinite pres. part. defining the quality or office, as ὁ σπείρων , ὁ πειράζων ?) <strong> in God</strong> (i. e. whose hope was directed towards, and rested in, God. Bengel remarks, “vera sanctitas, spes in Deum: est hoc epitheton pars subjecti”), <strong> adorned themselves, being in subjection to their own husbands</strong> (this clause describes the state in which the adornment was put on, to which it belonged: being thus in subjection, they were adorned with the meek and quiet spirit which belongs to it):</p>
