<p><strong> 13</strong> .] <strong> but in as far as</strong> ( <strong> καθό</strong> , not “ <em> in that</em> ,” “ <em> inasmuch as</em> ,” E. V., nor <em> quando</em> , Pott: see reff.) <strong> ye are partakers with the sufferings of Christ</strong> (i. e. have a share, in your own persons, of those sufferings which He personally bare: cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:10">2 Corinthians 4:10</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="php+3:10">Philippians 3:10</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:13">Hebrews 13:13</span> &amp;c. It is not the sufferings of Christ mystical in His Body the church (cf. Col 1:24 ) which are meant: in these the readers might bear their part, but could hardly be said κοινωνεῖν ), <strong> rejoice, that</strong> ( <strong> ἵνα</strong> simply of the scope of that joy, as the preparation for what follows) <strong> ye may also at</strong> (in, i. e. “in the day or time of:” not to be taken with χαρῆτε , as indicating that at which or because of which the joy takes place) <strong> the revelation of His glory rejoice</strong> (aor.: χαίρετε before, of the habit of life; now <strong> χαρῆτε</strong> , of the single event of that day) <strong> exulting</strong> (“quia prius illud cum dolore et tristitia mixtum est, secundum cum exultatione conjungit.” Calv.).</p>
