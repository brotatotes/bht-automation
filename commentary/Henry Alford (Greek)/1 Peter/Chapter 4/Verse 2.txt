<p><strong> 2</strong> .] <strong> with a view</strong> ( <strong> εἰς τό</strong> depends on ὁπλίσασθε , the intermediate general sentence being parenthetical) <strong> no longer</strong> ( <strong> μηκέτι</strong> , subjective) <strong> by the lusts of men</strong> (as your rule: what is called the normal dative: not, as Wies. al., = δικαιοσύνῃ ζήσωμεν , ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:24">1 Peter 2:24</span> ; cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:10-13">Romans 6:10-13</span> ; this βιῶσαι κ . τ . λ . is a very different matter from ζῇν in those places. <strong> ἀνθρώπων</strong> , put forward for contemptuous emphasis, as opposed to θεοῦ , which gains more majesty by not being thus put forward. What the lusts are, is shewn in 1Pe 4:3 ), <strong> but by the will of God</strong> (according to that which God wills, as your rule) <strong> to live</strong> (the 2 aor. βιῶναι is more common) <strong> the rest of your time in the flesh</strong> (cf. τὸν τῆς παροικίας ὑμῶν χρόνον , ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:17">1 Peter 1:17</span> .
