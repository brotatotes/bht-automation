<p><strong> 6</strong> .] <strong> Humble yourselves</strong> (on the medial signification of some verbs in the aor. 1 pass. in the N. T., see Winer, § 39. 2. The commonest example is ἀποκριθείς . Cf. also διεκρίθη , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:21">Matthew 21:21</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:20">Romans 4:20</span> , &amp;c.) <strong> therefore</strong> (the same spirit as before continues through this and the following verses: the μέριμνα here, and the παθήματα , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:9">1 Peter 5:9</span> , keeping in mind their persecutions and anxieties, as also does κραταιὰν χεῖρα , see below) <strong> under the mighty hand of God</strong> (on the expression, see reff., LXX. The strong hand of God is laid on the afflicted and suffering, and it is for them to acknowledge it in lowliness of mind), <strong> that He may exalt you</strong> (the Apostle refers to the often repeated saying of our Lord, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:13">Matthew 23:13</span> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:11">Luke 14:11</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:14">Luke 18:14</span> . The same is also found in the O. T., <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ps+18:27">Psalms 18:27</span> ; Pro 29:23 ) <strong> in</strong> (the) <strong> time</strong> ( <strong> appointed</strong> ) ( <strong> ἐν καιρῷ</strong> is one of those phrases in which the article is constantly omitted: see reff., and Winer, § 19. 1. This humility implies patience, waiting God’s time: “ut nimiæ festinationi simul obviam eat,” Calv. The καιρός need not necessarily be understood as Bengel (“Petrus sæpe spectat diem judicii”) of the end; it is more general: cf. ἐν καιροῖς ἰδίοις , 1Ti 6:15 ):</p>
