<p><strong> 2</strong> .] <strong> προπαθόντες</strong> , <strong> having previously suffered</strong> : reff. On the fact, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:0">Acts 16:0</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐπαῤῥησιασ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] Lünemann seems to be right (against De W.) in rendering it <strong> we were confident</strong> , not ‘ <em> we were free of speech</em> .’ See however, on the other side, Ellic.’s note.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἡμῶν</strong> , because all true confidence is in God as <em> our God</em> . This word reproduces the feeling with which Paul and Silas opened their ministry among them: διὰ τὸν ἐνδυναμοῦντα θεὸν τοῦτο ποιῆσαι τεθαῤῥήκαμεν . Œcum.
	</p>
<p><strong> λαλῆσαι</strong> is infinitive of the object after ἐπαῤῥησ . <strong> we had the confidence to speak</strong> : as E. V., <strong> were bold to speak</strong> . This seems more probable than with De W., Mey. on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:20">Ephesians 6:20</span> , and Ellic., to regard it as the epexegetical inf. “defining still more clearly the <em> oral</em> nature of the boldness.” Chrys. can hardly be quoted on that side, as Ellic. doubtfully.
	</p>
<p><strong> τοῦ θεοῦ</strong> , for solemnity, to add to the weight of their εἴσοδος .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐν πολλῷ ἀγῶνι</strong> ] <strong> in</strong> (amidst) <strong> much conflict</strong> , viz. under outward circumstances conflicting much with our work: and therefore that work could be no κενόν , which was thus maintained.</p>
