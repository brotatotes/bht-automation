<p><strong> δεόμενοι</strong> belongs to the question of 1Th 3:9 q. d., ‘what thanks can we render, &amp;c., proportioned to the earnestness of our prayers, &amp;c.?’ So that δεόμενοι would best be rendered <strong> praying as we do</strong> .
	</p>
<p><strong> εἰς τό</strong> direction, or aim, of the prayers.
	</p>
<p><strong> καταρτίσαι τὰ ὑστ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] τὰ ἐλλείποντα πληρῶσαι , Thdrt.: cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:12">2 Corinthians 9:12</span> . These <strong> ὑστερήματα</strong> were consequences of their being as yet novices in the faith: partly theoretical, e.g. their want of stability respecting the παρουσία , and of fixed ideas respecting those who had fallen asleep in Christ, partly practical, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:1">1 Thessalonians 4:1</span> . One can hardly conceive a greater perverseness than that of Baur, who takes this passage for a proof that the Thessalonian church had been long in the faith.</p>
