<p><strong> 9</strong> .] And this vigour of life shews itself in the earnest desire of abundant thanksgiving: so the <strong> γάρ</strong> accounts for, and specifies the action of, the ζωή just mentioned.
	</p>
<p><strong> τίνα</strong> , <strong> what</strong> i.e. what sufficient ?
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀνταπ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] reff.: <em> thanks</em> is itself a <em> return</em> for God’s favours: see especially ref. Ps.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐπί</strong> , may be taken as above (ref. y), or as <strong> for</strong> , <strong> in return for</strong> : the two meanings in fact run up into one.
	</p>
<p><strong> πάσ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τῇ χαρᾷ</strong> , <strong> all the joy</strong> : i.e. not the joy from so many different sources, but the joy in its largeness and depth: q. d. τῇ χαρᾷ τῇ μεγάλῃ .
	</p>
<p><strong> ᾗ</strong> attr. for ἥν , see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:10">Matthew 2:10</span> ; not as <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:29">John 3:29</span> , see note there.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἔμπρ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> θεοῦ ἡμ</strong> <strong> .</strong> shews the joy to be of the very highest and best, no joy of this world, or of personal pride, but one which will bear, and does bear, the searching eye of God, and is <em> His</em> joy ( Joh 15:11 ).</p>
