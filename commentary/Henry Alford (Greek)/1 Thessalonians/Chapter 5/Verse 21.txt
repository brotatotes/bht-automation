<p><strong> 21</strong> .] <strong> πάντα δὲ δοκιμάζετε</strong> refers back to the foregoing: <strong> but try all</strong> (such χαρίσματα ): see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:10">1 Corinthians 12:10</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:29">1 Corinthians 14:29</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:1">1 John 4:1</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> τὸ καλὸν κατέχετε</strong> is best regarded as beginning a new sentence, and opposed to ἀπὸ παντ . εἴδ . κ . τ . λ . which follows: not however as disconnected from the preceding, but suggested by it. In this, and in all things, <strong> hold fast the good</strong> .</p>
