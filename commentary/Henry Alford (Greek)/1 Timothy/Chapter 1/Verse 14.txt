<p><strong> 14</strong> .] <strong> but</strong> (contrast still to his former state, and epexegetical of ἠλεήθη ; not to ἠλεήθ ., ‘not only so, but,’ as Chr., De W., al.) <strong> the grace of our Lord</strong> (His mercy shewn to me but not in strengthening me for His work, endowing me with spiritual gifts, &amp;c., as Chr., al.: for the ἠλεήθην is the ruling idea through the whole, and he recurs to it again <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:16">1 Timothy 1:16</span> , never having risen above it to that of his higher gifts) <strong> superabounded</strong> (to be taken not comparatively, but superlatively, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:20">Romans 5:20</span> , note) <strong> with</strong> (accompanied by) <strong> faith and love</strong> (see the same pauline expression, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:23">Ephesians 6:23</span> , and note there) <strong> which are</strong> ( <strong> τῆς</strong> probably improperly used by attraction for τῶν : there is no reason why πίστις as well as ἀγάπη should not be designated as ἐν χριστῷ Ἰησοῦ ) <strong> in</strong> (as their element, and, as it were, <em> home</em> ) <strong> Christ Jesus</strong> (all these three abounded grace, the objective side of God’s ἔλεος to him: Christian faith and love the contrast to his former hatred and unbelief, God’s gifts, the subjective side. This is much better than to regard μετὰ πίστεως καὶ ἀγάπης as giving that wherein the χάρις ὑπερεπλεόνασεν ):</p>
