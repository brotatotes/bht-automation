<p><strong> 16</strong> .] Not a repetition of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:4">1 Timothy 5:4</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:8">1 Timothy 5:8</span> , but an extension of the same duty to more distant relatives than those there spoken of. <strong> If any believing</strong> [ <strong> man or</strong> ] <strong> woman has widows</strong> (in [his or] her family dependent in any degree, however distant e.g. as sister, or sister-in-law, aunt, niece, cousin, &amp;c.), <strong> let such person relieve them</strong> (see above, 1Ti 5:10 ), <strong> and let the church not be burdened</strong> (with their support: “later and less correct form for βαρύνειν ;” see Ellic.), <strong> that it may relieve those who are widows in reality</strong> (really χῆραι destitute of help).</p>
