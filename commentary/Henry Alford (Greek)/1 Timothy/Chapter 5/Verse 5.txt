<p><strong> ἡ ὄντως χήρα</strong> , as opposed to the widow just described; <strong> κ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> μεμονωμένη</strong> , as contrasting her condition with that of her who has children or grandchildren. Thus what follows is said more for moral eulogy of such a widow, than as commending her to the charity of the church: but at the same time, as pointing out that one who thus places her hopes and spends her time, is best deserving of the Church’s help.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἤλπικεν</strong> , ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:10">1 Timothy 4:10</span> , has set and continues to set her hope.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐπὶ τὸν θεόν</strong> , on God as its portion and ultimate aim, as distinguished from ἐπὶ τῷ θεῷ , ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:10">1 Timothy 4:10</span> , on God as its present stay.
	</p>
