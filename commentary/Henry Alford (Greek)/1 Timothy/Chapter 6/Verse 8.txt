<p><strong> 8</strong> .] <strong> but</strong> (contrast to the avaricious, who forget this, or knowing it do not act on it: not as De W., = οὖν , which would be a direct inference from the preceding verse) <strong> having</strong> (if we have) <strong> food</strong> (the δια - gives the sense of ‘sufficient for our continually recurring wants,’ ‘the needful supply of nourishment:’ the plur. corresponds to the plur. ἔχοντες , and implies ‘in each case’) <strong> and covering</strong> (some take it of both clothing and dwelling: perhaps rightly, but not on account of the plural: see above: Chrys., al., of clothing only, τοιαῦτα ἀμφιέννυσθαι , ἃ σκεπάσαι μόνον ἡμᾶς ὀφείλει καὶ περιστεῖλαι τὴν γύμνωσιν . These words occur together (Huther) in Sextus Empiricus ix. 1), <strong> with these</strong> (so ἀγαπάω , στέργω , χαίρω , &amp;c. take a dative of the cause or object of the feeling. See ref. Luke, and Matthiæ, § 403) <strong> we shall be sufficiently provided</strong> (the fut. has an authoritative sense: so in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:48">Matthew 5:48</span> , and Xen. Hell. ii. 3. 34, cited by Huther, ὑμεῖς οὖν , ἐὰν σωφρονῆτε , οὐ τούτου , ἀλλ ʼ ὑμῶν φείσεσθε : but is not therefore equivalent to an imperative, ‘let us be content:’ for its sense is not properly subjective but objective ‘to be sufficed,’ or ‘sufficiently provided:’ and it is passive, not middle).</p>
