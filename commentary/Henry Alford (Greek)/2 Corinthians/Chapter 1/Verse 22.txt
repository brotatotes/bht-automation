<p><strong> τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἀῤῥ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> πν</strong> <strong> .] the pledge or token of the Spirit</strong> : genitive of apposition: the Spirit <em> is</em> the token. ἀῤῥ ., πρόδομα , Hesych [3] : ἡ ἐπὶ ταῖς ὠναῖς παρὰ τῶν ὠνουμένων διδομένη προκαταβολὴ ὑπὲρ ἀσφαλείας , Etymol. in Wetst., where see examples. “It is remarkable that the same word עֵרָבוֹן , is used in the same sense in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ge+38:17-18">Genesis 38:17-18</span> , from עָרַב , to ‘mix’ or ‘exchange,’ and thence to ‘pledge,’ as <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jer+30:21">Jeremiah 30:21</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ne+5:3">Nehemiah 5:3</span> . It was therefore probably derived by the Greeks from the language of Phœnician traders, as ‘tariff,’ ‘cargo,’ are derived, in English and other modern languages, from Spanish traders.” Stanley.
