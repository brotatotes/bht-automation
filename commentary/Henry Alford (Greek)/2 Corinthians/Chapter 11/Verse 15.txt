<p><strong> 15.</strong> ] <strong> εἰ καί</strong> , <strong> if also</strong> , i.e. <em> as well as himself</em> , or perhaps better applying to the whole sentence, <strong> if, also …
	</strong></p>
<p><strong> μετασχ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ὡς</strong> , i.e. μετασχ . καὶ γίνονται ὡς : so <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:29">Romans 9:29</span> , ὡς Γόμοῤῥα ἂν ὡμοιώθημεν .
	</p>
<p><strong> αὐτός</strong> , the father of falsehood and wrong ( Joh 8:44 ), is directly opposed to δικαιοσύνη θεοῦ , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:33">Matthew 6:33</span> , that manifestation of God by which He is known to us in the Gospel, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:17">Romans 1:17</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ὧν τὸ τέλ</strong> .] <strong> Of whom</strong> (notwithstanding this disguise) <strong> the end shall be correspondent to their works</strong> (not to their pretensions).</p>
