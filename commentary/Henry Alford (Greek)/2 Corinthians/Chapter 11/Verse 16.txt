<p><strong> 16.</strong> ] <strong> πάλιν</strong> referring to <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:1">2 Corinthians 11:1</span> , not <em> repeating</em> what he had there said, but again <em> taking up the subject</em> , and expanding that request. The ἀνέχομαι of 2Co 11:1 in fact implies both requests of this verse: the not regarding him as a fool for boasting, or <em> even if they did</em> ( εἰ δὲ μήγε after a negative sentence implies ‘ <em> but if it cannot be so</em> ,’ ‘if you will not grant this,’ see reff.
	</p>
<p><strong> κἄν</strong> elliptical: the full construction would be κἂν ὡς ἄφρονα δέξασθαι δέῃ , δέξασθέ με : so in reff.) <em> as a fool</em> (i.e. yielding to me the toleration and hearing which men would not refuse even to one of whose folly they were convinced) <em> receiving him</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> κἀγώ</strong> , <em> as well as they</em> .</p>
