<p><strong> 22.</strong> ] “The three honourable appellations with which the adversaries magnified themselves, resting on their Jewish extraction, are arranged so as to form a <em> climax</em> : so that Ἑβραῖοι refers to the <em> nationality</em> , Ἰσραηλῖται to the <em> theocracy</em> (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:4">Romans 9:4</span> ff.), and σπέρμα Ἀβρ to the <em> claim to a part in the Messiah</em> (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:7">Romans 9:7</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:1">Romans 11:1</span> , al.).” Meyer. The interrogative form of the sentence is much more lively and consistent with the spirit of the context than the affirmative, as given by Erasm., Luther, Estius, al.</p>
