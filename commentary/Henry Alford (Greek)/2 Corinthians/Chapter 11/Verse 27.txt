<p><strong> 27.</strong> ] <strong> by labour and weariness, by watchings</strong> (see on ch. 2Co 6:5 ) <strong> frequently</strong> (the ἐν is here resumed, perhaps arbitrarily, perhaps also because κόπος and μόχθος are more directly instrumental, ἀγρυπν ., &amp;c., more conditionally), <strong> by hunger and thirst, by fastings frequently</strong> (voluntary fastings, ‘ad purificandam mentem et edomandam carnem,’ as Estius, see also ch. 2Co 6:5 note. De W. here too (see also Stanley) holds to ‘involuntary fastings;’ but he is clearly wrong, for νηστ . is distinguished from λιμ . κ . δίψ .), <strong> in cold and nakedness</strong> (insufficient clothing: or, literally, when thrust into prison after his scourgings, or after his shipwrecks).</p>
