<p><strong> 29.</strong> ] ‘Cura certe συμπάθειαν generat: quæ facit, ut omnium affectus in se suscipiat Christi minister, omnium personas induat, quo se accommodet omnibus,’ Calv.
	</p>
<p>Olsh., after Emmerling, strangely understands, ‘ <em> Who is weak, if I am not weak</em> ?’ i.e. ‘Who can be called weak, if I am not so?’
	</p>
<p>The <strong> ἀσθένεια</strong> of the τις may be in various ways; in <em> faith</em> , as Rom 14:1 al., or in <em> purpose</em> , or in <em> courage</em> : that of the Apostle, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:22">1 Corinthians 9:22</span> , was a sympathetic weakness, a leaning to the same infirmity for the weak brother’s sake, but also a veritable θορυβοῦμαι κ . ταράσσομαι (as Chrys., p. 614) in himself, on the weak brother’s account.
	</p>
<p><strong> τίς σκανδ</strong> .] “Non priore, sed hac versiculi parte addit <em> ego</em> : nam illic infirmo se accommodat: hic dissimilem se scandalizantis fatetur, partes a scandalizante neglectas scandalizati causa <em> ipse</em> suscipiens. Partes a scandalizante neglectæ sunt amor, prudentia, &amp;c. Idem tamen Paulus etiam partes scandalizati, sive incommodum quod scandalizatus sentit, in se suscipit.” Bengel.
	</p>
<p><strong> πυροῦμαι</strong> , with zeal, or with indignation.</p>
