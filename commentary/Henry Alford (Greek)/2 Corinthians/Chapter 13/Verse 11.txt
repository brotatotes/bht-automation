<p><strong> 11.</strong> ] <em> General exhortations</em> . “Severius scripserat Paulus in tractatione; nunc benignius, re tamen ipsa non dimissa.” Bengel.
	</p>
<p><strong> χαίρ</strong> ., <strong> rejoice</strong> , scil. <em> in the Lord</em> , as <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="php+3:1">Philippians 3:1</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="php+4:4">Philippians 4:4</span> . So also <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:16">1 Thessalonians 5:16</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> καταρτ</strong> ., τέλειοι γίνεσθε καὶ ἀναπληροῦτε τὰ λείποντα , Chrys., ib.: amend “yourselves,” Stanley.
	</p>
<p><strong> παρακαλ</strong> ., <strong> take comfort</strong> ; a recurrence in the end of the Epistle to the spirit with which it began; see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:6-7">2 Corinthians 1:6-7</span> , and, for the need they had of comfort, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:8-13">2 Corinthians 7:8-13</span> . This is better <strong> than</strong> ‘ <em> comfort</em> (or ‘ <em> exhort</em> ’) <em> one another</em> ,’ which would more naturally be expressed by παρακαλεῖτε ἀλλήλους , or ἑαυτούς , see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:18">1 Thessalonians 4:18</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:11">1 Thessalonians 5:11</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:13">Hebrews 3:13</span> ; also Heb 10:25 and note.
	</p>
<p><strong> τὸ αὐτὸ φρ</strong> . belongs to ἀγάπη , <strong> εἰρηνεύετε</strong> to εἰρήνη .
	</p>
<p><strong> καί</strong> , ‘and then.’</p>
