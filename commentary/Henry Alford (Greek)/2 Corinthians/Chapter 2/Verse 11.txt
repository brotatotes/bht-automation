<p><strong> 11.</strong> <strong> ἵνα μὴ</strong> <strong> …</strong> ] follows out the δι ʼ ὑμᾶς <strong> to prevent Satan getting any advantage over us</strong> (the Church generally: or better, <em> us Apostles)</em> , in robbing us of some of our people, viz. in causing the penitent offender to despair and fall away from the faith. Chrys. remarks: πλεονεξίαν εἰκότως ἐκάλεσεν , ὅταν καὶ διὰ τῶν ἡμετέρων κρατῇ . τὸ γὰρ δι ʼ ἁμαρτίας λαμβάνειν , ἴδιον αὐτῷ ἐστι · τὸ μέντοι διὰ μετανοίας , οὐκέτι · ἡμέτερον γάρ , οὐκ ἐκείνου , τὸ ὅπλον . p. 462. The word has yet another propriety: the offender was to be delivered over τῷ σατανᾷ εἰς ὄλεθρον τῆς σαρκός care must be taken lest we πλεονεκτηθῶμεν ὑπὸ τοῦ ς ., and his <em> soul perish likewise</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> οὐ γὰρ</strong> <strong> …] </strong> <strong> αὐτοῦ</strong> before <strong> τὰ νοήμ</strong> . for emphasis: such devices, <em> as coming from him</em> , are special matters of observation and caution to every Christian minister; much more to him who had the care of all the churches. See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:8">1 Peter 5:8</span> .
	</p>
<p>The personality and agency of the Adversary can hardly be recognized in plainer terms than in both these passages.</p>
