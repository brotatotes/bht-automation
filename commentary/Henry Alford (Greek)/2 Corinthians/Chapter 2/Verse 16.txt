<p><strong> 16</strong> b.] In order to understand the connexion, we must remember that the purpose of vindicating his apostolic commission is in the mind of Paul, and about to be introduced by a description of the office, its requirements, and its holders. This purpose already begins to press into its service the introductory and apologetic matter, and to take every opportunity of manifesting itself. In order then to exalt the dignity and shew the divine authorization of his office, he asks this question: <strong> And</strong> (see remarks at 2Co 2:2 ) for (to accomplish) <strong> these things</strong> (this so manifold working in the believers and unbelievers, this emission of the εὐωδία χριστοῦ every where), <strong> who is sufficient?</strong> He does not express the answer, but it is too evident to escape any reader, indeed it is supplied in terms by ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:5">2 Corinthians 3:5</span> , οὐχ ὅτι ἱκανοί ἐσμεν λογίσασθαί τι ἀφ ʼ ἑαυτῶν ὡς ἐξ ἑαυτῶν , ἀλλ ʼ ἡ ἱκανότης ἡμῶν ἐκ τοῦ θεοῦ .
	</p>
<p>Meyer remarks that <strong> πρὸς ταῦτα</strong> is put first, in the place of emphasis, to detain the attention on its weighty import, and then τίς purposely put off till the end of the question, to introduce the interrogation unexpectedly; as in Herod. 2:33, σοὶ δὲ κ . τούτοισι τοῖσι πρήγμασι τί ἔστι ; Plato, Symp. p. 204, ὁ ἐρῶν τῶν καλῶν τί ἐρᾷ ;</p>
