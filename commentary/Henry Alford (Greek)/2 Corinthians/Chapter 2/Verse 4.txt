<p><strong> 4</strong> .] <em> Explanation</em> ( γάρ ) <em> that he did not write in levity of purpose</em> , but <em> under great trouble of mind</em> , not to grieve them, but to testify his love. <strong> ἐκ</strong> , of the <em> inducement</em> <strong> διὰ</strong> , <em> of the condition</em> : he wrote, <strong> out of much tribulation</strong> (inward, of spirit, not outward) <strong> and anguish</strong> ( συνοχή , ‘ <em> angustiæ</em> ’) <strong> of heart, with</strong> (q. ‘through,’ the state being the vehicle of the action, see reff.) <strong> many tears.
	</strong></p>
<p><strong> τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἀγάπην</strong> , before the conjunction <strong> ἵνα</strong> , for special emphasis: see reff.
