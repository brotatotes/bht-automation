<p><strong> 16.</strong> ] <strong> Wherefore</strong> (on account of the hope implied in the faith spoken of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:14">2 Corinthians 4:14</span> , which he is about to expand) <strong> we do not shrink</strong> (as in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:1">2 Corinthians 4:1</span> ; but <em> now</em> , owing to <em> despair</em> ), <strong> but</strong> (on the contrary) <strong> though even</strong> (not ‘ <em> even if</em> ,’ putting a <em> case</em> ; εἰ καί with ind. asserts the <em> fact</em> , as in εἰ καὶ σπένδομαι , Php 2:17 ) <strong> our outward man is</strong> [ <strong> being</strong> ] <strong> wasted away</strong> (i.e. our <em> body</em> , see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:22">Romans 7:22</span> , <em> is</em> , by this continued νέκρωσις and ἐνέργεια τοῦ θανάτου , <em> being worn out</em> : he is not as yet speaking of dissolution by death, but only of gradual approximation to it), <strong> yet</strong> ( ἀλλά in the apodosis after a hypothetic clause, introduces a strong and marked contrast: so Hom. Il. α . 81, εἴπερ γάρ τε χόλον γε καὶ αὐτῆμαρ καταπέψῃ , ἀλλά τε καὶ μετόπισθεν ἔχει κότον , ὄφρα τελέσσῃ : see other examples in Hartung, Partikellehre, ii. 40) <strong> our inner (man) is [being] renewed</strong> (contrast, subordinately to διαφθείρεται , but mainly to ἐγκακοῦμεν ) <strong> day by day</strong> ( ἡμ . καὶ ἡμ ., so Hebr. יוֹם וָיוֹם , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="es+3:4">Esther 3:4</span> ; an expression not found (Meyer) even in the LXX): i.e. ‘our spiritual life, the life which testifies the life of Jesus, even in our mortal bodies ( 2Co 4:11 ), is continually fed with fresh accessions of grace:’ see next verse. So Chrys., πῶς ἀνακαινοῦται ; τῇ πίστει , τῇ ἐλπίδι , τῇ προθυμίᾳ , τὸ λοιπὸν δεῖ (al. τῷ λοιπὸν ) κατατολμᾷν τῶν δεινῶν . ὅσῳ γὰρ ἂν μυρία πάσχῃ τὸ σῶμα , τοσούτῳ χρηστοτέρας ἔχει τὰς ἐλπίδας ἡ ψυχή , καὶ λαμπροτέρα γίνεται , καθάπερ χρυσίον πυρούμενον ἐπιπλέον . p. 500.</p>
