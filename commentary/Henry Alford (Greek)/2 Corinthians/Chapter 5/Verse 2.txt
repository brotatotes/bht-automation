<p><strong> 2.</strong> ] <strong> For also</strong> (our knowledge, that we possess such a building of God, even in case of our body being dissolved, is testified by the <em> earnest desire</em> which we have, to put on that new body <em> without such dissolution taking place</em> . See the similar argument in Rom 8:18-19 ) <strong> in this</strong> (viz. σκήνει , as Beza, Meyer, Olsh., al. The rendering ἐν τούτῳ , ‘ <em> wherefore</em> ,’ some referring it to the foregoing, ‘propter hoc quod dictum est,’ Est., some to the following, is inconsistent with ὄντες ἐν τῷ σκήνει , which is parallel with it, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:4">2 Corinthians 5:4</span> . The stress is not necessarily on <strong> ἐν</strong> , ‘ <em> in</em> this,’ as contrasted with ‘out of this,’ as Meyer, who joins καί with ἐν τούτῳ ; but see above) <strong> we groan</strong> (see Rom 8:23 ), <strong> longing</strong> (i.e. because we desire, the reason of στενάζομεν . ἐπιποθ ., not <em> ardently</em> desire: the prep. does not intensify, but denotes the direction of the wish, as ἀνέμου μὴ προσεῶντος , Act 27:7 ) <strong> to put on over this</strong> (‘ <em> superinduere</em> :’ viz. <em> by being alive at the day of Christ</em> , and <em> not</em> dissolved as in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:1">2 Corinthians 5:1</span> : see on 2Co 5:4 below.
	</p>
<p>The similitude is slightly changed: the <em> house</em> is now to be <em> put on</em> , as an outer garment, <em> over the fleshly body</em> ) <strong> our dwelling-place</strong> (‘ οἰκία est quiddam magis absolutum, οἰκητήριον , domicilium, respicit <em> incolam</em> :’ Bengel. So Eur. Orest. 1113, ὥσθ ʼ Ἑλλὰς αὐτῇ σμικρὸν οἰκητήριον ) <strong> from heaven</strong> (i.e. = ἐκ θεοῦ <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:1">2 Corinthians 5:1</span> , but treated now as if <em> brought with</em> the Lord at His coming, and put upon us who are alive and remain then.
	</p>
