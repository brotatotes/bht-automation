<p><strong> βελίαρ</strong> ] Heb. בְּלִיַּעַל , <em> ‘contemptibleness,’ ‘wickedness:’</em> found 1Sa 2:12 al., and variously translated by the LXX. Theod. has retained the original form in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jdg+19:22">Judges 19:22</span> . It appears to have been subsequently personified, and used, as here, for a name of the Evil One (see Stanley). The termination - αρ is stated by Meyer to have arisen from the frequent permutation of λ and ρ in the dialect of the Grecian Jews.</p>
