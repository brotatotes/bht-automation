<p><strong> 7.</strong> <strong> ἀλλὰ καὶ</strong> <strong> …</strong> ] <strong> not only … but also with the comfort with which he was comforted concerning you:</strong> i.e. ‘we shared in the comfort which Titus felt in recording to us your desire,’ &amp;c. see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:13">2 Corinthians 7:13</span> . <em> He</em> rejoiced in announcing the news: <em> we</em> in hearing them. There is no inaccuracy of construction, as De W. supposes.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐπιπόθησιν</strong> , either longing <em> to see me</em> , or longing <em> to fulfil my wishes</em> . The former is the more simple.
	</p>
<p><strong> ὀδυρμόν</strong> , ἐπὶ τῇ ἐπιτιμήσει μου τῇ ἐν τῇ πρώτῃ ἐπιστολῇ , as Œcum.
	</p>
<p><strong> ζῆλον ὑπὲρ ἐμοῦ</strong> ] The art. is omitted after ζῆλον , as in τῶν ἀδελφῶν μου τῶν συγγενῶν κατὰ σάρκα , because the words ζῆλον ὑπὲρ ἐμοῦ cohere in the sense, and form as it were but one, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="col+1:4">Colossians 1:4</span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:13">2 Corinthians 4:13</span> , v. r.): and Winer, edn. 6, § 20. 2.
	</p>
<p><strong> μᾶλλον</strong> , viz.‘than before, at the mere coming of Titus.’ The emphasis is on μᾶλλον from its position.</p>
