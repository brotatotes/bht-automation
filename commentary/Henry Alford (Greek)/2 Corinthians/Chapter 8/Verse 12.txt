<p><strong> 12.</strong> ] Explanation of <strong> ἐκ τοῦ ἔχειν</strong> , that on it, προθυμία being presupposed, and not on absolute quantity, acceptability depends. <strong> For if a willing mind is present, according to what it may happen to possess, it is acceptable, not according to what it possesseth not</strong> . The construction of the sentence is simple enough: προθυμία being the subject throughout, quasi-personified: readiness in God’s service is accepted, if its exertion be commensurate with its means, and is not measured by an unreasonable requirement of what it has not.</p>
