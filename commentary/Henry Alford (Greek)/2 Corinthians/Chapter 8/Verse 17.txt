<p><strong> 17.</strong> ] <em> Proof of this</em> ; that Titus <em> received indeed</em> ( μέν ) Paul’s exhortation to go to them (said, to shew his subordination, or perhaps to authenticate his authorization by the Apostle), but in reality ( δέ ) was <em> too ready to go, to need any exhortation</em> ; and therefore <em> went forth</em> (the past tense of the epistolary style, as ‘ <em> dabam</em> ,’ &amp;c., indicating things which <em> will have passed</em> before the letter is received) <em> of his own accord to them</em> .</p>
