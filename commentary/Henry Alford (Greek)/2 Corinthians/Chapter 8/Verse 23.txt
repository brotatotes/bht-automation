<p><strong> 23.</strong> ] <em> General recommendation of the three</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> εἴτε ὑπ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> Τίτου</strong> ] <strong> Whether concerning Titus</strong> (we may supply λέγω or γράφω , or as in E. V., ‘any enquire:’ or we need not supply any thing), <strong> he is my partner and</strong> (especially) <strong> my fellow-worker towards you: whether our brethren</strong> (be in question: viz. the two mentioned but generalized by the absence of the article ‘ <em> whether</em> [ <em> any</em> ] <em> brethren of ours</em> ’), <strong> they are Apostles</strong> (in the more general sense of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:14">Acts 14:14</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:6">1 Thessalonians 2:6</span> ; Php 2:25 ) <strong> of the churches</strong> (i.e. ‘are of the churches, what we are of the Lord’ persons sent out with authority), <strong> the glory of Christ</strong> (i.e. men whose work tends to Christ’s glory).</p>
