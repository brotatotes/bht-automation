<p><strong> 8.</strong> ] Lest his last words should be misunderstood, he explains the spirit in which they were said: not as a command, but by way of inducement, by mention of the earnestness of others, and to try the genuineness of their love.
	</p>
<p><strong> κατ</strong> <strong> ʼ </strong> <strong> ἐπιτ</strong> .] not, <em> ‘in consequence of a command from God,’</em> as Dr. Burton, but, <strong> by way of command</strong> ( 1Co 7:6 ).
	</p>
<p><strong> διὰ τῆς</strong> is not = διὰ τήν , <em> ‘by occasion of,’</em> as E. V.: but treats the ἑτέρων σπουδή as the <em> instrument by which,</em> in the way of emulation, the effect was to be produced.
	</p>
<p>The participial construction is as in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:14">1 Corinthians 4:14</span> .</p>
