<p><strong> 3 5.</strong> ] <em> Proof of this.</em> There is no difficulty, and no ellipsis, in the construction. <strong> For according to their power, I testify, and beyond their power, voluntarily, with much exhortation beseeching of us the grace and fellowship of the ministry to the saints</strong> (i.e. to allow them a share in that grace and fellowship), <strong> and not as we expected</strong> (i.e. far beyond our expectation), <strong> but themselves they gave first</strong> (i.e. <em> above all:</em> as the inducing motive: not first in point of <em> time,</em> but in point of <em> importance</em> , see Rom 2:9-10 ) <strong> to the Lord, and to us by the will of God</strong> (the Giver of grace, who made them willing to do this: not = κατὰ τὸ θέλ . τ . θ ., which only <em> expresses</em> (whatever it may imply) <em> consonance with</em> the divine will: <strong> διὰ τοῦ θελ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> θ</strong> <strong> .</strong> makes the divine will the <em> agent</em> ).</p>
