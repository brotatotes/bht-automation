<p><strong> 4.</strong> ] <strong> lest perchance if Macedonians should come with me</strong> (to you: to bring me on my way, or to bear the Macedonian collection.
	</p>
<p>We may infer from this expression, that neither of the two brethren above mentioned, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:18">2 Corinthians 8:18</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:22">2 Corinthians 8:22</span> , was a Macedonian), <strong> and should find you unprepared</strong> (with your collection, see 2Co 9:2 ) <strong> we</strong> (who have boasted), <strong> not to say you</strong> (who were boasted of), <strong> should be put to shame, in the matter of this confidence</strong> (respecting you. <strong> ὑπόστασις</strong> , as elsewhere in N. T. and LXX, see reff., <em> subjective</em> : the attempt to give it here the meaning of ‘ <em> foundation</em> ,’ ‘ <em> matter boasted of</em> ,’ as Chrys., Theophyl., Erasm., Grot., al., Rück., Olsh., is unnecessary, and has probably been induced by the gloss τῆς καυχ inserted from ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:17">2 Corinthians 11:17</span> ; but see there also).</p>
