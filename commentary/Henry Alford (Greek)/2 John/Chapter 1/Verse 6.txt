<p><strong> 6</strong> .] <strong> And</strong> (“ <em> eine eigenthumliche Kreisbewegung der Gedanken, wie Johannes sie liebt</em> .” Düsterd.) <strong> this is the love</strong> ( <strong> ἡ ἀγάπη</strong> here is subject, not predicate: <strong> the love</strong> (intended by this command) <strong> is this</strong> , i. e. may be thus described), <strong> that</strong> (the explicative ἵνα of St. John) <strong> we walk according to His commandments. The commandment</strong> (the one commandment in which God’s other commandments are summed up) <strong> is this, even as ye heard from the beginning that ye should walk in it</strong> (the apodosis to αὕτη ἐστίν begins with καθώς : = “is this, even that which ye heard from the beginning, that ye should walk in it,” viz. in ἀγάπη .
