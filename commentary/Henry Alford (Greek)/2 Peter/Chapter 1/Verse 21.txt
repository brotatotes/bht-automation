<p><strong> 21</strong> .] <em> Reason of the above position</em> . <strong> For prophecy was never</strong> (at any time: <strong> ποτέ</strong> belongs to the negative, and though pointing, as do likewise the aorr., to a state of things passed away, and therefore not to be referred to N. T. prophecies, (see on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:1">2 Peter 2:1</span> ,) must not be rendered as E. V. (after Beza, as usual) “ <em> in old time</em> ”) <strong> sent</strong> (‘allata,’ vulg.: cf. above, 2Pe 1:17-18 ) <strong> after the will</strong> (dat. of the cause; or rule, by or according to which: as in τίς στρατεύεται ἰδίοις ὀψωνίοις ποτέ ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:7">1 Corinthians 9:7</span> ; cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:5">1 Corinthians 11:5</span> ; Heb 12:18 ) <strong> of man: but men spoke from God</strong> (spoke as with the voice of, as emissaries from, God: the <strong> ἀπο</strong> of ἀποστέλλω and ἀπόστολος . Besides critical considerations, probability seems against the reading ἅγιοι , in that, on account of the repetition, ἁγίου .… ἅγιοι , the stress, in the latter part of the sentence, would be laid on the fact of ἁγιότης , which does not form any logical contrast to ἰδίας ἐπιλύσεως , instead of on the fact of the φορά and the λαλιά coming from God, which <em> does</em> ), [ <strong> being</strong> ] <strong> borne</strong> (borne along, carried onward, as a ship by the wind, reff. Acts. “Impulsos fuisse dicit, non quod mente alienati fuerint (qualem in suis prophetis ἐνθουσιασμόν fingunt Gentiles) sed qui nihil a se ipsis ausi fuerint, tantum obedienter sequuti sint Spiritum ducem.” Calv. See besides reff., Jos. Antt. iv. 6. 5, οὐκ ὢν ἐν ἑαυτῷ , τῷ δὲ θείῳ πνεύματι κεκινημένος : Macrob. i. 23, speaking of the processions carrying the image of the Sun at Heliopolis, “ferunturque divino spiritu, non suo arbitrio, sed quo deus ropellit vehentes”) <strong> by the Holy Spirit</strong> .</p>
