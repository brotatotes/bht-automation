<p><strong> 2</strong> . <strong> and many shall follow after</strong> (see on ch. 2Pe 1:16 ) <strong> their licentiousnesses</strong> (the connexion of depraved moral conduct with erroneous doctrine was in the early ages of the church almost universal: see the Pastoral Epistles passim, and below <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:18-19">2 Peter 2:18-19</span> . In [6] Jude, the two are expressed co-ordinately: τὴν τοῦ θεοῦ ἡμῶν χάριτα μετατιθέντες εἰς ἀσέλγειαν , κ . τὸν μόνον δεσπότην κ . κύρ . ἡμ . <strong> Ἰ</strong> . χ . ἀρνούμενοι ) <strong> on whose account</strong> ( <em> by reason of whom</em> , i. e. from the ἀσέλγειαι of those who follow after the false teachers: for to these, and not to the false teachers themselves, is the <strong> οὕς</strong> most likely referable. It is those who, seeming to be in the way of truth, yet favour and follow false teachers, that cause most scandal to the way of truth itself) <strong> the way of truth</strong> (reff. and Ep. Barnab. 5, p. 734, “homo habens viam veritatis”) <strong> shall be evil spoken of</strong> (“ab iis qui foris sunt, discrimen ignorantibus verorum et falsorum Christianorum.” Bengel):
	</p>
