<p><strong> 21</strong> .] <em> Reason of these last words</em> . <strong> For it were</strong> (that use of the imperfect without ἄν , answering to the Latin “faciebam, ni:” see on Rom 9:3 ) <strong> better for them not to have known the way of righteousness</strong> (viz. the Christian life: cf. ἡ ὁδὸς τῆς ἀληθείας , 2Pe 2:2 ) <strong> than, having known it</strong> (dat. instead of accus. by a very common attraction), <strong> to turn back</strong> (not perf., but aor.: now implying merely the final character of the act) <strong> from</strong> (out of, as out of a way) <strong> the holy commandment</strong> (the moral law of the gospel: here so designated, because it is of moral corruption that the Apostle is treating) <strong> delivered to them</strong> (cf. ref. Jude, τῇ ἅπαξ παραδοθείσῃ τοῖς ἁγίοις πίστει : where the arrangement of words is the same as here: παραδοθείσῃ being thrown forward and having the emphasis).</p>
