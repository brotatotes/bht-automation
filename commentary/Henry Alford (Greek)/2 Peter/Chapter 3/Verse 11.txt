<p><strong> 11</strong> .] <strong> These things being thus to be dissolved</strong> ( <strong> τούτων</strong> , this heaven and earth which surround us. According to the reading in the text, there is no particle of inference: but the inference is all the more vivid. <strong> οὕτως</strong> : viz. in the manner just described. <strong> λυομένων</strong> , the <em> present</em> implying destiny, as ὁ ἐρχόμενος , He that should come: cf. Winer, § 40. 2. <em> a.</em> It <em> might</em> be, with οὖν , a present <em> proper</em> , “are in course of dissolution;” but οὕτως forbids this: for they <em> are not</em> in course of dissolution by fire ῥοιζηδόν &amp;c.), <strong> what manner of men</strong> (if we take <strong> ποταπούς</strong> interrogatively, we must not, as some (Pott, Meyer in his translation), put our interrogation at ὑμᾶς , or as others (Griesb., al.) at εὐσεβείας : far better carry on the question to the end of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:12">2 Peter 3:12</span> , as more like the fervent style of our Epistle. But (reff.) ποταπός seems in the N. T. never directly to ask a question, but always to belong to an exclamation. Certainly reff. Luke are close approaches to the interrogatory sense, so that I would not, as Huther, altogether exclude it, but only protest against dividing the sentence. Still I prefer the non-interrogatory form, as in the other reff. On the word, see note, 1Jn 3:1 ) <strong> ought ye to be</strong> (when the event comes: <strong> ὑπάρχειν</strong> seems to imply some fact supervening upon the previously existing state: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:20-21">Acts 16:20-21</span> ; Act 16:37 and notes) <strong> in holy behaviours and pieties</strong> (the plurals mark the holy behaviour and piety in all its different forms and examples. The words <em> may</em> be referred to ὑπάρχειν : but thus the strong ποταπούς would only be weakened, and it stands far better alone. So that I would join ἐν ἁγίαις κ . τ . λ . with what follows)</p>
