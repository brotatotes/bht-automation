<p><strong> 13</strong> .] <em> The positive result of that day, as regards the church</em> . <strong> But</strong> (contrast to the destructive effects of the day lately dwelt on: not “ <em> nevertheless</em> ” as E. V., which looks as if the two effects were in antagonism, and the earth were to be <em> annihilated</em> , of which idea there is no trace. The flood did not annihilate the earth, but changed it; and as the new earth was the consequence of the flood, so the final new heavens and earth shall be of the fire) <strong> according to His</strong> (God’s) <strong> promise</strong> (viz., that written in ref. Isa.) <strong> we</strong> (no stress, as is almost unavoidable in the E. V. “ <em> Nevertheless we, according to his promise</em> :” there is no ἡμεῖς , nor is the distinction drawn between us and any other class of persons) <strong> expect new heavens and a new earth, in which</strong> (heavens and earth, plur.) <strong> righteousness dwelleth</strong> (ref. Isa., cf. also οὐκ ἀδικήσουσιν , … ἐπὶ τῷ ὄρει τῷ ἁγίῳ μου , λέγει κύριος , of Isa 65:25 ).</p>
