<p><strong> 3</strong> . <strong> καθὼς ἄξιόν ἐστιν</strong> ] <strong> as it is right</strong> refers to the whole preceding sentence.
	</p>
<p><strong> ὅτι</strong> , not ‘ <em> that</em> ,’ εὐχαριστεῖν ὅτι which would make καθὼς ἄξ . ἐστ . flat and superfluous, but <strong> because</strong> , dependent on the clause preceding, καθὼς ἄξ . ἐστιν , <strong> it is right, because</strong> &amp;c.
	</p>
<p><strong> “</strong> <strong> ὀφείλομεν</strong> expresses the duty of thanksgiving from its <em> subjective</em> side as an inward conviction, <strong> καθὼς ἄξιόν ἐστιν</strong> , on the other hand, from the <em> objective</em> side, as something answering to the state of circumstances.” Lün.
	</p>
<p><strong> ὑπεραυξάνει</strong> ] ‘Frequentavit hujus generis voce Paulus ( ὑπερλίαν <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:5">2 Corinthians 11:5</span> , ὑπερπλεονάζω <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:14">1 Timothy 1:14</span> , ὑπερπερισσεύομαι <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:4">2 Corinthians 7:4</span> (cf. also Rom 5:20 ), ὑπερνικάε <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:37">Romans 8:37</span> , ὑπερυψόω Php 2:9 ), non quod iis delectaretur, sed quia vir vehemens natura duce sua cogitata gravibus verbis enuntiavit.’ Fritzsche ad <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:20">Romans 5:20</span> .
	</p>
