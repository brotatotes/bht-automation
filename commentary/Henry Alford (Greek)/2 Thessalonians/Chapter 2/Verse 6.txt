<p><strong> 6</strong> .] <strong> And now</strong> (not <em> temporal</em> , but as νυνὶ δέ in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:13">1 Corinthians 13:13</span> , ‘rebus sic stantibus’ ‘now’ in our argument. We most not for a moment think of the ungrammatical rendering of Whitby, Masker., Heydenr., Schrader, OlSh., B.-Crus., and Wieseler, ‘ <em> that which at present hinders</em> ,’ which must be τὸ νῦν κατέχον : and for which <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:7">2 Thessalonians 2:7</span> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:3">Romans 12:3</span> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:17">1 Corinthians 7:17</span> , are no precedent whatever, not presenting any case of inversion of an adverb from its emphatic place between an article and a participle.
	</p>
<p><strong> νῦν</strong> is a mere adverb of passage, and the stress is on τὸ κατέχον ) <strong> ye know that which hinders</strong> (viz. ‘ <em> him</em> ’ the man of sin: not, <em> the Apostle from speaking freely</em> , as Heinsius, nor the <em> coming of Christ</em> ) <strong> in order that</strong> (the aim of κατέχον (in God’s purposes) q. d. ‘that which keeps him back, that he may not be revealed before his,’ &amp;c.) <strong> he may be revealed</strong> (see on 2Th 2:3 ) <strong> in his own time</strong> (the time appointed him by God: reff.).</p>
