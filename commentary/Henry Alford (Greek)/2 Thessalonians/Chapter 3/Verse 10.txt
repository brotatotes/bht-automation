<p><strong> 10</strong> .] <strong> καὶ γάρ</strong> , and we carried this further: we not only set you an example, but inculcated the duty of diligence by special precept. The <strong> γάρ</strong> is co-ordinate with that in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:7">2 Thessalonians 3:7</span> . The καί does not bring out ὅτε ἦμεν πρ . ὑμᾶς as a new feature, as Thdrt., for of this period the last three verses have treated but it brings out <strong> τοῦτο</strong> , on which the stress lies, as an additional element in the reminiscence. This seems to me clearly to be the force here, and not the merely conjunctive, as Ellic. maintains, <strong> τοῦτο</strong> , viz. what follows.
