<p><strong> 13</strong> .] <strong> δέ</strong> ye who are free from this fault. On ἐγκ . and ἐκκ . see notes on 2Co 4:1 and <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:9">Galatians 6:9</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> καλοποιοῦντες</strong> , from the context, cannot mean ‘ <em> doing good</em> ’ ( <em> to others</em> ), but <strong> doing well</strong> , living diligently and uprightly: see also <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:9">Galatians 4:9</span> , where the same general sentiment occurs. Chrys.’s meaning is surely far-fetched: στέλλεσθε μέν , φησιν , ἀπ ʼ αὐτῶν κ . ἐπιτιμᾶτε αὐτοῖς , μὴ μὴν περιΐδητε λιμῷ διαφθαρέντας .</p>
