<p><strong> 16</strong> .] <em> Concluding wish</em> . On <strong> αὐτὸς δέ</strong> , see on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:16">2 Thessalonians 2:16</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ὁ κύριος τῆς εἰρήνης</strong> ] As the Apostle constantly uses ὁ θεὸς τῆς εἰρ . for the God of Peace (see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:33">Romans 15:33</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:20">Romans 16:20</span> :<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+13:11">2 Corinthians 13:11</span> , al.), we here must understand our Lord Jesus Christ.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἡ εἰρήνη</strong> must not be understood only of <em> peace with one another</em> : for there has been no special mention of mutual disagreement in this Epistle: but of <em> peace in general</em> , outward and inward, here and hereafter, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:17">Romans 14:17</span> . See Fritz, on Romans, vol. i, p. 22.
	</p>
<p>The stress is on ὑμῖν <strong> May the Lord of Peace give</strong> <em> you</em> ( <strong> that</strong> ) <strong> Peace always in every way</strong> (whether it be outward or inward, for time or for eternity).
	</p>
<p><strong> μετὰ πάντων ὑ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] therefore with the ἀτάκτως περιπατοῦντες also (Lün.): not as Jowett, pleonastic. The man who was to be admonished as an ἀδελφός , would hardly be excluded from the Apostle’s parting blessing.</p>
