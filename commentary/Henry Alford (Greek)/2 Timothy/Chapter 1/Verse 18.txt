<p><strong> 18</strong> .] <strong> May the Lord grant to him to find mercy from the Lord</strong> (the account to be given of the double κύριος , κυρίου , here is simply this that δῴη ὁ κύριος had become so completely a formula, that the recurrence was not noticed. This, which is Huther’s view, is far better than to suppose the second κυρ . merely = ἑαυτοῦ , or to enter into theological distinctions between κύριος as the Father, and παρὰ κυρίου as from the Son, the Judge) <strong> in that day</strong> (see on 2Ti 1:12 ): <strong> and how many services he did</strong> (to me: or, to the saints: the general expression will admit of either) <strong> in Ephesus</strong> (being probably an Ephesian, cf. ch. 2Ti 4:19 ), <strong> thou knowest well</strong> (the comparative is not for the positive, here or any where: but the signification is, ‘better, than that I need remind thee’).</p>
