<p><strong> ἐπήρθη</strong> <strong> ] “was taken up</strong> , we may understand of the commencing ascent … <strong> ὑπέλαβεν</strong> by a pregn. constr involves the idea of <em> away</em> as well as <em> up</em> , and hence takes after it <strong> ἀπό</strong> . This verb describes the close of the scene, as far as it was visible to the spectators.” Hackett.
	</p>
<p><strong> νεφέλη</strong> ] There was a manifest propriety in the last withdrawal of the Lord, while ascending, not consisting in a <em> disappearance</em> of His Body, as on former occasions since the Resurrection; for thus might His abiding Humanity have been called in question. As it was, He went up, past the visible boundary of Heaven, the cloud, <em> in human form</em> , and so we think of and pray to Him.</p>
