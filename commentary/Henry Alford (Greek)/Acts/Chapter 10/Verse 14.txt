<p><strong> 14.</strong> ] Peter rightly understands the command as giving him free choice of <em> all</em> the creatures shewn to him. We cannot infer hence that the sheet contained <em> unclean animals only</em> . It was a mixture of clean and unclean, the aggregate, therefore, being <em> unclean</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> κύριε</strong> ] So Cornelius to the angel, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:4">Acts 10:4</span> . It is here addressed to the unknown heavenly speaker.
	</p>
<p>On the clean and unclean beasts, &amp;c., see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="le+11:0">Leviticus 11:0</span> .</p>
