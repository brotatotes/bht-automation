<p><strong> 41</strong> .] Bengel would understand συνεφ . κ . συνεπ . of <em> previous intercourse during His ministry</em> , and parenthesize οὐ παντὶ … αὐτῷ , finding a difficulty in their having eaten and drunk with Him <em> after His Resurrection</em> . But this would make the significant οἵτινες (“people who”).… αὐτῷ very flat and unmeaning, especially after <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:39">Acts 10:39</span> ; whereas the fact of their having eaten and drunk with Him <em> after</em> His Resurrection gives most important testimony to the reality and identity of His risen Body. And there is no real difficulty in it: <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:41">Luke 24:41</span> ; Luk 24:43 and Joh 21:12 give us instances; and, even if συνεπίομεν is to be pressed, it is no contradiction to <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:18">Luke 22:18</span> , which only refers to one particular kind of drinking.
