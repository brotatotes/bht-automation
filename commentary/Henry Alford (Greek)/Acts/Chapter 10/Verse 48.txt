<p><strong> 48. </strong> <strong> προσέταξεν</strong> ] As the Lord Himself when on earth <em> did not baptize</em> ( Joh 4:2 ), so did not ordinarily the Apostles (see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:13-17">1 Corinthians 1:13-17</span> , and note). Perhaps the same reason may have operated in both cases, lest those baptized by our Lord, or by the chief Apostles, should arrogate to themselves pre-eminence on that account. Also, which is implied in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:17">1 Corinthians 1:17</span> , as compared with <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:2">Acts 6:2</span> , the ministry of the Word was esteemed by them their higher and paramount duty and office, whereas the subordinate ministration of the ordinances was committed to those who διηκόνουν τραπέζαις .
	</p>
