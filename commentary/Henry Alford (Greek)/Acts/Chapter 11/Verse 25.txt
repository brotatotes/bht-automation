<p><strong> 25.</strong> ] This therefore took place after ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:30">Acts 9:30</span> ; <em> how long after</em> , we have no hint in the narrative, and the question will be determined by various persons according to the requirements of their chronological system. Wieseler and Schrader make it not more than from half a year to a year: Dr. Burton, who places the conversion of Saul in A.D. 31, nine years. Speaking <em> à priori</em> , it seems very improbable that any considerable portion of time should have been spent by him before the great work of his ministry began. Even supposing him during this retirement to have preached in Syria and Cilicia, judging by the analogy of his subsequent journeys, <em> a few months</em> at the most would have sufficed for this. For my own view, see Prolegg. to Acts, § vi.</p>
