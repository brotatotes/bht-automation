<p><strong> 12. </strong> <strong> συνιδών</strong> ] Not, <em> considerans</em> (as Vulg., Beza, Grot.): nor, ‘ <em> being aware of the place of meeting</em> ,’ with reference to what follows (Meyer), against which the aorist is decisive, importing some single act and not a state: but, as reff., referring to what <em> went before</em> ( οἶδα ἀληθῶς κ . τ . λ .), <strong> having become aware of it.
	</strong></p>
<p><strong> Ἰωάννου</strong> <strong> ]</strong> It is uncertain whether this John Mark was the same as the Evangelist Mark: but they have been generally believed to be the same. For a full account of him, see Prolegomena to Mark (Vol. I. § i.). His mother Mary was not sister, but aunt of Barnabas: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="col+4:10">Colossians 4:10</span> , note.</p>
