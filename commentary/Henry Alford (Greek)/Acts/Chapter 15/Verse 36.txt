<p><strong> 36. </strong> <strong> μετὰ δέ τινας ἡμ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] <em> How long</em> , we are not informed: but perhaps (?) during this time took place that visit of Peter to Antioch mentioned <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:11">Galatians 2:11</span> ff. when he sacrificed his Christian consistency and better persuasions to please some Judaizers, and even Barnabas was led away with the dissimulation. On this occasion Paul boldly rebuked him. See, on the whole occurrence, notes to Gal. l. c.
	</p>
<p><strong> δή</strong> , see above, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:2">Acts 13:2</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐν αἷς</strong> , because πᾶσαν πόλιν involves a plurality: so Xen. Mem. i. 2. 62, ἐάν τις φανερὸς γένηται … τούτοις θάνατός ἐστιν ἡ ζημία : cf. Herm. ad Viger. p. 40.</p>
