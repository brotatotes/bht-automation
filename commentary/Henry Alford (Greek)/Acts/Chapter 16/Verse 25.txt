<p><strong> 25. </strong> <strong> προσευχ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ὕμν</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] Not as E. V., ‘ <em> prayed and sang praises</em> ,’ but, <strong> praying, sang praises</strong> , or <strong> in their prayers, were singing praises</strong> . The distinction of modern times between prayer and praise, arising from our attention being directed to the <em> shape</em> rather than to the essence of devotion, was unknown in these days: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="col+4:2">Colossians 4:2</span> .
	</p>
<p>‘Nihil crus sentit in nervo, quum animus in cœlo est.’ Tertullian ad Martyres, c. 2, vol. i. p. 623.
	</p>
<p>The imperfects shew that they <em> were singing</em> , and the prisoners (in the outer prison) <em> listening</em> , when the earthquake happened.</p>
