<p>As the assertion of Creation contradicted the Epicurean <em> error</em> , so this laid hold of that portion of truth, which, however disguised, that school had apprehended: ‘Omnis enim per se divûm natura necesse est | Immortali ævo summa cum pace fruatur. |.… | Ipsa suis pollens opibus, <em> nihil indiga nostri</em> ,’ Lucret. i. 57. There is a verse in 2Ma 14:35 , remarkable, as compared with the thoughts and words of Paul here: σύ , κύριε , τῶν ὅλων ἀπροσδεὴς ὑπάρχων , εὐδοκήσας ναὸν τῆς σῆς κατασκηνώσεως ἐν ἡμῖν γενέσθαι .
