<p><strong> 34. </strong> <strong> Διονύσιος ὁ Ἀρ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] Nothing more is known of him. Euseb. H. E. iii. 4; iv. 23, relates that he was bishop of Athens, and Niceph. iii. 11, that he died a martyr. The writings which go by his name are undoubtedly spurious.
	</p>
<p><strong> γυνή</strong> ] Not, as Chrys., de Sacerd. iv. 7, vol. i. p. 412, seems to infer from the form of the expression, ἠκολούθησεν αὐτῷ μετὰ τῆς γυναικός , <em> the wife</em> of Dionysius: this would have been ἡ γυνὴ αὐτοῦ .</p>
