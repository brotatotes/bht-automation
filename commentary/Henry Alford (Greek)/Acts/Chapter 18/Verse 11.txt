<p><strong> 11</strong> .] The year and a half may extend either to his <em> departure</em> , or to the incident in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:12">Acts 18:12</span> ff. Meyer would confine it to the latter, taking ἐκάθισεν in the sense of ‘ <em> remained in quiet</em> :’ but (see reff.) it will hardly bear such emphasis: and seeing that the incident in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:12">Acts 18:12</span> ff. was a notable fulfilment of the promise, for though they set on him, <em> they could not hurt him</em> , I should be disposed to take the other view, and regard Act 18:12 to ἱκανάς , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:18">Acts 18:18</span> , to have happened during this time.</p>
