<p><strong> 1. </strong> <strong> τὰ ἀνωτερικὰ μέρη</strong> ] By this name were known the eastern parts of Asia Minor, beyond the river Halys, or in comparison with Ephesus, in the direction of that river. So Herodotus, speaking as a Halicarnassian, calls even the neighbourhood of Sardis τὰ ἄνω τῆς Ἀσίας , i. 177; including in the term, however, many of the inland districts, Assyria, Babylonia, &amp;c. So that the reading ἀνατολικά , which is found in three cursives and Theophyl-sif., is a good gloss.
	</p>
<p><strong> τινας μαθητάς</strong> ] These seem to have been in the same situation as Apollos, see on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:25">Acts 18:25</span> . They cannot have been mere disciples of John, on account of πιστεύσαντες , which can bear no meaning but that of believing <em> on the Lord Jesus</em> : but they had received only John’s baptism, and had had no proof of the descent of the Holy Spirit, nor knowledge of His gifts.</p>
