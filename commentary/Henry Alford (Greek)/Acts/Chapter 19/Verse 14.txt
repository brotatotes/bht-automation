<p><strong> 14. </strong> <strong> ἀρχιερέως</strong> ] The word must be used in a wide sense. He may have been chief of the priests resident at Ephesus: or perhaps chief of one of the twenty-four courses.
	</p>
<p><strong> τινες</strong> does not belong to <strong> ἑπτά</strong> , see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:23">Acts 23:23</span> , but stands alone, recalling the τινες of the preceding verse.
	</p>
<p>Without the οἱ it would be, ‘ <em> certain men,</em> &amp;c. <em> were attempting this</em> ,’ ἦσαν and ποιοῦντες being taken together. With it, <strong> They were</strong> (it was) <strong> certain men, seven sons,</strong> &amp;c. <strong> who attempted this</strong> .</p>
