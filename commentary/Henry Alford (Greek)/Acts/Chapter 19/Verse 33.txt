<p><strong> 33.</strong> ] <strong> ἐκ τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ὄχλ</strong> <strong> .</strong> <strong> some of the multitude.
	</strong></p>
<p><strong> προεβ</strong> . <strong> urged forward</strong> , through the crowd; the Jews pushing him on from behind, ‘propellentibus.’
	</p>
<p>It is uncertain whether this Alexander is mentioned elsewhere (but see on 2Ti 4:14 ). He appears to have been a Christian convert from Judaism, whom the Jews were willing to expose as a victim to the fury of the mob: or perhaps one of themselves, put forward to clear them of blame on the occasion.</p>
