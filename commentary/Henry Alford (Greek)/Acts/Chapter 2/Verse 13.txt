<p><strong> 13.</strong> <strong> ἕτεροι</strong> ] Probably native Jews, who did not understand the foreign languages. Meyer supposes, persons previously hostile to Jesus and his disciples, and thus judging as in Luk 7:34 they judged of Himself.
	</p>
<p><strong> γλεύκους</strong> ] יַיִן , see ref. Job.
	</p>
<p><em> Sweet wine</em> , not necessarily <em> new</em> wine (nor is the “spiritual sense of the passage” any reason why a meaning should be given to the word which it need not bear. That sense in fact remains without the meaning in question): perhaps made of a remarkably sweet small grape, which is understood by the Jewish expositors to be meant by שׂרֵק or שׂרֵקָה , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ge+49:11">Genesis 49:11</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="isa+5:2">Isaiah 5:2</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jer+2:21">Jeremiah 2:21</span> , and still found in Syria and Arabia (Winer, Realw.). Suidas interprets it, τὸ ἀποστάλαγμα τῆς σταφυλῆς πρὶν πατηθῇ .</p>
