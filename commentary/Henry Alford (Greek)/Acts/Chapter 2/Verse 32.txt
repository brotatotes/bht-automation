<p><strong> 32</strong> .] From Act 2:25 has been employed in substantiating the Resurrection as the <em> act of God announced by prophecy</em> in old time: now the <em> historical fact</em> of its accomplishment is affirmed, and the vouchers for it produced.
	</p>
<p><strong> οὗ</strong> ] either masc., see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:8">Acts 1:8</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:31">Acts 13:31</span> , or neut. The former seems most probable as including the latter. ‘We are His witnesses,’ would imply, ‘We testify to this His work,’ which work implied the Resurrection.
	</p>
<p><strong> πάντες</strong> , first and most properly <em> the Twelve</em> : but, secondarily, the whole body of believers, all of whom, at this time, had probably seen the Lord since His Resurrection; see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:6">1 Corinthians 15:6</span> .</p>
