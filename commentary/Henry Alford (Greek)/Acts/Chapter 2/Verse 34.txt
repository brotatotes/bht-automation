<p><strong> 34</strong> .] This <em> exaltation</em> of Christ is also proved from prophecy and from the same passage with which Jesus Himself had silenced His enemies. See notes, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:41">Matthew 22:41</span> ff.
	</p>
<p><strong> δέ</strong> is not ‘ <em> for</em> ,’ which would destroy the whole force of the sentence: the Apostle says, <strong> For David himself is not ascended into the heavens</strong> , <em> as he would be if the former prophecy applied to him</em> : BUT <strong> he himself says</strong> , removing all doubt on the subject, &amp;c. The rendering δέ , <em> for</em> , makes it appear as if the ἀνέβη εἰς τ . οὐρ . were a mistaken inference from <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ps+110:1">Psalms 110:1</span> , whereas that passage is adduced to preclude its being made from the other.</p>
