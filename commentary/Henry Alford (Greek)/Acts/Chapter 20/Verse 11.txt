<p><strong> 11.</strong> ] The intended breaking of bread had been put off by the accident.
	</p>
<p><strong> τὸν ἄρτ</strong> ., as ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:42">Acts 2:42</span> . Were it not for that usage, the article here might import, ‘the bread which it was intended to break,’ alluding to ἄρτ . above.
	</p>
<p><strong> γευσάμενος</strong> ] <strong> having made a meal</strong> , see reff. The agape was a veritable meal. Not ‘ <em> having tasted it</em> ,’ viz. the bread which he had broken; though that is implied, usage decides for the other meaning.
	</p>
<p><strong> οὕτως</strong> ] ‘ <em> After so doing</em> :’see reff.</p>
