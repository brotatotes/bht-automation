<p><strong> 2.</strong> ] Notices of this journey may be found <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:12-13">2 Corinthians 2:12-13</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:5-6">2 Corinthians 7:5-6</span> . He delayed on the way some time at Troas, waiting for Titus, broke off his preaching there, though prosperous, in distress of mind at his non-arrival, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:12-13">2 Corinthians 2:12-13</span> , and sailed for Macedonia, where Titus met him, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:6">2 Corinthians 7:6</span> . That Epistle was written during it, from Macedonia (see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:2">2 Corinthians 9:2</span> , καυχῶμαι , ‘I am boasting’). He seems to have gone to the confines at least of Illyria, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:19">Romans 15:19</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> αὐτούς</strong> ] The Macedonian brethren: so ch. Act 16:10 al., see reff., and Winer, edn. 6, § 22.3.
	</p>
<p><strong> Ἑλλάδα</strong> ] Achaia, see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:21">Acts 19:21</span> .</p>
