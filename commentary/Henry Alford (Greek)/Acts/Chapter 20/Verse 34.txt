<p>χρεία with a gen. of the <em> person in want</em> , is an expression of Paul only; see among reff.
	</p>
<p>ὑπηρετεῖν is used only twice more; once <em> by</em> Paul, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:36">Acts 13:36</span> , once <em> of</em> Paul, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:23">Acts 24:23</span> .
	</p>
<p>The construction is varied in this sentence.
	</p>
<p><strong> ταῖς χρ</strong> . μου , καὶ (not τῶν ὄντων , but) τοῖς οὖσιν μετ ʼ ἐμοῦ . This is not without meaning his friends were among his χρεῖαι he supplied by his labour, not his and their wants, but <em> his wants</em> and <em> them</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> αἱ χ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> αὗται</strong> ] also [strikingly] in Paul’s manner: compare τῶνδεσμῶν τούτων , ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:29">Acts 26:29</span> , and ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:20">Acts 28:20</span> .</p>
