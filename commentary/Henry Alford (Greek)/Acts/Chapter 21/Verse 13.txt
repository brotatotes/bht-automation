<p><strong> 13.</strong> ] The τότε , which has been changed in the rec. for the ordinary copula, gives solemnity to the answer about to be related: q. d. It <strong> was then that Paul said</strong> .
	</p>
<p><strong> συνθρύπτοντες</strong> ] The present part. does not imply the <em> endeavour</em> merely, here or any where else, but as Meyer quotes from Schaefer, Eurip. Phœn. 79, ‘Vere incipit actus, sed ob impedimenta caret eventu.’
	</p>
<p><strong> γάρ</strong> ] Either, ‘ <em> your proceeding is in vain, for</em> …’ or ‘ <em> cease to do so, for</em> .…’
	</p>
<p><strong> εἰς Ἱερ</strong> ] <strong> on my arrival at:</strong> the motion to, which was the subject in question, is combined with that which might result on it: see reff. and ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:39">Acts 2:39</span> .</p>
