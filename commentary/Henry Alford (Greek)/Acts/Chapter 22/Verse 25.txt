<p><strong> 25.</strong> ] <strong> And while they were binding him down with the thongs</strong> . Dr. Bloomfield quotes from Dio Cassius, xi. 49, Ἀντίγονον ἐμαστίγωσε σταυρῷ προδήσαντες , and explains rightly, I think, the προ in both verbs to allude to the <em> position</em> of the prisoner, which was, bent forward, and tied with a sort of gear made of leather to an inclined post. De W. and others render τοῖς ἱμᾶσιν , ‘ <em> for the scourge</em> ’ (dat. commodi); but why should μάστιξιν be varied? and can it be shewn (as Dr. B. asks) that the word in the plural will bear this meaning?
	</p>
<p><strong> ἑκατόνταρχον</strong> ] The ‘centurio supplicio præpositus’ of Tacitus and Seneca, standing by to superintend the punishment.
