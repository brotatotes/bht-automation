<p><strong> 12.</strong> ] <strong> οἱ Ἰουδ</strong> . as opposed to Paul, the subject of the former verse. The copyists thought it unlikely that <em> all</em> the Jews were engaged in it, and so altered it to τινες τῶν Ἰουδ ., and then transposed it for euphony.
	</p>
<p>Wetstein and Lightf. adduce instances of similar conspiracies, not to eat or drink till some object be gained. See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1sa+14:24">1 Samuel 14:24</span> ff.; and Jos. Antt. xv. 8. 3, 4.</p>
