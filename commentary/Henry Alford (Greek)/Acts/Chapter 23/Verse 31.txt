<p><strong> 31</strong> .] ANTIPATRIS, forty-two Roman miles from Jerusalem, and twenty-six from Cæsarea, was built by Herod the Great, and called in honour of his father. It was before called Kapharsaba (Jos. Antt. xiii. 15. 1; xvi. 5. 2). In Jerome’s time (Epitaph. Paulæ, 8, vol. i. p. 696) it was a ‘semirutum oppidum’ (Winer, Realw.).
	</p>
<p>They might have well made so much way during the night and the next day, for the text will admit of that interpretation, τῇ ἐπαύρ . being not necessarily the morrow <em> after they left Jerusalem</em> , but <em> after they arrived at Antipatris</em> .</p>
