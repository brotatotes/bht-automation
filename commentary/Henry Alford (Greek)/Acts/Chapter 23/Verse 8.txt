<p><strong> 8</strong> .] See note, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:7">Matthew 3:7</span> , for both Pharisees and Sadducees: and for an account of the doctrine of the latter, Jos. Antt. xviii. 1. 4; B. J. ii. 8. 14. In the latter place he says, ψυχῆς τὴν διαμονήν , καὶ τὰς καθ ʼ ᾅδου τιμωρίας καὶ τιμὰς ἀναιροῦσι .
	</p>
<p>The former μήτε has been altered to μηδέ to suit τὰ ἀμφότερα , because with ἀναστ . <strong> μήτε</strong> ἄγγ . μήτε πν . <em> three</em> things are mentioned (and thus we have <em> hæc omnia</em> as a var.): whereas, if μηδέ is read, the two last are coupled, and form <em> only one</em> . But τὰ ἀμφ . is used of <em> both things</em> , the one being the <em> resurrection</em> , the other the <em> doctrine of spiritual existences</em> : the two specified classes of the latter being combined generically. τὰ ἀμφ ., <strong> them both</strong> , <strong> both of them</strong> , <strong> the two</strong> .</p>
