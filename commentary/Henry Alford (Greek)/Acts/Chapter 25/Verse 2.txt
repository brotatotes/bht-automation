<p><strong> 2.</strong> <strong> οἱ ἀρχ</strong> .] It has been imagined, that ὁ ἀρχ . of the rec. has been a correction to suit the former part of the narrative. But it may be that οἱ ἀρχιερεῖς has been substituted for it, to suit the assertion of Festus, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:15">Acts 25:15</span> . So Meyer and De Wette. The High Priest now was Ishmael the son of Phabi, Jos. Antt. xx. 8. 11 [see chronological table in Prolegg.].
	</p>
<p><strong> πρῶτοι</strong> is more general than πρεσβύτεροι , though most of the <em> first men</em> must have been members of the Sanhedrim. Festus, relating this application, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:15">Acts 25:15</span> , calls them πρεσβύτεροι .</p>
