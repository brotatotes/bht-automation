<p><strong> 22.</strong> ] <strong> ἐβουλόμην</strong> does not (as Calv.) imply any <em> former</em> wish of Agrippa to hear Paul. It is, as Meyer explains it, a modest way of expressing a wish, formed in this case while the procurator was speaking, but spoken of by Agrippa as if now past by, and therefore not pressed. We say somewhat similarly, ‘ <em> I was wishing</em> .’ See ref. Rom. and note there. Cf. Aristoph. Av. 1027: ἐκκλησιάσαι δ ʼ οὖν ἐδεόμην οἴκοι μένων : and see other examples in Bernhardy, Syntax, p. 373 ff. Agrippa, as a Jew, is anxious to hear Paul’s defence, as a matter of national interest. The procurator’s ready consent is explained, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:26">Acts 25:26</span> .</p>
