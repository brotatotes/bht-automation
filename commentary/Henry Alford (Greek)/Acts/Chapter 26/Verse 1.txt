<p><span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:1">Acts 26:1</span> <strong> .</strong> ] The <em> stretching out of the hand</em> by a speaker was not, as Hammond supposes, the same as the κατασείειν τῇ χειρί of ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:17">Acts 12:17</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:16">Acts 13:16</span> . The latter was to ensure silence; but this, a formal attitude usual with orators. Apuleius, Met. ii. p. 54 (Meyer), describes it very precisely: ‘Porrigit dextram et ad instar oratorum conformat articulum, duobusque infimis conclusis digitis ceteros eminentes porrigit.’ The hand was <em> chained</em> τούτων τ . δεσμ ., <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:29">Acts 26:29</span> .</p>
