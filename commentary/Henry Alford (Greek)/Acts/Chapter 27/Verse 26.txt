<p><strong> 26. </strong> <strong> δεῖ</strong> ] Spoken prophetically, as also <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:31">Acts 27:31</span> ; not perhaps from actual revelation imparted in the vision, but by a power imparted to Paul himself of penetrating the future at this crisis, and announcing the Divine counsel.
	</p>
<p>Mr. Humphry compares and contrasts the speech of Cæsar to the pilot under similar circumstances: τόλμα κ . δέδιθι μηθέν , ἀλλὰ ἐπιδίδου τῇ τύχῃ τὰ ἱστία καὶ δέχου τὸ πνεῦμα , τῷ πνέοντι πιστεύων , ὅτι Καίσαρα φέρεις καὶ τὴν Καίσαρος τύχην , Plut. de Fortun. Rom. p. 518.</p>
