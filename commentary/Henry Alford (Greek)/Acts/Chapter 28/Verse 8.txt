<p><strong> δυσεντερίῳ</strong> ] δυσεντερία, Ἀττικῶς· -ριον, Ἕλληνες . Mœris; <strong> dysentery</strong> . Dr. Falconer makes this an argument against ‘Melita Africana’ being meant. “Such a place, dry and rocky, and remarkably healthy, was not likely to produce a disease which is almost peculiar to moist situations.” But Mr. Smith answers, that the changed circumstances of the island might produce this change also: and besides, that he is informed by a physician of Valetta, that the disease is by no means uncommon in Malta.
