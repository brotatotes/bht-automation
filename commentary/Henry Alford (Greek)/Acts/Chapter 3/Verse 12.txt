<p><strong> 12</strong> . <strong> ἀπεκρίνατο</strong> ] viz. to their expressions of astonishment implied in ἔκθαμβοι . See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:25">Matthew 11:25</span> . <strong> ἀπεκρίνατο</strong> never signifies ‘ <em> made an address</em> ,’ as Bloomf.; but always ‘answered:’ cf. ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:8">Acts 5:8</span> , note. This second discourse of Peter may be thus divided: <em> This is no work of ours, but of God, for the glorifying of Jesus</em> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:12-13">Acts 3:12-13</span> : <em> whom ye denied and killed, but God hath raised up</em> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:13-15">Acts 3:13-15</span> : <em> through whose name this man is made whole</em> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:16">Acts 3:16</span> : <em> ye did it in ignorance, but God thereby fulfilled His counsel</em> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:17-18">Acts 3:17-18</span> . <em> Exhortation to repent, that ye may be forgiven, and saved by this Jesus Christ at His coming</em> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:19-21">Acts 3:19-21</span> ; <em> whose times have been the subject of prophecy from the first</em> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:21">Acts 3:21</span> . <em> Citations to prove this</em> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:22-24">Acts 3:22-24</span> ; <em> its immediate application to the hearers, as Jews</em> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:25-26">Acts 3:25-26</span> . There the discourse seems to be broken off, as ch. Act 4:1 relates.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐπὶ τούτῳ</strong> ] not, <em> at this</em> (event): but <strong> at this man</strong> , compare αὐτόν below, which would not be used at the <em> first</em> mention of one <em> then present</em> .
	</p>
<p>Their error was not the <em> wonder</em> itself, though even that would shew ignorance and weakness of faith, for it was truly no wonderful thing that had happened, viewed by a believer in Jesus, but their wondering <em> at the Apostles</em> , as if they had done it by their own power. ‘Ergo,’ says Calvin, ‘hoc est perperam obstupescere, quum in hominibus mentes nostræ subsistunt.’
	</p>
<p><strong> δυνάμει</strong> , <strong> power</strong> , such as <em> magical craft</em> , or any other supposed means of working miracles: <strong> εὐσεβείᾳ</strong> <strong> meritorious efficacy</strong> with God, so as to have obtained this from Him <em> on our own account</em> . The distinction is important: ‘ <em> holiness</em> ,’ of the E. V., is not expressive of εὐσεβ ., which bears in it the idea of <em> operative, cultive</em> piety, rather than of <em> inherent character</em> .</p>
