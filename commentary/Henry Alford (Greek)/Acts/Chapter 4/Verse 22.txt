<p><strong> 22.</strong> ] <strong> πλ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τεσσ</strong> <strong> .</strong> for πλ . ἢ τεσσ ., as sometimes in classical Greek; so οὐκ ἔλασσον πέντε καὶ εἴκοσι , Thucyd. vi. 95. See Winer, edn. 6, § 37, 5. The constr. <strong> ἐφ</strong> <strong> ʼ </strong> <strong> ὃν γεγόνει</strong> (see as in reff.) is accounted for by the sense involved in it being the <em> access</em> , so to speak, of the event <em> to</em> the person mentioned. In the note on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="re+4:2">Revelation 4:2</span> , I have noticed that καθῆσθαι ἐπί is commonly used when the fact is announced for the <em> first time</em> , with an accus.: but afterwards when the same fact is again referred to, with a gen. or dat.
