<p><strong> 23.</strong> ] <strong> τοὺς ἰδίους</strong> , the other Apostles, and possibly some others assembled with them. There is nothing in Act 4:31 to mark that only the Apostles were present on this occasion.
	</p>
<p><strong> 24</strong> ] <strong> ὁμοθ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ᾖραν φων</strong> <strong> .</strong> not, as Meyer supposes, literally all speaking together in a known formula of prayer, but led by some <em> one</em> , and all assenting; not τὰς φωνάς , but φωνήν : see note on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:6">Acts 2:6</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> σὺ</strong> [ <strong> ὁ θεὸς</strong> ] <strong> ὁ ποι</strong> <strong> .: Thou art God</strong> (or, if ὁ θεός be omitted, <strong> He</strong> ) <strong> who hast made</strong> : not <em> Thou O God who hast made</em> : in this latter case, the first sentence would go on to the end of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:26">Acts 4:26</span> , and there abruptly end, without any prayer being expressed: whereas now it is an acknowledgment that it was the <em> same God</em> , who was now <em> doing</em> these things, that bad beforetime prophesied them of Christ.</p>
