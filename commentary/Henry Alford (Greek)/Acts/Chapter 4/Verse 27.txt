<p><strong> 27</strong> .] The <strong> γάρ</strong> implies an acknowledgment of the truth of God in the fulfilment of the prophecy: <em> Thou art the God who hast</em> , &amp;c., <em> for</em> these events have happened accordingly.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐν τῇ πόλει ταύτῃ</strong> <strong> .</strong> which has been excluded from the text on account of its apparent redundance, answers to ἐπὶ Σιὼν ὄρος τὸ ἅγιον αὐτοῦ , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ps+2:6">Psalms 2:6</span> . See also <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:37">Matthew 23:37</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:33">Luke 13:33</span> . The parts of this verse correspond accurately to those of the prophecy just quoted.
	</p>
<p><strong> παῖδα</strong> , <strong> servant</strong> , as be fore, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:26">Acts 3:26</span> . Jesus, <em> the Servant of</em> Jehovah, is the antitype and completion of David, and of all other servants of the Lord: what is said of them only partially and hyperbolically, is said literally and entirely of Him.</p>
