<p><strong> 9.</strong> ] <strong> εἰ</strong> , <strong> if</strong> , with an implication of the fact being so: see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:17">Acts 11:17</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐν τίνι</strong> , not ‘by (in) <em> whom</em> ,’ this is not yet brought forward: but <strong> wherein</strong> , in what, as the conditional element. No <em> person</em> had been mentioned in the question, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:7">Acts 4:7</span> , nor does Peter afterwards say ἐν Ἰησοῦ χρ ., but ἐν τῷ ὀνόμ . Ἰ . χρ . On the other hand, <strong> ἐν τούτῳ</strong> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:10">Acts 4:10</span> , may very well be masculine, as referring to Ἰησοῦς χρ . Himself, included in the previous words τῷ ὀν . Ἰ . χρ .: it may also be neuter, ‘in this Name:’ but the masc. is preferable, on account of οὗτος following so soon in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:11">Acts 4:11</span> .</p>
