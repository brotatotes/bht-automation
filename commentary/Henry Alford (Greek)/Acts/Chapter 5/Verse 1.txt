<p><strong> 1</strong> .] <strong> Ἀνανίας</strong> , עֲנַנְיָה , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ne+3:23">Nehemiah 3:23</span> , or חֲנַנְיָה , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="da+1:6">Daniel 1:6</span> , in LXX: also <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1ch+3:21">1 Chronicles 3:21</span> , al.= <em> The cloud of God</em> , or <em> The mercy of God</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> Σαπφείρῃ</strong> , perhaps from the Greek σάπφειρος , sapphire, or from the Syriac שפירא , beautiful (Grot.).
	</p>
<p>The crime of these two is well described by Meyer: ‘By the sale of their field, and the bringing in of the money they in fact professed to give the <em> whole price</em> as a gift of brotherly love to the common stock: but their aim was to get for themselves the credit of holy love and zeal by <em> one portion</em> of the price, whereas they had selfishly kept back the other portion for themselves. They wished to serve <em> two</em> masters, but to appear to serve only <em> One</em> .’</p>
