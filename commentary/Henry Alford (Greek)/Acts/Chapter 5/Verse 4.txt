<p><strong> 4</strong> .] <strong> While it remained, did it not remain thine own</strong> ? i.e. was it not in thine absolute power? <strong> and when sold, was it not</strong> (i.e. the price of it) <strong> in thine own power</strong> , to do with it what seemed good to thee?
	</p>
<p><strong> τί ὅτι</strong> , i.e. τί ἐστιν ὅτι : see reff.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἔθου ἐν τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> καρδ</strong> <strong> .</strong> , = שׂוּם עַל־לֵב , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="da+1:8">Daniel 1:8</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mal+2:2">Malachi 2:2</span> . Satan suggested the lie, which Ananias ought to have repelled: instead of that, <em> he put it in his heart</em> , placed it there where the springs of action are, and it passed out into an act.
	</p>
<p><strong> οὐκ ἐψ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἀνθ</strong> <strong> ., </strong> <strong> ἀλλὰ τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> θ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] This οὐκ , ἀλλά , is not always an absolute and exclusive negation and assertion, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:37">Mark 9:37</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:44">John 12:44</span> . But here it seems to be so, and to imply, ‘Thine attempt to deceive was not to deceive <em> us</em> , men; but to deceive the Holy Ghost, God, abiding in His church, and in us its appointed superintendents.’ This verse is of weighty doctrinal import, as proving the Deity of the Holy Spirit; unless it be held, that the Holy Spirit whom ( Act 5:3 ) Ananias attempted to deceive, and God to whom he lied, are <em> different</em> . ‘Hæc est sententia: Ananias mentitus est Deo et ejus Spiritui, non hominibus et Petro. Aude si potes, Sociniane, ita dicere: mentitus est non Spiritui Sancto et Petro, sed Deo.’ Bengel.</p>
