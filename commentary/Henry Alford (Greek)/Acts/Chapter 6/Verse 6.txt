<p><strong> 6.</strong> ] <strong> ἐπέθηκαν</strong> , viz. the Apostles. Their office of giving themselves to <em> prayer</em> is here specially exercised.
	</p>
<p>The <em> laying on of hands</em> , the earliest mention of which is connected with <em> blessing only</em> ( Gen 48:14 ), was prescribed to Moses as the form of conferring office on Joshua, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="nu+27:18">Numbers 27:18</span> , and from that time was used on such occasions by the Jews. From its adoption by the Apostles, it has ever been the practice of the Christian church in <em> ordaining</em> , or <em> setting apart</em> her ministers. It was also used by the Apostles on those who, having been baptized, were to be fully endowed with the gifts of the Holy Spirit: see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:17">Acts 8:17</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:6">Acts 19:6</span> , and <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:2">Hebrews 6:2</span> .</p>
