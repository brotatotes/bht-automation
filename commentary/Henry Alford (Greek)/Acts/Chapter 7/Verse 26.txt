<p><strong> 26.</strong> ] <strong> αὐτοῖς</strong> , to them, <em> two of them</em> , taken as representing his brethren the children of Israel.
	</p>
<p><strong> συνήλασεν</strong> , not imperf., ‘ <em> he endeavoured to unite</em> :’ the aorist will not bear this sense: nor is it needed: the act, <em> on Moses’ part</em> , was complete; not ‘ <em> he would have set them at one</em> ’ (E. V.), but, <strong> he set them at one</strong> . If the explanatory reading συνήλλασσεν be taken, we then have the imperfect force “ <em> he was reconciling</em> ,” or “ <em> attempted to reconcile</em> ,” them.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἄνδρες ἀδελφοί</strong> should be taken together, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ge+13:8">Genesis 13:8</span> , ἄνθρωποι ἀδελφοί ἐσμεν ἡμεῖς . See also ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:14">Acts 2:14</span> (De W.).</p>
