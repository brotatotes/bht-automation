<p><strong> 30. </strong> <strong> ἐτ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τεσς</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] This follows from the tradition of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:23">Acts 7:23</span> , combined with <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ex+7:7">Exodus 7:7</span> , ‘Moses in palatio Pharaonis degit XL annos, in Midiane XL annos, et ministravit Israel XL annos.’ Bereshith Rabba, f. 115. 3. (Mey.)
	</p>
<p><strong> Σινᾶ</strong> ] <em> Horeb</em> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ex+3:1">Exodus 3:1</span> . But both were points of the same mountain range, and the names were convertibly used. In Exod., Levit., and Numb., the law is said to have been given from <em> Sinai</em> ; in Deut. from <em> Horeb</em> . ‘The desert of Mount Sina’ is the desert in which Mt. S. is situated. So ‘the Peak of Derbyshire,’ originally no doubt some single hill, has come to mean the whole district in which that hill is situated.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἄγγελος</strong> ] Here, as continually in the O. T., the angel bears the authority and presence of God Himself: which angel, since God <em> giveth not his glory to another</em> , must have been the <em> great Angel of the covenant</em> , the מַלְאַךְ פָּנָיו of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="isa+63:9">Isaiah 63:9</span> , ‘ <em> the Angel of His Presence</em> ,’ the SON OF GOD. See below on εἰς διαταγὰς ἀγγέλων , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:53">Acts 7:53</span> .
	</p>
<p>Stier remarks, that this <em> second</em> appearance of God, <em> to Moses</em> (see Act 7:2 ), introduces the <em> legal</em> dispensation, as the <em> first, to Abraham</em> , the <em> patriarchal</em> .
	</p>
<p>The readings of the LXX, as well as of our text, vary between πυρὶ φλογός (B) and φλογὶ πυρός (A). The Heb. is בְּלבַּת־אַשׁ . The construction is, <strong> in the fiery flame</strong> (or, <strong> the flaming fire) of a bush</strong> .</p>
