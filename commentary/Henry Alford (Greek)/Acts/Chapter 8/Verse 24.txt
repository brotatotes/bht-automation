<p><strong> 24.</strong> ] Simon speaks here much as Pharaoh, Exod. (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:28">Acts 8:28</span> ; Act 9:28 ) <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:17">Acts 10:17</span> , who yet hardened his heart afterwards (Stier). It is observable also that he wishes merely for the <em> averting of the punishment</em> . The words ὅπως μηδὲν ἐπέλθῃ ἐπ ʼ ἐμὲ ὧν εἰρήκατε seem remarkably to set forth the mere terror of the carnal man, without any idea of the ἐμέ becoming <em> another man</em> in thoughts and aims.</p>
