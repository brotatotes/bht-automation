<p><strong> 32.</strong> ] Perhaps it is best to render, <strong> The contents of</strong> the (passage of) <strong> Scripture</strong> which he <strong> was reading were as follows:</strong> see περιέχει , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:6">1 Peter 2:6</span> . Cicero indeed appears to use περιοχή in the sense of a ‘paragraph,’ or ‘chapter;’ ad Attic. iii. 25, ‘At ego ne Tironi quidem dictavi, qui totas περιοχάς persequi solet, sed Spintharo syllabatim.’ The citation is from the LXX-A, with only the variation of αὐτοῦ inserted after ταπεινώσει (and [ δέ ] before γενεάν ).</p>
