<p>AZOTUS or ASHDOD (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jos+13:3">Joshua 13:3</span> ; 1Sa 5:5 al.) was one of the five principal cities of the Philistines, never, though nominally in Judah, thoroughly subjugated by the Jews: it was taken by Tartan the Assyrian general ( Isa 20:1 ), again by Psammetichus, Herod. ii. 157; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jer+25:20">Jeremiah 25:20</span> , again by Judas Maccabæus ( 1Ma 5:68 ) and Jonathan (ib. 1Ma 10:84 ), and by the latter destroyed; rebuilt by Gabinius (Jos. Antt. xiv. 5. 3. B. J. i. 7. 7), and belonged to the kingdom of Herod, who left it in his will to his sister Salome (Antt. xvii. 8. 1; 11. 5). At present, it is a small village, retaining the name Esdud, but no remains. (Robinson, ii. 629; iii. 1, 232. Winer, Realw.)
