<p><strong> 15. </strong> <strong> σκ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἐκλογῆς</strong> ] A genit. of quality: as we say, ‘the man of his choice.’ See Winer, edn. 6, § 34. 3, b.
	</p>
<p>Paul often uses this word σκεῦος in a similar meaning, see reff., especially <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:0">Romans 9:0</span> , &amp;c., where it is in illustrating God’s sovereign power in election.
	</p>
<p><strong> βαστάσαι</strong> , perhaps in reference to the metaphor in σκεῦος .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐθνῶν</strong> ] This would hardly be understood at the time: it was afterwards on a remarkable occasion repeated to Paul by the Lord in a vision (see ch. Act 22:21 ), and was regarded by him as the specific command which gave the direction to his ministry, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:7-8">Galatians 2:7-8</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> βασιλ</strong> .] Agrippa, and probably Nero.</p>
