<p><strong> 29</strong> .] His own personal part in this general work <strong> for which end</strong> (viz. the παραστῆσαι , &amp;c.) <strong> I also</strong> ( <strong> καί</strong> implies the addition of a new particular over and above the καταγγέλλειν , carrying it onwards even to this) <strong> toil in conflict</strong> (of spirit; in the earnestness with which he strove for this end, see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="col+2:1-3">Colossians 2:1-3</span> ; not, with adversaries: this was so, but is not relevant here. See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="php+1:30">Philippians 1:30</span> . 1Th 2:2 ), <strong> according to</strong> (after the proportion of, as is to be expected from) <strong> His</strong> (Christ’s see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="php+4:13">Philippians 4:13</span> ; not God’s, as Chrys., Grot., Calv., al.) <strong> working which worketh</strong> (not passive, as Est. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:6">Galatians 5:6</span> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:20">Ephesians 3:20</span> , and Fritzsche on Rom 7:5 ) <strong> in me in power</strong> (reff.: there is no allusion to miraculous gifts, as Ambrst., Mich., al.).</p>
