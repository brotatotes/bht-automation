<p><strong> 28</strong> .] <strong> Let him that stealeth</strong> (not ‘ <em> that stole</em> ,’ as E. V.; ‘ <em> qui furabatur</em> ,’ Vulg.: cf. reff., and Winer, § 45. 7. Stier remarks well, that the word lies between κλέψας and κλέπτης : the former would be too mild, the latter too strong) <strong> steal no longer, but rather</strong> ( οὐ γὰρ ἀρκεῖ παύσασθαι τῆς ἁμαρτίας , ἀλλὰ καὶ τὴν ἐναντίαν αὐτῆς ὁδὸν μετελθεῖν , Thl.: similarly Chr.) <strong> let him labour, working</strong> (cf. besides reff., Joh 6:27 and note) <strong> with his hands</strong> (contrast to his former idleness for good, and bad use of those hands) <strong> that which is good</strong> ( τὸ ἀγ . ‘antitheton ad furtum prius manu piceata commissum.’ Beng.), <strong> in order that</strong> (as a purpose to be set before every Christian in his honest labour) <strong> he may have to impart to him that has need</strong> .</p>
