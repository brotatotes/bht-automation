<p><strong> 5</strong> .] <strong> one Lord</strong> (as the Head of the Church: in this verse he grounds the co-existence of the ἓν σῶμα κ . ἓν πνεῦμα in the three great facts on which it rests the first objective, <strong> εἷς κύριος</strong> the second subjective, <strong> μίαπίστις</strong> the third compounded of the two, <strong> ἓν βάπτισμα</strong> ), <strong> one faith</strong> (in that one Lord: the subjective medium by which that one Lord is apprehended and appropriated: not ‘fides <em> quæ</em> creditur,’ but ‘fides <em> quâ</em> creditur:’ but it is necessarily understood, that this subjective faith has for its object the One Lord just mentioned) <strong> one baptism</strong> (the objective seal of the subjective faith, by which, as a badge, the members of Christ are outwardly and visibly stamped with His name.
