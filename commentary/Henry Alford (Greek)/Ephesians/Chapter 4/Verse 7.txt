<p><strong> 7</strong> .] <strong> But</strong> (the contrast is between <strong> ἐν πᾶσιν</strong> and <strong> ἑνὶ ἑκάστῳ</strong> the general, and the particular. And the connexion is as a motive to keep the unity of the Spirit ‘none is overlooked: each has his part in the distribution of the gifts of the One Spirit, which part he is bound to use for the well-being of the whole’) <strong> to each one of us was given</strong> (by Christ, at the time of His exaltation when He bestowed gifts on men) [ <strong> the</strong> ] <strong> grace</strong> (which was then bestowed: <em> the</em> unspeakable gift, or, if the art. be omitted, grace, absolutely, was distributed to each κατά &amp;c.) <strong> according to the measure of</strong> (subjective genitive: the amount of: cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:3">Romans 12:3</span> , ἑκάστῳ ὡς ὁ θεὸς ἐμέρισεν μέτρον πίστεως ) <strong> the gift of Christ</strong> (‘ <em> Christ’s gift</em> ;’ the gift bestowed by Christ. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:15">2 Corinthians 9:15</span> : not, ‘the gift which Christ received,’ for He is the subject and centre here so Calv., ‘porro Christum facit auctorem, quia sicut a Patre fecit initium, ita in ipsum vult nos et nostra omnia colligere.’
