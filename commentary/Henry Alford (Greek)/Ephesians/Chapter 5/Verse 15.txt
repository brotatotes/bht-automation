<p><strong> 15</strong> .] He now resumes the hortative strain, interrupted by the digression of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:12-14">Ephesians 5:12-14</span> . <strong> Take heed then</strong> (there is not any immediate connexion with the last verse: but the <strong> οὖν</strong> resumes from the περιπατεῖτε in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:8">Ephesians 5:8</span> , and that which followed it there) <strong> how ye walk strictly</strong> (the construction is exactly as in ref. 1 Cor., ἕκαστος δὲ <strong> βλεπέτω πῶς</strong> ἐποικοδομεῖ . ‘Take heed, of what sort your ἀκριβῶς περιπατεῖν is:’ the implication being, ‘take heed not only that your walk be exact, strict, but also <em> of what sort</em> that strictness <em> is</em> not only that you <em> have</em> a rule, and <em> keep to it</em> , but that that rule be the <em> best</em> one.’ So that a double exhortation is involved. See Ellic. here: and the Fritzschiorum Opuscula, pp. 208 f., note), ( <strong> namely</strong> ) <strong> not as unwise, but as wise</strong> (qualification of the <strong> ἀκριβῶς περιπατεῖτε</strong> , and expansion of the πῶς ( μή , subj.): no περιπατοῦντες need be supplied after μή , as Harl.), <strong> buying up for yourselves (the) opportunity</strong> (viz. of good, whenever occurring; let it not pass by, but as merchants carefully looking out for vantages, make it your own: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="col+4:5">Colossians 4:5</span> . The compound ἐξ - does not suggest the question ‘ <em> from whom</em> ’ it is to be bought, as Beng., Calv., al., nor imply mere <em> completeness</em> , as Mey., but rather refers to the ‘ <em> collection out of</em> ’ (see reff. Gal.), the buying <em> up</em> , as we say: culling your times of good out of a land where there are few such flowers. The middle gives the reflexive sense: cf. ref. Dan.), <strong> because the days</strong> (of your time, in which you live) <strong> are evil</strong> (see above. ὁ ἐξαγοραζόμενος τὸν ἀλλότριον δοῦλον , ἐξαγοράζεται κ . κτᾶται αὐτόν . ἐπεὶ οὖν ὁ καιρὸς δουλεύει τοῖς πονηροῖς , ἐξαγοράσασθε αὐτόν , ὥστε καταχρήσασθαι αὐτῷ τρὸς εὐσέβειαν . Severianus, in Cramer’s Catena).</p>
