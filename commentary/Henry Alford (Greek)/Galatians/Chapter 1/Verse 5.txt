<p><strong> 5</strong> . <strong> ᾧ ἡ δόξα</strong> ] So (reff.) on other occasions, when speaking of the wonderful things of God, St. Paul adds a doxology. “In politeia, quando regum aut principum nomina appellamus, id honesto quodam gestu, reverentia, et genuflexione facere solemus. Multo magis cum de Deo loquimur, genu cordis flectere debemus.” Luther. In ἡ δόξα , <strong> the glory</strong> κατ ʼ ἐξοχήν , or ‘the glory which is His,’ the article is probably inserted for solemnity. “In this and similar forms of doxology, excepting the angelic doxology, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:14">Luke 2:14</span> , and that of the multitude, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:38">Luke 19:38</span> , δόξα regularly takes the article when used alone: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:36">Romans 11:36</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:27">Romans 16:27</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:21">Ephesians 3:21</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="php+4:20">Philippians 4:20</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:18">2 Timothy 4:18</span> ; Heb 13:21 ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:18">2 Peter 3:18</span> . When joined with one or more substantives, it appears sometimes with the article (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:11">1 Peter 4:11</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="re+1:6">Revelation 1:6</span> ; Rev 7:12 ): sometimes without it (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:10">Romans 2:10</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:17">1 Timothy 1:17</span> ; Jud 1:25 ).” Ellicott.
