<p><strong> 8</strong> .] <strong> But</strong> (no matter who they are οἱ ταρ . &amp;c.) <strong> even though</strong> (in καὶ εἰ , καὶ ἐάν , &amp;c., the force of the καί is distributed over the whole supposition following, see Hartung, Partikell. i. 139; and ἐάν is distinguished from εἰ , in supposing a case which has never occurred, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:1">1 Corinthians 13:1</span> , and a full explanation in Herm. on Viger, p. 832) <strong> we</strong> (i.e. usually, ‘ <em> I, Paul</em> :’ but perhaps <em> used</em> here on account of οἱ σὺν ἐμοὶ πάντες ἀδελφοί , Gal 1:2 ) <strong> or an angel from heaven</strong> ( ἄγγ . ἐξ οὐρ . to be taken together, not ἐξ οὐρ . εὐαγγ .: introduced here as the highest possible authority, next to a divine Person: even were this possible, were the highest rank of created beings to furnish the preacher, &amp;c. See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:1">1 Corinthians 13:1</span> . Perhaps also, as Chrys., there is a reference to the new teachers having sheltered themselves under the names of the great Apostles: μὴ γάρ μοι Ἰάκωβον εἴπῃς , φησί , καὶ Ἰωάννην · κἂν γὰρ τῶν πρώτων ἀγγέλων ᾖ τις τῶν ἐξ οὐρανοῦ διαφθειρόντων τὸ κήρυγμα κ . τ . λ . Then he adds: ταῦτα δὲ οὐχ ὡς καταγινώσκων τ . ἀποστόλων φησίν , οὐδὲ ὡς παραβαινόντων τὸ κήρυγμα , ἄπαγε · εἴτε γὰρ ἡμεῖς , εἴτε ἐκεῖνοι , φησίν , οὕτω κηρύσσομεν · ἀλλὰ δεῖξα . βουλόμενος ὅτι ἀξίωμα προσώπων οὐ προσίεται , ὅταν περὶ ἀληθείας ὁ λόγος ᾖ ), preach (evangelize: it is impossible to preserve in English the εὐαγγέλιον , and in it the reference back to Gal 1:6-7 ) <strong> to you other than what</strong> ( παρά (reff.) as in παρὰ δόξαν , παρὰ τοὺς ὅρκους , παραβαίνειν , &amp;c. not merely ‘ <em> against</em> ,’ nor merely ‘ <em> besides</em> ,’ but indicating ‘ <em> beyond</em> ,’ in the sense of overstepping the limit into a new region, i.e. it points out <em> specific difference</em> . The preposition is important here, as it has been pressed by Protestants in the sense of ‘ <em> besides</em> ,’ against Roman Catholic tradition, and in consequence maintained by the latter in the sense of ‘ <em> against</em> .’ It in fact includes both) <strong> we preached</strong> (evangelized) <strong> to you, let him be accursed</strong> ( <em> of God</em> : no reference to ecclesiastical excommunication: for an <em> angel</em> is here included. See note, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:3">Romans 9:3</span> , and compare ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:10">Galatians 5:10</span> ; also Ellic.’s and Bagge’s notes here).</p>
