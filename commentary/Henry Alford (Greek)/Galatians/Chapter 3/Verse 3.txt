<p><strong> 3</strong> .] <strong> Are ye so</strong> (to such an extent, emph.) <strong> foolish</strong> (as viz. the following fact would prove)? <strong> Having begun</strong> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="php+1:6">Philippians 1:6</span> , where the same two verbs occur together, and <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:6">2 Corinthians 8:6</span> , where προενήρξατο is followed by ἐπιτελέσῃ . Understand, ‘the Christian life’) <strong> in the Spirit</strong> (dative of the manner in which, reff. The Spirit, i.e. the Holy Spirit, guiding and ruling the spiritual life, as the ‘essence and active principle’ (Ellic.) of Christianity, contrasted with the flesh, the element in which the law worked), <strong> are ye now being completed</strong> (passive here, not mid., cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="php+1:6">Philippians 1:6</span> , where the active is used: and for the passive, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:32">Luke 13:32</span> . The middle does not appear to occur in the N. T., though it does in classical Greek, e.g. Polyb. ii. 58. 10, μηθὲν ἀσεβὲς ἐπιτελεσαμένοις . Diod. Sic. xii. 54, μεγάλας πράξεις ἐπιτελεσάμενοι ) in (dative, as above) <strong> the flesh?</strong></p>
