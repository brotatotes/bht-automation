<p><strong> 7</strong> .] Statement of the conclusion from the foregoing, and corroboration, from it, of ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:29">Galatians 3:29</span> . The second person singular individualizes and points home the inference. Meyer remarks that this individualization has been gradually proceeding from Gal 4:5 ἀπολάβωμεν , ἔστε , εἶ .
	</p>
<p><strong> διὰ θεοῦ</strong> ] The rec. θεοῦ διὰ χριστοῦ seems to have been an adaptation to the similar passage, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:17">Romans 8:17</span> .
	</p>
<p>On the text, Windischmann remarks, “ διὰ θεοῦ combines, on behalf of our race, the whole before-mentioned agency of the Blessed Trinity: the Father has sent the Son and the Spirit, the Son has freed us from the law, the Spirit has completed our sonship; and thus the redeemed are heirs through the tri-une God Himself, not through the law, nor through fleshly descent.”</p>
