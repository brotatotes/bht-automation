<p><strong> φαρμ</strong> <strong> .</strong> , either ‘ <em> poisonings</em> ,’ or ‘ <em> sorceries</em> .’ The latter is preferable, as more frequently its sense in the LXX and N. T. (reff.), and because (Mey.) Asia was particularly addicted to sorceries ( Act 19:19 ).

<p>Wetst. N. T. ii. p. 147, traces in a note the later meanings of <strong> αἵρεσις</strong> . Here διχοστ ., <strong> divisions</strong> , seems to lead to αἱρέσ ., <strong> parties</strong> , composed of those who have <em> chosen</em> their self-willed line and adhere to it. Trench quotes Aug. (cont. Crescon. Don. ii. 7 (9), vol. ix. p. 471): “ <em> Schisma</em> est recens congregationis ex aliquâ sententiarum diversitate dissensio: <em> hæresis</em> autem schisma inveteratum.” But we must not think of an ecclesiastical meaning only, or chiefly here.</p>
