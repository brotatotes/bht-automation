<p><strong> 7 12</strong> .] <em> He laments their deflexion from their once promising course, and denounces severely their perverters</em> . <strong> Ye were running well</strong> (‘hoc est, omnia apud vos erant in felici statu et successu, vivebatis optime, contendebatis recta ad vitam æternam quam vobis pollicebatur verbum,’ &amp;c. Luther): <strong> who</strong> (see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:1">Galatians 3:1</span> , the question expresses astonishment) <strong> hindered you</strong> (Polyb. xxiv. 1. 12, uses ἐγκόπτειν with a dative, διὰ τὸ τὸν Φίλιππον ἐγκόπτειν τῇ δικαιοδοσίᾳ : Ellic. quotes, in connexion with the view of the primary notion being that of hindering by breaking up a road, Greg. Naz. Or. xvi. p. 260, ἢ κακίας ἐγκοπτομένης δυσπάθεια τῶν πονηρῶν , ἢ ἀρετῆς ὁδοποιουμένης εὐπάθεια τῶν βελτιόνων ) <strong> that ye should not</strong> ( μή before πείθεσθαι is not pleonastic, but the construction, so often occurring, of a negative after verbs of hindering, is in fact a pregnant one, μὴ πείθεσθαι being the <em> result</em> of the hindrance: q. d. ὥστε μὴ π . or καὶ ἐποίησε μὴ π . See Bernhardy, Syntax, ix. 6 b, who quotes one example very apposite to this, ἐμποδὼν ἡμῖν γένηται τὴν θεὸν μὴ ʼ ξελκύσαι , Aristoph) <strong> obey the truth</strong> (i.e. submit yourselves to the true Gospel of Christ.
