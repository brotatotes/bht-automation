Cyr. vi. 3. 11, ὦ Ζεῦ μέγιστε , λαβεῖν μοι γένοιτο αὐτόν ), <strong> except in the Cross</strong> (the atoning death, as my means of reconcilement with God) <strong> of our Lord Jesus Christ</strong> (the full name for solemnity, and ἡμῶν to involve his readers in the duty of the same abjuration), <strong> by means of whom</strong> (not so well, ‘of <em> which</em> ’ ( τοῦ σταυροῦ ) as many Commentators; the greater antecedent, <strong> τοῦ κυρ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἡμ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> Ἰ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> χ</strong> <strong> .</strong> , coming after the σταυρῷ , has thrown it into the shade. Besides, it could hardly be said of the Cross, <strong> δι</strong> <strong> ʼ </strong> <strong> οὗ</strong> ) <strong> the world</strong> (the whole system of unspiritual and unchristian men and things.
