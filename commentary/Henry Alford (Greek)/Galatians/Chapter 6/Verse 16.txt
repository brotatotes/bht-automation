<p><strong> 16</strong> .] <strong> And as many</strong> (reference to the ὅσοι of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:12">Galatians 6:12</span> ; and in κανόνι to the εὐπροσωπ . and πηλίκοις γράμμ .? see above) <strong> as shall walk by this rule</strong> (of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:15">Galatians 6:15</span> . κανών is a ‘straight rule,’ to detect crookedness: hence a <em> norma vivendi</em> . The dative is <em> normal</em> ), <strong> peace be</strong> (not ‘ <em> is</em> :’ it is the apostolic blessing, so common in the <em> beginnings</em> of his Epistles: see also Eph 6:23 ) <strong> upon them</strong> (come on them from God; reff., and <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:25">Luke 2:25</span> ; Luk 2:40 al. freq.) <strong> and</strong> (and indeed, ‘ <em> und zwar</em> :’ the καὶ explicative, as it is called: see reff.) <strong> upon the Israel of God</strong> (the subject of the whole Epistle seems to have given rise to this expression. Not the Israel after the flesh, among whom these teachers wish to enrol you, are blessed: but the ISRAEL OF GOD, described ch. 3. ult., εἰ δὲ ὑμεῖς χριστοῦ , ἄρα τοῦ Ἀβραὰμ σπέρμα ἐστέ . Jowett compares, though not exactly parallel, yet for a similar apparent though not actual distinction, 1Co 10:32 ).</p>
