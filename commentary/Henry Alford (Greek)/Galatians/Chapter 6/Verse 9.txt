<p><strong> 9</strong> .] <strong> But</strong> (in our case, let there be no chance of the alternative: see Hartung, Partikell. i. 166) <strong> in well-doing</strong> (stress on <strong> καλόν</strong> ) <strong> let us not be faint-hearted</strong> (on <strong> ἐγκ</strong> <strong> .</strong> and <strong> ἐκ</strong> κ ., see note, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:1">2 Corinthians 4:1</span> . It seems doubtful, whether such a word as ἐκκακέω exists at all in Greek, and whether its use by later writers and place in lexicons is not entirely due to these doubtful readings. See Ellic.’s note): <strong> for in due time</strong> (an expression of the pastoral Epistles, see reff., and Prolegomena to those Epistles, § i. 32, and note) <strong> we shall reap, if we do not faint</strong> (so reff., and Isocr., p. 322 a, ἵν ʼ οὖν μὴ παντάπασιν ἐκλυθῶ , πολλῶν ἔτι μοι λεκτέων ὄντων ). Thdrt., al., join μὴ ἐκλ . with θερίσομεν , πόνου δίχα θερίσομεν τὰ σπειρόμενα · … ἐπὶ μὲν γὰρ τῶν αἰσθητῶν σπερμάτων καὶ ὁ σπόρος ἔχει πόνου , κ . ὁ ἀμητὸς ὡσαύτως · διαλύει γὰρ πολλάκις τοὺς ἀμῶντας κ . τὸ τῆς ὥρας θερμόν · ἀλλ ʼ ἐκεῖνος οὐ τοιοῦτος ὁ ἀμητός · πόνου γάρ ἐστι κ . ἱδρῶτος ἐλεύθερος . But though such a rendering would be unobjectionable (not requiring <strong> οὐ</strong> for μή , as Rück., al., for as Mey. rightly, the particle being subjective, μή would be in place), it would give a very vapid sense: whereas the other eminently suits the exhortation μὴ ἐγκ .</p>
