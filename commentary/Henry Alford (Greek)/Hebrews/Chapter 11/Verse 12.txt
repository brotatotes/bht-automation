<p><strong> 12</strong> .] <em> Wonderful result of this faith of Abraham and Sarah</em> . <strong> Wherefore also</strong> ( <strong> διὸ καί</strong> , which occurs again ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:12">Hebrews 13:12</span> , is frequent in St. Luke and St. Paul, see reff.) <strong> from one sprung there</strong> (the reading is doubtful, but ἐγεν . ἀπό seems to suit better the <em> father</em> , whereas ἐγενν . ἀπό , ‘these were born from,’ would almost necessarily be said of the <em> mother</em> ) <strong> and that</strong> (there is no foundation for Lünemann’s notion, that the plur. <strong> ταῦτα</strong> has reference to the two circumstances, the deadness of Abraham and the unbelief of Sarah: ταῦτα in such sentences is perpetually the collective plural, = τοῦτο . Cf. Kühner, Gram. § 667 c, who gives as examples, Plato, Rep. iii. p. 404 B, Ὅμηρος … ἐν ταῖς τῶν ἡρώων ἑστιάσεσιν οὔτε ἰχθύσιν αὐτοὺς ἑστιᾷ , καὶ ταῦτα ἐπὶ θαλάττῃ τῇ Ἑλλησπόντῳ ὄντας : Demosth. c. Phorm. Extr., θανάτῳ ζημιώσαντες εἰσαγγελθέντα ἐν τῷ δήμῳ , καὶ ταῦτα πολίτην ὑμέτερον ὄντα , “quamvis civis vester esset”) ( <strong> from one</strong> ) <strong> deadened</strong> (past that vital power which nature requires: see ref. Rom.) <strong> even as</strong> (it may be asked what is the subject to ἐγενήθησαν ? Some supply τέκνα or ἔκγονοι , see Winer, § 64. 3: but it is better to make the whole, καθώς to the end, the virtual subject, latent in καθώς = ὡμοιωμένοι τοῖς ἄστρ . κ . τ . λ .) <strong> the stars of the heaven in multitude, and as the sand which is by the lip</strong> (margin, cf. παρὰ χεῖλος ἑκατέρου τοῦ ποταμοῦ in ref. Herod. and Polyb. v. 14. 6; iii. 43. 8 al. fr. in index) <strong> of the sea which is innumerable</strong> (so ran the promises to Abraham, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ge+13:16">Genesis 13:16</span> , καὶ ποιήσω τὸ σπέρμα σου ὡς τὴν ἄμμον τῆς γῆς : <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ge+15:5">Genesis 15:5</span> , ἀνάβλεψον δὴ εἰς τὸν οὐρανόν , καὶ ἀρίθμησον τοὺς ἀστέρας , εἰ δυνήσῃ ἐξαριθμῆσαι αὐτούς · καὶ εἶπεν , Οὕτως ἔσται τὸ σπέρμασου : and more fully <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ge+22:17">Genesis 22:17</span> , πληθύνων πληθυνῶ τὸ σπέρμα σου ὡς τοὺς ἀστέρας τοῦ οὐρανοῦ , καὶ ὡς τὴν ἄμμον τὴν παρὰ τὸ χεῖλος τῆς θαλάσσης . The comparison with the sand as indicating great number is frequently found in the O. T., e. g. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ge+41:49">Genesis 41:49</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jos+11:4">Joshua 11:4</span> ; 1Sa 13:5 ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2sa+17:11">2 Samuel 17:11</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1ki+4:29">1 Kings 4:29</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="isa+10:22">Isaiah 10:22</span> . Cf. also Herod. i. 48, οἶδα δ ʼ ἐγὼ ψάμμου τ ʼ ἀριθμόν , καὶ μέτρα θαλάσσης , and Pind. Olymp. ii. in fine, ἐπεὶ ψάμμος ἀριθμὸν περιπέφευγεν ).</p>
