<p><strong> 13</strong> .] <strong> In</strong> (according to, consistently with, in the course of: not this time πίστει , because their deaths were not the results of their faith, but merely according to and consistent with it) <strong> faith died these all</strong> (there is no need to say with Œc., Thl., Primas., al., ἐξῃρημένου τοῦ Ἐνώχ : the promises began with Abraham, and it is evident from the end of our verse, and from <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:15">Hebrews 11:15</span> , that the reference is solely to the patriarchs), <strong> not having received</strong> (the participial clause conditions and substantiates the κατὰ πίστιν … ἀπέθανον : and for this reason it is μὴ λαβ . and not οὐ : ‘as those who did not receive’ &amp;c.) <strong> the promises</strong> (plur., because the promise was again and again repeated to the patriarchs, see the citations from Gen. above, and add <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ge+17:5-8">Genesis 17:5-8</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ge+26:3-4">Genesis 26:3-4</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ge+28:13-14">Genesis 28:13-14</span> . The <strong> ἐπαγγελία</strong> , here as so often comprehends τὸ ἐπηγγελμένον ), <strong> but having seen them from afar</strong> ( καὶ πεισθέντες , see var. readd., has come in from a gloss: so Chrys., οὗτοι πεπεισμένοι ἦσαν περὶ αὐτῶν ὡς καὶ ἀσπάσασθαι αὐτάς : Œc., καὶ ἀσπασάμενοι · πεισθέντες ), <strong> and greeted them</strong> (“From afar they saw the promises in the reality of their fulfilment, from afar they greeted them as the wanderer greets his longed-for home even when he only comes in sight of it at a distance, drawing to himself as it were magnetically and embracing with inward love that which is yet afar off. The exclamation, ‘I have waited for thy salvation, O Lord,’ <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ge+49:18">Genesis 49:18</span> , is such an ἀσπασμός , such a greeting of salvation from afar.” Delitzsch.
