<p><strong> 14</strong> .] <strong> For</strong> (justification of the assertion, that it was κατὰ πίστιν that they ran and finished their course, by the inference from their own confession) <strong> they who say such things make manifest</strong> (so <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:15">Acts 23:15</span> ; where see examples in Wetst. The word in this sense is pure classical Greek: cf. Plato, Soph. p. 244, ὑμεῖς αὐτὰ ἡμῖν ἐμφανίζετε ἱκανῶς , τί ποτε βούλεσθε σημαίνειν , ὁπόταν ὂν φθέγγησθε ; and p. 218, ζητοῦντι καὶ ἐμφανίζοντι τί ποτε ἐστίν ) <strong> that they seek after</strong> (in <strong> ἐπι</strong> ζητέω , the preposition implies the direction of the wish or yearning) <strong> a home</strong> (our English word ‘country,’ without some possessive pronoun, does not give the idea strongly enough. Even Bleek, who might have given it, <em> dass sie ein Baterland suchen</em> , has rendered, <em> dass sie nach der Heimath suchen</em> : οἱ ξένους ἑαυτούς , φησίν , ὀνομάζοντες , δηλοῦσιν ὡς οὐδὲν οἰκεῖον κρίνουσι τῶν παρόντων , ἀλλ ʼ ἑτέρων ἐπιθυμοῦσι πραγμάτων . Thdrt.).</p>
