<p><strong> 30</strong> .] <em> A second example of the strength of faith in Israel generally</em> . <strong> By faith</strong> (of Israel, who obeyed the command of Joshua through all the days, which to the unbeliever would seem irrational. Cf. Chrys., οὐ γὰρ δὴ σαλπίγγων ἠχὴ λίθους οἵα τε καταβάλλειν ἐστί , κἂν μυρία τις ἔτη σαλπίζῃ , ἀλλ ʼ ἡ πίστις πάντα δύναται ), <strong> the walls of Jericho</strong> (more commonly τῆς Ἱεριχώ : but our Writer frequently omits the demonstrative article, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:17">Hebrews 11:17</span> ; ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:7">Hebrews 4:7</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:11">Hebrews 7:11</span> ; Heb 9:4 ) <strong> fell</strong> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jos+6:5">Joshua 6:5</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jos+6:20">Joshua 6:20</span> . In the former of these it is πεσεῖται τὰ τείχη , in the latter ἔπεσεν ἅπαν τὸ τεῖχος : our Writer uses the plural verb with τείχη : each and every defence fell together), <strong> having been compassed about</strong> (see the narrative in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jos+6:0">Joshua 6:0</span> ) <strong> during seven days</strong> ( <strong> ἐπί</strong> , of time, with an accusative, gives the whole duration: see reff., and Winer, 49. 1. 2).</p>
