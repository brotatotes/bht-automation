Numa, § 19, ἀπὸ Καίσαρος , τοῦ κατηγωνισαμένου Πομπήϊον , &amp;c. The acts referred to may be Gideon’s victory over the Midianites (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jdg+7:0">Judges 7:0</span> ), Barak’s over the Canaanites (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jdg+9:0">Judges 9:0</span> ), Samson’s over the Philistines (Judges 14 ff.), Jephthah’s over the Ammonites (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jdg+9:0">Judges 9:0</span> .), David’s over the Philistines (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2sa+5:17-25">2 Samuel 5:17-25</span> ; 2Sa 8:1 ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2sa+21:15">2 Samuel 21:15</span> ff.), Moabites, Syrians, Edomites (ib. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2sa+8:2">2 Samuel 8:2</span> ff.), Ammonites (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2sa+10:12">2 Samuel 10:12</span> , ff.)), <strong> wrought righteousness</strong> (so Samuel, the righteous judge, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1sa+12:3-4">1 Samuel 12:3-4</span> ; David, the righteous king, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2sa+8:15">2 Samuel 8:15</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1ch+18:14">1 Chronicles 18:14</span> ; and indeed in a wide sense all of them, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jer+23:5">Jeremiah 23:5</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eze+45:9">Ezekiel 45:9</span> , τοῦτο κοινὸν τῶν ἁγίων ἁπάντων , as Thdrt.), <strong> obtained promises</strong> (the words are capable of two senses: 1. got from God spoken promises, as e. g. the Prophets: or 2. obtained the fulfilment of promises. (1) is taken by Chrys. (referring it to the promise to David that his seed should sit on his throne), Thdrt., Primas., Schlicht., Bleek, Ebrard, al. But it seems to me altogether improbable that the Writer should thus illustrate faith by a fact which, though it may have accompanied faith in the recipient, was certainly no fruit or direct triumph of it: and that in the face of such sayings as Jos 21:45 and <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1ki+8:56">1 Kings 8:56</span> , and of Gideon’s trials of God. The objection which is brought against (2), that it is inconsistent with μὴ λαβόντες τὰς ἐπαγγελίας , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:13">Hebrews 11:13</span> , and with οὐκ ἐκομίσαντο τὴν ἐπαγγελίαν , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:39">Hebrews 11:39</span> , is very simply answered: it is not said that they ἐπέτυχον <strong> τῶν</strong> ἐπαγγελιῶν or τῆς ἐπαγγελίας , but anarthrously: they obtained promises, but not <em> the</em> promises which were yet future. And so most Commentators), <strong> stopped the mouths of lions</strong> (referring principally, it may be, to Daniel, of whom it is said, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="da+6:22">Daniel 6:22</span> , that God sent his angel and stopped ( וּסֲגַר , ἐνέφραξε Theodotion; LXX freely, ἔσωσέν με ἀπὸ τῶν λ .) the mouths of the lions: where notice also the addition ( Heb 11:23 Theod.), ὅτι ἐπίστευσεν ἐν τῷ θεῷ αὐτοῦ .
