<p><strong> 15</strong> .] <strong> Through Him</strong> (placed first, as carrying all the emphasis through Him, not by means of the Jewish ritual observances) <strong> therefore</strong> (this <strong> οὖν</strong> gathers its inference from the whole argument, Heb 13:10-14 ) <strong> let us offer up</strong> (see on ref.) <strong> a sacrifice of praise</strong> ( <strong> θυσία αἰνέσεως</strong> is the term for a thank-offering in the law: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="le+7:12">Leviticus 7:12</span> (5, LXX). Cf. reff. and Ps. 49:23, θυσία αἰνέσεως δοξάσει με , and <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ps+115:17">Psalms 115:17</span> ( Psa 116:8 ), σοὶ θύσω θυσίαν αἰνέσεως . The Commentators quote an old saying of the Rabbis, “Tempore futuro omnia sacrificia cessabunt, sed laudes non cessabunt.” Cf. Philo de Victim. Offer. § 3, vol. ii. p. 253, τὴν ἀρίστην ἀνάγουσι θυσίαν , ὕμνοις τὸν εὐεργέτην καὶ σωτῆρα θεὸν γεραίροντες ) <strong> continually</strong> (not at fixed days and seasons, as the Levitical sacrifices, but all through our lives) <strong> to God, that is, the fruit of lips</strong> ( καρπὸν χειλέων is from Hosea (ref.), where the LXX give ἀνταποδώσομεν καρπὸν χειλέων ἡμῶν as the rendering of נְשַׁלְּמָה פָרִים שְׂפָתינוּ , “we will account our lips as calves” (for a sacrifice): E. V., “we will render the calves of our lips.” The fruit of the lips is explained by the next words to be, a good confession to God) <strong> confessing to His name</strong> (i. e. the name of God, as the ultimate object to which the confession, δι ʼ - αὐτοῦ , Jesus, is referred. For the construction, see reff.).</p>
