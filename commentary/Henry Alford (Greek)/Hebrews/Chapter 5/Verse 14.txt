Here again there manifestly is a mixture of the figure and the thing signified: on account of what follows, we must necessarily understand these αἰσθητήρια of the inner organs of the soul: as Œc., τὰ τῆς ψυχῆς αἰσθητήρια λέγει ) <strong> exercised</strong> (reff.) <strong> with a view to</strong> (so in ref. 1 Tim., γύμαναζε δὲ σεαυτὸν πρὸς εὐσέβειαν : see also reff. here. <strong> πρός</strong> most likely belongs to γεγυμνασμένα , not to the whole τῶν .… ἐχόντων , because of the art. τά , which makes γεγυμνασμένα a predicate, not an epithet. The reference here of good and evil is manifestly not to <em> moral</em> qualities, but to excellence and inferiority, wholesomeness and corruptness in doctrine.
