<p><strong> 13</strong> .] <em> Confirmation of the position that a change is made in the law, by another fact indicative of a change in the priesthood</em> . <strong> For He with reference to whom</strong> (cf. reff.: and ὠς ἐπὶ τὸ πᾶν εἰπεῖν , Plato, Legg. ii. p. 667 D) <strong> these things</strong> (viz. the promise in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ps+110:0">Psalms 110:0</span> .: not, these which I am now saying) <strong> are said, is member of</strong> ( <em> hath taken part in</em> : the perfect implying the enduring of His humanity) <strong> a different tribe</strong> (from that of Levi, which has been already sufficiently indicated in the preceding context), <strong> of which</strong> (sprung from which, coming from which, see reff.) <strong> no one hath</strong> (ever, to this day) <strong> given attention</strong> (applied himself, see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:1">Hebrews 2:1</span> , note; and reff. So Demosth. p. 10. 25, τῷ πολέμῳ προσέχειν : Xen. Mem. iv. 1. 2, ταχὺ μανθάνειν οἷς προσέχοιεν : Polyæn. p. 415, ταῖς γεωργίαις προσεῖχον ) <strong> to the altar</strong> (i. e. as a general and normal practice, had any thing to do with the service of the priesthood).</p>
