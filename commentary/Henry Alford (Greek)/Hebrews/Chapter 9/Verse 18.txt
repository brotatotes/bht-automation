<p><strong> 18</strong> .] <strong> Whence</strong> ( τουτέστι , διότι ἀναγκαῖόν ἐστι τὸ θάνατον προηγεῖσθαι τῆς διαθήκης . Thl.) <strong> neither has the first</strong> ( διαθήκη , testament) <strong> been inaugurated</strong> (perf., inasmuch as the rites &amp;c. belonging to it were still subsisting. <strong> ἐγκαινίζω</strong> is an Alexandrine verb: used in the LXX for to <em> re-create</em> or make anew: also for to put forth as new, to <em> inaugurate</em> : see reff., and numerous citations in Trommius. Notice that the reference is, <em> here</em> , simply to the first encænia of the law when it was put forth as new: not to any subsequent renewal of sacrifices by death: this is presently alluded to, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:21">Hebrews 9:21</span> ff. Thl. gives for ἐγκεκαίνισται , τουτέστι , τὴν ἀρχὴν τῆς συντάσεως κ . τῆς βεβαιώσεως ἔλαβεν ) <strong> without</strong> (apart from, free from the exhibition of) <strong> blood</strong> .</p>
