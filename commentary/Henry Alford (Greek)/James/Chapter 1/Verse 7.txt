<p><strong> 7</strong> .] <strong> for</strong> (takes up and repeats the former γάρ : not as Calvin, “non ergo existimet,” nor as Huther, = <em> namlich</em> ) <strong> let not that man</strong> (said with a certain slight expression of contempt) <strong> think</strong> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:17">Matthew 5:17</span> , μὴ νομίσητε ὅτι κ . τ . λ .) <strong> that he shall receive any thing</strong> (sc. τῶν αἰτουμένων : some things, as life, food, raiment, &amp;c., he does continually receive) <strong> from the Lord</strong> (i. e. as usually in this Epistle, from GOD. So ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:10">James 4:10</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:15">James 4:15</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:4">James 5:4</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:10-11">James 5:10-11</span> ; see at each of those places. On the other hand, ὁ κύριος , ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:7">James 5:7</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:14-15">James 5:14-15</span> , is used of Christ. Hofmann remarks that where the Father is not expressly distinguished from the Son by the context, the Godhead, in its unity, is to be understood by ὁ θεός : and the same may be said of ὁ κύριος ).</p>
