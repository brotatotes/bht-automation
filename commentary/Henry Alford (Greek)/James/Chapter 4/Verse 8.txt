<p><strong> 8</strong> .] <strong> draw near to God, and He will draw near</strong> (here better ‘ <em> will</em> :’ in speaking of the divine dealings, positive declarations are better softened: cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:23">John 16:23</span> , E. V. Not that this is always observed: cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="re+7:17">Revelation 7:17</span> , E. V.) <strong> to you</strong> . But it is only the pure in heart and hand that can approach God: therefore <strong> Purify your hands</strong> (the hands being the external organs of action, and becoming polluted by the act, as e. g. by blood in the act of murder: cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="isa+1:15">Isaiah 1:15</span> , αἱ γὰρ χεῖρες ὑμῶν αἵματος πλήρεις : <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="isa+59:3">Isaiah 59:3</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:8">1 Timothy 2:8</span> . And, for both the particulars here mentioned, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ps+23:4">Psalms 23:4</span> , ἀθῷος χερσὶ καὶ καθαρὸς τῇ καρδίᾳ ), <strong> ye sinners: and make chaste your hearts</strong> (in allusion to μοιχαλίδες above), <strong> ye double-minded</strong> (ye whose affections are divided between God and the world. The Apostle is addressing not two classes of persons, but one and the same: “Eosdem vocat <em> peccatores</em> et <em> duplices animo</em> ,” Calv.).</p>
