<p><strong> 1</strong> .] <strong> Go to now</strong> (see above, ch. Jam 4:13 ), <strong> ye rich, go weep</strong> (the imper. aor. gives the command a concentrated force, as that which ought to be done at once and without delay), <strong> howling</strong> (the part. is not merely a rhetorical reduplication of κλαύσατε , but describes the mode of the κλαῦσαι by a stronger and more graphic word, in the present, as thus habitual during the κλαῦσαι . <strong> ὀλολύζειν</strong> (reff.) is a word in the O. T. confined to the prophets, and used, as here, with reference to the near approach of God’s judgments. Thus in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="isa+13:6">Isaiah 13:6</span> , ὀλολύζετε , ἐγγὺς γὰρ ἡμέρα κυρίου ) <strong> over your miseries which are coming on</strong> (no supply of ὑμῖν (see digest) is required after ἐπερχ . These miseries are not to be thought of as the natural and determined end of all worldly riches, but are the judgments connected with the coming of the Lord: cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:8">James 5:8</span> , ἡ παρουσία τοῦ κυρίου ἤγγικεν . It may be that this prospect was as yet intimately bound up with the approaching destruction of the Jewish city and polity: for it must be remembered that they are Jews who are here addressed).</p>
