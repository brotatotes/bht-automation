<p><strong> 23.</strong> ] These words, which by the other Evangelists are spoken <em> of</em> John as the fulfilment of the prophecy, appear from this place to have been first so used <em> by himself</em> . They introduce the great closing section of the prophecy of Isaiah (ch. 40 66) so full of the rich promises and revelations of the Messiah and His kingdom.
	</p>
<p><strong> εὐθύνατε</strong> is used as compendiously expressing ἑτοιμάσατε … εὐθείας ποιεῖτε .
	</p>
<p>By implication, the Baptist, quoting this opening prophecy of himself, announces the approaching fulfilment of the whole section.</p>
