<p><strong> 16.</strong> ] <strong> θωμᾶς</strong> <strong> ,</strong> in Aramaic הּאֹמָא = δίδυμος .
	</p>
<p>The remark means, <strong> Let us also go</strong> (with our Master, implied in the <strong> καί</strong> <strong> ), that we may die with Him</strong> (not, with Lazarus, as Grot.). This is in exact accord with the character of Thomas, as shewn in ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:5">John 14:5</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:25">John 20:25</span> ; ever ready to take the dark view, but deeply attached to his Lord.</p>
