<p><strong> 32.</strong> ] The words of Mary are fewer, and her action more impassioned, than those of her sister: she was perhaps interrupted by the arrival of the Jews: cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:33">John 11:33</span> .
	</p>
<p>Kühner, Gram. § 627, Anm. 4, remarks that when the genitive of the enclitic personal pronoun is prefixed to its substantive, a slight sense of the <em> dativus commodi</em> is given: “non mihi frater mortuus esset.”</p>
