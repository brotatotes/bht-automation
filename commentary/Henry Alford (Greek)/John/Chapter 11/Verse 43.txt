<p><strong> 43.</strong> ] Some (Chrys., Lampe) suppose that the revivification had taken place before εὐχαριστῶ σοι , and these words were <em> merely a summoning forth</em> . But this is highly improbable. The comparison of ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:25">John 5:25</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:28">John 5:28</span> , which are analogically applicable, makes it clear that <strong> ἀκούσαντες</strong> ζήσονται is the physical as well as the spiritual order of things.
	</p>
<p><strong> κραυγάζειν</strong> was not His wont: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:19">Matthew 12:19</span> . This cry signified <em> that greater one</em> , which all shall hear, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:28">John 5:28</span> .</p>
