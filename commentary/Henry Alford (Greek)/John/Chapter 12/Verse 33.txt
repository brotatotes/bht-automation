<p><strong> 33.</strong> ] <strong> ποίῳ θαν</strong> <strong> .</strong> can hardly mean more than <strong> by what manner of death.</strong> Lampe (“non nude significat <em> quo genere mortis</em> , sed in sensu latiori <em> qualitatem mortis</em> , etiam <em> internam</em> involvit, adeoque ad fructus etiam hujus mortis respicit”) and Stier find in the word the whole consequences and character of His Death: but see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:32">John 18:32</span> .
	</p>
<p>John does not say that this was <em> all</em> that ὑψωθῶ meant, but that it was its first and obvious reference.</p>
