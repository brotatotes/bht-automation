<p><strong> 41.</strong> ] <strong> ὅτι εἶδ</strong> <strong> ., because he saw.</strong> “This apocalyptic vision was the occasion of that prophecy.” Meyer.
	</p>
<p><strong> αὐτοῦ</strong> <strong> ,</strong> <em> of Christ</em> . The Evangelist is giving his judgment, having ( Luk 24:45 ) had his understanding opened to understand the Scriptures, <em> that the passage in Isaiah is spoken of Christ</em> . And indeed, strictly considered, the glory which Isaiah saw <em> could only</em> be that of the Son, Who is the ἀπαύγασμα τῆς δόξης of the Father, Whom no eye hath seen.
	</p>
