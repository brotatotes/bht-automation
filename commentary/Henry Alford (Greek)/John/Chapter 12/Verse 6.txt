<p><strong> 6.</strong> ] <strong> γλωσσόκομον</strong> <strong> ,</strong> ἀγγεῖον τῶν αὐλητικῶν γλωττῶν , Phryn. (De Wette), to keep the reeds, or <em> tongues</em> , of wind instruments: thus, generally, any kind of pouch, or money-chest. See LXX, and Josephus, in reff.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐβάσταζεν</strong> ] It seems hardly possible, with St. John’s use of <strong> βαστάζειν</strong> in ch. Joh 20:15 before us, altogether to deny that the sense of <em> carrying off</em> , i.e. <em> purloining</em> , may be here intended. And we have examples in Josephus somewhat analogous: e.g. Antt. vii. 15. 3, where Hyrcanus the High Priest, wishing to give Antiochus Eusebes money to raise the siege, καὶ ἀλλαχόθεν οὐκ εὐπορῶν , ἀνοίξας ἕνα οἶκον τῶν ἐν τῷ Δαυίδου μνήματι , καὶ <strong> βαστάσας</strong> τρισχίλια τάλαντα , μέρος ἔδωκεν Ἀντιόχῳ .… See also ib. ix. 4. 5; xii. 5. 4: and Polyb. i. 48. 2. And so Origen, Theophyl., alli [161] .; contra Lücke, De Wette, Tholuck, alli [162] .
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [161] alli = some cursive mss.
