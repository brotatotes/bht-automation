<p><strong> 18.</strong> ] <strong> I say it not</strong> (viz. the ἐὰν ποιῆτε αὐτά ) of you <em> all:</em> for there is one who can never be μακάριος . Our Lord repeats His ἀλλ ʼ οὐχὶ πάντες of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:10">John 13:10</span> , and the sad recollection leads to His trouble in spirit, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:21">John 13:21</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐγὼ οἶδα</strong> ] The <strong> ἐγώ</strong> is emphatic; and the reason of its emphasis is given in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:19">John 13:19</span> .
	</p>
<p>Connexion: ‘It might be supposed that this treachery has come upon Me unawares; but it is not so: I (for my part) know whom I have selected (viz. <em> the whole twelve</em> , see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:70">John 6:70</span> ; not only the true ones (Stier), as in ch <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:16">John 15:16</span> , said when Judas was not present): but this has been done by the determinate counsel and foreknowledge of God, declared in the Scriptures.’
	</p>
<p>On the citation, see LXX. The words here are given freely, the LXX having ἐμεγάλυνεν ἐπ ʼ ἐμὲ πτερνισμόν .
	</p>
<p>This is another instance of the direct and unhesitating application of the words of the Psalms by our Lord to Himself.
