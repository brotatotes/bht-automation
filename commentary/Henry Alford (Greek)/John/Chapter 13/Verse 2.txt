<p><strong> τοῦ δ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἤδη βεβ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] The construction of the text, according to the true reading, is involved and difficult. But its <em> meaning</em> will be immediately perceived, if we render βεβληκότος εἰς τὴν καρδίαν , <strong> suggested,</strong> <strong> proposed,</strong> viz. to the mind of Judas. <strong> The devil having by this time suggested</strong> (to Judas) <strong> that Judas Iscariot the son of Simon</strong> (i.e. <strong> that he</strong> ) <strong> should betray Him.</strong> The interpretation of βεβλ . εἰς τ . κ ., “ <em> having conceived in his</em> (the devil’s) <em> mind</em> ” (Meyer), is wholly unworthy of a scholar, and simply absurd. Judas had before this covenanted with the Sanhedrim to betray Him, Mat 26:14 and [174] , which must here be meant by <em> the devil having put it into his heart:</em> the thorough self-abandonment to Satan which led to the actual deed, being designated <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:27">John 13:27</span> .
	</p>
