<p><strong> 22.</strong> ] In Matt. and Mark they express their questioning in <em> words</em> . St. Luke’s συνζητεῖν πρὸς ἑαυτούς would appear to imply the same. We seem called on here to decide a much-controverted question, <em> where in John’s narrative the institution of the Lord’s supper is to be inserted?</em> I believe certainly <em> before</em> this announcement, as in Luke: and if before it, <em> perhaps before the washing of the disciples’ feet:</em> for I see no break which would admit it between our Joh 13:1 and <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:21">John 13:21</span> .</p>
