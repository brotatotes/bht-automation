<p><strong> 29.</strong> ] The <em> first</em> supposition agrees with <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:1">John 13:1</span> , that it was πρὸ τῆς ἑορτῆς τοῦ πάσχα . Had it been the night of the passover, the next day being hallowed as a sabbath, nothing <em> could have been bought</em> . On the whole question see notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:17">Matthew 26:17</span> , and ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:28">John 18:28</span> . On the <em> second</em> supposition, see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:5">John 12:5</span> . The gift to the poor might be, to <em> help them to procure their paschal lamb</em> .</p>
