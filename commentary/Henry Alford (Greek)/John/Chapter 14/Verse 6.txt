<p><strong> 6.</strong> ] Our Lord, as Lücke (after Bengel) remarks (ii. 596), inverts the order of Thomas’s question, and in answering it practically, <em> for them</em> , speaks of ‘the Way’ first. <em> He</em> is THE WAY; not merely the Forerunner; which would imply on our part only an outward connexion with Him as His <em> followers:</em> but <em> the way</em> , in and on which we must go, having an inner union with and in Him (De Wette): see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:20">Hebrews 10:20</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἡ ἀλήθεια</strong> more than ὅτι ἀληθεύω κ . πάντως ἔσται ταῦτα , Euth. It is another side of the same idea of <em> the Way;</em> God being true, and only approached by and in truth. Christ is THE TRUTH, in Whom only ( Col 2:3 that Knowledge of Him is gained, which (ch. Joh 17:3 ) is eternal life.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἡ ζωή</strong> not merely because οὐδὲ ὁ θάνατος διαστήσει ὑμᾶς ἐμοῦ , Euthym [193] , but as being THE LIFE (see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:19">John 14:19</span> : Gal 2:20 ) of all His, in Whom only they who live can come to the living Father (ch. Joh 6:57 ).
	</p>
