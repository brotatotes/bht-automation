<p><strong> 1.</strong> ] The Vine and branches stand in a much nearer connexion than the Shepherd and the sheep, or the lord of the vineyard and the vines; and answer to the Head and members in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:23">Ephesians 5:23</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:30">Ephesians 5:30</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="col+2:19">Colossians 2:19</span> , linked together by a common organization, and informed by one and the same life.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἡ ἀληθινή</strong> <strong> ,</strong> not only, ‘by which prophecy is fulfilled:’ not only, “in which the organism and qualities of the vine are most nobly realized” (Tholuck), but as in ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:9">John 1:9</span> , <strong> true,</strong> i.e. <strong> original,</strong> <em> archetypal</em> . The material creations of God are only inferior examples of that finer spiritual life and organism in which the creature is raised up to partake of the divine nature; only <strong> ἀντίτυπυ</strong> <strong> , </strong> <strong> τῶν ἀληθινῶν</strong> <strong> ,</strong> <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:24">Hebrews 9:24</span> ; ὑποδείγματα τῶν ἐν τοῖς οὐρ ., ib. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:23">Hebrews 9:23</span> : see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:32">John 6:32</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ὁ γεωργός</strong> <strong> ,</strong> not only the tiller of the land, but the vine-planter and dresser; He who has originated the relation between the vine and branches by planting the Vine in this earth (the nature of man), and who looks for and ensures the bringing forth of fruit.</p>
