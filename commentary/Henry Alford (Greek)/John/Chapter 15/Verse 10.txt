<p><strong> 10.</strong> ] The way thus to remain is prescribed; even that way of simple obedience to <em> His</em> Will, which He followed to the Will of the Father.
	</p>
<p>On <strong> τῇ ἀγάπῃ μου</strong> <strong> ,</strong> see above: in the <em> last</em> clause, αὐτοῦ is <em> prefixed</em> , as Meyer well says, to denote the high consciousness of bliss and dignity in abiding in the Father’s love.</p>
