<p><strong> 17.</strong> ] <strong> ταῦτα</strong> refers (as almost always in John, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:11">John 15:11</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:21">John 15:21</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:1">John 16:1</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:25">John 16:25</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:33">John 16:33</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:1">John 17:1</span> ; Joh 18:1 alli [216] .) back to what has gone before. ‘The object of my enjoining these things on you is (for all since Joh 15:12 has been an expansion of <strong> καθὼς ἠγ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ὑμ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ) that ye love one another’ (see 1Jn 4:11 ). Then from the indefiniteness of this word ἀλλήλους our Lord takes occasion to forewarn them that however wide their love to one another, they cannot bring all within this category; there will be ὁ κόσμος , which will hate them.
