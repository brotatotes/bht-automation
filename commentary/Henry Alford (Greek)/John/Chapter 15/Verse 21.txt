<p><strong> 21.</strong> ] <strong> ἀλλά</strong> nay, so far is this from being so, that it is on this very account, <em> because</em> ye belong to Me, that they will thus treat you.
	</p>
<p><strong> ταῦτα πάντα</strong> all that is implied in μισεῖν and διώκειν .
	</p>
<p><strong> τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> π</strong> <strong> .,</strong> ‘these things, all of them:’ not π . τ ., ‘all, every one of, these things:’ the former order gives the ταῦτα in the gross, ‘all this treatment,’ the latter in the particular, so that not one is excepted from the category.
	</p>
<p>It was on account of bearing the Name of Christ that the Christians were subjected to persecution in the early ages, and that they are even now hated by those who know Him not: but this is to them comfort and joy, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:41">Acts 5:41</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:10">2 Corinthians 12:10</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:17">Galatians 6:17</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:14">1 Peter 4:14</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> οὐκ οἴδασιν</strong> <strong> ,</strong> not, ‘ <em> They know Him not as having sent Me</em> ’ but <strong> they know not</strong> (absolutely) <strong> Him who has sent Me.</strong> Ignorance of God (not desiring the knowledge of His ways) is the great cause of hostility to Christ and His servants.</p>
