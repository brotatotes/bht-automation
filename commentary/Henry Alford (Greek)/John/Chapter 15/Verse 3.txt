<p><strong> 3. </strong> <strong> καθαροί</strong> ] See ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:10">John 13:10</span> . In <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:26">Ephesians 5:26</span> , we have both the washing διὰ τὸν λόγον , and the word ( ἐν ῥήματι ), united. The <em> word</em> of Christ dwelling in them by Faith (see Joh 15:7 ) is the purifying principle (ch. Joh 17:17 ). But the καθαροί here is not = κεκαθαρμένοι , <em> pruned</em> , in the sense of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:2">John 15:2</span> . The ἤδη limits it to their present capacities and standing. There was more pruning at hand, when the sap should begin to flow, when the Spirit should be shed abroad; and this future handling of the γεωργός is indicated by μείνατε ἐν ἐμοί .…</p>
