<p><strong> 16.</strong> ] The mode of expression is (purposely) enigmatical; the <strong> θεωρεῖτε</strong> and <strong> ὄψεσθε</strong> not being co-ordinate; the first referring merely to physical, the second also to spiritual sight. So before, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:19">John 14:19</span> , where see note.
	</p>
<p>The ὄψεσθε <em> began to be fulfilled</em> at the Resurrection; then received its <em> main fulfilment</em> at the day of Pentecost; and shall have its <em> final completion</em> at the great return of the Lord hereafter. Remember again, that in all these prophecies we have a perspective of continually unfolding fulfilments presented to us: see note on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:3">John 14:3</span> .</p>
