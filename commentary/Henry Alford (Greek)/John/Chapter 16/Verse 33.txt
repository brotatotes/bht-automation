<p><strong> ἔχετε</strong> <strong> ,</strong> of their normal state in the world.
	</p>
<p>This <strong> θλῖψις</strong> is not only persecution <em> from the world</em> , but trouble, inward distress, <em> while we are in the world</em> , ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:11">John 17:11</span> ; a comforting sign that we are not <em> of</em> the world (see Stier, v. 373, edn. 2).
	</p>
<p>And this latter idea is implied <em> between</em> the two clauses: ‘Be of good cheer; for ye belong not to the world, but to Me, who have (proleptically again, by that which is now at hand) overcome the world, so that it shall have no power over you, externally by persecution, or internally by temptations or discouragements.’ See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:4-5">1 John 5:4-5</span> .</p>
