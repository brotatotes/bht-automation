<p><strong> 12. </strong> <strong> ἐφύλαξα</strong> ] See ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:28-30">John 10:28-30</span> . The aor. should be adhered to again: <strong> I</strong> <strong> kept them.</strong> The Lord here, as Cyril remarks, compares <em> His</em> keeping of His own, to that by <em> the Father</em> , in a way only accountable by both Persons being of equal Power and Dignity.
	</p>
<p><strong> οὐδεὶς</strong> <strong> … </strong> <strong> εἰ μὴ</strong> <strong> …</strong> ] So that Judas was of the number οὓς δέδωκάς μοι of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:9">John 17:9</span> , shewing us (1) the sense in which those words must be understood (see above); and (2) that of such persons it is true that there is for them no ‘gratia irresistibilis,’ no ‘keeping in God’s Name’ independently of their ‘keeping God’s word,’ <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:6">John 17:6</span> , which Judas did not do.
	</p>
<p><strong> ὁ υἱ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἀπ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] See ref. 2 Thess. As the other disciples by true τήρησις of the divine ῥήματα given to them, rose from being natural men to be the children of God, so Judas, through want of the same, sunk from the state of the natural man to that of the lost the children of the devil (Olsh. nearly).
	</p>
<p>Remark, it is not οὐδένα … <strong> ἀπώλεσα</strong> <strong> ,</strong> εἰ μὴ τὸν υἱὸν τῆς ἀπ .: Christ did not lost him (compare ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:9">John 18:9</span> , where there is no exception), but <em> he lost himself</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἡ γραφή</strong> in which this was indicated, viz. the passages alleged by Peter, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:20">Acts 1:20</span> ; see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:18">John 13:18</span> . Beware again of any evasion of the full telic sense of ἵνα .</p>
