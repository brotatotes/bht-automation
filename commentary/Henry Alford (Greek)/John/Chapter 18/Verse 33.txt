<p>Pilate summoned Jesus in, who had been as yet outside with the Jews. This was the formal <em> reception</em> of the case before him; as the Roman soldiers must now have formally taken charge of Jesus, as servants of the Roman authorities: having previously, when granted by Pilate to the Chief Priests, acted as <em> their</em> police.
	</p>
<p>The judgments of the Romans were always public and sub dio, see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:13">John 19:13</span> ; but the enquiries and examinations might be private. In this case Pilate appears to have wished to obtain an account from Jesus apart from the clamours of the chief priests and the mob.</p>
