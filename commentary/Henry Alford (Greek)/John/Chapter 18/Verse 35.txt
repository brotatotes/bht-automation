<p><strong> 35.</strong> ] Pilate at once repudiates the idea of <em> his</em> having any share in Jewish expectations, or taking any personal interest in Jewish matters: all his information he has derived from the public accusation of the people and chief priests. Then in <strong> τί ἐπ</strong> <strong> .</strong> is implied, ‘There is no definiteness in their charge: let me have thine own account, thy ex-parte statement, that I may at least know <em> something</em> definite of the case.’</p>
