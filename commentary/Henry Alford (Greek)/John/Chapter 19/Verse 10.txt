<p><strong> 10.</strong> ] As in ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:35">John 18:35</span> , Pilate at once recoils from his better conscience into the state-pride of office. “Objurgans increpatio timori præcedenti plane contraria.” Lampe. This very boast was a self-conviction of injustice. No just judge has any such power as this, to punish or to loose (see 2Co 13:8 ); but only patiently to enquire and give sentence according to the truth.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐμοί</strong> <strong> ,</strong> emphatic: it perhaps being implied, ‘Thou hast, I know, refused to reply to others before.’
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀπολῦσαι</strong> <strong> ,</strong> <em> first</em> seems most natural, as appealing most to the prisoner: <strong> σταυρῶσαι</strong> follows, as the alternative in case the other is rejected.</p>
