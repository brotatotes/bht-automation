<p><strong> 30.</strong> ] <strong> τετέλεσται</strong> expresses the fulfilling of that appointed course of humiliation, obedience, and suffering, which the Lord Jesus had undertaken. (“Verbum τελέω convenit rebus, τελειόω scripturæ sacræ,” Bengel.) That was now over, the redemption of man accomplished, and from this time “the joy that was set before Him” begins. It is beyond the purpose of a note to bring out the many meanings of this most important and glorious word. There is an admirable sermon on it by Schleiermacher (vol. ii. serm. 10); and Stier’s Comment, vi. 473 ff., should be read.
	</p>
<p><strong> κλίνας τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> κεφαλήν</strong> ] We have the minuteness of an eye-witness, on whom every particular of this solemn moment made an indelible impression.
	</p>
<p><strong> παρέδωκεν τὸ πνεῦμα</strong> viz. in the words given by Luke, πάτερ , εἰς χεῖράς σου παρατίθεμαι τὸ πνεῦμα , which was also the φωνὴ μεγάλη of Matt. and Mark. This παραδιδόναι was strictly a voluntary and determinate act no <em> coming on</em> of death, which had no power over Him, see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:18">John 10:18</span> , and note on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:46">Luke 23:46</span> .</p>
