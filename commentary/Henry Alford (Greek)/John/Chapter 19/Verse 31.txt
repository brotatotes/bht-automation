<p><strong> 31.</strong> ] On the Jewish custom, see note, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:57">Matthew 27:57</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἦ γὰρ μεγ</strong> <strong> .…,</strong> being as it was (see note on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:28">John 18:28</span> , and Mat 26:17 ) <em> a double sabbath:</em> the coincidence of the first day of unleavened bread ( Exo 12:16 ) with an ordinary sabbath.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἵνα κατεαγ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] The <em> crurifragium</em> was sometimes appended to the punishment of crucifixion, see Friedlieb, p. 164, but does not appear to have been inflicted for the purpose of causing death, which indeed it would not do. Friedlieb supposes that the term involved in it the ‘ <em> coup de grâce</em> ,’ which was given to all executed criminals, and that the piercing with the spear was this death-blow, and was also inflicted on the thieves.</p>
