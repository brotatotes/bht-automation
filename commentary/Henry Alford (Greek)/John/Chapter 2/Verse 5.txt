<p><strong> 5.</strong> ] There certainly seems beneath this narrative to lie <em> some incident which is not told us</em> . For not only is Mary <em> not repelled</em> by the answer just given, but she is <em> convinced that the miracle will be wrought</em> , and she is not without an anticipation of the <em> method of working it:</em> for how should He require the aid of the servants, except the miracle were to take place according to the form here related? I believe we shall find, when all things are opened to us, that there had been a previous hint given her, where or how I would not presume to say, by our Lord, of His intention and the manner of performing it, and that her fault was, <em> the too rash hastening on of what had been His fixed purpose</em> .</p>
