<p><strong> 13.</strong> ] Here again the finest psychological truth underlies the narrative. The other women (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+16:5">Mark 16:5</span> ; Luk 24:5 ) <em> were afraid</em> at the vision; but now Mary, having but one thought or desire, to recover the lost Body of her Lord ( τὸν κύριόν <strong> μου</strong> ), <em> feels no fear</em> .
	</p>
<p>The angels doubtless are proceeding further to assure her as they did the women before: but this is broken off by the appearance of the Lord Himself, or perhaps by Mary’s turning away.</p>
