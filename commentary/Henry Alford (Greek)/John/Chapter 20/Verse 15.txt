<p>It is idle to enquire <em> why</em> she thought Him to be the gardener (see specimens of such speculations in Lücke and Stier in loc.): but I may once for all observe that we must believe the clothing of His risen Body to have been <em> that which He pleased to assume;</em> not earthly clothing, but perhaps some semblance of it. Certainly, in this case, He <em> was clothed;</em> or she must at once have recognized Him. But see on στραφεῖσα below.
