<p><strong> 21.</strong> ] ‘Peace be unto you’ is solemnly repeated, as the introduction of the sending which follows. The ministers and disciples of the Lord are messengers of <em> peace</em> . This view is more natural than that of Euthym [255] : ὑπὸ πολλῆς χαρᾶς ὡς εἰκὸς θορυβοῦντας καταστέλλει , ἵνα προσέχωσιν οἷς μέλλει ἐρεῖν .
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [255] Euthymius Zigabenus, 1116
	</p>
<p><strong> καθώς</strong> ] He confirms and grounds their Apostleship on the present glorification of Himself, whose Apostleship ( Heb 3:1 ) on earth was now ended, but was to be continued by this sending forth of them. This commission was not now first given them, but now first fully assured to them: and their sending forth by Him their glorified Head, was to be, in character and process, like that of Himself by the Father.</p>
