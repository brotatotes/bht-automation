<p><strong> 31.</strong> ] The mere <em> miracle-faith</em> , so often reproved by our Lord, is not that intended here. This is faith in <em> Himself</em> , as the Christ the Son of God: and the Evangelist means, that enough is related in this book to be a ground for such a faith, by shewing us His glory manifested forth (see ch. Joh 2:11 ).
	</p>
<p><strong> πιοτ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ζωὴν ἔχ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] Thus he closes almost in the words of his prologue, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:4">John 1:4</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:12">John 1:12</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐν τῷ ὀν</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> αὐτ</strong> <strong> .</strong> (see reff. Acts, 1 Cor.) is the whole standing of the faithful man in Christ, by which and in which he has life eternal.</p>
