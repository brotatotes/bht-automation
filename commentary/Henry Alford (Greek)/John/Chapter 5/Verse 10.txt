<p><strong> 10.</strong> ] <strong> οἱ Ἰουδαῖοι</strong> <strong> ,</strong> never the <em> multitude</em> , but always those in authority of some kind, whom John ever puts forward as the representatives of the whole people in their rejection of the Lord.
	</p>
<p><strong> οὐκ ἔξεστιν</strong> ] The bearing of burdens on the Sabbath was forbidden not only by the glosses of the Pharisees, but <em> by the law itself</em> . See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ne+13:15-19">Nehemiah 13:15-19</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ex+31:13-17">Exodus 31:13-17</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jer+17:21-22">Jeremiah 17:21-22</span> . And our Lord does not, as in another case ( Luk 13:15-16 ), appeal here to the reasonableness of the deed being done on the Sabbath, <em> salvo sabbato</em> , but takes altogether loftier ground, as being One greater than the Sabbath. The whole kernel of this incident and discourse is <em> not, that it is lawful to do works of mercy on the Sabbath:</em> but <em> that the Son of God</em> (here) <em> is Lord of the Sabbath</em> .</p>
