<p><strong> 40.</strong> ] I would connect these words with the former, and regard them as describing the inconsistency of those who think that they ζωὴν ἔχειν in the Scriptures, and yet will not come to Him of whom they testify, ἵνα ζωὴν ἔχωσιν . So that <strong> καί</strong> will be spoken in a fine irony, <strong> And ye will not come to Me!
	</strong></p>
<p>Observe, this command to the Jews to <em> search</em> their Scriptures, applies <em> à fortiori</em> to Christians; who are yet, like them, in danger of idolizing a mere written book, believing that <em> in the Bible</em> they have eternal life, and missing the personal knowledge of Him of whom the Scriptures testify.
	</p>
<p>The <strong> οὐ θέλετε</strong> here sets forth strikingly <em> the freedom of the will</em> , on which the unbeliever’s condemnation rests: see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:19">John 3:19</span> .</p>
