<p><strong> 15.</strong> ] After such a recognition, nothing was wanting but that the multitudes who were journeying to the Passover should take Jesus with them and proclaim Him king of the Jews in the holy City itself.
	</p>
<p>The other three Evangelists, while they do not give any intimation of this reason of our Lord’s withdrawal, relate the <em> fact</em> , and Luke preserves in the very next verse <em> a trace of its motive</em> , by the question ‘ <em> Whom do the people say that I am?</em> ’ and the answer, expressing the very confession of the people here.</p>
