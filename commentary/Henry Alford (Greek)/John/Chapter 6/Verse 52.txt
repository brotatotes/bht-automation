<p><strong> 52.</strong> ] The inference conveyed in <strong> φαγεῖν</strong> <strong> ,</strong> which <em> first comes from the Jews themselves</em> , is yet a right one. If He is the Bread, and that Bread is His Flesh, we must <em> eat His Flesh</em> , though not in the sense here meant by them. They contended against one another, probably some having more insight into the possibility of a spiritual meaning than others.</p>
