<p><strong> 9.</strong> ] <strong> κριθίνους</strong> <strong> ,</strong> the usual barley bread of the lower orders.
	</p>
<p><strong> ὀψάρια</strong> = ἰχθύδια , Suidas, but of later Greek usage: at first used to signify any thing subsidiary to bread as a relish, such as meat of all kinds, and condiments. Later however, from fish being, in the deeply coast-indented country of Greece, the most common animal food, it came to be applied to that alone or principally (see art. Opsonium in the Dictionary of Gr. and Rom. Antiquities).</p>
