<p><strong> 10.</strong> ] <strong> οὐ φαν</strong> <strong> .</strong> , i.e. not in the usual caravan-company, nor probably by the usual way. Whether the Twelve were with Him, we have no means of judging: probably so, for they appear ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:2">John 9:2</span> ; and after their becoming once attached to the Person of our Lord as Apostles, we find no trace of his having been for any long time separated from them, except during their mission <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:0">Matthew 10:0</span> , which was long ago accomplished.</p>
