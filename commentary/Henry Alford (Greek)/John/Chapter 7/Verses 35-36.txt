<p>The <strong> διασπ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> Ἑλλ</strong> <strong> .</strong> must not be interpreted ‘the Hellenistic Jews,’ for the Ἕλληνες are always distinguished from the Jews; and this would convey hardly any meaning. The sense of διασπορά is, see reff. James, 1 Pet., ‘the country where Jews lay scattered,’ as qualified by the succeeding genitive, where one occurs, as here. So here <strong> ἡ δ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> Ἑλ</strong> <strong> .</strong> means ‘ <em> the dispersed in the Gentile world;</em> ’ and their intent is, to convey contempt and mockery.
