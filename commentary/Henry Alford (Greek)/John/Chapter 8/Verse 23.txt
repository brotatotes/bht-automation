<p><strong> 23.</strong> ] ‘Ye cannot come where I am going, because we both shall return thither whence we came: I to the Father from Whom ( <strong> ἐκ τῶν ἄνω</strong> ) I came: ye to the earth and under the earth (for that more awful meaning surely is not excluded) whence ye came’ ( <strong> ἐκ τῶν κάτω</strong> ).
	</p>
<p>Then <strong> ὁ κόσμος οὗτος</strong> of course does not <em> only</em> imply ‘ <em> this present state of things</em> ,’ but involves the deeper meaning, of the <em> origin</em> of that state of things (see Joh 8:44 ) and its <em> end</em> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:24">John 8:24</span> .</p>
