<p><strong> 34.</strong> ] <strong> ποιῶν τὴν ἁμαρτ</strong> <strong> .,</strong> not = ἁμαρτάνων , for that <em> all do;</em> but = ἐργαζόμενος τὴν ἀνομίαν , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:23">Matthew 7:23</span> . It implies <strong> living in the practice of sin</strong> , <strong> doing sin</strong> , as a habit: see reff. The mere moral sentiment of which this is the spiritual expression, was common among the Greek and Roman philosophers. See Wetstein: also <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:12">Romans 6:12</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:19">2 Peter 2:19</span> .</p>
