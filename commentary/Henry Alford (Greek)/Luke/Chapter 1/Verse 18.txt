<p><strong> 18.</strong> ] The birth of John, involving <em> human generation</em> , but <em> prophetically announced</em> , and <em> supernatural</em> , answers to the birth of Isaac in the O.T.
	</p>
<p>But Abraham’s faith was a strong contrast to the unbelief of Zacharias: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:19">Romans 4:19</span> . De Wette, without noticing the above remark (which is Olshausen’s), says, “the same doubt, which Abraham also entertained in a similar case;” so that we have here, as often elsewhere, in the interpretation of Scripture (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ge+15:6">Genesis 15:6</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ge+15:8">Genesis 15:8</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ge+17:17">Genesis 17:17</span> ; Gen 18:12 ), De Wette versus Paul (Rom. as above): the fact being, that the case Gen 15:8 was not similar.
	</p>
<p><strong> πρεσβύτης</strong> ] The <em> Levites</em> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="nu+4:3">Numbers 4:3</span> ; Num 8:24-25 ) became superannuated at the age of <em> fifty:</em> but it appears, by extracts from the Rabbinical writings given by Lightfoot, that this was not the case with the priests.</p>
