<p><strong> 32. </strong> <strong> Δαυεὶδ τοῦ π</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> αὐτ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] This announcement makes it almost certain (but see note above) that <em> Mary also</em> was of the house of David. No astonishment is expressed by her at this part of the statement, and yet, from the nature of her question, it is clear that <em> she did not explain it by supposing Joseph to be the destined father of her child</em> .
