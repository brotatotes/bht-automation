<p><strong> 66.</strong> ] <strong> λέγοντες</strong> carries a slightly logical force with it; almost = ‘ <em> for they said</em> .’
	</p>
<p><strong> ἄρα</strong> refers back to the circumstances which have happened <strong> What then shall,</strong> &amp;c.: see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:25">Luke 8:25</span> : <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:18">Acts 12:18</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> καὶ γὰρ χεὶρ κ</strong> <strong> .…,</strong> a remark inserted by the Evangelist himself, not a further saying of the speakers in the verse before, as Kuinoel and others maintain. The γάρ refers back to the question just asked, q. d., ‘And they might well enquire thus, for’ &amp;c.</p>
