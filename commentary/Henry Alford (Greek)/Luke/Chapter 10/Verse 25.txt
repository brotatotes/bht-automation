<p><strong> νομικός</strong> <strong> ,</strong> a kind of scribe, = νομοδιδάσκαλος , ch. Luk 5:17 whose especial office it was to teach the law, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:13">Titus 3:13</span> ; = εἷς τῶν γραμματέων , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:28">Mark 12:28</span> .
	</p>
<p>There is no reason to suppose that the lawyer had any hostile intention towards Jesus, rather perhaps a self-righteous spirit (see Luk 10:29 ), which wanted to see what this Teacher could inform <em> him, who knew so much already</em> . Thus it was a <em> tempting</em> or <em> trying</em> of Jesus, though not <em> to entangle</em> Him: for whatever had been the answer, this could hardly have followed.
	</p>
<p><strong> τί ποιήσας</strong> ] He doubtless expects to hear of <em> some great deed;</em> but our Lord refers him back to the Law of which he was a teacher.</p>
