<p><strong> 14.</strong> ] <strong> ἄνθρ</strong> <strong> .,</strong> a word of solemn reproof: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:1">Romans 2:1</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:20">Romans 9:20</span> . The <strong> ἄνθρ</strong> <strong> .</strong> also forms a definite subject for <strong> ὑμᾶς</strong> to refer to, … ‘ <em> men</em> ,’ i.e. mankind in general. This question is expressed in almost the very words of the Egyptian rejecting the arbitration of Moses, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ex+2:14">Exodus 2:14</span> ; and may shew us the essential difference of the two offices of Moses and Christ.</p>
