<p><strong> τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἀρχ</strong> <strong> .</strong> [ <strong> τ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] <strong> Φ</strong> <strong> ., of the chief men of the Pharisees;</strong> or, if the <strong> τῶν</strong> be omitted, <strong> of the Pharisees who were rulers.</strong> Though the Pharisees had no official rulers as such, they had men to whom they looked up, as Hillel, Schammai, Gamaliel, &amp;c. <p><strong> φ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἄρτ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] The Jews used to give entertainments on the Sabbath, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ne+8:9-12">Nehemiah 8:9-12</span> ; Tob 2:1 . The practice latterly became an abuse, ‘Hodiernus dies sabbati est: hunc in præsenti tempore otio quodam corporaliter languido et fluxo et luxurioso celebrant Judæi.’ Aug [94] in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ps+91:1">Psalms 91:1</span> , Enarr. § 2, vol. iv. Again, ‘observa diem Sabbati, non Judaicis deliciis …’ in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ps+32:2">Psalms 32:2</span> , Enarr. ii. § 6.
