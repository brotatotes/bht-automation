<p><strong> 17. </strong> <strong> εἰς ἑαυτὸν ἐλθών</strong> ] Similar expressions seem to occur in the Heb. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="de+30:1">Deuteronomy 30:1</span> (where Sy [97] . renders “Redi in temetipsum;” but Gesen. understands an accus. “si revocabis ea”); <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1ki+8:47">1 Kings 8:47</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="isa+46:8">Isaiah 46:8</span> . Before this, he was <em> beside himself</em> . The most dreadful torment of the lost, in fact that which constitutes their state of torment, will be this εἰς ἑαυτὸν ἐλθεῖν , when too late for repentance.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [97] The Peschito (or simple) Syriac version. Supposed to have been made as early as the <em> second century</em> . The text as edited is in a most unsatisfactory state.
	</p>
