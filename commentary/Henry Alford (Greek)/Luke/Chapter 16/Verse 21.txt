<p><strong> 21.</strong> ] It would seem that he <em> did</em> obtain this wish, and that, as in ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:16">Luke 15:16</span> , the <strong> ἐπιθ</strong> <strong> .</strong> must mean, he <strong> looked for</strong> it, <strong> willingly took</strong> it.
	</p>
<p>The <strong> ἀλλὰ καί</strong> seems also to imply, that he <em> got the crumbs:</em> this verse, relating the two points of contrast to the rich man: his only food, the crumbs, with which he longed to fill his belly, but could not: his only clothing, nakedness and sores, and instead of the boon companions of the rich man, none to pity him but the dogs, who <strong> ἐπέλειχον</strong> certainly in pity, not ‘ <em> dolorem exasperantes</em> ’ (Bengel) his sores, as they do their own. Such was the state of the two in this world.</p>
