<p><strong> 6.</strong> ] See on Matt. ( Luk 17:20 ) <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:21">Luke 21:21</span> . On this occasion some particular tree of the sort was close at hand, and furnished the instance, just as the Mount of Transfiguration in the former of those passages, and the Mount of Olives in the latter.
	</p>
<p><strong> συκάμινος</strong> is <em> the mulberry-tree;</em> not very common in Palestine, but still found there. It must not be confounded with συκομορέα , ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:4">Luke 19:4</span> , which is the Egyptian fig. See note there.
	</p>
<p>Notice the different tenses with <strong> ἄν</strong> <strong> :</strong> ἐλέγετε ἄν , <em> ye would say:</em> ὑπήκουσεν ἄν , <em> it would</em> (even while you were speaking) <em> have obeyed</em> .
	</p>
