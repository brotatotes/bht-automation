<p><strong> 25 30.</strong> ] The events which must precede the coming: and (1) <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:25">Luke 17:25</span> , as regards <em> the Lord Himself</em> , His sufferings and rejection, primarily by this generation, but in implication, by the world; and (2) <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:26-30">Luke 17:26-30</span> , which unfold this implication as regards the whole world, which shall be in its state of carelessness and sensuality at that time: see notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:37-39">Matthew 24:37-39</span> . The example of <em> the days of Lot</em> is added here, and thereby the sanction of the Lord of Truth given to another part of the sacred record, on which modern scepticism has laid its unhallowed hands.</p>
