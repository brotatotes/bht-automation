<p><strong> 2.</strong> ] <strong> Ζακχαῖος</strong> = זַכַּי , ‘pure,’ <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ezr+2:9">Ezra 2:9</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ne+7:14">Nehemiah 7:14</span> ; also found in the Rabbinical writings, see Lightfoot. He was not a Gentile, as Tertullian supposed, (contr. Marc. iv. 37, vol. ii. p. 451,) but a Jew, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:9">Luke 19:9</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀρχιτ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] Probably an administrator of the revenue derived from <em> balsam</em> , which was produced in abundance in the neighbourhood.</p>
