<p><strong> 20.</strong> ] <strong> σουδάριον</strong> is <em> sudarium</em> , from ‘sudor,’ one of those Latin words which entered, with Roman habits, into the language of the East. Buxtorf, Lex. Talm. p. 1442, gives an account of various usages of the word in the Targums. Schöttg., in loc., shews by Rabbinical citations that the Jews used the σουδάριον for wrapping and keeping their money in.
	</p>
<p>Luk 19:25 is parenthetical, spoken by <em> the standers-by in the parable</em> , in surprise at such a decision: then in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:26">Luke 19:26</span> <strong> ,</strong> the King answers them.</p>
