<p><strong> συκομορ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] The Egyptian fig, a tree (Pliny xiii. 14: Dioscor. i. 182, cited by Winer) like the mulberry in appearance, size, and foliage, but belonging generically to the fig-trees. It grows to a great size and height: see Winer, Realwörterbuch, under Maulbeer-feigenbaum. See also on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:6">Luke 17:6</span> . Notice the changes of subject here, ἀνέβη ( Ζακχ .) … ἵνα ἴδῃ αὐτόν , ὅτι ἐκείνης ἤμελλεν ( ὁ Ἰησ .) διέρ .… κ . σπεύσας ( Ζακχ .). See ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:15">Luke 15:15</span> : and a curious and characteristic note in Wordsw. here.</p>
