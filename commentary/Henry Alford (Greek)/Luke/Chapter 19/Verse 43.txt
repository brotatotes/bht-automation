<p><strong> 43.</strong> ] <strong> ὅτι</strong> declares, not ‘ <em> the things hidden from thine eyes</em> ,’ so that it should be rendered, ‘ <em> namely, that the days shall come</em> ,’ &amp;c.: but the awful reason which there was for the fervent wish just expressed <strong> for,</strong> or <strong> because.
	</strong></p>
<p><strong> χάρακα</strong> <strong> ,</strong> a mound with palisades. The account of its being built is in Jos. B. J. ver. 6. 2. When the Jews destroyed this, Titus built a wall round them (ib. 12. 2), see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="isa+29:2-4">Isaiah 29:2-4</span> , to which our Lord here tacitly refers.</p>
