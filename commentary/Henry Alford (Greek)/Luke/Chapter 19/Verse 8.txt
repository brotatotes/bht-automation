<p><strong> 8.</strong> ] This need not have taken place <em> in the morning;</em> much more probably it was immediately on our Lord’s entrance into the house, while the multitude were yet murmuring in the court, and in their presence. Our Lord’s answer, <strong> σήμερον</strong> <strong> … </strong> <strong> τῷ οἴκῳ τούτῳ</strong> <strong> ,</strong> looks as if He were just entering the house, not just leaving it; and the <strong> σήμ</strong> <strong> .</strong> must be the same with that in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:5">Luke 19:5</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> σταθείς</strong> has something formal and pre-determined about it: he stood forward, with some effort and resolve: see on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:11">Luke 18:11</span> ff.
	</p>
