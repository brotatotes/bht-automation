<p><strong> 25.</strong> ] It appears that this Symeon might have been Symeon the son of Hillel, and father of Gamaliel, mentioned in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:34">Acts 5:34</span> ff. But we have no means of ascertaining this. It is no objection to it that he is here merely ἄνθρωπος , seeing that Gamaliel himself is only φαρισαῖός τις in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:34">Acts 5:34</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> παράκλ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:20">Acts 28:20</span> . It was a common form of adjuration among the Jews, ‘Ita videam consolationem, si’ &amp;c., referring to <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="isa+40:1">Isaiah 40:1</span> .
	</p>
<p>On the general expectation of deliverance at this time, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:1">Matthew 2:1</span> ff.</p>
