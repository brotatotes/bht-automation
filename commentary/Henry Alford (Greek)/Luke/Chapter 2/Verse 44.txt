<p><strong> 44.</strong> ] <strong> συνοδ</strong> <strong> .,</strong> the company forming the <em> caravan</em> , or band of travellers; all who came from the same district travelling together for security and company.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἦλθ</strong> <strong> .… </strong> <strong> ἀνεζ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] The interpretation that ‘ <em> they went a day’s journey, seeking him</em> ,’ is simply absurd: for they would have turned back sooner: a few minutes might have sufficed for the search. It was <em> not till they laid up for the night</em> that they missed him, as at that time ( φέρεις μητέρι παῖδα ) they would naturally expect his return to their own tent. Olshausen remarks, that being accustomed to his thoughtfulness and obedience, they were free from anxiety, till they discovered He really was not in the company.</p>
