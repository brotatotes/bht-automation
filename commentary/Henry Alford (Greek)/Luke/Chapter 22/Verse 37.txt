<p><strong> 37.</strong> ] The connexion is this: ‘Your situation among men will be one of neglect and even of danger; for I myself (see Mat 10:24-25 ) am about to be reckoned among transgressors.’
	</p>
<p>By the very form of the expression it is evident, that the sword alluded to could have no reference to <em> that night’s danger</em> , or the <em> defending Him from it</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> τὸ περὶ ἐμ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τέλος ἔχει</strong> ] The prophecy cited closes the section of Isaiah, which eminently predicts the Lord’s sufferings (ch. Isa 52:13 to Isa 53:12 ).
	</p>
<p><strong> τὸ περὶ ἐμοῦ</strong> supply γεγραμμένον , or perhaps more generally, ‘determined in the counsel of God.’
	</p>
<p><strong> τέλος ἔχει</strong> does not merely mean ‘ <em> must be fulfilled</em> ,’ which would be an assertion without any special reference here but (as E. V.) <strong> have an end;</strong> are coming to the <em> completion</em> of their accomplishment. So <strong> τετέλεσται</strong> <strong> ,</strong> <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:30">John 19:30</span> .</p>
