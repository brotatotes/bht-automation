<p><strong> 66. </strong> <strong> ὡς ἐγ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἡμ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] Some trace of a meeting of the Sanhedrim after daylight I believe our Evangelist to have found, see Mat 27:1 and to have therefore related as then happening, the following account of what really took place at the former meeting.
	</p>
<p><strong> λέγοντες</strong> but first took place the μαρτυρία referred to in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:71">Luke 22:71</span> ; and the person who said this was the high-priest, and with an adjuration, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:63">Matthew 27:63</span> . The ordinary rendering is the most natural and correct: <strong> If thou art</strong> (not if thou <em> be</em> ) <strong> the Christ, tell us.</strong> The others, ‘ <em> Tell us whether thou be the Christ;</em> ’ and, ‘ <em> Art thou the Christ? tell us</em> ’ (see the question in Luk 22:49 ), are forced and unusual.</p>
