<p><strong> 66 71.</strong> ] HEARING BEFORE THE COUNCIL. (Probably) <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:1">Matthew 27:1</span> .<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:1">Mark 14:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:1">Mark 14:1</span> . It seems probable that Luke here gives us an account of a <em> second and formal judgment held in the morning</em> . The similarity of the things said at the two hearings may be accounted for by remembering that they were both more or less formal processes in legal courts, one the precognition, the other, the decision, at which the things said before would be likely to be nearly repeated.</p>
