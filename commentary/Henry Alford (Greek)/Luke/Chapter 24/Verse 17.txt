<p><strong> 17.</strong> ] He had apparently been walking with them some little time before this was said.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀντιβάλλειν λόγους</strong> implies to dispute with some earnestness: but there is no <em> blame</em> implied in the words. Possibly, though both were sad, they may have taken <em> different views:</em> and in the answer of Cleopas we have that of the one who was most disposed to abandon all hope.</p>
