<p><strong> 33.</strong> ] ‘Jam non timent iter nocturnum, quod antea dissuaserant ignoto comiti.’ Bengel. The <em> whole eleven</em> were not there Thomas was not present, if at least the appearance which follows be the same as that in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:19">John 20:19</span> , which there seems no reason to doubt. Some have derived an argument from this incompleteness in their number, for the second of the travellers being also an Apostle: see above on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:13">Luke 24:13</span> .
	</p>
<p>Who these οἱ σὺν αὐτοῖς are, we learn from <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:14">Acts 1:14</span> .</p>
