<p><strong> 4.</strong> ] <strong> ἐπέστ</strong> <strong> .</strong> does not determine the <em> position</em> of the angels. It is merely <strong> came upon them</strong> under ordinary circumstances; <strong> appeared to them,</strong> in a supernatural connexion: see reff. On the <em> two</em> angels here, see note on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+16:5">Mark 16:5</span> ; to which I will just add, that the Harmonistic view, as represented by Greswell [Diss. vi., vol. 3], strangely enough puts together the angel in <strong> Matthew,</strong> and the angel in Mark, and makes the <em> two</em> angels in Luke: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:10">Acts 1:10</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἄνδρες</strong> to all appearance; the Evangelist does not mean that they <em> were</em> such, as clearly appears from what follows.</p>
