<p><strong> 51.</strong> ] <strong> διέστη</strong> not, ‘ <em> He went a little distance from them previous to His ascension</em> ,’ as Meyer would interpret it; but the two verbs belong to one and the same incident, <strong> He was parted from them and borne up into heaven.</strong> We need not understand, ‘ <em> by an angel</em> ,’ or ‘ <em> by a cloud</em> ,’ nor need <strong> ἀνεφ</strong> <strong> .</strong> be middle; the <em> absolute passive</em> is best.
	</p>
<p>The tense is <em> imperfect</em> , signifying the <em> continuance of the going up</em> during the <strong> προσκυν</strong> <strong> .</strong> of the next verse.
	</p>
<p>The more particular account of the Ascension is given <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:9-12">Acts 1:9-12</span> , where see notes. That account is in perfect accordance with this, but supplementary to it.</p>
