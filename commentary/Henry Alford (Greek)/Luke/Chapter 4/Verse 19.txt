<p><strong> 19. </strong> <strong> ἐνιαυτ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> κυρ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> δεκ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="le+25:8-17">Leviticus 25:8-17</span> , where in Luk 4:10 we find that liberty was proclaimed to all in the land in the year of jubilee (in the prophecy, <strong> κηρύξαι</strong> = καλέσαι LXX). No countenance is given by this expression to the extraordinary inference from it of some of the Fathers (Clement of Alex., Origen), that the Lord’s public ministry lasted <em> only a year, and something over</em> . Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:13">John 2:13</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:4">John 6:4</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:1">John 13:1</span> .</p>
