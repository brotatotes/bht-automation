<p><strong> 21.</strong> ] <strong> ἤρξ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> δὲ λέγειν</strong> implying that the following words are merely the substance of a more expanded discourse, which our Lord uttered to that effect: see another occasion in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:4-5">Matthew 11:4-5</span> , where the same truth was declared by a series of gracious acts of mercy.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἡ γρ</strong> <strong> .</strong> κ . τ . λ .] Not ‘ <em> this Scripture which is in your ears</em> ’ as the Syriac (Etheridge’s translation, p. 407); which would be ἡ γρ . αὕτη ἡ ἐν τ . ὠ ., and even then an unusual form of construction: but, <strong> is fulfilled in your hearing,</strong> by My proclaiming it, and My course of ministry.</p>
