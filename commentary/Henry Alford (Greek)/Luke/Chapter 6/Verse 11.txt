<p><strong> 11. </strong> <strong> ἀνοίας</strong> ] It does not appear that this word can ever mean, as in some former editions, ‘madness,’ rage of a senseless kind: certainly it does not in reff., nor in Herod. vi. 69 or Thuc. iii. 48, there carelessly referred to. The proper meaning, ‘senselessness,’ ‘wicked folly,’ must be kept to. See Ellicott’s note on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:9">2 Timothy 3:9</span> , to which I owe this correction.
	</p>
