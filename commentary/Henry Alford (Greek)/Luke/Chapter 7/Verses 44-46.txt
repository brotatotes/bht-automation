<p><strong> ἀφ</strong> <strong> ʼ </strong> <strong> ἧς εἰσῆλθ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] These words will explain one difficulty in the circumstances of the anointing: how such a woman came into the guest-chamber of such a Pharisee.
	</p>
<p>She appears by them to have entered <em> simultaneously with our Lord and His disciples</em> . Nor do Luk 7:36-37 at all preclude this idea: <strong> ἐπιγνοῦσα ὅτι κατάκειται</strong> may mean, ‘having knowledge that He was going to dine,’ &amp;c. If she came in His train, the Pharisee would not exclude her, as He was accustomed to gather such to hear Him: it was the <em> touching</em> at which he wondered.</p>
