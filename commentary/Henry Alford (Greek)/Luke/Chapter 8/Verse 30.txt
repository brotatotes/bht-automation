<p><strong> 30.</strong> ] Lightfoot (on Mar 5:9 ) quotes instances of the use of ל ̇ גיון , for a great number, in the Rabbinical writings. The fact of <em> many dœmons</em> having entered into this wretched man, sets before us terribly the utter break-up of his personal and rational being. The words will not bear any figurative rendering, but must be taken literally (see Luk 8:2 of this chap., and ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:24">Luke 11:24</span> ff.); viz. that in the same sense in which other poor creatures were possessed by <em> one evil spirit</em> (see note on [71] Matt.), this man, and Mary Magdalene, were possessed by <em> many</em> .
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [71] When, in the Gospels, and in the Evangelic statement, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:23-25">1 Corinthians 11:23-25</span> , the sign (║) occurs in a reference, it is signified that the word occurs <em> in the parallel place</em> in the other Gospels, which will always be found indicated <em> at the head of the note</em> on the paragraph. When the sign (║) is <em> qualified</em> , thus, ‘║ Mk.,’ or ‘║ Mt. Mk.,’ &amp;c., it is signified that the word occurs <em> in the parallel place in that Gospel or Gospels, but not in the other or others</em> .</p>
