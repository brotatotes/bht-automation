<p><strong> 49.</strong> ] Little marks of accuracy come out in each of the two fuller accounts. Here we have <strong> ἔρχεταί τις</strong> <strong> ,</strong> which was doubtless the <em> exact</em> fact: in Mark ἔρχονται , generally expressed. In Mark again we learn not only that Jesus heard, but παρακούσας τὸν λόγον λαλούμενον , i.e. it was not <em> reported</em> to Him, but He <em> overheard it being said</em> , which is a minute detail not given here. Nothing could more satisfactorily mark the independent authority of the two narratives.</p>
