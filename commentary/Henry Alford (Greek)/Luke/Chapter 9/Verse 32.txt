<p><strong> 32.</strong> ] <strong> διαγρ</strong> <strong> .,</strong> not ‘ <em> when they were awake</em> ,’ as E. V. but <strong> having kept awake</strong> through the whole. The word occurs in this sense in Herodian iii. 4, πάσης τῆς νυκτὸς … διαγρηγορήσαντες . It seems to be expressly used here to shew that it was <em> not merely a vision</em> , seen in sleep.</p>
