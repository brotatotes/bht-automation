<p><strong> 43, 44.</strong> ] <strong> πάντες</strong> the multitude in contrast with <strong> ὑμεῖς</strong> of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:44">Luke 9:44</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> τοὺς λ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τ</strong> <strong> .,</strong> not (Meyer), ‘ <em> the foregoing discourses and</em> <em> wonders:</em> ’ that would give no sense, for the disciples were thinking exclusively of those already: nor strictly (Stier, but corrected in edn. 2) ‘ <em> what I am about to tell you</em> ,’ so that τοὺς λ . τ . should be [76] with τὸ ῥῆμα below: but <strong> these sayings,</strong> of which this was now <em> the second;</em> ‘these intimations which I make to you from time to time respecting My sufferings and death.’ The <em> Resurrection</em> , expressly mentioned in the others, is omitted here.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [76] When, in the Gospels, and in the Evangelic statement, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:23-25">1 Corinthians 11:23-25</span> , the sign (║) occurs in a reference, it is signified that the word occurs <em> in the parallel place</em> in the other Gospels, which will always be found indicated <em> at the head of the note</em> on the paragraph. When the sign (║) is <em> qualified</em> , thus, ‘║ Mk.,’ or ‘║ Mt. Mk.,’ &amp;c., it is signified that the word occurs <em> in the parallel place in that Gospel or Gospels, but not in the other or others</em> .</p>
