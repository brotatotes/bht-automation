<p><strong> 19. </strong> <strong> ἔγραψεν</strong> <strong> … </strong> <strong> ἵνα</strong> ] This is one of the cases where purpose and purport are mingled in the <strong> ἵνα</strong> . See on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:13">1 Corinthians 14:13</span> . It is better to take it so than with Meyer to suppose ἵνα dependent on <em> volo</em> understood.</p>
