<p><strong> 28.</strong> ] The motive, as shewn by the subordination of ἀκούσας to προσελθών , and of εἰδώς to ἐπηρώτησεν , seems to have been, <em> admiration of our Lord’s wise answer</em> , and a desire to be instructed further by Him.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐντ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> πρώτη πάντ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] This was one of the μάχαι νομικαί ( Tit 3:9 ), <em> which was the greatest commandment</em> . The Scribes had many frivolous enumerations and classifications of the commands of the law.
	</p>
<p><strong> πάντων</strong> <strong> ,</strong> not πασῶν : <strong> πρώτη</strong> <strong> -</strong> <strong> πάντων</strong> is treated almost as <em> one word</em> , so that <strong> πάντων</strong> does not belong to ἐντ . understood, but, q. d. ‘first-of-all of the commandments.’</p>
