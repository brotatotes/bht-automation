<p><strong> 15.</strong> ] <strong> τὸν κόσμον ἅπαντα</strong> = πάντα τὰ ἔθνη , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:19">Matthew 28:19</span> ; see note there.
	</p>
<p><strong> κηρύσσειν τὸ εὐαγγέλιον</strong> <strong> ,</strong> without the addition of τῆς βασιλείας (Matt.) or τοῦ θεοῦ ( Luk 1:14 only, Luke), is in <em> Mark’s manner</em> (see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:10">Mark 13:10</span> ; Mar 14:9 ). It only once occurs in Matt., viz. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:13">Matthew 26:13</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> πάσῃ τῇ κτ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] Not to <em> men</em> only, although men only can hear the <em> preaching</em> of the Gospel; <em> all creation</em> is redeemed by Christ see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="col+1:15">Colossians 1:15</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="col+1:23">Colossians 1:23</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:19-23">Romans 8:19-23</span> . ‘Hominibus, primario, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+16:16">Mark 16:16</span> , reliquis creaturis, secundario. Sicut maledictio, ita benedictio patet. Creatio per Filium, fundamentum redemtionis et regni.’ Bengel in loc.
	</p>
<p><strong> κτίσις</strong> appears never in the N.T. to be used of <em> mankind alone</em> . Bengel’s ‘reliquis creaturis secundario’ may be illustrated in the blessings which Christianity confers on the inferior creatures and the face of the earth by bringing civilization in its wake.
	</p>
<p>By these words <em> the missionary office is bound upon the Church through all ages, till every part of the earth shall have been evangelized</em> .</p>
