<p><strong> 17.</strong> ] <strong> Βοανηργές</strong> = בְּנֵי רֶגֶשׁ , <em> Sheva</em> being expressed by οα in Aramaic (Meyer, from Lightf.), perhaps on account of their <em> vehement and zealous disposition</em> , of which we see marks <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:54">Luke 9:54</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:38">Mark 9:38</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:37">Mark 10:37</span> ; see also <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:10">2 John 1:10</span> ; but this is uncertain.
	</p>
<p><strong> ὀνόματα</strong> <strong> ,</strong> since <em> both</em> bore the name, and the Hebrew word is plural.
	</p>
<p>There is an interesting notice of the catalogues of the Apostles, and the questions arising out of them, in the Lectures of Bleek on the three Gospels, published since his death by Holzmann, Leipzig, 1862.</p>
