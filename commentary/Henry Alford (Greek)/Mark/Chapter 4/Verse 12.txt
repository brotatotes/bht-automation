<p><strong> 12.</strong> ] We must keep the <strong> ἵνα</strong> strictly to its telic meaning <strong> in order that</strong> . When God transacts a matter, it is idle to say that the result is not the purpose. He doeth all things after the counsel of His own will. Matt., as usual, quotes a prophecy; Mark hardly ever except at the beginning of his Gospel; Luke, very seldom.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀφ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> αὐτ</strong> <strong> .</strong> = ἰάσομαι αὐτούς Matt., <strong> it should be forgiven them</strong> ; i.e. ‘ <em> forgiveness should be extended to them</em> :’ no need to supply any thing, as the gloss of the re [11] . does: the expression is impersonal.
	</p>
