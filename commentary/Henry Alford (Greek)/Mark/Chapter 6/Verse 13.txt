<p><strong> 13. </strong> <strong> ἤλειφον ἐλαίῳ</strong> ] This oil was not used <em> medicinally</em> , but as <em> a vehicle of healing power committed to them</em> ; a symbol of a deeper thing than the oil itself could accomplish. That such anointing has nothing in common with the extreme unction of Romanists, see proved in note on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:14">James 5:14</span> .
	</p>
<p>See for instances of such symbolic use of external applications, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2ki+5:14">2 Kings 5:14</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:23">Mark 8:23</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:6">John 9:6</span> , &amp;c.</p>
