<p><strong> 34. </strong> <strong> ἐξελθών</strong> ] <strong> having disembarked,</strong> most probably. Meyer would render it, ‘ <em> having come forth from his solitude</em> ,’ in Matt., and ‘ <em> having disembarked</em> ’ here: but I very much doubt the former. There is nothing in Matt. to imply that He had <em> reached his place of solitude before</em> the multitudes came up. John indeed, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:3-7">Mark 6:3-7</span> , <em> seems</em> to imply this; but He may very well have mounted the hill or cliff from the sea before He saw the multitudes, and this would be <em> on his disembarkation</em> .
	</p>
<p>To shew how arbitrary is the assumption of Mark having combined Matt. and Luke, see how easily the same might be said of <em> Luke himself</em> , with regard to <em> Matt.</em> and <em> Mark</em> here: ἐθεράπευσεν τοὺς ἀῤῥώστους αὐτῶν , Matt.: ἤρξατο διδάσκειν αὐτ . πολ ., Mark; ἐλάλει αὐτοῖς περὶ τ . βας . τ . θ ., κ . τοὺς χρείαν ἔχ . θεραπείας ἰᾶτο , Luke: = Matt. † Mark.</p>
