<p><strong> 19. </strong> <strong> γενεά</strong> ] not addressed to <em> the man</em> , as unbelieving, nor to the disciples, but <em> generally</em> , to the race and generation among whom the Lord’s ministry was fulfilled. The additional words καὶ διεστραμμένη (Matt. Luke) are probably from <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="de+32:5">Deuteronomy 32:5</span> ; see further ib. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="de+32:20">Deuteronomy 32:20</span> , where ἄπιστος is also expressed by νἱοὶ οἷς οὐκ ἔστι πίστις ἐν αὐτοῖς . The question is not asked in a spirit of longing to be gone from them, but of holy impatience of their hardness of heart and unbelief. In this the father, disciples, Scribes, and multitude are equally involved.</p>
