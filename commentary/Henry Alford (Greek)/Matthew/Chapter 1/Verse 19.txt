<p><strong> 19. </strong> <strong> ἀνήρ</strong> ] so called, though they were as yet but betrothed: so in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ge+29:21">Genesis 29:21</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="de+22:24">Deuteronomy 22:24</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> δίκαιος</strong> ] <strong> just;</strong> καὶ μὴ θ . being, as the <strong> μή</strong> plainly shews, not the explanation of δίκαιος , but an additional particular. He was a strict observer of the law, and (yet) not willing to expose her. The sense of ‘ <em> kind</em> ,’ ‘ <em> merciful</em> ,’ is inadmissible.
	</p>
<p><strong> λάθρα</strong> ] Not ‘ <em> without any writing of divorcement</em> ,’ which would have been unlawful; but according to the form prescribed in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="de+24:1">Deuteronomy 24:1</span> . The husband might either do this, or adopt the stronger course of bringing his wife (or betrothed, who had the same rights, Maimon. in Wetstein, and Philo de legg. spec [8] , ad cap. 6 <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="es+7:0">Esther 7:0</span> decal. § 12, vol. ii. p. 311, αἱ ὁδμολογίαι γάμοις ἰσοδυναμοῦσι ) to justice openly. The punishment in this case would have been death by stoning. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="de+22:23">Deuteronomy 22:23</span> . Maimonides (quoted by Buxtorf de divort.) says, “Femina ex quo desponsata est, licet nondum a viro cognita, est uxor viri, et si sponsus earn velit repudiare, oportet, ut id faciat libello repudii.”
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [8] The Latin readings contained in a MS. ‘Speculum’ at Rome. Published by Mai.
	</p>
