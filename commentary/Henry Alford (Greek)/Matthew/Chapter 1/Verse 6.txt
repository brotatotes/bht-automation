<p><strong> 6. </strong> <strong> τῆς τοῦ Οὐ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] This construction, which is not properly elliptical, but possessive (Grotius compares ‘Hectoris Andromache,’ Virg., Meyer, <em> Luther’s Katharina</em> , and Bernhardy, Syntax, p. 160, Διὸς Ἄρτεμις , Ζηνὸς Ἀπόλλων Plut. de Pyth. or. p. 402, Ἱππίου Ἀρχεδίκην Thuc. vi. 59, &amp;c.), occurs in the Gospels to designate various relations: see reff.</p>
