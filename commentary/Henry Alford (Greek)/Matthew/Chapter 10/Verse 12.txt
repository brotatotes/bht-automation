<p><strong> 12. </strong> <strong> τὴν οἰκίαν</strong> ] Not <em> the</em> house of the ἄξιος , for this would be sure to be worthy; but <em> any house</em> , as is necessary from the subsequent ἐὰν ᾖ ἡ οἰκ . ἀξ ., which on the other supposition (Meyer, &amp;c.) would have been ascertained already. The full command as to their conduct, <em> from arriving to departing</em> , is given in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:11">Matthew 10:11</span> . Then, the subject being taken up again at their <em> arrival</em> in the city, the method of ἐξέτασις is prescribed to them in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:12-13">Matthew 10:12-13</span> . When they enter into an house, (so, idiomatically, E. V.,) they are to salute it: and if on enquiry it prove worthy, then &amp;c. See notes on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:1">Matthew 9:1</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:28">Matthew 9:28</span> .</p>
