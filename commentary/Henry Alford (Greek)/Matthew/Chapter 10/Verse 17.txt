<p><strong> 17. </strong> <strong> προσέχετε</strong> ] The wisdom of the serpent is needed for this part of their course; the simplicity of the dove for the μὴ μεριμνήσητε in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:19">Matthew 10:19</span> .
	</p>
<p>The <strong> δέ</strong> turns from the internal character to behaviour in regard of outward circumstances.
	</p>
<p><strong> συνέδρια</strong> ] See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:6-7">Acts 4:6-7</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:40">Acts 5:40</span> . They are the <em> courts of seven</em> (on which see Deu 16:18 ), appointed in every city, to take cognizance of causes both civil and criminal, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:21">Matthew 5:21</span> : here perhaps put for any courts of assembly in general.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐν τ. συν. μαστιγ. ὑ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:19">Acts 22:19</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:11">Acts 26:11</span> . Euseb. Hist. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ec+5:16">Ecclesiastes 5:16</span> , quoting a book against the Montanists, οὐδὲ μὴν οὐδὲ ἐν συναγωγαῖς Ἰουδαίων τῶν γυναικῶν τις ἐμαστιγώθη ποτέ, ἢ ἐλιθοβολήθη· οὐδαμόσε οὐδαμῶς . The scourging in the synagogues is supposed to have been inflicted by order of the Tribunal of Three, who judged in them.</p>
