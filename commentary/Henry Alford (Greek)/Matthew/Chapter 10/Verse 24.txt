<p><strong> 24.</strong> ] This proverb is used in different senses in Luk 6:40 and <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:16">John 13:16</span> . The view <em> here</em> is, that disciples must <em> not expect a better lot</em> than their Master, but be well satisfied if they have no worse. The threefold relation of our Lord and His followers here brought out may thus be exemplified from Scripture: μαθητής and διδάσκαλος , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:1">Matthew 5:1</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:8">Matthew 23:8</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:20">Luke 6:20</span> ; δοῦλος and κύριος , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:13">John 13:13</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:35-48">Luke 12:35-48</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:1">Romans 1:1</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:1">2 Peter 1:1</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:1">Jude 1:1</span> ; οἰκοδεσπότης and οἰκιακοί , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:26-29">Matthew 26:26-29</span> [116] : <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:30">Luke 24:30</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:45">Matthew 24:45</span> ff. [117] .
