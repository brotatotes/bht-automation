<p><strong> 29.</strong> ] <strong> μάθετε ἀπ</strong> <strong> ʼ </strong> <strong> ἐμοῦ</strong> <strong> ,</strong> both ‘ <em> from My example</em> ,’ which however is the lower sense of the words, and ‘ <em> from My teaching</em> ,’ from which alone the ἀνάπαυσις can flow; the ἀποκάλυψις of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:25">Matthew 11:25</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:27">Matthew 11:27</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> εὑρήσετε ἀνάπ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ψ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ὑμ</strong> <strong> .</strong> quoted from Jer 6:16 Heb. Thus we have it revealed here, that the rest and joy of the Christian soul is, <em> to become like Christ;</em> to attain by His teaching this πραότης and ταπεινότης of His.
	</p>
<p>Olshausen makes an excellent distinction between ταπεινὸς τῇ καρδίᾳ , an attribute of divine Love in the Saviour, and ταπεινὸς or πτωχὸς τῷ πνεύματι , ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:3">Matthew 5:3</span> : <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="pr+29:23">Proverbs 29:23</span> , which can only be said of sinful man, knowing his unworthiness and need of help.
	</p>
<p><strong> καρδία</strong> is <em> only here</em> used of Christ. (Stier on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:1">John 14:1</span> .)</p>
