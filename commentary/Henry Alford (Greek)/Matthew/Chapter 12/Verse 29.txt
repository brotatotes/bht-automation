<p><strong> 29.</strong> ] Luke has the word ἰσχυρότερος applied to the spoiler in this verse; a title given to our Lord by the Baptist, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:11">Matthew 3:11</span> [123] , and also in prophecy, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="isa+40:10">Isaiah 40:10</span> ( μετὰ ἰσχύος , LXX). See also <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="isa+53:12">Isaiah 53:12</span> (LXX); <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="isa+49:24-25">Isaiah 49:24-25</span> . See note on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:21">Luke 11:21</span> f., which is the fuller report of this parabolic saying.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [123] When, in the Gospels, and in the Evangelic statement, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:23-25">1 Corinthians 11:23-25</span> , the sign (║) occurs in a reference, it is signified that the word occurs <em> in the parallel place</em> in the other Gospels, which will always be found indicated <em> at the head of the note</em> on the paragraph. When the sign (║) is <em> qualified</em> , thus, ‘║ Mk.,’ or ‘║ Mt. Mk.,’ &amp;c., it is signified that the word occurs <em> in the parallel place in that Gospel or Gospels, but not in the other or others</em> .</p>
