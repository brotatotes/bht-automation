<p><strong> 3.</strong> ] It appears from <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1sa+21:6">1 Samuel 21:6</span> , that hot bread had been put in on the day of David’s arrival; which therefore, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="le+24:8">Leviticus 24:8</span> , was a sabbath. The example was thus doubly appropriate. Bengel maintains, on the commonly received interpretation of σάβ . δευτερόπρωτον <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:1">Luke 6:1</span> , that 1Sa 21:1-15 was the lesson for the day. But the Jewish calendar of lessons cannot be shewn to have existed in the form which we now have, in the time of the Gospel history.</p>
