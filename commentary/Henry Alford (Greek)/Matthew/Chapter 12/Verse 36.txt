<p><strong> 36.</strong> ] <strong> ῥῆμα ἀργόν</strong> is nom. pendens, as ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:14">Matthew 10:14</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:32">Matthew 10:32</span> . αἱρετώτερόν σοι ἔστω λίθον εἰκῆ βάλλειν , ἢ λόγον ἀργόν , Pythag. in Stobæus, xxxiv. 11. Wets [124] .
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [124] Wetstein.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀργός</strong> = ἀεργός , and is perhaps best taken here in its milder and negative sense, as not yet determined on till the judgment: so that our Lord’s saying is a deduction “a minori,” and if of every ῥῆμα ἀργόν , then how much more of every ῥῆμα πονηρόν !</p>
