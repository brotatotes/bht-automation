<p><strong> 20. </strong> <strong> κοφίνους</strong> ] in the construction, is in apposition with τὸ περισσεῦον .
	</p>
<p>The <em> cophinus</em> was the usual accompaniment of the Jew: see Juv [137] Sat. iii.14 ‘Judæis, quorum cophinus fœnumque supellex;’ and Sat. vi. 542. Reland, whom Schöttgen (in loc.) follows, supposes that the basket was to carry their own meats on a journey, for fear of pollution by eating those of the Gentiles, and the hay to sleep on for the same reason.
	</p>
