<p><strong> 25.</strong> ] The <em> fourth watch</em> according to the <em> Roman</em> calculation, which was by this time common among the Jews (who themselves divided the night into three parts or watches). This would be, near the vernal equinox which this was, <em> between three and six in the morning</em> .

<p><strong> ἀπῆλθεν πρὸς αὐτ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] a mixed construction for ἀπῆλθεν ἀπὸ τοῦ ὄρους καὶ ἦλθεν πρὸς αὐτ . The words <strong> περιπατ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἐπὶ τὴν θάλ</strong> <strong> .</strong> (or <strong> τῆς θαλάσσης</strong> <strong> ,</strong> the gen. of the mere appearing on the spot, the accus. of motion, <em> over the sea</em> . Webst. and Wilk. cite ἐπὶ πολλὰ ἀλήθην Od. ξ . 120, ἐπ ʼ ἔννεα κεῖτο πέλεθρα Od. λ . 577) are common to the three Evangelists, and can have no other meaning here, than that the Lord <em> walked bodily on the surface of the water</em> . The passages commonly cited to shew that ἐπί with a gen. can mean ‘on the bank of,’ are not applicable here, being all after verbs of <em> rest</em> , not of motion. 4 Kings <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:7">Matthew 2:7</span> : Dan 8:2 Theod.: <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:1">John 21:1</span> . In ref. Job we read of the Almighty, ὁ τανύσας τὸν οὐρανὸν μόνος καὶ περιπατῶν ὡς ἐπ ʼ ἐδάφους ἐπὶ θαλάσσης . Mark adds καὶ ἤθελεν παρελθεῖν αὐτούς : John, καὶ ἐγγὺς τοῦ πλοίου γινόμενον . See notes on John.</p>
