<p><strong> 16, 17.</strong> ] <strong> δότε αὐτοῖς ὑμεῖς φ</strong> <strong> .,</strong> which is common to the three first Evangelists, is considerably expanded in the more detailed account of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:1-36">John 3:1-36</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:1-54">John 4:1-54</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:1-47">John 5:1-47</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:1-71">John 6:1-71</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:1-53">John 7:1-53</span> ; it was <em> Andrew who spoke</em> in our <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:17">Matthew 14:17</span> , and the five loaves and two fishes were <em> brought by a lad:</em> <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:8-9">John 6:8-9</span> . They were <em> barley loaves</em> and ( <em> salt</em> ) <em> fish;</em> ibid. And we have (perhaps, but see note there) the vast concourse accounted for in John by the fact that <em> the Passover was at hand</em> , and so they were collected on their journey to Jerusalem.
	</p>
