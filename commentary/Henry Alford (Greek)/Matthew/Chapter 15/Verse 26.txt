<p><strong> 26. </strong> <strong> κυναρίοις</strong> ] No <em> further</em> contempt is indicated by the <em> diminutive</em> , still less any allusion to the <em> daughter</em> of the woman: the word is commonly used of <em> tame</em> dogs, as diminutives frequently express familiarity. So in Xen. Cyr. viii. 4. 20, εἰ δὲ μεγάλην γαμεῖς , ἤν ποτε βούλῃ αὐτὴν ὀρθὴν φιλῆσαι , προσάλλεσθαί σε δεήσει ὡς <strong> τὰ κυνάρια</strong> <strong> .</strong></p>
