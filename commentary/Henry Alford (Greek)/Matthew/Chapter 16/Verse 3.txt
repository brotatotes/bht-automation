<p><strong> 3.</strong> ] Polybius iv. 21. 1, speaks of the ἠθῶν αὐστηρίαν (of the Arcadians) ἥτις αὐτοῖς παρέπεται διὰ τὴν τοῦ περιέχοντος ( ἀέρος ) ψυχρότητα καὶ στυγνότητα .
	</p>
<p>‘Si circa occidentem rubescunt nubes, serenitatem futuræ diei spondent; concavus oriens pluvias prædicit; idem ventos cum ante exorientem eum nubes rubescunt: quod si et nigræ rubentibus intervenerint ( πυῤῥάζει στυγνάζων ) et pluvias.’ Plin. Hist. Nat. xviii. 35.
	</p>
<p><strong> πρόσωπον</strong> <strong> ,</strong> because στυγνός and στυγνάζω are properly used of sadness and obscurity in the visage of man.
	</p>
<p><strong> τῶν καιρῶν</strong> <strong> , of times,</strong> generally. The Jews had been, and were, most blind to the signs of the times, at all the great crises of their history; and also particularly to the times in which they were <em> then</em> living. The sceptre had departed from Judah, the lawgiver no longer came forth from between his feet, the prophetic weeks of Daniel were just at their end; yet they discerned none of these things.</p>
