<p><strong> 10.</strong> ] The occasion of this enquiry was, that they had just seen Elias withdrawn from their eyes, and were enjoined not to tell the vision. How ( οὖν ) should this be? If this was not the coming of Elias, <em> was he yet to come?</em> If it was, how was it so <em> secret</em> and so short?
	</p>
<p>On <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:12">Matthew 17:12</span> , see note on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:14">Matthew 11:14</span> .
	</p>
<p>Our Lord speaks here plainly <em> in the future</em> , and uses the very word of the prophecy <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mal+4:6">Malachi 4:6</span> . The <em> double</em> allusion is only the assertion that the Elias (in spirit and power) who foreran our Lord’s first coming, was a partial fulfilment of the great prophecy which announces the <em> real Elias</em> (the words of Malachi will hardly bear any other than a personal meaning), who is to forerun His greater and second coming.</p>
