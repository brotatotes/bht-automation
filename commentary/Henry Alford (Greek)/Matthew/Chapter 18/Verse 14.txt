<p><strong> 14.</strong> ] This verse sets forth to us the <em> work of the Son as accomplishing the will of the Father;</em> for it is unquestionably the Son who is the Good Shepherd, searching for the lost, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:11">Matthew 18:11</span> . For similar declarations see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eze+18:23">Ezekiel 18:23</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eze+33:11">Ezekiel 33:11</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:9">2 Peter 3:9</span> .
	</p>
<p>The inference from this verse is ‘then whoever despises or scandalizes one of these little ones, acts in opposition to the will of your Father in Heaven.’ Observe, when the dignity of the little ones was asserted, it was πατρός <strong> μου</strong> : now that a motive directly acting on the conscience of the Christian is urged, it is πατρὸς <strong> ὑμῶν</strong> .</p>
