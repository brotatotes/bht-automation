<p><strong> 10.</strong> ] <strong> αἰτία</strong> <strong> ,</strong> not the <em> cause</em> of divorce just mentioned; nor, the <em> condition</em> of the man with his wife: but <strong> the account to be given,</strong> ‘ <em> the original ground and principle</em> ,’ of the relationship of man and wife: ἐὰν τοιαύτη ἐστὶν ἡ αἰτία τῆς συζυγίας , Euthym [151] , who however mentions other renderings. The disciples apprehend that the trials and temptations of marriage would prove sources of sin and misery. This question and its answer are peculiar to Matthew.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [151] Euthymius Zigabenus, 1116
	</p>
<p>Meyer refers αἰτία back to the αἰτία in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:3">Matthew 19:3</span> , and understands it to mean <em> the only reason justifying divorce;</em> but the above interpretation seems to me preferable.</p>
