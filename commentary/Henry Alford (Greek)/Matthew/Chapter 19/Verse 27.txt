<p><strong> 27.</strong> ] The disciples, or rather Peter speaking for them, recur to the ἕξεις θησ . ἐν οὐρ . said to the young man, and enquire what <em> their</em> reward shall be, who have done all that was required of them. He does not ask respecting <em> salvation</em> , but <em> some pre-eminent reward</em> , as is manifest by the answer. The ‘all’ which the Apostles had left, was not in every case contemptible. The sons of Zebedee had hired servants ( Mar 1:20 ), and Levi (Matthew?) could make a great feast in his house. But whatever it was, it <em> was their all</em> .</p>
