<p><strong> ἐργάτας</strong> ] in the primary meanings of the parable, ‘apostles, prophets, ministers:’ distinct from the <em> vines</em> in the vineyard. But inasmuch as every workman is himself subject to the treatment of the husbandman (see Joh 15:1-2 ), and every man in the Kingdom of God is in some sense or other a worker on the rest, the distinction is not to be pressed the parable ranges over both comparisons.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀμπελῶνα</strong> ] not the Jewish church <em> only</em> , as Greswell, Parables, iv. 355 ff., maintains. The Jewish Church was God’s vineyard especially and typically; <em> His Church in all ages</em> is His <em> true</em> vineyard, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:1">John 15:1</span> .
	</p>
<p>Our language admits of the idiom <strong> εἰς</strong> τὸν ἀμ . αὐ . being exactly rendered <strong> into his vineyard,</strong> E. V.</p>
