<p><strong> 3. </strong> <strong> πάντα οὖν ὅσα ἄν</strong> ] The οὖν here is very significant: <em> because</em> they sit on Moses’ seat: and this clears the meaning, and shews it to be, ‘all things which they, as successors of Moses, out of his law, command you to observe, do;’ there being a distinction between their lawful teaching as expounders of the law, and their frivolous traditions superadded thereto, and blamed below.
	</p>
<p><strong> ποιήσατε</strong> <strong> ,</strong> do, as occasion arises. <strong> τηρεῖτε</strong> <strong> ,</strong> observe, having respect to them as a constant rule of conduct. The <em> present</em> binds on the habitual practice to the mere momentary act of the <em> aorist</em> .</p>
