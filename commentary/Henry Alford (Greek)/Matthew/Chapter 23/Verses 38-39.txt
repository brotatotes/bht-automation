<p><strong> 38, 39.</strong> ] This is our Lord’s last and solemn departure from the temple the <em> true</em> μεταβαίνωμεν ἐντεῦθεν (‘motus excedentium Deorum.’ Tacitus).
	</p>
<p><strong> οἶκος ὑμῶν</strong> ] no more <em> God’s</em> , but <strong> your house</strong> said primarily of the temple, then of Jerusalem, and then of the whole land in which ye dwell.
	</p>
<p><strong> οὐ μή με ἴδητε</strong> ] He did not shew Himself to all the people after His resurrection, but only to chosen witnesses, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:41">Acts 10:41</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἕως ἂν εἴπ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] until that day, the subject of all prophecy, when your repentant people shall turn with true and loyal Hosannas and blessings to greet ‘Him whom they have pierced:’ see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="de+4:30-31">Deuteronomy 4:30-31</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ho+3:4-5">Hosea 3:4-5</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="zec+12:10">Zechariah 12:10</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="zec+14:8-11">Zechariah 14:8-11</span> . Stier well remarks, ‘He who reads not this in the prophets, reads not yet the prophets aright.’</p>
