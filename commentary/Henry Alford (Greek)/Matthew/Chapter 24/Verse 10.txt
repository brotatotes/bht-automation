<p><strong> 10.</strong> ] See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:16">2 Timothy 4:16</span> , and the repeated warnings against apostasy in the Epistle to the Hebrews. The persons spoken of in this verse are <em> Christians</em> . ‘Primo conrepti qui fatebantur, deinde <em> indicio eorum</em> multitude ingens.’ Tac. Ann. xv. 44. On <strong> μισ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἀλλ</strong> <strong> .,</strong> compare the deadly hatred borne to Paul and his work by the Judaizers. In the Apocryphal works called the Clementines, which follow teaching similar to that of the factions adverse to Paul in the Corinthian Church, he is hinted at under the name ὁ ἐχθρὸς ἄνθρωπος (Ep. Pet. to <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:1-26">James 2:1-26</span> , and Recognitions, i. 70, cited by Stanley, Essays on Apostolic Age, p. 377). These Judaizing teachers, among others, are meant by the ψευδοπροφῆται , as also that plentiful crop of heretical teachers which sprang up every where with the good seed of the Gospel when first sown.
