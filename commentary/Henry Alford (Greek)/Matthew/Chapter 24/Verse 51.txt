<p><strong> 51.</strong> ] <strong> διχ</strong> <strong> .</strong> refers to the punishment of cutting, or sawing asunder: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="da+2:5">Daniel 2:5</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="da+3:29">Daniel 3:29</span> ; Sus. ver. 59; see also <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:12">Hebrews 4:12</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:37">Hebrews 11:37</span> . The expression here is perhaps not without a symbolical reference to that dreadful <em> sundering of the conscience and practice</em> which shall be the reflective torment of the condemned: and by the mingling and confounding of which only is the anomalous life of the wilful sinner made in this world tolerable.</p>
