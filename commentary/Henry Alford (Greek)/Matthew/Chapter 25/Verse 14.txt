<p><strong> 14. </strong> <strong> ὥσπ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> γ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] The ellipsis is rightly supplied in the E. V., <strong> For the Kingdom of Heaven is as a man,</strong> &amp;c. We have this parable and the preceding one alluded to in very few words by <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:34-36">Mark 13:34-36</span> . In it we have the <em> active</em> side of the Christian life, and <em> its</em> danger, set before us, as in the last the contemplative side. There, the foolish virgins failed <em> from thinking their part too easy</em> here the wicked servant fails <em> from thinking his too hard</em> . The parable is still concerned with <em> Christians</em> ( <strong> τοὺς ἰδίους δούλους</strong> ), and <em> not the world at large</em> .
	</p>
<p>We must remember the relation of master and slave, in order to understand his delivering to them his property, and punishing them for not fructifying with it.</p>
