<p><strong> 41 43.</strong> ] It is very important to observe the distinction between the blessing, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:34">Matthew 25:34</span> , and the curse here. ‘Blessed <em> of my Father</em> :’ but not ‘cursed of my Father,’ because all man’s salvation <em> is of God</em> all his condemnation <em> from himself</em> . ‘The Kingdom, <em> prepared for you</em> ;’ but ‘the fire, which has been prepared for <em> the devil and his angels</em> ’ (notice <strong> τὸ</strong> πῦρ <strong> τὸ</strong> αἰών . <strong> τὸ</strong> ἡτοιμ … greater definiteness could not be given: that particular fire, that eternal fire, created for a special purpose) not, for <em> you</em> : because <em> there is election to life</em> but there is <em> no reprobation to death</em> : a <em> book of Life</em> but <em> no book of Death</em> ; no hell <em> for</em> man because the blood of Jesus hath purchased life <em> for all</em> : but they who will <em> serve the devil, must share with him in the end</em> .
	</p>
<p>The <em> repetition</em> of all these particulars shews how exact even for every individual the judgment will be. Stier excellently remarks, that the <em> curse</em> shews the <em> termination of the High Priesthood of Christ</em> , in which office He only intercedes and blesses. Henceforth He is King and Lord his enemies being now for ever put under his feet.</p>
