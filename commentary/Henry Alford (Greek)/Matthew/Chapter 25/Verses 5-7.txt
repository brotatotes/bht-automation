<p><strong> 5 7. </strong> <strong> χρονίζ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] compare ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:48">Matthew 24:48</span> . But the thought of the foolish virgins is very different from that of the wicked servant: his ‘there will be plenty of time, my Lord tarrieth;’ theirs, ‘surely He will soon be here, there is no need of a store of oil.’ This may serve to shew how altogether diverse is the ground of the two parables.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐν</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> πᾶσ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> κ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἐκ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] I believe no more is meant here than that all, being weak by nature, gave way to drowsiness: as indeed the wakefulness of the holiest Christian, compared with what it should be, is a sort of slumber: but, the while, how much difference was there between them!
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐνύστ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] <em> dormitabant</em> : we have Aristoph. Vesp. 12, ὕπνος νυστακτής and Plato, Rep. p. 405 c, speaks of a νυστάζων δικαστής . Wordsw., after Hilary, understands this verse of sleep in death. But, not to mention that this will not fit the machinery of the parable (see below on Mat 25:8 ), it would assume ( <strong> πᾶσαι</strong> ) that none of the faithful would be living on earth when the Lord comes.
	</p>
<p><strong> κραυγὴ γ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="isa+62:5-7">Isaiah 62:5-7</span> ; and the porter’s duty, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:34">Mark 13:34</span> . This warning cry is <em> before</em> the coming: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:10">Matthew 25:10</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> γέγονεν</strong> <strong> ,</strong> not, <em> was</em> , but to be rendered <em> present</em> , graphically setting the reality before us: <strong> there ariseth a cry</strong> .
	</p>
<p><strong> πᾶσαι</strong> ] <em> All</em> now seem alike all wanted their lamps trimmed but for the neglectful, there is not wherewith! It is not enough to <em> have burnt</em> , but to <em> be burning</em> , when He comes. Raise the wick as they will, what avails it if the oil is spent?
	</p>

Virgil’s Moretum, ‘Et producit acu stupas humore carentes.’ ” Webst. and Wilk.</p>
