<p><strong> 12.</strong> ] I can hardly think that our Lord would have said this, unless there had been in Mary’s mind a distinct reference to His burial, in doing the act. All the company surely knew well that His death, and that by crucifixion, was near at hand: can we suppose one who so closely observed His words as Mary, not to have been possessed with the thought of that which was about to happen? The προέλαβεν μυρίσαι μου τὸ σῶμα of Mark ( Mar 14:8 ), and the ἵνα εἰς τὴν ἡμ . τοῦ ἐνταφ . μου τηρήσῃ αὐτό of John ( Joh 12:7 ), point even more strongly to <em> her intention</em> .</p>
