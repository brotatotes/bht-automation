<p><strong> ἐξορκίζω σε</strong> <strong> ,</strong> ‘I put thee under an oath,’ the form of which follows. The junction of <strong> ὁ υἱὸς τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> θ</strong> <strong> .</strong> with <strong> χριστός</strong> must not be pressed beyond the meaning which Caiaphas probably assigned to it viz. the title given to the Messiah from the purport of the prophecies respecting Him. It is however a very different thing when our Lord by his answer <em> affirms this</em> , and invests the words with their fullest meaning and dignity.</p>
