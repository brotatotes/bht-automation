<p><strong> 17.</strong> ] In John’s narrative, the suggestion of liberating Barabbas seems to come from the Jews themselves; but not necessarily so: he may only be giving, as before, a general report of what passed. The <strong> συνηγμ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> οὖν αὐτ</strong> <strong> .</strong> seems to imply that a great crowd had collected outside the Prætorium while the trial was going on. It is possible that the addition τὸν λεγόμενον χριστόν , which Pilate could hardly have heard from the Jews, may have been familiar to him by his wife’s mention of Jesus. See below.</p>
