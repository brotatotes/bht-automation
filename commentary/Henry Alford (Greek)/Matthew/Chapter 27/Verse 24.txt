<p><strong> 24.</strong> ] Peculiar to Matt.
	</p>
<p><strong> οὐδὲν ὠφελεῖ</strong> ] rightly rendered in E. V. <strong> that he prevailed nothing</strong> not ‘that <em> it</em> prevailed nothing.’ The <em> washing of the hands</em> , to betoken innocence from blood-guiltiness, is prescribed <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="de+21:6-9">Deuteronomy 21:6-9</span> , and Pilate uses it here as intelligible to the Jews.
	</p>
<p>The Greeks would have used the gen. after <strong> ἀθῷος</strong> without <strong> ἀπό</strong> <strong> :</strong> so ἀθῷος πληγῶν , Aristoph. Nub. 1413. See Kühner, Gram. ii. p. 164.</p>
