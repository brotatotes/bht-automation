<p><strong> 34.</strong> ] It was customary to give a stupefying drink to criminals on their way to execution: of which our Lord would not partake, having shewn by tasting it, that he was aware of its purpose.
	</p>
<p>In Mark’s account it is ἐσμυρνισμένος οἶνος and though οἶνος and ὄξος might mean the same, ἐσμυρνισμένος and μετὰ χολ . μεμιγ . cannot. We may observe here (and if the remark be applied with caution and reverence, it is a most useful one), how Matt. often adopts in his narrative <em> the very words of prophecy</em> , where one or more of the other Evangelists give the matter of fact detail: see above on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:15">Matthew 26:15</span> , and compare with this verse, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ps+69:21">Psalms 69:21</span> .</p>
