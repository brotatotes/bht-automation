<p><strong> 18. </strong> <strong> προσελθ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] They appear to have first seen Him at a distance, probably on the top of the mountain. This whole introduction, <strong> προσελθ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἐλάλ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> αὐτ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> λέγ</strong> <strong> .,</strong> forbids us to suppose that the following words are a mere compendium of what was said on various occasions. Like the opening of ch. 5, it carries with it a direct assertion that what follows was spoken <em> then, and there</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐδόθη μοι</strong> κ.τ.λ. ] The words are a reference to ref. Dan. (LXX), which compare. <em> Given</em> , by the Father, in the fulfilment of the Eternal Covenant, in the Unity of the Holy Spirit. <em> Now first</em> is this covenant, in its fulness, proclaimed upon earth. The Resurrection was its last seal the Ascension was the <em> taking possession</em> of the Inheritance.
