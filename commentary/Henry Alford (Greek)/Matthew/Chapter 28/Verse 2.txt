<p><strong> 2.</strong> ] This must not be taken as pluperfect, which would be altogether inconsistent with the text.
	</p>
<p><strong> καὶ ἰδοὺ</strong> <strong> … </strong> <strong> ἐγένετο</strong> must mean that the women were <em> witnesses of the earthquake</em> , and <em> that which happened</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> σεισμός</strong> was not <em> properly</em> an earthquake, but was the sudden opening of the tomb by the descending Angel, as the <strong> γάρ</strong> shews. The rolling away was not done naturally, but by a shock, which = σεισμός .
	</p>
<p>It must not be supposed that the Resurrection of our Lord took place <em> at this time</em> , as sometimes imagined, and represented in paintings. It <em> had taken place before</em> ; <strong> ἠγέρθη κ</strong> <strong> .</strong> <strong> τ</strong> <strong> .</strong> <strong> λ</strong> <strong> .</strong> are the words of the Angel. It was not <em> for Him</em> , to whom (see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:19">John 20:19</span> ; Joh 20:26 ) the stone was <em> no hindrance</em> , but <em> for the women and His disciples</em> , that it was rolled away.</p>
