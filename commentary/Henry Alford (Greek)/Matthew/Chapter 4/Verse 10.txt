<p><strong> 10.</strong> ] Our Lord at once repels him openly; not that He did not <em> know him before</em> , but because he had <em> thus openly</em> tempted Him; but not even this of His own power or will; He adds, <em> for</em> it is written, again, as Man, appealing to the Word of God. There does not appear to be sufficient ground for the distinction sometimes set up between the meanings of προσκυνεῖν with the dative and the same verb with the accusative. See, besides reff., <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ge+49:8">Genesis 49:8</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ex+11:8">Exodus 11:8</span> .
	</p>
