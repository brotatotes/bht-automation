<p><strong> 12. </strong> <strong> ἀνεχώρ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] not ‘ <em> returned</em> ,’ but <strong> retired, withdrew;</strong> see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:22">Matthew 2:22</span> , and note. No notice is given <em> whence</em> this withdrawal took place. The narrative is evidently taken up after an interval, and without any intention that it should follow closely on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:11">Matthew 4:11</span> . Wieseler, Chron. Synops. pp. 162 ff., sees in this a proof that St. Matthew recognized a ministry in Judæa during the interval. I cannot quite think this, but certainly he does not <em> exclude</em> it.</p>
