<p><strong> 17. </strong> <strong> ἀπὸ τότε</strong> ] That is, <em> began His ministry in Galilee</em> . The account of Matthew, being that of an eye-witness, begins where his own experience began. It is not correct to suppose, as some of the German Commentators have done, (De Wette, Strauss,) that this preaching of repentance was <em> of a different character from the after-teaching of our Lord;</em> we recognize the same formula, though only partly cited, in ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:7">Matthew 10:7</span> : <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:10">Luke 10:10</span> , and find our Lord <em> still preaching repentance</em> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:3">Luke 13:3</span> , after repeated declarations of His Messiahship.</p>
