<p><strong> 2. </strong> <strong> ἀνοίξας τὸ στ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> αὐ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] as in reff., a solemn introduction to some discourse or advice of importance.

<p><strong> αὐτούς</strong> ] i.e. τοὺς μαθητάς . The discourse (see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:13-14">Matthew 5:13-14</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:20">Matthew 5:20</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:48">Matthew 5:48</span> ; ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:9">Matthew 6:9</span> ; Mat 7:6 ) was spoken directly to the disciples, but (see Mat 7:28-29 ) also generally to the multitudes. It is a divine commentary on the words with which His own and the Baptist’s preaching opened: μετανοεῖτε · ἤγγικεν γὰρ ἡ βασ . τ . οὐρανῶν . It divides itself into various great sections, which see below.</p>
