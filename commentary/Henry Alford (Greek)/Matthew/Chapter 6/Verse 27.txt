<p><strong> 27.</strong> ] These words do not relate to the <em> stature</em> , the adding a cubit to which (= a foot and a half) would be a very great addition, instead of a very small one, as is implied here, and expressed in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:26">Luke 12:26</span> , εἰ οὖν <strong> οὐδὲ ἐλάχιστον</strong> δύνασθε , κ . τ . λ ., but to the <em> time of life</em> of each hearer; as Theophylact on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:26">Luke 12:26</span> , ζωῆς μέτρα παρὰ μόνῳ τῷ θεῷ , καὶ οὐκ αὐτύς τις ἕκαστος ἑαυτῷ ὁριστὴς τῆς ζωῆς . So Hammond, Wolf, Rosenm., Kuinoel, Olsh., De Wette, Meyer, Stier, Tholuck, &amp;c. &amp;c.: and the <em> context</em> seems imperatively to require it: for the object of food and clothing is not to <em> enlarge the body</em> , but to prolong life. The application of measures of space to time is not uncommon. See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ps+39:5">Psalms 39:5</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="job+9:25">Job 9:25</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:7">2 Timothy 4:7</span> . In Stobæus, xcviii. 13, we have cited from Mimnermus, ἡμεῖς δ ʼ οἷά τε φύλλα φύει πολυάνθεμος ὥρη | ἔαρος , ὅτ ʼ αἶψ ʼ αὐγὴ αὔξεται ἠελίου , | τοῖς ἴκελοι , πήχυιον ἐπὶ χρόνον ἄνθεσιν ἥβης | τερπόμεθα . Alcæus (Athen [84] x. 7) says, δάκτυλος ἁμέρα : and Diog. Laert. viii. 16 (Thol.) σπιθαμὴ τοῦ βίου .
