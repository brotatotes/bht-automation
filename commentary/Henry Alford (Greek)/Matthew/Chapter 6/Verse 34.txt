<p><strong> 34. </strong> <strong> ἡ γὰρ αὔρ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] <strong> for the morrow will care for it,</strong> viz. for ἡ αὔριον mentioned above: i.e., <em> will bring care enough about its own matters:</em> implying, “after all your endeavour to avoid worldly cares, you will find quite enough, and more of them when to-morrow comes, about to-morrow itself: do not then increase those of to-day by introducing them before their time.’ A hint, as is the following κακία , that in this state of sin and infirmity the command of Mat 6:31 will never be completely observed.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀρκετὸν</strong> <strong> </strong> <strong> κακία</strong> : thus, οὐκ ἀγαθὸν πολυκοιρανίη , Il. β . 204. And the same construction frequently occurs, both in Greek and Latin authors.</p>
