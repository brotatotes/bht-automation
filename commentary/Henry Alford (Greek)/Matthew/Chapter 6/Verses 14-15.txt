<p><strong> 14, 15.</strong> ] Our Lord returns ( <strong> γάρ</strong> ) to explain the only part of the prayer which <em> peculiarly</em> belonged to the new law of love, and enforces it by a solemn assurance. On the sense, cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:25">Mark 11:25</span> , and the remarkable parallel Sir 28:2 ; ἄφες ἀδίκημα τῷ πλησίον σου , κ . τότε δεηθέντος σου αἱ ἁμαρτίαι σου λυθήσονται . See Chrysostom’s most eloquent appeal on this verse, Hom. xix. 7, p. 255, end.</p>
