<p><strong> 22.</strong> ] <strong> ἐν ἐκείνῃ τῇ ἡμέρᾳ</strong> perhaps refers to <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:19">Matthew 7:19</span> : or it may be the expression so common in the Prophets of <em> the great day of the Lord:</em> e.g. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="isa+2:20">Isaiah 2:20</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="isa+25:9">Isaiah 25:9</span> , a [91] . fr. So the Jews called the great day of judgment “that day,” see Schöttgen, Hor. i. p. 82.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [91] alii = some cursive mss.
	</p>
<p><strong> τῷ σῷ ὀνόματι</strong> perhaps = <strong> ἐν</strong> τ . σ . ὀν ., <em> jussu et auctoritate tua</em> , but better <strong> by thy Name,</strong> that name having, as Meyer, filled out our belief and been the object of our confession of faith. The dative in this case is <em> instrumental</em> , cf. Winer, § 31. 7.
	</p>
