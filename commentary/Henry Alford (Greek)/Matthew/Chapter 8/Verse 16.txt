<p><strong> 16.</strong> ] <em> at sunset</em> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:32">Mark 1:32</span> : <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:40">Luke 4:40</span> . From St. Mark we learn that <em> the whole city was collected at the door;</em> from St. Luke, that <em> the dæmons cried out and said, ‘Thou art Christ the Son of God</em> .’ And from both, that <em> our Lord permitted them not to speak, for they knew Him</em> . They brought the sick in the evening, either because it was cool, or because the day’s work was over, and men could be found to carry them, or perhaps because it was the sabbath (see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:21">Mark 1:21</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:29">Mark 1:29</span> ; Mar 1:32 ), which ended at sunset.</p>
