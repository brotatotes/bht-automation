<p><strong> 20.</strong> ] The <strong> κράσπεδον</strong> <strong> ,</strong> see ref. Num., was the fringe or tassel which the Jews were commanded to wear on each corner of their outer garment, as a sign that they were to be holy unto God. The article, as in ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:36">Matthew 14:36</span> , designates the particular tassel which was touched.</p>
