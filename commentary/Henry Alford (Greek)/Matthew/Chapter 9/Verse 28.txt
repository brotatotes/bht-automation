<p><strong> 28. </strong> <strong> τὴν οἰκίαν</strong> ] εἰκός , πιστοῦ τινος εἶναι τὴν οἰκίαν , εἰς ἣν κατήχθη . Euthym [109] Or, the house which our Lord inhabited at Capernaum (De Wette and others); but I conceive that ἡ οἰκία need not mean any particular house, merely, as we sometimes use the expression, <em> the house</em> , as opposed to <em> the open air:</em> see note on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:1">Matthew 9:1</span> .
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [109] Euthymius Zigabenus, 1116
	</p>
<p><strong> τοῦτο ποιῆσαι</strong> ] i.e. the healing, implied in ἐλέησον ἡμᾶς .
	</p>
<p><strong> υἱὸς Δ</strong> <strong> … </strong> <strong> κύριε</strong> ] See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ps+110:1">Psalms 110:1</span> , and ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:45">Matthew 22:45</span> ; also ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:23">Matthew 12:23</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:30-31">Matthew 20:30-31</span> .
	</p>
<p><em> Touching</em> , or <em> anointing</em> the eyes, was the ordinary method which our Lord took of impressing on the blind the action of the divine power which healed them. Ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:34">Matthew 20:34</span> : <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:25">Mark 8:25</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:6">John 9:6</span> .</p>
