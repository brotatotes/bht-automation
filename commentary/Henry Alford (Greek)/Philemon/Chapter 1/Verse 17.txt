<p><strong> 17</strong> .] takes up again the sentiment (and the construction) broken off at the end of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:12">Philemon 1:12</span> . The κοινωνία referred to is that shewn by the ἀγάπη of him, common to both, mentioned in the last verse: but extending far wider than it, even to the community of faith, and hope, and love between them as Christian men: not that of goods, as Bengel: ‘ut tua sint mea et mea tua.’</p>
