<p><strong> 20</strong> .] <strong> according to</strong> (for it is ‘ <em> our confidence</em> , which hath great recompense of reward,’ <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:35">Hebrews 10:35</span> f.) <strong> my expectation</strong> (not, ‘ <em> earnest</em> expectation,’ which never seems to be the sense of <strong> ἀπό</strong> in composition: still less is ἀπό superfluous: but καραδοκεῖν signifies to <em> ‘attend,’ ‘look out’</em> ( παρὰ τὴν κάραν ὅλην δοκεῖν (‘ <em> observare’</em> ), Thl. ad loc.); and <strong> ἀπό</strong> adds the signification of ‘ <em> from a particular position</em> ,’ or better still that of <em> exhaustion, ‘look out until it be fulfilled</em> ,’ as in ‘ <em> ex</em> pectare,’ <strong> ἀπεκδέχιμαι</strong> <strong> , </strong> <strong> ἀπέχω</strong> , &amp;c. See the word thoroughly discussed the Fritzschiorum Opuscula, p. 150 ff.) <strong> and hope that</strong> (Est., al., take ὅτι argumentatively, <em> because</em> : but thus the expectation and hope will have no explanation, and the flow of the sentence will be broken) <strong> in nothing</strong> (in no point, no particular, see ref. It should be kept quite indefinite, not specified as Chrys. ( κἂν ὁτιοῦν γένηται ). ‘ <em> In none</em> ’ (of those to whom the Gospel is preached) as Hoelemann, is beside the purpose no <em> persons</em> are adduced, but only the most general considerations) <strong> I shall be ashamed</strong> (general: have reason to take shame for my work for God, or His work in me), <strong> but</strong> (on the contrary: but perhaps after the ἐν οὐδενί this need not be pressed) <strong> in all</strong> (as contrasted with ἐν οὐδενί above) <strong> boldness</strong> (contrast to <em> shame</em> : boldness <em> on my part</em> , seeing that life or death are both alike glorious for me and thus I, my body, the passive instrument in which Christ is glorified, shall any-how be bold and of good cheer in this His glorification of Himself in me) <strong> as always, now also</strong> (that I am in the situation described above, Php 1:17 ) <strong> Christ shall be magnified</strong> ( δειχθήσεται ὅς ἐστι , Thdrt.: by His Kingdom being spread among men. So Ellicott, saying rightly that it is more than ‘ <em> praised</em> ,’ as in my earlier editions) <strong> in my body</strong> ( <em> my body</em> being the <em> subject</em> of <em> life</em> or <em> death</em> , in the occurrence of either of which he would not be ashamed, the one bringing active service for Christ, the other union with Him in heaven, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="php+1:21">Philippians 1:21</span> ff.), <strong> either by</strong> (means of) <strong> life or by</strong> (means of) <strong> death</strong> .</p>
