<p><strong> 26</strong> .] <strong> that your matter of boasting</strong> (not, as Chr., ‘ <em> mine in you</em> :’ nor, as commonly rendered, ‘ <em> your boasting</em> ’ ( καύχησις ). Their Christian matter of boasting in him was, the possession of the Gospel, which they had received from him, which would abound, be assured and increased, by his presence among them) <strong> may abound in Christ Jesus</strong> (its field, element of increase, it being a Christian matter of glorying) <strong> in me</strong> (its field, element, of <em> abounding in Christ Jesus</em> , I being the worker of that which furnishes this material) <strong> by means of my presence again with you</strong> .</p>
