<p><strong> 3</strong> .] See the similar expressions, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:9">Romans 1:9</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:4">1 Corinthians 1:4</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:16">Ephesians 1:16</span> ; Col 1:3 ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:2">1 Thessalonians 1:2</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:4">Philemon 1:4</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐπί</strong> here with a dative is hardly distinguishable in English from the same preposition with a genitive in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:9">Romans 1:9</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:16">Ephesians 1:16</span> ; <strong> at</strong> , or <strong> in</strong> : the primitive idea of such construction being <em> addition</em> by close adherence: ‘my whole remembrance of you is <em> accompanied with</em> thanks to God.’ <strong> πάση τῇ μνείᾳ</strong> must not be rendered as in E. V. (so even Conyb.) ‘ <em> every remembrance</em> ,’ but <strong> my whole remembrance</strong> . The expression <em> comprehends in one</em> all such remembrances: but the article forbids the above rendering: cf. πᾶσα ἡ πόλις , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:10">Matthew 21:10</span> ; also ib. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:29">Matthew 6:29</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:1">Mark 4:1</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:3">Luke 3:3</span> ; Winer, § 18. 4. Some (Maldon., Bretschn., al.) take <strong> ἐπί</strong> as assigning the reason for <strong> εὐχαριστῶ</strong> (as 1Co 1:4 ), and <strong> μνείᾳ ὑμῶν</strong> as meaning, ‘ <em> your remembrance of me</em> ,’ viz. in sending me sustenance. But this is evidently wrong: for the ground of εὐχαριστῶ follows, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="php+1:5">Philippians 1:5</span> . <strong> μνεία</strong> here, <strong> remembrance</strong> , not ‘ <em> mention</em> ,’ which meaning it only gets by ποιεῖσθαι being joined to it, ‘to make an <em> act of</em> remembrance,’ i.e. to <em> mention</em> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:9">Romans 1:9</span> ; Eph 1:16 ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:2">1 Thessalonians 1:2</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:4">Philemon 1:4</span> .</p>
