<p><strong> 21</strong> .] <strong> for all (my present companions)</strong> (who these were, we know not: they are characterized, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="php+4:21">Philippians 4:21</span> , merely as οἱ σὺν ἐμοὶ ἀδελφοί certainly not Luke whether Demas, in transition between Phm 1:24 and <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:10">2 Timothy 4:10</span> , we cannot say) <strong> seek their own matters, not those of Jesus Christ</strong> (no weakening of the assertion must be thought of, as that of rendering οἱ πάντες , <em> many</em> , or <em> most</em> , or understanding the assertion, <em> care more about &amp;c. than &amp;c.</em> , as many Commentators: nor must it be restricted to the <em> love of ease</em> , &amp;c., <em> unwillingness to undertake so long a journey</em> , as Chr., Œc, Thl.: both οἱ πάντες and the assertion are absolute).</p>
