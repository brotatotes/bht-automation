<p><strong> 19</strong> .] an assurance taken up from τῷ θεῷ above, <strong> μου</strong> because he (Paul) was the receiver: this was his return to them: ‘qui quod servo ejus datur remunerabitur.’ Beng.
	</p>
<p><strong> πληρώσει</strong> <strong> …</strong> all refers to <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="php+4:16">Philippians 4:16</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="php+4:18">Philippians 4:18</span> ; as ye πεπληρώκατέ μου τὴν χρείαν . It is an <em> assurance</em> , not a <em> wish</em> (- σαι ). <strong> πᾶσαν</strong> , not only in the department alluded to, but in <strong> all</strong> . Meyer refers to the beatitudes in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:0">Matthew 5:0</span> and especially St. Luke’s χορτασθήσεσθε and γελάσετε , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:21">Luke 6:21</span> , as illustrative.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐν δόξῃ</strong> ] to be connected with <strong> πληρώσει</strong> , not with <strong> τὸ πλοῦτος αὐτοῦ</strong> : not, <em> gloriously</em> , as many Commentators, which is weak and flat in the extreme: but <strong> δόξα</strong> is the instrument and element by and in which ‘all your need’ will be supplied: <strong> in glory</strong> , cf. Ps. 16:15 LXX: but not only at the coming of Christ (as Meyer, according to his wont), but in the whole glorious imparting to you of the unsearchable riches of Christ, begun and carried on here, and completed at that day.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐν χριστῷ Ἰησοῦ</strong> ] and this filling (or, ‘this glory,’ but then <em> perhaps</em> τῇ would have been expressed) is, consists, and finds its sphere and element, <strong> in Christ Jesus</strong> .</p>
