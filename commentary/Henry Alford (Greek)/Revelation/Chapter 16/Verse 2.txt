<p><strong> 2</strong> .] <strong> And the first departed</strong> (each angel, as his turn comes, leaves the heavenly scene, and from the space between heaven and earth, empties his vial on the appointed object) <strong> and poured out his vial into the earth</strong> (the γῆ , which before in Rev 16:1 was general, is now particular, and correlative with the objects of the other vials, cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="re+16:2-3">Revelation 16:2-3</span> , εἰς τὴν θάλασσαν , εἰς τοὺς ποταμούς ): <strong> and there came</strong> (took place: <em> fell</em> , as E. V.) <strong> an evil</strong> ( <strong> κακόν</strong> , in itself) <strong> and painful</strong> ( <strong> πονηρόν</strong> , to the sufferers, ἐπίπονον , Suidas. See reff.) <strong> sore upon the men that had the mark of the beast and that worshipped his image</strong> (see above ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="re+13:15-17">Revelation 13:15-17</span> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="re+14:9-10">Revelation 14:9-10</span> . The allegorical and historical interpretations have been very various: see them in Elliott, vol. iv. Notice the parallel with the sixth Egyptian plague, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ex+9:8">Exodus 9:8</span> ff.
