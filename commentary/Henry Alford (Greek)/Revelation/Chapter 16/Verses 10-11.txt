<p><strong> 10, 11</strong> .] <strong> And the fifth poured out his vial upon the throne of the beast</strong> (given to it by the dragon, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="re+13:2">Revelation 13:2</span> . That is, on the spot where the power and presence of the beast had its proper residence): <strong> and his kingdom</strong> (those lands which owned his rule) <strong> became darkened</strong> (as in the ninth Egyptian plague, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ex+10:21">Exodus 10:21</span> ff., the darkness is specially sent over the land, not occasioned by any failure of the lights of heaven). <strong> And they</strong> (the inhabitants: the subjects of the beast. They are by and by identified with those who had received his mark) <strong> chewed their tongues</strong> (the word μασάομαι is confined to the comedians and later Gr. prose. ἡ τῶν γλωσσῶν μάσησις τὴν ὑπερβολὴν τῆς ὀδύνης δηλοῖ , says Andreas) <strong> from</strong> ( <strong> ἐκ</strong> , of the source of the action: see reff.) <strong> their pain</strong> (viz. under which they were previously suffering: not, that occasioned by the darkness, which would not of itself occasion pain: see below), <strong> and blasphemed the God of heaven</strong> (see ch. Rev 11:13 ) <strong> by reason of</strong> ( <strong> ἐκ</strong> as above) <strong> their pains and their sores</strong> (these words bind on this judgment to that of the first and following vials, and shew that they are cumulative, not simply successive. The sores, and pains before mentioned, are still in force), <strong> and repented not of</strong> (see ch. Rev 9:20-21 ) <strong> their works</strong> .</p>
