<p><strong> 17 19</strong> .] <em> The lamentation of the shipmasters, &amp;c</em> . <strong> And every pilot and every one who saileth any whither</strong> (the same expression, without the preposition, is found in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:2">Acts 27:2</span> . The words here import, all sailors from place to place), <strong> and sailors and as many as make traffic of the sea</strong> ( τ . θάλασσαν ἐργάζεσθαι , ‘mare exercere,’ to live by seafaring, is abundantly illustrated by Wetst, from the classics and later writers), <strong> stood afar off, and cried out when they saw the smoke of her burning, saying, Who is like to</strong> <strong> the great city? And they cast</strong> [ <strong> on</strong> ] <strong> earth upon their heads</strong> (see besides ref. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eze+27:30">Ezekiel 27:30</span> ; also <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1sa+4:12">1 Samuel 4:12</span> ; 2Sa 1:2 ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2sa+13:19">2 Samuel 13:19</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2sa+15:32">2 Samuel 15:32</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="job+2:12">Job 2:12</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="la+2:10">Lamentations 2:10</span> ; and the numerous references in Winer, art. Trauer), <strong> and cried out weeping and mourning, saying, Woe, woe, the great city, in</strong> ( <strong> ἐν</strong> is ambiguous at first appearance: but from what follows it cannot be merely local, as E. V. “ <em> wherein</em> ,” but must be of the conditional element in which: “ <em> whereby</em> ” would more nearly give it in our idiom) <strong> which all who have their ships in the sea became rich out of her costliness</strong> (her costly treasures: concrete meaning for the abstract term): <strong> for in one hour she hath been laid waste</strong> .</p>
