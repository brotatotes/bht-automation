<p><strong> 5</strong> .] <strong> For</strong> (proof of the impossibility of legal righteousness, as declared even in the law itself) <strong> Moses describes</strong> (reff.) <strong> the righteousness which is of</strong> (abstr. not implying that it has ever been attained, but rather presupposing the contrary) <strong> the law, that</strong> ( ὅτι recitantis, not γράφ . ὅτι , in which case we should have αὐτήν . The <em> eam</em> of some versions has apparently arisen from misunderstanding ὅτι ) <strong> the man who hath done them</strong> (the ordinances of the law) <strong> shall live in</strong> (in the strength of, by means of, as his status) <strong> it</strong> (the righteousness accruing by such doing of them).
	</p>
