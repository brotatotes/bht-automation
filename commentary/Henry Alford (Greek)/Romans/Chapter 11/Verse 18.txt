<p><strong> 18.</strong> ] <strong> do not boast against the branches</strong> (which were broken off): <strong> but if thou boastest against them</strong> (know that … or let this consideration humble thee, that … Similarly <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:16">1 Corinthians 11:16</span> , εἰ δέ τις δοκεῖ φιλόνεικος εἶναι , ἡμεῖς τοιαύτην συνήθειαν οὐκ ἔχομεν , κ . τ . λ . See Winer, edn. 6, § 66.1 a) <strong> it is not thou that bearest the root, but the root thee</strong> . The ground of humiliation is “Thou partakest of thy blessings solely by union with God’s spiritual church, which church has for its root that Father of the faithful, from whom they are descended. Regard them not therefore with scorn.” This is expanded further in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:20">Romans 11:20</span> .</p>
