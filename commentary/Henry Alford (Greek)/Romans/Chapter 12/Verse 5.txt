<p><strong> 5. </strong> <strong> τὸ δὲ καθ</strong> <strong> ʼ </strong> <strong> εἷς</strong> ] <strong> But [severally</strong> , i.e.] <strong> as regards individuals</strong> . A solœcism for τὸ δὲ εἷς καθ ʼ ἕνα , as ἓν καθ ʼ ἕν in ref. Rev. Wetst., on ref. Mark, gives many examples of it.
	</p>
<p><strong> Members of one another</strong> = fellow-members with one another, members of the body of which we one with another are members.</p>
