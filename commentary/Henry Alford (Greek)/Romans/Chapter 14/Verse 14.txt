<p><strong> 14.</strong> ] <em> The general principle laid down</em> , that <em> nothing is by its own means</em> , i.e. for any thing in itself ( φύσει , Chrys.), <em> unclean, but only in reference to him who reckons it to be so</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> πέπεισμ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἐν κυρ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> Ἰησ</strong> .] These words give to the persuasion the weight, not merely of Paul’s own λογίζομαι , but of apostolic authority. He is persuaded, in his capacity as connected with Christ Jesus, <em> as having the mind of Christ</em> .</p>
