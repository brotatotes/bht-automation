<p><strong> 16.</strong> ] <em> Your strength of faith</em> (Orig [121] , Calv., Beza, Grot., Estius, Bengel, Olsh., al., interpret <strong> τὸ ἀγ</strong> . ‘ <em> your freedom</em> ,’ as in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:29">1 Corinthians 10:29</span> ; but here the contrast is between the <em> weak</em> and the <em> strong</em> : so De W. Chrys. leaves it doubtful: ἢ τὴν πίστιν φησίν , ἢ τὴν μέλλουσαν ἐλπίδα τῶν ἐπάθλων , ἢ τὴν ἀπηρτισμένην εὐσέβειαν ) <em> is a good thing; let it not pass into bad repute</em> : use it so that it may be honoured, and encourage others.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [121] Origen, b. 185, d. 254</p>
