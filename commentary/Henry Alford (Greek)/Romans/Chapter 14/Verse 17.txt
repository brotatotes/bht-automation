<p><strong> 17.</strong> ] For it is not worth while to let it be disgraced and become useless for such a trifle; for no part of the advance of Christ’s gospel can be bound up in, or <em> consist in, meat and drink</em> : but in <strong> righteousness</strong> ( ὁ ἐνάρετος βίος , Chrys., but of course to be taken in union with the doctrine of the former part of the Epistle <em> righteousness by justification, bringing forth the fruits of faith</em> , which would be hindered by faith itself being disturbed), <strong> and peace</strong> ( ἡ πρὸς τὸν ἀδελφὸν εἰρήνη , ᾗ ἐναντιοῦται αὕτη ἡ φιλονεικία , id.) <strong> and joy</strong> ( ἡ ἐκ τῆς ὁμονοίας χαρά , ἣν ἀναιρεῖ αὕτη ἡ ἐπίπληξις , id.) in the <strong> Holy Ghost:</strong> in connexion with, under the indwelling and influence of, as χαίρετε ἐν κυρίῳ ( Php 4:4 ) and the expressions ἐν κυρ ., ἐν χριστῷ , generally: not, as De W., ‘ <em> joy which has its ground in the Holy Ghost</em> ,’ though this is <em> true</em> . So, on the other hand, a man under the influence of, possessed by an evil spirit, is called ἄνθρωπος ἐν πνεύματι ἀκαθάρτῳ , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:23">Mark 1:23</span> .</p>
