<p><strong> 6.</strong> ] The words in brackets were probably omitted from the similar ending φρονεῖ of both clauses having misled some early copyists; but perhaps it may have been intentionally done, after the observation of the Lord’s Day came to be regarded as binding.
	</p>
<p><strong> φρονῶν</strong> , <strong> taking account of</strong> , ‘regarding.’
	</p>
<p><strong> εὐχαριστεῖ</strong> , adduced as a practice of both parties, shews the universality among the early Christians of <em> thanking God at meals</em> : see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:3-4">1 Timothy 4:3-4</span> . The εὐχαριστία of the μὴ ἐσθίων was over his ‘dinner of herbs.’
	</p>
<p><strong> κυρίῳ</strong> is CHRIST.</p>
