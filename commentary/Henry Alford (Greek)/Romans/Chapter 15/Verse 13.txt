<p><strong> 13.</strong> ] The hortatory part of the Epistle, as well as the preceding section of it ( Rom 15:5 ), concludes with a solemn wish for the spiritual welfare of the Roman church.
	</p>
<p>The words <strong> τῆς ἐλπίδος</strong> connect with ἐλπιοῦσι of the foregoing verse, as was the case with τῆς ὑπομονῆς κ . τῆς παρακλήσεως in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:5">Romans 15:5</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> χαρᾶς κ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> εἰρήνης</strong> , as the happy result of faith in God, and unanimity with one another; see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:17">Romans 14:17</span> .
	</p>
<p>Rom 15:14 <strong> to <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:27">Romans 16:27</span></strong> <strong> .</strong> ] CONCLUSION OF THE EPISTLE. PERSONAL NOTICES, RESPECTING THE APOSTLE HIMSELF ( Rom 15:14-33 ), RESPECTING THOSE GREETED ( Rom 16:1-16 ), AND GREETING: TOGETHER WITH WARNINGS AGAINST THOSE WHO MADE DIVISIONS AMONG THEM ( Rom 16:16-23 ); AND CONCLUDING DOXOLOGY ( Rom 16:24-27 ).</p>
