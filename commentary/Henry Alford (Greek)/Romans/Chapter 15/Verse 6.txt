<p><strong> 6. </strong> <strong> τὸν θεὸν κ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> πατ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] De Wette regards τὸν θεὸν as independent of Ἰησοῦ χρ ., ‘ <em> God, and the Father of our Lord Jesus Christ</em> .’ The usage of the article will not decide the matter, because on either rendering, the accusatives both refer to the same Person: but the ordinary one, <strong> the God and Father …</strong> is preferable on account of its simplicity.</p>
