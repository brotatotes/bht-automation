<p><strong> 2.</strong> ] <strong> ἐν κυρίῳ</strong> , <em> in a Christian manner</em> , as mindful of your common Lord: <strong> ἀξίως τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἁγίων</strong> , ‘ <em> in a manner worthy of saints</em> ;’ i.e. ‘ <em> as saints ought to do</em> ,’ refers to προσδέξησθε , and therefore to <em> their</em> conduct to her; not, ‘as saints <em> ought to be received</em> .’
	</p>
<p><strong> παραστῆτε</strong> ] Her business at Rome may have been such as to require the help of those resident there.
	</p>
<p><strong> προστάτις πολλῶν</strong> ] This may refer to a part of the deaconess’s office, the attending on the poor and sick of her own sex.
	</p>
<p><strong> κ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἐμοῦ αὐτοῦ</strong> ] when and where, we know not. It is not improbable that she may have been, like Lydia, one whose heart the Lord opened at the first preaching of Paul, and whose house was his lodging.</p>
