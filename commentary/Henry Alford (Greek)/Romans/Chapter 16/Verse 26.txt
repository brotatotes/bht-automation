<p><strong> 26.</strong> ] See ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:2">Romans 1:2</span> . The prophetic writings were the storehouse out of which the preachers of the gospel took their demonstrations that Jesus was the Christ: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:28">Acts 18:28</span> ; more especially, it is true, <em> to the Jews</em> , who however are here included among πάντα τὰ ἔθνη .
	</p>
<p><strong> κατ</strong> <strong> ʼ </strong> <strong> ἐπιταγ</strong> . may refer either to the <em> prophetic writings</em> being drawn up by the command of God, or to the <em> manifestation of the mystery by the preachers of the gospel</em> thus taking place. The latter seems best to suit the sense. <strong> αἰωνίου</strong> refers back to χρ . αἰωνίοις [the word should have been kept scrupulously the same in English, not as here and in Mat 22:46 rendered by two different English terms].
	</p>
<p>The first εἰς indicates the <em> aim</em> in order to their becoming obedient to the faith: the second, the <em> local extent</em> of the manifestation.</p>
