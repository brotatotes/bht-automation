<p><strong> 26.</strong> <strong> πρὸς τὴν ἔνδ</strong> . κ . τ . λ .] The art. distinguishes this ἔνδειξις from the former, as the <em> fuller and ultimate object</em> , of which that ἔνδειξις was a subordinate part: <strong> with a view to the</strong> (or His) <strong> manifestation of his righteousness in this present time</strong> . The shewing forth that He was righteous throughout His dealings with the whole world, by means of setting forth an adequate and complete propitiation in the death of Christ, was <em> towards</em> , formed a subsidiary manifestation to, His <em> great manifestation of His righteousness</em> (same sense as before, <em> judicial</em> righteousness, <em> justice</em> ) <em> under the Gospel</em> . The joining πρὸς τὴν ἔνδειξ . κ . τ . λ . with ἐν τῇ ἀνοχῇ τ . θεοῦ (Beza, Rückert <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ex+2:0">Exodus 2:0</span> , Thol., al.) would draw off the attention from the leading thought of the sentence to a digression respecting the ἀνοχὴ τ . θ ., which is not probable.
	</p>
<p><strong> εἰς τὸ εἶναι</strong> κ . τ . λ .] <strong> in order that He may be</strong> ( <em> shewn to be</em> : the whole present concern is with ἔνδειξις , the <em> exhibition to men</em> of the righteousness of God) <strong> just and</strong> (yet, on the other side) <strong> justifying him who is of (the) faith in Jesus</strong> ( τὸν ἐκ πίστ . Ἰης ., him who belongs to, stands in, works from as his standing-point, faith in Jesus: see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:8">Romans 2:8</span> , note, and reff.).</p>
