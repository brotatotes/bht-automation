<p><strong> 6. </strong> <strong> λέγ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τὸν μακ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] <strong> pronounces the blessedness</strong> , ‘the <em> congratulation</em> :’ in allusion perhaps to the Heb. form, אַשְׁרֵי ‘( <em> O) the blessings of</em> ,’.… It is very clear that this righteousness must be χωρὶς ἔργων , because its imputation consists in the remission and hiding of <em> offences</em> , whereas none can be legally righteous in whom there is any, even the smallest offence.</p>
