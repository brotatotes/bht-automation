<p>The form of the question, with ἢ καί , presupposes an affirmative answer to the latter clause; which affirmative answer is then made the ground of the argumentation in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:10-12">Romans 4:10-12</span> : <strong> On the uncircumcision</strong> (-cised) <strong> also. For we say</strong> , &amp;c. The stress is on <strong> τῷ Ἀβραάμ</strong> , not on <strong> ἡ πίστις</strong> <strong> : for we say that</strong> TO ABRAHAM <strong> faith was reckoned for righteousness</strong> .</p>
