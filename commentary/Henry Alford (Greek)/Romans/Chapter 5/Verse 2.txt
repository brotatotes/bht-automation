<p><strong> 2.</strong> ] <strong> Through whom we have also</strong> (so διὸ [ καί ], ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:24">Romans 1:24</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:22">Romans 4:22</span> , where καί , if read, serves to shew the coherence and likelihood of that which is asserted, answering almost to our ‘as might be expected’) <strong> had our access</strong> (the persons spoken of having come to the Father by Christ, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:18">Ephesians 2:18</span> , the access is treated of as a thing past. τῇ πίστει and ἐν τῇ πίστει appear to have been glosses, explanatory of the method of access. The access would <em> normally</em> take place in baptism) <strong> into this grace</strong> (namely, the grace of justification, apprehended and held fast subjectively (from what follows); not, τὸ πάντων ἐπιτυχεῖν τῶν διὰ βαπτίσματος ἀγαθῶν (Chrys. al.), which is inconsistent with ἐν ᾗ ἑστήκ .: not, ‘ <em> the Gospel</em> ’ (Fritz.), for the same reason; not, ‘ <em> hope of blessedness</em> ’ (Beza), for that <em> follows</em> : least of all ‘ <em> the grace of the apostolic calling</em> ’ (Semler), which is quite beside the purpose) <strong> wherein we Stand</strong> (see parallels in reff. 1 and 2 Cor.; i.e. <em> abide accepted and acquitted with God</em> ; see also <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:12">1 Corinthians 10:12</span> , and ch. Rom 11:20 ); <strong> and</strong> (couple to εἰρήν . ἔχωμεν , not to ἐν ᾖ ἑστήκ .) <strong> glory in the hope</strong> ( καυχάομαι is found with ἐπί , ἐν , περί , ὑπέρ , and (Thol.) with an acc. of the object. In Heb 3:6 we have τὸ καύχημα τῆς ἐλπίδος ) <strong> of the glory of God</strong> (of sharing God’s glory by being with Christ in His kingdom, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:24">John 17:24</span> , see reff.).</p>
