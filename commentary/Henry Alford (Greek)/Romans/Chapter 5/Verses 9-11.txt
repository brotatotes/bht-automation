<p><strong> 9 11</strong> .] The Apostle further <em> shews the blessed fruits of justification</em> , viz. <em> salvation</em> , both <em> from wrath</em> , and <em> with life</em> . The <em> argument</em> proceeds from the beginning of the chapter: but the <em> connexion</em> , as so frequent with St. Paul, is immediately with the parenthetical sentences just preceding. <strong> Much more then</strong> (if He <em> died</em> for us when <em> sinners</em> , a fortiori will He <em> save</em> us now that we are <em> righteous</em> by virtue of that His death) <strong> having been now justified by His blood</strong> (see remarks on ch. Rom 4:25 ) <strong> we shall be saved by Him from the wrath</strong> ( <em> to come</em> , or <em> of which we know</em> : force of the art.).</p>
