<p><strong> 15.</strong> ] <strong> τί οὖν</strong> (sc. ἐστίν ); = τί οὖν ἐροῦμεν ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:1">Romans 6:1</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἁμαρτήσωμεν</strong> ] <em> Must we imagine that we may sin</em> ? <strong> may we sin</strong> ? the aor. because he is speaking of <em> committing acts of sin</em> [not of a habit of living in sin, although that would be induced by such acts]: on the deliberative subjunctive, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:1">Romans 6:1</span> .
	</p>
<p>This question is not, any more than that of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:1">Romans 6:1</span> , put into the mouth of an objector, but is part of the Apostle’s own discourse, arising out of what has preceded, and answered by him in the following verses.</p>
