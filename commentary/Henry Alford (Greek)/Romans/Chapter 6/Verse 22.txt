<p><strong> 22.</strong> ] Contrast of your present state to that former one: <strong> freedom from sin as a master, servitude</strong> (compare ἀνθρώπινον λέγω , Rom 6:19 ) <strong> to God</strong> (a higher description than merely δικαιοσύνη , the actual antithesis to ἁμαρτία , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:18">Romans 6:18</span> . <em> The devil</em> would be the corresponding antithetical power: and not unfrequently appears in the teaching of Paul: but usually in casual expressions, as <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:27">Ephesians 4:27</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:11">Ephesians 6:11</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:26">2 Timothy 2:26</span> , not as the principal figure in a course of argument), <strong> fruit</strong> (see on καρπός , above, ver.21, and remark τὸν καρπόν , <em> your fruit</em> , fruit actually brought forth, q. d. ἔχετε καρπόν , καὶ ὁ καρπὸς ὑμῶν ἁγιασμός ) <strong> unto</strong> (leading unto perfect) <strong> sanctification, and the end</strong> (governed by ἔχετε ) <strong> life everlasting</strong> .</p>
