<p><strong> 2.</strong> ] <strong> For</strong> (a reason why there is no condemnation) <strong> the law</strong> ( <em> norma</em> , method = influence, as in ἕτερον νόμον , ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:23">Romans 7:23</span> , used here perhaps for sharper contrast to the νόμος ἁμαρτ . below) <strong> of the Spirit of life</strong> (the Lord and Giver of life life used in an incipient higher sense than ἔζων in ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:9">Romans 7:9</span> , see below) <strong> freed me</strong> (aor., referring to the time of his conversion. There is no stronger proof to my mind of the identity of the speaker in the first person throughout with the Apostle himself, than this extension of that form of speaking into this chapter: nothing more clearly shews, that there he was describing a really existing state within himself, but insulating, and as it were <em> exaggerating</em> it (as so often), to bring out more clearly the glorious deliverance to follow. If σε be read, the address is a general one to the reader, leading on to the ἡμῖν below: and the foregoing argument does not apply) <strong> in Jesus Christ</strong> (I follow the more regular grammatical arrangement in taking ἐν χρ . Ἰης . with the verb. Thus also Thol. and De Wette.
	</p>
<p>It may be taken (notwithstanding the absence of the art., at which indeed only tiros will stumble) with ζωῆς , as Luther, which seems to suit ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:23">Romans 6:23</span> , or with τοῦπν . τ . ζ ., as Piscator and Flatt, or with ὁ νόμ . τ . π . τ . ζ ., as Calv.) <strong> from the law of sin</strong> ( Rom 7:25 ) <strong> and death</strong> ( <em> death</em> again here bears a higher meaning than in ch. 7. <em> We are now on higher ground</em> : <strong> κατάκριμα</strong> having been mentioned, which is the punishment of sin, death now <em> involves that</em> , and is not only temporal misery, but eternal ruin also.
	</p>
<p>This ‘ <em> law of the Spirit of life</em> ’ having freed him from the law of sin and death, so that he serves another master, <em> all claim of sin on him is at an end</em> he is acquitted, and there is no condemnation for him).</p>
