<p><strong> 1</strong> .] <strong> But</strong> (contrast to the persons just described: ‘on the other hand’) <strong> do thou speak</strong> (not what they speak, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:11">Titus 1:11</span> ; but) <strong> the things which befit the healthy teaching</strong> (that teaching which is sound and wholesome, not teaching ἃ μὴ δεῖ ): viz., <strong> that the aged men</strong> (not = πρεσβυτέρους , which implies eldership, and not old age only) <strong> be sober</strong> (see note on 1Ti 3:2 ), <strong> grave</strong> (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:4">1 Timothy 3:4</span> , note), <strong> self-restrained</strong> (a better word for σώφρων would be a valuable discovery: see above on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:8">Titus 1:8</span> , and <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:9">1 Timothy 2:9</span> ; ‘ <em> discreet</em> ’ is good, but not adequate), <strong> healthy in their faith, in their love, in their patience</strong> (see ref. 1 Tim., where the same three are joined together. The datives are of the element or condition: the same was expressed with ἐν , ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:13">Titus 1:13</span> ; ἵνα ὑγιαίνωσιν ἐν τῇ πίστει . The articles should not be overlooked. The occurrence of τῇ ἀγάπῃ and τῇ ὑπομονῇ prevents us from rendering τῇ πίστει objective as in ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:13">Titus 1:13</span> , and compels us to take the subjective and reflective meaning).</p>
