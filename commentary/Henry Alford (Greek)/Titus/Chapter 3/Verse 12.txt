<p><strong> 12</strong> .] <strong> Whenever I shall have sent</strong> ( πέμψω , not fut. ind. but aor. subj.) <strong> Artemas</strong> (not elsewhere named: tradition makes him afterwards bishop of Lystra) <strong> to thee, or Tychicus</strong> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:21">Ephesians 6:21</span> , note: Col 4:7 ), <strong> hasten</strong> (make it thine earnest care) <strong> to come to me to Nicopolis</strong> (on the question which of the three cities of this name is here meant, see Prolegg. to Pastoral Epistles, § ii. 30, note): <strong> for there I have determined to spend the winter. Forward on their journey</strong> ((see below) the word here has the sense of ‘enable to proceed forward,’ viz. by furnishing with necessaries for the journey: so in ref. 3 John) <strong> with zeal Zenas the lawyer</strong> ( Ζηνᾶς = Ζηνόδωρος . Probably a Jewish scribe or jurist (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:35">Matthew 22:35</span> , note) who had been converted, and to whom the name of his former occupation still adhered, as in the case of Ματθαῖος ὁ τελώνης . Hippolytus and Dorotheus number him among the seventy disciples, and make him to have been subsequently bishop of Diospolis. There is an apocryphal ‘Acts of Titus’ bearing his name. Winer, Realw.) <strong> and Apollos</strong> (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:24">Acts 18:24</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:12">1 Corinthians 1:12</span> ; 1Co 16:12 ), <strong> that nothing may be wanting to them</strong> .</p>
