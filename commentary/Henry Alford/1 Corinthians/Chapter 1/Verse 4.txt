<p><strong> 4.</strong> <strong> τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> θεῷ μου</strong> ] so in reff. Rom. Phil.
	</p>
<p><strong> πάντοτε</strong> ] expanded in Php 1:4 into πάντοτε ἐν πάσῃ δεήσει μου .
	</p>
<p>The ἡ χάρις ἡ δοθεῖσα = τὰ χαρίσματα τὰ δοθέντα (see below on 1Co 1:7 ) a metonymy which has passed so completely into our common parlance, as to be almost lost sight of as such. ‘ <em> Grace</em> ’ is properly <em> in God</em> : the <em> gifts of grace</em> in us, given by that grace.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐν</strong> ] not, as Chrys., Theophyl., Œcum., for διὰ , [nor = <em> by</em> as E. V.,] but as usually in this connexion, <strong> in Christ</strong> , i.e. <em> to you as members of Christ</em> . So also below.</p>
