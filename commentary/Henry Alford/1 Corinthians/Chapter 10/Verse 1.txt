<p><strong> 1.</strong> ] <strong> γάρ</strong> joins to the preceding. He had been inculcating the necessity of <em> self-subduing</em> (ch. 1Co 9:24-27 ), and now enforces it in the particular departments of <em> abstaining from fornication, idolatry, &amp;c</em> ., by the example of the Jews of old.
	</p>
<p><strong> οὐ θέλω</strong> <strong> …</strong> , see reff.
	</p>
<p><strong> οἱ πατ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἡμῶν</strong> ] He uses this expression, not merely speaking for himself and his Jewish converts, but regarding the Christian church as a continuation of the Jewish, and the believer as the true descendant of Abraham.
	</p>
<p><strong> πάντες</strong> <strong> … </strong> <strong> πάντες</strong> <strong> … </strong> <strong> πάντες</strong> , each time with strong emphasis, as opposed to τοῖς πλείοσιν , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:5">1 Corinthians 10:5</span> . ALL had these privileges, as <em> all of you</em> have their counterparts under the Gospel: but <em> most of them</em> failed from rebellion and unbelief.
	</p>
<p><strong> ὑπὸ τὴν νεφ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἦσαν</strong> ] The pillar of cloud, the abode of the divine Presence, went before them, and was to them a <em> defence</em> : hence it is sometimes treated of as <em> covering</em> the camp, e.g. Ps. 104:39, διεπέτασε νεφέλην εἰς σκέπην αὐτοῖς : and thus they would be <em> under</em> it. So also Wis 10:17 ; Wis 19:7 , ἡ τὴν παρεμβολὴν σκιάζουσα νεφέλη . See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ex+13:21">Exodus 13:21</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ex+14:20">Exodus 14:20</span> .</p>
