The allegory is obviously not to be pressed minutely: for neither did they enter the cloud, nor were they<i> wetted by the waters of the sea</i>; but they<i> passed</i><i> under</i> both, as the baptized passes under the water, and it was said of them, Exod. xiv. 31, “T<i> hen the people feared the Lord, and believed the Lord, and his servant Moses.</i>”<br><br><b>32.</b>]<i> all offence</i><i> is to be avoided</i> (it being understood that this refers to<b> things indifferent</b>, for in<i> other things</i>, both Jews and Greeks<i> must be offended</i>, see ch. i. 23),<i> whether to Jews or Heathens</i> (both these out of the Church),<i> or to the Church of God</i> (their own brethren).
