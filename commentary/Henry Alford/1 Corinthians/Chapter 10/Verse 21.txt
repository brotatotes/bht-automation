<b> 21.</b>]<i> Reason of the wish,</i>—sententiously expressed without any “<i>for.</i>”<br><br><b> Ye cannot</b> applies of course to the<i> real spiritual participation</i> of the table of the Lord so as to profit by it: to<i> moral</i> possibility. The<b> cup of devils</b> is said, as corresponding to the cup ot which mention has been already made, not as some fancy, referring to the<i> libation</i> at an idol feast.<br><br><b> the table</b> is used in the sense of<i> the meats laid on the table.</i> From this passage probably, the “<i>Table of the Lord</i>” became an expression current in all ages of the Christian Church.
