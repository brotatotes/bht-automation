<b> 27.</b>] The same maxim applied to their conduct at<i> a banquet given by a heathen.</i> A<i> miscellaneous banquet</i>, and not a sacrificial feast, is meant. At such, there might be meat which had been offered to idols. Grotius says well on the words,<b> ye are disposed to go</b>, he admonishes them tacitly that they would do better by not going: but he does not prohibit their going: see above, ch. v. 10. On the words<b> for conscience</b><b> sake</b>, see above, ver. 25.
