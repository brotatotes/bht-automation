<p><strong> 19, 20.</strong> ] The inference from the preceding analogies would naturally be, that Paul was then representing the idols as <em> being in reality what the heathen supposed them to be</em> and the eater of meats offered to them, as partaking with the idol. This objection he meets, but with the introduction of a new fact to their consideration that the things which the heathen sacrifice, they sacrifice really to <em> devils</em> .</p>
