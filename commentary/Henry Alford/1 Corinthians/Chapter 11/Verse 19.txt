Our Lord had said “<i>It must needs be, that offences come,</i>” Matt. xviii. 7:—and Justin Martyr quotes among His sayings prophetic of division in the church, “<i>there shall be divisions</i> (<i>schisms</i>)<i> and heresies.</i>” From the pointed manner in which<i> there must</i><b> also</b> or<b> even be heresies</b>.... is said, I should be inclined to think that the Apostle<i> tacitly referred to the same saying of our Lord:</i><b> for there must be</b> (not only dissensions but)<b> even heresies</b> (not in the ecclesiastical or doctrinal sense, but indicating a further and more matured separation, where not only is there present: dissension, as in the Agapæ, but a deliberate choice and maintenance of party distinction. It does not appear, in spite of all that has been written in Germany on the supposed parties of ch. i. 10, that such separations had yet taken place among the Corinthians. Nor even in Clement’s Epistle, forty years after this, do we find any allusion to such, but only, as here, to a general spirit of dissension and variance)<b> among you, that the approved</b> [<b>also</b>] (i. e. as well as the other party, who would become manifest by their very conduct. This word<b> also</b> is found in some of our oldest MSS., and not in others)<b> may be made manifest among you</b>; viz. through a better and nobler spirit being shewn by them, than by the contentious and separatists.
