<b> 34.</b>] The Agapæ were not meals to satiate the bodily appetites, but for a higher and holier purpose: let the hungry take off the edge of his hunger at home: see ver. 22.<br><br><b> the rest</b>] viz.<i> things omitted</i> (probably matters of detail)<i> in the above</i><i> directions</i>. Perhaps they had asked him questions respecting the most convenient time or manner of celebration of the Lord’s supper: points on which primitive practice widely differed.<br><br><b> when I come,</b> literally,<b> whenever I shall have come:</b> the Apostle being uncertain as to the time.
