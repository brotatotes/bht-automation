<b> 8.</b>]<b> For</b> gives the reason of the former assertion, “<i>the woman is the glory of the man,</i>”—viz. that<b> the man is not</b> (emphasis on “IS,”— ‘takes not his being,’ in the fact of his original creation. The<i> propagation</i> of the species is not here in view)<b> out of the woman, but the woman out of the man</b> (compare Gen. ii. 28, “<i>She shall be called woman, because she was taken out of man</i>”).
