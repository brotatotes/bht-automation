<b> 15. is it therefore not of the body?</b>] These words here, and in the next verse, may be taken, “<i>it is not there-fore not of the body.</i>” But they are best taken as a question appealing to the sense of the reader: they thus have more of the vigour and abruptness of the Apostle’s style.<br><br><b> of the body,</b> i.e.<b> belonging to the body</b> as an aggregate.
