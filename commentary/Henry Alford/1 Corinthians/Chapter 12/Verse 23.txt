<p><strong> 23.</strong> ] So also in the case of the parts ἃ δοκοῦμεν ἀτιμότερα εἶναι on which <em> usage has set the stamp of dishonour</em> . Perhaps he alludes (as distinguished from τὰ ἀσχήμ . below) to those limbs which we conceal from sight in accordance with custom, but in the exposure of which there would be no absolute indecency. So Chrys., καλῶς εἶπε <strong> τὰ δοκοῦντα</strong> , καὶ <strong> ἃ δοκοῦμεν</strong> (but I should draw a distinction between the two, in accordance with the above explanation of ἀσθενέστ ., and render τὰ δοκοῦντα , <strong> which appear to be</strong> [of themselves], and ἃ δοκοῦμεν , <strong> which we think</strong> [conventionally]: notice also ὑπάρχειν and εἶναι , on which see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:20">Acts 16:20</span> , note) δεικνὺς ὅτι οὐ τῆς φύσεως τῶν πραγμάτων , ἀλλὰ τῆς τῶν πολλῶν ὑπονοίας ἡ ψῆφος . Hom. xxxi. p. 278.
