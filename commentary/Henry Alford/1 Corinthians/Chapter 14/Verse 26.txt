<p><strong> 26</strong> .] The rule for <em> all</em> , proceeding on the fact of each having his gift to contribute when they come together: viz. that all things must be done with a view to edification.
	</p>
<p><strong> τί οὖν ἐστιν</strong> ] See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:15">1 Corinthians 14:15</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ὅτ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> συν</strong> .] <strong> whenever ye happen to be assembling together</strong> : the <em> present</em> vividly describes each coming with his gift, eager to exercise it.
	</p>
<p><strong> ψαλμόν</strong> ] most probably <strong> a hymn of praise</strong> to sing in the power of the spirit, as did Miriam, Deborah, Symeon, &amp;c. See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:15">1 Corinthians 14:15</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> διδαχήν</strong> ] <strong> an exposition of doctrine</strong> or moral teaching: belonging to the gift of <em> prophecy</em> , as indeed do also <strong> ψαλμ</strong> . and <strong> ἀποκάλ</strong> ., the latter being something revealed to him, to be prophetically uttered.
	</p>
<p><strong> γλῶσσαν</strong> ] <strong> a tongue</strong> , i.e. an act of speaking in tongues: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:18">1 Corinthians 14:18</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:22">1 Corinthians 14:22</span> .
	</p>
