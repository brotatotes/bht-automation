<b> 26.</b>] The rule for<i> all</i>, proceeding on the fact of each having his gift to contribute when they come together: viz. that all things must be done with a view to edification.<br><br> Literally,<b> whenever ye happen to be assembling together</b>: the<i> present</i> vividly describes each coming with his gift, eager to exercise it.<br><br><b> a psalm</b>] most probably a<b> hymn of praise</b> to sing in the power of the spirit, as did Miriam, Deborah, Symeon, &c. See ver. 15.<br><br><b> a doctrine</b>]<b> an exposition of doctrine</b> or moral teaching: belonging to the gift of<i> prophecy</i>, as indeed do also<i> psalm</i> and<i> revelation</i>, the latter being something revealed to him, to be prophetically uttered.<br><br><b> a tongue</b>, i.e. an act of speaking in tongues: see vv. 18, 22.<br><br><b> an interpretation</b>] See below, and ver. 5.<br><br><b> Let all things be done unto edification</b>] THE GENERAL RULE, afterwards applied to the several gifts: and
