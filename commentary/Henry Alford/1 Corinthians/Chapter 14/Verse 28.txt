<b> 28.</b>]<b> But if there be not an interpreter, let him</b> (the speaker in a tongue)<b> be silent in the church; but</b> (so in original, i.e. let him not quench his gift,<i> but</i> exercise it in private)<b> let him speak for himself and for God</b> (i. e. in private, with only himself and God to witness it. Theophylact thinks it means, “<i>softly and quietly, to himself:</i>” which does not seem to agree with the conditions; the<i> speaking</i> being essential to the exercise of the gift).
