<b> 29.</b>]<b> two or three,</b> viz.<i> at one assembling;</i>—not<i> together;</i> this is plainly prohibited, ver. 30. There is no “<i>at the most,</i>” as in the other case, because he does not wish to seem as if he were limiting this most edifying of the gifts.<br><br><b> the others</b>, i.e. prophets,—or perhaps, any person possessing the gift of discerning of spirits mentioned ch. xii. 10 in immediate connexion with prophecy. Such would exercise that gift, to determine<i> whether the spirit was of God:</i> see ch. xii. 3; 1 John iv. 1–3.
