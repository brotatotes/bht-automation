<p><strong> 29.</strong> ] <strong> δέ</strong> , transitional.
	</p>
<p><strong> δύο ἢ τρεῖς</strong> , viz. <em> at one assemblings</em> ; not <em> together</em> ; this is plainly prohibited, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:30">1 Corinthians 14:30</span> . There is no τὸ πλεῖστον as in the other case, because he does not wish to seem as if he were limiting this most edifying of the gifts.
	</p>
<p><strong> οἱ ἄλλοι</strong> , scil. προφῆται , or perhaps, any person possessing the gift of διακρίσεις πνευμάτων , mentioned ch. 1Co 12:10 in immediate connexion with προφητεία . Such would exercise that gift, to determine <em> whether the spirit was of God</em> : see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:3">1 Corinthians 12:3</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:1-3">1 John 4:1-3</span> .</p>
