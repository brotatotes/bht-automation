<b> 4.</b>]<b> that he edifieth himself</b> does not necessarily involve his<i> understanding</i> what he speaks: the<i> exercise of the gift</i> in accordance with the prompting of the Spirit, may be regarded as an<i> edification</i>: the intensity of the feeling of prayer or praise in which he utters the words is edifying to him, though the words themselves are unintelligible. This view is necessary on account of what is said in ver. 5, that if he can<i> interpret,</i> he can edify not only himself but the church.<br><br><b> the church</b> (i. e. the assembled Christians): see note on ch, xi. 18.
