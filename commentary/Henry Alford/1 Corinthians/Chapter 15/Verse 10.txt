<b> 10. But by the grace of God</b>] “With the humiliating conviction of his own unworthiness is united the consciousness of that higher Power which worked on and in him,—and this introduces his chastened self-consciousness of the extent and success of his apostolic labours.” De Wette. The position of these words, and the repetition of “<b>his grace</b>” afterwards, shew the emphatic prominence which he assigns to the divine Grace.
