<b> 17. to no purpose</b>] The word is a different one from that used above, ver. 14, and more directly points at the<i> frustration</i> of all on which faith relies as accomplished,—e. g. the removal of the guilt and power of sin;—and of all to which hope looks forward, e.g. bliss after death for those who die in Christ. This is so, because Christ’s Resurrection<i> accomplished our justification</i> (Rom. iv. 25), and, through justification, our<i> future bliss</i>, even in the<i> disembodied state</i> (for that seems<i> here</i> to be treated of).
