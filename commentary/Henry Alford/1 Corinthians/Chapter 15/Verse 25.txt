<b> 25.</b>] See on the last verse:—this is the divine appointment with regard to the mediatorial kingdom, that it should last<i> till</i>, and only till, all enemies shall have been subdued to it.<br><br><b> till he</b>... viz. Christ, not<i> the Father</i>: it is parallel with the former sentence, “<i>when</i><b> He</b><i> shall have done away, &c.,</i>” and included in the mediatorial acts of Christ, who in His world’s course goes forth “<i>conquering and to conquer,</i>” Rev. vi. 2. It is otherwise in ver. 27: see there.
