<p><strong> 31.</strong> ] <strong> To die daily</strong> is a strong expression for to be daily in sight of death and expecting it. See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:11">2 Corinthians 4:11</span> .
	</p>
<p>This he strengthens by an asseveration, grounded on his boast of them as his work in Christ: not that this is immediately or proximately at stake in the matter, but much as we should say, “As I love you, it is true.” He would not think of deceiving those of whom he boasted before God in connexion with Christ.
	</p>
<p><strong> ὑμετ</strong> .] gen. obj., see reff. <strong> νή</strong> , the affirmative, as μά is the negative particle of adjuration: but ναὶ μά is often found in an affirmative sense: see Kühner, § 701.</p>
