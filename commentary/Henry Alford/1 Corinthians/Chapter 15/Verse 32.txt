<b> 32.</b>] The stress of the first clause is on the words<b> after the manner of men</b>, and its meaning,<b> merely as man</b>, i.e. ‘<i>according to this world’s views,</i>’ ‘as one who has no hope beyond the grave;’ see ref. If<i> thus</i> only he fought, &c., where was his profit (seeing he despised all those things which, with such a view, might compensate for such a fight,—fame, praise, &c.)?
