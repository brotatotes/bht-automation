<b> 38.</b>]<b> according as He pleased,</b> viz. at the creation: the past tense setting forth the<i> one act</i> of the divine Will giving to the particular seed the particular development at first, which the species retains: whereas “<i>pleaseth</i>” would imply a fresh act of the divine Will giving to every individual<b> seed</b> his own body. But the<i> whole gift to the species</i> being God’s, to continue or withhold, the present tense,<b> giveth</b>, still holds good.
