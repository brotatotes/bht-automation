<p><strong> 40.</strong> <strong> σώματα ἐπουράνια</strong> ] not, according to our modern expression, <em> heavenly bodies</em> , for they are introduced first <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:41">1 Corinthians 15:41</span> , and if we apply these words to them, we must suppose the Apostle to have imagined the stars to be endowed with bodies in the literal sense: for he is here comparing not figurative expressions, but physical realities: nor (as Chrys., al.) <em> the bodies of the righteous</em> , as opposed to those of the wicked; for in these there is no organic difference whatever: but, as Meyer and De Wette, ‘ <em> the bodies of angels</em> ,’ the only heavenly organisms of which we are aware (except indeed the Resurrection-Body of our Lord, and that of those few who have been taken into glory, which, as belonging to the matter <em> in question</em> , are not alleged) which will bear comparison with <em> bodies</em> on earth.
	</p>
<p><strong> δόξα</strong> belongs to the ἐπουράνια more strictly than to the ἐπίγεια . In <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:26">Luke 9:26</span> , we have ἐν τῇ δόξῃ αὐτοῦ καὶ τοῦ πατρὸς <strong> καὶ τῶν ἁγίων ἀγγέλων</strong> .</p>
