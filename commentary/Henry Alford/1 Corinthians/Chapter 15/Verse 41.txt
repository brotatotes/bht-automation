<b> 41.</b>] This third analogy is suggested by<b> glory</b> just before. There is no allusion whatever here, as some have imagined, to<i> different degrees of glorification</i> of the bodies of the blessed; the introduction of such an idea confuses the whole analogical reasoning: which is, that even various fountains of<i> light</i>, so similar in its aspect and properties, differ; the sun from the moon and the stars: the stars (and much more vividly would this be felt under the pure sky of the East than here) from one another: why not then a<i> body</i> here from a resurrection-<i>body</i>, both<i> bodies</i>, but<i> different?</i>
