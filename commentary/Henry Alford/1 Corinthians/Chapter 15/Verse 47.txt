Meyer has some excellent remarks here, with which I entirely agree: “Since the body of Adam is thus characterized as a ψυχικὸν σῶμα , as 1 Corinthians 15:45 , and psychical organism involves  mortality ( 1Co 15:44 ), it is clear that Paul treats of Adam  not as created exempt from death : in strict accordance with Genesis 2:7 ; Genesis 3:19 . Nor does this militate against his teaching that  death came into the world through sin . Romans 5:12 . For had our first parents not sinned, they would have remained in Paradise, and would, by the use of the  Tree of Life , which God  had not forbidden them ( Gen 2:16-17 ), have become immortal ( Gen 3:22 ). But they were driven out of Paradise,  ere yet they had tasted of this tree ( Gen 3:22 ), and so, according to the record in Genesis also, Death came into the world by sin.” See also some striking remarks on the verse in Genesis in Stier, ‘Andeutungen für glaübiges Schriftver-ständniss,’ pp. 202, 3.
