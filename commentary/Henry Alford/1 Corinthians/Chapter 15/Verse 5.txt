<b> 5.</b>] That the following appearances are related in chronological order, is evident from the use of the definite marks of sequence,<b> then, after that,... last of all.</b><br><br><b> he appeared to Cephas</b>] See Luke xxiv. 34.<br><br><b> the twelve</b>] used here popularly, as<i> decemviri</i>, and other like expressions, although the number was not full. The occasion referred to seems to be that in John xx. 19 ff.; Luke xxiv. 36 ff. Clearly we must not with Chrysostom suppose<i> Matthias</i> to be included as possibly having seen Him<i> after His ascension:</i> for the appearance is evidently<i> one and the same.</i>
