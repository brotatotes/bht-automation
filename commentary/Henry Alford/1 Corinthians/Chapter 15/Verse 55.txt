<b> 55.</b>] TRIUMPHANT EXCLAMATION<i> of the Apostle, realizing in his mind that glorious time;</i> expressed nearly in the terms of the prophetic announcement of Hosea, xiii. 14, which the Septuagint renders, “ ‘Where is thy cause, O Death? where is thy sting, O Hadés?” The figure of death as a<i> venomous beast</i> is natural, from the<i> serpent</i>, Gen. iii. Num. xxii.—The souls in Hadés being freed by the resurrection, Death’s victory is gone: sin being abolished by the change of the animal body (the source of sin) to the spiritual, his sting is powerless.
