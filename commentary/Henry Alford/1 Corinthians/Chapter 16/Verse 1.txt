<p><strong> εἰς τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἁγ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] = εἰς τοὺς πτωχοὺς τ . ἁγίων τῶν ἐν Ἱερουσαλήμ , ref. Rom. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:1">2 Corinthians 8:1</span> ff; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:1">2 Corinthians 9:1</span> ff.: and on the poverty of the church at Jerusalem, note on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:44">Acts 2:44</span> . That poverty was no doubt increased by the continual troubles with which Jerusalem was harassed in this, the distressful close of the Jewish national history. See other causes in Stanley. That the mother church of Christendom should be thus, in its need, sustained by the daughter churches, was natural; and it is at the same time an affecting circumstance, to find <em> him</em> the most anxious to collect and bear to them this contribution, whose former persecuting zeal had doubtless (see Act 26:10 ) made not a few of those saints <em> widows and orphans</em> .
