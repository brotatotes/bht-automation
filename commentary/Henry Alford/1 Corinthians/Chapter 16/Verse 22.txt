<b> 22.</b>] He adds, as in Col. iv. 18; Eph. vi. 24, some exhortation, or solemn sentence,<i> in his own hand</i>, as having especial weight. On the distinction between the two words used for “<i>to love,</i>” see notes on John xxi. 15. The negation here of the feeling of personal affection, “has no love in his heart for,” is worthy of note, as connected with the curse which follows.<br><br><b> let him be Anathema</b>] On<b> Anathema,</b> see note, Rom. ix. 3:–<b>let him be accursed.</b><br><br><b> Maran-atha</b> (see A.V.) is an Aramaic expression for<b> the Lord</b><b> cometh</b>, and is probably unconnected with<b> Anathema</b>, added perhaps as recalling some remembrance of the time when Paul was among them: at all events, as a weighty watchword tending to recall to them the nearness of His coming, and the duty of being found ready for it.
