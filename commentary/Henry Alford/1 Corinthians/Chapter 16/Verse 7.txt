<b> 7. For I am not willing this time to see you in passing</b>] There is a slight, but a very slight, reference to his change of purpose (see above): the word<b> now</b>, or<b> this time</b>, refers to the<i> occasion,</i> the news from ‘them of Chloe,’ which had made it advisable that he should not<i> now</i> pay them a mere passing visit.<br><br><b> for</b>] ground of his not wishing to see them—but not the ultimate one, see above.<br><br><b> permit</b>] literally,<b> shall have permitted me,</b> i.e. ‘if it shall so turn out, in the Lord’s direction of my work, that I shall then find my way open to do so.’
