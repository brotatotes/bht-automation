<p><strong> 8, 9</strong> .] <em> His present plan regarding his stay in Ephesus</em> (where he was writing).
	</p>
<p><strong> τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> πεντηκ</strong> .] viz. that <em> next coming</em> . This probably happened so, or nearly so, notwithstanding the tumult of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:0">Acts 19:0</span> ; for he already (see there 1Co 16:21-22 ) was <em> meditating his departure</em> , and had sent on two of his company, when the tumult occurred.
	</p>
<p><strong> θύρα</strong> , see reff.: <em> an opportunity of action</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> μεγάλη</strong> refers to the <em> extent</em> of the action thus opened before him: <strong> ἐνεργής</strong> , to its <em> requirements</em> : neither of them (though μεγάλη may be referred to θύρα ) properly agreeing with the <em> figure</em> , but both with the <em> reality</em> . Meyer compares Plato, Phædr. p. 245, A: μουσῶν ἐπὶ ποιητικὰς θύρας ἀφίκηται .
	</p>
