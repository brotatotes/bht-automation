<p><strong> 7.</strong> ] <strong> But we speak</strong> GOD’S <strong> wisdom</strong> (emphasis on θεοῦ : the wisdom which <em> God</em> possesses and has revealed) <strong> in a mystery</strong> ( ἐν μυστ . does not belong to τὴν ἀποκεκ ., as Theodoret and Grot., which must be τὴν ἐν μυστ . ἀποκ ., nor to σοφίαν , as Beza, Bengel, which though not absolutely, yet certainly here, seeing <strong> τὴν</strong> ἀποκεκρ . immediately follows, would require the art., τὴν ἐν μυστ ., but to λαλοῦμεν , ‘we <em> speak</em> God’s wisdom <em> in a mystery</em> ,’ i.e. as handling a mystery, dealing with a mystery. So τὴν σύνεσίν μου ἐν τῷ μυστ . τ . χριστοῦ , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:4">Ephesians 3:4</span> .
	</p>
<p>Estius and the Romanists, taking the connexion rightly, have wrested the meaning to support the <em> disciplina arcani</em> which they imagine to be here hinted at, explaining ἐν μυστ ., “non propalam et passim apud omnes, quia non omnes ea capiunt, sed … secreto et apud pauciores, scilicet eos qui spirituales et perfecti sunt,” Est.), <strong> which has been</strong> (hitherto) <strong> hidden</strong> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:25">Romans 16:25</span> ; ref. Col.): <strong> which God foreordained</strong> (nothing need be supplied, as ἀποκαλύπτειν , or the like, after προώρισεν ) <strong> before the ages</strong> (of time) <strong> to</strong> ( <em> in order to</em> , the purpose of this preordination) <strong> our glory</strong> (our participation in the things which He has prepared for them that love Him, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:9">1 Corinthians 2:9</span> ; δόξα , as contrasted with the bringing to nought of the ἄρχοντες ).</p>
