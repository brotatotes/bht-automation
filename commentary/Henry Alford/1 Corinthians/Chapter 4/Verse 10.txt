<p><strong> 10</strong> .] Again, the bitterest irony: ‘how different our lot from yours! How are you to be envied we, to be pitied!’
	</p>
<p>There is a distinction in διὰ χριστόν and ἐν χριστῷ q. d. <strong> We are foolish for Christ’s sake</strong> (on account of Christ, our connexion with Him does nothing but reduce us to be fools), <strong> whereas you are</strong> <strong> φρόνιμοι ἐν χριστῷ</strong> , have entered into full participation of Him, and grown up to be wise, subtle Christians.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀσθενεῖς</strong> <strong> </strong> <strong> ἰσχυροί</strong> are both to be understood <em> generally</em> : the ἀσθένεια is not here that of <em> persecution</em> , but that of ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:3">1 Corinthians 2:3</span> ; the <em> strength</em> is the high bearing of the Corinthians.
	</p>
<p><strong> Ye are in honour</strong> (in glorious repute, party leaders and party men, highly honoured and looked up to), <strong> whereas we are despised</strong> (without honour). Then ἄτιμοι leads him to enlarge on the disgrace and contempt which the Apostle met with at the hands of the world.</p>
