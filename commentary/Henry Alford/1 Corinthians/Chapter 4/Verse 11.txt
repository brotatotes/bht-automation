<b> 11.]</b><b> Even unto this present</b> hour is evidently not to be taken strictly as indicative of the situation of Paul at the<i> time of writing the Epistle,</i> but as generally describing the kind of life to which, then and always, he and the other Apostles were exposed. See, on the subject matter, 2 Cor. xi. 23–27.<br><br><b> are naked]</b> i.e. in want of sufficient clothing: cf. “<i>in</i><i> cold and nakedness</i>,” 2 Cor. xi. 27.<br><br><b> are buffeted]</b>, i.e. suffer insult: there is no need to press the strict meaning.
