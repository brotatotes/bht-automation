<p><strong> 14. </strong> <strong> οὐκ ἐντρέπων</strong> ] <strong> not as one who shames you</strong> , see reff., and ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:5">1 Corinthians 6:5</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:34">1 Corinthians 15:34</span> , and for the force of the participle, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:1">1 Corinthians 2:1</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> νουθετῶ</strong> contrasts with <strong> ἐντρέπων γράφω</strong> , the construction being purposely adopted, to set in a more vivid light the paternal intention: <strong> I am not writing these things</strong> ( 1Co 4:8-13 ) <strong> as shaming you, but I am admonishing you as my beloved children</strong> .</p>
