<b> 12.]</b><i> Ground of the above limitation.</i><b> them that are without]</b> It was among the Jews the usual term for the Gentiles. He means, ‘this might have been easily understood to be my meaning: for what concern have I with pronouncing sentence on the world without, or with giving rules of discipline for<i> them</i>? I could only have referred to persons<i> among yourselves</i>. ‘My judgment was meant to<i> lead your judgment.</i> This being the case, what concern had I with<i> those without?</i> Is it not on<i> those within</i>, that your judgments are passed?’
