<b> 5.</b>] The aim of the destruction of the flesh,—which he said, as Chrysostom remarks, “to put bounds to the devil, and not to allow him to advance further.” Thus the proposed punishment, severe as it might seem, would be in reality a merciful one, tending to the eternal happiness of the offender. A greater contrast to this can hardly be conceived, than the terrible forms of excommunication subsequently devised, and even now in use in the Romish church, under the fiction of delegated apostolic power. The delivering to Satan<i> for the destruction of the spirit</i>, can belong only to those who<i> do the work of Satan</i>.
