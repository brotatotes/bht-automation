<p><strong> 15</strong> .] Resumption of τὸ σῶμα τῷ κυρίῳ κ . ὁ κύριος τῷ σώματι . The two are so intimately connected, that the Lord <em> is a mystical Body, of which our bodies</em> , parts of ourselves in our perfect organization, <em> are members</em> . This Christian axiom is introduced as before (reff.) by οὐκ οἴδατε ὅτι .
	</p>
<p><strong> Having then</strong> ( οὖν , ‘concesso,’ that my body is a member = my members are members of Christ) <strong> alienated</strong> ([or, <strong> taken away</strong> ] ἄρας is not merely pleonastic, ‘ <em> Shall I take … and make them</em> .…’ as E. V. This is shewn by its position first in the sentence) <strong> the members of Christ</strong> (i.e. my own members) <strong> shall I make them an harlot’s members</strong> ? The expression πόρνης μέλη is put as coarsely and startlingly as possible, with the emphasis on πόρνης .
	</p>
<p><strong> ποιήσω</strong> may also be the aor. subj., ‘ <em> must I, have I any right to, make them</em> ?’ But μὴ γένοιτο answers better to the future.</p>
