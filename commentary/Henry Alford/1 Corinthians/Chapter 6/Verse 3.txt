<b> 3.]</b> The same glorious office of Christians is again referred to, and even a more striking point of contrast brought out.<br><br><b> angels]</b> always, where not otherwise specified,<i> good angels:</i> and therefore here; the “<i>ministering spirits</i>” of Heb. i. 14: but exactly<i> how</i> we shall judge them, is not revealed to us. Chrysostom and most Commentators, interpret, it of<i> bad angels</i>, or of<i> bad and good together</i>.<br><br><b> things that pertain to this life</b>: properly, matters relating to<i> a man’s livelihood</i>.—The meaning is,<b> civil causes</b>, matters of<i> mine</i> and<i> thine</i>.
