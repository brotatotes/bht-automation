<p><strong> 7.</strong> ] He gives his own censure of their going to law at all.
	</p>
<p><strong> μὲν οὖν</strong> as above, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:4">1 Corinthians 6:4</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ὅλως</strong> , <strong> altogether</strong> , without the aggravation of ἐπὶ ἀπίστων .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἥττημα</strong> <strong> , a falling short</strong> , viz. of your inheritance of the kingdom of God a hindrance in the way of your salvation: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:9">1 Corinthians 6:9</span> : not as ordinarily understood (see especially Estius in loc.) a <em> moral delinquency</em> (cf. the usage in reff.), nor an ἡττᾶσθαι τῇ ὀργῇ , as Œcum.
	</p>
<p><strong> κρίματα</strong> , <strong> matters of dispute</strong> , leading to κρίνεσθαι ; not = κρίσεις , μεθ ʼ <strong> ἑαυτῶν</strong> <strong> , with one another</strong> (reff.), as being brethren in Christ.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀδικεῖσθε</strong> and <strong> ἀποστερεῖσθε</strong> not passives, but middle (cf. Bernhardy, Syntax, chap. viii. § 4, p. 346: Menander frag.: οὗτος κράτιστός ἐστ ʼ ἀνήρ , ὦ Γοργία , ὅστις ἀδικεῖσθαι πλεῖστ ʼ ἐπίσταται βροτῶν : Hesiod. ἔργ . 347, εὖ μὲν μετρεῖσθαι παρὰ γείτονος , εὖδ ʼ ἀποδοῦναι ) <strong> allow yourselves to be wronged and defrauded</strong> . See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:39">Matthew 5:39</span> ff.</p>
