<p><strong> 9.</strong> ] ‘Ye <em> commit wrong</em> :’ this looks as if you had forgotten the rigid exclusion from the kingdom of God of <em> all wrong-doers</em> of every kind (included here under ἄδικοι ); see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:21">Galatians 5:21</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> μὴ πλανᾶσθε</strong> ] This caution would be most salutary and needful in a dissolute place like Corinth. It is similarly used, and with an express reference to ὁμιλίαι κακαί , ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:33">1 Corinthians 15:33</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> πόρνοι</strong> refers back to ch. 5, and is taken up again, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:12">1 Corinthians 6:12</span> ff.
	</p>
<p><strong> μαλακοί</strong> = παθικοί (see in Wetst.).
	</p>
