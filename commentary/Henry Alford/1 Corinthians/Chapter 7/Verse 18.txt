<p><strong> 18. </strong> <strong> ἐκλήθη</strong> ] <strong> Was any one called in circumcision</strong> , i.e. circumcised at the time of his conversion.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐπισπάσθω</strong> ] By a surgical operation; see Theophyl., Wetst., Winer, Realwörterbuch, art. Beschneidung, Jos. Antt. xii. 5. 1; 1Ma 1:15 ; Celsus de Re Medica, vii. 25 (in Wetst.). The practice usually was adopted by those who wished to appear like the Gentiles, and to cast off their ancient faith and habits. Among the Christians a strong anti-Judaistic feeling might lead to it.
	</p>
<p><strong> περιτεμνέσθω</strong> ] See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:2">Galatians 5:2</span> , al.</p>
