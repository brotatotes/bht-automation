<b> 25.</b>]<b> virgins</b> is not to be understood<i> unmarried persons of both sexes</i>, a meaning which, though apparently found in Rev. xiv. 4 (see note there), is perfectly unnecessary here, and appears to have been introduced from a mistaken view of vv, 26–28. The emphasis is on<b> commandment</b>—<b>command of the Lord have I none,</b> i.e.,<i> no expressed precept;</i> so that, as before, there is no marked comparison between “<i>the Lord,</i>” and “<i>I</i>.”
