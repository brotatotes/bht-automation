 27.] The expression,<b> so to be</b>,<i> re-stated</i> and<i> illustrated</i>: neither the married nor the unmarried are to<i> seek for a change</i>. ‘The general recommendation here is referable alike to all cases of marriage, and does not touch on the prohibition of ver. 10,—<i>only dissuading from a spirit of change in consideration of the</i> instant necessity. It seems better to take the verse thus, than to regard it as inserted to guard against misunderstanding of the preceding<i> judgment</i> of the Apostle.
