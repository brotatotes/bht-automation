<b> 31. using it to the full</b>] The word in the original appears here to imply that intense and greedy use which turns the legitimate use into a fault. This meaning is better than<i> ‘abuse’</i> (A. V.), which is allowable, and is adopted by many. The end of the verse<i> gives a reason for</i> the assertion that<i> the time is short</i>; the clauses which have intervened being subordinate to those words: see above.
