<b> 34</b>.] The literal rendering is—<b>Divided also is the</b> (married)<b> woman and the virgin</b>,<i> divided in interest</i> [i. e. in cares and pursuits] from one another: not merely,<i> different from one another</i> as would seem at first sight from the words of the text. On the question arising from different readings, see my Greek ‘Test—The judgment of marriage here pronounced by the Apostle must be taken, as the rest of the chapter,<i> with its accompanying conditions.</i> He is speaking of a pressing and quickly shortening period, which he regards as yet remaining before that day and hour of which neither he, nor any man knew.
