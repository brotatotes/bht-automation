<b> 5.] in order that ye may have undisturbed leisure for prayer.</b> The original (see in my Greek Test.) shews that the prayer meant is not ordinary, but extraordinary,—<i>seasons of urgent</i><i> supplication</i>.—The addition, in the received text, of the words “<i>fasting and</i>,” shews how such passages as this have been tampered with by the ascetics. The words are wanting in all our principal oldest authorities.
