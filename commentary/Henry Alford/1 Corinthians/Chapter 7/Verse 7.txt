<p><strong> 7</strong> .] <strong> I rather (</strong> <strong> δέ</strong> <strong> ) wish that all men were as I myself also am</strong> ( καί comparandi, so Xen. Anab. ii. l. 22, καὶ ἡμῖν ταὐτὰ δοκεῖ ἅπερ καὶ βασιλεῖ . See Hartung, Partikell. i. 126) viz., ἐν ἐγκρατείᾳ , which Chrys. seems to have read in the text; see below on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:8">1 Corinthians 7:8</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀλλὰ ἕκαστος</strong> … said in the most general way, as a milder expression of ‘all have not the gift of continence.’
	</p>
<p><strong> οὕτως</strong> <strong> … </strong> <strong> οὕτως</strong> ] both are said <em> generally</em> , not one <em> in the way in which I have it</em> (of continence), another <em> in the way of marrying</em> (i.e. though he have not <em> this</em> , and be therefore <em> better married</em> , yet has <em> some other</em> ), which should be ἐκείνως , but, <strong> one thus, and another thus</strong> , i.e. ‘one in one way, another in another.’</p>
