<b> 10.</b>]<i> Explanation how the</i><b> stumblingblock</b><i> may arise</i>.<br><br><b> any man,</b> i.e. any weak brother, see below.—The words<b> thee which hast knowledge</b> seem to imply that the weak brother<i> is aware of this</i>, and<i> looks up to thee as such.</i> The word rendered<b> emboldened is literally edified, built up,</b> not without a certain irony, seeing it is accompanied by a description of the man as<i> weak</i>,—for thus the building up would be without solid foundation—<i>a building up undo ruin,</i> as Calvin calls it.
