<b> 6.</b>]<b> yet</b> TO US (emphatic: however that_matter may be,<i> we</i> hold) there is ONE GOD,<b> the Father (the Father</b> answers to<b> Jesus Christ</b> in the parallel clause below, and serves to specify<i> what God—</i>viz.<i> the Father</i> of our Lord Jesus Christ),<b> of Whom</b> (as their Source of being)<b> are all things, and we unto</b> (i. e.<b> for) Him</b> (His purposes—to serve His will);<b> and one Lord Jesus Christ</b> (notice the<i> one</i> God opposed to<i> many gods</i>, and<i> one Lord to many lords</i>),<b> by Whom</b> (as Him by whom the Father made the worlds, John i. 3; Heb. i. 2)<b> are all things, and we</b> (but here secondly, we as His spiritual people, in the new creation)<b> by Him</b>.—The inference from the foregoing is that,<i> of itself</i>, the eating of meat offered to idols is a thing indifferent, and therefore allowed. The limitation of this licence now follows.
