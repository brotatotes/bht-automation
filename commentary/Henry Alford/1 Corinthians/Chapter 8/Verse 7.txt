<p><strong> 7.</strong> ] <strong> But</strong> ( <em> sondern</em> ) <strong> not in all is the knowledge</strong> (of which we have been speaking: i.e. see above, is not in them in their individual apprehension, though it is by their profession as Christians): <strong> but</strong> (aber) <strong> some through their consciousness</strong> (or, according to the other reading, <strong> habituation) to this day, of the</strong> (particular) <strong> idol</strong> (i.e. through their having an apprehension to this day of the <em> reality</em> of the idol, and so being conscientiously afraid of the meat offered, as belonging to <em> him</em> : not wishing to be connected with him. τῇ συνειδήσει ἕως ἄρτι is not = τῇ ἕως ἄρτι συν ., but ἕως ἄρτι stands separate, as above: so διὰ τῆς ἐμῆς παρουσίας πάλιν πρὸς ὑμᾶς , Php 1:26 ) <strong> eat it as offered to an idol, and their conscience, in that it is weak, is defiled</strong> . By <strong> ἕως ἄρτι</strong> , it is shewn that these ἀσθενεῖς must have belonged to the <em> Gentile</em> part of the Corinthian church: to those who had <em> once</em> , before their conversion, held these idols to be veritable gods. Had they been <em> Jewish</em> converts, it would not have been συνείδησις τοῦ εἰδώλου which would have troubled them, but apparent violation of the Mosaic law.</p>
