<b> 15.] none of these things</b> is best explained of the different forms of<i> power</i> which have been mentioned.<br><br><b> I have not written these things however</b>,<b> that it may be so</b> (viz., after the examples which I have alleged)<b> done to me</b> (in my):<b> for it were good for me rather to die</b> (or better for me to die),<b> than that any one should make void my (matter of) boasting</b>. In saying “to die,’ he does not mean, as Chrysostom and others have supposed,<i> of famine</i>, by not being supported, but only that he prefers this his boasting even to life itself.
