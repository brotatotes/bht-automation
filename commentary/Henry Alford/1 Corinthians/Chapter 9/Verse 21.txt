<b> 21. to them that are without law]</b> These are<i> the Heathen;</i> hardly, as Chrysostom supposes,<i> such as Cornelius</i>, fearing God but not under the law. St. Paul became as a Heathen to the Heathen, e.g., when he discoursed at Athens (Acts xvii.) in their own manner, and with arguments drawn from their own poets.
