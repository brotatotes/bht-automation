<b> 26.] I then</b> (emphatic—he recalls the attention from the incidental exhortation, and reminiscence of the Christian state, to the main subject, his own abstinence from receiving, and its grounds)<b> so run, as not uncertainly</b> (i. e. without any sure grounds of contending or any fixed object for which to contend; both these are included);<b> so fight I, as not striking the air</b> (and not my adversary). The allusion is not to a “sham fight,” or rehearsal of a fight with an<i> imaginary</i> adversary, but to a fight with a<i> real</i> adversary (viz. here,<i> the body</i>) in which the boxer vainly hits into the air, instead of striking his antagonist:
