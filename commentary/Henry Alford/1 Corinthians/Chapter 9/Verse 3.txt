<p><strong> 3</strong> .] This belongs to the <em> preceding</em> , not to the <em> following</em> verses:
	</p>
<p><strong> αὕτη</strong> , viz. the <em> fact of your conversion</em> : this word is the <em> predicate, not the subject</em> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:19">John 1:19</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:3">John 17:3</span> , and stands here in the emphatic place before the verb; referring to what went before. With <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:4">1 Corinthians 9:4</span> a new course of questions begins, which furnish no ἀπολογία .
	</p>
<p><strong> τοῖς ἐμὲ ἀνακρ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] For the dat. see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:33">Acts 19:33</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:19">2 Corinthians 12:19</span> : <strong> to those, who call me in question</strong> : ἐμέ , emphatic, as Chrys. says, of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:2">1 Corinthians 9:2</span> , κἂν βούληταί τις … μαθεῖν ποθεν ὅτι ἀπόστολός εἰμι , ὑμᾶς προβάλλομαι , p. 181.</p>
