<b> 6.] Or</b> (implying what the consequence would then be, see ch. vi. 2, 9: does not introduce a new “<i>liberty</i>,” but a consequence of the denial of the last two)<b> have I only and Barnabas</b> (why Barnabas? Perhaps on account of his former connexion with St. Paul, Acts xi. 30; xii. 25; xiii. 1-xv. 39; but this seems hardly enough reason for his being here introduced. It is not improbable that, having been at first associated with Paul, who appears<i> from the first</i> to have abstained from receiving sustenance from those among whom he was preaching, Barnabas, after his separation from our Apostle, may have retained the same self- denying practice, “This is the only time when he is mentioned in conjunction with St. Paul, since the date of the quarrel in Acts xy. 39.” Stanley)<b> not power to abstain from working</b> (i. e. power to look for our maintenance from the churches, without manual labour of our own)?
