<b> 9.]</b> (It does say them)<b> For in the law of Moses it is written, Thou shalt not muzzle an ox while treading out the corn.</b> It was and still is the custom in the East, to place the newly-reaped grain on a floor in the open air, and then, by means of oxen, or buffaloes, to crush out the grain from the husk, either by the feet of the beasts, or by machines dragged by them.
