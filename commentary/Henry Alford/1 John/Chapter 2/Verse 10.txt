<p><strong> 10</strong> .] <strong> He that loveth his brother abideth in the light</strong> (i. e. the continuance of the habit of brotherly love is a measure of and a guarantee for his continuance in that light whose great command is Love), <strong> and there is no occasion of stumbling in him</strong> (so E. V., excellently. For it is clear by the parallel in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:11">1 John 2:11</span> , that this is what is meant, and not that he gives no occasion of stumbling to others, as Calov., al., “Qui fratrem odit, ipse sibi offendiculum est, et incurrit in seipsum et in omnia intus et foris; qui amat, expeditum iter habet.” Bengel. Cf. also <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:9-10">John 11:9-10</span> , which is in more than one respect the key-text here. For it also explains the apparently difficult <strong> ἐν αὐτῷ</strong> , occurring as it does there in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:10">1 John 2:10</span> , ἐὰν δέ τις περιπατῇ ἐν τῇ νυκτί , προσκόπτει , ὅτι τὸ φῶς οὐκ ἔστιν ἐν αὐτῷ . The light, and the darkness, by which we walk safely, or stumble, are within ourselves; admitted into us by the eye, whose singleness fills the whole body with light).</p>
