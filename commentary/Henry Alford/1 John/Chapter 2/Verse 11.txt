<b> 11.] But</b> (whereas)<b> he that hateth his brother</b> (see above)<b> is in the darkness</b> (has never come out of it: corresponds to “<i>abideth in the light</i>” above: denotes his state, whereas “<i>walketh</i>” indicates more his outward acts)<b>, and walketh in the darkness, and knoweth not where he goeth, because the darkness blinded</b> (it is a matter of old standing: “<i>blinded</i>,” and not “<i>hath blinded</i>,” because it is no new effect of a state into which he has lately come, but the long past work of a state which is supposed to be gone by and is not)<b> his eyes.</b>
