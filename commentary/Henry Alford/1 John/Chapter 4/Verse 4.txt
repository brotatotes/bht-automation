The ground of this assurance follows)  them (  αὐτούς , the false prophets, thus identified with antichrist. The vulg. has the unjustified reading  eum , which is naturally referred to antichrist (Aug [68] , Bed [69] , and the R.-C. expositors generally); to the  world , “devincendo concupiscentiam,” by Lyra; to “antichrist and the world,” by Erasmus),  because greater is He (that is)  in you than he (  that is )  in the world .
