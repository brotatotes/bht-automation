<b> 12.</b>]<i> Conclusion of the whole argument</i> from ver. 6: dependent on the lust clause of ver. 11, and carrying it on a step further, even to the absolute identity as matter of possession for the believer, of the Son of God, and eternal life.<b> He that hath the Son, hath the life: he that hath not the Son of God, the life hath he not.</b> First notice the diction and arrangement, on which Bengel has well remarked, “The verse has two members: in the former ‘<i>of</i><i> God</i>’ is not added, for the faithful know the Son: in the other, it is added, that unbelievers may know how great a thing they lose.”
