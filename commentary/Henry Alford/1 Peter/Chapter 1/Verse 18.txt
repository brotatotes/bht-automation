<p><strong> 18</strong> .] <strong> knowing</strong> (being aware: this argument enhances the duty of godly fear by the consideration of the inestimable price at which they were redeemed. This consideration is urged through 1Pe 1:18-21 ) that <strong> not</strong> (emphatic) <strong> with corruptible things</strong> ( <strong> φθαρτοῖς</strong> subst.; not, as Luther, agreeing with ἀργυρ . ἢ χρυσίῳ ), <strong> silver or gold</strong> (notice ἀργυρίῳ ἢ χρυσίῳ , not ἀργύρῳ ἢ χρυτῷ . The diminutive forms stand generally (not always, cf. Palm and Rost in χρυσίον ) for the coined or wrought metal: and such a sense would be applicable here), <strong> were ye redeemed</strong> (bought out of, by the payment of a λύτρον , presently to be specified: see reff., and cf. ἀγοράζεσθαι , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:20">1 Corinthians 6:20</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:23">1 Corinthians 7:23</span> ; ἐξαγοράζεσθαι , Gal 3:13 ) <strong> out of your vain conversation</strong> ( <strong> ματαίας ἀναστρ</strong> ., “vana vivendi ratio, quæ, ubi tempus præteriit, nil reliqui fructus habet.” Beng.) <strong> delivered to you from your fathers</strong> (“ <em> unus</em> Pater imitandus <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:17">1 Peter 1:17</span> ; idem antitheton, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:9">Matthew 23:9</span> .” Bengel. This again makes it probable that the persons here more especially addressed are Gentile Christians.
