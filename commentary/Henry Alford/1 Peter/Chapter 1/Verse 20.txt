<p><strong> 20</strong> .] The preciousness and completeness of this redemption is further enhanced by <em> God’s foreordination</em> of it, and His <em> bringing it to glorious completion</em> in His due time. <strong> Who</strong> (viz. χριστός , as shewn by the αὐτόν and αὐτῷ below) <strong> hath been foreordained indeed</strong> (see on 1Pe 1:2 ) <strong> before the foundation of the world</strong> (see reff. The same thought is foremost in the Apostle’s speech in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:23">Acts 2:23</span> ; Act 3:18 ), <strong> but manifested</strong> (brought out of the κρυπτόν of God’s purposes into the φανερόν of Incarnation and historical world-fact. The same word occurs in ch. 1Pe 5:4 of the yet future manifestation of Christ at His second coming) <strong> at the end of the times</strong> (cf. ἐπ ʼ ἐσχάτου τῶν ἡμερῶν τούτων , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:1">Hebrews 1:1</span> , and note there: and for this substantive sense of <strong> ἐσχάτου</strong> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:8">Acts 1:8</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:47">Acts 13:47</span> . This φανέρωσις of Christ, as Wiesinger remarks, marks this as the end of the times, and this last time shall only endure so long, as this φανέρωσις requires) <strong> for your sakes</strong> (an additional and weighty intensification of their obligation)</p>
