<b> 20.]</b> The preciousness and completeness of this redemption is further enhanced by<i> God’s foreordination</i> of it, and His<i> bringing it to glorious completion</i> in His due time.<b> Who</b> (viz.<i> Christ</i>)<b> hath been foreordained indeed</b> (see on ver. 2)<b> before the foundation of the world</b> (the same thought is foremost in the Apostle’s speech in Acts ii. 23; iii, 18),<b> but manifested</b> (brought out of the<i> hiding-place</i> of God’s purposes into the open<i> display</i> of Incarnation and historical world-fact. The same word occurs in ch. v. 4 of the yet future manifestation of Christ at His second coming)<b> at the end of the times</b> (compare Heb. i. 1, and note there. This<i> manifestation</i> of Christ marks<i> this</i> as the<i> end of the times,</i> and this last time shall only endure so long, as this<i> manifestation</i> requires)<b> for your sakes</b> (an additional and weighty intensification of their obligation)<b> who are through Him</b> (not only through His manifestation; but through Him personally, made to you all that He is made as the medium of your faith in God: the resurrection and glory being included)<b> believers in God</b> (a similar specification is found at ver. 4)<b> who raised Him from the dead, and gave Him glory</b> ( “that we are redeemed from our vain conversation, is owing to the blood of Christ; but that we have faith and hope in God, is brought about by God having raised Christ from the dead, and given Him glory.” Hofmann),<b> so that your faith and hope are</b> (not, as A.V., and others, “that your faith and hope<i> might be;</i> but simply announcing a matter of fact. Your faith rests on Christ’s resurrection—it was God who raised Him: your hope, on Christ’s glorification: it is God who has given Him that glory. Closely accordant with this is St. Peter’s first public speech in the Acts, ii. 22 ff., where all that has happened to Christ is referred to God as the doer of it)<b> on</b> (resting on and in)<b> God.</b>
