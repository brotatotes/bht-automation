<b> 3.]</b><b> Blessed be</b> (this particular word, which is not the same as that so rendered in Matt. xxi. 9 and parallels; xxiii. 39; xxv. 36; Luke i. 28, &c., is used in the New Test. of God only: and so almost always in the Old Test.)<b> the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ</b> (so verbatim Eph. i. 3, where see note),<b> who according to his much</b><b> mercy</b> (compare “<i>being rich in mercy,</i>” Eph. ii. 4)<b> begat us again</b> (as in ver. 23 and elsewhere in the New Test., where the idea, though not the word occurs,—spoken of the new birth from the state of nature to state of grace, the work of God the Spirit [ver. 2], by means of the word [ver. 23], in virtue of Christ’s propitiatory sacrifice and of union with Him [vv. 2, 18: ch. ii. 24, iii. 18])<b> unto</b> (either unto as aim and end, being equivalent to “that we might have,” or<i> local,</i> unto, into; “so that we have.” The latter is here preferable, seeing that hope is not the aim, but the condition, of the Christian life)<b> a living hope</b> (<b>living,</b> as connected with<i> begetting again;</i> it is a life of hope, a life in which hope is the energizing principle. This is better than to understand it as contrasting our hope with that of the hypocrite, which shall perish: as Leighton, in some of his most beautiful language.<b> Hope</b> is not to be understood of the<i> object of hope,</i> but of hope properly so called, subjectively. This hope of the Christian “has life in itself, gives life, and looks for life as its object,” De Wette)<b> through the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead</b> (to what does this<b> through</b> refer? Æcumenius says, “Whence hath it life? From Jesus Christ, who arose from the dead.” Similarly Luther, Bengel, &c. But, while we retain distinctly the connexion of our<i> living</i> hope with the<i> life</i> of Him on whom it depends, it is much more natural to join this instrumental clause with the verb<b> begat,</b> as bringing in with it the whole clause,<b> us to a living hope,</b> by which it is defined. The resurrection of Christ, brining in life and the gift of the life-giving Spirit, is that which penetrates the new birth into a living hope),
