<b> 7.]</b><b> that</b> (end and aim of these temptations)<b> the proof</b> (see on James)<b> of your faith</b> (equivalent to<i> the fact of your faith being proved,</i> and so, by an easy transition, the result of that proof, the purified and proved faith itself),<b> more precious than gold which perisheth</b> (<b>more precious</b> is in apposition with<b> proof</b> above. No supply before ‘<i>gold,</i>’ such as ‘<i>of,</i>’ as in A.V., or ‘<i>that of,’</i> is legitimate. It is not ‘<i>the proof</i>’ which is precious, though the literal construction at first sight seems to be this, but the faith itself: see above),<b> yet is</b> (usually, habitually)<b> proved by fire</b> (the<b> yet</b> in this clause brings out this, that gold though perishable yet needs fire to try it—the inference lying in the background, how much more does your faith, which is being proved for eternity, not for mere temporary use, need a fiery trial?),<b> may be found</b> (finally and once for all, as the result of the judicial trial at that day)<b> unto</b> (having as its result)<b> praise and glory and honour</b> (<i>whose?</i> Here the matter treated of is the praise of the elect themselves. Some have pressed the meanings of the separate words: the<b> praise</b> being from the Judge, His “Well done, good servant:”<b> glory,</b> admission into His glory, ch. v. 1, 10: the<b> honour,</b> the dignity and personal honour thence accruing, ch. iii. 7. But perhaps, as in Rom. ii. 7, we should rather regard them here as cumulative)<b> in</b> (so literally: i.e. ‘at the day of:” the element, in time, in which it shall be manifested)<b> the revelation of Jesus Christ</b> (i. e. His return, who is now withdrawn from our sight, but shall then appear again: and with His<i> revelation</i> shall come also the<i> revelation of the sons of God,</i> Rom. viii. 19; 1 John iii. 2):
