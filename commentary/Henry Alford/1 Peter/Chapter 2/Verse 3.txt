<b> 3.] if, that is (if so be</b> expresses the same, viz. that the necessary condition of the above exhortation is assumed as having place in the readers),<b> ye</b> (have)<b> tasted</b> (the infant once put to the breast desires it again: the Apostle appeals to this their first taste as an incentive to subsequent ones)<b> that</b> (the formula is from the well-known and beautiful Ps. xxxiv.)<b> the Lord</b> (as Calvin observes, not simply<i> God</i> is here meant, but God as He is revealed to us in the person of Christ)<b> is good</b> (perhaps the simplest meaning, as applied to meats and drinks, is here intended. The Vulgate renders it<i> sweet</i>).
