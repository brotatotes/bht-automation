<b> 10.]</b> And this is to be so, not merely in the interchange of this world’s good offices, but also in the communication of the gifts of the Spirit, which are the common endowment of the whole body, individual Christians being only the stewards of them.<b> Each man even as</b> (in whatever quality and quantity: but the subsequent injunctions seem more to regard the quality than the quantity. It is otherwise in Eph. iv. 7; Rom. xii. 3. The<b> as</b> has no reference to the<i> manner</i> of reception)<b> he received a gift of grace</b> (see Rom. xii. 6 ff.; 1 Cor. xii. 4, 28; meaning, any one of the gifts known by that name),<b> to each other ministering it</b> (to the need of others; his store out of which he ministers being that gift thus bestowed upon him),<b> as</b> (being: or, as becometh: see ch. i. 14)<b> good stewards</b> (there is most likely a reference to our Lord’s parable of the talents)<b> of the various</b> (or manifold: see this illustrated, 1 Cor. xii. 4; Matt. xxv. 15; Luke xix. 13)<b> grace of God.</b>
