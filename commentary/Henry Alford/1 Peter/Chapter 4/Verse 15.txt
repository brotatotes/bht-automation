<p><strong> 15</strong> .] In the name of Christ, I say: <strong> for let no one of you suffer</strong> (reproach or persecution: suffer in any way) <strong> as</strong> (being) <strong> a murderer, or a malefactor</strong> (as opposed to ἀγαθοποιῶν , ch. 1Pe 3:17 ), <strong> or as</strong> (the repetition of ὡς separates the following word from the foregoing, as belonging to a separate class) <strong> a prier into other men’s matters</strong> ( ὁ ἐπισκεπτόμενος τὰ ἀλλότρια . “Hanc explicationem,” says Gerhard, “probat 1) ipsa vocis compositio, 2) veterum expositio, Tert [21] Cypr [22] Aug [23] (Œc., ὁ τὰ ἀλλότρια περιεργαζόμενος ), 3) temporis et loci circumstantia. Procul dubio quidam Christiani, ex incogitantia, temeritate et levitate, in actiones infidelium utpote vicinorum suorum curiosius inquirebant, eas proprio arbitrio redarguebant, ac judices eorum esse volebant, quod non pertinebat ad eorum vocationem.” Wies. suggests that the word probably alludes to the ἐπίσκοπος of the church, combining it with ἀλλοτριο -, to shew the incongruity).
