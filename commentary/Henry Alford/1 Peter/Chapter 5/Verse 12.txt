<b> 12.]</b><b> By Silvanus the faithful brother</b> (there seems to be no reason for distinguishing this Silvanus from the companion of St. Paul and Timothy, mentioned in 1 Thess. i. 1; 2 Thess i. 1; 2 Cor. i. 19, and known by the name of Silas in the Acts. See further in the Introduction),<b> as I reckon</b> (indicates the Apostle’s judgment concerning Silvanus, given, not in any disparagement of him, nor indicating that he was not known to St. Peter, but as fortifying him, in his mission to the churches addressed, with the Apostle’s recommendation, over and above the acquaintance which the readers may already have had with him),<b> I have written unto you</b> (these words “<i>unto you</i>” are taken by some, as the A.V., as dependent on “<i>faithful,</i>” which is harsh, leaves the verb “<i>I hare written</i>” without any object of address)<b> in</b> (literally,<i> by means of</i>,—as my vehicle of conveying my meaning)<b> few words</b> (this may perhaps refer to some more copious instructions Silvanus was to give them by word of mouth: or may serve to fix their attention more pointedly on that which had been thus concisely said)<b>, exhorting</b> (such in the main is the character of the Epistle)<b> and giving my testimony that this</b> (of which I have written to you; see below)<b> is the true grace</b> (as testified by the preaching of the Apostles to be covenanted and granted to them by God. This identification of the preached and written message with the true mind of God towards man, is not uncommon with our Apostle: e.g., ch. i. 12, 25 [ii. 10, 25]. The reason of this was not any difference, as some would have us believe, between the teachings of St. Peter and St. Paul, but the difficulty presented to the readers in the fact of the fiery trial of sufferings which they were passing through)<b> of God, in which stand ye</b> (the construction in the original is what is called<i> pregnant;</i> into which being admitted, stand in it. This is a short and earnest exhortation, containing in it the pith of what has been said by way of exhortation in the whole Epistle).
