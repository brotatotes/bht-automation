<p><strong> 10</strong> .] The especial aspect of the faith of the Thessalonians was <em> hope</em> : hope of the return of the Son of God from heaven: a hope, indeed, common to them with all Christians in all ages, but evidently entertained by them as pointing to an event more immediate than the church has subsequently believed it to be. Certainly these words would give them an idea of the <em> nearness</em> of the coming of Christ: and perhaps the misunderstanding of them may have contributed to the notion which the Apostle corrects, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:1">2 Thessalonians 2:1</span> ff.: see note there. By <strong> ὃν ἤγ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἐκ τῶν νεκρῶν</strong> , that whereby ( Rom 1:4 ) Jesus was declared to be the Son of God with power, is emphatically prefixed to His name.
	</p>
<p><strong> τὸν ῥυόμενον</strong> ] <strong> who delivereth</strong> : not = τ . ῥυσόμενον , still less as E. V., <em> past</em> , ‘ <em> who delivered</em> ,’ but descriptive of His office, = ‘our Deliverer,’ as ὁ πειράζων , &amp;c.
	</p>
