<p><strong> 6</strong> .] Further proof of the same, that ye are ἐκλεκτοί , by the method in which <em> you received</em> the Gospel thus preached by us. καὶ <strong> ὑμεῖς</strong> corresponds with τὸ εὐ . <strong> ἡμῶν</strong> above. It is somewhat difficult here to fix exactly the point of comparison, in which they imitated their ministers and Christ. Certainly it is not merely, in <em> receiving the word</em> for to omit other objections, this would not apply at all to Him: and therefore, not in any qualifying detail of their <em> method</em> of reception of the word not in δύναμις , nor in πν . ἁγ ., nor in πληρ . πολλ .
	</p>
<p>So far being clear, we have but one particular left, and that respects the circumstances under which, and the spirit with which: and here we find a point of comparison even with Christ Himself: viz. joyful endurance in spirit under sufferings. This it was in which they imitated the Apostles, and their divine Master, and which made them patterns to other churches (see below).
	</p>
