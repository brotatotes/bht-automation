<p><strong> ἤπιοι</strong> , <strong> mild</strong> : so Od. β . 47, πατὴρ δ ʼ ὡς ἤπιος ἦεν : Herodian iv. 1, ἤπιον ἄρχοντα κ . πατέρα : Pausan. Surely the reading νήπιοι , being (1) by far the <em> commoner word</em> , (2) so <em> easily introduced</em> by the final ν of the preceding word, can hardly, in the teeth of the sense, come under consideration: seeing too that the primary authorities are not unanimous.

<p><strong> ἐν μέσῳ ὑμ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] i.e. ‘in our converse with you;’ but with an allusion to our not lifting ourselves above you; ὡς εἷς ἐξ ὑμῶν , Œc. It is best to retain the comma after ὑμῶν , not as Lün., to place a colon: for though there is a break in the construction, it is one occasioned by the peculiar style of the Apostle, which should not be amended by punctuation. The emphasis on <strong> ἑαυτῆς</strong> should not be lost sight of <strong> as when a nurse</strong> (a <em> suckling mother</em> ) <strong> cherishes</strong> (reff.) <strong> her own children</strong> .
