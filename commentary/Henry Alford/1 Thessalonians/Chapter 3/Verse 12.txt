<p><strong> 12</strong> .] <strong> ὑμᾶς δέ</strong> emphatic ‘sive nos veniemus sivo minus,’ Bengel.
	</p>
<p><strong> ὁ κύριος</strong> may refer either to the Father, or to Christ. It is no objection to the former, that τ . θεοῦ κ . πατρ . ἡμ . is repeated below, any more than it is to the latter that τ . κυρ . ἡμ . <strong> Ἰ</strong> . is so repeated. I should rather understand (still, notwithstanding Ellic.’s note) it of the Father: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:8">2 Corinthians 9:8</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> πλεονάσαι</strong> ] transitive, see reff.: <strong> enlarge you</strong> not merely in <em> numbers</em> , as Thdrt., but in <em> yourselves</em> , in richness of gifts and largeness of faith and knowledge fill up your ὑστερήματα , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:10">1 Thessalonians 3:10</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> περισσεύσαι</strong> (reff.), <strong> make you to abound</strong> .
	</p>
<p><strong> εἰς πάντας</strong> ] <strong> toward all men</strong> , not, as Thdrt., πάντας τοὺς ὁμοπίστους , but as Est., ‘ <em> etiam infideles et vestræ salutisinimicos</em> .’
	</p>
