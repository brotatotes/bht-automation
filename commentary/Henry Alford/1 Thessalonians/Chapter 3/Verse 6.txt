<p><strong> 6</strong> .] <strong> ἄρτι δέ</strong> is by Lünem. (and De W. hesitatingly) separated by a comma from ἐλθόντος , and joined to παρεκλήθημεν <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:7">1 Thessalonians 3:7</span> . But the direct connexion of <strong> ἄρτι</strong> with an aorist verb is harsher than with an aorist participle, and <strong> παρεκλ</strong> <strong> .</strong> has already its <strong> διὰ τοῦτο</strong> , which refers back to the whole preceding clause as contained in the τοῦτο . I would therefore join ἄρτι with ἐλθόντος . <strong> But Timotheus having just now come</strong> &amp;c.

Thdrt. remarks: τρία τέθεικεν ἀξιέραστα , τὴν πίστιν , κ . τ . ἀγάπην , κ . τοῦ διδασκάλου τὴν μνήμην . δηλοῖ ἡ μὲν πίστις τῆς εὐσεβείας τὸ βέβαιον ἡ δὲ ἀγάπη τὴν πρακτικὴν ἀρετήν · ἡ δὲ τοῦ διδασκάλου μνήμη , κ . ὁ περὶ αὐτὸν πόθος , μαρτυρεῖ τῇ περὶ τὴν διδασκαλίαν στοργῇ .

<p><strong> πάντοτε</strong> belongs more naturally to the foregoing: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:4">1 Corinthians 1:4</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:58">1 Corinthians 15:58</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:18">Galatians 4:18</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:20">Ephesians 5:20</span> . “ ἐπιποθεῖν τι (huc etiam redire structuram ἐπιποθεῖν sq. infinitivo nemo nescit) idem valet quod πόθον ἔχειν ἐπί τι , desiderium ferre in aliquid versum, cf. LXX. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ps+42:0">Psalms 42:0</span> (41):1, ὃν τρόπον ἐπιποθεῖ ἡ ἔλαφος ἐπὶ τὰς πηγὰς τῶν ὑδάτων .” Fritz. in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:11">Romans 1:11</span> . So that <em> direction</em> , not intensity (which as Fritz. also remarks, after the analogy of περιπόθητος , should be expressed by <strong> περι</strong> -, not ἐπιποθεῖν ) is the force of the preposition.
