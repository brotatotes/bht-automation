<p><strong> 12</strong> .] <em> Purpose of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:11">1 Thessalonians 4:11</span></em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> εὐσχημόνως</strong> ] <strong> honourably</strong> : ἀτάκτως , 2Th 3:6 ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:11">2 Thessalonians 3:11</span> , is the opposite.
	</p>
<p><strong> πρός</strong> , <strong> with regard to</strong> : as in the proverb οὐδὲν πρὸς Διόνυσον , πρὸς Τιμόθεον πρᾶξαι , Demosth., p. 1185. See Bernhardy, p. 265.
	</p>
<p><strong> τοὺς ἔξω</strong> ] the unbelieving world (reff.).
	</p>
<p><strong> μηδενός</strong> (subjective, as ruled by the χρείαν ἔχητε ) is much better taken <em> neuter</em> than masculine; for as Lün. observes, to stand in need of <em> no man</em> , is for man an impossibility.</p>
