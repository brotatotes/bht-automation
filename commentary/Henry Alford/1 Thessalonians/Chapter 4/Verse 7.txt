<p><strong> 7</strong> .] This verse (see above) is in my view decisive for the above rendering of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:6">1 Thessalonians 4:6</span> . There is no mention here of <em> avarice</em> : nor is it possible to understand <strong> ἀκαθαρσία</strong> , when 1Th 4:3 has gone before, of any thing but carnal impurity. Chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:3">1 Thessalonians 2:3</span> , which is adduced to shew that it may here represent covetousness, is a very doubtful example: see there.
