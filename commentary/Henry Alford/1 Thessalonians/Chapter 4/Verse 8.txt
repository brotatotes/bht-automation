<p><strong> 8</strong> .] Hence, the sin of (rejecting) setting at nought such limitations and rules is a fearful one no less than that of setting at nought God the giver of the Holy Spirit. In <strong> ἄνθρωπον ἀθετεῖ</strong> there is an obvious allusion to ὑπερβαίνειν κ . πλεονεκτεῖν τ . ἀδελφόν above. There is no need to supply any thing after <strong> ἀθετων</strong> <strong> </strong> <strong> ὁ ἀθετῶν</strong> simply describes him who commits the act of rejecting; q. d. the <strong> rejecter</strong> <em> what</em> he rejects, is not to be supplied in the construction, but is clear from the context viz. τὸν ἀδελφὸν αὐτοῦ . The distinction between ἄνθρωπον (anarthrous) and <strong> τὸν</strong> θεόν , seems to be, that the former is indefinite; <strong> not</strong> (any) <strong> man, but</strong> (definite) <strong> God</strong> .
	</p>
<p><strong> τὸν</strong> [ <strong> καὶ</strong> ] <strong> δόντα</strong> ] q. d. who also is the AUTHOR of our sanctification.
	</p>
<p>[ <strong> καί</strong> ‘novum hic additur momentum,’ Bengel. It introduces <strong> a</strong> climax, whereby the sin is intensified.]
	</p>
<p><strong> δόντα</strong> , as being one great definite act of God by His Son.
	</p>
<p><strong> τὸ πν</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> αὐτοῦ τὸ ἅγ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] This form of expression (q. d. ‘His own ( αὐτοῦ emphatic) Spirit, the Holy One’) is probably chosen, and not τὸ ἅγ . πν αὐτοῦ , for precision, to bring out τὸ ἅγιον as connected with ἁγιασμός preceding.
