<b> 8.</b>] Hence, the sin of (rejecting) setting at nought such limitations and rules is a fearful one—no less than that of setting at nought God the giver of the Holy Spirit. In the words<b> despiseth</b><b> not man</b> there is an obvious allusion to<i> going beyond and overreaching</i> above. There is no need to supply any thing after<b> despiseth: he that despiseth</b> simply describes him who commits the act of despising,<b> the despiser</b>—<i>what</i> he despises, is not to be supplied in the construction, but is clear from the context—viz.<i> his brother</i>.
