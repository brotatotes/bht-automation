<p><strong> 14</strong> . <strong> ἀτάκτους</strong> ] This as ch. 1Th 4:11 , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:6">2 Thessalonians 3:6</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:11">2 Thessalonians 3:11</span> , certainly implies that there was reason to complain of this ἀταξία in the Thessalonian church. “ <strong> ἄτακτος</strong> is especially said of the soldier who does not remain in his rank: <em> so inordinatus</em> in Livy.” Lün.: hence <strong> disorderly</strong> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ὀλιγοψύχους</strong> ] such e.g. as needed the comfort of ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:13">1 Thessalonians 4:13</span> ff.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀντέχεσθε</strong> ] <strong> keep hold of</strong> (reff.) <strong> i.e. support</strong> .
	</p>
<p><strong> οἱ ἀσθενεῖς</strong> must, be understood of the spiritually weak, not the literally sick: see reff.
	</p>
<p><strong> πρὸς πάντας</strong> ] not, ‘all the foregoing’ ( ἀτάκτους , ὀλιγοψύχους , ἀσθενῶν ); but <strong> all men</strong> : cf. next verse.</p>
