<b> 4.</b>]<b> in darkness</b> refers back to “<i>in the night</i>” above—in the ignorance and moral slumber of the world which knows not God. Not, ‘<i>that</i> day,’ but the DAY—the meaning of the<b> day</b> as distinguished from<b> the darkness</b> being brought out, and<i> the day</i> being put in the place of emphasis accordingly. That this is so, is plain from what follows, ver. 5.
