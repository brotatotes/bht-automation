<p><strong> 4</strong> .] <strong> ἐν σκότει</strong> refers back to ἐν νυκτὶ above in the ignorance and moral slumber of the world which knows not God. τῷ παραβολικῷ ἐπέμεινε σχήματι , κ . σκότος μὲν καλεῖ τὴν ἄγνοιαν , ἡμέραν δὲ τὴν γνῶσιν , Thdrt. τὸν σκοτεινὸν κ . ἀκάθαρτον βίον φησί , Chrys. Both combined give the right meaning.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἵνα</strong> ] not ‘ <em> so that</em> ,’ here or any where else: but <strong> that, in order that</strong> : it gives the <em> purpose</em> in the divine arrangement: for with God all results are purposed.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἡ ἡμέρα</strong> ] not, ‘ <em> that</em> day,’ but <strong> the</strong> DAY the meaning of <strong> ἡμέρα</strong> as distinguished from <strong> σκότος</strong> being brought out, and ἡ ἡμέρα being put in the place of emphasis accordingly. This not having been seen, its situation was altered, to throw the <em> first</em> stress on ὑμᾶς , which properly has the <em> second</em> . That this is so, is plain from what follows, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:5">1 Thessalonians 5:5</span> .</p>
