<b> 14.]</b><i> Second reason</i>—as the woman was<i> last in being,</i> so she was<i> first in sin</i>—indeed<i> the only victim</i> of the Tempter’s deceit.<b> And Adam was not deceived</b> (the serpent<i> deceived</i> the woman: the woman did not<i> deceive</i> her husband, but<i> persuaded</i> him. We read of no communication between the serpent and the<i> man.</i> The “subtlest beast of all the field” knew his course better:<i> she</i> listened to the lower solicitation of sense and expediency: he to the higher one of conjugal ve)<b>, but the woman</b> (not now<i> Eve,</i> but generic, as the next clause shews: for Eve could not be the subject of what is said in the next verse)<b> having been seduced BY THE DECEIT</b> (the verb is one implying the full success of the<i> deceit</i>)<b> has become involved</b> (the thought is—the present state of transgression in which the woman [and the man too: but that is not treated here] by sin is constituted, arose [which was not. so in the man] from her originally having been<i> seduced by deceit</i>)<b> in transgression</b> (here as always, breach of a positive command: compare Rom. iv. 15).
