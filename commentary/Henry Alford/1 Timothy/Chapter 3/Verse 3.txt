<b> 3.]</b> not<b> a brawler</b> (properly, ‘<i>one in his cups,’</i> a man rendered petulant by much wine. And perhaps the literal meaning should not be lost sight of. At the same time the word and its cognates were often used without reference to wine: and it will be best to extend the meaning to signify rather the character, than the mere fact, of the circumstance),<b> not a striker</b> (this word also may have a literal and narrower, or a metaphorical and wider sense. In this latter it is taken by Theodoret. But perhaps the coarser literal sense is better, as setting forth more broadly the opposite to the character of a Christian<i> bishop</i>)<b>; but</b> (this contrast springs out of the two last, and is set off by them)<b> forbearing</b> (reasonable and gentle. See note on Phil. iv. 5),<b> averse from contention</b> (compare 2 Tim. ii. 24),<b> not a lover of money</b> (not as some render it,<i> liberal</i>: it is not the positive virtue of liberality, but the negative one of abstinence from love of money, which, though it may lead to the other in men who<i> have</i> money, is yet a totally distinct thing.
