<b> 7.</b>]<b> these things</b> most naturally applies to the characters just given of widows, not more generally: and in that case the words<i> “that they may be irreproachable”</i> must refer to the widows also, not to the<i> children</i> and<i> grandchildren</i>, or to these and the widows together, or more widely still. This narrower reference is confirmed by the next verse, which takes up the duty of the relations, being connected not by “<i>for,</i>” but by “<i>but.</i>”
