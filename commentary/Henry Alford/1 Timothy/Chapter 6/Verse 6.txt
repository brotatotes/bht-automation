<b> 6</b>.] He then goes off, on the mention of this erroneous view, to shew how it really stands with the Christian as to the desire of riches: its danger, and the mischief it has oceasioned.<b> But</b> (although they are in error in thus thinking, there<i> is</i> a sense in which such an idea is true, for)<b> godliness accompanied with contentment</b> (see above, and Phil. iv. 11)<b> is great</b> (means of)<b> gain</b> (alluding, not to the Christian’s reward in the next world,—but the [means of] gain is in the very fact of possessing piety joined with contentment, and thus being able to dispense with those things which we cannot carry away with us).
