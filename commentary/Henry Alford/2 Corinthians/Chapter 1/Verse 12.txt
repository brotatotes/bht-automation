<p><strong> καύχησις</strong> ] viewed in its ground and substance. But we must not say that it is <em> for</em> καύχημα : the Apostle regards the μαρτύριον and the καύχησις as coincident: it is not the testimony, &amp;c., <em> of which</em> he boasts, but <em> in which</em> his boasting itself consists.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἁγιότ</strong> .] ἁπλότητι seems to be a gloss from <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:5">Ephesians 6:5</span> : <strong> in holiness and sincerity of God</strong> : i.e. either ‘ <em> belonging to God</em> ,’ as ἡ δικαιος . αὐτοῦ , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:33">Matthew 6:33</span> , or ‘ <em> which is the gift of God</em> ,’ as in ref. Rom., or better than either, as E. V., ‘ <em> godly</em> ,’ i.e. maintained as in the service of and with respect to God. Calvin interprets it, ‘ <em> coram Deo</em> .’ See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:17">2 Corinthians 2:17</span> ; and on the senses of ἁγιότ . and ἁπλότ ., Stanley’s note.
	</p>
<p><strong> οὐκ ἐν σοφ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> σαρκ</strong> .] which fleshly wisdom is any thing but holy and pure, having many windings and insincerities in order to captivate men.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀλλ</strong> <strong> ʼ </strong> <strong> ἐν χάρ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> θεοῦ</strong> ] <strong> but in the grace of God</strong> , i.e. in that χάρις which he had received (ref. Rom.) εἰς ὑπακοὴν πίστεως ἐν πᾶσιν τοῖς ἔθνεσιν the grace of his apostleship. To this he often refers, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:3">Romans 12:3</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:15">Romans 15:15</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:2">Ephesians 3:2</span> , al.
	</p>
<p><strong> περισσοτέρως</strong> ] “Non quod apud alios minus sincere con-versatus fuisset; sed quia majora sinceræ suæ conversationis documenta apud Corinthios ostenderat: ut quibus gratis ac sine stipendio prædicasset evangelium, parcens eorum infirmitati.” Estius. But perhaps it may relate only to the longer time, and greater opportunities which he had had at Corinth for shewing his purity of purpose: so Calv., De W.</p>
