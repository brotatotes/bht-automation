<b> 22.]</b> This<i> sealing us</i> again cannot refer to the Apostles alone, nor is John vi. 27 any ground for such a reference,—but as in the other references, to all, sealed by the Holy Spirit to the day of redemption.<br><br><b> and gave...]</b> ‘And assured us of the fact of that sealing; Rom. viii. 16.<br><br><b> the earnest,</b> i.e.<b> the</b><b> pledge or token of the Spirit</b>: genitive of apposition: the Spirit<i> is</i> the token. The word in the original means the first deposit of purchase-money by which a bargain was ratified.
