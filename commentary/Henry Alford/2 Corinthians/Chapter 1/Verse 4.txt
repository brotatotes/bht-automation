This however will not explain all places where it occurs elsewhere: e.g. 1 Thessalonians 2:18 , ἠθελήσαμεν ἐλθεῖν πρὸς ὑμᾶς , ἐγὼ μὲν Παῦλος , καὶ ἅπαξ κ . δίς , where see note. So that after all perhaps it is best to regard it merely as an idiomatic way of speaking, when often only the singular is intended.
