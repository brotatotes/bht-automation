<b> 4.]</b><i> Enlargement of the idea.</i>—If the warfare were according to the flesh, its<i> weapons</i> would be carnal: whereas now, as implied, they are spiritual,<b>—powerful in the sight of God</b> (i. e.<i> ‘in a estimation, ‘after His rule of war-fare’</i>)<b> in order to pulling down of strong holds</b> (see Prov. xxi. 22. Stanley thinks that recollections of the Mithridatic and piratical wars may have contributed to this imagery. The second of these, not more than sixty years before the Apostle’s birth, and in the very scene of his earlier years, was ended by the reduction of 120 strong-holds, and the capture of more than 10, 000 prisoners).
