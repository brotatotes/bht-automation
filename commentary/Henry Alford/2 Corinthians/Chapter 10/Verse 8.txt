<b> 8.]</b> The<b> for</b> here shews that this verse is not the refutation of the charge of walking accord-ing to the flesh, but a reason rendered for the request made above; and the words<i> “in the flesh”</i> and<i> “according to the flesh”</i> allude only to the charge just men-tioned. This indeed is shewn by the use, and enlargement in vv. 4–6, of the idea of<i> warring</i>, instead of that of<i> walking</i>:—they who accuse us of walking after the flesh, shall find that we do not<i> war</i> after the flesh: therefore compel us not to use our weapons.<br><br><b> 8.]</b> This is shewn to be so. Even more boasting than he had ever yet made of his apostolic power, would not disgrace him, but would be borne out by the fact.
