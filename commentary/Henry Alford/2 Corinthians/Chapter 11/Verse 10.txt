<p><strong> 10.</strong> ] <strong> The truth of Christ is in me, that</strong> …; i.e. ‘ <em> I speak according to that truth of which Christ Himself was our example, when I say, that</em> …;’ there is no oath, nor even asseveration, as E. V. and most Commentators introduce. The expression is exactly analogous to <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:1">Romans 9:1</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἡ καύχ</strong> .…] <strong> this boasting</strong> (not = καύχημα , here or any where else) <strong> shall not be stopped</strong> (supply τὸ στόμα , which is not expressed, because καύχησις being itself a matter of <em> utterance</em> , suits the sense of the verb without it) <strong> as regards</strong> (or <strong> against) me</strong> ( καύχ . is as it were personified shall not have its mouth stopped as regards me) <strong> in the regions of Achaia</strong> (where the καύχησις is imagined as <em> being</em> and <em> speaking</em> ).</p>
