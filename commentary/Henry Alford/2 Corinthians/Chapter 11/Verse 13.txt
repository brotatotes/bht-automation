<p><strong> 13.</strong> ] <strong> For</strong> (see above: the γάρ implying also that the choice of the above line of conduct has been made in a conviction of their falsehood and its efficacy to detect it) <strong> such men are false Apostles</strong> (not, as Vulg. and most expositors, ‘ <em> such false Apostles are</em> <strong> ἐργ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> δόλ</strong> .,’ which destroys the whole emphasis of the sentence, wherein the <strong> ὑπερλίαν</strong> ἀπόστολοι of 2Co 11:5 are pronounced now to be <strong> ψευδ</strong> απόστολοι : and besides, suggests an irrelevant comparison between οἱ <strong> τοιοῦτοι</strong> ψ . and ψ . of some other kind.
	</p>
<p>On the sense, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="re+2:2">Revelation 2:2</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ὁ τοιοῦτος</strong> is a familiar designation with the Apostle, see reff.), <strong> dishonest workmen</strong> (in that they pretend to be teachers of the Gospel, and are in the mean time subserving their own ends), <strong> changing themselves into</strong> (in appearance: the pres. participle indicates their <em> habit</em> and continual endeavours to assume the shape) <strong> Apostles of Christ</strong> . By a <em> fair</em> comparison between us, this mask will be stript off; by the abundance of my sufferings, and distinctions vouchsafed by the Lord, my Apostolicity will be fully proved, and their Pseudapostolism shewn.</p>
