<p><strong> 19.</strong> ] Bitterly ironical. They were φρόνιμοι as <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:8">1 Corinthians 4:8</span> , κεκορεσμένοι so full of wisdom as to be able to tolerate complacently, looking down from the ‘sapientum templa serena,’ the follies of others. This, forsooth, encourages him to hope for their forbearance and patronage. Compare the earnestness of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:1-4">1 Corinthians 3:1-4</span> . And the irony does not stop here: it is not only matter of presumption that they <em> would</em> tolerate fools with complacency, but the matter of fact testified it: they <em> were doing this: and more</em> .</p>
