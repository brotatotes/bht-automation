<b> 21.] By way of disparagement I assume that WE</b> (emphatic)<b> were weak</b> (when we were among you). An ironical reminiscence of his own abstinence when among them from all these acts of self-exaltation at their expense; as much as to say (ironically), ‘I feel that I am much letting myself down by the confession that<i> I</i> was too weak ever to do any of these things among you.’ See this rendering defended in my Greek Test.<br><br><b> in foolishness]</b> see ver. 17.
