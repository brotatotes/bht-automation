<p><strong> 3.</strong> ] <em> But he fears their being seduced from their fidelity to Christ</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ὁ ὄφις</strong> ] He takes for granted that the Corinthians recognized the agency of Satan in the (well-known) serpent: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:13-15">2 Corinthians 11:13-15</span> , where his μετασχηματισμός for the sake of deceit is alluded to.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐν τῇ παν</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> αὐτοῦ</strong> ] <strong> in</strong> (i.e. <em> by means of</em> , as the <em> element</em> in which the deed was done) <strong> his versatility</strong> (or subtlety), <strong> so</strong> ( οὕτω has been a gloss from the margin) <strong> your thoughts</strong> (‘ <em> sentiments</em> ,’ ref. and ch. 2Co 10:5 ) <strong> be corrupted from</strong> (pregnant construction, = be corrupted, and seduced from) <strong> your simplicity</strong> (singleness of affection) <strong> and your chastity towards Christ</strong> ( <strong> εἰς χρ</strong> . is not = ἐν χριστῷ , as Vulg., E. V., Beza, Calvin, al.).</p>
