<b> 10.] Wherefore</b> (because of this relation to human weakness and divine power)<b> I am</b><br><br><b> well content</b> (it is the same word as that used Matt. iii. 17, “<i>in whom I am well pleased</i>”)<b> in infirmities</b> (four kinds of which are then specified,—all coming also, as well as<i> infirmities proper,</i> under the category of<b> infirmities</b>, as<i> hindrances and bafflings of human strength</i>),<b> in insults, in necessities, in persecutions, in distresses, on behalf of Christ: for whenever I am weak</b> (applying to all five situations above)<b>, then I am mighty.</b>
