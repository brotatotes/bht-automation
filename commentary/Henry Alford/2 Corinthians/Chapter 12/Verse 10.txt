<p><strong> 10.</strong> ] <strong> Wherefore</strong> (because of this relation to human weakness and divine power) <strong> I am well content</strong> [cf. the same expression Mat 3:17 ] <strong> in infirmities</strong> (four kinds of which are then specified, all coming also, as well as ἀσθ . <em> proper</em> , under the category of ἀσθένειαι , as <em> hindrances and bafflings of human strength</em> ), <strong> in insults, in necessities, in persecutions, in distresses, on behalf of Christ: for whenever I am weak</strong> (applying to all five situations above), then <strong> I am mighty</strong> . Wetst. quotes from Philo, Vita Mosis, i. 13, vol. ii. p. 92, μὴ ἀναπίπτετε . τὸ ἀσθενὲς ὑμῶν δύναμίς ἐστι .</p>
