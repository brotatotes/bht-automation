<b> 11.]</b><b> I am</b><b> BECOME</b> (the emphasis on the verb,—I am verily<i> become</i> a fool, viz. by this boasting, which I have now concluded, It is still ironical, spoken from the situation of his adversaries)<b> a fool: ye compelled me</b> (ye emphatic):<b> for I</b> (<b>I</b> also emphatic, but more with reference to what has passed: ‘<i>ye compelled me,</i> it was no doing of<i> mine</i>, for<i> I</i>, &c.’)<b> ought to have been recommended by you</b> (emphatic, by<i> you, not by himself</i>):<b> for I was nothing behind</b> (when I was with you)<b> these overmuch apostles</b> (see on ch. xi. 5: but here even more plainly than there, the expression cannot be applied to the other Apostles, seeing that the assertion would in that case be inconsistent with the fact—the Corinthians never having had an opportunity of comparing him with them),<b> even though I am nothing</b> (see similar expressions of humility, 1 Cor. xv. 9–11).
