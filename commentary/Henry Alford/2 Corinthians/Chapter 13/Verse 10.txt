<b> 10.]</b><b> For this cause</b> (<i> ‘because I wish and pray for your perfection</i>’).<br><br><b> these things,</b> ‘<i>this Epistle.’</i> “For I wish,” says Chrysostom, “that my sharpness should lie in my writings, and not in my acts.”<br><br><b> according to the power, &c.]</b> gives the reason why he did not wish to act<i> sharply</i>,<i>—because the power would seem to be exercised in a direction contrary to that intended by Him who gave it.</i>
