<p><strong> 10</strong> .] <em> Another assurance to encourage them in forgiving and reinstating the penitent;</em> that they need not be afraid of lack of apostolic authority or confirmation of their act from above he would ratify their forgiveness by his sanction.
	</p>
<p><strong> ῳ δὲ</strong> <strong> …</strong> ] ‘ <em> Your</em> forgiveness is <em> mine</em> :’ not said <em> generally</em> (as Meyer), but definitely, pointing at the one person here spoken of and no other.
	</p>
<p><strong> κἀγώ</strong> , scil. χαρίζομαι . Then he substantiates this assurance, by further assuring them, that his forgiveness of any fault in this case, if it takes place, takes place <em> on their account</em> . Meyer’s (former: now (4th edn.) abandoned) and Rückert’s rendering of <strong> κεχάρισμαι</strong> as <em> passive</em> , disturbs the whole sense of the passage, besides being inconsistent with the N.T. usage of the word, see reff.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐν προσώπῳ χριστοῦ</strong> ] either ‘ <em> in the presence of Christ</em> ,’ as in ref. Prov. (compare Mat 21:42 ), so Theodoret, Erasm., Beza, Calv., Olsh., De W., or, and far better, <strong> in the person of Christ</strong> , <em> acting as Christ</em> , in the same way as he had commanded the punishment ἐν τῷ ὀνόματι τοῦ κυρίου ἡμῶν Ἰησοῦ , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:4">1 Corinthians 5:4</span> ; so Vulg., Estius (who argues the matter at some length), Wetst., al.</p>
