<p><strong> 12</strong> .] To <em> Troas</em> , viz. on his journey from Ephesus, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:1-2">Acts 20:1-2</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:5-9">1 Corinthians 16:5-9</span> . “The art. perhaps indicates the region of ‘the Troad,’ rather than the city.” Stanley.
	</p>
<p><strong> εἰς τὸ εὐαγγ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> χρ</strong> .] <strong> for</strong> (the purpose of preaching) <strong> the Gospel of Christ</strong> . He had been before at Troas, but the vision of a Macedonian asking for help prevented his remaining there. He now revisited it, purposely to stay and preach. On his return to Asia he remained there seven days, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:6-12">Acts 20:6-12</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> καὶ θύρας</strong> <strong> …</strong> ] <strong> and an opportunity of apostolic action being afforded me;</strong> <strong> ἐν κυρίῳ</strong> defines the <em> sort</em> of action implied, and to which the door was opened. It is remarkable that in speaking of this journey, though not of the same place, Paul uses this expression, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:9">1 Corinthians 16:9</span> . Compare the interesting passage at Troas on his return from Europe the next spring, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:6-13">Acts 20:6-13</span> .</p>
