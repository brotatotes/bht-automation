<p><strong> 13.</strong> <strong> ἔσχηκα ἄνεσιν</strong> ] perf. in the sense of aorist, as ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:9">2 Corinthians 1:9</span> . <strong> I had not rest for my spirit</strong> (not, ‘ <em> in</em> my spirit:’ compare οὐχ εὑροῦσα ἡ περιστερὰ ἀνάπαυσιν τοῖς ποσὶν αὐτῆς , Gen 8:9 ). He could not with any tranquillity prosecute the spiritual duties opened to him at Troas.
	</p>
<p><strong> τῷ μὴ εὑρ</strong> .] <strong> by</strong> (reason of) <strong> my not finding</strong> : see reff.
	</p>
<p>Paul had sent Titus to Corinth, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:18">2 Corinthians 12:18</span> , partly to finish the collection for the saints, but principally to bring intelligence respecting the effect of the first Epistle. Probably it had been fixed that they should meet at Troas.
	</p>
<p><strong> τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἀδελ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> μου</strong> implies a relation closer than merely that of Christian brotherhood my <em> colleague</em> in the Apostleship.
	</p>
<p><strong> αὐτοῖς</strong> ] the disciples there: understood from the context.</p>
