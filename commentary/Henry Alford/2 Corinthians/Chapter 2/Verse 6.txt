<b> 6. to such a man]</b> Meyer remarks on the expression as being used in mildness, not to designate any particular person: but the same designation is employed in 1 Cor. v. 5.<b> this punishment:</b><i> what it was,</i> we are unable with certainty to say; but 1 Cor. v. seems to point to<i> excommunication</i> as forming at least a<i> part</i> of it. But it was not a formal and public, only a<i> voluntary individual abstinence from communion</i> with him, as is shewn by its being inflicted by “<i>the more part of them</i>” only: the anti-pauline party probably refusing compliance with the Apostle’s command.
