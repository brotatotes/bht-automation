<p><strong> 6.] Who also</strong> (= ‘qui idem;’ so Eur. Bacch. 572, ταῦτα καὶ καθύβρισ ʼ αὐτόν , ‘hæc eadem illi exprobravi.’ See Hartung, Partikellehre, i. p. 132) <strong> enabled us as ministers of the</strong> (or, as Stanley, “a:” but not necessarily from the omission of the art.: cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:24">Hebrews 12:24</span> , καὶ διαθήκης νέας μεσίτῃ Ἰησοῦ ) <strong> new Covenant</strong> (i.e. the <em> gospel</em> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:7">Ephesians 3:7</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="col+1:23">Colossians 1:23</span> , as distinguished from the <em> law</em> ; see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:25">1 Corinthians 11:25</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:24">Galatians 4:24</span> : the πλάκες λίθιναι and σάρκιναι are still borne in mind, and lead on to a fuller comparison of the two covenants), <strong> not of</strong> (governed by διακόνους , not by καινῆς διαθ . ‘ <em> ministers, not of</em> ’.…) <strong> letter</strong> (in which, viz. in formal and literal precept, the Mosaic law consisted), <strong> but of Spirit</strong> (in which, viz. in the inward guiding of the Spirit of God, the gospel consists. Bengel remarks: ‘Paulus etiam dum hæc <em> scripsit</em> , non literæ, sed <em> spiritus</em> ministerium egit. Moses in proprio illo officio suo, etiam cum haud scripsit, tamen in litera versatus est’): <strong> for the letter</strong> (mere formal and literal precept, of the law) <strong> killeth</strong> (as in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:0">Romans 7:0</span> , brings the knowledge of sin, its guilt and its punishment. The reference is not, as Meyer, to <em> natural death</em> , which is the result of sin even where there is <em> no law</em> ; nor as Chrys. to the law <em> executing punishment</em> ), <strong> but the Spirit</strong> (of the gospel, i.e. God’s Holy Spirit, acting in and through Christ, Who ἐγένετο εἰς <strong> πνεῦμα ζωοποιοῦν</strong> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:45">1 Corinthians 15:45</span> . See also below, 2Co 3:17 ) <strong> giveth life</strong> (not merely life eternal, but the <em> whole new life</em> of the man of God, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:4">Romans 6:4</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:11">Romans 6:11</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:2">Romans 8:2</span> ; Rom 8:10 ). On the history of this meaning of γράμμα , see Stanley’s note.</p>
