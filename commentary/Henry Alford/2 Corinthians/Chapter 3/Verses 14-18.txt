<p><strong> 14 18</strong> .] <em> The contrast is now made between the</em> CHILDREN OF ISRAEL, <em> on whose heart this vail still is in the reading of the O. T., and</em> US ALL (Christians), <em> who with uncovered face behold the glory of the Lord</em> . This section is parenthetical. Before and after it, the <em> ministry</em> is the subject: in it, <em> they to whom the ministry is directed</em> . But it serves to shew the <em> whole spirit</em> and <em> condition</em> of the two classes, and thus further to substantiate the character of openness and freedom asserted of the Christian ministry.</p>
