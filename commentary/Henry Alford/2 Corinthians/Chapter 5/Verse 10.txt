<b> 10.] For</b> (explanation and fixing of the words “<i>we may be well-pleasing unto Him</i>,” as to<i> when</i> and<i> how testified</i>)<b> we all</b> (and myself among the number)<b> must be made manifest</b> (not merely<i> ‘appear</i>,’ which is a most unfortunate rendering of the A.V., giving to the reader merely the idea of “appearing before,” as when summoned to a magistrate)<b> before the judgment seat</b> (the<i> bema</i>, or “lofty seat, raised on an elevated platform, usually at the end of the basilica, or hall of justice, so that the figure of the judge must: have been seen towering above the crowd which thronged the long nave of the building.” Stanley)<b> of Christ; that each one may receive</b> (the technical word for<i> receiving wages</i>)<b> the things (done) in the body</b> (literally,<b> through the body,</b> as a medium or organ of action),<b> according to the things which he did</b> (in the body),<b> whether</b> (it were)<b> good or bad</b> (singular, as abstract). I may observe that no more definite inference must be drawn from this verse as to the place which the saints of God shall hold in the general judgment, than it warrants; viz. that they as well as others, shall be manifested and judged by Him (Matt. xxv. 19):<i> when</i>, or<i> in company with whom,</i> is not here so much as hinted.
