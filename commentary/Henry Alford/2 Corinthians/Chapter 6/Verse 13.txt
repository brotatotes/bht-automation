<p><strong> 13.</strong> ] <strong> τὴν αὐτὴν ἀντιμ</strong> ., as τὸν ὅμοιον τρόπον <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:7">Jude 1:7</span> , κλισίας , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:14">Luke 9:14</span> , not governed by κατά understood, but in fact an accus. of a <em> remoter object</em> , answering in many cases exactly to the <em> further removed of the two accusatives</em> in the <em> double accusative</em> government. The sense seems to be compounded of τὸν αὐτὸν τρόπον and ἀντιμισθίαν , <strong> In the same manner, as a return for my largeness of heart to you</strong> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ὡς τέκνοις λ</strong> <strong> .</strong> explains ἀντιμισθίαν , it being naturally expected of children that they should <em> requite</em> the love and care of their parents, by corresponding love and regard.</p>
