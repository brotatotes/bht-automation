<b> 3.] I do not say it</b> (ver. 2)<b> for condemnation</b> (with a condemnatory view, in a spirit of blame: there is no “<i>you</i>,” or “<i>of you</i>,” expressed, nor should it be supplied. He means, ‘I do not say ver. 2 in any but a loving spirit’):<b> for</b> (and this shews it)<b> I have said before</b> (viz. ch. vi. 11 f. see note there),<b> that ye are in our hearts</b> (this was implied in ch, vi. 11)<b> to die together and live together</b>, i.e. ‘<i>so that I could die with you or live with you.</i>’
