<b> 9.]</b> Now, emphatic as distinguishing his present joy from his repentance:<i> now</i> that I know not only of your grief, but of its being grief which worked repentance.<br><br><b> after a godly manner]</b> literally, ‘<i>with reference to God,</i>’ i.e. with a mind regarding God’s estimate of your conduct.<br><br><b> in order that ye might in nothing be damaged by us</b>: not ‘<i>so that ye did not...</i>,’ as many Commentators:—the<i> divine purpose</i> of their grief is indicated; ‘God so brought it about, in order that your grief occasioned by me might have, not an injurious, but a beneficial effect.’
