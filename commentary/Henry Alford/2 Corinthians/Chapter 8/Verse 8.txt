<b> 8.]</b> Lest his last words should be misunderstood, he explains the spirit in which they were said: not as a command, but by way of inducement, by mention of the earnestness of others, and to try the genuineness of their love.<br><br><b> by means of]</b> not as A.V., “<i>by occasion of,</i>” which is an ungrammatical rendering of the original: he treats the<b> zeal of others</b> as<i> the instrument by which,</i> in the way of emulation, the effect was to be produced.
