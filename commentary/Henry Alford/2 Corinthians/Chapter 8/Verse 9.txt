<p><strong> ὅτι</strong> ] <strong> consisting in this, that …
	</strong></p>
<p><strong> πλ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ὤν</strong> ] The participle refers to the time when the historic act implied in the aorist ἐπτώχευσεν took place. <strong> He, being rich, became poor:</strong> not, as De W., merely by His renunciation of human riches during His life on earth, but <em> by His exinanition of His glory</em> ( Php 2:6-7 ), when, as Athanas. (contra Apol. ii. 11, vol. ii. (Migne), p. 757), τὴν πτωχεύσασαν φύσιν ἐν ἑαυτῷ ἀνελάβετο .
	</p>
<p>The stress is on <strong> δι</strong> <strong> ʼ </strong> <strong> ὑμᾶς</strong> , to raise the motive of gratitude the more effectually in them.
	</p>
<p><strong> τῇ ἐκ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> πτωχ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> πλουτήσητε</strong> ] <strong> that by His poverty</strong> (as the efficient cause) <strong> ye might become rich:</strong> viz. with the same wealth in which He was rich, the kingdom and glory of Heaven, including τὰ μυρία ἅπερ παρέσχεν ἡμῖν ἀγαθά as Chrys. (Hom. xvii. p. 559): who had just before said, εἰ μὴ πιστεύεις , ὅτι ἡ πτωχεία πλούτου ἐστὶ ποιητική , ἐννόησόν σου τὸν δεσπότην , καὶ οὐκέτι ἀμφιβάλῃς (al. - λεῖς ). See the various possible meanings discussed in Stanley’s note.</p>
