<p><strong> 13.</strong> ] <strong> they</strong> (the recipients) <strong> glorifying God</strong> (the participle as in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:11">2 Corinthians 9:11</span> , an anacoluthon) <strong> by means of</strong> (the proof, &amp;c., is <em> the occasion, by means of which</em> ) <strong> the proof</strong> (i.e. the tried reality the substantial help yielded by) <strong> of this</strong> (your) <strong> ministration, for the subjection of your confession as regards the Gospel of Christ</strong> (i.e. that your ὁμολογία , (= ‘you who confess Christ,’) ‘is really and truly subject in holy obedience, as regards the gospel of Christ.’ But εἰς must not be joined with ὑποταγῇ , as ‘ <em> obedience to</em> ,’ or (E. V.) ‘ <em> subjection unto</em> ,’ which is unexampled, and would more naturally have the art., τῇ εἰς : it is <strong> towards</strong> , ‘ <em> in reference to</em> ,’ as in ref.) <strong> and liberality of your contribution as regards them and as regards all men</strong> (the same remarks apply to εἰς as above). Meyerwould render ἁπλότητι τῆς κοινωνίας , ‘ <em> the genuineness of your fellowship</em> :’ but see note on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:8">Romans 12:8</span> , and <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:26">Romans 15:26</span> . He also makes τῇ ὑποταγῇ τῆς ὁμολ ., ‘your subjection <em> to</em> your confession,’ which perhaps <em> may be</em> , but disturbs the parallel of ἁπλότητι τ . κοιν .</p>
