<p><strong> 2.</strong> ] <strong> For</strong> (ground of περισσόν ἐστι ) <strong> I am aware of your readiness of which</strong> (reff.) <strong> I am in the habit of boasting concerning you to Macedonians</strong> (Bengel remarks on the pres., ‘adhuc erat Paulus in Macedonia’) <strong> that Achaia</strong> (not ὑμεῖς he relates his own words to the Macedonians) <strong> has been ready</strong> (viz. to <em> send off the money</em> : καὶ οὐδὲν λείπει εἰ μὴ τὸ ἐλθεῖν τοὺς δεξομένους τὰ χρήματα , Theophyl. The Apostle, judging by their readiness, had made this boast concerning them, supposing it was really so. That this is the sense is shewn by ἀπαρασκευάστους below, 2Co 9:4 ) <strong> from last year</strong> (reff.): <strong> and the zeal which proceeds from you</strong> (‘which has its source in you and whose influence goes forth from you:’ so ὁ ἐκ Πελοποννήσου πόλεμος , οἱ ἐκεῖθεν , and the like) <strong> stirred up the greater number of them</strong> (but not <em> only</em> the example of your zeal: see ch. 2Co 8:1 ).</p>
