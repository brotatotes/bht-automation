<p><strong> 11</strong> .] <strong> for thus</strong> (i. e. ταῦτα ποιοῦντες ) <strong> shall be richly</strong> (the adverb <strong> πλουσίως</strong> is not, as Huther, surprising, but most natural and obvious with the verb ἐπιχορηγηθήσεται , which is one of furnishing and ministering; therefore of quantity. The adverb belongs to the figure latent in the verb: and must therefore be interpreted in and with the interpretation of the verb: in which case it will indicate high degrees and fulness of glory) <strong> furnished to you</strong> (the verb seems expressly chosen in order to answer to ἐπιχορηγήσατε , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:5">2 Peter 1:5</span> ; “furnish forth your own lives with these Christian graces, so shall be furnished to you” &amp;c.) <strong> the</strong> [or, <strong> your</strong> ] <strong> entrance</strong> (which all Christians look for: not the fact of this entrance taking place, but the fact of its πλουσίως ἐπιχορηγηθῆναι , is that asserted) <strong> into the eternal kingdom of our Lord and Saviour Jesus Christ</strong> .</p>
