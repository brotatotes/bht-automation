<b> 15.] Moreover I will endeavour that ye may on every occasion have it in your power after my decease</b> (it is at least remarkable that, with the recollection of the scene on the mount of transfiguration floating in his mind, the Apostle should use so close together the words which were there also associated, viz.<b> tabernacle</b> and<b> decease</b> [<i>exodus</i>]: see Luke ix. 28 ff. The coincidence should not be forgotten in treating of the question of the genuineness of the Epistle)<b> to exercise the memory of these things.</b>
