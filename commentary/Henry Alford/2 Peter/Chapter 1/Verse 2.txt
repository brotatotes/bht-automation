<b> 2.]</b> Grace to<b> you and peace be multiplied</b> (so in 1 Pet. i. 2: but further specified here by what follows)<b> in</b> (as the vehicle, or conditional element of the multiplication)<b> the knowledge</b> (properly, full, or ripe knowledge: but this can hardly be given in English without too strong a phrase)<b> of God, and of Jesus our Lord</b> (every unusual expression, like<b> Jesus our Lord</b>, occurring only Rom. iv. 24, should be noticed as a morsel of evidence to the independence of the Epistle).
