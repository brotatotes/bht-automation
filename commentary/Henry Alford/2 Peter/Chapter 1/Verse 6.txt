<p><strong> 6</strong> .] <strong> and in your knowledge, self-restraint</strong> ( ἐγκράτεια , τὸ μηδενὶ ἀποσύρεσθαι πάθει , as Œc. “Temperance” is now too much used of one sort only of self-restraint, fully to express the word. The Commentators compare Sir 18:30 , where under the head ἐγκράτεια ψυχῆς is said, ὀπίσω τῶν ἐπιθυμιῶν σου μὴ πορεύου , καὶ ἀπὸ τῶν ὀρέξεών σου κωλύου . The connexion is: let such discriminating knowledge not be without its fruit, of steady holding in hand of the passions and tempers), <strong> and in your self-restraint, patient endurance</strong> (in afflictions and trials), <strong> and in your patient endurance, godliness</strong> (i. e. it is not to be mere brute Stoical endurance, but united with God-fearing and God-trusting. Or it <em> may</em> perhaps be used without direct reference to God, as in Dio Cass. xlviii. 5, διὰ τὴν πρὸς τὸν ἀδελφὸν εὐσέβειαν : but the other is much more likely in the N. T.: especially as the social virtues follow),</p>
