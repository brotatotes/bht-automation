<b> 6.] and in your knowledge, self-restraint</b> ( “<i>temperance</i>” is perhaps now too much used of one sort only of self-restraint, fully to express the word. The connexion is: let such discriminating knowledge not be without its fruit, of steady holding in hand of the passions and tempers);<b> and in your self-restraint, patient endurance</b> (in afflictions and trials);<b> and in your patient endurance, godliness</b> (i. e. it is not to be mere brute Stoical endurance, but united with God-fearing and God-trusting);
