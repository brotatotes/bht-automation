<b> 7.] and in your godliness, brotherly kindness</b> (not suffering your godliness to be moroseness, nor a sullen solitary habit of life, but kind and generous and courteous)<b>; and in your brotherly kindness, love</b> (universal kindness of thought, word, and act towards all: a catholic large-heartedness, not confining the spirit of<i> brotherly kindness</i> to<i> brethren</i> only, Matt. v. 46, 47. So that these two last correspond to the “<i>love for one another and to all</i>” of 1 Thess. iii. 12).
