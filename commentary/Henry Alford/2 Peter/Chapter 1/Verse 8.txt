<p><strong> 8</strong> .] <strong> For these things</strong> (the above-mentioned graces) <strong> being in you</strong> ( <strong> ὑπάρχειν</strong> of previous subsistence, εἶναι of mere matter-of-fact being: see note on Act 16:20 ) <strong> and multiplying</strong> (not merely as E. V. “ <em> abounding</em> :” see reff.) <strong> render you</strong> (not pres. for future, but as expressing the habitual character and function of these virtues) <strong> not idle</strong> ( ἀργός = ἄεργος ) <strong> nor yet</strong> ( <strong> οὐδέ</strong> introduces a slight climax: a man may be in some sense not unworkful, but yet unfruitful) <strong> unfruitful towards</strong> ( <strong> εἰς</strong> not = ἐν as E. V. after Luth., Calv., Grot., al.: these virtues are all regarded as so many steps in advancing towards the ἐπίγνωσις of Christ, which is the great complex end of the Christian life) <strong> the perfect knowledge</strong> (here, considering the place which it holds, it is well to give the full sense of <strong> ἐπί</strong> γνωσις ) <strong> of our Lord Jesus Christ</strong> (in Him are hid, ethically as well as doctrinally, all the treasures of wisdom and knowledge: the knowledge of Him is the imitation of Him: for as it is true that hereafter the seeing Him as He is will ensure our being perfectly like Him, so it is true that here the only way in any degree increasingly to see Him as He is, is to become increasingly like Him. He only can declare Christ, who reflects Christ).</p>
