They promise that liberty of the sons of God, being themselves in the bondage of corruption.  φθορά here, moral decay of sin, ending in perdition):  for by what (  ever )  a man is overcome, by the same he is also enslaved (cf. ref. John, πᾶς ὁ ποιῶν τὴν ἁμαρτίαν , δοῦλός ἐστιν τῆς ἁμαρτίας : and ref. Rom., ᾧ παριστάνετε ἑαυτοὺς δούλους εἰς ὑπακοήν , δοῦλοί ἐστε ᾧ ὑπακούετε . These passages were certainly in the Apostle’s mind.  ἡττάομαι , generally found with a gen. of the agent, has here a dat. The classical rendering here would be “in whatever a man is overcome (by another), in that particular he is also enslaved (by that other).” But the context makes it clear that the datives are intended to designate the agent, not the mode).
