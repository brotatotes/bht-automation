<b> 21.]</b><i> Reason of these last words</i>.<b> For it were better for them not to have known the way of righteousness</b> (viz. the Christian life:<i> the way of truth</i>, as in ver. 2)<b> than, having known it, to turn back from</b> (out of, as out of a way)<b> the holy commandment</b> (the moral law of the gospel: here so designated, because it is of moral corruption that the Apostle is treating)<b> delivered to them</b> (compare Jude 3, “<i>the faith once [for all] delivered to the saints</i>”).
