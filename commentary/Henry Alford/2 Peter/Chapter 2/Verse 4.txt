<p><strong> 4</strong> .] <em> First historical proof: the punishment of the apostate angels</em> . Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:6">Jude 1:6</span> . <strong> For</strong> (connect with the position immediately preceding, οἷς τὸ κρῖμα κ . τ . λ .) <strong> if God spared not angels having sinned</strong> (how, is not here specified; but Jude, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:6">2 Peter 2:6</span> , is more particular: see note there. <strong> ἁμαρτησάντων</strong> , anarthrous, is not = τῶν ἁμ ., “that sinned:” but carries a ratiocinative force, giving the reason of οὐκ ἐφείσατο : “for their sin”), <strong> but casting them into hell</strong> (the word is no where else found: but its meaning must be plain by analogy. Tartarus is no where else mentioned in the N. T. or LXX: there can be no doubt that it is used as equivalent to γέεννα . It seems best to take the verb absolutely, by itself, and join σειροῖς ζόφου to παρέδωκεν , as is done in E. V. So Huther after Calov., Pott, Wahl, al., against De Wette, Dietlein, al. The aor. participle is contemporary with the aor. verb παρέδωκεν , as in ἀποκριθεὶς εἶπε ) <strong> delivered</strong> ( <strong> them</strong> ) <strong> over</strong> (“ <strong> παρέδωκεν</strong> is here, as often, used with an implied idea of punishment.” Huther) <strong> to dens</strong> (so with the reading in txt: <strong> σειρός</strong> , the same as σίρος , or σιῤῥός , properly <em> a cave where corn is stored</em> , so Demosth. p. 100 ult., ὀλυρῶν τῶν ἐν τοῖς Θρᾳκίοις σιροῖς , also p. 135. 5. The form σειρός is found (as a var. read. in Demosth. also) in Pollux ix. 49; Phot. p. 504. 23; Varro de re rust. i. 57. The word is used for a <em> wolf’s den</em> , by Longus i. 11. The other reading, σειραῖς , has perhaps come from the δεσμοῖς ἀϊδίοις of [7] Jude, and would seem to suit the sense better: see there) <strong> of darkness</strong> (if the reading σειραῖς be retained, the expression is remarkably illustrated by Wis 17:17 , ἁλύσει σκότους ἐδέθησαν : and will probably mean, as there, that darkness itself is the chain, gen. of apposition) <strong> in custody</strong> (pres.: “ <em> being kept</em> .” The readings are in great confusion, from the combined influence of [8] Jude, and our 2Pe 2:9 ) <strong> unto</strong> (with a view to: or merely temporal, <em> until</em> : but this is not probable here, as the want of μεγάλης ἡμέρας , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:6">Jude 1:6</span> , removes all definite allusion to the <em> time</em> of the judgment) <strong> judgment:
