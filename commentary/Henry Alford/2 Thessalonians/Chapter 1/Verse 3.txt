<b> 3.</b>]<b> as it is meet</b>—refers to the whole preceding sentence.<br><br><b> because</b> does not state why we give thanks, but is dependent on the clause preceding, it is meet, because, &c.— “<b>We are bound</b> expresses the duty of thanksgiving from its<i> subjective</i> side as an inward conviction,—<b>as it is meet</b>, on the other hand, from the<i> objective</i> side, as something answering to the state of circumstances.” Lünemann.
