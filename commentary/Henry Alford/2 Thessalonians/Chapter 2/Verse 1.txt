<p><strong> 1</strong> .] <strong> But</strong> (passing from those things which he prays <em> for</em> them, to those which he prays <em> of</em> them) <strong> we entreat</strong> (reff.) <strong> you, brethren</strong> (to win their affectionate attention), <strong> in regard of</strong> (the Vulg., E. V., and many ancient Commentators, render ὑπέρ , ‘ <em> per</em> ,’ ‘ <em> by</em> ,’ and understand it as introducing a <em> formula jurandi</em> , as in Il. ω . 466, καί μιν ὑπὲρ πατρὸς … λίσσεο . But this construction is not found in the N. T.; and it is most unnatural that the Apostle should thus conjure them by that, concerning which he was about to teach them. It is best therefore to take <strong> ὑπέρ</strong> , as so often, <em> not quite</em> = περί , but very nearly so, the meaning ‘ <em> on behalf of</em> ’ being slightly hinted for the subject had been misrepresented, and justice is done to it by the Apostle; and so Chrys. ( περὶ τῆς παρουσίας τ . χριστοῦ ἐνταῦθα διαλέγεται κ . περὶ τῆς ἐπισυναγ . ἡμῶν ) al.: see reff.) <strong> the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ, and our gathering together</strong> (i.e. the gathering together of us, announced in 1Th 4:17 ) <strong> to Him</strong> (Lün. condemns <em> to</em> , and would render ‘ <em> up to</em> ’ as <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:17">1 Thessalonians 4:17</span> ; but so much does not seem to lie in the preposition),</p>
