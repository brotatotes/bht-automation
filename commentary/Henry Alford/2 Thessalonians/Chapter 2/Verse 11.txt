<b> 11.</b>]<b> And on this account</b> (because they did not receive, &c.)<b> God is sending to them</b> (not as A.V., ‘<i>shall send</i>:’ the verb is<i> present</i>, because the mystery of iniquity is already working. God’s<i> sending</i> must not for a moment be understood of<i> permissiveness</i> only on God’s part—He is the judicial sender and doer—it is He who hardens the heart which has chosen the evil way. All such distinctions are the merest folly: whatever God<i> permits</i>, he<i> ordains</i>)<b> the working of delusion</b> (is causing these seducing influences to work among them. The A.V. has weakened, indeed almost stultified the sentence, by rendering these words ‘<i>a strong delusion</i>,’ i.e. the passive state resulting, instead of the active cause),<b> in order that they should believe the falsehood</b> (which the mystery of sin is working among them):<b> that</b> (the higher or ultimate purpose of God)<b> all might be judged</b> (i. e. here ‘<i>condemned</i>,’ by the context)<b> who did not</b> (looking back over their time of probation)<b> believe the truth, but found pleasure in iniquity</b>.<br><br> I have above given the<i> rendering</i> of this important passage. For the history and criticism of its inter-pretation, see the Introduction,§v.
