<p><strong> 14. </strong> <strong> εἰς ὅ</strong> ] <strong> to which</strong> (i.e. the being saved in sanctification of the Spirit and belief of the truth) <strong> He</strong> (God) <strong> called you through our Gospel</strong> (our preaching of the Gospel to you), <strong> in order to</strong> (your) <strong> acquisition</strong> (see on 1Th 5:9 ) <strong> of the glory of our Lord Jesus Christ</strong> (i.e. your sharing in the glory which He <em> has</em> ; see ref. John: <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:17">Romans 8:17</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:29">Romans 8:29</span> ; not the glory of which He is the bestower or source, as Pelt, al. Equally wrong is the interpretation of Œc., Thl., Corn.-a-lap., al. ἵνα δόξαν περιποιήσῃ τῷ υἱῷ αὐτοῦ : of Luther, al., “ <em> zum herrlichen Eigenthum</em> ,” ‘ut essetis gloriosa possessio domini nostri J. C.:’ for, not to mention other objections, the whole context has for its purpose <em> the lot of the Thessalonians</em> as contrasted with that of those spoken of, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:10-12">2 Thessalonians 2:10-12</span> ; and the sense of περιποίησις is indicated by the parallel 1Th 5:9 ).</p>
