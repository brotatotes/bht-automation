<p><strong> 3</strong> .] Calvin says, “Ceterum de aliis magis quam do se anxium fuisse Paulum, ostendunt hæc ipsa verba. In eum maligni homines improbitatis suæ aculeos dirigebant, in eum totus impetus irruebat: curam interea suam ad Thessalonicenses convertit, nequid hæc illis tentatio noceat.”
	</p>
<p><strong> πιστός</strong> seems to be chosen in allusion to πίστις which has just preceded; but the allusion cannot be more than that of sound, as the things spoken of are wholly different.
	</p>
<p><strong> ὁ κύριος</strong> is <strong> our Lord:</strong> see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:16">2 Thessalonians 2:16</span> , and <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:5">2 Thessalonians 3:5</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> δέ</strong> , in contrast with the men just mentioned.
	</p>
<p><strong> στηρίξει</strong> ] in reference to his wish, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:17">2 Thessalonians 2:17</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> τοῦ πονηροῦ</strong> may mean ‘the evil one,’ as in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:19">Matthew 13:19</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:16">Ephesians 4:16</span> , al.: and so Ellic. But here the assurance seems, as before said, to correspond to the wish ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:17">2 Thessalonians 2:17</span> ; and thus στηρίξαι ἐν παντὶ ἔργῳ κ . λόγῳ ἀγαθῷ = στηρίξει κ . φυλάξει ἀπὸ τοῦ πονηροῦ : in which case τ . πον . is <em> neuter</em> . We may observe that the words are nearly a citation from the Lord’s prayer.</p>
