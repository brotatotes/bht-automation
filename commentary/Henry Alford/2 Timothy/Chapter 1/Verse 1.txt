<b> 1</b>.] See 1 Cor. i. 1; 2 Cor. i. 1; Eph. i. 1; Col. i. 1.<b> according to</b> (in pursuance of, with a view to the fulfilment of)<b> the promise of life, which is in Christ Jesus</b> (all this is to be taken with the word<i> Apostle</i>, not as following the<i> will of God</i>. Theodoret explains it well, “So that I proclaim to men the eternal life which hath been promised.” Chrysostom. sees, in this mention of the promise of life in Christ, a consolation to Timothy under present troubles. And this idea seems to be borne out by the strain of the subsequent portion of the Epistle, which is throughout one of confirmation and encouragement).
