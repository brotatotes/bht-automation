<p><strong> 19</strong> .] <em> Firm endurance</em> , notwithstanding this overturning of the faith of some, <em> of the church of God: its signs and seals</em> . <strong> Nevertheless</strong> (cf. Ellicott) <strong> God’s firm foundation standeth</strong> (not, as E. V. ungrammatically, ‘ <em> the foundation of God standeth sure</em> .’ But what is ὁ στερεὸς θεμ . τ . θεοῦ ? Very various interpretations have been given. παρασαλεῦσαι , says Thdrt., οὐ δύνανται τὴν τῆς ἀληθείας κρηπῖδα . ὁ θεὸς γὰρ τοῦτον τέθεικε τὸν θεμέλιον : Cocceius, Michaelis, Ernesti, explain it the <em> fundamental doctrine of the Resurrection</em> : Ambr., <em> the promises of God</em> : Bengel, Vatabl., <em> fidem Dei immotam</em> : Bretschn., al., <em> Christ</em> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:11">1 Corinthians 3:11</span> . Heinrichs, Rosenm., <em> the Christian religion</em> : Calv., Calov., Wolf, Corn.-a-lap., al., <em> Dei electionem</em> . Rather, as Mosh., Kypke, Heydenr., Mack, De W., Huther, Wiesinger, al., ἐκκλησία τεθεμελιωμένη ὑπὸ θεοῦ the congregation of the faithful, considered as a foundation of a building placed by God, the οἰκία spoken of in the next verse. So Estius: “Ipsa ecclesia rectissime firmum ac solidum Dei fundamentum vocatur, quia super petram, i.e. Christum, a Deo firmiter fundata, nullis aut Satanæ machinis aut tentationum fluctibus subverti potest aut labefactari: nam etsi quidam ab ea deficiunt, ipsa tamen in suis electis perseverat usque in finem.” He then cites <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:19">1 John 2:19</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:24">Matthew 24:24</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:28">John 10:28</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:35">Romans 8:35</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:39">Romans 8:39</span> ; and proceeds, “Ex his admodum fit verisimile, firmum Dei fundamentum intelligi fideles electos: sive, quod idem est, ecclesiam in electis.” Against the tottering faith of those just mentioned, he sets the στερεὸς θεμ ., and the ἕστηκεν . It cannot be moved: Heb 12:28 ), <strong> having</strong> (“ ‘ <em> seeing it hath</em> ,’ part. with a very faint causal force, illustrating the previous declaration: cf. Donalds. Gr. § 615.” Ellic.) <strong> this seal</strong> (probably in allusion to the practice of engraving inscriptions over doors (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="de+6:9">Deuteronomy 6:9</span> ; Deu 11:20 ) and on pillars and foundation stones ( Rev 21:14 ). The seal (inscription) would indicate <em> ownership</em> and <em> destination</em> : both of which are pointed at in the two texts following) (1) <strong> The Lord knoweth</strong> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+8:3">1 Corinthians 8:3</span> , note: ‘novit amanter (?), nec nosse desinit,’ as Bengel) <strong> them that are His</strong> (the LXX runs: ἐπέσκεπται καὶ ἔγνω ὁ θεὸς τοὺς ὄντας αὐτοῦ καὶ τοὺς ἁγίους , καὶ προσηγάγετο πρὸς ἑαυτόν ): <strong> and</strong> (2) <strong> Let every one that nameth the name of the Lord</strong> (viz. <em> as</em> his Lord: not exactly equivalent to ‘calleth on the name of the Lord’) <strong> stand aloof from iniquity</strong> (the passage in Isa. stands, ἀπόστητε , ἀπόστητε , ἐξέλθατε ἐκεῖθεν , καὶ ἀκαθάρτου μὴ ἅψησθε , … ἀφορίσθητε οἱ φέροντες τὰ σκεύη κυρίου . It is clearly no reason against this passage being here <em> alluded to</em> , that (as Conyb.) it is <em> expressly cited</em> <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:17">2 Corinthians 6:17</span> . Ellic. remarks, that it is possibly in continued allusion to <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="nu+16:26">Numbers 16:26</span> , ἀποσχίσθητε ἀπὸ τῶν σκηνῶν , τῶν ἀνθρώπων τῶν σκληρῶν τούτων ).</p>
