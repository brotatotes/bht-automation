<b> 3</b>.]<b> Suffer hardship with me</b> ( “<i>me</i>” is not expressed in the Greek. The word signifies, as Conybeare happily renders it,«Take thy<i> share</i> in suffering.’ The mention of the share binds it to what pre-cedes and follows, referring primarily to the Apostle himself, though doubtless having a wider reference to all who similarly suffer: see above, on the connexion of ver. 2)<b> as a good soldier of Jesus Christ</b>.
