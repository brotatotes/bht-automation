<p><strong> 9</strong> .] <strong> Notwithstanding</strong> (Ellic. well remarks that <strong> ἀλλά</strong> here after an affirmative sentence should have its full adversative force) <strong> they shall not advance further</strong> (in ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:16">2 Timothy 2:16</span> , it is said, ἐπὶ πλεῖον προκόψουσιν ἀσεβείας : and it is in vain to deny that there is an apparent and literal inconsistency between the two assertions. But on looking further into them, it is manifest, that while there the Apostle is speaking of an immediate spread of error, here he is looking to its ultimate defeat and extinction: as Chrys., κἂν πρότερον ἀνθήσῃ τὰ τῆς πλάνης , εἰς τέλος οὐ διαμενεῖ ): <strong> for their folly</strong> (unintelligent and senseless method of proselytizing and upholding their opinions (see ref. Luke), and indeed folly of those opinions themselves) <strong> shall be thoroughly manifested</strong> (ref. πάντ ʼ ἐποίησεν ἔκδηλα , Demosth. 24. 10) <strong> to all, as also that of those men was</strong> (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ex+8:18">Exodus 8:18</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ex+9:11">Exodus 9:11</span> ; but most probably the allusion is to their traditional end).</p>
