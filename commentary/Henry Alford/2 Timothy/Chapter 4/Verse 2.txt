Virgilii: ‘ <em> digna indigna</em> pati,’ Terentii: ‘cum milite isto <em> præsens absens</em> ut sies.’ ” So <em> fanda nefanda, plus minus, nolens volens</em> , &amp;c.), <strong> convict, rebuke</strong> (reff.), <strong> exhort, in</strong> (not ‘ <em> with</em> ;’ it is not the <em> accompaniment</em> of the actions, but the element, the temper in which they are to be performed) <strong> all</strong> (possible) <strong> long-suffering and teaching</strong> (not subjective, ‘ <em> perseverance in teaching</em> ,’ as Conyb.; but ‘ <em> teaching</em> ’ itself: it (objective) is to be the element in which these acts take place, as well as μακροθυμία (subjective). The junction is harsh, but not therefore to be avoided. Of course, hendiadys (= ἐν πάσῃ μακροθυμίᾳ διδαχῆς , Grot., Rosenm.) is out of the question.
