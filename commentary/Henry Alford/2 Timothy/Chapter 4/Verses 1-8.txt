<p><strong> 1 8</strong> .] <em> Earnest exhortation to Timotheus to fulfil his office; in the near prospect of defection from the truth, and of the Apostle’s own departure from life</em> . <strong> I adjure thee</strong> (ref.) <strong> before God, and Christ Jesus, who is about to judge living and dead</strong> ( λέγει τοὺς ἤδη ἀπελθόντας καὶ τοὺς τότε καταλειφθησομένους ζῶντας , Thl.: so also Thdrt., and Chrys., alt. 2: not as Chrys., alt. 1, ἁμαρτωλοὺς λέγει καὶ δικαίους ), <strong> and by</strong> (i.e. ‘and I call to witness,’ as in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="de+4:26">Deuteronomy 4:26</span> , διαμαρτύρομαι ὑμῖν τόν τε οὐρανὸν καὶ τὴν γῆν , the construction being changed from that in the first clause. This is better than with Huther, to take the accusatives as merely acc. jurandi, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:31">1 Corinthians 15:31</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:12">James 5:12</span> . With <strong> κατά</strong> , it would be, ‘at His, &amp;c.:’ cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:15">Matthew 27:15</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:27">Acts 13:27</span> ; Heb 3:8 ) <strong> his appearing</strong> (reff.) <strong> and his kingdom</strong> (these two, τ . ἐπιφ . αὐτοῦ κ . τ . βασ . αὐτοῦ , are not to be taken as a hendiadys, as Bengel, ‘ ἐπιφάνεια est revelatio et exortus regni’ but each has its place in the adjuration: His <em> coming</em> , at which we shall stand before Him; His <em> kingdom</em> , in which we hope to reign with Him),</p>
