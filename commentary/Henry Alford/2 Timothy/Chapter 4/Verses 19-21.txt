<p><strong> 19 21</strong> .] <em> Salutations and notices</em> . <strong> Salute Prisca and Aquila</strong> (see notes, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:2">Acts 18:2</span> ; Rom 16:3 ) <strong> and the house of Onesiphorus</strong> (himself probably deceased. See on ch. 2Ti 1:16 ). <strong> Erastus</strong> (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:22">Acts 19:22</span> , an Erastus was sent forward into Macedonia by the Apostle from Ephesus, and <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:23">Romans 16:23</span> , an Erastus sends greeting, who is described as the οἰκονόμος τῆς πόλεως (Corinth). This latter would seem to be the person here mentioned) <strong> abode in Corinth</strong> (on the inferences to be drawn from this, see Prolegg to Past. Epp. § ii. 30 f.), <strong> but Trophimus</strong> (he accompanied the Apostle from Greece into Asia, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:4">Acts 20:4</span> . He was an Ephesian, id. 21:29, and was with the Apostle in Jerusalem on his last visit there) <strong> I left</strong> (not ‘ <em> they</em> (the Asian brethren who came to Rome) <em> left</em> ,’ as Hug) <strong> in Miletus</strong> (see again this discussed in Prolegg. to this Epistle, § i. 5. Various conjectures have been made to escape the difficulty here presented: ἐν Μελίτῃ (Baronius, Beza, Grot., Est., &amp;c.) a Miletus in <em> Crete</em> (Michaelis, Schrader)) <strong> sick. Endeavour to come before winter</strong> (when the voyage would be impossible, and so the visit thrown over to another year. See also on 2Ti 4:13 ). <strong> Eubulus</strong> (otherwise unknown) <strong> greets thee, and Pudens</strong> (see excursus at the end of the Prolegg. to this Epistle on Pudens and Claudia), <strong> and Linus</strong> (Iren. iii. 3. 3, p. 176, οἱ ἀπόστολοι … Λίνῳ τὴν τῆς ἐπισκοπῆς (at Rome) λειτουργίαν ἐνεχείρισαν . τούτου τοῦ Λίνου Παῦλος ἐν ταῖς πρὸς Τιμόθεον ἐπιστολαῖς μέμνηται . So also Euseb. H. E. iii. 4), <strong> and Claudia</strong> (see excursus as before), <strong> and all the brethren</strong> .</p>
