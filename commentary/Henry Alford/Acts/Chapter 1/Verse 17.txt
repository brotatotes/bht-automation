<p><strong> 17.</strong> ] <strong> ὅτι</strong> , not ‘ <em> although</em> ’ (Kuinoel), but <strong> because</strong> : it gives the reason of the previous assertion, viz. that Judas held, and had betrayed, that place of high trust of which the prophecy spoke. Thus the <strong> ὅτι</strong> has reference to the <em> substance of the prophecy</em> , already in Peter’s mind, and serves to explain ἡ ἔπαυλις αὐτοῦ and ἡ ἐπισκοπὴ αὐτοῦ .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἔλαχεν τὸν κλῆρον</strong> ] not literally, but inasmuch as the <em> lot</em> of every man is regarded as being cast and appointed by God.
	</p>
<p>κλῆρος , first, the <em> lot</em> itself; then, <em> that apportioned by lot</em> ; then, <em> any species of apportionment</em> , whether <em> possession</em> , or <em> office</em> , as here.</p>
