<b> 17.</b>]<b> Because</b> gives the reason of the previous assertion, viz. that Judas held, and had betrayed, that place of high trust of which the prophecy spoke. Thus it has reference to the<i> substance of the prophecy</i>, already in Peter’s mind, and serves to explain the words “<i>his habitation</i>,” and “<i>his bishoprick</i>,” which occur in the prophecy.<br><br><b> had obtained the lot</b>] not literally, but inasmuch as the<i> lot</i> of every man is regarded as being cast and appointed by God.
