<p><strong> 5</strong> .] The Lord cites these words from the mouth of John himself, reff. Matt.; and thus announces to them that, as John’s mission was accomplished in baptizing <em> with water</em> , so now the great end of His own mission, the <em> Baptism with the Holy Ghost</em> , was on the point of being accomplished. Calvin remarks, that He speaks of the Pentecostal effusion as <em> being</em> the Baptism with the Holy Ghost, because it was a great representation on the whole Church of the subsequent continued work of regeneration on individuals: ‘Quasi totius Ecclesiæ communis baptismus.’ I may add, also because it was the <em> beginning</em> of a new period of spiritual influence, totally unlike any which had preceded. See ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:17">Acts 2:17</span> .
