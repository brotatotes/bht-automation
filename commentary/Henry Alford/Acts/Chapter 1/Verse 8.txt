<p><strong> 8</strong> .] ‘Quod optimum frænandæ curiositati remedium erat, Christus eos revocat tam ad Dei promissionem, quam ad mandatum.’ Calvin.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀλλά</strong> , ‘antitheton inter id quod discipulorum erat, vel non erat; tum inter id quod illo tempore futurum erat, et inter id quod in ulteriora reservatum erat.’ Bengel.
	</p>
<p><strong> δύνα</strong> <strong> ̇</strong> <strong> μιν</strong> , <em> that</em> power, <em> especially</em> , spoken of ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:33">Acts 4:33</span> , connected with their office of witnessing to the resurrection; but also all other spiritual power. See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:49">Luke 24:49</span> . μου , not emphatic, as Wordsw. here and often elsewhere: see note on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:18">Matthew 16:18</span> . The emphasis would be extremely out of place here: it was not their subordination to Him, but their office as witnesses, which was the contrast to their ambitious aspirings.
	</p>
<p><strong> μάρτυρες</strong> ] This was the peculiar work of the Apostles[: so they say of themselves, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:32">Acts 5:32</span> , ἡμεῖς ἐσμὲν αὐτοῦ μάρτυρες τῶν ῥημάτων τούτων ]. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:21-22">Acts 1:21-22</span> , and Prolegg. Vol. I. ch. i. § iii. 5.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἔν τε Ἱερ</strong> …] By the extension of their testimony, from Jerusalem to Samaria, and then indefinitely over the world, He reproves, by implication, their carnal anticipation of the restoration of the Kingdom to <em> Israel</em> thus understood. The Kingdom was to be one founded on μαρτυρία , and therefore reigning in the convictions of men’s hearts; and not confined to Judæa, but coextensive with the world.
	</p>
<p>They understood this command only of <em> Jews</em> scattered through the world, see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:19">Acts 11:19</span> .
	</p>
<p>De Wette observes, that these words contain <em> the whole plan of the Acts:</em> λήμψεσθε δύναμιν κ . τ . λ ., ch. Act 2:1 end; ἐν Ἱερουσαλήμ , ch. Act 3:1 to <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:7">Acts 6:7</span> ; then the martyrdom of Stephen dispersed them <em> through Judæa</em> , Act 6:8 to <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:3">Acts 8:3</span> ; they preach <em> in Samaria</em> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:4-40">Acts 8:4-40</span> ; and, from that point, the conversion of the Apostle of the <em> Gentiles</em> , the vision of Peter, the preaching and journeys of Paul. In their former mission, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:5-6">Matthew 10:5-6</span> , they had been expressly forbidden from preaching either to Samaritans or Gentiles.</p>
