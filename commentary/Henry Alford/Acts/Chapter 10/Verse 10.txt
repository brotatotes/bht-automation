<p><strong> ἔκστασις</strong> ] The distinction of this appearance from the ὅραμα above (though the usage is not always strictly observed) is, that in this case that which was seen was a revelation <em> shewn</em> to the eye of the beholder when rapt into a supernatural state, having, as is the case in a dream, <em> no objective reality</em> : whereas, in the other case, the thing seen <em> actually happened</em> , and was beheld by the person as an ordinary spectator, in the possession of his natural senses.</p>
