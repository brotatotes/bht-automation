<b> 11. tied by four rope-ends</b>] Not, as A.V., ‘<i>knit at the four corners,</i>’ but as in margin. The<i> ends of the ropes</i> were attached to the sheet, and, in the vision,<i> they only were seen.</i>—These four<i> ends</i> are not without meaning, directed as they are to the four parts of heaven, and intimating that men from the North, South, East, and West, now were accounted clean before God, and were called to a share in his kingdom: see Luke xiii. 29. We must not wander away into childish exaggeration of symbolism as some have done, interpreting the four ends of the<i> four gospels, &c.</i>
