<b> 14.</b>] Peter rightly understands the command as giving him free choice of<i> all</i> the creatures shewn to him. We cannot infer hence that the sheet contained<i> unclean animals only</i>. It was a mixture of clean and unclean,—the aggregate, therefore, being<i> unclean</i>.<br><br><b> Lord</b>] So Cornelius to the angel, ver. 4. It is here addressed to the unknown heavenly speaker.—On the clean and unclean beasts, &c., see Levit. xi.
