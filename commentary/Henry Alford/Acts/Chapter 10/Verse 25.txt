<b> 25. worshipped</b>] St. Luke, observes Bengel, has not added “<i>him</i>;” doubtless from an euphemistic motive. It was natural for Cornelius to think that one so pointed out by an angel must be deserving of the highest respect; and this respect he shewed in a way which proves him not to have altogether lost the heathen training of his childhood. He must have witnessed the rise of the custom of paying divine honours first to those who were clothed with the delegated power of the senate, and then, even more conspicuously, to him in whom the imperial majesty centered.
