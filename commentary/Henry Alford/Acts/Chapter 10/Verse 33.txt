<p><strong> 33</strong> .] The reading ἐνώπ . σου , for ἐνώπ . τοῦ θεοῦ , is remarkable, and had it more manuscript authority, would seem as if it might have been genuine. It was much more likely to have been altered into τ . θεοῦ (as making the expression more solemn), than the converse: and the sense, ‘We are all here present before <em> thee</em> ,’ follows better on the two preceding verses.
	</p>
