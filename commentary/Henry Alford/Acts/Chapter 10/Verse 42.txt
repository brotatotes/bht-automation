<b> 42. unto the people</b>] Here, as elsewhere (ver. 2; John xi. 50 al. fr.), the<i> Jewish people:</i> that was all which in the apostolic mind, up to this time, the command had absolutely enjoined. The further unfoldings of the Gospel had all been brought about over and above this first injunction. Ch. i. 8 is no obstacle to this interpretation; for although literally fulfilled by the leadings of Providence, as related in this book, they did not so understand it when spoken.
