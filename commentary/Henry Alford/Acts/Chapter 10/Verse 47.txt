<b> 47.</b>] One great end of the unexpected effusion of the Holy Spirit was, entirely to preclude the question which otherwise could not but have arisen, ‘Must not not these men be<i> circumcised before baptism?</i>’<br><br><b> the water... the Holy Ghost</b>] The TWO<i> great</i> PARTS<i> of full and complete baptism</i>: the latter infinitely greater than, but not superseding the necessity of, the, former. The article should here certainly be expressed:<b> Can any forbid</b> THE WATER<b> to these who have received</b> THE SPIRIT?—The expression<b> forbid</b>, used with<b> the water</b>, is interesting, as shewing that the practice was to<i> bring the water to the candidates, not the candidates to the water</i>. This, which would be implied by the word under any circumstances, is rendered certain, when we remember that they were assembled<i> in the house</i>.
