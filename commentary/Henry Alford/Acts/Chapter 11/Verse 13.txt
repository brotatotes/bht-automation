<b> 13. the angel</b>] The use of the definite article almost looks as if the history of Cornelius’s vision were known to the hearers. The difference between the vision of Cornelius and that of Peter is here again strikingly marked. While the latter is merely “praying in the city of Joppa,” no place nor circumstance being named, the former sees the angel ‘<i>standing in his house.</i>’—Notice also that Peter never names Cornelius in his speech—because he, his character and person, was absorbed in the category to which he belonged,—that of “<i>men uncircumcised.</i>”
