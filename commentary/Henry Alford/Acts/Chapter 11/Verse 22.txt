<p><strong> Βαρνάβαν</strong> ] himself a Cyprian, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:36">Acts 4:36</span> .
	</p>
<p>His mission does not seem exactly to have been correspondent to that of Peter and John to Samaria (nor can he in any distinctive sense, be said to have been <em> an Apostle, as they were</em> : see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:4">Acts 14:4</span> , and note): but more probably, from what follows, the intention was to <em> ascertain the fact</em> , and to <em> deter</em> these persons from the admission of the uncircumcised into the church: or, at all events, to use his discretion in a matter on which they were as yet doubtful. The choice of such a man, <em> one</em> by birth <em> with the agents</em> , and of a <em> liberal spirit</em> , shews sufficiently that they wished to deal, not harshly, but gently and cautiously, whatever their reason was.</p>
