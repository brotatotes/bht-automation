<p><strong> 30. </strong> <strong> πρεσβυτέρους</strong> ] These were the <em> overseers</em> or <em> presidents</em> of the congregation, an office borrowed from the synagogues, and established by the Apostles in the churches generally, see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:23">Acts 14:23</span> . They are in the N. T. identical with ἐπίσκοποι , see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:17">Acts 20:17</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:28">Acts 20:28</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:5">Titus 1:5</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:7">Titus 1:7</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:1-2">1 Peter 5:1-2</span> . So Theodoret on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="php+1:1">Philippians 1:1</span> , ἐπισκόπους τοὺς πρεσβυτέρους καλεῖ · ἀμφότερα γὰρ εἶχον κατ ʼ ἐκεῖνον τὸν καιρὸν τὰ ὀνόματα . The title ἐπίσκοπος , as applied to one person superior to the πρεσβύτεροι , and answering to our ‘bishop,’ appears to have been unknown in the apostolic times. Respecting the chronology of this journey to Jerusalem, see note on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:25">Acts 12:25</span> , and the table in the Prolegomena.</p>
