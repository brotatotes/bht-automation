<p><strong> 4. </strong> <strong> τέσσαρσιν τετραδίοις</strong> ] In military arrangements, Herod seems to have retained the Roman habits, according to which the night was divided into four watches, and each committed to four soldiers ( διδόασι φυλάκεια δύο · τὸ δὲ φυλάκειόν ἐστιν ἐκ τεσσάρων ἀνδρῶν , Polyb. vi. 33.7), to two of whom the prisoner was chained, the other two keeping watch before the doors of the prison, forming the <em> first</em> and <em> second guards</em> of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:10">Acts 12:10</span> . It is plain that this number being mentioned is no sign that the custody was only for <em> one night</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> μετὰ τὸ πάσχα</strong> ] (see above) <strong> after the days of the feast</strong> , i.e. after the 21st of Nisan. Herod, who (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:1">Acts 12:1</span> , note) observed rigorously the Jewish customs, would not execute a prisoner during the feast: ‘Non judicant die festo’ (Moed Katon <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:2">Acts 12:2</span> , Meyer).
	</p>
