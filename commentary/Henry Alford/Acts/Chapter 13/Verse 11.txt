<p><strong> 11. </strong> <strong> ἄχρι καιροῦ</strong> ] The punishment was only temporary, being accompanied with a gracious purpose to the man himself, to awaken repentance in him. The sense given to ἄχρι κ . by Tittmann and Meyer here and at ref. Luke, of ἕως τέλους , is one of which it seems to me incapable.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀχλὺς κ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> σκότος</strong> ] In the same precise and gradual manner is the healing of the lame man, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:8">Acts 3:8</span> , described: ἔστη (first), κ . περιεπάτει . So here, first a dimness came on him, then total darkness. And we may conceive this to have been evinced by his gestures and manner under the infliction.</p>
