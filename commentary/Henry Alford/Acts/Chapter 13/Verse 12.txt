<b> 12. at the doctrine of the Lord</b>] Hesitating as he had been before between the<i> teaching</i> of the sorcerer and that of the Apostle, he is amazed at the divine power accompanying the latter, and gives himself up to it. It is not said that he was<i> baptized:</i> but the supposition is not thereby excluded: see ver. 48; ch. xvii. 12, 34; xviii. 8, first part.
