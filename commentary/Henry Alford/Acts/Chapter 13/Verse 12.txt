<p><strong> 12. </strong> <strong> ἐπὶ τῇ διδ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> κυρ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] Hesitating as he had been before between the <em> teaching</em> of the sorcerer and that of the Apostle, he is amazed at the divine power accompanying the latter, and gives himself up to it. It is not said that he was <em> baptized</em> : but the supposition is not thereby excluded: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:48">Acts 13:48</span> ; ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:12">Acts 17:12</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:34">Acts 17:34</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:8">Acts 18:8</span> , first part.</p>
