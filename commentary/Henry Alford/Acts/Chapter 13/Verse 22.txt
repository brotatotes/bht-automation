<b> 22. he removed him</b>] i.e.<b> deposed him:</b> in this case, by his<i> death</i>, for David was not made king till then. Or perhaps the word may refer to the sentence pronounced against Saul, 1 Sam. xiii. 14, or xv. 23, 28, and the following verb,<b> raised up</b>, to the whole process of the exaltation of David to be king. But I prefer the former.<br><br><b> to whom he gave testimony, and said</b>] The two passages, Ps. lxxxix. 20, and 1 Sam. xiii. 14, are interwoven together: both were spoken of David, and both by prophetic inspiration. They are cited from memory, neither the words “<i>the son of Jesse,</i>” nor “<i>which shall fulfil all my will,</i>” being found in them. These latter words are spoken of<i> Cyrus</i>, see Isa. xliv. 28. That such citations are left in their present shape in our text, forms a strong presumption that we have the speeches of St. Paul word for word as delivered by him, and no subsequent general statement of what he said, in which case the citation would have been<i> corrected by the sacred text</i>.
