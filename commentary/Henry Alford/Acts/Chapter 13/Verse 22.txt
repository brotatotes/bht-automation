	 ᾧ κ  .   εἶπεν μ  . ] The two passages, Psalms 89:0 ; (88 LXX) 20, and 1 Samuel 13:14 , are interwoven together: both were spoken of David, and both by prophetic inspiration. They are cited from memory, neither τὸν τοῦ Ἰεσσαί nor ὅς … μου being found in them. These latter words are spoken of  Cyrus , see reff. That such citations are left in their present shape in our text, forms a strong presumption that we have the speeches of Paul verbatim as delivered by him, and no subsequent general statement of what he said, in which case the citations would have been  corrected by the sacred text .
