<p><strong> 29</strong> .] The two verbs ἐτέλεσαν and ἔθηκαν have still the same subject, viz. of οἱ κατοικοῦντες κ . τ . λ . De Wette rightly remarks, that Paul, in this compendious narrative, makes no distinction between friend and foe in what was done to our Lord, but regards both as fulfilling God’s purpose regarding him. I may add, that there is also a contrast between what <em> men</em> did to Him, and <strong> ὁ δὲ θεὸς</strong> ἤγειρεν αὐτόν .
	</p>
<p>Joseph and Nicodemus, be it observed, were both ἄρχοντες .
	</p>
<p>Paul touches but lightly on the cross of Christ, and hastens on to the great point, the Resurrection, as the fulfilment of prophecy and seal of the Messiahship of Jesus.</p>
