<p><strong> 38</strong> .] Paul speaks here of justification only in its <em> lowest</em> sense, as negative, and synonymous with remission of sins; he does not unfold here that higher sense of δικαιόω , the <em> accounting righteous</em> , which those who have from God are δίκαιοι ἐκ πίστεως . It is the first office of the Spirit by which he spoke, ἐλέγχειν περὶ ἁμαρτίας , before He ἐλέγχει περὶ δικαιοσύνης : therefore he dwells on the ἄφεσις ἁμαρτιῶν , merely just giving a glimpse of the great doctrine of justification, of which he had such wonderful things to write and to say.</p>
