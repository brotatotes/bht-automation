<p><strong> 29.</strong> ] On the construction of <strong> ἀπέχεσθαι</strong> with ἀπό in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:20">Acts 15:20</span> , and with a simple gen. here, Tittm., de Syn. N. T. p. 225, says well that the difference arises ‘non quoad rem ipsam, sed modo cogitandi, ita ut in priori formula sejunctionis cogitatio ad rem, in posteriori vero ad nos ipsos referatur.’ His following remarks are worth reading.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐξ ὧν</strong> <strong> , from which things</strong> ; not, as Meyer, ‘ <em> according to which precepts</em> ;’ see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:15">John 17:15</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> εὖ πράξ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] Not, ‘ <em> ye shall prosper</em> :’ but as καλῶς ἐποίησας , ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:33">Acts 10:33</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:6">3 John 1:6</span> , <strong> ye shall do well</strong> .
	</p>
<p>See the curious additions in var. readd.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἔῤῥωσθε</strong> ] The customary ‘valete’ of the conclusion of epistles.</p>
