<p><strong> 38. </strong> <strong> ἠξίον</strong> ] Not as Vulg. ‘rogabat:’ but ‘æquum censebat,’ as Beza. It gives Paul’s refusal in the strongest manner. The position of the accusatives also forcibly expresses his decided rejection of one who had not dared to face the dangers of the untried country before. <strong> But Paul thought proper (as to) one who had fallen off from them from Pamphylia, and had not gone with them to the work, not to take with them that man</strong> . We may well believe that Paul’s own mouth gave originally the character to the sentence.
	</p>
