<b> CHAP. XVI. 1.</b>] We have Derbe first, as lying nearest to the pass from Cilicia into Lycaonia and Cappadocia. Paul probably travelled by the ordinary road through the ‘Cilician gates,’ a rent or fissure in the mouutain-chain of Taurus, extending from north to south through a distance of eighty miles. See various interesting particulars in Conybeare and Howson, i.p. 301 ff. and notes.
