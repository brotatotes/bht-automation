<b> 13.</b>]<b> a</b> (or, the)<b> river side:</b> viz. the small stream Gangītés, or Gangas: not, as Meyer and De Wette, the Strymon, the nearest point of which was many miles distant. The name Krenīdés, formerly borne by the city, was derived from the fountains of this stream.—From many sources we learn, that it was the practice of the Jews to hold their assemblies for prayer<i> near water</i>, whether of the sea, or of rivers: probably on account of the frequent washings customary among them.
