<b> 16.</b>] This happened on other occasions; not on the same day. In ver. 15 is implied their taking up their abode with Lydia:—in this ver., that they<i> habitually resorted</i> to this place of prayer to teach, and that what follows happened on such occasions.—It may be remarked that the A.V. of this passage, ‘<i>as we went to prayer</i>,’ has given rise to a curious abuse of the expression ‘<i>going to prayer</i>,’ in the sense of ‘<i>beginning to pray</i>,’ among the lower classes in England.
