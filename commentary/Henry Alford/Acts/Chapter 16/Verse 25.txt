<b> 25.</b>] Not as A.V., ‘<i>prayed and sang praises</i>,’—but, as in margin,<b> in their prayers, were singing praises,</b> or,<b> praying, sing praises.</b> The distinction of modern times between prayer and praise, arising from our attention being directed to the<i> shape</i> rather than to the essence of devotion, was unknown in these days: see Col. iv. 2.— “Their legs in the stocks pained them not, whose souls were in heaven.” Tertullian. The prisoners (in the outer prison) were<i> listening</i> to their singing, when the earthquake happened.
