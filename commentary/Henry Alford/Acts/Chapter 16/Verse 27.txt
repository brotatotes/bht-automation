<p><strong> 27. </strong> <strong> ἤμελ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἑαυτ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἀναιρ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] The law de Custodia Reorum (Wetst.) says, ‘Ad commentariensem receptarum personarum custodia observatioque pertineat, nec putet, hominem abjectum atque vilem objiciendum esse judici, si reus modo aliquo fuerit elapsus. Nam ipsum volumus hujusmodi pœnæ consumi, cui obnoxius docebitur fuisse, qui fugerit.’ Dean Howson notices, by the examples of Cassius, Brutus, Titinius, and many of the proscribed, after the battle, that Philippi is famous in the annals of suicide (p. 361).</p>
