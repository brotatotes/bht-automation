<b> 3. took and circumcised him</b>] Every Israelite might perform the rite.<br><br><b> because of the Jews</b>] That he might not at once, wherever he preached, throw a stumbling-block before the Jews, by having with him one by birth a Jew, but uncircumcised. There was here no concession in doctrine at all, and no reference whatever to the duty of Timotheus himself in the matter. In the case of Titus, a<i> Greek</i>, he dealt otherwise, no such reason existing: Gal. ii. 3.
