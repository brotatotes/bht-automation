<b> 35.</b>] What had influenced the magistrates is not recorded. We can hardly suppose that the earthquake alone would have done so, as they would not have connected it with their prisoners; they may have heard what had taken place: but that, again, is hardly probable. I should rather set it down to calmer thought, repudiating the tumultuary proceeding of the evening before.<br><br><b> the serjeants</b>] i.e.<b> the lictors</b>— ‘bearers of the rods,’ which, and<i> not fasces</i>, were carried before the colonial duumviri.
