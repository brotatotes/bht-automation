<b> 8.</b>]<b> passing by Mysia</b>, i.e. as regarded their work of preaching (cf. ch. xx. 16),—and not ‘having passed by’ as<i> avoiding</i> it; for they could not get to the coast<i> without entering Mysia</i>.<br><br><b> Troas</b> (Alexandria Troas, in honour of Alexander the Great: now Eski Stamboul) was a colony enjoying the<i> Italian right</i> (see on ver. 12), and a free city, and was not reckoned as belonging to either of the provinces, Asia or Bithynia. Whether it was for this reason that Paul and his companions visited it, is uncertain. He may have had the design of crossing to Europe, if permitted, which the subsequent vision confirmed. See ch. xx. 5; 2 Cor. ii. 12; 2 Tim. iv. 13.
