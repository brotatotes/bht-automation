<p><strong> 6 9.</strong> ] This very cursory notice of a journey in which we have reason to think so much happened, the founding of the Galatian and Phrygian churches (see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:23">Acts 18:23</span> , where we find him, on his second visit, στηρίζων πάντας τοὺς μαθητάς ); the sickness of the Apostle alluded to <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:13">Galatians 4:13</span> ; the working of miracles and imparting of the Spirit mentioned <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:5">Galatians 3:5</span> ; the warmth and kindness of feeling shewn to Paul in his weakness, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:13-15">Galatians 4:13-15</span> , seems to shew that the narrator was not with him during this part of the route; an inference which is remarkably confirmed by the sudden resumption of circumstantial detail with the use of the first person, at <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:10">Acts 16:10</span> .</p>
