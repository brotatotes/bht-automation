<b> 28.</b>] There is no justification for<i> the pantheist</i> in this.—It is properly said only of the race of men, as being His offspring, bound to Him: proceeding from, and upheld by, and therefore living, moving, and being in Him:—but even in a wider sense<i> His Being</i>, though a separate objective Personality, involves and contains that of His creatures. See Eph. i. 10, where the same is said of Christ.
