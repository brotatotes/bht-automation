<p><strong> 5. </strong> <strong> προσλαβ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] <strong> Having taken to them</strong> , as their accomplices, to assist them in the ὀχλοποιῆσαι which follows.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀγοραίων</strong> ] Such men as Aristophanes calls πονηρὸς κἀξ ἀγορᾶς , Demosthenes, περίτριμμα ἀγορᾶς , Xenophon, τὸν ἀγοραῖον ὄχλον , Plutarch, ἀγοραίους καὶ δυναμένους ὄχλον συναγαγεῖν : see many other instances in Wetstein, who mentions the modern ‘canaille’ ( <em> canalicolœ</em> ). Cicero calls them ‘subrostrani:’ Plautus,‘subbasilicani.’ These may be alluded to in οἱ ἴδιοι συμφυλέται , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:14">1 Thessalonians 2:14</span> . (See note on ἀγοραῖοι , ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:38">Acts 19:38</span> .)
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐπιστ</strong> <strong> ., having fallen upon, beset.
	</strong></p>
<p><strong> Ἰάσονος</strong> ] With whom ( Act 17:7 ) Paul and Silas lodged. He appears, perhaps (?), again with Paul at Corinth, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:21">Romans 16:21</span> , but did not accompany him into Asia, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:4">Acts 20:4</span> .</p>
