<p><strong> 6. </strong> <strong> πολιτάρχας</strong> ] The following inscription, found on an arch <em> at Thessalonica</em> , is given from Boeckh, No. 1967, in C. and H. i. 395: πολειταρχουντων Σωσιπατρου του Κλεοπατρας και Λουκιου Ποντιου Σεκουνδου Πουβλιου Φλαουιου Σαβεινου Δημητριου του Φαυστου Δημητριου του Νικοπολεως Ζωιλου του Παρμενιωνος του και Μενισκου Γαιου Αγιλληιου Ποτειτου .… Here we have this very title applied to the Thessalonian magistrates, shewing the exact accuracy of our narrative; and, curiously enough, we have three of the <em> names</em> which occur here, or in the Epistles, as companions of Paul: viz. Sosipater ( <em> of Berœa</em> , ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:4">Acts 20:4</span> ; see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:21">Romans 16:21</span> , and note); Secundus ( <em> of Thessalonica</em> , ch. Act 20:4 ); and Gaius ( <em> the Macedonian</em> , note, ch. Act 19:29 ).
	</p>
<p><strong> τὴν οἰκ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἀναστ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] The words presuppose some rumour of Christianity and its spread having before reached the inhabitants of Thessalonica.</p>
