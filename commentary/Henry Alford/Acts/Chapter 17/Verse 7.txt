<p><strong> 7. </strong> <strong> οὗτοι πάντες</strong> ] <strong> All these people</strong> , i.e. <em> Christians, wherever found</em> . A wider acquaintance is shewn, or at least assumed, with the belief of Christians, than extended merely to Jason and his friends.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀπέναντι</strong> <strong> … </strong> <strong> πράσς</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] Not ‘ <em> do this in the face of the decrees,</em> ’ which would require τοῦτο with πράσς ., but as E. V. The δόγματα in this case would be the Julian ‘leges majestatis.’
	</p>
<p><strong> βασιλέα</strong> κ . τ . λ .] This false charge seems to have been founded on Paul’s preaching much at Thessalonica concerning the triumphant παρουσία of Christ. This appears again and again in his two Epistles: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:10">1 Thessalonians 1:10</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:19">1 Thessalonians 2:19</span> ; 1Th 3:13 ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:13-18">1 Thessalonians 4:13-18</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:1-2">1 Thessalonians 5:1-2</span> ; 2Th 1:5 ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:7-10">2 Thessalonians 1:7-10</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:1-12">2 Thessalonians 2:1-12</span> ; and particularly <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:5">2 Thessalonians 2:5</span> , where he refers to his having often told them of <em> these things</em> , viz. the course, and destruction of Antichrist, by whom these Jews might perhaps misrepresent Paul as designating Cæsar.</p>
