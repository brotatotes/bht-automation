 11 .] The year and a half may extend either to his  departure , or to the incident in Acts 18:12 ff. Meyer would confine it to the latter, taking ἐκάθισεν in the sense of ‘  remained in quiet :’ but (see reff.) it will hardly bear such emphasis: and seeing that the incident in Acts 18:12 ff. was a notable fulfilment of the promise, for though they set on him,  they could not hurt him , I should be disposed to take the other view, and regard Act 18:12 to ἱκανάς , Acts 18:18 , to have happened during this time.
