<p><strong> 14.</strong> ] Though manuscript authority is so strong against the οὖν , I have retained it, as also has Tischdf. (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ex+7:0">Exodus 7:0</span> [not <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ex+8:0">Exodus 8:0</span> ]). Its <em> omission</em> may be easily accounted for, from the copyists finding it unnecessary and seemingly out of place: but on no supposition can its <em> insertion</em> be rendered probable. It stands very appropriately here, referring to the complaint of the Jews, either as uttered by them, or perhaps recapitulated by Gallio: ‘ <em> Ye have charged this man with lawless conduct</em> . <strong> If now this had really been so.…’
