<p><strong> 15.</strong> ] ζητήματα has apparently been alterèd to ζήτημα to suit the sense, there being but <em> one</em> question before Gallio. But the plural expresses contempt: <strong> If it is questions, &amp;c</strong> .: as we should say, ‘a parcel of questions.’ See ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:29">Acts 23:29</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ὀνομάτων</strong> ] e.g. Paul asserted Jesus to be the Christ, which the Jews denied. This to a Roman would be a question of <em> names</em> .
