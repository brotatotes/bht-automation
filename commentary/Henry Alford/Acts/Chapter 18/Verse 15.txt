<b> 15. questions</b>] The plural expresses contempt:<b> If it is questions, &c.</b>: as we should say, ‘a parcel of questions.’ See ch. xxiii. 29.<br><br><b> names</b>] Paul asserted Jesus to be the Christ, which the Jews denied. This, to a Roman, would be a question of<i> names</i>.<br><br> So Lysias (ch. xxiii. 29) declined to decide Paul’s case, and Festus (ch. xxv. 20) though he did not altogether put the enquiry by, wished to judge it<i> at Jerusalem</i>, where he might have the counsel of those learned in the Jewish law.
