<b> 22. and gone up</b>] to<i> Jerusalem:</i> for (1) it would be out of the question to suppose that Paul made the long detour by Cæsarea<i> only to go up into the town from the beach</i>, as supposed by most of those who omit the disputed words in ver. 21, and<i> salute the disciples</i>,—and (2) the expression<b> he went down to Antioch</b>, which suits a journey from Jerusalem (ch. xi. 27), would not apply to one from Cæsarea.
