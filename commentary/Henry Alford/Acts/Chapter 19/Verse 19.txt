<b> 19. their books</b>] These books consisted of<i> magical formulæ</i>, or<i> receipt-books</i>, or<i> written amulets</i>. These last were celebrated by the name of<i> Ephesian scrolls</i>. They were copies of the mystic words engraved on the image of the Ephesian Artemis (Diana).<br><br><b> fifty thousand pieces of silver</b>] 50, 000 drachmæ, i.e. denarii: for the drachma of the Augustan and following ages was the Roman denarius—about 8½<i> d</i>. of our money: which makes the entire value about£1770.
