<p><strong> 3.</strong> ] Paul’s question establishes the above rendering, <strong> to what then</strong> ( οὖν , if ye did not so much as hear of the Holy Ghost at your first believing) <strong> were ye baptized</strong> ? If the question and answer in Act 19:2 regarded, as in E. V., the <em> whole interval</em> since their conversion, this enquiry would have been more naturally expressed in the perfect. See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:27">Galatians 3:27</span> , where there is the same necessity of preserving the historical sense of the aorists.
	</p>
<p><strong> εἰς τί</strong> ] <strong> unto</strong> (with a view to, as introductory to) <strong> what profession</strong> ? They answer, <strong> unto</strong> (that indicated by) <strong> the baptism of John</strong> , viz.: <em> repentance</em> , and <em> the believing on Jesus, then to come</em> , but <em> now</em> (see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:25">Acts 18:25</span> , note) <em> the object of our faith</em> .</p>
