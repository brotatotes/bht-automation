<b> 35.</b>]<b> The townclerk</b> is the nearest English office corresponding to that here mentioned in the original. He was the keeper of the archives, and public reader of decrees, &c., in the assemblies. The word here rendered<b> worshipper</b> probably means a<b> virger</b>, or adorner of the temple: here used as implying that Ephesus had the charge and keeping of the temple. The title is found on inscriptions as belonging to Ephesus; and seems to have been specially granted by the emperors to particular cities.
