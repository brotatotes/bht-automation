<p><strong> μηδενὸς</strong> κ . τ . λ .] <strong> There being no ground why</strong> (i.e. in consequence of which) we shall be able to give an account, i.e. ‘no ground whereon to build the possibility of our giving an account.’ The reading περὶ οὗ οὐ (see digest) seems to involve the sentence in almost inextricable confusion. To read περὶ τῆς συστ . τ . and take it in apposit. with περὶ οὗ , ‘hujus rei, videlicet conventus hujus’ (Bornemann), is very harsh.</p>
