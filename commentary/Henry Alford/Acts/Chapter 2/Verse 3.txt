<b> 3. cloven tongues like as of fire</b>] They were<i> not of fire</i>, as not possessing the burning power of fire, but only<i> as it were of fire, in appearance like that element.</i><br><br><b> it sat</b>, viz.<i> the appearance</i>; not<i> the Spirit</i>, nor<i> the tongue</i>, but the<i> appearance</i> described in the preceding clause. I understand the word<b> sat</b> as usually interpreted,<b> lighted on their heads</b>. This also was no effect of natural cause, either ordinarily or extraordinarily employed: see on ver. 2.
