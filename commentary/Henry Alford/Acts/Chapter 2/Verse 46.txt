	 κατ  ʼ   οἶκον ]  domi , ‘privatim’ (Beng.), as contrasted with ἐν τῷ ἱερῷ . So also Wolf, Scal., Heinr., Olsh., Meyer, De Wette: not,  domatim , ‘from house to house,’ as Erasm., Salmasius, Kuinoel, al.: the words  may bear that meaning (see Luk 8:1 ), but we have no trace of such a practice, of holding the ἀγάπαι successively at different houses.
	The κλάσις τ . ἄρτου took place at their  house of meeting , wherever that was: cf. ch. Acts 12:12 ; and see Act 2:42 note.
