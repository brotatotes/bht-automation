<b> 11.</b>] The intended breaking of bread had been put off by the accident. The article here may import, ‘the bread which it was intended to break,’ alluding to ver. 7 above.<br><br><b> and eaten</b>] The<i> agapé</i> was a veritable meal. Not ‘<i>having tasted it</i>,’ viz. the bread which he had broken;—though that is implied, usage decides for the other meaning.<br><br><b> so</b>] i.e. ‘<i>after so doing</i>.’
