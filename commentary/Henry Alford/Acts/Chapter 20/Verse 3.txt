<p><strong> 3. </strong> <strong> ποιήσας</strong> ] This stay was made at Corinth, most probably: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:6-7">1 Corinthians 16:6-7</span> ; and was during the winter, see below on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:5">Acts 20:5</span> . During it the Epistle to the Romans was written: see Prolegg. to Rom. § iv.
	</p>
<p><strong> μέλλοντι ἀνάγεσθαι</strong> ] This purpose, of going from Corinth to Palestine by sea, is implied ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:21">Acts 19:21</span> , and <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:3-7">1 Corinthians 16:3-7</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> τοῦ ὑποστρ</strong> .] The genit. is not (as Meyer) governed directly by γνώμης , which would be more naturally followed by εἰς τὸ ὑπ .: but denotes the purpose, as in reff.</p>
