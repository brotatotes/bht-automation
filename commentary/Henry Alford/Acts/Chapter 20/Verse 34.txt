<b> 34. these hands</b>] Also strikingly in Paul’s manner: compare “<i>these bonds</i>,” ch. xxvi. 29,—and ch. xxviii. 20. See 1 Cor. iv. 12, which he wrote when at Ephesus.—Observe,<b> ministered unto my necessities, and to them that were with me</b>. This is not without meaning—his friends were among his<i> necessities</i>—he supplied by his labour, not his and their wants, but<i> his wants</i> and<i> them</i>.
