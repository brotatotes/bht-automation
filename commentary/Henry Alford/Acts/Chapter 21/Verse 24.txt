<p><strong> 24. </strong> <strong> παραλαβών</strong> ] <strong> having taken to thyself</strong> , as comrades.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἁγν</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> σὺν αὐτ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] <strong> become a Nazarite with them</strong> . The same expression occurs in the LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="nu+6:3">Numbers 6:3</span> , in describing the Nazarite’s duties.
	</p>
<p><strong> δαπάν</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἐπ</strong> <strong> ʼ </strong> <strong> αὐτ</strong> .] “More apud Judæos receptum erat, et pro insigni pietatis officio habebatur, ut in pauperum Nasiræorum gratiam ditiores sumptus erogarent ad sacrificia (see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="nu+6:14">Numbers 6:14</span> ff.) quæ dum illi tonderentur, offerre necesse erat.” Kypke. Jos. Antt. xix. 6.1, relating Agrippa’s thank-offerings at Jerusalem, says, διὸ καὶ Ναζιραίων ξυρᾶσθαι διέταξε μάλα συχνούς .
	</p>
<p>On the shaving the head, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="nu+6:18">Numbers 6:18</span> .
	</p>
<p>De Wette remarks: ‘James and the elders made this proposal, assuming that Paul could comply with it <em> salvâ conscientiâ</em> , perhaps also as a proof, to assure themselves and others of his sentiments: and Paul accepted it <em> salvâ conscientiâ</em> . But this he could only have done on one condition, that he was sure by it not to contribute in these four Nazarites to the error of <em> justification by the works of the law</em> . He might keep, and encourage the keeping of the law, but not with the purpose of thereby deserving the approbation of God.’</p>
