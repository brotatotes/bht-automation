<b> 27. seven days</b>] Of the votive period: not (as some think) since Paul’s arrival in Jerusalem. Five days of the seven had passed: see on ch. xxiv. 11.<br><br><b> which were from Asia</b>] From Ephesus and the neighbourhood, where Paul had so long taught. “Paul, while intent on appeasing the believing Jews, incurs the furious hostility of his unbelieving enemies.” Calvin, who adds, ‘In how many ways had those who were at Jerusalem this Pentecost, already persecuted Paul in Asia?’—Notice the similarity of the charge against him to that against Stephen, ch. vi. 13.
