<p><strong> 27. </strong> <strong> αἱ ἑπτ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἡμ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] Of the votive period: not (as Chrys. and Bede [145] ) since Paul’s arrival in Jerusalem. Five days of the seven had passed: see on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:11">Acts 24:11</span> . Cf. on the whole, Bp. Wordsworth’s note.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [145] Bede, <em> the Venerable</em> , 731; Bedegr, a Greek MS. cited by Bede, nearly identical with Cod. “E,” mentioned in this edn only when it differs from E.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀπὸ τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> Ἀς</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] From Ephesus and the neighbourhood, where Paul had so long taught. ‘Paulus, dum fidelibus placandis intentus est (viz. the believing Jews), in hostium furorem incurrit (viz. of the unbelieving Asiatic Jews).’ Calv., in Meyer, who adds, ‘In how many ways had those who were at Jerusalem this Pentecost, already persecuted Paul in Asia?’
	</p>
<p>Notice the similarity of the charge against him to that against Stephen, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:13">Acts 6:13</span> .</p>
