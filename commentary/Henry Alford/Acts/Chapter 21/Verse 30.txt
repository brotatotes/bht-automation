<b> 30.</b>] The Levites shut the doors to prevent profanation by a riot, and possibly bloodshed, in the temple: hardly, as Bengel, lest Paul should use the temple as an asylum:—the right of asylum was only (Exod. xxi. 13, 14) for murder unawares. But by ver. 14 there, and by Joab’s fleeing to the altar, 1 Kings ii. 28 ff., we see that it was resorted to on other occasions.
