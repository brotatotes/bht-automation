<b> 23.</b>] They were not ‘<i>casting off their garments</i>,’ as preparing to stone him, or even as<i> representing</i> the action of such preparation: the former would be futile, as he was in the custody of the tribune,—the latter absurd, and not borne out by any known habit of the Jews: but<b> shaking their garments</b>, as shaking off the dust, abominating such an expression and him who uttered it. The casting dust into the air was part of the same gesture. Chrysostom explains it in this way.
