<p><strong> 23.</strong> <strong> δύο τινάς</strong> ] <strong> some two</strong> : see reff., and Winer, edn. 6, § 25. 2. <em> b</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> στρατιώτας</strong> , the ordinary heavy-armed legionary soldiers: distinguished below from the ἱππεῖς and δεξιολάβοι .
	</p>
<p><strong> δεξιολάβους</strong> ] This word has never been satisfactorily explained. Suidas, Phavorinus, Beza, Kuin., al, explain it παραφύλακες : Meursius, in his Glossarium Græcobarbarum, a kind of <em> military lictors</em> , παρὰ τὸ λαβεῖν τὴν τοῦ δεσμίου δεξιάν ; the Vulgate, <em> lancearios (spearmen</em> , E. V.): Meyer, a sort of light-armed troops, <em> rorarii</em> or <em> velites</em> , either <em> jaculatores</em> or <em> funditores</em> . He quotes a passage from Constantine Porphyrogenitus ( οἱ δὲ λεγόμενοι τουρμάρχαι εἰς ὑπουργίαν τῶν στρατηγῶν ἐτάχθησαν . σημαίνει δὲ τοιοῦτον ἀξίωμα τὸν ἔχοντα ὑφ ʼ ἑαυτὸν στρατιώτας τοξοφόρους πεντακοσίους , καὶ πελταστὰς τριακοσίους , καὶ δεξιολάβους ἑκατόν ) where they are distinguished from bowmen and peltastæ, and derives the name from <em> grasping the weapon with the right hand</em> , which the peltastæ and bowmen could not be said to do. The reading of Α, δεξιοβόλους ( <em> jaculantes dextrâ</em> Syr.), is apparently a correction.</p>
