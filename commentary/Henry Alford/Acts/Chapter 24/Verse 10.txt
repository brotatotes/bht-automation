<p><strong> 10. </strong> <strong> ἐκ πολλῶν ἐτῶν</strong> ] Felix was now in the seventh year of his procuratorship, which began in the twelfth year of Claudius, A.D. 52.
	</p>
<p>The contrast between Tertullus’s and Paul’s ‘captatio benevolentiæ’ is remarkable. The former I have characterized above. But the Apostle, using no flattery, yet alleges the one point which could really win attention to him from Felix, viz. his confidence arising from speaking before one <em> well skilled by experience in the manners and customs of the Jews</em> .</p>
