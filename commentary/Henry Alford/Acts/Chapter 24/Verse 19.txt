<b> 19.</b>] This also is a skilful argument on the part of the Apostle:—it being the custom of the Romans not to judge a prisoner without the accusers face to face, he deposes that<i> his real accusers were the Asiatic Jews</i>, who first raised the cry against him in the temple,—<i>not the Sanhedrim</i>, who merely received him at the hands of others,—and that<i> these were not present.</i>
