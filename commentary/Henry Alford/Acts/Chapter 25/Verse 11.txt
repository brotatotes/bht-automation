<b> 11. no man may</b> (literally,<b> can</b>)<b> deliver me</b>] Said of<i> legal</i> possibility: “it is not lawful for any man....” The dilemma here put by St. Paul is, “<i>If I am guilty, it is not by them, but by Cæsar, that I must be (and am willing to be) tried, sentenced, and punished. If I am innocent, and Cæsar acquits me, then clearly none will be empowered to give me up to them: therefore, at all events, guilty or innocent, I am not to be made their victim</i>.”
