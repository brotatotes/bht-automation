<b> 22. I would hear the man myself</b>] literally,<b> I was wishing to...</b> It is a modest way of expressing a wish, formed in this case while the procurator was speaking, but spoken of by Agrippa as if now passed by, and therefore not pressed. See Rom. ix. 3, and note there. Agrippa, as a Jew, is anxious to hear Paul’s defence, as a matter of national interest. The procurator’s curator’s ready consent is explained, ver. 26.
