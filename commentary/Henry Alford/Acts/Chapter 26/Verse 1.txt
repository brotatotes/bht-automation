<b> XXVI. 1.</b>] The<i> stretching out of the hand</i> by a speaker was not, as Hammond supposes, the same as the “<i>beckoning with the hand</i>” of ch. xii. 17; xiii. 16. The latter was to ensure silence; but this, a formal attitude usual with orators. Apuleius describes it very precisely. The hand was stretched out with the two lower fingers shut, and the rest straightened. St. Paul’s hand was<i> chained</i>—compare “<i>these bonds</i>,” ver. 29.
