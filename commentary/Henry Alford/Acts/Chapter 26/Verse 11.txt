<p><strong> 11. </strong> <strong> τιμωρῶν</strong> ] viz. <em> by scourging</em> ; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:17">Matthew 10:17</span> . <strong> ἠνάγκαζον</strong> does not imply that any <em> did</em> blaspheme (Christ: so Pliny, Ep. n. 97, speaks of ordering the Bithynian Christians ‘ <em> maledicere Christo</em> ,’ and adds, ‘quorum nihil cogi posse dicuntur qui sunt revera Christiani’): the imperf. only relates the <em> attempt</em> . The persecuting the Christians even to foreign cities, forms the transition to the narrative following.</p>
