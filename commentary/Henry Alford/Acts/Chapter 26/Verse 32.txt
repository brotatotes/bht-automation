<b> 32.</b>] Agrippa in these words delivers his judgment as a Jew: ‘<i>For aught I see, as regards our belief and practices, he might have been set at liberty.</i>’—But now he could not: for “by an appeal the power of the judge, from whom the appeal lies, is taken away, for acquittal as for condemnation. The whole cause in its integrity must be reserved for the superior court.” Grotius.
