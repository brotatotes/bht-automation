<b> CHAP. XXVII. 1–XXVIII. 31</b>] PAUL’ S VOYAGE TO ROME AND SOJOURN THERE. I cannot but express the benefit I have derived in my commentary on this section, from Mr. Smith’s now well-known treatise on the voyage and shipwreck of St. Paul: as also from various letters which he has from time to time put into my hands, tending further to elucidate the subject. The substance of these will be found embodied in an Appendix following the chronological table in the Introduction to the Acts.<br><br><b> 1. that we should sail</b>] Here we have again the<i> first person</i>, the narrator having, in all probability, remained in Palestine, and in the neighbourhood of Paul, during the interval since ch. xxi. 18.
