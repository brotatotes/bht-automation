<b> 10.</b>] From the use of<b> I perceive</b> here, and from the saying itself, it seems clear to me that Paul was not uttering<i> at present</i> any prophetic intimation, but simply his own sound judgment on the difficult question at issue. It is otherwise at vv. 22–24. As Smith remarks, “The event justified St. Paul’s advice. At the same time it may be observed, that a bay, open to nearly one-half the compass, could not have been a good winter harbour” (p. 47).
