<b> 16. running under</b>] i.e.<b> running under the lee of</b>. “St. Luke exhibits here, as on every other occasion, the most perfect command of terms, and gives the utmost precision his language by selecting the most appropriate:—they ran before the wind to<i> leeward of Clauda</i>, hence it is ‘<i>running under:</i>’ they sailed with a side wind<i> to leeward of Cyprus and Crete:</i> hence it is ‘<i>sailed under</i>’” (Smith, p. 61, note).
