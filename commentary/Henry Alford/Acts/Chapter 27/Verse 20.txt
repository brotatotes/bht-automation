<p><strong> 20.</strong> ] The sun and stars were the only guides of the ancients when out of sight of land. The expression, <strong> all hope was taken away</strong> , seems, as Mr. Smith has noticed, to betoken that a greater evil than the mere force of the storm (which perhaps had some little abated: χ . οὐκ ὀλίγου seems to imply that it still indeed raged, but not as before) was afflicting them, viz., <em> the leaky state of the ship</em> , which increased upon them, as is shewn by their successive lightenings of her.</p>
