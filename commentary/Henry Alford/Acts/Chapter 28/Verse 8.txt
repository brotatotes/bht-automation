Dr. Falconer makes this an argument against Malta being meant. “Such a place, dry and rocky, and remarkably healthy, was not likely to produce a disease which is almost peculiar to moist situations.” But Mr. Smith answers, that the changed circumstances of the island might produce this change also: and besides, that he is informed by a physician of Valetta, that the disease is by no means uncommon in Malta.<br><br><b> laid his hands on him</b>] It is remarkable, that so soon after the ‘<i>taking up of serpents</i>,’ we should read of Paul having ‘<i>laid his hands on the sick and they recovered</i>.’ See the two in close connexion, Mark xvi. 18.
