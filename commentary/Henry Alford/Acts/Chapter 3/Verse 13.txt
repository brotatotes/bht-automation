<b> 13. The God of Abraham, &c.]</b> “An appellation more frequent in the Acts than in the other books of the New Testament, and suitable to that period of time.” Bengel. “See,” says Chrysostom, “how he is always connecting God with their ancestors, that he may not seem to be introducing any new doctrine. Before (ch. ii.) he brought in the patriarch David, and now he introduces Abraham.”
