<b> 17.]</b><b> now</b>, introducing a new consideration: see 2 Thess. ii. 6. Here it softens the severer charge of ver. 14: sometimes it intensifies, as ch. xxii. 16; 1 John ii. 28:—especially with “<i>behold</i>,” ch, xiii. 11; xx. 22. No meaning such as ‘<i>now</i> that the real Messiahship of Him whom ye have slain is come to light’ (Meyer) is admissible.
