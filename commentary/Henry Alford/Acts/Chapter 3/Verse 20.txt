<p><strong> 20</strong> .] <strong> ἀποστείλῃ</strong> (see above), <em> literally</em> , not figuratively, by the Spirit: even if the word <strong> send</strong> be no where else applied to the second coming of the Lord, there is no reason why it should not be here: the whole ground and standing-point of these two orations of Peter are <em> peculiar</em> , and the very mention of the ‘times of refreshment’ <em> proceeding forth from the presence of the Father</em> would naturally lead to the position here assigned to the Son, as one <em> sent</em> by the Father. See below, on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:26">Acts 3:26</span> . Besides which, the aor. will not allow of the figurative interpretation, confining, as it does, the ‘ <em> sending</em> ’ to one definite event.
