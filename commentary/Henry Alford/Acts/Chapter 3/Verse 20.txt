<b> 20.] and that he may send</b> (see above),<i> literally</i>,—not figuratively, by the Spirit:—even if the word<b> send</b> be nowhere else applied to the second coming of the Lord, there is no reason why it should not be here: the whole ground and standing-point of these two orations of Peter are peculiar, and the very mention of the ‘times of refresh-ment’<i> proceeding forth from the presence of the Father</i> would naturally lead to the position here assigned to the Son, as one<i> sent</i> by the Father. See below, on ver. 26.
