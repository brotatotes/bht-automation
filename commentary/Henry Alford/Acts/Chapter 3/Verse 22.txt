<b> 22.]</b> This citation is a free but faithful paraphrase of the text in Deuteronomy.—That the words, as spoken by Moses, seem to point to the whole line of prophets sent by God, is not any objection to their being applied to Christ, but rather necessitates, and entirely harmonizes with, that application. See the parable Matt. xxi. 33–41, And<i> none</i> of the whole prophetic body entirely answered to the words<b> like unto me</b>,<i> but Christ</i>. The Jews therefore rightly understood it (though not always consistent in this, comp. John i. 21 with vi. 14) of the Messiah.
