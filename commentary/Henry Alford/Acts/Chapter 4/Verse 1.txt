<p><strong> οἱ ἱερεῖς</strong> , <em> the officiating priests</em> , as soon as they were released from their duties.
	</p>
<p>The <strong> στρατηγὸς τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἱεροῦ</strong> was the captain of the Levitical guard of the temple, mentioned by Jos. B. J. vi. 5. 3, δραμόντες δὲ οἱ τοῦ ἱεροῦ φύλακες ἤγγειλαν τῷ στρατηγῷ . We hear in Jos. Antt. xx. 6. 2, of ὁ στρατηγὸς Ἄνανος : and in B. J. ii. 12. 6, he is said to be son of the high priest Ananias. In <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mal+3:4">Malachi 3:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mal+3:4">Malachi 3:4</span> , we hear of the προστάτης τοῦ ἱεροῦ , who appears to have been the same officer. See Winer, Realw., art. Temple, end.
