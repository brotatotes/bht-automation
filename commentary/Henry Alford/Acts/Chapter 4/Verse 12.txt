<p><strong> 12</strong> .] In Jos. Antt. iii. 1. 5, Moses, praying to God for Israel, says, ἐν αὐτῷ γὰρ εἶναι τὴν σωτηρίαν αὐτοῦ , καὶ οὐκ ἐν ἄλλῳ . <strong> σωτηρία</strong> is used here in the higher sense of <strong> salvation</strong> , not with reference to the healing of the lame man. See reff. The article implies, ‘the salvation for which we all look;’ <strong> our salvation</strong> : ἐστὶν ἡ σωτ . is paraphrased in the next clause by <strong> δεῖ σωθῆναι</strong> ἡμᾶς .
	</p>
<p><strong> οὔτε γὰρ</strong> <strong> …</strong> ] lit. <strong> for neither is there another name under heaven (which is) given (by God) among men</strong> (not ‘ <em> to men</em> ,’ Vulg., Beza, Kuinoel), <strong> whereby we must be saved</strong> : i.e., as E. V. Dr. Burton’s rendering, ‘For neither is the name which is given among men, whereby we are to be saved, any other than this,’ is ungrammatical.</p>
