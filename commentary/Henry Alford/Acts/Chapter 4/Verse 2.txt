<b> 2.] in Jesus</b>,—not, as A.V., ‘<i>through</i> Jesus, but<b> in the person</b> (or example)<b> of Jesus</b>, alleging Him as an example of that which the Sadducees denied: preaching by implication, inasmuch as one resurrection would imply that of all, the resurrection of the dead. We have a similiar use of “<i>in</i>,” 1 Cor. iv. 6, where “<i>in us</i>” means, in the case of myself and Apollos. ‘The resurrection<i> through</i> Jesus’ does not appear on the present occasion to have formed part of their preaching.
