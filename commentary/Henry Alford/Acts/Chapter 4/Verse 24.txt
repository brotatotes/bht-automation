<b> 24. they lifted up their voice to God with one accord]</b> i.e. not, as Meyer supposes, literally all speaking together in a known formula of prayer, but led by some<i> one</i>, and all assenting; not “<i>their voices</i>,” but<b> their voice</b>: see note on ch. ii. 6.<br><br><b> thou art God, which hast made]</b> It. is an acknowledgment that it was the same God, who was now doing these things, that had beforetime prophesied them of Christ.
