<b> 28.]</b> There is an ellipsis in the thought between the verb<b> to do</b>, and its object<b> whatsoever</b>, &c.: “<i>to do</i> [as they thought, their own counsel; but really]<i> whatsoever Thy hand and Thy counsel determined</i>.”<br><br><b> thy hand and thy counsel]</b><b> thy hand</b> indicates the<i> Power</i>,<b> thy counsel</b> the<i> Wisdom ot God</i>. The Wisdom decreed, the Hand performed.
