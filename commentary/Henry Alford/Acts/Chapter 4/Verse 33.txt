<p><strong> 33</strong> .] The Apostles were the specially appointed witnesses of the Resurrection, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:22">Acts 1:22</span> ; and this their testimony they gave <em> with power</em> , i.e. with a special gift of the Holy Spirit to enforce and illustrate, to persuade and dispute on, those facts of which their own experience (see Act 4:20 ) informed them. That the Spirit did not inspire them with unbroken uniformity in <em> matters of fact</em> , our present Gospels, the remnants to us of this very testimony, sufficiently witness. Nor was this necessary: each man reported what he had heard and seen; and it was in the <em> manner</em> of delivering this report that the great power of the Spirit was shewn. See, on the whole subject, Prolegg. Vol. I. i. § iii. 5 ff.
	</p>
<p><strong> χάρις</strong> , better <strong> grace</strong> , i.e. from God, than <em> favour</em> , i.e. from the people, which would hardly be so absolutely designated.</p>
