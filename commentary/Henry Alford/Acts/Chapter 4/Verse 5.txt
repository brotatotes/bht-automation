<p><strong> 5.</strong> ] <strong> αὐτῶν</strong> , <em> of the Jews</em> ; a construction frequently used where there can be little chance of mistaking to whom or what the pronoun refers, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:44">John 8:44</span> , note; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:26">Romans 2:26</span> ; Winer, edn. 6, § 22. 3. 3 b. In this place, however, it <em> has been</em> mistaken: for Meyer refers αὐτῶν to the believers just mentioned, inasmuch as they were Jews: absurdly enough.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἄρχ. κ. πρεσβ. κ. γρ</strong> .] <em> The Sanhedrim</em> : see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:4">Matthew 2:4</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:59">Matthew 26:59</span> ; ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:21">Acts 5:21</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐν Ἱερουσαλήμ</strong> ] Why is this specified? The difficulty of accounting for it has led in some MSS. to ἐν being altered to <strong> εἰς</strong> , so as to imply that certain of them who dwelt out of town (Lightf. &amp;c.) were summoned <em> to Jerusalem</em> , I believe it merely implies that the meeting was not held <em> in the temple</em> , but <em> in the city</em> .</p>
