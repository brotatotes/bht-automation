<p><strong> 29.</strong> ] Peter, by word of mouth; the Apostles, as a body, by <em> assent</em> , implied in <em> his own utterance</em> and <em> their silence</em> . There is no ellipse of ἄλλοι before ἀπόστ .
	</p>
<p>This defence of Peter divides itself into the propositions of an ordinary syllogism (1) <em> The statement of the general truth that we must obey God rather than men:</em> (2) <em> The reduction of the present circumstances under that general truth</em> , as being the work of the God of their Fathers shewn in his having raised and glorified Jesus, for a definite purpose, to give, &amp;c. (3) <em> The identification of themselves with the course of action marked out by the</em> <strong> πειθαρχεῖν δεῖ</strong> <strong> …</strong> in that they were bearing witness to God’s work, under the inspiration of the Holy Spirit given them as men obedient to God.
	</p>
<p>The whole is a <em> perfect model of concise and ready eloquence, and of unanswerable logical coherence;</em> and a notable fulfilment of the promise, δοθήσεται ὑμῖν ἐν ἐκείνῃ τῇ ὥρᾳ τί λαλήσητε ( Mat 10:19 ).
	</p>
<p><strong> πειθαρχεῖν</strong> ] much stronger than ἀκούειν , ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:19">Acts 4:19</span> , as their conduct, in <em> persisting after prohibition</em> , had been more marked and determined. That was a mere ‘ <em> listening to</em> ’ the proposition then made to them: this, a course of deliberate action, chosen and entered on.
	</p>
<p><strong> θεῷ</strong> opposed to τῆς διδ . <strong> ὑμῶν</strong> of the H. P.; and to <strong> ἀνθρώπου τούτου</strong> . In the background, there would be the command of the angel, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:20">Acts 5:20</span> ; but it is not alleged: the great duty of preaching the Gospel of Christ is kept on its highest grounds.</p>
