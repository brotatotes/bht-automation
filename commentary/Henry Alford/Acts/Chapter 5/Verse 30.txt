<b> 30. The God of our fathers]</b> Thus binding on Christ and his work to the<i> covenant</i> whereof all present were partakers.<br><br><b> ye</b>, emphatic: answering to the emphatic “<i>upon</i><b> us</b>” of the High Priest.<br><br><b> on a tree (or, beam of wood)]</b> Compare the similar contrast in ch. iii. 14, 15. The manner of death is described thus barely and ignominiously, to waken compunction in the hearers, to whom the expression was well known as entailing curse and disgrace on the victim.
