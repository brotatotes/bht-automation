<p><strong> 33. </strong> <strong> διεπρίοντο</strong> ] sc. ταῖς καρδίαις as ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:54">Acts 7:54</span> . From its conjunction there with ἔβρυχον τ . ὀδόντας , it does not appear to have any connexion with the phrase πρίειν or διαπρίειν τ . ὀδ . with which Hesych [40] and Wetst. identify it. <strong> They were cut asunder</strong> (in heart). So Persius, iii. 8, ‘turgescit vitrea bilis: <em> Findor</em> , ut Arcadiæ pecuaria rudere credas.’ And Plautus, Bacch. ii. 3. 17, ‘Cor meum et cerebrum, Nicobule, <em> finditur</em> , Istius hominis ubi fit quaque mentio.’ And Euseb. H. E. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:1">Acts 5:1</span> (in Suicer, sub voce, where he cites other authorities also), ἐχαλέπαινον κ . διεπρίοντο καθ ʼ ἡμῶν .
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [40] Hesychius of Jerusalem, cent y . vi.
	</p>
