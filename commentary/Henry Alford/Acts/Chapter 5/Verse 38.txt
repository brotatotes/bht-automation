<p><strong> 38.</strong> ] <strong> ἐὰν ᾖ</strong> <strong> , </strong> <strong> εἰ</strong> <strong> … </strong> <strong> ἐστίν</strong> <strong> :</strong> implying by the first, perhaps, the manifold devices of human imposture and wickedness, any of which it might be, (q. d. ὅτι ἂν ᾖ ἐξ ἀνθρώπ .,) and all of which would equally come to nought, and, on the other hand, the solemnity and fixedness of the divine purpose, by the <em> indicative</em> , which are also intimated, in our text, by the <em> pres.</em> οὐ δύνασθε .
	</p>
<p>Or perhaps the indicative is used in the second place, because that is the <em> case assumed</em> , and on which the advice is founded [at all events the distinction ought to be preserved, which is not done in E. V.].
	</p>
<p><strong> ἡ βουλή</strong> ] The whole plan <strong> the scheme</strong> , of which this <strong> ἔργον</strong> , the fact under your present cognizance, forms a part.</p>
