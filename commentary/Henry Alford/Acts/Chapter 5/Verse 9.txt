<p><strong> 9</strong> .] To <em> try the omniscience</em> of the Spirit then visibly dwelling in the Apostles and the church, was, in the highest sense, <em> to tempt the Spirit of God</em> . It was a saying in their hearts ‘There is no Holy Spirit:’ and certainly approached very closely to a sin against the Holy Ghost. Peter characterizes the sin more solemnly this second time, because by the wife’s answer it was now proved to be no <em> individual lie</em> of a bad and covetous man, but a <em> preconcerted scheme</em> to deceive God.
	</p>
<p><strong> οἱ πόδες</strong> ] Not that Peter heard (Olsh.) the tread of the young men outside (they were probably barefooted), but it is an expression common in the poetical or lively description of the Hebrews, and indeed of all nations (see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="isa+52:7">Isaiah 52:7</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="na+1:15">Nahum 1:15</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:15">Romans 10:15</span> ; Eurip. Hippol. 656; Soph. Œd. Col. 890, al. freq.), making the member whereby the person acts, the actor. I take the words to mean, that the time was just at hand for their return: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:9">James 5:9</span> . The space of three hours was not too long: they would have to carry the corpse to the burying-ground, at a considerable distance from the city (Lightf.), and when there, to dig a grave, and bury it.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐξοίσουσιν</strong> ] This word, spoken <em> before her death, decisively proves</em> that death to have been not a <em> result</em> merely of her detection, but a judicial infliction.</p>
