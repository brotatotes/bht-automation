<b> 12.] the people</b>,<i> first</i>,—that by means of the popular feeling they might act upon the<b> elders and scribes</b>, the members of the Sanhedrim.<br><br><b> came upon him]</b> The same persons,—acting now by the authority of the Sanhedrim; Saul, among those from<i> Cilicia</i>, being, as is afterwards (ch. vii. 58) implied, among the foremost,—<b>came upon him, and seized him.</b>
