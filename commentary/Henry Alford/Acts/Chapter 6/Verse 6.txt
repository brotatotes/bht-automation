 6. ]  ἐπέθηκαν , viz. the Apostles. Their office of giving themselves to  prayer is here specially exercised.
	The  laying on of hands , the earliest mention of which is connected with  blessing only ( Gen 48:14 ), was prescribed to Moses as the form of conferring office on Joshua, Numbers 27:18 , and from that time was used on such occasions by the Jews. From its adoption by the Apostles, it has ever been the practice of the Christian church in  ordaining , or  setting apart her ministers. It was also used by the Apostles on those who, having been baptized, were to be fully endowed with the gifts of the Holy Spirit: see ch. Acts 8:17 ; Acts 19:6 , and Hebrews 6:2 .
