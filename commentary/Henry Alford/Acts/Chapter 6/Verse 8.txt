<b> 8.]</b> This is the first instance of any,<i> not an Apostle</i>, working signs and wonders. The power was perhaps conferred by the laying on of the Apostles’ hands; though, that having been for a special purpose merely, and the working miracles being a fulfilment of the promise, Mark xvi. 17, 18, to<i> believers</i>, I should rather refer the power to the<i> eminence of Stephen’s faith</i>.<br><br><b> full of grace</b>, i. e,<b> divine grace</b> (not ‘favour with the people’): the effects of which, the miracles were called<i> gifts</i><i> of Grace</i> (<i>charismata</i>, from<i> charis</i>, grace).
