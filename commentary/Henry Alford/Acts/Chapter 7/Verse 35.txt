<p><strong> 35.</strong> ] The second τοῦτον is repeated emphatically. So οὗτος again, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:36-38">Acts 7:36-38</span> [to impress on them God’s choice of one whom they rejected].
	</p>
<p><strong> ἠρνήσαντο</strong> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:27">Acts 7:27</span> . The rejecter of Moses there is regarded as the <em> representative of the nation</em> : see note on αὐτοῖς , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:26">Acts 7:26</span> . In this express mention of the rejection of Moses by the Jews and his election and mission by God, the parallel of Jesus Christ is no doubt in Stephen’s mind, and the inference intended to be drawn, that <em> it does not follow that</em> GOD REJECTS <em> those whom</em> THEY REJECTED.
	</p>
<p>The difficulty of <strong> ἀπέσταλκεν</strong> has caused it to be altered into the historic tense, ἀπέστειλεν . But the perf. sets forth not only the fact of God’s sending Moses <em> then</em> , but the endurance of his mission till now <strong> him hath God sent</strong> : with a closer reference than before, to Him whom God had <em> now</em> exalted as the true ἄρχοντα κ . λυτρωτήν . See ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:31">Acts 5:31</span> .</p>
