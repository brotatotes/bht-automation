<p><strong> 39.</strong> ] Another instance, brought home again by the words <strong> οἱ πατέρες ἡμῶν</strong> , of <em> rejection of God’s appointed messenger and servant</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐστράφησαν</strong> ] <strong> they turned back in their heart to Egypt</strong> : not, ‘ <em> they wished to return to Egypt</em> ,’ of which in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ex+32:0">Exodus 32:0</span> there is no trace (but later, in Num 14:4 ), and which would hardly suit <strong> προπορεύσονται</strong> ; but ‘they apostatized in heart to the Egyptian idolatries.’ The very title by which Aaron proclaims his idol, is, ‘These be thy gods, O Israel, which <em> brought thee up out of</em> the land of Egypt,’ <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ex+32:4">Exodus 32:4</span> . See also <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ne+9:18">Nehemiah 9:18</span> .</p>
