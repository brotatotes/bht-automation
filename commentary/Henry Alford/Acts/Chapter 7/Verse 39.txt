 39. ] Another instance, brought home again by the words  οἱ πατέρες ἡμῶν , of  rejection of God’s appointed messenger and servant .
	 ἐστράφησαν ]  they turned back in their heart to Egypt : not, ‘  they wished to return to Egypt ,’ of which in Exodus 32:0 there is no trace (but later, in Num 14:4 ), and which would hardly suit  προπορεύσονται ; but ‘they apostatized in heart to the Egyptian idolatries.’ The very title by which Aaron proclaims his idol, is, ‘These be thy gods, O Israel, which  brought thee up out of the land of Egypt,’ Exodus 32:4 . See also Nehemiah 9:18 .
