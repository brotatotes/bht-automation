<p><strong> 53.</strong> ] The use of <strong> οἵτινες</strong> , instead of οἱ , so very frequent in the Acts and Epistles, occurs when the clause introduced by it contains a <em> further explanation</em> of the position or classification of the person or persons alluded to, and not when the relative serves for simple identification. See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:25">Romans 1:25</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:32">Romans 1:32</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> εἰς διαταγὰς ἀγγέλων</strong> ] Many explanations have been given. Chrys. διαταχθέντα νόμον λέγει , τὸν ἐγχειρισθέντα αὐτῷ δι ʼ ἄγγελον τὸν ὀφθέντα αὐτῷ ἐν τῷ βάτῳ : and Œc [51] νόμον λαβόντας διατάξεις ἔχοντα , αἵτινες ἰσάγγελον ἐποίουν πολιτείαν ἔχειν τοὺς τελοῦντας αὐτόν . Heinsius and Lightfoot understand by ἀγγέλ . <em> the prophets</em> : Grot., Calov., and Krebs, ‘ <em> præsentibus angelorum ordinibus</em> ,’ taking διαταγάς = διατάξεις in the sense of divisions of an army ( Jdt 8:36 ), in which it never occurs, not to say that εἰς will not bear this: Beza, Calv., Pisc., Elsn., Hamm., Kuin., &amp;c., ‘ <em> ab angelis promulgatum</em> ,’ which εἰς will not bear ( ἐν ): Winer, Gr., edn. 6, § 32. 4, b, ‘ <em> as commands of angels</em> ’ (but see below), which, however, was not the fact (Mey., who refers to Jos. Antt. xv. 5. 3, ἡμῶν τὰ κάλλιστα τῶν δογμάτων καὶ τὰ ὁσιώτατα τῶν ἐν τοῖς νόμοις <strong> δι</strong> <strong> ʼ</strong> ἀγγέλων <strong> παρὰ τοῦ θεοῦ</strong> μαθόντων ): the Syriac version, ‘ <em> per mandatum angelorum</em> :’ Vulg. and Calv., ‘ <em> in dispositione</em> (or - <em> onibus</em> ) <em> angelorum</em> :’ Schöttg., ‘ <em> per ministerium angelorum</em> .’ These three last are precluded by the foregoing remarks. The key to the right rendering seems to be the similar expression in ref. Gal., ὁ νόμος <strong> διαταγεὶς δι</strong> <strong> ʼ </strong> <strong> ἀγγέλων</strong> . The law was <em> given</em> by God, but <em> announced</em> by angels. The people received God’s law then, <strong> εἰς διαταγὰς ἀγγέλων</strong> , <strong> at the injunction</strong> (a sense of διατ . amply justified, see Palm and Rost’s lex. διάταξις , and Polyb. iv. 19. 10; 87. 5: and preferred by Winer in his last edn., ut supra) <strong> of angels</strong> . So <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:41">Matthew 12:41</span> , μετενόησαν <strong> εἰς τὸ κήρυγμα</strong> Ἰῶνα , ‘they repented <em> at the preaching</em> of Jonas.’ The only other legitimate rendering, ‘as the injunctions of angels,’ comes under the objections made to Winer’s former view, above.
	</p>
