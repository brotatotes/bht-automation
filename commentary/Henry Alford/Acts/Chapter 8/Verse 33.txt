<b> 33.</b>] This stands in the Hebrew ‘He was taken away by distress and judgment’ (so in the margin of the A.V.): i.e. as Lowth, ‘by an oppressive judgment.’<br><br><b> his generation</b>] i.e.<b> the age in which he shall live</b>— ‘the wickedness of his contemporaries.’ The fathers, and Bede and some modern Commentators, explain ‘<i>His generation</i>’ of His eternal Sonship and His miraculous Incarnation. But the Hebrew does not seem to bear this out.
