<p><strong> 19. </strong> <strong> ἐνίσχ</strong> .] intrans. see reff.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἡμ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τινάς</strong> ] A few days; of quiet, and becoming acquainted with those as brethren, whom he came to persecute as infidels: but not to learn from them the gospel ( οὐδὲ γὰρ ἐγὼ παρὰ ἀνθρώπου παρέλαβον αὐτό , οὔτε ἐδιδάχθην , Gal 1:12 ), nor was the time longer than to admit of εὐθέως being used, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:20">Acts 9:20</span> , and indeed the same εὐθέως of the whole space (including his <em> preaching</em> in our Act 9:20-21 ) preceding the journey to Arabia, in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:16">Galatians 1:16</span> .
	</p>
<p>Pearson places that journey <em> before</em> our <strong> ἐγένετο δέ</strong> , which however is manifestly against the sense of the text: Michaelis and Heinrichs, between <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:19-20">Acts 9:19-20</span> , to which there is the same objection: Kuinoel and Olsh., after <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:25">Acts 9:25</span> , which the εὐθέως of Gal 1:16 will not allow: Neander and Meyer, in the ἡμέραι ἱκαναί of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:23">Acts 9:23</span> , which time however in our text is certainly allotted to the progress of his preaching in Damascus, and the increase of the hostility of the Jews in consequence. See below.</p>
