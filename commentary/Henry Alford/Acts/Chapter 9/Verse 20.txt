<b> 20. he preached Jesus</b>] The alteration to “<i>Christ</i>” has probably, as Meyer suggests, been made from doctrinal considerations, to fix on “<b>the Son of God</b>” the theological sense,—<i> that Christ is the Son of God</i>—instead of that which it now bears,—that<i> Jesus is the Son of God</i>, i.e. that Jesus of Nazareth,<i> as a matter of fact</i>, is the Son of God, i.e. the Messiah expected under that appellation.
