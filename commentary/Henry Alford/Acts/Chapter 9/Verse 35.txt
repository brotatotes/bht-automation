<b> 35. all that dwelt in L. and S. saw him;—which also</b> (this is the literal rendering, and is equivalent to and they)<b> turned to the Lord</b>] A general conversion of the inhabitants to the faith followed.<br><br><b> Saron</b>] Perhaps not a village, but the celebrated plain of that name [Sharon], extending along the coast from Cæsarea to Joppa, see Isa. xxxiii, 9; xxxv. 2; lxv. 10; Cant. ii. 1; 1 Chron. xxvii. 29. Mariti mentions a village Saren between Lydda and Arsuf (see Josh. xii. 18, marg. A.V.): but more recent travellers do not notice it.
