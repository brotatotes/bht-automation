<b> 28.</b>]<b> whom</b> (Christ)<b> we</b> (myself and Timothy: but generally, of all who were associated with him in this true preaching: not, ‘I,’ which here quite destroys the force. We preach Christ—not circumcision, not angel-worship, not asceticism, as the source of this hope)<b> proclaim</b> (as being this<b> hope</b><b> of the glory</b>),<b> warning</b> (see on Eph. vi. 4, and below)<b> every man, and teaching every man</b> (I am inclined with Meyer to take<b> warning</b> and<b> teaching</b> as corresponding in the main to the two great subjects of Christian preaching, repentance and faith)<b> in all wisdom</b> (these words represent the method of this teaching);<b> that we may present</b> (see above ver. 22)<b> every man</b> (notice the emphatic triple repetition of every man, shewing that the Apostle was jealous of every the least invasion, on the part of the false teachers, of those souls with whom he was put in charge. At the same time it carries a solemn individual appeal to those thus warned and taught)<b> perfect in Christ</b> (element of his perfection, in union with and life in Him,—comprehending both knowledge and practice. The presentation spoken of is clearly that at the great day of Christ’s appearing)?
