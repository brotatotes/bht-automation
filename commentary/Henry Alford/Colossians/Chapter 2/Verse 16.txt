<p><strong> 16</strong> .] <strong> Let no one therefore</strong> (because this is so that ye are complete in Christ, and that God in Him hath put away and dispensed with all that is secondary and intermediate) <strong> judge you</strong> (pronounce judgment of right or wrong over you, sit in judgment on you) <strong> in</strong> (reff.) <strong> eating</strong> (not, in St. Paul’s usage, <em> meat</em> ( βρῶμα ), see reff.; in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:32">John 4:32</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:27">John 6:27</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:55">John 6:55</span> , it seems to have this signification. Mey. quotes Il. τ . 210, Od. α . 191, Plato, Legg. vi. p. 783 c, to shew that in classical Greek the meanings are sometimes interchanged. The same is true of πόσις and πόμα ) <strong> and</strong> (or <strong> or</strong> ) <strong> in drinking</strong> (i.e. in the matter of the whole cycle of legal ordinances and prohibitions which regarded eating and drinking: these two words being perhaps taken not separately and literally, for there does not appear to have been in the law any special prohibition against <em> drinks</em> , but as forming together a category in ordinary parlance. If however it is desired to press each word, the reference of πόσις must be to the Nazarite vow, Num 6:3 ) <strong> or in respect</strong> (reff.: Chrys. and Thdrt. give it the extraordinary meaning of ‘in part,’ ἐν μέρει ἑορτῆς · οὐ γὰρ δὴ πάντα κατεῖχον τὰ πρότερα : Mey. explains it, ‘in the category of which is much the same as the explanation in the text) <strong> of a feast or new-moon or sabbaths</strong> (i.e. yearly, monthly, or weekly celebrations; see reff.),</p>
