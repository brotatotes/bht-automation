<b> 12</b>.] On Epaphras, see ch. i.'7 note. By mentioning Epaphras’s anxious prayers for them, he works further on their affections, giving them an additional motive for stedfastness, in that one of themselves was thus striving in prayer for them.<b> that ye may stand,—perfect and fully persuaded,—in</b> (be firmly settled in, without danger of vacillating or falling: the preposition belongs to the verb “<i>ye may stand</i>,” not to “<i>fully assured</i>”)<b> all the</b> (literally, ‘<i>in every</i>:’ but we cannot thus express it in English)<b> will of God.</b>
