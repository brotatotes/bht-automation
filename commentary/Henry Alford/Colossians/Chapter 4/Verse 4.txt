<b> 4.</b>] This second<b> that</b> gives the purpose of the previous verse, not the purpose of his<i> being in bonds</i>,—nor to be joined with “<i>praying</i>.” If that might be so, the door opened, &c.,—then he would make it known as he ought to do—then he would be fulfilling the requirements of that apostolic calling, from which now in his im-prisonment he was laid aside, Certainly this is the meaning,—and not, as ordinarily understood, that he might boldly declare the Gospel in<i> his imprisonment</i>.
