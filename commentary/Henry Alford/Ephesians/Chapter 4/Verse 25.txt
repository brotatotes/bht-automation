<b> 25.] Wherefore</b> (because of the general character of the<i> new man</i> as contrasted with the<i> old</i>, which has been given: “before, he spoke generally of the old man: now he describes it part by part.’ Chrysostom)<b> having put away</b> (the past tense should be noticed here: it was open to the Apostle to write “<i>putting away</i>,” but he prefers the past—because the man must have once for all put off falsehood as a characteristic before he enters the habit of speaking truth)<b> falsehood</b> (abstract)<b>, speak truth each one with his neighbour</b> (a quotation from the prophet Zechariah, see reff.)<b>: for we are members of one another</b> (Rom. xii. 5. of one another brings out the relation between man and man more strongly than if he had said,<i> of one body:</i> at the same time it serves to remind. them that all mutual duties of Christians are grounded on their union to and in Christ, and not on mere ethical considerations).
