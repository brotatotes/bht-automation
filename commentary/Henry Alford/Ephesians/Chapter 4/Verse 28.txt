 28 .]  Let him that stealeth (not ‘  that stole ,’ as E. V.; ‘  qui furabatur ,’ Vulg.: cf. reff., and Winer, § 45. 7. Stier remarks well, that the word lies between κλέψας and κλέπτης : the former would be too mild, the latter too strong)  steal no longer, but rather ( οὐ γὰρ ἀρκεῖ παύσασθαι τῆς ἁμαρτίας , ἀλλὰ καὶ τὴν ἐναντίαν αὐτῆς ὁδὸν μετελθεῖν , Thl.: similarly Chr.)  let him labour, working (cf. besides reff., Joh 6:27 and note)  with his hands (contrast to his former idleness for good, and bad use of those hands)  that which is good ( τὸ ἀγ . ‘antitheton ad furtum prius manu piceata commissum.’ Beng.),  in order that (as a purpose to be set before every Christian in his honest labour)  he may have to impart to him that has need .
