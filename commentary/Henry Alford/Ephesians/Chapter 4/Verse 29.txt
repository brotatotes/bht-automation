<b> 29.] Let every corrupt</b> (or,<b> worthless</b>; “that has no profitable work to do,” Chrysostom: not so much<i> ‘filthy</i>,’—see ch. v. 4)<b> saying not come forth</b> (so the original, according to the Greek idiom)<b> from your month, but whatever (saying) is good for building up of the (present) need</b> (the<b> need</b> is the<i> deficiency</i>; the part which needs<i> to be built up,</i> the defect to be supplied by edification),<b> that it may give grace</b> (minister spiritual benefit: be a means of conveying through you the grace of God)<b> to them that hear.</b>
