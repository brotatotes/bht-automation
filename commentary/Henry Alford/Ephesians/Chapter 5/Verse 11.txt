<p><strong> 11</strong> .] <strong> and have no fellowship with</strong> (better than ‘ <em> be not partakers in</em> ,’ as De W., which would require a genitive, see Demosth. p. 1299. 20, συγκεκοινωνήκαμεν τῆς δόξης ταύτης οἱ κατεστασιασμένοι : whereas the person <em> with whom</em> , is regularly put in the dative, e.g. Dio Cass. xxxvii. 41, συγκοινωνήσαντός σφισι τῆς συνωμοσίας , ib. lxxvii. 16, συνεκοινώνησαν αὐτῇ κ . ἕτεραι τρεῖς τῆς καταδίκης . And Php 4:14 furnishes no objection to this rendering) <strong> the unfruitful works of darkness</strong> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:19">Galatians 5:19</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:22">Galatians 5:22</span> ; on which Jer., vol. vii. p. 505, says ‘vitia in semetipsa finiuntur et pereunt, virtutes frugibus pullulant et redundant.’ See also the distinction in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:20-21">John 3:20-21</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:29">John 5:29</span> , between τὰ φαῦλα πράσσειν and τὰ ἀγαθὰ or τὴν ἀλήθειαν ποιεῖν ), <strong> but rather even reprove them</strong> (see reff., <em> in words</em> : not only abstain from fellowship with them, but attack them and put them to shame).</p>
