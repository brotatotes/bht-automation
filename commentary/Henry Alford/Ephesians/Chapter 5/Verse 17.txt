<b> 17.] On this account</b> (because ye have need so prudently to define your rule of life, and so carefully to watch for opportunities of good)<b> be not</b> (better than ‘do not become, which puts the<i> process of degeneracy</i> too strongly in English)<b> senseless (or foolish), but understand</b> (know intelligently,—as matter of fact, as the servant who knew his lord’s will and did it not, Luke xii. 47)<b> what is the will of the Lord.</b>
