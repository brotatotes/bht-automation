<b> 33.] Nevertheless</b> (not to go further into the mystical bearings of the subject)<b> you also</b> (as well as Christ)<b> every one, let each so love his own wife as himself; and the wife</b> (in the making out of the sentence, a verb must be supplied:—but the wife, for her part,— ‘<i>I order,</i>’ or, ‘<i>let her see</i>’)<b>, that she fear</b> (so literally: (Ecumenius says, “As a wife ought to fear, not as a slave”)<b> her husband.</b>
