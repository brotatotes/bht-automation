<b> 7.] Be not</b> (the distinction ‘<i>Become not</i>’ is unnecessary and indeed unsuitable: it is not a gradual ‘becoming,’ but ‘being,’ like them, which he here dehorts from. See note, ch. iv. last verse)<b> therefore</b> (since this is so—that God’s wrath comes on them)<b> partakers</b> (see ch. iii. 6)<b> with them</b> (the<i> sons</i> of disobedience, not the<i> sins</i>:—sharers in that which they have in common, viz. these practices: their<i> present</i> habitude, not,<i> their punishment,</i> which is future).
