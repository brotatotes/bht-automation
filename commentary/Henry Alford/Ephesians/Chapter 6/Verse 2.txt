<b> 2.] Honour thy father and thy mother; for such is the first commandment</b> (in the Decalogue, which naturally stands at the head of all God’s other commandments; and which, though not. formally binding on us as Christians, is quoted, in matters of eternal obligation [not of positive enactment], as an eminent example of God’s holy will) with a promise (i. e. with a special promise attached. The occurrence of the description of God as ‘shewing mercy unto thousands, &c.,’ after the second commandment, does not, as Jerome and others have thought, present any difficulty—for that is<i> no special promise attached to the commandment.</i> Nor does the fact that no other commandment occurs in the<i> decalogue</i> with a promise);
