<b> 20.] on behalf of which I am an ambassador</b> (<i>of Christ: to whom,</i> is understood: we need not supply,<i> to the court of Rome</i>)<b> in chains</b> (Wetst. remarks, an<i> ambassador in chains</i> was an unknown thing, ambassadors being, by the law of nations, inviolable. His being thus a captive ambassador, was all the more reason why they should pray earnestly that he might have boldness, &c.)<b>: that in</b> (the matter of, in dealing with)<b> it I may speak freely, as I ought to speak.</b>
