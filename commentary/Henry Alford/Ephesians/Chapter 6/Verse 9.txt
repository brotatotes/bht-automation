<b> 9.] And, ye masters, do the same things</b> (i. e. act, analogously, as<i> they</i> are to remember one whom they serve, so [below] are<i> ye</i>—and,<i> mutatis mutandis</i>, to act to them as they to you)<b> with regard to them, forbearing your</b> (usual: such as most masters do use)<b> threatening: knowing</b> (as ye do, see ver. 8)<b> that both of them and of yourselves the Master is in the heavens; and respect of persons</b> (warping of justice from regard to any man’s individual pre-eminence)<b> exists not with Him.</b>
