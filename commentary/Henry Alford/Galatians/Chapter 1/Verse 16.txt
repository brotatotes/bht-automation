<p><strong> 16</strong> .] <strong> ἀποκαλ</strong> <strong> .</strong> belongs to εὐδόκησεν , not to καλ . (Erasm.), nor to ἀφορ . and καλ . (Est., al.), <strong> to reveal his Son</strong> (viz. by that subsequent revelation, of which before, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:12">Galatians 1:12</span> ; not by his <em> conversion</em> , which, as above, answers to καλέσας ) <strong> in me</strong> (strictly: ‘ <em> within me</em> ,’ τῆς ἀποκαλύψεως καταλαμπούσης αὐτοῦ τὴν ψυχήν , Chrys.: not ‘ <em> through me</em> ’ (Jer., Erasm., Grot., &amp;c), which follows in ἵνα εὐαγγ . κ . τ . λ ., nor <em> in my case</em> (Rückert, al.), as manifested by me as an example to myself or to others, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:9">1 John 4:9</span> ; the context here requires that his own personal illumination should be the point brought out; nor ‘ <em> to me</em> ’ (Calv., al.), which though nearly equivalent to ‘ <em> in me</em> ,’ weakens the sense), &amp;c. Notice the <em> present</em> εὐαγγελίζωμαι , the ministry being not a single act, but a lasting occupation.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐν τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἔθν</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] the main object of his Apostleship: see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:7">Galatians 2:7</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:9">Galatians 2:9</span> . ‘ εὐθέως is really connected with ἀπῆλθον : but the Apostle, whose thoughts outrun his words, has interposed the negative clause, to anticipate his purpose in going away.’ Jowett.
	</p>
<p><strong> προσανεθ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] See reff. The classical sense is, ‘ <em> to lay on an additional burden</em> :’ and in middle voice, ‘ <em> on oneself</em> :’ cf. Xen. Mem. ii. 1. 8. The later sense, ‘ <em> to impart to</em> ,’ τινί τι , either, as here, with the view of <em> getting</em> , or as in ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:6">Galatians 2:6</span> , with that of <em> conferring</em> . The πρός in composition does not signify <em> addition</em> , but <em> direction</em> : see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:7">Acts 27:7</span> , note.
	</p>
<p><strong> σαρκὶ κ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> αἵμ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] i.e. with mankind, “generally with the idea of weakness and frailty,” Ellic. whose note see, and also reff.</p>
