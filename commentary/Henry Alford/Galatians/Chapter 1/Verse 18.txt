<p><strong> 18</strong> .] At first sight, it would appear as if the three years were to be reckoned from his <em> return</em> to <em> Damascus</em> : but on closer examination we see that μετὰ ἔτη τρ . stands in opposition to εὐθέως above, and the ἀνῆλθον κ . τ . λ . here answers to ἀπῆλθον κ . τ . λ . there. So that we must reckon them from his <em> conversion</em> : ὅτε δὲ εὐδόκησεν κ . τ . λ . ruling the whole narrative. See also on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:1">Galatians 2:1</span> .
	</p>
<p>This is the journey of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:26">Acts 9:26</span> , where see note. There is no real discrepancy between that account and this. The incident which led to his leaving Damascus (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:25">Acts 9:25</span> . 2Co 11:32-33 ) has not necessarily any connexion with his purpose in <em> going to Jerusalem</em> : a purpose which may have been entertained before, or determined on after, that incident. To this visit must be referred the vision of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:17-18">Acts 22:17-18</span> .
