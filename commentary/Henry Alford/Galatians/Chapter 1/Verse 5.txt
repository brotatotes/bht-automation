 5 .  ᾧ ἡ δόξα ] So (reff.) on other occasions, when speaking of the wonderful things of God, St. Paul adds a doxology. “In politeia, quando regum aut principum nomina appellamus, id honesto quodam gestu, reverentia, et genuflexione facere solemus. Multo magis cum de Deo loquimur, genu cordis flectere debemus.” Luther. In ἡ δόξα ,  the glory κατ ʼ ἐξοχήν , or ‘the glory which is His,’ the article is probably inserted for solemnity. “In this and similar forms of doxology, excepting the angelic doxology, Luke 2:14 , and that of the multitude, Luke 19:38 , δόξα regularly takes the article when used alone: see Romans 11:36 ; Romans 16:27 ; Ephesians 3:21 ; Philippians 4:20 ; 2 Timothy 4:18 ; Heb 13:21 ; 2 Peter 3:18 . When joined with one or more substantives, it appears sometimes with the article (1 Peter 4:11 ; Revelation 1:6 ; Rev 7:12 ): sometimes without it (Romans 2:10 ; 1 Timothy 1:17 ; Jud 1:25 ).” Ellicott.
