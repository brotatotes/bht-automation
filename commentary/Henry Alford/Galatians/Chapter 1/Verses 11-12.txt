	</p>
<p><strong> γν</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> γάρ</strong> ] The γάρ seems to have been corrected to δέ , as not applying immediately to the foregoing, or perhaps in reminiscence of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:1">1 Corinthians 15:1</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:1">2 Corinthians 8:1</span> . It refers back to <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:8-9">Galatians 1:8-9</span> . On <strong> γνωρ</strong> <strong> .</strong> , see note, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:1">1 Corinthians 15:1</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> κατὰ ἄνθρωπον</strong> ] <strong> according to man</strong> , as E. V. (see reff.): i.e. measured by merely human rules and considerations, as it would be were it of human origin: so βελτίονος ἢ κατ ʼ ἄνθρωπον νομοθέτου , Xen. Mem. iv. 4. 24, κατά cannot itself express the <em> origin</em> (as Aug., a-Lapide, Est., al.), though it is included by implication: see note <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:4">Galatians 1:4</span> , on κατὰ τὸ θέλημα .</p>
