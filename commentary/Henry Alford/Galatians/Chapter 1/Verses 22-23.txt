<p><strong> 22, 23</strong> .] ‘So far was I from being a disciple of the Apostles, or tarrying in their company, that the churches of Judæa, where they principally laboured, did not even know me by sight.’
	</p>
<p><strong> τῷ προσώπῳ</strong> , the referential, or adverbial dative: Donalds., Gramm. § 457.
	</p>
<p><strong> τῆς Ἰουδαίας</strong> excludes Jerusalem, where he <em> was known</em> . Jowett doubts this: but it seems to be required by <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:26-29">Acts 9:26-29</span> . Chrys. seems to mistake the Apostle’s purpose, when he says, ἵνα μάθη ̣ ς , ὅτι τοσοῦτον ἀπεῖχε τοῦ κηρύξαι αὐτοῖς περιτομήν , ὅτι οὐδὲ ἀπὸ ὄψεως γνώριμος ἦν αὐτοῖς : and Olshausen, in supposing him to be refuting the idea that he had learned the Gospel from other Christians in Palestine.</p>
