<b> 10.] which very thing I was also forward to do,</b>—viz., then and always: it was my habit. He uses the singular, because the plural could not correctly be predicated of the whole time to which the verb refers: for he parted from Barnabas shortly after the council in Acts xv. The proofs of this<i> forwardness</i> on his part may be found, Rom. xv. 15–27; 1 Cor. xvi. 1–4; 2 Cor. viii. ix.; Acts xxiv. 17: which, though they probably happened after the date of our Epistle, yet shew the bent of his habitual wishes on this point.
