<b> 18.]</b> See Rom. iv. 14.<b> For if the inheritance</b> (the general term for all the blessings promised to Abraham, as summed up in his Seed who was to inherit the land,—in other words, for the Kingdom of Christ: see 1 Cor. vi. 9, 10)<b> is of the law</b> (i. e. by virtue of the law, having as its ground the covenant of the law)<b>, it is no more</b> (not of time, but logical—the ‘negative’<i> follows</i> on the hypothesis)<b> of</b> (by virtue of)<b> promise: but</b> (the ‘but’ of a demonstration, appealing to a well-known fact)<b> to Abraham by promise hath God granted [it]</b> (and therefore it is not of the Law).
