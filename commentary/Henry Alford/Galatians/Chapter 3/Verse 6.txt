<b> 6.]</b> The reply to the foregoing question is understood: it is<b> by the hearing of faith</b>. And then enters the thought of God’s<i> working</i> as following upon Abraham’s faith. The fact of justification being now introduced, whereas before the<i> supplying the Spirit</i> was the matter enquired of, is no real departure from the subject, for both these belong to the “<i>beginning</i>” of ver. 3,—are concomitant and inseparable. On the verse, see note, Rom. iv. 3.
