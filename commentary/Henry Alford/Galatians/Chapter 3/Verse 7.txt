<b> 7.]</b> The verb is better taken indicatively, than imperatively. It is no objection to the indicative, that such knowledge could not well be predicated of the Galatians: it is not so predicated, but is here set before them as a thing which they ought to be acquainted with—<b>from this then you know,</b> as much as to say, “it is evident to all.” The imperative seems to me to lose the fine edge of the Apostle’s argumentative irony.
