<b> 13.] because of an infirmity of my flesh:</b> or,—<b>on account of bodily weakness</b>: all other renderings (e. g. ‘<i>in weakmess,</i> as A.V.) are ungrammatical, or irrelevant. The meaning is, that it was<i> on account of an illness</i> that he first preached in Galatia: i.e. that he was for that reason detained there, and preached, which otherwise he would not have done. On this, see Introduction,§ii. 3: the fact itself, I cannot help thinking, is plainly asserted here.
