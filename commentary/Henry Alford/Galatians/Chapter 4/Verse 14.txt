<b> 14.]</b> The<b> temptation</b> seems to have been the “thorn in the flesh” of 2 Cor. xii. 1 ff., whatever that was: perhaps something eonnected with his<i> sight</i>, or some nervous infirmity: see below, and notes on Acts xiii. 9; xxiii. 1. It was<i> their</i> temptation, because it tempted them to disparage and reject his preaching. The expression, which has been altered into “<i>my</i> temptation,” is one of those exquisite and delicate touches, which belong so peculiarly to St. Paul’s mind.<b></b>
