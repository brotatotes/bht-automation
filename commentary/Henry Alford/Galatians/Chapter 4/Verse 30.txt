<b> 30.] Nevertheless:</b> notwithstanding the fact of the persecution, just mentioned. The quotation is adapted from the Septuagint, where<i> my son Isaac</i> (as in our English text) stands for “<i>the son of the freewoman.</i>” We need hardly have recourse to the fact that God confirmed Sarah’s words, in order to prove this to be<i> Scripture</i>: the Apostle is allegorizing the whole history, and thus every part of it assumes a significance in the allegory.
