<b> 31.]</b> I am inclined to think, against Meyer, and others, that this verse is, as commonly taken, the conclusion from what has gone before: and that the<b> wherefore</b> is bound on to the word<i> inherit</i> preceding. For that we are<i> heirs</i>, is an acknowledged fact, established before, ch. iii. 29; ver. 7. And if we are, we are not the children of the handmaid, of whom it was said<i> that they should not inherit,</i> but of the freewoman, of whose son the same words asserted that he should inherit.
