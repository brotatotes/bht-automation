<p><strong> 1</strong> .] It is almost impossible to determine satisfactorily the reading (see var. readd.). In the fourth Edition I adopted that in the text, as being best attested by the most ancient authorities. <strong> With liberty did Christ make you free</strong> (i.e. ἐλεύθεροι is your rightful name and ought to be your estimation of yourselves, seeing that ἐλευθερία is your inheritance by virtue of Christ’s redemption of you).

<p><strong> Stand fast, therefore</strong> (reff. στήκω is unknown in classical Greek), <strong> and be not again</strong> (see note on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:9">Galatians 4:9</span> ; in fact, the whole world was under the law in the sense of its being God’s only revelation to them) <strong> involved</strong> (reff.) <strong> in the yoke of bondage</strong> (better than ‘ <em> a</em> yoke;’ an anarthrous noun or personal pronoun following another noun in the genitive often deprives that other noun of its article: e.g., τίς ἔγνω νοῦν κυρίου ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:16">1 Corinthians 2:16</span> ; see numerous instances in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="so+5:1">Song of Solomon 5:1</span> . Cf. Winer, § 19. 2, most of whose examples however are after prepositions. [See also Moulton, p. 155, note 6.] Wetst. quotes from Soph. Aj. 944, πρὸς οἷα δουλείας ζυγὰ χωροῦμεν ).</p>
