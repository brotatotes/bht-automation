<b> 11.]</b> The connexion appears to be this: the Apostle had apparently been charged with being a favourer of circumcision in other churches; as shewn e.g. by his having circumcised Timothy. After the preceding sharp denunciation of “<i>him that troubleth you</i>,” and “<i>whosoever he be</i>,” it is open to the adversaries to say, that Paul himself was one of their<i> troublers</i>, by his inconsistency. In the abruptness then of his fervid thoughts he breaks out in this self-defence.
