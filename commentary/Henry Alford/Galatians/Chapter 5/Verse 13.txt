<p><strong> 13</strong> CH. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:5">Galatians 6:5</span> .] THE THIRD or HORTATORY PORTION OF THE EPISTLE, not however separated from the former, but united to it by the current of thought: and, <strong> 13 15</strong> .] <em> Though free, be one another’s servants in love</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> γάρ</strong> gives the reason why the Apostle was so fervent in his denunciation of these disturbers; because they were striking at the very root of their Christian calling, which was <strong> for</strong> (on condition of; hardly, for the purpose of; see reff.) <strong> freedom. Only (make not)</strong> (so μή with the verb omitted and an accusative in μή ʼ μοιγε μύθους , Aristoph. Vesp. 1179; μὴ τριβὰς ἔτι , Soph. Antig. 577; μή μοι μυρίους μηδὲ δισμυρίους ξένους , Demosth. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="php+1:0">Philippians 1:0</span> . § 19. See more examples in Hartung, ii. 153) <strong> your liberty into</strong> (or, use it not for) <strong> an occasion</strong> (opportunity) <strong> for the flesh</strong> (for giving way to carnal passions), <strong> but by means of</strong> (your) <strong> love, be in bondage</strong> (opposition to ἐλευθερία ) <strong> to one another</strong> . Chrys. remarks, πάλιν ἐνταῦθα αἰνίττεται , ὅτι φιλονεικία κ . στάσις κ . φιλαρχία κ . ἀπόνοια ταύτης αἰτία τῆς πλάνης αὐτοῖς ἐγένετο · <strong> ἡ γὰρ τῶν αἱρέσεων μήτηρ ἡ τῆς φιλαρχίας ἐστὶν ἐπιθυμία</strong> .</p>
