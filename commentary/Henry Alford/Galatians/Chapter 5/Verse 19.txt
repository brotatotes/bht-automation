<b> 19.] manifest</b> (emphatic)<b>, plain to all,</b> not needing, like the more hidden fruits of the<i> Spirit</i>, to be educed and specified: and therefore more clearly amenable to law, which takes cognizance of<i> things open and manifest.</i> The word rendered<i> wantonness</i> is defined by the Greek writers as meaning ‘readiness for any pleasurable indulgence.’ It does not necessarily include “<i>lasciviousness</i>.”
