<b> 3.] Yea,</b> or<b> moreover</b>, introduces an addition, and a slight contrast— ‘not only will Christ not profit.... but....’<br><br><b> to every man who receives circumcision,</b>— ‘submits to be circumcised.’—The emphasis is on<b> every man,</b> substantiating, and carrying further, the last verse.<br><br><b> the whole</b> has the stress. The circumcised man became a ‘proselyte of righteousness,’ and bound to keep the whole law. “This true and serious consequence of circumcision the false Apostles had probably at least dissembled.” Meyer.
