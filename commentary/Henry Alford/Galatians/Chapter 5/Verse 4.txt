<p><strong> 4</strong> .] Explains and establishes still further the assertion of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:2">Galatians 5:2</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> Ye were annihilated from Christ</strong> (literally: the construction is a pregnant one, ‘ye were cut off from Christ, and thus made void:’ see ref. 2 Cor. ‘ <em> were</em> ,’ viz. at the time when you began your course of ἐν νόμῳ δικ .), <strong> ye who are being justified</strong> (‘endeavouring to be justified,’ ‘seeking justification:’ such is the force of the subjective <em> present</em> . So Thl. ὡς ὑπολαμβάνετε ) <strong> in</strong> (not ‘by:’ it is the element in which, as in the expression ἐν κυρίῳ ) <strong> the law, ye fell from</strong> (reff.: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:8">1 Corinthians 13:8</span> , note. Wetst. quotes from Plut., Agis and Cleom. p. 796, τῶν πλείστων ἐξέπεσεν ἡ Σπάρτη καλῶν : Gracch. p. 834, ἐκπεσεῖν κ . στέρεσθαι τῆς πρὸς τὸν δῆμον εὐνοίας . ‘So Plato, Rep. vi. 496, ἐκπεσεῖν φιλοσοφίας : Polyb. xii. 14. 7, ἐκπίπτειν τοῦ καθήκοντος ,’ Ellic.) grace.</p>
