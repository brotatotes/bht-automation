	 ἐλπίδα δικαιοσύνης ] Is this genitive  objective , the hope of righteousness, i.e. the hope whose object is perfect righteousness, or  subjective , the hope of righteousness, i.e. the hope which the righteous entertain viz. that of eternal life? Certainly I think the  former : for this reason, that ἐλπίδα has the emphasis, and ἐλπίδα δικ . ἀπεκδεχ . answers to δικαιοῦσθε above ‘  Ye think ye  have your righteousness in the law:  we , on the contrary, anxiously wait for the hope of righteousness (full and perfect).’ The phrase ἀπεκδέχεσθαι ἐλπίδα may be paralleled, Acts 24:15 ; Titus 2:13 ; Eur. Alcest. 130, τίν ʼ ἔτι βίου ἐλπίδα προσδέχωμαι ; Polyb. viii. 21. 7, ταῖς προσδοκωμέναις ἐλπίσιν .
