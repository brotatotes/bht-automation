<p><strong> 10</strong> .] <strong> ἄρα οὖν</strong> , <strong> so then</strong> : “the proper meaning of ἄρα , ‘ <em> rebus ita comparatis</em> ,’ is here distinctly apparent: its weaker ratiocinative force being supported by the collective power of οὖν .” Ellic.
	</p>
<p><strong> ὡς</strong> ] not ‘ <em> while</em> ’ (Olsh., al.), nor, ‘ <em> according as</em> ,’ i.e. ‘ <em> quotiescunque</em> ,’ nor, ‘since,’ causal (De W., Winer, al.), but <strong> as</strong> , i.e. <strong> in proportion as</strong> : let our beneficence be in proportion to our καιρός let the seed-time have its καιρὸς ἴδιος , as well as the harvest, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:9">Galatians 6:9</span> . Thus καιρός is a common term between the two verses.
	</p>
<p><strong> τὸ ἀγ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] <strong> the good thing</strong> : as we say, ‘he did the right thing:’ that which is (in each case) good.
	</p>

V. In <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="isa+58:7">Isaiah 58:7</span> ‘thy fellow-men’ are called οἱ οἰκεῖοι τοῦ σπέρματός σου : so also in the examples from the later classics in Wetst., οἰκεῖοι φιλοσοφίας , γεωγραφίας , ὀλιγαρχίας , τυραννίδος , τρυφῆς .</p>
