<b> 12.]</b> As my Epistle, so my practice: I have no desire to make a fair show outwardly: my<i> letters</i> are not<i> fair of show:</i> and I have no sympathy with these people who wish to make a fair show in the flesh. The term imports not<i> merely ‘in the flesh,’</i> but<b> in outward things,</b> which belong to man’s natural state: see ch. v. 19.<br><br><b> constrain you] are compelling you:—go about to compel you.</b>
