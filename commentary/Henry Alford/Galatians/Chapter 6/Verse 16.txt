<b> 16.] And as many</b> (reference to the “<i>as many</i>” of ver. 12)<b> as shall walk by this rule</b> (of ver. 15. The word means a ‘straight rule,’ to detect crookedness: hence a<i> rule of life</i>),<b> peace be</b> (not<i> ‘is:’</i> it is the apostolic blessing, so common in the<i> beginnings</i> of his Epistles: see also Eph. vi. 23)<b> upon them</b> (come on them from God)<b>, and</b> (and indeed)<b> upon the Israel of God</b> (the subject of the whole Epistle seems to have given rise to this expression. Not the Israel after the flesh, among whom these teachers wish to enrol you, are blessed: but the ISRAEL OF GOD, described ch. iii. 28, 29. Jowett compares, though not exactly parallel, yet for a similar apparent though not actual distinction, 1 Cor. x. 32).
