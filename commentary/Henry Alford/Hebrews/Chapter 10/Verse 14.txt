<p><strong> 14</strong> .] And He need not renew his sacrifice: <strong> For by one offering</strong> (we might read also μία γὰρ προσφορά , nominative: and Bengel prefers this, from the fact that in Heb 10:11 the sacrifices are the subject, αἵτινες οὐδέποτε δύνανται κ . τ . λ . But here more probably Christ is the subject throughout, and therefore the dative is better: there being no relative to connect with θυσίαν , as there) <strong> He hath perfected for ever them who are being sanctified</strong> (“The Writer says not τοὺς τελειωμένους , but τοὺς ἁγιαζομένους . Sanctification, i. e. the imputed and implanted purification from sins (for both these are alike contained in the idea), is the way whereby the objective perfection already provided in the self-sacrifice of Christ gradually renders itself subjective in men.” Delitzsch).</p>
