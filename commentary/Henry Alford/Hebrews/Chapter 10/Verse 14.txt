<b> 14.]</b> And He need not renew his sacrifice:<b> For by one offering He hath perfected for ever them who are being sanctified</b> ( “the Writer says not<i> ‘them that are being perfected,’</i> but<i> ‘them that are being</i><b> sanctified</b>.’ Sanctification, i.e. the imputed and implanted purification from sins [for both these are alike contained in the idea], is the way whereby the objective perfection already provided in the self-sacrifice of Christ gradually renders itself subjective in men.” Delitzsch).
