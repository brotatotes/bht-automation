<b> 32.</b>]<b> But</b> (in contrast to these fearful things which have been spoken of)<b> call ever to mind</b> (call over in your minds, one by one)<b> the former days, in which when</b> (<b>first</b>)<b> enlightened</b> (see on this, note, ch. vi. 4),<b> ye underwent</b> (with fortitude: which though not implied in the word, signifying mere endurance, yet is in the context)<b> much contest of sufferings</b> (the genitive may be either subjective, implying that your contest<i> consisted of</i> sufferings: or objective, that it was<i> waged with</i> sufferings, as the foe to be contended against: the former perhaps is the more probable from what follows);
