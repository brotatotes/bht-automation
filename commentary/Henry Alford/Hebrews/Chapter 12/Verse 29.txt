<b> 29.</b>]<b> For</b><b> moreover our God is a consuming fire</b> (the words are taken from Deut. iv. 24. Compare also Deut. ix. 3. And thus the fact that God’s anger continues to burn now, as then, against those who reject his Kingdom, is brought in; and in the background lie all those gracious dealings, by which the fire of God’s presence and purity becomes to His people, while it consumes their vanity and siu and earthly state, the fire of purity and light and love for their enduring citizenship of His kingdom).
