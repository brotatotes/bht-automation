<b> 18.</b>]<b> Pray for us</b> (here, as elsewhere, it is probably a mistake to suppose that the first person plural indicates the Writer alone. As Delitzsch observes, the passage from the<i> rulers</i> to the Writer individually would be harsh. And when Bleek finds in ver. 19 a proof that the Writer only is meant, he misses the point, that this<b> us</b>, including the Writer and his companions, is in fact a transition note between ver. 17 and ver. 19, See Eph. vi. 19; Rom. xv. 30; 2 Cor. i. 11):<b> for we are persuaded that we have a good conscience, desiring in all things to behave ourselves with seemliness</b> (i. e. to live without giving offence or scandal. This appears to point at some offence of the same kind as we know to have been taken at the life and teaching of St. Paul with reference to the law and Jewish customs).
