<b> 8.]</b> Confirmation of the above, as against an exception that might be taken, that notwithstanding the exclusion of many by unbelief, those who entered the promised land with Joshua<i> did enter</i> into that rest of God.<b> For if Joshua</b> (it does not appear that any parallel between the typical and the great final Deliverer is intended: but it could hardly fail to be suggested to the readers. Our translators, in retaining “Jesus” (the Greek form of<i> Joshua</i>) here, have introduced into the mind of the ordinary English reader utter confusion. It was done in violation of their instructions, which prescribed that all proper names should be rendered as they were commonly used)<b> had given them rest</b> (led them into this rest of which we are treating),<b> He</b> (God: the subject of<i> “limiteth”</i> and<i> “saying”</i> above)<b> would not speak</b> (not<i> ‘have spoken,’</i> as A.V.)<b> after this of another day.</b>
