<b> CHAP. VI. 1.] Therefore</b> (on the connexion, see below)<b> leaving</b> (as behind, and done with; in order to go on to another thing: not,<i> forgetting</i>, any more than the foundation is forgotten when we rear the house upon it)<b> the word of the beginning of Christ</b> (compare above, ch, vv. 12: that word, or discourse, which has respect to the fundamental and elementary things mentioned below),<b> let us press on to maturity</b> (a question of some difficulty has divided the Commentators here: whether this sentence be meant as expressing the resolution of the Writer, as we say, “let us now proceed” to this or that,—or as conveying an exhortation to the readers.
