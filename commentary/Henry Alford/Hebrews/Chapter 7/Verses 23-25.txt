<p><strong> 23 25</strong> .] <em> Further proof still of the superiority of Christ’s priesthood, in that the Levitical priests were continually removed by death: Christ is undying and abiding</em> . This point was slightly touched before in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:8">Hebrews 7:8</span> , and again in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:16">Hebrews 7:16</span> f.: in the first place, it was to shew the abiding nature of the superiority of the priesthood its endurance in Melchisedek, and in Christ, Melchisedek’s antitype, as contrasted with dying men who here receive tithes. In the second, it was to bring out the difference between the ordinances which constituted the two priesthoods: the one, the law of a carnal commandment, the other, the power of an endless life. Here, the <em> personal</em> contrast is dwelt on: the <em> many</em> , which change: the ONE, who abides.</p>
