<b> 18.] Whence</b> (because death must precede the validity of a testament)<b> neither has the first</b> (testament)<b> been inaugurated</b> (perfect, inasmuch as the rites, &c., belonging to it were still subsisting. Notice that the reference is,<i> here</i>, simply to the first inauguration of the law when it was put forth as new: not to any subsequent renewal of sacrifices by death: this is presently alluded to, vv. 21 ff.)<b> without</b> (apart from, free from the exhibition of)<b> blood.</b>
