<b> 22.]</b> The<i> swiftness to hear,</i> and<i> the reception of the truth</i> ave qualified, at the same time that they are enforced, by a caution.<b> But be ye doers of the word</b> (viz. of the<i> implanted word</i>, the<i> word of truth</i>. Observe, not only “<i>do,</i>” but<b> be doers</b>: the substantive means more than the verb; it carries an enduring, a sort of official force with it: ‘let this be your occupation’),<b> not hearers only</b>,<b> deceiving yourselves</b> (the “hearer only” does this, when he infers that the mere sound of the word received in his outward ear will suffice for him).
