We might have had all aorr.: but seeing that the  departing begins a permanent state of absence from the mirror, that is chosen to be designated by a perfect. The  forgetting is also a permanent state; but the Apostle rather chooses in this case to bring out the act itself, as one immediately ( εὐθύς ) and suddenly taking place. For similar conjunctions of perfects with aorr., cf. Luke 4:18 ; Mark 15:44 ; Heb 2:14 and note: 1 Corinthians 15:4 ; and Winer, § 40. 4),  and immediately forgot of what appearance he was (viz. in the mirror.
