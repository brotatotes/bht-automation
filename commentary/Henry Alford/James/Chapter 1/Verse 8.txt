<b> 8.] He is a man with two minds, unstable in all his ways</b> (such is the best way of taking this sentence, making it all predicate and all to apply to<b> that man</b> as its subject. The common way, to take “<i>a double-minded man</i>” as a new subject, as A.V. ‘a double-minded man is unstable,’ has this against it, that it makes the very unusual word “<b>doubled-souled,</b>” found here and in ch. iv. 8 for the first time in Greek literature, to be a mere usual epithet and word of passage).
