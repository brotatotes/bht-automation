<p><strong> 24</strong> .] <em> General inference from the example of Abraham</em> . <strong> Ye see</strong> (not imperative, nor interrogative) <strong> that by</strong> (from, out of, as a source) <strong> works a man is justified</strong> (accounted righteous before God, as above: not, as Calvin, “Fructibus cognoscitur et approbatur ejus justitia”), <strong> and not by</strong> (from) <strong> faith only</strong> (notice <strong> μόνον</strong> : St. James never says that a man is not justified by faith, provided that faith include in it the condition of obedience: but by faith μόνον , χωρὶς ἔργων , is no man justified. <strong> μόνον</strong> must be joined with πίστεως , not with οὐκ , as Theile, “Appositionis lege explenda est oratio: non solum fide, sed etiam operibus.… nempe cum fide conjungendis:” see similar instances of adverbs joined to substantives in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:31">1 Corinthians 12:31</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:23">2 Corinthians 11:23</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:23">Galatians 1:23</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="php+1:26">Philippians 1:26</span> ; and cf. Winer, § 54. 2, <em> b</em> ).</p>
