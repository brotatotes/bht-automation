<p><strong> 16</strong> .] <strong> But</strong> (contrast to the spirit of resignation to the divine will just recommended) <strong> now</strong> (as things now are, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:11">1 Corinthians 5:11</span> ; 1Co 14:6 ) <strong> ye boast in</strong> (not, as in ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:9">James 1:9</span> , “make your boast in:” the <strong> ἐν</strong> indicates the state, as in ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:18">James 3:18</span> , and Jam 4:3 especially. The <strong> ἀλαζονεία</strong> is the source, but not the material of the boasting) <strong> your vain-gloriousnesses</strong> (see note on ref. 1 John. Here <strong> ἀλαζονεία</strong> is the self-deceived and groundless confidence in the stability of life and health on which the worldly pride themselves. On this, as on its foundation, your boastful speeches, σήμερον καὶ αὔριον κ . τ . λ ., are built): <strong> all such boasting</strong> (all boasting so made and so grounded) <strong> is wicked</strong> .</p>
