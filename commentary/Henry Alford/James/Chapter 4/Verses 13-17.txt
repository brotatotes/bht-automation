<p><strong> 13 17</strong> .] <em> Against ungodly and presumptuous confidence in our worldly plans for the future</em> . This again falls into the previous context, where we are warned against hearts divided between God and the world. But, as has been rightly remarked as early as Bed [13] , and by many since, e. g. Œc., Semler, al., St. James, though carrying on the same subject, is no longer, from this place to ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:6">James 5:6</span> , addressing members of Christ’s church, but those without: the ungodly and the rich in this world. This however must be taken with just this reservation, that he addresses Christians in so far as they allow themselves to be identified with those others. This first paragraph, for example, might well serve as a warning for Christians who are in the habit of leaving God out of their thoughts and plans. That it is still <em> Jews</em> who are addressed, appears from <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:15">James 4:15</span> , and ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:4">James 5:4</span> .
	</p>
