<b> 10.] The world</b> is the created world, into which He came (ver. 9), which was made by Him (ver. 3), which nevertheless (i e. as here represented by<i> man</i>, the only creature who “<i>knows</i>”) knew, recognized Him not.<br><br><b> and</b> is as in ver. 5. The three members of the sentence form a climax;<b>—He was in the world</b> (and therefore the world should have known Him),<b> and the world was made by Him</b> (much more then should it have known Him),<b> and the world knew Him not</b>.
