<b> 20.]</b> He openly and formally confessed. This emphatic notice of his declaration seems to be introduced<i> not with any view of removing too high an estimate of John’s work and office</i>, as sometimes supposed, but rather to<i> shew the importance of his testimony</i>, which was so publicly and officially delivered,—that the Messiah was come (see ch. v. 33–35); and the way in which he depreciated himself in comparison with Him who came after him.
