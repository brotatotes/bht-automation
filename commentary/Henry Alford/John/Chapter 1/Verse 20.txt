<p><strong> 20.</strong> ] <strong> ὡμολόγησεν</strong> <strong> ,</strong> he openly and formally confessed. This emphatic notice of his declaration seems to be introduced <em> not with any view of removing too high an estimate of John’s work and office</em> , as sometimes supposed, but rather <em> to shew the importance of his testimony</em> , which was so publicly and officially delivered, that the Messiah was come (see ch. Joh 5:33-35 ); and the way in which he depreciated <em> himself</em> in comparison with Him who came after him.</p>
