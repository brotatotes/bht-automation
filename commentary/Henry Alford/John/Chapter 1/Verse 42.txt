<b> 42.]</b> This is evidently the<i> first bestowal of the new name</i> on Simon: and it is done from our Lord’s prophetic knowledge of his future character; see note on Matt. xvi. 18.<i> Kaphā</i> in Aramaic,<i> Kaph</i> in Hebrew, a<b> stone</b>. But the rendering of<i> Petros</i> in this verse should be as in margin,<b> Peter</b>, not as in A.V.,<i> a stone</i>. The Greek name<i> Peter</i> became the prevalent one in the apostolic Church very soon: St. Paul uses both names indiscriminately.
