<b> 12.]</b> The imagery is here again somewhat changed. The false shepherds are here compared to hirelings, i.e. those who serve<i> merely</i> for gain; the<i> hireling</i> who fulfils the character implied by the word. The idea is brought in by the words “<i>layeth down his life for the sheep,</i>” which introduce the thought of a time of danger, when the true and false shepherds are distinguished.<b></b>
