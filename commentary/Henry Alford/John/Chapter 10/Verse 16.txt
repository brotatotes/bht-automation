<p><strong> 16.</strong> ] The <strong> ἄλλα πρόβ</strong> <strong> .</strong> are the <em> Gentiles;</em> not the dispersion of the Jews, who were already in God’s αὐλή . By these wonderful words, as by those in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:10">Acts 18:10</span> , and by the conclusion of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:0">Matthew 25:0</span> . (see notes there), our Lord shews that, dark and miserable as the Gentile world was, <em> He had sheep even there</em> . Observe they are not <em> in other folds</em> , but scattered: see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:52">John 11:52</span> . Cf. also <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:14">Ephesians 2:14</span> ff.
	</p>
<p><strong> δεῖ με ἀγ</strong> <strong> .…</strong> ] i.e. in the purpose and covenant of the Father. The Lord speaks of <em> His</em> bringing them, and their hearing <em> His</em> voice: meaning that His servants in His name and by His power would accomplish this work. Admirably illustrative of the converse method of speaking which He employs <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:40">Matthew 25:40</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:45">Matthew 25:45</span> . The <strong> μία ποίμνη</strong> is remarkable <em> not</em> <strong> μία αὐλή</strong> <strong> ,</strong> <em> as characteristically, but erroneously rendered in E. V.:</em> not ONE FOLD, but ONE FLOCK; no one exclusive enclosure of an outward church, but one flock, all knowing the one Shepherd and known of Him. On <strong> εἷς ποιμήν</strong> compare <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:20">Hebrews 13:20</span> .</p>
