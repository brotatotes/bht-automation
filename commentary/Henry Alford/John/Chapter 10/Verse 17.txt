<b> 17.]</b><i> The speaking in allegories</i> is now over, and He speaks<i> plainly,</i>—<b>My Father.</b> In this wonderful verse lies the mystery of the love of the Father for the Son;—<i>because</i> the Son has condescended to the work of humiliation, and to earn the crown through the cross (see Phil. ii. 8, 9). The<b> that</b> here is strictly of the ultimate purpose,<b> in order that.</b> ‘Without this purpose in view,’ says Stier, ‘the Death of Christ would neither be lawful nor possible.’
