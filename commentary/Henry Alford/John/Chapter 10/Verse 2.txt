<b> 22]</b> This feast had become usual since the time when Judas Maccabæus purified the temple from the profanations of Antiochus. It was held on Chisleu (December) 25, and seven following days: see 1 Mace. iv. 41–59: 2 Mace. x. 1–8.<br><br><b> it was winter]</b> The notice is inserted to explain to Gentile readers the reason of our Lord’s walking in Solomon’s portico. This latter was on the east side of the temple, called also by Josephus, “<i>the Eastern porch.</i>” He says that it was an original work of Solomon, which had remained from the former temple.
