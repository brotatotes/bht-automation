<b> 34.]</b> The word<b> law</b> here is in its widest acceptation,—the whole Old Testament, as ch. xii. 34; xv. 25. The Psalm (lxxxii.) is directed against the injustice and tyranny of judges (not, the<i> Gentile rulers</i> of the world, nor, the<i> angels</i>) in Israel. And in the Psalm reference is made by “<i>I have said</i>” to previous places of Scripture where judges are so called, viz. Exod. xxi. 6; xxii. 9, 28.
