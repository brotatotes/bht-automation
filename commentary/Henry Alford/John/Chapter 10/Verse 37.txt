Having put the charge of<i> blasphemy</i> aside, our Lord again has recourse to the testimony of<i> His works,</i> at which He hinted ver. 32; and here, to their<i> character</i>, as admitted by them in ver. 33. ‘If they bear not the character of the Father, believe Me not: but if they do (which even yourselves admit), though ye may hate and disbelieve Me, recognize the unquestionable testimony of the works;—that ye may be led on to the higher faith of the unity of Myself and the Father.’
