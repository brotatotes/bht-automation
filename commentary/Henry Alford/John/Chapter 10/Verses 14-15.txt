<p><strong> 14, 15.</strong> ] The knowledge of His sheep here spoken of is more than the mere <em> knowing by name:</em> it is a knowledge corresponding to the Father’s knowledge of Him; i.e. entire, perfect, all-comprehensive: and <em> their</em> knowledge of <em> Him</em> corresponds to His of the Father, i.e. is intimate, direct, and personal: both being bound together by holy and inseparable Love. Beware of rendering [the former clause of] Joh 10:15 as in E. V. as an independent sentence, ‘ <em> As my Father knoweth me, even so know I the Father:</em> ’ it is merely the sequel to <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:14">John 10:14</span> , and should stand, <strong> as the Father knoweth me and I know the Father.
	</strong></p>
<p><strong> ὑπὲρ τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> προβ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] <strong> for</strong> those <strong> my sheep</strong> not, for <em> all;</em> that, <em> however true</em> , is not <em> the point</em> brought out <em> here:</em> the Lord lays down His life strictly and properly, and in the depths of the Divine counsel, <em> for those who are His sheep</em> .</p>
