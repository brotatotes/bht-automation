But]</b> This conjunction here is not merely a word of passage to another subject, but expresses a contrast to the sojourn in Peræa, and thus conveys the reason why our Lord’s retirement (see ch. x. 40) was broken in upon.<br><br> Bethany is designated as<i> ‘the village of Martha and Mary,’</i> to distinguish it from that Bethany be-yond Jordan, which has just been alluded to (not named, perhaps to avoid the con-fusion), ch. x. 40. Mary and Martha are mentioned as already well known from the current apostolic teaching (sec Introduction, chap. v.§ii, 11).
