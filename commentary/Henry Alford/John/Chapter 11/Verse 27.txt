<b> 27.]</b> Her confession, though embracing the great central point of the truth in the last verse, does not enter fully into it. Nor does she (ver. 40) seem to have adequately apprehended its meaning. “That He spoke great things about Himself, she knew: but in what sense He spoke them, she did not know: and therefore when asked one thing, she replies another.” Euthymius.<br><br><b> I</b>—emphatic: I for my part: and the word<b> believe</b> is in the original in the perfect tense, “<i>have believed and continue to believe:</i>” i.e. ‘<i>have convinced myself, and firmly believe</i>.’
