<b> 39.]</b> The corpse had not been embalmed, but merely ‘wrapped in linen clothes with spices, as the manner of the Jews is to bury,’—see ch. xix. 40, and ver. 44 below. The expression,<b> the sister of him that was dead,</b> as Meyer remarks, notes the natural horror of the<i> sister’s heart</i> at what was about to be done.
