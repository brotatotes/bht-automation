The counsel is given in subtilty, and was intended by Caiaphas in the sense of political expediency only. But it pleased God to make him, as High Priest, the special though involuntary organ of the Holy Spirit, and thus to utter by him a prophecy of the death of Christ and its effects. That this is the only sense to be given, appears from the consideration that the whole of verses 51, 52 cannot for a moment be supposed to have been in the mind of Caiaphas; and to divide it, and suppose the latter part to be the addition of the Evangelist, is quite unjustifiable.<br><br><b> high priest that year]</b> repeated again, ch. xviii. 13. He was High Priest during the whole Procuratorship of Pontius Pilate, eleven years. In the words<b> that year,</b> there is no intimation conveyed that the High Priesthood was changed every year, which it<i> was not:</i> but we must understand the words as directing attention to ‘<i>that (remarkable) year,</i>’ without any reference to time past or to come. THAT YEAR<i> of great events</i> had Caiaphas as its High Priest. See on ver. 57.<br><br><b> Ye know nothing at all]</b> Probably various methods of action had been suggested.<br><br> Observe<b> people</b> here, the usual term for the<i> chosen people,</i> and then<i> nation,</i> when it is regarded as a nation among the nations: compare also ver. 52.
