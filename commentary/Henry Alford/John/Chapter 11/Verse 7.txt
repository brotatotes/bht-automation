<p><strong> 7.</strong> ] If the <strong> οὖν</strong> in Joh 11:6 referred to this verse, the connexion must have been made by καὶ μετὰ τ .: the <strong> ἔπειτα</strong> cuts off all connexion ( Gal 1:18 ), and throws back the <strong> οὖν</strong> as explained above.
	</p>
<p>The question, why our Lord did not go at once on receiving the message, is not to be answered by any secondary reasons, such as the trial of the faith of those concerned, or the pressing nature of His own ministry in Peræa, but by referring back to <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:4">John 11:4</span> , because, for the glory of God, He would have the miracle happen as it did and no otherwise. Compare Meyer.</p>
