<p><strong> 11.</strong> ] <strong> ὑπῆγον</strong> <strong> , went away</strong> (to Bethany); there is something in the <strong> ὑπ</strong> <strong> -</strong> which almost always implies <em> away, out from under</em> , the persons or the place in the narrative. And so here, the ἀρχιερεῖς being the main subject of the sentence, the word gets the sense of ‘ <em> fell away:</em> ’ scil. from under their hand or power.</p>
