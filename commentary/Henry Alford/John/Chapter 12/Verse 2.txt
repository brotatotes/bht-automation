<p><strong> 2. </strong> <strong> ἐποίησαν</strong> ] It is not said <em> who</em> . It was (Matt., Mark) in the house of <em> Simon the leper</em> . From Lazarus being there, and Martha serving, he may have been a near relative of theirs. See notes on Matt.
	</p>
<p>Lazarus is mentioned throughout the incident, ns forming an clement in the unfolding of the hatred of the Jews which issued in the Lord’s death: notice the climax, from mere connecting mention in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:1">John 12:1</span> , then nearer connexion in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:2">John 12:2</span> , to his being the cause of the Jews flocking to Bethany in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:9">John 12:9</span> , and the joint object with Jesus of the enmity of the chief priests, in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:10">John 12:10</span> .</p>
