<b> 2. they made him a supper]</b> It is not said<i> who.</i> It was, from Matthew and Mark, in the house of<i> Simon the leper.</i> From Lazarus being there, and Martha serving, he may have been a near relative of theirs. See notes on Matthew.<br><br> Lazarus is mentioned throughout the incident, as forming an element in the unfolding of the hatred of the Jews which issued in the Lord’s death: notice the climax, from mere connecting mention in ver. 1, then nearer connexion in ver. 2,—to his being the cause of the Jews flocking to Bethany in ver. 9,—and the joint object with Jesus of the enmity of the chief priests, in ver. 10.
