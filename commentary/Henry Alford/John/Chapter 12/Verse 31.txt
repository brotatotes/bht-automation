<p><strong> 31.</strong> ] All this is a comment on ἐλήλυθεν ἡ ὥρα , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:23">John 12:23</span> : and now a different side of the subject is taken up, and one having immediate reference to the occasion: viz. the drawing of the Gentile world to Him.
	</p>
<p><strong> νῦν</strong> ] He speaks of Himself as having actually entered the hour of His passion, and views the result as already come.
	</p>
<p><strong> κρίσις</strong> <strong> ,</strong> not (Chrys., Cyril, Aug [169] , Grot.) “the <em> deliverance</em> of this world from the devil;” nor, “decision concerning this world,” <em> who</em> is to possess it (Bengel): but (see ch. Joh 16:11 ) <strong> judgment</strong> , properly so called, the work of the Spirit who was to come, on the world, which ὅλος ἐν τῷ πονηρῷ κεῖται , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:19">1 John 5:19</span> .
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [169] <em> Augustine, Bp. of Hippo</em> , 395 430
	</p>
<p><strong> ὁ ἄρχ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> κόσ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] The שַׂר הָעוֹלָם of the Jews, Satan, the ὁ θεὸς τοῦ αἰῶνος τούτου of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:4">2 Corinthians 4:4</span> ; see also <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:2">Ephesians 2:2</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:12">Ephesians 6:12</span> . Observe it is <strong> ἐκβληθήσεται</strong> <strong> ,</strong> not ἐκβάλλεται , because the casting out ( ἔξω , ἐκ τῆς ἀρχῆς , Euthym [170] , Grot., or better perhaps, out of ὁ κόσμος οὗτος , his former place) shall be gradual, as the <em> drawing</em> in the next verse. But after the death of Christ the casting out <em> began</em> , and its first-fruits were, the coming in of the Gentiles into the Church.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [170] Euthymius Zigabenus, 1116</p>
