<b> 41. because he saw]</b> “This apocalyptic vision was the occasion of that prophecy.” Meyer.<br><br><b> his glory:</b> i.e.<i> the glory of Christ.</i> The Evangelist is giving his judgment,—having (Luke xxiv. 45) had his understanding opened to understand the Scriptures,—<i>that the passage in Isaiah is spoken of Christ.</i> And indeed, strictly considered, the glory which Isaiah saw<i> could only</i> be that of the Son, Who is the<i> brightness</i> (shining forth)<i> of the glory</i> of the Father, Whom no eye hath seen, The last clause is independent of “<i>because</i>,” and contains another assertion,<b>—and he spake concerning Him.</b>
