	 ἐβάσταζεν ] It seems hardly possible, with St. John’s use of  βαστάζειν in ch. Joh 20:15 before us, altogether to deny that the sense of  carrying off , i.e.  purloining , may be here intended. And we have examples in Josephus somewhat analogous: e.g. Antt. vii. 15. 3, where Hyrcanus the High Priest, wishing to give Antiochus Eusebes money to raise the siege, καὶ ἀλλαχόθεν οὐκ εὐπορῶν , ἀνοίξας ἕνα οἶκον τῶν ἐν τῷ Δαυίδου μνήματι , καὶ  βαστάσας τρισχίλια τάλαντα , μέρος ἔδωκεν Ἀντιόχῳ .… See also ib. ix. 4. 5; xii. 5. 4: and Polyb. i. 48. 2. And so Origen, Theophyl., alli [161] .; contra Lücke, De Wette, Tholuck, alli [162] .
