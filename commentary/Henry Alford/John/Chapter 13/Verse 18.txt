<b> 18.] I say it not</b> (viz. the “<i>if ye do them</i>”) of you<i> all:</i> for there is one who can never be<i> blessed.</i> Our Lord repeats his words, “<i>but not all,</i>” of ver. 10, and the sad recollection leads to His trouble in spirit, ver. 21.<br><br><b> I know]</b> The<b> I</b> is emphatic; and the reason of its emphasis is given in ver. 19.<br><br> Connexion: ‘It might be supposed that this treachery has come upon Me unawares; but it is not so: I (for my part) know whom I have selected (viz.<i> the whole twelve;</i> see ch. vi. 70, not only the true ones, as in ch. xv. 16, said when Judas was not present): but this has been done by the determinate counsel and foreknowledge of God, declared in the Scriptures.’<br><br> The words of the citation here are given freely, the LXX having, “<i>magnified the lifting up of the heel against me.”</i><br><br> This is another instance of the direct and unhesitating application of the words of the Psalms by our Lord to Himself.<br><br><b> his heel]</b> Bengel observes that this saying is pertinent to the<i> washing of the feet,</i> and also to the custom of<i> reclining in eating bread.</i> See on ver, 23.<b></b>
