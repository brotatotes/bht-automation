<b> 22.]</b> In Matthew and Mark they express their questioning in words. St. Luke's<i> beginning to enquire among themselves</i> would appear to imply the same. We seem called on here to decide a much-controverted question,—<i>where, in St. John’s narrative, the institution of the Lord’s Supper is to be inserted?</i> I believe certainly<i> before</i> this announcement, as in Luke: and if before it,<i> perhaps before the washing of the disciples’ feet:</i> for I see no break which would admit it between our ver. 1 and ver. 21.
