<b> 38.]</b> The question is not answered—but Peter’s boast solemnly questioned. See a somewhat similar question, ch. i. 51. There was at the same time a startling<i> inversion</i> of the subsequent facts, in this boast; to which our Lord, I think, alludes in His question,-“<i>wilt thou lay down thy life for<b> Me</b>?</i>”<br><br> The words,<b> The cock shall not crow</b>, necessarily<i> imply</i>, as it<i> was night</i>, those also which follow in Matthew and Mark, “in this night,”—and bind the whole events of this chapter to ch. xviii.
