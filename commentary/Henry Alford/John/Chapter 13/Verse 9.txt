<b> 9.]</b> The warm-hearted Peter, upon learning that<i> exclusion</i> would be the consequence of not being washed, can hardly have enough of a cleansing so precious. There surely is implied in this answer an incipient apprehension of the meaning of our Lord’s words. The expression,<b> if I wash thee not,</b> has awakened in him, as the Lord’s presence did, Luke v. 8, a feeling of his own want of cleansing, his entire pollution.
