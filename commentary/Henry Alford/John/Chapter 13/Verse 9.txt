<p><strong> 9.</strong> ] The warm-hearted Peter, on learning that <em> exclusion</em> would be the consequence of not being washed, can hardly have enough of a cleansing so precious. There surely is implied in this answer an incipient apprehension of the meaning of our Lord’s words. The <strong> ἐὰν μὴ νίψω σε</strong> has awakened in him, as the Lord’s presence did, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:8">Luke 5:8</span> , a feeling of his own want of cleansing, his entire pollution. This sense (Stier, Bengel, Baumgarten-Crusius) is denied by Lücke and Olsh.</p>
