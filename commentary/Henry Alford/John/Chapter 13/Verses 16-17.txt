<p><strong> 16, 17.</strong> ] The proverbial expression <strong> οὐκ ἔστιν δ</strong> <strong> .…</strong> is used here in a different sense from ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:20">John 15:20</span> . Here it is, ‘if the Master thus humble Himself, much more should His servants and messengers:’ see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:24">Matthew 10:24</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:40">Luke 6:40</span> ; and on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:17">John 13:17</span> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:47-48">Luke 12:47-48</span> . The <em> mere recognition</em> of such a duty of humility, is a very much more easy matter than the <em> putting it in practice</em> .</p>
