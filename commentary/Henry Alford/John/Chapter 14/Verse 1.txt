	 πιστεύετε  both times is  imperative . So Cyr [182] , Nonn., Thl., Euth., Aug [183] , Hil [184] , Lampe, Lücke, De Wette, Stier, Tholuck (edn. 6), and A.V.R. Many (Erasmus, Beza, Grot., Olsh., also E. V.) take the first as indic., the second as imper., ‘  Ye believe in God: believe also in me .’ But this is inconsistent with the whole tenour of the discourse, which presupposes a want of belief in God in its full and true sense, as begetting  trust in Him. Luther takes  both as indicative. The command is intimately connected with ch. Joh 13:31-32  faith in the glorification of Christ in the Father , and  of the Father in Him .
