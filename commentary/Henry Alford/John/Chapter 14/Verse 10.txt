<p><strong> 10.</strong> ] See ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:30">John 10:30</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:38">John 10:38</span> , and for the latter clause ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:28">John 8:28</span> , where the contrast is, as here, purposely inexact in <em> diction</em> , <em> words</em> being placed in one member and <em> works</em> in the other: and, as there, <strong> ἔργα</strong> and <strong> ῥήματα</strong> are taken as correlative and co-extensive; all the working of the Lord Jesus being a λαλιά , a <em> revelation of the Father</em> . De Wette supposes both ἔργα and ῥήμ . to be understood in <em> both places</em> . Without the [ ὁ ], the sense will be, of course, the Father, abiding in Me.…
	</p>
<p><strong> ποιεῖ τὰ ἔργα αὐτοῦ</strong> ] <strong> doeth His works:</strong> they are not Mine, but His, done in and by Me: but ἐν ἐμοί , present and abiding, so that ὁ ἑωρακὼς ἐμὲ ἑώρακεν τὸν πατέρα .</p>
