<b> 11. for the very works’ sake]</b> See ch. x. 38. The object here seems to be, to fix their attention on the<i> works</i> as a plain testimony even to such as could not simply believe so deep a thing on His assertion, and one which—<b>12.]</b> should become a matter felt and known in themselves hereafter,—by virtue of their living union with Him who is gone to the Father, and become the dispenser and channel of the Spirit. ‘He who<i> believes</i> Christ speaking concerning Himself,<i> believes on</i> Christ.’ Bengel.
