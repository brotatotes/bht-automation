<b> 21.]</b><b> hath…and keepeth</b>,— “that is,” says Augustine, “<b>hath</b>, in memory, and<b> keepeth</b>, in life:” or perhaps more accurately, ‘He who<i> has</i> my commandments, as being my disciple by outward profession (not thus only: but<i> holds</i> them, by the inner possession of a living faith), and<i> keeps</i> them:’ see Luke xi. 28, And this<b> keeping</b> is more of the inner<i> will</i> to keep them, than the absolute observance, which can only follow on high degrees of spiritual advancement.
