<p><strong> ἐν ἐμοὶ οὐκ ἔχ οὐδ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] “Nullum scilicet omnino peccatum.” Aug [203] ibid. 2. This is the only true interpretation: <strong> has nothing in Me</strong> no point of appliance whereon to fasten his attack. But Meyer well observes, that this is rather <em> the fact to be assumed</em> as the <em> ground</em> of what is here said, than the <em> thing itself</em> which is said. De Wette, Lücke, Tholuck, and many others render it, “ <em> has no power over me</em> ,” οὐδὲν αἴτιον θανάτου , Euthym [204]
