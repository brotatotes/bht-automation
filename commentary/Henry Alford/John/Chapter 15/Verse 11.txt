<p><strong> 11.</strong> ] <strong> λελάλ</strong> <strong> .</strong> again proleptic, hastening to the end of the discourse, and treating it as ended.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἡ χαρὰ ἡ ἐμή</strong> <strong> ,</strong> not “ <em> joy concerning Me</em> ” (Euthym [212] ), nor “ <em> joy derived from Me</em> ” (De Wette), nor “ <em> My joy over you</em> ” (Aug [213] , Lampe, Lücke, former edd.), but <strong> My joy,</strong> properly speaking (see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:3">2 Corinthians 2:3</span> , ὅτι ἡ ἐμὴ χαρὰ πάντων ὑμῶν ἐστιν ): “His own holy exultation, the joy of the Son in the consciousness of the love of God, of His Unity with the Father: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:10">John 15:10</span> .” (Lücke, 3rd edn.)
	</p>
