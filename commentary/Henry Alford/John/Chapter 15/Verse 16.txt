	 ἔθηκα ]  appointed: see Acts 13:47 ; 1 Thessalonians 5:9 , and reff. Euth., Chrys., Thl. explain it ἐφύτευσα , in the parabolic sense. But the parable seems to be no further returned to than in the allusion implied in καρπός . ‘  Ordained ,’ in E. V., is objectionable, as conveying a wrong idea.
	 ὑπάγ  .   κ  .   καρ  .   φέρ  . ] ὑπ . probably merely expresses (see ref. and Matthew 18:15 ; Matthew 19:21 , and πορευόμενοι , Luk 8:14 ) the activity of living and developing principle; not the missionary journeys of the Apostles (Grot., Lampe, Meyer). The καρπός is not the Church, to be founded by the Apostles, and endure; this is evident, for  here the fruit is spoken of with reference to  themselves , and their ripening into the full stature of Christ. Much of their fruit will be necessarily the winning of others to Christ: but that is not  the prominent idea here.
