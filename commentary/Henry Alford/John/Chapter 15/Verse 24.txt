<p><strong> 24.</strong> ] He refers to the testimony of His <em> works</em> among them also, as leaving them again without excuse; they had had ocular witness of His mission.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐν αὐτοῖς</strong> not <em> to</em> them (as Aug [220] ), but as <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:22">Acts 2:22</span> , ἐν μέσῳ ὑμῶν .
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [220] <em> Augustine, Bp. of Hippo</em> , 395 430
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐποίησεν</strong> is, not only by external evidence, but also by internal, the right reading. πεποίηκεν (as Lücke remarks, ii. 643) would imply that the <strong> ἄλλοι</strong> referred to were contemporaries of our Lord, or, at all events, that their works still lasted.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἑωράκασιν</strong> does not refer to <em> the works</em> (as Lücke), but to καὶ ἐμὲ καὶ τὸν πατέρα μου , see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:9">John 14:9</span> .</p>
