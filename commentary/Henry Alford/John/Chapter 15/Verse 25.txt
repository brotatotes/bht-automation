<p><strong> 25.</strong> ] <strong> ἀλλ</strong> <strong> ʼ,</strong> but all this not as an <em> accidental</em> thwarting of My word and work among them, but as a matter predicted in Scripture.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἵνα</strong> <strong> ,</strong> with the fullest sense of <em> purpose</em> , as always, and most especially in this formula. Beware of the evasive ecbatic sense.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐν τῷ νόμῳ αὐτῶν</strong> ] See ch. Joh 10:34 and note. To suppose any irony in these words, as De W. does (“they are <em> true followers-out of their law</em> ”), is manifestly against the whole spirit of our Lord’s reference to the law. It is ‘ <em> their</em> law,’ “quem assidue terunt et jactant,” Bengel, as condemning them, though their boast and pride.
	</p>
<p><strong> δωρεάν</strong> <strong> ,</strong> not, “ <em> to no purpose</em> ,” as Bengel ( <em> vergeblich</em> ), but as E. V., <strong> without a cause,</strong> answering to πρόφασιν οὐκ ἔχουσιν , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:22">John 15:22</span> .
	</p>
<p>The citation is probably from the Messianic <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ps+69:0">Psalms 69:0</span> .</p>
