<p><strong> 8.</strong> ] <strong> ἐν τούτῳ</strong> belongs to the following, not the preceding: ἐν τούτῳ , ἵνα .… as in E. V., see reff.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐδοξάσθη</strong> again is proleptic, representing that in the spiritual dispensation the fact is habitually so. See on this sense of the aorist, Winer, edn. 6, § 40. 5. b. 2.
	</p>
<p>The <strong> πολὺς καρπός</strong> is not merely ‘large success in the apostolic mission,’ but ‘individual advance in bringing forth the fruits of the Spirit.’
	</p>
<p><strong> καὶ γένησθε</strong> ] <strong> and that ye may become My</strong> (true) <strong> disciples,</strong> ἀπαρτισθήσεσθε , Euthym [210] (reading the <em> future</em> , see below.) “Fundamentum Christianismi, fieri discipulum Christi: fastigium, esse discipulum Christi.” Bengel. According to the reading γενήσεσθε , the actual result of what precedes is stated: <em> and so ye shall become</em> …
	</p>
