The disciples are perplexed by this<b> little while</b>, as connected with what our Lord had before asserted ver. 10, “<i>I go to my Father</i>.”<i> That</i> seemed to them a long and hopeless withdrawal: how was it then to be reconciled with what he now said of a short absence? What was this<b> little while</b>? This connexion not being observed, has led to the insertion by the copyists of the clause<b> because I go to the Father</b> in ver. 16.
