<b> 5.</b>] This is occasioned by the foregoing, but in fact begins the new subject,<i> the condition of the Comforter’s coming</i>.<br><br><b> and none of you asketh me....</b>] They had (see ch. xiii, 36; xiv. 5) asked this<i> verbally</i> before: our Lord therefore cites the question here in some other and deeper sense than they had used it there. I believe the meaning to be: ‘None of you enquires into the NATURE<i> of My departure</i>, so as to appear anxious to know what advantages are to be derived from it; but (ver. 6) you are all given up to grief on account of what I have said’
