<b> 6.</b>] ‘Grief has filled, entirely occupied, your heart (not “<i>your hearts</i>,” but singular, as common to all, see Rom. i, 21), to the exclusion of any regard of my object in leaving you.’ ‘These are the same disciples who afterwards, when their risen Lord had ascended to heaven,—without any pang at parting with Him, returned with great joy to Jerusalem, Luke xxiv. 52.’ Augustine remarks that “there is beneath this mild rebuke a tacit consolation. For while He blames them in that they neglected enquiring whither He was going, He virtually excuses this their negligence, in that it arose from their being overwhelmed with sorrow.”
