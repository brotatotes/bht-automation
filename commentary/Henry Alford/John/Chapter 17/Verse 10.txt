<p><strong> 10.</strong> ] Compare ch. Joh 16:15 and note. “It were not so much if He had only said, ‘All Mine is Thine;’ for that we may all say, that all we have is God’s. But this is a far greater thing, that He inverts this and says, ‘All Thine is Mine.’ This can no <em> creature</em> say before God.” Luther, Stier, v. 418, edn. 2.
	</p>
<p>The E. V., ‘All Mine <em> are</em> Thine,’ &amp;c., gives the erroneous impression that <em> persons</em> only are meant, whereas it is <em> all things</em> , in the widest meaning, <em> the Godhead itself included</em> , of which this is asserted.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐν αὐτοῖς</strong> <strong> ,</strong> not ‘ <em> by their means</em> ,’ <strong> but in them;</strong> by that ἐγὼ ἐν αὐτοῖς of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:23">John 17:23</span> , the life of the vine in the branches; so that the fruit of the branches is the glory of the vine, by the sap of the vine living in the branches. All this again is proleptic.</p>
