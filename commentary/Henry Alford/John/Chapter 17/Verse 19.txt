It is clear against all Socinian inferences from this verse, that all that part of the sanctification of the Son of God implied in ch. x. 36 is here excluded: and only that intended, which is expressed Heb. ii. 10 by<i> making perfect through sufferings</i>. Of this, His Death was the crowning act, and was also the one to which the expression<b> for their sakes</b> most directly applies; but the whole is included.
