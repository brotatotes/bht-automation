<p><strong> 10.</strong> ] At this time took place the kiss of Judas, in accordance with the agreement entered into, and to assure the captors that the person thus offering Himself was indeed Jesus of Nazareth, and no substitute for him: see note on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:49">Matthew 26:49</span> . The other view, that the kiss took place first, before the incidents of our <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:4-9">John 18:4-9</span> (Friedlieb, Archäologie der Leidensgeschichte, p. 68), is to me quite inconceivable.
