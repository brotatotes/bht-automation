<b> 10.</b>] At this time took place the kiss of Judas, in accordance with the agreement entered into, and to assure the captors that the person thus offering himself was indeed Jesus of Nazareth, and no substitute for him: see note on Matt. ver. 49. The other view, that the kiss took place first, before the incidents of our verses 4–9, is to me quite inconceivable.<br><br> On Peter’s act, see Matt. ver. 51. The<i> names</i> of Peter and Malchus are only found<i> here</i>:—the fact that it was the<i> right</i> ear, only here and in Luke.<br><br> The (external) ear, though severed, was apparently still hanging on the cheek;—for our Lord is said in Luke xxii. 51, to have touched<i> his ear</i> in performing the healing.
