<p><strong> 30.</strong> ] They do not mention the charge of blasphemy brought against Him by the Sanhedrim, for fear of the entire rejection of their cause, as by Gallio, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:16">Acts 18:16</span> . The Procurators in such cases had a discretionary power. On what they did say, Grot. observes, “Quod probationibus deerat, id supplere volunt sua auctoritate.”</p>
