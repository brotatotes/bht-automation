<b> 31.</b>] This answer is best regarded as an ironical reproach founded on their apparently proud assertion in ver. 30—and amounting to this: ‘If you suppose I am to have such implicit confidence in your judgment concerning this prisoner as to take his guilt on your word, take him and put him to death (for so “<b>judge ye Him</b>” must be understood,—see below) according to your law;’ reminding them that the same Roman power which had reserved capital cases for his jurisdiction, also expected proper cognizance to be taken of them, and not that he should be the mere executioner of the Sanhedrim.
