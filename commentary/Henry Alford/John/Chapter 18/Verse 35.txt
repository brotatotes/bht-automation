<b> 35.</b>] Pilate at once repudiates the idea of<i> his</i> having any share in Jewish expectations, or taking any personal interest in Jewish matters: all his information he has derived from the public accusation of the people and chief priests. Then in the question,<b> What hast Thou done?</b>, is implied, ‘There is no definiteness in their charge: let me have thine own account, thy ex-parte statement, that I may at least know<i> something</i> definite of the case.’
