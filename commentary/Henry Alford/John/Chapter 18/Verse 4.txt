<b> 4.</b>] On our Lord’s foreknowledge of all that was to happen to Him, see Matt. xxvi. 45.<br><br><b> went forth</b>] probably, from the shade of the trees into the moonlight; hardly, as De Wette and Lücke suggest, from some building in the garden.<br><br><b> Whom seek ye?</b>) spoken,—as was the question to Judas in Matt. xxvi. 50, “<i>Friend, wherefore art thou come</i>?”—to carry reproof to the conscience of those addressed: and also to obtain for so solemn an act as the delivering Himself up to them, the formal declaration of their intention to take Him. “When men sought Him to make Him a king, He fled: now that they seek Him to put Him to death, He goes forth to meet them.” Stier.
