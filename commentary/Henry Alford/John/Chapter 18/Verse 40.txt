<p><strong> 40.</strong> ] They have not before cried out in this narrative: so that some circumstances must be pre-supposed which are not here related: unless Joh 18:30-31 be referred to.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἦν δὲ ὁ Β</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> λ</strong> <strong> .,</strong> in Mar 15:7 and <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:19">Luke 23:19</span> , a rioter; but doubtless also a robber, as such men are frequently found foremost in civil uproar. There is a solemn irony in these words of the Apostle a <em> Robber!</em> See the contrast strongly brought out, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:14">Acts 3:14</span> . Luthardt (after Krafft) remarks on the parallelism with <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="le+16:5-10">Leviticus 16:5-10</span> . Thus was Jesus “the goat upon which the Lord’s lot fell, to be offered for a sin-offering.” See the same idea expanded by Mr. Wratislaw, in the first of the sermons in his volume.</p>
