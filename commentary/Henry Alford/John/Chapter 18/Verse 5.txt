 5. ] Some among them knew Him ( Mat 26:55 ), others probably not. This answer may have been given by some one in authority among the Roman soldiers, who had it in command ‘to apprehend Jesus of Nazareth.’
	 εἱστήκει  …   μετ  ʼ   αὐτῶν ] I believe these words to be the description of an eyewitness; John detected Judas standing among them, and notices the detail, as is his constant habit, by way of enhancing the tragic character of the history. The synoptic narrative related the kiss which presently took place: but this self-tradition of our Lord was not related in it. John therefore adds this touch of exactness, to shew that the answer Ἰησοῦν τ . Ν . was not given because they were ignorant of His Person, so  as not to be able to say ‘Thee;’ but because they  feared to say it.
