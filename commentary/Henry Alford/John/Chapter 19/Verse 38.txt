<p><strong> 38.</strong> ] <strong> μετὰ ταῦτα</strong> not, ‘immediately after this’ but ‘soon after.’ The narrative implies, though it does not mention (as Mark and Luke do), that Joseph himself took down the Body from the cross. Lücke thinks the soldiers would have done this: but their duty seems only to have extended to the ascertaining of the fact of death. The <strong> ἀρθῶσιν</strong> of Joh 19:31 need not imply, ‘by their hands.’
	</p>
<p>It was customary to grant the bodies of executed persons to their friends. “Percussos sepeliri carnifex non vetat,” Quintil. Declam. vi.
