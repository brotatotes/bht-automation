<b> 38.</b>]<b> after these things</b>—not immediately after this, but soon after. The narrative implies, though it does not mention (as St. Mark and St. Luke do), that Joseph himself took down the Body from the cross. Lücke thinks the soldiers would have done this: but their duty seems only to have extended to the ascertaining of the fact of death. The words of ver. 31, “<i>that they might be taken away</i>,” need not imply, ‘by their hands.’
