<p><strong> 20 22.</strong> ] The same spirit of mockery of the Jews shewed itself in the title, as before, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:14">John 19:14</span> . They had prevailed on Pilate by urging this point, that Jesus had set Himself up for a king; and Pilate is willing to remind them of it by these taunts. Hence their complaint and his answer.
	</p>
<p>The Latin was the official language, the Greek that usually spoken, the Hebrew (i.e. Aramaic) that of the common people.
	</p>
<p><strong> ὃ γέγ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> γέγ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] The first perfect denotes the past action; the second that it was complete and unalterable.</p>
