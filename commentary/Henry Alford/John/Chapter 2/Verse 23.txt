<b> 23. when they beheld the miracles which he did]</b> “They believed on Him, but not firmly. Those converts believed in a stricter sense, who believed not owing to the miracles only, but owing to His teaching.” Euthymius.<i> What miracles</i> these were, is not related:—certainly some notable ones, see ch, iii. 2.<br><br> The mention of them precludes us from understanding ch. iv. 54, as indicating that the healing of the ruler’s son was<i> absolutely His second miracle</i>.
