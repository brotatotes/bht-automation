</b>The meaning is,<b> He did not trust Himself</b> (in the original, the same verb is used for<i> ‘believed</i>,’ in ver. 23, and for<i> ‘trust’</i> in this verse)<b> to them</b>,—i. e. treat them as true and earnest disciples: they entered into no spiritual relation with Him, and He in consequence into none with them. The fact of this being narrated shews that it made an impression on the Evangelist, and led him perhaps first to the conclusion which he here expresses, and which higher knowledge enabled him afterwards to place, as he here does, on its right ground;—
