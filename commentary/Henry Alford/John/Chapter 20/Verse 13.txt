<b> 13.</b>] Here again the finest psychological truth underlies the narrative. The other women (Mark, ver. 5. Luke, ver. 5)<i> were afraid</i> at the vision; but now Mary, having but one thought or desire, to recover the lost Body of<i> her</i> Lord,<i> feels no fear</i>.<br><br> The angels doubtless are proceeding further to assure her as they did the women before:—but this is broken off by the appearance of the Lord Himself, or perhaps by Mary’s turning away.
