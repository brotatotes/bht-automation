<b> 25.</b>] He probably does not name the Feet, merely because the Hands and Side would more naturally offer themselves to his examination than the Feet, to which he must stoop. He requires no more than had been granted to the rest: but he had<i> their testimony in</i><i> addition</i>, and therefore ample ground for faith to rest on. Olshausen calls him the ‘Rationalist among the Apostles.’
