 1, 2.   Μαρ  .   ἡ Μαγδ  . ] She was not alone (Matt., Mark, Luke). Does this appear in the  οἴδαμεν below? This is not, as Meyer says, precluded by the οἶδα in John 20:13 . Mary there speaks in her own person, which she might do however accompanied. Still, probably not. She uses the plural as involving all the disciples in her own feeling of ignorance and of consequent sorrow. So Meyer: and it is more natural to take it thus. One thing we may conclude for certain, that  she , for some reason,  did not see the vision related in Matt., Mark, and Luke.
