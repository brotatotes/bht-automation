<b> 22.</b>] The words<b> what is that to thee?</b> imply a rebuke;—not perhaps however so sharp a one as has been sometimes seen in them. They remind Peter of the distinctness of each man’s position and duty before the Lord; and the subsequent command,<b> follow thou Me</b>, directs his view along that course of duty and suffering, which was appointed for him by his divine Master. In the original, both<i> thou</i> and<i> Me</i> are in emphatic positions: “<i>His</i> appointed lot is no element in<i> thy</i> onward course: it is ME that<i> thou</i> must follow.”
