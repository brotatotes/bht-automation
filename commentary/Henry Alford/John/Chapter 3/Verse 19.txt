<p><strong> 19.</strong> ] The particular <em> nature</em> of this decided judgment is now set forth, <strong> that the Light</strong> (see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:4-5">John 1:4-5</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:7">John 1:7</span> , and notes) <strong> is come into the world</strong> ( ἐλήλυθεν , in reference perhaps to ἐλήλυθας , Joh 3:2 ), <strong> and men</strong> (= ὁ κόσμος , men in general; an awful revelation of the future reception of the Gospel) <strong> loved</strong> (the perversion of the affections and will is the deepest ruin of mankind) <strong> the darkness</strong> (see note on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:5">John 1:5</span> ; = the state of sin and unbelief) <strong> rather than</strong> (not = ‘ <em> and not</em> ,’ but as Bengel says, “Amabilitas lucis eos perculit, sed obhæserunt in amore tenebrarum,” see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:35">John 5:35</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:43">John 12:43</span> : 2Ti 3:4 ) <strong> the light, because their deeds were evil</strong> (their habits, thoughts, practices, all these are included, were perverted).
	</p>
