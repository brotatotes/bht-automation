<b> 2.] by night</b>,<i> for fear of the Jews</i>: see ch. xii, 42. The discourse seems to have taken place between Jesus and Nicodemus<i> alone</i>,—and may have been related by our Lord to the Evangelist afterwards. If this be deemed improbable (though I do not see why it should),—of the two other alternatives I would rather believe that St. John was present, than that Nicodemus should have so minutely related a conversation which in his then position he could not understand.
