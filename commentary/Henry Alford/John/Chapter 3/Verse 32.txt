<b> 32],</b> His witness is not, like John’s, only of what he has been forewarned to expect, but of that which He has seen and heard. But<b> no man</b>,—i. e. in reference to the<i> world</i>, into which He is come, the<i> darkness</i> in which His light shines,—<i>no one comparatively</i>,—receives His testimony. The state of men’s minds at Jerusalem with regard to Jesus must ere this have been well known to the Baptist.
