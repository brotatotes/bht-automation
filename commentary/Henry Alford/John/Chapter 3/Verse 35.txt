<b> 35.]</b> This, again, is the<i> ground why</i> the Father<b> gives not the Spirit by measure</b> (to Him): see Matt. xi. 27–29, with which this verse forms a remarkable point of connexion, shewing that what is commonly known as John’s form of expression was not confined to him, but originated higher, having its traces in the narrative of the other Gospels, which is confessedly, in its main features, independent of him.
