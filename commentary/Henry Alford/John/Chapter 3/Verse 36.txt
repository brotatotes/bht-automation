<b> 36.]</b> Compare ch. i. 12, 13; ver. 15. The word rendered “<i>believeth not</i>” may mean<b> disobeyeth</b>, and is so rendered Rom. ii. 8; x. 21: 1 Pet. ii. 7, and elsewhere. Unbelief implies disobedience.<br><br><b> abideth]</b> It was on him, see ver. 18, in his state of darkness and nature,—and can only be removed by faith in the Son of God, which he<i> has not</i>.
