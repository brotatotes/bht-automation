<b> 4.]</b> It is impossible that Nicodemus can have so entirely and stupidly misunderstood our Lord’s words, as his question here would seem to imply. The idea of new birth was by no means alien from the Rabbinical views. They described a proselyte when baptized as “like an infant just born.” Lightfoot. I agree with Stier in thinking that there was something of the spirit that<i> would not</i> understand, and the disposition to turn to ridicule what he heard. But together with this there was also considerable<i> real ignorance</i>. ‘The proselyte might be regarded as born again, when he became one of the seed of Abraham: this figure would be easily explained on the Judaical view: but that<i> every</i> man should need this, was beyond Nicodemus’s comprehension. He therefore rebuts the assertion with a reduction to an absurdity, which in spirit expresses, as in ch. vi. 60,— ‘This saying is hard; who can hear it?”<br><br><b> when he is old</b>: probably he himself was old, and he instances his own case.
