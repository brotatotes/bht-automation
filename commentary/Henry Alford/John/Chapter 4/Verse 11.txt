</b>Though “Sir” (the same word as that commonly rendered “<i>Lord</i>”) is not to be pressed as emphatic, it is not without import; it surely betokens a different regard of the stranger than the words “<i>thou being a Jew</i>” did:—* “She calls him ‘<i>Sir</i>,’ thinking Him to be some great man.” Euthymius. The course of her thoughts appears to be: “<i>Thou canst not mean living water</i> ( ‘bubbling up and leaping,’ Euthymius),<i> from</i><b> this</b><i> well, because thou hast no vessel to draw with, and it is deep; whence then hast thou</i> (knowest thou of, drawest thou)<i> the living water of which thou speakest?</i>
