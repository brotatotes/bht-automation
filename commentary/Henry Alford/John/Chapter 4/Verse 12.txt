<i>Our father Jacob was contented with</i><b> this</b>,<i> used it, and bequeathed it to us: if thou hast better water, and canst give it, thou must be greater than Jacob</i>.” There is something also of Samaritan nationality speaking here. Claiming Jacob as her father (Josephus says of the Samaritans, ‘When they see the Jews prospering, they call them their relatives, as being themselves sprung from Joseph; but when they see them in trouble, they profess to have no connexion with them’), she expresses by this question an appropriation of descent from him, such as almost to exclude, or at all events set at a greater distance, the Jews, to one of whom she believed herself to be speaking.
