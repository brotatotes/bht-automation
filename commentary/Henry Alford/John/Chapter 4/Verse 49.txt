<b> 49.]</b> Here is the same weakness of faith as there,—but our Lord’s last words have made visible impression. It is like the Syrophoenician woman’s rejoinder,— ‘Yea, Lord; but...,’ only the faith is of a far less noble kind than hers. He seems to believe it necessary that Jesus should be on the spot;—not that there was any thing strange or blameable in this, for Martha and Mary did the same, ch. xi. 21, 32:—and to think that it would be too late when his child<i> had expired</i>;—not, imagining that He to whom he spoke could<i> raise the dead</i>.
