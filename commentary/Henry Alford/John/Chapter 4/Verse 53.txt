The word<b> believed</b>,<i> absolutely</i>, implies that in the fullest sense he and all his<i> became disciples of Jesus</i>. It is very different from “<i>believed the word that Jesus spake</i>” in ver. 50—as believing on HIM must be always different from believing on<i> any thing else in the world</i>, be it<i> even His own word or His own ordinances</i>. The cure took place in the afternoon: the nobleman probably set out, as indeed the narrative implies, immediately on hearing our Lord’s assurance, and<i> spent the night on the way</i>.
