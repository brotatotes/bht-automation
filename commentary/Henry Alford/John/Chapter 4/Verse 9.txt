<b> 9. being a Jew]</b> She knew this perhaps by his dress, more probably by his dialect. ‘There seems to be a sort of playful triumph in the woman’s question, q.d. ‘even a Jew, when weary and athirst, can humble himself to ask drink of a Samaritan woman.’<br><br><b> for Jews have no dealings with Samaritans</b> are the words of the Evangelist to explain her question. The word rendered<b> have no dealings</b> is properly spoken of<i> trade</i>,—but hero is in a wider signification. The fact is abundantly illustrated in the Rabbinical writings.
