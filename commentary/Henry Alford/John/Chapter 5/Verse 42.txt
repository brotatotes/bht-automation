<p><strong> ἀγάπην</strong> ] Luthardt remarks, perhaps refining somewhat too much, <strong> τὴν ἀγάπην</strong> <strong> ,</strong> because “ <em> the love which ye ought to have</em> ” is imported: <strong> τοῦ θεοῦ</strong> “ <em> of</em> (for) <em> your God the God of Israel</em> .” So that the words are spoken, not of an ungodly mind in general, but of an absence of that love which God’s covenant people should have for Him. “They would none of Jesus: for they were not true Israelites.” This love, if they had it, would teach them, the whole heart, and soul, and mind, and strength being given to God, to seek honour <em> only from Him</em> , and thus to appreciate the glory which He hath given to His Son, and His testimony concerning Him.</p>
