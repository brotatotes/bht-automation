<b> 45.]</b> The work of Christ is not<b> to accuse</b>, even as He is Judge;—but to<i> judge</i>, by the appointment. of the Father. And therefore—though He has said so much of the unbelief of the Jews, and charged them in the last verse with breach of the central law of God—<i>He will not accuse them</i>; nay, it is not needful;—for Moses, whom they disbelieved, while vainly hoping in him (see above on ver. 39), “<i>making their boast in the law</i>,” Rom. ii. 17,—<i>already accused them</i>: see Deut. xxxi. 21, 26, and ch. vii. 19.
