<p><strong> 46.</strong> ] The former part of this verse should not be rendered as in E. V. ‘ <em> had ye believed Moses, ye would have believed me;</em> ’ but <strong> if ye believed Moses, ye would believe me</strong> . The imperfects render this necessary: the other rendering would require aorists.
	</p>
<p><strong> περὶ ἐμοῦ ἔγραψεν</strong> “ <em> nusquam non</em> .” Bengel. This is an important testimony by the Lord to the <em> subject</em> of the whole Pentateuch; it is <strong> περὶ ἐμοῦ</strong> <strong> .</strong> It is also a testimony to <em> the fact</em> , of Moses <em> having written those books</em> , which were then, and are still, known by his name.</p>
