<p><strong> 12.</strong> ] Peculiar to John. The command, one end of which was certainly to convince the disciples of the power which had wrought the miracle, is given by our Lord a moral bearing also. They collected the fragments <em> for their own use</em> , each in his κόφινος , the ordinary furniture of the travelling Jew (“ <em> quorum cophinus fœnumque supellex</em> ,” Juv [89] Sat. iii. 14), to carry his food, lest he should be polluted by that of the people through whose territory he passed: see note on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:32">Matthew 15:32</span> . Observe, that here the 12 baskets are filled with the fragments of the <em> bread alone:</em> but in Mark, with those of the fishes also.
