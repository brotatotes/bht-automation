<p><strong> 37.</strong> ] The whole body of believers on Christ are spoken of by Him, here and in ch. 17, <em> as given to Him by the Father</em> . But Bengel’s observation is very important: “ <strong> πᾶν</strong> vocula momentosissima, et, collatis iis quæ sequuntur, consideratu dignissima. Nam in sermonibus Jesu Christi, quod Pater ipsi dedit, id, et singulari numero et neutro genere, appellatur <em> omne;</em> qui ad ipsum, <em> Filium</em> , veniunt, ii masculino genere vel etiam plurali numero describuntur, <em> omnis</em> , vel <em> illi</em> . Pater Filio totam quasi massam dedit, ut omnes quos dedit unum sint; id universum Filius singulatim evolvit, in exsecutione. Hinc illud in xvii. 2, ut <em> omne quod dedisti</em> ei, <em> det eis</em> vitam æternam.” See also <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:4">1 John 5:4</span> . See further on πᾶν ὃ δίδωσίν μοι ὁ πατήρ , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:44">John 6:44</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> οὐ μὴ ἐκβ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἔξω</strong> does not refer here to the office of the Son of God <em> as Judge;</em> but is another way of expressing the grace and readiness with which He will receive all who come to Him.</p>
