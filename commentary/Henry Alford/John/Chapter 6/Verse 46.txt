<p><strong> 46.</strong> ] The connexion is: the mention of ἀκούσας παρὰ τοῦ πατρός might lead them to think of a personal communication from the Father to each man, and thus the necessity of the mission of the Son might be invalidated. This was the only way in which a Jew could misunderstand <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:45">John 6:45</span> ; he could not dream of a seeing of the Father with bodily eyes.
	</p>
<p><strong> ὁ ὢν παρὰ τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> θεοῦ</strong> is Jesus Himself: see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:29">John 7:29</span> . His knowledge of the Father is <em> complete</em> and immediate; ours, <em> partial</em> , and derived through Him only.</p>
