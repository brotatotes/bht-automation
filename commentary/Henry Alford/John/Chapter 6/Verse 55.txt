<b> 55.]</b> The sense is not here, ‘<i>My Flesh is the true meat</i>&c.,’ but<b> My Flesh is true meat</b>, i.e.<i> really</i> TO BE EATEN, which they doubted. The adverb, “<i>meat indeed</i>,” or “<i>drink indeed</i>,” as in A.V., falls short of the depth of the adjective. This verse is decisive against all explaining away or metaphorizing the passage. Food and drink, are not here mere metaphors;—rather are our common material food and drink mere shadows and imperfect types of this only real reception of refreshment and nourishment into the being.
