<p><strong> 60.</strong> ] Lampe shews by reff. and other citations that σκληρός “non tam <em> absurditatem</em> quam <em> impietatem</em> designat.” It seems clear that it was not the <em> difficulty</em> , so much as the <em> strangeness</em> of the saying, which scandalized them. It is the whole discourse, the turn given to it, the doctrine of the Bread of Life, the giving His Flesh and Blood to eat, at which they take offence.
	</p>
