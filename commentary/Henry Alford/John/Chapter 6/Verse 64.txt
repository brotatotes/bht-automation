<b> 64.]</b> ‘This accounts for your murmuring at what I said, that<i> ye do not believe.’</i><br><br><b> For Jesus knew...]</b> De Wette remarks, that the<i> foreknowledge</i> of our Lord with regard to Judas renders it impossible to apply the ordinary rules of moral treatment, as ‘Why did He then continue him as an Apostle? Why did He give him the charge of the purse, knowing him to be a thief?&c.”—to the ease: and it is therefore better not to judge at all on the matter.<br><br> The fact is, we come here to a form of the problem of<i> divine foreknowledge</i> and<i> human free-will</i>, which, in any of its endless combinations of expression, it is equally impossible for us to solve.<br><br><b> from the beginning</b>, from their first coming to Him;—the first beginning of their connexion with Him.
