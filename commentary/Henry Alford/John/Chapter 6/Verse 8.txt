<b> 8.]</b> Meyer remarks, that the words<b> one of His disciples</b> may seem strange, seeing that Philip also was this: but that it has its value in the narrative, seeing that, Philip, having been asked in vain, one from among the circle of the disciples answers, and is afterwards specified as having been Andrew.<br><br> In the three other Gospels, the loaves and fishes appear as the disciples’ own;—and we have thus a very simple but very instructive instance of the way in which differences in detail arose. They were their own,—but not till they had bought them.
