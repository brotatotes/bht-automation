<b> 12.] the multitudes</b> (the different groups of which<i> the multitude</i> was composed) would include the Galilean disciples, and those who had been baptized by the disciples in Judæa,—whose view the words “<i>He is a good man</i>” would represent,—as expressed mildly in protest against His enemies.<br><br><b> he deceiveth the multitude</b>, possibly in reference to the feeding of and then the discourse to the multitude, which had given so much offence.
