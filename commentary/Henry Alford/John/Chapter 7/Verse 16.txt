<b> 16.]</b> The words may bear two meanings:—either, ‘<i>the sense of Scripture which I teach is not my own, but that in which it was originally penned as a revelation from God</i>;’ or,<b> My teaching</b> (generally)<b> is not mine, but that of Him who sent me</b>. The latter is preferable, as agreeing better with what follows, and because the former assumes that He was expounding Scripture, which, though probable, is not asserted.
