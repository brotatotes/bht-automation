<b> 19.]</b> There is a close connexion with the foregoing. Our Lord now takes the<i> offensive</i> against them. The<i> being willing to do His will</i> was to be the great key to a tyme appreciation of His teaching: but of this there was no example among<i> them</i>:—and therefore it was that they were no fair judges of the teaching, but bitter opponents and persecutors of Jesus, of whom, had they been anxious to fulfil the law, they would have been earnest and humble disciples (ch. v. 46). The law was to be read before all Israel every seventh year in the feast of tabernacles (Deut. xxxi. 10–13):—whether this was such a year is uncertain: but this verse may allude to the practice, even if it was not.
