<p><strong> 50.</strong> ] The Jews had, since the sabbath-healing, condemned Jesus, and were seeking to kill him. But in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ex+23:1-2">Exodus 23:1-2</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="de+1:16-17">Deuteronomy 1:16-17</span> , justice is commanded to be done in the way here insisted on by Nicodemus. On the consistency, and development, of the character of Nicodemus, Luthardt has some valuable remarks, pp. 125 ff. [see on ch. Joh 19:39 ].</p>
