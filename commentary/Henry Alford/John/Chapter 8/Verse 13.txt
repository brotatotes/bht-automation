<p><strong> 13.</strong> ] See ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:31">John 5:31</span> . The assertion <em> there</em> was, that His own <em> unsupported</em> witness ( <em> supposing that possible</em> ) would not be trustworthy, but that His testimony <em> was</em> supported by, and in fact coincident with, that of the Father. The very same argument is here used, but <em> the other side of it</em> presented to us. He <em> does</em> witness of Himself, <em> because</em> His testimony is the testimony of the Father; He being the λόγος τοῦ θεοῦ , and the Father <em> witnessing in Him</em> .</p>
