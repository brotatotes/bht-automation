<b> 19.]</b> Augustine and others imagine that the Jews thought of a<i> human Father</i>, in thus speaking. But surely before this, as Stier remarks, the Jews must have become too well accustomed to the words “my Father,” from our Lord, to mistake their meaning. It is rather a question asked in mere scorn, by persons who know, but will not recognize, the meaning of a word uttered by another.
