<b> 23.]</b> ‘Ye cannot come where I am going, because we both shall return thither whence we came: I to the Father from Whom (<b>from above</b>) I came: ye to the earth and under the earth (for that more awful meaning surely is not excluded) whence ye came’ (<b>from beneath</b>).<br><br> Then the term<b> this world</b> of course does not<i> only</i> imply ‘<i>this present state of things</i>, but involves the deeper meaning, of the<i> origin</i> of that state of things (see ver. 44) and its<i> end</i>, ver. 24.
