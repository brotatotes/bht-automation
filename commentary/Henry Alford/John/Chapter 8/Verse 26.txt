<b> 26.]</b> He is, that which He speaks; and that, He has received from the Father;—He has His definite testimony to give, and His work to do: and therefore, though He has much that He could speak and judge about the Jews, He does it not, but overlooks their malice,—not answering it,—that He may go forward with the<i> speaking unto the world</i>, the revelation of Himself: the<i> truth</i> of which is all-important, and excludes less weighty things. This verse is in the closest connexion with the foregoing.
