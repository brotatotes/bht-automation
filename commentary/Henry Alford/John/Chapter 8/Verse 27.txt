 27.] They did not identify “<i>him that sent me</i>” with “<i>my Father</i>.” However improbable this may be, after the plain words “<i>the Father that sent me</i>,” in ver. 18, it is stated as a fact; and the Evangelist certainly would not have done s0 without some sure ground:— “It is probable, that they questioned one with another, ‘Who is he that sent him?’” Euthymius. There is no accounting for the ignorance of<i> unbelief</i>, as any minister of Christ knows by painful experience.
