<p><strong> 27.</strong> ] They did not identify ὁ πέμψας με with ὁ πατήρ μου . However improbable this may be after ὁ πέμψας με πατήρ , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:18">John 8:18</span> (De Wette), it is <em> stated as a fact;</em> and the Evangelist certainly would not have done so without some sure ground: εἰκὸς αὐτοὺς διαπορεῖν πρὸς ἀλλήλους λέγοντας Τίς ἐστιν ὁ πέμψας αὐτόν ; Euthym [128] There is no accounting for the <em> ignorance of unbelief</em> , as any minister of Christ knows by painful experience.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [128] Euthymius Zigabenus, 1116</p>
