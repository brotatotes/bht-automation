<b> 29.] left me not alone</b>, referring to the<i> appointment</i> of the Father by which His work was begun, and which the continued presence of the Father (<b>he that sent me is with me</b>) carries on through that work: see ch. xvi. 32.<br><br><b> because I do always....</b>; not<i> ‘for</i>,’ as if what follows were merely a<i> token that it is so</i>. The<b> doing always these things that please him</b> is the very essential being of the Son, and is the<i> cause why</i> the Father is ever with Him.
