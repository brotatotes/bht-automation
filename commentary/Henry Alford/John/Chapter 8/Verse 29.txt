<p><strong> 29.</strong> ] <strong> ἀφῆκεν</strong> <strong> ,</strong> aor. referring to the <em> appointment</em> of the Father by which His work was begun, and which the <strong> μετ</strong> <strong> ʼ </strong> <strong> ἐμοῦ ἐστιν</strong> carries on through that work: see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:32">John 16:32</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ὅτι</strong> <strong> ,</strong> <strong> because</strong> ; not ‘for,’ as if what follows were merely a <em> token</em> that it is so (Olsh.). The <strong> τὰ ἀρεστὰ αὐτ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ποιῶ πάντ</strong> <strong> .</strong> is the very essential being of the Son, and is the <em> cause why</em> the Father is ever with Him.</p>
