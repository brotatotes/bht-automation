<b> 3.</b>] St. John never mentions, “<i>the Scribes</i>” elsewhere, but usually calls the opponents of Jesus “<i>the Jews</i>,” or “<i>the rulers</i>.” “<i>The Scribes and Pharisees</i>” is a very common expression in the three Gospels.<br><br> The account gives no light as to the<i> capacity in which</i> these Scribes and Pharisees acted when they brought the woman. Probably,<i> only</i> as tempting Jesus, and not in the course of any legal proceedings against her. Such would have required (Lev. xx. 10; Deut. xxii. 22) that the<i> man also</i> should have been put to death.
