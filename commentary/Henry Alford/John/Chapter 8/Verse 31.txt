<b> 31.] continue in my word</b> means to “<i>abide in Me</i>,” ch. xv. 7, though that perhaps is spoken of a deeper entrance into the state of union with Christ.<i> Remaining in His word</i> is not merely<i> obeying His teaching</i>, but is the inner conviction of the truth of that revelation of Himself, which is<i> his word</i>.<b> ye are</b>, for probably they had given some outward token of believing on Him, e.g. that of ranging themselves among His disciples.
