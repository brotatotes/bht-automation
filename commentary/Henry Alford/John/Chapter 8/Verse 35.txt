<b> 35.]</b> I believe, with Stier and Bengel, the reference to be to Hagar and Ishmael, and Isaac: the<i> bond</i> and the<i> free</i>. They had spoken of themselves as the<i> seed of Abraham</i>. The Lord shews them that there may be, of that seed,<i> two kinds</i>; the<i> son</i>, properly so called, and the<i> slave</i>. The latter does not abide in the house for ever: it is not his right nor his position— ‘Cast out the bondwoman and her son.’ ‘But the<i> son</i> abideth ever. For the application, see on following verses.
