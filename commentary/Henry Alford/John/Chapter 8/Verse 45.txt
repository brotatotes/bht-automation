<b> 45.</b>] And the very reason why ye do not believe<i> Me</i> (as contrasted with<i> him</i>) is,<i> because I speak the truth;</i>—you<i> not being of the truth,</i> but of him who is falsehood itself. This implies a charge of wilful striving against known and recognized truth. Euthymius fills up the context— “<i>If I told you lies, ye would have believed me, as speaking that which belongs to your father</i>:” see ch. v. 43.
