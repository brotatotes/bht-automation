<b> 55.</b>] The sense is,<b> of Whom</b><b> ye say ‘He is our God,’ and</b> (not “<i>yet</i>” nor “<i>but</i>”)<b> know Him not.</b> Then what follows sets forth the contrast between them, the pretended children of Abraham, who know not Abraham’s God (the<i> liars</i>), and Him who knows Him, and keeps His word, so that His word works in and by Him; yea, He<i> is the Word of God.</i> His<i> allowing their denial</i> of this state of knowledge and union would be<i> as great a lie in Him,</i> as their<i> assumption</i> of it was<i> in them.</i>
