<b> 9</b>.] They had said, ver. 5, “<i>such women</i>”—they now perceive that they themselves were<i> such men</i>. There is no historical difficulty in this conduct of the Pharisees, as Olshausen finds;—they were struck by the power of the word of Christ. It was a case somewhat analogous to that in which His saying, “<i>I am he</i>,” struck His foes to the ground, ch. xviii. 6.
