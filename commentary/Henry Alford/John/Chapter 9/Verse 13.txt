 13.] The neighbours appear to have brought him to the<i> Pharisees,</i> out of hostility to Jesus (see ver. 12): and ver. 14 alleges the reason of this:—or perhaps from fear of the sentence alluded to in ver. 22. The “<i>Pharisees</i>” here may have been the court presiding over the synagogue, or one of the lesser local courts of Sanhedrim. Lücke inclines to think they were an assembly of the great Sanhedrim, whom St. John sometimes names<i> the Pharisees</i>:—see ch. vii. 47; xi. 46: Meyer regards them as some formal section of<i> the Pharisees,</i><i> as a body:</i> but were there such sections?
