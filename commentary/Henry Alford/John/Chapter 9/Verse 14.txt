<b> 14.</b>] Lightfoot cites from a Rabbinical treatise on the Sabbath, that<i> it was forbidden even to put saliva on the eyelids.</i> But the<i> making the clay,</i> as a servile work, seems to be here prominently mentioned. Meyer notices,—and it is interesting, as a minute mark of accuracy,—that the man, in verses 11 and 15, only relates what he himself, as being blind, had felt: he says nothing of the spittle.
