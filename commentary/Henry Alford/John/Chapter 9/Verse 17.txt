<p><strong> 17.</strong> ] The question is but <em> one</em> , as in E. V., <strong> What sayest thou of him, that he hath opened</strong> (i.e. for having opened) <strong> thine eyes?</strong> The stress is on <strong> σύ</strong> ‘What hast <em> thou</em> to say to it, seeing we are divided on the matter?’ Both parties are anxious to have the man’s own view to corroborate theirs.
