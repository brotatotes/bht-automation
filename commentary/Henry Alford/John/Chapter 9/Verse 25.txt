<b> 25.]</b> The man shrewdly evades the inference and states again the simple fact. We must render his words at the end of the verse, not “<i>whereas I WAS blind, now I see</i>,” as A.V.: but<b> being a blind man,</b> or as in text,<b> though a blind man, I now see.</b> The shrewd and naïve disposition of the man furnishes the key to the ænigmatical expression. He puts it to them as the problem, the fact of which he knows for certain but the reason of which it was for them to solve, that he, whom they all knew as a blind man, now saw.
