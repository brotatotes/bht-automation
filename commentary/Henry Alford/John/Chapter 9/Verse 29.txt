<b> 29.]</b> God<b> hath spoken,</b> not<i> spake</i>, is important: it betokens the abiding finality of God’s revelation to Moses, in their estimation: as if they said, “<i>We stand by</i> God’s revelation to Moses.”<b> from whence,—</b> ‘whether from God or not.’ But see ch. vii. 27, 28, where a very different reason is given for disbelieving Him to be the Christ.<b> 30.] Why herein is,</b>&c. This well expresses the sense of the original. Tho man takes what their words had conceded, and proceeds to argue upon it.<b> ye</b> is emphatic:<i> you,</i> whose business it is to know such things.
