<b> 5.</b>] This partly explains the<i> day</i> and<i> night</i> of the former verse, partly alludes to the nature of the healing about to take place. As before the raising of Lazarus (ch. xi. 25), He states that He is<i> the Resurrection and the Life;</i> so now, He sets forth Himself as the source of the archetypal spiritual light, of which the natural, now about to be conferred, is only a derivation and symbol.
