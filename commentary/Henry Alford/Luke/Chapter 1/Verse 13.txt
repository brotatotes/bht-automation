<b> 13.]</b> He had then<i> prayed for a son—</i>but as appears below, long since—for he now had ceased to look for an answer to his prayer. Many Commentators have thought his prayer was for the salvation of Israel by the appearance of the Messiah: but the former view appears more probable.<br><br><b> John</b>—i. e.<i> God is favorable:</i> we have it under the form of Johanan, 2 Kings xxv. 23; 1 Chron. iii. 24; 2 Chron. xxviii. 12.
