De Wette denies this interpretation, as contrary to all analogy: and yet himself explains the expression by saying that what the Messiah does, is in Scripture<i> ascribed to God as its doer</i> (similarly Meyer). But<i> why?</i> because Messiah is GOD WITH US. This expression is besides used (see Zech. xiv. 5) in places where the undoubted and sole reference is to the Messiah.<br><br><b> in the spirit and power]</b> As a type of partial fulfillment, of the personal coming of Elias in the latter days (see note on Matt. xi. 13, 14). Bleek remarks that it was not in the wonder working agency of Elias that John was like him, for ‘John did no miracle,” but in the power of his uttered persuasion.<br><br><b> to turn....]</b> The first member only of the sentence corresponds with Malachi. The angel gives the exposition of the second member,—which stands in the LXX, “<i>and the heart of a man towards his neighbor</i>” (in A.V. “<i>and the heart of the children to their fathers</i>”):—for of course that must be understood in the better sense, of the prevailing, and the bad becoming like them.
