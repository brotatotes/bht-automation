<b> 17.] before him</b>—i. e. “<i>the Lord their God</i>,” manifest in the flesh. De Wette denies this interpretation, as contrary to all analogy: and yet himself explains the expression by saying that what the Messiah does, is in Scripture<i> ascribed to God as its doer</i> (similarly Meyer). But<i> why?</i> because Messiah is GOD WITH US. This expression is besides used (see Zech. xiv. 5) in places where the undoubted and sole reference is to the Messiah.
