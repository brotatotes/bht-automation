<b> 22.]</b> They knew, by some excitement, visible in his manner. It was not his office to<i> pronounce the benediction,</i> but that of the other incensing priest; so that his ‘not being able to speak,’ must mean,<i> in answer to the enquiries</i> which his unusual appearance prompted. This answer he gave by a sign: and the question was also by signs; for (see ver. 62) he was<i> deaf</i>, as well as dumb, which indeed is the strict meaning of the word used in the original.
