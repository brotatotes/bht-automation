<b> 3. it seemed good to me also]</b> St. Luke by this classes himself with these<i> many</i>, and shews that he intended no disparagement nor blame to them, and was going to construct his own history from similar sources. The words which follow imply however a conscious superiority of his own qualification for the work. There is here no<i> expressed claim</i> to inspiration, but at the same time no<i> disclaimer</i> of it.<br><br><b> having traced down]</b> by research, and so become accurately acquainted with.
