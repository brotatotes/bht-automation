<p><strong> 37.</strong> ] The future, in Hebrew, expresses that which does not belong to any fixed time, but shall ever be so.
	</p>
<p><strong> ῥῆμα</strong> ] See reff., and above on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:4">Luke 1:4</span> . This place, and its original, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ge+18:14">Genesis 18:14</span> , which are sometimes quoted to shew that ῥῆμα may mean simply “a <em> thing</em> ,” are in fact most decisive against any such supposition. For the declaration amounts to this, “Hath the Lord spoken and can He not do it?”</p>
