<b> 4. instructed]</b> Theophilus had then been orally instructed in the narratives which form the subject of this Gospel: and St. Luke’s intention in writing it is, that he might have a<i> more accurate knowledge of these histories.</i> The word means literally,<b> catechized,</b><i> ‘catechetically taught.’</i><br><br><b> those sayings]</b> not as in A.V., to be<i> rendered</i> ‘things:’<i> neither the Greek nor the corresponding Hebrew word ever has this meaning</i>, as is commonly but erroneously supposed. In all the commonly-cited examples of this,<i> ‘things expressed in words’</i> are meant: here<b> the histories,—accounts.</b>
