<p><strong> 5.</strong> ] <strong> ἐξ ἐφ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> Ἀβ</strong> <strong> .,</strong> which was the <em> eighth</em> of the <em> four and twenty courses</em> of the priests (see ref. 1 Chron.). These courses kept their names and order, though not their descent, after the captivity. The courses, though called ἐφημερίαι , were of a <em> week’s</em> duration each: ἀπὸ σαββάτου ἐπὶ σάββατον , Jos. Antt. vii. 14. 7. Meyer observes that if any use is to be made of this note of time of fix the date, our reckoning must be made <em> backward</em> from the destruction of the temple, not <em> forward</em> from the restoration of the courses by Judas Maccabæus, because it is not certain <em> what course then began</em> the new order of things; whereas we have a fixed note for the destruction of the temple, that it was on the 9th of Ab, and the course in waiting was that of Jehoiar ib. Comm. ii. p. 194.
