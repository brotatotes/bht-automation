<p><strong> 62.</strong> ] The natural inference (see on Luk 1:22 ) from this verse is, that Zacharias was <em> deaf as well as dumb;</em> nor do I think Kuinoel, De Wette, Meyer, Olshausen, Bengel, Bleek, and Bp. Wordsworth have succeeded in invalidating this inference. There could have been no reason for <em> beckoning</em> , had Zacharias been able to hear articulate words. Bengel’s reason, adopted by Bp. W., “commodius est muto innuentes videre quam loquentes audire,” is surely too far-fetched.</p>
