<b> 27.</b>] The first part of this, together with Deut. xi. 13 ff., the Jews had written on their phylacteries, and recited night and morning: but not the second; so that the idea that Jesus<i> pointed</i> to the phylactery of the lawyer, will not hold.<br><br> Meyer thinks the man answered thus, because he had before heard our Lord cite these in connexion, and with an especial view to asking the question “<i>who is my neighbour?</i>” It may have been so;—but I should rather believe the same spirit with which he began, to have carried him on to this second question. The words<b> willing to justify Himself</b> seem to imply this; but see below.
