<p><strong> 27.</strong> ] The first part of this, together with <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="de+11:13">Deuteronomy 11:13</span> ff., the Jews had written on their phylacteries, and recited night and morning: but not the second; so that Kuinoel’s idea that Jesus pointed to the phylactery of the lawyer, will not hold.
	</p>
<p>Meyer thinks the man answered thus, because he had before heard our Lord cite these in connexion, and with an especial view to asking the question τίς ἐστίν μου πλησίον ; It may have been so; but I should rather believe the same spirit with which he began, to have carried him on to this second question.
