<b> 29.</b>] Meyer explains this: The questioner, having been by our Lord’s enquiry, “<i>how readest thou?</i>” himself thrown into the position of the answerer, yet, “<i>willing to justify himself,</i>” wishing to carry out the purpose with which he asked at first, and to cover what otherwise would be his shame at being answered by so simple a reply, and that his own,—asks, “<i>who is my neighbour?</i>”—I may observe that we need not take the whole of this explanation, but may well suppose that “<i>to justify himself</i>” may mean, ‘to get himself out of the difficulty:’ viz. by throwing on Jesus the definition of<i> one’s neighbour</i>, which was very narrowly and technically interpreted among the Jews, excluding Samaritans and Gentiles.
