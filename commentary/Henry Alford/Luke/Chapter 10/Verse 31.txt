<b> 31.</b>] Many priests journeyed this way, for Jericho was a priestly city; this man is perhaps represented as having been up to Jerusalem in the order of his course, and returning.<br><br> The Law and Prophets enjoined the act of mercy which this priest refused; see Exod. xxiii. 4, 5: Deut. xxii. 1–4: Isa. lviii. 7, not, it is true,<i> literally</i>,—and therefore he neglected it. He did not even go up to him to examine him, but passed by on the opposite side of the road.
