<b> 32.]</b> Not ‘<i>a greater than Jonas</i>,’ or ‘<i>than Solomon</i>:’ but Jonah here is used as equivalent to<i> the sign of</i> Jonah,—so that more applies to Him who<i> is the sign</i> to this generation:—a sign, greater, both in its<i> actuality</i>, its<i> significance</i>, and its<i> consequences</i>. The<i> order</i>, here, seems to be for the sake of climax;—for the undervaluing and not appreciating His<i> wisdom</i>, will not lie so heavy on them in the judgment, as the<i> rejection</i> of His<i> preaching of repentance</i>.
