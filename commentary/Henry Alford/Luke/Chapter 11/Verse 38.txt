<b> 38.]</b> The<i> expression</i> of this wonder is not stated, but it is probable that it found expression in some open way. Our Lord would hardly have so suddenly begun,<b> ye Pharisees</b>, unless something had been<i> said</i>, to which by assent they were parties. See His proceeding when<i> nothing was said</i>,—ch. vii. 39, 40.<b></b><br><br><b> washed]</b> literally<b> baptized</b>. This use of the word shews that it<i> did not imply necessarily immersion</i> of<i> the whole body;</i>—for it was only the hands which the Pharisees washed before meat.
