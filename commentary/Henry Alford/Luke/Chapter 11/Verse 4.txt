<p><strong> 4.</strong> ] <strong> καὶ γὰρ αὐτ</strong> <strong> .…</strong> expressed here more strongly than in Matt., as the plea for the exercise of the divine forgiveness to us, ‘ <em> for it is our own practice also to forgive:</em> ’ but notice, the difference there is no <strong> ἁμαρτία</strong> here between man and man, only the ordinary business word of this world.
	</p>
<p><strong> π</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ὀφείλοντι ἡμ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] This varied expression (see above) may serve to shew how far ‘ <em> Luke’s reporter</em> ’ (De Wette) was from misunderstanding the words of the Lord; that reporter, as Stier well observes, (Reden Jesu, iii. 126, edn. 2,) being no other than the <em> Holy Spirit Himself</em> , whose special guidance was promised in bringing to mind the things said by Jesus ( Joh 14:26 ).</p>
