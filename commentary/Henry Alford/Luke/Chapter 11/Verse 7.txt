<b> 7.</b>] We have an interesting fragment of domestic life here given us. The door is ‘barred,’ not only ‘shut;’ there is the trouble of unbarring it: the<i> father</i> and<i> children</i> are in bed (observe how in all the parables which place the Father, or the Husband, before us, the<i> Mother</i>, or the<i> Bride, does not appear</i>); and he cannot (i. e. will not,<i> cannot</i> from being overcome by reluctance) rise and give to him.
