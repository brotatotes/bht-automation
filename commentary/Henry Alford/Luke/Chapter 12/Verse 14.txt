<b> 14.] Man</b>... a word of solemn reproof; see Rom. ii. 1; ix. 20. The<b> man</b> also forms a definite subject for you to refer to,... ‘<i>men</i>,’ i.e. mankind in general. This question is expressed in almost the very words of the Egyptian rejecting the arbitration of Moses, Exod. ii. 14;—and may shew us the essential difference of the two offices of Moses and Christ.
