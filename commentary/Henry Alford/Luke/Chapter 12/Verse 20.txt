<b> 20.] God said unto him</b>,—perhaps it is meant, by some unmistakeable judgment; but more likely, as occurring in a parable, the words are to be literally taken. By supposing merely a<i> divine decree</i> to be meant,<i> without personal communication</i>, as Grotius, Kuinoel, and Trench do, we lose the impressive part of the parable, where the man’s selfishness and folly is brought into immediate contact with the solemn truth of his approaching death, which certainly our Lord intends us to contemplate.
