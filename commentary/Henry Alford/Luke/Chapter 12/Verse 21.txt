<p><strong> 21.</strong> ] <strong> οὕτως</strong> <strong> , thus:</strong> in utter confusion, and sudden destitution of all help and provision for eternity. There is no ἔσται : because the case, alas, is an every-day one in every place.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἑαυτῷ</strong> <strong> … </strong> <strong> εἰς θεὸν</strong> <strong> …</strong> ] The meaning of these expressions will be brought out thus: He who is rich <em> for himself</em> , laying up treasure <em> for himself</em> , is by so much robbing his real inward life, his life in and toward God, of its resources: he is laying up store for, providing for, the <em> flesh;</em> but the <em> spirit</em> , that which God looketh into and searcheth, is stripped of all its riches.
	</p>
<p>These words may also, as remarked on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:20">Luke 6:20</span> , shew that Luke does not, as supposed by some recent critics, use ‘riches’ as merely <em> this world’s wealth</em> , but with a deeper spiritual meaning.</p>
