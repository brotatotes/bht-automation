<b> 26.]</b><b> that thing which is least</b>: this shews the truth of the interpretation<b> age</b> (not “<i>stature</i>”) given in the note on Matthew. A<i> cubit</i> would not be<i> the least of things</i> to add to the stature, but a very large in-crease: whereas, as Trench observes, “a cubit would be infinitesimally small when compared to his length of life, that life being contemplated as a<i> course</i>, or<i> race</i>, which he may attempt, but ineffectually, to prolong.”
