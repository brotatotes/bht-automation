<p><strong> 26. </strong> <strong> ἐλάχιστον</strong> ] This shews the truth of the interpretation of <strong> ἡλικ</strong> <strong> .</strong> given in the note on Matt. A <em> cubit</em> would not be ἐλάχιστον to add to the stature, but a very large increase: [whereas, as Trench observes, “a cubit would be infinitesimally small when compared to his length of life, that life being contemplated as a <em> course</em> , or <em> race</em> , which he may attempt, but ineffectually, to prolong.”]</p>
