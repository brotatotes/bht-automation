<p><strong> 29.</strong> ] <strong> μετεωρίζ</strong> <strong> .,</strong> certainly not ‘ <em> nolite in sublime tolli</em> ,’ Vulg.; which Meyer approves, and Luther has adopted. For what have <em> high thoughts</em> to do with the present subject, which is, the duty of dismissing anxiety and over-carefulness, in confidence on God’s paternal care? It is, <strong> be not anxious,</strong> ‘at sea,’ tossed about between hope and fear. So Thucyd. (ii. 8) describes Greece as being πᾶσα μετέωρος when the two first cities were at war.</p>
