<p><strong> 35.</strong> ] There is a slight reference to, or rather another presentation of the truth set forth in, the parable of the virgins, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:1">Matthew 25:1</span> ff. But the image here is of servants waiting for their Lord to <em> return from</em> the wedding; left at home and bound to be in readiness to receive him. There is only a hint at the cause of his absence he is gone to a wedding: <strong> γάμοι</strong> may mean almost any feast or entertainment and the <em> main</em> thought here only is that he is away at a feast, and will return. But in the background lies the <em> wedding</em> in all its truth not brought out here, but elsewhere, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:1">Matthew 22:1</span> ff; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:1">Matthew 25:1</span> ff.
