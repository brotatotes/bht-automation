<b> 1.</b>] The words<b> at that season</b> may mean<b> at that very time</b>—viz. as He finished the foregoing discourse: but it is not<i> necessary</i> to interpret thus;—for, Matt. xii. 1; xiv. 1, the similar expression is certainly<i> indefinite</i>.<br><br> The opening words do not mean, as A.V., that these persons<i> were in the crowd, and remarked to the Lord concerning these Galilæans</i>, in consequence of what He had said ch. xii. 57:—such a finding of connexion is too fine-drawn. It is obvious that no connexion is intended between this incident and the foregoing discourse.<br><br><b> the Galilæans</b>] The historical fact is otherwise unknown. The way of speaking here shews that it was well known to the writer. It must have occurred at some feast in Jerusalem, on which occasions riots often took place, and in the outer court of the temple. Such slaughters were frequent, and would not be particularly recorded by the historians. This mingling of their blood with their sacrifices seems to have been thought by the narrators evidence that they were very depraved sinners: for this was their argument, and is unconsciously that of many at this day,— ‘the worse the affliction, the more deserved:’ see Gen. xlii. 21: Acts xxviii. 4.
