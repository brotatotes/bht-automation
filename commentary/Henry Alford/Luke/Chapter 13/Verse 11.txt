<b> 11. a spirit of infirmity</b>] Her weakness was the effect of permitted power of the evil one (ver. 16); but whether we are to find here a direct instance of<i> possession</i>, seems very doubtful. There is nothing in our Lord’s words addressed to her, to imply it: and in such cases He did not<i> lay on His hands, or touch</i>,—but only in cases of sickness or bodily infirmity.<br><br> The A.V. has here mistaken the position of the word which it renders “<i>in no wise.</i>’ It means<b> altogether</b>, and belongs to the verb<b> lift herself</b>.
