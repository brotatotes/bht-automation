<p><strong> 15. </strong> <strong> ὑποκριταί</strong> ] The Lord saw the real thoughts of his heart, that they were false, and inconsistent with his pretended zeal, and addressed the multitude as represented by him, their leader. A man hardly could give forth a doctrine so at variance with common sense and common practice, without some by-end, with which he covered his violation of truth. That by-end <em> here</em> was enmity to and jealousy of Jesus.
	</p>
<p>The instance chosen <em> exactly fits</em> the circumstances. A beast tied to the manger is confined down as this poor woman was.</p>
