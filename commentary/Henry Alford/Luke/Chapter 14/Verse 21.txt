<b> 21.</b>] The gathering of guests is still<i> in the city</i> (Matt. xxii. 7); that is, still<i> among the Jews.</i><br><br><b> the streets and lanes,</b> the broad and narrow streets: perhaps the<i> cities and villages</i> through which the Lord and his Apostles journeyed preaching.<br><br> Here appear again the very persons of ver. 13; the representatives of the wretched and despised: “the common people (<i>great multitude</i>),” Mark xii. 37: not perhaps without a hint, that only those who knew themselves to be spiritually poor and maimed and halt and blind would come to the Gospel feast.
