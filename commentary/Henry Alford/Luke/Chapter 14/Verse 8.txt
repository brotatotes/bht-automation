<b> 8.</b>] The whole of this has, besides its plain reference, a<i> deeper one,</i> linked into it by the important word<i> wedding,</i> carrying with it all that meaning which it always has when<i> relating</i> to the<i> Kingdom of God.</i> Both senses are obvious, and only one remark needed;—that all that<i> false</i> humility, by which men put themselves lowest and dispraise themselves<i> of set purpose to be placed higher,</i> is, by the very nature of our Lord’s parable,<i> excluded:</i> for that is not bond fide abasing one’s self. The exaltation at the hands of<br><br> the Host is not to be a<i> purposed end</i> to the guests, but will follow true humility.
