<p><strong> 18 20.</strong> ] <strong> ἀπὸ μιᾶς</strong> <strong> ,</strong> supply γνώμης : so ἀπὸ τῆς ἴσης , Thucyd. i. 15; so (ch. Luk 7:30 ) they had rejected John’s baptism, and ( Joh 7:48 ) the Lord himself. The saying is not to be taken strictly without exception, e.g. Nicodemus: but generically. So also <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:24">Luke 14:24</span> .
	</p>
<p>The <em> temper</em> of these self-excusers is threefold; the <em> excuses</em> themselves are threefold; their <em> spirit is one</em> . The first alleges an ἀνάγκη , he <em> must</em> go and see his land: the second not so much as this, only his own plan and purpose πορεύομαι : the third not so much as either of these, but rudely asserts οὐ δύναμαι (i.e. οὐ βούλομαι ) ἐλθεῖν . Also the <em> excuses themselves</em> are threefold. The first has his <em> worldly possession</em> (‘one to his farm,’ Mat 22:5 ) to go and see: the second his purchase (‘another to his merchandise,’ ibid.) of stock to prove: the third his home engagements and his lust to satisfy. <em> All</em> are detained by <em> worldliness</em> , in however varied forms.</p>
