<b> 13.</b>] The images of both the preceding parables are united here:—in his<i> taking his journey,</i> we have the straying sheep; in<i> his state when</i><i> he got into the far country,</i> the lost piece of money. But in this case the search is to be carried on<i> within him</i>—we are now on<i> higher ground</i> than in those two parables, “The far-off country represents forgetfulness of God.” Augustine.<br><br><b> profligate</b>] The old English word<b> retchless</b> expresses perhaps best the meaning, which is not ‘unsparing,’ but<b> incorrigible</b>, past hope of reclaim.
