<b> 16. husks</b>] ‘These are not the husks or pods of some other fruit, as of peas or beans, but<i> themselves a fruit,</i> that of the<i> carob</i> [or<i> caruba</i>, found not only in the East, but in South Europe, e.g. in abundance on the Riviera between Nice and Genoa. H.A.] like a bean-pod, though larger and more curved, thence called<i> keration</i> or little horn.... they have a hard dark outside and a dull sweet taste.... the shell or pod alone is eaten” Trench. His appetite even drove him to these for food;—<b>for</b> (this is the real sense involved in and)<b> no man gave</b> (aught) to him.
