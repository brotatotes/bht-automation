<p><strong> 22.</strong> ] All these gifts belong to his reception, not as a servant, but as a son: the <em> first</em> (best) <em> robe</em> , for him who came in rags, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="isa+61:10">Isaiah 61:10</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="re+3:18">Revelation 3:18</span> : not <em> the robe which he used to wear</em> <em> his former robe</em> this would not be consistent with the former part of the parable, in which he was not turned out with any disgrace, but left <em> as a son</em> and <em> of his own accord:</em> but <strong> a robe,</strong> ( <strong> yea</strong> ) <strong> the first and goodliest. The ring,</strong> a token of a distinguished and free person, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:2">James 2:2</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ge+41:42">Genesis 41:42</span> .
	</p>
<p>The <strong> shoes,</strong> also the mark of a free man (for slaves went barefoot), see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="zec+10:12">Zechariah 10:12</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:15">Ephesians 6:15</span> . These are the gifts of grace and holiness with which the returned penitent is clothed by his gracious Father, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="zec+3:4-5">Zechariah 3:4-5</span> .</p>
