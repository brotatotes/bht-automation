<p><strong> 23. </strong> <strong> τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> μόσχ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> σιτ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] So, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="jdg+6:25">Judges 6:25</span> , Gideon is commanded to kill τὸν μόσχον τὸν ταῦρον ὅς ἐστιν τῷ πατρί σου ( τ . μ . τ . σιτευτὸν τοῦ πατρός σου Α ): some calf fatted for a particular feast or anniversary, and standing in the stall. No allusion must be thought of to the <em> sacrificing of Christ:</em> which would be <em> wholly out of place</em> here, and is <em> pre-supposed</em> in the whole parable.
	</p>
