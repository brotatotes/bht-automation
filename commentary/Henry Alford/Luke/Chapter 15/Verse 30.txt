<p><strong> 30.</strong> ] <strong> ὁ υἱ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> σου οὗτος</strong> <strong> ,</strong> the last degree of scorn and contempt, just such as was shewn by the Pharisees towards the publicans and sinners (see ch. Luk 18:11 ). ‘I will not count such an impure person <em> my brother</em> .’
	</p>
<p><strong> σου τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> βίον</strong> <strong> ,</strong> a covert reproach of his father for having given it to him.
	</p>
<p><strong> μετὰ τῶν πορνῶν</strong> <strong> ,</strong> a charitable addition on the part of the elder brother, such as those represented by him always take care to make under similar circumstances. Even supposing it a necessary inference from the kind of life which he had been leading, it was one which nothing but the bitterest jealousy would have uttered at such a time.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἔθυ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> αὐ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> σ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> μ</strong> <strong> .</strong> parallel with ἁμαρτωλοὺς προσδέχεται , καὶ συνεσθίει αὐτοῖς , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:2">Luke 15:2</span> . ‘Thou hast not only made him equal to me, but hast received him into superior favour.’</p>
