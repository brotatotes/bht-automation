<p><strong> 4.</strong> ] It is the <em> Owner Himself</em> who goes to seek, see Ezek., Luk 15:11 <em> God in Christ</em> .
	</p>
<p>The <strong> ἑκατὸν πρόβ</strong> <strong> .</strong> are the house of Israel, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:6">Matthew 10:6</span> ; but in the <em> present</em> application, mankind (not, ‘ <em> believers in Christ:</em> ’ see on Luk 15:7 ).
	</p>
<p>The argument is to <em> their self-interest:</em> but the act on the part of the good Shepherd is, from the nature of the case, one of <em> love:</em> or, as Stier remarks, also human love for <em> his own;</em> for in Him, Love, and His glory, are one and the same thing.
	</p>
<p><strong> καταλ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τὰ ἐνν</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] These pass altogether into the background, and are lost sight of. The character of the good Shepherd is a sufficient warrant for their being well cared for. The <strong> ἔρημος</strong> is not a barren place, but one abounding in pastures (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:10">John 6:10</span> , compared with Mat 14:15 ).</p>
