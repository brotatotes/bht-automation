<b> 6.]</b> In this return to His house, must be understood the whole course of seeking and finding which the good Shepherd, either by Himself or His agents, now pursues in each individual case, even until He brings the lost sheep home into heaven to Himself—not in<i> reality</i>, so that it should not take place till the<i> death</i> of the penitent—but<i> by anticipation</i>,—till the<i> name is written in heaven</i>;—till the sinner is penitent. This is clear from the interpretation in ver. 7.<b> The friends and neighbours</b> represent the angels (and spirits of just men made perfect?).
