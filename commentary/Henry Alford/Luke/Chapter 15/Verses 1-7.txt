<p><strong> 1 7.</strong> ] THE LOST SHEEP. It does not appear where or when this [gathering of publicans and sinners to hear him] happened, but certainly in the progress of this same journey, and, we may well believe, consecutively on the discourses in the last chapter. This first parable had been spoken by our Lord before, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:12-14">Matthew 18:12-14</span> ; but, as Trench has remarked, (Par. in loc.,) with a different view: there, to bring out <em> the preciousness of each individual little one in the eyes of the good Shepherd;</em> here, to shew <em> that no sheep can have strayed so widely, but He will seek it and rejoice over it when found</em> . The second is peculiar to Luke.</p>
