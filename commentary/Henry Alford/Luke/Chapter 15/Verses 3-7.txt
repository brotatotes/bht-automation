<p><strong> 3 7.</strong> ] The man having the hundred sheep, is plainly <em> the Son of God, the Good Shepherd</em> . This had been his prophetic description, and that <em> in this very connexion</em> , of <em> seeking the lost</em> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eze+34:6">Ezekiel 34:6</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eze+34:11">Ezekiel 34:11</span> ff. This it is which gives so peculiar an interest to David as a type of Christ that he was <em> a shepherd:</em> ibid. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:23">Luke 15:23</span> . Our Lord plainly declares then by this parable and that I take to be the reason why it is placed <em> first</em> (see below) that the matter in which they had found fault with Him was the <em> very pursuit most in accordance with his divine Office of Shepherd</em> .</p>
