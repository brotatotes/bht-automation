<b> 13.]</b> See note on Matt. vi. 24. The connexion here is,—that we must, while put in trust with the<i> unrighteous mammon</i>, be serving<i> not it, but God.</i> The saying here applies admirably to the Pharisees and Publicans: the former were, to outward appearance, the servants of God, but inwardly served Mammon;—the latter,<i> by profession</i> in the service of Mammon, were, by coming to Jesus, shewing that they inwardly served God.
