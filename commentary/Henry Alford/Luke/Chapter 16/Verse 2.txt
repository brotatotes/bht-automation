<b> 2.</b>] It makes very little difference either in admissibility of construction or of sense, whether we render,<i> ‘why</i> do I hear this of thee?’ i.e. ‘what is the ground of this report?—what occasion has thou given for this being brought to me?” or, ‘<i>What is this that</i> I hear of thee?’ i.e. ‘give some account of it.’ I prefer rather the former, because no opportunity of explanation<i> what</i> it is, is given him, but he is commanded to produce his books, to shew<i> how</i> it has arisen.
