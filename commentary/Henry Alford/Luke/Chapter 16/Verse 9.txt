<b> 9.</b>] We now pass to the application at once—from the mouth of our Lord Himself. All that is dishonest and furtive in the character of the steward belonged entirely to him as a<i> son of this world:</i> but even in this character there was a point to praise and imitate. And the dishonesty itself is not inserted without purpose—viz. to shew us<i> how little the sons of this world scruple to use it, and how natural it is to them. Now, however,</i> we stand on higher ground:<i> to the pure, all things are pure:</i>—in bringing up the example into the purer air which the children of light breathe, its grosser parts drop off, and the finer only remain.
