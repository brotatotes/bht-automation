<p><strong> 8.</strong> ] <strong> ἕως φ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> κ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> π</strong> <strong> .,</strong> <strong> till I shall have eaten and drunken:</strong> see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:37">Luke 12:37</span> , where a different assurance seems to be given. But our Lord is here speaking of what we in our state of <em> service</em> are to <em> expect;</em> there, of what in our state of <em> manumission</em> (‘mensæ servos adhibere manumissionis erat species.’ Grotius, citing from Ulpian) and <em> adoption</em> , the <em> wonders of His grace will confer on us</em> . Here the question is of <em> right;</em> there, of <em> favour</em> .</p>
