<p><strong> 11 19.</strong> ] HEALING OF TEN LEPERS. It does not appear to what part of the last journey this is to be referred. There is no reason for supposing it to have been subsequent to what has just been related: this is not implied. It may have been at the very beginning of the journey. From the circumstance that these lepers were a mixed company of Jews and Samaritans, <strong> διὰ μέσον Σ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> κ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> Γ</strong> <strong> .</strong> probably means ‘ <em> between</em> Samaria and Galilee,’ on the frontiers of both. Meyer supposes <strong> αὐτός</strong> to mean ‘He for his part’ separate from the others going up to the feast, who would go direct through Samaria. Xen. has διὰ μέσου δὲ ῥεῖ τούτων ποταμός , i.e. ‘ <em> between</em> these walls.’ Anab. i. 4. 4.
