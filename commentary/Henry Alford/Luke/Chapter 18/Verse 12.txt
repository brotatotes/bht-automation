<p><strong> 12. </strong> <strong> νηστ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> δὶς τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> σ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] This was a <em> voluntary</em> fast, on the Mondays and Thursdays; the only prescribed fast in the year being the great day of atonement, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="le+16:29">Leviticus 16:29</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="nu+29:7">Numbers 29:7</span> . 	</p>
<p><strong> ἀποδ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> πάντα</strong> ] Here again, the law perhaps (but cf. Abraham’s practice, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ge+14:20">Genesis 14:20</span> ; and Jacob’s, Gen 28:22 ) only required tithe of the fruit of the field, and the produce of the cattle: see on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:23">Matthew 23:23</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> κτῶμαι</strong> ] Not <em> I possess</em> , which would be κέκτημαι but <strong> I acquire; of all my increase:</strong> see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="de+14:22">Deuteronomy 14:22</span> . His speech shews admirably what his πεποίθησις ἐφ ʼ ἑαυτῷ was.</p>
