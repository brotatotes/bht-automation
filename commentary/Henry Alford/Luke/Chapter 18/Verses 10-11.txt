 10, 11. ]  πρὸς ἑαυτόν belongs to  προσηύχ  . (cf. Mar 14:4 ), not to  σταθείς  : that would be καθ ʼ ἑαυτόν , see James 2:17 .  He stood (in the ordinary place),  and prayed thus with himself, as E. V., ‘  apud animum suum :’ such a prayer he would not dare to put up aloud (Meyer). The Church has admirably fitted to this parable the declaration of thankfulness in 1 Corinthians 15:9-10 (the two being the Epistle and Gospel for the Eleventh Sunday after Trinity), also made  by a Pharisee , and also on the ground ‘  that he was not as other men: ’ but how different in its whole spirit and effect! There, in the deepest humility, he ascribes it to the  grace of God that he laboured more abundantly than they all;  yet,  not   I,  but the grace of God that was with me.
