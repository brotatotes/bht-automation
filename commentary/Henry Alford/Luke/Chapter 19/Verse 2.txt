<b> 2.</b>]<b> Zacchæus</b> signifies in Hebrew, ‘pure;’ the name occurs in Ezra ii. 9: Neh. vii. 14. He was not a Gentile, as Tertullian supposed, but a Jew, see ver. 9.<br><br><b> chief among the publicans</b>] Probably an administrator of the revenue derived from<i> balsam,</i> which was produced in abundance in the neighbourhood.
