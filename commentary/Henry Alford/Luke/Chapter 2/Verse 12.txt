<b> 12.]</b> Olshausen hazards a conjecture, that the stable or cave may possibly have<i> belonged</i> to these shepherds. But I think the words<b> even unto</b>, or<b> as far as</b> to Bethlehem, ver. 15, do not look as if Bethlehem were their<i> home</i>. It seems clear that<i> the spot</i> was somehow known to them by the angel’s description.<br><br> Not<i> “the babe,”</i> as A.V.;—the angel, in giving the sign, generalizes the term: they were to know the truth of his words, by finding<b> a child</b> wrapped in swaddling clothes, lying in a manger.
