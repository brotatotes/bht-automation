<p><strong> 22.</strong> ] See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="le+12:1-8">Leviticus 12:1-8</span> , where however <em> the child</em> is not, as here, expressly included in the purification. (It is hardly possible that <em> Joseph</em> should be implied in the <strong> αὐτῶν</strong> <strong> ,</strong> as Euthym [25] , Meyer, interpret it.) The reading αὐτοῦ is remarkable, and hardly likely to have been a correction. αὐτῆς , adopted by the E. V., is almost without authority (see var. readd.), and is a manifest correction.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [25] Euthymius Zigabenus, 1116
	</p>
<p>Bengel denies that either the Lord or His mother wanted purification; and mentions that some render αὐτῶν ‘ <em> of the Jews</em> ,’ but does not approve of it ( Joh 2:6 is certainly no case in point). See the last note, on the necessity of purification for <em> both</em> .</p>
