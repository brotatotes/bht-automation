<b> 44.] the company</b> forming the<i> caravan</i>, or band of travelers;—all who came from the same district traveling together for security and company.<br><br> The interpretation that<i> ‘they went a day’s journey, seeking him,’</i> is simply absurd: for they would have turned back sooner: a few minutes might have sufficed for the search. It was<i> not till they laid up for the night</i> that they missed him, as at that time they would naturally expect his return to their own tent. Olshausen remarks, that being accustomed to His thoughtfulness and obedience, they were free from anxiety, till they discovered He really was not in the company.
