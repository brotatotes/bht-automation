<p><strong> 31.</strong> ] The <strong> οὐ κατ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τέκ</strong> <strong> .</strong> coming before <strong> καὶ ἀπέθ</strong> <strong> .</strong> is by a mixture of constructions <strong> and they had no children by her, and died, leaving none:</strong> not merely from the emphasis being on the leaving no children (as in Meyer). It is meant to express the absence of offspring <em> before</em> their death, <em> and after</em> .</p>
