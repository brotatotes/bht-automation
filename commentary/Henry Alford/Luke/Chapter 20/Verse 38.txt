<b> 38.</b>] On<b> all live unto him see</b> on Matt. vv. 31–33: but we have in this argument even a further generalization than in Matthew and Mark. There, it is a<i> covenant</i><i> relation</i> on which the matter rests: here, a life of<i> all,</i> living and dead,<i> in the sight of God,</i>—so that none are annihilated,—but in the regard of Him who inhabiteth Eternity, the being of<i> all is a living one,</i> in all its changes.
