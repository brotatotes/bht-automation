<b> 11.</b>] The<i> “goodman of the house”</i> was a man of some wealth, and could not be<i> identical with</i> the water-carrier (see notes on Matthew).<br><br> The Greek word rendered<b> guestchamber</b> is not here, as in ch. ii. 7, an<i> inn,</i> but a room set apart at this season of the feast, by residents in Jerusalem, in which parties coming from the country might eat the Passover. The question therefore would be well understood;—and the room being<b> spread</b>, and as Mark adds,<i> “prepared,”</i> would be no matter of surprise.
