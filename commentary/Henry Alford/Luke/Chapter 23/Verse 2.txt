<p><strong> 2.</strong> ] This charge was intended to represent the result of their previous judgment, <strong> εὕραμεν</strong> <strong> ;</strong> whereas, in fact, <em> no such matter had been before them:</em> but they falsely allege it before Pilate, knowing that it was the point on which his judgment was likely to be most severe. The words themselves which they use are not so false, as the spirit, and impression which they convey. The <strong> κωλύοντα φ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> Κ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> διδ</strong> <strong> .</strong> was, however, <em> false entirely</em> (see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:22">Luke 20:22</span> ff.); and is just one of those instances where those who are determined to effect their purpose by falsehood, do so, in spite of the fact having been precisely the contrary to that which they assert.</p>
