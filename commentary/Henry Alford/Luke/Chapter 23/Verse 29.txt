<p><strong> 29. </strong> <strong> ἔρχονται ἡμ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] Between this and then would be time for <em> that effectual weeping</em> , which might save both themselves and their children: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:37-38">Acts 2:37-38</span> , but of which few availed themselves. These few are remarkably hinted at in the change to the third person, which excludes them <strong> ἐροῦσιν</strong> <strong> ,</strong> i.e. not ‘ <em> men in general</em> ,’ nor ‘ <em> My enemies</em> ,’ but ‘the impenitent among you, those who weep merely tears of idle sympathy for Me, and none of repentance for themselves; those who are <em> in Jerusalem</em> and its misery, which My disciples <em> will not be</em> .’
	</p>
<p>On the saying itself, compare the whole of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ho+9:0">Hosea 9:0</span> , especially <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:12-16">Luke 23:12-16</span> .</p>
