<b> 29. the days are coming</b>] Between this and then, would be time for that<i> effectual weeping,</i> which might save both themselves and their children;—see Acts ii, 37, 38,—but of which few availed themselves. These few are remarkably hinted at in the change to the third person, which excludes them—<b>they shall say,</b> i.e. not ‘<b>men in</b><i> general,</i>’ nor<i> ‘My enemies,’</i> but ‘the impenitent among you,—those who weep merely tears of idle sympathy for Me, and none of repentance for themselves;—those who are in<i> Jerusalem</i> and its misery, which My disciples<i> will not be.’</i>
