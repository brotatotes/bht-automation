<p><strong> 32.</strong> ] The digest shews that the reading <strong> ἕτεροι κακοῦργοι δύο</strong> has diplomatically almost as great claims to be the true one as that in the text: and if we take the probabilities of alteration into account, it has even stronger claims. Of course it can bear but one meaning <strong> two other malefactors.</strong> That this should have been substituted for <strong> ἕτεροι δύο κακοῦργοι</strong> <strong> ,</strong> which may mean <strong> two other, malefactors</strong> (as rendered in E. V.), is simply inconceivable; that the transposition took place vice versa, is highly probable. This having now appeared by the additional evidence of the Codex Sinaiticus, it is impossible to annotate as was done in my earlier Editions.</p>
