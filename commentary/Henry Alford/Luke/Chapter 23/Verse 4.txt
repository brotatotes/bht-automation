We may remark (and on this see Matthew 27:18 ; Mar 15:10 ) that Pilate must have known well that a man who had really done that, whereof Jesus was accused, would be  no such object of hatred to the Sanhedrim . This knowledge was doubtless accompanied (as the above-cited verses imply) with a previous acquaintance with some of the sayings and doings of Jesus, from which Pilate had probably formed his own opinion that He was  no such King as His foes would represent Him. This is now confirmed by His own words (as related by John); and Pilate wishes to dismiss Him, finding no fault in Him.
