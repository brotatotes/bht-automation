That Cleopas says, Luke 24:24 ,  some of [  them that were with ]  us went, &c. must not be pressed too much, although it does certainly look as if he knew of more than one (see note there). The similarity in diction to John 20:5 ; John 20:10 ( παρακύψας βλέπει τὰ ὀθόνια κείμενα , and ἀπῆλθ . πρὸς ἑαυτ ., being common to the two passages) indicates a common origin, and, if I mistake not, one distinct from the rest of the narrative in this chapter. The meaning of  πρὸς ἑαυτόν  , as belonging to ἀπῆλθεν and not to θαυμάζων , is fixed by the expression in John, l. c.
