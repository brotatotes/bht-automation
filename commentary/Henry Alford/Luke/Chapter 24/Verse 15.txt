But this expression forbids the supposition that He was here,<i> strictly speaking, in another form,</i> as we find it less precisely expressed in Mark xvi. 12. The reason why they<br><br> did not know Him was (ver. 16), that<i> their eyes were supernaturally influenced,</i><i> so that they could not;—</i>see also ver. 31. No change took place in<i> Him</i>—nor apparently in<i> them,</i> beyond a power upon them, which prevented the recognition just so much as to delay it till aroused by the well-known action and manner of His breaking the bread. The<i> cause</i> of this was the will of the Lord himself, who would not be seen by them till the time when He saw fit.<br><br><b> drew near—</b>from<i> behind:</i> see ver. 18, where they take Him for an inhabitant of Jerusalem.
