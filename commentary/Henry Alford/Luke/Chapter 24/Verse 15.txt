	 αὐτὸς Ἰη  . ]  Jesus Himself, of whom they had been speaking. But this expression forbids the supposition that He was here,  strictly speaking , ἐν ἑτέρᾳ μορφῇ , as we find it less precisely expressed in Mark 16:12 . The reason why they did not know Him was ( Luk 24:16 ), that  their eyes were supernaturally influenced, so that they could not: see also Luke 24:31 . No change took place in  Him nor apparently in  them , beyond a power upon them, which prevented the recognition just so much as to delay it till aroused by the well-known action and manner of His breaking the bread. The  cause of this was the will of the Lord himself, who would not be seen by them till the time when He saw fit.
