 24.   ἀπῆλθόν τινες ] See Luk 24:12 and note. It is natural, even in accordance with Luke 24:12 , that the antithesis to τινές before, and the loose way of speaking to a stranger, who (they believed) was not acquainted with any among them, might cause them here to use τινές , without any reference to Peter being  accompanied . But what wonder, if the reports of such a day of anxiety and confusion were themselves disjointed and confused?
