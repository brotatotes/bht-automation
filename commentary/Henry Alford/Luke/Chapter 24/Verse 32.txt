<b> 32.</b>] ‘Was there not something heart-kindling in His discourse by the way, which would have led us to suppose that it was none but the Lord Himself?’ not that they<i> did</i> suppose it,—but the words are a sort of selfreproach for not having done so. Compare Matt. vii. 29.<br><br><b> he spoke to us,</b> not merely,<i> ‘with us,’</i> as A.V.: it<b> was</b> not so much a talking with them, as a discourse delivered<i> to them.</i>
