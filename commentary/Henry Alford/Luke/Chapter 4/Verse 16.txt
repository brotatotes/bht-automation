<p><strong> 16.</strong> ] <strong> οὗ ἦν τεθραμμένος</strong> = ἐν τῇ πατρίδι σου , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:23">Luke 4:23</span> : see Joh 4:44 and note.
	</p>
<p><strong> κατὰ τὸ εἰωθός</strong> refers to the <em> whole</em> of what He did it is not merely that He had been in the habit of <em> attending</em> the synagogues, but of <em> teaching</em> in them: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:15">Luke 4:15</span> . It was apparently the first time He had ever so taught in the synagogue at Nazareth.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀνέστη ἀναγν</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] The <em> rising up</em> was probably to shew His wish to <em> explain</em> the Scripture; for so ἀναγν . imports. Ezra is called an ἀναγνώστης τοῦ θείου νόμου , Jos. Antt. xi. 5. 1. The ordinary way was, for the ruler of the synagogue to call upon persons of any learning or note to read and explain. That the demand of the Lord was so readily complied with, is sufficiently accounted for by <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:14-15">Luke 4:14-15</span> . See reff.</p>
