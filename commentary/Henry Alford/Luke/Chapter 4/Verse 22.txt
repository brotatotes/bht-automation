<b> 22.</b>]<b> bare him witness: i.e. bare witness to him (that It was so),</b> The<b> words of grace</b> must be the discourse of which ver. 21 is a compendium.<b> they said,</b> viz. the<i> “all”</i> mentioned above, not merely some of them. While acknowledging the truth of what He said, and the power with which He said it, they wondered, and were jealous of Him, as being the son of Joseph—asking<i> “Whence hath this man these things?”</i> see Mark vi. 2–4. Between this verse and the next, the taking offence at Him is implied, for that is in a tone of reproof.
