<b> 23.</b>]<b> heal thyself</b>—not,<i> ‘raise thyself from thy obscure station,’</i> but,<b> exert thy powers of healing in thine own country,</b> as presently interpreted; the Physician being represented as an inhabitant of Nazareth, and<b> thyself</b> including His own citizens in it. Stier remarks, that the reproach was<i> repeated under the Cross.</i> Then, with a strictly individual application. On the miracles previously wrought in Capernaum, see note on ver, 14. That in John iv, 47–53 was one such.
