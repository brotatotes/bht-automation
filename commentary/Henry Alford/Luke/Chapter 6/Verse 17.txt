<p><strong> 17.</strong> ] Having descended from the mountain, He stood on a level place i.e. <em> possibly</em> , as has been suggested by some, <em> on a flat ledge or shelf on the side of the mountain;</em> but more naturally <em> below the mountain:</em> see on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:1">Matthew 5:1</span> . Whether Luke could thus have written <em> with the Gospel of Matthew before him</em> , I leave the reader to judge: premising, that is, the identity of the two discourses.</p>
