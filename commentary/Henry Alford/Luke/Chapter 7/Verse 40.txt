We must not however forget that in similar cases “<i>Jesus knowing their thoughts</i>” is inserted (Matt. ix. 4), and doubtless might also have been here.<br><br> There is an<i> inner personal appeal</i> in the words addressing the Pharisee. The calling by name—the especial<b> I have somewhat to say unto thee</b> refer to the<i> inner thoughts of the heart</i>, and at once bring the answer<b> Master, say on</b>, so different from “<i>This man, if he were a prophet</i>.”
