	 ἀφ  ʼ   ἧς εἰσῆλθ  . ] These words will explain one difficulty in the circumstances of the anointing: how such a woman came into the guest-chamber of such a Pharisee.
	She appears by them to have entered  simultaneously with our Lord and His disciples . Nor do Luk 7:36-37 at all preclude this idea:  ἐπιγνοῦσα ὅτι κατάκειται may mean, ‘having knowledge that He was going to dine,’ &c. If she came in His train, the Pharisee would not exclude her, as He was accustomed to gather such to hear Him: it was the  touching at which he wondered.
