<b> 35.] they went out</b>, viz. the people in the town and country; “<i>the whole city</i>” Mat-thew; here understood in ver. 34.<b> at the feet of Jesus]</b> This particularity denotes an eye-witness. The phrases common to Mark and Luke, e.g. clothed, and<b> in his right mind</b>, and<b> that they that saw it</b>, denote a common origin of the two narratives, which have however become considerably deflected, as comparison will shew.
