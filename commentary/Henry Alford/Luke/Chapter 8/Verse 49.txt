<b> 49.</b>] Little marks of accuracy come out in each of the two fuller accounts. Here we have<b> there cometh one</b>, which was doubtless the<i> exact</i> fact:—in Mark “<i>there came certain,</i>”—generally expressed. In Mark again we learn not only that Jesus heard, but that the message was not<i> reported</i> to Him, but He<i> overheard it being said</i>, which is a minute detail not given here. Nothing could more satisfactorily mark the independent authority of the two narratives.
