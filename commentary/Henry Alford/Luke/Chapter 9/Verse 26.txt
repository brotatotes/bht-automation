<p><strong> 26.</strong> ] After <strong> λόγους</strong> <strong> ,</strong> Mark adds ἐν τῇ γεν . ταύτῃ τῇ μοιχαλίδι καὶ ἁμαρτωλῷ .
	</p>
<p>Meyer remarks: ‘the Glory is threefold: (1) <em> His own</em> , which He has to and for Himself as the exalted Messiah: (2) <em> the glory of God</em> , which accompanies Him as coming down from God’s Throne: (3) <em> the glory of the angels</em> , who surround Him with their brightness.’</p>
