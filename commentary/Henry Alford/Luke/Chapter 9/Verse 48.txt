<b> 48.</b>] The discourse as here related has the closest connexion and harmony. The dispute had been, who (among the Twelve) should be greatest,—i. e. greatest<i> in the kingdom of heaven</i>: for other greatness is not to be thought of,—the minds of the disciples being always on this, as just about to appear; and our Lord reminds them, that no such precedence is to be thought of among those sent in His name; for that even a little child, if thus sent, is clothed with His dignity; and if there be any distinction among such, it is this, that he who is like that child, humblest and least, i.e. nearest to the spirit of his Lord,<i> he is the greatest</i>.
