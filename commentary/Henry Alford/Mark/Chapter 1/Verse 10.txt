<p><strong> 10.</strong> ] <strong> εὐθύς</strong> <strong> ,</strong> or <strong> -</strong> <strong> θέως</strong> <strong> ,</strong> is a favourite connecting word with Mark. St. Mark has here taken the oral account verbatim, and applied it to Jesus, ‘ <em> He saw</em> ,’ &amp;c. and αὐτόν must mean <em> Himself</em> : otherwise we must understand <strong> ὁ Ἰωάν</strong> <strong> .</strong> before <strong> εἶδεν</strong> <strong> ,</strong> and take <strong> ἀναβ</strong> <strong> .</strong> as <em> pendent</em> , which is very improbable.
	</p>
<p>The construction of the sentence is a remarkable testimony of the independence of Mark and Matt. even when parts of the narrative agree verbatim. See note on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:16">Matthew 3:16</span> .
