<p><strong> 11.</strong> ] σὺ εἶ , Mark, Luke; οὗτός ἐς ., Matt. ἐν ᾧ εὐδ ., Matt.; ἐν σοὶ εὐδ ., Mark and Luke. I mention these things to shew how extremely improbable it is that Mark had either Matt. or Luke before him. Such arbitrary alteration of documents could never have been the practice of any one seriously intent on an important work.</p>
