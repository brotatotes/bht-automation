<p>N.B. Throughout Mark, the parallel places in Matthew are to be consulted. Where the agreement is verbal, or nearly so, no notes are here appended, except grammatical and philological ones.

<p>1 <strong> 8.</strong> ] THE PREACHING AND BAPTISM OF JOHN. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:1-12">Matthew 3:1-12</span> .<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:1-17">Luke 3:1-17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:1-17">Luke 3:1-17</span> . The object of Mark being to relate <em> the official life and ministry</em> of our Lord, he begins with His <em> baptism</em> ; and as a necessary introduction to it, with <em> the preaching of John the Baptist</em> . His account of John’s baptism has many phrases in common with both Matt. and Luke; but from the additional prophecy quoted in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:2">Mark 1:2</span> , is certainly <em> independent</em> and <em> distinct</em> (see Prolegomena to the Gospp. ch. i. § ii.).</p>
