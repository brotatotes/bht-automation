<b> 4.]</b> The report of one of those sent: perhaps of Peter. The word rendered<b> a place where two ways met</b>, only means,<i> a road leading round a place</i>, and probably imports simply<b> the street</b>. Wordsw. interprets it, ‘<i><b>the back way,</b><b> w</b><b> hich led round the house.</b></i>’ But there does not appear to be any reason for supposing the word “<i>round</i>” to refer to the<i> house</i>, rather than to the whole block, or neighbourhood, of houses, round about which the street led. Dean Trench, on the A.V. p. 116, would render it “<i>a way round</i>,” “<i>a crooked lane</i>.”
