<b> 34. not far...]</b> This man had hold of that principle in which Law and Gospel are one: he stood as it were<i> at the door</i> of the Kingdom of God. He only wanted (but the want was indeed a serious one) repentance and faith to be<i> within it</i>. The Lord shews us here that even outside His flock, those who can answer<b> discreetly</b> (or<b> intelligently</b>)—who have knowledge of the<i> spirit</i> of the great command of Law and Gospel, are<i> nearer</i> to being of his flock, than the<i> formalists:</i>—but then, as Bengel adds, “If thou art not far off, come in: otherwise thou hadst better been far off.”
