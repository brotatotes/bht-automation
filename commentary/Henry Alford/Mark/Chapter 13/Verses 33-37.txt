	The  θυρωρ  . is the  door-porter, whose office it would be to look out for approaching travellers, answering especially to the ministers of the word, who are (Ezekiel 33:0 )  watchmen to God’s church.
	The construction of Mar 13:34 is remarkable; the participial clauses being in subordination to ἀφείς , and constituting part of the householder’s arrangements of departure, and the direct tense being assumed at ἐνετείλατο , as signifying what took place at his very going out of the door, where the porter would be stationed: as if it had been ἀφεὶς τ . οἰκ . αὐτοῦ ( καί , &c.) ἐνετείλατο κ . τ . λ .
