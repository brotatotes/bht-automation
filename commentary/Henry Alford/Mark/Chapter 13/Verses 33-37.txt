<p><strong> 33 37.</strong> ] Peculiar to Mark, and containing the condensed matter of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:43-47">Matthew 24:43-47</span> , and perhaps an allusion to the parable of the talents in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:0">Matthew 25:0</span> .
	</p>
<p>The <strong> θυρωρ</strong> <strong> .</strong> is the <strong> door-porter,</strong> whose office it would be to look out for approaching travellers, answering especially to the ministers of the word, who are (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eze+33:0">Ezekiel 33:0</span> ) <em> watchmen</em> to God’s church.
	</p>
<p>The construction of Mar 13:34 is remarkable; the participial clauses being in subordination to ἀφείς , and constituting part of the householder’s arrangements of departure, and the direct tense being assumed at ἐνετείλατο , as signifying what took place at his very going out of the door, where the porter would be stationed: as if it had been ἀφεὶς τ . οἰκ . αὐτοῦ ( καί , &amp;c.) ἐνετείλατο κ . τ . λ .</p>
