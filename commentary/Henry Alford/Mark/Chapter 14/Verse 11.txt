The only matters requiring notice are,—the words<b> when they heard</b> [it], i.e. ‘<i>the proposal</i>,’—and promised, implying, as does the word in Luke, that the money was<i> not paid now</i>, either as full wages, or as earnest-money,—but<i> promised</i>; and paid (most probably) when the Lord was brought before the Sanhedrim, which was what Judas undertook to do.
