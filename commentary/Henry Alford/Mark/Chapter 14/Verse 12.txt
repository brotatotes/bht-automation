<b> 12.]</b><b> when they killed the passover</b>, like St. Luke’s expression “<i>when the passover must be killed,</i>” denotes the ordinary day, when they (i. e. the Jews) sacrificed the Passover;—for that the Lord ate His Passover on that day, and at the usual time, is<i> the impression conveyed by the testimony the three Evangelists:</i> see notes on ew ver. 17, and Luke ver. 7.
