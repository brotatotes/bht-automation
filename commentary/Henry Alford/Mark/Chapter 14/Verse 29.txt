<p><strong> 29.</strong> ] <strong> εἰ καὶ πάντες</strong> <strong> if even all:</strong> καὶ εἰ πάντες ‘ <em> even if all</em> .’ The καί before εἰ intensifies the whole hypothesis: the και after εἰ intensifies only that word which it introduces in the hypothesis. See Klotz on Devar. p. 519 f.: where however the account is not quite as clear as might be desired. <strong> ἀλλά</strong> has here its full adversative exceptional force <strong> notwithstanding:</strong> cf. Il. θ . 153, 154, εἴπερ γάρ σ ʼ Ἕκτωρ γε κακὸν καὶ ἀνάλκιδα φήσει , <strong> ἀλλ</strong> <strong> ʼ</strong> οὐ πείσονται Τρῶες καὶ Δαρδανίωνες : and Klotz on Devar. p. 93.</p>
