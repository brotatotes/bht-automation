<p><strong> 41. </strong> <strong> ἀπέχει</strong> ] Scil. your γρηγορεῖν μετ ʼ ἐμοῦ . The Lord had no need of it any more, now that the hour had come: not, as Bengel, Kuinoel, alli [45] ., ‘Satis somnorum est:’ this, as Meyer observes, is refuted by the καθεύδετε λοιπόν . This meaning of ἀπέχει , <em> sufficit</em> , is found in very few and late, but those quite sufficient examples. Meyer mentions Pseud.-Anacreon, Od. xxviii. 33, <strong> ἀπέχει</strong> <strong> ,</strong> βλέπω γὰρ αὐτήν : and Cyril on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="hag+2:9">Haggai 2:9</span> , ἐμὸν φησὶ τὸ ἀργύριον καὶ ἐμὸν τὸ χρυσίον · τουτέστιν <strong> ἀπέχει</strong> <strong> ,</strong> καὶ πεπλήρωμαι , καὶ δεδέημαι τῶν τοιούτων οὐδενός .
	</p>
