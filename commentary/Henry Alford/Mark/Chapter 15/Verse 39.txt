<p><strong> οὕτως</strong> οὕτω δεσποτικῶς , Thl. There was something in the manner of this last cry so unusual and superhuman, that the Centurion (see on Matt.) was convinced that He must have been <em> that</em> <em> Person</em> , whom He was accused as having declared Himself to be. Observe the Latin <strong> κεντυρίων</strong> = ἑκατόνταρχος in [60] Matt. Luke.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [60] When, in the Gospels, and in the Evangelic statement, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:23-25">1 Corinthians 11:23-25</span> , the sign (║) occurs in a reference, it is signified that the word occurs <em> in the parallel place</em> in the other Gospels, which will always be found indicated <em> at the head of the note</em> on the paragraph. When the sign (║) is <em> qualified</em> , thus, ‘║ Mk.,’ or ‘║ Mt. Mk.,’ &amp;c., it is signified that the word occurs <em> in the parallel place in that Gospel or Gospels, but not in the other or others</em> .</p>
