<b> 44.]</b> There is no inconsistency, or but a very trifling one, with the order in John, ver. 31,<i> to break their legs and take them down</i>. The circumstances related there<i> had taken place</i>, but no<i> report of them had been made</i> to Pilate. And the Body of the Lord had not been taken down, for some reason which does not appear, but which we can easily guess:—if Joseph had declared to the soldiers his intention of begging the Body, nay, had immediately gone (perhaps with them) to Pilate for that purpose,—and<b> went in boldly</b> looks like a sudden and unannounced application,—they would have left the Body for him to take down.
