<p><strong> 44.</strong> ] There is no inconsistency, or but a very trifling one, with the order in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:31">John 19:31</span> , <em> to break their legs and take them down</em> . The circumstances related there <em> had taken place</em> , but no <em> report of them had been made</em> to Pilate. And the Body of the Lord had not been taken down, for some reason which does not appear, but which we can easily guess; if Joseph had declared to the soldiers his intention of begging the Body, nay, had immediately gone (perhaps with them) to Pilate for that purpose, and <strong> τολμήσας εἰσῆλθ</strong> <strong> .</strong> looks like a sudden and unannounced application, they would have left the Body for him to take down.
	</p>
