<p><strong> 46. </strong> <strong> ἀγορ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] Therefore it was <em> not the first day of unleavened bread</em> , which was one of <em> sabbatical sanctity;</em> as indeed the whole of this narrative shews, but such expressions as this more strikingly.
	</p>
<p><strong> καθαιρεῖν</strong> is the technical word for taking down bodies from the cross. See the examples in Kypke from Philo and Josephus. So is κατατιθέναι for placing bodies in the tomb: cf. ibid.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐν μνημ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] It is not said, but <em> implied</em> , both here and in Luke and John, that the tomb was <em> his own</em> for how should he place the Body there otherwise? The <em> newness</em> of the tomb is not mentioned here, but by the other three Evangelists.</p>
