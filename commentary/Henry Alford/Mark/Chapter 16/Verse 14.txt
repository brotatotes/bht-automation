<p><strong> 14.</strong> ] The following narrative, evidently intended by its author to represent what took place <em> at one and the same time</em> , joins together in one at least <em> four</em> appearances of the Lord: (1) that related in this verse and <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:36-49">Luke 24:36-49</span> ; (2) that on the mountain in Galilee ( Mat 28:16-20 ), when the words in Mar 16:15 were spoken; (3) some unrecorded appearance when the rest of these words ( Mar 16:16-18 ) were spoken, unless we consider the whole to have been said on the mountain in Galilee; and (4) the appearance which terminated with the Ascension.
	</p>
<p>The latter part of this Mar 16:14 appears to be an epitome of what our Lord said to them on several occasions see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:25">Luke 24:25</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:38">Luke 24:38</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:27">John 20:27</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:17">Matthew 28:17</span> .</p>
