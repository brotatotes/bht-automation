<b> 7.] But</b> breaks off the discourse and turns to a new matter—<b>But now rather</b> do ye...<br><br><b> and Peter]</b> It is hardly perhaps likely that the<i> denial of Peter</i> was the ground of this message, though it is difficult not to connect the two in the mind. The mention of him here is probably merely official—as the ‘first among equals’ We cannot say that others of the Apostles may not have denied their Master besides Peter.<br><br> It must not be concluded from this that we have a trace of Peter’s hand in the narrative.
