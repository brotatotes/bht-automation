<b> 19.]</b> The repetition in the last clause,<i> contained neither in Matthew nor Luke</i>, is inconsistent with the design of an abridger; and sufficiently shews the primary authority of this report, as also in that day, ver. 20. St. Mark especially loves these solemn repetitions: compare ch. ix. 42 ff. It is strange to see such a Commentator as De Wette calling the repetition,<i> in that day, a proof of carelessness.</i> It is a touching way, as Meyer well observes, of expressing ‘in that<i> dark day</i>.’
