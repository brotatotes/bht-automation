<p><strong> 40.</strong> ] How capricious, according to modern criticism, must this Evangelist have been, who compiled his narrative out of Matt. and Luke, adding minute particulars in leaving out here εἰδότες ὅτι ἀπέθανεν (Luke), <em> a detail so essential</em> , if Mark had really been what he is represented. Can testimony be stronger to the untenableness of such a view, and the independence of his narration? And yet such abound in every chapter.</p>
