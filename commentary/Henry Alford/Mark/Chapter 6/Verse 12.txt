<p><strong> 12.</strong> ] It is impossible to restrict the <strong> ἵνα</strong> after <strong> ἐκήρυξαν</strong> entirely to the telic meaning, as Meyer, who is a purist on this point, attempts to do. There is certainly the mingling of the purport and the purpose, so often found in this particle after verbs implying declaration or request. See this treated of in note, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:13">1 Corinthians 14:13</span> .</p>
