<b> 13.] anointed with oil</b>—this oil was not<i> medicinally</i>, but as a<i> vehicle of healing power committed to them</i>;—a symbol of a deeper thing than the oil itself could accomplish. That such anointing has nothing in common with the extreme unction of Romanists, see proved in note on James v. 14, See for instances of such symbolic use of external applications, 2 Kings v. 14: Mark viii. 23: John ix. 6, &c.
