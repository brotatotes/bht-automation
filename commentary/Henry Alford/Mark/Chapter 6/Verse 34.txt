<b> 34.]</b><b> when he came out</b>, i.e.<b> had disembarked</b>, most probably. Meyer would render it, ‘<i>having come forth from his solitude</i>’ in Matthew,—and ‘<i>having disembarked</i>’ here: but I very much doubt the former. There is nothing in Matthew to imply that He had<i> reached his place of solitude before</i> the multitudes came up. John indeed, vv. 3–7,<i> seems</i> to imply this; but He may very well have mounted the hill or cliff from the sea before He<b> saw</b> the multitudes, and this would be<i> on his disembarkation</i>.
