<p><strong> 34.</strong> ] He looked to heaven <em> in prayer</em> : see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:41-42">John 11:41-42</span> . He sighed, as Chrysostom (or Pseudo-Chrys.) in Cramer’s Catena, h. l., says, τὴν τοῦ ἀνθρώπου φύσιν ἐλεῶν , ἐς ποίαν ταπείνωσιν ἤγαγεν αὐτὴν ὅ τε μισόκαλος διάβολος , καὶ ἡ τῶν πρωτοπλάστων ἀπροσεξία : see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:36-38">John 11:36-38</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐφφαθά</strong> = אֶתְסַּתַּח (Sy [30] .-chald.), imperative Hithp. from סָּתַח , <em> aperuit</em> : the word used in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="isa+35:5">Isaiah 35:5</span> , “ <em> Then shall the ears of the deaf be unstopped, … and the tongue of the dumb sing</em> .”
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [30] yr the Peschito (or simple) Syriac version. Supposed to have been made as early as the <em> second century</em> . The text as edited is in a most unsatisfactory state.</p>
