<p><strong> 38.</strong> ] Only found besides in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:49-50">Luke 9:49-50</span> .
	</p>
<p>Notice the <em> repetition</em> of <strong> οὐκ ἀκολ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἡμ</strong> <strong> .</strong> as characteristic of Mark. The connexion of this remark with what goes before, is: ‘If the receiving any one, even a little child, <em> in thy Name</em> , be receiving Thee; were we doing right when we forbade one who <em> used thy Name</em> , but did not follow us?’ “Observent hoc,” says Bengel, “ <em> qui charismata alligant successioni canonicæ</em> .” This man actually did what the very Apostles themselves were specially appointed to do: and our Lord, so far from prohibiting, encourages him: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="nu+11:26-29">Numbers 11:26-29</span> .</p>
