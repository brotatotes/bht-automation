<b> 49.]</b> In order to understand this difficult verse, it will be necessary first to examine its connexion and composition. (1) What is<b> for</b>? It connects it with the solemn assertions in vv. 43–48,<b> it is better for thee</b>…, and furnishes a<i> reason why</i> it<b> is</b> better for us to cut off and cast away, &c.
