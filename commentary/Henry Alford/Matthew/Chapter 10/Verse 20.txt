<b> 20. For it is not ye....</b>] This shews the reference of the to a<i> future</i> mission of the Apostles, see John xv. 26, 27. (1) It is to be observed that our Lord never in speaking to His disciples says<i> our</i> Father, but either<i> my</i> Father (ch. xviii. 10), or<i> your</i> Father (as here), or both conjoined (John xx. 17); never leaving it to be inferred that God is in the same sense His Father and our Fa-ther. (2) It is also to be observed that in the great work of God in the world, human individuality sinks down and vanishes, and God alone, His Christ, His Spirit, is the worker.
