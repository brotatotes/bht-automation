<p><strong> 27.</strong> ] <em> An expansion of the duty of freeness and boldness of speech implied in the last verse</em> . The words may bear <em> two meanings:</em> either (1) that which Chrysostom gives, taking the expressions relatively, ἐπειδὴ μόνοις αὐτοῖς διελέγετο καὶ ἐν μικρᾷ γωνίᾳ τῆς Παλαιστίνης , διὰ τοῦτο εἶπεν “ ἐν τῇ σκοτίᾳ ,” καὶ “ εἰς τὸ οὖς ,” πρὸς τὴν μετὰ ταῦτα παῤῥησίαν ἐσομένην , Hom. xxxiv. 2, p. 390; or (2) as this part of the discourse relates to the <em> future</em> principally, the <em> secret speaking</em> may mean the communication which our Lord would hold with them hereafter by His Spirit, which they were to preach and proclaim. See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:20">Acts 4:20</span> . These senses do not exclude one another, and are possibly both implied.
	</p>
<p>There is no need, with Lightfoot and others, to suppose any allusion to a custom in the synagogue, in the words <strong> εἰς τὸ οὖς ἀκούετε</strong> <strong> .</strong> They are a common expression derived from common life: we have it in a wider sense <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:22">Acts 11:22</span> , and <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ge+50:4">Genesis 50:4</span> .
	</p>
