<b> 29. sparrows</b>] any small birds.<b> a farthing</b>] Gr.<i> assarion</i>. This word, derived from ‘<i>as,</i>’ was used in Greek and Hebrew to signify the meanest, most insignificant amount.<br><br><b> fall on the ground</b>] which birds do when struck violently, or when<i> frozen</i>, wet or starved: it is therefore equivalent to die: “<i>not one of them is forgotten before God,</i>” Luke xii. 6.
