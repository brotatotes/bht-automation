<p><strong> 40.</strong> ] Here in the conclusion of the discourse, the Lord recurs again to His Apostles whom He was sending out. From Mat 10:32 has been connected with πᾶς ὅστις , and therefore general.
	</p>
<p><strong> δέχεται</strong> <strong> ,</strong> see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:14">Matthew 10:14</span> ; but it has here the wider sense of not only receiving to house and board, but <em> receiving</em> in heart and life <em> the message</em> of which the Apostles were the bearers. On the sense of the verse, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:21">John 20:21</span> , and on <strong> τὸν ἀποστείλαντά με</strong> <strong> ,</strong> ἐγὼ ἀποστέλλω ὑμᾶς , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:16">Matthew 10:16</span> , and <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:1">Hebrews 3:1</span> . There is a difference between the representation of Christ by His messengers, which at most is only official, and even then broken by personal imperfection and infirmity (see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:11">Galatians 2:11</span> ; Gal 4:13-14 ), and the perfect unbroken representation of the Eternal Father by His Blessed Son, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:9">John 14:9</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:3">Hebrews 1:3</span> .</p>
