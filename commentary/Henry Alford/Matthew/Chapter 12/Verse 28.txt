<p><strong> 28.</strong> ] <strong> ἐν πνεύματι θ</strong> <strong> .</strong> = ἐν δακτύλῳ θ ., Luke; see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ex+8:19">Exodus 8:19</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἔφθασεν</strong> ] emphatic in position: but merely, <strong> has come upon you:</strong> not in the more proper sense of φθάνω , ‘is <em> already</em> upon you,’ i.e. ‘before you looked for it,’ as Stier and Wesley. It does not seem to occur in this latter sense in the N.T. But Fritzsche’s dictum, ad Rom. 2:356, “Alexandrinis scriptoribus φθάνειν nihil nisi <em> venire, pervenire, pertinere</em> valet,” certainly is not right; for we have it indisputably in the sense of to <em> anticipate, prevent</em> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:15">1 Thessalonians 4:15</span> .</p>
