<p><strong> 40.</strong> ] If it be necessary to make good the three days and nights during which our Lord was in the heart of the earth, it must be done by having recourse to the Jewish method of computing time. In the Jerusalem Talmud (cited by Lightfoot) it is said “that a day and night together make up a עוֹנָה (a νυχθήμερον ), and that any part of such a period is counted as the whole.” See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ge+40:13">Genesis 40:13</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ge+40:20">Genesis 40:20</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1sa+30:12-13">1 Samuel 30:12-13</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2ch+10:5">2 Chronicles 10:5</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2ch+10:12">2 Chronicles 10:12</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ho+6:2">Hosea 6:2</span> .</p>
