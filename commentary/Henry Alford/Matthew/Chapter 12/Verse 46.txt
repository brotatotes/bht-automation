<b> 46.</b>] In Mark iii. 21 we are told that his relations<i> went out to lay hold on Him, for they said, He is beside Himself</i>: and that the reason of this was his continuous labour in teaching, which<i> had not left time so much as to eat</i>. There is nothing in this care for his bodily health (from whatever source the act may have arisen on the part of his<i> brethren</i>, see John vii. 5) inconsistent with the known state of his<i> mother's</i> mind (see Luke ii. 19, 51).
