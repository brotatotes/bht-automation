<b> 5.</b>] The priests were ordered to offer double offerings on the Sabbath (Num. xxviii. 9, 10), and to place fresh (<i>hot</i>, and therefore baked that day) shewbread. In performing these commands they must commit many of what the Pharisees would call profanations of the Sabbath. So that, as Stier (ii. 4), not only does the sacred<i> history</i> furnish examples of exception to the law of the Sabbath from<i> necessity</i>, but the<i> Law itself ordains</i> work to be done on the Sabbath as a<i> duty</i>.
