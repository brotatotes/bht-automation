<p><strong> 11.</strong> ] The Kingdom of Heaven, like other Kingdoms, has its secrets ( <strong> μυστήριον</strong> <strong> ,</strong> see a definition by St. Paul in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:25">Romans 16:25</span> f., viz. χρόνοις αἰωνίοις σεσιγημένον , φανερωθὲν δὲ νῦν ) and inner counsels, which strangers must not know. These are only revealed to the humble diligent hearers, <strong> ὑμῖν</strong> <strong> :</strong> to those who were immediately around the Lord with the twelve; not <strong> ἐκείνοις</strong> = τοῖς λοιποῖς Luke, = ἐκείνοις τοῖς ἔξω Mark. (<span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:12-13">1 Corinthians 5:12-13</span> .)
	</p>
<p><strong> οὐ δέδοται</strong> = ἐν παραβολαῖς Luke, and τὰ πάντα γίνεται Mark.</p>
