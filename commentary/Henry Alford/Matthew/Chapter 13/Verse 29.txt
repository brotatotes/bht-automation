<b> 29.</b>] Jerome in loc, says: “Between wheat and tares, which we call<i> lolium</i>, as long as both are in the blade, and the stalk is not yet in ear, there is a great similitude, and discrimination is difficult, if not impossible.” Jerome, it must be remembered, resided in Palestine.<br><br> The comparison of kingdoms to<i> trees</i> was familiar to the Jews; see Daniel iv. 10–12, 20–22: Ezek, xxxi. 3–9; xvii. 22–24: Ps. lxxx. 8–11.
