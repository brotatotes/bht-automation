<b> 19. blessed</b>] St. Luke supplies “<i>them</i>,” i.e. the loaves and fishes: St. John has for it<b> gave thanks</b>. Both are one. The thanks to heaven is the blessing on the meat. This miracle was one of symbolic meaning for the Twelve, who had just returned from their mission, as pointing to the “<i>freely ye received, freely give</i>” of ch. x. 8 in a higher sense than they then could have understood it:—but see the symbolic import of the miracle treated in the notes to John vi.
