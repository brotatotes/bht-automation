<b> 4.</b>] The marriage was unlawful for these three reasons: (1)<i> The former husband of Herodias</i>, Philip,<i> was still living</i>. This is expressly asserted by Josephus. (2)<i> The former wife of Antipas was still living</i>, and fled to her father Aretas on hearing of his intention to marry Herodias. (3)<i> Antipas and Herodias were already related to one another within the forbidden degrees of consanguinity</i>. For she was daughter of Aristobulus, the brother of Antipas and Philip.
