<b> 15.</b>] The saying in ver. 11, which is clearly the subject of the question, was not strictly a<i> parable</i>, but a plain declaration; so that either Peter<i> took it</i> for a parable,—or the word must be taken in its wider sense of ‘an hard saying.’ Stier thinks that their questioning as to the meaning of parables in ch. xiii. had habituated them to asking for explanations in this form.
