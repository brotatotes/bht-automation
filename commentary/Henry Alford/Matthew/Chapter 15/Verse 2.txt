<b> 2.</b>] The Jews attached more importance to the traditionary exposition than to the Scripture text itself. They compared the written word to water; the traditionary exposition to the wine which must be mingled with it.<br><br> The duty of washing before meat is not inculcated in the law, but only in the traditions of the Scribes. So rigidly did the Jews observe it, that Rabbi Akiba, being imprisoned, and having water scarcely sufficient to sustain lifo given him, preferred dying of thirst to eating without washing his hands.<br><br> The “<i>elders</i>” here, as in Heb. xi. 2, must be taken to mean<b> the ancients</b>.
