<p><strong> 14.</strong> ] It is no contradiction to this verdict that some called him <em> the Son of David</em> (ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:27">Matthew 9:27</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:23">Matthew 12:23</span> ; Mat 15:22 ); for either these were or were about to become His disciples, or are quoted as examples of rare faith, or as in ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:23">Matthew 12:23</span> , it was the passing doubt on the minds of the multitude, not their settled opinion. The same may be said of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:26">John 7:26</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:31">John 7:31</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:42">John 4:42</span> . On our Lord’s being taken for John the Baptist, see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:2">Matthew 14:2</span> , from which this would appear to be the opinion of <em> the Herodians</em> .

It was not a metempsychosis, but a bodily resurrection which was believed. On Ἡλίαν , see note at ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:14">Matthew 11:14</span> .
