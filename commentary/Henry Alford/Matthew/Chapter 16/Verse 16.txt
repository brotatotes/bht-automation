<b> 16.</b>] The word<b> living</b> must not for a moment be taken here as it sometimes is used, (e. g. Acts xiv. 15,) as merely distinguishing the true God from dead idols: it is here emphatic, and imparts force and precision to<b> Son</b>.<br><br> That Peter when he uttered the words, understood by them in detail all that we now understand, is not of course asserted: bot that they were his testimony to the true Humanity and true Divinity of the Lord, in that sense of deep truth and reliance, out of which springs the Christian life of the Church.
