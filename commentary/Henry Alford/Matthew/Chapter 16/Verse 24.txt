<p><strong> 24.</strong> ] προσκαλεσάμενος τὸν ὄχλον σὺν τοῖς μαθ . αὐτοῦ , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:34">Mark 8:34</span> ; ἔλεγε δὲ πρὸς πάντας , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:23">Luke 9:23</span> . This discourse is a solemn sequel to our Lord’s announcement respecting Himself and the rebuke of Peter: teaching that not only He, but also His followers, must suffer and self-deny; that they all have a life to save, more precious than all else to them; and that the great day of account of that life’s welfare should be ever before them. On this and the following verse, see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:38-39">Matthew 10:38-39</span> . After <strong> τὸν στ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> αὐτοῦ</strong> <strong> ,</strong> Luke inserts καθ ʼ ἡμέραν .</p>
