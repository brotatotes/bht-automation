<p><strong> 25.</strong> ] <strong> τίς</strong> <strong> ,</strong> not τίς πλούσιος , which would have been a far shallower and narrower enquiry, but a general question <strong> what man?</strong> Besides the usual reason given for this question, ‘ <em> since all are striving to be rich</em> ,’ we must remember that the disciples yet looked for a temporal Kingdom, and therefore would naturally be dismayed at hearing that it was so difficult for any rich man to enter it.</p>
