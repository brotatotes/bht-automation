<p><strong> 8. </strong> <strong> πορευθέντες</strong> <strong> … </strong> <strong> ἐλθών</strong> ] The pleonastic use of these words, common as a Hebraism in the N.T. (see reff.), is also idiomatic in English; and it may be remarked, that although not strictly needed in the sentences where they occur, their insertion always gives fulness and accuracy to the meaning.</p>
