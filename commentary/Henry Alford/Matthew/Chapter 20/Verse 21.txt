<p><strong> 21.</strong> ] The <em> places close to the throne</em> were those of <em> honour</em> , as in Josephus, speaking of Saul (Antt. vi. 11. 9), τοῦ μὲν παιδὸς Ἰωνάθου ἐκ δεξιῶν , Ἀβενήρου δὲ τοῦ ἀρχιστρατήγου ἐκ τῶν ἑτέρων … In the Rabbinical work Midrasch Tehillim, cited by Wetstein, God, it is said, will seat the King Messiah at His right hand, and Abraham at his left.
	</p>
<p><em> One of these brethren</em> , John, the beloved disciple, had his usual place close to the Lord, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:23">John 13:23</span> ; <em> the other</em> was among the chosen Three (this request hardly can imply in their minds any idea of the rejection of Peter from his peculiar post of honour by the rebuke in ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:23">Matthew 16:23</span> , for since then had happened the occurrences in ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:1-8">Matthew 17:1-8</span> , and especially ib. Mat 17:24-27 ). <em> Both</em> were called Boanerges, or the sons of thunder, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:17">Mark 3:17</span> .
	</p>
<p>They thought the kingdom of God was <em> immediately to appear</em> , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:11">Luke 19:11</span> .</p>
