<b> 23.]</b> The<i> one</i> of these brethren was the<i> first of the Apostles to drink the cup Of suffering, and be baptized with the baptism of blood,</i> Acts xii. 1, 2: the<i> other had the longest experience among them of a life of trouble and persecution.</i><br><br> The last clause of the verse may be understood as in the text, ‘is not mine to give, but it shall be given to them for whom it is prepared of my Father;’ So Meyer, al.; or, ‘is not mine to give,<i> except</i> to those for whom,’&. So Chrysostom and others. If however we understand after but ‘it shall be given<i> by Me,’</i> the two interpretations come to the same.
