<p><strong> 25.</strong> ] The two clauses, … <strong> κατακυρ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> αὐτῶν</strong> and … <strong> κατεξ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> αὐτῶν</strong> <strong> ,</strong> are parallel, and αὐτῶν in both cases refers to τῶν ἐθνῶν . Grotius and others would take the second αὐτῶν to refer to οἱ ἄρχοντες , but wrongly.
	</p>
<p>Observe the <strong> κατα</strong> in composition in both verbs, signifying subjugation and oppression.</p>
