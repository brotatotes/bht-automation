<p><strong> 12.</strong> ] Compare the notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:13-18">John 2:13-18</span> . The cleansing related in our text is <em> totally distinct</em> from that related there. It is impossible to suppose that St. Matthew and St. John, or any one but moderately acquainted with the events which he undertook to relate, should have made such a gross error in chronology, as must be laid to the charge of one or other of them, if these two occurrences were <em> the same</em> . I rather view the omission of the first in the synoptic accounts as in remarkable consistency with what we otherwise gather from the three Gospels that their narrative is <em> exclusively Galilæan</em> [with one exception, Luk 4:44 in our text] <em> until this last journey to Jerusalem</em> , and consequently the first cleansing is passed over by them (see Prolegomena, circa init.). On the difference from Mark, see note on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:1">Matthew 21:1</span> . Both comings of Jehovah to His temple were partial fulfilments of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mal+3:1-3">Malachi 3:1-3</span> , which shall not receive its final accomplishment till His great and decisive visit at the latter day. The <strong> ἱερόν</strong> here spoken of was the <em> court of the Gentiles</em> .
