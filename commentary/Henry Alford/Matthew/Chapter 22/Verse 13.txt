These<b> servants</b> (<i>diaconoi</i>,<b> ministers</b>) are not the same as the above, but<i> the angels</i>, see ch. xiii. 41, 49. The ‘binding of his feet and hands’ has been interpreted of his being now<i> in the night</i>,<i> in which no man can work</i>; but I doubt whether this be not too fanciful. Rather should we say, with Meyer, that it is to render his escape from the outer darkness impossible.
