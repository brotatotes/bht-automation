 2.] The<i> householder</i> of the former parable is the KING here, who makes a<i> marriage</i> for his Son. The word thus rendered not always necessarily “a marriage,’ but any great celebration, as accession to the throne, or coming of age, &c. Here however the notion of a marriage is<i> certainly included;</i> and the interpretation is,<i> the great marriage supper</i> (Rev. xix. 9)<i> of the Son of God:</i> i.e. His fall and complete union to His Bride the Church in glory: which would be to the guests the ultimate result of accepting the invitation. See Eph. v. 25–27. The difficulty, of the<i> totality of the guests</i> in this case constituting<i> the Bride,</i> may be lessened by regarding the ceremony as an enthronization, in which the people are regarded as being espoused to their prince. On the whole imagery, compare Ps. xlv.
