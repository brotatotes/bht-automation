<b> 11.</b>] It may serve to shew us how little the letter of a precept has to do with its true observance, if we reflect that he who of all the Heads of sects has most notably violated this whole command, and caused others to do so, calls himself ‘<i>servus servorum Dei</i>’ ( “servant of the servants of God”).—It must be noted (see margin) that the word here rendered “<i>servant</i>” in the A.V., is not that usually so translated (<i>doulos</i>), which properly means<i> slave</i> or<i> bondsman:</i> but<i> diaconos</i>, which is in the same version rendered<i> minister</i> in chap. xx. 26.
