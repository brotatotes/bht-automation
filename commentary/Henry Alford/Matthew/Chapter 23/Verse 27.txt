<b> 27</b>.] The Jews used once a year (on the fifteenth of the month Adar)<i> to whitewash the spots where graves were</i>, that persons might not be liable to uncleanness by passing over them (see Num. xix. 16). This goes to the root of the mischief at once: “your heart is not a temple of the living God, but a grave of pestilent corruption: not a heaven, but a hell. And your religion is but the whitewash—hardly skin-deep.’
