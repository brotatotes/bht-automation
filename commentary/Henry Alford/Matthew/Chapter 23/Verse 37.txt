 37.] These words were before spoken by our Lord, Luke xiii. 34: see notes there, and compare ch. xxiv. 28.<br><br><b> how often would I have gathered</b> must be understood of all the messages of repentance and mercy sent by the prophets, for our Lord’s words embrace the whole time comprised in the historic survey of ver. 35, as well as His own ministry. On the similitude, see Deut. xxxii. 11: Ps. xvii. 8; xxxvi. 7; lvii. 1; lxi. 4: Isa, xxxi. 5: Mal. iv. 2.<br><br> ye would not] See Isa. xxviii. 12; xxx. 15, The tears of our Lord over the perverseness of Jerusalem are witnesses of<i> the freedom of man’s will to resist the grace of God.</i><br><br><b> your house</b>—no more<i> God's</i>, but<b> your house</b>—said primarily of the temple,—then of Jerusalem,—and then of the whole land in which ye dwell.<br><br><b> Ye shall not see me</b>—He did not shew Himself to all the people after His resurrection, but only to chosen witnesses, Acts x. 41.<br><br><b> till ye shall say]</b> until that day, the subject of all prophecy, when your repentant people shall turn with true and loyal Hosannas and blessings to greet ‘Him whom they have pierced: see Deut. iv. 30, 31: Hosea iii. 4, 5: Zech. xii. 10; xiv. 8–11. Stier well remarks, ‘He who reads not this in the prophets, reads not yet the prophets aright.’<i></i>
