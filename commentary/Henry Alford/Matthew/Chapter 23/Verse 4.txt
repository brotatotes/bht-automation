<p><strong> 4.</strong> ] The warning was, <em> imitate</em> them not for they do not themselves what they enjoin on others. And this verse must be strictly connected with <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:3">Matthew 23:3</span> . The <strong> φορτία</strong> then are not, as so often misinterpreted (even by Olshausen, i. 834), <em> human traditions</em> and observances, but the <em> severity of the law</em> , which they enforce on others, but do not observe (see Rom 2:21-23 ): answering to the βαρύτερα τοῦ νόμου of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:23">Matthew 23:23</span> . The irksomeness and unbearableness of these rites did not belong to the Law <em> in itself</em> , as rightly explained, but were created by the rigour and ritualism of these men, who followed the letter and lost the spirit: ‘omnem operam impendebant (says Grotius) ritibus urgendis et ampliandis.’
	</p>
<p><strong> τῷ δακ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> αὐτῶν</strong> <strong> ,</strong> not αὑτῶν : the emphasis is not on the pronoun, but on the <strong> δακτύλῳ</strong> <strong> .</strong> As a general rule, when the pron. is simply reflexive, the smooth breathing should always be printed.</p>
