<p><strong> 25 28.</strong> ] This woe is founded not on a literally, but a typically denoted practice of the Pharisees. Our Lord, in the ever-deepening denunciation of His discourse, has now arrived at the delineation of their <em> whole</em> character and practices by a parabolic similitude.
	</p>
<p><strong> γέμουσιν ἐξ</strong> ] not, ‘ <em> are filled by</em> ’ (Dr. Burton), but, <strong> are full of:</strong> מָלֵא מִן in Hebrew. The straining out of the gnat is a cleansing pertaining to the ἔξωθεν , as compared with the <em> inner composition of the wine itself</em> , of which the cup is full: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="re+18:3">Revelation 18:3</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἵνα γέν</strong> <strong> .</strong> The exterior is not in reality pure when the interior is foul: it is not ‘a clean cup,’ unless both exterior and interior be clean: ‘alias enim illa mundities externa non est mundities.’ Bengel.
	</p>
<p>Observe, the emphasis is on γένηται : “that its exterior also may not appear to be, but really <em> become</em> , pure.”
	</p>
<p><strong> τάφ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> κεκον</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] The Jews used once a year (on the fifteenth of the month Adar) to <em> whitewash the spots where graves were</em> , that persons might not be liable to uncleanness by passing over them (see Num 19:16 ).
	</p>
<p>This goes to the root of the mischief at once: ‘your heart is not a temple of the living God, but a grave of pestilent corruption: not a heaven, but a hell. And your religion is but the whitewash hardly skin-deep.’</p>
