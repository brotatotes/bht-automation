 16 18. ] The Christian Jews are said (Euseb. H. E. iii. 5) to have fled to  Pella , a town described by Josephus (B. J. iii. 3. 3) as the northernmost boundary of Peræa. Eusebius says they were directed thither by a certain prophetic intimation ( τινὰ χρησμόν ), which however cannot be  this ; as Pella is not  on the mountains , but beyond them (but in order to reach it would not they have to fly exactly  ἐπὶ τὰ ὄρη over, along, across them? See note on ch. Mat 18:12 ): Epiphanius (de mensuris et pond. § 15, vol. ii. p. 171) that they προεχρηματίσθησαν ὑπὸ ἀγγέλου .
