<p><strong> 32.</strong> ] The expression <strong> πάντα τὰ ἔθνη</strong> implies <strong> all the nations of the world,</strong> as distinguished from the ἐκλεκτοί <em> already gathered to Him</em> , just as the Gentiles were by that name distinguished from his chosen people the Jews. Among these are “the other sheep which He has, not of this fold,” <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:16">John 10:16</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀφοριεῖ</strong> κ . τ . λ .] see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eze+34:17">Ezekiel 34:17</span> . The <strong> sheep</strong> are those referred to in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:7">Romans 2:7</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:10">Romans 2:10</span> ; the <strong> goats</strong> in ib. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:8-9">Romans 2:8-9</span> , where this same <em> judgment according to works</em> is spoken of.</p>
