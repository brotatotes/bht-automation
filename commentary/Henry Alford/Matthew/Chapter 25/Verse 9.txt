No man can have more of this provision than will supply his own wants.<br><br><b> go ye rather]</b> This is not said in mockery, as some suppose: but in earnest.<br><br><b> them that sell]</b> These are the ordinary dispensers of the means of grace—<i>ultimately</i> of course God Himself, who alone can give his Spirit. The counsel was good, and well followed—but the time was past. Observe that those who sell are a<i> particular class</i> of persons—no mean argument for a<i> set and appointed ministry</i>; and moreover for a<i> paid</i> ministry. If they<i> sell</i>, they<i> receive</i> for the thing sold: compare our Lord's saying, Luke x. 7. This<i> selling</i> bears no analogy with the crime of Simon Magus in Acts viii.: compare our Lord’s other saying, Matt. x. 8.
