<p><strong> 16 18.</strong> ] The increase gained by each of the two faithful servants was <em> the full amount of their talents</em> : of each will be required as much as has been given.
	</p>
<p>“ <strong> εἰργάσατο</strong> is the technical term, common in the classics, and especially in Demosthenes: see Reiske’s index. <strong> ἐν</strong> is instrumental.” Meyer.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐποίησεν</strong> is not a Latinism ( <em> conficere pecuniam</em> ), but answers to ποιεῖν καρπόν ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:10">Matthew 3:10</span> .
	</p>
<p>The <em> third servant here</em> is not to be confounded with the <em> wicked servant</em> in ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:48">Matthew 24:48</span> . This one is <em> not actively an ill-doer</em> , but a <em> hider</em> of the money entrusted to him one who brings no profit: see on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:24">Matthew 25:24</span> .</p>
