<b> 33.]</b> Nothing can bear a greater impress of exactitude than this reply. Peter had been before warned (see note on Luke, vv. 31–34); and still remaining in the same spirit of self-confident attachment, now that he is included among the<i> all</i>, not specially addressed,—breaks out into this asseveration, which carries completely with it the testimony that it was<i> not the first</i>. Men do not bring themselves out so strongly, unless their fidelity has been previously attainted.
