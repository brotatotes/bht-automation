<b> 36.]</b> St. Mark alone, besides our account, mentions the name of the place—St. Luke merely calls it “<i>the place,</i>” in allusion to “<i>as wont</i>” before. St. John informs us that it was<i> a garden</i>. The name signifies ‘<i>an oil press</i>.’ It was at the foot of the Mount of Olives, in the valley of the Kedron, the other side of the brook from the city (John xviii. 1).
