<b> 40.]</b> St. Mark agrees, except in relating the beginning of the address in the<i> singular</i>——no doubt accurately—for it was Peter ( “Simon, who was no<i> Peter</i> on this occasion,” Stier), who had<i> pledged himself to go with him to prison and death.</i><br><br> The question is literally<b> could ye thus not watch...</b>?—it implies their<i> utter inability,</i> as shewn by their present state of slumber,<b> Are ye so entirely unable</b>, &c.<br><br><b> one hour</b> need not imply that our Lord had been absent<i> a whole hour:</i>—if it is to be taken in any close meaning, it would be that the<i> whole trial would last</i> about that time.
