<p><strong> 42.</strong> ] Mark merely says of this second prayer, τὸν αὐτὸν λόγον εἰπών . Luke gives it as ἐκτενέστερον προσηύχετο and relates in addition, that His sweat was like the fall of drops of blood on the ground: see notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:44">Luke 22:44</span> . (At what precise time the angel appeared to Him is uncertain: I should be inclined to think, <em> after the first prayer</em> , before He came to his disciples.)
	</p>
<p>The words are not exactly the same: “the Lord knew that the Father always heard Him ( Joh 11:42 ); and therefore He understands the continuance of His trial as the answer to His last words, <strong> as Thou wilt.</strong> ” Stier. Here therefore the prayer is, <strong> If it be not possible.… thy will be done.</strong> It is spoken in the fulness of self-resignation. ‘Jam addita <em> bibendi</em> mentione, propius ad bibendum se confert.’ Bengel.</p>
