<p><strong> 47.</strong> ] Judas is specified as εἷς τῶν δώδεκα , probably because the appellation, as connected with this part of his history, had become the usual one thus we have in Luke ὁ λεγόμενος Ἰούδ . εἷς τῶν δώδεκα fuller still. To the <em> reader</em> , this specification is not without meaning, though that meaning may not have been intended.
	</p>
<p><strong> ὄχλος πολύς</strong> ] consisting of (1) a detachment of the Roman cohort which was quartered in the tower of Antonia during the feast in case of an uproar, called ἡ σπεῖρα , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:3">John 18:3</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:12">John 18:12</span> . (2) The ὑπηρέται of the council, the same as the στρατηγοὶ τοῦ ἱεροῦ , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:52">Luke 22:52</span> . (3) Servants and others deputed from the high-priest to assist, see our <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:51">Matthew 26:51</span> . (4) Possibly, if the words are to be taken exactly ( Luk 22:52 ), some of the chief priests and elders themselves, forward in zeal and enmity. There is nothing improbable in this (as Meyer, Schleiermacher, &amp;c. maintain), seeing that we have these persons mixing among the multitude and stirring them up to demand the crucifixion of Jesus afterwards.
	</p>
<p><strong> ξύλων</strong> ] not <em> clubs</em> but <strong> staves,</strong> or any tumultuary weapons. The intention of the chief priests evidently was to produce an impression to the effect that a seditious plot was to be crushed, and resistance might be expected. John mentions also <em> lanterns</em> and <em> torches</em> to search perhaps in the dark parts of the garden, most of which would by this time be in the shade.</p>
