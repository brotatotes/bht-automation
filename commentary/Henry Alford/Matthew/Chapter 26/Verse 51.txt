<b> 51.]</b> The<b> one of them... was</b><i> Peter</i>;—John ver. 10. Why he was not mentioned, is idle to enquire: one supposition only must be avoided—that there is any<i> purpose</i> in the omission. It is absurd to suppose that the mention of his name in a book current only among Christians, many years after the fact, could lead to his apprehension, which did not take place<i> at the time</i>, although he was recognized as the striker in the palace of the High Priest, John ver. 26. The real reason of the non-apprehension was that the servant was<i> healed</i> by the Lord.
