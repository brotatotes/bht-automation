<b> 53.]</b> The Majesty of our Lord, and His Patience, are both shewn here.<br><br><b> twelve</b>—not perhaps so much from the number of the apostles, who were now “<i>the eleven</i>,” but from that of the<i> then</i> company, viz. the Lord and the eleven.<br><br><b> legions</b>—because they were Roman soldiers who were taking Him. The complement of the legion was about 6000 men. The power implied in<b> thinkest thou that I cannot</b>, shews the entire and continued free self-resignation of the Lord throughout—and carries on the same truth as He expressed John x. 18.
