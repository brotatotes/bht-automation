<p><strong> 53.</strong> ] The Majesty of our Lord, and His Patience are both shewn here.
	</p>
<p><strong> πλείω δώδ</strong> <strong> .</strong> is a strictly Attic idiom, the neuter πλεῖον or πλείω , and the unchanged construction omitting the ἤ . So Plato, Legg. vi. p. 759, ἔτη μὴ ἔλαττον ἑξήκοντα γεγονώς : Paus. x. 57. 295, οἱ ἄνθρωποι πλέον ἡμίσεις ἁλιεῖς εἰσι . See the matter discussed, and more examples given, in Phryn. Lobeck, p. 410.
	</p>
<p><strong> δώδεκα</strong> not perhaps so much from the number of the Apostles, who were now οἱ ἕνδεκα , but from that of the <em> then</em> company, viz. the Lord and the eleven.
	</p>
<p><strong> λεγεῶνας</strong> because they were Roman soldiers who were taking Him. The complement of the legion was about 6000 men. The power, implied in <strong> δοκεῖς ὅτι οὐ δύναμαι</strong> <strong> ,</strong> shews the entire and continued free self-resignation of the Lord throughout and carries on the same truth as He expressed <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:18">John 10:18</span> .</p>
