<p><strong> 64.</strong> ] By <strong> σὺ εἶπας</strong> <strong> ,</strong> more may perhaps be implied than by Mark’s ἐγώ εἰμι : <em> that</em> is a simple assertion: <em> this</em> may refer to the convictions and <em> admissions</em> of Caiaphas (see Joh 11:49 ). But this is somewhat doubtful. The expression is only used here and in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:25">Matthew 26:25</span> : and there does not appear to be any reference in it as said to Judas, to any previous admission of his.
	</p>
<p><strong> πλήν</strong> ] <strong> but</strong> i.e. ‘ <em> there shall be a sign of the truth of what I say, over and above this confession of Mine</em> .’
	</p>
<p><strong> ἀπ</strong> <strong> ʼ </strong> <strong> ἄρτι</strong> ] The glorification of Christ is by Himself said to <em> begin with his betrayal</em> , see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:31">John 13:31</span> ; <strong> from this time</strong> from the accomplishment of this trial now proceeding. In what follows, the whole process of the triumph of the Lord Jesus even till its end is contained. The <strong> ὄψεσθε</strong> is to the council, the representatives of the chosen people, so soon to be judged by Him to whom all judgment is committed the <strong> τῆς δυνάμεως</strong> in contrast to his present weakness <strong> καθήμενον</strong> even as they now sat to judge Him; and the <strong> ἐρχ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ἐπὶ τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ν</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> οὐρ</strong> <strong> .</strong> (see Dan 7:13 ) looks onward to the awful time of the end, when every eye shall see Him.</p>
