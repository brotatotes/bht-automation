<b> 7. an alabaster box]</b> It was the usual cruse or pot for ointment, with a long narrow neck, and sealed at the top. It was thought that the ointment kept best in these cruses. On the nature of the ointment, see note on “<i>spikenard</i>,” Mark xiv. 3.<br><br><b> his head]</b> His<i> feet</i>, according to John xii. 3. See Luke vii. 88, and note there.
