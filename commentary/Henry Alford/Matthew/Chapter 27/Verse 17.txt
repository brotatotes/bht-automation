<b> 17.]</b> In St. John’s narrative, the suggestion of liberating Barabbas seems to come from the Jews themselves; but not necessarily so: he may only be giving, as<br><br> before, a general report of what passed. The<b> when they were gathered together</b> seems to imply that a great crowd had collected outside the Prætoriam while the trial was going on.<br><br> It is possible that the addition, which is called Christ, which Pilate could hardly have heard from the Jews, may have been familiar to him by his wife’s mention of Jesus.
