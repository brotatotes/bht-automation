<b> 32.</b>] Previously, Jesus had<i> borne his own cross:</i> John, ver. 17. We have no data to ascertain any further particulars about this Simon of Cyrene. The only assumption which we are perhaps justified in making, is that he<b> was</b> afterwards known in the Church<b> as</b> a convert: see note on Mark, ver. 21. He<i> was coming from the country,</i> Mark, ibid.; Luke, ver. 26. Meyer suggests, to account for the selection of one out of the multitude present, that possibly<b> he was a slave;</b> the indignity of the service to be rendered Preventing their taking any other person.
