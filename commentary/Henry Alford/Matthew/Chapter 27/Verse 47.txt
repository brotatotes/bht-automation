<p><strong> 47.</strong> ] This was not said by the <em> Roman soldiers</em> , who could know nothing of Elias; nor was it a <em> misunderstanding</em> of the Jewish spectators, who must have well understood the import of ἡλί : nor again was it said in any apprehension, from the supernatural darkness, that Elias <em> might really come</em> (Olsh.); but it was replied in <em> intended mockery</em> , as <strong> οὗτος</strong> <strong> ,</strong> ‘this one among the three,’ clearly indicates. This is one of the cases where those who advocate an original Hebrew Gospel of Matthew are obliged to suppose that the Greek translator has <em> retained the original words</em> , in order to make the reason of the reply clear.</p>
