<b> 47.</b>] This was not said by the<i> Roman soldiers,</i> who could know nothing of Elias; nor was it<i> a misunderstanding</i> of the Jewish spectators, who must have well understood the import of Eli, nor again was it said in any apprehension, from the supernatural darkness, that Elias<i> might really come;</i> but it<b> was</b> replied in<i> intended mockery,</i> as the contemptuous<b> This man,</b>— ‘this one among the three,’—clearly indicates.
