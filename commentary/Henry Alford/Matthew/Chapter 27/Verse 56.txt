She is not to be confounded with Mary who anointed our Lord, John xii. 1, nor with the woman who did the same, Luke vii. 36: see Luke viii. 2.<br><br><b> Mary the mother of James....</b>] The wife of Alphæus or Clopas, John xix. 25: see note on ch. xiii. 55.<br><br> Mark adds<i> “the less”</i> to distinguish him from the brother of our Lord (probably not from the son of Zebedee).<br><br><b> The mother of the sons of Zebedee</b> (i. e. of James and John: not<i> “of Zebedee’s children,”</i> as<b> A.V.</b> curiously renders it)]<i> Salome,</i> Mark. Both<i> omit Mary the mother of Jesus</i>:—but we must remember, that if we are to take the group as described at this moment,<i> she was not present,</i> having been, as I believe (see note on John, ver. 27),<b> led away</b> by the beloved Apostle immediately on the speaking of the words, ‘Behold thy mother.’ And if this view be objected to, yet she could not be named here, nor in<b> Mark,</b> except separately from these three—for she could not have been well included among those who ministered to Him. There must have been also<i> another group,</i> of His<i> disciples,</i> within sight;—e. g.
