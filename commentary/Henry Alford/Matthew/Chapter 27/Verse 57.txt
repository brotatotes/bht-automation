<p><strong> 57.</strong> ] <em> Before sunset</em> , at which time the Sabbath, and that an high day, began: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="de+21:23">Deuteronomy 21:23</span> . The <em> Roman custom</em> was for the bodies to remain on the crosses till devoured by birds of prey: ‘non pasces in cruce corvos.’ Hor. Epist. 1:16. 48. On the other hand, Josephus, B. J. iv. 5. 2, says, Ἰουδαίων περὶ τὰς ταφὰς πρόνοιαν ποιουμένων ὥστε καὶ τοὺς ἐκ καταδίκης.… ἀνασταυρωμένους πρὸ δύντος ἡλίου καθελεῖν καὶ θάπτειν .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἦλθεν</strong> ] probably <em> to the Prætorium</em> . Meyer supposes, <em> to the place of execution</em> ; which is also possible, and seems supported by the ἦλθεν οὖν καὶ ἦρεν <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:38">John 19:38</span> , and ἦλθεν δὲ καὶ .… ib. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:39">John 19:39</span> , which certainly was <em> to Golgotha</em> .
	</p>
<p><strong> πλούσιος</strong> ] He was also a <em> counsellor</em> , i.e. one of the Sanhedrim: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:43">Mark 15:43</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:0">Luke 23:0</span> ;51.
	</p>
<p><strong> Ἀριμαθαίας</strong> ] Opinions are divided as to whether this was Rama in Benjamin (see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:18">Matthew 2:18</span> .), or Rama (Ramathaim) in Ephraim, the birth-place of Samuel. The form of the name is more like <em> the latter</em> .</p>
