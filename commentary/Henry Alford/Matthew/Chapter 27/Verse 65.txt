<b> 65.</b>]<b> Ye have</b>—the verb rendered may be either 1), indicative,<b> Ye have:</b>—but then the question arises,<i> What guard</i> had they? and if they had one, why go to Pilate? Perhaps we must understand some detachment placed at their disposal during the feast—but there does not seem to be any record of such a practice. That the guards<i> were under the Sanhedrim</i> is plain from ch. xxviii. 11, where they make their report,<i> not to Pilate,</i> but<i> to the chief priests:</i>—or 2), imperative; which doubtless it may be; see 2 Tim. i. 13 and note; and the sense here on that hypothesis would be,<b> Take a body of men for a guard.</b> And to this latter I rather incline: see the note in my Greek Test.
