<b> 2. Repent</b>] Used by the Baptist in the O.T. sense of<i> turning to God as His people</i>, from the spiritual idolatry and typical adultery in which the faithless among the Jews were involved. This, of course, included personal amendment in individuals. See Luke iii. 10–14. Josephus describes John as “ ‘commanding the Jews to practise virtue, and justice to their neighbour, and piety towards God, and thus to receive his baptism.”
