<b> 13. leaving Nazareth</b>] Not on account of the behaviour of the Nazarenes to Him after the preaching in the Synagogue, Luke iv. 28, 29, as sometimes supposed; see notes, ib. ver. 31.<br><br><b> Capernaum</b>] This town, on the borders of the lake of Gennesareth, was central in situation, and in the most populous and frequented part of Galilee. It besides was the residence of four at least of the Apostles, Andrew and Peter, and James and John—and probably of Matthew. “<i>Kephar Nahum,</i>”<i> the village of consolation</i>. So Josephus. It is from this time called ‘<i>His own city,</i>’ ch. ix. 1, see also ch. xvii. 24.
