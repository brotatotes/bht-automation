<b> 5. taketh him up</b>] power being most probably given to the tempter over the person of our Lord. In St. Luke, this temptation stands<i> third</i>. The real order is evidently that in the text; for otherwise our Lord’s final answer, ver. 10, would not be in its place. It may be observed, that St. Luke makes no assertion as to succession, only introducing each temptation with<i> and</i>: whereas “<i>then</i>” and “<i>again</i>” here seem to mark succession.
