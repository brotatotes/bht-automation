<b> 10. Thy kingdom come</b>]<b> Thy kingdom</b> here is the fulness of the accomplishment of the kingdom of God, so often spoken of in prophetic Scripture; and by implication, all that process of events which lead to that accomplishment. Meyer, in objecting to all ecclesiastical and spiritual meanings of ‘Thy kingdom,’ forgets that the one for which he contends exclusively,<i> the Messianic kingdom</i>, does in fact include or imply them all.
