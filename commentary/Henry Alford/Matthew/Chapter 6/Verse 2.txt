<b> 2. sound a trumpet</b>] A proverbial expression, not implying any such custom of the hypocrites of that day,-but the habit of self-laudation, and display of good works in general. Many Commentators, among whom are Calvin and Bengel, think that the words are to be taken literally: and Euthymins mentions this view. But Lightfoot says, that he finds no trace of such a practice among the customs in almsgiving.
