<b> 25. Therefore</b>] A direct inference from the foregoing verse: the plainer, since the verb signifies ‘to be distracted,’ ‘to have the mind drawn two ways.’ The A.V., ‘<i>Take no thought,</i>’ does not express the sense, but gives rather an exaggeration of the command, and thus makes it unreal and nugatory.<b> Take not anxious thought</b>, is far better. In Luke xii. 29 we have “<i>live not in careful suspense</i>” (A. V. marg.).
