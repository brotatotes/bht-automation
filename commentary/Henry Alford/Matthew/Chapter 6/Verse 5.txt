<b> 5. standing</b>] No stress must be laid on this word as implying ostentation; for it was the ordinary posture of prayer. See 1 Sam. i. 26: 1 Kings viii. 22. The command in Mark (xi. 25) runs, “<i>when ye stand praying</i>...” See also Luke xviii. 11, 13. Indeed, of the two positions of prayer, considering the place, kneeling would have been the more singular and savouring of ostentation. The<i> synagogues</i> were<i> places of prayer</i>; so that, as Theophylact, it is not the<i> place</i> which matters, but the<i> manner</i> and<i> intent</i>.
