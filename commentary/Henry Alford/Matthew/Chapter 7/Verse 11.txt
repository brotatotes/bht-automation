<p><strong> 11. </strong> <strong> πονηροί</strong> ] i.e. in comparison with God. It is not necessary to suppose a rebuke conveyed here, but only a general declaration of the corruption and infirmity of man. Augustine remarks, in accordance with this view, that the persons now addressed are the same who had been taught to say ‘ <em> Our Father</em> ’ just now. ταῦτα δὲ ἔλεγεν οὐ διαβάλλων τὴν ἀνθρωπίνην φύσιν οὐδὲ κακίζων τὸ γένος · ἀλλὰ πρὸς ἀντιδιαστολὴν τῆς ἀγαθότητος τῆς αὐτοῦ . Chrys. Hom. xxiii. 4, p. 290. Stier remarks, “This saying seems to me the strongest <em> dictum probans</em> for original sin in the whole of the Holy Scriptures.” R. J. i. 236.
	</p>
