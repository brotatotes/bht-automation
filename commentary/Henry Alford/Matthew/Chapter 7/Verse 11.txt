<b> 11. evil</b>] i.e. in comparison with God. It is not necessary to suppose a rebuke conveyed here, but only a general declaration of the corruption and infirmity of man. Augustine remarks, in accordance with this view, that the persons now addressed are the same who had been taught to say ‘<i>Our Father</i>’ just now. Stier remarks, “This saying seems to me the strongest proof of original sin in the whole of the holy scriptures.” Reden Jesu, i. 236.
