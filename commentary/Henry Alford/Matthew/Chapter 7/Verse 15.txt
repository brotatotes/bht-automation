<p><strong> 15.</strong> ] The connexion (with δέ ) is as Chrys. Hom. xxiii. 6, p. 292: καὶ γὰρ πρὸς τῷ στενὴν εἶναι , πολλοὶ καὶ οἱ ὑποσκελίζοντες τὴν ἐκεῖσε φέρουσάν εἰσιν ὁδόν : <em> strive to enter, &amp;c</em> .: <em> but</em> ( <strong> δέ</strong> <strong> ,</strong> not <em> accordingly</em> , as Webst. and Wilk.) <em> be not misled by persons who pretend to guide you into it, but will not do so in reality</em> .
	</p>
<p>These <strong> ψευδοπρ</strong> <strong> .,</strong> directly, refer to the false prophets who were soon to arise, to deceive, if possible, even the very elect, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:24">Matthew 24:24</span> ; and, indirectly, to all such false teachers in all ages.
	</p>
<p>In <strong> ἐνδύμασι προβ</strong> <strong> .</strong> there may be allusion to the prophetic dress, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:4">Matthew 3:4</span> : but most probably it only means that, in order to deceive, they put on the garb and manners of the sheep themselves.</p>
