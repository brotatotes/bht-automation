<p><strong> 23.</strong> ] As the words now stand, <strong> ὅτι</strong> is merely recitative, and cannot be (Meyer) ‘ <em> because</em> ,’ belonging to ἀποχωρ . Such an arrangement would be unprecedented. Orig [92] , Chrys., Cypr [93] , &amp;c., placed ὅτι οὐδ . ἔγν . ὑμ . <em> after</em> ἀποχ ., &amp;c., in which case the meaning ‘ <em> for, because</em> ,’ would be right. See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:25-27">Luke 13:25-27</span> .
	</p>

<p><strong> ὁμολογήσω</strong> is here a remarkable word, as a statement of the simple truth of facts, as opposed to the false colouring and self-deceit of the hypocrites ‘I will tell them the plain truth.’
	</p>
<p><strong> οὐδέποτε ἔγ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> ὑμ</strong> <strong> .,</strong> i.e., in the sense in which it is said, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:14">John 10:14</span> , γινώσκω τὰ ἐμὰ καὶ γινώσκομαι ὑπὸ τῶν ἐμῶν . Neither the preaching Christ, nor doing miracles in His Name, are infallible signs of being His genuine servants, but only the devotion of life to God’s will which <em> this knowledge</em> brings about.</p>
