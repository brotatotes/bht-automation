<b> 8. unto men</b>] Not plur. for sing. ‘<i>to a man,</i>’ nor, ‘<i>for the benefit of men;</i>’ but<b> to mankind</b>. They regarded this wonder-working as something by God granted to men—to mankind; and without supposing that<i> they</i> had before them the full meaning of their words, those words were true in the very highest sense. See John xvii. 8. In Mark they say, “<i>We never saw it in this fashion:</i>” in Luke, “<i>We have seen strange things to-day.</i>”
