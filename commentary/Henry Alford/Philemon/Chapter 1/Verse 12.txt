<b> 12</b>.]<b> mine own</b> (literally)<b> bowels</b>] There does not appear to be any allusion to the fact of sonship in this figure, as Chrysostom and Theodoret think: for thus the spiritual similitude would be confused, being here introduced materially. But the expression more probably means,<b> mine own heart</b>— ‘as dear to me as mine own heart.’ As to the construction (see var. readd.), it is an anacoluthon: the Apostle goes off into the relative clause, and loses sight, as so often, of the construction with which he began: taking it up again at ver. 17.
