<b> 19.</b>] The inference from this is, that the whole Epistle was autographic: for it would be unnatural to suppose the Apostle to break off his amanuensis here, and write this engagement with his own hand.<b> that I say not</b>] “This is a kind of reticence, when we say that we wish to omit that very thing which we wish most to say,” Grotius. Ellicott paraphrases, ‘repay: yes I say this, not doubting thee, but not wishing to press on thee all the claim that I might justly urge.’ And this may well be the right view.<b> thine own self</b>] Not thy goods merely. This shews that Philemon had been converted by St. Paul in person.
