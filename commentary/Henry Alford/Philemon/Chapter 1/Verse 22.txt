<b> 22</b>.]<b> But at the same time</b> (as thou fulfillest my request)<b> also</b>.... We may, perhaps, take this direction as serving to secure the favourable reception of Onesimus: for the Apostle would himself come and see how his request had fared. “For great would be the favour and honour shewn by Paul’s visit, Paul after his accession of years, Paul after his bonds,” Chrysostom. Or it may be, as Ellicott, that Philemon was not to consider the Epistle as a mere petition for Onesimus, but as containing special messages on other matters to himself.<b> Your</b> and<b> you</b> refer to those named in vv. 1, 2.
