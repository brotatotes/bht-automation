<p><strong> 5</strong> .] It is far better (with Thdrt., Grot., De W., all.) to take <strong> ἀγάπη</strong> and <strong> πίστις</strong> as to be distributed between εἰς τὸν κύριον Ἰησοῦν and εἰς πάντας τοὺς ἁγίους , than, with Meyer, to insist on the ἥν as a bar to this, and interpret πίστις in the wider sense (?) of ‘ <em> fidelity</em> ,’ or with Ellic. to split up πίστις into spiritual faith towards the Lord, and practical faith towards the saints. <strong> ἥν</strong> is naturally in concord with the nearest subst. The πρός of the rec. has perhaps been a correction for reverence sake. <strong> εἰς</strong> is ‘ <em> towards</em> ,’ but more as contributing to ‘towards the behoof of:’ whereas πρός is simple direction: cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:6">Philemon 1:6</span> .</p>
