<p><strong> 7</strong> .] If we read χάριν with the rec., it will be best interpreted by <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:15">2 Corinthians 1:15</span> , as a <strong> benefit</strong> , an outpouring of the divine χάρις not χάρ . ἔχειν in the sense of 1Ti 1:12 ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:3">2 Timothy 1:3</span> , ‘ <em> to give thanks</em> ’ for then it seems always to be followed by a dative. The <strong> γάρ</strong> gives a reason for the prayer of Phm 1:6 as De W., not, as Meyer, for the thanksgiving of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:4">Philemon 1:4</span> ; see above.
