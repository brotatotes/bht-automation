V., al.) <strong> the brethren in the Lord</strong> (this is the most natural connexion: see on πέποιθα , - <strong> ώς</strong> , standing first in the sentence, above, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="php+1:5">Philippians 1:5</span> . And so De W., al. Meyer, Ellic., Winer, § 20. 2, al., take <strong> ἐν κυρ</strong> <strong> .</strong> with <strong> πεποιθότας</strong> , as the element in which their confidence was exercised, as ἐν χριστῷ , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="php+1:13">Philippians 1:13</span> . To this <em> sense</em> there is no objection: but the other arrangement still seems to me, in spite of Ellic.’s note, more natural. No article is required before <strong> ἐν</strong> : see reff.) <strong> encouraged by</strong> (having confidence in) <strong> my bonds</strong> ( εἰ γὰρ μὴ θεῖον ἦν , φησί , τὸ κήρυγμα , οὐκ ἂν ὁ Παῦλος ἠνείχετο ὑπὲρ αὐτοῦ δεδέσθαι , Œc.) <strong> are venturing more abundantly</strong> (than before) <strong> to speak the word of God</strong> (it would certainly seem here, from the variations, as if the shorter reading were the original text) <strong> fearlessly</strong> .</p>
