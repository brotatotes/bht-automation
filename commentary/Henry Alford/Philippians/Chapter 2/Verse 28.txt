<p><strong> 28</strong> .] <strong> πάλιν</strong> most naturally, considering St. Paul’s habit of prefixing it to verbs, belongs to χαρῆτε : and there is here no reason to depart from his usage and attach it to <strong> ἰδόντες</strong> , as Beza, Grot., De W., all., have done. The <strong> κἀγὼ ἀλυπότερος ὦ</strong> is one of the Apostle’s delicate touches of affection. <strong> If</strong> <em> they rejoiced</em> in seeing Epaphroditus, <em> his own</em> trouble would be thereby lessened.</p>
