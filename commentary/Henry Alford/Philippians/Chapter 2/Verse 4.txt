<b> 4.] Each regarding not their own matters but each also the matters of others</b> ( “this second clause [Mey.] is a feebler contrast than might have been expected after the absolute negation in the first.” The<b> also</b> shews that that first is to be taken with some allowance, for by our very nature, each man must<i> look on his own things</i> in some measure).—On the nature of the strife in the Philippian Church, as shewn by the exhortations here, see Introduction,§ii. 7.
