<p><strong> 2</strong> .] <strong> βλέπετε</strong> , not, ‘ <em> beware of</em> ,’ as E. V. ( βλ . ἀπό , Mar 8:15 reff.), but as in reff., <strong> observe</strong> , with a view to avoid: cf. σκοπεῖν , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:17">Romans 16:17</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> τοὺς κύνας</strong> ] profane, impure persons. The appellation occurs in various references; but in the Jewish usage of it, uncleanness was the prominent idea: see, besides reff., <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="de+23:18">Deuteronomy 23:18</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="isa+56:10-11">Isaiah 56:10-11</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:26-27">Matthew 15:26-27</span> . The remark of Chrys. is worth noting in connexion with what follows: οὐκέτι τέκνα Ἰουδαῖοι . ποτὲ οἱ ἐθνικοὶ τοῦτο ἐκαλοῦντο , νῦν δὲ ἐκεῖνοι . But I would not confine it entirely to them, as the next clause certainly generalizes further.
	</p>
<p><strong> τοὺς κακοὺς ἐργάτας</strong> ] cf. δόλιοι ἐργάται , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:13">2 Corinthians 11:13</span> , ἐργάτην ἀνεπαίσχυντον , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:15">2 Timothy 2:15</span> , ἐργάζονται μὲν γὰρ , φησιν , ἀλλ ʼ ἑπὶ κακῷ . By <strong> ἐργάτας</strong> , he seems to point out persons who actually <em> wrought</em> , and professedly for the Gospel, but who were ‘evil workmen,’ not mere ‘ <em> evil-doers</em> .’
	</p>

	</p>
