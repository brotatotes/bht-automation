<p><strong> 3</strong> .] <strong> for</strong> WE <strong> are the</strong> <strong> περι</strong> τομή , <strong> the</strong> <em> real</em> <strong> CIRCUMcision</strong> (whether bodily circumcised, or not there would be among them some of both sorts: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:25">Romans 2:25</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:29">Romans 2:29</span> ; Col 2:11 ), <strong> who serve</strong> (pay religious service and obedience) <strong> by the Spirit of God</strong> (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:23-24">John 4:23-24</span> . The dative is instrumental, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:13">Romans 8:13</span> , expressing the agent, whereby our service is rendered: see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:5">Romans 5:5</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:14">Romans 8:14</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:1">Romans 12:1</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:14">Hebrews 9:14</span> . The emphasis is on it: for <em> both</em> profess a λατρεία . The <strong> θεοῦ</strong> is expressed for solemnity), <strong> and glory in</strong> (stress on <strong> καυχώμενοι</strong> , are not ashamed of Him and seek our boast in circumcision, or the law, but make our <em> boast</em> in Him) <strong> Christ Jesus, and trust not in the flesh</strong> (stress on <strong> ἐν σαρκί</strong> ‘but, in the Spirit in our union with Christ’).</p>
