<b> 16</b>.]<b> Since even in Thessalonica</b> (which was an early stage of my<i> departing from Macedonia</i>, before the departure was consummated. The<b> since</b> gives a reason for and proof of the former assertion—ye were the only ones, &c.,—and ye began as early as when I was at Thessalonica)<b> ye sent both once and twice</b> (so literally: the account of the expression being, that when the first arrived, they had sent<i> once</i>: when the second, not only once, but twice)<b> to my necessity.</b>
