<b> 19.</b>] An assurance taken up from the words “<i>to God</i>” above.<b> My</b><i> God</i>, because he (St. Paul) was the receiver: this was his return to them: “My God, who will pay back what is given to His servant.” Bengel.<b> shall fully supply</b>] All refers to vv. 16, 18;—<i>as ye supplied my want</i>.<b> every need</b>,—and not only in the department alluded to, but in<b> all</b>.<b> in glory</b>] To be connected with<b> shall fully supply</b>: not with<b> his riches in glory</b>: not,<i> gloriously</i>, as many Commentators, which is weak and flat in the extreme: but<b> glory</b> is the instrument and element by and in which ‘all your need’ will be supplied: in<b> glory</b>: but not only at the coming of Christ, but in the whole glorious imparting to you of the unsearchable riches of Christ, begun and carried on here, and completed at that day.<b> in Christ Jesus</b>] And this filling (or, ‘this glory’) is, consists, and finds its sphere and element,<b> in Christ Jesus</b>.
