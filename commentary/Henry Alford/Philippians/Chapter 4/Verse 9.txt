<b> 9.</b>] These general abstract things he now particularizes in the concrete as having been exemplified and taught by himself when among them. It is not,<i> both learned,</i> as A.V.: but as in text: which, besides what I have said recommending them above, were also recommended to you by my own example.<b> learned</b>] again, not as A.V., ‘<i>have learned</i>,’&c.—but all past,—referring to the time when he was among them.<b> Those things which</b> (not<i> ‘whatsoever</i> things:’ we are on generals no longer: nor would he recommend to them<i> all</i> his own sayings and doings; but the<i> also</i> expressly provides for their being of the kinds specified above)<b> ye also learned, and received</b> (here of receiving not by<i> word of mouth</i>, but by knowledge of his character: the whole is not doctrinal, but ethical),<b> and heard</b> (again not of preaching, but of his tried and acknowledged Christian character, which was in men’s mouths and thus heard),<b> and saw</b> (each for himself)<b> in me; these things</b><b> practise. and</b>] and then: see ver. 7.
