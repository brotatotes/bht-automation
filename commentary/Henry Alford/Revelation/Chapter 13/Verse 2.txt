<b>And the beast which I saw</b><b> was like to a leopard, and its feet as of a bear</b> (see reff.),<b> and its mouth as the mouth of a lion</b> (thus uniting in itself the three previous kingdoms of Dan. vii. 4 ff., the first of which was like a lion, the second like a bear, the third like a leopard; and in consequence representing, not the Roman Empire merely, but the aggregate of the Empires of this world as opposed to Christ and His kingdom),<b> And the dragon gave to it his might and his throne and great power</b> (i. e. this beast, this earthly persecuting power, was the vicegerent and instrument of the devil, the prince of this world, and used by him for his purposes of hostility against the remnant of the seed of the woman).
