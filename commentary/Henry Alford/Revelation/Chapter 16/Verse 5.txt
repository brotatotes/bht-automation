<b>And I heard the angel of the waters</b> (i. e. the angel who was set over the waters; see ch. vii. 1, xiv. 18)<b> saying, Thou art righteous who art and wast</b> (as in ch. xi. 2, the “<i>and art to come</i>” is omitted)<b> holy, because Thou didst judge thus</b> (lit., “these things:” viz. the issue mentioned in ver. 4; the turning the drinking-water into blood: “Thou didst inflict this judgment”):
