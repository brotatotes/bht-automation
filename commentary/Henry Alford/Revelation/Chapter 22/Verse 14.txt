<b>Blessed are they that wash their robes</b> (see the margin, and ch, vii. 14, where the expression is fuller, “in the Blood of the Lamb.” The difference in the readings is curious, being in the original that between<i> poîountes tas entolas autou</i> and<i> plunontes tas stolas autôn</i>, either of which might easily be mistaken for the other)<b> that they may have the power</b> (licence)<b> over the tree</b> (to eat of the tree)<b> of life, and may enter by the gates into the city.
