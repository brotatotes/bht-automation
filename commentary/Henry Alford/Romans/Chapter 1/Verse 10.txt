<p><strong> 10. </strong> <strong> εἴ πως</strong> ] <strong> if by any means</strong> . No subject of δεόμενος is expressed, but it is left to be gathered from this clause, as in Simon’s entreaty, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:24">Acts 8:24</span> , δεήθητε ὑμεῖς ὑπὲρ ἐμοῦ .… ὅπως μηδὲν ἐπέλθῃ ἐπ ʼ ἐμὲ ὧν εἰρήκατε , where ὅπως κ . τ . λ . is not the <em> contents</em> of the prayer, but the <em> end aimed at</em> by it.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἤδη ποτέ</strong> ] <strong> before long</strong> : lit., ‘ <em> at last, some day or other</em> .’
	</p>
<p><strong> εὐοδωθήσομαι</strong> ] <strong> I shall be allowed, prospered</strong> : see reff., and <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="de+28:29">Deuteronomy 28:29</span> ; and cf. Umbreit’s note. The rendering, ‘ <em> I might have a prosperous journey</em> ’ (Vulg. and E.V.), is etymologically incorrect; the passive of ὁδόω , ‘ <em> to shew the way,’ ‘to bring into the way</em> ,’ must be ‘ <em> to be shewn</em> the way,’ or ‘ <em> brought</em> into the way.’ So Herod. vi. 73, ὡς τῷ Κλεομενεϊ εὐωδώθη τὸ ἐς τὸν Δημάρητον πρῆγμα .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐν τῷ θελ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τοῦ θεοῦ</strong> ] <strong> In the course of, by, the will of God. </strong> <strong> ἐλθεῖν</strong> belongs to εὐοδωθήσομαι , not to δεόμενος .</p>
