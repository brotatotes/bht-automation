<b> 10. if by any means</b>] It is not said what was the substance of his prayer; only what was its aim and contemplated result. So in Simon’s entreaty, Acts viii. 24, “<i>Pray ye to the Lord for me</i>, [in order]<i> that none of these things which ye have spoken come upon me</i>,’ where the latter clause represents not the<i> contents</i> of the prayer, but the<i> end aimed</i> at by it.
