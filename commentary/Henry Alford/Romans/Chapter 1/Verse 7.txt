<b> 7.</b>] This verse follows, in the sense, close on ver. 1.<br><br><b> beloved of God</b>,<b> called to be saints</b>] Both these clauses refer to<i> all the Christians addressed</i>: not (as Bengel) the first to Jewish, the second to Gentile believers. No such distinction would be in place in an exordium which anticipates. the result of the Epistle—that Jew and Gentile are one in guilt, and one in Christ.<br><br><b> from God our Father, and the Lord Jesus Christ</b>] God is the Giver of grace and peace,—Christ the<i> Imparter</i>.
