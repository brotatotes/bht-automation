<p><strong> 10</strong> .] <strong> For</strong> (refers back to <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:6">Romans 10:6</span> , where the above words were ascribed to ἡ ἐκ πίστεως δικαιοσύνη , and explains how πιστεύς . ἐν τῇ καρδ . refer to the acquiring of righteousness) <strong> with the heart faith is exercised</strong> ( <strong> πιστεύεται</strong> , men believe) <strong> unto</strong> (so as to be available to the acquisition of) <strong> righteousness, but</strong> (q. d. ‘not only so: but there must be an outward confession, in order for justification to be carried forward to salvation’) <strong> with the mouth confession is made unto salvation</strong> .
	</p>
<p>Clearly the words δικ . and σωτ . are not used here, as De W., al., merely as different terms for the same thing, for the sake of the parallelism: but as Thol. quotes from Crell., σωτ . is the ‘terminus ultimus et apex justificationis,’ consequent not merely on the act of justifying faith as the other, but on a good confession before the world, maintained unto the end.</p>
