<p><strong> 21.</strong> ] <strong> for if God did not spare the natural branches</strong> (the branches which grew according to natural development, and were not engrafted), (supply ‘I fear,’ or ‘it is to be feared,’ or simply ‘fear,’ or ‘take heed,’ as in ref.) <strong> lest He shall also not spare</strong> THEE. The fut. ind. with μή πως , the apparent incongruity of which has probably caused the variety of reading, implies, as Herm., Soph. Aj. 272, observes with regard to the ind. pres., ‘ μὴ ἐστὶ ( ἔσται ) verentis quidem est ne quid nunc sit ( <em> futurum sit</em> ), sed indicantis simul, putare, se ita esse ( <em> futurum esse</em> ), ut veretur.’ See Winer, edn. 6, § 56. 2. b. β , and 64. i. 7. a, also <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="col+2:8">Colossians 2:8</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:12">Hebrews 3:12</span> .</p>
