<p><strong> 28.</strong> ] <strong> With regard indeed to the gospel</strong> (i.e. ‘viewed from the gospel side,’ looked on as we must look on them if we confine our view solely to the principles and character of the Gospel), <strong> they</strong> (the Jewish people considered as a whole) <strong> are enemies</strong> ( θεοῦ : not μου , as Theodoret, Luther, Grot., al. scil. in a state of exclusion from God’s favour: not active, ‘enemies to God,’ as Grot., Bengel) <strong> for your sakes; but with regard to the election</strong> (viz. of Israel to be God’s people, see Rom 11:1-2 not that of Christians, as Aug [111] al.: i.e. ‘looked on as God’s elect people’), <strong> they are beloved for the fathers’ sakes</strong> (i.e. not <em> for the merits</em> of the fathers, but because of the covenant with Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, so often referred to by God as a cause for His favourable remembrance of Israel).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [111] <em> Augustine, Bp. of Hippo</em> , 395 430</p>
