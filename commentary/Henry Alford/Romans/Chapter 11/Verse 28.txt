<b> 28.</b>]<b> With regard indeed to the gospel</b> (i. e. ‘viewed from the gospel-side,’—looked on as we must look on them if we confine our view solely to the principles and character of the Gospel)<b>, they</b> (the Jewish people considered as a whole)<b> are enemies</b> (i. e. hated by God, i.e. in a state of exclusion from God's favour)<b> for your sakes: but with regard to the election</b> (viz. of Israel to be God's people, see verses 1, 2—not that of Christians:—i. e. ‘looked on as God’s elect people’),<b> they are beloved for the fathers’ sakes</b> (i. e. not<i> for the merits</i> of the fathers, but because of the covenant with Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, so often referred to by God as a cause for His favourable remembrance of Israel).
