<p><strong> 9.</strong> ] <strong> And David saith, Let their table be for a snare and for a net</strong> ( θήρα more usually ‘a hunt,’ or the act of taking or catching, but here and in ref. <em> a net</em> , the instrument of capture. It is not in the Heb. nor in the LXX, and is perhaps inserted by the Apostle to give emphasis by the accumulation of synonymes), <strong> and for a stumbling-block and for a recompense to them</strong> (the LXX have εἰς παγίδα κ . εἰς ἀνταπόδοσιν κ . εἰς σκάνδαλον . The Heb. of εἰς ἀνταπόδοσιν , as at present pointed, is לִשְׁלוֹמִים , ‘to the secure.’ It has been supposed that the LXX pointed לִשִׁלּוּמִים or לְשִׁלּוֹמִים , ‘for retributions.’ See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ps+91:8">Psalms 91:8</span> ; but qu.?):</p>
