<p><strong> 10.</strong> ] <strong> in brotherly love</strong> (dat. of the respect or regard in which), <strong> affectionate.
	</strong></p>
<p><strong> φιλόστ</strong> .] properly of love of <em> near relations</em> ; agreeing therefore exactly with φιλαδελφία .
	</p>
<p><strong> προηγούμενοι</strong> ] “invicem prævenientes,” latt. μὴ μένε φιλεῖσθαι παρ ʼ ἑτέρου , ἀλλ ʼ αὐτὸς ἐπιπήδα τούτῳ καὶ κατάρχου , Chrys.: similarly Syr., Theophyl., Erasm., Luther: or, = ἀλλήλους ἡγούμενοι ὑπερέχοντας ἑαυτῶν , <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="php+2:3">Philippians 2:3</span> ; so Origen, Theodoret, Grot.: or, as in ref. 2 Macc. ‘ <em> setting an example to,’ ‘going before</em> ,’ which however does not seem to apply here, unless we render τῇ τιμῇ , ‘ <em> in yielding honour</em> :’ ‘in giving honour: anticipating one another’ (so Stuart).</p>
