<p><strong> 3</strong> .] And the <em> tendency</em> of these powers is <em> salutary</em> : to encourage good works, and discourage evil. It is not <em> necessary</em> to set a note of interrogation after ἐξουσίαν : the clause may be treated as hypothetical, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:18">1 Corinthians 7:18</span> . Tholuck observes, that this verse is a token that the Apostle wrote the Epistle <em> before the commencement of the Neronian persecution</em> . Had this been <em> otherwise</em> , the <em> principle</em> stated by him would have been <em> the same</em> ; but he could hardly have passed so apparent an exception to it without remark.</p>
