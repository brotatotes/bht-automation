<b> 6.</b>]<b> For this cause also</b> is<i> parallel with “wherefore”</i> ver. 5,—giving<i> another</i> result of the divine appointment of the civil power;—not<i> dependent on</i> ver. 5.<br><br><b> for they</b> (the authorities)<b> are ministers of God, attending continually to this very thing,</b> viz.<i> ministration</i>. Tertullian remarks, that what the Romans lost by the Christians refusing to bestow gifts on their temples, they gained by their conscientious payment of taxes.
