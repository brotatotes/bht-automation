<p><strong> 2.</strong> ] The <strong> ὃς μέν</strong> , the strong in faith, so indicated by what follows, is opposed to ὁ δὲ ἀσθενῶν (not to be taken ὁ δὲ , ἀσθενῶν , κ . τ . λ .), by which τὸν ἀσθενοῦντα of Rom 14:1 is resumed.
	</p>
<p><strong> πιστεύει φαγεῖν</strong> , either <strong> believes that he may</strong> ( ἐξεῖναι ) <strong> eat</strong> , or <strong> ventures to eat</strong> . The latter is favoured by ref. Acts, πιστεύομεν σωθῆναι , ‘ <em> we trust to be saved</em> ;’ though that also may be expanded into ‘we believe that we shall be saved,’ as E. V.
