<b> 21.</b>]<b> It is good not to eat flesh, nor to drink wine, nor</b> [to do]<b> any thing</b> (the Apostle, as so often, is deducing a general duty from the particular subject)<b> wherein</b> (in the matter of which)<b> thy brother stumbleth, or is offended</b> (see on ver. 13)<b>, or is weak</b> (Tholuck remarks that the three verbs form a gradual<i> anti-climax</i> from greater to less; “stumbleth, or even is offended, or even is weak”).
