 14. ]  αὐτὸς ἐγω ,  I myself , = ‘idem,’ Lat., ‘notwithstanding what I have written:’ see ch. Romans 7:25 , note. Meyer understands it, ‘  without information from others :’ Bengel and Olsh., ‘  I myself, as well as others :’ Rückert, ‘I not only wish it ( Rom 15:13 ), but  am persuaded for myself that it is so.’
	 καὶ αὐτοί ,  ye also yourselves , i.e. without exhortation of mine.
