<p><strong> 14.</strong> ] <strong> αὐτὸς ἐγω</strong> , <strong> I myself</strong> , = ‘idem,’ Lat., ‘notwithstanding what I have written:’ see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:25">Romans 7:25</span> , note. Meyer understands it, ‘ <em> without information from others</em> :’ Bengel and Olsh., ‘ <em> I myself, as well as others</em> :’ Rückert, ‘I not only wish it ( Rom 15:13 ), but <em> am persuaded for myself</em> that it is so.’
	</p>
<p><strong> καὶ αὐτοί</strong> , <strong> ye also yourselves</strong> , i.e. without exhortation of mine.</p>
