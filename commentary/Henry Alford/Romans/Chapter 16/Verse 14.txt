<p><strong> 14.</strong> ] These Christians of whom we have only the names, seem to be persons of less repute than the former. Hermas (= Hermodorus, Grot.) is thought by Origen (in loc. “Puto, quod Hermas iste sit scriptor libelli istius qui Pastor appellatur”), Eus [126] H. E. iii. 3, and Jerome, Catal. script, eccl., c. x., vol. ii., p. 846, to be the author of the ‘Shepherd.’ But this latter is generally supposed to have been the brother of Pius, bishop of Rome, about 150 A.D.

<p>The <strong> σὺν αὐτοῖς ἀδελφοί</strong> of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:14">Romans 16:14</span> , and <strong> σὺν αὐτοῖς πάντες ἅγιοι</strong> of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:15">Romans 16:15</span> , have been taken by De W. and Reiche to point to some separate associations of Christians, perhaps (De W.) assemblies as in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:5">Romans 16:5</span> ; or (Reiche) unions for missionary purposes.</p>
