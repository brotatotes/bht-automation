<b> 14.</b>] These Christians of whom we have only the names, seem to be persons of less repute than the former. Hermasis thought by Origen to be the author of the book called “The Shepherd of Hermas.” But this latter is generally supposed to have been the brother of Pius, bishop of Rome, about 150 A.D.<b> The brethren which are with them</b>, of ver. 14, and<b> all the saints which are with them</b>, of ver. 15, have been taken to point to some separate associations of Christians, perhaps assemblies as in ver. 5: or unions for missionary purposes.
