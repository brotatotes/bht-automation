<b> 21.</b>] Lucius must not be mistaken for Lucas (or Lucanus),—but was probably Lucius of Cyrene, Acts xiii. 1, see note there—Jason<i> may</i> be the same who is mentioned Acts xvii. 5, as the host of Paul and Silas at Thessalonica.—A ‘<i>Sopater</i> (son)<i> of Pyrrhus of Berea</i>’ occurs Acts xx. 4, but it is quite uncertain whether this Sosipater is the same person.<b> my Kinsmen</b>, see above, ver. 7. These persons may have been Jews; but we cannot tell whether the expression may not be used in a wider sense.
