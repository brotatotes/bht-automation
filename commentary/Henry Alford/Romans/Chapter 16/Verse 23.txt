 23. ] Gaius is mentioned 1 Corinthians 1:14 , as having been baptized by Paul.  The host of the whole church probably implies that the assemblies of the church were held in his house: or perhaps, that his hospitality to Christians was universal. Erastus, holding this office ( οἰκονόμος , the public treasurer, ὁ ἐπὶ τῆς δημοσίας τραπέζης , arcarius, Wetst., who quotes from inscriptions, Νείλῳ οἰκονόμῳ Ἀσίας , Secundus, arkarius Reip. Armerinorum), can hardly have been the same who was with the Apostle in Ephesus, Acts 19:22 . It is more probable that the Erastus of 2Ti 4:20 is identical with this than with that other.
