<p><strong> 25.</strong> ] <strong> κατά</strong> , <strong> in reference to</strong> , i.e. ‘in subordination to,’ and according to the requirements of.
	</p>
<p><strong> κήρυγμα Ἰησοῦ χρ</strong> . can hardly mean, as De W. and Meyer, ‘ <em> the preaching which Jesus Christ hath accomplished by me</em> ’ (ch. Rom 15:18 ), nor again as Chrys., ὃ αὐτὸς ἐκήρυξεν , <strong> but the preaching of Christ</strong> , i.e. making known of Christ, as the verb is used 1Co 1:23 ; 1Co 15:12 al. fr. So Calv., and most Commentators.
	</p>
<p><strong> κατὰ ἀποκ</strong> .] This second κατά is best taken, not as co-ordinate to the former one, and following στηρίξαι , nor as belonging to δυναμένῳ , which would be an unusual limitation of the divine Power, but as subordinate to κήρυγμα , <strong> the preaching of Jesus Christ according to, &amp;c</strong> . The omission of τό before κατὰ ἀποκ . is no objection to this.
	</p>
<p><strong> μυστ</strong> .] The <em> mystery</em> (see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:25">Romans 11:25</span> , note) <em> of the gospel</em> is often said to have been thus <em> hidden from eternity</em> in the counsels of God see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:9">Ephesians 3:9</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="col+1:26">Colossians 1:26</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:9">2 Timothy 1:9</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:2">Titus 1:2</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:20">1 Peter 1:20</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="re+13:8">Revelation 13:8</span> .</p>
