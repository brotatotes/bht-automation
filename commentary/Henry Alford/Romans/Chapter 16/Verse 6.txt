	De Wette remarks, that, notwithstanding the manuscript authority, εἰς ἡμᾶς is perhaps the more likely reading, (1) because the Apostle would hardly mention a service done to  themselves as a ground of salutation from him, and (2) because κοπιᾷν without being expressly followed by λόγῳ (1 Timothy 5:17 ; see Philippians 2:16 ; Col 1:29 ), said of  women , most likely implies acts of kindness peculiar to the sex.
