<p><strong> 11.</strong> ] This remark serves as the transition to what follows, not merely as the confirmation of what went before. As to what <em> preceded</em> , it asserts that though the Jew has had great advantages, he shall be justly judged for his use of them, not treated as a favourite of Heaven: as to what <em> follows</em> , it introduces a comparison between him and the Gentile to shew <em> how fairly</em> he will be, for those greater advantages, regarded as πρῶτος in responsibility. And thus we gradually (see note on Rom 2:1 ) pass to the direct comparison between him and the Gentile, and consideration of his state.</p>
