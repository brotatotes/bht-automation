<b> 18. knowest his will</b>] Literally,<b> the will</b>: God having been just mentioned, it is left to be inferred that the word<b> will</b> is to be referred to Him.<br><br><b> and approvest things that are more excellent;</b> or<b>, provest</b> (in the sense of sifting and coming to a conclusion on)<b> things which differ</b>. The words may be rendered either way; but the translation in the text is preferable: the other being somewhat flat in meaning, and not so applicable.<br><br> being (habitually, not in youth only)<b> instructed</b> (not merely catechetically but didactically, in the synagogues, &c.) out of the law.
