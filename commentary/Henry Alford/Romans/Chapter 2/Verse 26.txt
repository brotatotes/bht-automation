<p><strong> 26. </strong> <strong> ἡ ἀκροβ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] i.e. οἱ ἐν τῇ ἀκροβυστίᾳ .
	</p>
<p><strong> τὰ δικαίωμ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] plainly, the <em> moral</em> requirements, not the ceremonial: for one of the very first of the latter was, <em> to be circumcised</em> . The case is an <em> impossible one</em> : nor does the Apostle put it as possible, only as shewing manifestly, that circumcision, the sign of the covenant of the Law, was <em> subordinate to the keeping of the Law</em> itself. The articles shew how completely hypothetical the case is no less than entire fulfilment of all the moral precepts of the law being contemplated.
	</p>
<p><strong> οὐχὶ ἡ</strong> <strong> …</strong> ] ‘In such a case would not he be counted as a circumcised person?’</p>
