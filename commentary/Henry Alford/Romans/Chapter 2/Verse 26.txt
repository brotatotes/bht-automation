<b> 26. the uncircumcision</b>] i.e. those who are in the state of uncircumcision.<br><br><b> the ordinances</b>] Plainly the<i> moral</i> requirements, not the ceremonial: for one of the very first of the latter was,<i> to be circumcised</i>. The case is an<i> impossible one</i>: nor does the Apostle put it as possible, only as shewing manifestly, that circumcision, the sign of the covenant of the Law, was<i> subordinate to the keeping of the Law</i> itself.<br><br><b> shall not, &c.</b>] i.e. “In such a case would not he be counted as a circumcised person?”
