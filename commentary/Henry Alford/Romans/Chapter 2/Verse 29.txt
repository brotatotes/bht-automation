<b> 29.</b>]<b> inwardly or in secret</b>, as a qualification of the Jew, is parallel with<b> of the heart</b> as a qualification of<i> circumcision: both</i> designating the inner and spiritual reality, of which the name of Jew and the carnal circumcision are only the signs.— “<i>Circumcision of the heart</i>” is no new expression:—we have it virtually in Deut. x. 16; Jer. iv. 4: see also Acts vii. 51.
