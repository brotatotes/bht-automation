<b> 9.</b>]<b> tribulation and anguish</b> (or<i> distress</i>): the former signifies more the outward weight of objective infliction,—the latter the subjective feeling of the pressure. It is possible, in the case of the<i> suffering Christian</i>, for the former to exist without the latter: so 2 Cor. iv. 8,<i> troubled on every side, yet not distressed</i>,—where the Greek words are the same. But here the objective weight of infliction and the subjective weight of anguish, are co-existent.
