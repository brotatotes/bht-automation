<p><strong> 2.</strong> ] <strong> πολύ</strong> answers the first question of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:1">Romans 3:1</span> , but takes no account of the second, as it is virtually included in the first. Nor can it be properly regarded as answered in ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:1">Romans 4:1</span> ff. (see there).
	</p>
<p><strong> κατὰ πάντα τρ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] not merely <em> omnino</em> , but as E. V. (in) <strong> every way</strong> , i.e. in all departments of the spiritual life.
	</p>
<p><strong> πρῶτον</strong> ] The Apostle begins as if intending to instance <em> several</em> of these advantages, but having mentioned the greatest, leaves it to his reader to fill in the rest, and turns to establish what he has just asserted. For πρῶτον can only be <strong> first</strong> , ‘secondly,’ &amp;c., being to follow: not, ‘ <em> primarium illud</em> ’ (as Beza), nor ‘ <em> præcipue</em> ’ (as Calv.), nor ‘ <em> id quod præcipuum est</em> ’ (as Calov.), all of which are attempts to avoid the anacoluthon: compare a similar one at ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:8">Romans 1:8</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐπιστ</strong> <strong> .</strong> ] see reff. <strong> they were entrusted with.
	</strong></p>
<p><strong> τὰ λόγια τ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> θεοῦ</strong> ] These words look very like a reminiscence of Stephen’s apology, see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:38">Acts 7:38</span> . These oracles are not only the law of Moses, but all the revelations of God hitherto made of Himself <em> directly</em> , all of which had been entrusted to <em> Jews only</em> . By these they were received into a special covenant, which advantage is therefore included in their being entrusted with the divine oracles.</p>
