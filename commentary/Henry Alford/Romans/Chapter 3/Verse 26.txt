<b> 26.</b>] This second<b> shewing forth</b> is distinguished from that in the last verse, as the fuller and ultimate object, of which that was a subordinate part:—<b>with a view to the</b> (or His)<b> manifestation of His righteousness in this present time.</b> The shewing forth that He was righteous throughout His dealings with the whole world, by means of setting forth an adequate and complete propitiation in the death of Christ, was<i> towards</i>, formed a subsidiary manifestation to, His<i> great manifestation of His righteousness</i> (same sense as before,—judicial righteousness,<i> justice</i>)<i> under the Gospel</i>.
