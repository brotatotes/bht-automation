<p><strong> 3.</strong> ] And this advantage is not cancelled, nor the covenant annulled, by their disobedience.
	</p>
<p><strong> τί γάρ</strong> <strong> ;] For what</strong> ? (‘quid enim?’ Hor. Sat. i. 1. 7.) The γάρ confirms the preceding the τί indicates some difficulty, or anticipated objection to it.
	</p>
<p><strong> εἰ ἠπίστ</strong> <strong> . </strong> <strong> τινες</strong> ] If we place an interrogation at γάρ , we must render this, <strong> suppose some were unfaithful</strong> ; if only a comma, as in E. V., ‘ <em> For what if</em> …’ The former seems preferable, as more according to usage. See <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="php+1:18">Philippians 1:18</span> .
	</p>
<p><strong> ἠπίστησαν</strong> <strong> , did not believe</strong> . If this seem out of place here, where he is not speaking of faith or want of faith as yet, but of ἀδικία ( Rom 3:5 ) and moral guilt, we may meet the objection by remembering that unbelief is here taken more on its practical side, as involving <em> disobedience</em> , than on the other. They were ἄπιστοι , <em> unfaithful to the covenant</em> , the very condition of which was to walk in the ways of the Lord and observe his statutes. The word may have been chosen on account of ἐπιστεύθησαν above and τ . πίστιν τ . θεοῦ below.
	</p>
<p><strong> μὴ ἡ ἀπ</strong> . κ . τ . λ .] <strong> shall their unfaithfulness</strong> (to the covenant: see above, and Wis 14:25 ; in the root of the matter, their <em> unbelief</em> , as in reff.: and the substantive ἀπιστία is bound to the verb ἠπίστησαν , but its <em> rendering</em> must be ruled by the contrast to ἡ πίστις τοῦ θεοῦ , which <em> must</em> be “the <em> faithfulness</em> of God”) <strong> cancel</strong> (nullify) the <strong> faithfulness of God</strong> ? ‘Because they have broken faith on their part, shall God break faith also on His?’</p>
