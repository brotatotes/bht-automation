<p><strong> 12.</strong> <strong> καὶ</strong> ( εἰς τὸ εἶναι αὐτὸν ) <strong> πατέρα περιτομῆς</strong> <strong> .…</strong> ] <strong> And</strong> (that he might be) <strong> father of the circumcision</strong> (the circumcised) <strong> to those</strong> (dat. commodi ‘for those,’ ‘in the case of those’) <strong> who are not only</strong> (physically) <strong> of the circumcision, but also who walk</strong> (the inversion of the article appears to be in order to bring out more markedly τοῖς ἐκ περιτ . and τοῖς στοιχ ., who are not only οἱ ἐκ περιτ ., but also οἱ στοιχοῦντες .…) <strong> in the footsteps</strong> (reff.) <strong> of the faith of our father</strong> (speaking here as a Jew) <strong> Abraham</strong> (which he had) <strong> while he was in uncircumcision</strong> . (The art. would make it ‘during his uncircumcision,’ but the sense is better without it, the word being generalized.)</p>
