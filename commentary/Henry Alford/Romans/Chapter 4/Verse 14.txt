<p><strong> 14</strong> .] The supposition is now made which Rom 4:13 denied, and its consequences shewn. <strong> For if they who are of the law</strong> (who belong to the law, see reff.: not, ‘who keep the law,’ nor is δίκαιοι to be supplied) <strong> are inheritors</strong> (i.e. inherit ‘ejus rei causâ,’ <em> by virtue of</em> the law: they may be inheritors by the righteousness of faith, but <em> not quoad their legal standing</em> ), <strong> faith is</strong> (thereby) <strong> made empty</strong> (robbed of its virtue and rendered useless), <strong> and the promise is annulled</strong> (has no longer place). How and why so? The Apostle himself immediately gives the reason.</p>
