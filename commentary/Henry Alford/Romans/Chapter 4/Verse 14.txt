<b> 14.</b>] The supposition is now made which ver. 13 denied,—and its consequences shewn.<b> For if they which are of the law</b> (who belong to the law, not ‘who keep the law’)<b> be heirs</b> (i. e. inherit by virtue of the law; they may be inheritors by the righteousness of faith, but not Ly force of their legal standing),<b> faith is</b> (thereby)<b> made empty</b> (robbed of its virtue and rendered useless),<b> and the promise is annulled</b> (has no longer place). How and why so? The Apostle himself immediately gives the reason.
