<p><strong> 22.</strong> ] <strong> διό</strong> , on account of the nature of this faith, which the Apostle has now since Rom 4:18 been setting forth; because it was a simple unconditional credence of God and His promise. If we read <strong> καί</strong> , it imports besides being thus great and admirable, it was reckoned to him for righteousness: <strong> ἐλογίσθη</strong> , viz. τὸ πιστεῦσαι τῷ θεῷ .</p>
