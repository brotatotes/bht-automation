<b> 9.</b>] The form of the question, with<b> or... also</b>, presupposes an affirmative answer to the latter clause; which affirmative answer is then made the ground of the argumentation in vv. 10. 11, 12:—<b>upon the uncircumcision</b> (-cised)<b> also?</b><b> for we say</b>, &c.—The stress is on the words to<b> Abraham</b>, not on<i> faith</i>:<b> for we say that</b> TO ABRAHAM<b> faith was reckoned for righteousness.</b>
