<b> 11.</b>] A further step still. Not only has the reconciled man confidence that he shall escape God's wrath, but<i> triumphant</i> confidence,—joyful hope in God.—<b>And</b> (literally, but)<b> not only so, but also glorying in God</b> (not only shall we be saved, but that in a triumphant manner and frame of mind)<b> through our Lord Jesus Christ, through whom we have now</b> (not in contrast with the future glory, ‘<i>even now</i>, but as in ver. 9)<b> received (our) reconciliation</b> (so God: not as in A.V. “<i>the atonement</i>,” at least in the common theological acceptation of that term: for that is not here treated of, but our reconcilement to God).
