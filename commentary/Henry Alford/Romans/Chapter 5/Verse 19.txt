<p><strong> 19.</strong> ] <strong> For</strong> (in explanation of Rom 5:18 ) <strong> as by the disobedience of (the) one man the many</strong> (= πάντες ἄνθρωποι , but not so expressed here, because in the other limb of the comparison πάντ . ἄνθρ . could not be put, and this is conformed to it: see there) <strong> were made</strong> (not, ‘ <em> were accounted as</em> ’ (Grot. al.): nor ‘ <em> became by imputation</em> ’ (Beza, Bengel): nor ‘ <em> were proved to be</em> ’ (Koppe, Reiche, Fritz.): see reff.) <strong> sinners</strong> (not ὑπεύθυνοι κολἀσει , as Chrys., Theophyl.: ‘ <em> actual sinners by practice</em> ,’ is meant, the disobedience of Adam having been the <em> inlet</em> to all this: compare ἐφ ʼ ᾡ πάντες ἥμαρτον Rom 5:12 and the notes, on the <em> kind of sin</em> spoken of in this whole passage, as being both original and actual), <strong> so also</strong> (after the same manner or analogy likewise) <strong> by means of the obedience</strong> (unto death, see on last verse) of (the) <strong> One</strong> (man) <strong> shall</strong> ( <em> future</em> , because, as in ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:30">Romans 3:30</span> , justification, as regards the many, is <em> not yet completed</em> . De W.) <strong> the many</strong> (= πολλοί , compare <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:28">Matthew 26:28</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:45">Mark 10:45</span> , but thus expressed because πολλοί would not have answered in the other limb of the comparison. <em> In order to make the comparison more strict</em> , the πάντες who have been made sinners are <em> weakened</em> to the indefinite οἱ πολλοί , the πολλοί who shall be made righteous are <em> enlarged</em> to the indefinite οἱ πολλοί . Thus a <em> common term of quantity</em> is found <em> for both</em> , the one <em> extending to its largest</em> numerical interpretation, the other <em> restricted to its smallest</em> ) <strong> be made</strong> (see above) <strong> righteous</strong> (not by <em> imputation</em> merely, any more than in the other case: but ‘shall be made really and actually righteous, as completely so as the others were made really and actually sinners.’ When we say that man has no righteousness <em> of his own</em> , we speak of him as <em> out of Christ</em> : but <em> in Christ</em> and united to Him, he is <em> made righteous</em> , not by a fiction, or <em> imputation only</em> of Christ’s righteousness, but by a real and living spiritual union with a righteous Head as a righteous member, righteous <em> by means of</em> , as an effect of, the righteousness of that Head, but not merely righteous by transference of the Righteousness of that Head; just as in his natural state he is united to a sinful head as a sinful member, sinful by means of, as an effect of, the sinfulness of that Head, but not merely <em> by transference</em> of the sinfulness of that Head).
	</p>
<p>See the whole question respecting πάντες and οἱ πολλοί treated in Tholuck’s Comm. in loc.</p>
