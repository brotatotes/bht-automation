<p><strong> 3.</strong> ] <strong> And not only so</strong> (not only must we triumph in hope, which has regard to the future), <strong> but glorying in</strong> (not <em> amidst</em> ; the θλ . is the <em> ground</em> of triumph) [ <strong> our</strong> ] <strong> tribulations, knowing</strong> (because we know) <strong> that tribulation works endurance</strong> (supposing, i.e. we remain firm under it), <strong> and endurance, approval</strong> (of our faith and trust, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:9">2 Corinthians 2:9</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:13">2 Corinthians 9:13</span> ; not, ‘ <em> proof</em> ’ ( δοκιμασία ), as Grot.; nor ‘ <em> experience</em> ,’ as E. V., ‘ δοκιμή est qualitas ejus, qui est δόκιμος .’ Bengel, the <em> result</em> of proof), <strong> and approval</strong> (fresh) <strong> hope; and hope</strong> (but for αὕτη ἡ ἐλπ . as Olsh.) <strong> shames (us) not</strong> (by disappointing us; ‘mocks us not’); <strong> because God’s love</strong> (not ‘ <em> the love of God</em> ,’ i.e. <em> man’s love for God</em> , as Theodoret, and even Aug [26] , misled by the Latin; see reff., and compare the explicit τὴν ἑαυτοῦ ἀγάπην εἰς ἡμᾶς , which answers to this in Rom 5:8 ) <strong> is</strong> (has been) <strong> poured out</strong> (‘ <em> effusa</em> ,’ not ‘ <em> diffusa</em> ’ (Vulg.), which latter word perhaps misled Aug [27] , owing to whose mistake the true interpretation was lost for some centuries, although held by Orig [28] , Chrys., and Ambrose. See Trench on St. Augustine, ch. v. p. 89: i.e. ‘richly imparted’) <strong> in our hearts</strong> ( ἐν <em> may</em> be taken pregnantly, ἐκκέχ . εἰς καὶ μένει ἐν , or better, denotes the locality where the outpouring takes place, the heart being the seat of our love, and of appreciation and sympathy with <em> God’s love</em> ) <strong> by means of the Holy Spirit</strong> (who is the Outpourer, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:14">John 16:14</span> ; 1Co 2:9-10 ) <strong> which was given to us</strong> (Olsh. rightly refers the aorist part. to the Pentecostal effusion of the Holy Spirit).
