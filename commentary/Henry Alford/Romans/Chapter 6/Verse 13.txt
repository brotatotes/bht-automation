<p><strong> 13.</strong> ] <strong> Nor render</strong> (see reff.; as a soldier renders his service to his sovereign, or a servant to his master) <strong> your members</strong> (more particular than ‘your bodies;’ the individual members being instruments of different lusts and sins) <strong> as instruments</strong> (or, ‘ <em> weapons</em> ,’ as Vulg., most of the Greek expositors, and Luth., Calv., Beza, Tholuck, which latter defends this rendering by Paul’s fondness for military similitudes, and by the occurrence of ὀψώνια below, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:23">Romans 6:23</span> ; but as De W. observes, the comparison here is to <em> servitude</em> rather than <em> soldiership</em> ) <strong> of unrighteousness to sin; but render</strong> (the <em> present</em> imperat. above denotes habit, the exhortation guards against the recurrence of a devotion of the members to sin: this <em> aorist</em> imperat., on the other hand, as in ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:1">Romans 12:1</span> , denotes an <em> act</em> of self-devotion to God once for all, not a mere recurrence of the habit) <strong> yourselves</strong> (not merely <em> your members</em> , but your whole selves, body, soul, and spirit) <strong> to God, as alive from having been dead</strong> (as in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:4">Romans 6:4</span> ff. and Eph 2:1-5 ), <strong> and your members as instruments</strong> (see above) <strong> of righteousness to God</strong> (dat. ‘ <em> commodi</em> ,’ as indeed is τῇ ἁμαρτ . above, the dat. after παριστ . being there left to be supplied, because of τῆ ̣ ἁμ . following).</p>
