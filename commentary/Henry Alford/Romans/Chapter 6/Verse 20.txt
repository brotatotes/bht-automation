<p><strong> 20.</strong> ] <strong> γάρ</strong> introduces a <em> motive</em> for the foregoing: but the verse [properly] belongs to the <em> following</em> : for Rom 6:22 is the contrast to it. Meyer and Fritz. think it to be an explanation of <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:19">Romans 6:19</span> , but are certainly mistaken. <strong> For when ye were servants of sin, ye were free in relation to</strong> (dat. of regard or reference, Winer, edn. 6, § 31.1) <strong> righteousness</strong> .
	</p>
<p>There is doubtless a latent irony in the use of ἐλεύθεροι here; but it must not be brought out too strongly: it does not appear, till the <em> end</em> of that freedom is declared.</p>
