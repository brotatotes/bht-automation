<b> 23.</b>] The<i> ends</i> of the two courses placed, pointedly and antithetically, and the inherent difference, that whereas<b> death</b> (see above) is the<b> wages</b> (the word means the pay, or ration, of soldiers; compare the similitude in ver. 13, and remarks there)<b> of sin</b>, earned and paid down,—e<b> ternal life is</b> no such<i> wages</i>, nothing earned, but<b> the free gift of God</b> to His soldiers and servants;—and that<b> in</b> (not ‘through,’—<i>true</i> enough, but not implied in the preposition here: see above on ver. 11)<b> Christ Jesus our Lord</b>.
