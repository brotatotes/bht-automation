<p><strong> 17, 18.</strong> ] The dilemma solved for them by reference to the matter of fact: <em> that they were once servants of sin, but on receiving the gospel, obeyed its teaching: and consequently were freed from the service of sin, and became the servants of righteousness</em> : and this in the form of a thanksgiving to God ( 1Co 1:14 ) whose work in them it was.
	</p>
<p>There is a stress on <strong> ἦτε</strong> as referring to a state <em> past</em> . So <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:8">Ephesians 5:8</span> ; on account of which stress apparently the μέν , which would naturally follow it, is omitted.</p>
