<b> 20.</b>] The inference of ver. 17 restated, with the premiss of ver. 16 in the place of “<i>so now</i>:”—but its meaning is now clearer and deeper than then; we know now that the “<i>I</i>” which does not the evil thing, is<i> the better self</i> of the<i> inner man</i>,—whereas the “<i>me</i>” in which sin dwells and rules, though included in the complex self, is<i> the lower self, my flesh</i>. And so the way is now prepared for at once setting forth the<i> conflict</i> within us<i> between these two</i>.
