<b> 25.]</b> The thanksgiving sentence is (not, of course, constructionally, but logically) an<i> answer</i> to the<i> preceding question:</i><b> Thanks be to God</b> (who hath accomplished this)<b> through Jesus Christ our Lord.</b>—This exclamation and thanksgiving more than all convince me, that St. Paul speaks of<i> none other than himself</i>, and<i> carries out as far as possible</i> the misery of the conflict with sin in his members,<i> on purpose to bring in the glorious deliverance which follows.</i>—Compare 1 Cor. xv. 56, 57, where a very similar thanksgiving occurs.
