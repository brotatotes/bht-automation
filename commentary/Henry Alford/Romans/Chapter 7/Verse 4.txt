The  more violent word is used instead of ἀπεθάνετε , to recall the violent death of Christ, in which, and after the manner of which, believers have been put to death to the law and sin, and the  historic aorist to remind them of the great Event by which this was brought about)  by means of the (crucified)  Body (compare διὰ τῆς προσφορᾶς τοῦ σώματος τοῦ Ἰης . χρ ., Heb 10:10 )  of Christ, that you should become attached to another , (even)  to Him who was raised from the dead (alluding both to the comparison in Romans 7:2-3 , γένηται ἀνδρὶ ἑτέρῳ , and to ch. Romans 6:4-5 , ἵνα ὥσπ . ἠγέρθη χριστὸς κ . τ . λ .),  that we should (here strictly  final , as Thol., Meyer, De W., &c. Not merely  ecbatic , as Fritzsche)  bring forth fruit (alluding to καρπόν , ch. Romans 4:22 , and at the same time ( Luk 1:42 ) carrying on the similitude of marriage. Not that this latter must be pressed, for there is only an allusion to it: nor on the other hand need the least objection be raised to such an understanding of the words, as any one conversant with St. Paul’s way of speaking on this subject will at once feel: compare 2 Corinthians 11:2 ; Eph 5:30-32 )  to (dat. commodi, ‘  to the honour of ’)  God .
