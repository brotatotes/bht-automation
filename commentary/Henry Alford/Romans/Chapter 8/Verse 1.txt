Matthæi (Thol.): or rather perhaps he [now] uses πνεῦμα , regarding our spirits as possessed and penetrated by God’s Spirit)  is life (this would hardly be said if  only our human spirits were meant, but the description would be in the adjectival form)  on account of righteousness (not  here the imputed righteousness of justification, which is not now under treatment, but the  implanted righteousness of the sanctification of the Spirit . This appears not only from the context, but also from the διὰ ἁμαρτίαν , which answers to it).
