<b> 10.</b>]<b> Now</b> (in slight contrast to the last verse, he brings out one point, which might seem to be an exception to the blessed consequences of the life-giving power of Christ indwelling in us)<b> if Christ is in you</b> (i. e. if the Spirit of God dwelleth in you, see 2 Cor. iii. 17),<b> the</b> (your)<b> body indeed is dead by reason of sin</b> (still remains dead, see 2 Cor. iv. 11–14, under the power of death physical [and eternal?], because of sin which it,<i> of itself</i>, stands in, and serves);<b> but the</b> (your)<b> spirit</b> (perhaps he now uses<i> spirit</i>, regarding our spirits as possessed and penetrated by God’s Spirit)<b> is life</b> (this would hardly be said if<i> only</i><i> our human spirits</i> were meant, but the participle would be used, is<i> revived</i> or<i> quickened</i>)<b> by reason of righteousness</b> (not<i> here</i> the imputed righteousness of justification, which is not now under treatment, but the<i> implanted righteousness of the sanctification of the Spirit</i>. This appears not only from the context, but also from the words,<b> by reason of sin</b>, which answer to it).
