<p><strong> 30.</strong> ] <strong> but whom He fore-ordained, those he also called</strong> (in making the decree, He left it not barren, but provided for those circumstances, all at His disposal, by which such decree should be made effectual in them.
	</p>
<p><strong> ἐκάλεσεν</strong> , supply, εἰς τὴν ἑαυτοῦ βασιλείαν καὶ δόξαν <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:12">1 Thessalonians 2:12</span> ; other expressions are found in 1Co 1:9 ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:14">2 Thessalonians 2:14</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:12">1 Timothy 6:12</span> ; 1Pe 5:10 ): <strong> and whom He called, these He also justified</strong> (the Apostle, remember, is speaking entirely of <em> God’s acts</em> on behalf of the believer: he says nothing <em> now</em> of that faith, through which this justification is, <em> on his part</em> , obtained): <strong> but whom He justified, them He also glorified</strong> (He did not merely, in His premundane decree, acquit them of sin, but also <em> clothe them with glory</em> : the aorist ἐδόξασεν being used, as the other aorists, to imply the completion in the divine counsel of all these, which are to us, in the state of time, so many successive steps, simultaneously and irrevocably. So we have the perfect in <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:10">John 17:10</span> ; Joh 17:22 ).</p>
