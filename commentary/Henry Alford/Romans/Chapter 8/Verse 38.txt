<p><strong> 38</strong> .] <strong> For I am persuaded</strong> (a taking up and amplifying of the ὑπερνικῶμεν our victory is not only over these things, but Idare assert it over greater and more awful than these) <strong> that neither death, nor life</strong> (well explained by De W. as the two principal possible states of man, and not as = ‘any thing dead or living,’ as Calvin and Koppe), <strong> nor angels, nor principalities</strong> (whether good or bad; ἀρχή is used of good, <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="col+1:16">Colossians 1:16</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="col+2:15">Colossians 2:15</span> (see note); of bad ( 1Co 15:24 ?), <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:12">Ephesians 6:12</span> ; here, as <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:21">Ephesians 1:21</span> , <em> generally</em> .
	</p>
<p>ἄγγελοι , absolutely, seems never to be used of <em> bad</em> angels: if it here means good angels, there is no objection, as Stuart alleges, to the rhetorical supposition that they might attempt this separation, any more than to that of <em> an angel from heaven preaching another gospel</em> , Gal 1:8 ), <strong> nor things present nor things to come</strong> (no vicissitudes of <em> time</em> ), <strong> nor powers</strong> (some confusion has evidently crept into the arrangement. Ephr [65] Syr. reads, οὔτ . ἀρχαὶ οὔτ . ἐξουσίαι οὔτ . ἐνεστ . οὔτ . μέλλ . οὔτ . δυνάμεις οὔτ . ἄγγελοι ; Basil, οὔτε ἄγγ . οὔτ . ἀρχ . οὔτ . ἐξους . οὔτ . δυνάμεις οὔτ . ἐνεστ . οὔτ . μέλλ I follow, with Griesb., Lachm., Tischdf., the very strong consent of the ancient MSS.), <strong> nor height nor depth</strong> (no extremes of <em> space</em> ), <strong> nor any other created thing</strong> ( κτίσις cannot here be the whole creation, as Chrys., ὃ λέγει τοιοῦτόν ἐστιν · εἰ καὶ ἄλλη τοσαύτη κτίσις ἦν ὅση ἡ ὁρωμένη , ὅση ἡ νοητή , οὐδὲν ἄν με τῆς ἀγάπης ἐκείνης ἀπέστησε , but any <em> creature</em> , such as are all the things named) <strong> shall be able to sever us from the love of God which is in Christ Jesus our Lord</strong> (here plainly enough <em> God’s love to us in Christ</em> , to us, as we are in Christ, to us, manifested in and by Christ).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;"> [65] Ephrem Syrus, b. 299, d. 378</p>
