<b> 8.</b>]<b> That is</b> (that amounts, when the facts of the history are recollected, to saying),<b> Not they which are the children of the flesh</b> (begotten by natural generation, compare John i. 13, and Gal. iv. 29)<b> are the children of God; but the children of the</b><b> promise</b> (begotten not naturally, but by virtue of the divine promise [Gal. iv. 23], as Isaac)<b> are reckoned for seed.</b>
