<p><strong> 5</strong> .] <strong> For this reason I left thee behind</strong> (reff.: ἀπέλ . gives the mere fact of leaving behind when Paul left the island; κατέλ . would convey the idea of more permanence: cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:19">Acts 18:19</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:27">Acts 24:27</span> . This difference may have occasioned the alteration of the reading from ecclesiastical motives, to represent Titus as permanent bishop of Crete) <strong> in Crete</strong> (on the island, and the whole matter, see Prolegg.) <strong> that thou mightest carry forward the correction</strong> (already begun by me: ἐπι implying the furtherance, addition of διορθώματα . The middle voice, as so often, carries only so far the subjective sense, that whereas the active would state the <em> mere fact</em> of διόρθωσις , the middle implies that the subject uses his own agency: <em> facit per se</em> : see Krüger, Griechische Sprachlehre, p. 363, who calls this the <em> dynamic</em> middle. So Polybius, xxx. 5. 13, τὰ μὲν οὖν κατὰ τοὺς Καυνίους … ταχέως οἱ Ῥόδιοι <strong> διωρθώσαντο</strong> ) <strong> of those things which are defective</strong> (‘quæ ego per temporis brevitatem non potui expedire,’ Beng.: ὁ γὰρ τῆς εὐσεβείας λόγος παρεδίδοτο πᾶσι παρ ʼ αὐτοῦ , ἐλείπετο δὲ οἰκονομῆσαι τὰ κατὰ τοὺς πεπιστευκότας , καὶ εἰς ἁρμονίαν αὐτοὺς καταστῆσαι ταῖς ἐκκλησιαστικαῖς διατυπώσεσι . Theodr-Mops. in Huther), <strong> and</strong> ( <strong> καί</strong> brings out, among the matters to be attended to in the ἐπιδιόρθωσις , especially that which follows) <strong> mightest appoint city by city</strong> (reff.) <strong> elders</strong> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:14">1 Timothy 4:14</span> ; note on <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:17">Acts 20:17</span> . Thl. remarks, τοὺς ἐπισκόπους οὕτως ἐνταῦθά φησιν , ὡς καὶ ἐν τῇ πρὸς Τιμόθεον · κατὰ πόλεις δέ φησιν . οὐ γὰρ ἐβούλετο πᾶσαν τὴν νῆσον ἐπιτετράφθαι ἑνί , ἀλλ ʼ ἑκάστην πόλιν τὸν ἴδιον ποιμένα ἔχειν · οὕτω γὰρ καὶ ὁ πόνος κουφότερος , καὶ ἡ ἐπιμέλεια ἀκριβεστέρα ), <strong> as I prescribed</strong> (reff.) <strong> to thee</strong> (“ <strong> διεταξάμην</strong> refers as well to the <em> fact</em> of appointing elders, as to the <em> manner</em> of their appointment, which last particular is now expanded in directions respecting the characters of those to be chosen.” De W.):</p>
