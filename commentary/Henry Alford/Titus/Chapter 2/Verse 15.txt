<p><strong> 15</strong> .] gathers up all since <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:1">Titus 2:1</span> , where the general command last appeared, and enforces it on Titus. In ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="hac" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:1">Titus 3:1</span> , the train of thought is again resumed. <strong> These things</strong> (the foregoing: not, the following) <strong> speak and exhort</strong> (in the case of those who believe and need stirring up) <strong> and rebuke</strong> (in the case of those who are rebellious) <strong> with all imperativeness</strong> ( μετὰ αὐθεντίας καὶ μετὰ ἐξουσίας πολλῆς , Chrys. τουτέστι , μετὰ ἀποτομίας , Thl.). <strong> Let no man despise thee</strong> (addressed to Titus, not to the people, as Calv. (‘populum ipsum magis quam Titum hic compellat’): ‘so conduct thyself in thine exhortations, with such gravity, and such consistency, and such impartiality, that every word of thine may carry weight, and none may be able to cast slight on thee for flaws in any of these points’).</p>
