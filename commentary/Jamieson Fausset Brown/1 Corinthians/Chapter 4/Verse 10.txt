10. Irony. How much your lot
	(supposing it real) is to be envied, and ours to be pitied. 
	fools— (1 Corinthians 1:21;
	1 Corinthians 3:18; compare Acts 17:18;
	Acts 26:24). 
	for Christ's sake . . . in
	Christ—Our connection with Christ only entails on us the lowest
	ignominy, "ON ACCOUNT OF,"
	or, "FOR THE SAKE OF"
	Him, as "fools"; yours gives you full fellowship IN
	Him as "wise" (that is, supposing you really are all you
	seem, 1 Corinthians 3:18). 
	we . . . weak . . . ye . . .
	strong— (1 Corinthians 2:3; 2 Corinthians 13:9).
	we . . . despised— (2 Corinthians 13:9- :) because of our "weakness," and our not using
	worldly philosophy and rhetoric, on account of which ye Corinthians
	and your teachers are (seemingly) so "honorable." Contrast
	with "despised" the "ye (Galatians) despised
	not my temptation . . . in my flesh" (2 Corinthians 13:9- :).
