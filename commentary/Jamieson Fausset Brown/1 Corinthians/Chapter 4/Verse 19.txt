19. ALFORD
	translates, "But come I will"; an emphatical negation of
	their supposition (1 Corinthians 4:18). 
	shortly—after Pentecost
	(1 Corinthians 16:8). 
	if the Lord will—a wise
	proviso (James 4:15). He does not
	seem to have been able to go as soon as he intended. 
	and will know—take
	cognizance of. 
	but the power—I care
	not for their high-sounding "speech," "but" what
	I desire to know is "their power," whether they be really
	powerful in the Spirit, or not. The predominant feature of Grecian
	character, a love for power of discourse, rather than that of
	godliness, showed itself at Corinth.
