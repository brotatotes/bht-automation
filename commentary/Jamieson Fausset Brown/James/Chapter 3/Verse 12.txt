12. Transition from the mouth to
	the heart. 
	Can the fig tree,
	c.—implying that it is an impossibility: as before in  :- he had said it "ought not so to be." James
	does not, as Matthew (Matthew 7:16
Matthew 7:17), make the question, "Do
	men gather figs of thistles?" His argument is, No tree
	"can" bring forth fruit inconsistent with its nature,
	as for example, the fig tree, olive berries: so if a man speaks
	bitterly, and afterwards speaks good words, the latter must be so
	only seemingly, and in hypocrisy, they cannot be real. 
	so can no fountain . . . salt
	. . . and fresh—The oldest authorities read, "Neither can
	a salt (water spring) yield fresh." So the mouth that emits
	cursing, cannot really emit also blessing.
