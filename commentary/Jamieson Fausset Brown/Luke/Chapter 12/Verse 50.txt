50. But . . . a baptism,
	c.—clearly, His own bloody baptism, first to take place. 
	how . . . straitened—not,
	"how do I long for its accomplishment," as many understand
	it, thus making it but a repetition of  :- but "what a pressure of spirit is upon Me." 
	till it be accomplished—till
	it be over. Before a promiscuous audience, such obscure language was
	fit on a theme like this; but oh, what surges of mysterious emotion
	in the view of what was now so near at hand does it reveal!
