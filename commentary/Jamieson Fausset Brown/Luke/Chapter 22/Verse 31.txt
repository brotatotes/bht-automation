31-34. Simon, Simon—(See on  :-). 
	desired to have
—rather, "hath obtained you," properly "asked
	and obtained"; alluding to Job (Job 1:6-12;
	Job 2:1-6), whom he
	solicited and obtained that he might sift him as wheat, insinuating
	as "the accuser of the brethren" (Job 2:1-18.2.6- :), that he would find chaff enough in his religion, if
	indeed there was any wheat at all. 
	you—not Peter only, but
	them all.
