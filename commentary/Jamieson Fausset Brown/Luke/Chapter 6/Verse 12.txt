12, 13. went out—probably from
	Capernaum. 
	all night in prayer . . . and
	when . . . day, he called, c.—The work with which the next day
	began shows what had been the burden of this night's
	devotions. As He directed His disciples to pray for "laborers"
	just before sending themselves forth (see on  :-  :-), so here we find
	the Lord Himself in prolonged communion with His Father in
	preparation for the solemn appointment of those men who were to give
	birth to His Church, and from whom the world in all time was to take
	a new mould. How instructive is this!
