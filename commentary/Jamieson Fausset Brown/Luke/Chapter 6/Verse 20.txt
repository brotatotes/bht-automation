20, 21. In the Sermon on the
	Mount the benediction is pronounced upon the "poor in spirit"
	and those who "hunger and thirst after righteousness"
	(Matthew 5:3; Matthew 5:6).
	Here it is simply on the "poor" and the "hungry now."
	In this form of the discourse, then, our Lord seems to have had in
	view "the poor of this world, rich in faith, and heirs of
	the kingdom which God hath promised to them that love Him," as
	these very beatitudes are paraphrased by James (Matthew 5:6- :).
