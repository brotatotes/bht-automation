<p><b>20. Where</b>—nowhere; for God
	"brings them to naught" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>the wise</b>—generally. 
	</p>
<p><b>the scribe</b>—Jewish
	[ALFORD]. 
	</p>
<p><b>the disputer</b>—Greek
	[ALFORD]. Compare the Jew
	and Greek of this world contrasted with the godly wise, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:22">1 Corinthians 1:22</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:23">1 Corinthians 1:23</span>. VITRINGA
	thinks the reference is to the Jewish discourses in the synagogue,
	<i>daraschoth,</i> from a <i>Hebrew</i> root "to dispute."
	Compare "questions," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:3">Acts 26:3</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:9">Titus 3:9</span>. If so, "wise"
	refers to <i>Greek</i> wisdom (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:9- :">Titus 3:9- :</span>). Paul applies <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+33:18">Isaiah 33:18</span>
	here in a higher sense; there the primary reference was to temporal
	deliverance, here to external; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+33:18- :">Isaiah 33:18- :</span>, which is in threefold opposition to <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+33:18- :">Isaiah 33:18- :</span> there, sanctions this higher application; the Lord in the
	threefold character being the sole ground of glorying to His people. 
	</p>
<p><b>of this world . . . of this
	world</b>—rather, "dispensation (or <i>age</i>) . . . world";
	the <i>Greek</i> words are distinct. The former is here <i>this age</i>
	or <i>worldly order of things</i> in a moral point of view, as
	opposed to the Christian dispensation or order of things. The latter
	is the <i>world</i> viewed externally and cosmically. 
	</p>
<p><b>made foolish</b>—shown the
	world's philosophy to be folly, because it lacks faith in Christ
	crucified [CHRYSOSTOM].
	Has treated it as folly, and not used its help in converting and
	saving men (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:26">1 Corinthians 1:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:27">1 Corinthians 1:27</span>)
	[ESTIUS].</p>
