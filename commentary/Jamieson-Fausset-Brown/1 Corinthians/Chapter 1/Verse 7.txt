<p><b>7. ye come behind</b>—are inferior
	to other Christians elsewhere [GROTIUS].
	</p>
<p><b>in no gift</b>—not that all
	had all gifts, but different persons among them had different gifts
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:4">1 Corinthians 12:4</span>, c.). 
	</p>
<p><b>waiting for . . . coming of .
	. . Christ</b>—The crowning proof of their "coming behind in
	no gift." <i>Faith, hope,</i> and <i>love,</i> are all exercised
	herein (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:8">2 Timothy 4:8</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:13">Titus 2:13</span>).
	"Leaving to others their MEMENTO
	MORI (remember death), do thou earnestly cherish this joyous
	expectation of the Lord's coming" [BENGEL].
	The <i>Greek</i> verb implies, "to expect constantly, not only
	for a certain time, but even to the end till the expected event
	happens" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:19">Romans 8:19</span>,
	[TITTMANN, <i>Greek
	Synonyms of the New Testament</i>]).</p>
