<p><b>called</b>—according to His
	purpose (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:28">Romans 8:28</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>unto . . . fellowship of . .
	. Jesus</b>—to be fellow heirs with Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:28- :">Romans 8:28- :</span>), like Him sons of God and heirs of glory (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:30">Romans 8:30</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:14">2 Thessalonians 2:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:10">1 Peter 5:10</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:3">1 John 1:3</span>). CHRYSOSTOM
	remarks that the name of Christ is oftener mentioned in this than in
	any other Epistle, the apostle designing thereby to draw them away
	from their party admiration of particular teachers to Christ alone.</p>
