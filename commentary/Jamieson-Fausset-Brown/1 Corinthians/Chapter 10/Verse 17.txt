<p><b>17. one bread</b>—rather, "loaf."
	One loaf alone seems to have been used in each celebration. 
	</p>
<p><i><b>and</b></i><b> one
	body</b>—Omit "and"; "one loaf [that is], one body."
	"We, <i>the many</i> (namely, believers assembled; so the
	<i>Greek</i>), are one bread (by our partaking of the same loaf,
	which becomes assimilated to the substance of all our bodies; and so
	we become), one body" (with Christ, and so with one another). 
	</p>
<p><b>we . . . all</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"the whole of us."</p>
