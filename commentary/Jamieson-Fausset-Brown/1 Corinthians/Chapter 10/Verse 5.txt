<p><b>5. But</b>—though they had so many
	tokens of God's presence. 
	</p>
<p><b>many of them</b>—rather,
	"the majority of them"; "the whole part." All
	except Joshua and Caleb of the first generation. 
	</p>
<p><b>not</b>—in the <i>Greek</i>
	emphatically standing in the beginning of the sentence: "Not,"
	as one might have naturally expected, "with the more part of
	them was," &amp;c. 
	</p>
<p><b>God</b>—whose judgment
	alone is valid. 
	</p>
<p><b>for</b>—the event showed,
	they had not pleased God. 
	</p>
<p><b>overthrown</b>—literally,
	"strewn in heaps." 
	</p>
<p><b>in the wilderness</b>—far
	from the land of promise.</p>
