<p><b>6. were</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "came
	to pass as." 
	</p>
<p><b>our examples</b>—samples to
	us of what will befall us, if we also with all our privileges walk
	carelessly. 
	</p>
<p><b>lust</b>—the fountain of
	all the four other offenses enumerated, and therefore put first
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:14">James 1:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:15">James 1:15</span>;
	compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+106:14">Psalms 106:14</span>). A
	particular case of lust was that after flesh, when they pined for the
	fish, leeks, c., of Egypt, which they had left (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="nu+11:4">Numbers 11:4</span>
<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="nu+11:33">Numbers 11:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="nu+11:34">Numbers 11:34</span>).
	These are included in the "evil things," not that they are
	so in themselves, but they became so to the Israelites when they
	lusted after what God withheld, and were discontented with what God
	provided.</p>
