<p><b>17. in this</b>—which follows. 
	</p>
<p><b>I declare</b>—rather, "I
	enjoin"; as the <i>Greek</i> is always so used. The oldest
	manuscripts read literally "This I enjoin (you) not praising
	(you)." 
	</p>
<p><b>that</b>—<i>inasmuch as; in
	that</i> you, c. Here he qualifies his praise (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). "I said that I praised you for keeping the ordinances
	delivered to you but I must now give injunction in the name of the
	Lord, on a matter in which I praise you not; namely, as to the Lord's
	Supper (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:23">1 Corinthians 11:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:37">1 Corinthians 14:37</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>not for the better</b>—not
	so as to progress to what is better. 
	</p>
<p><b>for the worse</b>—so as to
	retrograde to what is worse. The result of such "coming
	together" must be "condemnation" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:37- :">1 Corinthians 14:37- :</span>).</p>
