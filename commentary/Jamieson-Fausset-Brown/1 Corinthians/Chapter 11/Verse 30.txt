<p><b>30. weak . . . sickly</b>—He is
	"weak" who has <i>naturally</i> no strength: "sickly,"
	who has <i>lost his strength</i> by disease [TITTMANN,
	<i>Greek Synonyms of the New Testament</i>]. 
	</p>
<p><b>sleep</b>—are being lulled
	in death: not a violent death; but one the result of sickness, sent
	as the Lord's chastening for the individual's salvation, the mind
	being brought to a right state on the sick bed (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>).</p>
