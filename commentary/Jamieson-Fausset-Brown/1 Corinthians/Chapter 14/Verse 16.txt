<p><b>16. Else . . . thou</b>—He changes
	from the <i>first</i> person, as he had just expressed <i>his own</i>
	resolution, "<i>I</i> will pray with the understanding,"
	whatever "thou" doest. 
	</p>
<p><b>bless</b>—the highest kind
	of prayer. 
	</p>
<p><b>occupieth the room of the
	unlearned</b>—one who, whatever other gifts he may possess, yet, as
	wanting the gift of interpretation, is reduced by the speaking in an
	unknown tongue to the position of one unlearned, or "a private
	person." 
	</p>
<p><b>say Amen</b>—Prayer is not
	a vicarious duty done by others <i>for</i> us; as in Rome's liturgies
	and masses. We must join <i>with</i> the leader of the prayers and
	praises of the congregation, and say aloud our responsive "Amen"
	in assent, as was the usage of the Jewish (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="de+27:15-26">Deuteronomy 27:15-26</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ne+8:6">Nehemiah 8:6</span>) and Christian primitive
	churches [JUSTIN MARTYR,
	<i>Apology,</i> 2. 97].</p>
