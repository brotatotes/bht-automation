<p><b>20. Brethren</b>—an appellation
	calculated to conciliate their favorable reception of his
	exhortation. 
	</p>
<p><b>children in understanding</b>—as
	preference of gifts abused to nonedification would make you (compare
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:1">1 Corinthians 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:16">Matthew 10:16</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:19">Romans 16:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:14">Ephesians 4:14</span>).
	The <i>Greek</i> for "understanding" expresses the will of
	one's <i>spirit,</i> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:6">Romans 8:6</span> (it
	is not found elsewhere); as the "heart" is the will of the
	"soul." The same <i>Greek</i> is used for "minded"
	in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:6">Romans 8:6</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>men</b>—full-grown. Be
	childlike, not childish.</p>
