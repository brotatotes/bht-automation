<p><b>29. two or three</b>—at one
	meeting (he does not add "at the most," as in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, lest he should seem to "quench prophesyings,"
	the most edifying of gifts), and these "one by one," in
	turn (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:27">1 Corinthians 14:27</span>, "by
	course," and <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:31">1 Corinthians 14:31</span>).
	Paul gives here similar rules to the prophets, as previously to those
	speaking in unknown tongues. 
	</p>
<p><b>judge</b>—by their power of
	"discerning spirits" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:31- :">1 Corinthians 14:31- :</span>), whether the person prophesying was really speaking under
	the influence of the Spirit (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:3">1 Corinthians 12:3</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:13">1 John 4:13</span>).</p>
