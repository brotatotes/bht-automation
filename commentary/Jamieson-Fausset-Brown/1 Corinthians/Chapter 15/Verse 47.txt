<p><b>47. of the earth</b>—inasmuch as
	being sprung from the earth, he is "earthy" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ge+2:7">Genesis 2:7</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ge+3:19">Genesis 3:19</span>, "dust thou art");
	that is, not merely earthly or born <i>upon</i> the earth, but
	<i>terrene,</i> or <i>of earth;</i> literally, "of <i>heaped</i>
	earth" or clay. "Adam" means <i>red earth.</i>
</p>
<p><b>the Lord</b>—omitted in the
	oldest manuscripts and versions. 
	</p>
<p><b>from heaven</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:13">John 3:13</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:31">John 3:31</span>). Humanity in Christ is
	generic. In Him man is impersonated in his true ideal as God
	originally designed him. Christ is the representative man, the
	federal head of redeemed man.</p>
