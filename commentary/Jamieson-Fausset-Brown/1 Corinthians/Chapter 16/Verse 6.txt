<p><b>6.</b> He did "abide and even
	winter" for the three WINTER
	months in Greece (Corinth), <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:3">Acts 20:3</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:6">Acts 20:6</span>; from which passage it
	seems that Paul probably left Corinth about a month before the "days
	of unleavened bread" or the Passover (so as to allow time to
	touch at Thessalonica and Berea, from which cities two of his
	companions were; as we read he did at Philippi); so that thus the
	three months at Corinth would be December, January, and February
	[BIRKS, <i>Horæ
	Apostolicæ</i>]. 
	</p>
<p><b>ye</b>—emphatical in the
	<i>Greek.</i>
</p>
<p><b>whithersoever I go</b>—He
	purposed to go to Judea (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:16">2 Corinthians 1:16</span>)
	from Corinth, but his plans were not positively fixed as yet (see on
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:1">1 Corinthians 16:1</span>; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:1- :">1 Corinthians 16:1- :</span>).</p>
