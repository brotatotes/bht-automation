<p><b>3. I</b>—the <i>preacher:</i> as
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:2">1 Corinthians 2:2</span> describes the <i>subject,</i>
	"Christ crucified," and <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:2- :">1 Corinthians 2:2- :</span> the <i>mode</i> of preaching: "my speech . . . not with
	enticing words," "but in demonstration of the Spirit."
	</p>
<p><b>weakness</b>—personal and
	bodily (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:10">2 Corinthians 10:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:7">2 Corinthians 12:7</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:9">2 Corinthians 12:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:13">Galatians 4:13</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>trembling</b>—(compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:13- :">Galatians 4:13- :</span>). Not <i>personal fear,</i> but a <i>trembling anxiety to
	perform a duty;</i> anxious conscientiousness, as proved by the
	contrast to "eye service" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:13- :">Galatians 4:13- :</span>) [CONYBEARE and
	HOWSON].</p>
