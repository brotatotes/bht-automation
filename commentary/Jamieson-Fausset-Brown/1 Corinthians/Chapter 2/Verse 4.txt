<p><b>4. my speech</b>—in private. 
	</p>
<p><b>preaching</b>—in public
	[BENGEL]. ALFORD
	explains it, <i>My discourse</i> on doctrines, and <i>my preaching</i>
	or announcement of facts. 
	</p>
<p><b>enticing</b>—rather,
	"persuasive." 
	</p>
<p><b>man's wisdom</b>—<i>man's</i>
	is omitted in the oldest authorities. Still "wisdom" does
	refer to "man's" wisdom. 
	</p>
<p><b>in demonstration of . . .
	Spirit,</b> c.—<i>Persuasion</i> is man's means of moving his
	fellow man. God's means is <i>demonstration,</i> leaving no doubt,
	and inspiring implicit faith, by the powerful working of the Spirit
	(then exhibited both outwardly by miracles, and inwardly by working
	on the heart, now in the latter and the more important way only,
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:29">Matthew 7:29</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:10">Acts 6:10</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:12">Hebrews 4:12</span>; compare also <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:12- :">Hebrews 4:12- :</span>). The same simple power accompanies divine truth now,
	producing certain persuasion and conversion, when the Spirit
	demonstrates by it.</p>
