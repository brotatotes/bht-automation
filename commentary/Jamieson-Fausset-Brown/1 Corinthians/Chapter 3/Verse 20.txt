<p><b>20.</b> Quotation from <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. There it is <i>of men;</i> here it is "of the wise."
	Paul by inspiration states the class of men whose "thoughts"
	(or rather, "reasonings," as suits the <i>Greek</i> and the
	sense of the context) the Spirit designated in the Psalm, "vanity,"
	namely, the "proud" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>) and worldly-wise, whom God in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span> calls "fools," though they "boast themselves"
	of their <i>wisdom</i> in pushing their interests (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>).</p>
