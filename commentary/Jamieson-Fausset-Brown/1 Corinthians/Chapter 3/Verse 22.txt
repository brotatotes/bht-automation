<p><b>22.</b> Enumeration of some of the
	"all things." The teachers, in whom they gloried, he puts
	first (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:12">1 Corinthians 1:12</span>). He omits
	after "Cephas" <i>or Christ</i> (to whom exclusively some
	at Corinth, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:12">1 Corinthians 1:12</span>, professed
	to belong); but, instead, substitutes "ye are Christ's"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:23">1 Corinthians 3:23</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>world . . . life . . . death
	. . . things present . . . things to come</b>—Not only shall they
	not "separate you from the love of God in Christ" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:38">Romans 8:38</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:39">Romans 8:39</span>), but they "all are
	yours," that is, are for you (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:39- :">Romans 8:39- :</span>), and belong to you, as they belong to Christ your Head (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:39- :">Romans 8:39- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>things present</b>—"things
	<i>actually</i> present" [ALFORD].</p>
