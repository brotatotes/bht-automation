<p><b>3. envying</b>—jealousy, <i>rivalry.</i>
	As this refers to their <i>feelings,</i> "strife" refers to
	their <i>words,</i> and "divisions" to their <i>actions</i>
	[BENGEL]. There is a
	gradation, or ascending climax: <i>envying</i> had produced <i>strife,</i>
	and strife <i>divisions</i> (factious parties) [GROTIUS].
	His language becomes severer now as He proceeds; in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span> he had only said "contentions," he now multiplies
	the words (compare the stronger term, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, than in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:21">1 Corinthians 3:21</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>carnal</b>—For "strife"
	is a "work of the flesh" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:21- :">1 Corinthians 3:21- :</span>). The "flesh" includes all feelings that aim not
	at the glory of God, and the good of our neighbor, but at gratifying
	self. 
	</p>
<p><b>walk as men</b>—as
	unregenerate men (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:21- :">1 Corinthians 3:21- :</span>). "After the flesh, not after the Spirit" of God,
	as becomes you as regenerate by the Spirit (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:4">Romans 8:4</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:25">Galatians 5:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:26">Galatians 5:26</span>).</p>
