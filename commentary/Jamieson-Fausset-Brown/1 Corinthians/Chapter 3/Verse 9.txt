<p><b>9.</b> Translate, as the <i>Greek</i>
	collocation of words, and the emphasis on "God" thrice
	repeated, requires, "For (in proof that "each shall receive
	reward according to his own labor," namely, from God) it is of
	God that we are the fellow workers (laboring <i>with,</i> but <i>under,</i>
	and <i>belonging</i> to Him as His servants, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:20">2 Corinthians 5:20</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:1">2 Corinthians 6:1</span>; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:1- :">2 Corinthians 6:1- :</span>; see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:1- :">2 Corinthians 6:1- :</span>) of God
	that ye are the field (or tillage), of God that ye are the building"
	[ALFORD]. "Building"
	is a new image introduced here, as suited better than that of
	husbandry, to set forth the different kinds of teaching and their
	results, which he is now about to discuss. "To edify" or
	"build up" the Church of Christ is similarly used (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:21">Ephesians 2:21</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:22">Ephesians 2:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:29">Ephesians 4:29</span>).</p>
