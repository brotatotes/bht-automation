<p><b>10.</b> Irony. How much your lot
	(<i>supposing it real</i>) is to be envied, and ours to be pitied. 
	</p>
<p><b>fools</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:21">1 Corinthians 1:21</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:18">1 Corinthians 3:18</span>; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:18">Acts 17:18</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:24">Acts 26:24</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>for Christ's sake . . . in
	Christ</b>—Our connection with Christ only entails on us the lowest
	ignominy, "ON ACCOUNT OF,"
	or, "FOR THE SAKE OF"
	Him, as "fools"; yours gives you full fellowship IN
	Him as "wise" (that is, <i>supposing you really are all you
	seem,</i> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:18">1 Corinthians 3:18</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>we . . . weak . . . ye . . .
	strong</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:3">1 Corinthians 2:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+13:9">2 Corinthians 13:9</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>we . . . despised</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+13:9- :">2 Corinthians 13:9- :</span>) because of our "weakness," and our not using
	worldly philosophy and rhetoric, on account of which ye Corinthians
	and your teachers are (seemingly) so "honorable." Contrast
	with "despised" the "ye (<i>Galatians</i>) despised
	not my temptation . . . in my flesh" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+13:9- :">2 Corinthians 13:9- :</span>).</p>
