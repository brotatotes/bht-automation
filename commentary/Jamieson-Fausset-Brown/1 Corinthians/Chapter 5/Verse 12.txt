<p><b>12. what have I to do</b>—You
	might have easily understood that my concern is not with unbelievers
	<i>outside</i> the Church, but that I referred to those within it. 
	</p>
<p><b>also</b>—Implying, <i>Those
	within</i> give me enough to do without those outside. 
	</p>
<p><b>do not ye,</b> &amp;c.—Ye
	judge your fellow citizens, not strangers: much more should I
	[BENGEL]. Rather, Is it
	not <i>your duty</i> to judge them that are within? <i>God</i> shall
	judge them that are without: do you look at home [GROTIUS].
	God is the Judge of the salvation of the heathen, not we (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). Paul here gives an anticipatory censure of their going
	to law with saints before heathen tribunals, instead of judging such
	causes among themselves within.</p>
