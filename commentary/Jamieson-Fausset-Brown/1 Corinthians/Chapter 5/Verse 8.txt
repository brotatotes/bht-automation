<p><b>8. not . . . old leaven</b>—of our
	unconverted state as Jews or heathen. 
	</p>
<p><b>malice</b>—the opposite of
	"sincerity," which allows no leaven of evil to be mixed up
	with good (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:6">Matthew 16:6</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>wickedness</b>—the opposite
	of "truth," which allows not evil to be mistaken for good.
	The <i>Greek</i> for "malice" means the evil <i>habit</i>
	of mind; "wickedness," the <i>outcoming</i> of the same in
	word and deed. The <i>Greek</i> for "sincerity" expresses
	literally, a thing which, when examined <i>by the sun's light,</i> is
	found pure and unadulterated.</p>
