<p><b>5. your shame</b>—Thus he checks
	their <i>puffed-up</i> spirit (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:34">1 Corinthians 15:34</span>).
	To shame you out of your present unworthy course of litigation before
	the heathen, I have said (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:4">1 Corinthians 6:4</span>),
	"Set the least esteemed in the Church to judge." Better
	even this, than your present course. 
	</p>
<p><b>Is it so?</b>—Are you in
	such a helpless state that, c.? 
	</p>
<p><b>not a wise man</b>—though
	ye admire "wisdom" so much on other occasions (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:5">1 Corinthians 1:5</span>
<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:22">1 Corinthians 1:22</span>). Paul alludes probably
	to the title, "cachain," or <i>wise man,</i> applied to
	each Rabbi in Jewish councils. 
	</p>
<p><b>no, not one</b>—not even
	one, amidst so many reputed among you for wisdom (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:18">1 Corinthians 3:18</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:6">1 Corinthians 4:6</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>shall be able</b>—when
	applied to. 
	</p>
<p><b>brethren</b>—literally,
	"brother"; that is, judge between brother and brother. As
	each case should arise, the arbitrator was to be chosen from the body
	of the church, such a wise person as had the charism, or gift, of
	church government.</p>
