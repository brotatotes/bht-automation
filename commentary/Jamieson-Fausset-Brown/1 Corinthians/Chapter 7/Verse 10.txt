<p><b>10. not I, but the Lord</b>—(Compare
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:12">1 Corinthians 7:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:25">1 Corinthians 7:25</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:40">1 Corinthians 7:40</span>). In ordinary cases he
	writes on <i>inspired apostolic authority</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:40- :">1 Corinthians 7:40- :</span>); but here on the <i>direct</i> authority of the <i>Lord
	Himself</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:11">Mark 10:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:12">Mark 10:12</span>).
	In both cases alike the things written are inspired by the Spirit of
	God "but not all for all time, nor all on the primary truths of
	the faith" [ALFORD]. 
	</p>
<p><b>Let not the wife
	depart</b>—literally, "be separated from." Probably the
	separation on either side, whether owing to the husband or to the
	wife, is forbidden.</p>
