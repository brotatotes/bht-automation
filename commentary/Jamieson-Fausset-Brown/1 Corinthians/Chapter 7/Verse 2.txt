<p><b>2.</b> Here the general rule is
	given 
	</p>
<p><i><b>to avoid</b></i><b>
	fornication</b>—More literally, "<i>on account of
	fornications,</i>" to which as being very prevalent at Corinth,
	and not even counted sins among the heathen, unmarried persons might
	be tempted. The <i>plural,</i> "fornications," marks
	irregular lusts, as contrasted with the <i>unity</i> of the marriage
	relation [BENGEL]. 
	</p>
<p><b>let every man have</b>—a
	positive command to all who have not the gift of continency, in fact
	to the great majority of the world (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). The dignity of marriage is set forth by Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), in the fact that it signifies the mystical union
	between Christ and the Church.</p>
