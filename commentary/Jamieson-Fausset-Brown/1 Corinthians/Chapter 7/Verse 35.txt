<p><b>35. for your own profit</b>—not to
	display <i>my</i> apostolic authority. 
	</p>
<p><b>not . . . cast a snare upon
	you</b>—image from <i>throwing a noose</i> over an animal in
	hunting. Not that by hard injunctions I may entangle you with the
	fear of committing sin where there is no sin. 
	</p>
<p><b>comely</b>—<i>befitting</i>
	under present circumstances. 
	</p>
<p><b>attend upon</b>—literally,
	"assiduously wait on"; <i>sitting down</i> to the duty.
	Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:39">Luke 10:39</span>, Mary; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:39- :">Luke 10:39- :</span>, "Anna . . . a widow, who departed not from the temple,
	but served God with fastings and prayers night and day" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:39- :">Luke 10:39- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>distraction</b>—the same
	<i>Greek</i> as "cumbered" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:39- :">Luke 10:39- :</span>, Martha).</p>
