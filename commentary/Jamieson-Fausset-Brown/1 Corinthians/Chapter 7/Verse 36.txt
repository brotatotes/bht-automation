36. behaveth . . . uncomely—is
	not treating his daughter well in leaving her unmarried beyond the
	flower of her age, and thus debarring her from the lawful
	gratification of her natural feeling as a marriageable woman. 
	need so require—if the
	exigencies of the case require it; namely, regard to the feelings and
	welfare of his daughter. Opposed to "having no necessity"
	(1 Corinthians 7:37). 
	let them marry—the
	daughter and her suitor.
