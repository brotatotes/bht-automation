<p><b>5. Defraud . . . not</b>—namely,
	of the conjugal duty "due" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>; compare the <i>Septuagint,</i> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>except it be</b>—"unless
	perchance" [ALFORD]. 
	</p>
<p><b>give yourselves to</b>—literally,
	"be at leisure for"; be <i>free from interruptions for;</i>
	namely, on some <i>special</i> "<i>season,</i>" as the
	<i>Greek</i> for "time" means (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ex+19:15">Exodus 19:15</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joe+2:16">Joel 2:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="zec+7:3">Zechariah 7:3</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>fasting and prayer</b>—The
	oldest manuscripts omit "fasting and"; an interpolation,
	evidently, of ascetics. 
	</p>
<p><b>come together</b>—The
	oldest manuscripts read, "be together," namely, in the
	regular state of the married. 
	</p>
<p><b>Satan</b>—who often thrusts
	in his temptations to unholy thoughts amidst the holiest exercises. 
	</p>
<p><b>for your incontinency</b>—<i>because
	of</i> your inability to "contain" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="zec+7:3- :">Zechariah 7:3- :</span>) your natural propensities, which Satan would take advantage
	of.</p>
