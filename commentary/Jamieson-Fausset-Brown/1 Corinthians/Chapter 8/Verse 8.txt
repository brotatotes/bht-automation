<p><b>8.</b> Other old manuscripts read,
	"Neither if we do not eat, are we the better: neither if we eat
	are we the worse": the language of the eaters who justified
	their eating thus [LACHMANN].
	In <i>English Version</i> Paul admits that "meat neither
	<i>presents</i> [so the <i>Greek</i> for 'commendeth'] us as
	commended nor as disapproved before God": it does not affect our
	standing before God (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:6">Romans 14:6</span>).</p>
