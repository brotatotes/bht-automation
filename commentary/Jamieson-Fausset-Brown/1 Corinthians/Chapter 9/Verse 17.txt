<p><b>17.</b> Translate, "If I be
	doing this (that is, preaching) of my own accord (which I am not, for
	the 'necessity' is laid on me which binds a servant to obey his
	master), I have a reward; but if (as is the case) involuntarily
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:15">Acts 9:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:15">Acts 22:15</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:16">Acts 26:16</span>); not of my own natural
	will, but by the constraining grace of God; (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:16">Romans 9:16</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:13-16">1 Timothy 1:13-16</span>), I have had a
	dispensation (of the Gospel) entrusted to me" (and so can claim
	no "reward," seeing that I only "have done that which
	was my duty to do," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:10">Luke 17:10</span>,
	but incur the "woe," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:10- :">Luke 17:10- :</span>, if I fail in it).</p>
