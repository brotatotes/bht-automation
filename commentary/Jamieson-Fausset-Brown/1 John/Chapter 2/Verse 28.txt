<p><b>28. little children</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"little sons," as in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>; believers of every stage and age. 
	</p>
<p><b>abide in him</b>—Christ.
	John repeats his monition with a loving appellation, as a father
	addressing dear children. 
	</p>
<p><b>when</b>—literally, "if";
	the uncertainty is not as to the fact, but <i>the time.</i>
</p>
<p><b>appear</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"be manifested." 
	</p>
<p><b>we</b>—both writer and
	readers. 
	</p>
<p><b>ashamed before him</b>—literally,
	"<i>from</i> Him"; shrink back <i>from Him</i> ashamed.
	Contrast "boldness in the day of judgment," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:21">1 John 3:21</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:14">1 John 5:14</span>. In the Apocalypse
	(written, therefore, BENGEL
	thinks, subsequently), Christ's coming is represented as put off to a
	greater distance.</p>
