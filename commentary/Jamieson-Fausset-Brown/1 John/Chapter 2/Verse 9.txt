<p><b>9-11.</b> There is no mean between
	<i>light and darkness, love</i> and <i>hatred, life</i> and <i>death,
	God</i> and the <i>world:</i> wherever spiritual <i>life</i> is,
	however weak, there <i>darkness</i> and <i>death</i> no longer reign,
	and <i>love</i> supplants <i>hatred;</i> and <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span> holds good: wherever <i>life</i> is not, <i>there death,
	darkness,</i> the <i>flesh,</i> the <i>world, and hatred,</i> however
	glossed over and hidden from man's observation, prevail; and <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span> holds good. "Where love is not, there hatred is; for
	the heart cannot remain a void" [BENGEL].
	</p>
<p><b>in the light</b>—as his
	proper element. 
	</p>
<p><b>his brother</b>—his
	neighbor, and especially those of the Christian brotherhood. The very
	title "brother" is a reason why love should be exercised. 
	</p>
<p><b>even until
	now</b>—notwithstanding that "the true light already has begun
	to shine" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:8">1 John 2:8</span>).</p>
