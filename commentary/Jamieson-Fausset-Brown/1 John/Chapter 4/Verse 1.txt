<p><b>1. Beloved</b>—the affectionate
	address wherewith he calls their attention, as to an important
	subject. 
	</p>
<p><b>every spirit</b>—which
	presents itself in the person of a prophet. The Spirit of truth, and
	the spirit of error, speak by men's spirits as their organs. There is
	but one Spirit of truth, and one spirit of Antichrist. 
	</p>
<p><b>try</b>—by the tests
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:2">1 John 4:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:3">1 John 4:3</span>).
	All believers are to do so: not merely ecclesiastics. Even an angel's
	message should be tested by the word of God: much more men's
	teachings, however holy the teachers may seem. 
	</p>
<p><b>because,</b> c.—the reason
	why we must "try," or <i>test</i> the spirits. 
	</p>
<p><b>many false prophets</b>—not
	"prophets" in the sense "foretellers," but organs
	of the spirit that inspires them, <i>teaching</i> accordingly either
	truth or error: "many Antichrists." 
	</p>
<p><b>are gone out</b>—as if from
	God. 
	</p>
<p><b>into the world</b>—said
	alike of good and bad prophets (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:3- :">1 John 4:3- :</span>). The world is easily seduced (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:4">1 John 4:4</span>
<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:5">1 John 4:5</span>).</p>
