<p><b>12.</b> <i>God,</i> whom <i>no man
	hath seen at any time,</i> hath appointed His children as the visible
	recipients of our outward kindness which flows from love to Himself,
	"whom <i>not</i> having <i>seen,</i> we love," compare
	<i>Notes,</i> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:11">1 John 4:11</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:11- :">1 John 4:11- :</span>. Thus <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:12">1 John 4:12</span>
	explains why, instead (in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:11">1 John 4:11</span>)
	of saying, "If God so loved us, we ought also to love <i>God,</i>"
	he said, "We ought also to love <i>one another.</i>" 
	</p>
<p><b>If we love one another, God
	dwelleth in us</b>—for God is love; and it must have been from Him
	dwelling in us that we drew the real love we bear to the brethren
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:8">1 John 4:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:16">1 John 4:16</span>).
	John discusses this in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:16- :">1 John 4:16- :</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>his love</b>—rather, "the
	love of Him," that is, "to Him" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:16- :">1 John 4:16- :</span>), evinced by our love to His representatives, our brethren. 
	</p>
<p><b>is perfected in us</b>—John
	discusses this in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:16- :">1 John 4:16- :</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:5">1 John 2:5</span>,
	"is perfected," that is, attains its proper maturity.</p>
