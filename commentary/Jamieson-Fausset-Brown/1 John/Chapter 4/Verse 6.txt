<p><b>6. We</b>—<i>true teachers</i> of
	Christ: in contrast to <i>them.</i>
</p>
<p><b>are of God</b>—and
	therefore <i>speak of God:</i> in contrast to "speak they of the
	world," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:5">1 John 4:5</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>knoweth God</b>—as his
	Father, being a child "<i>of God</i>" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:13">1 John 2:13</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:14">1 John 2:14</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>heareth us</b>—Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:14- :">1 John 2:14- :</span>, "Every one that is of the truth, heareth My voice."
	</p>
<p><b>Hereby</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:14- :">1 John 2:14- :</span>); by their confessing, or not confessing, Jesus; by the
	kind of reception given them respectively by those who know God, and
	by those who are of the world and not of God. 
	</p>
<p><b>spirit of truth</b>—<i>the
	Spirit</i> which comes from God and teaches <i>truth.</i>
</p>
<p><b>spirit of error</b>—<i>the
	spirit</i> which comes from Satan and seduces into <i>error.</i></p>
