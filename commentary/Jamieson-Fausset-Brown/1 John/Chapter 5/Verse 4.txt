<p><b>4. For</b>—(See on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). The reason why "His commandments are not grievous."
	Though there is a conflict in keeping them, the sue for the whole
	body of the regenerate is victory over every opposing influence;
	meanwhile there is a present <i>joy</i> to each believer in keeping
	them which makes them "not grievous." 
	</p>
<p><b>whatsoever</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"<i>all</i> that is begotten of God." The neuter expresses
	<i>the universal whole,</i> or <i>aggregate of the regenerate,</i>
	regarded as one collective body <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:6">John 3:6</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:37">John 6:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:39">John 6:39</span>,
	"where BENGEL
	remarks, that in Jesus' discourses, what the Father has given Him is
	called, in the singular number and neuter gender, <i>all</i>
	whatsoever; those who come to the <i>Son</i> are described in the
	masculine gender and plural number, <i>they all,</i> or singular,
	<i>every one.</i> The Father has given, as it were, the whole mass to
	the Son, that all whom He gave may be <i>one</i> whole: that
	<i>universal</i> whole the Son singly evolves, in the execution of
	the divine plan." 
	</p>
<p><b>overcometh</b>—habitually. 
	</p>
<p><b>the world</b>—all that is
	opposed to keeping the commandments of God, or draws us off from God,
	in this world, including our corrupt <i>flesh,</i> on which the
	world's blandishments or threats act, as also including Satan, <i>the
	prince of this world</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:31">John 12:31</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:30">John 14:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:11">John 16:11</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>this is the victory that
	overcometh</b>—<i>Greek</i> aorist, ". . . that <i>hath</i>
	(already) <i>overcome</i> the world": the <i>victory</i> (where
	<i>faith</i> is) hereby is implied as having been <i>already obtained</i>
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:13">1 John 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:4">1 John 4:4</span>).</p>
