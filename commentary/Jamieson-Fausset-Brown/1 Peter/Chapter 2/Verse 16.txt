<p><b>16. As free</b>—as "the
	Lord's freemen," connected with <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, <i>doing well as</i> being <i>free.</i> "Well-doing"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:15">1 Peter 2:15</span>) is the natural fruit
	of being <i>freemen</i> of Christ, made free by "the truth"
	from the bondage of sin. Duty is enforced on us to guard against
	licentiousness, but the <i>way</i> in which it is to be fulfilled, is
	by love and the holy instincts of Christian liberty. We are given
	<i>principles,</i> not <i>details.</i>
</p>
<p><b>not using</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"not <i>as having</i> your liberty for a veil (cloak) of
	<i>badness,</i> but as the servants of God," and therefore bound
	to <i>submit to every ordinance of man</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:15- :">1 Peter 2:15- :</span>) which is of God's appointment.</p>
