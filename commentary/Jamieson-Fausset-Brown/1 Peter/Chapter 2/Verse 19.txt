<p><b>19.</b> Reason for subjection even
	to froward masters. 
	</p>
<p><b>thankworthy</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). A course out of the common, and especially <i>praiseworthy</i>
	in the eyes of God: not as Rome interprets, earning merit, and so a
	work of supererogation (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>for conscience toward
	God</b>—literally, "consciousness of God": from a
	conscientious regard to God, more than to men. 
	</p>
<p><b>endure</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"patiently bear up under": as a superimposed burden
	[ALFORD]. 
	</p>
<p><b>grief</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"griefs."</p>
