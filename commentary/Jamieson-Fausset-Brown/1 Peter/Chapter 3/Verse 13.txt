<p><b>13. who . . . will harm you</b>—This
	fearless confidence in God's protection from harm, Christ, the Head,
	in His sufferings realized; so His members. 
	</p>
<p><b>if ye be</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"if ye have <i>become.</i>" 
	</p>
<p><b>followers</b>—The oldest
	manuscripts read "emulous," "zealous of" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>good</b>—The contrast in
	<i>Greek</i> is, "Who will do you <i>evil,</i> if ye be zealous
	of <i>good?</i>"</p>
