<p><b>16. Having a good conscience</b>—the
	secret spring of <i>readiness to give account</i> of our <i>hope.</i>
	So <i>hope</i> and <i>good conscience</i> go together in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:15">Acts 24:15</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:16">Acts 24:16</span>. Profession without
	practice has no weight. But those who <i>have a good conscience</i>
	can afford to give an account of their hope "with meekness."
	</p>
<p><b>whereas</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:16- :">Acts 24:16- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>they speak evil of you, as of
	evildoers</b>—One oldest manuscript reads, "ye are spoken
	against," omitting the rest. 
	</p>
<p><b>falsely accuse</b>—"calumniate";
	the <i>Greek</i> expresses malice shown in deeds as well as in words.
	It is translated, "despitefully use," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:44">Matthew 5:44</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:28">Luke 6:28</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>conversation</b>—life,
	conduct. 
	</p>
<p><b>in Christ</b>—who is the
	very element of your life as Christians. "In Christ"
	defines "good." It is your good walk <i>as Christians,</i>
	not as citizens, that calls forth malice (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:4">1 Peter 4:4</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:5">1 Peter 4:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:14">1 Peter 4:14</span>).</p>
