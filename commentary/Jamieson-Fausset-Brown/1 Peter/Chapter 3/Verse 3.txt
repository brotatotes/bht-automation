<p><b>3.</b> Literally, "To whom let
	there belong (namely, as their peculiar ornament) not the outward
	adornment (usual in the sex which first, by the fall, brought in the
	need of covering, <i>Note,</i> see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>) of," &amp;c. 
	</p>
<p><b>plaiting</b>—artificial
	braiding, in order to attract admiration. 
	</p>
<p><b>wearing</b>—literally,
	"putting round," namely, the head, as a diadem—the arm,
	as a bracelet—the finger, as rings. 
	</p>
<p><b>apparel</b>—showy and
	costly. "Have the blush of modesty on thy face instead of paint,
	and moral worth and discretion instead of gold and emeralds"
	[MELISSA].</p>
