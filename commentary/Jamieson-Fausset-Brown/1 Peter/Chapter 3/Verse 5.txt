<p><b>5. after this manner</b>—with the
	<i>ornament of a meek and quiet spirit</i> (compare the portrait of
	the godly wife, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>trusted</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"hoped." "Holy" is explained by "hoped in
	(so as to be '<i>united to,</i>' <i>Greek</i>) God." Hope in God
	is the spring of true holiness [BENGEL].
	</p>
<p><b>in subjection</b>—Their
	ornament consisted in their subordination. Vanity was forbidden (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>) as being contrary to female <i>subjection.</i></p>
