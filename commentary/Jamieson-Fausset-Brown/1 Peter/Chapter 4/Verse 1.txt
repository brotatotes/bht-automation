<p><b>1. for us</b>—supported by some
	oldest manuscripts and versions, omitted by others. 
	</p>
<p><b>in the flesh</b>—in His
	mortal body of humiliation. 
	</p>
<p><b>arm</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:11">Ephesians 6:11</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:13">Ephesians 6:13</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>the same mind</b>—of
	suffering with patient willingness what God <i>wills</i> you to
	suffer. 
	</p>
<p><b>he that hath suffered</b>—for
	instance, Christ first, and in His person the believer: a general
	proposition. 
	</p>
<p><b>hath ceased</b>—literally,
	"has been made to cease," <i>has obtained</i> by the very
	fact of His having suffered once for all, <i>a cessation from sin,</i>
	which had heretofore lain on Him (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:13- :">Ephesians 6:13- :</span>, especially, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:7">1 Peter 4:7</span>).
	The Christian is by faith one with Christ: as then Christ by death is
	judicially freed from sin; so the Christian who has in the person of
	Christ died, has no more to do with it judicially, and ought to have
	no more to do with it actually. "The flesh" is the sphere
	in which sin has place.</p>
