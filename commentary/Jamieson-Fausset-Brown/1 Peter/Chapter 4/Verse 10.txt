<p><b>10. every</b>—"even as <i>each</i>
	man hath received," in whatever degree, and of whatever kind.
	The Spirit's <i>gifts</i> (literally, "gift <i>of grace,</i>"
	that is, <i>gratuitously</i> bestowed) are the common property of the
	Christian community, each Christian being but a steward for the
	edifying of the whole, not receiving the gift merely for his own use.
	</p>
<p><b>minister the same</b>—not
	discontentedly envying or disparaging <i>the gift of another.</i>
</p>
<p><b>one to another</b>—<i>Greek</i>
	as in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:8">1 Peter 4:8</span>, "towards
	yourselves"; implying that all form but one body, and in seeking
	the good of other members they are promoting the good of <i>themselves.</i>
</p>
<p><b>stewards</b>—referring to
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:15">Matthew 25:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:13-26">Luke 19:13-26</span>.</p>
