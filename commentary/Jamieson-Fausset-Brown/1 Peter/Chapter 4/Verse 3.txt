<p><b>3. may suffice</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"is sufficient." Peter takes the lowest ground: for not
	even the past time ought to have been wasted in lust; but since you
	cannot recall it, at least lay out the future to better account. 
	</p>
<p><b>us</b>—omitted in oldest
	manuscripts. 
	</p>
<p><b>wrought</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"wrought out." 
	</p>
<p><b>Gentiles</b>—heathen: which
	many of you were. 
	</p>
<p><b>when,</b> c.—"walking
	as ye have done [ALFORD]
	in <i>lasciviousness</i>" the <i>Greek</i> means <i>petulant,
	immodest, wantonness,</i> unbridled conduct: not so much filthy lust.
	</p>
<p><b>excess of
	wine</b>—"wine-bibbings" [ALFORD].
	</p>
<p><b>abominable</b>—"nefarious,"
	"lawless idolatries," violating God's most sacred law; not
	that <i>all</i> Peter's readers (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>) <i>walked</i> in these, but many, namely, the Gentile
	portion of them.</p>
