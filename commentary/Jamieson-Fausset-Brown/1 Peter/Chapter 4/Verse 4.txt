<p><b>4. Wherein</b>—In respect to which
	abandonment of your former <i>walk</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>run not with them</b>—eagerly,
	in troops [BENGEL]. 
	</p>
<p><b>excess</b>—literally,
	"profusion"; a sink: stagnant water remaining after an
	inundation. 
	</p>
<p><b>riot</b>—profligacy. 
	</p>
<p><b>speaking evil</b>—charging
	you with pride, singularity, hypocrisy, and secret crimes (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:14">1 Peter 4:14</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:2">2 Peter 2:2</span>). However, there is no
	"of you" in the <i>Greek,</i> but simply "blaspheming."
	It seems to me always to be used, either directly or indirectly, in
	the sense of <i>impious reviling against God, Christ, or the Holy
	Spirit,</i> and the Christian religion, not merely against men as
	such; <i>Greek,</i> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:14">1 Peter 4:14</span>,
	below.</p>
