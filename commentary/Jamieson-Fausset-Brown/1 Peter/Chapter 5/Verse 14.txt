<p><b>14. kiss of charity</b>— <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, "an <i>holy</i> kiss": the token of love to God
	and the brethren. <i>Love</i> and <i>holiness</i> are inseparable.
	Compare the instance, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:37">Acts 20:37</span>.
	</p>
<p><b>Peace</b>—Peter's closing
	salutation; as Paul's is, "Grace be with you," though he
	accompanies it with "peace be to the brethren." "Peace"
	(flowing from <i>salvation</i>) was Christ's own salutation after the
	resurrection, and from Him Peter derives it. 
	</p>
<p><b>be with you all that are in
	Christ Jesus</b>—The oldest manuscripts omit "Jesus." In
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:24">Ephesians 6:24</span>, addressed to the same
	region, the same limitation of the salutation occurs, whence,
	perhaps, Peter here adopts it. Contrast, "Be <i>with you all,</i>"
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:24">Romans 16:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:23">1 Corinthians 16:23</span>.</p>
