<p><b>4. And</b>—"And so": as
	the result of "being ensamples" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>chief Shepherd</b>—the
	title peculiarly Christ's own, not Peter's or the pope's. 
	</p>
<p><b>when . . . shall
	appear</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "be manifested" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). Faith serves the Lord while still unseen. 
	</p>
<p><b>crown</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"<i>stephanos,</i>" a garland of <i>victory,</i> the prize
	in the Grecian games, woven of ivy, parsley, myrtle, olive, or oak.
	<i>Our</i> crown is distinguished from <i>theirs</i> in that it is
	"incorruptible" and "fadeth not away," as the
	leaves of theirs soon did. "The crown <i>of life.</i>" Not
	a <i>kingly</i> "crown" (a different <i>Greek</i> word,
	<i>diadema</i>): the prerogative of the Lord Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>glory</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"<i>the</i> glory," namely, <i>to be</i> then <i>revealed</i>
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:1">1 Peter 5:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:13">1 Peter 4:13</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>that fadeth not away</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"amaranthine" (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:13- :">1 Peter 4:13- :</span>).</p>
