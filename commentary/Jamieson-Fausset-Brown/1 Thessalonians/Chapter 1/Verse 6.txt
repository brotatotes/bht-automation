<p><b>6. And ye</b>—answering to "<i>For
	our</i> Gospel," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:5">1 Thessalonians 1:5</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>followers</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"imitators." The Thessalonians in their turn became
	"ensamples" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:7">1 Thessalonians 1:7</span>)
	for others to <i>imitate.</i>
</p>
<p><b>of the Lord</b>—who was the
	apostle of the Father, and taught the word, which He brought from
	heaven, under adversities [BENGEL].
	This was the point in which they imitated Him and His apostles,
	<i>joyful</i> witness for <i>the word in much affliction:</i> the
	second proof of their <i>election of God</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:7- :">1 Thessalonians 1:7- :</span>); <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:5">1 Thessalonians 1:5</span> is the first
	(see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:5">1 Thessalonians 1:5</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>received the word in much
	affliction</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:14">1 Thessalonians 2:14</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:2-5">1 Thessalonians 3:2-5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:5-10">Acts 17:5-10</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>joy of</b>—that is, <i>wrought</i>
	by "the Holy Ghost." "The oil of gladness"
	wherewith the Son of God was "anointed above His fellows"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+45:7">Psalms 45:7</span>), is the same oil with
	which He, by the Spirit, anoints His fellows too (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+61:1">Isaiah 61:1</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+61:3">Isaiah 61:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:17">Romans 14:17</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:20">1 John 2:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:27">1 John 2:27</span>).</p>
