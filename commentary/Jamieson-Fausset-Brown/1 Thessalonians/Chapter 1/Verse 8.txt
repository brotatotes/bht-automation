<p><b>8. from you sounded . . . the word
	of the Lord</b>—not that they actually became missionaries: but
	they, by the <i>report</i> which spread abroad of their "faith"
	(compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:8">Romans 1:8</span>), and by
	Christian merchants of Thessalonica who travelled in various
	directions, bearing "the word of the Lord" with them, were
	<i>virtually</i> missionaries, recommending the Gospel to all within
	reach of their influence by word and by example (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:8- :">Romans 1:8- :</span>). In "sounded," the image is that of a trumpet
	filling with its clear-sounding echo all the surrounding places. 
	</p>
<p><b>to God-ward</b>—no longer
	directed to idols. 
	</p>
<p><b>so that we need not to speak
	any thing</b>—to them in praise of your faith; "for (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:8- :">Romans 1:8- :</span>) they themselves" (the people in Macedonia, Achaia, and
	in every place) know it already.</p>
