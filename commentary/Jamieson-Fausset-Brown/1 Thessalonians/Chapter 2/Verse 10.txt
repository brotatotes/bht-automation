<p><b>10. Ye are witnesses</b>—as to our
	outward conduct. 
	</p>
<p><b>God</b>—as to our inner
	motives. 
	</p>
<p><b>holily</b>—towards God. 
	</p>
<p><b>justly</b>—towards men. 
	</p>
<p><b>unblamably</b>—in relation
	to ourselves. 
	</p>
<p><b>behaved ourselves</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"were made to be," namely, by God. 
	</p>
<p><b>among you that
	believe</b>—rather, "<i>before</i> (that is, in the eyes of)
	you that believe"; whatever we may have seemed in the eyes of
	the unbelieving. As <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:9">1 Thessalonians 2:9</span>
	refers to their outward occupation in the world; so <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:9- :">1 Thessalonians 2:9- :</span>, to their character among believers.</p>
