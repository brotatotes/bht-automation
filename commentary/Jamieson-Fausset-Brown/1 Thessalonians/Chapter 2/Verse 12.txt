<p><b>12. worthy of God</b>—"worthy
	<i>of the Lord</i>" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>); "worthily <i>of the saints</i>" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, <i>Greek</i>): ". . . <i>of the Gospel</i>" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>) ". . . <i>of the vocation</i> wherewith ye are called"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:1">Ephesians 4:1</span>). Inconsistency would
	cause God's name to be "blasphemed among the Gentiles" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:1- :">Ephesians 4:1- :</span>). The <i>Greek</i> article is emphatical, "Worthy of
	THE God who is calling
	you." 
	</p>
<p><b>hath called</b>—So one of
	the oldest manuscripts and <i>Vulgate.</i> Other oldest manuscripts,
	"Who <i>calleth</i> us." 
	</p>
<p><b>his kingdom</b>—to be set
	up at the Lord's coming. 
	</p>
<p><b>glory</b>—that ye may share
	His glory (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:22">John 17:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+3:4">Colossians 3:4</span>).</p>
