<p><b>2. even after that we had suffered
	before</b>—at Philippi (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>): a circumstance which would have deterred mere natural,
	unspiritual men from further preaching. 
	</p>
<p><b>shamefully
	entreated</b>—ignominiously scourged (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:22">Acts 16:22</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:23">Acts 16:23</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>bold</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:29">Acts 4:29</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:20">Ephesians 6:20</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>in our God</b>—The ground
	of our boldness in speaking was the realization of God as "OUR
	God." 
	</p>
<p><b>with much contention</b>—that
	is, literally, as of <i>competitors in a contest:</i> striving
	earnestness (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+1:29">Colossians 1:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+2:1">Colossians 2:1</span>).
	But here <i>outward</i> conflict with persecutors, rather than <i>inward</i>
	and mental, was what the missionaries had to endure (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:5">Acts 17:5</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:6">Acts 17:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+1:30">Philippians 1:30</span>).</p>
