<p><b>11.</b> Translate, "<i>May</i>
	God Himself, <i>even</i> our Father (there being but one article in
	the <i>Greek,</i> requires this translation, 'He who is at once God
	and our Father'), direct," c. The "Himself" stands in
	contrast with "we" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>) <i>we</i> desired to come but could not through Satan's
	hindrance; but if God <i>Himself</i> direct our way (as we pray),
	none can hinder Him (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:16">2 Thessalonians 2:16</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:17">2 Thessalonians 2:17</span>). It is a remarkable
	proof of <i>the unity of the Father and Son,</i> that in the <i>Greek</i>
	here, and in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:16">2 Thessalonians 2:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:17">2 Thessalonians 2:17</span>,
	the verb is <i>singular,</i> implying that the subject, the Father
	and Son, are but <i>one in essential Being,</i> not in mere unity of
	will. Almost all the chapters in both Epistles to the Thessalonians
	are sealed, each with its own prayer (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:23">1 Thessalonians 5:23</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:11">2 Thessalonians 1:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:16">2 Thessalonians 2:16</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:5">2 Thessalonians 3:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:16">2 Thessalonians 3:16</span>)
	[BENGEL]. Paul does not
	think the prosperous issue of a journey an unfit subject for prayer
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:10">Romans 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:32">Romans 15:32</span>)
	[EDMUNDS]. His prayer,
	though the answer was deferred, in about five years afterwards was
	fulfilled in his return to Macedonia.</p>
