<p><b>6.</b> Join "now" with
	"come"; "But Timotheus having <i>just now come</i>
	from you unto us" [ALFORD].
	Thus it appears (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:5">Acts 18:5</span>)
	Paul is writing from Corinth. 
	</p>
<p><b>your faith and charity</b>—
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:3">1 Thessalonians 1:3</span>; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:3- :">1 Thessalonians 1:3- :</span>, whence it seems their faith subsequently increased still
	more). <i>Faith</i> was the solid foundation: <i>charity</i> the
	cement which held together the superstructure of their practice on
	that foundation. In that <i>charity</i> was included their "good
	(kindly) remembrance" of their teachers. 
	</p>
<p><b>desiring greatly</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"having a yearning desire for." 
	</p>
<p><b>we also</b>—The desires of
	loving friends for one another's presence are reciprocal.</p>
