<p><b>9. For what thanks</b>—<i>what
	sufficient</i> thanks? 
	</p>
<p><b>render . . . again</b>—in
	return for His goodness (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>for you</b>—"concerning
	you." 
	</p>
<p><b>for all the joy</b>—on
	account of all the joy. It was "comfort," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, now it is more, namely, <i>joy.</i>
</p>
<p><b>for your sakes</b>—on your
	account. 
	</p>
<p><b>before our God</b>—It is a
	joy which will bear God's searching eye: a joy as in the presence of
	God, not self-seeking, but disinterested, sincere, and spiritual
	(compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:20">1 Thessalonians 2:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:11">John 15:11</span>).</p>
