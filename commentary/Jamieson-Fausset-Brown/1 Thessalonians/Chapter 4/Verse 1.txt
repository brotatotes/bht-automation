<p><b>1. Furthermore</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"As to what remains." Generally used towards the close of
	his Epistles (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:10">Ephesians 6:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:8">Philippians 4:8</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>then</b>—with a view to the
	<i>love</i> and <i>holiness</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:12">1 Thessalonians 3:12</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:13">1 Thessalonians 3:13</span>) which we have just
	prayed for in your behalf, we now give you exhortation. 
	</p>
<p><b>beseech</b>—"ask"
	as if it were a personal favor. 
	</p>
<p><b>by,</b> c.—rather as <i>Greek,</i>
	"IN the Lord Jesus"
	in communion with the Lord Jesus, as Christian ministers dealing with
	Christian people [EDMUNDS].
	</p>
<p><b>as ye . . . received</b>—when
	we were with you (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:13">1 Thessalonians 2:13</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>how</b>—<i>Greek,</i> the
	"how," that is, <i>the manner.</i>
</p>
<p><b>walk and . . . please
	God</b>—that is, "<i>and so</i> please God," namely, by
	your walk; in contrast to the Jews who "please not God"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:15">1 Thessalonians 2:15</span>). The oldest
	manuscripts add a clause here, "even as also ye do walk"
	(compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:10">1 Thessalonians 4:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:11">1 Thessalonians 5:11</span>).
	These words, which he was able to say of them with truth, conciliate
	a favorable hearing for the precepts which follow. Also the
	expression, "abound <i>more and more,</i>" implies that
	there had gone before a recognition of their already in some measure
	<i>walking so.</i></p>
