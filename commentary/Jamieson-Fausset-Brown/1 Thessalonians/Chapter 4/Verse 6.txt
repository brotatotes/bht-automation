<p><b>6. go beyond</b>—transgress the
	bounds of rectitude in respect to his "brother." 
	</p>
<p><b>defraud</b>—"overreach"
	[ALFORD]; "take
	advantage of" [EDMUNDS].
	</p>
<p><b>in any matter</b>—rather as
	<i>Greek,</i> "in <i>the</i> matter"; a decorous expression
	for the matter now in question; the conjugal honor of his neighbor as
	a husband, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:4">1 Thessalonians 4:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:4- :">1 Thessalonians 4:4- :</span> also confirms this view; the word "brother"
	enhances the enormity of the crime. It is your <i>brother</i> whom
	you wrong (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:4- :">1 Thessalonians 4:4- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>the Lord</b>—the coming
	Judge (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:7">2 Thessalonians 1:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:8">2 Thessalonians 1:8</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>avenger</b>—the Righter. 
	</p>
<p><b>of all such</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"concerning all <i>these things;</i>" in all such cases of
	wrongs against a neighbor's conjugal honor. 
	</p>
<p><b>testified</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"<i>constantly</i> testified [ALFORD].</p>
