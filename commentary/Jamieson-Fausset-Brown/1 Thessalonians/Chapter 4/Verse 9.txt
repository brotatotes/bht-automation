<p><b>9. brotherly love,</b> c.—referring
	here to acts of brotherly kindness in relieving distressed brethren.
	Some oldest manuscripts support <i>English Version</i> reading, "YE
	have" others, and those the weightiest, read, "WE
	have." <i>We</i> need not write, as <i>ye yourselves</i> are
	taught, and that by <i>God:</i> namely, in the heart by the Holy
	Spirit (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:45">John 6:45</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:11">Hebrews 8:11</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:20">1 John 2:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:27">1 John 2:27</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>to love</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"with a view to," or "to the end of your loving one
	another." Divine teachings have their confluence in love
	[BENGEL].</p>
