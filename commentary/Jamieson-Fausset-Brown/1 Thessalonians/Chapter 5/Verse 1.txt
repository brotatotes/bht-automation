<p><b>1. times</b>—the general and
	indefinite term for chronological periods. 
	</p>
<p><b>seasons</b>—the <i>opportune
	times</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="da+7:12">Daniel 7:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:7">Acts 1:7</span>).
	<i>Time</i> denotes quantity; <i>season,</i> quality. <i>Seasons</i>
	are parts of <i>times.</i>
</p>
<p><b>ye have no need</b>—those
	who watch do not need to be told <i>when</i> the hour will come, for
	they are always ready [BENGEL].
	</p>
<p><b>cometh</b>—present:
	expressing its <i>speedy</i> and awful <i>certainty.</i></p>
