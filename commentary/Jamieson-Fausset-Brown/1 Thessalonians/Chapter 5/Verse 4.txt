<p><b>4. not in darkness</b>—not in
	darkness of understanding (that is, spiritual ignorance) or of the
	moral nature (that is, a state of sin), <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>that</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "in
	order that"; with God results are all purposed. 
	</p>
<p><b>that day</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"THE day"; the
	<i>day</i> of the Lord (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:25">Hebrews 10:25</span>,
	"<i>the</i> day"), in contrast to "darkness." 
	</p>
<p><b>overtake</b>—unexpectedly
	(compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:35">John 12:35</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>as a thief</b>—The two
	oldest manuscripts read, "as (the daylight overtakes) <i>thieves</i>"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="job+24:17">Job 24:17</span>). Old manuscripts
	and <i>Vulgate</i> read as <i>English Version.</i></p>
