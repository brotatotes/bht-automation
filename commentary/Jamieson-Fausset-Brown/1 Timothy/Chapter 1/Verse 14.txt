<p><b>14. And</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "But."
	Not only so (was <i>mercy</i> shown me), but 
	</p>
<p><b>the grace</b>—by which "I
	obtained mercy" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:13">1 Timothy 1:13</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>was exceeding abundant</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"superabounded." Where sin abounded, grace did much more
	abound" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:20">Romans 5:20</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>with faith</b>—<i>accompanied
	with</i> faith, the opposite of "unbelief" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:20- :">Romans 5:20- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>love</b>—in contrast to "a
	blasphemer, persecutor, and injurious." 
	</p>
<p><b>which is in Christ</b>—as
	its element and home [ALFORD]:
	here as its source whence it flows to us.</p>
