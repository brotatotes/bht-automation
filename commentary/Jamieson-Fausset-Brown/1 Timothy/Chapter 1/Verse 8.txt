<p><b>8. But</b>—"<i>Now</i> we
	know" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:19">Romans 3:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:14">Romans 7:14</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>law is good</b>—in full
	agreement with God's holiness and goodness. 
	</p>
<p><b>if a man</b>—primarily, <i>a
	teacher;</i> then, every Christian. 
	</p>
<p><b>use it lawfully</b>—in its
	lawful place in the Gospel economy, namely, not as a means of a
	"'righteous man" attaining higher perfection than could be
	attained by the Gospel alone (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:8">1 Timothy 4:8</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:14">Titus 1:14</span>), which was the
	perverted use to which the false teachers put it, but as a means of
	awakening the sense of sin in the ungodly (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:9">1 Timothy 1:9</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:10">1 Timothy 1:10</span>; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:7-12">Romans 7:7-12</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:21">Galatians 3:21</span>).</p>
