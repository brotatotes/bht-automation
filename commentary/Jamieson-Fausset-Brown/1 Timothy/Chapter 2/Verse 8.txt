<p><b>8. I will</b>—The <i>active</i>
	wish, or desire, is meant. 
	</p>
<p><b>that men</b>—rather as
	<i>Greek,</i> "that <i>the</i> men," as distinguished from
	"the women," to whom he has something different to say from
	what he said to the men (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:9-12">1 Timothy 2:9-12</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:14">1 Corinthians 11:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:15">1 Corinthians 11:15</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:34">1 Corinthians 14:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:35">1 Corinthians 14:35</span>).
	The <i>emphasis,</i> however, is not on this, but on the precept of
	<i>praying,</i> resumed from <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:35- :">1 Corinthians 14:35- :</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>everywhere</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"in every place," namely, of public prayer. Fulfilling <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:35- :">1 Corinthians 14:35- :</span>, "<i>In every place</i> . . . from the rising of the
	sun even unto the going down of the same . . . incense shall be
	offered unto My name"; and Jesus' words, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:20">Matthew 18:20</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:21">John 4:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:23">John 4:23</span>.
	</p>
<p><b>lifting up holy hands</b>—The
	early Christians turned up their palms towards heaven, as those
	craving help do. So also Solomon (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ki+8:22">1 Kings 8:22</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+141:2">Psalms 141:2</span>). The Jews washed their
	hands before prayer (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+26:6">Psalms 26:6</span>).
	Paul figuratively (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="job+17:9">Job 17:9</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:8">James 4:8</span>) uses language alluding
	to this custom here: so <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+1:15">Isaiah 1:15</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+1:16">Isaiah 1:16</span>. The <i>Greek</i> for
	"holy" means hands <i>which have committed no impiety,</i>
	and <i>observed every sacred duty.</i> This (or at least the contrite
	desire to be so) is a needful qualification for effectual prayer
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+24:3">Psalms 24:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+24:4">Psalms 24:4</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>without wrath</b>—<i>putting</i>
	it <i>away</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:23">Matthew 5:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:24">Matthew 5:24</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:15">Matthew 6:15</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>doubting</b>—rather,
	"disputing," as the <i>Greek</i> is translated in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:15- :">Matthew 6:15- :</span>. Such things <i>hinder prayer</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:46">Luke 9:46</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:1">Romans 14:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:7">1 Peter 3:7</span>).
	BENGEL supports <i>English
	Version</i> (compare an instance, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ki+7:2">2 Kings 7:2</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:31">Matthew 14:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:22-24">Mark 11:22-24</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:6">James 1:6</span>).</p>
