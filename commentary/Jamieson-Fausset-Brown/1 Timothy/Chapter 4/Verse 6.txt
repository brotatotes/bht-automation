<p><b>6. If thou put . . . in
	remembrance</b>—rather as <i>Greek,</i> "If thou <i>suggest</i>
	to (bring under the notice of) the brethren," c. 
	</p>
<p><b>these things</b>—namely,
	the truths stated in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:4">1 Timothy 4:4</span>
<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:5">1 Timothy 4:5</span>, in opposition to the
	errors foretold, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:1-3">1 Timothy 4:1-3</span>.
	</p>
<p><b>minister</b>—"servant."
	</p>
<p><b>nourished up</b>—The <i>Greek</i>
	is <i>present,</i> not <i>past:</i> "<i>continually being</i>
	nourished in" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:5">2 Timothy 1:5</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:14">2 Timothy 3:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:15">2 Timothy 3:15</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>the words of faith</b>—rather,
	"the words of <i>the</i> faith" (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:15- :">2 Timothy 3:15- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>good doctrine</b>—"the
	good <i>teaching.</i>" Explanatory of "the faith," in
	opposition to the "teachings of demons" (<i>English
	Version,</i> "doctrines of devils," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:15- :">2 Timothy 3:15- :</span>) which Timothy was to counteract. Compare "sound
	doctrine" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:10">1 Timothy 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:3">1 Timothy 6:3</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:9">Titus 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:1">Titus 2:1</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>whereunto thou hast
	attained</b>—"the <i>course</i> of which thou hast <i>followed</i>";
	hast <i>followed along</i> by tracing its course and accompanying it
	[ALFORD]. Thou hast begun
	to follow up [BENGEL]. The
	same <i>Greek</i> occurs, "thou hast fully known" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:1- :">Titus 2:1- :</span>), "having had perfect understanding" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:1- :">Titus 2:1- :</span>). It is an undesigned coincidence that the <i>Greek</i> verb
	is used only by Paul and <i>Paul's companion,</i> Luke.</p>
