<p><b>7. refuse</b>—reject, avoid, <i>have
	nothing to do with</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:23">2 Timothy 2:23</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:10">Titus 3:10</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>old wives' fables</b>—anile
	myths (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:4">1 Timothy 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:9">1 Timothy 1:9</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:14">Titus 1:14</span>). They are "profane,"
	because leading away from "godliness" or "piety"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:4-7">1 Timothy 1:4-7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:20">1 Timothy 6:20</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:16">2 Timothy 2:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:1">Titus 1:1</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:2">Titus 1:2</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>exercise thyself</b>—literally,
	"exercise thyself" as one undergoing training in a
	gymnasium. Let thy self-discipline be not in ascetical exercises as
	the false teachers (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:3">1 Timothy 4:3</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:8">1 Timothy 4:8</span>; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:22">2 Timothy 2:22</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:23">2 Timothy 2:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:14">Hebrews 5:14</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:11">Hebrews 12:11</span>), but with a view to
	godliness or "piety" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:11">1 Timothy 6:11</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:12">1 Timothy 6:12</span>).</p>
