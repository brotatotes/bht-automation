<p><b>10. for good works</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"IN <i>honourable</i>
	(excellent) works"; the sphere or element <i>in</i> which the
	good report of her had place (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). This answers to <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:7">1 Timothy 3:7</span>,
	as to the bishop or presbyter, "He must have a good report of
	them which are without." 
	</p>
<p><b>if</b>—if, in addition to
	being "well reported of." 
	</p>
<p><b>she . . . brought up
	children</b>—either her own (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:4">1 Timothy 3:4</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:12">1 Timothy 3:12</span>), or those of others,
	which is one of the "good works"; a qualification adapting
	her for ministry to orphan children, and to mothers of families. 
	</p>
<p><b>lodged strangers</b>— <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:12- :">1 Timothy 3:12- :</span>, "given to hospitality" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:12- :">1 Timothy 3:12- :</span>); in the case of <i>presbyters.</i>
</p>
<p><b>washed . . . saints'
	feet</b>—after the example of the Lord (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:12- :">1 Timothy 3:12- :</span>); a specimen of the universal spirit of humbly "by
	love serving one another," which actuated the early Christians. 
	</p>
<p><b>relieved the
	afflicted</b>—whether by pecuniary or other relief. 
	</p>
<p><b>followed . . . good</b>—
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:15">1 Thessalonians 5:15</span>; compare instances in
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:35">Matthew 25:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:36">Matthew 25:36</span>).</p>
