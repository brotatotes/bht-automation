<p><b>13. withal</b>—"at the same
	time, moreover." 
	</p>
<p><b>learn</b>—usually in a good
	sense. But these women's "learning" is <i>idleness,
	trifling,</i> and <i>busybodies' tattle.</i>
</p>
<p><b>wandering</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"going about." 
	</p>
<p><b>from house to house</b>—of
	the members of the Church (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:6">2 Timothy 3:6</span>).
	"They carry the affairs of this house to that, and of that to
	this; they tell the affairs of all to all" [THEOPHYLACT].
	</p>
<p><b>tattlers</b>—literally
	"<i>trifling</i> talkers." In <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:6- :">2 Timothy 3:6- :</span>, translated "prating." 
	</p>
<p><b>busybodies</b>—mischievously
	<i>busy;</i> inconsiderately <i>curious</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:6- :">2 Timothy 3:6- :</span>). <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:19">Acts 19:19</span>,
	"curious," the same <i>Greek. Curiosity</i> usually springs
	from idleness, which is itself the mother of <i>garrulity</i>
	[CALVIN]. 
	</p>
<p><b>speaking</b>—not merely
	"<i>saying.</i>" The <i>subject-matter,</i> as well as the
	<i>form,</i> is involved in the <i>Greek</i> word [ALFORD].
	</p>
<p><b>which they ought not</b>—
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:11">Titus 1:11</span>).</p>
