<p><b>6. she that liveth in pleasure</b>—the
	opposite of such a widow as is described in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, and therefore one utterly undeserving of Church charity. The
	<i>Greek</i> expresses <i>wanton prodigality</i> and excess
	[TITTMANN]. The root
	expresses <i>weaving</i> at a fast rate, and so lavish excess (see on
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>dead while she liveth</b>—dead
	in the Spirit while alive in the flesh (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:22">Matthew 8:22</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:14">Ephesians 5:14</span>).</p>
