<p><b>1. servants</b>—to be taken as
	predicated thus, "Let as many as are under the yoke (as) slaves"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:9">Titus 2:9</span>). The exhortation is
	natural as there was a danger of Christian slaves inwardly feeling
	above their heathen masters. 
	</p>
<p><b>their own masters</b>—The
	phrase "their own," is an argument for submissiveness; it
	is not <i>strangers,</i> but <i>their own masters</i> whom they are
	required to respect. 
	</p>
<p><b>all honour</b>—<i>all</i>
	possible and fitting <i>honor;</i> not merely outward subjection, but
	that inward <i>honor</i> from which will flow spontaneously right
	outward conduct (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:9- :">Titus 2:9- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>that the name of God</b>—by
	which Christians are called. 
	</p>
<p><b>blasphemed</b>—Heathen
	masters would say, What kind of a God must be the God of the
	Christians, when such are the fruits of His worship (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:24">Romans 2:24</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:5">Titus 2:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:10">Titus 2:10</span>)?</p>
