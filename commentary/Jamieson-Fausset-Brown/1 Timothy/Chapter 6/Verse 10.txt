<p><b>10. the love of money</b>—not the
	money itself, but the <i>love</i> of it—the <i>wishing to be rich</i>
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:9">1 Timothy 6:9</span>) —"is <i>a</i>
	root (ELLICOTT and
	MIDDLETON: not as <i>English
	Version,</i> '<i>the</i> root') of all <i>evils.</i>" (So the
	<i>Greek</i> plural). The wealthiest may be rich not in a bad sense;
	the poorest may covet to be so (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:9- :">1 Timothy 6:9- :</span>). <i>Love of money</i> is not the sole root of evils, but
	it is a leading "root of bitterness" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:9- :">1 Timothy 6:9- :</span>), for "it destroys faith, the root of all that is
	good" [BENGEL]; its
	offshoots are "temptation, a snare, lusts, destruction,
	perdition." 
	</p>
<p><b>coveted after</b>—lusted
	after. 
	</p>
<p><b>erred from</b>—literally,
	"have been made to err from the faith" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:19">1 Timothy 1:19</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:1">1 Timothy 4:1</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>pierced</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:1- :">1 Timothy 4:1- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>with . . . sorrows</b>—"pains":
	"thorns" of the parable (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:1- :">1 Timothy 4:1- :</span>) which choke the word of "faith." "The
	prosperity of fools destroys them" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:1- :">1 Timothy 4:1- :</span>). BENGEL and
	WIESINGER make them the
	gnawings of conscience, producing remorse for wealth badly acquired;
	the harbingers of the future "perdition" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:1- :">1 Timothy 4:1- :</span>).</p>
