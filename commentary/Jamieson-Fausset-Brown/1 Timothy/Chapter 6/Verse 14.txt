<p><b>14. keep this commandment</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"the commandment," that is, the Gospel rule of life
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:5">1 Timothy 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:34">John 13:34</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:21">2 Peter 2:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:2">2 Peter 3:2</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>without spot,
	unrebukeable</b>—agreeing with "thou." Keep the
	commandment and so be without spot," c. "Pure" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:22">1 Timothy 5:22</span>
<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:27">Ephesians 5:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:27">James 1:27</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:14">2 Peter 3:14</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>until the appearing of . . .
	Christ</b>—<i>His coming in person</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:8">2 Thessalonians 2:8</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:13">Titus 2:13</span>). Believers then used in
	their practice to set before themselves the day of Christ as near at
	hand; we, the hour of death [BENGEL].
	The fact has in all ages of the Church been certain, the time as
	uncertain to Paul, as it is to us; hence, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:13- :">Titus 2:13- :</span>, he says, "in HIS
	times": the Church's true attitude is that of continual
	expectation of her Lord's return (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:8">1 Corinthians 1:8</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+1:6">Philippians 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+1:10">Philippians 1:10</span>).</p>
