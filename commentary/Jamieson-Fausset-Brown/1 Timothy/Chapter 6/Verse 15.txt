<p><b>15. in his times</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"<i>His own</i> [fitting] times" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). The plural implies successive stages in the manifestation
	of the kingdom of God, each having its own appropriate time, the
	regulating principle and knowledge of which rests with the Father
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:6">1 Timothy 2:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:9">2 Timothy 1:9</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:3">Titus 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:1">Hebrews 1:1</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>he shall show</b>—"display":
	an expression appropriate in reference to His "APPEARING,"
	which is stronger than His "coming," and implies its
	<i>visibility;</i> "manifest": <i>make visible</i> (compare
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:20">Acts 3:20</span>): "He" is <i>the
	Father</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:16">1 Timothy 6:16</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>blessed</b>—in Himself: so
	about to be the source of <i>blessing</i> to His people at Christ
	appearing, whence flows their "blessed hope" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:11">1 Timothy 1:11</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:13">Titus 2:13</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>only</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:3">John 17:3</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:27">Romans 16:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+15:4">Revelation 15:4</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>King of kings</b>—elsewhere
	applied also to Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+1:5">Revelation 1:5</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+17:14">Revelation 17:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+19:16">Revelation 19:16</span>).</p>
