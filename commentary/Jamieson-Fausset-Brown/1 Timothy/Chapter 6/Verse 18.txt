<p><b>18. do good</b>—like God Himself
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+119:68">Psalms 119:68</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:17">Acts 14:17</span>)
	and Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:38">Acts 10:38</span>).
	TITTMANN translates, "to
	do," or "act well"; as the <i>Greek</i> for "to
	be beneficent" is a distinct word, <i>agathopoiein.</i>
</p>
<p><b>rich in good works</b>—so
	"rich in faith," which produces good works (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:38- :">Acts 10:38- :</span>). Contrasted with "rich in this world," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:38- :">Acts 10:38- :</span>. Literally, it is "rich in honorable (right) works."
	<i>Greek,</i> "<i>kalois,</i>" "<i>ergois,</i>"
	are works good or <i>right</i> in themselves: "<i>agathois,</i>"
	good to another. 
	</p>
<p><b>ready to distribute</b>—free
	givers [ALFORD]; the heart
	not cleaving to possessions, but ready to impart to others. 
	</p>
<p><b>willing to communicate</b>—ready
	<i>contributors</i> [ALFORD]:
	liberal in admitting others to share our goods in <i>common with</i>
	ourselves (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:6">Galatians 6:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:16">Hebrews 13:16</span>).</p>
