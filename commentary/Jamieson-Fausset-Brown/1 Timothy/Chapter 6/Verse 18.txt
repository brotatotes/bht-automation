18. do good—like God Himself
	(Psalms 119:68; Acts 14:17)
	and Christ (Acts 10:38).
	TITTMANN translates, "to
	do," or "act well"; as the Greek for "to
	be beneficent" is a distinct word, agathopoiein.
rich in good works—so
	"rich in faith," which produces good works (Acts 10:38- :). Contrasted with "rich in this world," Acts 10:38- :. Literally, it is "rich in honorable (right) works."
	Greek, "kalois," "ergois,"
	are works good or right in themselves: "agathois,"
	good to another. 
	ready to distribute—free
	givers [ALFORD]; the heart
	not cleaving to possessions, but ready to impart to others. 
	willing to communicate—ready
	contributors [ALFORD]:
	liberal in admitting others to share our goods in common with
	ourselves (Galatians 6:6; Hebrews 13:16).
