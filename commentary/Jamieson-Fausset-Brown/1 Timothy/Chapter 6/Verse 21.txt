<p><b>21. Which some professing</b>—namely,
	professing these <i>oppositions of science falsely so called.</i>
</p>
<p><b>erred</b>—(See on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:11">1 Timothy 2:11</span>) —literally,
	"missed the mark" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:7">2 Timothy 3:7</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:8">2 Timothy 3:8</span>). True sagacity is
	inseparable from faith. 
	</p>
<p><b>Grace</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"<i>the</i> grace," namely, of God, for which we Christians
	look, and in which we stand [ALFORD].
	</p>
<p><b>be with thee</b>—He
	restricts the salutation to Timothy, as the Epistle was not to be
	read in public [BENGEL].
	But the oldest manuscripts read, "be with you"; and the
	"thee" may be a transcriber's alteration to harmonize with
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:22">2 Timothy 4:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:15">Titus 3:15</span>.
	</p>
<p><b>Amen</b>—omitted in the
	oldest manuscripts.</p>
