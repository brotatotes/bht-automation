<p><b>4. He is proud</b>—literally,
	"wrapt in smoke"; filled with the fumes of self-conceit
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:6">1 Timothy 3:6</span>) while "knowing
	nothing," namely, of the doctrine which is according to
	godliness (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:3">1 Timothy 6:3</span>), though
	arrogating pre-eminent knowledge (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:3- :">1 Timothy 6:3- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>doting about</b>—literally,
	"<i>sick</i> about"; the opposite of "<i>wholesome</i>"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:3">1 Timothy 6:3</span>). <i>Truth</i> is not
	the center <i>about</i> which his investigations move, but mere
	<i>word-strifes.</i>
</p>
<p><b>questions</b>—of
	controversy. 
	</p>
<p><b>strifes of words</b>—rather
	than about <i>realities</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:3- :">1 Timothy 6:3- :</span>). These stand with them instead of "godliness" and
	"wholesome words" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:3">1 Timothy 6:3</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:4">1 Timothy 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:9">Titus 3:9</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>evil surmisings</b>—as to
	those who are of a different party from themselves.</p>
