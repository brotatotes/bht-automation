4. He is proud—literally,
	"wrapt in smoke"; filled with the fumes of self-conceit
	(1 Timothy 3:6) while "knowing
	nothing," namely, of the doctrine which is according to
	godliness (1 Timothy 6:3), though
	arrogating pre-eminent knowledge (1 Timothy 6:3- :). 
	doting about—literally,
	"sick about"; the opposite of "wholesome"
	(1 Timothy 6:3). Truth is not
	the center about which his investigations move, but mere
	word-strifes.
questions—of
	controversy. 
	strifes of words—rather
	than about realities (1 Timothy 6:3- :). These stand with them instead of "godliness" and
	"wholesome words" (1 Timothy 6:3;
	1 Timothy 1:4; Titus 3:9).
	evil surmisings—as to
	those who are of a different party from themselves.
