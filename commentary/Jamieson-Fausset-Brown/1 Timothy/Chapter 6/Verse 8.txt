8. And—Greek, "But."
	In contrast to the greedy gain-seekers ( :-). 
	having—so long as we
	have food. (The Greek expresses "food sufficient in each
	case for our continually recurring wants" [ALFORD]).
	It is implied that we, as believers, shall have this ( :-). 
	raiment—Greek,
	"covering"; according to some including a roof to cover
	us, that is, a dwelling, as well as clothing. 
	let us be therewith
	content—literally, "we shall be sufficiently provided";
	"we shall be sufficed" [ALFORD].
