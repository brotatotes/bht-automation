<p><b>4. us</b>—idiomatic for <i>me</i>
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:18">1 Thessalonians 2:18</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>that we may . . . comfort
	them which are in any trouble</b>—Translate, as the <i>Greek</i> is
	the same as before, "tribulation." The apostle lived, not
	to himself, but to the Church; so, whatever graces God conferred on
	him, he considered granted not for himself alone, but that he might
	have the greater ability to help others [CALVIN].
	So participation in all the afflictions of man peculiarly qualified
	Jesus to be man's comforter in all his various afflictions (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+50:4-6">Isaiah 50:4-6</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:15">Hebrews 4:15</span>).</p>
