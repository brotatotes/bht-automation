<p><b>9. But</b>—"Yea." 
	</p>
<p><b>in God which raiseth the
	dead</b>—We had so given up all thoughts of life, that our only
	hope was fixed on the coming resurrection; so in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span> his hope of the resurrection was what buoyed him up in
	contending with foes, savage as wild beasts. Here he touches only on
	the doctrine of the resurrection, taking it for granted that its
	truth is admitted by the Corinthians, and urging its bearing on their
	practice.</p>
