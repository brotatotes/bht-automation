<p><b>10. letters</b>—implying that
	there had been already more letters of Paul received by the
	Corinthians than the one we have, namely, First Corinthians; and that
	they contained strong reproofs. 
	</p>
<p><b>say they</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"says one," "such a one" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>) seems to point to some definite individual. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>; a similar slanderer was in the Galatian Church. 
	</p>
<p><b>weak</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:7">2 Corinthians 12:7</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:3">1 Corinthians 2:3</span>). There was nothing of
	majesty or authority in his manner; he bore himself tremblingly among
	them, whereas the false teachers spoke with authoritative bearing and
	language.</p>
