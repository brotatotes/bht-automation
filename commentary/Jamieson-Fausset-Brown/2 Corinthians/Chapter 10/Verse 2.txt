<p><b>2. I beseech you</b>—Intimating
	that, as he can <i>beseech</i> in letters, so he can be severe in
	their presence. 
	</p>
<p><b>that I may not be</b>—that
	I may not <i>have to</i> be bold, &amp;c. 
	</p>
<p><b>with that confidence</b>—<i>that
	authoritative sternness.</i>
</p>
<p><b>I think</b>—I <i>am minded</i>
	to be. 
	</p>
<p><b>as if we walked according to
	the flesh</b>—His Corinthian detractors judged of him by
	themselves, as if he were influenced by fleshly motives, the desire
	of favor or fear of giving offense, so as not to exercise his
	authority when present.</p>
