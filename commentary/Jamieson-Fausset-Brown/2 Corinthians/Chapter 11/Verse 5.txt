<p><b>5. For</b>—My claim is superior to
	that of the false teachers, "For," c. 
	</p>
<p><b>I suppose</b>—I reckon
	[ALFORD]. 
	</p>
<p><b>I was not</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"That I have not been, and am not." 
	</p>
<p><b>the very chiefest
	apostles</b>—James, Peter, and John, the witnesses of Christ's
	transfiguration and agony in Gethsemane. Rather, "those overmuch
	apostles," those <i>surpassers of the apostles</i> in their own
	esteem. This sense is proved by the fact that the context contains no
	comparison between him and the apostles, but only between him and the
	false teachers <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:6">2 Corinthians 11:6</span> also
	alludes to these, and not to the apostles; compare also the parallel
	phrase, "false apostles" (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:6- :">2 Corinthians 11:6- :</span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:2">2 Corinthians 11:2</span>)
	[ALFORD].</p>
