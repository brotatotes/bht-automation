<p><b>20. For</b>—Assigning cause why
	they needed to be thus spoken to "for their edification";
	namely, his fear that at his coming he should find them "not
	such as he would," and so he should be found by them "such
	as they would not" like, namely, severe in punishing misconduct.
	</p>
<p><b>debates</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"strifes," "contentions." 
	</p>
<p><b>envyings</b>—The oldest
	manuscripts read "envying," <i>singular.</i>
</p>
<p><b>strifes</b>—"factions,"
	"intrigues," "factious schemes" [WAHL].
	<i>Ambitious self-seeking;</i> from a <i>Greek</i> root, "<i>to
	work for hire.</i>" 
	</p>
<p><b>backbitings, whisperings</b>—<i>open</i>
	"slanderings," and "whispering backbitings" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>swellings</b>—arrogant
	elation; puffing up of yourselves. <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, "great swelling words" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>).</p>
