<p><b>21. my God</b>—<i>his</i> God,
	however trying the humiliation that was in store for him. 
	</p>
<p><b>will humble me</b>—The
	indicative implies that the supposition <i>will actually be so.</i>
	The faithful pastor is "humbled" at, and "bewails"
	the falls of his people, as though they were his own. 
	</p>
<p><b>sinned already</b>—before
	my last coming [BENGEL],
	that is, before the second visit which he paid, and in which he had
	much at Corinth to rebuke. 
	</p>
<p><b>have not repented</b>—shall
	not have repented [ALFORD].
	</p>
<p><b>uncleanness</b>—for
	example, of married persons (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>fornication</b>—among the
	unmarried.</p>
