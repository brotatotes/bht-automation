<p><b>6. For</b>—Not but that I might
	glory as to "myself" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>); "FOR if I
	should desire to glory, I shall not be a fool"; for I have
	things to glory, or boast of which are good matter for glorying of
	(not mere external fleshly advantages which when he gloried in [<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>] he termed such glorying "folly," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:1">2 Corinthians 11:1</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:16">2 Corinthians 11:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:17">2 Corinthians 11:17</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>think of me</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"form his estimate respecting me." 
	</p>
<p><b>heareth of me</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"heareth aught from me." Whatever haply he heareth from me
	in person. If on account of healing a cripple (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:12">Acts 14:12</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:13">Acts 14:13</span>), and shaking off a
	viper (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:5">Acts 28:5</span>), the people
	thought him a god, what would they have not done, if he had disclosed
	those revelations? [ESTIUS].
	I wish each of you to estimate me by "what he sees" my
	<i>present</i> acts and "hears" my teaching to be; not by
	my boasting of <i>past</i> revelations. They who allow themselves to
	be thought of more highly than is lawful, defraud themselves of the
	honor which is at God's disposal [BENGEL]
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:44">John 5:44</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:43">John 12:43</span>).</p>
