<p><b>8. For</b>—"concerning this
	thing." 
	</p>
<p><b>thrice</b>—To his first and
	second prayer no answer came. To his third the answer came, which
	satisfied his faith and led him to bow his will to God's will. So
	Paul's master, Jesus, <i>thrice</i> prayed on the Mount of Olives, in
	resignation to the Father's will. The thorn seems (from <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, and <i>Greek,</i> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, "that he <i>may</i> buffet me") to have continued
	with Paul when he wrote, lest still he should be "overmuch
	lifted up." 
	</p>
<p><b>the Lord</b>—Christ. Escape
	from the cross is not to be sought even indirectly from Satan (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). "Satan is not to be asked to spare us" [BENGEL].</p>
