<p><b>5. Examine</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "Try
	(make trial of) yourselves." 
	</p>
<p><b>prove your own selves</b>—This
	should be your first aim, rather than "seeking a proof of Christ
	speaking <i>in me</i>" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>your own selves</b>—I need
	not speak much in proof of Christ being in me, your minister (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), for if ye try <i>your own selves</i> ye will see that
	Christ is also in you [CHRYSOSTOM],
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:10">Romans 8:10</span>). Finding Christ
	dwelling in yourselves by faith, ye may well believe that He speaks
	in me, by whose ministry ye have received this faith [ESTIUS].
	To doubt it would be the sin of Israel, who, after so many miracles
	and experimental proofs of God's presence, still cried (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:10- :">Romans 8:10- :</span>), "Is the Lord among us or not?" (Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:10- :">Romans 8:10- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>except ye be reprobates</b>—The
	<i>Greek</i> softens the expression, "<i>somewhat</i>
	reprobates," that is, not <i>abiding the</i> "<i>proof</i>"
	(alluding to the same word in the context); failing <i>when tested.</i>
