<p><b>17. not as many</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:18">2 Corinthians 11:18</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+2:21">Philippians 2:21</span>). Rather, "<i>the</i>
	many," namely, <i>the</i> false teachers of whom he treats
	(tenth through twelfth chapters, especially <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:13">2 Corinthians 11:13</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:3">1 Thessalonians 2:3</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>which corrupt</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"adulterating, as hucksters do wine for gain" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:2">2 Corinthians 4:2</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+1:22">Isaiah 1:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:3">2 Peter 2:3</span>,
	"Make <i>merchandise</i> of you"). 
	</p>
<p><b>as of sincerity . . . as of
	God</b>—as one speaking from (out of) sincerity, as from (that is,
	by the command of, and so in dependence on) God. 
	</p>
<p><b>in Christ's</b>—as united
	to Him in living membership, and doing His work (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:3- :">2 Peter 2:3- :</span>). The <i>whole</i> Gospel must be delivered such as it is,
	without concession to men's corruptions, and without selfish aims, if
	it is to be blessed with success (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:3- :">2 Peter 2:3- :</span>).</p>
