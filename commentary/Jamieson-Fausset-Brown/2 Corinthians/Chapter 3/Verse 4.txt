<p><b>4. And</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "But."
	"Such confidence, however (namely, of our 'sufficiency,' <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:5">2 Corinthians 3:5</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:6">2 Corinthians 3:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:16">2 Corinthians 2:16</span>
	—to which he reverts after the parenthesis—as ministers of the
	New Testament, 'not hinting,' <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:16- :">2 Corinthians 2:16- :</span>), we have through Christ (not through ourselves, compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:16- :">2 Corinthians 2:16- :</span>) toward God" (that is, in our relation to God and His
	work, the ministry committed by Him to us, for which we must render
	an account to Him). Confidence toward God is solid and real, as
	looking to Him for the strength needed now, and also for the reward
	of grace to be given hereafter. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:16- :">2 Corinthians 2:16- :</span>, "hope toward God." Human confidence is unreal in
	that it looks to man for its help and its reward.</p>
