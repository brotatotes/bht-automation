<p><b>9. ministration of condemnation</b>—the
	law regarded in the "letter" which "killeth"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:6">2 Corinthians 3:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:9-11">Romans 7:9-11</span>).
	The oldest existing manuscript seems to read as <i>English Version.</i>
	But most of the almost contemporary manuscripts, versions, and
	Fathers, read, "If to the ministration of condemnation there be
	glory." 
	</p>
<p><b>the ministration of
	righteousness</b>—the Gospel, which especially reveals the
	righteousness of God (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:17">Romans 1:17</span>),
	and imputes righteousness to men through faith in Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:21-28">Romans 3:21-28</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:3">Romans 4:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:22-25">Romans 4:22-25</span>),
	and imparts righteousness by the Spirit (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:22-45.4.25- :">Romans 4:22-45.4.25- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>exceed</b>—"abound."</p>
