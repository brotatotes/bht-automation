<p><b>1. Therefore</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"For this cause": Because we have the liberty-giving Spirit
	of the Lord, and with unveiled face behold His glory (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:17">2 Corinthians 3:17</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:18">2 Corinthians 3:18</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>seeing we have this
	ministry</b>—"The ministration of the Spirit" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:8">2 Corinthians 3:8</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:9">2 Corinthians 3:9</span>): the ministry of such a
	spiritual, liberty-giving Gospel: resuming <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:6">2 Corinthians 3:6</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:8">2 Corinthians 3:8</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>received mercy</b>—from
	God, in having had <i>this ministry</i> conferred on us (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:8- :">2 Corinthians 3:8- :</span>). The sense of "mercy" received from God, makes men
	active for God (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:8- :">2 Corinthians 3:8- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>we faint not</b>—in
	boldness of speech and action, and patience in suffering (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:2">2 Corinthians 4:2</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:8-16">2 Corinthians 4:8-16</span>, &amp;c.).</p>
