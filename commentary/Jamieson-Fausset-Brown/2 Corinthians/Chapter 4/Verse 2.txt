<p><b>2. renounced</b>—literally, "bid
	farewell to." 
	</p>
<p><b>of dishonesty</b>—rather,
	"of shame." "I am not <i>ashamed</i> of the Gospel of
	Christ" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:16">Romans 1:16</span>). Shame
	would lead to <i>hiding</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:16- :">Romans 1:16- :</span>); whereas "we use great plainness of speech" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:16- :">Romans 1:16- :</span>); "by <i>manifestation</i> of the truth." Compare
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:3">2 Corinthians 3:3</span>, "<i>manifestly
	declared.</i>" He refers to the disingenuous artifices of "many"
	teachers at Corinth (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:17">2 Corinthians 2:17</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:1">2 Corinthians 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:13-15">2 Corinthians 11:13-15</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>handling . . . deceitfully</b>—so
	"corrupt" or <i>adulterate</i> "the word of God"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:17">2 Corinthians 2:17</span>; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:3">1 Thessalonians 2:3</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:4">1 Thessalonians 2:4</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>commending</b>—recommending
	ourselves: recurring to <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:1">2 Corinthians 3:1</span>.
	</p>
<p><b>to</b>—to the verdict of. 
	</p>
<p><b>every man's conscience</b>—
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:11">2 Corinthians 5:11</span>). Not to men's carnal
	judgment, as those alluded to (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:11- :">2 Corinthians 5:11- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>in the sight of God</b>—
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:17">2 Corinthians 2:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:10">Galatians 1:10</span>).</p>
