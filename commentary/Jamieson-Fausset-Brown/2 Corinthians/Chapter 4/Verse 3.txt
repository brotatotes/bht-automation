<p><b>3. But if</b>—Yea, even if (as I
	grant is the case). 
	</p>
<p><b>hid</b>—rather (in
	reference to <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:13-18">2 Corinthians 3:13-18</span>),
	"veiled." "Hid" (<i>Greek,</i> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:13-47.3.18- :">2 Corinthians 3:13-47.3.18- :</span>) is said of that withdrawn from view altogether. "Veiled,"
	of a thing within reach of the eye, but <i>covered</i> over so as not
	to be seen. So it was in the case of Moses' face. 
	</p>
<p><b>to them</b>—in the case
	only of them: for <i>in itself</i> the Gospel is quite plain. 
	</p>
<p><b>that are lost</b>—rather,
	"that are perishing" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:13-47.3.18- :">2 Corinthians 3:13-47.3.18- :</span>). So the same cloud that was "light" to the people
	of God, was "darkness" to the Egyptian foes of God (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:13-47.3.18- :">2 Corinthians 3:13-47.3.18- :</span>).</p>
