<p><b>8.</b> <i>Greek,</i> "BEING
	hard pressed, yet not inextricably straitened; reduced to
	inextricable straits" (nominative to "we have," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>on every side</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"in every respect" (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, "always"; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). This verse expresses <i>inward</i> distresses; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, <i>outward</i> distresses (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). "<i>Without</i> were fightings; <i>within</i> were
	fears." The first clause in each member of the series of
	contrasted participles, implies the <i>earthiness</i> of the <i>vessels;</i>
	the second clause, <i>the excellency of the power.</i>
</p>
<p><b>perplexed, but not in
	despair</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "<i>not utterly</i> perplexed."
	As <i>perplexity</i> refers to the future, so "troubled" or
	"hard pressed" refers to the present.</p>
