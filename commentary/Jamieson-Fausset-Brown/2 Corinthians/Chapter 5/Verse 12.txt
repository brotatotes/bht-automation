<p><b>12. For</b>—the reason why he
	leaves the manifestation of his sincerity in preaching to their
	consciences (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:1">2 Corinthians 3:1</span>), namely,
	his not wishing to "commend" himself again. 
	</p>
<p><b>occasion to glory</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:1- :">2 Corinthians 3:1- :</span>), namely, as to our sincerity. 
	</p>
<p><b>in appearance</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"face" (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1sa+16:7">1 Samuel 16:7</span>).
	The false teachers gloried in their <i>outward appearance,</i> and in
	external recommendations (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1sa+16:7- :">1 Samuel 16:7- :</span>) their learning, eloquence, wisdom, riches, not in vital
	religion in their <i>heart.</i> Their conscience does not attest
	their inward sincerity, as mine does (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1sa+16:7- :">1 Samuel 16:7- :</span>).</p>
