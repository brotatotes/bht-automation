<p><b>4. For</b>—resuming <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>being burdened: not for
	that</b>—rather, "<i>in that</i> we desire <i>not</i> to have
	ourselves unclothed (of our present body), but clothed upon (with our
	heavenly body). 
	</p>
<p><b>that mortality,</b>
	c.—rather, "that what is mortal (our mortal part) may be
	swallowed up of (absorbed and transformed into) life." Believers
	shrink from, not the <i>consequences,</i> but the mere <i>act</i> of
	dying especially as believing in the possibility of their being found
	alive at the Lord's coming (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), and so of having their mortal body absorbed into the
	immortal without death. Faith does not divest us of all natural
	feeling, but subordinates it to higher feeling. Scripture gives no
	sanction to the contempt for the body expressed by philosophers.</p>
