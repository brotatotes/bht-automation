<p><b>1. workers together</b>—with God
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:4">Acts 15:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:9">1 Corinthians 3:9</span>).
	Not only as "ambassadors." 
	</p>
<p><b>beseech</b>—entreat (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:9- :">1 Corinthians 3:9- :</span>). He is describing his ministry, not exhorting directly. 
	</p>
<p><b>you also</b>—rather, "WE
	ALSO (as well as God, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:9- :">1 Corinthians 3:9- :</span>) beseech" or "entreat you": <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:14">2 Corinthians 6:14</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:15">2 Corinthians 6:15</span>, on to <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:15- :">2 Corinthians 6:15- :</span>, is part of this entreaty or exhortation. 
	</p>
<p><b>in vain</b>—by making the
	grace of God a ground for continuance in sin (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:15- :">2 Corinthians 6:15- :</span>). By a life of sin, showing that the word of reconciliation
	has been <i>in vain,</i> so far as you are concerned (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:15">Hebrews 12:15</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jdg+1:4">Judges 1:4</span>). "The grace of God"
	here, is "the reconciliation" provided by God's love
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:18">2 Corinthians 5:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:19">2 Corinthians 5:19</span>;
	compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:2">Galatians 2:2</span>).</p>
