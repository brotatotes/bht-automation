<p><b>2. For</b>—God's own promise is
	the ground of our exhortation. 
	</p>
<p><b>he saith</b>—<i>God the
	Father</i> saith to God the Son, and so to all believers who are
	regarded as one with Him. 
	</p>
<p><b>heard thee</b>—In the
	eternal purposes of my love I have hearkened to thy prayer for the
	salvation of thy people (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:9">John 17:9</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:15">John 17:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:20">John 17:20</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:24">John 17:24</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>accepted . . . accepted</b>—The
	<i>Greek</i> of the latter is more emphatic, "well-accepted."
	What was "<i>an accepted</i> time" in the prophecy (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:24- :">John 17:24- :</span>, <i>Hebrew,</i> "in the season of grace") becomes
	"<i>the well-accepted</i> time" in the fulfilment (compare
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+69:13">Psalms 69:13</span>). As it is <i>God's</i>
	time of <i>receiving</i> sinners, <i>receive ye</i> His grace: <i>accept</i>
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:1">2 Corinthians 6:1</span>) the word of
	reconciliation in His <i>accepted</i> time. 
	</p>
<p><b>in the day of salvation</b>—"in
	<i>a</i> day of salvation" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:18">Luke 4:18</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:19">Luke 4:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:21">Luke 4:21</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:42">Luke 19:42</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:7">Hebrews 3:7</span>).</p>
