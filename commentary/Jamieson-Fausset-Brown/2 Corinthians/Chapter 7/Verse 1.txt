<p><b>1. cleanse ourselves</b>—This is
	the conclusion of the exhortation (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:1">2 Corinthians 6:1</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:14">2 Corinthians 6:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:3">1 John 3:3</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+22:11">Revelation 22:11</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>filthiness</b>—"the
	unclean thing" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:17">2 Corinthians 6:17</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>of the flesh</b>—for
	instance, <i>fornication,</i> prevalent at Corinth (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:17- :">2 Corinthians 6:17- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>and spirit</b>—for
	instance, <i>idolatry,</i> direct or indirect (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:9">1 Corinthians 6:9</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+8:1">1 Corinthians 8:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+8:7">1 Corinthians 8:7</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:7">1 Corinthians 10:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:21">1 Corinthians 10:21</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:22">1 Corinthians 10:22</span>). The spirit (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:22- :">1 Corinthians 10:22- :</span>) receives pollution through the flesh, the instrument of
	uncleanness. 
	</p>
<p><b>perfecting holiness</b>—The
	<i>cleansing away</i> impurity is a positive step towards holiness
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:17">2 Corinthians 6:17</span>). It is not enough to
	begin; the end crowns the work (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:3">Galatians 3:3</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:7">Galatians 5:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+1:6">Philippians 1:6</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>fear of God</b>—often
	conjoined with the consideration of the most glorious promises
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:11">2 Corinthians 5:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:1">Hebrews 4:1</span>).
	Privilege and promise go hand in hand.</p>
