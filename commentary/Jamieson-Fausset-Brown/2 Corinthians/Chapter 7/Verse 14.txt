<p><b>14. anything</b>—that is, at all. 
	</p>
<p><b>I am not ashamed</b>—"I
	am not put to shame," namely, by learning from Titus that you
	did not realize the high character I gave him of you. 
	</p>
<p><b>as . . . all things . . . in
	truth, even so our boasting . . . is found a truth</b>—As our
	speaking <i>in general</i> to you was true (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), so our particular boasting to Titus <i>concerning you</i>
	is now, by his report, proved to be truth (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). Some oldest manuscripts read expressly, "concerning
	you"; this in either reading is the <i>sense.</i></p>
