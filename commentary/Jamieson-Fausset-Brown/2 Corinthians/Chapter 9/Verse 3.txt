<p><b>3. have I sent</b>—we should say,
	"I send"; whereas the ancients put it in the past, the time
	which it would be by the time that the letter arrived. 
	</p>
<p><b>the brethren</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:18">2 Corinthians 8:18</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:22">2 Corinthians 8:22</span>) —Titus and the two
	others. 
	</p>
<p><b>should be in vain in this
	behalf</b>—"should be proved futile <i>in this particular,</i>"
	however true in general (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:4">2 Corinthians 7:4</span>).
	A tacit compliment, softening the sharp monition. 
	</p>
<p><b>as I said</b>—as I was
	saying (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:2">2 Corinthians 9:2</span>).</p>
