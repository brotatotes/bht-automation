<p style=" ">The
	<i>loss</i>
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>)
	meant is here explained: the <i>not
	having God,</i>
	which results from <i>abiding
	not in the doctrine of Christ.</i></p>
<p style=" "><b>transgresseth</b>
	— The oldest manuscripts and versions read, “Every one who <i>takes
	the lead</i>”;
	literally, “goes,” or “leads on before”; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>,
	“He goeth before them” (not the same <i>Greek</i>).
	Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>,
	“Loveth to have the <i>pre-eminence.</i>”</p>
<p style=" "><b>hath
	not God</b>
	— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>).
	The second “of Christ” is omitted in the oldest manuscripts, but
	is understood in the sense.</p>
<p style=" "><b>He</b>
	— emphatical: <i>He
	and He alone.</i></p>
