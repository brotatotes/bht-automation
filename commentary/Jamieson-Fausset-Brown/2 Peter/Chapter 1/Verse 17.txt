<p><b>17. received . . . honour</b>—in
	the <i>voice</i> that spake to Him. 
	</p>
<p><b>glory</b>—in the <i>light</i>
	which shone around Him. 
	</p>
<p><b>came</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "was
	borne": the same phrase occurs only in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>; one of several instances showing that the argument against
	the authenticity of this Second Epistle, from its dissimilarity of
	style as compared with First Peter, is not well founded. 
	</p>
<p><b>such a voice</b>—as he
	proceeds to describe. 
	</p>
<p><b>from the excellent
	glory</b>—rather as <i>Greek,</i> "by (that is, uttered by)
	the magnificent glory (that is, by <i>God:</i> as His glorious
	manifested presence is often called by the Hebrews "the Glory,"
	compare "His Excellency," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="de+33:26">Deuteronomy 33:26</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+21:5">Psalms 21:5</span>)." 
	</p>
<p><b>in whom</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"<i>in regard to</i> whom" (accusative case); but <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+21:5- :">Psalms 21:5- :</span>, "in whom" (dative case) centers and rests My good
	pleasure. Peter also omits, as not required by his purpose, "hear
	Him," showing his independence in his inspired testimony. 
	</p>
<p><b>I am</b>—<i>Greek</i>
	aorist, past time, "My good pleasure <i>rested</i> from
	eternity."</p>
