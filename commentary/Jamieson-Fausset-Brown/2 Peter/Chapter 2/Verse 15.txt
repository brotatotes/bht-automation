<p><b>15. have</b>—Some of the seducers
	are spoken of as <i>already come,</i> others as <i>yet to come.</i>
</p>
<p><b>following</b>—out: so the
	<i>Greek.</i>
</p>
<p><b>the way</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="nu+22:23">Numbers 22:23</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="nu+22:32">Numbers 22:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+56:11">Isaiah 56:11</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>son of Bosor</b>—the same
	as <i>Beor</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="nu+22:5">Numbers 22:5</span>). This
	word was adopted, perhaps, because the kindred word <i>Basar</i>
	means <i>flesh;</i> and Balaam is justly termed <i>son of carnality,</i>
	as covetous, and the enticer of Israel to lust. 
	</p>
<p><b>loved the wages of
	unrighteousness</b>—and therefore wished (in order to gain them
	from Balak) to curse Israel whom God had blessed, and at last gave
	the hellish counsel that the only way to bring God's curse on Israel
	was to entice them to <i>fleshly lust</i> and <i>idolatry,</i> which
	often go together.</p>
