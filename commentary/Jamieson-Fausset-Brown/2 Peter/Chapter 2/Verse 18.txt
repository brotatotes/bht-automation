<p><b>18. allure</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "lay
	baits for." 
	</p>
<p><b>through</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"in"; the <i>lusts of the flesh</i> being the element IN
	which they lay their baits. 
	</p>
<p><b>much wantonness</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"by licentiousness"; the bait which they lay. 
	</p>
<p><b>clean escaped</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"really escaped." But the oldest manuscripts and <i>Vulgate</i>
	read, "scarcely," or "for but a little time";
	scarcely have they escaped from them who live in error (the ungodly
	world), when they are allured by these seducers into sin again (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>).</p>
