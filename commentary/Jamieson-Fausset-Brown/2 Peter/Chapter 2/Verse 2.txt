<p><b>2. follow</b>—out: so the <i>Greek.</i>
</p>
<p><b>pernicious ways</b>—The
	oldest manuscripts and <i>Vulgate</i> read, "licentiousness"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jdg+1:4">Judges 1:4</span>). False doctrine and
	immoral practice generally go together (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:18">2 Peter 2:18</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:19">2 Peter 2:19</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>by reason of whom</b>—"on
	account of whom," namely, the followers of the false teachers. 
	</p>
<p><b>the way of truth shall be
	evil spoken of</b>—"blasphemed" by those without, who
	shall lay on Christianity itself the blame of its professors' evil
	practice. Contrast <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:12">1 Peter 2:12</span>.</p>
