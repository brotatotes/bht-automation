<p><b>1. now</b>—"This now a second
	Epistle I write." Therefore he had lately written the former
	Epistle. The seven Catholic Epistles were written by James, John, and
	Jude, shortly before their deaths; previously, while having the
	prospect of being still for some time alive, they felt it less
	necessary to write [BENGEL].
	</p>
<p><b>unto you</b>—The Second
	Epistle, though more general in its address, yet <i>included</i>
	especially the same persons as the First Epistle was particularly
	addressed to. 
	</p>
<p><b>pure</b>—literally, "pure
	when examined by sunlight"; "sincere." <i>Adulterated
	with no error.</i> Opposite to "having the understanding
	<i>darkened.</i>" ALFORD
	explains, The mind, will, and affection, in relation to the outer
	world, being turned to God [the <i>Sun</i> of the soul], and not
	obscured by fleshly and selfish regards. 
	</p>
<p><b>by way of</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"in," "<i>in putting you in remembrance</i>"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:12">2 Peter 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:13">2 Peter 1:13</span>).
	Ye already <i>know</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:3">2 Peter 3:3</span>);
	it is only needed that I <i>remind</i> you (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:3- :">2 Peter 3:3- :</span>).</p>
