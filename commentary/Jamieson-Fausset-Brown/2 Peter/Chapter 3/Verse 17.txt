<p><b>17. Ye</b>—warned by the case of
	those "unlearned and unstable" persons (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>knowing . . . before</b>—the
	event. 
	</p>
<p><b>led away with</b>—the very
	term, as Peter remembers, used by Paul of Barnabas' being "carried,"
	<i>Greek,</i> "led away with" Peter and the other Jews in
	their hypocrisy. 
	</p>
<p><b>wicked</b>—"lawless,"
	as in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:7">2 Peter 2:7</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>fall from</b>—(<i>grace,</i>
<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:4">Galatians 5:4</span>: the true source of)
	"steadfastness" or <i>stability</i> in contrast with the
	"unstable" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:16">2 Peter 3:16</span>):
	"established" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:12">2 Peter 1:12</span>):
	all kindred <i>Greek</i> terms. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jdg+1:20">Judges 1:20</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jdg+1:21">Judges 1:21</span>.</p>
