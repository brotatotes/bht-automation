<p><b>9. slack</b>—slow, tardy, <i>late;</i>
	exceeding the due time, as though that time were already come. <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, "will not <i>tarry.</i>" 
	</p>
<p><b>his promise</b>—which the
	scoffers cavil at. <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:4">2 Peter 3:4</span>,
	"Where is the promise?" It shall be surely fulfilled
	"according to His promise" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:4- :">2 Peter 3:4- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>some</b>—the "scoffers."
	</p>
<p><b>count</b>—His promise to be
	the result of "slackness" (tardiness). 
	</p>
<p><b>long-suffering</b>—waiting
	until the full number of those appointed to "salvation"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:15">2 Peter 3:15</span>) shall be completed. 
	</p>
<p><b>to us-ward</b>—The oldest
	manuscripts, <i>Vulgate, Syriac,</i> c., read, "towards YOU."
	</p>
<p><b>any</b>—not desiring that
	any, yea, even that the scoffers, should perish, which would be the
	result if He did not give space for repentance. 
	</p>
<p><b>come</b>—<i>go and be
	received</i> to repentance: the <i>Greek</i> implies there is <i>room</i>
	for their being <i>received</i> to repentance (compare <i>Greek,</i>
<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:2">Mark 2:2</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:37">John 8:37</span>).</p>
