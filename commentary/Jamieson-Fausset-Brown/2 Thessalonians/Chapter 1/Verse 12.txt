<p><b>12. the name of our Lord Jesus</b>—Our
	Lord Jesus <i>in His manifested personality</i> as the God-man. 
	</p>
<p><b>in you, and ye in
	him</b>—reciprocal glorification; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, "<i>The Lord of hosts</i> shall be . . . a crown of
	glory and . . . a diadem of beauty unto . . . His people," with
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+62:3">Isaiah 62:3</span>, "<i>Thou</i>
	(Zion) shalt be a crown of glory in the hand of the Lord, and a royal
	diadem," c. (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:10">John 21:10</span>
<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:24">Galatians 1:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:14">1 Peter 4:14</span>).
	The believer's graces redound to Christ's glory, and His glory, as
	their Head, reflects glory on them as the members. 
	</p>
<p><b>the grace of our God and the
	Lord Jesus Christ</b>—There is but one <i>Greek</i> article to
	both, implying the inseparable unity of God and the Lord Jesus.</p>
