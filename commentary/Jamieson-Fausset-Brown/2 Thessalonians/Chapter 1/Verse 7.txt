<p><b>7. rest</b>—governed by "to
	recompense" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:6">2 Thessalonians 1:6</span>). The
	<i>Greek</i> is literally, "relaxation"; loosening of the
	<i>tension</i> which had preceded; <i>relaxing</i> of the strings of
	endurance now so tightly drawn. The <i>Greek</i> word for "rest,"
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:28">Matthew 11:28</span>, is distinct, namely,
	<i>cessation</i> from labor. Also, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:28- :">Matthew 11:28- :</span>, "A keeping of sabbath." 
	</p>
<p><b>with us</b>—namely, Paul,
	Silas, and Timothy, the writers, who are troubled like yourselves. 
	</p>
<p><b>when</b>—at the time when .
	. . ; not sooner, not later. 
	</p>
<p><b>with his mighty angels</b>—rather
	as the <i>Greek,</i> "with the angels of His might," or
	"power," that is, the angels who are the ministers by whom
	He makes His might to be recognized (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:41">Matthew 13:41</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:52">Matthew 13:52</span>). It is not <i>their</i>
	might, but His might, which is the prominent thought.</p>
