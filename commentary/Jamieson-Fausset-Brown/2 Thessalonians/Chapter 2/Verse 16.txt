<p><b>16, 17. himself</b>—by His own
	might, as contrasted with our feebleness; ensuring the efficacy of
	our prayer. Here <i>our Lord Jesus</i> stands first; in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, "God our Father." 
	</p>
<p><b>which . . . loved us</b>—in
	the work of our redemption. Referring both to <i>our Lord Jesus</i>
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:37">Romans 8:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:20">Galatians 2:20</span>)
	and God <i>our Father</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:20- :">Galatians 2:20- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>everlasting consolation</b>—not
	transitory, as worldly consolations in trials (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:38">Romans 8:38</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:39">Romans 8:39</span>). This for all time
	present, and then "good hope" for the future [ALFORD].
	</p>
<p><b>through grace</b>—rather as
	<i>Greek</i> "IN grace"; to be joined to "hath given."
	Grace is the element in which the gift was made.</p>
