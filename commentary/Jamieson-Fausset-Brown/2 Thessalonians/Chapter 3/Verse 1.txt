<p><b>1. Finally</b>—literally, "As
	to what remains." 
	</p>
<p><b>may have free
	course</b>—literally, "may run"; spread rapidly without a
	drag on the wheels of its course. That the new-creating word may
	"run," as "swiftly" as the creative word at the
	first (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+147:15">Psalms 147:15</span>). The
	opposite is the word of God being "bound" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+147:15- :">Psalms 147:15- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>glorified</b>—by sinners
	accepting it (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:48">Acts 13:48</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:23">Galatians 1:23</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:24">Galatians 1:24</span>). Contrast "evil
	spoken of" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:14">1 Peter 4:14</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>as it is with you</b>—
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:6">1 Thessalonians 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:10">1 Thessalonians 4:10</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:11">1 Thessalonians 5:11</span>).</p>
