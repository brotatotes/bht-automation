<p><b>18. grant unto him</b>—as well as
	"unto his house" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>the Lord</b>—who rewards a
	kindness done to His disciples as if done to Himself (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>of</b>—<i>from</i> the
	Lord; "the Lord" is emphatically put instead of "from
	Himself," for solemnity and emphasis (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>in how many things</b>—"how
	many acts of ministry he rendered." 
	</p>
<p><b>unto me</b>—omitted in the
	oldest manuscripts, so that the "ministered" may include
	services rendered <i>to others</i> as well as to Paul. 
	</p>
<p><b>very well</b>—rather as
	<i>Greek,</i> "Thou knowest better" (than I can tell thee,
	seeing that thou art more of a regular resident at Ephesus).</p>
