<p><b>12. suffer</b>—rather, as the
	<i>Greek</i> is the same as in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, "If we endure (with Him)" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>reign with him</b>—The
	peculiar privilege of the elect Church now suffering with Christ,
	then to reign with Him (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>).
	Reigning is something more than mere <i>salvation</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:17">Romans 5:17</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+3:21">Revelation 3:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+5:10">Revelation 5:10</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+20:4">Revelation 20:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+20:5">Revelation 20:5</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>deny</b>—<i>with the mouth.</i>
	As "believe" <i>with the heart follows,</i> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+20:5- :">Revelation 20:5- :</span>. Compare the opposite, "confess with thy mouth"
	and "believe in thine heart" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:9">Romans 10:9</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:10">Romans 10:10</span>). 
	</p>
