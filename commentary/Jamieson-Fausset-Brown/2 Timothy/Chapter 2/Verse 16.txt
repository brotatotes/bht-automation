<p><b>16. shun</b>—literally, "stand
	above," separate from, and superior to. 
	</p>
<p><b>vain</b>—opposed to "the
	truth" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:15">2 Timothy 2:15</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>babblings</b>—with loud
	voice: opposed to the temperate "word" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:15- :">2 Timothy 2:15- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>increase</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	advance"; literally, "strike forward": an image from
	pioneers <i>cutting</i> away all obstacles <i>before</i> an advancing
	army. They pretend <i>progress;</i> the only kind of <i>progress</i>
	they make is to a greater pitch of impiety. 
	</p>
<p><b>more ungodliness</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"a greater degree of impiety."</p>
