<p><b>18. erred</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"missed the aim" (see <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>is past already</b>—has
	already taken place. The beginnings of the subsequent Gnostic heresy
	already existed. They "wrested" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>) Paul's own words (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:4">Romans 6:4</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:6">Ephesians 2:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+2:12">Colossians 2:12</span>)
	"to their own destruction," as though the resurrection was
	merely the spiritual raising of souls from the death of sin. Compare
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:12">1 Corinthians 15:12</span>, where he shows all
	our hopes of future glory rest on the literal reality of the
	resurrection. To believe it past (as the Seleucians or Hermians did,
	according to AUGUSTINE
	[<i>Epistles,</i> 119.55, <i>To Januarius,</i> 4]), is to deny it in
	its true sense. 
	</p>
<p><b>overthrow</b>—trying to
	subvert "the foundation" on which alone faith can rest
	secure (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:19">2 Timothy 2:19</span>; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:19- :">2 Timothy 2:19- :</span>).</p>
