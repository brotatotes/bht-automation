<p><b>21. If a man . . . purge himself
	from these</b>—The <i>Greek</i> expresses "If one (for
	example, thou, Timothy) purify himself (so as to separate) <i>from
	among these</i>" (<i>vessels</i> unto dishonor). 
	</p>
<p><b>sanctified</b>—set apart as
	wholly consecrated to the Lord. 
	</p>
<p><b>and meet</b>—Some oldest
	manuscripts omit "and." 
	</p>
<p><b>the master's</b>—the
	Lord's. Paul himself was such a vessel: once one among those of
	earth, but afterwards he became by grace one of gold. 
	</p>
<p><b>prepared unto every good
	work</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:17">2 Timothy 3:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:1">Titus 3:1</span>).
	Contrast <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:16">Titus 1:16</span>.</p>
