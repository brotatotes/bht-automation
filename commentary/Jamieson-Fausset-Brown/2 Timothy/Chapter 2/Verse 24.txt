<p><b>24. not strive</b>—"The
	servant of the Lord" must imitate his master in not <i>striving
	contentiously,</i> though uncompromising in earnestly contending for
	the faith (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jdg+1:3">Judges 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:19">Matthew 12:19</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>gentle unto all men</b>—"patient"
	(<i>Greek,</i> "patient in bearing wrongs") in respect to
	adversaries. He is to be <i>gentle</i> so that he may occasion no
	evils; <i>patient</i> so that he may endure evils. 
	</p>
<p><b>apt to teach</b>—implying
	not only solid teaching and ease in teaching, but patience and
	assiduity in it [BENGEL].</p>
