<p><b>covetous</b>—Translate,
	"money-loving," a distinct <i>Greek</i> word from that for
	"covetous" (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+17:11- :">Revelation 17:11- :</span>).
	The cognate <i>Greek</i> substantive (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+17:11- :">Revelation 17:11- :</span>) is so translated, "the <i>love of money</i> is a
	(<i>Greek,</i> not 'the') root of all evil." 
	</p>
<p><b>boasters</b>—empty boasters
	[ALFORD]; boasting of
	having what they have not. 
	</p>
<p><b>proud</b>—overweening:
	literally, <i>showing</i> themselves <i>above</i> their fellows. 
	</p>
<p><b>blasphemous</b>—rather,
	"evil-speakers," revilers. 
	</p>
<p><b>disobedient to parents</b>—The
	character of the times is even to be gathered especially from the
	manners of the young [BENGEL].
	</p>
<p><b>unthankful</b>—The
	obligation to <i>gratitude</i> is next to that of <i>obedience to
	parents.</i>
</p>
<p><b>unholy</b>—irreligious
	[ALFORD]; inobservant of
	the offices of piety.</p>
