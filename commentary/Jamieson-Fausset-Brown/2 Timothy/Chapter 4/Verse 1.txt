<p><b>1. charge</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"adjure." 
	</p>
<p><b>therefore</b>—omitted in
	the oldest manuscripts. 
	</p>
<p><b>the Lord Jesus Christ</b>—The
	oldest manuscripts read simply, "Christ Jesus." 
	</p>
<p><b>shall judge</b>—His
	<i>commission</i> from God is mentioned, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>; his resolution to do so, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>; the execution of his commission, here. 
	</p>
<p><b>at his appearing</b>—The
	oldest manuscripts read, "and" for "at"; then
	translate, "(I charge thee <i>before God</i> . . . ) and <i>by</i>
	His appearing." 
	</p>
<p><b>and his kingdom</b>—to be
	set at His appearing, when we hope to reign with Him. His kingdom is
	real now, but not visible. It shall then be both real <i>and visible</i>
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:18">Luke 22:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:30">Luke 22:30</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+1:7">Revelation 1:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+11:15">Revelation 11:15</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+19:6">Revelation 19:6</span>). <i>Now</i> he reigns
	<i>in the midst of His enemies</i> expecting till they shall be
	overthrown (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+110:2">Psalms 110:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:13">Hebrews 10:13</span>).
	<i>Then</i> He shall reign with His adversaries prostrate.</p>
