<p><b>18. And the Lord shall,</b> c.—Hope
	draws its conclusions from the past to the future [BENGEL].
	</p>
<p><b>will preserve me</b>—literally,
	"will save" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+22:21">Psalms 22:21</span>),
	"will bring me safe to." Jesus is the Lord and the
	Deliverer (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+3:20">Philippians 3:20</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:10">1 Thessalonians 1:10</span>):
	He saves from evil; He gives good things. 
	</p>
<p><b>heavenly kingdom</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"His kingdom which is a heavenly one." 
	</p>
<p><b>to whom,</b> &amp;c.—<i>Greek,</i>
	"to whom be <i>the</i> glory unto the ages of ages." The
	very <i>hope</i> produces a doxology: how much greater will be the
	doxology which the actual <i>enjoyment</i> shall produce! [BENGEL].</p>
