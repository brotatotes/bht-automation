<p><b>15-26. in those days</b>—of
	expectant prayer, and probably towards the close of them, when the
	nature of their future work began more clearly to dawn upon them, and
	the Holy Ghost, already "breathed" on the Eleven (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), was stirring in Peter, who was to be the leading spirit
	of the infant community (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>the number . . . about an
	hundred and twenty</b>—Many, therefore, of the "five hundred
	brethren" who saw their risen Lord "at once" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), must have remained in Galilee.</p>
