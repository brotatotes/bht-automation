<p><b>25-29. as Peter was coming in,
	Cornelius met him</b>—a mark of the highest respect. 
	</p>
<p><b>fell down at his feet, and
	worshipped him</b>—In the East this way of showing respect was
	customary not only to kings, but to others occupying a superior
	station; but among the Greeks and Romans it was reserved for the
	gods. Peter, therefore, declines it as due to no mortal [GROTIUS].
	"<i>Those who claim to have succeeded Peter, have not imitated
	this part of his conduct</i>" [ALFORD]
	(therein only verifying <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:4">2 Thessalonians 2:4</span>,
	and compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+19:10">Revelation 19:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+22:9">Revelation 22:9</span>).</p>
