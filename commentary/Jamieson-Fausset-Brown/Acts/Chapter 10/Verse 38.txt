<p><b>38. Now God anointed Jesus of
	Nazareth</b>—rather, "Jesus of Nazareth (as the burden of that
	'published word'), how God anointed Him." 
	</p>
<p><b>with the Holy Ghost and with
	power</b>—that is, at His baptism, thus visibly proclaiming Him
	MESSIAH, "the Lord's
	Christ." See <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. For it is not His unction for personal holiness at His
	incarnation that is referred to—as many of the Fathers and some
	moderns take it—but His investiture with the insignia of the
	Messianic office, in which He presented Himself after His baptism to
	the acceptance of the people. 
	</p>
<p><b>went about doing good</b>—holding
	up the <i>beneficent</i> character of all His miracles, which was
	their predicted character (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+35:5">Isaiah 35:5</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+35:6">Isaiah 35:6</span>, c.). 
	</p>
<p><b>healing all that were
	oppressed of the devil</b>—whether in the form of demoniacal
	possessions, or more indirectly, as in her "whom Satan had bound
	with a spirit of infirmity eighteen years" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+35:6- :">Isaiah 35:6- :</span>) thereby showing Himself the Redeemer from all evil. 
	</p>
<p><b>for God was with him</b>—Thus
	gently does the apostle rise to the supreme dignity of Christ with
	which he closes, accommodating himself to his hearers.</p>
