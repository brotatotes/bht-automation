<p><b>4. What is it, Lord?</b>—language
	which, tremulously though it was uttered, betokened childlike
	reverence and humility. 
	</p>
<p><b>Thy prayers and thine
	alms</b>—The way in which both are specified is emphatic. The one
	denotes the spiritual outgoing of his soul to God, the other its
	practical outgoing to men. 
	</p>
<p><b>are come up for a memorial
	before God</b>—that is, as a <i>sacrifice</i> well-pleasing unto
	God, as an odor of a sweet smell (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>).</p>
