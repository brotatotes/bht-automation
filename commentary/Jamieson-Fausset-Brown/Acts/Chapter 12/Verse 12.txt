<p><b>12-17. he came to the house of Mary,</b>
	c.—who "must have had a house of some pretensions to receive a
	large number and, accordingly, we read that her brother Barnabas (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>) was a person of substance (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). She must also have been distinguished for faith and
	courage to allow such a meeting in the face of persecution"
	[WEBSTER and WILKINSON].
	To such a house it was natural that Peter should come. 
	</p>
<p><b>mother of John . . . Mark</b>—so
	called to distinguish him from the apostle of that name, and to
	distinguish her from the other Marys. 
	</p>
<p><b>where many were gathered
	together praying</b>—doubtless for Peter's deliverance, and
	continuing, no doubt, on this the last of the days of unleavened
	bread, which was their last hope, all night in prayer to God.</p>
