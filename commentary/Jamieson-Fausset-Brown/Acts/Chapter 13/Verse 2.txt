<p><b>2. As they ministered to the
	Lord</b>—The word denotes the performance of <i>official</i> duties
	of any kind, and was used to express the priestly functions under the
	Old Testament. Here it signifies the corresponding ministrations of
	the Christian Church. 
	</p>
<p><b>and fasted</b>—As this was
	done in other cases on special occasions (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:3">Acts 13:3</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:14">Acts 13:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:23">Acts 13:23</span>),
	it is not improbable that they had been led to expect some such
	prophetic announcement at this time. 
	</p>
<p><b>the Holy Ghost said</b>—through
	some of the prophets mentioned in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:23- :">Acts 13:23- :</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>Separate me</b>—So <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:23- :">Acts 13:23- :</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>for the work whereunto I have
	called them</b>—by some communication, perhaps, to themselves: in
	the case of Saul at least, such a designation was indicated from the
	first (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:21">Acts 22:21</span>). <i>Note.</i>—While
	the <i>personality</i> of the Holy Ghost is manifest from this
	language, His supreme <i>divinity</i> will appear equally so by
	comparing it with <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:4">Hebrews 5:4</span>.</p>
