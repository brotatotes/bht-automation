<p><b>47. For so hath the Lord commanded
	us, saying,</b> c.—These and other predictions must have been long
	before this brought vividly home to Paul's mind in connection with
	his special vocation to the Gentiles. 
	</p>
<p><b>I have set thee</b>—that
	is, Messiah from which Paul inferred that he was but following out
	this destination of his Lord, in transferring to the Gentiles those
	"unsearchable riches" which were now by the Jews rejected
	and despised.</p>
