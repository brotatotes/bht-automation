<p><b>11-13. in the speech of
	Lycaonia</b>—whether a corruption of the <i>Greek</i> tongue, which
	was well enough understood in this region, or the remains of some
	older tongue, is not known. 
	</p>
<p><b>The gods are come down to us
	in the likeness of men</b>—the language of an unsophisticated
	people. But "that which was a superstition in Lycaonia, and for
	which the whole "creation" groaned, became a reality at
	Bethlehem" [WEBSTER
	and WILKINSON].</p>
