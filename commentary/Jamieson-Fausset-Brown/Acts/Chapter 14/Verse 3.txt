<p><b>3. Long time therefore abode
	they</b>—because in spite of opposition they were meeting with so
	much success. 
	</p>
<p><b>speaking boldly in the
	Lord</b>—rather, "in dependence on the Lord," that is, on
	their glorified Head. 
	</p>
<p><b>who gave testimony to the
	word of his grace</b>—a notable definition of the Gospel, whose
	whole burden is GRACE. 
	</p>
<p><b>and granted</b>—"granting,"
	that is, who confirmed the Gospel by granting miraculous attestation
	to it. (The "and" is wanting in the best manuscripts).</p>
