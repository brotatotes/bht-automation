<p><b>26. Men that have
	hazarded</b>—literally, "rendered up," as in <i>will</i>
	they did. 
	</p>
<p><b>their lives for the name of
	our Lord Jesus Christ</b>—Noble testimony to those beloved men! It
	was doubtless prompted more immediately by the narrative they had
	just listened to from their own lips (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), and judiciously inserted in this letter, to give them the
	highest weight as the bearers of it, along with their own deputies. 
	</p>
<p><b>Judas and Silas . . . shall
	tell you the same . . . by mouth</b>—Mark here how considerate and
	tender it was to send men who would be able to say of Barnabas and
	Paul what could not be expected to come from themselves.</p>
