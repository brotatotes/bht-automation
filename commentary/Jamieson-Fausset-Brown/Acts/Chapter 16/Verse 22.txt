<p><b>22. the multitude rose up together
	against them</b>—so <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:28">Acts 19:28</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:34">Acts 19:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:30">Acts 21:30</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:18">Luke 23:18</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>the magistrates rent off
	their</b>—Paul's and Silas' 
	</p>
<p><b>clothes</b>—that is,
	ordered the lictors, or rod-bearers, to tear them off, so as to
	expose their naked bodies (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:18- :">Luke 23:18- :</span>). The word expresses the roughness with which this was done
	to prisoners preparatory to whipping. 
	</p>
<p><b>and commanded to beat
	them</b>—without any trial (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:18- :">Luke 23:18- :</span>), to appease the popular rage. Thrice, it seems, Paul
	endured this indignity (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:18- :">Luke 23:18- :</span>).</p>
