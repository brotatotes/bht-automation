<p><b>6-8. Now when they had gone
	throughout Phrygia and the region of Galatia</b>—proceeding in a
	northwesterly direction. At this time must have been formed "the
	churches of Galatia" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:2">Galatians 1:2</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:1">1 Corinthians 16:1</span>); founded, as we learn
	from the Epistle to the Galatians (particularly <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:1- :">1 Corinthians 16:1- :</span>), by the apostle Paul, and which were already in existence
	when he was on his <i>third</i> missionary journey, as we learn from
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:23">Acts 18:23</span>, where it appears
	that he was no less successful in Phrygia. <i>Why</i> these
	proceedings, so interesting as we should suppose, are not here
	detailed, it is not easy to say; for the various reasons suggested
	are not very satisfactory: for example, that the historian had not
	joined the party [ALFORD];
	that he was in haste to bring the apostle to Europe [OLSHAUSEN];
	that the main stream of the Church's development was from Jerusalem
	to Rome, and the apostle's labors in Phrygia and Galatia lay quite
	out of the line of that direction [BAUMGARTEN].
	</p>
<p><b>and were forbidden of the
	Holy Ghost</b>—speaking by some prophet, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:23- :">Acts 18:23- :</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>to preach the word in
	Asia</b>—not the great Asiatic continent, nor even the rich
	peninsula now called Asia Minor, but only so much of its western
	coast as constituted the Roman province of Asia.</p>
