<p><b>27. That they should seek the
	Lord</b>—That is the high end of all these arrangements of Divine
	Power, Wisdom, and Love. 
	</p>
<p><b>if haply they might feel
	after him</b>—as men groping their way in the dark. 
	</p>
<p><b>and find him</b>—a lively
	picture of the murky atmosphere of Natural Religion. 
	</p>
<p><b>though he be not far from
	every one of us</b>—The difficulty of finding God outside the pale
	of revealed religion lies not in His distance from us, but in our
	distance from Him through the blinding effect of sin.</p>
