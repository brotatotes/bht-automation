<p><b>31. Because he hath appointed a day
	in the which he will judge the world</b>—Such language beyond doubt
	teaches that the judgment will, in its essence, be a solemn judicial
	assize held upon all mankind <i>at once.</i> "Aptly is this
	uttered on the Areopagus, the seat of judgment" [BENGEL].
	</p>
<p><b>by that man whom he hath
	ordained</b>—compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:22">John 5:22</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:23">John 5:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:27">John 5:27</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:42">Acts 10:42</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>whereof he hath given
	assurance unto all men, in that he hath raised him from the dead</b>—the
	most patent evidence to mankind at large of the judicial authority
	with which the Risen One is clothed.</p>
