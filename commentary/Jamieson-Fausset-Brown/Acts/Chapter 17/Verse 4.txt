<p><b>4. consorted</b>—cast in their
	lot. 
	</p>
<p><b>with Paul and Silas</b>—Compare
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:5">2 Corinthians 8:5</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>of the chief women</b>—female
	proselytes of distinction. From the First Epistle to the
	Thessalonians it appears that the converts were nearly all Gentiles;
	not only such as had before been proselytes, who would be gained in
	the synagogue, but such as up to that time had been idolaters
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:9">1 Thessalonians 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:10">1 Thessalonians 1:10</span>).
	During his stay, while Paul supported himself by his own labor
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:9">1 Thessalonians 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:7-9">2 Thessalonians 3:7-9</span>),
	he received supplies once and again from the Philippians, of which he
	makes honorable acknowledgment (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:15">Philippians 4:15</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:16">Philippians 4:16</span>).</p>
