<p><b>1-4. came to Corinth</b>—rebuilt
	by Julius Cæsar on the isthmus between the Ægean and Ionian Seas;
	the capital of the Roman province of Achaia, and the residence of the
	proconsul; a large and populous mercantile city, and the center of
	commerce alike for East and West; having a considerable Jewish
	population, larger, probably, at this time than usual, owing to the
	banishment of the Jews from Rome by Claudius Cæsar (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). Such a city was a noble field for the Gospel, which, once
	established there, would naturally diffuse itself far and wide.</p>
