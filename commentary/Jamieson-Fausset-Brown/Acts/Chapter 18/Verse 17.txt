	and beat him before the

	And Gallio cared for none of
	those things—nothing loath, perhaps, to see these turbulent
	Jews, for whom probably he felt contempt, themselves getting what
	they hoped to inflict on another, and indifferent to whatever was
	beyond the range of his office and case. His brother eulogizes his
	loving and lovable manners. Religious indifference, under the
	influence of an easy and amiable temper, reappears from age to age.
