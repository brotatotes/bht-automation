<p><b>1-3. while Apollos was at
	Corinth</b>—where his ministry was so powerful that a formidable
	party in the Church of that city gloried in his type of preaching in
	preference to Paul's (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:12">1 Corinthians 1:12</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:4">1 Corinthians 3:4</span>), no doubt from the
	marked infusion of Greek philosophic culture which distinguished it,
	and which the apostle studiously avoided (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:4- :">1 Corinthians 3:4- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>Paul having passed through
	the upper coasts</b>—"parts," the interior of Asia Minor,
	which, with reference to the seacoast, was elevated. 
	</p>
<p><b>came to Ephesus</b>—thus
	fulfilling his promise (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:21">Acts 18:21</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>finding certain disciples</b>—in
	the same stage of Christian knowledge as Apollos at first, newly
	arrived, probably, and having had no communication as yet with the
	church at Ephesus.</p>
