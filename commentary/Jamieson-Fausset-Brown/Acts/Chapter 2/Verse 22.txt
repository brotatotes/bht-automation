<p><b>22-28. a man approved of God</b>—rather,
	"authenticated," "proved," or "demonstrated
	to be from God." 
	</p>
<p><b>by miracles . . . which God
	did by him</b>—This is not a low view of our Lord's miracles, as
	has been alleged, nor inconsistent with <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, but is in strict accordance with His progress from
	humiliation to glory, and with His own words in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. This view of Christ is here dwelt on to exhibit to the Jews
	the whole course of Jesus of Nazareth as the ordinance and doing of
	<i>the God of Israel</i> [ALFORD].</p>
