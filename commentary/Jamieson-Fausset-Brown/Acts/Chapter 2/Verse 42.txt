<p><b>42. continued steadfastly
	in</b>—"attended constantly upon." 
	</p>
<p><b>the apostles'
	doctrine</b>—"teaching"; giving themselves up to the
	instructions which, in their raw state, would be indispensable to the
	consolidation of the immense multitude suddenly admitted to visible
	discipleship. 
	</p>
<p><b>fellowship</b>—in its
	largest sense. 
	</p>
<p><b>breaking of bread</b>—not
	certainly in the Lord's Supper alone, but rather in frugal repasts
	taken together, with which the Lord's Supper was probably conjoined
	until abuses and persecution led to the discontinuance of the common
	meal. 
	</p>
<p><b>prayers</b>—probably,
	stated seasons of it.</p>
