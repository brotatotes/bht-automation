<p><b>6. And we sailed . . . from Philippi
	after the days of unleavened bread</b>—(that is, the Passover).
	This, compared with <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:8">1 Corinthians 16:8</span>,
	shows that the three months spent at Corinth (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:8- :">1 Corinthians 16:8- :</span>) were the winter months. 
	</p>
<p><b>came . . . to Troas</b>—for
	the third and last time. (See on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:8- :">1 Corinthians 16:8- :</span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:2">Acts 20:2</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>in the five days</b>—As it
	might have been done in two days, the wind must have been adverse.
	The vivid style of one now present will be here again observed. 
	</p>
<p><b>where we abode seven
	days</b>—that is, arriving on a Monday, they stayed over the Jewish
	sabbath and the Lord's Day following; Paul occupying himself,
	doubtless, in refreshing and strengthening fellowship with the
	brethren during the interval.</p>
