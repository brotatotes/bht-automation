<p><b>1. we were gotten</b>—"torn."
	</p>
<p><b>from them</b>—expressing
	the difficulty and pain of the parting. 
	</p>
<p><b>with a straight
	course</b>—running before the wind, as <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>unto Coos</b>—Cos, an
	island due south from Miletus, which they would reach in about six
	hours, and coming close to the mainland. 
	</p>
<p><b>the day following unto
	Rhodes</b>—another island, some fifty miles to the southeast, of
	brilliant classic memory and beauty. 
	</p>
<p><b>thence unto Patara</b>—a
	town on the magnificent mainland of Lycia, almost due east from
	Rhodes. It was the seat of a celebrated oracle of Apollo.</p>
