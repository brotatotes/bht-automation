<p><b>3. when we . . .
	discovered</b>—"sighted," as the phrase is. 
	</p>
<p><b>Cyprus, we left it on the
	left hand</b>—that is, steered southeast of it, leaving it on the
	northwest. 
	</p>
<p><b>sailed into</b>—"unto"
	</p>
<p><b>Syria, and landed at Tyre</b>—the
	celebrated seat of maritime commerce for East and West. It might be
	reached from Patara in about two days. 
	</p>
<p><b>there the ship was to unlade
	her burden</b>—which gave the apostle time for what follows.</p>
