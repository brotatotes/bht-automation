<p><b>8-10. next </b><i><b>day</b></i><b>
	we that were of Paul's company departed</b>—(The words "the
	were of Paul's company" are omitted in the best manuscripts.
	They were probably added as the connecting words at the head of some
	church lessons). 
	</p>
<p><b>and came to Cæsarea</b>—a
	run along the coast, southward, of some thirty miles. 
	</p>
<p><b>Philip the evangelist</b>—a
	term answering apparently very much to our <i>missionary</i>
	[HOWSON], by whose
	ministry such joy had been diffused over Samaria and the Ethiopian
	eunuch had been baptized (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>one of the seven</b>—deacons,
	who had "purchased to himself a good degree" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). He and Paul now meet for the first time, some twenty-five
	years after that time.</p>
