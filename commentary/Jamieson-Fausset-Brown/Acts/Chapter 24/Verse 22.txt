<p><b>When Lysias . . . shall come
	. . . I will how,</b> &amp;c.—Felix might have dismissed the case
	as a tissue of unsupported charges. But if from his interest in the
	matter he really wished to have the presence of Lysias and others
	involved, a brief delay was not unworthy of him as a judge.
	Certainly, so far as recorded, neither Lysias nor any other parties
	appeared again in the case. <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:2- :">Acts 24:2- :</span>, however, seems to show that <i>at that time</i> his
	prepossessions in favor of Paul were strong.</p>
