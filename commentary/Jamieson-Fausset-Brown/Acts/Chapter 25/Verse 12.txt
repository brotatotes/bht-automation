<p><b>12. Festus</b>—little expecting
	such an appeal, but bound to respect it. 
	</p>
<p><b>having conferred with the
	council</b>—his assessors in judgment, as to the admissibility of
	the appeal. 
	</p>
<p><b>said, Hast thou</b>—for
	"thou hast." 
	</p>
<p><b>to Cæsar shalt thou go</b>—as
	if he would add perhaps "and see if thou fare better." 
	</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. HEROD
	AGRIPPA II ON
	A VISIT TO FESTUS,
	BEING CONSULTED
	BY HIM ON PAUL'S
	CASE, DESIRES
	TO HEAR THE
	APOSTLE, WHO
	IS ACCORDINGLY
	BROUGHT FORTH.</p>
