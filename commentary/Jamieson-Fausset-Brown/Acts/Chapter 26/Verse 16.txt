<p><b>16-18. But rise,</b> c.—Here the
	apostle appears to condense into one statement various sayings of his
	Lord to him in visions at different times, in order to present at one
	view the grandeur of the commission with which his Master had clothed
	him [ALFORD]. 
	</p>
<p><b>a minister . . . both of
	these things which thou hast seen</b>—putting him on a footing with
	those "eye-witnesses and ministers of the word" mentioned
	in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:2">Luke 1:2</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>and of those in which I will
	appear to thee</b>—referring to visions he was thereafter to be
	favored with such as <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:9">Acts 18:9</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:10">Acts 18:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:17-21">Acts 22:17-21</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:11">Acts 23:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:1-10">2 Corinthians 12:1-10</span>,
	&amp;c. (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:12">Galatians 1:12</span>).</p>
