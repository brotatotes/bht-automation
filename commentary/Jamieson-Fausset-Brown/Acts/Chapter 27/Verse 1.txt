<p><b>1. we should sail,</b> c.—The "we"
	here reintroduces the historian as one of the company. Not that he
	had left the apostle from the time when he last included himself (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), but the apostle was parted from him by his arrest and
	imprisonment, until now, when they met in the ship. 
	</p>
<p><b>delivered Paul and certain
	other prisoners</b>—State prisoners going to be tried at Rome of
	which several instances are on record. 
	</p>
<p><b>Julius</b>—who treats the
	apostle throughout with such marked courtesy (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:3">Acts 27:3</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:43">Acts 27:43</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:16">Acts 28:16</span>),
	that it has been thought [BENGEL]
	he was present when Paul made his defense before Agrippa (see <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:16- :">Acts 28:16- :</span>), and was impressed with his lofty bearing. 
	</p>
<p><b>a centurion of Augustus'
	band</b>—the Augustan cohort, an honorary title given to more than
	one legion of the Roman army, implying, perhaps, that they acted as a
	bodyguard to the emperor or procurator, as occasion required.</p>
