<p><b>14, 15. a tempestuous</b>—"typhonic"
	</p>
<p><b>wind</b>—that is, like a
	<i>typhon</i> or tornado, causing a whirling of the clouds, owing to
	the meeting of opposite currents of air. 
	</p>
<p><b>called Euroclydon</b>—The
	true reading appears to be <i>Euro-aquilo,</i> or east-northeast,
	which answers all the effects here ascribed to it.</p>
