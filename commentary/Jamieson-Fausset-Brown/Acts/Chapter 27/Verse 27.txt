<p><b>27-29. when the fourteenth night was
	come</b>—from the time they left Fair Havens. 
	</p>
<p><b>as we were driven</b>—drifting
	</p>
<p><b>up and down in Adria</b>—the
	<i>Adriatic,</i> that sea which lies between Greece and Italy. 
	</p>
<p><b>about midnight the shipmen
	deemed</b>—no doubt from the peculiar sound of the breakers. 
	</p>
<p><b>that they drew near some
	country</b>—"that some land was approaching them." This
	nautical language gives a graphic character to the narrative.</p>
