<p><b>33-37. while day was coming
	on</b>—"until it should be day"; that is, in the interval
	between the cutting off of the boat and the approach of day, which
	all were "anxiously looking for" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>Paul</b>—now looked up to
	by all the passengers as the man to direct them. 
	</p>
<p><b>besought </b><i><b>them</b></i><b>
	all to take meat</b>—"partake of a meal." 
	</p>
<p><b>saying, This is the
	fourteenth day ye have tarried</b>—"waited for a breathing
	time." 
	</p>
<p><b>having eaten nothing</b>—that
	is, taken no regular meal. The impossibility of cooking, the
	occupation of all hands to keep down leakage, &amp;c., sufficiently
	explain this, which is indeed a common occurrence in such cases.</p>
