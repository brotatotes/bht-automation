<p><b>8. And hardly passing it</b>—"with
	difficulty coasting along it," from the same cause as before,
	the westerly current and head winds. 
	</p>
<p><b>came to . . . the Fair
	Havens</b>—an anchorage near the center of the south coast, and a
	little east of Cape Matala, the southern most point of the island. 
	</p>
<p><b>nigh whereunto was the city
	Lasea</b>—identified by the REVEREND
	GEORGE BROWN
	[SMITH, <i>Voyages and
	Shipwreck of St. Paul,</i> Appendix 3, Second Edition, 1856]. (To
	this invaluable book commentators on this chapter, and these notes,
