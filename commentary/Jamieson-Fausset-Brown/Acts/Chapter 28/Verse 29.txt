<p><b>29. the Jews departed, and had
	great</b>—"much" 
	</p>
<p><b>reasoning among
	themselves</b>—"This verse is wanting in many manuscripts [and
	omitted by several recent editors], but certainly without reason.
	Probably the words were regarded as superfluous, as they seem to tell
	us what we were told before, that Paul "departed" (see <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). But in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:25">Acts 28:25</span> it
	is the breaking off of the discourse that is meant, here the final
	departure from the house" [OLSHAUSEN].</p>
