<p><b>7, 8. possessions of the chief
	man</b>—"the first man." 
	</p>
<p><b>of the island</b>—He would
	hardly be so styled in the lifetime of his father, if his distinction
	was that of the <i>family.</i> But it is now ascertained that this
	was the proper <i>official</i> title of the Maltese representative of
	the Roman prætor to Sicily, to whose province Malta belonged; two
	inscriptions having been discovered in the island, one in <i>Greek,</i>
	the other in <i>Latin,</i> containing the same words which Luke here
	employs. 
	</p>
<p><b>who received us</b>—of
	Paul's company, but doubtless including the "courteous"
	Julius. 
	</p>
<p><b>and lodged us three days
	courteously</b>—till proper winter lodgings could be obtained for
	them.</p>
