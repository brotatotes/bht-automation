<p><b>31-37. place was shaken</b>—glorious
	token of the commotion which the Gospel was to make (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:26">Acts 16:26</span>),
	and the overthrow of all opposing powers in which this was to issue. 
	</p>
<p><b>they were all filled with the
	Holy Ghost, and spake,</b> c.—The Spirit rested upon the entire
	community, first, in the very way they had asked, so that they "spake
	the word with boldness" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:29">Acts 4:29</span>
<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:31">Acts 4:31</span>); next, in melting down
	all selfishness, and absorbing even the feeling of individuality in
	an intense and glowing realization of Christian unity. The community
	of goods was but an outward expression of this, and natural in such
	circumstances.</p>
