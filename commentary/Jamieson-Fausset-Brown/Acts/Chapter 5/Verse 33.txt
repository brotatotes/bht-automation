<p><b>33. cut to the heart and took</b>—"were
	taking." 
	</p>
<p><b>counsel to slay them</b>—How
	different this feeling and the effect of it from that "pricking
	of the heart" which drew from the first converts on the day of
	Pentecost the cry, "Men and brethren, what shall we do?"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:37">Acts 2:37</span>). The words used in
	the two places are strikingly different.</p>
