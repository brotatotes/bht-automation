<p><b>25. For he supposed his brethren
	would have understood,</b> c.—and perhaps imagined this a suitable
	occasion for rousing and rallying them under him as their leader thus
	anticipating his work, and so running unsent. 
	</p>
<p><b>but they understood
	not</b>—Reckoning on a spirit in them congenial with his own, he
	had the mortification to find it far otherwise. This furnishes to
	Stephen another example of <i>Israel's slowness to apprehend and fall
	in with the divine purposes of love.</i></p>
