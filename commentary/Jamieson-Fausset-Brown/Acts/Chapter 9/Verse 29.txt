<p><b>29. disputed against the
	Grecians</b>—(See on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:1">Acts 6:1</span>);
	addressing himself specially to them, perhaps, as being of his own
	class, and that against which he had in the days of his ignorance
	been the fiercest. 
	</p>
<p><b>they went about to slay
	him</b>—Thus was he made to feel, throughout his whole course, what
	he himself had made others so cruelly to feel, <i>the cost of
	discipleship.</i></p>
