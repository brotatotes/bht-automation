<p><b>3. he came near Damascus</b>—so <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. Tradition points to a bridge near the city as the spot
	referred to. Events which are the turning points in one's history so
	imprint themselves upon the memory that circumstances the most
	trifling in themselves acquire by connection with them something of
	their importance, and are recalled with inexpressible interest. 
	</p>
<p><b>suddenly</b>—At what time
	of day, it is not said; for artless simplicity reigns here. But he
	himself emphatically states, in one of his narratives, that it was
	"<i>about noon</i>" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), and in the other, "<i>at midday</i>" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), when there could be no deception. 
	</p>
<p><b>there shined round about him
	a light from heaven</b>—"a great light (he himself says) above
	the brightness of the sun," then shining in its full strength.</p>
