<p><b>17.</b> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>.) Translate as <i>Greek,</i> "And <i>He Himself</i>
	(the great HE) is
	(implying <i>divine essential being</i>) before all things," in
	<i>time,</i> as well as in dignity. Since He is before all things, He
	is <i>before even time,</i> that is, <i>from eternity.</i> Compare
	"the first-born of every creature" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>by him</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"IN Him" (as the
	conditional element of existence, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>) [ALFORD]. 
	</p>
<p><b>consist</b>—"subsist."
	Not only are called into being from nothing, but <i>are maintained in
	their present state.</i> The Son of God is the <i>Conserver,</i> as
	well as the <i>Creator</i> of all things [PEARSON].
	BENGEL less probably
	explains, "All things in Him come together into one <i>system:</i>
	the universe found its completion in Him" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+41:4">Isaiah 41:4</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+22:13">Revelation 22:13</span>). Compare as to GOD,
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:36">Romans 11:36</span>: similar language;
	therefore <i>Christ</i> must be God.</p>
