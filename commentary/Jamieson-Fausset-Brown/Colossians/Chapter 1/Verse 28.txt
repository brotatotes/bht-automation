<p><b>28. preach</b>—rather as <i>Greek,</i>
	"announce" or "proclaim." 
	</p>
<p><b>warning . . .
	teaching</b>—"Warning" is connected with <i>repentance,</i>
	refers to one's <i>conduct,</i> and is addressed primarily to the
	<i>heart.</i> "Teaching" is connected with <i>faith,</i>
	refers to <i>doctrines,</i> and is addressed primarily to the
	<i>intellect.</i> These are the two heads of evangelical teaching. 
	</p>
<p><b>every . . . every man</b>—without
	distinction of Jew or Gentile, great or small (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:12">Romans 10:12</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:13">Romans 10:13</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>in all wisdom</b>—with all
	the wisdom <i>in our method of teaching</i> that we possess: so
	ALFORD. But <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+1:9">Colossians 1:9</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+3:16">Colossians 3:16</span>, favor ESTIUS'
	view, which refers it to the <i>wisdom communicated to those being
	taught:</i> keeping back nothing, but instructing all in the perfect
	knowledge of the mysteries of faith which is the true <i>wisdom</i>
	(compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:6">1 Corinthians 2:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:7">1 Corinthians 2:7</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:8">1 Corinthians 12:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:17">Ephesians 1:17</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>present</b>—(See on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:17- :">Ephesians 1:17- :</span>); at Christ's coming. 
	</p>
<p><b>every man</b>—Paul is
	zealous lest the false teachers should seduce <i>one single</i> soul
	of Christ's people at Colosse. So each individual among them should
	be zealous for himself and his neighbor. Even one soul is of
	incalculable value. 
	</p>
<p><b>perfect in Christ</b>—who
	is the <i>element in living union with</i> whom alone each believer
	can find <i>perfection: perfectly instructed</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:17- :">Ephesians 1:17- :</span>) in doctrine, and <i>full grown</i> or <i>matured</i> in
	faith and practice. "Jesus" is omitted in all the oldest
	manuscripts.</p>
