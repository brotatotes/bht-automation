<p><b>3.</b> Thanksgiving for the "faith,
	hope, and love" of the Colossians. So in the twin Epistle sent
	at the same time and by the same bearer, Tychicus (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:15">Ephesians 1:15</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:16">Ephesians 1:16</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>We</b>—I and Timothy. 
	</p>
<p><b>and the Father</b>—So some
	of the oldest manuscripts read. But others better omit the "and,"
	which probably crept in from <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:16- :">Ephesians 1:16- :</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>praying always for you</b>—with
	thanksgiving (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:6">Philippians 4:6</span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:6- :">Philippians 4:6- :</span>.</p>
