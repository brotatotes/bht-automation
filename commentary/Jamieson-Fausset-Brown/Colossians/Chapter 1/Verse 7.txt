<p><b>7. As ye also learned</b>—"Also"
	is omitted in the oldest manuscripts. The insertion implied that
	those inserting it thought that <i>Paul</i> had preached the Gospel
	to the Colossians as well as Epaphras, Whereas the omission in the
	oldest manuscripts implies that <i>Epaphras alone</i> was the founder
	of the Church at Colosse. 
	</p>
<p><b>of</b>—"<i>from</i>
	Epaphras." 
	</p>
<p><b>dear</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"beloved." 
	</p>
<p><b>fellow servant</b>—namely,
	of Christ. In <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:23">Philemon 1:23</span> he calls
	him "my fellow prisoner." It is possible that Epaphras may
	have been apprehended for his zealous labors in Asia Minor; but more
	probable that Paul gave him the title; as his faithful companion in
	his imprisonment (compare <i>Note,</i> see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:23- :">Philemon 1:23- :</span>, as to MEYER'S
	conjecture). 
	</p>
<p><b>who is for you,</b>
	c.—Translate, "who is faithful in your behalf as a minister of
	Christ" hinting that he is one not to be set aside for the new
	and erroneous teachers (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:23- :">Philemon 1:23- :</span>). Most of the oldest manuscripts read, "for (or '<i>in
	behalf of</i>') US."
	<i>Vulgate,</i> however, with one of the oldest manuscripts, supports
	<i>English Version.</i></p>
