<p><b>20. Wherefore</b>—The oldest
	manuscripts omit "Wherefore." 
	</p>
<p><b>if ye be dead</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"if ye died (so as to be freed) from," c. (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:2">Romans 6:2</span>
<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:2">Romans 7:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:3">Romans 7:3</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:19">Galatians 2:19</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>rudiments of the world</b>—
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+2:8">Colossians 2:8</span>). Carnal, outward,
	worldly, legal ordinances. 
	</p>
<p><b>as though living</b>—as
	though you were not dead to the world like your crucified Lord, into
	whose death ye were buried (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:14">Galatians 6:14</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:1">1 Peter 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:2">1 Peter 4:2</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>are ye subject to
	ordinances</b>—By do ye submit to be made subject to ordinances?
	Referring to <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+2:14">Colossians 2:14</span>: you are
	again being made subject to "ordinances," the "handwriting"
	of which had been "blotted out" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+2:14- :">Colossians 2:14- :</span>).</p>
