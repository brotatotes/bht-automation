<p><b>21.</b> Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, "meat . . . drink." He gives instances of the
	"ordinances" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+2:20">Colossians 2:20</span>)
	in the words of their imposers. There is an ascending climax of
	superstitious prohibitions. The first <i>Greek</i> word (<i>hapse</i>)
	is distinguished from the third (<i>thiges</i>), in that the former
	means <i>close contact</i> and <i>retention:</i> the latter,
	<i>momentary contact</i> (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:1">1 Corinthians 7:1</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:17">John 20:17</span>, <i>Greek,</i> "Hold
	me not"; cling not to me"). Translate, "<i>Handle</i>
	not, <i>neither</i> taste, <i>nor even touch.</i>" The three
	refer to meats. "<i>Handle</i> not" (a stronger term than
	"nor even <i>touch</i>"), "nor taste" with the
	<i>tongue,</i> "nor even touch," however slight the
	contact.</p>
