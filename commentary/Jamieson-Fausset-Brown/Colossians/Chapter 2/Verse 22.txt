<p><b>22. Which</b>—things, namely, the
	three things handled, touched, and tasted. 
	</p>
<p><b>are to perish</b>—literally,
	"are constituted (by their very nature) for perishing (or
	'<i>destruction by corruption</i>') in (or '<i>with</i>') their using
	up (consumption)." Therefore they cannot really and lastingly
	defile a man (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:17">Matthew 15:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:13">1 Corinthians 6:13</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>after</b>—according to.
	Referring to <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+2:20">Colossians 2:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+2:21">Colossians 2:21</span>.
	All these "ordinances" are according to human, not divine,
	injunction. 
	</p>
<p><b>doctrines</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	teachings." ALFORD
	translates, "(doctrinal) systems."</p>
