<p><b>17.</b> Literally, "And
	everything whatsoever ye do . . . do all," c. this includes
	<i>words</i> as well as <i>deeds.</i>
</p>
<p><b>in the name of the Lord
	Jesus</b>—<i>as disciples called by His name as His,</i> seeking
	His guidance and help, and desiring to act so as to gain His approval
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:8">Romans 14:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:31">1 Corinthians 10:31</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:15">2 Corinthians 5:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:11">1 Peter 4:11</span>).
	Compare "in the Lord," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:11- :">1 Peter 4:11- :</span>, and "Christ is <i>all,</i>" <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:11- :">1 Peter 4:11- :</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>God and the Father</b>—The
	oldest manuscripts omit "and," which seems to have crept in
	from <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:20">Ephesians 5:20</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>by him</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"<i>through</i> Him" as the channel of His grace to us, and
	of our thanksgiving to Him (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:20- :">Ephesians 5:20- :</span>, end).</p>
