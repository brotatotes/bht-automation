<p><b>23. And</b>—omitted in the oldest
	manuscripts (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:7">Ephesians 6:7</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:8">Ephesians 6:8</span>). Compare the same
	principle in the case of all men, Hezekiah (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ch+31:21">2 Chronicles 31:21</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:11">Romans 12:11</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>do, do it</b>—two distinct
	<i>Greek</i> verbs, "Whatsoever ye <i>do, work at</i> it"
	(or "<i>labor at</i>" it). 
	</p>
<p><b>heartily</b>—not from
	servile constraint, but with hearty good will.</p>
