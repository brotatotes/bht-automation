<p><b>1. give</b>—<i>Greek</i> "render":
	literally, "afford." 
	</p>
<p><b>equal</b>—that is, as the
	slaves owe their duties to you, so you <i>equally</i> owe to them
	your duties as masters. Compare "ye masters do the <i>same</i>
	things" (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>).
	ALFORD translates,
	"fairness," "equity," which gives a large and
	liberal interpretation of justice in common matters (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>knowing</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>ye also</b>—as well as
	they.</p>
