<p><b>3. for us</b>—myself and Timothy
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+1:1">Colossians 1:1</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>a door of
	utterance</b>—Translate, "a door for the word." Not as in
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:19">Ephesians 6:19</span>, where power of
	"utterance" is his petition. Here it is an opportunity for
	<i>preaching the word,</i> which would be best afforded by his
	release from prison (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:9">1 Corinthians 16:9</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:12">2 Corinthians 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:22">Philemon 1:22</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+3:8">Revelation 3:8</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>to speak</b>—so that we may
	speak. 
	</p>
<p><b>the mystery of Christ</b>—
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+1:27">Colossians 1:27</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>for which . . . also</b>—<i>on
	account of which I am</i> (not only "an ambassador," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+1:27- :">Colossians 1:27- :</span>, but) ALSO in
	<i>bonds.</i></p>
