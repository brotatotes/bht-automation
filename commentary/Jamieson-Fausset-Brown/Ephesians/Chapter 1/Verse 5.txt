<p><b>5. predestinated</b>—more special
	in respect to the <i>end</i> and precise <i>means,</i> than "chosen"
	or <i>elected.</i> We are "chosen" <i>out of the rest of
	the world;</i> "predestinated" <i>to all things that secure
	the inheritance</i> for us (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:11">Ephesians 1:11</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:29">Romans 8:29</span>). "<i>Foreordained.</i>"
	</p>
<p><b>by Jesus</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"<i>through</i> Jesus." 
	</p>
<p><b>to himself</b>—the Father
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+1:20">Colossians 1:20</span>). ALFORD
	explains, "adoption . . . <i>into</i> Himself," that is, so
	that we should be <i>partakers of the divine nature</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+1:20- :">Colossians 1:20- :</span>). LACHMANN reads,
	"unto <i>Him.</i>" The context favors the explanation of
	CALVIN: God has regard <i>to
	Himself</i> and the glory of His grace (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:6">Ephesians 1:6</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:12">Ephesians 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:14">Ephesians 1:14</span>)
	as His ultimate end. He had one only-begotten Son, and He was pleased
	<i>for His own glory,</i> to choose out of a lost world many to
	become His adopted sons. Translate, "<i>unto</i> Himself." 
	</p>
<p><b>the good pleasure of his
	will</b>—So the <i>Greek</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:26">Matthew 11:26</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:21">Luke 10:21</span>). We cannot go beyond
	"the good pleasure of His will" in searching into the
	causes of our salvation, or of any of His works (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:21- :">Luke 10:21- :</span>). (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="job+33:13">Job 33:13</span>.) Why
	needest thou philosophize about an imaginary world of optimism? Thy
	concern is to take heed that thou be not bad. There was nothing in us
	which deserved His love (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:1">Ephesians 1:1</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:9">Ephesians 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:11">Ephesians 1:11</span>)
	[BENGEL].</p>
