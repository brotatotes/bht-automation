<p><b>6.</b> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:7">Ephesians 1:7</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:17">Ephesians 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:18">Ephesians 1:18</span>).
	The end aimed at (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+50:23">Psalms 50:23</span>),
	that is, that the glory of His grace may be praised by all His
	creatures, men and angels. 
	</p>
<p><b>wherein</b>—Some of the
	oldest manuscripts read, "<i>which.</i>" Then translate,
	"which He graciously bestowed on us." But <i>English
	Version</i> is supported by good manuscripts and the oldest versions.
	</p>
<p><b>us accepted</b>—a kindred
	<i>Greek</i> word to "grace": <i>charitos, echaritosen:</i>
	translate, "graciously accepted"; "made us subjects of
	His grace"; "embraced us in the arms of His grace"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:24">Romans 3:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:15">Romans 5:15</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>in the beloved</b>—pre-eminently
	so called (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:17">Matthew 3:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:5">Matthew 17:5</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:35">John 3:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+1:13">Colossians 1:13</span>).
	<i>Greek,</i> "Son of His love." It is only "IN
	HIS BELOVED"
	that He loves us (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:3">Ephesians 1:3</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:9">1 John 4:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:10">1 John 4:10</span>).</p>
