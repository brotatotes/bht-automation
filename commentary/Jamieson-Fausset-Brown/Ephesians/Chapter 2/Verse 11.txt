<p><b>11.</b> The <i>Greek</i> order in
	the oldest manuscripts is, "That in time past (literally, <i>once</i>)
	ye," c. Such remembrance sharpens gratitude and strengthens
	faith (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:19">Ephesians 2:19</span>) [BENGEL].
	</p>
<p><b>Gentiles in the flesh</b>—that
	is, Gentiles in respect to circumcision. 
	</p>
<p><b>called Uncircumcision</b>—The
	Gentiles were called (in contempt), and <i>were,</i> the
	Uncircumcision the Jews were called, but were not truly, the
	Circumcision [ELLICOTT]. 
	</p>
<p><b>in the flesh made by hands</b>—as
	opposed to the true "circumcision of the heart in the Spirit,
	and not the letter" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:29">Romans 2:29</span>),
	"made without the hands in putting off the body of the sins of
	the flesh by the circumcision of Christ" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:29- :">Romans 2:29- :</span>).</p>
