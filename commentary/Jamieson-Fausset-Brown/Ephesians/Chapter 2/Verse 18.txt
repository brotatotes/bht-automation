<p><b>18.</b> Translate, "For it is
	through Him (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:6">John 14:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:6- :">John 14:6- :</span>) that we have <i>our</i> access (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:12">Ephesians 3:12</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:2">Romans 5:2</span>), both of us, in (that
	is, united in, that is, "by," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:2- :">Romans 5:2- :</span>, <i>Greek</i>) one Spirit to the Father," namely, as
	our common Father, reconciled to both alike; whence flows the removal
	of all separation between Jew and Gentile. The <i>oneness</i> of "the
	Spirit," through which we both have our access, is necessarily
	followed by <i>oneness</i> of the body, the Church (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:2- :">Romans 5:2- :</span>). The distinctness of persons in the Divine Trinity appears
	in this verse. It is also fatal to the theory of sacerdotal priests
	in the Gospel through whom alone the people can approach God. All
	alike, people and ministers, can draw nigh to God through Christ,
	their ever living Priest.</p>
