<p><b>17. That</b>—So that. 
	</p>
<p><b>dwell</b>—abidingly make
	His abode (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:23">John 14:23</span>). Where
	the Spirit is there Christ is (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:16">John 14:16</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:18">John 14:18</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>by faith</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"<i>through</i> faith," which opens the door of the <i>heart</i>
	to Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:20">John 3:20</span>). It is not
	enough that He be on the tongue, or flit through the brain: the heart
	is His proper seat [CALVIN].
	"You being rooted and grounded in love" (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:20- :">John 3:20- :</span>), is in the <i>Greek</i> connected with this clause, not
	with the clause, "that ye may be able to comprehend."
	"Rooted" is an image from a <i>tree;</i> "grounded"
	(<i>Greek,</i> "founder," "having your foundations
	resting on"), from a <i>building</i> (compare <i>Notes,</i>, see
	on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:20">Ephesians 2:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+1:23">Colossians 1:23</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+2:7">Colossians 2:7</span>). Contrast <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:6">Matthew 13:6</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:21">Matthew 13:21</span>. "Love," the
	first-fruit of the Spirit, flowing from Christ's love realized in the
	soul, was to be the basis on which should rest their further
	comprehension of all the vastness of Christ's love.</p>
