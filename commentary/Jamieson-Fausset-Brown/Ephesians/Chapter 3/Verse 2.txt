<p><b>2. If</b>—The <i>Greek</i> does
	not imply doubt: "Assuming (what I know to be the fact, namely)
	that ye have heard," c. "If, as I presume," The
	indicative in the <i>Greek</i> shows that no doubt is implied:
	"Seeing that doubtless," &amp;c. He by this phrase
	delicately reminds them of their having heard from himself, and
	probably from others subsequently, the fact. See <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>
	showing that these words do not disprove the address of this Epistle
	<i>to the Ephesians.</i> Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>the dispensation</b>—"The
	office of dispensing, as a steward, the grace of God which was (not
	'is') given me to you-ward," namely, to dispense to you.</p>
