<p><b>20. unto him</b>—contrasted with
	<i>ourselves</i> and <i>our needs.</i> Translate, "that is able
	above all things (what is above all things) to do exceeding
	abundantly above what we ask or (even) think": <i>thought</i>
	takes a wider range than <i>prayers.</i> The word, <i>above,</i>
	occurs thrice as often in Paul's writings, as in all the rest of the
	New Testament, showing the warm exuberance of Paul's spirit. 
	</p>
<p><b>according to the power</b>—the
	indwelling Spirit (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:26">Romans 8:26</span>). He
	appeals to their and his experience.</p>
