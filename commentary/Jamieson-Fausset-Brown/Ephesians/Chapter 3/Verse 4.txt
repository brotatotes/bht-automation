<p><b>4. understand my knowledge</b>—"perceive
	my understanding" [ALFORD],
	or "intelligence." "When ye read," implies that,
	deep as are the mysteries of this Epistle, the way for all to
	understand them is to <i>read</i> it (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:15">2 Timothy 3:15</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:16">2 Timothy 3:16</span>). By <i>perceiving his
	understanding</i> of the mysteries, they, too, will be enabled to
	understand. 
	</p>
<p><b>the mystery of Christ</b>—The
	"mystery" is Christ Himself, once hidden, but now revealed
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+1:27">Colossians 1:27</span>).</p>
