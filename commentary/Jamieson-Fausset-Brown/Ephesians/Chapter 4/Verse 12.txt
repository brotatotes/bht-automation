<p><b>12. For</b>—<i>with a view to;</i>
	the ultimate aim. "Unto." 
	</p>
<p><b>perfecting</b>—The <i>Greek</i>
	implies <i>correcting</i> in all that is deficient, <i>instructing</i>
	and completing in number and all parts. 
	</p>
<p><b>for</b>—a different <i>Greek</i>
	word; the immediate object. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, "Let every one . . . please his neighbor <i>for</i>
	his good <i>unto</i> edification." 
	</p>
<p><b>the ministry</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"ministration"; without the article. The office of the
	ministry is stated in this verse. The good aimed at in respect to the
	Church (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:13">Ephesians 4:13</span>). The way of
	growth (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:14-16">Ephesians 4:14-16</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>edifying</b>—that is,
	<i>building up</i> as the temple of the Holy Ghost.</p>
