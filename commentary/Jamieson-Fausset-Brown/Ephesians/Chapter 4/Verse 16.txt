<p><b>16.</b> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>fitly joined together</b>—"being
	fitly framed together," as in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>; all the parts being in their proper position, and in mutual
	relation. 
	</p>
<p><b>compacted</b>—implying <i>firm
	consolidation.</i>
</p>
<p><b>by that which every joint
	supplieth</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "by means of every joint of the
	supply"; joined with "maketh increase of the body,"
	not with "compacted." "By every ministering
	(supplying) joint." The joints are the points of union where the
	supply passes to the different members, furnishing the body with the
	materials of its growth. 
	</p>
<p><b>effectual working</b>—
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:19">Ephesians 1:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:7">Ephesians 3:7</span>).
	According to the effectual <i>working of grace</i> in each member (or
	else, rather, "according to <i>each several member's working</i>"),
	proportioned to the measure of its need of supply. 
	</p>
<p><b>every part</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"each one part"; each individual part. 
	</p>
<p><b>maketh increase</b>—Translate,
	as the <i>Greek</i> is the same as <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:7- :">Ephesians 3:7- :</span>, "maketh (carrieth on) the <i>growth</i> of the body."</p>
