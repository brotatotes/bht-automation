<p><b>17. therefore</b>—resuming the
	exhortation which he had begun with, "I <i>therefore</i> beseech
	you that ye <i>walk</i> worthy," c. (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>testify in the Lord</b>—in
	whom (as our element) we do all things pertaining to the ministry
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:1">1 Thessalonians 4:1</span> [ALFORD]
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:1">Romans 9:1</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>henceforth . . . not</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"no longer"; resumed from <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:1- :">Romans 9:1- :</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>other</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"the <i>rest</i> of the Gentiles." 
	</p>
<p><b>in the vanity,</b> c.—as
	their element: opposed to "in the Lord." "Vanity of
	mind" is the <i>waste</i> of the rational powers on worthless
	objects, of which idolatry is one of the more glaring instances. The
	root of it is departure from the knowledge of the true God (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:18">Ephesians 4:18</span>
