<p><b>21. If so be that</b>—not implying
	doubt; assuming what I have no reason to doubt, that 
	</p>
<p><b>heard him</b>—The "Him"
	is emphatic: "heard <i>Himself,</i>" not merely heard <i>about</i>
	Him. 
	</p>
<p><b>taught by him</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"taught IN HIM,"
	that is, being in vital union with Him (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>as the truth is in
	Jesus</b>—Translate in connection with "taught"; "And
	in Him have been taught, according as is truth in Jesus." There
	is no article in the <i>Greek.</i> "Truth" is therefore
	used in the most comprehensive sense, truth in its essence, and
	highest perfection, in Jesus; "if <i>according as</i> it is thus
	in Him, ye have been so taught in Him"; in contrast to "the
	<i>vanity</i> of mind of the Gentiles" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:14">John 1:14</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:17">John 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:37">John 18:37</span>).
	Contrast <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:44">John 8:44</span>.</p>
