<p><b>24. Therefore</b>—Translate, as
	<i>Greek,</i> "But," or "Nevertheless," that is,
	though there be the difference of headships mentioned in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, <i>nevertheless,</i> thus far they are one, namely, in the
	subjection or submission (the same <i>Greek</i> stands for "is
	<i>subject,</i>" as for "<i>submit,</i>" <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:21">Ephesians 5:21</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:22">Ephesians 5:22</span>) of the Church to
	Christ, being the prototype of that of the wife to the husband. 
	</p>
<p><b>their own</b>—not in most
	of the oldest manuscripts, and not needed by the argument. 
	</p>
<p><b>in every thing</b>—appertaining
	to a husband's legitimate authority; "in the Lord" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:22- :">Ephesians 5:22- :</span>); everything not contrary to God.</p>
