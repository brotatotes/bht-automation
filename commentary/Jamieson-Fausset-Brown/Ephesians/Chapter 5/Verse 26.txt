<p><b>26. sanctify</b>—that is,
	consecrate her to God. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, meaning, "I <i>devote</i> Myself as a <i>holy</i>
	sacrifice, that My disciples also may be devoted or consecrated as
	holy in (through) the truth" [NEANDER]
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:11">Hebrews 2:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:10">Hebrews 10:10</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:12">Hebrews 13:12</span> see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:12- :">Hebrews 13:12- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>and cleanse</b>—rather, as
	<i>Greek,</i> "cleansing," without the "and." 
	</p>
<p><b>with the washing of
	water</b>—rather as <i>Greek,</i> "with," or "by the
	<i>laver</i> of <i>the</i> water," namely, <i>the</i> baptismal
	water. So it ought to be translated in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:12- :">Hebrews 13:12- :</span>, the only other passage in the New Testament where it occurs.
	As the bride passed through a purifying bath before marriage, so the
	Church (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+21:2">Revelation 21:2</span>). He
	speaks of baptism according to its high <i>ideal</i> and <i>design,</i>
	as if the inward grace accompanied the outward rite; hence he asserts
	of outward baptism whatever is involved in a believing appropriation
	of the divine truths it symbolizes, and says that Christ, by baptism,
	has purified the Church [NEANDER]
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:21">1 Peter 3:21</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>by the word</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"IN the word."
	To be joined with "cleansing it," or "her." The
	"word of faith" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:8">Romans 10:8</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:9">Romans 10:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:17">Romans 10:17</span>),
	of which confession is made in baptism, and which carries the real
	cleansing (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:3">John 15:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:17">John 17:17</span>)
	and regenerating power (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:23">1 Peter 1:23</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:21">1 Peter 3:21</span>) [ALFORD].
	So AUGUSTINE [<i>Tract 80,</i>
	in John], "Take away the word, and what is the water save water?
	Add the word to the element, and it becomes a sacrament, being itself
	as it were the visible word." The regenerating efficacy of
	baptism is conveyed in, and by, the divine word alone.</p>
