<p><b>5. this ye know</b>—The oldest
	manuscripts read, "Of this ye are sure knowing"; or as
	ALFORD, "This ye know
	being aware." 
	</p>
<p><b>covetous . . . idolater</b>—
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+3:5">Colossians 3:5</span>). The best reading may
	be translated, That is to say, literally, <i>which is</i> (in other
	words) <i>an idolater.</i> Paul himself had forsaken all for Christ
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:10">2 Corinthians 6:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:27">2 Corinthians 11:27</span>).
	Covetousness is worship of the creature instead of the Creator, the
	highest treason against the King of kings (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1sa+15:3">1 Samuel 15:3</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:24">Matthew 6:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+3:19">Philippians 3:19</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:15">1 John 2:15</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>hath</b>—The <i>present</i>
	implies the fixedness of the exclusion, grounded on the eternal
	verities of that kingdom [ALFORD].
	</p>
<p><b>of Christ and of God</b>—rather,
	as one <i>Greek</i> article is applied to both, "of Christ and
	God," implying their perfect oneness, which is consistent only
	with the doctrine that Christ is God (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:12">2 Thessalonians 1:12</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:21">1 Timothy 5:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:13">1 Timothy 6:13</span>).</p>
