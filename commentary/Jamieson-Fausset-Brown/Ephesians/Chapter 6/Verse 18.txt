<p><b>18. always</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "in
	every season"; implying <i>opportunity</i> and <i>exigency</i>
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+4:2">Colossians 4:2</span>). Paul uses the very
	words of Jesus in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:36">Luke 21:36</span> (a
	Gospel which he quotes elsewhere, in undesigned consonance with the
	fact of Luke being his associate in travel, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:23">1 Corinthians 11:23</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:18">1 Timothy 5:18</span>). Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:1">Luke 18:1</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:12">Romans 12:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:17">1 Thessalonians 5:17</span>.
	</p>
<p><b>with all</b>—that is, every
	kind of. 
	</p>
<p><b>prayer</b>—a sacred term
	for <i>prayer</i> in general. 
	</p>
<p><b>supplication</b>—a common
	term for a special kind of prayer [HARLESS],
	<i>an imploring request.</i> "Prayer" for obtaining
	blessings, "supplication" for averting evils which we fear
	[GROTIUS]. 
	</p>
<p><b>in the Spirit</b>—to be
	joined with "praying." It is he <i>in us,</i> as the Spirit
	of adoption, who prays, and enables us to pray (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:15">Romans 8:15</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:26">Romans 8:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:6">Galatians 4:6</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jdg+1:20">Judges 1:20</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>watching</b>—not sleeping
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:14">Ephesians 5:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+88:13">Psalms 88:13</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:41">Matthew 26:41</span>). So in the temple a
	perpetual watch was maintained (compare Anna, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:41- :">Matthew 26:41- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>thereunto</b>—"watching
	unto" (with a view to) prayer and supplication. 
	</p>
<p><b>with</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"in." <i>Persevering constancy</i> ("perseverance")
	<i>and</i> (that is, exhibited in) <i>supplication</i> are to be the
	element in which our watchfulness is to be exercised. 
	</p>
<p><b>for all saints</b>—as none
	is so perfect as not to need the intercessions of his fellow
	Christians.</p>
