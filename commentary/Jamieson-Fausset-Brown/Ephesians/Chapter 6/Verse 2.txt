<p><b>2.</b> Here the authority of
	<i>revealed law</i> is added to that of natural law. 
	</p>
<p><b>which is . . . promise</b>—The
	"promise" is not made the main motive to obedience, but an
	incidental one. The main motive is, because it is God's will (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, "Honor thy father and mother, <i>as the Lord thy God
	hath</i> COMMANDED <i>thee</i>");
	and that it is so peculiarly, is shown by His accompanying it "<i>with
	a promise.</i>" 
	</p>
<p><b>first</b>—in the decalogue
	with a <i>special</i> promise. The promise in the second commandment
	is a <i>general</i> one. Their duty is more expressly prescribed to
	children than to parents; for love descends rather than ascends
	[BENGEL]. This verse
	proves the law in the Old Testament is not abolished.</p>
