<p><b>21. that ye also</b>—as I have
	been discussing things relating to you, so that ye also may know
	about me (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+4:7">Colossians 4:7</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+4:8">Colossians 4:8</span>). NEANDER
	takes it, "Ye also," as well as the Colossians (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+4:8- :">Colossians 4:8- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>my affairs</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"the things concerning me." 
	</p>
<p><b>how I do</b>—how I fare. 
	</p>
<p><b>Tychicus</b>—an Asiatic,
	and so a fit messenger bearing the respective Epistles to Ephesus and
	Colosse (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:4">Acts 20:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:12">2 Timothy 4:12</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>a beloved brother</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"<i>the</i> beloved brother"; the same epithet as in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:12- :">2 Timothy 4:12- :</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>minister</b>—that is,
	<i>servant.</i>
</p>
<p><b>in the Lord</b>—in the
	Lord's work.</p>
