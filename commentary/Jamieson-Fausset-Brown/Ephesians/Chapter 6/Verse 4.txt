<p><b>4. fathers</b>—including <i>mothers;</i>
	the fathers are specified as being the fountains of domestic
	authority. Fathers are more prone to passion in relation to their
	children than mothers, whose fault is rather over-indulgence. 
	</p>
<p><b>provoke not</b>—irritate
	not, by vexatious commands, unreasonable blame, and uncertain temper
	[ALFORD]. <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, "lest they be discouraged." 
	</p>
<p><b>nurture</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"discipline," namely, <i>training</i> by chastening in <i>act</i>
	where needed (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="job+5:17">Job 5:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:7">Hebrews 12:7</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>admonition</b>—training by
	<i>words</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="de+6:7">Deuteronomy 6:7</span>;
	"catechise," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="pr+22:6">Proverbs 22:6</span>,
	<i>Margin</i>), whether of encouragement, or remonstrance, or
	reproof, according as is required [TRENCH].
	Contrast <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1sa+3:13">1 Samuel 3:13</span>, <i>Margin.</i>
</p>
<p><b>of the Lord</b>—such as the
	Lord approves, and by His Spirit dictates.</p>
