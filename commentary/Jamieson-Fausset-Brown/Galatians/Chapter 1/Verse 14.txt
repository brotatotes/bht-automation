<p><b>14. profited</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "I
	was becoming a proficient"; "I made progress." 
	</p>
<p><b>above</b>—beyond. 
	</p>
<p><b>my equals</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"Of mine own age, among my countrymen." 
	</p>
<p><b>traditions of my
	fathers</b>—namely, those of the Pharisees, Paul being "a
	Pharisee, and son of a Pharisee" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:6">Acts 23:6</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:5">Acts 26:5</span>). "MY fathers,"
	shows that it is not to be understood generally of the traditions <i>of
	the nation.</i></p>
