<p><b>15. separated</b>—"set me
	apart": in the purposes of His electing love (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:15">Acts 9:15</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:14">Acts 22:14</span>), in order to show in
	me <i>His</i> "<i>pleasure,</i>" which is the farthest
	point that any can reach in inquiring the causes of his salvation.
	The actual "separating" or "setting apart" to the
	work marked out for him, is mentioned in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:2">Acts 13:2</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:1">Romans 1:1</span>. There is an allusion,
	perhaps, in the way of contrast, to the derivation of Pharisee from
	<i>Hebrew,</i> "<i>pharash,</i>" "separated." I
	was once a so-called Pharisee or <i>Separatist,</i> but God had
	<i>separated</i> me to something far better. 
	</p>
<p><b>from . . . womb</b>—Thus
	merit in me was out of the question, in assigning causes for His call
	from <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:11">Acts 9:11</span>. Grace is the sole
	cause (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+22:9">Psalms 22:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+71:6">Psalms 71:6</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+49:1">Isaiah 49:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+49:5">Isaiah 49:5</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jer+1:5">Jeremiah 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:15">Luke 1:15</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>called me</b>—on the way to
	Damascus (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:3-8">Acts 9:3-8</span>).</p>
