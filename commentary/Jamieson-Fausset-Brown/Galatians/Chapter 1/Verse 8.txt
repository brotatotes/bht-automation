<p><b>8. But</b>—however weighty they
	may seem "who trouble you." Translate as <i>Greek,</i>
	"Even though we," namely, I and the brethren with me,
	weighty and many as we are (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:1">Galatians 1:1</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:2">Galatians 1:2</span>). The <i>Greek</i>
	implies a case supposed which never has occurred. 
	</p>
<p><b>angel</b>—in which light ye
	at first received me (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:14">Galatians 4:14</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:1">1 Corinthians 13:1</span>), and whose authority is
	the highest possible next to that of God and Christ. A new
	revelation, even though seemingly accredited by miracles, is not to
	be received if it contradict the already existing revelation. For God
	cannot contradict Himself (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="de+13:1-3">Deuteronomy 13:1-3</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ki+13:18">1 Kings 13:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:24">Matthew 24:24</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:9">2 Thessalonians 2:9</span>). The Judaizing teachers
	sheltered themselves under the names of the great apostles, James,
	John, and Peter: "Do not bring these names up to me, for even if
	an <i>angel,</i>" &amp;c. Not that he means, the apostles really
	supported the Judaizers: but he wishes to show, when the truth is in
	question, respect of persons is inadmissible [CHRYSOSTOM].
	</p>
<p><b>preach</b>—that is, "should
	preach." 
	</p>
<p><b>any other gospel . . .
	than</b>—The <i>Greek</i> expresses not so much "any other
	gospel <i>different</i> from what we have preached," as, "any
	gospel BESIDE that which
	we preached." This distinctly opposes the traditions of the
	Church of Rome, which are at once <i>besides</i> and <i>against</i>
	(the <i>Greek</i> includes both ideas) the written Word, our only
	"attested rule."</p>
