<p><b>23. faith</b>—namely, that just
	mentioned (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:22">Galatians 3:22</span>), of which
	Christ is the object. 
	</p>
<p><b>kept</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"kept in ward": the effect of the "shutting up"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:22">Galatians 3:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:2">Galatians 4:2</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:6">Romans 7:6</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>unto</b>—"with a view
	to the faith," c. We were, in a manner, morally forced to it, so
	that there remained to us no refuge but faith. Compare the phrase, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:6- :">Romans 7:6- :</span>, <i>Margin</i> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+31:8">Psalms 31:8</span>.
	</p>
<p><b>which should afterwards,</b>
	&amp;c.—"which was afterwards to be revealed."</p>
