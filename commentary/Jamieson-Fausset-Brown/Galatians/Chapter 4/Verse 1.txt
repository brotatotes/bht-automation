<p><b>1-7.</b> The fact of God's sending
	His Son to redeem us who were under the law (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), and sending the Spirit of His Son into our hearts (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), confirms the conclusion (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>) that we are "heirs according to the promise." 
	</p>
<p><b>the heir</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). It is not, as in earthly inheritances, the death of the
	father, but our Father's sovereign will simply that makes us heirs. 
	</p>
<p><b>child</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"one under age." 
	</p>
<p><b>differeth nothing,</b>
	c.—that is, has no more freedom than a slave (so the <i>Greek</i>
	for "servant" means). He is not at his own disposal. 
	</p>
<p><b>lord of all</b>—by title
	and virtual ownership (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:21">1 Corinthians 3:21</span>
<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:22">1 Corinthians 3:22</span>).</p>
