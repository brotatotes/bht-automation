<p><b>16.</b> Translate, "Am I <i>then</i>
	become your enemy (an enemy in your eyes) <i>by telling</i> you the
	truth" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:5">Galatians 2:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:14">Galatians 2:14</span>)?
	He plainly did not incur their enmity at his <i>first visit,</i> and
	the words here imply that he had <i>since then,</i> and <i>before</i>
	his now writing, incurred it: so that the occasion of his <i>telling</i>
	them the unwelcome truth, must have been at his second visit (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:14- :">Galatians 2:14- :</span>, see my <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:14- :">Galatians 2:14- :</span>). The
	fool and sinner hate a reprover. The righteous love faithful reproof
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+141:5">Psalms 141:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="pr+9:8">Proverbs 9:8</span>).</p>
