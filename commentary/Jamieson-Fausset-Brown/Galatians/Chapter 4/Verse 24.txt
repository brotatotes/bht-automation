<p><b>24. are an allegory</b>—rather,
	"are allegorical," that is, have <i>another besides the
	literal</i> meaning. 
	</p>
<p><b>these are the two
	covenants</b>—"these [women] are (that is, <i>mean;</i> omit
	'the' with all the oldest manuscripts) two covenants." As among
	the Jews the bondage of the mother determined that of the child, the
	children of the free covenant of promise, answering to Sarah, are
	free; the children of the legal covenant of bondage are not so. 
	</p>
<p><b>one from</b>—that is,
	<i>taking his</i> origin <i>from</i> Mount Sinai. Hence, it appears,
	he is treating of the moral law (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>) chiefly (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:18">Hebrews 12:18</span>).
	Paul was familiar with the district of Sinai in Arabia (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:18- :">Hebrews 12:18- :</span>), having gone thither after his conversion. At the gloomy
	scene of the giving of the Law, he learned to appreciate, by
	contrast, the grace of the Gospel, and so to cast off all his past
	legal dependencies. 
	</p>
<p><b>which gendereth</b>—that
	is, <i>bringing forth children</i> unto bondage. Compare the phrase
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:25">Acts 3:25</span>), "<i>children of
	the covenant</i> which God made . . . saying unto Abraham." 
	</p>
<p><b>Agar</b>—that is, Hagar.</p>
