<p><b>26.</b> This verse stands instead of
	the sentence which we should expect, to correspond to <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, "One from Mount Sinai," namely, <i>the other
	covenant</i> from the heavenly mount above, which is (answers in the
	allegory to) Sarah. 
	</p>
<p><b>Jerusalem . . . above</b>—
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:22">Hebrews 12:22</span>), "the
	heavenly Jerusalem." "New Jerusalem, which cometh down out
	of heaven from my God" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+3:12">Revelation 3:12</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+21:2">Revelation 21:2</span>). Here "<i>the
	Messianic theocracy,</i> which before Christ's second appearing is
	<i>the Church,</i> and after it, Christ's kingdom of glory"
	[MEYER]. 
	</p>
<p><b>free</b>—as Sarah was;
	opposed to "she is in bondage" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+21:2- :">Revelation 21:2- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>all</b>—omitted in many of
	the oldest manuscripts, though supported by some. "Mother of
	us," namely, <i>believers</i> who are already members of the
	invisible Church, the heavenly Jerusalem, hereafter to be <i>manifested</i>
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:22">Hebrews 12:22</span>).</p>
