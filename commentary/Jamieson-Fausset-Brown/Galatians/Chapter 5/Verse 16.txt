<p><b>16. This I say then</b>—Repeating
	in other words, and explaining the sentiment in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, What I mean is this." 
	</p>
<p><b>Walk in the Spirit</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"By (the rule of) the (Holy) Spirit." Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:16-18">Galatians 5:16-18</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:22">Galatians 5:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:25">Galatians 5:25</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:1-8">Galatians 6:1-8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:22">Romans 7:22</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:11">Romans 8:11</span>. The best way to keep
	tares out of a bushel is to fill it with wheat. 
	</p>
<p><b>the flesh</b>—the natural
	man, out of which flow the evils specified (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:11- :">Romans 8:11- :</span>). The spirit and the flesh mutually exclude one another.
	It is promised, not that we should have no evil lusts, but that we
	should "not <i>fulfil</i>" them. If the spirit that is in
	us can be at ease under sin, it is not a spirit that comes from the
	Holy Spirit. The gentle dove trembles at the sight even of a hawk's
	feather.</p>
