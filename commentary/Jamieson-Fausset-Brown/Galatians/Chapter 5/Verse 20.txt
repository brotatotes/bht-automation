<p><b>20. witchcraft</b>—sorcery;
	prevalent in Asia (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:19">Acts 19:19</span>;
	compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:21">Galatians 5:21</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>hatred</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"hatreds." 
	</p>
<p><b>variance</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"strife"; singular in the oldest manuscripts. 
	</p>
<p><b>emulations</b>—in the
	oldest manuscripts, singular—"emulation," or rather,
	"jealousy"; for the sake of one's own advantage. "Envyings"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:21">Galatians 5:21</span>) are even without
	advantage to the person himself [BENGEL].
	</p>
<p><b>wrath</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	plural, "passionate outbreaks" [ALFORD].
	</p>
<p><b>strife</b>—rather as <i>Greek,</i>
	"factions," "cabals"; derived from a <i>Greek</i>
	root, meaning "a worker for hire": hence, <i>unworthy means
	for compassing ends, factious practices.</i>
</p>
<p><b>seditions</b>—"dissensions,"
	as to secular matters. 
	</p>
<p><b>heresies</b>—as to sacred
	things (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:21- :">Galatians 5:21- :</span>).
	Self-constituted <i>parties;</i> from a <i>Greek</i> root, to <i>choose.</i>
	A <i>schism</i> is a more recent split in a congregation from a
	difference of opinion. <i>Heresy</i> is a schism become inveterate
	[AUGUSTINE, <i>Con.
	Crescon. Don.,</i> 2,7].</p>
