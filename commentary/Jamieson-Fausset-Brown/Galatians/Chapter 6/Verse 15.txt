<p><b>15. availeth</b>—The oldest
	manuscripts read, "is" (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). Not only are they of no <i>avail,</i> but they <i>are
	nothing.</i> So far are they from being matter for "glorying,"
	that they are "nothing." But Christ's cross is "all in
	all," as a subject for glorying, in "the new creature"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:10">Ephesians 2:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:15">Ephesians 2:15</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:16">Ephesians 2:16</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>new creature</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:16- :">Ephesians 2:16- :</span>). A <i>transformation by the renewal of the mind</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:16- :">Ephesians 2:16- :</span>).</p>
