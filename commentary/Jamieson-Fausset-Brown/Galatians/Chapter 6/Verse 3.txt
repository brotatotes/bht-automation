<p><b>3.</b> Self-conceit, the chief
	hindrance to forbearance and sympathy towards our fellow men, must be
	laid aside. 
	</p>
<p><b>something</b>—possessed of
	some spiritual pre-eminence, so as to be exempt from the frailty of
	other men. 
	</p>
<p><b>when he is nothing</b>—The
	<i>Greek</i> is subjective: "Being, if he would come to himself,
	and look on the real fact, nothing" [ALFORD]
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:2">Galatians 6:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:6">Galatians 6:6</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:3">Romans 12:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+8:2">1 Corinthians 8:2</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>deceiveth himself</b>—literally,
	"he mentally deceives himself." Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+8:2- :">1 Corinthians 8:2- :</span>, "deceiveth his own heart."</p>
