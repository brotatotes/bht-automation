<p><b>6.</b> From the mention of bearing
	one another's burdens, he passes to one way in which those burdens
	may be borne—by ministering out of their earthly goods to their
	spiritual teachers. The "but" in the <i>Greek,</i>
	beginning of this verse, expresses this: I said, Each shall bear his
	own burden; BUT I do not
	intend that he should not think of others, and especially of the
	wants of his ministers. 
	</p>
<p><b>communicate unto him</b>—"impart
	a share unto his teacher": literally, "<i>him that teacheth
	catechetically.</i>" 
	</p>
<p><b>in all good things</b>—in
	every kind of the good things <i>of this life,</i> according as the
	case may require (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:27">Romans 15:27</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:1">Romans 15:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:11">Romans 9:11</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:14">Romans 9:14</span>).</p>
