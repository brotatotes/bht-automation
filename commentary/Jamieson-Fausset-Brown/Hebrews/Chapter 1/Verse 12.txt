<p><b>12. vesture</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "an
	enwrapping cloak." 
	</p>
<p><b>fold them up</b>—So the
	<i>Septuagint,</i> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+102:26">Psalms 102:26</span>;
	but the <i>Hebrew,</i> "<i>change</i> them." The Spirit, by
	Paul, treats the <i>Hebrew</i> of the Old Testament, with
	independence of handling, presenting the divine truth in various
	aspects; sometimes as here sanctioning the <i>Septuagint</i> (compare
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+34:4">Isaiah 34:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+6:14">Revelation 6:14</span>);
	sometimes the <i>Hebrew;</i> sometimes varying from both. 
	</p>
<p><b>changed</b>—as one lays
	aside a garment to put on another. 
	</p>
<p><b>thou art the same</b>—
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+46:4">Isaiah 46:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mal+3:6">Malachi 3:6</span>).
	The same in nature, therefore in covenant faithfulness to Thy people.
	</p>
<p><b>shall not fail</b>—<i>Hebrew,</i>
	"shall not end." Israel, in the Babylonian captivity, in
	the hundred second Psalm, casts her hopes of deliverance on Messiah,
	the unchanging covenant God of Israel.</p>
