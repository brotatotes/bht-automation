<p><b>8. O God</b>—the <i>Greek</i> has
	the article to mark emphasis (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+45:6">Psalms 45:6</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+45:7">Psalms 45:7</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>for ever . . .
	righteousness</b>—<i>Everlasting duration</i> and <i>righteousness</i>
	go together (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+45:2">Psalms 45:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+89:14">Psalms 89:14</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>a sceptre of
	righteousness</b>—literally, "a rod of rectitude," or
	"straightforwardness." The oldest manuscripts prefix "and"
	(compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="es+4:11">Esther 4:11</span>).</p>
