<p><b>14. For</b>—The sacrifice being
	"for ever" in its efficacy (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>) needs no renewal. 
	</p>
<p><b>them that are
	sanctified</b>—rather as <i>Greek,</i> "them that <i>are being</i>
	sanctified." The sanctification (consecration to God) of the
	elect (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:2">1 Peter 1:2</span>) believers is
	perfect in Christ once for all (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:2- :">1 Peter 1:2- :</span>). (Contrast the law, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:19">Hebrews 7:19</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:9">Hebrews 9:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:1">Hebrews 10:1</span>).
	The development of that sanctification is progressive.</p>
