<p><b>24.</b> Here, as elsewhere, <i>hope</i>
	and <i>love</i> follow <i>faith;</i> the Pauline triad of Christian
	graces. 
	</p>
<p><b>consider</b>—with the mind
	attentively fixed on "one another" (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), contemplating with continual consideration the characters
	and wants of our brethren, so as to render mutual help and counsel.
	Compare "consider," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+41:1">Psalms 41:1</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:15">Hebrews 12:15</span>, "(All) looking
	diligently lest <i>any</i> fail of the grace of God." 
	</p>
<p><b>to provoke</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"<i>with a view to provoking</i> unto love," instead of
	provoking to hatred, as is too often the case.</p>
