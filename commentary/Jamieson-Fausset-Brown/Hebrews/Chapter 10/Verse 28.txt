<p><b>28.</b> Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:2">Hebrews 2:2</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:3">Hebrews 2:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:25">Hebrews 12:25</span>.
	</p>
<p><b>despised</b>—"set at
	naught" [ALFORD]:
	utterly and heinously violated, not merely some minor detail, but <i>the
	whole law and covenant;</i> for example, by idolatry (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:25- :">Hebrews 12:25- :</span>). So here <i>apostasy</i> answers to such an utter
	violation of the old covenant. 
	</p>
<p><b>died</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"dies": the normal punishment of such transgression, then
	still in force. 
	</p>
<p><b>without mercy</b>—literally,
	"mercies": removal out of the pale of mitigation, or a
	respite of his doom. 
	</p>
<p><b>under</b>—on the evidence
	of.</p>
