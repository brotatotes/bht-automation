<p><b>37, 38.</b> Encouragement to patient
	endurance by consideration of the shortness of the time till Christ
	shall come, and God's rejection of him that draws back, taken from
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="hab+2:3">Habakkuk 2:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="hab+2:4">Habakkuk 2:4</span>.
	</p>
<p><b>a little while</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="hab+2:4- :">Habakkuk 2:4- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>he that shall come</b>—literally,
	"the Comer." In Habakkuk, it is <i>the vision</i> that is
	said to be about to come. <i>Christ,</i> being the grand and ultimate
	subject of all prophetical vision, is here made by Paul, under
	inspiration, the subject of the Spirit's prophecy by Habakkuk, in its
	final and exhaustive fulfilment.</p>
