<p><b>23. parents</b>—So the <i>Septuagint</i>
	has the plural, namely, Amram and Jochebed (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>); but in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ex+2:2">Exodus 2:2</span>, the
	mother alone is mentioned; but doubtless Amram sanctioned all she
	did, and secrecy. being their object, he did not appear prominent in
	what was done. 
	</p>
<p><b>a proper child</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"a comely child." <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ex+2:2- :">Exodus 2:2- :</span>, "exceeding fair," <i>Greek,</i> "fair to
	God." The "faith" of his parents in saving the child
	must have had some divine revelation to rest on (probably at the time
	of his birth), which marked their "exceeding fair" babe as
	one whom God designed to do a great work by. His <i>beauty</i> was
	probably "the sign" appointed by God to assure their faith.
	</p>
<p><b>the king's commandment</b>—to
	slay all the males (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ex+1:22">Exodus 1:22</span>).</p>
