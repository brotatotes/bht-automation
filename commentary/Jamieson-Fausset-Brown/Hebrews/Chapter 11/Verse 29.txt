<p><b>29. they</b>—Moses and Israel. 
	</p>
<p><b>Red Sea</b>—called so from
	its red seaweed, or rather from Edom (meaning "red"), whose
	country adjoined it. 
	</p>
<p><b>which . . . assaying to
	do</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "of which (Red Sea) the Egyptians having
	made experiment." <i>Rashness</i> and <i>presumption</i>
	mistaken by many for <i>faith;</i> with similar rash presumption many
	rush into eternity. The same thing when done by the believer, and
	when done by the unbeliever, is not the same thing [BENGEL].
	What was <i>faith</i> in Israel, was <i>presumption</i> in the
	Egyptians. 
	</p>
<p><b>were drowned</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"were swallowed up," or "engulfed." They sank in
	the sands as much as in the waves of the Red Sea. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, "the <i>earth</i> swallowed them."</p>
