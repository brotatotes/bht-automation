<p><b>34. Quenched the violence of fire</b>—
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="da+3:27">Daniel 3:27</span>). Not merely "quenched
	the fire," but "quenched the power (so the <i>Greek</i>) of
	the fire." <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="da+3:19-30">Daniel 3:19-30</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="da+6:12-23">Daniel 6:12-23</span> record the last
	miracles of the Old Testament. So the martyrs of the Reformation,
	though not escaping <i>the fire,</i> were delivered from its having
	<i>power</i> really or lastingly to hurt them. 
	</p>
<p><b>escaped . . . sword</b>—So
	Jephthah (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jdg+12:3">Judges 12:3</span>); and so
	David escaped Saul's sword (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1sa+18:11">1 Samuel 18:11</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1sa+19:10">1 Samuel 19:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1sa+19:12">1 Samuel 19:12</span>);
	Elijah (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ki+19:1">1 Kings 19:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ki+6:14">2 Kings 6:14</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>out of weakness . . . made
	strong</b>—Samson (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jdg+16:28">Judges 16:28</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jdg+15:19">Judges 15:19</span>). Hezekiah (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jdg+15:19- :">Judges 15:19- :</span>). MILTON
	says of the martyrs, "They shook the powers of darkness with the
	irresistible power of weakness." 
	</p>
<p><b>valiant in fight</b>—Barak
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jdg+4:14">Judges 4:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jdg+4:15">Judges 4:15</span>).
	And the Maccabees, the sons of Matthias, Judas, Jonathan, and Simon,
	who delivered the Jews from their cruel oppressor, Antiochus of
	Syria. 
	</p>
<p><b>armies</b>—literally,
	"camps" referring to <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jdg+4:15- :">Judges 4:15- :</span>. But the reference may be to the Maccabees having put to
	flight the Syrians and other foes.</p>
