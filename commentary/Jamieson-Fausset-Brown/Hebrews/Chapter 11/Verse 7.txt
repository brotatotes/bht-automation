<p><b>7. warned of God</b>—The same
	<i>Greek,</i> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:5">Hebrews 8:5</span>,
	"admonished of God." 
	</p>
<p><b>moved with fear</b>—not
	mere slavish fear, but as in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:5- :">Hebrews 8:5- :</span>; see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:7">Hebrews 5:7</span>; <i>Greek,</i>
	"reverential fear": opposed to the world's sneering
	disbelief of the revelation, and self-deceiving security. Join "by
	faith" with "prepared an ark" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:7- :">Hebrews 5:7- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>by the which</b>—faith. 
	</p>
<p><b>condemned the world</b>—For
	since he believed and was saved, so might they have believed and been
	saved, so that their condemnation by God is by his case shown to be
	just. 
	</p>
<p><b>righteousness which is by
	faith</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "according to faith." A Pauline
	thought. Noah is first called "righteous" in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:7- :">Hebrews 5:7- :</span>. Christ calls Abel so, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:7- :">Hebrews 5:7- :</span>. Compare as to Noah's righteousness, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eze+14:14">Ezekiel 14:14</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eze+14:20">Ezekiel 14:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:5">2 Peter 2:5</span>,
	"a preacher of righteousness." Paul here makes <i>faith</i>
	the principle and ground of his righteousness. 
	</p>
<p><b>heir</b>—the consequence of
	sonship which flows from faith.</p>
