<p><b>9. sojourned</b>—as a "stranger
	and pilgrim." 
	</p>
<p><b>in</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"into," that is, he went <i>into</i> it and sojourned
	there. 
	</p>
<p><b>as in a strange country</b>—a
	country <i>not belonging to him,</i> but to others (so the <i>Greek</i>),
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:5">Acts 7:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:6">Acts 7:6</span>.
	</p>
<p><b>dwelling in
	tabernacles</b>—<i>tents:</i> as <i>strangers</i> and <i>sojourners</i>
	do: moving from place to place, as having no fixed possession of
	their own. In contrast to the abiding "city" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:6- :">Acts 7:6- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>with</b>—Their kind of
	dwelling being the same is a proof that their faith was the same.
	They all alike were content to wait for their good things hereafter
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:25">Luke 16:25</span>). Jacob was fifteen
	years old at the death of Abraham. 
	</p>
<p><b>heirs with him of the same
	promise</b>—Isaac did not inherit it from Abraham, nor Jacob from
	Isaac, but they all inherited it from God directly as "fellow
	heirs." In <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:12">Hebrews 6:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:15">Hebrews 6:15</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:17">Hebrews 6:17</span>, "the promise"
	means <i>the thing promised</i> as a thing in part <i>already
	attained;</i> but in this chapter "the promise" is of
	something still <i>future.</i> However, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:17- :">Hebrews 6:17- :</span>.</p>
