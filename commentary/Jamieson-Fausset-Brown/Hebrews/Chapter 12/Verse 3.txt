<p><b>3. For</b>—justifying his
	exhortation, "Looking unto Jesus." 
	</p>
<p><b>consider</b>—by way of
	comparison with yourselves, so the <i>Greek.</i>
</p>
<p><b>contradiction</b>—unbelief,
	and every kind of opposition (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>sinners</b>—<i>Sin</i>
	assails us. Not <i>sin,</i> but <i>sinners,</i> contradicted Christ
	[BENGEL]. 
	</p>
<p><b>be wearied and faint</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"lest ye weary fainting." Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+49:4">Isaiah 49:4</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+49:5">Isaiah 49:5</span>, as a specimen of Jesus
	not being <i>wearied out</i> by the <i>contradiction</i> and strange
	unbelief of those among whom He <i>labored,</i> preaching as never
	man did, and exhibiting miracles wrought by His inherent power, as
	none else could do.</p>
