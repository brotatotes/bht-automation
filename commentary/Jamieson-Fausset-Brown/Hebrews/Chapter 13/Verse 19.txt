<p><b>19. the rather</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"<i>I the more abundantly</i> beseech you." 
	</p>
<p><b>to do this</b>—to pray for
	me. 
	</p>
<p><b>that I may be restored to
	you</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:22">Philemon 1:22</span>). It is here
	first in the letter he mentions himself, in a way so unobtrusive, as
	not to prejudice his Hebrew readers against him, which would have
	been the result had he commenced this as his other Epistles, with
	authoritatively announcing his name and apostolic commission.</p>
