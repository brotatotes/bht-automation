<p><b>5. conversation</b>—"manner
	of life." The love of filthy lust and the love of filthy lucre
	follow one another as closely akin, both alienating the heart from
	the Creator to the creature. 
	</p>
<p><b>such things as ye
	have</b>—literally, "present things" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>I will never leave thee, nor
	forsake thee</b>—A promise tantamount to this was given to Jacob
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ge+28:15">Genesis 28:15</span>), to Israel (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="de+31:6">Deuteronomy 31:6</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="de+31:8">Deuteronomy 31:8</span>), to Joshua (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="de+31:8- :">Deuteronomy 31:8- :</span>), to Solomon (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ch+28:20">1 Chronicles 28:20</span>).
	It is therefore like a divine adage. What was said to them, extends
	also to us. He will neither withdraw His <i>presence</i> ("never
	leave thee") nor His <i>help</i> ("nor forsake thee")
	[BENGEL].</p>
