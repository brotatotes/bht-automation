<p><b>7. Remember</b>—so as to imitate:
	not to <i>invoke</i> in prayer, as Rome teaches. 
	</p>
<p><b>have the rule</b>—rather,
	"who have <i>had</i> the rule over you": your spiritual
	leaders. 
	</p>
<p><b>who</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "the
	which": such persons as. 
	</p>
<p><b>have spoken unto you</b>—"spake"
	(so the <i>Greek</i> aorist means) during their lifetime. This
	Epistle was among those written later, when many of the heads of the
	Jerusalem Church had passed away. 
	</p>
<p><b>whose faith</b>—even unto
	death: probably death by martyrdom, as in the case of the instances
	of <i>faith</i> in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:35">Hebrews 11:35</span>.
	Stephen, James the brother of our Lord and bishop of Jerusalem, as
	well as James the brother of John (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:35- :">Hebrews 11:35- :</span>), in the Palestinian Church, which Paul addresses, suffered
	martyrdom. 
	</p>
<p><b>considering</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"looking up to," "diligently contemplating all over,"
	as an artist would a model. 
	</p>
<p><b>the end</b>—the
	termination, at death. The <i>Greek,</i> is used of <i>decease</i>
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:31">Luke 9:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:15">2 Peter 1:15</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>of their conversation</b>—"manner
	of life": "religious walk" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:13">Galatians 1:13</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:22">Ephesians 4:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:12">1 Timothy 4:12</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:13">James 3:13</span>). <i>Considering</i> how
	they manifested the soundness of their faith by their holy <i>walk,</i>
	which they maintained even to <i>the end of that walk</i> (their
	death by martyrdom).</p>
