<p><b>2.</b> (Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>.) Argument <i>a fortiori.</i>
</p>
<p><b>spoken by angels</b>—the
	Mosaic law spoken by the ministration of angels (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="de+33:2">Deuteronomy 33:2</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+68:17">Psalms 68:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:53">Acts 7:53</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:19">Galatians 3:19</span>). When it is said, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:19- :">Galatians 3:19- :</span>, "God spake," it is meant He spake by angels as
	His mouthpiece, or at least angels repeating in unison with His voice
	the words of the Decalogue; whereas the Gospel was first spoken by
	the Lord alone. 
	</p>
<p><b>was steadfast</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"<i>was made</i> steadfast," or "confirmed": was
	enforced by penalties on those violating it. 
	</p>
<p><b>transgression</b>—by doing
	evil; literally, <i>overstepping</i> its bounds: a positive violation
	of it. 
	</p>
<p><b>disobedience</b>—by
	neglecting to do good: a negative violation of it. 
	</p>
<p><b>recompense</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:19- :">Galatians 3:19- :</span>).</p>
