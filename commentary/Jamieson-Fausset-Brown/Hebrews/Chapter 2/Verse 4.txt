<p><b>4. them</b>—rather, "God also
	[as well as Christ, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:3">Hebrews 2:3</span>]
	bearing witness to it," c., joining in attestation of <i>it.</i>"
	</p>
<p><b>signs and wonders</b>—performed
	by Christ and His apostles. "Signs" and miracles, or other
	facts regarded as <i>proofs</i> of a divine mission "wonders"
	are miracles viewed as prodigies, causing <i>astonishment</i>
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:22">Acts 2:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:33">Acts 2:33</span>);
	"powers" are miracles viewed as evidences of superhuman
	<i>power.</i>
</p>
<p><b>divers miracles</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"varied (miraculous) <i>powers</i>" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:33- :">Acts 2:33- :</span>) granted to the apostles after the ascension. 
	</p>
<p><b>gifts,</b> c.—<i>Greek,</i>
	"distributions." The gift of the Holy Spirit was given to
	Christ without measure (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:34">John 3:34</span>),
	but to us it is distributed in various measures and operations
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:3">Romans 12:3</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:6">Romans 12:6</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:4-11">1 Corinthians 12:4-11</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>according to his own
	will</b>—God's free and sovereign will, assigning one gift of the
	Spirit to one, another to another (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:32">Acts 5:32</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:5">Ephesians 1:5</span>).</p>
