<p><b>10. grieved</b>—displeased.
	Compare "walk contrary," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="le+26:24">Leviticus 26:24</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="le+26:28">Leviticus 26:28</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>that generation</b>—"<i>that</i>"
	implies alienation and estrangement. But the oldest manuscripts read,
	"this." 
	</p>
<p><b>said</b>—"<i>grieved,</i>"
	or "displeased," at their first offense. Subsequently when
	they hardened their heart in unbelief still more, He <i>sware</i> in
	His <i>wrath</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:11">Hebrews 3:11</span>); an
	ascending gradation (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:17">Hebrews 3:17</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:18">Hebrews 3:18</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>and they have not
	known</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "But these very persons," c. They
	perceived I was displeased with them, yet they, the same persons, did
	not a whit the more wish to know my ways [BENGEL]
	compare "but they," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:18- :">Hebrews 3:18- :</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>not known my ways</b>—not
	known practically and believingly the ways in which I would have had
	them go, so as to reach My rest (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:18- :">Hebrews 3:18- :</span>).</p>
