<p><b>13. one another</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"yourselves"; let each exhort himself and his neighbor. 
	</p>
<p><b>daily</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "on
	each day," or "day by day." 
	</p>
<p><b>while it is called
	To-day</b>—while the "to-day" lasts (the day of grace, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, before the coming of the day of glory and judgment at
	Christ's coming, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:25">Hebrews 10:25</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:37">Hebrews 10:37</span>). To-morrow is the day
	when idle men work, and fools repent. To-morrow is Satan's to-day; he
	cares not what good resolutions you form, if only you fix them for
	to-morrow. 
	</p>
<p><b>lest . . . of you</b>—The
	"you" is emphatic, as distinguished from "your
	fathers" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:9">Hebrews 3:9</span>). "That
	from among you no one (so the <i>Greek</i> order is in some of the
	oldest manuscripts) be hardened" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:9- :">Hebrews 3:9- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>deceitfulness</b>—causing
	you to "err in your heart." 
	</p>
<p><b>sin</b>—unbelief.</p>
