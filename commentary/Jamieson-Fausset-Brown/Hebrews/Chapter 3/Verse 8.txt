<p><b>8. Harden not your hearts</b>—This
	phrase here only is used of <i>man's</i> own act; usually of <i>God's</i>
	act (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:18">Romans 9:18</span>). When man is
	spoken of as the agent in hardening, the phrase usually is, "harden
	his neck," or "back" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:18- :">Romans 9:18- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>provocation . . .
	temptation</b>—"Massah-meribah," translated in <i>Margin</i>
	"tentation . . . chiding," or "strife" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:18- :">Romans 9:18- :</span>). Both names seem to refer to that one event, the
	murmuring of the people against the Lord at Rephidim for want of
	water. The first offense especially ought to be guarded against, and
	is the most severely reproved, as it is apt to produce many more.
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="nu+20:1-13">Numbers 20:1-13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="de+33:8">Deuteronomy 33:8</span>
	mention a second similar occasion in the wilderness of Sin, near
	Kadesh, also called Meribah. 
	</p>
<p><b>in the day</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"according to the day of."</p>
