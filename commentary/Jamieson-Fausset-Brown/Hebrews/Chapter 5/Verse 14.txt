<p><b>14. strong meat</b>—"solid
	food." 
	</p>
<p><b>them . . . of full
	age</b>—literally, "perfect": akin to "perfection"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:1">Hebrews 6:1</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>by reason of use</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"habit." 
	</p>
<p><b>senses</b>—organs of sense.
	</p>
<p><b>exercised</b>—similarly
	connected with "righteousness" in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:1- :">Hebrews 6:1- :</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>to discern both good and
	evil</b>—as a child no longer an infant (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:1- :">Hebrews 6:1- :</span>): so able to distinguish between sound and unsound doctrine.
	The mere child puts into its mouth things hurtful and things
	nutritious, without discrimination: but not so the adult. Paul again
	alludes to their tendency not to discriminate, but to be carried
	about by strange doctrines, in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:1- :">Hebrews 6:1- :</span>.</p>
