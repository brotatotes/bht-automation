<p><b>3. by reason hereof</b>—"on
	account of this" infirmity. 
	</p>
<p><b>he ought . . . also for
	himself, to offer for sins</b>—the Levitical priest ought; in this
	our High Priest is superior to the Levitical. The second "for"
	is a different <i>Greek</i> term from the first; "<i>in behalf
	of</i> the people . . . <i>on account</i> of sins."</p>
