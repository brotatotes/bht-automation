<p><b>11. And</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "But."
	</p>
<p><b>desire</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"<i>earnestly</i> desire." The language of fatherly
	affection, rather than command. 
	</p>
<p><b>every one of you</b>—implying
	that <i>all</i> in the Palestinian churches had not shown the same
	diligence as some of those whom he praises in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. "He cares alike for great and small, and overlooks
	none." "Every one of them," even those diligent in
	acts of LOVE (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), needed to be stimulated to <i>persevere</i> in the same
	diligence with a view to the full assurance of HOPE unto the end.
	They needed, besides love, patient perseverance, resting on <i>hope</i>
	and <i>faith</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:36">Hebrews 10:36</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:7">Hebrews 13:7</span>). Compare "the full
	assurance of faith," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:22">Hebrews 10:22</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:21">Romans 4:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:5">1 Thessalonians 1:5</span>.
	</p>
<p><b>unto the end</b>—the coming
	of Christ.</p>
