<p><b>12. be not</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"become not." In <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, he said, "Ye have become dull (<i>Greek,</i>
	'<i>slothful</i>') <i>of hearing</i>"; here he warns them not to
	become "slothful <i>absolutely,</i>" namely, also in mind
	and deed. He will not become slothful who keeps always <i>the end</i>
	in view; <i>hope</i> is the means of ensuring this. 
	</p>
<p><b>followers</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"imitators"; so in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, <i>Greek;</i> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:1">1 Corinthians 11:1</span>.
	</p>
<p><b>patience</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"<i>long-suffering</i> endurance." There is the
	<i>long-suffering patience,</i> or <i>endurance</i> of <i>love,</i>
<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:4">1 Corinthians 13:4</span>, and that of <i>faith,</i>
<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:15">Hebrews 6:15</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>them who . . . inherit the
	promises</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "who <i>are inheriting,</i>"
	c. to whom the promises are their inheritance. Not that they <i>have</i>
	actually entered on the <i>perfect</i> inheritance, which <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:13">Hebrews 11:13</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:39">Hebrews 11:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:40">Hebrews 11:40</span>
	explicitly denies; though doubtless the dead in Christ have, in the
	disembodied soul, a foretaste of it; but "them (enumerated in
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:2-40">Hebrews 11:2-40</span>) who in
	every age have been, are, or shall be, <i>inheritors</i> of the
	promises"; of whom Abraham is an illustrious example (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:2-58.11.40- :">Hebrews 11:2-58.11.40- :</span>).</p>
