<p><b>8. that which</b>—rather as <i>Greek</i>
	(no article), "But <i>if it</i> (the 'land,' <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>) bear"; not so favorable a word as "bringeth
	forth," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:7">Hebrews 6:7</span>, said of
	the good soil. 
	</p>
<p><b>briers</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"thistles." 
	</p>
<p><b>rejected</b>—after having
	been <i>tested;</i> so the <i>Greek</i> implies. <i>Reprobate . . .
	rejected</i> by the Lord. 
	</p>
<p><b>nigh unto cursing</b>—on
	the verge of being given up to its own barrenness by the just curse
	of God. This "nigh" softens the severity of the previous
	"It is impossible," c. (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:4">Hebrews 6:4</span>
<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:6">Hebrews 6:6</span>). The ground is not yet
	actually <i>cursed.</i>
</p>
<p><b>whose</b>—"of which
	(<i>land</i>) the end is unto burning," namely, with the
	consuming fire of the last judgment; as the land of Sodom was given
	to "brimstone, salt, and <i>burning</i>" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:6- :">Hebrews 6:6- :</span>); so as to the ungodly (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:10">Matthew 3:10</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:12">Matthew 3:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:19">Matthew 7:19</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:30">Matthew 13:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:6">John 15:6</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:10">2 Peter 3:10</span>). Jerusalem, which had
	so resisted the grace of Christ, was then nigh unto cursing, and in a
	few years was burned. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:10- :">2 Peter 3:10- :</span>, "<i>burned</i> up their city" an earnest of a
	like fate to all wilful abusers of God's grace (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:26">Hebrews 10:26</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:27">Hebrews 10:27</span>).</p>
