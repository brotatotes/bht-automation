<p><b>11. perfection</b>—absolute: "the
	bringing of man to his highest state, namely, that of salvation and
	sanctification." 
	</p>
<p><b>under it</b>—The reading in
	the oldest manuscripts is, "<i>Upon</i> it (that is, on the
	ground of it as the basis, the priest having to administer the law,
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mal+2:7">Malachi 2:7</span>: it being presupposed)
	the people (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:19">Hebrews 9:19</span>, '<i>all</i>
	the people') have received the law (the <i>Greek</i> is <i>perfect,</i>
	not aorist tense; implying the people were still observing the law)."
	</p>
<p><b>what further need</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:19- :">Hebrews 9:19- :</span>). For God does nothing needless. 
	</p>
<p><b>another</b>—rather as
	<i>Greek,</i> "that a <i>different</i> priest (one of a
	different order) should arise (<i>anew,</i> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:19- :">Hebrews 9:19- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>not be called</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"not be <i>said (to be)</i> after the order of Aaron," that
	is, that, when spoken of in the <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:19- :">Hebrews 9:19- :</span>, "He is not said to be (as we should expect, if the
	Aaronic priesthood was perfect) after the order of Aaron."</p>
