<p><b>28. For</b>—reason for the
	difference stated in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:27">Hebrews 7:27</span>,
	between His one sacrifice and their oft repeated sacrifices, namely,
	because of His entire freedom from the sinful <i>infirmity</i> to
	which they are subject. <i>He needed not, as they, to offer</i> FOR
	HIS OWN SIN; and being now
	exempt from death and "perfected for evermore," <i>He needs
	not to</i> REPEAT <i>His
	sacrifice.</i>
</p>
<p><b>the word</b>—"the
	word" confirmed by "the oath." 
	</p>
<p><b>which</b>—which <i>oath</i>
	was after the law, namely, in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:27- :">Hebrews 7:27- :</span>, abrogating the preceding law-priesthood. 
	</p>
<p><b>the Son</b>—contrasted with
	"men." 
	</p>
<p><b>consecrated</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"made perfect" once for all, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:10">Hebrews 2:10</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:9">Hebrews 5:9</span>; see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:9- :">Hebrews 5:9- :</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:9">Hebrews 5:9</span>. Opposed to
	"having infirmity." <i>Consecrated as a perfected priest</i>
	by His perfected sacrifice, and consequent anointing and exaltation
	to the right hand of the Father.</p>
