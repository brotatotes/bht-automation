<p><b>1. the sum</b>—rather, "the
	principal point"; for the participle is present, not <i>past,</i>
	which would be required if the meaning were "the sum." "The
	chief point in (or, 'in the case'; so the <i>Greek,</i> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:10">Hebrews 9:10</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:15">Hebrews 9:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:17">Hebrews 9:17</span>)
	the things which we are speaking," literally, "which are
	being spoken." 
	</p>
<p><b>such</b>—so transcendently
	pre-eminent, namely in this respect, that "He is set on the
	right hand of," c. Infinitely above all other priests in this
	one grand respect, He exercises His priesthood IN
	HEAVEN, not in the <i>earthly</i> "holiest place"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:12">Hebrews 10:12</span>). The Levitical
	high priests, even when they entered the Holiest Place once a year,
	only STOOD for a <i>brief
	space before the symbol</i> of God's throne but Jesus SITS
	<i>on the throne</i> of the Divine Majesty in the heaven itself, and
	this <i>for ever</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:11">Hebrews 10:11</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:12">Hebrews 10:12</span>).</p>
