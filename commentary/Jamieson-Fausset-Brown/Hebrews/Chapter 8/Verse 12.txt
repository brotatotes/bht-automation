<p><b>12. For,</b> c.—the <i>third</i>
	of "the better promises" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). The <i>forgiveness of sins</i> is, and will be, the root of
	this new state of inward grace and knowledge of the Lord. Sin being
	abolished, sinners obtain grace. 
	</p>
<p><b>I will be merciful</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"propitious" the <i>Hebrew,</i> "<i>salach,</i>"
	is always used of God only in relation to men. 
	</p>
<p><b>and their iniquities</b>—not
	found in <i>Vulgate, Syriac, Coptic,</i> and one oldest <i>Greek</i>
	manuscript; but most oldest manuscripts have the words (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>remember no more</b>—Contrast
	the law, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:3">Hebrews 10:3</span>.</p>
