<p><b>1. Then verily</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"Accordingly then." Resuming the subject from <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. In accordance with the command given to Moses, "the
	first covenant had," c. 
	</p>
<p><b>had</b>—not "has,"
	for as a <i>covenant</i> it no longer existed, though its rites were
	observed till the destruction of Jerusalem. 
	</p>
<p><b>ordinances</b>—of divine
	right and institution. 
	</p>
<p><b>service</b>—worship. 
	</p>
<p><b>a worldly sanctuary</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"its (literally, '<i>the</i>') sanctuary worldly," mundane
	consisting of the elements of the visible world. Contrasted with <i>the
	heavenly sanctuary.</i> Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:11">Hebrews 9:11</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:12">Hebrews 9:12</span>, "not of this
	building," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:24">Hebrews 9:24</span>.
	Material, outward, perishing (however precious its materials were),
	and also defective religiously. In <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:24- :">Hebrews 9:24- :</span>, "<i>the worldly sanctuary</i>" is discussed; in
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:6-10">Hebrews 9:6-10</span>, c., the
	"ordinances of worship." The outer tabernacle the Jews
	believed, signified <i>this world</i> the Holy of Holies, <i>heaven.</i>
	JOSEPHUS calls the outer,
	divided into two parts, "a secular and common place,"
	answering to "the earth and sea"; and the inner holiest
	place, the third part, appropriated to God and not accessible to men.</p>
