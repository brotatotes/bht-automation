<p><b>10. </b><i><b>Which</b></i><b>
</b>—sacrifices. 
	</p>
<p><i><b>stood</b></i><b>
</b>—consisted in [ALFORD];
	or, "have attached to them" only things which appertain to
	the use of foods, c. The rites of meats, &amp;c., go <i>side by side</i>
	with the sacrifices [THOLUCK
	and WAHL] compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>drinks</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="le+10:9">Leviticus 10:9</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="le+11:4">Leviticus 11:4</span>). Usage subsequently to
	the law added many observances as to meats and drinks. 
	</p>
<p><b>washings</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="le+11:4- :">Leviticus 11:4- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>and carnal ordinances</b>—One
	oldest manuscript, <i>Syriac</i> and <i>Coptic,</i> omit "and."
	"Carnal ordinances" stand in apposition to "sacrifices"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:9">Hebrews 9:9</span>). <i>Carnal</i>
	(outward, affecting only the <i>flesh</i>) is opposed to <i>spiritual.</i>
	Contrast "flesh" with "conscience" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:13">Hebrews 9:13</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:14">Hebrews 9:14</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>imposed</b>—as a burden
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:10">Acts 15:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:28">Acts 15:28</span>)
	continually pressing heavy. 
	</p>
<p><b>until the time of
	reformation</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "the <i>season</i> of
	<i>rectification,</i>" when the reality should supersede the
	type (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:8-12">Hebrews 8:8-12</span>).
	Compare "better," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:8-58.8.12- :">Hebrews 8:8-58.8.12- :</span>.</p>
