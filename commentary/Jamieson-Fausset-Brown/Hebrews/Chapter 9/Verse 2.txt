<p><b>2.</b> Defining "the worldly
	tabernacle." 
	</p>
<p><b>a tabernacle</b>—"the
	tabernacle." 
	</p>
<p><b>made</b>—built and
	furnished. 
	</p>
<p><b>the first</b>—the anterior
	tabernacle. 
	</p>
<p><b>candlestick . . .
	table</b>—typifying <i>light</i> and <i>life</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). The candlestick consisted of a shaft and six branches
	of gold, seven in all, the bowls made like almonds, with a knop and a
	flower in one branch. It was carried in Vespasian's triumph, and the
	figure is to be seen on Titus' arch at Rome. The <i>table</i> of
	shittim wood, covered with gold, was for the showbread (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>showbread</b>—literally,
	"the setting forth of the loaves," that is, the loaves set
	forth: "the show of the bread" [ALFORD].
	In the outer holy place: so the Eucharist continues until our
	entrance into the heavenly Holy of Holies (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>which,</b> &amp;c.—"which
	(tabernacle) is called the holy place," as distinguished from
	"the Holy of Holies."</p>
