<p><b>heat</b>—rather, "the
	hot wind" from the (east or) south, which scorches vegetation
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:55">Luke 12:55</span>). The "burning
	heat" of the sun is not at its <i>rising,</i> but rather at
	noon; whereas the scorching <i>Kadim</i> wind is often at sunrise
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jon+4:8">Jonah 4:8</span>) [MIDDLETON,
	<i>The Doctrine of the Greek Article</i>]. <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jon+4:8- :">Jonah 4:8- :</span> uses the <i>Greek</i> word for "heat." <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jon+4:8- :">Jonah 4:8- :</span>, "<i>bloweth</i> upon it," seems to answer to "the
	hot <i>wind</i>" here. 
	</p>
<p><b>grace of the fashion</b>—that
	is of the external appearance. 
	</p>
<p><b>in his ways</b>—referring
	to the burdensome extent of the rich man's devices [BENGEL].
	Compare "his ways," that is, his course of life, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jon+4:8- :">Jonah 4:8- :</span>.</p>
