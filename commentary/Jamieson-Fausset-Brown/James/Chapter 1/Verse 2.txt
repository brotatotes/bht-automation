<p><b>2. My brethren</b>—a phrase often
	found in James, marking community of nation and of faith. 
	</p>
<p><b>all joy</b>—cause for the
	highest joy [GROTIUS].
	Nothing but joy [PISCATOR].
	Count <i>all</i> "divers temptations" to be <i>each</i>
	matter of <i>joy</i> [BENGEL].
	</p>
<p><b>fall into</b>—unexpectedly,
	so as to be <i>encompassed by</i> them (so the original <i>Greek</i>).
	</p>
<p><b>temptations</b>—not in the
	limited sense of allurements to sin, but <i>trials</i> or distresses
	of any kind which test and purify the Christian character. Compare
	"tempt," that is, try, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. Some of those to whom James writes were "sick,"
	or otherwise "afflicted" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). Every possible trial to the child of God is a masterpiece
	of strategy of the Captain of his salvation for his good.</p>
