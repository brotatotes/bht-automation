<p><b>5. Hearken</b>—James <i>brings to
	trial</i> the self-constituted "judges" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>poor of this world</b>—The
	best manuscripts read, "those poor <i>in respect to the</i>
	world." In contrast to "the rich in this world" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). Not of course <i>all</i> the poor; but the poor, <i>as a
	class,</i> furnish more believers than the rich as a class. The rich,
	if a believer, renounces riches as his portion; the poor, if an
	unbeliever, neglects that which is the peculiar advantage of poverty
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:3">Matthew 5:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:26">1 Corinthians 1:26</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:27">1 Corinthians 1:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:28">1 Corinthians 1:28</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>rich in faith</b>—<i>Their</i>
	riches consist <i>in faith.</i> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:28- :">1 Corinthians 1:28- :</span>, "rich toward God." <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:28- :">1 Corinthians 1:28- :</span>, "rich in good works" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:28- :">1 Corinthians 1:28- :</span>; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:9">2 Corinthians 8:9</span>).
	Christ's poverty is the source of the believer's riches. 
	</p>
<p><b>kingdom . . . promised</b>—
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:32">Luke 12:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:9">1 Corinthians 2:9</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:8">2 Timothy 4:8</span>).</p>
