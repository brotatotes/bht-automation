<p><b>6.</b> The world's judgment of the
	poor contrasted with God's. 
	</p>
<p><b>ye</b>—Christians, from
	whom better things might have been expected; there is no marvel that
	men of the world do so. 
	</p>
<p><b>despised</b>—literally,
	"dishonored." To dishonor the poor is to dishonor those
	whom God honors, and so to invert the order of God [CALVIN].
	</p>
<p><b>rich</b>—as a class. 
	</p>
<p><b>oppress</b>—literally,
	"abuse their power against" you. 
	</p>
<p><b>draw you</b>—Translate, "is
	it not <i>they</i> (those very persons whom ye partially prefer, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>) that <i>drag</i> you (namely, with violence)"
	[ALFORD]. 
	</p>
<p><b>before . . . judgment
	seats</b>—instituting persecutions for religion, as well as
	oppressive lawsuits, against you.</p>
