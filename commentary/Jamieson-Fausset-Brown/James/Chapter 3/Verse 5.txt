<p><b>5. boasteth great things</b>—There
	is <i>great</i> moment in what the careless think "little"
	things [BENGEL]. Compare
	"a world," "the course of nature," "hell,"
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:6">James 3:6</span>, which illustrate how
	the little tongue's great words produce great mischief. 
	</p>
<p><b>how great a matter a little
	fire kindleth</b>—The best manuscripts read, "<i>how little</i>
	a fire kindleth how great a," &amp;c. ALFORD,
	for "matter," translates, "forest." But GROTIUS
	translates as <i>English Version,</i> "material for burning":
	a pile of fuel.</p>
