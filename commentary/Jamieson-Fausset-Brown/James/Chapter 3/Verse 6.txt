<p><b>6.</b> Translate, "The tongue,
	that world of iniquity, is a fire." As man's little world is an
	image of the greater world, the universe, so the tongue is an image
	of the former [BENGEL]. 
	</p>
<p><b>so</b>—omitted in the
	oldest authorities. 
	</p>
<p><b>is</b>—literally, "is
	constituted." "The tongue is (constituted), among the
	members, the one which defileth," c. (namely, as fire defiles
	with its smoke). 
	</p>
<p><b>course of nature</b>—"the
	orb (cycle) of creation." 
	</p>
<p><b>setteth on fire . . . is set
	on fire</b>—habitually and continually. While a man inflames
	others, he passes out of his own power, being consumed in the flame
	himself. 
	</p>
<p><b>of hell</b>—that is, of the
	devil. <i>Greek,</i> "Gehenna" found here only and in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. James has much in common with the Sermon on the Mount (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>).</p>
