<p><b>6. But</b>—"Nay, rather."
	</p>
<p><b>he</b>—God. 
	</p>
<p><b>giveth more grace</b>—ever
	increasing grace; the farther ye depart from "envy"
	[BENGEL]. 
	</p>
<p><b>he saith</b>—The same God
	who causes His spirit to dwell in believers (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), by the Spirit also speaks in Scripture. The quotation here
	is probably from <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="pr+3:34">Proverbs 3:34</span>; as
	probably <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="pr+21:10">Proverbs 21:10</span> was
	generally referred to in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:5">James 4:5</span>.
	In <i>Hebrew</i> it is "scorneth the scorners," namely,
	those who think "Scripture speaketh in vain." 
	</p>
<p><b>resisteth</b>—literally,
	"setteth Himself in array against"; even as they, like
	Pharaoh, set themselves against Him. God repays sinners in their own
	coin. "Pride" is the mother of "envy" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:5- :">James 4:5- :</span>); it is peculiarly satanic, for by it Satan fell. 
	</p>
<p><b>the proud</b>—The <i>Greek</i>
	means in derivation one who <i>shows himself above</i> his fellows,
	and so lifts himself against God. 
	</p>
<p><b>the humble</b>—the
	unenvious, uncovetous, and unambitious as to the world. Contrast <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:5- :">James 4:5- :</span>.</p>
