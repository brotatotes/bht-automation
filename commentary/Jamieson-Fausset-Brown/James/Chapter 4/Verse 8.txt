<p><b>8. Draw nigh to God</b>—So "cleave
	unto Him," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="de+30:20">Deuteronomy 30:20</span>,
	namely, by prayerfully (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:2">James 4:2</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:3">James 4:3</span>) "resisting Satan,"
	who would oppose our access to God. 
	</p>
<p><b>he will draw nigh</b>—propitious.
	</p>
<p><b>Cleanse . . . hands</b>—the
	outward instruments of action. None but the clean-handed can ascend
	into the hill of the Lord (justified through Christ, who alone was
	perfectly so, and as such "ascended" thither). 
	</p>
<p><b>purify . . . hearts</b>—literally
	"make chaste" of your spiritual <i>adultery</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:3- :">James 4:3- :</span>, that is, worldliness) "your hearts": the inward
	source of all impurity. 
	</p>
<p><b>double-minded</b>—divided
	between God and the world. The "double-minded" is at fault
	in <i>heart;</i> the <i>sinner</i> in his <i>hands</i> likewise.</p>
