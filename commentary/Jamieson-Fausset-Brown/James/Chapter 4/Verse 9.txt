<p><b>9. Be afflicted</b>—literally,
	"Endure misery," that is, mourn over your wretchedness
	through sin. <i>Repent with deep sorrow</i> instead of your present
	laughter. A blessed <i>mourning.</i> Contrast <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+22:12">Isaiah 22:12</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+22:13">Isaiah 22:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:25">Luke 6:25</span>.
	James does not add here, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:25- :">Luke 6:25- :</span>, "howl," where he foretells the <i>doom of the
	impenitent</i> at the coming destruction of Jerusalem. 
	</p>
<p><b>heaviness</b>—literally,
	"falling of the countenance," casting down of the eyes.</p>
