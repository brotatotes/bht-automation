<p><b>12. But above all</b>—as swearing
	is utterly alien to the Christian meek "endurance" just
	recommended. 
	</p>
<p><b>swear not</b>—through
	impatience, to which trials may tempt you (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:10">James 5:10</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:11">James 5:11</span>). In contrast to this
	stands the proper use of the tongue, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:11- :">James 5:11- :</span>. James here refers to <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:11- :">James 5:11- :</span>, &amp;c. 
	</p>
<p><b>let your yea be yea</b>—Do
	not use oaths in your everyday conversation, but let a simple
	affirmative or denial be deemed enough to establish your word. 
	</p>
<p><b>condemnation</b>—literally,
	"judgment," namely, of "the Judge" who "standeth
	before the doors" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:9">James 5:9</span>).</p>
