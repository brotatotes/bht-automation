<p><b>18. No man</b>—"No one,"
	in the widest sense. 
	</p>
<p><b>hath seen God</b>—by
	immediate gaze, or direct intuition. 
	</p>
<p><b>in the bosom of the Father</b>—A
	remarkable expression, used only here, presupposing <i>the Son's
	conscious existence distinct from the Father, and expressing His
	immediate and most endeared access to, and</i> <i>absolute
	acquaintance with, Him.</i>
</p>
<p><b>he</b>—emphatic; As if he
	should say, "He and He only hath declared Him," because He
	only <i>can.</i>
</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. THE BAPTIST'S
	TESTIMONY TO CHRIST.</p>
