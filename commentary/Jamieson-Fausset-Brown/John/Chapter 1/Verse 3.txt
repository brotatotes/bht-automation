<p><b>3. All things,</b> c.—all things
	<i>absolutely</i> (as is evident from <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:10">John 1:10</span>
<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+8:6">1 Corinthians 8:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+1:16">Colossians 1:16</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+1:17">Colossians 1:17</span>; but put beyond question
	by what follows). 
	</p>
<p><b>without Him was not any
	thing</b>—<i>not one thing.</i>
</p>
<p><b>made</b>—brought into
	being. 
	</p>
<p><b>that was made</b>—This is a
	denial of the <i>eternity</i> and <i>non-creation</i> of matter,
	which was held by the whole thinking world <i>outside of Judaism and
	Christianity:</i> or rather, its proper <i>creation</i> was never so
	much as dreamt of save by the children of <i>revealed religion.</i></p>
