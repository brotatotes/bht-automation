<p><b>31. The Jews . . . followed her . .
	. to the grave</b>—Thus <i>casually</i> were provided witnesses of
	the glorious miracle that followed, <i>not prejudiced,</i> certainly,
	<i>in favor</i> of Him who wrought it. 
	</p>
<p><b>to weep there</b>—according
	to Jewish practice, for some days after burial. 
	</p>
<p><b>fell at his feet</b>—more
	impassioned than her sister, though her words were fewer. (See on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>).</p>
