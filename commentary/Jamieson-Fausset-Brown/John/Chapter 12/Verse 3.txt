<p><b>3. spikenard</b>—or pure <i>nard,</i>
	a celebrated aromatic (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="so+1:12">Song of Solomon 1:12</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>anointed the feet of
	Jesus</b>—and "poured it on His head" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:7">Matthew 26:7</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:3">Mark 14:3</span>). The only use of this
	was to refresh and exhilarate—a grateful compliment in the East,
	amidst the closeness of a heated atmosphere, with many guests at a
	feast. Such was the form in which Mary's love to Christ, at so much
	cost to herself, poured itself out.</p>
