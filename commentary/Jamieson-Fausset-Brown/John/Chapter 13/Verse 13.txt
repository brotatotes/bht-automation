<p><b>13. Ye call me Master</b>—Teacher.
	</p>
<p><b>and Lord</b>—<i>learning</i>
	of Him in the one capacity, <i>obeying</i> Him in the other. 
	</p>
<p><b>and ye say well, for so I
	am</b>—The conscious dignity with which this claim is made is
	remarkable, following immediately on His laying aside the towel of
	service. Yet what is this whole history but a succession of such
	astonishing contrast from first to last?</p>
