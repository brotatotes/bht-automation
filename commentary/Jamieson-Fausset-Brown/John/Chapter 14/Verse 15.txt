<p><b>15-17. If ye love me, keep my
	commandments. And I will pray the Father,</b> c.—This connection
	seems designed to teach that the proper temple for the indwelling
	Spirit of Jesus is a heart filled with that love to Him which lives
	actively for Him, and so this was the fitting preparation for the
	promised gift. 
	</p>
<p><b>he shall give you another
	Comforter</b>—a word used only by John in his <i>Gospel</i> with
	reference to the Holy Spirit, in his <i>First Epistle</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), with reference to Christ Himself. Its proper sense is an
	"advocate," "patron," "helper." In this
	sense it is plainly meant of Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), and in this sense it comprehends all the <i>comfort</i> as
	well as <i>aid</i> of the Spirit's work. The Spirit is here promised
	as One who would <i>supply Christ's own place</i> in His absence. 
	</p>
<p><b>that he may abide with you
	for ever</b>—never go away, as Jesus was going to do in the body.</p>
