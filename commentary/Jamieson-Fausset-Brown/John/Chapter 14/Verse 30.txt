<p><b>30, 31. Hereafter I will not talk
	much with you</b>—"I have a little more to say, but My work
	hastens apace, and the approach of the adversary will cut it short."
	</p>
<p><b>for the prince of this
	world</b>—(See on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:1">John 14:1</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>cometh</b>—with hostile
	intent, for a last grand attack, having failed in His first
	formidable assault (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:1- :">John 14:1- :</span>) from which he "departed [only] <i>for a season</i>"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:13">John 14:13</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>and hath nothing in
	me</b>—<i>nothing of His own—nothing to fasten on.</i> Glorious
	saying! The <i>truth</i> of it is, that which makes the Person and
	Work of Christ the life of the world (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:14">Hebrews 9:14</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:5">1 John 3:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:21">2 Corinthians 5:21</span>).</p>
