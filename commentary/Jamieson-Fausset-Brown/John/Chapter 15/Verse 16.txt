<p><b>16. Ye have not chosen me, but I . .
	. you</b>—a wholesale memento after the lofty things He had just
	said about their mutual indwelling, and the unreservedness of the
	friendship they had been admitted to. 
	</p>
<p><b>ordained</b>—appointed. 
	</p>
<p><b>you, that ye should go and
	bring forth fruit</b>—that is, give yourselves to it. 
	</p>
<p><b>and that your fruit should
	remain</b>—showing itself to be an imperishable and ever growing
	principle. (Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="pr+4:18">Proverbs 4:18</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:8">2 John 1:8</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>that whatsoever ye shall ask,</b>
	&amp;c.—(See on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:1">John 15:1</span>).</p>
