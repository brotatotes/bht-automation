<p><b>12. I kept</b>—guarded. 
	</p>
<p><b>them in thy name</b>—acting
	as Thy Representative on earth. 
	</p>
<p><b>none of them is lost, but the
	son of perdition</b>—It is not implied here that the son of
	perdition was one of those whom the Father had given to the Son, but
	rather the contrary (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:18">John 13:18</span>)
	[WEBSTER and WILKINSON].
	It is just as in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:26">Luke 4:26</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:27">Luke 4:27</span>, where we are not to
	suppose that the woman of <i>Sarepta</i> (in Sidon) was one of the
	widows of <i>Israel,</i> nor Naaman the <i>Syrian</i> one of the
	lepers in <i>Israel,</i> though the language—the same as here—might
	<i>seem</i> to express it. 
	</p>
<p><b>son of perdition</b>—doomed
	to it (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:3">2 Thessalonians 2:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:21">Mark 14:21</span>).</p>
