<p><b>25. And Simon Peter was standing and
	warming himself. They said therefore . . . Art thou not also one of
	his disciples?</b>—In <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:71">Matthew 26:71</span>
	the <i>second</i> charge was made by "another maid, when he was
	gone out into the porch," who "saw him, and said unto them
	that were there, This [fellow] was also with Jesus of Nazareth."
	So also <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:69">Mark 14:69</span>. But in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:69- :">Mark 14:69- :</span> it is said, "After a little while" (from the time
	of the first denial), "another [<i>man</i>] saw him, and said,
	Thou art also of them." Possibly it was thrown at him by more
	than one; but these circumstantial variations only confirm the truth
	of the narrative. 
	</p>
<p><b>He denied it, and said, I am
	not</b>—in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:72">Matthew 26:72</span>, "He
	denied with an oath, I do not know the man." This was THE
	SECOND DENIAL.</p>
