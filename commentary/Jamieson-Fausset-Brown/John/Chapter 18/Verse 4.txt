<p><b>4-9. Jesus . . . knowing all things
	that should come</b>—were coming. 
	</p>
<p><b>upon him, went forth</b>—from
	the shade of the trees, probably, into open view, indicating His
	sublime preparedness to meet His captors. 
	</p>
<p><b>Whom seek ye?</b>—partly to
	prevent a rush of the soldiery upon the disciples [BENGEL];
	and see <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:51">Mark 14:51</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:52">Mark 14:52</span>,
	as showing a tendency to this: but still more as part of that courage
	and majesty which so overawed them. He would not wait to be <i>taken.</i></p>
