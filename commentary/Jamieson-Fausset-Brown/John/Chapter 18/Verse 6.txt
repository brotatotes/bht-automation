<p><b>6. As soon then as he said unto
	them, I am He, they went backward</b>—recoiled. 
	</p>
<p><b>and fell to the ground</b>—struck
	down by a power such as that which smote Saul of Tarsus and his
	companions to the earth (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). It was the glorious effulgence of the majesty of Christ
	which overpowered them. "This, occurring before His surrender,
	would show His <i>power</i> over His enemies, and so the <i>freedom</i>
	with which He gave Himself up" [MEYER].</p>
