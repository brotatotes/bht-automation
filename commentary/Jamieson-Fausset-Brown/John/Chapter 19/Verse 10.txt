<p><b>10. Then saith Pilate unto him,
	Speakest thou not to me?</b>—The "me" is the emphatic
	word in the question. He falls back upon the <i>pride of office,</i>
	which doubtless tended to blunt the workings of his conscience. 
	</p>
<p><b>knowest thou not that I have
	power to crucify thee, and have power to release thee?</b>—said to
	work upon Him at once by <i>fear</i> and by <i>hope.</i></p>
