<p><b>11. Thou couldest</b>—rather,
	"shouldst." 
	</p>
<p><b>have no power at all against
	me</b>—neither to crucify nor to release, nor to do anything
	whatever against Me [BENGEL].
	</p>
<p><b>except it were</b>—"unless
	it had been." 
	</p>
<p><b>given thee from above</b>—that
	is, "Thou thinkest too much of thy power, Pilate: against Me
	that power is none, save what is meted out to thee by special divine
	appointment, for a special end." 
	</p>
<p><b>therefore he that delivered
	me unto thee</b>—Caiaphas, too wit—but he only as representing
	the Jewish authorities as a body. 
	</p>
<p><b>hath the greater sin</b>—as
	having better opportunities and more knowledge of such matters.</p>
