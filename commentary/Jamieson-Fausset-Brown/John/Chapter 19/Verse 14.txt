<p><b>14. It was the preparation</b>—that
	is, the day before the Jewish sabbath. 
	</p>
<p><b>and about the sixth hour</b>—The
	true reading here is probably, "the <i>third</i> hour"—or
	nine A.M.—which agrees
	best with the whole series of events, as well as with the other
	Evangelists. 
	</p>
<p><b>he saith to the Jews, Behold
	your King!</b>—Having now made up his mind to yield to them, he
	takes a sort of quiet revenge on them by this irony, which he knew
	would sting them. This only reawakens their cry to despatch Him.</p>
