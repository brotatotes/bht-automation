<p><b>16. my Father's house</b>—How
	close the resemblance of these remarkable words to <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>; the same <i>consciousness of intrinsic relation to the
	temple</i>—as the seat of His Father's most august worship, and so
	the symbol of all that is due to Him on earth—dictating both
	speeches. Only, when but a youth, <i>with no authority,</i> He was
	simply "a SON IN His
	own house"; now He was "a SON
	OVER His own house" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), the proper Representative, and in flesh "the Heir,"
	of his Father's rights. 
	</p>
<p><b>house of merchandise</b>—There
	was nothing wrong in the merchandise; but to bring it, for their own
	and others' convenience, into that most sacred place, was a
	high-handed profanation which the eye of Jesus could not endure.</p>
