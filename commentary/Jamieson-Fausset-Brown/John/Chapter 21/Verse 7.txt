<p><b>7-11. that disciple whom Jesus
	loved, said, It is the Lord</b>—again having the advantage of his
	brother in quickness of recognition (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), to be followed by an alacrity in Peter <i>all his own.</i>
</p>
<p><b>he was naked</b>—his vest
	only on, worn next the body. 
	</p>
<p><b>cast himself into the sea</b>—the
	shallow part, not more than a hundred yards from the water's edge
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:8">John 21:8</span>), not meaning
	therefore to swim, but to get sooner to Jesus than in the full boat
	which they could hardly draw to shore.</p>
