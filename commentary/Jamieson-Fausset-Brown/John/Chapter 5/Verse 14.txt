<p><b>14. findeth him in the
	temple</b>—saying, perhaps, "I will go into Thy house with
	burnt offerings, I will pay my vows which my lips have uttered and my
	mouth hath spoken when I was in trouble" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+66:13">Psalms 66:13</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+66:14">Psalms 66:14</span>). Jesus, there Himself
	for His own ends, "findeth him there"—<i>not all
	accidentally,</i> be assured. 
	</p>
<p><b>sin no more,</b> &amp;c.—a
	glimpse this of the reckless life he had probably led <i>before</i>
	his thirty-eight years' infirmity had come upon him, and which not
	improbably had brought on, in the just judgment of God, his chronic
	complaint. Fearful illustration this of "the severity of God,"
	but glorious manifestation of our Lord's insight into "what was
	in man."</p>
