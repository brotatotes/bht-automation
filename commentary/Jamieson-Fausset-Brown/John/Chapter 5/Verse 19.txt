19, 20. the Son can do nothing of
	himself—that is, apart from and in rivalry of the
	Father, as they supposed. The meaning is, "The Son can have no
	separate interest or action from the Father." 
	for what things, &c.—On
	the contrary, "whatever the Father doeth that same doeth the
	Son," 
	likewise—"in the
	like manner." What claim to absolute equality with the Father
	could exceed this: not only to do "the same things," but to
	do them as the Father does them?
