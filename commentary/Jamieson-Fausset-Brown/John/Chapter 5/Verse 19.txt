<p><b>19, 20. the Son can do nothing of
	himself</b>—that is, <i>apart from</i> and <i>in rivalry of</i> the
	Father, as they supposed. The meaning is, "The Son can have no
	separate <i>interest</i> or <i>action</i> from the Father." 
	</p>
<p><b>for what things,</b> &amp;c.—On
	the contrary, "whatever the Father doeth that same doeth the
	Son," 
	</p>
<p><b>likewise</b>—"in the
	like manner." What claim to absolute equality with the Father
	could exceed this: not only to do "the same things," but to
	do them <i>as the Father does them?</i></p>
