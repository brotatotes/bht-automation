22. For the Father judgeth no man,
	&c.—rather, "For neither doth the Father judge any man,"
	implying that the same "thing was meant in the former verse of
	the quickening of the dead"—both acts being done, not by the
	Father and the Son, as though twice done, but by the Father
	through the Son as His voluntary Agent. 
	all judgment—judgment
	in its most comprehensive sense, or as we should say, all
	administration.
