<p><b>35. He was a burning and a shining
	light</b>—literally, "<i>the</i> burning and shining lamp"
	(or torch):—that is, "the great light of his day." Christ
	is never called by the humble word here applied to John—a
	<i>light-bearer</i>—studiously used to distinguish him from his
	Master, but ever <i>the Light</i> in the most absolute sense. (See on
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:6">John 1:6</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>willing for a season</b>—that
	is, till they saw that it pointed whither they were not prepared to
	go. 
	</p>
<p><b>to rejoice in his light</b>—There
	is a play of irony here, referring to the hollow delight with which
	his testimony tickled them.</p>
