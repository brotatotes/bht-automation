35. He was a burning and a shining
	light—literally, "the burning and shining lamp"
	(or torch):—that is, "the great light of his day." Christ
	is never called by the humble word here applied to John—a
	light-bearer—studiously used to distinguish him from his
	Master, but ever the Light in the most absolute sense. (See on
	John 1:6). 
	willing for a season—that
	is, till they saw that it pointed whither they were not prepared to
	go. 
	to rejoice in his light—There
	is a play of irony here, referring to the hollow delight with which
	his testimony tickled them.
