47. If ye believe not—(See  :-). 
	his writings . . . my words—a
	remarkable contrast, not absolutely exalting Old Testament
	Scripture above His own words, but pointing to the office of those
	venerable documents to prepare Christ's way, to the necessity
	universally felt for documentary testimony in revealed
	religion, and perhaps (as STIER
	adds) to the relation which the comparative "letter"
	of the Old Testament holds to the more flowing "words" of
	"spirit and life" which characterize the New Testament.
