<p><b>19. they see Jesus</b>—"about
	the fourth watch of the night" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:25">Matthew 14:25</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:48">Mark 6:48</span>), or between three and
	six in the morning. 
	</p>
<p><b>walking on the sea</b>—What
	Job (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="job+9:8">Job 9:8</span>) celebrates as the
	distinguishing prerogative of GOD,
	"WHO ALONE spreadeth
	out the heavens, and TREADETH
	UPON THE WAVES OF THE SEA"—What AGUR
	challenges as GOD'S
	unapproachable prerogative, to "GATHER
	THE WIND IN HIS FISTS,
	and BIND THE WATERS IN A GARMENT"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="pr+30:4">Proverbs 30:4</span>) —lo! this is here
	done <i>in flesh,</i> by "THE
	SON OF MAN." 
	</p>
<p><b>drawing nigh to the ship</b>—yet
	as though He "<i>would have passed by them,</i>" <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="pr+30:4- :">Proverbs 30:4- :</span> (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:28">Luke 24:28</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ge+18:3">Genesis 18:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ge+18:5">Genesis 18:5</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ge+32:24-26">Genesis 32:24-26</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>they were afraid</b>—"cried
	out for fear" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:26">Matthew 14:26</span>),
	"supposing it had been a spirit" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:26- :">Matthew 14:26- :</span>). He would appear to them at first like a dark moving speck
	upon the waters; then as a human figure, but—in the dark
	tempestuous sky, and not dreaming that it could be their Lord—they
	take it for a spirit. (How often thus we miscall our chiefest
	mercies—not only thinking them distant when they are near, but
	thinking the best the worst!)</p>
