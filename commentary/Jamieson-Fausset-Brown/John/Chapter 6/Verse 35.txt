<p><b>35. I am the bread of
	life</b>—Henceforth the discourse is all <i>in the first person,</i>
	"I," "Me," which occur in one form or other, as
	STIER reckons, thirty-five
	times. 
	</p>
<p><b>he that cometh to me</b>—to
	obtain what the soul craves, and as the only all-sufficient and
	ordained source of supply. 
	</p>
<p><b>hunger . . . thirst</b>—shall
	have conscious and abiding satisfaction.</p>
