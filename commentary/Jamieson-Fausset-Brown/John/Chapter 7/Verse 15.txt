<p><b>15. How knoweth . . .
	letters</b>—learning (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:24">Acts 26:24</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>having never learned</b>—at
	any rabbinical school, as Paul under Gamaliel. These rulers knew well
	enough that He had not <i>studied</i> under any human teacher—an
	important admission against ancient and modern attempts to trace our
	Lord's wisdom to human sources [MEYER].
	Probably His teaching on this occasion was <i>expository,</i>
	manifesting that unrivalled faculty and depth which in the Sermon on
	the Mount had excited the astonishment of all.</p>
