<p><b>17. If any man will do his will,</b>
	c.—"is willing," or "wishes to do." 
	</p>
<p><b>whether . . . of God, or . .
	. of myself</b>—from above or from beneath is divine or an
	imposture of Mine. A principle of immense importance, showing, on the
	one hand, that <i>singleness of desire to please God is the grand
	inlet to light on all questions vitally affecting one's eternal
	interests,</i> and on the other, that <i>the want of his,</i> whether
	perceived or not, <i>is the chief cause of infidelity amidst the
	light of revealed religion.</i></p>
