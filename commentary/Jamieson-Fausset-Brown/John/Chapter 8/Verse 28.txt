<p><b>28-30. When ye have lifted up the
	Son of man</b>—The plainest intimation He had yet given <i>in
	public</i> of <i>the manner and the authors</i> of His death. 
	</p>
<p><b>then shall ye know that I am
	</b><i><b>he</b></i><b>,</b> &amp;c.—that is, <i>find out,</i> or
	have sufficient evidence, how true was all He said, though they would
	be far from owning it.</p>
