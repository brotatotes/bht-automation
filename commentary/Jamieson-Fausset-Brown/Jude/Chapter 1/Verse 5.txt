	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>.)</p>
<p style=" "><b>therefore</b>
	— Other oldest manuscripts and <i>Vulgate</i>
	read, “But”; in contrast to the ungodly <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>though
	ye once</b>
	— rather, “once for all.” Translate, “I wish to remind you,
	<i>as</i>
	knowing ALL (namely, <i>that
	I am referring to;</i>
	so the oldest manuscripts, versions, and Fathers) <i>once
	for all.</i>”
	<i>As</i>
	already they know all the facts once for all, he needs only to
	“remind” them.</p>
<p style=" "><b>the
	Lord</b>
	— The oldest manuscripts and versions read, “Jesus.” So
	“Christ” is said to have accompanied the Israelites in the
	wilderness; so perfectly is Jesus one with the God of the Israelite
	theocracy.</p>
<p style=" "><b>saved</b>
	— brought safely, and into a state of safety and salvation.</p>
<p style=" "><b>afterward</b>
	— <i>Greek,</i>
	“secondly”; in the next instance “destroyed them that believed
	not,” as contrasted with His <i>in
	the first instance</i>
	having <i>saved</i>
	them.</p>
