<p style=" ">Concluding
	doxology.</p>
<p style=" "><b>Now</b>
	— <i>Greek,</i>
	“But.”</p>
<p style=" "><b>you</b>
	— Alford, on inferior authority, reads, “them.” <i>You</i>
	is in contradistinction to those <i>ungodly
	men</i>
	mentioned above.</p>
<p style=" "><b>keep
	... from falling</b>
	— rather, “guard ... (so as to be) <i>without
	falling,</i>”
	or <i>stumbling.</i></p>
<p style=" "><b>faultless</b>
	— <i>Greek,</i>
	“blameless.”</p>
<p style=" "><b>before
	the presence of his glory</b>
	— that is, <i>before
	Himself,</i>
	when He shall be revealed in <i>glory.</i></p>
<p style=" "><b>with
	exceeding joy</b>
	— literally, “with exultation” as of those who <i>leap</i>
	for joy.</p>
