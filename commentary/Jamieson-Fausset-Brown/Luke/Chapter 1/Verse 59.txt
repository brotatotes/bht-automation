<p><b>59. eighth day</b>—The law (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>) was observed, even though the eighth day after birth
	should be a sabbath (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:23">John 7:23</span>;
	and see <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+3:5">Philippians 3:5</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>called him</b>—literally,
	"were calling"—that is, (as we should say) "were for
	calling." The <i>naming</i> of children at baptism has its
	origin in the Jewish custom at circumcision (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ge+21:3">Genesis 21:3</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ge+21:4">Genesis 21:4</span>); and the names of Abram
	and Sarai were changed at its first performance (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ge+17:5">Genesis 17:5</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ge+17:15">Genesis 17:15</span>).</p>
