<p><b>77. to give knowledge of
	salvation</b>—To sound the note of a <i>needed</i> and <i>provided</i>
	"salvation" was the noble office of John, above all that
	preceded him; as it is that of all subsequent ministers of Christ;
	but infinitely loftier was it to be the "Salvation" itself
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:69">Luke 1:69</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:30">Luke 2:30</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>by the remission of . . .
	sins</b>—This stamps at once the <i>spiritual</i> nature of the
	salvation here intended, and explains <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:71">Luke 1:71</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:74">Luke 1:74</span>.</p>
