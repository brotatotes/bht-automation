<p><b>31, 32. came down a . . . priest . .
	. and a Levite</b>—Jericho, the second city of Judea, was a city of
	the priests and Levites, and thousands of them lived there. The two
	here mentioned are supposed, apparently, to be returning from <i>temple
	duties,</i> but they had not learnt what that meaneth, 'I will have
	mercy and not sacrifice' [TRENCH].
	</p>
<p><b>saw him</b>—It was not
	<i>inadvertently</i> that he acted. 
	</p>
<p><b>came and looked</b>—a
	further aggravation. 
	</p>
<p><b>passed by</b>—although the
	law expressly required the opposite treatment even of the <i>beast</i>
	not only of their <i>brethren,</i> but of their <i>enemy</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="de+22:4">Deuteronomy 22:4</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ex+23:4">Exodus 23:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ex+23:5">Exodus 23:5</span>;
	compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+58:7">Isaiah 58:7</span>).</p>
