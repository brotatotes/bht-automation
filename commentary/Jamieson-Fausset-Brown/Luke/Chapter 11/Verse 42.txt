<p><b>42. mint . . . rue,</b> c.—rounding
	on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="le+27:30">Leviticus 27:30</span>, which they
	interpreted rigidly. Our Lord purposely names the most trifling
	products of the earth, as examples of what they punctiliously exacted
	the tenth of. 
	</p>
<p><b>judgment and the love of
	God</b>—in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:25">Matthew 23:25</span>,
	"judgment, mercy, and <i>faith.</i>" The reference is to
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mic+6:6-8">Micah 6:6-8</span>, whose third
	element of all acceptable religion, "walking humbly with God,"
	comprehends both "love" and "faith." (See on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mic+6:6-33.6.8- :">Micah 6:6-33.6.8- :</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mic+6:6-33.6.8- :">Micah 6:6-33.6.8- :</span>). The same
	tendency to merge greater duties in less besets us still, <i>but it
	is the characteristic of hypocrites.</i>
</p>
<p><b>these ought ye,</b> c.—There
	is no need for one set of duties to jostle out another but of the
	<i>greater,</i> our Lord says, "Ye <i>ought to have done</i>"
	them; of the <i>lesser,</i> only "ye ought <i>not to leave them
	undone.</i>"</p>
