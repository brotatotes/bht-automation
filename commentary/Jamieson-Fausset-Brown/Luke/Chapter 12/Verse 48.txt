<p><b>48. knew not</b>—that is knew but
	<i>partially;</i> for <i>some</i> knowledge is presupposed both in
	the name "servant" of Christ, and his being liable to
	punishment at all. 
	</p>
<p><b>many . . . few
	stripes</b>—degrees of future punishment proportioned to the
	knowledge sinned against. Even heathens are not without knowledge
	enough for future judgment; but the reference here is not to such. It
	is a solemn truth, and though <i>general,</i> like all other
	revelations of the future world, discloses a tangible and momentous
	principle in its awards.</p>
