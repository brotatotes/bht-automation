<p><b>16. would fain have filled</b>—rather,
	"was fain to fill," ate greedily of the only food he could
	get. 
	</p>
<p><b>the husks</b>—"the
	hulls of a leguminous plant which in the East is the food of cattle
	and swine, and often the nourishment of the poorest in times of
	distress" [STIER]. 
	</p>
<p><b>no man gave . . . him</b>—not
	this food, for that he had, but <i>anything better</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). This was his lowest depth—<i>perishing unpitied, alone
	in the world,</i> and <i>ready to disappear from it unmissed!</i> But
	this is just the blessed turning-point; midnight before dawn of day
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ch+12:8">2 Chronicles 12:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ch+33:11-13">2 Chronicles 33:11-13</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jer+2:19">Jeremiah 2:19</span>).</p>
