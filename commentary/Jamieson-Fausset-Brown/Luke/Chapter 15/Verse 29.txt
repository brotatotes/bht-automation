<p><b>29. these many years . . . neither
	transgressed I at any time thy commandment</b>—The words are not to
	be pressed too far. He is merely contrasting <i>his constancy of love
	and service</i> with the conduct of his brother; just as Job,
	resenting the charge of <i>hypocrisy</i> by his friends, speaks as if
	nothing could be laid to his charge (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), and David too (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). <i>The father attests the truth of all he says.</i>
</p>
<p><b>never . . . a kid</b>—I say
	not a <i>calf,</i> but not even a kid. 
	</p>
<p><b>that I might make merry with
	my friends</b>—Here lay his misapprehension. It was no
	entertainment for the gratification of the prodigal: it was a
	<i>father's</i> expression of the joy <i>he</i> felt at his recovery.
	</p>
<p><b>thy son . . . thy living</b>—How
	unworthy a reflection on the common father of both, for the one not
	only to disown the other, but fling him over upon his father, as if
	he should say, Take him, and have joy of him!</p>
