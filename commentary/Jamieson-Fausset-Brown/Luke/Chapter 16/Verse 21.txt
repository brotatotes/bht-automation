<p><b>21. desiring to be fed with</b>—but
	was not [GROTIUS, BENGEL,
	MEYER, TRENCH,
	&amp;c.]. The words may mean indeed "was fain to feed on,"
	or "gladly fed on," as in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span> [ALFORD, WEBSTER
	and WILKINSON, &amp;c.].
	But the context rather favors the former. 
	</p>
<p><b>licked,</b> &amp;c.—a
	touching act of brute pity, in the absence of human relief. It is a
	case of heartless indifference, amidst luxuries of every kind, to one
	of God's poorest and most afflicted ones, presented daily before the
	eye.</p>
