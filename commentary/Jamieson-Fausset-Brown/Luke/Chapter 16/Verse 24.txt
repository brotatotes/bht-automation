<p><b>24. Father Abraham</b>—a
	well-founded, but unavailing, claim of natural descent (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:8">Luke 3:8</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:37">John 8:37</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>mercy on me</b>—who never
	showed any (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:3">James 2:3</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>send Lazarus</b>—the pining
	victim of his merciless neglect. 
	</p>
<p><b>that he may</b>—take me
	hence? No; that he dares not to ask. 
	</p>
<p><b>dip . . . tongue</b>—that
	is the <i>least</i> conceivable and the <i>most momentary</i>
	abatement of his torment; that is all. But even this he is told is
	(1) <i>unreasonable.</i></p>
