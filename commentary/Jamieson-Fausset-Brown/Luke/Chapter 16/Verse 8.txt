<p><b>8. the lord</b>—evidently the
	steward's lord, so called in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:3">Luke 16:3</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:5">Luke 16:5</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>commended,</b> c.—not for
	his "injustice," but "because he had done <i>wisely,</i>"
	or prudently with commendable <i>foresight</i> and <i>skilful
	adaptation of means to end.</i>
</p>
<p><b>children of this world</b>—so
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:34">Luke 20:34</span>; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:34- :">Luke 20:34- :</span> ("their portion in this life"); <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:34- :">Luke 20:34- :</span> ("mind earthly things"); <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+4:6">Psalms 4:6</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+4:7">Psalms 4:7</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>their generation</b>—or
	"for their generation"—that is, for the purposes of the
	"world" they are "of." The greater wisdom (or
	shrewdness) of the one, in <i>adaptation of means to ends,</i> and in
	energetic, determined prosecution of them, is none of it for <i>God</i>
	and <i>eternity</i>—a region they were never in, an atmosphere they
	never breathed, an undiscovered world, an unborn existence to
	them—but all for the purposes of their own grovelling and fleeting
	generation. 
	</p>
<p><b>children of light</b>—(so
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:36">John 12:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:8">Ephesians 5:8</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:5">1 Thessalonians 5:5</span>). Yet this is only "as
	night-birds see better in the dark than those of the day owls than
	eagles" [CAJETAN and
	TRENCH]. But we may learn
	lessons from them, as our Lord now shows, and "be wise as
	<i>serpents.</i>"</p>
