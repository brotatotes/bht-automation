<p><b>26-30. eat . . . married . . .
	planted</b>—all the ordinary occupations and enjoyments of life.
	Though the antediluvian world and the cities of the plain were
	awfully wicked, it is not their <i>wickedness,</i> but their
	<i>worldliness,</i> their unbelief and indifference to the future,
	their <i>unpreparedness,</i> that is here held up as a warning.
	<i>Note.</i>—These recorded events of Old Testament history—denied
	or explained away nowadays by not a few—are referred to here as
	<i>facts.</i></p>
