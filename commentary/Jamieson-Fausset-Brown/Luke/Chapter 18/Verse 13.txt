<p><b>13. standing afar off</b>—as
	unworthy to draw near; but that was the way <i>to get</i> near
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+34:18">Psalms 34:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+57:15">Isaiah 57:15</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>would not lift up</b>—blushing
	and ashamed to do so (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ezr+9:6">Ezra 9:6</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>smote,</b> c.—kept smiting
	for anguish (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:48">Luke 23:48</span>), and
	self-reproach (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jer+31:19">Jeremiah 31:19</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>be merciful</b>—"be
	propitiated," a very unusual word in such a sense, only once
	else used in the New Testament, in the sense of "making
	reconciliation" by sacrifice (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jer+31:19- :">Jeremiah 31:19- :</span>). There <i>may</i> therefore, be some allusion to this here,
	though not likely. 
	</p>
<p><b>a sinner</b>—literally,
	"<i>the</i> sinner"; that is, "If ever there was one,
	I am he."</p>
