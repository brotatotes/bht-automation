<p><b>7. shall not God</b>—not unjust,
	but the infinitely righteous Judge. 
	</p>
<p><b>avenge</b>—redeem from
	oppression. 
	</p>
<p><b>his own elect</b>—not like
	this widow, the object of indifference and contempt, but dear to Him
	as the apple of the eye (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="zec+2:8">Zechariah 2:8</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>cry day and night</b>—whose
	every cry enters into the ears of the Lord of Sabaoth (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="zec+2:8- :">Zechariah 2:8- :</span>), and how much more their incessant and persevering cries! 
	</p>
<p><b>bear long with them</b>—rather,
	"in their case," or "on their account" (as) <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="zec+2:8- :">Zechariah 2:8- :</span>, "for it"), [GROTIUS,
	DE WETTE,
	&amp;c.].</p>
