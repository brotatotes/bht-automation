<p><b>43. a trench</b>—a rampart; first
	of wood, and when this was burnt, a built wall, four miles in
	circuit, built in three days—so determined were they. This "cut
	off all hope of escape," and consigned the city to unparalleled
	horrors. (See JOSEPHUS,
	<i>Wars of the Jews,</i> 6.2; 12.3,4.) All here predicted was with
	dreadful literally fulfilled.
