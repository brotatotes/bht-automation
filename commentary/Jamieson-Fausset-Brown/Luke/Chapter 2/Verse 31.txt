<p><b>31, 32. all people</b>—all the
	peoples, mankind at large. 
	</p>
<p><b>a light to the Gentiles</b>—then
	in thick darkness. 
	</p>
<p><b>glory of thy people
	Israel</b>—already Thine, and now, in the believing portion of it,
	to be so more gloriously than ever. It will be observed that this
	"swan-like song, bidding an eternal farewell to this terrestrial
	life" [OLSHAUSEN],
	takes a more comprehensive view of the kingdom of Christ than that of
	Zacharias, though the kingdom they sing of is one.</p>
