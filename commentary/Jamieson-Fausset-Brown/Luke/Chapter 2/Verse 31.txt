31, 32. all people—all the
	peoples, mankind at large. 
	a light to the Gentiles—then
	in thick darkness. 
	glory of thy people
	Israel—already Thine, and now, in the believing portion of it,
	to be so more gloriously than ever. It will be observed that this
	"swan-like song, bidding an eternal farewell to this terrestrial
	life" [OLSHAUSEN],
	takes a more comprehensive view of the kingdom of Christ than that of
	Zacharias, though the kingdom they sing of is one.
