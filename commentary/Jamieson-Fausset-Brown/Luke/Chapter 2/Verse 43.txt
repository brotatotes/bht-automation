<p><b>43. as they returned</b>—If the
	duties of life must give place to worship, worship, in its turn, must
	give place to them. <i>Jerusalem</i> is good, but <i>Nazareth</i> is
	good, too; let him who neglects the one, on pretext of attending to
	the other, ponder this scene. 
	</p>
<p><b>tarried behind . . . Joseph
	and his mother knew not</b>—Accustomed to the discretion and
	obedience of the lad [OLSHAUSEN],
	they might be thrown off their guard.</p>
