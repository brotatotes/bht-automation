<p><b>13. my beloved son</b>—Mark (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>) still more affectingly, "Having yet therefore one son,
	his well-beloved"; our Lord thus severing Himself from all
	merely <i>human</i> messengers, and claiming <i>Sonship</i> in its
	loftiest sense. (Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>.) 
	</p>
<p><b>it may be</b>—"surely";
	implying the almost unimaginable guilt of <i>not</i> doing so.</p>
