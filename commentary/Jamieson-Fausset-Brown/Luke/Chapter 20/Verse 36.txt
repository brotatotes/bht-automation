<p><b>36. neither . . . die any
	more</b>—Marriage is ordained to perpetuate the human family; but
	as there will be no breaches by death in the future state, this
	ordinance will cease. 
	</p>
<p><b>equal</b>—or "like."
	</p>
<p><b>unto the angels</b>—that
	is, in the <i>immortality</i> of their nature. 
	</p>
<p><b>children of God</b>—not in
	respect of <i>character</i> but <i>nature;</i> "being the
	children of the resurrection" to an undecaying existence
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:21">Romans 8:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:23">Romans 8:23</span>).
	And thus the children of their Father's immortality (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:23- :">Romans 8:23- :</span>).</p>
