30, 31. he took . . . and blessed .
	. . and their eyes were opened—The stranger first startles them
	by taking the place of master at their own table, but on proceeding
	to that act which reproduced the whole scene of the last Supper, a
	rush of associations and recollections disclosed their guest, and He
	stood confessed before their astonished gaze—THEIR
	RISEN LORD! They
	were going to gaze on Him, perhaps embrace Him, but that moment He is
	gone! It was enough.
