<p><b>39-43. Behold,</b> c.—lovingly
	offering them both <i>ocular</i> and <i>tangible</i> demonstration of
	the reality of His resurrection. 
	</p>
<p><b>a spirit hath not</b>—an
	important statement regarding "spirits." 
	</p>
<p><b>flesh and bones</b>—He says
	not "flesh and <i>blood</i>" for the blood is the life of
	the animal and corruptible body (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), which "cannot inherit the kingdom of God" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>); but "flesh and bones," implying the <i>identity,</i>
	but <i>with diversity of laws,</i> of the resurrection body. (See on
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>).</p>
