<p><b>44-49. These are the words,</b>
	&amp;c.—that is, "Now you will understand what seemed so dark
	to you when I told you about the Son of man being put to death and
	rising again" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>while . . . yet with you</b>—a
	striking expression, implying that He was now, as the dead and risen
	Saviour, virtually dissevered from this scene of mortality, and from
	all ordinary intercourse with His mortal disciples. 
	</p>
<p><b>law . . . prophets . . .
	psalms</b>—the three Jewish divisions of the Old Testament
	Scriptures.</p>
