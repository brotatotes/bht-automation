<p><b>26. And when the unclean spirit had
	torn him</b>—Luke (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:35">Luke 4:35</span>)
	says, "When he had thrown him in the midst." Malignant
	cruelty—just showing what he <i>would</i> have done, if permitted
	to go farther: it was a last fling! 
	</p>
<p><b>and cried with a loud
	voice</b>—the voice of enforced submission and despair. 
	</p>
<p><b>he came out of him</b>—Luke
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:35">Luke 4:35</span>) adds, "and hurt
	him not." Thus impotent were the malignity and rage of the
	impure spirit when under the restraint of "the Stronger than the
	strong one armed" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:21">Luke 11:21</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:22">Luke 11:22</span>).</p>
