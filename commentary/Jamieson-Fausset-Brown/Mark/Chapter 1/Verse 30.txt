<p><b>30. But Simon's wife's mother lay
	sick of a fever</b>—Luke, as was natural in "the beloved
	<i>physician</i>" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+4:14">Colossians 4:14</span>),
	describes it professionally; calling it a "great fever,"
	and thus distinguishing it from that lighter kind which the Greek
	physicians were wont to call "small fevers," as GALEN,
	quoted by WETSTEIN, tells
	us. 
	</p>
<p><b>and anon</b>—immediately. 
	</p>
<p><b>they tell him of
	her</b>—naturally hoping that His compassion and power towards one
	of His own disciples would not be less signally displayed than
	towards the demonized stranger in the synagogue.</p>
