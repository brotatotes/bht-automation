<p><b>34. And he healed many that were
	sick of divers diseases, and cast out many devils</b>—In <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span> it is said, "He cast out the spirits with His word";
	or rather, "with a word"—a word of command. 
	</p>
<p><b>and suffered not the devils
	to speak, because they knew him</b>—Evidently they <i>would</i>
	have spoken, if permitted, proclaiming His Messiahship in such terms
	as in the synagogue; but once in one day, and that testimony
	immediately silenced, was enough. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. After this account of His miracles of healing, we have in
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:17">Matthew 8:17</span> this pregnant
	quotation, "That it might be fulfilled which was spoken by
	Esaias the prophet, saying (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:17- :">Matthew 8:17- :</span>), Himself took our infirmities, and bare our sicknesses."</p>
