<p><b>13. And they send unto him certain
	of the Pharisees</b>—"their disciples," says Matthew (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>); probably young and zealous scholars in that hardening
	school. 
	</p>
<p><b>and of the Herodians</b>—(See
	on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). In <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span> these willing tools are called "spies, which should
	feign themselves just [righteous] men, that they might take hold of
	His words, that so they might deliver Him unto the power and
	authority of the governor." Their plan, then, was to entrap Him
	into some expression which might be construed into disaffection to
	the Roman government; the Pharisees themselves being notoriously
	discontented with the Roman yoke. 
	</p>
