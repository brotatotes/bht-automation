<p><b>68. But he denied</b>—"before
	all" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:70">Matthew 26:70</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>saying, I know not, neither
	understand I what thou sayest</b>—in Luke (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:70- :">Matthew 26:70- :</span>), "I know Him not." 
	</p>
<p><b>And he went out into the
	porch</b>—the vestibule leading to the street—no doubt finding
	the fire-place too <i>hot</i> for him; possibly also with the hope of
	escaping—but that was not to be, and perhaps he dreaded that, too.
	Doubtless by this time his mind would be getting into a sea of
	commotion, and would fluctuate every moment in its resolves. 
	</p>
<p><b>AND THE COCK CREW</b>—(See
	on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:70- :">Matthew 26:70- :</span>). This, then, was the
	First Denial. 
	</p>

	</p>
