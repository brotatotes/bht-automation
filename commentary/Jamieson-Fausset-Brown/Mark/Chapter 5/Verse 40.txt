<p><b>40. And they laughed him to
	scorn</b>—rather, simply, "laughed at Him"—"knowing
	that she was dead" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:53">Luke 8:53</span>);
	an important testimony this to the reality of her death. 
	</p>
<p><b>But when he had put them all
	out</b>—The word is strong—"turned them all out";
	meaning all those who were making this noise, and any others that may
	have been there from sympathy, that only those might be present who
	were most nearly concerned, and those whom He had Himself brought as
	witnesses of the great act about to be done. 
	</p>
<p><b>he taketh the father and the
	mother of the damsel, and them that were with him</b>—Peter, and
	James, and John. 
	</p>
<p><b>and entereth in where the
	damsel was lying.</b></p>
