<p><b>21. And when a convenient day</b>—for
	the purposes of Herodias. 
	</p>
<p><b>was come, that Herod</b>—rather,
	"A convenient day being come, when Herod." 
	</p>

	supper to his lords, high captains, and chief </b><i><b>estates</b></i><b>
	of Galilee</b>—This graphic minuteness of detail adds much to the
