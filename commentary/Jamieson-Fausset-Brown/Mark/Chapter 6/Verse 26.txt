26. And the king was exceeding
	sorry—With his feelings regarding John, and the truths which so
	told upon his conscience from that preacher's lips, and after so
	often and carefully saving him from his paramour's rage, it must have
	been very galling to find himself at length entrapped by his own rash
	folly. 
	yet for his oath's sake—See
	how men of no principle, but troublesome conscience, will stick at
	breaking a rash oath, while yielding to the commission of the worst
	crimes! 
	and for their sakes which sat
	with him—under the influence of that false shame, which could
	not brook being thought to be troubled with religious or moral
	scruples. To how many has this proved a fatal snare! 
	he would not reject her.
