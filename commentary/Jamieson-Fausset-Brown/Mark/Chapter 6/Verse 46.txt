<p><b>46. And when he had sent them away,
	he departed into a mountain to pray</b>—thus at length getting that
	privacy and rest which He had vainly sought during the earlier part
	of the day; opportunity also to pour out His soul in connection with
	the extraordinary excitement in His favor that evening—which
	appears to have marked the zenith of His reputation, for it began to
	decline the very next day; and a place whence He might watch the
	disciples on the lake, pray for them in their extremity, and observe
	the right time for coming to them, in a new manifestation of His
	glory, on the sea.</p>
