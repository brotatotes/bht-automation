<p><b>32. But they understood not that
	saying</b>—"and it was hid from them, [so] that they preceived
	it not" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:45">Luke 9:45</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>and were afraid to ask
	him</b>—Their most cherished ideas were so completely dashed by
	such announcements, that they were afraid of laying themselves open
	to rebuke by asking Him any questions. But "they were exceeding
	sorry" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:23">Matthew 17:23</span>). While
	the other Evangelists, as WEBSTER
	and WILKINSON remark,
	notice their ignorance and their fear, Matthew, who was one of them,
	retains a vivid recollection of their sorrow. 
	</p>
