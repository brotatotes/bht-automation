<p><b>50. Salt is good; but if the salt
	have lost his saltness</b>—its power to season what it is brought
	into contact with. 
	</p>
<p><b>wherewith will ye season
	it?</b>—How is this property to be restored? See on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>Have salt in yourselves</b>—See
	to it that ye retain in yourselves those precious qualities that will
	make you a blessing to one another, and to all around you. 
	</p>
<p><b>and</b>—with respect to the
	miserable strife out of which all this discourse has sprung, in one
	concluding word. 
	</p>
<p><b>have peace one with
	another</b>—This is repeated in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>.</p>
