<p><b>12. And after they were brought to
	Babylon</b>—after the migration of Babylon. 
	</p>
<p><b>Jechonias begat Salathiel</b>—So
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ch+3:17">1 Chronicles 3:17</span>. Nor does this
	contradict <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jer+22:30">Jeremiah 22:30</span>, "Thus
	saith the Lord, Write ye this man (Coniah, or Jeconiah) childless";
	for what follows explains in what sense this was meant—"for no
	man of his seed shall prosper, sitting upon the throne of David."
	He <i>was</i> to have seed, but no <i>reigning</i> child. 
	</p>
<p><b>and Salathiel</b>—or
	Shealtiel. 
	</p>
<p><b>begat Zorobabel</b>—So
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ezr+3:2">Ezra 3:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ne+12:1">Nehemiah 12:1</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="hag+1:1">Haggai 1:1</span>. But it would appear from
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ch+3:19">1 Chronicles 3:19</span> that Zerubbabel was
	Salathiel's grandson, being the son of Pedaiah, whose name, for some
	reason unknown, is omitted.</p>
