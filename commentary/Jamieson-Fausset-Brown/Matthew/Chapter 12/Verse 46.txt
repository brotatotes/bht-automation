46. While he yet talked to the
	people, behold, his mother and his brethren—(See on  :-). 
	stood without, desiring to
	speak with him—"and could not come at Him for the press"
	(Luke 8:19). For what purpose
	these came, we learn from Mark 3:20;
	Mark 3:21. In His zeal and ardor
	He seemed indifferent both to food and repose, and "they went to
	lay hold of Him" as one "beside Himself." Mark (Mark 3:21- :) says graphically, "And the multitude sat about Him"—or
	"around Him."
