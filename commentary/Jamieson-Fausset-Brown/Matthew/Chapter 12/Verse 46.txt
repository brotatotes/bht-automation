<p><b>stood without, desiring to
	speak with him</b>—"and could not come at Him for the press"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:19">Luke 8:19</span>). For what purpose
	these came, we learn from <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:20">Mark 3:20</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:21">Mark 3:21</span>. In His zeal and ardor
	He seemed indifferent both to food and repose, and "they went to
	lay hold of Him" as one "beside Himself." Mark (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:21- :">Mark 3:21- :</span>) says graphically, "And the multitude sat about Him"—or
	"around Him."</p>
