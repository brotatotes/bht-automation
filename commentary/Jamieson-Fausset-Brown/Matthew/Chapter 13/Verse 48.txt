<p><b>48. Which, when it was full, they
	drew to shore</b>—for the separation will not be made till the
	number of the elect is accomplished. 
	</p>
<p><b>and sat down</b>—expressing
	the deliberateness with which the judicial separation will at length
	be made. 
	</p>
<p><b>and gathered the good into
	vessels, but cast the bad away</b>—literally, "the rotten,"
	but here meaning, "the foul" or "worthless" fish:
	corresponding to the "tares" of the other parable.</p>
