<p><b>52. Then said he unto them,
	Therefore</b>—or as we should say, "Well, then." 
	</p>
<p><b>every scribe</b>—or
	Christian teacher: here so called from that well-known class among
	the Jews. (See <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:34">Matthew 23:34</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>which is instructed unto the
	kingdom of heaven</b>—himself taught in the mysteries of the Gospel
	which he has to teach to others. 
	</p>
<p><b>is like unto a man that is an
	householder which bringeth forth</b>—"turneth" or
	"dealeth out." 
	</p>
<p><b>out of his treasure</b>—his
	store of divine truth. 
	</p>
<p><b>things new and old</b>—old
	truths in ever new forms, aspects, applications, and with ever new
	illustrations. 
	</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:34- :">Matthew 23:34- :</span>. HOW JESUS
	WAS REGARDED
	BY HIS RELATIVES.
