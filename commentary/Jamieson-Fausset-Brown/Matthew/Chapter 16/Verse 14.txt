<p><b>14. And they said, Some say that
	thou art John the Baptist</b>—risen from the dead. So that Herod
	Antipas was not singular in his surmise (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:1">Matthew 14:1</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:2">Matthew 14:2</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>some, Elias</b>—(Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:2- :">Matthew 14:2- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>and others, Jeremias</b>—Was
	this theory suggested by a supposed resemblance between the "Man
	of Sorrows" and "the weeping prophet?" 
	</p>
<p><b>or one of the prophets</b>—or,
	as Luke (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:8">Luke 9:8</span>) expresses it,
	"that one of the old prophets is risen again." In another
	report of the popular opinions which Mark (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:8- :">Luke 9:8- :</span>) gives us, it is thus expressed, "That it is a prophet
	[or], as one of the prophets": in other words, That He was a
	prophetical person, resembling those of old.</p>
