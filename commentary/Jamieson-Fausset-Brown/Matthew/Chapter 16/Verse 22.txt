<p><b>22. Then Peter took him</b>—aside,
	apart from the rest; presuming on the distinction just conferred on
	him; showing how <i>unexpected</i> and <i>distasteful</i> to them all
	was the announcement. 
	</p>
<p><b>and began to rebuke
	him</b>—affectionately, yet with a certain generous indignation, to
	chide Him. 
	</p>
<p><b>saying, Be it far from thee:
	this shall not be unto thee</b>—that is, "If I can help it":
	the same spirit that prompted him in the garden to draw the sword in
	His behalf (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:10">John 18:10</span>).</p>
