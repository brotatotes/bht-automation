<p><b>28. But the same servant went out,
	and found one of his fellow servants</b>—Mark the difference here.
	The first case is that of master and servant; in this case, both are
	on a footing of equality. (See <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, below.) 
	</p>
<p><b>which owed him an hundred
	pence</b>—If Jewish money is intended, this debt was to the other
	less than <i>one to a million.</i>
</p>
<p><b>and he laid hands on him, and
	took him by the throat</b>—he seized and throttled him. 
	</p>
<p><b>saying, Pay me that thou
	owest</b>—Mark the mercilessness even of the tone.</p>
