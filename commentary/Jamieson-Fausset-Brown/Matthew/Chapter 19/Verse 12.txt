<p><b>12. For there are some eunuchs which
	were so born from their mother's womb</b>—persons constitutionally
	either incapable of or indisposed to marriage. 
	</p>
<p><b>and there are some eunuchs
	which were made eunuchs of men</b>—persons rendered incapable by
	others. 
	</p>
<p><b>and there be eunuchs which
	have made themselves eunuchs for the kingdom of heaven's sake</b>—persons
	who, to do God's work better, deliberately choose this state. Such
	was Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:7">1 Corinthians 7:7</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>He that is able to receive
	it, let him receive it</b>—"He who feels this to be his proper
	vocation, let him embrace it"; which, of course, is as much as
	to say—"he only." Thus, all are left free in this matter.
	</p>
