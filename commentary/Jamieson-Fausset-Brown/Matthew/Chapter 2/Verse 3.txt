<p><b>3. When Herod the king had heard
	these things, he was troubled</b>—viewing this as a danger to his
	own throne: perhaps his guilty conscience also suggested other
	grounds of fear. 
	</p>
<p><b>and all Jerusalem with
	him</b>—from a dread of revolutionary commotions, and perhaps also
	of Herod's rage.</p>
