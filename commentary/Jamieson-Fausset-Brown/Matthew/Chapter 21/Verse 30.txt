<p><b>30. And he came to the second, and
	said likewise. And he answered and said, I </b><i><b>go,</b></i><b>
	sir</b>—"I, sir." The emphatic "I," here,
	denotes the self-righteous complacency which says, "God, I thank
	thee that <i>I</i> am not as other men" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>and went not</b>—<i>He</i>
	did not "afterward repent" and refuse to go; for there was
	here no <i>intention</i> to go. It is the class that "say and do
	not" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:3">Matthew 23:3</span>) —a
	falseness more abominable to God, says STIER,
	than any "I will not."</p>
