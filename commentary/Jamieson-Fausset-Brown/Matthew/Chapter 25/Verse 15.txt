<p><b>15. And unto one he gave five
	talents, to another two, and to another one</b>—While the
	<i>proportion of gifts</i> is different in each, the same <i>fidelity</i>
	is required of all, and equally rewarded. And thus there is perfect
	equity. 
	</p>
<p><b>to every man according to his
	several ability</b>—his natural capacity as enlisted in Christ's
	service, and his opportunities in providence for employing the gifts
	bestowed on him. 
	</p>
<p><b>and straightway took his
	journey</b>—Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:33">Matthew 21:33</span>,
	where the same departure is ascribed to God, after setting up the
	ancient economy. In both cases, it denotes the leaving of men to the
	action of all those spiritual laws and influences of Heaven under
	which they have been graciously placed for their own salvation and
	the advancement of their Lord's kingdom.</p>
