<p><b>64. Command therefore that the
	sepulchre be made sure</b>—by a Roman guard. 
	</p>
<p><b>until the third day</b>—after
	which, if He still lay in the grave, the imposture of His claims
	would be manifest to all. 
	</p>
<p><b>and say unto the people, he
	is risen from the dead</b>—Did they really fear this? 
	</p>
<p><b>so the last error shall be
	worse than the first</b>—the imposture of His pretended
	resurrection worse than that of His pretended Messiahship.</p>
