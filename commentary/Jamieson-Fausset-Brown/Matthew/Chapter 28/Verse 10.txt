<p><b>10. Then said Jesus unto them, Be
	not afraid</b>—What dear associations would these familiar
	words—now uttered in a higher style, but by the same Lips—bring
	rushing back to their recollection! 
	</p>
<p><b>go tell my brethren that they
	go into Galilee, and there shall they see me</b>—The brethren here
	meant must have been His brethren after the flesh (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>); for His brethren in the higher sense (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>) had several meetings with Him at Jerusalem <i>before</i>
	He went to Galilee, which they would have missed if they had been the
	persons ordered to Galilee to meet Him. 
	</p>
<p><i>The Guards Bribed</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>

	portion is peculiar to Matthew.</p>
