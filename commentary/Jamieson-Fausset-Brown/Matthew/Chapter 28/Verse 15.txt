<p><b>15. So they took the money, and did
	as they were taught</b>—thus consenting to brand themselves with
	infamy. 
	</p>
<p><b>and this saying is commonly
	reported among the Jews until this day</b>—to the date of the
	publication of this Gospel. The wonder is that so clumsy and
	incredible a story lasted so long. But those who are resolved <i>not</i>
	to come to the light will catch at straws. JUSTIN
	MARTYR, who flourished
	about A.D. 170, says, in
	his <i>Dialogue with Trypho the Jew,</i> that the Jews dispersed the
	story by means of special messengers sent to every country. 
	</p>
