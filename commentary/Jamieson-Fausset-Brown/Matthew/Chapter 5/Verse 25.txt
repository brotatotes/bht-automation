<p><b>25. Agree with thine adversary</b>—thine
	opponent in a matter cognizable by law. 
	</p>
<p><b>quickly, whiles thou art in
	the way with him</b>—"to the magistrate," as in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>lest at any time</b>—here,
	rather, "lest at all," or simply "lest." 
	</p>
<p><b>the adversary deliver thee to
	the judge, and the judge</b>—having pronounced thee in the wrong. 
	</p>
<p><b>deliver thee to the
	officer</b>—the official whose business it is to see the sentence
	carried into effect.</p>
