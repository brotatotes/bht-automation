<p><b>19. Lay not up for ourselves
	treasures upon earth</b>—hoard not. 
	</p>
<p><b>where moth</b>—a
	"clothes-moth." Eastern treasures, consisting partly in
	costly dresses stored up (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), were liable to be consumed by moths (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="job+13:28">Job 13:28</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+50:9">Isaiah 50:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+51:8">Isaiah 51:8</span>).
	In <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:2">James 5:2</span> there is an evident
	reference to our Lord's words here. 
	</p>
<p><b>and rust</b>—any "eating
	into" or "consuming"; here, probably, "wear and
	tear." 
	</p>
<p><b>doth corrupt</b>—cause to
	disappear. By this reference to moth and rust our Lord would teach
	how <i>perishable</i> are such earthly treasures. 
	</p>
<p><b>and where thieves break
	through and steal</b>—Treasures these, how <i>precarious!</i></p>
