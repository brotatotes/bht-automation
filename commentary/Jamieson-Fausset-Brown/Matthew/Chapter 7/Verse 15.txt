<p><b>15. Beware</b>—But beware. 
	</p>
<p><b>of false prophets</b>—that
	is, of teachers coming as authorized expounders of the mind of God
	and guides to heaven. (See <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:29">Acts 20:29</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:30">Acts 20:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:1">2 Peter 2:1</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:2">2 Peter 2:2</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>which come to you in sheep's
	clothing</b>—with a bland, gentle, plausible exterior; persuading
	you that the gate is not strait nor the way narrow, and that to teach
	so is illiberal and bigoted—precisely what the old prophets did
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eze+13:1-10">Ezekiel 13:1-10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eze+13:22">Ezekiel 13:22</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>but inwardly they are
	ravening wolves</b>—bent on devouring the flock for their own ends
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:2">2 Corinthians 11:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:3">2 Corinthians 11:3</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:13-15">2 Corinthians 11:13-15</span>).</p>
