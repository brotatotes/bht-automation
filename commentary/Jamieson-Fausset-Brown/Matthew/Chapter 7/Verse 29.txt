<p><b>29. For he taught them as </b><i><b>one</b></i><b>
	having authority</b>—The word "one," which our
	translators have here inserted, only weakens the statement. 
	</p>
<p><b>and not as the scribes</b>—The
	consciousness of divine authority, as Lawgiver, Expounder and Judge,
	so beamed through His teaching, that the scribes' teaching could not
	but appear drivelling in such a light.</p>
