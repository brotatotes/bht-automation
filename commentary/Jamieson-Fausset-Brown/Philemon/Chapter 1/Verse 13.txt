<p style=" "><b>I</b>
	— emphatical. I for my part. Since <i>I</i>
	had such implicit trust in him as to desire to keep him with me for
	his services, <i>thou</i>
	mayest.</p>
<p style=" "><b>I
	would have retained</b>
	— different <i>Greek</i>
	from the “would,” <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>,
	“I could have <i>wished,</i>”
	“I was <i>minded</i>”
	here; but “I was not <i>willing,</i>”
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>.</p>
<p style=" "><b>in
	thy stead</b>
	— that he might supply in your place all the services to me which
	you, if you were here, would render in virtue of the love you bear to
	me (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>).</p>
<p style=" "><b>bonds
	of the gospel</b>
	— my bonds endured for the Gospel’s sake (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>).</p>
