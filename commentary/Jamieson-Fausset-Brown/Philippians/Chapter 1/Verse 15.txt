<p><b>15.</b> "Some indeed <i>are
	preaching</i> Christ even <i>for</i> envy, that is, to carry out the
	<i>envy</i> which they felt towards Paul, on account of the success
	of the Gospel in the capital of the world, owing to his steadfastness
	in his imprisonment; they wished through envy to transfer the credit
	of its progress from him to themselves. Probably Judaizing teachers
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:1-23">Romans 14:1-23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:10-15">1 Corinthians 3:10-15</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:1">1 Corinthians 9:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:1-4">2 Corinthians 11:1-4</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>some also of</b>—rather,
	"for" 
	</p>
<p><b>good will</b>—answering to
	"the brethren" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:1-47.11.4- :">2 Corinthians 11:1-47.11.4- :</span>); some being <i>well disposed</i> to him.</p>
