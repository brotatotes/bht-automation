<p><b>28. terrified</b>—literally, said
	of horses or other animals startled or suddenly scared; so of sudden
	<i>consternation</i> in general. 
	</p>
<p><b>which</b>—your not being
	terrified. 
	</p>
<p><b>evident token of perdition</b>—if
	they would only perceive it (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). It attests this, that in contending hopelessly against you,
	they are only rushing on to their own perdition, not shaking your
	united faith and constancy. 
	</p>
<p><b>to you of salvation</b>—The
	oldest manuscripts read, "of <i>your</i> salvation"; not
	merely <i>your temporal safety.</i></p>
