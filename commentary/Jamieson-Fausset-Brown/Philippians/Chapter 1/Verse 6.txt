<p><b>6. confident</b>—This confidence
	nerves prayers and thanksgivings (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+1:3">Philippians 1:3</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+1:4">Philippians 1:4</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>this very thing</b>—<i>the
	very thing</i> which he prays for (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+1:4- :">Philippians 1:4- :</span>) is the matter of his believing confidence (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:24">Mark 11:24</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:14">1 John 5:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:15">1 John 5:15</span>).
	Hence the result is sure. 
	</p>
<p><b>he which hath begun</b>—God
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+2:13">Philippians 2:13</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>a good work</b>—Any work
	that God begins, He will surely finish (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+2:13- :">Philippians 2:13- :</span>). Not even men begin a work at random. Much more the fact of
	His beginning the work is a pledge of its completion (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+2:13- :">Philippians 2:13- :</span>). So as to the particular work here meant, the <i>perfecting
	of their fellowship in the Gospel</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+1:5">Philippians 1:5</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+37:24">Psalms 37:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+89:33">Psalms 89:33</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+138:8">Psalms 138:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:28">John 10:28</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:29">John 10:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:29">Romans 8:29</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:35-39">Romans 8:35-39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:1">Romans 11:1</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:2">Romans 11:2</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:17-19">Hebrews 6:17-19</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:17">James 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jdg+1:24">Judges 1:24</span>).
	As God cast not off Israel for ever, though chastening them for a
	time, so He will not cast off the spiritual Israel (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="de+33:3">Deuteronomy 33:3</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+27:3">Isaiah 27:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:5">1 Peter 1:5</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>perform it until</b>—"perfect
	it up to" [ALFORD,
	ELLICOTT, and others]. 
	</p>
<p><b>the day of . . . Christ</b>—
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+1:10">Philippians 1:10</span>). The Lord's coming,
	designed by God in every age of the Church to be regarded as near, is
	to be the goal set before believers' minds rather than their own
	death.</p>
