<p><b>10. at the name</b>—rather as
	<i>Greek,</i> "in the name." 
	</p>
<p><b>bow</b>—rather, "bend,"
	in token of worship. Referring to <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>; quoted also in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. To worship "in the name of Jesus," is to worship
	Jesus <i>Himself</i> (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+2:11">Philippians 2:11</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="pr+18:10">Proverbs 18:10</span>), or <i>God in Christ</i>
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:23">John 16:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:14">Ephesians 3:14</span>).
	Compare "Whosoever shall call upon <i>the name of the Lord</i>
	(that is, whosoever shall call on <i>the Lord in His revealed
	character</i>) shall be saved" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:13">Romans 10:13</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:2">1 Corinthians 1:2</span>); "all that call
	upon <i>the name of Jesus Christ our Lord</i>" (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:2- :">1 Corinthians 1:2- :</span>); "call on the Lord"; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:2- :">1 Corinthians 1:2- :</span>, "calling upon . . . and saying, Lord Jesus"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:14">Acts 9:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:21">Acts 9:21</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:16">Acts 22:16</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>of things in heaven</b>—angels.
	They worship Him not only as God, but as the ascended <i>God-man,</i>
	"Jesus" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:21">Ephesians 1:21</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:6">Hebrews 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:22">1 Peter 3:22</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>in earth</b>—men; among
	whom He tabernacled for a time. 
	</p>
<p><b>under the earth</b>—the
	dead; among whom He was numbered once (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:9">Romans 14:9</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:11">Romans 14:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:9">Ephesians 4:9</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:10">Ephesians 4:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+5:13">Revelation 5:13</span>).
	The demons and the lost may be included <i>indirectly,</i> as even
	they give homage, though one of fear, not love, to Jesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:11">Mark 3:11</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:31">Luke 8:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:19">James 2:19</span>,
	see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+2:11">Philippians 2:11</span>).</p>
