<p><b>22.</b> Rare praise (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>as a son with the
	father</b>—Translate, "as a <i>child (serveth) a father.</i>"
	</p>
<p><b>served with me</b>—When we
	might expect the sentence to run thus. "As a child <i>serveth a
	father,</i> so he <i>served me</i>"; he changes it to "served
	<i>with</i> me" in modesty; as Christians are not <i>servants</i>
	TO <i>one another,</i>"
	but <i>servants of God</i> WITH
	<i>one another</i> (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>in the gospel</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"<i>unto,</i>" or "<i>for</i> the Gospel."</p>
