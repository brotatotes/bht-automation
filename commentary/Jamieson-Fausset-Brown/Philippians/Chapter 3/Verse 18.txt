<p><b>18. many walk</b>—in such a
	manner. Follow not evildoers, because they are "many" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). Their numbers are rather a presumption against their being
	Christ's "little flock" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>often</b>—There is need of
	constant warning. 
	</p>
<p><b>weeping</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). A hard tone in speaking of the inconsistencies of
	professors is the very opposite of Paul's spirit, and David's (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), and Jeremiah's (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). The Lord and His apostles, at the same time, speak more
	strongly against empty professors (as the Pharisees), than against
	open scoffers. 
	</p>
<p><b>enemies of the cross of
	Christ</b>—in their <i>practice,</i> not in doctrine (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:14">Galatians 6:14</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:6">Hebrews 6:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:29">Hebrews 10:29</span>).</p>
