<p><b>10. But</b>—transitional
	conjunction. But "now" to pass to another subject. 
	</p>
<p><b>in the Lord</b>—He views
	everything with reference to Christ. 
	</p>
<p><b>at the last</b>—"at
	last"; implying he was expecting their gift, not from a selfish
	view, but as a "fruit" of their faith, and to "abound"
	to their account (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:11">Philippians 4:11</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:17">Philippians 4:17</span>). Though long in coming,
	owing to Epaphroditus' sickness and other delays, he does not imply
	their gift was too late. 
	</p>
<p><b>your care . . . hath
	flourished again</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "Ye have flourished again
	(<i>revived,</i> as trees sprouting forth <i>again</i> in spring) in
	your care <i>for</i> me." 
	</p>
<p><b>wherein ye were also
	careful</b>—in respect to which (<i>revival,</i> namely, the
	sending of a supply to me) "ye were also (all along) careful,
	but ye lacked opportunity"; whether from want of means or want
	of a messenger. Your "lack of service" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:17- :">Philippians 4:17- :</span>), was owing to your having "lacked opportunity."</p>
