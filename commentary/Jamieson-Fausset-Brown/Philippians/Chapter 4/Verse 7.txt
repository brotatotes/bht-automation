<p><b>7. And</b>—The inseparable
	consequence of thus laying everything before God in "prayer with
	thanksgiving." 
	</p>
<p><b>peace</b>—the dispeller of
	"anxious care" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:6">Philippians 4:6</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>of God</b>—coming from God,
	and resting in God (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:27">John 14:27</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:33">John 16:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="col+3:15">Colossians 3:15</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>passeth</b>—<i>surpasseth,</i>
	or <i>exceedeth,</i> all man's notional powers of understanding its
	full blessedness (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:9">1 Corinthians 2:9</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:10">1 Corinthians 2:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:20">Ephesians 3:20</span>;
	compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="pr+3:17">Proverbs 3:17</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>shall keep</b>—rather,
	"shall <i>guard</i>"; shall keep as a well-garrisoned
	stronghold (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+26:1">Isaiah 26:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+26:3">Isaiah 26:3</span>).
	The same <i>Greek</i> verb is used in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+26:3- :">Isaiah 26:3- :</span>. There shall be peace secure within, whatever outward
	troubles may besiege. 
	</p>
<p><b>hearts and minds</b>—rather,
	"hearts (the <i>seat</i> of the thoughts) and <i>thoughts</i>"
	or purposes. 
	</p>
<p><b>through</b>—rather as
	<i>Greek,</i> "<i>in</i> Christ Jesus." It is in Christ
	that we are "kept" or "guarded" secure.</p>
