<p><b>9. both</b>—rather, "The
	things <i>also</i> which ye have learned . . . these <i>practice</i>";
	the things which besides recommending them in words, have been <i>also</i>
	recommended <i>by my example,</i> carry into practice. 
	</p>
<p><b>heard</b>—though ye have
	not yet sufficiently "received" them. 
	</p>
<p><b>seen</b>—though ye have not
	as yet sufficiently "learned" them [BENGEL].
	</p>
<p><b>and</b>—"and then,"
	as the necessary result (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+4:7">Philippians 4:7</span>).
	Not only "the peace of God," but "the God of peace"
	Himself "shall be with you."</p>
