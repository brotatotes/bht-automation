<p><b>14.</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "But,"
	or "And." 
	</p>
<p><b>like wool</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"like <i>white</i> wool." The <i>color</i> is the point of
	comparison; signifying <i>purity</i> and glory. (So in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). Not <i>age,</i> for hoary hairs are the sign of decay. 
	</p>
<p><b>eyes . . . as . . .
	flame</b>—all-searching and penetrating like fire: at the same
	time, also, implying <i>consuming</i> indignation against sin,
	especially at His coming "in flaming fire, taking vengeance"
	on all the ungodly, which is confirmed as the meaning here, by
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+19:11">Revelation 19:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+19:12">Revelation 19:12</span>.</p>
