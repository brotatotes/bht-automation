<p><b>1. another mighty angel</b>—as
	distinguished from the <i>mighty angel</i> who asked as to the former
	and more comprehensive book (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), "Who is worthy to open the book?" 
	</p>
<p><b>clothed with a cloud</b>—the
	emblem of God coming in judgment. 
	</p>
<p><b>a</b>—A, B, C, and <i>Aleph</i>
	read "the"; referring to (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>) <i>the rainbow</i> already mentioned. 
	</p>
<p><b>rainbow upon his head</b>—the
	emblem of covenant mercy to God's people, amidst judgments on God's
	foes. Resumed from <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+4:3">Revelation 4:3</span> (see
	on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+4:3">Revelation 4:3</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>face as . . . the sun</b>—
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+1:16">Revelation 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+18:1">Revelation 18:1</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>feet as pillars of fire</b>—
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+1:15">Revelation 1:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eze+1:7">Ezekiel 1:7</span>).
	The angel, as representative of Christ, reflects His glory and bears
	the insignia attributed in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+1:15">Revelation 1:15</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+1:16">Revelation 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+4:3">Revelation 4:3</span>,
	to Christ Himself. The <i>pillar of fire</i> by night led Israel
	through the wilderness, and was the symbol of God's presence.</p>
