<p><b>18. the nations were angry</b>—alluding
	to <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ps+99:1">Psalms 99:1</span>, <i>Septuagint,</i>
	"The Lord is become King: let the peoples become <i>angry.</i>"
	Their anger is combined with <i>alarm</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ex+15:14">Exodus 15:14</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ki+19:28">2 Kings 19:28</span>, "thy <i>rage
	against Me</i> is come up into Mine ears, I will put My hook in thy
	nose," c.). Translate, as the <i>Greek</i> is the same. "The
	nations were <i>angered,</i> and Thy <i>anger</i> is come." How
	petty man's impotent <i>anger,</i> standing here side by side with
	that of the omnipotent God! 
	</p>
<p><b>dead . . . be judged</b>—proving
	that this seventh trumpet is at the end of all things, when the
	judgment on Christ's foes and the reward of His saints, long prayed
	for by His saints, shall take place. 
	</p>
<p><b>the prophets</b>—as, for
	instance, the two <i>prophesying witnesses</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ki+19:28- :">2 Kings 19:28- :</span>), and those who have showed them kindness for Christ's sake.
	Jesus shall come to effect by His presence that which we have looked
	for long, but vainly, in His absence, and by other means. 
	</p>
<p><b>destroy them which destroy
	the earth</b>—Retribution in kind (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+16:6">Revelation 16:6</span>
<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:27">Luke 19:27</span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:27- :">Luke 19:27- :</span>.</p>
