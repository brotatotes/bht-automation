<p><b>14. deceiveth them that dwell on the
	earth</b>—the earthly-minded, but not <i>the elect.</i> Even a
	miracle is not enough to warrant belief in a professed revelation
	unless that revelation be in harmony with God's already revealed
	will. 
	</p>
<p><b>by the means of those
	miracles</b>—rather as <i>Greek,</i> "<i>on account of</i>
	(because of; in consequence of) those miracles." 
	</p>
<p><b>which he had power to
	do</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "which were given him to do." 
	</p>
<p><b>in the sight of the
	beast</b>—"before him" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>which</b>—A, B, and C read,
	"who"; marking, perhaps, a personal Antichrist. 
	</p>
<p><b>had</b>—So B and ANDREAS
	read. But A, C, and <i>Vulgate</i> read, "hath."</p>
