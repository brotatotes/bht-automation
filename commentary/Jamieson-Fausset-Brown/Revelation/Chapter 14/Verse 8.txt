<p><b>8. another</b>—So <i>Vulgate.</i>
	But A, B, <i>Syriac,</i> and ANDREAS
	add, "a second"; "another, a second angel." 
	</p>
<p><b>Babylon</b>—here first
	mentioned; identical with <i>the harlot,</i> the apostate Church;
	distinct from <i>the beast,</i> and judged separately. 
	</p>
<p><b>is fallen</b>—anticipation
	of <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+18:2">Revelation 18:2</span>. A, <i>Vulgate,
	Syriac,</i> and ANDREAS
	support the second "is fallen." But B, C, and <i>Coptic</i>
	omit it. 
	</p>
<p><b>that great city</b>—A, B,
	C, <i>Vulgate, Syriac,</i> and <i>Coptic</i> omit "city."
	Then translate, "Babylon the great." The ulterior and
	exhaustive fulfilment of <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+21:9">Isaiah 21:9</span>.
	</p>
<p><b>because</b>—So ANDREAS.
	But A, C, <i>Vulgate,</i> and <i>Syriac</i> read, "which."
	B and <i>Coptic</i> omit it. Even reading "which," we must
	understand it as giving the <i>reason</i> of her fall. 
	</p>
<p><b>all nations</b>—A, B and C
	read, "all <i>the</i> nations." 
	</p>
<p><b>the wine of the wrath of her
	fornication</b>—<i>the wine of the wrath</i> of God, the
	consequence <i>of her fornication.</i> As she made the nations drunk
	with the wine of her fornication, so she herself shall be made drunk
	with the wine of God's wrath.</p>
