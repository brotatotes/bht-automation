<p><b>6. having</b>—So B reads. But A
	and C, read "who have": not that they had them yet (compare
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+15:7">Revelation 15:7</span>), but they are by
	anticipation described according to their office. 
	</p>
<p><b>linen</b>—So B reads. But
	A, C, and <i>Vulgate,</i> "a stone." On the principle that
	the harder reading is the one least likely to be an interpolation, we
	should read, "a stone pure ('and' is omitted in A, B, C, and
	ANDREAS), brilliant"
	(so the <i>Greek</i>): probably the diamond. With <i>English Version,</i>
	compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:10">Acts 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:30">Acts 10:30</span>.
	</p>
<p><b>golden girdles</b>—resembling
	the Lord in this respect (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+1:13">Revelation 1:13</span>).</p>
