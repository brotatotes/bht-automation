<p><b>2. went</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "went
	away." 
	</p>
<p><b>poured out</b>—So the angel
	cast fire into the earth previous to the series of trumpets (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>upon</b>—so <i>Coptic.</i>
	But A, B, C, <i>Vulgate,</i> and <i>Syriac</i> read, "into."
	</p>
<p><b>noisome</b>—literally,
	"evil" (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="de+28:27">Deuteronomy 28:27</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="de+28:35">Deuteronomy 28:35</span>). The very same <i>Greek</i>
	word is used in the <i>Septuagint</i> as here, <i>Greek,</i>
	"<i>helkos.</i>" The reason why the sixth Egyptian plague
	is the <i>first</i> here is because it was directed against the
	Egyptian magicians, Jannes and Jambres, so that they could not stand
	before Moses; and so here the plague is sent upon those who in the
	beast worship had practiced sorcery. As they submitted to the mark of
	the beast, so they must bear the mark of the avenging God. Contrast
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+7:3">Revelation 7:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eze+9:4">Ezekiel 9:4</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eze+9:6">Ezekiel 9:6</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>grievous</b>—distressing to
	the sufferers. 
	</p>
<p><b>sore upon the men</b>—antitype
	to the sixth Egyptian plague. 
	</p>
<p><b>which had the mark of the
	beast</b>—Therefore this first vial is subsequent to the period of
	the beast's rule.</p>
