<p><b>5. angel of the waters</b>—that
	is, presiding over the waters. 
	</p>
<p><b>O Lord</b>—omitted by A, B,
	C, <i>Vulgate, Syriac, Coptic,</i> and ANDREAS.
	</p>
<p><b>and shalt be</b>—A, B, C,
	<i>Vulgate,</i> and ANDREAS
	for this clause read, "(which art and wast) <i>holy.</i>"
	The Lord is now no longer He that <i>shall</i> come, for He <i>is
	come</i> in vengeance and therefore the third of the three clauses
	found in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+1:4">Revelation 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+1:8">Revelation 1:8</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+4:8">Revelation 4:8</span> is here and in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+4:8- :">Revelation 4:8- :</span> omitted. 
	</p>
<p><b>judged thus</b>—literally,
	"these things." "Thou didst inflict this judgment."</p>
