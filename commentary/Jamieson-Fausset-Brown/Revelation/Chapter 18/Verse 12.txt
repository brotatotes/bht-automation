<p><b>12.</b> (See on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>stones . . . pearls</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"stone . . . pearl." 
	</p>
<p><b>fine linen</b>—A, B, and C
	read <i>Greek,</i> "<i>bussinou</i>" for "<i>bussou,</i>"
	that is, "fine linen manufacture" [ALFORD].
	The manufacture for which <i>Egypt</i> (the type of the apostate
	Church, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+11:8">Revelation 11:8</span>) was famed.
	Contrast "the fine linen" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+11:8- :">Revelation 11:8- :</span>) put on Israel, and on the New Testament Church (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+11:8- :">Revelation 11:8- :</span>), the Bride, by God (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+11:8- :">Revelation 11:8- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>thyine wood</b>—the <i>citrus</i>
	of the Romans: probably the <i>cypressus thyoyides,</i> or the <i>thuia
	articulata.</i> "Citron wood" [ALFORD].
	A sweet-smelling tree of Cyrene in Lybia, used for incense. 
	</p>
<p><b>all manner vessels</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"every vessel," or "furniture."</p>
