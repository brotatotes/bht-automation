	fine linen—A, B, and C
	read Greek, "bussinou" for "bussou,"
	that is, "fine linen manufacture" [ALFORD].
	The manufacture for which Egypt (the type of the apostate
	Church, Revelation 11:8) was famed.
	Contrast "the fine linen" (Revelation 11:8- :) put on Israel, and on the New Testament Church (Revelation 11:8- :), the Bride, by God (Revelation 11:8- :). 
	thyine wood—the citrus
	of the Romans: probably the cypressus thyoyides, or the thuia
	articulata. "Citron wood" [ALFORD].
	A sweet-smelling tree of Cyrene in Lybia, used for incense. 
	all manner vessels—Greek,
	"every vessel," or "furniture."
