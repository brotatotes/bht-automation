<p><b>14.</b> Direct address to Babylon. 
	</p>
<p><b>the fruits that thy soul
	lusted after</b>—<i>Greek,</i> "thy autumn-ripe fruits of the
	lust (eager desire) of the soul." 
	</p>
<p><b>dainty</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"fat": "sumptuous" in food. 
	</p>
<p><b>goodly</b>—"splendid,"
	"bright," in dress and equipage. 
	</p>
<p><b>departed</b>—supported by
	none of our manuscripts. But A, B, C, <i>Vulgate, Syriac,</i> and
	<i>Coptic</i> read, "perished." 
	</p>
<p><b>thou shalt</b>—A, C,
	<i>Vulgate,</i> and <i>Syriac</i> read, "<i>They</i> (men) <i>shall</i>
	no more find them at all."</p>
