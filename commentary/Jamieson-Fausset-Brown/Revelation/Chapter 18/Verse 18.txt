18. when they saw—Greek,
	"horontes." But A, B, C, and ANDREAS
	read, Greek, "blepontes," "looking at."
	Greek, "blepo," is to use the eyes, to
	look: the act of seeing without thought of the object seen.
	Greek, "horao," refers to the thing seen
	or presented to the eyes [TITTMANN].
	smoke—so B, C. But A
	reads "place."

	due, by His servants ( :-). MARTIAL says
