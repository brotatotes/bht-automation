<p><b>2. mightily . . . strong</b>—not
	supported by manuscripts. But A, B, <i>Vulgate, Syriac,</i> and
	<i>Coptic</i> read, "with (literally, 'in') a mighty voice."
	</p>
<p><b>is fallen, is fallen</b>—so
	A, <i>Vulgate, Syriac,</i> and ANDREAS.
	But B and <i>Coptic</i> omit the second "is fallen"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+21:9">Isaiah 21:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jer+51:8">Jeremiah 51:8</span>).
	This phrase is here prophetical of her fall, still future, as <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jer+51:8- :">Jeremiah 51:8- :</span> proves. 
	</p>
<p><b>devils</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"demons." 
	</p>
<p><b>the hold</b>—a keep or
	prison.</p>
