<p><b>23. her children</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="isa+57:3">Isaiah 57:3</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eze+23:45">Ezekiel 23:45</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eze+23:47">Ezekiel 23:47</span>).
	Her proper adherents; not those who <i>suffer</i> her, but those who
	are begotten of her. A distinct class from the last in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eze+23:47- :">Ezekiel 23:47- :</span> (compare <i>Note,</i> see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eze+23:47- :">Ezekiel 23:47- :</span>), whose sin was less direct, being that only of connivance. 
	</p>
<p><b>kill . . . with death</b>—Compare
	the disaster that overtook the literal Jezebel's votaries of Baal,
	and Ahab's sons, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ki+18:40">1 Kings 18:40</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ki+10:6">2 Kings 10:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ki+10:7">2 Kings 10:7</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ki+10:24">2 Kings 10:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ki+10:25">2 Kings 10:25</span>.
	<i>Kill with death</i> is a Hebraism for <i>slay with most sure and
	awful death;</i> so "dying thou shalt die" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ki+10:25- :">2 Kings 10:25- :</span>). Not "die the common death of men" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ki+10:25- :">2 Kings 10:25- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>all the churches shall
	know</b>—implying that these addresses are designed for the
	catholic Church of all ages and places. So palpably shall God's hand
	be seen in the judgment on Thyatira, that the whole Church shall
	recognize it as God's doing. 
	</p>
<p><b>I am he</b>—the "I"
	is strongly emphatical: "that it is <i>I</i> am He who," c.
	</p>
<p><b>searcheth . . . hearts</b>—God's
	peculiar attribute is given to Christ. The "reins" are the
	seat of the desires the "heart," that of the thoughts. The
	<i>Greek</i> for "searcheth" expresses an accurate
	following up of all tracks and windings. 
	</p>
<p><b>unto every one of
	you</b>—literally, "unto you, to each." 
	</p>
<p><b>according to your works</b>—to
	be judged not according to the mere act as it appears to man, but
	with reference to the motive, <i>faith</i> and <i>love</i> being the
	only motives which God recognizes as sound.</p>
