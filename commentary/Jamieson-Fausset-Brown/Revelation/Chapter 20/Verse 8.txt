<p><b>8. Gog and Magog</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>; see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>).
	Magog is a general name for northern nations of Japheth's posterity,
	whose ideal head is Gog (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ge+10:2">Genesis 10:2</span>).
	A has but one <i>Greek</i> article to "Gog and Magog,"
	whereby the two, namely, the prince and the people, are marked as
	having the closest connection. B reads the second article before
	Magog wrongly. HILLER
	[<i>Onomasticon</i>] explains both words as signifying "lofty,"
	"elevated." For "quarters" the <i>Greek</i> is
	"corners." 
	</p>
<p><b>to battle</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"to <i>the</i> war," in A and B. But ANDREAS
	omits "the."</p>
