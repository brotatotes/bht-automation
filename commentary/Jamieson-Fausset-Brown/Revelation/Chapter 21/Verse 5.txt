<p><b>5. sat</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"sitteth." 
	</p>
<p><b>all things new</b>—not
	recent, but <i>changed from the old</i> (<i>Greek,</i> "<i>kaina,</i>"
	not "<i>nea</i>"). An earnest of this regeneration and
	transfiguration of nature is given already in the regenerate soul. 
	</p>
<p><b>unto me</b>—so <i>Coptic</i>
	and ANDREAS. But A, B,
	<i>Vulgate,</i> and <i>Syriac</i> omit. 
	</p>
<p><b>true and faithful</b>—so
	ANDREAS. But A, B,
	<i>Vulgate, Syriac,</i> and <i>Coptic</i> transpose, "faithful
	and true" (literally, "genuine").</p>
