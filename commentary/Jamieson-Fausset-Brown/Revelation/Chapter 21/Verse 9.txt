<p><b>9.</b> The same angel who had shown
	John <i>Babylon the harlot,</i> is appropriately employed to show him
	in contrast <i>new Jerusalem, the Bride</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). The angel so employed is the one that had the last seven
	plagues, to show that the ultimate blessedness of the Church is one
	end of the divine judgments on her foes. 
	</p>
<p><b>unto me</b>—A, B, and
	<i>Vulgate</i> omit. 
	</p>
<p><b>the Lamb's wife</b>—in
	contrast to her <i>who sat on many waters</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), (that is, intrigued with many peoples and nations of the
	world, instead of giving her undivided affections, as the Bride does,
	to the Lamb.</p>
