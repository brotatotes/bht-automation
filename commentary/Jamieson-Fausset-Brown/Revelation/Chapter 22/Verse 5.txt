<p><b>5. there</b>—so ANDREAS.
	But A, B, <i>Vulgate,</i> and <i>Syriac</i> read, "(there shall
	be no night) any longer"; <i>Greek,</i> "<i>eti,</i>"
	for "<i>ekei.</i>" 
	</p>
<p><b>they need</b>—A, <i>Vulgate,</i>
	and <i>Coptic</i> read the future, "they <i>shall</i> not have
	need." B reads, "(and there shall be) no need." 
	</p>
<p><b>candle</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"lamp." A, <i>Vulgate, Syriac,</i> and <i>Coptic</i> insert
	"light (<i>of a candle,</i> or <i>lamp</i>)." B Omits it. 
	</p>
<p><b>of the sun</b>—so A. But B
	omits it. 
	</p>
<p><b>giveth . . .
	light</b>—"illumines." So <i>Vulgate</i> and <i>Syriac.</i>
	But A reads, "<i>shall</i> give light." 
	</p>
<p><b>them</b>—so B and ANDREAS.
	But A reads, "<i>upon</i> them." 
	</p>
<p><b>reign</b>—with a glory
	probably transcending that of their reign in heaven with Christ over
	the millennial nations in the flesh described in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+20:4">Revelation 20:4</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+20:6">Revelation 20:6</span>; that reign was but for
	a limited time, "a thousand years"; this final reign is
	"unto the ages of the ages."</p>
