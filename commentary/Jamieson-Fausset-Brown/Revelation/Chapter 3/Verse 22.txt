<p><b>22, 23.</b> None but those who "keep
	themselves" are likely to "save" others. 
	</p>
<p><b>have compassion</b>—So one
	oldest manuscript reads. But two oldest manuscripts, <i>Vulgate,</i>
	c., read, "convict" "reprove to their conviction";
	"confute, so as to convince." 
	</p>
<p><b>making a difference</b>—The
	oldest manuscripts and versions read the accusative for the
	nominative, "when separating themselves" [WAHL],
	referring to <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jdg+1:19">Judges 1:19</span>; or "when
	contending with you," as the <i>Greek</i> is translated, <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jdg+1:19- :">Judges 1:19- :</span>.</p>
