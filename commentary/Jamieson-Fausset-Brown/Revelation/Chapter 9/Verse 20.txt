<p><b>20. the rest of the men</b>—that
	is, the ungodly. 
	</p>
<p><b>yet</b>—So A, <i>Vulgate,
	Syriac,</i> and <i>Coptic.</i> B and <i>Aleph</i> read, "did <i>not</i>
	even repent of," namely, so as to give up "the works,"
	c. Like Pharaoh hardening his heart against repentance
	notwithstanding the plagues. 
	</p>
<p><b>of their hands</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). Especially the idols <i>made by their hands.</i> Compare
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+13:14">Revelation 13:14</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+13:15">Revelation 13:15</span>,
	"the image of the beast" <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="re+13:15- :">Revelation 13:15- :</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>that they should not</b>—So
	B reads. But A, C, and <i>Aleph</i> read "that they shall not":
	implying a prophecy of <i>certainty</i> that it shall be so. 
	</p>
<p><b>devils</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"demons" which lurk beneath the idols which idolaters
	worship.</p>
