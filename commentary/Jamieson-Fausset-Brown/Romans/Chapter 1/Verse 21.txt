<p><b>21. Because that, when they knew
	God</b>—that is, while still retaining some real knowledge of Him,
	and ere they sank down into the state next to be described. 
	</p>
<p><b>they glorified him not as
	God, neither were thankful</b>—neither yielded the <i>adoration</i>
	due to Himself, nor rendered the <i>gratitude</i> which His
	beneficence demanded. 
	</p>
<p><b>but became vain</b>—(compare
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="jer+2:5">Jeremiah 2:5</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>in their
	imaginations</b>—thoughts, notions, speculations, regarding God;
	compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:19">Matthew 15:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:35">Luke 2:35</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:20">1 Corinthians 3:20</span>, <i>Greek.</i>
</p>
<p><b>and their
	foolish</b>—"senseless," "stupid." 
	</p>
<p><b>heart</b>—that is, their
	whole inner man. 
	</p>
<p><b>was darkened</b>—How
	instructively is the downward progress of the human soul here traced!</p>
