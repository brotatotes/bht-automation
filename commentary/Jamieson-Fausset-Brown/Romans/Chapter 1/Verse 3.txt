<p><b>3, 4. Concerning his Son Jesus
	Christ our Lord</b>—the grand burden of this "Gospel of God."
	</p>
<p><b>made of the seed of David</b>—as,
	according to "the holy scriptures," He behooved to be. (See
	on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>according to the flesh</b>—that
	is, in His <i>human</i> nature (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:5">Romans 9:5</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:14">John 1:14</span>); implying, of course,
	that He had <i>another</i> nature, of which the apostle immediately
	proceeds to speak.</p>
