<p><b>1. Brethren, my heart's desire</b>—The
	word here expresses "entire complacency," that in which the
	heart would experience full satisfaction. 
	</p>
<p><b>and prayer</b>—"supplication."
	</p>
<p><b>to God for Israel</b>—"for
	them" is the true reading; the subject being continued from the
	close of the preceding chapter. 
	</p>
<p><b>is, that they may be
	saved</b>—"for their salvation." Having before poured
	forth the anguish of his soul at the general unbelief of his nation
	and its dreadful consequences (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), he here expresses in the most emphatic terms his desire
	and prayer for their salvation.</p>
