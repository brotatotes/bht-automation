<p><b>12. For there is no difference</b>—or
	"distinction" 
	</p>
<p><b>between Jew and Greek; for
	the same Lord over all</b>—that is, not <i>God</i> (as CALVIN,
	GROTIUS, OLSHAUSEN,
	HODGE), but <i>Christ,</i>
	as will be seen, we think, by comparing <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:9">Romans 10:9</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:12">Romans 10:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:13">Romans 10:13</span>
	and observing the apostle's usual style on such subjects. (So
	CHRYSOSTOM, MELVILLE,
	BENGEL, MEYER,
	DE WETTE,
	FRITZSCHE, THOLUCK,
	STUART, ALFORD,
	PHILIPPI). 
	</p>
<p><b>is rich</b>—a favorite
	Pauline term to express the exuberance of that saving grace which is
	in Christ Jesus. 
	</p>
<p><b>unto all that call upon
	him</b>—This confirms the application of the preceding words to
	<i>Christ;</i> since to call upon the name of the Lord Jesus is a
	customary expression. (See <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:59">Acts 7:59</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:60">Acts 7:60</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:14">Acts 9:14</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:21">Acts 9:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:16">Acts 22:16</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:2">1 Corinthians 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:22">2 Timothy 2:22</span>).</p>
