<p><b>2. For I bear them record</b>—or,
	"witness," as he well could from his own sad experience. 
	</p>
<p><b>that they have a zeal
	of</b>—"for" 
	</p>
<p><b>God, but not according to
	knowledge</b>—(Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:3">Acts 22:3</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:9-11">Acts 26:9-11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:13">Galatians 1:13</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:14">Galatians 1:14</span>). He alludes to this
	well-meaning of his people, notwithstanding their spiritual
	blindness, not certainly to excuse their rejection of Christ and rage
	against His saints, but as some ground of hope regarding them. (See
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:13">1 Timothy 1:13</span>).</p>
