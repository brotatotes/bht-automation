<p><b>11. I say then, Have they
	stumbled</b>—"Did they stumble" 
	</p>
<p><b>that they should fall? God
	forbid; but</b>—the supplement "rather" is better
	omitted. 
	</p>
<p><b>through their fall</b>—literally,
	"trespass," but here best rendered "false step"
	[DE WETTE];
	not "fall," as in our version. 
	</p>
<p><b>salvation is come to the
	Gentiles, to provoke them to jealousy</b>—Here, as also in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span> (quoted from <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="de+32:21">Deuteronomy 32:21</span>),
	we see that emulation is a legitimate stimulus to what is good.</p>
