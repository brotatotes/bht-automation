<p><b>12. Now if the fall of them</b>—"But
	if their trespass," or "false step" 
	</p>
<p><b>be the riches of the</b>—Gentile
	</p>
<p><b>world</b>—as being the
	occasion of their accession to Christ. 
	</p>
<p><b>and the diminishing of
	them</b>—that is, the reduction of the <i>true</i> Israel to so
	small a remnant. 
	</p>
<p><b>the riches of the Gentiles;
	how much more their fulness!</b>—that is, their full recovery (see
	on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:1">Romans 11:1</span>); that is, "If
	an event so untoward as Israel's fall was the occasion of such
	unspeakable good to the Gentile world, of how much greater good may
	we expect an event so blessed as their full recovery to be
	productive?"</p>
