<p><b>17, 18. And if</b>—rather, "But
	if"; that is, "If notwithstanding this consecration of
	Abraham's race to God. 
	</p>
<p><b>some of the branches</b>—The
	mass of the unbelieving and rejected Israelites are here called
	"some," not, as before, to meet Jewish prejudice (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, and on "not all" in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), but with the opposite view of checking Gentile pride. 
	</p>
<p><b>and thou, being a wild olive,
	wert</b>—"wast" 
	</p>
<p><b>grafted in among them</b>—Though
	it is more usual to graft the superior cutting upon the inferior
	stem, the opposite method, which is intended here, is not without
	example. 
	</p>
<p><b>and with them partakest</b>—"wast
	made partaker," along with the branches left, the believing
	remnant. 
	</p>
<p><b>of the root and fatness of
	the olive tree</b>—the rich grace secured by covenant to the true
	seed of Abraham.</p>
