<p><b>24. For if thou wert cut</b>—"wert
	cut off" 
	</p>
<p><b>from the olive tree, which is
	wild by nature, and wast grafted contrary to nature into a good olive
	tree; how much more shall these,</b> c.—This is just the converse
	of <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:21">Romans 11:21</span>: "As the
	excision of the merely <i>engrafted</i> Gentiles through unbelief is
	a thing much more to be expected than was the excision of the <i>natural</i>
	Israel, before it happened so the restoration of Israel, when they
	shall be brought to believe in Jesus, is a thing far more in the line
	of what we should expect, than the admission of the Gentiles to a
	standing which they never before enjoyed."</p>
