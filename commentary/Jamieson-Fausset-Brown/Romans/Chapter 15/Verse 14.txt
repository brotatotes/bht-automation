<p><b>14, 15. And,</b> &amp;c.—rather,
	"Now I am persuaded, my brethren, even I myself, concerning you"
	</p>
<p><b>that ye also yourselves are
	full of goodness</b>—of inclination to all I have been enjoining on
	you 
	</p>
<p><b>filled with all knowledge</b>—of
	the truth expounded 
	</p>
<p><b>and able</b>—without my
	intervention. 
	</p>
<p><b>to admonish one another.</b></p>
