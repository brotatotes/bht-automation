<p><b>16. that I should be the</b>—rather,
	"a" 
	</p>
<p><b>minister</b>—The word here
	used is commonly employed to express the office of the priesthood,
	from which accordingly the figurative language of the rest of the
	verse is taken. 
	</p>
<p><b>of Jesus Christ</b>—"Christ
	Jesus," according to the true reading. 
	</p>
<p><b>to the Gentiles</b>—a
	further proof that the Epistle was addressed to a <i>Gentile</i>
	church. (See on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:13">Romans 1:13</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>ministering the gospel of
	God</b>—As the word here is a still more priestly one, it should be
	rendered, "ministering as a priest in the Gospel of God." 
	</p>
<p><b>that the offering up of the
	Gentiles</b>—as an oblation to God, in their converted character. 
	</p>
<p><b>might be acceptable, being
	sanctified by the Holy Ghost</b>—the end to which the ancient
	offerings typically looked.</p>
