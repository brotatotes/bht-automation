<p><b>19. Through mighty</b>—literally,
	"in the power of" 
	</p>
<p><b>signs and wonders</b>—that
	is, glorious miracles. 
	</p>
<p><b>by the power of the Spirit of
	God</b>—"the Holy Ghost," as the true reading seems to
	be. This seems intended to explain the efficacy of the word preached,
	as well as the working of the miracles which attested it. 
	</p>
<p><b>so that from Jerusalem, and
	round about unto</b>—"as far as" 
	</p>
<p><b>Illyricum</b>—to the
	extreme northwestern boundary of Greece. It corresponds to the modern
	Croatia and Dalmatia (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:10">2 Timothy 4:10</span>).
	See <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:1">Acts 20:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:2">Acts 20:2</span>.
	</p>
<p><b>I have fully preached the
	gospel of Christ.</b></p>
