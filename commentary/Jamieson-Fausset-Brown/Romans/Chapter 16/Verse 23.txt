<p><b>23. Gaius mine host, and</b>—the
	host 
	</p>
<p><b>of the whole church</b>—(See
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:4">Acts 20:4</span>). It would appear that
	he was one of only two persons whom Paul baptized with his own hand
	(compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:1">3 John 1:1</span>). His Christian
	hospitality appears to have been something uncommon. 
	</p>
<p><b>Erastus the
	chamberlain</b>—"treasurer." 
	</p>
<p><b>of the city</b>—doubtless
	of Corinth. (See <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:22">Acts 19:22</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:20">2 Timothy 4:20</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>and Quartus a brother</b>—rather,
	"the" or "our brother"; as Sosthenes and Timothy
	are called (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:1">1 Corinthians 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:1">2 Corinthians 1:1</span>,
	<i>Greek</i>). Nothing more is known of this Quartus.</p>
