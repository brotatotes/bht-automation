<p><b>4. who have for my life laid
	down</b>—"who did for my life lay down" 
	</p>
<p><b>their own necks</b>—that
	is, risked their lives; either at Corinth (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:6">Acts 18:6</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:9">Acts 18:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:10">Acts 18:10</span>),
	or more probably at Ephesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:30">Acts 19:30</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:31">Acts 19:31</span>; and compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:31- :">Acts 19:31- :</span>). They must have returned from Ephesus (where we last find
	them in the history of the Acts) to Rome, whence the edict of
	Claudius had banished them (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:31- :">Acts 19:31- :</span>); and doubtless, if not the principal members of that
	Christian community, they were at least the most endeared to our
	apostle. 
	</p>
<p><b>unto whom not only I give
	thanks, but also all the churches of the Gentiles</b>—whose special
	apostle this dear couple had rescued from imminent danger.</p>
