<p><b>3, 4. For what if some did not
	believe?</b>—It is the unbelief of the great body of the nation
	which the apostle points at; but as it sufficed for his argument to
	put the supposition thus gently, he uses this word "some"
	to soften prejudice. 
	</p>
<p><b>shall their unbelief make the
	faith of God</b>—or, "faithfulness of God." 
	</p>
<p><b>of none effect?</b>—"nullify,"
	"invalidate" it.</p>
