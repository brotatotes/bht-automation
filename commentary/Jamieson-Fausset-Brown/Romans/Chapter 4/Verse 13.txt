<p><b>13-15. For the promise,</b> &amp;c.—This
	is merely an enlargement of the foregoing reasoning, applying to the
	<i>law</i> what had just been said of <i>circumcision.</i>
</p>
<p><b>that he should be the heir of
	the world</b>—or, that "all the families of the earth should
	be blessed in him." 
	</p>
<p><i><b>was</b></i><b> not to
	Abraham or to his seed through the law</b>—in virtue of obedience
	to the law. 
	</p>
<p><b>but through the righteousness
	of faith</b>—in virtue of his simple faith in the divine promises.</p>
