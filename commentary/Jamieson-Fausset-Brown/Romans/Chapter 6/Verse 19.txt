<p><b>19. I speak after the manner of
	men</b>—descending, for illustration, to the level of common
	affairs. 
	</p>
<p><b>because of the infirmity of
	your flesh</b>—the weakness of your spiritual apprehension. 
	</p>
<p><b>for as ye have yielded</b>—"as
	ye yielded," the thing being viewed as now past. 
	</p>
<p><b>your members servants to
	Uncleanness and to Iniquity unto</b>—the practice of 
	</p>
<p><b>iniquity; even so now yield
	your members servants to Righteousness unto holiness</b>—rather,
	"unto (the attainment of) sanctification," as the same word
	is rendered in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:13">2 Thessalonians 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:30">1 Corinthians 1:30</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:2">1 Peter 1:2</span>: —that is, "Looking
	back upon the <i>heartiness</i> with which ye served Sin, and the
	<i>lengths</i> ye went to be stimulated now to like zeal and like
	exuberance in the service of a better Master."</p>
