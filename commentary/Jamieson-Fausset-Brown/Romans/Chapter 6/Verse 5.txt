5. For if we have been planted
	together—literally, "have become formed together."
	(The word is used here only). 
	in the likeness of his death,
	we shall be also in the likeness of his resurrection—that is,
	"Since Christ's death and resurrection are inseparable in their
	efficacy, union with Him in the one carries with it participation in
	the other, for privilege and for duty alike." The future
	tense is used of participation in His resurrection, because this is
	but partially realized in the present state. (See on  :-).
