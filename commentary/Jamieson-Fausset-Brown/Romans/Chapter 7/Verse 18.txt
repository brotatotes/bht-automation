<p><b>18. For,</b> c.—better, "For
	I know that there dwelleth not in me, that is in my flesh, any good."
	</p>
<p><b>for to will</b>—"desire."
	</p>
<p><b>is present with me but </b><i><b>how</b></i><b>
	to perform that which is good</b>—the supplement "how,"
	in our version, weakens the statement. 
	</p>
<p><b>I find not</b>—Here, again,
	we have the <i>double self</i> of the renewed man; "In me
	dwelleth no good; but this corrupt self is not my true self; it is
	but sin dwelling in my real self, as a renewed man."</p>
