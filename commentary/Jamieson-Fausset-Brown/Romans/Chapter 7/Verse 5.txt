<p><b>5. For when we were in the flesh</b>—in
	our unregenerate state, as we came into the world. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>the motions</b>—"passions"
	(<i>Margin</i>), "affections" (as in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), or "stirrings." 
	</p>
<p><b>of sins</b>—that is,
	"prompting to the commission of sins." 
	</p>
<p><b>which were by the law</b>—by
	occasion of the law, which fretted, irritated our inward corruption
	by its prohibitions. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>.
	</p>
<p><b>did work in our members</b>—the
	members of the body, as the instruments by which these inward
	stirrings find vent in action, and become facts of the life. See on
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>to bring forth fruit unto
	death</b>—death in the sense of <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. Thus hopeless is all holy fruit before union to Christ.</p>
