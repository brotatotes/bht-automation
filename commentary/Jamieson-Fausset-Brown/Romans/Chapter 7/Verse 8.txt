<p><b>8. For without the law</b>—that
	is, before its extensive demands and prohibitions come to operate
	upon our corrupt nature. 
	</p>
<p><b>sin was</b>—rather, "is"
	</p>
<p><b>dead</b>—that is, the
	sinful principle of our nature lies so dormant, so torpid, that its
	virulence and power are unknown, and to our feeling it is as good as
	"dead."</p>
