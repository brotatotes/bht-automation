<p><b>6. For</b>—a mere particle of
	transition here [THOLUCK],
	like "but" or "now." 
	</p>
<p><b>to be carnally
	minded</b>—literally, "the mind" or "minding of the
	flesh" (<i>Margin</i>); that is, the pursuit of fleshly ends. 
	</p>
<p><b>is death</b>—not only "ends
	in" [ALFORD, c.], but
	even now "is" carrying death into its bosom, so that such
	are "dead while they live" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:6">1 Timothy 5:6</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:1">Ephesians 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:5">Ephesians 2:5</span>)
	[PHILIPPI]. 
	</p>
<p><b>but to be spiritually
	minded</b>—"the mind" or "minding of the spirit";
	that is, the pursuit of spiritual objects. 
	</p>
<p><b>is life and peace</b>—not
	"life" only, in contrast with the "death" that is
	in the other pursuit, but "peace"; it is the very element
	of the soul's deepest repose and true bliss.</p>
