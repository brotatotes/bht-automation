<p><b>16. So then it is not of him that
	willeth</b>—hath the inward <i>desire</i>
</p>
<p><b>nor of him that
	runneth</b>—maketh active <i>effort</i> (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:24">1 Corinthians 9:24</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:26">1 Corinthians 9:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+2:16">Philippians 2:16</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+3:14">Philippians 3:14</span>). Both these are
	indispensable to salvation, yet salvation is owing to neither, but is
	purely "of God that showeth mercy." See on <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="php+3:14- :">Philippians 3:14- :</span>, "Work out your own salvation with fear and
	trembling: for it is God which, <i>out of His own good pleasure,</i>
	worketh in you both to <i>will</i> and to <i>do.</i>"</p>
