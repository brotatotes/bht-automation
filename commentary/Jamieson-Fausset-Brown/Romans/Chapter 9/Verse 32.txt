32, 33. Wherefore? Because they
	sought it not by faith, but as it were—rather
	simply, "as" 
	by the works of the law—as
	if it were thus attainable, which justification is not: Since,
	therefore, it is attainable only by faith, they missed it. 
	for—it is doubtful if
	this particle was originally in the text. 
	they stumbled at that
	stumbling-stone—better, "against the stone of stumbling,"
	meaning Christ. But in this they only did.
