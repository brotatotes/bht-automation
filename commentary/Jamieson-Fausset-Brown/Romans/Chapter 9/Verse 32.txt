<p><b>32, 33. Wherefore? Because they
	sought </b><i><b>it</b></i><b> not by faith, but as it were</b>—rather
	simply, "as" 
	</p>
<p><b>by the works of the law</b>—as
	if it were thus attainable, which justification is not: Since,
	therefore, it is attainable only by faith, they missed it. 
	</p>
<p><b>for</b>—it is doubtful if
	this particle was originally in the text. 
	</p>
<p><b>they stumbled at that
	stumbling-stone</b>—better, "against the stone of stumbling,"
	meaning <i>Christ.</i> But in this they only did.</p>
