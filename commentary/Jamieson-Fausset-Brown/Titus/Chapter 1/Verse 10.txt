<p><b>10. unruly</b>—"insubordinate."
	</p>
<p><b>and</b>—omitted in the
	oldest manuscripts. "There are many unruly persons, vain
	talkers, and deceivers"; "unruly" being predicated of
	both <i>vain talkers</i> and deceivers. 
	</p>
<p><b>vain talkers</b>—opposed to
	"holding fast the faithful word" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). "Vain jangling" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>); "foolish questions, unprofitable and vain" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). The source of the evil was corrupted Judaism (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). Many Jews were then living in Crete, according to
	JOSEPHUS; so the Jewish
	leaven remained in some of them after conversion. 
	</p>
<p><b>deceivers</b>—literally,
	"deceivers of the minds of others" (<i>Greek,</i> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>).</p>
