<p><b>6.</b> (Compare <i>Notes,</i> see on
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>.) The thing dwelt on here
	as the requisite in a bishop, is a good reputation among those over
	whom he is to be set. The immorality of the Cretan professors
	rendered this a necessary requisite in one who was to be a <i>reprover:</i>
	and their unsoundness in doctrine also made needful great
	steadfastness in the faith (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:9">Titus 1:9</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:13">Titus 1:13</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>having faithful children</b>—that
	is, <i>believing</i> children. He who could not bring his children to
	faith, how shall he bring others? [BENGEL].
	ALFORD explains,
	"established in the faith." 
	</p>
<p><b>not accused</b>—not merely
	not riotous, but "not (even) accused of riot" ("profligacy"
	[ALFORD]; "dissolute
	life" [WAHL]). 
	</p>
<p><b>unruly</b>—<i>insubordinate;</i>
	opposed to "in subjection" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:13- :">Titus 1:13- :</span>).</p>
