<p><b>9. Holding fast</b>—Holding firmly
	to (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:24">Matthew 6:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:13">Luke 16:13</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>the faithful</b>—true and
	trustworthy (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:15">1 Timothy 1:15</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>word as he has been
	taught</b>—literally, "the word (which is) according to the
	teaching" which he has received (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:15- :">1 Timothy 1:15- :</span>, end; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:14">2 Timothy 3:14</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>by</b>—Translate as <i>Greek,</i>
	"to exhort <i>in</i> doctrine (<i>instruction</i>) which is
	sound"; <i>sound doctrine</i> or <i>instruction</i> is the
	element IN which his
	<i>exhorting</i> is to have place . . . On "sound"
	(peculiar to the Pastoral Epistles), see <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:10">1 Timothy 1:10</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:3">1 Timothy 6:3</span>. 
	</p>
<p><b>convince</b>—rather,
	"reprove" [ALFORD],
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:13">Titus 1:13</span>).</p>
