<p><b>2. sober</b>—Translated
	"vigilant," as <i>sober</i> men alone can be (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>). But "sober" here answers to "not given to
	wine," <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:3">Titus 2:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:7">Titus 1:7</span>.
	</p>
<p><b>grave</b>—"dignified";
	behaving with <i>reverent propriety.</i>
</p>
<p><b>temperate</b>—"self-restrained";
	"discreet" [ALFORD],
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:8">Titus 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:9">1 Timothy 2:9</span>).
	</p>
<p><b>faith . . . charity</b>
	[love] <b>. . . patience</b>—combined in <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:9- :">1 Timothy 2:9- :</span>. "Faith, <i>hope,</i> charity" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:9- :">1 Timothy 2:9- :</span>). "Patience," <i>Greek,</i> "enduring
	perseverance," is the attendant on, and is supported by, "hope"
	(<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:7">1 Corinthians 13:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:3">1 Thessalonians 1:3</span>).
	It is the grace which especially becomes <i>old men,</i> being the
	fruit of ripened experience derived from trials overcome (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:3- :">1 Thessalonians 1:3- :</span>).</p>
