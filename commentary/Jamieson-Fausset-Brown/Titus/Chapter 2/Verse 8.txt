<p><b>8. speech</b>—discourse in public
	and private ministrations. 
	</p>
<p><b>he that is of the contrary
	part</b>—the adversary (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:9">Titus 1:9</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:25">2 Timothy 2:25</span>), whether he be heathen
	or Jew. 
	</p>
<p><b>may be ashamed</b>—put to
	confusion by the power of truth and innocence (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:5">Titus 2:5</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:10">Titus 2:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:14">1 Timothy 5:14</span>;
	<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:1">1 Timothy 6:1</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>no evil thing</b>—<i>in our
	acts,</i> or <i>demeanor.</i>
</p>
<p><b>of you</b>—So one of the
	oldest manuscripts. Other very old manuscripts read, "of US,"
	Christians.</p>
