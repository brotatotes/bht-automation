<p><b>7. That,</b> c.—the purpose which
	He aimed at in having "saved us" (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>), namely, "That being (having been) justified (<i>accounted
	righteous</i> through faith at our 'regeneration,' and <i>made
	righteous</i> by the daily 'renewing of the Holy Ghost') by His grace
	(as opposed to <i>works,</i> <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>) we should be made heirs." 
	</p>
<p><b>his grace</b>—<i>Greek,</i>
	"the grace of <i>the former,</i>" that is, God (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:4">Titus 3:4</span>
<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:15">Romans 5:15</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>heirs</b>— (<span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:15- :">Romans 5:15- :</span>). 
	</p>
<p><b>according to the hope of
	eternal life</b>— <span class="scriptRef" com="jfb" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:2">Titus 1:2</span>, and
	also the position of the <i>Greek</i> words, confirm <i>English
	Version,</i> that is, <i>agreeably to</i> the hope of eternal life;
	the eternal inheritance fully satisfying the hope. BENGEL
	and ELLICOTT explain it,
	"<i>heirs of eternal life,</i> in the way of hope," that
	is, not yet in actual possession. Such a <i>blessed hope,</i> which
	once was not possessed, will lead a Christian to practice holiness
	and meekness toward others, the lesson especially needed by the
	Cretans.</p>
