<p><b>3.</b><i>My defense.</i> Apart from the principal matter that he has at present in hand, it appears also to have been his intention to beat down, in passing, the calumnies of those who clamored against his call, as if he had been one of the ordinary class of ministers. “I am accustomed,” says he, “to put you forward as my shield, in the event of any one detracting from the honor of my Apostleship.” Hence it follows, that the Corinthians are injurious and inimical to themselves, if they do not acknowledge him as such, for if their faith was a solemn attestation of Paul’s Apostleship, and his <i>defense</i>, against slanderers, the one could not be invalidated without the other falling along with it. </p>
<p>Where others read — <i>those who interrogate me</i>, I have rendered it — <i>those that examine me</i> — for he refers to those who raised a dispute as to his Apostleship. <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(480)</sup></span> Latin writers, I confess, speak of a criminal being <i>interrogated</i> <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(481)</sup></span> according to the laws, but the meaning of the word <grk><blu><span lang="el-GR" style="">ἀνακρίνειν</span></blu></grk> which Paul makes use of, seemed to me to be brought out better in this way. </p>
<p><span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;">(480)</span> “<blu><span lang="fr-FR" style="">Ceux qui vouloyent mettre en debat son Apostolat, et le contreroller, comme on dit</span></blu>;” — “Those who were desirous to bring his Apostleship into dispute, and overhale it, as they say.” </p>
<p><span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;">(481)</span> The expression is made use of by Suetonius. (Aug. 33.) <blu>Reum ita fertur</blu> <blu><i>interrogasse</i></blu> . He is said to have <i>interrogated</i> the criminal in such a manner.) — <i>Ed</i>. </p>
