<p><b>8</b><i>For from you sounded forth</i>. Here we have an elegant metaphor, by which he intimates that their faith was so lively, <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(507)</sup></span> that it did, as it were, by its <i>sound</i>, arouse other nations. For he says that the word of God <i>sounded forth</i> from them, inasmuch as their faith was <i>sonorous</i> <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(508)</sup></span> for procuring credit for the gospel. He says that this had not only occurred in neighboring places, but this <i>sound</i> had also extended far and wide, and had been distinctly heard, so that the matter did not require to be published by him. <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(509)</sup></span> </p>
<p><span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;">(507)</span> “<blu><span lang="fr-FR" style="">Si viue et vertueuse</span></blu>;” — “So lively and virtuous.” </p>
<p><span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;">(508)</span> “<blu><span lang="fr-FR" style="">Auoit resonné haut et clair</span></blu>;” — “Had resounded loud and clear.” </p>
<p><span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;">(509)</span> “<blu><span lang="fr-FR" style="">Tellement que la chose n’ha point besoin d’estre par luy diuulgee et magnifiee d’auantage</span></blu>;” — “So that the matter does not need to be farther published and extolled by him.” </p>
