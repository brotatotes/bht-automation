<p><b>1</b><i>Wherefore, when we could no longer endure</i>. By the detail which follows, he assures them of the desire of which he had spoken. For if, on being detained elsewhere, he had sent no other to Thessalonica in his place, it might have seemed as though he were not so much concerned in regard to them; but when he substitutes Timothy in his place, he removes that suspicion, more especially when he prefers them before himself. Now that he esteemed them above himself, he shews from this, that he chose rather to be left alone than that they should be deserted: for these words, <i>we judged it good to be left alone</i>, are emphatic. Timothy was a most faithful companion to him: he had at that time no others with him; hence it was inconvenient and distressing for him to be without him. It is therefore a token of rare affection and anxious desire that he does not refuse to deprive himself of all comfort, with the view of relieving the Thessalonians. To the same effect is the word <grk><blu><span lang="el-GR" style="">εὐδοκήσαμεν</span></blu></grk>, which expresses a prompt inclination of the mind. <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(554)</sup></span> </p>
