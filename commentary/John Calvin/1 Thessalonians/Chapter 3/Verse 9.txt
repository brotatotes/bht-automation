<p><b>9</b><i>For what thanksgiving</i>. Not satisfied with a simple affirmation, he intimates how extraordinary is the greatness of his joy, by asking himself <i>what thanks he can render to God;</i> for by speaking thus he declares that he cannot find an expression of gratitude that can come up to the measure of his joy. He says that he rejoices <i>before God</i>, that is, truly and without any pretense. </p>
