<p><b>29.</b><i>Who is weak.</i> How many there are that allow all offenses to pass by unheeded — who either despise the infirmities of brethren, or trample them under foot! This, however, arises from their having no concern for the Church. For concern, undoubtedly, produces <grk><blu><span lang="el-GR" style="">συμπάθειαν</span></blu></grk> (<i>sympathy,</i>) <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(867)</sup></span> which leads the Minister of Christ to participate in the feelings of all, <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(868)</sup></span> and put himself in the place of all, that he may suit himself to all. </p>
<p><span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;">(867)</span> See Calvin’s <i>Harmony,</i> vol. 2, p. 232. </p>
<p><span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;">(868)</span> <sup>“</sup><blu><span lang="fr-FR" style="">Prend en soy les afflictions de tous</span></blu>;” — “Take upon himself the afflictions of all.” </p>
