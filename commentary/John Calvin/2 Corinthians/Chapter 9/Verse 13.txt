<p><b>13.</b><i>By the experiment of that administration</i> The term <i>experiment</i> here, as in a variety of other places, means <i>proof</i> or <i>trial</i> <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(733)</sup></span> For it was a sufficient token for bringing the love of the Corinthians to the test, — that they were so liberal to brethren that were at a great distance from them. Paul, however, extends it farther — to their concurrent obedience in the gospel. <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(734)</sup></span> For by such proofs we truly manifest, that we are obedient to the doctrine of the gospel. Now their concurrence appears from this — that alms are conferred with the common consent of all. </p>
<p><span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;">(733)</span> “<blu><span lang="fr-FR" style="">Tesmoignage, enseignement, ou experience</span></blu>;” — “Proof, voucher, or trial.” </p>
<p><span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;">(734)</span> “<blu><span lang="fr-FR" style="">Leur obeissance qu’ils rendoyent tons d’vn accord a l’euangile</span></blu>;” — “Their obedience which they rendered, all with one accord, to the gospel.” </p>
