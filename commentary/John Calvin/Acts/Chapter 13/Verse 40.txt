<p><b>40.</b><i>Take heed that that come not upon you.</i> Because he had to do with stiff-necked men, or at least there were diverse in the company which were stiff-necked (as if he meant with a hammer to soften their stubbornnesses) he addeth a chiding unto doctrine. For if the Jews had been obedient and willing to obey, undoubtedly he would have sought sweetly to allure them unto Christ. But it was either their sluggishness, or else their willfulness, that caused him to be more angry; like as all those must be cited to appear before God’s judgment-seat who contemn the grace of Christ and the horrible judgment of eternal death must be denounced to those. He signifieth, indeed, that there is yet place left for repentance, when he willeth <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(825)</sup></span> them to take heed; yet, notwithstanding, he telleth them therewithal, that unless they beware in time, the horrible vengeance of God is not far off. </p>
<p><i>Which is said in the prophets.</i> The place which is cited is taken out of the first chapter of Habakkuk, (<span class="scriptRef" com="cal" lang="eng" ref="hab+1:5">Habakkuk 1:5</span>;) but because all prophecies were gathered into one volume or body, Paul saith that it is written in the prophets. </p>
<p><span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;">(825)</span> “<blu>Hortatur</blu>,” exhorteth. </p>
