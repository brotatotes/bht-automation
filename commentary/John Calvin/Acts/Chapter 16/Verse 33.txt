<p><b>33.</b><i>He was baptized, and all his household.</i> Luke doth again commend the godly zeal of the keeper, that he did consecrate all his whole house to the Lord; wherein doth also appear the grace of God, in that he brought all his whole family unto a godly consent. And we must also note the notable exchange: he was of late about to murder himself, because he thought that Paul and the rest were escaped; but now laying aside all fear, he bringeth them home. − <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(223)</sup></span> So that we see how faith doth animate and encourage those to behave themselves stoutly who before had no heart. And surely, when we droop − <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(224)</sup></span> through fear and doubtfulness, there is no better matter of boldness than to be able to cast all our cares into God’s bosom; that no danger may terrify us from doing our duty, whilst that we look for an end at God’s hand, such as he shall see to be most profitable. − </p>
