<p><b>4.</b><i>But when Christ, our life, shall appear</i>. Here we have a choice consolation — that the coming of Christ will be the manifestation of our life. And, at the same time, he admonishes us how unreasonable were the disposition of the man, who should refuse to bear up <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(435)</sup></span> until that day. For if our life is shut up in Christ, it must be <i>hid</i>, until he shall <i>appear</i> </p>
<p><span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;">(435)</span> “<blu><span lang="fr-FR" style="">D’endurer et attendre</span></blu>;” — “To endure and wait.” </p>
