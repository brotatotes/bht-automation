<p><b>8.</b><i>But now</i> — that is, after having ceased to <i>live in the flesh</i>. For the power and nature of <i>mortification</i> are such, that all corrupt affections are extinguished in us, lest sin should afterwards produce in us its wonted fruits. What I have rendered <blu><i>indignationem</i></blu> , (<i>indignation</i>,) is in the Greek <grk><blu><span lang="el-GR" style="">θυμός</span></blu></grk> — a term, which denotes a more impetuous passionateness than <grk><blu><span lang="el-GR" style="">ὀργὴ</span></blu></grk>, (<i>anger</i>.) Here, however, he enumerates, as may easily be perceived, forms of vice that were different from those previously mentioned. </p>
