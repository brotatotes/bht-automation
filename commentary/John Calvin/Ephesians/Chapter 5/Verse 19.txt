<p><b>19.</b><i>To psalms, and hymns, and spiritual songs.</i> These are truly pleasant and delightful fruits. The <i>Spirit</i> means “joy in the Holy Ghost,” (<span class="scriptRef" com="cal" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:17">Romans 14:17</span>;) and the exhortation, <i>be ye filled</i>, (ver. 18,) alludes to deep drinking, with which it is indirectly contrasted<i>. Speaking to themselves,</i> is <i>speaking among themselves.</i> Nor does he enjoin them to sing inwardly or alone; for he immediately adds, <i>singing in your hearts;</i> as if he had said, “Let your praises be not merely on the tongue, as hypocrites do, but from the heart.” What may be the exact difference between <i>psalms</i> and <i>hymns,</i> or between <i>hymns</i> and <i>songs,</i> it is not easy to determine, though a few remarks on this subject shall be offered on a future occasion. <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(163)</sup></span> The appellation <i>spiritual,</i> given to these songs, is strikingly appropriate; for the songs most frequently used are almost always on trifling subjects, and very far from being chaste. </p>
