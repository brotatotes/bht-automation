<p><b>7.</b><i>Ye did run well</i>. The censure which the apostle administers for their present departure from the truth is mingled with approbation of their former course, for the express purpose that, by being brought to a sense of shame, they may return more speedily to the right path. The astonishment conveyed in the question, <i>who hindered you?</i> was intended to produce a blush. I have chosen to translate the Greek word<grk><blu><span lang="el-GR" style="">πείθσθαι,</span></blu></grk> <i>obey</i>, rather than <i>believe</i>, because, having once embraced the purity of the gospel, they had been led away from a course of obedience. </p>
