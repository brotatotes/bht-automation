<p><b>55.</b><i>Many from that country went up to Jerusalem.</i> It was not absolutely enjoined that they should <i>purify themselves before sacrificing the passover;</i> and, therefore, the Evangelist does not say that <i>all</i> came, but <i>many</i> No unclean person, indeed, was permitted to eat; but I say that this sanctification was undertaken voluntarily and from their own inclination, so that others were not forbidden to eat, though they had not been prepared by such a ceremony before the day of <i>the feast</i> </p>
