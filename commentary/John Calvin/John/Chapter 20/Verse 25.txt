<p><b>25.</b><i>Unless I see in his hands the print of the nails.</i> This points out the source of the vice to be, that every one wishes to be wise from his own understanding, and flatters himself beyond measure. <i>If I do not see,</i> says he, “and if I do not touch, <i>I will not believe.</i> ” <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(214)</sup></span> These words have no approach to faith, but it is what may be called a sensual judgment, by which I mean, a judgment which is founded on the perception of the senses, <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(215)</sup></span> The same thing happens to all who are so devoted to themselves <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(216)</sup></span> that they leave no room for the word of God. It is of no consequence, whether you read <i>the place,</i> or <i>the shape,</i> or The Print <i>of the nails;</i> for transcribers may have exchanged <grk><blu><span lang="el-GR" style="">τύπον</span></blu></grk> (<i>print</i>) for <grk><blu><span lang="el-GR" style="">τόπον</span></blu></grk>, (<i>place,</i>) or <grk><blu><span lang="el-GR" style="">τόπον</span></blu></grk> (<i>place</i>)for <grk><blu><span lang="el-GR" style="">τύπον</span></blu></grk>, (<i>print;</i>) but the meaning is not altered on that account. Let the reader, therefore, choose which of them he shall prefer. <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(217)</sup></span> </p>
