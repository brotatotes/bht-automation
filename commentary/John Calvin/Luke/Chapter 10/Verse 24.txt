<p><b><span class="scriptRef" com="cal" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:24">Luke 10:24</span></b><b>.</b><i>Many Prophets and Kings have desired to see.</i> The condition of the Church, at the present day, is justly pronounced to be preferable to that of the holy fathers, who lived under the Law; because to them was exhibited, under shadows and figures only, what is now openly manifested in the shining face of Christ. <i>The vail of the temple being rent,</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="cal" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:51">Matthew 27:51</span>,) we enter by faith into the heavenly sanctuary, and are freely permitted to approach to God. Although the fathers were satisfied with their lot, and enjoyed a blessed peace in their own minds, yet this did not prevent their desires from extending farther. Thus, Abraham <i>saw the day of Christ</i> afar off, <i>and rejoice,</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="cal" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:56">John 8:56</span>,) and yet longed to enjoy a nearer view, but did not obtain his wish. Simeon spoke the sentiments of all, <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(191)</sup></span> when he said, <i>Now thou sendest thy servant away in peace,</i> (<span class="scriptRef" com="cal" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:29">Luke 2:29</span>.) And indeed it was impossible that, under the burden of that curse by which the human race is crushed, they should be otherwise than altogether inflamed with the desire of a promised deliverance. <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(192)</sup></span> Let us therefore learn, that they breathed after Christ, like hungry persons, and yet possessed a serene faith; so that they did not murmur against God, but kept their minds in patient expectation till the full time of revelation. </p>
<p><span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;">(191)</span> “<blu><span lang="fr-FR" style="">Simeon disoit selon l’affection de tours les Peres</span></blu>;” — “Simeon spoke according to the feeling of all the Fathers.” </p>
<p><span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;">(192)</span> “<blu><span lang="fr-FR" style="">Et de faict, il ne se pouvoit faire que ces bons personnages ne fussent tous ravis, et comme enflambez d’un grand desir de la delivrance promise</span></blu>.” — “And indeed it was impossible that those good men should not be altogether transported, and as it were inflamed with a great desire of the promised deliverance.” </p>
