<p><b>17</b><i>That for the defense</i>. Those who truly loved Christ reckoned that it would be a disgrace to them if they did not associate themselves with Paul as his companions, when maintaining the cause of the gospel; and we must act in such a manner, as to give a helping hand, as far as possible, to the servants of Christ when in difficulty. <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(74)</sup></span> Observe, again, this expression — <i>for the defense of the gospel</i> For since Christ confers upon us so great an honor, what excuse shall we have, if we shall be traitors to his cause, <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(75)</sup></span> or what may we expect, if we betray it by our silence, but that <i>he</i> shall in return desert our cause, who is our sole <i>Advocate</i>, or Patron, <i>with the Father?</i> <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(76)</sup></span> (<span class="scriptRef" com="cal" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:1">1 John 2:1</span>.) </p>
<p><span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;">(74)</span> “<blu><span lang="fr-FR" style="">Estans en quelque necessite</span></blu>;” — “When they are in any emergency.” </p>
<p><span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;">(75)</span> “<blu><i>Praevaricatores</i></blu> ” The term is employed by classical writers in the sense of betraying the cause of one’s client, and by neglect or collusion assisting his opponent. See Quinct. 9:2. — <i>Ed</i>. </p>
<p><span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;">(76)</span> “<blu><span lang="fr-FR" style="">Si nous nous entendons auec la partie aduerse d’iceluy</span></blu>;” — “If we should connect ourselves with the party opposed to him.” </p>
