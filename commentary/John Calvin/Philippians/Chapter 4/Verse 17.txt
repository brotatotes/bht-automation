<p><b>17.</b><i>Not that I demand a gift.</i> Again he repels an unfavourable opinion that might be formed of immoderate cupidity, that they might not suppose that it was an indirect hint, <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(252)</sup></span> as if they ought singly to stand in the room of all, <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(253)</sup></span> and as if he abused their kindness. He accordingly declares, that he consulted not so much his own advantage as theirs. “While I receive from you,” says he, <i>“there</i> is proportionably much advantage that redounds to yourselves; for there are just so many articles that you may reckon to have been transferred to the table of accounts.” The meaning of this word <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(254)</sup></span> is connected with the similitude formerly employed of exchange or compensation in pecuniary matters. </p>
<p><span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;">(252)</span> “<blu><i><span lang="fr-FR" style="">Pour</span></i></blu> <blu><span lang="fr-FR" style="">les induire a continuer</span></blu>;” — “To induce them to hold on.” </p>
<p><span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;">(253)</span> “<blu><span lang="fr-FR" style="">Comme si eux deussent tenir la place de tous, et faire pour les autres</span></blu>;” — “As if they ought to hold the place of all, and to act in the room of others.” </p>
<p><span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;">(254)</span> Calvin evidently refers to the word <grk><blu><span lang="el-GR" style="">λόγον</span></blu></grk>, <i>(account,)</i> which the Apostle had made use of in <span class="scriptRef" com="cal" lang="eng" ref="php+4:15">Philippians 4:15</span>, in the phrase <grk><blu><span lang="el-GR" style="">εἰς λόγον δόσεω; καὶ λήψεω;</span></blu></grk>, <i>(in the matter of giving and receiving.)</i> It is noticed by <i>Beza,</i> that the Rabbins make use of a corresponding phrase <span>אשמו</span> <span>ןחמ</span> <i>(mattan umassa)</i> — <i>giving and taking.</i> — <i>Ed.</i> </p>
