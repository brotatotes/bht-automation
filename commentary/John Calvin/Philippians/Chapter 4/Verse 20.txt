<p><b>20</b><i>Now to our God and Father</i> This may be taken as a general thanksgiving, by which he closes the epistle; or it may be viewed as bearing more particularly upon the last clause in reference to the liberality shewn to Paul. <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(259)</sup></span> For in respect of the assistance which the Philippians had afforded him, it became him to reckon himself indebted to them for it in such a manner as to acknowledge, that this aid had been afforded to them by the mercy of God. </p>
<p><span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;">(259)</span> “<blu><span lang="fr-FR" style="">La liberalite de laquelle les Philippiens auoyent vse enuers sainct Paul</span></blu>;” — “The liberality which the Philippians had exercised towards St. Paul.” </p>
