<p><b>12.</b>It is added, <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(99)</sup></span> <i>There is no one who doeth kindness</i> By this we are to understand, that they had put off every feeling of humanity. For as the best bond of mutual concord among us is the knowledge of God, (as he is the common Father of all, he wonderfully unites us, and without him there is nothing but disunion,) so inhumanity commonly follows where there is ignorance of God, as every one, when he despises others, loves and seeks his own good. </p>
<p><span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;">(99)</span> This verse is literally the <i>Septuagint,</i> and as to meaning, a correct version of the Hebrew. “All have gone out of the way<grk><blu><span lang="el-GR" style="">— πάντες ἐξέκλιναν”</span></blu></grk> “is in Hebrew <heb><blu><span lang="he-IL" style="">הכל</span></blu> <blu><span lang="he-IL" style="">סר</span></blu></heb>, “the whole (or every one) has turned aside,” or revolted, or apostatized. Then, “they have become unprofitable” or useless, is <heb><blu><span lang="he-IL" style="">נאלחו</span></blu></heb>, “they are become putrid,” or Corrupted, like putrified fruit or meat, therefore useless, not fit for what they were designed — to serve God and to promote their own and the good of others. Idolatry was evidently this putrescence. — <i>Ed.</i> </p>
