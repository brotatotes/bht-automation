<p><b>16.</b>Very striking is the sentence that is added from Isaiah, <i>Ruin and misery are in all their ways;</i> <span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;"><sup>(102)</sup></span> for it is a representation of ferociousness above measure barbarous, which produces solitude and waste by destroying every thing wherever it prevails: it is the same as the description which <i>Pliny</i> gives of<i>Domitian.</i> </p>
<p><span style="color:#800000;font-weight:bold;">(102)</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="cal" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:15">Romans 3:15</span> are taken from <span class="scriptRef" com="cal" lang="eng" ref="isa+59:7">Isaiah 59:7</span>. Both the Hebrew and the <i>Septuagint</i> are alike, but Paul has abbreviated them, and changed two words in the Greek version, having put<grk><blu><span lang="el-GR" style="">οξει᾿</span></blu></grk> for <grk><blu><span lang="el-GR" style="">ταχινοι,</span></blu></grk> and <grk><blu><span lang="el-GR" style="">ἔγνωσαν</span></blu></grk> for <grk><blu><span lang="el-GR" style="">ὀίδασι,</span></blu></grk> and has followed that version in leaving out “innocent” before “blood.” — <i>Ed.</i> </p>
