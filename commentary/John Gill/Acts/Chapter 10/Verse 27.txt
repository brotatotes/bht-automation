<p><strong>And as he talked with him</strong>,.... About the excessive respect he showed him, and his own infirmity and frailty, and unworthiness, as a creature:</p>
<p><strong>he went in</strong>; to Cornelius's house, at the door, and through the porch, to some interior room and apartment in it:</p>
<p><strong>and found many that were come together</strong>; besides his family, many of his relations and intimate friends, whom he had got together on this occasion, that they might receive some advantage, as well as himself.</p>
