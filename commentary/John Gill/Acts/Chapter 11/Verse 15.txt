<p><strong>And as I began to speak</strong>,...., That is, whilst he was speaking; the word "begin" with Luke, both, in his Gospel and in this history, is used not to denote the time or order of any action, but the thing itself; as in <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:21">Luke 4:21</span> for otherwise it was towards the close, and not at the beginning of his discourse, that what follows happened:</p>
<p><strong>the Holy Ghost fell on them, as on us at the beginning</strong>; of our ministry, since the death, resurrection, and ascension of Christ; meaning on the day of Pentecost, and that the Holy Ghost fell on Cornelius and those that were with him, in a like visible form, and in the same kind of gifts, as speaking with tongues, as on them.</p>
