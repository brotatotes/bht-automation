<p><strong>And some of them were men of Cyprus</strong>,.... That is, some of the preachers, that were scattered abroad, were Jews born at Cyprus: such was Barnabas particularly, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:36">Acts 4:36</span> though he was not among these, as appears from <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:22">Acts 11:22</span> "and Cyrene"; such were Simon that carried the cross after Christ, and his sons Alexander and Rufus, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:21">Mark 15:21</span> and others that heard the apostles speak with tongues on the day of Pentecost, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:10">Acts 2:10</span></p>
<p><strong>which when they were come to Antioch, spake unto the Grecians</strong>; or Hellenist Jews, who were born and brought up in Greece, and spoke the Greek language; though the Alexandrian copy, and the Syriac version, read "Greeks", as if they were native Greeks, and properly Gentiles, to whom these ministers spoke the word of the Lord; but the former seems most likely.</p>
<p><strong>Preaching the Lord Jesus</strong>; the dignity of his person, as the Son of God; what he did and suffered to obtain salvation for lost sinners; his resurrection from the dead, ascension to heaven, and intercession; the virtue of his blood for peace and pardon of his sacrifice for atonement of sin, and of his righteousness for justification.</p>
