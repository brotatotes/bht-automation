<p><strong>And when Herod had sought for him and found him not</strong>,.... Neither in the prison, nor in any part of the city:</p>
<p><strong>he examined the keepers</strong>; of the prison, and those that were upon the watch, whether they had not been accessary to his escape:</p>
<p><strong>and commanded that they should be put to death</strong>: or brought forth, not before a judge to be tried and judged, because they had been examined by Herod already; but either that they should be carried and laid in bonds, or be led forth to suffer punishment; and so our version directs, and which is confirmed by the Syriac; and the Greeks say n, that απαγωγη is a kind of punishment:</p>
<p><strong>and he went down from Judea to Caesarea</strong>; not Peter, but Herod:</p>
<p><strong>and there abode</strong>; of this journey of Agrippa's to Caesarea, Josephus makes mention o; this place was distant from Jerusalem six hundred furlongs, or seventy five miles p.</p>
<p>n Harpocratian. Lex. p. 32. o Antiqu. l. 19. c. 2. sect. 2. p De Bello Jud. l. 1. c. 3. sect. 5.</p>
