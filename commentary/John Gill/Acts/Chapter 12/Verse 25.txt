<p><strong>And Barnabas and Saul returned from Jerusalem</strong>,.... Two of Beza's exemplars, the Complutensian edition and the Syriac version, add, "unto Antioch"; and certain it is, that from thence they were sent, and thither they returned, for we find them there in the beginning of the next chapter:</p>
<p><strong>when they had fulfilled their ministry</strong>; which was the carrying the relief, or the money collected by the Christians at Antioch, for the brethren in Judea, on account of the famine that was there:</p>
<p><strong>and took with them John, whose surname was Mark</strong>: the son of Mary, at whose house the disciples were met together, and praying; and where Peter first went, after he was delivered from prison, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:12">Acts 12:12</span> him they brought with them from Jerusalem to Antioch, being a kinsman of Barnabas; and a man of promising gifts and usefulness, and judged to be a fit companion with them in their travels, for the spread of the Gospel.</p>
