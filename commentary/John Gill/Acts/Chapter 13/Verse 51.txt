As Christ directed his apostles to do;  :-.And came unto Iconium, a city in Lycaonia; here it is placed both by Ptolomy c and Strabo d; Pliny says e, that"there was a tetrarchy granted out of Lycaonia, on that part which borders on Galatia, consisting of fourteen cities, the most celebrated city being Iconium.''It was called by the Syrians היק ענא, "Ik-ona", which signifies "the bosom of sheep"; the country round about it being famous for feeding great numbers of sheep; and here afterwards was a church of Christ, a bosom for his sheep; it is now in the hands of the Turks, and is called "Conia", or "Cogne".c Geograph. l. 5. c. 6. d Ib. l. 12. e Nat. Hist. l. 5. c. 27.
