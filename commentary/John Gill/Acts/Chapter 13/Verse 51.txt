<p><strong>But they shook off the dust of their feet against them</strong>,.... As Christ directed his apostles to do; <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>.</p>
<p><strong>And came unto Iconium</strong>, a city in Lycaonia; here it is placed both by Ptolomy c and Strabo d; Pliny says e, that</p>
<p>"there was a tetrarchy granted out of Lycaonia, on that part which borders on Galatia, consisting of fourteen cities, the most celebrated city being Iconium.''</p>
<p>It was called by the Syrians היק ענא, "Ik-ona", which signifies "the bosom of sheep"; the country round about it being famous for feeding great numbers of sheep; and here afterwards was a church of Christ, a bosom for his sheep; it is now in the hands of the Turks, and is called "Conia", or "Cogne".</p>
<p>c Geograph. l. 5. c. 6. d Ib. l. 12. e Nat. Hist. l. 5. c. 27.</p>
