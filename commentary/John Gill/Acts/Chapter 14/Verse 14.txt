<p><strong>Which when the apostles Barnabas and Paul heard of</strong>,.... As they quickly did, such a multitude being about the doors of the house, where they were: it may be observed, that Barnabas is here called an apostle, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:4">Acts 14:4</span> and is placed before Paul, not as being greater than he, but because of the opinion these Heathens had of him: they rent their clothes; as the Jews did, and were obliged to do, when they heard any blasphemy; <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:4- :">Acts 14:4- :</span>.</p>
<p><strong>And ran in among the people</strong>; in great haste, showing great concern of mind, and much indignation and resentment, at what they were about to do:</p>
<p><strong>crying out</strong>; aloud, and with great vehemence, that all might hear, and to express the greater dislike of the action.</p>
