<p><strong>And Judas and Silas being also prophets themselves</strong>,.... As well as Paul and Barnabas, and others, that were at Antioch; see</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:1">Acts 13:1</span> and by prophets are meant, not only such who had the gift of foretelling things to come; though such there were, and these might have that gift; but such who were able to explain the prophecies of the Old Testament, give the true sense of the Scriptures, and open them to the edification of others; wherefore having such gifts, they made use of them; Beza's ancient copy adds, "full of the Holy Ghost":</p>
<p><strong>and exhorted the brethren with many words</strong>; which does not so much design the prolixity of their discourses, and the frequency of their ministrations, though they might preach both long and often; as the richness of the matter of them, as the Syriac version suggests, rendering it, "with a rich word"; with copiousness, fulness, and abundance of Gospel truths, with which they comforted the brethren, giving them many useful instructions and exhortations:</p>
<p><strong>and confirmed them</strong>; in the doctrines of the Gospel, and particularly in their Christian liberty, and freedom from the law of Moses, in which the false teachers had endeavoured to stagger them.</p>
