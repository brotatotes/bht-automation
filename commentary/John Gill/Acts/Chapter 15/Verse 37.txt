<p><strong>And Barnabas determined</strong>,.... Or consulted, and contrived in his own mind, and purposed within himself; the Alexandrian copy, and two of Beza's, the Vulgate Latin, Syriac, and Ethiopic versions read, "he would", or was willing; he had a mind, a very great desire,</p>
<p><strong>to take with them John, whose surname was Mark</strong>; of whom see</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:12">Acts 12:12</span> he being his sister's son, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="col+4:10">Colossians 4:10</span> him they brought with them to Antioch, when they returned from Jerusalem thither, where they had been sent by the church at Antioch, with money for the relief of the poor saints; see <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:29">Acts 11:29</span> and who seems to have come with them from Jerusalem again; for thither he went, when he parted from them at Pamphylia, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:13">Acts 13:13</span>.</p>
