<p><strong>And the sergeants told these words unto the magistrates</strong>,.... They returned to them, and acquainted them with what the prisoners said:</p>
<p><strong>and they feared when they heard that they were Romans</strong>; they were not concerned for the injury they had done them; nor for the injustice and cruelty they had been guilty of; nor did they fear the wrath of God, and a future judgment; but they were put into a panic, when they found the men they had so ill used were Romans; lest they should be called to an account by the Roman senate, and be found guilty, and have their places taken away from them, and their persons punished.</p>
