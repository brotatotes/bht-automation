<p><strong>Sorrowing most of all for the words which he spake</strong>,.... In</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:25">Acts 20:25</span> it filled them with sorrow to part with him; but this was increased, and made almost intolerable by what he said,</p>
<p><strong>that they should see his face no more</strong>: could they have hoped to have seen him again, it would have made their parting with him more easy; but to be told they should never see him more in this world, it cut them to the heart; which shows what a share the apostle had in their hearts and affections, and not without good reason: however, that they might have a sight of him as long as they could, they went along with him to see him take shipping, and to see the last of him.</p>
<p><strong>And they accompanied him unto the ship</strong>: which lay at Miletus waiting for him.</p>
