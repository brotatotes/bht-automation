<p><strong>And after those days we took up our carriages</strong>,.... Or prepared themselves, both for their journey, and for whatever trials and exercises they were to meet with; they took up their bundles, which hitherto were brought by sea, and now put them upon beasts, going by land from Caesarea:</p>
<p><strong>and went up to Jerusalem</strong>; which stood on higher ground, and was, as Josephus n says, six hundred furlongs, or seventy five miles distant.</p>
<p>n De Bello Jud. l. 1. c. 3. sect. 5.</p>
