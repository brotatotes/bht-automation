<p><strong>And when he had saluted them</strong>,.... James and the elders with him; which was either done by a kiss, as the Arabic version adds; or by asking of their health, and wishing a continuance of it, and all prosperity to attend them: the Ethiopic version reads,</p>
<p><strong>they saluted him</strong>; and no doubt the salutations were reciprocal:</p>
<p><strong>he declared particularly what things God had wrought among the Gentiles by his ministry</strong>; it is very likely that this account begins where that ends, which he had delivered in the presence of James, and others, some years ago, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:12">Acts 15:12</span> and takes in all his travels and ministry, and the success of it; not only in Syria, Cilicia, and Lycaonia, after he had set out from Antioch again, but in Macedonia, Achaia, and Asia; as at Philippi, Thessalonica, Berea, Athens, Corinth, Ephesus, and elsewhere: he declared, what multitudes of souls were converted, and what numbers of churches were planted; and this he ascribes not to himself, but to the power and grace of God, which had attended his ministry; he was only an instrument, God was the efficient, and ought to have the glory.</p>
