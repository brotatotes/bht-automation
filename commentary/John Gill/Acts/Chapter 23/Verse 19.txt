<p><strong>Then the chief captain took him by the hand</strong>,.... Some have thought that the reason of this was, that he expected that the young man had brought him a present in his hand, from Paul; but this is to represent him as a sordid mercenary man, which ought not to be said, without sufficient proof; rather this should be considered as an instance of civility and humanity, and what showed him to be a man of breeding and good manners; and might be done partly out of respect to Paul, and partly to encourage the young man to use freedom in the account he was about to give him:</p>
<p><strong>and went with him aside privately</strong>; concluding by his coming from Paul, and perceiving by the account of the centurion, that he had a secret to communicate to him; wherefore it was acting a wise and prudent part to take him into a private room, and hear what he had to say:</p>
<p><strong>and asked him, what is that thou hast to tell me</strong>? thereby giving the young man an opportunity, and encouraging him to relate the secret to him.</p>
