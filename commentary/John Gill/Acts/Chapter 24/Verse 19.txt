<p><strong>Who ought to have been here before thee</strong>,.... For they were the only persons who saw him in the temple, and what he was doing; and who by an hideous outcry raised a mob upon him, who took things upon hearsay from them:</p>
<p><strong>and object, if they had ought against me</strong>; either with respect to sedition, or blasphemy, error or heresy, if they were capable of proving anything.</p>
