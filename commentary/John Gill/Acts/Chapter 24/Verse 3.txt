<p><strong>We accept it always, and in all places</strong>,.... The sense is, that the Jews observed with pleasure the provident care the governor took of their nation, and at all times spoke well of him; and wherever they came commended his conduct, and owned the favours they received from him, and the blessings they enjoyed under his government: and then giving him his title of honour,</p>
<p><strong>most noble Felix</strong>; Tertullus adds, that this the Jews did</p>
<p><strong>with all thankfulness</strong>; as sensible of the obligations they were under to him; but this was all a farce, mere artifice, and wretched flattery.</p>
