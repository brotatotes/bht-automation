<p><strong>For if I be an offender</strong>,.... Against the law of Moses, or the temple at Jerusalem, or Caesar the Roman emperor:</p>
<p><strong>or have committed anything worthy of death</strong>; by the laws of the Romans, as sedition, murder, c.</p>
<p><strong>I refuse not to die</strong> signifying that he did not decline going to Jerusalem, either through any consciousness of guilt, or fear of death; for if anything could be proved against him, that was of a capital nature, he did not desire to escape death; he was ready to die for it; this was no subterfuge, or shift, to evade or defer justice:</p>
<p><strong>but if there be none of these things</strong>; to be found, or proved, and made to appear:</p>
<p><strong>whereof these accuse me</strong>; pointing to the Jews, that came down to be his accusers, and had laid many and grievous charges against him:</p>
<p><strong>no man may deliver me unto them</strong>; not justly, or according to the Roman laws; suggesting that Festus himself could not do it legally;</p>
<p><strong>I appeal unto Caesar</strong>; to this the apostle was induced, partly by the conduct of the governor, who seemed inclined to favour the Jews; and partly by the knowledge he might have of their intention to lie in wait for him, should he go up to Jerusalem; and chiefly by the vision he had had, which assured him that he must bear witness of Christ at Rome, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:11">Acts 23:11</span>.</p>
