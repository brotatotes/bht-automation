<p><strong>Of whom I have no certain thing</strong>,.... No certain crime, charge, or accusation; nothing of any moment or consequence, no particular thing, nothing but a heap of confused notions, of I know not who or what:</p>
<p><strong>to write unto my lord</strong>; meaning the Roman emperor, under whom he served as governor of Judea:</p>
<p><strong>wherefore I have brought him before you</strong>; the whole company then present:</p>
<p><strong>and especially before thee, O King Agrippa</strong>; as being not only a man of eminence, dignity, and authority, but of knowledge in such matters, which the Jews accused Paul of; see <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:2">Acts 26:2</span>.</p>
<p><strong>That after examination had</strong>; of Paul, and his case;</p>
<p><strong>I might have somewhat to write</strong>; concerning him, and the charges exhibited against him to the emperor.</p>
