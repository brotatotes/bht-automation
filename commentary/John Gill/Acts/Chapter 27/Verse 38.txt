<p><strong>And when they had eaten enough</strong>,....] Were satisfied, having eaten a full meal:</p>
<p><strong>they lightened the ship</strong>; of its burden, that it might the better carry them to the shore, and that by the following method:</p>
<p><strong>and cast out the wheat into the sea</strong>; which seems to have been part of the ship's provision; or one part of their lading, which they brought from Egypt, and were carrying to Italy: they had cast out some of the goods of the ship before, and also the tackling of the ship, and now, last of all, the wheat; for what was eatable they reserved till last, not knowing to what extremity they might be reduced.</p>
