<p><strong>And now, brethren</strong>,.... He calls them brethren, because they were so according to the flesh; and to testify his cordial love and affection for them.</p>
<p><strong>I wot</strong>, or "I know",</p>
<p><strong>that through ignorance ye did it</strong>; delivered up Jesus into the hands of Pilate; denied him to be the Messiah before him; preferred a murderer to him, and put him to death.</p>
<p><strong>As did also your rulers</strong>; the members of the sanhedrim, some of them; see <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:8">1 Corinthians 2:8</span> for others of them knew him to be the Messiah, to be sent of God, by the miracles he did, and yet blasphemously ascribed them to Satan; and so sinning against light and knowledge, in such a malicious manner, sinned the sin against the Holy Ghost, to which ignorance is here opposed; and which did not excuse from sin: nor was it itself without sin; nor is it opposed to any sin, but to this now mentioned.</p>
