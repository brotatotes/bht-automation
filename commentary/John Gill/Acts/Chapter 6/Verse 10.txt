<p><strong>And they were not able to resist the wisdom</strong>,.... In Beza's most ancient copy, and in another manuscript it is added, "which was in him"; that divine wisdom, which the Spirit of wisdom gave him; they were not a match for him with respect to the knowledge of divine things; they could not answer the wise arguments he made use of, fetched out of the Scriptures of truth, in which he was well versed, and had a large knowledge of:</p>
<p><strong>and the Spirit by which he spake</strong>; that is, the Holy Spirit, as the above exemplars of Beza, and the Ethiopic version read; the meaning is, they could not resist the Holy Spirit, by which Stephen spake, so as to overcome him, or put Stephen to silence, or confute him; otherwise they did resist him, or oppose themselves to him, but in vain, and without success; for they always resisted the Holy Ghost in Christ and in his apostles, as their fathers before them resisted him in the prophets, as Stephen observes to them, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:51">Acts 7:51</span> hereby was fulfilled what our Lord promised to his disciples, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:19">Matthew 10:19</span>.</p>
