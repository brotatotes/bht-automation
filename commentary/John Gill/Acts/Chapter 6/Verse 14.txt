<p><strong>For we have heard him say</strong>,.... The Ethiopic version adds, "besides"; but rather these words are a reason, giving evidence to, and supporting the general charge:</p>
<p><strong>that this Jesus of Nazareth</strong>; Stephen spoke of, and whom they so called by way of contempt:</p>
<p><strong>shall destroy this place</strong>; meaning the temple, as the Ethiopic version renders it; and is the same charge, the false witnesses at Christ's examination brought against him:</p>
<p><strong>and shall change the customs which Moses delivered us</strong>; that is, the rites, ceremonies, institutions, and appointments of the Mosaic dispensation; and yet this is no other, than what the Jews themselves say will be done, in the times of the Messiah; for they assert p, that</p>
<p>"awbl dytel, "in time to come" (i.e. in the days of the Messiah) all sacrifices shall cease, but the sacrifice of thanksgiving.''</p>
<p>p Vajikra Rabba, sect. 9. fol. 153. 1. &amp; sect. 27. fol. 168. 4.</p>
