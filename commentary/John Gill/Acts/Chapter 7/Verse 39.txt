<p><strong>To whom our fathers would not obey</strong>,.... But often murmured against him, and were disobedient to him, and to the oracles he delivered to them, and so to God, whose oracles they were:</p>
<p><strong>but thrust him from them</strong>; as one of the two Hebrews did, when he interposed to make up the difference between them; and which was an emblem and presage of what that people would afterwards do; <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:27">Acts 7:27</span></p>
<p><strong>and in their hearts turned back again into Egypt</strong>; they wished themselves there again, they lusted after the fish, the cucumbers, the melons, leeks, onions, and garlic there; and went so far as to move for a captain, and even to appoint one to lead them back thither again.</p>
