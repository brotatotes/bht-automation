<p><strong>And said, behold, I see the heavens opened</strong>,.... As they were at the baptism of Christ, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>:</p>
<p><strong>and the son of man standing at the right hand of God</strong>; he calls Jesus "the son of man"; a name by which he often called himself in his state of humiliation; and that though he was now glorified, it being the name of the Messiah in <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ps+80:17">Psalms 80:17</span> as was well known to the Jews; and this Stephen said to show that God was on his side, and to let them know what honour was done him, what divine supports and comforts he had, and that he was an eyewitness of Jesus, and of his being alive, and in glory.</p>
