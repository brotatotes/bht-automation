<p><strong>And it came to pass</strong>,.... Or so it was;</p>
<p><strong>that he tarried many days in Joppa</strong>; conversing with the saints, confirming the disciples, and preaching the Gospel, to the conversion of sinners; and his abode</p>
<p><strong>was with one Simon a tanner</strong>; it seems as if persons of this trade used to dwell in towns near the sea, as fit for their business; so we read of some at Sidon, a city on the sea coast, as Joppa was;</p>
<p>"it happened at Sidon that a certain בורסי, "tanner", (the same word here used, adopted into the Hebrew language,) died, and he had a brother a tanner, c. r''</p>
<p>where the Gemara s distinguishes between a great tanner and a little tanner the latter, the gloss says, is one that is poor and has but few skins: which of these Simon was, cannot be said. This business was very contemptible with the Jews; they say t,</p>
<p>"woe to him whose trade is a tanner:''</p>
<p>and further observe u that they never make one a king, nor a high priest: but their doctors many of them were of as mean trades, as shoemakers, skinners, c. <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span> and Simon the Athenian philosopher was σκυτοτομος "a leather cutter" w and according to the Ethiopic version, this our Simon was a shoemaker; with him Peter chose to abide, and not with Dorcas.</p>
