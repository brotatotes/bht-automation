<p><strong>And the sound of a trumpet</strong>,.... <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ex+19:16">Exodus 19:16</span>, which made it still more awful, as the sound of the trumpet will at the last day:</p>
<p><strong>and the voice of words</strong>; of the ten words, or decalogue; which was as an articulate voice, formed by angels; and, therefore, the law is called the word spoken by angels, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:2">Hebrews 2:2</span> and is represented, as the voice of God himself, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ex+20:1">Exodus 20:1</span> who made use of the ministry of angels to deliver the law to Moses; "which" voice is called</p>
<p>קול דברים, "the voice of words", in <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="de+4:12">Deuteronomy 4:12</span>, and this voice,</p>
<p><strong>they that heard, entreated that the word should not be spoken to them any more</strong>: fearing that they should die; wherefore they desired Moses to be their mediator, and draw nigh to God, and hear his words, and speak them to them, from him, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ex+20:19">Exodus 20:19</span>.</p>
