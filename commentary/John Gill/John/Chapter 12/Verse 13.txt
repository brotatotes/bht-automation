<p><strong>Took branches of palm trees</strong>,.... The Ethiopic version adds, "and young shoots from Jerusalem"; these grew in great plenty, on one part of the Mount of Olives, that next to Bethany, from whence that town had its name; for it signifies the house of dates, the fruit of the palm tree; <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. And as that tree was a sign of joy and victory, they carried branches of it in their hands, as they met the King Messiah, who was about to make his public entrance into Jerusalem, in triumph; and where by his sufferings and death, he should gain the victory over sin, Satan, the world, and death; and lay a solid foundation for joy and peace, to all that believe in him: the Jews say n,</p>
<p>"if a man takes באיין, (the very Greek word here used,) palm tree branches in his hands, we know that he is victorious.''</p>
<p>The Persic version reads, "branches of olives".</p>
<p><strong>And went forth to meet him, and cried</strong>; when they came up to him, and as he passed by them:</p>
<p><strong>Hosanna, blessed is the King of Israel, that cometh in the name of the Lord</strong>; <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>.</p>
<p>n Vajikra Rabba, sect. 30. fol. 170. 3.</p>
