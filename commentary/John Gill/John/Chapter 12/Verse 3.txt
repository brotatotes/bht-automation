<p><strong>Then took Mary a pound of ointment of spikenard very costly</strong>,.... Worth three hundred pence, according to Judas's estimation of it. This Mary was the other sister of Lazarus; <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>,</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, concerning the nature and value of this ointment:</p>
<p><strong>and anointed the feet of Jesus</strong>; as he lay upon the bed or couch, at supper:</p>
<p><strong>and wiped his feet with her hair</strong>; <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>.</p>
<p><strong>And the house was filled with the odour of the ointment</strong>; see <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="so+1:3">Song of Solomon 1:3</span>; ointment of spikenard was very odoriferous: this may be an emblem of the sweet savour of Christ, in the ministration of the Gospel, throughout the whole world.</p>
