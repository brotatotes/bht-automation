<p><strong>So when they had rowed</strong>,.... For the wind being contrary, they could not make use of their sails, but betook themselves to their oars, and by that means got</p>
<p><strong>about five and twenty, or thirty furlongs</strong>; which were three or four miles, or little more than a league; no further had they got, though they had been rowing from the time it was dark, to the fourth watch, which was after three o'clock in the morning; all this while they had been tossed in the sea;</p>
<p><strong>they saw Jesus walking on the sea</strong>;</p>
