<p><strong>Then the Jews sought him at the feast</strong>,.... Some to take him and kill him, and others to hear his doctrine, and see his miracles: for all expected him at the feast, knowing it was always his custom, as it was his duty, as an Israelite, to attend at it:</p>
<p><strong>and said, where is he</strong>? not naming his name; either through contempt, which might be the case of the far greater part; or through fear of the Jews; or because that he was so well known.</p>
