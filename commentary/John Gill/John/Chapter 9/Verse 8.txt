<p><strong>The neighbours therefore, and they which before had seen him</strong>,.... For it seems the blind man was not a stranger, one that came out of the country to the city to beg; but a native of Jerusalem, that had long lived in a certain neighbourhood in it, and was well known to be what he was;</p>
<p><strong>that he was blind</strong>; the Alexandrian copy, and one of Beza's exemplars, and the Vulgate Latin version read, "that he was a beggar"; to which agree the Syriac, Arabic, and Ethiopic versions: wherefore they</p>
<p><strong>said, is this not he that sat and begged</strong>? they particularly remark his begging posture; he was not laid all along, as the lame man in <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:2">Acts 3:2</span>; nor did he go from door to door, as others were used to do, but he sat in some certain place, as blind men generally did; see</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:30">Matthew 20:30</span>.</p>
