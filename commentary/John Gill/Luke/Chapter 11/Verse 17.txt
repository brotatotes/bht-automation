<p><strong>But he knowing their thoughts</strong>,.... Being God omniscient,</p>
<p><strong>said unto them</strong>; the following parables, as they are called in <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:23">Mark 3:23</span> or proverbial expressions, very pertinent to the purpose, and sufficient to set aside the base calumnies of the Pharisees:</p>
<p><strong>every kingdom divided against itself, is brought to desolation</strong>; in process of time, division will end in destruction; and as it does in the kingdoms of the world, of which there have been fatal instances, so it would in the kingdom of Satan, was there in it a division, which the calumny of the Pharisees supposes:</p>
<p><strong>and an house divided against an house, falleth</strong>. The Persic version renders it, "an house divided from the foundation, falls"; the sense is, a family, in which one part is opposed to the other, issues in the ruin of both; <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:23- :">Mark 3:23- :</span>,</p>
