<p><strong>From the blood of Abel</strong>,.... "Righteous Abel", as in</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:35">Matthew 23:35</span> and so read the Arabic version here, and two manuscripts in the Bodleian library, and three of Stephens's copies; the Persic version renders it, "innocent Abel": he is mentioned because he was the first man that was slain, and he was slain because of his righteousness.</p>
<p><strong>Unto the blood of Zacharias</strong>; in the Cambridge copy of Beza's, it is added, "the son of Barachias", as in <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:35">Matthew 23:35</span> and so the Arabic version, "the son of Barasciah"; who he was,</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:35- :">Matthew 23:35- :</span></p>
<p><strong>which perished between the altar and the temple</strong>; or "the house", that is, the holy place: and the Ethiopic version renders it, "the holy house"; here he died, being slain by the Jews; see the note, as before.</p>
<p><strong>Verily I say unto you, it shall be required of this generation</strong>; as it was at the time of the destruction of Jerusalem.</p>
