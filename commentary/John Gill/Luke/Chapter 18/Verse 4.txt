<p><strong>And he would not for a while</strong>,.... He would give no ear to her cries, nor take her cause in hand, nor right her wrongs, and clear her of her adversary:</p>
<p><strong>but afterward he said within himself</strong>; as he was considering the matter in his own mind, and reflecting on this woman's case and the frequent application she had made to him:</p>
<p><strong>though I fear not God, nor regard man</strong>; a monster in iniquity he was, to say so of himself; for though the character belongs to many, there are few that are so impudent in sin, as to take it to themselves, and glory in it.</p>
