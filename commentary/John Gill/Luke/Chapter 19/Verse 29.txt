<p><strong>And it came to pass when he was come nigh</strong>,.... The other evangelists, Matthew and Mark, add "unto Jerusalem"; but this Luke designs afterwards, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:37">Luke 19:37</span> and therefore here means, as is expressed, that he was come nigh</p>
<p><strong>to Bethphage and Bethany</strong>; two tracts of land which reached from Mount Olivet to Jerusalem; so that when he was there, he was nigh unto the city:</p>
<p><strong>at the mount, called [the Mount] of Olives</strong>; or "Elaion", as the Ethiopic version, which retains the Greek word for it; and which has its name from the great number of olive trees that grew upon it:</p>
<p><strong>he sent two of his disciples</strong>; their names are not mentioned by any of the evangelists, but it is very probable they were Peter and John; of the places here mentioned; <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:37- :">Luke 19:37- :</span>.</p>
