<p><strong>To be taxed with Mary his espoused wife</strong>,.... Whom also he had married, though he had not known her in a carnal way; she came along with him to be taxed and enrolled also, because she was of the same family of David, and belonged to the same city:</p>
<p><strong>being great with child;</strong> very near her time, and yet, though in such circumstances, was obliged by this edict, to come to Bethlehem; and the providence in it was, that she might give birth there, and so the prophecy in <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mic+5:2">Micah 5:2</span> have its accomplishment: this was an instance, and an example, of obedience to civil magistrates.</p>
