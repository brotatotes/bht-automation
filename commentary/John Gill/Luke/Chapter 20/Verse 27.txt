<p>Ver. 27 <strong>Then came to him certain of the Sadducees</strong>,.... That is, "to Jesus", as the Persic version expresses it; and it was the same day, as Matthew says, on which the disciples of the Pharisees, and the Herodians, had been with him, putting the question about tribute to him: <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:16">Matthew 22:16</span></p>
<p><strong>which deny that there is any resurrection</strong>; that is, of the dead; that there ever was any instance of it, or ever will be: this was the distinguishing tenet of that sect; see <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:8">Acts 23:8</span></p>
<p><strong>and they asked him</strong>, the following question, after they had put a case to him.</p>
