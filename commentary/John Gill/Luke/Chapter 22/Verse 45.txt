<p><strong>And when he rose from prayer</strong>,.... The Syriac version reads, "from his prayer", having finished it; and the Persic and Ethiopic versions read, "from the place of prayer", or where he prayed:</p>
<p><strong>and was come to his disciples</strong>; to the three, which he had left about the distance of a stone's cast:</p>
<p><strong>he found them sleeping for sorrow</strong>; on his account; for he had signified unto them, how exceeding sorrowful he was; and they might perceive by his looks and gestures, the anxiety and distress of mind he was in, which must needs affect them; and besides, he had given them some intimations of his being to be betrayed by one of them, and of his sufferings and death, and speedy departure from them; and because of these things, sorrow had filled their hearts, and this had induced heaviness and sleep upon them; <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>.</p>
