<p><strong>And as soon as it was day</strong>,....</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>.</p>
<p><strong>The elders of the people</strong>; or "the presbytery of the people", that were chosen from among the people to sit in the sanhedrim; the Israelites, as distinct from priests and Levites, and the doctors:</p>
<p><strong>and the chief priests and the Scribes came together</strong>; which made up the great sanhedrim, or council of the nation:</p>
<p><strong>and led him into their council</strong>; or sanhedrim, the place where the sanhedrim sat, which was in the temple, and in the chamber called</p>
<p>לשכת הגזית, "the paved stone chamber" n; here they usually met, and so the Persic version renders it, "where their congregation was daily there".</p>
<p>n Misna Saobedrin, c. 10. sect. 2. &amp; Middot, c. 5. sect. 3.</p>
