<p><strong>And Pilate asked him, saying</strong>,.... Observing that it was said, he had given himself out to be a king; for as for what regarded religion, he took no notice of it; he put this question to him,</p>
<p><strong>art thou the King of the Jews</strong>? that their prophecies speak of, and they have expected;</p>
<p><strong>and he answered him and said, thou sayest it</strong>; that is, thou sayest well; it is as thou sayest, I am the King of the Jews;</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span> though he did not leave this without an explanation; without informing him what sort of a king he was, and what kind of a kingdom he had; see <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:36">John 18:36</span> which made Pilate perfectly easy, and desirous to release him, as appears by what follows.</p>
