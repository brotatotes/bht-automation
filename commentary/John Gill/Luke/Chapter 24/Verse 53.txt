<p><strong>And were continually in the temple</strong>,.... At the time of worship, at the hours of prayer, or of public service; and perhaps always privately in an upper room in it, where they, with others, met frequently, and continued, with one accord, in prayer and supplication; <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>, a practice worthy of imitation, whether in the public or private way:</p>
<p><strong>praising, and blessing God</strong>; for his Son Jesus Christ, who had died for their sins, was risen again for their justification, and was now ascended into heaven, to be their advocate there; and for all spiritual bless his Gospel to every creature.</p>
<p><strong>Amen</strong>; so let him be praised, and blessed by all the saints, for all that is contained in this Gospel. In a manuscript copy of Beza's, it is added,</p>
<p>"the Gospel according to Saint Luke was published fifteen years after the ascension of Christ;''</p>
<p>See Gill on the title of this Gospel "Lu 1:1".</p>
