<p><strong>And all flesh shall see the salvation of God.</strong> "By the salvation of God" is meant, the Messiah, the Lord Jesus Christ, the Saviour of God's appointing and sending; and who is the author of that salvation which God resolved on, contrived, and approved of; and is his ordinance for salvation, unto the ends of the earth, for all his elect; <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:30">Luke 2:30</span> whom a great number among the Jews should, and did see, with their bodily eyes; and whom not only God's elect among them, but also all of them among the Gentiles, should behold with an eye of faith, for themselves, as their Saviour and Redeemer. It is matter of question, what passage is here referred to; whether</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="isa+40:5">Isaiah 40:5</span> or <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="isa+3:10">Isaiah 3:10</span> the latter comes nearest to the words, and the former stands closely connected with the expressions before cited; though it is usual with the New Testament writers, to join together passages, which stand in different places of the same prophet, and even which are in different books; <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:33">Romans 9:33</span> compared with <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="isa+8:14">Isaiah 8:14</span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:5">Matthew 21:5</span> compared with</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="isa+62:11">Isaiah 62:11</span> and that agreeably to the method used by Jewish writers r.</p>
<p>r Vid. Surenhus. Biblos Katallages, de modis Allegandi, &amp;c. Thes. 7. p. 45, 46, 319.</p>
