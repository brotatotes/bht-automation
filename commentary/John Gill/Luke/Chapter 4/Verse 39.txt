<p><strong>And he stood over her</strong>,.... "At her head", as the Persic version reads: he inclined himself towards her, to see how she did, and to take her by the hand, and lift her up;</p>
<p><strong>and rebuked the fever</strong>. The Syriac and Persic versions read, "her fever", that which was upon her;</p>
<p><strong>and it left her</strong>, as Mark says, "immediately"; as soon as ever he had touched her hand, and rebuked the fever, and bid it be gone:</p>
<p><strong>and immediately she arose, and ministered unto them</strong>;</p>
