<p><strong>And looking round about upon them all</strong>,.... The Scribes and Pharisees, and the rest of the people in the synagogue;</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>.</p>
<p><strong>he said to the man</strong>; who had the withered hand,</p>
<p><strong>stretch forth thy hand, and he did so</strong>; he stretched it out, as the Syriac and Persic versions render it, which he was not able to do before:</p>
<p><strong>and his hand was restored well as the other</strong>; the phrase, "well as the other", is left out in one copy, and in the Vulgate Latin version; and so is the word "well" in the Syriac and Arabic versions; and the word "immediately" is added in the Ethiopic version. And certain it is, that his withered hand was restored sound and well as the other, directly.</p>
