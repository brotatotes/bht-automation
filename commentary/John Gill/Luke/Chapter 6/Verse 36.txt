<p><strong>Be ye therefore merciful</strong>,.... Tenderhearted, kind, beneficent to all men, friends and foes:</p>
<p><strong>as your Father also is merciful</strong>; that is your Father which is in heaven; who is good to all, and his tender mercies are over all his works: nothing is more common in Zohar y, and the Talmud z than to express the Divine Being by no other name, than "the Merciful"; אמר</p>
<p>רחמנא, "the Merciful said" so, and so; that is, God: and so the Arabians generally begin their books and chapters with these words, "in the name of God, exceeding merciful", or "the merciful commiserator": a saying much like to this in the text, is the Targum of Jonathan, on</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="le+22:28">Leviticus 22:28</span>.</p>
<p>"O my people, the children of "Israel, as your father",</p>
<p>רחמן, "is merciful" in heaven, so be ye merciful on earth.''</p>
<p>y Zohar in Lev. fol. 2. 2. &amp; 9. 4. &amp; 20. 1. &amp; 22. 1. z T. Bab. Moed Katon, fol. 15. 2.</p>
