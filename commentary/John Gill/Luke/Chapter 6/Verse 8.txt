<p><strong>But he knew their thoughts</strong>,.... Being the omniscient God; though they had said nothing of their intentions, he knew what they designed, should he heal the man with his withered hand, as they expected he would:</p>
<p><strong>and said to the man which had the withered hand, rise up, and stand forth in the midst</strong>. The Syriac and Persic versions add, "of the synagogue", and which is the true sense; <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span></p>
<p><strong>and he arose and stood forth</strong>; he rose up from his seat, and stood up in the midst of the synagogue, and of the people, that he might be seen of all.</p>
