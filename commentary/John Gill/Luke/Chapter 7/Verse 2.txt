<p><strong>And a certain centurion's servant</strong>,.... The same that Matthew makes mention of, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:5">Matthew 8:5</span>,</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:5- :">Matthew 8:5- :</span>.</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:5- :">Matthew 8:5- :</span>.</p>
<p><strong>who was dear unto him</strong>; to the centurion, being an honest, upright, faithful, and obliging servant; as Tabi was to Rabban Gamaliel, of whom his master said l,</p>
<p>"Tabi my servant, is not as other servants, כשר היה, "he is upright".''</p>
<p><strong>was sick</strong>: of a palsy; see <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:6">Matthew 8:6</span>,</p>
<p><strong>and ready to die</strong>; in all appearance his case was desperate, and there was no help for him by any human means, which makes the following cure, the more remarkable.</p>
<p>l T. Bab. Beracot, fol. 16. 2.</p>
