<p><strong>For he loveth our nation</strong>,.... The Jewish nation, which was Christ's nation, as well as theirs, he being a Jew; see <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:35">John 18:35</span>. This they mention as an argument to induce him to have a regard to the centurion, though he was a Gentile; since he was a friend of the Jews, and well affected and disposed to them, which was very rare: it was not common for the Gentiles to love the Jews, any more than the Jews the Gentiles; there was an hatred, yea, an enmity between them; but this man, very likely, was a proselyte to their religion, as the following instance seems to show:</p>
<p><strong>and he hath built us a synagogue</strong>; at his own private charge, and by the assistance of his soldiers under him, whom he might employ in this work: sometimes a single person built a synagogue at his own expense, and gave it to the citizens; of which the Jews say, o</p>
<p>"if a man builds an house, and afterwards devotes it to a synagogue, it is as a synagogue.''</p>
<p>o Piske Harosh Megilia, c. 4. art. 1.</p>
