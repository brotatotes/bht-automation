<p><strong>And he said unto them, where is your faith</strong>?.... That is, he said so to his disciples, who had professed faith in him, but now discovered very little:</p>
<p><strong>and they being afraid, wondered</strong>; being filled with awful sense of his majesty, were amazed at his power and authority, in rebuking the wind and sea, which at once obeyed him, and were still:</p>
<p><strong>saying one another</strong>; among themselves, privately:</p>
<p><strong>what manner of man is this? for he commandeth even the winds and water</strong>; or the sea, as the Vulgate Latin. The Syriac version reads both, "the floods and the sea";</p>
<p><strong>and they obey him</strong>: according to Matthew, these words seem to be spoken by the men of the ship, the mariners; but here, according to Luke, they seem to be the words of the disciples;</p>
