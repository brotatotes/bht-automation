<p><strong>Return to thine own house</strong>,.... Which very likely was in the city of Gadara, whither he went, and throughout the whole of which he published the account of the dispossession of the devils from him: Mark adds, "to thy friends"; relations, acquaintance, and countrymen:</p>
<p><strong>and show how great things God hath done unto thee</strong>; for none but God could effect such things, tacitly suggesting to him hereby, that he himself was God. Mark adds, "and hath had compassion on thee": signifying, that what he had done for him, did not arise from merit in the man, but from mercy in himself; <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>.</p>
<p><strong>And he went his way</strong>; he obeyed the orders of Christ, as love and gratitude obliged him:</p>
<p><strong>and published throughout the whole city</strong>; of Gadara, and not only there, but in the rest of the ten cities, called Decapolis, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:20">Mark 5:20</span> one of which was this of Gadara, as Pliny relates o:</p>
<p><strong>how great things Jesus had done unto him</strong>; having cast out a legion of devils from him, clothed him, and brought him to his right mind; and had not only delivered his body from a diabolical possession, but had given him spiritual and saving instructions for his soul, on which he had wrought a real work of grace.</p>
<p>o Lib. 5. c. 18.</p>
