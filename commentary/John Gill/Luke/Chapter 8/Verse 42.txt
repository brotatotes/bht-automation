<p><strong>For he had one only daughter</strong>,.... And so exceedingly dear to him:</p>
<p><strong>about twelve years of age</strong>;</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span></p>
<p><strong>And she lay a dying</strong>, or "was near death", as the Syriac and Persic versions; or "was just ready to die", as the Ethiopic version. The Vulgate Latin and Arabic versions render it, "she was dead", or "now dead", and which agrees with <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:18">Matthew 9:18</span>.</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:18- :">Matthew 9:18- :</span>.</p>
<p><strong>but as he went</strong>; along the streets of Capernaum, from Matthew's house; where he had been entertained with his disciples, and others, and where he had a conversation with some of the Pharisees and John's disciples, to the ruler's house:</p>
<p><strong>the people thronged him</strong>; such a vast multitude followed him to see the cure, that he was even crowded, and so pressed on all sides, that it was difficult to walk along.</p>
