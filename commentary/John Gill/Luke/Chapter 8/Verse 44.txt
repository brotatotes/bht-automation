<p><strong>Came behind him</strong>,.... In the press and crowd of people, being ashamed to come before him, and tell him her case:</p>
<p><strong>and touched the border of his garment</strong> the fringe the Jews were obliged to wear at the bottom of their garments, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="nu+15:38">Numbers 15:38</span> and which the more religious sort did, for by this they were distinguished from the common people: it is asked p,</p>
<p>"who is a plebeian, or one of the common people? every one that does not read "Keriat Shema", (i.e. hear, O Israel), c. <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="de+6:4">Deuteronomy 6:4</span> morning and evening, with the blessings belonging to it, the words of R. Meir: but the wise men say, whoever does not put on the "Tephillin" (the frontlets, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="de+6:8">Deuteronomy 6:8</span>) Ben Azzai says, whoever has not ציצית, "the fringe" on his garment''</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="de+6:8- :">Deuteronomy 6:8- :</span>. This woman was persuaded in her own mind, if she could but touch the clothes of Christ, she should be healed, and accordingly she was:</p>
<p><strong>and immediately her issue of blood staunched</strong> stopped, and was dried up; <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:28">Mark 5:28</span>.</p>
