<p><strong>And Jesus said, who touched me</strong>?.... This he said, not as ignorant of the person that had done it, but in order to discover her to the people, and the cure she had received, as well as her faith; <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>.</p>
<p><strong>When all denied</strong>; both the disciples and the multitude, as many as were near him, and who might be thought to have done it; all excepting the woman, who afterwards came and declared it; for it is very likely, that as soon as she had touched his garment, and got her cure, she drew further off:</p>
<p><strong>Peter, and they that were with him</strong>; the rest of the disciples, who were in company together, as appears from <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:31">Mark 5:31</span></p>
<p><strong>Said, Master, the multitude throng thee, and press thee, and sayest thou, who touched me</strong>? or "my garment", as the Ethiopic version reads;</p>
