<p><strong>Then he called his twelve disciples together</strong>,.... The Persic version reads, "all his twelve disciples", the other nine, besides the three that were with him, when he raised Jairus's daughter, recorded in the foregoing chapter; the Vulgate Latin, Arabic, and Ethiopic versions read, "the twelve apostles", for so Christ had named his disciples; <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>. The Syriac version only reads, "his own twelve"; and this is agreeably to Luke's way of speaking; see <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:1">Luke 8:1</span>.</p>
<p><strong>And gave them power and authority over all devils</strong>; that is, all kinds of devils, particularly to cast them out of the bodies of men, possessed by them:</p>
<p><strong>and to cure diseases</strong>; of all sorts.</p>
