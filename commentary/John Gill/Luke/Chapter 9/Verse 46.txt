<p><strong>Then there arose a reasoning among them</strong>,.... The Vulgate Latin, Syriac, and Arabic versions read, "a thought entered into them"; suggested very likely by Satan, which broke out into words, and issued in a warm dispute among them; and this was in the way, as they were travelling from Caesarea Philippi, to Capernaum; see <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:33">Mark 9:33</span>.</p>
<p><strong>Which of them should be greatest</strong>; that is, "in the kingdom of heaven", as in <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:1">Matthew 18:1</span> in the kingdom of the Messiah, which they expected would be a temporal one: wherefore the dispute was not about degrees in glory, nor in grace; nor who should be the greatest apostle and preacher of the Gospel; but who should be prime minister to the king Messiah, when he should set up his monarchy in all its grandeur and glory.</p>
