<p><strong>And sent messengers before his face</strong>,.... Who very likely were his two disciples, James and John, since they so highly resented the ill treatment their master met with from the Samaritans:</p>
<p><strong>and they went</strong>; before him:</p>
<p><strong>and entered into a village of the Samaritans</strong>; or "city", as the Vulgate Latin, Persic, and Ethiopic versions read, and so one of Stephens's copies; which lay in the way from Galilee to Judea, where the disciples had been forbid to enter, that is, in order to preach,</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:5">Matthew 10:5</span></p>
<p><strong>To make ready for him</strong>; to prepare a lodging, and proper food for him and his disciples, as they passed on in their journey, for his intention was not to make any stay there.</p>
