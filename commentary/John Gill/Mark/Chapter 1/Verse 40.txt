<p><strong>And there came a leper to him</strong>,.... After he was come down from a certain mountain, in Galilee, where he had been preaching to the people, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:1">Matthew 8:1</span>, and when be was in a certain city, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:12">Luke 5:12</span>, either Capernaum, or some other city of Galilee. This man was full of leprosy, as Luke says, and very probably deemed incurable; of the nature and symptoms of the leprosy, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:12- :">Luke 5:12- :</span>,</p>
<p><strong>beseeching him</strong>; to cure him of his leprosy:</p>
<p><strong>and kneeling down to him</strong>; in token of submission, respect, and reverence, and to worship him:</p>
<p><strong>and saying unto him, if thou wilt thou canst make me clean</strong>;</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:12- :">Luke 5:12- :</span>. Mark omits the word "Lord".</p>
