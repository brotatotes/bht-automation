<p><strong>And when they came nigh to Jerusalem</strong>,.... The Syriac and Ethiopic versions read, "when he came nigh"; that is, Jesus; though not without his disciples, nor the multitude:</p>
<p><strong>unto Bethphage and Bethany</strong>; two places so called, near Jerusalem: Bethphage began where Bethany ended, and reached to the city itself. The Vulgate Latin only makes mention of Bethany;</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>.</p>
<p><strong>At the Mount of Olives</strong>; near which, the above places were:</p>
<p><strong>he sendeth forth two of his disciples</strong>; perhaps Peter and John.</p>
