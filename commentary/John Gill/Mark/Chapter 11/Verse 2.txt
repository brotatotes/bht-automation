<p><strong>And saith unto them, go your way into the village</strong>,.... Either of Bethany or of Nob. The Ethiopic version renders it "the city", and so reads a copy of Stephens's: some have thought the city of Jerusalem is intended, but without any reason;</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>;</p>
<p><strong>over against you</strong>. The Syriac and Persic versions read, "over against us": the sense is the same; for Christ and his disciples were together: this suits with either of the above mentioned places:</p>
<p><strong>and as soon as ye be entered into it</strong>; are come to the town's end, and to one of the first houses in it,</p>
<p><strong>ye shall find a colt tied</strong>: Matthew says, "an ass tied, and a colt with her", <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:2">Matthew 21:2</span>; both no doubt true:</p>
<p><strong>whereon never man sat</strong>; which had never been backed and broke, and which makes it the more wonderful, that Christ should choose to ride upon it, and that that should quietly carry him:</p>
<p><strong>loose him, and bring him</strong>; that is, away to me.</p>
