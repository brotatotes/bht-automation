<p><strong>But if we shall say, of men</strong>,.... That John's baptism was an human invention, and he had no authority from God to preach and administer it,</p>
<p><strong>they feared the people</strong>; lest being enraged thereby they should, at once, rise up, and destroy them:</p>
<p><strong>for all men counted John, that he was a prophet indeed</strong>; a real prophet, one truly sent from God, and had his commission and credentials from him: this was the general sentiment of the people;</p>
