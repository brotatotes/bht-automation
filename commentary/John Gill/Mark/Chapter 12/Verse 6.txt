<p><strong>Having yet therefore one son, his well beloved</strong>,.... The Messiah, the Lord Jesus Christ, who is the one, and only Son of God his Father, his only begotten Son, for he has no other Son in the same way of filiation; and who is his dear Son, the Son of his love, who was loved by him before the foundation of the world; and whom he declared to be his beloved Son, both at his baptism, and at his transfiguration upon the mount, by a voice from heaven: this Son he having with him, in his bosom, as one brought up with him, and rejoicing before him,</p>
<p><strong>he sent him also last unto them</strong>; after all the prophets had been with them, when the last days were come, the end of the Jewish state, civil and ecclesiastical; see <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:1">Hebrews 1:1</span>;</p>
<p><strong>saying, they will reverence my son</strong>. The Syriac, Arabic, and Persic versions read, "perhaps they will reverence my son", as in <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:13">Luke 20:13</span>;</p>
