<p><strong>But they said not on the feast day</strong>,.... The feast of the passover, and of unleavened bread, which was nigh at hand, and would be two days hence, when there would be a great concourse of people from all parts to keep it: and therefore they did not choose to seize him, and put him to death at that time,</p>
<p><strong>lest there should be an uproar of the people</strong>; or among them, lest they should rise in his favour, and rescue him out of their hands;</p>
