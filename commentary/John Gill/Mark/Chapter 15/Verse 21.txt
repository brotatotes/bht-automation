<p><strong>And they compel one Simon a Cyrenian</strong>,....</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>;</p>
<p><strong>who passed by</strong>; as they were leading Jesus to be crucified:</p>
<p><strong>coming out of the country</strong>; from some country village hard by, according to the Syriac, and Vulgate Latin versions; or out of the field, as the Persic and Ethiopic: he might have been in the field, about some rural business; or, as Dr. Lightfoot conjectures, to fetch wood from thence, which was lawful to be done on a feast day, with some provisos, according to the Jewish canon, which runs thus t;</p>
<p>"they may bring wood out of the field, (i.e. on a feast day, as this was,) of that which is gathered together, and out of a place that is fenced about, and even of that which is scattered abroad: what is a fenced place? whatever is near to a city, the words of R. Judah. R. Jose says, whatever they go into by a door, and even within the border of the sabbath.''</p>
<p>And according to the commentators u, it must be wood that is gathered together, and that lies not in an open field, but in a fenced place, and this near the city; at least with in two thousand cubits, a sabbath day's journey.</p>
<p><strong>The father of Alexander and Rufus</strong>; who were men well known when Mark wrote his Gospel, and very likely men of eminence among Christians: mention is made of Alexander in <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:33">Acts 19:33</span> and of Rufus, in</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:13">Romans 16:13</span>, which some have thought the same as here; but whether they are or not, is not certain: however, they obliged "Simon"</p>
<p><strong>to bear his cross</strong>: the cross of Christ, after him;</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:13- :">Romans 16:13- :</span>.</p>
