<p><strong>Joseph of Arimathea, an honourable counsellor</strong>,.... A man of a good aspect, well dressed, and that behaved well and honourably in his office, as a counsellor: he seems to have been a priest, and one of the bench of priests that sat in the high priest's chamber, which is called, לשכת בלווטי, "the chamber of the counsellors" d; with whom he advised there, in matters of moment:</p>
<p><strong>which also waited for the kingdom of God</strong>; for the coming and kingdom of the Messiah, for the Gospel dispensation, the world to come, the Jews were so much in expectation of.</p>
<p><strong>Came and went in boldly unto Pilate</strong>; not now ashamed of Christ, or afraid openly to appear in his cause, and declare himself a lover of him, a believer in him, and a disciple of his, though he formerly was:</p>
<p><strong>and craved the body of Jesus</strong>; desired leave to take it down from the cross, and bury it; <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>.</p>
<p>d T. Bab. Yoma, fol. 3. 2. &amp; Hieros. Yoma, fol. 38. 3.</p>
