<p><strong>For they all saw him, and were troubled</strong>,.... Had it been only seen by one, it might have been thought a fancy, and the effect of mere imagination; but as every one saw it, it was out of all doubt that so it was, and which gave them the greater concern:</p>
<p><strong>and immediately he talked with them</strong>; as soon as they saw him, "that very moment", as the Syriac renders it; that so by hearing his voice their fears might be allayed:</p>
<p><strong>and saith unto them, be of good cheer, it is I, be not afraid</strong>;</p>
