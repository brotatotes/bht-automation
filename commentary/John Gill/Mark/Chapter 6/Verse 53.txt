<p><strong>And when they had passed over</strong>,.... The lake of Gennesaret, or sea of "Galilee",</p>
<p><strong>they came into the land of Gennesaret</strong>; <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>; to Capernaum, as appears from <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:17">John 6:17</span> for Capernaum was in the land of Gennesaret; to which agrees what Josephus says, that the land of Gennesaret was watered with a very excellent spring, which the inhabitants of that place called Capernaum h:</p>
<p><strong>and drew to the shore</strong>: this is omitted in the Syriac and Persic versions: and the Arabic reads, "and they cast anchors".</p>
<p>h De Bello Jud. l. 3. c. 35.</p>
