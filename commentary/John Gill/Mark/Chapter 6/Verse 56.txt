And whithersoever he entered, into villages, or cities, or country,.... Whether in smaller towns, or larger cities, or the fields, where were houses, here and there one:they laid the sick in the streets; or "markets", in any public places:and besought him that they but might touch the border of his garment; if they might not be admitted to touch his person, or he did not choose to lay his hands on them:and as many as touched him; or "it", the border of his garment, as they desired:were made whole; of whatsoever sickness, or disease, they were afflicted with;  :-.
