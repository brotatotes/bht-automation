<p><strong>In those days</strong>,.... The Ethiopic version reads, on that day; as if it was on the same day that the deaf man was healed; and so it might be; and on the third day from Christ's coming into those parts; and so is very properly expressed, "in those days"; see <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:31">Mark 7:31</span>, compared with the following verse:</p>
<p><strong>the multitude being very great</strong>: for the number of men that ate, when the following miracle was wrought, were about four thousand; see <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:9">Mark 8:9</span>. The Vulgate Latin, Arabic, and Ethiopic versions add, "again"; referring to the former miracle of the five thousand, who were fed with five loaves, and two fishes, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:44">Mark 6:44</span>.</p>
<p><strong>And having nothing to eat</strong>; what they might have brought with them being expended, and they in a desert, where nothing was to be had, nor bought for money:</p>
<p><strong>Jesus called his disciples to him, and saith unto them</strong>;</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:44- :">Mark 6:44- :</span>.</p>
