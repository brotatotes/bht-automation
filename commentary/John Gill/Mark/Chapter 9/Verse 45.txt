<p><strong>And if thy foot offend thee, cut it off</strong>,.... Such who are that to men, as the foot is to the body, the support of them through whom they have their maintenance and subsistence; and yet these, if they are a means of causing them to stumble and tail, or of leading out of the ways of Christ, and off from him, their company is to be shunned and abstained from;</p>
<p><strong>it is better for thee to enter halt into life</strong>. The Vulgate Latin version reads, "eternal life", which is undoubtedly intended by "life"; and so reads the Cambridge copy of Beza's; and the meaning is, that it is better to go alone without such company into heaven,</p>
<p><strong>than having two feet to be cast into hell, into the fire that never shall be quenched</strong>; <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="+:-"> :-</span>.</p>
