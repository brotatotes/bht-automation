<p><strong>And Peter answered and said to Jesus</strong>,.... He addressed himself to him, as being more familiar with him; as also because he was the principal person: wherefore he says,</p>
<p><strong>master, it is good for us to be here</strong>: the company and conversation were exceeding agreeable to him and his fellow disciples; and the glory that Christ appeared in surpassed every thing they had seen before:</p>
<p><strong>and let us make three tabernacles</strong>; or, as the Syriac, Arabic, and Ethiopic versions read, "and we will make", c. expressing not a petition, but a resolution to which the Persic version premises, "if thou wilt give us commandment"; submitting it to the will of Christ:</p>
<p><strong>one for thee, and one for Moses, and one for Elias</strong>;</p>
