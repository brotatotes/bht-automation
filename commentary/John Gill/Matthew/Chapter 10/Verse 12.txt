<p><strong>And when ye come into an house</strong>,.... Or the "house"; that is, the house of an hospitable man, when, upon inquiry, found out:</p>
<p><strong>salute it</strong>; meaning the inhabitants of it; or, as the Persic version reads, those of the household, especially the master of the family. Some copies add, saying, peace be to this house, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:5">Luke 10:5</span> and so read the Vulgate Latin, and Munster's Hebrew Gospel; and is a very just, and proper explanation of saluting: for the usual form of salutation among the Jews was in such words; of which</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:5- :">Luke 10:5- :</span> by which is meant all kind of happiness, and prosperity, temporal, spiritual, and eternal.</p>
