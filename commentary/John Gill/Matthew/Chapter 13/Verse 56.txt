<p><strong>And his sisters</strong>,.... Whose names, according to Epiphanius b, were Mary and Salome; whom he supposes were the daughters of Joseph, by a former wife; but rather of Alphaeus or Cleophas.</p>
<p><strong>Are they not all with us?</strong> Lived in the same town with them, were well known by them, and familiar with them.</p>
<p><strong>Whence then hath this man all these things?</strong> His wisdom and his mighty works; for since he had not them from any of their schools, and nurseries of learning, from their learned doctors and wise men; and could not have received them from his parents, and near relations, they could not devise from whence he should have them.</p>
<p>b Contr. Haeres. Tom. 2. 1. 3. Haeres. 78. &amp;. lib. Ancorat.</p>
