<p>Ver. 7 <strong>And they reasoned among themselves</strong>,.... Either what should be the meaning of this caution of Christ's, and upon what account he should say this to them; or they were anxiously concerned what they should do for provision:</p>
<p><strong>saying, because we have taken no bread</strong>; for the phrase, "it is", is a supplement, and is not in the original text, which confines the sense to the first way of interpretation; the words may be read without it, and confirms the other sense, and which receives strength from what follows.</p>
