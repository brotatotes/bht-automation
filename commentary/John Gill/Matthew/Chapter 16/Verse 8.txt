<p><strong>Which when Jesus perceived</strong>,.... Without hearing any of their debates, but by his omniscience; for he knew the doubts and unbelief, and anxious solicitude of their minds, as well as their private reasonings one with another:</p>
<p><strong>he said unto them, O ye of little faith</strong>; a phrase used upon a like occasion, when he would dissuade his disciples from an anxious distressing care about a livelihood, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:30">Matthew 6:30</span></p>
<p>See Gill "Mt 6:30":</p>
<p><strong>why reason ye among yourselves, because ye have brought no bread?</strong> blaming one another for your negligence and forgetfulness in this matter; distressing your minds, as if you should be famished and starved, because ye have not brought a quantity of bread, as you used to do with you.</p>
