<p><strong>Then went out to him Jerusalem</strong>,.... The uncommon appearance of this person, the oddness of his dress, the austerity of his life, together with the awfulness and importance of his doctrine, and the novelty of the ordinance of baptism he administered, and the Jews having had no prophet for some hundreds of years, and imagining he might be the Messiah, quickly drew large numbers of people to him. Some copies read "all Jerusalem": that is, the inhabitants of that city, a very large number of them; and "all Judea", a great number of people from all parts of that country. "All" is here put for "many". And</p>
<p><strong>all the region round about Jordan</strong>; multitudes from thence, which seems to be the same country with that which is called "beyond Jordan", <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:25">Matthew 4:25</span> and is distinguished from Judea as here. The Septuagint in <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="2ch+4:17">2 Chronicles 4:17</span> use the same phrase the Evangelist does here, and likewise in <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="ge+13:10">Genesis 13:10</span>.</p>
