<p><strong>Or if he ask a fish, will he give him a serpent</strong>?] Which is somewhat like a fish, especially an eel. Fish and bread are mentioned, because these were common food; see <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:41">Mark 6:41</span></p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:13">John 21:13</span> and particularly in Galilee, a fish country, where Christ now was, and from whence he had called his disciples, who were fishermen. In <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:12">Luke 11:12</span> it is added, "or if he shall ask an egg, will he offer him a scorpion?" which is used, as the rest, to show the absurdity and inhumanity of such conduct; and that indeed nothing of this kind is to be found among men, unless it be among monsters in nature.</p>
