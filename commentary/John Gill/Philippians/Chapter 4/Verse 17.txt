<p><strong>Not because I desire a gift</strong>,.... This commendation of them he entered into, not because he desired another present to be made to him, either by them or others; he was not a man of such a disposition, he was not like one of those that could never have enough; he was fully satisfied and highly contented with what he had; he was not like the false teachers, that made merchandise of men; he sought not theirs, but them:</p>
<p><strong>but I desire fruit that may abound to your account</strong>; he had planted them, or had been an instrument in planting of them, as trees of righteousness, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="isa+61:3">Isaiah 61:3</span>; and his great desire was to see fruits of righteousness grow upon them, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="php+1:11">Philippians 1:11</span>; by which sometimes are meant acts of beneficence, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:10">2 Corinthians 9:10</span>; and that these might be abundant and turn to their profit and advantage, as such fruit does; for God does not forget to recompence acts of bounty, and labours of love, but if even a cup of cold water is given to a prophet or minister of Christ, on account of his being so, it shall have its reward in the issue of things, upon the casting up of accounts, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:42">Matthew 10:42</span>; for the apostle still has reference unto that; his view was, that the balance might be on their side, and that much might be received by them; so that it was not for himself, but for their encouragement and future good, he said this; for as for himself he adds,</p>
