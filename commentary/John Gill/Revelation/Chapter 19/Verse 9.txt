<p><strong>And he saith unto me, write</strong>,.... What follows, because of the importance of it, and to show the certainty of it, and that it may be regarded and remembered: the person speaking is either the voice from the throne, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="re+19:5">Revelation 19:5</span> or the angel that attended John all along, and showed him this revelation, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="re+1:1">Revelation 1:1</span> or the angel that proposed to show him the judgment of the great whore, <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="re+17:1">Revelation 17:1</span>.</p>
<p><strong>Blessed are they which are called to the marriage supper of the Lamb</strong>; by which is meant the Gospel ministry and ordinances, and communion in them, to which the Jews will be called to partake of in the latter day; these at the first of the Gospel dispensation are called a "dinner", to which, the Jews were invited, but refused to come, and now a "supper", because made in the evening of that dispensation; to which being called with an effectual calling, they will come and partake of it; on which account they are pronounced blessed, being the bride, the Lamb's wife, having on his righteousness, partaking of his benefits, and being called unto, and made meet for eternal glory and happiness; or else these may design converted Gentiles, who will be invited to join with them, and will.</p>
<p><strong>And he saith unto me, these are the true sayings of God</strong>; the Syriac version reads, "these my true words are of God"; being true, it is plain they are of God, and being of God, it is certain they are true; for he is the God of truth, and cannot lie, and therefore may be depended upon.</p>
