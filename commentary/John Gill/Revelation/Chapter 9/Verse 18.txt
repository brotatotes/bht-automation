<p><strong>And by these three was the third part of men killed</strong>,.... The Alexandrian copy, and some others, the Complutensian edition, the Vulgate Latin, Syriac, and Ethiopic versions, read, "by these three plagues": as the western Roman empire is in the preceding chapter frequently expressed by the third part of several things, see <span class="scriptRef" com="geb" lang="eng" ref="re+8:7">Revelation 8:7</span>; so here the eastern Roman empire by the third part of men; vast numbers of the inhabitants of which were destroyed by the Turks, through the use of guns, out of which issued fire, smoke, and brimstone; and Constantinople, the metropolis of it, was taken in this way, as before observed, with the taking of which the empire ceased.</p>
<p><strong>By the fire, and by the smoke, and by the brimstone, which issued out of their mouths</strong>; that is, by the firing of guns.</p>
