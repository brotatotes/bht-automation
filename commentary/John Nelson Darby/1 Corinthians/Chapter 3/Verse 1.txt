	 3:1 fleshly; (i-18) <em> Sarkinos</em> . as <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:14">Romans 7:14</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:16">Hebrews 7:16</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:3">2 Corinthians 3:3</span> . This word is said to mean properly the material -- the composition of a thing. 'Carnal,' twice in verse 3, is <em> sarkinos</em> , a form used, in some places, to express either material or physical or moral ideas. It occurs also in <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:27">Romans 15:27</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:11">1 Corinthians 9:11</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:12">2 Corinthians 1:12</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:4">2 Corinthians 10:4</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:11">1 Peter 2:11</span> . This last passage, 'fleshly lusts,' shows how the material and moral thoughts run into one another.</p>
