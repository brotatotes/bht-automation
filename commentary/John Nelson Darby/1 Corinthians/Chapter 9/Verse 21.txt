<p>
	
	
	 9:21 law, (a-4) <em> Anomos</em> , 'without law'. This is important as showing the true force of the Greek word <em> anomia</em> employed elsewhere, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:4">1 John 3:4</span> , 'sin is lawlessness,' not 'transgression of law.' legitimately (b-16) Or, 'under law to Christ' (not under <em> the</em> law), i.e. rightfully, duly, subject to Him. I have said 'legitimately' to preserve the connection with law. See <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:39">Acts 19:39</span> , 'regular,' i.e. lawful and right.</p>
