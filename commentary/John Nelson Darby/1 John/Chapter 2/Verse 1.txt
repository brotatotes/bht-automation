	 2:1 children, (g-2) <em> Teknia</em> (a diminutive). It is a term of parental affection. It applies to Christians irrespective of growth. Used in vers. 12.28; chs. 3.7,18; 4.4; 5.21; <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:33">John 13:33</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:19">Galatians 4:19</span> patron (h-24) <em> Parakletos</em> , as 'comforter,' <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:16">John 14:16</span> ,<span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:26">John 14:26</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="joh+15:26">John 15:26</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:7">John 16:7</span> . Christ manages all our affairs for us above; the Holy Spirit below. I use 'patron' in the sense rather of the Roman patron, who maintained the interests of his clients in every way. So Christ on high; the Spirit here for saints.</p>
