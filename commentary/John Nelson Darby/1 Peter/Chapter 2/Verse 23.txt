<p>
	
	
	 2:23 into (i-15) The Greek means to deliver up into the hands of another, not committing a wrong to another to vindicate. The sense must be 'gave himself up to, suffered all, as accepting all from his hand.' gave himself up to take whatever he sent who would in the end righteously judge. It has the sense of committing any one to the care of another. See <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:26">Acts 14:26</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:40">Acts 15:40</span> . 'committed.' Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:30">John 19:30</span> . 'delivered up.'</p>
