	 1:4 encourages (a-2) <em> Parakaleo</em> , as <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:18">Matthew 2:18</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:4">Matthew 5:4</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:12">Acts 20:12</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:7">1 Thessalonians 3:7</span> . I do not say 'comfort,' as that is rather conveyed in <em> paramutheomai</em> , so translated in <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:11">1 Thessalonians 2:11</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:14">1 Thessalonians 5:14</span> ; 'console,' <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:19">John 11:19</span> ,<span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:31">John 11:31</span> ; and 'consolation,' <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:3">1 Corinthians 14:3</span> . The difference is slight, but here, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:0">Acts 20:0</span> , there is the sense of 'cheered,' and this might replace 'encourage' and 'encouragement' if there were a suitable noun.</p>
