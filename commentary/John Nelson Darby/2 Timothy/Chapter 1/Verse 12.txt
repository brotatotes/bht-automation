<p>
	
	
	 1:12 believed, (i-20) <em> Pisteuo</em> , 'believe,' occurs in different constructions; with the dative, as here, it means, 'to believe a person or thing;' compare <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:25">Matthew 21:25</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:24">John 5:24</span> and 47. Followed by <em> eis</em> with an accusative, as frequently in John, it is to believe on a person as an object of faith, as <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:1">John 14:1</span> ('ye believe on God, believe also on me'). Followed by <em> epi</em> with an accusative, it goes on to the idea of confidence. I rest my faith on him, yet with a more general idea of looking to him with this confidence. It is only used thus six times, so far as I am aware. <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:42">Acts 9:42</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:17">Acts 11:17</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:31">Acts 16:31</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:19">Acts 22:19</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:5">Romans 4:5</span> ,<span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:24">Romans 4:24</span> . Followed by <em> epi</em> with a dative, it is used only in <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:25">Luke 24:25</span> , and in the three passages where <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="isa+28:16">Isaiah 28:16</span> is quoted, <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:33">Romans 9:33</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:11">Romans 10:11</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:6">1 Peter 2:6</span> , where it is confidence in, or reliance upon, a person or thing. Followed by <em> en</em> with a dative, it occurs more seldom, and refers to believing in the truth of a thing, receiving a statement as true, as <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:15">Mark 1:15</span> ('believe in the glad tidings').</p>
