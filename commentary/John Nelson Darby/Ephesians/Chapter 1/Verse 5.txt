<p>
	
	
	 1:5 beforehand (d-5) The word translated 'marked out beforehand' involves purpose. It is not 'counsel,' though it is ' <em> according to</em> the good pleasure of his will,' and, in ver. 11, ' <em> according to</em> his purpose,' and ' <em> according to</em> the counsel of his own will.' The word <em> horizo</em> means 'mark out,' 'determine,' as in <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:4">Romans 1:4</span> ; here <em> pro</em> (beforehand) is prefixed. It refers generally to persons, but is applied to things as in <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:7">1 Corinthians 2:7</span> . When applied to persons, that to which they are destined is always added. adoption (e-7) Or 'sonship,' as <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:5">Galatians 4:5</span> .</p>
