	 10:40 for (c-18) Or perhaps 'to.' so <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:23">Matthew 20:23</span> . But the emphasis is on the words 'not mine to give;' wonderful perfectness and lowliness of the Lord! 'It is not mine to give,' is a complete phrase followed by the separate statement, 'but it is reserved for those for whom it is prepared.' it is for them, appropriated to them.</p>
