	 14:3 pure (b-30) Perhaps 'liquid.' The word is only found here and <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:3">John 12:3</span> , evidently a known kind of nard. It is by no means impossible it may be derived from the Latin <em> spicatae</em> , which was the best kind of nard; hence, doubtless, the English translation 'spikenard.' The sense is plain. that it was of the best and most precious kind. See Note, <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:3">John 12:3</span> .</p>
