	 15:4 holy; (b-16) <em> Hosios</em> , not <em> hagios</em> . It is used for mercy, grace, and of Christ, as the One in whom all gracious qualities are concentrated. (<span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="ps+89:1">Psalms 89:1</span> ,<span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="ps+89:2">Psalms 89:2</span> ,<span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="ps+89:19">Psalms 89:19</span> .) In men it means piety and uprightness. <em> Chesed</em> is so translated in the Old Testament. It is in general the sum of qualities which suit and form the divine character in man, as opposed to the human will. what God gives as consistent with himself, his character, and promise. The 'sure <em> mercies</em> ' of David is expressed by this word in the LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="isa+55:3">Isaiah 55:3</span> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:34">Acts 13:34</span> . God alone possesses the qualities which entitle him to worship as a pious man would understand it. <em> Hosios</em> is used in this general way for 'holy.' see Note i, <span class="scriptRef" com="dsn" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:26">Hebrews 7:26</span> . homage (c-24) See Note h, ch. 3.9. righteousnesses (d-29) See Note, ch. 19.8.</p>
