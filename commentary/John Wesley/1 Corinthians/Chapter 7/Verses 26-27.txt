<p><em>This is good for the present distress</em> — While any church is under persecution.</p>
<p><em>For a man to continue as he is</em> — Whether married or unmarried. St. Paul does not here urge the present distress as a reason for celibacy, any more than for marriage; but for a man's not seeking to alter his state, whatever it be, but making the best of it.</p>
