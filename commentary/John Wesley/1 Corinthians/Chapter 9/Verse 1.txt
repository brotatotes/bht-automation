<p><em>Am I not free? am I not an apostle?</em> — That is, Have not I the liberty of a common Christian? yea, that of an apostle? He vindicates his apostleship, <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:1-3">1 Corinthians 9:1-3</span>: his apostolical liberty, <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:4-19">1 Corinthians 9:4-19</span>.</p>
<p><em>Have I not seen Jesus Christ?</em> — Without this he could not have been one of those first grand witnesses.</p>
<p><em>Are not ye my work in the Lord</em> — A full evidence that God hath sent me? And yet some, it seems, objected to his being an apostle, because he had not asserted his privilege in demanding and receiving such maintenance from the churches as was due to that office.</p>
