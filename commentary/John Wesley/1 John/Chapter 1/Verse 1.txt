<p><em>That which was</em> — Here means, He which was the Word himself; afterwards it means, that which they had heard from him.</p>
<p><em>Which was</em> — Namely, with the Father, verse2, before he was manifested.</p>
<p><em>From the beginning</em> — This phrase is sometimes used in a limited sense; but here it properly means from eternity, being equivalent with, "in the beginning," <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:1">John 1:1</span>.</p>
<p><em>That which we</em> — The apostles. Have not only heard, but seen with our eyes, which we have beheld - Attentively considered on various occasions.</p>
<p><em>Of the Word of life</em> — He is termed the Word, <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:1">John 1:1</span>; the Life, <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:4">John 1:4</span>; as he is the living Word of God, who, with the Father and the Spirit, is the fountain of life to all creatures, particularly of spiritual and eternal life.</p>
