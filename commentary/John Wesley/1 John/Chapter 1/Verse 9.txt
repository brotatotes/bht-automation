<p>But if with a penitent and believing heart, we confess our sins, he is faithful - Because he had promised this blessing, by the unanimous voice of all his prophets.</p>
<p><em>Just</em> — Surely then he will punish: no; for this very reason he will pardon. This may seem strange; but upon the evangelical principle of atonement and redemption, it is undoubtedly true; because, when the debt is paid, or the purchase made, it is the part of equity to cancel the bond, and consign over the purchased possession.</p>
<p><em>Both to forgive us our sins</em> — To take away all the guilt of them.</p>
<p><em>And to cleanse us from all unrighteousness</em> — To purify our souls from every kind and every degree of it.</p>
