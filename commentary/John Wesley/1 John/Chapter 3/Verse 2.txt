<p><em>It doth not yet appear</em> — Even to ourselves.</p>
<p><em>What we shall be</em> — It is something ineffable, which will raise the children of God to be, in a manner, as God himself. But we know, in general, that when he, the Son of God, shall appear, we shall be like him - The glory of God penetrating our inmost substance.</p>
<p><em>For we shall see him as he is</em> — Manifestly, without a veil. And that sight will transform us into the same likeness.</p>
