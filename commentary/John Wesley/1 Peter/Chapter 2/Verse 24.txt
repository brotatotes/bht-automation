<p><em>Who himself bore our sins</em> — That is, the punishment due to them. In his afflicted, torn, dying body on the tree - The cross, whereon chiefly slaves or servants were wont to suffer.</p>
<p><em>That we being dead to sin</em> — Wholly delivered both from the guilt and power of it: indeed, without an atonement first made for the guilt, we could never have been delivered from the power.</p>
<p><em>Might live to righteousness</em> — Which is one only. The sins we had committed, and he bore, were manifold.</p>
