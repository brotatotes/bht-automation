<p><em>And there was a sharp contention</em> — Literally, a paroxysm, or fit of a fever. But nothing in the text implies that the sharpness was on both sides. It is far more probable that it was not; that St. Paul, who had the right on his side, as he undoubtedly had,) maintained it with love.</p>
<p><em>And Barnabas taking Mark with him, sailed away to Cyprus</em> — Forsaking the work in which he was engaged, he went away to his own country.</p>
