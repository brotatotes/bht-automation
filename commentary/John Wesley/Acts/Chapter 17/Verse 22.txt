<p><em>Then Paul standing in the midst of the Areopagus</em> — An ample theatre; said - Giving them a lecture of natural divinity, with admirable wisdom, acuteness, fulness, and courtesy. They inquire after new things: Paul in his divinely philosophical discourse, begins with the first, and goes on to the last things, both which were new things to them. He points out the origin and the end of all things, concerning which they had so many disputes, and equally refutes both the Epicurean and Stoic.</p>
<p><em>I perceive</em> — With what clearness and freedom does he speak! Paul against Athens!</p>
