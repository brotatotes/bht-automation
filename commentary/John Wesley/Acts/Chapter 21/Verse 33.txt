<p><em>Then the tribune</em> — Having made his way through the multitude, came near and took him - And how many great ends of providence were answered by this imprisonment? This was not only a means of preserving his life, (after he had suffered severely for worldly prudence,) but gave him an opportunity of preaching the Gospel safely, in spite of all tumult, <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:22">Acts 22:22</span>, yea, and that in those places to which otherwise he could have had no access, <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:40">Acts 21:40</span>.</p>
<p><em>And commanded him to be bound with two chains</em> — Taking it for granted he was some notorious offender. And thus the prophecy of Agabus was fulfilled, though by the hands of a Roman.</p>
