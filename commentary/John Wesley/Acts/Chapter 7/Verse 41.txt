<p><em>And they made a calf</em> — In imitation of Apis, the Egyptian god: and rejoiced in the works of their hands - In the god they had made42.</p>
<p><em>God turned</em> — From them in anger; and gave them up - Frequently from the time of the golden calf, to the time of Amos, and afterward.</p>
<p><em>The host of heaven</em> — The stars are called an army or host, because of their number, order, and powerful influence.</p>
<p><em>In the book of the prophets</em> — Of the twelve prophets, which the Jews always wrote together in one book.</p>
<p><em>Have ye offered</em> — The passage of Amos referred to, chap. v, 25, etc., <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="am+5:25">Amos 5:25</span> consists of two parts; of which the former confirms verse41, <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:41,42">Acts 7:41,42</span>of the sin of the people; the latter the beginning of verse42, concerning their punishment.</p>
<p><em>Have ye offered to me</em> — They had offered many sacrifices; but God did not accept them as offered to him, because they sacrificed to idols also; and did not sacrifice to him with an upright heart. <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="am+5:25">Amos 5:25</span>. </p>
