<p><em>Lest the bodies should remain on the cross on the Sabbath</em> — Which they would have accounted a profanation of any Sabbath, but of that in particular.</p>
<p><em>For that Sabbath was a great day</em> — Being not only a Sabbath, but the second day of the feast of unleavened bread (from whence they reckoned the weeks to pentecost:) and also the day for presenting and offering the sheaf of new corn: so that it was a treble solemnity.</p>
