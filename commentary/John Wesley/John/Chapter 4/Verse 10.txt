<p><em>If thou hadst known the gift</em> — The living water; and who it is - He who alone is able to give it: thou wouldst have asked of him - On those words the stress lies.</p>
<p><em>Water</em> — In like manner he draws the allegory from bread, <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:27">John 6:27</span>, and from light, 8:12; the first, the most simple, necessary, common, and salutary things in nature.</p>
<p><em>Living water</em> — The Spirit and its fruits. But she might the more easily mistake his meaning, because living water was a common phrase among the Jews for spring water.</p>
