<p>The instant she perceived this, she proposes what she thought the most important of all questions.</p>
<p><em>This mountain</em> — Pointing to Mount Gerizim. Sanballat, by the permission of Alexander the Great, had built a temple upon Mount Gerizim, for Manasseh, who for marrying Sanballat's daughter had been expelled from the priesthood and from Jerusalem, <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="ne+13:28">Nehemiah 13:28</span>. This was the place where the Samaritans used to worship in opposition to Jerusalem. And it was so near Sychar, that a man's voice might be heard from the one to the other.</p>
<p><em>Our fathers worshipped</em> — This plainly refers to Abraham and Jacob (from whom the Samaritans pretended to deduce their genealogy) who erected altars in this place: <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="ge+12:6,7">Genesis 12:6,7</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="ge+33:18,20">Genesis 33:18,20</span>. And possibly to the whole congregation, who were directed when they came into the land of Canaan to put the blessing upon Mount Gerizim, <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="de+11:29">Deuteronomy 11:29</span>.</p>
<p><em>Ye Jews say, In Jerusalem is the place</em> — Namely, the temple.</p>
