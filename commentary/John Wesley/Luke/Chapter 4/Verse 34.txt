<p><em>What have we to do with thee</em> — Thy present business is with men, not with devils.</p>
<p><em>I know thee who thou art</em> — But surely he did not know a little before, that he was God over all, blessed for ever; or he would not have dared to tell him, All this power is delivered to me, and I give it to whomsoever I will.</p>
<p><em>The Holy One of God</em> — Either this confession was extorted from him by terror, (for the devils believe and tremble,) or he made it with a design to render the character of Christ suspected. Possibly it was from hence the Pharisees took occasion to say, He casteth out devils by the prince of the devils.</p>
