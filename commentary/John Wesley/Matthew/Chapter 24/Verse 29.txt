<p><em>Immediately after the tribulation of those days</em> — Here our Lord begins to speak of his last coming. But he speaks not so much in the language of man as of God, with whom a thousand years are as one day, one moment. Many of the primitive Christians not observing this, thought he would come immediately, in the common sense of the word: a mistake which St. Paul labours to remove, in his Second Epistle to the Thessalonians.</p>
<p><em>The powers of the heavens</em> — Probably the influences of the heavenly bodies. <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:24">Mark 13:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:25">Luke 21:25</span>.</p>
