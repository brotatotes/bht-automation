<p><em>Who being in the essential form</em> — The incommunicable nature.</p>
<p><em>Of God</em> — From eternity, as he was afterward in the form of man; real God, as real man.</p>
<p><em>Counted it no act of robbery</em> — That is the precise meaning of the words,-no invasion of another's prerogative, but his own strict and unquestionable right.</p>
<p><em>To be equal with God</em> — the word here translated equal, occurs in the adjective form five or six times in the New Testament, <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:12">Matthew 20:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:34">Luke 6:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:18">John 5:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:17">Acts 11:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="re+21:16">Revelation 21:16</span>. In all which places it expresses not a bare resemblance, but a real and proper equalitg. It here implies both the fulness and the supreme height of the Godhead; to which are opposed, he emptied and he humbled himself.</p>
