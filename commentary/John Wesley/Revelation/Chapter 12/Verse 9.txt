<p><em>And the great dragon was cast out</em> — It is not yet said, unto the earth - He was cast out of heaven; and at this the inhabitants of heaven rejoice. He is termed the great dragon, as appearing here in that shape, to intimate his poisonous and cruel disposition.</p>
<p><em>The ancient serpent</em> — In allusion to his deceiving Eve in that form. Dragons are a kind of large serpent.</p>
<p><em>Who is called the Devil and Satan</em> — These are words of exactly the same meaning; only the former is Greek; the latter, Hebrew; denoting the grand adversary of all the saints, whether Jews or gentiles. He has deceived the whole world - Not only in their first parents, but through all ages, and in all countries, into unbelief and all wickedness; into the hating and persecuting faith and all goodness.</p>
<p><em>He was cast out unto the earth</em> — He was cast out of heaven; and being cast out thence, himself came to the earth. Nor had he been unemployed on the earth before, although his ordinary abode was in heaven.</p>
