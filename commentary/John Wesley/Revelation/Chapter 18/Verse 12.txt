<p><em>Merchandise of gold, …</em> — Almost all these are still in use at Rome, both in their idolatrous service, and in common life.</p>
<p><em>Fine linen</em> — The sort of it mentioned in the original is exceeding costly.</p>
<p><em>Thyine wood</em> — A sweet-smelling wood not unlike citron, used in adorning magnificent palaces.</p>
<p><em>Vessels of most precious wood</em> — Ebony, in particular, which is often mentioned with ivory: the one excelling in whiteness, the other in blackness; and both in uncommon smoothness.</p>
