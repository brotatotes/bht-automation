Merchandise of gold, … — Almost all these are still in use at Rome, both in their idolatrous service, and in common life.Fine linen — The sort of it mentioned in the original is exceeding costly.Thyine wood — A sweet-smelling wood not unlike citron, used in adorning magnificent palaces.Vessels of most precious wood — Ebony, in particular, which is often mentioned with ivory: the one excelling in whiteness, the other in blackness; and both in uncommon smoothness.
