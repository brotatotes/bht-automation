<p><em>For I am not ashamed of the gospel</em> — To the world, indeed, it is folly and weakness, <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:18">1 Corinthians 1:18</span>; therefore, in the judgment of the world, he ought to be ashamed of it; especially at Rome, the head and theatre of the world. But Paul is not ashamed, knowing it is the power of God unto salvation to every one that believeth - The great and gloriously powerful means of saving all who accept salvation in God's own way. As St. Paul comprises the sum of the gospel in this epistle, so he does the sum of the epistle in this and the following verse.</p>
<p><em>Both to the Jew, and to the gentile</em> — There is a noble frankness, as well as a comprehensive sense, in these words, by which he, on the one hand, shows the Jews their absolute need of the gospel; and, on the other, tells the politest and greatest nation in the world both that their salvation depended on receiving it, and that the first offers of it were in every place to be made to the despised Jews.</p>
