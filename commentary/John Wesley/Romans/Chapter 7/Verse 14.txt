<p><em>I am carnal</em> — St. Paul, having compared together the past and present state of believers, that "in the flesh," <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:5">Romans 7:5</span>, and that "in the spirit," <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:6">Romans 7:6</span>, in answering two objections, (Is then the law sin? <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:7">Romans 7:7</span>, and, Is the law death? <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:13">Romans 7:13</span>,) interweaves the whole process of a man reasoning, groaning, striving, and escaping from the legal to the evangelical state. This he does from <span class="scriptRef" com="wen" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:7">Romans 7:7</span>, to the end of this chapter.</p>
<p><em>Sold under sin</em> — Totally enslaved; slaves bought with money were absolutely at their master's disposal.</p>
