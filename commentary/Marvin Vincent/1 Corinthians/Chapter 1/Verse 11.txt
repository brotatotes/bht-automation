<p>It hath been declared [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδηλωθη] </span></span>. Rev., signified, which is hardly strong enough. The word means to make clear, or manifest [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δηλος] </span></span>. Compare ch. 3 13. It may imply that Paul was reluctant to believe the reports, but was convinced by unimpeachable testimony. </p>
<p>Of the household of Chloe [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των χλοης] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:10">Romans 16:10</span> for the form of expression. The persons may have been slaves who had come to Ephesus on business for their mistress, or members of her family. Chloe means tender verdure, and was an epithet of Demeter (Ceres), the goddess of agriculture and rural life. It is uncertain whether she belonged to the Corinthian or to the Ephesian church. </p>
<p>Contentions [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εριδες] </span></span>. Socrates in Plato's "Republic" distinguishes between disputing [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εριζειν</span></span>] and discussing [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαλεγεσθαι] </span></span>, and identifies contention [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερις</span></span>] with gainsaying [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντιλογια] </span></span>, "Republic," 5, 454. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:9">Titus 3:9</span>.</p>
