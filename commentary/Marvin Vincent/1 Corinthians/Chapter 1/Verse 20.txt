<p>Scribe [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γραμματευς] </span></span>. Always in the New Testament in the Jewish sense, an interpreter of the law, except <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:35">Acts 19:35</span>, the town - clerk. </p>
<p>Disputer [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συζητητης] </span></span>. Only here. Compare the kindred verb suzhtew to question with, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:27">Mark 1:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:23">Luke 22:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:9">Acts 6:9</span>; and suzhthsiv disputation, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:2">Acts 14:2</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:7">Acts 14:7</span>. Referring to Grecian sophistical reasoners, while scribe refers to rabbinical hair - splitters. </p>
<p>World [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιωνος] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:9">John 1:9</span>. More correctly, age or period. </p>
<p>Made foolish [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμωρανεν] </span></span>. Proved it to be practical folly; stupefied it. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:22">Romans 1:22</span>. Possibly with a latent suggestion of the judicial power of God to make it foolish.</p>
