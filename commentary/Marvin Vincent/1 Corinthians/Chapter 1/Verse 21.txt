<p>After that [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επειδη] </span></span>. Rev., correctly, seeing that. </p>
<p>By wisdom [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια της σοφιας] </span></span>. Better, as Rev., giving the force of the article, "through its wisdom." </p>
<p>Preaching [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κηρυγματος] </span></span>. Not the act, but the substance of preaching. Compare ver. 23. </p>
<p>To save [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωσαι] </span></span>. The word was technically used in the Old Testament of deliverance at the Messiah 's coming; of salvation from the penalties of the messianic judgment, or from the evils which obstruct the messianic deliverance. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joe+2:32">Joel 2:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+1:21">Matthew 1:21</span>; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:40">Acts 2:40</span>. Paul uses it in the ethical sense, to make one a partaker of the salvation which is through Christ. Edwards calls attention to the foregleam of this christian conception of the word in the closing paragraph of Plato's "Republic :" " And thus, Glaucon, the tale has been saved, and has not perished, and will save [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωσειεν</span></span>] us if we are obedient to the word spoken, and we shall pass safely over the river of forgetfulness and our soul will not be defiled. "</p>
