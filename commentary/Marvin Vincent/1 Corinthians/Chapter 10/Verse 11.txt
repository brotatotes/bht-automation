Admonition [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νουθεσιαν] </span></span>. See on the kindred verb to warn, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:31">Acts 20:31</span> Ends of the world [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα τελη των αιωνων] </span></span>. Lit., ends of the ages. So Rev. Synonymous with hJ sunteleia twn aijwnwn the consummation of the ages, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:26">Hebrews 9:26</span>. The phrase assumes that Christ 's second coming is close at hand, and therefore the end of the world. Ellicott acutely remarks that the plural, ends, marks a little more distinctly the idea of each age of preparation having passed into the age that succeeded it, so that now all the ends of the ages have come down to them. </p>
