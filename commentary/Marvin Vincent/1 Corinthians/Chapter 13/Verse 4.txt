<p>Suffereth long [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μακροθυμει] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:7">James 5:7</span>. </p>
<p>Is kind [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρηστευεται] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. See on crhstov, A. V., easy, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:30">Matthew 11:30</span>, and crhstothv good, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:12">Romans 3:12</span>. "The high charity which makes us servants Prompt to the counsel which controls the world." DANTE, "Paradiso," 21, 70, 71. </p>
<p>Vaunteth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περπερευεται] </span></span>. From perperov a braggart. Used of one who sounds his own praises. Cicero introduces a compound of the word in one of his letters to Atticus, describing his speech in the presence of Pompey, who had just addressed the senate on his return from the Mithridatic war. He says : "Heavens! How I showed off [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεπερπερευσαμην</span></span>] before my new auditor Pompey," and describes the various rhetorical tricks which he employed. </p>
<p>Puffed up [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυσιουται] </span></span>. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:6">1 Corinthians 4:6</span>, and compare ch. 8 1. Of inward disposition, as the previous word denotes outward display. The opposite is put by Dante : </p>
<p>"That swells with love the spirit well - disposed." " Paradiso, " 10, 144.</p>
