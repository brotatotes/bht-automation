<p>Easily provoked [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παροξυνεται] </span></span>. Easily is superfluous, and gives a wrong coloring to the statement, which is absolute : is not provoked or exasperated. The verb occurs only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:16">Acts 17:16</span>. The kindred noun paroxusmov, in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:39">Acts 14:39</span>, describes the irritation which arose between Paul and Barnabas. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:24">Hebrews 10:24</span>, stimulating to good works. It is used of provoking God, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+9:8">Deuteronomy 9:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+105:29">Psalms 105:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+65:3">Isaiah 65:3</span>. Thinketh no evil [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου λογιζεται το κακον] </span></span>. Lit., reckoneth not the evil. Rev., taketh not account of. The evil; namely, that which is done to love. "Love, instead of entering evil as a debt in its account - book, voluntarily passes the sponge over what it endures" (Godet).</p>
