<p>Understanding [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σρεσιν] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. </p>
<p>Originally, in a physical sense, the diaphragm. Denoting the reasoning power on the reflective side, and perhaps intentionally used instead of nouv (ver. 15), which emphasizes the distinction from ecstasy. </p>
<p>Children - be ye children [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παιδια - νηπιαζετε] </span></span>. The A. V. misses the distinction between children and babes, the stronger term for being unversed in malice. In understanding they are to be above mere children. In malice they are to be very babes. See on child, ch. 13 11. </p>
<p>Malice [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακια] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:21">James 1:21</span>. </p>
<p>Men [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελειοι] </span></span>. Lit., perfect. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:6">1 Corinthians 2:6</span>.</p>
