<p>With excellency [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθ υπεροχην] </span></span>. Lit., according to elevation or superiority. The noun occurs only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:2">1 Timothy 2:2</span>, where it is rendered authority. The phrase expresses the mode of his preaching. For similar adverbial phrases, see kaq uJperbolhn exceedingly or according to excess, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:13">Romans 8:13</span>; kata kratov mightily or according to might, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:20">Acts 19:20</span>. Construe with declaring. </p>
<p>Declaring [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταγγελλων] </span></span>. Rev., proclaiming. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:5">1 John 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:23">Acts 17:23</span>. Authoritative proclamation is implied. The word is found only in the Acts and in Paul. </p>
<p>Testimony [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυριον] </span></span>. Some of the best texts read musthrion mystery. So Rev. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:25">Romans 11:25</span>.</p>
