<p>To shame [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντρεπων] </span></span>. Lit., as shaming. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:37">Matthew 21:37</span>. The verb means to turn about, hence to turn one upon himself; put him to shame. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:14">2 Thessalonians 3:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:8">Titus 2:8</span>. Also, in the middle voice, in the sense of reverence; to turn one's self toward another. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:6">Mark 12:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:2">Luke 18:2</span>. The kindred noun ejntroph occurs twice : <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:5">1 Corinthians 6:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:34">1 Corinthians 14:34</span>. Compare Sophocles : "Think you he will have any regard [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντροπην</span></span>] for the blind man" (" Oedipus at Colonos, " 299).</p>
