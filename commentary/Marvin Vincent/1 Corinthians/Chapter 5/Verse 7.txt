<p>Leaven. Not the sinful man, but evil of every kind, in accordance with the more general statement of the leavening, power of evil in ver. 6. The apostle 's metaphor is shaped by the commands concerning the removal of leaven at the passover : <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+12:19">Exodus 12:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+13:7">Exodus 13:7</span>. Compare Ignatius; "Dispense, therefore, with the evil leaven that has grown old [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παλαιωθεισαν</span></span>] and that has gone sour [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενοξισασαν] </span></span>, and be changed into new leaven which is Jesus Christ" (Epistle to Magnesians, 10.). </p>
<p>New [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νεον] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:29">Matthew 26:29</span>. </p>
<p>Passover [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το πασχα] </span></span>. The Paschal lamb, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:12">Mark 14:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:7">Luke 22:7</span>.</p>
