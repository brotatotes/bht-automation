<p>Are lawful [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεστιν] </span></span>. There is a play between this word and ejxousiasqhsomai be brought under the power, which can hardly be accurately conveyed to the English reader. The nearest approach to it is : </p>
<p>"all things are in my power, but I shall not be brought under the power of any." </p>
<p>Will - be brought under the power [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξουσιασθησομαι] </span></span>. From ejxousia power of choice, permissive authority. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:10">Mark 2:10</span>. This in turn is derived from exesti it is permitted. See above on are lawful. This kinship of the two words explains the play upon them.</p>
