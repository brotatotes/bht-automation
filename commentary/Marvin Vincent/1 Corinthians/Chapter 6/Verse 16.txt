<p>He that is joined [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο κολλωμενος] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:15">Luke 14:15</span>. Compare Aeschylus : "The family has been glued [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεκολληται</span></span>] to misfortune" (" Agamemnon, " 1543). The verb is used <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+2:24">Genesis 2:24</span>, Sept., of the relation of husband and wife : shall cleave. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+10:20">Deuteronomy 10:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+11:22">Deuteronomy 11:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+13:11">Jeremiah 13:11</span>, of man's cleaving to God. </p>
<p>To a harlot [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη πορνη] </span></span>. Lit., the harlot. The article is significant : his harlot, or that one with whom he is sinning at the time. </p>
<p>Shall be one flesh [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσονται εις σαρκα μιαν] </span></span>. Lit., shall be unto one flesh : i e., from being two, shall pass into one. Hence Rev., rightly, shall become. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:15">Ephesians 2:15</span>.</p>
