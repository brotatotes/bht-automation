Flee. See Genesis 39:12. Socrates, in Plato's "Republic," relates how the poet Sophocles, in answer to the question "How does love suit with are?" replied : "Most gladly have I escaped that, and I feel as if I had escaped from a mad and furious master" (329). Sin [αμαρτημα] . See on Romans 3:25. Without the body [εκτος του σωματος] . Lit., outside. The body is not the instrument, but the subject. But in fornication the body is the instrument of the sin, and "inwardly as well as outwardly is made over to another."
