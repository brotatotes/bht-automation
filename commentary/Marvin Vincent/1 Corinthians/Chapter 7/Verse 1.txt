<p>It is good [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλον] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:11">John 10:11</span>. Not merely expedient, but morally salutary. The statement, however, is made in the light of circumstances, see ver. 26, and is to be read with others, such as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:2">2 Corinthians 11:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:4">Romans 7:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:28-33">Ephesians 5:28-33</span>, in all which marriage is made the type of the union between Christ and His Church. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:4">Hebrews 13:4</span>. </p>
<p>5 May give yourselves [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σχολασητε] </span></span>. Lit., may have leisure. Like the Latin phrase vacaare rei to be free for a thing, and so to devote one's self to it. </p>
<p>Incontinency [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακρασιαν] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:35">Matthew 23:35</span>, on which see note.</p>
