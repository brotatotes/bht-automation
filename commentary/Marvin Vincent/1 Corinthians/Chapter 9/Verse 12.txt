<p>Power [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξουσιας] </span></span>. Rev., correctly, right. The right to claim maintenance. </p>
<p>Suffer [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στεγομεν] </span></span>. Rev., bear. The primary meaning is to cover. So some render ch. 13 7, covereth for beareth. Hence to protect by covering, as with a tight ship or roof. So Aesehylus, of a ship : "The wooden house with sails that keeps out [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στεγων</span></span>] the sea" (" Suppliants, "126)." The tower keeps off [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστεγει</span></span>] the multitude of the enemy "(" Seven against Thebes," 220). And so, to bear up against, endure. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:1">1 Thessalonians 3:1</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:5">1 Thessalonians 3:5</span>. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:103">1 Thessalonians 3:103</span> Hinder [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγκοπην δωμεν] </span></span>. Lit., give hindrance. Rev., cause hindrance. Egkoph hindrance, only here in the New Testament. Primarily, an incision, and so used by the physician Galen. Compare the kindred verb ejgkoptw to cut into, also occurring in Hippocrates in the surgical sense. In the sense of cutting into one's way, it gets the meaning of hindrance. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:4">Acts 24:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:22">Romans 14:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:7">Galatians 5:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:18">1 Thessalonians 2:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:7">1 Peter 3:7</span>. Compare the Latin intercidere to divide, inter - rupt.</p>
