<p>Fight I [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυκτευω] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. Distinctively of fighting with the fists, and evidently in allusion to the boxing - match. Rev., in margin, box. Etymologically akin to pugmh the fist; see on oft, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:3">Mark 7:3</span>. Beateth the air. A boxer might be said to beat the air when practicing without an adversary. This was called skiamacia shadow - fighting. Or he might purposely strike into the air in order to spare his adversary; or the adversary might evade his blow, and thus cause him to spend his strength on the air. The two latter may well be combined in Paul 's metaphor. He strikes straight and does not spare. Compare Virgil, in the description of a boxing - match : </p>
