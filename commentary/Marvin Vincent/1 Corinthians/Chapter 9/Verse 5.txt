<p>A sister, a wife. Wrong Sister means a christian woman, a fellow - member of the Church, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:1">Romans 16:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:15">1 Corinthians 7:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:15">James 2:15</span>. It is in apposition with wife : A wife that is a sister on believer. So Rev. Such an one has also the right, like her husband, to be maintained by the Church. Some of the fathers claimed that not a wife was meant, but a female attendant, serviens mantrona, who contributed to the maintenance of the apostles as certain women ministered to Christ. There is no foundation for this. It is contradicted by the example of Peter cited at the end of this verse; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:14">Matthew 8:14</span>; and besides, the point of the argument is that these companions should be maintained. Such a practice, however, did grow up in the Church, but was abolished by the Council of Nicaea on account of its abuses. Stanley remarks that the fact of these women accompanying their husbands, may be explained by the necessity of females to gain access to and to baptize the female converts in Greece and in oriental countries; the same necessity which gave rise to the order of deaconesses.</p>
