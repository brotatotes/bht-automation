<p>Is - walketh - whither. The condition of him who hates is viewed as related to being, action, and tendency. </p>
<p>He goeth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπαγει] </span></span>. Or, is going. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:21">John 6:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:21">John 8:21</span>. </p>
<p>Hath blinded [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετυφλωσεν] </span></span>. For the image see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+6:10">Isaiah 6:10</span>. See on closed, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:15">Matthew 13:15</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:5">John 1:5</span>, and see note on katelaben, overtook; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:35">John 11:35</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:40">John 11:40</span>. The aorist tense, blinded, indicates a past, definite, decisive act. When the darkness overtook, it blinded. The blindness is no new state into which he has come.</p>
