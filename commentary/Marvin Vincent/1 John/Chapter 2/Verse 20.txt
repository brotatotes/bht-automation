<p>An unction [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρισμα] </span></span>. The word means that with which the anointing is performed - the unguent or ointment. In the New Testament only here and ver. 27. Rev., an anointing. The root of this word and of Cristov, Christ, is the same. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+1:1">Matthew 1:1</span>. the anointing is from the Anointed. </p>
<p>The Holy One. Christ. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:69">John 6:69</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:14">Acts 3:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:27">Acts 4:27</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:30">Acts 4:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+3:7">Revelation 3:7</span>. Ye know all things [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιδατε πα. ντα] </span></span>. The best texts read pantev, ye all know; in which case the connection is with the following clause : "I have not written unto you because ye know not the truth, but because ye know it."</p>
