<p>Hereby [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τουτω] </span></span>. Lit., in this. Characteristic of John. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:35">John 8:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:8">John 14:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:30">John 16:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:5">1 John 2:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:24">1 John 3:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:13">1 John 4:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:2">1 John 5:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:16">1 John 3:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:19">1 John 3:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:2">1 John 4:2</span>. The expression points to what follows, "if we keep His commandments," yet with a covert reference to that idea as generally implied in the previous words concerning fellowship with God and walking in the light. </p>
<p>We know [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκομεν] </span></span>. Or, perceive. By experience, from day to day; distinguished from oidamen we know, expressing absolute, immediate knowledge of a fact once for all. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:2">1 John 3:2</span>. </p>
<p>That we know [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτι εγνωκαμεν] </span></span>. Or, more literally, have come to know. John does not use the compound forms ejpiginwskein and ejpignwsiv (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:16">Matthew 7:16</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:4">Luke 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:13">Acts 4:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:28">Romans 1:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:17">Ephesians 1:17</span>, etc.), nor the kindred word gnwsiv knowledge (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:77">Luke 1:77</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:20">Romans 2:20</span>, etc.). </p>
<p>We keep His commandments [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τας εντολας αυτου τηρωμεν] </span></span>. A phrase peculiar to John and occurring elsewhere only <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:17">Matthew 19:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:14">1 Timothy 6:14</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:19">1 Corinthians 7:19</span>, we find thrhsiv ejntolwn the keeping of the commandments. On threw to keep, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:5">1 Peter 1:5</span>.</p>
