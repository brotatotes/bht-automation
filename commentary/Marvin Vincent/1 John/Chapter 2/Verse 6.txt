<p>He abideth in Him [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αυτω μενειν] </span></span>. To abide in God is a more common expression with John than to be in God, and marks an advance in thought. The phrase is a favorite one with John. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:4">John 14:4</span> sqq.; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:56">John 6:56</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:24">1 John 2:24</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:27">1 John 2:27</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:28">1 John 2:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:6">1 John 3:6</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:24">1 John 3:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:12">1 John 4:12</span> sq.; 15 sq. Bengel notes the gradation in the three phrases "to know Him, to be in Him, to abide in Him; knowledge, fellowship, constancy." </p>
<p>Ought [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφειλει] </span></span>. An obligation, put as a debt. See Luk 27:10, and on debts, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:12">Matthew 6:12</span>. The word expresses a special, personal obligation, and not as dei must, an obligation in the nature of things. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:9">John 20:9</span>, and compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:16">1 John 3:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:11">1 John 4:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:8">3 John 1:8</span>. </p>
<p>He [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινος] </span></span>. Always of Christ in the Epistles of John. See ejkeinhv, referring to aJmartia sin, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:16">1 John 5:16</span>.</p>
