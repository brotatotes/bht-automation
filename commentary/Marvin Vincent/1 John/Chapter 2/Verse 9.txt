<p>Hateth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μισων] </span></span>. The sharp issue is maintained here as in Christ 's words, "He that is not with me is against me" (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:23">Luke 11:23</span>). Men fall into two classes, those who are in fellowship with God, and therefore walk in light and love, and those who are not in fellowship with God, and therefore walk in darkness and hatred. "A direct opposition," says Bengel; where love is not, there is hatred. "The heart is not empty." See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:20">John 3:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:7">John 7:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:18">John 14:18</span> sqq.; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:14">John 17:14</span>. The word hate is opposed both to the love of natural affection [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλειν] </span></span>, and to the more discriminating sentiment - love founded on a just estimate [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπαν] </span></span>. For the former see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:25">John 12:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:18">John 14:18</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:19">John 14:19</span>; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:26">Luke 14:26</span>. For the latter, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:14">1 John 3:14</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:15">1 John 3:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:20">1 John 4:20</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:43">Matthew 5:43</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:24">Matthew 6:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:28">Ephesians 5:28</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:29">Ephesians 5:29</span>. "In the former case, hatred, which may become a moral duty, involves the subjection of an instinct. In the latter case it expresses a general determination of character" (Westcott). </p>
<p>His brother [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον αδελφον] </span></span>. His fellow - Christian. The singular, brother, is characteristic of this Epistle. See vv. 10, 11; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:10">1 John 3:10</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:15">1 John 3:15</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:17">1 John 3:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:20">John 4:20</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:21">John 4:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:16">John 5:16</span>. Christians are called in the New Testament, Christians (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:26">Acts 11:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:28">Acts 26:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:16">1 Peter 4:16</span>), mainly by those outside of the Christian circle. Disciples, applied to all followers of Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:11">John 2:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:61">John 6:61</span>) and strictly to the twelve (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:5">John 13:5</span> sqq.). In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:1">Acts 19:1</span>, to those who had received only John's baptism. Not found in John's Epistles nor in Revelation. Brethren. The first title given to the body of believers after the Ascension (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:15">Acts 1:15</span>, where the true reading is aJudelfwn brethren, for maqhtwn disciples). See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:30">Acts 9:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:23">Acts 10:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:29">Acts 11:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:10">1 Thessalonians 4:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:26">1 Thessalonians 5:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:14">1 John 3:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:5">3 John 1:5</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:10">3 John 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:23">John 21:23</span>. Under three forms : The believers (oiJ pistoi; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:45">Acts 10:45</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:12">1 Timothy 4:12</span>); they that believe (oiJ pisteuontev; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:7">1 Peter 2:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:7">1 Thessalonians 1:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:19">Ephesians 1:19</span>); they that believed (oiJ pisteusantev; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:44">Acts 2:44</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:32">Acts 4:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:3">Hebrews 4:3</span>).
