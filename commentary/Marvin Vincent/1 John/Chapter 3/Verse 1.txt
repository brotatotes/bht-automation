<p>Behold [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδετε] </span></span>. Lit., behold ye. The plural is peculiar. The usual form is the singular ide or ijdou. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:29">John 1:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:3">John 11:3</span>, etc.; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:35">John 4:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:26">John 19:26</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:27">John 19:27</span>. Elsewhere the plural is used of something actually visible (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:11">Galatians 6:11</span>). What manner of [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποταπην] </span></span>. The word is of infrequent occurrence in the New Testament, but is found in all the Synoptists and in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:11">2 Peter 3:11</span>. Only here in John's writings. Originally it means from what country or race; then, of what sort or quality. It is used of the quality of both persons and things. </p>
<p>Hath bestowed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεδωκεν] </span></span>. Emphasizing the endowment of the receiver. Compare carizomai, from cariv grace, favor, which emphasizes the goodwill of the giver. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:18">Galatians 3:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:9">Philippians 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:29">Philippians 1:29</span>. That [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα] </span></span>. See on Joh 25:13. </p>
<p>We should be called [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κληθωμεν] </span></span>. Or, named. As <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:23">Matthew 2:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:13">Matthew 21:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:13">Luke 1:13</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:31">Luke 1:31</span>, etc. The verb is never used by John of the divine call. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:3">John 10:3</span>, for kalei calleth, read fwnei. </p>
<p>The sons [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεκνα] </span></span>. Rev., better, children. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:12">John 1:12</span>. </p>
<p>And such we are [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">και εσμεν] </span></span>. Lit., and we are. Added by Rev., according to the best texts. A parenthetical, reflective comment, characteristic of John. See on 1 <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:2">1 John 1:2</span>.</p>
