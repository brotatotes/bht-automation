<p>Cain who was [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιν ην] </span></span>. Who is not in the Greek. The construction is irregular. Lit., as Rev., not as Cain was of the evil one. </p>
<p>Slew [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσφαξεν] </span></span>. The verb occurs only in John, and only here outside of Revelation. Originally, to slay by cutting the throat; so in Homer, of cattle : </p>
<p>"the suitor train who slay [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σφαζουσι</span></span>] His flocks and slow - paced beeves with crooked horns." " Odyssey, " 1, 92. </p>
<p>To slaughter victims for sacrifice : </p>
<p>"Backward they turned the necks of the fat beeves, And cut their throats [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσφαζαν] </span></span>, and flayed the carcasses." " Iliad, " 1, 459. </p>
<p>Thence, generally, to slay or kill. </p>
<p>Wherefore [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριν τινος] </span></span>. Lit., on account of what. Carin for the sake of, on account of, is elsewhere placed after the genitive. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:1">Ephesians 3:1</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:14">Ephesians 3:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:14">1 Timothy 5:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:19">Galatians 3:19</span>.</p>
