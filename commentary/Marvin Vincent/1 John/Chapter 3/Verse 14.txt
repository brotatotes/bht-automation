<p>We know. Emphatic; we as distinguished from the world. </p>
<p>Have passed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεατβεβηκαμεν] </span></span>. Lit., have passed over. </p>
<p>From death [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ του θανατου] </span></span>. Lit., out of the death. The article marks it as one of the two spheres in which men must be; death or life. The death, the life, present one of those sharp oppositions which are characteristic of the Epistle; as love, hatred; darkness, light; truth, a lie. O qanatov the death, occurs in John's Epistles only here and in the next clause. In the Gospel, only <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:24">1 John 5:24</span>. Personified in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+1:18">Revelation 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+6:8">Revelation 6:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+9:6">Revelation 9:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+20:13">Revelation 20:13</span>. Unto life [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις την ζωην] </span></span>. Rev., better, into. Compare enter into the life, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:8">Matthew 28:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:17">Matthew 19:17</span>. </p>
<p>Because. The sign of having passed into life; not the ground. </p>
<p>We love the brethren [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπωμεν του, ς αυδελφους] </span></span>. The only occurrence of the phrase. Elsewhere, love one another, or love his brother. See on 2 9. </p>
