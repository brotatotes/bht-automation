<p>Beloved. Again the recognition of danger from false spirits prompts this affectionate address. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:21">1 John 3:21</span>. </p>
<p>Try [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκιμαζετε] </span></span>. Better, as Rev., prove. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:7">1 Peter 1:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:55">Luke 12:55</span>. Compare the phrase discerning of spirits, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:10">1 Corinthians 12:10</span>. </p>
<p>Of God [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ] </span></span>. Out of : proceeding from. </p>
<p>False prophets [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψευδοπροφηται] </span></span>. The term is applied in the New Testament to rivals of true prophets under the old dispensation (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:26">Luke 6:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:1">2 Peter 2:1</span>), and to rivals of the apostles under the gospel economy (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:15">Matthew 7:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:11">Matthew 24:11</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:24">Matthew 24:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:22">Mark 13:22</span>). In Revelation to "the embodied power of spiritual falsehood" (xvi. 13; Mr 19:20; Mr 20:10). The false prophet supports his claims by signs and portents (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:24">Matthew 24:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:6">Acts 13:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+19:20">Revelation 19:20</span>) and is thus distinguished from the false teacher. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:1">2 Peter 2:1</span>, where the two terms occur together. </p>
<p>Are gone out [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαληλυθασιν] </span></span>. The perfect tense indicates that the influence of their going out on their false mission is in operation at the present.</p>
