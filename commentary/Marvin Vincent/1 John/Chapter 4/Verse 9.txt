<p>Toward us [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ημιν] </span></span>. Wrong. Not "among us," as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:14">John 1:14</span>, nor "in us;" but as Rev., in margin, in our case. 67 Sent [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεσταλκεν] </span></span>. John describes the incarnation as a sending, more frequently than in any other way. Apostellw is to send under commission, as an envoy. The perfect tense, hath sent, points to the abiding results of the sending. See on 3 5. </p>
<p>His only - begotten Son [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον υιον αυτου τον μονογενη] </span></span>. Lit., His Son, the only - begotten (Son). A mode of expression common in John, enlarging upon the meaning of a noun by the addition of an adjective or a participle with the article. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:2">1 John 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:7">1 John 2:7</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:8">1 John 2:8</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:25">1 John 2:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:4">1 John 5:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:41">John 6:41</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:44">John 6:44</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:50">John 6:50</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:51">John 6:51</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:1">John 14:1</span>, etc. On only - begotten, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:14">John 1:14</span>.</p>
