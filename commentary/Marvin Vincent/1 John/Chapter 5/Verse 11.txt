<p>Hath given [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδωκεν] </span></span>. The aorist tense, gave. So Rev. The reference is to the historic fact of the gift. So <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:23">1 John 3:23</span> : "We should love one another as He gave [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδωκεν</span></span>] us commandment." Ver 24 "We know that He abideth in us by the Spirit which He gave [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδωκεν</span></span>] us." On the other hand, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:1">1 John 3:1</span> : "Behold what manner of love the Father hath bestowed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεδωκεν</span></span>] upon us." The gift of love abides in the fact that we are now children of God (ver. 2). </p>
<p>Eternal life [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωην αιωνιον] </span></span>. Compare the phrase thn zwhn thn aijwnion the life, the eternal life (i. 2), and hJ aijwniov zwh the eternal life (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:3">John 17:3</span>). For the distinction between the phrases see on 1 2. The phrase here, without either article, merely defines the character of the life.</p>
