<p>Have I written [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγραψα] </span></span>. Lit., I wrote. John speaks as looking back over his Epistle and recalling the aim with which he wrote. See on 2 13. May know [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειδητε] </span></span>. Not perceive [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινωσκειν] </span></span>, but know with settled and absolute knowledge. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:24">John 2:24</span>. </p>
<p>Ye have eternal life [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωην εχετε αιωνιον] </span></span>. The Greek order is peculiar, "ye may know that life ye have eternal." The adjective eternal is added as an after - thought. So Westcott : "that ye have life - yes, eternal life." </p>
<p>Unto you that believe. In the A. V., these words follow have I written. The Rev. follows the Greek order. The words, like eternal, above, are added as an after - thought, defining the character of the persons addressed. On the name [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το ονομα</span></span>] . See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:23">John 2:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:12">John 1:12</span>.</p>
