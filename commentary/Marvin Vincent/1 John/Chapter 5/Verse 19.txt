<p>We are of God [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ του θεου εσμεν] </span></span>. For the phrase ei=nai ejk to be from, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:46">John 1:46</span>. For ejsmen we are, see on 3 1. John expresses the relation of believers to God by the following phrases : To be born or begotten of God, gennhqhnai ejk tou Qeou (v. 1; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:29">1 John 2:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:7">1 John 4:7</span>) : denoting the initial communication of the new life. To be of God, ei=nai ejk tou Qeou (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:47">John 8:47</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:10">1 John 3:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:6">1 John 4:6</span>) : denoting the essential connection in virtue of the new life. Child of God, teknon Qeou (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:12">John 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:1">1 John 3:1</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:10">1 John 3:10</span>) : denoting the relation established by the new life. </p>
<p>World [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοσμος] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:9">John 1:9</span>. </p>
<p>Lieth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κειται] </span></span>. The word is stronger than ejsti is, indicating the passive, unprogressive state in the sphere of Satan 's influence. "While we are from God, implying a birth and a proceeding forth, and a change of state, the kosmov the world, all the rest of mankind, remains in the hand of the evil one" (Alford). </p>
<p>In wickedness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω πονηρω] </span></span>. Rev., better, in the evil one. The expression to lie in has a parallel in Sophocles' "Anti - gone :" ejn uJmin gar wJv Qew keimeqa tlamonev "Wretched we lie in you as in a God" (247).</p>
