<p>Did signify [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδηλου] </span></span>. Imperfect tense : better, was declaring, all along through the prophetic age, in successive prophets. See the same verb in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:13">1 Corinthians 3:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:14">2 Peter 1:14</span>. </p>
<p>When it testified beforehand [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προμαρτυρομενον] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. </p>
<p>Of Christ [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις χριστον] </span></span>. Lit., unto Christ. So Rev., in margin. The sufferings destined for Christ, as in ver. 10 he speaks of the grace, eijv uJmav, unto you; i e., destined to come unto you. Peter was especially concerned to show that the sufferings of Christ were in fulfilment of prophecy, because it was a subject of dispute with the Jews whether the Christ was to suffer (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:18">Acts 3:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:22">Acts 26:22</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:23">Acts 26:23</span>). </p>
<p>The glory [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τας δοξας] </span></span>. Rev., correctly, the glories. The plural is used to indicate the successive steps of his glorification; the glory of his resurrection and ascension, of the last judgment, and of the kingdom of heaven.</p>
