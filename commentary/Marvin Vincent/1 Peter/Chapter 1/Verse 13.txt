<p>Gird up [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναζωσαμενοι] </span></span>. Lit., having girded up. Used here only. The metaphor is suggested by the girding up of the loose eastern robes preparatory to running or other exertion. Perhaps recalling the words of Christ, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:35">Luke 12:35</span>. Christ 's call is a call to active service. There is a fitness in the figure as addressed to sojourners and pilgrims (ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:1">1 Peter 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:11">1 Peter 2:11</span>), who must be always ready to move. </p>
<p>Mind [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διανοιας] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:30">Mark 12:30</span>. </p>
<p>Be sober [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νηφοντες] </span></span>. Lit., being sober. Primarily, in a physical sense, as opposed to excess in drink, but passing into the general sense of self - control and equanimity. </p>
<p>Hope to the end [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελειως ελπισατε] </span></span>. Better, as Rev., set your hope perfectly : wholly and unchangeably; without doubt or despondency. That is to be brought [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την φερομενην] </span></span>. Lit., which is being brought, as Rev., in margin. The object of hope is already on the way.</p>
