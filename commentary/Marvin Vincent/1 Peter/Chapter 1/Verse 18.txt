<p>Ye were redeemed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελυτρωθητε] </span></span>. The verb occurs only in two other passages, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:21">Luke 24:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:14">Titus 2:14</span>. It carries the idea of a ransom - price (lutron, from luw, to loose). </p>
<p>With silver or gold [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αργυριω η χρυσιω] </span></span>. Lit., with silver or gold money; the words meaning, respectively, a small coin of silver or of gold. Conversation. Rev., manner of life. See on ver. 15. </p>
<p>Received by tradition from your fathers [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πατροπαραδοτου] </span></span>. A clumsy translation; improved by Rev., handed down from your fathers. The word is peculiar to Peter.</p>
