<p>But with the precious blood of Christ. The word Cristou, of Christ, stands at the end of the sentence, and is emphatic. Render, as Rev., with precious blood as of a lamb, etc., even the blood of Christ. Lamb. Peculiarly appropriate from Peter. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:35-42">John 1:35-42</span>. The reference is to a sacrificial lamb. </p>
<p>Without blemish [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμωμου] </span></span>. Representing the Old - Testament phrase for absence of physical defect (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+12:5">Exodus 12:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+22:20">Leviticus 22:20</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:14">Hebrews 9:14</span>). </p>
<p>Without spot [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασπιλου] </span></span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:14">1 Timothy 6:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:27">James 1:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:14">2 Peter 3:14</span>. In each case in a moral sense.</p>
