<p>According to [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα] </span></span>. In virtue of; in accordance with. </p>
<p>Foreknowledge [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προγνωσιν] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:23">Acts 2:23</span>, in Peter's sermon at Pentecost. He is distinguishing there between foreknowledge and determinate counsel. </p>
<p>The Father. Implying that the relation contemplated by the divine foreknowledge is a new relation of sonship. </p>
<p>In sanctification [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αγιασμω] </span></span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:13">2 Thessalonians 2:13</span>. The spiritual state in which the being elected to salvation is realized. The word is peculiarly Pauline, occurring eight times in Paul 's epistles, and besides only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:14">Hebrews 12:14</span>. </p>
<p>Unto obedience [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις] </span></span>. Note the three prepositions : according to [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span>] the foreknowledge; in [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span>] sanctification; unto [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span>] obedience. The ground, sphere, and end of spiritual sanctification. </p>
<p>Sprinkling [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ραντισμον] </span></span>. Here in a passive sense - the being sprinkled. Properly, the ritualistic act of sprinkling blood or water. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="nu+19:19">Numbers 19:19</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="nu+19:21">Numbers 19:21</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:13">Hebrews 9:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:24">Hebrews 12:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="nu+19:9">Numbers 19:9</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="nu+19:13">Numbers 19:13</span>, where the water in which were the ashes of the red heifer is called udwr rJantismou, water of sprinkling (Septuagint), which the A. V. and Rev. Old Testament render water of separation. The word and its kindred verb occur only in Hebrews and Peter. </p>
<p>Jesus Christ. The foreknowledge of the Father, the sanctification of the Spirit, the obedience and sprinkling of the blood of Jesus Christ the Son. The Father foreknowing, the Son atoning, the Spirit applying the Son's work in sanctifying. "The mystery of the Trinity and the economy of our salvation are intimated in this verse" (Bengel). </p>
<p>Grace and peace [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρις - ειρηνη] </span></span>. Pauline terms. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:7">Romans 1:7</span>. The salutation is peculiar by the addition of be multiplied, which occurs <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:2">2 Peter 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:2">Jude 1:2</span>, and nowhere else in the salutations of the epistles. It is found, however, in the Septuagint, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="da+4:1">Daniel 4:1</span> (LXX 3 31), and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="da+6:25">Daniel 6:25</span>. Professor Salmond observes : "If the Babylon from which Peter writes can be taken to be the literal Babylon (see on 5 13), it might be interesting to recall the epistles introduced by salutations so similar to Peter's, which were written from the same capital by two kings, Nebuchadnezzar and Darius, of two great dynasties, and addressed to all their provinces."</p>
