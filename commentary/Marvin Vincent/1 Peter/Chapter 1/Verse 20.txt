Foreordained [προεγνωσμενου] . Lit., and better, foreknown, as Rev. Manifested [φανερωθεντος] . Observe the difference in tense. Foreknown is the perfect participle, has been known from all eternity down to the present : "in reference to the place held and continuing to be held by Christ in the divine mind" (Salmond). Manifested is the aorist participle, pointing to a definite act at a given time. In these last times [επ εσχατου των χρονων] . Lit., as Rev., at the end of the times.
