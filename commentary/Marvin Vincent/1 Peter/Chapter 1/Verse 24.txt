<p>Of man. Following the reading ajnqrwpou, in the Septuagint, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+40:6">Isaiah 40:6</span>, which Peter quotes here. But the best texts read aujthv, of it, or, as Rev., thereof. </p>
<p>Withereth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξηρανθη] </span></span>. Literally, the writer puts it as in a narrative of some quick and startling event, by the use of the aorist tense : withered was the grass. Similarly, the flower fell [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεπεσεν] </span></span>. Lit., fell off, the force of ejk.</p>
