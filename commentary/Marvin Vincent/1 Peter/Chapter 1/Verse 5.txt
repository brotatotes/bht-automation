<p>Kept [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φρουρουμενους] </span></span>. A military term. Lit., garrisoned. Rev., guarded. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:32">2 Corinthians 11:32</span>, and the beautiful metaphorical use of the word at <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+4:7">Philippians 4:7</span>, "shall guard your hearts." The present participle indicates something in progress, a continuous process of protection. Hence, lit., who are being guarded. "The inheritance is kept; the heirs are guarded" (Bengel). </p>
<p>By [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span>] the power; through [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια</span></span>] faith; unto [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span>] salvation. By, indicating the efficient cause; through, the secondary agency; unto, the result. </p>
<p>Salvation. Note the frequent occurrence of this word, vv. 9, 10. Ready [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετοιμην] </span></span>. Stronger than about to be, or destined to be, implying a state of waiting or preparedness, and thus harmonizing with reserved.</p>
