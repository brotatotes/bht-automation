Beloved [αγαπητοι] . A favorite term with Peter, occurring eight times in the epistles. See the phrase, our beloved Barnabas and Paul, Acts 14:25, in the letter sent by the council at Jerusalem to the Gentile Christians, the account of which, doubtless, came from Peter. Compare our beloved brother Paul, 2 Peter 3:15. Strangers [παροικους] . Rev., sojourners. Compare ch. 1 17, "the time of your sojourning [παροικιας] ." Which [αιτινες] . The compound pronoun denotes a class, of that kind which, classifying all fleshly desires in one category.
