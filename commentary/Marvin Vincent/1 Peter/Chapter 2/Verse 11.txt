<p>Beloved [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπητοι] </span></span>. A favorite term with Peter, occurring eight times in the epistles. See the phrase, our beloved Barnabas and Paul, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:25">Acts 14:25</span>, in the letter sent by the council at Jerusalem to the Gentile Christians, the account of which, doubtless, came from Peter. Compare our beloved brother Paul, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:15">2 Peter 3:15</span>. </p>
<p>Strangers [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παροικους] </span></span>. Rev., sojourners. Compare ch. 1 17, "the time of your sojourning [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παροικιας] </span></span>." </p>
<p>Which [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιτινες] </span></span>. The compound pronoun denotes a class, of that kind which, classifying all fleshly desires in one category.</p>
