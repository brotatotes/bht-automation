<p>Put to silence [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιμουν] </span></span>. A very graphic word, meaning to muzzle or gag. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:9">1 Corinthians 9:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:18">1 Timothy 5:18</span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:12">Matthew 22:12</span>. Ignorance [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγνωσιαν] </span></span>. In classical Greek it is an ignorance arising from not coming into contact with the person or thing to be known. It occurs only once again in the New Testament, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:34">1 Corinthians 14:34</span>. Here is signifies not want of acquaintance, but of understanding; a state of ignorance. </p>
<p>Of foolish men [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των αφρονων ανθρωπων] </span></span>. Of the foolish men; the article referring to those just mentioned, who speak against them as evil - doers.</p>
