<p>Coming [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσερχομενοι] </span></span>. Indicating a close [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς</span></span>] and an habitual (present participle) approach and an intimate association. </p>
<p>A living stone [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λιθον ζωντα] </span></span>. Omit as unto. So Rev. The words are in apposition with whom (Christ). Compare Peter's use of the same word, stone, in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:11">Acts 4:11</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:42">Matthew 21:42</span>. It is not the word which Christ uses as a personal name for Peter [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πετρος] </span></span>; so that it is not necessary to infer that Peter was thinking of his own new name. </p>
<p>Disallowed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποδεδοκιμασμενον] </span></span>. Rev., rejected. See on the simple verb, ch. 1 7. The word indicates rejection after trial. </p>
<p>Of God [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα θεω] </span></span>. Of in the A. V. is equivalent to by; but para has a stronger sense, implying the absolute power of decisive choice which is with God. Render, as Rev., with God; i e., God being judge; and compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:26">Matthew 19:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:11">Romans 2:11</span>. </p>
<p>Precious [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντιμον] </span></span>. At ch. 1 19 (precious blood) another word is used [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιμιος] </span></span>, denoting essential preciousness. The word here indicates the preciousness as recognized or held in honor.</p>
