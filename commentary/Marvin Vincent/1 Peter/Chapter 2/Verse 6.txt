<p>It is contained [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιεχει] </span></span>. From peri, round about, and ecw, to hold. Hence, to contain or comprehend. So <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:9">Luke 5:9</span>, he was astonished [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θαμβος αυτον περιεσχες] </span></span>; lit., astonishment held him encompassed. Also, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:25">Acts 23:25</span>, " He wrote a letter after this manner [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιεχουσαν τον τυπον τουτον] </span></span>; lit., containing this form. The verb here is impersonal. The kindred word perioch occurs only in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:32">Acts 8:32</span>, rendered place; i e., the passage of scripture : either the contents of the passage or the section of the book circumscribed or marked off. </p>
<p>In the scripture [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν γραφη] </span></span>. The best texts reject the article. Grafh means a passage of scripture. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:10">Mark 12:10</span>. Hence Rev., in scripture; margin, in a scripture. </p>
<p>Behold I lay, etc. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:33">Romans 9:33</span>. </p>
<p>Precious. See on ver. 4.</p>
