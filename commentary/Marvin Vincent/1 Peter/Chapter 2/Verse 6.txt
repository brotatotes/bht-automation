It is contained [περιεχει] . From peri, round about, and ecw, to hold. Hence, to contain or comprehend. So Luke 5:9, he was astonished [θαμβος αυτον περιεσχες] ; lit., astonishment held him encompassed. Also, Acts 23:25, " He wrote a letter after this manner [περιεχουσαν τον τυπον τουτον] ; lit., containing this form. The verb here is impersonal. The kindred word perioch occurs only in Acts 8:32, rendered place; i e., the passage of scripture : either the contents of the passage or the section of the book circumscribed or marked off. In the scripture [εν γραφη] . The best texts reject the article. Grafh means a passage of scripture. See on Mark 12:10. Hence Rev., in scripture; margin, in a scripture. Behold I lay, etc. See Romans 9:33. Precious. See on ver. 4.
