<p>By which [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ω] </span></span>. Wrong. Rev., correctly, in which : in the spiritual form of life; in the disembodied spirit. </p>
<p>Went and preached [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορευθεις εκηρυξεν] </span></span>. The word went, employed as usual of a personal act; and preached, in its ordinary New - Testament sense of proclaiming the Gospel. </p>
<p>To the spirits [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευμασιν] </span></span>. As in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:23">Hebrews 12:23</span>, of disembodied spirits, though the word yucai, souls, is used elsewhere (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+6:9">Revelation 6:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+20:4">Revelation 20:4</span>). </p>
<p>In prison [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν φυλακη] </span></span>. Authorities differ, some explaining by <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:4">2 Peter 2:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:6">Jude 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+20:7">Revelation 20:7</span>, as the final abode of the lost. Excepting in the last passage, the word occurs nowhere else in the New Testament in a metaphorical sense. It is often translated watch (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:25">Matthew 14:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:8">Luke 2:8</span>); hold and cage (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+18:2">Revelation 18:2</span>). Other explain as Hades, the kingdom of the dead generally.</p>
