<p>A gift [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρισμα] </span></span>. Originally, something freely given : a gift of grace [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρις] </span></span>. Used in New Testament (a) of a blessing of God graciously bestowed, as upon sinners (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:15">Romans 5:15</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:16">Romans 5:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:29">Romans 11:29</span>); (b) of a gracious divine endowment : an extraordinary gift of the Holy Spirit dwelling and working in a special manner in the individual (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:14">1 Timothy 4:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:6">2 Timothy 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:6">Romans 12:6</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:8">Romans 12:8</span>). So here. </p>
