<p>The spirit of glory and of God [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το της δοξης και το του θεου πνευμα] </span></span>. Lit., the spirit of glory and that of God. The repetition of the article identifies the spirit of God with the spirit of glory : the spirit of glory, and therefore the spirit of God : who is none other than the spirit of God himself. Hence Rev., better, the spirit of glory and the spirit of God. Resteth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναπαυεται] </span></span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+11:2">Isaiah 11:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:6">Luke 10:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="nu+11:25">Numbers 11:25</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="nu+11:26">Numbers 11:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:31">Mark 6:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:45">Matthew 26:45</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+14:13">Revelation 14:13</span>. Also, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:28">Matthew 11:28</span>, where the word is used in the active voice, to give rest or refreshment.</p>
