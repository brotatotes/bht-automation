<p>Followers [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιμηται] </span></span>. More literally and better, imitators. Only once outside of Paul 's writings, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:12">Hebrews 6:12</span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:9">1 Thessalonians 3:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:0">1 Thessalonians 2:0</span> Thessalonians 7; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:16">1 Corinthians 4:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:1">1 Corinthians 11:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:12">Galatians 4:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+3:17">Philippians 3:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+4:9">Philippians 4:9</span>. </p>
<p>And of the Lord. Guarding against any possible imputation of self - assertion or conceit. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:1">1 Corinthians 11:1</span>. </p>
<p>Tribulation [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θλιψει] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:21">Matthew 13:21</span>. Referring especially to persecutions at the hands of the Jews (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:5">Acts 17:5</span> ff.), which probably continued after Paul 's departure from Thessalonica.</p>
