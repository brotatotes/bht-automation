<p>Also - we. Also may point to an expression of thanksgiving in a letter from the Thessalonians to Paul. You say "we give thanks to God." We also give thanks. Comp. ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:2">1 Thessalonians 1:2</span>. </p>
<p>When ye received the word of God which ye heard of us [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραλαβοντες λογον ακοης παρ ημων του θεου] </span></span>. Rend. when ye received the word of the message (which came) from us, even the word of God. The words the word of the message from us form one conception, governed by paralabontev having received or when ye received; therefore from us is not to be taken as depending on having received, as Rev. when ye received from us the word, etc. Of God (supply the word) is added in order to correct any possible false impression made by from us. Akoh in N. T. means the sense of hearing, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:14">Matthew 13:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:17">1 Corinthians 12:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:8">2 Peter 2:8</span> : or the organ of hearing = ear, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:35">Mark 7:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:1">Luke 7:1</span> : or a thing heard, a report, rumor, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:38">John 12:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:16">Romans 10:16</span>. The phrase logov ajkohv or thv ajkohv the word of hearing, or word of the message, signifies the word which is heard. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:2">Hebrews 4:2</span>. See on the fame, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:37">Luke 4:37</span>. </p>
<p>Effectually worketh [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεργειται] </span></span>. Referring to the word, not to God. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:13">Philippians 2:13</span>. In the middle voice as here, used only by Paul and James, and only of things. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:20">Ephesians 3:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:29">Colossians 1:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:16">James 5:16</span>, and footnote on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:29">Colossians 1:29</span>. The noun ejnergeia, P o. It means power in exercise, and is used only of superhuman power.</p>
