<p>In Christ Jesus. Seems to be added to distinguish the Christian churches in Judaea from the synagogues of the Jews, which would claim to be churches of God. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:22">Galatians 1:22</span>, and see on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:1">1 Thessalonians 1:1</span>. In Christ Jesus, in Christ, in Jesus, in the Lord, in him, are common Pauline formulas to denote the most intimate communion with the living Christ. These phrases are not found in the Synoptic Gospels. En ejmoi in me (Christ) is frequent in the Fourth Gospel. The conception is that of a sphere or environment in which a Christian or a church lives, as a bird in the air, or the roots of a tree in the soil. 20 Countrymen [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμφυλετων] </span></span>. N. T. o. o LXX Not in pre - Christian Greek writers. Lit. belonging to the same tribe or clan. The reference is to the Gentile persecutors who were instigated by the Jews.</p>
