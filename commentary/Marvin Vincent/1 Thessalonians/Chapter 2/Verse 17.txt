<p>Being taken from you [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απορφανισθεντες] </span></span>. N. T. o. o LXX Rev. better, being bereaved of you. From ojrfanov bereft. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:40">Mark 12:40</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:18">John 14:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:27">James 1:27</span>. The word suggests the intimate personal fellowship of the writer with his readers. The separation was like that between parents and children. Comp. vv. 7, 8. </p>
<p>For a short time [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς καιρον ωρας] </span></span>. N. T. o. Lit. for the season of an hour. Comp. Lat. horae momentum. Stronger than the usual phrase prov wran for an hour : see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:8">2 Corinthians 7:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:5">Galatians 2:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:15">Philippians 1:15</span>. Comp. prov kairon for a season, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:13">Luke 8:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:5">1 Corinthians 7:5</span>. The more abundantly [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περισσοτερως] </span></span>. Rev. the more exceedingly. Paul uses this adverb very freely, and outside of his letters it appears only <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:1">Hebrews 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:19">Hebrews 13:19</span>. He is much given to the use of comparatives, and sometimes heaps them together : see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:37">Romans 8:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:13">2 Corinthians 7:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:17">2 Corinthians 4:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:20">Ephesians 3:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:23">Philippians 1:23</span>.</p>
