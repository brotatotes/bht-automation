<p>Hope. Used of the object of hope, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:5">Colossians 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:1">1 Timothy 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:18">Hebrews 6:18</span>. </p>
<p>Joy - crown [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρα - στεφανος] </span></span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+4:1">Philippians 4:1</span>. The phrase crown of rejoicing or boasting, in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="pr+16:31">Proverbs 16:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eze+16:12">Ezekiel 16:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eze+23:42">Ezekiel 23:42</span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+61:3">Isaiah 61:3</span>, stefanov kallouv crown of beauty, and Soph. Aj. 465. stef eujkleiav crown of renown. The Thessalonians were "a chaplet of victory of which Paul might justly make his boast in the day of the Lord" (Ellicott). For stefanov see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+4:4">Revelation 4:4</span>. </p>
