<p>Having suffered before [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προπαθοντες] </span></span>. N. T. o. Although we had suffered. </p>
<p>Having been shamefully entreated [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υβρισθεντες] </span></span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:32">Matthew 22:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:5">Acts 14:5</span>. This may have been added because propaqontev alone might denote the experience of something good; but it is more probably intended as an expansion and illustration of that word. Paul 's sensitiveness to personal indignity appears in the narrative in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:0">Acts 16:0</span>, which gives the historical explanation of the two words. It appears frequently in 2nd Corinthians. </p>

<p>We waxed bold [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαρρησιασαμεθα] </span></span>. Only once elsewhere in Paul, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:20">Ephesians 6:20</span>. Frequent in Acts. Always in N. T. in connection with speaking. Derived from pan every, and rJhsiv speaking. Hence parrhsia boldness, bold speaking out of every word. The noun is very often used adverbially, as parrhsia boldly or openly, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:32">Mark 8:32</span>; see also <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:20">John 18:20</span>. In Acts always meta parrhsiav with boldness, comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:16">Hebrews 4:16</span>. En parrhsia in boldness, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:4">John 7:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:29">John 16:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:19">Ephesians 6:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:20">Philippians 1:20</span>. Both the verb and the noun are found in LXX See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+26:13">Leviticus 26:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="pr+10:10">Proverbs 10:10</span>; Wisd. 5 1; 1 Macc. 4 18; Sir. 6 11. In our God [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω θεω ημων] </span></span>. Const. with we waxed bold. Their boldness was not mere natural courage, but was inspired by God. There is a slight emphasis on our God, as contrasted with the idols from which they had turned (ch <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:9">1 Thessalonians 1:9</span>). The phrase only here in N. T. </p>
<p>Gospel of God (eujaggelion tou Qeou). For the phrase see Mk. i. 14; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:1">Romans 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:16">Romans 14:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:7">2 Corinthians 11:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:8">1 Thessalonians 2:8</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:9">1 Thessalonians 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:17">1 Peter 4:17</span>. It points to the monotheistic character of the gospel. </p>
<p>In much contention [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν πολλω αγωνι] </span></span>. Better conflict. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+2:1">Colossians 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:27">Philippians 1:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:12">1 Timothy 6:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:1">Hebrews 12:1</span>. Agwn originally of a contest in the arena; but it is used of any struggle, outward or inward.</p>
