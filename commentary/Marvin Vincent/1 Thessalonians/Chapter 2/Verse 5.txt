<p>Used we flattering words [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν λογω κολακιας εγενηθημεν] </span></span>. Better, were we found using flattering discourse. Very literally and baldly it is, we came to pass in discourse of flattery. It means more than the mere fact that they were not flatterers : rather, they did not prove to be such in the course of their work. Similar periphrases with ejn are found, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:44">Luke 22:44</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:17">Acts 22:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:7">2 Corinthians 3:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:7">Philippians 2:7</span>; with eijv, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:42">Matthew 21:42</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:10">Mark 12:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:17">Luke 20:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:11">Acts 4:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:5">1 Thessalonians 3:5</span>. Kolakia flattery, N. T. o. o LXX Rare in Class. Logw is explained by some as report or rumor. Common report did not charge us with being flatterers. This meaning is admissible, but the other is simpler. Paul says that they had not descended to flattery in order to make the gospel acceptable. They had not flattered men's self - complacency so as to blind them to their need of the radical work which the gospel demands. </p>
<p>Cloke of covetousness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προφασει πλεονεξιας] </span></span>. For profasiv see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:22">John 14:22</span>. Properly pretext : pro before, fasiv a word or saying. Others, less probably, from profainein to cause to shine forth or before. Paul means that he had not used his apostolic office to disguise or conceal avaricious designs. </p>
<p>God is witness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεος μαρτυς] </span></span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:9">Romans 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:23">2 Corinthians 1:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:8">Philippians 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:10">1 Thessalonians 2:10</span>. God or the Lord is witness is a common O. T. formula : see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+31:44">Genesis 31:44</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+31:50">Genesis 31:50</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1sa+12:5">1 Samuel 12:5</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1sa+12:6">1 Samuel 12:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1sa+20:23">1 Samuel 20:23</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1sa+20:42">1 Samuel 20:42</span>; Wisd. 1 6. For testimony to his conduct, he appeals to the Thessalonians (as ye know) : for testimony to his motives, he appeals to God. Comp. ver. 10, where there is the double appeal.</p>
