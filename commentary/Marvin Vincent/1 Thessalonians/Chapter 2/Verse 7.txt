<p>Gentle [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηπιοι] </span></span>. This reading is adopted b; y Tischendorf, Weiss, and the Rev. T. Westcott and Hort read nh. pioi babes. This gives a stronger and bolder image, and one which falls in better with the course of thought, in which Paul is asserting his innocence of guile and flattery, and not of harshness. </p>
<p>Among you [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν μεσω υμων] </span></span>. Better, and more literally, in the midst of you, which implies more intimate intercourse than among you. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:27">Luke 22:27</span>. </p>
<p>Nurse [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τροφος] </span></span>. N. T. o. In Class. sometimes of a mother, and so probably here. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:19">Galatians 4:19</span>. </p>
<p>Cherisheth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θαλπη] </span></span>. P o. Here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:29">Ephesians 5:29</span>. The verb originally means to warm. See LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+22:6">Deuteronomy 22:6</span>. </p>
<p>Her own children. Note the inversion of metaphor. Paul is first the babe, then the nurse or mother. For similar instances see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:2">1 Thessalonians 5:2</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:4">1 Thessalonians 5:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:13-16">2 Corinthians 3:13-16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:1">Romans 7:1</span> ff. See Introduction to 2 Corinthians, Vol. 3, p. 19</p>
