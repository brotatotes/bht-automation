Being affectionately desirous [ομειρομενοι] . N. T. o. Once in LXX, Job 3:21. The figure of the nursing mother is continued. She is not satisfied with nursing the child, but interests herself affectionately in all that concerns it. We were willing [ηυδοκουμεν] . Better, we were pleased. Imperfect tense : we continued to entertain and manifest our affectionate solicitude. The verb occasionally in later Greek, and often in LXX In N. T. it is used of God 's decrees, as Luke 12:32; 1 Corinthians 1:21; Galatians 1:15; Colossians 1:19; and of the free determination and plans of men, as Romans 14:26; 2 Corinthians 5:8; 1 Thessalonians 3:1. Souls [ψυχας] . Better lives. See on Romans 11:3; 1 Corinthians 14:45; Mark 12:30.
