<p>Being affectionately desirous [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομειρομενοι] </span></span>. N. T. o. Once in LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="job+3:21">Job 3:21</span>. The figure of the nursing mother is continued. She is not satisfied with nursing the child, but interests herself affectionately in all that concerns it. </p>
<p>We were willing [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηυδοκουμεν] </span></span>. Better, we were pleased. Imperfect tense : we continued to entertain and manifest our affectionate solicitude. The verb occasionally in later Greek, and often in LXX In N. T. it is used of God 's decrees, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:32">Luke 12:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:21">1 Corinthians 1:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:15">Galatians 1:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:19">Colossians 1:19</span>; and of the free determination and plans of men, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:26">Romans 14:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:8">2 Corinthians 5:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:1">1 Thessalonians 3:1</span>. </p>
<p>Souls [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψυχας] </span></span>. Better lives.
