<p>Minister [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονον] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:26">Matthew 20:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:35">Mark 9:35</span>. Not in the official sense of deacon which occurs only in the Pastorals. Diakonov minister and diakonia ministry or service are common expressions of service to Christ or to men. Paul habitually uses them in this way. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:25">Acts 1:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:4">Acts 6:4</span>. Diakonoi is used of ministers of Satan, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:15">2 Corinthians 11:15</span>, and diakonov of the civil magistrate, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:4">Romans 13:4</span>. See Introduction to the Pastoral Epistles. 23 Fellow laborer. Omit from text. 24 To establish [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στηριξαι] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:32">Luke 22:32</span>; Introd. to Catholic Epistles, Vol. 1, p. 625; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:10">1 Peter 5:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:12">2 Peter 1:12</span>.</p>
