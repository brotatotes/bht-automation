<p>Study [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλοτιμεισθαι] </span></span>. P o. Make it your aim. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:20">Romans 14:20</span> (see note); <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:9">2 Corinthians 5:9</span>. Often in Class. Lit. to be fond of honor : hence to strive for honor, to be ambitious. </p>
<p>To be quiet [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ησυχαζειν] </span></span>. Note the paradox, strive to be quiet. For similar instances see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:20">Romans 1:20</span>, unseen things clearly seen : <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:22">Romans 1:22</span>, wise, be fooled (comp. Horace, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ob+1:34">Obadiah 1:34</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ob+1:2">Obadiah 1:2</span>, insaniens sapientia) : <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:2">2 Corinthians 8:2</span>, poverty abounded unto riches : <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:10">2 Corinthians 7:10</span>, repentance, not to be repented of. The disturbances rebuked in the second Epistle may have begun to show themselves, so that there is a possible allusion to the idle busybodies of <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:11">2 Thessalonians 3:11</span>.</p>
