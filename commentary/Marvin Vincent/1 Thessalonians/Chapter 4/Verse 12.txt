<p>Honestly [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευσχημονως] </span></span>. P o. Better, seemly. From euj well and schma figure or fashion. The literal sense is suggested by the familiar phrase in good form. The contrast appears in ajtaktwv disorderly, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:6">2 Thessalonians 3:6</span>. Paul has in view the impression to be made by his readers on those outside of the church. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:13">Romans 13:13</span>, and comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:40">1 Corinthians 14:40</span>. </p>
<p>Of nothing [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηδενος] </span></span>. Either neuter, of nothing, or masculine, of no man. In the latter case it would refer to depending upon others for their support, which some, in view of the immediately expected parousia, were disposed to do, neglecting their own business.</p>
