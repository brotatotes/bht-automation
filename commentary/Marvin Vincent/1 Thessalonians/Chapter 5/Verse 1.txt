<p>Times - seasons [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρονων - καιρων] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:7">Acts 1:7</span>. With special reference to the Lord 's coming. The plural is used because Paul is thinking of a number of incidents attending the preparation and accomplishment of the second advent, and occurring at different times. The collocation times and seasons only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:7">Acts 1:7</span>. Kairov is the suitable time, cronov the time measured by duration. Hence kairov a juncture, an occasion, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:3">Matthew 16:3</span>. The distinction is so well marked that have the phrases cronou kairov the right moment of the time, and eukairov cronov the opportune moment. See Soph. Elec. 1292.</p>
