<p>Who died. Frequently the resurrection is coupled with the death of Christ by Paul, as ch. 4 14; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+3:10">Philippians 3:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+2:12">Colossians 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+3:1-4">Colossians 3:1-4</span>. Not so here; but the thought of resurrection is supplied in live together with him. </p>
<p>Wake or sleep. Whether we are alive or dead at Christ 's appearing. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:9">Romans 14:9</span>. Kaqeudein in N. T. always literally of sleep, except here, and possibly <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:14">Ephesians 5:14</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:39">Mark 5:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:52">Luke 8:52</span>, it is contrasted with death. In LXX in the sense of death, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+87:5">Psalms 87:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="da+12:2">Daniel 12:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2sa+7:12">2 Samuel 7:12</span>.</p>
