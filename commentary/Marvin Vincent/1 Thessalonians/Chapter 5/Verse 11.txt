<p>Comfort [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρακαλειτε] </span></span>. Rev. renders exhort; but comfort suits better the general drift of the passage, and corresponds with ch. 4 18. There is some force in Bornemann's suggestion that the two meanings may be combined. Exhort each other to be of good heart. </p>
<p>Edify [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικοδομειτε] </span></span>. Lit. build up. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:32">Acts 20:32</span>. The metaphorical sense habitually in Paul. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+8:1">1 Corinthians 8:1</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+8:10">1 Corinthians 8:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:23">1 Corinthians 10:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:4">1 Corinthians 14:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:20">Ephesians 2:20</span>. In O. T. mostly in the literal sense. See however LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ru+4:11">Ruth 4:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+27:5">Psalms 27:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+88:2">Psalms 88:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+31:4">Jeremiah 31:4</span>.</p>
