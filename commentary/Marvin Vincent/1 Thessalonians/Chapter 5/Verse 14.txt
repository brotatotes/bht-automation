<p>Them that are unruly [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους ατακτους] </span></span>. N. T. o The A. V. is more vigorous and less stilted than Rev. disorderly. From aj not and tassein draw up or arrange. Those who are out of line. Comp. the adverb ajgaqov disorderly, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:6">2 Thessalonians 3:6</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:11">2 Thessalonians 3:11</span>. Probably referring to the idlers and busybodies described there. </p>
<p>Feeble - minded [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ολιγοψυχους] </span></span>. N. T. o. Better fainthearted. Oligov little and yuch soul. Those of little heart. oClass. In LXX see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="pr+14:29">Proverbs 14:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+25:5">Isaiah 25:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+54:6">Isaiah 54:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+57:15">Isaiah 57:15</span>. Oligoyucia faint - heartedness, o N. T. LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+6:9">Exodus 6:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+54:8">Psalms 54:8</span>. Comp. Ps. of Sol., 16 11. </p>
<p>Support [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντεχεσθε] </span></span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:24">Matthew 6:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:9">Titus 1:9</span>. Anti against and ecesqai to hold one's self. The primary sense is, keeping one's self directly opposite to another so as to sustain him.</p>
