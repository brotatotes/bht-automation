<p>When they shall say. The prediction is thrown into dramatic form. Cometh upon [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισταται] </span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:34">Luke 21:34</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:36">Luke 21:36</span>. Often in N. T. of a person coming suddenly upon another; as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:9">Luke 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:4">Luke 24:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:1">Acts 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:7">Acts 12:7</span>. Travail [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωδιν] </span></span>. Birth - throe. Only here in its literal sense. Elsewhere as a strong figure of sorrow or pain. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:8">Matthew 24:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:8">Mark 13:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:24">Acts 2:24</span>. For the figure in O. T. see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+13:6-8">Isaiah 13:6-8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+37:3">Isaiah 37:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mic+4:9">Micah 4:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ho+13:3">Hosea 13:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+13:21">Jeremiah 13:21</span>. </p>
<p>Shall not escape [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη εκφυγωσιν] </span></span>. A. V. misses the force of the double negative. They shall in no wise escape.</p>
