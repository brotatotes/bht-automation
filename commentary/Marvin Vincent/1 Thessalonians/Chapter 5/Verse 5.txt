<p>Ye are all. In the text gar for should be inserted after pantev all. Ye are not in darkness for ye are sons of light. </p>
<p>Children of light [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υιοι φωτος] </span></span>. More correctly, sons of light. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:17">Mark 3:17</span>, and comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:8">Luke 16:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:36">John 12:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:8">Ephesians 5:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:12">Colossians 1:12</span>. The Christian condition is habitually associated in N. T. with light : see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:14">Matthew 5:14</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:16">Matthew 5:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:21">John 3:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:12">John 8:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:18">Acts 26:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:9">1 Peter 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:7">1 John 1:7</span>. The contrary condition with darkness : see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:19">John 3:19</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:20">John 3:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:8">Ephesians 5:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:9">1 Peter 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:16">Matthew 4:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:23">Matthew 6:23</span>, etc. </p>
<p>Of the night - of darkness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυκτος - σκοτους] </span></span>. The genitive marks an advance of thought from ejn skotei in darkness, ver. 4. En indicates the element in which one is. The genitive, of darkness, points to nature and origin. To belong to darkness is more than to be in darkness.</p>
