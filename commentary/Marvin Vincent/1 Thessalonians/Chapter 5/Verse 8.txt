<p>Putting on [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδυσαμενοι] </span></span>. The son of day clothes himself for the day's work or battle. The same association of ideas as in vv. 6, 8, is found in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:12-14">Romans 13:12-14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+16:15">Revelation 16:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:13">1 Peter 1:13</span>. Comp. LXX, Bar 5:2. </p>
<p>Breastplate - helmet. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:14">Ephesians 6:14</span>. The figures are not original with Paul. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+59:17">Isaiah 59:17</span>; Wisd. 5 18, 19. Notice that only defensive armor is mentioned, in accordance with the darkness and uncertainty of the last time; and that the fundamental elements of Christian character, faith, hope, and love, are brought forward again as in ch. 1 3; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:13">1 Corinthians 13:13</span>. For the figure of the armed soldier, comp. also <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:12">Romans 13:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:4">2 Corinthians 10:4</span>.</p>
