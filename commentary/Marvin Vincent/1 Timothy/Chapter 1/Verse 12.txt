<p>Hath enabled [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδυναμωσαντι] </span></span>. An unclassical word, found in Paul and Acts. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:22">Acts 9:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+4:13">Philippians 4:13</span>. Three times in the Pastorals. Counted [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγησατο] </span></span>. A common Pauline word. </p>
<p>Putting [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεμενος] </span></span>. Better appointing. The participle defines counted me faithful. He counted me faithful in that he appointed, etc. </p>
<p>Into the ministry [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις διακονιαν] </span></span>. Better, appointing me to his service. The conventional phrase "the ministry" gives a wrong impression. The term is general, covering every mode of service, either to God or to men. Diakonoi ministers is used of the secular ruler, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:4">Romans 13:4</span>. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:5">1 Corinthians 12:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:15">1 Corinthians 16:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:7">2 Corinthians 3:7</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:8">2 Corinthians 3:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:12">Ephesians 4:12</span>, and on minister, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:26">Matthew 20:26</span>.</p>
