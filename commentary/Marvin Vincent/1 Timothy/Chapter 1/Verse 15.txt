<p>This is a faithful saying [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστος ο λογος] </span></span>. Better, faithful is the saying. A favorite phrase in these Epistles. o P. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:1">1 Timothy 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:9">1 Timothy 4:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:11">2 Timothy 2:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:8">Titus 3:8</span>. </p>
<p>Worthy of all acceptation [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πασης αποδοχης αξιος] </span></span>. The phrase only here and ch. 4 9. Apodoch Past o o LXX Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:41">Acts 2:41</span>, ajpodexamenoi ton logon received his word. Pashv all or every describes the reception of which the saying is worthy as complete and excluding all doubt. </p>
<p>Came into the world [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηλθεν εις τον κοσμον] </span></span>. The phrase is unique in the Pastorals, and does not appear in Paul. It is Johannine. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:9">James 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:19">James 3:19</span>; Jas 21:27; Jas 12:46. </p>
<p>To save sinners [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναρτωλους σωσαι] </span></span>. The thought is Pauline, but not the phrase. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:56">Luke 9:56</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:10">Luke 19:10</span>. </p>
<p>Chief [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτος] </span></span>. Or foremost. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:9">1 Corinthians 14:9</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:8">Ephesians 3:8</span>. This expression is an advance on those.</p>
