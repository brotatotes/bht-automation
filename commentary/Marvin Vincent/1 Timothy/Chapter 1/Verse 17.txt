<p>King eternal [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασιλει των αιωνων] </span></span>. Lit. the king of the ages. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+14:3">Revelation 14:3</span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:2">Hebrews 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:3">Hebrews 11:3</span>. In LXX, Tob. 6 10. For kindred expressions in LXX, see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+14:18">Exodus 14:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1sa+13:13">1 Samuel 13:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+9:7">Psalms 9:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+28:10">Psalms 28:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+73:12">Psalms 73:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+144:13">Psalms 144:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+145:0">Psalms 145:0</span>. See also additional note on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:9">2 Thessalonians 1:9</span>. </p>
<p>Immortal [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφθαρτω] </span></span>. Lit. Incorruptible. In Paul, applied to God only, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:23">Romans 1:23</span>. </p>
<p>Invisible [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αορτω] </span></span>. Applied to God, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:15">Colossians 1:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:27">Hebrews 11:27</span>. The only wise God [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μονω θεω] </span></span>. Wise should be omitted. Rend. The only God. Sofw wise was interpolated from Rom 16:27 - the only instance in which Paul applies the term to God. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:4">Jude 1:4</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:25">Jude 1:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:21">Luke 5:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:4">James 5:4</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:4">James 5:4</span>. </p>
<p>Honor and glory [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιμη και δοξα] </span></span>. This combination in doxology only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+5:12">Revelation 5:12</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+5:13">Revelation 5:13</span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+4:9">Revelation 4:9</span>. In doxologies Paul uses only doxa glory, with the article, the glory, and with to whom or to him. (Be). </p>
<p>Forever and ever [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τους αιωνας των αιωνων] </span></span>. Lit unto the aeons of the aeons. The formula in Paul, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:26">Romans 16:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:5">Galatians 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+4:20">Philippians 4:20</span>. Also in Hebrews and 1 Peter, and often in Revelation The doxology as a whole is unique in N. T.</p>
