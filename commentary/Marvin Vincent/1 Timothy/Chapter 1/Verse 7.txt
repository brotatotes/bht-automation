<p>Desiring [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θελοντες] </span></span>. The participle is explanatory and confirmatory of the preceding statement : since they desire. </p>
<p>Teachers of the law [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νομοδιδασκαλοι] </span></span>. o P. It occurs in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:17">Luke 5:17</span> and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:34">Acts 5:34</span>. Nomov is, apparently, the Mosaic law. These teachers may have been arbitrary interpreters of that law, but in what way, cannot be shown. </p>
<p>Understanding [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νοουντες] </span></span>. Better, though they understand. </p>
<p>What they say - whereof they affirm [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">α λεγουσιν - περι τινων διαβεβαιουνται] </span></span>. The latter expression is an advance on the former, as appears not only from the verbs themselves, but from the different pronominal expressions. They know not what they say, nor what kind of things they are of which they speak so confidently. The compound diabebaiouswai to affirm, Past o. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:8">Titus 3:8</span>. The false teachers announce their errors with assurance.</p>
