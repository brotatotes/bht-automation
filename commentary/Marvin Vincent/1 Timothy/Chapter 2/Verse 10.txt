<p>Professing [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαγγελλομεναις] </span></span>. In the sense of professing only in the Pastorals. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:2">Titus 1:2</span>, and everywhere else in N. T. it means promise. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:5">Acts 7:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:21">Romans 4:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:19">Galatians 3:19</span>, etc. </p>
<p>Godliness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεοσεβειαν] </span></span>. N. T. o. Several times in LXX The adjective qeosebhv worshipping God, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:31">John 9:31</span>. It is = eujsebeia. See ver. 2. Const. by good works with professing godliness : omit the parenthesis which - godliness; take which [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο</span></span>] as = with that which [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τουτω ο</span></span>] and construe it with adorn. The whole will then read : "That women adorn themselves in modest apparel, with shamefastness and sobriety; not with braided hair, or gold, or pearls, or costly array, but (adorn themselves) with that which becometh women professing godliness through good works." 100</p>
