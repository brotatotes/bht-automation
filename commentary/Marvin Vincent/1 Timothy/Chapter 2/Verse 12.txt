<p>Suffer [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιτρεπω] </span></span>. Lit. turn over to; thence, permit. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:34">1 Corinthians 14:34</span>. </p>
<p>Usurp authority [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυθεντειν] </span></span>. N. T. o. o LXX, o Class. It occurs in late ecclesiastical writers. The kindred noun aujqenthv one who does a thing with his own hand, Wisd. 12 6, and also in Herodotus, Euripides, and Thucyelides. Auqentia right, 3 Macc. 2 29. The verb means to do a thing one's self; hence, to exercise authority. The A. V. usurp authority is a mistake. Rend. to have or exercise dominion over.</p>
