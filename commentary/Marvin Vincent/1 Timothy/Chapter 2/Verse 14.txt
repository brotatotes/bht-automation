<p>Was not deceived [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ ηπατηθη] </span></span>. Once in Paul, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:6">Ephesians 5:6</span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:3">2 Corinthians 11:3</span>. Rev. beguiled. As it is evident that Adam was beguiled, the interpreters have tried many ways of explaining the expression, either by supplying prwtov first, or by saying (as Bengel) that the woman did not deceive the man, but persuaded him; or by supplying by the serpent, or so long as he was alone; or by saying that Eve was directly and Adam indirectly deceived. </p>
<p>Being deceived [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαπατηθεισα] </span></span>. completely or thoroughly beguiled. Was in the transgression [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν παραβασει γεγονεν] </span></span>. A. V. misses the force of gegonen. Ginesqai ejn often signifies the coming or falling into a condition, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:11">Acts 12:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:17">Acts 22:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+1:10">Revelation 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:3">1 Corinthians 2:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:7">2 Corinthians 3:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:5">1 Thessalonians 2:5</span>. Rend. hath fallen into transgression.</p>
