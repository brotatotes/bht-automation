<p>These also [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">και ουτοι δε] </span></span>. As well as the Bishops. No mention is made of a proving of the Bishops, but this may be fairly assumed. Comp. not a novice, ver. 6. </p>
<p>Be proved [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκιμαζεσθωσαν] </span></span>. Common in Paul; only here in Pastorals. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:7">1 Peter 1:7</span>. Not implying a formal examination, but a reference to the general judgment of the Christian community as to whether they fulfil the conditions detailed in ver. 8. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:22">1 Timothy 5:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:2">2 Timothy 2:2</span>. Let them use the office of a deacon [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονειτωσαν] </span></span>. Much better, let them serve as deacons. In this sense only in the Pastorals. Comp. ver. 13. 104 The verb is very common in N. T. </p>
<p>Being blameless [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεγκλητοι οντες] </span></span>. Rather, unaccused : if no charge be preferred against them. In Paul, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:8">1 Corinthians 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:22">Colossians 1:22</span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:6">Titus 1:6</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:7">Titus 1:7</span>. It is a judicial term. The participle ontev signifies provided they are.</p>
