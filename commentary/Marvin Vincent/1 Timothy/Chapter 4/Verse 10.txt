<p>Therefore [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τουτο] </span></span>. More correctly, to this end; or with a view to this. </p>
<p>We labor and strive [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοπιωμεν και αγωνιζομεθα] </span></span>. Both Pauline words. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:29">Colossians 1:29</span>, where the two are found together as here. Also on kopou labor, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:3">1 Thessalonians 1:3</span>, and kopiwntav, and laboring <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:12">1 Thessalonians 5:12</span>. Comp. ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:17">1 Timothy 5:17</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:6">2 Timothy 2:6</span>. Both words denote strenuous and painful effort. 111 The kai; has an ascensive force : </p>
<p>"we labor, yea struggle." </p>
<p>We trust in [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηλπικαμεν επι] </span></span>. Better, have set our hope on. The verb with ejpi in Pastorals, in Paul, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:12">Romans 14:12</span>, a citation, and in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:13">1 Peter 1:13</span>.</p>
