<p>To reading [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγνωσει] </span></span>. Three times in N. T. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:15">Acts 13:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:14">2 Corinthians 3:14</span>. The verb ajnaginwskein usually of public reading. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:16">Luke 4:16</span>. So in LXX In post - classical Greek, sometimes of reading aloud with comments. See Epictetus, Diss. 3, 23, 20. Dr. Hatch says : "It is probable that this practice of reading with comments... may account for the coordination of 'reading' with 'exhortation' and 'teaching' in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:13">1 Timothy 4:13</span>." </p>
<p>Exhortation [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη παρακλησει] </span></span>. Often in Paul. See on consolation, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:24">Luke 6:24</span>, comfort, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:31">Acts 9:31</span>, and comforter, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:16">John 14:16</span>.</p>
