<p>Take heed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεχε] </span></span>. Only here in Pastorals, and once in Paul, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:16">Philippians 2:16</span>. Quite frequent in LXX Lit. hold upon, fasten thy attention on, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:7">Luke 14:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:5">Acts 3:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:22">Acts 19:22</span>. In LXX, in the sense of apply, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="job+18:2">Job 18:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="job+30:26">Job 30:26</span>; or forbear, refrain, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ki+22:6">1 Kings 22:6</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ki+22:15">1 Kings 22:15</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:16">Philippians 2:16</span>, to hold out or present, a sense which is found only in Class. Unto thyself and unto the doctrine [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σεαυτω και τη διδασκαλια] </span></span>. Better, to thyself and to thy teaching. The order is significant. Personality goes before teaching. </p>
<p>Continue in them [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιμενε αυτοις] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:1">Romans 6:1</span>. In LXX only <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+12:39">Exodus 12:39</span>. Autoiv is neuter, referring to these things, ver. 15. A. V. in them is indefinite and ambiguous. Better, continue in these things. </p>
