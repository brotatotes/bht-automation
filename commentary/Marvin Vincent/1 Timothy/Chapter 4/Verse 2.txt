<p>Speaking lies in hypocrisy [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν υποκρισει ψευδολογων] </span></span>. Wrong. Rend., through the hypocrisy of men that speak lies. Upokrisiv hypocrisy once in Paul, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:13">Galatians 2:13</span>, see note. See also on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:13">Matthew 23:13</span>. The phrase ejn uJpokrisei only here. Yeudologov speaking lies, N. T. o. o LXX Rare in Class. </p>
<p>Having their conscience seared with a hot iron [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν υποκρισει ψευδολογων</span></span>] Better, branded in their own conscience. With a hot iron is superfluous. The verb N. T. o. o LXX, o Class. The metaphor is from the practice of branding slaves or criminals, the latter on the brow. These deceivers are not acting under delusion, but deliberately, and against their conscience. They wear the form of godliness, and contradict their profession by their crooked conduct (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:5">2 Timothy 3:5</span>). The brand is not on their brow, but on their conscience. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:15">Titus 1:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:11">Titus 3:11</span>.</p>
