<p>If thou put the brethren in remembrance of these things [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταυτα υποτιθεμενος τοις αδελφοις] </span></span>. The verb only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:4">Romans 16:4</span>. Lit. to put under; so almost without exception in LXX See, for instance, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+28:18">Genesis 28:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+47:29">Genesis 47:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+17:12">Exodus 17:12</span>. So <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:4">Romans 16:4</span>. Hence, metaphorically, to suggest, which is, literally, to carry or lay under. Tauta these things are those mentioned vv. 4, 5. In the Pastorals it is only here that aJudelfoi brethren means the members of the church to whose superintendent the letter is addressed. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:21">2 Timothy 4:21</span>, they are the Christians of the church from which the letter comes; in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:2">1 Timothy 6:2</span>, Christians in general; and in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:1">1 Timothy 5:1</span>, without any ecclesiastical sense. </p>
<p>Minister of Jesus Christ [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονος χριστου ιησου] </span></span>. Rendering Christ himself a service by setting himself against ascetic errors. For diakonov minister see on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:8">1 Timothy 3:8</span>. Here in the general sense of servant, without any official meaning. Paul 's more usual phrase is servant of God : servant [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διακονος</span></span>] of Christ twice, and diakonov Ihsou Cristou not at all. Paul uses doulov bond - servant with Jesus Christ. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:23">2 Corinthians 11:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:7">Colossians 1:7</span>; and comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:1">Romans 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:10">Galatians 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:1">Philippians 1:1</span>. </p>

<p>Whereunto thou hast attained [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η παρηκολουθηκας] </span></span>. Wrong. Rend., which thou hast closely followed. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:10">2 Timothy 3:10</span>. The verb means, primarily, to follow beside, to attend closely. In this literal sense not in N. T. To attend to or follow up, as a disease. So Plato, Rep. 406 B, parakolouqwn tw noshmati qanasimw perpetually tending a mortal disease. To follow up a history or a succession of incidents, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:3">Luke 1:3</span>. o P. The writer means that Timothy, as a disciple, has closely attended to his course of Christian instruction.</p>
