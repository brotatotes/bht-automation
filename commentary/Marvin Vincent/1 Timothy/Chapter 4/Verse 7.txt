<p>Shun [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραιτου] </span></span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+5:11">Leviticus 5:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:23">2 Timothy 2:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:10">Titus 3:10</span>. o P. The primary meaning is to ask as a favor (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:6">Mark 14:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:19">Hebrews 12:19</span>). Mostly in this sense in LXX, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1sa+20:6">1 Samuel 20:6</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1sa+20:28">1 Samuel 20:28</span>. To deprecate; to prevent the consequences of an act by protesting against and disavowing it, as 3 Macc. 6 27. To beg off, get excused, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:18">Luke 14:18</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:19">Luke 14:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:0">Luke 4:0</span> Macc. 11 2. To decline, refuse, avoid, as here, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:11">Acts 25:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:25">Hebrews 12:25</span>. </p>
<p>Profane. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:9">1 Timothy 1:9</span>, and comp. ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:20">1 Timothy 6:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:16">2 Timothy 2:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:16">Hebrews 12:16</span>. </p>
<p>Old wives ' [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γραωδεις] </span></span>. N. T. o. o LXX From grauv an old woman, and eidov form. </p>
<p>Fables [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυθους] </span></span>. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:4">1 Timothy 1:4</span>, and comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:4">2 Timothy 4:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:14">Titus 1:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:16">2 Peter 1:16</span>. </p>
<p>Exercise [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γυμναζε] </span></span>. o P. Only here in Pastorals. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:14">Hebrews 5:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:11">Hebrews 12:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:14">2 Peter 2:14</span>. From gumnov naked. In Class. Of training naked in gymnastic exercises; also, metaphorically, of training for or practicing an art or profession.</p>
