<p>They learn [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μανθανουσιν] </span></span>. To be taken absolutely, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:31">1 Corinthians 14:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:7">2 Timothy 3:7</span>. They go about under the influence of an insatiable curiosity, and meet those who "creep into houses and take captive silly women" (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:7">2 Timothy 3:7</span>), and learn all manner of nonsense and error. </p>
<p>Going about [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιερχομεναι] </span></span>. o P. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:13">Acts 19:13</span>. </p>
<p>Tattlers [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φλυαροι] </span></span>. N. T. o. Comp. 4 Macc. 5 10. The verb fluarein to prate, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:10">3 John 1:10</span>. </p>
<p>Busybodies [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιεργοι] </span></span>. In this sense only here. Comp. ta perierga curious arts, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:19">Acts 19:19</span>. The participle periergazomenoi busybodies, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:11">2 Thessalonians 3:11</span>. See note. Rend. the whole passage : "And withal, being also idle, they learn, gadding about from house to house; and not only (are they) idle, but tattlers also, and busybodies, speaking things which they ought not." 123</p>
