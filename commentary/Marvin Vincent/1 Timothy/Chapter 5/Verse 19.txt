<p>Receive not an accusation [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηγοριαν μη παραδεχου] </span></span>. Neither word in Paul. For accusation see on. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:45">John 5:45</span>. It means a formal accusation before a tribunal. The compound verb para emphasises the giver or transmitter of the thing received : to receive from another. But [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκτος ει μη] </span></span>. Except. A pleonastic formula, except in case. The formula in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:5">1 Corinthians 14:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:9">1 Corinthians 14:9</span>. </p>
<p>Before [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επι] </span></span>. Or on the authority of. On condition that two witnesses testify. The O. T. law on this point in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+19:15">Deuteronomy 19:15</span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:16">Matthew 18:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:17">John 8:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:0">John 9:0</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:1">1 Corinthians 13:1</span>.</p>
