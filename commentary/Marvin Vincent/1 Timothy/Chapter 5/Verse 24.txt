<p>Open beforehand [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προδηλοι] </span></span>. A. V. wrong in giving pro a temporal force, whereas it merely strengthens dhloi evident, manifest. The meaning is openly manifested to all eyes. In N. T. only here, ver. 25, and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:14">Hebrews 7:14</span>. In LXX, see Judith 8 29; 2 Macc. 3 17; 14 39. </p>
<p>Going before to judgment (proagousai eijv krisin). Proagein, o P. In N. T. habitually with a local meaning, either intransitive, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:9">Matthew 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:22">Matthew 14:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:9">Mark 11:9</span>; or transitive, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:6">Acts 12:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:5">Acts 17:5</span>. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:1">Acts 17:1</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:25">Acts 17:25</span> The meaning here is that these open sins go before their perpetrator to the judgment - seat like heralds, proclaiming their sentence in advance. Krisin, not specifically of the judgment of men or of the final judgment of God, or of the sentence of an ecclesiastical court - but indefinitely. The writer would say : no judicial utterance is necessary to condemn them of these sins. The word in Paul, only <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:5">2 Thessalonians 1:5</span>. </p>
<p>They follow after (ejpakolouqousin). The verb only here, ver. 24, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:21">1 Peter 2:21</span>, and (the disputed) <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+16:20">Mark 16:20</span>. The sins follow up the offender to the bar of judgment, and are first made openly manifest there.</p>
