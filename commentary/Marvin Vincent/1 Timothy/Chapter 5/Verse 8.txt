<p>Provide [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προνοει] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:17">Romans 12:17</span>. </p>
<p>His own - those of his own house [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των ιδιων - οικειων] </span></span>. His own relations, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:11">John 1:11</span>. Those who form part of his family, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:10">Galatians 6:10</span>. </p>
<p>He hath denied the faith [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την πιστιν ηρνηται] </span></span>. The verb not in Paul, but Quite often in Pastorals. The phrase only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+2:13">Revelation 2:13</span>. Faith demands works and fruits. By refusing the natural duties which Christian faith implies, one practically denies his possession of faith. Faith does not abolish natural duties, but perfects and strengthens them " (Bengel). Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:14-17">James 2:14-17</span>. </p>
<p>Infidel [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απιστου] </span></span>. Better, unbeliever. One who is not a Christian, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:6">1 Corinthians 6:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:12">1 Corinthians 7:12</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:13">1 Corinthians 7:13</span>, etc. Even an unbeliever will perform these duties from natural promptings.</p>
