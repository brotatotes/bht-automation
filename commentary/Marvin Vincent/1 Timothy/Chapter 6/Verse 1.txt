<p>As many servants as are under the yoke <span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">(οσοι εισιν υπο ζυγον δουλοι] </span></span>. Incorrect. Rather, as many as are under the yoke as bondservants. As bondservants is added in explanation of under the yoke, which implies a hard and disagreeable condition. Yoke is used only here of the state of slavery. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:1">Galatians 5:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:10">Acts 14:10</span>, of the Mosaic law. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:29">Matthew 11:29</span>. </p>
<p>Their own [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους ιδιους] </span></span>. Lit. private, personal, peculiar, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:8">1 Corinthians 3:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:7">1 Corinthians 7:7</span>. Sometimes strange, eccentric. Constrasted with dhmosiov public or koinov common. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:32">Acts 4:32</span>. Sometimes without emphasis, substantially = possessive pronoun, just as Lat. proprius passes into suus or ejus, or oijkeiov belonging to one's house into the simple one's own. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:10">Galatians 6:10</span>, and comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:5">Matthew 22:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:14">Matthew 25:14</span>. In LXX commonly with the emphatic sense. Very often in the phrase kat' ijdian privately, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:34">Mark 4:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:10">Luke 9:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:2">Galatians 2:2</span>, but nowhere in Pastorals. </p>
<p>Masters [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεσποτας] </span></span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:9">Titus 2:9</span>, and see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:1">2 Peter 2:1</span>. Not in Paul, who styles the master of slaves kuriov Lord. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:9">Ephesians 6:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+4:1">Colossians 4:1</span>. </p>
<p>Count [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγεισθωσαν] </span></span>. Implying a more conscious, a surer judgment, resting on more careful weighing of the facts. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:3">Philippians 2:3</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:6">Philippians 2:6</span>. Be not blasphemed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη - βλασφημηται] </span></span>. Or be evil spoken of. See on blasphemy, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:22">Mark 7:22</span>, and be evil spoken of, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:16">Romans 14:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:30">1 Corinthians 10:30</span>. Paul uses the word, but not in the active voice as in the Pastorals.</p>
