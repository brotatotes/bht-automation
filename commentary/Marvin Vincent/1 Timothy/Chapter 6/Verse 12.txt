<p>Fight the good fight [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγωνιζου τον καλον αγωνα] </span></span>. A phrase peculiar to the Pastorals. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:7">2 Timothy 4:7</span>. Not necessarily a metaphor from the gymnasium or arena, although ajgwn contest was applied originally to athletic struggles. But it is also used of any struggle, outward or inward. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+2:1">Colossians 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+4:12">Colossians 4:12</span>. </p>
<p>Lay hold [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλαβου] </span></span>. o P. Frequent in Luke and Acts. Occasionally in this strong sense, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:20">Luke 20:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:26">Luke 23:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:17">Acts 18:17</span>, but not usually. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:23">Mark 8:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:47">Luke 9:47</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:27">Acts 9:27</span>. </p>
<p>Professed a good profession [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωμολογησας την καλην ομολογιαν] </span></span>. Both the verb and the noun in Paul, but this combination only here. For the use of kalov good see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:18">1 Timothy 1:18</span>, and ver. 12. Rend. confessed the good confession, and see on your professed subjection, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:13">2 Corinthians 9:13</span>. It is important to preserve the force of the article, a point in which the A. V. is often at fault.</p>
