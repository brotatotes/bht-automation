<p>In his times [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιροις ιδιοις] </span></span>. Better, his own seasons, or its own seasons. wither the seasons proper to the appearing, or the seasons which God shall see fit to select. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:6">1 Timothy 2:6</span> Potentate [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυναστης] </span></span>. Only here of God. Very often in LXX See Sir. 46 5; q 2 Macc. 12 15, etc. In Class. applied to Zeus (Soph. Antig. 608). In Aesch. Agam. 6, the stars are called lamproi dunastai bright rulers, as the regulators of the seasons. </p>
<p>Of kings [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των βασιλευοντων] </span></span>. Lit. of those who rule as kings. Only here for the noun, basilewn. Basileuv bsilewn king of kings, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+17:14">Revelation 17:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+19:16">Revelation 19:16</span>. </p>
<p>Of lords [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυριευοντων] </span></span>. Lit. of those who Lord it. Only here for the noun kuriwn. See kuriov kuriwn Lord of lords, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+19:16">Revelation 19:16</span>; comp. LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+10:17">Deuteronomy 10:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+135:3">Psalms 135:3</span>. Probably liturgical.</p>
