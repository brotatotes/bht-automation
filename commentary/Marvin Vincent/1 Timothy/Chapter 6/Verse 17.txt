<p>Them that are rich in this world [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις πλουσιοις εν τω νυν αιωνι] </span></span>. forming one conception. Chrysostom says :; "Rich in this world, for others are rich in the world to come." Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:25">Luke 16:25</span>. Plousiov rich, by Paul only metaphorically. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:9">2 Corinthians 8:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:4">Ephesians 2:4</span>. The phrase oJ nun aijwn the now age, only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:19">Titus 2:19</span>, the usual expression being oJ aijwn ou=tov this age or world, which is not found in Pastorals. </p>
<p>Be not highminded [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη υψηλοφρονειν] </span></span>. The verb N. T. o. o LXX, o Class. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:20">Romans 11:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:16">Romans 12:16</span>. </p>
<p>Uncertain riches [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλουτου αδηλοτητι] </span></span>. A rendering which weakens the sense by withdrawing the emphasis from the thought of uncertainty. Rend. the uncertainty of riches. For a similar construction see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:4">Romans 6:4</span>. Adhlothv uncertainty, N. T. o. o LXX Originally obscurity. Ploutov wealth, frequent in Paul, but never in the material sense. The play upon the word rich in this and the next verse will be noticed. </p>
<p>To enjoy [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις απολαυσιν] </span></span>. Lit. for enjoyment. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:25">Hebrews 11:25</span>. See 3 Macc. 7 16. In class. occasionally, but the verb ajpolauein to have enjoyment or benefit is common. A contrast is implied between being highminded on account of wealth - cherishing and worshipping it - and rightly enjoying it. The true character of such enjoyment is shown in the next verse.</p>
