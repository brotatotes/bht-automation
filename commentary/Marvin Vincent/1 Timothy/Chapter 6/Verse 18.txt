<p>Do good [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαθοεργειν] </span></span>. In this uncontracted form, N. T. o. o LXX, o Class. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:17">Acts 14:17</span>. The usual word is ajgaqopoiein, see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:4">Mark 3:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:9">Luke 6:9</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:33">Luke 6:33</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:35">Luke 6:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:15">1 Peter 2:15</span>. o P. who has ejrgazesqai to ajgaqon to work that which is good, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:10">Romans 2:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:10">Galatians 6:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:28">Ephesians 4:28</span>. Good works [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργοις καλοις] </span></span>. For kalov see on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:7">1 Timothy 3:7</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:11">John 10:11</span> : for ajgaqov on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:7">Romans 5:7</span>. </p>
<p>Ready to distribute [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευμεταδοτους] </span></span>. N. T. o. o LXX, o Class. For the verb metadidonai to impart to the poor, see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:11">Luke 3:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:28">Ephesians 4:28</span>. Willing to communicate [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινωνικους] </span></span>. N. T. o. o LXX See on fellowship, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:48">Acts 2:48</span>, and comp. koinwnein to partake, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:22">1 Timothy 5:22</span>, and koinov common, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:14">Titus 1:14</span>. Stronger than the preceding word, as implying a personal share in the pleasure imparted by the gift.</p>
