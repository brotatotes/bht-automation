<p>CHAPTER I </p>
<p>1 An apostle by the will of God. So 2nd Corinthians, Ephesians, Colosians. 1st Corinthians adds called or by call [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλητος] </span></span>. </p>
<p>According to the promise, etc. [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατ επαγγελιαν] </span></span>. Apostolov kata does not appear in any of the Pauline salutations. In 1 Timothy. kat' ejpitaghn according to the commandment, and in Titus kata pistin etc., according to the faith, etc. Kat' ejpaggelian, though in other connections, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:23">Acts 13:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:29">Galatians 3:29</span>. Epaggelia, primarily announcement, but habitually promise in N. T. In Pastorals only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:8">1 Timothy 4:8</span>. With the promise of the life in Christ goes the provision for its proclamation. Hence the apostle, in proclaiming "ye shall live; through Christ," is an apostle according to the promise. </p>
