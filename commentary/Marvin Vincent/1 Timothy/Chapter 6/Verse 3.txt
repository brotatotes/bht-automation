<p>Teach otherwise [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετεροδιδασκαλει] </span></span>. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:3">1 Timothy 1:3</span>. </p>
<p>Consent [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσερχεται] </span></span>. Lit. draw nigh. To approach as one who confidingly accepts another's proffer. Hence, to assent to. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:28">Acts 10:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:4">1 Peter 2:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:16">Hebrews 4:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:22">Hebrews 10:22</span>. Often in LXX, and habitually in the literal sense. The figurative sense, Sir. 1 27, 30; 4 15; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:26">Hebrews 6:26</span>. o P. The phrase only here. </p>
<p>Of our Lord, etc. Either concerning our Lord, or spoken by him. Probably the latter, according to N. T. usage, in which word of the Lord or word of God commonly means the word that proceeds from God. The phrase words of our Lord Jesus Christ only here. </p>
<p>Doctrine which is according to godliness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη κατ ευσεβειαν διδασκαλια] </span></span>. The phrase only here. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:10">1 Timothy 1:10</span>. For eujsebeia, on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:2">1 Timothy 2:2</span>.</p>
