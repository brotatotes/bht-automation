<p>Perverse disputings [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαπαρατριβαι] </span></span>. N. T. o. o LXX, o Class. Paratribh, is a rubbing against. Dia signifies constinuance. The meaning therefore is continued friction. Hence wearing discussion; protracted wrangling. 127 Of corrupt minds [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διεφθαρμενων τον νουν] </span></span>. More correctly, corrupted in mind. The verb not common in N. T. In Paul only <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:16">2 Corinthians 4:16</span>. Only here in Pastorals. Diafqora corruption only in Acts. Comp. katefqarmenoi ton noun corrupted in mind, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:8">2 Timothy 3:8</span>. </p>
<p>Destitute of the truth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεστερημενων της αληθειας] </span></span>. Rev. bereft of the truth. In N. T. commonly of defrauding, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:19">Mark 10:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:7">1 Corinthians 6:7</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:8">1 Corinthians 6:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:5">1 Corinthians 7:5</span>. The implication is that they once possessed the truth. They put it away from themselves (ch. 1 19; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:14">Titus 1:14</span>). Here it is represented as taken away from them. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:8">Romans 1:8</span>. </p>
<p>Gain is godliness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορισμον ειναι την ευσεβειαν] </span></span>. Wrong. Rend. that godliness is a way (or source) of gain. Porismov, only here and ver. 6, is a gain - making business. See Wisd. 13 19; 14 2. They make religion a means of livelihood. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:11">Titus 1:11</span>.</p>
