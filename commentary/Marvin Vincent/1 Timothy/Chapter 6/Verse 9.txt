<p>They that will be rich [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οι βουλομενοι πλουτειν] </span></span>. Better, they that desire to be rich. lt is not the possession of richess but the love of them that leads men into temptation. </p>
<p>Fall [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμπιπτουσιν] </span></span>. o P. Lit. fall into; but invariably in N. T. with eijv into. Temptation [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειρασμον] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:13">Matthew 6:13</span>. </p>
<p>Foolish [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανοητους] </span></span>. Foolish answers to several words in N. T., ajnohtov, ajsunetov, afrwn, mwrov. Anohtov not understanding; a want of proper application of the moral judgment or perception, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:9">Luke 24:9</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:5">Luke 24:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:1">Galatians 3:1</span>. See notes on both. Afrwn is senseless, stupid, of images, beasts. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:20">Luke 12:20</span>, note. Asunetov approaches the meaning of ajnohtov unintelligent. See Sir. 22 13, 15; 27 12. It also implies a moral sense, wicked, Wisd. 1 5; 11 15; Sir. 14 7. On the etymological sense, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:25">Matthew 11:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:33">Mark 12:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:47">Luke 2:47</span>. Mwrov is without forethought, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:26">Matthew 7:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:3">Matthew 25:3</span>; without learning, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:27">1 Corinthians 1:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:18">1 Corinthians 3:18</span>; with a moral sense, empty, useless, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:23">2 Timothy 2:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:9">Titus 3:9</span>; and impious, godless, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:22">Matthew 5:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+43:8">Psalms 43:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+5:21">Jeremiah 5:21</span>. Hurtful [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλαβερας] </span></span>. N. T. o. LXX once, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="pr+10:26">Proverbs 10:26</span>. </p>
<p>Drown [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βυθιζουσι] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:7">Luke 5:7</span>, note. A strong expression of the results of avarice. </p>
<p>Destruction [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ολεθρον] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:9">1 Thessalonians 1:9</span>, and additional note. Perdition [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απωλειαν] </span></span>. It is unsafe to distinguish between oleqrov destruction in general, and ajpwleia as pointing mainly to destruction of the soul. Apwleia sometimes of spiritual destruction, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:28">Philippians 1:28</span>; but also of destruction and waste in general, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:4">Mark 14:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:20">Acts 8:20</span>. One is reminded of Virgil, Aen 3:56 : "Quid non mortalia pectora cogis, Auri sacra fames?"</p>
