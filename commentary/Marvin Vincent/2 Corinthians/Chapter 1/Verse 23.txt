<p>I call God for a record [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον θεον επικαλουμαι] </span></span>. Rev., better, witness. A common classical idiom. Compare Plato : "Next will follow the choir of young men under the age of thirty, who will call upon the God Paean to testify to the truth of these words" (" Laws, "664). Homer :" For the gods will be the best witnesses "(" Iliad," 22, 254). Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:9">Romans 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:20">Galatians 1:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:8">Philippians 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:5">1 Thessalonians 2:5</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:10">1 Thessalonians 2:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+31:50">Genesis 31:50</span>, Sept. This particular form of expression occurs nowhere else in the New Testament. The verb is often translated appeal, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:11">Acts 25:11</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:12">Acts 25:12</span>. Also to call upon, in the sense of supplication, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:12">Romans 10:12</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:13">Romans 10:13</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:14">Romans 10:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:2">1 Corinthians 1:2</span>. </p>
