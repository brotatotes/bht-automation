<p>The Father of mercies [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο πατηρ των οικτιρμων] </span></span>. Equivalent to the compassionate Father. Compare the phrases Father of glory, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:17">Ephesians 1:17</span>; spirits, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:9">Hebrews 12:9</span>; lights, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:17">James 1:17</span>. Oijktirmov mercy, from oiktov pity or mercy, the feeling which expresses itself in the exclamation oi oh! on seeing another's misery. The distinction between this and eleov, according to which oijktirmov signifies the feeling, and eleov the manifestation, cannot be strictly held, since the manifestation is often expressed by oijktirmov. See Sept., <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+24:6">Psalms 24:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+102:4">Psalms 102:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+118:77">Psalms 118:77</span>. </p>
<p>All comfort [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πασης παρακλησεως] </span></span>. The earliest passage in the New Testament where this word comfort or its kindred verb is applied to God. Compare paraklhtov comforter, advocate, of the Holy Spirit, in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:16">John 14:16</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:26">John 14:26</span>, etc. All is better rendered every : the God of every consolation.</p>
