<p>Casting down [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθαιρουντες] </span></span>. Not the weapons, but we : we war, casting down, etc. </p>
<p>High thing [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υψωμα] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:39">Romans 8:39</span>. Falling in with the metaphor of strongholds. High military works thrown up, or lofty natural fastnesses with their battlements of rock. The word is also used in the Septuagint and Apocrypha of mental elevation, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="job+24:24">Job 24:24</span>, where the Septuagint reads "his haughtiness hath harmed many." </p>
<p>Exalteth itself [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαιρομενον] </span></span>. Rev., is exalted. Aeschylus uses a similar metaphor in Atossa's dream of the two women whom Xerxes yoked to his chariot : "And the one towered [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επουργουτο</span></span>] loftily in these trappings" (" Persae, " 190). </p>
<p>Bringing into captivity [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιχμαλωτιζοντες] </span></span>. Or leading away captive. The military metaphor is continued; the leading away of the captives after the storming of the stronghold. See on captives, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:18">Luke 4:18</span>. The campaign against the Cilician pirates resulted in the reduction of a hundred and twenty strongholds and the capture of more than ten thousand prisoners. Thought [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νοημα] </span></span>. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:14">2 Corinthians 3:14</span>. </p>
<p>To the obedience of Christ. In pursuance of the metaphor. The obedience is the new stronghold into which the captives are led. This is indicated by the preposition eijv into or unto.</p>
