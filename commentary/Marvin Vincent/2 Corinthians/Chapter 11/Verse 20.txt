<p>Bringeth you into bondage [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταδουλοι] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:4">Galatians 2:4</span>, where it is used of the efforts of the Jewish party to bring the christian Church under the ceremonial law. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:1">Galatians 5:1</span>. </p>
<p>Devour [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεσθιει] </span></span>. Your property. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:14">Matthew 23:14</span>. </p>
<p>Take [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανει] </span></span>. A. V. supplies of you, evidently with reference to property, which has already been touched upon in devour. The meaning is to take as a prey, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:5">Luke 5:5</span>. </p>
<p>Exalteth himself [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαιρεται] </span></span>. As ch. 10 5. It is noticeable that these are the only two instances out of nineteen in the New Testament where the word is used figuratively. </p>
<p>Smite you on the face. The climax of insult. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:39">Matthew 5:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:64">Luke 22:64</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:2">Acts 23:2</span>. Also the injunction to a bishop not to be a striker, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:3">1 Timothy 3:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:7">Titus 1:7</span>. Stanley notes the decree of the Council of Braga, A. D. 675, that no bishop, at his will and pleasure, shall strike his clergy.</p>
