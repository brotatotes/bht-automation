<p>Hebrews. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:1">Acts 6:1</span>. </p>
<p>Israelites. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:12">Acts 3:12</span>, and compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+3:5">Philippians 3:5</span>, and the phrase Israel of God, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:16">Galatians 6:16</span>, and an Israelite indeed, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:48">John 1:48</span>. Seed of Abraham. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:9">Matthew 3:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:33">John 8:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:7">Romans 9:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:1">Romans 11:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:16">Galatians 3:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:16">Hebrews 2:16</span>. The three names are arranged climactically, Hebrews pointing to the nationality; Israelites to the special relation to God 's covenant; seed of Abraham to the messianic privilege. Compare with the whole, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+3:4">Philippians 3:4</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+3:5">Philippians 3:5</span>.</p>
