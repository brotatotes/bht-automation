<p>l knew [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιδα] </span></span>. Rev., correctly, I know. </p>
<p>Above fourteen years ago [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προ ετων δεκατεσσαρων] </span></span>. Above, of A. V., is due to a misunderstanding of the Greek idiom. Lit., before fourteen years, that is, fourteen years ago, as Rev. </p>
<p>Caught up [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρπαγεντα] </span></span>. Compare Dante : </p>
<p>"Thou knowest, who didst lift me with thy light" " Paradiso, " 1, 75. </p>
<p>The verb suits the swift, resistless, impetuous seizure of spiritual ecstasy. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:12">Matthew 11:12</span>; and compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:39">Acts 8:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:17">1 Thessalonians 4:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+12:5">Revelation 12:5</span>. </p>
<p>Third heaven. It is quite useless to attempt to explain this expression according to any scheme of celestial gradation. The conception of seven heavens was familiar to the Jews; but according to some of the Rabbins there were two heavens - the visible clouds and the sky; in which case the third heaven would be the invisible region beyond the sky. Some think that Paul describes two stages of his rapture; the first to the third heaven, from which he was borne, as if from a halting - point, up into Paradise.</p>
