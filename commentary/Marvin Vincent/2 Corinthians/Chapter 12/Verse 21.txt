<p>Among you [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς υμας] </span></span>. Better, as Rev., before. In my relation to you. </p>
<p>Shall bewail [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πενθησω] </span></span>. Lament with a true pastor 's sorrow over the sin. </p>
<p>Many [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολλους] </span></span>. With special reference to the unchaste. </p>
<p>Sinned - already [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προημαρτηκοτων] </span></span>. Rev., heretofore. Only here and ch. 13 2. The perfect tense denotes the continuance of the sin. Heretofore probably refers to the time before his second visit. </p>
<p>Have not repented [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη μετανοησαντων] </span></span>. The only occurrence of the verb in Paul 's writings. Metanoia repentance, occurs only three times : <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:4">Romans 2:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:9">2 Corinthians 7:9</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:10">2 Corinthians 7:10</span>. </p>
<p>Of the uncleanness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επι τη ακαθαρσια] </span></span>. Connect with bewail, not with repent. There are no examples in the New Testament of the phrase metanoein ejpi to repent over, though such occur in the Septuagint. Lasciviousness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασελγεια] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:22">Mark 7:22</span>. </p>
