<p>Lest Satan should get an advantage of us [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα μη πλεονεκτηθωμεν υπο του σατανα] </span></span>. Lit., in order that we be not made gain of, or overreached, by Satan. Rev., that no advantage may be gained over us. The verb, from pleon more, and ecw to have, appears in the noun pleonexia greed of gain, covetousness. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:29">Romans 1:29</span>. </p>
<p>Are ignorant - devices [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγνοουμεν - νοηματα] </span></span>. A paronomasia (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:29-31">Romans 1:29-31</span>). As nearly as possible, "not know his knowing plots."</p>
