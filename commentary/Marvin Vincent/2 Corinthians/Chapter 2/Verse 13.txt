<p>Rest [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεσιν] </span></span>. Rev., relief. See on liberty, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:23">Acts 24:23</span>. </p>
<p>Taking my leave [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποταξαμενος] </span></span>. The verb means, primarily, to set apart or separate; hence to separate one's self, withdraw, and so to take leave of. The A. V. gives this sense in every case, except <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:46">Mark 6:46</span>, where it wrongly renders sent away. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:61">Luke 9:61</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:18">Acts 18:18</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:21">Acts 18:21</span>. Ignatins, ajpotaxamenov tw biw having bid farewell to the life, that is, this lower life (Epistle to Philadelphia, 11.).</p>
