<p>An epistle of Christ ministered by us [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιστολη χριστου διακονηθεισα υφ ημων] </span></span>. An epistle written by Christ through our ministry; that is, you, as the converted subjects of our ministry, are an epistle of Christ. Others explain : an epistle of which Christ forms the contents, thus making the apostles the writers. For the expression ministered by us, compare ch. 8 19, 20; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:12">1 Peter 1:12</span>. </p>
<p>Ink [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μελανι] </span></span>. From melav black. Only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:12">2 John 1:12</span> (see note), and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:13">3 John 1:13</span>. </p>
<p>The Spirit. Instead of ink. </p>
<p>Fleshy tables of the heart [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλαξιν καρδιας σαρκιναις] </span></span>. The best texts read kardiaiv the dative case in apposition with tables. Render, as Rev., tables which are hearts of flesh. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eze+11:19">Ezekiel 11:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+17:1">Jeremiah 17:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+31:33">Jeremiah 31:33</span>. For of flesh, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:14">Romans 7:14</span>.</p>
