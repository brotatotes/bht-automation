<p>Dishonesty [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της αισχυνης] </span></span>. Rev., more correctly, shame. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:12">Ephesians 5:12</span>. </p>
<p>Craftiness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πανουργια] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:23">Luke 20:23</span>. </p>
<p>Handling deceitfully [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δολουντες] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. Primarily, to ensnare; then to corrupt. Used of adulterating gold, wine, etc. See on which corrupt, ch. 2 17. This verb has a narrower meaning than the one used there [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καπηλευειν] </span></span>; for, while that means also to corrupt, it adds the sense for gain 's sake. The Vulgate renders both by the same word, adulterantes. Compare Dante : </p>
<p>"Thus did Sabellius, Arias, and those fools Who have been even as swords unto the Scriptures In rendering distorted their straight faces." " Paradiso, " 13, 128 - 130.</p>
