<p>The God of this world [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο θεος του αιωνος τουτου] </span></span>. The phrase occurs only here. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:2">Ephesians 2:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:12">Ephesians 6:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:31">John 12:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:30">John 14:30</span>. Satan is called God in the rabbinical writings. </p>
<p>"The first God is the true God; but the second God is Samuel." " The matron said, 'Our God is greater than thy God; for when thy God appeared to Moses in the bush, he hid his face; when, however, he saw the serpent, which is my God, he fled. " ' The light [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον φωτισμον] </span></span>. Only here and ver. 6. Lit., the illumination, act of enlightening. </p>
<p>Image of God. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:15">Colossians 1:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:5">John 17:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:6">Philippians 2:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+3:21">Philippians 3:21</span>. Christ 's light is also God 's. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:3">Hebrews 1:3</span>, Rev., effulgence (ajpaugasma, compare aujgasai shine, in this passage). Theodoret says : "The effulgence is both from the fire and with the fire, and has the fire as its cause, yet is not divided from the fire; for whence comes the fire, thence also comes the effulgence." </p>
<p>Shine [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυγασαι] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. From aujgh brightness, which also occurs but once, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:11">Acts 20:11</span>, daybreak. In classical Greek of the sun especially. Rev., dawn is legitimate as a translation, but hardly here, since Paul is going back to the figure of ch. 3 18.</p>
