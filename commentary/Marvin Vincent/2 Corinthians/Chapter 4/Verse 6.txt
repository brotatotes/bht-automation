<p>Who commanded the light to shine [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ειπων φως λαμψαι] </span></span>. The correct reading is lamyei shall shine; so that we should render, it is God that said light shall shine. So Rev. </p>
<p>To give the light of the knowledge [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς φωτισμον της γνωσεως] </span></span>. Lit., for the illumination, as ver. 4. In order that the knowledge may lighten. Knowledge, if not diffused, is not of the nature of light. </p>
<p>In the face of Jesus Christ. Containing the thought of ch. 3 18. The knowledge of the divine glory becomes clear revelation to men in the face of Christ as it appears in the Gospel : "So that in this seen countenance that clear - shining knowledge has the source of its light, as it were, its focus" (Meyer). 144</p>
