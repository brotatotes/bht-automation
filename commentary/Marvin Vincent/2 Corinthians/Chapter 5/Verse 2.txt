<p>In this. Tabernacle. As if pointing to his own body. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:54">1 Corinthians 14:54</span>. </p>
<p>Earnestly desiring [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιποθουντες] </span></span>. The participle has an explanatory force, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:7">Acts 27:7</span>, "because the wind did not suffer us." We groan because we long. Rev., longing. The compounded preposition ejpi does not mark the intensity of the desire, but its direction. </p>
<p>To be clothed upon [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επενδυσασθαι] </span></span>. Only here and ver. 4. Compare ejpenduthv fisher's coat, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:7">John 21:7</span> (see note). Lit., to put on over. The metaphor changes from building to clothing, a natural transformation in the mind of Paul, to whom the hail - cloth woven for tents would suggest a vesture. </p>
<p>House [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικητηριον] </span></span>. Not oijkia house, as ver. 1. This word regards the house with special reference to its inhabitant. The figure links itself with building, ver. 1, as contrasted with the unstable tent. </p>
<p>From heaven [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ ουρανου] </span></span>. As from God, ver. 1.</p>
