<p>As workers together with Him [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεργουντες] </span></span>. Lit., working together. With Him is implied in the compounded oun with. That it refers to God, not to the fellow - Christians, is evident from the parallel <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:9">1 Corinthians 3:9</span>, laborers together with God, and because the act of exhortation or entreaty in which the fellowship is exhibited is ascribed to God in ch. 5 20. The phrase Qeou paredroi assessors of God, occurs in Ignatius' letter to Polycarp. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+16:20">Mark 16:20</span>. </p>
<p>In vain [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις κενον] </span></span>. Lit., to what is vain. Equivalent to the phrase to no purpose.</p>
