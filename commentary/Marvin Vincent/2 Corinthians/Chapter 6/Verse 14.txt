<p>Unequally yoked [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετεροζυγουντες] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. Not in classical Greek, nor in Septuagint, though the kindred adjective eJterozugov of a diverse kind, occurs <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+19:19">Leviticus 19:19</span>. Unequally gives an ambiguous sense. It is not inequality, but difference in kind, as is shown by the succeeding words. The suggestion was doubtless due to the prohibition in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+22:9">Deuteronomy 22:9</span>, against yoking together two different animals. The reference is general, covering all forms of intimacy with the heathen, and not limited to marriage or to idolfeasts. </p>
<p>The different shades of fellowship expressed by five different words in this and the two following verses are to be noted. </p>
<p>Fellowship [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετοχη] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. The kindred verb metecw to be partaker is found only in Paul 's epistles and in Hebrews : metocov partner, partaker, only in Hebrews and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:7">Luke 5:7</span>. Having part with is the corresponding English expression. </p>
<p>Righteousness - unrighteousness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιοσυνη - ανομια] </span></span>. Lit., what sharing is there unto righteousness and lawlessness ? Dikaiosunh righteousness, though the distinctively Pauline sense of righteousness by faith underlies it, is used in the general sense of rightness according to God 's standard. </p>
<p>Communion [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινωνια] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:10">Luke 5:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:42">Acts 2:42</span>.</p>
