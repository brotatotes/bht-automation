<p>Imprisonments [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυλακαις] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:21">Acts 5:21</span>. </p>
<p>Tumults [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακαταστασιαις] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:9">Luke 21:9</span>, and compare ajkatastatov unstable, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:8">James 1:8</span>. This is one of the words which show the influence of political changes. From the original meaning of unsettledness, it developed, through the complications in Greece and in the East after the death of Alexander, into the sense which it has in Luke - political instability. One of the Greek translators of the Old Testament uses it in the sense of dread or anxious care. </p>
<p>Watchings [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγρυπνιαις] </span></span>. Only here and ch. 11 27. See on the kindred verb, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:33">Mark 13:33</span>. For the historical facts, see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:25">Acts 16:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:7-11">Acts 20:7-11</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:31">Acts 20:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:8">2 Thessalonians 3:8</span>. </p>
<p>Fastings [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νηστειαις] </span></span>. Mostly of voluntary fasting, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:21">Matthew 17:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:23">Acts 14:23</span>; but voluntary fasting would be out of place in an enumeration of hardships.</p>
