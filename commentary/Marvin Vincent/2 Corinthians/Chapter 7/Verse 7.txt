<p>Comfort. The manner in which Paul, so to speak, fondles this word, is most beautiful. Compare ch. 1 4 - 6. </p>
<p>Mourning [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδυρμον] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:18">Matthew 2:18</span>. It implies a verbal expression of grief. Cebes, a disciple of Socrates, in his Pinax 147 represents Luph Lupe, Sorrow, as a woman, with her head bowed upon her breast; Odunh Odune, consuming Grief, follows, tearing her hair. By her side is Odurmov Odurmos, Lamentation, a revolting, emaciated figure, whose sister is Aqumia Athumia, Despondency.</p>
