<p>Repentance [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετανοιαν] </span></span>. See on the kindred verb repent, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:2">Matthew 3:2</span>, and compare on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:29">Matthew 21:29</span>. Repentance is different from regret of ver. 8, indicating a moral change, as is shown by the next clause. Ye might receive damage [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζημιωθητε] </span></span>. Rev., might suffer loss. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:26">Matthew 16:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:25">Luke 9:25</span>. This somewhat obscure sentence means that the salutary moral results of the apostle 's letter compensated for the sorrow which it caused. The epistle which won them to repentance was no damage to them.</p>
