<p>Purposeth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προαιρειται] </span></span>. Read prohrhtai, perfect tense, hath purposed. </p>
<p>Grudgingly [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ λυπης] </span></span>. Lit., out of sorrow. </p>
<p>Cheerful [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιλαρον] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. See on the kindred iJlarothv cheerfulness, note on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:8">Romans 12:8</span>. </p>
<p>God loveth, etc. From <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="pr+22:9">Proverbs 22:9</span>, where the Hebrew is, a kind man shall be blessed. Sept., God blesseth a man who is cheerful and a giver.</p>
