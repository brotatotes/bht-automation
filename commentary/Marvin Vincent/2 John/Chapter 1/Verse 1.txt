<p>The elder [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο πρεσβυτερος] </span></span>. The word is used originally of seniority in age. So <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:25">Luke 14:25</span>. Afterward as a term of rank or office. Applied to members of the Sanhedrim (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:21">Matthew 16:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:12">Acts 6:12</span>). Those who presided over the Christian assemblies or churches (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:30">Acts 11:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:17">1 Timothy 5:17</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:19">1 Timothy 5:19</span>). The twenty - four members of the heavenly court in John's vision (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+4:4">Revelation 4:4</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+4:10">Revelation 4:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+5:5">Revelation 5:5</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+5:6">Revelation 5:6</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+5:8">Revelation 5:8</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+5:11">Revelation 5:11</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+5:1">Revelation 5:1</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+5:4">Revelation 5:4</span>). Here, with reference to official position, coupled, presumably, with age. </p>
<p>Unto the elect lady [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκλεκτη κυρια] </span></span>. An expression which baffles all the commentators. It is supposed by some that the title describes a person, by others, a society. The views of the former class as to the person designated, are </p>
<p>(1.) That the letter was addressed to a certain Babylonian named Electa. </p>
<p>(2.) To a person named Kyria. </p>
<p>(3.) To Electa Kyria, a compound proper name. Those who regard the phrase as describing a society, divide on the question whether a particular Christian society or the whole Church is intended. It is impossible to settle the question satisfactorily. </p>
<p>Children [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τεκνοις] </span></span>. May be taken either in a literal or in a spiritual sense. For the later, see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:2">1 Timothy 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:25">Galatians 4:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:4">3 John 1:4</span>. Compare also vv. 4, 13. The explanation turns on the meaning of ejklekth kuria. If it mean the Church, children will have the spiritual sense. If it be a proper name, the literal. </p>
<p>Whom [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ους] </span></span>. Comprehensive, embracing the mother and the children of both sexes. </p>
<p>I love [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαπω] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:20">John 5:20</span>. </p>
<p>In the truth (ejn ajlhqeia. Omit the. The expression in truth marks the atmosphere or element of truth in which something is said, or felt, or done. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:17">John 17:17</span>. In truth is equivalent to truly, really. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:6">Colossians 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:19">John 17:19</span>. </p>
<p>That have known [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οι εγνωκοτες] </span></span>. Either have come to know, or as Rev., know. The perfect tense of ginwskw, to learn to know, is rendered as a present : I have learned to know, therefore I know. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:3">1 John 2:3</span>.</p>
