<p>If there come any [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ει τις ερχεται] </span></span>. Better, Rev., if anyone cometh. The indicative mood assumes the fact : if anyone comes, as there are those that come. Cometh is used in an official sense as of a teacher. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:5">1 John 3:5</span>. </p>
<p>Bring [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φερει] </span></span>. For the use of the verb see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:29">John 18:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:18">Acts 25:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:11">2 Peter 2:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:17">2 Peter 1:17</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:18">2 Peter 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:13">1 Peter 1:13</span>. </p>
<p>Neither bid him God speed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">και χαιρειν αυτω μη λεγετε] </span></span>. Lit., and say not unto him "greeting!" Cairein rejoice, hail, was the customary form of salutation. It was also used in bidding farewell; but in the New Testament always of greeting (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:23">Acts 14:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:26">Acts 23:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:1">James 1:1</span>). 70 "Now whoever cometh and teacheth you all these things, before spoken, receive him; but if the teacher himself turn aside and teach another teaching, so as to overthrow this, do not hear him" (" Teaching of the Twelve Apostles, " ch. 11. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:10">Matthew 10:10</span>).</p>
