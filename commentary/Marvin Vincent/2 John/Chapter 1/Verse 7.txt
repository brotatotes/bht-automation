<p>Deceivers [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλανοι] </span></span>. See on we deceive ourselves, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:8">1 John 1:8</span>. Are entered into [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξηλθαν εις] </span></span>. Rev., are gone forth into. The A. V. follows the reading eijshlqon entered into. The tense is the aorist, strictly rendered, went forth. It may indicate a particular crisis, at which they went forth from the Christian society. </p>
<p>Who confess not [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οι μη ομολογουντες] </span></span>. The article with the participle describes the character of this class of deceivers, and does not merely assert a definite fact concerning them. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:41">Mark 14:41</span>, "other women which came up with Him" [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αι συνσνσβασαι] </span></span>. Confess. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:23">Matthew 7:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:32">Matthew 10:32</span>. </p>
<p>Is come [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομενον] </span></span>. Wrong. The verb is in the present participle, coming, which describes the manhood of Christ as still being manifested. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:5">1 John 3:5</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:2">1 John 4:2</span> we have the manifestation treated as a past fact by the perfect tense, ejlhluqo. ta has come. Rev., that Jesus Christ cometh. So in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:10">1 Thessalonians 1:10</span>, thv ojrghv thv ejrcomenhv is the wrath which is coming; which has already begun its movement and is advancing : not merely, as A. V., the wrath to come, which makes it wholly a future event. See on lingereth, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:3">2 Peter 2:3</span>. </p>
<p>An antichrist [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο αντιχριστος</span></span>] Rev, rendering the definite article, the antichrist. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:18">1 John 2:18</span>.</p>
