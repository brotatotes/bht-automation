<p>Whosoever transgresseth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πας ο παραβαινων] </span></span>. The best texts read proagwn goeth onward. So Rev., with taketh the lead in margin. The meaning is, whosoever advances beyond the limits of Christian doctrine. Others explain of those who would set themselves up as teachers, or take the lead. Such false progress is contrasted with abiding in the teaching. On the construction, pav every one, with the article and participle, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:3">1 John 3:3</span>. </p>
<p>Abideth - in [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενων εν] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:6">1 John 2:6</span>. </p>
<p>Doctrine [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδαχη] </span></span>. Better, as Rev., teaching. </p>
<p>Of Christ. Not the teaching concerning Christ, but the teaching of Christ Himself and of His apostles. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:3">Hebrews 2:3</span>. So according to New Testament usage. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:19">John 18:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:12">Acts 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+2:14">Revelation 2:14</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+2:15">Revelation 2:15</span>. In the doctrine of Christ. Omit of Christ. Didach teaching, is used thus absolutely, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:17">Romans 16:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:9">Titus 1:9</span>.</p>
