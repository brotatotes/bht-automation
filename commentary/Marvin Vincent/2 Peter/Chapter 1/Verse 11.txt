<p>Shall be ministered abundantly [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλουσιως επιχορηγηθησεται] </span></span>. On the verb see ver. 5. Rev., shall be richly supplied. We are to furnish in our faith : the reward shall be furnished unto us. Richly, indicating the fulness of future blessedness. Professor Salmond observes that it is the reverse of "saved, yet so as by fire" (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:15">1 Corinthians 3:15</span>). </p>
<p>Everlasting kingdom [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιωνιον βασιλειαν] </span></span>. In the first epistle, Peter designated the believer 's future as an inheritance; here he calls it a kingdom. Eternal, as Rev., is better than everlasting, since the word includes more than duration of time.</p>
