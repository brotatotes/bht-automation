<p>We have not followed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου εξακολουθησαντες] </span></span>. A strong compound, used only here and ch. 2 2, 15. The ejx gives the force of following out; in pursuance of; closely. </p>
<p>Cunningly devised [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σεσοφισμενοις] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:15">2 Timothy 3:15</span>, in which latter passage it has a good sense, to make thee wise. Here, in a bad sense, artfully framed by human cleverness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σοφια] </span></span>. Compare feigned words, ch. 2 3. </p>
<p>Fables [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυθοις] </span></span>. This word, which occurs only here and in the Pastoral Epistles, is transcribed in the word myth. The reference here may be to the Jewish myths, rabbinical embellishments of Old - Testament history; or to the heathen myths about the descent of the gods to earth, which might be suggested by his remembrance of the transfiguration; or to the Gnostic speculations about aeons or emanations, which rose from the eternal abyss, the source of all spiritual existence, and were named Mind, Wisdom, Power, Truth, etc. </p>
<p>Coming [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρουσιαν] </span></span>. Or presence. Compare ch. 3 4. Another word, ajpokaluyiv, revelation, is used in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:7">1 Peter 1:7</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:13">1 Peter 1:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:13">1 Peter 4:13</span>, to describe the appearing of Christ. </p>
<p>Eye - witnesses [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εποπται] </span></span>. See on behold, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:12">1 Peter 2:12</span>. Only here in New Testament. Compare the different word in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:2">Luke 1:2</span>, aujtoptai, eye - witnesses. </p>
<p>Majesty [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεγαλειοτητος] </span></span>. Used in only two passages besides this : <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:43">Luke 9:43</span>, of the mighty power (Rev., majesty) of God, as manifested in the healing of the epileptic child; and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:27">Acts 19:27</span>, of the magnificence of Diana.</p>
