<p>When there came [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεχθεισης] </span></span>. Lit., having been born. Compare come (Rev., ver. 18); moved (ver. 21); and rushing wind, lit., a wind born along (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:2">Acts 2:2</span>). </p>
<p>From [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο] </span></span>. Lit., by. </p>
<p>Excellent [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεγαλοπρεπους] </span></span>. Or sublime. Only here in New Testament. In Septuagint (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+33:26">Deuteronomy 33:26</span>), as an epithet of God, excellency. The phrase excellent glory refers to the bright cloud which overshadowed the company on the transfiguration mount, like the shekinah above the mercy - seat.</p>
