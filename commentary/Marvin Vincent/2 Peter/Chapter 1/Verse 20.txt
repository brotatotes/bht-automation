<p>Is [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γινεται] </span></span>. More literally, arises or originates. </p>
<p>Private [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιδιας] </span></span>. See on ver. 3. His own. Rev., special, in margin. Interpretation [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλυσεως] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. Compare the cognate verb expounded (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:34">Mark 4:34</span>) and determined (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:39">Acts 19:39</span>). The usual word is eJrmhneia (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:10">1 Corinthians 12:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:26">1 Corinthians 14:26</span>). Literally, it means loosening, untying, as of hard knots of scripture.</p>
