<p>Whereby [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι ων] </span></span>. Lit., through which; viz., his glory and virtue. Note the three occurrences of dia, through, in vv. 3, 4. </p>
<p>Are given [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεδωρηται] </span></span>. Middle voice; not passive, as A. V. Hence Rev., correctly, he hath granted. See on ver. 3. </p>
<p>Exceeding great and precious promises. Rev., his exceeding great, etc., by way of rendering the definite article, ta. </p>
<p>Precious [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τιμια] </span></span>. The word occurs fourteen times in the New Testament. In eight instances it is used of material things, as stones, fruit, wood. In Peter it occurs three times : <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:7">1 Peter 1:7</span>, of tried faith; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:19">1 Peter 1:19</span>, of the blood of Christ; and here, of God 's promises. </p>
<p>Promises [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαγγελματα] </span></span>. Only in this epistle. In classical Greek the distinction is made between ejpaggelmata, promises voluntarily or spontaneously made, and uJposceseiv, promises made in response to a petition. </p>
<p>Might be partakers [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενησθε κοινωνοι] </span></span>. Rev., more correctly, may become, conveying the idea of a growth. See note on koinwnov, partaker, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:1">1 Peter 5:1</span>; and compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:10">Hebrews 12:10</span>. </p>
<p>Having escaped [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποφυγοντες] </span></span>. Only in this epistle. To escape by flight. Through lust [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν επιθυμια] </span></span>. Rev. renders by lust, as the instrument of the corruption. Others, in lust, as the sphere of the corruption, or as that in which it is grounded.</p>
