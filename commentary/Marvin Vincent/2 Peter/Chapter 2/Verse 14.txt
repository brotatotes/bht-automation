<p>Eyes. Another illustration of Peter's emphasis on sight. It is the instrument of evil no less than of good. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:28">Matthew 5:28</span>. </p>
<p>Adultery [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μοιχαλιδος] </span></span>. Lit., an adulteress, but used as an adjective <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:39">Matthew 12:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:4">Matthew 16:4</span>. </p>
<p>That cannot cease [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακαταοαυστους] </span></span>. Only here, in New Testament. Compare hath ceased (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:1">1 Peter 4:1</span>). </p>
<p>Beguiling [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δελεαζοντες] </span></span>. Only here, ver. 18, and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:14">James 1:14</span>. From delear, a bait. An appropriate word from Peter the fisherman. Rev., enticing. </p>
<p>Unstable [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αστηρικτους] </span></span>. A compound of the word at <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:10">1 Peter 5:10</span>, stablish. See note there, and on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:12">2 Peter 1:12</span>. </p>
<p>An heart they have exercised [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καρδιαν γεγυμνασμενην εχοντες] </span></span>. The A. V. is awkward. Better, Rev., having a heart exercised. Exercised is the word used for gymnastic training, from which gymnastic is derived. With covetous practices. The A. V. follows the old reading, pleonexiaiv. The best texts read pleonexiav, covetousness. Rev., therefore, rightly, in covetousness. </p>
<p>Cursed children [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταρας τεκνα] </span></span>. Lit., children of cursing; and so Rev. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:17">Mark 3:17</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:14">1 Peter 1:14</span>.</p>
