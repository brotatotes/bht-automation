<p>Right [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευθειαν] </span></span>. Lit., straight, which is the radical meaning of right. Are gone astray [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απλανηθησαν] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:24">Mark 12:24</span>. </p>
<p>Following [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξακολουθησαντες] </span></span>. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:16">2 Peter 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:2">2 Peter 2:2</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:11">Jude 1:11</span>. The way. Note the frequent occurrence of the word way in the story of Balaam (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="nu+22:0">Numbers 22:0</span>), and Peter's use of the same phrase, as here, the right ways of the Lord, in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:10">Acts 13:10</span>. </p>
<p>Bosor. Rev. gives Beor, the Old Testament form of the name. Wages of unrighteousness. See on ver. 13.</p>
