<p>Wells [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πηγαι] </span></span>. Better, as Rev., springs; yet the Rev. has retained well at <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:14">John 4:14</span>, where the change would have given more vividness to Christ 's metaphor, which is that of an ever upleaping, living fountain. Without water. As so often in the East, where the verdure excites the traveler's hope of water. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+2:13">Jeremiah 2:13</span>, and the contrast presented in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+48:11">Isaiah 48:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="pr+10:11">Proverbs 10:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="pr+13:14">Proverbs 13:14</span>. </p>
<p>Clouds. The A. V. has followed the Tex. Rec., nefelai, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:12">Jude 1:12</span>. The correct reading is oJmiclai, mists, found only here in New Testament. So Rev. </p>
<p>With a tempest [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο λαιλαπος] </span></span>. Rev., by a storm. The word occurs only twice elsewhere - <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:37">Mark 4:37</span>; Luk 7:23 - in the parallel accounts of the storm on the lake, which Jesus calmed by his word. There on the lake Peter was at home, as well as with the Lord on that occasion; and the peculiar word describing a whirlwind - one of those sudden storms so frequent on that lake (see note on the word, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:37">Mark 4:37</span>) - would be the first to occur to him. Compare Paul 's similar figure, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:14">Ephesians 4:14</span>. Blackness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζοφος] </span></span>. See on ver. 4, and compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:13">Jude 1:13</span>. </p>
<p>Of darkness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του σκοτους] </span></span>. Lit., the darkness, denoting a well - understood doom. </p>
<p>Is reserved [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τετηρηται] </span></span>. Lit., hath been reserved, as Rev. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:4">1 Peter 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:4">2 Peter 2:4</span>. </p>
<p>Forever. The best texts omit.</p>
