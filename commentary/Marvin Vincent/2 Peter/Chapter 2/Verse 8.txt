<p>Dwelling [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγκατοικων] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. Dwelling, and therefore suffering continually, from day to day. </p>
<p>In seeing [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλεμματι] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. Usually of the look of a man from without, through which the vexation comes to the soul. "Vexed his righteous soul." </p>
<p>Vexed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβασανιζεν] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:24">Matthew 4:24</span>, torments. The original sense is to test by touchstone or by torture. See on toiling, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:48">Mark 6:48</span>. Rev. gives tormented, in margin. </p>
<p>Unlawful [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανομοις] </span></span>. Rev., lawless. Only here in New Testament with things. In all other cases it is applied to persons.</p>
