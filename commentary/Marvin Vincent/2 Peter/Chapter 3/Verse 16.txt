<p>Hard to be understood [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυσνοητα] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. They that are unlearned and unstable [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οι αμαθεις και αστηρικτοι] </span></span>. Both words are peculiar to Peter. On the latter, see on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:14">2 Peter 2:14</span>. </p>
<p>Wrest (streblousin). Only here in New Testament. Meaning, originally, to hoist with a windless or screw; to twist or dislocate the limbs on a rack. It is a singularly graphic word applied to the perversion of scripture. The other scriptures [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τας λοιπας γραφας] </span></span>. Showing that Paul 's epistles were ranked as scripture. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:10">Mark 12:10</span>.</p>
