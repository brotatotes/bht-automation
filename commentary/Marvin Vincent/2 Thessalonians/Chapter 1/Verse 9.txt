<p>Shall be punished [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικην τισουσιν] </span></span>. The verb (N. T. o.) means to pay or render. Lit. shall pay penalty. </p>
<p>Everlasting destruction [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ολεθρον αιωνιον] </span></span>. The phrase nowhere else in N. T. In LXX, 4 Macc. 10 15. Rev. properly, eternal destruction. It is to be carefully noted that eternal and everlasting are not synonymous. See additional note at the end of this chapter. </p>
<p>From the presence [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο προσωπου] </span></span>. Or face. Apo from has simply the sense of separation. Not from the time of the Lord 's appearing, nor by reason of the glory of his presence. Proswpon is variously translated in A. V. Mostly face : also presence, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:13">Acts 3:13</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:19">Acts 3:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:41">Acts 5:41</span> : person, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:16">Matthew 22:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:21">Luke 20:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:6">Galatians 2:6</span> : appearance, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:12">2 Corinthians 5:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:1">2 Corinthians 10:1</span>; fashion, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:11">James 1:11</span>. <p>Glory of his power [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξης της ισχυος αυτου] </span></span>. For glory see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:12">1 Thessalonians 2:12</span>. Iscuv power, not often in Paul. It is indwelling power put forth or embodied, either aggressively or as an obstacle to resistance : physical power organized or working under individual direction. An army and a fortress are both ijscurov. The power inhering in the magistrate, which is put forth in laws or judicial decisions, is ijscuv, and makes the edicts ijscura valid and hard to resist. Dunamiv is the indwelling power which comes to manifestation in ijscuv The precise phrase used here does not appear elsewhere in N. T. In LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+2:10">Isaiah 2:10</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+2:19">Isaiah 2:19</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+2:21">Isaiah 2:21</span>. The power [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυναμις</span></span>] and glory of God are associated in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:30">Matthew 24:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:26">Mark 13:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:27">Luke 21:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+4:11">Revelation 4:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+19:1">Revelation 19:1</span>.
