<p>Hath chosen [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειλατο] </span></span>. The only case in N. T. in which this word is used of God 's election. LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+26:18">Deuteronomy 26:18</span>, of God 's choosing Israel to be his peculiar people. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:22">Philippians 1:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:25">Hebrews 11:25</span>. From the beginning [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απ αρχης] </span></span>. Not elsewhere in Paul. His usual expressions are pro twn aijwnwn before the ages (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:7">1 Corinthians 2:7</span>) : pro katabolhv kosmou before the foundation of the world (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:4">Ephesians 1:4</span>) : ajpo twn aijwnwn from the ages (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:9">Ephesians 3:9</span>). Before eternal times [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προ χρονων αιωνιων</span></span>] is found <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:9">2 Timothy 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:2">Titus 1:2</span>.</p>
