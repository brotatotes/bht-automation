<p>Deceive [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαπατηση] </span></span>. Better beguile; since the word means not only making a false impression, but actually leading astray. </p>
<p>Except there come a falling away. Before except insert in translation the day shall not come. Such ellipses are common in Paul. </p>
<p>Falling away [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστασια] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:21">Acts 21:21</span>. Comp. LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jos+22:22">Joshua 22:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ch+29:19">2 Chronicles 29:19</span>. </p>
<p>The man of sin - the son of perdition [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ανθρωπος της ανομιας, ο υιος της απωλειας] </span></span>. See on children of light, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:5">1 Thessalonians 5:5</span>. The phrase man of sin (lawlessness) does not occur elsewhere, either in N. T. or LXX Son of perdition is found <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:12">John 17:12</span>, o LXX : tekna apwlei. av children of perdition (A. V. transgression), <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+57:4">Isaiah 57:4</span>. The man of sin has been thought to refer to Caligula, Titus, Simon Magus, Nero, the Pope of Rome, Luther, Mahomet, etc.</p>
