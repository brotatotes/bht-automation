The salutation of Paul with mine own hand [ασπασμος τη εμη χειρι παυλου] . Rev. properly, "the salutation of me Paul." The genitive of me is contained, according to a familiar Greek idiom, in the possessive pronoun my. Paul had apparently been employing an amanuensis. In every epistle. Comp. 1 Corinthians 16:21; Colossians 4:18.
