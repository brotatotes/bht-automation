<p>Any man's bread [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρτον παρα τινος] </span></span>. Lit. bread from any one, or at any man's hand. </p>
<p>For nought [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δωρεαν] </span></span>. The word is a noun, meaning a gift. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:10">John 4:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:38">Acts 2:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:15">Romans 5:15</span>. The accusative often adverbially as here; as a gift, gratis. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:8">Matthew 10:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:24">Romans 3:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+21:6">Revelation 21:6</span>. Labor and travail. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:3">1 Thessalonians 1:3</span>. </p>
<p>Be chargeable [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιβαρησαι] </span></span>. P o. Better, burden. By depending upon them for pecuniary support. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:3-18">1 Corinthians 9:3-18</span>, and see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:6">1 Thessalonians 2:6</span>.</p>
