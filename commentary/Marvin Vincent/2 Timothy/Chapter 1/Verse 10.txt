<p>Made manifest [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανερωθεισαν] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:16">1 Timothy 3:16</span>. In contrast With the preceding clause, this marks the historical fulfillment in time of the eternal, divine counsel. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:3">Titus 1:3</span>. There is an implication that the divine counsel was hidden until the fitting time : comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:5">Ephesians 3:5</span>, and see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:26">Colossians 1:26</span>. </p>
<p>By the appearing [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια της επιφανειας] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:8">2 Thessalonians 2:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:14">1 Timothy 6:14</span>. </p>
<p>Who hath abolished [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταργησαντος] </span></span>. Better, since he made of none effect. In Pastorals only here. frequent in Paul. See on make without effect, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:3">Romans 3:3</span>, and comp. is swallowed up, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:54">1 Corinthians 14:54</span>. Notice the association of the verb with ejpifaneia appearing in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:8">2 Thessalonians 2:8</span>. Brought to light [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωτισαντος] </span></span>. Only here in Pastorals. In Paul, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:5">1 Corinthians 4:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:18">Ephesians 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:9">Ephesians 3:9</span>. </p>
<p>Immortality [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφθαρσιαν] </span></span>. Better, incorruption. With this exception, only in Paul. See Wisd. 2 23; 6 9; 4 Macc. 9 22; 17 12.</p>
