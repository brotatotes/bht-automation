<p>A teacher of the Gentiles [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδασκαλος εθνων] </span></span>. Omit of the Gentiles. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:7">1 Timothy 2:7</span>, from which the words were probably transferred when the three Epistles were jointly edited. Paul calls himself an apostle, and describes himself as preaching [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κηρυσσων] </span></span>; but he nowhere calls himself didaskalov a teacher, although he uses didaskein to teach, of himself, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:17">1 Corinthians 4:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:28">Colossians 1:28</span>. He also uses didach teaching, of matter given by him to the converts, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:17">Romans 6:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:17">Romans 16:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:6">1 Corinthians 14:6</span>. He distinguishes between the apostle and the teacher, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:28">1 Corinthians 12:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:11">Ephesians 4:11</span>.</p>
