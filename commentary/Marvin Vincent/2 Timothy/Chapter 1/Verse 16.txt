<p>Onesiphorus. Mentioned again, chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:19">2 Timothy 4:19</span>. </p>
<p>Refreshed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεψυζεν] </span></span>. N. T. o. Several times in LXX; often in Class. Anayuxiv refreshing, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:19">Acts 3:19</span>; and katayucein to cool, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:24">Luke 16:24</span>. Originally to cool; to revive by fresh air. </p>
<p>Chain [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλυσιν] </span></span>. Once in Paul, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:20">Ephesians 6:20</span>. Several times in Mark, Luke, and Acts. It may mean handcuffs or manacles (see Lightfoot, Philippians, ed. of 1896, page 8), but is not limited to that sense either in classical or later Greek. See Hdt 9:74; Eurip. Orest. 984. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:4">Mark 5:4</span> is not decisive.</p>
