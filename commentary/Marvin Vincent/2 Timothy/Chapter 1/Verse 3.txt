<p>I thank God [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαριν εχω τω θεω] </span></span>. Lit. I have thanks to God. The phrase in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:9">Luke 17:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:47">Acts 2:47</span>; o P. unless <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:15">2 Corinthians 1:15</span>; 130 <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:12">1 Timothy 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:28">Hebrews 12:28</span>; 3rd John verse 4. Paul uses eujcaristw I give thanks (not in Pastorals) or eujloghtov oJ qeov blessed be God (not in Pastorals). The phrase carin ecw is a Latinism, habere gratiam, of which several are found in Pastorals. </p>
<p>I serve [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λατρευω] </span></span>. In Pastorals only here. <p>That without ceasing [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως αδιαλειπτον] </span></span>. The passage is much involved. Note </p>
<p>(1) that carin ecw tw qew I thank God must have an object. </p>
<p>(2) That object cannot be that he unceasingly remembers Timothy in his prayers. </p>
<p>(3) That object, though remote, is uJpomnhsin labwn when I received reminder verse 5). </p>
