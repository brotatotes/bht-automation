<p>From these [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο τουτων] </span></span>. From such persons as are described as; 'vessels "unto dishonor." Some attempt to relieve the awkwardness of this figure by referring these to persons mentioned in vv. 16, 17. Unto honor [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τιμην] </span></span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:5">1 Timothy 4:5</span>. Set al art to noble and holy uses, as belonging to God. <p>Meet [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευχρηστον] </span></span>. From euj well and crasqai to use. Hence, easy to make use of, useful. The A. V. meet, is fit, suitable. Rend. serviceable. In contrast with to no profit, verse 14. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:11">Philippians 1:11</span>, where the contrast with acrhstov useless is brought out. Only here, chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:11">2 Timothy 4:11</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:11">Philippians 1:11</span>. </p>
<p>For the master's use [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω δεσποτη] </span></span>. Use is superfluous. Rend. for the master. The master of the household. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:1">1 Timothy 6:1</span>. </p>
