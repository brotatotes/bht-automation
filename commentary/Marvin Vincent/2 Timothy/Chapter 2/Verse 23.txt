<p>Foolish [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μωρας] </span></span>. In Pastorals only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:9">Titus 3:9</span>. Mwrov means dull, sluggish, stupid : applied to the taste, flat, insipid : comp. mwranqh have lost his savor, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:13">Matthew 5:13</span>. In Pastorals never substantively, a fool, but so in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:18">1 Corinthians 3:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:10">1 Corinthians 4:10</span>. Comp. afrwn, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:36">1 Corinthians 14:36</span>. </p>
<p>Unlearned [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαιδευτους] </span></span>. Rev. ignorant is better; but the meaning at bottom is undisciplined : questions of an untrained mind, carried away with novelties : questions which do not proceed from any trained habit of thinking. </p>
<p>Questions [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζητησεις] </span></span>. Better, questionings. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:4">1 Timothy 6:4</span>. Avoid [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραιτου] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:7">1 Timothy 4:7</span> Better, refuse or decline. Gender [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεννωσι] </span></span>. Only here in Pastorals. In Paul, metaphorically, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:15">1 Corinthians 4:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:10">Philippians 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:24">Galatians 4:24</span>.</p>
