<p>The servant of the Lord [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δουλον κυριου] </span></span>. The teacher or other special worker in the church. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:1">Titus 1:1</span>; Rom. i. l; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:10">Galatians 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:1">Philippians 1:1</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+4:12">Colossians 4:12</span>. Of any Christian, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:22">1 Corinthians 7:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:6">Ephesians 6:6</span>. The phrase is often applied to the Old Testament prophets as a body : see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="am+3:7">Amos 3:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+7:25">Jeremiah 7:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ezr+9:11">Ezra 9:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="da+9:6">Daniel 9:6</span>. To Joshua, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:2">Jude 1:2</span>:8; to David, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+77:70">Psalms 77:70</span>. </p>
