<p>May recover themselves [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανανηψωσιν] </span></span>. Lit. may return to soberness. N. T. o. See on be sober, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:6">1 Thessalonians 5:6</span>. A similar connection of thought between coming to the knowledge of God and awaking out of a drunken stupor, occurs <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:34">1 Corinthians 14:34</span>. </p>
<p>Out of the snare of the devil [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ της του διαβολου παγιδος] </span></span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+124:7">Psalms 124:7</span>. The phrase snare of the devil, only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:7">1 Timothy 3:7</span> (note). The metaphor is mixed; return to soberness out of the snare of the devil. </p>
<p>Who are taken captive [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εζωγρημενοι] </span></span>. Or, having been held captive. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:10">Luke 5:10</span> (note on thou shalt catch!. </p>
<p>By him [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπ αυτου] </span></span>. The devil. </p>
<p>At his will [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τοεκεινου θελημα] </span></span>. Better, unto his will : that is, to do his (God 's) will. </p>
<p>The whole will then read : "And that they may return to soberness out of the snare of the devil (having been held captive by him) to do God 's will." </p>
