<p>The husbandman that laboreth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον κοπιωντα γεωργον] </span></span>. The verb implies hard. wearisome toil. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:3">1 Thessalonians 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:12">1 Thessalonians 5:12</span>. Gewrgov Husbandman, only here in Pastorals. o P. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:1">John 14:1</span>. </p>
<p>Must be first partaker [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δει πρωτον - μεταλαμβανειν] </span></span>. Better, Must be the first to partake. His is the first right to the fruits of his labor in the gospel. The writer seems to have in his eye <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:7">1 Corinthians 9:7</span>, where there is a similar association of military service and farming to illustrate the principle that they who proclaim the gospel should live of the gospel. Metalambanein to partake, o P, and only here in Pastorals. Paul uses metecein. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:10">1 Corinthians 9:10</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:12">1 Corinthians 9:12</span>; x. 17, 21, 30.</p>
