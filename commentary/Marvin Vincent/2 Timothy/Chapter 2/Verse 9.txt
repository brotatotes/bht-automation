<p>Wherein I suffer trouble [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ω κακοπαθω] </span></span>. Wherein refers to the gospel. Kakopaqein only here, chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:5">2 Timothy 4:5</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:13">James 5:13</span>. LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:10">John 4:10</span>. </p>
<p>As an evildoer [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως κακουργος] </span></span>. Only here and in Luke. Better, malefactor. The meaning is technical. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:32">Luke 23:32</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:33">Luke 23:33</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:39">Luke 23:39</span>. Unto bonds [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεχρι δεσμων] </span></span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:8">Philippians 2:8</span>, mecri qanatou unto death : <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:4">Hebrews 12:4</span>, mecriv aimatov unto blood. Const. with I suffer trouble But the word of God is not bound [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα ο λογος του θεου ου δεδεται] </span></span>. Nevertheless, although I am in bonds, the gospel which I preach will prevail in spite of all human efforts to hinder it. Word of God often in Paul. In Pastorals, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:5">1 Timothy 4:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:5">Titus 2:5</span>. Bound, in Paul metaphorically, as here, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:2">Romans 7:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:27">1 Corinthians 7:27</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:39">1 Corinthians 7:39</span>.</p>
