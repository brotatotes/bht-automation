<p>Lovers of their own selves [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλαυτοι] </span></span>. Better, lovers of self. N. T. o. LXX Aristotle, De Repub. 2 5, says : "It is not loving one's self, but loving it unduly, just as the love of possessions." </p>
<p>Covetous [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιλαργυροι] </span></span>. Better, lovers of money. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:14">Luke 16:14</span>. For the noun filarguria love of money, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:10">1 Timothy 6:10</span>. Love of money and covetousness are not synonymous. Covetous is pleonekthv; see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:10">1 Corinthians 5:10</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:11">1 Corinthians 5:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:6">Ephesians 5:6</span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:29">Romans 1:29</span>. </p>
<p>Boasters [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλαζονες] </span></span>. Or swaggerers. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:30">Romans 1:30</span>. See on ajlazoneiaiv boastings, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:16">James 4:16</span>. </p>
<p>Proud [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερηφανοι] </span></span>. Or haughty. See on uJperhfania pride, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:22">Mark 7:22</span>. </p>
<p>Blasphemers [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλασφημοι] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:13">1 Timothy 1:13</span>. Better, railers. <p>Unthankful [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχαριστοι] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:35">Luke 6:35</span>. </p>
