<p>Traitors [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προδοται] </span></span>. Or betrayers. Only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:16">Luke 6:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:52">Acts 7:52</span>. Heady [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προπετεις] </span></span>. Precipitate, reckless, headstrong in the pursuit of a bad end under the influence of passion. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:36">Acts 19:36</span>. In LXX, slack, loose, hence foolish, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="pr+10:14">Proverbs 10:14</span>, and dividing or parting asunder, as the lips; of one who opens his lips and speaks hastily or thoughtlessly, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="pr+13:3">Proverbs 13:3</span>. Comp. Sir. 9 18. </p>
<p>High - minded [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τετυφωμενοι] </span></span>. Better, besotted or clouded with pride. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:6">1 Timothy 3:6</span>, and comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:4">1 Timothy 6:4</span>. </p>
<p>Lovers of pleasure more than lovers of God [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φιληδονοι μαλλον ηφιλοθεοι] </span></span>. Pleasure - lovers rather than God - lovers. Both words N. T. o. o LXX</p>
