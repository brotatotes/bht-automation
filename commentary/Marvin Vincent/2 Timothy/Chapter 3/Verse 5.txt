<p>A form [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μορφωσιν] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:20">Romans 2:20</span>. Morfh Form (for the want of any other rendering) is the expression or embodiment of the essential and permanent being of that which is expressed Morfwsiv, lit. forming or shaping. Yet the meaning differs in different passages. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:20">Romans 2:20</span>, morfwsiv is the truthful embodiment of knowledge and truth as contained in the law of God. Here, the mere outward semblance, as distinguished from the essential reality. </p>
<p>The power [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την δυναμιν] </span></span>. The practical virtue. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:20">1 Corinthians 4:20</span>. It is impossible to overlook the influence of <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:29-31">Romans 1:29-31</span> in shaping this catalogue. </p>
