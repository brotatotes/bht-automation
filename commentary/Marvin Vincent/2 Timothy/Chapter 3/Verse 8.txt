<p>As [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ον τροπον] </span></span>. The formula occurs in the Synoptic Gospels (see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:37">Matthew 23:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:34">Luke 13:34</span>), and in Acts (i. 11; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:28">Acts 7:28</span>), but not in Paul. Jannes and Jambres. According to tradition, the names of the chiefs of the magicians who opposed Moses. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+7:11">Exodus 7:11</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+7:22">Exodus 7:22</span>. </p>
<p>Of corrupt minds [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεφθαρμενοι τον νουν] </span></span>. Better, corrupted in mind. The verb, N. T. o. Comp. diefqarmenwn ton noun corrupted in mind, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:5">1 Timothy 6:5</span>. </p>
<p>Reprobate [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδοκιμοι] </span></span>. In Pastorals only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:16">Titus 1:16</span>. A Pauline word. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:28">Romans 1:28</span>, and castaway, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:27">1 Corinthians 9:27</span>.</p>
