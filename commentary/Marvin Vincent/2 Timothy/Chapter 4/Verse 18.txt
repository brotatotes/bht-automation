<p>Every evil work [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ εργου πονηρου] </span></span>. Every design and attempt against him and his work. Ponhrov evil cannot be limited to evil on its active side. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:13">1 Corinthians 5:13</span>. The word is connected at the root with penesqai to be needy, and ponein to toil; and this connection opens a glimpse of that sentiment which associated badness with a poor and toiling condition. The word means originally full of or oppressed by labors; thence, that which brings annoyance or toil. Comp. hJmera ponhra evil day, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:16">Ephesians 5:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:13">Ephesians 6:13</span> : elkov ponhron a grievous sore, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+16:2">Revelation 16:2</span>. </p>
<p>Heavenly kingdom [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την βασιλειαν την επουρανιον] </span></span>. The phrase N. T. o. Epouraniov heavenly only here in Pastorals. Mostly in Paul and Hebrews. Heavenly kingdom, here the future, glorified life, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:9">1 Corinthians 6:9</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:10">1 Corinthians 6:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:50">1 Corinthians 14:50</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:29">Luke 13:29</span>. In the same sense, kingdom of Christ and of God, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:5">Ephesians 5:5</span>; kingdom of their Father, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:43">Matthew 13:43</span>; my Father 's kingdom, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:29">Matthew 26:29</span>; kingdom prepared for you, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:34">Matthew 25:34</span>; eternal kingdom of our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:11">2 Peter 1:11</span>.</p>
