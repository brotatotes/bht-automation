<p>Salute [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασπασαι] </span></span>. Very often in Paul. The singular only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:15">Titus 3:15</span>. </p>
<p>Prisca and Aquila. They appear in Corinth, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:2">Acts 18:2</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:3">Acts 18:3</span>; in Ephesus, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:18">Acts 18:18</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:26">Acts 18:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:19">1 Corinthians 16:19</span>. </p>
<p>Onesiphorus. Profit - bringer. Comp. chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:16">2 Timothy 1:16</span>. One of the punning names so common among slaves. Comp. Chresimus, Chrestus, Onesimus, Symphorus, all of which signify useful or helpful.</p>
