<p>Be instant [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιστηθι] </span></span>. Better, be ready. Once in Paul, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:3">1 Thessalonians 5:3</span>. Frequent in Luke and Acts. Lit. stand by, be at hand, be present. To come suddenly upon, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:38">Luke 2:38</span>. Hence, be ready. Instant signifies urgent, importunate, persevering. Lat. instant to press upon. Thus Latimer, "I preached at the instant request of a curate." So N. T., <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:12">Romans 12:12</span>, "Continuing instant in prayer." </p>
<p>In season [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευκαιρως] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:11">Mark 14:11</span>. LXX once, Sir. 18</p>
