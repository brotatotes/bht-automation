<p>Erastus. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:22">Acts 19:22</span>, sent by Paul with Timothy to Macedonia from Ephesus. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:23">Romans 16:23</span>, the city - treasurer who sends salutations. He cannot be certainly identified with the one mentioned here. The writer merely selects names of well - known companions of Paul. </p>
<p>Trophimus. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:4">Acts 22:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:9">Acts 21:9</span>. </p>
<p>Sick [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασθενουντα] </span></span>. By Paul mostly in a moral sense, as weak in the faith, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:19">Romans 4:19</span>; the law was weak, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:3">Romans 8:3</span>; the weak brother, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+8:11">1 Corinthians 8:11</span>. Of bodily sickness, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:26">Philippians 2:26</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:27">Philippians 2:27</span>.</p>
