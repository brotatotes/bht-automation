What he is now suffering is the beginning of the end. Spendesqai to be poured out as a libation, only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:17">Philippians 2:17</span> (note). In the active voice quite often in LXX </p>
<p>Departure [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναλυσεως] </span></span>. N. T. o. o LXX Comp. ajnalusai to depart, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:23">Philippians 1:23</span>. The figure is explained by some of loosing a Ship from its moorings; by others of breaking camp. In Philippians the latter is the more probable explanation, because Paul 's situation in the Custody of the Praetorians at Rome would naturally suggest a military metaphor, and because he is habitually sparing of nautical metaphors. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:1">2 Corinthians 5:1</span>, and Clement of Rome, ad Corinth. 44 "Blessed are the presbyters who have gone before, seeing that their departure [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναλυσιν</span></span>] was fruitful and ripe."</p>
