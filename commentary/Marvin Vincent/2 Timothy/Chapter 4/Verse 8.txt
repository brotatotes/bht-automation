<p>Henceforth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λοιπον] </span></span>. Lit. as to what remains. Loipon or to loipon either finally, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+13:11">2 Corinthians 13:11</span>; or henceforth as here, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:41">Mark 14:41</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:29">1 Corinthians 7:29</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:13">Hebrews 10:13</span> : or for the rest, besides, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:1">1 Thessalonians 4:1</span> (note); <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:1">2 Thessalonians 3:1</span>. </p>
<p>There is laid up [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκειται] </span></span>. Or laid away. In Pastorals only here. In Paul, see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:5">Colossians 1:5</span> (note). <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:20">Luke 19:20</span> of the pound laid up in a napkin. </p>
<p>A crown of righteousness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο της δικαιοσυνης στεφανος] </span></span>. The phrase N. T. o. See on stefanoutai is crowned, chapter. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:5">2 Timothy 2:5</span>. Rend. the crown. Judge [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κριτης] </span></span>. Comp. verse 1. Mostly in Luke and Acts. o P. Only here in Pastorals. Applied to Christ, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:42">Acts 10:42</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:9">James 5:9</span>; to God, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:28">Hebrews 12:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:12">James 4:12</span>. </p>
<p>Shall give [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποδωσει] </span></span>. Most frequent in Synoptic Gospels. It may mean to give over or away, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:58">Matthew 27:58</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:8">Acts 5:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:16">Hebrews 12:16</span> : or to give back, recompose, as here, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:4">Matthew 6:4</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:6">Matthew 6:6</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:18">Matthew 6:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:6">Romans 2:6</span>. At that day [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν εκεινη τη ημερα] </span></span>. See on chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:12">2 Timothy 1:12</span>. That love his appearing [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις ηγαπηκοσι την επιφανειαν αυτου] </span></span>. For love rend. have loved. Appearing, Christ 's second coming : see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:14">1 Timothy 6:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:8">2 Thessalonians 2:8</span>. The phrase N. T. o. Some have interpreted appearing as Christ 's first coming into the world, as chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:10">2 Timothy 1:10</span>; but the other sense is according to the analogy of <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:9">1 Corinthians 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+3:20">Philippians 3:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:28">Hebrews 9:28</span>.</p>
