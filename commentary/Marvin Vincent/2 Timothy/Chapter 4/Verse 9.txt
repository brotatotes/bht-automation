<p>Do thy diligence [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπουδασον] </span></span>. Earnestly endeavor. See on chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:15">2 Timothy 2:15</span>, and comp. chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:3">2 Timothy 1:3</span>. Do diligence and give diligence (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:10">2 Peter 1:10</span>) are old English phrases. So Chaucer : </p>
<p>"And night and day dide ever his diligence Hir for to please." Manciple's T. 141. </p>
<p>"And ech of hem doth al his diligence To doon unto the feste reverence." Clerke's T. 195</p>
