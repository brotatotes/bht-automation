<p>Follow [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μιμου] </span></span>. More correctly, as Rev., imitate. Elsewhere only <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:7">2 Thessalonians 3:7</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:9">2 Thessalonians 3:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:7">Hebrews 13:7</span>. The kindred word mimhthv imitator, uniformly rendered follower in A. V., occurs <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:16">1 Corinthians 4:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:1">1 Corinthians 11:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:1">Ephesians 5:1</span>. Hence our word mimic; also pantomime. Mimov means both an actor and a kind of prose drama, intended as a familiar representation of life and character, and without any distinct plot. That which is evil - that which is good [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το κακον - το αγαθον] </span></span>. Compare ta ajgaqa good, ta faula evil, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:29">John 5:29</span>.</p>
