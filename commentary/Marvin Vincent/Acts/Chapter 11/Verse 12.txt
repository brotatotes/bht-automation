<p>Nothing doubting [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηδεν διακρινομενον] </span></span>. The Rev. renders making no distinction, taking the verb in its original sense, which is to separate or distinguish. The rendering seems rather strained, doubting being a common rendering in the New Testament and giving a perfectly good sense here. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:21">Matthew 21:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:23">Mark 11:23</span>, and note on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:6">James 1:6</span>. It was natural that Peter should hesitate. </p>
<p>The six brethren. The men of Joppa who had gone with Peter to Cornelius, and had accompanied him also to Jerusalem, either as witnesses for him or for their own vindication, since they had committed the same offense.</p>
