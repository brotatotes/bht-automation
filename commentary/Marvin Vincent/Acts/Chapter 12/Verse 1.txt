<p>That time [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκεινον τον καιρον] </span></span>. More correctly, that juncture. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:7">Acts 1:7</span>. The date is A. D. 44. </p>
<p>Herod the king. Called also Agrippa, and commonly known as Herod Agrippa I, the grandson of Herod the Great. </p>
<p>Stretched forth his hands [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεβαλεν τας χειρας] </span></span>. Lit. laid on his hands. The A. V. is wrong, and so is the Rev. Render, laid hands on certain of the church to afflict them. </p>
<p>Vex [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακωσαι] </span></span>. Vex is used in the older and stronger sense of torment or oppress. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+22:21">Exodus 22:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="nu+25:17">Numbers 25:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:22">Matthew 14:22</span>. Its modern usage relates rather to petty annoyances. Rev., better, afflict.</p>
