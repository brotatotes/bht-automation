<p>Ministered [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λειτουργουντων] </span></span>. See on the kindred noun ministration, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:23">Luke 1:23</span>. This noun has passed through the following meanings : 1. A civil service, especially in the technical language of Athenian law. 2. A function or office of any kind, as of the bodily organs. 3. Sacerdotal ministration, both among the Jews and the heathen (see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:6">Hebrews 8:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:21">Hebrews 9:21</span>). 4. The eucharistic services. 5. Set forms of divine worship (Lightfoot, "On Philippians," 2, 17). Here, of the performance of Christian worship. Our word liturgy is derived from it. </p>
<p>Separate. The Greek adds dh, now, which is not rendered by A. V. or Rev. It gives precision and emphasis to the command, implying that it is for a special purpose, and to be obeyed at the time. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:15">Luke 2:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:36">Acts 14:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:20">1 Corinthians 6:20</span>.</p>
