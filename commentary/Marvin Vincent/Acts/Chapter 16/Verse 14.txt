<p>Lydia. An adjective : the Lydian; but as Lydia was a common name among the Greeks and Romans, it does not follow that she was named from her native country. </p>
<p>A seller of purple. On purple, see note on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:19">Luke 16:19</span>. </p>
<p>Thyatira. The district of Lydia, and the city of Thyatira in particular, were famous for purple dyes. So Homer : </p>
<p>"As when some Carian or Maeonian 22 dame Tinges with purple the white ivory, To form a trapping for the cheeks of steeds" Iliad, 4, 141. </p>
<p>An inscription found in the ruins of Thyatira relates to the guild of dyers. Heard [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκουεν] </span></span>. Imperfect, was hearing while we preached.</p>
