Lydia. An adjective : the Lydian; but as Lydia was a common name among the Greeks and Romans, it does not follow that she was named from her native country. A seller of purple. On purple, see note on Luke 16:19. Thyatira. The district of Lydia, and the city of Thyatira in particular, were famous for purple dyes. So Homer : "As when some Carian or Maeonian 22 dame Tinges with purple the white ivory, To form a trapping for the cheeks of steeds" Iliad, 4, 141. An inscription found in the ruins of Thyatira relates to the guild of dyers. Heard [ηκουεν] . Imperfect, was hearing while we preached.
