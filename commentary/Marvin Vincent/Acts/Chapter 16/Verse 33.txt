<p>He took [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραλαβων] </span></span>. Strictly, "took them along with [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα</span></span>] him :" to some other part of the prison. </p>
<p>Washed their stripes [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελουσεν απο των πληγων] </span></span>. Properly, "washed them from [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span>] their stripes." The verb louein expresses the bathing of the entire body (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:23">Hebrews 10:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:37">Acts 9:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:22">2 Peter 2:22</span>); while niptein commonly means the washing of a part of the body (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:17">Matthew 6:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:3">Mark 8:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:5">John 13:5</span>). The jailer bathed them; cleansing them from the blood with which they were besprinkled from the stripes.</p>
