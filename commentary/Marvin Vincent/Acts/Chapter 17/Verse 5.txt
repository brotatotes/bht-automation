<p>Of the baser sort [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγοραιων] </span></span>. From ajgora, the market - place; hence loungers in the market - place; the rabble. Cicero calls them subrastrani, those who hung round the rostra, or platform for speakers in the forum; and Plautus, subbasilicani, the loungers round the court - house or exchange. The word occurs only here and ch. 19 38, on which see note. Gathered a company [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οχλοποιησαντες] </span></span>. Rev., better, a crowd.. Only here in New Testament.</p>
