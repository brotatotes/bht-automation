<p>Let me speak [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξον ειπειν] </span></span>. Lit., it is permitted me. Rev., I may. It is allowable for him to speak, because the facts are notorious. </p>
<p>Freely [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετα παρρησιας] </span></span>. Lit., with freedom. The latter word from pan, all, and rJhsiv, speech; speaking everything, and therefore without reserve. The patriarch [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πατριαρχου] </span></span>. From arcw, to begin, and patria, a pedigree. Applied to David as the father of the royal family from which the Messiah sprang. It is used in the New Testament of Abraham (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:4">Hebrews 7:4</span>), and of the sons of Jacob (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:8">Acts 7:8</span>). </p>
<p>He is dead and buried [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετελευτησε και εταφη] </span></span>. Aorists, denoting what occurred at a definite past time. Rev., rightly, he both died and was buried. His sepulchre is with us. Or among us [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ημιν] </span></span>. On Mount Zion, where most of the Jewish kings were interred in the same tomb.</p>
