<p>Other [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετεροις] </span></span>. And various. </p>
<p>Did he testify [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διεμαρτυρετο] </span></span>. The preposition dia gives the force of solemnly, earnestly. </p>
<p>Save yourselves [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωθητε] </span></span>. More strictly, be ye saved. </p>
<p>Untoward [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκολιας] </span></span>. Lit., crooked. Toward in earlier English meant docile, apt. The opposite is froward (fromward). So Shakespeare : </p>
<p>"'Tis a good hearing when children are toward, But a harsh hearing when women are froward." Taming of the Shrew, 5, 2. </p>
<p>"Spoken like a toward prince." </p>
<p>3 Henry VI, 2, 2. </p>
<p>Untoward, therefore, meant intractable, perverse. So Shakespeare : </p>
<p>"What means this scorn, thou most untoward knave?" K. John, 1, 1. </p>
<p>"And if she be froward, Then hast thou taught Horensio to be untoward." Taming of the Shrew, 4, 5. </p>
<p>Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+32:5">Deuteronomy 32:5</span>.</p>
