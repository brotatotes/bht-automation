<p>When this was noised abroad [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενομενης δε της φωνης τευτης] </span></span>. Wrong. Lit., And this sound having taken place. Rev., correctly, when this sound was heard. The sound of the rushing wind. </p>
<p>Were confounded [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεχυθη] </span></span>. Lit., was poured together; so that confound (Latin, confundere) is the most literal rendering possible. Used only by Luke and in the Acts. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:32">Acts 19:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:31">Acts 21:31</span>. </p>
<p>Heard [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκουον] </span></span>. Imperfect, were hearing. </p>
<p>Language [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαλεκτω] </span></span>. Rather, dialect; since the foreigners present spoke, not only different languages, but different dialects of the same language. The Phrygians and Pamphylians, for instance, both spoke Greek, but in different idioms; the Parthians, Medes, and Elamites all spoke Persian, but in different provincial forms.</p>
