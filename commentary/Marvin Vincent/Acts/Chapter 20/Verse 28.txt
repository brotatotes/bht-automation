<p>To yourselves and to all the flock. To yourselves first, that you may duly care for the flock. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:16">1 Timothy 4:16</span>. </p>
<p>Overseers [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισκοπους] </span></span>. Denoting the official function of the elders, but not in the later ecclesiastical sense of bishops, as implying an order distinct from presbyters or elders. The two terms are synonymous. The elders, by virtue of their office, were overseers. 26 To feed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιμαινειν] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:6">Matthew 2:6</span>. The word embraces more than feeding; signifying all that is included in the office of a shepherd : tending, or shepherding. </p>
<p>Purchased [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιεποιησατο] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:13">1 Timothy 3:13</span>. See on peculiar people, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:9">1 Peter 2:9</span>. The verb means, originally, to make [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιεω</span></span>] to remain over and above [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περι</span></span>] : hence to keep or save for one's self; to compass or acquire.</p>
