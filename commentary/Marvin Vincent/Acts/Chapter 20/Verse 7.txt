<p>First [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη μια] </span></span>. Lit., "the one day." The cardinal numeral here used for the ordinal. </p>
<p>Week [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαββατων] </span></span>. The plural used for the singular, in imitation of the Hebrew form. The noun Sabbath is often used after numerals in the signification of a week. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:1">Matthew 28:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+16:1">Mark 16:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:19">John 20:19</span>. To break bread. The celebration of the eucharist, coupled with the Agape, or love - feast. </p>
<p>Preached [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διελεγετο] </span></span>. Better, as Rev., discoursed with them. It was a mingling of preaching and conference. Our word dialogue is derived from the verb.</p>
