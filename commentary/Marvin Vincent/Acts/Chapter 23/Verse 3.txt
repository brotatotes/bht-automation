<p>Shall smite thee [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τυπτειν σε μελλει] </span></span>. More strictly, is about to smite. The words are not an imprecation, but a prophecy of punishment for his violent dealing. According to Josephus, in the attack of the Sicarii upon Jerusalem, he was dragged from his hiding - place, in a sewer of the palace, and murdered by assassins. </p>
<p>Thou whited wall. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:27">Matthew 23:27</span>. </p>
<p>Contrary to the law [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρανομων] </span></span>. A verb. Lit., transgressing the law.</p>
