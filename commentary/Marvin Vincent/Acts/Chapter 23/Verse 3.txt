Shall smite thee [τυπτειν σε μελλει] . More strictly, is about to smite. The words are not an imprecation, but a prophecy of punishment for his violent dealing. According to Josephus, in the attack of the Sicarii upon Jerusalem, he was dragged from his hiding - place, in a sewer of the palace, and murdered by assassins. Thou whited wall. Compare Matthew 23:27. Contrary to the law [παρανομων] . A verb. Lit., transgressing the law.
