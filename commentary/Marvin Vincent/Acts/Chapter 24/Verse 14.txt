The way. See on ch. Acts 9:2. A sect. See on verse 5. The word is commonly used in an indifferent sense, as signifying merely a school or party. So ch. Acts 14:5; Acts 28:29. Here, however, in a bad sense - a schisomatic sect, as in 1 Corinthians 11:19. Worship [λατρευω] . Better, as Rev., serve. See on Luke 1:74. God of my fathers [τω πατρωω θεω] . A familiar classical phrase, and therefore well known to Felix. Thus Demosthenes calls Apollo the patrwov (ancestral God) of Athens. Socrates is asked (Plato, "Euthydemus," 302), "Have you an ancestral Zeus [ζευς πατρωος] ? So, frequently, in the classics. Similarly, the Roman phrase, Di patrii," the gods of the forefathers.
