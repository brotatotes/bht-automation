<p>Pestilent fellow [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λοιμον] </span></span>. Lit., a plague or pest. </p>
<p>Ringleader [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρωτοστατην] </span></span>. Originally, one who stands first on the right of a line; a file - leader. Thus Thucydides says that all armies when engaging are apt to thrust outward their right wing; and adds, "The first man in the front rank [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο πρωτοστατης</span></span>] of the right wing is originally responsible for the deflection" (v., 71). Here, of course, metaphorically, as A. V. and Rev. Only here in New Testament. </p>
<p>Sect [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιρεσεως] </span></span>. See on heresies, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:1">2 Peter 2:1</span>. </p>
<p>Nazarenes. The only passage in scripture where this term is used to denote the Christians. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:23">Matthew 2:23</span>.</p>
