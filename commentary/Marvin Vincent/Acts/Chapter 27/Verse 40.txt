<p>Taken up [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιελοντες] </span></span>. Wrong. The word means to remove, and refers here to cutting the anchor - cables, or casting off, as Rev. </p>
<p>Committed themselves [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειων] </span></span>. Wrong. The reference is to the anchors. Rev., correctly, left them in the sea. </p>
<p>Rudder bands [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζευκτηριας των πηδαλιων] </span></span>. Lit., the bands of the rudders. The larger ships had two rudders, like broad oars or paddles, joined together by a pole, and managed by one steersman. They could be pulled up and fastened with hands to the ship; as was done in this case, probably to avoid fouling the anchors when they were cast out of the stern. The bands were now loosened, in order that the ship might be driven forward. </p>
<p>Mainsail [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρτεμωνα] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. Probably the foresail. So Rev. </p>
<p>Made toward [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατειχον] </span></span>. Lit., held; bore down for. </p>
