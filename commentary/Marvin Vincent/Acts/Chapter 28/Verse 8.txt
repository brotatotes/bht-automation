<p>Sick [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεχομενον] </span></span>. Lit., taken or holden. See on taken, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:38">Luke 4:38</span>. Fever [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυρετοις] </span></span>. Lit., fevers. This peculiarly medical use of the plural is confined to Luke in the New Testament. It denotes successive and varying attacks of fever. </p>
<p>Bloody flux [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυσεντερια] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. Our word dysentery is nearly a transcript of it. Hippocrates often speaks of the two complaints in combination. </p>
<p>Healed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιασατο] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:19">Luke 6:19</span>.</p>
