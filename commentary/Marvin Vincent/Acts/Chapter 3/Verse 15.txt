<p>The Prince of life [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρχηγον της ζωης] </span></span>. The Greek brings out by the position of these words what Bengel calls "the magnificent antithesis" between a murderer and the Prince of life. "Ye demanded a murderer, but the Prince of life ye killed." This is the only place where the phrase occurs. 'Archgov, though sometimes rendered prince, means, primarily, beginning, and thence originator, author. Better here as Rev., in margin, author, and so by Rev. at <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:10">Hebrews 2:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:2">Hebrews 12:2</span>.</p>
