<p>He took [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιασας] </span></span>. The verb means originally to press or squeeze; and hence implies taking hold with a firm grasp. </p>
<p>Feet [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασεις] </span></span>. A peculiar, technical word, used by Luke only, and described by Galen as the part of the foot lying beneath the leg, upon which the leg directly rests, as distinguished from the tarsov, the flat of the foot between the toes and heel, and pedion, the part next the toes. Ankle - bones [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σφυρα] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. Also technical. Some of the best texts read sfudra, but the meaning is the same. Received strength [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εστερεωθησαν] </span></span>. Used by Luke only. Compare " the churches were established (ch. 16 5), and the kindred noun sterewma, steadfastness (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+2:5">Colossians 2:5</span>). In medical language applied to the bones in particular.</p>
