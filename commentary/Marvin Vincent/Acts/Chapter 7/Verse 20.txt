<p>Time [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιρω] </span></span>. Better, season or juncture. "Sad, seasonable" (Bengel). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:7">Acts 1:7</span>. </p>
<p>Exceeding air [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αστειος τω θεω] </span></span>. Lit., fair unto God : a Hebrew superlative. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jon+3:3">Jonah 3:3</span> : great unto God; A. V., exceeding great. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+10:9">Genesis 10:9</span>, of Nimrod : a mighty hunter before the Lord. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:4">2 Corinthians 10:4</span> : mighty unto God; i e., in God 's sight. 'Asteiov, fair (only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:23">Hebrews 11:23</span>), is from astu, a town, and means originally town - bred; hence refined, elegant, comely. The word is used in the Septuagint of Moses (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+2:2">Exodus 2:2</span>), and rendered goodly. The Jewish traditions extol Moses ' beauty. Josephus says that those who met him, as he was carried along the streets, forgot their business and stood still to gaze at him.</p>
