Time [καιρω] . Better, season or juncture. "Sad, seasonable" (Bengel). See on Acts 1:7. Exceeding air [αστειος τω θεω] . Lit., fair unto God : a Hebrew superlative. Compare Jonah 3:3 : great unto God; A. V., exceeding great. Genesis 10:9, of Nimrod : a mighty hunter before the Lord. 2 Corinthians 10:4 : mighty unto God; i e., in God 's sight. 'Asteiov, fair (only here and Hebrews 11:23), is from astu, a town, and means originally town - bred; hence refined, elegant, comely. The word is used in the Septuagint of Moses (Exodus 2:2), and rendered goodly. The Jewish traditions extol Moses ' beauty. Josephus says that those who met him, as he was carried along the streets, forgot their business and stood still to gaze at him.
