<p>That came after [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαδεξαμενοι] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. The verb originally means to receive from one another, in succession; and that appears to be the more simple and natural rendering here : having received it (from Moses). Rev., very neatly, in their turn. </p>
<p>Jesus. Joshua. The names are the same, both signifying Savior. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+1:21">Matthew 1:21</span>. </p>
<p>Into the possession [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη κατασχεσει] </span></span>. Rev., when they entered on the possession. 15 Before the face [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο προσωπου] </span></span>. More strictly, "away from the face." The same expression occurs in the Septuagint, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+11:23">Deuteronomy 11:23</span>.</p>
