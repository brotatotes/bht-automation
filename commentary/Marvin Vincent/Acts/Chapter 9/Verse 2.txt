Of this way [της οδου] . Rev., more correctly, "the way." A common expression in the Acts for the Christian religion : "the characteristic direction of life as determined by faith on Jesus Christ" (Meyer). See ch. Acts 19:9; Acts 22:4; Acts 24:22. For the fuller expression of the idea, see ch. Acts 16:17; Acts 18:25. Women. Paul three times alludes to his persecution of women as an aggravation of his cruelty (ch. Acts 8:3; Acts 9:2; Acts 22:4).
