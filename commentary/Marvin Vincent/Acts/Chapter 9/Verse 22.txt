<p>Confounded. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:6">Acts 2:6</span>. </p>
<p>Proving [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συμβιβαζων] </span></span>. The verb means to bring or put together : hence to compare and examine, as evidence, and so to prove. Used in the literal and physical sense in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:16">Ephesians 4:16</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+2:2">Colossians 2:2</span>, of being knit together in love. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:16">1 Corinthians 2:16</span>, of instructing, building up, by putting together. In this sense the word occurs in the Septuagint. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+10:11">Leviticus 10:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:8">Jude 1:8</span>:8. </p>
<p>The Christ. Note the article. Not a proper name, but an appellative. See on ver. 20.</p>
