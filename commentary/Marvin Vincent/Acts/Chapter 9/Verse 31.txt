<p>Comfort [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρακλησει] </span></span>. From parakalew, to call toward or to one's side for help. The word is rendered in the New Testament both exhortation and consolation. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:15">Acts 13:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:8">Romans 12:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:17">2 Corinthians 8:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:5">Hebrews 12:5</span>; and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:25">Luke 2:25</span> (see note); <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:16">2 Thessalonians 2:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:4">Matthew 5:4</span>. In some passages the meaning is disputed, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:1">Philippians 2:1</span>, where, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:3">1 Corinthians 14:3</span>, it is joined with paramuqion or paramuqia, the meaning of which also varies between incentive and consolation or assuagement. Here exhortation is the rendering approved by the best authorities, to be construed with was multiplied : was multiplied by the exhortation of the Holy Ghost; i e., by the Holy Spirit inspiring the preachers, and moving the hearts of the hearers.</p>
