<p>Walk worthy [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιπατησαι αξιως] </span></span>. The phrase occurs <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:1">Ephesians 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:12">1 Thessalonians 2:12</span>. Rev. gives the correct adverbial rendering worthily. </p>
<p>Unto all pleasing [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις πασαν αρεσκειαν] </span></span>. So as to please God in all ways. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:1">1 Thessalonians 4:1</span>, Areskeia pleasing, only here in the New Testament. In classical Greek it has a bad sense, obsequiousness, cringing. Compare men - pleasers, ch. 3 22. </p>
<p>In the knowledge [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις την επιγνωσιν] </span></span>. Lit. unto the knowledge. The best texts read th ejpignwsei "by the knowledge :" by means of.</p>
