<p>Power [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξουσιας] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:10">Mark 2:10</span>. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:18">Mark 2:18</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:5">Mark 2:5</span> Translated [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετεστησεν] </span></span>. The word occurs five times in the New Testament : of putting out of the stewardship, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:4">Luke 16:4</span>; of the removal of Saul from the kingdom, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:22">Acts 13:22</span>; of Paul turning away much people, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:26">Acts 19:26</span>; and of removing mountains, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:2">1 Corinthians 13:2</span>. A change of kingdoms is indicated. </p>
<p>Kingdom. Hence God 's kingdom is in the present, no less than in heaven. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:20">Luke 6:20</span>. </p>
<p>Of His dear Son [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του υιου της αγαπης αυτου] </span></span>. Lit., of the Son of His love. So Rev. The Son who is the object of His love, and to whom, therefore, the kingdom is given. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+2:7">Psalms 2:7</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+2:8">Psalms 2:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:3-9">Hebrews 1:3-9</span>. It is true that love is the essence of the Son as of the Father; also, that the Son's mission is the revelation of the Father 's love; but, as Meyer correctly says, "the language refers to the exalted Christ who rules."</p>
