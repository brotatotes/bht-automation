<p>He is [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτος εστιν] </span></span>. Both words are emphatic. Estin is, is used as in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:58">John 8:58</span> (see note), to express Christ 's absolute existence. "He emphasizes the personality, is the preexistence" (Lightfoot). For similar emphasis on the pronoun, see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:14">Ephesians 2:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:10">Ephesians 4:10</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:11">Ephesians 4:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:2">1 John 2:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+19:15">Revelation 19:15</span>. </p>
<p>Before all things. In time. </p>
<p>By Him [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αυτω</span></span>] . In Him as ver. 16. So Rev. </p>
<p>Consist [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεστηκεν] </span></span>. Cohere, in mutual dependence. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:28">Acts 27:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:3">Hebrews 1:3</span>. For other meanings of the verb, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:5">Romans 3:5</span>. Christ not only creates, but maintains in continuous stability and productiveness. "He, the All - powerful, All - holy Word of the Father, spreads His power over all things everywhere, enlightening things seen and unseen, holding and binding all together in Himself. Nothing is left empty of His presence, but to all things and through all, severally and collectively, He is the giver and sustainer of life.... He, the Wisdom of God, holds the universe in tune together. He it is who, binding all with each, and ordering all things by His will and pleasure, produces the perfect unity of nature and the harmonious reign of law. While He abides unmoved forever with the Father, He yet moves all things by His own appointment according to the Father 's will" (Athanasius).</p>
