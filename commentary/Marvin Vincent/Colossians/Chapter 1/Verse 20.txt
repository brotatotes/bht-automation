<p>Having made peace [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειρηνοποιησας] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. Having concluded peace; see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:21">John 3:21</span>. The participle is parallel with to reconcile, and marks peace - making and reconciliation as contemporaneous. The kindred eijrhnopoiov peacemaker, only in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:9">Matthew 5:9</span>. The phrase making peace, in which the two factors of this verb appear separately, occurs only <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:15">Ephesians 2:15</span>. </p>
<p>To reconcile [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκαταλλαξαι] </span></span>. Only here, ver. 21, and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:16">Ephesians 2:16</span>. The connection is : it was the good pleasure of the Father (ver. 19) to reconcile. The compounded preposition ajpo gives the force of back, hinting at restoration to a primal unity. So, in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:12-16">Ephesians 2:12-16</span>, it occurs as in ver. 21, in connection with ajphllotriwmenoi alienated, as if they had not always been strangers. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:12">Ephesians 2:12</span>. Others explain to reconcile wholly. For the verb katallassw to reconcile, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:10">Romans 5:10</span>. </p>
<p>All things [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα παντα] </span></span>. Must be taken in the same sense as in vers. 16, 17, 18, the whole universe, material and spiritual. 191 The arrangement of clauses adopted by Rev. is simpler.</p>
