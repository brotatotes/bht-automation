<p>And the Father. Some of the best texts omit and. So Rev. The form with and is the more common. Compare ch. 3 17. </p>
<p>Praying always for you. Rather connect always with we give thanks, and render we give thanks for you always, praying, or in our prayers. </p>
<p>According to the Greek order, praying for you (as Rev. and A. V.), would make for you unduly emphatic.</p>
