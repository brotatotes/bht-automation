<p>We also. Marking the reciprocal feeling of Paul and Timothy with that of the Colossians. </p>
<p>Pray - desire [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσευχομενοι - αιτουμενοι] </span></span>. The words occur together in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:24">Mark 11:24</span>. The former is general, the latter special. Rev. make request is better than desire. The A. V. renders indiscriminately ask and desire. Rev. alters desire to ask. Desire in the sense of ask occurs in Shakespeare and Spenser. </p>
<p>Knowledge [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιγνωσιν] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:20">Romans 3:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:6">Philippians 1:6</span>. Full knowledge. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:21">Romans 1:21</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:28">Romans 1:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:12">1 Corinthians 13:12</span>, where Paul contrasts ginwskein to know gnwsiv knowledge, with ejpiginwskein to know fully, ejpignwsiv full knowledge. Here appropriate to the knowledge of God in Christ as the perfection of knowledge. </p>
<p>Wisdom and spiritual understanding [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σοφια και συνεσει πνευματικη] </span></span>. Rev., better, applies spiritual to both - spiritual wisdom and understanding. The kindred adjectives sofov wise and sunetov prudent, occur together, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:25">Matthew 11:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:21">Luke 10:21</span>. For sofia wisdom, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:33">Romans 11:33</span>, and on wise, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:13">James 3:13</span>. For sunesiv understanding, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:33">Mark 12:33</span>, and prudent, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:25">Matthew 11:25</span>. The distinction is between general and special. Understanding is the critical apprehension of particulars growing out of wisdom, which apprehension is practically applied by fronhsiv prudence, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:17">Luke 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:8">Ephesians 1:8</span>. Spiritual is emphatic, as contrasted with the vain philosophy of false teachers.</p>
