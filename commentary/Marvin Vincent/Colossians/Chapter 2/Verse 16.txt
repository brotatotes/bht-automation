<p>Therefore. Conclusion from the canceling of the bond. The allusions which follow (vers. 16 - 19) are to the practical and theoretical forms of the Colossian error, as in vers. 9 - 15; excessive ritualism, asceticism, and angelic mediation. </p>
<p>Judge [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρινετω] </span></span>. Sit in judgment. </p>
<p>Meat - drink [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βρωσει - ποσει] </span></span>. Properly, eating, drinking, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+8:4">1 Corinthians 8:4</span>; but the nouns are also used for that which is eaten or drunk, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:32">John 4:32</span> (see note); <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:27">John 6:27</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:55">John 6:55</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:17">Romans 14:17</span>. For the subject - matter compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:17">Romans 14:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+8:8">1 Corinthians 8:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:10">Hebrews 9:10</span>, and note on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:19">Mark 7:19</span>. The Mosaic law contained very few provisions concerning drinks. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+10:9">Leviticus 10:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+11:34">Leviticus 11:34</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+11:36">Leviticus 11:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="nu+6:3">Numbers 6:3</span>. Hence it is probable that the false teachers had extended the prohibitions as to the use of wine to all Christians. The Essenes abjured both wine and animal food. In respect [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν μερει] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:10">2 Corinthians 3:10</span>. Lit., in the division or category. </p>
<p>Holyday [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εορτης] </span></span>. Festival or feast - day. The annual festivals. The word holyday is used in its earlier sense of a sacred day. </p>
<p>New moon [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νουμηνιας] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. The monthly festivals. The festival of the new moon is placed beside the Sabbath, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+1:13">Isaiah 1:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eze+46:1">Ezekiel 46:1</span>. The day was celebrated by blowing of trumpets, special sacrifices, feasting, and religious instruction. Labor was suspended, and no national or private fasts were permitted to take place. The authorities were at great pains to fix accurately the commencement of the month denoted by the appearance of the new moon. Messengers were placed on commanding heights to watch the sky, and as soon as the new moon appeared, they hastened to communicate it to the synod, being allowed even to travel on the Sabbath for this purpose. The witnesses were assembled and examined, and when the judges were satisfied the president pronounced the words it is sanctified, and the day was declared new moon. </p>
<p>Sabbath days [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαββατων] </span></span>. The weekly festivals. Rev., correctly, day, the plural being used for the singular. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:31">Luke 4:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:7">Acts 20:7</span>. The plural is only once used in the New Testament of more than a single day (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:2">Acts 17:2</span>). The same enumeration of sacred seasons occurs 1Ch 33:31; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ch+2:4">2 Chronicles 2:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ch+31:3">2 Chronicles 31:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eze+45:17">Ezekiel 45:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ho+2:11">Hosea 2:11</span>.</p>
