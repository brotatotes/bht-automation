<p>Beguile [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραλογιζηται] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:22">James 1:22</span>. See note. Rev., delude. So Ignatius, speaking of the duty of obedience to the bishop, says : "He that fails in this, does not deceive the visible bishop, but attempts to cheat [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραλογιζεται</span></span>] the Invisible" (Epistle to Magnesians, 3.). The word is found in the Septuagint, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jos+9:22">Joshua 9:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1sa+19:17">1 Samuel 19:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2sa+21:5">2 Samuel 21:5</span>. </p>
<p>Enticing words [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιθανολογια] </span></span>. Rev., persuasiveness of speech. Only here in the New Testament. In classical Greek, of probable argument as opposed to demonstration. So Plato : "Reflect whether you are disposed to admit of probability [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιθανολογια</span></span>] and figures of speech in matters of such importance" (" Theaetetus, " 163). Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:4">1 Corinthians 2:4</span>.</p>
