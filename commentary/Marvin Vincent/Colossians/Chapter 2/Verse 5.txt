<p>Order [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταξιν] </span></span>. Or orderly array. A military metaphor, quite possibly suggested by Paul 's intercourse with the soldiers in his confinement. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:13">Philippians 1:13</span>. </p>
<p>Steadfastness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στερεωμα] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:9">1 Peter 5:9</span>. The kindred adjective stereov solid, occurs <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:19">2 Timothy 2:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:12">Hebrews 5:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:9">1 Peter 5:9</span>; and the verb stereow to make solid, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:7">Acts 3:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:5">Acts 16:5</span>. The military metaphor is continued. Faith is represented as a host solidly drawn up : your solid front, close phalanx. The verb is found in this sense in the Apocrypha, 1 Macc. 10 50, "ejsterewse ton polemon, he solidified the battle; massed his lines. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eze+13:5">Ezekiel 13:5</span>, where the noun has the sense of stronghold :" They stood not ejn sterewmati in the stronghold. "So <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+17:2">Psalms 17:2</span>," The Lord is my strength; " stronghold or bulwark. The firmament, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+1:6">Genesis 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eze+1:22">Ezekiel 1:22</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="es+9:22">Esther 9:22</span>, of the confirmation of a letter.</p>
