<p>One another - one another [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλληλων - εαυτοις] </span></span>. Lit., one another - yourselves. For a similar variation of the pronoun see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:32">Ephesians 4:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:8-10">1 Peter 4:8-10</span>. The latter pronoun emphasizes the fact that they are all members of Christ 's body - everyone members one of another - so that, in forgiving each other they forgive themselves. Quarrel [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μομφην] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. Cause of blame. Rev., complaint. The A. V. uses quarrel in its earlier sense of cause of complaint. So Shakespeare : </p>
<p>"The lady Beatrice hath a quarrel to you." " Much Ado, " 2, 1. </p>
<p>"Against whom comest thou, and what's thy quarrel?" " Richard ii, " 1, 3, 33. </p>
<p>Holinshed : "He thought he had a good quarrel to attack him." It was used of a plaintiff's action at law, like the Latin querela.</p>
