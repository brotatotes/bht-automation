<p>Wives, etc. Compare the parallel passages, Eph 5:22 - vi. 9. See also 1Pe 2:18 - iii. 7; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:1-5">Titus 2:1-5</span>. </p>
<p>Is fit [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανηκεν] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:8">Philippians 1:8</span>. The imperfect tense, was fitting, or became fitting, points to the time of their entrance upon the christian life. Not necessarily presupposing that the duty remained unperformed. </p>
<p>Lightfoot illustrates by ought, the past tense of owed, and says, "the past tense perhaps implies an essential a priori obligation." </p>
<p>In the Lord. Connect with is fitting, and compare well - pleasing in the Lord, ver. 20.</p>
