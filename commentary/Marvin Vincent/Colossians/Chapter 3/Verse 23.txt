<p>Ye do - do it [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιητε - εργαζεσθε] </span></span>. Rev., correctly, ye do - work; the latter being the stronger term as opposed to idleness. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:9">James 2:9</span>. An idle man may do. Compare ejrgasia diligence, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:58">Luke 12:58</span>. Heartily [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ ψυχης] </span></span>. Lit., from the soul. With a personal interest. Note that the apostle uses both heart (kardiav, ver. 22) and soul [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψυχης] </span></span>; and in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:7">Ephesians 6:7</span>, adds met' eujnoiav with good disposition (A. V., good will). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:3">Romans 11:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:23">Romans 7:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:21">Romans 1:21</span>. Compare sumyucoi of one accord, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:2">Philippians 2:2</span>; ijsoyucon like - minded, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:20">Philippians 2:20</span>; mia yuch with one mind, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:27">Philippians 1:27</span>.</p>
