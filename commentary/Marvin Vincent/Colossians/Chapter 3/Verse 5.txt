<p>Mortify [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νεκρωσατε] </span></span>. Only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:19">Romans 4:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:12">Hebrews 11:12</span>. Mortify is used in its literal sense of put to death. </p>
<p>So Erasmus : "Christ was mortified and killed." And Shakespeare : </p>
<p>"- his wildness mortified in him, Seemed to die too." </p>
<p>"I Henry v, 1, 26" </p>
<p>Members [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μελη] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:13">Romans 6:13</span>. The physical members, so far as they are employed in the service of sin. The word falls in with the allusions to bodily austerities in ch. 2. </p>
<p>Which are upon the earth. Compare ver. 2. The organs of the earthly and sensuous life. </p>
<p>Fornication, etc. In apposition with members, denoting the modes in which the members sinfully exert themselves. </p>
<p>Inordinate affection, evil concupiscence [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παθος, επιθυμιαν κακην] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:26">Romans 1:26</span>. </p>
<p>And covetousness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">και πλεονεξιαν] </span></span>. And has a climactic force; and especially; see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:29">Romans 1:29</span>. </p>
<p>Which is [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ητις εστιν] </span></span>. The compound relative, explanatory and classifying. Seeing it stands in the category of. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:5">Ephesians 5:5</span>. Idolatry. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:10">1 Corinthians 5:10</span>.</p>
