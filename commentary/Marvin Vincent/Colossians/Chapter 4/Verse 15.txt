<p>Nymphas. Probably contracted from Nymphodorus, as Artemas from Artemidorus (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:12">Titus 3:12</span>) : Zenas from Zenodorus (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:13">Titus 3:13</span>); Olympas from Olympiodorus (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:15">Romans 16:15</span>). </p>
<p>The Church. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:2">Philippians 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:5">Romans 16:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:19">1 Corinthians 16:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:12">Acts 12:12</span>. </p>
<p>His house [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτου] </span></span>. Others read aujtwn their (so Rev., Lightfoot, Meyer). Others, as Westcott and Hort, aujthv her, regarding the name as female, Nympha. It is difficult, however, to know to whom the plural can refer. Some explain, Nymphas and his family. Meyer refers it to the brethren at Laodicaea and Nymphas, and thinks that the allusion is to a foreign church in filial association with the church at Laodicaea, and holding its meetings in the same place.</p>
