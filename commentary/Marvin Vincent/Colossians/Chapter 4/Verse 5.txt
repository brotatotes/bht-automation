<p>Those that are without [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους εξω] </span></span>. As <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:12">1 Corinthians 5:12</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:13">1 Corinthians 5:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:12">1 Thessalonians 4:12</span>. Compare touv esw those within, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:12">1 Corinthians 5:12</span>. Redeeming the time [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον καιρον εξαγοραζομενοι] </span></span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:16">Ephesians 5:16</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="da+2:8">Daniel 2:8</span>, Sept. The word is used in the New Testament only by Paul, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:13">Galatians 3:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:5">Galatians 4:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:16">Ephesians 5:16</span>. The compounded preposition ejx has the meaning out of; as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:13">Galatians 3:13</span>, "Christ redeemed us out of the curse," etc., and out and out, fully. So here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:16">Ephesians 5:16</span>, buy up. Rev., in margin, buying up the opportunity. The favorable opportunity becomes ours at the price of duty.</p>
