<p>Unto the redemption, etc. Construe with ye were sealed. </p>
<p>Of the purchased possession [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της περιποιησεως] </span></span>. See on peculiar, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:9">1 Peter 2:9</span>. The word originally means a making to remain over and above; hence preservation; preservation for one's self; acquisition; the thing acquired, or a possession. Used here collectively for the people possessed, as the circumcision for those circumcised, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+3:3">Philippians 3:3</span>; the election for those chosen, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:7">Romans 11:7</span>. Rev., God 's own possession, God 's own being inserted for the sake of clearness. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+43:21">Isaiah 43:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:28">Acts 20:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:14">Titus 2:14</span>. </p>
<p>Unto the praise of His glory. Construe with ye were sealed : Ye were sealed unto the redemption, etc.; setting forth God 's purpose as it contemplates man. Ye were sealed unto the praise of His glory; God 's purpose as it respects Himself</p>
