<p>Which [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ην] </span></span>. Refer to working (ver. 19). </p>
<p>He wrought [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενηργησεν] </span></span>. The best texts read ejnhrghken, perfect tense, He hath wrought. The verb is kindred with working (ver. 19). </p>
<p>In Christ. In the case of Christ. Christ 's dead body was the point on which this working of divine power was exhibited. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:11">Romans 8:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:14">2 Corinthians 4:14</span>. </p>
<p>When He raised [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγειρας] </span></span>. Or, in that He raised. </p>
<p>And set [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">και εκαθισεν] </span></span>. Rev., made Him to sit. The best texts read kaqisav having seated, or in that He caused him to sit. </p>
<p>Right hand. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:56">Acts 7:56</span>. </p>
<p>In the heavenly places. See on ver. 2. Local. Not merely of a spiritual state, which does not suit the local expressions made to sit and right hand.</p>
