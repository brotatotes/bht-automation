<p>Far above [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερανω] </span></span>. Lit., over above. See on ver. 19. Connect with made Him to sit. </p>
<p>Principality, power, etc. These words usually refer to angelic powers; either good, as ch. 3 10; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:16">Colossians 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+2:10">Colossians 2:10</span>; or bad, as ch. 6 12; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:24">1 Corinthians 14:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+2:15">Colossians 2:15</span>; or both, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:38">Romans 8:38</span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:16">Colossians 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+2:15">Colossians 2:15</span>. Here probably good, since the passage relates to Christ 's exaltation to glory rather than to His victory over evil powers. And every name that is named. And has a collective and summary force - and in a word. Every name, etc. Whatever a name can be given to. "Let any name be uttered, whatever it is, Christ is above it; is more exalted than that which the name so uttered affirms" (Meyer). Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:9">Philippians 2:9</span>. "We know that the emperor precedes all, though we cannot enumerate all the ministers of his court : so we know that Christ is placed above all, although we cannot name all" (Bengel). </p>
<p>Not only in this world, etc. Connect with which is named. For world [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιωνι] </span></span>, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:9">John 1:9</span>.</p>
