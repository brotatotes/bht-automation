<p>Having predestinated [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προορισας] </span></span>. Rev. foreordained. From pro before, oJrizw to define, the latter word being from opov a boundary. Hence to define or determine beforehand. </p>
<p>Adoption [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υιοθεσιαν] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:15">Romans 8:15</span>. Never used of Christ. Good pleasure [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευδοκιαν] </span></span>. Not strictly in the sense of kindly or friendly feeling, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:14">Luke 2:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:15">Philippians 1:15</span>, but because it pleased Him, see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:21">Luke 10:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:26">Matthew 11:26</span>. The other sense, however, is included and implied, and is expressed by in love.</p>
