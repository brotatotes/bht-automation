<p>We have. Or are having. The freely bestowed (ver. 6) is thus illustrated by experience. The divine purpose is being accomplished in the lives of believers. </p>
<p>Redemption [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την απολυτρωσιν] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:24">Romans 3:24</span>. Note the article : our redemption. </p>
<p>Through His blood. Further defining and explaining in whom. </p>
<p>Forgiveness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφεσιν] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:3">Luke 3:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:15">James 5:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:25">Romans 3:25</span>. Forgiveness specifies the peculiar quality of redemption. </p>
<p>Sins [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραπτωματων] </span></span>. Rev., better, trespasses. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:14">Matthew 6:14</span>. Riches. See on glory, ver. 6, and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:4">Romans 2:4</span>.</p>
