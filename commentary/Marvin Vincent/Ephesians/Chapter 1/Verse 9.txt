<p>Having made known. The participle is explanatory of which He made to abound, etc. : in that He made known. </p>
<p>The mystery of His will. For mystery, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:25">Romans 11:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:26">Colossians 1:26</span>. Another key - word of this epistle. God 's grace as manifested in redemption is a mystery in virtue of its riches and depth - as the expression of God 's very nature. The mystery of the redemption in Christ, belonging to the eternal plan of God, could be known to men only through revelation - making known. Of his will; pertaining to his will. Compare ch. 3 9. </p>
<p>Purposed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προεθετο] </span></span>. Only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:13">Romans 1:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:25">Romans 3:25</span> (note). </p>
<p>In Himself [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν αυτω] </span></span>. The best texts read aujtw in Him; but the reference is clearly to God, not to Christ, who is expressly mentioned in the next verse.</p>
