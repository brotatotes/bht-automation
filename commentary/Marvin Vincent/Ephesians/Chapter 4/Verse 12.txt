<p>For the perfecting [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς τον καταρτισμον] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. In classical Greek of refitting a ship or setting a bone. The preposition for denotes the ultimate purpose. Ministering and building are means to this end. Hence its emphatic position in the sentence. For perfecting, see on mending, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:21">Matthew 4:21</span>; perfected, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:16">Matthew 21:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:40">Luke 6:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:10">1 Peter 5:10</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:10">1 Corinthians 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:21">Hebrews 13:21</span>. The radical idea of adjustment is brought out in ver. 13. </p>
<p>For the work of the ministry [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις εργον διακονιας] </span></span>. Rev., much better, unto the work of ministering. Eijv unto, marks the immediate purpose of the gift. He gave apostles, etc., unto the work of ministering and building, for the perfecting, etc. The prevailing sense of diakonia ministry, in the New Testament, is spiritual service of an official character. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:25">Acts 1:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:4">Acts 6:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:24">Acts 20:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:13">Romans 11:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:12">1 Timothy 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:5">2 Timothy 4:5</span>. </p>
<p>Edifying [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικοδομην] </span></span>. Rev., building up. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:32">Acts 20:32</span>. Notice the combination of perfecting and building. Building defines the nature of the work of ministry, and perfecting comes through a process.</p>
