<p>Children [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νηπιοι] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:6">1 Corinthians 2:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:1">1 Corinthians 3:1</span>. As to the connection, ver. 13 states the ultimate goal of christian training; ver. 14 that which is pursued with a view to the attainment of that goal. Ver. 14 is subordinate to ver. 13, as is shown by the retention of the same figure, and is remotely dependent on vers 11, 12. The remote end, ver. 13, is placed before the more immediate one, as in ver. 12. See note. </p>
<p>Tossed to and fro [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλυδωνιζομενοι] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. See on wave, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:6">James 1:6</span>. For Paul 's use of nautical metaphors, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:23">Philippians 1:23</span>. Compare Plato : "Socrates. In a ship, if a man having the power to do what he likes, has no intelligence or skill in navigation, do you see what will happen to him and to his fellow - sailors ? Alcibiades. Yes, I see that they will all perish" (" Alcibiades, " 1, 135). </p>
<p>Wind of doctrine. Or of the teaching. The different teachings of philosophers or of religious quacks are represented as winds, blowing the unstable soul in every direction. </p>
<p>Sleight [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυβεια] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. From kubov a cube or die. Lit., dice - playing. </p>
<p>Cunning craftiness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πανουργια] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:23">Luke 20:23</span>. The craft which gamblers use. </p>
<p>Whereby they lie in wait to deceive [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς την μεθοδειαν της πλανης] </span></span>. Lit., tending to the system of error. Rev., after the wiles of error. Meqodeia means a deliberate planning or system. Of error includes the idea of deceit or delusion. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:64">Matthew 27:64</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:27">Romans 1:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:18">2 Peter 2:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:17">2 Peter 3:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:20">James 5:20</span>. Error organizes. It has its systems and its logic. Ellicott remarks that here it is almost personified.</p>
