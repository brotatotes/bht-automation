<p>Fitly joined - compacted [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συναρμολογουμενον - συμβιβαζομενον] </span></span>. The present participles denote present, continuous progress. The two participles represent respectively the ideas of harmony or adaptation and compactness or solidity. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:22">Acts 9:22</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+2:2">Colossians 2:2</span>. </p>
<p>By that which every joint supplieth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια πασης αφης της επιχορηγιας] </span></span>. Lit., through every joint of the supply. For joint, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+2:19">Colossians 2:19</span>; for supply, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:5">2 Peter 1:5</span>. The supply specifies it as peculiarly Christ 's. The phrase joint of the supply signifies joint whose office or purpose it is to supply. Construe with the two participles, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+2:19">Colossians 2:19</span>. </p>
<p>According to the working. Construe with maketh increase. </p>
<p>In the measure of every part. According as each part works in its own proper measure. </p>
<p>Maketh. Notice the peculiar phrase; the whole body maketh increase of the body. It is a living organism, and its growth is produced by vital power within itself. </p>
<p>In love. As the element in which the upbuilding takes place. Compare ch. 3 17 - 19.</p>
