<p>Who [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιτινες] </span></span>. Explanatory and classifying : men of the class which. Being past feeling [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απηλγηκοτες] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. Lit, the verb means to cease from feeling pain. Hence to be apathetic. Have given themselves over [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεδωκαν] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:12">Matthew 4:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:27">Matthew 11:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:2">Matthew 26:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:29">Mark 4:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:2">Luke 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:23">1 Peter 2:23</span>. The verb is frequently used of Christ giving Himself for the world. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:25">Romans 4:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:20">Galatians 2:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:5">Ephesians 5:5</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:25">Ephesians 5:25</span>. It indicates a complete surrender. Meyer says, "with frightful emphasis." Where men persistently give themselves up to evil, God gives them up to its power. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:24">Romans 1:24</span>. </p>
<p>Lasciviousness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασελγεια] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:22">Mark 7:22</span>. </p>
<p>To work [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις εργασιαν] </span></span>. Lit., to a working. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:25">Acts 19:25</span>, used of a trade. Not precisely in this sense here, yet with a shade of it. They gave themselves up as to the prosecution of a business. The eijv unto is very forcible. </p>
<p>With greediness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν πλεονεξια] </span></span>. The noun commonly rendered covetousness : in an eager grasping after more and more uncleanness. Not with, but in, as the state of mind in which they wrought evil.</p>
