<p>That ye put away. Dependent upon ye were taught, and specifying the purport of the teaching. </p>
<p>The old man. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:6">Romans 6:6</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+3:9">Colossians 3:9</span>. </p>
<p>Which is corrupt [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον φθειρομενον] </span></span>. The A. V. misses the force of the participle. The verb is passive, which is being corrupted, 170 and marks the progressive condition of corruption which characterizes "the old man." Rev., correctly, waxeth corrupt. </p>
<p>According to the deceitful lusts [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα τας επιθυμιας της απατης] </span></span>. Rev., correctly, lusts of deceit. On the vicious rendering of similar phrases in A. V., see on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:19">Ephesians 1:19</span>. Deceit is personified.</p>
