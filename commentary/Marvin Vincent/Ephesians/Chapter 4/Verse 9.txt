<p>Now that He ascended. vers. 9 and 10 are parenthetical, showing what the ascension of Christ presupposes. By descending into the depths and ascending above all, He entered upon His function of filling the whole universe, in virtue of which function He distributes gifts to men. See ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:23">Ephesians 1:23</span>. Rev., properly, inserts this, thus giving the force of the article which calls attention to the fact of ascension alluded to in the quotation. "Now the or this 'He ascended." ' What is it but. What does it imply? </p>
<p>Descended first [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">και κατεβη] </span></span>. His ascent implies a previous descent. A. V. reads first, following the Tex. Rec. prwton. Rev., correctly, He also descended. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:13">John 3:13</span>. </p>
<p>The lower parts of the earth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα κατωτερα μερη της γης] </span></span>. The under world. The reference is to Christ 's descent into Hades. Some give the words a comparative force, deeper than the earth.</p>
