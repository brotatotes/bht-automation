<p>Walk in love. As imitators of God who is love. </p>
<p>Loved us [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημας</span></span>] The correct reading is uJmav you. </p>
<p>Gave [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεδωκεν] </span></span>. To death Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:25">Romans 4:25</span>, where the same verb was delivered is followed by was raised. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:32">Romans 8:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:20">Galatians 2:20</span>. </p>
<p>Offering - sacrifice [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσφοραν - θυσιαν] </span></span>. Offering, general, including the life as well as the death of Christ : sacrifice, special : on the cross. Properly, a slain offering. </p>
<p>A sweet smelling savor [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οσμην ευωδιας] </span></span>. Rev., correctly, odor of a sweet smell. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:14">2 Corinthians 2:14</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:15">2 Corinthians 2:15</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:16">2 Corinthians 2:16</span>. The Septuagint, in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+1:9">Leviticus 1:9</span>, uses this phrase to render the Hebrew, a savor of quietness. For [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span>] expresses design, that it might become, or result : so that it became.</p>
