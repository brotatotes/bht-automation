<p>Therefore [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα] </span></span>. Rev, correctly, but. Offsetting the relation of savior. The comparison does not hold in respect of salvation, but it does hold in respect of subjection. </p>
<p>26 Sanctify and cleanse [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγιαση καθαρισας] </span></span>. Rev., might sanctify, having cleansed. The Rev. brings out the proper succession of sanctification as a consequence of cleansing : might sanctify after having cleansed. </p>
<p>With the washing of water [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω λουτρω του υδατος] </span></span>. Loutron washing is properly laver. Note the article, the laver, as something well known. There is no satisfactory evidence for the meaning washing. The allusion is to baptism. Some find a reference to the bride 's bath before marriage. </p>
<p>By the word [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ρηματι] </span></span>. Rev., correctly, with the word. To be connected with having cleansed it by the laver of water : not with might sanctify, nor with the laver of water alone, as a descriptive epithet. With the word describes that which accompanies the rite and which is the peculiar element of baptismal purification. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:3">John 14:3</span>. Augustine says : "Take away the word, and what is the water but water ?"</p>
