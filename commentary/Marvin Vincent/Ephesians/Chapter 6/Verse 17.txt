<p>Take the helmet of salvation [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την περικεφαλαιαν του σωτηριου δεξασθε] </span></span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+59:17">Isaiah 59:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:8">1 Thessalonians 5:8</span>. Take is a different word from that used in vers. 13, 16. It is receive as from God. The meaning is the helmet which is salvation. The protection for the head. The helmet was originally of skin, strengthened with bronze or other metal, and surmounted with a figure adorned with a horsehair crest. It was furnished with a visor to protect the face. </p>
<p>Sword of the Spirit [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαχαιραν του πνευματος] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+6:4">Revelation 6:4</span>. The word of God serves both for attack and to parry the thrusts of the enemy. Thus Christ used it in His temptation. It is the sword of the Spirit, because the Spirit of God gives it and inspires it. The Spirit's aid is needed for its interpretation. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:10">John 14:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:12">Hebrews 4:12</span>, in which latter passage the image is sacrificial. </p>
<p>Word of God [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρημα θεου] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:37">Luke 1:37</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:2">Luke 3:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:4">Luke 4:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:17">Romans 10:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:5">Hebrews 6:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:3">Hebrews 11:3</span>.</p>
