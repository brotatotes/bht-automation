<p>I certify [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γνωριζω] </span></span>. Or, I make known. Certify, even in older English, is to assure or attest, which is too strong for gnwrizein to make known or declare. This, which in the New Testament is the universal meaning of gnwrizein, and the prevailing sense in LXX, is extremely rare in Class., where the usual sense is to become acquainted with. For the formula see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:13">1 Thessalonians 4:13</span>. </p>
<p>After man [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα ανθρωπον] </span></span>. According to any human standard. The phrase only in Paul. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:5">Romans 3:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:3">1 Corinthians 3:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:8">1 Corinthians 9:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:32">1 Corinthians 14:32</span>. Kata ajnqrwpouv according to men, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:6">1 Peter 4:6</span>.</p>
