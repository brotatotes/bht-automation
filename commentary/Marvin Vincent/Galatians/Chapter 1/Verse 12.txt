<p>Of man [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα ανθρωπου] </span></span>. Better, from man. Para from emphasises the idea of transmission, and marks the connection between giver and receiver. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:13">1 Thessalonians 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:1">1 Thessalonians 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:14">2 Timothy 3:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:22">Acts 10:22</span>. In the Gospels and Acts paralambanein usually means to take, in the sense of causing to accompany, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:5">Matthew 4:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:1">Matthew 17:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:36">Mark 4:36</span>, etc. Scarcely ever in the sense of receive : see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:4">Mark 7:4</span>. In Paul only in the sense of receive, and only with para, with the single exception of <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:23">1 Corinthians 11:23</span> [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο] </span></span>. The simple lambanw usually with para, but with ajpo, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:27">1 John 2:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:22">1 John 3:22</span>. </p>
<p>By the revelation of Jesus Christ [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι αποκαλυψεως ιησου χριστου] </span></span>. Not, by Jesus Christ being revealed to me, but, I received the gospel by Jesus Christ 's revealing it to me. The subject of the revelation is the gospel, not Christ. Christ was the revealer. Rev. (it came to me) through revelation of Jesus Christ.</p>
