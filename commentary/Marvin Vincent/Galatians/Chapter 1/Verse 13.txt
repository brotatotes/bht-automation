<p>Conversation [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναστροφην] </span></span>. Better, manner of life. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:15">1 Peter 1:15</span>. </p>
<p>In the Jews ' religion [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω ιουδαισμω] </span></span>. Only here and verse 14. Lit. in Judaism. It signifies his national religious condition. In LXX, 2 Macc. 2 21; 8 2; 14 38; 4 Macc. 4 26. </p>
<p>Beyond measure [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθ υπερβολην] </span></span>. P?. Lit. according to excess. The noun primarily means a casting beyond, thence superiority, excellency. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:7">2 Corinthians 4:7</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:17">2 Corinthians 4:17</span>. It is transliterated in hyperbole. For similar phrases comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:1">1 Corinthians 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:20">Acts 19:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:17">Acts 3:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:23">Acts 25:23</span>. </p>
<p>Wasted [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επορθουν] </span></span>. Better, laid waste. In Class. applied not only to things - cities, walls, fields, etc. - but also to persons. So <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:21">Acts 9:21</span>.</p>
