<p>Gave himself for our sins. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:28">Matthew 20:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:25">Ephesians 5:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:6">1 Timothy 2:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:14">Titus 2:14</span>. Purposely added with reference to the Galatians' falling back on the works of the law as the ground of acceptance with God. For or with reference to sins [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περι</span></span>] expresses the general relation of Christ 's mission to sin. The special relation, to atone for, to destroy, to save and sanctify its victims, is expressed by uJper on behalf of. The general preposition, however, may include the special Out of this present evil world [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ του αιωνος του ενεστωτος πονηρου] </span></span>. Lit. out of the world, the present (world which is) evil. For aijwn age or period, see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:9">John 1:9</span>, and additional note on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:9">2 Thessalonians 1:9</span>. Here it has an ethical sense, the course and current of this world 's affairs as corrupted by sin. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:4">2 Corinthians 4:4</span>. Enestwtov, present, as contrasted with the world to come. Elsewhere we have oJ nun aijwn the now world (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:17">1 Timothy 6:17</span>); oJ aijwn toukosmou the period of this world (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:2">Ephesians 2:2</span>); oJ aijwn outov= this world or age (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:2">Romans 7:2</span>). Enestwtov, not impending, as some expositors, - the period of wickedness and suffering preceding the parousia (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:3">2 Thessalonians 2:3</span>), which would imply a limitation of Christ 's atoning work to that period. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:2">2 Thessalonians 2:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:1">2 Timothy 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:26">1 Corinthians 7:26</span>. The sense of present as related to future is clear in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:38">Romans 8:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:22">1 Corinthians 3:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:9">Hebrews 9:9</span>. For the evil character of the present world as conceived by Paul, see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:2">Romans 12:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:6">1 Corinthians 2:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:4">2 Corinthians 4:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:2">Ephesians 2:2</span>.</p>
