<p>Forever and ever [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τους αιωνας των αιωνων] </span></span>. Lit. unto the ages of the ages. See additional note on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:9">2 Thessalonians 1:9</span>, and comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:27">Romans 16:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+4:20">Philippians 4:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:17">1 Timothy 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:18">2 Timothy 4:18</span>. Often in Revelation. In LXX habitually in the singular : see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+88:29">Psalms 88:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+110:3">Psalms 110:3</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+110:30">Psalms 110:30</span>. In the doxology the whole period of duration is conceived as a succession of cycles.</p>
