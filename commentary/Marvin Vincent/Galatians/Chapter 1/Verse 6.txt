<p>I marvel [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θαυμαζω] </span></span>. Often by Greek orators of surprise as something reprehensible. So in New Testament <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:6">Mark 6:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:21">John 7:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:38">Luke 11:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:27">John 4:27</span>. </p>
<p>So soon [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτως ταχεως] </span></span>. Better, so quickly. Paul does not mean so soon after a particular event, as their conversion, or his last visit, or the entry of the false teachers, - but refers to the rapidity of their apostasy; tacewv being used absolutely as always. </p>
<p>Removed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετατιθεσθε] </span></span>. A. V. misses the sense of the middle voice, removing or transferring yourselves, and also the force of the continuous present, are removing or going over, indicating an apostasy not consummated but in progress. The verb is used in Class. of altering a treaty, changing an opinion, desertion from an army. For other applications see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:16">Acts 7:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:12">Hebrews 7:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:5">Hebrews 11:5</span>. Comp. LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+27:17">Deuteronomy 27:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="pr+23:10">Proverbs 23:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+29:17">Isaiah 29:17</span>. Lightfoot renders are turning renegades. </p>
<p>Him that called [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του καλεσαντος] </span></span>. God. Not neuter and referring to the gospel. Calling, in the writings of the apostles, is habitually represented as God 's work. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:30">Romans 8:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:11">Romans 9:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:9">1 Corinthians 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:15">Galatians 1:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:12">1 Thessalonians 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:15">1 Peter 1:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:9">1 Peter 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:3">2 Peter 1:3</span>. </p>
<p>Into the grace [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν χαριτι] </span></span>. Into is wrong. It should be by. </p>
<p>Another gospel [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετερον] </span></span>. Rather a different, another sort of gospel. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:24">Matthew 6:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:7">Luke 16:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:10">Luke 18:10</span>. In illustration of the differences between allov another and eterov different, see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:8-10">1 Corinthians 12:8-10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:40">1 Corinthians 14:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:4">2 Corinthians 11:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:23">Romans 8:23</span>.</p>
