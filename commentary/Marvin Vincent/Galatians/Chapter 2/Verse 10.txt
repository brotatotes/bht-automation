<p>Only. With only this stipulation. </p>
<p>We should remember [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μνημονευωμεν] </span></span>. The only instance in N. T. of this verb in the sense of beneficent care. No instance in LXX In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+9:12">Psalms 9:12</span>, there is the thought but not the word. </p>
<p>The poor [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των πτωχων] </span></span>. The poor Christians of Palestine. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:17">Acts 24:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:26">Romans 14:26</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:27">Romans 14:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:3">1 Corinthians 16:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:1">2 Corinthians 9:1</span>. For the word, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:3">Matthew 5:3</span>. In LXX ordinarily of those who are oppressors, or of those who are quiet in contrast with the lawless. </p>
<p>The same which [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο - αυτο τουτο] </span></span>. Lit. which, this very thing. The expression is peculiarly emphatic, and brings out the contrast between Judaising hostility and Paul 's spirit of loving zeal. Rev. which very thing.</p>
