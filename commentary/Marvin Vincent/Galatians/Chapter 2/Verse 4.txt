<p>The false brethren [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους ψευδαδελφους] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:26">2 Corinthians 11:26</span>. Christians in name only; Judaisers; anti - Paulinists. The article marks them as a well known class. </p>
<p>Unawares brought in [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεισακτους] </span></span>. N. T. o. Lit. brought in by the side, and so insidiously, illegally. Vulg. subintroductos. o 70 Strabo (xvii. 1) uses it as an epithet of Ptolemy, "the sneak." Comp. pareisaxousin shall privily bring in, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:1">2 Peter 2:1</span>; and pareiseduhsan crept in privily, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:4">Jude 1:4</span>. Brought in, not from Jerusalem into the church at Antioch, nor into the Pauline churches generally, but into the Christian brotherhood to which they did not rightfully belong. </p>
<p>Who [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιτινες] </span></span>. The double relative introduces the explanation of the two preceding epithets : false brethren, privily brought in, since they came in privily to spy out our liberty. </p>
<p>Came in privily [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεισηλθον] </span></span>. Lit. came in beside. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:20">Romans 5:20</span>, where it implies nothing evil or secret, but merely something subsidiary. The aorist has a pluperfect sense, indication the earlier intrusion of these persons into the Christian community. </p>
<p>To spy out [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατασκοπησαι] </span></span>. N. T. o. In LXX, of spying out a territory, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2sa+10:3">2 Samuel 10:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ch+19:3">1 Chronicles 19:3</span>. </p>
<p>Liberty [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελευθεριαν] </span></span>. Freedom from Mosaism through justification by faith. </p>
<p>Bring us into bondage [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταδουλωσουσιν] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:20">2 Corinthians 11:20</span>. Bring us into subjection to Jewish ordinances. The compound verb indicates abject subjection.</p>
