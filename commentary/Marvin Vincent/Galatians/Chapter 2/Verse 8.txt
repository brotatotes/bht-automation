<p>He that wrought effectually [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ενεργησας] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:13">1 Thessalonians 2:13</span>. Rev. omits effectually, but it is fairly implied in the verb. 48 Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:6">1 Corinthians 12:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:13">Philippians 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:29">Colossians 1:29</span>. The reference is to God, not to Christ. </p>
<p>In Peter [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πετρω] </span></span>. Better, for Peter. In Peter would be ejn Petrw. </p>
<p>Unto the apostleship [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις] </span></span>. Not merely with reference to the apostleship, but with the design of making him an apostle. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:12">2 Corinthians 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:29">Colossians 1:29</span>. Observe how Paul puts himself on an equality with Peter. </p>
<p>Unto the Gentiles [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις τα εθνη] </span></span>. To make me an apostle to the Gentiles.</p>
