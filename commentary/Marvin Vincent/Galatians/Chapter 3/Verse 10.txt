<p>Under the curse (uJpo kataran). Better, under curse. There is no article. The phrase is general = accursed. Comp. uJf' aJmartian under sin, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:9">Romans 3:9</span>. The specific character of the curse is not stated. It is not merely the wrath of God as it issues in final destruction (Meyer); but it represents a condition of alienation from God, caused by violation of his law, with all the penalty which accrues from it, either in this life or the next. </p>
<p>Cursed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επικαταρατος] </span></span>. Only here and verse 13. o Class. In LXX, see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+3:14">Genesis 3:14</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+3:17">Genesis 3:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+27:16-20">Deuteronomy 27:16-20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+65:20">Isaiah 65:20</span>; Wisd. 3 12; 14 8, etc. </p>
<p>Continueth - in [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμμενει] </span></span>. The expression is figurative, the book of the law being conceived as a prescribed district or domain, in which one remains or out of which he goes. Comp. continue in the faith, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:22">Acts 14:22</span>; in the covenant, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:9">Hebrews 13:9</span>; in the things which thou hast learned, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:14">2 Timothy 3:14</span>.</p>
