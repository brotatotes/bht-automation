<p>After the manner of men [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα ανθρωπον] </span></span>. According to human analogy; reasoning as men would reason in ordinary affairs. The phrase is peculiar to Paul. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:5">Romans 3:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:3">1 Corinthians 3:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:8">1 Corinthians 9:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:32">1 Corinthians 14:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:11">Galatians 1:11</span>. Comp. ajnqrwpinov as a man, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:19">Romans 6:19</span>. </p>
<p>Though it be - yet. The A. V. and Rev. give the correct sense, but the order of the Greek is peculiar. %Omwv yet properly belongs to ouJudeiv no man : "Though a man's covenant yet no man disannulleth it." But omwv is taken out of its natural place, and put at the beginning of the clause, before ajnqrwpou, so that the Greek literally reads : "Yet a man's covenant confirmed no one disannulleth, etc." A similar displacement occurs <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:7">1 Corinthians 14:7</span>. </p>
<p>Covenant [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαθηκην] </span></span>. Not testament. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:28">Matthew 26:28</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:16">Hebrews 9:16</span>. </p>
<p>Confirmed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κεκυρωμενην] </span></span>. P o. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:8">2 Corinthians 2:8</span>. In LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+23:20">Genesis 23:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+25:30">Leviticus 25:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+4:0">Leviticus 4:0</span> Macc. 7 9. From kurov supreme power. Hence the verb carries the sense of authoritative confirmation, in this case by the contracting parties. </p>
<p>Disannulleth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αθετει] </span></span>. See on bring to nothing, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:19">1 Corinthians 1:19</span>. Rev. maketh void. </p>
<p>Addeth thereto [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιδιατασσεται] </span></span>. N. T. o. Adds new specifications or conditions to the original covenant, which is contrary to law. Comp. ejpidiaqhkh a second will or codicil, Joseph B. J. 2 2, 3; Ant 17:9, 4. The doctrine of the Judaisers, while virtually annulling the promise, was apparently only the imposing of new conditions. In either case it was a violation of the covenant.</p>
