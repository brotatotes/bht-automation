<p>But the office of the law as a jailer was designed to be only temporary, until the time when faith should come. It was to hold in custody those who were subjected to sin, so that they should not escape the consciousness of their sins and of their liability to punishment. Faith [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την πιστιν] </span></span>. The subjective faith in Christ which appropriates the promise. See on chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:23">Galatians 1:23</span>. </p>
<p>We were kept [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφρουρουμεθα] </span></span>. Better, kept in ward, continuing the figure in shut up, verse 22. The imperfect tense indicates the continued activity of the law as a warder. </p>
<p>Under the law [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο νομον] </span></span>. Const. with were kept in ward, not with shut up. We were shut up with the law as a warder, not for protection, but to guard against escape. Comp. Wisd. 17 15. The figure of the law as pedagogue (verse 24) is not anticipated. The law is conceived, not as the prison, but as the warder, the Lord or despot, the power of sin (see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:56">1 Corinthians 14:56</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:0">Romans 7:0</span>), by whom those who belong to sin are kept under lock and key - under moral captivity, without possibility of liberation except through faith. </p>
<p>Shut up unto the faith [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνκλειομενοι εις την πιστιν] </span></span>. Eijv unto or for expresses the object of keeping in ward. It is not temporal, until, which is a rare usage in N. T., but with a view to our passing into the state of faith. Which should afterwards be revealed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μελλουσαν - αποκαλυφθηναι] </span></span>. The position of mellousan emphasizes the future state of things to which the earlier conditions pointed. The faith was first revealed at the coming of Christ and the gospel.</p>
