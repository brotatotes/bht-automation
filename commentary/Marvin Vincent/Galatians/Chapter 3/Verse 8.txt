<p>The scripture [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η γραφη] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:18">1 Timothy 5:18</span>. The particular passage cited below. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:10">Mark 12:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:5">2 John 1:5</span>:47 footnote. Foreseeing [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προιδουσα] </span></span>. The passage of Scripture is personified. Comp. hath concluded, verse 22. The Jews had a formula of reference, "What did the Scripture see?" Would justify [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιοι] </span></span>. Better justifieth. The present tense. The time foreseen was the Christian present. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:13">1 Corinthians 3:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:2">Matthew 26:2</span>. </p>
<p>Preached before the gospel [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προευηγγελισατο] </span></span>. N. T. o. An awkward translation. Better, preached the gospel before - hand. </p>
<p>All nations [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παντα τα εθνη] </span></span>. From <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+18:18">Genesis 18:18</span>; comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+22:18">Genesis 22:18</span>, LXX <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+12:3">Genesis 12:3</span> reads pasai aiJ fulai all the tribes. Ta eqnh was the collective term by which all non - Jews were denoted, and is more suitable to Paul 's Gentile audience. </p>
<p>Shall be blessed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενευλογηθησονται] </span></span>. In N. T. only here. LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+12:3">Genesis 12:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+18:18">Genesis 18:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+22:18">Genesis 22:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+26:4">Genesis 26:4</span>; Sir 64 21. The blessing is the messianic blessing of which the Gentiles are to partake - the imparting of the Spirit as the new life principle and the pledge of future blessedness in Christ. This blessing Abraham shared on the ground of his faith, and believers shall share it as the true spiritual children of Abraham. </p>
<p>In thee [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν σοι] </span></span>. Not, through thy posterity, Christ, but in the fact that thou art blessed is involved the blessedness of the Gentiles through faith, in so far as they shall be justified by faith, and through justification receive the Holy Spirit.</p>
