<p>Now I say [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγω δε] </span></span>. Introducing a continued, explanatory discussion. Comp. chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:17">Galatians 3:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:16">Galatians 5:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:12">1 Corinthians 1:12</span>. </p>
<p>The heir [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο κληρονομος] </span></span>. See on inheritance, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:4">1 Peter 1:4</span>. The article is generic as in the mediator, chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:20">Galatians 3:20</span>. </p>
<p>A child [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νηπιος] </span></span>. A minor. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:1">1 Corinthians 3:1</span>. Used by Paul in contrast with teleiov full grown. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:13">Ephesians 4:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:20">1 Corinthians 14:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+3:15">Philippians 3:15</span>. The Jews called proselytes or novices babes. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:20">Romans 2:20</span>. </p>
<p>Lord of all. Legally, by right of birth, though not actually.</p>
