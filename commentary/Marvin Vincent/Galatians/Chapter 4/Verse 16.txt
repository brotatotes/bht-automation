Therefore [ωστε] . Better, so then : seeing that your love for me has waned. Your enemy [εχθρος υμων] . 'Ecqrov enemy, in an active sense, as is shown by the next clause. Not passive, an object of hatred, which would have the pronoun in the dative. Because I tell you the truth [αληθευων υμιν] . 'Alhqeuein, only here and Ephesians 4:15, means to speak the truth or to deal truly. The present participle refers to the same time as gegona I am become, the time of his second visit. The clause is usually construed as interrogative (A. V.). It is rather a direct statement with a slight interrogative suggestion. "So then, I am become your enemy, am I"
