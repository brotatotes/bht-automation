<p>Therefore [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωστε] </span></span>. Better, so then : seeing that your love for me has waned. </p>
<p>Your enemy [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχθρος υμων] </span></span>. 'Ecqrov enemy, in an active sense, as is shown by the next clause. Not passive, an object of hatred, which would have the pronoun in the dative. </p>
<p>Because I tell you the truth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αληθευων υμιν] </span></span>. 'Alhqeuein, only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:15">Ephesians 4:15</span>, means to speak the truth or to deal truly. The present participle refers to the same time as gegona I am become, the time of his second visit. The clause is usually construed as interrogative (A. V.). It is rather a direct statement with a slight interrogative suggestion. "So then, I am become your enemy, am I"</p>
