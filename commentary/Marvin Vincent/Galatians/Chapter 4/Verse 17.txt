<p>They zealously affect you (zhlousin uJmav). They are zealously paying you court in order to win you over to their side. Affect, in this sense, is obsolete. It is from affectare, to strive after, earnestly desire. So Shaks. Tam. of Shr. 1 1 40 : </p>
<p>"In brief, sir, study what you most affect." Ben Johnson, Alchem. 3 2 : </p>
<p>"Pray him aloud to name what dish he affects." As a noun, desire. So Chaucer, Troil. and Cress. 3 1391 : "As Crassus dide for his affectis wronge" (his wrong desires). Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:31">1 Corinthians 12:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:1">1 Corinthians 14:1</span>. </p>
<p>Not well [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου καλως] </span></span>. Not in an honorable way. </p>
<p>Nay [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλα] </span></span>. So far from dealing honorably. </p>
<p>They would exclude you [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκκλεισαι υμας θελουσιν] </span></span>. From other teachers who do not belong to their party - those of anti - Judaising views who formed the sounder part of the church. </p>
<p>That ye might affect them [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα αυτους ζηλουτε] </span></span>. So that in your isolation from others, you might be led to seek affiliation with them.</p>
