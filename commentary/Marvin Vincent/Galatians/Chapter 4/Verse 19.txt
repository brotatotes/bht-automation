<p>I travail in birth again [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παλιν ωδινω] </span></span>. Better as Rev. of whom I am again in travail. Wdinw only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+12:2">Revelation 12:2</span>.<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:27">Galatians 4:27</span> is a quotation. The metaphorical use of the word is frequent in O. T. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+7:14">Psalms 7:14</span>; Sir. 19 11; 31 5; 63 17; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mic+4:10">Micah 4:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+26:18">Isaiah 26:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+66:8">Isaiah 66:8</span>. Paul means that he is for the second time laboring and distressed for the Galatian converts, with the same anguish which attended his first efforts for their conversion. The metaphor of begetting children in the gospel is found in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:15">1 Corinthians 4:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:10">Philippians 1:10</span>. It was a Jewish saying : "If one teaches the son of his neighbor the law, the Scripture reckons this the same as though he had begotten him." </p>
<p>Until Christ be formed in you [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεχρις ου μορφωθη χριστος εν υμιν] </span></span>. The forming of Christ in them, their attainment of the complete inner life of Christians, is the object of the new birth. By their relapse they have retarded this result and renewed Paul 's spiritual travail. The verb morfoun N. T. o. The idea under different aspects is common.
