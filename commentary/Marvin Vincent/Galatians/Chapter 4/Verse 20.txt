<p>I desire [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηθελον] </span></span>. Better, I could wish, the imperfect tense referring to a suppressed conditional clause, as if it were possible. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:22">Acts 25:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:3">Romans 9:3</span>. </p>
<p>To change my voice [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλαξαι την φωνην μου] </span></span>. To address you, not with my former severity, so as to make you think me your enemy, but affectionately, as a mother speaks to her children, yet still telling them the truth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αληθευων] </span></span>. </p>
<p>I stand in doubt of you [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απορουμαι εν υμιν] </span></span>. Lit. I am perplexed in you. For this use of ejn, comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:16">2 Corinthians 7:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:24">Galatians 1:24</span>. Paul 's perplexity is conceived as taking place in the readers. For the verb, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:20">Mark 6:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:8">2 Corinthians 4:8</span>. Paul means : " I am puzzled how to deal with you; how to find entrance to your hearts. </p>
<p>21 - 31. Paul now defends the principle of Christian freedom from the law by means of an allegorical interpretation of the history of Abraham 's two sons. He meets the Jusaisers on their own Old Testament ground, going back to the statement of chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:7">Galatians 3:7</span>.</p>
