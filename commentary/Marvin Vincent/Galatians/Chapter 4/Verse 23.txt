<p>Was born [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γεγεννηται] </span></span>. Has been born, or is born : perfect tense, treating the historical fact as if present. </p>
<p>After the flesh [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα σαρκα] </span></span>. According to the regular course of nature. Very common in Paul. </p>
<p>By promise [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι επαγγελιας] </span></span>. Most editors retain the article, the promise of <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+17:16">Genesis 17:16</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+17:19">Genesis 17:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+18:10">Genesis 18:10</span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:9">Romans 9:9</span>. In virtue of the promise; for according to natural conditions he would not have been born.</p>
