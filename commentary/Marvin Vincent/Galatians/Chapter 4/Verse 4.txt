<p>Fullness of the time [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το πληρωμα του χρονου] </span></span>. The moment by which the whole pre - messianic period was completed. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:10">Ephesians 1:10</span>. It answers to the time appointed of the Father (verse 2). For plhrwma see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:16">John 1:16</span>. The meaning of the word is habitually passive - that which is completed, full complement. There are frequent instances of its use with the genitive, as "fullness of the earth, blessing, time, the sea, Christ," in all which it denotes the plenitude or completeness which characterizes the nouns. 69 Sent forth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαπεστειλεν] </span></span>. From himself : from his heavenly glory. This does not mean that God then, for the first time, embodied what had previously been a mere ideal, but that he sent forth a preexisting person. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:6">Philippians 2:6</span>. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:7">Philippians 2:7</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:0">Philippians 2:0</span> Made of a woman [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενομενον] </span></span>. Or born. Repeated, and expressing the fact that Christ became a man, as distinguished from his prehistoric form of being. </p>
<p>Under the law. The earthly being of Christ began under the law. He was not only of human birth, but of Jewish birth; subjected to all the ordinances of the law, as circumcision for instance, like any other Jewish boy.</p>
