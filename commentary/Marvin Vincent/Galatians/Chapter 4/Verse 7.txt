<p>Servant [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δουλος] </span></span>. Bondservant. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:26">Matthew 20:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:35">Mark 9:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:1">Romans 1:1</span>. </p>
<p>Then an heir [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">και κληρονομος] </span></span>. Kai marks the logical sequence. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:17">Romans 8:17</span>. The figure is based upon Roman, not upon Jewish, law. According to Roman law, all the children, sons and daughters, inherited alike. According to Jewish law, the inheritance of the sons was unequal, and the daughters were excluded, except where there were no male heirs. Thus the Roman law furnished a more truthful illustration of the privileges of Christians. Comp. chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:28">Galatians 3:28</span>. </p>
<p>Of God through Christ. The correct reading is dia qeou through God, omitting Christ.</p>
