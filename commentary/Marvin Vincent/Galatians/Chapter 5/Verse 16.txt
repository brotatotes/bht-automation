<p>Walk [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιπατειτε] </span></span>. Frequent in a metaphorical sense for habitual conduct. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:5">Mark 7:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:12">John 8:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:21">Acts 21:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:4">Romans 6:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:4">Romans 8:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:3">1 Corinthians 3:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+3:18">Philippians 3:18</span>. Never by Paul in the literal sense. In the Spirit [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευματι] </span></span>. Rather, by the Spirit, as the rule of action. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:16">Galatians 6:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+3:16">Philippians 3:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:12">Romans 4:12</span>. </p>
<p>Fulfill [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελεσητε] </span></span>. Bring to fulfillment in action. See on do the law, ver. 3. </p>
<p>The lust [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιθυμιαν] </span></span>. Frequent in Paul, and usually in a bad sense; but see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:23">Philippians 1:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:17">1 Thessalonians 2:17</span>, and comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:15">Luke 22:15</span>. The phrase lust or lusts of the flesh occurs also <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:3">Ephesians 2:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:18">2 Peter 2:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:16">1 John 2:16</span>. It means, not the mere sensual desire of the physical nature, but the desire which is peculiar to human nature without the divine Spirit.</p>
