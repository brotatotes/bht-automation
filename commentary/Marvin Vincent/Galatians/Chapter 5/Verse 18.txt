<p>The question is, which of these two powers shall prevail. If the Spirit, then you are free men, no longer under the law. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:11">Romans 6:11</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:14">Romans 6:14</span>. Under the law [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπο νομον] </span></span>. The Mosaic law. We might have expected, from what precedes, under the flesh. But the law and the flesh are in the same category. Circumcision was a requirement of the law, and was a work of the flesh. The ordinances of the law were ordinances of the flesh (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:10">Hebrews 9:10</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:13">Hebrews 9:13</span>); the law was weak through the flesh (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:3">Romans 8:3</span>). See especially, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:2-6">Galatians 3:2-6</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+3:3">Philippians 3:3</span> ff. Paul explains his grounds for confidence in the flesh as his legal righteousness. The whole legal economy was an economy of the flesh as distinguished from the Spirit.</p>
