<p>Witchcraft [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φαρμακια] </span></span>. Or sorcery. Elsewhere only <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+18:23">Revelation 18:23</span>. From farmakon a drug. In LXX, see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+7:11">Exodus 7:11</span>; Wisd. 12 4; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+47:9">Isaiah 47:9</span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:19">Acts 19:19</span>, perierga curious arts, note. </p>
<p>Wrath [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυμοι] </span></span>. Lit. wraths. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:36">John 3:36</span>. </p>
<p>Strife [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εριθιαι] </span></span>. More correctly, factions. From eriqov a hired servant. Eriqia is, primarily, labor for hire (see Tob. 2 11), and is applied to those who serve in official positions for hire or for other selfish purposes,; and, in order to gain their ends, promote party spirit or faction. Seditions [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διχοστασιαι] </span></span>. Better, divisions. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:17">Romans 16:17</span>. Once in LXX, 1 Macc. 3 29. </p>
<p>Heresies [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιρεσεις] </span></span>. In Paul only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:19">1 Corinthians 11:19</span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:1">2 Peter 2:1</span>. Parties, into which divisions crystallize.</p>
