<p>Meekness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πραυτης] </span></span>. See on meek, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:5">Matthew 5:5</span>. </p>
<p>Temperance [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγκρατεια] </span></span>. Only here by Paul. He alone uses ejgkrateuesqai to have continency, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:9">1 Corinthians 7:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:25">1 Corinthians 9:25</span>. See on is temperate, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:0">1 Corinthians 9:0</span>; 25. The word means self - control, holding in hand the passions and desires. So Xen. Mem 1 2, 1, of Socrates, who was ejgkratestatov most temperate as to sexual pleasures and pleasures of the appetite. </p>
<p>Such [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοιουτων] </span></span>. Such things, not persons. </p>
<p>There is no law [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ εστιν νομος] </span></span>. Against such virtues there is no law to condemn them. The law can bring no charge against them. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:9">1 Timothy 1:9</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:10">1 Timothy 1:10</span>.</p>
