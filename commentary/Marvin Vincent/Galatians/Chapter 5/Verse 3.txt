<p>Again [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παλιν] </span></span>. Probably with reference to what he had said at his last visit. </p>
<p>Every man. Emphasizing and particularising the general to you, you, in verse 2. </p>
<p>A debtor [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οφειλετης] </span></span>. In N. T. mostly of one under moral obligation. So in the sense of sinner, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:12">Matthew 6:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:4">Luke 13:4</span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:14">Romans 1:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:12">Romans 8:12</span>. Similarly the verb ojfeilein to owe, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:4">Luke 11:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:10">Luke 17:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:1">Romans 14:1</span>, etc., though it is frequent in the literal sense. </p>
<p>To do the law [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιησαι] </span></span>. Rare in N. T. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:19">John 7:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:13">Romans 2:13</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:25">Romans 2:25</span> [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρασσης] </span></span>. Threin to observe the law, the tradition, the commandment, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:17">Matthew 19:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:9">Mark 7:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:15">John 14:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:5">Acts 14:5</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:10">James 2:10</span> : plhroun to fulfill the law, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:8">Romans 13:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:14">Galatians 5:14</span>; comp. ajnaplhroun <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:2">Galatians 6:2</span> : fulassein to keep or guard the law, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:53">Acts 7:53</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:24">Acts 21:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:13">Galatians 6:13</span> : also with commandments, word of God or of Christ, ordinances of the law, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:20">Matthew 19:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:20">Mark 10:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:28">Luke 11:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:47">John 12:47</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:26">Romans 2:26</span>. Telein to carry out the law, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:27">Romans 2:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:8">James 2:8</span>. Poihsai is to perform what the law commands : threin to observe, keep an eye on with the result of performing : fulassein to guard against violation : telein to bring to fulfillment in action. </p>
<p>The whole law [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ολον] </span></span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:10">James 2:10</span>. Submission to circumcision commits one to the whole law. It makes him a party to the covenant of the law, and the law requires of every one thus committed a perfect fulfillment, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:10">Galatians 3:10</span>.</p>
