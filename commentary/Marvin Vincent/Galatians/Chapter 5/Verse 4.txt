<p>Christ is become of no effect unto you [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηργηθητε απο χριστου] </span></span>. Incorrect. Lit. ye were brought to nought from Christ. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:2">Romans 7:2</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:6">Romans 7:6</span>. Your union with Christ is dissolved. The statement is compressed and requires to be filled out. "Ye were brought to nought and so separated from Christ." For similar instances see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:3">Romans 9:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:3">Romans 11:3</span>. The ajpo from properly belongs to the supplied verb of separation. For the verb katargein see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:3">Romans 3:3</span>. </p>
<p>Ye are fallen from grace [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της χαριτος εξεπεσατε] </span></span>. For a similar phrase see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:17">2 Peter 3:17</span>. Having put yourselves under the economy of salvation by law, you have fallen out of the economy of salvation by the grace of Christ. Paul 's declarations are aimed at the Judaisers, who taught that the Christian economy was to be joined with the legal. His point is that the two are mutually exclusive. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:4">Romans 4:4</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:5">Romans 4:5</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:14">Romans 4:14</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:16">Romans 4:16</span>. The verb ejkpiptein to fall out, in the literal sense, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:7">Acts 12:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:11">James 1:11</span>. In Class. of seamen thrown ashore, banishment, deprivation of an office, degeneration, of actors being hissed off the stage.</p>
