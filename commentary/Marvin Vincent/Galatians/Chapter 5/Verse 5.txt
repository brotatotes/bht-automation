<p>For we [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ημεις γαρ] </span></span>. Gar for introduces a proof of the preceding statement, by declaring the contrary attitude of those who continue under the economy of grace. Ye who seek to be justified by the law are fallen from grace; for we, not relying on the law, by faith wait for the hope of righteousness. </p>
<p>Through the Spirit [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευματι] </span></span>. The Holy Spirit who inspires our faith. Not as Lightfoot, spiritually. The words pneumati ejk pistewv are not to be taken as one conception, the Spirit which is of faith, but present two distinct and coordinate facts which characterize the waiting for the hope of righteousness; namely, the agency of the Holy Spirit, in contrast with the flesh (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:6">Romans 7:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:4">Romans 8:4</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:15">Romans 8:15</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:16">Romans 8:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:13">Ephesians 1:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:22">Ephesians 2:22</span>), and faith in contrast with the works of the law (comp. chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:3">Galatians 3:3</span>, and see chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:16">Galatians 2:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:3">Galatians 3:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:17">Romans 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:22">Romans 3:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:30">Romans 9:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:6">Romans 10:6</span>). </p>
<p>By faith [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ πιστεως] </span></span>. Const. with wait, not with righteousness. </p>
<p>Wait for [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεκδεχομεθα] </span></span>. Quite often in Paul, and only twice elsewhere, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:28">Hebrews 9:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:20">1 Peter 3:20</span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+3:20">Philippians 3:20</span>. </p>
<p>The hope of righteousness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιδα δικαιοσυνης] </span></span>. Hope for the object of hope, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:24">Romans 8:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:5">Colossians 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:18">Hebrews 6:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:13">Titus 2:13</span>. The phrase means that good which righteousness causes us to hope for. Comp. hope of the calling (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:18">Ephesians 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:4">Ephesians 4:4</span>) : hope of the gospel (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:23">Colossians 1:23</span>). 81</p>
