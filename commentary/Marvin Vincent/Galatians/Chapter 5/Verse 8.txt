<p>This persuasion [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η πεισμονη] </span></span>. Or, the persuasion. N. T. o. o LXX, o Class. It occurs in Ignatius, Rom. iii. and Just. Mart. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+1:53">Revelation 1:53</span>. The sense is not passive, your being persuaded, but active, the persuasion which the Judaising teacher s exert over you. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:4">1 Corinthians 1:4</span>, piqoiv logoiv persuasive words. There may be a slight word play on peiqesqai and peismonh. Obedience to the truth is the result of the persuasive power of the truth. </p>
<p>Him that calleth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του καλουντος] </span></span>. Very often applied to God by Paul. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:30">Romans 8:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:11">Romans 9:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:9">1 Corinthians 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:15">1 Corinthians 7:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:15">Galatians 1:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:12">1 Thessalonians 2:12</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:7">1 Thessalonians 4:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:24">1 Thessalonians 5:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:14">2 Thessalonians 2:14</span>. The persuasion to subject yourselves to the Jewish law does not proceed from him who called you to freedom in Christ.</p>
