<p>Overtaken in a fault [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προλημφθη - εν τινι παραπτωματι] </span></span>. The verb means lit. to take before; to anticipate or forestall. Elsewhere only <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:8">Mark 14:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:21">1 Corinthians 11:21</span>. LXX, Wisd. 18 17. Not, be detected in the act by some one else before he can escape, but surprised by the fault itself; hurried into error. Thus prohas the sense of before he is aware, and ejn is instrumental, by. 86 For fault or trespass, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:14">Matthew 6:14</span>. Spiritual [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευματικοι] </span></span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:1">1 Corinthians 3:1</span>. Mostly in Paul. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:5">1 Peter 2:5</span>. Those who have received the Spirit and are led by him. See ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:2">Galatians 3:2</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:3">Galatians 3:3</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:5">Galatians 3:5</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:14">Galatians 3:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:6">Galatians 4:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:5">Galatians 5:5</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:16">Galatians 5:16</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:18">Galatians 5:18</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:25">Galatians 5:25</span>. He leaves it to the readers' own conscience whether or not they answer to this designation. </p>
<p>Restore [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταρτιζετε] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:21">Matthew 4:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:16">Matthew 21:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:40">Luke 6:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:10">1 Peter 5:10</span>. The word is used of reconciling factions, as Hdt 5:28; of setting bones; of mending nets, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:19">Mark 1:19</span>; of equipping or preparing, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:22">Romans 9:22</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:5">Hebrews 10:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:3">Hebrews 11:3</span>; of manning a fleet, or supplying an army with provisions. Usually by Paul metaphorically as here. The idea of amendment is prominent : set him to rights : bring him into line. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+13:11">2 Corinthians 13:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:10">1 Corinthians 1:10</span>. </p>
<p>Spirit of meekness. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:21">1 Corinthians 4:21</span>. Led by the Spirit of God, whose fruit is meekness (v. 23). For the combinations of pneuma with genitives, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:4">Romans 8:4</span>, p. 87. </p>
<p>Considering [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκοπων] </span></span>. Only in Paul, except <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:35">Luke 11:35</span>. The verb means to look attentively; to fix the attention upon a thing with an interest in it. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:17">Romans 16:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:18">2 Corinthians 4:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:4">Philippians 2:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+3:17">Philippians 3:17</span>. Hence, often, to aim at (comp. skopon mark, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+3:14">Philippians 3:14</span>). Schmidt (Syn.) defines : "To direct one's attention upon a thing, either in order to obtain it, or because one has a peculiar interest in it, or a duty to fulfill toward it. Also to have an eye to with a view of forming a right judgment." Notice the passing to the singular number - "considering thyself." The exhortation is addressed to the conscience of each. Before you deal severely with the erring brother, consider your own weakness and susceptibility to temptation, and restore him in view of that fact.</p>
