<p>As we have opportunity [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως καιρον εχωμεν] </span></span>. As there is a proper season for reaping, there is likewise a proper season for sowing. As this season comes to us, let us sow to the Spirit by doing good. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:16">Ephesians 5:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+4:5">Colossians 4:5</span>. </p>
<p>Let us do good [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εργαζωμεθα το αγαθον] </span></span>. Let us work the good. For the distinctive force of ejrgazesqai see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:5">3 John 1:5</span>; and for poiein to do, on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:21">John 3:21</span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+3:23">Colossians 3:23</span> where both verbs occur. To ajgaqon is, of course, the morally good as distinguished from what is merely useful or profitable, but includes what is beneficent or kindly. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:14">Philippians 1:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:28">Ephesians 4:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+3:6">1 Thessalonians 3:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:7">Romans 5:7</span>. Here, in a general sense, embracing all that is specified in vv. 1, 2, 3, 10. </p>
<p>Unto them who are of the household of faith [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς τους οικειους της πιστεως] </span></span>. Prov combines with the sense of direction that of active relation with. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:56">Matthew 13:56</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:16">Mark 9:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:1">John 1:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:25">Acts 3:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:25">Acts 28:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:12">1 Thessalonians 4:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:20">Hebrews 9:20</span>. Frequently in Class. of all kinds of personal intercourse. See Ho<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ob+1:0">Obadiah 1:0</span>. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ob+1:14">Obadiah 1:14</span> 331; 19 288; Thucyd. 2 59; 4 15; 7 82; Hdt 1:61. Oikeioi of the household, rare in N. T. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:19">Ephesians 2:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:8">1 Timothy 5:8</span>. Quite often in LXX of kinsmen. It is unnecessary to introduce the idea of a household here, as A. V., since the word acquired the general sense of pertaining or belonging to. Thus oijkeioi filosofiav or gewgrafiav belonging to philosophy or geography, philosophers, geographers. So here, belonging to the faith, believers.</p>
