<p>Henceforth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του λοιπου] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:10">Ephesians 6:10</span>. Commonly to loipon. The genitive is temporal; at any time in the future as distinguished from throughout the future. </p>
<p>Trouble me [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοπους μοι - παρεχετε] </span></span>. Lit. give me troubles; make it necessary for me to vindicate my apostolic authority and the divine truth of my gospel. </p>
<p>Bear in my body. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:10">2 Corinthians 4:10</span>. </p>
<p>Marks [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στιγματα] </span></span>. N. T. o. The wounds, scars, and other outward signs of persecutions and sufferings in the service of Christ. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:23">2 Corinthians 11:23</span> ff. The metaphor is the brands applied to slaves in order to mark their owners. Hence Rev., I bear branded. Brands were also set upon soldiers, captives, and servants of temples. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+13:16">Revelation 13:16</span>, and comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+7:3">Revelation 7:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+14:1">Revelation 14:1</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+14:9">Revelation 14:9</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+14:11">Revelation 14:11</span>. The scars on the apostle 's body marked him as the bondservant of Jesus Christ. The passage naturally recalls the legend of Francis of Assisi.</p>
