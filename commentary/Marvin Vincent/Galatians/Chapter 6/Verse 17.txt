Henceforth [του λοιπου] . Only here and Ephesians 6:10. Commonly to loipon. The genitive is temporal; at any time in the future as distinguished from throughout the future. Trouble me [κοπους μοι - παρεχετε] . Lit. give me troubles; make it necessary for me to vindicate my apostolic authority and the divine truth of my gospel. Bear in my body. Comp. 2 Corinthians 4:10. Marks [στιγματα] . N. T. o. The wounds, scars, and other outward signs of persecutions and sufferings in the service of Christ. Comp. 2 Corinthians 11:23 ff. The metaphor is the brands applied to slaves in order to mark their owners. Hence Rev., I bear branded. Brands were also set upon soldiers, captives, and servants of temples. See on Revelation 13:16, and comp. Revelation 7:3; Revelation 14:1, Revelation 14:9, Revelation 14:11. The scars on the apostle 's body marked him as the bondservant of Jesus Christ. The passage naturally recalls the legend of Francis of Assisi.
