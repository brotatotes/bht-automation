<p>Prove [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκιμαζετω] </span></span>. In Class. of assaying metals Comp. LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="pr+8:10">Proverbs 8:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="pr+17:3">Proverbs 17:3</span>; Sir. 2 5; also <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:13">1 Corinthians 3:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:7">1 Peter 1:7</span>. It is the classical verb for testing money; see Plato, Tim. 65 100 Dokimazein and purousqai to burn or try by fire occur together, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+9:7">Jeremiah 9:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+11:6">Psalms 11:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+65:10">Psalms 65:10</span>. Generally, to prove or examine, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:28">1 Corinthians 11:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:21">1 Thessalonians 5:21</span>. To accept that which is approved, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:3">1 Corinthians 16:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:22">2 Corinthians 8:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:4">1 Thessalonians 2:4</span>. </p>
<p>Rejoicing [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το καυχημα] </span></span>. Better, as giving the force of the article, "his glorying." Kauchma is the matter or ground of glorying, see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:2">Romans 4:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:15">1 Corinthians 9:15</span>; not the act [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καυχησις] </span></span>, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:27">Romans 3:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:12">2 Corinthians 1:12</span>. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:89">2 Corinthians 1:89</span> In himself [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις εαυτον] </span></span>. Better, with regard to himself, or as concerns. For this use of eijv see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:20">Romans 4:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:2">Romans 14:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:6">Romans 16:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:16">Ephesians 3:16</span>. Not, he will keep his glorying to himself or abstain from boasting. He means that if, on examination, one finds in himself anything to boast of, his cause of boasting will lie simply and absolutely in that, and not in his merit as compared, to his own advantage, with that of another. </p>
<p>Another [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον ετερον] </span></span>. Better, the other, or, as Rev., his neighbor. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:24">Matthew 6:24</span>.</p>
