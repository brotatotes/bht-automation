<p>Bear ye one another's burdens : every man shall bear his own burden. A kind of paradox of which Paul is fond. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:12">Philippians 2:12</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:13">Philippians 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:8-10">2 Corinthians 6:8-10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:10">2 Corinthians 7:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:10">2 Corinthians 12:10</span>. Paul means, no one will have occasion to claim moral superiority to his neighbor, for [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γαρ</span></span>] each man's self - examination will reveal infirmities enough of his own, even though they may not be the same as those of his neighbor. His own burdens will absorb his whole attention, and will leave him no time to compare himself with others. </p>
<p>His own burden [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το ιδιον φορτιον] </span></span>. For idion own, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:1">1 Timothy 6:1</span>. With fortion burden comp. barh burdens, ver. 2. It is doubtful whether any different shade of meaning is intended. Originally barh emphasizes the weight of the burden, fortion simply notes the fact that it is something to be born [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φερειν] </span></span>, which may be either light or heavy. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:30">Matthew 11:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:4">Matthew 23:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+37:4">Psalms 37:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:46">Luke 11:46</span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:10">Acts 27:10</span>, the lading of a ship.</p>
