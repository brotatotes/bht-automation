<p>To his flesh [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις την σαρκα εαυτου] </span></span>. Rather, his own flesh. Eiv into : the flesh being conceived as the soil into which the seed is cast. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:22">Matthew 13:22</span>. His own, because the idea of personal, selfish desire is involved. </p>
<p>Corruption [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φθοραν] </span></span>. Primarily, destruction, ruin; but it also has the sense of deterioration, decay, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:42">1 Corinthians 14:42</span>. Comp. Aristotle, Rhet. 3 3, 4 "And thou didst sow [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εσπειρας</span></span>] shamefully [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αισχρως</span></span>] and didst reap [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εθερισας</span></span>] miserably [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακως] </span></span>." See also Plato, Phaedrus, 260 D, and on defile, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:17">Romans 3:17</span>. </p>
<p>The Spirit. The Holy Spirit : not the higher nature of man. </p>
<p>Eternal life [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζωην αιωνιον] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:9">2 Thessalonians 1:9</span> (additional note).</p>
