<p>Be weary [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενκακωμεν] </span></span>. Lit. faint or lose heart. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:13">2 Thessalonians 3:13</span>. </p>
<p>In due season [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιρω ιδιω] </span></span>. In the season which is peculiarly the harvest - time of each form of well - doing. See on ver. 5. </p>
<p>Faint [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκλυομενοι] </span></span>. Only here in Paul. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:32">Matthew 14:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:3">Mark 8:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:3">Hebrews 12:3</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:5">Hebrews 12:5</span>. Lit. to be loosened or relaxed, like the limbs of the weary.</p>
