<p>Iniquity [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανομιαν] </span></span>. Lit. lawlessness. </p>
<p>Hath anointed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχρισεν] </span></span>. See on Christ, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+1:1">Matthew 1:1</span>. The ideas of the royal and the festive unction are combined. The thought includes the royal anointing and the fullness of blessing and festivity which attend the enthronement. </p>
<p>Oil of gladness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελαιον αγαλλιασεως] </span></span>. The phrase N. T. o. o LXX Agalliasiv exultant joy. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:44">Luke 1:44</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:46">Acts 2:46</span>, and the verb ajgalliasqai, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:12">Matthew 5:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:21">Luke 10:21</span>, etc. The noun only here in Hebrews, and the verb does not occur. </p>
<p>Fellows [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετοχους] </span></span>. With exception of <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:7">Luke 5:7</span>, only in Hebrews. Lit. partakers. In the Psalm it is applied to other kings : here to angels.</p>
