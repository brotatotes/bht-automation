<p>Let us consider one another [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατανοωμεν αλληλους] </span></span>. Take careful note of each other's spiritual welfare. For the verb see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:23">James 1:23</span>. It denotes attentive, continuous care. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:1">Hebrews 3:1</span>. To provoke [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις παροξυσμον] </span></span>. Lit. with a view to incitement. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:39">Acts 14:39</span>. From paroxunein to sharpen. Hence to stimulate. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:39">Acts 14:39</span>, the result of provocation; irritation or contention. Here the act of incitement. Twice in LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+29:28">Deuteronomy 29:28</span>; (27) <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+29:0">Jeremiah 29:0</span> (32.) 3, 7; for the Hebrew qatsaph anger, wrath, altercation. The Hebrew derivation is from qatseph a splinter. The new economy demands mutual care on the part of the members of the Christian community. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:25">1 Corinthians 12:25</span>. They must stir up each other's religious affections and ministries.</p>
