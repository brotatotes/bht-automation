<p>We sin willfully [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκουσιως αμαρτανοντων ημων] </span></span>. JEkousiwv willfully, only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:2">1 Peter 5:2</span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:14">Philippians 1:14</span>, kat' eJkousion of free will. See LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="nu+14:3">Numbers 14:3</span>. The willful sin is the abandonment of Christianity for Judaism. </p>
<p>The knowledge [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιγνωσιν] </span></span>. Only here in Hebrews. Very common in Paul. For the word, and the phrase knowledge of the truth, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:4">1 Timothy 2:4</span>. The truth is the revelation through Christ. </p>
<p>There remaineth no more sacrifice for sins [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκετι περι αμαρτιων απολειπεται θυσια] </span></span>. Of course not. For the Levitical sacrifices are abolished. It is Christ 's sacrifice or none.</p>
