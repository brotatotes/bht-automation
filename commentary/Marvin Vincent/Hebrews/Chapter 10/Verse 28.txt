<p>He that despised [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφετησας τις] </span></span>. Lit. one that despised; any transgressor. The verb only here in Hebrews. The kindred noun ajqethsiv only in Hebrews. See ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:18">Hebrews 7:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:26">Hebrews 9:26</span>. </p>
<p>Died [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποθνησκει] </span></span>. Lit. dieth. According to the ordinance as it now stands in the law. </p>
<p>Without mercy [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χωρις οικτιρμων] </span></span>. The phrase N. T. o. For the noun see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:3">2 Corinthians 1:3</span>. </p>
<p>Under two or three witnesses [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επι δυσιν η τρισιν μαρτυσιν] </span></span>. As in LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+17:6">Deuteronomy 17:6</span>. jEpi with dative signifying on condition of two or three witnesses testifying. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:17">1 Timothy 5:17</span>, where the same phrase occurs with the genitive, before, in the presence of. Comp. also <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+19:15">Deuteronomy 19:15</span>.</p>
