<p>Whilst ye were made a gazing - stock [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεατριζομενοι] </span></span>. N. T. o. o LXX, o Class. Lit. exhibited in the theater. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:9">1 Corinthians 4:9</span>. Whilst ye became companions [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοινωνοι γενηθεντες] </span></span>. Rend. by becoming partakers. More than companionship is implied. For koinwnoi see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:10">Luke 5:10</span>. The noun and its kindred verb in N. T. almost exclusively of ethical and spiritual relations, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:22">1 Timothy 5:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:13">1 Peter 4:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:11">2 John 1:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:18">1 Corinthians 10:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:7">2 Corinthians 1:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:17">Philippians 1:17</span>. Even when applied to pecuniary contributions they imply Christian fellowship as the basis of the liberality. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:13">Romans 12:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:27">Romans 14:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+4:15">Philippians 4:15</span>. </p>
<p>Of them that were so used [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των ουτως αναστρφομενων] </span></span>. Rend. "of them that fared thus." Others render " who conducted themselves thus "; endured their persecutions, so bravely. But the outwv can refer only to made a gazing - stock.</p>
