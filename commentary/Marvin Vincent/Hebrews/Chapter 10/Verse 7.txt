<p>In the volume of the book [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν κεφαλιδι βιβλιου] </span></span>. Kefaliv, N. T. o, is a diminutive, meaning little head. Lat. capitellum or capitulum. The extremity or end, as the capital of a column. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+26:32">Exodus 26:32</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+26:37">Exodus 26:37</span>. Sometimes the column itself, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+40:18">Exodus 40:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="nu+3:36">Numbers 3:36</span>. Said to be used of the tips or knobs of the rollers around which parchments were rolled, but no instances are cited. A roll of parchment, a book - roll, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eze+2:9">Ezekiel 2:9</span>. Meaning here the Scriptures of the O. T. for Hebrew megilla. Kefaliv is found in LXX with bibliou book, only <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eze+2:9">Ezekiel 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+39:7">Psalms 39:7</span>. For, biblion book, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:13">2 Timothy 4:13</span>.</p>
