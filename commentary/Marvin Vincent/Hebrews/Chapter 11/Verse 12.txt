<p>As good as dead [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νενεκρωμενου] </span></span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:19">Romans 4:19</span>. As good as is an addition of A. V. The Greek reads and that a dead man. Comp. nekrwsin deadness applied to Sarah, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:19">Romans 4:19</span>. </p>
<p>Stars - sand. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+22:17">Genesis 22:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+32:12">Genesis 32:12</span>. </p>
<p>By the seashore [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα το χειλος της θαλασσης] </span></span>. Lit. by the lip of the sea. The phrase N. T. o. Very often in LXX, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+22:17">Genesis 22:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+14:30">Exodus 14:30</span> : lip of a river, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+41:17">Genesis 41:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+7:15">Exodus 7:15</span> : of a brook, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+2:36">Deuteronomy 2:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+3:12">Deuteronomy 3:12</span> : of Jordan, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ki+2:13">2 Kings 2:13</span>. So in Class. The vigor thus supernaturally imparted to Abraham does not appear to have exhausted itself in the generation of Isaac; since, according to <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+25:2">Genesis 25:2</span>, Abraham became by Keturah the father of six sons after the death of Sarah.</p>
