<p>Blessed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλογησεν] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:13">John 12:13</span>. </p>
<p>Concerning things to come [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">και περι μελλοντων] </span></span>. A. V. Omits kai which gives an emphasis to the following words. Isaac pronounced a blessing, and that concerning things to come; things beyond the lifetime of Jacob and Esau. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+27:29">Genesis 27:29</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+27:39">Genesis 27:39</span>. The blessing was an act of faith. Isaac 's confidence in the power of his blessing to convey the good which it promised was "the assurance of things hoped for, the conviction of things not seen," founded on the promise of <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+17:5">Genesis 17:5</span>.</p>
