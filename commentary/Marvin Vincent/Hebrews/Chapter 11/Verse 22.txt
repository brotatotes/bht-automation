<p>When he died [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τελευτων] </span></span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+1:26">Genesis 1:26</span>, LXX The verb means to finish or close, with life understood. Always in this sense in N. T. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:19">Matthew 2:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:18">Matthew 9:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:2">Luke 7:2</span>, etc. Never used by Paul. Rend. "when near his end." </p>
<p>Made mention of [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περι - εμνημονευσεν] </span></span>. See on ver. 15. A. V. has remembered in marg. Remembered is appropriate here. Joseph on his death - bed remembered the promise of God to give the land of Canaan to the seed of Abraham (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+12:7">Genesis 12:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+13:15">Genesis 13:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+14:7">Genesis 14:7</span>), and also the prediction to Abraham that his descendants should pass four hundred years in bondage in a strange land, and should afterward be brought out thence, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+14:13">Genesis 14:13</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+14:14">Genesis 14:14</span>. </p>
<p>The departing of the children of Israel [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της εξοδου των υιων ισραηλ] </span></span>. Exodov only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:31">Luke 9:31</span> (note) and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:15">2 Peter 1:15</span> (note). JOi uiJoi jIsrahl is one of several phrases in N. T. denoting the chosen people. There are also house [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οικος</span></span>] and people [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαος</span></span>] of Israel, and Israel of God, and Israel according to the flesh. </p>
<p>And gave commandment [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">και ενετειλατο] </span></span>. Kai and so; in consequence of his remembering the prophecy of the exodus. The verb indicates a specific injunction [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντολη] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:14">1 Timothy 6:14</span>.</p>
