<p>He sojourned in [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρωκησεν εις] </span></span>. The verb lit. to dwell beside or among. Paroikov, a foreigner dwelling in a state without rights of citizenship. In Class. only in the sense of neighbor. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:18">Luke 24:18</span>. The verb of rest with the preposition of motion (only here) signifies that he went into the land and dwelt there. Usually with ejn in, but sometimes with the simple accusative, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:18">Luke 24:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+17:8">Genesis 17:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+6:4">Exodus 6:4</span>. Land of promise [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γην της επαγγελιας] </span></span>. Note the article, omitted in A. V., the promise : the land which was designated in the promise of God. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+12:7">Genesis 12:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+13:15">Genesis 13:15</span>. The phrase N. T. o. There is no corresponding phrase in O. T. </p>
<p>Strange [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλοτριαν] </span></span>. Another [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλη</span></span>] land than his own. So LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+14:13">Genesis 14:13</span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:6">Acts 7:6</span>. </p>
<p>In tabernacles [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν σκηναις] </span></span>. Or tents, as a migratory people, without a permanent home. </p>
<p>The heirs with him [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των συνκληρονομων] </span></span>. Joint - heirs or fellow - heirs. o LXX, o Class. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:17">Romans 8:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:6">Ephesians 3:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:7">1 Peter 3:7</span>. The three, Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, are mentioned because they cover the entire period of the sojourn in Canaan. Faith inspired these to endure patiently their unsettled life, since it assured them of a permanent home in the future.</p>
