<p>Wherefore [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διο] </span></span>. Because chastening is thus necessary, and serves for wholesome discipline, and issues in holiness.. </p>
<p>Lift up [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανορθωσατε] </span></span>. Found in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:13">Luke 13:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:16">Acts 14:16</span> [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χιτν] </span></span>. Occasionally in LXX It signifies to set up, make, erect. In O. T. to establish, as a throne (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2sa+7:13">2 Samuel 7:13</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2sa+7:16">2 Samuel 7:16</span>); a house (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2sa+7:26">2 Samuel 7:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ch+17:24">1 Chronicles 17:24</span>); to raise up one who is down (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+145:9">Psalms 145:9</span>; Sir. 11 12). In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:16">Acts 14:16</span>, to build anew. By medical writers, to straighten; to set dislocated parts of the body. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:13">Luke 13:13</span>. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:23">Luke 13:23</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:8">Luke 13:8</span> The translation here should be more general : not lift up, which is inappropriate to paralyzed knees, but set right; brace. As falling in with the thought of this passage, comp. the LXX of <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+17:35">Psalms 17:35</span>, which, for the A. V. "thy gentleness hath made me great," gives "thy discipline hath established me or set me up." See also <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+19:8">Psalms 19:8</span>. </p>
<p>The hands which hang down [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τας παρειμενας χειρας] </span></span>. Rend. the slackened or weakened hands. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+35:3">Isaiah 35:3</span>; Sir. 25 23; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2sa+4:1">2 Samuel 4:1</span>. The verb parienai (only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:42">Luke 11:42</span>) originally means to let pass, disregard, neglect; thence to relax, loosen. See Clem. Rom. Ad Corinth. 34, who associates it with nwqrov slothful (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:11">Hebrews 5:11</span>). </p>
<p>And the feeble knees [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">και τα παραλελυμενα γονατα] </span></span>. For feeble rend. palsied. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:18">Luke 5:18</span>.</p>
