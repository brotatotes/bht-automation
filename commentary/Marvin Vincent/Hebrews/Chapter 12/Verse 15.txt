<p>Looking diligently [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επισκοπουντες] </span></span>. A. V. gives diligently as the force of ejpi; but ejpi signifies direction rather than intensity. The idea is exercising oversight. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:2">1 Peter 5:2</span>. </p>
<p>Fail of [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υστερων απο] </span></span>. Rend. "fall back from," implying a previous attainment. The present participle marks something in progress : "lest any one be falling back." </p>
<p>Root of bitterness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ριζα πικριας] </span></span>. From LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+29:18">Deuteronomy 29:18</span>. A bad man in the church. Riza of a person, 1 Macc. 1 10. </p>
<p>Springing up [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανω φυουσα] </span></span>. The participle pictures the springing up in progress; the root gradually revealing its pernicious character. </p>
<p>Trouble [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενοχλη] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:18">Luke 6:18</span>, see note. </p>
<p>Many be defiled (mianqwsin oiJ polloi). Rend. "the many" : the majority of the church. For the verb see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:28">John 18:28</span>.</p>
