<p>That which was commanded [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το διαστελλομενον] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:36">Mark 7:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:24">Acts 14:24</span>. </p>
<p>Touch [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θιγη] </span></span>. Elsewhere in N. T. only ch. 11 28 and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+2:21">Colossians 2:21</span>. LXX only <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+19:12">Exodus 19:12</span>. It implies a touching or grasping which affects the object (comp. ver. 18 on yhlafan). In Class. often of touching or handling some sacred object which may be desecrated by the one who lays hands on it. See Soph. Philoct. 667; Oed. Tyr. 891, 899. So here, the touch of the mountain was profanation. </p>
<p>Shall be stoned [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λιθοβολησεται] </span></span>. Found in Matthew, Luke, and Acts. In LXX see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+19:13">Exodus 19:13</span>. Comp. ejliqasqhsan, ch. 11 37. The correct text omits or thrust through with a dart.</p>
