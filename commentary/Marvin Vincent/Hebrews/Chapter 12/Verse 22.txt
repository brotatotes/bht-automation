<p>To an innumerable company of angels (muriasin ajggelwn). On this whole passage (22 - 24) it is to be observed that it is arranged in a series of clauses connected by kai. Accordingly muriasin to myriads or tens of thousands stands by itself, and panhgurei festal assembly goes with ajggelwn angels. Muriav (see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:1">Luke 12:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:19">Acts 19:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+5:11">Revelation 5:11</span>; quite often in LXX) is strictly the number ten thousand. In the plural, an innumerable multitude. So A. V. here. Rend. "to an innumerable multitude," placing a comma after muriasin, and connecting of angels with the next clause. This use of muriasin without a qualifying genitive is justified by numerous examples. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+24:60">Genesis 24:60</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+32:30">Deuteronomy 32:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+33:2">Deuteronomy 33:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1sa+18:7">1 Samuel 18:7</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1sa+18:8">1 Samuel 18:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+90:7">Psalms 90:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="so+5:10">Song of Solomon 5:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="da+7:10">Daniel 7:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="da+11:12">Daniel 11:12</span>; Sir. 47 6; 2 Macc. 8 20; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:14">Jude 1:14</span>. Ciliadev thousands is used in the same way. See IsaLXX 22; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="da+7:10">Daniel 7:10</span> </p>
