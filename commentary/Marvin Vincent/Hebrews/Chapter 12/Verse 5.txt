<p>Ye have forgotten [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκλελησθε] </span></span>. N. T. o. Common in Class., o LXX The simple verb lanqanein means to escape notice; to be unseen or unknown. Middle and passive, to let a thing escape; forget. Some render interrogatively, "have ye forgotten?" Speaketh unto you [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμιν διαλεγεται] </span></span>. The verb always in the sense of mutual converse or discussion. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:34">Mark 9:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:2">Acts 17:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:19">Acts 18:19</span>. Rend. "reasoneth with you." </p>
<p>My son, etc. From <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="pr+3:11">Proverbs 3:11</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="pr+3:12">Proverbs 3:12</span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="job+5:17">Job 5:17</span>. </p>
<p>Despise not [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη ολιγωρει] </span></span>. N. T. o. LXX only in this passage. Quite often in Class. It means to make little of [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ολιγος] </span></span>. </p>
<p>Chastening [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παιδειας] </span></span>. Mostly in Hebrews See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:4">Ephesians 6:4</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:16">2 Timothy 3:16</span>.</p>
