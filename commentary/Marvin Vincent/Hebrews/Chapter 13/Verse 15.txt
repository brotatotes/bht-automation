<p>By him therefore [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δι αυτου] </span></span>. Rend. "through him." Omit therefore. A. V. follows T. R. oun. Through Jesus, and not through the Jewish ritual. Let us offer [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναφερωμεν] </span></span>. Lit. bring up the offering to the altar. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:21">James 2:21</span>, where the full phrase occurs. For the phrase offer up through Jesus Christ, comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:5">1 Peter 2:5</span>. </p>
<p>The sacrifice of praise [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυσιαν αινεσεως] </span></span>. The Levitical term for a thank - offering. See LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+7:2">Leviticus 7:2</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+7:3">Leviticus 7:3</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+7:5">Leviticus 7:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ch+29:31">2 Chronicles 29:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ch+33:16">2 Chronicles 33:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+49:14">Psalms 49:14</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+49:23">Psalms 49:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+106:22">Psalms 106:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+115:8">Psalms 115:8</span>. Ainesiv praise, N. T. o. Often in LXX, o Class. For "the sacrifice" rend. "a sacrifice." The sacrifice of thanksgiving is to take the place of the animal sacrifice. For the emphasis on thanksgiving in N. T. see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:20">Ephesians 5:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:12">Colossians 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:18">1 Thessalonians 5:18</span>. The Rabbins had a saying, "in the future time all sacrifices shall cease; but praises shall not cease." Philo says : "They offer the best sacrifice who glorify with hymns the savior and benefactor, God." That is the fruit of our lips [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τουτεστιν καρπον χειλεων] </span></span>. Omit our. From LXX of <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ho+14:3">Hosea 14:3</span>, where the Hebrew reads, "we will account our lips as calves" (offered in sacrifice). Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+57:19">Isaiah 57:19</span>. Giving thanks to his name [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ομολογουντων τω ονοματι αυτου] </span></span>. The phrase N. T. o, o LXX Rend. "of lips which make confession to his name."</p>
