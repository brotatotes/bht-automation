<p>Make you perfect [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καταρτισαι υμας] </span></span>. The verb is aptly chosen, since the readers are addressed as a body - the flock of Christ. The prayer is for the complete mutual adjustment of all the members of the flock into a perfected whole, fitted to do the perfect will of God. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:10">1 Peter 5:10</span>, and comp. notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:17">2 Timothy 3:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:10">1 Corinthians 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+13:11">2 Corinthians 13:11</span>. Ignatius uses the word of the church 's being joined [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηρτισμενοι</span></span>] in common subjection to the Bishops and the Presbytery (Eph. ii), and of himself as one composed or settled into union [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις ενωσιν] </span></span>, that is, avoiding division in the church (Philad. 8); and again to the Smyrnaeans (1) "I have perceived that ye are settled or compacted in faith immovable, being, as it were, nailed on the cross of the Lord Jesus Christ in flesh and in spirit." </p>
<p>In every good work [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν παντι αγαθω] </span></span>. A. V. follows T. R. ergw work. Rend. "in every good thing." </p>
<p>To do his will [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις το ποιησαι το θελημα αυτου] </span></span>. To the end that you do, etc. </p>
<p>Working in you [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιων εν ημιν] </span></span>. Rend. "in us." A. V. follows T. R. uJmin you. For "working" rend. "doing." The word plays on poihsai to do. "Make you perfect to do his will, he doing in us what is well - pleasing in his sight." </p>
<p>That which is well - pleasing in his sight [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το αυαρεστον ενωπιον αυτου] </span></span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:10">Ephesians 5:10</span>. The phrase N. T. o. Euareston usually with the simple dative, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:1">Romans 12:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:8">Romans 14:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:10">Ephesians 5:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+4:18">Philippians 4:18</span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:22">1 John 3:22</span>.</p>
