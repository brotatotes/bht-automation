<p>Suffer the word of exhortation [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανεχεσθε του λογου της παρακλησεως] </span></span>. For "suffer," rend. "bear with." See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:14">Acts 18:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:1">2 Corinthians 11:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:3">2 Timothy 4:3</span>. Do not become impatient at my counsels in this letter. The word of exhortation refers to the entire epistle which he regards as hortatory rather than didactic or consolatory. The phrase only in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:15">Acts 13:15</span>. </p>
<p>I have written a letter unto you [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεστειλα υμιν] </span></span>. A. V. supplies a letter. Rend. "I have written unto you." The verb only here, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:20">Acts 14:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:25">Acts 21:25</span>. Lit. to send, not letters only. Sometimes with ejpistolai or ejpistolav letters added, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ne+6:19">Nehemiah 6:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ne+1:0">Nehemiah 1:0</span> Macc. 12 7. In N. T. always of sending a letter. </p>
<p>In a few words [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια βραχεων] </span></span>. There is a suggestion of apology. Do not grow impatient. The letter is short. The phrase N. T. o, but comp. dij ojligwn, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:12">1 Peter 5:12</span>, and ejn ojligw briefly, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:3">Ephesians 3:3</span>.</p>
