<p>Them that are in bonds [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των δεσμιων] </span></span>. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:34">Hebrews 10:34</span>. As bound with them [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως συνδεδεμενοι] </span></span>. N. T. o. As if you were fellow - prisoners. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:14-26">1 Corinthians 12:14-26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:29">2 Corinthians 11:29</span>. Public intercession for prisoners has formed a part of the service of the church from the earliest times. See the prayer at the close of Clem. Rom Ad Corinth. 59. It also occurs in the daily morning service of the synagogue. </p>
<p>Which suffer adversity [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακουχουμενων] </span></span>. Rend. are evil entreated. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:37">Hebrews 11:37</span>. </p>
<p>As being yourselves also in the body [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως και αυτοι οντες εν σωματι] </span></span>. As subject like them to bodily sufferings. Not in the body - the church, which would require the article. The expression ejn swmati in the sense of being still alive, only in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:2">2 Corinthians 12:2</span></p>
