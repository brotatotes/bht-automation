<p>Marriage is honorable in all (timiov oJ gamov ejn pasin). Gamov everywhere else in N. T. a wedding or wedding feast, often in the plural, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:2">Matthew 22:2</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:3">Matthew 22:3</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:4">Matthew 22:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:36">Luke 12:36</span>. Timiov honorable or held in honor. Often in N. T. precious, of gold, stones, etc., as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:12">1 Corinthians 3:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+17:4">Revelation 17:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+18:12">Revelation 18:12</span> : of life, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:24">Acts 20:24</span> : the fruits of the earth, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:7">James 5:7</span>; the blood of Christ, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:19">1 Peter 1:19</span>; the divine promises, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:4">2 Peter 1:4</span>. Rend. "let marriage be had in honor." The statement is hortatory, as suiting the character of the entire context, and especially the gar for; "for whoremongers," etc. En pasin in all respects, " as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:11">1 Timothy 3:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:5">2 Timothy 4:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:9">Titus 2:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:18">Colossians 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+4:12">Philippians 4:12</span>. If as A. V., the more natural expression would be para pasin as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:26">Matthew 19:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:8">Acts 26:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:13">Romans 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:6">2 Thessalonians 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:27">James 1:27</span>. En pasin in all things appears in this chapter, ver. 18. 245 There are many points in which marriage is to be honored besides the avoidance of illicit connections. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:6">1 Thessalonians 4:6</span>. </p>
<p>God will judge [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρινει ο θεος] </span></span>. Note the emphatic position of oJ qeov. He will judge and condemn infractions of the marriage - bond, however social sentiment may condone them.</p>
