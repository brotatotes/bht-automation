<p>This acknowledgment as brethren the writer represents as prophetically announced by Messiah in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+22:22">Psalms 22:22</span>. The Psalm is the utterance of a sufferer crying to God for help in the midst of enemies. The Psalmist declares that God has answered his prayer, and that he will give public thanks therefore. </p>
<p>Unto my brethren [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοις αδελφοις μου] </span></span>. His brethren in the worshipping assembly. This is applied by our writer to the human brotherhood at large, and Christ is represented as identifying himself with them in thanksgiving. </p>
<p>Will I sing praise unto thee [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμνησω σε] </span></span>. Rare in N. T. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:30">Matthew 26:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:26">Mark 14:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:25">Acts 16:25</span>. Lit. hymn thee. Often in the Greek liturgies.</p>
