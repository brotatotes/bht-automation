<p>The word spoken by angels [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο δι αγγελλων λαληθεις λογος] </span></span>. The Mosaic legislation which was conveyed through the mediation of angels. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+33:2">Deuteronomy 33:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:38">Acts 7:38</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:53">Acts 7:53</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:19">Galatians 3:19</span>, on which see note. The agency of angels indicates the limitations of the legal dispensation; its character as a dispensation of the flesh. Hence its importance in this discussion. The abolition of the old limitations is the emancipation of man from subordination to the angels. The O. T. is made to furnish proof that such subordination is inconsistent with man's ultimate destiny to sovereignty over all creation. </p>
<p>Was steadfast [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγενετο βεβαιος] </span></span>. Rend. proved sure : realized itself in the event as securely founded in the divine holiness, and eternal in its principles and obligations. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:18">Matthew 5:18</span>. </p>
<p>Transgression and disobedience [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραβασις και παρακοη] </span></span>. </p>
<p>Parabasiv is a stepping over the line; the violation of a positive divine enactment. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:23">Romans 2:23</span>. Parakoh only in Paul and Hebrews, is a disobedience which results from neglecting to hear; from letting things drift by. It is noticeable how often in O. T. obedience is described as hearing, and disobedience as refusing to hear. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+14:26">Exodus 14:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+19:5">Exodus 19:5</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+19:8">Exodus 19:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+23:22">Exodus 23:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jos+1:18">Joshua 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+28:12">Isaiah 28:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+30:9">Isaiah 30:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+11:10">Jeremiah 11:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+32:23">Jeremiah 32:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+35:16">Jeremiah 35:16</span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:57">Acts 7:57</span>. </p>
<p>A just recompense of reward [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδικον μισθατοδοσιαν] </span></span>. Endikos just, only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:8">Romans 3:8</span>. o LXX, quite frequent in Class., but mainly in poetry. The meaning is substantially the same as dikaiov as it appears in the familiar phrase dikaiov eijmiwith the infinitive : thus, dikaiov eijmi kolazein I am right to punish, that is, I have a right, etc., right or justice being regarded as working within a definite circle. Misqapodosia recompense only in Hebrews. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:35">Hebrews 10:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:26">Hebrews 11:26</span>. o LXX, o Class., where the word is, misqodosia. From misqov wages and ajpodidonai to pay off or discharge. The reference is, primarily, to the punishments suffered by the Israelites in the wilderness. Comp. ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:16">Hebrews 3:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:28">Hebrews 10:28</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:5">1 Corinthians 10:5</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:6">1 Corinthians 10:6</span>.</p>
