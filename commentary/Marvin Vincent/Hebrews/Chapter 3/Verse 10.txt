<p>They shall not enter into my rest [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ει ελευσονται εις την καταπαυσιν μου] </span></span>. Lit. if they shall enter, etc. A common Hebraistic formula in oaths. Where God is speaking, as here, the ellipsis is "may I not be Jehovah if they shall enter." Where man is speaking, "so may God punish me if "; or" God do so to me and more if. " Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:12">Mark 8:12</span>; LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+14:23">Genesis 14:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+1:35">Deuteronomy 1:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ki+1:51">1 Kings 1:51</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ki+2:8">1 Kings 2:8</span>. Sometimes the ellipsis is filled out, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1sa+3:17">1 Samuel 3:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2sa+3:35">2 Samuel 3:35</span>. Katapausin rest, only in Hebrews, and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:49">Acts 7:49</span>. The verb katapauein to lay to rest also only in Acts and Hebrews. In Class. the verb sometimes means to kill or to depose from power. In the original citation the reference is to Canaan. Paul uses klhronomia inheritance in a similar sense.</p>
