<p>Harden not [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη σκληρυνητε] </span></span>. In N. T. mostly in this epistle. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:9">Acts 19:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:18">Romans 9:18</span>, see note. The group of kindred words consists of sklhrov hard (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:0">Matthew 25:0</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:0">Matthew 24:0</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:14">Jude 1:14</span>); sklyrothv hardness (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:5">Romans 2:5</span>); sklhrunein to harden (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:9">Acts 19:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:18">Romans 9:18</span>); and the compounds sklhrokardia hardness of heart (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:8">Matthew 19:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:5">Mark 10:5</span>), and sklhrotrachlov stiff - necked (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:0">Acts 7:0</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:0">Acts 5:0</span>). All occur in LXX, with the addition of sklhrwv hardly, painfully (not in N. T.). In the provocation [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω παραπικρασμω] </span></span>. Only here and ver. 15. In LXX only <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+94:8">Psalms 94:8</span>. The verb parapikraineinto provoke, only in ver. 16. Often in LXX The simple verb pikrainein to make bitter, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+3:19">Colossians 3:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+8:11">Revelation 8:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+10:9">Revelation 10:9</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+10:10">Revelation 10:10</span>. From pikrov bitter, pungent : hence to stir up to bitterness, to irritate. Comp. LXX <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eze+2:4">Ezekiel 2:4</span>. In the day (kata thn hJmeran). Kata in a temporal sense, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:1">Acts 12:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:23">Acts 19:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:27">Acts 27:27</span>. Comp. kat' ajrcav in the beginning, ch. 1 10. Of temptation [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του πειρασμου] </span></span>. Rend. "of the temptation," referring to a definite event, the murmuring against Moses at Rephidim on account of the lack of water, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+17:1-7">Exodus 17:1-7</span>. In that passage the LXX gives for the two proper names Massah and Meribah, peirasmov temptation, which is correct, and loidorhsiv railing or reviling, which is loose, since Meribah signifies strife. 181 In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+94:0">Psalms 94:0</span>, LXX renders Meribah parapikrasmov provocation, which is inexact, and Massah peirasmov temptation, which is correct.</p>
