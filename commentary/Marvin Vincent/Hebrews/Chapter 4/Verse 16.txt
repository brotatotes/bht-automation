<p><b>Come - unto (</b><span class="_800000"><span style=""><b>προσερχώμεθα</b></span></span><b>)</b></p>
<p>oP., often in Hebrews, and commonly in the same sense as here - approach to God through the O.T. sacrifices or the sacrifice of Christ. Paul's word <span class="_800000"><span style="">προσαγωγή</span></span><span style=""> </span>access expresses the same idea. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:18">Ephesians 2:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:12">Ephesians 3:12</span>. The phrase <i>come boldly</i> expresses a thought which the Epistle emphasizes - that Christianity is the religion of <i>free access</i> to God. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:12-13">2 Corinthians 3:12-13</span>.</p>
<p><b>Unto the throne of grace (</b><span class="_800000"><span style=""><b>τῷ θρόνῳ τῆς χάριτος</b></span></span><b>)</b></p>
<p>The phrase N.T.o Throne of <i>glory</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:28">Matthew 19:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:31">Matthew 25:31</span>: <i>of majesty</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+8:1">Hebrews 8:1</span>. In Revelation <i>throne</i> occurs over forty times, either <i>the</i> throne, or <i>his</i> throne, or throne <i>of God</i>. Once throne <i>of the beast</i>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+16:10">Revelation 16:10</span>. <i>Throne of grace</i> expresses grace as the gift of divine power.</p>
<p><b>Mercy - grace (</b><span class="_800000"><span style=""><b>ἔλεος </b></span></span><span class="_800000"><b>- </b></span><span class="_800000"><span style=""><b>χάριν</b></span></span><b>)</b></p>
<p><i>Mercy</i> for past sins; <i>grace</i> for future work, trial, and resistance to temptation.</p>
<p><b>To help in time of need (</b><span class="_800000"><span style=""><b>εἰς εὔκαιρον βοήθειαν</b></span></span><b>)</b></p>
<p>Lit. for <i>seasonable help</i>, or <i>help in good time</i>; before it is too late; while there is still time to seek God's rest. Others, however, explain, <i>when it is needed</i>; or, <i>before temptation leads to sin</i>.</p>
