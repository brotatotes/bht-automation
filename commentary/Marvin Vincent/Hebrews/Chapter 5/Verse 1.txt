<p>Every high priest [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πας αρχιερευς] </span></span>. Every Levitical high priest. Arciereuv o P. </p>
<p>Taken [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανομενος] </span></span>. Rend. being taken, or since he is taken : not who is taken. The point is that the high priest 's efficiency for men depends on his being taken from among men. </p>
<p>Is ordained [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθισταται] </span></span>. Constituted priest. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:5">Titus 1:5</span>. </p>
<p>For men [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ ανθρωπων] </span></span>. On behalf of men. </p>
<p>In things pertaining to God [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα προς τον θεον</span></span>] . As respects his relation to God. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:17">Hebrews 2:17</span>. </p>
<p>That he may offer [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα προσφερη] </span></span>. Prosferein, lit. to bring to (the altar). Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:23">Matthew 5:23</span>. o P., who, however, has the kindred noun prosfora offering. Very often in LXX; nineteen times in Hebrews, and always, with one exception (ch. 12 7), in the technical sense, as here. Gifts - sacrifices [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δωρα - θυσιας] </span></span>. Dwra offerings generally : qusiav bloody sacrifices. The distinction, however, is not constantly observed. Thus, qusiai, of unbloody offerings, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+4:3">Genesis 4:3</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+4:5">Genesis 4:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+2:1">Leviticus 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="nu+5:15">Numbers 5:15</span> : dwra, of bloody offerings, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+4:4">Genesis 4:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+1:2">Leviticus 1:2</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+1:3">Leviticus 1:3</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+1:10">Leviticus 1:10</span>. </p>
<p>For sins [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερ αμαρτιων] </span></span>. In this the priest 's efficiency is especially called out, and he who has not genuine compassion for the sinful cannot do this efficiently. Hence the words which follow.</p>
