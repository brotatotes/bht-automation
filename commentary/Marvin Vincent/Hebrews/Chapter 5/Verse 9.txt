<p>And being made perfect [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">και τελεωθεις] </span></span>. Comp. ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:10">Hebrews 2:10</span>. The fundamental idea in teleioun is the bringing of a person or thing to the goal fixed by God. Comp. ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:11">Hebrews 7:11</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:19">Hebrews 7:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:9">Hebrews 9:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:1">Hebrews 10:1</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:14">Hebrews 10:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:40">Hebrews 11:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:23">Hebrews 12:23</span>. Here of Christ 's having reached the end which was contemplated in his divinely - appointed discipline for the priesthood. The consummation was attained in his death, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:8">Philippians 2:8</span> : his obedience extended even unto death. </p>
<p>The author of eternal salvation [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιτιος σωτηριας αιωνιου] </span></span>. jAitiov, N. T. o, an adjective, causing. Comp. captain of salvation, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:10">Hebrews 2:10</span>. The phrase swthria aojwniov eternal salvation N. T. o, but see LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+14:17">Isaiah 14:17</span>. Not everlasting salvation, but a salvation of which all the conditions, attainments, privileges, and rewards transcend the conditions and limitations of time. </p>
<p>Unto all them that obey him (pasin toiv uJpkouousin aujtw). Obey points to obedience, ver. 8, and salvation to save, ver. 7. If the captain of salvation must learn obedience, so must his followers. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:8">2 Thessalonians 1:8</span>.</p>
