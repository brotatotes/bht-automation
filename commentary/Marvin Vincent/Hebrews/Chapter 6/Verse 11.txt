<p>We desire [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιθυμουμεν] </span></span>. Strongly, earnestly. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:17">Matthew 13:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:15">Luke 22:15</span>. The manifestations just mentioned make the writer desire that they may exhibit more of the spirit which animates their beneficent works. </p>
<p>Each [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκαστον] </span></span>. He is concerned, not only for the body of believers, but for each member. </p>
<p>To the full assurance of hope unto the end [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς την πληροφοριαν της ελπιδος αρχι τελους] </span></span>. That is, we desire that each of you exhibit the same diligence to develop your hope, which is in danger of failing, into full assurance, unto the end of the present season of trial with its happy consummation. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:24">Romans 8:24</span>. For plhroforia see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:5">1 Thessalonians 1:5</span>, and comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:21">Romans 4:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:5">Romans 14:5</span>. It is practically the same whether we translate full development or full assurance. The two meanings coalesce. Hope develops into full assurance.</p>
