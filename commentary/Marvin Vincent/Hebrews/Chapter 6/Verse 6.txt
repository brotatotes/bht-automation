<p>If they shall fall away [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">και πααπεσοντας] </span></span>. Lit. and having fallen away. Comp. pesh fall, ch. 4 11. Parapiptein, N. T. o. It means to deviate, turn aside. Comp. LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eze+14:13">Ezekiel 14:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eze+14:8">Ezekiel 14:8</span>. </p>
<p>To renew them again [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παλιν ανακαινιζειν] </span></span>. The verb N. T. o. Anakainoun to renew, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:16">2 Corinthians 4:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+3:10">Colossians 3:10</span>. </p>
<p>Seeing they crucify to themselves - afresh [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανασταυρουντας εαυτοις] </span></span>. In the Roman classical use of the word, ajna has only the meaning up : to nail up on the cross. Here in the sense of anew, an idea for which classical writers had no occasion in connection with crucifying. 195 Eautoiv for themselves. So that Christ is no more available for them. They declare that Christ 's crucifixion has not the meaning or the virtue which they formerly attached to it. </p>
<p>The Son of God. Marking the enormity of the offense. </p>
<p>Put him to an open shame [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραδειγματιζοντας] </span></span>. N. T. o. Rarely in LXX Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="nu+25:4">Numbers 25:4</span>, hang them up. From para beside, deiknunai to show or point out. To put something alongside of a thing by way of commending it to imitation or avoidance. To make an example of; thence to expose to public disgrace. Deigma example, only <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:7">Jude 1:7</span>. Deigmatizein to make a public show or example, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+1:19">Matthew 1:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+2:15">Colossians 2:15</span>. See additional note at the end of this chapter.</p>
