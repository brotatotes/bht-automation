<p>The inevitableness of the punishment illustrated by a familiar fact of nature. </p>
<p>The earth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γη] </span></span>. Or the land. Personified. Comp. aujtomath hJ gh the land of itself, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:28">Mark 4:28</span>, see note. </p>
<p>Which drinketh in [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η πιουσα] </span></span>. Appropriates the heavenly gift of rain, the richness of which is indicated by that cometh oft upon it. </p>
<p>Herbs [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βοτανην] </span></span>. Grass, fodder. N. T. o. </p>
<p>Meet for them by whom it is dressed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευθετον εκεινοις δι ους και γεωργειται] </span></span>. For euqeton, lit. well placed, thence fit or appropriate, see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:62">Luke 9:62</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:8">Luke 14:8</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:5">Luke 14:5</span>. Gewrgein to till the ground, N. T. o. Rend. tilled. Dress is properly to trim. The natural result of the ground's receiving and absorbing the rains is fruitfulness, which redounds to the benefit of those who cultivate it. </p>
<p>Receiveth blessing from God [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεταλαμβανει ευλογιας απο του θεου] </span></span>. Rend. partaketh of blessing. The blessing is increased fruitfulness. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:12">Matthew 13:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:2">John 14:2</span>.</p>
