As the law prescribed that the priesthood should be of the order of Aaron, a new priesthood, not of that order, must set aside the law. Pertaineth to another tribe [φυλης ετερας μετεσχηκεν] . Lit. hath partaken of another tribe. Not only another, but a different tribe; one not specially set apart to sacerdotal service. Of which no man gave attendance at the altar [αφ ης ουδεις προσεσχηκεν τω θυσιαστηριω] . Prosecein originally to bring to; bring the mind to; attend to. See on ch. Hebrews 2:1. qusiasthrion altar, o Class. Strictly an altar for the sacrifice of victims; but used of the altar of incense, Luke 1:11; Revelation 8:3; comp. Exodus 30:1. See on Acts 17:23. It was also used of the enclosure in which the altar stood. See Ignat. Eph. v; Trall. Acts 7:0 See Lightfoot's interesting note, Ignatius and Polycarp, Vol 2 p. 43.
