<p>As the law prescribed that the priesthood should be of the order of Aaron, a new priesthood, not of that order, must set aside the law. Pertaineth to another tribe [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυλης ετερας μετεσχηκεν] </span></span>. Lit. hath partaken of another tribe. Not only another, but a different tribe; one not specially set apart to sacerdotal service. </p>
<p>Of which no man gave attendance at the altar [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφ ης ουδεις προσεσχηκεν τω θυσιαστηριω] </span></span>. Prosecein originally to bring to; bring the mind to; attend to. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:1">Hebrews 2:1</span>. qusiasthrion altar, o Class. Strictly an altar for the sacrifice of victims; but used of the altar of incense, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:11">Luke 1:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+8:3">Revelation 8:3</span>; comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+30:1">Exodus 30:1</span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:23">Acts 17:23</span>. It was also used of the enclosure in which the altar stood. See Ignat. Eph. v; Trall. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:0">Acts 7:0</span> See Lightfoot's interesting note, Ignatius and Polycarp, Vol 2 p. 43.</p>
