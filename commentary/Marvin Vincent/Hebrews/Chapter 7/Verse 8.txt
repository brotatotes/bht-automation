<p>Here [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωδε] </span></span>. In the Levitical economy. </p>
<p>Men that die receive tithes. The emphasis is on ajpoqnhskontev dying. The Levites are dying men, who pass away in due course, and are succeeded by others. </p>
<p>But there [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκει δε] </span></span>. In the case of Melchisedec. </p>
<p>(He receiveth them of whom) it is witnessed that he liveth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρουμενος οτι ζη] </span></span>. The Greek is very condensed : being attested that he liveth. The A. V. fills it out correctly. Melchisedec does not appear in Scripture as one who dies, and whose office passes to another. See on abideth continually, ver. 3.</p>
