<p>The covenant which I will make [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η διαθηκη ην δοαθησομαι] </span></span>. The noun and the verb are cognate - the arrangement which I will arrange. A covenant [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαθηκη</span></span>] is something arranged [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διατιθεσθαι</span></span>] between two parties. See the same combination, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:25">Acts 3:25</span>. </p>
<p>I will put my laws [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διδους νομους μου] </span></span>. Lit. giving my laws : const. with I will make : "the covenant which I will make by giving my laws." Mind [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διανοιαν] </span></span>. The moral understanding. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:30">Mark 12:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:51">Luke 1:51</span>. Hearts, kardiav, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:21">Romans 1:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:10">Romans 10:10</span>. </p>
<p>A God - a people [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις θεον - εις λαον] </span></span>. Lit. unto a God, etc. A Hebraistic form of expression, eijv signifying the destination of the substantive verb. The sense is, I will be to them to serve as a God; or my being as related to them will amount to my being a God to them. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:5">Matthew 19:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:18">2 Corinthians 6:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:5">Hebrews 1:5</span>.</p>
