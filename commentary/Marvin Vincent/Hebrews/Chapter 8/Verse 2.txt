<p>A minister [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λειτουργος] </span></span>. Sat down as a minister. From an old adjective leitov or leitov (found only in this compound), belonging to the people, and ergon work. Hence, originally, the service of the state in a public office. In LXX and N. T. leitourgov minister, leitourgein to minister, and leitourgia ministry are used both of priestly service to God and of service to men. Leitourgia in LXX rarely of the service of the priests, often of the Levites. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ki+1:4">1 Kings 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ki+19:21">1 Kings 19:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ki+4:43">2 Kings 4:43</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ki+6:15">2 Kings 6:15</span>. Leitourgouv <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:7">Hebrews 1:7</span>, in the general sense of servants of God. Of the sanctuary [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των αγιων] </span></span>. The heavenly sanctuary. Ta agia the most holy place, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:8">Hebrews 9:8</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:12">Hebrews 9:12</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:25">Hebrews 9:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:19">Hebrews 10:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:11">Hebrews 13:11</span>. Comp. agia ajgiwn holy of holies, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:3">Hebrews 9:3</span>. %Agia holy places generally, but with special reference to the innermost sanctuary, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:24">Hebrews 9:24</span>. </p>
<p>The true tabernacle [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">της σκηνης της αλυθινης] </span></span>. Explanatory oftwn aJgiwn. The form of expression is emphatic : the tabernacle, the genuine one, as compared with the tabernacle in the wilderness. For ajliqinov real, genuine, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:9">John 1:9</span>. Skhnh a tent. For different shades of meaning, comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:4">Matthew 17:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:9">Luke 16:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:43">Acts 7:43</span>. In this epistle always of the tabernacle in the wilderness.</p>
