<p>For finding fault with them [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεμφομενος αυτους] </span></span>. 204 Them signifies the possessors of the first covenant. The prophet says what follows by way of blame. The passage cited is <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+38:0">Jeremiah 38:0</span>, LXX (A. V. 31); 31 - 34. The writer assumes that Jeremiah's new covenant means the Christian covenant. </p>
<p>I will make [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συντελεσω] </span></span>. Rend. I will conclude or consummate. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:13">Luke 4:13</span>. Only here in Hebrews, and once in Paul, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:28">Romans 9:28</span>, a citation. </p>
<p>With the house [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επι] </span></span>. The preposition marking direction toward. A new covenant [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαθηκην καινην] </span></span>. Always kainh in the phrase new covenant, except <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:24">Hebrews 12:24</span>, where we have nea. For the distinction see note there, and on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:29">Matthew 26:29</span>.</p>
