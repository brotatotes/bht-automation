<p>The impotence of the gifts and sacrifices lay in the fact that they were only symbolic ordinances. </p>
<p>Which stood in [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επι] </span></span>. The passage should be read thus : "according to which are offered gifts and sacrifices which cannot perfect the worshipper as touching the conscience, being mere ordinances of the flesh on the ground of [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επι ρεστινγ υπον</span></span>] meats," etc. </p>
<p>Meats and drinks and divers washings [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βρωμασιν και πομασιν και διαφοροις βαπτισμοις] </span></span>. Brwmasin, clean and unclean meats. pomasin drinks, concerning which the Levitical law laid down no prescriptions except as to abstinence in the case of a Nazarite vow, and of the priests when they were about to officiate. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="nu+6:3">Numbers 6:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+10:9">Leviticus 10:9</span>. For baptismoiv washings see on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:2">Hebrews 6:2</span>. </p>
<p>And carnal ordinances [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιωματα σαρκος] </span></span>. Omit and. The phrase is a general description of meats, etc. Lit. ordinances of the flesh. Imposed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επικειμενα] </span></span>. Some interpreters find in this the suggestion of a burden, which these ceremonial observances assuredly were. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:10">Acts 14:10</span>. This, however, is not probable. </p>
<p>Until the time of reformation [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεχρι καιρου διορθωσεως] </span></span>. </p>
<p>Diorqwsiv N. T. o, o LXX, occasionally in Class. Diorqwma correction, amendment, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:2">Acts 24:2</span>. Diorqwsiv lit. making straight : used by medical writers of straightening a distorted limb. The verb diorqoun (not in N. T.) in LXX of mending one's ways, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+7:3">Jeremiah 7:3</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+7:5">Jeremiah 7:5</span>; Wisd. 9 18. Of setting up or establishing, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+16:5">Isaiah 16:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+42:7">Isaiah 42:7</span>. "The time of reformation" is the Christian age, when God made with his people a better covenant. It was inaugurated by the death of Christ. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:2">Hebrews 1:2</span>. The gifts and offerings were only provisional, to tide the people over to the better time.</p>
