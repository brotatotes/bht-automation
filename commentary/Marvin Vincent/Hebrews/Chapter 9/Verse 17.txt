<p>For a testament is of force after men are dead [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαθηκη γαρ επι νεκροις βεβαια] </span></span>. Rend. "for a covenant is of force (or sure) over (or upon) dead (victims)." Comp. Soph. Elect. 237; Eurip. Ion. 228; Aesch. Eumen. 316; Hdt 4:162. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+21:5">Leviticus 21:5</span>. </p>
<p>Otherwise it is of no strength at all while the testator liveth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επει μη τοτε ισχυει οτε ζη ο διαθεμενος] </span></span>. Rend. "since it hath not then force when the institutor is alive" : until he has been representatively slain.</p>
