For a testament is of force after men are dead [διαθηκη γαρ επι νεκροις βεβαια] . Rend. "for a covenant is of force (or sure) over (or upon) dead (victims)." Comp. Soph. Elect. 237; Eurip. Ion. 228; Aesch. Eumen. 316; Hdt 4:162. See also Leviticus 21:5. Otherwise it is of no strength at all while the testator liveth [επει μη τοτε ισχυει οτε ζη ο διαθεμενος] . Rend. "since it hath not then force when the institutor is alive" : until he has been representatively slain.
