<p>Almost all things [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σχεδον - παντα] </span></span>. The A. V. is wrong. Sxedon almost or nearly is prefixed to the entire clause, and applies to both its members. Rend. "and I may almost say, it is in blood," etc. Almost provides for such exceptions as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+19:10">Exodus 19:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+32:30-32">Exodus 32:30-32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+5:11-13">Exodus 5:11-13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+14:5">Leviticus 14:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+16:26-28">Leviticus 16:26-28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+22:6">Leviticus 22:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="nu+16:46-48">Numbers 16:46-48</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="nu+31:23">Numbers 31:23</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="nu+31:24">Numbers 31:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+51:1-17">Psalms 51:1-17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+32:1">Psalms 32:1</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+32:2">Psalms 32:2</span>.
