<p>The inferiority of the ancient system was proved by the old tabernacle itself : by its division into two parts, both of which were inaccessible to the people. </p>
<p>Always [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια παντος] </span></span>. Rend. continually. The phrase is usually found in connection with matters involving relations to God - worship, sacrifice, etc. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:10">Matthew 18:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:53">Luke 24:53</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:25">Acts 2:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:2">Acts 10:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:16">2 Thessalonians 3:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:5">Hebrews 13:5</span>. </p>
