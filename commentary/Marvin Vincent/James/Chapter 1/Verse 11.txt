<p>For the sun is no sooner risen, etc. [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανετειλεν γαρ ο ηλιος] </span></span>. By the use of the aorist tense James graphically throws his illustration into the narrative form : "For the sun arose - and withered," etc. </p>
<p>With a burning heat [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω καυσωνι] </span></span>. Rev., with the scorching wind. The article denotes something familiar; and the reference may be to the scorching east - wind (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="job+1:19">Job 1:19</span>, Sept.; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eze+17:10">Ezekiel 17:10</span>), which withers vegetation. Some of the best authorities, however, prefer the rendering of the A. V. </p>
<p>Falleth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξεπεσεν] </span></span>. Aorist tense. Lit., fell off. </p>
<p>The grace of the fashion [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευπρεπεια του προσωπου] </span></span>. Lit., the beauty of its face or appearance. Eujprepeia only here in New Testament. </p>
<p>Fade away [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρανθησεται] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:4">1 Peter 1:4</span>. </p>
<p>Ways [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πορειαις] </span></span>. Rev., goings. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:22">Luke 13:22</span>. His goings to and fro in acquiring riches.</p>
