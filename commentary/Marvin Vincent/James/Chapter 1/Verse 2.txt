<p>All joy [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πασαν χαραν] </span></span>. Joy follows up the rejoice of the greeting. The all has the sense of wholly. Count it a thing wholly joyful, without admixture of sorrow. Perhaps, as Bengel suggests, the all applies to all kinds of temptations. </p>
<p>When [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οταν] </span></span>. Lit., whenever : better, because it implies that temptation may be expected all along the Christian course. </p>
<p>Ye fall into [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιπεσητε] </span></span>. The preposition peri, around, suggests falling into something which surrounds. Thus Thucydides, speaking of the plague at Athens, says, "The Athenians, having fallen into [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιπεσοντες</span></span>] such affliction, were pressed by it." </p>
<p>Divers [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποικιλοις] </span></span>. Rev., manifold. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:6">1 Peter 1:6</span>. </p>
<p>Temptations [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πειρασμοις] </span></span>. In the general sense of trials. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:13">Matthew 6:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:6">1 Peter 1:6</span>.</p>
