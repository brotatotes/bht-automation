<p>A double - minded man is unstable, etc. The A. V. puts this as an independent apophthegm, which is wrong. The sentence is a comment and enlargement upon that man. "Let not that man think," etc., "a doubleminded man, unstable in all his ways." So Rev. </p>
<p>Double - minded [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διψυχος] </span></span>. Peculiar to James, here and ch. 4 8. Not deceitful, but dubious and undecided. </p>
<p>Unstable [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακαταστατος] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. The kindred ajkatastasia, confusion, is found ch. 3 16, and elsewhere.</p>
