<p>Ye have respect to persons [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσωπολημπτειτε] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. See on ver. 1. </p>
<p>Ye commit sin [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτιαν εργαζεσθε] </span></span>. Lit., "work sin." Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:23">Matthew 7:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:35">Acts 10:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:33">Hebrews 11:33</span>. The phrase is rather stronger than the more common aJmartian poiein, to do sin, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:34">John 8:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:15">James 5:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:29">1 Peter 2:29</span>. The position of sin is emphatic : "it is sin that ye are working." </p>
<p>And are convinced [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεγχομενοι] </span></span>. Rather, as Rev., convicted. The word, which is variously rendered in A. V. tell a fault, reprove, rebuke, convince, while it carries the idea of rebuke, implies also a rebuke which produces a conviction of the error or sin. See on John viii. 46. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:20">John 3:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:9">John 8:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:24">1 Corinthians 14:24</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:25">1 Corinthians 14:25</span>.</p>
