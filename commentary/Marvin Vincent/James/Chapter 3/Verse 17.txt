<p>First. Emphasizing its inner quality, pure, as distinguished from its outward expressions. The idea is not first numerically, but first essentially. The other qualities are secondary as outgrowths of this primary quality. Gentle [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιεικης] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:18">1 Peter 2:18</span>. </p>
<p>Easy to be intreated [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευπειθης] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. </p>
<p>Without partiality [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδιακριτος] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament and very rare in classical Greek. Rev., without variance or doubting. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:6">James 1:6</span>. </p>
