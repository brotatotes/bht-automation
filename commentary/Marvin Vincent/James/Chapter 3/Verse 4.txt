<p>The ships. See Introduction, on James' local allusions. Dean Howson observes that "there is more imagery drawn from mere natural phenomena in the one short epistle of James than in all St. Paul 's epistles put together." </p>
<p>So great. As the ship which conveyed Paul to Malta, which contained two hundred and seventy - six persons (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:37">Acts 27:37</span>). </p>
<p>Fierce [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκληρων] </span></span>. More literally, and better, as Rev., rough. The word primarily means hard, harsh. </p>
<p>Helm [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πηδαλιου] </span></span>. Better, rudder, as Rev. The rudder was an oar worked by a handle. Helm and rudder were thus one. The word occurs only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:40">Acts 27:40</span>. </p>
<p>The governor listeth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η ορμη του ευθυνοντος βουλεται] </span></span>. Lit., the impulse or desire of the steersman wisheth. 'Ormh, impulse, only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:5">Acts 14:5</span>, of an assault, onset. </p>
<p>The governor [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του ευθυνοντος] </span></span>. Rev., steersman. Lit., of him who is guiding. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:23">John 1:23</span>. From 'Ormh, straight.</p>
