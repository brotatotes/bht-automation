<p>Go to now [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγε νυν] </span></span>. Go to is an obsolete phrase, though retained in Rev. It is a formula for calling attention : come now. </p>
<p>Such a city [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηνδε την πολιν] </span></span>. More accurately, as Rev., this city. Continue there a year [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τοιησομεν εκει ενιαυτον] </span></span>. Lit., we will make a year. See, for the same form of expression, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:33">Acts 14:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:23">Acts 18:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:25">2 Corinthians 11:25</span>. Better, as Rev., spend a year there. (Compare the A. V., <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:23">Acts 18:23</span>, rightly retained by Rev.) The word poihsomen implies more than mere continuance; rather, a doing something with the year. </p>
<p>And. The frequent use of the copulative gives a lively tone to the passage, expressive of the lightness and thoughtlessness of a careless spirit. Buy and sell [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμπορευσομεθα] </span></span>. Rev., more concisely, trade. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:3">2 Peter 2:3</span>.</p>
