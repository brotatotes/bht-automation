<p>Whereas ye know not [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιτινες ουκ επιστασθε] </span></span>. The pronoun marking a class, as being of those who know not. </p>
<p>What shall be on the morrow [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το της αυριον] </span></span>. Lit., the thing of the morrow. The texts vary. Westcott and Hort read, Ye know not what your life shalt be on the morrow, for ye are a vapor : thus throwing out the question. </p>
<p>What is your life ? [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποια] </span></span>. Lit., of what kind or nature. </p>
<p>It is even a vapor [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ατμις γαρ εστιν] </span></span>. But all the best texts read ejste, ye are. So Rev., which, however, retains the question, what is your life ? </p>
<p>Appeareth - vanisheth. Both participles, appearing, vanishing. </p>
<p>And then [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επειτα και] </span></span>. The kai placed after the adverb then is not copulative, but expresses that the vapor vanishes even as it appeared.</p>
