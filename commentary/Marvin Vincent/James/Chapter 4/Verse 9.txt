<p>Be afflicted [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταλαιπωρησατε] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. The kindred noun talaipwria, misery, occurs ch. 5 1. </p>
<p>Mourn [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πενθησατε] </span></span>. Used of grief that is manifested. So mostly in New Testament, and very commonly joined, as here, with weep. So <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+16:10">Mark 16:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:25">Luke 6:25</span>, etc. In the next sentence occurs the kindred noun penqov mourning, into which laughter, also something manifest, is to be changed. Heaviness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηφειαν] </span></span>. Properly, a casting down of the eyes. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:13">Luke 18:13</span>. Only here in New Testament.</p>
