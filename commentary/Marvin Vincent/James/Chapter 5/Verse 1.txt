<p>Go to. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:13">James 4:13</span>. </p>
<p>Weep and howl [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλαυσατε ολολυζοντες] </span></span>. Lit., weep, howling. The latter is a descriptive word, ol - ol - uz - o. Only here in New Testament, and denoting a more demonstrative and passionate expression of grief than weeping. </p>
<p>Miseries [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταλαιπωριαις] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:16">Romans 3:16</span>. See on be afflicted, ch. 4 9. </p>
<p>That shall come upon [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επερχομεναις] </span></span>. Present participle. More correctly, as Rev., that are coming.</p>
