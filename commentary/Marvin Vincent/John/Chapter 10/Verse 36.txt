<p>Sanctified [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγιασεν] </span></span>. Better, as Rev., in margin, consecrated. The fundamental idea of the word is separation and consecration to the service of Deity. See note on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:10">Acts 26:10</span>, on the kindred adjective agiov, holy or consecrated. </p>
<p>The Son of God. There is no article. Its absence directs us to the character rather than to the person of Jesus. The judges, to whom the quotation in ver. 35 refers, were called gods, as being representatives of God. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+21:6">Exodus 21:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+22:8">Exodus 22:8</span>, where the word rendered judges is elohim, gods. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+22:28">Exodus 22:28</span>, gods appears in the A. V. 35 Jesus ' course of reasoning is, if these judges could be called gods, how do I blaspheme in calling myself Son of God, since the Father has consecrated me and sent me on a special mission to the world ?</p>
