<p>He hath blinded, etc. These words of Isaiah are repeated five times in the New Testament as the description of the Jewish people in its latest stage of decay. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:13">Matthew 13:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:12">Mark 4:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:10">Luke 8:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:40">John 12:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:26">Acts 28:26</span>. </p>
<p>Hardened [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπωρωκεν] </span></span>. See on the kindred noun pwrwsiv, hardness, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:5">Mark 3:5</span>. </p>
<p>Understand [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νοησωσιν] </span></span>. Rev., better, perceive. Mark has suniwsin, understand. See on understanding, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:47">Luke 2:47</span>. </p>
