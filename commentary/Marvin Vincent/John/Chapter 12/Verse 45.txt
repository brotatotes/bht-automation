<p>Seeth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεωρει] </span></span>. Rev., properly, beholdeth. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:9">John 14:9</span>. The word is purposely chosen to mark an intent, continuous contemplation of Christ, issuing in ever larger knowledge of the Father. </p>
<p>I am come [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εληλυθα] </span></span>. The perfect tense, pointing to the abiding result of His manifestation. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:43">John 5:43</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:28">John 7:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:42">John 8:42</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:28">John 16:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:37">John 18:37</span>. Abide in darkness. The phrase occurs only here. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:9">1 John 2:9</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:11">1 John 2:11</span>; also 1Jo 8:12; 1Jo 12:35.</p>
