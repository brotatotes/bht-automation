<p>Believe not [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη πιστευση] </span></span>. The best texts read fulaxh, keep (them). </p>
<p>Came [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηλθον] </span></span>. The aorist tense, pointing to the purpose of the coming, as I am come (ver. 46) to the result. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:14">John 8:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:39">John 9:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:10">John 10:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:27">John 12:27</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:47">John 12:47</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:22">John 14:22</span>. Both tenses are found in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:42">John 8:42</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:28">John 16:28</span>.</p>
