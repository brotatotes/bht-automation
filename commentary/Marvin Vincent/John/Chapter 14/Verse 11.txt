<p>Believe me [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστευετε μοι] </span></span>. The plural of the imperative : "believe ye me." Compare believest thou, ver. 10. These words are addressed to the disciples collectively, whose thought Philip had voiced. </p>
<p>Or else [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ει δε μη] </span></span>. Literally, but if not. If you do not believe on the authority of my personal statement. </p>
<p>For the very works' sake [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια τα εργα αυτα] </span></span>. Literally, on account of the works themselves, irrespective of my oral testimony.</p>
