Believe me [πιστευετε μοι] . The plural of the imperative : "believe ye me." Compare believest thou, ver. 10. These words are addressed to the disciples collectively, whose thought Philip had voiced. Or else [ει δε μη] . Literally, but if not. If you do not believe on the authority of my personal statement. For the very works' sake [δια τα εργα αυτα] . Literally, on account of the works themselves, irrespective of my oral testimony.
