<p>I come [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχομαι] </span></span>. I am coming. Spoken of His departure to the Father. </p>
<p>Holy [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγιε] </span></span>. See on saints, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:10">Acts 26:10</span>; also on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:15">1 Peter 1:15</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:20">1 John 2:20</span>, and righteous Father [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιε] </span></span>, ver. 25. This epithet, now first applied to the Father, contemplates God, the holy One, as the agent of that which Christ desires for His disciples - holiness of heart and life; being kept from this evil world. </p>
<p>Those whom [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ους] </span></span>. The correct reading is w=, referring to name. Thy name which Thou hast given me. So in ver. 12. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:9">Philippians 2:9</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:10">Philippians 2:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+2:17">Revelation 2:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+19:12">Revelation 19:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+22:4">Revelation 22:4</span>.</p>
