<p>Sanctify [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγιασον] </span></span>. Constantly used in the Septuagint to express the entire dedication and consecration of both persons and things to God. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+29:1">Exodus 29:1</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+29:36">Exodus 29:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+40:13">Exodus 40:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+22:2">Leviticus 22:2</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+22:3">Leviticus 22:3</span>. Rev., in margin, consecrate. See on 10 36. </p>
<p>Through thy truth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τη αληθεια σου] </span></span>. The best texts omit thy. Through [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span>] is to be rendered literally, in, marking the sphere or element of consecration. Rev., sanctify them in the truth. </p>
<p>Thy word [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο λογος ο σος] </span></span>. Properly, the word which is thine. See on 14 9.</p>
