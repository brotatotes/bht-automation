<p>A band (thn speiran). Properly, the band. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:16">Mark 14:16</span>; also on centurion, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:2">Luke 7:2</span>; and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:31">Acts 21:31</span>. The band, or cohort, was from the Roman garrison in the tower of Antonia. </p>
<p>Officers [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπηρετας] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:25">Matthew 5:25</span>. Sent from the Sanhedrim. The temple police. The Synoptists speak of the body which arrested Jesus as oclov, a multitude or rabble; but both Matthew and Mark mention the band [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπειρα</span></span>] later in the narrative (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:27">Matthew 27:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:16">Mark 14:16</span>). </p>
<p>Lanterns [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φανων] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. A detail peculiar to John. Though it was full moon, it was feared that Jesus might hide and escape.</p>
