<p>Woman. Implying no severity nor disrespect. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:13">John 20:13</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:15">John 20:15</span>. It was a highly respectful and affectionate mode of address. </p>
<p>What have I to do with thee [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τι εμοι και σοι] </span></span>. Literally, what is there to me and to thee. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:7">Mark 5:7</span>, and compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:29">Matthew 8:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:19">Matthew 27:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:24">Mark 1:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:28">Luke 8:28</span>. It occurs often in the Old Testament, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2sa+16:10">2 Samuel 16:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ki+17:18">1 Kings 17:18</span>, etc. Though in a gentle and affectionate manner, Jesus rejects her interference, intending to supply the demand in His own way. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:6">John 6:6</span>. Wyc., What to me and to thee, thou woman ? </p>
<p>Mine hour is not yet come. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:20">John 8:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:23">John 12:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:1">John 13:1</span>. In every case the coming of the hour indicates some crisis in the personal life of the Lord, more commonly His passion. Here the hour of His Messianic manifestation (ver. 11).</p>
