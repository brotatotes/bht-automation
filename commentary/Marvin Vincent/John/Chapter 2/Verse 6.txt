<p>Water - pots [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υδριαι] </span></span>. Used by John only, and only in the Gospel, ver. 7; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:28">John 4:28</span>. Water - pots is literally correct, as the word is from udwr, water. Of stone. Because less liable to impurity, and therefore prescribed by the Jewish authorities for washing before and after meals. </p>
<p>After the manner of the purifying, etc. That is, for the purifications customary among the Jews. </p>
<p>Containing [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χωρουσαι] </span></span>. From cwrov, a place or space. Hence, to make room or give place, and so, to have space or room for holding something. Firkins [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετρητας] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. From metrew, to measure; and therefore, properly, a measurer. A liquid measure containing nearly nine gallons.</p>
