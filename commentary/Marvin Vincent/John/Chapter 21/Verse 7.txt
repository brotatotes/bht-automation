<p>Fisher's coat [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επενδυτην] </span></span>. An upper garment or blouse. Only here in the New Testament. In the Septuagint, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1sa+18:4">1 Samuel 18:4</span>, the robe which Jonathan gave to David. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2sa+13:18">2 Samuel 13:18</span>, the royal virgin garment of Tamar. The kindred verb, ejpenduomai, occurs twice (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:2">2 Corinthians 5:2</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:4">2 Corinthians 5:4</span>), meaning "to be clothed upon," with the house which is from heaven, i e., clothed as with an upper garment. See on that passage. </p>
<p>Naked. Not absolutely, but clothed merely in his undergarment or shirt.</p>
