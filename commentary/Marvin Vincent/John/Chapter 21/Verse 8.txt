<p>A little ship [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τω πλοιαριω] </span></span>. The noun is diminutive. Rev., the little boat. It is hardly probable that this refers to a smaller boat accompanying the vessel. Compare the alternation of ploion and ploiarion in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:17">John 6:17</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:19">John 6:19</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:21">John 6:21</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:22">John 6:22</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:24">John 6:24</span>. </p>
<p>Two hundred cubits. A little over a hundred yards. </p>
<p>With fishes [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των ιχθυων] </span></span>. Or, the net of the fishes. So Wyc, Rev., full of fishes.</p>
