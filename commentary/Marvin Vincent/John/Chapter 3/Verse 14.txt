<p>Must [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δει] </span></span>. Must signifies the eternal necessity in the divine counsels. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:26">Luke 24:26</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:46">Luke 24:46</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:54">Matthew 26:54</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:31">Mark 8:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:34">John 12:34</span>. Lifted up [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υψωθηναι] </span></span>. The following are the uses of the word in the New Testament : The exaltation of pride (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:23">Matthew 11:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:15">Luke 10:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:11">Luke 14:11</span>). The raising of the humble (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:52">Luke 1:52</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:10">James 4:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:6">1 Peter 5:6</span>). The exaltation of Christ in glory (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:33">Acts 2:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:31">Acts 5:31</span>). The uplifting on the cross (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:14">John 3:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:28">John 8:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:32">John 12:32</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:34">John 12:34</span>). The reference here is to the crucifixion, but beyond that, to the glorification of Christ. It is characteristic of John to blend the two ideas of Christ 's passion and glory (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:28">John 8:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:32">John 12:32</span>). Thus, when Judas went out to betray him, Jesus said, "Now is the Son of man glorified" (xiii. 31). Hence the believer overcomes the world through faith in Him who came not by water only, but by water and blood (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:4-6">1 John 5:4-6</span>).</p>
