<p>Sent [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεστειλεν] </span></span>. See on 1 6. Sent rather than gave (ver. 16), because the idea of sacrifice is here merged in that of authoritative commission. </p>
<p>His Son. The best texts read ton, the, for aujtou, his. </p>
<p>Condemn [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρινη] </span></span>. Better, as Rev., judge. Condemn is katakrinw, not used by John (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:18">Matthew 20:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:33">Mark 10:33</span>, etc.). The verb krinw means, originally, to separate. So Homer, of Ceres separating the grain from the chaff (" Iliad, " 5 501). Thence, to distinguish, to pick out, to be of opinion, to judge. See on Hypocrite, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:13">Matthew 23:13</span>. </p>
<p>World. The threefold repetition of the word has a certain solemnity. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:10">John 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:19">John 14:19</span>.</p>
