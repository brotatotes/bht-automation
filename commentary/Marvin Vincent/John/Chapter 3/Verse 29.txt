<p>The bride. A common figure in the Old testament prophecies, of the relation between Jehovah and His people (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eze+16:0">Ezekiel 16:0</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ho+2:19">Hosea 2:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mal+2:11">Malachi 2:11</span>). See also on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+1:21">Matthew 1:21</span>, concerning Hosea. </p>
<p>Friend of the bridegroom. Or groomsman. The term is appropriate to Judaea, the groomsmen not being customary in Galilee. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:15">Matthew 9:15</span>, where the phrase children of the bridechamber is used. (See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:19">Mark 2:19</span>). In Judaea there were two groomsmen, one for the bridegroom, the other for his bride.
