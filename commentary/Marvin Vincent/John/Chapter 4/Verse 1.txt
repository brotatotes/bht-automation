Therefore. Pointing back to John 3:22, and the controversy which arose about the two baptisms. The Lord. See on Matthew 21:3. Knew [εγνω] , or perceived. See on 2 24. Pharisees. John never alludes to the Sadducees by name. The Pharisees represented the opposition to Jesus, the most powerful and dangerous of the Jewish sects. Made and baptized. Both verbs are in the present tense. The narrator puts himself at the scene of the story : is making and baptizing.
