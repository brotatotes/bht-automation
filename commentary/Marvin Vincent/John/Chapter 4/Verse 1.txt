<p>Therefore. Pointing back to <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:22">John 3:22</span>, and the controversy which arose about the two baptisms. </p>
<p>The Lord. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:3">Matthew 21:3</span>. </p>
<p>Knew [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγνω] </span></span>, or perceived. See on 2 24. </p>
<p>Pharisees. John never alludes to the Sadducees by name. The Pharisees represented the opposition to Jesus, the most powerful and dangerous of the Jewish sects. </p>
<p>Made and baptized. Both verbs are in the present tense. The narrator puts himself at the scene of the story : is making and baptizing.</p>
