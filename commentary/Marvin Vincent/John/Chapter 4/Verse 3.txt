<p>He left [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφηκε] </span></span>. The verb means literally to send away, dismiss. It is used of forgiving offenses (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:43">Matthew 6:43</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:15">James 5:15</span>); of yielding up (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:50">Matthew 27:50</span>); of letting alone (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:14">Matthew 19:14</span>); of allowing or permitting (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:12">Luke 6:12</span>). See on these passages. Its employment here is peculiar. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:28">John 16:28</span>, of Christ 's leaving the world. </p>
