<p>Meat (brwsin). Originally the act of eating (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+2:16">Colossians 2:16</span>), but often of that which is eaten. A parallel is found in the vulgar phrase, a thing is good or poor eating. The word is always used by Paul in its original sense. </p>
<p>Know not of [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ οιδατε] </span></span>. Incorrect. Rev., rightly, ye know not; i e., you do not know its virtue.</p>
