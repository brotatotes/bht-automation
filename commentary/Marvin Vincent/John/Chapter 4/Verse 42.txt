<p>Said [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεγον] </span></span>. The imperfect tense : said to the woman as they successively met her. </p>
<p>Saying [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαλιαν] </span></span>. Another word is designedly substituted for logon, word (vv. 39, 41). In ver. 39 logov, word, is used of the woman, from the Evangelist 's standpoint, as being a testimony to Christ. Here the Samaritans distinguish between the more authoritative and dignified word of Jesus, and the talk of the woman. Rev., speaking. Compare the kindred verb lalew, in vv. 26, 27; also <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:43">John 8:43</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:73">Matthew 26:73</span>. </p>
<p>The Christ. The best texts omit. </p>
<p>The Savior [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο σωτηρ] </span></span>. John uses the word only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:14">1 John 4:14</span>. See on Jesus, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+1:21">Matthew 1:21</span>. It is significant that this conception of Christ should have been first expressed by a Samaritan.</p>
