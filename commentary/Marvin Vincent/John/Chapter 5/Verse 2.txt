<p>Sheep - market [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη προβατικη] </span></span>. The word is an adjective pertaining to sheep, which requires to be completed with another word, not with ajgora, market, but with pulh, gate. This gate was near the temple on the east of the city. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ne+3:1">Nehemiah 3:1</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ne+3:32">Nehemiah 3:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ne+12:39">Nehemiah 12:39</span>. Some editors join the adjective with the following kolumbhqra, pool, making the latter word kolumbhqra (the dative case), and reading the sheep - pool. Wyc., a standing water of beasts. </p>
<p>Pool [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολυμβηθρα] </span></span>. In the New Testament only in this chapter and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:7">John 9:7</span>, </p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:1">John 5:1</span>1Properly, a pool for swimming, from kolumbaw, to dive. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ec+2:6">Ecclesiastes 2:6</span> (Sept.,) it is used of a reservoir in a garden. The Hebrew word is from the verb to kneel down, and means, therefore, a kneeling - place for cattle or men when drinking. In ecclesiastical language, the baptismal font, and the baptistery itself. </p>
<p>Called [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιλεγομενη] </span></span>. Strictly, surnamed, the name having perhaps supplanted some earlier name. </p>
<p>Bethesda [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βηθεσδα] </span></span>. Commonly interpreted House of Mercy; others House of the Portico. The readings also vary. Tischendorf and Westcott and Hort give bhqzaqa, Bethzatha, House of the Olive. The site cannot be identified with any certainty. Dr. Robinson thinks it may be the Fountain of the Virgin, the upper fountain of Siloam. See Thomson's "Land and Book," " Southern Palestine and Jerusalem, " pp. 458 - 461. </p>
<p>Porches [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στοας] </span></span>. Cloisters, covered porticoes.</p>
