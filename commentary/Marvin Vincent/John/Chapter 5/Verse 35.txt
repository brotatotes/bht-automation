<p>A burning and shining light [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο λυχνος ο καιομενος και φαινων] </span></span>. Rev., correctly, the lamp that burneth and shineth. Lucnov, lamp, as contrasted with the light [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φως] </span></span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:5">John 1:5</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:7">John 1:7</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:8">John 1:8</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:9">John 1:9</span>; and compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:12">John 8:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:5">John 9:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:46">John 12:46</span>. Wyc., lantern. The Baptist did not, like Jesus, shine by his own light. The definite article with lamp, points to it as a familiar household object. Burning hints at the fact that the lamp gives but a transitory light. In burning the oil is consumed. </p>
<p>Ye were willing. Again the emphatic uJmeiv, ye. </p>
<p>To rejoice [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγαλλιασθηναι] </span></span>. The word signifies exultant, lively joy. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:12">Matthew 5:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:47">Luke 1:47</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:21">Luke 10:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:6">1 Peter 1:6</span>. The interest in the Baptist was a frivolous, superficial, and short - lived excitement. Bengel says, "they were attracted by his brightness, not by his warmth."</p>
