<p>Ye believe. Again the emphatic ye, the reason for the emphasis being given in the succeeding clause. </p>
<p>Which receive [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαμβανοντες] </span></span>. Literally, receiving (as ye do) : seeing that ye receive. </p>
<p>Seek not the honor that cometh from God only [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">και την δοξαν την μονου θεου ου ζητειτε] </span></span>. The Rev. gives it capitally, following the Greek order : and the glory that cometh from the only God ye seek not. Not God only, which entirely overlooks the force of the definite article; but the only God. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:15">1 Timothy 6:15</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:16">1 Timothy 6:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:3">John 17:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:27">Romans 16:27</span>.</p>
