<p>Had rowed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εληλακοτες] </span></span>. Literally, had driven or propelled (the boat). </p>
<p>Five and twenty, etc. The lake being about forty furlongs, six miles, at its broadest, they had gone only a little more than half - way. </p>
<p>They see [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεωρουσι] </span></span>. Rev., behold; with an intent gaze. See on 1 18. Both Luke and John use this word frequently. </p>
<p>Drawing nigh. Literally, becoming nigh. Wyc., to be made next to the boat. Mark adds, He would have passed by them, and Luke that they thought Him a phantom.</p>
