The Father 's will. Omit the Father 's. Render, the will of Him, etc. That of all which He hath given me [ινα παν ο δεδωκε μοι] . The construction is a peculiar and broken one. All which He hath given, stands alone as an absolute nominative; a very emphatic and impressive mode of statement. Literally it reads, that all which He hath given me I should lose nothing out of it. At the last day [εν τη εσχατη ημερα] . The phrase occurs only in John.
