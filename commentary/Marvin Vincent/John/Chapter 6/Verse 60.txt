<p>Hard [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκληρος] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:24">Matthew 25:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:14">Jude 1:14</span>. According to the Greek order, hard is this saying. </p>
<p>Hear it [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυτου ακουειν] </span></span>. Aujtou may be rendered Him, but this is not probable. Hear means a docile hearing, with a view to receiving what is heard. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:3">John 10:3</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:16">John 10:16</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:27">John 10:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:47">John 12:47</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:37">John 18:37</span>.</p>
