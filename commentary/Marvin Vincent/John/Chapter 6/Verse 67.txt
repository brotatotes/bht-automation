<p>The twelve. John assumes that the number is known. It is implied in the twelve baskets of fragments. As in so many other instances in this Gospel, facts of the synoptic narrative are taken for granted as familiar. Will ye also go away? [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη και υμεις θελετε υπαγειν] </span></span>. The interrogative particle mh shows that a negative answer is expected. Surely ye will not. Will ye go is not the future tense of the verb to go, but is expressed by two words, do ye will [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θελετε] </span></span>, to go away [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπαγειν] </span></span>. Rev., would ye. On the verb to go away, see on they went (ver. 21).</p>
