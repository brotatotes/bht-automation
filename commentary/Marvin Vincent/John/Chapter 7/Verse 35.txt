Will He go [ουτος μελλει πορευεσθαι] . Literally, whither does this man intend to go, or whither is He thinking of going? The A. V. misses the contemptuous insinuation in this man (Rev.). We shall not find him [ημεις] . The pronoun is emphatic; we, the religious leaders, the wise men, who scrutinize the claims of all professed teachers and keep a watchful eye on all impostors. The dispersed among the Gentiles [την διασποραν των ελληνων] . Literally, the dispersion of the Greeks. The Jews who remained in foreign lands after the return from the Captivity were called by two names : 1. The Captivity, which was expressed in Greek by three words, viz., ajpoikia, a settlement far from home, which does not occur in the New Testament; metoikesia, change of abode, which is found in Matthew 1:11, Matthew 1:12, Matthew 1:17, and always of the carrying into Babylon; aijcmalwsia, a taking at the point of the spear; Ephesians 4:8; Revelation 13:10. 2. The Dispersion [διασπορα] . See on 1 Peter 1:1; James 1:1. The first name marks their relation to their own land; the second to the strange lands. The Gentiles (%Ellhnav). Literally, the Greeks. So Rev. See on Acts 6:1.
