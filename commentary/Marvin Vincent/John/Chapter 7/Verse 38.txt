<p>The scripture hath said. There is no exactly corresponding passage, but the quotation harmonizes with the general tenor of several passages, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+55:1">Isaiah 55:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+58:11">Isaiah 58:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="zec+13:1">Zechariah 13:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="zec+14:8">Zechariah 14:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eze+47:1">Ezekiel 47:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joe+3:18">Joel 3:18</span>. Belly [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κοιλιας] </span></span>. The word is often used in the Old Testament for the innermost part of a man, the soul or heart. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="job+14:35">Job 14:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="job+32:19">Job 32:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="pr+18:8">Proverbs 18:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="pr+20:27">Proverbs 20:27</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="pr+20:30">Proverbs 20:30</span>. The rite of drawing and pouring out the water pointed back to the smitten rock in the desert. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+17:6">Exodus 17:6</span>, "there shall come water out of it," is literally, "there shall come water from within him." The word belly here means the inmost heart of the believer, which pours forth spiritual refreshment. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:4">1 Corinthians 10:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:14">John 4:14</span>. </p>
<p>Shall flow [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρευσουσιν] </span></span>. The word occurs only here in the New Testament. </p>
<p>Rivers. A type of abundance. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="nu+20:11">Numbers 20:11</span>. </p>
<p>Living water. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:10">John 4:10</span>.</p>
