<p>Ye are from beneath [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ των κατω εστε] </span></span>. A phrase peculiar to John and to his Gospel. Jesus states the radical antagonism between His opposers and Himself, as based upon difference of origin and nature. They spring from the lower, sensual, earthly economy; He from the heavenly. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:15">James 3:15</span> sqq. </p>
<p>From above [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ των ανω] </span></span>. Also peculiar to John's Gospel. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+3:1">Colossians 3:1</span>. On the phrase to be of [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειναι εκ</span></span>] see on 1 46. </p>
<p>Ye are of this world [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ του κοσμου τουτου εστε] </span></span>. Peculiar to John, and occurring in the First Epistle. On kosmou, world, see on 1 9. Ye are of this earthly order or economy.</p>
