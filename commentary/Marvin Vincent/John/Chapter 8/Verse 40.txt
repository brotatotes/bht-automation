<p>A man [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωπον] </span></span>. Used only here by the Lord of Himself. To this corresponds His calling the Devil a manslayer at ver. 44. Perhaps, too, as Westcott remarks, it may suggest the idea of the human sympathy which, as a man, He was entitled to claim from them. </p>
<p>This did not Abraham. In the oriental traditions Abraham is spoken of as "full of loving - kindness."</p>
