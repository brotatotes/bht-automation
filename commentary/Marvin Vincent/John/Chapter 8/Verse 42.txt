<p>I proceeded forth - from God [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ του θεου εξηλθον] </span></span>. Rev., came forth. The phrase occurs only here and in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:28">John 16:28</span>. Exelqein is found in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:3">John 13:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:30">John 16:30</span>, and emphasizes the idea of separation; a going from God to whom He was to return (and goeth unto God). Exelqein para (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:27">John 16:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:8">John 17:8</span>), is going from beside, implying personal fellowship with God. Exelqein ejk, here, emphasizes the idea of essential, community of being : </p>
<p>"I came forth out of." </p>
<p>And am come [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκω] </span></span>. As much as to say, and here I am. </p>
<p>Of myself [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απ εμαυτου] </span></span>. Of my own self - determination, independently, but my being is divinely derived. See on 7 17.</p>
