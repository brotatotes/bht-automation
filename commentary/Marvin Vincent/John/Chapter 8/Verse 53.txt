<p>Art thou [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μη συ] </span></span>. Thou, emphatic, and the negative interrogative particle implying a negative answer, thou art not surely greater. </p>
<p>Which is dead [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οστις] </span></span>. The compound pronoun ostiv, which, is used explicatively, according to a familiar New Testament usage, instead of the simple relative. The sense is, seeing that he is dead. The compound relative properly indicates the class or kind to which an object belongs. Art thou greater than Abraham, who is himself one of the dead? So <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+3:5">Colossians 3:5</span>. "Mortify covetousness, seeing it is [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ητις εστιν</span></span>] idolatry."
