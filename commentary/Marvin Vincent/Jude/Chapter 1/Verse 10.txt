<p>Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:12">2 Peter 2:12</span>. </p>
<p>They know not [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ οιδασιν] </span></span>. Mental comprehension and knowledge, and referring to the whole range of invisible things; while the other verb in this verse, also translated by A. V. know (ejpistantai, originally of skill in handicraft), refers to palpable things; objects of sense; the circumstances of sensual enjoyment. Rev. marks the distinction by rendering the latter verb understand. </p>
<p>Naturally [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυσικως] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. Compare fusika, natural, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:12">2 Peter 2:12</span>.</p>
