<p>Raging [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγρια] </span></span>. Rev., wild, which is better, as implying quality rather than act. Waves, by nature untamed. The act or expression of the nature is given by the next word. </p>
<p>Foaming out [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαφριζοντα] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+57:20">Isaiah 57:20</span>. </p>
<p>Shame [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αισχυνας] </span></span>. Lit., shames or disgraces. </p>
<p>Wandering stars. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:17">2 Peter 2:17</span>. Possibly referring to comets, which shine a while and then pass into darkness. "They belong, not to the system : they stray at random and without law, and must at last be severed from the lights which rule while they are ruled" (Lumby). </p>
<p>Blackness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζοφος] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:4">2 Peter 2:4</span>. </p>
<p>Of darkness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του σκοτους] </span></span>. Lit., "the darkness," the article pointing back to the darkness already mentioned, ver. 6.</p>
