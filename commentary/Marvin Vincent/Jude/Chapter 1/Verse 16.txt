<p>Murmurers [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γογγυσται] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. Doubtless, originally, with some adaptation of sound to sense, gongustai. It is used of the cooling of doves. </p>
<p>Complainers [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεμψιμοιροι] </span></span>. From memfomai, to find fault with, and moira, a part or lot. Lit., blamers of their lot. </p>
<p>Great swelling words. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:18">2 Peter 2:18</span>. </p>
<p>Having men's persons in admiration [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θαυμαζοντες προσωπα] </span></span>. The Rev., shewing respect of persons, is neater, but the A. V. more literal : admiring the countenances. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+19:21">Genesis 19:21</span>, Sept., "I have accepted thee :" lit., have admired thy face. </p>
<p>Because of advantage. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:3">2 Peter 2:3</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:14">2 Peter 2:14</span>. </p>
<p>Beloved. Compare ver. 3.</p>
