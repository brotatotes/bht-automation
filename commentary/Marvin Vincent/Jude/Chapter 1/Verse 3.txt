<p>Beloved. Occurring at the beginning of an epistle only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:2">3 John 1:2</span>. </p>
<p>When I gave all diligence [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πασαν σπουδην ποιουμενος] </span></span>. Lit., making all diligence; the phrase found only here. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:11">Hebrews 6:11</span>, we find "shew diligence" [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενδεικνυσθαι] </span></span>; and in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:5">2 Peter 1:5</span>, "adding diligence." See note there. </p>
<p>The common salvation. The best texts add hJmwn, of us. So Rev., "our common salvation." </p>
<p>It was needful [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναγκην εσχον] </span></span>. Lit., I had necessity. Alford, I found it necessary. Rev., I was constrained. </p>
<p>Earnestly contend [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επαγωνιζεσθαι] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. </p>
<p>The faith. The sum of what Christians believe. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:7">Acts 6:7</span>. </p>
<p>Once [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαξ] </span></span>. Nor formerly, but once for all. So Rev., "No other faith will be given," says Bengel.</p>
