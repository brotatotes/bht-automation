<p>With the whole verse compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:1">2 Peter 2:1</span>. </p>
<p>Crept in unawares [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρεισεδυσαν] </span></span>. Rev., privily. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:1">2 Peter 2:1</span>. The verb means to get in by the side [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρα] </span></span>, to slip in by a side - door. Only here in New Testament. </p>
<p>Ordained [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προγεγραμμενοι] </span></span>. The meaning is in dispute. The word occurs four times in New Testament. In two of these instances pro has clearly the temporal sense before (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:4">Romans 14:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:3">Ephesians 3:3</span>). In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:1">Galatians 3:1</span>, it is taken by some in the sense of openly, publicly (see note there). It seems better, on the whole, to take it here in the temporal sense, and to render written of beforehand, i e., in prophecy as referred to in vv. 14, 15. So the American Rev. </p>
<p>Lasciviousness. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:3">1 Peter 4:3</span>. </p>
<p>Lord God. God is omitted in the best texts. On Lord [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεσποτην] </span></span>, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:1">2 Peter 2:1</span>.</p>
