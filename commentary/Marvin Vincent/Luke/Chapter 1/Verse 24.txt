<p>Conceived [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνελαβεν] </span></span>. Mr. Hobart (" Medical Language of Luke ") says that the number of words referring to pregnancy, barrenness, etc., used by Luke, is almost as large as that used by Hippocrates. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:31">Luke 1:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:24">Luke 1:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:5">Luke 2:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:7">Luke 1:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:28">Luke 20:28</span>. All of these, except <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:24">Luke 1:24</span>, are peculiar to himself, and all, of course, in common use among medical writers. Hid [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιεκρυβεν] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. Peri signifies completely; entire seclusion.</p>
