<p>Having had perfect understanding [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρηκολουθηκοτι] </span></span>. Incorrect. The verb means to follow closely, and hence to trace accurately. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:10">2 Timothy 3:10</span>, where Rev. reads thou didst follow for thou hast fully known. Rev. renders here having traced the course. The word occurs frequently in medical writings, and sometimes, as here, with ajkribwv, accurately. Tynd., having searched out diligently. </p>
<p>From the very first [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανωθεν] </span></span>. Lit., from above; the events being conceived in a descending series. </p>
<p>Accurately [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακριβως] </span></span>. From akron, the highest or farthest point. Hence to trace down to the last and minutest detail. In order [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθεξης] </span></span>. Used by Luke only.</p>
