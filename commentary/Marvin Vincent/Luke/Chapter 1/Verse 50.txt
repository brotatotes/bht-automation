<p>Mercy [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεος] </span></span>. The word emphasizes the misery with which grace (see on verse 30) deals; hence, peculiarly the sense of human wretchedness coupled with the impulse to relieve it, which issues in gracious ministry. Bengel remarks, "Grace takes away the fault, mercy the misery." </p>
<p>From generation to generation [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις γενεας και γενεας] </span></span>. Lit., as Rev., unto generations and generations. </p>
<p>Fear [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φοβουμενος] </span></span>. The word is used in both a good and a bad sense in the New Testament. For the latter, see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:46">Matthew 21:46</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:20">Mark 6:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:32">Mark 11:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:4">Luke 12:4</span>. For the former, as here, in the sense of godly reverence, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:2">Acts 10:2</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:22">Acts 10:22</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:35">Acts 10:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+3:22">Colossians 3:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+14:7">Revelation 14:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+14:4">Revelation 14:4</span>.</p>
