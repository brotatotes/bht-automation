Hath holpen [αντελαβετο] . The verb means to lay hold on : thence to grasp helpfully or to help. To lay hold in the sense of partaking (1 Timothy 6:2), carries us back to the primitive meaning of the word according to its composition : to receive instead of, or in return [αντι] , and suggests the old phrase to take up for, espouse the cause of. Wyc., has took up, but probably not in this sense. Servant [παιδος] . Often child, son or daughter, but here servant, in allusion to Isaiah 41:8. Meyer truthfully says that the theocratic notion of sonship is never expressed by paiv. See Rev., Acts 3:13, Acts 3:26; Acts 4:27, Acts 4:30.
