Writing table [πινακιδιον] . Table was formerly used in the sense of tablet. Thus Shakspeare : "Yea, from the table of my memory, I'll wipe away all trivial fond records." Hamlet, 1, 5. Tynd., writing tables. The meaning is a little writing tablet, probably covered with wax. Only here in the New Testament. Used by medical writers of a physician 's note book. Wyc., has a poyntel, i e., a style for writing. Wrote, saying. A Hebrew form of expression. See 2 Kings 10:6.
