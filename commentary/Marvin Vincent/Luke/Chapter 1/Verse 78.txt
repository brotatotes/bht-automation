<p>mercy [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σπλαγχνα ελεους] </span></span>. Lit., bowels of mercy. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:8">1 Peter 3:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:11">James 5:11</span>. Rev. gives heart of mercy in margin. Wyc., frightfully, entrails of mercy. </p>
<p>The day - spring from on high [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανατολη εξ υψους] </span></span>. Lit., the rising. The word occurs in the Septuagint as a rendering of branch, as something rising or springing up, by which the Messiah is denoted (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+23:5">Jeremiah 23:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="zec+6:12">Zechariah 6:12</span>). Also of the rising of a heavenly body (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+60:19">Isaiah 60:19</span>, Sept.). Compare the kindred verb arise [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανατελλω</span></span>] in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+60:1">Isaiah 60:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mal+4:2">Malachi 4:2</span>. This latter is the sense here. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:2">Matthew 2:2</span>. Wyc. has he springing up from on high. </p>
<p>Hath visited [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεσκεψατο] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:36">Matthew 25:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:12">1 Peter 2:12</span>. Some, however, read ejpiskeyetai, shall visit. So Rev.</p>
