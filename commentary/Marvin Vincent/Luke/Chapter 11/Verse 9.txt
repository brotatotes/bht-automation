<p>Ask [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αιτειτε] </span></span>. The word for the asking of an inferior (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:20">Acts 12:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:2">Acts 3:2</span>); and hence of man from God (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:7">Matthew 7:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:5">James 1:5</span>). Christ never uses the word of his own asking from the Father, but always ejrwtw, as asking on equal terms. Martha shows her low conception of his person when she uses the term of his asking God (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:22">John 11:22</span>). 8 Ask, seek, knock. "The three repetitions of the command are more than mere repetitions; since to seek is more than to ask, and to knock than to seek" (Trench, " Parables ").</p>
