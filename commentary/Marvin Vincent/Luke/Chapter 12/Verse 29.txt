<p>Be ye of doubtful mind [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετεωριζεσθε] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. The verb primarily means to raise to a height; buoy up, as with false hopes; and so to unsettle, or excite, or keep in fluctuation. Thus Thucydides says of the war between Athens and Sparta : "All Hellas was excited [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μετεωρος</span></span>] by the coming conflict between the two chief cities" (ii. 8).</p>
