<p>Strive. Used only by Luke and Paul, except <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:36">John 18:36</span>. Originally to contend for a prize in the public games; and thus conveying a sense of struggle. The kindred noun, ajgwnia, agony, is used of Christ 's struggle in Gethsemane (ch. 22 44). Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:12">1 Timothy 6:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:7">2 Timothy 4:7</span>. </p>
<p>Strait gate [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στενης θυρας] </span></span>. Rev., narrow door. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:13">Matthew 7:13</span>. The door of a house, and not a gate, is meant (ver. 25). In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:13">Matthew 7:13</span>, where the image is of a gate opening into a way, pulh, gate, is used.</p>
