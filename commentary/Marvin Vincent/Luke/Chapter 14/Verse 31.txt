To make war against another king [ετερω βασιλει συμβαλειν εις πολεμον] . Lit., to come together with another king for war. So Rev., to encounter another king in war. "Out he flashed, And into such a song, such fire for fame, Such trumpet - blowings in it, coming down To such a stern and iron - clashing close, That when he stopped we longed to hurl together." TENNYSON, Idyls of the King. With ten thousand (ejn deka ciliasin). Lit., in ten thousands : i e., in the midst of; surrounded by. Compare Jude 1:14.
