<p>Beggar. See on poor, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:3">Matthew 5:3</span>. </p>
<p>Lazarus. Abbreviated from 'Eleazarov, Eleazar, and meaning God a help. "It is a striking evidence of the deep impression which this parable has made on the mind of Christendom, that the term lazar should have passed into so many languages as it has, losing altogether its signification as a proper name" (Trench). </p>
<p>Was laid [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβεβλητο] </span></span>. Lit., was thrown : cast carelessly down by his bearers and left there. </p>
<p>Gate [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πυλωνα] </span></span>. The gateway, often separated from the house or temple. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:71">Matthew 26:71</span>, it is rendered porch. </p>
<p>Full of sores [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειλκωμενος] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. The regular medical term for to be ulcerated. John uses the kindred noun elkov, an ulcer (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+16:2">Revelation 16:2</span>). See next verse.</p>
