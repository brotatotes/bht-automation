<p>Stood [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σατθεις] </span></span>. Lit., having been placed. Took his stand. It implies taking up his position ostentatiously; striking an attitude. But no necessarily in a bad sense. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:8">Luke 19:8</span>; and compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:20">Acts 5:20</span>. Standing was the ordinary posture of the Jews in prayer. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:5">Matthew 6:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:25">Mark 11:25</span>. </p>
<p>Prayed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσηυχετο] </span></span>. Imperfect : began to pray, or proceeded to pray. Other men [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οι λοιποι των ανθρωπων] </span></span>. Lit., the rest of men. See on ver. </p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:0">Luke 18:0</span>:9A Jewish saying is quoted that a true Rabbin ought to thank God every day of his life; 1, that he was not created a Gentile; 2, that he was not a plebeian; 3, that he was not born a woman. </p>
<p>Extortioners. As the publicans. </p>
<p>This publican. Lit., this (one), the publican. This publican here. "He lets us see, even in the general enumeration, that he is thinking of the publican, so, afterward, he does not omit directly to mention him" (Goebel).</p>
