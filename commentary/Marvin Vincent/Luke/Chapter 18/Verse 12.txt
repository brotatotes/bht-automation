<p>Twice in the week. The law required only one fast in the year, that on the great day of Atonement (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+16:29">Leviticus 16:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="nu+29:7">Numbers 29:7</span>); though public memorial fasts were added, during the Captivity, on the anniversaries of national calamities. The Pharisees fasted every Monday and Thursday during the weeks between the Passover and Pentecost, and again between the Feast of Tabernacles and that of the Dedication of the Temple. </p>
<p>I give tithes [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποδεκατω] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:23">Matthew 23:23</span>. </p>
<p>Possess [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κτωμαι] </span></span>. Wrong. The Israelite did not pay tithes of his possessions, but only of his gains - his annual increase. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+28:22">Genesis 28:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+14:22">Deuteronomy 14:22</span>. Besides, the verb, in the present tense, does not mean to possess, but to acquire; the meaning possess being confined to the perfect and pluperfect. Rev., get. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:9">Matthew 10:9</span> (Rev.); <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:28">Acts 22:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:19">Luke 21:19</span> (on which see note); <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:4">1 Thessalonians 4:4</span> (Rev.).</p>
