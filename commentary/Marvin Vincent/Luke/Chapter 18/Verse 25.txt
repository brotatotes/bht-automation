Camel. See on Matthew 19:24. To go through the eye of a needle [δια τρηματος βελονης εισελθειν] . Rev., more literally, to enter in through a needle 's eye. Both Matthew and Mark use another word for needle [ραφις] ; see on Mark 10:25. Luke alone has belonh, which, besides being an older term, is the peculiar word for the surgical needle. The other word is condemned by the Greek grammatrians as barbarous.
