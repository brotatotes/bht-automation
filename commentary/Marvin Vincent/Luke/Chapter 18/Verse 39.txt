<p>Cried [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκραζεν] </span></span>. A stronger word than ejbohsen, cried, in the previous verse, which is merely to cry or shout, while this is to cry clamorously; to scream or shriek. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:23">Matthew 14:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:5">Mark 5:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:28-34">Acts 19:28-34</span>. </p>
<p>To be brought unto [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αχθηναι προς] </span></span>. Used by Luke alone in the sense of bringing the sick to Christ. He also uses the compound verb prsoagw, which was a common medical term for bringing the sick to a physician, both in that and in other senses. See ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:41">Luke 9:41</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:20">Acts 16:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:27">Acts 27:27</span>. ===<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:0">Luke 19:0</span> </p>
