<p>Stood [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σταθεις] </span></span>. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:11">Luke 18:11</span>. Describing a formal act, as of one who is about to make a solemn declaration. He was like the Pharisee in attitude, but not in spirit. The more formal word for standing, applied to the Pharisee in the temple, is here used of the publican. </p>
<p>I give. Not, It is my practice to give. Zacchaeus' statement is not a vindication, but a vow. "I now give by way of restoration." </p>
<p>If I have taken anything by false accusation [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ει τι εσυκοφαντησα] </span></span>. If - anything does not state a merely possible case, as if Zacchaeus were unconscious of any such extortion; but is a milder way of saying "Whatever I have taken." See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:14">Luke 3:14</span>. It is an odd coincidence, nothing more, that the fig - mulberry (sycamore) should occur in connection with the fig - shewer (sycophant). It was common for the publicans to put a fictitious value on property or income, or to advance the tax to those unable to pay, and then to charge usurious interest on the private debt. On the harsh exaction of such debts, see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:28">Matthew 18:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:58">Luke 12:58</span>. </p>
<p>Fourfold. The restoration required of a thief (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+22:1">Exodus 22:1</span>).</p>
