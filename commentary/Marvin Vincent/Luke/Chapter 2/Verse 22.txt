<p>The days of her purification [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αι ημεραι του καθαρισμου αυτης] </span></span>. The A. V. follows the reading aujthv, her : but all the best texts read aujtwn, their; the plural including Joseph with Mary as partaking of the ceremonial defilement. The mother of a child was levitically unclean for forty days after the birth of a son, and for eighty days after the birth of a daughter. Women on this errand commonly rode to the temple on oxen; that the body of so large a beast between them and the ground might prevent any chance of defilement from passing over a sepulchre on the road. For details, see Edersheim, "Life and Times of Jesus," 1, 195; "The Temple," page 302; Geikie, "Life and Words of Christ," 1, 127. </p>
