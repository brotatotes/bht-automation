<p>Devout [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευλαβης] </span></span>. Used by Luke only. The kindred word, eujlabeia, godly fear, occurs twice : <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:7">Hebrews 5:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:28">Hebrews 12:28</span>. From eu, well, and lambanw, to take hold of. Hence of a circumspect or cautious person who takes hold of things carefully. As applied to morals and religion, it emphasizes the element of circumspection, a cautious, careful observance of divine law; and is thus peculiarly expressive of Old Testament piety, with its minute attention to precept and ceremony. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:5">Acts 2:5</span>. Consolation of Israel. Compare hope of Israel, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:20">Acts 28:20</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+41:1">Isaiah 41:1</span>. The Messianic blessing of the nation. Of the Messiah himself, Rest. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+11:10">Isaiah 11:10</span>. A common form of adjuration among the Jews was, So may I see the consolation.</p>
