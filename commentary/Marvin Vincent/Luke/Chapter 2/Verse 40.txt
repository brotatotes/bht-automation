<p>The child grew, etc. The Jews marked the stages of a child 's development by nine different terms : the new born babe (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+9:6">Isaiah 9:6</span>); the suckling (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+11:8">Isaiah 11:8</span>); the suckling beginning to ask for food (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="la+4:4">Lamentations 4:4</span>); the weaned child (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+28:9">Isaiah 28:9</span>); the child clinging to its mother (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+40:7">Jeremiah 40:7</span>); the child becoming firm and strong (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+7:14">Isaiah 7:14</span>, of the virgin mother); the youth, literally, he that shakes himself free; the ripened one, or warrior (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+31:8">Isaiah 31:8</span>).</p>
