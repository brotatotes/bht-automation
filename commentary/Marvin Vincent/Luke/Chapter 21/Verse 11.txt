<p>Earthquakes. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:8">Matthew 13:8</span>. </p>
<p>Famines and pestilences [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λιμοι και λοιμοι] </span></span>. Some texts reverse the order of the words. A paronomasia or combination of like - sounding words : limoi, loimoi. Especially common in Paul 's epistles. </p>
<p>Fearful sights [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φοβητρα] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament, and rare in classical Greek. In Septuagint, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+19:17">Isaiah 19:17</span>. Not confined to sights, but fearful things. Rev., better, terrors. Used in medical language by Hippocrates, of fearful objects imagined by the sick.</p>
