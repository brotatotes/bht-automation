Edge [στοματι] . Lit., the mouth. So Wyc. Either in the sense of the foremost part, or picturing the sword as a devouring monster. In Hebrews 11:33, Hebrews 11:34, the word is used in both senses : "the mouths of lions;" " the edge of the sword. " Led away captive. See on captives, ch. 4 18. Trodden down. Denoting the oppression and contempt which shall follow conquest.
