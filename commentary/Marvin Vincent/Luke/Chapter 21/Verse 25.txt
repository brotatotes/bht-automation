<p>Signs [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σημεια] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:24">Matthew 24:24</span>. </p>
<p>Distress [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνοχη] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:4">2 Corinthians 2:4</span>. Kindred with sunecomenh, taken (ch. 4 38), on which see note. The original idea of the word is being held in a tight grasp. </p>
<p>With perplexity, the sea and the waves roaring. The A. V. follows the reading hjcoushv, the participle, roaring. The proper reading is hjcouv, the noun, the roaring. Render perplexity for the roaring of the sea, etc. 'Hcw, roaring, is properly a returned sound, an echo. Generally a ringing sound, as of the blows on an anvil. </p>
<p>Waves [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σαλου] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. The radical notion of the word is unsteady motion, especially the rolling swell of the sea. Rev., better, billows.</p>
