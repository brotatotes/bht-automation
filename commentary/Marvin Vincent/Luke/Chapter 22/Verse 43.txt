<p>There appeared [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωφθη] </span></span>. The word most commonly used in the New Testament of seeing visions. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:3">Matthew 17:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:4">Mark 9:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:11">Luke 1:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:43">Luke 22:43</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:17">Acts 2:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:35">Acts 7:35</span>. The kindred noun ojptasia, wherever it occurs in the New Testament, means a vision. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:22">Luke 1:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:23">Luke 24:23</span>, etc. </p>
<p>Strengthening [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενισχυων] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:19">Acts 9:19</span>. See on was not able, ch. 14 30; and cannot, ch. 16 3. Commonly intransitive; to prevail in or among. Used transitively only by Hippocrates and Luke.</p>
