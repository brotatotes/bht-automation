<p>They were instant [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεκειντο] </span></span>. Instant, in the sense of urgent, pressing. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:4">Luke 7:4</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:12">Romans 12:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:2">2 Timothy 4:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:4">Luke 7:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:7">Acts 26:7</span>. The verb means to lie upon, and answers to our vulgarism, to lay one's self down to work. Compare Aristophanes, "Knights," 253 : kajpikeimenov boa, roar with all your might. Lit., roar, lying down to it. </p>
<p>Their voices. Omit of the chief priests. </p>
<p>Prevailed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατισχυον] </span></span>. Had power [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ισχυς</span></span>] to bear down [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα</span></span>] the remonstrances of Pilate. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:18">Matthew 16:18</span>.</p>
