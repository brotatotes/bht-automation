<p>Two other. The possible omission of a comma before malefactors in the A. V. might make a very awkward and unpleasant statement. Better Rev., two others, malefactors. </p>
<p>Put to death [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναιρεθηναι] </span></span>. Lit., to take up and carry away; so that the Greek idiom answers to our taken off. So Shakespeare : </p>
<p>"The deep damnation of his taking off." MacBeth, 1, 7. </p>
<p>"Let her who would be rid of him, devise His speedy taking off." Lear, 5, 1.</p>
