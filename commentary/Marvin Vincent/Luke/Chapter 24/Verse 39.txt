<p>Handle [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψηλαφησατε] </span></span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:1">1 John 1:1</span>. The word occurs also <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:27">Acts 17:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:18">Hebrews 12:18</span>. "It never expresses the so handling an object as to exercise a moulding, modifying influence upon it, but at most a feeling of its surface; this, it may be, with the intention of learning its composition (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+27:12">Genesis 27:12</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+27:21">Genesis 27:21</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+27:22">Genesis 27:22</span>); while, not seldom, it signifies no more than a feeling for or after an object, without any actual coming in contact with it at all" (Trench, " Synonyms "). Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:27">Acts 17:27</span>. Used of groping in the dark, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="job+5:14">Job 5:14</span>; of the blind, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+49:10">Isaiah 49:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+28:29">Deuteronomy 28:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:16">Jude 1:16</span>:26. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:18">Hebrews 12:18</span>.</p>
