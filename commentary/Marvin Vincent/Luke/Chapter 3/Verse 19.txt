<p>Being reproved [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεγχομενος] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:9">James 2:9</span>. </p>
<p>Evils [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πονηρων] </span></span>. Of several words in the New Testament denoting evil, this emphasizes evil in its activity. Hence Satan is oJ ponhrov, the evil one. An evil eye (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:22">Mark 7:22</span>) is a mischief working eye. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:22">Mark 7:22</span>. Added [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσεθηκεν] </span></span>. Used by Luke twice as often as in all the rest of the New Testament. A very common medical word, used of the application of remedies to the body, as our apply, administer. So Hippocrates, "apply wet sponges to the head;" and Galen, "apply a decoction of acorns," etc. </p>
