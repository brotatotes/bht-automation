<p>Went abroad [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διηρχετο] </span></span>. Dia, throughout the region. Wyc., the word walked about. </p>
<p>Came together [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνηρχοντο</span></span>] Imperfect. Kept coming together, or were coming. </p>
<p>To be healed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θεραπευεσθαι] </span></span>. Originally, to be an attendant, to do service; and therefore of a physician, to attend upon, or treat medically. In classical writers it has also the meaning to heal, as undoubtedly in the New Testament, and in Luke (xiii. 14; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:14">Acts 4:14</span>, etc.). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:7">Matthew 8:7</span>, and compare ijaomai, to heal, in verse 17. </p>
<p>Infirmities [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασθενειων] </span></span>. A strictly literal rendering; aj, not, and sqenov, strength, exactly answering to the Latin in, not, and firmus, strong.</p>
