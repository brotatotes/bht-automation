<p>Taken with a palsy [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραλελυμενος] </span></span>. Rev., more neatly, palsied. </p>
<p>Whenever Luke mentions this disease, he uses the verb and not the adjective paralutikov, paralytic (as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:24">Matthew 4:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:6">Matthew 8:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:3-10">Mark 2:3-10</span>; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:7">Acts 8:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:33">Acts 9:33</span>); his usage in this respect being in strict accord with that of medical writers.</p>
