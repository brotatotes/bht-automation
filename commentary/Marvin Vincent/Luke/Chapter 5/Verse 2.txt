<p>Ships [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλοια] </span></span>. Used of vessels in general. Some texts read ploiapia, a diminutive form, meaning little boats. </p>
<p>Were washing. From the sand and pebbles accumulated during the night 's work. Luke uses four different words for washing or cleansing : plunw, here, see also <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+7:14">Revelation 7:14</span>; ajpomassw, of wiping the dust from the feet, only at chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:11">Luke 10:11</span>; ejkmassw, of the woman wiping Christ 's feet with her hair, chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:38">Luke 7:38</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:44">Luke 7:44</span>; ajpolouw, of washing away sins, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:16">Acts 22:16</span>; louw, of washing the prisoners ' stripes and the body of Dorcas, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:33">Acts 16:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:37">Acts 9:37</span>. The reading ajpoplunw is rejected by the best texts, so that ajpomassw is the only one peculiar to Luke. All the words were common in medical language.</p>
