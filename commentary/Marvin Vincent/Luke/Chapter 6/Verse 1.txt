<p>The second after the first [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δευτεροπρωτω] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. Many high authorities omit it, and its exact meaning cannot be determined. Rev. omits. </p>
<p>Went through [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαπορευεσθαι] </span></span>. Rev., was going. Compare paraporeuesqai, went along beside - <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:23">Mark 2:23</span>. </p>
<p>Cornfields. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:1">Matthew 12:1</span>. </p>
<p>Plucked [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετιλλον] </span></span>. Imperfect; were plucking, as they walked. In classical Greek the word is used mostly of pulling out hair or feathers. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:23">Mark 2:23</span>. </p>
<p>Did eat [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ησθιον] </span></span>. Imperfect, were eating. </p>
<p>Rubbing [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψωχοντες] </span></span>. The verb means to rub small.</p>
