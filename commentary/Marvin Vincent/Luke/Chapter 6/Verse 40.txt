<p>Perfect [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηρτισμενος] </span></span>. Rev., rendering the participle more literally, perfected. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:21">Matthew 4:21</span>. The word signifies to readjust, restore, set to rights, whether in a physical or a moral sense. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:10">1 Corinthians 1:10</span>, where Paul exhorts to be perfectly joined together [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατηρτισμενοι</span></span>] in opposition to being divided. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:1">Galatians 6:1</span>, it is used of restoring a brother taken in a fault. Hence the meaning to perfect, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:12">Ephesians 4:12</span>. Used in medical language of setting a bone or joint.</p>
