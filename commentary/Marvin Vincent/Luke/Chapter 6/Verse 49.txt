<p>Upon the earth without a foundation. Matthew, upon the sand. The two men are conceived as alike selecting a spot where the sand overlies the rock. The one builds directly upon the sand, the other digs through and down into the rock. </p>
<p>It fell [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεσεν] </span></span>. But the best texts read sunepesen, fell together, collapsed. Rev., fell in. Only here in New Testament. In medical language used of falling - in of parts of the body. Thus Hippocrates, "the temple fallen in : the limb quickly collapses or shrivels." Matthew uses the simple verb epesen, fell. </p>
<p>Ruin [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρηγμα] </span></span>. Lit., breaking. Only here in New Testament. A medical term for a laceration or rupture. Matthew has ptwsiv, the fall. ===<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:0">Luke 7:0</span> </p>
