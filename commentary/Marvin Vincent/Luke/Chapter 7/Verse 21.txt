<p>Diseases - plagues [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νοσωνμαστιγων] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:23">Matthew 4:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:10">Mark 3:10</span>. Marking the two classes of disease recognized in medical writings, chronic and acute. </p>
<p>Evil spirits [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευματων] </span></span>. On ponhrov, evil, see ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:19">Luke 3:19</span>. It is applied to evil spirits by Luke only, with the single exception of <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:45">Matthew 12:45</span>. In accordance with its signification of evil on its active side, it is applied in medicine to that which spreads destruction or corruption; as the poison of serpents. Note, moreover, that Luke distinguishes here between disease and demoniac possession, as often. See ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:17">Luke 6:17</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:18">Luke 6:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:2">Luke 8:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:32">Luke 13:32</span>. He gave [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εχαρισατο] </span></span>. More is expressed by this verb than simple giving. He gave as a free, gracious, joy - giving gift. See on cariv, favor, ch. 1 30; and compare freely give, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:32">Romans 8:32</span>. Also, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:12">1 Corinthians 2:12</span>.</p>
