<p>Gorgeously apparelled [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ιματισμω ενδοξω] </span></span>. Lit., in splendid clothing. </p>
<p>Live delicately [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρυφη υπαρχοντες] </span></span>. Lit., are in luxury. On uJparcontev, are, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:15">James 2:15</span>. On trufh, luxury, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:13">2 Peter 2:13</span>, the only other place where it occurs. Compare the kindred verb trufaw, to live in luxury, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:5">James 5:5</span>. </p>
<p>Kings' courts [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βασιλειοις] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. Often rendered palaces. Sometimes, in later Greek, applied to a capital or royal city, a royal treasury, and a royal diadem.</p>
