<p>Went [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επορευετο] </span></span>. The imperfect tense is explained by what follows. He was going, was on the way, when he was met by the second messenger from the centurion. </p>
<p>Friends. Possibly kinsmen, not elders now. </p>
<p>Trouble [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκυλλου] </span></span>. Lit., worry. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:36">Matthew 9:36</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:35">Mark 5:35</span>. </p>
<p>Worthy [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ικανος] </span></span>. Lit., sufficient. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:11">Matthew 3:11</span>, "worthy to bear;" and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:5">2 Corinthians 3:5</span>, "not that we are sufficient [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ικανοι] </span></span>, but our sufficiency [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ικανοτης</span></span>] is of God." It is also used in the sense of much, many, long. See ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:12">Luke 7:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:27">Luke 8:27</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:32">Luke 8:32</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:9">Luke 20:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:23">Acts 9:23</span>.</p>
