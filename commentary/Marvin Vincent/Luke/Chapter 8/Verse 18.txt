<p>How ye hear [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πως] </span></span>. The manner of hearing. Mark has ti, what ye hear; the matter. </p>
<p>Seemeth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοκει] </span></span>. Peculiar to Luke. Rev. renders "thinketh he hath," as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:26">James 1:26</span>, on which see note. Wyc., guesseth; Tynd., supposeth. </p>
<p>19 - 21. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:46-50">Matthew 12:46-50</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:31-35">Mark 3:31-35</span>. </p>
<p>Come at him [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συντυχειν] </span></span>. Only here in New Testament. The word properly carries the idea of an accidental meeting, and slightly so here. Jesus was lost in the crowd, and his friends could not fall in with him. </p>
<p>22 - 25; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:57-62">Luke 9:57-62</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:18-27">Matthew 8:18-27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:35-41">Mark 4:35-41</span>.</p>
