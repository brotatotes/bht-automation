<p>Let us go over unto the other side of the lake. Wyc. has, pass we over the standing water. On lake, see on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:1">Luke 5:1</span>. </p>
<p>Launched forth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανηχθησαν] </span></span>. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:3">Luke 5:3</span>. The verb literally means to lead up; hence to lead up to the high sea, or take to sea; put to sea. It is the word used of Jesus ' being led up into the wilderness and the mount of temptation (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:1">Matthew 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:22">Luke 2:22</span>); also of bringing up a sacrifice to an idol - altar (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:41">Acts 7:41</span>). Often in Acts in the accounts of Paul 's voyages.</p>
