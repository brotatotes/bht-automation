<p>He fell asleep [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφυπνωσεν] </span></span>. Very graphic. He fell off [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απο</span></span>] into sleep. </p>
<p>Came down [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεβη] </span></span>. More vivid than either Matthew or Mark, who have there arose. The word describes the action of the sudden storms which literally came down from the heights surrounding the lake. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:24">Matthew 8:24</span>. </p>
<p>Storm [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαιλαψ] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:37">Mark 4:37</span>. Matthew has seismov, a shaking. </p>
<p>See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:24">Matthew 8:24</span>. </p>
<p>They were filling with water [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεπληρουντο] </span></span>. Used by Luke only. </p>
<p>Mark, as usual, goes into minuter detail, and describes how the waves beat into the boat. Note the imperfects : they were filling; they were beginning to be in danger, contrasted with the instantaneous descent of the storm expressed by the aorist came down.</p>
