<p>He had commanded [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρηγγελλεν] </span></span>. Imperfect tense. Rev. does not improve by reading he commanded. The imperfect expresses the simultaneousness of the exorcism and the cry torment me not. Better, for he was commanding. So the Am. Rev. </p>
<p>It had seized [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνηρπακει] </span></span>. Used by Luke only. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:12">Acts 6:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:15">Acts 27:15</span>. The verb literally means to snatch and carry away with [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συν] </span></span>. </p>
<p>He was kept bound [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδεσμευετο φυλασσομενος] </span></span>. Lit., he was bound, being guarded. Rev., was kept under guard and bound. The A. V. does not sufficiently bring out the vigilance with which he was attended. </p>
<p>Chains and fetters. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:4">Mark 5:4</span>. </p>
<p>Breaking [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαρρησσων] </span></span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:4">Mark 4:4</span>, and see note there. </p>
<p>Was driven, etc. Peculiar to Luke.</p>
