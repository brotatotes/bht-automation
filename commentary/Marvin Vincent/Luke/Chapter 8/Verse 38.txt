<p>Besought [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδεετο] </span></span>. Imperfect : was beseeching. See on prayers, ch. 5 33. Rev., prayed. Beseech is used to render parakalew (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:10">Mark 5:10</span>). See on consolation, ch. 6 24. Parakalew, beseech, is used of prayer to God in only one instance, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:8">2 Corinthians 12:8</span>, where Paul besought the Lord to remove the thorn in the flesh. Frequently of requests to Christ while on earth. Deomai, to pray, often of prayer to God (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:38">Matthew 9:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:2">Luke 10:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:22">Acts 8:22</span>). It is noticeable that in ver. 28, where the demons address Christ as the Son of the highest God, they say deonai, I pray. In vv. 31, 32, where they ask not to be sent away, and to be allowed to enter into the swine, they say parakalew, I beseech. The restored man, recognizing Jesus ' divine power, prayed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδειτο</span></span>] to be with him. The distinction, however, must not be closely pressed. The two words seem to be often used interchangeably in the New Testament.</p>
