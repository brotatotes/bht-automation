<p>Brake and gave [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεκλασενεδιδου] </span></span>. Note the two tenses, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:41">Mark 6:41</span>, and see note there. </p>
<p>To set before [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραθειναι] </span></span>. Lit., to set beside, since the table was at the side of the guest. A common word for serving up a meal. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:8">Luke 10:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:34">Acts 16:34</span>. From the sense of placing beside, comes that of putting in charge, committing (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:48">Luke 12:48</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:46">Luke 23:46</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:18">1 Timothy 1:18</span>). Hence the kindred noun paraqhkh (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:12">2 Timothy 1:12</span>), a deposit : that which I have committed.</p>
