<p>Gain [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κερδησας] </span></span>. A merchant 's word. Jesus is putting the case as a common - sense question of profit and loss. </p>
<p>Lose [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απολεσας] </span></span>. "When he might have been saved" (Bengel). This word, in classical Greek, is used : </p>
<p>1. Of death in battle or elsewhere. </p>
<p>2. Of laying waste, as a city or heritage. </p>
<p>3. Of losing of life, property, or other objects. As an active verb, to kill or demolish. </p>
<p>4. Of being demoralized, morally abandoned or ruined, as children under bad influences. In New Testament of killing (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:13">Matthew 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:14">Matthew 12:14</span>). Of destroying and perishing, not only of human life, but of material and intellectual things (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:19">1 Corinthians 1:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:27">John 6:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:22">Mark 2:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:7">1 Peter 1:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:11">James 1:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:11">Hebrews 1:11</span>). Of losing (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:6">Matthew 10:6</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:42">Matthew 10:42</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:4">Luke 14:4</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:6">Luke 14:6</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:8">Luke 14:8</span>). Of moral abandonment (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:24">Luke 14:24</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:32">Luke 14:32</span>). Of the doom of the inpenitent (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:28">Matthew 10:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:3">Luke 13:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:15">John 3:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:28">John 10:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:9">2 Peter 3:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:12">Romans 2:12</span>). </p>
<p>Cast away [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζημιωθεις] </span></span>. Another business term. The word means to fine, amerce, mulct; to punish by exacting forfeit. Hence Rev., correctly, forfeit his own self. See on win your souls, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:19">Luke 21:19</span>. Also on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:26">Matthew 16:26</span>.</p>
