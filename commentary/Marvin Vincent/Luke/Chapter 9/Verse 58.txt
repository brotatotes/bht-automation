<p>Birds [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πετεινα] </span></span>. Strictly, flying fowl. The common word for bird in the New Testament. Opniv occurs <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:37">Matthew 23:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:34">Luke 13:34</span>; but both times in the sense of hen. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:37">Matthew 23:37</span>. Orneon is found in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+18:2">Revelation 18:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+19:17">Revelation 19:17</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+19:21">Revelation 19:21</span>; and pthnon, another form for the word in this passage, occurs <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:30">1 Corinthians 14:30</span>. </p>
