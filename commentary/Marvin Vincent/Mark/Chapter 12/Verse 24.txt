<p>Therefore [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια τουτο] </span></span>. A rendering which obscures the meaning. The words point forward to the next two clauses. The reason of your error is your ignorance of the scriptures and the power of God. Hence Rev., correctly, Is it not for this cause that ye err? </p>
<p>Err [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πλανασθε] </span></span>. Lit., wander out of the way. Compare Latin errare. Of the wandering sheep, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:12">Matthew 18:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:25">1 Peter 2:25</span>. Of the martyrs wandering in the deserts, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+11:38">Hebrews 11:38</span>. Often rendered in the New Testament deceive. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:5">Mark 13:5</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:6">Mark 13:6</span>. Compare ajsterev planhtai, wandering stars (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:13">Jude 1:13</span>), from which our word planet.</p>
