<p>With all thy heart [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ ολης της καρδιας σου] </span></span>. Lit., out of thy whole heart. The heart, not only as the seat of the affections, but as the center of our complex being - physical, moral, spiritual, and intellectual. Soul [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ψυχης] </span></span>. The word is often used in the New Testament in its original meaning of life. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:20">Matthew 2:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:28">Matthew 20:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:10">Acts 20:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:3">Romans 11:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:11">John 10:11</span>. Hence, as an emphatic designation of the man himself. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:18">Matthew 12:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:38">Hebrews 10:38</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:19">Luke 21:19</span>. So that the word denotes "life in the distinctness of individual existence" [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρεμερ] </span></span>. See farther on yucikov, spiritual, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:44">1 Corinthians 14:44</span>. </p>
<p>Mind [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διανοιας] </span></span>. The faculty of thought : understanding, especially the moral understanding.</p>
