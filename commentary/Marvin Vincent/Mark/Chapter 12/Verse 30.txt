With all thy heart [εξ ολης της καρδιας σου] . Lit., out of thy whole heart. The heart, not only as the seat of the affections, but as the center of our complex being - physical, moral, spiritual, and intellectual. Soul [ψυχης] . The word is often used in the New Testament in its original meaning of life. See Matthew 2:20; Matthew 20:28; Acts 20:10; Romans 11:3; John 10:11. Hence, as an emphatic designation of the man himself. See Matthew 12:18; Hebrews 10:38; Luke 21:19. So that the word denotes "life in the distinctness of individual existence" [χρεμερ] . See farther on yucikov, spiritual, 1 Corinthians 14:44. Mind [διανοιας] . The faculty of thought : understanding, especially the moral understanding.
