<p>Joseph of Arimathaea [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιωσηφ ο απο αριμαθαιας] </span></span>. Lit., Joseph, he from Arimathaea : the article indicating a man well known. </p>
<p>Honorable [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευσχημων] </span></span>. Compounded of eu, well, and schma, form, shape, figure. On the latter word, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:2">Matthew 17:2</span>. In its earlier use this adjective would, therefore, emphasize the dignified external appearance and deportment. So Plato, noble bearing (" Republic, " 413). Later, it came to be used in the sense of noble; honorable in rank. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:50">Acts 13:50</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:12">Acts 17:12</span>. </p>
<p>Counsellor. A member of the Sanhedrim, as appears from <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:51">Luke 23:51</span>. Went in boldly [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τολμησας εισηλθεν] </span></span>. Lit., having dared went in. Daring all possible consequences.</p>
