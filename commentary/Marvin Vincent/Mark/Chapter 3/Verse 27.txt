<p>Spoil [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαρπασαι] </span></span>. Mark uses the stronger and more vivid compound verb, where Matthew employs the simple aJrpasai. The verb means, primarily, to tear in pieces; to carry away, as the wind; to efface, as footsteps. So, generally, to seize as plunder, snatching right and left. </p>
<p>His goods [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τα σκευη] </span></span>. Lit., his vessels. So Wyc. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:16">Mark 10:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:15">Acts 9:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:11">Acts 10:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:20">2 Timothy 2:20</span>. The special object of the robber may be precious vessels of gold or silver; but the word is probably used in its general sense of household gear.</p>
