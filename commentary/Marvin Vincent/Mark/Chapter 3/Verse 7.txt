<p>Withdrew. Mark alone notes no less than eleven occasions on which Jesus retired from his work, in order to escape his enemies or to pray in solitude, for rest, or for private conference with his disciples. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:12">Mark 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:7">Mark 3:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:31">Mark 6:31</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:46">Mark 6:46</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:24">Mark 7:24</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:31">Mark 7:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:2">Mark 9:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:1">Mark 10:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:34">Mark 14:34</span>. </p>
<p>A great multitude [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πολυ πληθος] </span></span>. Compare verse 8, where the order of the Greek words is reversed. In the former case the greatness of the mass of people is emphasized; in the latter, the mass of people itself.</p>
