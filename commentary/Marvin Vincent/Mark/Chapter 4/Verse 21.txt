<p>A candle [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο λυχνος] </span></span>. Properly, the lamp, as Rev. </p>
<p>Brought [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ερχεται] </span></span>. Lit., cometh. Doth the lamp come? This impersonation or investing the lamp with motion is according to Mark's lively mode of narrative, as is the throwing of the passage into the interrogative form. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:16">Luke 8:16</span>. The lamp : the article indicating a familiar household implement. So also "the bed" and "the stand." </p>
<p>Bushel [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μοδιον] </span></span>. The Latin modius. One of Mark's Latin words. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:15">Matthew 5:15</span>. The modius was nearer a peck than a bushel. </p>
<p>Bed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κλινην] </span></span>. A couch for reclining at table. </p>
<p>Candlestick [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυχνιαν] </span></span>. Rev., correctly, stand; i e., lamp stand. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:15">Matthew 5:15</span>.</p>
