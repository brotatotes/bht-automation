Knowing [επιγνους] . Rev., perceiving. Lit., having fully known. That virtue had gone out of him [την εξ αυτου δυναμιν εξελθουσαν] . More correctly as Rev., that the power proceeding from him had gone forth. The object of the Savior 's knowledge was thus complex : 1st, his power; 2nd, that his power had gone forth, This and the following sentence are peculiar to Mark.
