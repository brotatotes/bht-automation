<p>Had a quarrel against him [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενειχεν αυτω] </span></span>. There is some dispute about the rendering. The Rev. renders Set herself against him, with no alternative translation in the margin; and in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:53">Luke 11:53</span>, Press upon him vehemently, with set themselves against him in the margin. I see no objection to rendering was angry at him, taking ejneicen aujtw with an ellipsis of colon, anger. Very literally, had within herself [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν</span></span>] anger against him. So Herodotus, 1, 118. Astyages concealing the anger [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον χολον</span></span>] which he felt toward him [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οι ενειχε] </span></span>. 6 119, ejneice sfi deinon colon, nourished a fierce anger against them. So Moulton, Grimm, and De Wette. </p>
<p>Desired [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηθελεν] </span></span>. Imperfect tense, was desiring all along. Her demand for John's murder was the result of a long - cherished wish.</p>
