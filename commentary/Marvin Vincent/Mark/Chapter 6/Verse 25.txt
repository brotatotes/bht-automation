<p>Mark's narrative emphasizes the eager haste with which the murder was pushed. She came in straightway and demanded the boon forthwith. </p>
<p>By and by [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαυτης] </span></span>. Obsolete in the old sense of immediately. The A. V. translates eujquv, straightway, in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:21">Matthew 13:21</span>, by and by : eujqewv, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:17">Mark 4:17</span>, immediately : and the same word in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:9">Luke 21:9</span>, by and by. Exauthv is rendered immediately, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:33">Acts 10:33</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:11">Acts 11:11</span> : straightway, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:30">Acts 23:30</span> : presently, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:23">Philippians 2:23</span>. Rev., forthwith. The expression by and by in older English was sometimes used of place. Thus Chaucer. </p>
