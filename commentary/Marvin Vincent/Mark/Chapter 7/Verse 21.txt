<p>Evil Thoughts [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαλογισμοι οι κακοι] </span></span>. Thoughts, those which are evil. So Rev., in margin. Thoughts that are evil. The word dialogismoi, thoughts, does not in itself convey a bad sense; and hence the addition of adjectives denoting evil, as here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:4">James 2:4</span>. Radically, it carries the idea of discussion or debate, with an under - thought of suspicion or doubt, either with one's own mind, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:22">Luke 5:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:8">Luke 6:8</span>; or with another, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:46">Luke 9:46</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:14">Philippians 2:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:1">Romans 14:1</span>.</p>
