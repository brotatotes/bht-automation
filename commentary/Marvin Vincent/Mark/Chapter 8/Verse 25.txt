<p>Made him look up. The best texts omit, and substitute die bleyen, he looked stedfastly. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:5">Matthew 7:5</span>. Instead of vaguely staring, he fixed his eyes on definite objects. </p>
<p>He saw [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενεβλεπεν] </span></span>. Imperfect tense. Continuous action. He saw and continued to see. Compare the aorist tense above : He looked stedfastly, fastened his eyes, denoting the single act, the first exercise of his restored sight. </p>
<p>Every man. Following the reading apantav. But the best texts read apanta, all things. So Rev. </p>
<p>Clearly [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τηλαυγως] </span></span>. From thle, far, aujgh, shining. The farthest things were clearly seen.</p>
