It taketh him [καταλαβη] . Lit., seizeth hold of him. Our word catalepsy is derived from this. Teareth [ρησσει] . Rev., dasheth down, with rendeth in margin. The verb is a form of rJhgnumi, to break. The form rJhssw is used in classical Greek of dancers beating the ground, and of beating drums. Later, in the form rJassein, a term of fighters : to fell, or knock down, which is the sense adopted by Rev. Gnasheth with his teeth. Rev., grindeth. This and the pining away are peculiar to Mark.
