<p>Of a strong man [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του ισχυρου] </span></span>. Rev. rightly gives the force of the article, the strong man. Christ is not citing a general illustration, but is pointing to a specific enemy - Satan. How can I despoil Satan without first having conquered him? </p>
<p>Goods [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκευη] </span></span>. The word originally means a vessel, and so mostly in the New Testament. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:16">Mark 11:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:29">John 19:29</span>. But also the entire equipment of a house, collectively : chattels, house - gear. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:31">Luke 17:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:17">Acts 27:17</span>, of the gear or tackling of the ship. Rev., lowered the gear.</p>
