<p>Adulterous [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μοιχαλις] </span></span>. A very strong and graphic expression, founded upon the familiary Hebrew representation of the relation of God 's people to him under the figure of marriage. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+73:27">Psalms 73:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+57:3">Isaiah 57:3</span> sqq.; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+61:5">Isaiah 61:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eze+23:27">Ezekiel 23:27</span>. Hence idolatry and intercourse with Gentiles were described as adultery; and so here, of moral unfaithfulness to God. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:4">James 4:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+2:20">Revelation 2:20</span> sqq. Thus Dante : </p>
<p>"Where Michael wrought Vengeance upon the proud adultery." Inf., 7, 12.</p>
