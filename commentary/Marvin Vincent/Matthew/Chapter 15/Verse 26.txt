<p>Children 's [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">των τεκνων] </span></span>. Bengel observes that while Christ spoke severely to the Jews, he spoke honorably of them to those without. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:22">John 4:22</span>. </p>
<p>Dogs [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυναριοις] </span></span>. Diminutive : little dogs. In ver. 27, Wyc. renders the little whelps, and Tynd., in both verses, whelps. The picture is of a family meal, with the pet house - dogs running round the table. </p>
<p>Their masters. The children are the masters of the little dogs. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:28">Mark 7:28</span>, "the children 's crumbs."</p>
