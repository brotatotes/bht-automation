<p>Turned [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">στραφεις] </span></span>. Not toward Peter, but away from him. </p>
<p>Get thee behind me. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:10">Matthew 4:10</span>. </p>
<p>Offense [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σκανδαλον] </span></span>. Rev., better, stumbling - block. See on 5 29. Not, thou art offensive, but thou art in my way. Dr. Morison, "Thou art not, as before, a noble block, lying in its right position as a massive foundation - stone. On the contrary, thou art like a stone quite out of its proper place, and lying right across the road in which I must go - lying as a stone of stumbling." </p>
<p>Savorest not [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου φρονεις] </span></span>. Rev., better, mindest not. Thy thoughts and intents are not of God, but of men. Savorest follows the Vulgate sapis, from sapere, which means 1st, to have a taste or flavor of : 2nd, to have sense or discernment. Hence used here as the rendering of fronein, to be minded. Thus Wyc., <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:11">1 Corinthians 13:11</span>, "When I was a child I savored [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εφρονουν</span></span>] as a child." The idea is, strictly, to partake of the quality or nature of.</p>
