Lowering [στυγναζων] . The verb means to have a gloomy look. Dr. Morison compares the Scotch gloaming or glooming. Cranmer, the sky is glooming red. The word is used only here and at Mark 10:22, of the young ruler, turning from Christ with his face overshadowed with gloom. A. V., he was sad. Rev., his countenance fell. 9, 10. Note the accurate employment of the two words for basket. See on 14 20.
