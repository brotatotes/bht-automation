<p>Daughter of Sion. Jerusalem. Compare daughter of Babylon for the city of Babylon (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+137:8">Psalms 137:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+47:1">Isaiah 47:1</span>); daughter of Tyre for the city or people of Tyre (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+45:12">Psalms 45:12</span>); daughter of my people (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+22:4">Isaiah 22:4</span>). </p>
<p>Sitting [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επιβεβηκως] </span></span>. Lit., having gone upon, or mounted. Rev., riding. </p>
<p>Foal of an ass [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υιον υποζυγιου] </span></span>. Lit., son of a beast - of - burden. Upozugion, from uJpo, beneath, zugov, a yoke. Wyc., son of a beast - under - yoke. The phrase emphasized the humble state of Jesus. He is mounted, not on a stately charger with embroidered and jewelled housings, nor even on an ass for the saddle, the Eastern ass being often of great beauty and spirit, and in demand for this purpose. He rides on a common beast - of - burden, furnished with the everyday garments of his disciples. </p>
<p>Garments [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ιματια] </span></span>. Outer garments. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:40">Matthew 5:40</span>.</p>
