<p>Ye Tithe [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποδεκατουτε] </span></span>. ajpo, from, dekatow, to take a tenth. Tithe is tenth; also in older English, tethe, as tethe hest, the tenth commandment. A tething was a district containing ten families. </p>
<p>Mint [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηδυοσμον] </span></span>. hJduv, sweet, ojsmh, smell. A favorite plant in the East, with which the floors of dwelling and synagogues were sometimes strewn. </p>
<p>Anise - Cummin [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανηθονκυμινον] </span></span>. Rev. renders anise, dill in margin. Used as condiments. The tithe of these plants would be very small; but to exact it would indicate scrupulous conscientiousness. The Talmud tells of the ass of a certain Rabbi which had been so well trained as to refuse corn of which the tithes had not been taken. </p>
<p>Faith [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πιστιν] </span></span>. Rather faithfulness, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:3">Romans 3:3</span>, Rev. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:22">Galatians 5:22</span>, Rev.</p>
