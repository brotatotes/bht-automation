<p>Carcase [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πτωμα] </span></span>. From piptw, to fall. Originally a fall, and thence a fallen body; a corpse. Compare Lat. cadaver, from cado, to fall. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:29">Mark 6:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+11:8">Revelation 11:8</span>. On the saying itself, compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="job+39:30">Job 39:30</span>. </p>
<p>Eagles [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αετοι] </span></span>. Rev. puts vultures in margin. The griffon vulture is meant, which surpasses the eagle in size and power. Aristotle notes how this bird scents its prey from afar, and congregates in the wake of an army. In the Russian war vast numbers were collected in the Crimea, and remained until the end of the campaign in the neighborhood of the camp, although previously scarcely know in the country.</p>
