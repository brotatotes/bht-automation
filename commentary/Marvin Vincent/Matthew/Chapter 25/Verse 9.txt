<p>Not so, lest, etc. [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μηποτε ου μη αρκεση] </span></span>. The Greek does not give the blunt negative of the A. V. It is a more courteous form of refusal, making the reason for refusing to supply the place of the negative. Give us of your oil, say the foolish. The wise reply, Lest perchance there be not by any means [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου μη, τηε δουβλε νεγατιςε</span></span>] enough. The Rev. gives it very happily. Per adventure there will not be enough, etc.</p>
