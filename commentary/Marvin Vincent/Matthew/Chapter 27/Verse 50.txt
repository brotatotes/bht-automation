<p>Yielded up the ghost [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αφηκε το πνευμα] </span></span>. Lit., dismissed his spirit. Rev., yielded up his spirit. The fact that the evangelists, in describing our Lord 's death, do not use the neuter verb, eqanen, he died, but he breathed out his life (ejxepneuse, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:37">Mark 14:37</span>), he gave up his spirit (paredwke to pneuma, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:30">John 19:30</span>), seems to imply a voluntary yielding up of his life. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:18">John 10:18</span>. Augustine says, "He gave up his life because he willed it, when he willed it, and as he willed it."</p>
