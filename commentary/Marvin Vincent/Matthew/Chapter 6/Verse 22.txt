<p>Single [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απλους] </span></span>. The picture underlying this adjective is that of a piece of cloth or other material, neatly folded once, and without a variety of complicated folds. Hence the idea of simplicity or singleness (compare simplicity from the Latin simplex; semel, once; plicare, to fold). So, in a moral sense, artless, plain, pure. Here sound, as opposed to evil or diseased. Possibly with reference to the double - mindedness and indecision condemned in ver. 24. </p>
<p>Full of light [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φωτεινον] </span></span>. Bengel says, "As if it were all eye."</p>
