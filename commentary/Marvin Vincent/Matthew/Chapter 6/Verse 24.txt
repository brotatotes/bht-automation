<p>The other [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετερον] </span></span>. Implying distinction in quality rather than numerical distinction [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλλος] </span></span>. For example, " whoever smiteth thee on thy right cheek, turn to him the other [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">την αλλην] </span></span>; i e., the other one of the two (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:39">Matthew 5:39</span>). At Pentecost, the disciples began to speak with other [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετεραις</span></span>] tongues; i e., different from their native tongues. Here the word gives the idea of two master of distinct or opposite character and interests, like God and Mammon. </p>
<p>Hold to [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθεξεται] </span></span>. The preposition ajnti, against, indicates holding to the one master as against the other. He who is for God must be against Mammon.</p>
