<p>Yea [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ναι] </span></span>. A confirmatory particle, gathering up the whole previous intercession for Onesimus. So <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:26">Matthew 11:26</span>. even so; Rev., yea. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:51">Luke 11:51</span>, verily; Rev., yea. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:5">Luke 12:5</span>, yea. </p>
<p>Let me have joy [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οναιμην] </span></span>. Or help. Lit., may I profit. Again a play upon the name Onesimus. The verb is frequently used with reference to filial doties. Ignatius employs it, in one instance, directly after an allusion to another Onesimus (Ephesians, 2.).</p>
