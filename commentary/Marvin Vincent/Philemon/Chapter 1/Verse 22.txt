<p>Withal [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμα] </span></span>. Simultaneously with the fulfillment of my request. A lodging. Paul is expecting a speedy liberation. His original plan of going from Rome to Spain has apparently been altered. Lightfoot observes that "there is a gentle compulsion in this mention of a personal visit to Colossae. The apostle would thus be able to see for himself that Philemon had not disappointed his expectations." </p>
<p>I shall be given [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρισθησομαι] </span></span>. A beautiful assumption of his correspondent's affection for him, in that his visit to them will be a gracious gift [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χαρις] </span></span>. The word is also used of granting for destruction, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:11">Acts 25:11</span>; or for preservation, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:14">Acts 3:14</span>.</p>
