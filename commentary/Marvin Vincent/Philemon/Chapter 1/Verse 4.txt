<p>Thank - always. Construe with thank. For similar introductory thanksgivings compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:8">Romans 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:4">1 Corinthians 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:16">Ephesians 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:3">Philippians 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:3">Colossians 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:2">1 Thessalonians 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:3">2 Thessalonians 1:3</span>. Making mention [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μνειαν ποιουμενος] </span></span>. Mneia primarily means remembrance, so that the phrase expresses the two ideas, mentioning thee when I call thee to mind. </p>
<p>In my prayers [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επι] </span></span>. On the occasions of. </p>
<p>Thy love and faith - toward [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς</span></span>] the Lord Jesus and toward [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις</span></span>] all saints. The clauses are arranged crosswise, 209 love referring to saints, faith to Christ. Toward. Two different prepositions are thus translated. Practically the difference is not material, but prov toward, with pistiv faith is unusual. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+1:8">1 Thessalonians 1:8</span>. Eijv is the preposition of contact; to, unto; faith exerted upon.</p>
