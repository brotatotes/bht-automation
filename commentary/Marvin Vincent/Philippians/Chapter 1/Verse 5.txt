<p>For your fellowship [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επι τη κοινωνια υμων] </span></span>. Connect with I thank God. For fellowship, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:3">1 John 1:3</span>. The word sometimes has the meaning of almsgiving, contributions, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:26">Romans 14:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:16">Hebrews 13:16</span>. Though here it is used in the larger sense of sympathetic cooperation, yet it is no doubt colored by the other idea, in view of the Philippians' pecuniary contributions to Paul. See ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+4:10">Philippians 4:10</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+4:15">Philippians 4:15</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+4:16">Philippians 4:16</span>. </p>
<p>In the Gospel (eijv to eujaggelion). Lit., unto the Gospel : Rev., in furtherance of.</p>
