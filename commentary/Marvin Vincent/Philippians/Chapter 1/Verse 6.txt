<p>Being confident [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεποιθως] </span></span>. With a slightly causative force : since I am confident. </p>
<p>Hath begun - will perform [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εναρξαμενος - επιτελεσει] </span></span>. The two words occur together, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:6">2 Corinthians 8:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:3">Galatians 3:3</span>. Both were used of religious ceremonials. So Euripides : "But come! Bring up the sacrificial meal - basket" [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξαρχου κανα] </span></span>; that is, begin the offering by taking the barley - meal from the basket (" Iphigenia in Aulis, " 435). Some find the sacrificial metaphor here, and compare ch. 2 17, see note. Perform, better as Rev., perfect. Perform, in its older and literal sense of carrying through (per) or consummating would express the idea; but popular usage has identified it with do.</p>
