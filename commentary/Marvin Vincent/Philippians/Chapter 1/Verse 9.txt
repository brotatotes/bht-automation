<p>Judgment [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αισθησει] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. Rev., better, discernment : sensitive moral perception. Used of the senses, as Xenophon : "perception of things sweet or pungent" (" Memorabilia, "1, 4, 5). Of hearing :" It is possible to go so far away as not to afford a hearing "(" Anabasis," 4, 6, 13). The senses are called aijsqhseiv. See Plato, "Theaetetus," 156. Plato uses it of visions of the gods (" Phaedo, " 111). Compare aijsqhthria senses, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:14">Hebrews 5:14</span>. Discernment selects, classifies, and applies what is furnished by knowledge.</p>
