<p>Murmurings [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γογγυσμων] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:16">Jude 1:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:41">John 6:41</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:10">1 Corinthians 10:10</span>. </p>
<p>Disputings [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαλογισμων] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:21">Mark 7:21</span>. It is doubtful whether disputings is a legitimate meaning. The kindred verb dialogizomai is invariably used in the sense of to reason or discuss, either with another or in one's own mind, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:7">Matthew 16:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:25">Matthew 21:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:6">Mark 2:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:17">Luke 12:17</span>. The noun is sometimes rendered thoughts, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:19">Matthew 14:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:21">Mark 7:21</span>; but with the same idea underlying it, of a suspicion or doubt, causing inward discussion. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:8">1 Timothy 2:8</span>. Better here questionings or doubtings. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:1">Romans 14:1</span>. The murmuring is the moral, the doubting the intellectual rebellion against God.</p>
