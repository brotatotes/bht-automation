<p>The circumcision. The abstract term for those who are circumcised. In the Old Testament, circumcision was a metaphor for purity. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+26:41">Leviticus 26:41</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+10:16">Deuteronomy 10:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+30:6">Deuteronomy 30:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eze+44:7">Ezekiel 44:7</span>, etc. </p>
<p>Worship God in the spirit [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πνευματι θεω λατρευοντες] </span></span>. The correct reading is qeou of God. Render, as Rev., worship by the Spirit of God. Worship. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+22:3">Revelation 22:3</span>. Paul uses the Jews ' word which denoted their own service of Jehovah as His peculiar people. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:7">Acts 26:7</span>. A Jew would be scandalized by the application of this term to Christian worship. </p>
<p>Rejoice in Christ Jesus [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καυχωμενοι] </span></span>. Rev., better, glory. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+9:23">Jeremiah 9:23</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+9:24">Jeremiah 9:24</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:31">1 Corinthians 1:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:17">2 Corinthians 10:17</span>. In the flesh. External privileges of every kind.</p>
