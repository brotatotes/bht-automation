<p>Circumcised the eighth day [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιτομη οκταημερος] </span></span>. Lit., eight days old in circumcision; or passing the eighth day. For the idiom, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:39">John 11:39</span>, and compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:13">Acts 28:13</span>. Converts to Judaism were circumcised in maturity : Ishmaelites in their thirteenth year. He was thus shown to be neither a heathen nor an Ishmaelite. </p>
<p>Of the stock of Israel. Not a proselyte, but of the original stock [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γενους] </span></span>; not grafted into the covenant race. A descendant of Jacob, not an Idumaean nor an Ishmaelite. For Israel, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:12">Acts 3:12</span>, and compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:4">Romans 9:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:1">Romans 11:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:47">John 1:47</span>. Descended not from Jacob, the supplanter, but from Israel, the prince of God. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+32:28">Genesis 32:28</span>. </p>
<p>Of the tribe of Benjamin. Not from one of the lost tribes, but from that which gave to Israel its first king; which alone was faithful to Judah at the separation under Rehoboam, and which had always held the post of honor in the army. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:5">Jude 1:5</span>:14; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ho+5:8">Hosea 5:8</span>. Benjamin only of the twelve patriarchs was born in the land of promise. Mordecai, the deliverer of the Jews from Haman was a Benjamite. Paul 's own original name, Saul, was probably derived from Saul the son of Kish, the Benjamite. </p>
<p>A Hebrew of the Hebrews [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβραιος εξ εβραιων] </span></span>. The (Hebrews) of the A. V. gives a wrong coloring to the phrase, as if Paul were claiming to be preeminently a Hebrew among other Hebrews He means a Hebrew from [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξ</span></span>] Hebrew parents. Rev., a Hebrew of Hebrews, which is no special improvement. The expression implies characteristics of language and manners. He might be an Israelite and yet a child of Greek - speaking Jews : but his parents had retained their native tongue and customs, and he himself, while understanding and speaking Greek, also spoke in Hebrew on occasion. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:40">Acts 21:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:2">Acts 22:2</span>. </p>
<p>The law. The Mosaic law. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:12">Romans 2:12</span>. The validity of that law was the principle upheld by the Judaizers. </p>
<p>A Pharisee. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:6">Acts 23:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:14">Galatians 1:14</span>. Compare on the whole verse, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:22">2 Corinthians 11:22</span>.</p>
