<p>Content [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αυταρκης] </span></span>. Lit., self - sufficient. Only here in the New Testament. A stoic word, expressing the favorite doctrine of the sect, that man should be sufficient to himself for all things; able, by the power of his own will, to resist the shock of circumstance. Paul is self - sufficient through the power of the new self : not he, but Christ in him. The kindred noun aujtarkeia sufficiency, occurs <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:8">2 Corinthians 9:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:6">1 Timothy 6:6</span>.</p>
