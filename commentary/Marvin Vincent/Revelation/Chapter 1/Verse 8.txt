<p>Alpha and Omega [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">το α και το ω] </span></span>. Rev., rightly, gives the article, "the Alpha," etc. The words are explained by the gloss, properly omitted from the text, the beginning and the ending. The Rabbinical writers used the phrase from Aleph to Tav, to signify completely, from beginning to end. Thus one says, "Adam transgressed the whole law from Aleph even to Tav." Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+41:4">Isaiah 41:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+43:10">Isaiah 43:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+44:6">Isaiah 44:6</span>. </p>
<p>The Lord [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο κυριος] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:3">Matthew 21:3</span>. The best texts read Kuriov oJ Qeov the Lord the God. Rev., the Lord God. </p>
<p>Which is, etc. See on verse 4. "God, as the old tradition declares, holding in His hand the beginning, middle, and end of all that is" (Plato, "Laws," 715). </p>
<p>The Almighty [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο παντοκρατωρ] </span></span>. Used only once outside of Revelation, in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:18">2 Corinthians 6:18</span>, where it is a quotation. Constantly in the Septuagint.</p>
