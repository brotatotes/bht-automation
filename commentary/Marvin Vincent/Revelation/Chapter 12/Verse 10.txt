<p>Saying in heaven [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λεγουσαν εν τω ουρανω] </span></span>. The correct reading joins in heaven with great voice. So Rev. I heard a great voice in heaven. Now [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρτι] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+13:33">John 13:33</span>. </p>
<p>Is come [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγενετο] </span></span>. Lit., came to pass. Alford says : "It is impossible in English to join to a particle of present time, such as arti now, a verb in aoristic time. We are driven to the perfect in such cases." </p>
<p>Salvation, power, the kingdom. All have the article : the salvation, etc. So Rev. The phrase, now is come the salvation, etc., means that these are realized and established. Some, less correctly, render, now is the salvation, etc., become our God 's Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:6">Luke 3:6</span>. </p>
<p>Power [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξουσια] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:10">Mark 2:10</span>. Rev., authority. </p>
<p>The accuser of our brethren [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο κατηγορος των αδελφων ημων] </span></span>. The correct form of the Greek for accuser is a transcript of the Rabbinical Hebrew, kathgwr. The Rabbins had a corresponding term sunhgwr for Michael, as the advocate of God 's people. The phrase is applied to Satan nowhere else in the New Testament. </p>
<p>Is cast down [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεβληθη] </span></span>. The aorist tense. Once and for all. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:31">John 12:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:8">John 16:8</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:11">John 16:11</span>. </p>
<p>Which accuseth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο κατηγορων] </span></span>. Lit., the one. The article with the present participle expresses what is habitual.</p>
