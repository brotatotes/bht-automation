<p>:<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+12:0">Revelation 12:0</span>_4 <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+12:0">Revelation 12:0</span>:4Of the stars of heaven. Some expositors find an allusion to the fallen angels (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:6">Jude 1:6</span>). </p>
<p>Did cast them to the earth. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="da+8:10">Daniel 8:10</span>. </p>
<p>To devour her child as soon as it was born [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ινα οταν τεκη το τεκνον αυτης καταφαγη] </span></span>. Rev., more literally, that when she was delivered he might devour her child. Professor Milligan says : "In these words we have the dragon doing what Pharaoh did to Israel (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+1:15-22">Exodus 1:15-22</span>), and again and again, in the Psalms and the Prophets, Pharaoh is spoken of as the dragon (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+74:13">Psalms 74:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+27:1">Isaiah 27:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+51:9">Isaiah 51:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eze+29:3">Ezekiel 29:3</span>). Nor is it without interest to remember that Pharaoh 's crown was wreathed with a dragon (the asp or serpent of Egypt), and that just as the eagle was the ensign of Rome, so the dragon was that of Egypt. Hence the significance of Moses ' rod being turned into a serpent."</p>
