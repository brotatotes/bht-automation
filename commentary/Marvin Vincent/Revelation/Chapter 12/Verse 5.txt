<p>A man - child [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υιον αρρενα] </span></span>. Lit., a son, a male. The correct reading is arsen, the neuter, not agreeing with the masculine individual [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υιον σον</span></span>] but with the neuter of the genus. The object is to emphasize, not the sex, but the quality of Masculinity - power and vigor. Rev., a son, a man - child. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:21">John 16:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+20:15">Jeremiah 20:15</span>. </p>
<p>To rule [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιμαινειν] </span></span>. Lit., to shepherd or tend. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+2:6">Matthew 2:6</span>. A rod of iron. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+2:9">Psalms 2:9</span>, and see on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+2:27">Revelation 2:27</span>. </p>
<p>Was caught up [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηρπασθη] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:12">Matthew 12:12</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+23:10">Acts 23:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:23">Jude 1:23</span>.</p>
