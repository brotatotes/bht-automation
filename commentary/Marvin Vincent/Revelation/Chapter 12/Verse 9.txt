<p>The great dragon [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο δρακων ο μεγας] </span></span>. Lit., the dragon, the great (dragon). </p>
<p>That old serpent [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο οφις ο αρχαιος] </span></span>. Lit., the serpent, the old (serpent). For this habitual construction in John, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+4:9">1 John 4:9</span>. For ajrcaiov old, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:7">1 John 2:7</span>, and compare "he was a murderer ajp' ajrchv from the beginning," <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:44">John 8:44</span>; ajrch beginning being etymologically akin to ajrcaiov old. </p>
<p>The Devil. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:1">Matthew 4:1</span>. </p>
<p>Satan. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:18">Luke 10:18</span>. </p>
