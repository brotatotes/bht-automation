<p>From the foundation of the world. These words may be construed with slain or with written. In favor of the latter is ch. 18 8; of the former, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:19">1 Peter 1:19</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:20">1 Peter 1:20</span>. Alford, pertinently as I think, urges the position of the words in favor of the connection with slain, and says that had it not been for the apparent difficulty of the sense thus conveyed, no one would have thought of going so far back as to hath been written for a connection. Render, as Rev., the lamb that hath been slain from the foundation of the world. Katabolh foundation is literally a throwing or laying down, from kataballw to throw down; hence a laying down of a foundation.</p>
