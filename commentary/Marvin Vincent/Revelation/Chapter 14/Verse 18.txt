<p>Altar [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θυσιαστηριου] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:23">Acts 17:23</span>. </p>
<p>Which has power [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξων εξουσιαν] </span></span>. Lit., having power. Some texts add the article oJ. So Rev., "he that hath power." </p>
<p>Fire. In the Greek with the article, the fire. </p>
<p>Cry [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κραυγη] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:42">Luke 1:42</span>. </p>
<p>Thy sharp sickle. Lit., thy sickle, the sharp. </p>
<p>Gather [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τρυγησον] </span></span>. From trugh dryness, included in the notion of ripeness, and hence the vintage, harvest. The verb means therefore to gather ripe fruit. It occurs only in this chapter and in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:44">Luke 6:44</span>. Grapes [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σταφυλαι] </span></span>. The noun in the singular means also a bunch of grapes. </p>
<p>Are fully ripe [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκμασαν] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. From ajkmh, transcribed in acme, the highest point. Hence the verb means to reach the height of growth, to be ripe.</p>
