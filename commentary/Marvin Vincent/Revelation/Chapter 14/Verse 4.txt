<p>Were not defiled [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουκ εμολυνθησαν] </span></span>. The verb means properly to besmear or besmirch, and is never used in a good sense, as miainein (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:28">John 18:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:8">Jude 1:8</span>), which in classical Greek is sometimes applied to staining with color. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:4">1 Peter 1:4</span>. </p>
<p>Virgins [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παρθενοι] </span></span>. Either celibate or living in chastity whether in married or single life. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:1-7">1 Corinthians 7:1-7</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:29">1 Corinthians 7:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:2">2 Corinthians 11:2</span>. </p>
<p>First - fruits [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απαρχη] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:18">James 1:18</span>.</p>
