<p>A sea of glass [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θαλασσαν υαλινην] </span></span>. Rev., better, a glassy sea. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+4:6">Revelation 4:6</span>. </p>
<p>Had gotten the victory over the beast [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νικωντας εκ του θηριου] </span></span>. The expression is peculiar. Lit., conquered out of The construction is unique in the New Testament. The phrase signifies, not as A. V., victory over, but coming triumphant out of [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ] </span></span>. So Rev., that come victorious from the beast. </p>
<p>Over his mark. Omit. </p>
<p>Standing on [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επι] </span></span>. Better, as Rev., by : on the shore of, as did the Israelites when they sang the song alluded to in ver 3. </p>
<p>The harps of God. Omit the. Instruments devoted wholly to His praise. Compare ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+5:8">Revelation 5:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+14:2">Revelation 14:2</span>.</p>
