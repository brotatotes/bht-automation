<p>Who shall not fear Thee? See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+10:7">Jeremiah 10:7</span>. Omit thee. </p>
<p>Holy [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οσιος] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:75">Luke 1:75</span>. The term is applied to Christ in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:27">Acts 2:27</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:35">Acts 2:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:26">Hebrews 7:26</span>. To God only here and ch. 16 5, where the correct reading is oJ osiov thou holy one, instead of oJ ejsomenov which shalt be. All nations shall come. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+86:9">Psalms 86:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+2:2-4">Isaiah 2:2-4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+66:9">Isaiah 66:9</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+66:3">Isaiah 66:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mic+4:2">Micah 4:2</span>. </p>
<p>Judgments [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιωματα] </span></span>. Not merely divine decisions, but righteous acts generally. So Rev. Primarily, the word signifies that which has been deemed right so as to have the force of law. Hence an ordinance (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:6">Luke 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:1">Hebrews 9:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:32">Romans 1:32</span>). A judicial decision for or against (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:16">Romans 5:16</span>). A righteous deed. See ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+19:8">Revelation 19:8</span>.</p>
