<p>Behold - shame. These words are parenthetical. </p>
<p>As a thief. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:43">Matthew 24:43</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:39">Luke 12:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:2">1 Thessalonians 5:2</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:4">1 Thessalonians 5:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+3:10">2 Peter 3:10</span>. </p>
<p>Watcheth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γρηρορων] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:35">Mark 13:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:8">1 Peter 5:8</span> Keepeth his garments. "During the night the captain of the Temple made his rounds. On his approach the guards had to rise and salute him in a particular manner. Any guard found asleep when on duty was beaten, or his garments were set on fire. The confession of one of the Rabbins is on record that, on a certain occasion, his own maternal uncle had actually undergone the punishment of having his clothes set on fire by the captain of the Temple" (Edersheim, "The Temple," etc.). </p>
<p>Shame [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασχημοσυνην] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:27">Romans 1:27</span>. From aj not and schma fashion. Deformity, unseemliness; nearly answering to the phrase not in good form.</p>
