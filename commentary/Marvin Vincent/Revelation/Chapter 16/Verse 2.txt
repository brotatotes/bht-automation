<p>And the first went. Each angel, as his turn comes, with draws (uJpagete, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:21">John 6:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:21">John 8:21</span>) from the heavenly scene. </p>
<p>There fell [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγενετο] </span></span>. Lit., there came to pass. Rev., it became. Elliott, very aptly, there broke out. </p>
<p>Noisome and grievous [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κακον και πονηρον] </span></span>. Similarly the two cognate nouns kakia and ponria malice and wickedness occur together in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:8">1 Corinthians 5:8</span>. Ponhrov emphasizes the activity of evil. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:19">Luke 3:19</span>. </p>
<p>Sore [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελκος] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:20">Luke 16:20</span>. Compare the sixth Egyptian plague, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+9:8-12">Exodus 9:8-12</span>, where the Septuagint uses this word elkov boil. Also of the boil or scab of leprosy, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+13:18">Leviticus 13:18</span>; king Hezekiah 's boil, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ki+20:7">2 Kings 20:7</span>; the botch of Egypt, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+28:27">Deuteronomy 28:27</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+28:35">Deuteronomy 28:35</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="job+2:7">Job 2:7</span> (Sept.) the boils are described as here by ponhrov sore.</p>
