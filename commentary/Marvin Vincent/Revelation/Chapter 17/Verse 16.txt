<p>Upon the beast [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επι] </span></span>. Read kai and : "the ten horns - and the beast." </p>
<p>Desolate [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηρημωμενην] </span></span>. Lit., desolated, the verb being in the perfect participle. </p>
<p>Shall eat her flesh. A token of extreme hostility. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+27:2">Psalms 27:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mic+3:3">Micah 3:3</span>. Xenophon, speaking of the hatred between the pure Spartans and the Helots, says that no one of the pure Spartans could conceal his readiness to eat the Helot raw. Notice the plural sarkav flesh, and see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:3">James 5:3</span>. </p>
<p>Burn [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατακαυσουσιν] </span></span>. Rev., giving the force of kata down, burn utterly. According to some interpreters the figure is changed from the woman to a city; but this is unnecessary, as the language is probably taken from the punishment of fornication on the part of a priest 's daughter (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+21:9">Leviticus 21:9</span>; compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+20:14">Leviticus 20:14</span>).</p>
