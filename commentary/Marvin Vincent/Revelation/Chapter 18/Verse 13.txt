<p>Cinnamon [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κιναμωμον] </span></span>. Mentioned as one of the ingredients of the holy oil for anointing (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+30:23">Exodus 30:23</span>), and as a perfume for the bed (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="pr+7:17">Proverbs 7:17</span>). </p>
<p>And spice [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">και αμωμον] </span></span>. These words are added by the best texts. A fragrant Indian plant, with seed in grape - like clusters, from which ointment was made. Preparations for the hair were made from it. Virgil, describing the coming golden age, says : "The Assyrian amomum shall spring up as a common plant" (" Eclogue "4, 25; Compare" Eclogue " 3, 89). Forbiger (Virgil) says that the best was raised in Armenia, a poorer quality in Media and Pontus. </p>
<p>Fine flour [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σεμιδαλιν] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. </p>
<p>Cattle [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κτηνη] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:34">Luke 10:34</span>. </p>
<p>Merchandise of horses. Merchandise is not in the text. It resumes the construction of gomon merchandise with the genitive in ver. 12. </p>
<p>Chariots [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρεδων] </span></span>. A Latin word though of Gallic origin, rheda. It had four wheels.</p>
