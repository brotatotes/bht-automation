<p>Mightily with a strong voice [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ισχυι φωνη μεγαλη] </span></span>. Lit., in strength with a great voice. Omit megalh great, and read ijscura fwnh with a mighty voice. So Rev. </p>
<p>Babylon - is fallen. The Rev. improves on the A. V. by placing fallen in the emphatic position of the Greek : "Fallen, fallen is Babylon." Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+21:9">Isaiah 21:9</span>. </p>
<p>Is become [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγενετο] </span></span>. Lit., became. </p>
<p>Devils [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δαιμονων] </span></span>. Properly, demons, which Rev., strangely commits to the margin. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:34">Mark 1:34</span>. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+13:20-22">Isaiah 13:20-22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+34:13-15">Isaiah 34:13-15</span>. Also on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:24">Luke 11:24</span>. </p>
<p>Hold [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυλακη] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:19">1 Peter 3:19</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:21">Acts 5:21</span>. Rev., in margin, prison. </p>
<p>Cage [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυλακη] </span></span>. The word rendered above hold. Rev., hold. Some, however, explain it, not as a cage where they are kept, but as a place of safety to which they resort. </p>
<p>Bird [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ορνεου] </span></span>. Only in Revelation, here, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+19:17">Revelation 19:17</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+19:21">Revelation 19:21</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+1:39">Jeremiah 1:39</span>.</p>
