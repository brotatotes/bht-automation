<p>Have reached [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηκολουθησαν] </span></span>. Lit., followed. But the best texts read ejkollhqhsan clave. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+51:9">Jeremiah 51:9</span>. For different applications of the verb see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:5">Matthew 19:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:15">Luke 14:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:13">Acts 5:13</span>. Compare the classical phrase for following up closely a fleeing foe, hoerere in terga hostium, to cleave to the backs of the enemy. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="zec+14:5">Zechariah 14:5</span> (Sept.), "The valley of the mountains shall reach [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγκολληθησεται</span></span>] unto Azal." The radical idea of the metaphor is that of following or reaching after so as to be joined to.</p>
