<p>Dwellest [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατοικεις] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:26">Luke 11:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:5">Acts 2:5</span>. </p>
<p>Seat [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θρονος] </span></span>. Rev., rightly, throne, which is a transcript of the Greek word. Better than seat, because it is intended to represent Satan as exercising dominion there. The word is used in the New Testament of a kingly throne (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:32">Luke 1:32</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:52">Luke 1:52</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:30">Acts 2:30</span>) : of the judicial tribunal or bench (Mt 29:28; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:30">Luke 22:30</span>) : of the seats of the elders (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+4:4">Revelation 4:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+11:16">Revelation 11:16</span>). Also, by metonymy, of one who exercises authority, so, in the plural, of angels (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:16">Colossians 1:16</span>), thrones belonging to the highest grade of angelic beings whose place is in the immediate presence of God. </p>
