<p>He laid hold [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκρατησεν] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:3">Mark 8:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:11">Acts 3:11</span>.. </p>
<p>The dragon [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον δρακοντα] </span></span>. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+12:3">Revelation 12:3</span>. The word is commonly derived from edrakon, the second aorist tense of derkomai to see clearly, in allusion to the sharp sight of the fabled dragon. </p>
<p>Old [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αρχαιον] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:7">1 John 2:7</span>. </p>
<p>The Devil. Note the three epithets : the Old Serpent, the Devil, Satan. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:1">Matthew 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:18">Luke 10:18</span>.</p>
