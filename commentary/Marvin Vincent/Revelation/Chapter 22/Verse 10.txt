<p>Seal [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σφραγισης] </span></span>. Rev., seal up. This word occurs eighteen times in Revelation and twice in the Gospel, and only five times elsewhere in the New Testament. It means to confirm or attest (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:33">John 3:33</span>); to close up for security (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:66">Matthew 27:66</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+20:3">Revelation 20:3</span>); to hide or keep secret (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+10:4">Revelation 10:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+22:10">Revelation 22:10</span>); to mark a person or thing (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+7:3">Revelation 7:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:13">Ephesians 1:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:30">Ephesians 4:30</span>) Time [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καιρος] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:1">Matthew 12:1</span>.</p>
