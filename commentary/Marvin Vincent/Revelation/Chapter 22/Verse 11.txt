<p>Unjust [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδικων] </span></span>. Rev., better, unrighteous. </p>
<p>Let him be unjust [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αδικησατω] </span></span>. The verb means to do wickedly. Hence Rev., correctly, let him do unrighteousness. </p>
<p>He which is filthy [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο ρυπων] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. On the kindred noun rJupov filth, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:21">1 Peter 3:21</span>. Ruparia filthiness occurs only in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:21">James 1:21</span>; and the adjective rJuparov filthy only in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:2">James 2:2</span>. Let him be filthy [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ρυπωσατω] </span></span>. The best texts read rJupanqhtw let him be made filthy. So Rev. </p>
<p>Let him be righteous [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιωθητω] </span></span>. Read dikaiosunhn poihsatw let him do righteousness. So Rev. </p>
<p>Let him be holy [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγιασθητω] </span></span>. Rev., giving literally the force of the passive voice, let him be made holy.</p>
