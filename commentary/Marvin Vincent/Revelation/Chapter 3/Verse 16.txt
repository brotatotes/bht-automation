<p>Lukewarm [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χλιαρος] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. </p>
<p>Foremost and most numerous among the lost, Dante places those who had been content to remain neutral in the great contest between good and evil. </p>
<p>"Master, what is this which now I hear? What folk is this, which seems by pain so vanquished? And he to me :" This miserable mode Maintain the melancholy souls of those Who lived withouten infamy or praise. Commingled are they with that caitiff choir. Of angels, who have not rebellious been, Nor faithful were to God, but were for self. The heavens expelled them, not to be less fair; Nor them the nethermore abyss receives, For glory none the damned would have from them. " </p>
<p>"Inferno," 3, 33 - 42. </p>
<p>I will [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μελλω] </span></span>. I am about or have in mind. Not a declaration of immediate and inexorable doom, but implying a possibility of the determination being changed. </p>
<p>Spue [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμεσαι] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+18:28">Leviticus 18:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="le+20:22">Leviticus 20:22</span>.</p>
