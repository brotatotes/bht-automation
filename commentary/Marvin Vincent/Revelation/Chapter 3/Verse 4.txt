<p>Thou hast a few names. The best texts insert ajlla but between these words and the close of the preceding verse. So Rev. But, notwithstanding the general apathy of the Church, thou hast a few, etc. Compare verse 1, thou hast a name, and see on chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+11:13">Revelation 11:13</span>. Names is equivalent to persons, a few who may be rightly named as exceptions to the general conception. </p>
<p>Even in Sardis. Omit kai even. </p>
<p>Defiled [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εμολυναν] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:4">1 Peter 1:4</span>. </p>
<p>Garments. See the same figure, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:23">Jude 1:23</span>. The meaning is, have not sullied the purity of their Christian life. </p>
<p>In white [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν λευκοις] </span></span>. With iJmatioiv garments understood. See on chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+2:17">Revelation 2:17</span>, and compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="zec+3:3">Zechariah 3:3</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="zec+3:5">Zechariah 3:5</span>. "White colors are suitable to the gods" (Plato, "Laws," 12, 956). So Virgil, of the tenants of Elysium : </p>
<p>"Lo, priests of holy life and chaste while they in life had part; Lo, God - loved poets, men who spake things worthy Phoebus' heart : And they who bettered life on earth by new - found mastery; And they whose good deeds left a tale for men to name them by : And all they had their brows about with snowy fillets bound." " Aeneid, " 6, 661 - 665 </p>
<p>The same shall be clothed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτος περιβαλειται] </span></span>. For ou=tov this, or the same, read outwv thus : "shall thus be arrayed." so Rev. The verb denotes a solemn investiture, and means literally to throw or put around.</p>
