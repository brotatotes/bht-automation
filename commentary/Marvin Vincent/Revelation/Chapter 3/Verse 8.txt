<p>I have set [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεδωκα] </span></span>. Lit., I have given. For a similar phrase see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:51">Luke 12:51</span>. </p>
<p>An open door (quran ajnewgmenhn). Rev., more literally, a door opened. This is variously explained. Some refer it to the entrance into the joy of the Lord; others to the initiation into the meaning of scripture; others again to the opportunity for the mission - work of the Church. In this last sense the phrase is often used by Paul. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:9">1 Corinthians 16:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:12">2 Corinthians 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+4:3">Colossians 4:3</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:27">Acts 14:27</span>. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:77">Acts 14:77</span> I have given is appropriate, since all opportunities of service are gifts of God. See on chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+2:7">Revelation 2:7</span>. For thou hast [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτι εχεις] </span></span>. Some texts make behold - shut parenthetical, and render oti that, defining thy works, etc. So Rev. </p>
<p>A little strength [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μικραν δυναμιν] </span></span>. This would mean, thou hast some power, though small. Many, however, omit the indefinite article in translating, and render thou hast little strength; i e., thou art poor in numbers and worldly resources. So Alford, Trench, and D?ieck. And [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">και] </span></span>. John's single copula instead of a particle of logical connection. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:10">John 1:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:46">John 6:46</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:5">1 John 1:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:20">John 8:20</span>. </p>
<p>Hast kept my word [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ετηρησας μου τον λογον] </span></span>. Rev., rendering the aorist more strictly, didst keep. For the phrase, see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:6">John 17:6</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:8">John 17:8</span>.</p>
