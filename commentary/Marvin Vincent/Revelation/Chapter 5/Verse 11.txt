<p>Ten thousand times ten thousand [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μυριαδες μυριαδων] </span></span>. Lit., ten thousands of ten thousands. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+68:17">Psalms 68:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="da+8:10">Daniel 8:10</span>. Muriav, whence the English myriad, means the number ten thousand. So, literally, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:19">Acts 19:19</span>, ajrguriou muriadav pente fifty - thousand pieces of silver; lit., five ten - thousands. In the plural used for an unlimited number. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:1">Luke 12:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:20">Acts 21:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:22">Hebrews 12:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:14">Jude 1:14</span>. </p>
<p>Thousands [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χιλιαδες] </span></span>. Ciliav, a collective term like, muriav, meaning the number one thousand, is almost invariably used with men in Revelation. See chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+7:4">Revelation 7:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+11:13">Revelation 11:13</span>. Only once with a material object (chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+21:16">Revelation 21:16</span>). With inferior objects cilioi a thousand is used. See chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+11:3">Revelation 11:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+12:6">Revelation 12:6</span>. These words are the theme of Alford's noble hymn - "Ten thousand times ten thousand In sparkling raiment bright, The armies of the ransomed saints Throng up the steeps of light : 'Tis finished, all is finished, Their fight with death and sin; Fling open wide the golden gates, And let the victors in."</p>
