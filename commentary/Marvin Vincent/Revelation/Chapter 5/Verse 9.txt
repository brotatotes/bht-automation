<p>They sing. Present tense, denoting the continuous, unceasing worship of heaven, or possibly, as describing their "office generally rather than the mere one particular case of its exercise" (Alford). </p>
<p>Redeem [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηγορασας] </span></span>. Lit., purchase, as Rev. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:8">John 4:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:5">John 6:5</span>. Us. Omit us and supply men, as Rev. </p>
<p>With Thy blood [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν τω αιματι σου] </span></span>. Lit., "in Thy blood." The preposition in is used Hebraistically of the price; the value of the thing purchased being contained in the price. </p>
<p>Kindred [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φυλης] </span></span>. Rev., tribe. Often in the New Testament of the twelve tribes of Israel. </p>
<p>People, nation [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαου, εθνους] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:9">1 Peter 2:9</span>.</p>
