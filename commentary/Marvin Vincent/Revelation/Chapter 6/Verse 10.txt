<p>They cried [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκραζον] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+5:5">Mark 5:5</span>. </p>
<p>How long [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εως ποτε] </span></span>. Lit., until when. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="zec+1:12">Zechariah 1:12</span>. </p>
<p>O Lord [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο δεσποτης] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:1">2 Peter 2:1</span>. Only here in Revelation. Addressed to God rather than to Christ, and breathing, as Professor Milligan remarks, "the feeling of Old Testament rather than of New Testament relation." Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:24">Acts 4:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:4">Jude 1:4</span>. </p>
<p>True [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αληθινος] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:9">John 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+3:7">Revelation 3:7</span>. </p>
<p>Judge [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κρινεις] </span></span>. Originally the verb means to separate; thence the idea of selection : to pick out, and so to discriminate or judge. </p>
<p>Avenge [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκδικεις] </span></span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:3">Luke 18:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:19">Romans 12:19</span>. </p>
<p>On the earth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επι της γης] </span></span>. Earth, in Revelation, is generally to be understood of the ungodly earth.</p>
