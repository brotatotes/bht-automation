<p>I said [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ειρηκα] </span></span>. Lit., I have said. Rev., renders by the present, I say. See on cried. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:15">John 1:15</span>. </p>
<p>Sir [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυριε] </span></span>. Add mou my, and render, as Rev., my Lord. An address of reverence as to a heavenly being. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:3">Matthew 21:3</span>. </p>
<p>Which came [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οι ερχομενοι] </span></span>. The present participle. Hence, as Rev., which come. </p>
<p>Out of great tribulation [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εκ της θλιψεως της μεγαλης] </span></span>. Lit., out of the tribulation, the great (tribulation). Rev., properly, gives the force of the article, "the great." See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:21">Matthew 13:21</span>. </p>
<p>Have washed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επλυναν] </span></span>. The aorist tense. Rev., correctly, they washed. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:2">Luke 5:2</span>, on which see note. For the New Testament words for washing, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:33">Acts 16:33</span>. </p>
<p>Made them white. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+1:18">Isaiah 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+51:7">Psalms 51:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:3">Mark 9:3</span>. Milligan remarks that robes are the expression of character, and compares the word habit used of dress.</p>
