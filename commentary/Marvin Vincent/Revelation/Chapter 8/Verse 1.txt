<p>When [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οτε] </span></span>. Read otan, the indefinite particle with the indicative mood. For a similar construction, see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:19">Mark 11:19</span> (correct reading). Alford observes that it occurs in the opening of this seal only, giving it an indefiniteness which does not belong to any of the rest. </p>
<p>There was [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εγενετο] </span></span>. More literally, come to pass. Rev., there followed. About [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ως] </span></span>. A usual form of expression with John. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:39">John 1:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:19">John 6:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:18">John 11:18</span>.</p>
