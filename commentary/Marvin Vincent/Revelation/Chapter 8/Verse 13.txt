<p>An angel [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ενος αγγελου] </span></span>. For angel read ajetou eagle. Lit., one eagle. The eagle is a symbol of vengeance in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+28:49">Deuteronomy 28:49</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ho+8:1">Hosea 8:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="hab+1:8">Habakkuk 1:8</span>. </p>
<p>Mid heaven [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεσουρανηματι] </span></span>. Only in Revelation, here, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+14:6">Revelation 14:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+19:17">Revelation 19:17</span>. It means, properly, the meridian, the highest point in the heavens which the sun occupies at noon; not the space between heaven and earth. </p>
