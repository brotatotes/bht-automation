<p>Fall [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεπτωκοτα] </span></span>. Lit., fallen. The star had fallen before and is seen as fallen. Rev., properly construes star with from heaven instead of with fallen. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+14:12">Isaiah 14:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:18">Luke 10:18</span>. </p>
<p>Of the bottomless pit [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του φρεατος της αβυσσου] </span></span>. Rev., of the pit of the abyss. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:6">John 4:6</span>, and compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:5">Luke 14:5</span>. It is not however a pit that is locked, but the long shaft leading to the abyss, like a well - shaft, which, in the East, is oftener covered and locked.</p>
