<p>They had a king over them (ecousin ejf' aujtwn basilea). Render, as Rev., they have over them as king. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="pr+30:27">Proverbs 30:27</span>. Hence distinguished from the natural locusts. </p>
<p>In Hebrew [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβραιστι] </span></span>. Used only by John. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:2">John 5:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:13">John 19:13</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:17">John 19:17</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:20">John 19:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+16:16">Revelation 16:16</span>. </p>
<p>Abaddon. Meaning destruction. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="job+26:6">Job 26:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="job+28:22">Job 28:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="pr+14:11">Proverbs 14:11</span>. Here the Destroyer, as is evident from the Greek equivalent Apolluwn Apollyon destroyer. Perdition is personified. It is after John's manner to give the Hebrew with the Greek equivalent. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:38">John 1:38</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:42">John 1:42</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:25">John 4:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:7">John 9:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:16">John 11:16</span>, etc.</p>
