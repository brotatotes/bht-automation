<p>Repented not of the works [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ουτε μετενοησαν εκ των εργων] </span></span>. Lit., "Out of the works." The preposition ejk out of with repent, denotes a moral change involving an abandonment of evil works. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:2">Matthew 3:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:29">Matthew 21:29</span>. </p>
<p>Works of their hands. Not their course of life, but the idols which their hands had made. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+4:28">Deuteronomy 4:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+134:15">Psalms 134:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+7:4">Acts 7:4</span>. Devils [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δαιμονια] </span></span>. More properly, demons. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:34">Mark 1:34</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:20">1 Corinthians 10:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:1">1 Timothy 4:1</span>. </p>
<p>See, hear, walk. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="da+5:23">Daniel 5:23</span>.</p>
