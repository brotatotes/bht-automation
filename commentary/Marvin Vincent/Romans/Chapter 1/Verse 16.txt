<p>For [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">γαρ] </span></span>. Marking the transition from the introduction to the treatise. "I am ready to preach at Rome, for, though I might seem to be deterred by the contempt in which the Gospel is held, and by the prospect of my own humiliation as its preacher, I am not ashamed of it." The transition occupies vers. 16, 17. </p>
<p>The Gospel. Omit of Christ. </p>
<p>Power [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυναμις] </span></span>. Not merely a powerful means in God 's hands, but in itself a divine energy. </p>
<p>First. Not principally, nor in preference to the Greek; but first in point of time. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:22">John 4:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:1">Romans 3:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:1">Romans 9:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:24">Matthew 14:24</span>.</p>
