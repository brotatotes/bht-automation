<p>Brethren. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+2:9">1 John 2:9</span>. An expression of affectionate interest and indicating emotion. </p>
<p>My heart 's desire [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η ευδοκια της εμης καρδιας] </span></span>. More literally, the good will of my heart. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:14">Luke 2:14</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:15">Philippians 1:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+2:13">Philippians 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:5">Ephesians 1:5</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:9">Ephesians 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:11">2 Thessalonians 1:11</span>. </p>
<p>Prayer [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δεησις] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:33">Luke 5:33</span>. </p>
<p>To God [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προς] </span></span>. Implying communion. See on with God, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:1">John 1:1</span>. </p>
<p>For Israel. The best texts substitute aujtwn for them; those described in the last three verses of ch. 9. Bengel remarks that Paul would not have prayed had they been utterly reprobate. </p>
<p>That they may be saved [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις σωτηριαν] </span></span>. Lit., unto (their) salvation.</p>
