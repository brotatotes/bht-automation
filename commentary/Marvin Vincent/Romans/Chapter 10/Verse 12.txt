<p>For. Explaining the whosoever of ver. 11. </p>
<p>Difference. Better, as Rev., distinction. See on 3 22. </p>
<p>Jew and Greek. On Greek, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:1">Acts 6:1</span>. Greeks here equivalent to Gentiles. </p>
<p>Lord [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κυριος] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:3">Matthew 21:3</span>. The reference is disputed : some Christ, others God. Probably Christ. See ver. 9, and compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:36">Acts 10:36</span>. The hearing which is necessary to believing comes through the word of Christ (ver. 17, where the reading is Christ instead of God). </p>
<p>That call upon [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επικαλουμενους] </span></span>. See on appeal, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:11">Acts 25:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:7">James 2:7</span>. That invoke Him as, Lord : recalling vers. 9, 10. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joe+2:32">Joel 2:32</span>.</p>
