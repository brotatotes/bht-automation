<p>Be sent (ajpostalwsin). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:16">Matthew 10:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:29">Mark 4:29</span>. </p>
<p>Beautiful [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωραιοι] </span></span>. From wra the time of full bloom or development. Hence the radical idea of the word includes both blooming maturity and vigor. Appropriate here to the swift, vigorous feet. Plato (" Republic, " 10. 601) distinguishes between faces that are beautiful [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καλων</span></span>] and blooming [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ωραιων] </span></span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ge+2:9">Genesis 2:9</span> (Sept.) of the trees of Eden. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:27">Matthew 23:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:2">Acts 3:2</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:10">Acts 3:10</span>. </p>
<p>Feet. Emphasizing the rapid approach of the messenger. "In their running and hastening, in their scaling obstructing mountains, and in their appearance and descent from mountains, they are the symbols of the earnestly - desired, winged movement and appearance of the Gospel itself" (Lange). Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="na+1:15">Nahum 1:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:15">Ephesians 6:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:15">Romans 3:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:9">Acts 5:9</span>. Paul omits the mountains from the citation. Omit that preach the gospel of peace. </p>
<p>Bring glad tidings. See on Gospel, Matthew, superscription. </p>
<p>16 Obeyed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπηκουσαν] </span></span>. See on obedience and disobedience, ch. 5 19. Also on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:29">Acts 5:29</span>. Obeyed as the result of listening, and so especially appropriate here. Compare head and hear, ver. 14. For the same reason hearken (Rev.) is better than obeyed. </p>
<p>Report [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακοη] </span></span>. Lit., hearing. Similarly, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:1">Matthew 14:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:7">Mark 13:7</span>. Compare the phrase word of hearing, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:13">1 Thessalonians 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:2">Hebrews 4:2</span> (Rev.); and hearing of faith, i e., message of faith, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:2">Galatians 3:2</span>.</p>
