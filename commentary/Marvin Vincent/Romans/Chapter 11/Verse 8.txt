<p>It is written. Three quotations follow, two of which we blended in this verse : <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+29:10">Isaiah 29:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+29:3">Deuteronomy 29:3</span>. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+29:55">Deuteronomy 29:55</span> Hath given [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οδωκεν] </span></span>. Heb., poured out. Sept., given to drink. </p>
<p>Slumber [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατανυξεως] </span></span>. Heb., deep sleep. Only here in the New Testament. Lit., pricking or piercing, compunction. Compare the kindred verb katenughsan were pricked, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:37">Acts 2:37</span>. Rev. renders stupor, the secondary meaning; properly the stupefaction following a wound or blow.</p>
