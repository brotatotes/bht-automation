It is written. Three quotations follow, two of which we blended in this verse : Isaiah 29:10; Deuteronomy 29:3. Deuteronomy 29:55 Hath given [οδωκεν] . Heb., poured out. Sept., given to drink. Slumber [κατανυξεως] . Heb., deep sleep. Only here in the New Testament. Lit., pricking or piercing, compunction. Compare the kindred verb katenughsan were pricked, Acts 2:37. Rev. renders stupor, the secondary meaning; properly the stupefaction following a wound or blow.
