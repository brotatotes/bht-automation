<p>Exhortation. Aimed at the heart and will. See on consolation, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:24">Luke 6:24</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:3">1 Corinthians 14:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:36">Acts 4:36</span>, where Rev. gives son of exhortation. </p>
<p>He that giveth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο μεταδιδους] </span></span>. Earthly possessions. The preposition meta indicates sharing with. He that imparteth. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:28">Ephesians 4:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:11">Luke 3:11</span>. </p>
<p>Simplicity [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απλοτητι] </span></span>. See on single, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:22">Matthew 6:22</span>, and compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:5">James 1:5</span>, where it is said that God gives aJplwv simply. See note there. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:2">2 Corinthians 8:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:11">2 Corinthians 9:11</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+9:13">2 Corinthians 9:13</span>, the A. V. gives liberality; and in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:5">James 1:5</span>, liberally. Rev. accepts this in the last - named passage, but gives singleness in margin. In all the others liberality is, at best, very doubtful. The sense is unusual, and the rendering simplicity or singleness is defensible in all the passages. </p>
<p>He that ruleth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο προισταμενος] </span></span>. Lit., he that is placed in front. The reference is to any position involving superintendence. No special ecclesiastical office is meant. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:8">Titus 3:8</span>, to maintain good works; the idea of presiding over running into that of carrying on or practicing. See note there. Compare also prostativ succorer, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:2">Romans 16:2</span>, and see note. </p>
<p>With diligence [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν σπουδη] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jude+1:3">Jude 1:3</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:25">Mark 6:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:39">Luke 1:39</span>, it is rendered haste. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:11">2 Corinthians 7:11</span>, carefulness (Rev., earnest care). In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+7:12">2 Corinthians 7:12</span>, care (Rev., earnest care). In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:8">2 Corinthians 8:8</span>, forwardness (Rev., earnestness). In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:16">2 Corinthians 8:16</span>, earnest care. With cheerfulness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν ιλαροτητι] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. It reappears in the Latin hilaritas; English, hilarity, exhilarate. "The joyful eagerness, the amiable grace, the affability going the length of gayety, which make the visitor a sunbeam penetrating into the sick - chamber, and to the heart of the afflicted."</p>
