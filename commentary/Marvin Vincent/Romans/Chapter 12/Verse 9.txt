<p>Love [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η αγαπη] </span></span>. The article has the force of your. See on loveth, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:20">John 5:20</span>. </p>
<p>Without dissimulation [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανυποκριτος] </span></span>. Rev., without hypocrisy. See on hypocrites, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:13">Matthew 23:13</span>. </p>
<p>Abhor [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποστυγουντες] </span></span>. Lit., abhorring. The only simple verb for hate in the New Testament is misew. Stugew, quite frequent in the classics, does not occur except in this compound, which is found only here. The kindred adjective stughtov hateful, is found <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:3">1 Timothy 3:3</span>. The original distinction between misew and stugew is that the former denotes concealed and cherished hatred, and the latter hatred expressed. The preposition ajpo away from, may either denote separation or be merely intensive. An intense sentiment is meant : loathing. </p>
<p>Cleave [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κολλωμενοι] </span></span>. See on joined himself, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:15">Luke 14:15</span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:34">Acts 17:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:16">1 Corinthians 6:16</span>.</p>
