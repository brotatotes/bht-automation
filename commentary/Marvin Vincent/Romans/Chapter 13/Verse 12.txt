<p>Is far spent [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προεκοψεν] </span></span>. The A. V. gives a variety of renderings to this verb. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:52">Luke 2:52</span>, increased; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:14">Galatians 1:14</span>, profited; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:9">2 Timothy 3:9</span>, proceed; 13, wax. The word originally means to beat forward or lengthen out by hammering. Hence to promote, and intransitively to go forward or proceed. </p>
<p>Let us cast off [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποθωμεθα] </span></span>. As one puts off the garments of the night. For this use of the simple tiqhmi, see on giveth his life, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:11">John 10:11</span>.</p>
