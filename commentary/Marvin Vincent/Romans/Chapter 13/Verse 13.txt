<p>Honestly [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ευσχημονως] </span></span>. Honest is originally honorable, and so here. Compare Wyclif's version of <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:23">1 Corinthians 12:23</span> : "And the members that be unhonest have more honesty; for our honest members have need of none." From euj well, schma fashion. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:2">Matthew 17:2</span>. Hence becomingly. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:40">1 Corinthians 14:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:12">1 Thessalonians 4:12</span>. The word refers more particularly to the outward life, and thus accords with walk, and in the day the time of observation. </p>
<p>Rioting [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κωμοις] </span></span>. Lit., revellings. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:3">1 Peter 4:3</span>. </p>
<p>Drunkenness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μεθαισ] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:34">Luke 21:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:10">John 2:10</span>. </p>
<p>Wantonness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ασελγειαις] </span></span>. See on lasciviousness, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:22">Mark 7:22</span>. All these three are plural : riotings, drunkennesses, wantonnesses. </p>
<p>Envying [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ζηλω] </span></span>. Rev., jealousy. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:14">James 3:14</span>.</p>
