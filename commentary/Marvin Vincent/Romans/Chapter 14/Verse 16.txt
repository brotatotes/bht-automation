<p>Your good [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υμων το αγαθον] </span></span>. Referring, most probably, to the liberty of the strong. Others think that the whole Church is addressed, in which case good would refer to the gospel doctrine. 68 Be evil spoken of [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">βλασφημεισθω] </span></span>. See on blasphemy, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:22">Mark 7:22</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:30">1 Corinthians 10:30</span>, it is used of evil - speaking by members of the Church, which favors the reference of good to the strong.</p>
