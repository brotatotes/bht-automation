<p>Assist [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">παραστητι] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:3">Acts 1:3</span> It is used as a legal term, of presenting culprits or witnesses in a court of justice. Compare prove, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:13">Acts 24:13</span>. From this, and from the term prostativ succorer, it has been inferred that Phoebe was going to Rome on private legal business (see Conybeare and Howson). This is a mere fancy. </p>
<p>Succorer [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προστατις] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. The word means patroness. It may refer to her official duties. The word is an honorable one, and accords with her official position.</p>
