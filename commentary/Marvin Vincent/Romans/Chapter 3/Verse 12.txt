<p>They are together become unprofitable [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμα ηχρειωθησαν] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament : Together carries forward the all. The Hebrew of the Psalm means have become corrupt. The Greek word is to become useless. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:6">John 14:6</span>. </p>
<p>Good [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">χρησττοτητα] </span></span>. Only in Paul 's writings. The radical idea of the word is profitableness. Compare have become unprofitable. Hence it passes readily into the meaning of wholesomeness. See on, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:30">Matthew 11:30</span>. It is opposed by Paul ' to ajpotomia abruptness, severity (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:22">Romans 11:22</span>). It is rendered kindness in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:7">Ephesians 2:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+3:12">Colossians 3:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:22">Galatians 5:22</span>. Paul, and he only, also uses ajgaqwsunh for goodness. The distinction as drawn out by Jerome is that ajgaqwsunh represents a sterner virtue, showing itself in a zeal for truth which rebukes, corrects, and chastises, as Christ when He purged the temple. Crhstothv is more gentle, gracious, and kindly Bishop Lightfoot defines it as a kindly disposition to one's neighbor, not necessarily taking a practical form, while ajgaqwsunh energizes the crhstothv.</p>
