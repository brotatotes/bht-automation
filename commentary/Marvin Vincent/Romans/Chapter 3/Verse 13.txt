<p>Open sepulchre [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ταφος ανεωγμενος] </span></span>. Lit., a sepulchre opened or standing open. Some explain the figure by the noisome exhalations from a tomb. Others refer it to a pit standing open and ready to devour, comparing <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jer+5:16">Jeremiah 5:16</span>, where the quiver of the Chaldaeans is called an open sepulchre. So Meyer and Morison. Godet compares the phrase used of a brutal man : "it seems as if he would like to eat you." Compare Dante's vision of the lion : </p>
<p>"With head uplifted and with ravenous hunger, So that it seemed the air was afraid of him." " Inferno, " 1, 47. </p>
<p>Have used deceit [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εδολιουσαν] </span></span>. Hebrew, they smoothed their tongues. </p>
<p>Guile is contrasted with violence in the previous clause. Wyc., with their tongues they did guilingly. The imperfect tense denotes perseverance in their hypocritical professions.</p>
