<p>Now [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νυνι</span></span>] Logical, not temporal. In this state of the case. Expressing the contrast between two relations - dependence on the law and non - dependence on the law. </p>
<p>Without the law. In a sphere different from that in which the law says "Do this and live." </p>
<p>Is manifested [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πεφανερωται] </span></span>. Rev., hath been manifested, rendering the perfect tense more strictly. Hath been manifested and now lies open to view. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:1">John 21:1</span>, and on revelation, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+1:1">Revelation 1:1</span> The word implies a previous hiding. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:22">Mark 4:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:26">Colossians 1:26</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:27">Colossians 1:27</span>. </p>
<p>Being witnessed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαρτυρουμενη] </span></span>. Borne witness to; attested. The present participle indicates that this testimony is now being borne by the Old Testament to the new dispensation.</p>
