<p>That sinned [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αμαρτησαντος] </span></span>. The better supported reading. Some MSS. and versions read aJmarthmatov transgression. </p>
<p>Of one. Some explain, one man, from the preceding (one) that sinned. Others, one trespass, from ver. 17. </p>
<p>The judgment [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κριμα] </span></span>. Judicial sentence. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:7">1 Corinthians 6:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:29">1 Corinthians 11:29</span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2pe+2:3">2 Peter 2:3</span>. </p>
<p>Condemnation [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατακριμα] </span></span>. See on shall be damned, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+16:16">Mark 16:16</span>. A condemnatory sentence. </p>
<p>Justification [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δικαιωμα] </span></span>. Not the subjective state of justification, but a righteous act or deed. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+19:8">Revelation 19:8</span>; see on ver. 18. </p>
<p>The word is sometimes rendered orinance, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:1">Hebrews 9:1</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:10">Hebrews 9:10</span>; an appointment of God having the force of law. So <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:32">Romans 1:32</span>, where Rev. gives ordinance for judgment, and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:26">Romans 2:26</span>, ordinances for righteousness.</p>
