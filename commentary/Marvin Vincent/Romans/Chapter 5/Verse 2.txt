<p>Access [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">προσαγωγην] </span></span>. Used only by Paul. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:18">Ephesians 2:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:12">Ephesians 3:12</span>. Lit., the act of bringing to. Hence some insist on the transitive sense, introduction. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:18">1 Peter 3:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:13">Ephesians 2:13</span>. The transitive sense predominates in classical Greek, but there are undoubted instances of the intransitive sense in later Greek, and some illustrations are cited from Xenophon, though their meaning is disputed. 33 Into this grace. Grace is conceived as a field into which we are brought. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:6">Galatians 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:4">Galatians 5:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:12">1 Peter 5:12</span>. The; state of justification which is preeminently a matter of grace. </p>
<p>In hope [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επ ελπιδι] </span></span>. Lit., on the ground of hope.</p>
