<p>After the manner of men [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανθρωπινον] </span></span>. Lit., what is human, popularly. He seems to have felt that the figures of service, bondage, etc., were unworthy of the subject, and apologizes for his use of the image of the slave mart to enforce such a high spiritual truth, on the ground of their imperfect spiritual comprehension. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:6">2 Corinthians 2:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:1">1 Corinthians 3:1</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:2">1 Corinthians 3:2</span>. </p>
<p>To iniquity unto iniquity [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τη ανομια εις την ανομιαν] </span></span>. Iniquity issuing in an abiding iniquitous state. Lit., lawlessness. It is used by John as the definition of sin, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:4">1 John 3:4</span>. </p>
<p>Holiness [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγιασμον] </span></span>. Rev., sanctification. For the kindred adjective agiov holy, see on saints, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:10">Acts 26:10</span>. Agiasmov is used in the New Testament both of a process - the inauguration and maintenance of the life of fellowship with God, and of the resultant state of sanctification. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:3">1 Thessalonians 4:3</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+4:7">1 Thessalonians 4:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:13">2 Thessalonians 2:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:15">1 Timothy 2:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:2">1 Peter 1:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:14">Hebrews 12:14</span>. It is difficult to determine which is meant here. The passages in Thessalonians, Timothy, and Hebrews, are cited by interpreters on both sides. As in ver. 22 it appears that sanctification contemplates a further result (everlasting life), it is perhaps better to understand it as the process. Yield your members to righteousness in order to carry on the progressive work of sanctification, perfecting holiness (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+7:1">1 Corinthians 7:1</span>).</p>
