<p>Know ye not [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αγνοειτε] </span></span>. The expression is stronger : are ye ignorant. So Rev. The indicative mood presupposes an acquaintance with the moral nature of baptism, and a consequent absurdity in the idea of persisting in sin. </p>
<p>So many as [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οσοι] </span></span>. Rev., all we who. Put differently from we that (oitinev, ver. 2) as not characterizing but designating all collectively. Baptized into [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εις] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:19">Matthew 28:19</span>. The preposition. denotes inward union, participation; not in order to bring about the union, for that has been effected. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:12">1 Corinthians 12:12</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:13">1 Corinthians 12:13</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:27">1 Corinthians 12:27</span>. </p>
<p>Into His death. As He died to sin, so we die to sin, just as if we were literally members of His body. Godet gives an anecdote related by a missionary who was questioning a converted Bechuana on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+3:3">Colossians 3:3</span>. The convert said : "Soon I shall be dead, and they will bury me in my field. My flocks will come to pasture above me. But I shall no longer hear them, and I shall not come forth from my tomb to take them and carry them with me to the sepulchre. They will be strange to me, as I to them. Such is the image of my life in the midst of the world since I believed in Christ."</p>
