<p>We are buried with [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνεταφημεν] </span></span>. Rev., more accurately, were buried. Therefore, as a natural consequence of death. There is probably an allusion to the immersion of baptism. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+3:3">Colossians 3:3</span>. </p>
<p>Into death. Through the baptism into death referred to in ver. 3. Both A. V. and Rev. omit the article, which is important for the avoidance of the error buried into death. </p>
<p>Glory [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δοξης] </span></span>. The glorious collective perfection of God See on 3 23. Here the element of power is emphasized, which is closely related to the idea of divine glory. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:11">Colossians 1:11</span>. All the perfections of God contribute to the resurrection of Christ - righteousness, mercy, wisdom, holiness. </p>
<p>We might walk [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιπατησωμεν] </span></span>. Lit., walk about, implying habitual conduct. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:9">John 11:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1jo+1:6">1 John 1:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:4">3 John 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:44">Luke 11:44</span>. </p>
<p>In newness of life [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εν καινοτητι ζωης] </span></span>. A stronger expression than new life. It gives more prominence to the main idea, newness, than would be given by the adjective. Thus <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:17">1 Timothy 6:17</span>, uncertainty of riches; not uncertain riches, as A. V.</p>
