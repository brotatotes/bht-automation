<p>I do [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεργαζομαι] </span></span>. See on ver. 8. Accomplish, achieve. Here appropriately used of carrying out another's will. I do not perceive the outcome of my sinful life. </p>
<p>I allow not [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ου γινωσκω] </span></span>. Allow is used by A. V. in the earlier English sense of approve. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:48">Luke 11:48</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:22">Romans 14:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:4">1 Thessalonians 2:4</span>. Shakespeare : "Thou shalt hold the opinion of Pythagoras as I will allow of thy wits" (" Twelfth Night, "4, 2). But the meaning of ginwskw is not approve, but recognize, come to know, perceive. Hence Rev., I know not. Paul says :" What I carry out I do not recognize in its true nature, as a slave who ignorantly performs his master's behest without knowing its tendency or result. " </p>
<p>I would [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">θελω] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+1:19">Matthew 1:19</span>. Rather desire than will in the sense of full determination, as is shown by I consent (ver. 16), and I delight in (ver. 22). </p>
<p>Do I not [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρασσω] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:21">John 3:21</span>. Rev., correctly, practice : the daily doing which issues in accomplishment [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατεργαζομαι] </span></span>. </p>
<p>Do I [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ποιω] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:21">John 3:21</span>. More nearly akin to katergazomai I accomplish, realize. "When I have acted [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρασσω</span></span>] I find myself face to face with a result which my moral instinct condemns" (Godet). I do not practice what I would, and the outcome is what I hate.</p>
