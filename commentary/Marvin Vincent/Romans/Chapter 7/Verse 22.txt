<p>I delight in [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συνηδομαι] </span></span>. Lit., I rejoice with. Stronger than I consent unto (ver., 16). It is the agreement of moral sympathy. </p>
<p>The inward man [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τον εσω ανθρωπον] </span></span>. The rational and moral I, the essence of the man which is conscious of itself as an ethical personality. Not to be confounded with the new man (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:24">Ephesians 4:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+3:10">Colossians 3:10</span>). 41 It is substantially the same with the mind (ver. 23).</p>
