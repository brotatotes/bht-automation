<p>Earnest expectation [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αποκαραδοκια] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:20">Philippians 1:20</span>. From ajpo away kara the head, dokein to watch. A watching with the head erect or outstretched. Hence a waiting in suspense. 'Apo from, implies abstraction, the attention turned from other objects. The classical student will recall the watchman in the opening of Aeschylus' "Agamemnon," awaiting the beacon which is to announce the capture of Troy. </p>
<p>Creature [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κτισεως] </span></span>. The word may signify either the creative act (as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:20">Romans 1:20</span>), or the thing created (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:6">Mark 10:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:19">Mark 13:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+16:15">Mark 16:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+1:23">Colossians 1:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:13">Hebrews 4:13</span>). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:13">1 Peter 2:13</span>. Here in the latter sense. The interpretations vary : 1. The whole unredeemed creation, rational and irrational. 2. All creation, except humanity. The point of difference is the inclusion or exclusion of humanity. The second explanation is preferable, the non - rational creation viewed collectively, animate and inanimate. Equivalent to all nature. </p>
<p>Waiteth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">απεκδεχεται] </span></span>. Only in Paul and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:28">Hebrews 9:28</span>. The whole passage, with the expressions waiting, sighing, hoping, bondage, is poetical and prophetic. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ps+19:2">Psalms 19:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+11:6">Isaiah 11:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+14:8">Isaiah 14:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+55:12">Isaiah 55:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+65:17">Isaiah 65:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eze+31:15">Ezekiel 31:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eze+37:0">Ezekiel 37:0</span>.; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="hab+2:11">Habakkuk 2:11</span>.</p>
