<p>Helpeth [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">συναντιλαμβανεται] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:40">Luke 10:40</span>, on which see note. "Lambanetai taketh. Precisely the same verb in precisely the same phrase, which is translated 'took our infirmities '," <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:17">Matthew 8:17</span> (Bushnell). </p>
<p>As we ought [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">καθο δει] </span></span>. Not with reference to the form of prayer, but to the circumstances : in proportion to the need. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:12">2 Corinthians 8:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+4:13">1 Peter 4:13</span>. </p>
<p>Maketh intercession for [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">υπερεντυγχανει] </span></span>. Only here in the New Testament. The verb ejntugcanw means to light upon or fall in with; to go to meet for consultation, conversation, or supplication. So <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+25:24">Acts 25:24</span>, "dealt with," Rev., "made suit." Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:34">Romans 8:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:2">Romans 11:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:25">Hebrews 7:25</span>. </p>
<p>Which cannot be uttered [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αλαλητοις] </span></span>. This may mean either unutterable or unuttered..</p>
