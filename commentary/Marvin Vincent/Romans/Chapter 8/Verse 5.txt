<div class="comm">They that are (οἱ ὄντες)<p>Wider in meaning than walk, which expresses the manifestation of the condition expressed by are.</p><p>Do mind (φρονοῦσιν)</p><p>The verb primarily means to have understanding; then to feel or think (<a href="//bible.cc/1_corinthians/13-11.htm">1 Corinthians 13:11</a>); to have an opinion (<a href="//bible.cc/romans/12-3.htm">Romans 12:3</a>). Hence to judge (<a href="//bible.cc/acts/28-22.htm">Acts 28:22</a>; <a href="//bible.cc/galatians/5-10.htm">Galatians 5:10</a>; <a href="//bible.cc/philippians/3-15.htm">Philippians 3:15</a>). To direct the mind to something, and so to seek or strive for (<a href="//niv.scripturetext.com/matthew/16-23.htm">Matthew 16:23</a>, note; <a href="//bible.cc/philippians/3-19.htm">Philippians 3:19</a>; <a href="//bible.cc/colossians/3-2.htm">Colossians 3:2</a>). So here. The object of their thinking and striving is fleshly. </p></div>
