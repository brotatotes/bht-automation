<p>A power or influence, the character, manifestations, or results of which are more peculiarly defined by qualifying genitives. Thus spirit of meekness, faith, power, wisdom. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:2">Romans 8:2</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:15">Romans 8:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:21">1 Corinthians 4:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:13">2 Corinthians 4:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:1">Galatians 6:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:17">Ephesians 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:7">2 Timothy 1:7</span>, etc. </p>
<p>These combinations with the genitives are not mere periphrases for a faculty or disposition of man. By the spirit of meekness or wisdom, for instance, is not meant merely a meek or wise spirit; but that meekness, wisdom, power, etc., are gifts of the Spirit of God. This usage is according to Old Testament analogy. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+28:3">Exodus 28:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+31:3">Exodus 31:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+35:31">Exodus 35:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="isa+11:2">Isaiah 11:2</span>.</p>
