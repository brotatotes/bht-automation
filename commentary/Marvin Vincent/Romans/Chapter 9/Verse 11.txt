<p>Purpose according to election [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η κατ εκλογην προθεσις</span></span>] For proqesiv purpose, see on the kindred verb proeqeto, ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:25">Romans 3:25</span>, and compare ch <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:28">Romans 8:28</span>. The phrase signifies a purpose so formed that in it an election was made. The opposite of one founded upon right or merit. For similar phrases see <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:20">Acts 19:20</span>; kata kratov according to might, mightily; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:13">Romans 7:13</span>, kaq' uJperbolhn according to excess, exceedingly See note Might stand [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μενη] </span></span>. Lit., abide, continue : remain unchangeable. This unchangeableness of purpose was conveyed in His declaration to Rebecca. Contrast with come to nought (ver. 6). </p>
