<p>Saith. Present tense. "There is an element of tirelessness in the utterance. If the scripture ever spoke at all, it continued and continues to speak. It has never been struck dumb" (Morison). </p>
<p>Pharaoh. The original meaning of the word is now supposed to be the double house or palace. Compare the Sublime Porte. </p>
<p>Raised thee up [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εξηγειρα] </span></span>. Hebrew, caused thee to stand. Sept., diethrhqhv thou wast preserved alive. Only once elsewhere in the New Testament, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:14">1 Corinthians 6:14</span>, of raising from the dead. The meaning here is general, allowed thee to appear; brought, thee forward on the stage of events, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="zec+11:16">Zechariah 11:16</span>. So the simple verb in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:11">Matthew 11:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:52">John 7:52</span>. Other explanations are, preserved thee alive, as Sept., excited thee to opposition, as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="hab+1:6">Habakkuk 1:6</span>; creded thee. </p>
<p>Might be declared [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">διαγγελη] </span></span>. Published abroad, thoroughly [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δια] </span></span>. So Rev. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:60">Luke 9:60</span>. "Even to the present day, wherever throughout the world Exod. is read, the divine intervention is realized" (Godet).</p>
