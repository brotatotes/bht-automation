Heaviness, sorrow [λυπη οδυνη] . Heaviness, so Wyc. and Tynd., in the earlier sense of sorrow. So Chaucer : "Who feeleth double sorrow and heaviness But Palamon?" "Knight's Tale," 1456 Shakespeare : "I am here, brother, full of heaviness." 2 "Henry iv," 4, 5, 8 Rev., sorrow. Odunh is better rendered pain. Some derive it from the root ed eat, as indicating, consuming pain. Compare Horace, curae edares devouring cares. Only here and 1 Timothy 6:10, Heart. See on ch. Romans 1:21.
