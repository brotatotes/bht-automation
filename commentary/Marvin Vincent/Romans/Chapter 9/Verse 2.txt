<p>Heaviness, sorrow [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λυπη οδυνη] </span></span>. Heaviness, so Wyc. and Tynd., in the earlier sense of sorrow. So Chaucer : </p>
<p>"Who feeleth double sorrow and heaviness But Palamon?" </p>
<p>"Knight's Tale," 1456 </p>
<p>Shakespeare : </p>
<p>"I am here, brother, full of heaviness." </p>
<p>2 "Henry iv," 4, 5, 8 </p>
<p>Rev., sorrow. Odunh is better rendered pain. Some derive it from the root ed eat, as indicating, consuming pain. Compare Horace, curae edares devouring cares. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:10">1 Timothy 6:10</span>, Heart. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:21">Romans 1:21</span>.</p>
