<p>I could wish [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ηυχομην] </span></span>. Or pray as <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+13:7">2 Corinthians 13:7</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+13:9">2 Corinthians 13:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:16">James 5:16</span>. Lit., I was wishing; but the imperfect here has a tentative force, implying the wish begun, but stopped at the outset by some antecedent consideration which renders it impossible, so that, practically, it was not entertained at all. So Paul of Onesimus : "Whom I could have wished [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εβουλομην</span></span>] to keep with me," if it had not been too much to ask (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:13">Philippians 1:13</span>). Paul would wish to save his countrymen, even at such sacrifice, if it were morally possible. Others, however, explain the imperfect as stating an actual wish formerly entertained. 50 Accursed from Christ [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αναθεμα απο του χριστου] </span></span>. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:8">Galatians 1:8</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:9">Galatians 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:3">1 Corinthians 12:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:22">1 Corinthians 16:22</span>. See on offerings, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:5">Luke 21:5</span>. Set apart to destruction and so separated from Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+1:21">Philippians 1:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+3:8">Philippians 3:8</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+3:20">Philippians 3:20</span>). An expression of deep devotion. "It is not easy to estimate the measure of love in a Moses and a Paul. For our limited reason does not grasp it, as the child cannot comprehend the courage of warriors" (Bengel). Compare Moses, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+32:32">Exodus 32:32</span>.</p>
