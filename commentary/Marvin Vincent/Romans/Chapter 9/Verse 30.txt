<p>Attained [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατελαβεν] </span></span>. See on perceived, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:13">Acts 4:13</span>, and taketh, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:18">Mark 9:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:5">John 1:5</span>. Compare attained (efqasen, ver. 31). Rev., arrive at. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:28">Matthew 12:28</span>. The meaning is substantially the same, only the imagery in the two words differs; the former being that of laying hold of a prize, and the latter of arriving at a goal. The latter is appropriate to following after, and is carried out in stumbling (ver. 32). </p>
<p>Even [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δε</span></span>] or and that. Subjoining something distinct and different from what precedes, though not sharply opposed to it. Attained righteousness, that is not that arising from these works, but from faith.</p>
