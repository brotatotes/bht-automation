<p>Who [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">οιτινες] </span></span>. The double relative characterizes the Israelites with their call and privileges as such that for them he could even wish himself accursed. </p>
<p>Israelites. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:12">Acts 3:12</span>. </p>
<p>Adoption. See on ch. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:15">Romans 8:15</span>. Israel is always represented as the Lord 's son or first - born among all peoples. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+4:22">Exodus 4:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+14:1">Deuteronomy 14:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ho+11:1">Hosea 11:1</span>. </p>
<p>The glory. The visible, luminous appearance of the divine presence was called by the Israelites the glory of Jahveh, or, in rabbinical phrase, the Shekinah. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+24:16">Exodus 24:16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+40:34">Exodus 40:34</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+40:35">Exodus 40:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eze+1:28">Ezekiel 1:28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:5">Hebrews 9:5</span>. Not the final glory of God 's kingdom; for this belongs to the Gentiles as well as to the Jews. </p>
<p>The covenants [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αι διαθηκαι] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:28">Matthew 26:28</span>. Those concluded with the patriarchs since Abraham. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:16">Galatians 3:16</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:17">Galatians 3:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:12">Ephesians 2:12</span>. The plural never occurs in the Old Testament. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:16">Hebrews 9:16</span>. The giving of the law [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η νομοθεσια] </span></span>. The act of giving, with a secondary reference to the substance of the law; legislation. </p>
<p>The service [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η λατρεια] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+16:2">John 16:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:74">Luke 1:74</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="re+22:3">Revelation 22:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="php+3:3">Philippians 3:3</span>. Here the sum total of the Levitical services instituted by the law. </p>
<p>The promises. The collective messianic promises on which the covenants were based. The word originally means announcement. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:4">Acts 1:4</span>.</p>
