<p>Holding fast [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">αντεχομενον] </span></span>. Only here in Pastorals. In Paul, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:14">1 Thessalonians 5:14</span> (note). </p>
<p>The faithful word [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">του πιστου λογου] </span></span>. The trustworthy, reliable word. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:15">1 Timothy 1:15</span> (note). </p>
<p>As he hath been taught [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">κατα την διδαχην] </span></span>. Lit. according to the teaching. Const. with word. Agreeing with the apostolic teaching. For didach teaching see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:2">2 Timothy 4:2</span>. </p>
<p>May be able by sound doctrine both to exhort [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">δυνατος η και παρακαλειν εν τη διδασκαλια τη υγιαινουση] </span></span>. Rend. "may be able both to exhort in the sound teaching." For dunatov able or powerful, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:12">2 Timothy 1:12</span>. Used by Paul in the phrase eij dunaton if it be possible, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:18">Romans 12:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:15">Galatians 4:15</span> : to dunaton that which is possible, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:22">Romans 9:22</span> : of God, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:21">Romans 4:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:23">Romans 11:23</span> : of men, in the ethical sense, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:1">Romans 14:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+12:10">2 Corinthians 12:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+13:9">2 Corinthians 13:9</span>. </p>
<p>Convince [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ελεγχειν] </span></span>. Better, convict. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:20">John 3:20</span>, and ejlegmon, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:16">2 Timothy 3:16</span>. </p>
<p>The gainsayers [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">τους αντιλεγοντας] </span></span>. In Pastorals only here and chapter <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:9">Titus 2:9</span>. Once in Paul, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+10:21">Romans 10:21</span>, cit. Mostly in Luke and Acts. Glainsay, Angl. Sax. gegn (Germ. gegen) "against," and;; say. "Wiclif, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:15">Luke 21:15</span> : For I schalgyue to you mouth and wisdom, to whiche alle youre aduersaries schulen not mowe agenstonde, and agenseye."</p>
