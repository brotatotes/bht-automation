<p>Speak thou [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λαλει] </span></span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:18">Matthew 28:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:26">John 8:26</span>. Become [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πρεπει] </span></span>. Originally, to stand out; be conspicuous. Thus Homer, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ob+1:8">Obadiah 1:8</span> <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ob+1:172">Obadiah 1:172</span> : meta de prepei ajgromenoisin he is conspicuous among those who are assembled. Eurip. Hel. 215; Zeuv prepwn dij aijqerov Zeus shining clearly through the aether. Hence, to become conspicuously fit; to become; beseem. In N. T. in the impersonal forms prepon ejstin it is becoming (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:15">Matthew 3:15</span>); prepei it becometh (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:3">Ephesians 5:3</span>); eprepen it became (<span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+2:10">Hebrews 2:10</span>). With a subject nominative, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:10">1 Timothy 2:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:26">Hebrews 7:26</span>.</p>
