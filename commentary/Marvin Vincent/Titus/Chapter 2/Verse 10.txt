<p>Purloining [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">νοσφιζομενους] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:2">Acts 5:2</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:3">Acts 5:3</span>. LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jos+7:1">Joshua 7:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="jos+2:0">Joshua 2:0</span> Macc. 4 32. Often in Class. From nosfi apart. The fundamental idea of the word is to put far away from another; to set apart for one's self; hence to purloin and appropriate to one's own use. Purloin is akin to prolong : prolongyn or purlongyn "to put fer awey." Old French porloignier or purloignier. </p>
<p>Shewing all good fidelity [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">πασαν πιστιν ενδεικνομενος αγαθην] </span></span>. The phrase N. T. o. This is the only instance in N. T. of ajgaqov with pistiv. </p>
<p>Adorn the doctrine (thn didaskalian kosmwsin). The phrase N. T. o. For kosmwsin adorn, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:9">1 Timothy 2:9</span>.</p>
