<p>This teaching or doctrine which is to be adorned by the lives of God 's servants - the teaching of the gospel - is now stated in vv. 11 - 15. The grace of God [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">η χαρις του θεου] </span></span>. A common Pauline phrase. The exact phrase only here in Pastorals. It is the ultimate ground of salvation. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:9">2 Timothy 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:5">Ephesians 2:5</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:8">Ephesians 2:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:15">Galatians 1:15</span>. </p>
<p>That bringeth salvation [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">σωτηριος] </span></span>. Lit. saving. N. T. o. Const. with cariv grace. The saving grace of God. </p>
<p>Hath appeared [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">επεφανη] </span></span>. Only in Pastorals, Luke, and Acts. In the active voice, to bring to light, show. See on ejpifaneia appearing, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:14">1 Timothy 6:14</span>. </p>
<p>To all men. Const. with that bringeth salvation, not with hath appeared. The grace of God which is saving for all men. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:4">1 Timothy 2:4</span>.</p>
