<p>A peculiar people (laon periousion). Laov people only here in Pastorals. In Paul ten times, always in citations. Most frequently in Luke and Acts; often in Hebrews and Revelation. Periousiov N. T. o. A few times in LXX, always with laov. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+19:5">Exodus 19:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ex+23:22">Exodus 23:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+7:6">Deuteronomy 7:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+14:2">Deuteronomy 14:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="de+26:18">Deuteronomy 26:18</span>. The phrase was originally applied to the people of Israel, but is transferred here to believers in the Messiah - Jews and Gentiles. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:10">1 Peter 2:10</span>. Periousiov is from the participle of perieinai to be over and above : hence periousia abundance, plenty. Periousiov also means possessed over and above, that is, specially selected for one's own; exempt from ordinary laws of distribution. Hence correctly represented by peculiar, derived from peculium, a private purse, a special acquisition of a member of a family distinct from the property administered for the good of the whole family. Accordingly the sense is given in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:14">Ephesians 1:14</span>, where believers are said to have been sealed eijv ajpolutrwsin thv peripoihsewv with a view to redemption of possession, or redemption which will give possession, thus = acquisition. So <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:9">1 Peter 2:9</span>, where Christians are styled laov eijv peripoihsin a people for acquisition, to be acquired by God as his peculiar possession. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:9">1 Thessalonians 5:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:14">2 Thessalonians 2:14</span>, and peripoieisqai to acquire, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:28">Acts 20:28</span>. The phrase kaqarizein laon to purify the people, in LXX, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ne+12:30">Nehemiah 12:30</span>; Judith 16 18. </p>

<p>Despise [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">περιφρονειτω] </span></span>. N. T. o. Occasionally in Class. From peri beyond, fronein to be minded. To set one's self in thought beyond; hence; contemn, despise. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:12">1 Timothy 4:12</span>. The exhortation is connected with authority. Tit. is to claim respect for his office and for himself as bearing it. </p>
