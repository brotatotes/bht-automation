<p>Sound speech [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">λογον υγιη] </span></span>. Ugihv sound, only here in Pastorals. The usual form is the participle, as uJgiainontwn logwn, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:13">2 Timothy 1:13</span>; uJgiainousi logoiv, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:3">1 Timothy 6:3</span>. </p>
<p>That cannot, be condemned [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ακαταγνωστον] </span></span>. N. T. o. o Class. See 2 Macc. 4 47. </p>
<p>He that is of the contrary part [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ο εξ εναντιας] </span></span>. The phrase N. T. o. See <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:39">Mark 14:39</span>. The heathen opposer is meant. Comp. blasphemed, verse 5, and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:1">1 Timothy 6:1</span>. Enantiov contrary, in Paul only <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1th+2:15">1 Thessalonians 2:15</span>. </p>
<p>May be ashamed [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">εντραπη] </span></span>. Only here in Pastorals. <p>Evil thing [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">φαυλον] </span></span>. Only here in Pastorals. In Paul, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:11">Romans 9:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:10">2 Corinthians 5:10</span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:20">John 3:20</span>.</p>
