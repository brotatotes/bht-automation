<p>To please them well in all things (ejn pasin eujarestouv einai). Wrong. Const. in all things with to be in subjection. Note the position of ejn pasin in <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:11">1 Timothy 3:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:15">1 Timothy 4:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:7">2 Timothy 2:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:5">2 Timothy 4:5</span>, and comp. uJpakouein kata panta obey in all things, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+3:20">Colossians 3:20</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="col+3:22">Colossians 3:22</span>; and uJpotassetai - ejn panti is subject in everything, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:24">Ephesians 5:24</span>. Euarestov well pleasing, only here in Pastorals. Almost exclusively in Paul. See also <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+13:21">Hebrews 13:21</span>. Euarestwv acceptably, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:28">Hebrews 12:28</span>.</p>
