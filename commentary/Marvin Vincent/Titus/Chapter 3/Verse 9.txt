<p>Strivings about the law [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">μαχας νομικας] </span></span>. The phrase N. T. o. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:7">1 Timothy 1:7</span>. Nomikov mostly in Luke. Everywhere except here a lawyer, with the article or tiv. </p>
<p>Unprofitable [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ανωφελεις] </span></span>. Only here and <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:18">Hebrews 7:18</span>. </p>
<p>Vain [<span class="_800000"><span lang="el-GR">ματαιοι] </span></span>. Only here in Pastorals. Twice in Paul, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:20">1 Corinthians 3:20</span>, cit.; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:17">1 Corinthians 14:17</span> (note). Very frequent in LXX The sense is aimless or resultless, as mataiov eujch a prayer which cannot obtain fulfilment. The questions, genealogies, etc., lead to no attainment or advancement in godliness. Comp. mataiologia jangling, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:6">1 Timothy 1:6</span> : mataiologoi vain talkers, verse 10 mataiothv vanity, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:20">Romans 8:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:17">Ephesians 4:17</span> : ejmataiwqhsan were made vain, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:21">Romans 1:21</span> : mathn in vain, <span class="scriptRef" com="vnt" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:9">Matthew 14:9</span>.</p>
