<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:27">1 Corinthians 10:27</span>. <strong> If one of them that believe not biddeth you to a feast</strong> not an idolatrous festival, but a social feast.
	</p>
<p><strong> and ye are disposed to go. </strong> Strange to say, this is understood by some (as Grotius and Alford) as a tacit way of dissuading them from going. Clearly it is a tacit permission to go, and is merely expressed to pave the way for the direction following,
	</p>
<p><strong> whatsoever is set</strong> <strong> before you, eat, asking no questions for conscience sake.
	</strong></p>
