<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:28">1 Corinthians 10:28</span>. <strong> But if any man say unto you, This hath been offered in sacrifice, eat not,</strong> <strong> for his sake that shewed it</strong> for your informant’s sake.
	</p>
<p><strong> and for conscience sake: conscience, I say,</strong> <strong> not thine own,</strong> <strong> but the other’s</strong> the conscience of some <strong> weak </strong> brother who might be present. [1]
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3.6em;"> [1] The words following in the received text “for the earth is the Lord’s, and the fulness thereof” are an unauthorised and meaningless repetition of the statement in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:26">1 Corinthians 10:26</span>.
	</p>
<p><strong> for why is my liberty judged by another man’s conscience? </strong> ‘When I eat what he knows to have been sacrificed to an <strong> </strong> idol, <em> his </em> conscience is hurt, but mine is not, provided I buy and eat it simply as wholesome food; for my liberty is not to fee judged by his want of light on this subject.’
	</p>
