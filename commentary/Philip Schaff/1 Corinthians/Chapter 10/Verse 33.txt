<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+10:33">1 Corinthians 10:33</span>. <strong> even as I also please all men in all things</strong> all things indifferent (as the next verse makes plain).
	</p>
<p><strong> not seeking mine</strong> <strong> own profit, but the</strong> <strong><em> profit </em></strong> <strong> of the many, that they may be saved. </strong> (See on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:22">1 Corinthians 9:22</span>.) Within the limits of Christian consistency, and so far as was fitted to win others to Christ, he was ready to go; but concessions compromising his own conscience, and against the real good of others, he would never make. Indeed, on one occasion, when a fellow-apostle yielded on this point, his indignation was roused, and he was constrained to administer a rebuke (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:11">Galatians 2:11</span>, etc.).
	</p>
