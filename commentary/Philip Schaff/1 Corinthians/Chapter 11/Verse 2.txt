<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:2">1 Corinthians 11:2</span>. <strong> Now I praise you</strong> [1] <strong> that ye remember me in all things</strong> all the instructions I gave you, <strong> and hold fast the traditions. </strong> The word means ‘things delivered’ in any way, whether orally or in writing, as is plain from what follows.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:1.8em;"> [1] Though the word “brethren” occurs at the opening of chap. 10 and chap. 12 with no counter evidence, it is wanting here in the four oldest MSS.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> even as I delivered them to you. </strong> Even the Rhemish Version renders it “precepts,” the Authorised Version ordinances, that is, the directions which he gave them for their guidance.
	</p>
