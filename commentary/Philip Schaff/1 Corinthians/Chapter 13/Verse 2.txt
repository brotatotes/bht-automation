<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+13:2">1 Corinthians 13:2</span>. <strong> And though I have the gift of prophecy</strong> a gift above tongues (chap. 14), for uttering the mind of God by immediate inspiration, but often for the opening of the Scriptures, to which the reference is here, as appears from the next words.
	</p>
<p><strong> and understand all mysteries, and all knowledge:</strong> ‘Though I could lay bare the whole scheme of God towards the Church, from its most rudimentary to its ripest form in Scripture, without love I am nothing’ (see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:22">Matthew 7:22</span>).
	</p>
<p><strong> and though I have all faith, so as to remove mountains</strong> that gift of which our Lord speaks (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:20">Matthew 17:20</span>, and see on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+12:9">1 Corinthians 12:9</span>), which enabled its possessor, on giving the word of command, to work stupendous miracles (an example of which may be seen in Peter, <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:6">Acts 3:6</span>, etc.).
	</p>
