<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:2">1 Corinthians 14:2</span>. <strong> For he that speaketh in a tongue speaketh not onto men,</strong> <strong> but unto God</strong> the Giver of that gift; <strong> for no man</strong> (in the absence of an interpreter) <strong> understandeth him;</strong> <strong> but in the spirit </strong> (as distinct from his conscious understanding) <strong> he speaketh mysteries</strong> (see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+14:14-15">1 Corinthians 14:14-15</span>). By “mysteries” is meant truths which, under the ancient economy, were but partially understood, but now fully disclosed (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:7">1 Corinthians 2:7</span>).
