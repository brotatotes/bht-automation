<p><em> The certainty of the Resurrection of Believers from the Resurrection of Christ, </em> 1-23.
	</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:1">1 Corinthians 15:1</span>. <strong> Now I make known unto you, brethren, the gospel which I preached unto you, which</strong> <strong> also ye received, by which also ye are saved </strong> <em> (Gr. </em> ‘being saved,’ see on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:18">1 Corinthians 1:18</span>), <strong><em> I make known, I say, </em></strong> <strong> in</strong> <strong> what words I preached</strong> <strong> it unto you </strong> that is, ‘on what footing I placed it,’ namely, on the fact of Christ’s resurrection <strong> if ye hold</strong> <strong> it fast, except ye believed in vain</strong> (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:1">2 Corinthians 6:1</span>). The construction of the sentence is involved, but this seems clearly the sense.
	</p>
