<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:43">1 Corinthians 15:43</span>. <strong> it is sown in dishonour </strong> becoming so repulsive that one is fain to say with Abraham, of the dearest object in life, “Bury my dead out of my sight,” <strong> it is raised in glory </strong> resplendent and ravishing to behold (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:3">Matthew 28:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:29-31">Luke 9:29-31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:2">1 John 3:2</span>), <strong> it is sown in weakness </strong> the lifeless corpse absolutely powerless, <strong> it is raised in power-</strong> endued with inexhaustible energy.
