<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:53">1 Corinthians 15:53</span>. <strong> For this corruptible must put on incorruption, and this mortal </strong> (of ours) [1] <strong> put on immortality. </strong> The same figure of “putting off” and “putting on” the <em> dress </em> of our present and future state is found in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:2-4">2 Corinthians 5:2-4</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3.6em;"> [1] Not “this mortal” of <em> mine, </em> as Meyer and Alford unnaturally take it. It is the mortality of the saints at large, a Cor. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:1">1 Corinthians 5:1</span>, “ <em> our </em> earthly house.”
	</p>
