<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:1">1 Corinthians 2:1</span>. <strong> And I, brethren, when I came unto you, came not with excellency of speech </strong> (as a rhetorician), <strong> or</strong> <strong> of wisdom </strong> (as a <strong> </strong> philosopher), <strong> proclaiming to you the testimony of God</strong> [1] that concerns His Son.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:1.8em;"> [1] Or, according to another reading, which has striking support, ‘the mystery of God.’ But the evidence for the received reading, given above, appears to us stronger, and with Meyer we think the word ‘mystery’ crept in here from <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:7">1 Corinthians 2:7</span>.
