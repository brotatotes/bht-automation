<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+2:3">1 Corinthians 2:3</span>. <strong> And I was with you in weakness, and in fear, and in much trembling. </strong> It is remarkable that nowhere else does the apostle so speak, <strong> </strong> nor does he seem to have anywhere else felt such an oppressive consciousness of his insufficiency (see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:15-17">2 Corinthians 2:15-17</span>); and it is worthy of notice that the historian of the Acts (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:5">Acts 18:5</span>) refers to these very feelings at Corinth in the following unusual terms: ‘But when Silas and Timothy came down from Macedonia (to Corinth), Paul was <em> constrained by the word </em> (as the true text is), testifying to the Jews that Jesus was <strong> </strong> the Christ.’
