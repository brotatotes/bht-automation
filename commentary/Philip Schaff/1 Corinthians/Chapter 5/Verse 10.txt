<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:10">1 Corinthians 5:10</span>. <strong> not altogether with the fornicators of this world, or with the covetous, and extortioners, or with idolaters; for then must ye needs go out</strong> <strong> of the world. </strong> Observe the caution “not <em> altogether</em> ” <em> </em> restricting the allowed intercourse with them to what was <em> necessary </em> and <em> safe. </em> The collocation of “the covetous and extortioners” with “fornicators and idolaters” sounds strange to us; but it is a favourite classification with our apostle (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:3">Ephesians 5:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="col+3:5">Colossians 3:5</span>). Perhaps the explanation of this may be found in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:19-21">Galatians 5:19-21</span>, where these are all ranked under the head of “works of the flesh,” any one of which might, according to individual bent, stir up another. </p>
