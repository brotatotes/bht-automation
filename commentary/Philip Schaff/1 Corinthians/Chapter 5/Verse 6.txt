<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:6">1 Corinthians 5:6</span>. <strong> Your glorying is not good</strong> is out of place, unseemly. </p>
<p><strong> Know ye not that a little leaven leaveneth</strong> <strong> the whole lamp?</strong> ‘’Tis only one case, ye say; but are ye so ignorant as not to know that the communicative properties of good and evil are as leaven (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:3">Matthew 13:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:13">Matthew 5:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:33">1 Corinthians 15:33</span>), and that the leavening property of evil is greater than that of good?’ “ <em> One </em> sinner destroyeth much good.” In a church gathered, like that of Corinth, out of a proverbially licentious city, and themselves before conversion no better than others (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:9-11">1 Corinthians 6:9-11</span>), how dangerous the presence of such an offender, going out and in among them in full fellowship, must be obvious to every one.
