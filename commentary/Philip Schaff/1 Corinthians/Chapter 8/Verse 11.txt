<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+8:11">1 Corinthians 8:11</span>. <strong> for through thy knowledge he that</strong> <strong> is weak perisheth, the brother</strong> [1] <strong> for whose sake Christ died. </strong> It might seem that “for” here is inappropriate; but the thought is this: ‘The wrong you thus do, through your uncalled-for exercise <em> of </em> liberty, is far greater than you think; <em> for </em> whoever is thus the means of leading a Christian brother to violate his conscience is helping so far to destroy his soul.’ Well might Olshausen say, in view of so affecting a statement, ‘The worth of even the poorest, weakest brother could not be more emphatically expressed.’
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3.6em;"> [1] Such is the correct reading here. </p>
