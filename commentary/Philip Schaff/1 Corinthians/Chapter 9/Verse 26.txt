<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:26">1 Corinthians 9:26</span>. <strong> I</strong> <strong> therefore </strong> (for my part) <strong> so run, not as uncertainly</strong> without definite aim, regardless of what is at stake, and what is required in order to win.
	</p>
<p><strong> so fight I. </strong> Here the figure changes from <em> running </em> to <em> wrestling, </em> from the speed of the racer to the aggressions of the pugilist <strong> as not beating the air</strong> who, taking an uncertain or inaccurate aim, misses his antagonist, and strikes only the air: not so do I fight; I put <em> skill </em> as well as <em> energy </em> into this “good fight of faith.”
	</p>
