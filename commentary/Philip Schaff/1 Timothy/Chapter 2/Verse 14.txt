<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:14">1 Timothy 2:14</span>. (2) The woman was in that first typical history the one directly deceived by the Tempter, Adam’s sin being thought of as more against light and knowledge,’ and so <em> ‘she has come to be in the state of a transgressor.’ </em> The implied thought, of course, is that that greater liability to deception continues now; and this was probably strengthened by what the apostle actually saw of the influence of false teachers over the minds of women (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:6-7">2 Timothy 3:6-7</span>). The history of the fall seemed to him acted over again. Comp. the position of the woman Jezebel in the Church of Thyatira (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="re+2:20">Revelation 2:20</span>), and the false prophetesses in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eze+13:17">Ezekiel 13:17</span>.
