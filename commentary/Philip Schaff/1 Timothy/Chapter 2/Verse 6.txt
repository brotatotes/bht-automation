<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:6">1 Timothy 2:6</span>. <strong> A ransom for all.</strong> The words at once repeat and interpret those which St. Matthew (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:28">Matthew 20:28</span>) records as spoken by our Lord Himself. There a ‘ransom’ simply, here ‘a ransom paid as in exchange;’ there ‘ <em> instead of many,’ </em> here ‘ <em> on behalf of all.’
	</em></p>
<p><strong> Gave</strong> <strong><em> </em></strong> <strong> himself. </strong> Not limited to the death upon the cross, though culminating in that highest act of self-surrender.
	</p>
<p><strong> To be testified. </strong> Better ‘ <em> the testimony,’ i.e. the </em> witness which was needed, and in its own special season was given (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:4">Galatians 4:4</span>), to make known to men the saving will of God.
	</p>
