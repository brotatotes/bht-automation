<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:17">1 Timothy 6:17</span>. <strong> Charge them that are rich in this world.</strong> It is quite after St. Paul’s manner to return in this way to the subject from which he had been led away by the train of thought that issued in a doxology. Before, he had spoken of those who set their hearts on becoming rich. Now, he deals with those whom he finds rich by inheritance or otherwise.
	</p>
<p><strong> High-minded.</strong> The state of one who forms great and ambitious schemes in which be himself is the centre.
	</p>
<p><strong> Nor trust in uncertain riches.</strong> Better, ‘ <em> nor to fix their hope on the un-certainty of riches.’
	</em></p>
<p><strong> In God who giveth us richly all things to enjoy.</strong> If we seek for riches, God gives richly; but that which He gives brings with it no cares and sorrows, like earthly wealth, but tends, whether it be outward or inward good, to direct and immediate enjoyment.
	</p>
