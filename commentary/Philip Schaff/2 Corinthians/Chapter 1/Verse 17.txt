<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:17">2 Corinthians 1:17</span>. <strong> When I therefore was thus minded, did I shew fickleness? </strong> This shews that his original plan had somehow become known at Corinth either through Timothy (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:17">1 Corinthians 4:17</span>), or in the letter referred to in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:9">1 Corinthians 5:9</span> and that advantage had been taken of the change to his prejudice, <strong> that with me there should be yea yea, and nay nay?</strong> ‘saying yes and no to the same thing as suits the whim of the moment.’ That this is the meaning is clear from what follows.
	</p>
