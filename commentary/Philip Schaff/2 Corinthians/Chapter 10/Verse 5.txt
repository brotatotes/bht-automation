<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:5">2 Corinthians 10:5</span>. <strong> casting down imaginations </strong> (or ‘reasonings’), <strong> and every high thing that is exalted </strong> (or ‘exalteth itself) <strong> against the knowledge of God, and bringing every thought into captivity to the obedience of Christ. </strong> The reference here is to the pride of human reason, which takes upon itself to judge of things supernatural and spiritual on purely natural principles. This was working perilously in the church of Corinth; but, says the apostle, the weapons of our warfare are able to cast all that to the ground, and bring every thought (‘every conception’ [1] ), like a captive, into absolute obedience to what Christ demands in thought and action;
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3.6em;"> [1] νόημα.
