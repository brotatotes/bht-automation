<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:12">2 Corinthians 11:12</span>. <strong> But what I do, that I will do, that I may out off occasion </strong> from them <strong> which desire occasion; that wherein they glory, they may be found even as we. </strong> Opinions differ as to what is meant here. But since it is clear that his opponents were irritated at his declining that temporal support which they freely received, the meaning seems plainly to be that by persisting in his course, in spite of their taunts, he meant to compel them either to “be as he-was,” gratuitous preachers, or to hold their peace.
