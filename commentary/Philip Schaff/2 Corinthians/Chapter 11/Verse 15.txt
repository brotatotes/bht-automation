<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:15">2 Corinthians 11:15</span>. <strong> It is no great thing therefore </strong> (nothing surprising) <strong> if his ministers also fashion themselves as ministers of righteousness: whose end shall be according to their works</strong> “for whatsoever a man soweth, that shall he also reap” (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:7">Galatians 6:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:21">Romans 6:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="php+3:19">Philippians 3:19</span>).
	</p>
<p>Advancing now to the things of which he was well entitled to boast, if boasting was at all permissible and wise, <strong> </strong> he begins by deprecating the supposition that in so doing he was playing the fool; and if they should still say that he was, then claiming the liberty permitted even to fools.
