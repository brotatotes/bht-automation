<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:23">2 Corinthians 11:23</span> <em> .</em> <strong> Are they ministers of Christ? (I speak as one beside himself) I more. </strong> Be it so that his opponents <em> are </em> ministers of Christ, but at least he himself will claim to be one, and a great deal more, referring to the labours and sufferings which he is now to detail, exceeding that (perhaps) of all the other apostles together,
	</p>
<p><strong> in</strong> <strong> labours more abundantly, in prisons more abundantly.</strong> [1] <strong> </strong> Clement, in his (First) Epistle to the Corinthians (5), referring to the labours of our apostle, says: “under-going imprisonment seven times.” But since the imprisonment at Philippi (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:24">Acts 16:24</span>) is the only one recorded before the date of this Epistle, Clement, says Lightfoot (p. 48, note 3), must have derived his more precise information from some other source,
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3.6em;"> [1] This clause, which in the received text comes after the next one, precedes it in the best text.
	</p>
<p><strong> in stripes more abundantly</strong> referred to before (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:5">2 Corinthians 6:5</span>), and particularized in the next two verses,
	</p>
<p><strong> in deaths oft</strong> hairbreadth escapes from it: compare <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:11">2 Corinthians 4:11</span>, “always delivered unto death for Jesus’ sake,” and see Rom. 16:34. Of such narrow escapes the apostle’s life was full.
	</p>
