<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:26">2 Corinthians 11:26</span>. <strong> in journeyings often, in perils of rivers</strong> having perhaps to swim across swollen rivers at the risk of life, <strong> in perils of robbers</strong> for some of the mountainous regions which he traversed are known to have been infested with robbers, and indeed are so still, <strong> in perils from mine own countrymen </strong> <em> (Gr. </em> ‘race’), <strong> in perils from</strong> <strong> the Gentiles</strong> sometimes goaded on by fanatical Jews, as at Philippi (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:0">Acts 16:0</span>) and Thessalonica (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+17:0">Acts 17:0</span>); but sometimes by interested idolaters, as at Ephesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:0">Acts 19:0</span>), as at Damascus, Jerusalem, Pisidia, Antioch, Iconium, Lystra, and Corinth itself, <strong> in perils in the city, in perils in the wilderness . . . the sea . . . amongst false brethren</strong> Judaizers, such as described in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:4">Galatians 2:4</span>.
