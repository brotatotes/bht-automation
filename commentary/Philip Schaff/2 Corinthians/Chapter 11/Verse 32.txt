<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:32">2 Corinthians 11:32</span>. <strong> In Damascus the governor </strong> ( <em> Gr. </em> ‘ethnarch’) <strong> under Aretas the king</strong> of that division of Arabia which had Petra for its capital, <strong> guarded the city</strong> <strong> of the Damascenes</strong> showing that though not within its natural boundaries, it had fallen into the hands of this king, <strong> in</strong> <strong> order to</strong> <strong> take me</strong> instigated, no doubt, by the Jews, who would represent him as a disturber of the peace. Thus his “weakness” began at the very outset of his ministry, making himself feel what he had till then striven to do to others.
	</p>
