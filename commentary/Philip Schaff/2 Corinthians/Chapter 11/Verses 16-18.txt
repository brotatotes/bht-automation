<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:16-18">2 Corinthians 11:16-18</span> <strong> .</strong> <strong> I say again, Let no man think me foolish; but if ye do so, yet as foolish receive me, that I also may glory a little, </strong> etc. He feels the conflict between what may be called legitimate boasting and what is mere folly. His boasting was “not after the Lord;” but being wrung from him in self-vindication, it was not foolishness, but only “as in foolishness.”
