<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:12">2 Corinthians 2:12</span>. <strong> Now when I came to Troas</strong> <strong> probably</strong> <strong> not the </strong> city only, but the region of ‘the Troad.’ It lay on the coast of Mysia, and commercially its importance was considerable, <strong> for the gospel of Christ, and a door was opened unto me of the Lord </strong> (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:9">1 Corinthians 16:9</span>). His object was to take advantage of this journey for missionary purposes, and the field here being open and <strong> </strong> rich for such work, he would fain have made some stay in it, but for his feverish anxiety for tidings from Corinth, of which he was disappointed by his not finding Titus waiting him there, as he expected.
