<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:5">2 Corinthians 2:5</span>. <strong> But if any hath caused sorrow</strong> referring delicately and indirectly to the offender, <strong> he hath caused sorrow, not to me, but in part </strong> (that I press not too heavily) to you all. This is somewhat obscurely expressed, but the emphatic “me” seems to give the meaning thus: ‘The wrong has been done, not to me, but to some extent (for I would not press you too hard) to you all.’
