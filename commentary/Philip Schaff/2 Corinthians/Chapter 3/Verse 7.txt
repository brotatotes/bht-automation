<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:7">2 Corinthians 3:7</span>. <strong> But if the ministration of death</strong> that of the law, which by reason of our inability to keep it, becomes to us a ministration of death, written ( <em> Gr. ‘</em> in letters’) <strong> and engraven on stones, came with glory</strong> with a glory expressive of the righteous claims of Jehovah on His reasonable creatures, <strong> so that the children of Israel could not look stedfastly upon the face of Moses for the glory of his face.</strong> The allusion is to <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ex+34:30">Exodus 34:30</span>, all so awe-struck with the appearance of Moses when he came down from the Mount, that they were afraid to come nigh him, <strong> which glory was passing away</strong> was expressive only of what was transitory.
