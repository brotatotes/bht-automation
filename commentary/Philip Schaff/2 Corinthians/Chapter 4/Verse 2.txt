<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:2">2 Corinthians 4:2</span>. <strong> but we have renounced the hidden things of shame</strong> those arts for gaining converts (referred to in chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:17">2 Corinthians 2:17</span>) which those who practise them are fain to “hide,” from their “shameful” character, <strong> not walking in craftiness</strong> such as was used to <em> entrap </em> our Lord into some treasonable utterance (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:23">Luke 20:23</span>), and of Satan’s <em> subtilty </em> in beguiling Eve (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:3">2 Corinthians 11:3</span>); employed either in adulterating the truth (as in chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:17">2 Corinthians 2:17</span>), or in so trimming as to keep out of view or shade off those features of it which are distasteful to the natural mind, and bring into artful prominence whatever may attract,
	</p>
<p><strong> but by the manifestation of the truths</strong> the naked truth, whose intrinsic majesty and Divine claims are its best recommendation, </p>
<p><strong> commending ourselves to every man’s conscience</strong> in which it ever finds an echo, when once apprehended, and that even though resisted,
	</p>
<p><strong> in the sight of God</strong> to whom alone we look for judgment upon our work (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:3">1 Corinthians 4:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:5">1 Corinthians 4:5</span>).
	</p>
