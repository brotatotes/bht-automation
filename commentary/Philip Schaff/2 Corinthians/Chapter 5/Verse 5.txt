<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:5">2 Corinthians 5:5</span>. <strong> Now he that wrought us for this very thing is God, who gave unto us the earnest of the Spirit</strong> ( <em> i.e.,</em> ‘the Spirit as the earnest’). This “groaning” is not the mere feeling which nature forces out from many a weary spirit, especially under the manifold ills of life; it is that instinct of the new nature, “wrought of God,” which is eternal life itself begun in the souls of all that believe (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:24">John 5:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:26">John 11:26</span>), and the earnest of all that this will yet be we have in the Spirit given unto us. This last thought is one the apostle often dwells on (see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:13-14">Ephesians 1:13-14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:23">Romans 8:23</span>),
