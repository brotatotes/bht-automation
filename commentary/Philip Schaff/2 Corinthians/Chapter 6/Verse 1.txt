<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:1">2 Corinthians 6:1</span>. <strong> And working together </strong> <em> with him, </em> <strong> we entreat also that ye receive not the grace of God</strong> compare <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:24">Acts 24:24</span>, “the gospel of the grace of God,” and <strong> <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:42">Acts 13:42</span></strong>, “persuaded them to continue in the grace of God,” in vain: ‘Having pleaded with the <em> unreconciled</em>, <em> </em> as though God were pleading by us, no longer to resist the yearning desire and the gracious provision of God in Christ for their reconciliation, we now turn to you, <em> already reconciled</em>, <em> </em> and, in the same capacity of “workers together with Him,” entreat you to turn that grace to practical account, by “walking worthy of the vocation wherewith ye are called.”’
