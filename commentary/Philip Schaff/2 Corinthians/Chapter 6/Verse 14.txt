<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:14">2 Corinthians 6:14</span>. <strong> Be not unequally yoked with unbelievers. </strong> The figure is drawn from the heterogeneous yoking of animals in a team (compare <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="de+22:10">Deuteronomy 22:10</span>, “Thou shalt not plough with an ox and an ass together;” and see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="le+19:19">Leviticus 19:19</span>); and though the more immediate reference probably is to too intimate association with their unconverted fellow-citizens at feasts, and especially to intermarriage with heathens, it is doubtless meant to embrace all entangling association with those whose close fellowship would have a deadening effect on their religious life,
	</p>
