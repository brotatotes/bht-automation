<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:15">2 Corinthians 6:15</span>. <strong> And what concord hath Christ with Belial?</strong> From the use of this proper name, and the antithesis with Christ, it evidently is meant (like Beelzebub in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:24">Matthew 12:24</span>) as a synonym for Satan (Stanley),
	</p>
<p><strong> or what portion hath a believer with an unbeliever?</strong> It is not their having different speculative opinions about religion that makes intimate fellowship between them impracticable; it is that a “believer” is supposed to breathe a different atmosphere, to live for fundamentally different ends, and to have entirely different interests and tastes and modes of action, from an “unbeliever.” (See on chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:16-17">2 Corinthians 5:16-17</span>.)
	</p>
