<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+6:9">2 Corinthians 6:9</span>. <strong> as unknown</strong> by the great indifferent world, <strong> and </strong> <strong><em> yet</em></strong> <strong> well known</strong> by all who take the trouble to hear us, and specially by all who have felt the quickening power of our preaching (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+3:2">2 Corinthians 3:2</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:2">2 Corinthians 4:2</span>). We can say of ourselves as our Master said to the high priest, “We ever speak openly to the world; in secret have we said nothing” (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:20">John 18:20</span>); as dying, and behold, <strong> we live</strong> ‘We seem to “die daily” in our Master’s service (see on chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+4:10-12">2 Corinthians 4:10-12</span>); but lo! despite all this, through that spring of new life which is in us and the power of Christ resting on us, here we still are;’ <strong> as chastened, and not killed.</strong> The words are taken from <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ps+118:18">Psalms 118:18</span> (LXX.), “The Lord hath chastened me sore, but He hath not given me over unto death.” Probably the thing here meant is simply that in all he had to endure as a minister of Christ, however unjust on the part of others, he experienced a chastening effect from the Divine hand, keeping him down and mellowing his character.
