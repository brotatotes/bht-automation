<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:3">2 Corinthians 8:3</span>. <strong> For according to their power, yea, and beyond their power, </strong> <strong><em> they gave </em></strong> <strong> of their own accord.
	</strong></p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+8:4">2 Corinthians 8:4</span>. <strong> beseeching us with much entreaty</strong>. The apostle would hardly let them give what they offered, as being too much in their circumstances, but they insisted: <strong> in the matter of this grace,</strong> [1] <strong> and the fellowship in the ministering to the saints</strong> that we would not deny them the privilege of having their own share in this good work.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3.6em;"> [1] The received reading here is most certainly wrong, Beza, from whom it was no doubt adopted by our translators, admits that he took it from the Greek <em> Scholia</em>, <em> </em> the Complutensian, Polyglot, and two Latin MSS. It is absent from all ancient MSS., is found in scarcely any of the Cursives, and in no ancient version save the Gothic.
	</p>
