<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2th+1:4">2 Thessalonians 1:4</span>. <strong> So that we ourselves. </strong> The growth of the Thessalonians was not a benefit which terminated with themselves; but the apostle also shared in the advantage which accrued from it. Their growth was a commendation of his work. In them, he himself could and did boast.
	</p>
<p><strong> In</strong> <strong> the churches of God.</strong> It is at all times right and profitable that the vigour and prosperity of one church should be known in all, both for their rebuke and for their encouragement; but it was eminently so in primitive times, when churches situated amidst a heathen population must have felt isolated and forlorn.
	</p>
<p><strong> Your</strong> <strong> patience end faith. </strong> ‘The Thessalonians evinced <em> faith, </em> in its proper and usual sense, in bearing up in their tribulations and <em> believing </em> on Him while bearing His cross’ (Ellicott).
	</p>
