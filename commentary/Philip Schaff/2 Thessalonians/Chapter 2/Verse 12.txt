<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:12">2 Thessalonians 2:12</span>. <strong> That they may all be judged. </strong> Ellicott says: ‘It need scarcely be said that κριθω ͂ σιν is not <em> per se “</em> might be damned,” but simply “might be judged,” the further idea of an unfavourable judgment being supplied by the <em> context.’ </em> However, the familiar use of the word ‘judgment’ to denote the judicial <em> punishment </em> of men by God, shows that the fact that in the majority of cases judgment results in punishment has told upon terminology.
	</p>
<p><strong> Took pleasure in unrighteousness.</strong> This was the reason of their rejecting the love of truth from their minds; they loved the darkness because their deeds were evil.
