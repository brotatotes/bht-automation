<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2th+2:8">2 Thessalonians 2:8</span>. <strong> And then. </strong> As soon as the restraining influence is removed.
	</p>
<p><strong> The lawless one. </strong> That is, the Man of Sin above spoken of.
	</p>
<p><strong> Whom the Lord Jesus shall destroy.</strong> ‘Before describing his appearance, the apostle, as it were by way of consolation to the Church, anticipates his destruction’ (Jowett).
	</p>
<p><strong> With the breath of his month.</strong> An expression denoting the ease with which omnipotence accomplishes its object. In <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ps+33:6">Psalms 33:6</span> it is used of creative power, the hosts of heaven were made ‘by the breath of His mouth.’ Comp. also <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="isa+11:4">Isaiah 11:4</span>, ‘With the breath of His lips shall He slay the wicked.’
	</p>
<p><strong> With the manifestation of his coming.</strong> The word translated in the Authorised Version ‘brightness’ means simply <em> manifestation </em> or <em> appearance, </em> as of an enemy’s fleet coming in sight, or of gods making themselves visible to their worshippers, and points here therefore to the <em> visible </em> coming of Christ: His mere appearing will be sufficient to destroy His enemies.
	</p>
