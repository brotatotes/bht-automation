<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:14">2 Timothy 1:14</span>. <strong> That good thing which was committed unto thee.</strong> Taken in connexion with the foregoing reference to the healthy or health-giving words, the phrase includes what has been technically called the ‘ <em> depositum fidei;’ </em> but it has, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:12">2 Timothy 1:12</span>, a wider range
	</p>
<p>not the doctrine or the truth only, but all of which Timothy had been made, if one may so speak, the trustee,
	</p>
<p>all spiritual gifts that he had himself received, and the Church committed to his charge.
	</p>
<p><strong> Through the Holy Ghost which dwelleth in us. </strong> The plural of the pronoun is generic, not personal of Paul and Timothy only. The apostle <strong> </strong> assumes that the Holy Spirit is actually dwelling in all believers, enabling them to do that which by nature they cannot do.
	</p>
