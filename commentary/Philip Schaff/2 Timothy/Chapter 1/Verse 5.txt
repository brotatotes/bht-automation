<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:5">2 Timothy 1:5</span>. <strong> When I call to remembrance. </strong> Warm as the words sound, there is just the shadow of a misgiving in them. He has to call to mind the past in order to feel confident for the future.
	</p>
<p><strong> Thy grandmother Lois.</strong> We now see the reason of his reference to his own family. His remembrance of their piety had helped him. A like remembrance might help his disciple. The form of expression, ‘which dwelt,’ suggests the thought that mother as well as grandmother was dead. We learn from <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:1">Acts 16:1</span>, that the former, and therefore probably the latter also, was a Jewess.
	</p>
<p><strong> And I am persuaded.</strong> The Greek <em> ‘but’ </em> suggests a mental contrast in the slight misgiving implied in ‘I am persuaded.’ We do not commonly say, ‘I am sure you will feel’ when we <em> are </em> quite sure.
	</p>
