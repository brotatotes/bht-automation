<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:11">2 Timothy 2:11</span>. <strong> It is a faithful saying. </strong> The rhythmical form of the sentence that follows suggests the thought that we have a fragment of one of the ‘spiritual songs’ of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:19">Ephesians 5:19</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="col+3:16">Colossians 3:16</span>, uttered under prophetic inspiration, accepted by the Church, used in its worship, taught to children and to converts.
	</p>
<p><strong> If we be dead with him.</strong> The Greek tense points to a definite act, <em> ‘if we died</em>,’ and interpreted by <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:3-4">Romans 6:3-4</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="col+2:12">Colossians 2:12</span>, throws us back upon the mystical union with the death of Christ into which believers enter at their baptism.
	</p>
