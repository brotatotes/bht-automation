<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:2">2 Timothy 2:2</span>. <strong> The things that thou hast heard. </strong> Probably the same as the ‘sound words’ of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:18">2 Timothy 1:18</span>.
	</p>
<p><strong> Among many witnesses. </strong> Better ‘ <em> with’ </em> or ‘ <em> through,’ </em> the presence of the witnesses, presbyters and others, being thought of as a <strong> </strong> condition of the act referred to. This, as the right rendering, ‘ <em> which thou heardest,</em> ’ implies, was a charge as to the substance of the Christian faith delivered when Timothy was appointed by the laying on of hands to his special work.
	</p>
<p><strong> Commit thou.</strong> The idea of the trust or deposit is continued. There is to be a perpetuation of the trust, and each man’s work for the time being is to choose faithful successors, who in their turn shall teach what they have learnt to others.
	</p>
