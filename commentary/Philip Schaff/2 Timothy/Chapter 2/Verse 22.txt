<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:22">2 Timothy 2:22</span>. <strong> Flee also youthful lusts. </strong> The English suggests too exclusively the thought of simple sensual desires, and these were doubtless prominent in St. Paul’s thoughts, but the words have a wider range, and include a young man’s vanity or ambition or impressiveness as <strong> </strong> well.
	</p>
<p><strong> Peace, with them. </strong> Better, ‘ <em> peace with them</em>,’ without the comma, as connecting the last words of the verse with the word to which they of right belong.
	</p>
