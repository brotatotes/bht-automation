<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:14">2 Timothy 3:14</span>. <strong> Thou hast learned, and hast been assured of.</strong> As in other cases, the English perfect hides the force of the Greek. Better, <em> ‘Thou didst learn and wast persuaded of</em>.’
	</p>
<p><strong> From whom.</strong> The received text gives the singular, the better MSS. the plural, The former would point to St. Paul, the latter to Lois and Eunice as well, perhaps (looking to the ‘from a child’ of the next verse) to them chiefly.
	</p>
