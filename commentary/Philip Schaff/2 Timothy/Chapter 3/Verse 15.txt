<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ti+3:15">2 Timothy 3:15</span>. <strong> The holy Scriptures.</strong> The Greek noun is not that usually employed in the New Testament, but answers rather to ‘sacred literature’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:24">Acts 26:24</span>). It is used, however, of the Old Testament books by Josephus.
	</p>
<p><strong> Which make thee wise.</strong> The English is literal enough, but the Greek implies somewhat more of systematic education.
	</p>
<p><strong> Through faith which is in Christ Jesus.</strong> The addition is remarkable. St. Paul’s experience had taught him that without that faith the study of the sacred writings might lead only to endless questionings and logomachies. Targums and the Talmud remain as if to show how profitless such a study might become.
	</p>
