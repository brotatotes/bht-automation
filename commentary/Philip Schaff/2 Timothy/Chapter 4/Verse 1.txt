<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:1">2 Timothy 4:1</span>. <strong> I charge thee therefore.</strong> As the apostle is conscious that he is drawing near the end of his letter, he passes, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:21">1 Timothy 5:21</span>, into a more solemn strain of exhortation. Better, ‘ <em> I adjure.’
	</em></p>
<p><strong> At</strong> <strong><em> </em></strong> <strong> his appearing and his kingdom. </strong> A better supported reading gives ‘ <em> by </em> His appearing,’ as a formula of adjuration following naturally on the opening words.
	</p>
