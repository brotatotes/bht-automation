<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:14">2 Timothy 4:14</span>. <strong> Alexander the coppersmith.</strong> Possibly identical with the man of the same name in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:33">Acts 19:33</span>, and with the false teacher of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:20">1 Timothy 1:20</span>. Enmity against St. Paul is a common element in all three cases. As a coppersmith, there may have been a trade connexion with the craftsmen employed by Demetrius.
	</p>
<p><strong> Did. </strong> Literally ‘ <em> showed</em>,’ <em> </em> but English usage would hardly admit the word in this context
	</p>
<p><strong> The Lord reward him.</strong> The better reading gives the future, ‘ <em> will reward him</em>.’
	</p>
