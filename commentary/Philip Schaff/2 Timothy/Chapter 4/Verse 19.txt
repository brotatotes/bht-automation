<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:19">2 Timothy 4:19</span>. <strong> Aquila and Priscilla. </strong> Partly, perhaps, from their trade as tentmakers, partly from their work as Christian preachers, their life was one of constant change, first at <strong> </strong> Rome and then at Corinth (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:2">Acts 18:2</span>), then at Ephesus (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:18-19">Acts 18:18-19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:26">Acts 18:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+16:19">1 Corinthians 16:19</span>), then at Rome again (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:3">Romans 16:3</span>), lastly, as here, once more at Ephesus.
	</p>
<p><strong> The household of Onesiphorus. </strong> Probably thus described as having lost their head and father (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:16">2 Timothy 1:16</span>).
	</p>
