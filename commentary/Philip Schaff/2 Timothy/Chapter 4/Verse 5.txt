<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:5">2 Timothy 4:5</span>. <strong> Watch thou</strong>. There is a special emphasis on the pronoun. ‘ <em> Thou</em>, <em> </em> whatever others may do, shouldst watch.’
	</p>
<p><strong> Endure afflictions. </strong> The same word as in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ti+2:9">2 Timothy 2:9</span>.
	</p>
<p><strong> Do the work of an evangelist.</strong> It is probable that the word was the formal designation of Timothy’s usual work, as it was of that of Philip (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:8">Acts 21:8</span>). His present work at Ephesus was that of a Vicar-Apostolic on a special mission, but that was not to make him forgetful of his more normal duties.
	</p>
<p><strong> Make full proof of.</strong> The English is expressive, but not more so than the literal <em> ‘fill up the measure </em> of thy ministry.’
	</p>
