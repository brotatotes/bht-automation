<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:7">2 Timothy 4:7</span>. <strong> I have fought the good fight.</strong> The Greek is wider in its range, and takes in every kind of contest <em> ‘I have striven a good strife’ </em> would perhaps be nearer. The words that follow show that St. Paul, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:24">1 Corinthians 9:24</span>, is thinking specially of the Greek games.
	</p>
<p><strong> I have kept the faith.</strong> What had been expressed before figuratively is now stated simply. So far he has not failed; he has kept the faith (in its objective sense) which had been committed to him.
	</p>
