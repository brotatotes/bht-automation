<p><em> Arrival and Reception of the Messengers at Joppa, </em> 17-23.
	</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:17">Acts 10:17</span>. <strong> While Peter doubted in himself. </strong> Again we should give close attention to the coincidence of <em> time. </em> It is manifestly intended that we are to see here the marks of a providential pre-arrangement. The messengers who had been ‘drawing nigh to the city’ when the apostle’s trance began (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:9">Acts 10:9</span>) were now actually at the gate, having inquired their way to the house, where Peter, at the close of the vision, was in anxious perplexity concerning its meaning.
	</p>
<p><strong> Before the gate.</strong> This was the outside door or gate which led, according to the fashion of eastern dwellings in all ages, into the inner court of the house (see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:13-14">Acts 12:13-14</span>).
	</p>
