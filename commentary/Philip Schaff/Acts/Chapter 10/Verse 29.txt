<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:29">Acts 10:29</span>. <strong> Without gainsaying, as soon as I was sent for. </strong> He says that he had at once obeyed instructions which he felt to be Divine (see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:21">Acts 10:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:23">Acts 10:23</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:12">Acts 11:12</span>).
	</p>
<p><strong> I ask for what intent ye have sent for me. </strong> Peter knew what the messengers had told him; but it was still needful that Cornelius should make his own statement. This is a case in which every step is to be made firm. The apostle asks for a full and authentic confirmation of what he had heard from the messengers.
	</p>
