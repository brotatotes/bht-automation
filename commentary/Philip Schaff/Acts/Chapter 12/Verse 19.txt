<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+12:19">Acts 12:19</span>. <strong> And</strong> <strong> he went</strong> <strong> down from Jerusalem to Cæsarea. </strong> No doubt bitterly disappointed at not being able to comply with the Jewish desire in the matter of putting to death the famous Nazarene leader, Herod left his Jewish capital for a short season, as he thought, and went down to Cæsarea, then the second city in his broad kingdom. Josephus mentions a desire to be present at games to be celebrated in honour of Claudius Cæsar as a reason for this removal of the king to Cæsarea.
	</p>
