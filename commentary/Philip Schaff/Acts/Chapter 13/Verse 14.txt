<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:14">Acts 13:14</span>. <strong> They came to Antioch in Pisidia. </strong> Antioch in Pisidia was one of the many Antiochs (see note on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:4">Acts 13:4</span> <strong> ) </strong> built by Seleucus Nicator, about 350 <strong> </strong> years before the visit of Paul and Barnabas. It was a city of considerable importance, and a Roman colony (on the meaning of ‘colony,’ see note on chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:12">Acts 16:12</span>). Vast ruins of the once celebrated Pisidian capital were identified some forty years ago by an English traveller.
