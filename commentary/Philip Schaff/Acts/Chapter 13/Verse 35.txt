<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:35">Acts 13:35</span>. <strong> Wherefore he saith also in another psalm, Thou shalt not suffer thine Holy One to see corruption. </strong> He, that is, God; as in the preceding verse, ‘He <strong> (</strong> God <strong> ) </strong> said on this wise, I <strong> </strong> will give you the sure mercies of David.’ Although in the psalm quoted (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ps+16:0">Psalms 16:0</span>) David is speaking, he is only speaking evidently the words put into his mouth by God. David is the interpreter, so <strong> </strong> to speak, of the Holy Spirit. Although the body of Jesus was laid in the tomb, <em> it underwent no corruption</em>, and until the day of resurrection lay as though on a couch (see Calvin’s note here).</p>
