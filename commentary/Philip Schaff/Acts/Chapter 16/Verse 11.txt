<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:11">Acts 16:11</span>. <strong> We came with a straight course to Samothracia, </strong> or ‘we ran with a straight course.’ The same word occurs again in the same sense in chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:1">Acts 21:1</span>. Luke, observes Hackett, observes almost a technical precision in the use of such terms. His account of the voyage to Rome shows a surprising familiarity with sea life.
