<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:38">Acts 16:38</span>. <strong> And the Serjeants. </strong> Here, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+16:35">Acts 16:35</span>, literally, rod-bearers, lictors, officials who attended upon the magistrates and carried out their orders. In a ‘colony’ these officers carried staves, not as in Rome, fasces.
	</p>
<p><strong> And they</strong> <strong> feared. </strong> Hackett quotes from Lucian a case of false imprisonment, in which the governor of a province not only acknowledged his error, but paid a large sum of money to those whom he had injured, in order to bribe them to be silent.
	</p>
