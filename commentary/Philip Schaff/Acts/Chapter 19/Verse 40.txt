<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:40">Acts 19:40</span>. <strong> For we are in danger to be called in question for this day’s uproar. </strong> Men of the rank of the ‘town-clerk’ of Ephesus well knew how probable it was that a tumultuous meeting which endangered the public peace would be inquired into by the Roman officials. The prized liberties of their city might in consequence have been forfeited. There was a Roman law which made it a capital offence to raise a riot. ‘Qui cœtum et concursum fecerit capitate sit’ (Seneca, <em> Controv. </em> iii. 8). ‘Qui cœtum et concursum fecerit capite puniatur Sulpicius Victor’ <em> (Instit. orat., </em> quoted by Gloag).
