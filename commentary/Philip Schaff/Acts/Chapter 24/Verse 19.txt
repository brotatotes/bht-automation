<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+24:19">Acts 24:19</span>. <strong> Who ought to have been here before thee, and object, if they had ought against me. </strong> This was happily urged by the apostle, as it was the Roman custom not to judge a prisoner on any charge unless the accusers were present. Paul urges that <em> his </em> accusers really were not the Sanhedrim nor the Jews then in court, but certain foreign pilgrims from Ephesus (Asia). These we hear nothing of now; they had doubtless tarried behind in Jerusalem, or had already set out on their return journey.
