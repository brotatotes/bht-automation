<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:18">Acts 27:18</span>. <strong> The next day they lightened the ship. </strong> This is said in general terms to have been done under the pressure of the storm; but there is little doubt that there was a more specific reason, that the danger which had been apprehended had occurred in fact, that, in spite of the undergirding, the ship had sprung a leak, and that already the water was gaining in the hold. What particular things were thrown overboard on this day we cannot tell; but, of course, they would be such heavy things as could most easily be spared. Compare <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="jon+1:5">Jonah 1:5</span>.
