<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:24">Acts 27:24</span>. <strong> Fear not, Paul. </strong> It is a natural inference from these words that St. Paul himself had been experiencing fear. That he was not a stranger to fear, is evident from chap, <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:9">Acts 18:9</span>. Nor is he ashamed to own this in his letters.
	</p>
<p><strong> God hath given thee all them that sail with thee. </strong> This seems to show that he had been engaged not only in prayer, but in intercessory prayer. Compare the language in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="phm+1:22">Philemon 1:22</span>. The exclamation ι ̓ δου ̀ in the passage before us appears to denote something unexpected.
	</p>
