<p><em> Attempt of the Sailors to leave the Ship baffled by the Apostle, </em> 30 - 32 .
	</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:30">Acts 27:30</span>. <strong> When they had let down the boat into the sea. </strong> This was the boat which had cost so much trouble before (see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:16">Acts 27:16</span>). They had already lowered it down from the davits, when St. Paul perceived their purpose, and with <strong> </strong> great presence of mind frustrated it in the most sagacious way.
	</p>
<p><strong> Under colour as though they would have cast anchor out of the foreship</strong>. No more plausible excuse could be given to the passengers. It was impossible to know whether the four anchors at the stern would hold; and the ship would be made more steady by putting out additional anchors at the bow. See note on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:41">Acts 27:41</span>.
	</p>
