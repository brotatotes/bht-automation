<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:31">Acts 27:31</span>. <strong> The centurion and the soldiers</strong>. St. Paul said nothing to the sailors, but spoke at once to his friend the centurion; and he used with him and the soldiers the argument which is of greatest weight in such a case, the appeal to self-preservation.
	</p>
<p><strong> Except these abide in the ship, ye cannot be saved. </strong> Much has been written on this in connection with the assurance previously given that they should all certainly be saved. It is quite useless to write any more on the subject. The same difficulty meets us everywhere.
	</p>
