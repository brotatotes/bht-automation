<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+27:34">Acts 27:34</span>. <strong> This is for your health. </strong> The correct meaning is: ‘This is essential for your safety.’ Their imminent danger was not death from starvation, but from drowning in the waves. They had laborious and difficult work before them; and it was necessary that they should recruit their strength.
	</p>
<p><strong> There shall not a hair fall from the head of any of you. </strong> This was a proverb denoting entire safety and exemption from the slightest harm. See <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1ki+1:52">1 Kings 1:52</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:30">Matthew 10:30</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:18">Luke 21:18</span>.
	</p>
