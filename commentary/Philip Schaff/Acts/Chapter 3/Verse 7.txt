<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+3:7">Acts 3:7</span>. <strong> And he took him by the right hand. </strong> To the word of command, Peter, following his Master’s example in such cases (see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:27">Mark 9:27</span>), grasps him by the right hand, thus encouraging him to obey the command to arise. On the use of such outward instrumentality, Chrysostom remarks: ‘So, too, Christ did; ofttimes would He heal with a word, ofttimes with an act; frequently, too, He would stretch out His hand where their faith was too weak, that it (the miracle of healing) might not seem to have worked of itself’. (Chrysostom, quoted in Alford).
	</p>
<p><strong> His feet and ankle bones received strength. </strong> Commentators remark on the accuracy and exactness of the description. They are the words of one who had received the professional training of a physician.
