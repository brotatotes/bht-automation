<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:2">Acts 4:2</span>. <strong> Being grieved that they taught the people. </strong> The anger of the priests and captain of the Temple, whose duty it was to preserve a reverential order among the crowds who worshipped in the great sanctuary of Israel, was easily aroused by the Sadducees against these unauthorized teachers who were making such a rapid progress in the affections of the people (see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:4">Acts 4:4</span>).
	</p>
<p><strong> And taught through Jesus the resurrection from the dead. </strong> Here we have the real ground of the hostility of the powerful Sadducee party; they were troubled at this public announcement of the resurrection of the Crucified, well knowing that if this single instance of one being raised from the dead were substantiated before the people, their creed would be at once discredited.
	</p>
