<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+4:21">Acts 4:21</span>. <strong> Finding nothing how they might punish them, because of the people. </strong> The evident good-will of the people no doubt procured the dismissal of the apostles this time without punishment.
	</p>
<p><strong> For all glorified God for that which was done</strong>. No penalty, such as scourging or imprisonment, would then have been tolerated by popular sentiment. But besides this public feeling working in favour of the disciples of Jesus, it is more than probable that in the Sanhedrim itself several members secretly favoured the new sect. Some have supposed that Nicodemus and Joseph of Arimathea were members of this council. That the powerful R. Gamaliel, one certainly of the most influential of the Sanhedrim leaders, was disposed to favour them we know from <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:34">Acts 5:34</span>.
	</p>
