<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:29">Acts 9:29</span>. <strong> And disputed against the Grecians. </strong> These Grecians or Hellenists were Jews who, in the ordinary intercourse of life, used the Greek language (see note on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+6:1">Acts 6:1</span>). It has been suggested that these disputes were probably held in the same Cilician synagogue at Jerusalem, of which Saul in old days had been so distinguished <strong> a </strong> Rabbi, and where he held his famous disputation with Stephen, the martyr deacon.
	</p>
