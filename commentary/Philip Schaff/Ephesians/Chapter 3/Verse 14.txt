<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+3:14">Ephesians 3:14</span>. <strong> For this cause. </strong> On the resumption and connection, see last section.
	</p>
<p><strong> I bow my knees. </strong> So <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="php+2:10">Philippians 2:10</span>. The full form is rhetorical. The reference is not to the actual bending of the knees, but to his earnest prayer.
	</p>
<p><strong> Unto the Father. </strong> God the Father, so in chaps. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:18">Ephesians 2:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:20">Ephesians 5:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="col+1:12">Colossians 1:12</span>, without any added phrase, since the words ‘of our Lord Jesus Christ’ are not found in the oldest and best manuscripts, are rejected expressly by some of the Fathers, and by nearly every modern editor of any critical judgment. The grand thought of the passage is obscured by the insertion.
	</p>
