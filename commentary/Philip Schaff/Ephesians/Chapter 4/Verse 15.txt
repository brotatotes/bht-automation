<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:15">Ephesians 4:15</span>. <strong> But </strong> introduces the positive side, in contrast with <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:14">Ephesians 4:14</span>
</p>
<p><strong> Holding the truth</strong>. Not simply ‘speaking the truth,’ but ‘being true,’ following truth, walking in truth. ‘Holding the truth ‘is correct, if ‘the truth ‘is not referred to true doctrine.
	</p>
<p><strong> In love. </strong> Some connect this with ‘grow,’ but it is better to join it with the participle, which otherwise would stand awkwardly alone. ‘A true-seeking and true-being with loving caution and kind allowance’ (Alford).
	</p>
<p><strong> May grow up into</strong> <strong> him</strong>; ‘unto and into Him’ as the goal and standard of our growth (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:13">Ephesians 4:13</span>), with a secondary thought, afterwards unfolded (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:16">Ephesians 4:16</span>), of the incorporation of all the body in Him.
	</p>
<p><strong> In all things, </strong> all those things in which Christian growth consists, faith, truth, knowledge, love, etc.
	</p>
<p><strong> Who is the head, even Christ.</strong> Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:22-23">Ephesians 1:22-23</span>. The position of the phrase renders it very emphatic. Growth is possible only because the Living Christ is the Head.
	</p>
