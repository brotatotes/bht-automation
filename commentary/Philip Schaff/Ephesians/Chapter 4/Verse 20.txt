<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:20">Ephesians 4:20</span>. <strong> But ye</strong>, over against the ‘Gentiles’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:17">Ephesians 4:17</span>), whose walk has been described (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:18-19">Ephesians 4:18-19</span>).
	</p>
<p><strong> Did</strong> <strong> not thus learn Christ. </strong> The tense is historical, at the time of conversion. ‘Not thus ‘is put rhetorically for ‘in an entirely different way.’ <strong> </strong> That different way is detailed in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:22-24">Ephesians 4:22-24</span>. ‘Christ’ is the Personal Object they learned, as is evident from <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:21">Ephesians 4:21</span>. It is not simply ‘the doctrine of Christ; or,’ about Christ; ‘the peculiar phrase suggests that in nothing else is a Person so directly and fully the object of the knowledge obtained.
	</p>
