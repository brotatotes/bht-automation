<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:27">Ephesians 4:27</span>. <strong> Nor yet. </strong> A slight change in the form of the negative, sustained by the best authorities, shows that this is another prohibition; hence ‘neither’ is inexact. The reference is, however, still to anger.
	</p>
<p><strong> Give place </strong> (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:19">Romans 12:19</span>), give free play, room in the heart, <strong> to the devil, </strong> <em> i.e.,</em> Satan. It is doubtful if the word <em> diabolos </em> ever means simply slanderer, or blasphemer, when used as a noun. The clause gives a reason why sinful anger should be avoided: it opens to Satan the heart which has been redeemed from his power by Christ.
	</p>
