<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:23">Ephesians 5:23</span>. <strong> Because a husband is head of his wife. </strong> The basis of the duty is this unalterable fact ‘A husband,’ as an example of the class (the article is not found in Greek); ‘his wife’ brings out the force of the article, pointing to the definite person in the supposed case.
	</p>
<p><strong> As Christ also is head of the church</strong>; ‘His Church,’ but there is no other than His. On Christ as Head of the Church, comp. chaps. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:22">Ephesians 1:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:15">Ephesians 4:15</span>.
	</p>
<p><strong> He himself is the Saviour </strong> of <strong> the body</strong>; lit., ‘Himself the Saviour of the body.’ This clause distinguishes Christ from the husband ‘In Christ’s case the Headship is united with, nay gained by, His having <em> saved </em> the body in the process of Redemption: so that I am not alleging Christ’s Headship as one entirely identical with that other, for He has a claim to it and an office in it peculiar to Himself’ (Alford).
	</p>
