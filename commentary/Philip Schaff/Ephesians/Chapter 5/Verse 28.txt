<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:28">Ephesians 5:28</span>. <strong> Thus; </strong> in this manner, as Christ loved the Church; not to be referred to the following ‘as.’
	</p>
<p><strong> Ought husbands also to love their own wives. </strong> ‘Also’ is well supported, and shows that the example of Christ is referred to. ‘Own’ is emphatic
	</p>
<p><strong> As their own bodies. </strong> Not, ‘as if they were,’ but’ since they are,’ the husband being the head of the wife, etc. (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:23">Ephesians 5:23</span>). ‘Thus’ indeed Christ loved the Church, but the Apostle has not yet brought out that thought.
	</p>
<p><strong> He who loveth,</strong> etc. This general proposition is self-evident.
	</p>
