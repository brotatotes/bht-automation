<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:3">Ephesians 5:3</span>. <strong> But. </strong> The exhortation forbids what is in marked contrast with the previous injunction.
	</p>
<p><strong> Fornication</strong>; to be taken in its strict sense, since this was scarcely accounted a sin among the heathen of that time.
	</p>
<p><strong> And all uncleanness</strong>; every kind of impurity.
	</p>
<p><strong> Or covetousness. </strong> ‘Or’ sets this sin by itself, giving special emphasis to the prohibition, while the mention of it here indicates its close connection with sensual sins; comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:19">Ephesians 4:19</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:5">Ephesians 5:5</span>. ‘Covetousness’ is ‘greed, avarice, unconquerable love of appropriation, morbid lust of acquisition, carrying in itself a violation of almost every precept of the Decalogue’ (Eadie). Monsters of avarice have often been monsters of lust (comp. Trench, <em> Synonyms N. T</em>.).
	</p>
<p><strong> Let it not be even named among </strong> you. ‘It’ refers to each of the sins mentioned. None of them should be talked about unnecessarily. It is incorrect to explain: ‘let it not be told of you.’
	</p>
<p><strong> As becometh saints</strong>; ‘meaning, that if it were talked of, such conversation would be unbecoming the holy ones of God’ (Alford). Notice the apt use of the term ‘saints.’
	</p>
