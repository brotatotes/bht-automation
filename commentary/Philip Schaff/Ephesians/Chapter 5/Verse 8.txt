<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:8">Ephesians 5:8</span>. <strong> For. </strong> TO become partakers with those who indulge in these sins would be a <strong> </strong> relapse.
	</p>
<p><strong> Ye were once</strong>; ‘were’ is emphatic; it is <em> past
	</em></p>
<p><strong> Darkness; </strong> ‘not merely living or abiding in it (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:19">Romans 2:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:4">1 Thessalonians 5:4</span>), but themselves actual and veritable darkness’ (Ellicott).
	</p>
<p><strong> But now are ye light in</strong> <strong> the Lord</strong>. More than ‘enlightened;’ they have themselves become ‘light’ (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:13">Ephesians 5:13</span>), and that ‘in fellowship with the Lord,’ the source of light and life to men. The word ‘light’ is ‘a <strong> </strong> comprehensive designation of the Divine life and character, both ethical and intellectual in its meaning, in contrast with darkness’ (Braune). Comp. marginal references.
	</p>
<p><strong> Walk as children of light</strong>; let your conduct correspond with what you, by grace, have become. Christian exhortation always has this tone.
	</p>
