<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:23">Ephesians 6:23</span>. <strong> Peace to the brethren, </strong> etc. This double greeting is quite peculiar: it is in the third person, although <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:21-22">Ephesians 6:21-22</span> were in the second; ‘peace’ comes before ‘grace’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:24">Ephesians 6:24</span>), reversing the usual order, while the two-fold form is quite as peculiar. (See further, at close of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:24">Ephesians 6:24</span>. J ‘Peace ‘is to be taken as usual; comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:2">Ephesians 1:2</span>. ‘Brethren’ refers to the readers (= ‘you’); the next verse seems to extend the benediction.
	</p>
<p><strong> And love with faith</strong>; the latter is pre-supposed in the use of the term ‘brethren;’ in inseparable connection with this he wishes for love, Christian love of the brethren. Without faith love cannot exist; ‘faith is the characteristic of proper love (as <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:6">Galatians 5:6</span>), love is the characteristic of proper faith’ (Harless).
	</p>
<p><strong> From God the Father, </strong> etc. The form is the usual one; comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:2">Ephesians 1:2</span>.
	</p>
