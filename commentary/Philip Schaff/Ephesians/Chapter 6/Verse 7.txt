<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:7">Ephesians 6:7</span>. <strong> With good will. </strong> The word is not that sometimes rendered ‘good pleasure,’ but another which implies a well-disposed mind. The work is to be clone ‘from the soul,’ heartily (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:6">Ephesians 6:6</span>), but this phrase refers to the disposition to the master.
	</p>
<p><strong> Doing service, as to the Lord, and not to men. </strong> This virtually sums up <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:6">Ephesians 6:6</span>, and returns to the motive to <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+6:5">Ephesians 6:5</span>: ‘as to Christ.’
	</p>
