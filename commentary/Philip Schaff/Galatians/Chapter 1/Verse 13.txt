<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:13">Galatians 1:13</span>. <strong> For ye heard </strong> (when I was with you) <strong> of my former manner of life </strong> (or, <strong> conduct</strong>) <strong> in Judaism, </strong> <em> i.e., </em> the Jewish religion as opposed <strong> </strong> to Christianity, the religion of the Jewish hierarchy and the Pharisaic school, not the genuine religion of the Old Testament. Paul appeals to the well-known fact of his past career as a persecutor, which formed a part of his teaching, and conclusively proved that no mere human teaching could have converted him. All his antecedents were of such a character that nothing but a divine intervention could produce so great a change.
	</p>
<p><strong> That beyond measure I persecuted the church of God and was destroying it, </strong> or ‘labored to destroy it’ (the same word as in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+9:21">Acts 9:21</span>). Paul intended to annihilate Christianity, was actually employed in the attempt and carried it out as far as he could (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:4">Acts 22:4</span>). ‘I persecuted this way (or, belief) <em> even to death</em> ’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:10-11">Acts 26:10-11</span>).
	</p>
