<p><strong> Syria, </strong> the province of which Antioch was the capital.
	</p>
<p><strong> Cilicia</strong>, the province adjoining Syria. Paul was a native of Tarsus, its capital, and a famous seat of learning. The object of his journey was no doubt to preach the gospel, as appears from <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+15:23">Acts 15:23</span>, where churches are mentioned in those regions. In Tarsus, Barnabas met him somewhat later, and took him to Antioch, where they remained a whole year, and then they went together to Jerusalem (A. D. 44) on a benevolent mission (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:25-30">Acts 11:25-30</span>).
	</p>
