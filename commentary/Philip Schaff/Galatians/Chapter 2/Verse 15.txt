<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:15">Galatians 2:15</span>. <strong> </strong> Many commentators close here the speech of Paul to Peter; others with <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:16">Galatians 2:16</span>; still others with <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:18">Galatians 2:18</span>. But the words, ‘we <em> who are Jews </em> by nature,’ would not suit the Galatians, most of whom were Gentiles by birth, and there is no mark of a return of the speech to the Galatians till <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:1">Galatians 3:1</span>.
	</p>
<p><strong> We Jews by birth, and not sinners, </strong> <em> i.e.,</em> gross sinners without law and without God, like the heathen. The two words were almost synonymous in the mouth of the Jew. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:13">Matthew 9:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:34">Luke 7:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:12">Romans 2:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:12">Ephesians 2:12</span>.
	</p>
