<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:18">Galatians 2:18</span>. The sin is the other way, in going back from Christ to Moses, from the gospel of freedom to the law of bondage. Paul speaks with delicate consideration in the first person, but really means Peter and the Judaizers. He supposes a case which actually occurred, and exposes its folly. Peter in this case proved himself an architect of ruin.
	</p>
<p><strong> The things which I pulled down, </strong> the Mosaic ordinances, in this case the Levitical law of meats. Paul frequently uses the metaphor of building; comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+3:10-14">1 Corinthians 3:10-14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+5:1">2 Corinthians 5:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+10:4">2 Corinthians 10:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:20">Romans 15:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:20-22">Ephesians 2:20-22</span>.
	</p>
<p><strong> I prove myself to be a transgressor </strong> of the law itself, by rebuilding it on the ruins of the gospel contrary to its own spirit and intent to prepare the way for the gospel as its fulfillment.
	</p>
