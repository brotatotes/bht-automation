<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:26">Galatians 3:26</span>. <strong> For ye all are sons of God</strong>. ‘All,’ Jews and Gentiles alike (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:27-28">Galatians 3:27-28</span>). ‘Sons’ (not ‘children,’ E. V.), implies here the idea of age and freedom, as distinct from the state of childhood and pupilage under the training of the pædagogue. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:6-7">Galatians 4:6-7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:14-15">Romans 8:14-15</span>. <em> </em> Paul uses the term ‘sons’ and ‘children’ of God mostly in opposition to slaves (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:7">Galatians 4:7</span>). John uses the term ‘children’ of God with reference to their new birth (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:12">John 1:12</span>; 1Jn 3:1-2 ; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1jo+3:10">1 John 3:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1jo+5:2">1 John 5:2</span>).
	</p>
<p><strong> By the faith</strong>, which is the act of a freeman, in <strong> Christ Jesus</strong> (the dative in Greek) <em> i.e.</em>, reposing in Christ, or (if we prefer connecting the words with ‘sons’ <em> ) </em> by virtue of your life-union with Christ, being grounded and rooted in Him.
	</p>
