<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:8">Galatians 3:8</span>. <strong> The Scripture, </strong> personified, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:22">Galatians 3:22</span>, for the author of the Scripture.
	</p>
<p><strong> Justifieth, </strong> now when Paul wrote, and at all times. It is the normal present indicating the only way of God’s justification, and sure accomplishment of his purpose.
	</p>
<p><strong> Declared beforehand the good tidings. </strong> The promise to Abraham was an anticipation of the gospel, <em> i.e.</em>, the good tidings of salvation by Christ.
	</p>
<p><strong> In thee, </strong> as the spiritual father. Quotation from two passages (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ge+12:3">Genesis 12:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ge+18:18">Genesis 18:18</span>), which are fused into one. The blessing promised includes the whole Christian salvation, which implies justification, <em> i.e., </em> the remission of sins and imputation of Christ’s merit.
	</p>
