<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:16">Galatians 4:16</span>. <strong> So then have I become your enemy by telling you the truth?</strong> He puts the conclusion politely and delicately in the form of a question instead of direct assertion. Others translate: ‘Therefore (because ye loved me so much) I have become (in the opinion of the Judaizing teachers) your enemy by telling you the truth.’ In the Judaizing pseudo-Clementine writings Paul is called an ‘enemy,’ and ‘lawless’ or ‘antinomian’ Some substitute ‘hateful to you’ for ‘jour enemy’ (taking the Greek word in the passive <strong> sense, </strong> as <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:10">Romans 5:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:18">Romans 11:18</span>). ‘By telling you the truth,’ refers to the second visit of Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:23">Acts 18:23</span>), when the Judaizers had probably already done much mischief.
	</p>
