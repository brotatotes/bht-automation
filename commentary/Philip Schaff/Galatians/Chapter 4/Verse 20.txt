<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:20">Galatians 4:20</span>. <strong> But (or, yea) I could</strong> <strong> with to be present with you now, and to change my voice, </strong> to adapt my speech more fully to your present condition and wants, <strong> </strong> to use severity or gentle persuasion as may be best (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:21">1 Corinthians 4:21</span>). Others: to change my present tone from severity to gentleness, to mitigate the effect of my written rebuke (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+2:5">2 Corinthians 2:5</span> ff.). But the former interpretation better suits the following clause. His wish to visit the Galatians again, was never gratified as far as we know.
	</p>
<p><strong> For I am perplexed about you. </strong> I am at a loss how to address you, I know not what to think of you, I cannot understand your conduct. He fears the worst, <strong> </strong> yet hopes for the best.
	</p>
