<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:10">Galatians 5:10</span>. <strong> I have confidence toward </strong> ( <strong> or</strong> <strong> in regard to) you in the Lord, </strong> etc. Paul hopes that the Galatians will return from their error, and this hope is grounded in his communion with Christ in whom he lived and moved. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="php+2:24">Philippians 2:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:4">2 Thessalonians 3:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:14">Romans 14:14</span>.
	</p>
<p><strong> He that troubleth you, </strong> all the false teachers. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:12">Galatians 5:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+11:4">2 Corinthians 11:4</span>.
	</p>
<p><strong> Shall bear his judgment, </strong> God’s judgment of condemnation. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:3">Romans 2:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:2">Romans 13:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:29">1 Corinthians 11:29</span>. The guilty must ‘bear’ the sentence as a burden.
	</p>
<p><strong> whosoever he be, </strong> whatever be his character and position. (Jerome thinks even of Peter, but without any good reason; for Peter agreed with Paul in principle and failed only in practice at Antioch.)
	</p>
