<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+5:21">Galatians 5:21</span>. <strong> Of which I forewarn you, as I did tell you before, </strong> on my former visits (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+1:9">Galatians 1:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:13">Galatians 4:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:16">Galatians 4:16</span>), when I preached to you the gospel which is death to all forms of immorality, and demands conformity to the holy character of Christ. <strong> They who do such things shall not inherit the kingdom of God, </strong> unless they be converted and sanctified. A hard and terrible word, yet most true (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+6:9-10">1 Corinthians 6:9-10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:50">1 Corinthians 15:50</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="re+22:15">Revelation 22:15</span>). Heaven is the abode of absolute purity, and nothing unclean can enter therein. ‘Without sanctification no man shall see the Lord.’ <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:14">Hebrews 12:14</span>.
