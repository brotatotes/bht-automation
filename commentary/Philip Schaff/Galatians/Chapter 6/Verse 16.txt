<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:16">Galatians 6:16</span>. <strong> And as many as shall walk according to this rule</strong>, etc. Rising above all earthly distinctions to the height of Christian contemplation, Paul pronounces a benediction to all who walk according to the rule indicated in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:15">Galatians 6:15</span>. The Greek term for ‘rule’ <em> (canon) </em> is the same which is now used for the Sacred Scriptures as the rule of the Christian faith and practice.
	</p>
<p><strong> Peace be on them</strong> <strong> and mercy, ‘</strong> Peace’ with God and with themselves, the precious fruit of the atonement and the greatest Christian blessing, which the world cannot give nor take away (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:27">John 14:27</span>). ‘Mercy’ is coupled with peace (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:2">1 Timothy 1:2</span> <em> ; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ti+1:2">2 Timothy 1:2</span></em>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:3">2 John 1:3</span>). In the other Pauline Epistles we have ‘grace and peace’ in the salutation.
	</p>
<p>And (namely) <strong> upon</strong> <strong> the Israel of God</strong>, the true children of Israel, the people of God, as distinct from the mere carnal descendants. The believing Christians generally (not the Jewish Christians exclusively) are meant (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:29">Galatians 3:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+4:26">Galatians 4:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:6-8">Romans 9:6-8</span>).
	</p>
