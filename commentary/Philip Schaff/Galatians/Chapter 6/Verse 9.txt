<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:9">Galatians 6:9</span>. <strong> But let</strong> <strong> us not be weary </strong> (lose heart) <strong> in well </strong> doing. Not only in regard to the duty of liberality, but in every good work. (Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2th+3:13">2 Thessalonians 3:13</span>.) ‘Fatigue is not weariness. In well-doing we are more apt to be weary than fatigued’ (Riddle).
	</p>
<p><strong> In</strong> <strong> due season we shall reap, </strong> in the time of harvest (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:15">1 Timothy 6:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1ti+6:15">1 Timothy 6:15</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:3">Titus 1:3</span>). ‘The due season is God’s season’ (Riddle). <strong> If</strong> <strong> we faint not,</strong> ‘as husbandmen overcome with heat and fatigue.’ (Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="jas+5:7">James 5:7</span>.)
	</p>
