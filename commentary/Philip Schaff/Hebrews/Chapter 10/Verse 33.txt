<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:33">Hebrews 10:33</span>. <strong> Partly in that ye became a spectacle of shame </strong> ‘a theatrical spectacle’ a term taken from those who were exposed in the theatre to shameful punishment (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+4:11">1 Corinthians 4:11</span>) <strong> in the scornful taunts </strong> (you suffered) <strong> and in active persecution, and partly in that ye became partakers </strong> (partners) <strong> with those who were living and suffering in this way. </strong> The word ‘living’ is not passive, but is repeatedly found in the Epistles to describe the actual condition of a man’s life (chap. Heb 13:18 ; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2co+1:12">2 Corinthians 1:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1ti+3:15">1 Timothy 3:15</span>). Such ‘reproach and affliction’ is recorded in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:18">Acts 5:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:40">Acts 5:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:3">Acts 8:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:19">Acts 11:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+22:19">Acts 22:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+26:10-11">Acts 26:10-11</span>, and in the history of Paul himself (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:27">Acts 21:27</span>). All those instances must have been familiar to Hebrew believers.
	</p>
