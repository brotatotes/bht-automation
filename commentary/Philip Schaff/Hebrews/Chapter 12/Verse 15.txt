<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:15">Hebrews 12:15</span>. <strong> Looking diligently.</strong> The word is used generally of pastoral oversight, but is here used to enforce mutual watchfulness and discipline; a truth set forth also in chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="heb+10:24">Hebrews 10:24</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:12">Hebrews 3:12</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:1">Hebrews 4:1</span>.
	</p>
<p><strong> Lest any man fail of</strong> (come short of by willfully relinquishing) <strong> the grace of God. </strong> The characteristic of the Gospel is ‘grace,’ apart from the works of the Law; and a man falls from it who puts himself at a distance from the blessing, and so gives it up.
	</p>
<p><strong> Lest any root,</strong> or plant, of bitterness, <strong> trouble</strong> the sacred enclosure of the Church, <strong> and thereby the many</strong> (the larger part of the ground even) <strong> be defiled</strong> (corrupted).
	</p>
