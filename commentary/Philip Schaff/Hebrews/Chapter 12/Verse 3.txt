<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:3">Hebrews 12:3</span>. <strong> For </strong> (He suffered as well as you, therefore you may well) <strong> consider </strong> (properly, compare His case with your own, and gather the lessons) <strong> him who hath endured </strong> (it is His permanent character that is described) <strong> such contradiction </strong> (not in words only, but hostility of every kind, even treason (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:12">John 19:12</span>)) <strong> of sinners against themselves </strong> ( <em> i.e</em> <strong><em> </em></strong> of those who, in thus acting, sinned <em> against their own souls</em>) <em> , </em> the other reading, ‘against Himself,’ has also good authority; ‘themselves’ suggests a fresh reason why the Hebrew Christians should not join ‘a gainsaying people’ by rejecting the Gospel.
	</p>
<p><strong> Lest ye grow weary and faint in your souls. </strong> Still the athlete’s figure. As the limbs grow faint (loose) in the race, so the soul in the Christian conflict. Principle is strengthened by thoughtfulness; for want of consideration Israel perished, as well as from want of knowledge.
	</p>
