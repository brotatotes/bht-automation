<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:9">Hebrews 3:9</span>. <strong> When</strong>; rather ‘where,’ a common meaning of the Greek word.
	</p>
<p><strong> Tempted me</strong>, <strong> proved me</strong>. The true reading is, ‘tempted me in’ (or by) ‘proving’ [me]. Strong passion is some excuse for sin. When men tempt God to try how far they may go, and how much He will bear, there is a shamelessness in their state of heart that is without excuse.
	</p>
<p><strong> And saw my works. </strong> Either the punishment God inflicted, which failed to lead them to repentance (as the word is used in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ps+64:10">Psalms 64:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="isa+5:12">Isaiah 5:12</span>), or my mighty works, punishment in part, but chiefly mercy, and disregarding both they became the more guilty.
	</p>
