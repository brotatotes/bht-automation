<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="heb+4:7">Hebrews 4:7</span>. <strong> Again. </strong> To continue the argument and to correct another misconstruction. He has already shown that the rest of God of which he here speaks is not the rest of God after creation; he now proceeds to show, by a further examination of the Psalm, that neither is it the rest of Canaan.
	</p>
<p><strong> He limiteth </strong> (still further defines the day and consequently the rest of which he speaks) <strong> a certain day, saying in David </strong> (as we say ‘in Daniel’), not <em> ‘by</em> ’ David, nor, as Bengel holds, ‘in,’ <em> i.e </em> by the Spirit dwelling in and inspiring him.
	</p>
<p><strong> A long time </strong> (some 500 years) <strong> after </strong> they had entered Canaan, <em> as it is said in the forequoted passage </em> (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:7">Hebrews 3:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="heb+3:15">Hebrews 3:15</span>).
	</p>
<p><strong> Today if ye hear his voice, harden not your hearts. </strong> Some think the words ‘Today’ look forward to the time of the Gospel (translating ‘today,’ <em> i.e </em> as it said a long time before the day comes; so Dr. J. Brown and others; but if this be the meaning, it would surely be needless for the writer to prove by argument that the entering into rest had not yet come).
	</p>
<p><strong> A long time </strong> points back to the entrance into Canaan, and ‘as it has been said before’ (the true reading) points simply to the previous quotations.
	</p>
