<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="heb+5:8">Hebrews 5:8</span>. <strong> Though he were a Son</strong>; more accurately, ‘ <strong> though he was Son</strong> ’ (there is no conditional thought expressed, but a strong assertion); literally, though being [in His own nature] Son, <strong> yet learned</strong> <strong> he his obedience</strong> (not obedience simply, but the obedience He practised, or the obedience which was to fit Him for His office) by (really the source of His knowledge) <strong> the things which he suffered.
	</strong></p>
<p>Son. The absence of the article again calls attention to His relation to the Father (see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:2">Hebrews 1:2</span>).
	</p>
