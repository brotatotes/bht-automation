<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:13">Hebrews 7:13</span>. The writer now proves the completeness of the change of the priesthood.
	</p>
<p><strong> For he of whom</strong> (not ‘to whom,’ Dr. J. Brown and others, the preposition being used to denote that to which a word or thing refers) <strong> these things</strong> (the words in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ps+110:0">Psalms 110:0</span>) <strong> are said</strong> (see the end of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="heb+7:11">Hebrews 7:11</span>) <strong> hath partaken</strong> <strong> of</strong> (better than ‘pertaineth’), hath become a member of, <strong> a different tribe</strong> (the words describe an already existing fact, and intimate that he had joined the tribe), <strong> of which tribe no man hath ever</strong> (the full force of the corrected text) <strong> given attendance</strong> (the word means to bestow labour or attention upon <strong> anything</strong>, see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:13">1 Timothy 4:13</span>) <strong> at the altar</strong>.
	</p>
