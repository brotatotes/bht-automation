<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:21">Hebrews 9:21</span>. <strong> Moreover, the tabernacle and all the vessels of the ministry</strong> (the service) <strong> he sprinkled in like manner with blood</strong> (probably later: it was certainly done every year, <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="le+16:16-20">Leviticus 16:16-20</span>. Josephus, however, gives the same fact as occurring at the inauguration of the covenant, and in very similar words, <em> Antiq. iii.</em> 8, 6).
	</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="heb+9:22">Hebrews 9:22</span>. <strong> And according to the law almost all things</strong> (some were purified with water, <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ex+19:10">Exodus 19:10</span>, etc.; others with water and the ashes of the heifer, <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="nu+31:22-24">Numbers 31:22-24</span>; but things which were specially appropriated to the worship of God) <strong> are cleansed with</strong> (in) <strong> blood</strong>; <strong> and apart from shedding of blood </strong> the word here brings up the language of the Lord’s Supper, ‘Shed for you’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:20">Luke 22:20</span>)
	</p>
<p><strong> there is no remission </strong> (forgiveness). The ‘almost’ of the first clause applies also to the second (see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="le+5:11-13">Leviticus 5:11-13</span>). The need on blood and the significance of it may be seen in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="le+17:11">Leviticus 17:11</span>.
	</p>
