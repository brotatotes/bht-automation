<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:20">James 2:20</span>. <strong> But wilt thou know,</strong> or rather, ‘Art thou willing to know,’ to recognise this truth? implying that such knowledge was not palatable to him.
	</p>
<p><strong> O vain man;</strong> that is, O empty man, puffed up with pride, trusting to thy outward privileges, but without seriousness and spiritual life.
	</p>
<p><strong> that faith without works is dead.</strong> Some manuscripts read ‘is idle,’ that is, inoperative or useless; a reading which makes no alteration in the sense. Faith without works is properly not faith at all <strong> ,</strong> but reprobate faithlessness.
	</p>
