<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:10">James 3:10</span>. <strong> Out of the same mouth proceedeth blessing and cursing. My brethren, these things ought</strong> <strong> not so to be. </strong> There is here a moral incongruity. ‘The annals of Christendom,’ observes Dean Plumptre, ‘show that the necessity for the warning has not passed away. Councils formulating the faith, and uttering their curses on heretics; <strong> </strong> <em> Te Deums </em> chanted at an <em> Auto da Fe, </em> or after a massacre of St. Bartholomew; the railings of religious parties who are restrained from other modes of warfare, present the same melancholy inconsistency.’
	</p>
