<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:14">James 3:14</span>. <strong> But if ye have bitter envying </strong> zeal or emulation in a bad sense, as is evident from the epithet ‘bitter,’
	</p>
<p><strong> and strife,</strong> or rather factiousness, contention, party-strife; the reference being specially to religious controversies.
	</p>
<p><strong> in your hearts, glory not,</strong> boast not, <strong> and lie not,</strong> by a false pretence to wisdom and knowledge, <strong> against the truth:</strong> not subjective, ‘against veracity,’ being destitute of the truth, which would render the passage tautological; but objective, ‘against the truth of God,’ namely the Gospel.
	</p>
