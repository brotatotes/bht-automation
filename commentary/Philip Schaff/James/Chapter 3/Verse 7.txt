<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="jas+3:7">James 3:7</span>. <strong> For every kind:</strong> literally, every nature or disposition.
	</p>
<p><strong> of beasts, and of birds, and of serpents, and of things in the sea: </strong> the inferior creation arranged under its usual fourfold classification beasts of the earth, fowls of heaven, creeping things, and fish of the sea.
	</p>
<p><strong> is tamed </strong> better, ‘is subdued,’ as we can hardly say that all the inferior animals are tamed, many of them being incapable of being so; but they may all be subdued.
	</p>
<p><strong> and hath been tamed, </strong> subdued.
	</p>
<p><strong> of mankind: </strong> literally, ‘by the nature of men,’ answering to the nature of the inferior animals mentioned above; hence ‘by human nature.’
	</p>
