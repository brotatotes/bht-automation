<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:8">John 1:8</span>. <strong> He was not the Light, but he was that he might bear witness concerning the Light. </strong> The thought of the greatness of the witness borne by John underlies the words of this verse. Great as the Baptist was, he was not the Light. What he <em> was</em> is not expressed, but only the purpose which he was to fulfil (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:23">John 1:23</span>). It is very possible that the words may have had a special application to the opinions which (as we learn from <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:25">Acts 18:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:3">Acts 19:3</span>) existed at Ephesus with regard to the mission of John.
	</p>
