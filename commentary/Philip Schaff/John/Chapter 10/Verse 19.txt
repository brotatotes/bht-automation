<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:19">John 10:19</span>. <strong> There arose a</strong> <strong> division again among</strong> <strong> the Jews because of</strong> <strong> these words.</strong> The effect related in chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:43">John 7:43</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:16">John 9:16</span>, is again produced. This time however (as in chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:31">John 8:31</span>) ‘the Jews’ themselves are divided. The preceding parable therefore must have been spoken in the hearing of many who were hostile to Jesus, as well as of Pharisees (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+9:40">John 9:40</span>) who may have been half convinced.</p>
