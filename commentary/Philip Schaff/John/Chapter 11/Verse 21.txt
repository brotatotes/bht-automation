<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:21">John 11:21</span>. <strong> Martha therefore said unto Jesus, Lord, if thou hadst been here</strong>, <strong> my brother</strong> <strong> had not died. </strong> Her first words express no reproach, but <strong> </strong> only the <strong> </strong> bitter thought of help come too late. In His presence her brother could not have died (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:15">John 11:15</span> <strong> ). O</strong> f the possibility that Jesus might have <strong> </strong> spoken the word of help, even though their message might reach Him too late to bring Him to their dying brother, she says nothing, though the <strong> </strong> Jews, unchecked by the reverence of love, freely ask the question among themselves (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:37">John 11:37</span>).</p>
