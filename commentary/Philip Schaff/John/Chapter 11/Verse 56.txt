<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:56">John 11:56</span>. <strong> They sought therefore for Jesus, and spake among themselves, as they stood in the temple-courts, What think ye, that he will not come to the feast? </strong> The language is that of <strong> </strong> earnest and interested inquiry. Those who are talking together are friendly to Jesus, and hopeful and expectant that He will appear at the <strong> </strong> festival. The groups assemble in the temple-courts, where many of them may have come to bring offerings for purification (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:55">John 11:55</span>), and where Jesus had been wont to teach. The word ‘therefore’ at the beginning of this verse seems to point to the privacy into which Jesus had retired (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:54">John 11:54</span>). These pilgrims came to Jerusalem, hoping to meet with Jesus, but they saw Him not: they sought Him therefore, etc. (comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:11">John 7:11</span>).</p>
