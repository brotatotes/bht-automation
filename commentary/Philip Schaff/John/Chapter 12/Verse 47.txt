<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:47">John 12:47</span>. <strong> And if any one shall have heard my sayings and have guarded them not. </strong> It is necessary here to introduce an unusual word in the translation. To ‘keep’ the sayings or words of Jesus is a phrase which often meets us in this Gospel (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:51">John 8:51</span>, etc.): ‘guard’ is an un common word with the Evangelist, found only here and in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:25">John 12:25</span>, and (in conjunction with ‘keep’) in chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:12">John 17:12</span>. That the sayings may be kept and not lost from memory and life, they must be guarded with all care, and <em> watchfully</em> <strong> </strong> observed. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:26">Matthew 7:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:49">Luke 6:49</span>. </p>
<p><strong> I</strong> <strong> judge him not: for I came not to judge the world, but to save the world. </strong> Comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:17">John 3:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:15">John 8:15</span>.</p>
