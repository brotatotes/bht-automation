<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:30">John 18:30</span>. <strong> They answered and said unto him, If this man were not an evil-doer, we should not have delivered him up unto thee. </strong> There is pride in the reply, a lofty sense of their own <strong> </strong> importance and dignity, that importance and dignity which they are so soon to sacrifice. The person whom we bring before thee is a malefactor: is it not enough that <em> we</em> say so, and that <em> we</em> deliver him up to thee?</p>
