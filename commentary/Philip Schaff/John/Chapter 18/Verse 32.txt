<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:32">John 18:32</span>. <strong> That the word of Jesus might be fulfilled, which he spake, signifying by what manner of death he was about to die. </strong> The ‘word’ referred to is chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:14">John 3:14</span>, or still more probably chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:32">John 12:32</span>. The appeal to Pilate paved the way for the ‘lifting on high’ there spoken of. The Jewish mode of putting to death was stoning. Crucifixion was a Roman punishment, and could be inflicted by the Roman power alone. Hence, accordingly, the fulfilment of that ‘word’ of Jesus by the very persons who seemed to have Him completely in their hands. So far from its being <strong> so, </strong> they were in His.</p>
