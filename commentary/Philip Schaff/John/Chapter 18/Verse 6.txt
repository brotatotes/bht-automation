<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:6">John 18:6</span>. <strong> When therefore he said unto them,</strong> <strong> I am he, they went backward and fell to the ground. </strong> It is <strong> </strong> the Divine majesty and innocence of Jesus that produced the effect. Like the buyers and sellers in <strong> </strong> the temple, the history of whose terror at the presence of the Redeemer is <strong> </strong> vouched for by the testimony of the earlier gospels as much as by that of the fourth, they are overwhelmed with awe, and fall before Him (comp. on <strong> <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:16">John 2:16</span></strong>). As soon as they recover, Jesus repeats His question.</p>
