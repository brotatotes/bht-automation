<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:46">John 4:46</span>. <strong> He came therefore again into Cana of Galilee, where he made the water wine.</strong> His coming revives the fame of that first miracle, and the report of His arrival quickly spreads.
	</p>
<p><strong> And there was a certain king’s officer, whose son was sick at Capernaum.</strong> This officer was probably in the (civil or military) service of Herod Antipas, a Tetrarch, but often styled a king (see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:1">Matthew 14:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:9">Matthew 14:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:14">Mark 6:14</span>, etc.). The officer himself may have been in attendance on the court in Tiberias, but his son (probably an only son, as the Greek literally means ‘of whom <em> the</em> son . . .’) was lying ill at Capernaum.</p>
