<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:48">John 4:48</span>. <strong> </strong> Jesus therefore said unto him, Except ye see signs and wonders, ye will not believe.
	</p>
<p>The charge against the father is that his apparent faith is only thinly-veiled unbelief. The words seem most suitably addressed to a Jew (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:39">Matthew 12:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:1">Matthew 16:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:22">1 Corinthians 1:22</span>): on the other hand, the officer’s connection with the court leads rather to belief that he was a Gentile. As to ‘signs,’ see the notes on chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:11">John 2:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:23">John 2:23</span>. As a ‘sign’ is the highest, so a ‘wonder’ is the least noble name for a miracle. In so far as the miracle is a prodigy and excites amazement, it is a ‘wonder.’</p>
