<p>The general object aimed at in the relation of the story of Nicodemus in chap. 3 is pursued in the account given us in this section of the interview of Jesus, first with the Samaritan woman, and then with the inhabitants of Sychar, who are brought by her to listen to His teaching. The subordinate parts are ( 1 ) <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:1-4">John 4:1-4</span>, introductory, after the manner of the introduction to the story of Nicodemus in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:23-25">John 2:23-25</span>; ( 2 ) <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:5-26">John 4:5-26</span>, interview with the Samaritan woman; ( 3 ) <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:27-30">John 4:27-30</span>, the mission of the woman to her fellow-townsmen; ( 4 ) <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:31-38">John 4:31-38</span>, the conversation of Jesus with His disciples, in regard to the nature and success of their work; ( 5 ) <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:39-42">John 4:39-42</span>, the work of Jesus among the inhabitants of Sychar.</p>
