<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:19">John 6:19</span>. <strong> So when they had rowed about five and twenty or thirty furlongs. </strong> If the wind had driven them southwards soon after their starting, they would be near the eastern coast at a point where the lake is about forty furlongs broad. If therefore they had rowed twenty-five or thirty furlongs, they would not be far from ‘the midst of the sea’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:47">Mark 6:47</span>). The agreement between the two narratives is clearly ‘undesigned,’ and therefore the more interesting.
	</p>
<p><strong> They behold Jesus walking on the sea, and drawing nigh unto the boat: and they were afraid. </strong> When Jesus drew near to the boat, it was the ‘fourth watch’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:25">Matthew 14:25</span>), and therefore the darkest part of the night; some eight or nine hours had passed since they left Him with the multitude. The wind was boisterous, the sea raging, their strength was spent with rowing (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:48">Mark 6:48</span>), when suddenly they behold Jesus walking on the sea, in the immediate neighbourhood of the boat. They knew not that it was He, and were terrified.</p>
