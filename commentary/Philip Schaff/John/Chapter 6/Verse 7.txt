<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:7">John 6:7</span>. <strong> Philip answered him, Two hundred pennyworth of bread is not sufficient for them, that every one may take a little. </strong> As the number of the men alone proved to be five thousand, an expenditure of 200 ‘pence’ ( <em> i.e.</em> 200 <em> denarii</em>) <strong><em> </em></strong> would allow less than a <em> denarius</em>, or about eight-pence of our money, to twenty-five persons, and that sum would not purchase in ordinary times more than five or six ounces of bread for each. Philip might well say that it was ‘not sufficient for them.’</p>
