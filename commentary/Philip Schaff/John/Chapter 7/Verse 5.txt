<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:5">John 7:5</span>. <strong><em> </em></strong> <strong> For not even did his brethren believe in him. </strong> This verse seems to afford an unanswerable argument against those who hold that amongst these ‘brothers’ of our Lord were included two or three of the twelve apostles. How long this unbelief lasted we cannot tell: the words of Paul in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+15:7">1 Corinthians 15:7</span>, ‘Then He appeared to James,’ make it very probable that it was by our Lord’s resurrection from the dead that the brothers were led to a true belief in that Divine mission which, in spite of the earlier miracles they had witnessed, they had refused to accept.
	</p>
