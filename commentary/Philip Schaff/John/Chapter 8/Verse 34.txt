<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+8:34">John 8:34</span>. <strong> Jesus answered them, Verily, verily, I say unto you. Every one that doeth sin is a slave of sin. </strong> Jesus directs them to a slavery of which they have not thought, slavery to sin. Every one who is living a life of sin is a slave; each act of sin is no mere accident of his life, but a token of its nature, a mark of a bondage in which he is continually held. The word ‘doeth’ is not the same as that which is used in chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:20">John 3:20</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:29">John 5:29</span> in connection with evil: that had reference to the commission of particular acts, this to the general course of life, when sin <em> is chosen</em>, ‘Evil be thou my good.’ The thought is best illustrated by <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:0">Romans 6:0</span> and (especially) <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:0">Romans 7:0</span>.</p>
