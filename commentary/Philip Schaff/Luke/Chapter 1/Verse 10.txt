<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:10">Luke 1:10</span>. <strong> Were praying.</strong> The smoke of the incense was symbolical of acceptable prayer rising to God; comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ps+141:2">Psalms 141:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="re+5:8">Revelation 5:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="re+8:3-4">Revelation 8:3-4</span>. It was the custom to pray <strong> without,</strong> <em> i.e.:,</em> in the courts of the men and women, <strong> at the hour of incense</strong>, <em> i.e.</em>, while it was burnt. This was probably at the time of the morning sacrifice, as the allotment seems to have just occurred. Josephus tells of a vision to John Hyrcanus, the high-priest, while offering incense.
