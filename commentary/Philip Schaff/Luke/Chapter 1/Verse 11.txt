<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:11">Luke 1:11</span>. Appeared to him. An actual angelic appearance. The pious priest, engaged in this high duty, alone in the holiest spot into which he could enter, at the most sacred moment, would be in a state of religious susceptibility; but the revelation itself came from without, from a personal spirit sent by God. The presence of angels in the place dedicated to God, even at such a time of corruption, is suggestive.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.42em;"><strong> On the</strong> <strong> right side of the altar of incense.</strong> Probably on the right of Zacharias: the right side (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:33">Matthew 25:33</span>), indicative of a blessing, was in this case the north side of the altar, where the table of the shew-bread stood. ‘The temple, so often the scene of the manifestation of the glory of the Lord, becomes again the centre, whence the first rays of light secretly break through the darkness.’
	</p>
