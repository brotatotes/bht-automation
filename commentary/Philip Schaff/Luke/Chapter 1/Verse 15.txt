<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:15">Luke 1:15</span>. <strong> He shall be great in the sight of the Lord.</strong> Spiritual, not temporal, greatness is promised.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.42em;"><strong> Neither wine nor strong drink. </strong> ‘Sikera,’ the Greek word here used, refers to liquors of an intoxicating character, not prepared from grapes. He was to be a Nazarite (see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="nu+6:0">Numbers 6:0</span>). Such vows were not unusual in New Testament times (see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:24">Acts 21:24</span>). John ranks with Isaac, as a son begotten in old age; with Samson and Samuel, as granted to the barren in answer to prayer, and as a Nazarite (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="jdg+13:5">Judges 13:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1sa+1:12">1 Samuel 1:12</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.42em;"><strong> Filled with the Holy Ghost,</strong> not with wine (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+5:18">Ephesians 5:18</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.42em;"><strong> Even from his mother’s womb</strong>. ‘From his very birth,’ hence the Holy Spirit may work in and on infants.
	</p>
