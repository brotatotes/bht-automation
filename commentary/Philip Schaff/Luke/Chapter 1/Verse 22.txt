<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:22">Luke 1:22</span>. <strong> They perceived.</strong> They probably asked why he had remained so long, and at once found that he was both deaf (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:62">Luke 1:62</span>) and dumb, as the word ‘speechless’ implies. From this they inferred <strong> that he had seen a vision in the temple,</strong> which was confirmed by Zacharias himself; for he (on his part, in response) <strong> was making signs to them,</strong> doubtless trying to hint what had happened. ‘When the voice of the preacher (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="isa+40:0">Isaiah 40:0</span>) is announced, the priesthood of the Old Testament becomes silent’ (Chemnitz), or can, at best, only make signs.
