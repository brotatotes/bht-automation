<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:42">Luke 1:42</span>. <strong> Blessed art thou among women.</strong> Blessed by God, beyond other women, rather than blessed by other women, although the latter followed as a consequence (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:27">Luke 11:27</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Blessed is the fruit of thy womb.</strong> Elisabeth had heard nothing of Mary’s situation, so far as we know, but speaks of it by inspiration.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"> <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:43">Luke 1:43</span>, <strong> Whence is this to me?</strong> Utterance of humility.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> The mother of my Lord</strong>, <em> i.e.,</em> the Messiah. This recognition was through inspiration. The designation ‘mother of God,’ which came into general use in the fifth century, is not found in the Bible.
	</p>
