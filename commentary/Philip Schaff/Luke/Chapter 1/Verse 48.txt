<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:48">Luke 1:48</span>. <strong> Hath looked upon</strong>; see chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:38">Luke 9:38</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> The</strong> <strong> low estate</strong>. Not humility of mind, but humility of station, of external condition.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> For, behold, from henceforth</strong>. In proof that the Lord had thus looked upon her low estate.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> All generations shall call me blessed.</strong> Recognize the blessedness bestowed on her by God, as already declared by Elisabeth (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:48">Luke 1:48</span>). Comp. the instance given in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:27">Luke 11:27</span>, and the significant reply of our Lord, which accepts the blessedness of his mother and yet cautions against excesses in this direction.
	</p>
