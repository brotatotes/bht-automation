<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:55">Luke 1:55</span>. <strong> As he spake unto our fathers</strong>. This is parenthetical, for the original plainly shows that to <strong> Abraham and his seed</strong>, should be joined to the word ‘mercy,’ at the close of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:54">Luke 1:54</span>. Yet God’s remembrance of His mercy is connected with His truthfulness to His promise. The promise: ‘In thy seed shall all the nations of the earth be blessed’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ge+22:18">Genesis 22:18</span>), indicates the universal character of God’s mercy.
	</p>
<p><strong> For ever</strong>. This also should be connected with ‘mercy.’ God has helped Israel in order to remember His mercy forever.
	</p>
