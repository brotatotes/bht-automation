<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:63">Luke 1:63</span>. <strong> A writing tablet.</strong> A tablet smeared with wax, on which they wrote with a style <em> (stylus),</em> a sharp instrument adapted for the purpose, the usual mode in those days.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Saying</strong>. A Hebrew form of expression as applied to writing, but natural enough.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> His name is John.</strong> Not ‘shall be;’ the declaration of the angel (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:13">Luke 1:13</span>) had already settled that question. Bengel: ‘This first writing of the New Testament begins with <em> grace</em> ’ (in allusion to the meaning of the name).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Marvelled.</strong> At this agreement of the father and the mother.
	</p>
