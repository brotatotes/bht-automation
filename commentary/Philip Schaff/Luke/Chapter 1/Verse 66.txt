<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:66">Luke 1:66</span>. <strong> What then shall this child be</strong>? ‘What then,’ <em> i.e.,</em> in view of these remarkable circumstances, a connection of thought not fully brought out in the E. V.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> For</strong>, or, ‘for indeed.’ This is a remark of the Evangelist, justifying what was said.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> The hand</strong>, etc. This common Old Testament figure means that the power of the Lord was present with him. Luke uses the same phrase in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+11:21">Acts 11:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:11">Acts 13:11</span>, and the same figure in a number of cases.
	</p>
