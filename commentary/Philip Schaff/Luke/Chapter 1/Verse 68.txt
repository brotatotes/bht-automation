<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:68">Luke 1:68</span>. <strong> Blessed</strong>. Latin: <em> Benedictus</em>, hence the name.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> For he hath visited,</strong> etc. The past tenses throughout are used because the eye of prophecy regards these certain future events as having already taken place.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Wrought redemption.</strong> This sums up the benefits bestowed by the Messiah, regarding them from the <em> priestly </em> point of view. It is very unlikely, that a priest would apply such a word to political deliverance alone.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> His people.</strong> Comp, the previous clause: ‘the God of Israel.’
	</p>
