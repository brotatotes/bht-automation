<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:71">Luke 1:71</span>. <strong> Salvation from our enemies.</strong> The word ‘salvation ‘is taken up again from <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:69">Luke 1:69</span>, the intervening verse being parenthetical (like the first clause of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:55">Luke 1:55</span>, which expresses the same thought). That political deliverance was in the mind of Zacharias cannot be doubted, but certainly not that alone. ‘But he chiefly prizes this political liberation as the means to a higher end, the reformation of Divine worship; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:74-75">Luke 1:74-75</span>.’ Van Oosterzee.</p>
