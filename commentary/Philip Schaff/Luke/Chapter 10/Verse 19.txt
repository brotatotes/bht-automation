<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:19">Luke 10:19</span>. <strong> I have given. </strong> The correct reading expresses an abiding fact. The Lord augments by a new promise the joy He has just confirmed.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Authority, </strong> delegated power here.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> To tread on serpents and scorpions. </strong> The promise is doubtless literal, so far as necessary to manifest higher spiritual power. In view of the connection we must accept an allusion to <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ge+3:15">Genesis 3:15</span>: ‘bruise the head of the serpent,’ and perhaps to <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ps+91:13">Psalms 91:13</span> also.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Over all the power of the enemy,</strong> <em> i.e., </em> Satan. What precedes also, as the original indicates, belongs to ‘the power of the enemy.’
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> In any wise injure you, </strong> though apparent hurt may come.
	</p>
