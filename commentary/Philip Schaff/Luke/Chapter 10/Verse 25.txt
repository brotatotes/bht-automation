<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:25">Luke 10:25</span>. <strong> A certain lawyer. </strong> A kind of scribe whose business it was to teach the law.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> And tempted, </strong> or, ‘trying,’ <strong> him. </strong> This implies a cold, self-righteous spirit, rather than a hostile one. He probably wished to see whether our Lord would teach anything in conflict with the law of Moses, or simply whether He could teach <em> him </em> anything new. The two states of mind are not very far removed from each other: Pharisaism, in its self-righteousness, may present either a conceit of orthodoxy or self-conceit.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Matter, what shall I do? </strong> He doubtless expected in reply the mention of some new thing, or at least some great thing.
	</p>
