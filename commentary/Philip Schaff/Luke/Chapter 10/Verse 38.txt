<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:38">Luke 10:38</span>. <strong> As they journeyed. </strong> During the great journey from Galilee to Jerusalem, spoken of in this part of the Gospel.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> A certain village. </strong> Luke does not say Bethany. The name is Tar more familiar to us than it would have been to Theophilus.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Martha. </strong> The name means ‘lady,’ answering to the Greek word used in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2jo+1:5">2 John 1:5</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Into her house. </strong> She was probably the elder sister, and hence the hostess. There is no proof that she was a widow, or the wife of Simon the leper (see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:6">Matthew 26:6</span>). In this first mention of her, as receiving our Lord, doubtless with great joy, we have an intimation of her character.
	</p>
