<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:45">Luke 11:45</span>. <strong> One of the lawyers </strong> (see on chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:25">Luke 10:25</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Thou reproachest us also, </strong> who are in official, ecclesiastical position. The man was not a Sadducee, but a Pharisee, and probably felt that the censure applied to him. He would shelter his character behind his office! Doubtless he would imply, as his successors have done: in touching us, the God-appointed officials, you are blaspheming.
	</p>
