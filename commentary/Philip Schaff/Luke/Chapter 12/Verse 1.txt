<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:1">Luke 12:1</span>. <strong> In the mean time. </strong> Literally: in which things, <em> i.e., </em> during those just related.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> When many thousands, </strong> lit., ‘the myriads,’ etc. ‘Myriads’ is used indefinitely here.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> First. </strong> May join this with what follows: ‘first of all beware,’ but we prefer the usual connection with ‘said,’ etc. He speaks to His disciples now, to the multitude afterwards (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:13">Luke 12:13</span> ff.).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Leaven of the Pharisees, </strong> <em> i.e., </em> their doctrine (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:12">Matthew 16:12</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Which is hypocrisy. </strong> Not strictly that the leaven was hypocrisy, but that their leaven (doctrine) was of such a kind <em> that its essence was hypocrisy. </em> This is reason why they should beware of it.
	</p>
