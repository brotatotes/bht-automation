<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:14">Luke 12:14</span>. <strong> Man. </strong> In a tone of reproof, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:1">Romans 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:20">Romans 9:20</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Who made me a judge? </strong> etc. Moses assumed this position and was reproached for it by one of his countrymen in language closely resembling this (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ex+2:14">Exodus 2:14</span>); Christ expressly rejects it. The one was the founder of a state, the other of a spiritual kingdom. A purely worldly case, our Lord declines to consider. It has been remarked that He repeatedly considered the question of <em> divorce; </em> which shows that marriage and divorce are not purely <em> secular </em> matters, but of a <em> religious </em> character.
	</p>
