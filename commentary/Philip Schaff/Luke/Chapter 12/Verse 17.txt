<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:17">Luke 12:17</span>. <strong> What shall I do? </strong> He does not appear as a grasping speculator, but as one whom wealth, by a very natural process, made discontented, anxious, and perplexed. The proper answer to his question is found in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:33">Luke 12:33</span>. But this prosperous man says, ‘my fruits,’ not God’s gifts; that too when the increase was due to God’s Providence. This feeling is as sinful in its way as recognized crimes.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"> <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:18">Luke 12:18</span>. <strong> This will I do, </strong> etc. He proposed to do just what every man of ordinary business sagacity would do. He was not a ‘fool,’ from a commercial point of view. He represents the great mass of <em> successful </em> men.
	</p>
