<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:59">Luke 12:59</span>. <strong> Then shalt by no means come out thence. </strong> Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:26">Matthew 5:26</span>. This figure represents the danger of punishment in view of failure to know and do what is right, and it must have an important and definite meaning. Those who come unreleased before the Judge at the last day, will be punished forever. Any other sense is out of keeping with the strong language of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:46">Luke 12:46</span>, and of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:56">Luke 12:56</span> (‘ye hypocrites’).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Mite. </strong> Greek, ‘lepton,’ the smallest of coins then in use. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:42">Mark 12:42</span>.
