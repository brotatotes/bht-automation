<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:3">Luke 13:3</span>. <strong> Unless ye repent. </strong> It does not follow that those addressed were Galileans. If <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:47-54">John 11:47-54</span> refers to a time preceding this incident, then this intelligence may have been brought to our Lord to warn Him against the danger awaiting Him and His disciples at Jerusalem. He warns His hearers of their danger. He corrects their mistake in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+13:2">Luke 13:2</span>, but here bases His warning upon the truth which lay back of it, namely, that sin is often punished in this world. Hence each should repent of his own sins, rather than be over-anxious to interpret calamities, as judgments upon others for their sins.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Ye shall all in like manner perish, </strong> <em> i.e.</em>, by the Roman sword. At the destruction of Jerusalem, it was the temple especially that ran with blood.
	</p>
