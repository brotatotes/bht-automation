<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:1">Luke 14:1</span>. <strong> One of the rulers of the Pharisees. </strong> Possibly a member of the Sanhedrin, but certainly one of the influential, leading men of the party.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> On the Sabbath. </strong> The Jews gave feasts on the Sabbath, the food being prepared the day previous. The custom gave rise to great abuses, though doubtless the letter of the fourth commandment was observed. A number of guests were present, mainly Pharisees (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:3">Luke 14:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:7">Luke 14:7</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Were watching him. </strong> The Pharisees, since that class was last spoken of, were watching if He would do or say anything which would furnish a pretext for opposing Him. The hospitality was <strong> </strong> hostile.
	</p>
