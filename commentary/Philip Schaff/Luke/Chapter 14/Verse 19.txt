<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:19">Luke 14:19</span>. <strong> I have bought five yoke of oxen. </strong> This one too is hindered by his possessions, but he does not plead necessity; he was <strong> going to prove them, </strong> had started as it were, and preferred not to alter his plan. The first represents one so <em> pressed </em> with business, that he thinks he cannot find time to attend to a higher obligation which he still acknowledges <strong> ;</strong> the second, one so <em> interested </em> in his worldly plans that he will not relinquish them, though he feels that he must excuse his conduct
	</p>
