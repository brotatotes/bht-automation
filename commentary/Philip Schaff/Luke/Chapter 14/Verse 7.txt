<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:7">Luke 14:7</span>. A <strong> parable, </strong> in the widest sense, since the language is to be taken literally, though made the basis of a general moral lesson (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:11">Luke 14:11</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Them that were hidden. </strong> The invited guests, evidently numerous, were now arriving.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> The chief places. </strong> We supply ‘at table’ to avoid ambiguity. The coveted places (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:6">Matthew 23:6</span>,) were at the middle table, joining the two side tables. At a large feast this table would be long, and the places numerous.
	</p>
