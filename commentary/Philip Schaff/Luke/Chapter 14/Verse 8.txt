<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:8">Luke 14:8</span>. <strong> To a marriage feast. </strong> The greatest festivity, where questions of place were (and are still) considered of most importance. The figure suggests a reference to the feast of the kingdom of God, but this is not the primary thought. Our Lord immediately after represents the class whom He is now addressing as invited to that feast, but not attending it (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:18">Luke 14:18</span>). The mention of an ordinary feast might have made the rebuke too pointed.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> More honorable, </strong> etc. Such an one would be entitled to the higher place, and at a wedding would obtain it, as the next verse shows. But this result is not the main reason for not taking the highest place.
	</p>
