<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:10">Luke 15:10</span>. <strong> He that is faithful, </strong> etc. Lest it should seem strange that so much importance is attached to the proper use of perishing and unrighteous wealth, remember the great principle: ‘He that is faithful,’ etc.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> That which is least, </strong> or ‘a very little.’ This refers to earthly possessions, and the faithfulness is the wise and prudent conduct suggested by the parable.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> In much. </strong> In this case this is equivalent to: ‘the true riches,’ ‘your own,’ the inheritance and possession of the sons of the light. But the principle is general, and capable of a great variety of applications. This verse opposes the view that the service of mammon is meant in the parable, for according to that interpretation it is by being <em> unfaithful </em> to mammon that true fidelity is to be reached.
	</p>
