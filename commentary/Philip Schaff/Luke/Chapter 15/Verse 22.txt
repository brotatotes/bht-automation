<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:22">Luke 15:22</span>. <strong> But the Father. </strong> The father’s acts respond <strong> ;</strong> but not according to the worthiness of the son.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Bring forth quickly. </strong> ‘Quickly,’ omitted in the E. V., is suggestive. </p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> The best robe. </strong> The upper garment of the higher classes among the Jews. (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:38">Mark 12:38</span>.) A comparison with <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="isa+61:10">Isaiah 61:10</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="re+3:18">Revelation 3:18</span> suggests as probable an allusion to the robe of righteousness provided for us by Christ.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> A ring, </strong> ‘seal ring,’ worn only by freemen, as also <strong> shoes, </strong> since slaves went barefoot. Some explain <strong> :</strong> the ring, the seal of the Spirit, the shoes, ‘the preparation of the gospel of peace.’ The sense of the whole verse is <strong> :</strong> God will restore the penitent, and give him, out of love, all that is necessary to mark him as a son.
	</p>
