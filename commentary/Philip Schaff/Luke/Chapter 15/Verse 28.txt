<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:28">Luke 15:28</span>. <strong> But he was angry. </strong> The occasion of the anger was the answer given by the servant; the reason of the anger is found in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:29-30">Luke 15:29-30</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Came out and entreated him. </strong> The father left the feast of joy to kindly urge the elder brother. This represents the long-suffering of God toward the self-righteous, the efforts to bring them to a better mind. The parable itself, spoken to the Pharisees (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:3">Luke 15:3</span>), was an entreaty to the elder brother.
	</p>
