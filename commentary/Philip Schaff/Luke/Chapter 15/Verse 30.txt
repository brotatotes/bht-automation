<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:30">Luke 15:30</span>. <strong> When this thy son came. </strong> He will not say ‘brother.’ In expressing contempt of his brother the greatest sin against his father is uttered; so Pharisees sin most heinously against God in their feelings and acts towards their fellow-men.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Devoured thy living. </strong> There is a reproach of the father implied here also.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> With harlots. </strong> It was preeminently Pharisaical to recall just then this fact
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Thou killedst, </strong> etc. In <strong> </strong> contrast with the latter part of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:29">Luke 15:29</span>.
	</p>
