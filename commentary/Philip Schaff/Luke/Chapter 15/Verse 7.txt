<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+15:7">Luke 15:7</span>. <strong> An hundred measures. </strong> The Hebrew measure (‘cor’) is here spoken of, equal to ten ephahs.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Write eighty. </strong> The variation in the amount deducted is without any special meaning. Still we may find in it a proof of the steward’s prudence. He knew the men with whom he had to deal and acted accordingly. Christian men too often slight such knowledge, but this parable condemns putting a premium on ignorance.
	</p>
