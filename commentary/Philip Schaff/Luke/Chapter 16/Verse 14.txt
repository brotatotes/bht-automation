<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:14">Luke 16:14</span>. <strong> And the Pharisees also. </strong> The preceding parable was addressed to the disciples (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:1">Luke 16:1</span>), but the Pharisees <strong> heard all these things. </strong> A continued act is meant, here and in what follows <strong> :</strong> <strong> and they scoffed at him. </strong> Their feeling was <strong> :</strong> This man makes riches of little account, but we know better; we can keep our wealth and our piety too. Hence the next verse is aimed at their semblance of piety, which was the basis of their derision of Him.
	</p>
