<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+16:27-28">Luke 16:27-28</span>. <strong> I pray thee therefore, </strong> etc. His brethren were living as he had done. ‘This is the <em> believing and trembling </em> of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:9">James 2:9</span>. His eyes are now opened to the truth <strong> ;</strong> and no wonder that his natural sympathies are awakened for his brethren. That a <em> lost spirit </em> should feel and express such sympathy is not to be wondered at; the misery of such will be very much heightened by the awakened and active state of those higher faculties and feelings which selfishness and the body kept down here.’ Alford.
