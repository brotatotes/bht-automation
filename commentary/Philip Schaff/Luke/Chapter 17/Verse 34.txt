<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:34">Luke 17:34</span>. <strong> I say unto you. </strong> Solemn introduction.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> In that night. </strong> Night is the time of surprise and terror, and the return of the Lord had already been set forth figuratively as occurring at night (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:35-39">Luke 12:35-39</span>); but <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:35-36">Luke 17:35-36</span>, refer to the day-time.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Two men on one bed. </strong> Peculiar to Luke. Illustrating the <em> separation </em> of those previously closely associated together. Husband and wife are not referred to, however. There will be a separation between the faithful and the unfaithful, as well as a gathering of the elect out of the world. This illustration gives prominence to the former idea, the next to the latter.
	</p>
