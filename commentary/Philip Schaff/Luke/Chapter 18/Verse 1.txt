<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:1">Luke 18:1</span>. <strong> Unto them, </strong> <em> i.e., </em> the disciples.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> To this end, </strong> not in order that, but <em> to show, </em> <strong> that they </strong> (the disciples) <strong> ought always to pray. </strong> Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1th+5:17">1 Thessalonians 5:17</span>: ‘Pray without ceasing.’ The latter refers to the believer’s prevailing frame of mind; this, to unwearied petition for the same object believed to be in accordance with God’s will. It shows the conflict of prayer in the distressed and suffering disciple.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Not to faint, </strong> not to be discouraged. The danger of discouragement arises from the delay in receiving an answer, while the ‘adversary’ continues to harass.’
	</p>
