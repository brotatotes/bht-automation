<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:12">Luke 19:12</span>. <strong> Therefore, </strong> with this purpose, in view of this improper expectation.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> A certain nobleman. </strong> This ‘well-born’ man represents the Lord Jesus; an indirect intimation of His kingly descent and dignity.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Went into a far country, </strong> etc. The journey was to the residence of the supreme authority. Archelaus, who had built a magnificent royal palace at Jericho, had made such a journey to Rome. The Lord was to go to heaven, the home of God; in the moral sense, ‘a far country.’
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> To return, </strong> <em> i.e., to </em> the kingdom, situated where the nobleman had resided. Our Lord will certainly ‘return.’
	</p>
