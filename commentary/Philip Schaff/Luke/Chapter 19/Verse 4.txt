<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:4">Luke 19:4</span>. <strong> San on before. </strong> An evidence of great desire, especially in a man of wealth.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> A sycamore tree. </strong> The Egyptian fig tree, resembling in many respects the mulberry tree. Not identical with ‘sycamine’ (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+17:6">Luke 17:6</span>), and altogether different from the modern sycamore. The derivation of the word favors the spelling: sycomore (fig-mulberry). See accompanying cut.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Was to pass that way. </strong> This shows that it was known which way Jesus would take. Hence the strong probability that he was on the direct way to Jerusalem.
	</p>
