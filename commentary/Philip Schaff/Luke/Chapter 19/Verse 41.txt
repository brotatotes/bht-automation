<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:41">Luke 19:41</span>. <strong> And when he drew nigh, seeing the city. </strong> Tradition, assuming that our Lord took the direct road, over the summit of the Mount of Olives, points out the spot as half-way down the western slope. But it is more probable that the road taken was the main or southern one, passing between two peaks (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:2">Matthew 21:2</span>). Comp. Stanley <em> (Sinai and Palestine, </em> p. 187). ‘Jesus has reached the edge of the plateau; the holy city lies before His view. What a day it would be for it, if the bandage fell from its eyes! But what has just passed between Him and the Pharisees present has awakened in His heart the conviction of the insurmountable resistance which He is about to meet. Then Jesus, seized, and, as it were, wrung by the contrast between what is and what might be, breaks out into sobs.’ (Godet.)
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Wept over it. </strong> An outburst of grief, not silent tears now, as at the grave of Lazarus (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+11:35">John 11:35</span>). Peculiar to Luke.
	</p>
