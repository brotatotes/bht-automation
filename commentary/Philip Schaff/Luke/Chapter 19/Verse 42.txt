<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:42">Luke 19:42</span>. <strong> If thou hadst known. </strong> The pathetic expression of a fruitless wish.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> In this day. </strong> That day of entry is meant, as concentrating in itself all the intimations and proofs of His Messiahship, and becoming a direct offer of Himself for their acceptance; comp. ‘the time of thy visitation’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:44">Luke 19:44</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Even thou, </strong> as well as the disciples, who now testify their knowledge by their shouts of Hosanna.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> The things which belong unto peace</strong>! ‘Peace’ here certainly includes the idea of deliverance, safety. Perhaps as originally uttered there was an allusion to the name Jerusalem (Salem = peace). What was necessary for this peace was the recognition of Jesus as the Messiah.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> But now they are hid from thine eyes. </strong> The city, as a whole, rejected Him that day, though its positive antagonism came later in the week. This hiding was according to the righteous counsel of God (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:25-26">Matthew 11:25-26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:37">John 12:37</span>, etc.; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:7">Romans 11:7</span>, etc.); but the personal guilt of the inhabitants was directly involved, as is evident from the emotion of our Lord.
	</p>
