<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:7">Luke 19:7</span>. <strong> They all murmured. </strong> Scarcely the disciples, but the crowd of Jews, among whom doubtless were many priests, since Jericho was a priestly city.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> To lodge. </strong> Not necessarily to remain over night. The same word occurs in this sense in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:39">John 1:39</span>, but the time of day is there specified, to show that it has that meaning.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> A sinner. </strong> Zaccheus, as a publican, would be thus termed, whatever his character had been. Especially in a priestly city like Jericho would the chief publican be an object of scorn. But his own confession (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+19:8">Luke 19:8</span>) implies that he deserved the name.
	</p>
