<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Daily </strong> (comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:37">Luke 21:37</span>). On Monday and Tuesday. On the last named day, He solemnly and formally took leave of the temple; see on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:1">Matthew 24:1</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> The chief men of the people. </strong> The worldly aristocracy in distinction from the common people. There were Sadducees as well as priests and scribes among His opponents.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> And they could not find, </strong> etc. This perplexity had begun some time before (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+7:30-53">John 7:30-53</span>), but was now reaching its height.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> For the people. </strong> Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:37">Mark 12:37</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> All hung upon him, listening. </strong> The E.V. omits the striking figure of the original. The attitude of the people was an obstacle to the hostile rulers. But malicious craft found its opportunity in a few short days. Luke here, as often elsewhere, gives a sketch of events afterwards narrated in detail.
	</p>
