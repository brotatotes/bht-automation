<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:12">Luke 2:12</span>. <strong> The sign</strong>. No sign had been asked for (comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:36">Luke 1:36</span>); when Zacharias requested one, he was punished (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:18">Luke 1:18</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:20">Luke 1:20</span>). The dispensation of <em> faith</em> <strong> </strong> is beginning.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> A babe.</strong> Not ‘ <em> the </em> babe.’ They were to look for a child born that day, wrapped and lying in a manger. There could be but one such.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> A manger</strong>, not ‘ <em> the </em> manger.’ This implies that the place was not one well known to the shepherds. Hence the stable could scarcely have belonged to them. Some suppose that a secret influence guided them to the spot, but after such a revelation they would seek, <em> it</em> necessary, among the stables of a small place like Bethlehem.</p>
