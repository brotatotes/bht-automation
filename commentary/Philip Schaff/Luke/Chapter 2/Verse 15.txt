<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:15">Luke 2:15</span>. <strong> The shepherds</strong>. The angels went to heaven; the shepherds sought what the angels had praised: the former, to continue the song of ‘glory in the highest;’ the latter, to discover ‘peace on earth.’
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Now</strong>, <em> i.e.</em>, at once.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Even</strong> <strong> unto</strong> <strong> Bethlehem</strong>. As far as Bethlehem; as though it were not their usual place of resort.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> This thing</strong>, lit., ‘saying;’ the same word is used in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:17">Luke 2:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:19">Luke 2:19</span>. The simple faith of these shepherds is a token that they were men ‘in whom He is well-pleased’ and hence chosen to receive this revelation.</p>
