<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:19">Luke 2:19</span>. <strong> But Mary</strong>. Still in the foreground.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Kept</strong> <strong> all these sayings</strong>. She kept, or more exactly, she was keeping, continued to keep, in her memory, all these sayings, <em> i.e. </em> all these things now spoken of.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Pondering them in her</strong> <strong> heart</strong>. Revolving, comparing, reflecting upon them in the quietude of her heart. She possessed ‘the ornament of a meek and quiet spirit’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1pe+3:4">1 Peter 3:4</span>). This accurate detail favors the view that the account was derived, at least indirectly, from her. Evidently she had <em> not</em> a full understanding of the matter.</p>
