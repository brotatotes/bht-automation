<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:22">Luke 2:22</span>. <strong> Their purification.</strong> This refers to Mary and Joseph, rather than to Mary and the child. In <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="le+12:4-6">Leviticus 12:4-6</span>, there is no hint of the purification of the child. The presence of Joseph was required by the law respecting the redemption of the first-born (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:23">Luke 2:23</span>), and the ceremonial uncleanness, which lasted until the fortieth day in the case of a male child (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="le+12:2-4">Leviticus 12:2-4</span>), affected the husband.</p>
