<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:25">Luke 2:25</span>. <strong> Simeon</strong>. According to some, this was the son of the famous Rabbi Hillel, and father of Gamaliel the teacher of Paul (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+5:34">Acts 5:34</span>). The Rabbis say that Jesus was born in the days of Rabbi Simeon, son of Hillel. But the name was very common; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:26">Luke 2:26</span> suggests that this man did not live long afterwards, while Rabbi Simeon was alive in A. D. 13 ; and the <strong> </strong> language here does not point to a famous man. Another untrustworthy tradition describes him as blind, but receiving his sight on the approach of the child Jesus.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Righteous</strong>, as regards the law, <strong> and</strong> <strong> devout,</strong> religiously conscientious (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+2:5">Acts 2:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+8:2">Acts 8:2</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Waiting for the consolation of Israel</strong>, <em> i.e.</em>, for the coming of the Messiah to console Israel after the sorrows ( <em> dolores Messice</em>)which according to the common belief should precede that coming. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:38">Luke 2:38</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> And the Holy Spirit was upon him</strong>. This explains the subsequent revelation.</p>
