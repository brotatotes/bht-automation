<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:40">Luke 2:40</span>. <strong> And the child grew</strong>. Comp, the account of John’s youth (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:80">Luke 1:80</span>). The next section illustrates what is stated in this verse, and <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:52">Luke 2:52</span> repeats and extends the statement. Growth of body is mentioned first, a point not to be overlooked.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> And waxed</strong> <strong> strong.</strong> The words ‘in spirit’ are inserted from chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:80">Luke 1:80</span>, and refer the statement to mental and spiritual development; but without this interpolation the sense <em> is: </em> Our Lord in His genuine human development, grew strong as he grew in body, had a healthy physical growth.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Being</strong> (or ‘becoming’) <strong> filled with</strong> <strong> wisdom</strong>. In mind and spirit too He grew. This being filled with wisdom was an increase of knowledge in proportion to His physical growth, including as the next incident (especially <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:49">Luke 2:49</span>) plainly implies, an increasing consciousness of God as His Father, an awakening of His own divine-human consciousness, a recognition of Himself, a revelation of the wisdom belonging to His Divine nature. For this ‘wisdom ‘was in Him and is distinguished from what is stated next: <strong> and the grace of God </strong> (the <strong> </strong> favor of <strong> </strong> God His Father) <strong> was upon him</strong>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:52">Luke 2:52</span>.
	</p>
