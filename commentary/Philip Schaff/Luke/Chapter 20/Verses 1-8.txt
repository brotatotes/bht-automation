<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:1-8">Luke 20:1-8</span>. OUR LORD QUESTIONED AS TO HIS AUTHORITY. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:23-27">Matthew 21:23-27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:27-33">Mark 11:27-33</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> On</strong> <strong><em> </em></strong> <strong> one of the days. </strong> On Tuesday morning, as we think.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Preaching the gospel, </strong> or ‘good tidings.’ Peculiar to Luke.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Came upon him. </strong> This suggests the formality and ‘solemnity of the proceeding, since all three classes of the Sanhedrin were represented.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Or</strong> <strong> who is he, </strong> etc. Or, <em> i.e., </em> to speak more definitely Matthew and Mark have ‘and.’
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> All the people will stone us. </strong> Thus Luke expresses more fully the thought: ‘they feared the people.’
	</p>
