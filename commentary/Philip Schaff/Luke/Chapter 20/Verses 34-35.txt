<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:34-35">Luke 20:34-35</span>. Peculiar to Luke, who however omits the solemn opening rebuke: ‘Ye do err,’ etc. (Matt., Mark). </p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> The torn of this world; </strong> here used in the <em> physical </em> sense, <strong><em> i.e.,</em></strong> <em> </em> those actually living in the present order of things.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Marry, and are given in marriage. </strong> There is no reference to the moral character of the persons thus described; ‘this world’ simply meaning the period preceding the resurrection at the return of the Messiah. The verse cannot be used to prove the superior holiness of celibacy.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Accounted worthy, </strong> <em> i.e., </em> at the coming of the Lord. Here the moral character is spoken of.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> To obtain that world, </strong> the state of life after the coming of the Messiah, which is introduced by <strong> the resurrection from the dead. </strong> This means the <em> first </em> resurrection of the <em> righteous </em> (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+14:14">Luke 14:14</span>), and the statement probably includes those believers who are living at the Second Advent.
	</p>
