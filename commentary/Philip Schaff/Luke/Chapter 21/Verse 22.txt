<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:22">Luke 21:22</span>. <strong> Days of vengeance. </strong> Of God’s vengeance, not of man’s. Comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:8">Luke 18:8</span>. Even Titus seems to have been conscious that he was a minister of Divine retribution.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> All things which are written may be fulfilled. </strong> Our Lord then asserts that this retribution bad been already prophesied in the Old Testament. ‘All things’ points to more than one prediction. That of Daniel, quoted by Matthew and Mark, is certainly included, but, others also, beginning with <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="de+28:15">Deuteronomy 28:15</span>, etc., and running through the whole prophetic period.
	</p>
