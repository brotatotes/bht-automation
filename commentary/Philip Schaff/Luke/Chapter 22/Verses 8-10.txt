<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:8">Luke 22:8</span>. <strong> And he sent. </strong> It is doubtful whether the question of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:9">Luke 22:9</span> is identical with that mentioned by the other evangelists, or whether the disciples had made a previous inquiry omitted here. The simplest solution is that they came for the purpose of inquiring, were then bidden as here, and then actually inquired.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Peter and John. </strong> Named here only. The chief Apostles were sent; hence the message was a solemn one.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"> <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:10">Luke 22:10</span>. <strong> There shall meet you. </strong> The original implies coming together, so that both go the same way. In other respects the account agrees closely with that of Mark.
	</p>
