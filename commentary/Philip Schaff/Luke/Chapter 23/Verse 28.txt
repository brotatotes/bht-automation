<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:28">Luke 23:28</span>. <strong> Daughters of Jerusalem. </strong> A natural address, but solemn and pointing to their relation to a doomed city.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Weep not for me. </strong> Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="heb+12:2">Hebrews 12:2</span>. He not only endures the cross, but forgets His sorrows, so heavy, to tell the truth to those who manifested for Him only a human sympathy.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> But weep for yourselves. </strong> Appropriate words for those who even now make of the crucifixion a mere popular tragedy. Doubtless many of these very women lived until the siege of Jerusalem, about forty years afterwards, but the catastrophe was to fall most directly upon their children: <strong> and for your children. </strong> Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:25">Matthew 27:25</span>: ‘His blood be on us and on our children.’
	</p>
