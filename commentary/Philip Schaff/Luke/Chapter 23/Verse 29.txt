<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:29">Luke 23:29</span>. <strong> Bays are coming. </strong> As certainly coming, as He was going to death.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> They shall say. </strong> ‘They’ refers to those in Jerusalem, especially the women in Jerusalem, <em> at the time foretold. </em> His disciples would not be there, and there is here implied a warning to escape. But the whole tone of the prediction implies also that few of them do so.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Blessed, </strong> etc. A fearful woe is introduced by the word ‘Blessed.’ <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ho+9:12-16">Hosea 9:12-16</span>, contains the same thought as this verse. The days will be so terrible that it will be a curse to be a mother instead of a blessing. When being a mother is reckoned a curse, the days are indeed evil!
	</p>
