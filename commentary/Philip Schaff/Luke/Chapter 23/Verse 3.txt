<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:3">Luke 23:3</span>. <strong> And Pilate asked him. </strong> This took place within the praetorium (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:33">John 18:33</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Art thou the King of the Jews? </strong> Pilate’s question implies some knowledge of the Messianic expectations of the Jews.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Thou sayest it</strong> = Yes. So Matthew and Mark. But fuller details of the interview are given by John (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:34-38">John 18:34-38</span>). Pilate’s language in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:4">Luke 23:4</span> implies some further conversation.
	</p>
