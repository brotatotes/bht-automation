<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:7">Luke 23:7</span>. <strong> Herod’s jurisdiction. </strong> As an inhabitant of Galilee, Jesus was under the authority of Herod Antipas, who was Tetrarch of Galilee and Perea.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> He sent him. </strong> The word used is a legal term generally applied to the transfer of a cause from a lower to a higher tribunal. Hence it was not to get Herod’s opinion, but to relieve himself by transferring his prisoner to Herod’s judgment. There may have been a thought of thus doing a courtesy to reconcile Herod. Their quarrel (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:12">Luke 23:12</span>) had probably been caused by some question of jurisdiction. </p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> In these days. </strong> Probably for the purpose of attending the Passover feast.
	</p>
