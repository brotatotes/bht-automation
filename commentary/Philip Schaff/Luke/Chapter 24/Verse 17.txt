<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:17">Luke 24:17</span>. <strong> What communications?</strong> Some earnest disputing is meant, though no blame is implied. This implies also that He walked with them for a time before He thus spoke.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> And they stood still, looking sad. </strong> This is the reading now generally accepted. It suggests that the interruption was unwelcome, as does the response of Cleopas (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:18">Luke 24:18</span>). The other reading may be taken as two questions: ‘as ye walk? and why are ye sad?’ or rendered as in the E. V. A briefer reading gives: ‘as ye walk (being) sad?’
	</p>
