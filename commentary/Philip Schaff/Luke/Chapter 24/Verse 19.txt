<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:19">Luke 24:19</span>. <strong> What things?</strong> <em> </em> Our Lord says nothing in regard to either point which Cleopas had assumed (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:18">Luke 24:18</span>), but puts a question to draw them out. It was the wisdom of love, concealing without falsehood or deceit.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> And they said. </strong> Probably Cleopas, the other chiming in. But it is unnecessary to portion out the discourse.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> The things concerning Jesus of Nazareth. </strong> They give Him the human name, of which a stranger might have heard.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> A prophet, mighty in word and deed. </strong> The sphere of His power was both in word and in deed. A similar expression is applied by Stephen to Moses.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Before God and all the people. </strong> By word and deed He had attested Himself as a Prophet, not only in the eyes of the people, the mass of whom thus regarded Him, but before the face of God.
	</p>
