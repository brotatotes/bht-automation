<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:20">Luke 24:20</span>. <strong> And how. </strong> The connection is with <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:18">Luke 24:18</span>; Hast not known how?
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Our rulers. </strong> These disciples were therefore Jews; and they probably thought their new companion was also of their race.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Delivered him. </strong> This was the act of the rulers.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> To be condemned to death. </strong> Lit., to the condemnation of death, <em> i.e., </em> by Pilate.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> And crucified him. </strong> Here, as so often, this is spoken of as the act of the chief-priests and rulers.
	</p>
