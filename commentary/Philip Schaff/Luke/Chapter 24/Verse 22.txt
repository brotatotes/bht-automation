<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:22">Luke 24:22</span>. <strong> Moreover. </strong> Here too there is a contrast, as much as to say: We were well-nigh hopeless, yet other occurrences aroused our hope, without however fulfilling it (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:24">Luke 24:24</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Of our company, </strong> cherishing the same <strong> hope.
	</strong></p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"> Amazed us.  This strong expression indicates the effect produced upon them in their perplexed state of mind, by the strange, but unsatisfactory state of things mentioned in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:23-24">Luke 24:23-24</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Having been early, </strong> etc. This should be joined with what follows. It begins the account of the facts that amazed them.
	</p>
