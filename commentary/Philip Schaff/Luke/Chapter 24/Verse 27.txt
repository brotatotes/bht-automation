<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:27">Luke 24:27</span>. <strong> Beginning from Moses and from all the prophets. </strong> Taking each in order, Moses first, and then beginning with each of the others in turn.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> In all the Scriptures, </strong> going through the whole Old Testament.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> The things concerning himself. </strong> The reproof of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:25">Luke 24:25</span>, and the phrase ‘in all the Scriptures,’ point to an explanation of the Old Testament as a whole, as typifying and prophesying of Him. Godet: ‘In studying the Scriptures for Himself, He had found Himself in them everywhere (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:39-40">John 5:39-40</span>). He had now only to let this light which filled His heart ray forth from Him.’
	</p>
