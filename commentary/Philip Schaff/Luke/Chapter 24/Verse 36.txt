<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:36">Luke 24:36</span>. <strong> And as they spake these things. </strong> Mark’s account hints at unbelief, and their subsequent fear suggests the same.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> He himself stood. </strong> A sudden miraculous appearing is meant, corresponding to the disappearance in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:31">Luke 24:31</span>. John’s account (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:16">Luke 20:16</span>), telling of closed doors, confirms this view.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> In the midst of them. </strong> A stronger expression than ‘among them.’
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Peace be unto you. </strong> Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:19">John 20:19</span>. The ordinary Jewish salutation, but meaning more in this case. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:40">Luke 24:40</span>.
	</p>
