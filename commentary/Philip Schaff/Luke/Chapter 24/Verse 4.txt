<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:4">Luke 24:4</span>. <strong> Perplexed thereabout</strong>. A natural state of mind, even if they had some hope of His rising, for now He seemed lost to them. Comp. Mary Magdalene’s expression (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:2-13">John 20:2-13</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Two men. </strong> This was the form of the angelic appearance.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Stood by them. </strong> As this word (comp. chap, <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:9">Luke 2:9</span>: ‘the angel of the Lord stood by them’) does not necessarily imply a standing position, there is no difficulty in reconciling this with <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+16:5">Mark 16:5</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> In shining garments. </strong> The word used implies that the brilliancy was like that of lightning. At such a time the presence of a multitude of angels was, so to speak, natural, and hence a variety of appearances.
	</p>
