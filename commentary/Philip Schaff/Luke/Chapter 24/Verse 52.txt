<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:52">Luke 24:52</span>. <strong> Worshipped him. </strong> As He went up; hence a more exalted worship than the homage accorded Him during His ministry.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> With great joy. </strong> Terror at His bodily presence (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:37">Luke 24:37</span>), joy after His bodily disappearance and exaltation, which was a pledge of the victory of His cause (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+14:28">John 14:28</span>). They rejoiced in His glory, and in the promise of the Spirit; doubtless their joy was itself ‘a prelude to Pentecost.’ (Bengel.)
	</p>
