<p>ON THE RESURRECTION. See the <em> Introductory Note to <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:0">Matthew 28:0</span></em> <em> . </em> Of the five appearances there grouped as occurring on the day of the resurrection, Luke omits all mention of (1) and (2). He, however, details the appearances of (4) and (5) in the subsequent part of this chapter, telling of (3) in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:34">Luke 24:34</span>, and then passing over the others, (which are, however, alluded to in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:3">Acts 1:3</span>) he closes with an account of the last appearance (10), which ended with the Ascension (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:50-51">Luke 24:50-51</span>; comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+1:6-10">Acts 1:6-10</span>). Luke narrates only that vision of two angels which was witnessed by the whole company of women (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+16:5">Mark 16:5</span>).
