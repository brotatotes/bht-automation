<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:16-17">Luke 3:16-17</span>. Comp, the accounts of Matthew and Mark, also <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:26-27">John 1:26-27</span>. The latter passage probably refers to a later interview with delegates from Jerusalem, though the language may have been used more than once.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> With water</strong>, not ‘in water,’ as in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:11">Matthew 3:11</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> In the Holy Spirit and fire</strong>. Not in fire of judgment, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:11">Matthew 3:11</span>. Notice the variations of ‘with’ and ‘in.’ The striking figure is repeated: <strong> Whose fan is in his</strong> hand, etc.</p>
