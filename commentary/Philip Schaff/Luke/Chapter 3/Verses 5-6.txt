<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:5-6">Luke 3:5-6</span>. <strong> Every valley</strong>, etc. Luke adds <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="isa+40:4">Isaiah 40:4</span>, and part of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:5">Luke 3:5</span>. The removal of natural obstacles from the path of an approaching conqueror represents the removal of moral hindrances, by means of John’s preaching of repentance, before the coming Messiah.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> The salvation of God</strong>. The ‘salvation’ spoken of by Simeon (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+2:30">Luke 2:30</span>).</p>
