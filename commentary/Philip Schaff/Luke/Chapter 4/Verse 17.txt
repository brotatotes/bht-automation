<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:17">Luke 4:17</span>. <strong> And there was delivered to him</strong>, by the attendant of the synagogue.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> A roll of the prophet</strong> <strong> Isaiah</strong> <em> , </em> probably containing that book alone. The reading of the Law had already taken place, and that from the Prophets was to begin (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+13:15">Acts 13:15</span>). The passage for the day was from Isaiah. But it cannot <em> be</em> proven that the order of Scripture lessons, appointed by the Rabbins, was in use at that time.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Found the place where it was written</strong>. When He unrolled the book, His eye fell, accidentally some would say, <em> providentially</em> we say, upon this passage. There is no reference to looking for an <em> appointed</em> or appropriate passage. All calculations as to the time of year, based on the reading of this part of Isaiah, are therefore excluded.
	</p>
