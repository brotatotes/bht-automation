<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:2">Luke 4:2</span>. <strong> During forty days, being tempted by the</strong> <strong> devil</strong>. ‘During forty days’ may be joined either to what precedes or what follows. The former seems preferable, though ‘being tempted’ indicates a continued trial, which culminated in the assaults detailed by Matthew and Luke. The temptation continued <em> during </em> forty days (so <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:13">Mark 1:13</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> He did eat</strong> <strong> nothing. </strong> Entire abstinence day and night (Matthew) is meant.</p>
