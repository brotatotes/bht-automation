<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:20">Luke 4:20</span>. <strong> And he closed the roll,</strong> or, ‘rolling up the roll.’ How much he read is not known; the usual lesson from the prophets is said to have comprised twenty-one verses.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> To the attendant</strong>, whose duty it would be to put the roll back in its place.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> And sat down,</strong> to explain what He had read, that being the usual position of those making such expositions. It was our Lord’s usual posture when teaching. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:1">Matthew 5:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:1">Mark 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:3">Mark 13:3</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> And the eyes of all in the synagogue,</strong> etc. The man brought up among them was about to address them for the first time; the report from other places had preceded this visit; the passage read was remarkable, and doubtless there was something in the appearance of our Lord, especially under these circumstances, which would command unusual attention.</p>
