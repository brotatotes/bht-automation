<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:29">Luke 4:29</span>. <strong> And they rose up,</strong> tumultuously from their seats in the synagogue.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Cast him forth.</strong> Forced Him out, expelled Him.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Led him</strong>. That He was in their custody is evident
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Unto the brow of the hill whereon their city was built.</strong> Nazareth still answers to this description. The precipice was probably that behind the Maronite church at the present head of the town, and not the so-called Mount of Precipitation, which lies two miles from Nazareth.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Throw him down</strong> <strong> head-long.</strong> Compare the Tarpeian rock at Rome, from which the Roman mob cast unpopular persons.
	</p>
