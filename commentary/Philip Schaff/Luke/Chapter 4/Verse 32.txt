<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:32">Luke 4:32</span>. <strong> At his teaching.</strong> Not simply at the manner, as in Nazareth.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> For his word was with authority</strong>. The same idea is expressed in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:28-29">Matthew 7:28-29</span>. The comparison with the astonishment in Nazareth suggests, that they felt more than the <em> tone</em> of authority; they must have felt the authority itself. He not only claimed power in His words, but exercised it with His words.
	</p>
