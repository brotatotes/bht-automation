<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:17">Luke 5:17</span>. <strong> On one of those days.</strong> Probably referring, but very indefinitely, to the preaching tour of chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:44">Luke 4:44</span>. Pharisees and teachers of the law. Peculiar to Luke; but the other Evangelists speak of the ‘scribes’ as objecting.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Out of every</strong> <strong> Village,</strong> etc. From all parts, not necessarily from each and every village.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Jerusalem.</strong> Probably they had come with hostile purpose, since on this occasion we first discover an indication of antagonism.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> And the power of the Lord was</strong> <strong> with him to heal.</strong> Some authorities read: ‘that he should heal them.’ ‘Lord’ refers to God, although Luke often applies the term to our Lord.</p>
