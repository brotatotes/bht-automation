<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:5">Luke 5:5</span>. <strong> Master.</strong> Not ‘teacher,’ but a title of respect, not involving a close personal relation.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> We toiled</strong>. Not ‘have toiled,’ for that implies that they had just stopped. Peter gives an account of the last night’s labor.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> All night. </strong> The usual time for fishing, comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:2">John 21:2</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> But</strong>, not ‘nevertheless.’
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> At thy word</strong>. On account of thy word. This involved faith, yet the proverbial superstition of fishermen may have entered here.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> I will let down the nets. </strong> He speaks as the director of the fishing party. The significance of this verse for ‘fishers of men’ is obvious.</p>
