<p>The choice of the Twelve (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:12-16">Luke 6:12-16</span>). Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:13-19">Mark 3:13-19</span>; Matthew gives the list of Apostles in the account of their being sent forth (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:0">Matthew 10:0</span>; comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:7">Mark 6:7</span> ff.; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:1-6">Luke 9:1-6</span>). <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:17-19">Luke 6:17-19</span> describe the multitudes to whom a discourse (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:20-49">Luke 6:20-49</span>) was delivered, which seems to be identical with the sermon on the mount, see the Chapter Comments on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:0">Matthew 5:0</span>. Accepting, but without insisting upon, the identity of the discourses, we find Luke much fuller than Matthew in detailing the circumstances, but less full and exact in the report of the discourse.</p>
