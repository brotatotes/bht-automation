<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:46-49">Luke 6:46-49</span>. This close is the same as in Matthew. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:45">Luke 6:45</span> here answers to <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:21-23">Matthew 7:21-23</span>, omitting the allusion to the last day, and taking the form of a direct exhortation.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Digged and went deep</strong> (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:48">Luke 6:48</span>), <em> i.e.</em> digged again and again, until he reached the proper foundation.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Because it had been well builded.</strong> <strong><em> </em></strong> This reading, now generally accepted by scholars, complements the expression of Matthew: ‘founded upon the rock.’ Yet even here the main reference is to the foundation. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:49">Luke 6:49</span> here is even more graphic than the parallel passage.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> On the earth without a foundation</strong>, is = ‘on the sand.’ Off the true Rock there is no foundation, all is sand.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Straightway</strong> belongs to all that follows.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> It fell in</strong>, in a heap.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> The ruin</strong>, breach, the result of ‘the fall’ (Matthew). See on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:24-27">Matthew 7:24-27</span>.
	</p>
