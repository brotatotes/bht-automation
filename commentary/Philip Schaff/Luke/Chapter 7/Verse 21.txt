<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:21">Luke 7:21</span>. <strong> In that hour,</strong> etc. This is implied in the answer given by Matthew (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:4-5">Luke 7:4-5</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Diseases and plagues </strong> (Greek, ‘scourges’), <strong> and of evil spirits.</strong> Luke, the physician, distinguishes the possessed from the diseased.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"> <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:22-28">Luke 7:22-28</span> are almost word for word the same as <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:4-11">Matthew 11:4-11</span>. In <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:28">Luke 7:28</span> the word ‘prophet’ is to be omitted.</p>
