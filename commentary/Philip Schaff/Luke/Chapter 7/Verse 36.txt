<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:36">Luke 7:36</span>. <strong> One of the Pharisees</strong>. <strong><em> </em></strong> ‘Simon’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:40">Luke 7:40</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> That he would eat with him.</strong> There is no evidence of an improper motive. With all his scruples, the Pharisee shows no hostility. Pride may indeed have entered. Our Lord, who came ‘eating and drinking’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:34">Luke 7:34</span>), accepted the invitation.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Sat down to meat.</strong> As always, ‘reclined at table,’ the head toward the table, the body supported by the left arm and the feet turned outward. The sandals were usually removed before eating.</p>
