<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:45">Luke 8:45</span>. <strong><em> </em></strong> <strong> And when all denied</strong>. It is not certain that the woman did so; she may have hidden herself in the crowd.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Peter,</strong> etc. Peculiar to Luke. The question of our Lord was to draw out the woman’s faith. Mark’s account implies that He knew who had done it
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> The multitudes press thee,</strong> etc. ‘Press’ and ‘crush’ are strong terms. Some find here ‘a solemn warning to all who crowd on Christ,’ a rebuke to familiarity in hymns, etc. Peter in those days might have rebuked the crowd; our. Lord did not. The touch of faith and the accidental touch differ: no virtue flows out in the case of the latter. The cure was not magical.</p>
