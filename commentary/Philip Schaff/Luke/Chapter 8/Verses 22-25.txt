<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:22-25">Luke 8:22-25</span>. The Storm on the Lake. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:23-27">Matthew 8:23-27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+4:35-41">Mark 4:35-41</span>. Before starting, the incidents mentioned in chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:57-62">Luke 9:57-62</span> probably occurred (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:18-22">Matthew 8:18-22</span>). Luke’s account is brief, agreeing more closely with that of Mark.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> On one of the days</strong> (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:22">Luke 8:22</span>). The indefiniteness indicates that Luke had not consulted Mark’s Gospel.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> A boat</strong> Mark: ‘the boat.’ <strong> </strong> <em> i.e.</em> <strong><em> ,</em></strong> <strong> </strong> the one from which He had been teaching.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Came down</strong> (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:23">Luke 8:23</span>). Either from the sky, or from the hill-sides, since the sudden storms would roll down the valleys and burst upon the lake.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> They were filling</strong>, <strong> </strong> <em> i.e.</em> <strong> ,</strong> <strong><em> </em></strong> the boat was becoming full. The original brings out the sudden coming down of the storm, and then the gradual effect, filling the boat and putting them in danger.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Being awakened</strong>, or ‘awaking.’ So Mark; in Matthew it is simply ‘arising.’ Luke (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:25">Luke 8:25</span>) agrees with Mark, in putting the rebuke of the disciples AFTER the rebuke of the elements. Matthew reverses the order, but the former is probably more exact.</p>
