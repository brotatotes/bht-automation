<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:52">Luke 9:52</span>. <strong> Messengers.</strong> Supposed, but without reason, to have been the two sons of Zebedee.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Samaritans.</strong> The direct route towards Jerusalem from Galilee lay through Samaria. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:5">Matthew 10:5</span>; and <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:9">John 4:9</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"> To make ready for him. To provide food and shelter for Him and the large party accompanying Him. Yet they probably also announced His coming as the Messiah; since in Samaria this was not concealed (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:26">John 4:26</span>) as in Judea and Galilee.</p>
