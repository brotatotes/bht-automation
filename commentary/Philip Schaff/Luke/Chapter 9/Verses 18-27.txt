<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:18-27">Luke 9:18-27</span>. THE CONFESSION OF PETER <strong> , </strong> etc. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:13-28">Matthew 16:13-28</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:27-38">Mark 8:27-38</span>. This account agrees closely with the others, although briefer.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> As he was praying alone</strong> (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:18">Luke 9:18</span>). Peculiar to Luke. The prayer was a preparation for the revelation. The disciples joined Him, and ‘in the way’ (Mark) the conversation took place.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Unto all </strong> (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:23">Luke 9:23</span>). See <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:34">Mark 9:34</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> When he cometh</strong>, etc. (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:26">Luke 9:26</span>). Luke’s account is fullest in this clause. Meyer: ‘The glory is threefold: ( 1 .) <em> His own,</em> which He has of and for Himself as the exalted Messiah; ( 2 .) <em> the glory of God</em>, which accompanies Him as coming down from God’s throne; ( 3 .) <em> the glory of the angels</em>, who surround Him with their brightness.’</p>
