<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:46-50">Luke 9:46-50</span>. THE DISCIPLES REBUKED <strong> </strong> for their emulation and exclusiveness. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:1-5">Matthew 18:1-5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:33-40">Mark 9:33-40</span>; especially the latter. In the briefer narrative of Luke there is nothing at variance with the other accounts.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"> <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:46">Luke 9:46</span> declares the fact of a dispute, and <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:47">Luke 9:47</span> assumes that it was not spoken out before our Lord, but perceived by Him and brought to judgment. Luke notes the perception of their thought; Mark, the way in which the matter was brought up by our Lord; Matthew, their submission of the question to His decision.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> He that is not against you is for you </strong> (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:50">Luke 9:50</span>). This reading is to be accepted, and it presents substantially the same thought as that of the E. V. (and of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:40">Mark 9:40</span>). The disciples (‘you’) represent Christ and His people (‘us’). On the connection of thought in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:49-50">Luke 9:49-50</span>, see notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:38">Mark 9:38</span>.
	</p>
