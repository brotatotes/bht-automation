<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:26">Mark 1:26</span>. <strong> Had torn him</strong> <em> .</em> A paroxysm attended the dispossession (comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:26">Mark 9:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:42">Luke 9:42</span>); not a natural convulsion, but the malicious act of the demon. <em> Cried with a loud voice.</em> The act of the demon, not a cry of pain from the demoniac. Luke (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+4:35">Luke 4:35</span>) adds that the demon ‘hurt him not.’ The graphic and minute description forbids the view that this was a cure of epilepsy.
