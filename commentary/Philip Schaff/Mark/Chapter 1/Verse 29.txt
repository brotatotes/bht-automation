<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:29">Mark 1:29</span>. <strong> And straightway.</strong> On the ‘sabbath day’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:21">Mark 1:21</span>) after the occurrence in the synagogue.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> The house of Simon and Andrew.</strong> Now living in Capernaum (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:14">Matthew 8:14</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> With James and John.</strong> Mark alone mentions these. The particularity favors the theory that Peter had told Mark of it. The twelve were not yet chosen; though these four had been called to follow Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:17">Mark 1:17</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:20">Mark 1:20</span>).
	</p>
