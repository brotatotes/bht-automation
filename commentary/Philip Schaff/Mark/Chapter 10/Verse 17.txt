<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:17">Mark 10:17</span>. <strong> On</strong> (lit., ‘into’) <strong> the way</strong>. On His journey to Jerusalem, as He finally left Perea.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> There ran one</strong>, etc. Peculiar to Mark. This eagerness and respect was the more remarkable, since the man was a ‘ruler’ (Luke), and ‘very rich.’ Still the enthusiasm was also that of youth (Matthew: ‘the young man’).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Good Master, what shell I do?</strong> Matthew: ‘Master, what good thing?’ Both ideas were no doubt included in the original question, but in Matthew’s narrative the one point (‘good thing’) is taken up, in Mark and Luke the other (‘good master’). Both what was good, and who was good, had been misapprehended by the questioner.
	</p>
