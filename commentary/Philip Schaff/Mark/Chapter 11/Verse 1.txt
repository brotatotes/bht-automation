<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:1">Mark 11:1</span>. <strong> And he began to speak unto them in parables.</strong> A series of parables was spoken. Matthew records three; Mark and Luke preserve the principal one only. Comp. the emphatic language of Luke (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+20:9">Luke 20:9</span>): ‘this parable;’ and the words: ‘Hear another parable’(<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:33">Matthew 21:33</span>). All three accounts show that the parable was spoken in the presence of the people, but directly to the parties who had assailed Him (‘to them’), and ‘against them’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:12">Mark 11:12</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> A pit for the wine-press</strong>. A verbal variation from Matthew’s account. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:33">Matthew 21:33</span>.
	</p>
