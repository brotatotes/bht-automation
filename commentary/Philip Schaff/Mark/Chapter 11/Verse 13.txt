<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:13">Mark 11:13</span>. Afar off, or, ‘from afar.’ Mark presents the appearance of the tree in the distance: having leaves.
	</p>
<p><strong> If haply.</strong> Because it had leaves. This scarcely implies doubt in <em> His</em> mind, since the design was to teach the Apostles a very important lesson.
	</p>
<p><strong> For it was not the season of figs.</strong> The full season had not come, yet the leaves gave promise of fruit. The failure was then in the <em> barrenness</em> of the tree, a fit symbol of the pretentious hypocrisy of the Jewish hierarchy. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:19">Matthew 21:19</span>.
	</p>
