<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:4">Mark 11:4</span>. <strong> Found the colt.</strong> Mark is more detailed here: perhaps Peter was one of those sent (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:8">Luke 22:8</span>), where Peter and John are the two sent into the city.
	</p>
<p><strong> At the door without.</strong> Probably the door of the owner’s house.
	</p>
<p><strong> In the open street</strong>, or, ‘lane.’ The E. V. following the Latin Vulgate, paraphrases: ‘in a place where two ways meet.’ The phrase refers first to a way round, <em> i.e.,</em> round a block of houses, then to the street of a town (usually winding in the East).
	</p>
