<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:23-24">Mark 11:23-24</span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:21-22">Matthew 21:21-22</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> This mountain</strong>. Probably pointing to Mount Moriah, where stood the temple, the centre of the Jewish worship and the bulwark of the hypocritical hierarchy.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> What he saith cometh to pass.</strong> The present tense of <em> certainty</em>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Therefore</strong> <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:24">Mark 11:24</span> connects the promise with the <em> faith of miracles</em> (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:23">Mark 11:23</span>), and hence the <em> primary</em> application is to the Twelve.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> All things</strong>. ‘All’ is emphatic.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Pray and ask for</strong>. The correct reading is more striking.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Believe that ye</strong> <strong> have received.</strong> The original implies, that when you asked <em> you received</em>, God at once granted your request, so that the answer comes before the fulfilment, which is spoken of as future: <strong> ye shall have them</strong>, lit., ‘it shall be to you.’
	</p>
