<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+12:33">Mark 12:33</span>. <strong> With all the understanding. </strong> The scribe substitutes ‘understanding’ for ‘mind,’ which seems to express the same thought less abstractly. Mark preserves the answer in full.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Is</strong> <strong> much more than</strong>. Better, ‘more acceptable to God, and more useful to the worshipper.’
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> All whole burnt offerings and sacrifices.</strong> ‘Burnt offerings,’ <em> i.e.</em>, those commanded in the law. Such things took up the whole attention of legalists. It was a bold saying in those times and in that place. Christ’s atoning sacrifice is the centre of the gospel, but he who has a correct theory on this subject, without being led to the love here spoken of, is but a Pharisee at heart, below the standard of this man.
	</p>
