<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:24">Mark 13:24</span>, ff. From this point, the reference is to the second coming of Christ, the fulfilment of ‘these things all’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:4">Mark 13:4</span>), in the widest sense. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:29">Matthew 24:29</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> But</strong>. Here almost equivalent to ‘nevertheless;’ although I have foretold you all things, yet the subsequent tribulations may still astonish you.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> After that tribulation.</strong> The length of the interval is not definitely indicated. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:29">Matthew 24:29</span>, and <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:24">Luke 21:24</span>.On the rest of the verse and <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:25">Mark 13:25</span> see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:29-30">Matthew 24:29-30</span>, where a number of details are added. Comp, also, <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:25-26">Luke 21:25-26</span>, where the language is quite <em> </em> different.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> And the stars shall be falling</strong>. This vivid form is peculiar to Mark.
	</p>
