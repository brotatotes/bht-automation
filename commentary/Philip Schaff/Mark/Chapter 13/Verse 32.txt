<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:32">Mark 13:32</span>. <strong> Neither the Son.</strong> Here distinguished from ‘angels,’ as above them, since there is a climax, ‘angels,’ ‘the Son,’ ‘the Father.’ The verse is to be taken in its plain sense (see on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:36">Matthew 24:36</span>) as part of the mystery of Christ’s humiliation, a self-limitation, a self-emptying of the God-man.
	</p>
