<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:14-20">Mark 13:14-20</span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:15-22">Matthew 24:15-22</span>. Here, as there, the prominent reference is to the destruction of Jerusalem, answering the question of the disciples more directly than what precedes. ‘Spoken of by Daniel the prophet,’ is to be omitted; probably inserted from Matthew.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Where it ought not</strong> (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:14">Mark 13:14</span>) is less definite than ‘in the holy place’ (Matthew). ‘Your flight’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:18">Mark 13:18</span>) was probably inserted to conform with Matthew.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> The like</strong> (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:19">Mark 13:19</span>). Peculiar to Mark, who gives a peculiarly solemn form of this prediction, in accordance with his style.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Whom he chose </strong> (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:20">Mark 13:20</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Did he shorten the days. </strong> In this vivid way, the choice of believers, and the shortening of the days are spoken of as <em> past</em>, both being parts of God’s purpose, which will be fulfilled.
	</p>
