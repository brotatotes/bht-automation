<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:30">Mark 14:30</span>. <strong> That thou</strong>, emphatic, ‘even thou.’
	</p>
<p>The detailed form of this saying, together with Mark’s relation to Peter, suggests that this is the exact form in which it was uttered. Peter afterwards (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:72">Mark 14:72</span>), and doubtless always called to mind this saying.
	</p>
<p><strong> Today</strong>. During the twenty-four hours, beginning at sundown.
	</p>
<p><strong> This night.</strong> The prediction becomes more specific.
	</p>
<p><strong> Before</strong> <strong> the cock crow twice.</strong> Before the usual time of the second cock-crowing, about three o’clock in the morning. This was usually called ‘cock-crowing’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:35">Mark 13:35</span>).
	</p>
