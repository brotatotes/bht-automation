<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:61">Mark 14:61</span>. <strong> The high-priest asked him.</strong> Putting Him on oath, according to <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:63">Matthew 26:63</span>
</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> The Son of the Blessed,</strong> <em> i.e.,</em> of God, since the Rabbis used a word of this meaning as the ordinary name for God. It occurs only here in the New Testament. The action of the high-priest indicates that this implied a distinct question: Do you claim, in claiming to be the Messiah (‘the Christ’), to be also ‘the Son of God.’
	</p>
