<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:10-11">Mark 14:10-11</span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:14-16">Matthew 26:14-16</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:3-6">Luke 22:3-6</span>. Both accounts are fuller.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> He that was one of the twelve,</strong> lit., ‘ <em> the</em> one of the Twelve,’ pointing to a well-known one.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> That</strong> (in order that) <em> </em> <strong> he might deliver him up unto them. </strong> The definite purpose of Judas is brought out more fully here than by Matthew. The delivery to them involved all the rest of our Lord’s sufferings.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> When they heard it.</strong> Hearing the proposal. This is peculiar to Mark.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Promised.</strong> The money was probably not paid until the night of the betrayal.
	</p>
