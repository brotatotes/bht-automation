Luk 22:19-20 ; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:23-25">1 Corinthians 11:23-25</span>; also <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:51">John 6:51</span> ff. The peculiarities of Mark’s account, which resembles closely that of Matthew, are merely the omission of ‘eat’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:22">Mark 14:22</span>); the insertion of the clause: <strong> And they all drank of it</strong> (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:23">Mark 14:23</span>); the omission of the words: ‘for the remission of sins,’ after <strong> for many</strong> (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:24">Mark 14:24</span>), and the slightly briefer form of the thought in ver. On the incidents which occurred before the departure to the Mount of Olives, and the probability that Peter’s denial was twice foretold, see notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:30-46">Matthew 26:30-46</span>.
