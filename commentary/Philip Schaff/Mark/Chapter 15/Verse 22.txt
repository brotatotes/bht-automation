<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:22">Mark 15:22</span>. <strong> To the place Golgotha. </strong> More correctly perhaps: place of Golgotha, answering to <strong> Place of a Skull,</strong> since Golgotha means ‘skull,’ and Luke (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:33">Luke 23:33</span>) calls the place simply ‘skull.’ This is an additional reason for supposing that the name was owing to the conical shape of the ground. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:33">Matthew 27:33</span>.
	</p>
