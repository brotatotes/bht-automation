<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+16:14">Mark 16:14</span>. <strong> Afterward.</strong> ‘Later’ not ‘last,’ though the word may bear such a meaning. This was the last manifestation of that day, and is fully detailed by Luke (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:36">Luke 24:36</span>, etc.) and John (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:19-23">John 20:19-23</span>). Mark joins with it the last revelation of our Lord on earth. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+16:15">Mark 16:15</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Sat at meat.</strong> In strict accordance with <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:41-43">Luke 24:41-43</span>, though evidently independently written.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Upbraided them with their unbelief. </strong> He instructed, as well as upbraided them; but the matter is here described from one point of view. This ‘unbelief’ was in the fact of His resurrection.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Hardness of heart.</strong> They seem to have remained that day in an intellectual and moral stupor.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Because,</strong> etc. The specific reproach was that in the face of sufficient evidence they doubted a glorious fact, which He, whom they loved, had predicted again and again.
	</p>
