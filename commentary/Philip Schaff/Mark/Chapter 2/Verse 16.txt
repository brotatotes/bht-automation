<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:16">Mark 2:16</span>. See on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:11">Matthew 9:11</span>. Both Mark and Luke, in different forms, say that these <strong> scribes were of the Pharisees</strong>, <em> i.e.,</em> of that party.
	</p>
<p><strong> When they saw that he eateth.</strong> It is probable that they came, not as guests, but toward the close of the feast, so that they may or may not have actually witnessed this as lookers on. Luke (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:30">Luke 5:30</span>) represents the objection as made against the disciples. Their criticism probably included both the master and His followers. The correct form: He eateth ……sinners! points to an exclamation of surprise, which may have preceded the hostile question. On <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:17">Mark 2:17</span>, see on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:12">Matthew 9:12</span>.
	</p>
