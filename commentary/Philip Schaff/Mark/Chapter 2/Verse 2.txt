<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:2">Mark 2:2</span>. The description of Mark is here minute, but is paraphrased in the common version.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Insomuch that not even the parts about</strong> (or ‘towards’) <strong> the door</strong> (much less the house), <strong> could any longer hold them</strong> <em> .</em> This suggests a constantly increasing crowd, at length filling even the porch leading from the interior court <strong> to the door</strong>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> He was</strong> <strong> </strong> <strong> speaking the word</strong>, <em> i.e., </em> ‘was teaching’ (Luke). He was doing this when this incident occurred. From Luke’s account we infer that He had already healed others on this occasion.
	</p>
