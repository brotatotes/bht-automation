<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:28">Mark 2:28</span>. <strong> So that the Son of man it Lord also of the Sabbath. </strong> The connection here differs from that of the other accounts, and the idea is more full. Since the Sabbath was made for the benefit of man, it follows that <em> the Son of Man</em> (the Messiah, but especially in His character as the Head and Representative of humanity) is Lord (Sovereign over all that belongs to the interest of man and hence) <em> also of the Sabbath; i.e.,</em> not for its abolition, but for its true fulfilment; comp, <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:17">Matthew 5:17</span>. See further on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:8">Matthew 12:8</span>.
	</p>
