<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:1">Mark 3:1</span>. <strong> He entered again.</strong> On the next Sabbath (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:6">Luke 6:6</span>). ‘Again’ may refer to <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:21">Mark 1:21</span>. In that case the place was Capernaum.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> The synagogue</strong> <em> .</em> It is doubtful whether we should render: ‘the ‘or ‘a synagogue.’ Matthew says definitely ‘their synagogue,’ i.e. <em> ,</em> that of His opponents. Luke adds that ‘He taught there.’
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Withered.</strong> This word suggests disease or accident as the cause. It was the ‘right hand’ (Luke).
	</p>
