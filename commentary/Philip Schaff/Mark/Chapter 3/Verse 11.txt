<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:11">Mark 3:11</span>. <strong> And unclean spirits</strong>. The demon identified himself with the person, since the confession was undoubtedly that of the evil spirit.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Whenever they saw him.</strong> This was the usual effect
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Fell down before him and cried.</strong> The possessed man fell down, and his voice uttered the cry; but both acts are attributed to the evil spirit; hence the intimate possession.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> The son of God.</strong> <em> </em> Comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:24">Mark 1:24</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:34">Mark 1:34</span>.
	</p>
