<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:13">Mark 3:13</span>. <strong> Into the mountain</strong> <em> .</em> Probably the mount of Beatitudes (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:1">Matthew 5:1</span>); or possibly the hill country in contrast with the seashore. Our Lord spent the previous night in prayer, choosing the Apostles in the morning (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:12-13">Luke 6:12-13</span>).
	</p>
<p><strong> Whom he himself would.</strong> The freedom of choice is made prominent. He gathered a larger number of disciples about Him and chose out twelve (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:13">Luke 6:13</span>). This verse probably refers to the latter act. Strictly speaking, this was rather the formal announcement of His choice, for most of them (seven at least, had been specially called before this time.
	</p>
<p><strong> They went,</strong> lit., ‘went away’ (i.e., from the others) <em> unto him.
	</em></p>
