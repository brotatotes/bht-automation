<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:22">Mark 3:22</span>. <em> </em> <strong> The scribes that came down from Jerusalem.</strong> Mark thus defines the parties, while Matthew (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:23">Matthew 12:23</span>) states the occasion of the accusation. The purpose of their coming was doubtless to entrap and oppose Him, and hence the place was probably Capernaum, since they would go to His headquarters.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> He hath Beelzebub.</strong> See on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:25">Matthew 10:25</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:24">Matthew 12:24</span>. Mark, however, both here and in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:20">Mark 3:20</span>, states with greatest definiteness that they charged Him, not only with exercising Satanic power, but with being Himself possessed by an evil spirit.
	</p>
