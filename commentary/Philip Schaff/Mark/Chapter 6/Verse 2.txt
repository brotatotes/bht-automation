<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:2">Mark 6:2</span>. <strong> The sabbath-day.</strong> Mark, here as so often, is more specific than Matthew.
	</p>
<p><strong> Many,</strong> according to many ancient authorities, ‘the many,’ the multitude of this city.
	</p>
<p><strong> What is the wisdom given unto this man? </strong> This acknowledgement of His wisdom conveys a sneer. More graphic than Matthew’s statement.
	</p>
<p><strong> And such mighty works</strong> (or, ‘powers’) <strong> wrought by his hands.</strong> We may supply either ‘whence are,’ or ‘what are.’ The latter seems to give the sense of the correct reading. It is plain, from <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:5">Mark 6:5</span>, that they referred to miracles in other places.
	</p>
