<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:21">Mark 6:21</span>. <strong> A convenient day,</strong> <em> i.e.,</em> for the purpose which Herodias cherished, not for Herod’s feast, which took place at the fixed time.
	</p>
<p><strong> When Herod on his birthday.</strong> Herodias planned the schemes beforehand.
	</p>
<p><strong> Lords,</strong> etc. Political servants and military officials, then leading men of the land. ‘His’ belongs to the first class only. Strictly speaking, Herod had no <strong> chief captains </strong> (chiliarchs) of his own.
	</p>
