<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:34">Mark 6:34</span>. <strong> And he came forth. </strong> Either disembarked from the boat, or, more probably, came out from His retirement. Upon landing they went up some hill or cliff and from that point saw the great crowd. (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:3">John 6:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:5">John 6:5</span>). It is not certain that the needed rest was obtained.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Had compassion,</strong> etc. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:36">Matthew 9:36</span>, which tells of the same feelings on an earlier, but similar occasion.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> He began to teach them many things</strong>. This shows what He deemed their greatest need to be, although at the same time ‘He healed their sick’ (Matthew); comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:11">Luke 9:11</span>. ‘Began ‘may mean, either that He began at once, or that He only began, the day being already far spent. The former is more probable.
	</p>
