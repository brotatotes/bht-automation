<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:6">Mark 6:6</span>. He marvelled because of their unbelief. To be taken literally. On another occasion our Lord ‘marvelled’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:10">Matthew 8:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:9">Luke 7:9</span>) at the great faith of a heathen centurion. Both instances indicate the great importance of faith.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Went round about. </strong> The unbelief of Nazareth did not stop our Lord’s activity. This circuit was closely connected with the sending forth of the Twelve (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:7">Mark 6:7</span>); hence it seems to be identical with that mentioned in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:35">Matthew 9:35</span>, if we refer the latter to a distinct journey. It would be the <em> third circuit</em> through Galilee, which began with this rejection at Nazareth and continued until the return of the Apostles, when they all withdrew (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:30">Mark 6:30</span>).
	</p>
