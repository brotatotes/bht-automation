<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:2">Mark 7:2</span>. <strong> When they saw,</strong> <em> i.e.,</em> on some very recent occasion.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> That some of his disciples ate their bread.</strong> ‘This incident naturally brings to view the constant and intrusive <em> surveillance</em> to which our Lord and His disciples were subjected’ (J. A. Alexander).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Defiled,</strong> or ‘common.’ Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+10:14-15">Acts 10:14-15</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> That is unwashen hands.</strong> This explanation shows that the Gospel was written for Gentile readers. The clause: ‘they found fault,’ is to be omitted, the construction is broken by the explanation of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:3-4">Mark 7:3-4</span>.
	</p>
