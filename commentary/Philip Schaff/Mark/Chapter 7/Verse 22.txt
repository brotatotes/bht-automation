<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:22">Mark 7:22</span>. <strong> Covetings, </strong> lit., ‘covetousnesses,’ grasping, greedy desires, with the attending peculiarities.
	</p>
<p><strong> Wickednesses.</strong> ‘Malignities;’ evil dispositions.
	</p>
<p><strong> Deceit.</strong> Fraud, as distinguished from actual theft.
	</p>
<p><strong> Lasciviousness.</strong> Sensual excess.
	</p>
<p><strong> An evil eye. </strong> A figure for <em> envy.
	</em></p>
<p><strong> Blasphemy.</strong> Proud and spiteful anger, manifesting itself in abusive language against God.
	</p>
<p><strong> Pride.</strong> Self exaltation, leading to arrogance towards God and man.
	</p>
<p><strong> Foolishness.</strong> Senselessness, unreasoning folly, in thought, as well as in the words and acts which result. A fearful catalogue, true to nature still. How well our Lord, the purest of the pure, knew the depths of iniquity from which He would save sinful men!
	</p>
