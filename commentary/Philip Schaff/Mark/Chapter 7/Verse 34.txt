<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:34">Mark 7:34</span>. <strong> And looking up to heaven. </strong> In <em> prayer</em>, perhaps to show His connection with God the Father in heaven, over against the magical influences which may have been assumed by the people of that district; perhaps to affect the deaf and dumb man, who could perceive this.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> He sighed.</strong> In sympathy, always felt, but here expressed; perhaps also in distress at the ignorance and superstition He would overcome.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Ephphatha.</strong> The precise word used, translated into Greek by Mark, meaning <strong> be thou opened</strong> (thoroughly). It is closely related to the Hebrew word used in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="isa+35:5">Isaiah 35:5</span>. The command was addressed to the man, as shut up from the world by the defect of these two senses.
	</p>
