<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:8">Mark 7:8</span>. <strong> Yet let go the commandment of God, </strong> etc. This verse is peculiar to Mark, ‘setting forth their <em> depreciating</em> of God’s command in comparison with human tradition, before their absolute <em> violation</em> of that command in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:10-11">Mark 7:10-11</span>.’ (Alford.)
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.18em;"><strong> Tradition of men.</strong> ‘Men ‘as in contrast to ‘God,’ implying that the ‘elders’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:5">Mark 7:5</span>) had no other than human authority. The rest of the verse is to be omitted, according to the best authorities.
	</p>
