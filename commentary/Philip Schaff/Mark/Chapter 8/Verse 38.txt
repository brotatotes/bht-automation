<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:38">Mark 8:38</span>. <strong> Shall be ashamed of me, and my words.</strong> Disown me and reject my words. The two terms correspond with those in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:35">Mark 8:35</span>: ‘for my sake and the gospel’s.’ There is a hint of the same thought in Matthew’s account (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:27">Matthew 16:27</span>), and something analogous is found in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:33">Matthew 10:33</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> In this adulterous and sinful generation. </strong> Com. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:39">Matthew 12:39</span>. These words, peculiar to Mark in this connection, suggest that being ashamed of Christ is the result of paying attention to the verdict of such a generation.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> The son of man </strong> (now lowly, despised and rejected of men) <strong> also be ashamed</strong> (disown and reject). Cometh. At the Second Advent
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> In the glory of his Father. </strong> See <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:27">Matthew 16:27</span>. Luke is fuller: ‘In His own glory, and the Father’s, and of the holy angels.’
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Holy angels.</strong> Matthew: ‘His angels.’
	</p>
