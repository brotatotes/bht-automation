<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:34">Mark 9:34</span>. <strong> But they held their peace</strong>. In shame and contusion. The thought of their heart had been perceived (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:47">Luke 9:47</span>).
	</p>
<p><strong> Who was the greater</strong>. The dispute was occasioned by the preference given to Peter, James, and John, rather than by the promise to Peter (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:18-19">Matthew 16:18-19</span>). They probably thought that their rank <em> now</em> would determine their rank in the future kingdom. The question of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:1">Matthew 18:1</span>, may have been put after the saying of the next verse and before the child was brought (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:36">Mark 9:36</span>). In any case it was more humble than the dispute had been.
	</p>
