<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+1:11">Matthew 1:11</span>. <strong> Josiah. </strong> The next king was Jehoia-kim (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ki+24:6">2 Kings 24:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ch+26:8">2 Chronicles 26:8</span>). He was forcibly placed on the throne by the king of Egypt, hence unworthy of mention.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> The removal. </strong> Spoken of indefinitely, as it extended over a <strong> </strong> considerable period of time during three successive reigns. The word used does not necessarily imply a forcible removal, the Jews being accustomed to speak of the Captivity in this mild way. The course is downward through these royal generations.</p>
