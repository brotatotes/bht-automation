<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+1:13">Matthew 1:13</span>. <strong> Abiud. </strong> This name is not mentioned among the sons of Zerubbabel in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1ch+3:19-20">1 Chronicles 3:19-20</span>. He is supposed by some to be identical with <em> Hananiah</em> (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1ch+3:19">1 Chronicles 3:19</span>); by others with <em> Hodaiah</em> (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1ch+3:24">1 Chronicles 3:24</span>), one of his descendants, who is further supposed to be the Judah of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+3:26">Luke 3:26</span>; all this, however, is conjecture. The downward course reaches its lowest point in the humble carpenter of Nazareth. The promised Saviour was to be ‘a root out of a dry ground’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="isa+53:2">Isaiah 53:2</span>).</p>
