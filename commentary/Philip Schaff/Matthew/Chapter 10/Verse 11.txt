<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:11">Matthew 10:11</span>. <strong> And into whatsoever city or town, </strong> etc. Left to choose their own precise route, their work involved the exercise of judgment and prudence, it was not a mere mechanical routine.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Who in it </strong> (in the city or town) <strong> is worthy. </strong> This refers either to hospitable or to pious character, probably to both, since they are often united. Those who bore such a reputation might indeed be unworthy (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:13">Matthew 10:13</span>), but pious people easily find each other out. The next clause assumes that they had found the right place.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> There abide till ye depart. </strong> In this fixed abode they were not <strong> </strong> to give unnecessary trouble (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+10:7">Luke 10:7</span>). They were not social visitors but messengers of the gospel. The time of the ministry may be wasted by social exactions.
	</p>
