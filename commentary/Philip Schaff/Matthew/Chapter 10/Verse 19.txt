<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:19">Matthew 10:19</span>. <strong> But. </strong> Here the simplicity of the dove is to be exercised.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Be not anxious, </strong> <em> i.e.,</em> do not be unduly concerned; comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:34">Matthew 6:34</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> How or what, </strong> neither about the <em> form</em> nor the <em> substance</em>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> For it shall be given you. </strong> A promise of special inspiration for particular emergencies, <strong> in that hour; </strong> hence not an encouragement to laziness regarding pulpit preparation. ‘How’ comes first; studied eloquence checks the natural utterances of the heart, which are always the best defence: ‘when the orator wholly disappears, the True Orator will appear.’ The promise is: <strong> what ye shall speak </strong> shall be given.
	</p>
