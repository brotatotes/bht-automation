<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:20">Matthew 10:20</span>. <strong> It is not ye, </strong> etc. Inspiration for their defence is an indirect proof of the inspiration of the apostolic writings, since the purpose of both is ‘testimony’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:18">Matthew 10:18</span>), and writing was a permanent, and hence the most important, testimony. The inspiration affects both <em> what</em> is said and <em> how</em> it is said. The human form is influenced by the Divine substance revealed.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Your Father. </strong> Never ‘our Father,’ except in the Lord’s Prayer, which He taught others to use. God is our Father in a different sense; Christ’s sonship differs from ours, and He calls God simply ‘Father’ or ‘My Father.’
	</p>
