<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:31">Matthew 10:31</span>. <strong> Fear ye not therefore. </strong> In <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:25">Matthew 10:25</span> the motive was drawn from the relation to Christ, here <strong> </strong> from the relation to God: <strong> ye are of more value, </strong> <em> i.e.</em>, in the sight of God, who is ‘your Father’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:29">Matthew 10:29</span>). ‘The humblest of God’s creatures have their value in His sight: how much more human beings. Especially Christians, but above all, the witnesses of Jesus.’
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"> The scope of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:25-31">Matthew 10:25-31</span> is: A right sense of our immortality consists in the feeling that we are perfectly safe in the keeping of our Father; let us then not fear men, but boldly and fully proclaim the truth we have from our Master who also suffered from men.
	</p>
