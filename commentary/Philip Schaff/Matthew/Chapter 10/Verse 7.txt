<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:7">Matthew 10:7</span>. <strong> And as ye go preach, </strong> proclaim, announce. The matter of their preaching was the approach of the <strong> kingdom of heaven </strong> (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:2">Matthew 3:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:17">Matthew 4:17</span>). Their mission was preparatory; the gospel tells of a kingdom already come. As yet <strong> </strong> they were not instructed to proclaim the King, but were sent rather to announce the kingdom (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:7">Matthew 10:7</span>), ‘to teach men its nature, and to prove it at hand by their miracles. If men had faith in the words of the Apostles, they would soon come to Jesus to be taught by Him.’ (Andrews.)
	</p>
