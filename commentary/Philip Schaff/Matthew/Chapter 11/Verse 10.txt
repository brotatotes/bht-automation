<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:10">Matthew 11:10</span>. <strong> It is written. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mal+3:1">Malachi 3:1</span></strong>. The last of the prophets had foretold of John. His office as forerunner of Christ made him greater than them all.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Behold I send my messenger before thy face</strong>; etc. The original prophecy is: ‘Behold I send my messenger before <em> my</em> face,’ etc. (The latter part of the verse contains a direct reference to the Messiah.) Here, and in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+1:2">Mark 1:2</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+7:27">Luke 7:27</span>, it is changed into a promise of God <em> to</em> Christ Our Lord on His own authority (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:9">Matthew 11:9</span>: ‘I say unto you’), applies the phrase,’ my messenger,’ to John, and the word ‘thy’ to Himself, thus appropriating a pronoun referring to God. Comp. His discourse on a previous occasion (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+5:17-47">John 5:17-47</span>), in which He refers to His relation to the Father, to John, and to the Old Testament prophets.
	</p>
