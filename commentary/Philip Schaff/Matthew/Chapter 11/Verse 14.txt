<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:14">Matthew 11:14</span>. <strong> And if ye are willing to receive it. </strong> The Jews expected that Elijah would rise from the dead, hence many would not receive it. The popular notions on the whole subject of prophecy were incorrect; for in the day of fulfilment our Lord thus prefaces an explanation.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> He is Elijah, </strong> etc. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mal+4:5">Malachi 4:5</span>, applied to John before his birth by the angel Gabriel (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:17">Luke 1:17</span>). Not the entire fulfilment of the prophecy, for John himself (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:21">John 1:21</span>) said he was not Elijah, and our Lord afterwards intimated that another coming of Elijah is to be expected (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:11">Matthew 17:11</span>).
	</p>
