<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:20">Matthew 11:20</span>. <strong> Then began he. </strong> Probably ‘pointing to a pause or change of manner of our Lord.’
	</p>
<p><strong> To upbraid. </strong> Often used of men in a bad sense, here, implying moral disapproval and righteous indignation.
	</p>
<p><strong> Wherein most of his mighty works were done. </strong> Probably only the smallest part of our Lord’s miracles are detailed by the Evangelists (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+21:25">John 21:25</span>). We have no account of any miracles in ‘Chorazin’ and ‘Bethsaida’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+11:21">Matthew 11:21</span>).
	</p>
<p><strong> Because they repented not. </strong> The object of the miracles was to lead to repentance.
	</p>
