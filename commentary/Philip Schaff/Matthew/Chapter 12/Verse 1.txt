<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:1">Matthew 12:1</span>. <strong> At that time, </strong> or ‘season.’ Here used indefinitely. See <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:1">Luke 6:1</span>, as to the date.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Through the grain fields. </strong> The grain was probably barley, which ripens in April in that region and is usually harvested in May.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> His disciples. </strong> Not the ‘Twelve’ exclusively, probably including most of them.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> And began to pluck ears of grain. </strong> Permitted by the Mosaic law (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="de+23:25">Deuteronomy 23:25</span>). The word ‘began’ hints that they were interrupted by the objection of the Pharisees.
	</p>
