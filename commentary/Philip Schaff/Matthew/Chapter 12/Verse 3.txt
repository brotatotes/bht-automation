<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:3">Matthew 12:3</span>. <strong> Have ye not read what David did. </strong> All three Evangelists record this main argument against the Pharisees. The case of David (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1sa+21:1-6">1 Samuel 21:1-6</span>) was peculiarly in point. The Pharisees insisted that their mode of observing the Sabbath was needful, if a man would be a patriotic Jew and acceptable to God, but a model of Jewish piety had, according to the Scriptures, violated the law as they construed it.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Hungry, </strong> as His disciples had been.
