<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:14">Matthew 13:14</span>. <strong> In them, </strong> lit., ‘to them,’ in the sense, ‘in their care’
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> It fulfilled. </strong> A stronger word than that commonly used; a complete fulfilment, which may or may not have been preceded by a partial one.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Isaiah </strong> (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="isa+6:9-10">Isaiah 6:9-10</span>). Quoted in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:40">John 12:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+28:26-27">Acts 28:26-27</span>; comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:8">Romans 11:8</span>; referred to by Mark and Luke, but not formally quoted.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> By hearing, </strong> etc. The sense of the original prophecy is given, but not its form. In Isaiah is a command; here a strong prediction, indicating that judgment is a result of what is done <em> by</em> man as well as what is done <em> to</em> man.
	</p>
