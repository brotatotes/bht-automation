<p>These three parables relate <strong> </strong> mainly to human effort in the development of the kingdom of heaven. The last one corresponds to the second, while the fifth and sixth form a pair; the transition of thought being easy and natural in every case. <em> </em> The Hidden Treasure (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:44">Matthew 13:44</span>), finding without seeking; The Pearl of great price (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:45-46">Matthew 13:45-46</span>), seeking and finding; in both cases, proper effort to appropriate the valuable possession; The Net cast into the Sea (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:47-50">Matthew 13:47-50</span>), the Church and her efforts, the mixed result and the final separation. Application in the form of a comparison (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:51-52">Matthew 13:51-52</span>).
