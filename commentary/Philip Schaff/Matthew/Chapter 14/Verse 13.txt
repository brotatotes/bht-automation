<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:13">Matthew 14:13</span>. <strong> Now when Jesus heard it.</strong> <em> </em> This was not the only cause of the retirement (see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:31">Mark 6:31</span>). The Twelve had returned and the multitudes gave Him and them no rest. Besides this gathering of multitudes would make Herod more suspicious.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Into a desert place apart. </strong> Not a ‘desert ‘in the modem sense, but a thinly inhabited district; in Gaulonitis near Bethsaida Julias, on the <em> eastern</em> shore of the lake of Tiberias (see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:10">Luke 9:10</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:1">John 6:1</span>), in the dominions of Philip the Tetrarch. Our Lord would avoid Herod as well as seek rest for His disciples.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> They followed him. </strong> Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:33">Mark 6:33</span>. The popularity of our Lord continued.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> By land. </strong> This is the usual meaning of the Greek phrase, which is literally rendered: ‘on foot.’
	</p>
