<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:2">Matthew 14:2</span>. <strong> This is John the Baptist</strong>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:7-9">Luke 9:7-9</span>. This does not imply a belief in the transmigration of souls, nor prove that Herod was a Sadducee (although some infer this from <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:15">Mark 8:15</span>); it is the perplexed and terrified utterance of a guilty conscience.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Therefore</strong>, etc. John had wrought no miracle (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:41">Matthew 10:41</span>), but Herod supposed that the rising from the dead had resulted in higher powers.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Powers</strong>, or ‘mighty works’ as in chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:54">Matthew 13:54</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:58">Matthew 13:58</span>. Herod’s desire to see our Lord was at best a patronizing condescension to the gospel.
	</p>
