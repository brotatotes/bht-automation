<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:28">Matthew 14:28</span>. <strong> And Peter answered. </strong> The silence of the other Evangelists is remarkable, but casts no doubt upon the truthfulness of Matthew’s account. The occurrence is strikingly in accordance with Peter’s impulsive character, ‘almost a rehearsal’ of the subsequent denial.
	</p>
<p><strong> If it be thou.</strong> <em> </em> Not the language of doubt Peter’s fault lay in the words: <strong> bid me</strong>, etc., which betray a desire to outdare the other disciples; comp, the boast: ‘Though all should be offended,’ etc. (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:33">Matthew 26:33</span>).
