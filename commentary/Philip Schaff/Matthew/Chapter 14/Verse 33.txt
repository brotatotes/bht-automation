<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:33">Matthew 14:33</span>. <strong> They that were in the boat</strong> <em> .</em> Probably manners and others exclusive of the disciples. The effect produced upon the latter is declared in strong terms, <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+6:51-52">Mark 6:51-52</span>.
	</p>
<p><strong> The Son of God</strong> <em> , </em> lit., son of God. Probably only a recognition of His Messiahship, but the miracle would exalt their notions respecting the Messiah. For the first time <em> men</em> owned our Lord as the Son of God. John the Baptist had done so by Divine commission (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:34">John 1:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+3:35-36">John 3:35-36</span>).
