<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Declare,</strong> <em> i.e.,</em> ‘expound’
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> The parable.</strong> <em> </em> That of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:11">Matthew 15:11</span> (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:17">Mark 7:17</span>). The declaration in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:11">Matthew 15:11</span>, was a ‘hard saying ‘to those who were born Jews, and hence Peter might have called it a ‘parable,’ especially as our Lord had so often taught the deeper truths in that form. Or the disciples, with their Jewish education, might have thought: this saying to which the Pharisees so much object is not to be taken literally, it must be a parable. The censure of the next verse favors this explanation.
	</p>
