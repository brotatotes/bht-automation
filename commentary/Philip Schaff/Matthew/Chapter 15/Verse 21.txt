<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:21">Matthew 15:21</span>. <strong> And Jesus withdrew.</strong> Partly in consequence of the hostility of the Pharisees; partly to seek retirement (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:24">Mark 7:24</span>); He designed also, to signify, through the incident which was to follow, the future admission of the Gentiles into His kingdom. </p>
<p><strong> Into the parts</strong>. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:24">Mark 7:24</span>: ‘borders.’ He may not have passed much beyond the frontier <em> .
	</em></p>
<p><strong> Tyre and Sidon</strong>. Phoenicia, here named from its chief cities, was north of upper Galilee, and inhabited by Gentiles. The Jewish world was closing against our Lord; the Gentile world was not yet open. He sought seclusion near the border line, but ‘He could not be hid’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:24">Mark 7:24</span>). The heathen mother found Him: she was a type of the longing, suffering Gentile world.
	</p>
