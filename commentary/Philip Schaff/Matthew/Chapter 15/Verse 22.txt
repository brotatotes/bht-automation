<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:22">Matthew 15:22</span>. <strong> A Canaanitish woman</strong> <em> .</em> Her race, not her country, is thus noted. Mark, ‘a Greek,’ <em> i.e.</em>, a heathen by religion, ‘a Syro-Phoenician by nation.’ The Phoenicians were the descendants of the remnant of the old Canaanites.
	</p>
<p><strong> Came out.</strong> Probably from a distance.
	</p>
<p><strong> Son of David.</strong> She knew and probably shared in the Messianic hopes of the Jews. At least she had heard of our Lord, and believed that He could help her. Her request; <em> Have mercy on me</em>, exhibits her faith, far more than the title she used.
	</p>
<p><strong> Grievously possessed with a demon</strong>, lit, ‘badly demonized.’ Such possessions were therefore not confined to the Jews.
	</p>
