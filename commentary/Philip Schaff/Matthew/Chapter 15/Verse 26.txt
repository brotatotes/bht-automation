<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:26">Matthew 15:26</span>. <strong> It is not meet.</strong> The reply is not harsh, nor is it a refusal (Mark: ‘Let the children first be fed’). It calls forth the woman’s faith, and convinces the disciples that it is ‘proper’ to bless this heathen woman.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> To take</strong> <em> </em> (lit., ‘to take away’) <strong> the children’s bread</strong>. All present understood this as referring to the blessings provided for the Jews.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> To the dogs,</strong> lit., ‘little dogs.’ A reference to the large savage dogs so common in the East, would be very contemptuous; household dogs are meant; a sense the woman skilfully used.
	</p>
