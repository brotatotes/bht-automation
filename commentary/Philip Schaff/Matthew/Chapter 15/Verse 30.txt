<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:30">Matthew 15:30</span>. <strong> Great multitudes.</strong> Even in this retired place He was not allowed to rest long. The crowds came <strong> having with them</strong> <em> ,</em> <em> i.e.,</em> <strong><em> </em></strong> bringing with them, a great variety of afflicted ones.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Dumb.</strong> Mark mentions one case in particular (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:32-35">Mark 7:32-35</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Maimed.</strong> The first mention of this class, i.e. <em> ,</em> those wounded or diseased in hand or foot; our word ‘maimed’ implies a loss of the member.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Cast them down.</strong> This may refer to the rudeness of these mountaineers, or to their haste, or to their confidence; probably the three explanations are to be combined.
	</p>
