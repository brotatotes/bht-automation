<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:39">Matthew 15:39</span>. <strong> Into the boat.</strong> Probably one awaiting Him.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Into the borders of Magadan</strong> <em> ,</em> according to the best authorities. (‘Magdalan’ is also found.) Mark: ‘Into the parts of Dalmanutha.’ This was probably a village not far from Magadan. Our Lord, pursued by the hostility of the Jews and seeking retirement, landed at an obscure locality between the two places. The site of Magdala (Magadan), now called <em> Madschel </em> (‘Migdol,’ <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="jos+19:38">Joshua 19:38</span>), is north of Tiberias and directly east of Cana, on the <em> western</em> shore of the lake, since the next voyage (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:5">Matthew 16:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:13">Mark 8:13</span>) was across the lake to the eastern side.
	</p>
