<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:4">Matthew 15:4</span>. <strong> For God said</strong> (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:10">Mark 7:10</span>), in the law of Moses. Our Lord assumes that God spoke through this law. The precepts cited are apt, since the Pharisees upheld tradition as delivered by the ‘fathers.’
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> He that revileth,</strong> etc. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ex+21:17">Exodus 21:17</span>. Our Lord quotes, not the promise in the Decalogue, but the penalty given elsewhere. ‘Revileth,’ lit., ‘speaketh evil of,’ comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:39">Mark 9:39</span>, which shows that ‘curseth’ is too strong a term.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Surely die</strong>. In the original Hebrew: ‘dying he shall die;’ in the original Greek of this passage: ‘let him end with death,’ both equivalent to: ‘he shall surely die; ‘this penalty is to be inflicted upon him.
	</p>
