<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:5">Matthew 15:5</span>. <strong> But ye say. </strong> God said one thing, ‘ye say’ another, and though you quote tradition, it has only your own authority.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> It is a gift to God, all that, </strong> etc. ‘That from which thou mightest have been benefited by me, is an offering to God.’ The Rabbins taught that by saying ‘corban’ of his possessions (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:11">Mark 7:11</span>), a man was absolved from the duty of caring for his parents, yet the brief expression was not considered sufficient to bind the party to devote his property to religious uses.
	</p>
