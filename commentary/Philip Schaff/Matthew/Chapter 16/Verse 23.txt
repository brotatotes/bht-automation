<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:23">Matthew 16:23</span>. <strong> But he turned.</strong> Not turned from Peter, but turned round.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Said unto Peter.</strong> In the presence of all the disciples (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:33">Mark 8:33</span>), whom Peter again represented to a certain extent.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Get thee behind me,</strong> ‘avaunt,’ ‘begone.’ Comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:10">Matthew 4:10</span>, where the same words are addressed to Satan himself.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Satan</strong>. The meaning ‘adversary’ is too weak. There was a Satanic influence at work in Peter, though he was not conscious of it. ‘Has Satan come again?’ The Apostle himself was no doubt startled.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Thou art a stumbling-block unto me,</strong> or ‘stone of stumbling.’ Perhaps a further allusion to Peter’s name. Comp, his own words (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1pe+2:7">1 Peter 2:7</span>), in which the same contrast is found. Not without a caution for those claiming to be the successors of Peter.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Thou mindest not the things of God</strong>, <em> i.e.,</em> as represented by Christ, not regarding God’s purpose in the foretold death.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> The things of men,</strong> <em> i.e.</em>, he had carnal views, expected the temporal exaltation of the Messiah. Human nature is here represented as opposed to God, and under the influence of Satan. A rebuke for all who have a sentimental admiration for Jesus of Nazareth, but stumble at the cross, which belongs to ‘the things of God.’
	</p>
