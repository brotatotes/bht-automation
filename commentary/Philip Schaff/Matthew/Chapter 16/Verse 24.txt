<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:24">Matthew 16:24</span>. <strong> Unto his disciples</strong>. To others also whom He called about Him (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:34">Mark 8:34</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:23">Luke 9:23</span>: ‘to all’).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> If any one would come after me.</strong> A general statement, involving on this occasion the question, will you follow me even to the death, which, I have assured you, must come. Unlike worldly leaders, Christ declares the darker side of His service; He asks for willing followers. A religion of <em> force</em> cannot be Christ’s religion.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Deny himself</strong>. Let him renounce self as the object of supreme regard; this involves the relinquishment of all that interferes with the higher object.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Take up his cross.</strong> The person to be crucified bore his own cross; the death was a painful and shameful one. The reference is to readiness to <em> endure for</em> Christ, even death in its worst form. It includes of course all minor forms of endurance. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:23">Luke 9:23</span>, where ‘daily’ is added. Continuous cross-bearing is implied here.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Follow me</strong>. Here in the path of suffering, but also in the path of holiness and in the path to glory, as the following verses suggest.
	</p>
