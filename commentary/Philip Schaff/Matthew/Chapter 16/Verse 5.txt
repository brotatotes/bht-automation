<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:5">Matthew 16:5</span>. <strong> And the disciples coming to the other side.</strong> To the eastern shore. It is improbable that this conversation took place during the voyage (see below).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Forgot to take bread.</strong> Provisions were not indispensable for so short a voyage. The original suggests that the neglect occurred after they landed. They had but one loaf in the boat (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:14">Mark 8:14</span>), and started on a land journey to Cesarea Philippi (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:13">Matthew 16:13</span>), through a region comparatively desolate, without making provision for it. The visit to Bethsaida on the way (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:22">Mark 8:22</span>), at a time when our Lord was avoiding public notice, may have been for the purpose of obtaining a supply.
	</p>
