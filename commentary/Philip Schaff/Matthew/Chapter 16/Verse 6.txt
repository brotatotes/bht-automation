<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:6">Matthew 16:6</span>. <strong> The leaven of the Pharisees and Sadducees.</strong> ‘Leaven; ‘figure for a permeating spiritual influence, generally an evil one (comp, however chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:33">Matthew 13:33</span>). Their want of bread made the illustration apt. They were now withdrawing, both bodily and spiritually, from the Jews; hence there is probably a reference to <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ex+12:15-17">Exodus 12:15-17</span>; comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+5:7">1 Corinthians 5:7</span>. The two opposing sects are here connected (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:1">Matthew 16:1</span>); Mark, however (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+8:15">Mark 8:15</span>), substitutes ‘the leaven of Herod.’ The Sadducees had already joined the Pharisees in opposing Christ, and Herod may have been in some alliance with them. Politicians often coquet with religious parties.
	</p>
