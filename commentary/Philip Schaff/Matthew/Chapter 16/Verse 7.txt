<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:7">Matthew 16:7</span>. <strong> And they reasoned among themselves.</strong> In their own hearts and then with each other; not in dispute, but in earnest conversation.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> It is because we took no bread.</strong> An unspiritual but not altogether unreasonable thought. As Jews they would naturally think about not eating bread with these sects; but this would imply separation from the whole nation, and separate provision for their wants, which they had forgotten. General anxiety about worldly things would follow.
	</p>
