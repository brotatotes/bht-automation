<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:1">Matthew 17:1</span>. <strong> After six days, </strong> Luke: ‘about an eight days,’ <em> i.e.,</em> ‘about a week.’
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Peter and James and John his brother.</strong> His companions in Gethsemane (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:35">Matthew 26:35</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:37">Mark 14:37</span>), Peter the leader, James the first to suffer martyrdom, and John the beloved disciple who lingered longest on earth.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> A high mountain apart.</strong> The transfiguration probably took place in the <em> night.</em> <strong> </strong> 1 . Jesus had gone up into the mountain to pray (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:28">Luke 9:28</span>), which He usually did at night (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:12">Luke 6:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:37">Luke 21:37</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:39">Luke 22:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+14:23-24">Matthew 14:23-24</span>). 2 . The Apostles were heavy with sleep. 3 . They did not descend till the next day (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:37">Luke 9:37</span>) 4 . The transfiguration itself could be seen to better advantage at night than in daylight. On Mount Hermon snow would be visible, adding a natural splendor to the scene.
	</p>
