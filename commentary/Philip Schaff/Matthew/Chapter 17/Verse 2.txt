<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:2">Matthew 17:2</span>. <strong> And he was transfigured before them,</strong> as witnesses. Peter afterwards mentions it (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1pe+1:16-18">1 Peter 1:16-18</span>) <em> </em> and John alludes to it (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:14">John 1:14</span>). <em> </em> The change in His appearance took place while He was praying (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:29">Luke 9:29</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> His face did shine as the snow, and his garments became white as the light. </strong> Mark: ‘And his garments became glistening, exceeding white; such as no fuller on earth can so whiten them.’ Luke: ‘The fashion of his countenance was altered, and his raiment became white and dazzling.’ No explanation is possible that denies the supernatural element. Our Lord’s inherent glory burst forth; added to this there was an external heavenly illumination affecting His garments and surrounding Moses and Elijah, reaching its highest manifestation in the luminous cloud spoken of in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:5">Matthew 17:5</span>.
	</p>
