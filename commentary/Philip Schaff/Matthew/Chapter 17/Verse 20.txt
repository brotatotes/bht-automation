<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:20">Matthew 17:20</span>. <strong> Because</strong> <strong> of your little faith</strong>. A general answer, the specific one is recorded by Mark (and in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:21">Matthew 17:21</span>, which is to be omitted). The attempt showed some faith, the failure ‘little faith.’ The revelation of our Lord’s death may have caused despondency and doubt.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> As a grain</strong> <strong> of mustard seed</strong>. Small, yet living (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:33">Matthew 13:33</span>), and capable of rapid increase, while their faith had decreased.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Ye shall say unto</strong> <strong> this mountain</strong>. Probably pointing to one in sight. Comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:21">Matthew 21:21</span>. This promise of power to remove the most formidable obstacles, is misunderstood, only when power over material things is deemed greater than spiritual power.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Nothing shall be impossible unto you. </strong> The statement is limited by the preceding part of the verse. Comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:22">Matthew 21:22</span>.
	</p>
