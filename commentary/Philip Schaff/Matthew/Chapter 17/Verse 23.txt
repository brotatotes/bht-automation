<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:23">Matthew 17:23</span>. <strong> They were exceeding sorry</strong>. No remonstrance now, but sorrow, partly from natural affection, partly from the dashing of their false hopes. The strife as to who should be greatest, which followed (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:1">Matthew 18:1</span>), shows that their views were still incorrect; Mark and Luke speak of their failure to understand. Men are still slow to learn the meaning of the death and resurrection of our Lord.
	</p>
