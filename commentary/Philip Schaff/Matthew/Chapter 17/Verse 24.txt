<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:24">Matthew 17:24</span>. <strong> Capernaum</strong>. His usual residence, hence the place where the temple tax would be collected from Him.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> They that received the</strong> <strong> half-shekel</strong>, which every male Jew above twenty of age paid (in addition to the tithes) for the support of the temple. Not a Roman tax, although changed into this after the destruction of Jerusalem. The receivers were not publicans, but those acting for the Jewish authorities. The value of a shekel is variously estimated from 50 to 70 cents ( <em> 2</em> <em> s. </em> <em> 3</em> <em> d.</em> to <em> 3</em> <em> s</em>.).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Doth not your master?</strong> They expected an affirmative answer. The temple tax was obligatory; see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ex+30:13">Exodus 30:13</span> ff. (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ch+24:5-6">2 Chronicles 24:5-6</span>). Josephus implies the same obligation.
	</p>
