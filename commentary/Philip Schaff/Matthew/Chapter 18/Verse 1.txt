<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:1">Matthew 18:1</span>. <strong> In that hour</strong>. As Peter returned from paving the temple tax. According to Mark (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:33">Mark 9:33</span>), our Lord first asked them about their dispute on this subject ‘in the way,’ probably to Capernaum. Hence the declaration: ‘surely then the sons are free’ (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:26">Matthew 17:26</span>), could not have occasioned this discourse. Nor did they answer His question (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+9:34">Mark 9:34</span>); His knowledge of their thoughts (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+9:47">Luke 9:47</span>) probably shamed them. An indication of the moral <strong> </strong> power of His Person.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Who then</strong>, etc. ‘Then’ hints at a previous discussion.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> The greater</strong>. Priority, not primacy. This gives room for a more general discussion.
	</p>
