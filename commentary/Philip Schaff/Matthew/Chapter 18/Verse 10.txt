<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:10">Matthew 18:10</span>. <strong> See</strong>. Little ones are made to offend through contempt or disregard for them in their littleness.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.18em;"><strong> These little ones.</strong> A direct address to the disciples in view of their question: Who shall be the greater? ‘Little ones,’ not Christians in general, nor even truly humble Christians, but rather weak, growing Christians, including children, who may and ought to be Christians.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.18em;"><strong> Their angels in heaven</strong>. They are not to be despised, since they enjoy angelic guardianship. Both the words and form are against the explanation: ‘their spirits after death.’ The incorrect order of the common version encourages this view, which is a reaction from the Romish angel-worship.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.18em;"><strong> Do always behold</strong>. An allusion to the fact that the ministers of eastern kings had access to them; suggesting that these angels were not actively employed, ‘as if God were through them always looking upon the little ones.’ The general sense is: God’s <em> highest</em> angels represent the <em> least</em> subjects of His kingdom. ‘Christ Himself, as the Great Advocate and Intercessor, is the central point of their angelic guardianship.’
	</p>
