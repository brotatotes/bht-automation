<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:28">Matthew 18:28</span>. <strong> An hundred pence</strong> <em> (denaries)=£</em> <em> 3</em> <strong><em> </em></strong> or $ 15 . A comparatively small sum. The transgressions of our fellowmen against us are trifling in comparison to our sin against God.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Took</strong> <strong> him by the throat</strong>. Allowed by the Roman law. An unforgiving spirit is quick to apply the harshest legal measures.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Pay whatever thou owest</strong>. His own debt fully forgiven, yet he insists: He who owes must pay! The payment of ‘a just debt,’ is demanded; the worst crimes have been committed under plea of ‘justice.’ That the servant ‘went out’ may be significant, since it is true that when we ‘go out’ from, forsake the presence of, our forgiving Lord, we become unforgiving. Only when near Him are we like Him.
	</p>
