<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:3">Matthew 18:3</span>. <strong> Except ye turn.</strong> As the context shows, return from this path of ambition to childlike <em> humility</em>; not implying that they had never been converted. Conversion should follow every fall. The wider application is to the absolute necessity of conversion (turning ourselves to God) in entering the kingdom of heaven. The necessity of regeneration, of which true conversion is a manifestation, is declared in John (John 3 : 37 [<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+6:63">John 6:63</span>?]).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> And</strong> <strong> become as little children</strong>. In what respect is shown in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:4">Matthew 18:4</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Ye shall in no wise enter</strong>. ‘Instead of discussing who shall be greater, you need to inquire whether you have entered it.’ This is not denied, but the ambitious question, opposed to the humility which is essential, should raise a doubt.
	</p>
