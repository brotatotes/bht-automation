<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:5">Matthew 18:5</span>. <strong> And whoso shall receive one such child.</strong> The consequence and evidence of humility; still more prominent in the other accounts. The primary reference is to children in years, but the context (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:6">Matthew 18:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:9">Matthew 18:9</span>) extends it to children in spirit. The general application is to those apparently small, those needing and receiving instruction, forbidding pride and a hierarchical spirit on the part of Christ’s disciples. ‘Shall receive,’ <em> i.e., </em> into spiritual fellowship. This implies that little children can be Christians and members of Christ’s Church.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> In my name</strong>, <em> i.e., </em> on the ground of my name; referring either to those who receive, or to those who were received, probably to both.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Receiveth me</strong>, since the ‘little one’ represents Christ. Mark and Luke insert here a remark of John’s, about one who cast out devils in Christ’s name, without following with them. The hierarchical spirit manifested in forbidding him was rebuked in part by what follows.
	</p>
