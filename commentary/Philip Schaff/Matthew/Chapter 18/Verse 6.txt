<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+18:6">Matthew 18:6</span>. <strong> Cause to offend</strong>, or ‘stumble.’ By pride, to cause others to fall into unbelief (the opposite of ‘receiving’); not a mere wounding of over sensitive feelings, or offending a morbid and incorrect sense of right. Such an application would destroy all right as well as all hope. A warning in regard to our treatment of humble Christians, especially of Christian children.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.18em;"><strong> One of these little ones which believe in me. </strong> The weak, unpretending, outwardly insignificant, the children, the poor, the ignorant, and the weak-minded are all included. Only he who feeds the lambs can feed the sheep (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+20:15">John 20:15</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.18em;"><strong> It is</strong> <strong> profitable for him that</strong> (to this end). This would be the purpose subserved by such conduct.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.18em;"><strong> A great</strong> <strong> millstone</strong>. The large stone used in a mill driven by asses.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.18em;"><strong> He be sunk in the depth of the sea</strong>. Capital punishment by drowning was common among the Greeks and Romans, probably not among the Jews. The profit of dominating over the conscience, is a burden about the neck of the offender which involves his destruction. A warning both to individual and ecclesiastical bodies. The principle proved true in the case of the Jewish hierarchy.
	</p>
