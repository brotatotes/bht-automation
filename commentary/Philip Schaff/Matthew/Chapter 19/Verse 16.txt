<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:16">Matthew 19:16</span>. <strong> Behold</strong>. The circumstance was remarkable in view of the opposition of the Pharisees.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> One came</strong>. This young ruler, who ran and kneeled to Christ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:17">Mark 10:17</span>), was an honest, earnest seeker after truth and life, with some admiration for, and confidence in, Jesus as a human teacher. But he was in error, as honest and earnest seekers may be.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> What good thing</strong>, etc. Whether a Pharisee or not, he thought to earn eternal life. Hence the passage must not be wrested in favor of legalism.
	</p>
