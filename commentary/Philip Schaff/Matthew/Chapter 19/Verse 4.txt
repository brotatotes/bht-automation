<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+19:4">Matthew 19:4</span>. <strong> Have ye not read</strong>, etc. An implied rebuke for their misunderstanding of the Scripture teaching on this point.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> He who made them</strong>, etc. The historical truth of the narrative in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ge+1:11">Genesis 1:11</span>. is assumed as the basis of an important argument. The <em> creation</em> of man is affirmed.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Male and female</strong> (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ge+1:27">Genesis 1:27</span>). The question of the Pharisees is answered by what God <em> did, </em> in the original creation of man, instituting the sexual relation, and marriage as an indissoluble union between one man and one woman.
	</p>
