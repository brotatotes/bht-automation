<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:20">Matthew 20:20</span>. <strong> The mother of the sons of Zebedee</strong>. Salome, according to an ancient tradition, the daughter of Joseph by a previous marriage; more probably the sister of Mary the mother of Jesus. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:25">John 19:25</span>, and notes on chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:21">Matthew 4:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:2">Matthew 10:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:55">Matthew 13:55</span>. The request was suggested by her sons (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:35">Mark 10:35</span>), James and John, who were called Boanerges (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+3:17">Mark 3:17</span>) and had been with Jesus on the Mount of Transfiguration (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:1">Matthew 17:1</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Worshipping him,</strong> <em> i.e.,</em> <strong><em> </em></strong> saluting <strong> </strong> Him with reverence, as was usual in asking favor of a king.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Asking somewhat</strong>. She asked a favor but did not at once tell what it was, probably because doubtful of the propriety of the request.
	</p>
