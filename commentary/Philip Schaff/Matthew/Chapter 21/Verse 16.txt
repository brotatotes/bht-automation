<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:16">Matthew 21:16</span>. <em> </em> <strong> Hearest thou what these are saying</strong>? They seem to complain that <em> children</em> express a religious sentiment, and contemptuously hint that only children call Him Messiah. Bigotry can always find some trifle on which to ground its objections.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Did ye new read?</strong> A pointed rebuke, for He quotes from the Book it was their business to read.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Out of the month of babes</strong>, etc. From <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ps+8:2">Psalms 8:2</span>, which speaks of the great God being glorified by His insignificant creatures, although we find in it a typical reference to the Messiah. Lange: 1 . The praise of the Messiah is the praise of God. 2 . The praise of children is a praise which God Himself has prepared for Himself, the miraculous energy of His Spirit. 3 . <em> The scribes might fill up the rest: Thou hast prepared praise ‘on account of Thine adversaries to bring to silence the enemy and the accuser.’
	</em></p>
