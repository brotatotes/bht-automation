<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:21">Matthew 21:21</span>. <strong> If ye have faith</strong>. Comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+17:20">Matthew 17:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:22">Mark 11:22</span>. Such faith also could perhaps exist only in Christ Himself, but as it was approximated by the disciples their power would correspond.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> To this mountain</strong>. Either the Mount of Olives, the size and exceeding difficulty being thus emphasized, or the mount on which the temple stood. The latter reference suggests that they in their faith should bring about the destruction of the Jewish theocracy. Punitive power is spoken of; hence the faith required forbids arbitrariness and also an unforgiving spirit (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+11:25-26">Mark 11:25-26</span>, where the latter thought is brought out). This promise has a spiritual application to all believers, but gives no encouragement to fanatical attempts at working miracles.
	</p>
