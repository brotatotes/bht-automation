<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:10">Matthew 22:10</span>. <strong> And those servants</strong>. Including all gospel messengers ever since.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Both bad and</strong> <strong> good</strong>. All kinds of people, without regard to their apparent moral character. The acceptance of the invitation was (and is) the great concern of the king’s servants.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> And the wedding was</strong> <strong> filled with guests</strong>. The Jews, by their rejection of the gospel, did not frustrate the grace of God. Besides the remarkable fulfilment in the early Christian centuries, there is a reference to the Church as gathered ever since from all parts of the world, of ‘bad and good,’ and containing some without ‘a wedding garment.’
	</p>
