<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:12">Matthew 22:12</span>. <strong> Friend</strong>. The word used in chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:13">Matthew 20:13</span>, and addressed to Judas (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:50">Matthew 26:50</span>). It means ‘companion,’ without implying friendship.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> How camest thou!</strong> It was a bold intrusion, a despising of the king, to appear in his own ordinary dress. This points to the pride of self-righteousness. Some think it indicates lawlessness or hypocrisy.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> He was speechless. </strong> There can be no excuse for failure to have on the wedding garment, to be righteous through and in Christ Jesus.
	</p>
