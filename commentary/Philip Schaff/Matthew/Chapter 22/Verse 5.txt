<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:5">Matthew 22:5</span>. <strong> But they made light of it</strong>. All had a guilty contempt for the invitation which was manifested however in two distinct forms: Some <strong> went away</strong>, in indifferent worldliness; others became persecutors of the messengers (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:6">Matthew 22:6</span>). Many refer ‘made light of it’ to the indifferent class alone, but the other view is more grammatical. All modes of rejecting the gospel, even persecution, are really making light of it
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> One</strong> <strong> to his own farm</strong>. ‘His own,’ in a selfish spirit.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> His merchandise</strong>. Worldliness is here represented by the two leading occupations of men. The application is, primarily, to the irreligious and careless Jewish people; then to all such in any age.
	</p>
