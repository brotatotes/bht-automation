<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:7">Matthew 22:7</span>. <strong> He sent his armies</strong>. The Roman armies which destroyed Jerusalem were the unconscious instruments of God’s (the king’s) wrath. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="isa+10:5">Isaiah 10:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="isa+13:5">Isaiah 13:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="jer+25:9">Jeremiah 25:9</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joe+2:25">Joel 2:25</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Destroyed these murderers</strong>. Both the indifferent and hostile, alike guilty.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Burned their city</strong>. Jerusalem is meant, no longer <em> His,</em> but ‘their city.’ The destruction precedes the invitation to the Gentiles (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:8-10">Matthew 22:8-10</span>). The final rejection of the Jews and the substitution of the Gentiles took place at the destruction of Jerusalem, although the gospel had been proclaimed to the Gentiles for forty years before.
	</p>
