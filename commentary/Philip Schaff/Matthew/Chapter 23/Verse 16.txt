<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:16">Matthew 23:16</span>. <strong> Ye blind guides</strong>. Wilfully blind, self-deluded (‘fools and blind,’ <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:17">Matthew 23:17</span>), they persisted in leading others astray. The method here spoken of is that of arbitrary distinctions in regard to oaths, perverting religion and morality.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Who say</strong>. Thus they taught.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> By the temple.</strong> A common oath, comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:34-37">Matthew 5:34-37</span>, where kindred oaths are referred to, and all swearing forbidden.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> It is nothing, </strong> <em> i.e.,</em> not binding; like the ‘mental reservation’ allowed and taught by the Jesuits.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> By the gold of the temple. </strong> Either the gold which adorned it, or the gold in its treasury.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> He is a debtor.</strong> This they regarded as a binding oath. Whatever their reason may have been, the Pharisees thus put the gold above the temple. A sign of covetousness, and of a tendency to exalt church ornaments above the house of God itself.
	</p>
