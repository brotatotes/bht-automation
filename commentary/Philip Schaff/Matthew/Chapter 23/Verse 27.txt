<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+23:27">Matthew 23:27</span>. <strong> Whited sepulchres.</strong> On the 15 th of Adar, before the Passover, the Jews whitewashed all spots where graves were situated. This was done to prevent the passage over them, which occasioned Levitical defilement (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="nu+19:16">Numbers 19:16</span>; comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eze+39:15">Ezekiel 39:15</span>, from which passage the custom is derived).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Outwardly indeed appear beautiful. </strong> Beside the ‘whitening,’ much care was bestowed upon sepulchres by the wealthy Jews.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Full of dead men’s bones,</strong> etc. Comp. the proper sanitary regulation of Mosaic law concerning dead bodies (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="nu+5:2">Numbers 5:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="nu+6:6">Numbers 6:6</span>).
	</p>
