<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:32">Matthew 24:32</span>. <strong> Now from the fig tree learn the parable, namely, what follows</strong>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Putteth forth leaves,</strong> or ‘its leaves.’ The blossoms precede the leaves, and when the leaves come, the fruit season is near. Comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:19">Matthew 21:19</span>. The cursing of the barren fig tree may be in mind even here. Alford: ‘As that, in its judicial unfruitfulness, emblematized the Jewish people, so here the putting forth of the fig tree from its state of winter dryness, symbolizes the future reviviscence of that race.’
	</p>
