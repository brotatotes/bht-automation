<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:48">Matthew 24:48</span>. <strong> But if that evil servant. </strong> The form is changed from that in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:45">Matthew 24:45</span>, as if to intimate that such cases would readily occur, without need of special inquiry. The verse is a caution to the faithful to persevere, and a warning to those who intrude into the ministry.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Shall say</strong>, not openly, for the official position forbids that; but <strong> in his heart</strong>, and in his conduct (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:49">Matthew 24:49</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> My</strong> <strong> lord delayeth to come</strong>. This implies that a long delay would occur. The servant began well, and still recognizes Christ as His Lord (‘my lord’). The spring of all his evil conduct was unbelief; whether the Lord came sooner or later, his duty remained the same.
	</p>
