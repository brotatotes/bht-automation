<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:49">Matthew 24:49</span>. <strong> Beat his fellow-servants</strong>. The faithful ones, since the others would join with him. He plays the lord over God’s heritage (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1pe+5:3">1 Peter 5:3</span>), abusing instead of nourishing the household (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:45">Matthew 24:45</span>). Unfaithfulness to Christ, speedily manifests itself in such conduct: censure of others, pride toward others, despotism over others, who are ‘fellow-servants.’
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Shall eat</strong> <strong> and drink</strong> <strong> with the drunken</strong>. To show laxity of conduct toward the evil members of the household, and to invite the world to help him revel. Beating the fellow-servants leads to worldliness and immorality.
	</p>
