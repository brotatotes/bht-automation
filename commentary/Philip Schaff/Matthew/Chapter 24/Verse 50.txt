<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:50">Matthew 24:50</span>. <strong> The lord of that servant.</strong> Christ is still ‘lord’ of the unfaithful and sinful servant.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Shall</strong> <strong> come</strong>. Doubt of His coming does not hinder it.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> In a day</strong>, etc. The unexpected, sudden coming is again brought forward. To the unfaithful our Lord often comes suddenly in this world, to correct while hope of amendment remains, but <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:51">Matthew 24:51</span> refers to something final. Before the Second Advent, when the whole Church shall be tried as to faithfulness, the coming to individuals is at death.
	</p>
