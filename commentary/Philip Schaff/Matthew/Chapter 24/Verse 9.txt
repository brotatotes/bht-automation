<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:9">Matthew 24:9</span>. Then, <em> i.e.</em>, ‘during this time,’ not ‘after this.’ See <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+21:12">Luke 21:12</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> They shall deliver you up</strong>, etc. Soon literally fulfilled. But it may now be referred to the spirit of persecution, always latent in the world and to break out in the last times.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Hated of all the nations.</strong> (Mark and Luke <strong> :</strong> ‘of all men;’ comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:22">Matthew 10:22</span>). The Roman historian Tacitus speaks of the early Christians as a hated race of men. But to be universally abhorred is not a proof of being a Christian. It must be for my name’s sake. This hatred has not ceased; it will probably manifest itself anew in startling form.
	</p>
