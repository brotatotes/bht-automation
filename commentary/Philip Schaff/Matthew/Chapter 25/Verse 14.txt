<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:14">Matthew 25:14</span>. <strong> For it is.</strong> The events illustrated in the previous parable, ‘The kingdom of heaven’ is not specific enough. The omission of ‘the Son of man,’ etc. (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:13">Matthew 25:13</span>) forbids our supplying ‘he is.’
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> As when a man going into another country,</strong> ‘going abroad.’ Here Christ is represented as a man of wealth; in Luke as a nobleman gone to receive a kingdom.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> His own</strong> <strong> servants,</strong> the professed followers of Christ, not merely the ministry.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> And delivered unto</strong> <strong> them his goods.</strong> The spiritual blessings which are ‘his;’ <strong> </strong> more general than chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:45">Matthew 24:45</span>, where the office of the ministry is plainly referred to.
	</p>
