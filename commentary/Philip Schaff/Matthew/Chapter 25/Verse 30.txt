<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:30">Matthew 25:30</span>. Comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+8:12">Matthew 8:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+22:13">Matthew 22:13</span>. An obvious allusion to the Marriage Supper of the Lamb, so that this and the preceding parable must refer to the same point in the future. In Luke, the nobleman becomes a king, who punishes his rebellious servants; here the parable closes with the just administration of the landowner, although the King comes into all the more glorious prominence in the last parable, <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:31">Matthew 25:31</span> ff.
	</p>
