<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:31">Matthew 25:31</span>. <strong> Now when the Son of man shall come.</strong> An interval is hinted at, but not asserted.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.18em;"><strong> In his glory.</strong> Comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:30">Matthew 24:30</span>. The ‘great glory’ culminates in ‘His glory’ (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+17:5">John 17:5</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.18em;"><strong> And all the angels with him. </strong> ‘All the angels,’ ‘all the nations;’ the former interested and active in the work of man’s salvation. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="heb+1:14">Hebrews 1:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+13:40">Matthew 13:40</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:31">Matthew 24:31</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+12:8">Luke 12:8</span>. It is an objection to the pre-millenial view that it must include the redeemed among these ‘angels.’
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.18em;"><strong> Sit upon.</strong> The sitting expresses finished victory.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.18em;"><strong> The throne of his glory.</strong> More than glorious throne;’ the throne peculiar to, manifesting, His glory. What and where it will be, we do not know; nor are these the most important questions.
	</p>
