<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+25:32">Matthew 25:32</span>. <strong> Shall be gathered.</strong> Whether voluntarily or involuntarily is not stated; but all submit (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="php+2:10">Philippians 2:10</span>). All the nations, all mankind. The pre-millenial view excepts ‘the elect,’ but of this exception there is here no hint. Even if gathered before (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+24:31">Matthew 24:31</span>), they may appear again as their Master does, at the public declaration of the gracious judgment, indicated by previously gathering them out in the days of tribulation.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.18em;"><strong> Shall separate them.</strong> A process which is further described.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.18em;"><strong> As the shepherd.</strong> Christ is really the Shepherd of all mankind.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.18em;"><strong> Separateth the sheep from the goats,</strong> lit, ‘the lambs (gentle, tractable) from the he-goats’ (proverbially wild, intractable, of less value, to which the idea of wantonness, uncleanness may be added). Together in the pasture, they are now divided.
	</p>
