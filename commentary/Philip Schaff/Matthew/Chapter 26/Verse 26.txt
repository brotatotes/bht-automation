<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:26">Matthew 26:26</span>. <strong> As they were eating.</strong> During the paschal feast, hence this was probably not the usual breaking of the Passover cakes.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Took bread.</strong> The unleavened cakes, used on these occasions, easily broken.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> And blessed.</strong> As was the custom. Luke and Paul say: ‘gave thanks,’ which is the same thing. The word ‘Eucharist’ (‘thanksgiving ‘) is a common name of the Lord’s Supper, as a feast of thanksgiving. Our Lord probably did not Himself partake.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Take, eat; this is my body</strong>. (See note above.)
	</p>
