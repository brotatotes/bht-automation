<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:29">Matthew 26:29</span>. <strong> I shall not drink henceforth.</strong> He is done with earthly rites, and at this sad moment points them to a future reunion at the Marriage Supper of the Lamb. The ordinance now receives its prophetic meaning (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+11:26">1 Corinthians 11:26</span> ‘till He come’), directing believers to the <strong> perfect </strong> vision and fruition of that time, through the foretaste which this sacrament is designed to give. It is a tame interpretation which finds here only a declaration that the Jewish Passover is superseded by the Lord’s Supper.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Drink it with you new,</strong> on some peculiar and exalted festal occasion.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> My Father’s kingdom.</strong> Not to be weakened into ‘in the Christian dispensation.’ It points to the victory of the Church, not to its conflicts; and the continued celebration of the Lord’s Supper is an expression of assured victory on the part of His militant Church.
	</p>
