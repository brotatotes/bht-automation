<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:33">Matthew 26:33</span>. <strong> But Peter answered. </strong> Instead of laying hold of the comforting part of the promise, Peter reverts to the first part.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> If all... </strong> I <strong> will never be offended. </strong> The utterance of affection, yet of self-confidence and arrogance, since ‘all’ refers to the other disciples. Hence he was allowed to fall lower than the rest. This reply differs from that given by Luke and John. Its tone points to a previous declaration respecting his want of fidelity.
	</p>
