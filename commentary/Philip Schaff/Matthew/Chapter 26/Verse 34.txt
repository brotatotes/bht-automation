<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:34">Matthew 26:34</span>. <strong> Before the cook crow. </strong> Mark: ‘Before a cock crow twice.’ The first cock crow is about midnight, and heard by few; the second, about three in the morning, is usually called ‘cock-crowing’ (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+13:35">Mark 13:35</span>). The latter is referred to here: Our Lord meant the actual cock-crowing to be a warning for Peter (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:75">Matthew 26:75</span>). It is said that the inhabitants of Jerusalem kept no fowls because they scratched up unclean worms. But this is not certain, and such a prohibition would not affect the Roman residents.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Thrice deny me.</strong> Deny knowing me (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:34">Luke 22:34</span>), a denial of any relation to Christ, virtually a denial of faith in Him, as the Son of God; in contrast with the previous confession (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+16:16">Matthew 16:16</span>).
	</p>
