<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:35">Matthew 26:35</span>. <strong> Even if I must die with thee. </strong> In Luke and John, something like this precedes the prediction of the denial: in Matthew and Mark it occurs at this point. This favors the view that two different occasions are referred to.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> In like manner said also all the disciples.</strong> The ardent spokesman influenced the rest. Their asseverations were probably not so strong, but were as inconsiderate. So ‘all’ forsook Him (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:56">Matthew 26:56</span>), but Peter alone denied Him.
	</p>
