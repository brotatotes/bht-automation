<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:37">Matthew 26:37</span>. <strong> Peter and the two sons of Zebedee.</strong> These three witnesses of His Divine glory on the Mount, were chosen to witness His human anguish in the valley. Yet they did not witness it (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:40">Matthew 26:40</span>). Their <strong> nearness </strong> seems to have been in some way a comfort to Him, though they could not help Him.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> And began to be sorrowful and sore troubled. </strong> Two ideas: first, that He was troubled with woe that falls upon Him; second, that He felt forsaken, had a weight of trouble that drove Him into solitude.
	</p>
