<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:40">Matthew 26:40</span>. <strong> Sleeping.</strong> Not sound asleep, as we infer from <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:43">Matthew 26:43</span>, but in a dozing, drowsy state. Excessive sorrow has this result (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:45">Luke 22:45</span>). Spiritual influences, too, exhaust the body. Their drowsiness does not prove insensibility; they had, however, been warned to watch.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Unto Peter, </strong> who had promised most.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> What,</strong> or ‘so then.’ This indicates disappointment, if not displeasure. His chosen friends had failed to comfort Him in this crisis.
	</p>
