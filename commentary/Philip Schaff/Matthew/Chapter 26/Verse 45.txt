<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:45">Matthew 26:45</span>. <strong> Sleep on now. </strong> Not ‘do ye still sleep? ‘but a permission, <em> i.e.,</em> Sleep on now, if you can. It is not ironical; the circumstances forbid that. They could not take their rest, for the betrayer was coming.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Behold, the hour is at hand. </strong> The hour of His enemies, the hour of darkness (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:53">Luke 22:53</span>), but with special reference to the approach of the betrayer. It is not certain that the band of Judas had already appeared.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Is betrayed into the hands of sinners. </strong> Our Lord had predicted (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+20:18-19">Matthew 20:18-19</span>), that He would be delivered to the chief priests and Gentiles; ‘sinners ‘here includes both. There is special significance in the choice of this word at such a time.
	</p>
