<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:47">Matthew 26:47</span>. <strong> Judas</strong> knew the place. He had probably represented to the rulers the ease with which our Lord could now be taken, and overruled their decision to wait (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:5">Matthew 26:5</span>). This haste favors the view that avarice was his leading motive.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> One of the twelve. </strong> Usually thus termed; here the phrase emphasizes the treachery.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> With him a great multitude. </strong> Composed of a detachment of the Roman cohort stationed in the castle Antonia (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:3">John 18:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:12">John 18:12</span>; ‘the band’); of the Jewish temple-watch (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:52">Luke 22:52</span>; ‘the captains of the temple ‘); of others, including servants and dependents of the high-priest (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:51">Matthew 26:51</span>) and, in all probability, some fanatical chief-priests and elders also (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:52">Luke 22:52</span>), who wished to witness the religious (!) capture.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> With swords and clubs;</strong> the latter in the hands of the rabble accompanying the armed soldiers. The size of the crowd may have been a recognition of our Lord’s power or designed to produce the impression on Pilate that some great plot was to be crushed, and on the people that Jesus was a great criminal. They had lanterns and torches (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:3">John 18:3</span>), for although the moon was full, they expected to take Him in a deep valley, where these might be needed.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> From the chief-priests and elders of the people,</strong> the national authorities, at whose wish the Roman authorities acted.
	</p>
