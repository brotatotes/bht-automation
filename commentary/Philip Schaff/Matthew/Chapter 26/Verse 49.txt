<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:49">Matthew 26:49</span>. <strong> And straightway.</strong> <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+18:5">John 18:5</span>, indicates that Judas appeared at first as if not directly belonging to the crowd, but soon moved in advance of them, as they fell back. He was probably excited as well as dissembling.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Hail, Rabbi. </strong> A deceitful address.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Kissed him.</strong> A stronger word than that used in the last verse (so in Mark’s account). Meyer: ‘The sign was the simple kissing; but the performance was more emphatic, a caressing, corresponding with the purpose of Judas to make sure, and with the excitement of his feelings.’
	</p>
