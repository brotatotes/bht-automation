<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:55">Matthew 26:55</span>. <strong> Multitudes.</strong> Especially the rulers and temple-guards (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+22:52">Luke 22:52</span>). Mark (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+14:48">Mark 14:48</span>) says ‘answers,’ <em> i.e.,</em> to their actions, not their words. He was probably bound, at this time, but His protest does not imply a desire to resist.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> As against a robber,</strong> not ‘a thief,’ against whom no such display of force would be needed.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Sat.</strong> Unmolested and unlike a robber.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Daily. </strong> From day to day, as during the past week.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> In the temple, </strong> the most public place in Jerusalem.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Teaching.</strong> Not unobserved, so that you needed to seek me; nor yet riotous or robbing, as your present conduct implies.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> And ye took me not. </strong> They dared not (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+21:46">Matthew 21:46</span>); the method now adopted showed the malignity of an evil conscience, and also a deceitful purpose to turn the current against Him.
	</p>
