<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:9">Matthew 26:9</span>. The best authorities omit ‘ointment’ here, but it is necessary to supply it
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.18em;"><strong> Sold for much. </strong> Pliny says that a pound of this ointment cost more than four hundred <em> denarii</em> (comp.’ three hundred pence,’ Mark and John).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.18em;"><strong> Given to the poor.</strong> This suggestion, put forward by Judas, was with him a mere pretext (see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+12:6">John 12:6</span>); the other disciples may have honestly felt it. Judas may have hoped to get the money in his possession, but not necessarily to make off with it; his intention was scarcely ripe enough for such a scheme. Those who hold trust funds, even for benevolent purposes, are often as unscrupulous in adding to them as in increasing, their private store.
	</p>
