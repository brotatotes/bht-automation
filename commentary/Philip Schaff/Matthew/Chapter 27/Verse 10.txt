<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:10">Matthew 27:10</span>. <strong> And they gave them for the potter’s field. </strong> In the prophecy we read: ‘ <em> to</em> the potter,’ a phrase of which many fanciful explanations have been given. The thirty pieces were paid to the potter for the field, and we have here a simple expansion, showing the full symbolical meaning of the prophecy.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> As the Lord appointed me.</strong> This may readily be referred to the command given to Zechariah, without searching for something similar in Jeremiah.
	</p>
