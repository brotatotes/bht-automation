<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:24">Matthew 27:24</span>. <strong> When Pilate saw that he prevailed nothing.</strong> The mob triumphed (see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:23">Luke 23:23</span>). It was a dangerous time for an insurrection and Pilate would have been called to account for it, since the Jews were constantly presenting complaints at Rome. He could not have made a defence to his superiors; so he preferred to sanction wrong, knowing and confessing it to be such.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Took water and washed his hands, </strong> etc. A symbolical act, well understood by the Jews (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="de+21:6">Deuteronomy 21:6</span>), to express freedom from guilt. But he condemned himself, even while he washed his hands.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> This righteous man.</strong> Significant language just here, when ‘this righteous man ‘is about to suffer the punishment of one (Barabbas) confessedly guilty. He suffered, the just for the unjust.
	</p>
