<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:25">Matthew 27:25</span>. <strong> His blood,</strong> <em> i.e.</em>, the guilt of the punishment, if He be innocent, <strong> be upon us.</strong> Pilate formally puts the responsibility upon them; but in a fanatical hate they assume it themselves, even adding, <strong> and on our children.</strong> Peculiar to Matthew, who wrote mainly for Jewish Christians. The imprecation has been a fearful legacy from that generation. But the curse will be turned to a blessing, and the blood of Christ be on that people in its cleansing, healing power (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:25-26">Romans 11:25-26</span>). As the persecutions of the Jews have been mainly through unjust civil enactments, compare the last cry of the chief priests: ‘We have no king but Cesar’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:15">John 19:15</span>).
	</p>
