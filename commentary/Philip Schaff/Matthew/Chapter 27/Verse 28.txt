<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:28">Matthew 27:28</span>. <strong> And they stripped him. </strong> Some ancient authorities read ‘clothed him.’ His clothing was replaced after the scourging, and probably also the robe which Herod had put on Him to mock Him (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:11">Luke 23:11</span>), usually supposed to have been white, marking Him as a <em> candidate</em> for royal honors. This robe was removed, and instead they put on him a scarlet robe, the sign of His having attained royal honors. It was probably an ordinary military cloak. Mark and John speak of it as ‘purple;’ but imperial or royal purple is more scarlet than blue.
	</p>
