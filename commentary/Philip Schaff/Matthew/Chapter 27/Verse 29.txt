<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:29">Matthew 27:29</span>. <strong> A crown of thorns.</strong> This would wound as well as mock Him, though the latter was the chief design. It is difficult to determine what kind of thorns was used. Alford says: ‘Hasselquist, a Swedish naturalist, supposes a very common plant, <em> naba</em> or <em> nubka</em> of the Arabs, with many small and sharp spines; soft, round, and pliant branches; leaves much resembling ivy, of a very deep green, as if in designed mockery of a victor’s wreath.’
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> And a reed in his right hand,</strong> as a mock sceptre. The original, according to the best authorities, represents the passive demeanor of Christ, as if His hand did not close on the reed.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> They bowed the knee.</strong> In feigned homage, greeting Him in the usual form: Hail, King of the Jews! A symbolical meaning may be found in all this mock-adoration.
	</p>
