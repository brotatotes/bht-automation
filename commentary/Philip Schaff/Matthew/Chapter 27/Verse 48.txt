<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:48">Matthew 27:48</span>. <strong> Straightway one of them. </strong> This was occasioned by our Lord’s cry: ‘I thirst’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:28">John 19:28</span>), but all occurred in quick succession.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Took a sponge. </strong> It would be impossible to use a cup.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Vinegar.</strong> The sour wine (without the ‘myrrh’) used by the soldiers, and placed there in a vessel for their refreshment. The soldiers had offered Him drink (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:36">Luke 23:36</span>) hours earlier, so that this was probably not one of them.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> A reed. </strong> ‘Hyssop’ according to John. This was to reach it to Him. The head of one crucified would be about two feet above that of one standing on the ground.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Gave him to drink. </strong> He drank (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:30">John 19:30</span>), and this reception of refreshment from one who still mocked is a token that His love vanquishes the world’s hate.
	</p>
