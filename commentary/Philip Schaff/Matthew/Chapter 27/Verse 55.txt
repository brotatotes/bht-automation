<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:55">Matthew 27:55</span>. <strong> Many women. </strong> Luke (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:49">Luke 23:49</span>) speaks of ‘all His acquaintance’ before these women. John was certainly present, probably some of the other disciples.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Beholding from afar. </strong> At one time a few ventured near the cross (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:25-27">John 19:25-27</span>), but not ‘many.’
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Who had followed.</strong> For some time, since the journey from Galilee was not direct.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Ministering unto him, </strong> <em> i.e.,</em> while they followed Him. Comp, on this ministry, <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+8:2">Luke 8:2</span>. Others, who had followed Him to Jerusalem, are distinguished from these (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+15:41">Mark 15:41</span>), but it is not necessary to suppose there were two separate groups of women.
	</p>
