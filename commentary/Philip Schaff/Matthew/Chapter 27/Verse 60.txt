<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+27:60">Matthew 27:60</span>. <strong> In his own new tomb.</strong> Peculiar to Matthew, but implied in the other accounts. The fact that it was ‘new’ (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+23:53">Luke 23:53</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:41">John 19:41</span>), seems designed to overcome any suspicion as to the identity of Him who rose. The location was in a ‘garden’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+19:41">John 19:41</span>), near the spot of the crucifixion and hence well adapted for the hurried burial.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> In the rock,</strong> an artificial excavation, probably prepared at great cost. It seems to have been cut horizon-tally and not downward.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> He rolled a great stone.</strong> The common method of closing sepulchres.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> To the door.</strong> There was but one entrance.
	</p>
