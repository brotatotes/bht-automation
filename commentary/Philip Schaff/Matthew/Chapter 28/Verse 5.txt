<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:5">Matthew 28:5</span>. <strong> Fear not ye</strong> <em> .</em> Let the soldiers fear, but not <em> ye</em>. This indicates that all were still outside the tomb.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> For I know.</strong> <em> </em> He came to help them in this hour of perplexity, not to frighten them.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Who hath been crucified.</strong> The form used is common in the New Testament, pointing to what <em> has</em> happened, but with present results.
	</p>
