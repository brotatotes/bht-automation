<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:7">Matthew 28:7</span>. <strong> Tell his disciples.</strong> This is a message to the disciples, as a body. Women bore the first glad tidings from beyond the grave.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> He goeth before you into Galilee</strong>; as had been foretold in chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+26:32">Matthew 26:32</span>. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+10:4">John 10:4</span>. The gathering of the flock in Galilee was a measure of prudence, to prevent persecution, and to dissociate them from the old temple.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> There shall ye see him</strong> <strong><em> .</em></strong> Still part of the message, and yet indirectly applicable to the hearers also.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Lo, I have told you.</strong> These words, adding solemnity to the important announcement, are peculiar to Matthew, and a mark of accuracy.
	</p>
