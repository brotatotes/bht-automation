<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+28:8">Matthew 28:8</span>. <strong> And they departed quickly from the tomb</strong> <em> .</em> As we suppose, the visits of Peter and John, and of Mary Magdalene, occurred next; then these two women met the others, and returning with them, all entered the tomb, where the message was repeated (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+24:3-8">Luke 24:3-8</span>). The word ‘quickly’ is not against this, for the events must have taken place in rapid succession.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> With fear and great joy.</strong> A natural state of mingled feeling, in view of what they had seen and heard. <em> Fear</em> at what they had seen, <em> joy</em> at what they had heard, and both mingled because the latter seemed too good to be true. The same state of mind is indicated in all the accounts.
	</p>
