<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:10">Matthew 3:10</span>. <strong> And even now, </strong> while I am speaking.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.3em;"><strong> The axe is lying at the root of the trees. </strong> The figure of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:8">Matthew 3:8</span> (‘fruit’) is carried out. The axe (Divine judgments) has not been applied as yet, but is ready for use, implying that ‘the trees’ were unfruitful, or of a bad kind. A striking declaration of imminent destruction.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Therefore, </strong> because of the position of the axe.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Bringeth not forth good fruit. </strong> There may be <em> blossoms</em>, professions, and yet no fruit, or the fruit may be <em> bad.
	</em></p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> If hewn down. </strong> Not ‘will be;’ the present tense represents a certain and immediate future action, or a general law of the ‘kingdom’ which John heralded.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Into the fire, </strong> continued figure, setting forth the effect, God’s wrath.</p>
