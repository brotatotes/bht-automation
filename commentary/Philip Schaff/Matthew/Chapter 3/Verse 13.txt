<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:13">Matthew 3:13</span>. <strong> Than. </strong> Probably about six months after John began to preach; comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:1">Matthew 3:1</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Cometh,</strong> as in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:1">Matthew 3:1</span>, a coming forth into public view.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> From Galilee, </strong> from His home in Nazareth, a long distance.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> To be baptised by him. </strong> Jesus who was sinless, came to a baptism ‘unto repentance.’ This condescension formed a part of the obedience to the Divine law (see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:15">Matthew 3:15</span>), rendered by Him as a member of the Jewish nation. The Jews were baptized in token of uncleanness, so He, ‘numbered with the transgressors,’ must needs go through the rites and purifications prescribed for them. This act closes the concealed life of quiet subjection and legal submission, opening the public life of mediatorial satisfaction. Hence He was baptized, both to fulfil all righteousness and to receive the Divine attestation; certainly not merely to honor John.</p>
