<p>The <em> culmination</em> of the ministry of John in the baptism of Jesus. The accompanying attestation: to John, a revelation that this was the Christ; to Jesus his <em> Messianic inauguration</em>. It therefore marks an epoch in the Gospel history, and doubtless in the consciousness of the God-Man Himself (see notes on <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:16-17">Matthew 3:16-17</span>). While fulfilling all righteousness (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:15">Matthew 3:15</span>), the well-beloved Son receives witness from the Father (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:17">Matthew 3:17</span>), and is baptized with the Holy Ghost (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:16">Matthew 3:16</span>). A solemn introduction into His public ministry.</p>
