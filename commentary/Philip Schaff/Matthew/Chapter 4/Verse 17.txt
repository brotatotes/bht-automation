<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:17">Matthew 4:17</span>. <strong> From that time. </strong> Either, of this settlement in Capernaum, or the imprisonment of John the Baptist.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Jesus began to preach. </strong> The beginning of the ministry in Galilee, to an account of which Matthew confines himself. During most of the time he was probably an eyewitness.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Repent: for the kingdom, </strong> etc. Comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:2">Matthew 3:2</span>. Jesus ‘began’ with the message of His forerunner. The expression ‘at hand,’ indicates that Jesus had not yet publicly declared Himself to be the Messiah. But John had announced Him; He had been accepted as such by Andrew, Philip, and Nathanael (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:41">John 1:41</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:45">John 1:45</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+1:49">John 1:49</span>),and by many others (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:1">John 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:39">John 4:39</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:41">John 4:41</span>). As He afterwards sent out His disciples with the same formula (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+10:7">Matthew 10:7</span>), His preaching at this period was not of a different character from His subsequent teachings.</p>
