<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+4:21">Matthew 4:21</span>. <strong> Going on from thence. </strong> (Mark: ‘a little further.’) All four had assisted in the great draught of fishes (see <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:7">Luke 5:7</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:10">Luke 5:10</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"> James, <em> i.e.</em>, Jacob. Probably the older brother.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> John, </strong> the Apostle and Evangelist. The detailed account he gives of our Lord’s previous ministry and miracles suggests that he was among the ‘disciples,’ he mentions (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:2">John 2:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+2:11-12">John 2:11-12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:1">John 4:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:8">John 4:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:27">John 4:27</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="joh+4:31">John 4:31</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> In the boat, </strong> a fishing boat (not a ‘ship’), probably drawn up on the shore.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Mending, </strong> or ‘putting their nets in order,’ preparing them for use. The wider sense is perhaps to be preferred.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> He called them, </strong> probably using the same words.</p>
