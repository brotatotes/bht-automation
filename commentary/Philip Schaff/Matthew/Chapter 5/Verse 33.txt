<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:33">Matthew 5:33</span>. A summary of the Mosaic precepts in regard to swearing; negatively, <strong> Thou shalt not swear falsely</strong>; <strong> </strong> positively, <strong> but shalt perform to the Lord thine oaths. </strong> (Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="le+19:12">Leviticus 19:12</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="nu+25:2">Numbers 25:2</span>.) The twofold mistake of the Jews, answered by our Lord: that only false swearing, and swearing by the name of God, were forbidden. They probably considered no oaths binding, save those in which the name of God occurred; this error, though not mentioned, is necessarily opposed.</p>
