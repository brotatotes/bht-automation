<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:41">Matthew 5:41</span>. <strong> Impress thee.</strong> <strong><em> </em></strong> The word is borrowed from the Persian, and refers to couriers pressing men and beasts into the public service, a matter very obnoxious to the Jews; it includes also the quartering of soldiers, and military requisitions, etc.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> A mile,</strong> <strong><em> </em></strong> a thousand Roman paces, about 1 , 520 yards (less than an English mile), but the proportion, one to two, is the main point. Endure double hardship, even when it seems most unjust, rather than angrily refuse.</p>
