<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+6:4">Matthew 6:4</span>. <strong> That</strong>, ‘in order that’. The mode should be chosen with a view to secrecy.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> In secret;</strong> more than ‘secretly.’ Literally, ‘in the hidden’ (place).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Thy Father who seeth in secret,</strong> in this hidden place, who is ever and everywhere present ‘Himself’ is probably to be omitted; if retained, it implies: without regard to the verdict of man.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Shall recompense thee.</strong> The terms differ from those applied to the hypocrites. The idea there is of ‘hire;’ the hypocrites have received that for which they worked; God <em> gives </em> this reward: ‘of grace, not of works.’ ‘Openly’. has but slight authority; it is literally: ‘in the open’ (place), <em> i.e.,</em> in the greatest publicity, before, men and angels at the last day. The position in which almsgiving is placed by our Lord, as well as chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:42">Matthew 5:42</span>, snow that it is a Christian duty, which can be fully discharged only in person.
	</p>
