<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:11">Matthew 7:11</span>. <strong> If ye then, being evil. </strong> An argument from the less to the greater; ‘if,’ equivalent to ‘since.’ An incidental proof of hereditary sin and general depravity. Yet some elements of good remain, such as humanity and parental affection.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Good gifts to your children. </strong> This is the rule.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> How much more. </strong> The difference is infinite.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Your Father who is in heaven.</strong> He was to be thus addressed in prayer (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+5:9">Matthew 5:9</span>); real prayer is based on this relation.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Good things. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+11:13">Luke 11:13</span></strong>, ‘the Holy Spirit,’ which is the best of the ‘good things;’ he who receives the Holy Spirit may expect all the rest, as far as ‘good’ for him. God gives good gifts only, and what He gives is always good.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> To them that ask him. </strong> Prayer is the condition which God appoints; hence trust and prayer help each other, in fact coincide.
	</p>
