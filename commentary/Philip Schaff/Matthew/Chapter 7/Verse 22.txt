<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:22">Matthew 7:22</span>. <strong> Many.</strong> The number of ‘false teachers’ is large, much more that of hypocrites.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> In that day.</strong> ‘The great day of the Lord;’ whether it be one day of account for all, or the particular day for each.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Lord,</strong> Lord. The confession (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+7:21">Matthew 7:21</span>) now becomes a cry for help.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Did we not prophecy,</strong> or preach. It those seeming to do much in Christ’s name are cast out, much more will others be.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> By thy name, </strong> <em> i.e., </em> called by thy name, and prophesying by the authority of thy name.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Cast out demons;</strong> the greatest exercise of healing power.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Mighty works. </strong> The word usually means ‘miracles.’ Judged by external results hypocrites may appear successful in spiritual works; such may have shared in the miraculous power of the early Church. Their self-deception continues to the very bar of final judgment.
	</p>
