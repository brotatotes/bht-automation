<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:27">Matthew 9:27</span>. <strong> And as Jesus passed by from thence. </strong> Probably as He left the house of the ruler, certainly while on a journey.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Two blind men followed him</strong>. Peculiar to Matthew. Blindness was common in the East, and it was natural that the sufferers consorted. To follow Him, they need only let the crowd take them along.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Crying out and saying, Have mercy on us, thou son of David.</strong> Blind men naturally use their voices a great deal. The title, ‘Son of David,’ applied to Christ by all the blind men whose recovery is mentioned by Matthew, certainly implied His Messiahship.
	</p>
