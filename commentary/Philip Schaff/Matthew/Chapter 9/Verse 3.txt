<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:3">Matthew 9:3</span>. <strong> Certain of the scribes.</strong> Many ‘Pharisees and doctors of the law’ were present, from all parts of the land (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+5:17">Luke 5:17</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Said within themselves,</strong> <em> i.e.,</em> in their hearts, as is plain from <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+2:6">Mark 2:6</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> This man,</strong> not necessarily a term of contempt
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Blasphemeth.</strong> The parallel passages base the charge on the correct premise, that God only can forgive sins. The language of our Lord must therefore have been authoritative.
	</p>
