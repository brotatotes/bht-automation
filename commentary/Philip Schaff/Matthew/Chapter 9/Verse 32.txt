<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+9:32">Matthew 9:32</span>. <strong> As they went forth, </strong> <em> i.e., </em> the blind men. This miracle must, therefore, have immediately followed the last.
	</p>
<p><strong> Behold</strong>. Another remarkable case, mentioned by Matthew alone. Both he (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+12:22">Matthew 12:22</span> ff.) and Luke (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+6:14">Luke 6:14</span> ff.) mention a similar case. Still another is mentioned by Mark (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+7:32">Mark 7:32</span> ff.)
	</p>
<p><strong> They brought to him</strong>. Probably the friends of the man, but not necessarily meaning more than: ‘there was brought.’
	</p>
<p><strong> A dumb man possessed with a demon</strong>, ‘a dumb demoniac,’ the dumbness being the effect of the possession.
	</p>
