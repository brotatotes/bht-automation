<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="php+4:11">Philippians 4:11</span>. <strong> Not that I speak in respect of want.</strong> This is not my cause of joy, that what was lacking unto me has been supplied by your bounty. It is not that I have felt want, and have been relieved from it.
	</p>
<p><strong> for I have learned. </strong> The apostle uses three verbs: ‘I have learned,’ ‘I know,’ ‘I have found the secret,’ as though he would mark something progressive in his description of what his life as a Christian preacher has taught him.
	</p>
<p><strong> in whatsoever state I am, therein to be content.</strong> He had all with him that he needed in having Christ, and of this sustaining presence the apostle was often assured. </p>
