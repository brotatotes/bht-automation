<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="re+11:3">Revelation 11:3</span>. The voice is continued, and the use of the word my connected with the <strong> two witnesses </strong> seems to indicate that it is the Lord who speaks, though in all probability by means of the ‘strong angel’ mentioned in chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="re+10:1">Revelation 10:1</span>. The witnesses receive both the words of their prophecy and the power to utter them. The duty of ‘prophesying’ laid upon them is that of proclaiming the truth of God for the instruction or warning of men; while the clothing <strong> with sackcloth, a</strong> rough cloth of goats ‘or of camels’ hair, reminds us of Elijah and the Baptist (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ki+1:8">2 Kings 1:8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+3:4">Matthew 3:4</span>), and indicates the sufferings which the witnesses shall endure in delivering their message (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ki+19:1">2 Kings 19:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ps+30:11">Psalms 30:11</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="isa+22:12">Isaiah 22:12</span>).
	</p>
