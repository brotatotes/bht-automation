<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="re+18:2">Revelation 18:2</span>. He cried with <strong> a mighty voice.</strong> This is the only passage in the book in which a voice is spoken of as ‘mighty,’ the usual appellation being ‘great.’ In chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="re+19:6">Revelation 19:6</span> we read of ‘mighty thunders’ and it is impossible to doubt, therefore, that this voice is described in a similar way, not because all men are to hear it, but because it is to strike all with awe and terror (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="re+18:8">Revelation 18:8</span>).
	</p>
<p><strong> Babylon the great is fallen, is fallen.</strong> These words have already met us at chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="re+14:8">Revelation 14:8</span> (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="isa+21:9">Isaiah 21:9</span>), but the description is now enlarged, Old Testament passages such as <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="isa+13:21">Isaiah 13:21</span>, <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="jer+51:37">Jeremiah 51:37</span>, supplying the particulars.
	</p>
<p>Everything about the city is chanced into a wild and hateful desert. The unclean beasts and birds themselves that are driven into her ruins regard them as a prison.
	</p>
