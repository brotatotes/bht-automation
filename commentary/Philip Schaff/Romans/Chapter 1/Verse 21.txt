<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:21">Romans 1:21</span>. <strong> Because. </strong> The fact which renders them inexcusable is now stated.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Though knowing God. </strong> Although they had the knowledge indicated in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:20">Romans 1:20</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Did not glorify him as God. </strong> What worship they rendered was not in accordance with the knowledge they had. ‘Glorify’ refers to praising God for what He is.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Nor give thanks;</strong> <em> i.e.</em>, did not praise Him for all his benefits.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Became vain in their thoughts. </strong> ‘Imaginations’ is inexact; ‘thoughts,’ discussions, reasonings, are meant (comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:15">Romans 2:15</span>).’ The conceptions, ideas, and reflections, which they formed for themselves regarding the Deity, were wholly devoid of any intrinsic value corresponding with the truth’ (Meyer.) ‘Vanity’ is a characteristic term for idol-worship; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="de+32:21">Deuteronomy 32:21</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ki+17:5">2 Kings 17:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="jer+2:5">Jeremiah 2:5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+14:15">Acts 14:15</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Senseless, </strong> or, ‘without understanding,’ as the word is translated in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:31">Romans 1:31</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Heart. </strong> Here, as so often in the Bible, this refers to the whole inner man.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Was darkened. </strong> (Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+4:18">Ephesians 4:18</span>.) This is the culmination of the process: not worshipping and thanking God, although they knew Him, they became vain in their reasonings; this made their heart senseless, and thus it was darkened, deprived of the truth which it might have had (formerly had) from the light of nature.
	</p>
