<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:26">Romans 1:26</span>. <strong> For this cause</strong>; namely, because of the apostasy described in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:25">Romans 1:25</span>. But as that passage repeats in another form the thought of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:23">Romans 1:23</span>, so this verse takes up anew the thought of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:24">Romans 1:24</span>. The uncleanness to which the heathen were given up took a <strong> </strong> special and aggravated form; as <strong> vile passions, </strong> lit., ‘passions of dishonor.’ Those are truthfully described, and yet with modest reticence.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> For both</strong>; or, ‘even’; but the former seems preferable on account of ‘also’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:27">Romans 1:27</span>).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Women; </strong> lit., ‘females.’ Abundant evidence of such unnatural crime is found in heathen writers.
	</p>
