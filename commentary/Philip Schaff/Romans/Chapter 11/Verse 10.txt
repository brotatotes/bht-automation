<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:10">Romans 11:10</span>. <strong> Let their eyes be darkened</strong>, etc. The reference is not to old age, but to some more sudden blinding. This verse explains the ‘recompense’ of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:9">Romans 11:9</span>. Spiritual blindness is one form of the punishment
	</p>
<p><strong> Their back do thou bow</strong> <strong> down alway</strong>. The Hebrew means: ‘make their loins to waver,’ but the LXX., here followed closely, presents the same thought under another figure. Present loss of strength is meant, representing spiritual servitude, under the yoke of legalism, rather than that of Roman conquerors.
	</p>
<p>Meyer thinks the retribution is for want of faith in Christ; Godet, with more reason, says: ‘the rejection of Jesus by the Jews was the <em> effect</em>, not the <em> cause</em> of the hardening. The cause Paul has said clearly enough (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+9:31-33">Romans 9:31-33</span>) was the obstinacy of their own righteousness.’
	</p>
