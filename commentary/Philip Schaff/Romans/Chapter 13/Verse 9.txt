<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+13:9">Romans 13:9</span>. <strong> For this, </strong> etc. Four out of the five commandments in the second table of the law are cited: The received text inserts the ninth commandment also, but on insufficient authority. The seventh commandment here precedes the sixth, as elsewhere in the New Testament (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mr+10:19">Mark 10:19</span>, received text; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+18:20">Luke 18:20</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="jas+2:11">James 2:11</span>). The same order occurs in some MSS. of the LXX; and Paul may have followed these. The tenth commandment is given in brief form. It forbids the most frequent cause of a violation of the rights of others. Only the second table is recalled, because duties to our neighbor are under discussion.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> If there be, </strong> etc. This includes the omitted commandment, whether Paul had this in mind or not
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Summed up again. </strong> The Greek word answers exactly to our word ‘recapitulate,’ to bring together <em> again </em> under one head. Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:10">Ephesians 1:10</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> This saying, </strong> lit, ‘word,’ a term applied to the commandment.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Thou shalt love, </strong> etc. The commandments were more than prohibitory, as this recapitulation by Moses plainly showed; see marginal references also.
	</p>
