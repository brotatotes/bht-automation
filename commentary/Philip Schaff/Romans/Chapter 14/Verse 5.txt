<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+14:5">Romans 14:5</span>. <strong> One man esteemeth one day above another;</strong> lit, ‘judges day above day;’ distinguishes one day from another, the reference probably being to the Jewish feasts and fasts. This is a second point of difference, but not so prominent as the first, which is emphasized throughout. The occasion of offence would be more frequent in the matter of eating and drinking
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Another esteemeth every day alike;</strong> lit., ‘judgeth every day.’
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Let every man be fully persuaded in his own mind.</strong> He does not say ‘spirit,’ but, ‘mind;’ the practical reason is to be exercised in the decision of matters of personal duty; the full conviction of an <em> educated </em> conscience should be sought for, not fancied spiritual intuitions.
	</p>
