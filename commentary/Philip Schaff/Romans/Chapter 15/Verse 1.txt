<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:1">Romans 15:1</span>. <strong> Now we that are strong. </strong> ‘Then’ is incorrect, though the connection is logically with what precedes.
	</p>
<p><strong> Ought to bear, </strong> as a burden is borne.
	</p>
<p><strong> The infirmities of the</strong> <strong> weak</strong>; all such weaknesses of faith, but particularly those referred to in the previous discussion. This bearing is often simply forbearing, but frequently involves forgiving, and self-denying. ‘Thus they, in themselves strong and free, become the servants of the weak, as Paul was servant of all; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:19">1 Corinthians 9:19</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+9:22">1 Corinthians 9:22</span> ’ (Meyer). He is indeed strong who can thus bear.
	</p>
<p><strong> And not to</strong> <strong> please ourselves. </strong> Such moral selfishness is involved in disregarding the weaknesses of the brethren who have false scruples.
	</p>
