<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:14">Romans 15:14</span>. <strong> Now I am persuaded, my brethren. </strong> This direct affectionate address indicates the return to more personal matters.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.18em;"><strong> Even I myself, </strong> or, ‘I myself also,’ The former implies: ‘even I, the one who has just admonished you, have this favorable conviction respecting you; the latter, ‘I of myself,’ without the testimony of others, or, ‘I as well as others,’ The second view accords with <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:25">Romans 7:25</span>, but the implied contrast in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:15">Romans 15:15</span> seems to favor the first.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.18em;"><strong> Ye also yourselves; </strong> ‘without any exhortation of mine’ (Alford).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.18em;"><strong> Are full</strong> <strong> of goodness</strong>; moral excellence in general, though it may be specially applied to kindness.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.18em;"><strong> Filled with all knowledge</strong>; Christian knowledge, moral as well as intellectual.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.18em;"><strong> Able also to admonish one another; </strong> without assistance from without. This is a special result of the preceding qualities ascribed to them. It requires abundance of goodness as well as of knowledge to fit us for mutual admonition.
	</p>
