<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:15">Romans 15:15</span>. <strong> But </strong> (though I am convinced of this, yet), <strong> brethren I have written </strong> (lit., ‘I wrote,’ in this Epistle) <strong> the more boldly</strong> (in contrast with the assurance of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:14">Romans 15:14</span> respecting their goodness, etc.) <strong> unto you in some measure; </strong> <em> i.e.</em>, in special places; the phrase qualifies the verb, not ‘more boldly’ as if the sense were: ‘somewhat too boldly.’
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> As putting you in remembrance again</strong>; simply as one who reminds you.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Because of the grace that was given, </strong> etc. His apostolic office is referred to in this phrase (comp. marginal references); this was the ground and reason of his boldness. But notice the humility of the great Apostle.
	</p>
