<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:26">Romans 15:26</span>. <strong> For Macedonia and Achaia. </strong> Personification for the Christians in these provinces; the latter included Greece proper.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Thought it good; </strong> ‘were well-pleased,’ willingly did this service.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> A</strong> <strong> certain contribution.</strong> The Greek word means ‘fellowship,’ ‘communion,’ and is allied with ‘communicate’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+6:6">Galatians 6:6</span>). No contribution belongs to Christian charity, unless it is willingly bestowed and as a matter of fellowship.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> For the poor</strong> <strong> saints which are at Jerusalem. </strong> Community of goods evidently did not exist in the church of Jerusalem.. The number of poor saints there need occasion little surprise.
	</p>
