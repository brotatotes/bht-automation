<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:30">Romans 15:30</span>. <strong> Now I beseech you, brethren. </strong> This fervent exhortation is the natural expression of his confidence in them and of the anticipation he has respecting what awaits him at Jerusalem (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:22">Acts 20:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:10">Acts 21:10</span>, etc.)
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> By our Lord Jesus Christ, and by the love of the Spirit.</strong> ‘For’ is incorrect in both cases; ‘through’ is the literal sense, with the same force as in chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+12:1">Romans 12:1</span>, presenting a motive. ‘Love of the Spirit’ is that affection wrought by the Holy Spirit. Between Paul and the mass of his readers personally unknown to him, only such a love could be urged as a motive. It is more extended than personal affection.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> That ye strive together with me </strong> (the figure is that of a contest in the games) <strong> in your prayers, </strong> etc. ‘Your’ brings out the force of the article; the possessive pronoun, though found in some authorities, must be rejected.
	</p>
