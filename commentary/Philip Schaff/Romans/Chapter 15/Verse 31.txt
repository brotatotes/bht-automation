<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:31">Romans 15:31</span>. <strong> That, </strong> etc. The purpose and purport of the prayer.
	</p>
<p><strong> That are disobedient. </strong> Comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+11:30">Romans 11:30</span>. The word may mean ‘unbelieving,’ and in any case the two senses are closely related, but the unbelief of the Jews is here regarded as disobedience to the gospel.
	</p>
<p><strong> And that my</strong> <strong><em> </em></strong> <strong> ministration </strong> (of alms) <strong> may become acceptable </strong> (the same word as in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:16">Romans 15:16</span>) <strong> to the saints. </strong> Besides the hostility of the Jews, he must encounter the doubts of the Jewish Christians, whom he however calls ‘saints.’ On the state of feeling here hinted at, see Excursuses, G <em> alatians, </em> chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:1-14">Romans 2:1-14</span>.
	</p>
