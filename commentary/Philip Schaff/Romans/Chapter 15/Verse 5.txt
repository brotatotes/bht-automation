<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:5">Romans 15:5</span>. <strong> Now the God of patience and comfort </strong> (as in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:4">Romans 15:4</span>). ‘He well knows that the Scripture itself is inefficacious without help of the God of the Scriptures’ (Godet). He is the source of the patience and comfort they afford
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Grant you to be of the same mind</strong> <strong> one toward another, </strong> Thus the Apostle returns to the leading thought of the section. ‘To be of the same mind <em> ’ </em> points to harmony of feeling in their intercourse rather than to unanimity of opinion on the disputed points of practice. For such harmony patience and comfort are needed; only the God of patience and comfort can produce these, but He produces them through the Scriptures.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> According to Christ Jesus. </strong> According to His example (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+15:3">Romans 15:3</span>), but also according to His will as Head of the Church and according to His Spirit as the Life of the Church.
	</p>
