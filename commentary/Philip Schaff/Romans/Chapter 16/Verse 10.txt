<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:10">Romans 16:10</span>. <strong> Apelles. </strong> Not to be confounded with ‘Apollos.’ The name occurs in Horace (Sat. i. v. 100) as that of a Jew. He may have been a freedman, as some suppose, but the name was not uncommon. There are various conjectures as to the grouping of freedmen and slaves in these salutations.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> The approved in Christ; </strong> one whose Christian steadfastness had been tested.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Of the household of Aristobulus</strong>; the Christians in that household (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:11">Romans 16:11</span>), probably slaves. There is no evidence that the person named was a believer; the phrase used has been thought by some to indicate that he was dead.
	</p>
