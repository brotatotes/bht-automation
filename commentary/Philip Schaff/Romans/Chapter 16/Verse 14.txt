<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:14">Romans 16:14</span>. The numerous group here referred to was probably intimately associated, and less known to the Apostle.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.24em;"><strong> Hormes, Patrobas, Hermas </strong> is the order of the best authorities. The last named person can scarcely be the author of the <em> Shepherd of Hermas, </em> since that work was probably not written before the middle of the second century.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.24em;"><strong> The brethren who are with them.</strong> Comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:15">Romans 16:15</span>. The two phrases may refer to household churches, or to associations of Christians for business purposes. The former seems more probable. In that case <em> five </em> assemblies are indicated.
	</p>
