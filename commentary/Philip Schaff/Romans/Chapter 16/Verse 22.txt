<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:22">Romans 16:22</span>. <strong> I Tertius. </strong> Otherwise unknown; probably an Italian, though some have sought to identify him with Silas, because the Hebrew word answering to Tertius sounds like Silas.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> Who write the epistle. </strong> ‘Wrote’ is more literal, but ‘write’ gives the sense of this epistolary aorist. Paul seems to have dictated most of his letters. Comp. <em> Galatians, </em> chap. 6. It was natural that the amanuensis, as a Christian brother, would send his salutation in the first person. In <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:23">Romans 16:23</span> the dictation is resumed.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> In</strong> <strong> the Lord. </strong> It is more natural to connect this with ‘salute’ though the Greek order permits it to be joined with ‘write.’
	</p>
