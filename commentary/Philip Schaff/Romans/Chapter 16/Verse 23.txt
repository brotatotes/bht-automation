<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:23">Romans 16:23</span>. <strong> Gains mine host. </strong> Paul was lodging with this man, as he had previously done with Aquila and Justus (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+18:1-7">Acts 18:1-7</span>). The name occurs in connection with Paul in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1co+1:14">1 Corinthians 1:14</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:29">Acts 19:29</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+20:4">Acts 20:4</span>. The same person is probably meant in the first instance, probably in the last, and possibly in all three.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> And of the whole </strong> church. This may mean that a household church met with him, or that he was universal in his hospitality to Christians.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Erastus the treasurer </strong> (lit., ‘steward’) <strong> of</strong> <strong> the city</strong>; of Corinth. This may be the person mentioned in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+19:22">Acts 19:22</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2ti+4:20">2 Timothy 4:20</span>; but in that case he had relinquished his office before the time.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Quartus the brother; </strong> some Christian brother, known to the believers at Rome, but totally unknown to us.
	</p>
