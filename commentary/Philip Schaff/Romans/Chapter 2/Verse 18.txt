<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:18">Romans 2:18</span>. <strong> And knowest his will;</strong> lit, ‘the will,’ evidently God’s will, as revealed in the law.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Approvest the things that are excellent;</strong> or, ‘dost distinguish the things that differ.’ Both translations are verbally exact, the latter being more in accordance with usage. But it gives so tame a sense here, in this glowing rebuke, that the other is to be preferred.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Being instructed,</strong> etc. This was the means by which the will of God was known, and the excellent things approved. There is a reference to the public reading and exposition of the law in the synagogue.
	</p>
