<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:21">Romans 2:21</span>. <strong> Thou therefore. ‘</strong> Therefore’ <em> </em> sums up what has been previously said. ‘Being such an one, to thee, I say,’ etc. The questions imply surprise at such a state of things, and rebuke it.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Teachest thou not thyself. </strong> This is the general accusation, that the conduct of the Jew did not agree with his knowledge and assumed position, set forth in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:17-20">Romans 2:17-20</span>. These specifications follow, with a summing up of the result in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:23">Romans 2:23</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Dost thou steal</strong>. <strong> </strong> In this charge there is probably a reference ‘to the passionate and treacherous method of transacting business adopted by the Jews; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="jas+4:13">James 4:13</span>.’ (Lange.)
	</p>
