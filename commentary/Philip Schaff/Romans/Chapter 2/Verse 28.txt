<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:28">Romans 2:28</span>. <strong> For.</strong> This introduces the proof of the previous positions, <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:27">Romans 2:27</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> He is not a Jew who is one outwardly</strong>. This gives the sense of the original; but in this and the succeeding verse the construction is peculiar. The one who shows only the outward marks of a Jew is not a true Jew.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Which is outward</strong>. The same phrase just rendered ‘outwardly.’
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> In the flesh</strong>. This is a further explanation of ‘outward,’ and is to be taken literally.
	</p>
