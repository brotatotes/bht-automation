<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:5">Romans 2:5</span>. <strong> But. </strong> With this tendency of the goodness of God is contrasted the conduct of man. Instead of being thereby led to repentance, men allow themselves to fancy that God’s goodness is a <strong> </strong> proof that He will not punish sin.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> After thy hardness and impenitent heart. </strong> As might be expected from, in accordance with and occasioned by, thy hardness, etc.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Treasurest up for thyself</strong>; thou for thyself, not God for thee. ‘The despising of the riches of God’s goodness in forbearance and long suffering is the heaping up of a treasure of wrath’ (Lange).
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> In the day of wrath</strong>; wrath which will be revealed in the day of wrath; ‘against’ is quite incorrect.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> And revelation, </strong> etc. This qualifies ‘day.’ God’s ‘righteous judgment’ (one word in Greek) will not be fully revealed until the great day of final judgment
	</p>
