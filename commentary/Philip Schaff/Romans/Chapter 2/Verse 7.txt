<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:7">Romans 2:7</span>. <strong> By endurance</strong>, perseverance, steadfastness, rather than ‘patience,’ is the idea of the word, and the preposition in the original points to the standard according to which the action is performed.
	</p>
<p><strong> In good work. </strong> The singular is used to express the character as a unit. (‘Well-doing’ obscures the correspondence with ‘works,’ <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:6">Romans 2:6</span>.) The whole phrase qualifies the verb.
	</p>
<p><strong> Seek for glory and honor and incorruption. </strong> Future salvation is thus described as the object of pursuit; it is ‘glory,’ because of its splendid manifestation; ‘honor,’ because it is a reward; ‘incorruption,’ because it is eternal. Whether any who are not Christians have thus sought, is not declared by the Apostle; comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+2:14">Romans 2:14</span>.
	</p>
<p><strong> Eternal life. </strong> This is what God will render to the class just spoken of. The phrase is distinctively Christian.
	</p>
