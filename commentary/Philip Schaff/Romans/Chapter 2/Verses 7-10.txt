<p>A To them that by endurance in good work
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3.6em;"> Seek for gory and honor and incorruption,
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:7.2em;"> Eternal life: B But to them that are self-seeking
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3.6em;"> And disobey the truth, but obey unrighteousness
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:7.2em;"> Shall be wrath and indigination. B Tribulation and anguish,
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3.6em;"> Upon every soul of man that is working out evil,
	</p>
<p>Of the Jew first, and also of the Greek A But glory and honor and peace,
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:3.6em;"> To every man that is working good,
	</p>
<p>To the Jew first, and also to the Greek The first and fourth, second and third stanzas are respectively parallel, but the lines in the first and second, give (1) the <em> character, </em> (2) the <em> pursuits</em>, <em> </em> and the <em> reward </em> of the opposite classes, the third and fourth stanzas reverse this order.
