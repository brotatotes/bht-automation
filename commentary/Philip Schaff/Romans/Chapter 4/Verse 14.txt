<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:14">Romans 4:14</span>. <strong> For if, </strong> etc. The proof of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:13">Romans 4:13</span> is now given (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:14-17">Romans 4:14-17</span>), from the nature of the law, and the consequent necessity of faith as the ground of inheritance.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> They that are of the law. </strong> Comp. the contrasted idea, chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:26">Romans 3:26</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+3:7">Galatians 3:7</span>. Those who belong to the law are of that party whose religious life springs from the law, and who are legalists in character.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Faith is made void, </strong> is made empty and continues so, there is no use of it.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.06em;"><strong> Of none effect. </strong> The promise is made permanently invalid. Why so? The reason is given in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:15">Romans 4:15</span>.
	</p>
