<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:23">Romans 4:23</span>. <strong> How it was not written for his sake alone. </strong> The rest of the chapter states in plain language the application of the case of Abraham to the gospel believers. Thus Paul shows that God is the God of <em> all </em> believers, and that we establish the law through faith (chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:28-31">Romans 3:28-31</span>). The phrase ‘it is written,’ which occurs here, is not the usual one: it denotes the past historical act of writing, and emphasizes the <em> design </em> of God’s Spirit in causing it to be written; the usual phrase points to the permanent validity of the Scriptural quotation. Here, as throughout the Epistle, the Apostle insists that the whole Old Testament pointed to the <em> universality </em> of Christianity.
