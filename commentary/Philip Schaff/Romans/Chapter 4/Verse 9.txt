<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:9">Romans 4:9</span>. <strong> Is this blessedness then, </strong> etc. ‘This pronouncing blessed, then, <em> is it </em> upon,’ etc. The reference is to David’s words. The inference, in the form of a question, is, that this declaration of blessedness affects <strong> the uncircumcision also, </strong> for an affirmative answer to this clause is implied in the form of the original.
	</p>
<p><strong> For we say </strong> ( <em> i.e., </em> in accordance with the quotation in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:3">Romans 4:3</span>). This begins the proof from the case of Abraham, by restating the Scriptural fact. The further facts and conclusion follow. ‘That’ should be omitted.
	</p>
<p><strong> To Abraham, </strong> etc. The emphasis rests on ‘Abraham,’ as the emended order indicates.
	</p>
<p><strong> His faith, </strong> lit., ‘the faith,’ the faith just spoken of in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+4:3">Romans 4:3</span>.
	</p>
