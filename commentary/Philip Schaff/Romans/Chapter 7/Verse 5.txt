<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:5">Romans 7:5</span>. <strong> For. </strong> A confirmation of the statement that they should bring forth fruit to God.
	</p>
<p><strong> When we were in the flesh, </strong> <em> i.e., </em> in the natural condition of depravity (see Excursus at next section); still under the law is the negative side.
	</p>
<p><strong> The passions of sins. </strong> The passions which led to sins seems a better explanation than either ‘sinful passions,’ or the passions produced by sins.
	</p>
<p><strong> Which were through the law;</strong> occasioned by the law, since the law brought them to light, but aggravated them, as is shown in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:7-8">Romans 7:7-8</span>.
	</p>
<p><strong> Wrought in our members</strong>; to be explained literally as in chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:13">Romans 6:13</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:19">Romans 6:19</span>.
	</p>
<p><strong> To bring forth fruit to death. </strong> Parallel to the last clause of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:4">Romans 7:4</span>, hence expressing the aim as well as the consequence of the working of the passions. ‘Death’ is to be explained as in chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+6:21">Romans 6:21</span>.
	</p>
