<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:22-23">Romans 7:22-23</span>. We have four phrases contrasted in pairs: ‘The law of God;’ ‘another law in my members,’ etc.; ‘the law of my mind;’ ‘the law of sin and death,’ etc. Each phrase has its distinct meaning, while those forming pairs are closely related: The law of God is the Mosaic law, but the law of the mind is the same law so far as it is <em> operative </em> in the mind; the law in the members is the law of sin, so far as it is operative in the members; the extreme contrast is between the law of God and the law of sin and death. ‘The law’ of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+7:21">Romans 7:21</span> is this principle of moral conflict which the Apostle found in his experience.
	</p>
