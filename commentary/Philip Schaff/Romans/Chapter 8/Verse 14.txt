<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:14">Romans 8:14</span>.
	</p>
<p><strong> For as many as, </strong> etc. This introduces the reason why we ‘shall live <strong> ,</strong> ’ indicating again that the mortifying (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:13">Romans 8:13</span>) is the work of the Holy Spirit.
	</p>
<p><strong> Led by the Spirit of God</strong>; continuously and specially moved by the Spirit, in their whole life. ‘The passive form expresses its complete dominion, without at the same time denying the voluntary <em> being led </em> on the part of the human will’ (Lange).
	</p>
<p><strong> These are sons of God.</strong> These, and none other. In the Epistle to the Galatians there is a similar line of argument, but with more of a polemical purpose; yet even here there is an implied contrast with the Jewish notion that by birth they were entitled to this sonship.
	</p>
