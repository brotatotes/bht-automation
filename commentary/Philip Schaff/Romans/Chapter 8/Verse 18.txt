<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:18">Romans 8:18</span>. <strong> For. </strong> This connects the verse with the whole thought culminating in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:17">Romans 8:17</span> (see above), and not with ‘glorified’ alone.
	</p>
<p><strong> I reckon. </strong> No doubtful calculation is implied; comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+3:28">Romans 3:28</span>. Alford paraphrases: ‘I myself am one who have embraced this course, being convinced that.’
	</p>
<p><strong> Insignificant in comparison with. </strong> This paraphrase gives the correct sense; ‘not worthy’ is more literal, but objectionable as suggesting the idea of merit, which is foreign to the course of thought.
	</p>
<p><strong> The glory</strong> <strong> which shall be</strong> <strong> revealed. </strong> At the end of ‘the present time <strong> ,</strong> ’ when full redemption comes with the coming of the Lord.
	</p>
<p><strong> In us</strong>. In us and upon us, or, ‘to usward <strong> ,</strong> ’ as the phrase is rendered in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+1:19">Ephesians 1:19</span>. Of this glory Christians are the subjects, the possessors, and the centre also, for <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:19-23">Romans 8:19-23</span> represent the creation as sharing in it.
	</p>
