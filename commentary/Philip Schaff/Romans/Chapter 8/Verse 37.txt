<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:37">Romans 8:37</span>. <strong> Nay</strong>; literally, ‘but.’ Some connect this with <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:35">Romans 8:35</span>, making <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:36">Romans 8:36</span> parenthetical, but this is not necessary, for the course of thought is unbroken, and this verse is antithetical to both <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:35-36">Romans 8:35-36</span>.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> In all these things</strong>; just mentioned.
	</p>
<p style="margin-left:0.12em;"><strong> We are more than conquerors</strong>. A single word in the Greek: ‘over conquer;’ we are over-victorious. This tone of triumph is not selfish, for the abounding victory is through him who loved us. That the reference is to Christ, appears from the context <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:35">Romans 8:35</span> (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:39">Romans 8:39</span>); from the tense used, which points to one crowning <strong> </strong> act of love (comp. chap. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+5:6">Romans 5:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ga+2:20">Galatians 2:20</span>), His death on the cross. Since His love conquered death, even in death we cannot be separated from His love, but are more than conquerors.</p>
