<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:5">Romans 8:5</span>. <strong> For they, </strong> etc. In chap. 7 the contrast was between the workings of the law and the flesh in the same person; in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:5-8">Romans 8:5-8</span> the Apostle contrasts two classes of persons; snowing why the righteousness of the law is fulfilled in one class, and cannot be in the other.
	</p>
<p><strong> That are cording to the flesh. </strong> The same idea as in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:4">Romans 8:4</span>, but under a slightly different aspect: walking according to the flesh pointing to the outward life; being according to the flesh, to the carnal state.
	</p>
<p><strong> Mind the things of the flesh; </strong> they think of, care for, strive to obtain, those things which belong to the ‘flesh,’ which includes all that gratifies the depraved heart; ‘not merely sensual things, but all things which do not belong to the category of the things of the Spirit’ (Hodge).
	</p>
<p><strong> The things of the Spirit, </strong> those things which belong to the Holy Spirit.
	</p>
