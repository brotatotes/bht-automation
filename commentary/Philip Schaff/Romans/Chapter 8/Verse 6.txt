<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:6">Romans 8:6</span>. <strong> For the mind of</strong> <strong> the flesh. </strong> Explanation of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:5">Romans 8:5</span>. The word ‘mind’ corresponds with the verb ‘mind’ in the last verse; it is that which embodies the thinking, caring, striving; the disposition, we might call it
	</p>
<p><strong> Is death</strong>; amounts to death. ‘Death is here conceived of as present (comp. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1ti+5:6">1 Timothy 5:6</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:1">Ephesians 2:1</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="eph+2:5">Ephesians 2:5</span>), not merely as a result, but as a characteristic mark, an immanent definition of the carnal mind’ (Philippi).
	</p>
<p><strong> The mind of the Spirit. </strong> Here also the Holy Spirit; the minding, striving, which comes from the Holy Spirit.
	</p>
<p><strong> Life and peace. </strong> ‘Life’ is to be taken in its full sense, in contrast with ‘death;’ ‘peace ‘is added, probably to prepare for <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:7">Romans 8:7</span>, where ‘enmity’ is introduced.
	</p>
