<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:8">Romans 8:8</span>. <strong> And. </strong> Not, ‘so then,’ but a simple continuation of the thought of <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:7">Romans 8:7</span>.
	</p>
<p><strong> They that are in the flesh. </strong> Substantially the same as: ‘they that are according to the flesh’ (<span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+8:5">Romans 8:5</span>), but stronger, and presenting a better contrast to the full gospel phrases: ‘in Christ’ ‘in the Spirit’
	</p>
<p><strong> Cannot please God, </strong> because of the character of the mind of the flesh. By this negative expression, ‘it is said, in a mild way, that they are objects of Divine displeasure, children of wrath’ (Lange).
	</p>
