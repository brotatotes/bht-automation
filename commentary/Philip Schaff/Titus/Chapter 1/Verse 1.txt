<p><em> The Salutation, </em> 1-4.
	</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:1">Titus 1:1</span>. The title servant of God, occurring in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="jas+1:1">James 1:1</span>, but nowhere else in Paul’s inscriptions, is a peculiarity which tells against suspicion of forgery.
	</p>
<p><strong> According</strong> to (better, ‘with a view to’), introduces the double design of his apostle-ship, viz: (1) the production of faith in God’s chosen ones (or of that genuine faith which only the chosen have) (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+1:5">Romans 1:5</span>); and (2) the production in believers of <strong> a full knowledge (</strong> not ‘acknowledging’ <strong> ) of the truth,</strong> or entire Gospel revelation. A dual design: faith and knowledge. Against certain errorists, this truth tends to godliness, a Pauline word frequent in Pastoral Epistles, denoting that religious fear of God in the heart which penetrates and rules the whole conduct.
	</p>
