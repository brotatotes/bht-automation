<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:2">Titus 1:2</span>. These two ends of the apostolate rest upon (not ‘in’) the <strong> hope of eternal life</strong>, which is the sum of Old Testament prediction, fulfilled in the Gospel.
	</p>
<p><strong> Cannot lie.</strong> Cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="heb+6:18">Hebrews 6:18</span>.
	</p>
<p><strong> Before the world began;</strong> literally, before eternal times, meaning probably from the most ancient periods, as in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="lu+1:70">Luke 1:70</span>. To understand with Ellicott and Alford ‘from all eternity,’ gives an incorrect sense. <strong> Promised</strong> must then mean ‘decreed to promise.’
	</p>
