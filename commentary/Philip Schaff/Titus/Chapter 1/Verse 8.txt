On the contrary, he should be (6) <strong> hospitable, </strong> having then frequent occasion to entertain brethren on their travels (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="3jo+1:5-8">3 John 1:5-8</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:2">Romans 16:2</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ro+16:23">Romans 16:23</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="ac+21:16">Acts 21:16</span> <strong> , </strong> etc.); (7) <strong> a lover of good, </strong> <em> i.e. </em> disposed to generous actions; (8) <strong> sober</strong>: this word, which with its derivatives occurs so often in Pastoral Epistles, denotes ‘the well-balanced state of mind resulting from habitual self-restraint’ (Ellicott). In that time of morbid religious excitement, Paul greatly missed as he grew older moral and intellectual soundness or perfect sanity. (9) <strong> Just </strong> toward men; (10) <strong> holy </strong> towards God, combining piety with purity; and (11) <strong> temperate, </strong> as to one’s own appetites and passions self-restrained.
