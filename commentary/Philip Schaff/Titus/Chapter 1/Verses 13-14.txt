<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:13">Titus 1:13</span>. Paul boldy adds his own testimony to base on it an exhortation to a sharp or severe handling of the people.
	</p>
<p><strong> Rebuke </strong> is ‘confute,’ as in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:9">Titus 1:9</span> the elders were to do.
	</p>
<p><strong> Sharply, </strong> or unsparingly, with a view to their becoming <strong> sound in the faith. </strong> The Gospel has power to subdue the wildest natures.
	</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:14">Titus 1:14</span> defines the evil to be cured (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1ti+1:4">1 Timothy 1:4</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1ti+4:1">1 Timothy 4:1</span>).
	</p>
<p><strong> Fables; </strong> literally, ‘myths, fantastic fictions about the world of spirits, nourished by the secret teaching traditional among the Jews’ (Matthies); ‘Rabbinical fables and fabrications, whether in history or doctrine’ (Ellicott). Word only found in Pastoral Epistles and in <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="2pe+1:16">2 Peter 1:16</span>.
	</p>
<p>The <strong> commandments </strong> were late glosses on the Mosaic law with no moral basis, chiefly turning on distinction between clean and unclean (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="mt+15:9">Matthew 15:9</span> and Mark vii 7). Against these last, <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:15">Titus 1:15</span> lays down the broad rule of Christian faith which cuts false asceticism to the root.
	</p>
