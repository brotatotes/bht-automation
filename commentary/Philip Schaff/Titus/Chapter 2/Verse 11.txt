<p><em> Basis in Christian doctrine for the foregoing admonitions, </em> 11-15.
	</p>
<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:11">Titus 2:11</span>. Christ’s work is the <strong> appearance, </strong> or literally, epiphany, of that Divine <strong> grace</strong> or ‘favour’ to man (cf. <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:4">Titus 3:4</span>) which had previously been concealed. Grace is the ground of redemption; redemption the manifestation of grace.
	</p>
<p><strong> The grace that is saving appeared</strong> (not ‘hath appeared,’ for word refers to a definite past event), ‘like the dawn’ (Wordsworth). We may either read, ‘the grace that saves all men appeared,’ <em> i.e. </em> men of all races and orders; or, ‘grace appeared to all men, bringing salvation.’ Former perhaps to be preferred.
	</p>
