<p><span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="tit+3:4">Titus 3:4</span>. Two very human words are selected to describe God’s grace in its manifestation, because Paul is enforcing kindness among men. God set the example of benevolence and philanthropy. Read ‘love-toward-man’ as one word.
	</p>
<p><strong> God our</strong> <strong> Saviour</strong>; rather, ‘our Saviour God,’ cf. 1Ti 1:1 ; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="1ti+2:3-5">1 Timothy 2:3-5</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="tit+1:3">Titus 1:3</span>; <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:10">Titus 2:10</span>.
	</p>
<p><strong> Appeared </strong> looks back to <span class="scriptRef" com="scn" lang="eng" ref="tit+2:11">Titus 2:11</span>. The Divine character is the root of salvation, not human merit.
	</p>
